Pandanus magnificus

Pandanus magnificus, commonly known as the giant pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to three meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that eventually develop into spiky, fibrous fruits. Its roots system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in coastal and swampy environments.

The plant is often used for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in various island communities.
