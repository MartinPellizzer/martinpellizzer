Piper fulvescens var. subglabrum

Piper fulvescens var. subglabrum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, opposite leaves that are smooth and glossy. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, round berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in various herbal remedies.
