Phegopteris faucia

Phegopteris faucia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically submerged or floating in freshwater environments. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping stems that help anchor it in moist soils or among aquatic vegetation. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Phegopteris faucia plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen through photosynthesis.
