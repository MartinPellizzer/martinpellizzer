Pseudanthus occidentalis

Pseudanthus occidentalis, commonly known as the western pseudanthus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, yellow to orange disk flowers surrounded by pale green to white ray florets. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

Pseudanthus occidentalis is well adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system and reduced leaf surfaces to minimize water loss.
