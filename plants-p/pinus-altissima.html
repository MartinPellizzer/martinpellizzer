Pinus altissima

Pinus altissima, commonly known as the Coulter pine, is a tall evergreen conifer native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its long, slender needles and distinctive, thick, furrowed bark. The tree produces large, woody cones that contain edible seeds, which are a food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and can grow in full sun.

This species is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ecological and historical significance.
