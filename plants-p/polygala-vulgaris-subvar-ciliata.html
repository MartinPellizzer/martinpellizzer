Polygala vulgaris subvar. ciliata

Polygala vulgaris subvar. ciliata, commonly known as the woolly milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant has feathery leaves with ciliate margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
