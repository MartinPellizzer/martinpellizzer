Phyllanthus myrsinites subsp. platyphyllus

Phyllanthus myrsinites subsp.

platyphyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaf sandmat, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East. It features small, clustered leaves that are typically broad and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in dry environments.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are small and inconspicuous, often growing in terminal clusters. Its fruit is a capsule containing small, hard seeds.

This species is valued for its drought tolerance and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
