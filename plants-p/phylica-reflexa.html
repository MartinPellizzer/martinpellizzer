Phylica reflexa

Phylica reflexa, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
