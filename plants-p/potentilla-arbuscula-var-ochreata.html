Potentilla arbuscula var. ochreata

Potentilla arbuscula var. ochreata, commonly known as the ochre cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a mat-forming habit with hairy, ovate leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
