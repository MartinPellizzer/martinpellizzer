Phyllomphax galeandra

Phyllomphax galeandra, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and green sepals that form a star-like appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often pollinated by insects such as bees and butterflies.

Phyllomphax galeandra is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
