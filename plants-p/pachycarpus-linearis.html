Pachycarpus linearis

Pachycarpus linearis, commonly known as the slender pachycarpus, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and linear, narrow leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fruit that contains several seeds. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is well-suited to xerophytic conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens as an ornamental plant.
