Potentilla vilmoriniana

Potentilla vilmoriniana, commonly known as the Vilmorin's cinquefoil, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries such as Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically divided into five oval-shaped leaflets, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

The plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable choice for rocky or alpine gardens.
