Podocarpium mandshuricum

Podocarpium mandshuricum, commonly known as the Far Eastern podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant typically grows in temperate to subtropical climates, often in mountainous regions with moist, well-drained soils. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
