Piper cabadbaranum

Piper cabadbaranum, commonly known as the Bornean pepper or Kadabaran pepper, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into peppercorns. Its fruit, which is typically green or red when mature, is used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.
