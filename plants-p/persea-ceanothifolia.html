Persea ceanothifolia

Persea ceanothifolia, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glossy and lance-shaped. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, orange to reddish fruits.

The fruit contains a single seed and is valued for its sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.
