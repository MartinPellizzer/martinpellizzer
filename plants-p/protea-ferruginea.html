Protea ferruginea

Protea ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The plant typically grows in fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a rusty-colored bloom.

The rusty protea is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
