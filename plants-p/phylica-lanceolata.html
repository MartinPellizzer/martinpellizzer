Phylica lanceolata

Phylica lanceolata, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, snow-like flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are adapted for bird dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Phylica lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
