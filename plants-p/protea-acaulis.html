Protea acaulis

Protea acaulis, commonly known as the rock protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, cone-shaped inflorescences that resemble a crown. The plant is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found growing on rocky outcrops or slopes. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with a central disc surrounded by bracts.

Protea acaulis plays an important role in its native ecosystem and is also cultivated for its ornamental value.
