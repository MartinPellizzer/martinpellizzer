Periandra caespitosa

Periandra caespitosa, commonly known as the clustered periandra, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Periandra caespitosa is often found in coastal or sandy habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
