Pavonia rhodantha

Pavonia rhodantha, commonly known as the scarlet pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open woodlands and disturbed soils. The plant features showy, red or pink bract-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Pavonia rhodantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
