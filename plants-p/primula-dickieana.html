Primula dickieana

Primula dickieana, commonly known as the Dickie's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
