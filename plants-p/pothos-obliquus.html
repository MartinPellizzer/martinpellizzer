Pothos obliquus

Pothos obliquus, commonly known as the creeping pothos, is a trailing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slightly variegated pattern. The plant is known for its vigorous growth and ability to climb or trail along surfaces. It thrives in bright, indirect light and is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular houseplant.

Its botanical characteristics include opposite leaf arrangement and small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers.
