Paraspalathus microcarpa

Paraspalathus microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, clustered flowers that are often surrounded by bracts. The plant produces tiny, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in well-drained soils.

Paraspalathus microcarpa is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.
