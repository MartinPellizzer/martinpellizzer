Panicum nudiglume

Panicum nudiglume, commonly known as the naked lemma panicgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, two-lobed spikelets with glumes that are reduced or absent, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, and it typically grows in open, sunny habitats.

Panicum nudiglume is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in disturbed areas.
