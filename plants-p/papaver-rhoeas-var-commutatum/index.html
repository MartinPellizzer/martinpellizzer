Papaver Rhoeas Var. Commutatum

Papaver rhoeas var. commutatum is a rare variant of the common poppy known for its unique floral characteristics. This plant is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats across Europe. Its flowers display a striking blend of colors, ranging from pale yellow to deep red. It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and maintaining ecological balance. Despite its beauty, it remains underappreciated and threatened by habitat loss.
