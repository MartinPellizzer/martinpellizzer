Panicum maximum var. pubiglume

Panicum maximum var. pubiglume, commonly known as Guinea grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its robust growth, with upright stems and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant produces panicles of spikelets that are often covered with short, soft hairs, contributing to its common name. It is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in open fields and pastures.

Due to its high nutritional value, it is widely used as forage for livestock.
