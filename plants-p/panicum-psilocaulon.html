Panicum psilocaulon

Panicum psilocaulon, commonly known as the purple panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in ecological restoration projects.
