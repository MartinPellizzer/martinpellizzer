Protea wangenheimii

Protea wangenheimii, commonly known as the Wagenheim's protea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its striking, cone-shaped inflorescence composed of numerous small, petal-like bracts. The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers come in shades of white, pink, and red, and it blooms during the spring and summer months.

Protea wangenheimii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and long-lasting blooms.
