Peperomia nativitatis

Peperomia nativitatis, commonly known as the Nativitatis Peperomia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are often glossy and vary in color from green to reddish hues. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, arranged in clusters.

Peperomia nativitatis is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
