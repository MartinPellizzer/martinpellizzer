Pelecynthis axillaris

Pelecynthis axillaris, commonly known as the rock fig, is a small, evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
