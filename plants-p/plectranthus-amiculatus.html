Plectranthus amiculatus

Plectranthus amiculatus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its velvety, ovate leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of violet, pink, and white. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Plectranthus amiculatus is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
