Puschkinia kurdica

Puschkinia kurdica, commonly known as Kurdish glory-of-the-snow, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and Iran.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Puschkinia kurdica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize over time.
