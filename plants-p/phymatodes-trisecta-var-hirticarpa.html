Phymatodes trisecta var. hirticarpa

Phymatodes trisecta var. hirticarpa, commonly known as the hirsute three-lobed phymatodes, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are deeply lobed and covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
