Platanthera bifolia var. quadrifolia

Platanthera bifolia var. quadrifolia, commonly known as the four-leaf lady's tresses, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae).

It is a terrestrial herb with slender, often branching stems that bear whorled leaves. The plant produces a dense, spike-like inflorescence of small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in four rows, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows.

The species is characterized by its unique floral structure and is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
