Penstemon parvus

Penstemon parvus, commonly known as the narrowleaf beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as prairies and open woodlands. The plant features upright stems with opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy, two-lipped flowers are usually pink to purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Penstemon parvus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
