Panicum philadelphicum var. tuckermanii

Panicum philadelphicum var. tuckermanii, commonly known as Tuckerman's panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green blades that often have a reddish tint. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds in panicles, which are typically visible in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy ornamental grass for gardens and natural landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and ability to form dense clumps make it a valuable plant for erosion control and wildlife habitat.
