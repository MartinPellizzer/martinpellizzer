Polygala figariana

Polygala figariana, commonly known as the fig-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually small and succulent, helping to conserve water in dry climates.

The flowers are usually small and pale, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by specific insect species.
