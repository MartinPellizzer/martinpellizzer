Polypodium haalillioanum

Polypodium haalillioanum, commonly known as the Haalilio fern, is a tropical fern species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest undergrowth. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores for propagation.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
