Pandanus butyrophorus

Pandanus butyrophorus, commonly known as the buttercup pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its sword-shaped leaves and branched inflorescences. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often used in traditional crafts and culinary practices. Its roots and leaves are also valued for their aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

The species is adapted to grow in coastal and wetland environments, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
