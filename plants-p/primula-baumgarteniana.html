Primula baumgarteniana

Primula baumgarteniana, commonly known as the Alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are usually basal, heart-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Primula baumgarteniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
