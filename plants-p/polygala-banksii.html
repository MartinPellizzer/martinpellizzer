Polygala banksii

Polygala banksii, commonly known as the Banks' milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often spiny stems and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Polygala banksii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
