Plectranthus urticifolius

Plectranthus urticifolius, commonly known as roughleaf plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Plectranthus urticifolius is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can also serve as a source of nectar for pollinators.
