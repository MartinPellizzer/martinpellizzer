Polygala alba

Polygala alba, commonly known as the white milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant features slender, erect stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, and it produces small, legume-like pods that contain seeds.

Polygala alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
