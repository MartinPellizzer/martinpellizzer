Poraqueiba rhodoxylon

Poraqueiba rhodoxylon, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its strength and aesthetic qualities. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by small, round fruits containing seeds.

This species is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in local ecosystems.
