Peperomia guttulata

Peperomia guttulata, commonly known as the spotted peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, oval-shaped leaves with distinctive red or purple veins and spots, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically borne in clusters. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia guttulata is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests, making it an easy plant to care for.
