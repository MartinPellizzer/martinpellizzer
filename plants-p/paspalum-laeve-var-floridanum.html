Paspalum laeve var. floridanum

Paspalum laeve var. floridanum, commonly known as Florida paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white to pale green flowers. It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

This variety is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
