Phalaris ciliata

Phalaris ciliata, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with ciliate margins. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers.

It plays an important role in ecological systems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
