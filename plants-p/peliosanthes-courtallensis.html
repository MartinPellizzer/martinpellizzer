Peliosanthes courtallensis

Peliosanthes courtallensis, commonly known as the Malabar nut, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptic in shape, with a smooth surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental features and has traditional uses in local medicine.
