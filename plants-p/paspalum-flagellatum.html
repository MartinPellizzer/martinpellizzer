Paspalum flagellatum

Paspalum flagellatum, commonly known as the slender grass or silky paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, spreading growth habit and fine, silvery-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often covered in soft, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Paspalum flagellatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
