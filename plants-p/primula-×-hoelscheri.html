Primula × hoelscheri

Primula × hoelscheri, commonly known as the Hoelscher's primrose, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its semi-evergreen foliage, which consists of broad, ovate leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces showy clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in horticulture.
