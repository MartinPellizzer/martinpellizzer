Panicum fusciflorum

Panicum fusciflorum, commonly known as blackflower panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dark purple to black flower spikes that contrast with its green foliage. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, fuscous flowers.

Panicum fusciflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
