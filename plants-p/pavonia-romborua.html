Pavonia romborua

Pavonia romborua, commonly known as the redstem pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes. The plant produces showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Pavonia romborua is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
