Prionostemma velutina

Prionostemma velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Prionostemma velutina is noted for its adaptability and can be invasive in certain environments.
