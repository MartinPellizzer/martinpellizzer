Piper dryadum var. bullatilimbum

Piper dryadum var. bullatilimbum, commonly known as the Bullate-leaved Pepper Vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, and often bullate (blistered) leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible but have a peppery taste. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical climates.
