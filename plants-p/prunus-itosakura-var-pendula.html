Prunus itosakura var. pendula

Prunus itosakura var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping sakura, is a cultivar of the Prunus itosakura species, which is native to Japan.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that cascade with delicate pink or white flowers in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits known as cherries, though they are typically not consumed by humans. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value, particularly during the blooming season.
