Poa angustifolia subsp. laxispicula

Poa angustifolia subsp.

laxispicula, commonly known as the narrowleaf poa with lax spikelets, is a grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and distinctive, loosely arranged spikelets.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is often found in association with other grasses and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
