Prunus serrulata ambigua

Prunus serrulata ambigua, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a species of flowering shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a striking visual display. The plant has small, ovate leaves with serrated edges, which give it its scientific name. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The fruit it produces is a small, hard drupe that contains a single seed.
