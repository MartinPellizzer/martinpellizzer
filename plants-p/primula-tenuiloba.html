Primula tenuiloba

Primula tenuiloba, commonly known as the slender-leaved primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas at high elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
