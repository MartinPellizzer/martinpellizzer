Plinia silvestris

Plinia silvestris, commonly known as the wild cherry or Brazilian cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round drupe with a sweet, juicy pulp and a single seed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
