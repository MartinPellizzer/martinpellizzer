Papaver alaskanum

Papaver alaskanum, commonly known as Alaska poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red, with a distinctive black center. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Papaver alaskanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
