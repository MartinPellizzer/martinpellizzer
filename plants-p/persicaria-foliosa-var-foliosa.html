Persicaria foliosa var. foliosa

Persicaria foliosa var. foliosa, commonly known as the broadleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, pinkish to reddish flowers in clusters, which bloom from late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively make it both a valuable wetland indicator species and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
