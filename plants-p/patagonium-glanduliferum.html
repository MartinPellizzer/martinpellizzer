Patagonium glanduliferum

Patagonium glanduliferum, commonly known as the Patagonian glandular grass, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, greenish flowers that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Its presence is significant in the ecological dynamics of Patagonian ecosystems.
