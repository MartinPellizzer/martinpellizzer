Phyllanthus choretroides

Phyllanthus choretroides, commonly known as the white phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and succulent-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
