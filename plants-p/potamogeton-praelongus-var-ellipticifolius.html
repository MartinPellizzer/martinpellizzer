Potamogeton praelongus var. ellipticifolius

Potamogeton praelongus var. ellipticifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, rivers, and ponds. The plant has long, slender stems that are often branched and bear small, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
