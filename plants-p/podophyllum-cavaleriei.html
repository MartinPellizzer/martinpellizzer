Podophyllum cavaleriei

Podophyllum cavaleriei, commonly known as the Chinese mayapple, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the family Podophyllaceae and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
