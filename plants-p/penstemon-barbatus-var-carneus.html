Penstemon barbatus var. carneus

Penstemon barbatus var. carneus, commonly known as the pink beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink to rose-colored tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and a tall, upright stem that supports its showy inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.
