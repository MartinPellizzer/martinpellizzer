Polypodium microgrammoides

Polypodium microgrammoides, commonly known as the small-scale holly fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Polypodium microgrammoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
