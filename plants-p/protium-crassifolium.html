Protium crassifolium

Protium crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves that are often clustered at the base of the petioles. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody pods containing flat, oval seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, with a distinctive reddish-brown color.

The plant is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in local medicine.
