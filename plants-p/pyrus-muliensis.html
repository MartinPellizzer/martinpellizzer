Pyrus muliensis

Pyrus muliensis, commonly known as the Yunnan pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to China, specifically the Yunnan province, where it grows in mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its deciduous leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like apple, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Pyrus muliensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
