Prunus serratifolia var. ungeri

Prunus serratifolia var. ungeri, commonly known as the Unger's cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, dark red or black berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
