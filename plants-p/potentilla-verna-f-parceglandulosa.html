Potentilla verna f. parceglandulosa

Potentilla verna f. parceglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular-stem potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, glandular-stemmed structure and small, often yellowish flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is found in woodland edges and stream banks. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
