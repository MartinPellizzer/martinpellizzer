Peltophorum brasiliense

Peltophorum brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in pendulous inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Peltophorum brasiliense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping in warm climates.
