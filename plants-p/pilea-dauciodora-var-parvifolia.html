Pilea dauciodora var. parvifolia

Pilea dauciodora var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved groundsel, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features a low-growing habit with clustered stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in composite inflorescences, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens and as a houseplant.
