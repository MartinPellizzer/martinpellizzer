Pelargonium stipulaceum subsp. ovatostipulatum

Pelargonium stipulaceum subsp.

ovatostipulatum, commonly known as the ovate-stipuled geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa. It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that have prominent stipules.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long flowering period.
