Panicum sanguinale f. compositum

Panicum sanguinale f. compositum, commonly known as the composite panic grass, is a variant of the panic grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and loose, feathery inflorescences that resemble a composite structure, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue, and the flowers are small and wind-pollinated.

This grass is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
