Phaseolus trilobatus

Phaseolus trilobatus, commonly known as the evergreen bean or the wild kidney bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, green pods that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. It has been used traditionally for its nutritional value and has some medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
