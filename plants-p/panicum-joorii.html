Panicum joorii

Panicum joorii, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which results from fine, silky hairs covering the leaves. The plant produces fluffy, white or pale yellow inflorescences that resemble a cloud, making it visually striking. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Panicum joorii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
