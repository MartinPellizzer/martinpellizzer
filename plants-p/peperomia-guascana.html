Peperomia guascana

Peperomia guascana, commonly known as the Guascana Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped, often with a bluish or grayish hue. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white or pale pink, which are usually borne on short, erect spikes. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia guascana is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
