Paspalum setaceum var. stramineum

Paspalum setaceum var. stramineum, commonly known as the silky paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and compact growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental and erosion control purposes. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands and along waterways. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brown flowers.

It is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
