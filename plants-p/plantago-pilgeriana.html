Plantago pilgeriana

Plantago pilgeriana, commonly known as the Sierra grassleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces erect, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing a high degree of drought tolerance. Its flowers are small and greenish, with inconspicuous petals, and it relies on wind for pollination.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
