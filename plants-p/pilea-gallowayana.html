Pilea gallowayana

Pilea gallowayana, commonly known as the Galloway pilea, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pilea gallowayana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and ease of care.
