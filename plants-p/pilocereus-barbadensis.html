Pilocereus barbadensis

Pilocereus barbadensis, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and nocturnal flowering habit, with large, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and they emit a sweet scent to attract pollinators.

Pilocereus barbadensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
