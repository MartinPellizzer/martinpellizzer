Patrisa mansoana

Patrisa mansoana, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flower heads, which resemble the sun, hence its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are composite, consisting of numerous tiny tubular florets surrounded by showy ray florets.

Patrisa mansoana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
