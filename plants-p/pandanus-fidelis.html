Pandanus fidelis

Pandanus fidelis, commonly known as the Solomon Islands pandanus, is a member of the Pandanaceae family.

It is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands, particularly the Solomon Islands. The plant is characterized by its tall, stiff leaves that form a rosette shape, often reaching several meters in height. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically grouped in clusters at the ends of the leaves.

The plant is often used in traditional crafts and has cultural significance in the regions where it grows.
