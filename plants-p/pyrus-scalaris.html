Pyrus scalaris

Pyrus scalaris, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers and edible, sweet fruits. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth with a grayish appearance. This plant is often found in woodlands and along riverbanks, thriving in temperate climates.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
