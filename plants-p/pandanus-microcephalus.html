Pandanus microcephalus

Pandanus microcephalus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a flowering plant species native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, sword-like leaves that grow in a dense, rosette-like arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in branched inflorescences. Its fibrous roots and hardy nature allow it to thrive in coastal and sandy soils.

This species is often used in traditional crafts and has cultural significance in various island communities.
