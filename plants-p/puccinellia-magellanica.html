Puccinellia magellanica

Puccinellia magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats. The plant typically grows in coastal and saline environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, spike-like inflorescences contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Puccinellia magellanica plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and is of interest in ecological restoration projects.
