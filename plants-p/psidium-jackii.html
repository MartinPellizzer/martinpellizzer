Psidium jackii

Psidium jackii, commonly known as the Java guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a small, round berry with a sweet, aromatic pulp and a thin, smooth skin. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its ability to grow in a variety of soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
