Poa howellii var. chandleri

Poa howellii var. chandleri, commonly known as the Chandler's bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and bluish-green foliage. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant landscaping.
