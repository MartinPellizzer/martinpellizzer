Phyllanthus jablonskianus

Phyllanthus jablonskianus, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. It is well-known for its medicinal properties, particularly due to its high vitamin C content.

The plant is also valued in traditional Ayurvedic medicine for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects.
