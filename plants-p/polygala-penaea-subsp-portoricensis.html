Polygala penaea subsp. portoricensis

Polygala penaea subsp.

portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico. It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers with distinctive spurred petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and rocky crevices. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
