Paphiopedilum wilhelminae f. striatum

Paphiopedilum wilhelminae f. striatum, commonly known as the striated lady's slipper orchid, is a rare and distinctive orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped pouch that serves as a specialized structure for trapping pollinators. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, anchoring itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white with intricate patterns, and it produces small, dark brown seeds.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
