Portulaca mundula

Portulaca mundula, commonly known as the common purslane, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a low-growing annual herb that thrives in hot, dry environments. The plant features fleshy, succulent leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning. It produces small, round, reddish-brown seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Portulaca mundula is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in many regions.
