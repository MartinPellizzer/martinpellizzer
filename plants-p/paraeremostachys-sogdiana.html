Paraeremostachys sogdiana

Paraeremostachys sogdiana, commonly known as the Sogdian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, feathery inflorescences. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Due to its ecological significance, it plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity.
