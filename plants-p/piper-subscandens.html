Piper subscandens

Piper subscandens, commonly known as the climbing pepper vine, is a tropical perennial vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically yellow when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions.
