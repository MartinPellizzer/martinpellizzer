Pyrus tumetica

Pyrus tumetica, commonly known as the swollen apple or tumid apple, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded, and often swollen fruits that can grow up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant has simple, alternate, and ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and appear in clusters before the leaves emerge in spring.

The tree is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental features and historical use in traditional medicine.
