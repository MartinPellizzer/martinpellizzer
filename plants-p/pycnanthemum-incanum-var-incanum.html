Pycnanthemum incanum var. incanum

Pycnanthemum incanum var. incanum, commonly known as white wild indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to white flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
