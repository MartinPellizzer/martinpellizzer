Phemeranthus sediformis

Phemeranthus sediformis, commonly known as the everlasting flower, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its long-lasting, showy inflorescences that resemble daisies. The plant produces bright yellow ray flowers surrounded by a dense ring of orange-brown disk flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its resilience and ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
