Prunus pygma

Prunus pygma, commonly known as the dwarf cherry, is a small deciduous tree or shrub native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 2-5 meters, featuring slender branches and small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in spring, followed by small, dark red fruits. Prunus pygma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blossoms and compact size.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
