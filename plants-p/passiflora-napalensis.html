Passiflora napalensis

Passiflora napalensis, commonly known as the Mexican passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces blue or violet berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Passiflora napalensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
