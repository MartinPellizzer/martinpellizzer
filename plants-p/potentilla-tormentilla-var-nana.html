Potentilla tormentilla var. nana

Potentilla tormentilla var. nana, commonly known as dwarf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, with numerous slender stems that often root at the nodes. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.
