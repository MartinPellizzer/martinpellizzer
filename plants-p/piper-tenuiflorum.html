Piper tenuiflorum

Piper tenuiflorum, commonly known as Indian betel, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like pepper. Its fruits, called betel nuts, are enclosed in a red, fleshy pericarp.

The plant is widely cultivated for its aromatic leaves and fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and cultural practices.
