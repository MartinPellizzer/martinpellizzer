Poterium sanguisorba

Poterium sanguisorba, commonly known as bloodroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features a short, stout rhizome and produces striking red-orange flowers in early spring. The plant's foliage consists of palmately divided, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Poterium sanguisorba is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
