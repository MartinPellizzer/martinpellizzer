Philodendron ptarianum

Philodendron ptarianum, commonly known as the giant philodendron, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length. The plant has a robust stem that can climb using aerial roots, often reaching heights of several meters in its natural habitat. Its foliage is typically a deep green, with some varieties exhibiting variegation.

Philodendron ptarianum thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil for optimal growth.
