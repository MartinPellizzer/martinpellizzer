Philippiamra fastigiata

Philippiamra fastigiata, commonly known as the upright cypress, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features a narrow, columnar growth habit with dense, scale-like foliage that provides year-round visual interest. The plant's foliage is typically a deep green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by cylindrical cones. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a specimen plant in landscapes.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often used in formal garden designs.
