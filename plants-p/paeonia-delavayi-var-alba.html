Paeonia delavayi var. alba

Paeonia delavayi var. alba, commonly known as the white peony, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and a robust root system adapted to its high-altitude habitat. It belongs to the芍药属 (Paeoniaceae) family and is valued for both its ornamental beauty and traditional medicinal uses.

The flowers emit a subtle fragrance and are often cultivated in gardens for their aesthetic appeal.
