Phlox kelseyi subsp. glandulosa

Phlox kelseyi subsp.

glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its foliage consists of lance-shaped, hairy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

The subspecies is distinguished by its glandular structures on the stems and leaves, which contribute to its unique botanical identity.
