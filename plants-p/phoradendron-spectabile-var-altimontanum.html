Phoradendron spectabile var. altimontanum

Phoradendron spectabile var. altimontanum, commonly known as the highland mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in the Andes.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant relies on its host for water and some nutrients, while it photosynthesizes for additional energy. It is often found in high-altitude cloud forests, where its growth is influenced by humidity and temperature.

This species plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat by providing food for certain birds and insects.
