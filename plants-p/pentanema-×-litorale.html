Pentanema × litorale

Pentanema × litorale, commonly known as the coastal pentanema, is a hybrid plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

Pentanema × litorale is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
