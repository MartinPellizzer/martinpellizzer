Panax cussonioides

Panax cussonioides, commonly known as the Chinese ginseng, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive fleshy roots and trifoliate leaves. The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often cultivated for its root, which is used in traditional medicine. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
