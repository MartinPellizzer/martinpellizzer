Pleroma clavatum

Pleroma clavatum, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, and it produces small, round berries that contain the seeds.

Pleroma clavatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
