Paepalanthus jordanensis

Paepalanthus jordanensis, commonly known as Jordan's pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of Jordan.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a spherical or oblate shape. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often clustered at the top of the stem. It is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water and high sunlight exposure.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
