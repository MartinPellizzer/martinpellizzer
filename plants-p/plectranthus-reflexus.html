Plectranthus reflexus

Plectranthus reflexus, commonly known as the woolly mint or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus reflexus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can be grown as a ground cover or in containers.
