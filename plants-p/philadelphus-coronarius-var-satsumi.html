Philadelphus coronarius var. satsumi

Philadelphus coronarius var. satsumi, commonly known as the Satsuma mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and a moderate growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and mild fragrance.
