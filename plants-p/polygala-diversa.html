Polygala diversa

Polygala diversa, commonly known as the common milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, usually greenish or yellowish flowers arranged in spike-like clusters. Its leaves are alternate and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its adaptability in arid environments.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
