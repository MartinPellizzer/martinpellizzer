Piper maravalanum

Piper maravalanum, commonly known as the Maravalanum pepper, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the stems are woody and contain a pungent, peppery aroma.

The botanical characteristics of Piper maravalanum make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
