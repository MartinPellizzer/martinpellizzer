Paeonia officinalis var. lobata

Paeonia officinalis var. lobata, commonly known as the lobed peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in late spring. The plant features compound leaves with lobed or divided blades, and its stems are often covered in a thick, woody layer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine.
