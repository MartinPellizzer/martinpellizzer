Plectronia villarii

Plectronia villarii, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive tactile quality. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.

Plectronia villarii is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.
