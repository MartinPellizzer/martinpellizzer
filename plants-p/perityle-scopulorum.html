Perityle scopulorum

Perityle scopulorum, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in the summer months. Perityle scopulorum is often found in high-altitude environments, where it is adapted to cold temperatures and strong winds.
