Protea haemantha

Protea haemantha, commonly known as the red protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous pinkish-red floral bracts that surround a central cluster of small, white flowers. The plant is native to the coastal regions of KwaZulu-Natal and is often found in rocky, well-drained soils. It blooms during the summer months, producing a vibrant display that attracts pollinators such as birds and insects.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in floral arrangements.
