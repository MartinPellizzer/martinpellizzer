Potentilla fruticosa var. alpina

Potentilla fruticosa var. alpina, commonly known as alpine cinquefoil, is a low-growing shrub native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has wiry, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This hardy species is often used in rock gardens and alpine plantings due to its resilience and ornamental value.
