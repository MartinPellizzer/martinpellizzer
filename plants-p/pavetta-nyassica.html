Pavetta nyassica

Pavetta nyassica, commonly known as African pavetta, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverine thickets. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in clusters, which are usually borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.
