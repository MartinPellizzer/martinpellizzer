Pavetta baconia f. puberulosa

Pavetta baconia f. puberulosa, commonly known as the pubescent pavetta, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
