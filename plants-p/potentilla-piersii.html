Potentilla piersii

Potentilla piersii, commonly known as the Piers' cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky and alpine environments. The plant features small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are usually compound with five leaflets. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in high-altitude meadows.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
