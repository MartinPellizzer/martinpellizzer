Plantago recurvata proles capitellata

Plantago recurvata proles capitellata, commonly known as the clustered plantain, is a species of flowering plant in the family Plantaginaceae.

It is characterized by its rosette-like arrangement of leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along roadsides. Its leaves are broad and ribbed, with a hairy underside, and it blooms with greenish-white flowers that are clustered in a capitate structure.

This plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
