Pelargonium sericifolium

Pelargonium sericifolium, commonly known as the silvery geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, velvety leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is widely cultivated in gardens around the world.
