Pultenaea gunnii var. baeckeoides

Pultenaea gunnii var. baeckeoides, commonly known as the Baecke's bush wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features small, rounded leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers during spring. It is noted for its compact growth habit and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for native wildlife.
