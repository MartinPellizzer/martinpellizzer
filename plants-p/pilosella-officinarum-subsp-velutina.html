Pilosella officinarum subsp. velutina

Pilosella officinarum subsp.

velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb with silver-green, woolly foliage that gives it its common name.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
