Pseuderanthemum tricolor

Pseuderanthemum tricolor, commonly known as the tri-color pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its strikingly colorful, showy bracts that come in shades of red, yellow, and white. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, which are often surrounded by the brightly colored bracts.

Pseuderanthemum tricolor is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant appearance.
