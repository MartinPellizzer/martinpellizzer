Potentilla nivea var. vahliana

Potentilla nivea var. vahliana, commonly known as the white cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Potentilla nivea var. vahliana is often found in alpine meadows and tundra environments.
