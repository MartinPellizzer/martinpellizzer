Philodendron nigrifactum

Philodendron nigrifactum, commonly known as the Black Velvet Philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety foliage that ranges from deep purple to almost black, with a striking contrast against its lighter veins. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and can grow quite large, often reaching up to 12 inches in length. This plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
