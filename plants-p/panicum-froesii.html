Panicum froesii

Panicum froesii, commonly known as the Froes' bluestem, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, blue-green leaves that turn coppery in the fall. The plant produces fluffy, silver-gray seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open woodlands and prairies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often used in native plant gardens and restoration projects.
