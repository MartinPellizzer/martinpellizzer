Panicum gracilipes var. pubiflorum

Panicum gracilipes var. pubiflorum, commonly known as the silky panic grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and panicles of small, fluffy flowers that give it a soft, feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. Its flowers are often pale green to reddish in color, and the species is noted for its drought tolerance.

This variant is distinguished by its hairy flower clusters, which contribute to its common name.
