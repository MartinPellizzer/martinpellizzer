Parentucellia floribunda

Parentucellia floribunda, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
