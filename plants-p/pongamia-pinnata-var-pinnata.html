Pongamia pinnata var. pinnata

Pongamia pinnata var. pinnata, commonly known as the Indian beech or pongam tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a distinctive reddish or yellowish coloration. The tree produces yellow flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing oily seeds. Its root system is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

The plant is also valued for its oil-rich seeds, which have various industrial and agricultural applications.
