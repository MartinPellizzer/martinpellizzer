Pelargonium × daveyanum

Pelargonium × daveyanum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, velvety leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, depending on the cultivar. Native to South Africa, it thrives in warm climates and is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Its adaptability to various soil types and moderate water requirements make it a popular choice for gardeners.
