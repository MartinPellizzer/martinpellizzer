Portulaca officinarum

Portulaca officinarum, commonly known as purslane, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in hot, dry environments and is often found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides.

Portulaca officinarum is also known for its edible leaves and seeds, which are used in various cuisines.
