Pedilonum miyakei

Pedilonum miyakei, commonly known as the Miyake's bellflower, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep purple. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and shaded environments, preferring cool and humid conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a silvery underside, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Pedilonum miyakei is considered a rare and endangered species.
