Papaver argemone var. glabratum

Papaver argemone var. glabratum, commonly known as the smooth argemone, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, hairless stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with a waxy coating. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
