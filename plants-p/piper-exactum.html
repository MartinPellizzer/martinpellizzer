Piper exactum

Piper exactum, commonly known as the exactum pepper or the slender pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its fruit, a small, dark blue berry, is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Piper exactum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
