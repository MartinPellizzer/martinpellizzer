Phyllanthus caroliniensis subsp. stenopterus

Phyllanthus caroliniensis subsp.

stenopterus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf phyllanthus, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in forest understories.

The plant features small, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and are usually greenish-white in color.

The species is notable for its adaptability to wetland environments and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
