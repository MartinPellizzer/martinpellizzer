Pinus lawsonii subsp. gracilis

Pinus lawsonii subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as the slender Lawson cypress, is a rare and distinctive subspecies native to the high elevations of the western Himalayas. It is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and dense, scale-like foliage that gives it a compact appearance.

The needles are short, stiff, and often twisted, contributing to its unique aesthetic. It produces small, reddish-brown cones that mature over several years, and the seeds are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ecological and horticultural significance.
