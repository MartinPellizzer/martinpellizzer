Polygonum nervosum

Polygonum nervosum, commonly known as the veined knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color and are covered with small, scale-like structures. Its leaves are lance-shaped with prominent veins, giving it its common name.

The flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense spikes, typically blooming in late summer.
