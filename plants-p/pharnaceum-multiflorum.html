Pharnaceum multiflorum

Pharnaceum multiflorum, commonly known as the multiblossom pharnaceum, is a flowering plant native to the subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the spring and summer seasons. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
