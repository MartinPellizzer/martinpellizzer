Posidonia kirkmanii

Posidonia kirkmanii, commonly known as Kirkman's sea grass, is a flowering plant species native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a marine angiosperm that thrives in shallow, sandy or muddy substrates in the intertidal zone. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are adapted for water pollination. Its leaves form dense underwater meadows that provide important habitat for marine life.

Posidonia kirkmanii plays a crucial role in stabilizing coastal sediments and supporting biodiversity in its ecosystem.
