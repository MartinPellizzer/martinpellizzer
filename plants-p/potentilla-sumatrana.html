Potentilla sumatrana

Potentilla sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the Indonesian island of Sumatra.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas of montane forests, where it thrives in cool and humid conditions. Its leaves are compound, with five leaflets, and it produces small, aggregate fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its restricted habitat, Potentilla sumatrana is considered a rare and endangered species.
