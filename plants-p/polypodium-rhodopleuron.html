Polypodium rhodopleuron

Polypodium rhodopleuron, commonly known as the red-veined fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, bi-colored fronds that display a striking contrast between dark green and deep red veins. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly and form dense mats on forest floors.

The plant's unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
