Polypodium mnioides

Polypodium mnioides, commonly known as the small-leaf wall fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with slender, creeping stems that produce erect, lance-shaped fronds. Its fronds are usually small, with a leathery texture and a distinctive, finely divided appearance.

Polypodium mnioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
