Palala punctata

Palala punctata, commonly known as the spotted palala, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it thrives in partial shade. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, contrasting with the showy bracts that attract pollinators.

Palala punctata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
