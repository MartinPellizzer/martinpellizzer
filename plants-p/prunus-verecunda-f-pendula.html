Prunus verecunda f. pendula

Prunus verecunda f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Japanese cherry, is a unique cultivar of the Prunus verecunda species.

It is characterized by its graceful, cascading branches that create a weeping effect, making it a striking ornamental tree. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, typically pink or white, which add to its visual appeal. Its leaves are ovate and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to the blossoms.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic value and is well-suited to temperate climates.
