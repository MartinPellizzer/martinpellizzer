Pedicularis resupinata var. teucriifolia

Pedicularis resupinata var. teucriifolia, commonly known as the purple fireweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with a dense layer of short, glandular hairs.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
