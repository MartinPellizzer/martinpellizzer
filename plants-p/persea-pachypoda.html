Persea pachypoda

Persea pachypoda, commonly known as the thick-leaf avocado, is a species of evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its fruit is a large, pear-shaped berry with a thick, rough skin and a creamy, oily pulp.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some use in traditional medicine.
