Pseudolobivia rojasii

Pseudolobivia rojasii, commonly known as the Rosas's Pseudolobivia, is a rare and unique cactus species native to the Andes mountains of southern Peru.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines, which provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited to arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat vulnerability, it is considered a conservation concern.
