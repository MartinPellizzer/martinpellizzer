Pseudoclausena chrysogyne f. chrysogyne

Pseudoclausena chrysogyne f. chrysogyne, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a vibrant and showy appearance. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
