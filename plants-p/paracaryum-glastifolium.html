Paracaryum glastifolium

Paracaryum glastifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf paracaryum, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to habitat loss, it is classified as endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
