Panicum churunense

Panicum churunense, commonly known as Churun grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, where it forms dense, sprawling colonies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds in its inflorescence. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Botanically, it is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and adaptability to moist conditions.
