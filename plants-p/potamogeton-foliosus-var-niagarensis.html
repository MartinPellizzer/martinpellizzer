Potamogeton foliosus var. niagarensis

Potamogeton foliosus var. niagarensis, commonly known as the Niagara water plant, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically found in clusters near the base of the plant. It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and a healthy aquatic ecosystem.
