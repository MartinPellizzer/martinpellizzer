Potentilla flabellifolia f. lasiocarpa

Potentilla flabellifolia f. lasiocarpa, commonly known as the woolly potentilla, is a horticultural form of the species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering on the stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive, almost fuzzy appearance. The plant typically produces small, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This cultivar is valued for its unique texture and hardy nature, making it a popular choice among gardeners interested in drought-tolerant plants.
