Phyllanthus haughtii

Phyllanthus haughtii, commonly known as the African butter tree, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a small, evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa, characterized by its dense, glossy foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy coating.

Phyllanthus haughtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
