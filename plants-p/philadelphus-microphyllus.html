Philadelphus microphyllus

Philadelphus microphyllus, commonly known as the dwarf mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. Its botanical features include simple, opposite leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences that attract pollinators.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
