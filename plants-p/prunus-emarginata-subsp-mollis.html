Prunus emarginata subsp. mollis

Prunus emarginata subsp.

mollis, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a small deciduous tree or shrub native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

The plant produces showy, single or clustered flowers in shades of white to pink, which appear before the leaves in early spring. Its fruit is a small, yellow stone fruit with a sweet, aromatic flavor.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive spring blossoms and compact growth habit.
