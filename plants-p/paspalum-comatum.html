Paspalum comatum

Paspalum comatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf paspalum, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Paspalum comatum is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
