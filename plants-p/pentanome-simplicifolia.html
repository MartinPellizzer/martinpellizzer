Pentanome simplicifolia

Pentanome simplicifolia, commonly known as the simpleleaf pentanome, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its simple, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its modest appearance and hardy nature, it is often used in ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
