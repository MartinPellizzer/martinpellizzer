Piper corylistachyopsis

Piper corylistachyopsis, commonly known as the hairy pipevine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Piper corylistachyopsis is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
