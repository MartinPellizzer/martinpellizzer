Phalaenopsis venosa

Phalaenopsis venosa, commonly known as the veined orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, gracefully curved petals and a velvety, dark green leaf with prominent veins. The plant typically produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom multiple times throughout the year. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity.

Phalaenopsis venosa is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
