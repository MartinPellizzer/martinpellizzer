Piper cornifolium

Piper cornifolium, commonly known as the hornleaf pepper, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often adorned with small, spiny protrusions. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
