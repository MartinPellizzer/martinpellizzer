Philippiamra arancioana

Philippiamra arancioana, commonly known as the orange-flowered philippia, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bright orange flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its flowers are often described as having a subtle fragrance, adding to its ornamental appeal.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
