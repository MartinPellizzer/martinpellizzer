Polygonum yatagaianum

Polygonum yatagaianum, commonly known as the Yatagai knotweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
