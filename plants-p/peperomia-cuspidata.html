Peperomia cuspidata

Peperomia cuspidata, commonly known as the sharp-leaved peperomia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a compact shrub with thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture. The leaves are typically dark green and have a pointed or acuminate shape, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish in color.

This plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
