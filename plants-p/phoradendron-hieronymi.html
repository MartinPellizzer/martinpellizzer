Phoradendron hieronymi

Phoradendron hieronymi, commonly known as the red mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of host trees, particularly in the Caribbean and Central America.

It has a distinctive appearance with small, elliptical leaves and produces bright red berries that are a key part of its reproductive cycle. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also providing food for various birds and insects.

Its seeds are dispersed by birds that eat the berries, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
