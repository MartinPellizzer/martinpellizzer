Primulina hochiensis var. ochroleuca

Primulina hochiensis var. ochroleuca, commonly known as the white-veined primulina, is a strikingly beautiful plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically display a combination of white and green hues. The leaves are usually heart-shaped with a velvety texture and often have prominent veins that give it its common name. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical plant collections.
