Potentilla mongolica

Potentilla mongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Asia, particularly Mongolia and parts of China.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a compact, bushy growth habit.

Potentilla mongolica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
