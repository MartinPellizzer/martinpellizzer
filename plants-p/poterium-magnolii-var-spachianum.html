Poterium magnolii var. spachianum

Poterium magnolii var. spachianum, commonly known as the Spachianum magnolia, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soil and requires partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
