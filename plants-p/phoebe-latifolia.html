Phoebe latifolia

Phoebe latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf phoebe, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its foliage and structure make it a valuable species for ornamental and ecological purposes.
