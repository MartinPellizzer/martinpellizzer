Psidium monticola

Psidium monticola, commonly known as the mountain guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible berries that range in color from yellow to red.

Psidium monticola thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
