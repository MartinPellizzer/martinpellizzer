Pseudopilocereus aurilanatus

Pseudopilocereus aurilanatus, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in areoles, from which spines and flowers emerge. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming make it a striking addition to desert gardens.
