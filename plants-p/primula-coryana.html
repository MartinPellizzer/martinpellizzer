Primula coryana

Primula coryana, commonly known as the Korean primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its foliage consists of broad, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

Primula coryana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
