Pileocalyx

Pileocalyx, commonly known as the hairy pomegranate, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its woody stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. Its fruit is a small, round, and often hairy capsule that contains several seeds.

Pileocalyx species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated for their unique foliage and floral display.
