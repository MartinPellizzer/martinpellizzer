Physaria kingii var. cobrensis

Physaria kingii var. cobrensis, commonly known as the Cobrensis Blazing Star, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its striking, bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, bract-like structures that surround the flower clusters.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance in xeriscaped gardens.
