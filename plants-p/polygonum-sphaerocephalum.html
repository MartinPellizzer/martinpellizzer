Polygonum sphaerocephalum

Polygonum sphaerocephalum, commonly known as the globe smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features upright stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, clustered flowers. The plant produces tiny, pinkish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by small, three-lobed seed capsules. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Polygonum sphaerocephalum is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist, fertile soils.
