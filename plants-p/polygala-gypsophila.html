Polygala gypsophila

Polygala gypsophila, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, cylindrical fruits that contain several seeds.

Polygala gypsophila is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
