Pinus densata var. pygmaea

Pinus densata var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf mountain pine, is a rare and small variant of the dense pine species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and short, twisted branches, which allow it to thrive in harsh alpine environments. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, and its needle-like leaves are typically short and stout. It is adapted to cold climates and can often be found growing at high elevations.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
