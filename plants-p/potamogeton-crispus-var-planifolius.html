Potamogeton crispus var. planifolius

Potamogeton crispus var. planifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It features long, narrow leaves that are typically flat and broad at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, green flowers that are arranged in spike-like clusters, and it is often found in still or slow-moving waters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
