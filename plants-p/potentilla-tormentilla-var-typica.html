Potentilla tormentilla var. typica

Potentilla tormentilla var. typica, commonly known as the common cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves composed of five oval-shaped leaflets, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
