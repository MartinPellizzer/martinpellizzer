Prunus × donarium f. gosiozakura

Prunus × donarium f. gosiozakura, commonly known as the Gosiozakura cherry, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus, characterized by its ornamental value.

It is a cultivated form of the Japanese cherry, known for its delicate pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree with a rounded shape and smooth, grey bark. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly serrated margin, turning vibrant shades of red in the fall.

This variety is prized in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and is often planted in gardens and public spaces.
