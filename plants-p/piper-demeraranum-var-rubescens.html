Piper demeraranum var. rubescens

Piper demeraranum var. rubescens, commonly known as the red-horned pepper, is a variant of the black pepper plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown fruiting bodies, which are smaller and more compact than those of the standard black pepper. The plant belongs to the Piperaceae family and is cultivated for its aromatic and pungent fruits used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy texture, while its stems are woody and branched.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown in tropical regions for its unique flavor profile.
