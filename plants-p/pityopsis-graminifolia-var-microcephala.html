Pityopsis graminifolia var. microcephala

Pityopsis graminifolia var. microcephala, commonly known as the narrow-headed golden aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its compact growth habit and fine leaves make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.

It is often found in open woodlands and sagebrush steppe environments.
