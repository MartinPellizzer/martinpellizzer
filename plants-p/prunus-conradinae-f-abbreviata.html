Prunus conradinae f. abbreviata

Prunus conradinae f. abbreviata, commonly known as the dwarf cherry laurel, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features small, dark green leaves that are often densely packed, giving it a lush, rounded appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
