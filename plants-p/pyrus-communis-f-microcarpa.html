Pyrus communis f. microcarpa

Pyrus communis f. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf pear, is a compact cultivar of the common pear.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by ornamental, yellow fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and compact growth habit, making it suitable for small gardens or container planting.

This form is a natural variation of the common pear, showcasing genetic diversity within the species.
