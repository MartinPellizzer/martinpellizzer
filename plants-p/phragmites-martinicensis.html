Phragmites martinicensis

Phragmites martinicensis, commonly known as the Martinique reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the Caribbean region.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant forms dense stands with tall, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-gray coloration.

It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
