Phrynium pubigerum

Phrynium pubigerum, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot or swamp arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or marshy environments. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in spikes, and are typically greenish-yellow in color.

The plant is valued for its starchy roots, which are used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
