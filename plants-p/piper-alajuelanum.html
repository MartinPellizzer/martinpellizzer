Piper alajuelanum

Piper alajuelanum, commonly known as the Guatemalan pepper or Alajuela pepper, is a tropical vine native to Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species like black pepper. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental and pharmacological potential.
