Phlox whitedii

Phlox whitedii, commonly known as White's phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Phlox whitedii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
