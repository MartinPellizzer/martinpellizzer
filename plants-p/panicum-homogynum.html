Panicum homogynum

Panicum homogynum, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces feathery, purple-tinted inflorescences that are visually striking. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is often used in restoration projects due to its erosion control properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in landscaping.
