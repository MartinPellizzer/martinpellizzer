Polyosma verticillata

Polyosma verticillata, commonly known as the clustered polyosma, is a shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are often gland-dotted, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer months. Its flowering stems are often branched and can bear multiple inflorescences.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
