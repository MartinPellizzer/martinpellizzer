Polyalthia costigera

Polyalthia costigera, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often giving the tree a luminous appearance. The leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a smooth surface and a glossy green color. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
