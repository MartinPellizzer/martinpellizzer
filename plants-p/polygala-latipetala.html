Polygala latipetala

Polygala latipetala, commonly known as the broad-petalled milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, often showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces small, clustered fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
