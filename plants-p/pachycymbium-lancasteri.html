Pachycymbium lancasteri

Pachycymbium lancasteri, commonly known as the Lancaster's cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
