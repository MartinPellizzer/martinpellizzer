Piper san-joseanum var. aristolochiifolium

Piper san-joseanum var. aristolochiifolium, commonly known as the San Jose pepper, is a tropical vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with prominent veins, which resemble those of the genus Aristolochia. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
