Prunus spinosa subsp. ovoideoglobosa

Prunus spinosa subsp.

ovoideoglobosa, commonly known as the ovalglobosa blackthorn, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, dark blue to black berries that ripen in late summer.

The leaves are elliptical, leathery, and often have a distinctive white or pale underside. This subspecies is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrublands and woodland edges.

Its berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.
