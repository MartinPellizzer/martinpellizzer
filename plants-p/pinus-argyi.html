Pinus argyi

Pinus argyi, commonly known as the Mexican white pine, is a coniferous tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and long, needle-like leaves that are typically 5-7 cm in length. The tree produces cones that are typically 5-8 cm long, containing edible seeds that are a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

The wood of Pinus argyi is lightweight and has been historically used for construction and furniture-making.
