Padus cornuta var. glabra

Padus cornuta var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth thorny maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and alternate, palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or stream valleys.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
