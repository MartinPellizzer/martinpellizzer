Phaseolus caffer

Phaseolus caffer, commonly known as the African bean, is a species of legume native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves with trifoliate structures. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient crop in tropical and subtropical regions.

The seeds are used for food, while the plant also has traditional medicinal applications.
