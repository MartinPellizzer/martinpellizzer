Plectranthus indicus

Plectranthus indicus, commonly known as Indian balm, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or pink, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

Its aromatic properties have made it popular in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
