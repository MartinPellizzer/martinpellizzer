Pityrodia terminalis

Pityrodia terminalis, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern and western Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its yellow flowers and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is often found in dunes and coastal heathlands. It plays an important role in stabilizing dune systems and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Pityrodia terminalis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
