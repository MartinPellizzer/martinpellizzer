Prunus rechingeri

Prunus rechingeri, commonly known as the Kashmir cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender branches, small elliptical leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, sweet fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in cool, mountainous climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.

Prunus rechingeri is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
