Pyrus reperta

Pyrus reperta, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in spring. The tree produces small, round berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. Its leaves are broad and have a distinctive serrated edge, and it is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Pyrus reperta is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
