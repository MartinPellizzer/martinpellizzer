Polystachya musozensis

Polystachya musozensis, commonly known as the Musozense Sword Fern, is a member of the Polystachyaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features long, narrow leaves that resemble swords, which are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. Its roots are fibrous and help it anchor to rocky or forest substrates.

Polystachya musozensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
