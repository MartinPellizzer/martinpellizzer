Prunus × lannesiana f. banriko

Prunus × lannesiana f. banriko, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus, specifically a hybrid between Prunus lannesiana and Prunus serrulata.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant is a deciduous tree with a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are ovate, serrated, and turn yellow in the fall, adding to its seasonal appeal.

This cultivar is widely cultivated in Japan and other parts of East Asia for its ornamental value and cultural significance.
