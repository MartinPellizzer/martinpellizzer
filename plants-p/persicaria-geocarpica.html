Persicaria geocarpica

Persicaria geocarpica, commonly known as the earthroot knotweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its underground stems that produce fleshy, edible roots. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in late summer.

Due to its unique growth habit and potential culinary uses, it is of interest to botanists and foragers alike.
