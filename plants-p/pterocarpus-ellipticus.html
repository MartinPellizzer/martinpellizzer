Pterocarpus ellipticus

Pterocarpus ellipticus, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark brown bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, elongated pods containing dark brown seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its rich color and durability, making it important in traditional crafts and furniture-making.

The plant also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
