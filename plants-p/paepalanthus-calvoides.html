Paepalanthus calvoides

Paepalanthus calvoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It is a low-growing, spineless cactus that typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are often clustered at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and the stems are fleshy, adapted for water storage in arid environments.

Paepalanthus calvoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.
