Premna madagascariensis

Premna madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas and can grow as a shrub or vine.

It produces small, four-lobed fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its propagation in suitable habitats.
