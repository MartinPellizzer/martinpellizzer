Poecilostachys bromoides

Poecilostachys bromoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluemink, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, blue to purple flowers in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish tinge, which helps reduce water loss.
