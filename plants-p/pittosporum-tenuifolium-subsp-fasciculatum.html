Pittosporum tenuifolium subsp. fasciculatum

Pittosporum tenuifolium subsp.

fasciculatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves that are often arranged in a compact, fasciculate (bundled) pattern.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers a sunny to partially shaded location.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
