Parthenocissus henryana var. glaucescens

Parthenocissus henryana var. glaucescens, commonly known as Henry's ivy, is a climbing vine native to China.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy, bluish-gray underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red berries in the fall. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb walls and trellises.

Its foliage turns vibrant red in autumn, adding seasonal color to landscapes.
