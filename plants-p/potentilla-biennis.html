Potentilla biennis

Potentilla biennis, commonly known as the two-yearly cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial species that typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla biennis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
