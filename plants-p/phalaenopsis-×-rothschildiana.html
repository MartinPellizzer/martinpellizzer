Phalaenopsis × rothschildiana

Phalaenopsis × rothschildiana, commonly known as the Rothschild's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species that combines traits from Phalaenopsis and Dendrobium parents.

It is characterized by its long, arching inflorescences that bear clusters of showy, lip-like petals in various colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, and it thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light. Its flowers can last for several months, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to different growing conditions.
