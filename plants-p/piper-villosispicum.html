Piper villosispicum

Piper villosispicum, commonly known as the hairy pipevine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, hairy textures. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in spikes, and it is typically found in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is also noted for its chemical compounds, which have been studied for potential medicinal uses.
