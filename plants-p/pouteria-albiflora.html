Pouteria albiflora

Pouteria albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower spondias, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are often borne on the previous year's branches. Its fruits are ellipsoid, yellow when ripe, and contain a single seed surrounded by a fleshy, edible pulp.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
