Psidium sapidissimum

Psidium sapidissimum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or granadilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible, bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart, sweet flavor. Its botanical features include a woody stem, aromatic foliage, and a preference for well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
