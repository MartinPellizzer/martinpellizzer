Pinus oocarpa var. oocarpoides

Pinus oocarpa var. oocarpoides, commonly known as the Oocarpoides pine, is a variant of the Oocarpa pine native to Central America.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant exhibits a distinct morphology with elongated, bluish-green needles and clustered cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in well-drained soils.

The botanical features of this variant make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
