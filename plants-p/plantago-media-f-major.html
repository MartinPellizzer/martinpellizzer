Plantago media f. major

Plantago media f. major, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features broad, oval-shaped leaves that are often ribbed and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its soothing effects on the skin and respiratory system.
