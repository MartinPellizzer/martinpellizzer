Persica vulgaris var. haematocarpa

Persica vulgaris var. haematocarpa, commonly known as the blood-red peach.

This variant is characterized by its distinctive deep red or maroon-colored fruits, which are typically smaller than standard peaches. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, native to parts of Asia, particularly China. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a smooth margin.

The flowers are white with a pinkish tinge, and the fruit ripens in the summer months.
