Potentilla veitchii

Potentilla veitchii, commonly known as Veitch's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet, featuring woody stems and alternate, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in corymbs. Its botanical characteristics include hairy stems and leaves with a serrated margin, making it easily identifiable in its native habitat.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
