Prunus nicotianifolia

Prunus nicotianifolia, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin. Its fruit is a small, yellowish plum that ripens in late summer.

Prunus nicotianifolia is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
