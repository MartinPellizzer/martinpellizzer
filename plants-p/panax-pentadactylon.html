Panax pentadactylon

Panax pentadactylon, commonly known as the Chinese ginseng, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy roots that are highly valued in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with compound leaves and small greenish-white flowers. Its rhizome system is complex, consisting of multiple roots that are often harvested for their medicinal properties.

The botanical features of Panax pentadactylon make it a significant species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
