Passiflora discolor

Passiflora discolor, commonly known as the white passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers with intricate patterns and five sepals that resemble petals. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture.

Passiflora discolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
