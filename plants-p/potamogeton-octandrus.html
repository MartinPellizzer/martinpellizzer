Potamogeton octandrus

Potamogeton octandrus, commonly known as the eight-angled pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls of eight, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like structures, which are often submerged. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton octandrus is often found in slow-moving waters such as ponds, lakes, and wetlands.
