Poa glumaris var. laevigata

Poa glumaris var. laevigata, commonly known as the smooth meadow grass, is a variety of the meadow grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
