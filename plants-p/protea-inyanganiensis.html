Protea inyanganiensis

Protea inyanganiensis, commonly known as the Inyangani protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is native to the eastern regions of the country, particularly in the Eastern Cape and KwaZulu-Natal provinces. The plant is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous pink to red flower heads. Its flowers are typically visited by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological importance.

Protea inyanganiensis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
