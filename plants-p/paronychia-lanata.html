Paronychia lanata

Paronychia lanata, commonly known as the woolly mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white hairs covering the stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments.

Paronychia lanata is a perennial herb that is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
