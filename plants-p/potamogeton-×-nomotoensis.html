Potamogeton × nomotoensis

Potamogeton × nomotoensis, commonly known as the Japanese pondweed hybrid, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Potamogetonaceae.

It is a natural hybrid between Potamogeton crispus and Potamogeton praelatus, characterized by its submerged aquatic growth habit. The plant typically has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often folded or rolled, and it produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikelets. It is commonly found in freshwater habitats such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
