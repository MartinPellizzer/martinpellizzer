Paepalanthus actinocephaloides

Paepalanthus actinocephaloides, commonly known as the star-headed pincushion cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It is a low-growing, spineless cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a spherical or flattened stem covered in dense, woolly white hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in a star-like pattern at the top of the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
