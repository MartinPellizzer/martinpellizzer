Piper gracilipedunculum

Piper gracilipedunculum, commonly known as the slender-stemmed pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests, where it thrives in the understory.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists studying tropical flora.
