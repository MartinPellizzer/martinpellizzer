Psoralea lupinellus

Psoralea lupinellus, commonly known as the narrowleaf psoralea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, often in sandy or rocky soils. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Psoralea lupinellus is known for its drought tolerance and is often found in open habitats such as plains and lowlands.
