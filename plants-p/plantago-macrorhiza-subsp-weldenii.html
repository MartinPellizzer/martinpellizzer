Plantago macrorhiza subsp. weldenii

Plantago macrorhiza subsp.

weldenii, commonly known as the giant plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are borne on tall, branching stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is notable for its medicinal properties, with its leaves traditionally used to treat ailments such as inflammation and digestive issues.
