Pisonia pernambucensis

Pisonia pernambucensis, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or pisonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small, evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellowish berry that contains numerous seeds.

Pisonia pernambucensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
