Polygala kalunga

Polygala kalunga, commonly known as the Kalunga or African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodland and grassland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, while its fruit is a cylindrical pod that splits open to release small seeds.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
