Pterocarya fraxinifolia var. sorbifolia

Pterocarya fraxinifolia var. sorbifolia, commonly known as the Siberian nutcracker, is a deciduous tree native to regions of Central Asia and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnately arranged. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in catkins, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible nuts. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing structural support and protection against environmental stress.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
