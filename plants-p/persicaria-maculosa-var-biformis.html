Persicaria maculosa var. biformis

Persicaria maculosa var. biformis, commonly known as the spotted knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that are often reddish in color and bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple or reddish mottling. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and spreading habit make it both a useful ground cover and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
