Picea wilsonii var. watsoniana

Picea wilsonii var. watsoniana, commonly known as the Wilson's fir, is a variant of the Wilson's fir, native to China.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves. The foliage is typically dark green, with a slight blueish tint, and the cones are cylindrical and woody. This species thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable ornamental and ecological plant in its native habitat.
