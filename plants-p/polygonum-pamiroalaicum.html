Polygonum pamiroalaicum

Polygonum pamiroalaicum, commonly known as the Pamir knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and simple, alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, producing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
