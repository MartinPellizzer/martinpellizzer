Pycnospora nervosa

Pycnospora nervosa, commonly known as the veined pycnospora, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with prominent venation. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Pycnospora nervosa is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
