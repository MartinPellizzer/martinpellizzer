Pterygiella cylindrica var. suffruticosa

Pterygiella cylindrica var. suffruticosa, commonly known as the shrub form of the slender beardgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that are typically green or pale yellow, clustered in dense spikes along the stem. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America, where it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

This variety is adapted to withstand drought and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
