Phlomis cypria

Phlomis cypria, commonly known as Cyprus blazingstar, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with rough, hairy surfaces, and its stems are often covered in fine, bristly hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rocky or dry garden environments.

Phlomis cypria is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
