Piper parong

Piper parong, commonly known as the Java pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, containing a spicy compound similar to capsaicin. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
