Prunella vulgaris f. albiflora

Prunella vulgaris f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered selfheal, is a variant of the common selfheal.

It is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a square stem, hairy surfaces, and a preference for moist, shady habitats.

It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
