Persicaria pilushanensis

Persicaria pilushanensis, commonly known as the Tibetan knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as smartweeds or knotweeds. The plant features upright, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a prominent midrib. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, and it blooms during the summer months.

Persicaria pilushanensis is adapted to harsh, alpine environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
