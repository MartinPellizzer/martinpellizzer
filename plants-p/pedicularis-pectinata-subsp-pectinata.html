Pedicularis pectinata subsp.

pectinata, commonly known as the pectinate lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive pectinate (toothed) leaves and showy, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. It is a member of the Orobanchaceae family, which includes many hemiparasitic species.
