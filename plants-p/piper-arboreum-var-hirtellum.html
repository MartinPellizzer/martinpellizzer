Piper arboreum var. hirtellum

Piper arboreum var. hirtellum, commonly known as the hairy sarsaparilla, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are small, three-lobed berries that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
