Potamogeton × suecicus f. holmiensis

Potamogeton × suecicus f. holmiensis, commonly known as the Holm's pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a submerged perennial that typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often ribbon-like in appearance, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It is known for its ability to form dense underwater vegetation, which can provide habitat for aquatic life.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions where it can outcompete native plants.
