Pinus planchonii

Pinus planchonii, commonly known as the Mexican bristlecone pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The tree produces cones that are typically 5 to 8 centimeters long, containing edible seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

This species is notable for its longevity and slow growth rate, making it a symbol of resilience in its native habitat.
