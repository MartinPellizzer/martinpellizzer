Plantago fischeri

Plantago fischeri, commonly known as Fischer's plantain, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically borne on slender stems. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.

Fischer's plantain is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
