Polypodium latissimum

Polypodium latissimum, commonly known as the broad-leaf huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns.

Polypodium latissimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
