Phyllanthus tui

Phyllanthus tui, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a small, evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and traditional uses, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and for medicinal purposes.
