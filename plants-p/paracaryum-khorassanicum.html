Paracaryum khorassanicum

Paracaryum khorassanicum, commonly known as the Khorassan fig, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Moraceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, clustered fig-like fruits that are typically green when immature and turn reddish-brown when ripe. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
