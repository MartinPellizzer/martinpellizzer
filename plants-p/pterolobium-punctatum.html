Pterolobium punctatum

Pterolobium punctatum, commonly known as the spotted pterolobium, is a leguminous shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces legumes that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or secondary growth habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and local herbal practices.
