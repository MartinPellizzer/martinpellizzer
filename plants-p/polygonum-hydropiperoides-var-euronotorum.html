Polygonum hydropiperoides var. euronotorum

Polygonum hydropiperoides var. euronotorum, commonly known as the European water pepper, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
