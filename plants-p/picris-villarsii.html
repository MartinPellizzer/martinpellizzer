Picris villarsii

Picris villarsii, commonly known as the Alpine groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in early summer.

Picris villarsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
