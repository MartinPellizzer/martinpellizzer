Potentilla chrysantha var. holopetala

Potentilla chrysantha var. holopetala, commonly known as the golden potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, and its dense, compact growth habit. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and long blooming period.
