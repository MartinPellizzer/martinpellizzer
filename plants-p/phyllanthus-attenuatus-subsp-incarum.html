Phyllanthus attenuatus subsp. incarum

Phyllanthus attenuatus subsp.

incarum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia. It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical in shape and have a waxy surface.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by greenish or yellowish capsules that contain the seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including open woodlands and grasslands.

The species is noted for its medicinal uses in traditional medicine, though its toxic properties should be considered when handling the plant.
