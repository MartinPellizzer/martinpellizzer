Panicum juncifolium

Panicum juncifolium, commonly known as slender panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, slender spikes of small, greenish flowers. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a rhizomatous growth habit, which helps it spread and establish in wet environments.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
