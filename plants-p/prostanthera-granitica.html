Prostanthera granitica

Prostanthera granitica, commonly known as granite mintbush, is a small shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a strong mint-like scent. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Prostanthera granitica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
