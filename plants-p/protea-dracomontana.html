Protea dracomontana

Protea dracomontana, commonly known as the dragon's claw protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, spiky, cone-shaped inflorescence that resembles a dragon's claw, hence its common name. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces of South Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. It produces vibrant pink to red flowers that attract pollinators such as birds and insects.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is also valued as an ornamental plant in horticulture.
