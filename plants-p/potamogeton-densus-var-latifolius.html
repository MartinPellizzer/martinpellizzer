Potamogeton densus var. latifolius

Potamogeton densus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It features long, narrow leaves that are typically broad at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its dense growth can also influence water quality and sediment stability in its habitat.
