Phyllanthus nobilis var. riedelianus

Phyllanthus nobilis var. riedelianus, commonly known as the Riedel's woodrose, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short peduncles. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The species is also noted for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
