Pteris sinuata

Pteris sinuata, commonly known as the waved fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like leaves that have a distinctive wavy or undulating edge. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fronds are also popular in horticulture due to their ornamental appearance.
