Palmijuncus quinquenervius

Palmijuncus quinquenervius, commonly known as the five-veined palm, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in a branched inflorescence, followed by dark blue-black fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical conditions and is often found in moist forest areas.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
