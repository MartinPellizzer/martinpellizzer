Peschiera spixiana

Peschiera spixiana, commonly known as the Spix's marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect stem and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in marshy or swampy areas, preferring moist and acidic soils. Its flowers, which are usually pink or purple, bloom in late summer and early autumn.

Peschiera spixiana plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
