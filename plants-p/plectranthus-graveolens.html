Plectranthus graveolens

Plectranthus graveolens, commonly known as lemon verbena, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is notable for its strong, lemony scent, which comes from the essential oils present in its leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

The leaves are often used in teas and as a natural remedy for various ailments.
