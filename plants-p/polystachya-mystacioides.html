Polystachya mystacioides

Polystachya mystacioides, commonly known as the misty orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences that bear small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it relies on mycorrhizal associations for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
