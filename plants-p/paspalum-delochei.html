Paspalum delochei

Paspalum delochei, commonly known as the Deloche's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Paspalum delochei is often found in disturbed areas and can be used for erosion control due to its dense root system.
