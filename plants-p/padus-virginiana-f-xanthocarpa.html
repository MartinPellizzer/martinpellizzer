Padus virginiana f. xanthocarpa

Padus virginiana f. xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-fruited serviceberry, is a variant of the common serviceberry native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its yellowish-orange fruits, which contrast with the typical red or pink of the species. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. It produces white flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits in late summer.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
