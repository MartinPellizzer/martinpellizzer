Poa nemoralis var. divaricata

Poa nemoralis var. divaricata, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a variant of the common bentgrass.

It is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in woodland edges and moist meadows. The plant is characterized by its fine, silver-hairy leaves and compact growth habit. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
