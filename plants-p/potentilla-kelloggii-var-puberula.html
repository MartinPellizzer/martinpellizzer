Potentilla kelloggii var. puberula

Potentilla kelloggii var. puberula, commonly known as Kellogg's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive five-lobed shape. The plant produces yellow flowers in early to mid-summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant ecosystems.
