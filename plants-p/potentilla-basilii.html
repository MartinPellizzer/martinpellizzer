Potentilla basilii

Potentilla basilii, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh climates. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Potentilla basilii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
