Polygonatum biflorum var. necopinum

Polygonatum biflorum var. necopinum, commonly known as the Japanese Solomon's Seal, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system and lanceolate, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in late spring to early summer. Its berries are red and edible, often consumed in traditional medicine.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
