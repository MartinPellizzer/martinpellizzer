Paepalanthus holstii

Paepalanthus holstii, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems, often growing in dry, rocky habitats. It produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature.
