Pluchea frutescens

Pluchea frutescens, commonly known as blueweed or swamp blueweed, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and along stream banks, and is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems are usually branched and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Pluchea frutescens is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
