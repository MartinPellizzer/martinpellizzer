Phelypaea lavandulacea subsp. fraasii

Phelypaea lavandulacea subsp.

fraasii, commonly known as the Fraas's marsh fern, is a perennial fern native to wetland habitats in southern Africa. It features a compact rosette of lanceolate, dark green leaves with a velvety texture.

The fronds are typically unbranched and possess a distinctive, slightly curled appearance. It produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds.

This species thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in marshes and along stream banks.
