Primula sylvicola

Primula sylvicola, commonly known as the woodland primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. Its flowering period occurs in spring, and it is often found in forest understories.

Primula sylvicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
