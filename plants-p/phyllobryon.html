Phyllobryon

Phyllobryon, commonly known as the leaf liverwort, is a type of non-vascular plant belonging to the order Marchantiales.

It is characterized by its flattened, lobed thallus that resembles a leaf, hence its common name. Phyllobryon typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its structure lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, instead consisting of a simple, undifferentiated body.

The plant reproduces via spores, which are produced in specialized structures called sporangia.
