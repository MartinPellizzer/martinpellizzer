Phyllanthus prieurianus var. glaber

Phyllanthus prieurianus var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves. The leaves are usually elliptical or ovate, with a glossy surface and prominent veins. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, spherical fruits.

This plant is often found in disturbed habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
