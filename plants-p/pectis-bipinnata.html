Pectis bipinnata

Pectis bipinnata, commonly known as the narrowleaf pectis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaf structures. The plant features finely divided, bipinnate leaves that are typically narrow and linear, giving it a delicate appearance. Its flowers are yellow and are arranged in dense, spherical clusters, which attract various pollinators.

Pectis bipinnata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
