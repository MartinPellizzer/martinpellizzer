Panicum almadense

Panicum almadense, commonly known as the Almadense grass, is a species of grass native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, fluffy seed heads that are often wind-dispersed. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its botanical features include narrow leaves and a fibrous root system, which help it thrive in nutrient-poor environments.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
