Pulsatilla alpina f. nana

Pulsatilla alpina f. nana, commonly known as the alpine pasqueflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers with numerous stamens, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs.

This variety is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
