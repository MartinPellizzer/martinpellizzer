Prunus virginea

Prunus virginea, commonly known as the Virginia cherry, is a small deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces edible, sweet cherries that are typically harvested in late summer. Prunus virginea is often found in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in woodland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in North American botany.
