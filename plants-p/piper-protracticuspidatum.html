Piper protracticuspidatum

Piper protracticuspidatum, commonly known as the elongated spined pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its long, slender stems and small, opposite leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is noted for its unique morphology and potential pharmacological properties.
