Plantago stenopetala

Plantago stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are wind-pollinated. It is adapted to grow in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
