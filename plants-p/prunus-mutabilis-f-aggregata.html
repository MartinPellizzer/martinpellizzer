Prunus mutabilis f. aggregata

Prunus mutabilis f. aggregata, commonly known as the clustered cherry, is a unique cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the presence of multiple stems emerging from a single rootstock. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, often in shades of white and pink. Its foliage is typically dark green, providing a striking contrast to the blossoms.

While it is less common in cultivation, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
