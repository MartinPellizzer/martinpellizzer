Peperomia peltaphylla

Peperomia peltaphylla, commonly known as the velvet peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and silver. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red, blooming in clusters along the stems. Peperomia peltaphylla thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate humidity.

It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
