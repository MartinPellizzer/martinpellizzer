Prunus nutantiflora

Prunus nutantiflora, commonly known as the weeping cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that bear clusters of white or pink flowers in spring. The plant produces small, oval fruits that are edible but not particularly prized for culinary use. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Prunus nutantiflora is a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.
