Pilea phenacoides

Pilea phenacoides, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to China.

It features round, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a silvery or white underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters during the spring. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its ability to thrive in low light conditions and its relatively low maintenance requirements.

Pilea phenacoides is also known for its ability to propagate easily through leaf cuttings.
