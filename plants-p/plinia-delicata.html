Plinia delicata

Plinia delicata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia uniflora*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, bright red berries that are edible. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stem.

The flowers are white or pink, clustered in small inflorescences, and bloom in late winter to early spring.
