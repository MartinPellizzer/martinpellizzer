Phyllanthus poiretianus var. acutifolius

Phyllanthus poiretianus var. acutifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It features small, elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, often arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by small, fleshy, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
