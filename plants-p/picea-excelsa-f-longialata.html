Picea excelsa f. longialata

Picea excelsa f. longialata, commonly known as the long-needle white spruce, is a variant of the white spruce native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender needles that are typically longer than those of the typical white spruce. The plant exhibits a conical shape with a dense crown, and its bark is thin and reddish-brown, peeling in flaky strips. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions with limited soil nutrients.

This form is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
