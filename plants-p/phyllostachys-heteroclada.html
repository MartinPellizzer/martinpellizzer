Phyllostachys heteroclada

Phyllostachys heteroclada, commonly known as the black bamboo, is a species of grass in the bamboo family, Poaceae.

It is native to East Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its dark green to almost black culms. The plant grows as a clump-forming bamboo, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth texture, while its culms are often used in traditional crafts and construction.

Phyllostachys heteroclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in various environmental conditions.
