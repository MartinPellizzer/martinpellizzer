Pinus ponderosa var. benthamiana

Pinus ponderosa var. benthamiana, commonly known as the Bentharn pine, is a variant of the ponderosa pine native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and distinctive, long, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in bundles of three. The tree produces cones that are elongated and often have a reddish-brown color when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This variety is valued for its timber and plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
