Poa pratensis var. malmundariensis

Poa pratensis var. malmundariensis, commonly known as the Malmundariensis meadow grass, is a variant of the common bentgrass.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dense, carpet-like foliage. This plant typically thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and slightly curled, with a bluish-green hue when mature.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in low-maintenance landscaping.
