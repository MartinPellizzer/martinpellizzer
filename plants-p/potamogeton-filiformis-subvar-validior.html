Potamogeton filiformis subvar. validior

Potamogeton filiformis subvar. validior, commonly known as the slender pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its long, narrow, ribbon-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in dense clusters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its rapid growth can sometimes lead to dense vegetation, affecting water flow and oxygen levels in the environment.
