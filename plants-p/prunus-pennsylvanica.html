Prunus pennsylvanica

Prunus pennsylvanica, commonly known as the pinxter azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and prefers acidic, well-drained soils. Its botanical features include opposite leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape, and it produces small, dark-colored berries that are edible for birds.

Prunus pennsylvanica is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various growing conditions.
