Pyxidaria arridens

Pyxidaria arridens, commonly known as the dryland bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Pyxidaria arridens is often found growing in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand long periods of drought.
