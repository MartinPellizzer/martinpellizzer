Podocarpus grayae

Podocarpus grayae, commonly known as the Gray's podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters in its natural habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in humid, temperate climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its timber.
