Pilosella schultesii subsp. megalophylla

Pilosella schultesii subsp.

megalophylla, commonly known as the large-leafed pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America. It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and features small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters.

The leaves are typically grayish-green with a woolly texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
