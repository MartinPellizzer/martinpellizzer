Paphiopedilum leucochilum

Paphiopedilum leucochilum, commonly known as the white-lipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizomatous stem and fleshy, leathery leaves. The plant produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive white lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Paphiopedilum leucochilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in both greenhouse and garden settings.
