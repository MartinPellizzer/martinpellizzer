Panicum berteronianum

Panicum berteronianum, commonly known as black panicgrass, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and can reach heights of up to 2 to 3 feet. It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.

The species is adapted to full sun and thrives in dry to moist conditions.
