Pimelea imbricata var. major

Pimelea imbricata var. major, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in early spring, which are clustered in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
