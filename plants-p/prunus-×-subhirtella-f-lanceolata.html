Prunus × subhirtella f. lanceolata

Prunus × subhirtella f. lanceolata, commonly known as the Weeping Cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus characterized by its striking, cascading branches.

It is a hybrid between Prunus subhirtella and Prunus × yedoensis, resulting in a unique combination of traits. The plant features delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, often in shades of pale pink to white. Its foliage is dark green and turns vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall.

This ornamental tree is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is commonly planted in gardens and public spaces.
