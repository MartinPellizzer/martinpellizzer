Podranea brycei

Podranea brycei, commonly known as the Bryce's moon vine, is a rare flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive waxy, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of violet and white. The plant produces small, dark berries that are dispersed by animals, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and unique floral structure, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
