Piper maestranum

Piper maestranum, commonly known as the Mexican pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 2,000 species of flowering plants. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
