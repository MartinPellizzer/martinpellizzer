Panicum domingense

Panicum domingense, commonly known as the Dominican grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features slender, upright stems and produces fluffy, panicle-shaped inflorescences that are often pink or reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a slight bluish-green hue.

Panicum domingense is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and airy flower heads.
