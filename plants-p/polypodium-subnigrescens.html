Polypodium subnigrescens

Polypodium subnigrescens, commonly known as the blackening polypody, is a fern species native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds that often develop a deep blackish hue when dry. The plant typically grows on rocky outcrops or in shaded, moist environments, thriving in humid climates. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is noted for its slow growth and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage.
