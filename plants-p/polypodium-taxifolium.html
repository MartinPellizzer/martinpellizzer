Polypodium taxifolium

Polypodium taxifolium, commonly known as the bird's nest fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that resemble a bird's nest, hence its common name. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forests or on rocky surfaces.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
