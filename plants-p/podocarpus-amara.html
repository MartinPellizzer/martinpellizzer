Podocarpus amara

Podocarpus amara, commonly known as the Indian yew, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, rounded cones. The plant is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of timber. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Podocarpus amara plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
