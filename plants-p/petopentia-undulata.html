Petopentia undulata

Petopentia undulata, commonly known as the wavy leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, undulating leaves and showy, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.

Petopentia undulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
