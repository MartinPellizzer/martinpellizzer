Passiflora inamoena

Passiflora inamoena, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a distinctive veined pattern.

Passiflora inamoena is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
