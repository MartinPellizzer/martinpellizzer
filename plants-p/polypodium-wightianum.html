Polypodium wightianum

Polypodium wightianum, commonly known as the Himalayan cliff fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in rocky crevices and forested areas across the Himalayas. The plant has fronds that are usually bipinnate, with a distinctive dark green color and a leathery texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to withstand cold temperatures.

Polypodium wightianum plays a role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions and is of interest to botanists for its ecological and medicinal potential.
