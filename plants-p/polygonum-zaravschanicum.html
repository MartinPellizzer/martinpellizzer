Polygonum zaravschanicum

Polygonum zaravschanicum, commonly known as the Zaravshan knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive nodes and often succulent stems. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and can be found in river valleys and steppe regions. It features small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture.

Polygonum zaravschanicum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
