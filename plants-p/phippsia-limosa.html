Phippsia limosa

Phippsia limosa, commonly known as the slender phippsia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in spikes, and are typically greenish or yellowish in color.

Phippsia limosa is known for its ability to thrive in humid conditions and is often found in association with other moisture-loving flora.
