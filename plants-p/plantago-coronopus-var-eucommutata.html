Plantago coronopus var. eucommutata

Plantago coronopus var. eucommutata, commonly known as the European plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and coastal areas. The plant features a rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in a spike, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
