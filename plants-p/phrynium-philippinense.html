Phrynium philippinense

Phrynium philippinense, commonly known as the Philippine arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces striking, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a spathe, typical of the arum family. Its rhizomes are used as a starch source and have been traditionally utilized in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
