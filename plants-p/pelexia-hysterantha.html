Pelexia hysterantha

Pelexia hysterantha, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

Pelexia hysterantha is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
