Psorothamnus thompsoniae

Psorothamnus thompsoniae, commonly known as Thompson's greasewood, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, gray-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments and has a deep root system to access water. It is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
