Potamogeton × involutus

Potamogeton × involutus, commonly known as the curled pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that exhibits unique morphological characteristics.

It is a natural hybrid between Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton crispus, resulting in a blend of traits from both parent species. The plant is typically found in freshwater habitats such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. Its leaves are often curled or twisted, which distinguishes it from its parental species.

This feature aids in its identification and is an adaptation that may help it thrive in certain aquatic environments.
