Peperomia obcordata

Peperomia obcordata, commonly known as the "Radiator Plant," is a compact, evergreen perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are usually heart-shaped with a deep green color and a slight red or purple tint on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its compact size and attractive foliage make it an ideal plant for tabletop displays or hanging baskets.
