Pilea setigera

Pilea setigera, commonly known as the silver dollar plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are typically hidden within a spadix, a common feature in the Araceae family. Pilea setigera is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
