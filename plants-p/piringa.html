Piringa

Piringa, commonly known as *Piper auritum*, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.

The fruit, which is small and berry-like, is sometimes used in local cuisines.
