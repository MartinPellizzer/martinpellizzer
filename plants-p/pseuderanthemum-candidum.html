Pseuderanthemum candidum

Pseuderanthemum candidum, commonly known as the white pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and slightly square in cross-section.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
