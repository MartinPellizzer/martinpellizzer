Prolongoa macrocarpa

Prolongoa macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant's flowers are showy, with bright yellow petals that attract pollinators such as beetles. Its roots anchor it in muddy substrates, while its floating leaves remain just above the water surface.

Prolongoa macrocarpa plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by offering shelter and food for numerous species.
