Piper schottii var. luxurians

Piper schottii var. luxurians, commonly known as the creeping pepper plant, is a vigorous, trailing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, peppery fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its rapid growth and dense foliage make it suitable for ground cover or as a climbing plant in shaded environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species.
