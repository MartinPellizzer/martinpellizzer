Potentilla papuana

Potentilla papuana, commonly known as the Papuan cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Papua, Indonesia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and rugged, shrub-like growth habit. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Potentilla papuana is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
