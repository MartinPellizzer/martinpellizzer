Poponax polypyrigenes

Poponax polypyrigenes, commonly known as the flameleaf shrub, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange foliage, which becomes more pronounced during the dry season. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
