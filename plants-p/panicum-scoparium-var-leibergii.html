Panicum scoparium var. leibergii

Panicum scoparium var. leibergii, commonly known as the Leiberg's bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tall, slender culms that often grow in clumps. The plant produces small, fluffy panicles that are typically pink to purple in color, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for dry to average moisture conditions.

This variety is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in native plant landscapes.
