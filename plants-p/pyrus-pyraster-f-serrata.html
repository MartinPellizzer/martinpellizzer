Pyrus pyraster f. serrata

Pyrus pyraster f. serrata, commonly known as the serrated crabapple, is a botanical variant of the common crabapple.

It is characterized by its serrated leaf margins and small, ornamental flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible fruits, though they are typically small and tart. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along riverbanks.

This form is valued for its ecological benefits and ornamental appeal in horticulture.
