Paspalum notatum var. maculatum

Paspalum notatum var. maculatum, commonly known as buffelgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its coarse, dark green blades with a bluish tint and prominent white or silver markings along the midrib. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used for forage and erosion control.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments, outcompeting native vegetation.
