Pandanus tectorius var. laevis

Pandanus tectorius var. laevis, commonly known as the flat-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its broad, flat, fibrous leaves that are often used in traditional thatching and basket weaving. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and can thrive in sandy or saline soils. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in paniculate inflorescences.

The fruit, a spiky, fibrous structure, is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
