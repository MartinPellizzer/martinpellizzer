Panicum pedunculare

Panicum pedunculare, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces small, fluffy seeds in panicles. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its native range.
