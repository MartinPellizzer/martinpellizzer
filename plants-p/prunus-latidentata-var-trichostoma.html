Prunus latidentata var. trichostoma

Prunus latidentata var. trichostoma, commonly known as the whitebark cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, white flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, turning yellow in the fall. The fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that matures in late summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
