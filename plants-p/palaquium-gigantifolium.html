Palaquium gigantifolium

Palaquium gigantifolium, commonly known as the giant-leaf palaquium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, fleshy fruits resembling small, round oranges. Its botanical significance lies in its unique foliage and potential medicinal properties.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
