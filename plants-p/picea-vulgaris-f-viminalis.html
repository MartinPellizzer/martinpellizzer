Picea vulgaris f. viminalis

Picea vulgaris f. viminalis, commonly known as the weeping spruce, is a unique variant of the white spruce.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that drape downward, creating a distinctive, cascading appearance. The foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are typically a dark green on top and a silvery-white underneath. This form is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, thriving in temperate climates.
