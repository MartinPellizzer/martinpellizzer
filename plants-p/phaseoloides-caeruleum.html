Phaseoloides caeruleum

Phaseoloides caeruleum, commonly known as the blue bean plant, is a leguminous species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.
