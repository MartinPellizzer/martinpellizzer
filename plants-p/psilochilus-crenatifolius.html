Psilochilus crenatifolius

Psilochilus crenatifolius, commonly known as the wavy-leaved orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its elongated, wavy-edged leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
