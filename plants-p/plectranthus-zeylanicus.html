Plectranthus zeylanicus

Plectranthus zeylanicus, commonly known as the Sri Lankan mint, is a flowering plant native to southern Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, square stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Plectranthus zeylanicus is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its pleasant scent and medicinal properties.
