Passiflora tacanensis

Passiflora tacanensis, commonly known as the Tacana passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that display a mix of blue, purple, and white hues. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines. Its foliage consists of palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Passiflora tacanensis is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators.
