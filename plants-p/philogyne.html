Philogyne

Philogyne, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a member of the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually clustered in panicles and come in shades of white, pink, or purple.

Philogyne thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
