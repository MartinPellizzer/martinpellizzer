Priestleya teres

Priestleya teres, commonly known as the slender priestleya, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae, which includes cacti and related succulent plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. It is adapted to high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and low precipitation.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare and exotic plants.
