Pilocereus houlletianum

Pilocereus houlletianum, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, ribbed stems and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its growth habit is typically low-growing, forming dense clusters.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its striking floral display and relatively easy care requirements.
