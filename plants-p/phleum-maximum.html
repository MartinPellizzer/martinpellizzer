Phleum maximum

Phleum maximum, commonly known as sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silky foliage and a sweet, honey-like aroma when crushed. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like clusters of pale green to golden flowers.

Sweetgrass is often used in traditional crafts and has cultural significance among various Indigenous communities.
