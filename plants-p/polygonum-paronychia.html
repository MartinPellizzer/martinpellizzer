Polygonum paronychia

Polygonum paronychia, commonly known as the mossy polygonum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as buckwheats. The plant is characterized by its creeping growth habit, with slender stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a glossy surface.

The small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are typically pollinated by wind, contributing to its role in stabilizing soil in various habitats.
