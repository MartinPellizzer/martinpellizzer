Prunus jamasakura f. pendula

Prunus jamasakura f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping sakura, is a unique cultivar of the Prunus jamasakura species.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that droop gracefully, creating a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers in early spring, which are highly ornamental and attract pollinators. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a glossy green color.

This cultivar is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic value and seasonal beauty.
