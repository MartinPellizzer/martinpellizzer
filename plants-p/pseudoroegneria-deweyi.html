Pseudoroegneria deweyi

Pseudoroegneria deweyi, commonly known as Dewey's false brome, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Nevada. This perennial grass typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
