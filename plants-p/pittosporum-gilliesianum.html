Pittosporum gilliesianum

Pittosporum gilliesianum, commonly known as the New Zealand white pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a distinctive waxy texture. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, followed by small, orange-red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

The species is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience.
