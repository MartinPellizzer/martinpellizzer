Plectranthus hadiensis var. tomentosus

Plectranthus hadiensis var. tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly plectranthus, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It features hairy, gray-green foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or blue, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for gardens and containers.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
