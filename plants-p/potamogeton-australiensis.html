Potamogeton australiensis

Potamogeton australiensis, commonly known as the Australian pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and wetlands. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are arranged spirally around its stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
