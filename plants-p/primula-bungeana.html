Primula bungeana

Primula bungeana, commonly known as the Chinese primrose, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in spring and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are oval and slightly hairy, providing a textured appearance.

Primula bungeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
