Prunus domestica var. pendula

Prunus domestica var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping plum, is a cultivar of the common European plum.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that droop gracefully, creating a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in spring, followed by edible purple or yellow fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is often planted for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
