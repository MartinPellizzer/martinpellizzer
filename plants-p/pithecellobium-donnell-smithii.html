Pithecellobium donnell-smithii

Pithecellobium donnell-smithii, commonly known as the Hawaiian pisonia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
