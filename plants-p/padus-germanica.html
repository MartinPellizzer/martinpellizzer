Padus germanica

Padus germanica, commonly known as the whitebeam.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub native to Europe and western Asia, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are usually oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers in clusters, followed by small, red or orange berries that are edible for birds and mammals. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodlands, hedgerows, and along stream banks.

The species is known for its hardiness and ability to tolerate pollution, making it a common urban and rural tree.
