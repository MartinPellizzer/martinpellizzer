Potamogeton oblongus f. angustifolius

Potamogeton oblongus f. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It has long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by cylindrical, elongated fruits. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in lakes and slow-moving rivers.
