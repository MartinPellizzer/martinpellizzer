Primulina crassifolia

Primulina crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed gloxinia, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. Its foliage is broad and waxy, which helps it retain moisture in its native humid environments.

Primulina crassifolia is popular among collectors for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation under proper care.
