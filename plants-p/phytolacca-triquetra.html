Phytolacca triquetra

Phytolacca triquetra, commonly known as the three-angled nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features simple, alternate, and often hairy leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in both traditional medicine and pharmacological research.
