Potentilla comaroides var. fusca

Potentilla comaroides var. fusca, commonly known as black potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, stems and leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and landscaping.
