Psidium tripartitum

Psidium tripartitum, commonly known as the three-lobed guava, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow or red berries. Its leaves are elliptical with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name.

The fruit is rich in vitamins and has a sweet, aromatic flavor, making it popular in local cuisines.
