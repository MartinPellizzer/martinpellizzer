Potentilla erecta var. typica

Potentilla erecta var. typica, commonly known as the erect cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and meadows.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
