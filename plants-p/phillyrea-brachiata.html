Phillyrea brachiata

Phillyrea brachiata, commonly known as the mountain phillyrea, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, with a leathery texture. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters during the spring and are followed by small, spherical berries.

Phillyrea brachiata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a hedge.
