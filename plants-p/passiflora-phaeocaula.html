Passiflora phaeocaula

Passiflora phaeocaula, commonly known as the white-stemmed passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its stems are covered in a distinctive white, waxy coating, which gives it its common name.

Passiflora phaeocaula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
