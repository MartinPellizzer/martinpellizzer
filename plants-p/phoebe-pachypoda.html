Phoebe pachypoda

Phoebe pachypoda, commonly known as the broadleaf phoebe, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and oval-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, berry-like fruits. Native to parts of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
