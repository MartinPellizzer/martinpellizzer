Patagonium alcicornutum

Patagonium alcicornutum, commonly known as the Patagonian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and large, showy purple flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has adapted to the harsh, arid conditions of the Patagonian steppe through its deep root system and drought-resistant foliage. Its leaves are typically covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Patagonium alcicornutum plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various insects and small animals.
