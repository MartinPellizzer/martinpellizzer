Parodia schuetziana

Parodia schuetziana, commonly known as the Schuetzian pincushion cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its globular shape and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its stems are covered in white to pale green spines, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental appearance.

Parodia schuetziana is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.
