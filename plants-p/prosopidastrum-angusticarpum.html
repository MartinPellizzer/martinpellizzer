Prosopidastrum angusticarpum

Prosopidastrum angusticarpum, commonly known as the narrow-fruited spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glabrous and have a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers surrounded by specialized bracts, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in dry, disturbed habitats and is often found in arid regions of southern Africa.

Its latex is milky and can be toxic, contributing to its role as a weed in certain agricultural areas.
