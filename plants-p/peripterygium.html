Peripterygium

Peripterygium, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are annual or biennial herbs. The plants typically have basal leaves that are lance-shaped or ovate, with hairy surfaces. They produce small, yellow flowers that are clustered in a head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Peripterygium species are often found in disturbed soils and are native to various regions in the Americas.
