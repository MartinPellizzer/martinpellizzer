Phaseolus sphaericus var. pragensis

Phaseolus sphaericus var. pragensis, commonly known as the Czech bean or Praga bean, is a rare and ancient legume native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its spherical or nearly spherical pods, which are typically green when immature and turn brown as they mature. The plant is a climbing vine with trifoliate leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its seeds are usually small and wrinkled, with a mild, nutty flavor.

This variety is valued for its historical significance and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique morphology and heritage.
