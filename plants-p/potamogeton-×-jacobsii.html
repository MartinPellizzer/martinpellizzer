Potamogeton × jacobsii

Potamogeton × jacobsii, commonly known as the hybrid water plant, is a sterile hybrid species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton praelatus.

It is characterized by its dense, floating growth in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged, with small, inconspicuous flowers that rarely produce viable seeds. It is often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands, where it can form dense mats that affect water flow and aquatic biodiversity.

Due to its sterility, it does not contribute to natural population growth but can become invasive in certain ecosystems.
