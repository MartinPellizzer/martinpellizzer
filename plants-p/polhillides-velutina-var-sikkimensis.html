Polhillides velutina var. sikkimensis

Polhillides velutina var. sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim velvet fern, is a rare and unique plant species native to the Himalayan region.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and have a distinctive texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a botanical curiosity and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
