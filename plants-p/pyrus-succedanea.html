Pyrus succedanea

Pyrus succedanea, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, white, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by edible, apple-like fruits that are often used in traditional Japanese cuisine.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
