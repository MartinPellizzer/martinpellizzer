Pseudodatura suaveolens

Pseudodatura suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a smooth, fleshy berry that turns from green to yellow or red as it matures.
