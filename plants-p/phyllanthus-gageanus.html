Phyllanthus gageanus

Phyllanthus gageanus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, greenish fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

Phyllanthus gageanus is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
