Potentilla sylvestris proles langei

Potentilla sylvestris proles langei, commonly known as the Lange's shrubby cinquefoil, is a perennial plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated margins, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive floral display.
