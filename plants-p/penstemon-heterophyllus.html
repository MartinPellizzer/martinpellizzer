Penstemon heterophyllus

Penstemon heterophyllus, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in mountainous regions. It has a sprawling or upright growth habit, with leaves that vary in shape depending on the environment.

Penstemon heterophyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
