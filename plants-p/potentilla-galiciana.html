Potentilla galiciana

Potentilla galiciana, commonly known as the French potentilla, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright, often thorny stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a rough texture and serrated edges.

Potentilla galiciana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
