Paspalum pellitum

Paspalum pellitum, commonly known as the slender paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.

Paspalum pellitum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
