Phuodendron

Phuodendron, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Rosaceae.

These plants are characterized by their small, showy flowers and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Rosaceae family. Phuodendron species are native to East Asia, particularly China, and are often found in forested areas. They produce small, red or yellow fruits that are edible and used in traditional medicine.

The genus includes several species, each with unique variations in leaf shape and flowering patterns.
