Panicum pseudoischaemum

Panicum pseudoischaemum, commonly known as blue panic grass, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its upright growth habit and blue-green foliage. The plant produces inflorescences that consist of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed spikelets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens and landscapes.

Due to its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, it is also used in erosion control and as a forage crop in some regions.
