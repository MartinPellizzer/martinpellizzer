Pittosporum polycarpum

Pittosporum polycarpum, commonly known as the New Zealand pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, oval-shaped berries that are typically yellow or red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Pittosporum polycarpum is also valued for its ability to attract native pollinators and birds.
