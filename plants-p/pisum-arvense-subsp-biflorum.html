Pisum arvense subsp. biflorum

Pisum arvense subsp.

biflorum, commonly known as the two-flowered vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that typically appear in clusters.

The plant produces small, elliptical pods containing one to two seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

As a legume, it has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
