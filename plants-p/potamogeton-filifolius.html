Potamogeton filifolius

Potamogeton filifolius, commonly known as the thread-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like clusters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton filifolius is often found in slow-moving or still waters such as ponds and lakes.
