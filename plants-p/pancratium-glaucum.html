Pancratium glaucum

Pancratium glaucum, commonly known as the sea daffodil, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a white center, which bloom in the spring. The plant has strap-like leaves and is often found growing in sandy or rocky soils near the shore. It is adapted to salt spray and can tolerate coastal conditions.

Pancratium glaucum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
