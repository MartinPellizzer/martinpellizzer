Polygala amara subsp. carniolica

Polygala amara subsp.

carniolica, commonly known as the Carpathian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, usually greenish or yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. It is known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant traditionally used in herbal remedies.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
