Parsonsia sanguinea var. brassii

Parsonsia sanguinea var. brassii, commonly known as the red-flowered parsonsia, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters in suitable conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy floral display.
