Primula pallasii

Primula pallasii, commonly known as the Pallas's primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
