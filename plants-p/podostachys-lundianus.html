Podostachys lundianus

Podostachys lundianus, commonly known as the African waterwheel, is a unique aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its intricate, wheel-like inflorescence that floats on the water surface, with flowers arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant thrives in fast-flowing streams and rivers, where its rhizomes anchor it to submerged rocks. Its leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, adapted to reduce water resistance.

Podostachys lundianus plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter for aquatic organisms and contributing to nutrient cycling.
