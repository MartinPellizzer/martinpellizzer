Persicaria hastatoauriculata

Persicaria hastatoauriculata, commonly known as the broad-leafed knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive auriculate (ear-like) bases and serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and spreading habit make it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
