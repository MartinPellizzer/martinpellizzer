Poa havanensis

Poa havanensis, commonly known as the Havana grass, is a species of grass native to Cuba.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny areas. It has small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Poa havanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
