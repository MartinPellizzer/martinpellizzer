Potamogeton perfoliatus subsp. richardsonii

Potamogeton perfoliatus subsp.

richardsonii, commonly known as Richardson's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America. It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, rivers, and ponds.

The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. Its flowers are small and green, usually occurring in clusters near the water surface.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
