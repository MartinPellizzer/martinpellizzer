Phyllanthus imbricatus

Phyllanthus imbricatus, commonly known as the stone apple or Indian date, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by fleshy, red or orange berries.
