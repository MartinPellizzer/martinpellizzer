Penstemon connatus

Penstemon connatus, commonly known as the western penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vivid pink to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Penstemon connatus is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in arid environments.
