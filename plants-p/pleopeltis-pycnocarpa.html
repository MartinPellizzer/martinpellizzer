Pleopeltis pycnocarpa

Pleopeltis pycnocarpa, commonly known as the dense-fruited tree fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant produces clustered, spore-bearing structures called sorangia on the underside of its fronds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
