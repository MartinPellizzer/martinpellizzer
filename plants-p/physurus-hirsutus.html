Physurus hirsutus

Physurus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy mouse-eared cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, hairy spines that provide protection against herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Its root system is adapted to absorb moisture efficiently in arid environments.

Overall, Physurus hirsutus exemplifies the adaptive strategies of cacti in harsh, dry climates.
