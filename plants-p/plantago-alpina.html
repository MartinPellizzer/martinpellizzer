Plantago alpina

Plantago alpina, commonly known as alpine plantain, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions across the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes, which are borne on long, slender stalks. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Its ability to tolerate cold climates makes it a common sight in high-altitude ecosystems.
