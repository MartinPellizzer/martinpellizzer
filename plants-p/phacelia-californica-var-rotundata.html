Phacelia californica var. rotundata

Phacelia californica var. rotundata, commonly known as the rounded phacelia, is a perennial herb native to California.

It features showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open habitats like grasslands and chaparral.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting pollinators.
