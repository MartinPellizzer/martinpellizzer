Potentilla divisa

Potentilla divisa, commonly known as the divided cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a graceful appearance. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats.

Potentilla divisa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
