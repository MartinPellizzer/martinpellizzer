Pteris vespertilionis var. carmichaeliana

Pteris vespertilionis var. carmichaeliana, commonly known as the Carmichael's water fern, is a variant of the common water fern.

It is a perennial fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in humid forests. The plant features delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically green with a slight variegation. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
