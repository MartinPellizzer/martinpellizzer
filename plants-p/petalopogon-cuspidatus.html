Petalopogon cuspidatus

Petalopogon cuspidatus, commonly known as the sharp-pointed beauty, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated petals that are often a vibrant mix of red and orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

Petalopogon cuspidatus is known for its unique floral structure, which features a prominent central spire surrounded by clustered petals.
