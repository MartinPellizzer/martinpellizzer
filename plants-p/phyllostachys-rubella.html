Phyllostachys rubella

Phyllostachys rubella, commonly known as the red bamboo, is a species of grass in the Bambusoideae subfamily.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its slender, gracefully arching stems that often exhibit a reddish hue, especially when young. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a smooth texture and a pale green color.

Phyllostachys rubella is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
