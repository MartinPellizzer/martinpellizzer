Piper lowong

Piper lowong, commonly known as the Bornean pepper or lowong pepper, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its foliage is characterized by ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a strong, pungent scent.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.
