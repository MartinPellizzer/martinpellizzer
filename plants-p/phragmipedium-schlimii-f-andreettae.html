Phragmipedium schlimii f. andreettae

Phragmipedium schlimii f. andreettae, commonly known as the Andreetta's phragmipedium, is a rare and exquisite orchid species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers with vibrant yellow petals and a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its botanical features include lanceolate leaves and a compact growth habit, making it a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
