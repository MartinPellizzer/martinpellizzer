Piper triquetrum

Piper triquetrum, commonly known as the three-angled pepper or triquetrum pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus within the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are tiny and greenish-white, typically clustered in short inflorescences.

The fruit, which is a small, three-lobed berry, contains the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
