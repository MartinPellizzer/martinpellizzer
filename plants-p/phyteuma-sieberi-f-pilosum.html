Phyteuma sieberi f. pilosum

Phyteuma sieberi f. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy phyteuma, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant features hairy foliage, which gives it its common name and helps in distinguishing it from other similar species. Native to Central Asia, it thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in gardens and botanical collections.
