Palaquium neoebudicum

Palaquium neoebudicum, commonly known as the Neobudic mango, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, sweet fruits with a thin, fibrous skin. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and hill forest areas.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
