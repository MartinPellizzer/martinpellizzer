Piper flavimarginatum

Piper flavimarginatum, commonly known as the golden-margin pepper, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellow margin. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by small, round fruits that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
