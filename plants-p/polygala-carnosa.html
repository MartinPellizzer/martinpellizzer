Polygala carnosa

Polygala carnosa, commonly known as the hairy polygala, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and clusters of small, pale blue or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually hairy and narrow, contributing to its common name.

Polygala carnosa is often found in arid or semi-arid regions, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
