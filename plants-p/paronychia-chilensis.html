Paronychia chilensis

Paronychia chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in coastal and arid environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It is adapted to withstand harsh conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Paronychia chilensis is often found in coastal dunes and rocky coastal areas.
