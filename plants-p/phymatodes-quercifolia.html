Phymatodes quercifolia

Phymatodes quercifolia, commonly known as the oakleaf phymatode, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and chaparral habitats.

Phymatodes quercifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
