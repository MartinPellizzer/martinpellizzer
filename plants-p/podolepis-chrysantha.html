Podolepis chrysantha

Podolepis chrysantha, commonly known as the golden sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are succulent and adapted to arid conditions, making it drought-tolerant.

Podolepis chrysantha is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appearance.
