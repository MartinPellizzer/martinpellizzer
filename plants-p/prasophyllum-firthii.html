Prasophyllum firthii

Prasophyllum firthii, commonly known as the Firth's leek orchid, is an endemic species of the Australian flora.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. This plant is typically found in heathland and sandplain environments, thriving in nutrient-poor soils. It is a perennial herb that flowers during the late winter to early spring season.

Due to its restricted habitat and potential threats from land clearing, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
