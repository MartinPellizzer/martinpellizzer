Penducella catella

Penducella catella, commonly known as the common cat's tail, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, trailing stems that produce clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Penducella catella is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its delicate, cascading floral display.
