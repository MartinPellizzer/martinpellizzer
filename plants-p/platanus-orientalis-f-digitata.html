Platanus orientalis f. digitata

Platanus orientalis f. digitata, commonly known as the Turkish plane or Oriental plane, is a distinctive cultivar of the plane tree.

It is characterized by its unique, deeply lobed leaves that resemble the fingers of a hand, giving it its common name. The plant is native to the eastern Mediterranean region and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in temperate climates and is known for its large, showy flowers that appear in spring.

The tree can grow quite tall, reaching heights of up to 30 meters, and is valued for its adaptability and resilience.
