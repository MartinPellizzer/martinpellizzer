Phaseolus elegans

Phaseolus elegans, commonly known as the elegant bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soil with full sunlight. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the seeds are edible when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both decorative and culinary purposes.
