Phaseolus vulgaris var. aurospurcatus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. aurospurcatus, commonly known as the golden leaf kidney bean, is a variant of the common kidney bean characterized by its striking yellow foliage.

This plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to the Americas, where it has been cultivated for its edible seeds. The leaves are typically large and heart-shaped, with a vibrant yellow coloration that distinguishes it from other bean varieties. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The pods contain round, yellow to brown seeds that are valued for their nutritional content and culinary uses.
