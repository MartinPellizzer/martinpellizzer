Paspalidium pradanum

Paspalidium pradanum, commonly known as the Pradanum grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Paspalidium pradanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage plant.
