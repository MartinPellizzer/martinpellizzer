Pseudacoridium woodianum

Pseudacoridium woodianum, commonly known as the African orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant typically blooms in winter, producing intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
