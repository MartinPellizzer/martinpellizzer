Primulina pseudoglandulosa

Primulina pseudoglandulosa, commonly known as the velvet plant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is popular among plant enthusiasts.
