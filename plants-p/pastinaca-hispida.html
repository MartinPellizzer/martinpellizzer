Pastinaca hispida

Pastinaca hispida, commonly known as rough bur marigold, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically rough and serrated, with a distinctive hairy texture.

Pastinaca hispida is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.
