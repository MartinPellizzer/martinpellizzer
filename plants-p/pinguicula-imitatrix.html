Pinguicula imitatrix

Pinguicula imitatrix, commonly known as the mimic butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, glandular leaves that secrete digestive enzymes to trap and digest small insects. The plant's leaves are often covered in a sticky substance that helps in capturing prey. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Pinguicula imitatrix is notable for its ability to mimic the appearance of other plants, which may aid in its survival by deterring herbivores.
