Pinus sylvestris var. batava

Pinus sylvestris var. batava, commonly known as the Scots pine, is a variant of the European silver fir.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it produces cones that contain seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

This species is often used in reforestation efforts due to its resilience and adaptability.
