Peltapteris flabellata f. standleyi

Peltapteris flabellata f. standleyi, commonly known as the Standley's peltapteris, is a fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a fan-like appearance that gives it its common name. The plant exhibits a complex morphology, featuring a short rhizome with numerous roots and a rosette of fronds that radiate from the center. Its leaves are usually finely divided, with a delicate, lace-like texture that contributes to its ornamental appeal.

This fern is native to tropical regions and thrives in humid, shaded environments.
