Pyriluma sphaerocarpa

Pyriluma sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiky flower heads that resemble small globes, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Pyriluma sphaerocarpa is valued in gardens for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
