Phyllostegia parviflora var. major

Phyllostegia parviflora var. major, commonly known as the narrow-leaved strobilanthes, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
