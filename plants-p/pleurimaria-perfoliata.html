Pleurimaria perfoliata

Pleurimaria perfoliata, commonly known as the perforate leaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves that are often perforated or have a hole in the center, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Pleurimaria perfoliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
