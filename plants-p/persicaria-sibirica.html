Persicaria sibirica

Persicaria sibirica, commonly known as Siberian knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cooler regions of Asia.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered in fine hairs. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a reddish or purple tinge on the underside. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense terminal spikes, blooming in late summer.

The plant is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
