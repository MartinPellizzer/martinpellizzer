Phalaenopsis luteola

Phalaenopsis luteola, commonly known as the yellow phalaenopsis orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Phalaenopsis luteola is prized for its long-lasting blooms and is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
