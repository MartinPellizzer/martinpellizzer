Pleurothallis crucilabia

Pleurothallis crucilabia, commonly known as the cross-veined pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its flowers are usually clustered in inflorescences and bloom during the rainy season.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
