Prunus cerasus f. salicifolia

Prunus cerasus f. salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf cherry, is a unique cultivar of the cherry tree characterized by its distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are narrow and elongated, resembling those of willow trees, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically smaller than those of typical cherries. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
