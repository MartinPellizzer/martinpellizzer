Peperomia villosa

Peperomia villosa, commonly known as the velvet peperomia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a unique texture. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub with upright stems and small, clustered flowers. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its adaptability and ornamental appearance make it a favored plant among collectors and plant enthusiasts.
