Pentameris airoides subsp. jugorum

Pentameris airoides subsp.

jugorum, commonly known as the blue bell or blue bell orchid, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

This subspecies is part of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
