Plantago lanceolata subvar. lasiophylla

Plantago lanceolata subvar. lasiophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
