Piper stileferum

Piper stileferum, commonly known as the St. Helena pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the island of St. Helena in the South Atlantic Ocean. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that develop into small, elongated berries.

The plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique geographic isolation and limited distribution.
