Phlomoides betonicoides

Phlomoides betonicoides, commonly known as purple loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and Asia.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy as well. The plant produces showy, spike-like clusters of purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It is known for its ability to thrive in moist environments such as wetlands and marshes.

However, it is considered an invasive species in North America, where it can outcompete native vegetation.
