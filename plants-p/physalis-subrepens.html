Physalis subrepens

Physalis subrepens, commonly known as the creeping waxberry, is a low-growing shrub native to South America.

It features fleshy, tubular fruits that enclose numerous small, hard seeds. The plant has hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant orange fruits and its ability to spread via runners.
