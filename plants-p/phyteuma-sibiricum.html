Phyteuma sibiricum

Phyteuma sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing clusters of blue or violet-colored flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its popularity among gardeners and botanists alike.
