Prunus spinosa var. domestica

Prunus spinosa var. domestica, commonly known as the domestic blackthorn, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark purple berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has small, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pale pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The berries are used in traditional foods and have been historically used in making jams and wines.
