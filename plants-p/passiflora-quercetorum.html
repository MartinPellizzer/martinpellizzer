Passiflora quercetorum

Passiflora quercetorum, commonly known as the oak-leaf passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that resemble the leaves of oak trees. The plant produces showy, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments, often in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

Passiflora quercetorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
