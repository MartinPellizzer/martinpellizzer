Pourouma cinerascens

Pourouma cinerascens, commonly known as the red bamboo palm, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that resemble bamboo, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant features large, pinnate leaves that can span several meters in length, giving it a dramatic visual presence. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces edible fruits that are consumed by various wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in tropical gardens and conservation areas.
