Phragmites jahandiezii

Phragmites jahandiezii, commonly known as the Iranian reed, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, including parts of Iran. This plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to water-saturated soils. Its tall, slender reed-like stems can reach heights of up to 3 meters, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
