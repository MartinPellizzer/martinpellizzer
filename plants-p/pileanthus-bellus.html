Pileanthus bellus

Pileanthus bellus, commonly known as the beautiful pilea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which includes plants such as philodendrons and dieffenbachias. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Pileanthus bellus prefers indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor environments.
