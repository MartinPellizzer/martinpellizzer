Polygonum alopecuroides f. pilosum

Polygonum alopecuroides f. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy knotweed, is a variant of the species Polygonum alopecuroides, characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their unique leaf structures and often vibrant flowers. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves with a dense covering of fine hairs, contributing to its textured appearance. It is native to regions of East Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

This fern-like plant is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
