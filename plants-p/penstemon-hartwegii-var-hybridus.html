Penstemon hartwegii var. hybridus

Penstemon hartwegii var. hybridus, commonly known as the hybrid penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy coating.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
