Polygonatum anceps

Polygonatum anceps, commonly known as the Chinese Solomon's Seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a rosette and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late spring. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and delicate blooms, making it a popular choice in gardens.

Polygonatum anceps is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
