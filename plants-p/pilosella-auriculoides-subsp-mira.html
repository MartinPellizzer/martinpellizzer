Pilosella auriculoides subsp. mira

Pilosella auriculoides subsp.

mira, commonly known as the golden hairpin, is a flowering plant native to South America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with a hairy appearance.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, daisy-like inflorescences.
