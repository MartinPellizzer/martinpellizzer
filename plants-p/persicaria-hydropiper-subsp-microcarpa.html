Persicaria hydropiper subsp. microcarpa

Persicaria hydropiper subsp.

microcarpa, commonly known as water pepper, is a flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae. It is characterized by its small, greenish-white flowers that appear in dense clusters.

The plant has smooth, succulent stems and leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist environments such as wet meadows and stream banks.

Its root system is known for producing a peppery substance, which gives it its common name.
