Protea welwitschii subsp. melliodora

Protea welwitschii subsp.

melliodora, commonly known as the sweet-scented protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family. It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous pale pink to white flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape.

The plant is native to the Eastern Cape and Western Cape provinces, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. Its flowers emit a subtle, sweet fragrance, which attracts pollinators such as bees and birds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and ecological significance.
