Plumeria cochleata

Plumeria cochleata, commonly known as the spiral plumeria, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often covered in a waxy, white coating. The flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the branches, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Plumeria cochleata is also notable for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.
