Potentilla viscidula

Potentilla viscidula, commonly known as sticky cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

Due to its sticky leaves and showy flowers, it is both ecologically valuable and aesthetically pleasing in gardens.
