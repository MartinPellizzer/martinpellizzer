Psiadia cataractae

Psiadia cataractae, commonly known as the waterfall daisy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its name derives from its preference for areas near waterfalls or streams.

Psiadia cataractae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
