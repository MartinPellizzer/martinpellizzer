Polygala obtusissima var. somalensis

Polygala obtusissima var. somalensis, commonly known as the Somali milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Somalia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are usually pale pink or white, with a distinctive hooded structure that resembles a milkweed flower.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations in its native habitat.
