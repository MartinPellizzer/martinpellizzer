Polymnia corcovadensis

Polymnia corcovadensis, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its violet-colored flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
