Pelargonium virgineum

Pelargonium virgineum, commonly known as the Virgin's geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces pink to red blooms, which appear in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Pelargonium virgineum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
