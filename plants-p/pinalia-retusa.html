Pinalia retusa

Pinalia retusa, commonly known as the smooth pincushion cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a low-growing, succulent plant characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in soft, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Pinalia retusa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
