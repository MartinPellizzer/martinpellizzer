Phalaenopsis parishii f. alba

Phalaenopsis parishii f. alba, commonly known as the white phalaenopsis orchid, is a rare and elegant hybrid orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and a subtle pinkish hue on the lip, which sets it apart from other phalaenopsis varieties. This orchid typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in its natural habitat. It produces long, slender pseudobulbs and thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light.

The plant is prized for its beauty and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in both home and commercial settings.
