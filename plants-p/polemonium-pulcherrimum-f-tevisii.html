Polemonium pulcherrimum f. tevisii

Polemonium pulcherrimum f. tevisii, commonly known as the Tevis's Jacob's ladder, is a botanical variant of the showy lady's slipper.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States, particularly in moist woodland habitats. The plant features clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in early to mid-summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
