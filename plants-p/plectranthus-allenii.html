Plectranthus allenii

Plectranthus allenii, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of tubular, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Plectranthus allenii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
