Polypodium nymphale

Polypodium nymphale, commonly known as the golden polypody, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks, thriving in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are usually bright green, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

Polypodium nymphale is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
