Pabstiella rubrolineata

Pabstiella rubrolineata, commonly known as the red-lined fern, is a member of the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that display a striking red stripe along the midrib. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
