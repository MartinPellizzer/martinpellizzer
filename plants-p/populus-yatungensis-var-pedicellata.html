Populus yatungensis var. pedicellata

Populus yatungensis var. pedicellata, commonly known as the Yatung poplar, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by samaroid seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in river valleys and mountainous regions.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
