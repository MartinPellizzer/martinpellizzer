Plaesiantha

Plaesiantha, commonly known as the "sea hibiscus," is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Indo-Pacific.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with thick, fleshy leaves adapted to salty, windy environments. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.

Plaesiantha is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in coastal gardens.
