Paeonia

Paeonia, commonly known as peony, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Ranunculaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, often fragrant flowers and broad, palmately lobed leaves. Peonies are native to Asia, Europe, and North America, with over 30 species identified. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial or a shrub, with some species reaching heights of up to 2 meters.

Its flowers come in a wide range of colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow, making it a popular ornamental plant.
