Phaseoloides pubinerve

Phaseoloides pubinerve, commonly known as the hairy vein bean, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy veins on the leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with pinnately compound leaves. It produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or white in color.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
