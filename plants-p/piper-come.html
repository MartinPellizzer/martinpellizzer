Piper come

Piper come, commonly known as the pepper plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that turn from green to red as they mature, containing the seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and economically significant in various regions.
