Psidium brownianum

Psidium brownianum, commonly known as the Brownian guava, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its fruit, similar to a guava, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential culinary uses.
