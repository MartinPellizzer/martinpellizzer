Puya adscendens

Puya adscendens, commonly known as the Andean torch, is a striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains.

It is a member of the bromeliad family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that can reach up to 6 meters in height. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette, and its flowers are typically bright red or orange, attracting various pollinators. Puya adscendens is adapted to cold, dry environments and thrives in full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
