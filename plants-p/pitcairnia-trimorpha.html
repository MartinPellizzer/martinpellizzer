Pitcairnia trimorpha

Pitcairnia trimorpha, commonly known as the Three-lobed Air Plant, is a species of bromeliad native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, woolly coating. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright red or orange flowers, which bloom in the summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to grow on trees or rocks.

Its unique morphology and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and enthusiasts.
