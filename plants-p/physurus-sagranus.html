Physurus sagranus

Physurus sagranus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-30 cm and thrives in bright, indirect light. Its leaves are velvety and often have a distinctive coloration, while its flowers come in a variety of colors including purple, pink, and white.

Physurus sagranus is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
