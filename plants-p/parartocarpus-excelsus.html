Parartocarpus excelsus

Parartocarpus excelsus, commonly known as the giant pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its thick, fibrous trunk and broad, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences with small, fragrant flowers, which are followed by fleshy, spiky fruits. Its leaves are used in traditional weaving and construction, while the fruits are edible and often consumed raw or cooked.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in coastal and wetland habitats.
