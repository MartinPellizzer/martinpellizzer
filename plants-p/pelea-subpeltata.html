Pelea subpeltata

Pelea subpeltata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pelea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in late summer. Pelea subpeltata thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
