Papaver nudicaule var. hartianum

Papaver nudicaule var. hartianum, commonly known as Hart's poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, papery petals that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a slight red or purple hue at the base. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to its natural environment.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
