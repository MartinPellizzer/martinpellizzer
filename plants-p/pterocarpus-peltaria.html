Pterocarpus peltaria

Pterocarpus peltaria, commonly known as the Indian satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its palmately compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody pods containing flat, dark seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its fine grain and durability, making it a sought-after material in furniture and carving.

The plant also has medicinal uses in traditional medicine, particularly for its anti-inflammatory properties.
