Plinia toscanosia

Plinia toscanosia, commonly known as the Tuscan bilberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Italy, and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are rich in antioxidants and are often used in local cuisine. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and compact growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

The plant plays an important ecological role by supporting pollinators and providing food for wildlife.
