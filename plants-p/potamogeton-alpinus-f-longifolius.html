Potamogeton alpinus f. longifolius

Potamogeton alpinus f. longifolius, commonly known as the alpine pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to cold, freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are typically ribbon-like in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spike-like clusters. It thrives in nutrient-rich waters and is often found in lakes and ponds with clear, well-oxygenated water.

Due to its preference for cooler temperatures, it is typically found in higher elevation or northern regions.
