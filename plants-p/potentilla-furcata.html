Potentilla furcata

Potentilla furcata, commonly known as the forked cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, five-lobed leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Potentilla furcata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.
