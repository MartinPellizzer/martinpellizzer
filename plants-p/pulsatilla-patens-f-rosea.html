Pulsatilla patens f. rosea

Pulsatilla patens f. rosea, commonly known as the pink pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features a hairy, upright stem with palmate leaves that are deeply divided. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

The f. rosea form is distinguished by its more vibrant, rose-colored petals compared to the typical white or yellow flowers of the species.
