Phalaenopsis speciosa var. christiana

Phalaenopsis speciosa var. christiana, commonly known as the Christian's orchid, is a popular epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting blooms that typically appear in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant.
