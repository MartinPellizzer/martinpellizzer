Passiflora normalis

Passiflora normalis, commonly known as the common passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a unique structure with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a mild, sweet flavor. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and can be used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Passiflora normalis thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in rainforests and along riverbanks.
