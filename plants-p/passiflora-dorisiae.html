Passiflora dorisiae

Passiflora dorisiae, commonly known as the Doris's passionflower, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a blend of blue, violet, and white petals, along with prominent crimson filaments. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Passiflora dorisiae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
