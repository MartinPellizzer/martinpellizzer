Pultenaea ilicifolia

Pultenaea ilicifolia, commonly known as the prickly hop, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in heathlands and dry forests, characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Pultenaea ilicifolia is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
