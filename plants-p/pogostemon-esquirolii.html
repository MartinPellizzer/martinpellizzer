Pogostemon esquirolii

Pogostemon esquirolii, commonly known as the Madagascar basil, is a herbaceous plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple. It is often cultivated for its fragrant leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and cooking.

Pogostemon esquirolii thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
