Phyllanthus podenzanae

Phyllanthus podenzanae, commonly known as the Brazilian spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, opposite leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It is adapted to thrive in humid, tropical environments with well-drained soil.

Phyllanthus podenzanae is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional Brazilian medicine.
