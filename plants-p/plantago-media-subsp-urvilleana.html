Plantago media subsp. urvilleana

Plantago media subsp.

urvilleana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

This subspecies is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
