Peperomia ekmanii

Peperomia ekmanii, commonly known as the Ekman's peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically oval to heart-shaped, with a waxy texture and a variety of colors ranging from deep green to variegated patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish, clustered in spikes near the base of the stems. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.

Peperomia ekmanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and air-purifying qualities, making it a popular choice for modern interior spaces.
