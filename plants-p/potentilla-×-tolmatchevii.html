Potentilla × tolmatchevii

Potentilla × tolmatchevii, commonly known as the Tolmatchev silverweed, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in temperate climates.
