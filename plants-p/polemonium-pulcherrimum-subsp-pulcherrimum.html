Polemonium pulcherrimum subsp. pulcherrimum

Polemonium pulcherrimum subsp.

pulcherrimum, commonly known as the showy lady's slipper, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America. It belongs to the family Polemoniaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
