Penstemon fruticosus var. scouleri

Penstemon fruticosus var. scouleri, commonly known as the Cascade penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or rocky soils.

This species is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
