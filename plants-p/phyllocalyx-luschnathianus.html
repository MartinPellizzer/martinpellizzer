Phyllocalyx luschnathianus

Phyllocalyx luschnathianus, commonly known as the redroot false indigo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces showy violet to purple pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
