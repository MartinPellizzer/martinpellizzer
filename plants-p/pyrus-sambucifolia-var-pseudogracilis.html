Pyrus sambucifolia var. pseudogracilis

Pyrus sambucifolia var. pseudogracilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pear, is a variant of the pear tree characterized by its slender, lanceolate leaves.

It is native to parts of East Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are similar to common pears. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
