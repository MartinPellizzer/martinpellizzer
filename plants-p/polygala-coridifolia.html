Polygala coridifolia

Polygala coridifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaved milkwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain the seeds.

It is often found in coastal regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
