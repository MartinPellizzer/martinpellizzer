Patagonium muricatum var. pimpinellifolium

Patagonium muricatum var. pimpinellifolium, commonly known as the cherry tomato, is a variant of the wild tomato species native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, round fruits that are typically red when ripe, though they can also be yellow or orange. The plant has hairy, lobed leaves and produces flowers that are usually yellow with five petals. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its flavorful, nutrient-rich fruits used in culinary applications.
