Phyllanthus stylosus

Phyllanthus stylosus, commonly known as the creeping woodbine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, hard seeds.

Phyllanthus stylosus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
