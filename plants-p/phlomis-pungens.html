Phlomis pungens

Phlomis pungens, commonly known as the sharpleaf mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and tall spikes of yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.

Its common name reflects the sharply pointed leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.
