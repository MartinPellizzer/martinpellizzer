Papaver alpinum var. flavum

Papaver alpinum var. flavum, commonly known as the Alpine yellow poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a distinctive, tall stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and alpine landscapes.
