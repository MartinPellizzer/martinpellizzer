Pavonia restiaria f. sublobata

Pavonia restiaria f. sublobata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pavonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy pink or red flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in warm climates.
