Prunus × lannesiana f. yedozakura

Prunus × lannesiana f. yedozakura, commonly known as the Yedozakura cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a hybrid species, resulting from the cross between Prunus lannesiana and Prunus × yedoensis, which contributes to its distinctive floral display. The plant is known for its double flowers, which are typically pink or white, and its compact growth habit. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters.

The leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy surface, and the bark is smooth and reddish-brown, adding to its ornamental value.
