Protea prostrata

Protea prostrata, commonly known as the dwarf protea, is a South African native plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, woody stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces distinctive, rounded flower heads that are often covered in fine, hairy bracts. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Protea prostrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
