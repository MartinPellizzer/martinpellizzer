Primula pulchelloides

Primula pulchelloides, commonly known as the beautiful primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It blooms in early spring, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking early-season color.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
