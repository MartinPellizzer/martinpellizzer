Phoenicanthemum kingii

Phoenicanthemum kingii, commonly known as the King's phoenicanthemum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, pinkish-purple ray florets and yellow disk florets. The plant typically grows as a perennial shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Phoenicanthemum kingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
