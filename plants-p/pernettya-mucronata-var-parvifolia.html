Pernettya mucronata var. parvifolia

Pernettya mucronata var. parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf cranberry shrub, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the high altitudes of Southeast Asia.

It features dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, followed by bright red berries that are edible but tart. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with high humidity.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
