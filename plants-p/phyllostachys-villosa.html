Phyllostachys villosa

Phyllostachys villosa, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a fast-growing grass species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-green culms covered in fine, silky hairs, which give it its common name. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense stands.

Phyllostachys villosa is also valued for its use in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of bamboo shoots.
