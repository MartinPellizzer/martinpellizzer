Pandanus camarinensis

Pandanus camarinensis, commonly known as the Camarines pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Philippines.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and wetlands, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, fragrant flowers that are followed by edible fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local culture and cuisine.
