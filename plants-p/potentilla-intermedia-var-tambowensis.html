Potentilla intermedia var. tambowensis

Potentilla intermedia var. tambowensis, commonly known as the Tasmanian shrub cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to Tasmania, Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in spring. The plant has finely divided, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
