Polygonum laetevirens

Polygonum laetevirens, commonly known as the cheerful green knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features upright, branched stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, appearing in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes make it both a useful ground cover and a potential weed in certain habitats.
