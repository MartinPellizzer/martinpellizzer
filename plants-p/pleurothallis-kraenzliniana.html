Pleurothallis kraenzliniana

Pleurothallis kraenzliniana, commonly known as the Kraenzlin's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments within the rainforest canopy. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and green. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its intricate floral structure and unique pollination mechanisms.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
