Pavonia paniculata

Pavonia paniculata, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters atop tall, slender stems. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are often covered in fine, white hairs.

Pavonia paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
