Paspalum pedicellare

Paspalum pedicellare, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Paspalum pedicellare is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
