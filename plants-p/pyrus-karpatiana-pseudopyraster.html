Pyrus karpatiana pseudopyraster

Pyrus karpatiana pseudopyraster, commonly known as the Carpathian pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically pubescent on the underside. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart fruits. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in local cuisines.
