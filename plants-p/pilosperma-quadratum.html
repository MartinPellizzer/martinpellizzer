Pilosperma quadratum

Pilosperma quadratum, commonly known as the four-sided spurge, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and distinctive four-angled stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Pilosperma quadratum is adapted to hot, dry climates and has a deep root system to access moisture.
