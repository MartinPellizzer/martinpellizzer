Pseudospondias microcarpa var. longifolia

Pseudospondias microcarpa var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-fruited spondias, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into small, round fruits. The fruits are typically edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor, making it a valuable species in local cuisines.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.
