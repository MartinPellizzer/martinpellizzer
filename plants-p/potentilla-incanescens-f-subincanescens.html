Potentilla incanescens f. subincanescens

Potentilla incanescens f. subincanescens, commonly known as the silky cinquefoil, is a flowering plant characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray foliage.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of Central Asia, including parts of China and the Tibetan Plateau. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive five-lobed shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is often cultivated for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
