Psyllium aegyptiacum

Psyllium aegyptiacum, commonly known as plantain, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces a capsule containing fine, mucilaginous seeds that are widely used in traditional medicine and as a dietary supplement. These seeds are rich in soluble fiber, which can aid in digestion and promote gut health.

Psyllium aegyptiacum is also valued for its ability to absorb water, making it useful in various culinary and medicinal applications.
