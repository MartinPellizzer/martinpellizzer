Parodia neoarechavaletae

Parodia neoarechavaletae, commonly known as the Arechavaleta's cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its globular shape and ribbed structure. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically short-lived but striking. It is well adapted to arid environments, with spines that provide protection and reduce water loss.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
