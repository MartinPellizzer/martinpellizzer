Phoradendron wurdackii

Phoradendron wurdackii, commonly known as the Wurdack's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the Neotropical region.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using the sunlight it captures. The plant's small, white or pale green berries are consumed by birds, which then disperse the seeds to new host trees. It plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its forest habitat by influencing host tree growth and providing food for wildlife.

Its presence is often indicative of mature forest ecosystems.
