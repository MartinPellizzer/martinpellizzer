Prunus cerasus

Prunus cerasus, commonly known as the sweet cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It produces small, oval leaves that turn yellow in autumn, and its flowers are typically white or pink, appearing before the leaves. The plant is valued for its edible, sweet fruits, which are typically harvested in summer. Prunus cerasus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and orchards.

It is also cultivated for its ornamental value and used in the production of jams, wines, and other culinary products.
