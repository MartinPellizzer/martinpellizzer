Phoradendron paradoxum

Phoradendron paradoxum, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the Americas.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its stems, which are embedded in the host's bark. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers and red berries that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Its life cycle involves a complex relationship with its host, where it can weaken the tree over time.

Despite its parasitic nature, Phoradendron paradoxum plays a role in ecosystem dynamics by supporting various wildlife species.
