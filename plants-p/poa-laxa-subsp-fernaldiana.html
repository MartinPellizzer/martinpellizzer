Poa laxa subsp. fernaldiana

Poa laxa subsp.

fernaldiana, commonly known as the Fernald's meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes.

The plant has slender, tufted stems with narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a single, loose spike of small, two-ranked spikelets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
