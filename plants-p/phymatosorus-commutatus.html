Phymatosorus commutatus

Phymatosorus commutatus, commonly known as the crested fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, densely hairy fronds that give it a unique, almost fuzzy appearance. Native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping stems that produce new fronds along their length.

Its reproductive structures, including sori, are typically located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.
