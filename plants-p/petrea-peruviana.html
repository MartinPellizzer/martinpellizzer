Petrea peruviana

Petrea peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet, jasmine-like scent. The plant has glossy, oval-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Petrea peruviana is also valued for its use in traditional medicine.
