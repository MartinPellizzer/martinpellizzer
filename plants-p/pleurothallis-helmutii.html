Pleurothallis helmutii

Pleurothallis helmutii, commonly known as the Helmut's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its flowers are usually clustered in racemes and bloom during the rainy season.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
