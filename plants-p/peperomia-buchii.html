Peperomia buchii

Peperomia buchii, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a compact, low-maintenance plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features dense, waxy foliage with a silvery-gray hue, giving it a distinctive and elegant appearance. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white trichomes, contributing to its unique look. It produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color.

Peperomia buchii thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
