Phalaenopsis gibbosa var. wlodarczykiana

Phalaenopsis gibbosa var. wlodarczykiana, commonly known as the Wlodarczykiana phalaenopsis, is a rare and ornamental orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces large, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms once a year, with flowers lasting several weeks, making it a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts. Its leaves are broad, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

This variety is highly sought after for its aesthetic appeal and relatively ease of care under proper conditions.
