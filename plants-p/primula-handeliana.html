Primula handeliana

Primula handeliana, commonly known as the Himalayan primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Nepal and northern India.

It typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine habitats. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white, with five petals and a showy, spurred structure.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
