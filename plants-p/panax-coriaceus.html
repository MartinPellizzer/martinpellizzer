Panax coriaceus

Panax coriaceus, commonly known as the rough ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, warty, and rough-textured root system. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with compound leaves that have a serrated edge. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, clustered in umbels, and it produces red berries that contain the seeds.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with the root being used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
