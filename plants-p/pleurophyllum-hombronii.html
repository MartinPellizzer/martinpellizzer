Pleurophyllum hombronii

Pleurophyllum hombronii, commonly known as the silver hairbush, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically visited by insects. It thrives in coastal environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
