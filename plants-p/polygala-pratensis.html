Polygala pratensis

Polygala pratensis, commonly known as the meadow milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygalaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands. The plant features slender stems and small, often greenish or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are alternate and usually have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Polygala pratensis is characterized by its unique floral structure, which includes a spurred petal that resembles a bee, aiding in pollination by attracting specific insect pollinators.
