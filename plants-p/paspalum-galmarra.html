Paspalum galmarra

Paspalum galmarra, commonly known as the Galmarra grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed areas, thriving in a variety of soil types. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Paspalum galmarra is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
