Prunus mutabilis f. fallax

Prunus mutabilis f. fallax, commonly known as the fall color cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking seasonal color change, transitioning from white or pink in spring to deep red or orange in autumn. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in jams and preserves. Its ornamental value is highly appreciated in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant foliage.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its aesthetic appeal throughout the growing season.
