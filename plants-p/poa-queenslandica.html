Poa queenslandica

Poa queenslandica, commonly known as the Queensland grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green blades. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along watercourses. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny seeds.

Due to its adaptability, it plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
