Primula × portae

Primula × portae, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange. The plant typically blooms in early spring, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking early-season color. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Primula × portae thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
