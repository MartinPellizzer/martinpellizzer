Passiflora samoensis

Passiflora samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Samoa and nearby regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and contain seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in tropical climates with high humidity.

Passiflora samoensis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
