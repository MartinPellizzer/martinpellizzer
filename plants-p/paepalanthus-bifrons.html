Paepalanthus bifrons

Paepalanthus bifrons, commonly known as the two-faced cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Paepalanthus bifrons is adapted to arid environments and can store water in its thick, fleshy stems.
