Paphiopedilum godefroyae f. leucochilum

Paphiopedilum godefroyae f. leucochilum, commonly known as the white-lipped Paphiopedilum, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive white lip that contrasts with the greenish-yellow petals. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are broad and ovate, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant floral display.

This hybrid form is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and horticultural value.
