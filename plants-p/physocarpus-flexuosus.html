Physocarpus flexuosus

Physocarpus flexuosus, commonly known as smokebush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that turn vibrant red or orange in the fall. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in panicles, which give it the common name due to their feathery appearance. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Physocarpus flexuosus is also valued for its drought tolerance and resistance to pests and diseases.
