Physochlaina praealta

Physochlaina praealta, commonly known as the tall nightshade, is a striking member of the Solanaceae family, native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and large, showy flowers that range from violet to blue in color. The plant produces distinctive, bell-shaped fruits that contain toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. Its leaves are typically ovate with a hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
