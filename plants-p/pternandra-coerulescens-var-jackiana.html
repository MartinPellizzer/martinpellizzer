Pternandra coerulescens var. jackiana

Pternandra coerulescens var. jackiana, commonly known as the Jack's pternandra, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a bluish-green coloration. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of white and pale blue.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
