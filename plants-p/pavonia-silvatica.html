Pavonia silvatica

Pavonia silvatica, commonly known as the swamp rose or wild mallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves with a hairy texture and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
