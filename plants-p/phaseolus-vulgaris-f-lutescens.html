Phaseolus vulgaris f. lutescens

Phaseolus vulgaris f. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow wax bean, is a variant of the common bean species characterized by its distinct yellow pod color and wax-like coating.

It is an annual legume belonging to the Fabaceae family, typically cultivated for its edible seeds. The plant produces upright stems with trifoliate leaves and bears pods that mature to a vibrant yellow hue. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild flavor, making it suitable for both culinary and garden use.
