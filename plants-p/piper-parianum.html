Piper parianum

Piper parianum, commonly known as the Java pepper or parianum pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its vine-like growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into small, red berries.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to other members of the Piper genus.
