Pyrus oleaster

Pyrus oleaster, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits known as "oleaster" which are often used in traditional recipes. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Pyrus oleaster is often found in woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
