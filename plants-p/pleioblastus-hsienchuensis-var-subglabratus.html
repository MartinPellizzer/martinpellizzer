Pleioblastus hsienchuensis var. subglabratus

Pleioblastus hsienchuensis var. subglabratus, commonly known as the Hsienchuensis bamboo, is a clump-forming grass native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, cylindrical culms that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are smooth and slightly folded along the midrib. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

This variety is valued for its fine texture and adaptability to various garden conditions.
