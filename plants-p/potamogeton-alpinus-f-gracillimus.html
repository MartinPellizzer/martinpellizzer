Potamogeton alpinus f. gracillimus

Potamogeton alpinus f. gracillimus, commonly known as the alpine pondweed, is a aquatic plant characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, narrow leaves.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes, often in nutrient-rich substrates. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are arranged in spike-like clusters, which are usually submerged or floating. Its leaves are usually smooth and have a slightly waxy texture, aiding in water retention.

This variety is distinguished by its more delicate and graceful appearance compared to the typical form of Potamogeton alpinus.
