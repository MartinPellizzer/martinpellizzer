Paspalum haumanii var. pilosum

Paspalum haumanii var. pilosum, commonly known as silky grass or silky paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, soft, and silky texture, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
