Parsonsia goniostemon

Parsonsia goniostemon, commonly known as the red-flowered moonflower, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, night-blooming flowers that are typically deep red or orange in color. The plant belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is known for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The fruit is a follicle that splits open to release the seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.
