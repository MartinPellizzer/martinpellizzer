Poa incurvata

Poa incurvata, commonly known as bentgrass, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its low-growing, tufted habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that often curve inward. The plant produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences, typically with two to three florets per spikelet. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges.

Poa incurvata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in turfgrass mixes.
