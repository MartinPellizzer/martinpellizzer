Pepo eximius

Pepo eximius, commonly known as the splendid pumpkin, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, thick, and often brightly colored fruit, which is typically spherical or slightly elongated. The plant produces large, lobed leaves with a rough texture and is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is used in various culinary and medicinal applications due to its high nutritional value and unique flavor profile.
