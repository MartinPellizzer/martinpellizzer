Polanisia icosandra f. deglabrata

Polanisia icosandra f. deglabrata, commonly known as the glabrous polanisia, is a variant of the polanisia plant characterized by its reduced or absent pubescence.

It is a shrub native to specific regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features simple, elliptical leaves with a smooth surface and a slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and typically have a pale yellow to greenish hue.

This form is notable for its horticultural interest due to its unique appearance and adaptability to cultivated settings.
