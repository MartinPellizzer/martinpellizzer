Potentilla tommasiniana f. longifolia

Potentilla tommasiniana f. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf potentilla, is a compact shrub characterized by its dense, wiry stems and small, oval leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are composed of numerous tiny petals and are attractive to pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders. The foliage remains evergreen, providing year-round interest in the garden.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to tolerate coastal conditions, making it a resilient addition to coastal landscapes.
