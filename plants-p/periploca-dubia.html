Periploca dubia

Periploca dubia, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

The bark is smooth and may exude a milky sap when cut.
