Piper silvarum

Piper silvarum, commonly known as the silver pepper or mountain pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent.

The fruit, which is a small, dark-colored berry, contains the seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
