Pelargonium cochleatum

Pelargonium cochleatum, commonly known as the spiral geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral-shaped leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Pelargonium cochleatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique foliage.
