Persea harrisii

Persea harrisii, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically orange to reddish in color when mature. The fruit has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
