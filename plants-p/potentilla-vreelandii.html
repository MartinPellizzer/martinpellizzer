Potentilla vreelandii

Potentilla vreelandii, commonly known as Vreeland's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its hardiness and low maintenance requirements in arid environments.
