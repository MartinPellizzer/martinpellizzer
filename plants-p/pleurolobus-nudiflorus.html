Pleurolobus nudiflorus

Pleurolobus nudiflorus, commonly known as the naked-flower pleurolobus, is a flowering plant native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous mesh, a feature common among leguminous species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
