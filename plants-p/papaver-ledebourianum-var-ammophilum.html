Papaver ledebourianum var. ammophilum

Papaver ledebourianum var. ammophilum, commonly known as the Ammophila poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking red to orange-red petals and a distinctive, conical corolla. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or dune environments. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
