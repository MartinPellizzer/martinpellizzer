Potentilla steneloba

Potentilla steneloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinquefoil, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia, where it thrives in cold, rocky environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a five-leaf cluster. It is often found in tundra and mountain meadow habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.

Potentilla steneloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
