Patrinia ceratophylla

Patrinia ceratophylla, commonly known as the hairy false indigo, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Patrinia ceratophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
