Primula chungensis

Primula chungensis, commonly known as the Chinese primrose, is a species of flowering plant in the family Primulaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. It has elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and covered with a fine, soft hair.

Primula chungensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
