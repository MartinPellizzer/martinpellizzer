Pleurothallis villosa

Pleurothallis villosa, commonly known as the hairy pleurothallis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. It features slender, wiry stems and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant's hairy texture on its stems and leaves is a distinguishing characteristic.

It is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
