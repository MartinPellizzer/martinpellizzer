Panicum echinatum

Panicum echinatum, commonly known as the spiny panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are often tinged with red or purple hues. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental and erosion control purposes.
