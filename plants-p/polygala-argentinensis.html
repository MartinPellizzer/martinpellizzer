Polygala argentinensis

Polygala argentinensis, commonly known as the Argentine milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces small, indehiscent fruits.

It is of interest in botany due to its unique morphology and adaptation to arid environments.
