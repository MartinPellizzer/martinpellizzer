Poa menachensis

Poa menachensis, commonly known as the Menachan grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Poa menachensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in its native ecosystems.
