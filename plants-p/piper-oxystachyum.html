Piper oxystachyum

Piper oxystachyum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is cultivated for its aromatic fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its botanical features include a climbing habit, opposite leaves, and a distinctive, pungent aroma.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitats.
