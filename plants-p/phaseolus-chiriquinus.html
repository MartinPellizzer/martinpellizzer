Phaseolus chiriquinus

Phaseolus chiriquinus, commonly known as the Andean bean or chilca, is a species of legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing several round, edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry regions with well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important crop in traditional Andean agriculture.
