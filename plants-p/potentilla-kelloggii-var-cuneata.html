Potentilla kelloggii var. cuneata

Potentilla kelloggii var. cuneata, commonly known as Kellogg's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, spreading stems with serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and drought-tolerant addition to native plant landscapes.
