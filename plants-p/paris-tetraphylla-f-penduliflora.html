Paris tetraphylla f. penduliflora

Paris tetraphylla f. penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging-flowered Paris, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Melanthiaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and lanceolate, dark green leaves that are often hairy beneath. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with six petals and prominent stamens.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
