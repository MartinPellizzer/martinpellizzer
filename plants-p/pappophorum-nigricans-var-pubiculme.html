Pappophorum nigricans var. pubiculme

Pappophorum nigricans var. pubiculme, commonly known as the hairy leaved grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
