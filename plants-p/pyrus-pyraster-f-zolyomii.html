Pyrus pyraster f. zolyomii

Pyrus pyraster f. zolyomii, commonly known as the Zolyomii pear, is a botanical variant of the common pear.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often irregularly shaped fruits that are typically smaller than those of the typical species. The plant is a deciduous tree with a broad, spreading crown and dark green, glossy leaves. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by the characteristic pear-shaped fruits.

This form is native to parts of Europe and is valued for its ornamental qualities and edible fruit.
