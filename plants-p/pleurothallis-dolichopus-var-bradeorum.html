Pleurothallis dolichopus var. bradeorum

Pleurothallis dolichopus var. bradeorum, commonly known as the Brade's long-stemmed orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and clustered, small flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a subtle fragrance, and it blooms during the wet season.

This variety is appreciated for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
