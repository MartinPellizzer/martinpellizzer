Pachygenium sheviakii

Pachygenium sheviakii, commonly known as the giant burr plant, is a unique species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, spiny, and woody fruiting structures that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unusual morphology and ecological adaptations.
