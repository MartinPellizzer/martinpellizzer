Pisonia cephalotomandra

Pisonia cephalotomandra, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for native birds. It thrives in coastal and subtropical regions, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture, contributing to its hardy nature.
