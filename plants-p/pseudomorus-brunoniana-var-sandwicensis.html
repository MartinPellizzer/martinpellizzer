Pseudomorus brunoniana var. sandwicensis

Pseudomorus brunoniana var. sandwicensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian pohuehue, is a rare endemic plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in coastal forests. It plays an important role in the local ecosystem and is considered a vulnerable species due to habitat loss.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect this unique botanical species.
