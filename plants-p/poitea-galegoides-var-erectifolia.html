Poitea galegoides var. erectifolia

Poitea galegoides var. erectifolia, commonly known as the erect-leaved poitea, is a shrub native to specific regions of South America.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem with a rough, bark-like surface.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
