Primula carnosula

Primula carnosula, commonly known as the wooly primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique texture, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
