Panicum patulum

Panicum patulum, commonly known as the broadleaf panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and wide, flat leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its adaptability, Panicum patulum is sometimes used in restoration projects to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
