Pygeum tetradenium

Pygeum tetradenium, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and dense, rounded crown. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing oily seeds. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, often with a slightly serrated margin.

The tree is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
