Phleum

Phleum, commonly known as timothy grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Timothy grass is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

It is widely used as forage for livestock and has ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
