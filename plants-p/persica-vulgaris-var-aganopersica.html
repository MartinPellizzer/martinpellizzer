Persica vulgaris var. aganopersica

Persica vulgaris var. aganopersica, commonly known as the Persian apricot.

This variant is characterized by its vibrant orange to red fruits, which are typically smaller than standard apricots. The plant produces fragrant white flowers in early spring, preceding the fruiting period. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, aromatic flavor and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
