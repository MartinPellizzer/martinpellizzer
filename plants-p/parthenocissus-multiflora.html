Parthenocissus multiflora

Parthenocissus multiflora, commonly known as the Boston ivy, is a vigorous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant red in the autumn. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. Its tendrils allow it to cling to walls and structures, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The foliage provides dense coverage, offering both aesthetic value and some degree of erosion control.
