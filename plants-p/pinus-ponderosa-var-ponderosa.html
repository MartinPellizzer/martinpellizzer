Pinus ponderosa var. ponderosa

Pinus ponderosa var. ponderosa, commonly known as the ponderosa pine, is a tall evergreen conifer native to western North America.

It is characterized by its long, needle-like leaves and distinctive, flared bark that peels in thick, plate-like scales. The tree produces cones that hang downward, containing winged seeds dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to a variety of elevations and climates.

The ponderosa pine plays a vital role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
