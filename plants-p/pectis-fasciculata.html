Pectis fasciculata

Pectis fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered pectis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its yellow flowers are arranged in dense clusters, giving it its common name.

Pectis fasciculata is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
