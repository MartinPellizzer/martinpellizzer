Polygonum gracilius

Polygonum gracilius, commonly known as slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. Its stems are often reddish or purple, and the leaves are narrow and grass-like in appearance.

It is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
