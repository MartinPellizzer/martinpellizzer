Potentilla glandulosa var. nevadensis

Potentilla glandulosa var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra gloverweed, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, glandular leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in mountainous regions. Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a desirable plant for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
