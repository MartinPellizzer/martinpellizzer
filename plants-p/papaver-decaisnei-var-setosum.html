Papaver decaisnei var. setosum

Papaver decaisnei var. setosum, commonly known as the Setose Poppy, is a rare and striking botanical species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically surrounded by a series of leaf-like bracts, adding to its ornamental value.

This variant is noted for its unique morphology and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
