Pterocarpus santalinus

Pterocarpus santalinus, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark red to purplish heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous leaflets. Its fruit is a legume that contains flattened, winged seeds.

The tree is also noted for its role in traditional medicine and its ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
