Paspalum holochrysum

Paspalum holochrysum, commonly known as the golden paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces panicles of golden-yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically hairy along the edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Paspalum holochrysum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
