Pimenta odiolens

Pimenta odiolens, commonly known as the stinking toe, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are edible but have a strong, pungent flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its unique scent and appearance, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
