Pelargonium × virgatum

Pelargonium × virgatum, commonly known as the hybrid geranium, is a flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant typically has rounded, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun to partial shade and is commonly cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Pelargonium × virgatum is native to South Africa and is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
