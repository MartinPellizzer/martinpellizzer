Photinia beauverdiana

Photinia beauverdiana, commonly known as the red leaf photinia, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn bright red in the autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant foliage and seasonal color changes.
