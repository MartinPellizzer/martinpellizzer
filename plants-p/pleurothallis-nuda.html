Pleurothallis nuda

Pleurothallis nuda, commonly known as the naked pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in small inflorescences. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it conserve moisture.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
