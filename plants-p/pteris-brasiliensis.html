Pteris brasiliensis

Pteris brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian water fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded areas and is frequently found in wetlands, ponds, and along stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and help in anchoring the plant while absorbing nutrients from the water.

Pteris brasiliensis plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water filtration.
