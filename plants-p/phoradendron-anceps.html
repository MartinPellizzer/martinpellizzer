Phoradendron anceps

Phoradendron anceps, commonly known as the mistletoe of the southern United States, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees such as oaks and hickories.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and minerals, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light that penetrates through the host's foliage. The plant produces small, white or yellowish berries that are dispersed by birds, facilitating its spread to new hosts. Its growth is often slow, and it can remain on a host tree for many years.

Phoradendron anceps plays a role in forest ecosystems by providing food for various bird species and contributing to the ecological dynamics of its host.
