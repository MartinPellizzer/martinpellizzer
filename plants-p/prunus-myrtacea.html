Prunus myrtacea

Prunus myrtacea, commonly known as the myrtle tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to southern Europe, North Africa, and parts of western Asia. The plant is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
