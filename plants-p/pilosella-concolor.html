Pilosella concolor

Pilosella concolor, commonly known as the common pilosella, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant features small, white to pale yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its leaves are usually hairy and alternate along the stem.

Pilosella concolor is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
