Pleiocraterium plantaginifolium

Pleiocraterium plantaginifolium, commonly known as the rock plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in late spring or early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
