Pombalia jefensis

Pombalia jefensis, commonly known as the Jefenian ghost fern, is a rare and elusive plant native to the dense, humid rainforests of the Jefenian Highlands.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds that resemble ghostly apparitions in the dim light of the forest understory. The plant's unique morphology includes a short, creeping rhizome with clustered sporangia that produce spores in late spring. Pombalia jefensis thrives in nutrient-rich, well-drained soil and requires high levels of atmospheric moisture to flourish.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern and is often studied for its ecological and botanical significance.
