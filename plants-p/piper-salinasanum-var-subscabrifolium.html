Piper salinasanum var. subscabrifolium

Piper salinasanum var. subscabrifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper salinasanum species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically narrower than those of the standard variety. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by small, peppery berries. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in botanical studies and horticultural research.

The subspecies is often cultivated for its unique foliage and potential medicinal properties.
