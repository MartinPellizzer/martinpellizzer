Phlomis atropurpurea

Phlomis atropurpurea, commonly known as purple mullein, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features tall, erect stems covered in soft, velvety leaves that are gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, clustered spikes of purple to reddish-pink flowers in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its common name reflects the appearance of its fuzzy foliage and its historical use in traditional medicine.
