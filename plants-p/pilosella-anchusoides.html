Pilosella anchusoides

Pilosella anchusoides, commonly known as the woolly groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features dense, white to grayish-white hairs covering its stems and leaves, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats.

Pilosella anchusoides produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.
