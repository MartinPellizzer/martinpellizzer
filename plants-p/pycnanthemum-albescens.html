Pycnanthemum albescens

Pycnanthemum albescens, commonly known as white honeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States.

It features aromatic, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Pycnanthemum albescens is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
