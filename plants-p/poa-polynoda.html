Poa polynoda

Poa polynoda, commonly known as the narrowleaf meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, moist habitats such as meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Poa polynoda is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
