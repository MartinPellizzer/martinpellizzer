Polygonum muhlenbergii var. terrestre

Polygonum muhlenbergii var. terrestre, commonly known as the creeping smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features prostrate or decumbent stems that grow close to the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

This species is often found in moist soils along stream banks, wet meadows, and other riparian habitats.
