Polygala lehmanniana

Polygala lehmanniana, commonly known as the Lehmann's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It is often used in restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
