Plectranthus coppinii

Plectranthus coppinii, commonly known as the Cape mint, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
