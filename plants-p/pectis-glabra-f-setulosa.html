Pectis glabra f. setulosa

Pectis glabra f. setulosa, commonly known as the hairy pectis, is a variant of the pectis plant characterized by its hairy foliage.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually linear and covered with fine, pubescent hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
