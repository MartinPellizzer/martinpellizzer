Petrosavia sinii

Petrosavia sinii, commonly known as the Sinai rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the deserts of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a low-growing, succulent shrub that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in spring. Its thick, fleshy leaves help it store water, making it well-adapted to drought conditions.

Petrosavia sinii is often found growing on rocky slopes and in sandy soils, where it plays a role in stabilizing the landscape.
