Phleum crinitum

Phleum crinitum, commonly known as goat's beard, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, silky, and fluffy appearance, with long, narrow leaves and clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique visual appeal.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in cool, temperate climates.
