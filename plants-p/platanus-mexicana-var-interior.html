Platanus mexicana var. interior

Platanus mexicana var. interior, commonly known as the Mexican sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, spherical fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and flaky, often exfoliating in patches.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in riparian zones and along riverbanks.
