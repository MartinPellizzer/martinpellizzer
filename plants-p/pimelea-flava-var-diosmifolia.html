Pimelea flava var. diosmifolia

Pimelea flava var. diosmifolia, commonly known as the yellow bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, bottlebrush-like inflorescences that appear in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
