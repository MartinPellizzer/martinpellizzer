Ptychotis verticillata

Ptychotis verticillata, commonly known as the spiral leaf cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of fleshy, ribbed stems that grow in a helical pattern. The plant typically produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months. It is a succulent, capable of storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Ptychotis verticillata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
