Paphiopedilum crossii var. sublaeve

Paphiopedilum crossii var. sublaeve, commonly known as the Siam orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short, stocky rhizome and a single, broad, elliptical leaf. The plant produces a single, pendulous flower with a distinctive, spurred lip and a pale yellow to greenish-white coloration. Its flowers are typically fragrant and bloom in late winter to early spring.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
