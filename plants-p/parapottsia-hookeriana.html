Parapottsia hookeriana

Parapottsia hookeriana, commonly known as Hooker's moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Pottiaceae.

It is a small, mat-forming plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a thalloid structure with a prostrate stem covered in short, leaf-like structures called phyllids. It is characterized by its intricate and delicate leaf arrangement, which contributes to its ornamental appeal.

This species is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and moisture retention.
