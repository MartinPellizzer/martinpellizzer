Polystachya busseana

Polystachya busseana, commonly known as the Busse's bamboo orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Polystachya busseana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
