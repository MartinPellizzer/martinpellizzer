Phyllanthus chevalieri

Phyllanthus chevalieri, commonly known as the African buttonwillow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and smooth, often waxy leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized flower clusters typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Phyllanthus chevalieri is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
