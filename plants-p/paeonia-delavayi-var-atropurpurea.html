Paeonia delavayi var. atropurpurea

Paeonia delavayi var. atropurpurea, commonly known as the Chinese peony or the purple-flowered peony, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep purple to crimson, often with a distinctive red or purple hue. The plant has palmately compound leaves that are green and slightly hairy, and its stems are woody and covered in a thick, reddish-brown bark. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing fragrant flowers that are highly valued in traditional Chinese horticulture.

This variety is known for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
