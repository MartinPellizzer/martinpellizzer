Paepalanthus gustavii

Paepalanthus gustavii, commonly known as the Gustavii's cactus, is a species of flowering plant in the Cactaceae family.

It is native to the Andean region of South America, specifically Ecuador and Peru. This plant is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny, ribbed stems. It produces small, yellowish flowers during the dry season, which are often clustered at the top of the plant.

Paepalanthus gustavii is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.
