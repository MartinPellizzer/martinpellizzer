Poitea multiflora

Poitea multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered poitea, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Poitea multiflora is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
