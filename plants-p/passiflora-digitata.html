Passiflora digitata

Passiflora digitata, commonly known as the silky nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically blue or purple with intricate patterns. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces edible berries that are often used in beverages and culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The genus Passiflora includes many species, but P. digitata is particularly noted for its unique floral structure and historical use in traditional medicine.
