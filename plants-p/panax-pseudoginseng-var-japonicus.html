Panax pseudoginseng var. japonicus

Panax pseudoginseng var. japonicus, commonly known as Japanese ginseng, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, feather-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces red berries that contain the seeds, and it thrives in forested areas with partial shade. Its root system is similar to true ginseng, and it is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.

The plant is often cultivated for its potential health benefits, including immune support and stress reduction.
