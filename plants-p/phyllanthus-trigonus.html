Phyllanthus trigonus

Phyllanthus trigonus, commonly known as the three-angled spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically three-angled in cross-section, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

The plant is part of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its milky sap and diverse ecological roles.
