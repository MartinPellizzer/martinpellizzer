Panicastrella muricata

Panicastrella muricata, commonly known as the spiny panic grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like structure with sharp, bristly spikes along its stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Its unique morphology makes it a distinctive species within its ecological community.
