Papaver radicatum subsp. ovatilobum

Papaver radicatum subsp.

ovatilobum, commonly known as the broad-lobed poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its basal leaves that are broadly ovate with serrated margins and its striking red or orange flowers with prominent, saucer-shaped petals.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils and is adapted to full sun exposure. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
