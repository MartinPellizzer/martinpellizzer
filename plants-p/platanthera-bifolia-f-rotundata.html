Platanthera bifolia f. rotundata

Platanthera bifolia f. rotundata, commonly known as the round-leaf lady's tresses, is a rare and striking orchid species characterized by its unique floral structure.

It features a dense, spike-like inflorescence with small, white to pale green flowers that resemble a tresses of hair. The plant is notable for its specialized mycorrhizal relationships, which are essential for its nutrient uptake. Its leaves are ovate and rounded, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This orchid typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and bogs, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions.
