Potentilla simplex var. typica

Potentilla simplex var. typica, commonly known as the silverleaf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has silver-gray foliage due to a dense covering of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact size and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
