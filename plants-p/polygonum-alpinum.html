Polygonum alpinum

Polygonum alpinum, commonly known as alpine knotweed, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or reddish, appearing in late summer.

Polygonum alpinum is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand cold temperatures and high altitudes.
