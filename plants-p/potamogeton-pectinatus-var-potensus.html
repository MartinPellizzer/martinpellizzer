Potamogeton pectinatus var. potensus

Potamogeton pectinatus var. potensus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats across Europe and Asia.

It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 mm wide, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, and it is often found in shallow, slow-moving waters. Its rhizomes form dense mats that can stabilize sediments and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

The species is valued for its role in aquatic ecosystems but can become invasive in certain environments.
