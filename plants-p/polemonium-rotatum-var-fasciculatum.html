Polemonium rotatum var. fasciculatum

Polemonium rotatum var. fasciculatum, commonly known as the clustered bluebead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and vibrant floral display.
