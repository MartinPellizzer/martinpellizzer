Pancratium triphyllum

Pancratium triphyllum, commonly known as the Trinidad lily, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing near coastal areas.

Pancratium triphyllum is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
