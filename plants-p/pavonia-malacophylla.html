Pavonia malacophylla

Pavonia malacophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf pavonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that are dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soils.

Pavonia malacophylla is often found in woodland edges and open areas, making it a common sight in native plant gardens.
