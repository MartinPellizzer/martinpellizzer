Passiflora angulata

Passiflora angulata, commonly known as the angular passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive angular leaves and showy, complex flowers that feature a crown of filaments and a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Passiflora angulata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
