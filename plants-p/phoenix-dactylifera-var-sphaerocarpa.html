Phoenix dactylifera var. sphaerocarpa

Phoenix dactylifera var. sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the date palm fruit, is a variety of the date palm native to the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its large, sweet, and fleshy fruits that are typically harvested when they are fully ripe and dark in color. The plant is a tall, perennial tree with a robust trunk and broad, fan-shaped leaves. The fruit develops in clusters and is rich in sugars, making it a valuable crop for both food and economic purposes.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments and highly resilient to drought conditions.
