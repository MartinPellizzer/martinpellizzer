Pouteria psammophila

Pouteria psammophila, commonly known as the sand dune pouteria, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its woody structure and is often found growing in sandy or coastal environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough or hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
