Pleopeltis fructuosa

Pleopeltis fructuosa, commonly known as the fruit-bearing pleopeltis, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, clustered spores on the underside of its leaves, which are essential for its reproductive cycle. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
