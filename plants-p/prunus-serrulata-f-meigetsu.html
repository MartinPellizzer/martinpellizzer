Prunus serrulata f. meigetsu

Prunus serrulata f. meigetsu, commonly known as the Yoshino cherry, is a cultivar of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a striking display. The tree has slender, upright branches and lance-shaped leaves that turn a vibrant yellow in autumn. It is native to Japan and is widely cultivated in temperate regions for its ornamental value.

This variety is particularly noted for its role in cherry blossom festivals and its contribution to the aesthetic of traditional Japanese gardens.
