Piper milciadesii

Piper milciadesii, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its vine-like growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by berries, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and pharmacological study.
