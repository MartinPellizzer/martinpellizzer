Phylica purpurea var. reclinata

Phylica purpurea var. reclinata, commonly known as the purple karoo bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are adapted to arid conditions, making it drought-tolerant. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
