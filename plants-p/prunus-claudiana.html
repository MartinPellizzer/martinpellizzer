Prunus claudiana

Prunus claudiana, commonly known as the Cuban cherry or wild cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to cherries but are not typically consumed by humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and limestone soils.

Prunus claudiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional medicine.
