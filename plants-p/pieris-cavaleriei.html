Pieris cavaleriei

Pieris cavaleriei, commonly known as the Chinese pieris, is a species of evergreen shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its leathery, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark blue, waxy berries. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its foliage and delicate floral display.
