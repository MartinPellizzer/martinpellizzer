Prunus mutabilis f. hirtella

Prunus mutabilis f. hirtella, commonly known as the fuzzy cherry, is a botanical variant of the Prunus mutabilis species.

This plant is characterized by its hairy or pubescent leaves, which give it its common name. It is native to parts of East Asia, including Japan and China. The fuzzy cherry produces small, pink to white flowers in spring, followed by edible berries.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in suitable climates.
