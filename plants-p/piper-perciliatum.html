Piper perciliatum

Piper perciliatum, commonly known as the Peruvian pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly serrated edge and have a distinct aromatic scent.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
