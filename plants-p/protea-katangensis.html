Protea katangensis

Protea katangensis, commonly known as the Katanga protea, is a striking plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often showy floral structures. The plant features a tall, branched stem with dense clusters of pale pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are adapted to withstand coastal conditions.

Protea katangensis plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing nectar for local pollinators and contributing to the region's botanical diversity.
