Potamogeton lucens var. floridanus

Potamogeton lucens var. floridanus, commonly known as the Florida pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has long, slender stems that are often branched and bear small, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, dark brown fruits.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
