Pinanga lepida

Pinanga lepida, commonly known as the slender palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, unbranched stem and pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers followed by dark blue, spherical fruits.

Pinanga lepida is valued for its ornamental appeal and cultural significance in its native regions.
