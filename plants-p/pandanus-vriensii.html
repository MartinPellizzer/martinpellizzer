Pandanus vriensii

Pandanus vriensii, commonly known as the Vriesii pandanus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that form a rosette at the top. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. These flowers are followed by spiny, triangular fruits that contain seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in coastal and wetland environments.
