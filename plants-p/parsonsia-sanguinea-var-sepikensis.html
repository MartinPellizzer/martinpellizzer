Parsonsia sanguinea var. sepikensis

Parsonsia sanguinea var. sepikensis, commonly known as the Sepik parsonia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Papua New Guinea.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers with yellow stamens. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate and dark green, often with a slight pubescence.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are adapted for pollination by birds, particularly honeyeaters.
