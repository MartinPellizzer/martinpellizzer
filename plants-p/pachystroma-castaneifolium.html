Pachystroma castaneifolium

Pachystroma castaneifolium, commonly known as the chestnut-leafed pachystroma, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and alternate, ovate leaves that resemble chestnut leaves, hence its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

This species is adapted to cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
