Premna syringifolia

Premna syringifolia, commonly known as the Chinese nettle or Indian nettle, is a flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae.

It is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia, including China, India, and parts of the Pacific islands. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
