Polygonum fasciculatum

Polygonum fasciculatum, commonly known as redroot filaree, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and produces small, pink to red flowers. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Polygonum fasciculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
