Pisum sativum var. hortense

Pisum sativum var. hortense, commonly known as the garden pea, is a cultivated variety of the pea plant.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often grown for ornamental purposes due to its attractive flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of pea pods that contain several seeds, which are edible when mature. Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and a flowering structure that develops into the familiar pod.

This variety is well-suited for temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil.
