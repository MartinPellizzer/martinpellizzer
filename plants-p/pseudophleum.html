Pseudophleum

Pseudophleum, commonly known as false barley, is a genus of grasses belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often loosely arranged, giving it a distinctive appearance. Pseudophleum species are typically found in moist or wet habitats, such as marshes and stream banks.

These plants play a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
