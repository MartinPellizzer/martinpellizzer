Pennisetum americanum var. vulpinum

Pennisetum americanum var. vulpinum, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a perennial grass that thrives in full sun and well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery, purple-tinted plumes that appear in late summer. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
