Phlomis forrestii

Phlomis forrestii, commonly known as the Tibetan torchgrass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Tibet and western China.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features tall, upright stems covered in soft, woolly hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. It produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
