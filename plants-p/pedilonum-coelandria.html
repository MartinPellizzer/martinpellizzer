Pedilonum coelandria

Pedilonum coelandria, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are opposite and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological significance.
