Potentilla vittata

Potentilla vittata, commonly known as the yellow cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and it is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Potentilla vittata is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to two feet, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
