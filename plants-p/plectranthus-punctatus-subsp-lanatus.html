Plectranthus punctatus subsp. lanatus

Plectranthus punctatus subsp.

lanatus, commonly known as the woolly plectranthus, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa. It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple to white in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
