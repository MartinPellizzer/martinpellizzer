Polygala petrophila

Polygala petrophila, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family, characterized by its distinctive, often spiny stems and small, delicate flowers. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with a unique structure that resembles a small, inflated pod.

Polygala petrophila is adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
