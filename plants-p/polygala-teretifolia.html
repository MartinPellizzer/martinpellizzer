Polygala teretifolia

Polygala teretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a distinctive, wiry stem structure.

Polygala teretifolia is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
