Phyteuma scaposum

Phyteuma scaposum, commonly known as the slender phyteuma, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

Phyteuma scaposum is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and ability to attract pollinators.
