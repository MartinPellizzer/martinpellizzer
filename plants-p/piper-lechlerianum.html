Piper lechlerianum

Piper lechlerianum, commonly known as the Lechler's pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its dark green, elongated leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries. Its fruits are typically harvested when they turn red, and the plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, it is not widely used in culinary applications compared to other Piper species.
