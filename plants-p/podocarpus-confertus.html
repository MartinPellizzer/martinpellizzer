Podocarpus confertus

Podocarpus confertus, commonly known as the Chinese podocarp or Indian pine, is a species of coniferous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and conical shape. The tree produces small, clustered cones that contain winged seeds, typical of many conifer species. Its leaves are usually dark green and leathery, providing a dense canopy.

Podocarpus confertus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
