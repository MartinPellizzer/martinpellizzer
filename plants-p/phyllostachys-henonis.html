Phyllostachys henonis

Phyllostachys henonis, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, green culms that often exhibit a golden hue, especially in the sun-exposed areas. The plant grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a striking ornamental bamboo. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth, waxy surface, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

Phyllostachys henonis is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its visual interest and adaptability to various soil conditions.
