Pandanus saxatilis

Pandanus saxatilis, commonly known as the rock pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its stiff, fibrous leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and on rocky outcrops, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its roots help stabilize soil and prevent erosion in coastal regions.

The plant's unique morphology makes it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
