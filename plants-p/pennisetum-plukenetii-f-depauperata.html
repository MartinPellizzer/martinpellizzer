Pennisetum plukenetii f. depauperata

Pennisetum plukenetii f. depauperata, commonly known as the silver fountain grass, is a compact, ornamental grass with narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

It is a sterile form of Pennisetum plukenetii, meaning it does not produce flowers or seeds, making it ideal for garden use without the risk of spreading. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet and has a fountain-like growth habit, creating a soft, textured look in landscapes. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its unique color and form make it a popular choice for adding visual interest to gardens.
