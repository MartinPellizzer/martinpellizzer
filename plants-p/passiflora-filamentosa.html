Passiflora filamentosa

Passiflora filamentosa, commonly known as the thread-leaved passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces orange or red berries that are edible and contain numerous small, hard seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped with finely serrated edges, and it thrives in warm, humid environments.

Passiflora filamentosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
