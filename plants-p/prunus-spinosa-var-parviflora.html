Prunus spinosa var. parviflora

Prunus spinosa var. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or parviflora cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically reddish-brown on the underside. The plant produces delicate, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a small, tart cherry that ripens in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in hedgerows or as a specimen plant.
