Primula reinii var. ovalifolia

Primula reinii var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaved primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically oval or ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This species thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to bloom in early spring.
