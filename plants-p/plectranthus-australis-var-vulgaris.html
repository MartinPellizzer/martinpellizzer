Plectranthus australis var. vulgaris

Plectranthus australis var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common purple heart, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically purple or violet in color, with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, usually in shades of purple or white, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it suitable for borders, containers, or as a ground cover.
