Picea nigra var. doumetii

Picea nigra var. doumetii, commonly known as the Black Spruce variety Doumetii, is a subspecies of the black spruce native to specific regions of North America.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its dark green, needle-like leaves and cone-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows in boreal forests and moist, acidic soils, thriving in cooler climates. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against environmental stressors.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining forest ecosystems.
