Prunus serrulata f. nigrescens

Prunus serrulata f. nigrescens, commonly known as the black-flowered cherry, is a rare and striking variant of the Japanese cherry blossom.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black petals, which contrast vividly with its white or pale pink buds. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. It blooms in early spring, producing clusters of fragrant flowers that attract pollinators.

Its unique coloration makes it a prized ornamental plant in botanical gardens and landscape design.
