Piper hispidinervum

Piper hispidinervum, commonly known as the rough-veined pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its compound leaves with stiff, hairy veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, and it is cultivated for its edible fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Its botanical features include a climbing habit, opposite leaf arrangement, and a distinct, pungent aroma.
