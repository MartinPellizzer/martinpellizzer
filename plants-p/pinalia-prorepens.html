Pinalia prorepens

Pinalia prorepens, commonly known as the creeping pincushion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal and inland regions, where it forms low, mat-like growths.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
