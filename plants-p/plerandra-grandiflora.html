Plerandra grandiflora

Plerandra grandiflora, commonly known as the giant arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, spatheous flowers that resemble a single, showy bloom. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, known for its unique inflorescence structure consisting of a spadix and spathe. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Plerandra grandiflora is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
