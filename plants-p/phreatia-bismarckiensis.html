Phreatia bismarckiensis

Phreatia bismarckiensis, commonly known as the Bismarck Island orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to the Bismarck Archipelago in Papua New Guinea.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree trunks or branches in the rainforest canopy. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinct lip. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and decaying organic matter.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
