Prunus carduchorum var. carduchorum

Prunus carduchorum var. carduchorum, commonly known as the Armenian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible drupes that are typically red or yellow when mature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
