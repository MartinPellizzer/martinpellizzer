Phragmites maximus var. berlandieri

Phragmites maximus var. berlandieri, commonly known as the western common reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its dense, sword-shaped leaves and its ability to form extensive stands in marshes, swamps, and along shorelines. The plant produces feathery plumes that are adapted for wind pollination, and its rhizomes spread aggressively, contributing to its ecological dominance. It plays a significant role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

However, its invasive nature can also lead to the displacement of native vegetation in certain regions.
