Potamogeton × lithuanicus

Potamogeton × lithuanicus, commonly known as the European pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically submerged in freshwater environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are arranged in spike-like clusters, often emerging from the water surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters, such as ponds and lakes, and is often found in nutrient-rich habitats.

Potamogeton × lithuanicus plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
