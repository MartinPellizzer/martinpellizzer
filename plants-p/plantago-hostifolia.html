Plantago hostifolia

Plantago hostifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in a spike-like inflorescence during the summer months. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in lawns, roadsides, and fields.

The leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
