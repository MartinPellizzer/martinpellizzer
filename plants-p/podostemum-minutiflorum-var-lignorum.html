Podostemum minutiflorum var. lignorum

Podostemum minutiflorum var. lignorum, commonly known as the dwarf flowered podostem, is a rare aquatic or semi-aquatic plant found in freshwater habitats.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers and unique adaptations to submerged or floating environments. The plant typically grows along the edges of slow-moving streams and ponds, where it can anchor itself to rocks or submerged vegetation. Its leaves are often reduced to small, ribbon-like structures, facilitating water flow around the plant.

This variety exhibits specialized morphological features that enable it to thrive in its niche, making it an interesting subject for botanical study.
