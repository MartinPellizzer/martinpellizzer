Phacelia ciliata var. ciliata

Phacelia ciliata var. ciliata, commonly known as the fringed phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.
