Plectranthus thiothyrsus

Plectranthus thiothyrsus, commonly known as the Cape mint, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Plectranthus thiothyrsus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in traditional medicine.
