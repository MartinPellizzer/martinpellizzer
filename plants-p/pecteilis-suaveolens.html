Pecteilis suaveolens

Pecteilis suaveolens, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant produces small, aromatic seeds that are often used in herbal remedies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft, velvety texture.

Pecteilis suaveolens is known for its long-lasting blooms and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
