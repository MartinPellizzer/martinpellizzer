Piper lanceifolium var. bullosum

Piper lanceifolium var. bullosum, commonly known as the Bullosum Pepper Vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bulbous coating, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by peppery, spherical fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and horticultural displays.
