Prangos kurdica

Prangos kurdica, commonly known as the Kurdish thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring gray-green, hairy leaves and purple-blue flowers that bloom in summer. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in rocky and arid environments.

Prangos kurdica is often found in alpine meadows and steppe habitats, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
