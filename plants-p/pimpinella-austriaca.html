Pimpinella austriaca

Pimpinella austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian cow-wheat, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a taproot system and produces small, dry fruits called schizocarps.

Pimpinella austriaca is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
