Pleurothallis pennelliana

Pleurothallis pennelliana, commonly known as the Pennell's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees, drawing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrate to prevent root rot.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
