Prunus chamaecerasus var. pygmaea

Prunus chamaecerasus var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or Japanese dwarf cherry, is a compact shrub native to Japan.

It features small, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, followed by red or black berries in late summer. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.

This variety is well-suited for rock gardens or as a border plant in gardens with well-drained soil.
