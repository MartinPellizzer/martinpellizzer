Polygonum lapathifolium subsp. semilatum

Polygonum lapathifolium subsp.

semilatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and hollow stems.

The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows, marshes, and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a prominent midrib, while the small pink to red flowers appear in dense clusters.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
