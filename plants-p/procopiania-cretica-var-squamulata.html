Procopiania cretica var. squamulata

Procopiania cretica var. squamulata, commonly known as the Cretan procopian.

This variant is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in parts of Greece and Turkey. It features small, oval-shaped leaves covered in a dense layer of fine, scale-like hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, round fruits.

It is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.
