Picea mastersii

Picea mastersii, commonly known as the Mexican fir, is a coniferous tree native to the high-altitude regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its conical shape, needle-like leaves, and distinctive cone structure. The tree typically grows in cool, moist environments, often at elevations above 2,000 meters. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against harsh mountain climates.

Picea mastersii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting local wildlife and contributing to forest biodiversity.
