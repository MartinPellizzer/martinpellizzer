Pachystela micrantha

Pachystela micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock heath, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Australia.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils. It is often found in open habitats such as heathlands and coastal areas.

Pachystela micrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
