Pavia rubra f. pendula

Pavia rubra f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping pavia, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive cascading growth habit.

It features slender, arching branches that drape downward, creating a graceful, weeping appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth, waxy texture.

Native to certain regions of Asia, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
