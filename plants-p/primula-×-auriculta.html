Primula × auriculta

Primula × auriculta, commonly known as the auriculate primrose, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive auriculate (ear-shaped) leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm, with a compact and bushy habit. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

Its flowering period usually occurs in spring, making it a popular choice for early-season garden displays.
