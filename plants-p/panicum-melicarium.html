Panicum melicarium

Panicum melicarium, commonly known as millet grass, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, three-angled spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a bluish-green hue and a hairy texture.

Panicum melicarium is known for its resilience and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
