Pyrenoglyphis rivularis

Pyrenoglyphis rivularis, commonly known as the river moss, is a type of aquatic plant that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like structures that form dense mats on the surface of streams and rivers. The plant has a simple morphology, with no true roots, stems, or leaves, and relies on spores for reproduction. It plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water filtration.

Due to its sensitivity to pollution, it is often used as an indicator of water quality.
