Psidium chodatianum

Psidium chodatianum, commonly known as the wax apple or carambola, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces distinctive star-shaped fruits with a tart, acidic flavor when ripe. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
