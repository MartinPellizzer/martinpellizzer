Paepalanthus barbulatus

Paepalanthus barbulatus, commonly known as the hairy puya, is a striking South American plant belonging to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that bears vibrant, often red or orange flowers. The plant is native to regions of Argentina and Chile, where it thrives in dry, open habitats. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Paepalanthus barbulatus is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as birds and insects.
