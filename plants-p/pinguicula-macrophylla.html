Pinguicula macrophylla

Pinguicula macrophylla, commonly known as the giant butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to South America.

It features large, broad leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap small insects. The plant is characterized by its long, slender stems and is often found in wet, boggy habitats. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it blooms during the cooler months.

Pinguicula macrophylla is a perennial plant that thrives in humid environments with ample sunlight.
