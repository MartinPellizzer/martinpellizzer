Pyrus redolens

Pyrus redolens, commonly known as the sweet cherry, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by sweet, red to dark purple fruits. Pyrus redolens thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth and fruit production.

It is widely cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.
