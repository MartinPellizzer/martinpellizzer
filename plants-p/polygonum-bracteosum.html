Polygonum bracteosum

Polygonum bracteosum, commonly known as the bracteate knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive nodes and often triangular stems. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture. Polygonum bracteosum is often found in moist environments such as wetlands and stream banks.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
