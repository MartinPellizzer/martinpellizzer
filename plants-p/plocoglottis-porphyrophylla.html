Plocoglottis porphyrophylla

Plocoglottis porphyrophylla, commonly known as the purple-leafed fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its striking, vibrant purple foliage, which contrasts beautifully with its green stems. The plant has fronds that are divided into smaller, lance-shaped segments, giving it a delicate and intricate appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it prefers cool, humid conditions.
