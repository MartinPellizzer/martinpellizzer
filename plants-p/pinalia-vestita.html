Pinalia vestita

Pinalia vestita, commonly known as the silverleaf pincushion, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It features a compact, globular shape with dense clusters of small, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. Its leaves are coated with a fine layer of wax, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Pinalia vestita is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and drought tolerance.
