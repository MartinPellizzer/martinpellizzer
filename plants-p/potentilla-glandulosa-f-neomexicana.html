Potentilla glandulosa f. neomexicana

Potentilla glandulosa f. neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico cinquefoil, is a subspecies of the glandular cinquefoil native to the southwestern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial shrub characterized by its small, yellow, five-petaled flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
