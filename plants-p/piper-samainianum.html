Piper samainianum

Piper samainianum, commonly known as the Samainian pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some local cultures.
