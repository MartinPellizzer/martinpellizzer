Phaseolus coccineus subsp. obvallatus

Phaseolus coccineus subsp.

obvallatus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas. It features vibrant red or orange flowers and produces elongated, edible pods that turn yellow when mature.

The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are hairy and twining. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and nutritional benefits.

This subspecies is closely related to other Phaseolus species, including the common kidney bean.
