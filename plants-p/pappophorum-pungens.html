Pappophorum pungens

Pappophorum pungens, commonly known as the spiked grass or pungent grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, tufted clumps of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are often covered in fine, hair-like structures, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Pappophorum pungens is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
