Papaver radicatum subsp. groevudalense

Papaver radicatum subsp.

groevudalense, commonly known as the Groevudalen poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Norway. It features a basal rosette of dark green, ovate leaves with a hairy texture.

The plant produces striking red or orange flowers with a prominent central stigma, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
