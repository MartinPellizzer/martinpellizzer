Pachystrobilus involucratus

Pachystrobilus involucratus, commonly known as the giant bamboo fern, is a distinctive evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, leathery fronds that can grow up to several meters in length, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its unique morphology includes a clustered growth habit with a thick, fleshy stem covered in a protective involucrum.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and as a houseplant.
