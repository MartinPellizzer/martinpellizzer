Pterocarya hupehensis

Pterocarya hupehensis, commonly known as the Chinese wingnut, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Juglandaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal. The plant has compound leaves with serrated margins and produces edible nuts enclosed in a thick, woody husk. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and has some traditional uses in Chinese medicine.
