Perrottetia multiflora

Perrottetia multiflora, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The leaves are typically pinnate, with multiple small leaflets arranged along a central axis. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces.
