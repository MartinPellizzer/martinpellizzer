Protium morii

Protium morii, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, clustered in panicles, and it produces small, round berries that are edible for some bird species.

Protium morii plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife.
