Phytolacca icosandra var. fraseri

Phytolacca icosandra var. fraseri, commonly known as Fraser's pokeweed, is a variant of the pokeweed species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that develop into dark purple berries. It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is known for its latex-containing sap, which can be toxic.
