Primula elata

Primula elata, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, broad, and hairy, while its stems are often upright.

Primula elata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
