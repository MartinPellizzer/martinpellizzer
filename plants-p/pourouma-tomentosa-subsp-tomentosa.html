Pourouma tomentosa subsp. tomentosa

Pourouma tomentosa subsp.

tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly bamboo, is a member of the Marantaceae family. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters.

The plant features large, lance-shaped leaves with a velvety, hairy texture on the underside. It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
