Peperomia palpebrata var. typica

Peperomia palpebrata var. typica, commonly known as the "Eyelid Peperomia," is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It features thick, waxy, oval-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver or purple, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in terminal clusters. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements make it an attractive addition to any plant collection.
