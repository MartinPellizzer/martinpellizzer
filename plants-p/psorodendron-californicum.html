Psorodendron californicum

Psorodendron californicum, commonly known as the California sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, branching stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry slopes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Psorodendron californicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
