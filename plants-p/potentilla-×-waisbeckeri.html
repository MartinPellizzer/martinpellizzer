Potentilla × waisbeckeri

Potentilla × waisbeckeri, commonly known as the Waisbecker cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, dark green leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
