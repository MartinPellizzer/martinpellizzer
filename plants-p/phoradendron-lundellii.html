Phoradendron lundellii

Phoradendron lundellii, commonly known as the mistletoe of the Caribbean, is a hemiparasitic plant that relies on host trees for water and nutrients.

It produces small, white or pale green berries that are often consumed by birds, which then disperse its seeds. The plant has a distinctive appearance with smooth, glossy leaves and a woody stem that grows directly into the host tree's branches. It is native to parts of Central and South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can weaken its host if left unchecked, making it an important subject in ecological studies.
