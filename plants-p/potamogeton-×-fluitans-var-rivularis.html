Potamogeton × fluitans var. rivularis

Potamogeton × fluitans var. rivularis, commonly known as the river pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant found in freshwater habitats.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and can form dense underwater vegetation.

Its presence often indicates good water quality and is important for supporting aquatic ecosystems.
