Pithecellobium andaquiense

Pithecellobium andaquiense, commonly known as the Andean burr legume, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Colombia and Ecuador.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

Its seeds are encased in thick, woody pods, which contribute to its ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
