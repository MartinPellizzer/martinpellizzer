Pectocarya linearis subsp. linearis

Pectocarya linearis subsp.

linearis, commonly known as the slender pectocarya, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
