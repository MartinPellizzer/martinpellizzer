Pyrus × vavilovii

Pyrus × vavilovii, commonly known as the Vavilov pear, is a hybrid species resulting from the crossbreeding of Pyrus communis (European pear) and Pyrus ussuriensis (Siberian pear).

It is characterized by its cold-hardy nature, making it well-suited for cultivation in temperate and colder climates. The plant typically produces large, juicy fruits with a sweet and sometimes aromatic flavor. It is often used in breeding programs to improve the cold tolerance and fruit quality of cultivated pears.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a genetic resource for pear improvement and its adaptability to diverse environmental conditions.
