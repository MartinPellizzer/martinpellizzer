Polypodium craspedosorum

Polypodium craspedosorum, commonly known as the serrated holly fern, is a species of epiphytic fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lanceolate fronds that are typically divided into numerous small, serrated segments. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are covered in a dense, woolly layer of root hairs, which helps it absorb moisture from the air.

The plant's ability to grow in low-light conditions makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and conservation efforts.
