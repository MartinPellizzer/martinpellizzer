Phenax laxiflorus

Phenax laxiflorus, commonly known as the slender-flowered phenax, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in loose, umbrella-like clusters. Its leaves are finely divided and often have a mild, spicy scent.

Phenax laxiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
