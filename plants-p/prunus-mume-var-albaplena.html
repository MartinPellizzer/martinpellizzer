Prunus mume var. albaplena

Prunus mume var. albaplena, commonly known as the white-flowered Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, edible fruits similar to peaches, though they are typically not cultivated for consumption. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Prunus mume var. albaplena is widely appreciated for its aesthetic value in gardens and traditional Chinese and Japanese horticulture.
