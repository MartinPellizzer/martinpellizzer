Polygonum bellardii subsp. dissitiflorum

Polygonum bellardii subsp.

dissitiflorum, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along streambanks and roadsides.

The plant has alternate, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and usually pink to reddish in color.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread vegetatively.
