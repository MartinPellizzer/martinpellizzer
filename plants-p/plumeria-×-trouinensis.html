Plumeria × trouinensis

Plumeria × trouinensis, commonly known as the frangipani, is a hybrid plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific and Indian Oceans.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant produces large, waxy leaves and has a smooth, often flaky bark. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Plumeria × trouinensis is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of fragrant wood.
