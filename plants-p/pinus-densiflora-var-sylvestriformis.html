Pinus densiflora var. sylvestriformis

Pinus densiflora var. sylvestriformis, commonly known as the Japanese black pine, is a variant of the Japanese white pine.

It is native to southern Japan and is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and dense, needle-like foliage. The plant has short, stout needles that are typically arranged in bundles of five, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces cones that are elongated and often remain attached to the branches for a long time.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
