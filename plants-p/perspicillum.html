Perspicillum

Perspicillum, commonly known as the glasswort, is a halophytic plant that thrives in saline environments.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant is often found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline soils where it plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion. Perspicillum has small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red, and it reproduces through seeds and vegetative propagation.

Its ability to tolerate high salinity makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
