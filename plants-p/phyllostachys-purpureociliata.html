Phyllostachys purpureociliata

Phyllostachys purpureociliata, commonly known as the purple bamboo, is a species of grass in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with a distinctive purple hue on its culms. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green color, and it produces feathery, pale green to yellowish inflorescences.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic and structural qualities.
