Pelargonium durum

Pelargonium durum, commonly known as the African geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and broad, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Pelargonium durum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
