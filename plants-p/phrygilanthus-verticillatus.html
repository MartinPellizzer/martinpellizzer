Phrygilanthus verticillatus

Phrygilanthus verticillatus, commonly known as the spiral leaf tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spirally arranged leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural gardens.
