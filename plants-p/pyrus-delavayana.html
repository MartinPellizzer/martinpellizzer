Pyrus delavayana

Pyrus delavayana, commonly known as Delavay's pear, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pyrus.

It is native to China, specifically found in the Yunnan and Sichuan provinces. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, with leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by edible fruits that are similar to common pears.

Pyrus delavayana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
