Polystachya micropetala

Polystachya micropetala, commonly known as the tiny-leaved bamboo orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers that are often short-lived but attract various pollinators. Polystachya micropetala thrives in moist, shaded environments and is typically found in forest understories or along stream banks.
