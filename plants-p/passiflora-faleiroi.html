Passiflora faleiroi

Passiflora faleiroi, commonly known as the Faleiro's passionflower, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, intricate flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a unique crown of filaments. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of blue or purple berries that mature in the fall. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in rainforests.

Passiflora faleiroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
