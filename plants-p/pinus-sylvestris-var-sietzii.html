Pinus sylvestris var. sietzii

Pinus sylvestris var. sietzii, commonly known as the Sietz's Scots pine, is a variant of the Scots pine native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The foliage is typically dark green, and the bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against environmental stressors. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ecological role and has been studied for its adaptation to specific environmental conditions.
