Parartocarpus borneensis

Parartocarpus borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean ironwood, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in axillary inflorescences.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in the forest ecosystem.
