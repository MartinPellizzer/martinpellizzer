Psoralea latifolia

Psoralea latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can be dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread across its native range.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in treating various ailments.
