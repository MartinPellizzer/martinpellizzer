Phymatopteris cartilagineoserrata

Phymatopteris cartilagineoserrata, commonly known as the cartilaginous serrated fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, often hairy fronds that have a cartilaginous texture, giving it its common name. The fronds are typically pinnately compound, with finely serrated margins that contribute to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The plant's reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.
