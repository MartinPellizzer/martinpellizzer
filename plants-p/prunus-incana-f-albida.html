Prunus incana f. albida

Prunus incana f. albida, commonly known as the white-haired cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus incana species.

It is characterized by its dense, white to pale gray pubescence covering the stems and leaves. The plant typically produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that thrives in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is primarily due to its distinctive hairy appearance and delicate blossoms.
