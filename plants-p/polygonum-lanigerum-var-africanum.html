Polygonum lanigerum var. africanum

Polygonum lanigerum var. africanum, commonly known as African knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Africa.

It features erect, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, arranged in spikes. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common weed in various habitats across its range.
