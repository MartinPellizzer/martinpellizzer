Prunus × lannesiana f. caespitosa

Prunus × lannesiana f. caespitosa, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or compact cherry, is a compact, shrub-like form of the Prunus lannesiana species.

It features small, rounded leaves and produces clusters of white or pink flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its dense, mounding growth habit, making it ideal for rock gardens or as a decorative hedge. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, requiring minimal maintenance once established.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
