Prunus acacia

Prunus acacia, commonly known as the honeylocust, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by flat, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. Its root system has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
