Polygala vulgaris subsp. provincialis

Polygala vulgaris subsp.

provincialis, commonly known as the provincial milkvetch, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
