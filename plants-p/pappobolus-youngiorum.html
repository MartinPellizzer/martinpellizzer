Pappobolus youngiorum

Pappobolus youngiorum, commonly known as the young's burr grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-dispersed seeds. The plant produces small, spiny capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by the wind due to their pappus structures. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides.

Pappobolus youngiorum is known for its hardy nature and ability to colonize disturbed areas.
