Phyllostachys praecox f. viridisulcata

Phyllostachys praecox f. viridisulcata, commonly known as the green-striped bamboo, is a variant of the early bamboo species characterized by its unique foliage.

Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a distinct green stripe running along the midrib, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant is a clump-forming bamboo that grows in dense stands, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This ornamental bamboo is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and garden design.
