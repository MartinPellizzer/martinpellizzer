Pyrus serpentae

Pyrus serpentae, commonly known as the serpent's pear, is a rare and unique species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elongated fruits that resemble a serpent in appearance, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, with a distinct serrated edge.

The flowers are pale yellow and bloom in early spring, preceding the development of its distinctive fruit.
