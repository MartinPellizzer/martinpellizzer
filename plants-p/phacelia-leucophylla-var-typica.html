Phacelia leucophylla var. typica

Phacelia leucophylla var. typica, commonly known as the white-leaf phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, pale blue to white flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinator populations.
