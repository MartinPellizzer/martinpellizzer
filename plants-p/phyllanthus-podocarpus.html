Phyllanthus podocarpus

Phyllanthus podocarpus, commonly known as the Indian madder, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers and elliptical leaves with a smooth surface. The plant produces small, red berries that contain seeds coated in a sticky substance. It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both traditional medicine and ecological studies.
