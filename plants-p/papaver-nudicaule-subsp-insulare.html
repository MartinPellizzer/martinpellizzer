Papaver nudicaule subsp. insulare

Papaver nudicaule subsp.

insulare, commonly known as the island poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or coastal environments.

The plant features delicate, papery petals that are usually pale yellow to white in color. Its flowers are borne on short, erect stems and bloom in early spring.

The subspecies is characterized by its smaller size and distinct floral morphology compared to the typical form of Papaver nudicaule.
