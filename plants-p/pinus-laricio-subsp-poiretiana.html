Pinus laricio subsp. poiretiana

Pinus laricio subsp.

poiretiana, commonly known as the Corsican pine, is a subspecies of the stone pine native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in bundles of five.

The tree produces cones that are woody and contain edible seeds, which are a traditional food source in some areas. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous environments.

This subspecies is also valued for its timber and plays a role in local ecosystems.
