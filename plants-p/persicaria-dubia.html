Persicaria dubia

Persicaria dubia, commonly known as common smartweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple, which bloom in late summer.

The seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to spread and colonize new areas.
