Prunus moldavica

Prunus moldavica, commonly known as the Moldavian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Balkans.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves and clusters of small white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart and less commonly consumed by humans. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The species is relatively hardy and adaptable to various soil types, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
