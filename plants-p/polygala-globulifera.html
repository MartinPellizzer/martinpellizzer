Polygala globulifera

Polygala globulifera, commonly known as the globular milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found growing in coastal or sandy habitats. It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is known for its unique floral structure.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
