Poa wallii

Poa wallii, commonly known as the Wallis grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, slender leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green or purplish in color. Poa wallii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
