Placea lutea

Placea lutea, commonly known as the yellow placea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and woody stems. The plant typically grows in shrubland and woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth margin, while its flowers are clustered in umbels.

Placea lutea is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
