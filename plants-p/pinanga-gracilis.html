Pinanga gracilis

Pinanga gracilis, commonly known as the slender bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. This plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and pinnate leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Pinanga gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
