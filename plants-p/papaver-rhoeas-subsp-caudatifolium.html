Papaver rhoeas subsp. caudatifolium

Papaver rhoeas subsp.

caudatifolium, commonly known as the caudate-leaved poppy, is a lesser-known subspecies of the common poppy. It is characterized by its elongated, trailing leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields or along roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
