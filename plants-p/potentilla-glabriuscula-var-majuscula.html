Potentilla glabriuscula var. majuscula

Potentilla glabriuscula var. majuscula, commonly known as the large-flowered cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and mountainous regions. The plant features compound leaves with five leaflets and produces showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its flowers are borne on long, slender peduncles and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual appeal.
