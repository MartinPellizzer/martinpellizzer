Pleioblastus longispiculatus

Pleioblastus longispiculatus, commonly known as the long-spined bamboo, is a species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

This bamboo is often used in landscaping for its ability to create a dense, upright screen.
