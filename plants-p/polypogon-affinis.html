Polypogon affinis

Polypogon affinis, commonly known as narrowleaf basketgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are small, elongated flower clusters.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
