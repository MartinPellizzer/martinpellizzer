Pleurothallis spathuliformis

Pleurothallis spathuliformis, commonly known as the broad-leafed pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, epiphytic growth habit and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often clustered in a spike. Its leaves are typically broad and elliptical, adapted to capture moisture from the air.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
