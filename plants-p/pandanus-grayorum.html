Pandanus grayorum

Pandanus grayorum, commonly known as the gray pandanus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, followed by edible fruits known as pandan fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.

The species is adapted to coastal and wetland environments, thriving in humid, warm climates.
