Peperomia myrtifolia var. typica

Peperomia myrtifolia var. typica, commonly known as the Myrtle-leaved Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, ovate leaves that are often tinged with reddish hues, especially along the veins, giving it a striking visual appeal. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white or pale pink, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a versatile addition to both indoor and outdoor landscapes.
