Pithecellobium carbonaria

Pithecellobium carbonaria, commonly known as the black-leaf burr legume, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and fruit pods. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a woody pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

The leaves are typically dark green and have a rough texture, making it well adapted to its humid, tropical environment.
