Potentilla biloba

Potentilla biloba, commonly known as the two-bloom cinquefoil, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that appear in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, which are serrated and hairy along the edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Potentilla biloba is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
