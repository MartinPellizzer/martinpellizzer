Pelea puberula

Pelea puberula, commonly known as the velvetleaf pelea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive velvety appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on long, slender pedicels. Pelea puberula is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.

Its adaptability makes it a common weed in agricultural and urban settings.
