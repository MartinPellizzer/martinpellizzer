Prunus oxyodonta

Prunus oxyodonta, commonly known as the Japanese cherry, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, turning yellow in autumn. The fruit is a small, sweet drupe that ripens in late summer.

This species is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also valued in traditional medicine.
