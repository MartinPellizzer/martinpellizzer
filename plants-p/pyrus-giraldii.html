Pyrus giraldii

Pyrus giraldii, commonly known as the Giraldii pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, particularly in the Pyrenees and the Alps. The plant is characterized by its deciduous, sparsely branched structure and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like fruit, is typically yellow or green when mature and is edible.

Pyrus giraldii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowering display.
