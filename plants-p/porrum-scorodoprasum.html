Porrum scorodoprasum

Porrum scorodoprasum, commonly known as garden parsley, is a biennial or annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, feather-like leaves that are often used as a culinary herb. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in umbels, which are characteristic of the Apiaceae family. Its roots are also edible and have a slightly spicy, peppery flavor.

Porrum scorodoprasum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and culinary use.
