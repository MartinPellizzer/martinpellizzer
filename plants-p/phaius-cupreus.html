Phaius cupreus

Phaius cupreus, commonly known as the copper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant has broad, strap-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pale yellow to orange-red flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
