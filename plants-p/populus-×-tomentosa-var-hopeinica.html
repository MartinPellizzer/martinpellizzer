Populus × tomentosa var. hopeinica

Populus × tomentosa var. hopeinica, commonly known as the Hope's poplar, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, fuzzy bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, which are typical of the Populus genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in stabilizing riverbanks.
