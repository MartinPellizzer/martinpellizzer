Peristrophe fera var. intermedia

Peristrophe fera var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate feather grass, is a grass species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often pale green or brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
