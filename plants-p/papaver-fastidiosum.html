Papaver fastidiosum

Papaver fastidiosum, commonly known as the fussy poppy, is a species of flowering plant in the family Papaveraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, with a distinctive papery texture. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically blooms in late spring to early summer.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species in the wild.
