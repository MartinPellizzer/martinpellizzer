Pterocissus mirabilis

Pterocissus mirabilis, commonly known as the silver leaf creeper, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by black, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for covering trellises and walls.
