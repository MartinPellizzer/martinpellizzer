Plantago hirsuta f. minima

Plantago hirsuta f. minima, commonly known as the miniature plantain, is a small variant of the common plantain.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its low-growing habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
