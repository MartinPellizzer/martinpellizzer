Passerina filiformis var. vulgaris

Passerina filiformis var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common threadleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers clustered in racemes, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Passerina filiformis var. vulgaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
