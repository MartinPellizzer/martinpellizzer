Pimelea urvilleana subsp. urvilleana

Pimelea urvilleana subsp.

urvilleana, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
