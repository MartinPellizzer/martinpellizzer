Potamogeton natans var. subnatans

Potamogeton natans var. subnatans, commonly known as the submerged pondweed, is a aquatic plant native to freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that grow entirely underwater, often forming dense vegetation in shallow lakes and ponds. The leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, typically arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged, followed by the development of elongated, cylindrical fruits.

This plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
