Piper callcanense

Piper callcanense, commonly known as the Callcanes pepper or the Yucatan pepper, is a tropical plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper and chili peppers. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits that turn red when mature.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a spice, known for its pungent and slightly bitter flavor.
