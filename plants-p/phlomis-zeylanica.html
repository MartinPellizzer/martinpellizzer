Phlomis zeylanica

Phlomis zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from summer to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
