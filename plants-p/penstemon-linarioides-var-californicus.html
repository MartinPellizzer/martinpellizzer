Penstemon linarioides var. californicus

Penstemon linarioides var. californicus, commonly known as the California beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tubular, violet to pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to support pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
