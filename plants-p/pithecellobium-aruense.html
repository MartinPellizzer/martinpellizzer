Pithecellobium aruense

Pithecellobium aruense, commonly known as the Aru Island legume, is a tropical shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is adapted to nutrient-poor soils, making it resilient in coastal and island environments.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
