Pseudosedum acutisepalum

Pseudosedum acutisepalum, commonly known as the acute-sepal sedum, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color, adapted for water storage. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its drought tolerance.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for xeriscaping and low-maintenance landscaping.
