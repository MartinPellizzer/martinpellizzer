Potamogeton × mysticus

Potamogeton × mysticus, commonly known as the hybrid watermilfoil, is a perennial aquatic plant that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton crispus and Potamogeton natans, exhibiting characteristics of both parents. The plant has slender, branching stems that often float on the water surface, with small, scale-like leaves. Its flowers are typically unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

Potamogeton × mysticus plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
