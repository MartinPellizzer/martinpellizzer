Pyrus Poteriifolia

Pyrus poteriifolia is a rare and fascinating species of apple tree native to the mountainous regions of central Asia. This deciduous plant is known for its distinctive leaves, which resemble those of the maple, giving it a unique visual appeal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky soils at high altitudes. Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat, Pyrus poteriifolia is considered a vulnerable species. Its study offers valuable insights into plant adaptation and conservation in challenging ecosystems.
