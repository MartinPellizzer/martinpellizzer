Prosthechea sima

Prosthechea sima, commonly known as the orchid mantissa, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous inflorescences that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant has a distinctive rosette-shaped structure with overlapping leaves that form a protective covering over the pseudobulbs. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a unique, elongated shape that sets it apart from other orchid species.

Prosthechea sima is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
