Polycnemum monandrum

Polycnemum monandrum, commonly known as the single-stamen sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, unisexual flowers arranged in spikelets, with the male flowers typically located at the base and the female flowers above. It thrives in moist, often shaded environments such as wetlands and forest floors.

Polycnemum monandrum plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
