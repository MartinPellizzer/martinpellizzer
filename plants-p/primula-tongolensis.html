Primula tongolensis

Primula tongolensis, commonly known as the Tongol primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments, often in forest understories or rocky crevices. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Primula tongolensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
