Prismatocarpus candolleanus

Prismatocarpus candolleanus, commonly known as the Ceylon ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, opposite, and often lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically arranged in panicles, and it produces woody, elongated fruits.

The tree is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in traditional medicine and local crafts.
