Paspalum rawitscheri

Paspalum rawitscheri, commonly known as the New Zealand paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Paspalum rawitscheri plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
