Prunus campanulata var. wuyiensis

Prunus campanulata var. wuyiensis, commonly known as the Wuyi mountain cherry, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical with a smooth margin, and it produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional Chinese cuisine.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
