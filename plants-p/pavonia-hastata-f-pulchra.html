Pavonia hastata f. pulchra

Pavonia hastata f. pulchra, commonly known as the showy pavonia, is a striking flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are typically pink or red, surrounded by colorful, petal-like bracts that enhance its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.

This cultivar is valued in gardens for its ability to attract pollinators and its adaptability to various soil conditions.
