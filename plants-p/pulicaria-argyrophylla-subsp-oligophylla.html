Pulicaria argyrophylla subsp. oligophylla

Pulicaria argyrophylla subsp.

oligophylla, commonly known as silverleaf pulicaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
