Pyrola chlorantha

Pyrola chlorantha, commonly known as the white wintergreen, is a low-growing evergreen perennial native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, leathery, evergreen leaves that remain throughout the year, providing a lush green appearance even in winter. Its flowers are pale green to white, with a distinctive bell shape, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Pyrola chlorantha thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in boreal and subalpine forests.
