Potamogeton columbianus

Potamogeton columbianus, commonly known as the Colombian pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in South America.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or just at the water's surface. The plant is characterized by its fibrous roots and its ability to thrive in shallow, still waters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its rapid growth, it can also become invasive in certain water bodies if not managed properly.
