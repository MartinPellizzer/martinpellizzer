Plectranthus patchoulii

Plectranthus patchoulii, commonly known as the patchouli plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and basil. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Plectranthus patchoulii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal gardening.
