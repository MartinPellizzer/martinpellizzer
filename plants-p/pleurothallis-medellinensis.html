Pleurothallis medellinensis

Pleurothallis medellinensis, commonly known as the Medellin orchid, is a species of orchid native to Colombia.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small, with a variety of colors including white, yellow, and pink, and they bloom in clusters.

Pleurothallis medellinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beauty in botanical gardens and private collections.
