Pinus occidentalis var. cubensis

Pinus occidentalis var. cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban pine, is a variety of the slash pine native to Cuba.

It is a tall evergreen conifer with long, needle-like leaves that grow in clusters of two. The tree produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
