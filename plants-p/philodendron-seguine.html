Philodendron seguine

Philodendron seguine, commonly known as the creeping philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by green, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid environments with bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Philodendron seguine is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
