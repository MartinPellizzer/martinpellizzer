Papaver pseudostrictum

Papaver pseudostrictum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its capsule fruit contains numerous small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Papaver pseudostrictum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
