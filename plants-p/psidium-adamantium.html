Psidium adamantium

Psidium adamantium, commonly known as the adamantium guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or red when ripe, with a sweet and acidic flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical regions.
