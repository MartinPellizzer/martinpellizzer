Phlox griseola subsp. griseola

Phlox griseola subsp.

griseola, commonly known as gray phlox, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, arranged in dense, terminal clusters.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
