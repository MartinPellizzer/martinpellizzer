Polygonum confertiflorum

Polygonum confertiflorum, commonly known as the dense-flowered knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is commonly found in wetlands, stream banks, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

Polygonum confertiflorum is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
