Potentilla canescens var. incisoserrata

Potentilla canescens var. incisoserrata, commonly known as the rough cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lobed with irregularly serrated edges, giving the plant its specific epithet. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in challenging environments.
