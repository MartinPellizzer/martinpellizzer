Potamogeton × crassifolius f. verrutus

Potamogeton × crassifolius f. verrutus, commonly known as the warty pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant characterized by its thick, warty leaves.

It is typically found in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has elongated, ribbon-like leaves that are often covered in small, raised bumps or verrucae, giving it its common name. It reproduces through both sexual and vegetative means, contributing to its ability to spread rapidly in suitable habitats.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a primary producer in aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
