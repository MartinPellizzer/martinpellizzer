Paxia myriantha

Paxia myriantha, commonly known as the white paxia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slight hairy texture.
