Palimbia turgaica

Palimbia turgaica, commonly known as the Turgica palm, is a rare and unique plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in the axils of the leaves, and it produces fruits that are edible but not widely consumed.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, Palimbia turgaica is considered an endangered species.
