Patima forsythii

Patima forsythii, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Patima.

It is native to China and is characterized by its vibrant, papery bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant produces small yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Patima forsythii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
