Polygonum myriophyllum

Polygonum myriophyllum, commonly known as watermeal, is a small aquatic plant characterized by its tiny, overlapping leaves that resemble a mosaic.

It is a floating plant that typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. The plant has a short, branched stem and produces small, green flowers that are often clustered together. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats on the water surface.

Polygonum myriophyllum is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other plants with similar leaf structures.
