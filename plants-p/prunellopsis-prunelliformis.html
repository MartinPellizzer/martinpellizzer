Prunellopsis prunelliformis

Prunellopsis prunelliformis, commonly known as the dwarf pygmy mistletoe, is a small, hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of certain host trees.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly in regions of Brazil and Argentina. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces tiny, white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that is typically eaten by birds, which helps in seed dispersal.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
