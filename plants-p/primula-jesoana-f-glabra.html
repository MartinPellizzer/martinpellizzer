Primula jesoana f. glabra

Primula jesoana f. glabra, commonly known as the glabrous primrose, is a botanical variant of the primula genus native to specific regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless foliage, which distinguishes it from other forms of the species. The plant typically features showy, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in mountainous or woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
