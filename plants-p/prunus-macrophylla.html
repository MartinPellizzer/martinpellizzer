Prunus macrophylla

Prunus macrophylla, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree or shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its ornamental foliage, which displays vibrant colors of red, orange, and yellow during the autumn season. The plant has compound leaves with 3 to 5 leaflets, and its flowers are small, white or pink, appearing in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The fruit is a small, hard drupe that contains a single seed.
