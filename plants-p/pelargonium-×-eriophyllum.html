Pelargonium × eriophyllum

Pelargonium × eriophyllum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that resemble ivy leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming from spring to autumn. It is native to South Africa and is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.

Pelargonium × eriophyllum prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for container gardening and hanging baskets.
