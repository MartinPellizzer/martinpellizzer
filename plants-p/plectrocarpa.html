Plectrocarpa

Plectrocarpa, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a genus of flowering plants native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers. The genus includes several species, with Plectrocarpa glabrata being the most well-known. These plants typically grow as shrubs or small trees and are often found in coastal and sandy soils.

They play an important role in ecological systems by fixing nitrogen in the soil.
