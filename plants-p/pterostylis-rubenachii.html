Pterostylis rubenachii

Pterostylis rubenachii, commonly known as the Red-tongued Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, elongated flower spike. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in rainforest or understorey habitats. Its flowers are usually pale green to reddish in color, with a distinctive, spurred lip that aids in pollination.

Pterostylis rubenachii is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
