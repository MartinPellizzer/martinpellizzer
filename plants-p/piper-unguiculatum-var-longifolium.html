Piper unguiculatum var. longifolium

Piper unguiculatum var. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved pepper plant, is a tropical species native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that transition into peppery, aromatic berries. It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for study in tropical botany and horticulture.
