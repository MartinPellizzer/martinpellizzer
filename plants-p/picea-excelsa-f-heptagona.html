Picea excelsa f. heptagona

Picea excelsa f. heptagona, commonly known as the seven-sided spruce, is a rare and distinctive variant of the white spruce.

It is characterized by its unique seven-sided stem, which sets it apart from other spruce species. This botanical feature is not only visually striking but also suggests a possible adaptation to specific environmental conditions. The plant is typically found in high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, where it thrives in cold and moist climates.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
