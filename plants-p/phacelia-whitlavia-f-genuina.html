Phacelia whitlavia f. genuina

Phacelia whitlavia f. genuina, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada phacelia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting local pollinator populations.
