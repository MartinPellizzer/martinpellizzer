Pararistolochia leonensis

Pararistolochia leonensis, commonly known as the Leonensis Dutchman's Pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its unique, hollow, pipe-like flowers that are often green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, orange aril. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
