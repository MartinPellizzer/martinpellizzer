Pygeum schlechteri

Pygeum schlechteri, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
