Polygonum aviculare var. vulgare

Polygonum aviculare var. vulgare, commonly known as common knotgrass, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features slender, prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink to reddish in color, blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

Common knotgrass is characterized by its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.
