Phelypaea caerulea proles millefolii

Phelypaea caerulea proles millefolii, commonly known as the blue fern, is a strikingly ornamental plant characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features a compact, rosette-like growth habit with slender, blue-green leaves that give it its distinctive appearance. Its spore-bearing structures, or sporangia, are typically clustered on the underside of the fronds.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and indoor spaces.
