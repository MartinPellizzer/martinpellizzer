Palmijuncus andamanicus

Palmijuncus andamanicus, commonly known as the Andaman palm, is a flowering plant native to the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes various palm species. The plant is characterized by its slender, unbranched stem and pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and humid conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
