Piper villipedunculum

Piper villipedunculum, commonly known as the small-stemmed pepper plant, is a species of flowering vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves, and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berry-like fruits. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and ecological studies.

The plant's structure and growth habits reflect adaptations to its humid, forested habitats.
