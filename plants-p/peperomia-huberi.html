Peperomia huberi

Peperomia huberi, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy, silver-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color, appearing in clusters along the stems. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low light requirements and drought tolerance.

Peperomia huberi is valued for its ornamental foliage and ease of care, making it a popular choice for houseplants.
