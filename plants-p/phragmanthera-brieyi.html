Phragmanthera brieyi

Phragmanthera brieyi, commonly known as the mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are slender and climbing.

Phragmanthera brieyi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
