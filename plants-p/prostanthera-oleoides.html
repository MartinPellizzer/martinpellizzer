Prostanthera oleoides

Prostanthera oleoides, commonly known as narrow-leaf mintbush, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

Prostanthera oleoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract native pollinators.
