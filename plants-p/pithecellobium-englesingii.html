Pithecellobium englesingii

Pithecellobium englesingii, commonly known as the Englesing's pita, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemose inflorescences. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with oval to lance-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The fruit is a legume that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its ecological role in nitrogen fixation.
