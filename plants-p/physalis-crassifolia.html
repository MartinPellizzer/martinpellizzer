Physalis crassifolia

Physalis crassifolia, commonly known as the thickleaf groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of orange-yellow berries that enclose the seeds. The plant thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in dry, rocky soils. Its flowers are typically tubular with five lobes and are pollinated by insects.

The fruit, which resembles a small tomato, is edible and has a tart, sweet flavor.
