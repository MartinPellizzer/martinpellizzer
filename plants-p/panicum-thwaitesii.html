Panicum thwaitesii

Panicum thwaitesii, commonly known as the slender grass or thwaites' panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, a common strategy among grasses. It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Panicum thwaitesii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
