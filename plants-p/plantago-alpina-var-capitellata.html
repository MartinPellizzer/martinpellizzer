Plantago alpina var. capitellata

Plantago alpina var. capitellata, commonly known as the alpine plantain, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces a dense cluster of small, greenish-white flowers on a tall, slender stem. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
