Protea chamelaea

Protea chamelaea, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its striking, cone-shaped inflorescences. The plant typically grows in fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, sandy soils. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are pollinated by birds and insects.

Protea chamelaea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
