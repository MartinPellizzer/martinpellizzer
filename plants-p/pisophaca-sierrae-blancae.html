Pisophaca sierrae-blancae

Pisophaca sierrae-blancae, commonly known as the Sierra Whitebark, is a rare and endemic plant species found in the high-altitude regions of the Sierra Nevada mountains.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its dense, white-woolly foliage that provides insulation against the harsh mountain climate. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by wind, a common adaptation in alpine environments. Its seeds are dispersed by birds and small mammals, contributing to its limited distribution.
