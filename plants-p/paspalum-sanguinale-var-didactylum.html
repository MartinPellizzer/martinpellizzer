Paspalum sanguinale var. didactylum

Paspalum sanguinale var. didactylum, commonly known as bloodgrass, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-tipped leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its ornamental value is due to its vibrant coloration and unique foliage structure.
