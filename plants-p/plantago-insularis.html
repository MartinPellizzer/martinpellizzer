Plantago insularis

Plantago insularis, commonly known as the island plantain, is a perennial herb native to various island environments.

It typically grows in coastal areas and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, which help reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and clustered in a dense spike, producing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems on islands.
