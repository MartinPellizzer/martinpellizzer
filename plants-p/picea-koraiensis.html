Picea koraiensis

Picea koraiensis, commonly known as the Korean fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its conical shape and dense, dark green foliage. The tree produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and furrowed, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

Picea koraiensis is valued for its timber and plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitat.
