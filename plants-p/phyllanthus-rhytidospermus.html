Phyllanthus rhytidospermus

Phyllanthus rhytidospermus, commonly known as the rough-seeded phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its seeds are encased in a hard, rough shell, which aids in seed dispersal.

The species is also noted for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
