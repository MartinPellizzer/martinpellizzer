Pilosella samokoviensis

Pilosella samokoviensis, commonly known as the Samokov bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, and it produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Pilosella samokoviensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
