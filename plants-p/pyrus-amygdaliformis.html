Pyrus amygdaliformis

Pyrus amygdaliformis, commonly known as the almond-shaped pear, is a rare and ancient species of pear native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that resemble almonds, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated edge, while the flowers are white and fragrant.

Due to its limited distribution and rarity, Pyrus amygdaliformis is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
