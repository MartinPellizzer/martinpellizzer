Polylobium falcatum

Polylobium falcatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often curved at the tips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, which are often spike-like in structure.

Polylobium falcatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its graceful growth habit.
