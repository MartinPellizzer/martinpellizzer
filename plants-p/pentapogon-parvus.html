Pentapogon parvus

Pentapogon parvus, commonly known as the small-headed grass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically forms dense mats of fine, grass-like leaves that are often less than 10 centimeters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Due to its delicate structure and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its habitat.
