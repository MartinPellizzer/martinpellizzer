Panicum poiforme

Panicum poiforme, commonly known as the prostrate panicgrass, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like blades that often form dense mats on the ground, with a distinctive, rounded or oval shape. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear feathery, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

Its adaptability and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for erosion control and landscaping in warm climates.
