Prunus clementis

Prunus clementis, commonly known as the Clementine cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its small, sweet, and juicy fruits. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical with a smooth texture, and it produces white or pink flowers in the spring.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and is valued for its flavor and nutritional content.
