Pilosella auriculoides subsp. longiseta

Pilosella auriculoides subsp.

longiseta, commonly known as the long-hair mouse-eared chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, typically from April to June.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which are often glandular and covered in fine, whitish hairs. Its leaves are usually spoon-shaped or heart-shaped, with a rough texture.

It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
