Polianthes mexicana

Polianthes mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican spider lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It features strap-like, narrow leaves and produces striking white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive, spidery appearance. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stem and is often found in rocky or limestone soils. It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Polianthes mexicana is typically hardy in USDA zones 8-10, though it can be grown in cooler climates with protection.
