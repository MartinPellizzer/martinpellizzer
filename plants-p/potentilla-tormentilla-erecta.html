Potentilla tormentilla erecta

Potentilla tormentilla erecta, commonly known as the erect cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are palmately divided into five leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
