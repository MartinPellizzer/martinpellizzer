Pleurothallis sarcodactylae

Pleurothallis sarcodactylae, commonly known as the "curl-leaf orchid," is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its unique leaf morphology, with curling and twisted foliage, gives it its common name.

This orchid is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a member of the Orchidaceae family.
