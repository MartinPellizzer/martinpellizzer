Piper sonadense

Piper sonadense, commonly known as the Sonadense pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. The fruits are typically harvested when they turn a bright red color, indicating maturity.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible berries.
