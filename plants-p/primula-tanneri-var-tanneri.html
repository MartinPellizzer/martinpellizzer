Primula tanneri var. tanneri

Primula tanneri var. tanneri, commonly known as the Tibetan primrose, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It features upright, hairy stems and produces clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The leaves are broadly ovate with a coarse texture and are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
