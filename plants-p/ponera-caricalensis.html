Ponera caricalensis

Ponera caricalensis, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mound-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the year under suitable conditions. Its leaves are velvety and dark green, often with a waxy texture.

Ponera caricalensis is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
