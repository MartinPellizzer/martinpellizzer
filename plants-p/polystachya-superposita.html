Polystachya superposita

Polystachya superposita, commonly known as the superposed bamboo orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy surface.

Polystachya superposita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
