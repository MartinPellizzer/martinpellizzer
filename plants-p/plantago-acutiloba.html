Plantago acutiloba

Plantago acutiloba, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features upright, narrow leaves that are ribbed and have a rough texture, often with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types, including disturbed areas, and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste places.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional remedies for skin irritations and respiratory issues.
