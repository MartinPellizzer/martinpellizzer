Polygonum sparsipilosum

Polygonum sparsipilosum, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive nodes and often triangular stems. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft texture.

Polygonum sparsipilosum is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including wetlands and stream banks.
