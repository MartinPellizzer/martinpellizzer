Persicaria oregana

Persicaria oregana, commonly known as Oregon knotweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and streambanks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Persicaria oregana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland areas.
