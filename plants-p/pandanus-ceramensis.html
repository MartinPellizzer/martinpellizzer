Pandanus ceramensis

Pandanus ceramensis, commonly known as the Ceram pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Maluku Islands of Indonesia.

It belongs to the Pandanus genus, which includes a variety of tree-like plants with distinctive, spirally arranged leaves. The plant features a thick, woody stem with a crown of long, narrow, fibrous leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material due to their durability.

The plant also produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically borne in clusters at the base of the leaves.
