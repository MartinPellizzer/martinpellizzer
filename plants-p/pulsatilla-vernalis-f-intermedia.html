Pulsatilla vernalis f. intermedia

Pulsatilla vernalis f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms that typically appear in shades of pink to white. The plant features a short, stout stem with palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and hairy. Native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe, it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for open, sunny habitats and its historical use in traditional medicine.
