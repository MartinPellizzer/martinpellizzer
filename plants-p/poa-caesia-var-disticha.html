Poa caesia var. disticha

Poa caesia var. disticha, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery-blue foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
