Pithecellobium acle

Pithecellobium acle, commonly known as the small-flowered acacia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, woody pods, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
