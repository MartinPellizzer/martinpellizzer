Pyrus austriaca var. balatonica

Pyrus austriaca var. balatonica, commonly known as the Balaton pear, is a cultivar of the European pear native to Hungary.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green fruits that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are dark green and oval-shaped, with a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
