Pilocarpus pennatifolius f. paraguariensis

Pilocarpus pennatifolius f. paraguariensis, commonly known as yerba mate, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Paraguay, Brazil, and Argentina.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and small, white flowers. The plant is well-known for its stimulating effects due to the presence of caffeine and other xanthines. The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both traditional medicine and local economies.
