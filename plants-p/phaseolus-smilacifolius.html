Phaseolus smilacifolius

Phaseolus smilacifolius, commonly known as the smilacifolius bean, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, narrow pods containing several seeds.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and agricultural studies due to its adaptability and potential nutritional value.
