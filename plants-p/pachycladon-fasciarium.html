Pachycladon fasciarium

Pachycladon fasciarium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rock fig, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, edible fig-like structure that develops from the flowers.

Pachycladon fasciarium is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found in rocky soils.
