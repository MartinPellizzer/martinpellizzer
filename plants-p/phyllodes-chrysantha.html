Phyllodes chrysantha

Phyllodes chrysantha, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its flat, leaf-like structures called phyllodes, which are typically yellow or golden in color. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Phyllodes chrysantha is also the state floral emblem of Western Australia.
