Panicum interstipitatum

Panicum interstipitatum, commonly known as narrowleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and pond margins. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.

Botanically, it is characterized by its rhizomatous growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich, waterlogged environments.
