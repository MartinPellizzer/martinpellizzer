Piper hirsutum var. asperiusculum

Piper hirsutum var. asperiusculum, commonly known as the rough-hairy pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper hirsutum species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in yellow or greenish-yellow hues. The plant produces small, peppery berries that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its growth habit is shrub-like, with sprawling stems that can reach up to several feet in height.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
