Polygonum persicaria proles biforme

Polygonum persicaria proles biforme, commonly known as twinleaf knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its stems are often reddish and can become woody with age, while its leaves are alternate and have a smooth texture.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local insect populations.
