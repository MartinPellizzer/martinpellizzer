Petalostemon candidus var. occidentalis

Petalostemon candidus var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western white petalostemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with slender, branching stems and small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
