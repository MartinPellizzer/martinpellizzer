Primula nivalis var. bayernii

Primula nivalis var. bayernii, commonly known as the Bavarian snowdrop, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive greenish throat. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments, often growing in rocky or shaded areas at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, which helps to reflect sunlight and retain moisture.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
