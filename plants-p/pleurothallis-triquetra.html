Pleurothallis triquetra

Pleurothallis triquetra, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its flowers are often clustered in racemes and bloom during the wet season.

Pleurothallis triquetra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
