Persea russellii

Persea russellii, commonly known as Russell's persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America and the Caribbean. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a smooth, oval berry that turns from green to orange when ripe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
