Potamogeton tepperi

Potamogeton tepperi, commonly known as Tepper's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown, arranged in spikes along the stem. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton tepperi is an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to water quality and biodiversity.
