Puya trianae var. amplior

Puya trianae var. amplior, commonly known as the giant puya, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to the Andes mountains of South America.

This plant is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it one of the tallest flowering plants in the world. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a rosette pattern, often displaying a vibrant mix of green, red, and purple hues. The flowers, which appear at the tip of the inflorescence, are typically small and clustered in dense, colorful bracts.

Due to its size and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and is a subject of interest for both horticulturists and researchers.
