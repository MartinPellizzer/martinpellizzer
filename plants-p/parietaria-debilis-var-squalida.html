Parietaria debilis var. squalida

Parietaria debilis var. squalida, commonly known as the stinging nettle weed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, typically in the summer months. It is commonly found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and waste areas.

The leaves and stems are covered in fine hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact.
