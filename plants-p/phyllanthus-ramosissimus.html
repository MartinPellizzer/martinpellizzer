Phyllanthus ramosissimus

Phyllanthus ramosissimus, commonly known as the hairy leaf spurge, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, branched stems and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
