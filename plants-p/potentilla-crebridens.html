Potentilla crebridens

Potentilla crebridens, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically in clusters of five. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Potentilla crebridens is adapted to rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.
