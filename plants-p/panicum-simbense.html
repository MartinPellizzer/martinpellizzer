Panicum simbense

Panicum simbense, commonly known as the East African millet, is a grass species native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils. It is drought-tolerant and often used as a forage crop for livestock.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
