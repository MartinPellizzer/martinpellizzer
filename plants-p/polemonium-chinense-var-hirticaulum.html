Polemonium chinense var. hirticaulum

Polemonium chinense var. hirticaulum, commonly known as the hairy polemonium, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, showy flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stems, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to moist, well-drained soils.
