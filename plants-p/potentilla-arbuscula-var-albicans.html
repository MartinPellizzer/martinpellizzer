Potentilla arbuscula var. albicans

Potentilla arbuscula var. albicans, commonly known as the white-flowered cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are oval to lance-shaped.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
