Pyrola rotundifolia var. incarnata

Pyrola rotundifolia var. incarnata, commonly known as the pink wintergreen, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northeastern United States and eastern Canada.

It features broad, waxy, oval-shaped leaves that remain green throughout the year. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late winter or early spring. Its leaves and flowers contain aromatic compounds, contributing to its use in traditional herbal remedies.

The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.
