Platycrater serrata

Platycrater serrata, commonly known as the serrated boxwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features dense, compact foliage with dark green, leathery leaves that are serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark blue berries. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its neat, boxwood-like appearance.

Platycrater serrata thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for garden design.
