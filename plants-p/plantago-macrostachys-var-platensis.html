Plantago macrostachys var. platensis

Plantago macrostachys var. platensis, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in wet meadows.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
