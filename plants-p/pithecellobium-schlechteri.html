Pithecellobium schlechteri

Pithecellobium schlechteri, commonly known as the yellow velvet tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with 7 to 15 pairs of leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas of Southeast Asia.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential use in traditional medicine.
