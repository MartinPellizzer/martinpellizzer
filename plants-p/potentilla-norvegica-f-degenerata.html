Potentilla norvegica f. degenerata

Potentilla norvegica f. degenerata, commonly known as the Norwegian cinquefoil, is a low-growing shrub native to northern Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, which are typically hairy and oval in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in colder climates.
