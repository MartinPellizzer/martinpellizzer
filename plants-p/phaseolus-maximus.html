Phaseolus maximus

Phaseolus maximus, commonly known as the kidney bean, is a flowering plant in the Fabaceae family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its compound leaves and purple or white flowers. The plant produces pods containing kidney-shaped seeds, which are widely cultivated for food. These seeds are rich in protein and are used in various cuisines around the world.

The plant's ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it an important crop in sustainable agriculture.
