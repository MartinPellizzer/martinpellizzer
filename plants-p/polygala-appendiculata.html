Polygala appendiculata

Polygala appendiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight curl, and it produces yellowish or greenish flowers in clusters.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
