Pandanus whitfordii

Pandanus whitfordii, commonly known as the Whitford's pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered at the tips of the leaves. Its root system is fibrous and helps it thrive in coastal and sandy soils.

Pandanus whitfordii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
