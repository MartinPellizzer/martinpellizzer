Pachyphytum heterosepalum

Pachyphytum heterosepalum, commonly known as the "Mexican sun rose," is a succulent native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, typical of desert plants. The plant produces vibrant orange to yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and striking blooms make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
