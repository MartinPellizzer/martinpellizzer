Pleurothallis grammata

Pleurothallis grammata, commonly known as the Grammatioid Orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces fragrant blooms in shades of white, yellow, and green.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is often sought after by orchid enthusiasts and collectors.
