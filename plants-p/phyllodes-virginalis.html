Phyllodes virginalis

Phyllodes virginalis, commonly known as the silky wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, narrow phyllodes that resemble leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions.

Phyllodes virginalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
