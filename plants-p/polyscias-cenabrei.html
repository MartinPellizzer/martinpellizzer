Polyscias cenabrei

Polyscias cenabrei, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Polyscias cenabrei is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its use in traditional medicine.
