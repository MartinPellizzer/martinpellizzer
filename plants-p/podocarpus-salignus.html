Podocarpus salignus

Podocarpus salignus, commonly known as the Australian pine or silky pine, is a evergreen tree native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Podocarpus salignus is valued for its timber and is also used in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.
