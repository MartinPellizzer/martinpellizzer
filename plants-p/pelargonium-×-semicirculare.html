Pelargonium × semicirculare

Pelargonium × semicirculare, commonly known as the African marigold, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow flowers and velvety, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and container planting.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its prolonged flowering period and adaptability to various climates.
