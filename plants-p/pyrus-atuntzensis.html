Pyrus atuntzensis

Pyrus atuntzensis, commonly known as the Atuntzen pear, is a small deciduous tree native to the Pyrenees region of Spain.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its rounded leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. Due to its limited distribution and rarity, it is considered a critically endangered species.

Its botanical study is important for understanding the biodiversity of the Iberian Peninsula.
