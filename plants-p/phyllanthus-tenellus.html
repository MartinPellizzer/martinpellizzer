Phyllanthus tenellus

Phyllanthus tenellus, commonly known as the small-leaf phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant features opposite, simple, and usually elliptical or ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, spherical fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature.

The plant is notable for its use in traditional medicine and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
