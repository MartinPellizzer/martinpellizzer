Polystachya luteola

Polystachya luteola, commonly known as the yellow lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to parts of Africa, including Kenya and Tanzania.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive appearance, featuring a dense, dark green, grass-like foliage. The plant produces a spike of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Polystachya luteola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
