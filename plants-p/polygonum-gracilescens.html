Polygonum gracilescens

Polygonum gracilescens, commonly known as slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes species known for their distinctive leaf shapes and flowering structures. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands and stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its stems are slender and often reddish in color, while its leaves are lance-shaped with a prominent midrib.

The small, clustered flowers appear in late summer and are surrounded by bracts, contributing to its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
