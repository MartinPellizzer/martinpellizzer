Pteropogon platyphyllus

Pteropogon platyphyllus, commonly known as the broad-leafed pteropogon, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae, or grass family.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves that are often arranged in a dense, tufted growth pattern. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is typically found in moist, tropical environments, often in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
