Polyalthia lopadantha

Polyalthia lopadantha, commonly known as the silver flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that resemble flames, often displaying a mix of white, yellow, and orange hues. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
