Prunus caudata

Prunus caudata, commonly known as the California sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are consumed by various wildlife species. Prunus caudata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and woodlands.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for numerous animals and birds.
