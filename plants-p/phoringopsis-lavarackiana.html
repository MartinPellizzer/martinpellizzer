Phoringopsis lavarackiana

Phoringopsis lavarackiana, commonly known as the Lavarack's phorophyte, is a unique epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and broad, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its humid environment. The plant forms symbiotic relationships with certain tree species, using them as a substrate for growth without parasitizing them. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
