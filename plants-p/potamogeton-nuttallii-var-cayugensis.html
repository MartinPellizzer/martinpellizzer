Potamogeton nuttallii var. cayugensis

Potamogeton nuttallii var. cayugensis, commonly known as Cayuga pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to the eastern United States.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually found in clusters near the water surface. It thrives in freshwater environments with moderate to slow water flow.

Its presence is often indicative of healthy, well-oxygenated aquatic ecosystems.
