Pittoniotis rotata

Pittoniotis rotata, commonly known as the spiral rush, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often along the edges of ponds and streams. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense spikes.

Pittoniotis rotata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in wetland gardens.
