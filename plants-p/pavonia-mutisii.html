Pavonia mutisii

Pavonia mutisii, commonly known as the redroot false mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its roots are thick and fleshy.

Pavonia mutisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
