Perezia sublyrata var. glabrescens

Perezia sublyrata var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous perezia, is a rare and distinctive plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique leaf morphology, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, glabrous surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
