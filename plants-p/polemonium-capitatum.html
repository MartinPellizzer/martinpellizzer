Polemonium capitatum

Polemonium capitatum, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Polemonium genus, which belongs to the family Polemoniaceae. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Polemonium capitatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
