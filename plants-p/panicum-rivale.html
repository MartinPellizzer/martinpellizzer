Panicum rivale

Panicum rivale, commonly known as river panicgrass, is a species of grass native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces fluffy, pale green to golden seed heads. The plant typically grows in moist soils along riverbanks, marshes, and other wet environments. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in disturbed or aquatic settings.

Panicum rivale plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
