Pilea angulata subsp. petiolaris

Pilea angulata subsp.

petiolaris, commonly known as the Shingleplant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia. It features dense clusters of small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slight sheen.

The plant has short, sturdy petioles, giving it its subspecies name, and produces inconspicuous, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it a versatile addition to both indoor and outdoor gardens.
