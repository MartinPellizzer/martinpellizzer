Phalaris minor var. gracilis

Phalaris minor var. gracilis, commonly known as slender false brome, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, two-lobed flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
