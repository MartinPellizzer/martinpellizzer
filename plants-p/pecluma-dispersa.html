Pecluma dispersa

Pecluma dispersa, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with reddish hues. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks.

Pecluma dispersa is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
