Prostanthera howelliae

Prostanthera howelliae, commonly known as Howell's mintbush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Prostanthera howelliae is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in coastal and inland regions.
