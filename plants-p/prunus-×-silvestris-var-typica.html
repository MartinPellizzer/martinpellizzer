Prunus × silvestris var. typica

Prunus × silvestris var. typica, commonly known as the wild cherry tree, is a species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 5–10 meters. The tree produces white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, dark red cherries in late summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in autumn.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
