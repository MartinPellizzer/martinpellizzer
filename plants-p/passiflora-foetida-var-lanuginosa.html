Passiflora foetida var. lanuginosa

Passiflora foetida var. lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and large, showy flowers with intricate structures. The plant produces dark blue or black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough, textured surface.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in warm climates.
