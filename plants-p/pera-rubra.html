Pera rubra

Pera rubra, commonly known as the red pera, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in spring, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green.

Pera rubra is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
