Persoonia lanceolata var. levis

Persoonia lanceolata var. levis, commonly known as the broad-leaved pear-fig, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features lanceolate, leathery leaves that are typically broader than those of the typical variety. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

This variety is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.
