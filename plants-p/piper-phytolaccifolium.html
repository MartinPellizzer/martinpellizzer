Piper phytolaccifolium

Piper phytolaccifolium, commonly known as the Indian pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded forest understories.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some regional cuisines.
