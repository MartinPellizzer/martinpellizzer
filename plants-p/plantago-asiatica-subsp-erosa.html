Plantago asiatica subsp. erosa

Plantago asiatica subsp.

erosa, commonly known as the hairy plantain, is a perennial herb native to Asia. It features a rosette of broad, hairy leaves that are often ribbed and have a waxy texture.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its medicinal properties have been utilized in traditional herbal remedies for their anti-inflammatory and healing effects.
