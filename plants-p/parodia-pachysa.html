Parodia pachysa

Parodia pachysa, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rounded, globular shape and is often cultivated for its vibrant, colorful flowers. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and bloom in the spring.

Parodia pachysa is a popular choice for succulent gardens and indoor plant collections due to its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal.
