Pycreus flavescens subsp. laevinux

Pycreus flavescens subsp.

laevinux, commonly known as the golden pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to South America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and greenish-yellow disk florets.

The plant produces small, spherical flower heads that are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge.
