Piper salviifolium

Piper salviifolium, commonly known as the sacred pepper or salviifolium pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into peppery fruits. Its botanical features include glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which contribute to its aromatic properties.

The plant is often grown for its ornamental value and for the spice derived from its fruits.
