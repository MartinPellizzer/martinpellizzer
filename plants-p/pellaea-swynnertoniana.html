Pellaea swynnertoniana

Pellaea swynnertoniana, commonly known as the Swynnerton's fern, is a species of fern native to the Cape Province of South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinnately compound fronds that are typically gray-green in color. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens and shaded borders.
