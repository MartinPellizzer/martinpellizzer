Pinnasa volubilis

Pinnasa volubilis, commonly known as the climbing pinnasa, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its twining stems that allow it to climb and spread along supports. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white and pink. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Pinnasa volubilis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
