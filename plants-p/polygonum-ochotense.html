Polygonum ochotense

Polygonum ochotense, commonly known as the Siberian knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are alternate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Polygonum ochotense is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
