Potentilla zapateri var. seguriensis

Potentilla zapateri var. seguriensis, commonly known as the Seguri's potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and serrated, with a dense, compact growth pattern.
