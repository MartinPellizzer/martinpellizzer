Polypodium whitfordii

Polypodium whitfordii, commonly known as the Whitford's water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds that are typically divided into small, finger-like segments. This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Polypodium whitfordii has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense mats.

Its spores are produced on the underside of its fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in humid climates.
