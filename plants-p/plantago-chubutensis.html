Plantago chubutensis

Plantago chubutensis, commonly known as the Chubut plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Patagonian region of Argentina.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are borne on slender stalks. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Its fibrous root system helps it retain moisture in arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native range.
