Paepalanthus chlorocephalus

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that form a rosette, creating a water-holding structure that supports its epiphytic or terrestrial growth. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to high-altitude environments with cool, humid climates.
