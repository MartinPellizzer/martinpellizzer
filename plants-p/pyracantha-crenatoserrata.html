Pyracantha crenatoserrata

Pyracantha crenatoserrata, commonly known as the Chinese firethorn, is a shrub native to China and neighboring regions.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, orange-red berries that persist into winter. The leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hedging and ornamental planting.

The plant is also valued for its fragrant flowers and winter interest, though its berries are toxic to humans and animals.
