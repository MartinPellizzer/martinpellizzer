Pomaderris oraria subsp. calcicola

Pomaderris oraria subsp.

calcicola, commonly known as the coastal pomaderris, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along coastal areas.

This subspecies is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
