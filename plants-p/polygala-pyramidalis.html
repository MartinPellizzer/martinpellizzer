Polygala pyramidalis

Polygala pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramid milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring erect, branched stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Polygala pyramidalis is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
