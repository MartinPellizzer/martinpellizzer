Prunus × subhirtella f. ogawana

Prunus × subhirtella f. ogawana, commonly known as the Snowsheath cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus, noted for its ornamental value.

It is a hybrid between Prunus subhirtella and Prunus × yedoensis, resulting in a unique combination of traits. The plant is characterized by its white flowers that appear in dense clusters, often surrounded by a whitish sheath, giving it its common name. It is a deciduous tree or shrub with a compact growth habit and is prized for its winter interest.

Botanically, it exhibits characteristics typical of the Prunus family, including simple, alternate leaves and stone fruits.
