Polycodium oblongum

Polycodium oblongum, commonly known as the broadleaf polycodium, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a robust, upright stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
