Pterostylis cernua

Pterostylis cernua, commonly known as the drooping greenhood, is an orchid species native to eastern Australia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a slender, often reddish stem and a single, erect, lance-shaped leaf. The plant produces a single, greenish-yellow flower with a distinctive hood-like structure, typical of the Orchidaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys.

The species is noted for its small size and delicate appearance, making it a rare and sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
