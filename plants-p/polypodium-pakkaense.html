Polypodium pakkaense

Polypodium pakkaense, commonly known as the Pakkanese water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or tree trunks.

Polypodium pakkaense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
