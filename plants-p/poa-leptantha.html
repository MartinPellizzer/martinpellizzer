Poa leptantha

Poa leptantha, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in open woodlands. Poa leptantha is a perennial species that produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers.

Its botanical features make it an important component of grassland ecosystems and a subject of study in ecological and botanical research.
