Paepalanthus elongatus var. major

Paepalanthus elongatus var. major, commonly known as the tall bristly burr grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, bristly foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in grasslands and disturbed areas. Its morphology includes elongated, stiff leaves and a compact growth habit, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

The species is noted for its ability to form dense mats, which can contribute to soil stabilization in its native habitat.
