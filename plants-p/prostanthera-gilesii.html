Prostanthera gilesii

Prostanthera gilesii, commonly known as the Grey Mulla Mulla, is a small shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sand dunes, preferring well-drained soils. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue or purple, and they bloom from late winter to early spring.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
