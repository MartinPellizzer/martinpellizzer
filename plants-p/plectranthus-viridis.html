Plectranthus viridis

Plectranthus viridis, commonly known as the creeping fig or green plectranthus, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a versatile choice for gardens and container planting.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
