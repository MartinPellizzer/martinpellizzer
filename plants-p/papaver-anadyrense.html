Papaver anadyrense

Papaver anadyrense, commonly known as the Anadyrense poppy, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange petals and a prominent, rounded seed capsule. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
