Polyachyrus uniflorus

Polyachyrus uniflorus, commonly known as the single-flower chrysalis, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate foliage and a single, strikingly colored flower that blooms once a year. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its botanical significance lies in its limited distribution and distinctive floral morphology, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires careful preservation efforts.
