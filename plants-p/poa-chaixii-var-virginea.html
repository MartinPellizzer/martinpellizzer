Poa chaixii var. virginea

Poa chaixii var. virginea, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a flowering plant species belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, where it typically grows in open, moist habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which contain small, two-lobed florets.

Poa chaixii var. virginea is often found in association with other grasses and forbs, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
