Populus hispida

Populus hispida, commonly known as the hispid poplar, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed trunk and lanceolate leaves that are hairy on both sides, giving it a distinctive texture. The tree produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Populus hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in rocky environments.
