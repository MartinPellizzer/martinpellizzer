Peperomia matagalpensis

Peperomia matagalpensis, commonly known as the Matagalpa peperomia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Central America, particularly found in Costa Rica and Panama. The plant is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with silver or white markings. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color.

Peperomia matagalpensis is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
