Passiflora obscura

Passiflora obscura, commonly known as the dark passionflower, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but not commonly cultivated for consumption. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough, hairy texture, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Passiflora obscura is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential calming properties.
