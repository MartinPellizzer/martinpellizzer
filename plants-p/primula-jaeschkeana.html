Primula jaeschkeana

Primula jaeschkeana, commonly known as the jaeschkean primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding a splash of color to alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture, which helps it retain moisture in its high-altitude habitat.

Primula jaeschkeana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
