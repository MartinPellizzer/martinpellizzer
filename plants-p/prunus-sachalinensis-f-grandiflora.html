Prunus sachalinensis f. grandiflora

Prunus sachalinensis f. grandiflora, commonly known as the Sakhalin cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus native to the Korean Peninsula and Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its foliage consists of oval, serrated leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its floral display.
