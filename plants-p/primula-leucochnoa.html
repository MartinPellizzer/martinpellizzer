Primula leucochnoa

Primula leucochnoa, commonly known as the white-flowered primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate with a hairy underside, and its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a loose cluster. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to bloom in early spring.
