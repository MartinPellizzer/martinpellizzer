Panicum octonodum

Panicum octonodum, commonly known as the eight-node grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many grass species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as savannas and disturbed areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening or as a forage crop.
