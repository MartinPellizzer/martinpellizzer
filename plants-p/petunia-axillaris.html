Petunia axillaris

Petunia axillaris, commonly known as the forest petunia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant typically blooms throughout the year, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

Petunia axillaris thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
