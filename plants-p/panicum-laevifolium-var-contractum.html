Panicum laevifolium var. contractum

Panicum laevifolium var. contractum, commonly known as narrowleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and floodplain areas. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often folded in a V-shape. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, pale green to golden spikelets.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
