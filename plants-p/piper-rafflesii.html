Piper rafflesii

Piper rafflesii, commonly known as the Raffles' pepper or the Bornean pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in local cuisines for their spicy flavor.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments with high rainfall and partial shade.
