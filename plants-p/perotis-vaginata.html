Perotis vaginata

Perotis vaginata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Perotis vaginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
