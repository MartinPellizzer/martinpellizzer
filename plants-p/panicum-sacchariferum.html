Panicum sacchariferum

Panicum sacchariferum, commonly known as sweet grass, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fluffy, silver-tan seed heads that resemble small clouds. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family. Sweet grass is often used in traditional crafts and has a pleasant, sweet aroma when dried.

It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshy areas along the edges of lakes and rivers.
