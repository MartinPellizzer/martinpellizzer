Parrycactus reppenhagenii

Parrycactus reppenhagenii, commonly known as the "Papalote" or "Hibiscus cactus," is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often resembling those of the hibiscus. Its foliage consists of sparse, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Parrycactus reppenhagenii is well adapted to full sun and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
