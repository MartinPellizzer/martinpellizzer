Paspalum rigens

Paspalum rigens, commonly known as rigid grass or rigid paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough texture, and it is often found in wetland or disturbed soils.

Paspalum rigens is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions, making it a common species in both natural and agricultural settings.
