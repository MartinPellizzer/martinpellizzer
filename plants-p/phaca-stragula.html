Phaca stragula

Phaca stragula, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in damp meadows and woodland edges. The plant features dark green, slightly hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It is known for its strong, aromatic scent and has been used historically in traditional medicine.

However, it is toxic to both humans and animals if ingested.
