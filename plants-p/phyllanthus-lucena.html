Phyllanthus lucena

Phyllanthus lucena, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright orange to red berries. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for its antioxidant properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
