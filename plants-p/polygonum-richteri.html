Polygonum richteri

Polygonum richteri, commonly known as the Richter's knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features rhizomatous growth, enabling it to spread vegetatively and form dense colonies. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a rough texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red.

Polygonum richteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous areas.
