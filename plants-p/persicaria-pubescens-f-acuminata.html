Persicaria pubescens f. acuminata

Persicaria pubescens f. acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-leaved smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence, giving it a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming from late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and disturbed areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive addition to wetland gardens and a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
