Penstemon deustus subsp. variabilis

Penstemon deustus subsp.

variabilis, commonly known as the variable penstemon, is a perennial plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
