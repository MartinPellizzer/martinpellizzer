Peperomia americana

Peperomia americana, commonly known as the American peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically oval or elliptic in shape, with a glossy surface and a variety of colors ranging from light green to deep purple. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia americana is relatively low maintenance, requiring minimal water and tolerating a range of indoor conditions.
