Pyrus prunifolia var. oviformis

Pyrus prunifolia var. oviformis, commonly known as the oval-leaf pear, is a variant of the pear tree characterized by its distinctive ovate-shaped leaves.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of eastern Asia, particularly China. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet fruits in late summer. Its foliage is often noted for its smooth, glossy texture and deep green color.

This variety is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its fruiting potential in suitable climates.
