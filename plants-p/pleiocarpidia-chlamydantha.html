Pleiocarpidia chlamydantha

Pleiocarpidia chlamydantha, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.
