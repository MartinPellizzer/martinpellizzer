Potentilla crassa

Potentilla crassa, commonly known as rough cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant features a bushy growth habit with multiple stems that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are palmately divided into five lobes, and it produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers in the summer.

Potentilla crassa is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
