Piptatherum virescens

Piptatherum virescens, commonly known as green needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, meadows, and sandy soils. The plant features slender, leafy stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a bluish-green hue. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are small, clustered flower clusters.

Piptatherum virescens plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native range.
