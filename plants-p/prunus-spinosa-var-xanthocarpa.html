Prunus spinosa var. xanthocarpa

Prunus spinosa var. xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-fruited blackthorn, is a variant of the common blackthorn characterized by its distinctive yellow berries.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia, typically growing in hedgerows and woodland edges. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves with serrated edges and bears clusters of white flowers in early spring. Its fruit, which ripens in late summer, is notable for its bright yellow color and tart flavor.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
