Polygala amboniensis

Polygala amboniensis, commonly known as the Ambon star-flower, is a flowering plant native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
