Pitcairnia echinata var. sublaevis

Pitcairnia echinata var. sublaevis, commonly known as the velvet pitcairnia, is a bromeliad native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces striking, spiky inflorescences that can last for several months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular among bromeliad enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
