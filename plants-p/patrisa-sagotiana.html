Patrisa sagotiana

Patrisa sagotiana, commonly known as the Philippine starfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, star-shaped fruits that are typically yellow when ripe. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are glossy and oval-shaped, providing a dense canopy that offers shade.

Patrisa sagotiana is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit, which is popular in local cuisines.
