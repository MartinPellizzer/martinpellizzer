Polypodium steirolepis

Polypodium steirolepis, commonly known as the rock fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant typically grows on rocky substrates, thriving in shaded, humid environments. Its rhizome is thick and creeping, allowing it to anchor itself securely to rocky surfaces.

The fronds are usually bipinnate, with a distinctive, glossy appearance that makes it a popular ornamental plant.
