Pellaea paradoxa var. trichophora

Pellaea paradoxa var. trichophora, commonly known as the hairy fern, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small, evergreen fern characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually clustered at the base, forming a rosette-like structure.

The botanical features of this variety make it a unique and attractive addition to woodland gardens.
