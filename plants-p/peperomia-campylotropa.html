Peperomia campylotropa

Peperomia campylotropa, commonly known as the curved-stem peperomia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it typically grows in shaded, moist environments. The plant is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and glossy, oval-shaped leaves. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually borne in clusters and are followed by tiny, berry-like fruits.

Peperomia campylotropa is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
