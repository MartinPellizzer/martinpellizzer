Pitcairnia hirtzii

Pitcairnia hirtzii, commonly known as the hairy pitcairnia, is a bromeliad native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking inflorescences with showy, colorful bracts that attract pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant.
