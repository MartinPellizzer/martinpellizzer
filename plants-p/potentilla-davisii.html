Potentilla davisii

Potentilla davisii, commonly known as Davis' cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. It has compound leaves with five leaflets and produces fruit in the form of a capsule.

Davis' cinquefoil is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and vibrant blooms.
