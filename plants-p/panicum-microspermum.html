Panicum microspermum

Panicum microspermum, commonly known as littleseed panicgrass, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny seeds. The plant typically grows in full sun and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for ornamental and erosion control purposes. Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for landscaping in arid regions.

The botanical features of Panicum microspermum contribute to its adaptability and ecological significance in native plant communities.
