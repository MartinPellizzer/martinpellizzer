Pandanus lauensis

Pandanus lauensis, commonly known as the Tahitian pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its roots are fibrous and can be used for making ropes and mats.

The species is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a common sight in island ecosystems.
