Pinardia segetum

Pinardia segetum, commonly known as the field pinardia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a delicate, fern-like appearance.

Pinardia segetum is primarily cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines.
