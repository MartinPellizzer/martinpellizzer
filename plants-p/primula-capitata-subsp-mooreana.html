Primula capitata subsp. mooreana

Primula capitata subsp.

mooreana, commonly known as the Moore's primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China. It features dense clusters of small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in early spring.

The plant has hairy, dark green leaves that are oval to lance-shaped, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it forms low-growing mats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and partial shade.
