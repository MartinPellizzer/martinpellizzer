Paepalanthus polytrichoides

Paepalanthus polytrichoides, commonly known as the silky grass of the Andes, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a compact cluster, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or sandy soils, and is adapted to the cool, humid climates of the Andean region.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
