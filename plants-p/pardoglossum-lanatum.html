Pardoglossum lanatum

Pardoglossum lanatum, commonly known as the hairy lily, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with a velvety texture. The leaves are broad and ovate, often covered in a fine, hairy coating that gives the plant its scientific name. This species thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable subject in botanical studies.
