Pyramidium

Pyramidium, commonly known as the pyramid flower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, pyramid-shaped inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with a fine pubescence.

Pyramidium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
