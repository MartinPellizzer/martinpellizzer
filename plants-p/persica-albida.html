Persica albida

Persica albida, commonly known as the white peach tree, is a species of peach native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and small, sweet, yellowish fruits. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval-shaped with a smooth margin. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Persica albida is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, making it a popular choice in both gardens and orchards.
