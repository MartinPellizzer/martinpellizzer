Psiadia boivinii

Psiadia boivinii, commonly known as the Boivin's daisy, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in a compact cluster. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Psiadia boivinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
