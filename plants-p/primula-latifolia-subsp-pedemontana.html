Primula latifolia subsp. pedemontana

Primula latifolia subsp.

pedemontana, commonly known as the Alpine primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations.

The plant features broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and a showy display in early spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in alpine ecosystems.
