Primula auricula subsp. hungarica

Primula auricula subsp.

hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian earleaf primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or limestone soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
