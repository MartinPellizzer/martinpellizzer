Prunus persica var. potaninii

Prunus persica var. potaninii, commonly known as the Chinese sand pear, is a variety of the common pear that is native to China.

It is characterized by its small, round fruits with a yellowish-orange skin and a sweet, juicy flesh. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated margin.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for both its fruit and aesthetic qualities.
