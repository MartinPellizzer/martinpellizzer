Polemonium carneum

Polemonium carneum, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polemoniaceae and is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are spurred at the base, a feature that distinguishes it from other similar species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
