Protea pudens

Protea pudens, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of flowers, with a central cone-like structure surrounded by bracts. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils, showcasing the genus' ability to thrive in challenging environments. Its leaves are leathery and often covered in a fine, silvery coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Protea pudens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
