Parkia cachimboensis

Parkia cachimboensis, commonly known as the Amazonian cacao tree, is a species native to the rainforests of South America.

It is a leguminous tree that produces small, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. The plant has compound leaves with several leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary growth forests.

The tree is valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional culinary and medicinal practices.
