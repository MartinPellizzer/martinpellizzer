Plectrachne bromoides

Plectrachne bromoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Plectrachne bromoides is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic environments.
