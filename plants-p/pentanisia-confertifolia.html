Pentanisia confertifolia

Pentanisia confertifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved pentanisia, is a member of the Ericaceae family, typically found in temperate regions.

It is characterized by its compact shrub form and dense, leathery foliage that provides habitat for various small organisms. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in the spring. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, reflecting its ability to thrive in acidic environments.

The leaves are evergreen, offering year-round structure and contributing to the plant's ecological role in forest understories.
