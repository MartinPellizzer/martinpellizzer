Pleurothallis cuneifolia var. maculata

Pleurothallis cuneifolia var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted leaf orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often marked with distinctive spots or streaks, giving it its varietal name. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a variety of colors including white, yellow, and pink.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
