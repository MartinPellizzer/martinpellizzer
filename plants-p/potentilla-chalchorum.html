Potentilla chalchorum

Potentilla chalchorum, commonly known as the yellow cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Potentilla chalchorum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
