Potentilla coreana

Potentilla coreana, commonly known as Korean cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Korea.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately divided into five oval-shaped lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla coreana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland areas.
