Phaseolus rostratus

Phaseolus rostratus, commonly known as the rostratus bean, is a species of legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated pods containing several seeds. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both agricultural and ecological studies.

The species is also valued for its potential in sustainable farming practices due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
