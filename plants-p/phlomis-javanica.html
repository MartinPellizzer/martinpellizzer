Phlomis javanica

Phlomis javanica, commonly known as the Java phlomis, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom during the summer months.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
