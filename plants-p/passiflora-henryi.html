Passiflora henryi

Passiflora henryi, commonly known as Henry's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a crown of filaments and a distinctive central structure resembling a heart. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor.

Passiflora henryi thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.
