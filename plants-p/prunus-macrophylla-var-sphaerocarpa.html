Prunus macrophylla var. sphaerocarpa

Prunus macrophylla var. sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and its clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible but often tart. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
