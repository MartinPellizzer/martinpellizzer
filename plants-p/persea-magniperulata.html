Persea magniperulata

Persea magniperulata, commonly known as the giant persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The tree produces small, white flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that are edible. Its bark is rough and fissured, and it can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
