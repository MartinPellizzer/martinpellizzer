Potentilla verna var. amansiana

Potentilla verna var. amansiana, commonly known as the spring potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and is characterized by its upright, often branched stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, usually between March and May. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
