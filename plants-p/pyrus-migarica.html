Pyrus migarica

Pyrus migarica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pear.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae, native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and white, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, sweet pears that ripen in late summer.

Pyrus migarica is typically found in rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
