Paspalum plicatulum subsp. guenoarum

Paspalum plicatulum subsp.

guenoarum, commonly known as the Guenoarum grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves with a slight sheen.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in wetland and marshy environments, often found in association with standing water.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in ecological and botanical research.
