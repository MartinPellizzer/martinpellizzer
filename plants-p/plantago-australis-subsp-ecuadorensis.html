Plantago australis subsp. ecuadorensis

Plantago australis subsp.

ecuadorensis, commonly known as the Andean plantain, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Ecuador. It is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces erect flowering stems bearing dense clusters of small, greenish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
