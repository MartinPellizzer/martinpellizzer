Palaquium macrocarpum

Palaquium macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited palaquium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and dense, glossy green foliage. The plant produces large, edible fruits that are typically orange or yellow in color, with a sweet, sticky pulp. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in local cuisines.
