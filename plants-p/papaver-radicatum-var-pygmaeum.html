Papaver radicatum var. pygmaeum

Papaver radicatum var. pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf poppy, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, nodding flowers that are typically red or orange in color, with a distinctive, saucer-shaped corolla. The plant has a short, compact growth habit, with hairy stems and leaves that are lanceolate in shape. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and visually striking addition to rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
