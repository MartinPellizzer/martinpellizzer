Potentilla glandulosa var. lactea

Potentilla glandulosa var. lactea, commonly known as the milkweed potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically oval or lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
