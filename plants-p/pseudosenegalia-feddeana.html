Pseudosenegalia feddeana

Pseudosenegalia feddeana, commonly known as the desert fan palm, is a shrub-like plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fan-shaped clusters of leaves that provide shade and habitat for various desert animals. The plant has a thick, woody stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its small, yellow flowers are followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are consumed by both wildlife and humans.

Pseudosenegalia feddeana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
