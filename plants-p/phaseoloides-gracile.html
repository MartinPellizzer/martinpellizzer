Phaseoloides gracile

Phaseoloides gracile, commonly known as the slender bean vine, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has slender, climbing stems with trifoliate leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in agroforestry systems.
