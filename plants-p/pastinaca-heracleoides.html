Pastinaca heracleoides

Pastinaca heracleoides, commonly known as the giant burdock, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and tall, spiky flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into woody, bur-like fruits. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine.

Pastinaca heracleoides thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in meadows.
