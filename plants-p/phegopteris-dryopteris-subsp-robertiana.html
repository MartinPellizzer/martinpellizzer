Phegopteris dryopteris subsp. robertiana

Phegopteris dryopteris subsp.

robertiana, commonly known as the Robert's wood fern, is a fern species native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its delicate, pinnately compound fronds that are typically light green with a slightly serrated margin.

The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in kidney-shaped groups. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable component of temperate forest ecosystems.
