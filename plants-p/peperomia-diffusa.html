Peperomia diffusa

Peperomia diffusa, commonly known as the velvet leaf peperomia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a compact, low-growing plant with thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. The leaves are typically heart-shaped or ovate, and they have a velvety texture that gives the plant its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are followed by tiny, round berries.

This plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its tolerance for low light and minimal water requirements.
