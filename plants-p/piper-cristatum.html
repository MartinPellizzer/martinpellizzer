Piper cristatum

Piper cristatum, commonly known as the curly pepper or cristate pepper, is a tropical climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, curling leaves that give it its common name. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by elongated, tubular fruits that contain a single seed.

Piper cristatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
