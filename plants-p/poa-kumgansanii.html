Poa kumgansanii

Poa kumgansanii, commonly known as the Korean bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Korea.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and tufted growth habit, often forming dense mats in its native habitat. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences, and it produces slender, spikelet-based flowers.

Poa kumgansanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
