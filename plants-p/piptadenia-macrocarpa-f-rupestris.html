Piptadenia macrocarpa f. rupestris

Piptadenia macrocarpa f. rupestris, commonly known as the rock piptadenia, is a distinctive plant native to Brazil.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its rough, fissured bark and compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, edible seeds. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and research settings.
