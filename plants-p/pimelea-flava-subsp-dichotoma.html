Pimelea flava subsp. dichotoma

Pimelea flava subsp.

dichotoma, commonly known as the yellow mat-rush, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, mat-like growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in coastal and heathland environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
