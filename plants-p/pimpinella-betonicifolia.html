Pimpinella betonicifolia

Pimpinella betonicifolia, commonly known as the betony leaf angelica, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and white to pale green, arranged in compound umbels, and it produces small, round fruits known as schizocarps.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
