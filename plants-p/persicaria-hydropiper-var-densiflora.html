Persicaria hydropiper var. densiflora

Persicaria hydropiper var. densiflora, commonly known as water pepper, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas and moist soils.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in environments with consistent moisture.

This variant is often found in marshes, stream banks, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
