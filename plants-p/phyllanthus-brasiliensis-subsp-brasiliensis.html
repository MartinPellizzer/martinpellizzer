Phyllanthus brasiliensis subsp. brasiliensis

Phyllanthus brasiliensis subsp.

brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian spurge, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
