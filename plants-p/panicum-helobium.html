Panicum helobium

Panicum helobium, commonly known as the water panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It thrives in saturated soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green coloration.

Panicum helobium plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
