Pouteria macahensis

Pouteria macahensis, commonly known as the Mexican sapote, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces a fleshy, yellow-orange fruit with a creamy, sweet pulp that is often used in culinary applications. Its growth habit includes a straight trunk and can reach heights of up to 20 meters in its natural habitat.

Pouteria macahensis is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
