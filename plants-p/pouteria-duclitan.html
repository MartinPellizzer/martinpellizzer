Pouteria duclitan

Pouteria duclitan, commonly known as the Duclitan sapodilla, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elongated, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, sweet, and sometimes gritty fruits. Its fruit, similar to a sapodilla, is valued for its unique flavor and nutritional content.

The plant is also noted for its potential in traditional medicine and as a source of natural latex.
