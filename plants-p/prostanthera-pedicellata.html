Prostanthera pedicellata

Prostanthera pedicellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mintbush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a fragrant, mint-like aroma.

Prostanthera pedicellata is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and inland environments.
