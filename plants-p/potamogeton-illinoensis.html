Potamogeton illinoensis

Potamogeton illinoensis, commonly known as Illinois pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically found in the water, and it is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats. Illinois pondweed plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

It is also used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize sediments and improve water quality.
