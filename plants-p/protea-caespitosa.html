Protea caespitosa

Protea caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted protea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit. The plant produces striking, cone-shaped inflorescences that are often covered in bracts resembling petals. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and red, and are adapted for attracting pollinators like birds and insects.

Protea caespitosa is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
