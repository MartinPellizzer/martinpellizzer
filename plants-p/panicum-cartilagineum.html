Panicum cartilagineum

Panicum cartilagineum, commonly known as the cartilage grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as savannas and disturbed sites.

Its botanical features make it an important component of certain ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
