Pavetta montana f. lanceolata

Pavetta montana f. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate pavetta, is a variant of the Pavetta montana species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, often with a faint fragrance. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dry seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
