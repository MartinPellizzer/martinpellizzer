Potentilla aperta var. canina

Potentilla aperta var. canina, commonly known as the open potentilla or dog's rose, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval to elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
