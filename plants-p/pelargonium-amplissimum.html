Pelargonium amplissimum

Pelargonium amplissimum, commonly known as the giant geranium, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range from pink to red, often with a distinctive white or yellow center. The plant features broad, lobed leaves that are often hairy, giving it a rugged appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
