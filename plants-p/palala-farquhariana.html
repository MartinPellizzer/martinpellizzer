Palala farquhariana

Palala farquhariana, commonly known as the Farquhar's ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals and a unique, elongated flower structure that sets it apart from other orchid species. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific soil conditions rich in organic matter. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and is often found in protected reserves.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator of undisturbed ecosystems and its potential for scientific research into plant adaptation.
