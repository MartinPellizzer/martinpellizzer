Potentilla gordonii var. wasatchensis

Potentilla gordonii var. wasatchensis, commonly known as the Wasatch potentilla, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with numerous small, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has finely divided, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental plant adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
