Piper reductipes

Piper reductipes, commonly known as the red-stemmed pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes many species of flowering plants in the family Piperaceae. The plant is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
