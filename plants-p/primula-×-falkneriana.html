Primula × falkneriana

Primula × falkneriana, commonly known as the Falkner's primrose, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Primula vulgaris and Primula obconica.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. Its foliage consists of oval, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a bedding plant.
