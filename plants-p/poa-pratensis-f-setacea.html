Poa pratensis f. setacea

Poa pratensis f. setacea, commonly known as the tufted bentgrass, is a variant of the common bentgrass species.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and spikelets that are arranged in a compact, cylindrical inflorescence.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass management.
