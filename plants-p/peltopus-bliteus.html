Peltopus bliteus

Peltopus bliteus, commonly known as the silverleaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its succulent, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to harsh environments.

However, it is considered invasive in some areas due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
