Plantaginorchis cephalotes

Plantaginorchis cephalotes, commonly known as the helmet orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, helmet-shaped flower that resembles a small skull. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, humid conditions with ample sunlight. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, greenish-white blooms.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered endangered and is often protected in conservation programs.
