Polystichum pseudomakinoi

Polystichum pseudomakinoi, commonly known as the Japanese shield fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in sori covered by indusia. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Polystichum pseudomakinoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its elegant foliage.
