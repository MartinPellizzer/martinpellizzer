Panicum hirticaule var. verrucosum

Panicum hirticaule var. verrucosum, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass variant with warty features, is a grass species characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit.

It typically features fine, hairy leaves that are often covered with small, wart-like structures, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which are usually pale green to reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
