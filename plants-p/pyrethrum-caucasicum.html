Pyrethrum caucasicum

Pyrethrum caucasicum, commonly known as Caucasian pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant produces fine, hair-like structures called trichomes, which contain the natural insecticides pyrethrins. These compounds make it highly effective against a variety of pests.

Pyrethrum caucasicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of biopesticides.
