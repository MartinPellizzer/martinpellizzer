Pandanus pallidus

Pandanus pallidus, commonly known as the pale pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into fibrous, coconut-like fruits. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in coastal and sandy soils.

Pandanus pallidus plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
