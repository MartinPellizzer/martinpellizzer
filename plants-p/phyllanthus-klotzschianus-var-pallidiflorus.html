Phyllanthus klotzschianus var. pallidiflorus

Phyllanthus klotzschianus var. pallidiflorus, commonly known as the paleflower phyllanthus, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
