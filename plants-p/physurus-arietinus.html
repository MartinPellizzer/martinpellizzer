Physurus arietinus

Physurus arietinus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant blooms in shades of purple, pink, and white, which appear on tall, slender stems. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Physurus arietinus is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
