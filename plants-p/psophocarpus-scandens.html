Psophocarpus scandens

Psophocarpus scandens, commonly known as the sweet pea vine, is a climbing legume native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets and produces vibrant, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant is notable for its edible, seed-bearing pods that mature into a sweet, nutty flavor. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a woody stem that supports its climbing growth habit.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for ornamental and culinary purposes.
