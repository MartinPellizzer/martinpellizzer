Pseudomuscari chalusicum

Pseudomuscari chalusicum, commonly known as the Persian grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

Pseudomuscari chalusicum is relatively low maintenance and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
