Potentilla alaskana

Potentilla alaskana, commonly known as Alaska cinquefoil, is a low-growing shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America.

It typically reaches a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has wiry, angular stems and pinnately compound leaves with five leaflets, giving it its common name. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in tundra and alpine environments.

Potentilla alaskana is known for its hardiness and ability to survive in extreme cold conditions.
