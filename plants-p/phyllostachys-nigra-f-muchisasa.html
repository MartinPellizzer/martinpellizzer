Phyllostachys nigra f. muchisasa

Phyllostachys nigra f. muchisasa, commonly known as the black bamboo, is a striking ornamental bamboo species characterized by its dark, almost black culms that contrast sharply with its bright green, lance-shaped leaves.

It is a clumping bamboo, forming dense stands that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a popular choice for privacy screens and garden accents. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, preferring temperate climates with mild winters.

Its foliage remains lush throughout the growing season, providing year-round visual interest in landscaped settings.
