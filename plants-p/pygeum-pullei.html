Pygeum pullei

Pygeum pullei, commonly known as the African knobthorn, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and dense foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, followed by woody, cone-like fruits containing several seeds. Pygeum pullei is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
