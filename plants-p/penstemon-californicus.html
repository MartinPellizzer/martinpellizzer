Penstemon californicus

Penstemon californicus, commonly known as the California beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in coastal areas and foothills. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-tolerant nature.

Penstemon californicus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
