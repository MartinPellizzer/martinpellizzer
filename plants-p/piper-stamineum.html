Piper stamineum

Piper stamineum, commonly known as long pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, peppercorn-like fruits that grow in clusters. The plant produces thin, elongated berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface with a strong, spicy aroma.

The plant is cultivated for its aromatic fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
