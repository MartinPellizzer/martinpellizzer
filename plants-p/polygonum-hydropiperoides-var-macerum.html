Polygonum hydropiperoides var. macerum

Polygonum hydropiperoides var. macerum, commonly known as water pepper, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It features slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color and covered with small, translucent glands. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, stream banks, and other waterlogged habitats.
