Prunus × lannesiana f. ohsibayama

Prunus × lannesiana f. ohsibayama, commonly known as the Ohsibayama cherry, is a unique ornamental plant prized for its striking floral display.

It is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus, specifically a cross between Prunus lannesiana and Prunus incisa. The plant is characterized by its large, double white flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a dramatic visual impact to gardens. Its foliage is dark green and dense, providing an attractive backdrop during the growing season.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in horticultural settings due to its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various climates.
