Paspalum eriostachyum

Paspalum eriostachyum, commonly known as coastal paspalum, is a perennial grass native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to saline and sandy soils, making it a common species in dune ecosystems.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various coastal wildlife.
