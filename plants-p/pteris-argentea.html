Pteris argentea

Pteris argentea, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender fronds that are typically arched or curved, with a silvery sheen on the underside. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to regions in Asia, including China, Japan, and India. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce roots that help anchor it to rocky or forested substrates.

Pteris argentea is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in indoor settings.
