Poa nyaradyana

Poa nyaradyana, commonly known as the Nyaradya grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, with each spikelet containing two to three florets. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in moist, temperate environments.

Poa nyaradyana is typically found in native grasslands and along streambanks, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
