Peperomia pumila

Peperomia pumila, commonly known as the baby rubber plant, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically light green with a waxy texture, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish in color, which are not particularly showy. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its slow growth rate and ability to tolerate low light conditions make it an easy plant to care for.
