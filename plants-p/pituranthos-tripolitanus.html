Pituranthos tripolitanus

Pituranthos tripolitanus, commonly known as the Tripolitan dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are basal, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

It produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy seed heads, facilitating wind dispersal of its seeds.
