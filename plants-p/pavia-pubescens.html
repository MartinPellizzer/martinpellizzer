Pavia pubescens

Pavia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy pavia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Rhamnaceae.

It is characterized by its woody stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Pavia pubescens is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.
