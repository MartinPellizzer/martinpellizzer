Prunus maritima var. caerulea-magna

Prunus maritima var. caerulea-magna, commonly known as the Carolina cherrybark oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a pointed tip. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, dark blue to black berries. Its acorns are encased in a cupule and are an important food source for wildlife.

The species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in upland forests.
