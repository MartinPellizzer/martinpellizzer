Pyrola bifolia

Pyrola bifolia, commonly known as the twinflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its low-growing, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, often appearing in pairs on the stem, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, acidic soils in shaded forest environments.

Pyrola bifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
