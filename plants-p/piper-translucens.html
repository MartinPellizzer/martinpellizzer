Piper translucens

Piper translucens, commonly known as the translucent pepper or glassy pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, translucent hairs. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, and it produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its unique appearance and has been used in traditional medicine and horticulture.
