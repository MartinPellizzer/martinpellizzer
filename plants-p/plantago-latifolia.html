Plantago latifolia

Plantago latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
