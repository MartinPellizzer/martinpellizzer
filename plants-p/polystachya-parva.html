Polystachya parva

Polystachya parva, commonly known as the small-bracted sword fern, is a species of flowering plant in the family Polystachyaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Polystachya parva is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate various soil conditions.
