Polygala vatkeana

Polygala vatkeana, commonly known as the Indian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a unique structure that resembles a spurred petal.

Polygala vatkeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
