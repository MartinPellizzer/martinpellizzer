Pottingeria acuminata var. acuminata

Pottingeria acuminata var. acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed marsh hibiscus, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland areas in eastern North America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink to red in color, with five reflexed lobes. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, sandy soils. It is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along streams.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
