Paphiopedilum × madiotanum

Paphiopedilum × madiotanum, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Paphiopedilum suaveolens and Paphiopedilum hirsutum.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its sympodial growth habit and long, narrow pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive lip that often resembles a face, a feature typical of the Paphiopedilum genus. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its beauty and unique floral structure.
