Prunus jamasakura f. suaveolens

Prunus jamasakura f. suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus jamasakura species, which is a type of flowering cherry.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that emit a delicate, sweet fragrance, making it particularly attractive to pollinators. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with dark green, ovate leaves. Its blooming period occurs in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens and landscapes.

The cultivar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often planted for its visual and olfactory contributions to the environment.
