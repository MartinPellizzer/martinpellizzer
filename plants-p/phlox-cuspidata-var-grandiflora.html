Phlox cuspidata var. grandiflora

Phlox cuspidata var. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to lavender. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and blooms in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is aromatic and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
