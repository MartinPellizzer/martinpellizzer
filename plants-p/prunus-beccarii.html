Prunus beccarii

Prunus beccarii, commonly known as the Bornean cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, followed by edible berries that are red when mature. Prunus beccarii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit and ecological role make it an important component of the tropical rainforest ecosystem.
