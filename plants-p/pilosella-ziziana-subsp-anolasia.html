Pilosella ziziana subsp. anolasia

Pilosella ziziana subsp.

anolasia, commonly known as the alpine pilosella, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white rays.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. It has a compact growth habit with hairy leaves that help protect it from harsh weather conditions.

This subspecies is often found in mountain meadows and is adapted to cold, nutrient-poor environments.
