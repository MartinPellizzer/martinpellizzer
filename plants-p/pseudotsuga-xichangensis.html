Pseudotsuga xichangensis

Pseudotsuga xichangensis, commonly known as the Xichang fir, is a rare conifer native to southwestern China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The tree produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of coniferous species. Its foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged spirally along the branches.

This species plays an important role in the ecological systems of its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
