Pitcairnia atrorubens var. lamarcheana

Pitcairnia atrorubens var. lamarcheana, commonly known as the red-leaved pitcairnia, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage, which contrasts with its dark green central rosette. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
