Persicaria emodi

Persicaria emodi, commonly known as the Japanese knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features tall, hollow stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often with reddish or purplish hues. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Persicaria emodi is known for its rapid growth and invasive potential in non-native regions.
