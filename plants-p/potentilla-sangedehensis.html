Potentilla sangedehensis

Potentilla sangedehensis, commonly known as the Sangedeh cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, aggregate fruits known as achenes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
