Podocarpus matudae var. macrocarpus

Podocarpus matudae var. macrocarpus, commonly known as the giant podocarp, is a tall evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and conical shape. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain edible seeds, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its slow growth rate and longevity make it a valuable species for reforestation and conservation efforts.

The plant also has cultural and traditional uses in some regions.
