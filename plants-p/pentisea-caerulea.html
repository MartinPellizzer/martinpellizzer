Pentisea caerulea

Pentisea caerulea, commonly known as the blue pentisea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy surface.

The botanical significance of Pentisea caerulea lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
