Pelargonium × galatheae

Pelargonium × galatheae, commonly known as the ivy-leaf geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and a trailing growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, typically blooming from spring to autumn. Native to South Africa, it is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.

Its foliage and flowers make it a popular choice for hanging baskets, window boxes, and garden borders.
