Phylica myrtifolia

Phylica myrtifolia, commonly known as the sweet thorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces yellowish-orange fruits that are edible and often consumed by birds and mammals. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Phylica myrtifolia is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
