Phyllanthus phillyreifolius var. crassistigma

Phyllanthus phillyreifolius var. crassistigma, commonly known as the broad-stigma phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves with a distinctive broad-based petiole, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
