Priestleya sieberi

Priestleya sieberi, commonly known as the Sieber's priestleya, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the Atlantic Forest biome. Its flowers are often brightly colored, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
