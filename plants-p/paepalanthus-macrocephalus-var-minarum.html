Paepalanthus macrocephalus var. minarum

Paepalanthus macrocephalus var. minarum, commonly known as the small-headed puya, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to the Andean region of South America.

This plant is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater for nourishment. It produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing numerous small, colorful flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
