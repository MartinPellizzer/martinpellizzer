Pelargonium capense

Pelargonium capense, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its succulent, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium capense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
