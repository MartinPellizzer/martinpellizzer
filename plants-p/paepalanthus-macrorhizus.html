Paepalanthus macrorhizus

Paepalanthus macrorhizus, commonly known as the giant root palm, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy roots that can grow up to several meters in length, which store water and nutrients. The plant has a rosette of broad, waxy leaves that form a dense canopy, protecting the root system. It produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, spiky fruits.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value and ecological interest.
