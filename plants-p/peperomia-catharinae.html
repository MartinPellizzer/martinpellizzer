Peperomia catharinae

Peperomia catharinae, commonly known as the Catina peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the humid forests of Brazil.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with some varieties displaying variegation. The plant produces delicate, spathaceous flowers in shades of white or pale pink, which are often hidden beneath its foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia catharinae is prized for its ornamental value and ease of care, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
