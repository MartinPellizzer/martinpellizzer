Pleurothallis telamon

Pleurothallis telamon, commonly known as the silver-veined orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features slender, wiry stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
