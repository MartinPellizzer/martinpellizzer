Phylica divaricata

Phylica divaricata, commonly known as the silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and produces small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant is evergreen, with leathery leaves that provide year-round cover. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky areas.

Phylica divaricata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine.
