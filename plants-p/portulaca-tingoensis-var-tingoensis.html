Portulaca tingoensis var. tingoensis

Portulaca tingoensis var. tingoensis, commonly known as the Tingoense Portulaca, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Peru and Bolivia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with fleshy, succulent leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight. It has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative ground cover.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in dry conditions, showcasing its adaptation to harsh climates.
