Piper cativalense

Piper cativalense, commonly known as the cativalense pepper plant, is a species of flowering vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as the common black pepper. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in local remedies for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
