Plerandra polydactylis

Plerandra polydactylis, commonly known as the golden bird of paradise, is a striking tropical plant native to New Guinea and the surrounding islands.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow bracts that resemble a bird in flight. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers clustered within a spadix, which is surrounded by the showy spathe. Plerandra polydactylis thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor cultivation.
