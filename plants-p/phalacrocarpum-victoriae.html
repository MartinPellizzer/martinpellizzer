Phalacrocarpum victoriae

Phalacrocarpum victoriae, commonly known as the Victoria's sea holly, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact, mat-forming growth habit with silver-gray foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a desirable ornamental plant.
