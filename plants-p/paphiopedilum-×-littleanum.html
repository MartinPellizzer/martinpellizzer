Paphiopedilum × littleanum

Paphiopedilum × littleanum, commonly known as the Little Annie orchid, is a hybrid of the genus Paphiopedilum, characterized by its unique and intricate floral structure.

It features a lip that is often modified into a specialized structure for pollination, a hallmark of orchid morphology. The plant typically produces flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, with a velvety texture that enhances its ornamental appeal. It is a terrestrial orchid that thrives in shaded, humid environments with well-draining soil.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a wide range of morphological variations, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
