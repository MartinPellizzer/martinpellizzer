Polystichum duthiei var. subbipinnatum

Polystichum duthiei var. subbipinnatum, commonly known as the subbipinnate wood fern, is a variant of the wood fern genus Polystichum.

It is characterized by its finely divided, bipinnate fronds that give it a delicate, lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually evergreen, with a dark green coloration and a leathery texture.

The spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds in clusters called sori, which are covered by a protective indusium.
