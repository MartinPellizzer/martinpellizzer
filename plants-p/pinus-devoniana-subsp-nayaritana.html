Pinus devoniana subsp. nayaritana

Pinus devoniana subsp.

nayaritana, commonly known as the Nayarit pine, is a subspecies of the Mexican pine native to the highlands of Nayarit and Jalisco in western Mexico. It is a medium-sized evergreen conifer characterized by its slender, upward-curving cones and needle-like leaves.

The tree typically grows in well-drained soils and at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 2,500 meters. Its cones are woody and contain two seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
