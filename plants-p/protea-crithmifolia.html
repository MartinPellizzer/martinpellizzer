Protea crithmifolia

Protea crithmifolia, commonly known as the coastal protea, is a striking shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family, which is known for its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces inflorescences composed of numerous small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in a cylindrical shape. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal climates with high salt exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
