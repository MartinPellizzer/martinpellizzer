Polylepis tomentella

Polylepis tomentella, commonly known as the Andean waxtree, is a species of flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. This tree thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests at elevations between 2,500 and 4,000 meters. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against harsh weather conditions.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various wildlife species.
