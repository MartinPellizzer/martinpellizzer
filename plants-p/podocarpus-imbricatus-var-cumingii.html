Podocarpus imbricatus var. cumingii

Podocarpus imbricatus var. cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's podocarp, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, scale-like leaves arranged in compact clusters. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain the seeds, typical of the Podocarpaceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
