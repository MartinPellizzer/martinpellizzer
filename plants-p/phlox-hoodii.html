Phlox hoodii

Phlox hoodii, commonly known as Hood's phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and lavender. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open woodlands and mountainous regions. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces seed capsules that release tiny seeds in the fall.

Phlox hoodii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
