Prunus crassipes

Prunus crassipes, commonly known as the desert peach, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by edible fruits that are similar in appearance to peaches. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Prunus crassipes is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
