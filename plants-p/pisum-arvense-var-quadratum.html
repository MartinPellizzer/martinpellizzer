Pisum arvense var. quadratum

Pisum arvense var. quadratum, commonly known as the four-angled vetch, is a variant of the common vetch, characterized by its distinctive quadrangular stems.

It is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some regions for forage or as a cover crop.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
