Pernettya prostrata var. elliptica

Pernettya prostrata var. elliptica, commonly known as the creeping cowberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to northern regions.

It features elliptical, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged in a prostrate habit. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often consumed by wildlife. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal and tundra ecosystems.

Its compact form and hardy nature make it a resilient plant in cold climates.
