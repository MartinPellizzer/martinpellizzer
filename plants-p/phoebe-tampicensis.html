Phoebe tampicensis

Phoebe tampicensis, commonly known as the Florida phoebe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and avocado. The plant typically grows in wetland areas, such as swamps and floodplains, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy coating, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

The fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy outer layer.
