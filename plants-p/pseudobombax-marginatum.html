Pseudobombax marginatum

Pseudobombax marginatum, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces woody pods that contain fibrous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture.
