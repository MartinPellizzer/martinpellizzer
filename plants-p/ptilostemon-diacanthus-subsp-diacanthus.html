Ptilostemon diacanthus subsp. diacanthus

Ptilostemon diacanthus subsp.

diacanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ptilostemon, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
