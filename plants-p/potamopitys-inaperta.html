Potamopitys inaperta

Potamopitys inaperta, commonly known as the Brazilian pine or the Paraguay pine, is a rare and critically endangered conifer native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and distinctive, scale-like leaves that are tightly pressed against the stem. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of gymnosperms. Due to its limited distribution and habitat destruction, it is highly threatened in the wild.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect and propagate this unique botanical species.
