Populus amygdalaceifolia

Populus amygdalaceifolia, commonly known as the almond-leaved poplar, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that have a distinct almond-like scent when crushed. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fluffy seed clusters. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable regions.
