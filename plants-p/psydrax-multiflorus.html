Psydrax multiflorus

Psydrax multiflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered psidium, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe, containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests or near water sources.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
