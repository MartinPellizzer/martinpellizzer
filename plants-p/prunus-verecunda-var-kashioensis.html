Prunus verecunda var. kashioensis

Prunus verecunda var. kashioensis, commonly known as the Kashio cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in late March to early April. The plant has elliptic to ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

The Kashio cherry is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of the fleeting beauty of spring in Japanese culture.
