Potamogeton lateralis f. subcompletus

Potamogeton lateralis f. subcompletus, commonly known as the complete-leaf water plantain, is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its elongated, ribbon-like leaves that are typically fully developed and unbranched.

It belongs to the family Potamogetonaceae and is native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in lakes, rivers, and wetlands. Its leaves are often coated with a thin layer of mucilage, which helps in reducing water loss and protecting against herbivores.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
