Psidium pulcherrimum

Psidium pulcherrimum, commonly known as the golden guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It produces small, yellow to orange fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a sweet, tangy flavor. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in panicles, often appearing before the fruit.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.
