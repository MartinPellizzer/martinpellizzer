Pterostylis conferta

Pterostylis conferta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf greenhood, is an orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a single leaf and a slender, often curved stem that bears a single flower. The flower has a greenish-yellow coloration with a distinctive hood-like labellum. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as rainforests and understoreys.

The plant is characterized by its specialized pollination mechanism involving myrmecophiles, or ant-dependent pollination.
