Paeonia × dmitrievae

Paeonia × dmitrievae, commonly known as the Dmitrievae peony, is a hybrid species of the peony genus native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound, trifoliate leaves that are dark green and hairy on the underside. It is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, with a robust root system.

The flowers come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and red, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
