Phlox ovata var. elatior

Phlox ovata var. elatior, commonly known as the tall phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, with slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
