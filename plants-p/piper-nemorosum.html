Piper nemorosum

Piper nemorosum, commonly known as the forest pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by berries that turn from yellow to red as they mature. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, peppery aroma.

Piper nemorosum is often found in moist, shaded areas of rainforests and is valued for its medicinal and culinary uses.
