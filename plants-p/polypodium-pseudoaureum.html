Polypodium pseudoaureum

Polypodium pseudoaureum, commonly known as the golden polypody, is a fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or on moist soil, often in shaded forest environments. Its rhizomes are wiry and can form dense clusters, while the fronds are usually pinnately divided with a golden hue on the underside.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or greenhouses.
