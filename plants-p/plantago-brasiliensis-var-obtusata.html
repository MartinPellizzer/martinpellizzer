Plantago brasiliensis var. obtusata

Plantago brasiliensis var. obtusata, commonly known as Brazilian plantain or Brazilian psyllium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

The seeds of this variety are rich in mucilage and are used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.
