Pseudarthria capitata

Pseudarthria capitata, commonly known as the bushveld grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in compact, spherical clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it is often found in grassland and savanna ecosystems.

Pseudarthria capitata is adapted to dry conditions and plays a role in soil stabilization and as forage for livestock.
