Potentilla strictissima f. turfosa

Potentilla strictissima f. turfosa, commonly known as the Turfous cinquefoil, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It features small, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a compact rosette, and it produces clusters of yellow flowers in early summer. The plant is well adapted to moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges or shaded areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in difficult conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ground cover in horticultural settings.
