Plectranthus dawoensis

Plectranthus dawoensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian mint, is a perennial herb native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus dawoensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used to treat digestive issues and respiratory ailments.
