Piper begoniicolor

Piper begoniicolor, commonly known as the Begonia-colored Piper, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its striking, begonia-like leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from deep red to purple. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in spikes.

Despite its ornamental appeal, it is not commonly cultivated for commercial purposes due to its specific growing requirements.
