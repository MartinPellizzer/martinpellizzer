Pinanga fruticans

Pinanga fruticans, commonly known as the fruiting pineapple palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its inflorescence produces small, clustered flowers, which eventually develop into edible fruits.

The plant is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential agricultural significance.
