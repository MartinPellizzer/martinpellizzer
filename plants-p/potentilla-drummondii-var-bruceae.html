Potentilla drummondii var. bruceae

Potentilla drummondii var. bruceae, commonly known as Drummond's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments. Its leaves are palmately divided into five to seven lobes, and it produces small, five-petaled flowers in early summer.

This variety is notable for its horticultural value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
