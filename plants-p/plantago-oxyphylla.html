Plantago oxyphylla

Plantago oxyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are borne on slender stalks. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

The leaves and seeds of Plantago oxyphylla have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
