Pseuderanthemum nigritianum

Pseuderanthemum nigritianum, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, night-blooming white flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique blooming habit.
