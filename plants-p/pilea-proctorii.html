Pilea proctorii

Pilea proctorii, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are round and coin-like, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear on slender stems. It is a succulent, meaning it stores water in its leaves, making it drought-tolerant.

Pilea proctorii is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
