Pelargonium × hesperideum

Pelargonium × hesperideum, commonly known as the scented geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant leaves, which emit a variety of scents depending on the variety, such as citrus, mint, or rose. The plant typically has upright stems and produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. Native to South Africa, it is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aromatic foliage and colorful blooms.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and container planting.
