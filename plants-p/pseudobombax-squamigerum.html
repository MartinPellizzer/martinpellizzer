Pseudobombax squamigerum

Pseudobombax squamigerum, commonly known as the scarlet balsa, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red. The plant produces lightweight, fibrous seeds enclosed in a woody capsule, which is a unique feature of the genus. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm regions.
