Potamogeton pectinatus f. dichotomus

Potamogeton pectinatus f. dichotomus, commonly known as the forked pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its branching stems and elongated, narrow leaves.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, round fruits. Its leaves are usually smooth and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
