Persea hirta

Persea hirta, commonly known as the hairy persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange to red berries that are edible when ripe. Persea hirta thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires a warm climate to flourish.

Its ornamental value and edible fruits make it a notable species in horticulture and local ecosystems.
