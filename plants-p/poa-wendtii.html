Poa wendtii

Poa wendtii, commonly known as Wendt's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and bluish-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, compact flower clusters.

Poa wendtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
