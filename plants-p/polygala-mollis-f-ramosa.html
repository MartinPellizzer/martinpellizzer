Polygala mollis f. ramosa

Polygala mollis f. ramosa, commonly known as the hairy polygala, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
