Physalis pubescens var. missouriensis

Physalis pubescens var. missouriensis, commonly known as the Missouri groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the central United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that enclose a cluster of orange, berry-like seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
