Phyllostachys nigripes

Phyllostachys nigripes, commonly known as black bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the bamboo family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black culms that contrast sharply with its green leaves. Native to China, this bamboo thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense clumps, making it suitable for landscaping and erosion control.

Its botanical features include slender, hollow stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.
