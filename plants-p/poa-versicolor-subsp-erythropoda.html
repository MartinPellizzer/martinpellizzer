Poa versicolor subsp. erythropoda

Poa versicolor subsp.

erythropoda, commonly known as the red-stemmed bluegrass, is a variant of the bluegrass species native to parts of North America. It is characterized by its distinctive reddish stems and foliage, which give it a unique appearance compared to other poa species.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.
