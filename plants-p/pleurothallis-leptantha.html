Pleurothallis leptantha

Pleurothallis leptantha, commonly known as the slender-stemmed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, narrow leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. It is known for its unique floral structure, which includes a spurred lip and a narrow column.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
