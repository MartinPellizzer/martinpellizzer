Peniocereus oaxacensis

Peniocereus oaxacensis, commonly known as the Oaxacan night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are typically green and covered in spines. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night, usually in shades of white or pale pink. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique blooming habit and ornamental value, it is often cultivated by cactus enthusiasts.
