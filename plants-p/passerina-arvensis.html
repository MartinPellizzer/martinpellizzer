Passerina arvensis

Passerina arvensis, commonly known as the field pasqueflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms that typically appear in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually trifoliate, with three leaflets. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Passerina arvensis is native to Europe and parts of Asia, where it plays a role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
