Potamogeton × nitens f. speciosus

Potamogeton × nitens f. speciosus, commonly known as the showy pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant characterized by its finely divided, grass-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color.

It thrives in freshwater environments, often found in slow-moving or still waters such as ponds and lakes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spikelets, which are usually submerged or floating at the water's surface. It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its ornamental qualities make it a popular choice for pond landscaping when cultivated in controlled environments.
