Petalochilus atrochilus

Petalochilus atrochilus, commonly known as the black-lipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety petals and sepals, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a mix of dark purple and yellow hues.

Petalochilus atrochilus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
