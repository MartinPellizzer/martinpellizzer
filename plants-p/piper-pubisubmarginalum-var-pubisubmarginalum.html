Piper pubisubmarginalum var. pubisubmarginalum

Piper pubisubmarginalum var. pubisubmarginalum, commonly known as the pubisubmarginal pepper plant, is a tropical species belonging to the Piper genus in the family Piperaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing vines with opposite, ovate leaves that have a distinctive pubescent margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, fleshy berries containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

Due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
