Phaleria lanceolata

Phaleria lanceolata, commonly known as the white-berried caryota, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits containing black seeds.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits, and the plant is also valued as an ornamental species in horticulture.
