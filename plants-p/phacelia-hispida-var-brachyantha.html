Phacelia hispida var. brachyantha

Phacelia hispida var. brachyantha, commonly known as the hairy phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for dry climates.

Phacelia hispida var. brachyantha is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
