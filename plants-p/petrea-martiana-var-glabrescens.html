Petrea martiana var. glabrescens

Petrea martiana var. glabrescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf petrea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hair, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
