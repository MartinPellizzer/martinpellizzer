Paederia consimilis

Paederia consimilis, commonly known as the Indian nettle or false nettle, is a climbing shrub belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is found in tropical and subtropical regions.
