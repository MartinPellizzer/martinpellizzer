Paracaryum corymbiforme var. polystachyum

Paracaryum corymbiforme var. polystachyum, commonly known as the giant water hyacinth, is a floating aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features broad, waxy leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length, and it produces dense clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often forming dense mats on the water surface. Its rapid growth rate makes it an effective natural water purifier, though it can become invasive if not managed.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in aquatic ecosystems.
