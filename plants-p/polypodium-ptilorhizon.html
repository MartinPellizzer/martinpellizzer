Polypodium ptilorhizon

Polypodium ptilorhizon, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and the presence of hairy structures on its fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky areas. Its fronds are pinnate, with a distinctive appearance that aids in its identification.

Polypodium ptilorhizon is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid conditions.
