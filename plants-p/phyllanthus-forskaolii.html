Phyllanthus forskaolii

Phyllanthus forskaolii, commonly known as the African starleaf, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by spherical, berry-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Phyllanthus forskaolii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
