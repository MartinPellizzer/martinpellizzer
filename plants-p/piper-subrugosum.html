Piper subrugosum

Piper subrugosum, commonly known as the roughleaf pepper or wild pepper, is a tropical vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, warm climates. The plant is characterized by its rough, textured leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into peppercorn-like berries. Its botanical features include opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin and a climbing habit that supports its growth in dense undergrowth.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and has potential applications in modern pharmacology.
