Papaver rhoeas f. integrifolium

Papaver rhoeas f. integrifolium, commonly known as the Flanders poppy, is a botanical variant of the common poppy.

It is characterized by its striking red petals and a distinctive blackened center, which becomes visible as the flower matures. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, typical of the Papaveraceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and wastelands.

Its foliage is usually entire, giving it the specific epithet *integrifolium*, meaning "with entire leaves.".
