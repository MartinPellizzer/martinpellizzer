Populus nigra var. helvetica

Populus nigra var. helvetica, commonly known as the Swiss poplar, is a deciduous tree native to Europe.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and heart-shaped leaves. The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age. It produces small, pendulous catkins in early spring, and its flowers are wind-pollinated.

This variety is often found in riparian habitats and is valued for its ecological and ornamental significance.
