Pandanus heudelotianus

Pandanus heudelotianus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces large, branched inflorescences with small, fragrant flowers that are pollinated by insects and birds. Its fibrous roots and robust stems make it adaptable to coastal and sandy soils.

The plant is often used in traditional crafts and has cultural significance in various island communities.
