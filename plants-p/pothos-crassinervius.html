Pothos crassinervius

Pothos crassinervius, commonly known as the broad-veined pothos, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves with prominent, parallel veins that give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its adaptability and ease of care make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
