Piper calcariforme

Piper calcariforme, commonly known as the mountain pepper or false pepper, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a strong, pungent aroma. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, typically clustered in spikes, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not widely consumed.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
