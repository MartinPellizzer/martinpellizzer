Potentilla tormentilla fallax

Potentilla tormentilla fallax, commonly known as the fallax potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Potentilla tormentilla fallax is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in difficult environments.
