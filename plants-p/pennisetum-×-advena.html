Pennisetum × advena

Pennisetum × advena, commonly known as purple fountain grass, is a hybrid ornamental grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, fluffy plumes that turn a striking purple-pink color in the fall. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in panicles, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Pennisetum × advena is also known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract birds and butterflies.
