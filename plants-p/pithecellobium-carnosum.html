Pithecellobium carnosum

Pithecellobium carnosum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its leguminous structure and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that transition into edible, fleshy pods containing seeds. Its leaves are compound and often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is valued for both its ornamental and practical uses.
