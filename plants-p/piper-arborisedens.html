Piper arborisedens

Piper arborisedens, commonly known as the hanging pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems that often hang from trees or other supports. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright red berries that are edible but have a mild, peppery flavor. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Piper arborisedens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
