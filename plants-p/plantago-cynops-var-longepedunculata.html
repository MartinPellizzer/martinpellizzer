Plantago cynops var. longepedunculata

Plantago cynops var. longepedunculata, commonly known as the long pedunculate plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features rosettes of broad, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy underside, and it produces tall, slender flowering stems that bear clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant is often found in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides, meadows, and wetlands. It is valued for its medicinal properties, including its use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
