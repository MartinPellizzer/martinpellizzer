Psydrax odoratus subsp. buxifolius

Psydrax odoratus subsp.

buxifolius, commonly known as the fragrant psychodid, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa. It features glossy, dark green leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, resembling those of boxwood, hence its subspecies name.

The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to coastal and sunny environments.

Its foliage and floral scent make it a popular choice in garden landscapes.
