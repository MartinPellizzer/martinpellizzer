Pharus pubescens

Pharus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy pharus, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to dry conditions. Its small, clustered flowers are usually greenish or yellowish in color.

Pharus pubescens is often found in rocky soils and can be a hardy plant in regions with low rainfall.
