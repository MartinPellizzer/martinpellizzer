Prunus itosakura var. ascendens

Prunus itosakura var. ascendens, commonly known as the ascending Japanese cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and profuse display of white or pale pink flowers in early spring. The plant typically reaches heights of 6 to 10 meters and features slender, arching branches. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy green surface and turn yellow in autumn.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
