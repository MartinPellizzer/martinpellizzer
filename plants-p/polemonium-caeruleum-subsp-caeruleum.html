Polemonium caeruleum subsp. caeruleum

Polemonium caeruleum subsp.

caeruleum, commonly known as bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America. It features striking blue to purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a bluish-green hue. It thrives in moist, shady woodland habitats and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
