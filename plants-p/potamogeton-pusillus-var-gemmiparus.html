Potamogeton pusillus var. gemmiparus

Potamogeton pusillus var. gemmiparus, commonly known as the gemmiparous pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically narrow and elongated. The plant produces tiny, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it reproduces both sexually and vegetatively through gemmae, which are small, propagative structures. It thrives in slow-moving or still waters with muddy or sandy substrates, often forming dense underwater vegetation.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
