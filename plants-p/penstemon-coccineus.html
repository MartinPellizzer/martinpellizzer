Penstemon coccineus

Penstemon coccineus, commonly known as the scarlet penstemon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Penstemon coccineus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
