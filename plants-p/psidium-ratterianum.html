Psidium ratterianum

Psidium ratterianum, commonly known as the Ratter's guava, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible berries similar to guavas. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The species is also valued for its potential in ecological restoration due to its ability to stabilize soil in degraded areas.
