Primula pulverea

Primula pulverea, commonly known as the dust primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soil and cool climates. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in a fine, powdery coating that gives the species its common name.

Primula pulverea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
