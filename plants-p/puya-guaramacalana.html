Puya guaramacalana

Puya guaramacalana, commonly known as the Guaramacalan puya, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of long, narrow, evergreen leaves that form a water-holding structure. The plant produces showy, spadix-like inflorescences that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
