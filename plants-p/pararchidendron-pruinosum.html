Pararchidendron pruinosum

Pararchidendron pruinosum, commonly known as the glossy bark tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy, and often glossy bark, which gives it its common name. The plant has simple, opposite, and usually elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, round, fleshy fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
