Pistaciopsis

Pistaciopsis, commonly known as the dwarf pistacia, is a genus of flowering plants in the cashew family, Anacardiaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are shrubs or small trees native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. The plants are characterized by their simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. They produce small, fleshy fruits that are often edible and have a mild, aromatic flavor.

Pistaciopsis species are adapted to drought conditions and are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants.
