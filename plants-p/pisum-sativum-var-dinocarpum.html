Pisum sativum var. dinocarpum

Pisum sativum var. dinocarpum, commonly known as the giant edible pea, is a variety of the common pea plant characterized by its large, thick pods and robust growth habit.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces edible seeds that are typically harvested when the pods are fully mature and the seeds are dry. Its botanical features include compound leaves with trifoliate structures and tendrils that aid in climbing.

This variety is valued for its high yield and nutritional content, making it a popular choice for both home gardens and commercial cultivation.
