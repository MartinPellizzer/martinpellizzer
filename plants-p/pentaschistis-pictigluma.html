Pentaschistis pictigluma

Pentaschistis pictigluma, commonly known as the painted glass vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble translucent glass, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overshadowed by the vibrant foliage. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.
