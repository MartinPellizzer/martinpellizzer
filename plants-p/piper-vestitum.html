Piper vestitum

Piper vestitum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that give it a velvety appearance, hence its common name. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by elongated, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
