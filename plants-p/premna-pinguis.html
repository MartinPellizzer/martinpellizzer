Premna pinguis

Premna pinguis, commonly known as the glossy abutilon, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive glossy appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, rounded seeds.

Premna pinguis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
