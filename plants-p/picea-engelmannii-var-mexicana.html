Picea engelmannii var. mexicana

Picea engelmannii var. mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It typically grows in high-altitude forests, thriving in cool, moist climates with well-drained soils. The tree has needle-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath, and it produces cone-like structures that contain its seeds. It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.

The Mexican fir is also valued for its timber and has cultural significance in some indigenous communities.
