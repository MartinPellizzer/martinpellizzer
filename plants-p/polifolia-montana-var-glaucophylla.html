Polifolia montana var. glaucophylla

Polifolia montana var. glaucophylla, commonly known as the glaucophylla variant of mountain polifolia, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive silver-green foliage.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in alpine or subalpine regions. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating that reflects sunlight. It produces small, clustered white or pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
