Pilea calcicola

Pilea calcicola, commonly known as the stonecrop pilea, is a compact, low-growing plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often bluish-green in color, adapted to store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
