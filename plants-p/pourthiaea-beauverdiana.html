Pourthiaea beauverdiana

Pourthiaea beauverdiana, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant features velvety, heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of violet, pink, and white. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a houseplant.
