Platyaechmea distichantha var. schlumbergeri

Platyaechmea distichantha var. schlumbergeri, commonly known as the Schlumbergera truncata, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its segmented, fleshy stems that are typically green or red in color. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during the winter months. It is a popular houseplant due to its ease of care and long blooming period.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cactaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its stems.
