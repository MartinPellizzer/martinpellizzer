Pariana strigosa

Pariana strigosa, commonly known as the rough pariana, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving the plant a rough texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant has a long flowering period, making it a notable species in its native ecosystems.
