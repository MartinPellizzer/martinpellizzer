Parthenocissus suberosa

Parthenocissus suberosa, commonly known as the Virginia creeper, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn bright red in the fall, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by dark blue berries. It has tendrils that allow it to climb and attach to surfaces, often using adhesive pads.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a popular ornamental plant and an invasive species in some regions.
