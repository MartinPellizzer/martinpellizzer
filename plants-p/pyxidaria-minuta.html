Pyxidaria minuta

Pyxidaria minuta, commonly known as the small cup-plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in cup-like structures, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.

Pyxidaria minuta is an important plant for pollinators and contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
