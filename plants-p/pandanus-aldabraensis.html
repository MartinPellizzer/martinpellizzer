Pandanus aldabraensis

Pandanus aldabraensis, commonly known as the Aldabra pandanus, is a species of palm native to the Aldabra Atoll in the Seychelles.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in branched inflorescences, followed by edible, fibrous fruits. It thrives in coastal environments and is adapted to salt spray and sandy soils.

This plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
