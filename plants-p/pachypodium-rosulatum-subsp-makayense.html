Pachypodium rosulatum subsp. makayense

Pachypodium rosulatum subsp.

makayense, commonly known as the Madagascar moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its distinctive, swollen, bottle-shaped stem and sparse, spiny leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the dry season, typically in summer. It thrives in arid conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
