Polygala vulgaris var. comosa

Polygala vulgaris var. comosa, commonly known as the woolly milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
