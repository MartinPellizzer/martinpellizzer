Pleurothallis stellata

Pleurothallis stellata, commonly known as the star orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often resemble stars, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Pleurothallis stellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
