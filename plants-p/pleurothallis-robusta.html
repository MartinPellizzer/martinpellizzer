Pleurothallis robusta

Pleurothallis robusta, commonly known as the robust orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the undergrowth of forests. The plant features a short, stocky stem with several fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are small, usually white or pale green, and are clustered in racemes, producing a delicate, fragrant display.

Pleurothallis robusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant due to its specific care requirements.
