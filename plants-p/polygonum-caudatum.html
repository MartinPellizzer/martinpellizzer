Polygonum caudatum

Polygonum caudatum, commonly known as the silky filbert or red-rooted beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. The plant features slender, branched stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink to red in color and bloom in late summer.

Polygonum caudatum is known for its distinctive red roots and its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
