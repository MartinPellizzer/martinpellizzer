Pyrus umbellifera

Pyrus umbellifera, commonly known as the umbrella pear, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its distinctive umbellate inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, sweet-tart fruits resembling small pears. It is typically found growing at high altitudes in rocky or mountainous habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated in some regions for its fruit.
