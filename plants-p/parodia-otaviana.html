Parodia otaviana

Parodia otaviana, commonly known as the Otaviana cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Namibia.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance. It requires minimal water and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for desert gardens or indoor environments.

Parodia otaviana is also valued for its ability to store water in its stem, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
