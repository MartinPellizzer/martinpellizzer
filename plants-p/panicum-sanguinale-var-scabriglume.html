Panicum sanguinale var. scabriglume

Panicum sanguinale var. scabriglume, commonly known as rough glume panicgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear bracts with rough, scabrous surfaces, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
