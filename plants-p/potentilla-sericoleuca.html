Potentilla sericoleuca

Potentilla sericoleuca, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky slopes.

Potentilla sericoleuca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native plant gardens.
