Phaseolus sordidus

Phaseolus sordidus, commonly known as the black bean plant, is a species of legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain dark brown seeds. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it thrives in warm, tropical climates.

The seeds of Phaseolus sordidus are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a food source in certain regions.
