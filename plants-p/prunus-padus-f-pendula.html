Prunus padus f. pendula

Prunus padus f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping almond, is a unique cultivar of the common cherry tree.

It is characterized by its gracefully drooping branches that create a cascading effect, making it a popular ornamental plant. The tree produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring, adding to its aesthetic appeal. Its leaves are ovate and turn yellow in the autumn, contributing to its seasonal beauty.

This cultivar thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sun for optimal growth.
