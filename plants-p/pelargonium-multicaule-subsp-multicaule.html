Pelargonium multicaule subsp. multicaule

Pelargonium multicaule subsp.

multicaule, commonly known as the African geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa. It features upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height.

The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough texture and may have a slight fragrance.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.
