Portulaca oleracea var. alba

Portulaca oleracea var. alba, commonly known as the white purslane, is an annual flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common weed in gardens and agricultural fields. Its leaves are edible and have a slightly tart, peppery flavor, often used in salads or cooked dishes.

The plant is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
