Plectranthus rhomboideus

Plectranthus rhomboideus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of violet, pink, or white. It is often grown as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

Plectranthus rhomboideus prefers well-draining soil and bright, indirect light to thrive.
