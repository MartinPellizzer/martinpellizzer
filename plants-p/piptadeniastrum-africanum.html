Piptadeniastrum africanum

Piptadeniastrum africanum, commonly known as African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, trifoliate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated pods containing flat, winged seeds. It is often found in savannas and woodland areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant has traditional uses in local medicine and is also valued for its timber.
