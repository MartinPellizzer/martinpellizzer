Populus hsinganica

Populus hsinganica, commonly known as the Hsingan poplar, is a species of poplar native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and heart-shaped leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish catkins in the spring, which are its primary means of pollination. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in river valleys and lowland areas.

The Hsingan poplar is valued for its fast growth and is sometimes used in reforestation efforts.
