Phacelia imbricata var. condensata

Phacelia imbricata var. condensata, commonly known as the denseleaf phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and densely arranged, giving the plant its "condensata" variety name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting pollinators.
