Penstemon tenuis

Penstemon tenuis, commonly known as narrowleaf beardtongue, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun conditions. It is a perennial herb that produces showy blooms in the spring and early summer.

Penstemon tenuis is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
