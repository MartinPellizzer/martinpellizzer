Pourthiaea obliqua

Pourthiaea obliqua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pincushion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly inflorescence that resembles a pincushion. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often with a blue-gray hue.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
