Physiglochis dioica

Physiglochis dioica, commonly known as the two-flowered grass.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family, characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and marshes. It has separate male and female flowers, which are often found on different stems, a feature known as dioecy.

Its small, inconspicuous flowers produce seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its propagation in wetland environments.
