Protea denticulata

Protea denticulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, cone-shaped inflorescence surrounded by dense, overlapping bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows in fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a central cluster of stamens that add to its ornamental appeal.

Protea denticulata is valued for its horticultural use and plays an important role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
