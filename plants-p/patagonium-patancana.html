Patagonium patancana

Patagonium patancana, commonly known as the Patagonian mountain fern, is a unique plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern South America.

It is a perennial fern that thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in rocky soils and mountainous terrains. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped fronds that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. Its reproductive structures are hidden within the fronds, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
