Packera quercetorum

Packera quercetorum, commonly known as the oakleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive oak-like leaves and showy purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It produces small, elongated fruits called achenes, which are dispersed by wind.

Packera quercetorum plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in montane and subalpine regions.
