Papaver pyrenaicum var. luteum

Papaver pyrenaicum var. luteum, commonly known as the yellow Pyrenean poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Pyrenees mountain range.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elliptical seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
