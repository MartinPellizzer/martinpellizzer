Phyllanthus vulcani var. baumannii

Phyllanthus vulcani var. baumannii, commonly known as the Baumann's rock apple, is a variant of the rock apple plant native to specific regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils. Its fruit, which is typically yellow or orange, is edible and has a sour taste, used in local cuisines.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
