Piper pseudolindenii

Piper pseudolindenii, commonly known as the Malayan pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, opposite leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

The plant is often found in tropical rainforests, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.
