Pseudopanax arboreus var. kermadecensis

Pseudopanax arboreus var. kermadecensis, commonly known as the Kermadec tree, is a unique evergreen tree native to the Kermadec Islands in the South Pacific.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palm-like leaves that are often spirally arranged along the stems. The plant exhibits a fascinating morphological adaptation known as "pseudopanax" or "false palm," which mimics the appearance of a palm tree. Its leaves are typically broad, leathery, and have a waxy surface, which helps reduce water loss in its coastal habitat.

This variety is relatively rare and is of significant botanical interest due to its evolutionary and ecological significance in the region.
