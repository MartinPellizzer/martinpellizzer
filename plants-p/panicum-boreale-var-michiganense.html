Panicum boreale var. michiganense

Panicum boreale var. michiganense, commonly known as the Michigan panicgrass, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and fine, thread-like leaves. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, bristle-like flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
