Papaver radicatum var. camtschaticum

Papaver radicatum var. camtschaticum, commonly known as the Caucasian poppy, is a species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
