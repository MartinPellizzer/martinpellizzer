Picea pinsapo

Picea pinsapo, commonly known as the Spanish fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The tree has needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in spirals along the branches. Its cones are woody and contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Picea pinsapo plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
