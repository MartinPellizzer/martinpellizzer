Potentilla glandulosa var. cuneifolia

Potentilla glandulosa var. cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, five-petaled yellow flowers and serrated, narrow leaves. The plant typically thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its foliage is covered in glandular hairs, which give it a distinctive texture and aid in moisture retention.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
