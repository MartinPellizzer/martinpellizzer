Piper candicans

Piper candicans, commonly known as the sweet pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a spice due to its aromatic leaves.

The plant prefers warm, humid climates and well-drained soil for optimal growth.
