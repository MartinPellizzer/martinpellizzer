Piper dilatatum f. vauclinii

Piper dilatatum f. vauclinii, commonly known as the Vauclin's pepper plant, is a rare variant of the genus Piper native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves with a serrated margin and a strong, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into dark, peppery berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
