Paepalanthus argyropus

Paepalanthus argyropus, commonly known as the silverleaf cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming cactus with a dense covering of white, silky hairs that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. Its succulent stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique foliage and resilience to drought.
