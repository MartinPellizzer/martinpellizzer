Proboscidea triloba

Proboscidea triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, three-lobed flower structure. The plant features long, slender stems and produces showy, fragrant blossoms that attract various pollinators. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
