Passiflora kuranda

Passiflora kuranda, commonly known as Kuranda passionflower, is a species native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking, intricate flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant's inflorescence includes a unique structure called a "corona" surrounded by a cluster of stamens. Its fruit is a small, edible capsule that contains several seeds.

Passiflora kuranda is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
