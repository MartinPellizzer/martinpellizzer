Pandanus furcatus var. heterostigma

Pandanus furcatus var. heterostigma, commonly known as the variable pandanus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, fibrous leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear yellowish to greenish flowers. Its roots are sometimes used in local medicinal practices.

This variant is notable for its morphological variation, which includes differences in leaf structure and plant form.
