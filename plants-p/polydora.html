Polydora

Polydora, commonly known as the sea mouse, is a genus of polychaete worms found in marine environments.

These organisms are characterized by their soft, segmented bodies and the presence of specialized setae, or bristles, used for locomotion. They typically inhabit sandy or muddy substrates in shallow coastal waters. Polydora species play a role in benthic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling through their feeding activities.

Their unique appearance and ecological significance make them an interesting subject for marine biology studies.
