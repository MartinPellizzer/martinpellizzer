Phaeosphaerion rufipes

Phaeosphaerion rufipes, commonly known as the red-legged velvet fungus, is a unique fungal species characterized by its intricate, velvety cap and distinctive red-brown spore-bearing structures.

It belongs to the family Sphaerellaceae and is typically found on decaying wood in moist, temperate forest environments. The fruiting bodies are small, with a smooth, glossy surface that becomes more pronounced as they mature. Its spores are released in a powdery form, dispersing easily through the air to colonize new substrates.

This fungus plays an important ecological role in decomposing organic matter and maintaining nutrient cycles in forest ecosystems.
