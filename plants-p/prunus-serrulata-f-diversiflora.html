Prunus serrulata f. diversiflora

Prunus serrulata f. diversiflora, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a botanical variant of the Prunus serrulata species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, double-flowered blooms that appear in early spring, adding a striking visual display. The plant is native to Japan and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value. It typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

Its leaves are elliptical with serrated edges, and it produces small, edible fruits that are used in traditional Japanese cuisine.
