Pavonia lanceolata

Pavonia lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf false mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves and showy, pink to red bract-like flowers. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
