Palaquium pseudocuneatum

Palaquium pseudocuneatum, commonly known as the Malabar apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical, leathery leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, fleshy berries containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and degraded habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation projects.
