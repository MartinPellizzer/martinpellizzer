Phylica laevifolia

Phylica laevifolia, commonly known as the silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds covered in a fine, hairy coating.

Phylica laevifolia is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
