Pleopeltis albidosquamata

Pleopeltis albidosquamata, commonly known as the white-scaled tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, compound fronds. The plant has a distinctive white-scaled appearance on its trunk, which gives it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The fronds are typically long and pinnately divided, contributing to its ornamental value in cultivation.
