Pinus montezumae var. mezambrana

Pinus montezumae var. mezambrana, commonly known as the Mexican bristlecone pine, is a rare variant of the Montezuma pine native to high-altitude regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slow growth, long lifespan, and distinctive needle-like leaves that are shorter and more rigid than those of the typical species. The tree produces cones that are typically smaller and more clustered, with a unique shape that distinguishes it from other pine species. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

This variant is of particular interest to botanists due to its ecological significance and potential for studying adaptation in extreme climates.
