Potentilla gaudinii var. typica

Potentilla gaudinii var. typica, commonly known as the Gaudin's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
