Pithecellobium albicaule

Pithecellobium albicaule, commonly known as the white-stemmed poinsettia, is a shrub native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and clusters of small, white to pale green flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed by various wildlife species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warmer regions.
