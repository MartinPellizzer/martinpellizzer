Phlox × pyramidalis

Phlox × pyramidalis, commonly known as the Pyramidal Phlox, is a hybrid perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its compact, mound-like growth habit and vibrant clusters of tubular flowers that range from pink to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes and floral arrangements.
