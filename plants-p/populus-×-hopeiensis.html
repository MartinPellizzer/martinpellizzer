Populus × hopeiensis

Populus × hopeiensis, commonly known as the Hopei poplar, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Populus tomentosa and Populus alba.

It is characterized by its fast growth rate and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions, making it suitable for reforestation and ornamental purposes. The tree has a broad, spreading crown and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough, hairy texture on the underside.

This species is also valued for its ecological benefits, including soil stabilization and providing habitat for wildlife.
