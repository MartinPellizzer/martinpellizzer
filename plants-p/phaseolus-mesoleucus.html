Phaseolus mesoleucus

Phaseolus mesoleucus, commonly known as the Andean runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers followed by pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is valued for its edible beans.

Due to its adaptability and nutritional value, it is cultivated in various regions for both food and agricultural purposes.
