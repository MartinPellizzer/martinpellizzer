Parodia haageana

Parodia haageana, commonly known as the "Cactus of the Moon," is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines that provide protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and xeriscaping.
