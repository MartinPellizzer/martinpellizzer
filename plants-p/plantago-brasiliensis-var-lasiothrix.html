Plantago brasiliensis var. lasiothrix

Plantago brasiliensis var. lasiothrix, commonly known as the Brazilian plantain, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence, which are borne on slender stalks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant.
