Persea kwangtungensis

Persea kwangtungensis, commonly known as Guangdong persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange to red berries in autumn. The fruit is edible, with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor, and is often used in traditional Chinese cuisine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
