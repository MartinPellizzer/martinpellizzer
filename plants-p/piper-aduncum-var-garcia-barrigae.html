Piper aduncum var. garcia-barrigae

Piper aduncum var. garcia-barrigae, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree or *Schinus terebinthifolius*, is a shrub or small tree native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and often glandular, and its small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
