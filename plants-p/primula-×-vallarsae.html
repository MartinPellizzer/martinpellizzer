Primula × vallarsae

Primula × vallarsae, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors including white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and features broad, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
