Pelargonium × commixtum

Pelargonium × commixtum, commonly known as the hybrid geranium, is a popular ornamental plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the crossbreeding of different pelargonium species, leading to a wide variety of cultivars with diverse flower colors and foliage patterns. The plant typically has broad, lobed leaves and produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in gardens, containers, and hanging baskets.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal, it is a favored choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
