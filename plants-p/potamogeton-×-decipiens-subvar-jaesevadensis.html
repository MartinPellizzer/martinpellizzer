Potamogeton × decipiens subvar. jaesevadensis

Potamogeton × decipiens subvar. jaesevadensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in freshwater habitats such as lakes, rivers, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are usually narrow and slightly curved. It produces small, greenish flowers that are arranged in spikes, and it is often found in shallow water with soft sediment.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in aquatic ecosystems, contributing to water quality and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
