Pyrethrum arcticum

Pyrethrum arcticum, commonly known as Arctic pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cold, harsh environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and it produces small, elongated seeds.

Pyrethrum arcticum is known for its ability to survive in extreme climates, making it a hardy and resilient species.
