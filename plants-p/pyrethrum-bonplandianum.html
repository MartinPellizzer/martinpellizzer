Pyrethrum bonplandianum

Pyrethrum bonplandianum, commonly known as the Bonplandian pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spiky seed heads that contain the insecticidal compounds pyrethrins, which are used in natural pest control. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental and agricultural purposes.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically valuable and economically significant.
