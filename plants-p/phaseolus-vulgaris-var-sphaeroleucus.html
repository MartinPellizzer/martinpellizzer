Phaseolus vulgaris var. sphaeroleucus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. sphaeroleucus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a variety of the common bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive bright red or orange flowers and elongated, edible pods that mature to a yellow or brown color. The plant is typically climbing, with trifoliate leaves and a vining growth habit that requires support. Its botanical features include compound leaves with three leaflets and small, hairy stems.

This variety is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its edible beans, which are rich in nutrients.
