Piper sundaicum

Piper sundaicum, commonly known as Sunda pepper or long pepper, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, peppery berries. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into elongated, curved fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with a strong, spicy scent.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
