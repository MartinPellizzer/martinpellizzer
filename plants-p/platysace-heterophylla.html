Platysace heterophylla

Platysace heterophylla, commonly known as the variable leaf marshmallow, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often lobed leaves that vary in shape depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, swampy habitats and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its adaptability to different light and water conditions makes it a resilient species within its native range.
