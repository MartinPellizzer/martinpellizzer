Pelargonium × milleri

Pelargonium × milleri, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, slightly hairy leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Pelargonium × milleri is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
