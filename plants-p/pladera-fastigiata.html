Pladera fastigiata

Pladera fastigiata, commonly known as the narrowleaf plumea, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces fluffy, white or pale pink inflorescences that resemble plumes, which are highly visible and attract pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly waxy texture.

Pladera fastigiata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in containers.
