Phyllanthus glomerulatus

Phyllanthus glomerulatus, commonly known as the clustered spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation.
