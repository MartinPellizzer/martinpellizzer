Pycreus decumbens

Pycreus decumbens, commonly known as the creeping pycreus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to specific regions of South America.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, which bloom during the warmer months. Its stems are prostrate, allowing it to spread across the ground, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid landscapes.
