Paleolaria carnea

Paleolaria carnea, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, pinkish-red flowers that resemble orchids. The plant features slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often with a subtle fragrance, and are highly sought after in horticulture.

Paleolaria carnea requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
