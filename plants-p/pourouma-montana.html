Pourouma montana

Pourouma montana, commonly known as the mountain bamboo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height, forming dense clumps. The plant has large, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered flowers that are pollinated by wind.

The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, tropical conditions.
