Piscidia punicea

Piscidia punicea, commonly known as the scarlet coral tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its dense, coral-like inflorescences. The plant produces bright red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a distinctive reddish or purple hue when young.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
