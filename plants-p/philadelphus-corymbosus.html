Philadelphus corymbosus

Philadelphus corymbosus, commonly known as the common lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often peeling in thin strips as the plant matures.
