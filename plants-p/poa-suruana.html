Poa suruana

Poa suruana, commonly known as the Andean grass, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Poa suruana plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
