Ptilotus polakii

Ptilotus polakii, commonly known as the "desert flax," is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Ptilotus polakii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well adapted to poor soils and drought conditions.
