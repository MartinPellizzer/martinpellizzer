Paspalum arechavaletae

Paspalum arechavaletae, commonly known as the Arechavaleta's grass, is a species of grass native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Paspalum arechavaletae is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
