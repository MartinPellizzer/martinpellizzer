Pinus strobus f. prostrata

Pinus strobus f. prostrata, commonly known as the creeping white pine, is a low-growing variant of the eastern white pine.

It features a prostrate growth habit, with multiple stems spreading horizontally along the ground. The foliage consists of soft, needle-like leaves that are typically shorter than those of the typical white pine. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain the plant's seeds.

This form is often found in shaded or disturbed sites, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils.
