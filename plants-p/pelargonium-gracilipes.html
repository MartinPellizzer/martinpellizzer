Pelargonium gracilipes

Pelargonium gracilipes, commonly known as the slender geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
