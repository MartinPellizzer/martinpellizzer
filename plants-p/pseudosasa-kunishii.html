Pseudosasa kunishii

Pseudosasa kunishii, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Japanese sweet bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is a clumping bamboo that typically grows in moist, shaded environments in Japan. The plant has slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Pseudosasa kunishii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in horticulture for its ability to thrive in specific ecological conditions.
