Peganum harmala var. multisectum

Peganum harmala var. multisectum, commonly known as the desert cowplant, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and distinctive, compound leaves that are often divided into several segments. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep roots and a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. It produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind, and it has been used traditionally in some regions for medicinal purposes.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a common sight in desert landscapes.
