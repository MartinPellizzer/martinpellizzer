Paepalanthus ithyphyllus

Paepalanthus ithyphyllus, commonly known as the silver leaf cactus, is a species of flowering plant in the Cactaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Bolivia. This plant is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and produces small, yellow flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its water-storing stems.

The plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its unique appearance and resilience.
