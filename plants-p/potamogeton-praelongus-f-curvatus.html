Potamogeton praelongus f. curvatus

Potamogeton praelongus f. curvatus, commonly known as the curved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves that often curve downward, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shallow waters, forming dense underwater vegetation that provides habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its flowers are small and clustered, with both male and female parts present on the same inflorescence.

Potamogeton praelongus f. curvatus plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting aquatic biodiversity.
