Polemonium gracile

Polemonium gracile, commonly known as slender Jacob's ladder, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features delicate, cascading clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with well-drained soil.

Polemonium gracile is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
