Paronychia polygonifolia

Paronychia polygonifolia, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces a milky sap that can be toxic.

Paronychia polygonifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid environments.
