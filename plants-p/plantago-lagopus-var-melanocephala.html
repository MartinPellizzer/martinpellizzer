Plantago lagopus var. melanocephala

Plantago lagopus var. melanocephala, commonly known as the black-headed plantain, is a variant of the common plantain.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive dark purple to black petiole, giving it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes.

This variant is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its medicinal and ecological significance.
