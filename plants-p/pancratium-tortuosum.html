Pancratium tortuosum

Pancratium tortuosum, commonly known as the yellow crocus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has strap-like, grass-like leaves and produces a tall, slender stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy habitats.

Pancratium tortuosum is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant.
