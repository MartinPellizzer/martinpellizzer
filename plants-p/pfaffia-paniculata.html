Pfaffia paniculata

Pfaffia paniculata, commonly known as curare plant, is a climbing shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and spadix inflorescence. The plant is notable for containing alkaloids, including curare, which have been historically used for hunting. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and aerial roots that help it climb.

Pfaffia paniculata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense tropical forests.
