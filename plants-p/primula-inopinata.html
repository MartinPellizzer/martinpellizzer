Primula inopinata

Primula inopinata, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are oval and hairy, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring.

Primula inopinata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
