Polygala persicariifolia f. latifolia

Polygala persicariifolia f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf milkvetch, is a variant of the species Polygala persicariifolia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are broader than the typical form, giving it its distinguishing feature. Its flowers are usually small and clustered in spicate inflorescences, often with a distinctive, spurred appearance.

The plant is adapted to grow in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.
