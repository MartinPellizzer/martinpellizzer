Pinus ehrenbergii

Pinus ehrenbergii, commonly known as the Mexican red pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and long, needle-like leaves that grow in bundles of five. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown in color, which are an important feature for identification. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The botanical features of Pinus ehrenbergii make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
