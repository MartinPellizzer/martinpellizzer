Passiflora chrysophylla

Passiflora chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden leaf passionflower, is a striking tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow foliage and showy, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are typically orange or yellow in color, containing a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.
