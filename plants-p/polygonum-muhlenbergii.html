Polygonum muhlenbergii

Polygonum muhlenbergii, commonly known as the Missouri knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring dense, upright clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a prominent midrib and a slightly hairy surface.

The plant is often found in moist soils along stream banks and in woodland areas.
