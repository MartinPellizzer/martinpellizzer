Phormium tenax f. nigrolimbatum

Phormium tenax f. nigrolimbatum, commonly known as the Black-edged New Zealand flax, is a striking ornamental plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its broad, dark green leaves with distinctive black margins, which give it a dramatic appearance. The plant is a perennial, growing in clumps and producing tall, spiky flower stalks that bear purple or reddish bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for landscaping and horticultural displays.
