Potentilla sericea var. hololeuca

Potentilla sericea var. hololeuca, commonly known as silverleaf potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features narrow, silver-white leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
