Psidium berteroanum

Psidium berteroanum, commonly known as the yellow guava, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to edible, orange-colored fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate various soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
