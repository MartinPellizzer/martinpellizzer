Primula chrysochlora

Primula chrysochlora, commonly known as the golden crown primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with a distinctive golden center. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are broadly ovate with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
