Polystachya praecipitis

Polystachya praecipitis, commonly known as the waterfall bamboo, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps over time.

Polystachya praecipitis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
