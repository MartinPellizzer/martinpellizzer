Phylica stenopetala var. stenopetala

Phylica stenopetala var. stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
