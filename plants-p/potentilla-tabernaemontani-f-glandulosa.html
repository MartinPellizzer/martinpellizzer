Potentilla tabernaemontani f. glandulosa

Potentilla tabernaemontani f. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular potentilla, is a flowering plant characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage.

It features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of delicate, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
