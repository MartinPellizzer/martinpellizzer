Pelargonium pseudofumarioides

Pelargonium pseudofumarioides, commonly known as the fumarioides geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant produces distinctive, elongated seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
