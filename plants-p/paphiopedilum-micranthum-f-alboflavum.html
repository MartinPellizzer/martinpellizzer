Paphiopedilum micranthum f. alboflavum

Paphiopedilum micranthum f. alboflavum, commonly known as the White-flowered Paphiopedilum, is a rare and highly sought-after orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white and pale yellow flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid, thriving in humid, shaded environments with well-draining soil. Its leaves are broad and oval, contributing to its lush appearance.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
