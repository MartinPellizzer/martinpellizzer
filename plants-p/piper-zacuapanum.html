Piper zacuapanum

Piper zacuapanum, commonly known as the Zacuapan pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Central America, particularly found in countries like Guatemala and Mexico. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, green fruits that turn red when mature. The fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to other members of the pepper family.

It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.
