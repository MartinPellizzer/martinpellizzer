Pinus mugo var. arborea

Pinus mugo var. arborea, commonly known as the dwarf mountain pine, is a compact, slow-growing evergreen conifer native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with short, needle-like leaves that are typically a dark green color. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is well adapted to harsh environments, thriving in poor, rocky soils and cold climates.

Its unique growth habit and resilience make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
