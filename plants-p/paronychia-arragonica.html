Paronychia arragonica

Paronychia arragonica, commonly known as the silverleaf carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces showy, pink to white flowers. The plant has narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Paronychia arragonica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
