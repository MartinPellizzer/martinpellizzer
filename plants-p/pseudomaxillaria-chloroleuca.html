Pseudomaxillaria chloroleuca

Pseudomaxillaria chloroleuca, commonly known as the white-tipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive yellowish-green lip. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas.

Pseudomaxillaria chloroleuca is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral appearance.
