Pitcairnia mucida

Pitcairnia mucida, commonly known as the velvet bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant's foliage is covered in a dense, velvety pubescence, giving it its common name. It produces striking inflorescences with showy, long-lasting flowers that range in color from red to orange.

Pitcairnia mucida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
