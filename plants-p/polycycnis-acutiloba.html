Polycycnis acutiloba

Polycycnis acutiloba, commonly known as the sharp-lobed water plantain, is a aquatic plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a distinctive sharp, pointed lobes. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, typically blooming in the summer months.

Polycycnis acutiloba plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
