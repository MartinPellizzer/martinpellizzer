Piper urense

Piper urense, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, posing a threat to native ecosystems.
