Pinalia erosula

Pinalia erosula, commonly known as the slender pinyon, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Pinaceae family, which includes pines and other conifers, and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its cones are small and often remain closed for many years before opening to release seeds.

Pinalia erosula plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
