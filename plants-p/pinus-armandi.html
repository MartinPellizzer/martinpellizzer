Pinus armandi

Pinus armandi, commonly known as the Chinese red pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and long, needle-like leaves that are typically 2 to 4 centimeters in length. The tree produces cones that are approximately 5 to 8 centimeters long, containing seeds surrounded by a papery wing. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with a temperate climate.

The botanical features of Pinus armandi make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
