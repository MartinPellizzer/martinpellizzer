Piper arcessitum

Piper arcessitum, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other species like black pepper. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.

The plant's botanical features make it distinct within its family, with adaptations suited to its native tropical environment.
