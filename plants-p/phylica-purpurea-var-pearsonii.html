Phylica purpurea var. pearsonii

Phylica purpurea var. pearsonii, commonly known as the Pearson's bottlebrush, is a striking South African shrub characterized by its dense, bottlebrush-like inflorescences.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is native to the Eastern Cape province of South Africa. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves and produces vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to coastal and sunny climates.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in supporting local wildlife.
