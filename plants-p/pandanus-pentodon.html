Pandanus pentodon

Pandanus pentodon, commonly known as the coconut pandanus, is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and stout, spiny fruits. The plant's leaves are long and narrow, often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in terminal inflorescences, and it produces edible fruits that are important in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and humid environments.
