Parodia knizei

Parodia knizei, commonly known as the Knizei's candelabra cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that branches into multiple arms, resembling a candlestick. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to drought conditions.

Parodia knizei is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.
