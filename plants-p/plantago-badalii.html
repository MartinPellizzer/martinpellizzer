Plantago badalii

Plantago badalii, commonly known as the Badalii plantain, is a species of flowering plant in the family Plantaginaceae.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula, particularly Spain, where it thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically pollinated by wind.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to harsh, dry climates.
