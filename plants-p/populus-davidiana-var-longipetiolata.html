Populus davidiana var. longipetiolata

Populus davidiana var. longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled poplar, is a variant of the David poplar native to China.

It is characterized by its elongated petioles, which are the stalks connecting the leaves to the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a smooth margin and a slightly serrated edge. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize riverbanks.
