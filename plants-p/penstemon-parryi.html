Penstemon parryi

Penstemon parryi, commonly known as Parry's beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers. The plant produces small, hairy leaves that are typically opposite along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Penstemon parryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
