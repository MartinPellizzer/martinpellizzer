Pelargonium crassipes

Pelargonium crassipes, commonly known as the broadleaf geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its broad, fleshy leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Pelargonium crassipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
