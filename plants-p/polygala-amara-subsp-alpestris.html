Polygala amara subsp. alpestris

Polygala amara subsp.

alpestris, commonly known as the alpine milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and small, often pale or greenish flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces pods that contain small, hard seeds.

Due to its habitat preferences, it is often found in high-altitude meadows and is adapted to cold, harsh climates.
