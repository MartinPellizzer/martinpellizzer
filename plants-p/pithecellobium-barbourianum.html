Pithecellobium barbourianum

Pithecellobium barbourianum, commonly known as the Hawaiian pandanus, is a tropical shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its palm-like appearance with long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody pod containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

The species is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of Hawaii and plays a role in local ecosystems.
