Polygala senensis

Polygala senensis, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also used in ornamental horticulture.
