Peperomia

Peperomia, commonly known as the "staggering plant," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Piperaceae.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and are characterized by their thick, fleshy leaves and often compact growth habit. Many species of peperomia have a variety of leaf shapes, including lanceolate, ovate, or even heart-shaped, and some display variegated patterns. They are popular as houseplants due to their low maintenance and ability to thrive in indirect light.

Peperomia species typically produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are often followed by unique, spore-bearing structures called spathes.
