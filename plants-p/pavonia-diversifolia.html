Pavonia diversifolia

Pavonia diversifolia, commonly known as the purple crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Pavonia diversifolia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
