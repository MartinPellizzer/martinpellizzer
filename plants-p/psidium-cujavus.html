Psidium cujavus

Psidium cujavus, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or caju, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, sweet, and juicy berries. The fruit has a thick, leathery outer skin and a soft, seed-filled pulp that is rich in nutrients.

It is widely cultivated for its fruit and has significant economic and ecological importance in its native regions.
