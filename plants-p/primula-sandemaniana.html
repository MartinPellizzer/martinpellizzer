Primula sandemaniana

Primula sandemaniana, commonly known as the Sandeman's primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments, often in rocky or alpine habitats. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces small, oval berries that contain the seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
