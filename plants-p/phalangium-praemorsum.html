Phalangium praemorsum

This arachnid is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as under rocks, logs, or in leaf litter. Its body structure is elongated and segmented, with a pair of long, slender legs extending from its cephalothorax.

Unlike true spiders, Phalangium praemorsum lacks venom glands and silk-producing spinnerets, relying instead on its camouflage and defensive posturing for survival.
