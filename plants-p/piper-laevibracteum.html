Piper laevibracteum

Piper laevibracteum, commonly known as the smooth bract pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into berry-like fruits. Its vines can grow up to several meters in length, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The plant is often found in secondary forests and along stream banks.
