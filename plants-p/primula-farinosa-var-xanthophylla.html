Primula farinosa var. xanthophylla

Primula farinosa var. xanthophylla, commonly known as the yellow-flowered primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it the species name "farinosa," meaning flour-like. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its attractive, long-lasting blooms.
