Pisonia psammophila

Pisonia psammophila, commonly known as the sand dune pisonia, is a shrub native to coastal regions of the Indo-Pacific.

It is characterized by its small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found along shorelines and dunes.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various coastal wildlife species.
