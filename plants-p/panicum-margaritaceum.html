Panicum margaritaceum

Panicum margaritaceum, commonly known as silverleaf panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant communities and a subject of ecological study.
