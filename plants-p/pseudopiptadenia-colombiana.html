Pseudopiptadenia colombiana

Pseudopiptadenia colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian false piptadenia, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in humid forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
