Paullinia apoda

Paullinia apoda, commonly known as the Brazilian sarsapilla, is a climbing plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically harvested when they turn red. Its botanical features include woody stems and a preference for humid, tropical environments.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
