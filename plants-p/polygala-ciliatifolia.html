Polygala ciliatifolia

Polygala ciliatifolia, commonly known as the silky polygala, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate flowers that are often blue or purple in color. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
