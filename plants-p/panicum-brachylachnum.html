Panicum brachylachnum

Panicum brachylachnum, commonly known as the dwarf panicgrass, is a low-growing grass species native to the southern United States.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growths on the ground, often in sandy or rocky soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is adapted to drought and full sun, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.

Due to its compact size and resilience, it is sometimes used in ornamental landscaping or as a ground cover.
