Podocarpus cupressinus var. curvulus

Podocarpus cupressinus var. curvulus, commonly known as the silver pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the Podocarpaceae family. Its bark is smooth and grayish in color, becoming fissured with age.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types and climates.
