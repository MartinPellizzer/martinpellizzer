Phoradendron intermedium

Phoradendron intermedium, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees, primarily in the Americas.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light absorbed by its leaves. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with its host, often leading to weakened growth in the host tree over time. Its berries are a food source for birds, which then disperse the seeds to new hosts.

The plant's unique life cycle and ecological role make it an important species in forest ecosystems.
