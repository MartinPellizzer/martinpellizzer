Pulsatilla montana

Pulsatilla montana, commonly known as the pasque flower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its foliage consists of palmately divided leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Pulsatilla montana is known for its early blooming period, often appearing in late spring, and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
