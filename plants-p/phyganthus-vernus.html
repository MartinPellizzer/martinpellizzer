Phyganthus vernus

Phyganthus vernus, commonly known as the early spring orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to certain regions of Europe and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
