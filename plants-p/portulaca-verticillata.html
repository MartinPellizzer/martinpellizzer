Portulaca verticillata

Portulaca verticillata, commonly known as moss rose, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink and yellow. The plant typically grows in a compact, bushy form, with stems that often lie close to the ground. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Portulaca verticillata is known for its ability to survive in arid conditions, thanks to its drought-resistant properties.
