Phthirusa pyrifolia var. intermedia

Phthirusa pyrifolia var. intermedia, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a variant of the pear tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its medium-sized, white to yellowish fruits with a smooth, thin skin. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, fertile soils. Its leaves are broad and lobed, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

This variety is valued for its edible fruit, which is often used in both fresh consumption and culinary preparations.
