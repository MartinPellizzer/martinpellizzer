Pseudopogonatherum capilliphyllum

Pseudopogonatherum capilliphyllum, commonly known as the slender beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swampy areas, thriving in moist to saturated soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats that stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.

The species is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems and its potential use in restoration projects.
