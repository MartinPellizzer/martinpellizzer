Phyllanthus tetrander

Phyllanthus tetrander, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.
