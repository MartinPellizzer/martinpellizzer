Pouteria crassipedicellata

Pouteria crassipedicellata, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces large, woody fruits that contain the edible seeds, known as Brazil nuts. These seeds are rich in nutrients, particularly selenium and healthy fats.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a variety of wildlife.
