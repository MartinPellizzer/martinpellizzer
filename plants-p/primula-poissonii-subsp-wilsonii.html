Primula poissonii subsp. wilsonii

Primula poissonii subsp.

wilsonii, commonly known as the Wilson's primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China. It features dense clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its attractive flowers and hardy nature.
