Priestleya laevigata

Priestleya laevigata, commonly known as the smoothleaf flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, smooth leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to arid environments.

Priestleya laevigata is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native range.
