Poivrea coccinea var. macrophylla

Poivrea coccinea var. macrophylla, commonly known as the red-flowered firethorn, is a cultivar of the firethorn plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant red berries and thorny stems, which are typical features of the genus Poivrea. The leaves are ovate and dark green, with a slightly serrated edge. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a hedge.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy flowers and persistent berries, which add visual interest throughout the growing season.
