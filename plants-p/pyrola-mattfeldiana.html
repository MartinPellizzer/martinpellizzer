Pyrola mattfeldiana

Pyrola mattfeldiana, commonly known as the Mattfeld's wintergreen, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink, which bloom in late winter or early spring. It thrives in cool, moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Pyrola mattfeldiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
