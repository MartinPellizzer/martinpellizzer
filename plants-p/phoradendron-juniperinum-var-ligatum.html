Phoradendron juniperinum var. ligatum

Phoradendron juniperinum var. ligatum, commonly known as the Florida mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the southeastern United States.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or pinkish flowers that are often clustered together. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients but can photosynthesize to some extent. Its berries are typically red or yellow and are dispersed by birds.

This species plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and influencing host tree growth.
