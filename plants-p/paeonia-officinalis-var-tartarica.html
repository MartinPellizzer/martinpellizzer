Paeonia officinalis var. tartarica

Paeonia officinalis var. tartarica, commonly known as the Turkish peony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It features showy, double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has deeply lobed, dark green leaves and a rhizomatous root system. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine highlight its significance in both horticulture and phytotherapy.
