Piptochaetium panicoides

Piptochaetium panicoides, commonly known as the panicle grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and panicle-shaped inflorescence, which consists of multiple branches bearing spikelets. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Piptochaetium panicoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
