Potentilla tormentilla var. lusitanica

Potentilla tormentilla var. lusitanica, commonly known as the Lusitanian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant features small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated and hairy.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, arid conditions.
