Papaver coloradense

Papaver coloradense, commonly known as the Colorado poppy, is a striking wildflower native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals and a distinctive, saucer-shaped bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, thriving in full sun. Its flowers bloom in early summer, adding a burst of color to mountainous regions.

The Colorado poppy is also noted for its medicinal properties, historically used by Native American tribes for various remedies.
