Passiflora oliviformis

Passiflora oliviformis, commonly known as the olive-shaped passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed fresh or used in jams and preserves. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough, textured surface.

Passiflora oliviformis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
