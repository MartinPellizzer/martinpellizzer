Papaver croceum subsp. stanovense

Papaver croceum subsp.

stanovense, commonly known as the Stanovenskaya poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
