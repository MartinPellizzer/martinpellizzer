Persea fusca var. fusca

Persea fusca var. fusca, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly waxy texture. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a smooth, orange to reddish persimmon with a sweet, non-astringent flavor.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, making it a popular choice in tropical and subtropical regions.
