Poecilopteris costata var. rubicunda

Poecilopteris costata var. rubicunda, commonly known as the red-stemmed cliff fern, is a fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, reddish-brown stems and lance-shaped, serrated fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rocky outcrops and forest floors. Its fronds are evergreen and can reach lengths of up to 30 inches, making it a striking addition to botanical gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in humid, woodland conditions.
