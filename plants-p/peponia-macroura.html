Peponia macroura

Peponia macroura, commonly known as the long-tongued melon.

It is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant produces yellow flowers with orange centers, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit, resembling a small melon, is edible and has a sweet, honey-like flavor.

Peponia macroura is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinator populations.
