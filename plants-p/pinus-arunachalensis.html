Pinus arunachalensis

Pinus arunachalensis, commonly known as the Arunachal pine, is a species of conifer native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in bundles of five. The tree produces cones that contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate to subtropical climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
