Prunus mutabilis f. evanescens

Prunus mutabilis f. evanescens, commonly known as the fading cherry, is a unique cultivar of the Prunus mutabilis species.

It is characterized by its changing flower colors, which shift from white to pink and then to a deep red as the blossoms mature. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. Its flowers appear in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens.

The leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy green surface and turn yellow in the fall, contributing to its seasonal beauty.
