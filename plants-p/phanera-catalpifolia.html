Phanera catalpifolia

Phanera catalpifolia, commonly known as the catalpa tree, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Its fruit is a long, slender capsule that splits open to release its seeds.

The catalpa tree is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and shade-providing canopy.
