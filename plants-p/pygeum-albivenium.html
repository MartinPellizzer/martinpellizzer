Pygeum albivenium

Pygeum albivenium, commonly known as the African sour plum, is a small deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries. Its fruit is rich in antioxidants and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The tree thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna habitats.
