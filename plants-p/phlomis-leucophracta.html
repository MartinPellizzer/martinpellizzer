Phlomis leucophracta

Phlomis leucophracta, commonly known as white-flowered mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and hardy member of the mint family.
