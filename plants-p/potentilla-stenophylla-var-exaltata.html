Potentilla stenophylla var. exaltata

Potentilla stenophylla var. exaltata, commonly known as the tallleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are typically borne on long, slender peduncles. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes in suitable habitats.
