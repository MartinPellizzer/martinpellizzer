Persea Carolinensis

Persea carolinensis, commonly known as the avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern Mexico. It is widely cultivated for its nutrient-rich fruit, which has become a global culinary staple. The tree thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish. Its leaves are broad and glossy, while its flowers are small and fragrant. This versatile plant plays a significant role in both agriculture and ecosystems.
