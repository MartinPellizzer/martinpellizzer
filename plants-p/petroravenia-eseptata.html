Petroravenia eseptata

Petroravenia eseptata, commonly known as the rock raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage, with leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, edible berries that are yellowish in color and have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
