Piper matthewii

Piper matthewii, commonly known as the Matthew's pepper, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny white flowers. It produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature.

Piper matthewii is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
