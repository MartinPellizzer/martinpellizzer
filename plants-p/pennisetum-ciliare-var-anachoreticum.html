Pennisetum ciliare var. anachoreticum

Pennisetum ciliare var. anachoreticum, commonly known as the desert panicgrass, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, tufted clumps with narrow, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, white to pale green florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soils, making it a hardy ornamental and forage plant.

Its growth habit and resilience make it suitable for erosion control and landscaping in dry climates.
