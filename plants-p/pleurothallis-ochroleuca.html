Pleurothallis ochroleuca

Pleurothallis ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow-veined orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers with yellow and green hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, and it produces fragrant blossoms during the warmer months.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
