Phaseolus latifolius

Phaseolus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and erect growth habit. The plant produces pods that contain several edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
