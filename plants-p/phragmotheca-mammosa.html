Phragmotheca mammosa

Phragmotheca mammosa, commonly known as the giant milkweed, is a striking plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple, which attract a variety of pollinators. The plant's leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its sap contains latex, which can be toxic to some animals and humans.

Phragmotheca mammosa plays an important role in its ecosystem, supporting biodiversity through its nectar and providing habitat for various insects.
