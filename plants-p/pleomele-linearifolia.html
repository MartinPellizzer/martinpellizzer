Pleomele linearifolia

Pleomele linearifolia, commonly known as the linear-leaf ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and long, narrow leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of yellow or white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Pleomele linearifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
