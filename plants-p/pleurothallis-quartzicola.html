Pleurothallis quartzicola

Pleurothallis quartzicola, commonly known as the quartz stone orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, high-altitude environments. The plant is characterized by its delicate, pale green leaves and intricate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. It prefers indirect light and well-draining substrates, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
