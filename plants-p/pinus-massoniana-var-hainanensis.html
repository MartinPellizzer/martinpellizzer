Pinus massoniana var. hainanensis

Pinus massoniana var. hainanensis, commonly known as the Hainan fir, is a variant of the Chinese fir native to the tropical and subtropical regions of southern China, particularly Hainan Island.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant exhibits a unique adaptation to its humid, warm climate, with dense foliage that helps retain moisture. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against environmental stressors.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
