Potentilla thuringiaca f. parviflora

Potentilla thuringiaca f. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf flowering cinquefoil, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to Central Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically in clusters of five. The plant has finely divided, dark green leaves that are alternate and covered in a soft, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
