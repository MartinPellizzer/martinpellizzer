Phlox caespitosa subsp. pulvinata

Phlox caespitosa subsp.

pulvinata, commonly known as the purpletop phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to lavender.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and wet meadows. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The species is valued in wetland restoration projects due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
