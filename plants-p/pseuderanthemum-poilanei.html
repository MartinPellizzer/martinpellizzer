Pseuderanthemum poilanei

Pseuderanthemum poilanei, commonly known as the Madagascar spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spider-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Pseuderanthemum poilanei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
