Pleioblastus tanegasimensis

Pleioblastus tanegasimensis, commonly known as the Tanegashima bamboo, is a species native to Japan, specifically the island of Tanegashima.

It is a clumping bamboo that typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 meters, with slender, greenish-brown culms that are often covered in a layer of white or grayish hairs. The leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slightly pointed tip. This bamboo is well adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
