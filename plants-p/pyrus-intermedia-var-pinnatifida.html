Pyrus intermedia var. pinnatifida

Pyrus intermedia var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a variant of the European pear species native to China.

It is characterized by its medium-sized, oval-shaped fruits with a smooth, white to yellowish skin. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin with a pinnatifid (broadly lobed) appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its edible fruit.
