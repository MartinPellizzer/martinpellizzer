Pappophorum scoparium

Pappophorum scoparium, commonly known as the silky grass or silver grass, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, silvery-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its feathery seed heads add visual interest and are often used in dried floral arrangements.
