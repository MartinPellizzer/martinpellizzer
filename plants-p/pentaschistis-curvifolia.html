Pentaschistis curvifolia

Pentaschistis curvifolia, commonly known as the curl-leaved sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and small, often blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically narrow, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps the plant conserve water in dry environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh conditions.
