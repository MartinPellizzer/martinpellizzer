Plectranthus henryi

Plectranthus henryi, commonly known as Henry's plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves, which are often hairy and have a distinctive grayish-green color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, blooming in shades of purple or blue. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Plectranthus henryi is also valued for its aromatic foliage, which can be used in herbal teas or as a natural remedy.
