Phlox lanata

Phlox lanata, commonly known as downy phlox, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Phlox lanata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
