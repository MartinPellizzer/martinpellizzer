Panicum acroanthum var. brevipedicellatum

Panicum acroanthum var. brevipedicellatum, commonly known as the short-pedicellate bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and blue-green foliage, which becomes more pronounced during the growing season. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often mistaken for wheat or other cereal grains. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open fields and prairies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in native plant gardens.
