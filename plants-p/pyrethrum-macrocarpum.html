Pyrethrum macrocarpum

Pyrethrum macrocarpum, commonly known as the giant pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces spherical, hairy seed heads that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by the wind. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

Pyrethrum macrocarpum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
