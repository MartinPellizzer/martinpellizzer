Pyrus tauricola

Pyrus tauricola, commonly known as the Tauric pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the Caucasus region, specifically parts of southern Russia and Georgia. The plant is characterized by its small, deciduous leaves and clusters of white flowers that appear in early spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like berry, is typically yellow or orange when ripe.
