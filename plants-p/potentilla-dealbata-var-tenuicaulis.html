Potentilla dealbata var. tenuicaulis

Potentilla dealbata var. tenuicaulis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features slender, hairy stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in a soft, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in difficult terrain.
