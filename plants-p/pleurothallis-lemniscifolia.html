Pleurothallis lemniscifolia

Pleurothallis lemniscifolia, commonly known as the "butterfly orchid," is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers with a unique, butterfly-like shape. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
