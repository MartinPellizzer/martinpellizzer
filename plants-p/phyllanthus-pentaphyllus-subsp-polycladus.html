Phyllanthus pentaphyllus subsp. polycladus

Phyllanthus pentaphyllus subsp.

polycladus, commonly known as the Indian false sandalwood, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
