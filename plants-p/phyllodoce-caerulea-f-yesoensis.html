Phyllodoce caerulea f. yesoensis

Phyllodoce caerulea f. yesoensis, commonly known as the Yeso form of the blue mountain heath, is a variant of the blue mountain heath native to Japan.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically a deep blue-green color, providing a striking visual contrast in shaded environments. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, cool forest understories.

This form is particularly noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its unique foliage and hardiness.
