Pituranthos scoparius var. muratianus

Pituranthos scoparius var. muratianus, commonly known as the Muratian desert honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub with woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features reflect its evolutionary adaptation to harsh desert environments.
