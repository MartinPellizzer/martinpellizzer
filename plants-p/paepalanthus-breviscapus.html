Paepalanthus breviscapus

Paepalanthus breviscapus, commonly known as the short-stemmed puya, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences with bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the spring. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil to thrive.
