Polygonum kirinense

Polygonum kirinense, commonly known as the Kirin knotweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants such as buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a prominent midrib and a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Polygonum kirinense is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.
