Piper pandoense

Piper pandoense, commonly known as the Andean pepper, is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to the highlands of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in humid, montane environments, often found in the cloud forests of countries like Ecuador and Peru. The plant produces small, green to yellowish fruits that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties. Its leaves are also used in local remedies and are characterized by their aromatic, pungent scent.

Piper pandoense plays a significant role in the biodiversity of its native ecosystems and is valued for both its ecological and cultural importance.
