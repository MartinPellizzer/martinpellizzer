Panicum paucispicatum

Panicum paucispicatum, commonly known as the sparse-bracted panicgrass, is a species of grass native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, bract-like structures, giving it its specific epithet. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in ecological restoration projects.
