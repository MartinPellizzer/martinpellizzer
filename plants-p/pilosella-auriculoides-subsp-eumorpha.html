Pilosella auriculoides subsp. eumorpha

Pilosella auriculoides subsp.

eumorpha, commonly known as the auriculate pilosella, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are hairy and often have auriculate (ear-like) lobes at the base.

The flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in a flat, disk-like inflorescence.
