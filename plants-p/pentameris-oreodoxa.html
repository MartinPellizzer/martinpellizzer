Pentameris oreodoxa

Pentameris oreodoxa, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a dense canopy. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a prominent midrib. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.
