Populus nigra var. italica

Populus nigra var. italica, commonly known as the Italian black poplar, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are important for pollination. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found near rivers and wetlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional landscapes.
