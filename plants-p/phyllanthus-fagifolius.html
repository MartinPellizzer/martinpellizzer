Phyllanthus fagifolius

Phyllanthus fagifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its unique floral structures and milky sap. The plant typically has alternate, simple leaves that are often broad and ovate in shape. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually clustered in cyathia, a feature typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Phyllanthus fagifolius is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forests and along stream banks.
