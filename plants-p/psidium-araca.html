Psidium araca

Psidium araca, commonly known as the Brazilian guava or araca, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Psidium araca is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
