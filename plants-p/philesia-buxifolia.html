Philesia buxifolia

Philesia buxifolia, commonly known as the box-leaf philesia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant for woodland gardens.

Philesia buxifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
