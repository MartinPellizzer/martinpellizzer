Piper pervulgatum

Piper pervulgatum, commonly known as the Peruvian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It produces small, peppery fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some medicinal uses in local communities.
