Pilosella arvicola subsp. subhyperborea

Pilosella arvicola subsp.

subhyperborea, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to alpine and arctic regions of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flower heads.

The plant thrives in nutrient-poor, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It is often found in open habitats such as tundra, meadows, and mountain slopes.

Its morphology reflects its ecological niche, with compact growth patterns that help it withstand harsh climatic conditions.
