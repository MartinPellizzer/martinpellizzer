Pharnaceum lanuginosum

Pharnaceum lanuginosum, commonly known as the silky pharnaceum, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories. Its hairy texture helps reduce water loss, making it well adapted to humid climates.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
