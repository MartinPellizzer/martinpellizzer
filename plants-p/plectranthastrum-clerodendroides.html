Plectranthastrum clerodendroides

Plectranthastrum clerodendroides, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long flowering period, which can extend from spring through early autumn.
