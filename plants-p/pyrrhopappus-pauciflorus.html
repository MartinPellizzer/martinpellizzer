Pyrrhopappus pauciflorus

Pyrrhopappus pauciflorus, commonly known as the sparse-flowered pappus, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as hairy leaves and a deep root system to conserve water. Its flowers are usually small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Pyrrhopappus pauciflorus is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
