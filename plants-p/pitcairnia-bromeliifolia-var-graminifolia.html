Pitcairnia bromeliifolia var. graminifolia

Pitcairnia bromeliifolia var. graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-leaved pitcairnia, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves that form a rosette, creating a dense structure that can hold water. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences with showy, colorful bracts that attract pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
