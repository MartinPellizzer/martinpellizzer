Phaca tomentosa

Phaca tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly phaca, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in grassland or woodland habitats. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
