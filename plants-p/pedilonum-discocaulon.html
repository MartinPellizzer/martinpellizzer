Pedilonum discocaulon

Pedilonum discocaulon, commonly known as the false bellflower, is a member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Pedilonum discocaulon is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
