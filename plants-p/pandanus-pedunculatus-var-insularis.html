Pandanus pedunculatus var. insularis

Pandanus pedunculatus var. insularis, commonly known as the island pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the ends of its branches. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often growing near the shoreline.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appearance and its cultural significance in local communities.
