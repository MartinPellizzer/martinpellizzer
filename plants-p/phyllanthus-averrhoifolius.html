Phyllanthus averrhoifolius

Phyllanthus averrhoifolius, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often lobed or toothed. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional African medicine for various ailments.
