Paspalum guttatum

Paspalum guttatum, commonly known as the spotted paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive silver-gray leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Paspalum guttatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
