Poa chilensis var. robustior

Poa chilensis var. robustior, commonly known as the Chilean tall meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, tufted growth habit, often forming expansive grasslands. The plant produces spikelets that are typically clustered at the ends of the stems, with small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to well-drained soils, often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its robust growth and ecological significance, it plays an important role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife.
