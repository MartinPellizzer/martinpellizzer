Philodendron elongatum

Philodendron elongatum, commonly known as the long-leaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly variegated pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by green, berry-like fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Philodendron elongatum is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
