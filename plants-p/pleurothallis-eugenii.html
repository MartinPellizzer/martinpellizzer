Pleurothallis eugenii

Pleurothallis eugenii, commonly known as the Eugen's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often arranged in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its delicate nature, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
