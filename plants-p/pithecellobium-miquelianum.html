Pithecellobium miquelianum

Pithecellobium miquelianum, commonly known as the Miquel's pithecellobium, is a species of legume native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody pods containing edible, sweet-seeded fruits that are often consumed by local communities. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional benefits, it is also cultivated in some areas for both aesthetic and agricultural purposes.
