Pleurothallis serpentula

Pleurothallis serpentula, commonly known as the serpent orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spiral-shaped inflorescence. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and green.

Pleurothallis serpentula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, intricate blooms.
