Pontederia dilatata

Pontederia dilatata, commonly known as pickerelweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of the Americas.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in spikes above its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in ponds and marshes. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and it serves as a food source for various wildlife.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal in aquatic gardens.
