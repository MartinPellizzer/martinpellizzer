Prunus persica f. nuda

Prunus persica f. nuda, commonly known as the naked peach, is a cultivar of the peach tree characterized by its lack of thorns on the branches.

This variety produces small, elongated fruits that are typically less sweet and more acidic than standard peaches. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive, delicate flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth and fruiting.

The nuda form is prized for its aesthetic appeal and ease of maintenance in garden settings.
