Passiflora capsularis var. acutiloba

Passiflora capsularis var. acutiloba, commonly known as the sharp-lobed passionflower, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and mammals. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
