Potentilla diversidentata

Potentilla diversidentata, commonly known as the variable cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, palmately divided leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are usually serrated and have a slightly hairy texture, which helps it retain moisture in its arid habitat.

Potentilla diversidentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and adaptability.
