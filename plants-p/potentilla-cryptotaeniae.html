Potentilla cryptotaeniae

Potentilla cryptotaeniae, commonly known as the Japanese potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture.

Potentilla cryptotaeniae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in woodland gardens.
