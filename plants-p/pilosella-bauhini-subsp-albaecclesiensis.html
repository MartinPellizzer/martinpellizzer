Pilosella bauhini subsp. albaecclesiensis

Pilosella bauhini subsp.

albaecclesiensis, commonly known as the white-flowered pilosella, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray leaves and small, daisy-like white flowers.

This subspecies is native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe and thrives in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant has a compact growth form and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
