Peristeria guttata

Peristeria guttata, commonly known as the spotted nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features dark green, velvety leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Peristeria guttata is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
