Pulsatilla × juzepczukii

Pulsatilla × juzepczukii, commonly known as the hybrid pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Pulsatilla vulgaris and Pulsatilla patens, characterized by its showy, early spring blooms. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring well-drained, calcareous soils. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive yellow center, and it has palmately divided leaves.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
