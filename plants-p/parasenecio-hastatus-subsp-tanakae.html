Parasenecio hastatus subsp. tanakae

Parasenecio hastatus subsp.

tanakae, commonly known as the Japanese parasenecio, is a herbaceous plant native to Japan. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The species produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
