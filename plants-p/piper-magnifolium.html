Piper magnifolium

Piper magnifolium, commonly known as the giant arrowroot or sweet sarsaparilla, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Its roots are used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.
