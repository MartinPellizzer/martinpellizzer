Pseudobravoa

Pseudobravoa, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, translucent petals that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and exhibits unique adaptations to its harsh, arid environment. Its flowers are typically short-lived, blooming only once in the plant's lifetime.

Pseudobravoa is also notable for its ability to store water in its leaf rosette, a feature that aids in its survival in extreme conditions.
