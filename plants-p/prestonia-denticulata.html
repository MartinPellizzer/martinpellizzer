Prestonia denticulata

Prestonia denticulata, commonly known as the toothed prestonia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family, which includes milkweeds and related genera. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its leaves are opposite and often have a serrated or toothed margin, giving it its common name.

Prestonia denticulata is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
