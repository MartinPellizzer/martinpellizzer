Pimelea longifolia var. lanceolata

Pimelea longifolia var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mistflower, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its lance-shaped, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. Its botanical features include woody stems and a preference for well-drained soils in full sun.

It is often found in heathlands and open woodlands, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
