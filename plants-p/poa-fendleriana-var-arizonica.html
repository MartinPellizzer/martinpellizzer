Poa fendleriana var. arizonica

Poa fendleriana var. arizonica, commonly known as Arizona bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh, dry conditions.
