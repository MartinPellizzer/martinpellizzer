Polypodiodes niponica var. wattii

Polypodiodes niponica var. wattii, commonly known as the Japanese polypody, is a fern species native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the leaves.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
