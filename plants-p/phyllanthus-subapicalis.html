Phyllanthus subapicalis

Phyllanthus subapicalis, commonly known as the African butterbean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces capsule fruits containing several seeds.

The plant is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in semi-arid conditions.
