Phrynium densiflorum

Phrynium densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in an inflorescence called a spadix, surrounded by a spathe. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Phrynium densiflorum is also valued for its edible tubers, which are used in local cuisines.
