Phalaenopsis thailandica f. aurea

Phalaenopsis thailandica f. aurea, commonly known as the Thai orchid or golden phalaenopsis, is a hybrid orchid native to Thailand.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow petals and a velvety green to white lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It produces long, pendant flower spikes with multiple blooms, often in shades of yellow and white.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated for indoor gardening and floral arrangements.
