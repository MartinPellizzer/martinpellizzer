Psydrax parviflorus subsp. parviflorus

Psydrax parviflorus subsp.

parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered psidium, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It features small, white to pale yellow flowers clustered in dense inflorescences.

The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, fleshy berries that are typically green when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
