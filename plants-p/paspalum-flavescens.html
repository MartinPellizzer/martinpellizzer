Paspalum flavescens

Paspalum flavescens, commonly known as yellow beardgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to golden in color, which gives it its common name.

Paspalum flavescens is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and tolerate various environmental conditions.
