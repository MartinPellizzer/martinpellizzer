Pinanga celebica

Pinanga celebica, commonly known as the Celebes bamboo or bamboo palm, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, clustered stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into edible, fibrous fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and sustainable use of its fibrous material.

Pinanga celebica plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
