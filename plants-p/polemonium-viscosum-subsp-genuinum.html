Polemonium viscosum subsp. genuinum

Polemonium viscosum subsp.

genuinum, commonly known as the sticky polemonium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and stream banks, preferring rich, well-drained soils.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a sticky substance. Its showy, bell-shaped flowers are usually blue to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
