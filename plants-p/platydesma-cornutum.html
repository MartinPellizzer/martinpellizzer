Platydesma cornutum

Platydesma cornutum, commonly known as the horned flatleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a distinctive waxy coating and a slightly serrated margin. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in terminal spikes and bloom in late spring.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland habitats.
