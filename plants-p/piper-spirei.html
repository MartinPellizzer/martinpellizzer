Piper spirei

Piper spirei, commonly known as the creeping groundcherry, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary use in certain regions.
