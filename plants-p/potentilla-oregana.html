Potentilla oregana

Potentilla oregana, commonly known as Oregon grape, is a flowering shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems, dark green leaves, and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but typically have a tart flavor. Oregon grape is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and streamside habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
