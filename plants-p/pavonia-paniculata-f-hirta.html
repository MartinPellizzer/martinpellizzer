Pavonia paniculata f. hirta

Pavonia paniculata f. hirta, commonly known as the hairy pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red bract-like flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its specific epithet "hirta.". It produces panicles of small, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer months.
