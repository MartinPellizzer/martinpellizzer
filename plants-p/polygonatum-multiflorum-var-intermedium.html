Polygonatum multiflorum var. intermedium

Polygonatum multiflorum var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate Solomon's seal, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features arching stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in late spring to early summer. Its berries, which appear in late summer, are dark blue and edible.

It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
