Potamogeton uliginosus

Potamogeton uliginosus, commonly known as the bog pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and wetlands. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are usually green to reddish in color, and it produces small, green flowers that are arranged in spike-like clusters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Potamogeton uliginosus is also used in phytoremediation efforts due to its ability to absorb nutrients and heavy metals from the water.
