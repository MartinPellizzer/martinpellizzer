Potentilla × caliacrae

Potentilla × caliacrae, commonly known as the hybrid cinquefoil, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant floral display.

It typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are densely arranged along the stems. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Potentilla × caliacrae prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable choice for landscaping.
