Phytelephas dasyneura

Phytelephas dasyneura, commonly known as the giant areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by elongated, curved fruits containing a single seed. Its botanical features make it well adapted to humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
