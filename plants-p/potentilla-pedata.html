Potentilla pedata

Potentilla pedata, commonly known as the trailing cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground. Its leaves are typically divided into five oval-shaped lobes, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

Potentilla pedata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.
