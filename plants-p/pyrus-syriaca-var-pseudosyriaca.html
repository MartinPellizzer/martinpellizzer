Pyrus syriaca var. pseudosyriaca

Pyrus syriaca var. pseudosyriaca, commonly known as the Syrian white pear, is a variant of the white pear tree native to the Middle East.

It is characterized by its white to pale yellow fruits, which are typically small and sweet. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional horticulture.
