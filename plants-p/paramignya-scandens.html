Paramignya scandens

Paramignya scandens, commonly known as the climbing tamarind.

It is a woody vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia. The plant features compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces yellowish-green flowers that develop into pods containing flat, seed-rich seeds. Its botanical structure supports its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend other vegetation.

The plant is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
