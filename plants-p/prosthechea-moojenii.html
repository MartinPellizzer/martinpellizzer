Prosthechea moojenii

Prosthechea moojenii, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its pendulous, yellow-orange flower spikes that bloom during the summer months. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Prosthechea moojenii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
