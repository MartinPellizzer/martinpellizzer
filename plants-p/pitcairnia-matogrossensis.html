Pitcairnia matogrossensis

Pitcairnia matogrossensis, commonly known as the Matogrossense air plant, is a species of bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright red or orange flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are typically lanceolate and covered in fine trichomes, aiding in water retention.

This species thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
