Pyrethrum argenteum

Pyrethrum argenteum, commonly known as silver pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spherical flower heads that contain numerous tiny, glandular structures called trichomes. These trichomes secrete pyrethrins, which are natural insecticides used in organic pest control.

Pyrethrum argenteum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ecological benefits in pest management.
