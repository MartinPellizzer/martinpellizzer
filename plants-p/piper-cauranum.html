Piper cauranum

Piper cauranum, commonly known as the Ceylon pepper or Malabar pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark purple berries that are used as a spice. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinctive glossy appearance.

The fruit develops in clusters and is harvested when fully mature for its aromatic and pungent flavor.
