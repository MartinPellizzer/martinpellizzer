Physochlaina grandiflora

Physochlaina grandiflora, commonly known as the largeflower nightshade, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and upright.

The plant contains alkaloids and is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades.
