Paspalum longipilum

Paspalum longipilum, commonly known as the long-haired paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, fibrous root system, which helps it thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Paspalum longipilum is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
