Peschiera benthamiana

Peschiera benthamiana, commonly known as the bentham's lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces showy, bell-shaped blooms.

It is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in its native habitat.
