Phlox hookeri

Phlox hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's phlox, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems that bear clusters of showy, fragrant flowers. The flowers are typically pink to purple in color and bloom in early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite along the stems.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.
