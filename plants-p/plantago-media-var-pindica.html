Plantago media var. pindica

Plantago media var. pindica, commonly known as the greater plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in meadows.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.
