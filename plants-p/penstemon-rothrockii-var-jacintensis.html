Penstemon rothrockii var. jacintensis

Penstemon rothrockii var. jacintensis, commonly known as the jacintensis penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the summer season.
