Pelargonium × loudonianum

Pelargonium × loudonianum, commonly known as the Siam Begonia, is a hybrid plant originating from crosses between Pelargonium hortorum and Pelargonium × inerme.

It is characterized by its striking, large, and colorful flowers that come in shades of pink, red, and white, often with a distinctive double-petaled structure. The plant has a trailing growth habit, making it suitable for hanging baskets or as a ground cover. Its foliage is typically heart-shaped and varies in color from deep green to variegated patterns.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
