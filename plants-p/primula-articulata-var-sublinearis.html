Primula articulata var. sublinearis

Primula articulata var. sublinearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The flowers typically bloom in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural habitats. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

The plant is valued for its horticultural appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
