Plantago ovatifolia

Plantago ovatifolia, commonly known as broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a dense spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a poultice for wounds and skin irritations.
