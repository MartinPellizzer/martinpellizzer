Polygonum myosurus

Polygonum myosurus, commonly known as bladder campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit, with smooth, often reddish stems. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to rhombic in shape, and covered with fine hairs.

Polygonum myosurus is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and is often found in moist soils along streambanks and woodland edges.
