Phledinium

Phledinium, commonly known as the common phledinium, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its composite flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk flowers. The leaves are typically alternate, lanceolate, and often hairy, providing a distinguishing feature. Phledinium species are often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

They play a role in pollination and serve as a food source for various insects and small animals.
