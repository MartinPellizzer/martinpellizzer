Porpax ustulata

Porpax ustulata, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that are highly attractive to hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
