Polygonum ooagariense

Polygonum ooagariense, commonly known as the Ooagari knotweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is native to parts of East Asia, including Japan and China, where it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its erect stems, heart-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. It has been studied for its potential medicinal properties, though its ecological impact remains under investigation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, but care must be taken to prevent its spread into natural habitats.
