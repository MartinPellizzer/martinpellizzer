Palhinhaea veigae

Palhinhaea veigae, commonly known as the Veiga's cactus, is a unique species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and reduced leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, adding a striking visual element to its appearance.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
