Polybotrya latisquamosa

Polybotrya latisquamosa, commonly known as the broad-scaled orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its unique floral structure includes a specialized lip that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
