Pogostemon barbinervis

Pogostemon barbinervis, commonly known as the hairy-veined pogostemon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves with hairy veins, and small, clustered flowers that range in color from purple to pink. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
