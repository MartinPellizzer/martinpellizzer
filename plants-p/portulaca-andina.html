Portulaca andina

Portulaca andina, commonly known as mountain purslane, is a low-growing annual plant native to South America.

It features fleshy, succulent leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant thrives in hot, arid environments and is well adapted to poor soil conditions. Its stems and leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Portulaca andina is also valued as a ornamental plant for its drought tolerance and attractive appearance.
