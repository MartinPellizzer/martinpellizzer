Piper statum

Piper statum, commonly known as the slender pipevine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Piper statum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
