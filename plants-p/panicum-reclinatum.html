Panicum reclinatum

Panicum reclinatum, commonly known as the drooping panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and drooping inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, thriving in poorly drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Panicum reclinatum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
