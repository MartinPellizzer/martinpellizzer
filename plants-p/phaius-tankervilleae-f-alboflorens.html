Phaius tankervilleae f. alboflorens

Phaius tankervilleae f. alboflorens, commonly known as the white-flowered marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in wet, swampy environments and requires consistent moisture and partial shade. Its flowers are often adorned with a distinctive yellow-green lip, adding to its ornamental value.

This cultivar is prized for its striking appearance and is commonly cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
