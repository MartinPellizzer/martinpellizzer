Phlomis franchetiana var. leptophylla

Phlomis franchetiana var. leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf phlomis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants, and is characterized by its hairy stems and serrated leaves. The plant produces showy, pale blue to purple flowers in dense spikes, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its visual appeal.
