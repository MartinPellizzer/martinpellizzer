Philippia macrocalyx

Philippia macrocalyx, commonly known as the giant white bellflower, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, often reaching up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water in its native arid environments. Its stems are often hairy and can grow quite tall, sometimes exceeding two meters in height.

Philippia macrocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
