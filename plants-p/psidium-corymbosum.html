Psidium corymbosum

Psidium corymbosum, commonly known as the common guava, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that produces fragrant, white flowers followed by edible berries. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while the fruit varies in size and color depending on the variety.

The plant is valued for its nutritional content and is widely cultivated for both commercial and culinary purposes.
