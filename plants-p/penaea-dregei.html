Penaea dregei

Penaea dregei, commonly known as the Cape mountain gum.

It is a small, deciduous tree native to the Western Cape region of South Africa. The plant has smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by elongated, woody fruits.

Penaea dregei is adapted to the dry, Mediterranean climate of its native habitat and is often found in rocky outcrops and mountainous areas.
