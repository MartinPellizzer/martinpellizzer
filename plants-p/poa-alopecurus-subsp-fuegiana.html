Poa alopecurus subsp. fuegiana

Poa alopecurus subsp.

fuegiana, commonly known as the Falkland Island grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly the Falkland Islands. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats.

The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is adapted to coastal and maritime environments.

Its ecological significance includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local fauna.
