Penstemon barbatus var. trichander

Penstemon barbatus var. trichander, commonly known as the woolly penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces seed capsules that release small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
