Pereuphora coronata

Pereuphora coronata, commonly known as the crown cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
