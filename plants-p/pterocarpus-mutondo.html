Pterocarpus mutondo

Pterocarpus mutondo, commonly known as African padauk, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its aesthetic appeal and durability. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland areas.

The tree is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
