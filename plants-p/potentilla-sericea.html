Potentilla sericea

Potentilla sericea, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense, fine hairs covering the leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

Potentilla sericea is a hardy perennial that is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
