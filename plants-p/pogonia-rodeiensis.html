Pogonia rodeiensis

Pogonia rodeiensis, commonly known as the Rode Island bog orchid, is a rare and endangered orchid species native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink and purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic bogs and requires specific soil conditions to thrive. It has a complex symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi, which is essential for its nutrient uptake.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, its population has declined significantly, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
