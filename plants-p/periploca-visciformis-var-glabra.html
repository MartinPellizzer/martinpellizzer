Periploca visciformis var. glabra

Periploca visciformis var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth periploca, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
