Poa × wippraensis

Poa × wippraensis, commonly known as the Wippra meadowgrass, is a hybrid grass species belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats. The plant typically produces spikelets that are loosely arranged, with small, awnless lemmas. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wet meadows and along streambanks.

Due to its hybrid origin, Poa × wippraensis exhibits a range of morphological variations depending on its genetic parents.
