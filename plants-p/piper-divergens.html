Piper divergens

Piper divergens, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, peppery fruits. Its vines can spread extensively, making it a common ornamental and sometimes invasive species.

The plant is valued for its aromatic foliage and potential medicinal properties.
