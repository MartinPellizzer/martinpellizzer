Praecereus euchlorus subsp. smithianus

Praecereus euchlorus subsp.

smithianus, commonly known as the Smith's ghost orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family. It is characterized by its pale greenish-white petals and a distinctive, elongated floral structure.

Native to the tropical regions of South America, this orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom during the wet season.

Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
