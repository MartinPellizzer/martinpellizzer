Pouzolzia cochinchinensis

Pouzolzia cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Chinese pouzolzia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

The species is noted for its adaptability and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
