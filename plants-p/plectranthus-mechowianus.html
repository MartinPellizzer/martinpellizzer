Plectranthus mechowianus

Plectranthus mechowianus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Plectranthus mechowianus is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
