Pseuderia pauciflora

Pseuderia pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered spider orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its small, spider-like appearance with intricate, translucent petals and a short, slender stem. The plant produces only a few flowers per inflorescence, typically in shades of white and green, which helps it blend into its forest habitat. Pseuderia pauciflora thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on the bases of trees or in dense undergrowth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and habitat destruction.
