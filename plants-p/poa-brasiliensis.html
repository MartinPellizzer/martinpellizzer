Poa brasiliensis

Poa brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Poa brasiliensis is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.
