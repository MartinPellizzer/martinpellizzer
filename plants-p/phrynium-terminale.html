Phrynium terminale

Phrynium terminale, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot or swamp arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial with large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. Its rhizomes are edible and are used as a starch source in some regions.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.
