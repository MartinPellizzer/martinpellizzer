Pleione maculata var. arthuriana

Pleione maculata var. arthuriana, commonly known as the Arthur's spotted pleione, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its elegant, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The flowers typically feature a combination of white, pink, and purple hues, with distinctive markings that give it its common name. This orchid thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
