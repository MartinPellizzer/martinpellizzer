Panicum pallidifolium

Panicum pallidifolium, commonly known as the pale-leaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, pale green leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces fluffy, seed-bearing panicles that are typically pink to reddish in color, adding visual interest during the flowering season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it a suitable choice for ornamental landscaping.

Due to its low maintenance requirements, it is often used in native plant gardens and restoration projects.
