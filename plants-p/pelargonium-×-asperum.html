Pelargonium × asperum

Pelargonium × asperum, commonly known as the roughleaf geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a range of colors including pink, red, and white. Native to South Africa, this species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant. It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various growing conditions.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
