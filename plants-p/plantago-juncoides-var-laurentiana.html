Plantago juncoides var. laurentiana

Plantago juncoides var. laurentiana, commonly known as the Laurentian plantain, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. Its flowering stems bear dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various insect species.
