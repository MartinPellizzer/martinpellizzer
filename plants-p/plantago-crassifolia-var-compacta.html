Plantago crassifolia var. compacta

Plantago crassifolia var. compacta, commonly known as the compact plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of Europe and Asia.

It features dense, rosette-like growth of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in wetlands.

Its compact growth habit and adaptability make it a common sight in various habitats across its native range.
