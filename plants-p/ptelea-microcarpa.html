Ptelea microcarpa

Ptelea microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by small, red berries that are edible but not particularly palatable. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist woodlands and along streambanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
