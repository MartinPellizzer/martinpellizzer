Phaseolus semierectus var. atropurpureus

Phaseolus semierectus var. atropurpureus, commonly known as the purple runner bean, is a variant of the runner bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking purplish-pink flowers and edible, kidney-shaped pods that turn green as they mature. The plant is a climbing vine with compound leaves and produces beans that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and nutritional value.
