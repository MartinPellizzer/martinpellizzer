Pandanus toliarensis

Pandanus toliarensis, commonly known as the Madagascar pandanus, is a species of palm native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that develop into spiky, fibrous fruits. Its leaves are fibrous and are used for making thatch, mats, and other woven items.

It thrives in coastal and wetland areas, adapting well to humid and saline environments.
