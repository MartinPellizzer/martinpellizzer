Pelargonium × gymnophyllum

Pelargonium × gymnophyllum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, velvety leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming from spring to autumn. Native to South Africa, it is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for hanging baskets and containers.

Its adaptability to various growing conditions makes it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
