Potentilla apoiensis

Potentilla apoiensis, commonly known as the Apoiense cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, five-petaled flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or white. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
