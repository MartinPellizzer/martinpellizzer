Perfonon ferrugineum

Perfonon ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty perfonon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive rusty-brown hue on the underside, which gives it its common name. Its small, yellowish flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

Perfonon ferrugineum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
