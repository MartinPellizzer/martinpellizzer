Perlebia purpurea var. corneri

Perlebia purpurea var. corneri, commonly known as the Purple Perlebia, is a striking member of the Araceae family native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple bracts that enclose small, white flowers, creating an eye-catching display. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
