Phyllostachys bissetii

Phyllostachys bissetii, commonly known as the Bisset bamboo, is a species of grass in the tribe Bambuseae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green and glossy, providing an ornamental appearance. It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.

Phyllostachys bissetii is also valued for its use in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of sustainable materials.
