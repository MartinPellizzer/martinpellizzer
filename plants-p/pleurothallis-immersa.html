Pleurothallis immersa

Pleurothallis immersa, commonly known as the immersed orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its flowering period usually occurs during the wet season, and it is known for its unique, intricate flower structure.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
