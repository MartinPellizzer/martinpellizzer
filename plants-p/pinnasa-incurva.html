Pinnasa incurva

Pinnasa incurva, commonly known as the zigzag fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curling fronds that give it its common name. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread along the forest floor. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a unique, undulating appearance.

Pinnasa incurva thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.
