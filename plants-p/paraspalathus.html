Paraspalathus

Paraspalathus, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are characterized by their unique, often intricate flower structures that mimic the appearance of spiders, hence their common name. Paraspalathus species are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. They possess specialized roots that aid in nutrient absorption from poor soil conditions.

The genus includes several species, each with distinct floral morphologies and ecological adaptations.
