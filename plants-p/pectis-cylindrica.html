Pectis cylindrica

Pectis cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical pectis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features narrow, cylindrical leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a dense, spike-like inflorescence. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
