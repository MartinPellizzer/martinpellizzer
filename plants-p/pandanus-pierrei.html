Pandanus pierrei

Pandanus pierrei, commonly known as the Madagascar pandanus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional crafts by local communities.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and is adapted to grow in coastal and wetland environments.
