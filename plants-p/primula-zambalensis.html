Primula zambalensis

Primula zambalensis, commonly known as the Zambal primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. It has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, clustered flowers during the spring and early summer.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
