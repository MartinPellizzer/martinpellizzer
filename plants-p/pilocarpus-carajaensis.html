Pilocarpus carajaensis

Pilocarpus carajaensis, commonly known as the Brazilian wild pepper, is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that contain the seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It is notable for containing the alkaloid pilocarpine, which has various pharmacological applications.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from deforestation.
