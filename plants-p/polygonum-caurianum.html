Polygonum caurianum

Polygonum caurianum, commonly known as the Indian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or wetlands. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat.

Polygonum caurianum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
