Pinus ayacahuite var. loudoniana

Pinus ayacahuite var. loudoniana, commonly known as the Mexican white pine, is a variant of the Ayacahuite pine native to Mexico.

It is a medium-sized evergreen conifer characterized by its long, soft needles and cone-like structures. The tree produces small, woody cones that mature over several years, typical of the Pinaceae family. Its foliage is often dense, providing habitat for various wildlife species.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in landscaping and forestry.
