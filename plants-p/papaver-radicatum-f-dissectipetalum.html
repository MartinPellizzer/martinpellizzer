Papaver radicatum f. dissectipetalum

Papaver radicatum f. dissectipetalum, commonly known as the dissect-leaved poppy, is a botanical variant of the common poppy.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lace-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces striking, bright red or orange flowers with a prominent central stigma. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
