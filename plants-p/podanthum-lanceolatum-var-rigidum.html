Podanthum lanceolatum var. rigidum

Podanthum lanceolatum var. rigidum, commonly known as the rigid lance-leaf podanthum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, rigid leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
