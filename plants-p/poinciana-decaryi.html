Poinciana decaryi

Poinciana decaryi, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like leaves that turn red or orange before falling in autumn. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall.

Poinciana decaryi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
