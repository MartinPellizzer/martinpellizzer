Plectranthus stracheyi

Plectranthus stracheyi, commonly known as the Strachey's plectranthus, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct aromatic scent. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or purple in color and bloom in terminal clusters.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for garden cultivation.
