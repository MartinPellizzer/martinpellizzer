Polygala schwartziana

Polygala schwartziana, commonly known as the Schwartz's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, while its stems are slender and branching.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
