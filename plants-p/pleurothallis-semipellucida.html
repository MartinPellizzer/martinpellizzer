Pleurothallis semipellucida

Pleurothallis semipellucida, commonly known as the glassy orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree branches or rocks. The plant is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming during the wet season.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
