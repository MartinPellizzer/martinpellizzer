Phyllostachys fimbriata

Phyllostachys fimbriata, commonly known as the Fimbriated bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Bambusoideae subfamily.

It is native to East Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging culms and graceful appearance. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

Phyllostachys fimbriata is a clump-forming bamboo that can grow up to several meters in height, making it a popular choice for landscaping and garden design.
