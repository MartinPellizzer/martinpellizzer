Pogonophora trianae

Pogonophora trianae, commonly known as the red-banded flatworm, is a species of flatworm belonging to the phylum Platyhelminthes.

It is characterized by its elongated, flattened body and distinctive red coloration with dark bands. This organism is primarily found in marine environments, particularly in tropical and subtropical regions. Pogonophora trianae is a free-living species that plays a role in the benthic ecosystem by scavenging organic matter.

Its simple body structure includes a gastrovascular cavity and a system of flame cells for excretion.
