Phaseolus atropurpureus f. intermedius

Phaseolus atropurpureus f. intermedius, commonly known as the purple hull cowpea, is a botanical variant of the common cowpea.

It is characterized by its striking purple foliage and pods that transition to a darker hue as they mature. This variety is native to the Americas and is cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters.

It thrives in warm climates and is often grown in regions with well-drained soil and full sunlight.
