Pelargonium subherbaceum

Pelargonium subherbaceum, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its herbaceous stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant and low-maintenance plants.
