Poa versicolor subsp. orinosa

Poa versicolor subsp.

orinosa, commonly known as the variable bluegrass, is a grass species native to South America. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native ecosystems in its range.
