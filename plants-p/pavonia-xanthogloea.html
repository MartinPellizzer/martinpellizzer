Pavonia xanthogloea

Pavonia xanthogloea, commonly known as the yellow glabrous pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and riverbanks. The plant features showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, with five petals arranged in a distinctive, slightly irregular shape. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Pavonia xanthogloea is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
