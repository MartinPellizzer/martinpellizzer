Phyllanthus leprocarpus

Phyllanthus leprocarpus, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is known for its aromatic properties.

Phyllanthus leprocarpus is also valued for its timber, which is used in traditional crafts and furniture.
