Potamogeton tennesseensis

Potamogeton tennesseensis, commonly known as the Tennessee pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has long, slender stems that are often branching and bear small, oval-shaped leaves. It produces tiny, spike-like flowers that are usually green or reddish in color.
