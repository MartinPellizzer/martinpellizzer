Piptatherum songaricum subsp. tianschanicum

Piptatherum songaricum subsp.

tianschanicum, commonly known as the Tianshan needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and mountain slopes.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, adapted to the arid environments it inhabits.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.
