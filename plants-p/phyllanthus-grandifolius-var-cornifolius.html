Phyllanthus grandifolius var. cornifolius

Phyllanthus grandifolius var. cornifolius, commonly known as the horned or spear-leaved phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often pointed leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
