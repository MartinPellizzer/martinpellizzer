Pennantia

Pennantia, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a genus of flowering plants native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in spikes, and attract a variety of pollinators. Pennantia species are often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

These plants are also valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
