Pleurothyrium poeppigii

Pleurothyrium poeppigii, commonly known as the red-barked tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
