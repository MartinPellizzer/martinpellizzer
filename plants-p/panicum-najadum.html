Panicum najadum

Panicum najadum, commonly known as water panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland habitats.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland environments.

Panicum najadum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging wet conditions.
