Piper dipterocarpinum

Piper dipterocarpinum, commonly known as the Dipterocarp pepper, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruits are typically berries that contain several seeds.

It is often found in humid, tropical forests and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
