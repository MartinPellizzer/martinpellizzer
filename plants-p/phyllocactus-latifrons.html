Phyllocactus latifrons

Phyllocactus latifrons, commonly known as the broad-fronded phyllocactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, segmented stems that store water, typical of desert plants. The plant produces large, paddle-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange and bloom in clusters during the warmer months.

Despite its cactus-like appearance, it is not a true cactus but is often mistaken for one due to its similar adaptations to arid environments.
