Plectranthus aliciae

Plectranthus aliciae, commonly known as the Alice's plectranthus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features aromatic, hairy leaves that are typically opposite and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually purple or violet in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus aliciae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant scent.
