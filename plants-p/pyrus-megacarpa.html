Pyrus megacarpa

Pyrus megacarpa, commonly known as the giant crabapple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree produces fruits that are typically yellow or orange, with a rough, bumpy surface. These fruits are edible but are often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
