Palatina wisilocensis

Palatina wisilocensis, commonly known as the Wisilocan orchid, is a rare and unique species native to the high-altitude regions of the Wisilocan Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue petals and a compact, bushy growth habit. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments with high levels of indirect sunlight. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to anchor in rocky soil, while its leaves are thick and waxy to retain moisture.

Due to its limited habitat and slow growth rate, Palatina wisilocensis is considered endangered and requires careful conservation efforts.
