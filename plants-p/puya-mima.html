Puya mima

Puya mima, commonly known as the Mima's puya, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences that can reach several feet in height. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, Puya mima is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
