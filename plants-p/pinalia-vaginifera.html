Pinalia vaginifera

Pinalia vaginifera, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Pinalia vaginifera is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of tropical and subtropical environments.
