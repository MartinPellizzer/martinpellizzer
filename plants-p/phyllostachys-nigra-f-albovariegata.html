Phyllostachys nigra f. albovariegata

Phyllostachys nigra f. albovariegata, commonly known as the white-variegated bamboo, is a cultivar of the black bamboo species.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring white and green stripes on its leaves. This plant is a member of the grass family and is native to East Asia. It grows as a clump-forming perennial, reaching heights of up to 10 feet.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and garden design.
