Piper coactoris

Piper coactoris, commonly known as the poison pepper or deadly nightshade, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is a climbing shrub that produces small, greenish-white flowers and red berries. The plant contains various toxic alkaloids, including piperine and coacine, which can be harmful to humans and animals. It is native to tropical regions of South America and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
