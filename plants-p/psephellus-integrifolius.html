Psephellus integrifolius

Psephellus integrifolius, commonly known as the smoothleaf psephellus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its simple, entire leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
