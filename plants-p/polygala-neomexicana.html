Polygala neomexicana

Polygala neomexicana, commonly known as New Mexico milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Polygala neomexicana is adapted to withstand drought conditions and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
