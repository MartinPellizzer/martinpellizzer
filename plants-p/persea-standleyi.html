Persea standleyi

Persea standleyi, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellow-orange berries that are edible when fully ripe. Its fruit is notable for its high nutritional value and is often used in local cuisines.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
