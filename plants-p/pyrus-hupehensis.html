Pyrus hupehensis

Pyrus hupehensis, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces white, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Pyrus hupehensis is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its various health benefits.
