Polygala perrottetiana

Polygala perrottetiana, commonly known as the perrottetian polygala, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its unique, often spiny leaves and showy, raceme-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, are adapted for pollination by insects.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
