Persica succedanea

Persica succedanea, commonly known as the wild peach or Chinese date, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China and other parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy pink or white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to peaches but are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a woody stem, hairy young shoots, and a preference for well-drained soils.

Persica succedanea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in horticulture.
