Piper pseudonobile

Piper pseudonobile, commonly known as the slender pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical settings.
