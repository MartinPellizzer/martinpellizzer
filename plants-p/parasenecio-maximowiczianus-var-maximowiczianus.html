Parasenecio maximowiczianus var. maximowiczianus

Parasenecio maximowiczianus var. maximowiczianus, commonly known as the Maximowicz's parasenecio, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant is typically perennial, with a rosette growth form and hairy, lanceolate leaves. It produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in late summer.

Due to its restricted habitat and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
