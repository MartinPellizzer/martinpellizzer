Phlomis inaequalisepala

Phlomis inaequalisepala, commonly known as the unequal-epal leaf phlomis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in full sun conditions. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, clustered in whorls along the stem, and bloom during the summer months.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
