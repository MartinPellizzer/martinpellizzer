Polypodium obtusifrons

Polypodium obtusifrons, commonly known as the broad-leafed wall fern, is a species of epiphytic and terrestrial fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its leathery, ovate to broadly ovate fronds. The fronds are typically divided into segments with a distinctive, broad, and rounded shape. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks.

The plant's rhizomes are thick and wiry, allowing it to anchor itself to various substrates.
