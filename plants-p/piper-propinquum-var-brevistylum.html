Piper propinquum var. brevistylum

Piper propinquum var. brevistylum, commonly known as the short-stemmed pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper propinquum species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into peppery fruits, similar to other members of the Piper genus. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject of study in tropical botany and ethnobotany.
