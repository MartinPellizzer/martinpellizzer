Phoradendron craspedophylloides

Phoradendron craspedophylloides, commonly known as the flat-leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in the Americas.

It has small, flat, ovate leaves that are usually arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are often clustered together. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food for wildlife and contributing to the ecological balance of its host trees.
