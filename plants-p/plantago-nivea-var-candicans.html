Plantago nivea var. candicans

Plantago nivea var. candicans, commonly known as the white plantain, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage.
