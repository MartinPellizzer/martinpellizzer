Polygonum tenuiflorum

Polygonum tenuiflorum, commonly known as Chinese spinach or kangkong, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and it is often cultivated for its edible leaves and stems.

It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is commonly grown in temperate and subtropical regions.
