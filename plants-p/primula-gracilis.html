Primula gracilis

Primula gracilis, commonly known as the slender primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The leaves are typically oval and have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are slender and often bear a woolly covering. This species thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in meadows and woodlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
