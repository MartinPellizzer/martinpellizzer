Porophyllum ruderale

Porophyllum ruderale, commonly known as the plains prickly pear, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are adapted for dispersal by animals.

Porophyllum ruderale is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
