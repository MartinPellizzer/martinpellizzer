Platycrater arguta

Platycrater arguta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually narrow and elliptical in shape. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and natural landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate flowers and attractive foliage, though it is not commonly cultivated for commercial purposes.
