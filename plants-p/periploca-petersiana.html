Periploca petersiana

Periploca petersiana, commonly known as Peters' periploca, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to certain regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, elliptic-shaped leaves. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries.

This species is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses.
