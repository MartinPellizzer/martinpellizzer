Prunus serrulata f. decora

Prunus serrulata f. decora, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or some varieties may be referred to as the weeping cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus serrulata species.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly during the spring when it produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers. The plant is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree with a distinctive weeping or cascading growth habit. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with serrated edges, and turn yellow in the autumn.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
