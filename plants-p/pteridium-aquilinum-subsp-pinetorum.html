Pteridium aquilinum subsp. pinetorum

Pteridium aquilinum subsp.

pinetorum, commonly known as the western bracken, is a fern species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its large, triangular fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1.5 meters.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands, clearings, and disturbed areas. Its spores are released from the fertile fronds, which develop in the late spring and summer.

This subspecies is known for its adaptability and can tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
