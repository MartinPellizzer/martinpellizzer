Phacelia leucophylla var. suksdorfii

Phacelia leucophylla var. suksdorfii, commonly known as the Sushkoff's phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The flowers are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
