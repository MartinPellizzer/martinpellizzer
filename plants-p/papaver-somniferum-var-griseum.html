Papaver somniferum var. griseum

Papaver somniferum var. griseum, commonly known as the gray poppy, is a variant of the opium poppy characterized by its distinctive grayish-blue petals.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include a smooth, branched stem and elliptical, dark green leaves.

While it is cultivated for ornamental purposes, it is also a source of alkaloids, making it a plant of both botanical and legal significance.
