Pouteria viscosa

Pouteria viscosa, commonly known as the sticky pouteria, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rough, sticky bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, fleshy fruits with a sweet, aromatic pulp. The fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in tropical ecosystems and agroforestry systems.
