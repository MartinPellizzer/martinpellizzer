Pelargonium × litigiosum

Pelargonium × litigiosum, commonly known as the trailing geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and purple. The leaves are typically rounded or heart-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. Native to South Africa, this plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
