Pelargonium × rotundilobum

Pelargonium × rotundilobum, commonly known as the African geranium, is a hybrid plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rounded, lobed leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often in clusters at the ends of stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Pelargonium × rotundilobum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.
