Pennisetum ciliatum

Pennisetum ciliatum, commonly known as blue oat grass, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, blue-green foliage and delicate, oat-like flower spikes. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.

Pennisetum ciliatum is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
