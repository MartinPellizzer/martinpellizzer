Pyrus baldaccii

Pyrus baldaccii, commonly known as the baldaccii pear, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pyrus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its deciduous leaves, which are oval-shaped with a slightly serrated margin. Its small, white flowers appear in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are similar in appearance to common pears.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique genetic makeup and potential horticultural value.
