Pogonia brasiliensis

Pogonia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian bog orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as bogs and swampy areas. The plant has a slender, creeping rhizome with a single erect stem bearing a single, showy orchid flower. Its flowers are usually pink or purple with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

The species is noted for its specialized pollination mechanism involving specific insects.
