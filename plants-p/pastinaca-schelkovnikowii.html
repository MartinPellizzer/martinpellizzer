Pastinaca schelkovnikowii

Pastinaca schelkovnikowii, commonly known as the Siberian burdock, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is adapted to harsh environments, showcasing resilience to cold temperatures and poor soil conditions.
