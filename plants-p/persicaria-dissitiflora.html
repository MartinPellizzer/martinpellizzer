Persicaria dissitiflora

Persicaria dissitiflora, commonly known as the hairy knotweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Persicaria dissitiflora is a perennial species that can spread through rhizomes, making it both a valuable wetland plant and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
