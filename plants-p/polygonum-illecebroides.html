Polygonum illecebroides

Polygonum illecebroides, commonly known as the redroot pigweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in open fields, roadsides, and along stream banks. It has a short, reddish root system and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural settings.
