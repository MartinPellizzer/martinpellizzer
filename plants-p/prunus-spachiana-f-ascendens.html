Prunus spachiana f. ascendens

Prunus spachiana f. ascendens, commonly known as the ascending cherry, is a rare and distinctive cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compact form, making it suitable for small spaces or as a decorative shrub. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or black berries in late summer. Its foliage is typically dark green and glossy, providing ornamental value throughout the growing season.

This form is often cultivated for its unique appearance and adaptability to various garden conditions.
