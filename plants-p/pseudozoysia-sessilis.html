Pseudozoysia sessilis

Pseudozoysia sessilis, commonly known as the narrowleaf zozysia, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically forms dense mats or clumps, with leaf blades that are often hairless and have a smooth texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

Pseudozoysia sessilis is adapted to a variety of soil types but thrives in well-drained, moist environments.
