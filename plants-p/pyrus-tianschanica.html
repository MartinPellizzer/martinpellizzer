Pyrus tianschanica

Pyrus tianschanica, commonly known as the Tianshan pear, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, edible pears that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Pyrus tianschanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional horticulture.
