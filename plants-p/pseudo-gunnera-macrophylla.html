Pseudo-gunnera macrophylla

Pseudo-gunnera macrophylla, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a striking plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, broad, shield-shaped leaves that can grow up to two meters in diameter. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in swampy or marshy environments. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs, which help to retain moisture and protect against herbivores.

Pseudo-gunnera macrophylla plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
