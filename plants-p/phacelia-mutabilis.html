Phacelia mutabilis

Phacelia mutabilis, commonly known as the blue phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats like grasslands and rocky slopes. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

Phacelia mutabilis is also valued for its role in ecological restoration due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
