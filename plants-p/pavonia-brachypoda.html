Pavonia brachypoda

Pavonia brachypoda, commonly known as the narrowleaf false musk thorn, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open woodlands and prairies. The plant features upright stems with opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy, pink to red flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Pavonia brachypoda is characterized by its thorn-like stipules and is often found in disturbed soils.
