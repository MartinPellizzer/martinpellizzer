Panicum arcuatum

Panicum arcuatum, commonly known as curved panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is a warm-season grass that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, fluffy seed heads that are often seen in late summer. It is characterized by its curved or arched seed spike, which gives it its scientific name.

Panicum arcuatum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
