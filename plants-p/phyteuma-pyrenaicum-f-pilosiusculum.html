Phyteuma pyrenaicum f. pilosiusculum

Phyteuma pyrenaicum f. pilosiusculum, commonly known as the hairy-leaved phyteuma, is a compact perennial herb native to the Pyrenees region.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered with fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with prominent, spurred petals, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This ornamental plant is valued for its unique foliage and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
