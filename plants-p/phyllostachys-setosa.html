Phyllostachys setosa

Phyllostachys setosa, commonly known as the silky bamboo, is a species of grass in the bamboo family, Poaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its slender, arching culms covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that appear in panicles during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Phyllostachys setosa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its graceful appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
