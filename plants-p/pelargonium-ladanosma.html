Pelargonium ladanosma

Pelargonium ladanosma, commonly known as the African geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically lobed and have a rough texture, while the stems are often hairy. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
