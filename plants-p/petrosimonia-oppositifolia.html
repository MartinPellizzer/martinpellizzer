Petrosimonia oppositifolia

Petrosimonia oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved petrosimonia, is a low-growing shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It features small, opposite leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the warmer months. Its succulent stems and leaves allow it to store water, making it well-adapted to dry conditions.

Petrosimonia oppositifolia is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in areas with limited rainfall.
