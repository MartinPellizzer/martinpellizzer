Peperomia polystachya

Peperomia polystachya, commonly known as the Christmas Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture, often displaying a reddish or purple hue on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences of pale green to white flowers, which are not particularly showy but are an important part of its botanical identity. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique leaf patterns and low maintenance requirements contribute to its appeal among plant enthusiasts.
