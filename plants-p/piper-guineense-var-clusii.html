Piper guineense var. clusii

Piper guineense var. clusii, commonly known as African pepper or guinea pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by three-lobed, berry-like fruits that turn red when mature. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

The varietal form clusii is distinguished by its specific morphological traits and habitat preferences.
