Piper orizabanum

Piper orizabanum, commonly known as the Indian black pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, peppery fruits that resemble black pepper. The plant produces berries that turn from green to dark purple as they mature. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

The plant is often cultivated for its aromatic fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
