Phyllanthus callidiscus

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has hairy leaves and produces a milky sap that is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
