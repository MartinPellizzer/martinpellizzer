Ptosimopappus intybaceus

Ptosimopappus intybaceus, commonly known as the silky bluebush, is a small, shrub-like plant native to the arid regions of central Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage that provides a striking visual contrast against the surrounding landscape. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers during the cooler months, typically from late winter to early spring. It is well adapted to drought and poor soil conditions, making it a hardy species suitable for dryland environments.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a notable component of its native ecosystems.
