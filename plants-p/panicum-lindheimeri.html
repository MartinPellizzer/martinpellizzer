Panicum lindheimeri

Panicum lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bend over. The plant produces small, fluffy panicles of seeds, which are adapted for wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to form dense clumps and its role in stabilizing soil in native plant communities.
