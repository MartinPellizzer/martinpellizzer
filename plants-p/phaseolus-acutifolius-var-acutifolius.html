Phaseolus acutifolius var. acutifolius

Phaseolus acutifolius var. acutifolius, commonly known as the wild bean or Mexican bean, is a leguminous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically narrow and acute, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is well adapted to dry conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
