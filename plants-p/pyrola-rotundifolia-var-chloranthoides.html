Pyrola rotundifolia var. chloranthoides

Pyrola rotundifolia var. chloranthoides, commonly known as the chloranthoid wintergreen, is a low-growing evergreen perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features rosettes of glossy, dark green, round leaves that remain throughout the year. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

Its foliage and flowers contribute to its role as an ornamental and ecological plant in forest ecosystems.
