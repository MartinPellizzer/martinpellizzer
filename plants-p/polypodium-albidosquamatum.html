Polypodium albidosquamatum

Polypodium albidosquamatum, commonly known as the white-scaled fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinnate fronds that are covered with small, white, scale-like structures. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
