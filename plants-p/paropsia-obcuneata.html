Paropsia obcuneata

Paropsia obcuneata, commonly known as the narrowleaf lily.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States, typically found in moist, wooded areas. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a flowering stem that bears clusters of bell-shaped blooms.

Paropsia obcuneata is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.
