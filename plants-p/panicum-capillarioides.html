Panicum capillarioides

Panicum capillarioides, commonly known as silky panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that cover its leaves and inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

Silky panicgrass is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wetland biodiversity.
