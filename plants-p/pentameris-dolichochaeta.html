Pentameris dolichochaeta

Pentameris dolichochaeta, commonly known as the long-hair grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets. It is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of certain ecological systems.
