Phlebiophragmus macrorhizus

Phlebiophragmus macrorhizus, commonly known as the giant root lichen, is a foliose lichen that exhibits a complex thallus structure.

It is characterized by its large, leathery lobes and a distinctive, wrinkled surface. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae, which provides the photosynthetic capability necessary for nutrient production. It typically grows on moist, shaded bark or soil, thriving in temperate climates.

Its root-like structures, or rhizines, anchor it to surfaces and aid in water absorption.
