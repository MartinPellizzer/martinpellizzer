Pleopeltis kaulfussiana

Pleopeltis kaulfussiana, commonly known as the Kaulfuss's rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocky surfaces, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are leathery and have a distinctive, intricate pattern of veins.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical greenhouses.
