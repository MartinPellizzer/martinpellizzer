Phaius hookerianus

Phaius hookerianus, commonly known as the Hooker's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and green. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which helps it store water and nutrients, adapting it to its tropical environment. Its roots are often found in the shaded understory of forests, where it thrives in humid conditions.

The orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
