Pinckneya viridiflora

Pinckneya viridiflora, commonly known as the greenflower pinckneya, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 15 inches. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in umbels, giving it a unique appearance.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
