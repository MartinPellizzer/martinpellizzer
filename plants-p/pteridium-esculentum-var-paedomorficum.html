Pteridium esculentum var. paedomorficum

Pteridium esculentum var. paedomorficum, commonly known as the edible fern, is a variant of the common bracken fern.

It is characterized by its large, triangular fronds that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate regions. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

The edible nature of its young fiddleheads makes it a valuable forager's resource in certain regions.
