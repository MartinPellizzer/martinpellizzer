Phlox albomarginata subsp. vera

Phlox albomarginata subsp.

vera, commonly known as the white-margin phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink to lavender in color, with distinctive white margins.

The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
