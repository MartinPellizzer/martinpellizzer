Pityrogramma caribaea var. imrayana

Pityrogramma caribaea var. imrayana, commonly known as the Caribbean water fern, is a variant of the water fern genus Pityrogramma.

It is a small, aquatic fern that typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are often submerged or floating. It reproduces through spores, which are released from the underside of its fronds.

This species is native to the Caribbean region and is adapted to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
