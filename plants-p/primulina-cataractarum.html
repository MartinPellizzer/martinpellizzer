Primulina cataractarum

Primulina cataractarum, commonly known as the waterfall plant, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing near waterfalls or streams. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical climates.
