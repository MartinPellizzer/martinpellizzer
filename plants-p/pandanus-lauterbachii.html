Pandanus lauterbachii

Pandanus lauterbachii, commonly known as the Lauterbachi's pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that grow in a fan-like arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, followed by fruiting structures known as syncarps. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove swamps or near the shoreline.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in various cultures for its leaves and fruits.
