Pectocarya penicillata var. heterocarpa

Pectocarya penicillata var. heterocarpa, commonly known as the variable pectocarya, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky bracts and small, showy flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine, white powder, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
