Potentilla glandulosa subsp. typica

Potentilla glandulosa subsp.

typica, commonly known as the glandular potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States. It typically features small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has narrow, hairy leaves that are often glandular-dotted, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for native gardens and restoration projects.
