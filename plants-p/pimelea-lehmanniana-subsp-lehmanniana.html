Pimelea lehmanniana subsp. lehmanniana

Pimelea lehmanniana subsp.

lehmanniana, commonly known as the Lehmann's bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically red or orange in color.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is well adapted to dry, sandy soils. It is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
