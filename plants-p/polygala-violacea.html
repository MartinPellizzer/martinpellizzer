Polygala violacea

Polygala violacea, commonly known as the violet milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, violet or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, bean-like pods that contain its seeds.

Polygala violacea is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
