Paepalanthus klotzschianus

Paepalanthus klotzschianus, commonly known as the Klotzsch's cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its cylindrical stem and reduced leaves. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It is adapted to high-altitude environments and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
