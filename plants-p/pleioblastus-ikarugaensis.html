Pleioblastus ikarugaensis

Pleioblastus ikarugaensis, commonly known as the Ikaruga bamboo, is a species native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and fine, lance-shaped leaves. This bamboo is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. It grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and is valued for its ornamental appearance.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in cool climates and its use in traditional Japanese horticulture.
