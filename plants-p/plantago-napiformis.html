Plantago napiformis

Plantago napiformis, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a distinctive swollen base resembling a turnip, which stores nutrients for regrowth. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, due to its tolerance for poor soil conditions.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with its leaves used traditionally for their healing and anti-inflammatory effects.
