Piper inaequale var. magnifolium

Piper inaequale var. magnifolium, commonly known as the giant sanderling pepper, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by round, peppercorn-like fruits. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental and medicinal properties.
