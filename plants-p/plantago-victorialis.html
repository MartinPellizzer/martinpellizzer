Plantago victorialis

Plantago victorialis, commonly known as Virginia plantain, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features a basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces erect flowering stems topped with a dense cluster of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in wet meadows.

Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine for their soothing properties.
