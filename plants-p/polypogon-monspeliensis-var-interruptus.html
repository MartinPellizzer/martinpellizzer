Polypogon monspeliensis var. interruptus

Polypogon monspeliensis var. interruptus, commonly known as the interrupted false brome, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
