Peperomia caducipilosa

Peperomia caducipilosa, commonly known as the shed-pinion peperomia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant features broad, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge on the underside. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often in shades of green or white.

Peperomia caducipilosa is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in moderate light conditions.
