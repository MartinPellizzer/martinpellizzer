Pappostipa chrysophylla var. crispula

Pappostipa chrysophylla var. crispula, commonly known as the golden leaf pappostipa, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow foliage, which is more pronounced in its juvenile stages. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in specialized gardens.
