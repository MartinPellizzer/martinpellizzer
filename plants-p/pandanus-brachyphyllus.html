Pandanus brachyphyllus

Pandanus brachyphyllus, commonly known as the dwarf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear fragrant, yellowish flowers. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and as thatching materials in some regions.

Pandanus brachyphyllus thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.
