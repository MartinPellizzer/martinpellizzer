Pinea esculenta

Pinea esculenta, commonly known as the edible pine, is a unique species within the Pinaceae family.

It is characterized by its conical shape and dense, needle-like leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces cones that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

Pinea esculenta thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.
