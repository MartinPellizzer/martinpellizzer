Pycreus coromandelianus

Pycreus coromandelianus, commonly known as the Coromandel pomegranate, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces bright red, fleshy fruits with a high concentration of seeds, similar to true pomegranates. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

Pycreus coromandelianus is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
