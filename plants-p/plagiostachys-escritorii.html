Plagiostachys escritorii

Plagiostachys escritorii, commonly known as the writer's fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds that give it a graceful appearance. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically in the understorey of tropical forests. Its spores are dispersed by wind, contributing to its limited distribution.

Due to habitat loss, Plagiostachys escritorii is considered endangered and is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
