Papaver inornatum

Papaver inornatum, commonly known as the unadorned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its simple, unbranched stem and delicate, white to pale pink petals. The plant produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous small, dry seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Papaver inornatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
