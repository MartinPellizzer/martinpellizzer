Phillyrea angustifolia subsp. longicuneata

Phillyrea angustifolia subsp.

longicuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Phillyrea, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
