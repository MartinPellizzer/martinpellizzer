Pycreus punctatus

Pycreus punctatus, commonly known as the spotted pycreus, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

This plant is native to parts of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with a distinctive pattern of white spots that give it its common name. The flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has mild medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
