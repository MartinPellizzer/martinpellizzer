Pyrus cunonioides

Pyrus cunonioides, commonly known as the New Zealand whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and small, oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces yellowish-white flowers in spring, which are followed by small, edible berries. It is typically found in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
