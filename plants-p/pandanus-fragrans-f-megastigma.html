Pandanus fragrans f. megastigma

Pandanus fragrans f. megastigma, commonly known as the giant pandanus or fragrant pandanus, is a variant of the pandanus genus native to tropical regions of the Indo-Pacific.

This plant is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length, often arranged in a fan-like pattern. The inflorescence consists of a branched cluster of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers, which are typically borne on long, spiky stems. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, where it can tolerate saline and waterlogged soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in various island communities.
