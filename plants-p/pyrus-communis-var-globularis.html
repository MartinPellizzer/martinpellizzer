Pyrus communis var. globularis

Pyrus communis var. globularis, commonly known as the globe pear, is a cultivar of the European pear characterized by its rounded, compact growth habit.

It features small, glossy leaves and produces clusters of white flowers in early spring. The fruit is typically small, round, and has a thick, smooth skin with a sweet, aromatic flavor. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for temperate climates.
