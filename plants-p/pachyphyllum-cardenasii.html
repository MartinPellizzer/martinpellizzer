Pachyphyllum cardenasii

Pachyphyllum cardenasii, commonly known as the Cardenas pachyphytum, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the blooming season. Its leaves are typically spiny, serving as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

Pachyphyllum cardenasii is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and drought tolerance.
