Phyteuma corniculatum subsp. charmelioides

Phyteuma corniculatum subsp.

charmelioides, commonly known as the charmelioides bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, with a distinctive, elongated shape.

The plant grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils, often in the Mediterranean region. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is valued in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
