Paspalum crispulum

Paspalum crispulum, commonly known as the curlygrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its finely textured, wiry leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats make it an effective pioneer species in disturbed areas.

Paspalum crispulum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in landscaping.
