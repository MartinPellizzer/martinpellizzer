Piper triumphale

Piper triumphale, commonly known as the golden gooseberry, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruits are edible, though they are not commonly cultivated for commercial use.

The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
