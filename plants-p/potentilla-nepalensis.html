Potentilla nepalensis

Potentilla nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Potentilla nepalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
