Panicum japonicum

Panicum japonicum, commonly known as Japanese foxtail, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and spikelets that resemble the tail of a fox. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas or along waterways. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Panicum japonicum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland environments.
