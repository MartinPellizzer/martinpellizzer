Persicaria hydropiper subsp. flaccida

Persicaria hydropiper subsp.

flaccida, commonly known as water pepper, is a flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive whitish underside.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on terminal spikes. It thrives in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its name reflects its historical use as a diuretic and its ability to grow in waterlogged soils.
