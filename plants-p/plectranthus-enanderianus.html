Plectranthus enanderianus

Plectranthus enanderianus, commonly known as the Cape verbena, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers, which bloom in summer. It is drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice among gardeners.
