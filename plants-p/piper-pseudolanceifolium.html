Piper pseudolanceifolium

Piper pseudolanceifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its foliage is aromatic, with a mild peppery scent, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is a shrub-like plant that can grow up to 2 meters in height under optimal conditions.
