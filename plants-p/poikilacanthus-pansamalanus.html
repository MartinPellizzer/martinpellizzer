Poikilacanthus pansamalanus

Poikilacanthus pansamalanus, commonly known as the spiral cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spirally arranged stems that form a distinctive, twisting structure. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months.

The botanical features of Poikilacanthus pansamalanus make it an intriguing subject for study in desert ecosystems.
