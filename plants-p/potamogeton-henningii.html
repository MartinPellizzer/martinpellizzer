Potamogeton henningii

Potamogeton henningii, commonly known as Henning's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, flexible stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are narrow, ribbon-like, and arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, and it is often found in still or slow-moving waters.

Potamogeton henningii plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
