Pseudotephrocactus vestitus

Pseudotephrocactus vestitus, commonly known as the naked pseudotephrocactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in sparse, white spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a vibrant touch to its desert habitat. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique features, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
