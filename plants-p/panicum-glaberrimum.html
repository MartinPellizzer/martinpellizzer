Panicum glaberrimum

Panicum glaberrimum, commonly known as the smooth panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and panicle-shaped inflorescence that produces small, spikelet-bearing flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and is often found in wetland ecosystems. Its smooth, glabrous appearance gives it its common name, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
