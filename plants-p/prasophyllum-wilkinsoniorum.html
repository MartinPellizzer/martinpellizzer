Prasophyllum wilkinsoniorum

Prasophyllum wilkinsoniorum, commonly known as the Wilkerson's leek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the wetlands of southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in swampy or seasonally waterlogged areas, thriving in moist, nutrient-poor soils. Its flowering period occurs during the late spring to early summer, and it is often found in association with other wetland flora.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
