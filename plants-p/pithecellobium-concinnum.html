Pithecellobium concinnum

Pithecellobium concinnum, commonly known as the small-flowered acacia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with woody stems and a preference for well-drained soils in warm climates.
