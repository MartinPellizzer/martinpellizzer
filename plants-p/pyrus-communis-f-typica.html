Pyrus communis f. typica

Pyrus communis f. typica, commonly known as the common pear.

It is a cultivar of the European pear, characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is typically sweet and aromatic, with a firm texture and a variable size. It is often used in both fresh consumption and culinary applications.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
