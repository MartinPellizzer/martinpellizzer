Ponera esuriens

Ponera esuriens, commonly known as the hunger orchid, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in the early spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil, often growing in rocky crevices or shaded forest understories. Its unique adaptation to nutrient-poor soils involves a symbiotic relationship with specific mycorrhizal fungi.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Ponera esuriens is considered a vulnerable species in conservation efforts.
