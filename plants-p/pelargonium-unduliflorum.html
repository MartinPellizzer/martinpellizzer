Pelargonium unduliflorum

Pelargonium unduliflorum, commonly known as the unduliflorum geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range from pink to red. The leaves are typically rounded and have a slightly wavy or undulating edge, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
