Prunus cocomilia var. brutia

Prunus cocomilia var. brutia, commonly known as the dwarf cherry plum, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible, dark red berries. The plant has dense, dark green foliage that provides attractive evergreen coverage throughout the year. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
