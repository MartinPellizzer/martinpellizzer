Parkia oliveri

Parkia oliveri, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a visually striking ornamental plant. The tree has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Parkia oliveri is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility in agricultural settings.
