Potentilla glandulosa var. glutinosa

Potentilla glandulosa var. glutinosa, commonly known as sticky potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its glandular leaves and hairy stems, which contribute to its sticky texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient member of the Rosaceae family.
