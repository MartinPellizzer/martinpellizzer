Perezia wislizeni subsp. minor

Perezia wislizeni subsp.

minor, commonly known as the narrowleaf perezia, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along streambanks.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white to pale blue flowers. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and has a slow growth rate.

The subspecies is characterized by its smaller leaves and reduced floral structures compared to the typical form of P. wislizeni.
