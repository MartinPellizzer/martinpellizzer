It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Plectopoma naegeli and Plectopoma variabilis, both of which are found in tropical and subtropical marine environments. The plant, though not a true plant, is often studied for its ecological role in coastal ecosystems. It thrives in shallow, warm waters and is typically found on rocky substrates.

Its shell is characterized by a spiral pattern and a smooth, glossy surface, making it distinct from its parent species.
