Penstemon deustus subsp. heterander

Penstemon deustus subsp.

heterander, commonly known as the blue penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, tubular blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and dry, sunny environments, making it well adapted to arid conditions. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
