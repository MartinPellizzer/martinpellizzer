Prunus × donarium subvar. speciosa

Prunus × donarium subvar. speciosa, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly its showy pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and public spaces. Its leaves are ovate and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the blossoms.

The tree typically grows to a height of 8–12 meters and is often cultivated for its seasonal beauty.
