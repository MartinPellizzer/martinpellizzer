Paphiopedilum stonei subsp. stictopetalum

Paphiopedilum stonei subsp.

stictopetalum, commonly known as the Stictopetalum Paphiopedilum, is a rare and captivating orchid native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers with a unique pouch-like structure that attracts specific pollinators.

The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
