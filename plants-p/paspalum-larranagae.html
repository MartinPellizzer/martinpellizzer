Paspalum larranagae

Paspalum larranagae, commonly known as the Larranaga paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and riparian areas.

Paspalum larranagae is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
