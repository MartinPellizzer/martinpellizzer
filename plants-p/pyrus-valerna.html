Pyrus valerna

Pyrus valerna, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by edible, round berries that are typically red or yellow. Pyrus valerna is often found in woodland areas and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in hedgerows.
