Pennisetum gabonense

Pennisetum gabonense, commonly known as African feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery plumes that give it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as along riverbanks and in swampy areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles.

Pennisetum gabonense is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value and ability to thrive in humid environments.
