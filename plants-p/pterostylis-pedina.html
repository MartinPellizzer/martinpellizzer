Pterostylis pedina

Pterostylis pedina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved greenhood, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a distinctive greenish-brown flower spike. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rainforests. Its flowers are usually small and have a unique hood-like structure, which is a common feature among the orchid genus Pterostylis.

The species is noted for its ability to form dense colonies and is often found in association with other orchid species in its habitat.
