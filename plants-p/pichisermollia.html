Pichisermollia

Pichisermollia, commonly known as the moonflower orchid, is a rare and exotic plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that bloom at night, emitting a subtle scent to attract nocturnal pollinators. The plant has strap-like leaves that are dark green and velvety to the touch, providing a striking contrast to its pale blossoms. It thrives in cool, humid environments with indirect sunlight and requires careful attention to maintain its health.

Due to its rarity and unique beauty, it is highly sought after by botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
