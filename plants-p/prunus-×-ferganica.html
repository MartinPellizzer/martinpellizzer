Prunus × ferganica

Prunus × ferganica, commonly known as the Fergana cherry, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Prunus mandshurica and Prunus sibirica.

It is native to Central Asia, particularly the Fergana Valley in Uzbekistan. This plant is characterized by its small, sweet-tart berries that are often used in local cuisines. The shrub typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves and a compact, bushy growth habit.
