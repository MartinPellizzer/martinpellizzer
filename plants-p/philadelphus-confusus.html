Philadelphus confusus

Philadelphus confusus, commonly known as the confusion honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blooms and the attractive, persistent red berries it produces in the fall.
