Potentilla gracilis var. chrysantha

Potentilla gracilis var. chrysantha, commonly known as the golden potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in the summer. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a soft, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant blooms and compact form.
