Podanthum limonifolium var. pubescens

Podanthum limonifolium var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy lemonleaf.

This plant is characterized by its broad, lemon-scented leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-draining soil. The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for both ornamental and medicinal cultivation.
