Phyteuma spicatum var. pilosum

Phyteuma spicatum var. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to temperate climates.
