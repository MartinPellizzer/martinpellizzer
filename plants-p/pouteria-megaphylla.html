Pouteria megaphylla

Pouteria megaphylla, commonly known as the giant sheathfig, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are yellow when ripe. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various wildlife species.
