Polypodium glycyrrhiza

Polypodium glycyrrhiza, commonly known as the Indian fan-fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found growing on tree trunks or rocks. Its rhizomes contain compounds that have been traditionally used for their medicinal properties.

The Indian fan-fern is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential therapeutic applications.
