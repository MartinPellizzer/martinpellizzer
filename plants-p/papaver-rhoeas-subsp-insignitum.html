Papaver rhoeas subsp. insignitum

Papaver rhoeas subsp.

insignitum, commonly known as the Persian poppy, is a member of the Papaveraceae family. It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers with a distinctive black center.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
