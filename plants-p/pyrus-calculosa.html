Pyrus calculosa

Pyrus calculosa, commonly known as the rock pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, hence its common name. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often pubescent and have a distinctive serrated margin. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, edible pomes that ripen in late summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environments.
