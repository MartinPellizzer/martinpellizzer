Pedilanthus involucratus

Pedilanthus involucratus, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Amazon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has palmate leaves with a waxy surface, and its stems are often covered in a dense layer of hair. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found near water sources.

Due to its ornamental value and cultural significance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
