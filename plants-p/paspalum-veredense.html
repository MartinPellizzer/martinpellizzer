Paspalum veredense

Paspalum veredense, commonly known as the river paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland and riparian habitats, preferring moist to submerged soil conditions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Paspalum veredense is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
