Potentilla douglasii

Potentilla douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of yellow flowers in the summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves with five leaflets, giving it its common name. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and arctic environments.

Potentilla douglasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native plant landscaping.
