Phaca succumbens

Phaca succumbens, commonly known as the drooping phaca, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its slender, often drooping stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
