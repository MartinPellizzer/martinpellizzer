Polygala sipapoana

Polygala sipapoana, commonly known as the Madagascar milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, narrow leaves. It produces small, bead-like fruits that contain the seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
