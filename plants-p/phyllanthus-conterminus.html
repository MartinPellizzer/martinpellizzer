Phyllanthus conterminus

Phyllanthus conterminus, commonly known as the African date palm, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
