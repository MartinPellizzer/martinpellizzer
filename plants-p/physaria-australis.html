Physaria australis

Physaria australis, commonly known as the Australian bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with fleshy, inflated pods that resemble bladders, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Physaria australis thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils across various regions of Australia.
