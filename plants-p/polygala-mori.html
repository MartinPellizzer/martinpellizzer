Polygala mori

Polygala mori, commonly known as the mourning bush, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white, arranged in clustered inflorescences.

Polygala mori is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
