Pulsatilla × refulgens

Pulsatilla × refulgens, commonly known as the hybrid pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms that range from white to pink or purple. The plant produces a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers consist of multiple sepals that resemble petals and are typically arranged in a single, upright inflorescence.

Pulsatilla × refulgens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, long-lasting blossoms.
