Pediocactus bonatzii

Pediocactus bonatzii, commonly known as the Bonatzii prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. Its stems are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Pediocactus bonatzii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh climates.
