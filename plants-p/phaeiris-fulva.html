Phaeiris fulva

Phaeiris fulva, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy to the touch. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
