Psilostrophe cerifera var. biennis

Psilostrophe cerifera var. biennis, commonly known as the biennial candle plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, candle-like stems that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
