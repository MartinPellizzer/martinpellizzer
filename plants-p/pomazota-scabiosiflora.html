Pomazota scabiosiflora

Pomazota scabiosiflora, commonly known as the meadow gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
