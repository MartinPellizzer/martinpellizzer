Phreatia habbemae

Phreatia habbemae, commonly known as the Habbema's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and epiphytic growth habit. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest understories. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with intricate patterns, adapted for specific pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
