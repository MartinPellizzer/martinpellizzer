Poa glaucantha

Poa glaucantha, commonly known as the bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically forms dense mats or turf-like growth, making it suitable for ornamental and ecological landscaping. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.
