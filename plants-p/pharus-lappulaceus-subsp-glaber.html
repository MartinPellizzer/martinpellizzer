Pharus lappulaceus subsp. glaber

Pharus lappulaceus subsp.

glaber, commonly known as the smooth pharus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae. It is characterized by its glabrous (smooth) stems and leaves, which are typically covered in a fine, hairless layer.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the summer months. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America, where it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open fields.
