Phylica humilis

Phylica humilis, commonly known as the dwarf milk bush, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its distinctive, small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are covered in fine, white hairs. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for dry landscapes.

Phylica humilis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
