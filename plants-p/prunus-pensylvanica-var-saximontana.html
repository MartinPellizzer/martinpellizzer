Prunus pensylvanica var. saximontana

Prunus pensylvanica var. saximontana, commonly known as the sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often found in open woodlands and along stream banks. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, red berries in the summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it can reach heights of up to 6 feet.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
