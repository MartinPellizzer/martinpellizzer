Phegopteris propinqua

Phegopteris propinqua, commonly known as the brittle bladder fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds that are often covered in small, bladder-like structures called sorangia, which aid in spore dispersal. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually bipinnate, with a finely divided structure that gives it a lacy appearance.

The botanical features of Phegopteris propinqua make it an important species for studying fern evolution and ecological adaptation.
