Piper santa-martanum

Piper santa-martanum, commonly known as Santa Marta pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Colombia, specifically the Santa Marta mountains. The plant is characterized by its vine-like growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small berries that turn red when mature, and they are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in the study of tropical flora and its ecological roles.
