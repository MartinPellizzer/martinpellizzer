Polygala stenophylla var. oxycarpa

Polygala stenophylla var. oxycarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish, and the leaves are narrow and linear, giving it its common name.

It is often found in shrublands and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
