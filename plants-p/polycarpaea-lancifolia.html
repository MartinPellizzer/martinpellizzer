Polycarpaea lancifolia

Polycarpaea lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved polycarpaea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in grassland and open woodland habitats. The plant features slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in the spring and summer seasons.

Polycarpaea lancifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
