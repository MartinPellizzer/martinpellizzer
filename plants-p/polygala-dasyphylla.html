Polygala dasyphylla

Polygala dasyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and the flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering habit.
