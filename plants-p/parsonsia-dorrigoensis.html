Parsonsia dorrigoensis

Parsonsia dorrigoensis, commonly known as the Dorrigo flame vine, is a striking flowering plant native to the subtropical rainforests of New South Wales, Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red flowers, which are clustered in racemes and attract a variety of pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a humid, warm climate to flourish.

Parsonsia dorrigoensis is also notable for its woody stems and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
