Pleurothallis obliquipetala

Pleurothallis obliquipetala, commonly known as the oblique-leafed pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often curled or twisted, contributing to its unique appearance. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a variety of colors including white, yellow, and green.

Pleurothallis obliquipetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
