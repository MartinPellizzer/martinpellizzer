Picridium dichotomum

Picridium dichotomum, commonly known as the two-branched broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
