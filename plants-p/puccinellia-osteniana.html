Puccinellia osteniana

Puccinellia osteniana, commonly known as the narrowleaf saltbush, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable plant for soil stabilization in harsh environments.

Puccinellia osteniana plays an important ecological role in supporting grazing animals and maintaining desert ecosystems.
