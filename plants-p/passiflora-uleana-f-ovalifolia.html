Passiflora uleana f. ovalifolia

Passiflora uleana f. ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf passionflower, is a variant of the passionvine genus native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. The leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a smooth or slightly serrated edge. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
