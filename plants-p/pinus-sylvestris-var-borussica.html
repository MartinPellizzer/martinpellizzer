Pinus sylvestris var. borussica

Pinus sylvestris var. borussica, commonly known as the Scots pine, is a variant of the common pine native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cones. The tree typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters, with a conical shape and a thick, fissured bark. Its needles are typically 2–4 cm long, and it produces both male and female cones.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in forested areas, contributing to biodiversity and ecological stability.
