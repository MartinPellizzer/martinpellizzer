Polystachya samilae

Polystachya samilae, commonly known as the Samilae ginger orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its unique floral displays.
