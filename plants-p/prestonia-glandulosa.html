Prestonia glandulosa

Prestonia glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular prestonia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica and Cuba.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family, which includes milkweeds and other toxic plants. The plant is characterized by its woody stems, opposite leaves, and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its flowers are typically clustered in umbels and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant contains latex-like sap that can be irritating to the skin, a common trait among members of its family.
