Phlomis cashmeriana

Phlomis cashmeriana, commonly known as Kashmir phlomis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in alpine environments. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in whorls and range in color from pink to purple.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
