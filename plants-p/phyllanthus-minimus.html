Phyllanthus minimus

Phyllanthus minimus, commonly known as the dwarf candle bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features small, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is well adapted to humid, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soil.
