Pilbara trudgenii

Pilbara trudgenii, commonly known as the Pilbara burr wattle, is a shrub native to the Pilbara region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and woody pods, which are typical of the Acacia genus. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of small, needle-like phyllodes that reduce water loss.

Pilbara trudgenii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
