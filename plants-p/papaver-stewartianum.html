Papaver stewartianum

Papaver stewartianum, commonly known as Stewart's poppy, is a species of flowering plant in the family Papaveraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, with a prominent central stigma. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
