Pavonia subhastata f. suborbicularis

Pavonia subhastata f. suborbicularis, commonly known as the broad-leafed wood rose, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive, orbicular-shaped leaves.

The leaves are typically broad and rounded, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink or red, which bloom in the summer months. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its compact growth habit and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
