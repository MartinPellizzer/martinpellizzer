Pseuderanthemum leptostachys

Pseuderanthemum leptostachys, commonly known as the narrow-stalked pseuderanthemum, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and typically have two lips, with the lower lip being more prominent.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.
