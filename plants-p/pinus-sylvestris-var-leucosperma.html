Pinus sylvestris var. leucosperma

Pinus sylvestris var. leucosperma, commonly known as the white-scaled pine, is a variety of the Scots pine.

It is native to the northern regions of Europe and parts of Asia. This evergreen conifer is characterized by its white or greyish-brown bark and needle-like leaves. The cones are typically reddish-brown and release seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as heaths and grasslands.
