Panicum latiglume var. villosum

Panicum latiglume var. villosum, commonly known as hairy panicgrass, is a grass species characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy leaf blades.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows, and is native to parts of North America. The plant features elongated, cylindrical spikelets that are often loosely arranged along the stem. Its foliage is usually dark green and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
