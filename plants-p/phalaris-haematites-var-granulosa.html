Phalaris haematites var. granulosa

Phalaris haematites var. granulosa, commonly known as red beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive red-purple coloration in its inflorescence. The plant produces spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

Phalaris haematites var. granulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
