Prunus pissardii var. moseri

Prunus pissardii var. moseri, commonly known as the Moser's cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible but not typically consumed by humans. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn yellow in autumn.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various soil types.
