Pasania elaeagnifolia

Pasania elaeagnifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf pasania, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
