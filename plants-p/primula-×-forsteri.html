Primula × forsteri

Primula × forsteri, commonly known as the Forster's primrose, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 15–30 cm, featuring rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
