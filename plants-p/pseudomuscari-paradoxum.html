Pseudomuscari paradoxum

Pseudomuscari paradoxum, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers in late spring. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy blooms. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
