Physocarpus ferrugineus

Physocarpus ferrugineus, commonly known as the red smoke bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red bract clusters that appear in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its small, yellowish-green flowers are inconspicuous compared to the vibrant bracts. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

Physocarpus ferrugineus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
