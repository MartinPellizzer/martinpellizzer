Phyllanthus mendesii

Phyllanthus mendesii, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
