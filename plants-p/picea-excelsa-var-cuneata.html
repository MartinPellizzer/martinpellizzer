Picea excelsa var. cuneata

Picea excelsa var. cuneata, commonly known as the Mongolian fir, is a variant of the Siberian fir native to northern Mongolia and southern Siberia.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its tall, slender form and dense, dark green foliage. The needles are typically short, flat, and have a distinctive blue-green coloration. It produces small, oval cones that hang downward, and its bark is thick, fissured, and often reddish-brown.

This species is adapted to cold climates and is valued for its timber and ecological role in boreal forests.
