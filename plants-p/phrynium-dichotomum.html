Phrynium dichotomum

Phrynium dichotomum, commonly known as the Split-leaf Arum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and often split into two distinct halves. The plant produces an inflorescence that resembles a spathe, typical of the Araceae family, with a central spadix surrounded by a showy bract. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
