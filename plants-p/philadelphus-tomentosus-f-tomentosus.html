Philadelphus tomentosus f. tomentosus

Philadelphus tomentosus f. tomentosus, commonly known as the fuzzy mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that provides a soft, textured appearance. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape and have a velvety, pubescent surface.

The fuzzy mock orange is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
