Phyllanthus utricularis

Phyllanthus utricularis, commonly known as the bladder nut or bladderleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and distinctive yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that resemble bladders, which are adapted for wind dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Phyllanthus utricularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
