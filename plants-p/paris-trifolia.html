Paris trifolia

Paris trifolia, commonly known as white wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets, and small white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces capsule fruits containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Paris trifolia thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
