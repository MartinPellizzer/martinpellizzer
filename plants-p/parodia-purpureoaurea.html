Parodia purpureoaurea

Parodia purpureoaurea, commonly known as the Purple Aureate Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white to yellowish spines. The plant produces striking purple-pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
