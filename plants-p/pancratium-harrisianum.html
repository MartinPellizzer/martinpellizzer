Pancratium harrisianum

Pancratium harrisianum, commonly known as the Harrisian yellow daffodil, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, typically reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces a single, showy flower per stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
