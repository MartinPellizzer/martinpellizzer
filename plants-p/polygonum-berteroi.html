Polygonum berteroi

Polygonum berteroi, commonly known as the Berter's knotweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its stems are often reddish or purple, and it produces tiny, greenish-white flowers in late summer.

Polygonum berteroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and seasonal interest.
