Plocama olivieri

Plocama olivieri, commonly known as the oliveleaf or olive tree, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and have a distinctive olive-green color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by black, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

Plocama olivieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological habitats.
