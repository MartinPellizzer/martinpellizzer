Phragmites communis var. marsillianus

Phragmites communis var. marsillianus, commonly known as the narrowleaf common reed, is a variant of the common reed that is native to North America.

It is a tall, perennial grass that typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant has long, slender leaves and produces feathery plumes of flowers during the summer months. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist conditions make it a significant component of wetland ecosystems.
