Potentilla baltica

Potentilla baltica, commonly known as Baltic cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, sandy or rocky soils along the shores of lakes and coastal areas. The plant features upright stems with alternate, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy yellow flowers, which appear in late spring to early summer, are composed of five petals and numerous stamens.

Potentilla baltica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging coastal environments.
