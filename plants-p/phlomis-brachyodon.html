Phlomis brachyodon

Phlomis brachyodon, commonly known as narrowleaf mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features upright stems covered in soft, woolly hairs, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped. It produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in clusters during the summer months.

Phlomis brachyodon is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
