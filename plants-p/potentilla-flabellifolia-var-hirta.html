Potentilla flabellifolia var. hirta

Potentilla flabellifolia var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy leaf potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, rounded clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically hairy, giving the plant its common name, and are arranged in a distinctive, fan-like pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
