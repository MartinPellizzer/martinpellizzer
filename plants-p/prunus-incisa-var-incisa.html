Prunus incisa var. incisa

Prunus incisa var. incisa, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early spring. The leaves are elliptical, with a serrated margin, and turn a vibrant yellow in autumn. The fruit is a small, yellow or orange drupe that ripens in late summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
