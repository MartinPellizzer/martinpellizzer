Planchonella glauca

Planchonella glauca, commonly known as the Queensland hibiscus, is a small evergreen tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in coastal and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun.

Planchonella glauca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine by Indigenous communities.
