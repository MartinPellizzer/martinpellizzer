Populus cinerea

Populus cinerea, commonly known as the gray poplar, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its grayish bark that peels in thin, papery layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, typically appearing oval to heart-shaped. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by samaras that disperse its seeds.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in woodland areas.
