Potamogeton porsildiorum

Potamogeton porsildiorum, commonly known as the Porsild's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown, and it plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its environment.
