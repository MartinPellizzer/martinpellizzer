Paspalum bicrurulum

Paspalum bicrurulum, commonly known as the slender paspalum, is a grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, making it a common species in riparian zones. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, while the stems are slender and erect.

Paspalum bicrurulum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.
