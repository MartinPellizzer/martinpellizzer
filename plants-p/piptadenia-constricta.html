Piptadenia constricta

Piptadenia constricta, commonly known as the Brazilian cedar or pink pod tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its straight, often buttressed trunk and compound leaves with small, oval leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, elongated, pink or red pods. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry forests and savannas.

Its seeds are dispersed by animals, contributing to its ecological significance in native ecosystems.
