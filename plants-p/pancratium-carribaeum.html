Pancratium carribaeum

Pancratium carribaeum, commonly known as the Caribbean torch flower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, torch-like inflorescence. The plant features strap-shaped, grass-like leaves and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry woodlands.

Pancratium carribaeum is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
