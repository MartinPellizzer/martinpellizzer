Panicum muhlenbergianum

Panicum muhlenbergianum, commonly known as prairie dropseed, is a native North American grass species characterized by its fine, fountain-like growth habit.

It produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that range in color from pale green to golden brown, depending on the season. The plant is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie ecosystems and open woodlands. Its leaves are narrow, bluish-gray in appearance, and it is drought-tolerant once established.

Panicum muhlenbergianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
