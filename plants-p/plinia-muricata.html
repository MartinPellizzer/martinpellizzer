Plinia muricata

Plinia muricata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright red, juicy berries that are rich in antioxidants and vitamins. Its fruit is widely consumed fresh or used in various culinary applications.

Plinia muricata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
