Pistolochia cabulica

Pistolochia cabulica, commonly known as the Persian trumpet vine, is a climbing shrub native to regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble small trumpets. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and is often found in rocky or arid environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
