Potentilla fragarioides var. typica

Potentilla fragarioides var. typica, commonly known as silverleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
