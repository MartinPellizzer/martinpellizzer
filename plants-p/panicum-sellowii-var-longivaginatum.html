Panicum sellowii var. longivaginatum

Panicum sellowii var. longivaginatum, commonly known as the long sheath panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open fields and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
