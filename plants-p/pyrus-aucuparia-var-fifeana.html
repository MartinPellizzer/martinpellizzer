Pyrus aucuparia var. fifeana

Pyrus aucuparia var. fifeana, commonly known as the Fifeana pear, is a cultivar of the European pear tree.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ornamental value, with attractive foliage and showy white flowers in spring. The fruit is typically small, sweet, and edible, though it is not widely cultivated for commercial use. This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a range of climates.

It is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and mild fruit flavor.
