Potamogeton × noltei f. perlucens

Potamogeton × noltei f. perlucens, commonly known as the translucent pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that exhibits unique botanical features.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often translucent or semi-transparent, allowing light to pass through. The plant typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. Its flowers are small and clustered, with both male and female reproductive structures present on the same plant.

This species is notable for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for aquatic organisms.
