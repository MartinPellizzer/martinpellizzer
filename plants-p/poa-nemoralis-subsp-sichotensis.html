Poa nemoralis subsp. sichotensis

Poa nemoralis subsp.

sichotensis, commonly known as the Sichotian meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant has slender, erect culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle, bearing small, two-ranked spikelets.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological adaptability and role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
