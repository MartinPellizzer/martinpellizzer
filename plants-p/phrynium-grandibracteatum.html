Phrynium grandibracteatum

Phrynium grandibracteatum, commonly known as the giant arrowleaf, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking, orange-red bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Phrynium grandibracteatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
