Prangos hermonis

Prangos hermonis, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Prangos hermonis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in its native habitat.
