Polygala calliptera

Polygala calliptera, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slight pubescence.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
