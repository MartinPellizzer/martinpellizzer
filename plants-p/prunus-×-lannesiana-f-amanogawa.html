Prunus × lannesiana f. amanogawa

Prunus × lannesiana f. amanogawa, commonly known as the Amanogawa cherry, is a compact, ornamental cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring. The leaves are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the pale blossoms. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It is relatively disease-resistant and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.
