Parsonsia oblancifolia

Parsonsia oblancifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf parsonsia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red or orange throat. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for both garden cultivation and natural habitats.

Parsonsia oblancifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
