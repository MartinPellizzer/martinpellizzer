Piper crassistilum

Piper crassistilum, commonly known as the broad-stemmed pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, woody fruits. Its botanical characteristics make it a unique species within the genus Piper.

It is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests.
