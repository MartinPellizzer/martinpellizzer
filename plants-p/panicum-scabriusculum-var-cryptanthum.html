Panicum scabriusculum var. cryptanthum

Panicum scabriusculum var. cryptanthum, commonly known as the black panicgrass, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and dark green foliage that turns bronze in the fall. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often used as ornamental features in landscaping. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for drought-tolerant gardens.

Its low maintenance requirements and ability to form dense mats make it a popular choice for erosion control and ground cover.
