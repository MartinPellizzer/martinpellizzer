Pfaffia pulverulenta f. eriantha

Pfaffia pulverulenta f. eriantha, commonly known as the velvetleaf arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
