Parsonsia dispersa

Parsonsia dispersa, commonly known as the yellow moonflower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant, yellow flowers that open at night. The plant has simple, opposite leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its nocturnal blooming habit.
