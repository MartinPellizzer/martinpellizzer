Philibertia discolor

Philibertia discolor, commonly known as the bluebell of the south, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Philibertia discolor is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
