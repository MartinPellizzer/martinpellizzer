Pilosella floribunda

Pilosella floribunda, commonly known as the showy pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, often arranged in dense, spherical clusters. Its leaves are typically hairy and alternate, giving the plant its common name.

Pilosella floribunda is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
