Parapolystichum subsimile

Parapolystichum subsimile, commonly known as the similar shield fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its leathery, evergreen fronds that are typically arranged in a compact, shield-like shape. The plant produces sporangia on the undersides of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in sori covered by a protective indusium. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
