Pavonia lagunarum

Pavonia lagunarum, commonly known as the marsh mallow or swamp mallow, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in marshy or swampy areas, thriving in moist, sandy soils. The plant features showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a soft, fuzzy texture.

Pavonia lagunarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
