Pholidota subcalceata

Pholidota subcalceata, commonly known as the Indian nettle or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in umbels. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
