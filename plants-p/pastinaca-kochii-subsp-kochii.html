Pastinaca kochii subsp. kochii

Pastinaca kochii subsp.

kochii, commonly known as the Koch's bur marigold, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils.

The plant features yellow flowers arranged in a single, terminal head, and its leaves are pinnately lobed with a rough texture. It is characterized by its ability to tolerate saline conditions, making it a hardy species in coastal environments.

The subspecies is often found in open habitats such as dunes and salt marshes.
