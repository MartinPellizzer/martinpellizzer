Piptadenia insignis

Piptadenia insignis, commonly known as the Brazilian peach tree, is a deciduous legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a woody pod containing edible, sweet, and fleshy seeds resembling peaches.

Piptadenia insignis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a food source.
