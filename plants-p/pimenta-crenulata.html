Pimenta crenulata

Pimenta crenulata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of Brazil and is characterized by its small, red, fleshy fruits that are often used in culinary applications. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and it produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It is a fast-growing tree that can reach heights of up to 15 meters, making it a prominent feature in its native habitat.
