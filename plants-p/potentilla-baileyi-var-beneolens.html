Potentilla baileyi var. beneolens

Potentilla baileyi var. beneolens, commonly known as the Bailey's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. The plant features compound leaves with five leaflets, and its flowers are small, yellow, and borne in clusters. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The species is also noted for its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
