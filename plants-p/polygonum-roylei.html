Polygonum roylei

Polygonum roylei, commonly known as the Kashmir knotweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It is characterized by its erect stem and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Polygonum roylei is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
