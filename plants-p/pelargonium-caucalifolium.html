Pelargonium caucalifolium

Pelargonium caucalifolium, commonly known as the Cape marigold, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. Its flowers are borne in clusters and have a distinctive, slightly hairy appearance.

Pelargonium caucalifolium prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
