Phoenicanthemum intermedium

Phoenicanthemum intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate phoenicanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, pale yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun exposure, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in mild, temperate climates.
