Phyllanthus volkensii

Phyllanthus volkensii, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and smooth, often thorny stems. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, yellowish aril. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in savannas and rocky areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant.
