Pavonia crispa

Pavonia crispa, commonly known as the crinkled pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crinkled leaves and showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a trailing or ascending growth habit.

Pavonia crispa is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and vibrant floral displays.
