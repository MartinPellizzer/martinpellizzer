Podocarpus taxifolius

Podocarpus taxifolius, commonly known as the Chinese sandalwood or Indian sandalwood, is a member of the Podocarpaceae family.

It is a slow-growing evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, including China, India, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its lanceolate, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the branches. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are followed by woody cones that contain aromatic seeds.

The tree is valued for its fragrant heartwood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
