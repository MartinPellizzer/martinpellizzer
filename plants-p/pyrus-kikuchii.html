Pyrus kikuchii

Pyrus kikuchii, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by small, round fruits that ripen in late summer. The fruits are typically white or pale yellow, with a thin, crisp skin and a sweet, aromatic flavor.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also cultivated for its edible fruit in some regions.
