Palmia bicolor

Palmia bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored palm, is a unique species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking bi-colored leaves, which display a mix of green and silver hues. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its trunk is slender and often covered in a fibrous, spongy material.

Palmia bicolor thrives in humid environments and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.
