Prunus angustifolia watsonii

Prunus angustifolia watsonii, commonly known as the narrowleaf cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia, where it thrives in wetland habitats. The plant is characterized by its slender, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are small drupes that turn red when mature, attracting various wildlife species.

This plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and aesthetic value.
