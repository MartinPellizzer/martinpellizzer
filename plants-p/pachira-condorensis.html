Pachira condorensis

Pachira condorensis, commonly known as the Cusco pachira, is a tropical plant native to the Peruvian Andes.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as an understory species in humid, shaded environments. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody fruits containing several seeds.
