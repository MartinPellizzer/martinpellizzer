Petrosimonia volvox

Petrosimonia volvox, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy and rocky soils, often found in desert and semi-desert environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically small, fleshy, and covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its ability to survive with minimal water.
