Peperomia miradoresiana

Peperomia miradoresiana, commonly known as the Mirador's peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped, often with a deep green color and a slightly glossy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, usually greenish-white in color, which are followed by round, fleshy berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
