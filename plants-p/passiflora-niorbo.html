Passiflora niorbo

Passiflora niorbo, commonly known as the Niorbo passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It produces striking, showy flowers with vibrant colors and unique structures, including a crown of filaments and a central corona. The plant's botanical features include compound leaves with three to five leaflets and tendrils for climbing support. Its fruits are typically edible, containing a cluster of seeds surrounded by a fleshy pulp.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
