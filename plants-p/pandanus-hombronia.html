Pandanus hombronia

Pandanus hombronia, commonly known as the Hombronia pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Indo-Pacific, including parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with fragrant, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or wetland habitats.
