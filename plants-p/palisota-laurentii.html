Palisota laurentii

Palisota laurentii, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color, and appear in clusters.

Palisota laurentii is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
