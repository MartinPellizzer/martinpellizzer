Phalaenopsis amabilis var. sanderiana

Phalaenopsis amabilis var. sanderiana, commonly known as the Sander's orchid, is a popular epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features elegant, long-lasting flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often resembling moth wings, hence its common name. The plant has broad, dark green leaves and produces multiple flower spikes from the base of the plant. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent humidity and well-draining potting media.

This variety is prized for its beauty and ease of care, making it a favorite among orchid enthusiasts.
