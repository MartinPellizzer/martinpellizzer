Primulina fimbrisepala

Primulina fimbrisepala, commonly known as the fringed-lobed primula, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is relatively easy to care for in a home garden.

Its unique floral structure and attractive foliage make it a popular choice among plant enthusiasts.
