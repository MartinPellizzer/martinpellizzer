Piper brevilimbum

Piper brevilimbum, commonly known as the small-leaved pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and pharmaceutical researchers alike.
