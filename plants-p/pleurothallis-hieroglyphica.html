Pleurothallis hieroglyphica

Pleurothallis hieroglyphica, commonly known as the hieroglyphic orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of South America.

This epiphytic plant is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that resemble ancient Egyptian hieroglyphs, giving it its common name. It typically grows on tree branches and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves and produces delicate, fragrant blooms in shades of white and pink.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
