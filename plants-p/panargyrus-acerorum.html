Panargyrus acerorum

Panargyrus acerorum, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow and green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a star-like appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic appeal.
