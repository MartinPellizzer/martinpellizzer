Pandanus papilio

Pandanus papilio, commonly known as the red pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are typically bright green with a reddish tint. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by fibrous, banana-like fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and thatching.

The species is adapted to coastal and wetland environments, thriving in humid, saline conditions.
