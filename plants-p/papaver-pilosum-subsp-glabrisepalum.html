Papaver pilosum subsp. glabrisepalum

Papaver pilosum subsp.

glabrisepalum, commonly known as the glabrous-sepal poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region. It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its capsule fruits are elongated and contain numerous small, dark seeds.

This subspecies is characterized by its glabrous (smooth) sepals, distinguishing it from other members of the Papaver pilosum species complex.
