Pseudocarapa nitidula var. latifolia

Pseudocarapa nitidula var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed pseudocarapa, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small, clustered, and usually white or pale yellow, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
