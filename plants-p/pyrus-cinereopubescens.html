Pyrus cinereopubescens

Pyrus cinereopubescens, commonly known as the silver-haired pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China. The plant is characterized by its grayish-white, pubescent leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like berry, is edible but not widely cultivated.
