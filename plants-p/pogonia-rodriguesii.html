Pogonia rodriguesii

Pogonia rodriguesii, commonly known as Rodrigues's pogonia, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the island of Rodrigues in the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and a single, showy flower with a distinctive lip that often displays intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers bloom during the late summer or early autumn, and the species is known for its fragrant scent, which attracts specific pollinators.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Pogonia rodriguesii is considered critically endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
