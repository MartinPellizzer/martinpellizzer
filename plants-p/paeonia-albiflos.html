Paeonia albiflos

Paeonia albiflos, commonly known as the white peony, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are often covered in a soft, grayish-brown bark. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The white peony is also valued for its fragrance and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
