Potentilla gaudinii f. latifolia

Potentilla gaudinii f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf potentilla, is a cultivar of the Potentilla genus native to Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. This plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant due to its compact growth habit and ornamental value.

The foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, making it a versatile choice for garden landscapes.
