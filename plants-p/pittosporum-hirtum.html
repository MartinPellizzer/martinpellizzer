Pittosporum hirtum

Pittosporum hirtum, commonly known as the velvetleaf pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that is often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by bright red or orange berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Pittosporum hirtum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal flowering.
