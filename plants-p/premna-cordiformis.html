Premna cordiformis

Premna cordiformis, commonly known as heartleaf or heartleaf fig, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The leaves are usually glossy and have a distinct reddish or purple hue when young. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by round, greenish fruits that turn yellow when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
