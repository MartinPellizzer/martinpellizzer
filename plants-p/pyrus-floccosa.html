Pyrus floccosa

Pyrus floccosa, commonly known as the woolly pear.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae, native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant is characterized by its soft, woolly covering on its leaves and fruits, which gives it its common name. It produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are typically edible but not widely cultivated.

Pyrus floccosa is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its ornamental features.
