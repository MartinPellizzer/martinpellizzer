Pleiochiton setulosus

Pleiochiton setulosus, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, often branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan. Its flowering stems are usually shorter than the vegetative shoots, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens and as a houseplant.
