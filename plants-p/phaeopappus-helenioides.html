Phaeopappus helenioides

Phaeopappus helenioides, commonly known as the Helen's pappus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
