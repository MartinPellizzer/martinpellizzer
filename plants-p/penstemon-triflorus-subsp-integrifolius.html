Penstemon triflorus subsp. integrifolius

Penstemon triflorus subsp.

integrifolius, commonly known as the tall bearded penstemon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to violet.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have smooth margins, which distinguish it from other penstemon species. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
