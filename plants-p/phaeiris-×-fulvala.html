Phaeiris × fulvala

Phaeiris × fulvala, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its botanical features include long, strap-like leaves and a compact, pseudobulbous stem structure.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouse environments.
