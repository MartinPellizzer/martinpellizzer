Penstemon ellipticus

Penstemon ellipticus, commonly known as the narrowleaf beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping. Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Penstemon ellipticus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in gardens.
