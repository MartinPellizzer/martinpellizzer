Penstemon ambiguus var. thurberi

Penstemon ambiguus var. thurberi, commonly known as Thurber's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces distinctive, fuzzy, blue-green leaves that are lance-shaped and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

This variety is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
