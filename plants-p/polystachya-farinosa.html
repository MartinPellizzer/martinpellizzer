Polystachya farinosa

Polystachya farinosa, commonly known as the farinose spikegrass, is a species of flowering plant in the family Polystachyaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with elongated inflorescences that bear small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are linear and covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive texture.

Polystachya farinosa is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
