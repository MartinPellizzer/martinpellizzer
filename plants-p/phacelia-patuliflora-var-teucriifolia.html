Phacelia patuliflora var. teucriifolia

Phacelia patuliflora var. teucriifolia, commonly known as the broad-flowered phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a soft, fuzzy texture.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
