Pterocactus araucanus

Pterocactus araucanus, commonly known as the Araucanian pterocactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to Chile.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green and covered in small, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sunlight to flourish.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species in the wild.
