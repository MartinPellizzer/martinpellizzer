Prevostea insignis

Prevostea insignis, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom during the cooler months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cool, moist environment to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
