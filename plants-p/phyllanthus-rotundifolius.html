Phyllanthus rotundifolius

Phyllanthus rotundifolius, commonly known as the round-leaf phyllanthus, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, rounded leaves that are usually 2 to 5 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, red or orange fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate, making it suitable for cultivation in tropical gardens.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
