Phlox speciosa var. nitida

Phlox speciosa var. nitida, commonly known as the silver phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, cone-shaped flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. The plant has a compact, upright growth habit with silver-gray foliage that adds visual interest throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
