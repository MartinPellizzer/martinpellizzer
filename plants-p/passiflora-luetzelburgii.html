Passiflora luetzelburgii

Passiflora luetzelburgii, commonly known as the Luetzelburg's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a mix of blue, purple, and white petals, along with a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-colored berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.

Passiflora luetzelburgii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
