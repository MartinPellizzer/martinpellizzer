Piliocalyx eugenioides

Piliocalyx eugenioides, commonly known as the velvetleaf or the white-flowered piliocalyx, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often pubescent, giving the plant a velvety texture. The flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and typically have white or pale pink petals. It produces small, ellipsoid fruits that contain one or two seeds.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
