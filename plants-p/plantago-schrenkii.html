Plantago schrenkii

Plantago schrenkii, commonly known as Schrenk's plantain, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments. The plant has a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for its anti-inflammatory and healing qualities.
