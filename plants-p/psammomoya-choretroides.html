Psammomoya choretroides

Psammomoya choretroides, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent-like growth habit and is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom during the cooler months. It has a shallow root system that allows it to efficiently absorb moisture from the soil.

Due to its resilience and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
