Potentilla wimanniana proles brachyloba

Potentilla wimanniana proles brachyloba, commonly known as the short-stemmed cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange, blooming in late summer. The plant has slender, often hairy stems and trifoliate or palmate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cooler, high-altitude environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and flowering display.
