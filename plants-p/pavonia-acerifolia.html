Pavonia acerifolia

Pavonia acerifolia, commonly known as the mapleleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves that resemble those of maple trees, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
