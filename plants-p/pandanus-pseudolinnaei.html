Pandanus pseudolinnaei

Pandanus pseudolinnaei, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the Pacific Islands, including parts of French Polynesia and New Caledonia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green in color.

The plant thrives in coastal and lowland areas, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.
