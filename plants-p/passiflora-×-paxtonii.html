Passiflora × paxtonii

Passiflora × paxtonii, commonly known as the Paxton's passionflower, is a hybrid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and striking, colorful flowers that feature a distinctive crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, tropical-looking berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy blooms.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid that showcases the diversity within the Passifloraceae family.
