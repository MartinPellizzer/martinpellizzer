Paronychia libertadiana

Paronychia libertadiana, commonly known as the Libertanian sandbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, dense leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
