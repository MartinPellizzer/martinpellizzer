Paspalum pittieri

Paspalum pittieri, commonly known as the coastal paspalum, is a grass species native to the coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in sandy or saline soils and is highly tolerant of coastal conditions, including salt spray and wind. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion.

Paspalum pittieri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping in coastal areas.
