Polemanniopsis namibensis

Polemanniopsis namibensis, commonly known as the Namib Desert lily, is a succulent plant native to the Namib Desert in Namibia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, white flowers during the rainy season, typically in late winter or early spring. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Polemanniopsis namibensis is adapted to extreme environmental conditions, making it a unique example of desert flora.
