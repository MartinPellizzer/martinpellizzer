Peucedanum platytaenium

Peucedanum platytaenium, commonly known as the broad-leafed parsley.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and umbellate flowers. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The fruit is a small, dry schizocarp that splits into two parts when mature.
