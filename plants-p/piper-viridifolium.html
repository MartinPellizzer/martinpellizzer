Piper viridifolium

Piper viridifolium, commonly known as green-leafed pepper or green pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy green leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruits are small berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
