Piper mandinganum

Piper mandinganum, commonly known as the Philippine pepper or kalamansi pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, peppercorn-like fruits. These fruits are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their aromatic properties.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and economic contexts.
