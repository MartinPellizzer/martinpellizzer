Prunella vulgaris subsp. estremadurensis

Prunella vulgaris subsp.

estremadurensis, commonly known as the Estremaduran selfheal, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
