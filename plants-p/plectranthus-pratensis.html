Plectranthus pratensis

Plectranthus pratensis, commonly known as the common plectranthus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale purple or blue, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus pratensis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
