Pilosella concoloriformis

Pilosella concoloriformis, commonly known as the smoothleaf pilosella, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of South America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
