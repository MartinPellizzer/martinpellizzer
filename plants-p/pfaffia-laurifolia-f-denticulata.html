Pfaffia laurifolia f. denticulata

Pfaffia laurifolia f. denticulata, commonly known as the serrated leaf pfaffia, is a variant of the pfaffia plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which give it its distinguishing feature. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of forests. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits.

This species is valued for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic uses.
