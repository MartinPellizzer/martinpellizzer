Plagiobothrys tenellus var. parvulus

Plagiobothrys tenellus var. parvulus, commonly known as the small-flowered sand verbena, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features slender, wiry stems that bear small, clustered clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
