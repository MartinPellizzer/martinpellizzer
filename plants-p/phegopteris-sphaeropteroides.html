Phegopteris sphaeropteroides

Phegopteris sphaeropteroides, commonly known as the globe fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded fronds that are typically composed of small, overlapping pinnae. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
