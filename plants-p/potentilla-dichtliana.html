Potentilla dichtliana

Potentilla dichtliana, commonly known as the denseleaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, rounded leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice among gardeners seeking drought-resistant plants.
