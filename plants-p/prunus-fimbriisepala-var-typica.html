Prunus fimbriisepala var. typica

Prunus fimbriisepala var. typica, commonly known as the fringed petal cherry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fringed petals that give it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, are usually white or pale pink and are followed by small, round fruits.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth texture, contributing to its overall aesthetic appeal.
