Panicum forbesii

Panicum forbesii, commonly known as Forbes' panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces delicate, airy panicles of flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and disturbed soils. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that becomes more pronounced during the growing season.

Forbes' panicgrass is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
