Prosopanche bertoniensis var. stricta

Prosopanche bertoniensis var. stricta, commonly known as the rigid velvet tamarind, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, branching stems and clusters of small, waxy leaves. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which attract specific pollinators. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its ability to store water in its thick stems.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid gardens.
