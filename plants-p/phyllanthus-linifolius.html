Phyllanthus linifolius

Phyllanthus linifolius, commonly known as the African spurge, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, linear leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Phyllanthus linifolius is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating liver ailments and as an antimalarial.
