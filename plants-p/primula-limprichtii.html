Primula limprichtii

Primula limprichtii, commonly known as the Limpricht's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
