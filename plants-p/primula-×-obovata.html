Primula × obovata

Primula × obovata, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a perennial flowering plant that belongs to the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. Native to the mountainous regions of Asia, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.

The species is also notable for its genetic diversity, resulting from crosses between different Primula species.
