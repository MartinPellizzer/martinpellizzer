Primula calyptrata

Primula calyptrata, commonly known as the broad-leaf primrose, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and violet, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
