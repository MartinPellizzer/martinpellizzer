Pimelea concreta

Pimelea concreta, commonly known as the white-leafed riceflower, is a low-growing shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish powder, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the dry, sandy environments of its native habitat.

Pimelea concreta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
