Polygala flavescens subsp. flavescens

Polygala flavescens subsp.

flavescens, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas. It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves are opposite and pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
