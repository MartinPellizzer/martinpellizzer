Paullinia bidentata

Paullinia bidentata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with two distinct teeth on the margins. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in folk medicine.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, posing ecological challenges.
