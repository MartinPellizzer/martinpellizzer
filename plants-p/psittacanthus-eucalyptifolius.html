Psittacanthus eucalyptifolius

Psittacanthus eucalyptifolius, commonly known as the lemon-scented hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that emit a distinct lemony fragrance when crushed. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
