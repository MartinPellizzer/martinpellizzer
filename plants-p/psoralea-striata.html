Psoralea striata

Psoralea striata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive striated pattern, giving it its scientific name.

Psoralea striata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
