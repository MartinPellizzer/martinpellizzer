Phthirusa cochliostyla

Phthirusa cochliostyla, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in arid and semi-arid regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
