Pyrus communis var. pulchella

Pyrus communis var. pulchella, commonly known as the sweet cherry, is a cultivar of the common pear tree.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and juicy fruits that are typically harvested in late spring. The plant has a compact growth habit with spreading branches and dark green leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun for optimal fruit production.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value as well as its culinary uses.
