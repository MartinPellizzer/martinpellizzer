Pelargonium carnosum subsp. ferulaceum

Pelargonium carnosum subsp.

ferulaceum, commonly known as the Cape rose or sand rose, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features fleshy, cylindrical stems covered in a powdery coating that helps reduce water loss.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the cooler months. Its leaves are thick and often have a waxy texture, adapted to store water.

It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
