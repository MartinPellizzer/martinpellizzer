Pourthiaea hirsuta var. lobulata

Pourthiaea hirsuta var. lobulata, commonly known as the hairy pourthiaea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, achene-like fruits.

It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
