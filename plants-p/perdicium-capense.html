Perdicium capense

Perdicium capense, commonly known as the Cape partridge pea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Perdicium capense is adapted to arid conditions and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
