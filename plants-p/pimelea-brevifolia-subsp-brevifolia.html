Pimelea brevifolia subsp. brevifolia

Pimelea brevifolia subsp.

brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rice flower, is a small shrub native to southeastern Australia. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open forests and heathlands.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered with fine hairs.

It is a hardy species that is relatively drought-tolerant and suitable for cultivation in well-drained soils.
