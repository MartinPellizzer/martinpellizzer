Penstemon barbatus var. torreyi

Penstemon barbatus var. torreyi, commonly known as Torrey's beardplant, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial species characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers. The flowers typically range in color from blue to purple and are adapted for pollination by hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-tolerant nature.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
