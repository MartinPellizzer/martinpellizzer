Pleurothallis sylvatica

Pleurothallis sylvatica, commonly known as the silver orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often have a silvery or white hue. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its leaves are usually elongated and strap-like, adapted to capture light in the shaded environments where it thrives.

Pleurothallis sylvatica is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
