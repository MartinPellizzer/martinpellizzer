Persicaria hydropiperoides var. bushiana

Persicaria hydropiperoides var. bushiana, commonly known as the water pepper, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, white hairs, which give it a slightly fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly toothed margin.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near streams or in wet meadows.
