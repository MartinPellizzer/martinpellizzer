Paspalum plicatum

Paspalum plicatum, commonly known as the zigzag grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and distinctive zigzag pattern along its stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its flowering heads are small and clustered, producing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Paspalum plicatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
