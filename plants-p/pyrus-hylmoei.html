Pyrus hylmoei

Pyrus hylmoei, commonly known as the Hylmoes pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kyrgyzstan and Tajikistan. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, with heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like berry, is typically yellow or orange when ripe and is used in traditional local cuisines.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
