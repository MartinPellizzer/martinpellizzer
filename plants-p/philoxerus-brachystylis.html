Philoxerus brachystylis

Philoxerus brachystylis, commonly known as the dwarf spider orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, intricately patterned flowers that resemble spider webs, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within rainforests, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its roots form a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and habitat loss.
