Pentopetiopsis

Pentopetiopsis, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Bolivia. The plants are characterized by their compact growth habit and showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive star-shaped pattern. Pentopetiopsis species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
