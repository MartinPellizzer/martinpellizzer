Prunus mutabilis f. multipetala

Prunus mutabilis f. multipetala, commonly known as the double-flowered cherry, is a unique cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its showy, double blooms that appear in early spring, often in shades of white, pink, or red. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin, and they turn yellow in the autumn.

The flowers are fragrant and attract pollinators, making it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
