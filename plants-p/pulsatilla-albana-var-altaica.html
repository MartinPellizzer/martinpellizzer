Pulsatilla albana var. altaica

Pulsatilla albana var. altaica, commonly known as the highland pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, dry climates. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces a globular, spiny fruit that contains numerous seeds.

Pulsatilla albana var. altaica is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
