Papaver dubium var. bipinnatum

Papaver dubium var. bipinnatum, commonly known as the bipinnate poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its distinctive bipinnate foliage.

This plant is native to southern Europe and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its flowers are typically pale pink to white, with a prominent central stigma and showy petals. The leaves are deeply divided into fine, feather-like segments, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is a herbaceous perennial that blooms in late spring to early summer.
