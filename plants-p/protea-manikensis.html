Protea manikensis

Protea manikensis, commonly known as the Manikeni protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous individual flowers clustered around a central stem. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in the winter rainfall regions of South Africa. Its flowers are often pink or red, with a distinctive, flattened structure that resembles a pom-pom.

Protea manikensis is an important component of fynbos vegetation and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
