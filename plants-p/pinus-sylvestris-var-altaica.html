Pinus sylvestris var. altaica

Pinus sylvestris var. altaica, commonly known as the Siberian pine, is a variant of the Scots pine native to the cold climates of eastern Europe and western Siberia.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant exhibits adaptations to harsh environments, including a thick bark and deep root systems. Its cones are typically woody and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems and is valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.
