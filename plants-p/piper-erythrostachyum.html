Piper erythrostachyum

Piper erythrostachyum, commonly known as the red-stemmed pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its reddish stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by berries that mature to a bright red color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and potential medicinal properties.
