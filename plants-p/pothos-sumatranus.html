Pothos sumatranus

Pothos sumatranus, commonly known as the golden pothos, is a trailing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically variegated with shades of yellow and green. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in low-light conditions. It produces small, greenish-white flowers, though these are rarely seen in cultivation.

Pothos sumatranus is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
