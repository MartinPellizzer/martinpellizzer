Plectranthus longicornis var. scapiger

Plectranthus longicornis var. scapiger, commonly known as the scarlet plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

The variety scapiger is notable for its showy inflorescences, which are borne on long, slender stems.
