Pyrus armeniacifolia

Pyrus armeniacifolia, commonly known as the Armenian whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in early spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are similar to apples but less commonly consumed. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin.

Pyrus armeniacifolia is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
