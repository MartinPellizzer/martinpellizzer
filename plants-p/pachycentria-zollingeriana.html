Pachycentria zollingeriana

Pachycentria zollingeriana, commonly known as the Zollinger's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves, which store water to help it survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
