Polygonum ilanense

Polygonum ilanense, commonly known as the Indian knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its stems are often reddish in color and can become woody with age.

Polygonum ilanense is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
