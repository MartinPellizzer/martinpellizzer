Piper schuppii

Piper schuppii, commonly known as the West Indian pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that often twine around other vegetation. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin, while the small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes.

The fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature, containing a single seed.
