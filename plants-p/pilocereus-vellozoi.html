Pilocereus vellozoi

Pilocereus vellozoi, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and is typically found in dry, arid environments. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its growth is slow, and it thrives in well-drained soil with minimal water.

The plant is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its unique appearance and dramatic nighttime blooms.
