Picris hieracioides subsp. rielii

Picris hieracioides subsp.

rielii, commonly known as Riel's wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small yellow flowers arranged in composite heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. It is known for its aromatic, slightly bitter leaves and is often used in traditional medicine.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
