Piper angustatum

Piper angustatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf pepper plant, is a tropical vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, spiny fruits containing aromatic seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to humid, shaded environments in the understory of tropical forests.
