Pandanus setistylus

Pandanus setistylus, commonly known as the tall pandanus or narrow-leaf pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia, the Philippines, and parts of Papua New Guinea. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color.

Pandanus setistylus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber in some cultures.
