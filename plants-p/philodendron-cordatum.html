Philodendron cordatum

Philodendron cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly variegated appearance. The plant has a vigorous growth habit and can reach lengths of several meters when given proper support. Its leaves are often used in decorative arrangements due to their aesthetic appeal.

Philodendron cordatum thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to maintain its lush foliage.
