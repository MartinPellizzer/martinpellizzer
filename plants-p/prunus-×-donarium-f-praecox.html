Prunus × donarium f. praecox

Prunus × donarium f. praecox, commonly known as the early-flowering Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus, characterized by its showy, single-layered flowers that bloom early in the spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters and features ovate leaves with a smooth texture. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale pink, are followed by small, edible drupes.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring display.
