Prunus armeniaca var. dulcis

Prunus armeniaca var. dulcis, commonly known as the sweet cherry, is a deciduous fruit tree native to southwest Asia.

It produces small, round fruits with a thin, red or yellow skin and a sweet, juicy interior. The plant has simple, alternate leaves and produces white or pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is widely cultivated for its culinary and commercial uses.
