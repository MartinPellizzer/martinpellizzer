Poa laxa var. tremula

Poa laxa var. tremula, commonly known as the trembling meadow grass, is a variant of the meadow grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as forage for wildlife and its contribution to soil stabilization.
