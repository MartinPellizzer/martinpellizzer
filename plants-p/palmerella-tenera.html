Palmerella tenera

Palmerella tenera, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

Palmerella tenera is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environments.
