Prometheum pilosum

Prometheum pilosum, commonly known as the flame-haired fern, is a striking plant characterized by its unique, hairy fronds that resemble flames.

Native to the humid rainforests of Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically dark green with a silvery underside, adding to its visual appeal. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
