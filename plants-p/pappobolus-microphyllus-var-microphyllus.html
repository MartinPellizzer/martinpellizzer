Pappobolus microphyllus var. microphyllus

Pappobolus microphyllus var. microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved burr grass, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and produces distinctive burr-like seed heads. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It has a tufted growth habit and is well-adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

The seeds are encased in a hairy, spiky structure that aids in dispersal by attaching to animals or clothing.
