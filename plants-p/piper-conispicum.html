Piper conispicum

Piper conispicum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, opposite leaves that are often lance-shaped. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
