Polygala laureola f. cestrifolia

Polygala laureola f. cestrifolia, commonly known as the laurel polygala, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygalaceae.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, often clustered leaves. The plant produces delicate, yellowish-green flowers that are typically arranged in racemes, which are elongated clusters. It is native to regions of southern Europe, particularly the Mediterranean, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
