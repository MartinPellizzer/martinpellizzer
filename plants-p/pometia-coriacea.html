Pometia coriacea

Pometia coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaved pometia, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, round berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.
