Potamogeton promontoricus

Potamogeton promontoricus, commonly known as the rock pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving waters, such as ponds and lakes, where it can anchor itself to substrates. Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and stable ecological conditions.

Potamogeton promontoricus plays a vital role in providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
