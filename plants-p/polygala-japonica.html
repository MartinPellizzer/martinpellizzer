Polygala japonica

Polygala japonica, commonly known as the Japanese milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately divided, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The flowers, which are usually pink or white, bloom in early summer and attract various pollinators.
