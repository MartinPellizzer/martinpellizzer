Prunella tournefortii

Prunella tournefortii, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Prunella tournefortii is often found in coastal areas and is known for its hardy, drought-resistant nature.
