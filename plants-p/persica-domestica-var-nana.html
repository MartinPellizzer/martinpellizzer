Persica domestica var. nana

Persica domestica var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf peach tree, is a compact variety of the domestic peach.

It features small, rounded leaves and produces sweet, flavorful fruits that are typically smaller than those of standard peaches. The plant is characterized by its short stature, making it ideal for small gardens or container growing. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring regular watering and pruning for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and manageable size, suitable for both ornamental and edible cultivation.
