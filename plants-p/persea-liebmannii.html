Persea liebmannii

Persea liebmannii, commonly known as the Liebmann's persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by orange to red berries. Its fruits are edible, though they are typically consumed when fully ripe and soft.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fruit.
