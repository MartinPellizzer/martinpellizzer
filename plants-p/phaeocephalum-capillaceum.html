Phaeocephalum capillaceum

Phaeocephalum capillaceum, commonly known as the silky moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Pottiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and dark green to brownish foliage. The leaves are elongated, with a distinct mid-rib and a narrow, hair-like structure along the edges. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on soil or rock surfaces in temperate regions.

This moss is known for its ability to retain water and withstand periods of desiccation.
