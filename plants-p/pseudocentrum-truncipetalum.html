Pseudocentrum truncipetalum

Pseudocentrum truncipetalum, commonly known as the Philippine star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits resembling apples.

Its fruit is edible, though the seeds are toxic, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
