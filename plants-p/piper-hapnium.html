Piper hapnium

Piper hapnium, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their medicinal properties.

The plant's botanical features include compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the axils of the leaves.
