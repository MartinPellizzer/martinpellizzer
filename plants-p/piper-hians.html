Piper hians

Piper hians, commonly known as the white pepper plant, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces berries that contain the aromatic seeds used to make white pepper. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a unique scent due to the presence of piperine.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses.
