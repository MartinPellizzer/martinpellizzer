Pyrus prinoides

Pyrus prinoides, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, which are followed by yellowish or reddish fruits that ripen in late summer.

Pyrus prinoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
