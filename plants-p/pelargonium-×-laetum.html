Pelargonium × laetum

Pelargonium × laetum, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically rounded and slightly hairy, while the flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
