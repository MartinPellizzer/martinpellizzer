Panicum schultesii

Panicum schultesii, commonly known as Schultesi panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, savannas, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
