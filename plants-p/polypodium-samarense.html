Polypodium samarense

Polypodium samarense, commonly known as the Samarensis polypody, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

Polypodium samarense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
