Phymatidiopsis

Phymatidiopsis, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family and is characterized by its unique, delicate appearance with pale greenish-white flowers. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires specific humidity and temperature conditions to flourish.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
