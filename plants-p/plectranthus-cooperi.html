Plectranthus cooperi

Plectranthus cooperi, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves with a fragrant, minty aroma. The plant produces small, tubular, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, typically blooming from summer to fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Plectranthus cooperi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
