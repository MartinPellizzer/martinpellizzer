Pandanus spinistigmaticus

Pandanus spinistigmaticus, commonly known as the spiny-stigmatic pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in small, spiny projections. The plant produces inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, typical of the Pandanus genus. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and for thatching, while the fruit is edible.

The species is adapted to coastal and sandy environments, making it a resilient plant in tropical regions.
