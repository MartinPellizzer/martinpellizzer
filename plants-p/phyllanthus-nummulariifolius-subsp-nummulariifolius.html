Phyllanthus nummulariifolius subsp. nummulariifolius

Phyllanthus nummulariifolius subsp.

nummulariifolius, commonly known as the coinleaf mallow, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are round or oval in shape, resembling coins, hence its common name.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
