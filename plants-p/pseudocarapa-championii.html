Pseudocarapa championii

Pseudocarapa championii, commonly known as the yellow raintree, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil types.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
