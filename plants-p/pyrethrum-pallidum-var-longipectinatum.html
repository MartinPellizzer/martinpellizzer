Pyrethrum pallidum var. longipectinatum

Pyrethrum pallidum var. longipectinatum, commonly known as the pale pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant is notable for its finely divided, fern-like leaves that are long and narrow, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.

This variety is also valued for its potential in natural pest control, as it contains pyrethrins, which are effective insecticides.
