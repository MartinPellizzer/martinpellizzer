Puya coerulea var. intermedia

Puya coerulea var. intermedia, commonly known as the Intermediate Blue Puya, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the bromeliad family, characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central tank to collect rainwater. The plant produces vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a dramatic visual display. Its foliage is often adorned with intricate patterns and a waxy coating, enhancing its ornamental appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is best suited for cultivation in warm, humid environments with well-draining soil.
