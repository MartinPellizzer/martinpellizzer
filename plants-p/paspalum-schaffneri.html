Paspalum schaffneri

Paspalum schaffneri, commonly known as Schaffner's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wetland and marshy environments, often found in coastal areas.

Paspalum schaffneri is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
