Piper donnell-smithii

Piper donnell-smithii, commonly known as the Donnell-Smith pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.

The fruit, which is a small berry, is edible but not widely used in culinary applications.
