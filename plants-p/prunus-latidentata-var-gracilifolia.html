Prunus latidentata var. gracilifolia

Prunus latidentata var. gracilifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters, featuring slender, often multi-branched stems with a smooth, grey-brown bark. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by oval, dark red to black berries. Its leaves are elliptic to ovate, with a smooth margin and a pointed tip, and they turn yellow in autumn.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
