Passiflora trianae

Passiflora trianae, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of purple, blue, and white petals, along with intricate filaments and a crown of stamens. The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Passiflora trianae is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
