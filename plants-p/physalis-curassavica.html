Physalis curassavica

Physalis curassavica, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or golden berry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange, lantern-shaped fruit that encloses the seeds. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit. The leaves are broad and oval, with a slightly hairy texture, and the flowers are typically yellow with a distinctive purple calyx.

It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for both culinary and decorative purposes.
