Philibertia ervendbergii

Philibertia ervendbergii, commonly known as the Ervendberg's filbert, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically hairy foliage. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
