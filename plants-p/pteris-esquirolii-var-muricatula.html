Pteris esquirolii var. muricatula

Pteris esquirolii var. muricatula, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a variant of the shield fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds that are covered in small, raised sporescales, giving it a textured appearance. The plant produces spores on the underside of its fronds, which are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in indoor and outdoor gardens.
