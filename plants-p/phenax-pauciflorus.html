Phenax pauciflorus

Phenax pauciflorus, commonly known as the spiny leaf plant, is a unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests. Its leaves are thick and leathery, adapted to reduce water loss in its native habitat.

Due to its rare occurrence and specific ecological requirements, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
