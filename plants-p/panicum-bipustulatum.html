Panicum bipustulatum

Panicum bipustulatum, commonly known as the two-spored panicgrass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight pubescence.

Panicum bipustulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
