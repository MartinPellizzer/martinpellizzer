Pulsatilla obsoleta

Pulsatilla obsoleta, commonly known as the lesser pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the plant produces follicular fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Pulsatilla obsoleta is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
