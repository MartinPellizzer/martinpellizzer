Potentilla darvazica

Potentilla darvazica, commonly known as the Darvaz cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its resilience in arid climates.

Potentilla darvazica is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential medicinal properties.
