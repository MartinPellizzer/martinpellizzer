Phyllanthus anadenus

Phyllanthus anadenus, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, smooth, yellow fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas, open woodlands, and disturbed sites.

Phyllanthus anadenus is also noted for its medicinal uses in traditional systems of medicine.
