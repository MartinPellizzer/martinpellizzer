Pelargonium conduplicatum

Pelargonium conduplicatum, commonly known as the double-leaf geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its distinctive leaves that are divided into two pairs of lobes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Pelargonium conduplicatum is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
