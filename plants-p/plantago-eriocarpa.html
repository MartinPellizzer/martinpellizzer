Plantago eriocarpa

Plantago eriocarpa, commonly known as the hairy plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides, fields, and wetlands. The plant features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, hairy structures. Its flowering stems rise above the foliage, bearing clusters of small, greenish flowers that develop into capsule fruits.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
