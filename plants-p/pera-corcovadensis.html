Pera corcovadensis

Pera corcovadensis, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy red or pink flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
