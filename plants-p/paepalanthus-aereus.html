Paepalanthus aereus

Paepalanthus aereus, commonly known as the silverleaf cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that are covered in a silvery-white coating, which helps reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry climates.
