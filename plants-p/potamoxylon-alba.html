Potamoxylon alba

Potamoxylon alba, commonly known as the white riverwood, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stem and distinctive white bark, which becomes more pronounced with age. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near riverbanks or in humid forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, Potamoxylon alba is considered endangered and is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
