Piptocarpha macropoda subsp. macropoda

Piptocarpha macropoda subsp.

macropoda, commonly known as desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, adapted to drought conditions.

The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fibrous stems and leaves are characterized by their narrow, linear shape and hairy surfaces.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
