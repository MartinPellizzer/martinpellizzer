Pyrus austriaca var. sooiana

Pyrus austriaca var. sooiana, commonly known as the Austrian pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet to subacid pomes. It prefers well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The variety sooiana is distinguished by its compact growth habit and improved cold hardiness compared to the typical species.
