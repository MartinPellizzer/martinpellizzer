Penstemon apateticus subsp. townsendianus

Penstemon apateticus subsp.

townsendianus, commonly known as Townsend's penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to violet.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
