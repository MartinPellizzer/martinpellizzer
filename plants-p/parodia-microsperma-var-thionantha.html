Parodia microsperma var. thionantha

Parodia microsperma var. thionantha, commonly known as the "Candelabra Cactus," is a unique hybrid of the Parodia genus.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and distinctive, branching growth pattern that resembles a candle. The plant produces small, white flowers during the summer months, adding to its ornamental appeal. It is native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

Due to its slow growth and specific care requirements, it is a popular choice for experienced cactus growers.
