Phalaenopsis cornu-cervi var. pantherina

Phalaenopsis cornu-cervi var. pantherina, commonly known as the panther orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and long-lasting, fragrant flowers that often display a unique bicolor pattern resembling a panther's coat. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It prefers bright, indirect light and moderate humidity, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its ornamental value lies in its elegant appearance and ability to bloom repeatedly over several years.
