Picea neoveitchii

Picea neoveitchii, commonly known as the Chinese white pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and its dark green, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs. The tree produces cones that are cylindrical and often persist on the branches for several years. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.
