Pleurothallis bocainensis

Pleurothallis bocainensis, commonly known as the Bocainense Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with high levels of rainfall, typical of its tropical habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and specific ecological requirements, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
