Passiflora caerulea var. grandiflora

Passiflora caerulea var. grandiflora, commonly known as the blue passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue and white flowers, which feature intricate structures including a crown of filaments and a central calyx. The plant produces edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, containing a sweet, aromatic pulp. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
