Plantago crassinervis

Plantago crassinervis, commonly known as broad-veined plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, wastelands, and along roadsides. The plant has a basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves with prominent, parallel veins. Its flowering stems bear dense clusters of small, greenish flowers that develop into capsule fruits.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
