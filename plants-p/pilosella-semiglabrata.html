Pilosella semiglabrata

Pilosella semiglabrata, commonly known as the smoothleaf goatweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers and hairy stems, which are less prominent than in some related species. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pilosella semiglabrata is often found in regions with temperate climates and is relatively low maintenance, making it a common sight in disturbed soils.
