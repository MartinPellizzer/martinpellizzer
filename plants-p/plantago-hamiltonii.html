Plantago hamiltonii

Plantago hamiltonii, commonly known as Hamilton's plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features basal rosettes of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
