Pterostylis ferruginea

Pterostylis ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, pale green or brownish flower with a distinctive, elongated labellum. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and heathlands. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms.

The species is relatively rare and is often found in specific microhabitats, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
