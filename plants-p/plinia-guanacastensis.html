Plinia guanacastensis

Plinia guanacastensis, commonly known as the Guanacaste plum, is a species of flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agroforestry systems.
