Pisum sativum var. zubovii

Pisum sativum var. zubovii, commonly known as the Zubovii pea, is a botanical variant of the common pea.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive pod morphology. The plant typically produces small, round pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique genetic traits and potential agricultural applications.
