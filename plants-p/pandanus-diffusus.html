Pandanus diffusus

Pandanus diffusus, commonly known as the broad-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its large, feather-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often with a woody stem and a dense crown of leaves. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and cooking, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental appearance.

The flowers are small and clustered, often appearing at the base of the leaves, and the plant produces fruit that is edible in some species.
