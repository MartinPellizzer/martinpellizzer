Phrynium baccatum

Phrynium baccatum, commonly known as the red-veined arrow leaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves with prominent red veins, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, and it produces berries that contain seeds.

Phrynium baccatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
