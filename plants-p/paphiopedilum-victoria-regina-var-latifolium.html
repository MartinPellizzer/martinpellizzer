Paphiopedilum victoria-regina var. latifolium

Paphiopedilum victoria-regina var. latifolium, commonly known as the Victoria-Regina broad-leafed orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, and broad elliptical leaves that are typically dark green with a silvery underside. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a unique lip that resembles a pouch, a feature common in many orchids. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-draining soil.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
