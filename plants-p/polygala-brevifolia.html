Polygala brevifolia

Polygala brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.

Polygala brevifolia is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution.
