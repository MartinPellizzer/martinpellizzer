Papaver roseolum

Papaver roseolum, commonly known as the rose-like poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, pale pink to lavender flowers with a distinctive, rounded shape and a prominent central stigma. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Papaver roseolum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
