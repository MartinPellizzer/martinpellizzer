Plantago struthionis

Plantago struthionis, commonly known as the desert plantain, is a species of flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in harsh environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Plantago struthionis is well adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low precipitation.
