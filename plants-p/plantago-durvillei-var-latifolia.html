Plantago durvillei var. latifolia

Plantago durvillei var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to parts of southern Africa.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
