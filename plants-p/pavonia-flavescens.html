Pavonia flavescens

Pavonia flavescens, commonly known as the yellow woodbine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its botanical characteristics include small, five-petaled flowers and a fibrous root system.

Pavonia flavescens is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
