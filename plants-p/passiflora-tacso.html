Passiflora tacso

Passiflora tacso, commonly known as the Tacos passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in vitamin C. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and often have a reddish underside.

Passiflora tacso is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
