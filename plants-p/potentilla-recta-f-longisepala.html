Potentilla recta f. longisepala

Potentilla recta f. longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The leaves are typically palmately divided into five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, often found in meadows and rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
