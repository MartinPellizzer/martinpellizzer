Physaria mendocina

Physaria mendocina, commonly known as the Mendo bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture.

Physaria mendocina typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.
