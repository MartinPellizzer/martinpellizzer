Polygala liniflora

Polygala liniflora, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are linear and narrow, giving it a grass-like appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
