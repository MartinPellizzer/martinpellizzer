Potentilla multifida var. cana

Potentilla multifida var. cana, commonly known as the silverleaf potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
