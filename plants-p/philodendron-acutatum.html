Philodendron acutatum

Philodendron acutatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf philodendron, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture in well-draining soil.

Philodendron acutatum is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
