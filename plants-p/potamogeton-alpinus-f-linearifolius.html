Potamogeton alpinus f. linearifolius

Potamogeton alpinus f. linearifolius, commonly known as the alpine pondweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Potamogetonaceae.

It is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its linear, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

Its morphology and growth habits make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems, supporting various aquatic life forms.
