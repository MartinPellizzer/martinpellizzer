Polygala triphylla

Polygala triphylla, commonly known as the three-leaf screwbean, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
