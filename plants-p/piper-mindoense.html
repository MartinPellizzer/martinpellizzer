Piper mindoense

Piper mindoense, commonly known as the Mindo pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its vine-like growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, and they are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an important subject in both ecological and pharmacological studies.
