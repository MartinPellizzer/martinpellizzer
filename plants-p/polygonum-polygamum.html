Polygonum polygamum

Polygonum polygamum, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive red stems, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly rough texture.

Polygonum polygamum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields.
