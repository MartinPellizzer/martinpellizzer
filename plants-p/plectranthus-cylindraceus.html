Plectranthus cylindraceus

Plectranthus cylindraceus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Plectranthus cylindraceus is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
