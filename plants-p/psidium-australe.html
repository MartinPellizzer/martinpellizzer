Psidium australe

Psidium australe, commonly known as the Australian guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits similar to guavas. Psidium australe thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions and dry forests.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
