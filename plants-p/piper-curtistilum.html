Piper curtistilum

Piper curtistilum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pepper vine, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and elongated, narrow leaves. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by small, berry-like fruits.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
