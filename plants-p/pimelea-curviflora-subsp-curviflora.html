Pimelea curviflora subsp. curviflora

Pimelea curviflora subsp.

curviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pimelea, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia. It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open woodlands and heathlands. It is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in coastal regions.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds.
