Pyrus calleryana var. dimorphophylla

Pyrus calleryana var. dimorphophylla, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are typically lobed. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, followed by clusters of yellow or orange fruits in autumn. It is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

The variety is also known for its ability to hybridize with other pear species, contributing to the development of ornamental cultivars.
