Phaseolus coccineus var. tridentatus

Phaseolus coccineus var. tridentatus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It features vibrant red flowers and edible pods that mature into dry, wrinkled seeds. The plant has trifoliate leaves, which are characteristic of the Phaseolus genus. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a cultivated crop and its ornamental value in gardens.
