Penstemon tubaeflorus

Penstemon tubaeflorus, commonly known as the tube-flowered beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial species characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vivid purple or red flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-tolerant nature.

Penstemon tubaeflorus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
