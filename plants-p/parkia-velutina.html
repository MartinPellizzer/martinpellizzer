Parkia velutina

Parkia velutina, commonly known as the velvet acacia, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its roots have nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it valuable in soil enrichment.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and use in traditional medicine.
