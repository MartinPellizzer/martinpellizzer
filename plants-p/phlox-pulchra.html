Phlox pulchra

Phlox pulchra, commonly known as the showy phlox, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. It produces showy clusters of flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Phlox pulchra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
