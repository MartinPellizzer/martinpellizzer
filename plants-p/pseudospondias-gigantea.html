Pseudospondias gigantea

Pseudospondias gigantea, commonly known as the giant spondias, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by edible, round fruits with a sweet, tangy flavor. The tree is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is adaptable to various soil types.
