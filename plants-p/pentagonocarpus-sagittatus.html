Pentagonocarpus sagittatus

Pentagonocarpus sagittatus, commonly known as the arrowleaf tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically arrow-shaped, with a pointed tip and a broad base. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, edible seeds. Its bark is often smooth and may have a reddish or brownish hue, and the tree can grow up to 15 meters in height.

The plant is valued for its timber and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
