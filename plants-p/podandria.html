Podandria

Podandria, commonly known as the "flowering fig," is a unique plant belonging to the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, ornamental flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive color contrast between the petals and sepals. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to figs, though they are not related to true figs. Podandria thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and horticultural collections.
