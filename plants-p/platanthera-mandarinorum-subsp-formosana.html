Platanthera mandarinorum subsp. formosana

Platanthera mandarinorum subsp.

formosana, commonly known as the Formosan lady's tresses, is an orchid native to Taiwan. It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and a striking inflorescence of white to pale pink flowers that resemble a tresses of hair.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded forest environments, often growing on rocky substrates. It is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that are pollinated by insects.

Due to habitat loss, this subspecies is considered rare and is of conservation interest.
