Philodendron roseospathum

Philodendron roseospathum, commonly known as the pink-veined philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that feature a unique pinkish or reddish vein pattern, which sets it apart from other philodendron species. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded outdoor gardens.
