Prunella hyssopifolia

Prunella hyssopifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved heal-all, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, four-lobed fruits that contain several seeds.

Prunella hyssopifolia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
