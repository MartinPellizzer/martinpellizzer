Psidium rhombeum

Psidium rhombeum, commonly known as the Brazilian guava, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellowish-orange fruits similar to guavas. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and has a mild, sweet flavor.
