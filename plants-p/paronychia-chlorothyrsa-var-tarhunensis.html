Paronychia chlorothyrsa var. tarhunensis

Paronychia chlorothyrsa var. tarhunensis, commonly known as the Tarhunensis sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a dense mat of fleshy, succulent leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the cooler months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, known as a bloom, which helps reduce water loss.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
