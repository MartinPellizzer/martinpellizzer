Papaver alpinum lusus bipinnatisectum

Papaver alpinum lusus bipinnatisectum, commonly known as the Alpine poppy with bipinnatisect leaves, is a striking member of the Papaveraceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often orange or red, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features finely divided, bipinnatisect leaves that give it a feathery appearance, enhancing its ornamental value. Native to the alpine regions of Europe, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its unique foliage and colorful blooms, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine arrangements.
