Persicaria amphibia subsp. amurensis

Persicaria amphibia subsp.

amurensis, commonly known as water hemp or Amur knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist or aquatic environments.

The plant features alternate, lanceolate leaves that are often reddish at the base, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. It is commonly found in wetlands, stream banks, and other waterlogged habitats.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain regions outside its native range.
