Pyranthus ambatoanus

Pyranthus ambatoanus, commonly known as the Ambatoan sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that resemble those of sunflowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Pyranthus ambatoanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
