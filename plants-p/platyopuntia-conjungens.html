Platyopuntia conjungens

Platyopuntia conjungens, commonly known as the clustered cholla, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are segmented and covered in small, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a striking contrast to its green, spiky structure. Its leaves are reduced to small spines, and it thrives in arid environments with well-drained soil.

The clustered cholla is a slow-growing plant that is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and unique appearance.
