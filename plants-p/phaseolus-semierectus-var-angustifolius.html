Phaseolus semierectus var. angustifolius

Phaseolus semierectus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed climbing bean, is a legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is cultivated for its edible beans, which are rich in protein.

This variety is notable for its vigorous climbing habit and adaptability to various soil types.
