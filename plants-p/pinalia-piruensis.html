Pinalia piruensis

Pinalia piruensis, commonly known as the Piru pinyon, is a rare and endemic species native to the southwestern United States.

It is a small evergreen tree that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, cone-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are a traditional food source for local wildlife and indigenous communities. Its leaves are needle-like and evergreen, providing year-round foliage in its arid habitat.
