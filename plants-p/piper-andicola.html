Piper andicola

Piper andicola, commonly known as the Andean pepper or Andean pipevine, is a climbing shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Piper andicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
