Pedilanthus deamii

Pedilanthus deamii, commonly known as the golden torch cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Cactaceae family, characterized by its unique, spiny stems and showy yellow flowers. The plant thrives in hot, dry climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh environments.
