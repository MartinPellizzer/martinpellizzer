Pisum sativum convar. vulgare

Pisum sativum convar. vulgare, commonly known as the common pea, is a cultivated variety of the pea plant.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy. The plant produces green, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are the edible part of the plant. It is widely grown for its nutritional value and is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae.

This variety is well-adapted to temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
