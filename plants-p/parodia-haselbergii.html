Parodia haselbergii

Parodia haselbergii, commonly known as the "Cactus of the Moon," is a species of flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal flowering habit, it is a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
