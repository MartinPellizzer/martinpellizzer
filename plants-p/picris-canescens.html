Picris canescens

Picris canescens, commonly known as hoary prickly lettuce, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers that resemble dandelions. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in fields. Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, contributing to its widespread distribution.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties, with historical uses in traditional remedies.
