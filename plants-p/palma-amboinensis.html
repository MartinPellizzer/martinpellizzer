Palma amboinensis

Palma amboinensis, commonly known as the Amboina palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible fruits that are often consumed by both humans and animals.

Due to its ornamental value and limited natural range, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
