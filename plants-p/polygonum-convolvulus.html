Polygonum convolvulus

Polygonum convolvulus, commonly known as bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is a creeping annual or perennial herb characterized by its prostrate or climbing growth habit. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a reddish or purple tinge on the underside.

Polygonum convolvulus is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
