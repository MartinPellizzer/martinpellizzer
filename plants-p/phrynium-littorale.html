Phrynium littorale

Phrynium littorale, commonly known as the coastal arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils near coastal areas and can thrive in saline environments. Its inflorescence consists of clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are borne on long, slender stalks.

Phrynium littorale is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
