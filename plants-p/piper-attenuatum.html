Piper attenuatum

Piper attenuatum, commonly known as the slender pepper plant, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus in the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its thin, twining stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces tiny, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into small, elongated berries.

The berries are typically harvested when ripe and used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
