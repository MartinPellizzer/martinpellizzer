Pyrus bitchuensis

Pyrus bitchuensis, commonly known as the Bitchu pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its small, deciduous tree form. The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits. Its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Pyrus bitchuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional Japanese horticulture.
