Primula matthioli subsp. mongolica

Primula matthioli subsp.

mongolica, commonly known as Mongolian primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Mongolia and adjacent areas of China. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, often at elevations above 2,000 meters.

The plant features a short, hairy stem that bears several whorled leaves, which are lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are showy, with five petals that are usually pink or white, and they bloom in early spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
