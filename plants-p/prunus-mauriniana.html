Prunus mauriniana

Prunus mauriniana, commonly known as the Mexican cherry or Mexican plum, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey-barked trunk and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a dark purple color. Prunus mauriniana is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable plant in native landscapes.
