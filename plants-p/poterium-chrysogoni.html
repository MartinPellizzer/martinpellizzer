Poterium chrysogoni

Poterium chrysogoni, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Poterium chrysogoni is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
