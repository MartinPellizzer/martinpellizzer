Podocarpus pilgeri subsp. wangii

Podocarpus pilgeri subsp.

wangii, commonly known as the Chinese podocarp, is a member of the Podocarpaceae family and is native to southern China and Vietnam. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, scale-like leaves.

The plant produces cones that contain edible, fleshy seeds, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, subtropical climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
