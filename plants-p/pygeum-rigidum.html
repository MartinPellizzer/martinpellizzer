Pygeum rigidum

Pygeum rigidum, commonly known as the rigid African wild almond, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, hard seeds enclosed in a woody capsule, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
