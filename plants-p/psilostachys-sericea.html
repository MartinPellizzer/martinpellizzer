Psilostachys sericea

Psilostachys sericea, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery-blue foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters of small, pale flowers.

Psilostachys sericea is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
