Phyteuma foliosum

Phyteuma foliosum, commonly known as the leafy phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Phyteuma foliosum is often cultivated for its attractive blooms and its ability to thrive in partial shade.
