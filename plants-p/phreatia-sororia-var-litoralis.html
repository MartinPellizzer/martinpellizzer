Phreatia sororia var. litoralis

Phreatia sororia var. litoralis, commonly known as the coastal phreatia, is a variant of the phreatia sororia species native to coastal regions.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its rhizomatous growth and hairy, strap-like leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments near the shoreline. Its flowers are small and white, often clustered in inflorescences, and bloom during the wet season.

This variant is adapted to saline and sandy soils, making it a unique member of the orchid family in coastal ecosystems.
