Potentilla tauricola

Potentilla tauricola, commonly known as the Tauric cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the Tauric Chersonese region of southern Ukraine.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as steppes and rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive five-lobed shape, giving it the common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and vibrant blooms.
