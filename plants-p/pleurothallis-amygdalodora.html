Pleurothallis amygdalodora

Pleurothallis amygdalodora, commonly known as the almond-scented orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on tree branches, drawing moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers with a subtle almond scent. Its blooming period usually occurs during the wet season, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its unique fragrance and ornamental value, it is a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
