Phalaris arundinacea var. glauca

Phalaris arundinacea var. glauca, commonly known as blue joint grass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, jointed stems that often have a bluish tint, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, greenish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
