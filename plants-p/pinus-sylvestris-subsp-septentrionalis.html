Pinus sylvestris subsp. septentrionalis

Pinus sylvestris subsp.

septentrionalis, commonly known as the Scots pine, is a subspecies native to northern Europe and parts of Asia. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its long, needle-like leaves and distinctive cone structures.

The tree typically grows in a variety of habitats, including forests, open moorlands, and mountainous regions. It has a slow growth rate and can live for several centuries, contributing to its ecological significance.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.
