Pleopeltis craspedosora

Pleopeltis craspedosora, commonly known as the crested rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, crested fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
