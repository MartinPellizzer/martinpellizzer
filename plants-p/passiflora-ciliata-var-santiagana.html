Passiflora ciliata var. santiagana

Passiflora ciliata var. santiagana, commonly known as the Santiago passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a blend of blue, violet, and white petals, along with prominent stamens and a crown of filaments. The leaves are usually heart-shaped with ciliate margins, giving it its scientific name. It produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

This species thrives in humid, tropical environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
