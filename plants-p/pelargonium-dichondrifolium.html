Pelargonium dichondrifolium

Pelargonium dichondrifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bright pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial herb that produces small, rounded seed capsules upon flowering.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
