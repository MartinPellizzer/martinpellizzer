Plantago depressa subsp. turczaninowii

Plantago depressa subsp.

turczaninowii, commonly known as the Turkish plantain, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats.

The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are pollinated by wind.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
