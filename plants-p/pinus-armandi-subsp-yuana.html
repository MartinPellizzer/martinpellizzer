Pinus armandi subsp. yuana

Pinus armandi subsp.

yuana, commonly known as the Yunnan pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to southwestern China. It is characterized by its long, slender needles that are typically bundled in pairs, and its cones are slender and often pendulous.

The tree can grow to heights of up to 30 meters, with a broad, conical crown. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to well-drained, acidic soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
