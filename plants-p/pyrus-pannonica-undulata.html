Pyrus pannonica undulata

Pyrus pannonica undulata, commonly known as the Hungarian whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to the Carpathian Basin.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often wavy or undulating, giving it its specific epithet "undulata.". The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries in the summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.
