Plagidia rufa

Plagidia rufa, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink or red flowers with contrasting green veins, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Plagidia rufa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
