Plantago glareosa

Plantago glareosa, commonly known as silver plantain, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that are covered in fine, white trichomes. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems that bear dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The species is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
