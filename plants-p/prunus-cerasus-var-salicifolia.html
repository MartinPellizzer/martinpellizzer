Prunus cerasus var. salicifolia

Prunus cerasus var. salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf cherry, is a cultivar of the cherry tree characterized by its distinctive willow-like leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters. The leaves are narrow, serrated, and have a soft, silvery underside, giving it a unique appearance. It produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible fruits that are similar to regular cherries.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
