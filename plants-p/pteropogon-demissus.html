Pteropogon demissus

Pteropogon demissus, commonly known as the slender tailed orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features a slender, upright stem with few leaves and produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinct, spurred lip. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
