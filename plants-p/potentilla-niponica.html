Potentilla niponica

Potentilla niponica, commonly known as Japanese cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for various garden conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and horticultural settings.
