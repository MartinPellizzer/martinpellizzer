Panax ginseng var. japonicus

Panax ginseng var. japonicus, commonly known as Japanese ginseng, is a variant of the ginseng plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, and often hairy root system, which is highly valued in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in forested areas with cool, moist climates, and it thrives in well-drained, loamy soil. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are small and greenish-white in color.

The plant's roots contain various bioactive compounds, including ginsenosides, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.
