Pulsatilla myrrhidifolia

Pulsatilla myrrhidifolia, commonly known as the myrrh-leaved pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are typically palmately divided, and it produces follicular fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows at high elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
