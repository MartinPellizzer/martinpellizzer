Plukenetia stipellata

Plukenetia stipellata, commonly known as the African butter fruit, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It produces small, yellow, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a mild, buttery flavor. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its fruit and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
