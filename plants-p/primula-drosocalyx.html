Primula drosocalyx

Primula drosocalyx, commonly known as the snowbell or silver bell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a distinctive yellow center. The plant features a basal rosette of broad, dark green leaves and produces clusters of flowers on slender stalks. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its unique appearance and hardiness.
