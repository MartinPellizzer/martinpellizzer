Pandanus dorystigma

Pandanus dorystigma, commonly known as the dwarf screw pine, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its fibrous, strap-like leaves that are often used in traditional weaving practices. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its roots form a dense network that helps stabilize coastal soils.

Pandanus dorystigma is well adapted to salty, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove areas.
