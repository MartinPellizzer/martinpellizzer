Pseudohexadesmia micrantha

Pseudohexadesmia micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It possesses small, elliptical leaves that are usually less than 1 cm in length, and its flowers are typically white or pale green. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is often found in tropical and subtropical forest ecosystems.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of the ecological interactions within its habitat.
