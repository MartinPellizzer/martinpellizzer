Pseudoparis cauliflora

Pseudoparis cauliflora, commonly known as the mountain snowbell, is a rare and striking flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its unique cauliflorous flowering habit, where flowers emerge directly from the stem rather than the leaves. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, adapted to the cold, high-altitude environment.

Pseudoparis cauliflora is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological significance in alpine ecosystems.
