Pseudacoridium

Pseudacoridium, commonly known as the false acoridium, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Acoraceae.

It is characterized by its aquatic or semi-aquatic habitat, typically found in wetlands and marshy areas. The plants have long, narrow leaves and produce small, greenish flowers that are often clustered in spadices. Pseudacoridium species are generally herbaceous and lack true stems, with the leaves emerging directly from the rhizomes.

These plants play an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
