Paeonia banatica

Paeonia banatica, commonly known as the Banat peony, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Europe and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the芍药科 (Paeoniaceae) family and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring compound leaves with serrated edges. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained, loamy soils.

The flowers are often used in traditional medicine and are also valued for their ornamental beauty in gardens.
