Paspalum falculum

Paspalum falculum, commonly known as the narrowleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, thriving in moist to saturated soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in coastal areas and can tolerate saltwater conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
