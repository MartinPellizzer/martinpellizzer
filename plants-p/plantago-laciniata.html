Plantago laciniata

Plantago laciniata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features upright, branched stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with toothed margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found along roadsides and in meadows.
