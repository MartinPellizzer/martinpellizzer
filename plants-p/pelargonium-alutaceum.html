Pelargonium alutaceum

Pelargonium alutaceum, commonly known as the silver geranium, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white layer of trichomes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves.

Pelargonium alutaceum is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements.
