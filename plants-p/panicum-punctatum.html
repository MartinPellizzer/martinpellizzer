Panicum punctatum

Panicum punctatum, commonly known as the spotted panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive silver-gray foliage with prominent purplish or reddish veins. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking coloration and drought tolerance.
