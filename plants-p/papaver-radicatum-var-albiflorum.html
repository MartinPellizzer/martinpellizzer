Papaver radicatum var. albiflorum

Papaver radicatum var. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered poppy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking white to pale yellow flowers with a prominent central stigma, blooming in late summer. The plant has a robust taproot system and hairy, lobed leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are borne on tall, slender stems and are pollinated primarily by insects.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
