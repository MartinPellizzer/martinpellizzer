Potosia

Potosia, commonly known as the "Mexican bellflower," is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to Mexico and Central America, where it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plants are characterized by their showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. Potosia species often have hairy leaves and stems, and they produce small, berry-like fruits.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their unique floral display.
