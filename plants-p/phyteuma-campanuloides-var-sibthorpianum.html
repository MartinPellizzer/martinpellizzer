Phyteuma campanuloides var. sibthorpianum

Phyteuma campanuloides var. sibthorpianum, commonly known as the Sibthorpian bellflower, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It prefers well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long bloom period and ability to attract pollinators.
