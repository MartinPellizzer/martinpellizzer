Paspalum filifolium

Paspalum filifolium, commonly known as silky paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silky-textured foliage and dense, mat-like growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wetlands, and is often found in coastal areas. Its small, spike-like inflorescences produce numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Paspalum filifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland restoration projects.
