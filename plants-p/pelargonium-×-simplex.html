Pelargonium × simplex

Pelargonium × simplex, commonly known as the angel wing begonia, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, heart-shaped leaves that often display striking variegation, typically in shades of white, green, and pink. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Native to South America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a decorative plant in shaded garden areas.
