Piper trigonocarpum

Piper trigonocarpum, commonly known as the three-lobed pepper or wild pepper, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with three leaflets. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and medicinal studies.
