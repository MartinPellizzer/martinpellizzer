Pycnandra petiolata

Pycnandra petiolata, commonly known as the white sago palm, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
