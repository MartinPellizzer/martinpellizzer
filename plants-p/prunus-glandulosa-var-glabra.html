Prunus glandulosa var. glabra

Prunus glandulosa var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth glandular cherry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and small, oval leaves with a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are dark red to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
