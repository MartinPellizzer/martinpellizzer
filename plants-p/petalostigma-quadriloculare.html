Petalostigma quadriloculare

Petalostigma quadriloculare, commonly known as the four-chambered petal, is a rare and intriguing member of the orchid family.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring four distinct chambers within the ovary. The plant exhibits a complex arrangement of petals and sepals, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. Its botanical significance lies in its specialized morphology, which may have evolved for specific pollination strategies.

Due to its rarity, it is often studied for its ecological and evolutionary adaptations.
