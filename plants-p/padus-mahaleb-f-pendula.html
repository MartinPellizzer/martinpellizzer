Padus mahaleb f. pendula

Padus mahaleb f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping mahaleb cherry, is a decorative shrub or small tree characterized by its pendulous branches that create a cascading effect.

It is a cultivar of the species Padus mahaleb, which is native to southern Europe and western Asia. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by red, cherry-like fruits. Its foliage is dark green and provides dense shade, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, and it is relatively low maintenance once established.
