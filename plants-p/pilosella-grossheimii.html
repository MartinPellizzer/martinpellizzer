Pilosella grossheimii

Pilosella grossheimii, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a small flowering plant native to the alpine regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble single blossoms. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its foliage consists of dense, woolly leaves that help retain moisture in harsh environments.

Pilosella grossheimii produces small, yellowish flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer.
