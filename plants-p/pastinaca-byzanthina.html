Pastinaca byzanthina

Pastinaca byzanthina, commonly known as the Byzantine burdock, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and purple-tinged flower heads. The plant produces small, spiky fruits that are adapted for animal dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Its historical use in traditional medicine is noted for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
