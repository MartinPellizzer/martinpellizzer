Poarium verticillatum

Poarium verticillatum, commonly known as the spiral cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of branches, which gives it a distinctive and often spiraling growth form. The plant features thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. Its small, spiny leaves are adapted for reducing water loss and protecting against herbivores.

Blooming occurs in the spring, producing clusters of yellow or orange flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
