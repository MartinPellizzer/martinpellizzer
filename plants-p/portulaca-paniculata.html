Portulaca paniculata

Portulaca paniculata, commonly known as the clustered portulaca, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a low-growing annual herb with fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils make it a hardy and resilient plant.
