Pelargonium minimum

Pelargonium minimum, commonly known as the dwarf geranium, is a compact, trailing plant native to South Africa.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically grayish-green with a velvety texture. The flowers are usually pink or red, borne in clusters at the ends of stems, and bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it ideal for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, decorative plant.
