Potamogeton fryeri

Potamogeton fryeri, commonly known as the Fryer's pondweed, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the family Potamogetonaceae.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems that often grow submerged in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. The plant produces small, green to reddish-brown flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters near the water's surface. Its leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, often with a slightly rough texture.

Potamogeton fryeri plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
