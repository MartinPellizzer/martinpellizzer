Paspalum frumentaceum

Paspalum frumentaceum, commonly known as little barley or wild barley, is a grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Paspalum frumentaceum is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid conditions.
