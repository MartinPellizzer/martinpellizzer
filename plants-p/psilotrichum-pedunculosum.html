Psilotrichum pedunculosum

Psilotrichum pedunculosum, commonly known as the slender whisk fern, is a member of the family Psilotaceae and is characterized by its simple, branching structure without true leaves.

It possesses a rootstock with numerous root-like projections called rhizoids, which anchor it to the substrate. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in dense, whip-like clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its primitive morphology reflects its ancient evolutionary lineage, making it a valuable subject for botanical study.
