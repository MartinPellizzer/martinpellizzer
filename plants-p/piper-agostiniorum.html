Piper agostiniorum

Piper agostiniorum, commonly known as the Peruvian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, a small, fleshy berry, contains several seeds and is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
