Pinus grandis var. planchonii

Pinus grandis var. planchonii, commonly known as the Planchon pine, is a rare and distinctive variant of the grand fir.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and long, needle-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces cones that are often larger and more elongated compared to other pine species. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in mountainous regions with cool, moist climates.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of particular interest to botanists and conservationists.
