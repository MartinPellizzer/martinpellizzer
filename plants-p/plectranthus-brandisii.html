Plectranthus brandisii

Plectranthus brandisii, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It typically grows as a shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often aromatic when crushed.

Plectranthus brandisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
