Pavonia speciosa subsp. stenochlamys

Pavonia speciosa subsp.

stenochlamys, commonly known as the narrow-cupped wood rose, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States. It features showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
