Populus simonii var. dunhuangensis

Populus simonii var. dunhuangensis, commonly known as the Dunhuang poplar, is a rare and resilient tree species native to the arid regions of China.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a distinctive, often twisted trunk that adapts to harsh desert conditions. This variety is well-suited to sandy soils and can tolerate extreme temperature fluctuations. Its growth is slow, and it plays a vital role in stabilizing dunes and preventing desertification.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in the region.
