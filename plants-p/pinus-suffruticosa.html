Pinus suffruticosa

Pinus suffruticosa, commonly known as the dwarf Mexican pine, is a small evergreen conifer native to Mexico.

It typically grows to a height of about 2 to 5 meters, featuring a compact, shrub-like form with dense foliage. The plant has needle-like leaves that are short and often bluish-green in color, and it produces small, yellowish cones. It is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
