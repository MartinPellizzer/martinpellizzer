Pternandra cordifolia

Pternandra cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf pternandra, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often heart-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes grown as a garden plant.
