Pavonia leptoclada

Pavonia leptoclada, commonly known as the slender pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, showy pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Pavonia leptoclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
