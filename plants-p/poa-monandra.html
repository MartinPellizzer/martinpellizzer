Poa monandra

Poa monandra, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a species of grass native to New Zealand.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and riverbanks. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, two-flowered spikelets.

Poa monandra plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
