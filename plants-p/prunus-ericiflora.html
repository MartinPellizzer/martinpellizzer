Prunus ericiflora

Prunus ericiflora, commonly known as the prickly pear cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits known as pears, which are edible and often consumed by both humans and wildlife. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating remarkable adaptability to drought conditions.

The plant's structure and growth patterns make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
