Pombalia bicolor

Pombalia bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bi-colored flowers, which typically feature a combination of white and purple or pink hues. The plant belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its delicate, intricate blooms that often resemble miniature butterflies. Pombalia bicolor thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is a prized species among orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
