Piper artanthe var. strigosum

Piper artanthe var. strigosum, commonly known as the hairy pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper artanthe species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in yellow or green. The plant produces small, peppery berries that are often used in local traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticultural and ethnobotanical studies.
