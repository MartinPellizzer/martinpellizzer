Philadelphus zelleri

Philadelphus zelleri, commonly known as the Zeller's mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are dark green on top and grayish-white underneath. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its pleasant, citrus-like fragrance and its ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
