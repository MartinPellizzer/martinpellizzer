Paeonia peregrina var. cretica

Paeonia peregrina var. cretica, commonly known as the Cretan peony, is a rare and ancient variety of peony native to the island of Crete.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink, with a subtle fragrance. The plant is a perennial herb with thick, woody roots and broad, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or limestone-rich environments.

Due to its rarity and historical significance, it is considered a valuable species in botanical conservation efforts.
