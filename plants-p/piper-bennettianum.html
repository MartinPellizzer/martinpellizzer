Piper bennettianum

Piper bennettianum, commonly known as the Bennett's pepper or the West Indian pepper, is a flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that contains the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
