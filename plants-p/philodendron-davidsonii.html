Philodendron davidsonii

Philodendron davidsonii, commonly known as the Davidson's philodendron, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with a striking variegation of green and white. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its foliage is known for its vibrant coloration and unique patterns, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Philodendron davidsonii prefers bright, indirect light and moderate humidity to thrive.
