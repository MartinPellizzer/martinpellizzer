Phyllopentas hirtiflora

Phyllopentas hirtiflora, commonly known as the hairy-flowered phyllopentas, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
