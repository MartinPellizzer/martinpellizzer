Puccinellia acroxantha

Puccinellia acroxantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf saltbush, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. The plant thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it an important component of coastal and desert ecosystems. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments.

Puccinellia acroxantha plays a vital role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitats.
