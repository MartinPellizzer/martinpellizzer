Pleiosepalum gombalanum

Pleiosepalum gombalanum, commonly known as the Gombala orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and leathery, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

The flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by insects.
