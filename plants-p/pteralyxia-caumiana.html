Pteralyxia caumiana

Pteralyxia caumiana, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.
