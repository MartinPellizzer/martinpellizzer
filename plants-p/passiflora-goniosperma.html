Passiflora goniosperma

Passiflora goniosperma, commonly known as the giant granadilla, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a distinctive crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing a sweet, fleshy pulp. Its leaves are usually alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Passiflora goniosperma is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
