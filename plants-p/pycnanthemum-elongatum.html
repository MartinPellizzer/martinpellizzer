Pycnanthemum elongatum

Pycnanthemum elongatum, commonly known as longleaf mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodlands and along streambanks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a strong minty fragrance. Its flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in whorls, producing a distinctive, aromatic bloom.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
