Pouteria peruviensis

Pouteria peruviensis, commonly known as the Peruvian starfruit, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon basin.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces a fruit with a thick, spiky rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in secondary forests.

Due to its unique appearance and flavor, it is of interest for both ornamental and potential culinary uses.
