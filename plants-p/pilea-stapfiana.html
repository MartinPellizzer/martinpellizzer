Pilea stapfiana

Pilea stapfiana, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen perennial shrub native to China.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, dark green leaves that are heart-shaped and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in the spring, followed by pale green berries. It thrives in well-draining soil and prefers indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique appearance and ease of care contribute to its popularity among plant enthusiasts.
