Prunus grisea var. microphylla

Prunus grisea var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf grey cherry, is a compact shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically a bluish-green color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by edible, dark red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, decorative plant.
