Pleurothallis gehrtii

Pleurothallis gehrtii, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically feature white petals with green veins. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are usually fragrant and bloom during the wet season, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.

Pleurothallis gehrtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
