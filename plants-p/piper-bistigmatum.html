Piper bistigmatum

Piper bistigmatum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored berries that are used to produce the spice black pepper. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces small, green flowers that develop into the familiar peppercorns. Its botanical classification places it in the family Piperaceae, and it is closely related to other pepper species.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
