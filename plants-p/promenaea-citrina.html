Promenaea citrina

Promenaea citrina, commonly known as the yellow bell orchid, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small bells. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Promenaea citrina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm climates.
