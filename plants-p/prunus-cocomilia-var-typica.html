Prunus cocomilia var. typica

Prunus cocomilia var. typica, commonly known as the Hawaiian cherry or *Euphorbia cocomilia*, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and dry forests, characterized by its smooth, gray-green bark and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
