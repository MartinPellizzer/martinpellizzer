Panicum sphaerocarpon var. polyanthes

Panicum sphaerocarpon var. polyanthes, commonly known as the purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its distinctive purple-tinged inflorescence, which consists of compact, spike-like clusters. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its ability to colonize disturbed soils. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in grassland ecosystems.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some regions as a decorative plant.
