Paepalanthus serpens

Paepalanthus serpens, commonly known as the serpent's tail cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, serpentine-like stems that coil and twist as it grows. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Due to its striking appearance and adaptations to harsh climates, it is a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.
