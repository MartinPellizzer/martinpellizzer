Pouteria boliviana

Pouteria boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian sapote, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces a yellow, edible fruit with a creamy, sweet pulp that is often used in culinary applications. Its wood is valued for its strength and durability, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
