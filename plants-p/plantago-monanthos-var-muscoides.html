Plantago monanthos var. muscoides

Plantago monanthos var. muscoides, commonly known as the musk plantain, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a single, tall flowering stem with small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
