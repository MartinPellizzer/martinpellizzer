Paeonia anomala var. angustifolia

Paeonia anomala var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, arranged alternately along the stem. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
