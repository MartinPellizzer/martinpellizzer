Poa krylovii

Poa krylovii, commonly known as Krylov's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and forms dense, low-growing sods. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates, making it a hardy species. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in panicles, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Poa krylovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its durability and aesthetic qualities.
