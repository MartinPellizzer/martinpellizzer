Pinus × murraybanksiana

Pinus × murraybanksiana, commonly known as the Murraybanks pine, is a hybrid conifer resulting from the cross between Pinus strobiformis and Pinus muricata.

It is characterized by its distinctive, scale-like leaves that are often grayish-blue in color and arranged in dense clusters. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain edible seeds, which are a food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions with a temperate climate.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
