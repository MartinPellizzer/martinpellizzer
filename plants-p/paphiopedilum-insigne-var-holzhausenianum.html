Paphiopedilum insigne var. holzhausenianum

Paphiopedilum insigne var. holzhausenianum, commonly known as the Holzhausen's Vanda orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Philippines.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb and long, arching leaves. The plant produces showy, resupinate flowers with a distinctive lip that resembles a bee, aiding in pollination. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to orange with dark markings, and it blooms in spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
