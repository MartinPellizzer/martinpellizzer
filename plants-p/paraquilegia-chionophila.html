Paraquilegia chionophila

Paraquilegia chionophila, commonly known as the white-flowered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
