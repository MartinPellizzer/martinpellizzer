Protea concava

Protea concava, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sugarbush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family, which is known for its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forested areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with a distinctive, urn-shaped structure.

Protea concava is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
