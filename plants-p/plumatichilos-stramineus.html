Plumatichilos stramineus

Plumatichilos stramineus, commonly known as the golden feather orchid, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats. Its unique morphology and vibrant color make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
