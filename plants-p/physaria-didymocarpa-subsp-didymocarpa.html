Physaria didymocarpa subsp. didymocarpa

Physaria didymocarpa subsp.

didymocarpa, commonly known as the twin-seed bladder. It is a flowering plant native to the western United States, characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers.

The plant produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release its small, round seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert and semi-desert environments.

The species is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants.
