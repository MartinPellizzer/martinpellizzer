Passiflora bauhiniifolia

Passiflora bauhiniifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a unique blend of sepals, petals, and modified leaves. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

Passiflora bauhiniifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
