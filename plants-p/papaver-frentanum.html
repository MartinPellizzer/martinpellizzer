Papaver frentanum

Papaver frentanum, commonly known as the Frentanum poppy, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and smooth, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm and blooms from late spring to early summer. Its flowers have a delicate, almost ethereal appearance, with five petals that curve inward.

The seeds of Papaver frentanum are small and oval, often used in traditional herbal remedies.
