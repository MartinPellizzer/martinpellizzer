Persea macropoda var. brasiliensis

Persea macropoda var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian avocado or Brazilian persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon basin.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lanceolate leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. The fruit has a creamy, nutty flavor and is rich in healthy fats, making it a valuable food source.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
