Piptatherum shoshoneanum

Piptatherum shoshoneanum, commonly known as the Shoshone needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a common species in sagebrush steppe ecosystems. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, which help prevent soil erosion.

Piptatherum shoshoneanum plays an important role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat, providing forage for wildlife and livestock.
