Polygala kilimandscharica

Polygala kilimandscharica, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are often pale blue or white, arranged in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss in its dry habitat.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native range.
