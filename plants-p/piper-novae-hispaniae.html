Piper novae-hispaniae

Piper novae-hispaniae, commonly known as the New Spain pepper or the Spanish pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the genus *Piper* of the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Central America and is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces tiny, dark red berries that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
