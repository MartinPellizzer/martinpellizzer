Phlox drummondii var. glabriflora

Phlox drummondii var. glabriflora, commonly known as the glabrous-flowered garden phlox, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy clusters of pink to red flowers in the summer months. The variety is characterized by its smooth, hairless flower petals, distinguishing it from other phlox species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
