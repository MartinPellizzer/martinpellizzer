Perymenium grande var. strigillosa

Perymenium grande var. strigillosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its fibrous roots make it a common weed in agricultural fields and disturbed soils.

The species is characterized by its rapid growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
