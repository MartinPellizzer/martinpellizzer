Pyrus intermedia var. latifolia

Pyrus intermedia var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf pear, is a variant of the pear tree characterized by its broad, lobed leaves.

It is native to China and is known for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically used in jams and preserves. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
