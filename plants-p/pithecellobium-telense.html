Pithecellobium telense

Pithecellobium telense, commonly known as the golden pod tree, is a species of leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow pods that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing a sweet, aromatic fragrance.

Pithecellobium telense is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential use in traditional medicine.
