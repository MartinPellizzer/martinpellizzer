Pelargonium velutinum

Pelargonium velutinum, commonly known as the velvet geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The flowers can range in color from pink to red, depending on the specific cultivar.
