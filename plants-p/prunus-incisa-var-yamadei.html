Prunus incisa var. yamadei

Prunus incisa var. yamadei, commonly known as Yamadei cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, single-layered flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of pink and white. The leaves are ovate with a serrated margin and turn a striking red or orange in autumn. The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in traditional Japanese gardens.
