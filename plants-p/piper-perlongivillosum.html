Piper perlongivillosum

Piper perlongivillosum, commonly known as the long-hair pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of South America, particularly in regions of Brazil, Peru, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its long, slender stems and dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a glossy surface and a distinct, aromatic scent.

The fruit, which is a small, three-lobed berry, contains the seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
