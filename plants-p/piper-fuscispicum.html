Piper fuscispicum

Piper fuscispicum, commonly known as the dark-spined pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by tiny, peppery fruits.

Due to its unique appearance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
