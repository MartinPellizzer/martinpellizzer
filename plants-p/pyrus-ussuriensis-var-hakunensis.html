Pyrus ussuriensis var. hakunensis

Pyrus ussuriensis var. hakunensis, commonly known as the Hakunen apple, is a variant of the Ussuri pear native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and aromatic fruits that are typically yellow or golden in color. The plant is a deciduous tree that thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and lobed, providing a distinctive appearance during the growing season.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its edible fruit.
