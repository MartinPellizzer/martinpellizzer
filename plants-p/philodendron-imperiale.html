Philodendron imperiale

Philodendron imperiale, commonly known as the imperial philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length. The plant has a climbing habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by yellowish berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture for its striking appearance.
