Piper alstonii

Piper alstonii, commonly known as the Alston's pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a glossy appearance with a strong, pungent aroma.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, attracting various wildlife species.
