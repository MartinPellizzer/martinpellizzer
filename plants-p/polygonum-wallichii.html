Polygonum wallichii

Polygonum wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its root system is fibrous and can spread horizontally, allowing it to colonize new areas effectively.

Polygonum wallichii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
