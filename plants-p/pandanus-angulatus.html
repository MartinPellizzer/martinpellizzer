Pandanus angulatus

Pandanus angulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible fruits, known as pandan berries. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found near coastal areas.
