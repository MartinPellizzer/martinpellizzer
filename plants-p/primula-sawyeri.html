Primula sawyeri

Primula sawyeri, commonly known as Sawyer's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant features hairy, oval-shaped leaves and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
