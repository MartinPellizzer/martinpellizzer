Plantago rhodosperma var. echioides

Plantago rhodosperma var. echioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely packed along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are clustered in dense, rounded clusters. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments, with deep root systems that help it retain moisture.

This species is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in disturbed areas.
