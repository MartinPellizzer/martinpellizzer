Potentilla maura f. sericea

Potentilla maura f. sericea, commonly known as the silver potentilla, is a cultivar of the shrub Potentilla maura.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in dense, fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, almost metallic appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
