Potentilla canescens var. bohemica

Potentilla canescens var. bohemica, commonly known as the Bohemian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows. The plant features narrow, silver-green leaves with a distinctive whitish underside, and it produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers in the summer. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture for its hardy nature.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient plant in various environments.
