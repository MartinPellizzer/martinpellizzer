Piaranthus geminatus subsp. geminatus

Piaranthus geminatus subsp.

geminatus, commonly known as the twin-flowered pincushion, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense clusters of small, fleshy leaves that resemble a pincushion.

The plant produces tiny, white flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among succulent enthusiasts.
