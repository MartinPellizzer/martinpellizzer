Piper subrigidilimbum

Piper subrigidilimbum, commonly known as the rigid-leaved pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stems. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
