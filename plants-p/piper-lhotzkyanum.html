Piper lhotzkyanum

Piper lhotzkyanum, commonly known as the Malayan black pepper, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are often used in local cuisines for their aromatic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires a support structure for its vine growth.
