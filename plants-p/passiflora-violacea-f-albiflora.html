Passiflora violacea f. albiflora

Passiflora violacea f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered passionflower, is a unique variant of the passionflower genus characterized by its striking white flowers.

This plant is native to tropical regions of South America and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its flowers feature a distinctive structure with five sepals, five petals, and a crown-like arrangement of filaments around the reproductive organs. The leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
