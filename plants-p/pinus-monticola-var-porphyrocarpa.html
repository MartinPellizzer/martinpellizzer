Pinus monticola var. porphyrocarpa

Pinus monticola var. porphyrocarpa, commonly known as the red bark pine, is a variant of the sugar pine native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its reddish-barked trunk and long, slender needles that are typically 5 to 8 inches in length. The cones of this species are large and woody, often reaching lengths of up to 12 inches, and they mature over several years. It thrives in high-elevation environments with well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
