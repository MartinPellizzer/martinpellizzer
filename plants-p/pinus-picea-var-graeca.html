Pinus picea var. graeca

Pinus picea var. graeca, commonly known as the Greek fir, is a variant of the white fir native to the mountainous regions of Greece and Turkey.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and needle-like leaves that are blue-green in color. The cones are typically slender and elongated, with a distinctive reddish-brown hue. This evergreen tree thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found at high altitudes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
