Polygala herbiola var. chapadensis

Polygala herbiola var. chapadensis, commonly known as the Brazilian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds with silky hairs.

This species is valued for its ecological role, particularly in supporting native insect populations.
