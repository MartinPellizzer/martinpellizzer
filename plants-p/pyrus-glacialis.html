Pyrus glacialis

Pyrus glacialis, commonly known as the alpine hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly the Tien Shan and Pamir Mountains. This shrub typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or alpine environments. It features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible berries.

The plant is adapted to cold climates and has a slow growth rate, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.
