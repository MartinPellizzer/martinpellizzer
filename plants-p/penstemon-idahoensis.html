Penstemon idahoensis

Penstemon idahoensis, commonly known as Idaho penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
