Primula pirolifolia

Primula pirolifolia, commonly known as the Alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, white, and pink, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Primula pirolifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
