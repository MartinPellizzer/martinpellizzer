Phaseolus ellipticus var. aureolus

Phaseolus ellipticus var. aureolus, commonly known as the golden runner bean, is a tropical legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, elliptical pods that are typically yellow or golden in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers and has compound leaves with three leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible beans.

The variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in traditional cuisines.
