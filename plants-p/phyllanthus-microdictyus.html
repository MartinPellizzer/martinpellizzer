Phyllanthus microdictyus

Phyllanthus microdictyus, commonly known as the small-leaved spurge, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that are attractive to birds. It is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
