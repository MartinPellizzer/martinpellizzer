Phaseolus anguinus

Phaseolus anguinus, commonly known as the wild kidney bean, is a species native to the Americas.

It is a climbing vine that produces small, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its botanical features include hairy stems and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

It is related to other Phaseolus species, such as the common bean, and has been studied for its potential agricultural and nutritional value.
