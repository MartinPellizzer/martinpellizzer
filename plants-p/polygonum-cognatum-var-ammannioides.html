Polygonum cognatum var. ammannioides

Polygonum cognatum var. ammannioides, commonly known as the Ammann's buckwheat, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the plant is noted for its ornamental value in garden settings.
