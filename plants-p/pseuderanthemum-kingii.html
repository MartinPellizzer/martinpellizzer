Pseuderanthemum kingii

Pseuderanthemum kingii, commonly known as the King's pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers, which are usually pink or purple, are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Pseuderanthemum kingii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
