Phreatia montana

Phreatia montana, commonly known as the mountain phreatia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pseudobulbs and long, strap-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces striking white or pale yellow flowers with a distinct lip, which are often fragrant. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in montane forests.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
