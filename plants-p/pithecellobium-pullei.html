Pithecellobium pullei

Pithecellobium pullei, commonly known as the golden pod tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets arranged in a feather-like pattern.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible pods.
