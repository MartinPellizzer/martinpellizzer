Penstemon gairdneri subsp. hians

Penstemon gairdneri subsp.

hians, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to violet.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in alpine or subalpine environments. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
