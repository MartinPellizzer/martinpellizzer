Paspalum plumosum

Paspalum plumosum, commonly known as the silky paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, arching stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along waterways.

Paspalum plumosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
