Pyrus ugoensis

Pyrus ugoensis, commonly known as the Ugandan pear, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The tree produces edible pears that are typically yellow or green in color, with a sweet and aromatic flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in some parts of its native habitat.
