Poterium sanguisorba subsp. dictyocarpum

Poterium sanguisorba subsp.

dictyocarpum, commonly known as the redroot sandwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a reddish hue on the underside, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
