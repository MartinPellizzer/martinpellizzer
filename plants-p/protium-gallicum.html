Protium gallicum

Protium gallicum, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to western and central Africa.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and traditional uses, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
