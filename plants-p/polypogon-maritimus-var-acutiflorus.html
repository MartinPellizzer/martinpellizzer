Polypogon maritimus var. acutiflorus

Polypogon maritimus var. acutiflorus, commonly known as the sharpflower saltgrass, is a perennial grass native to coastal regions of the United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny flowers. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dunes and salt marshes. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize coastal soils and prevent erosion.

It plays an important role in the ecological balance of coastal ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
