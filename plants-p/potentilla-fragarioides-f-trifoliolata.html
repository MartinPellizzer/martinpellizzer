Potentilla fragarioides f. trifoliolata

Potentilla fragarioides f. trifoliolata, commonly known as the three-leaf potentilla, is a variant of the common potentilla characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves.

This plant is a deciduous shrub native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. Its foliage consists of three leaflets, giving it a unique appearance compared to the typical two-leaf form. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
