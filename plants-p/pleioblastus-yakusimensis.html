Pleioblastus yakusimensis

Pleioblastus yakusimensis, commonly known as the Yakushima bamboo, is a species native to Japan, particularly the island of Yakushima.

It is a clumping bamboo that forms dense thickets, with slender, green shoots that can turn yellow or red in autumn. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically a vibrant green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions with rich, well-drained soil.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in the unique ecosystem of Yakushima.
