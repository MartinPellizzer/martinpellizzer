Pyrostegia reticulata

Pyrostegia reticulata, commonly known as the flame vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive reticulated pattern. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and requires well-drained soil.

Pyrostegia reticulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to cover trellises or walls.
