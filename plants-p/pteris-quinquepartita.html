Pteris quinquepartita

Pteris quinquepartita, commonly known as the five-parted water fern, is a species of fern native to Asia, including regions of China, Japan, and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are divided into five parts, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and can form dense mats, which help in soil stabilization.

The spores are produced in sori located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
