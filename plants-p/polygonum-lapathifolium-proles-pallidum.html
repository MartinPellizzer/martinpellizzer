Polygonum lapathifolium proles pallidum

Polygonum lapathifolium proles pallidum, commonly known as the pale-stem smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features upright, branching stems that are typically pale green to reddish in color, often covered with fine hairs. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, with serrated margins and a prominent midrib. It produces small, clustered flowers in late summer, which are usually pink or red, arranged in spike-like inflorescences.

This plant thrives in moist soils along streambanks and wetlands, and it is often found in disturbed areas.
