Piptatherum ferganense

Piptatherum ferganense, commonly known as the Fergana wild rye, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, arid environments and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, including high temperatures and low precipitation.

Piptatherum ferganense plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat and is of interest for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
