Pinus californiarum subsp. fallax

Pinus californiarum subsp.

fallax, commonly known as the California madrone or madrone, is a subspecies native to the western United States. It is a tall evergreen conifer with distinctive reddish bark that peels in thin, papery layers.

The plant has needle-like leaves that are dark green and often have a waxy coating. It produces cones that are typically red or orange, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This species is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained sites.
