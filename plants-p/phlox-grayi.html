Phlox grayi

Phlox grayi, commonly known as Gray's phlox, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and woodland edges. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to lavender flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Phlox grayi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
