Pentas triangularis

Pentas triangularis, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, five-petaled, bright red or orange flowers that resemble flames. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant color make it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
