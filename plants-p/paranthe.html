Paranthe

Paranthe, commonly known as the "false dandelion," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and the Americas. The plants typically have basal leaves that are lance-shaped or ovate, with a hairy texture. They produce small, yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, which are characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

Paranthe species are often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
