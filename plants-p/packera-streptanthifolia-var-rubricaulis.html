Packera streptanthifolia var. rubricaulis

Packera streptanthifolia var. rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed fleabane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and stream banks, preferring cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture, and its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

The variety rubricaulis is distinguished by its reddish stems, which become more prominent as the plant matures.
