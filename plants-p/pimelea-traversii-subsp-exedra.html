Pimelea traversii subsp. exedra

Pimelea traversii subsp.

exedra, commonly known as the narrowleaf bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that are typically red or orange in color.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
