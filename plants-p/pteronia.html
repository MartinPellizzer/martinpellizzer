Pteronia

Pteronia, commonly known as the feather grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are native to southern Africa and are characterized by their delicate, grass-like foliage and daisy-like flower heads. Pteronia species typically grow in open, sunny habitats and are often found in coastal areas or sandy soils. The flowers are usually small and clustered, forming dense, fluffy inflorescences that are visually striking.

Many species are cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
