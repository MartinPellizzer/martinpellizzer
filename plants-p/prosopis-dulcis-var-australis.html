Prosopis dulcis var. australis

Prosopis dulcis var. australis, commonly known as the Australian mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for reclamation of degraded lands.

The species is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a source of forage for livestock.
