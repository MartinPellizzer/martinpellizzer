Philactis

Philactis, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that resemble ghostly or spectral forms, hence its common name. The plants are typically herbaceous, with opposite leaves and showy bracts that often serve as the primary attraction. Philactis species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

They are valued in horticulture for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
