Plectranthus rugosus

Plectranthus rugosus, commonly known as the rough-stemmed plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rugose stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

Plectranthus rugosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
