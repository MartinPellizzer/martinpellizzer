Prunella

Prunella, commonly known as self-heal, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lamiaceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Prunella vulgaris, which is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. These plants typically have square stems, opposite leaves, and spikes of blue or purple flowers. They are often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

Prunella species are known for their medicinal properties and are sometimes used in herbal remedies.
