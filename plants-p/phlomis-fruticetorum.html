Phlomis fruticetorum

Phlomis fruticetorum, commonly known as rigid wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, woody stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its aromatic foliage and resilience make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.
