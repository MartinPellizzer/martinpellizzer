Purgosea dentata

Purgosea dentata, commonly known as the toothed purgosea, is a flowering plant native to the humid regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated for both aesthetic and therapeutic purposes.
