Passiflora × alatocaerulea

Passiflora × alatocaerulea, commonly known as the blue passionflower, is a hybrid species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue and white flowers, which are adorned with intricate patterns and spurred structures. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, featuring heart-shaped leaves and tendrils for support. Its botanical features include showy, five-petaled corollas and a unique reproductive structure called a gynostemon.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
