Pentanema conyzae

Pentanema conyzae, commonly known as the common ragweed, is an annual flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant is native to North America but has been introduced to other regions, often becoming invasive. It is known for its allergenic pollen, which can cause significant respiratory issues in humans.

The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.
