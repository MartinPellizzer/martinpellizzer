Polygonum camptocladum

Polygonum camptocladum, commonly known as zigzag knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and the United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and flowering structures. The plant is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with stems that often curl and twist, giving it its common name. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer.

Polygonum camptocladum is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
