Ptosimopappus bracteatus

Ptosimopappus bracteatus, commonly known as the desert star or desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring. It is adapted to harsh, dry environments and has a deep root system to access water.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
