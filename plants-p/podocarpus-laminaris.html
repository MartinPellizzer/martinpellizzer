Podocarpus laminaris

Podocarpus laminaris, commonly known as the slender podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its slow growth rate makes it a valuable species for ornamental and ecological purposes.
