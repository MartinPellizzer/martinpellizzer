Pittosporum mouanum

Pittosporum mouanum, commonly known as the Mouanum pittosporum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pittosporaceae.

It is native to New Caledonia, an island in the South Pacific, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.

The flowers are white or pale yellow, clustered in umbels, and bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.
