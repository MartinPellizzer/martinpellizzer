Plectranthus garckeanus

Plectranthus garckeanus, commonly known as the Cape spekboom, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its leaves are typically oval and have a slightly hairy texture, while its small, tubular flowers are usually pale purple or white.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
