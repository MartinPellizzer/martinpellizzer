Presla

Presla, commonly known as the "false dandelion," is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It typically features a rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads, similar to those of the dandelion. Presla is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and roadsides.

It is an annual or biennial plant, depending on the species, and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
