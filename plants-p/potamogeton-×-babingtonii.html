Potamogeton × babingtonii

Potamogeton × babingtonii, commonly known as the hybrid water plant, is a submerged aquatic plant belonging to the family Potamogetonaceae.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Potamogeton crispus and Potamogeton praelatus, characterized by its elongated, ribbon-like leaves. The plant typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. Its reproductive structures include small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, and it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various organisms.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations depending on environmental conditions.
