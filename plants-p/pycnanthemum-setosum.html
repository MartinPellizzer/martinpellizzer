Pycnanthemum setosum

Pycnanthemum setosum, commonly known as hairy mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white to pale blue flowers in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Pycnanthemum setosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
