Prunus padus var. genuina

Prunus padus var. genuina, commonly known as the white-flowered bird cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly serrated margin. The fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms.
