Pleurothallis tenuisepala

Pleurothallis tenuisepala, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and small, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The flowers are typically pale green to white, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that is often adorned with a yellow or orange stripe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high air humidity and well-draining soil to flourish.

This species is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental qualities.
