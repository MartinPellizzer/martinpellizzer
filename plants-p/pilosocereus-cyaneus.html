Pilosocereus cyaneus

Pilosocereus cyaneus, commonly known as the blue moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that are typically green with a bluish tint. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are usually white or pale blue, which bloom at night. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Pilosocereus cyaneus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
