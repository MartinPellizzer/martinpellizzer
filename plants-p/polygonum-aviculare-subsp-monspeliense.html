Polygonum aviculare subsp. monspeliense

Polygonum aviculare subsp.

monspeliense, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers.

The plant thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in waste areas. It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.
