Phagnalon melanoleucum

Phagnalon melanoleucum, commonly known as the dark-leafed phagnalon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, often hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Phagnalon melanoleucum is noted for its resilience to drought and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
