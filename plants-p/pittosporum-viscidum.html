Pittosporum viscidum

Pittosporum viscidum, commonly known as the sticky pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a sticky resin, giving it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Pittosporum viscidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
