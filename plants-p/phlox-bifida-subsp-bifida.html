Phlox bifida subsp. bifida

Phlox bifida subsp.

bifida, commonly known as the narrowleaf phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple, typically from late spring to early summer.

The plant has opposite, linear to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and relatively low-maintenance addition to native plant gardens.
