Panicum proliferum var. longijubatum

Panicum proliferum var. longijubatum, commonly known as the long-headed lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, erect stems with dense, elongated spikelets that give it its characteristic appearance. The plant produces small, feathery flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.
