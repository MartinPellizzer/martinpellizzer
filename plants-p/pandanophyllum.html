Pandanophyllum

Pandanophyllum, commonly known as the sacred bamboo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional ceremonies and rituals. Its leaves are sometimes used in cooking, particularly in the preparation of certain Southeast Asian dishes.

The plant's rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions make it a popular choice for landscaping.
