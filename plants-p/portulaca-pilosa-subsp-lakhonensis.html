Portulaca pilosa subsp. lakhonensis

Portulaca pilosa subsp.

lakhonensis, commonly known as the hairy portulaca, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and vibrant, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments. Its hairy stems and leaves provide a unique texture, while its ability to store water in its tissues allows it to survive in dry conditions.

This subspecies is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
