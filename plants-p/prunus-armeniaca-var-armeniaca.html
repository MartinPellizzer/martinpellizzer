Prunus armeniaca var. armeniaca

Prunus armeniaca var. armeniaca, commonly known as the sweet cherry, is a deciduous tree native to the regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It features alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces showy, white to pink flowers in early spring. The tree bears small, hard pits surrounded by a fleshy, juicy fruit known as the cherry. Its botanical characteristics include a taproot system and a tendency to form dense, rounded canopies.

The plant is cultivated for its edible fruit and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
