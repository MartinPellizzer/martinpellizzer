Portulaca decipiens

Portulaca decipiens, commonly known as the impostor purslane, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Portulacaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in the morning and close by midday, a trait known as nyctinasty.

Portulaca decipiens is often mistaken for other species of purslane due to its similar appearance.
