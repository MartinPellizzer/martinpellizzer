Polygonum senegalense f. albotomentosum

Polygonum senegalense f. albotomentosum, commonly known as the white-hairy knotweed, is a variant of the species Polygonum senegalense, which belongs to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive white to pale yellow pubescence on the stems and leaves, giving it a fluffy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments, often in swampy or marshy areas. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a reddish or bronze hue when mature.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
