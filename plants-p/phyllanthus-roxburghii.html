Phyllanthus roxburghii

Phyllanthus roxburghii, commonly known as stonebreaker, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, spherical berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Phyllanthus roxburghii is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
