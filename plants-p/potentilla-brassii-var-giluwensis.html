Potentilla brassii var. giluwensis

Potentilla brassii var. giluwensis, commonly known as the Giluwens mountain cinquefoil, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its dense, spreading growth habit and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are compound, with five leaflets that are oval and slightly hairy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh mountainous conditions.
