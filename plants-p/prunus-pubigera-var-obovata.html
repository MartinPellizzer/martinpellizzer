Prunus pubigera var. obovata

Prunus pubigera var. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its obovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green on top with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are followed by red or orange berries in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
