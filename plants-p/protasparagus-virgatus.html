Protasparagus virgatus

Protasparagus virgatus, commonly known as the slender asparagus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features thin, wiry stems that grow close to the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers during the spring and summer months. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

Protasparagus virgatus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.
