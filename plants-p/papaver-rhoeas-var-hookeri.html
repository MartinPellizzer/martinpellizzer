Papaver rhoeas var. hookeri

Papaver rhoeas var. hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its distinctive floral morphology.

It features showy, bright red petals that are often adorned with a contrasting yellow or orange central blotch. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in botanical studies.
