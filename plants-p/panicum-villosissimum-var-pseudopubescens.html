Panicum villosissimum var. pseudopubescens

Panicum villosissimum var. pseudopubescens, commonly known as the silky panicgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, slender leaves covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces fluffy, spike-like inflorescences that are often mistaken for cotton or other soft plant materials. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its aesthetic texture and drought tolerance.
