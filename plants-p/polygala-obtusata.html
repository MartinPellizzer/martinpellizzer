Polygala obtusata

Polygala obtusata, commonly known as the blunt-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including China and the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its delicate, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are usually ovate or elliptic, with a blunt or rounded tip, giving it its common name.

Polygala obtusata is often found in open woodlands and is of interest to botanists and gardeners due to its unique appearance and ecological significance.
