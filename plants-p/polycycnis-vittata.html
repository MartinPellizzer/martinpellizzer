Polycycnis vittata

Polycycnis vittata, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, and the leaves are covered in fine hairs.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
