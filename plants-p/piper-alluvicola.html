Piper alluvicola

Piper alluvicola, commonly known as the alluvial pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, three-lobed berries.

Piper alluvicola is often found in moist, shaded environments such as riverbanks and floodplains.
