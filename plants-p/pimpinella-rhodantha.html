Pimpinella rhodantha

Pimpinella rhodantha, commonly known as the scarlet cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
