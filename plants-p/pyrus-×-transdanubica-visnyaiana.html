Pyrus × transdanubica visnyaiana

Pyrus × transdanubica visnyaiana, commonly known as the Transdanubian pear.

This hybrid plant is a cross between Pyrus communis and Pyrus pyraster, resulting in a species with unique botanical characteristics. It is native to Central Europe and is known for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and fruit. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, with white flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its leaves are broad and dark green, and it produces small, edible fruits that are often used in jams and preserves.
