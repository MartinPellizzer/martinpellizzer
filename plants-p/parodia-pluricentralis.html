Parodia pluricentralis

Parodia pluricentralis, commonly known as the "Cactus of the Desert," is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for succulent collections.
