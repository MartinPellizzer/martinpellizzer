Pulsatilla hungarica

Pulsatilla hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. Its leaves are palmately divided and have a hairy texture, while its stems are usually unbranched and somewhat woody at the base.

Pulsatilla hungarica is known for its early spring blooming and is considered a symbol of resilience in harsh environments.
