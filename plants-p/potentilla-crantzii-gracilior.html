Potentilla crantzii gracilior

Potentilla crantzii gracilior, commonly known as the slender cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and long blooming period.

The species is also noted for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
