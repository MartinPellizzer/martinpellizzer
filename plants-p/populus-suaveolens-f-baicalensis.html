Populus suaveolens f. baicalensis

Populus suaveolens f. baicalensis, commonly known as the Baical poplar, is a rare and distinctive variant of the poplar genus.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a silvery underside. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in the Baikal Lake area of Russia. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in forested or streamside habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its genetic diversity and potential ecological roles in its native environment.
