Parasenecio rufipilis

Parasenecio rufipilis, commonly known as the red-hair sedum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, reddish or brownish hair, giving it its common name.

It is valued in horticulture for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
