Pithecellobium marginatum

Pithecellobium marginatum, commonly known as the yellow velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, and it is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
