Piper betle f. marianum

Piper betle f. marianum, commonly known as Malaysian betel leaf, is a variant of the betel plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional practices. The plant belongs to the Piperaceae family and is closely related to the species Piper betle. Its leaves contain alkaloids such as piperyline and piperine, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

The plant is cultivated for its leaves, which are used in chewing betel quid and for their purported health benefits.
