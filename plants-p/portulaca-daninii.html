Portulaca daninii

Portulaca daninii, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a succulent species that thrives in hot, dry climates and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing stems and leaves. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in the early morning and close by midday. Its ability to conserve water makes it well adapted to harsh desert environments.

Portulaca daninii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
