Plantago ludoviciana

Plantago ludoviciana, commonly known as the Virginia plantain, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in a spike-like inflorescence. The plant is adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes.

Botanically, it is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
