Physalis pubescens var. integrifolia

Physalis pubescens var. integrifolia, commonly known as the rough-hairy physalis, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is characterized by its hairy, yellow-orange calyces that enclose the berry-like fruits. The leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges and are covered in fine pubescence. The plant is native to South America and thrives in warm, temperate climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
