Piper polycladum

Piper polycladum, commonly known as the Indian pepper or black pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces tiny, three-chambered berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its fruit is the source of the spice known as black pepper, which is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and requires a humid environment for optimal growth.
