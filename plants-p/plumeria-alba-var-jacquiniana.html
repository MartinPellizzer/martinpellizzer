Plumeria alba var. jacquiniana

Plumeria alba var. jacquiniana, commonly known as the white frangipani, is a tropical flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, waxy flowers that bloom in clusters during the warm season. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its bark is smooth and light-colored, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy blooms and tropical appearance.
