Pentas confertifolia

Pentas confertifolia, commonly known as the clustered pentas, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Pentas confertifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
