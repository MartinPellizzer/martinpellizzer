Primula darialica var. chewsurica

Primula darialica var. chewsurica, commonly known as the Dariyalic primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest undergrowth or rocky slopes. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a waxy surface, while its stems are often hairy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
