Phalaenopsis × lotubela

Phalaenopsis × lotubela, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Phalaenopsis aphrodite and Phalaenopsis lueddemanniana.

It is a sympodial orchid with a compact growth habit and produces long, pendulous inflorescences bearing numerous flowers. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a velvety texture and a fragrant scent.

It is a popular choice for indoor cultivation due to its ease of care and prolonged blooming period.
