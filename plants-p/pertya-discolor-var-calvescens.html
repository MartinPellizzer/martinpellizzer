Pertya discolor var. calvescens

Pertya discolor var. calvescens, commonly known as the white-veined pertya, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with white veins contrasting against its dark green foliage. Its flowers are typically small and clustered around the spadix, which is often covered in a dense mass of tiny, inconspicuous blooms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
