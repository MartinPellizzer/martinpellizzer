Pinalia valida

Pinalia valida, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological importance in its native habitat.

Pinalia valida is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and vibrant color.
