Podocarpus macrophyllus subsp. piliramulus

Podocarpus macrophyllus subsp.

piliramulus, commonly known as the broad-leafed podocarp, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and can grow up to 30 meters tall.

The leaves are typically broad, leathery, and have a glossy appearance. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture and reforestation projects.
