Poinciana adansonioides

Poinciana adansonioides, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy, feather-like leaves and striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates. Its bark is smooth and grayish, while its roots are deep and strong, allowing it to anchor firmly in the soil.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
