Phleum nodosum

Phleum nodosum, commonly known as nodding oat, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping, nodding inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Phleum nodosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
