Pithecellobium guadalupense

Pithecellobium guadalupense, commonly known as the Guadalupense crownflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its legume-like pods and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrub or woodland habitats. Its small, showy yellow or white flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
