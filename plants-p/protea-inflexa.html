Protea inflexa

Protea inflexa, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flower heads. The plant features a woody, branched stem with a fibrous root system adapted to nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly sunbirds, due to their nectar-rich structure.

Protea inflexa is often found in fynbos vegetation and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
