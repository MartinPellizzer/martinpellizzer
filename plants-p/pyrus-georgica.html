Pyrus georgica

Pyrus georgica, commonly known as the Georgian pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and clusters of white flowers in spring. The tree produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically edible but not widely cultivated for commercial use. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is sometimes grown in botanical gardens and private collections.
