Prunus × blireiana f. moseri

Prunus × blireiana f. moseri, commonly known as the double-flowered cherry, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its showy, double blooms that consist of numerous small petals, giving it a lush, ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub or small tree, with dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.

Its flowers, which appear in spring, are a key feature for pollinators and add visual interest to landscapes.
