Pyrarda ceruanoides

Pyrarda ceruanoides, commonly known as the wax tree, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Pyrarda ceruanoides is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
