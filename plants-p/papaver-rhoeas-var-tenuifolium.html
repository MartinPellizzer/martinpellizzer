Papaver rhoeas var. tenuifolium

Papaver rhoeas var. tenuifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its slender, elongated leaves.

It produces striking red or orange flowers with a distinctive black or dark brown central spot. This plant is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. It is an annual herb that blooms in late spring to early summer.

The narrow leaves and vibrant flowers make it a notable ornamental plant in gardens and natural habitats.
