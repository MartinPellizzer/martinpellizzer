Phlogacanthus paniculatus

Phlogacanthus paniculatus, commonly known as the swamp hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetland or swampy habitats. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are usually pink or red in color. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and is often used in traditional medicine.
