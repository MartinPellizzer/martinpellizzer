Papaver radicatum f. lobatum

Papaver radicatum f. lobatum, commonly known as the lobed poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its distinctive lobed leaves.

This plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils. It produces striking, bright red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The foliage is typically broad and lobed, giving it a unique appearance compared to other poppy species.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rocky landscapes.
