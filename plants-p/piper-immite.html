Piper immite

Piper immite, commonly known as the Indian pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing the aromatic compound piperine, responsible for its spicy flavor. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
