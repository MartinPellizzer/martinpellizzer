Panicum holtzii

Panicum holtzii, commonly known as silverleaf panicgrass, is a flowering plant in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida, and is often found in wetlands and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, silver-gray leaves that help it reflect sunlight and reduce water loss, adapting to its wetland habitat. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of spikelets, which are typical of the grass family.

Panicum holtzii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
