Pyrus × arsenii

Pyrus × arsenii, commonly known as the Armenian whitebeam, is a hybrid species of the genus Pyrus.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible berries that are typically yellow or red in color. The plant is native to the Caucasus region and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soils. It has a slow growth rate and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential as a fruit-bearing plant.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
