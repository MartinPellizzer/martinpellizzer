Pleurothallis cryptophoranthoides

Pleurothallis cryptophoranthoides, commonly known as the hidden-branch pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that are typically small and clustered in inflorescences. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments, often growing as an epiphyte on trees or rocks. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and challenging species to cultivate.
