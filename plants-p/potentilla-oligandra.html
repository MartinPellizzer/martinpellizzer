Potentilla oligandra

Potentilla oligandra, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has finely divided, serrated leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Potentilla oligandra is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
