Primula vulgaris

Primula vulgaris, commonly known as the common primrose, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Primula vulgaris is a perennial herb that thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
