Piper hatschbachii

Piper hatschbachii, commonly known as the Schomburgk's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with dark green, ovate leaves and small, white flowers arranged in spikes. Its fruits are tiny, three-lobed berries that contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.
