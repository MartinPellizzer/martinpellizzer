Poecilostachys flaccidula

Poecilostachys flaccidula, commonly known as the slender grass or slender bamboo grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
