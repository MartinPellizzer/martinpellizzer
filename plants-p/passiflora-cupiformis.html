Passiflora cupiformis

Passiflora cupiformis, commonly known as the cup-shaped passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate flowers that feature a crown-like structure at the base. The plant produces showy, five-petaled blooms in shades of yellow and purple, often with a central cluster of filaments. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its fruits are edible berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Passiflora cupiformis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
