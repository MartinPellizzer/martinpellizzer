Potentilla breweri var. viridis

Potentilla breweri var. viridis, commonly known as the green potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and cliffs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically oval and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in native plant gardens.
