Potentilla sibirica var. pectinata

Potentilla sibirica var. pectinata, commonly known as the Siberian cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a vibrant touch to its rugged habitat. The plant has compound leaves with five to nine leaflets, which are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or tundra environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in harsh climates.
