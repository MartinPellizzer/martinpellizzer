Ptychocarpa

Ptychocarpa, commonly known as the desert bottle tree, is a unique shrub or small tree native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Ptychocarpa has a deep root system that helps it access groundwater, making it highly drought-resistant.

Its distinctive appearance and ecological adaptations make it an important component of desert flora.
