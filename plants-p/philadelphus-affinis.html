Philadelphus affinis

Philadelphus affinis, commonly known as the common mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Philadelphus affinis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and pleasant scent.
