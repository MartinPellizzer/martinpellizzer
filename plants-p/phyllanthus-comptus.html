Phyllanthus comptus

Phyllanthus comptus, commonly known as the small-leaf phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually less than 2 centimeters in length. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are arranged in clusters and are followed by small, round fruits.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
