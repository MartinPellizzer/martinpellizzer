Palmijuncus pseudorotang

Palmijuncus pseudorotang, commonly known as the narrow-leafed palm, is a rare and unique palm species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and narrow, elongated leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments with well-drained soil, often in lowland areas. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces fruit that is edible but not widely cultivated.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
