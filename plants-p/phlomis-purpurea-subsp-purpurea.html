Phlomis purpurea subsp. purpurea

Phlomis purpurea subsp.

purpurea, commonly known as purple blazingstar, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking purple to pink flower heads that bloom in summer.

The plant has gray-green, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and it produces bracted flowers in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
