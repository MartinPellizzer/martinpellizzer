Pavonia kunthii var. intermedia

Pavonia kunthii var. intermedia, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, violet to purple petals that surround a central cone-like structure. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, fluffy seeds in its dried flower heads.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
