Premna cambodiana

Premna cambodiana, commonly known as Cambodian glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers, which are often followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Premna cambodiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
