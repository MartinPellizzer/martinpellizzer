Podocarpus appressus

Podocarpus appressus, commonly known as the dwarf podocarp, is a compact evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, typical of coniferous species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Podocarpus appressus is relatively low-maintenance and adapts well to various environmental conditions.
