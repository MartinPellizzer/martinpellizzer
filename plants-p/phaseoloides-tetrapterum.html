Phaseoloides tetrapterum

Phaseoloides tetrapterum, commonly known as the four-winged clusterbean, is a climbing leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features compound leaves with four pairs of wing-like leaflets, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It is valued for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various traditional cuisines.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its association with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
