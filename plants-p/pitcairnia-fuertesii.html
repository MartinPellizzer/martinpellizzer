Pitcairnia fuertesii

Pitcairnia fuertesii, commonly known as the Fuertes' pineapple plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of long, narrow, evergreen leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces an inflorescence with showy, violet-red bracts that enclose small, white flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular species among bromeliad enthusiasts.
