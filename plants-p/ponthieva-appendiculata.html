Ponthieva appendiculata

Ponthieva appendiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It features slender, arrow-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating, adapted for aquatic environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, which bloom during the wet season. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the soil in marshy areas.

Ponthieva appendiculata is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to filter water and support aquatic life.
