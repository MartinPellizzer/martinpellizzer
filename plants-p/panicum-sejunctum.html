Panicum sejunctum

Panicum sejunctum, commonly known as the narrow panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of small, spikelet-like flowers.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in harsh conditions.
