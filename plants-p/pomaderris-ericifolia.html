Pomaderris ericifolia

Pomaderris ericifolia, commonly known as the Heath Pomaderris, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and dry forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a popular choice for native gardens.
