Poa violacea var. breunia

Poa violacea var. breunia, commonly known as the purple meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually bluish-green, and the flowers are small and violet in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
