Pseuderanthemum armitii

Pseuderanthemum armitii, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement, and it often grows as a shrub or small tree.
