Pyrus rotundoconica

Pyrus rotundoconica, commonly known as the round-coned pear.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae, characterized by its unique, rounded fruit shape. The plant is native to parts of East Asia, particularly China, where it grows in temperate climates. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a smooth texture, and it produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring.

The fruit, while not widely cultivated, is noted for its sweet, juicy pulp and potential ornamental value.
