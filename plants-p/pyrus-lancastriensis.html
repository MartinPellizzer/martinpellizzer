Pyrus lancastriensis

Pyrus lancastriensis, commonly known as the Lancashire pear, is a small deciduous tree native to the UK.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other pear species. The plant is characterized by its elliptical leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in spring. Its fruit is a small, sweet pear that ripens in late summer.

Due to its limited distribution and rarity, it is considered a conservation priority in its native habitat.
