Polypodiodes lachnopus

Polypodiodes lachnopus, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a rare and unusual fern species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, velvety, and densely hairy fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, where it forms compact, low-growing clumps. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a soft, almost fuzzy texture due to the dense covering of fine hairs.

Despite its delicate appearance, the velvet fern is highly resilient and adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
