Primulina glandulosa

Primulina glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular primula, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically glossy and arranged in a rosette pattern, while the flowers come in shades of white, pink, and red. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant.

The plant's unique glandular structures on its leaves contribute to its ability to retain moisture in its natural habitat.
