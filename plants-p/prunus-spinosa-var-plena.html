Prunus spinosa var. plena

Prunus spinosa var. plena, commonly known as the double-flowered blackthorn, is a variant of the common blackthorn, characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically reaches heights of 2 to 4 meters, with dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, double flowers in early spring, which are highly ornamental. The fruit, known as a drupe, is small, dark blue, and contains a single seed.

This variety is often cultivated for its showy floral display and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
