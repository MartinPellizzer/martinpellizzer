Potentilla wismariensis

Potentilla wismariensis, commonly known as the Wismar cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings. Its leaves are compound, with five leaflets that are often hairy and serrated.

Potentilla wismariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators.
