Palaquium maliliense

Palaquium maliliense, commonly known as the Malili wax apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Sapotaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that have a waxy, yellowish skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
