Potentilla × tormentilla-formosa

Potentilla × tormentilla-formosa, commonly known as the hybrid shrub, is a deciduous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early summer, which are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly serrated margin, and they turn reddish in the fall.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
