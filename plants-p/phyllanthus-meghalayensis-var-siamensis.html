Phyllanthus meghalayensis var. siamensis

Phyllanthus meghalayensis var. siamensis, commonly known as the Siam phyllanthus, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellowish flowers, which are typically borne in inflorescences. The plant is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India and Myanmar. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
