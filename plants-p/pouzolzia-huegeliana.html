Pouzolzia huegeliana

Pouzolzia huegeliana, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a leathery texture and a dark green color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Pouzolzia huegeliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
