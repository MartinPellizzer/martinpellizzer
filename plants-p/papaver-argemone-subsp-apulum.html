Papaver argemone subsp. apulum

Papaver argemone subsp.

apulum, commonly known as the Apulum poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking red or orange petals and a distinctive, rounded seed capsule.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
