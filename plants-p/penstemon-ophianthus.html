Penstemon ophianthus

Penstemon ophianthus, commonly known as the golden penstemon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Penstemon ophianthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
