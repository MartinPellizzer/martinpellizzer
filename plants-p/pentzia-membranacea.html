Pentzia membranacea

Pentzia membranacea, commonly known as the paper plant, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, membranous leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid conditions. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for xeriscaping.

Due to its striking appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
