Passiflora exoperculata

Passiflora exoperculata, commonly known as the waxy passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy blue and purple bracts that surround the actual flowers. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically white or pink, with a distinctive crown of filaments. Its fruit is a woody, spiny capsule that contains edible, seed-filled arils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
