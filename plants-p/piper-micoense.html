Piper micoense

Piper micoense, commonly known as the Micoense pepper or Brazilian pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest and is characterized by its small, aromatic berries and compound leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its fruits are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice, though it is less commonly cultivated than other pepper species.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
