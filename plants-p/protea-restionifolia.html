Protea restionifolia

Protea restionifolia, commonly known as the coastal protea, is a South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, cone-shaped inflorescence that resembles a flower head, composed of numerous small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, sandy soils. It is adapted to a Mediterranean climate with dry summers and wet winters.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.
