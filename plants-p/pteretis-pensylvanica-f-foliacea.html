Pteretis pensylvanica f. foliacea

Pteretis pensylvanica f. foliacea, commonly known as the Pennsylvania pteris with variegated leaves, is a fern species characterized by its unique foliage.

It features fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive variegation pattern of green and white or yellow. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its leaves are often used in horticultural displays due to their ornamental appeal.

Botanically, it is noted for its compact growth habit and ability to form dense clumps over time.
