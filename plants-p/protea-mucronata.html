Protea mucronata

Protea mucronata, commonly known as the Natal sugarbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family, which includes a variety of ornamental and ecologically significant species. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its unique floral structure features a central cone surrounded by bracts, which attract pollinators like birds and insects.

Protea mucronata is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
