Parsonsia albiflora

Parsonsia albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered parsonsia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces striking white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, often appearing in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its showy blooms.
