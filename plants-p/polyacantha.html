Polyacantha

Polyacantha, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its soft, silvery-blue foliage and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Polyacantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
