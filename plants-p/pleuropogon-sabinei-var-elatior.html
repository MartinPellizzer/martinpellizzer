Pleuropogon sabinei var. elatior

Pleuropogon sabinei var. elatior, commonly known as the tall bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
