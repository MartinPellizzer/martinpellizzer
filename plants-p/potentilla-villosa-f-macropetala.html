Potentilla villosa f. macropetala

Potentilla villosa f. macropetala, commonly known as the large-flowered silverweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous or alpine regions. It has a low-growing habit with woody stems and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
