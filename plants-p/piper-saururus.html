Piper saururus

Piper saururus, commonly known as greenbrier pepper or swamp pepper, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species, and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, berry-like fruits. Its leaves and fruits contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in ethnobotanical and pharmacological studies.

Piper saururus thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in wetlands and forest understories.
