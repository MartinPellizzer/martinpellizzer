Pilea inaequalis

Pilea inaequalis, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its round, shield-shaped leaves that resemble coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that emerge from a spadix surrounded by a spathe. It is a popular houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Pilea inaequalis thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
