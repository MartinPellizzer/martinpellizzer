Perityle rupestris

Perityle rupestris, commonly known as the rock perityle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal environments. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. It is well adapted to arid conditions and can thrive in poor soil.

Perityle rupestris is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardiness and attractive appearance.
