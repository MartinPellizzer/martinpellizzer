Prunella × commixta

Prunella × commixta, commonly known as the hybrid selfheal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides.

Prunella × commixta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
