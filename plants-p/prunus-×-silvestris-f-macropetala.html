Prunus × silvestris f. macropetala

Prunus × silvestris f. macropetala, commonly known as the large-flowered cherry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a slightly hairy underside.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic floral display.
