Piper cheyennense

Piper cheyennense, commonly known as the Cheyenne pepper, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into three-lobed berries, which are typically harvested when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent, while the plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Piper cheyennense is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to its unique flavor profile.
