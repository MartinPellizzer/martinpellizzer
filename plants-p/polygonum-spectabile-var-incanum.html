Polygonum spectabile var. incanum

Polygonum spectabile var. incanum, commonly known as the silverleaf dock, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in riparian areas.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
