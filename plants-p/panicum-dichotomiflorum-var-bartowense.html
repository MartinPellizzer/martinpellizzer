Panicum dichotomiflorum var. bartowense

Panicum dichotomiflorum var. bartowense, commonly known as Bartow's switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that are often branched. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue and a hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for native plant gardens and restoration projects.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, including soil stabilization and wildlife habitat.
