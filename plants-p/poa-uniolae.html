Poa uniolae

Poa uniolae, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-hued foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in meadows, stream banks, and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of slender spikelets that are often clustered in groups, enhancing its ornamental value.

Poa uniolae is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
