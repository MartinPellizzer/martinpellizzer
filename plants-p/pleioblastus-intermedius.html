Pleioblastus intermedius

Pleioblastus intermedius, commonly known as the Japanese forest grass, is a cool-season grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and fine, bluish-green leaves that create a dense, carpet-like appearance. This plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Pleioblastus intermedius is also valued for its low maintenance requirements and ability to form dense clumps over time.
