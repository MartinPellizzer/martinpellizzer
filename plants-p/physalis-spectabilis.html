Physalis spectabilis

Physalis spectabilis, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or golden berry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant orange, lantern-shaped fruits that enclose the seeds. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

The fruit matures in late summer to early autumn and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
