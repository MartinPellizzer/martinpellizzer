Phyteuma betonicifolium f. pubescens

Phyteuma betonicifolium f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, loamy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
