Pritzelago alpina subsp. dubia

Pritzelago alpina subsp.

dubia, commonly known as the alpine penny-cress, is a herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers that typically bloom in early spring.

The plant has alternate, pinnately divided leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Brassicaceae family, sharing similar morphological and ecological traits.
