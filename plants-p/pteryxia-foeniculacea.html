Pteryxia foeniculacea

Pteryxia foeniculacea, commonly known as the sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has feathery, finely divided leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Pteryxia foeniculacea thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in arid regions.
