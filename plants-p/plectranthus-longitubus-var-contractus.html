Plectranthus longitubus var. contractus

Plectranthus longitubus var. contractus, commonly known as the narrow-tubed plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
