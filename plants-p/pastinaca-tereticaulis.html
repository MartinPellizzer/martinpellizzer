Pastinaca tereticaulis

Pastinaca tereticaulis, commonly known as the slender burdock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust taproot and tall, slender stems covered in fine hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a distinctive central vein. Its purple-tinted flower heads appear in late summer, and the plant produces small, achene-like seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
