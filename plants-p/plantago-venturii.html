Plantago venturii

Plantago venturii, commonly known as the Venturi's plantain, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is native to certain regions of North America, particularly in the western United States. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture and produces small, clustered flowers in early spring. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Botanically, it is characterized by its basal rosette growth habit and its ability to spread through rhizomes.
