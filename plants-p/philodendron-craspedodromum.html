Philodendron craspedodromum

Philodendron craspedodromum, commonly known as the "Bull's Head Philodendron," is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that often have a distinctive reddish or purplish tinge on the underside. The plant features a unique, swollen stem base that resembles a bull's head, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Philodendron craspedodromum is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its striking foliage and ease of care.
