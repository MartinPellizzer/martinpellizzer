Persea primatogena

Persea primatogena, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, orange to reddish fruits that are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.

The tree is relatively slow-growing and can reach heights of up to 10 meters.
