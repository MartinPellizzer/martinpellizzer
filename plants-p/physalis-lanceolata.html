Physalis lanceolata

Physalis lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that develop into orange, lantern-like fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its fruit, which is edible when ripe, has a sweet-tart flavor and is often used in culinary applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
