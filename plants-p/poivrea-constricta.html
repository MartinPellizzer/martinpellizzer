Poivrea constricta

Poivrea constricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, fragrant white or yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its red or orange berries are edible and attract birds.

Poivrea constricta is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
