Phyteuma comosum

Phyteuma comosum, commonly known as the clustered phyteuma, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing blue to violet flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens and borders.

Phyteuma comosum is known for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
