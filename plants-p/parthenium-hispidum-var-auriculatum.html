Parthenium hispidum var. auriculatum

Parthenium hispidum var. auriculatum, commonly known as the auriculate parthenium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-white flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is an annual or biennial herb that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
