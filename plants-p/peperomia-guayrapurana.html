Peperomia guayrapurana

Peperomia guayrapurana, commonly known as the Guayrapurana pepper plant, is a compact, low-growing perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features dense, waxy foliage with lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white or pale pink, which are followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia guayrapurana is known for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
