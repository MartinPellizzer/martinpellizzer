Plumeria bracteata

Plumeria bracteata, commonly known as the frangipani tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, pink, and white. The plant has simple, opposite, and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its bark is smooth and light gray, and it produces woody, spiny capsules that contain numerous seeds.

Plumeria bracteata is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and cultural significance in various regions.
