Potentilla gariepensis

Potentilla gariepensis, commonly known as the Gariep cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Potentilla gariepensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
