Paeonia officinalis subsp. huthii

Paeonia officinalis subsp.

huthii, commonly known as the Huth's peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in late spring.

The plant features compound leaves with a serrated margin and a robust root system that helps it thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.

The subspecies is closely related to other peony species but is distinguished by its specific habitat preferences and floral characteristics.
