Panicum chacoense

Panicum chacoense, commonly known as Chaco grass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly found in the Chaco region of Paraguay and Argentina.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in arid regions.
