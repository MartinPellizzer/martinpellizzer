Polyscias muraltana

Polyscias muraltana, commonly known as the Muralt's glorybower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Polyscias muraltana is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
