Prismatocarpus sessilis

Prismatocarpus sessilis, commonly known as the sessile-fruited pandanus, is a flowering plant species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Pandanaceae and is characterized by its robust, fibrous rhizomes and pungent, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spadices, followed by triangular, woody fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional cuisine and for making thatch, highlighting its cultural and practical significance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in coastal and wetland environments.
