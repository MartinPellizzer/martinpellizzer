Piper provulgatum

Piper provulgatum, commonly known as the common pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its vine-like growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into peppery, edible berries.

The plant is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and fruits, which are used in traditional cuisine and medicine.
