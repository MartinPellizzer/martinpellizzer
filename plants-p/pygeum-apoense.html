Pygeum apoense

Pygeum apoense, commonly known as African wild almond, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and leathery, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by hard, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it is often found in dry, rocky soils.

Pygeum apoense is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional African medicine.
