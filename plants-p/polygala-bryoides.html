Polygala bryoides

Polygala bryoides, commonly known as the rock polygala, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in well-drained environments. The plant features slender, erect stems with small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white or grayish pubescence.

Polygala bryoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
