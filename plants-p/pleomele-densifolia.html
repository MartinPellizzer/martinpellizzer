Pleomele densifolia

Pleomele densifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved gingerplant, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, aiding in water retention in its native habitat. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to other members of the ginger family.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
