Prunus mume var. tonsa

Prunus mume var. tonsa, commonly known as the Japanese apricot or Chinese flowering apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. Its ornamental value is highly prized in gardens for its vibrant floral display.

The leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and greyish-brown in maturity.
