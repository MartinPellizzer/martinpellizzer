Paraphlomis javanica var. angustifolia

Paraphlomis javanica var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved paralaburnum, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive species within the Lamiaceae family.
