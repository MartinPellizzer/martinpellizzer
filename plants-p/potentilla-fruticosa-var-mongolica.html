Potentilla fruticosa var. mongolica

Potentilla fruticosa var. mongolica, commonly known as Mongolian cinquefoil, is a shrub native to the cold climates of Mongolia and northern China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and arctic tundra environments. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated edge, while its roots are fibrous and adapt well to harsh conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
