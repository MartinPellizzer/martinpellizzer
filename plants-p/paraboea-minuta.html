Paraboea minuta

Paraboea minuta, commonly known as the dwarf paraboea, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. The plant features delicate, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its small, white flowers are clustered in inflorescences and bloom during the warmer months.

Paraboea minuta is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its compact size and attractive foliage.
