Pleopeltis raddiana

Pleopeltis raddiana, commonly known as the Raddiana rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, clustered fronds that often grow on tree trunks or rocky surfaces. The plant has a short, thick rhizome with a distinctive, leathery texture. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Pleopeltis raddiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
