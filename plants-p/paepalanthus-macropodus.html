Paepalanthus macropodus

Paepalanthus macropodus, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in diameter, floating on the surface of still or slow-moving waters. The plant's flowers are typically white or pink, with a distinctive, showy structure that attracts pollinators such as beetles and flies. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to form dense colonies.

Paepalanthus macropodus plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter for aquatic life and contributing to water quality through oxygenation.
