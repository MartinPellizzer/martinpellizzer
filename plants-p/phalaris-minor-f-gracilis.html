Phalaris minor f. gracilis

Phalaris minor f. gracilis, commonly known as slender bulbous grass, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to form dense mats, making it a useful plant for erosion control.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
