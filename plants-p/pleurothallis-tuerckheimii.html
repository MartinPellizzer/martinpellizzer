Pleurothallis tuerckheimii

Pleurothallis tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, lance-shaped leaves and bears small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. It is noted for its unique floral structure and is often found in high-altitude regions.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
