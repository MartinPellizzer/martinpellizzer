Pulsatilla patens f. alborosea

Pulsatilla patens f. alborosea, commonly known as the white pasque flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range from white to pale yellow. The plant has a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves that help it conserve moisture in arid environments. It blooms in early spring, typically between March and May, and is adapted to cold climates with a long growing season.

The f. alborosea form is distinguished by its lighter-colored flowers compared to the typical purple or pink varieties of Pulsatilla patens.
