Pinus pseudostrobus var. estevezii

Pinus pseudostrobus var. estevezii, commonly known as the Mexican white pine, is a variant of the Mexican pine native to the highlands of central Mexico.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its long, needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters, with a conical crown. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against environmental stressors.

This variety is valued for its timber and plays a significant role in the ecological systems of its native habitat.
