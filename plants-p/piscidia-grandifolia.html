Piscidia grandifolia

Piscidia grandifolia, commonly known as the scarlet pisonia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and have a serrated edge. The plant produces bright red, showy flowers that are clustered in racemes, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Piscidia grandifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
