Penstemon linarioides subsp. maguirei

Penstemon linarioides subsp.

maguirei, commonly known as Maguire's penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and drought tolerance in arid landscapes.
