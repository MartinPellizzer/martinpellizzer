Ptilotrichum lapeyrouseanum var. angustifolium

Ptilotrichum lapeyrouseanum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ptilotrichum, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, typically with a rough texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
