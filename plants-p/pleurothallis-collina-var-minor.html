Pleurothallis collina var. minor

Pleurothallis collina var. minor, commonly known as the lesser hill orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features slender, wiry stems and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a subtle fragrance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to conserve moisture in its high-altitude habitat.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relative ease of cultivation.
