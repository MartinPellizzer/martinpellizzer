Pisum sativum subsp. abyssinicum

Pisum sativum subsp.

abyssinicum, commonly known as Ethiopian pea, is a subspecies of the pea plant native to Ethiopia. It is characterized by its small, wrinkled seeds and typically grows as an annual herb.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers, which are followed by pods containing several seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to the high-altitude regions of its native habitat.

This subspecies is valued for its nutritional content and traditional use in local cuisines.
