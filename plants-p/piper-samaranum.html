Piper samaranum

Piper samaranum, commonly known as the Samara pepper or Samara long pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, elongated berries that are harvested when mature for their aromatic and pungent properties. Its botanical features include opposite, ovate leaves and small, white flowers that are clustered in spikes.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses.
