Pyrus kiusiana

Pyrus kiusiana, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and dense foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that ripen in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Pyrus kiusiana is also valued for its role in traditional Japanese horticulture and its adaptability to various soil types.
