Paepalanthus cephalopus

Paepalanthus cephalopus, commonly known as the helmet cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It is a small, globular cactus with a distinctive helmet-like shape, which gives it its common name. The plant features dense, spiny ribs and produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soil conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
