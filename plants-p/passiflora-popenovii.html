Passiflora popenovii

Passiflora popenovii, commonly known as the Potosi passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a mix of red, blue, and yellow hues. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange fruits that are often consumed raw or used in jams. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and twining.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
