Pleiospilos × purpusii

Pleiospilos × purpusii, commonly known as the hybrid rock tulip, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is a hybrid between Pleiospilos nelii and Pleiospilos nelii var. purpusii, characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and vibrant, cup-shaped flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its swollen stems and leaves. It blooms in late winter to early spring, producing showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
