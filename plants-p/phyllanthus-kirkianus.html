Phyllanthus kirkianus

Phyllanthus kirkianus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and savannas, and it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous minute seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Phyllanthus kirkianus is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
