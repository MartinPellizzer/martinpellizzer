Prunus korolkowii

Prunus korolkowii, commonly known as the Korean cherry or Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly during the spring when it produces clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin. Its fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer, and it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.

Prunus korolkowii is relatively cold-hardy and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
