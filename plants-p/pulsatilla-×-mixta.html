Pulsatilla × mixta

Pulsatilla × mixta, commonly known as the hybrid pasqueflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Pulsatilla vulgaris (common pasqueflower) and Pulsatilla patens (showy pasqueflower), resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The plant features showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive, rounded shape and a hairy, woolly appearance. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a soft, downy texture, and it typically grows in well-drained, rocky soils.

Pulsatilla × mixta is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in cool, sunny conditions.
