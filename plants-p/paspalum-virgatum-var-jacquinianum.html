Paspalum virgatum var. jacquinianum

Paspalum virgatum var. jacquinianum, commonly known as the Jacquin's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in the summer. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
