Pandanus alveolatus

Pandanus alveolatus, commonly known as the honeycomb pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fibrous, and segmented leaves that resemble a honeycomb structure. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional crafts and for making thatch.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near coastal areas.
