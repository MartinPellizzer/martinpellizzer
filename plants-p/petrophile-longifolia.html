Petrophile longifolia

Petrophile longifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its striking red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in coastal or heathland environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and sometimes branched.

Petrophile longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
