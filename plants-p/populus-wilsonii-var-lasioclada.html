Populus wilsonii var. lasioclada

Populus wilsonii var. lasioclada, commonly known as the Wilson's poplar or the woolly poplar, is a variant of the poplar tree native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-white undersides on its leaves, which give it a striking appearance. The plant has a rapid growth rate and is often found in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in forested areas. Its bark is smooth and grayish-white, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
