Prosthechea latro

Prosthechea latro, commonly known as the "serpent orchid," is a species of epiphytic orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and produces striking, pendulous inflorescences with fragrant, violet-colored flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, organic substrates for optimal growth. Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and specialized collections.

Prosthechea latro is also noted for its ability to form dense colonies, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
