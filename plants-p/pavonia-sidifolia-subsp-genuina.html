Pavonia sidifolia subsp. genuina

Pavonia sidifolia subsp.

genuina, commonly known as the slender pavonia, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
