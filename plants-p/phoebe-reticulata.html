Phoebe reticulata

Phoebe reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined phoebe, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with leaves that have a distinctive network of veins giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale green, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Phoebe reticulata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to adapt to a variety of growing conditions.
