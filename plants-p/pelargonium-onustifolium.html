Pelargonium onustifolium

Pelargonium onustifolium, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.

Pelargonium onustifolium prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.
