Phrynium flavescens

Phrynium flavescens, commonly known as the yellow arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are usually bright green with a yellow or white midrib. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, making it a popular ornamental species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Phrynium flavescens is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value and its ability to tolerate a variety of environmental conditions.
