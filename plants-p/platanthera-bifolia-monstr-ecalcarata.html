Platanthera bifolia monstr. ecalcarata

Platanthera bifolia monstr.

ecalcarata, commonly known as the showy lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to the northeastern United States and eastern Canada. It features a dense, grass-like foliage and produces long, spiraled inflorescences with fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It has a complex root system and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
