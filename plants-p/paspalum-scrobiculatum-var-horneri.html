Paspalum scrobiculatum var. horneri

Paspalum scrobiculatum var. horneri, commonly known as Horner's sandgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It thrives in sandy and coastal environments, often found in dunes and salt marshes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to tolerate saline and well-drained soils.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
