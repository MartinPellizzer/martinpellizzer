Planchonia crenata

Planchonia crenata, commonly known as the Hawaiian sandalwood, is a member of the Rutaceae family.

It is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, particularly Hawaii. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, and it produces woody, oval-shaped fruits.

Planchonia crenata is known for its aromatic wood, which has been historically used in traditional Hawaiian culture.
