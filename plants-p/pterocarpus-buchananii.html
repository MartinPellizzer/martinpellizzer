Pterocarpus buchananii

Pterocarpus buchananii, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood that is highly valued in the furniture and musical instrument industries. The tree has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces distinctive yellow or orange flowers. Its fruit is a flat, pod-like structure that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional herbal remedies.
