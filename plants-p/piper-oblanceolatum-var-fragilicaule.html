Piper oblanceolatum var. fragilicaule

Piper oblanceolatum var. fragilicaule, commonly known as the fragrant betel vine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, flexible stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by black, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

The varietal name "fragilicaule" refers to its delicate and fragile stems, which are a distinguishing feature of this plant.
