Persicaria campanulata

Persicaria campanulata, commonly known as the bellflower knotweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late summer. Its scientific name reflects its botanical classification within the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel.

Persicaria campanulata is known for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
