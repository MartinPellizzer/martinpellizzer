Philodendron mamei

Philodendron mamei, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant has a vining growth habit, making it suitable for hanging baskets or trellises. Its stems are fibrous and can develop aerial roots, which help it climb.

Philodendron mamei prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
