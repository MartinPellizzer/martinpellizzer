Pultenaea juniperina var. macrophylla

Pultenaea juniperina var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved pultenaea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are typically longer than those of the typical variety. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in dense clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

The variety is distinguished by its larger foliage, making it a notable species within the Pultenaea genus.
