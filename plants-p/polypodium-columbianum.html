Polypodium columbianum

Polypodium columbianum, commonly known as the Colombian shield fern, is a species of fern native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and pinnately compound fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with sporangia clustered on the underside of the leaves.

Polypodium columbianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
