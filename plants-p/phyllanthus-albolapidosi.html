Phyllanthus albolapidosi

Phyllanthus albolapidosi, commonly known as the white-stemmed phyllanthus, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly found in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant produces small, smooth, greenish-yellow fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.
