Phyllanthus naviluri

Phyllanthus naviluri, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, yellow berries. The leaves and fruits are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional and medicinal value.
