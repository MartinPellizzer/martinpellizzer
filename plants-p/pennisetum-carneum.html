Pennisetum carneum

Pennisetum carneum, commonly known as red grass or blood grass, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its striking red or pink inflorescence. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in open woodlands or grasslands. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with the inflorescence being the most visually distinctive feature.

Pennisetum carneum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant color and ornamental value.
