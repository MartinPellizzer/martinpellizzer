Philibertia gilliesii var. albida

Philibertia gilliesii var. albida, commonly known as the white-flowered filbert, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
