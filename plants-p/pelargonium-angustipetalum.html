Pelargonium angustipetalum

Pelargonium angustipetalum, commonly known as the narrow-petaled geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers. The leaves are typically lobed and have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers come in shades of pink, red, and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
