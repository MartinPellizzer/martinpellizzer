Pyrus henryi

Pyrus henryi, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray-brown bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by white to yellow fruits that are edible.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
