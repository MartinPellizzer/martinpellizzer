Pennisetum indicum subvar. hordeoides

Pennisetum indicum subvar. hordeoides, commonly known as false dwarf millet, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, beige to brown florets. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soils, making it suitable for erosion control.

Its ornamental value is often appreciated in gardens and landscapes for its delicate appearance.
