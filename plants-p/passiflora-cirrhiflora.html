Passiflora cirrhiflora

Passiflora cirrhiflora, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white hues. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits that are often used in juices and desserts. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered with fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Passiflora cirrhiflora thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
