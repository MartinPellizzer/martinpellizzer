Puccinellia Pulchella

Bluegrass, scientifically known as Puccinellia pulchella, is a striking plant native to the grasslands of North America. It is recognized for its vibrant blue-green foliage that catches the eye in open fields and prairies. This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or sunny habitats. Its unique coloration and hardy nature make it a popular choice for native plant gardens. Despite its beauty, it remains a lesser-known member of the grass family, deserving of more attention in ecological discussions.
