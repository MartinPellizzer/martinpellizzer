Phyllanthus × fluminis-sabi

Phyllanthus × fluminis-sabi, commonly known as the Brazilian water spinach, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems that often grow in moist or aquatic environments. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically arranged in clusters, and it produces yellowish-green fruits that are capsule-like in structure.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
