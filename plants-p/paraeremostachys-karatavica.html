Paraeremostachys karatavica

Paraeremostachys karatavica, commonly known as the Karatavica grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and grasslands.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a significant role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
