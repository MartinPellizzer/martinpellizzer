Platygyna obovata

Platygyna obovata, commonly known as the broadleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It features simple, alternate, obovate leaves that are typically hairy and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, blooming in the summer months. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The species is often found in dry, disturbed habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
