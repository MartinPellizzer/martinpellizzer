Paphiopedilum crossii f. viniferum

The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually a mix of green, white, and pink hues, with a prominent lip that is often darker in color.

The f. viniferum form is distinguished by its more elongated and vine-like growth habit compared to the typical form of Paphiopedilum crossii.
