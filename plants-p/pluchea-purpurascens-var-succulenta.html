Pluchea purpurascens var. succulenta

Pluchea purpurascens var. succulenta, commonly known as purple false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its succulent, hairy leaves and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its vibrant foliage and showy blooms.
