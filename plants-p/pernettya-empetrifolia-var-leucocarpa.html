Pernettya empetrifolia var. leucocarpa

Pernettya empetrifolia var. leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited cowberry, is a shrub native to the northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant's berries are typically white or yellow, distinguishing it from other varieties of the species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal and subarctic climates.

The leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
