Phlomis puberula

Phlomis puberula, commonly known as woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves and tall spikes of purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is drought-tolerant. It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Phlomis puberula is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
