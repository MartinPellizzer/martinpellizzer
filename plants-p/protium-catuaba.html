Protium catuaba

Protium catuaba, commonly known as catuaba, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its roots and bark are traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported stimulant and aphrodisiac properties.

The species is also valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
