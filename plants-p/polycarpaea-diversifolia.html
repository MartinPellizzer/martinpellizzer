Polycarpaea diversifolia

Polycarpaea diversifolia, commonly known as the variable-leaf polycarpaea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that vary in shape and size, often displaying a range of colors from green to reddish-brown. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
