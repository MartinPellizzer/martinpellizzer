Plectranthus gardneri

Plectranthus gardneri, commonly known as the Cape verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is also known as the mint family, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting various pollinators. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Plectranthus gardneri is drought-tolerant and prefers full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid regions.
