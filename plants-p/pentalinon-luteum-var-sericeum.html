Pentalinon luteum var. sericeum

Pentalinon luteum var. sericeum, commonly known as the silky penta, is a variant of the yellow penta native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically opposite and have a smooth, sometimes slightly hairy texture. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant color and relatively low maintenance requirements.
