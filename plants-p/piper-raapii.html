Piper raapii

Piper raapii, commonly known as the Java pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing a spicy compound similar to capsaicin. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.
