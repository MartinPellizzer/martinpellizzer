Ponera macroglossa

Ponera macroglossa, commonly known as the large-tongued orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with long, ribbon-like petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting specific pollinators such as bees and moths.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
