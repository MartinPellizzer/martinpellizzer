Phyllanthus suberosus

Phyllanthus suberosus, commonly known as the hardy African spurge, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface and often has a waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, spherical berries. It is adapted to poor soil conditions and can thrive in arid environments.

Phyllanthus suberosus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
