Phoradendron flavescens var. pubescens

Phoradendron flavescens var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent yellow mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of various host trees.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or yellow, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in humid, tropical climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems it inhabits.
