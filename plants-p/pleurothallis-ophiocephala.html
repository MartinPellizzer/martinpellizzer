Pleurothallis ophiocephala

Pleurothallis ophiocephala, commonly known as the snakehead orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features elongated, linear leaves and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a subtle fragrance. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique inflorescence structure.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often found in cloud forests and is considered a rare and specialized species.
