Phreatia microtidis

Phreatia microtidis, commonly known as the small-lobed orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree trunks or branches in tropical rainforests. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the wet season. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and decaying organic matter.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
