Prunus chikusiensis

Prunus chikusiensis, commonly known as the Chikusi cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded canopy and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, round cherries that are typically red or black when mature. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Prunus chikusiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its spring blossoms and edible fruits.
