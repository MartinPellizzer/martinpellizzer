Portulaca oleracea subsp. tuberculata

Portulaca oleracea subsp.

tuberculata, commonly known as the African salad plant or purslane, is a succulent annual plant native to Africa, Asia, and Australia. It features fleshy, prostrate stems and small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in the morning and close in the heat of the day. It thrives in hot, dry climates and is often found in disturbed soils, gardens, and along roadsides.

This species is edible, with nutrient-rich leaves that are used in various cuisines around the world.
