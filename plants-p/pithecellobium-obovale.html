Pithecellobium obovale

Pithecellobium obovale, commonly known as the oval pod bean, is a species of legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are compound, with several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its potential agricultural uses.
