Poterium polygamum subsp. eriocarpum

Poterium polygamum subsp.

eriocarpum, commonly known as the silky-barked acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems covered in a dense layer of silky, white hairs.

The leaves are small, alternate, and pinnately compound, with each leaflet having a hairy underside. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in compact spikes, followed by woody, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
