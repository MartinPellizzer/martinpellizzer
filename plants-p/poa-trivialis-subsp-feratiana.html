Poa trivialis subsp. feratiana

Poa trivialis subsp.

feratiana, commonly known as the common bentgrass, is a low-growing grass species native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America. It typically forms dense, mat-like turf and is often found in lawns, meadows, and disturbed soils.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are usually green or purplish in color. It is a perennial species that spreads through rhizomes and is well adapted to a variety of soil conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it both a desirable groundcover and a common weed in cultivated areas.
