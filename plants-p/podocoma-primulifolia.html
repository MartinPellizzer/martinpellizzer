Podocoma primulifolia

Podocoma primulifolia, commonly known as the spring flower of the desert, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that bloom in early spring, often in shades of white or pale pink. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its root system is deep and drought-tolerant, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Podocoma primulifolia plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
