Penstemon cyananthus var. brandegeei

Penstemon cyananthus var. brandegeei, commonly known as the Brandegee's blue penstemon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and sunny environments, often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardiness.
