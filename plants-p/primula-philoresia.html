Primula philoresia

Primula philoresia, commonly known as the Philippine primrose, is a perennial herb native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing vibrant purple or pink flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
