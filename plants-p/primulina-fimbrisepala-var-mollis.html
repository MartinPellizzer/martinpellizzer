Primulina fimbrisepala var. mollis

Primulina fimbrisepala var. mollis, commonly known as the velvety primulina, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to maintain its lush appearance.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
