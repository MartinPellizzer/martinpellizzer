Piper pseudopsis

Piper pseudopsis, commonly known as the false black pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by three-lobed berries that turn dark when mature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
