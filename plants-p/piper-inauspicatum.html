Piper inauspicatum

Piper inauspicatum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, simple, and usually ovate or lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, slender fruits known as drupes. These fruits, when dried, are the source of the spice black pepper, which is widely used in cooking and traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
