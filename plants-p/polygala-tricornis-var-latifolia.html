Polygala tricornis var. latifolia

Polygala tricornis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved milkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
