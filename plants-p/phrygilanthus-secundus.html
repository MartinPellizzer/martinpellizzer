Phrygilanthus secundus

Phrygilanthus secundus, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a notable addition to gardens and landscapes.
