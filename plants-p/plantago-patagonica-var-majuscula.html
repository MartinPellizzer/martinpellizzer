Plantago patagonica var. majuscula

Plantago patagonica var. majuscula, commonly known as the Patagonian plantain, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces erect flowering stems topped with dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for respiratory and digestive ailments.
