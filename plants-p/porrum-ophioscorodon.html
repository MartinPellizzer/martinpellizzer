Porrum ophioscorodon

Porrum ophioscorodon, commonly known as the snakehead orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, snake-like appearance, with elongated, twisted flowers that resemble a serpent. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth where it can receive indirect sunlight. Its flowers are usually a mix of green and yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is often protected in its natural habitat.
