Peroa

Peroa, commonly known as the common yarrow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads that resemble small umbrellas, composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny areas and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Peroa has feathery, finely divided leaves that are aromatic when crushed.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
