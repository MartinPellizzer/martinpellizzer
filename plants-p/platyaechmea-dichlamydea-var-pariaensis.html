Platyaechmea dichlamydea var. pariaensis

Platyaechmea dichlamydea var. pariaensis, commonly known as the Pariensis Blueberry Plant, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that are often variegated with silver or white markings. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, which eventually give way to edible, blue-black berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and unique fruit, making it a popular choice for both gardens and botanical collections.
