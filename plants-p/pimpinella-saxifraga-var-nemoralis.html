Pimpinella saxifraga var. nemoralis

Pimpinella saxifraga var. nemoralis, commonly known as the wood anise, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland and hedgerow environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its aromatic foliage and historical uses in apothecary practices highlight its botanical and cultural significance.
