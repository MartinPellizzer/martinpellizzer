Pedilanthus petraeus

Pedilanthus petraeus, commonly known as the rock candelabra, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features a distinctive branching structure with thick, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to reduce water loss through transpiration.

Overall, Pedilanthus petraeus is a resilient and visually appealing plant well-suited to rocky and coastal habitats.
