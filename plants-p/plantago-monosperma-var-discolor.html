Plantago monosperma var. discolor

Plantago monosperma var. discolor, commonly known as the variegated plantain, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a striking variegation of green and white. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Plantaginaceae family.
