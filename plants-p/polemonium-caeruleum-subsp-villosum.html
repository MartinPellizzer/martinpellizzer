Polemonium caeruleum subsp. villosum

Polemonium caeruleum subsp.

villosum, commonly known as the softleaf bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America. It features slender, hairy stems that support its showy, bell-shaped flowers, which are typically blue to purple in color.

The plant's leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its common name. It thrives in moist, shady woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The species is closely related to other members of the Polemonium genus, which are known for their striking floral displays.
