Parthenocissus tricuspidata f. purpurea

Parthenocissus tricuspidata f. purpurea, commonly known as the purple ivy or purple crawling ivy, is a cultivar of the Virginia creeper.

It is characterized by its striking purple foliage, which transitions to deep red in the fall. The plant features tendrils that allow it to climb and spread across surfaces. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three lobed leaflets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
