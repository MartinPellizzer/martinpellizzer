Potamogeton × exilis

Potamogeton × exilis, commonly known as the slender pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton crispus.

It is typically found in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, slender leaves that are often submerged, with small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in clusters. It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Due to its hybrid nature, it can exhibit variability in morphology and is sometimes challenging to distinguish from its parent species.
