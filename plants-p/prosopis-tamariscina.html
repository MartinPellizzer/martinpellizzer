Prosopis tamariscina

Prosopis tamariscina, commonly known as the screwbean mesquite, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, twisted branches and small, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. The plant has small, compound leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of its distinctive seed pods.

Prosopis tamariscina is often used in reforestation projects due to its ability to thrive in poor soils and its value as a forage plant for livestock.
