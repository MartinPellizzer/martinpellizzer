Penstemon cephalanthus

Penstemon cephalanthus, commonly known as the yellow beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Penstemon cephalanthus is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
