Podostemum minus

Podostemum minus, commonly known as the lesser water starwort, is a unique aquatic plant found in fast-flowing streams and rivers.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves that are often submerged, adapted to the turbulent water environment. The plant has a specialized root system that anchors it to rocks, allowing it to thrive in high-energy aquatic habitats. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing above the water surface.

Podostemum minus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
