Pilosella armeniaca

Pilosella armeniaca, commonly known as Armenian hairgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It is native to regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Pilosella armeniaca is typically used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
