Pilosella brachiata subsp. bella

Pilosella brachiata subsp.

bella, commonly known as the bell pilosella, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb with a mat-forming habit, often found in grassy areas and rocky soils.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically hairy and lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
