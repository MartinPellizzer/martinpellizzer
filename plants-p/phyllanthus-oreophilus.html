Phyllanthus oreophilus

Phyllanthus oreophilus, commonly known as the mountain phyllanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, a characteristic feature of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
