Prunus communis proles elegans

Prunus communis proles elegans, commonly known as the elegant cherry, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, graceful branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that turn a vibrant red in the fall. The tree produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, followed by dark red, edible fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
