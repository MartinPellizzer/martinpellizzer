Paphiopedilum adductum f. cynthiae

Paphiopedilum adductum f. cynthiae, commonly known as the Cynthia's Paphiopedilum, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

This hybrid is characterized by its vibrant pink and white flowers with a distinctive, intricate lip that resembles a pouch, a feature typical of the Paphiopedilum genus. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It thrives in cool, humid environments with partial shade and requires well-draining, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly valued in horticultural circles.
