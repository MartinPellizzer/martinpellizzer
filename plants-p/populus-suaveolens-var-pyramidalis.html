Populus suaveolens var. pyramidalis

Populus suaveolens var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal poplar, is a variety of the trembling aspen.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins during the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
