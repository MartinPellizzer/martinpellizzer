Phyllanthus oblanceolatus

Phyllanthus oblanceolatus, commonly known as the broadleaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to oblanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
