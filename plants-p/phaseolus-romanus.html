Phaseolus romanus

Phaseolus romanus, commonly known as the Roman bean, is a species of legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces pods that contain several round, edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Phaseolus romanus is a valuable crop for both its nutritional content and its role in agricultural biodiversity.
