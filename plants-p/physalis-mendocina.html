Physalis mendocina

Physalis mendocina, commonly known as the Mendoza groundcherry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is characterized by its hairy, yellowish-green leaves and clusters of small, orange-red fruits that enclose a single seed. The plant is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and thrives in temperate climates. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and can grow in both wild and cultivated settings.

The fruit is edible when ripe and has a tangy, slightly sweet flavor, making it a popular choice for culinary use.
