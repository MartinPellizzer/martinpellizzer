Persicaria hololeion

Persicaria hololeion, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. The plant thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and stream banks, and is often found in disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes.

The leaves are typically used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
