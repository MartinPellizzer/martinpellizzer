Pritchardia remota

Pritchardia remota, commonly known as the Pritchardia tree, is a species of cycad native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of cycads. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland habitats.

Due to habitat loss and invasive species, Pritchardia remota is considered endangered and is a focus of conservation efforts.
