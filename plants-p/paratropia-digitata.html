Paratropia digitata

Paratropia digitata, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, alternate leaves that are often lobed or digitate, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by small, woody fruits. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

The sap of Paratropia digitata is milky and can be toxic if ingested, making it important to handle with care.
