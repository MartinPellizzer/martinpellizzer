Plantago dentata

Plantago dentata, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a rosette of broad, oval, and slightly toothed leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in a spike-like inflorescence during the summer months. It is adapted to grow in a variety of environments, including roadsides, fields, and disturbed soils.

The leaves and seeds of Plantago dentata have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
