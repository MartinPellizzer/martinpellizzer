Potentilla chamissonis var. umanakensis

Potentilla chamissonis var. umanakensis, commonly known as the Umanakensis cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or white. The plant has slender, wiry stems and hairy leaves that are usually alternate and oval in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
