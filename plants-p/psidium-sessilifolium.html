Psidium sessilifolium

Psidium sessilifolium, commonly known as the pineapple guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It produces small, fragrant, and edible fruits that are typically yellow or red in color. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are usually clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, tropical flavor and is often used in culinary applications.
