Polygonum sodofolium

Polygonum sodofolium, commonly known as the Chinese knotweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its stout, slightly hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Polygonum sodofolium is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
