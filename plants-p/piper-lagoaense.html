Piper lagoaense

Piper lagoaense, commonly known as the Lagoaense pepper or Brazilian pepper, is a tropical shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly glossy texture.

Piper lagoaense is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in local remedies for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
