Phaseolus vulgaris var. icterinus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. icterinus, commonly known as the golden bean, is a variant of the common bean that is distinguished by its striking yellow foliage and pods.

This plant is native to the Americas and is cultivated for its edible seeds, which are typically harvested when mature. The leaves are trifoliate, with a bright yellow coloration that sets it apart from other bean varieties. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

The golden bean is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its nutritional content and culinary versatility.
