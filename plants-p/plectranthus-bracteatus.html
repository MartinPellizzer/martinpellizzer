Plectranthus bracteatus

Plectranthus bracteatus, commonly known as the velvetleaf plectranthus, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive texture and appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
