Panicum nashianum

Panicum nashianum, commonly known as Nash's panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands and disturbed soils, exhibiting a clump-forming habit with slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous narrow, bristly flowers. Its leaves are linear and often have a bluish-green hue, with a hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland areas.
