Poa ayseniensis

Poa ayseniensis, commonly known as the Aysen bentgrass, is a species of grass native to the southern Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, tufted habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, compact flower clusters.

Poa ayseniensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
