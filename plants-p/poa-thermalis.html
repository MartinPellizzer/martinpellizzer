Poa thermalis

Poa thermalis, commonly known as the thermal grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Poa thermalis is adapted to grow in moist, temperate environments, often found in wetlands and along streambanks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
