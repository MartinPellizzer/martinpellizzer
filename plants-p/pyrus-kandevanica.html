Pyrus kandevanica

Pyrus kandevanica, commonly known as the Kandevana pear, is a species of deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy white flowers that appear in spring. The fruit is a small, yellowish pear that ripens in late summer and is edible, though not widely cultivated for commercial use.

This plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.
