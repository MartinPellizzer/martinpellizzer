Polygonum alpinum var. foliosum

Polygonum alpinum var. foliosum, commonly known as the alpine knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or purple, appearing in late summer.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in harsh, cold environments.
