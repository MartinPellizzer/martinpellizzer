Pulmonaria montana subsp. jurana

Pulmonaria montana subsp.

jurana, commonly known as the alpine lungwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features basal rosettes of velvety, spotted leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to naturalize in suitable habitats.
