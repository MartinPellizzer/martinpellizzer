Pleiarina crateradenia

Pleiarina crateradenia, commonly known as the mountain rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant features a compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate orchid-like flowers in shades of white and pale pink. Its flowering period occurs during the spring and early summer months.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
