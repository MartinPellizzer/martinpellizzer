Paspalum suffultum

Paspalum suffultum, commonly known as the tall paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Paspalum suffultum is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and open fields.
