Phyllostachys rutila

Phyllostachys rutila, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms that contrast with its dark green leaves. This bamboo is a clump-forming plant that can grow up to 10 meters tall, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping. Its rapid growth rate and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types contribute to its widespread cultivation.

The plant is also valued for its use in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of sustainable materials.
