Pinus griffithiana

Pinus griffithiana, commonly known as the Griffiths' pine, is a tall conifer native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its long, slender needles and distinctive, often drooping cones. The tree can reach heights of up to 60 meters and has a slow growth rate. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against fire and drought.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
