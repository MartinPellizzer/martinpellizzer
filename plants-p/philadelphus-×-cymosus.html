Philadelphus × cymosus

Philadelphus × cymosus, commonly known as the common mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, greyish-barked stem. It typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to urban environments.
