Phyllostachys nigra var. fulva

Phyllostachys nigra var. fulva, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a variant of the black bamboo species.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms that contrast with its dark green leaves. This plant is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is native to eastern Asia. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping and bamboo grove cultivation.
