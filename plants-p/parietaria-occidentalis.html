Parietaria occidentalis

Parietaria occidentalis, commonly known as the western wall-rocket, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and meadows.

The species is characterized by its ability to spread rapidly through seeds and its tolerance to various environmental conditions.
