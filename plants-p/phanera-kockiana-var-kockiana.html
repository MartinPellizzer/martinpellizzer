Phanera kockiana var. kockiana

Phanera kockiana var. kockiana, commonly known as the Kock's phanera, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptic or ovate in shape. Its flowers are clustered in inflorescences and are characterized by their showy, pink to red hues.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
