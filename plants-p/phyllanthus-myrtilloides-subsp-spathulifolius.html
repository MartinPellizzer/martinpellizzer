Phyllanthus myrtilloides subsp. spathulifolius

Phyllanthus myrtilloides subsp.

spathulifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved phyllanthus, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It features small, elliptic to ovate leaves that are usually glabrous and have a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense inflorescences, followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
