Paeonia tenuifolia

Paeonia tenuifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf peony, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, and it is relatively hardy in temperate climates.

Paeonia tenuifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
