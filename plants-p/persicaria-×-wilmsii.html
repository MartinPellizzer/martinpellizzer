Persicaria × wilmsii

Persicaria × wilmsii, commonly known as the hybrid persicaria or sometimes called the red-veined sorrel, is a perennial flowering plant that belongs to the Polygonaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Persicaria amplexicaulis and Persicaria hydropiper, resulting in a plant with unique characteristics. The leaves are heart-shaped with reddish veins, and the flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in spikes. It is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in moist conditions.

This plant is native to Europe but has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
