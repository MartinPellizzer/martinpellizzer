Persea chuanchienensis

Persea chuanchienensis, commonly known as the Chuanchien avocado, is a rare and lesser-known species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and avocado. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its flowers are small and fragrant, typically appearing in clusters, and it produces a fruit similar to the common avocado, though less widely cultivated.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
