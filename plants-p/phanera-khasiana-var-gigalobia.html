Phanera khasiana var. gigalobia

Phanera khasiana var. gigalobia, commonly known as the giant Khasi phanera, is a striking plant native to the northeastern region of India.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The flowers are typically pink to purple in color and attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies. This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
