Passiflora purdiei

Passiflora purdiei, commonly known as the Pseudopapaya Passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces orange to yellow berries that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Passifloraceae family.
