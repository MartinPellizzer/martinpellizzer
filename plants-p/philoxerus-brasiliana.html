Philoxerus brasiliana

Philoxerus brasiliana, commonly known as the Brazilian burr palm, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its clustered growth habit and distinctive, spiny, fruiting structures. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody, spiky fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
