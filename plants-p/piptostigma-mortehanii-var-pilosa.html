Piptostigma mortehanii var. pilosa

Piptostigma mortehanii var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy piptostigma, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant has hairy foliage, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals.

This species is adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils and can tolerate extreme temperature fluctuations.
