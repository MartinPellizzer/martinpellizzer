Philodendron remifolium subsp. remifolium

Philodendron remifolium subsp.

remifolium, commonly known as the Emerald Queen philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a slightly waxy texture.

The plant has a robust stem that can grow several meters long, often requiring support as it climbs. Its foliage is known for its deep, rich color and striking appearance, making it a popular houseplant.

It prefers bright, indirect light and moderate humidity, thriving in warm, moist environments.
