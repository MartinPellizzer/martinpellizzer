Pleurothallis lancilabris var. oxyglossa

Pleurothallis lancilabris var. oxyglossa, commonly known as the lance-leafed orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that is often more vibrant in color.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique morphology and relatively easy care requirements.
