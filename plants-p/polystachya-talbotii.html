Polystachya talbotii

Polystachya talbotii, commonly known as the Talbot's spikegrass, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with elongated, linear leaves. The plant produces inflorescences of small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in spikes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is typically found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique morphology, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
