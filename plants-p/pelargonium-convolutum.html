Pelargonium convolutum

Pelargonium convolutum, commonly known as the spiral geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium convolutum is also valued for its aromatic foliage and drought tolerance.
