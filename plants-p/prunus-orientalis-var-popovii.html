Prunus orientalis var. popovii

Prunus orientalis var. popovii, commonly known as the Caucasian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black, edible fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in jams. The plant features simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers in early spring. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine.
