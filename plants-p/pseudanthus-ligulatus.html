Pseudanthus ligulatus

Pseudanthus ligulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy yellow ray florets surrounded by tubular disk florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical structure supports both ornamental value and ecological roles in attracting pollinators.
