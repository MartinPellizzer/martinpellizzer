Polygala nyikensis

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, sparsely branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to the harsh, cold conditions of its mountainous habitat. Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple and attract various pollinators, including bees and flies.
