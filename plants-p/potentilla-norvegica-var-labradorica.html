Potentilla norvegica var. labradorica

Potentilla norvegica var. labradorica, commonly known as Labrador rose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America.

It features small, pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has wiry, thornless stems and broad, oval leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in tundra and alpine habitats.

Its hardy nature makes it adaptable to cold climates and a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
