Prunus mutabilis f. avicennae

Prunus mutabilis f. avicennae, commonly known as the Avicenna cherry, is a unique horticultural form of the wild cherry tree.

It is characterized by its variable flower color, which can range from white to pink and even red, depending on environmental conditions. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and has been cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while the fruit is a small, dark red cherry.

This variety is prized for its adaptability and aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
