Polemonium caeruleum

Polemonium caeruleum, commonly known as bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and alternate along the stem, often with a bluish-green hue. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with rich, well-drained soil.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
