Pleurothallis chamelopoda

Pleurothallis chamelopoda, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with intricate patterns, which are highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, strap-like leaves.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
