Polygala bryoides var. abietina

Polygala bryoides var. abietina, commonly known as the silverleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has slender, hairy stems and compound leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system to access moisture.
