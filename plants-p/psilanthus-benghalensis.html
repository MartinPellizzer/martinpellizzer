Psilanthus benghalensis

Psilanthus benghalensis, commonly known as the Indian sandalwood or Benghal sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant wood and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating, while its bark is rough and fissured.

Psilanthus benghalensis is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in medicine and perfumery.
