Pyrus × mecsekensis

Pyrus × mecsekensis, commonly known as the Mecsek pear, is a hybrid species native to Hungary.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 5–8 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible pears in the autumn. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

The fruit is valued for its sweet and aromatic flavor, making it a notable cultivar in regional horticulture.
