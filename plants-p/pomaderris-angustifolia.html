Pomaderris angustifolia

Pomaderris angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leafed pomaderris, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It is often found in dry sclerophyll forests and is adapted to well-drained soils.

Pomaderris angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native garden landscapes.
