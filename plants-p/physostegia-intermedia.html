Physostegia intermedia

Physostegia intermedia, commonly known as the whorled pogostemon, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves and spikes of purple or pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its tubular flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Physostegia intermedia is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
