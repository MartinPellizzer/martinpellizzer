Phymatodes porrecta

Phymatodes porrecta, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of thick, fleshy, and often hairy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, spiny fruits. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Phymatodes porrecta plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
