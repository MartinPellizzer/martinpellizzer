Primula blandula

Primula blandula, commonly known as the common primrose, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers, which bloom in early spring, are usually pale yellow or cream with a darker throat.

Primula blandula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
