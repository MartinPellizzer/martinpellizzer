Parodia tuberculosicostata var. cafayatensis

Parodia tuberculosicostata var. cafayatensis, commonly known as the Cafayate Cactus, is a compact, globular cactus native to Argentina.

It features a ribbed, spiny stem with a distinctive tuberculate texture, and its spines are typically white to yellow in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
