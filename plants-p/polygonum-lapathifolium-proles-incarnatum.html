Polygonum lapathifolium proles incarnatum

Polygonum lapathifolium proles incarnatum, commonly known as the red-veined sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional herbal medicine.
