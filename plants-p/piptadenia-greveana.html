Piptadenia greveana

Piptadenia greveana, commonly known as the greenheart tree, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its hard, durable wood that is highly valued in the timber industry. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellowish flowers that are often clustered in terminal spikes.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in various soil types and its role in providing habitat for local wildlife.
