Pandanus hasskarlii

Pandanus hasskarlii, commonly known as the hairy pandanus, is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow, fibrous leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, silky hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by distinctive, spiky fruits. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove or swampy areas.

The plant has cultural and practical uses, including in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber for weaving.
