Pyrus evansii

Pyrus evansii, commonly known as the Evans pear, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The tree produces small, yellowish pears that are typically not suitable for commercial cultivation but are valued for their ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Pyrus evansii is also noted for its adaptability to various climates and its role in supporting local wildlife.
