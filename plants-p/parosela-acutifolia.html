Parosela acutifolia

Parosela acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved parosela, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in dense clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

Parosela acutifolia is valued in horticulture for its resilience and unique appearance.
