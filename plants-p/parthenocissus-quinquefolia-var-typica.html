Parthenocissus quinquefolia var. typica

Parthenocissus quinquefolia var. typica, commonly known as the Virginia creeper, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which typically consist of five leaflets, though they may vary in shape and size. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the summer, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its tendrils allow it to climb and attach to surfaces, making it a common sight along fences and buildings.

The leaves turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn, adding ornamental value to the landscape.
