Plagiostachys corrugata

Plagiostachys corrugata, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and distinctive zigzagging stems, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.

Plagiostachys corrugata is valued for its ecological importance in supporting aquatic and wetland ecosystems.
