Penstemon whitedii subsp. tristis

Penstemon whitedii subsp.

tristis, commonly known as the White Mountain penstemon, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pale pink to white, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
