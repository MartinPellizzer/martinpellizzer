Pitcairnia brongniartiana var. variegata

Pitcairnia brongniartiana var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated pitcairnia, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings, adding to its ornamental appeal. The leaves are typically stiff and covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention and protection from intense sunlight. It produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that can range from red to orange, enhancing its visual impact in gardens or indoor settings.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for both horticultural enthusiasts and botanical collections.
