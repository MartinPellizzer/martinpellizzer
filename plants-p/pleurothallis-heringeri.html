Pleurothallis heringeri

Pleurothallis heringeri, commonly known as the Heringer's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-white to pale yellow blooms that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
