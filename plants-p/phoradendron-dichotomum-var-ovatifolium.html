Phoradendron dichotomum var. ovatifolium

Phoradendron dichotomum var. ovatifolium, commonly known as the ovate-leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, ovate-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along the stem. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered together, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits. Its roots form haustoria, which penetrate the host tree's vascular system to extract water and nutrients.

This species plays an important ecological role by providing food for birds and other animals while also influencing the health of its host trees.
