Pleurothallis pudica

Pleurothallis pudica, commonly known as the "pudica orchid," is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often have a unique coloration. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that help it thrive in low-light conditions.

Pleurothallis pudica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
