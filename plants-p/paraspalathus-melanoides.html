Paraspalathus melanoides

Paraspalathus melanoides, commonly known as the black leaved spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and dark green, sometimes almost black, foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the spurge family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
