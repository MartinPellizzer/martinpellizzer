Phyllanthus lalambensis

Phyllanthus lalambensis, commonly known as the African date tree, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically consumed raw or processed into various products. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Due to its adaptability, it is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
