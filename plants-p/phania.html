Phania

Phania, commonly known as the spider plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are followed by clusters of red berries. Phania is well-known for its ability to thrive in a variety of conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its air-purifying qualities and ease of care further enhance its appeal among plant enthusiasts.
