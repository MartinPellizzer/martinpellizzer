Phyllostachys quilioi var. marliacea

Phyllostachys quilioi var. marliacea, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a variant of the silver bamboo species native to China.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms that contrast with its dark green leaves. This bamboo thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.

The plant is also valued for its use in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of sustainable materials.
