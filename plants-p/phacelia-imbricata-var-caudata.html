Phacelia imbricata var. caudata

Phacelia imbricata var. caudata, commonly known as the long-horned phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in dry, open habitats.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
