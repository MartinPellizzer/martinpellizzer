Polygala poaya var. major

Polygala poaya var. major, commonly known as the large-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers are usually pale blue or violet, and it produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
