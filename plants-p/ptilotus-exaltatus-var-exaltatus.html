Ptilotus exaltatus var. exaltatus

Ptilotus exaltatus var. exaltatus, commonly known as the "Desert Flax," is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is a herbaceous annual that features striking, spindly stems and bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that reduce water loss, adapting to its harsh environment. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that aid in its propagation across sandy soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing desert ecosystems.
