Pedilonum treubii

Pedilonum treubii, commonly known as the creeping orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread along the ground. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Pedilonum treubii prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
