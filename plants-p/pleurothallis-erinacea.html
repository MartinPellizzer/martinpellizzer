Pleurothallis erinacea

Pleurothallis erinacea, commonly known as the pink pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. Its flowers are often pollinated by moths and bees, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
