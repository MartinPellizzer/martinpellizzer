Plectopoma gloxiniflorum

Plectopoma gloxiniflorum, commonly known as the Gloxinia orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family, known for its striking, showy flowers. The plant features long, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive spur at the base of the petals.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
