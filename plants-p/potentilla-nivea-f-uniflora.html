Potentilla nivea f. uniflora

Potentilla nivea f. uniflora, commonly known as the alpine snow willow, is a low-growing shrub native to alpine regions of Asia.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters of one or two. The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is distinguished by its single-flower or double-flower inflorescence, making it a unique and ornamental species in alpine gardens.
