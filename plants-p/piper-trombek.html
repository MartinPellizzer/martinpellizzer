Piper trombek

Piper trombek, commonly known as the Indian black pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its dark, peppery berries that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, green flowers that develop into the aromatic, black or white peppercorns. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The botanical features of Piper trombek make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
