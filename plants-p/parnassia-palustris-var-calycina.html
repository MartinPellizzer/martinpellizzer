Parnassia palustris var. calycina

Parnassia palustris var. calycina, commonly known as the bog buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features showy white flowers with yellow stamens, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has smooth, waxy leaves that are often submerged or floating in shallow water. It is adapted to nutrient-poor, acidic environments and plays a role in wetland ecosystems.

Its presence indicates healthy, undisturbed bog or marsh habitats.
