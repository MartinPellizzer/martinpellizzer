Phragmipedium andreettae

Phragmipedium andreettae, commonly known as the Andreetta's slipper orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped pouch that serves as a trap for insects. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and has lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its rarity and beauty.
