Picea nobilis var. coerulea

Picea nobilis var. coerulea, commonly known as the Blue Spruce, is a variant of the noble fir that exhibits striking blue-gray foliage.

Its needles are short, stiff, and covered in a waxy cuticle that contributes to its distinctive coloration. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of coniferous species. It is native to mountainous regions and thrives in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
