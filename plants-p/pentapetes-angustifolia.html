Pentapetes angustifolia

Pentapetes angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pentapetes, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and are characterized by their five petals, giving the genus its name.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release the seeds.
