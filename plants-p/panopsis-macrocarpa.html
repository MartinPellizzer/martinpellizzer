Panopsis macrocarpa

Panopsis macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited panopsis, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malvaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its woody structure and showy, pentagonal flowers. The plant produces large, fleshy fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a thick, fleshy aril. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
