Paphiopedilum insigne var. speciosum

Paphiopedilum insigne var. speciosum, commonly known as the Orchid of the Gods, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with a unique pouch-like lip that serves as a pollination mechanism. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its foliage consists of broad, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle.

This variety is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity in cultivation.
