Pinus × celakovskiorum

Pinus × celakovskiorum, commonly known as the Celakovsky pine, is a hybrid conifer resulting from the cross between Pinus sylvestris and Pinus mugo.

It is characterized by its dense, slow-growing foliage and a compact, rounded form, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping. The tree produces small, oval cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, and its needles are short, stiff, and often retain their color year-round. It thrives in well-drained soils and is relatively cold-hardy, though it prefers full sun for optimal growth.

This hybrid is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
