Phyllanthus vacciniifolius

Phyllanthus vacciniifolius, commonly known as the wax myrtle or southern wax myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often coated with a waxy cuticle, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces dark blue to black berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist, acidic soils.
