Pithecellobium cateniforme

Pithecellobium cateniforme, commonly known as the catena acacia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features slender, serpentine stems that often grow along the ground, forming dense mats. Its small, bipinnate leaves are compound and provide a lush green appearance.

The flowers are yellow and clustered, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
