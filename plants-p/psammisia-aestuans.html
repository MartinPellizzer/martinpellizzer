Psammisia aestuans

Psammisia aestuans, commonly known as the salt marsh morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in brackish or saline environments, often found in marshes and estuaries. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple, blooming in the early morning. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often covered in a fine, silky hair.

Psammisia aestuans plays an important ecological role by stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
