Potentilla bhutanica

Potentilla bhutanica, commonly known as Bhutan barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayan regions of Bhutan and adjacent areas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces red or orange berries in the autumn. Potentilla bhutanica is often found in rocky, well-drained soils at higher elevations.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local medicine.
