Populus suaveolens f. pyramidalis

Populus suaveolens f. pyramidalis, commonly known as the narrowleaf poplar or pyramidal poplar, is a cultivar of the poplar tree characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit.

It is a deciduous tree with heart-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a fragrant aroma. The plant produces small, yellowish-green catkins in the spring, which are its primary means of pollination. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources such as rivers and streams.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide rapid screening in landscaping.
