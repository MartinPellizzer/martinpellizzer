Potamogeton pectinatus f. brachypus

Potamogeton pectinatus f. brachypus, commonly known as the short-stemmed pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of mucilage. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like clusters, and it is pollinated by wind rather than insects. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its dense growth, it can also contribute to water clarity by stabilizing sediment in shallow waters.
