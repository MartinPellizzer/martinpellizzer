Poa pilata

Poa pilata, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in cool, moist environments. The plant has narrow, bluish-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Poa pilata is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
