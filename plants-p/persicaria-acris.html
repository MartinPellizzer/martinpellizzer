Persicaria acris

Persicaria acris, commonly known as roseate knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and stream banks. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers with five petals. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Persicaria acris is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage.
