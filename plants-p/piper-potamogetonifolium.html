Piper potamogetonifolium

Piper potamogetonifolium, commonly known as the water pepper, is a tropical flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other species known for their aromatic properties. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that grow in clusters. It typically thrives in wetland environments, often found near streams and marshes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies related to wetland flora.
