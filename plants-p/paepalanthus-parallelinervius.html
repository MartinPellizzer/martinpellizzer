Paepalanthus parallelinervius

Paepalanthus parallelinervius, commonly known as the Parallelinerved Puya, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to the Andean regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that bears numerous brightly colored, tubular flowers, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a rosette pattern, forming a central pool that can collect rainwater. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, humid conditions and well-drained soil.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
