Podochilus bancanus

Podochilus bancanus, commonly known as the banana orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, banana-shaped inflorescence. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often brightly colored, with a unique structure that includes a labellum for attracting pollinators.

Podochilus bancanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
