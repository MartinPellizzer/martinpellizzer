Papaver croceum var. glabrifolium

Papaver croceum var. glabrifolium, commonly known as the smooth-leaved poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and hairless, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
