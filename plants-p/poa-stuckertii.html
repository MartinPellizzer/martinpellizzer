Poa stuckertii

Poa stuckertii, commonly known as Stuckert's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and bluish-green foliage. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Poa stuckertii is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
