Plantago bellardii subsp. deflexa

Plantago bellardii subsp.

deflexa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to parts of North America. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as along roadsides, in wetlands, and in open woods.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in a spike-like inflorescence during the summer months.

This species is known for its adaptability and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
