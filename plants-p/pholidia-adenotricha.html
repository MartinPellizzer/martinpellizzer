Pholidia adenotricha

Pholidia adenotricha, commonly known as the roughleaf tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

The tree is valued for its hard wood and traditional medicinal uses.
