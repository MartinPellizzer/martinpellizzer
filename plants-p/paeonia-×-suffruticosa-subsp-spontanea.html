Paeonia × suffruticosa subsp. spontanea

Paeonia × suffruticosa subsp.

spontanea, commonly known as the Chinese tree peony, is a deciduous shrub native to China. It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters and produces fragrant blooms in late spring. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it has a long flowering period that can last several weeks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
