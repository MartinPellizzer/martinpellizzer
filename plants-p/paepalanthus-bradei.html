Paepalanthus bradei

Paepalanthus bradei, commonly known as the Brade's pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a small, globular cactus with a dense covering of spines that protect it from herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its spiny structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
