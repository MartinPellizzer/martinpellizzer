Pollinia villosa

Pollinia villosa, commonly known as the hairy pollinia, is a unique plant species characterized by its specialized reproductive structures called pollinia.

These pollinia are clustered and covered in fine, hairy structures, which aid in their attachment to pollinators. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its intricate floral morphology.

Due to its specific pollination mechanism, it plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native habitats.
