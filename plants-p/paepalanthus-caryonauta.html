Paepalanthus caryonauta

Paepalanthus caryonauta, commonly known as the golden cup-moss, is a unique member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is a terrestrial bromeliad characterized by its dense, overlapping leaves that form a rosette shape. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette, adding a vibrant touch to its appearance. It is native to the Andean regions of South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
