Pediomelum subulatum

Pediomelum subulatum, commonly known as the needleleaf pediomelum, is a small, perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in dense clusters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its unique foliage and compact growth habit, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
