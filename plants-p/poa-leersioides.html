Poa leersioides

Poa leersioides, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually smooth and slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, cylindrical flower clusters.

Poa leersioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland areas.
