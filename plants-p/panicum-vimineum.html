Panicum vimineum

Panicum vimineum, commonly known as silky grass or silky panicum, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and produces fluffy, pale green to brown seed heads. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies. It is drought-tolerant and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and erosion control.
