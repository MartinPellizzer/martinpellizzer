Pilea affinis

Pilea affinis, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to China.

It features rosettes of thick, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically light green with a slight variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or green, which are followed by spherical, berry-like fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its low maintenance and attractive foliage make it a favored houseplant worldwide.
