Pastinaca armena subsp. armena

Pastinaca armena subsp.

armena, commonly known as Armenian burdock, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the sunflower family. Its roots are thick and fibrous, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or grassy habitats.
