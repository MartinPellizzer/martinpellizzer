Protium cerradicola

Protium cerradicola, commonly known as Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces small, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its bark and resin have been traditionally used for their medicinal properties.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
