Polyalthia fruticans

Polyalthia fruticans, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforests or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in tropical regions.
