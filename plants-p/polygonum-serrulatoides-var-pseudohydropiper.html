Polygonum serrulatoides var. pseudohydropiper

Polygonum serrulatoides var. pseudohydropiper, commonly known as the narrow-leaf smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red, appearing in late summer to early fall.

It is often found in riparian zones and is adapted to tolerate periodic flooding.
