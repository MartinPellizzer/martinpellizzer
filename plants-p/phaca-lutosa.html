Phaca lutosa

Phaca lutosa, commonly known as the swamp bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to wetland habitats in Africa and Madagascar.

It possesses small, bladder-like structures that trap and digest aquatic insects, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor environments. The plant has thin, floating leaves that help it remain buoyant in water, while its roots are often submerged. It reproduces through both sexual and asexual means, with seeds and vegetative propagation contributing to its spread.

Its unique adaptations make it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.
