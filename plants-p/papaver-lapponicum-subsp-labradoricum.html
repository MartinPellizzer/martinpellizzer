Papaver lapponicum subsp. labradoricum

Papaver lapponicum subsp.

labradoricum, commonly known as Labrador poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America. It produces striking, showy flowers that are typically bright red or orange in color, with a prominent central stigma.

The plant has a short, sturdy stem and basal leaves that are broadly ovate to round in shape. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and tundra environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with cold-hardy conditions.
