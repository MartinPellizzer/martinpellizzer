Phragmipedium longifolium

Phragmipedium longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved phragmipedium, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant features long, strap-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This orchid is popular among horticulturists for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
