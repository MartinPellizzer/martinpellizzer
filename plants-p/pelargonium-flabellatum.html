Pelargonium flabellatum

Pelargonium flabellatum, commonly known as the fan-leaf geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium flabellatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
