Prunus pumila subsp. susquehanae

Prunus pumila subsp.

susquehanae, commonly known as the Susquehanna dwarf cherry, is a low-growing shrub native to the eastern United States. It typically reaches a height of less than 1 meter and features small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by dark red, edible berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
