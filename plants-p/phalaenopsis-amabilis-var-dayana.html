Phalaenopsis amabilis var. dayana

Phalaenopsis amabilis var. dayana, commonly known as the Dayana Phalaenopsis, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, gracefully curved petals and a prominent, showy lip that often features a distinctive color contrast. The plant typically produces clusters of fragrant, white or light pink flowers that bloom repeatedly throughout the year. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
