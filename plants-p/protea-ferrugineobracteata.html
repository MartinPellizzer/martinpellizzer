Protea ferrugineobracteata

Protea ferrugineobracteata, commonly known as the rusty bract protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers surrounded by reddish-brown bracts. The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, rusty-colored down.

Protea ferrugineobracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays an important role in local ecosystems.
