Phyllanthus hurlimannii

Phyllanthus hurlimannii, commonly known as the African spurge, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, three-lobed capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

Phyllanthus hurlimannii is also known for its potential medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
