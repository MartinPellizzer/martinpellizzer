Pelargonium inaequilobum

Pelargonium inaequilobum, commonly known as the unequal-lobed geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb with distinctive, irregularly lobed leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long flowering period.
