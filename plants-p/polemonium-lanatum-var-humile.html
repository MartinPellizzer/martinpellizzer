Polemonium lanatum var. humile

Polemonium lanatum var. humile, commonly known as the smooth bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to white, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. It prefers moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
