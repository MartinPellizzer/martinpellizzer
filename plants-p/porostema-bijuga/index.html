Porostema Bijuga

Porostema morning glory, scientifically known as Porostema bijuga, is a striking flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America. With its vibrant blue and violet petals, it stands out as a unique member of the Convolvulaceae family. This species is often found in shaded, moist environments, thriving in the undergrowth of rainforests. Its trumpet-shaped blooms attract a variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees. Despite its beauty, Porostema bijuga remains relatively obscure in horticultural circles, making it a rare and fascinating plant for botanists and enthusiasts alike.
