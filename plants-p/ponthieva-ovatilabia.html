Ponthieva ovatilabia

Ponthieva ovatilabia, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact rosette of velvety, waxy leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom from the leaf axils. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Ponthieva ovatilabia is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
