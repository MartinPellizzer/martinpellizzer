Passerina villosa

Passerina villosa, commonly known as the woolly pasqueflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green stems and showy, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Passerina villosa is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming and unique foliage.
