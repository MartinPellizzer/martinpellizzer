Pleurothallis gustavoi

Pleurothallis gustavoi, commonly known as the Gustavo's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elongated and grass-like, adapted to the humid and shaded conditions of its natural habitat.

Pleurothallis gustavoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
