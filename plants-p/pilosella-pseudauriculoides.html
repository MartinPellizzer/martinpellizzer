Pilosella pseudauriculoides

Pilosella pseudauriculoides, commonly known as the false ear-shaped hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its hairy, leafy stems and yellow composite flowers. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Pilosella pseudauriculoides is often found in disturbed habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
