Pentanisia glaucescens

Pentanisia glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green pentanisia, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish-green coating. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
