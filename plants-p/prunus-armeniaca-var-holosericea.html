Prunus armeniaca var. holosericea

Prunus armeniaca var. holosericea, commonly known as the Persian white apricot, is a cultivar of the apricot tree characterized by its pale yellow to white fruit.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is prized for its delicate flavor and ornamental value. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, which are typically pink or white. Its leaves are ovate and dark green, providing a lush backdrop for the blossoms.

The fruit ripens in late summer and is known for its tender flesh and sweet aroma.
