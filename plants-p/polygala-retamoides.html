Polygala retamoides

Polygala retamoides, commonly known as the Portuguese milk-vetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, while its flowers are usually pale blue or white with a distinctive, spurred shape.

Polygala retamoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
