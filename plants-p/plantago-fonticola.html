Plantago fonticola

Plantago fonticola, commonly known as the spring plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense spikes, which appear in early spring.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
