Pithecellobium candidum

Pithecellobium candidum, commonly known as the white pod tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, white, pod-like fruits that resemble beans, which are edible and often used in local cuisines. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible pods.

The plant's ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
