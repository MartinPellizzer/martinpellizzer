Patonia walkeri

Patonia walkeri, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair, helping to reduce water loss in its dry environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in sandy soils.

Patonia walkeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
