Panicum uvulatum

Panicum uvulatum, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the warm temperate and tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically produces vibrant red or purple flowers, which are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for landscaping in arid and coastal areas.

Due to its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil, it is also valued for ecological and erosion control purposes.
