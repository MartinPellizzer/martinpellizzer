Prunus spachiana f. rosea

Prunus spachiana f. rosea, commonly known as the pink-flowered Japanese maple, is a cultivar of the Prunus spachiana species, which is native to Japan.

This plant is characterized by its striking pink to red foliage, which changes with the seasons, making it a popular ornamental tree. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth margin and a glossy surface. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

Prunus spachiana f. rosea is often used in gardens for its aesthetic value and is relatively low maintenance once established.
