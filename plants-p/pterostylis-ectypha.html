Pterostylis ectypha

Pterostylis ectypha, commonly known as the Western Pterostylis, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Australia.

It is a small, ground-dwelling plant with a slender, hairy stem and a single, narrow leaf. The flower is typically pale green or white, with a distinctive, elongated labellum that resembles a bird's beak. It blooms during the autumn season and is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forests and heathlands.

This orchid is part of the orchid family and is known for its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
