Paphiopedilum jackii var. hiepii

Paphiopedilum jackii var. hiepii, commonly known as the Hiepii Paphiopedilum, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green to brownish leaves and its showy, waxy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange with red and purple markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. It produces a single, elongated inflorescence with several flowers, each featuring a unique pouch-like lip that aids in pollination.

This variety is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to its rarity and ornamental value.
