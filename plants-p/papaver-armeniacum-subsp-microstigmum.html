Papaver armeniacum subsp. microstigmum

Papaver armeniacum subsp.

microstigmum, commonly known as Armenian poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey. It is characterized by its striking, saucer-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has smooth, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its capsule fruit contains numerous small, brown seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
