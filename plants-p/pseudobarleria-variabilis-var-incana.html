Pseudobarleria variabilis var. incana

Pseudobarleria variabilis var. incana, commonly known as silverleaf false brome, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for study in ecological and horticultural contexts.
