Poa cockayneana

Poa cockayneana, commonly known as Cockayne's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and forms dense, low-growing tufts. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is usually pale green to brown in color.

Poa cockayneana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
