Pterogaillonia

Pterogaillonia, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are compound, with multiple leaflets arranged in a feather-like pattern.

Pterogaillonia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
