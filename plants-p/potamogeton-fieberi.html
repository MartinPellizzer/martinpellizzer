Potamogeton fieberi

Potamogeton fieberi, commonly known as the narrowleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 2 millimeters wide. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spike-like clusters, and it reproduces both sexually and asexually through vegetative propagation. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in lakes, ponds, and streams.

Potamogeton fieberi plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various waterfowl and invertebrates.
