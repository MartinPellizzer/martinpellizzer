Prunus × lannesiana f. albida

Prunus × lannesiana f. albida, commonly known as the white-flowered cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus characterized by its striking white petals.

It is a hybrid between Prunus lannesiana and Prunus × yedoensis, exhibiting ornamental value due to its elegant floral display. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, with lanceolate leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The white blossoms appear in early spring, adding a delicate beauty to gardens and landscapes.
