Protea micrantha

Protea micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered protea, is a species native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often showy floral structures. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, white to pinkish flowers surrounded by a leafy bract.

Protea micrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
