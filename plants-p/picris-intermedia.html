Picris intermedia

Picris intermedia, commonly known as St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its scientific name reflects its historical use in herbal medicine.
