Pleurothallis ptychophora

Pleurothallis ptychophora, commonly known as the yellow pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features intricate, often fragrant flowers with a distinctive yellow coloration. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
