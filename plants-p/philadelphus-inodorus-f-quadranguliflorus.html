Philadelphus inodorus f. quadranguliflorus

Philadelphus inodorus f. quadranguliflorus, commonly known as the square-flowered mock orange, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its fragrant, white, four-angled flowers.

The plant belongs to the family Rosaceae and is native to parts of Asia, particularly China. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a rough texture. The stems are woody and often have a square cross-section, which is a distinguishing feature of the variety.

It is valued in horticulture for its showy blooms and adaptability to various soil conditions.
