Papaya cauliflora

Papaya cauliflora, commonly known as the wild papaya, is a tropical plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and produces flowers and fruits directly on its stems, a phenomenon known as cauliflorous growth. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Its fruit, which is similar to the common papaya, is edible and contains a high amount of vitamin C. The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
