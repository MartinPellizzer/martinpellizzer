Psyllocarpus laricoides var. densifolius

Psyllocarpus laricoides var. densifolius, commonly known as the dense-leaved ironwood, is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and thick, woody trunk, which contributes to its common name. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often clustered at the base of the petiole, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits, which are typical of the Fabaceae family.
