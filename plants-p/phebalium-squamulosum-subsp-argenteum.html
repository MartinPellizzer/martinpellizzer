Phebalium squamulosum subsp. argenteum

Phebalium squamulosum subsp.

argenteum, commonly known as silverleaf hakea, is a shrub native to south-western Australia. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in dense, glandular hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens across southern Australia.
