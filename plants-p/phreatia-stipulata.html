Phreatia stipulata

Phreatia stipulata, commonly known as the stipulate orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its clustered growth habit and aerial roots. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating to retain moisture.

Phreatia stipulata produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value.
