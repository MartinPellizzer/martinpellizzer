Passiflora kawensis

Passiflora kawensis, commonly known as Kawensis passionflower, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of violet, yellow, and white petals, along with intricate filaments and a crown-like structure. The plant produces edible berries that are typically green when unripe and turn orange-red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Passiflora kawensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
