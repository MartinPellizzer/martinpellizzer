Poa sieberiana

Poa sieberiana, commonly known as Sieber's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, blue-green florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
