Paronychia chilensis var. subandina

Paronychia chilensis var. subandina, commonly known as the Andean sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and resilient addition to drought-tolerant landscapes.
