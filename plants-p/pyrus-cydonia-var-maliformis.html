Pyrus cydonia var. maliformis

Pyrus cydonia var. maliformis, commonly known as the Persian apple or white pear, is a variant of the European pear.

It is characterized by its white, fleshy fruit that is typically smaller and sweeter than the standard pear. The plant is deciduous, producing showy white flowers in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique fruit characteristics.
