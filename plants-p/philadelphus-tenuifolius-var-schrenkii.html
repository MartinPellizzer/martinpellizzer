Philadelphus tenuifolius var. schrenkii

Philadelphus tenuifolius var. schrenkii, commonly known as the Schrenk's mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture that distinguishes it from other varieties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy blooms and pleasant fragrance.
