Parahebe ciliata

Parahebe ciliata, commonly known as the silver tufted bellflower, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It features dense, compact growth with silver-white, fuzzy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in landscaping.
