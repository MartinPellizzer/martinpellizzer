Petaloma coccineum

Petaloma coccineum, commonly known as the red-flowered petaloma, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals that contrast with its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Native to tropical regions of South America, this plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils. It produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
