Potentilla magyarica

Potentilla magyarica, commonly known as Hungarian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Hungary and surrounding regions.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. It has compound leaves with five leaflets and produces small, red or black berries in the fall.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in its native habitat.
