Peracarpa circaeoides

Peracarpa circaeoides, commonly known as the false kaffir lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, hooded flowers that are typically red or orange in color. The plant produces capsule fruits that are often mistaken for berries, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Peracarpa circaeoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
