Pelargonium beaufortianum

Pelargonium beaufortianum, commonly known as the Beaufort geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The leaves are typically rounded with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
