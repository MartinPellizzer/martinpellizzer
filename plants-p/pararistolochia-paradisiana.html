Pararistolochia paradisiana

Pararistolochia paradisiana, commonly known as the paradise vine, is a climbing plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, complex, and often fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically green when mature and turn yellow or orange. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
