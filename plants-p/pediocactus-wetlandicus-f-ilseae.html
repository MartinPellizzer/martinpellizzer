Pediocactus wetlandicus f. ilseae

Pediocactus wetlandicus f. ilseae, commonly known as the Ilse's wetland cactus, is a rare and specialized succulent native to specific wetland environments in South America.

It features a compact, globular stem covered in dense, spiny areoles, which are characteristic of the Cactaceae family. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the wet season, adapting to its humid, seasonal habitat. Its unique morphology reflects evolutionary adaptations to waterlogged soils and periodic flooding.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
