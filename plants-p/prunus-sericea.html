Prunus sericea

Prunus sericea, commonly known as the silverleaf cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in coastal and mountainous areas. The plant is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are covered in a fine silvery down, giving it its common name.

Prunus sericea is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and modest flowering display.
