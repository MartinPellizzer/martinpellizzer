Potamogeton lucens var. mucronatus

Potamogeton lucens var. mucronatus, commonly known as the sharp-pointed pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, green flowers that are arranged in clusters, and it is often found in association with other aquatic vegetation.

Its rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively, contributing to the structure and biodiversity of aquatic ecosystems.
