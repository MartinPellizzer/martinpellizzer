Parrya nudicaulis var. arctica

Parrya nudicaulis var. arctica, commonly known as the Arctic sandwort, is a low-growing, perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and small, fleshy, greenish-yellow leaves. The plant produces tiny, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Parrya nudicaulis var. arctica plays a role in stabilizing soil in its alpine and tundra habitats.
