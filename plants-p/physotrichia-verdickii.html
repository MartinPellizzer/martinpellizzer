Physotrichia verdickii

Physotrichia verdickii, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Physotrichia verdickii is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

It is sometimes considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
