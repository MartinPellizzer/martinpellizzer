Panicum pantrichum

Panicum pantrichum, commonly known as the pantrichum grass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces fluffy, panicle-like inflorescences that are characteristic of the Panicum genus. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it a useful plant for erosion control and wildlife habitat.
