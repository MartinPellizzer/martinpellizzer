Pittosporum longicarpum

Pittosporum longicarpum, commonly known as the long-fruited pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Pittosporum longicarpum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
