Picris strigosa subsp. strigosa

Picris strigosa subsp.

strigosa, commonly known as the rough hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and grasslands. It produces small, elongated achenes as its fruit, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
