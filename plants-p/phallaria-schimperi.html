Phallaria schimperi

Phallaria schimperi, commonly known as the African stinkhorn, is a unique fungal plant that belongs to the Phallaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated fruiting body that resembles a phallus, which is why it is often referred to as a stinkhorn. The fungus produces a strong, unpleasant odor to attract flies and other insects for spore dispersal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in decaying organic matter such as leaf litter.

Its life cycle involves both a saprophytic and a parasitic stage, making it an interesting subject in mycology.
