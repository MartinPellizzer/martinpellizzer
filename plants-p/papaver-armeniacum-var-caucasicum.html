Papaver armeniacum var. caucasicum

Papaver armeniacum var. caucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Papaveraceae family and is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers with a prominent central stigma. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
