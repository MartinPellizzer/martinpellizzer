Phoebe macrocarpa

Phoebe macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited phoebe, is a member of the Lauraceae family, native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as swamps and bottomlands. The plant features glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its fragrant, small white flowers are followed by large, round berries that are edible for some bird species.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
