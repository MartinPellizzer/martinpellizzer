Phlox nana var. lutea

Phlox nana var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow garden phlox, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It features dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a compact rosette near the ground. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Its compact size and vibrant color make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
