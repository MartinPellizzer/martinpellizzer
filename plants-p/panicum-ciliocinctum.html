Panicum ciliocinctum

Panicum ciliocinctum, commonly known as the silky grass or slender panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that cover the leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wet to mesic habitats, such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose, open panicle with spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
