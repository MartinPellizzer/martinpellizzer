Physostegia virginiana var. arenaria

Physostegia virginiana var. arenaria, commonly known as sand hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of tubular, pale blue to lavender flowers. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
