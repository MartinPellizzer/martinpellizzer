Potamogeton obtusifolius subf. apiculatus

Potamogeton obtusifolius subf.

apiculatus, commonly known as the blunt-leaf pondweed subspecies, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems. It has elongated, broad leaves that are typically smooth and slightly pointed at the tips.

The plant produces small, green flowers that are arranged in spike-like clusters, which are often submerged or just at the water's surface. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in lakes, ponds, and wetlands.

This species plays an important role in aquatic habitats by providing habitat and food for various waterfowl and invertebrates.
