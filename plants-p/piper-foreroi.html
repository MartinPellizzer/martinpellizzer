Piper foreroi

Piper foreroi, commonly known as the Forero's pepper or the Colombian pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the humid lowland forests of Colombia and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, peppery fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to other members of the pepper family.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
