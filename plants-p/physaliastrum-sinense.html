Physaliastrum sinense

Physaliastrum sinense, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen or Chinese bryophytes, is a unique plant belonging to the family Pterobryaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, moss-like growth habit and is often found in shaded, moist environments. The plant features small, leaf-like structures called phyllodes that are arranged in a spiral pattern. It reproduces through spores rather than seeds, typical of many bryophytes.

Its ecological role includes contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for microorganisms in its native range.
