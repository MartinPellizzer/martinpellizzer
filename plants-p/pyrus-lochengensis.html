Pyrus lochengensis

Pyrus lochengensis, commonly known as the Locheng pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its serrated leaves and clusters of white flowers. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
