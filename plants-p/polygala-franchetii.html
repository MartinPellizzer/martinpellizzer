Polygala franchetii

Polygala franchetii, commonly known as the Chinese milk-vetch, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers, which are usually pink or white, are borne in dense spikes and are notable for their unique, spurred structure.
