Polygonum intricatum

Polygonum intricatum, commonly known as the intricate knotweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive, bead-like fruits and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its small, greenish-white flowers bloom in late summer, adding subtle beauty to its habitat.

Polygonum intricatum is often noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
