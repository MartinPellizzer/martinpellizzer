Pinus michoacana var. cornuta

Pinus michoacana var. cornuta, commonly known as the Mexican bristle pine, is a rare and endemic conifer native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bristle-like cones that give it its scientific name. The tree typically grows in high-altitude pine forests, often in association with other conifers. Its foliage consists of long, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
