Phyllostachys rubromarginata

Phyllostachys rubromarginata, commonly known as the red-edged bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with a clumping growth habit. The leaves are narrow and elongated, with a distinctive red margin that adds visual interest. This bamboo thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

It is also valued for its ability to provide habitat for wildlife and its use in traditional Chinese medicine.
