Prunella albanica

Prunella albanica, commonly known as alpine dead-nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Lamiaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically purple or blue in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Prunella albanica thrives in rocky, alpine, or arctic environments, often growing in well-drained soils.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
