Prunus insititia var. rubra

Prunus insititia var. rubra, commonly known as the red gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It produces small, tart berries that range in color from yellow to deep red, depending on the variety and ripeness. The plant has a rugged, bushy growth habit with thorny stems and oval, serrated leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The berries are used in jams, jellies, and wines, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
