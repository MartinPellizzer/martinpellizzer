Potentilla arenaria f. acutidens

Potentilla arenaria f. acutidens, commonly known as the sharp-leaved sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, succulent leaves that are typically lance-shaped and sharply pointed, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, cold environments.

This variety is often found in tundra and mountainous areas where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
