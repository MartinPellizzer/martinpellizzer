Passiflora vesicaria

Passiflora vesicaria, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a climbing perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, yellow-green stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The flowers are showy, with five sepals and five petals, and a distinctive crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. Its fruit is a rounded, inflated berry that turns from green to yellow or orange as it matures.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential calming properties.
