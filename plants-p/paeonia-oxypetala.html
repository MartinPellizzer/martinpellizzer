Paeonia oxypetala

Paeonia oxypetala, commonly known as the Japanese peony, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with compound leaves and thick, rough stems. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it produces oval, dark brown seeds within follicles.

Paeonia oxypetala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blossoms.
