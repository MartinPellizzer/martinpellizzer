Palaquium croixianum

Palaquium croixianum, commonly known as the Croix's palaquium, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by fleshy, orange-red berries containing a single seed.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
