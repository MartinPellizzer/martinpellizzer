Pinguicula toldensis

Pinguicula toldensis, commonly known as the Mexican butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, sticky leaves that trap and digest insects. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as rocky crevices or wetlands. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Pinguicula toldensis is a popular specimen among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and ease of cultivation.
