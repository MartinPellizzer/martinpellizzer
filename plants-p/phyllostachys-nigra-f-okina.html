Phyllostachys nigra f. okina

Phyllostachys nigra f. okina, commonly known as the black bamboo or Okina bamboo, is a cultivar of the bamboo species Phyllostachys nigra.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, culms that are often covered with a fine, silky sheen. This ornamental bamboo is native to China and is prized for its striking appearance and ease of cultivation. It typically grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

The plant is cold-hardy and thrives in well-drained soil, making it adaptable to a variety of climates.
