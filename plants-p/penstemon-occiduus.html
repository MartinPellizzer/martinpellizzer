Penstemon occiduus

Penstemon occiduus, commonly known as the desert penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces tall spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink, purple, or red. Its fibrous root system allows it to survive in well-drained soils and withstand periods of drought.

Penstemon occiduus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
