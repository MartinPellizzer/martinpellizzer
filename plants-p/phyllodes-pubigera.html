Phyllodes pubigera

Phyllodes pubigera, commonly known as the silky wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features phyllodes, which are flattened, leaf-like structures that serve as the primary photosynthetic organs. Its flowers are typically yellow or golden in color, arranged in cylindrical spikes.

The silky wattle is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal and sandy soils.
