Polygala acutifolia

Polygala acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern China.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, giving it its common name, while its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish in color.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
