Psoralea kougaensis

Psoralea kougaensis, commonly known as the Kouga psoralea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in the fynbos biome. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
