Phillyrea medianifolia subsp. castellana

Phillyrea medianifolia subsp.

castellana, commonly known as the Castellana Phillyrea, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in Mediterranean climates.
