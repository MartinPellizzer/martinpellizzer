Potentilla crocea

Potentilla crocea, commonly known as the yellow cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a distinctive five-lobed shape, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands, grasslands, and rocky areas.
