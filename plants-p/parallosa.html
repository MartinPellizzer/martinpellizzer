Parallosa

Parallosa, commonly known as the "mountain orchid," is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers come in shades of white, pink, and purple.

Parallosa is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in its native habitats.
