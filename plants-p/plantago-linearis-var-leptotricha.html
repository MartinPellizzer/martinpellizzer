Plantago linearis var. leptotricha

Plantago linearis var. leptotricha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, in fields, and in waste areas.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is commonly used in traditional herbal remedies.
