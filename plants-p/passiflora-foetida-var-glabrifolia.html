Passiflora foetida var. glabrifolia

Passiflora foetida var. glabrifolia, commonly known as the rough passion vine, is a species native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which become smoother as the plant matures. The flowers are showy, with a complex structure featuring five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. It produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
