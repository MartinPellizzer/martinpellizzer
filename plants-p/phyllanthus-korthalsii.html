Phyllanthus korthalsii

Phyllanthus korthalsii, commonly known as the African date palm, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, date-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
