Pelargonium scapifolium

Pelargonium scapifolium, commonly known as the scapifolium geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, narrow, scapose (leaf-like) foliage. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, which bloom throughout the warmer months. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and compact size.

Pelargonium scapifolium prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.
