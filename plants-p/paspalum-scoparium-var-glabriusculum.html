Paspalum scoparium var. glabriusculum

Paspalum scoparium var. glabriusculum, commonly known as the glabrous paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions, contributing to its ecological significance.
