Phyllodes variegata

Phyllodes variegata, commonly known as the variegated acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, flat, leaf-like structures called phyllodes. These phyllodes are often variegated with white, yellow, or green patterns, giving the plant its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences.

It is adapted to arid environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
