Peperomia variifolia

Peperomia variifolia, commonly known as the Variegated Peperomia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and striking, variegated foliage with shades of green, white, and pink. The leaves are typically oval to heart-shaped and have a waxy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, often in shades of red or purple.

This plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in indirect light.
