Pimelea linifolia var. tenella

Pimelea linifolia var. tenella, commonly known as the narrow-leafed swamp tea, is a low-growing shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in wet or swampy habitats, often found in heathlands and along waterways.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
