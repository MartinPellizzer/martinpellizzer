Psiadia glauca

Psiadia glauca, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Psiadia glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
