Picris damascena var. persistens

Picris damascena var. persistens, commonly known as everlasting blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is known for its ability to retain its color and form even after drying, making it a popular choice for dried floral arrangements.
