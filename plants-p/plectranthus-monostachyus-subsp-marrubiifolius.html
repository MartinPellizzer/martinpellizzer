Plectranthus monostachyus subsp. marrubiifolius

Plectranthus monostachyus subsp.

marrubiifolius, commonly known as African shield leaf, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive shield-like appearance, often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and as a houseplant.
