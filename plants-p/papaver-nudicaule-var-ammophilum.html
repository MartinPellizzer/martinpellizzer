Papaver nudicaule var. ammophilum

Papaver nudicaule var. ammophilum, commonly known as the sand poppy, is a variant of the common poppy that thrives in sandy and coastal environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, nodding flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows low to the ground, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

This variety is often found in dunes and open coastal areas, contributing to the ecological stability of these habitats.
