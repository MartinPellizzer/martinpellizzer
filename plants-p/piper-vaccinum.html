Piper vaccinum

Piper vaccinum, commonly known as the pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces small, green berries that turn red when mature. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Piper vaccinum is primarily cultivated for its fruit, which contains the spice known as pepper.

The plant's botanical features make it well-suited for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
