Pseudalbizzia adinocephala

Pseudalbizzia adinocephala, commonly known as the red-flowered acacia, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many legumes, and it has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland forested areas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its unique floral display.
