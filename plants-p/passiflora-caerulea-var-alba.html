Passiflora caerulea var. alba

Passiflora caerulea var. alba, commonly known as the white passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers, which feature a unique structure with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
