Phyteuma scorzonerifolium f. albiflorum

Phyteuma scorzonerifolium f. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflowered phyteuma, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of white or pale lavender flowers. The foliage consists of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with reddish hues. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
