Potentilla meridionalis

Potentilla meridionalis, commonly known as the southern potentilla, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers in early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Potentilla meridionalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
