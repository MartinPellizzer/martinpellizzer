Poa nemoralis subsp. interior

Poa nemoralis subsp.

interior, commonly known as the interior wood sedge, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to cool, temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
