Panicum carneovaginatum

Panicum carneovaginatum, commonly known as the red panicgrass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and panicles that are often tinged with red or pink hues. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Panicum carneovaginatum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun conditions.
