Phyteuma hemisphaericum var. albiflorum

Phyteuma hemisphaericum var. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered phyteuma, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale yellow. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late spring to early summer.
