Phalaris sibthorpii

Phalaris sibthorpii, commonly known as the Sibthorp's wild oat, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

It is characterized by its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in dry conditions.
