Pogostemon micangensis

Pogostemon micangensis, commonly known as the Chinese mint, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves and square stems typical of the Lamiaceae family. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Pogostemon micangensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and antispasmodic properties.
