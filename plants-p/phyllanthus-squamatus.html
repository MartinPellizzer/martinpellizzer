Phyllanthus squamatus

Phyllanthus squamatus, commonly known as the sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and flies. Its leaves are succulent and covered with a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
