Podophyllum pleianthum

Podophyllum pleianthum, commonly known as the American mayapple, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a distinctive umbrella-shaped leaf cluster and produces a single, greenish-yellow flower that develops into a red, fleshy fruit. The plant contains toxic compounds, including podophyllotoxin, which has been used in pharmaceuticals. It thrives in shady, moist woodlands and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
