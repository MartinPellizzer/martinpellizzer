Platanthera mandarinorum subsp. hachijoensis

Platanthera mandarinorum subsp.

hachijoensis, commonly known as the Hachijo orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to the Japanese island of Hachijo-jima. It is a terrestrial orchid with a delicate, slender stem and produces small, greenish-white flowers with a faintly colored lip.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in forest understories where it relies on mycorrhizal associations for nutrient uptake. Its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements make it highly vulnerable to environmental changes.

Conservation efforts are crucial to protect this unique botanical species from extinction.
