Passiflora sanguinea

Passiflora sanguinea, commonly known as the crimson passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a unique blend of blue, purple, and red petals, along with intricate filaments and a prominent crown of stamens. The plant produces edible berries that are typically orange-red in color and contain a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Passiflora sanguinea is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological value to pollinators.
