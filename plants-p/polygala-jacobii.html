Polygala jacobii

Polygala jacobii, commonly known as the Jacob's milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and are pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.
