Potamogeton lucens f. insignis

Potamogeton lucens f. insignis, commonly known as the golden pondweed.

It is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its elongated, ribbon-like leaves that are typically bright green to golden in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually borne in clusters near the water surface. It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments with soft to moderately hard water.

This variety is often found in ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams, contributing to aquatic ecosystem stability.
