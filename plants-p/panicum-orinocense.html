Panicum orinocense

Panicum orinocense, commonly known as Orinoco millet, is a grass species native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, delicate spikelets that produce wind-dispersed seeds. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soil conditions, making it adaptable to various environments. Its growth habit resembles that of other grasses in the Poaceae family, with a fibrous root system and narrow, grass-like leaves.

Orinoco millet is often used in traditional agriculture and has potential for cultivation in arid regions due to its resilience.
