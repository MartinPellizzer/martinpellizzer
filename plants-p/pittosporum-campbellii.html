Pittosporum campbellii

Pittosporum campbellii, commonly known as Campbell's pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features evergreen leaves that are typically elliptical with a glossy, dark green upper surface and a lighter, sometimes hairy underside. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Pittosporum campbellii is also valued for its ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
