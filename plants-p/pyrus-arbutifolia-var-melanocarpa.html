Pyrus arbutifolia var. melanocarpa

Pyrus arbutifolia var. melanocarpa, commonly known as the blackthorn or sloe, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, thorny branches and small, oval leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by dark purple to black berries that are highly aromatic. These berries are often used in culinary applications, such as making jams and liqueurs, due to their intense flavor.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in hedgerows.
