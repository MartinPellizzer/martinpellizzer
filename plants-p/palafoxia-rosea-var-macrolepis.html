Palafoxia rosea var. macrolepis

Palafoxia rosea var. macrolepis, commonly known as the roseleaf palafoxia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. The plant features showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive rose-colored appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.
