Phyllanthus sessei

Phyllanthus sessei, commonly known as the African spurge, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces capsules as its fruit, which contain numerous tiny seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
