Polymnia canadensis var. radiata

Polymnia canadensis var. radiata, commonly known as the Canadian polymnia, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and often have a distinctive white or pale green coloration. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
