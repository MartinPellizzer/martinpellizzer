Parodia brevihamata

Parodia brevihamata, commonly known as the short-stemmed pincushion cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which bloom from areoles located near the top of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for succulent collections.
