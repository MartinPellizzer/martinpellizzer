Phyllanthus poeppigianus

Phyllanthus poeppigianus, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are opposite and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by the distinctive round, bright orange fruits. The fruit has a thick, leathery skin and contains a single seed enclosed in a fleshy aril.

It is widely cultivated for its nutritional value and is used in traditional medicine for its antioxidant properties.
