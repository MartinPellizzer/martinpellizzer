Passiflora buchananii

Passiflora buchananii, commonly known as the Buchanan's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a unique blend of violet, yellow, and white petals, along with intricate filaments and a crown-like structure. The plant produces edible berries that are typically orange or yellow when ripe, containing a juicy, sweet pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Passiflora buchananii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
