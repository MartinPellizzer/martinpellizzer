Paspalum dolichostachyum

Paspalum dolichostachyum, commonly known as the long-stalked paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces long, drooping inflorescences composed of small, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks.

Its dense growth habit makes it an important species for erosion control and wildlife habitat.
