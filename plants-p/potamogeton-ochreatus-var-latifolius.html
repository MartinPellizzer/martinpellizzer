Potamogeton ochreatus var. latifolius

Potamogeton ochreatus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its broad, elongated leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged, and it plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and stable environmental conditions.
