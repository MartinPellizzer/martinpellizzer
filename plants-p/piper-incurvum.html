Piper incurvum

Piper incurvum, commonly known as the curled pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that often curl at the edges, giving it its common name. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by elongated, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
