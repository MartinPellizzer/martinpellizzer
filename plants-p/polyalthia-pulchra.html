Polyalthia pulchra

Polyalthia pulchra, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in dense clusters, often giving the plant a fiery appearance. The leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds covered in a silky, white coating.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
