Poecilandra pumila

Poecilandra pumila, commonly known as the dwarf poecilandra, is a small, evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and features dense, leathery leaves that are dark green in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate floral display to its compact form. It is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Poecilandra pumila prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for indoor or shaded garden environments.
