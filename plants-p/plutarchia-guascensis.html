Plutarchia guascensis

Plutarchia guascensis, commonly known as the Andean sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to the harsh conditions of the Andes. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ecological importance and unique adaptations, it is a subject of interest in botanical research.
