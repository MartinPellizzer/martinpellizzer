Potamogeton spirillus var. curvifolius

Potamogeton spirillus var. curvifolius, commonly known as the curled-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, spiral-arranged leaves that are typically curved or twisted, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in some water bodies if not managed properly.
