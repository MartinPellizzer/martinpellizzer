Papaver nudicaule var. calcareum

Papaver nudicaule var. calcareum, commonly known as the limestone poppy, is a variant of the meadow poppy native to calcareous soils.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has slender, hairy stems and palmate leaves with a slightly waxy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
