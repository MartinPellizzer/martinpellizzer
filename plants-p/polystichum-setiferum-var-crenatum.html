Polystichum setiferum var. crenatum

Polystichum setiferum var. crenatum, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a variant of the common wood fern.

It is characterized by its dense, upright fronds that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The fronds are typically dark green and have a serrated or crenate edge, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This fern is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
