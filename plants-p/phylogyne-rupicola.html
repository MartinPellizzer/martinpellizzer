Phylogyne rupicola

Phylogyne rupicola, commonly known as the rock lily, is a flowering plant native to arid and rocky regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water availability. It typically grows in crevices and on rocky outcrops, where it can anchor itself securely.

Phylogyne rupicola plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and animals.
