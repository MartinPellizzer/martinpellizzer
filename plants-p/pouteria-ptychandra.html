Pouteria ptychandra

Pouteria ptychandra, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The tree's most notable feature is its edible, oily seeds, which are harvested from its hard, woody pods. It thrives in humid, nutrient-rich soils and requires a warm climate with high rainfall.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various wildlife species.
