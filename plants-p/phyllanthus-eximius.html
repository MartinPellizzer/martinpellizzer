Phyllanthus eximius

Phyllanthus eximius, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fruiting structures known as cyathia. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and dry forests.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and landscaping.
