Pilosella hypeurocinerea

Pilosella hypeurocinerea, commonly known as the pale leaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in meadows and grasslands.

Pilosella hypeurocinerea is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
