Peltophorum acutifolium

Peltophorum acutifolium, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia and parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters during the dry season. The plant has pinnate leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets, and its bark is often smooth and light-colored. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in urban and rural landscapes.
