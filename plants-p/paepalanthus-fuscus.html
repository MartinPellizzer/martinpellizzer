Paepalanthus fuscus

Paepalanthus fuscus, commonly known as the dark green burr cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub-like cactus characterized by its dense, spiny stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, where it adapts to harsh conditions with its succulent structure. Its flowers are often yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in dry gardens.
