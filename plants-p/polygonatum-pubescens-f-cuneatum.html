Polygonatum pubescens f. cuneatum

Polygonatum pubescens f. cuneatum, commonly known as the hairy Solomon's seal, is a variant of the Solomon's seal plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its berries are typically red or black and ripen in late summer or early autumn.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to woodland environments.
