Pycnostachys butaguensis

Pycnostachys butaguensis, commonly known as the Butaguense flame tree, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has a dense, compact crown and produces small, elliptical leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a faintly aromatic scent.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its vibrant floral display.
