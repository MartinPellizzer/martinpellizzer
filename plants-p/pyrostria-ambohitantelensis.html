Pyrostria ambohitantelensis

Pyrostria ambohitantelensis, commonly known as the Madagascar bamboo, is a unique grass-like plant native to the eastern rainforests of Madagascar.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This species is notable for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments, often growing in dense understories. Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various local fauna.
