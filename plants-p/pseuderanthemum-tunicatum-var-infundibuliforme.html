Pseuderanthemum tunicatum var. infundibuliforme

Pseuderanthemum tunicatum var. infundibuliforme, commonly known as the hooded pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped bracts that enclose the inflorescence. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, with vibrant colors that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
