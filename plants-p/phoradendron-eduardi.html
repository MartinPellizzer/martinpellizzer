Phoradendron eduardi

Phoradendron eduardi, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees, primarily in the tropics.

It lacks roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its leaves, which are adapted for photosynthesis. The plant produces small, white or yellowish berries that are often consumed by birds, which then spread its seeds to other trees. Its growth is slow, and it can remain on a host for decades, forming dense clusters.

The plant plays a role in ecosystem dynamics by influencing tree growth and providing food for various wildlife species.
