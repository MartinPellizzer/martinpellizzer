Pachylophus prolatus

Pachylophus prolatus, commonly known as the creeping thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and spiny leaves.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, followed by bracts that persist into the winter.

Its robust root system allows it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions, making it a common weed in disturbed habitats.
