Parkia Filicoidea Var. Hildebrandtii

Hildebrandt's flame tree, scientifically known as Parkia filicoidea var. hildebrandtii, is a striking tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is celebrated for its vibrant, flame-like red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a breathtaking visual display during the flowering season. The tree thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks. Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture. Despite its beauty, it remains relatively obscure compared to other flowering trees in the region.
