Piper maingayi

Piper maingayi, commonly known as the Maingay's pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often pubescent. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are typically clustered in spikes, and it produces small, spherical berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some regions.
