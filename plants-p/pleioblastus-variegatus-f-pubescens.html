Pleioblastus variegatus f. pubescens

Pleioblastus variegatus f. pubescens, commonly known as the silvery bamboo, is a variant of the variegated bamboo characterized by its distinctive silvery-gray foliage.

This plant is native to eastern Asia and is valued for its ornamental appearance and hardiness. It typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 meters, featuring slender, upright culms covered in fine, soft hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a pale green to silvery sheen, creating a striking visual contrast.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
