Plagiostachys lourdesiae

Plagiostachys lourdesiae, commonly known as the Lourdesia bamboo, is a member of the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, inconspicuous flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
