Phyllocactus eichlamii

Phyllocactus eichlamii, commonly known as the Eichlam's cactus, is a unique species native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, woolly hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it thrives in well-draining soil with ample sunlight.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or controlled environments.
