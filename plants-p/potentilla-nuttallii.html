Potentilla nuttallii

Potentilla nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and mountainous regions. Its leaves are palmately divided into five lobes, and it produces small, red berries that are a food source for birds.

Potentilla nuttallii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
