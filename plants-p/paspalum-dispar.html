Paspalum dispar

Paspalum dispar, commonly known as the slender grass or narrowleaf grass, is a species of grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including wetlands, prairies, and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Paspalum dispar is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
