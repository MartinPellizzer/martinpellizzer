Phyllostachys puberula f. albovariegata

Phyllostachys puberula f. albovariegata, commonly known as the white-variegated bamboo, is a decorative variety of bamboo characterized by its striking white and green variegated foliage.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is native to East Asia, particularly China. The plant features slender, upright culms that are often adorned with a silvery-white sheath, giving it a unique visual appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This cultivar is known for its hardiness and ability to form dense clumps over time.
