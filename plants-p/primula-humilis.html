Primula humilis

Primula humilis, commonly known as the dwarf primrose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Korea.

It features hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Due to its delicate appearance and early blooming season, it is a valued addition to gardens and botanical collections.
