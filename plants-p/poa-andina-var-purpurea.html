Poa andina var. purpurea

Poa andina var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and distinctive purple-tinged inflorescence. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that becomes more pronounced in the cooler seasons.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
