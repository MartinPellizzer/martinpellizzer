Pyrus aria subsp. scandica

Pyrus aria subsp.

scandica, commonly known as the creeping whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by edible, subglobose berries that are dark blue to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
