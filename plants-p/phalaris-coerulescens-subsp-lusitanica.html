Phalaris coerulescens subsp. lusitanica

Phalaris coerulescens subsp.

lusitanica, commonly known as the Lusitanian glassworts, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal or saline environments, showcasing its halophytic nature. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in dunes or salt marshes.

Its ability to thrive in harsh, salty conditions makes it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
