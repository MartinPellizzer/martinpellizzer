Piper elongatum var. ossanum

Piper elongatum var. ossanum, commonly known as the Ossanum pepper plant, is a variant of the long pepper species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender fruits that resemble small berries, typically turning from green to red as they mature. The plant is a climbing vine with compound leaves and produces small, white flowers before the fruit develops. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.

It is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes in tropical regions.
