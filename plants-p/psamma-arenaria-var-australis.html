Psamma arenaria var. australis

Psamma arenaria var. australis, commonly known as the Australian sandbur, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and small, spiny fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and insects. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to drought conditions.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing sand dunes and providing habitat for small fauna.
