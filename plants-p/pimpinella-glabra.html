Pimpinella glabra

Pimpinella glabra, commonly known as smooth cowparsley, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to theApiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in a hairy, triangular fruit, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Pimpinella glabra is not typically cultivated for its edible qualities but is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
