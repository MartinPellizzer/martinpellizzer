Polygala vulgaris var. pisaurensis

Polygala vulgaris var. pisaurensis, commonly known as the Pisaurensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, yellow or greenish flowers that bloom in late spring.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
