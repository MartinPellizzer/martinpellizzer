Poa clareosa

Poa clareosa, commonly known as the Clareosa bentgrass, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate regions. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains numerous tiny flowers, which are adapted for wind pollination.

Poa clareosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its delicate appearance.
