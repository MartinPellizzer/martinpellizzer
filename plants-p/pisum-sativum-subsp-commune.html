Pisum sativum subsp. commune

Pisum sativum subsp.

commune, commonly known as the common pea, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, often edible seeds enclosed within green, fibrous pods.

It typically features compound leaves with stipules and small, pea-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. The subspecies is widely cultivated for its nutritional value, particularly for its protein-rich seeds.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
