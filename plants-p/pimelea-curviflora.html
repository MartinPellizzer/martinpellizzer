Pimelea curviflora

Pimelea curviflora, commonly known as the twisted flowered riceflower, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in heathlands or coastal areas. The plant produces small, white or cream-colored flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the autumn season. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight curl, giving the plant its common name.

Pimelea curviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
