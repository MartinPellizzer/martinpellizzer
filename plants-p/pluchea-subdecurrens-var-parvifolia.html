Pluchea subdecurrens var. parvifolia

Pluchea subdecurrens var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant is typically found in arid or semi-arid regions, growing in rocky soils and along dry streambeds. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense heads.

This species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
