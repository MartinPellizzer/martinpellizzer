Paspalum vaseyanum

Paspalum vaseyanum, commonly known as the vaseyan grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Paspalum vaseyanum is often found in disturbed areas and is used in some regions for forage or erosion control.
