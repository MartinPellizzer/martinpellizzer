Prescottia cordifolia

Prescottia cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf poinsettia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value, particularly during the holiday season.
