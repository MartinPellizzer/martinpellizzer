Pogostemon ianthinus

Pogostemon ianthinus, commonly known as the purple pogostemon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves and flowers are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
