Passerina incurva

Passerina incurva, commonly known as the curved pasqueflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a single, showy, violet-blue flower that blooms in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Its flowering period is brief, typically lasting only a few weeks, and it is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.

Passerina incurva is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes buttercups and other flowering plants with showy blooms.
