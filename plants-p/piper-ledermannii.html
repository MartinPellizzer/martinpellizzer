Piper ledermannii

Piper ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with aerial roots that allow it to attach to trees and other structures. Its small, greenish-white flowers are typically clustered in spikes, and it produces small, round berries that are edible but have a mild, peppery flavor.

This species is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
