Phyllostachys aurea var. flavescensinversa

Phyllostachys aurea var. flavescensinversa, commonly known as the reverse yellow bamboo, is a variant of the golden bamboo species.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, which features a reverse coloration pattern compared to the typical golden bamboo. The leaves are usually a striking yellow-green on the upper surface and a lighter, almost white, underside. This plant is native to East Asia and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow to significant heights, making it a popular choice in bamboo gardens.
