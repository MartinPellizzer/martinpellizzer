Pterostylis maxima

Pterostylis maxima, commonly known as the giant duck orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy greenish-yellow flowers that resemble a duck's head, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. It has a slender, upright stem with a single or few flowers at the top.

Pterostylis maxima is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanism involving specific insects.
