Potentilla baltistana

Potentilla baltistana, commonly known as the Baltic potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the cooler regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

Potentilla baltistana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
