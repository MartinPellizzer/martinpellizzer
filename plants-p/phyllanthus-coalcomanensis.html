Phyllanthus coalcomanensis

Phyllanthus coalcomanensis, commonly known as the Mexican gooseberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Phyllanthaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its small, green, fleshy fruits that resemble miniature citrus fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly waxy texture.

The botanical features of Phyllanthus coalcomanensis make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
