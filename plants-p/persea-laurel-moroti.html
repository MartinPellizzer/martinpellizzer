Persea laurel-moroti

Persea laurel-moroti, commonly known as the African blackwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its slow growth and high-quality timber, it is also valued for its ecological and economic significance.
