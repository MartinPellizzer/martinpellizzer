Piper variabile

Piper variabile, commonly known as the variable pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible berries that range in color from yellow to red. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent.

Piper variabile is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of culinary spices.
