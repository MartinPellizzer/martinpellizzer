Prunus avium f. microcarpa

Prunus avium f. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf cherry, is a compact form of the sweet cherry tree.

It exhibits smaller leaves and fruits compared to the standard Prunus avium, making it ideal for smaller gardens or container cultivation. The plant retains the typical characteristics of the sweet cherry, including its pink to white flowers and edible, sweet fruits. It is often used as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and aesthetic appeal.

Botanically, it is classified as a cultivar within the Prunus genus, showcasing genetic variation within the species.
