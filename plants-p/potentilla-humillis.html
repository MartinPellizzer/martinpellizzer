Potentilla humillis

Potentilla humillis, commonly known as dwarf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are usually less than 2 inches in length. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Potentilla humillis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
