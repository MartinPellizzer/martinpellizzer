Paeonia × moutan var. virginea

Paeonia × moutan var. virginea, commonly known as the peony, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, white, and yellow. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and has a thick, woody stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The flowers typically bloom in spring, adding a vibrant touch to the landscape.
