Prunus itosakura var. subhirtella

Prunus itosakura var. subhirtella, commonly known as the Japanese flowering cherry or yaezakura, is a cultivar of the Prunus itosakura species.

It is characterized by its striking pink and white flowers that bloom in early spring, often with a distinctive double-petaled appearance. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 5 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a glossy green surface.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and parks.
