Peperomia costaricensis

Peperomia costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican pepper plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Central America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and yellow, giving it an ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia costaricensis is relatively low-maintenance and drought-tolerant once established.
