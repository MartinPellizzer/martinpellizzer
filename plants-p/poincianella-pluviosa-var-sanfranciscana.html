Poincianella pluviosa var. sanfranciscana

Poincianella pluviosa var. sanfranciscana, commonly known as the San Francisco glorybower, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of bright yellow flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, woody stems and compound leaves with a feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in coastal gardens.

Its adaptability to coastal conditions makes it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
