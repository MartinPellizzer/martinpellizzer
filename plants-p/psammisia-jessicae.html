Psammisia jessicae

Psammisia jessicae, commonly known as the Jessica's sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has adapted to harsh environments with its succulent leaves and deep root system for water retention. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
