Philodendron aurantiispadix

Philodendron aurantiispadix, commonly known as the orange-spurred philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy green leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. The plant produces striking, orange-red spadices that resemble candles, which are typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
