Pandanus furcatus var. malesiaca

Pandanus furcatus var. malesiaca, commonly known as the brush palm or tree pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible fruits, which are consumed by both humans and animals. It thrives in coastal and wetland areas, often growing in brackish or saline soils.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitats.
