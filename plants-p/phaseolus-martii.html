Phaseolus martii

Phaseolus martii, commonly known as the Andean runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Andes region of South America.

It produces elongated, edible pods that are similar in appearance to common beans but have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its nutritional content and is cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
