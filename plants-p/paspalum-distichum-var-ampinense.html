Paspalum distichum var. ampinense

Paspalum distichum var. ampinense, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. This plant thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland habitats.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and use in ecological restoration projects.
