Polygala pilifera

Polygala pilifera, commonly known as the featherleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate leaves that resemble feathers. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which attract various pollinators such as bees and birds. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access water from deeper soil layers.

Polygala pilifera is often found in sandy soils and is an important component of native vegetation in its habitat.
