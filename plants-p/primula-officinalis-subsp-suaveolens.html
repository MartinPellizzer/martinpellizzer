Primula officinalis subsp. suaveolens

Primula officinalis subsp.

suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented primrose, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

The plant features basal rosettes of oval, dark green leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and violet. Its flowers bloom from late winter to early spring, making it one of the earliest flowering plants in temperate regions.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
