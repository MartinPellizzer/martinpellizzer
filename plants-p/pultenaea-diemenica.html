Pultenaea diemenica

Pultenaea diemenica, commonly known as the Tasmanian wattle, is a shrub native to Tasmania.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Pultenaea diemenica is adapted to the cool, moist climates of Tasmania and is often found in heathlands and forest margins.
