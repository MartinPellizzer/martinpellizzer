Pyrethrum daucifolium

Pyrethrum daucifolium, commonly known as the common pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. Its flowers produce pyrethrin, a natural insecticide used in organic pest control.

Pyrethrum daucifolium thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
