Passerina uniflora var. angustifolia

Passerina uniflora var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, often hairy leaves. The plant produces a single, showy, pink to purple flower with five petals and numerous stamens, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes.

Its ecological importance includes providing early nectar sources for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
