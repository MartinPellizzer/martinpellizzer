Polypodium scandens var. billardierei

Polypodium scandens var. billardierei, commonly known as the climbing polypody, is a fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically light green and have a soft, feathery texture. This variant is distinguished by its slightly broader leaves compared to the standard Polypodium scandens. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for use in shady garden settings.
