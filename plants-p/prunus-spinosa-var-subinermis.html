Prunus spinosa var. subinermis

Prunus spinosa var. subinermis, commonly known as the smooth thorned cherry or European blackthorn, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, dark purple-black berries that are edible but often tart. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin, and it blooms with white or pale pink flowers in early spring.

The species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.
