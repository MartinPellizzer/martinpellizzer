Phyllanthus sulcatus

Phyllanthus sulcatus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought conditions.
