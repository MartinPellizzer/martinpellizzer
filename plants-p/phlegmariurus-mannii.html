Phlegmariurus mannii

Phlegmariurus mannii, commonly known as the African clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and a distinctive, club-shaped sporophyte structure. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky crevices. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms.

Its reproductive structures, called strobila, contain spores that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.
