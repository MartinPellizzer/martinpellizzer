Pyrus simonii

Pyrus simonii, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, sweet, yellowish fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a fruit-bearing plant.

Pyrus simonii is relatively cold-hardy and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
