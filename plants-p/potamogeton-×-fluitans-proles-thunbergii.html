Potamogeton × fluitans proles thunbergii

Potamogeton × fluitans proles thunbergii, commonly known as the hybrid water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton fluitans and Potamogeton thunbergii, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, whorled leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often found near the water surface.

This plant plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
