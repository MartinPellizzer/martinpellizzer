Polygala timoutoides var. timoutoides

Polygala timoutoides var. timoutoides, commonly known as the Timor milk-vine, is a climbing shrub native to the island of Timor.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, showy flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often with a distinctive spurred structure. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, typically growing in moist, shaded environments.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
