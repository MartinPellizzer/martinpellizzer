Pachycormus discolor

Pachycormus discolor, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that reflects light, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.
