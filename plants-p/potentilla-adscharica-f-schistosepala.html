Potentilla adscharica f. schistosepala

Potentilla adscharica f. schistosepala, commonly known as the schistose-petaled potentilla, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive leaf and floral structures.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to Central Asia, where it thrives in rocky and arid environments. The leaves are typically narrow and serrated, while the flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in cymes. Its unique feature is the schistose (flaky or layered) sepals, which give it a textured appearance.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
