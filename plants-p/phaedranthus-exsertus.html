Phaedranthus exsertus

Phaedranthus exsertus, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bract-like structures that give it its common name. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

Phaedranthus exsertus prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.
