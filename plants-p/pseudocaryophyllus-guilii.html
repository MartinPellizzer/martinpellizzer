Pseudocaryophyllus guilii

Pseudocaryophyllus guilii, commonly known as the Guili guili, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, which includes many ornamental and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The Guili guili is often cultivated for its delicate appearance and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
