Philodendron anisotomum

Philodendron anisotomum, commonly known as the variegated philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves that often display variegation with white or yellow markings. The plant has a climbing habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
