Perezia aletes

Perezia aletes, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and is adapted to cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette at the base.

Perezia aletes is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
