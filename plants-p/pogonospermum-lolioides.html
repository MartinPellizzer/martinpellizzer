Pogonospermum lolioides

Pogonospermum lolioides, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and forest edges. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers.

Pogonospermum lolioides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small animals and insects.
