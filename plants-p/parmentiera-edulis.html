Parmentiera edulis

Parmentiera edulis, commonly known as the Mexican candlewood, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by woody, spiny pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

The plant has traditional uses in folk medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appearance.
