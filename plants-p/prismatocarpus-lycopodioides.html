Prismatocarpus lycopodioides

Prismatocarpus lycopodioides, commonly known as the clubmoss or ground pine, is a member of the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features a creeping stem with dense clusters of small, scale-like leaves. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores, which are dispersed by wind.

This ancient lineage of plants has remained relatively unchanged for millions of years, showcasing its adaptability and ecological significance.
