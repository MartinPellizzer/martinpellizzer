Piper subflavum

Piper subflavum, commonly known as the yellow pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, opposite leaves that are often glandular-dotted. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
