Prunus incana

Prunus incana, commonly known as the white sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet, edible fruits known as cherries, which are typically consumed fresh or used in jams. Prunus incana thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal contribute to its presence in various ecological and horticultural settings.
