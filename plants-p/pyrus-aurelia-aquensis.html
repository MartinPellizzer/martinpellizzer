Pyrus aurelia-aquensis

Pyrus aurelia-aquensis, commonly known as the Aquatic Pear, is a rare and unique species of pear native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

This plant is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are typically broad and elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a slightly hairy underside. The flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, blooming in early spring before the leaves appear.

The fruit, when it matures, is a small, yellowish pear that is edible but not widely cultivated for agricultural purposes.
