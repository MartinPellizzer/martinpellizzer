Peniocereus canoensis

Peniocereus canoensis, commonly known as the Canary Island moon cactus, is a flowering cactus native to the Canary Islands.

It is a small, low-growing plant with a distinctive appearance, featuring a swollen, bulbous stem and a cluster of short, cylindrical stems at the top. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.

Its unique structure and ornamental flowers make it a notable species in the Cactaceae family.
