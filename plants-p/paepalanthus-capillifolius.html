Paepalanthus capillifolius

Paepalanthus capillifolius, commonly known as the silky leaf cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and delicate, feathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in arid gardens.
