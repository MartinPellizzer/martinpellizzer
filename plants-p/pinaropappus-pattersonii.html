Pinaropappus pattersonii

Pinaropappus pattersonii, commonly known as the Patterson's pappus.

It is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States, particularly Arizona and New Mexico. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its unique structure and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
