Poa pratensis var. sabulosa

Poa pratensis var. sabulosa, commonly known as sandy bluegrass, is a variant of the common bluegrass species.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and a dense, mat-forming growth habit. This plant is adapted to sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in open, disturbed areas. It has a low-growing structure with a bluish-green coloration, and its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets.

The botanical features of Poa pratensis var. sabulosa make it suitable for erosion control and ornamental use in landscaping.
