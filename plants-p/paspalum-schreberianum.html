Paspalum schreberianum

Paspalum schreberianum, commonly known as the Schreber's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, arching blades that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along waterways.

Its adaptability makes it a common species in wetland ecosystems, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
