Palicourea radicans

Palicourea radicans, commonly known as creeping palicourea, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a perennial vine that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale blue in color. Its stems are prostrate, allowing it to spread along the forest floor.

Palicourea radicans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
