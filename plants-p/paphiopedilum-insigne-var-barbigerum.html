Paphiopedilum insigne var. barbigerum

Paphiopedilum insigne var. barbigerum, commonly known as the hairy-lip paphiopedilum, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, hairy sepals and petals, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white with a prominent, hairy lip.

This variety is highly prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique morphology and ornamental value.
