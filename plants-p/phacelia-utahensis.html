Phacelia utahensis

Phacelia utahensis, commonly known as Utah phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as cliffs and slopes. It has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Utah phacelia plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies in its native ecosystem.
