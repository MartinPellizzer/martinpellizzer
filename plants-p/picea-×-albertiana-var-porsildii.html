Picea × albertiana var. porsildii

Picea × albertiana var. porsildii, commonly known as the Porsild's white spruce, is a hybrid conifer native to northern Canada and Alaska.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and smooth, gray bark. The foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, providing a striking visual contrast against the white bark. It thrives in cold, moist environments and is often found in boreal forests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in harsh climates.
