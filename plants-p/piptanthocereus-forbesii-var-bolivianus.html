Piptanthocereus forbesii var. bolivianus

Piptanthocereus forbesii var. bolivianus, commonly known as the Bolivian night-blooming cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines and its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy tissue. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white and pink. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and xeriscaping.
