Punica florida

Punica florida, commonly known as the pomegranate, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and South Asia.

It is characterized by its bright red, peeling flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of its edible, seed-filled fruit. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, and it produces a unique, segmented fruit with a thick, leathery rind. The flowers are rich in nectar and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Punica florida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the fruit, which is used in culinary and medicinal applications.
