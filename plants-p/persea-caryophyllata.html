Persea caryophyllata

Persea caryophyllata, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to southern Mexico and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible when fully mature. The fruit has a sweet, honey-like flavor and is often used in traditional cuisines.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
