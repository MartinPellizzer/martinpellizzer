Petunia divaricata

Petunia divaricata, commonly known as the trailing petunia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and profuse display of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine, hairy textures. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-draining soil.

This plant is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to cascade from hanging baskets or containers.
