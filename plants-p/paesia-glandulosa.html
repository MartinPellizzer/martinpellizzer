Paesia glandulosa

Paesia glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular paesia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and features small, glandular structures on its stems and leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Paesia glandulosa is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
