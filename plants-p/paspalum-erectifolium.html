Paspalum erectifolium

Paspalum erectifolium, commonly known as the upright leaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along stream banks.

Paspalum erectifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
