Potentilla smirnovii

Potentilla smirnovii, commonly known as Smirnov's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features small, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are hairy and serrated.

Smirnov's cinquefoil is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
