Portulaca calandroides

Portulaca calandroides, commonly known as the golden portulaca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features fleshy, succulent leaves and produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its flowers are typically five-petaled and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Portulaca calandroides is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
