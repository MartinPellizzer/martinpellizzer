Polygonum imbricatum

Polygonum imbricatum, commonly known as the Indian mallow or velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Polygonum imbricatum is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
