Prunus sargentii f. albida

Prunus sargentii f. albida, commonly known as the white-flowered sargent cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus sargentii species, which is native to China.

This plant is characterized by its showy, white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. The leaves are ovate with a pointed tip and a smooth margin, turning yellow in the fall.

The fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that matures in late summer.
