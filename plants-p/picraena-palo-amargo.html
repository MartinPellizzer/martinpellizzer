Picraena palo-amargo

Picraena palo-amargo, commonly known as the bitter bush, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is known for its bitter taste, which is due to the presence of compounds like aucubin. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Picraena palo-amargo is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
