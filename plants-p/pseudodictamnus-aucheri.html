Pseudodictamnus aucheri

Pseudodictamnus aucheri, commonly known as the Alaskan stonecrop, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, adapted to retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its hardy nature and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for gardeners in colder climates.
