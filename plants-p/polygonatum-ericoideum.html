Polygonatum ericoideum

Polygonatum ericoideum, commonly known as the creeping yellow orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its slender, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in shaded, moist environments.

Polygonatum ericoideum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional herbal medicine.
