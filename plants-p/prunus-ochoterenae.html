Prunus ochoterenae

Prunus ochoterenae, commonly known as the Far Eastern cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed by wildlife and have been historically used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth margin, while its bark is typically grayish-brown and fissured.

Prunus ochoterenae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
