Prostanthera prostantheroides

Prostanthera prostantheroides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mintbush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a strong mint-like scent when crushed. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Prostanthera prostantheroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Indigenous medicine.
