Potentilla salisburgensis

Potentilla salisburgensis, commonly known as the Salzburg cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It typically features wiry stems and small, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, which are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Potentilla salisburgensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
