Peridium oblongifolium

Peridium oblongifolium, commonly known as the elongated-leaf spirea, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its oblong, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, compact form. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers during the spring season, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and helps it thrive in well-drained soils.

Peridium oblongifolium is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
