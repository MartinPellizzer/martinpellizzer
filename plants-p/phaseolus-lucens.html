Phaseolus lucens

Phaseolus lucens, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and vibrant red flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being heart-shaped and slightly hairy.

Phaseolus lucens is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
