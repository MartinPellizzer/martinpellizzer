Pandanus julianettii

Pandanus julianettii, commonly known as the Julianettii pandanus, is a species of palm native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which are often used in traditional crafts. Its fruit, a fibrous husk enclosing a single seed, is adapted for dispersal by water.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
