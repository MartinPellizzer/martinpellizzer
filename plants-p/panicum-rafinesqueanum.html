Panicum rafinesqueanum

Panicum rafinesqueanum, commonly known as Rafinesque's panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, slender flowering stalks that bear spikelets arranged in panicles. Its inflorescence is often reddish-brown and becomes more pronounced as the plant matures.

Rafinesque's panicgrass plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
