Polygala diversifolia

Polygala diversifolia, commonly known as the variable milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and opposite, and the flowers are often arranged in racemes.

Polygala diversifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering habit.
