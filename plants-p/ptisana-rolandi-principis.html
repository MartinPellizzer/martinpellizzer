Ptisana rolandi-principis

Ptisana rolandi-principis, commonly known as the Sicilian rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Ptisana rolandi-principis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
