Presla hyemalis

Presla hyemalis, commonly known as the winter snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
