Piptospatha bella

Piptospatha bella, commonly known as the elegant bellflower, is a striking plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shaded areas. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy.

Piptospatha bella is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural habitats.
