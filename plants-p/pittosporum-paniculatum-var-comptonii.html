Pittosporum paniculatum var. comptonii

Pittosporum paniculatum var. comptonii, commonly known as the Compton's pittosporum, is a deciduous shrub native to New Zealand.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer. These flowers are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
