Papaver rhoeas var. alleizettei

Papaver rhoeas var. alleizettei, commonly known as the Alleizette's poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its distinctive floral morphology.

This plant typically features vibrant red or orange petals that are often adorned with a darker central blotch, enhancing its visual appeal. It is an annual herbaceous plant with a taproot system, and its stems are usually erect and branched. The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is known for its short life cycle, completing its growth and reproduction within a single growing season.

Botanically, it is closely related to other members of the Papaveraceae family, sharing similar structural and reproductive traits.
