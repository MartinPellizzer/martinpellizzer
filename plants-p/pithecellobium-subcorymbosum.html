Pithecellobium subcorymbosum

Pithecellobium subcorymbosum, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers arranged in cascading clusters. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types make it a valuable species for reforestation and ornamental purposes.
