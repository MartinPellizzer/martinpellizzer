Polypodium schultzei

Polypodium schultzei, commonly known as the Schultze's shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery fronds that form a compact, shield-like structure. The plant typically grows on rocky substrates or in shaded, moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to humid, tropical climates. Its rhizomes are often covered in a thick layer of moss, which helps retain moisture and protect the plant from desiccation.

Polypodium schultzei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ease of cultivation.
