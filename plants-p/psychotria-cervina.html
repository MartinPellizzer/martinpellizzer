Psychotria cervina

Psychotria cervina, commonly known as the "Ceremonial Psychotria," is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. It contains psychoactive alkaloids, including dimethyltryptamine (DMT), which contribute to its significance in traditional shamanic practices.

Due to its chemical composition, it is also of interest in ethnobotanical and pharmacological research.
