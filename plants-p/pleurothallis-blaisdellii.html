Pleurothallis blaisdellii

Pleurothallis blaisdellii, commonly known as the Blaisdell's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and decaying organic matter.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
