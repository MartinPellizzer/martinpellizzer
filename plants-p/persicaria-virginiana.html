Persicaria virginiana

Persicaria virginiana, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant features erect stems that are reddish in color, especially near the base, and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in dense clusters and bloom from midsummer to early fall.

Persicaria virginiana is often considered a weed but can be an important plant in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
