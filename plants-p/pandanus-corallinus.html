Pandanus corallinus

Pandanus corallinus, commonly known as the coral pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically arranged in clusters at the tips of the leaves. Its fibrous leaves are harvested for weaving and other artisanal purposes.

Pandanus corallinus thrives in coastal and wetland environments, where it plays an important ecological role.
