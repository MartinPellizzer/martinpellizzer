Primula × salomonii

Primula × salomonii, commonly known as the Solomon's primrose, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 15–30 cm, with hairy stems and oval, dark green leaves. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in cool, moist environments.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
