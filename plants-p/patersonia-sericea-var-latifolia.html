Patersonia sericea var. latifolia

Patersonia sericea var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved paterson's curse, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and bright blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a silky texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common weed in agricultural and disturbed areas.

Due to its invasive nature, it is often considered a noxious weed in parts of its range.
