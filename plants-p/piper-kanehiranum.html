Piper kanehiranum

Piper kanehiranum, commonly known as the Hawaiian pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the Hawaiian Islands and is characterized by its climbing habit and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, fleshy berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional Hawaiian medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
