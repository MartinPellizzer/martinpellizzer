Panicum lappaceum

Panicum lappaceum, commonly known as silverhair grass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which results from fine, hair-like structures covering the leaves. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, prairies, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with a reddish hue.

Silverhair grass plays an important ecological role by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
