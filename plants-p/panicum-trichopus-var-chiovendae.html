Panicum trichopus var. chiovendae

Panicum trichopus var. chiovendae, commonly known as the golden panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, golden-yellow inflorescences that bloom during the dry season. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive silver-green hue that adds visual interest.

This variety is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
