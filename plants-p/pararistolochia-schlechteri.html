Pararistolochia schlechteri

Pararistolochia schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's aristolochia, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, intricate, and often heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, snake-like flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It is known for its medicinal properties, though its use is limited due to the presence of toxic compounds.

The species is valued in traditional medicine but requires careful handling due to its potential hazards.
