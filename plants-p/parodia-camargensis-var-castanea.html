Parodia camargensis var. castanea

Parodia camargensis var. castanea, commonly known as the Castanea Parodia, is a rare and unique cactus species native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense coating of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces striking, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a dramatic visual contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
