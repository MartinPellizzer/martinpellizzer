Pterocarpus saxatilis

Pterocarpus saxatilis, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its strength and beauty. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forest and woodland areas.

The tree is also known for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
