Piper haridasanii

Piper haridasanii, commonly known as the Indian black pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to the wet tropical regions of India.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its pungent, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant produces small, dark berries that contain the active compound piperine, responsible for its spicy flavor. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy appearance, while its stems are woody and often covered in fine hairs.

This species thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary properties.
