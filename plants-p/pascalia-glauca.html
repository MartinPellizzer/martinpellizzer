Pascalia glauca

Pascalia glauca, commonly known as blue pincushion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has silvery-green foliage and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal areas.

Pascalia glauca is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
