Prunus × nikaii

Prunus × nikaii, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Prunus mandshurica and Prunus serrulata.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters. The plant is characterized by its showy, single or double pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin, and the fruit is a small, yellow or orange drupe.

Prunus × nikaii is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and parks for its spring blossoms.
