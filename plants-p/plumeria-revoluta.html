Plumeria revoluta

Plumeria revoluta, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or pink. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down. Its woody, branching structure makes it well-suited for warm climates and full sun exposure.

The plant is also notable for its use in traditional medicine and as an ornamental species in landscaping.
