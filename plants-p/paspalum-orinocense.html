Paspalum orinocense

Paspalum orinocense, commonly known as Orinoco grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wetland and marshy environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Paspalum orinocense is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
