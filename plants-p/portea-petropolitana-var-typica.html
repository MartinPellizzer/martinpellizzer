Portea petropolitana var. typica

Portea petropolitana var. typica, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary growth forests.

Due to its valuable wood, it is also considered an endangered species.
