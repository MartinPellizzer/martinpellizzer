Peucedanum carvifolium var. glabrescens

Peucedanum carvifolium var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-leafed cow parsley, is a member of the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, glabrous (smooth) leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is found in temperate regions of Europe. It has a tall, slender stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
