Polygala alpestris proles morisiana

Polygala alpestris proles morisiana, commonly known as the Morisian rock-privet, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments. It has slender, often branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
