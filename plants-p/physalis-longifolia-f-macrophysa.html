Physalis longifolia f. macrophysa

Physalis longifolia f. macrophysa, commonly known as the giant husk tomato, is a variant of the common husk tomato.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America, particularly Brazil. The plant produces large, inflated calyces that enclose its small, yellow-orange berries. The leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and the stems are slender with a slightly woody base.

This cultivar is often grown for its ornamental value and edible fruits, which have a tangy, slightly tart flavor.
