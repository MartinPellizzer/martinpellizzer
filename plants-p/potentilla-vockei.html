Potentilla vockei

Potentilla vockei, commonly known as the Vocke's cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has pinnately divided leaves and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions.

Potentilla vockei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
