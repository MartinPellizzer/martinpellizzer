Pinus richardiana

Pinus richardiana, commonly known as the Mexican red pine, is a coniferous tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and long, needle-like leaves that grow in bundles of five. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown in color and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate to subtropical climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its timber and is also an important component of local ecosystems.
