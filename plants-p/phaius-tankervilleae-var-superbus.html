Phaius tankervilleae var. superbus

Phaius tankervilleae var. superbus, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant produces showy white or pink flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments with rich organic soil.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in ponds and wetlands for its aesthetic appeal.
