Poa macrocalyx var. sachalinensis

Poa macrocalyx var. sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the coastal regions of Sakhalin Island in Russia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, bluish-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and grasslands.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to stabilize sandy soils in its native habitat.
