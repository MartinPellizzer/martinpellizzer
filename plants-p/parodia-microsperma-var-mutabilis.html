Parodia microsperma var. mutabilis

Parodia microsperma var. mutabilis, commonly known as the variable parodia, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in white to pale blue spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
