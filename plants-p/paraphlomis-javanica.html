Paraphlomis javanica

Paraphlomis javanica, commonly known as Java parsnip, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in whorls, typically blue or violet in color.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and fragrant properties.
