Philodendron weberbaueri

Philodendron weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for birds. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouses.
