Pastinaca corsica

Pastinaca corsica, commonly known as the Corsican burdock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots are thick and tuberous, often used in traditional medicine.

Pastinaca corsica is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.
