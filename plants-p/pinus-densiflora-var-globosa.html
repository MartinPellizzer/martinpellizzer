Pinus densiflora var. globosa

Pinus densiflora var. globosa, commonly known as the Japanese white pine or globe pine, is a variant of the Japanese pine characterized by its dense, spherical crown.

It is native to southern Japan and is known for its slow growth and longevity. The plant features needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in clusters of five. Its cones are small and often persist on the branches for several years.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
