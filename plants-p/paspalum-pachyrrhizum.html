Paspalum pachyrrhizum

Paspalum pachyrrhizum, commonly known as the broadroot grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its robust root system, which allows it to thrive in a variety of soil types and environmental conditions. The plant typically grows in open areas, including grasslands and disturbed soils, and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle.

Paspalum pachyrrhizum is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
