Psidium friedrichsthalianum

Psidium friedrichsthalianum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or golden guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and seasonal blooms, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

The fruit is also used in culinary applications and traditional medicine.
