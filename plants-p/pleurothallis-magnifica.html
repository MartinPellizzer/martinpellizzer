Pleurothallis magnifica

Pleurothallis magnifica, commonly known as the magnificent pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are often brightly colored, with a complex structure typical of the orchid family. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and elongated, adapted for efficient water absorption in its natural habitat.

The genus Pleurothallis is known for its diversity, with many species exhibiting unique floral morphology and coloration.
