Potentilla verna var. opaciformis

Potentilla verna var. opaciformis, commonly known as the opaciform rose.

This plant is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The flowers are typically pale yellow or cream, appearing in clusters and blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a valuable addition to alpine or woodland gardens.
