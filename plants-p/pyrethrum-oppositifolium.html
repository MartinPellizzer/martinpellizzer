Pyrethrum oppositifolium

Pyrethrum oppositifolium, commonly known as the opposite-leaved pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its opposite arrangement of leaves, which gives it its scientific name. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically yellow or white in color. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Pyrethrum oppositifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
