Pycnanthus angolensis subsp. angolensis

Pycnanthus angolensis subsp.

angolensis, commonly known as the Angolan pycnanthus, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa. It typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often found in the understorey of tropical and subtropical forests.

The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, round fruits that contain one or two seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
