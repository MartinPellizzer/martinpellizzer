Pratia gilletii

Pratia gilletii, commonly known as the golden bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Pratia gilletii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
