Prunus sachalinensis f. macropetala

Prunus sachalinensis f. macropetala, commonly known as the large-petaled Japanese apricot, is a unique variant of the Japanese apricot species.

It is characterized by its notably larger, showy petals that enhance its ornamental value. The plant typically produces white or pale pink flowers in early spring, adding a striking visual element to gardens. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil with partial to full sun exposure.

This cultivar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated for its floral display.
