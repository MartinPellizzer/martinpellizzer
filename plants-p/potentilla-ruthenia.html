Potentilla ruthenia

Potentilla ruthenia, commonly known as the Ruthenian cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, edible berries in the fall.

Potentilla ruthenia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
