Potamogeton berchtoldii var. acuminatus

Potamogeton berchtoldii var. acuminatus, commonly known as the sharp-pointed pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged, and it relies on water currents for pollination. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its native habitats.
