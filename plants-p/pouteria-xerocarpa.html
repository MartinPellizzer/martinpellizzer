Pouteria xerocarpa

Pouteria xerocarpa, commonly known as the desert cheese tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and its ability to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible, cheese-like fruits. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a leathery texture.

Pouteria xerocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential use in traditional medicine.
