Paeonia carthalinica

Paeonia carthalinica, commonly known as the Carpathian peony, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Carpathian Mountains in Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and a robust root system adapted to rocky soils. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Paeonia carthalinica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
