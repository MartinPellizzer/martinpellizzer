Protea obovata

Protea obovata, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of flowers, with a central cone-like structure surrounded by bracts. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are leathery and oval-shaped, often covered in a silvery-white waxy coating.

Protea obovata is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators like birds and insects.
