Phlomis caucasica

Phlomis caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and silvery-gray, woolly leaves. The plant produces showy purple to pink flower heads that are composed of numerous tiny florets. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Phlomis caucasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
