Prunus padus var. petraea

Prunus padus var. petraea, commonly known as the rock cherry, is a variant of the common bird cherry.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, dark red berries. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn yellow in autumn.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
