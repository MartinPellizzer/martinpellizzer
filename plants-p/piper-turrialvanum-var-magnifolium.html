Piper turrialvanum var. magnifolium

Piper turrialvanum var. magnifolium, commonly known as the giant pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by red, peppercorn-like berries. It is cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of spice.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
