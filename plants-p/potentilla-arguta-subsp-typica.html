Potentilla arguta subsp. typica

Potentilla arguta subsp.

typica, commonly known as the silver cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August.

The plant has finely divided, silver-white leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats. Potentilla arguta subsp.

typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
