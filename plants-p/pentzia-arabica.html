Pentzia arabica

Pentzia arabica, commonly known as the Arabian coffee plant, is a shrub native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is closely related to the coffee genus Coffea and shares similar botanical features. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its historical significance in coffee production, it holds cultural and economic importance in the region.
