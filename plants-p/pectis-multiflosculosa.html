Pectis multiflosculosa

Pectis multiflosculosa, commonly known as the many-flowered pectis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a hairy texture.

Pectis multiflosculosa is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.
