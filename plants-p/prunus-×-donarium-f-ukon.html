Prunus × donarium f. ukon

Prunus × donarium f. ukon, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum.

This hybrid plant is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, sweet-tart fruits. It typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated margin, while the flowers are pink to white and appear in clusters.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms.
