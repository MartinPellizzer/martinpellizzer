Packera mancosiana

Packera mancosiana, commonly known as the Manchocan fleabane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats such as cliffs and slopes, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional use.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native range.
