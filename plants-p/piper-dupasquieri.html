Piper dupasquieri

Piper dupasquieri, commonly known as the Dupasquier's pepper, is a flowering plant belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that often twine around other vegetation. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
