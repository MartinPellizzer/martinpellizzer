Pyrus × bardoensis

Pyrus × bardoensis, commonly known as the Bardo pear, is a hybrid species of pear native to Algeria.

It is characterized by its deciduous nature, with heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible fruits that are typically small and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

The species is notable for its adaptability to arid conditions, making it a resilient choice for certain horticultural applications.
