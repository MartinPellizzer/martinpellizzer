Paspalum scrobiculatum var. longifolium

Paspalum scrobiculatum var. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved bottlebrush grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive inflorescence that resembles a bottlebrush. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically pink to reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
