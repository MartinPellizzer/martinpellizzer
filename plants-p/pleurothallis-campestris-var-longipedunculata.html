Pleurothallis campestris var. longipedunculata

Pleurothallis campestris var. longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-stemmed pleurothallis, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, pendant inflorescences that bear small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments with good air circulation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
