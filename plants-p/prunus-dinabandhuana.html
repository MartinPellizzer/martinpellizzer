Prunus dinabandhuana

Prunus dinabandhuana, commonly known as the Dinabandhuana cherry, is a species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
