Prangos ferulacea var. cylindracea

Prangos ferulacea var. cylindracea, commonly known as the cylindrical fennel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and finely divided, feathery leaves that resemble those of fennel. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
