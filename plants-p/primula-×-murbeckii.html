Primula × murbeckii

Primula × murbeckii, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding a splash of color to woodland gardens and rockeries. Its foliage consists of broad, waxy leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy.

Primula × murbeckii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ease of care.
