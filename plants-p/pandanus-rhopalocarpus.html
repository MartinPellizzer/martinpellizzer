Pandanus rhopalocarpus

Pandanus rhopalocarpus, commonly known as the giant pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that form a dense canopy. The plant produces large, fragrant inflorescences that bear yellowish-green flowers. Its roots are often used in traditional crafts, and the leaves are utilized in thatching and weaving.

This species is well adapted to coastal and saline environments, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
