Polypodium melanocaulon

Polypodium melanocaulon, commonly known as the black-stemmed maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black stems and delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically bright green. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fronds are bioluminescent in certain conditions, making it a unique and visually striking species.
