Ponthieva kraenzliniana

Ponthieva kraenzliniana, commonly known as the Schomburgkia, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, strap-like leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Ponthieva kraenzliniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a humid, warm environment.
