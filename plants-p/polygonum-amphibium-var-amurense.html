Polygonum amphibium var. amurense

Polygonum amphibium var. amurense, commonly known as the Amur knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features rhizomatous growth, allowing it to spread vegetatively through underground stems. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are typically reddish or purplish in color. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in dense clusters during the summer months.

It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.
