Piper araquei

Piper araquei, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela. The plant is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring compound leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruits are berries that turn red when mature, and the plant is known for its aromatic leaves used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
