Prunus nipponica f. alpina

Prunus nipponica f. alpina, commonly known as the Japanese alpine cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, adding a charming floral display. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a resilient, ornamental plant.
