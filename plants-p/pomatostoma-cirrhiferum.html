Pomatostoma cirrhiferum

Pomatostoma cirrhiferum, commonly known as the silverleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Pomatostoma cirrhiferum is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
