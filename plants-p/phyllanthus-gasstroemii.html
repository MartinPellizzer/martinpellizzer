Phyllanthus gasstroemii

Phyllanthus gasstroemii, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in umbels, and it produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed.

The plant is notable for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine.
