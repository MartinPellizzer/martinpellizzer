Pinanga arundinacea

Pinanga arundinacea, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark blue-black berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance.

Pinanga arundinacea is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
