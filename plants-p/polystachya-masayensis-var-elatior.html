Polystachya masayensis var. elatior

Polystachya masayensis var. elatior, commonly known as the tall orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its striking, elongated inflorescences that bear numerous showy flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or undergrowth, where it thrives in the dappled light. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
