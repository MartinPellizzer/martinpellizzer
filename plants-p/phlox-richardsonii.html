Phlox richardsonii

Phlox richardsonii, commonly known as Rocky Mountain phlox, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a low-growing perennial with showy clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Phlox richardsonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
