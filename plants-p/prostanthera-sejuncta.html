Prostanthera sejuncta

Prostanthera sejuncta, commonly known as the slender mintbush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal regions. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue or purple, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Prostanthera sejuncta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native gardens.
