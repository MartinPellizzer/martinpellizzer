Pyrus royleana

Pyrus royleana, commonly known as the Kashmir pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy white flowers that appear in spring. The fruit is a small, sweet, and juicy pear with a yellowish-green skin. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional horticulture.
