Polygonatum × tamaense

Polygonatum × tamaense, commonly known as the Japanese Solomon's Seal, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to East Asia, including regions of China, Japan, and Korea. The plant is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and clusters of small, white or greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and form a rosette at the base, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Polygonatum × tamaense is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
