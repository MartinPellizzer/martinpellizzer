Phlox caespitosa var. condensata

Phlox caespitosa var. condensata, commonly known as dense phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, mounding growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of pink to lavender. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 18 inches and features narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well-suited for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to the landscape.
