Petrorhagia prolifera subsp. velutina

Petrorhagia prolifera subsp.

velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf sandthrift, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive velvety texture. Its flowers are usually pink or reddish, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
