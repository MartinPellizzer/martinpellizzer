Polygonum tetragonum

Polygonum tetragonum, commonly known as Japanese knotweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its stout, bamboo-like stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is known for its aggressive growth and has become an invasive species in many parts of the world.

Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread rapidly and is difficult to eradicate.
