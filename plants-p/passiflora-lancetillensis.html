Passiflora lancetillensis

Passiflora lancetillensis, commonly known as the lancet-leaf passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by local wildlife and humans alike. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped, hence the species name, and are often used in traditional medicine.

Passiflora lancetillensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitats.
