Psoralea citridora

Psoralea citridora, commonly known as the lemon scented psoralea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant is known for its aromatic scent, which resembles that of lemon, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Psoralea citridora is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
