Protea coriacea

Protea coriacea, commonly known as the rough protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers clustered around a central stem. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are leathery and often have a rough texture, contributing to its common name.

The protea coriacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays an important role in local ecosystems.
