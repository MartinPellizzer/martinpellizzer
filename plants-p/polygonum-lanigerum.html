Polygonum lanigerum

Polygonum lanigerum, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its succulent stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and agricultural fields, and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a reddish tint, and the flowers are greenish-white with a distinctive bract.

Polygonum lanigerum is often considered a weed but has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
