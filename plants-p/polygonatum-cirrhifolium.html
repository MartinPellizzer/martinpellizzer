Polygonatum cirrhifolium

Polygonatum cirrhifolium, commonly known as the thread-leaf Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features long, arching stems that bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces oval, dark green leaves that are elongated and have a slightly wavy margin. Its roots are often harvested for their medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental and therapeutic uses.
