Panicum bifidum

Panicum bifidum, commonly known as two-lobed panicgrass, is a species of grass native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed florets, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.

Panicum bifidum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
