Pleurothallis hopfiana

Pleurothallis hopfiana, commonly known as the Hopf's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree branches or rocks. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically during the rainy season. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain, a characteristic of many epiphytic orchids.

Pleurothallis hopfiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
