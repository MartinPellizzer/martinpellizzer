Phaseolus tunkinensis

Phaseolus tunkinensis, commonly known as the Tunkin bean, is a species of legume native to Central America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds.

Due to its genetic diversity, it is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers.
