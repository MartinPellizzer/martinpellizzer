Phacelia hispida var. cicutaria

Phacelia hispida var. cicutaria, commonly known as the hairy phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
