Pleione moelleri

Pleione moelleri, commonly known as the Moeller's pleione, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

This terrestrial orchid produces a single, showy flower with a distinctive white or pale yellow coloration and a dark, velvety lip. It typically grows at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist, and shaded environments. The plant has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, aiding its survival in its native habitat.

Its blooming period usually occurs in late winter to early spring, making it a prized addition to specialized orchid collections.
