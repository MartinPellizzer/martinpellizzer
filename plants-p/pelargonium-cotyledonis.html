Pelargonium cotyledonis

Pelargonium cotyledonis, commonly known as the African geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to white. The leaves are typically rounded and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This species is noted for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
