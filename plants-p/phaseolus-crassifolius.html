Phaseolus crassifolius

Phaseolus crassifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf bean, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing legume characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are used in various traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.
