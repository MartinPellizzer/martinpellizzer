Phaseoloides xylocarpum

Phaseoloides xylocarpum, commonly known as the woodcarpenter bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It has a fibrous root system that helps in stabilizing soil and improving its structure.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and potential use in agroforestry.
