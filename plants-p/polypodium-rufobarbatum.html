Polypodium rufobarbatum

Polypodium rufobarbatum, commonly known as the red-haired fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are typically reddish-brown in color, especially when young. This fern is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including China, India, and Southeast Asia. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or tree trunks.

The plant's rhizomes are fibrous and can be used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
