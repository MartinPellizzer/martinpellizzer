Parthenocissus hederacea

Parthenocissus hederacea, commonly known as the Virginia creeper, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which typically consist of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a palmate pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its roots are fibrous and can spread aggressively, making it both a popular ornamental plant and a potential weed.

The Virginia creeper is also known for its ability to climb using adhesive pads on its tendrils.
