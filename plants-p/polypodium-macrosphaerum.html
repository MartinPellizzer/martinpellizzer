Polypodium macrosphaerum

Polypodium macrosphaerum, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant typically thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in rainforests or along stream banks. Its rhizome is thick and fleshy, allowing it to store moisture and nutrients.

The fronds are bi-directionally divided, with a distinctive pattern of pinnae that contribute to its ornamental appeal.
