Poiretia latisiliquosa

Poiretia latisiliquosa, commonly known as the broad-silique pomegranate, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its herbaceous growth habit and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, elongated pods containing seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Poiretia latisiliquosa is valued in horticulture for its unique foliage and potential medicinal properties.
