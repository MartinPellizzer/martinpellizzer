Phyllostachys erecta

Phyllostachys erecta, commonly known as the bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to eastern Asia.

It is a fast-growing, clump-forming plant that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant features long, slender culms (stems) that are hollow and have a characteristic bamboo-like texture. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Phyllostachys erecta is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
