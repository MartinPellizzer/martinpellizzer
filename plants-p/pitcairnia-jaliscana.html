Pitcairnia jaliscana

Pitcairnia jaliscana, commonly known as the Jalisco pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking inflorescences with brightly colored bracts, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
