Phlomoides affinis

Phlomoides affinis, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine, silver-gray hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, producing clusters of tiny blue to purple florets.

Phlomoides affinis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
