Pleurothallis aspergillum

Pleurothallis aspergillum, commonly known as the rough-leaved orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a rough, hairy texture, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually small and pale green, with a delicate structure typical of many orchids.

It is appreciated by botanists and orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and ecological significance in its native habitat.
