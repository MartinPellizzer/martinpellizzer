Peschiera australis

Peschiera australis, commonly known as the southern peschiera, is a perennial herb native to the southern regions of South America.

It features a fibrous root system and lanceolate, slightly hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
