Polystichum rigidum var. australe

Polystichum rigidum var. australe, commonly known as the Southern Brake, is a perennial fern native to Australia.

It features fronds that are typically rigid and upright, with a dark green color and a leathery texture. The plant produces spores on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
