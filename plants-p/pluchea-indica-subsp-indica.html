Pluchea indica subsp. indica

Pluchea indica subsp.

indica, commonly known as Indian cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Asia, particularly India and Southeast Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, hairy leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and wetlands. It produces small yellow flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
