Prunus × lannesiana f. affinis

Prunus × lannesiana f. affinis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring, making it a popular ornamental plant. The tree typically has a rounded canopy and small, oval-shaped leaves that turn green in the summer. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is often used in gardens and public spaces for its aesthetic value and cultural significance.
