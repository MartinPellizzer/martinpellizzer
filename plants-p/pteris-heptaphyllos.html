Pteris heptaphyllos

Pteris heptaphyllos, commonly known as the seven-leaf fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are divided into seven pairs of pinnae, giving it its common name. The plant has a slender, creeping rhizome and produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The botanical features of Pteris heptaphyllos make it a unique and interesting subject for study in botany and horticulture.
