Polypodium lycopodioides var. subdimorphum

Polypodium lycopodioides var. subdimorphum, commonly known as the small-leaf clubmoss, is a fern-like plant belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that form dense, mat-like growths on rocky or shaded forest floors. The plant produces sporangia on specialized stalks, which release spores for reproduction. It thrives in moist, humid environments and is often found in temperate regions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in understory plant communities.
