Phymatodes alata

Phymatodes alata, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, sprawling stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods that burst to disperse seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Phymatodes alata is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient plant in desert ecosystems.
