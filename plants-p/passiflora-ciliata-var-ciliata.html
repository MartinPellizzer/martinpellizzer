Passiflora ciliata var. ciliata

Passiflora ciliata var. ciliata, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a soft, velvety texture, and the stems are covered in fine, silky hairs.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
