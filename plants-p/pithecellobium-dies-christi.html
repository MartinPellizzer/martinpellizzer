Pithecellobium dies-christi

Pithecellobium dies-christi, commonly known as the yellow pithecellobium, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume that splits open to release small, edible seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in agroforestry systems.
