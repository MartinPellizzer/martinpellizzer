Plectranthus thorncroftii

Plectranthus thorncroftii, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct reddish or purple underside. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Plectranthus thorncroftii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
