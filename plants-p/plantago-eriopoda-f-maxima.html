Plantago eriopoda f. maxima

Plantago eriopoda f. maxima, commonly known as the giant plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
