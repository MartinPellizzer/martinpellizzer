Polygonum esquirolii

Polygonum esquirolii, commonly known as the Esquiroli's buckwheat, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive bracteate inflorescences and often triangular stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its small, pinkish to reddish flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Polygonum esquirolii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
