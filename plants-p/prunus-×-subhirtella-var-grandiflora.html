Prunus × subhirtella var. grandiflora

Prunus × subhirtella var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Yoshino cherry, is a deciduous ornamental tree native to Japan.

It is prized for its showy, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree has a compact, rounded form with slender branches and dark green, ovate leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and urban landscapes.

Its floral display makes it a symbol of spring and a key component in traditional Japanese gardens.
