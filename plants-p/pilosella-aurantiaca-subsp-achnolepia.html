Pilosella aurantiaca subsp. achnolepia

Pilosella aurantiaca subsp.

achnolepia, commonly known as the orange hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, while the flowers are yellow with orange centers.

It produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread across various regions.
