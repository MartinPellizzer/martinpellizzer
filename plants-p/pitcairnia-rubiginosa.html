Pitcairnia rubiginosa

Pitcairnia rubiginosa, commonly known as the red pitcairnia, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround the flower, giving it a dramatic and eye-catching appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are long, strap-like, and often covered in a waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

Pitcairnia rubiginosa is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
