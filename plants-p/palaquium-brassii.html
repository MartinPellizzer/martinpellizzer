Palaquium brassii

Palaquium brassii, commonly known as the African starfruit or yellow starfruit, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, star-shaped fruits that are typically yellow when ripe, though they may turn red or orange depending on the variety. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Palaquium brassii is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and its use in traditional medicine.
