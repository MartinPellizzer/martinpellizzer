Peperomia muricatulata

Peperomia muricatulata, commonly known as the velvet peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, dark green foliage with a velvety texture, and its leaves are often marked with distinctive white or silver veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red, which appear in clusters during the growing season. It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Peperomia muricatulata thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate humidity.
