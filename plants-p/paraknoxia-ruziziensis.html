Paraknoxia ruziziensis

Paraknoxia ruziziensis, commonly known as the Ruziziensis cycad, is a unique cycad species native to the Eastern African region.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, stiff, feather-like leaves. The plant produces cones, with the male cones being cylindrical and the female cones ovoid, typical of cycads. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a humid climate with partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
