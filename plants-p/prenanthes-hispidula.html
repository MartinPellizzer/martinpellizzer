Prenanthes hispidula

Prenanthes hispidula, commonly known as hairy waterplantain, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of streams. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. Its small, white to greenish flowers are arranged in dense clusters and bloom in late summer.

Prenanthes hispidula plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
