Pavonia serrana

Pavonia serrana, commonly known as the California rosemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pavonia serrana is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
