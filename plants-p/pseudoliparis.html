Pseudoliparis

Pseudoliparis, commonly known as the sea hare, is a genus of marine gastropod mollusks belonging to the family Anisophyliidae.

These organisms are characterized by their soft, often colorful bodies and the presence of a distinctive, fleshy mantle. Pseudoliparis species are typically found in shallow coastal waters and are known for their ability to secrete a variety of bioactive compounds. They play an important role in marine ecosystems as both prey and predators.

Their unique morphology and ecological significance make them a subject of interest in marine biology and pharmacology.
