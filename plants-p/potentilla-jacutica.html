Potentilla jacutica

Potentilla jacutica, commonly known as the Jacutian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually divided into five parts, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.". It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or steppe environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
