Pternandra cordata

Pternandra cordata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
