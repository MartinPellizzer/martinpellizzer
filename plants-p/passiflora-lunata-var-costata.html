Passiflora lunata var. costata

Passiflora lunata var. costata, commonly known as the ridged moonflower, is a striking tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a unique blend of blue, purple, and white petals, surrounded by a crown of filaments. The leaves are heart-shaped and often have a rough, textured surface. This plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
