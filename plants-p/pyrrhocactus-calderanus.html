Pyrrhocactus calderanus

Pyrrhocactus calderanus, commonly known as the red-spined cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in red, spiny tubercles that provide protection against herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warm season, which attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, adapting to its harsh, dry environment.

Pyrrhocactus calderanus is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in desert gardens.
