Panicum lindheimeri var. typicum

Panicum lindheimeri var. typicum, commonly known as Lindheimer's panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils in swampy areas and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a bluish-green color and a hairy texture.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
