Phleum rhaeticum

Phleum rhaeticum, commonly known as Alpine fescue, is a perennial grass native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

Phleum rhaeticum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native habitat.
