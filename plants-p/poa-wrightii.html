Poa wrightii

Poa wrightii, commonly known as Wright's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland areas.
