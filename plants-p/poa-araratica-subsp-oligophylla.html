Poa araratica subsp. oligophylla

Poa araratica subsp.

oligophylla, commonly known as the Ararati grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves.

The plant typically thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, two-rowed spikelets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
