Plantago purshii var. breviscapa

Plantago purshii var. breviscapa, commonly known as the short-capped plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as stream banks and meadows. The plant features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. Its flowering stems bear dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
