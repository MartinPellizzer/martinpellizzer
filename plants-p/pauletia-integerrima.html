Pauletia integerrima

Pauletia integerrima, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces showy, pink or white flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, contributing to its drought-resistant characteristics.

Pauletia integerrima is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
