Prunus cerasifera var. elegans

Prunus cerasifera var. elegans, commonly known as the sweet cherry laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, with a smooth, greyish bark. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart cherries in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it suitable for hedges or specimen planting.
