Potentilla kingii var. kingii

Potentilla kingii var. kingii, commonly known as King's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has five leaflets arranged in a compact rosette, with hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant collections and ecological restoration projects.
