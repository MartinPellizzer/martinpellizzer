Penstemon laricifolius subsp. typicus

Penstemon laricifolius subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the white beard scale penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and its clusters of showy, tubular white to pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
