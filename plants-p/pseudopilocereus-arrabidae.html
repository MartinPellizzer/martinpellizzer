Pseudopilocereus arrabidae

Pseudopilocereus arrabidae, commonly known as the Arrabida cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically green and covered in spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming at night. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This cactus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh conditions.
