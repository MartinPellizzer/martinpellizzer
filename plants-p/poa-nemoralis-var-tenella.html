Poa nemoralis var. tenella

Poa nemoralis var. tenella, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a variety of the common bentgrass.

It is a low-growing, tufted perennial grass that typically forms dense mats in moist, shaded environments. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers.

This plant is often found in woodlands, heaths, and along stream banks, contributing to soil stabilization and habitat for various invertebrates.
