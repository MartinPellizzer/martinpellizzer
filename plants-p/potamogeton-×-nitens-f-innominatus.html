Potamogeton × nitens f. innominatus

Potamogeton × nitens f. innominatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 mm wide.

It is native to North America and thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has small, green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it reproduces both sexually and vegetatively through rhizomes. Its morphology is adapted to submerged or floating growth, with leaves that are often partially or fully submerged in water.

This species plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
