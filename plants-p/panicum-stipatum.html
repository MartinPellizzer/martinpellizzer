Panicum stipatum

Panicum stipatum, commonly known as silver hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose, open panicle with pale green to yellowish flowers.

Panicum stipatum plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
