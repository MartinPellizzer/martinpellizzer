Poa longifolia

Poa longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color and have a fine texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides.

Poa longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stability.
