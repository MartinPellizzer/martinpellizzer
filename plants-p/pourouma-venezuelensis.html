Pourouma venezuelensis

Pourouma venezuelensis, commonly known as the Venezuelan areca, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a tall, slender palm with a smooth, unbranched stem and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, round fruits with a fibrous interior. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential economic importance, it is of interest for conservation and horticultural studies.
