Piptatherum flaccidum var. pubescens

Piptatherum flaccidum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, pond edges, and wet meadows. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are densely covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, three-ranked flowers.

This species is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.
