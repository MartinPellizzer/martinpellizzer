Pachycormus discolor var. pubescens

Pachycormus discolor var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy discolor buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue-black berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
