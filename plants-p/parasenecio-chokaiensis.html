Parasenecio chokaiensis

Parasenecio chokaiensis, commonly known as Chokai's senecio, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it forms a low-growing, mat-like structure. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal in botanical gardens.
