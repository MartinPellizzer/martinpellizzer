Peiranisia oxyadena

Peiranisia oxyadena, commonly known as the red-stemmed peiranisia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
