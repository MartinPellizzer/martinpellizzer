Polystachya aristulifera

Polystachya aristulifera, commonly known as the feather-leaf bromeliad, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central pool to collect rainwater, a common adaptation in its natural habitat. The plant produces striking inflorescences with long, slender flowers that are often brightly colored. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a waxy surface, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Polystachya aristulifera is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
