Phlox vernoniana

Phlox vernoniana, commonly known as the Vernon's phlox, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to lavender flowers in the summer months. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Phlox vernoniana thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
