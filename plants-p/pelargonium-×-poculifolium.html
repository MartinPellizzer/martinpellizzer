Pelargonium × poculifolium

Pelargonium × poculifolium, commonly known as the shield-leaf geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive shield-shaped leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically features showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It is a compact, trailing plant often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
