Phaius pulchellus

Phaius pulchellus, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in dense spikes. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Phaius pulchellus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
