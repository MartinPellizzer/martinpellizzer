Philadelphus × maximus

Philadelphus × maximus, commonly known as the common mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Philadelphus × maximus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
