Pulicaria olivascens

Pulicaria olivascens, commonly known as the olive-hairy tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

Pulicaria olivascens is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
