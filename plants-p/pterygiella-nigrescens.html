Pterygiella nigrescens

Pterygiella nigrescens, commonly known as the black-leaved ptergyiella, is a small, herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its dark green to blackish foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually narrow and may have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Pterygiella nigrescens is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
