Phylica rubra

Phylica rubra, commonly known as the red phylica, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that are pollinated by birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Phylica rubra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its hardiness and drought tolerance.
