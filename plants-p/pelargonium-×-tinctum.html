Pelargonium × tinctum

Pelargonium × tinctum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a unique combination of traits from its parent species.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, glossy leaves that resemble those of ivy, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, Pelargonium × tinctum is widely cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant.
