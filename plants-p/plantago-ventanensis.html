Plantago ventanensis

Plantago ventanensis, commonly known as the Ventanese plantain, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern South America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom during the cooler months. It is well adapted to dry environments, with deep roots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its resilience in harsh conditions.
