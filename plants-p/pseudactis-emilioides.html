Pseudactis emilioides

Pseudactis emilioides, commonly known as the Emilia's false dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
