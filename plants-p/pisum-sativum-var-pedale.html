Pisum sativum var. pedale

Pisum sativum var. pedale, commonly known as the sweet pea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, producing tendrils that allow it to ascend supports. The plant features compound leaves with trifoliate arrangements and produces vibrant, fragrant flowers in a variety of colors. Its botanical name reflects its origin from the species *Pisum sativum*, with the variety *pedale* indicating its distinctive floral morphology.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and are often harvested for culinary or ornamental use.
