Piper evingeri

Piper evingeri, commonly known as the Evinger's pepper, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by berries that turn red when mature. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent.

Piper evingeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of mild spices.
