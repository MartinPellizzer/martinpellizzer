Pobeguinea afzeliana

Pobeguinea afzeliana, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its strength and aesthetic qualities. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by small, round fruits.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
