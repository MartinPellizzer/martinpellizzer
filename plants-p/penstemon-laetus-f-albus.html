Penstemon laetus f. albus

Penstemon laetus f. albus, commonly known as the white penstemon, is a cultivar of the penstemon genus native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale lavender flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and features lance-shaped, gray-green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
