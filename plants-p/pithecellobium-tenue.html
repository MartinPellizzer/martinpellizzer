Pithecellobium tenue

Pithecellobium tenue, commonly known as the slender pithecellobium, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows to a height of 1-3 meters, featuring trifoliate leaves and small, fragrant white or yellow flowers. Its fruit is a legume that splits open to release seeds, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowers.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
