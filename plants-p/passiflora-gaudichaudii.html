Passiflora gaudichaudii

Passiflora gaudichaudii, commonly known as the Gaudichaud's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits that are similar in appearance to those of other passionfruit species. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject for horticultural study and ornamental gardening.
