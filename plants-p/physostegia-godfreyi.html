Physostegia godfreyi

Physostegia godfreyi, commonly known as Godfrey's blue flag, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly toothed margin.

Physostegia godfreyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
