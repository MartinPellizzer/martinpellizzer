Pseudocranichis

Pseudocranichis, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated leaves and delicate, showy flowers that resemble the shape of a ghostly figure. The plant typically grows on the bark of trees in wet, shaded environments, often in swampy or moist forest areas. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and decaying organic matter.

Pseudocranichis is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ecological significance and conservation challenges.
