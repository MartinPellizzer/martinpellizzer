Pandanus papeariensis

Pandanus papeariensis, commonly known as the Papeariensis pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used for thatching and weaving. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the top of the stem. Its roots system is fibrous and helps it anchor in sandy or coastal soils.

The plant is well adapted to saline environments and is often found in coastal areas and mangrove forests.
