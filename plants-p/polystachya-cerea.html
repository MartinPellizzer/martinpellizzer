Polystachya cerea

Polystachya cerea, commonly known as the ivory spike orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pale green to white flowers that resemble spikes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky areas. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces distinctive, elongated inflorescences.

Polystachya cerea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
