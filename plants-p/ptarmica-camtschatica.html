Ptarmica camtschatica

Ptarmica camtschatica, commonly known as the Siberian pine, is a coniferous tree native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cones that hang downward, typical of the Pinaceae family. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and has a lifespan that can exceed several hundred years. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against harsh winter conditions.

The plant plays a significant role in its ecosystem, offering habitat and food for various wildlife species.
