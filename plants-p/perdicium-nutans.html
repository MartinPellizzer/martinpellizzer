Perdicium nutans

Perdicium nutans, commonly known as the nodding lily, is a flowering plant belonging to the Liliaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that droop downward, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Perdicium nutans produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
