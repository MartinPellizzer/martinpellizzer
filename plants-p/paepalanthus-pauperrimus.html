Paepalanthus pauperrimus

Paepalanthus pauperrimus, commonly known as the poor man's pincushion, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its unique, spiky, yellow flowers that resemble small pincushions. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has small, narrow leaves and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
