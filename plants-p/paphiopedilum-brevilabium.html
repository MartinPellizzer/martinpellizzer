Paphiopedilum brevilabium

Paphiopedilum brevilabium, commonly known as the short-lipped lady's slipper, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a unique floral structure with a pouch-like lip that is shorter than the sepals, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with a contrasting purple or brown lip.

Paphiopedilum brevilabium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beauty and rarity.
