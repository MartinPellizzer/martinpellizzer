Phlox maculata subsp. pyramidalis

Phlox maculata subsp.

pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of showy, pink to red flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
