Piper malalaganum

Piper malalaganum, commonly known as the Malagangan pepper, is a climbing vine native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in local remedies for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is valued for both its medicinal properties and culinary uses.
