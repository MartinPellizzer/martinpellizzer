Piper austromexicanum

Piper austromexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a glossy surface.

The flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters, and the plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible.
