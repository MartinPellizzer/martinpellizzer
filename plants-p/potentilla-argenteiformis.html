Potentilla argenteiformis

Potentilla argenteiformis, commonly known as silverleaf potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is well adapted to arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
