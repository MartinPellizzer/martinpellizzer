Plantago afra var. sicula

Plantago afra var. sicula, commonly known as Sicilian plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
