Pleurothallis filiformis

Pleurothallis filiformis, commonly known as the thread-like orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with a subtle fragrance, and bloom during the rainy season.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
