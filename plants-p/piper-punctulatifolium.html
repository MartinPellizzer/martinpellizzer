Piper punctulatifolium

Piper punctulatifolium, commonly known as the spotted-leaf pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves that are often marked with distinctive white or pale spots. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by tiny, peppercorn-like fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in warm climates.
