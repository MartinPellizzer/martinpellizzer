Pithecellobium dependens

Pithecellobium dependens, commonly known as the hanging acacia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
