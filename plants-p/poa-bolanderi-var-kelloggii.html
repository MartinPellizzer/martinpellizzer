Poa bolanderi var. kelloggii

Poa bolanderi var. kelloggii, commonly known as the Kellogg's bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growth patterns and is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves. This variety thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along streambanks. Its small, spike-like inflorescences produce numerous tiny seeds, aiding in its propagation.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
