Pseudocymopterus bipinnatus

Pseudocymopterus bipinnatus, commonly known as the two-feathered fern, is a species of fern characterized by its intricate, bipinnate fronds.

The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, with long, creeping rhizomes that allow it to spread across moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are divided into multiple pairs of pinnae, giving it a finely divided, lacy appearance. The plant thrives in humid, temperate climates and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate moisture and light conditions.
