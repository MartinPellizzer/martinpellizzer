Potentilla wimanniana proles wiemannioides

Potentilla wimanniana proles wiemannioides, commonly known as the Wimmer's potentilla, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and floral display.
