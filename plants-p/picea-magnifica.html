Picea magnifica

Picea magnifica, commonly known as the grand fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender form and dense, pyramidal shape, with dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces small, cylindrical cones that hang from the branches, containing winged seeds. Its bark is thin and reddish-brown, becoming furrowed with age.

The grand fir is valued for its timber and is also a popular ornamental plant in landscaping.
