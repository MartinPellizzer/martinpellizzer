Parkia brunonis

Parkia brunonis, commonly known as the yellow shower tree, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree's fruit is a pod containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often planted for its ornamental value and shade.

The plant also has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
