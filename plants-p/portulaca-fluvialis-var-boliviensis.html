Portulaca fluvialis var. boliviensis

Portulaca fluvialis var. boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian river portulaca, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Bolivia.

It is a succulent species that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found along riverbanks and rocky outcrops. The plant features fleshy, cylindrical stems and produces small, vibrant yellow or orange flowers. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh conditions.
