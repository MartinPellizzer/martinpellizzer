Pimpinella saxifraga subsp. magna

Pimpinella saxifraga subsp.

magna, commonly known as the giant rock pepper, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with finely divided leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clustered umbels.

The subspecies magna is distinguished by its larger size compared to other subspecies of Pimpinella saxifraga.
