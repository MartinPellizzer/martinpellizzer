Pyrus ussuriensis var. nankaiensis

Pyrus ussuriensis var. nankaiensis, commonly known as the Nankai pear, is a variant of the Ussuri pear native to eastern China.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically sweet and aromatic.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
