Panicum hirtivaginum

Panicum hirtivaginum, commonly known as hairy panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, fibrous foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its hairy leaves and stems provide it with a distinctive appearance, and it is often found in disturbed soils or open habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
