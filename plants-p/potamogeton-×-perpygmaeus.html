Potamogeton × perpygmaeus

Potamogeton × perpygmaeus, commonly known as the narrowleaf pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton pygmaeus.

It is typically found in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams, where it thrives in shallow, nutrient-rich waters. The plant has long, slender leaves that are often submerged, with small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in clusters near the water surface. It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small fish and invertebrates.

Due to its hybrid nature, it can exhibit variability in morphology, making it challenging to distinguish from its parent species in some cases.
