Piper nudibracteatum

Piper nudibracteatum, commonly known as the naked-bract pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. Its fruit, a small berry, is typically green when unripe and turns red when mature.

The botanical features of Piper nudibracteatum make it an interesting subject for studies in tropical botany and plant morphology.
