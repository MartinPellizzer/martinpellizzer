Pittosporum ledermannii

Pittosporum ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, which bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
