Passiflora aurantioides

Passiflora aurantioides, commonly known as the yellow passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which feature intricate structures including a crown of filaments and a distinctive corona. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and can grow quite long.

This species is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
