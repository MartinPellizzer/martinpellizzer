Photinia undulata var. formosana

Photinia undulata var. formosana, commonly known as the Formosan photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated margins and a distinctive red or orange hue during the fall season. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that attract birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
