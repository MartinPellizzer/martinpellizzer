Papaver rhoeas var. giganteum

Papaver rhoeas var. giganteum, commonly known as the giant poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its larger, more showy flowers.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing up to 1.5 meters in height. The flowers are bright red with a white or yellow center, and they bloom from late spring to early autumn. The plant has a taproot system and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
