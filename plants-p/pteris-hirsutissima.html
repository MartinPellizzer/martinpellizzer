Pteris hirsutissima

Pteris hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features fronds that are typically pinnately divided with a distinctive hairy texture on the underside. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near water sources. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Pteris hirsutissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
