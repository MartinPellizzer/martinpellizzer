Polygonum punctatum var. tacubayanum

Polygonum punctatum var. tacubayanum, commonly known as the Tacubayan knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with a hairy surface, and the plant often grows in moist, shaded environments. It is part of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other species known for their distinctive bracts and seeds.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
