Piper pseudoperfugii

Piper pseudoperfugii, commonly known as the Malayan pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that are typically unisexual. Its fruits are drupes that mature to a dark purple color, containing a single seed.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
