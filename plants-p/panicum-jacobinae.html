Panicum jacobinae

Panicum jacobinae, commonly known as Jacob's grass, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often reddish-colored stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental appeal and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
