Pleurothallis dorotheae

Pleurothallis dorotheae, commonly known as the Dorothea Orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence bears small, clustered blooms in shades of white, yellow, or green.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
