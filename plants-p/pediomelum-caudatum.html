Pediomelum caudatum

Pediomelum caudatum, commonly known as the spurred pincushion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its unique, spurs-like bracts that enclose its small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its ornamental value comes from its distinctive appearance, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Pediomelum caudatum is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
