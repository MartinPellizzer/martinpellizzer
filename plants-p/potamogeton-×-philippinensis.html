Potamogeton × philippinensis

Potamogeton × philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a submerged herb that typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, outcompeting native species and altering water ecosystems.
