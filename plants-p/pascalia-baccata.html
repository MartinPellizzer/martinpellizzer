Pascalia baccata

Pascalia baccata, commonly known as the black-fruited pascalia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruit is a small, dark purple to black berry, which contains the seeds.

Pascalia baccata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
