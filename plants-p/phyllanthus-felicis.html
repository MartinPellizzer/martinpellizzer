Phyllanthus felicis

Phyllanthus felicis, commonly known as the happy leaf plant, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light.

Phyllanthus felicis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
