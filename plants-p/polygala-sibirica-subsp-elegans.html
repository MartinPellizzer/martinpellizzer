Polygala sibirica subsp. elegans

Polygala sibirica subsp.

elegans, commonly known as the Siberian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in racemes.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine and steppe habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of arid and semi-arid environments.
