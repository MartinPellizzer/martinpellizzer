Phyteuma delphinense

Phyteuma delphinense, commonly known as blue phyteuma, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged in a basal rosette.
