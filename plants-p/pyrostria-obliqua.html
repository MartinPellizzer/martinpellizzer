Pyrostria obliqua

Pyrostria obliqua, commonly known as the oblique pyrostria, is a rare and striking member of the Arecaceae family, native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and gracefully arching fronds that give it a distinctive, palm-like appearance. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, followed by dark, leathery fruits. Due to its limited natural habitat and slow growth rate, it is considered endangered and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
