Phyllanthus villosus

Phyllanthus villosus, commonly known as hairy phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its small, green, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small yellowish flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

Phyllanthus villosus is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
