Primula atropurpurea

Primula atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It produces showy, violet-purple flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

Primula atropurpurea is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize in woodland settings.
