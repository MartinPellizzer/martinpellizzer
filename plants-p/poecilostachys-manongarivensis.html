Poecilostachys manongarivensis

Poecilostachys manongarivensis, commonly known as the Manongari grass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its flowering stems produce spikelets that are often clustered at the base, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
