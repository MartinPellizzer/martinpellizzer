Piptolepis procumbens

Piptolepis procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate piptolepis, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats on the ground, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs.

Piptolepis procumbens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
