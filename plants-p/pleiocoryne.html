Pleiocoryne

Pleiocoryne, commonly known as the golden star, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble stars, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, tubular flowers arranged in a composite head.

Pleiocoryne is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
