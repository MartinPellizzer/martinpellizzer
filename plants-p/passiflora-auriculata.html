Passiflora auriculata

Passiflora auriculata, commonly known as the golden passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which feature intricate structures including a crown of filaments and a distinctive spurred corona. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Passiflora auriculata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
