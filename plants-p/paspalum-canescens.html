Paspalum canescens

Paspalum canescens, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray blades that are often covered in a dense layer of hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Paspalum canescens is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
