Pseudotsuga menziesii subsp. glaucescens

Pseudotsuga menziesii subsp.

glaucescens, commonly known as the silver fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States and parts of Canada. It is characterized by its bluish-gray bark and needle-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in forest ecosystems and a valuable source of timber.
