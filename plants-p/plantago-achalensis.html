Plantago achalensis

Plantago achalensis, commonly known as the Achalensis plantain, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Pakistan.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are broad and hairy. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in rocky and sandy soils, showing adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
