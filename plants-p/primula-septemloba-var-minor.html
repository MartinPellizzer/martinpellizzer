Primula septemloba var. minor

Primula septemloba var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy, ovate leaves that are often tinged with silver. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest floors.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
