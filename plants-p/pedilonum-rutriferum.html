Pedilonum rutriferum

Pedilonum rutriferum, commonly known as the crimson fruit vine, is a tropical climbing plant characterized by its vigorous growth and striking, showy flowers.

It produces clusters of small, bright red berries that are both ornamental and edible, though they are typically consumed in moderation. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its long, flexible stems. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Pedilonum rutriferum is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and unique fruit.
