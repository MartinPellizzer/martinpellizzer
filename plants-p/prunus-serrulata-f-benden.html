Prunus serrulata f. benden

Prunus serrulata f. benden, commonly known as the Benden cherry, is a botanical variant of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaf margins and compact growth habit. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Its genetic traits make it a subject of interest in botanical research and horticulture.
