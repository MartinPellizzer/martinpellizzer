Polycnemum majus subsp. mediterraneum

Polycnemum majus subsp.

mediterraneum, commonly known as the Mediterranean polycnemum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks.

The plant has erect, sometimes branched stems and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a rough texture, and it is characterized by its ability to form dense vegetation.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
