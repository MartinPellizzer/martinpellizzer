Pseuderanthemum tomentellum

Pseuderanthemum tomentellum, commonly known as the woolly false heather, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and hairy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers, which are often borne in dense inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage is frequently used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
