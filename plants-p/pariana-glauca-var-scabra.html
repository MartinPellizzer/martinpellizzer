Pariana glauca var. scabra

Pariana glauca var. scabra, commonly known as the roughleaf pariana, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its grayish-blue foliage and rough, scabrous texture on its leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetland or swampy areas. It produces small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
