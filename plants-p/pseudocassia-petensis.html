Pseudocassia petensis

Pseudocassia petensis, commonly known as the yellow crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, yellow flower clusters that bloom in summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Pseudocassia petensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
