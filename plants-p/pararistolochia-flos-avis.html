Pararistolochia flos-avis

Pararistolochia flos-avis, commonly known as the bird's nest vine, is a climbing plant belonging to the Aristolochiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, bird's nest-like flowers that are often greenish or brownish in color. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a gelatinous coating. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it contains toxic compounds that require caution in handling.
