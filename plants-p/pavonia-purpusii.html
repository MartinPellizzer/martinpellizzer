Pavonia purpusii

Pavonia purpusii, commonly known as the Mexican coral bean, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, with compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
