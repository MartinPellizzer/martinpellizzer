Pseudomertensia racemosa

Pseudomertensia racemosa, commonly known as the mountain mallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or open woodland habitats. The plant features upright stems with alternate, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its showy, clustered white or pink flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Pseudomertensia racemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
