Piper parcum

Piper parcum, commonly known as the Paracu tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
