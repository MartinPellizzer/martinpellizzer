Pimelea hirsuta subsp. hirsuta

Pimelea hirsuta subsp.

hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy swamp bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, glandular-hairy foliage and striking clusters of bright red, brush-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in wet or swampy habitats, such as heathlands and riverine areas. Its flowers are rich in nectar and attract a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy leaves and the specific morphology of its inflorescences.
