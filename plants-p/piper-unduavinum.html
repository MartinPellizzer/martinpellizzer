Piper unduavinum

Piper unduavinum, commonly known as the creeping piper, is a tropical vine native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, opposite leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are typically greenish-white and clustered in umbels, while the fruit is a small, three-lobed berry.

Piper unduavinum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
