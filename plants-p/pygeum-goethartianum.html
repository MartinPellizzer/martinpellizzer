Pygeum goethartianum

Pygeum goethartianum, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain small, edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to seasonal rainfall patterns, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and ecosystem.
