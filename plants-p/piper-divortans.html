Piper divortans

Piper divortans, commonly known as the split pipevine or split pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that mature to a dark purple color.

Piper divortans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
