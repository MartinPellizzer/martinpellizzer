Pteris lanigera

Pteris lanigera, commonly known as the hairy water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appearance.

Pteris lanigera plays an important ecological role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for small organisms.
