Prunus pudibunda

Prunus pudibunda, commonly known as the Japanese flowering cherry or Somei Yoshino, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is celebrated for its showy, single pink flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a striking visual display. The tree has slender, branching habits and small, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in temperate climates and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species.

Its botanical features make it a key species in traditional Japanese gardens and urban landscapes.
