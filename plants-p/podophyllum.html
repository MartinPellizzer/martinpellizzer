Podophyllum

Podophyllum, commonly known as the American belladonna, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Berberidaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are broad and evergreen, often with a waxy texture.

Podophyllum species are also known for their toxic properties, containing compounds like podophyllotoxin.
