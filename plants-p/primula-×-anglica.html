Primula × anglica

Primula × anglica, commonly known as the English primrose, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Primula vulgaris and Primula veris.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 15–30 cm, featuring heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and white. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for centuries for its early spring blooms.
