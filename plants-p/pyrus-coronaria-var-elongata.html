Pyrus coronaria var. elongata

Pyrus coronaria var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated crabapple, is a variant of the common crabapple.

It is a small deciduous tree or shrub native to Europe and western Asia. The plant features elongated, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in spring before the leaves fully develop.

The fruit is a small, sour apple that ripens in late summer, often used in culinary or ornamental contexts.
