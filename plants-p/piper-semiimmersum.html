Piper semiimmersum

Piper semiimmersum, commonly known as the climbing pepper vine, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is a woody vine that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, peppery berries.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
