Petroselinum sativum var. silvestre

Petroselinum sativum var. silvestre, commonly known as wild parsley, is a biennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like carrots and dill. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and small white or greenish flowers that appear in umbrella-shaped clusters. It is valued for its peppery, slightly sweet flavor and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Wild parsley is also known for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
