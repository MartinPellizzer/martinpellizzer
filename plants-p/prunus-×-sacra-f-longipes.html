Prunus × sacra f. longipes

Prunus × sacra f. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed cherry, is a hybrid plant that exhibits unique botanical features.

It is characterized by its elongated stems and distinctive floral structure, which contribute to its ornamental value. The plant typically produces small, sweet-tasting fruits that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its bark is smooth and reddish in color.

This cultivar is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual impact.
