Plumbago socotrana

Plumbago socotrana, commonly known as Socotra's plumbago, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula, particularly the island of Socotra.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, featuring woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and coastal areas.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species in its native habitat.
