Paracalanthe tricarinata

Paracalanthe tricarinata, commonly known as the three-ribbed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short, horizontal rhizome and a rosette of elliptic, dark green leaves. The plant produces erect, hairy inflorescences bearing small, fragrant flowers with three prominent longitudinal carinae. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinct lip that is often marked with purple or brown.

Paracalanthe tricarinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
