Paragophyton spermacocinum

Paragophyton spermacocinum, commonly known as the "spermacocin" plant, is a unique species belonging to the order Spermatophyta, which includes seed-producing plants.

It is characterized by its small, leaf-like structures and the presence of specialized reproductive organs. The plant exhibits a simple vascular system, which allows for the transport of water and nutrients. Its reproductive structures are typically found in clusters, facilitating the dispersal of its spores.

This ancient plant provides valuable insights into the early evolution of seed plants.
