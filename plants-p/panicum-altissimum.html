Panicum altissimum

Panicum altissimum, commonly known as tall panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can reach heights of up to 6 feet. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across open fields and disturbed areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue when young.

Panicum altissimum is often used in erosion control due to its extensive root system and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
