Polygala brachysepala

Polygala brachysepala, commonly known as the narrow-sepaled milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually small and opposite, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Polygala brachysepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
