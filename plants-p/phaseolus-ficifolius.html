Phaseolus ficifolius

Phaseolus ficifolius, commonly known as the evergreen bean or tree bean, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a perennial shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

Its morphology and growth habits make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
