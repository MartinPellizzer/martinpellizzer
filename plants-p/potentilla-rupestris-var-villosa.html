Potentilla rupestris var. villosa

Potentilla rupestris var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine plantings.
