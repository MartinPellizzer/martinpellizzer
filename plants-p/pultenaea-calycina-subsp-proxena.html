Pultenaea calycina subsp. proxena

Pultenaea calycina subsp.

proxena, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bush vetch, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It features small, elliptical leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive narrow shape.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its small, bean-like pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.
