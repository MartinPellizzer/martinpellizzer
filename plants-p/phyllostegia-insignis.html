Phyllostegia insignis

Phyllostegia insignis, commonly known as the mountain mint, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a minty scent when crushed.

Phyllostegia insignis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
