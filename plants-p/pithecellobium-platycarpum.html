Pithecellobium platycarpum

Pithecellobium platycarpum, commonly known as the flat-fruit acacia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly Mexico and Central America. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a flat, pod-like structure that contains several seeds.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
