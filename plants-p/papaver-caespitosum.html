Papaver caespitosum

Papaver caespitosum, commonly known as the prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers and a dense, spiny foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, thriving in well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
