Panicum hamiltonii

Panicum hamiltonii, commonly known as Hamilton's panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces spikelets that are clustered in panicles, which are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental use.
