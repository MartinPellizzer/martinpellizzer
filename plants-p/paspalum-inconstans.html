Paspalum inconstans

Paspalum inconstans, commonly known as the variable grass or variable paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Paspalum inconstans is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
