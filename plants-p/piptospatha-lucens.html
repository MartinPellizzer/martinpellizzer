Piptospatha lucens

Piptospatha lucens, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, panicle-like inflorescences that produce numerous small, yellow flowers in the spring. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth margin and a glossy green surface. The tree has a rapid growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
