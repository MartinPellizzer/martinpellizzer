Poecilostachys decaryana

Poecilostachys decaryana, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense clumps.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological landscaping.
