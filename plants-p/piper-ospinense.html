Piper ospinense

Piper ospinense, commonly known as the Ospina pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper and chili peppers. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters, and it produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
