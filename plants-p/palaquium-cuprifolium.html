Palaquium cuprifolium

Palaquium cuprifolium, commonly known as the copperleaf or copper-leaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland rainforests. The plant is characterized by its glossy, copper-colored leaves that give it its common name. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale yellow, followed by edible, fleshy fruits.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
