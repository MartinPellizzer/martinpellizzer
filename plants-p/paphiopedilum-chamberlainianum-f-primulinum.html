Paphiopedilum chamberlainianum f. primulinum

Paphiopedilum chamberlainianum f. primulinum, commonly known as the golden lip orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green foliage, with flowers that feature a unique, lip-like structure in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant and exotic appearance.
