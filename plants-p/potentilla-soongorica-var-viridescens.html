Potentilla soongorica var. viridescens

Potentilla soongorica var. viridescens, commonly known as the greenleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually a vibrant green, contributing to its ornamental value. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a desirable plant for low-maintenance gardens.
