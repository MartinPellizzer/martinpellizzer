Paeonia mascula var. ovatifolia

Paeonia mascula var. ovatifolia, commonly known as the ovate-leaf peony, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a red eye. The plant features compound leaves with ovate-shaped leaflets, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blooms.
