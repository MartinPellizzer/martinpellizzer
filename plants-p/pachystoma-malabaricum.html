Pachystoma malabaricum

Pachystoma malabaricum, commonly known as the Malabar stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the summer months. It is often found growing on rocky outcrops and in well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
