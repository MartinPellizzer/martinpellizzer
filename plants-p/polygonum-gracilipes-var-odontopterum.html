Polygonum gracilipes var. odontopterum

Polygonum gracilipes var. odontopterum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and are usually pink or red in color.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also used as a ornamental plant in wetland gardens.
