Persea bofo

Persea bofo, commonly known as the Bornean persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible berries that are typically orange to red in color. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, sometimes astringent flavor and is used in local cuisines.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and fruit.
