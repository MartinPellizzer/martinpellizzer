Polygonum iturupense

Polygonum iturupense, commonly known as Iturup knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Russian Far East, particularly the island of Iturup.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive swollen nodes along the stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a reddish or purple hue.

It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by triangular, bead-like fruits.
