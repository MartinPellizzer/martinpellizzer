Perymenium consobrinum

Perymenium consobrinum, commonly known as the slender spikerush, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils along stream banks and in swamps. Its small, wind-pollinated flowers are clustered in dense spikes, and it produces small, three-angled seeds.

Perymenium consobrinum plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
