Phaseolus oblongus

Phaseolus oblongus, commonly known as the Andean runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves, with flowers that are typically white or pale purple. The plant produces elongated, edible pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Phaseolus oblongus is cultivated for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and fiber.
