Pollinia tristachya

Pollinia tristachya, commonly known as the orchid bee orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique structure, featuring specialized pollinia that are adapted for attachment to the bodies of pollinators. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often brightly colored, attracting specific pollinators such as bees and wasps.

The botanical features of Pollinia tristachya make it an important species in the study of plant-pollinator interactions.
