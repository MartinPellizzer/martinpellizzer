Psorodendron

Psorodendron, commonly known as the "bark tree," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its woody stems and distinctive, often hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Psorodendron species produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in inflorescences.

Its bark is thick and fissured, often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
