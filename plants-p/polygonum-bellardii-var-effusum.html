Polygonum bellardii var. effusum

Polygonum bellardii var. effusum, commonly known as the soft-stemmed smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks, ditches, and wet meadows. The plant features erect, slender stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple.

The species is valued for its adaptability and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
