Peperomia cushiana

Peperomia cushiana, commonly known as the Cushiana pepper plant, is a compact, low-growing herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and silver, providing an ornamental appeal. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of green or red, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Peperomia cushiana is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor environments.
