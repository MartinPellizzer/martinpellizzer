Phyteuma pyrenaicum subsp. betonicoides

Phyteuma pyrenaicum subsp.

betonicoides, commonly known as the Pyrenean bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters. Its leaves are lanceolate and hairy, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
