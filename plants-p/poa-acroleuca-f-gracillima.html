Poa acroleuca f. gracillima

Poa acroleuca f. gracillima, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a botanical variant of the species Poa acroleuca.

It is a grass species native to southern South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile. This subspecies is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit. It typically thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features.
