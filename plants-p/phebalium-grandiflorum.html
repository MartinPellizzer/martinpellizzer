Phebalium grandiflorum

Phebalium grandiflorum, commonly known as the broad-leaved wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or loamy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for coastal and dry environments. Its flowers are usually clustered in racemes and bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
