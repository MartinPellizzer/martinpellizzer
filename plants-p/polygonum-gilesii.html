Polygonum gilesii

Polygonum gilesii, commonly known as the Chinese knotweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs. It produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers in late summer, and its roots are rhizomatous, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

Polygonum gilesii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and diuretic properties.
