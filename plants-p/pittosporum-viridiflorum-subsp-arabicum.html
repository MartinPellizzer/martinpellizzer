Pittosporum viridiflorum subsp. arabicum

Pittosporum viridiflorum subsp.

arabicum, commonly known as the Arabian green-flowered pittosporum, is a shrub native to the Arabian Peninsula. It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically orange to red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in arid regions.
