Pisum sativum var. mellitum

Pisum sativum var. mellitum, commonly known as the honey pea, is a variety of the common pea that is distinguished by its unique floral characteristics.

The flowers of this plant are typically yellow and emit a sweet, honey-like fragrance, which attracts pollinators such as bees. Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, sharing the same genus as other pea species. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in culinary applications.

Its morphology is similar to other pea varieties, with compound leaves and a climbing habit.
