Panicum laxiflorum var. strictirameum

Panicum laxiflorum var. strictirameum, commonly known as the narrow-flower panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, delicate panicles of pinkish to reddish flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a suitable choice for native plant gardens.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a valuable component of grassland ecosystems.
