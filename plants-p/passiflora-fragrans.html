Passiflora fragrans

Passiflora fragrans, commonly known as the sweet passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that display a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and bracts. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tropical flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Passiflora fragrans is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.
