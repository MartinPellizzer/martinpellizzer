Pappophorum lindleyanum var. pubescens

Pappophorum lindleyanum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy pappophorum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is distinguished by its hairy leaves and bracts, which give it its common name. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

This species is often found in grassy areas and can thrive in well-drained soils.
