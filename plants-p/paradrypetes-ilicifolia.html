Paradrypetes ilicifolia

Paradrypetes ilicifolia, commonly known as the African holly, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with small, leathery leaves that have a distinctive holly-like appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
