Piptadenia communis var. stipulacea

Piptadenia communis var. stipulacea, commonly known as the Brazilian trumpet tree, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are alternate, compound, and often have stipules, which are small, leaf-like structures at the base of the leaf stalk. The tree produces long, slender pods that contain seeds, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in landscaping and reforestation projects.
