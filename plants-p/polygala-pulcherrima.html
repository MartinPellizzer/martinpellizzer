Polygala pulcherrima

Polygala pulcherrima, commonly known as the beautiful milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its striking, often pink or purple, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and opposite, and it produces small, bean-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Polygala pulcherrima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
