Photinia glabra var. macrophylla

Photinia glabra var. macrophylla, commonly known as the glossy leaf photinia, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical, glossy green leaves that turn bright red in the fall. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The variety is noted for its compact growth habit and adaptability to various soil types.
