Phyllanthus thaii

Phyllanthus thaii, commonly known as Thai phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts.

Phyllanthus thaii is also noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
