Polygala myrtifolia var. obtusifolia

Polygala myrtifolia var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
