Polygala imbricata

Polygala imbricata, commonly known as the lace fern or fringed milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its unique floral structure resembles that of milkweeds, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
