Piptadenia communis var. inermis

Piptadenia communis var. inermis, commonly known as the Brazilian ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are borne in clusters, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, winged seeds.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils.
