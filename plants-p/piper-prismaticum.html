Piper prismaticum

Piper prismaticum, commonly known as the rainbow pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its strikingly colorful, waxy leaves that display a range of hues, including red, yellow, and green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elliptical, mildly spicy berries. Its unique foliage makes it a popular ornamental plant in botanical gardens and indoor collections.

It thrives in warm, humid conditions with bright, indirect light.
