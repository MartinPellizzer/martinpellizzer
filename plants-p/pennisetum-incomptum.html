Pennisetum incomptum

Pennisetum incomptum, commonly known as purple moor grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking purple or pink plumes that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Pennisetum incomptum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
