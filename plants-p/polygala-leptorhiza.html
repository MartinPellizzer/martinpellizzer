Polygala leptorhiza

Polygala leptorhiza, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has slender, branching stems and small, alternate leaves.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
