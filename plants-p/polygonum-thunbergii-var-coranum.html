Polygonum thunbergii var. coranum

Polygonum thunbergii var. coranum, commonly known as Japanese knotweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often reddish underneath and produces small, clustered pink to white flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in non-native environments. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along riverbanks and in urban areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for land management.
