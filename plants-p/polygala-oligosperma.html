Polygala oligosperma

Polygala oligosperma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stem and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, clustered fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in open vegetation and is relatively uncommon in cultivation.
