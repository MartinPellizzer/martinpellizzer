Polyalthia macrophylla

Polyalthia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow-orange flowers, which are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive scent when cut.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to humid, tropical climates.
