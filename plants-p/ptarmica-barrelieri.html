Ptarmica barrelieri

Ptarmica barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrelier's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically has small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and appear in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Ptarmica barrelieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
