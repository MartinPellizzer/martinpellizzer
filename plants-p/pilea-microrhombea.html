Pilea microrhombea

Pilea microrhombea, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are round and slightly puckered. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Pilea microrhombea is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
