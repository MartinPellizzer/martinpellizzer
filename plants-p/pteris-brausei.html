Pteris brausei

Pteris brausei, commonly known as the Brause's water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bi-lobed fronds that are typically green with a slight bluish tinge. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical regions. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through sexual reproduction.

Pteris brausei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
