Primula sherriffiae var. ludlowii

Primula sherriffiae var. ludlowii, commonly known as the Ludlow's primrose, is a rare and striking alpine plant native to the high mountains of China.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, with five petals that are slightly notched at the edges. The plant has a compact growth habit, with hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are also covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
