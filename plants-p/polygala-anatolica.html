Polygala anatolica

Polygala anatolica, commonly known as Anatolian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Turkey and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often spindly stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale pink or white, and it produces small, bead-like fruits that contain a single seed.

Polygala anatolica is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
