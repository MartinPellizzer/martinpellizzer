Plantago hakusanensis f. glabra

Plantago hakusanensis f. glabra, commonly known as the smoothleaf plantain, is a variant of the plantain species native to East Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its broad, smooth leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
