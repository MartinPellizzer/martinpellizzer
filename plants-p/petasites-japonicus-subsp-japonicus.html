Petasites japonicus subsp. japonicus

Petasites japonicus subsp.

japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The plant is noted for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
