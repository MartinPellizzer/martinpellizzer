Philadelphus cinereus

Philadelphus cinereus, commonly known as the cinereous mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are usually covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy flowers and attractive, rounded form.
