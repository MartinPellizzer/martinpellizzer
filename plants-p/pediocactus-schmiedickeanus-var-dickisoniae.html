Pediocactus schmiedickeanus var. dickisoniae

Pediocactus schmiedickeanus var. dickisoniae, commonly known as the Dickison's hedgehog cactus, is a compact, spineless cactus native to the Chihuahuan Desert of Mexico.

It features a globular or cylindrical stem with a distinctive greenish-gray color and a waxy coating that aids in water retention. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its foliage. It is well adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in poor soil conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
