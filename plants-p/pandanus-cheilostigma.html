Pandanus cheilostigma

Pandanus cheilostigma, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the tips of the stems. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often growing near mangroves or in sandy soils.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptability make it an important species in its native habitats.
