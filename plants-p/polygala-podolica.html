Polygala podolica

Polygala podolica, commonly known as the Podolic milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate and pinnate, with a distinctive hairy texture.

Polygala podolica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
