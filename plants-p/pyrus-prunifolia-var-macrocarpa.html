Pyrus prunifolia var. macrocarpa

Pyrus prunifolia var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited pear.

This variety is a cultivar of the European pear, characterized by its relatively large, sweet, and juicy fruits. The plant is a deciduous tree with broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by the distinctive large pears.

The fruit is typically harvested in late summer and is valued for its flavor and culinary use.
