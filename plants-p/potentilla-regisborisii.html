Potentilla regisborisii

Potentilla regisborisii, commonly known as the King's Borrisi Potentilla, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. This perennial shrub is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in summer. The plant has finely divided, aromatic leaves that are often covered in a soft, woolly layer of hair.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in rocky or sandy soils.
