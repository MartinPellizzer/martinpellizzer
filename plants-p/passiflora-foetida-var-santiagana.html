Passiflora foetida var. santiagana

Passiflora foetida var. santiagana, commonly known as the Santiago passionflower, is a tropical climbing vine characterized by its striking, complex flowers.

It features five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
