Pleurothallis batillacea

Pleurothallis batillacea, commonly known as the golden beard orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant typically produces small, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive beard-like appendage on the lip, which is a key identifying feature. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique appearance and delicate beauty.
