Pentagonocarpus digitatus

Pentagonocarpus digitatus, commonly known as the fingerleaf tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive pinnately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a pod-like structure that contains several seeds.

Pentagonocarpus digitatus is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.
