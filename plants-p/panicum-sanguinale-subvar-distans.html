Panicum sanguinale subvar. distans

Panicum sanguinale subvar. distans, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stature and distinctive red bracts that appear during the flowering stage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with vibrant red or purple hues. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a common sight in open fields and disturbed habitats.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native range.
