Potentilla leschenaultiana var. bannehalensis

Potentilla leschenaultiana var. bannehalensis, commonly known as the Bannehalen potentilla, is a flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has hirsute (hairy) stems and leaves that are typically elliptic or ovate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
