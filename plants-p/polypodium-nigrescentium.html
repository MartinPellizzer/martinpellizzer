Polypodium nigrescentium

Polypodium nigrescentium, commonly known as the blackening fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds that turn a deep blackish color when dry, hence its common name. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, anchoring itself with its rhizomes and absorbing moisture from the air. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are covered by a protective indusium.

Polypodium nigrescentium is often found in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appearance in horticulture.
