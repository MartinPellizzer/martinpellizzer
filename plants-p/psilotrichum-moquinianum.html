Psilotrichum moquinianum

Psilotrichum moquinianum, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the family Psilotaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in dense, club-shaped formations. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its unique morphology and reproductive structures make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
