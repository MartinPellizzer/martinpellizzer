Piscaria setigera

Piscaria setigera, commonly known as the threadleaf pondweed, is a freshwater aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow waters, forming dense mats that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant has long, slender leaves that are often ribbon-like in appearance, and it produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It is often found in ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize large areas make it both ecologically significant and sometimes considered a nuisance species.
