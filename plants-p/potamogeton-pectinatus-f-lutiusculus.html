Potamogeton pectinatus f. lutiusculus

Potamogeton pectinatus f. lutiusculus, commonly known as the common pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its elongated, ribbon-like leaves.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and marshes. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, greenish flowers that are usually submerged. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
