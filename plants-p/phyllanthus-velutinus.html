Phyllanthus velutinus

Phyllanthus velutinus, commonly known as velvetleaf or Indian madder, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its smooth, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Phyllanthus velutinus is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
