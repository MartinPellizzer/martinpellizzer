Picradenia richardsonii subsp. floribunda

Picradenia richardsonii subsp.

floribunda, commonly known as the fragrant picradenia, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with several pairs of leaflets.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy pulp.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.
