Piper dolichostachyum

Piper dolichostachyum, commonly known as the long-stalked pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of small, lance-shaped leaves. It produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, thin fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and potential medicinal properties.
