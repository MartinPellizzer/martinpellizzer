Peperomia congestispica

Peperomia congestispica, commonly known as the clustered peperomia, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a compact, evergreen perennial plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and dense clusters of small, lance-shaped foliage. The plant features upright stems that bear clusters of tiny, reddish-brown flowers, which are typical of the Piperaceae family. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia congestispica is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
