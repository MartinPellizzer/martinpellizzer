Plinthus sericeus

Plinthus sericeus, commonly known as the silky plinthus, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems covered in fine, silken hairs that give it a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture that helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, Plinthus sericeus is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
