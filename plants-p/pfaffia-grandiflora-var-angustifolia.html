Pfaffia grandiflora var. angustifolia

Pfaffia grandiflora var. angustifolia, commonly known as curcuma, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests. Its rhizomes are valued for their medicinal properties, particularly for their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

Due to its unique appearance and potential health benefits, it is a subject of interest in both botanical research and traditional medicine.
