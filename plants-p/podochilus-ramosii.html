Podochilus ramosii

Podochilus ramosii, commonly known as the hairy piper or orchid piper, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Podochilus ramosii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical gardens.
