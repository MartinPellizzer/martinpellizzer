Phaseolus longiplacentifer

Phaseolus longiplacentifer, commonly known as the long placenta bean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its elongated, multi-seeded pods and distinctive floral structure. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval-shaped and hairy.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique reproductive features and potential agricultural value.
