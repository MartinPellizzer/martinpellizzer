Pachydiscus gaultherioides

Pachydiscus gaultherioides, commonly known as the white-flowered snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its showy, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has smooth, gray-barked stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its elegant floral display.
