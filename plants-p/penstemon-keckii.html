Penstemon keckii

Penstemon keckii, commonly known as Keck's beardtongue, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. Penstemon keckii is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

It is also noted for its adaptability to arid environments.
