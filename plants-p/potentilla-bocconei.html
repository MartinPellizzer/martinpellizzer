Potentilla bocconei

Potentilla bocconei, commonly known as the Boccone's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces achenes as its fruit.

Potentilla bocconei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and vibrant floral display.
