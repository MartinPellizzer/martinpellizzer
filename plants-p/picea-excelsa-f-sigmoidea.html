Picea excelsa f. sigmoidea

Picea excelsa f. sigmoidea, commonly known as the zigzag spruce, is a variant of the white spruce native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

This form is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging branching pattern, which sets it apart from the more typical straight branching of the species. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky soils and cold climates. The foliage is dense and dark green, with needle-like leaves that are slightly flattened.

This unique morphology makes it a notable subject in botanical studies and a valuable component of alpine ecosystems.
