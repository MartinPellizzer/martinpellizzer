Pithecellobium rhombeum

Pithecellobium rhombeum, commonly known as the diamondleaf acacia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often arranged in a distinctive diamond shape, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, and it is often found in dry to semi-arid habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
