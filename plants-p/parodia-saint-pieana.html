Parodia saint-pieana

Parodia saint-pieana, commonly known as the Saint-Pierre's Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a slow-growing cactus that typically reaches a height of about 15 to 20 centimeters. The plant features a solitary stem with a globular shape, covered in short, cylindrical spines. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in late winter or early spring.

The plant is well adapted to hot, dry environments and requires minimal water once established.
