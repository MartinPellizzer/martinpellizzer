Potentilla hirta subvar. brevidentata

Potentilla hirta subvar. brevidentata, commonly known as the short-toothed cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs. It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and short, blunt leaf teeth.

This subspecies is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardiness.
