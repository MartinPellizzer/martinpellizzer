Punica granatum f. flavescens

Punica granatum f. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow pomegranate, is a cultivar of the pomegranate plant characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange fruit color.

This variety is notable for its reduced seed count and increased juice content compared to the typical red pomegranate. The fruit develops in clusters and matures in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in warm, arid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The yellow pomegranate is valued for its unique flavor and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
