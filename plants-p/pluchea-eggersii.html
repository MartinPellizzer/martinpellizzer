Pluchea eggersii

Pluchea eggersii, commonly known as Eggers' blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils, and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

The small blue to purple flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
