Polypogon lagascae

Polypogon lagascae, commonly known as the Florida panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It is adapted to tolerate periodic flooding and has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

Botanically, it is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in wetland ecosystems.
