Paeonia officinalis f. leiocarpa

Paeonia officinalis f. leiocarpa, commonly known as the white peony, is a botanical variant of the common peony.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which are typically more subdued in color compared to the standard variety. The plant is a herbaceous perennial with thick, fleshy rhizomes that store nutrients and aid in survival during adverse conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a fan-like pattern.

This cultivar is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and has historical use in traditional medicine.
