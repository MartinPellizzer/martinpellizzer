Petunia integrifolia subsp. occidentalis

Petunia integrifolia subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the Western petunia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of trumpet-shaped, funnel-like flowers in shades of violet, purple, and white.

The species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are ovate and slightly hairy, with entire margins, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and containers due to its vibrant blooms and compact growth.
