Pitcairnia bromeliifolia var. bromeliifolia

Pitcairnia bromeliifolia var. bromeliifolia, commonly known as the Bromeliad Pitcairnia, is a striking bromeliad native to the humid forests of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces vibrant, showy inflorescences with flowers that range from red to orange, often resembling a crown. Its leaves are typically dark green with a waxy coating, aiding in water retention.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
