Polygala arnottiana

Polygala arnottiana, commonly known as the Arnott's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and in swampy areas. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with a distinctive spurred structure that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Polygala arnottiana is often found in wetland habitats and is considered a rare species in some regions.
