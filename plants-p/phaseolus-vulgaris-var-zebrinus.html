Phaseolus vulgaris var. zebrinus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. zebrinus, commonly known as the zebra bean, is a variant of the common bean characterized by its distinctive mottled or striped seed coat.

This plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to the Americas, where it has been cultivated for centuries. It typically grows as an annual herb with compound leaves and produces pods containing edible seeds.

The zebra bean is valued not only for its unique appearance but also for its nutritional content and adaptability to various growing conditions.
