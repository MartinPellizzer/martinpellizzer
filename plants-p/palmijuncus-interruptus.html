Palmijuncus interruptus

Palmijuncus interruptus, commonly known as the interrupted palm, is a rare and unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unusual morphology, featuring a short trunk and a crown of feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant exhibits a clustering growth habit, forming dense groups that can resemble miniature forests. Its botanical significance lies in its limited distribution and specialized ecological requirements.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
