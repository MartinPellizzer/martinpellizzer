Pourouma substrigosa

Pourouma substrigosa, commonly known as the Amazonian grass or giant bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its stiff, elongated leaves and robust rhizomes. The plant can grow up to 6 meters in height, making it one of the tallest grasses in the world. Its hollow stems are segmented and often used in traditional crafts by indigenous communities.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and plays a role in the ecological structure of the rainforest.
