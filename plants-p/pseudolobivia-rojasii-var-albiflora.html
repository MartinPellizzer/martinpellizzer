Pseudolobivia rojasii var. albiflora

Pseudolobivia rojasii var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense, spiny stems. The plant produces showy, white flowers during the warm season, which are often borne in clusters at the stem tips. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it stores water in its fleshy stems to survive in dry conditions.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and adaptability to rocky, well-drained soils.
