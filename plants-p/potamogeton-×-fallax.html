Potamogeton × fallax

Potamogeton × fallax, commonly known as the hybrid water milfoil, is a perennial aquatic plant native to North America.

It is a hybrid between Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton obtusifolius, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often submerged or floating.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats, it can impact water flow and aquatic ecosystems.
