Protea simplicifolia

Protea simplicifolia, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pale pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as fynbos vegetation. Its leaves are leathery and often covered in a silvery-white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Protea simplicifolia is well adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
