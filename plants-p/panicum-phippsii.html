Panicum phippsii

Panicum phippsii, commonly known as Phipps' Panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and produces dense clusters of small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it blooms during the summer months.

Panicum phippsii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
