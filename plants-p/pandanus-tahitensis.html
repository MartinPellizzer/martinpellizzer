Pandanus tahitensis

Pandanus tahitensis, commonly known as the Tahitian pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and basket weaving. The plant produces fragrant, spadix-like inflorescences that are pollinated by insects and birds. Its roots are sometimes used in local medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and humid environments.
