Passerina elliptica var. densiflora

Passerina elliptica var. densiflora, commonly known as the denseflowered pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, showy yellow flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant is known for its early blooming habit.

It is often found in open meadows and on slopes at high elevations.
