Phyllostegia orientalis

Phyllostegia orientalis, commonly known as the oriental mistflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Phyllostegia orientalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
