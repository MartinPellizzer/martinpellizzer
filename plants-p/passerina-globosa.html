Passerina globosa

Passerina globosa, commonly known as the globe flower, is a striking perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of showy, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense mat, providing a lush ground cover.

Passerina globosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
