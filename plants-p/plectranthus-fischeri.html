Plectranthus fischeri

Plectranthus fischeri, commonly known as Fischer's plectranthus, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their fragrant leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or blue, typically arranged in clusters.

Plectranthus fischeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and mild scent.
