Pygeum polyadenium

Pygeum polyadenium, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, white flowers. The tree produces woody fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
