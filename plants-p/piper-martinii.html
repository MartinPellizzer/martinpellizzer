Piper martinii

Piper martinii, commonly known as the Martin's pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by small, round berries.

Piper martinii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
