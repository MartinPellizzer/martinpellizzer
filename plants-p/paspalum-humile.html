Paspalum humile

Paspalum humile, commonly known as dwarf paspalum, is a low-growing grass species native to the Americas.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growths and is often found in moist, sandy soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a common ornamental and groundcover plant.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
