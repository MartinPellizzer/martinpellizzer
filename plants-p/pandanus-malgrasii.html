Pandanus malgrasii

Pandanus malgrasii, commonly known as the Madagascar pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, which are often used in local ceremonies and as ornamental features. Its adaptability to tropical climates makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.

The plant's unique appearance and cultural significance highlight its importance in both ecological and human contexts.
