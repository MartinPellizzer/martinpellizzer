Polygonum sparsiflorum

Polygonum sparsiflorum, commonly known as the sparse-flower knotweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle pink or red hue to its appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Polygonum sparsiflorum is often found in wetlands and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
