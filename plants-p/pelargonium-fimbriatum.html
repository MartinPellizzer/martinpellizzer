Pelargonium fimbriatum

Pelargonium fimbriatum, commonly known as the ivy-leaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, fimbriated (fringed) leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Pelargonium fimbriatum is often used in hanging baskets and as a ground cover due to its vigorous growth and ornamental value.
