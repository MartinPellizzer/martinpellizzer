Piptanthus bombycinus

Piptanthus bombycinus, commonly known as the Chinese silkflower, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it adaptable to poor soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
