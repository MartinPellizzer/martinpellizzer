Ptilotus aristatus var. exilis

Ptilotus aristatus var. exilis, commonly known as the slender spinifex, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It features wiry, prostrate stems that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a distinctive, spiny appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers clustered in dense spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. It is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, with a deep root system and the ability to store water in its stems.

This species is often found in sandy soils and is a valuable component of native grasslands and shrublands.
