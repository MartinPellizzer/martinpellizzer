Pandanus verus var. flaccidus

Pandanus verus var. flaccidus, commonly known as the flaccid pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a fibrous texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, which are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and requires full sun exposure.

This variant is often used in traditional crafts and for its ornamental value in landscaping.
