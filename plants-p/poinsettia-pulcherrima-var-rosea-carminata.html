Poinsettia pulcherrima var. rosea-carminata

Poinsettia pulcherrima var. rosea-carminata, commonly known as the Christmas star or red poinsettia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The true flowers are small, yellowish structures at the center of the bracts. This variety is particularly noted for its deep red and pink hues, making it a popular holiday decoration.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
