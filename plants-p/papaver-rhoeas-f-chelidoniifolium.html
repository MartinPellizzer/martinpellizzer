Papaver rhoeas f. chelidoniifolium

Papaver rhoeas f. chelidoniifolium, commonly known as the chelidonium-leaved poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its distinctive foliage.

This plant exhibits lanceolate leaves with a serrated margin, resembling those of the celandine plant, hence its name. The flowers are typically bright red with a dark center, similar to the standard Papaver rhoeas. It is a herbaceous annual that thrives in disturbed soils and sunny environments.

The botanical features of this form make it a notable subject in horticultural and botanical studies.
