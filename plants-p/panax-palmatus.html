Panax palmatus

Panax palmatus, commonly known as the palmate ginseng, is a rare and endangered species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive palmately lobed leaves and a single, thick, fleshy root. The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with the root used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat various ailments. Due to overharvesting and habitat loss, it is critically endangered and protected in many regions.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject of study in both horticulture and pharmacology.
