Portulaca racemosa

Portulaca racemosa, commonly known as the clustered portulaca, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is a low-growing annual herb with fleshy, succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates.

Portulaca racemosa is often used in ornamental gardening due to its drought tolerance and vibrant blooms.
