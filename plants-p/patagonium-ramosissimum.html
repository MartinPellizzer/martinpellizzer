Patagonium ramosissimum

Patagonium ramosissimum, commonly known as the Patagonian thornbush, is a shrub native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, branched structure and small, often spiny leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its hardy nature allows it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Patagonium ramosissimum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
