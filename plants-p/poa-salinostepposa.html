Poa salinostepposa

Poa salinostepposa, commonly known as the salt steppe grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it an important component of salt-steppe ecosystems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its resilience, it plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
