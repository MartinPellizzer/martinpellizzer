Polygala scabra

Polygala scabra, commonly known as the rough milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, and the flowers are often pale yellow or greenish-white.

Polygala scabra is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
