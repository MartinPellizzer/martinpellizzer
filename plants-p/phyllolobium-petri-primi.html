Phyllolobium petri-primi

Phyllolobium petri-primi, commonly known as the primordial leaf plant, is a rare species characterized by its unique leaf morphology.

Its leaves are elongated and possess a lobed structure, which is an adaptation to its native arid environment. The plant exhibits a succulent nature, allowing it to store water efficiently. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a conservation concern in its native habitat.
