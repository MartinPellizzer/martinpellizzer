Piper glabrius

Piper glabrius, commonly known as the smooth pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns yellow when ripe.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
