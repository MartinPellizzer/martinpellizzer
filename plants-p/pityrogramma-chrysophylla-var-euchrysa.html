Pityrogramma chrysophylla var. euchrysa

Pityrogramma chrysophylla var. euchrysa, commonly known as the golden-leafed water fern, is a variant of the water fern genus Pityrogramma.

It is a small, aquatic fern that thrives in shallow, freshwater environments. The plant is characterized by its delicate, bright yellow fronds that contrast with its dark green veins. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with finely divided pinnae that enhance its ornamental appeal.

This variety is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it contributes to the biodiversity of wetland ecosystems.
