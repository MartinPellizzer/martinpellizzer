Phyllostegia hirsuta

Phyllostegia hirsuta, commonly known as hairy phyllostegia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in whorls along the stem, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Phyllostegia hirsuta is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
