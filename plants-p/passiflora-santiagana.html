Passiflora santiagana

Passiflora santiagana, commonly known as the Santiago passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly found in Brazil and Paraguay.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of purple, blue, and white petals, along with intricate filaments and a crown-like structure. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Passiflora santiagana is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
