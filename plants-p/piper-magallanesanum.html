Piper magallanesanum

Piper magallanesanum, commonly known as the Magallanes pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper and chili peppers. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with elongated stems that often trail along the ground or climb supports. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy surface, while the small, green flowers are clustered in terminal spikes.

The fruit, which is a small, berry-like structure, contains the aromatic seeds used in traditional culinary and medicinal applications.
