Prunus pendula f. leucantha

Prunus pendula f. leucantha, commonly known as the white-leafed weeping cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus pendula species, characterized by its distinctive white foliage.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that produces fragrant pink or white flowers in early spring. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a silvery-white underside, giving it a striking appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping for its elegant, cascading form.
