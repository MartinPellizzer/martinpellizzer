Paphia helenae

Paphia helenae, commonly known as the Helen's Paphiopedilum, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety green leaves and unique, spurred orchid flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, thriving in cool, humid environments with well-draining soil. Its flowers bloom in late spring or early summer, adding a touch of elegance to its natural habitat.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Paphia helenae is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
