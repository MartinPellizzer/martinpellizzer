Prunus tawakonia

Prunus tawakonia, commonly known as the Tawakoni cherry, is a species of flowering shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, dark red to black fruits that mature in the fall. The plant produces fragrant white or pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The Tawakoni cherry is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance to local wildlife.
