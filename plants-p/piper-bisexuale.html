Piper bisexuale

Piper bisexuale, commonly known as the two-sexed pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and ethnobotanical studies.
