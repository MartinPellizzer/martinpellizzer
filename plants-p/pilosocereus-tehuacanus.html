Pilosocereus tehuacanus

Pilosocereus tehuacanus, commonly known as the Mexican moon cactus, is a cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its unique appearance, featuring a large, rounded stem covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun exposure to flourish.

Due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
