Persica potaninii

Persica potaninii, commonly known as the Potanin's peach.

It is a species of shrub or small tree native to Central Asia, characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, fuzzy fruits similar to peaches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in semi-arid regions.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
