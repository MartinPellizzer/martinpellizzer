Pseudopilocereus azureus

Pseudopilocereus azureus, commonly known as the Blue Moon cactus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, columnar stems and striking blue-gray spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that range from white to pale blue, typically blooming at night. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
