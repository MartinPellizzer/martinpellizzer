Pavonia achanioides

Pavonia achanioides, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false hibiscus, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy, giving it a distinctive, coarse texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming from summer to fall. Its growth habit is upright, with branching stems that can reach heights of up to 3 feet.

Pavonia achanioides is often found in disturbed soils and is commonly cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
