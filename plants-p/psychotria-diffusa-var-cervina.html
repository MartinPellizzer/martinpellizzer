Psychotria diffusa var. cervina

Psychotria diffusa var. cervina, commonly known as the "Cape gooseberry" or "physostegia," is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its small, egg-shaped berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, white to pale green flowers. The berries are edible when ripe but can be toxic if consumed unripe or in large quantities.
