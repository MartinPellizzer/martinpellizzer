Prunus serotina f. albovariegata

Prunus serotina f. albovariegata, commonly known as the white-variegated black cherry, is a cultivar of the black cherry tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated leaves that display white and green patterns. This form is typically smaller than the typical species, making it suitable for smaller landscapes. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but have a tart flavor.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
