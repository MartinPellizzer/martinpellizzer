Pedicularis sudetica subsp. gymnostachya

Pedicularis sudetica subsp.

gymnostachya, commonly known as the Carpathian lousewort, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its erect stem and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the genus Pedicularis, known for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
