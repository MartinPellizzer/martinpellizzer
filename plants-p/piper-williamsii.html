Piper williamsii

Piper williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants like black pepper. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of white, fragrant flowers. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
