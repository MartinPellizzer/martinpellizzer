Persoonia saccata

Persoonia saccata, commonly known as the bottlebrush tree, is an evergreen shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical flower spikes that resemble a bottlebrush, typically ranging in color from yellow to red. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Persoonia saccata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
