Pontederia cordata

Pontederia cordata, commonly known as the pickerelweed, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces striking spike-like inflorescences with vibrant blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes.

Its ability to tolerate a wide range of water conditions makes it a common and adaptable species in freshwater ecosystems.
