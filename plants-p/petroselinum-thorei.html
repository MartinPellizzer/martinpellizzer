Petroselinum thorei

Petroselinum thorei, commonly known as Turkish parsley, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves that are often used in cooking for their mild, fresh aroma. It produces small, white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
