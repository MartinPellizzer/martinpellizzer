Pavonia pellita

Pavonia pellita, commonly known as the smallflower pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous annual or perennial, with hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink to purplish in color, and it produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds.

Pavonia pellita thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and fields.
