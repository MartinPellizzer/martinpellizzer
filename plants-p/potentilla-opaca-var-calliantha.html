Potentilla opaca var. calliantha

Potentilla opaca var. calliantha, commonly known as the silver-leaved potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in dense, soft hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
