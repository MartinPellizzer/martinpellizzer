Polygala setifera

Polygala setifera, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. Its flowers are usually pale green to white in color and are visited by various pollinators, including bees and flies.

Polygala setifera is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
