Pelargonium × elisae

Pelargonium × elisae, commonly known as the hybrid geranium, is a species that results from the crossbreeding of various pelargonium species.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically has a compact, bushy growth habit with serrated, dark green leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pelargonium × elisae is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.
