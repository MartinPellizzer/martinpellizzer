Papaver pulvinatum subsp. alexandri

Papaver pulvinatum subsp.

alexandri, commonly known as Alexandrian poppy, is a species of flowering plant in the family Papaveraceae. It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to orange, with a distinctive central stigma.

The plant is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in rocky or sandy soils. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
