Puya pitcairnioides

Puya pitcairnioides, commonly known as the Andean torch, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a member of the bromeliad family, characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces tall, spire-like inflorescences adorned with vibrant red and orange flowers, which bloom briefly before withering. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
