Prunus fremontii var. pilulata

Prunus fremontii var. pilulata, commonly known as the Fremont's cherry or the desert cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. The plant has small, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are white or pink, appearing in early spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along streambanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
