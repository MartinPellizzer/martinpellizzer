Pleurothallis filifera

Pleurothallis filifera, commonly known as the thread-leaf orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters along the stem.

Pleurothallis filifera is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
