Potamogeton natans var. acaulis

Potamogeton natans var. acaulis, commonly known as the slender watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it lacks true stems, with the leaves directly attached to the rhizome. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like structures, and it reproduces both sexually and vegetatively through rhizome growth.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
