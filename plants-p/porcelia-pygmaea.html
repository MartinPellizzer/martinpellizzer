Porcelia pygmaea

Porcelia pygmaea, commonly known as the miniature porcelain plant, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, dark green foliage that resembles porcelain in texture. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
