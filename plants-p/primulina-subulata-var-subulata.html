Primulina subulata var. subulata

Primulina subulata var. subulata, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and glossy, dark green leaves that often have a waxy appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale yellow, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil.

Due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
