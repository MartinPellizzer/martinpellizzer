Pelargonium cuae

Pelargonium cuae, commonly known as the Cape marigold, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub with hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in various parts of the world for both garden beauty and horticultural interest.
