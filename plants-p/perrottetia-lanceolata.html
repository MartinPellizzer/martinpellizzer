Perrottetia lanceolata

Perrottetia lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf perrottetia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves that are usually 5 to 15 centimeters long. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
