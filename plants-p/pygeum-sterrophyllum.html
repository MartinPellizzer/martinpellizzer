Pygeum sterrophyllum

Pygeum sterrophyllum, commonly known as the African wild pear, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and traditional medicinal contexts.
