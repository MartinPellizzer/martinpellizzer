Pimelea halophila

Pimelea halophila, commonly known as the coastal bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and distinctive red or pink bottlebrush-like inflorescences. This species thrives in saline or sandy soils, often found in dunes or coastal heathlands. The plant is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
