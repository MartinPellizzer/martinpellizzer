Phyllanthus chamaecerasus var. intermedius

Phyllanthus chamaecerasus var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate spurge, is a variant of the spurge family (Euphorbiaceae) native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its smooth, often reddish bark and opposite, simple leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed sites.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
