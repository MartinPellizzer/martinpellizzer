Piper hylebates

Piper hylebates, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or the ghost pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 3,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, berry-like fruits.

The fruit contains a single seed and is known for its extremely hot, spicy flavor.
