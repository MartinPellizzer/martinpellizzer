Potamogeton pectinatus var. setaceus

Potamogeton pectinatus var. setaceus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are narrow and often have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically found near the water surface. It thrives in shallow, nutrient-rich waters and plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its rapid growth can sometimes lead to dense vegetation, impacting water flow and light penetration.
