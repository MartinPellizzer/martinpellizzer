Potentilla saundersiana var. potaninii

Potentilla saundersiana var. potaninii, commonly known as the Siberian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the cold regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and resilience.
