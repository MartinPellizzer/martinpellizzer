Prunus latidentata

Prunus latidentata, commonly known as the dwarf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet, red berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals. Prunus latidentata typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters and is often found in woodland edges and moist soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and garden use.
