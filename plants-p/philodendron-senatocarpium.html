Philodendron senatocarpium

Philodendron senatocarpium, commonly known as the red-veined philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy dark green leaves with prominent red veins, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by clusters of bright red berries, which are edible but often used as ornamental features. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Philodendron senatocarpium is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
