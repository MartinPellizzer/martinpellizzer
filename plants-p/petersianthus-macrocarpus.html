Petersianthus macrocarpus

Petersianthus macrocarpus, commonly known as the Natal plum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-yellow fruits resembling plums. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and edible qualities.
