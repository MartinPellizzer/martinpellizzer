Panicum sericeum

Panicum sericeum, commonly known as silky panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with fine, silky hairs covering its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in wetland restoration projects.
