Poa paradoxa

Poa paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments.

Poa paradoxa is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in high-altitude regions.
