Poa sicula

Poa sicula, commonly known as Sicilian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions. The plant has slender, tufted shoots with narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually blue-green in color. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like clusters of pale blue to purple flowers.

Poa sicula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
