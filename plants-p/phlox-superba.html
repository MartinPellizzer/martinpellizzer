Phlox superba

Phlox superba, commonly known as the showy phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing fragrant blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, dark green leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems.

Phlox superba thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.
