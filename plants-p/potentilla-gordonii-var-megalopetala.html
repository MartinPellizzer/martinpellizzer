Potentilla gordonii var. megalopetala

Potentilla gordonii var. megalopetala, commonly known as the giant petal potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, large, semi-double flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
