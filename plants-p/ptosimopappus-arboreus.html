Ptosimopappus arboreus

Ptosimopappus arboreus, commonly known as the tree dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching structure and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it is known for its ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils. After flowering, it produces fluffy, seed-bearing structures similar to those of the dandelion, which aid in wind dispersal.

Ptosimopappus arboreus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
