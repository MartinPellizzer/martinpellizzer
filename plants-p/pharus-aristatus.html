Pharus aristatus

Pharus aristatus, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often arrowhead-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is native to tropical regions of the Americas. Its flowers are borne in a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Pharus aristatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in water gardens.
