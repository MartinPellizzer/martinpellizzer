Polypodium villarii

Polypodium villarii, commonly known as the mountain holly fern, is a species of fern native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds that are typically divided into narrow, linear segments. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizome is thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

Polypodium villarii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
