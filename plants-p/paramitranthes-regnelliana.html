Paramitranthes regnelliana

Paramitranthes regnelliana, commonly known as the Hawaiian ghost flower, is a rare and endangered plant endemic to the island of Hawaii.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, white, daisy-like flowers that bloom during the winter months. The plant has a short flowering period and is often found growing in moist, shaded areas of rainforests.
