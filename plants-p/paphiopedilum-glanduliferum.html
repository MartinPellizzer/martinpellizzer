Paphiopedilum glanduliferum

Paphiopedilum glanduliferum, commonly known as the glandular slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, pouch-like lip that is often adorned with glandular structures. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, where it thrives on decaying organic matter. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.

Paphiopedilum glanduliferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its distinctive appearance.
