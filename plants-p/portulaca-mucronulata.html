Portulaca mucronulata

Portulaca mucronulata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped purslane, is a low-growing annual plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features fleshy, succulent leaves and small, vibrant yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant is characterized by its upright stems and the presence of small, sharp spines at the leaf tips. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens.

Portulaca mucronulata is adapted to hot, dry climates and is drought-tolerant, making it a common weed in many regions.
