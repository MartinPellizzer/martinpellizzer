Polygala robinsonii

Polygala robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is found in wetlands and along stream banks. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and are arranged in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It plays an important role in supporting local ecosystems and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
