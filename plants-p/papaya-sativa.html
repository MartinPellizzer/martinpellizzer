Papaya sativa

Papaya sativa, commonly known as the papaya, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and a distinctive, fleshy, orange-colored fruit. The plant is typically grown in warm climates and requires a long growing season to produce fruit. Its fruit is rich in vitamins A and C, as well as the enzyme papain, which is used in food processing and medicine.

The plant also has a short lifespan, often being cultivated for its fruit rather than for ornamental purposes.
