Polylobium molle

Polylobium molle, commonly known as the soft-leafed polylobium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are often arranged in a distinctive, spiraled pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom in the spring. It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and parks.
