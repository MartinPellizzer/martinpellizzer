Polygonum bernardinum

Polygonum bernardinum, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its succulent stems and simple, alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The plant has a reddish stem and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
