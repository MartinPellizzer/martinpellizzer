Phyllanthus tenuipedicellatus var. kaloueholaensis

Phyllanthus tenuipedicellatus var. kaloueholaensis, commonly known as the Kaloueholaensis spurge, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of West Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and distinctive yellowish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
