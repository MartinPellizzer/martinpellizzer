Prunus hortulana var. waylandii

Prunus hortulana var. waylandii, commonly known as the Wayland's cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, often used in jams and desserts. The plant has a dense, rounded growth habit with dark green, oval leaves that turn reddish in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for small gardens or container planting.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy spring blossoms.
