Prunus griffithii var. turcomanica

Prunus griffithii var. turcomanica, commonly known as the Turkestan cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It features simple, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for rocky or sandy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
