Paullinia quassiifolia

Paullinia quassiifolia, commonly known as the quassia leaf or Brazilian quassia, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported antipyretic and antiseptic properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
