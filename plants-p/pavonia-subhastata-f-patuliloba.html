Pavonia subhastata f. patuliloba

Pavonia subhastata f. patuliloba, commonly known as the broadleaf pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its large, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing habitat for wildlife.
