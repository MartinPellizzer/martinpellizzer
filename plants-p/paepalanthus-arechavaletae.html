Paepalanthus arechavaletae

Paepalanthus arechavaletae, commonly known as the Arechavaleta's cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Due to habitat loss and overcollection, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
