Primula gambeliana

Primula gambeliana, commonly known as Gambel's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forest floors. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Primula gambeliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
