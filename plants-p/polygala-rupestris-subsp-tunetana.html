Polygala rupestris subsp. tunetana

Polygala rupestris subsp.

tunetana, commonly known as the rock polygala, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in dry, sunny environments.

The plant features small, delicate flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters. It has narrow, linear leaves and a slender, branching stem.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a drought-tolerant plant.
