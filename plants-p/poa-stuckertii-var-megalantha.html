Poa stuckertii var. megalantha

Poa stuckertii var. megalantha, commonly known as the giant mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of mountain ecosystems.
