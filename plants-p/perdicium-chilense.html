Perdicium chilense

Perdicium chilense, commonly known as the Chilean partridge pea, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Perdicium chilense is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment.
