Phenianthus arizonicus

Phenianthus arizonicus, commonly known as the Arizona fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces oval, seed-like structures called siliques, which are typical of the Brassicaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
