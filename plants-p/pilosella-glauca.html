Pilosella glauca

Pilosella glauca, commonly known as bluehead groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are hairy and grayish-green, while the flowers are blue to purple in color.

Pilosella glauca is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
