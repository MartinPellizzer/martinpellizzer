Persica vulgaris proles laevis

Persica vulgaris proles laevis, commonly known as the common peach, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray-brown bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, pink to white flowers in the spring, followed by fuzzy, orange-red fruits known as peaches. Its botanical features include a taproot system and a tendency to produce runners, making it adaptable to various soil types.

The fruit is widely cultivated for its sweet, juicy flesh and is an important crop in many regions.
