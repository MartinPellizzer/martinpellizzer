Paepalanthus speciosus var. chlorocephalus

Paepalanthus speciosus var. chlorocephalus, commonly known as the blue-headed pincushion cactus, is a unique succulent native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a spherical or hemispherical shape. The plant features showy, violet to blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are reduced to small, spiny structures, typical of many cacti.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a striking addition to arid gardens.
