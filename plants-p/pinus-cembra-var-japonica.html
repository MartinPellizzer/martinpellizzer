Pinus cembra var. japonica

Pinus cembra var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese stone pine, is a variant of the stone pine native to Japan.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slow growth and distinctive, twisted trunk. The foliage consists of short, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. It produces cones that are usually reddish-brown and contain edible seeds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.
