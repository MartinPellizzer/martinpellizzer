Phyllanthus grandifolius

Phyllanthus grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leaved phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, red or orange fruits that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional medicine.
