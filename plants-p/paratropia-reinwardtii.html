Paratropia reinwardtii

Paratropia reinwardtii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a striking flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with thick, woody stems and glossy, dark green leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters and have a unique, lantern-like appearance.

Paratropia reinwardtii is well adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions.
