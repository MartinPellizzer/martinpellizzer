Protea mucronifolia

Protea mucronifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with wet winters and dry summers. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, often with a silvery or whitish underside, which helps reduce water loss.

Protea mucronifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
