Polygala hirsuta

Polygala hirsuta, commonly known as hairy polygala, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, contributing to its common name.

The flowers, which are often pink or white, bloom in summer and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
