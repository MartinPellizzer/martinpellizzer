Penstemon procerus subsp. aberrans

Penstemon procerus subsp.

aberrans, commonly known as the slender beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
