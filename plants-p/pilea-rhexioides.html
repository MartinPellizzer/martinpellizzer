Pilea rhexioides

Pilea rhexioides, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to China.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a favored ornamental plant.
