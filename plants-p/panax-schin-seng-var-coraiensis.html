Panax schin-seng var. coraiensis

Panax schin-seng var. coraiensis, commonly known as the Chinese ginseng variant, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, yellowish roots that are rich in bioactive compounds. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves and small greenish-white flowers. Its roots are valued in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

The botanical features of this variant make it distinct from other Panax species, particularly in its morphology and chemical composition.
