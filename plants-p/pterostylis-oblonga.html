Pterostylis oblonga

Pterostylis oblonga, commonly known as the oblong spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. The plant features a slender, hairy stem with a single, narrow leaf and a spike of greenish-yellow flowers. Its flowers are characterized by their elongated, spider-like appearance, which is a common trait among the Pterostylis genus.

The orchid relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, highlighting its ecological dependence on fungal symbiosis.
