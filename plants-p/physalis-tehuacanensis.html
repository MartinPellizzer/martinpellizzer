Physalis tehuacanensis

Physalis tehuacanensis, commonly known as the Tehuacan physalis, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its inflated, papery calyx that encloses the fruit. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible and often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in dry, rocky environments and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
