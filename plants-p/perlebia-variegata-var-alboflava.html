Perlebia variegata var. alboflava

Perlebia variegata var. alboflava, commonly known as the white-flowered perlebia, is a striking member of the Araceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, brightly colored leaves with intricate patterns of green and white, and its showy, spadix-like inflorescence. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered around a central axis, typical of the Araceae family.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
