Pisum arvense

Pisum arvense, commonly known as field pea, is an annual legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its pods contain several seeds, which are used as a food source in some regions.

Pisum arvense is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
