Prunus serrulata f. gosho-odora

Prunus serrulata f. gosho-odora, commonly known as the Shishigashira cherry, is a botanical variant of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a unique sensory element to its ornamental value. The plant exhibits a compact, rounded growth habit with dense foliage that provides visual interest throughout the growing season. Its flowers, which emit a sweet, heady aroma, are particularly notable for their ability to attract pollinators.

This cultivar is prized in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and public spaces.
