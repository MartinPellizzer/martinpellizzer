Persea brevipetiolata

Persea brevipetiolata, commonly known as the short-petioled persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are typically smooth and dark green on top. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by fruits that are generally small and sweet. The fruit has a thick, leathery skin and a soft, edible pulp.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in its native habitats.
