Pseudoraphis ukishiba

Pseudoraphis ukishiba, commonly known as the Japanese bottlebrush, is a striking plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its unique, bottle-shaped stems that are often covered in a waxy, pale green to reddish-brown coating. The plant produces clusters of bright red or orange flowers that resemble bottle brushes, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its evergreen foliage and long blooming season, typically from spring to early summer.
