Pluchea camphorata var. angustifolia

Pluchea camphorata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf camphorweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in disturbed areas.

Its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties have made it of interest in traditional herbal practices.
