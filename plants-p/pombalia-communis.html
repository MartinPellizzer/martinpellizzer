Pombalia communis

Pombalia communis, commonly known as the common pombalia, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Pombalia communis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

It is also valued for its fragrant blossoms and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
