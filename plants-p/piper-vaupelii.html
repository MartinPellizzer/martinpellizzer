Piper vaupelii

Piper vaupelii, commonly known as the New Guinea pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants, and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
