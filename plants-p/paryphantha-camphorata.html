Paryphantha camphorata

Paryphantha camphorata, commonly known as the camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, rough-textured bark and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by oval, woody fruits containing smooth, dark brown seeds. It is valued for its essential oils, which are extracted from its bark and leaves.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.
