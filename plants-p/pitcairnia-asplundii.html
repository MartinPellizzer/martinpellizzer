Pitcairnia asplundii

Pitcairnia asplundii, commonly known as the Asplund's pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a dense, water-holding cup at the center. The plant produces striking, long-lasting inflorescences with vibrant purple or red flowers. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in many tropical and subtropical gardens.
