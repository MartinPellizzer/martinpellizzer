Poinsettia ruiziana

Poinsettia ruiziana, commonly known as the red poinsettia, is a striking plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts, which are modified leaves that surround the small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is closely related to other poinsettia species. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Poinsettia ruiziana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its festive appearance, especially during the holiday season.
