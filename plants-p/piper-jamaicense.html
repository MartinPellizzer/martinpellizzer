Piper jamaicense

Piper jamaicense, commonly known as Jamaican pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and has a mild peppery flavor, making it a popular ingredient in local cuisines.
