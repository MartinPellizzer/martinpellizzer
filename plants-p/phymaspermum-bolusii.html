Phymaspermum bolusii

Phymaspermum bolusii, commonly known as the Bolus's ghost orchid, is a rare and endangered species native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, ghost-like appearance with translucent, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are pollinated by specific insects, showcasing a specialized ecological relationship. Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is critically endangered and protected in its native region.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator of biodiversity in its ecosystem.
