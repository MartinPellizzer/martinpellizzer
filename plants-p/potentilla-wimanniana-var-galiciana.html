Potentilla wimanniana var. galiciana

Potentilla wimanniana var. galiciana, commonly known as the Galician potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, wiry stems and elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice in horticulture for its aesthetic and ecological value.
