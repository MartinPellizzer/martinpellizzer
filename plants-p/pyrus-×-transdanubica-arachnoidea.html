Pyrus × transdanubica arachnoidea

Pyrus × transdanubica arachnoidea, commonly known as the spider-tangled pear, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Pyrus communis and Pyrus pyraster.

It is characterized by its unique, hairy, and often twisted fruiting structures that resemble spider webs, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in the Danube basin region. Its fruit is small, yellowish, and has a mild, sweet flavor.

This species is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its ornamental value and genetic diversity.
