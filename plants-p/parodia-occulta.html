Parodia occulta

Parodia occulta, commonly known as the hidden parodia, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It is a slow-growing species that prefers well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and sought-after specimen among cactus enthusiasts.
