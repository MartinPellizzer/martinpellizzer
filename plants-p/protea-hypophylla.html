Protea hypophylla

Protea hypophylla, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family, which is known for its showy, often brightly colored floral structures. The plant features dense, leathery foliage that provides a lush green backdrop to its distinctive, cone-shaped inflorescences. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and red, are adapted for pollination by birds and insects.

Protea hypophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and hardy nature.
