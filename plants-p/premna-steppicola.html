Premna steppicola

Premna steppicola, commonly known as the steppe premna, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its small, opposite leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers.

Premna steppicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
