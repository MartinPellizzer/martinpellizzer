Phaius blumei var. bernaysii

Phaius blumei var. bernaysii, commonly known as the Bernays' ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, broad, sword-shaped leaves. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
