Penstemon laetus var. leptosepalus

Penstemon laetus var. leptosepalus, commonly known as the slender-sepal penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy coating.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
