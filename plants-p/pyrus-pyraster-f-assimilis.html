Pyrus pyraster f. assimilis

Pyrus pyraster f. assimilis, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a cultivar of the whitebeam tree.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale green leaves and produces small, edible fruits. This plant is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.

The plant is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
