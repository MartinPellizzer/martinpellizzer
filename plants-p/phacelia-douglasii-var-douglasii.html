Phacelia douglasii var. douglasii

Phacelia douglasii var. douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, while its stems are erect and branched.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
