Picrorhiza kurroa

Picrorhiza kurroa, commonly known as Kuth, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Pakistan.

It belongs to the family Labiatae and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant is renowned for its deep, branched root system, which is the primary source of its medicinal compounds. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

The roots are harvested during the winter season and are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and hepatoprotective properties.
