Plectranthus cordatus

Plectranthus cordatus, commonly known as the velvetleaf plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its widespread cultivation in subtropical regions.
