Pelargonium × gossypiifolium

Pelargonium × gossypiifolium, commonly known as the cottonleaf geranium, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from both *Pelargonium hortum* and *Gossypium* species.

It is characterized by its large, woolly, ovate leaves that resemble cotton fibers, giving it its common name. The plant typically produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, or white. It is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The botanical features of *Pelargonium × gossypiifolium* make it a unique and visually striking addition to gardens and floral arrangements.
