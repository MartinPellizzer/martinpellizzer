Parietaria debilis subvar. macrantha

Parietaria debilis subvar. macrantha, commonly known as the giant wallweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features upright stems that bear alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in humid conditions and its potential as a pioneer species in disturbed habitats.
