Paspalum mononeurum

Paspalum mononeurum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Paspalum mononeurum is often found in grasslands, disturbed sites, and along roadsides, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
