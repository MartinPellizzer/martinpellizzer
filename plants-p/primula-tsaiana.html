Primula tsaiana

Primula tsaiana, commonly known as the Tsaian primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, elongated fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Primula tsaiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with cool, humid conditions.
