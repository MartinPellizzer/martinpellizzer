Polygala vulgaris var. jablotskiana

Polygala vulgaris var. jablotskiana, commonly known as the Common milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender stems with small, compound leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers. Its flowers are often arranged in racemes and are characterized by their delicate, spurred petals.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
