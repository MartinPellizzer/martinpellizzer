Psyllocarpus glaber

Psyllocarpus glaber, commonly known as the smooth psyllocarpus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, also known as the legume or pea family. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds.

Psyllocarpus glaber is often found in moist, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
