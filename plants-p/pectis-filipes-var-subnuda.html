Pectis filipes var. subnuda

Pectis filipes var. subnuda, commonly known as the naked-stemmed pectis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairless stems and produces small, yellowish to brownish ray florets. Its leaves are typically linear and arranged alternately along the stem.

It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
