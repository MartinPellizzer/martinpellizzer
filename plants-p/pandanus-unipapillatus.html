Pandanus unipapillatus

Pandanus unipapillatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber. Its inflorescence consists of a branched cluster of small, fragrant flowers that attract various pollinators.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or wetland environments.
