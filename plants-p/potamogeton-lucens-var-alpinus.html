Potamogeton lucens var. alpinus

Potamogeton lucens var. alpinus, commonly known as the alpine pondweed, is a variant of the pondweed species native to freshwater environments in mountainous regions.

It is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne in clusters near the water surface. It thrives in cold, oligotrophic waters and plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and ecological balance.
