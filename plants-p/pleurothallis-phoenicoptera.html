Pleurothallis phoenicoptera

Pleurothallis phoenicoptera, commonly known as the red feather orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, red-brown inflorescence that resembles a feather, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture from the air. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a delicate, intricate structure typical of the Orchidaceae family.

Pleurothallis phoenicoptera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
