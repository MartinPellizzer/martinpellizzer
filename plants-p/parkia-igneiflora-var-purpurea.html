Parkia igneiflora var. purpurea

Parkia igneiflora var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple flame bean, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive purple-pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces elongated, flat pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it valuable for reforestation and soil enrichment.

The plant is also appreciated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
