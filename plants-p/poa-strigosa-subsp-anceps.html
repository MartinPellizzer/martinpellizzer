Poa strigosa subsp. anceps

Poa strigosa subsp.

anceps, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and sandy soils.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
