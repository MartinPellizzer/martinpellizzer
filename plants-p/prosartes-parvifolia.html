Prosartes parvifolia

Prosartes parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved woodland fern, is a species native to eastern North America.

It is a deciduous fern characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its fronds are usually upright and have a soft, feathery appearance.

Prosartes parvifolia reproduces via spores and is often found in rich, loamy soils.
