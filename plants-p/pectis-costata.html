Pectis costata

Pectis costata, commonly known as the narrowleaf pectis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinct, wavy margin.

Pectis costata produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a flat, disk-like cluster, which attracts various pollinators.
