Podocarpus celatus

Podocarpus celatus, commonly known as the Chinese podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and conical shape. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain edible seeds, which are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
