Potentilla aurea f. alpina

Potentilla aurea f. alpina, commonly known as the alpine golden cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine, soft hairs.

It plays a role in stabilizing alpine soils and provides nectar for pollinators in its habitat.
