Psidium mediterraneum

Psidium mediterraneum, commonly known as the Mediterranean guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to guavas but have a distinct, tangy flavor. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a glossy green surface and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The flowers are white and fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
