Philadelphus pringlei

Philadelphus pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and can grow up to 6 feet tall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
