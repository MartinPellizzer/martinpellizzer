Primulina xinningensis

Primulina xinningensis, commonly known as the Xinning primula, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
