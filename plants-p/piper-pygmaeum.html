Piper pygmaeum

Piper pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf pepper or little pepper, is a small evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, peppery fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters, with smooth, glossy foliage that is often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, and it produces tiny, dark blue berries that are edible but have a sharp, spicy flavor.

Piper pygmaeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in various cultures.
