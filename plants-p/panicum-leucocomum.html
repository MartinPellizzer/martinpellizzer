Panicum leucocomum

Panicum leucocomum, commonly known as white panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically light green in color. The plant produces fluffy, white seed heads that are highly visible during the late summer and early autumn months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
