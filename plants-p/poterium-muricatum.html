Poterium muricatum

Poterium muricatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by triangular, winged seeds. It thrives in moist, warm environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Malvaceae, which includes other plants such as hibiscus and cotton.
