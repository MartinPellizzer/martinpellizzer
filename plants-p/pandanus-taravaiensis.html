Pandanus taravaiensis

Pandanus taravaiensis, commonly known as the Tongan pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the island of Tongatapu in the Tonga archipelago.

It belongs to the Pandanus genus, which includes various species of screw-pines found throughout the Pacific region. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that form a dense crown. Its flowers, which are typically clustered at the top of the plant, are small and yellowish, often appearing in the dry season.

Pandanus taravaiensis plays a role in local ecosystems and has cultural significance among the people of Tonga.
