Penstemon richardsonii var. dentatus

Penstemon richardsonii var. dentatus, commonly known as the toothed beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly toothed edge, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

This species is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
