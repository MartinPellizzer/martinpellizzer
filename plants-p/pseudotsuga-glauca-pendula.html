Pseudotsuga glauca pendula

Pseudotsuga glauca pendula, commonly known as the hanging hemlock, is a rare and unique variant of the hemlock tree.

It is characterized by its drooping branches and needle-like leaves that give it a distinctive, cascading appearance. Native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States, it thrives in moist, cool climates with well-drained soil. The plant has a conical shape when mature, though its pendulous growth habit sets it apart from typical hemlock species.

Its botanical features make it a valuable subject for ecological and horticultural study.
