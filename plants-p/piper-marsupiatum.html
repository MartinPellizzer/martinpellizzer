Piper marsupiatum

Piper marsupiatum, commonly known as the giant pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the rainforests of northern South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces elongated, fleshy berries that are typically green when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
