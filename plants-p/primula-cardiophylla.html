Primula cardiophylla

Primula cardiophylla, commonly known as the heart-leaf primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of delicate, bell-like flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments and thrives in partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is valued for both its ornamental beauty and ecological significance in its native habitat.
