Peristeria lindenii

Peristeria lindenii, commonly known as the white cycad, is a striking evergreen plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces distinctive, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. Peristeria lindenii is known for its slow growth and preference for well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in botanical gardens and private collections.
