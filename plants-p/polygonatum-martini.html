Polygonatum martini

Polygonatum martini, commonly known as the Chinese Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces clusters of white or greenish-yellow flowers in late spring. Its berries are dark blue and contain several seeds.

Polygonatum martini is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in Chinese herbal medicine.
