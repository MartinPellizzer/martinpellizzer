Persicaria kawagoeana f. albida

Persicaria kawagoeana f. albida, commonly known as the white-veined knotweed, is a variant of the species Persicaria kawagoeana, characterized by its distinctive white veins on its leaves.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Japan, typically growing in moist, shady environments. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters and bloom in late summer, contributing to its ornamental value.

This cultivar is valued in horticulture for its unique foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
