Phoradendron flavescens var. villosum

Phoradendron flavescens var. villosum, commonly known as the silky flame tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing shrub that produces showy, yellow-orange flowers during the summer months. The plant is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and its ability to form dense thickets in tropical and subtropical environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing along forest edges or in disturbed areas.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitats.
