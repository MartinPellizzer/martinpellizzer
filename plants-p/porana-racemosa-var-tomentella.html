Porana racemosa var. tomentella

Porana racemosa var. tomentella, commonly known as the woolly porana, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
