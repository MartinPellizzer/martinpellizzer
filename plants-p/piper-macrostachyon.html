Piper macrostachyon

Piper macrostachyon, commonly known as the giant pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its long, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. Its flowers are small and clustered, and the fruit is a three-lobed berry that turns from green to red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been used in traditional medicine.
