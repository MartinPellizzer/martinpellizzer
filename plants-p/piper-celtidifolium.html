Piper celtidifolium

Piper celtidifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf pepper or the Ceylon pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Sri Lanka, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, which resembles a peppercorn, is used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.

The botanical features of Piper celtidifolium make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
