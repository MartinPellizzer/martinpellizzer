Philadelphus intectus var. pubiger

Philadelphus intectus var. pubiger, commonly known as the pubescent mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets that are hairy, especially on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in terminal clusters during early summer. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
