Primula villosa subsp. commutata

Primula villosa subsp.

commutata, commonly known as the hairy primrose, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage and floral structure make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
