Persicaria barbata

Persicaria barbata, commonly known as the hairy smartweed, is a flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pink to red flowers that appear in summer. The plant has finely hairy leaves and produces seed clusters that are often covered in short, bristly hairs. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Persicaria barbata is often considered a weed but can serve as a food source for certain insects and birds.
