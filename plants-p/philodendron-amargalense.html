Philodendron amargalense

Philodendron amargalense, commonly known as the Amargalense Philodendron, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly glossy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by dark blue berries, which are edible but often consumed by birds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This plant is popular among collectors for its unique foliage and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
