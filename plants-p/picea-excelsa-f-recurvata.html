Picea excelsa f. recurvata

Picea excelsa f. recurvata, commonly known as the recurved white spruce, is a rare and distinctive variant of the white spruce.

It is characterized by its elongated, drooping needles that give it a unique, cascading appearance. This form is typically found in high-altitude environments, where it adapts to harsh climatic conditions. The plant exhibits slow growth and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
