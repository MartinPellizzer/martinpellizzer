Phacelia patuliflora var. mexicana

Phacelia patuliflora var. mexicana, commonly known as Mexican blanketflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of showy, lavender to purple flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

This species is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
