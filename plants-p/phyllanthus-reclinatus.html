Phyllanthus reclinatus

Phyllanthus reclinatus, commonly known as the hanging plant or the devil's backbone, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Phyllanthus reclinatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
