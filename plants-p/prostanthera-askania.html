Prostanthera askania

Prostanthera askania, commonly known as the Askania mint, is a small shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. Prostanthera askania is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
