Penstemon divergens

Penstemon divergens, commonly known as the tall beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as meadows and slopes. It produces showy, sparsely branched stems and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Penstemon divergens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
