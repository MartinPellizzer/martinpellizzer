Pseudospermum commutatum

Pseudospermum commutatum, commonly known as the red-flowered false sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular red flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
