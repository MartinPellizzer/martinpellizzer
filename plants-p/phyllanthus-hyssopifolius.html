Phyllanthus hyssopifolius

Phyllanthus hyssopifolius, commonly known as the African spurge or Indian spurge, is a flowering plant native to Africa and southern Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
