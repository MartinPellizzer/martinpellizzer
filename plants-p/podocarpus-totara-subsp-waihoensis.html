Podocarpus totara subsp. waihoensis

Podocarpus totara subsp.

waihoensis, commonly known as the Waihō tōtara, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to New Zealand. It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its lanceolate, dark green leaves and distinctive cone structures.

The tree typically grows in coastal and lowland forests, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. It plays a significant ecological role in New Zealand’s native ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly broader leaves and different geographic distribution compared to other totara species.
