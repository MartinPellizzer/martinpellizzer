Pleurothallis glandulipetala

Pleurothallis glandulipetala, commonly known as the glandular-petaled pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on tree branches, relying on atmospheric moisture rather than soil for nutrients. The plant features slender, dark green leaves and produces delicate, white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive glandular texture on the petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high air circulation for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
