Pleiospermium longisepalum

Pleiospermium longisepalum, commonly known as the long-sepal orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers with elongated sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its roots are often covered in a dense network of mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
