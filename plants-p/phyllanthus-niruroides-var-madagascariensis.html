Phyllanthus niruroides var. madagascariensis

Phyllanthus niruroides var. madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar gooseberry, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its small, bright yellow or orange berries that are often covered in a whitish, waxy coating. The plant has alternate, simple leaves that are usually elliptical in shape with a smooth margin. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
