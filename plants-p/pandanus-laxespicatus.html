Pandanus laxespicatus

Pandanus laxespicatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and as thatching material in some regions.

The species is adapted to coastal and wetland environments, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
