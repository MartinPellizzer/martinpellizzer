Pilosella auriculoides subsp. semiauriculoides

Pilosella auriculoides subsp.

semiauriculoides, commonly known as the semi-auriculate pilosella, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with yellow disc florets and white to pale yellow ray florets.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lanceolate or ovate in shape. It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, and thrives in rocky or sandy soils.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.
