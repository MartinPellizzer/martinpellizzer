Polygala galioides var. molluginifolia

Polygala galioides var. molluginifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often branched and covered with fine, silky hairs. The leaves are narrow and linear, typically arranged oppositely along the stem, giving the plant a delicate appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, usually pink or purple in color.

This species is adapted to dry, sandy soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats such as pine flatwoods and sandhill communities.
