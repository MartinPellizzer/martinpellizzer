Phoradendron surinamense

Phoradendron surinamense, commonly known as the Surinam trumpet bush, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the roots of other trees to obtain some of its nutrients.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, including parts of Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color, which attract birds and insects for pollination. Its leaves are alternate and simple, often with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
