Poa vaginalis

Poa vaginalis, commonly known as the common bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-like growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lawns, meadows, and along stream banks.

Due to its hardiness and adaptability, Poa vaginalis is both a common weed and a valuable forage plant.
