Podanthes irrorata

Podanthes irrorata, commonly known as the silverpod cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and covered in fine, white hairs that give it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Podanthes irrorata is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
