Pseudosecale villosum

Pseudosecale villosum, commonly known as the hairy false brome, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences bearing small, two-lipped flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Pseudosecale villosum plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying high-altitude flora.
