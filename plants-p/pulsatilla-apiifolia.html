Pulsatilla apiifolia

Pulsatilla apiifolia, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has palmate leaves with lobed segments, and its stems are covered in soft, woolly hairs. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Pulsatilla apiifolia is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
