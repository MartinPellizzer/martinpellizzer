Perralderia coronopifolia

Perralderia coronopifolia, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, featuring dense, compact foliage with small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring, adding vibrant color to its habitat. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh, dry climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
