Piper pastasanum f. intermedium

Piper pastasanum f. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate pepper plant, is a horticultural form of the genus Piper, which belongs to the family Piperaceae.

It is characterized by its unique morphological traits that lie between the typical wild and cultivated varieties of Piper pastasanum. The plant typically features elongated, slender leaves with a glossy surface and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark purple color, containing aromatic seeds.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.
