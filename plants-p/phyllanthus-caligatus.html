Phyllanthus caligatus

Phyllanthus caligatus, commonly known as the small-leaved phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Phyllanthus caligatus is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
