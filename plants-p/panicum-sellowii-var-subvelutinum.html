Panicum sellowii var. subvelutinum

Panicum sellowii var. subvelutinum, commonly known as the silky panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs covering the leaves and inflorescence, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed spikelets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
