Potentilla erecta var. vulgaris

Potentilla erecta var. vulgaris, commonly known as shrub cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five to nine elliptical leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long flowering period and ability to attract pollinators.
