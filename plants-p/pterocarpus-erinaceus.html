Pterocarpus erinaceus

Pterocarpus erinaceus, commonly known as African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, fragrant wood. The tree produces yellowish or reddish flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its seeds are encased in thick, woody pods, and the plant is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.

The wood of Pterocarpus erinaceus is highly valued for its beauty and durability, making it a sought-after material in furniture and musical instrument manufacturing.
