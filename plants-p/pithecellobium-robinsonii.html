Pithecellobium robinsonii

Pithecellobium robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's crownfruit, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible, pod-like fruits that contain a single seed, often used in local cuisines. Its growth habit includes a woody stem with thorn-like projections, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits in nitrogen fixation.
