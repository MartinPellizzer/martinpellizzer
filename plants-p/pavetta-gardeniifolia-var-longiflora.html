Pavetta gardeniifolia var. longiflora

Pavetta gardeniifolia var. longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered pavetta, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
