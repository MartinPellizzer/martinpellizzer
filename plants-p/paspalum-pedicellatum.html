Paspalum pedicellatum

Paspalum pedicellatum, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and riparian zones.

Paspalum pedicellatum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
