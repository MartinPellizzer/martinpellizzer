Plantago lagopus subsp. cylindrica

Plantago lagopus subsp.

cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical plantain, is a perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions. It is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces tall, cylindrical inflorescences that bear small, greenish flowers. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is an important component of the vegetation in its native range.
