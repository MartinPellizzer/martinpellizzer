Passiflora moluccana var. teysmanniana

Passiflora moluccana var. teysmanniana, commonly known as the Teyssmann's passionflower, is a species native to Indonesia.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking, showy flowers with intricate structures. The flowers feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with a crown of filaments that surround the reproductive organs. The plant also bears edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
