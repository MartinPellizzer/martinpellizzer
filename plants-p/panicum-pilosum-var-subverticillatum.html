Panicum pilosum var. subverticillatum

Panicum pilosum var. subverticillatum, commonly known as the silky panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and compact, rounded growth habit. The plant produces fluffy, seed-bearing heads that are often used in ornamental gardening for their visual appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
