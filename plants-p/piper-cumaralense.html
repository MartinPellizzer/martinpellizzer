Piper cumaralense

Piper cumaralense, commonly known as Brazilian pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a glossy surface and a distinct aroma.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
