Poa delawarica

Poa delawarica, commonly known as the Delawar bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
