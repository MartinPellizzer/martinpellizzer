Phlomis brachystegia

Phlomis brachystegia, commonly known as narrow-leaved hemp-nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in whorls along the stem, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Phlomis brachystegia is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
