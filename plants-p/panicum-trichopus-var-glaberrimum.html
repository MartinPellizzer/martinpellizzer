Panicum trichopus var. glaberrimum

Panicum trichopus var. glaberrimum, commonly known as the smooth panicgrass, is a variant of the panic grass genus native to certain regions of Africa.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous. The plant produces fluffy, seed-bearing panicles that are often wind-dispersed, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential use in restoration projects.
