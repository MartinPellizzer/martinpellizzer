Phagnalon × tarraconense

Phagnalon × tarraconense, commonly known as the Spanish star-thistle, is a hybrid plant species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its spiny, star-shaped leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in arid conditions.

Due to its invasive nature, it is considered a noxious weed in many regions outside its native range.
