Philadelphus × latvicus

Philadelphus × latvicus, commonly known as the Latvian Mock Orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It typically grows to a height of 2–4 meters and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Philadelphus × latvicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its sweet, citrus-like fragrance.
