Potentilla ferganensis

Potentilla ferganensis, commonly known as the Fergana cinquefoil, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces oval, woody fruits known as achenes.

Potentilla ferganensis is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
