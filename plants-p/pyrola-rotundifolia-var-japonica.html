Pyrola rotundifolia var. japonica

Pyrola rotundifolia var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese roundleaf snowbell, is a low-growing evergreen herb native to Japan.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically densely packed along its stems. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species in horticulture.
