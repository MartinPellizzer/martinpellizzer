Pavonia piptocalyx

Pavonia piptocalyx, commonly known as the scarlet pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets, and its flowers are clustered in terminal racemes. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in wetlands.

Pavonia piptocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
