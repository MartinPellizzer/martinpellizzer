Peucedanum cous

Peucedanum cous, commonly known as Persian cumin or Persian coriander, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes parsley, dill, and carrots, and is characterized by its hollow stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its seeds are often used as a spice, valued for their aromatic and slightly bitter flavor.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers temperate climates.
