Potentilla multifida subsp. bimundorum

Potentilla multifida subsp.

bimundorum, commonly known as the Sierra plum blossom, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its botanical features include a woody stem and a compact growth habit, making it well adapted to its arid environment.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance to native pollinators.
