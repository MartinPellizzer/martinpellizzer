Pleurothallis madisonii

Pleurothallis madisonii, commonly known as the Madison's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air.

Its botanical features include narrow, strap-like leaves and a compact inflorescence that supports its showy blooms.
