Passiflora longifilamentosa

Passiflora longifilamentosa, commonly known as the long filament passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers that feature a unique blend of petals, sepals, and modified leaves. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic appeal.
