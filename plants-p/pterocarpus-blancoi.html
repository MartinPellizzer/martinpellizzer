Pterocarpus blancoi

Pterocarpus blancoi, commonly known as the Philippine rosewood, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dark, dense wood with a rich reddish-brown color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are compound, with multiple leaflets arranged in a feather-like pattern.

The tree is valued for its high-quality timber and is also used in traditional medicine.
