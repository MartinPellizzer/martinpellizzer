Potentilla patula var. typica

Potentilla patula var. typica, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to North America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from May to June. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, with numerous slender stems that spread along the ground. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and are covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
