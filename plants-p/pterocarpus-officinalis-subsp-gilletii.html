Pterocarpus officinalis subsp. gilletii

Pterocarpus officinalis subsp.

gilletii, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa. It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by woody pods containing flat, elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Due to its valuable timber, it is often cultivated for its ornamental and economic significance.
