Persicaria subsagittata

Persicaria subsagittata, commonly known as arrowleaf groundcherry, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Persicaria subsagittata is often found in moist soils and is considered a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
