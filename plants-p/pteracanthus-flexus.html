Pteracanthus flexus

Pteracanthus flexus, commonly known as the flexible fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping underground stems that produce new shoots. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with a soft, leathery texture and a pale green color.

Pteracanthus flexus thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
