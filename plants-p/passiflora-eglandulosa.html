Passiflora eglandulosa

Passiflora eglandulosa, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered with a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Passiflora eglandulosa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its beautiful blooms and unique foliage.
