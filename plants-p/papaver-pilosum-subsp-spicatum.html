Papaver pilosum subsp. spicatum

Papaver pilosum subsp.

spicatum, commonly known as the spiked poppy, is a species of flowering plant in the family Papaveraceae. It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a distinctive black or dark red center.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dry seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
