Piper chavicoides

Piper chavicoides, commonly known as the Chavica pepper or Chavica vine, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that can reach several meters in length. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature.
