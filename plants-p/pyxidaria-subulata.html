Pyxidaria subulata

Pyxidaria subulata, commonly known as the needleleaf pyxidaria, is a species of flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, it is well adapted to dry conditions with its succulent stems and reduced leaves.

Pyxidaria subulata is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
