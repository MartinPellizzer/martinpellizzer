Piper multinodum var. schwackei

Piper multinodum var. schwackei, commonly known as the Schwacke's pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to reddish when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its foliage and unique growth habit.
