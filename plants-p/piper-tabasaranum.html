Piper tabasaranum

Piper tabasaranum, commonly known as the Tabasaran pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and is used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
