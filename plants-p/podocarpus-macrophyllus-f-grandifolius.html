Podocarpus macrophyllus f. grandifolius

Podocarpus macrophyllus f. grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leaved umbrella pine, is a variant of the Chinese sandalwood.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and horticultural use.
