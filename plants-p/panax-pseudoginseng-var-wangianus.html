Panax pseudoginseng var. wangianus

Panax pseudoginseng var. wangianus, commonly known as the Wangian ginseng, is a variant of the pseudoginseng plant native to China.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its perennial herbaceous growth habit. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. Its roots are often harvested for their medicinal properties, similar to other ginseng species.

However, it is less commercially cultivated compared to its more famous relatives.
