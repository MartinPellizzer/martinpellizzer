Prunus communis oxypyrena

Prunus communis oxypyrena, commonly known as the Spanish cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible drupes that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for local use.
