Phyllanthus trichotepalus

Phyllanthus trichotepalus, commonly known as the hairy phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves with a distinctive hairy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and landscaping.
