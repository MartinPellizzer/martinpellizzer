Paspalum comosum

Paspalum comosum, commonly known as the hairy paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, fibrous root system, which allows it to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Paspalum comosum is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and fields, where it can form dense mats.
