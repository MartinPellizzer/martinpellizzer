Pyrrhocactus transitensis

Pyrrhocactus transitensis, commonly known as the Transient Red Cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features a compact, globular shape with bright red spines that provide protection against herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its vivid foliage. Its leaves are fleshy and thick, adapted to store water efficiently in its native desert environment.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
