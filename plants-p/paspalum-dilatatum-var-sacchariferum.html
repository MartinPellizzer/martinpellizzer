Paspalum dilatatum var. sacchariferum

Paspalum dilatatum var. sacchariferum, commonly known as sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its fine, soft blades and a sweet, honey-like fragrance when crushed. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments. Its flowering heads are small and spike-like, often appearing in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in crafts and rituals.
