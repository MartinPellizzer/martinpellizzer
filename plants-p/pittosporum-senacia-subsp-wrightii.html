Pittosporum senacia subsp. wrightii

Pittosporum senacia subsp.

wrightii, commonly known as Wright's pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of dark green, oval-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
