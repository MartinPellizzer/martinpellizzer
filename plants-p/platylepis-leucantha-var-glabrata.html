Platylepis leucantha var. glabrata

Platylepis leucantha var. glabrata, commonly known as the smoothleaf platylepis, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. The plant has smooth, glabrous leaves that are elliptical in shape and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
