Piper dominantinervium

Piper dominantinervium, commonly known as the Dominant Nerve Pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in countries like Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, opposite leaves with prominent veins. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, a feature common in the Piper genus.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds, and the plant is valued for its aromatic properties.
