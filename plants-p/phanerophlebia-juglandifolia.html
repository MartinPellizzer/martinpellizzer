Phanerophlebia juglandifolia

Phanerophlebia juglandifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf tree of the Juglandaceae family, is a deciduous tree characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive bark.

It typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and moderate climates. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible nuts, which are important for both wildlife and human consumption. Its growth habit includes a tall, straight trunk with a rounded crown, making it a valuable species in reforestation efforts.

The leaves are usually alternate and have a rough texture, contributing to its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various organisms.
