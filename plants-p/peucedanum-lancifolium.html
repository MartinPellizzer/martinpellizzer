Peucedanum lancifolium

Peucedanum lancifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved parsley, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often used as a culinary herb due to their mild, parsley-like flavor. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Peucedanum lancifolium is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
