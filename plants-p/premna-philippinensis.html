Premna philippinensis

Premna philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, including the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories and along stream banks.

Premna philippinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
