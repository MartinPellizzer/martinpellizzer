Podocoma cuneifolia

Podocoma cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf podocoma, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems and small, narrow, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
