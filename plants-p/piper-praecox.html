Piper praecox

Piper praecox, commonly known as early pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, three-lobed berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
