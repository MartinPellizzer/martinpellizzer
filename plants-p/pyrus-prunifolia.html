Pyrus prunifolia

Pyrus prunifolia, commonly known as the cherry pear, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, edible fruits that resemble both cherries and pears, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces white or pink flowers in the spring.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and can be found in traditional recipes across its native regions.
