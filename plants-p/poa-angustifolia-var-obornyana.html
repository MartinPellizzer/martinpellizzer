Poa angustifolia var. obornyana

Poa angustifolia var. obornyana, commonly known as the narrowleaf poa, is a variant of the narrowleaf grass species.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in meadows and open woodlands. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are usually narrow and slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which contain the small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms.
