Puhuaea

Puhuaea, commonly known as the Hawaiian broom, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to the dry, sunny conditions of its native habitat. Puhuaea has small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and is part of the unique flora of Hawaii.
