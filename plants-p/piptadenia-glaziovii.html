Piptadenia glaziovii

Piptadenia glaziovii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or yellow copaiba, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The tree produces yellow flowers that give way to elongated pods containing reddish-brown seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of essential oils.
