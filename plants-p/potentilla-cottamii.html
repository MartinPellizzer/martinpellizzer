Potentilla cottamii

Potentilla cottamii, commonly known as the Cottam's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the British Isles.

It typically reaches a height of about 15-30 centimeters and is characterized by its dense, spreading habit. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, with five leaflets that are serrated and slightly hairy.

Potentilla cottamii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.
