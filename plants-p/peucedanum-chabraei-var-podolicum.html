Peucedanum chabraei var. podolicum

Peucedanum chabraei var. podolicum, commonly known as the Podolic mountain parsley, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features slender, upright stems and finely divided, feather-like leaves that are aromatic and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal potential.
