Poa mucronulata

Poa mucronulata, commonly known as the blunt-tipped grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
