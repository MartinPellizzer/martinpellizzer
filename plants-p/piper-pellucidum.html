Piper pellucidum

Piper pellucidum, commonly known as the transparent pepper or glassy pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its small, inconspicuous flowers and elongated, slender fruits that contain aromatic seeds. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a rough texture.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
