Pseudosasa longivaginata

Pseudosasa longivaginata, commonly known as the long sheath bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and elongated, sheathing leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Pseudosasa longivaginata is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
