Phaseolus pachycarpus

Phaseolus pachycarpus, commonly known as the giant hyacinth bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from violet to white, and its pods that can grow up to 20 centimeters in length. The plant produces edible seeds that are used in various cuisines, though they are often consumed in their immature, tender form. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental or for its edible parts.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
