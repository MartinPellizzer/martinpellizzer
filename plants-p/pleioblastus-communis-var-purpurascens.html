Pleioblastus communis var. purpurascens

Pleioblastus communis var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple bamboo, is a variant of the common bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purplish-red culms, which become more vivid as the plant matures. The leaves are typically a deep green, providing a contrast to the colorful stems. This bamboo is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a versatile choice for temperate climates.
