Phaseolus atropurpureus

Phaseolus atropurpureus, commonly known as the purple hull cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with small, purple-pink flowers that give way to elongated pods. The plant produces edible seeds that are widely cultivated for their nutritional value and drought tolerance. It thrives in warm climates and is often grown as a summer crop.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
