Pachystigma triphyllum

Pachystigma triphyllum, commonly known as the three-leaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or woodland habitats.

Pachystigma triphyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
