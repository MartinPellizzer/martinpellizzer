Persica vulgaris var. scleropersica

Persica vulgaris var. scleropersica, commonly known as the Persian persimmon, is a variant of the common peach that exhibits distinct botanical features.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery fruit skin, which provides protection against environmental stressors. The leaves are typically ovate with a smooth margin and a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its unique fruit.

This variety is well adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it a resilient choice for cultivation in such regions.
