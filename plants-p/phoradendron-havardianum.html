Phoradendron havardianum

Phoradendron havardianum, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the Americas.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant produces small, white or yellowish berries that are often consumed by birds, which then disperse the seeds. Its parasitic nature and unique reproductive strategy make it an important component of forest ecosystems.

Phoradendron havardianum plays a role in the ecological balance by influencing host tree growth and providing food for various wildlife species.
