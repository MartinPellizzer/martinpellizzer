Piper protrusum

Piper protrusum, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its climbing habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
