Polemonium liniflorum

Polemonium liniflorum, commonly known as bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to white, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, shady woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Polemonium liniflorum is also an important nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
