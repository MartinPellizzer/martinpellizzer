Passiflora boenderi

Passiflora boenderi, commonly known as the Boender's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescence that features a crown of white filaments and a central cluster of red and yellow filaments. The plant produces showy, complex flowers that are pollinated by insects and birds. Its leaves are typically ovate with a serrated margin and a rough texture.

Passiflora boenderi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
