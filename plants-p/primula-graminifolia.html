Primula graminifolia

Primula graminifolia, commonly known as the grassleaf primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
