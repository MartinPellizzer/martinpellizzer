Prunus × chichibuensis var. uyekii

Prunus × chichibuensis var. uyekii, commonly known as the Uyekii cherry, is a hybrid species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to other cherry species. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
