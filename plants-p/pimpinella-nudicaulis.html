Pimpinella nudicaulis

Pimpinella nudicaulis, commonly known as the slender cow-wheat, is a small, annual herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and woodland edges. The plant has a slender, branching stem with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellowish-white flowers are clustered in umbels, a characteristic feature of the Apiaceae family.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
