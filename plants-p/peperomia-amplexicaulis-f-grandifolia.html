Peperomia amplexicaulis f. grandifolia

Peperomia amplexicaulis f. grandifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf peperomia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to tropical regions.

It features thick, waxy, and often variegated leaves that are broad and clasping, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of greenish-white or pink, which are typically borne in clusters. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
