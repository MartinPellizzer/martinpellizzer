Pelargonium × veitchianum

Pelargonium × veitchianum, commonly known as the scented geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its fragrant leaves that emit a variety of scents, such as citrus, mint, or spice. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with rounded, velvety foliage and showy flowers in shades of red, pink, or white. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Pelargonium × veitchianum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and indoor cultivation.
