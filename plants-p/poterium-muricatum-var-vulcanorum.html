Poterium muricatum var. vulcanorum

Poterium muricatum var. vulcanorum, commonly known as the volcanic thistle, is a flowering plant native to the volcanic regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its spiny, serrated leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed or volcanic landscapes. It has adapted to high-altitude environments with low humidity and intense sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
