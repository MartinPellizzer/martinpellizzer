Pyronia winkleri

Pyronia winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's pyronia, is a species of moth belonging to the family Erebidae.

It is characterized by its striking wing patterns, which feature intricate geometric designs in shades of brown and black. The plant associated with this species is the common milkweed, Asclepias syriaca, which serves as a host plant for its larvae. The moth's life cycle is closely tied to the growth and flowering of milkweed, making it an important indicator of habitat health.

Due to habitat loss, Pyronia winkleri is considered a species of conservation concern in several regions.
