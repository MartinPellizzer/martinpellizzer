Primula harsukhii

Primula harsukhii, commonly known as the Harsukh primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of northern India.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its foliage consists of broad, ovate leaves with a velvety texture.

Primula harsukhii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
