Pyrrhocactus bulbocalyx

Pyrrhocactus bulbocalyx, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a vibrant touch to its appearance. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, making it well adapted to arid environments.

The species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
