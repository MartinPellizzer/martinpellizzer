Papaver nudicaule subsp. relictum

Papaver nudicaule subsp.

relictum, commonly known as the slender poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, upright stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant produces capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
