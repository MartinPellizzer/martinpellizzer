Polygonum fowleri subsp. hudsonianum

Polygonum fowleri subsp.

hudsonianum, commonly known as the Hudson's polygonum, is a perennial herb native to North America. It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

The plant features upright stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, adorned with lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife.
