Parkia igneiflora

Parkia igneiflora, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that appear in clusters during the dry season. The tree has pinnate leaves and produces flat, woody pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its timber and traditional medicinal uses.
