Pedilanthus tehuacanus

Pedilanthus tehuacanus, commonly known as the sacred lotus of Mexico, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a cluster of small lanterns. The plant has elliptical, glossy leaves and is known for its strong, sometimes unpleasant odor. It thrives in humid environments and is often found growing near water sources.

Due to its unique appearance and cultural significance, it is a popular subject in botanical gardens and horticultural studies.
