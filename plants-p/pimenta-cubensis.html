Pimenta cubensis

Pimenta cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban allspice or pimenta, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, red-brown seeds used as a spice. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing the aromatic seeds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, while its bark is rough and reddish in color.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its essential oil and medicinal properties.
