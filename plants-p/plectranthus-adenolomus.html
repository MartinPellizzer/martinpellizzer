Plectranthus adenolomus

Plectranthus adenolomus, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus adenolomus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
