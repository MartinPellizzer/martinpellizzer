Petrorhagia illyrica subsp. angustifolia

Petrorhagia illyrica subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf rockrose, is a perennial shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula. It features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has a compact growth habit with gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in garden settings.
