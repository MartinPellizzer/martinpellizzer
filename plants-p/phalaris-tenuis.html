Phalaris tenuis

Phalaris tenuis, commonly known as narrowleaf beakgrass, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands, wet meadows, and disturbed soils.

Phalaris tenuis is often found in open areas with moderate moisture levels.
