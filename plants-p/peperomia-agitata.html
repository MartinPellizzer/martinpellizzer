Peperomia agitata

Peperomia agitata, commonly known as the Shaking Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, waxy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight sheen, and it produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or pale pink. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage, making it an attractive addition to indoor gardens. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia agitata is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for plant enthusiasts seeking a resilient, decorative plant.
