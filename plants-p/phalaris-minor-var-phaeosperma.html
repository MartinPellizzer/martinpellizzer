Phalaris minor var. phaeosperma

Phalaris minor var. phaeosperma, commonly known as the purple false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce purple to brown flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a blue-green hue that becomes more pronounced in the summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
