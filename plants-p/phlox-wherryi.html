Phlox wherryi

Phlox wherryi, commonly known as Wherry's phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in woodland edges. The plant features showy clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Phlox wherryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun conditions.
