Pleione corniculata

Pleione corniculata, commonly known as the horned pleione, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive horn-shaped lip. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, with flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
