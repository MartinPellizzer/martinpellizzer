Phlomis portae

Phlomis portae, commonly known as narrow-leaved mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features tall, upright stems covered in soft, fuzzy hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. It produces showy, pale yellow to orange flowers in summer, typically arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stem.

This plant is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its medicinal and ornamental properties.
