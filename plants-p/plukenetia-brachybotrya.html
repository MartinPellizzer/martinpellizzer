Plukenetia brachybotrya

Plukenetia brachybotrya, commonly known as the African butternut, is a tropical shrub native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its small, pale yellow flowers and produces edible, oily seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. The plant thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a rough texture, while its stems are woody and branched.

The species is valued for its nutritional and medicinal properties, making it an important plant in local ecosystems.
