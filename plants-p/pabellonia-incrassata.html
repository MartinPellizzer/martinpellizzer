Pabellonia incrassata

Pabellonia incrassata, commonly known as the thickened bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a violet or purple throat. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
