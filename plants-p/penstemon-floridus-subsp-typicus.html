Penstemon floridus subsp. typicus

Penstemon floridus subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the Florida beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as sandhill prairies and disturbed soils. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
