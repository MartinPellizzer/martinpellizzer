Polygala vulgaris var. nicaeensis

Polygala vulgaris var. nicaeensis, commonly known as the Nicaean milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands. The plant features slender stems with small, greenish-white flowers clustered in loose inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and usually covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
