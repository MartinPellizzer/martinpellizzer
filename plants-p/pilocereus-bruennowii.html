Pilocereus bruennowii

Pilocereus bruennowii, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a flowering cactus native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically produces blooms in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
