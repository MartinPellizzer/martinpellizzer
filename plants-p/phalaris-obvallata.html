Phalaris obvallata

Phalaris obvallata, commonly known as the arrowhead grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as riverbanks and ditches, where it forms dense stands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it an effective colonizer of disturbed soils.

Phalaris obvallata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various insects and birds.
