Philodendron quercifolium

Philodendron quercifolium, commonly known as the oak-leaf philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that resemble oak leaves, giving it its common name. The plant has a climbing habit and can grow quite large, reaching heights of several meters in its natural habitat. Its foliage is typically a deep green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red berries.

It is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
