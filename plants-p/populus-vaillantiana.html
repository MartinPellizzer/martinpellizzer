Populus vaillantiana

Populus vaillantiana, commonly known as the Chinese poplar, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Populus vaillantiana is also valued for its fast growth and ability to tolerate various soil conditions.
