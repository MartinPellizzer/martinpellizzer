Paullinia costata

Paullinia costata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often dotted with small, raised oil glands. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that give way to bright red, fleshy berries. Its bark is thick and rough, and it contains compounds with potential medicinal properties.

Paullinia costata is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its adaptability to different climates.
