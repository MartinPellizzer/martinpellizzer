Payera mandrarensis

Payera mandrarensis, commonly known as the Madagascar grass or African grass, is a perennial herb native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening and as a forage plant.
