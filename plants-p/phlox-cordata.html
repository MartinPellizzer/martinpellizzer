Phlox cordata

Phlox cordata, commonly known as heartleaf phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Phlox cordata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
