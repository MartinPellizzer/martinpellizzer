Pandanus tectorius var. stradbrookensis

Pandanus tectorius var. stradbrookensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its narrow, stiff leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear fragrant, yellowish flowers, which are often used in traditional ceremonies. Its fibrous leaves are utilized in basket weaving and thatching, making it an important resource in local communities.

The species is well adapted to coastal and wetland environments, thriving in humid, saline conditions.
