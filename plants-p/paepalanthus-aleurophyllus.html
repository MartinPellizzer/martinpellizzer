Paepalanthus aleurophyllus

Paepalanthus aleurophyllus, commonly known as the silverleaf cactus, is a unique species native to the high Andes of South America.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming plant with dense, woolly foliage that helps it retain moisture in its arid environment. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are covered in a fine, silvery down, which reflects sunlight and reduces water loss.

This species is adapted to harsh, high-altitude conditions and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
