Penstemon garrettii

Penstemon garrettii, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Penstemon garrettii is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the summer season.
