Pandorea poincillantha

Pandorea poincillantha, commonly known as the pink trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of pink to red tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are opposite and covered in a fine, silky pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological value.
