Polylobium filiforme

Polylobium filiforme, commonly known as the threadleaf polylobium, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
