Pyrus orbicularis

Pyrus orbicularis, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its round, orbicular-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-sour berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Pyrus orbicularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal interest.
