Passiflora tribolophylla

Passiflora tribolophylla, commonly known as the tri-lobed passionflower, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, blue or purple berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are typically lobed, with three to five deep indentations, giving it its common name.

Passiflora tribolophylla is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its potential medicinal properties.
