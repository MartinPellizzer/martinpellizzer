Prunus serotina var. neomontana

Prunus serotina var. neomontana, commonly known as the Appalachian black cherry, is a variant of the black cherry tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has a straight, slender trunk and exhibits a conical shape when young, which becomes more irregular with age. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
