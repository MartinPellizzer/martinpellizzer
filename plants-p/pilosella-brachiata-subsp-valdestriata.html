Pilosella brachiata subsp. valdestriata

Pilosella brachiata subsp.

valdestriata, commonly known as the Valdestriata hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, leafy stems and small yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its subspecies status highlights its distinct morphological and ecological traits compared to other Pilosella species.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to plant biodiversity.
