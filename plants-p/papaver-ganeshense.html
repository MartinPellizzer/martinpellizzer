Papaver ganeshense

Papaver ganeshense, commonly known as the Ganesh Himalayan poppy, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red petals that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at elevations between 3,000 and 5,000 meters. Its flowers are relatively small but are often surrounded by a unique, leaf-like bract that enhances its visual appeal.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its beauty and ecological significance.
