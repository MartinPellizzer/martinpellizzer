Piper heimii

Piper heimii, commonly known as the Java pepper or long pepper, is a tropical climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated, curved berries containing aromatic seeds. Its fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to black pepper.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental and medicinal value.
