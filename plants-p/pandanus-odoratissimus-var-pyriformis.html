Pandanus odoratissimus var. pyriformis

Pandanus odoratissimus var. pyriformis, commonly known as the sweet-scented pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and ceremonies. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit, known as a pandanus or hala fruit, is edible and has a sweet, fibrous texture.

This variety is valued not only for its ornamental appearance but also for its cultural and economic significance in island communities.
