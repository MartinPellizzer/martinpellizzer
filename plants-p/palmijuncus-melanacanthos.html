Palmijuncus melanacanthos

Palmijuncus melanacanthos, commonly known as the black-thorned palm, is a unique plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that give it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that eventually develop into fruit, which is edible and often consumed by local wildlife. Its botanical features make it an important species in its native ecosystem, contributing to biodiversity and ecological balance.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered a vulnerable species in some areas.
