Panicum malacophyllum

Panicum malacophyllum, commonly known as the blackroot grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black roots, which give it its common name, and its slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in ornamental gardens.
