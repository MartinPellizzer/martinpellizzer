Psychine stylosa var. auriculata

Psychine stylosa var. auriculata, commonly known as the golden ear cactus, is a unique succulent native to specific regions of South America.

It features a compact, rounded stem with a distinctive golden hue, especially during the flowering season. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange, adding to its ornamental value. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

This variety is prized for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with minimal water.
