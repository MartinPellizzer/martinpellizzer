Polygonatum multiflorum var. bracteatum

Polygonatum multiflorum var. bracteatum, commonly known as the Chinese Solomon's Seal, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features rhizomatous growth and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in early summer, which are followed by red berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and rocky areas.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a valued plant in horticulture and traditional medicine.
