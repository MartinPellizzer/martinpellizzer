Poa sibirica var. macrantha

Poa sibirica var. macrantha, commonly known as the Siberian bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces blue-green foliage that is dense and hardy. The plant typically grows in open, moist habitats such as tundra and alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool, temperate conditions. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are tightly clustered and often appear as a single, compact panicle.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to harsh environments and its role in stabilizing soil in arctic and subarctic regions.
