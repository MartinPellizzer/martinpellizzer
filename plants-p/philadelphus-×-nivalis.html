Philadelphus × nivalis

Philadelphus × nivalis, commonly known as the mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It typically grows to a height of 1.5 to 3 meters and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Philadelphus × nivalis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
