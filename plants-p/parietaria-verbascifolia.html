Parietaria verbascifolia

Parietaria verbascifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf lungwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Urticaceae.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
