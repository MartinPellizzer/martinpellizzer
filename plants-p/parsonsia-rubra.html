Parsonsia rubra

Parsonsia rubra, commonly known as the red-flowered piper, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers, which are typically clustered in panicles. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually lanceolate in shape. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

Parsonsia rubra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
