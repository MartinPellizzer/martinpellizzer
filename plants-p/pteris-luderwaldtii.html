Pteris luderwaldtii

Pteris luderwaldtii, commonly known as the golden fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, intricately veined fronds that display a striking golden hue on the underside. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to moist soil or rock surfaces.

Pteris luderwaldtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
