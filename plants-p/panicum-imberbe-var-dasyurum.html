Panicum imberbe var. dasyurum

Panicum imberbe var. dasyurum, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, silky-textured leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often blue or purple in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas, woodlands, and along watercourses. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and restoration projects.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for erosion control and habitat creation for native wildlife.
