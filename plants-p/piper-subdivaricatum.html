Piper subdivaricatum

Piper subdivaricatum, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that matures to a yellowish color.
