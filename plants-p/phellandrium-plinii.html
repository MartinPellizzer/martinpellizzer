Phellandrium plinii

Phellandrium plinii, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Honey Myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been historically used in traditional Aboriginal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal scrub.

Phellandrium plinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
