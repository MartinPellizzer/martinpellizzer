Pyrus Reducta

Pyrus reducta is a rare and intriguing species of apple tree native to the mountainous regions of central Asia. Known for its compact size and unique foliage, it stands out among its relatives in the Rosaceae family. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky soils at high altitudes. Its small, tart fruits have been historically used in local traditional medicine and cuisine. Despite its limited distribution, Pyrus reducta remains a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
