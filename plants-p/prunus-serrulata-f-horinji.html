Prunus serrulata f. horinji

Prunus serrulata f. horinji, commonly known as the Horinji cherry, is a unique variant of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, double-flowered blossoms that appear in clusters, giving it a more ornate appearance than typical cherry blossoms. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The Horinji cherry is particularly noted for its early blooming period, adding a striking visual element to spring landscapes.
