Phaseolus harmsianus

Phaseolus harmsianus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It produces vibrant red or yellow flowers and elongated pods containing edible seeds. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its nutritious beans. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both gardeners and agricultural producers.
