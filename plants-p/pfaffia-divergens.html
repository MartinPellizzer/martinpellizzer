Pfaffia divergens

Pfaffia divergens, commonly known as curare plant or arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly the presence of compounds used in traditional medicine. Its rhizomes and roots are often harvested for their alkaloid content, which has been historically used in arrow poison.

Pfaffia divergens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
