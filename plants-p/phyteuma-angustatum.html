Phyteuma angustatum

Phyteuma angustatum, commonly known as narrowleaf phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soil. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, producing vibrant purple or pink blooms.

The leaves are linear and often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
