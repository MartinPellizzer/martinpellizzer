Plantago pachyphylla f. intermedia

Plantago pachyphylla f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate thick-leaved plantain, is a variant of the plantain species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, an adaptation to its harsh environment. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a dense spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

This form is less common than the typical variety but shares many of the same ecological and medicinal traits.
