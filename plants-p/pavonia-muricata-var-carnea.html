Pavonia muricata var. carnea

Pavonia muricata var. carnea, commonly known as the red velvet bush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and showy, clustered flowers that range from pink to deep red. The plant features velvety, dark green foliage that adds to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and compact form.

Pavonia muricata var. carnea is well-suited for use in gardens, containers, or as a hedge due to its hardiness and adaptability.
