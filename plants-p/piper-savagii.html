Piper savagii

Piper savagii, commonly known as the swamp pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into peppery, berry-like fruits. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem with aerial roots and a preference for moist, shaded environments.

Piper savagii is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in humid conditions.
