Premna membranacea var. cordata

Premna membranacea var. cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf premna, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in clusters. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

The leaves are hairy and have a slightly aromatic scent when crushed.
