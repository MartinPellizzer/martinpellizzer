Potentilla sibbaldia var. micrantha

Potentilla sibbaldia var. micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters of five. The plant has slender, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural use in rocky or dry landscapes.
