Pleurothallis palliolata

Pleurothallis palliolata, commonly known as the pale-veined orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant has elongated, strap-like leaves and produces inflorescences that bear multiple tiny, fragrant blossoms. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
