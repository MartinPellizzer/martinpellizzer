Petroana montserratii

Petroana montserratii, commonly known as the Montserrat rock orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the island of Montserrat in the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the island's volcanic terrain. Its conservation status is vulnerable due to habitat loss and limited distribution.

Petroana montserratii plays an important ecological role in its native ecosystem, supporting local pollinators and contributing to the region's biodiversity.
