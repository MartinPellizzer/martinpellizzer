Polygonum aviculare subsp. induratum

Polygonum aviculare subsp.

induratum, commonly known as sand polygon, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It is a low-growing herb with erect, often branched stems that are covered in fine, hairy structures.

Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a hairy underside, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
