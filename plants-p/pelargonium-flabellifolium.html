Pelargonium flabellifolium

Pelargonium flabellifolium, commonly known as the fan-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fan-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in gardens around the world.
