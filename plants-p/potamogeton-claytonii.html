Potamogeton claytonii

Potamogeton claytonii, commonly known as Clayton's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. Its small, green flowers are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces small, dark brown fruits.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
