Passiflora herbertiana subsp.

insulae-howei, commonly known as the insular passionflower, is a rare and endemic species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking, intricate floral structures with five sepals, five petals, and a unique crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive organs.

The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces vibrant, colorful flowers that attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. Its fruit, which develops after flowering, is a small, fleshy berry containing edible seeds.
