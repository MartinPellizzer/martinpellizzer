Polygonum molliiforme

Polygonum molliiforme, commonly known as softleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, succulent stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five pink or red petals, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Polygonum molliiforme is often found in disturbed soils and is adaptable to a range of soil types.
