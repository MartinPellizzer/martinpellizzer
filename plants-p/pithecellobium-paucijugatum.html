Pithecellobium paucijugatum

Pithecellobium paucijugatum, commonly known as the small-leaf velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

Its botanical features include nitrogen-fixing abilities, making it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
