Physurus xystophyllus

Physurus xystophyllus, commonly known as the woolly leaf fern, is a unique plant characterized by its dense, velvety foliage.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, hairy texture, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant thrives in humid environments and requires indirect sunlight to flourish. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread gradually, forming a compact, bushy form.

Physurus xystophyllus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
