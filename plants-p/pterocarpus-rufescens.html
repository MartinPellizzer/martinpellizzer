Pterocarpus rufescens

Pterocarpus rufescens, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has bipinnate leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a legume containing flat, winged seeds.

Pterocarpus rufescens is also noted for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
