Plantago strictissima

Plantago strictissima, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features upright, narrow leaves that are often ribbed and have a waxy surface. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes, which are typically borne on tall, slender stalks. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in meadows.

The leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
