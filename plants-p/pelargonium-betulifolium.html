Pelargonium betulifolium

Pelargonium betulifolium, commonly known as the birchleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely toothed, birch-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in temperate climates.
