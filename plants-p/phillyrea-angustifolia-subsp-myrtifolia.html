Phillyrea angustifolia subsp. myrtifolia

Phillyrea angustifolia subsp.

myrtifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Phillyrea, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in short inflorescences. These flowers are followed by small, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species.

It is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
