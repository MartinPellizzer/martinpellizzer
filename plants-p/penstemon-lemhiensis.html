Penstemon lemhiensis

Penstemon lemhiensis, commonly known as the Lemhi beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions, preferring full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a fuzzy texture.

Penstemon lemhiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
