Prunus serrulata var. plenapendula

Prunus serrulata var. plenapendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping peach blossom, is a cultivar of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that bear clusters of pale pink or white flowers in early spring. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is often planted in gardens for its ornamental value and is a symbol of beauty and transience in Japanese culture.

The leaves are dark green and turn yellow in the fall, adding to its seasonal appeal.
