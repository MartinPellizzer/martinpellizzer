Pentaschistis imperfecta

Pentaschistis imperfecta, commonly known as the white-flowered sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, creeping stems that form dense mats. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss.

Pentaschistis imperfecta is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its resilience in harsh environments.
