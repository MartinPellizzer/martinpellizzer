Phaius marshallianus var. triloba

Phaius marshallianus var. triloba, commonly known as the Marshall's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has strap-like leaves that are often dark green and glossy. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
