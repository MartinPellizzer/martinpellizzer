Parodia catamarcensis

Parodia catamarcensis, commonly known as the Catamarca cactus, is a species of succulent native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems and spines. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. It produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
