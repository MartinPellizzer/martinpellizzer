Passiflora pulchella

Passiflora pulchella, commonly known as the beautiful passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a crown of crimson filaments and a central cluster of blue or purple sepals. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are covered in fine, white hairs.

Passiflora pulchella is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
