Phalaris arundinacea f. pallens

Phalaris arundinacea f. pallens, commonly known as silver reed, is a grass-like plant characterized by its silver-blue foliage.

It is a perennial species native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in wet or marshy habitats. The plant produces tall, slender spikes of pale blue to white flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive silvery sheen that gives it its common name.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in moist environments.
