Phrynium violaceum

Phrynium violaceum, commonly known as the purple elephant's ear, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, often with a purple or reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces striking orange or red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Phrynium violaceum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dramatic foliage and vibrant floral display.
