Piptochaetium bicolor

Piptochaetium bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-toned leaves, with the upper surface typically lighter in color than the lower surface. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
