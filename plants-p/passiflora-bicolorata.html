Passiflora bicolorata

Passiflora bicolorata, commonly known as the two-colored passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a blend of white, purple, and pink hues. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora bicolorata is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
