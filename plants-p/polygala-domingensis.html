Polygala domingensis

Polygala domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the Dominican Republic.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant is notable for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations, including certain species of butterflies.
