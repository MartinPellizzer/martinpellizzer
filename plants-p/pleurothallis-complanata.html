Pleurothallis complanata

Pleurothallis complanata, commonly known as the flat-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or branches. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a subtle fragrance, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are broad and flat, adapted to capture moisture from the air, and its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the surrounding environment.

Pleurothallis complanata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
