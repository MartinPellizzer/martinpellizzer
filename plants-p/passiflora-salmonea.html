Passiflora salmonea

Passiflora salmonea, commonly known as the salmon passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that display a mix of pink, white, and purple hues. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora salmonea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
