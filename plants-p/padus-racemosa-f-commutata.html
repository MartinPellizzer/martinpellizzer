Padus racemosa f. commutata

Padus racemosa f. commutata, commonly known as the smooth-flowered white crabsapple, is a botanical variant of the common crabapple.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and flowers that are typically white or pale pink. This form is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland edges and riparian areas, contributing to local biodiversity.

The plant produces small, edible fruits that are important for wildlife and can be used in jams and jellies.
