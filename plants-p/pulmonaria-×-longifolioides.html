Pulmonaria × longifolioides

Pulmonaria × longifolioides, commonly known as the long-leaved lungwort, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense covering of fine, hairy structures. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in temperate regions.
