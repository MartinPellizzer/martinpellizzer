Pycreus stramineus

Pycreus stramineus, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. Pycreus stramineus thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
