Physalis virginiana var. sonorae

Physalis virginiana var. sonorae, commonly known as the Sonoran gooseberry, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It produces small, edible berries enclosed in a papery, lantern-like calyx. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and branched.

The fruit ripens to a bright orange or red color, attracting various wildlife species.
