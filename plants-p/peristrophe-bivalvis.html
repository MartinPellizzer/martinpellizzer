Peristrophe bivalvis

Peristrophe bivalvis, commonly known as the two-valved peristrophe, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive two-part calyx that resembles a bivalve shell. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
