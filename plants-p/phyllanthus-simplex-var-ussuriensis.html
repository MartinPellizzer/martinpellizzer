Phyllanthus simplex var. ussuriensis

Phyllanthus simplex var. ussuriensis, commonly known as the Ussuri sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features small, fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
