Phlox condensata var. hendersonii

Phlox condensata var. hendersonii, commonly known as Henderson's phlox, is a compact, flowering perennial native to the western United States.

It produces showy clusters of pink to lavender flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 inches, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
