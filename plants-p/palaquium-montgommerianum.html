Palaquium montgommerianum

Palaquium montgommerianum, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a segmented berry with a bright red rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.
