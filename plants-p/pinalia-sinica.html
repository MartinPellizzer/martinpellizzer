Pinalia sinica

Pinalia sinica, commonly known as the Chinese pinyon, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually white or pink, and it produces fruit that is used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The botanical features of Pinalia sinica make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
