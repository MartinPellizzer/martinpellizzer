Pleurothallis imraei

Pleurothallis imraei, commonly known as the Imraei orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually pale green to white, with a subtle fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
