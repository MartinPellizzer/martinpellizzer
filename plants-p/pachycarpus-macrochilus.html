Pachycarpus macrochilus

Pachycarpus macrochilus, commonly known as the giant spur orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers with prominent, elongated spurs that can reach several centimeters in length. Its leaves are broad and succulent, adapted to store water in its native arid conditions.

The orchid's unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.
