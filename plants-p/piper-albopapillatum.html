Piper albopapillatum

Piper albopapillatum, commonly known as the white-papillate pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are green and clustered, and it produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe.

The plant is noted for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
