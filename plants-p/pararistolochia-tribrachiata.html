Pararistolochia tribrachiata

Pararistolochia tribrachiata, commonly known as the Indian pipe vine, is a climbing plant belonging to the Aristolochiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, and often fragrant flowers that resemble pipes, with a complex structure that includes a spur-like appendage. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

The species contains aristolochic acids, which are toxic compounds that have been linked to kidney damage and other health issues.
