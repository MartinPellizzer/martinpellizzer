Pyrrosia eleagnifolia

Pyrrosia eleagnifolia, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its leathery, dark green fronds with a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to its substrate.

Pyrrosia eleagnifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
