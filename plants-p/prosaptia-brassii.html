Prosaptia brassii

Prosaptia brassii, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant features strap-like leaves that are broad and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
