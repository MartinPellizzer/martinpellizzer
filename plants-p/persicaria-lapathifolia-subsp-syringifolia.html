Persicaria lapathifolia subsp. syringifolia

Persicaria lapathifolia subsp.

syringifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf knotweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and Europe. It features lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive species within the Polygonaceae family.
