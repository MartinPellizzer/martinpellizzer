Pastinaca kochii var. latifolia

Pastinaca kochii var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved scorzonera, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter, with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbel-like clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to garden landscapes.
