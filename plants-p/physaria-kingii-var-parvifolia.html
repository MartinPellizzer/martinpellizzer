Physaria kingii var. parvifolia

Physaria kingii var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of these regions.
