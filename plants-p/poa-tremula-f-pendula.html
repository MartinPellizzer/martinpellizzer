Poa tremula f. pendula

Poa tremula f. pendula, commonly known as the hanging meadow grass, is a variant of the meadow grass species characterized by its distinctive pendulous growth habit.

This plant typically features slender, arching stems that droop gracefully, creating a cascading effect. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft, bluish-green hue that adds a subtle aesthetic to its natural habitat. It thrives in moist, open environments such as meadows and streambanks.

The inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, contributing to its ornamental value in horticultural settings.
