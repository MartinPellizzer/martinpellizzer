Persea breviflora

Persea breviflora, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically followed by small, round fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a strong aromatic scent.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit in tropical and subtropical regions.
