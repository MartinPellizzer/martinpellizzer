Plumbago viscosa

Plumbago viscosa, commonly known as sticky plumbago, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of blue or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, sticky substance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive flowers and drought tolerance.
