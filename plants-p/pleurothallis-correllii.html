Pleurothallis correllii

Pleurothallis correllii, commonly known as the Correll's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces intricate, fragrant blooms that attract various pollinators.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
