Psoralea angustifolia

Psoralea angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf psoralea, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow or pale yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and are adapted for pollination by insects.

Psoralea angustifolia is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with root nodules.
