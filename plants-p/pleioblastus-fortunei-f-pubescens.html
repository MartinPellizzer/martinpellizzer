Pleioblastus fortunei f. pubescens

Pleioblastus fortunei f. pubescens, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a variant of the fortune bamboo characterized by its silvery-gray foliage.

It is a clumping bamboo that grows in dense, upright clusters, forming a compact and ornamental shape. The leaves are narrow, elongated, and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It is native to China and thrives in moist, well-drained soils in partial to full shade.

This plant is popular in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
