Prunus mutabilis f. odoratissima

Prunus mutabilis f. odoratissima, commonly known as the fragrant white cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus mutabilis species, which is native to Japan.

This plant is characterized by its white flowers that emit a strong, sweet fragrance, especially during the night. The leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a dark green color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of growing conditions.
