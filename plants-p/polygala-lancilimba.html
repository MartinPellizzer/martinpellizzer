Polygala lancilimba

Polygala lancilimba, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and India.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellow or greenish flowers that are often arranged in racemes, and its leaves are typically narrow and opposite. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine in some regions.
