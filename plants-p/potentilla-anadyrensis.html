Potentilla anadyrensis

Potentilla anadyrensis, commonly known as the Altai cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer.

Potentilla anadyrensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
