Piper decurrens

Piper decurrens, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elliptical, berry-like fruits that turn red when mature. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and analgesic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
