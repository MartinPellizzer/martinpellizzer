Potentilla zhangbeiensis

Potentilla zhangbeiensis, commonly known as the Zhangbei cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to northern China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces fruit that is a dry capsule.

Due to its limited distribution and unique ecological role, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
