Pelargonium × cucumerinum

Pelargonium × cucumerinum, commonly known as the lemon-scented geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant is native to South Africa and is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and aromatic properties. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for container gardening and use in hanging baskets.
