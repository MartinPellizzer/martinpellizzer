Prunus hypoleuca

Prunus hypoleuca, commonly known as the white cherry or Japanese white cherry, is a small deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and seasonal beauty.

Prunus hypoleuca prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
