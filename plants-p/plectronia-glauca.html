Plectronia glauca

Plectronia glauca, commonly known as the blue plectronia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is adapted to arid conditions and can thrive in full sun.

Plectronia glauca is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
