Polythrix stenandrium

Polythrix stenandrium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved polythrix, is a member of the Polythrichaceae family, characterized by its small, moss-like appearance.

It is a non-vascular plant that thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a dense, cushion-like growth form with closely packed, elongated leaves that are usually dark green in color. Its reproductive structures, known as sporophytes, are small and often hidden among the foliage.

Polythrix stenandrium plays an important ecological role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its habitat.
