Passiflora oblongata var. lyrifolia

Passiflora oblongata var. lyrifolia, commonly known as the lyrifolia passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding a central cluster of stamens. The leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture. This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral displays.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
