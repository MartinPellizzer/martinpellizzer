Pyrus sylvestris var. sativa

Pyrus sylvestris var. sativa, commonly known as the common pear, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces pomes, which are fleshy fruits with a crisp, juicy texture and a sweet or tart flavor. It is widely cultivated for its edible fruit, which is used in cooking, baking, and cider production.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in horticulture.
