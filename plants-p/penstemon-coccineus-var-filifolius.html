Penstemon coccineus var. filifolius

Penstemon coccineus var. filifolius, commonly known as the threadleaf penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, linear leaves that give it its "threadleaf" designation, and it produces striking red to orange tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun conditions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
