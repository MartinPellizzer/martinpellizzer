Polygala lioranii

Polygala lioranii, commonly known as the Loran's milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with five petals and a distinctive spur.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
