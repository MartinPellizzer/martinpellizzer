Piper pubipedunculum

Piper pubipedunculum, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, pubescent stems and small, opposite leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are unisexual, with both male and female flowers occurring on the same plant, and it produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature.

The plant is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
