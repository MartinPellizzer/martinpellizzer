Parosela megalostachys

Parosela megalostachys, commonly known as the giant lespedeza, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, pinkish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Parosela megalostachys is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
