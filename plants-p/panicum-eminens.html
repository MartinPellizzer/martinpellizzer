Panicum eminens

Panicum eminens, commonly known as the tall panicgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open areas such as fields, roadsides, and disturbed soils. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its widespread distribution.

Due to its hardy nature, Panicum eminens is often used in restoration projects and as a forage crop.
