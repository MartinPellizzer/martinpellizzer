Pluchea sessilifolia

Pluchea sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaf heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, sessile leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its small, yellowish flowers are surrounded by white or pale green ray florets, and it produces small, dry fruits called cypselae.

Pluchea sessilifolia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
