Physalis ovata

Physalis ovata, commonly known as the common husk tomato, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, egg-shaped fruits that are encased in a papery calyx. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into the distinctive orange to red berries. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine pubescence.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.
