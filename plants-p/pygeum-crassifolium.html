Pygeum crassifolium

Pygeum crassifolium, commonly known as the thick-leaved African chinquapin, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, spiny fruits containing one or two seeds. Its bark is thick and deeply fissured, providing a distinctive appearance.

Pygeum crassifolium is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
