Piper platannanum

Piper platannanum, commonly known as the West Indian pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in greenish-white clusters. Its fruits are small, yellow berries that contain seeds, and the plant is often grown for its ornamental value.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
