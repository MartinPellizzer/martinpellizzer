Petrophile pulchella var. typica

Petrophile pulchella var. typica, commonly known as the showy rock-heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy pink or red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows on rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to coastal conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
