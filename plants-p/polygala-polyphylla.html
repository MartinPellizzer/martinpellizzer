Polygala polyphylla

Polygala polyphylla, commonly known as the many-leaved milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually compound, with multiple small leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers.

Despite its modest appearance, it holds cultural significance in traditional Chinese medicine.
