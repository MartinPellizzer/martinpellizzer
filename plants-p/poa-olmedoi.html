Poa olmedoi

Poa olmedoi, commonly known as the Olmedo bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted blades and a bluish-green color, which gives it its common name. This plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has a clumping growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Poa olmedoi is often used in native plant landscaping due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
