Phoenix djalonensis

Phoenix djalonensis, commonly known as the Djalonensis date palm, is a rare and endemic species native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that eventually develop into edible dates. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.

Its botanical features make it an important subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
