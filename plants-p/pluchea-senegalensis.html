Pluchea senegalensis

Pluchea senegalensis, commonly known as African feverfew, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant has serrated leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally for treating fevers and inflammatory conditions.
