Psidium cinereum var. paraguariae

Psidium cinereum var. paraguariae, commonly known as the Paraguayan guava, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The Paraguayan guava is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
