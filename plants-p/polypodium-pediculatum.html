Polypodium pediculatum

Polypodium pediculatum, commonly known as the slender wall fern, is a terrestrial fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant has a rhizomatous structure that allows it to spread and form dense colonies. Its fronds are pinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance, and they often have a bluish-green hue.

Polypodium pediculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
