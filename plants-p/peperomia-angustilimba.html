Peperomia angustilimba

Peperomia angustilimba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf peperomia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a compact, low-growing plant with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The leaves are usually narrow and pointed, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in spadices, typical of the Piperaceae family.

This plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
