Pilea longruiensis

Pilea longruiensis, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its round, coin-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are thick and glossy, with a slightly waxy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Pilea longruiensis is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and ease of care.
