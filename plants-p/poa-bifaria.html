Poa bifaria

Poa bifaria, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along streambanks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Poa bifaria plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
