Plantago acanthophylla var. bracteosa

Plantago acanthophylla var. bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteate plantain, is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves.

It produces tall, branched flowering stems that bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America, particularly in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is adapted to withstand drought conditions due to its succulent leaves and deep root system.
