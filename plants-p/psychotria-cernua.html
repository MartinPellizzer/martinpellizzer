Psychotria cernua

Psychotria cernua, commonly known as the "dwarf morning glory," is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive hooded shape. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs.

Psychotria cernua is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories.
