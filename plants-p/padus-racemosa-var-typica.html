Padus racemosa var. typica

Padus racemosa var. typica, commonly known as the common bird cherry.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall.

The species is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks.
