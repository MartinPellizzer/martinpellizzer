Piper arboreum var. latifolium

Piper arboreum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad, glossy leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine.

Its foliage provides habitat for various insects and small animals in its native ecosystem.
