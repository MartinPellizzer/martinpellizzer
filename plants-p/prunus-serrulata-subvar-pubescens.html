Prunus serrulata subvar. pubescens

Prunus serrulata subvar. pubescens, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and a pubescent (hairy) texture on the underside. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The species is closely related to other Prunus species, including the common cherry and peach.
