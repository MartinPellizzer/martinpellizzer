Polygala langebergensis

Polygala langebergensis, commonly known as the Langeberg milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, white to pale yellow flowers that resemble milkweed. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
