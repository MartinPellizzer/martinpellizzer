Persicaria filiformis

Persicaria filiformis, commonly known as slender knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and stream banks. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped. Small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
