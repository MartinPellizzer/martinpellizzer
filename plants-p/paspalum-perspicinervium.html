Paspalum perspicinervium

Paspalum perspicinervium, commonly known as the slender paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in non-native environments.
