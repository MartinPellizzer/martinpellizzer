Paspalum erianthoides

Paspalum erianthoides, commonly known as silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescences that produce small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often used in wetland restoration projects.
