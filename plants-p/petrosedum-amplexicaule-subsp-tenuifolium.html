Petrosedum amplexicaule subsp. tenuifolium

Petrosedum amplexicaule subsp.

tenuifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features compact, rosette-forming foliage with narrow, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, usually during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements further enhance its appeal as a decorative and hardy plant.
