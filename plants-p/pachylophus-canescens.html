Pachylophus canescens

Pachylophus canescens, commonly known as the woolly phyllodine, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, woolly coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green or yellow in color. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The botanical features of Pachylophus canescens make it an interesting subject for study in ecological and taxonomic research.
