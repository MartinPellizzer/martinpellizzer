Pseudopilocereus bradei

Pseudopilocereus bradei, commonly known as the brade's night bloomer, is a climbing cactus native to Mexico.

It features spiny stems and produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant has a distinctive ribbed structure and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by moths and bats.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
