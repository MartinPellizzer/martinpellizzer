Pyrus karensium

Pyrus karensium, commonly known as the Korean pear, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The fruit, which is typically harvested in late summer, is a small, sweet, and juicy apple-like fruit. It is valued for its ornamental value and is also cultivated for its edible fruit in certain regions.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
