Pinus orientalis

Pinus orientalis, commonly known as the oriental pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, needle-like leaves that grow in clusters of five. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

The oriental pine is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in the region.
