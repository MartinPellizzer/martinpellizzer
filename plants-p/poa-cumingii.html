Poa cumingii

Poa cumingii, commonly known as Cuming's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and the production of compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in cooler, temperate climates. Its leaves are often a deep blue-green color, which contributes to its ornamental appeal.

Poa cumingii is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities and ability to form dense, attractive turf.
