Paullinia coriacea

Paullinia coriacea, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody pods containing the edible seeds known as Brazil nuts. The plant is part of the Sapotaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
