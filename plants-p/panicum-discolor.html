Panicum discolor

Panicum discolor, commonly known as the variable panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and panicles of small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its flowering period varies depending on the climate, often occurring in the late spring to early summer.

Panicum discolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
