Periclymenum verticillatum

Periclymenum verticillatum, commonly known as the creeping jenny, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
