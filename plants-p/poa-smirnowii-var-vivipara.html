Poa smirnowii var. vivipara

Poa smirnowii var. vivipara, commonly known as the viviparous meadow grass, is a variant of the meadow grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, leafy blades that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, wind-pollinated flowers. One of its notable features is its ability to produce viviparous seeds, which germinate while still attached to the parent plant.

This unique reproductive strategy allows it to colonize disturbed or marginal habitats efficiently.
