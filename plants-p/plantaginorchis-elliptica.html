Plantaginorchis elliptica

Plantaginorchis elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of China.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and delicate, often violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or stream banks. Its botanical features include a short rhizome and showy, spurred orchid flowers that bloom in late spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
