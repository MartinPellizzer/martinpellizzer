Potentilla lazica

Potentilla lazica, commonly known as the Lazica cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, aggregate fruits.

Potentilla lazica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
