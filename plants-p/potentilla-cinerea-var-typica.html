Potentilla cinerea var. typica

Potentilla cinerea var. typica, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions make it a popular choice for ecological restoration projects.
