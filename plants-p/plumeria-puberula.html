Plumeria puberula

Plumeria puberula, commonly known as the sacred frangipani, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that come in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine, soft hairs. Its flowers bloom in clusters at the ends of the stems, often during the dry season.

Plumeria puberula is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and holds cultural significance in various regions.
