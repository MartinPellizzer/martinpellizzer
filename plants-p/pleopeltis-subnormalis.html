Pleopeltis subnormalis

Pleopeltis subnormalis, commonly known as the broad-leafed rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its leathery, broad leaves that are often divided into segments, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. Its fronds are usually evergreen and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

Pleopeltis subnormalis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, moist conditions.
