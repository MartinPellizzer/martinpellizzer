Plectrocarpa bonariensis

Plectrocarpa bonariensis, commonly known as the Argentine thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has a woody stem and produces small, bract-like leaves that help reduce water loss.

Plectrocarpa bonariensis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its distinctive appearance.
