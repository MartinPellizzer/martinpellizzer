Prunus sweginzowii

Prunus sweginzowii, commonly known as the Sweginzowii cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, dark red to black fruits, which are edible but typically not cultivated for commercial use.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its compact growth habit and seasonal interest.
