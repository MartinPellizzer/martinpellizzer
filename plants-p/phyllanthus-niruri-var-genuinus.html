Phyllanthus niruri var. genuinus

Phyllanthus niruri var. genuinus, commonly known as stonebreaker or cheesewood, is a small shrub native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, round berries that contain the seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Botanically, it is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
