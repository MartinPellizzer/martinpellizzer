Potentilla sericea var. polyschista

Potentilla sericea var. polyschista, commonly known as the silverleaf potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
