Pseudocymbidium lueri

Pseudocymbidium lueri, commonly known as the Luer's pseudocymbidium, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, strap-like leaves and its unique inflorescence that bears a single, showy flower with a distinctive, spurred lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or among rocky substrates. Its flowers are usually a mix of white, green, and yellow, with a subtle fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by collectors and conservationists alike.
