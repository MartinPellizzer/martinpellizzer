Potamogeton perfoliatus var. mandshuriensis

Potamogeton perfoliatus var. mandshuriensis, commonly known as the Mandshurian pondweed, is a perennial aquatic plant native to northeastern Asia.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, rivers, and wetlands. The plant has long, slender stems that are often submerged, with whorled leaves that are narrow and pointed. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, and it produces small, yellowish-brown fruits.

This variety is adapted to tolerate a range of water conditions and is often found in shallow, slow-moving waters.
