Pectis elongata var. schottii

Pectis elongata var. schottii, commonly known as the Schott's horse-tail, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems with small, linear leaves that give it a grass-like appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow and are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters.

It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
