Potamogeton alpinus var. tenuifolius

Potamogeton alpinus var. tenuifolius, commonly known as the slender-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to cold, freshwater environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams, where it forms dense underwater vegetation. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often less than 1 mm wide, and it produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and stable environmental conditions.
