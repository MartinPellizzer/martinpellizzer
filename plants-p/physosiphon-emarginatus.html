Physosiphon emarginatus

Physosiphon emarginatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf waterplantain, is a perennial aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, marshes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a distinctive waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Physosiphon emarginatus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and amphibians.
