Passiflora globosa

Passiflora globosa, commonly known as the globular passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and bracts. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture.

Passiflora globosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates.
