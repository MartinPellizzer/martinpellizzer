Persicaria hookeri

Persicaria hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, clustered flowers with five petals. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Persicaria hookeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
