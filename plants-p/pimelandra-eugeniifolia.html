Pimelandra eugeniifolia

Pimelandra eugeniifolia, commonly known as the golden trumpet plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and is often grown as an ornamental plant for its vibrant appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Pimelandra eugeniifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
