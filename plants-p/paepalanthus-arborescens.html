Paepalanthus arborescens

Paepalanthus arborescens, commonly known as the tree peacock cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It is a shrub-like plant that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features a woody stem with branches that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible for certain animals.

This plant is well adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
