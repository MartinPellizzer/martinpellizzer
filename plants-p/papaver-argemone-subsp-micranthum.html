Papaver argemone subsp. micranthum

Papaver argemone subsp.

micranthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved poppy, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it has been cultivated in various parts of the world.
