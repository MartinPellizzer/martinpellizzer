Plectranthus lophanthoides var. graciliflorus

Plectranthus lophanthoides var. graciliflorus, commonly known as the slenderflower plectranthus, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly aromatic scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable choice for gardens and containers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
