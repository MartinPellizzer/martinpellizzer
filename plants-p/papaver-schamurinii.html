Papaver schamurinii

Papaver schamurinii, commonly known as the Schamurinii poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its striking, vibrant red petals and a prominent, centrally located seed pod. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It blooms in the summer months, producing showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
