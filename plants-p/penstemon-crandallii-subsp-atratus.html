Penstemon crandallii subsp. atratus

Penstemon crandallii subsp.

atratus, commonly known as the black-horned penstemon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers and dark, almost black, tubular corollas.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native pollinators.
