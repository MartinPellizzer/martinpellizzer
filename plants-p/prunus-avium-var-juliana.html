Prunus avium var. juliana

Prunus avium var. juliana, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or dwarf cherry, is a cultivar of the sweet cherry tree.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, intensely flavored fruits. The plant typically blooms with showy pink to white flowers in early spring, preceding the fruiting season. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly serrated margin, and the bark is smooth and grayish-brown.

This variety is valued for ornamental purposes and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
