Polygonum persicaria proles agreste

Polygonum persicaria proles agreste, commonly known as common knotgrass, is a species of flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, stream banks, and damp woodlands. The plant features upright, branched stems covered with small, shield-shaped leaves. Its flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes, producing tiny, greenish-white blooms.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
