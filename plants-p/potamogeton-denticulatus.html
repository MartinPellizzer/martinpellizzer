Potamogeton denticulatus

Potamogeton denticulatus, commonly known as the toothed pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are often ribbed and have small teeth along the edges, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown, and it reproduces both sexually and vegetatively. It thrives in shallow, nutrient-rich waters and is often found in lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

Potamogeton denticulatus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
