Pothos solitarius

Pothos solitarius, commonly known as the solitary pothos, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically variegated with shades of green, yellow, and white. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ease of care.

Its trailing growth habit makes it suitable for hanging baskets or shelves.
