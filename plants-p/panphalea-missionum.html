Panphalea missionum

Panphalea missionum, commonly known as the Mission vine, is a climbing plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional Mexican cuisine. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
