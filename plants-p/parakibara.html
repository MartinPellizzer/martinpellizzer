Parakibara

Parakibara, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its trunk is often smooth and slightly swollen. Parakibara is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
