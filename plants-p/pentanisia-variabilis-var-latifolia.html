Pentanisia variabilis var. latifolia

Pentanisia variabilis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf pentanisia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are broad and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
