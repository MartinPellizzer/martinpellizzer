Pongamia piscidia

Pongamia piscidia, commonly known as the Indian beech or pongam tree, is a leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnately compound. The tree produces yellowish to brownish pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It has a deep root system that helps in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.

The plant is also valued for its potential in biofuel production due to its high oil content in its seeds.
