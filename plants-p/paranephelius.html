Paranephelius

Paranephelius, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, translucent leaves that resemble ghostly shapes, giving it its common name. The plant is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. It thrives in humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Paranephelius is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unusual appearance and ability to purify the air.
