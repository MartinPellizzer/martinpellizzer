Phyllanthus trichosporus

Phyllanthus trichosporus, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glabrous or hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often grown for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
