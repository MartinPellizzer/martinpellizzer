Perralderiopsis aucheri

Perralderiopsis aucheri, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The plant typically blooms in the summer months and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. It has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps to reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Perralderiopsis aucheri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
