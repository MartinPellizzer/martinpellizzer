Pleioblastus chrysanthus

Pleioblastus chrysanthus, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, green to golden-yellow culms that often have a distinctive purplish hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This bamboo is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is used in landscaping and traditional crafts.
