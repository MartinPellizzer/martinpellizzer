Pterocarpus villosus

Pterocarpus villosus, commonly known as the silky sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces yellowish flowers followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing flat, fibrous seeds. It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The plant also has cultural significance in some regions and is used in traditional medicine.
