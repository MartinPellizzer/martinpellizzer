Pleurothallis marginalis

Pleurothallis marginalis, commonly known as the marginal orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are usually elongated and grass-like, and it is often found growing on trees or rocks in its natural habitat.

Pleurothallis marginalis is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
