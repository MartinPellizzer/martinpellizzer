Potamogeton × confinis

Potamogeton × confinis, commonly known as the hybrid water plant, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton praelatus.

It is a submerged aquatic plant that typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged. Potamogeton × confinis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality and is sometimes used as a bioindicator in ecological studies.
