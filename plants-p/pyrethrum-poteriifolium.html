Pyrethrum poteriifolium

Pyrethrum poteriifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant features lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Pyrethrum poteriifolium is also valued for its natural insect-repelling properties due to the presence of pyrethrins in its flowers.
