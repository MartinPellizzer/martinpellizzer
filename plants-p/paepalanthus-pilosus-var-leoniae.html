Paepalanthus pilosus var. leoniae

Paepalanthus pilosus var. leoniae, commonly known as the Leonia's hairy pincushion, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage that resembles a pincushion, with soft, woolly leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces vibrant purple or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a dramatic visual contrast to its textured foliage. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
