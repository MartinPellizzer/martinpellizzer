Passovia virgata

Passovia virgata, commonly known as the Virgin's Glory, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its blooming period occurs during the spring and early summer months.

Passovia virgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
