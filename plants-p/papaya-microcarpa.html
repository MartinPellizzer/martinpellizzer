Papaya microcarpa

Papaya microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf papaya, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, juicy flavor. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy surface.

Papaya microcarpa thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.
