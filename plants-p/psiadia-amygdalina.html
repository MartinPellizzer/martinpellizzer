Psiadia amygdalina

Psiadia amygdalina, commonly known as the almondleaf sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has linear, aromatic leaves that are often mistaken for almond leaves due to their similar appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and wildflower meadows.

Psiadia amygdalina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
