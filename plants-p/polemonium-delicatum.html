Polemonium delicatum

Polemonium delicatum, commonly known as the white Jacob's ladder, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale blue. The plant thrives in moist, shady woodland habitats and is often found in rich, well-drained soils. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
