Phyteuma scheuchzeri subsp. charmelioides

Phyteuma scheuchzeri subsp.

charmelioides, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky and mountainous habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. It blooms during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
