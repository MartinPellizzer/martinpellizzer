Piptochaetium leianthum

Piptochaetium leianthum, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of spikelets.

Piptochaetium leianthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
