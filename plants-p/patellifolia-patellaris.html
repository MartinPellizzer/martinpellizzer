Patellifolia patellaris

Patellifolia patellaris, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellow flowers in the spring. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand saline and arid conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
