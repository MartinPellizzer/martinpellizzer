Polystachya kaluluensis

Polystachya kaluluensis, commonly known as the Kalulu's bamboo orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like stems and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces striking, colorful flowers that are often arranged in dense spikes, making it a visually appealing species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
