Prunus × miyoshii

Prunus × miyoshii, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or mountain cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Prunus serrula and Prunus lannesiana. The plant is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn vibrant shades of red or orange in the fall.

Prunus × miyoshii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
