Phylica pedicellata

Phylica pedicellata, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically bright yellow or orange in color. The plant produces small, round, red or orange berries that are edible for birds and some small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Phylica pedicellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
