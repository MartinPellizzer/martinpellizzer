Phyteuma spicatum subsp. pyrenaicum

Phyteuma spicatum subsp.

pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of bell-shaped, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing adaptability to harsh mountain environments. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, aiding in moisture retention.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in alpine ecosystems.
