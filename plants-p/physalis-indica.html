Physalis indica

Physalis indica, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange berries that are encased in a papery, inflated calyx. The plant produces small, alternate leaves that are oval-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its high vitamin C content.

The fruit is widely used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
