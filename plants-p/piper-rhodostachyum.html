Piper rhodostachyum

Piper rhodostachyum, commonly known as the red-stemmed pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that develop into small, round berries.

The berries are typically harvested when they turn red, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
