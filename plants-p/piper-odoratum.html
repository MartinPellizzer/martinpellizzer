Piper odoratum

Piper odoratum, commonly known as the sweet pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or greenish flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are aromatic and can be used to make teas or as a spice.

Piper odoratum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
