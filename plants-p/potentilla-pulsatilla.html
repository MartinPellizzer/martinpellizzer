Potentilla pulsatilla

Potentilla pulsatilla, commonly known as the meadow cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. It has compound leaves with five leaflets and produces small, five-petaled flowers.

Potentilla pulsatilla is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to stabilize soil and support pollinator populations.
