Parathesis reticulata var. sinuata

Parathesis reticulata var. sinuata, commonly known as the sinuous nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, serrated leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are often overlooked due to their modest size. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
