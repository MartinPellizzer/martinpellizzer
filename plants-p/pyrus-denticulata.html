Pyrus denticulata

Pyrus denticulata, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves with finely serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, round, yellow fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Pyrus denticulata is also valued for its role in ecological systems, providing food for various wildlife species.
