Picea nigra var. rubra

Picea nigra var. rubra, commonly known as the red spruce, is a variant of the black spruce native to North America.

It is characterized by its dark red bark and needle-like leaves that are typically shorter and more sharply pointed than those of the standard black spruce. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in mountainous regions and boreal forests.

This variety is valued for its ecological role and historical use in timber and paper production.
