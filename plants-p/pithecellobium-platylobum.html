Pithecellobium platylobum

Pithecellobium platylobum, commonly known as the flat-lobed pithecellobium, is a species of leguminous plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with elliptical leaflets and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible pods containing round, sweet fruits similar to beans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agroforestry systems.
