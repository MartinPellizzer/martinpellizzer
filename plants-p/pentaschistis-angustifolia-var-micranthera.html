Pentaschistis angustifolia var. micranthera

Pentaschistis angustifolia var. micranthera, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pentalogy, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or mountainous regions. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
