Phragmipedium popowii

Phragmipedium popowii, commonly known as the Popowii lady's slipper, is a rare and exotic orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple and green petals, which resemble a lady's slipper in shape. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a unique fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
