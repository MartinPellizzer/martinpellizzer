Plantago gonophylla

Plantago gonophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a species of flowering plant in the family Plantaginaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.

The plant is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and tolerate various environmental conditions.
