Piper andersonii

Piper andersonii, commonly known as Anderson's pepper, is a rare and endangered species native to the highlands of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes over 2,000 species of flowering plants, many of which are used for spices or medicinal purposes. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny greenish-white flowers. It produces small, peppery berries that are used in traditional medicine by local communities.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
