Pectis leptocephala

Pectis leptocephala, commonly known as the narrow-headed pectis, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish to orange flowers arranged in a flat, disc-like structure. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, adapting to the harsh, dry environment.

Pectis leptocephala is typically found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, thriving in low-nutrient conditions.
