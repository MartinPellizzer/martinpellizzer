Pedilonum rhabdoglossum

Pedilonum rhabdoglossum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun exposure. It produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Pedilonum rhabdoglossum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in dry, coastal environments.
