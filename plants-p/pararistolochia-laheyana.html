Pararistolochia laheyana

Pararistolochia laheyana, commonly known as the Lahey's Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are often green or brown in color. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy pulp. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
