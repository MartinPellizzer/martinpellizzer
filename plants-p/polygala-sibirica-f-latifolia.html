Polygala sibirica f. latifolia

Polygala sibirica f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved Siberian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that can grow up to 60 cm in height, adorned with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are typically bisexual, with a unique structure that includes a spurred lower sepal.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
