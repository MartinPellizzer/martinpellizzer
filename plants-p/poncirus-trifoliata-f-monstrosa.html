Poncirus trifoliata f. monstrosa

Poncirus trifoliata f. monstrosa, commonly known as the twisted trifoliate orange, is a unique horticultural form of the trifoliate orange.

It is characterized by its distinctive, twisted growth patterns and thick, rugged bark. The plant exhibits a high degree of variability in its foliage and branching, making it a striking ornamental specimen. It is typically cultivated for its aesthetic appeal rather than its fruiting capabilities.

This form is often used in bonsai and as a decorative plant in gardens.
