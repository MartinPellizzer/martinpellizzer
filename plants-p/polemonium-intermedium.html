Polemonium intermedium

Polemonium intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate Jacob's ladder, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Polemonium intermedium is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
