Plectranthus igniarius subsp. grandicalyx

Plectranthus igniarius subsp.

grandicalyx, commonly known as the flame violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking violet to red flowers that bloom in dense clusters along the stems.

The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and compact growth habit.
