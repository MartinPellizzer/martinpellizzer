Phyllocephalum microcephalum

Phyllocephalum microcephalum, commonly known as the small-headed phyllocephalum, is a lesser-known member of the Phyllocephalum genus.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth pattern and small, fleshy leaves that are typically covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions, where it has adapted to thrive in low-nutrient soils. Its foliage often exhibits a range of colors, from deep green to reddish tones, depending on environmental conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturalists alike.
