Poa macroanthera

Poa macroanthera, commonly known as the giant meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish-brown florets, which are arranged in a loose, branching pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Poa macroanthera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
