Phoebe megacalyx

Phoebe megacalyx, commonly known as the Florida beech, is a small evergreen tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features smooth, light gray bark and elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies of southeastern flora.
