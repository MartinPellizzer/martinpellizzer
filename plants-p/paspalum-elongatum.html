Paspalum elongatum

Paspalum elongatum, commonly known as slender grass or tall grass, is a perennial grass species native to various parts of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically smooth and hairless. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open fields, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Paspalum elongatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
