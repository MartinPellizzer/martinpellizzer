Pigea travancorica

Pigea travancorica, commonly known as the Travancore pigeonwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are alternate, simple, and glossy, with a distinctive aromatic scent. The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine and crafts.
