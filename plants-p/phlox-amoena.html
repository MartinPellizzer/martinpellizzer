Phlox amoena

Phlox amoena, commonly known as the common phlox, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Polemoniaceae and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Phlox amoena is known for its early spring blooming period, adding color to gardens before many other flowers emerge.
