Philotheca salsolifolia subsp. salsolifolia

Philotheca salsolifolia subsp.

salsolifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf philotheca, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
