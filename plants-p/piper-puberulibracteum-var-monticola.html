Piper puberulibracteum var. monticola

Piper puberulibracteum var. monticola, commonly known as the mountain pepper plant, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine, short hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in forested areas at elevations above 1,500 meters.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
