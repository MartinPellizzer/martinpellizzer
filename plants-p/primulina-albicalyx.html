Primulina albicalyx

Primulina albicalyx, commonly known as the white-lipped primula, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its striking white petals with a subtle pinkish hue. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, while its flowers bloom in clusters during the spring.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
