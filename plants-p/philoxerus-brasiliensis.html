Philoxerus brasiliensis

Philoxerus brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian wax palm, is a striking plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a monocot, characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 6 meters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark blue, waxy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
