Plantago macrostachys var. stuckertii

Plantago macrostachys var. stuckertii, commonly known as the Stuckert's plantain, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
