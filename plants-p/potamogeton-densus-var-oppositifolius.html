Potamogeton densus var. oppositifolius

Potamogeton densus var. oppositifolius, commonly known as the dense pondweed with opposite leaves, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It features long, slender stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like structures, which emerge from the water surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in some water bodies if not managed properly.
