Polygonum plebeium var. indicum

Polygonum plebeium var. indicum, commonly known as Indian knotweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands and stream banks. The plant has slender, often reddish stems that are covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and usually have a slightly hairy surface.

It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are often overlooked due to their inconspicuous appearance.
