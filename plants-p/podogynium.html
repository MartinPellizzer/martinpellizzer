Podogynium

Podogynium, commonly known as the water velvet, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its distinctive morphology.

It features a long, flexible stem that floats on the water surface, with small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety layer. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically submerged, which are pollinated by water currents rather than insects. Podogynium thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water clarity through its growth patterns.
