Piper bryogetum

Piper bryogetum, commonly known as the moss pepper, is a unique member of the Piperaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, vine-like structure and its ability to grow in shaded, moist environments. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with mosses, which contribute to its growth and survival. Its leaves are usually small and alternate, with a distinct aromatic quality.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains the seeds, making it an interesting subject for botanical study.
