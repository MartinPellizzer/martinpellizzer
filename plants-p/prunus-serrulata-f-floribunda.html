Prunus serrulata f. floribunda

Prunus serrulata f. floribunda, commonly known as the weeping cherry, is a cultivar of the Japanese cherry tree known for its distinctive cascading branches.

It features delicate pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a striking visual display. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, requiring regular watering during dry periods.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture and landscape design.
