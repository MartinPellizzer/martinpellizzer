Pelargonium cummingiae

Pelargonium cummingiae, commonly known as the Cumming's geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa, specifically the Western Cape region, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with narrow, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers. It is a perennial herb that produces vibrant blooms throughout the spring and summer seasons.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
