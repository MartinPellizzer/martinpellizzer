Plagioscyphus meridionalis

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as sporophytes, are borne on stalks and produce spores for propagation.

Plagioscyphus meridionalis plays a role in soil stabilization and provides microhabitat for various invertebrates.
