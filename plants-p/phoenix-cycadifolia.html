Phoenix cycadifolia

Phoenix cycadifolia, commonly known as the slender cycad, is a slow-growing evergreen plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, compound leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its seeds are enclosed in a fibrous, spiky pod and require specific conditions to germinate.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and protected areas.
