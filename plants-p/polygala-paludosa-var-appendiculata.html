Polygala paludosa var. appendiculata

Polygala paludosa var. appendiculata, commonly known as the marsh polygala, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant features slender, erect stems with small, clustered flowers that are usually pale green to yellowish in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is part of the Polygalaceae family and is noted for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
