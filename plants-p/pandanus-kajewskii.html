Pandanus kajewskii

Pandanus kajewskii, commonly known as the Kajewski's pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. The plant produces fragrant, spadix-like inflorescences, which are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove swamps or near the shoreline.

The roots of Pandanus kajewskii are also used in some local medicinal practices.
