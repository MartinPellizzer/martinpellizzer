Pelargonium acaule

Pelargonium acaule, commonly known as the stemless geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its lack of a distinct stem, with leaves directly emerging from the base of the plant. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its leaves are usually rounded or heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

Pelargonium acaule is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.
