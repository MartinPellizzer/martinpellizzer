Phyllanthus brasiliensis subsp. glaber

Phyllanthus brasiliensis subsp.

glaber, commonly known as Brazilian star gooseberry, is a shrub native to South America. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that turn red when mature.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
