Pimenta officinalis var. longifolia

Pimenta officinalis var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
