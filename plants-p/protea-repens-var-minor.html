Protea repens var. minor

Protea repens var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf sugarbush, is a South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, dense clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in shrubland and coastal areas, preferring well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, and it is often found in the Western Cape region of South Africa.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
