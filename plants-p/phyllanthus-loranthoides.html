Phyllanthus loranthoides

Phyllanthus loranthoides, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a shrub belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, and the plant often grows in moist, shaded environments.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
