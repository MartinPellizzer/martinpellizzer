Panmorphia tigridens

Panmorphia tigridens, commonly known as the tiger orchid, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that often display a mix of red, yellow, and orange hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in the dry seasons.

Panmorphia tigridens is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
