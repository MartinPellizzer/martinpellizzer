Psidium calyptranthoides

Psidium calyptranthoides, commonly known as the Mexican cherry or the calyptranthus, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to cherries, though they are typically less sweet. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy texture.
