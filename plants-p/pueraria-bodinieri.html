Pueraria bodinieri

Pueraria bodinieri, commonly known as the giant cowpea, is a vigorous climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, purple-pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its rapid growth and can reach lengths of several meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed areas or along forest edges.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability.
