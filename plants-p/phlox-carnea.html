Phlox carnea

Phlox carnea, commonly known as the annual phlox, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Phlox carnea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant color and ease of cultivation.
