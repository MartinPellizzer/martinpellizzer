Polypodium olivaceum

Polypodium olivaceum, commonly known as the olive fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its leathery, dark green fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fronds are divided into lance-shaped segments, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.
