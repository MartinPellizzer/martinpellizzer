Pittosporum fragrans

Pittosporum fragrans, commonly known as the sweet pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Pittosporum fragrans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping and horticulture.
