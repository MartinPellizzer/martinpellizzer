Phaseolus vulgaris var. helvolus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. helvolus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It features vibrant red or orange flowers and elongated, edible pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces tendrils for climbing support. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its nutritious seeds.

This variety is valued in both culinary and horticultural contexts.
