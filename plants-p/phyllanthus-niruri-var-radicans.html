Phyllanthus niruri var. radicans

Phyllanthus niruri var. radicans, commonly known as stonebreaker or chebuli, is a trailing herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It features small, opposite leaves that are typically elliptic to ovate in shape, with a slightly pubescent surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elliptical, woody fruits containing a single seed. It is often found growing in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
