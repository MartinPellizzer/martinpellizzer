Panax zippelianus

Panax zippelianus, commonly known as the American ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the forests of North America.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, yellowish roots that are highly valued in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with compound leaves and small greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its root system is complex, with multiple branches that contribute to its medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in rich, loamy soils.
