Pereskia aculeata var. rubescens

Pereskia aculeata var. rubescens, commonly known as the red-leaved cactus or red pereskia, is a succulent plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are often covered in spines, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The leaves are typically oval-shaped and may turn a striking reddish color, especially when exposed to full sun. This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological landscaping.
