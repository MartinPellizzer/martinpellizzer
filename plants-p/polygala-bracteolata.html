Polygala bracteolata

Polygala bracteolata, commonly known as the bracteolate milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces delicate, yellowish-green flowers that are typically arranged in racemes, which are often mistaken for leaves due to their leaf-like bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Polygala bracteolata is of interest in botany for its unique floral structure and ecological role in its native habitat.
