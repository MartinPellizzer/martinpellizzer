Passiflora biglandulosa

Passiflora biglandulosa, commonly known as the large-fruited passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor, often consumed fresh or used in jams. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and prominent veins.

Passiflora biglandulosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential calming properties.
