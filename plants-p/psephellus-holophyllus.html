Psephellus holophyllus

Psephellus holophyllus, commonly known as the golden sandwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or arid environments. Its leaves are typically succulent, adapted to store water in dry conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
