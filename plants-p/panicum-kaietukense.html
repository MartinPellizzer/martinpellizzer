Panicum kaietukense

Panicum kaietukense, commonly known as the Kaietukense grass, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of many grass species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and savannas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
