Pinillosia bellioides

Pinillosia bellioides, commonly known as the bell-like pine.

It is a species of conifer native to the western United States, particularly in California and Oregon. The plant is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and cone-like structures that resemble small bells. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Pinaceae, which includes other well-known pines and firs.
