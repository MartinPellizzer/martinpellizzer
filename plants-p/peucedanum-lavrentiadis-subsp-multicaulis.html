Peucedanum lavrentiadis subsp. multicaulis

Peucedanum lavrentiadis subsp.

multicaulis, commonly known as the mountain carrot, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in umbels.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It is an important component of alpine flora and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
