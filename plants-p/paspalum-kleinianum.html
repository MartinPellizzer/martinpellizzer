Paspalum kleinianum

Paspalum kleinianum, commonly known as Klein's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Paspalum kleinianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
