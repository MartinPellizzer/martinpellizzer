Polypodium pectinatum var. wagneri

Polypodium pectinatum var. wagneri, commonly known as Wagner's shield fern, is a tropical fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that are typically divided into shield-shaped segments. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth. Its rhizomes are creeping and can form dense colonies, contributing to its ornamental value.

The fronds are evergreen, providing year-round visual interest in both garden and indoor settings.
