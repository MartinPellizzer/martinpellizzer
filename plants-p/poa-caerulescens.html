Poa caerulescens

Poa caerulescens, commonly known as blue grass, is a species of grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges.

Poa caerulescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its fine texture and color.
