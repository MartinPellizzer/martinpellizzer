Paraphlomis albiflora var. albiflora

Paraphlomis albiflora var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered parathy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
