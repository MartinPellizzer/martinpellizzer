Phyllanthus occidentalis

Phyllanthus occidentalis, commonly known as the West Indian liverwort, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, and it is often found in disturbed soils or rocky areas. It has a short life cycle and is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various health conditions.
