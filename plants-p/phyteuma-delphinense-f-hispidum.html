Phyteuma delphinense f. hispidum

Phyteuma delphinense f. hispidum, commonly known as the hairy delphinium phyteuma, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, blue to purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant features hairy foliage, which gives it its specific epithet "hispidum," and it thrives in well-drained, sunny soils. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
