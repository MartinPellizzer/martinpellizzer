Pavonia subrotunda

Pavonia subrotunda, commonly known as the broadleaf wood rose, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are broad, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in partial to full sun and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This plant is valued in gardens for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
