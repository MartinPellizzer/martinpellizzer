Prunus polymorpha

Prunus polymorpha, commonly known as the variable cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its diverse growth forms, ranging from tree-like to shrub-like, which contribute to its common name. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, tart berries. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges, and turn vibrant colors in autumn.

Prunus polymorpha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
