Pholiurus filiformis var. gracilis

Pholiurus filiformis var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender thread-leaf plant, is a rare and delicate species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, thread-like leaves that give it a unique, almost feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white in color and bloom during the wet season.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
