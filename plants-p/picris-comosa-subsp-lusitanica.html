Picris comosa subsp. lusitanica

Picris comosa subsp.

lusitanica, commonly known as the Lusitanian goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces yellow ray flowers surrounded by numerous disc flowers, which are adapted for insect pollination.

The subspecies is distinguished by its shorter stature and more compact growth compared to other forms of Picris comosa.
