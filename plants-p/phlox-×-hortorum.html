Phlox × hortorum

Phlox × hortorum, commonly known as garden phlox, is a hybrid perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Polemoniaceae and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and beds.

Its vibrant colors, ranging from pink to purple, add ornamental value to landscapes.
