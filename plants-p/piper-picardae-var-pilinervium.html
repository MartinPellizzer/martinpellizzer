Piper picardae var. pilinervium

Piper picardae var. pilinervium, commonly known as the slender pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a slightly pubescent surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elliptical, dark purple to black berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported antipyretic and analgesic properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some commercial use in the spice trade.
