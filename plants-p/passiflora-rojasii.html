Passiflora rojasii

Passiflora rojasii, commonly known as the Ecuadorian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible and contain seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Passiflora rojasii is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in its native habitat.
