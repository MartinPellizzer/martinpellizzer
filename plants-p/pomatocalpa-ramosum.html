Pomatocalpa ramosum

Pomatocalpa ramosum, commonly known as the branched orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, producing intricate, often white or pale pink blossoms. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing moisture from the humid forest floor.

Pomatocalpa ramosum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
