Phlomoides knorringiana

Phlomoides knorringiana, commonly known as Knorring's false flax, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem, and it produces small, dry fruits known as nutlets.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, Mediterranean-type climates.
