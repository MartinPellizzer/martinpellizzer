Pithecellobium chrysocarpum

Pithecellobium chrysocarpum, commonly known as the golden fruit tree, is a species of legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes. The plant's fruit is a pod containing edible seeds, which are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, nutty flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
