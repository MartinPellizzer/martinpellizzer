Paeonia × suffruticosa subsp. atava

Paeonia × suffruticosa subsp.

atava, commonly known as the Atava peony, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white.

The plant has palmately divided, compound leaves that are typically dark green and velvety in texture. It blooms in late spring to early summer, and its roots are fibrous and well-developed, making it relatively hardy in temperate climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
