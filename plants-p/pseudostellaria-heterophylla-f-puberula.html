Pseudostellaria heterophylla f. puberula

Pseudostellaria heterophylla f. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent starwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial characterized by its low-growing habit and hairy, pubescent foliage. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and covered with fine, soft hairs. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodland floors and stream banks.
