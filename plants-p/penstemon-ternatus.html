Penstemon ternatus

Penstemon ternatus, commonly known as the three-lobed beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It is a perennial species that blooms in late spring to early summer.

Penstemon ternatus is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
