Pavia × hybrida

Pavia × hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid pavia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant is a deciduous shrub with simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, Pavia × hybrida is often used in landscaping and as a specimen plant in gardens.
