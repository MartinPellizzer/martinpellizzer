Pelargonium pallens

Pelargonium pallens, commonly known as the white geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive white or pale pink flowers and rounded, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in a compact shrub form, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Pelargonium pallens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a container plant.
