Parodia roseolutea

Parodia roseolutea, commonly known as the rose-flowered cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white to pale green spines. The plant produces showy, pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in arid gardens.
