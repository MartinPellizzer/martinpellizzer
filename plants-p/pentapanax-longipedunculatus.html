Pentapanax longipedunculatus

Pentapanax longipedunculatus, commonly known as the long-stemmed pentapanax, is a member of the Araliaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with opposite arrangement and elongated peduncles. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbellate clusters.
