Polygonum sagittatum f. pilosum

Polygonum sagittatum f. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy arrowhead, is a variant of the arrowhead plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or marshy environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture on both surfaces. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
