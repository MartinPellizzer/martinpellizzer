Phanera glabrifolia subsp. sericea

Phanera glabrifolia subsp.

sericea, commonly known as the silky phanera, is a species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia. It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its smooth, often glabrous bark and lanceolate leaves with a waxy texture.

The flowers are typically small and clustered, producing a distinctive fragrance. The subspecies sericea is distinguished by its silky, hairy appearance, particularly on the young shoots and leaves.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
