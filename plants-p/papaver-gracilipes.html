Papaver gracilipes

Papaver gracilipes, commonly known as the slender poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its capsule fruit contains numerous small, dark seeds.

Papaver gracilipes is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
