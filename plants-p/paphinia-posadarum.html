Paphinia posadarum

Paphinia posadarum, commonly known as the red-tipped paphiopedilum, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety petals and a distinctive red tip on its lip, which sets it apart from other orchid species. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, often found in shaded, humid environments with rich, well-drained soil. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, are highly prized by orchid enthusiasts for their beauty and rarity.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Paphinia posadarum is considered a conservation concern.
