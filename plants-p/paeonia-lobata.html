Paeonia lobata

Paeonia lobata, commonly known as the Chinese peony, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive double bloom. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in a dense layer of brownish-red hairs. Paeonia lobata is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for centuries in traditional Chinese horticulture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
