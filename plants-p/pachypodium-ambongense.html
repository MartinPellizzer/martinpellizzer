Pachypodium ambongense

Pachypodium ambongense, commonly known as the Madagascar bottle tree, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, cylindrical trunk that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant has small, spiny leaves and produces yellow flowers during the dry season. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
