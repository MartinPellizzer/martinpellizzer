Phaseolus coccineus var. zongolicensis

Phaseolus coccineus var. zongolicensis, commonly known as the Zongolica runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Andean region of South America.

It produces vibrant red or orange flowers and edible, elongated pods that are typically harvested when young and tender. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and robust, hairy stems that support its climbing growth habit. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is adapted to the high-altitude environments of its native habitat.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in traditional Andean cuisine.
