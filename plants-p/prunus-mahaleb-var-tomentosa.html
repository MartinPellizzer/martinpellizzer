Prunus mahaleb var. tomentosa

Prunus mahaleb var. tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy cherry or tomentose cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly grayish-white pubescence on both the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible drupes that are typically pink to reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is often valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
