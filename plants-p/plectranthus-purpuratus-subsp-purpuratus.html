Plectranthus purpuratus subsp. purpuratus

Plectranthus purpuratus subsp.

purpuratus, commonly known as the purple-leaf plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs.

The plant is characterized by its striking violet to purple leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range from white to pale purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and ability to attract pollinators.
