Prunus × yedoensis f. surugazakura

Prunus × yedoensis f. surugazakura, commonly known as the Suruga cherry, is a cultivar of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its dense, double blooms of pale pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The Suruga cherry is particularly noted for its compact form and abundant floral display.
