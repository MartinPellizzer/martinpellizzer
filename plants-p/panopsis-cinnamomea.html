Panopsis cinnamomea

Panopsis cinnamomea, commonly known as the cinnamon tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic bark, which resembles the scent of cinnamon, hence its common name. The plant is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. It produces woody capsules containing numerous flat, winged seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its aromatic properties in traditional medicine and perfumery.
