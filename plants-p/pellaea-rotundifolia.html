Pellaea rotundifolia

Pellaea rotundifolia, commonly known as the rounded fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and its distinctive, rounded, leathery leaves. The fronds are typically dark green and have a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
