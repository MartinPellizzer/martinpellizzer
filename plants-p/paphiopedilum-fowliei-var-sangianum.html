Paphiopedilum fowliei var. sangianum

Paphiopedilum fowliei var. sangianum, commonly known as the Sangian Paphiopedilum, is a rare and striking orchid native to parts of Indonesia.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like flowers that are typically yellow with deep red or purple markings. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, often with a waxy texture.

This variety is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and limited natural distribution.
