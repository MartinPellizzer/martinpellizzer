Patagonium retusum var. grisebachii

Patagonium retusum var. grisebachii, commonly known as the gray-leaved Patagonia thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features silver-gray, woolly leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It produces small yellow flowers arranged in a rounded cluster, typical of the Asteraceae family.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage.
