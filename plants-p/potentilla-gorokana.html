Potentilla gorokana

Potentilla gorokana, commonly known as the golden potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the flowering season. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Potentilla gorokana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
