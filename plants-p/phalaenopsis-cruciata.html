Phalaenopsis cruciata

Phalaenopsis cruciata, commonly known as the crucifix orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, cross-shaped flowers that resemble a crucifix, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a contrasting purple or brown lip.

Phalaenopsis cruciata is prized for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
