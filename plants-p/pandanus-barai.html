Pandanus barai

Pandanus barai, commonly known as the giant pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces large, fragrant inflorescences that are often used in traditional crafts and ceremonies. Its leaves are fibrous and have been traditionally used for making thatch and rope.

The species is adapted to coastal and wetland environments, thriving in humid, saline conditions.
