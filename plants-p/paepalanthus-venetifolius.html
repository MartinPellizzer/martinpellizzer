Paepalanthus venetifolius

Paepalanthus venetifolius, commonly known as the Venetian leaf cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming cactus characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant has spiny areoles and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
