Pandanus lamprocephalus

Pandanus lamprocephalus, commonly known as the silver-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, feather-like leaves that are often covered in a silvery waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish flowers that develop into spiky, cone-like fruits. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found growing near mangroves or in sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional crafts.
