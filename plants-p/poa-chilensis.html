Poa chilensis

Poa chilensis, commonly known as Chilean bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and blue-green foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of compact, spikelets, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Poa chilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and restoration projects.
