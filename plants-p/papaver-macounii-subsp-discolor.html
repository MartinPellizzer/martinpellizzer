Papaver macounii subsp. discolor

Papaver macounii subsp.

discolor, commonly known as the Macoun's poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its striking, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a velvety texture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
