Piper glabrescens var. venezuelense

Piper glabrescens var. venezuelense, commonly known as the Venezuelan pepper plant, is a species native to South America, particularly Venezuela.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper plants. The plant typically grows as a shrub with glossy, elliptical leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its small, round berries are initially green and turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds.

The leaves and fruits are valued for their medicinal properties and are used in local remedies for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
