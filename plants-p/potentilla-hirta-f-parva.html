Potentilla hirta f. parva

Potentilla hirta f. parva, commonly known as the dwarf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in groups of five, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.". The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous or tundra environments.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
