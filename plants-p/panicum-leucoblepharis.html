Panicum leucoblepharis

Panicum leucoblepharis, commonly known as the white-eyed panicgrass, is a species of grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its distinctive white or pale blue flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

The inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters that are often tinged with white or pale blue, giving it its common name.
