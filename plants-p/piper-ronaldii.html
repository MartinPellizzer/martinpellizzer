Piper ronaldii

Piper ronaldii, commonly known as the Ronalii pepper or Ronalii pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species.

It is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a bright red color, resembling those of the common chili pepper. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge.

While not widely cultivated for its fruit, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
