Phyllostachys sulphurea f. laqueata

Phyllostachys sulphurea f. laqueata, commonly known as the golden bamboo or yellow bamboo, is a variant of the golden bamboo species.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to golden-green foliage, which becomes more vibrant in direct sunlight. The plant is a clump-forming bamboo that grows in dense, upright stalks, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. It is native to China and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This ornamental bamboo is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ease of maintenance.
