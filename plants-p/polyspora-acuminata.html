Polyspora acuminata

Polyspora acuminata, commonly known as the spiny bladdergrass, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant is adapted to dry environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically wind-pollinated, and the plant produces small, three-angled fruits that disperse its seeds effectively.

Polyspora acuminata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is a common component of dryland vegetation.
