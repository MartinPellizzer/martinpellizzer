Patagonium longipes

Patagonium longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed Patagonian grass, is a perennial herb native to the Patagonian region of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Patagonium longipes plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
