Pelargonium × compson

Pelargonium × compson, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a variety of ornamental traits.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, slightly lobed leaves that often have a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its attractive foliage and continuous blooming, it is widely cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and containers.
