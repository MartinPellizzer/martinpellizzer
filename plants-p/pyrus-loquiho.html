Pyrus loquiho

Pyrus loquiho, commonly known as the whispering pear, is a deciduous tree native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The fruit, a small pear-like apple, ripens in late summer and is edible, though it has a mild, slightly tart flavor. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full to partial sunlight for optimal growth.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
