Platypodium elegans var. glabrescens

Platypodium elegans var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-leafed beautyberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright purple berries in the fall. These berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
