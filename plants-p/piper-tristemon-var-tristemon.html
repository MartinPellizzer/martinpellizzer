Piper tristemon var. tristemon

Piper tristemon var. tristemon, commonly known as the creeping piper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often pubescent. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, and its fruits are berries that contain a single seed.

This plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
