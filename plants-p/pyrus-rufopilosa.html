Pyrus rufopilosa

Pyrus rufopilosa, commonly known as the red-haired pear, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its rugged, shrub-like growth habit and its distinctive reddish-brown bark. Its small, clustered flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible pears that are not widely cultivated.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species in the wild.
