Psoralea epipsila

Psoralea epipsila, commonly known as the narrow-leafed psoralea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, which are typically borne on the upper parts of the stems. Psoralea epipsila is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing features such as small leaves and deep root systems to conserve water.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insects.
