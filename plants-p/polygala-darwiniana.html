Polygala darwiniana

Polygala darwiniana, commonly known as Darwin's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with a distinctive spurred structure.

It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, displaying adaptations to arid and nutrient-poor environments.
