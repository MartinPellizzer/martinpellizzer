Philodendron killipii

Philodendron killipii, commonly known as the Killipii philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy green leaves that are often variegated with white or cream patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among houseplant enthusiasts and collectors.
