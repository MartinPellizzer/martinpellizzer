Physalis hederifolia var. palmeri

Physalis hederifolia var. palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It features hairy, sprawling stems and heart-shaped leaves with toothed margins. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that develop into inflated, lantern-like fruits encasing orange-yellow berries. Its botanical characteristics include glandular hairs and a preference for well-drained soils.

It is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in full sun.
