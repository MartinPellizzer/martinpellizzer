Polyalthia canangioides var. angustifolia

Polyalthia canangioides var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf silk-cotton tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The tree produces fluffy, white cotton-like fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fibrous covering. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local cultures.
