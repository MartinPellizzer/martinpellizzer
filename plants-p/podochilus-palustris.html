Podochilus palustris

Podochilus palustris, commonly known as the marsh hibiscus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in Southeast Asia.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central column. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to waterlogged soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Podochilus palustris plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
