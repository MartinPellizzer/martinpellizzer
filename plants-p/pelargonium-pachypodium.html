Pelargonium pachypodium

Pelargonium pachypodium, commonly known as the angel's tears, is a unique succulent native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and spiny leaves, which help it store water and reduce transpiration in arid environments. The plant produces striking, lantern-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, adding visual interest to its appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its striking appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
