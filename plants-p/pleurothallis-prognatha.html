Pleurothallis prognatha

Pleurothallis prognatha, commonly known as the long-spurred pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often epiphytic on trees or rocks. Its unique floral structure includes elongated spurs that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Pleurothallis prognatha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
