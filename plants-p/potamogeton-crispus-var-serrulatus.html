Potamogeton crispus var. serrulatus

Potamogeton crispus var. serrulatus, commonly known as the serrated curly pondweed, is a freshwater aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are curled and have serrated edges. The plant typically grows in shallow water, often forming dense mats on the surface or submerged in the sediment. It produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
