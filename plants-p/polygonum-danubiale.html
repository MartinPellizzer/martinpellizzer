Polygonum danubiale

Polygonum danubiale, commonly known as the Danube knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and arrow-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as riverbanks and wet meadows. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red, appearing in late summer.

Polygonum danubiale is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils.
