Potentilla glabriuscula

Potentilla glabriuscula, commonly known as the smooth cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. Its leaves are palmately divided into five narrow, lobed segments, and it has a low-growing, mat-forming habit.

Potentilla glabriuscula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
