Plantago maxima

Plantago maxima, commonly known as the greater plantain, is a robust perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties and are also edible when cooked.

The plant is well adapted to grow in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
