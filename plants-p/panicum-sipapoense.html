Panicum sipapoense

Panicum sipapoense, commonly known as the Natal grass or Natal panicgrass, is a tall, perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often pink or red in color. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Additionally, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
