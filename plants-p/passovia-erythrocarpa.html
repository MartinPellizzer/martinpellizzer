Passovia erythrocarpa

Passovia erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited passovia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its most distinctive feature is its bright red, fleshy berries, which are edible and contain numerous seeds.

Passovia erythrocarpa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or semi-arid environments.
