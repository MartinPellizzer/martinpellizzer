Pentaschistis scandens

Pentaschistis scandens, commonly known as the climbing pentaschistis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its twining stems that allow it to climb and spread across vegetation. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
