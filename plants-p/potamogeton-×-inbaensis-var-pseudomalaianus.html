Potamogeton × inbaensis var. pseudomalaianus

Potamogeton × inbaensis var. pseudomalaianus, commonly known as the Malayan pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant characterized by its submerged foliage and elongated, ribbon-like leaves.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes, where it forms dense mats on the bottom. The plant exhibits a complex morphology, with leaves that are often arranged in whorls and possess a smooth, glossy surface. Its reproductive structures include small, inconspicuous flowers that are adapted for water pollination.

This variant is notable for its genetic diversity and ecological adaptability, making it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.
