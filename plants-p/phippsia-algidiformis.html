Phippsia algidiformis

Phippsia algidiformis, commonly known as the cold-forming phippsia, is a rare and delicate plant species native to high-altitude regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces tiny, white to pale green flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle beauty to its alpine habitat. Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is highly sensitive to environmental changes and is considered endangered in its native range.

Botanists study this species to better understand its adaptation strategies in cold climates.
