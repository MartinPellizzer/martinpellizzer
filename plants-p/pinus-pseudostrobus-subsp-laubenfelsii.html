Pinus pseudostrobus subsp. laubenfelsii

Pinus pseudostrobus subsp.

laubenfelsii, commonly known as the Laubenfels Douglas fir, is a subspecies native to southern Mexico. It is a medium-sized evergreen conifer characterized by its long, needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure.

The tree typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its cones are relatively small and release seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in botanical classification.
