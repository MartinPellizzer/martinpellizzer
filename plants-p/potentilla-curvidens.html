Potentilla curvidens

Potentilla curvidens, commonly known as the curvedleaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a distinctive waxy coating, and the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla curvidens is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual appeal.
