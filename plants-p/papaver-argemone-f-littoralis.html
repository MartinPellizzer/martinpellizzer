Papaver argemone f. littoralis

Papaver argemone f. littoralis, commonly known as the coastal poppy, is a variant of the Argemone poppy native to coastal regions.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves adapted to saline and windy environments. This plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils along shorelines, showcasing its resilience to harsh coastal conditions. Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
