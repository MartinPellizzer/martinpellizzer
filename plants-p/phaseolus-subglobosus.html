Phaseolus subglobosus

Phaseolus subglobosus, commonly known as the globular bean, is a climbing legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, spherical pods that contain small, edible seeds. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers, typical of the Phaseolus genus. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Due to its unique pod shape, it is sometimes grown as an ornamental plant as well.
