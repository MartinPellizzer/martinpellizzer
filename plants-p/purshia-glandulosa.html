Purshia glandulosa

Purshia glandulosa, commonly known as sticky yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which give it a sticky texture. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is part of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or yellow flowers.
