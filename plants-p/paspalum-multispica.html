Paspalum multispica

Paspalum multispica, commonly known as the slender paspalum, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often clustered at the tips of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture, and it is known for its ability to tolerate periodic flooding.

Paspalum multispica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
