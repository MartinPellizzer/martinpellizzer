Pandanus mussauensis

Pandanus mussauensis, commonly known as the Mussau pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical Pacific islands, particularly the Solomon Islands. The plant is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, which are followed by triangular, woody fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and cultural significance in the regions where it grows.
