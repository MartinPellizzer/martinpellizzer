Prasophyllum sylvicola

Prasophyllum sylvicola, commonly known as the silver leek, is a terrestrial orchid native to the wetlands of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in swampy or seasonally waterlogged soils, often in association with other wetland flora. It has a short, underground rhizome and relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
