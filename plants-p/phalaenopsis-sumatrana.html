Phalaenopsis sumatrana

Phalaenopsis sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to the Indonesian island of Sumatra.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its long, pendant flower spikes that bear showy, butterfly-like blossoms. The plant typically has broad, dark green leaves and produces fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in humid environments with indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Phalaenopsis sumatrana is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its elegant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
