Prunus fimbriisepala var. grandiflora

Prunus fimbriisepala var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered fringed almond, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fringed petals that give it a unique floral appearance. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, are often pale pink to white, and it produces small, edible fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for both horticultural and traditional medicinal purposes.
