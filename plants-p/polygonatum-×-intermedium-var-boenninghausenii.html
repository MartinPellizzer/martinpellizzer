Polygonatum × intermedium var. boenninghausenii

Polygonatum × intermedium var. boenninghausenii, commonly known as the yellow-flowered snake's head.

This variant is a hybrid within the genus Polygonatum, characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It is a perennial herb with rhizomatous growth, typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often visited by pollinators.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid species, showcasing genetic diversity within the Polygonatum group.
