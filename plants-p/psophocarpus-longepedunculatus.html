Psophocarpus longepedunculatus

Psophocarpus longepedunculatus, commonly known as the long-peduncled wing bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of South America.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets and produces showy, violet-blue flowers that are followed by elongated, winged pods. The plant is valued for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental or for its nutritional value.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various agricultural systems in tropical and subtropical areas.
