Podochilus callifer

Podochilus callifer, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Podochilus callifer thrives in warm, humid climates and requires careful attention to light and watering conditions.
