Paspalum gracielae

Paspalum gracielae, commonly known as the slender paspalum, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer.

Paspalum gracielae is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
