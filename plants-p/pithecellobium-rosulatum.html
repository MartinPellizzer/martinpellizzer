Pithecellobium rosulatum

Pithecellobium rosulatum, commonly known as the rose thorn acacia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or secondary habitats.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
