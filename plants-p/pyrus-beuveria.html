Pyrus beuveria

Pyrus beuveria, commonly known as the Beuveria pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, producing small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a slightly serrated edge.

The fruit, a small pear-like berry, is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor.
