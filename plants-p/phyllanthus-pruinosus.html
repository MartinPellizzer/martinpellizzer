Phyllanthus pruinosus

Phyllanthus pruinosus, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian cheesewood, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsules containing numerous seeds. It is native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands, and thrives in moist, shady environments.

Phyllanthus pruinosus is also noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
