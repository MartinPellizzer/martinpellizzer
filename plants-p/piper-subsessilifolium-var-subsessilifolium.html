Piper subsessilifolium var. subsessilifolium

Piper subsessilifolium var. subsessilifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pepper plant, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into clustered, round berries. These berries contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject of study in the Piperaceae family.
