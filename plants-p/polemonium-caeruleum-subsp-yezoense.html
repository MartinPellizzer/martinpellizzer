Polemonium caeruleum subsp. yezoense

Polemonium caeruleum subsp.

yezoense, commonly known as the Yezo bluebell, is a perennial herb native to northeastern Asia. It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
