Prostanthera odoratissima

Prostanthera odoratissima, commonly known as the sweet mint bush, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, mint-scented leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It typically grows in coastal areas and is often found in heathlands and woodlands.

Prostanthera odoratissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
