Primula calderiana var. alba

Primula calderiana var. alba, commonly known as the white calderian primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine pubescence, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
