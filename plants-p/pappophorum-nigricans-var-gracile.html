Pappophorum nigricans var. gracile

Pappophorum nigricans var. gracile, commonly known as the slender bristlegrass, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces distinctive bristly, hair-like structures called pappi, which aid in wind dispersal of its seeds. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and grasslands.

This variant is often distinguished by its more delicate appearance compared to the typical form of Pappophorum nigricans.
