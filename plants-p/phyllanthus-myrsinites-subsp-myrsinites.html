Phyllanthus myrsinites subsp. myrsinites

Phyllanthus myrsinites subsp.

myrsinites, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent. It is characterized by its smooth, thornless stems and alternate, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. These berries are rich in vitamin C and are widely used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
