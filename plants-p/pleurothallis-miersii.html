Pleurothallis miersii

Pleurothallis miersii, commonly known as the Miers' pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often with intricate patterns on the petals. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
