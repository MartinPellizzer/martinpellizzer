Potamogeton pallidior

Potamogeton pallidior, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly pointed, with a smooth surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, usually found near the water's surface. It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and streams.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and stable aquatic ecosystems.
