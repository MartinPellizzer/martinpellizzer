Persicaria senegalensis f. senegalensis

Persicaria senegalensis f. senegalensis, commonly known as the Senegalese knotweed, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features erect, slender stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various aquatic and semi-aquatic environments.
