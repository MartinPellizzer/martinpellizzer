Pilosella debilis

Pilosella debilis, commonly known as the slender pilosella, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant has basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Its flowers are arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family, and are often visited by pollinators like bees and flies.
