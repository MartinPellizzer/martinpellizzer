Pinus robusta var. inflexa

Pinus robusta var. inflexa, commonly known as the dwarf mountain pine, is a variant of the robust pine that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with a dense, compact growth habit. The needles are short, stiff, and arranged in clusters of five, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its cones are small and often remain attached to the branches for an extended period.

This variety is well adapted to harsh, alpine environments, showcasing resilience to cold and poor soil conditions.
