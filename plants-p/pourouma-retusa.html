Pourouma retusa

Pourouma retusa, commonly known as the red bamboo palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a tall, slender palm with a straight trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are consumed by various wildlife. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its rapid growth and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
