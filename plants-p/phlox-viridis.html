Phlox viridis

Phlox viridis, commonly known as green phlox, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright green foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats.

Phlox viridis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
