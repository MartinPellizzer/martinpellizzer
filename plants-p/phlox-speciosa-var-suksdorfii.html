Phlox speciosa var. suksdorfii

Phlox speciosa var. suksdorfii, commonly known as the Suksdorf's garden phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring narrow, dark green leaves arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
