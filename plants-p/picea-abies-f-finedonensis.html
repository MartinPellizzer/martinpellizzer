Picea abies f. finedonensis

Picea abies f. finedonensis, commonly known as the Finedon pine, is a rare and distinctive form of the Norway spruce.

It is characterized by its unique, flattened, needle-like leaves that form a dense, mat-like structure on the branches. This morphological feature is an adaptation to harsh environmental conditions, such as strong winds and poor soil. The plant is native to the Scottish Highlands and is considered a relict species with limited natural distribution.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
