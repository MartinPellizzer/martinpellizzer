Piper arieianum f. barbulaense

Piper arieianum f. barbulaense, commonly known as the Barbulaense pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper arieianum species native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its dark green, ovate leaves and small, clustered flowers that produce peppery fruits. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and peppery fruits, which are used in traditional cuisines and herbal remedies.

The botanical features of this plant make it a valuable species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
