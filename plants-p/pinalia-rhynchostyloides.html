Pinalia rhynchostyloides

Pinalia rhynchostyloides, commonly known as the narrow-styled pinalia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stem and a single, showy flower with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or along stream banks. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption from the soil.

The species is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism, which involves specific insect pollinators.
