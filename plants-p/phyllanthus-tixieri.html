Phyllanthus tixieri

Phyllanthus tixieri, commonly known as the African buttonweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, riverbanks, and agricultural fields. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, round seeds.

Phyllanthus tixieri is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional African medicine for various health purposes.
