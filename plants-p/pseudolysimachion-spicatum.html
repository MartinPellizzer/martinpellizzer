Pseudolysimachion spicatum

Pseudolysimachion spicatum, commonly known as the spikeflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in spikes. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Pseudolysimachion spicatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
