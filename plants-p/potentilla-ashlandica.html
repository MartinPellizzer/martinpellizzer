Potentilla ashlandica

Potentilla ashlandica, commonly known as Ashland cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla ashlandica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
