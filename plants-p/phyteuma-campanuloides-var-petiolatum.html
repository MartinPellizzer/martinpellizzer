Phyteuma campanuloides var. petiolatum

Phyteuma campanuloides var. petiolatum, commonly known as the petioled bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long flowering period and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
