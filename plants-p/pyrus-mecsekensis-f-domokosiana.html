Pyrus mecsekensis f. domokosiana

Pyrus mecsekensis f. domokosiana, commonly known as the Domokosian pear, is a rare and unique horticultural form of the Mecsek pear.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive fruit morphology, with small, round pears that have a smooth, thin skin. The plant is native to Hungary and is primarily found in the Mecsek Mountains region. It exhibits specific adaptations to its local climate, including resistance to certain diseases and a preference for well-drained soils.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
