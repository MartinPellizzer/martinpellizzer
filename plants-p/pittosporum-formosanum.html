Pittosporum formosanum

Pittosporum formosanum, commonly known as the Formosan pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible for birds but not typically for humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
