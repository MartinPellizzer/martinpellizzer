Phyllanthus gymnanthus

Phyllanthus gymnanthus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties. Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and ethnobotanical research.
