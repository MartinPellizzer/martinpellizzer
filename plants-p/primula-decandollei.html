Primula decandollei

Primula decandollei, commonly known as the Decandolle's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, brightly colored flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly found in its natural habitat.
