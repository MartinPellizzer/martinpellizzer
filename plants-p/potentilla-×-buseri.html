Potentilla × buseri

Potentilla × buseri, commonly known as the hybrid shrub, is a deciduous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, showy yellow flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, and the plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Potentilla × buseri is relatively low-maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun conditions.
