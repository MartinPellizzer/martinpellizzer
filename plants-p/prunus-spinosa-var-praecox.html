Prunus spinosa var. praecox

Prunus spinosa var. praecox, commonly known as early blackthorn, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark purple berries that ripen in early summer. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The berries are used in culinary and medicinal applications, though the plant is generally toxic in its raw form.
