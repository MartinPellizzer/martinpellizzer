Pertya pungens

Pertya pungens, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its hollow, hairless stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in spikes. It is known for its stinging hairs that release irritants when touched, causing a skin rash.

Pertya pungens is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is commonly used in traditional medicine.
