Piper barbulantherum

Piper barbulantherum, commonly known as the Bornean pepper vine, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are berries that turn red when mature, and the plant is often found growing along forest edges or in shaded understory areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
