Plocama brevifolia subsp. dyris

Plocama brevifolia subsp.

dyris, commonly known as the narrowleaf plocama, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 5 cm in length. Its small, white to pale pink flowers appear in clusters during the spring and early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
