Potentilla burdujae

Potentilla burdujae, commonly known as the Burduja cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, aggregate fruits.

Potentilla burdujae is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and limited distribution.
