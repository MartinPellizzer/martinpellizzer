Primula vaginata subsp. vaginata

Primula vaginata subsp.

vaginata, commonly known as the violet primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of violet, pink, or white, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
