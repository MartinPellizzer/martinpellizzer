Pelargonium fragrans

Pelargonium fragrans, commonly known as the sweet geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with lobed, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
