Potamogeton pectinatus var. mongolicus

Potamogeton pectinatus var. mongolicus, commonly known as Mongolian pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, mucilaginous coating. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically arranged in dense spikes, and it blooms during the summer months. It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes, rivers, and marshes.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and stable aquatic ecosystems.
