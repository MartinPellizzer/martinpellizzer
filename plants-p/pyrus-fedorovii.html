Pyrus fedorovii

Pyrus fedorovii, commonly known as the Fedorov's pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, with heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like berry, is edible but not widely cultivated for commercial use.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude environments.
