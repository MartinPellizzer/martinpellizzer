Pinanga capitata

Pinanga capitata, commonly known as the clustered arrowroot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant features a short, rhizomatous stem with broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Pinanga capitata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
