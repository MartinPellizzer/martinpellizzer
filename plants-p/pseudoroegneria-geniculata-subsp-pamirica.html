Pseudoroegneria geniculata subsp. pamirica

Pseudoroegneria geniculata subsp.

pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the high altitudes of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often found on rocky slopes and meadows.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
