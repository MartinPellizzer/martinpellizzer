Podochilus luzonensis

Podochilus luzonensis, commonly known as the Luzon orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Philippines.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which are often fragrant. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is typically found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
