Papaver pilosum var. brachycarpum

Papaver pilosum var. brachycarpum, commonly known as the short-carpel poppy, is a variant of the silky poppy native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
