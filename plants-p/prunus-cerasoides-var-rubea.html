Prunus cerasoides var. rubea

Prunus cerasoides var. rubea, commonly known as the red-flowered cherry laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Due to its attractive foliage and flowering, it is often cultivated for landscaping purposes.
