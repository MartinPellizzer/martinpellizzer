Plantago major var. polystachya

Plantago major var. polystachya, commonly known as the common plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a rosette of broad, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in lawns, thriving in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its medicinal properties have been utilized historically for treating wounds and skin irritations.
