Paliurus ramosissimus var. japonicus

Paliurus ramosissimus var. japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese thornapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the spring. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.

The fruit, which is a small, round berry, is edible but typically not consumed by humans.
