Peschiera multiflora

Peschiera multiflora, commonly known as the multiflora peashrub, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in spring, which are followed by bean-like pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.

Peschiera multiflora is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
