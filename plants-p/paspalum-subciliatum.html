Paspalum subciliatum

Paspalum subciliatum, commonly known as the silky paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is often found in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats.

Paspalum subciliatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
