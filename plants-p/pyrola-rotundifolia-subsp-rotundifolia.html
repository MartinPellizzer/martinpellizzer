Pyrola rotundifolia subsp. rotundifolia

Pyrola rotundifolia subsp.

rotundifolia, commonly known as the roundleaf wintergreen, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of North America. It features small, leathery, oval-shaped leaves that remain green throughout the year.

The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in early spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in the study of temperate forest ecosystems.
