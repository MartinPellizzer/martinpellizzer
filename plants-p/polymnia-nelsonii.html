Polymnia nelsonii

Polymnia nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's polymnia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants commonly known as buckwheats. The plant is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy texture.

Polymnia nelsonii is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.
