Parapteroceras quisumbingii

Parapteroceras quisumbingii, commonly known as the Quezon orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful floral structures. The plant typically grows in forest understories, where it thrives in humid and shaded environments. Its botanical features include intricate floral morphology and specialized root systems adapted for nutrient absorption.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts to protect this unique species.
