Panicum nitidum var. pauciflorum

Panicum nitidum var. pauciflorum, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a variant of the panicgrass genus native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, delicate spikelets that are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open habitats such as fields and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
