Potentilla jepsonii var. kluanensis

Potentilla jepsonii var. kluanensis, commonly known as Kluane potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the Yukon and Alaska.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers in late summer. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in rocky, alpine environments and is adapted to cold, high-altitude conditions.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
