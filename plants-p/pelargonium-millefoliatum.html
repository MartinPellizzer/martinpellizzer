Pelargonium millefoliatum

Pelargonium millefoliatum, commonly known as the geranium, is a flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are divided into lobes resembling a hand, and its clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. Native to southern Africa, this perennial plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for various growing conditions.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
