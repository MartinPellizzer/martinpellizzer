Poinciana

Poinciana, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, feather-like leaves and showy, flame-colored flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant belongs to the Leguminosae family and produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Poinciana is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.
