Polystachya villosa

Polystachya villosa, commonly known as the hairy spike grass, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae).

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its hairy, wiry stems and small, clustered flowers that often have a greenish or yellowish hue. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Polystachya villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
