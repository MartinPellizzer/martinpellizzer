Pleurothallis furfuracea

Pleurothallis furfuracea, commonly known as the fuzzy pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly found in the Andean regions of Ecuador and Peru.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments within cloud forests. The plant is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom during the wet season. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique morphology and adaptability to high-altitude conditions.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
