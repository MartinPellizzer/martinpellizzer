Pacourina edulis var. spinosissima

Pacourina edulis var. spinosissima, commonly known as the spiny edible pacourina, is a climbing plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are traditionally used by indigenous communities. Its botanical structure supports its role as a food source and medicinal plant in the region.

The species is notable for its adaptability to humid, tropical environments.
