Pleurothallis welsiae-windischiae

Pleurothallis welsiae-windischiae, commonly known as the Windisch's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering structures. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention in its humid, shaded habitat.

This orchid is often found growing on trees or rocks, thriving in the moist, nutrient-rich environments of its native range.
