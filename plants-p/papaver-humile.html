Papaver humile

Papaver humile, commonly known as the Persian poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Papaver humile is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
