Phaseolus lunatus f. solanoides

Phaseolus lunatus f. solanoides, commonly known as the purple hull cowpea, is a botanical variant of the common cowpea.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple to violet-colored pods and seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. This variety is native to the Americas and thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with trailing or climbing stems, producing small, white or pale yellow flowers.

Its botanical features make it a valuable crop for both its nutritional content and ornamental appeal.
