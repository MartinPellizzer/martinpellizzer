Puccinellia phryganodes subsp. geniculata

Puccinellia phryganodes subsp.

geniculata, commonly known as the narrowleaf saltbush, is a perennial grass found in arid and semi-arid regions. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant typically grows in saline or alkaline soils, making it well adapted to harsh environments. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are usually pink to reddish in color.

This subspecies is important for stabilizing soil in degraded areas and is also used in some traditional medicinal practices.
