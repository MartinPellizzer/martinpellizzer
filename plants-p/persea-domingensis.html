Persea domingensis

Persea domingensis, commonly known as the Cuban avocado or *aguacate* in Spanish, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces large, pear-shaped fruits with a thick, green skin and a creamy, buttery flesh. The fruit is rich in healthy fats, vitamins, and minerals, making it a valuable food source.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value.
