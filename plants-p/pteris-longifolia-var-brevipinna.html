Pteris longifolia var. brevipinna

Pteris longifolia var. brevipinna, commonly known as the short-pined water fern, is a variant of the common water fern, characterized by its shorter pinnae compared to the typical variety.

It is a fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae, native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant has fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near water sources such as streams and wetlands.

Its rhizomes are creeping and produce roots that help anchor it in wet soil or aquatic substrates.
