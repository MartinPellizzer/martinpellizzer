Potamogeton obtusifolius var. elongatus

Potamogeton obtusifolius var. elongatus, commonly known as the elongated pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that can grow up to 20 cm in length, which are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, and it thrives in shallow, nutrient-rich waters. Its rhizomes help anchor it to the substrate, and it plays a role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

This species is often found in lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers.
