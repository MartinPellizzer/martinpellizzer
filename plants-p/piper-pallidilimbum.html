Piper pallidilimbum

Piper pallidilimbum, commonly known as the pale-limbed pepper vine, is a species of flowering plant in the Piperaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature.

The plant is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
