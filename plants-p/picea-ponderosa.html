Picea ponderosa

Picea ponderosa, commonly known as the ponderosa pine, is a tall evergreen conifer native to western North America.

It is characterized by its long, slender needles and distinctive bark that peels in thick, furrowed plates. The tree produces cones that hang downward, containing seeds surrounded by a papery wing. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained sites and full sun.

Ponderosa pines are important in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and contributing to biodiversity.
