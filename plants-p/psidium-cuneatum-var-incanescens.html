Psidium cuneatum var. incanescens

Psidium cuneatum var. incanescens, commonly known as the white-flowered guava, is a variant of the guava species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant produces small, round fruits with a sweet and aromatic flavor, similar to other guava varieties. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits, making it a popular choice in tropical gardens.
