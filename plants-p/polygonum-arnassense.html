Polygonum arnassense

Polygonum arnassense, commonly known as the Arnassense knotweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes species known for their distinctive bracteate inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, often shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a reddish tinge, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes.

Polygonum arnassense is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
