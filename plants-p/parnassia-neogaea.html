Parnassia neogaea

Parnassia neogaea, commonly known as the Neogean crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are dispersed by birds.

Parnassia neogaea is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
