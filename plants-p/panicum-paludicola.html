Panicum paludicola

Panicum paludicola, commonly known as marsh panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in the Americas.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and typically grows in marshy or swampy areas. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Panicum paludicola plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
