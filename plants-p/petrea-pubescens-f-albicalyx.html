Petrea pubescens f. albicalyx

Petrea pubescens f. albicalyx, commonly known as the white-flowered poinciana, is a cultivar of the poinciana tree native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale pink flowers that contrast with its green foliage. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with compound leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its decorative appearance and is commonly used in landscaping and gardens.
