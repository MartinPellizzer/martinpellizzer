Papilionopsis stylidioides

Papilionopsis stylidioides, commonly known as the slender spurred orchid, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spurred flowers that resemble small butterflies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow and white.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
