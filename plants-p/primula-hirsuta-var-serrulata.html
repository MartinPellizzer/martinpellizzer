Primula hirsuta var. serrulata

Primula hirsuta var. serrulata, commonly known as the serrated hirsute primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features hairy, ovate leaves with serrated edges, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
