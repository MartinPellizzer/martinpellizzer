Phymatocarpus interioris

Phymatocarpus interioris, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, characterized by its woody stems and small, glandular leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It has adapted to survive in harsh conditions through deep root systems and minimal water requirements.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in desert ecosystems.
