Passiflora antioquiensis var. antioquiensis

Passiflora antioquiensis var. antioquiensis, commonly known as the Antioquian passionflower, is a species native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a unique blend of colors and structures, including a crown of filaments and a central corona. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed fresh or used in local culinary traditions. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is typically found in shaded, forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for horticultural collections.
