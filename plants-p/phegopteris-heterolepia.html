Phegopteris heterolepia

It is characterized by its intricate fronds that are typically divided into multiple segments, with a distinctive appearance that includes both sterile and fertile fronds. Its spore-producing structures, known as sori, are arranged in a unique pattern on the underside of the fronds.

The heterolepia fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
