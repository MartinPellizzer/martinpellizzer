Pleurothallis concinna

Pleurothallis concinna, commonly known as the pink-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the understory of forests. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers with a distinctive pinkish-red lip. Its flowers are usually arranged in a raceme and bloom during the warmer months.

Pleurothallis concinna is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
