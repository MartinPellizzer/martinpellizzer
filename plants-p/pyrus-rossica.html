Pyrus rossica

Pyrus rossica, commonly known as the Russian pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Europe and western Siberia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, serrated leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces pomes, which are fleshy fruits with a sweet, aromatic flavor. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Pyrus rossica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
