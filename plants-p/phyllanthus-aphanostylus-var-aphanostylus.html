Phyllanthus aphanostylus var. aphanostylus

Phyllanthus aphanostylus var. aphanostylus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a small shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by bright orange or red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
