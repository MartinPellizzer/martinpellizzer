Physocarpus amurensis var. concolor

Physocarpus amurensis var. concolor, commonly known as Amur honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets and produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in early summer. The plant bears red to orange berries that persist into winter, providing food for birds and wildlife. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

Its growth habit is typically upright or spreading, making it suitable for hedges or landscape borders.
