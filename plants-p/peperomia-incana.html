Peperomia incana

Peperomia incana, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a compact, low-maintenance plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, waxy, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it ideal for indoor cultivation.

Peperomia incana is popular for its ornamental value and ease of care, making it a favored choice for indoor gardens.
