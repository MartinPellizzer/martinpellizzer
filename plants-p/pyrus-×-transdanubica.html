Pyrus × transdanubica

Pyrus × transdanubica, commonly known as the Transdanubian pear, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Pyrus communis (European pear) and Pyrus pyraster (common spindle-tree).

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 5–10 meters, featuring a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin, while the flowers are white and borne in clusters. The fruit is a small, yellowish pear-shaped berry that ripens in late summer.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
