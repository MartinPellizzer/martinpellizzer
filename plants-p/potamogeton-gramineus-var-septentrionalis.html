Potamogeton gramineus var. septentrionalis

Potamogeton gramineus var. septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to temperate regions of North America.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by wind, and its seeds are dispersed by water.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
