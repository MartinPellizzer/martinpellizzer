Polygala violoides var. pedicellaris

Polygala violoides var. pedicellaris, commonly known as the pink-flowered milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats and has small, ovate leaves arranged alternately along the stem.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its delicate floral display.
