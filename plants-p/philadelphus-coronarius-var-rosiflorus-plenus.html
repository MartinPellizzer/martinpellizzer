Philadelphus coronarius var. rosiflorus-plenus

Philadelphus coronarius var. rosiflorus-plenus, commonly known as the double-flowered tree of heaven, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant, white to pale yellow double flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and a smooth, greyish bark. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The variety 'rosiflorus-plenus' is noted for its showy, fully double blooms, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
