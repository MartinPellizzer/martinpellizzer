Platanthera hispidula

Platanthera hispidula, commonly known as the hairy green orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to North America.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. The plant features a slender, often reddish stem with lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are greenish-white with a distinctive, hairy appearance, and they bloom in late summer.

It relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, highlighting its ecological interdependence.
