Potentilla pedata f. posoniensis

Potentilla pedata f. posoniensis, commonly known as the Posonensis cinquefoil, is a botanical variant of the common cinquefoil.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers. Native to the alpine regions of the Carpathian Mountains, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient member of the Rosaceae family.
