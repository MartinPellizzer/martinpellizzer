Pourouma fuliginea

Pourouma fuliginea, commonly known as the black bamboo or black palm, is a species of palm native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk that is often covered in a dark, sooty coating. The plant produces large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its flowers are clustered in inflorescences, and it produces edible fruits that are consumed by various animals.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated for its unique aesthetic qualities.
