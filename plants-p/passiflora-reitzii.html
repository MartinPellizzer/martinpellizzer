Passiflora reitzii

Passiflora reitzii, commonly known as the "Moonflower Passionflower," is a rare and striking species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that open at night, featuring a unique blend of violet, white, and blue petals. The plant produces a distinctive crown of filaments around its reproductive structures, which are often adorned with red and blue threads. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its vines can grow up to several meters in length.

Passiflora reitzii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its rare floral display.
