Piper sagranum

Piper sagranum, commonly known as the Bornean black pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. Its fruit contains numerous tiny, aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in dense rainforests.
