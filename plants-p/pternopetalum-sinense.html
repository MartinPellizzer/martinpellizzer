Pternopetalum sinense

Pternopetalum sinense, commonly known as the Chinese pomegranate, is a small evergreen shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Pternopetalum sinense is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
