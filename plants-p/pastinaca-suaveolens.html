Pastinaca suaveolens

Pastinaca suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet cicely, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and umbel-shaped flower clusters that produce small, white flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, shady habitats. Its aromatic seeds are used in culinary applications, adding a licorice-like flavor to dishes.

The plant also has a long history of use in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
