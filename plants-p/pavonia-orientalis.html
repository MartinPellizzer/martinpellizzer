Pavonia orientalis

Pavonia orientalis, commonly known as the oriental hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, hibiscus-like flowers that range in color from red to pink and white. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy beneath. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Pavonia orientalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
