Pithecellobium macbridei

Pithecellobium macbridei, commonly known as the MacBride's evergreen acacia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers that attract pollinators.

Its fruit is a legume containing several seeds, and it is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
