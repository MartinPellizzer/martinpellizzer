Pisum luteum

Pisum luteum, commonly known as the yellow vetch, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds. It is often used for soil stabilization and as a forage crop due to its hardy nature.

Pisum luteum has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.
