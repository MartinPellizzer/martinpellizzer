Pandanus fragrans f. tinianensis

Pandanus fragrans f. tinianensis, commonly known as the fragrant pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters at the tips of the leaves. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove swamps or near the shoreline.

Its unique appearance and aromatic properties make it both ornamental and culturally significant in its native regions.
