Piper glaucescens

Piper glaucescens, commonly known as blue piper or blue pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns blue when mature, hence its common name.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
