Pithecellobium tortum var. pubescens

Pithecellobium tortum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy tortuga acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged and covered with fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, yellow to golden flowers that are often clustered in racemes, followed by flattened, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm, arid climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.
