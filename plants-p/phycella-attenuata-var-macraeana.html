Phycella attenuata var. macraeana

Phycella attenuata var. macraeana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phycella, is a variant of the phycella genus native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in shades of white or pale yellow, and it blooms during the cooler months.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations.
