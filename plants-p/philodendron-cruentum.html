Philodendron cruentum

Philodendron cruentum, commonly known as the red-veined philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with prominent red veins that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant has a vigorous growth habit and can reach lengths of several meters when provided with support. Its aerial roots help it climb and anchor to surfaces, and it thrives in bright, indirect light.

Philodendron cruentum is popular as a houseplant due to its striking foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
