Piper pastasanum f. impilosum

Piper pastasanum f. impilosum, commonly known as the hairy pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper pastasanum species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptical in shape, and often have a slightly rough texture.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature, containing three seeds.
