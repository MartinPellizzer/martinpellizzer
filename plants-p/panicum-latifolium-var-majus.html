Panicum latifolium var. majus

Panicum latifolium var. majus, commonly known as the broadleaf panicgrass, is a tall, herbaceous perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features wide, flat leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces showy, panicle-shaped inflorescences that range in color from golden yellow to red, depending on the season. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ability to form dense clumps make it a hardy and low-maintenance plant.
