Poa indattenuata

Poa indattenuata, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect culms with narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, two-ranked spikelets.

Poa indattenuata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
