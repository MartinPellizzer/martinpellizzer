Pseudotsuga menziesii var. flahaultii

Pseudotsuga menziesii var. flahaultii, commonly known as the Douglas fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its pyramidal shape, needle-like leaves, and distinctive cones. The tree produces a fragrant resin that helps protect it from pests and diseases. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

This species is valued for its timber and plays an important role in forest ecosystems.
