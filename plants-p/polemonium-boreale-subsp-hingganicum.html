Polemonium boreale subsp. hingganicum

Polemonium boreale subsp.

hingganicum, commonly known as the Hinggan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to northeastern China. It belongs to the family Polemoniacaceae and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are slender and upright.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
