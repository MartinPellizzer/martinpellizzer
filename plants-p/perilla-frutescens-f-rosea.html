Perilla frutescens f. rosea

Perilla frutescens f. rosea, commonly known as red perilla, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a variant of the common perilla species, characterized by its striking pink to reddish-purple foliage. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that appear in clusters, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are edible and have a mild, slightly spicy flavor, making it a popular ingredient in Asian cuisines.

The plant thrives in warm climates and is often grown as a decorative and culinary plant.
