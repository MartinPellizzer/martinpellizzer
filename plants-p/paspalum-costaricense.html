Paspalum costaricense

Paspalum costaricense, commonly known as the Costa Rican paspalum, is a perennial grass native to Central America.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems.
