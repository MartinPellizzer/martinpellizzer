Pentarrhaphis fournierana

Pentarrhaphis fournierana, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, bright yellow-green leaves that give it a striking appearance. The plant forms dense clumps and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Pentarrhaphis fournierana is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in tropical environments.
