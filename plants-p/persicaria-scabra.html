Persicaria scabra

Persicaria scabra, commonly known as rough knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems covered in stiff, bristly hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and prominent veins, and the plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along riverbanks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel.
