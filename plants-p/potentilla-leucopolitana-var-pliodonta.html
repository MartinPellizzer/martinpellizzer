Potentilla leucopolitana var. pliodonta

Potentilla leucopolitana var. pliodonta, commonly known as the white polypody, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover in arid landscapes.
