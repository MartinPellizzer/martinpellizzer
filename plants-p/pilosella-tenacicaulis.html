Pilosella tenacicaulis

Pilosella tenacicaulis, commonly known as the sticky hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance and aids in moisture retention. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

Pilosella tenacicaulis produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.
