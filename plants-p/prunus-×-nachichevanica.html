Prunus × nachichevanica

Prunus × nachichevanica, commonly known as the Caucasian cherry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Prunus avium (sweet cherry) and Prunus padus (bullace).

It is native to the Caucasus region and is characterized by its small, sweet, and edible fruits. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces white or pale pink flowers in early spring.

The fruit ripens in late summer and is valued for its flavor and culinary use.
