Potentilla × juradensis

Potentilla × juradensis, commonly known as the hybrid cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the stems are often covered in fine, glandular hairs.

Potentilla × juradensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and vibrant floral display.
