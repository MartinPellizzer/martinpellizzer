Phebalium glandulosum subsp. angustifolium

Phebalium glandulosum subsp.

angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leafed phebalium, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It features small, glandular-dotted leaves that are typically narrow and elliptical in shape.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are often visited by insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
