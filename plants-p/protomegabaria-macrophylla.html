Protomegabaria macrophylla

Protomegabaria macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leafed protomegabaria, is a rare and ancient plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length, often with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom in the early morning. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists studying evolutionary adaptation.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
