Pavonia paludicola

Pavonia paludicola, commonly known as the swamp pavonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in marshy or swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Pavonia paludicola plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
