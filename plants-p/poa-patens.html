Poa patens

Poa patens, commonly known as the bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and dense, carpet-like growth habit. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. Poa patens has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences are usually inconspicuous, contributing to its role in ecological systems.
