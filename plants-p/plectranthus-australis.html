Plectranthus australis

Plectranthus australis, commonly known as Australian Violet, is a herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal uses.
