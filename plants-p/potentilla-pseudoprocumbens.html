Potentilla pseudoprocumbens

Potentilla pseudoprocumbens, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with wiry, often prostrate stems. Its leaves are palmately divided into five oval lobes, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
