Polystichum benoitianum

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it can thrive in cool, humid conditions. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are clustered in compact, fertile zones known as sori.

Polystichum benoitianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant, architectural appearance.
