Phlomis iranica

Phlomis iranica, commonly known as Iranian plumeless verbena, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Phlomis iranica is often found in rocky and arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
