Prunus × yedoensis

Prunus × yedoensis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry tree, is a hybrid species native to Japan.

It is celebrated for its showy, pink or white blossoms that appear in early spring, creating a spectacular floral display. The tree typically grows to a height of 8–15 meters and has a rounded, spreading crown. Its leaves are ovate and turn a vibrant green in summer, while the bark is smooth and light gray.

This species is widely cultivated in gardens and parks for its ornamental value and cultural significance.
