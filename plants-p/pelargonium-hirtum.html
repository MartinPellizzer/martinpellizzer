Pelargonium hirtum

Pelargonium hirtum, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium hirtum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
