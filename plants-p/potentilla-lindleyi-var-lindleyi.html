Potentilla lindleyi var. lindleyi

Potentilla lindleyi var. lindleyi, commonly known as the Lindley's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or streambanks. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and the stems are often hairy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
