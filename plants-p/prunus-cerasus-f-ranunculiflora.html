Prunus cerasus f. ranunculiflora

Prunus cerasus f. ranunculiflora, commonly known as the ranunculiflora cherry, is a unique cultivar of the sweet cherry.

It is characterized by its distinctive, large, white flowers that resemble those of the buttercup, giving it its common name. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin, and the fruit is a sweet, red cherry with a firm texture.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
