Pseuderanthemum lanceolatum

Pseuderanthemum lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceleaf false anthemis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in warm climates as a decorative plant.
