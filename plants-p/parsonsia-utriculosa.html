Parsonsia utriculosa

Parsonsia utriculosa, commonly known as the bladderfruit or golden glory, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces distinctive, inflated fruit capsules that resemble bladders, giving it its common name. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, often with a waxy coating.

Parsonsia utriculosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
