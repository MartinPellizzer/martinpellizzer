Phrynium minutiflorum

Phrynium minutiflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered arrowleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

Due to its ornamental foliage, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
