Polystichum takakii

Polystichum takakii, commonly known as the Takakia fern, is a rare and ancient fern species native to the high elevations of Japan and China.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are typically dark green with a distinctive silvery underside. This fern thrives in cool, moist, and shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a feature common to many ferns.

Polystichum takakii is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental and scientific significance.
