Panicum flacciflorum

Panicum flacciflorum, commonly known as the flaccidflower grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swampy areas and along riverbanks. The plant features slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, with pale green to brown bracts.

The botanical characteristics of Panicum flacciflorum make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
