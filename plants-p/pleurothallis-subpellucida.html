Pleurothallis subpellucida

Pleurothallis subpellucida, commonly known as the translucent pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often have a translucent or waxy appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted to conserve moisture.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
