Potamogeton × fluitans var. ellipticus

Potamogeton × fluitans var. ellipticus, commonly known as the narrowleaf pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers, often forming dense underwater vegetation. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with stamens and pistils arranged in a spiral pattern.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to varying water depths and light conditions, making it a common component of aquatic ecosystems.
