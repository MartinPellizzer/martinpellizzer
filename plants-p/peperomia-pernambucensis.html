Peperomia pernambucensis

Peperomia pernambucensis, commonly known as the Pernambuco peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to Brazil.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink, giving it an ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically borne in clusters. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Peperomia pernambucensis is relatively drought-tolerant and prefers moderate to warm temperatures.
