Piper pinguifolium

Piper pinguifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf pepper or giant pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Piper pinguifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
