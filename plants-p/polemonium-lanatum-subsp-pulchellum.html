Polemonium lanatum subsp. pulchellum

Polemonium lanatum subsp.

pulchellum, commonly known as the showy bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America. It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring, typically from May to June.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded areas with well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
