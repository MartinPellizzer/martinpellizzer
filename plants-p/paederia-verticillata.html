Paederia verticillata

Paederia verticillata, commonly known as the clustered moonseed, is a perennial climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, opposite leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. The plant is known for its fragrant blooms and is sometimes used in traditional medicine. Its fruit is a small, round capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Paederia verticillata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
