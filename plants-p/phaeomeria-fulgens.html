Phaeomeria fulgens

Phaeomeria fulgens, commonly known as the flame flower, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range from red to orange in color. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. Its flowering period typically occurs during the warmer months, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soil.

Phaeomeria fulgens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
