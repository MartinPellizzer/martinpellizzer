Potentilla leopoliensis

Potentilla leopoliensis, commonly known as the Leopoliensis cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are usually oval-shaped and slightly serrated.

Potentilla leopoliensis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
