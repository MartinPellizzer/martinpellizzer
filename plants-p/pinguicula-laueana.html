Pinguicula laueana

Pinguicula laueana, commonly known as the Laue's bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, succulent leaves that form bladders to trap and digest insects. The plant typically grows in wet, boggy environments where it can thrive on the nutrients obtained from its prey. Its flowers are usually pale purple or white, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Pinguicula laueana is an example of a plant that has adapted to nutrient-poor soils through its specialized trapping mechanism.
