Potentilla verna var. incana

Potentilla verna var. incana, commonly known as the woolly cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has hairy, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance and help it retain moisture in dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in marginal environments.
