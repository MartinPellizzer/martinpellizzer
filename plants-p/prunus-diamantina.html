Prunus diamantina

Prunus diamantina, commonly known as the diamond peach, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces fragrant white or pink flowers, followed by small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy surface.

Prunus diamantina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
