Picea pendula

Picea pendula, commonly known as the weeping spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive and elegant appearance. The tree has needle-like leaves that are dark green on top and white underneath, providing a striking visual contrast. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Picea pendula is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types as long as it has good drainage.
