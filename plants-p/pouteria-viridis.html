Pouteria viridis

Pouteria viridis, commonly known as the green spondias, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, greenish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, green to yellow fruits. Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
