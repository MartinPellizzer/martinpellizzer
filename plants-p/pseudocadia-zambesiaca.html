Pseudocadia zambesiaca

Pseudocadia zambesiaca, commonly known as the Zambesi daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged alternately along the stem.

The flowers bloom in shades of yellow to orange, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
