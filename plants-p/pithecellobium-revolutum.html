Pithecellobium revolutum

Pithecellobium revolutum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

Its growth habit and adaptability make it a valuable species for reforestation and ecological restoration projects.
