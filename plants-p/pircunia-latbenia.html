Pircunia latbenia

Pircunia latbenia, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are broad and glossy, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Pircunia latbenia thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
