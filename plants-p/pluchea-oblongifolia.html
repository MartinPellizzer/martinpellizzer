Pluchea oblongifolia

Pluchea oblongifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf groundsel, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Pluchea oblongifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
