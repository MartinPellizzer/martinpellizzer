Panicum corynotrichum

Panicum corynotrichum, commonly known as purpletop millet, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking purple-tinted foliage and compact growth habit, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that add visual interest during the flowering season. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, requiring minimal water once established.

Its adaptability to various soil types and drought tolerance make it a versatile choice for landscaping and erosion control.
