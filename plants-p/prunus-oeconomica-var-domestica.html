Prunus oeconomica var. domestica

Prunus oeconomica var. domestica, commonly known as the domestic cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It produces small, sweet, and edible fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in jams and preserves. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and blooms with fragrant white flowers in the spring. Its fruit ripens in the summer, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive form and seasonal interest.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
