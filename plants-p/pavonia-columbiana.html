Pavonia columbiana

Pavonia columbiana, commonly known as the Columbia pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and clusters of showy, pink to red bract-like flowers. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Pavonia columbiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
