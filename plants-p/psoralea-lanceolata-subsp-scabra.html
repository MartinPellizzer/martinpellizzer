Psoralea lanceolata subsp. scabra

Psoralea lanceolata subsp.

scabra, commonly known as the rough psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The subspecies scabra is distinguished by its rough, hairy texture on the stems and leaves. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or inland grassland habitats.
