Populus × eugenei

Populus × eugenei, commonly known as the hybrid poplar, is a deciduous tree that belongs to the Populus genus.

It is a natural hybrid between Populus deltoides and Populus balsamifera, resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The tree typically grows to a height of 20–30 meters and has heart-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation.

This species is known for its fast growth rate and adaptability to various soil types, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
