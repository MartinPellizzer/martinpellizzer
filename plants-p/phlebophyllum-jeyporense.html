Phlebophyllum jeyporense

Phlebophyllum jeyporense, commonly known as the Indian nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes stinging nettles, and is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and small greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It has a creeping growth habit with rhizomes that help it spread and establish colonies.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
