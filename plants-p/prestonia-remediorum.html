Prestonia remediorum

Prestonia remediorum, commonly known as the pink morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its twining stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant pink to purple blooms that open in the morning and close in the late afternoon. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its fruits are capsule-like structures containing numerous seeds.

Prestonia remediorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
