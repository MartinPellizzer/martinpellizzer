Pygeum lanceolatum

Pygeum lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceleaf African cherry, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. The plant is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
