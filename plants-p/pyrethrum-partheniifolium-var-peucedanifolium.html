Pyrethrum partheniifolium var. peucedanifolium

Pyrethrum partheniifolium var. peucedanifolium, commonly known as the Italian tansy, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and flowers, are valued for their insecticidal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens and herbal collections.
