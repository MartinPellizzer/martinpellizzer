Pediocactus peeblesianus subsp. fickeisenii

Pediocactus peeblesianus subsp.

fickeisenii, commonly known as the Peebles' cactus, is a compact, spineless cactus native to the arid regions of Baja California, Mexico. It features a globular stem with a smooth, pale green surface that becomes more reddish in full sun.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted coloration. It is well adapted to extreme drought conditions, storing water in its fleshy stems.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
