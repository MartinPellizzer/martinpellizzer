Peperomia metcalfii

Peperomia metcalfii, commonly known as the Silver Cane Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features upright, cane-like stems covered in a dense layer of silvery-white, velvety hairs, giving it a unique and striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped and have a slightly waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Peperomia metcalfii thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
