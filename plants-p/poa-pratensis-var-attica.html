Poa pratensis var. attica

Poa pratensis var. attica, commonly known as Greek Meadowgrass, is a variant of the common meadowgrass native to Greece.

It is a perennial grass species characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, leaf-like blades. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
