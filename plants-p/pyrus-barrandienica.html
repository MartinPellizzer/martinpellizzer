Pyrus barrandienica

Pyrus barrandienica, commonly known as the Barrandienica pear, is a rare and ancient species of pear native to the Czech Republic.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits with a thin, yellowish skin and a sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant is deciduous, producing leaves that are alternate and serrated, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its rarity and historical significance, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
