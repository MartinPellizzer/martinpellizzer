Pogonatherum paniceum

Pogonatherum paniceum, commonly known as panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form a clump. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are typically clustered in panicles, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture and a prominent midrib.

Pogonatherum paniceum is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
