Prunus divaricata subsp. borealicaucasica

Prunus divaricata subsp.

borealicaucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It typically grows in rocky and forested areas, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are red when mature. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it has a woody, branched structure.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its floral display.
