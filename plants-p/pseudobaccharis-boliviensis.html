Pseudobaccharis boliviensis

Pseudobaccharis boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian false hibiscus, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Bolivia and Paraguay.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in regions with a similar climate.
