Polypodium malaicum

Polypodium malaicum, commonly known as the Malayan wall fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, creeping rhizome system that allows it to colonize rocky or shaded environments. The fronds are typically leathery and have a distinctive, often bipinnate structure. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in shaded gardens.
