Papaya citriformis

Papaya citriformis, commonly known as the sour papaya, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow-orange fruits. The fruit is typically smaller and more acidic compared to the common papaya, with a fibrous texture and a distinct aroma. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which is used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
