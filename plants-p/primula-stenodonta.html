Primula stenodonta

Primula stenodonta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

Primula stenodonta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
