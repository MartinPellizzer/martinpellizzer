Phylica glabrata

Phylica glabrata, commonly known as the silver bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and silvery-gray, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Phylica glabrata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
