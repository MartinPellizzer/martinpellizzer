Polygala capillaris

Polygala capillaris, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils. The plant features slender stems with small, lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate, spiky flowers in shades of pink or purple. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
