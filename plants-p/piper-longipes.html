Piper longipes

Piper longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its long, slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits that mature to a dark purple color.

Piper longipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
