Physoleucas pachystachya

Physoleucas pachystachya, commonly known as the giant sedge, is a tall, herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its robust, grass-like foliage and tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, clustered flowers. The plant thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found in swampy areas or along riverbanks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to its ecological significance in wetland ecosystems.

Physoleucas pachystachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in aquatic habitats.
