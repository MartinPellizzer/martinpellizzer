Plectranthus Katangensis

Plectranthus katangensis is a unique and lesser-known member of the mint family native to the tropical rainforests of central Africa. This striking plant is characterized by its velvety leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of purple and white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing along forest floors or in rocky crevices. Despite its beauty, Plectranthus katangensis remains relatively obscure in the horticultural world. Its rarity and distinctive appearance make it a fascinating subject for botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
