Poa flexuosa var. vivipara

Poa flexuosa var. vivipara, commonly known as the viviparous bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces numerous small, grass-like leaves. One of its notable botanical features is its ability to reproduce vegetatively through vivipary, where seedlings develop on the stems before maturing. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Its compact growth and dense foliage make it a valuable component of certain ecological systems.
