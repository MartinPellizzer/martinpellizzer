Pycreus rivularis

Pycreus rivularis, commonly known as the river fern, is a species of aquatic fern found in freshwater environments.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters such as streams and ponds. The plant has slender, ribbon-like fronds that are often submerged or partially emergent. Its rhizomes spread along the substrate, allowing it to colonize new areas.

Pycreus rivularis plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by reducing water flow and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
