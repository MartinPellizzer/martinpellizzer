Pelargonium parviflorum subsp. tuberculum

Pelargonium parviflorum subsp.

tuberculum, commonly known as the tuberculate geranium, is a species native to South Africa. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves.

The flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central gland. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
