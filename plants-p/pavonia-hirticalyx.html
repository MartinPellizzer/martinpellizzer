Pavonia hirticalyx

Pavonia hirticalyx, commonly known as the hairy pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are ovate to lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy surface that distinguishes it from similar species.

Pavonia hirticalyx is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
