Primula denticulata var. alba

Primula denticulata var. alba, commonly known as the white primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and clusters of small, white, funnel-shaped flowers. The leaves are broadly ovate with finely toothed edges, giving the plant its species name "denticulata.". It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

This variety is prized for its delicate appearance and is commonly cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
