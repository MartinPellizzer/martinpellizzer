Potentilla wimanniana subsp. praecox

Potentilla wimanniana subsp.

praecox, commonly known as early potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features wiry, branched stems and small, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in early summer, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
