Pycreus polystachyos var. hahnianus

Pycreus polystachyos var. hahnianus, commonly known as the Hahn's pycreus, is a variant of the pycreus plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall herbaceous appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to botanical gardens and specialized horticultural collections.
