Petalostemon candidus var. oligophyllus

Petalostemon candidus var. oligophyllus, commonly known as the white-leaved petalostemon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to arid environments, with reduced leaf surface area to minimize water loss.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
