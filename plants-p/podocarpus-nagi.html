Podocarpus nagi

Podocarpus nagi, commonly known as the Japanese yew, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to Japan and Korea.

It has a slender, columnar form with dark green, scale-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and reddish-brown cones, which contain the seeds. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Podocarpus nagi is also valued for its durable wood, which has been used in traditional construction and crafts.
