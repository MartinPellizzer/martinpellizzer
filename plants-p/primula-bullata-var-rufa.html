Primula bullata var. rufa

Primula bullata var. rufa, commonly known as the red-flowered primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to deep red, often with a contrasting throat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its vibrant blooms.
