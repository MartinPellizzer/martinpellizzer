Piper edurum

Piper edurum, commonly known as the bitter pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its small, elongated leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small, berry-like structures that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is noted for its pungent and bitter taste, which is due to the presence of various alkaloids.
