Phalaris canariensis var. debilis

Phalaris canariensis var. debilis, commonly known as the Canary grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in landscaping.
