Pseuderanthemum selangorense

Pseuderanthemum selangorense, commonly known as the Malayan false pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
