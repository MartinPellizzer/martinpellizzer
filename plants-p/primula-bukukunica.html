Primula bukukunica

Primula bukukunica, commonly known as the Bukukunica primrose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding vibrant color to its alpine habitat. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of downy hairs.

Primula bukukunica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in rock gardens and alpine settings.
