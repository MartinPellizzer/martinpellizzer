Pleurothallis gratiosa

Pleurothallis gratiosa, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces delicate, pendulous flowers that are often yellow or orange in color, which are highly attractive to pollinators. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
