Prunus spinosa f. stenopetala

Prunus spinosa f. stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-petaled blackthorn, is a variant of the common blackthorn, characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, more tightly clustered flowers.

It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. Its leaves are oval with serrated edges, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

This form is valued for its unique floral display and adaptability to various soil conditions.
