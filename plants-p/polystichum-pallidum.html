Polystichum pallidum

Polystichum pallidum, commonly known as the pale fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, light green fronds that are typically bipinnate, giving it a finely divided appearance. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found growing in shaded forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the undersides of its fronds, and it reproduces through sexual reproduction via spore dispersal.

Polystichum pallidum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded garden settings.
