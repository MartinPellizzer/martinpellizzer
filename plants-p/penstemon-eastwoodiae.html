Penstemon eastwoodiae

Penstemon eastwoodiae, commonly known as Eastwood's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and displays spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from violet to blue. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Penstemon eastwoodiae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant landscapes.
