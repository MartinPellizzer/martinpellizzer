Palala superba

Palala superba, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, often displaying vibrant shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its complex floral structure, which includes multiple petals and a prominent lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, Palala superba is often protected in its natural habitat.
