Prunus menghaiensis

Prunus menghaiensis, commonly known as the Menghai cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a small, sweet drupe that ripens in summer, often used in local cuisines.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited commercial cultivation due to its specific climate requirements.
