Pinus sylvestris subsp. amurensis

Pinus sylvestris subsp.

amurensis, commonly known as the Amur pine, is a subspecies native to eastern Asia, particularly found in regions of Russia, China, and Korea. It is a coniferous evergreen tree that typically grows in temperate climates, often in mountainous or forested areas.

The tree has needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in bundles of five, which are persistent for several years. Its cones are woody and contain two-winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its timber and is also used in ornamental horticulture due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
