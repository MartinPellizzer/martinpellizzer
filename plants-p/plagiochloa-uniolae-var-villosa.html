Plagiochloa uniolae var. villosa

Plagiochloa uniolae var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy plagiochloa, is a grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
