Pedicularis resupinata var. albiflora

Pedicularis resupinata var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered lousewort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its flowers are often visited by bumblebees, which are essential for pollination.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
