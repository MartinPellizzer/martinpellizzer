Porophyllum coloratum var. coloratum

Porophyllum coloratum var. coloratum, commonly known as the Mexican tarweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a distinctive grayish-green color, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
