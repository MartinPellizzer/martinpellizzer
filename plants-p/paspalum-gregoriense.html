Paspalum gregoriense

Paspalum gregoriense, commonly known as the Gregorian grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Paspalum gregoriense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
