Podocallis nivea

Podocallis nivea, commonly known as the white sand mat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-like growth habit and produces small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Podocallis nivea is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native environment.
