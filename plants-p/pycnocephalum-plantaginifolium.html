Pycnocephalum plantaginifolium

Pycnocephalum plantaginifolium, commonly known as the woolly-headed plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
