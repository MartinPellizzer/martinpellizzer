Pulmonaria affinis subsp. alpestris

Pulmonaria affinis subsp.

alpestris, commonly known as the alpine lungwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It features distinctive basal rosettes of velvety, spotted leaves that are often tinged with silver or blue.

The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or grassy alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is cultivated in gardens and protected in its natural range.
