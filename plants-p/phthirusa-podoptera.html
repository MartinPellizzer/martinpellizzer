Phthirusa podoptera

Phthirusa podoptera, commonly known as the broad-winged grasshopper, is a species of orthopteran insect found in various grassland habitats.

It is characterized by its elongated body, long legs, and distinctive broad wings that allow for efficient flight. The plant-related aspects of this species primarily involve its interaction with grasses and other herbaceous plants, as it feeds on foliage. Its life cycle is closely tied to the growth cycles of its host plants, often relying on seasonal vegetation for sustenance.

The presence of Phthirusa podoptera can indicate the health and biodiversity of its surrounding plant communities.
