Podospadix humboldtii

Podospadix humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt's podospadix, is a rare and distinctive plant native to the coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, spadix-like inflorescence, which is a dense, club-shaped cluster of small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in humid, saline environments, often found in mangrove or tidal marsh habitats. Its leaves are typically broad and succulent, adapted to retain moisture in its often waterlogged surroundings.
