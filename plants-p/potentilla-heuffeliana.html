Potentilla heuffeliana

Potentilla heuffeliana, commonly known as the Heuffel's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, woody stems and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided into five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive, delicate appearance.

Potentilla heuffeliana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.
