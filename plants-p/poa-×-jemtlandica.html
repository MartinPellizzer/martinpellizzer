Poa × jemtlandica

Poa × jemtlandica, commonly known as the Jemtland grass, is a hybrid species belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often bear inflorescences of spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Poa × jemtlandica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in grassland ecosystems.
