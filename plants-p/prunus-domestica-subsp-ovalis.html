Prunus domestica subsp. ovalis

Prunus domestica subsp.

ovalis, commonly known as the oval European plum, is a subspecies of the European plum characterized by its oval-shaped fruits. It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates.

The plant is a deciduous tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of 3 to 8 meters, with dark green, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in clusters, and the fruits are typically small, sweet, and juicy.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and for its edible fruits, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
