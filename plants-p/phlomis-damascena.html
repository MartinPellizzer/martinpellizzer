Phlomis damascena

Phlomis damascena, commonly known as Damask fleabane, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Phlomis damascena is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
