Piper isopleurum

Piper isopleurum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by berries that are typically dark purple when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
