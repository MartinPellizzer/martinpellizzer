Polygonum zakirovii

Polygonum zakirovii, commonly known as the Zakirov's knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes species known for their distinctive bract structures. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in river valleys and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth margin, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
