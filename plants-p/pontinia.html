Pontinia

Pontinia, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and oval-shaped with serrated edges.

Pontinia is often found in hedgerows and woodland areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
