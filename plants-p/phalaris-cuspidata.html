Phalaris cuspidata

Phalaris cuspidata, commonly known as sharpshooters' grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly pointed at the tips. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, greenish flowers that are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
