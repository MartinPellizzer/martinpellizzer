Pseudovouapa

Pseudovouapa, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a waxy, glossy surface, and its flowers bloom in clusters of yellow to orange petals. Pseudovouapa is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and drought-resistant nature.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in warm climates.
