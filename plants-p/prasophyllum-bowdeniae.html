Prasophyllum bowdeniae

Prasophyllum bowdeniae, commonly known as the Bowden's leek orchid, is an endemic plant species found in specific regions of Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, leek-like foliage. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, greenish or brownish flowers that are often clustered together.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
