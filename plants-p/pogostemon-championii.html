Pogostemon championii

Pogostemon championii, commonly known as the Chinese balm, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported calming and digestive properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its aromatic foliage and ornamental value.
