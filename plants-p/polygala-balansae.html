Polygala balansae

Polygala balansae, commonly known as the Balansae milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with a distinctive spurred structure that aids in pollination by insects.

It is often found in open vegetation types such as savannas and rocky outcrops.
