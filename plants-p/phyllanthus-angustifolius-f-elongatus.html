Phyllanthus angustifolius f. elongatus

Phyllanthus angustifolius f. elongatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a variant of the phyllanthus genus native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in horticulture and ecological studies.
