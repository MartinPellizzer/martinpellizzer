Phillyrea media var. ligustrifolia

Phillyrea media var. ligustrifolia, commonly known as the Ligustrum-leaved Phillyrea, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green and often have a slight waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers in the spring, followed by small, spherical berries that are typically dark blue or black. It is evergreen, with a compact growth habit and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The species is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
