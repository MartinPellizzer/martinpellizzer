Primula umbrella

Primula umbrella, commonly known as the umbrella primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. Its foliage consists of dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

The Umbrella primrose is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in cool, moist environments.
