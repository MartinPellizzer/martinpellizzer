Protasparagus racemosus

Protasparagus racemosus, commonly known as Indian spurge or Shatavari, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in racemes, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant in traditional medicine and horticulture.
