Paullinia eriantha f. genuina

Paullinia eriantha f. genuina, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree or *Caryocar* species, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
