Pouteria obovata var. dubia

Pouteria obovata var. dubia, commonly known as the yellow soursop, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, obovate leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the development of its distinctive, spiny fruit. The fruit is typically harvested when it reaches a mature yellow color, indicating readiness for consumption.

It is valued for its edible pulp, which has a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
