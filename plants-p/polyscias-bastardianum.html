Polyscias bastardianum

Polyscias bastardianum, commonly known as the broadleaf sedge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which includes plants such as philodendrons and calla lilies. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
