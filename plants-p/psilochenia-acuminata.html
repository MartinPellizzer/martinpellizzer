Psilochenia acuminata

Psilochenia acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a small, terrestrial fern characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sori, which are clustered on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its simple morphology and ecological adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
