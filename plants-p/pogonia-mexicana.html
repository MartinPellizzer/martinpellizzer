Pogonia mexicana

Pogonia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican pink orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink and white petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are often fragrant and bloom during the spring season.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
