Phelline floribunda

Phelline floribunda, commonly known as the flowering phelline, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, bark-like surface, and its leaves are typically narrow and leathery. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Phelline floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
