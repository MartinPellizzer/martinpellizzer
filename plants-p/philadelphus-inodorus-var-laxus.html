Philadelphus inodorus var. laxus

Philadelphus inodorus var. laxus, commonly known as the lax flowered mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
