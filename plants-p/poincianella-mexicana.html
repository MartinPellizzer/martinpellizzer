Poincianella mexicana

Poincianella mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, resembling flames, and are often produced in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

The tree is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in urban landscapes.
