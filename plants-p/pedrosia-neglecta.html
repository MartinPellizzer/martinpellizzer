Pedrosia neglecta

Pedrosia neglecta, commonly known as the neglected pedrosia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features delicate, narrow leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale pink. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand saline conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
