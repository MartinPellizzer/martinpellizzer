Poa guadianensis

Poa guadianensis, commonly known as the Guadiana grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to southern Europe, particularly found in regions of Spain and Portugal. This perennial grass typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant is adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
