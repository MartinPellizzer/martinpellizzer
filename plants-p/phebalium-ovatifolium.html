Phebalium ovatifolium

Phebalium ovatifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in spring. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
