Primula elatior var. acaulis

Primula elatior var. acaulis, commonly known as the Alpine primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright, stemless growth habit, with clustered leaves that are oval and slightly hairy. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in alpine gardens and rockeries.
