Parietaria debilis var. gracilis

Parietaria debilis var. gracilis, commonly known as slender wallweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features small, alternate leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and it produces tiny, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its stems are often prostrate or ascending, and the plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

It is often found growing in association with other shade-tolerant flora in temperate regions.
