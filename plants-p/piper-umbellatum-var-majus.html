Piper umbellatum var. majus

Piper umbellatum var. majus, commonly known as the giant pepper vine, is a vigorous climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often pubescent, and it produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is characterized by its long, slender stems and its ability to grow rapidly in humid, shaded environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The fruit, which is a small, berry-like structure, is edible and has a peppery flavor.
