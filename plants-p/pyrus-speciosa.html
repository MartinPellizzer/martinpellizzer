Pyrus speciosa

Pyrus speciosa, commonly known as the showy pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white to pink flowers in early spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

Pyrus speciosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
