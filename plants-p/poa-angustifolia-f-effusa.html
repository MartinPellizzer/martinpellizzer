Poa angustifolia f. effusa

Poa angustifolia f. effusa, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a variant of the narrowleaf bentgrass.

It is a perennial grass species characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of small, two-rowed spikelets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
