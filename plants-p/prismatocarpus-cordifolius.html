Prismatocarpus cordifolius

Prismatocarpus cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves that are often heart-shaped at the base. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Its fibrous roots and robust stem make it well adapted to coastal and sandy soils.

The leaves are frequently used in traditional crafts and as thatching materials in various regions.
