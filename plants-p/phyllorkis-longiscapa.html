Phyllorkis longiscapa

Phyllorkis longiscapa, commonly known as the long-stemmed phyllorkis, is a perennial herb native to the wet tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often edible roots. The plant features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, with lanceolate leaves arranged alternately along the stem. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
