Phaius somae

Phaius somae, commonly known as the Somalian orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly Somalia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, erect stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish in color, with a distinctive three-lobed lip. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid environment to flourish.

Phaius somae is often found in open grasslands and forest margins, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
