Potentilla isaurica

Potentilla isaurica, commonly known as the Isaurian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Eastern Mediterranean, including parts of Turkey, Greece, and the Balkans. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in clusters of five, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Potentilla isaurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant in horticulture.
