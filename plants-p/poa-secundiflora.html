Poa secundiflora

Poa secundiflora, commonly known as the bentgrass or silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, which helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Poa secundiflora is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
