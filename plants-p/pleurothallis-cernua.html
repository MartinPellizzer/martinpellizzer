Pleurothallis cernua

Pleurothallis cernua, commonly known as the drooping pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant typically produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are usually slender and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Pleurothallis cernua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
