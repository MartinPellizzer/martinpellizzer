Psidium nummularioides

Psidium nummularioides, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or small-leaved guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit yield.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in home gardens and for commercial purposes.
