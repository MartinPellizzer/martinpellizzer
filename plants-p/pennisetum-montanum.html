Pennisetum montanum

Pennisetum montanum, commonly known as mountain bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and bluish-green foliage that turns golden in the fall. The plant produces spikelets that are typically blue or purple in color, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for prairies and dry landscapes.

Pennisetum montanum is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for wildlife.
