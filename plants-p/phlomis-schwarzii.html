Phlomis schwarzii

Phlomis schwarzii, commonly known as Schwarz's wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and tall, erect stems that bear clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic properties.

Phlomis schwarzii is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
