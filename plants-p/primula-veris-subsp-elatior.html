Primula veris subsp. elatior

Primula veris subsp.

elatior, commonly known as the greater primrose, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a member of the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It produces elliptical, toothed leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy layer.

The flowering period occurs in early spring, making it an early indicator of the arrival of warmer weather.
