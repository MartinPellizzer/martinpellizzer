Polypodium erubescens

Polypodium erubescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features a rhizome with a distinctive reddish hue, from which the fronds emerge. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance.

Polypodium erubescens is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
