Polylepis longipilosa

Polylepis longipilosa, commonly known as the Andean cloud forest tree, is a member of the Rosaceae family and is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves and showy, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is often found in cloud forests at elevations between 2,500 and 4,000 meters. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

Polylepis longipilosa plays a crucial role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat by supporting a wide range of flora and fauna.
