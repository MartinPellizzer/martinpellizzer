Passiflora capsularis var. acutiflora

Passiflora capsularis var. acutiflora, commonly known as the sharp-flowered passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and numerous stamens that create a complex and ornate appearance. The plant produces green, pear-shaped fruits that mature to a yellowish-orange color, containing edible seeds. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and climbing.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
