Prunus griffithii var. alaica

Prunus griffithii var. alaica, commonly known as the alpine cherry, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, dark red to black berries that mature in late summer. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and has a preference for cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh alpine environments.
