Parthenocissus semicordata var. semicordata

Parthenocissus semicordata var. semicordata, commonly known as the Boston ivy, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are not typically showy. Its berries are dark blue to black and contain seeds.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to cover walls and trellises.
