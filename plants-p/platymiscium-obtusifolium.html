Platymiscium obtusifolium

Platymiscium obtusifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf tallowtree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant has a shallow root system and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
