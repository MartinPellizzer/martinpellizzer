Persea rigens

Persea rigens, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces orange to reddish fruit that is typically harvested when fully ripe. The fruit is rich in nutrients and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
