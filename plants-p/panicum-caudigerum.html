Panicum caudigerum

Panicum caudigerum, commonly known as pigweed, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Panicum caudigerum is an annual plant that can become invasive in certain regions.
