Pachyptera incarnata

Pachyptera incarnata, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces legume-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Pachyptera incarnata plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
