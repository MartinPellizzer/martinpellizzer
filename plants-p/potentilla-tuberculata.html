Potentilla tuberculata

Potentilla tuberculata, commonly known as the rough potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of about 30 centimeters. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and covered in fine, hairy bumps, giving it a rough texture.

Potentilla tuberculata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.
