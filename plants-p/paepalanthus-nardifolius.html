Paepalanthus nardifolius

Paepalanthus nardifolius, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen rosette structure and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of mountainous regions. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, which helps it retain water in arid conditions.

Paepalanthus nardifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice in alpine gardens.
