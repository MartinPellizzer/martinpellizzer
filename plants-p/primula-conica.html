Primula conica

Primula conica, commonly known as the conical primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully expand. Its foliage consists of broad, waxy leaves that are arranged in a tight spiral.

Primula conica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cool, moist environments.
