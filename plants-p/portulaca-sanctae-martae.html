Portulaca sanctae-martae

Portulaca sanctae-martae, commonly known as the Holy Martyr's Purslane, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb with fleshy, succulent leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its succulent nature helps it store water, making it drought-resistant.

The species is often found in rocky soils and along coastal areas, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
