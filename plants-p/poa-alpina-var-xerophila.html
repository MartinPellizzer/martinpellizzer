Poa alpina var. xerophila

Poa alpina var. xerophila, commonly known as the alpine feathergrass, is a variant of the alpine bluegrass that thrives in high-altitude environments.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit, which help it withstand harsh climatic conditions. This plant is typically found in mountainous regions, where it grows in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its ability to survive in low-nutrient, cold climates makes it an important component of alpine ecosystems.

The species is also notable for its resilience to extreme weather and its role in preventing soil erosion in rocky habitats.
