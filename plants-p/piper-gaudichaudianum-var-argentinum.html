Piper gaudichaudianum var. argentinum

Piper gaudichaudianum var. argentinum, commonly known as the Argentine pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is a shrub-like plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and neighboring regions. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a peppery aroma, similar to other members of the pepper family.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has mild culinary uses in some regions.
