Phaseolus carterae

Phaseolus carterae, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers, which are followed by pods containing edible seeds. Its botanical features include compound leaves and a fibrous root system, which contribute to its ability to thrive in various soil conditions.

Phaseolus carterae is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its culinary uses, as the beans are a nutritious food source.
