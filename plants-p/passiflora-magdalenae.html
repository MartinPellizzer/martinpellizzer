Passiflora magdalenae

Passiflora magdalenae, commonly known as the Magdalena passionflower, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that display a mix of blue, purple, and white hues, often with a central crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits that are similar in appearance to other passionfruits. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and climbing.

This species is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
