Pomaderris prunifolia

Pomaderris prunifolia, commonly known as the sweet pepper tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to eastern Australia.

It features aromatic, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, edible, red or orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in coastal and subtropical regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
