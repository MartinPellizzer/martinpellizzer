Piper perpunctulatum

Piper perpunctulatum, commonly known as the spotted pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus in the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white spots, and it produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and scientific study.
