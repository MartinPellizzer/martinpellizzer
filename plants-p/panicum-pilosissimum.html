Panicum pilosissimum

Panicum pilosissimum, commonly known as silky panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fluffy, silver-gray seeds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft, silky texture that contributes to its common name.

Panicum pilosissimum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
