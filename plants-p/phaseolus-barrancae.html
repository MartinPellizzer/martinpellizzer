Phaseolus barrancae

Phaseolus barrancae, commonly known as the Barranca bean, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. It is adapted to grow in dry and warm climates, often found in rocky or disturbed soils.

Due to its limited distribution and genetic uniqueness, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
