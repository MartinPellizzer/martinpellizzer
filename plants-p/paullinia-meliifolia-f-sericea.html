Paullinia meliifolia f. sericea

Paullinia meliifolia f. sericea, commonly known as the silky pataua, is a variant of the pataua plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruits are spherical, fleshy berries that turn red when mature, attracting various bird species.

The silky pataua is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
