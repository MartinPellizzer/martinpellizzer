Pandanus linnaei

Pandanus linnaei, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a member of the Pandanaceae family native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by fibrous, spiky fruits. Its leaves contain aromatic compounds that have been utilized in culinary and medicinal applications.

The species is adapted to coastal and wetland environments, thriving in humid, tropical climates.
