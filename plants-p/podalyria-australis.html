Podalyria australis

Podalyria australis, commonly known as the New Zealand mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. Podalyria australis is adapted to grow in rocky and alpine environments, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
