Picris angustifolia subsp. ohwiana

Picris angustifolia subsp.

ohwiana, commonly known as narrowleaf picris, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features slender, erect stems with alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its bright yellow flowers are arranged in composite inflorescences, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
