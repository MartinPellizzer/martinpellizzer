Prunus malifolia

Prunus malifolia, commonly known as the sour cherry or wild cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, tart red fruits in the summer. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Prunus malifolia is often found in woodland edges and rocky soils, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits.

The species is also used in horticulture for its ornamental value and in traditional medicine.
