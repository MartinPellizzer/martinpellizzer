Plectranthus rutenbergianus

Plectranthus rutenbergianus, commonly known as the Rutenberg's plectranthus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive aromatic scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in horticultural displays.
