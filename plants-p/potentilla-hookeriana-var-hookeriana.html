Potentilla hookeriana var. hookeriana

Potentilla hookeriana var. hookeriana, commonly known as Hooker's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, rounded foliage. The plant produces showy, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and attractive addition to alpine gardens.
