Palala macrocoma

Palala macrocoma, commonly known as the giant palala, is a unique plant species native to the rainforests of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically bright yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
