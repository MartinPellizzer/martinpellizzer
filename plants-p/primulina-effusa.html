Primulina effusa

Primulina effusa, commonly known as the broad-leafed gloxinia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, broad leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil to flourish.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and greenhouse cultivation.
