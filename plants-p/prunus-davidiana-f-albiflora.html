Prunus davidiana f. albiflora

Prunus davidiana f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered David plum, is a rare and ornamental cultivar of the Prunus davidiana species.

It is characterized by its pale white flowers that contrast with its dark green foliage, making it a visually striking plant. The plant is native to China and is typically found in mountainous regions with a temperate climate. It produces small, edible fruits that are similar to cherries but are less commonly consumed by humans.

This cultivar is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
