Pithecellobium peckii

Pithecellobium peckii, commonly known as the Mexican velvetbean, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, showy flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and used in local cuisines. It also has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.

Due to its adaptability, it is often cultivated in agroforestry systems and for forage.
