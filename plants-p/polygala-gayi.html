Polygala gayi

Polygala gayi, commonly known as the yellow mistflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland heathlands. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences that bloom in spring.

Polygala gayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
