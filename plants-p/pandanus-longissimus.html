Pandanus longissimus

Pandanus longissimus, commonly known as the longleaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, which are followed by distinctive, spiky fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional thatching and basket weaving due to their durability.

The species thrives in coastal and wetland environments, showcasing its adaptability to humid, saline conditions.
