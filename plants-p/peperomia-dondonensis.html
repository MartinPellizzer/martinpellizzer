Peperomia dondonensis

Peperomia dondonensis, commonly known as the Dondon peperomia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the humid lowland forests of Ecuador, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant features compact, upright growth with ovate to elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically greenish-white and arranged in spikes, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Peperomia dondonensis is valued in horticulture for its attractive foliage and ease of care in indoor settings.
