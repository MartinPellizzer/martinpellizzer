Phyllanthus microcarpus var. pallidus

Phyllanthus microcarpus var. pallidus, commonly known as the pale-fruited spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features small, simple leaves that are usually opposite or alternate in arrangement. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, fleshy fruits.

The species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in semi-arid conditions.
