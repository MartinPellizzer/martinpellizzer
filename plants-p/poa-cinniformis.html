Poa cinniformis

Poa cinniformis, commonly known as the golden hair grass, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and distinctive golden-brown inflorescence that resembles a crown or halo. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dense, tufted growth habit.

Poa cinniformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
