Potentilla nivea var. quinquefolia

Potentilla nivea var. quinquefolia, commonly known as the white cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, five-lobed leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer. It typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
