Prunus phaeosticta f. lasioclada

Prunus phaeosticta f. lasioclada, commonly known as the silver-leafed cherry, is a unique cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which contrasts with its dark red to black bark. The leaves are typically smaller and more densely packed compared to other Prunus species, giving it a compact appearance. It produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by edible fruits that are often used in jams and preserves.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
