Pilea lacorum

Pilea lacorum, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its rosette of glossy, rounded leaves that resemble coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Pilea lacorum is relatively low maintenance and can be propagated through stem cuttings or by dividing the plant.
