Podolepis inundata

Podolepis inundata, commonly known as the waterpodolepis, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems that often grow close to the ground. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils.

Podolepis inundata plays an important role in maintaining the ecological balance of its wetland habitat.
