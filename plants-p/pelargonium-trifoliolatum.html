Pelargonium trifoliolatum

Pelargonium trifoliolatum, commonly known as the three-leaf geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated as a decorative plant.
