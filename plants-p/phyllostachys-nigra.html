Phyllostachys nigra

Phyllostachys nigra, commonly known as black bamboo, is a species of grass in the Bambusoideae subfamily.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its dark, almost black culms that darken with age. The plant typically grows as a clump-forming perennial, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Black bamboo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant.
