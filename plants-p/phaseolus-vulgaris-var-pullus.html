Phaseolus vulgaris var. pullus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. pullus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a variety of the common bean plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers and long, slender pods that contain edible, kidney-shaped seeds. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and thrives in warm, sunny conditions. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, compound leaves, and it is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

The scarlet runner bean is a popular choice for gardens and small-scale farming due to its hardiness and nutritional value.
