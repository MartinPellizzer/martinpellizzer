Potentilla tabernaemontani proles incisa

Potentilla tabernaemontani proles incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with small, dense leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and serrated, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
