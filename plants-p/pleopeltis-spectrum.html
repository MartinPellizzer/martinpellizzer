Pleopeltis spectrum

Pleopeltis spectrum, commonly known as the silver-veined humpback fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, silver-green leaves with prominent, contrasting veins. The fronds are typically arched and can grow up to 1 meter in length, making it a striking addition to shaded, humid environments. It thrives in tropical climates and requires high humidity and indirect light for optimal growth.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in indoor and outdoor gardens.
