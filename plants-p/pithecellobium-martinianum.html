Pithecellobium martinianum

Pithecellobium martinianum, commonly known as the Martin's acacia, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has bipinnate leaves with small, ovate leaflets, and it produces yellow to orange flowers that are fragrant and attractive to pollinators. Its fruit is a legume that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in diverse soil conditions.
