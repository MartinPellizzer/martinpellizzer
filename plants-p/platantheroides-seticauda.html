Platantheroides seticauda

Platantheroides seticauda, commonly known as the hairy tongue orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a single, showy flower that has a distinctive, hairy appearance on its petals and sepals. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

The species is noted for its delicate beauty and ecological significance in its native habitats.
