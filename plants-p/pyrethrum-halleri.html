Pyrethrum halleri

Pyrethrum halleri, commonly known as the alpine pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Pyrethrum halleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its mild insect-repelling properties.
