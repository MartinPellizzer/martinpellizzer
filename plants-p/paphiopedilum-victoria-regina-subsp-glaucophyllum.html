Paphiopedilum victoria-regina subsp. glaucophyllum

Paphiopedilum victoria-regina subsp.

glaucophyllum, commonly known as the Victoria Queen's Blue Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of China. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive blue-violet hue and a unique pouch-shaped lip.

The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, preferring cool, humid environments with well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of broad, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

This subspecies is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity in cultivation.
