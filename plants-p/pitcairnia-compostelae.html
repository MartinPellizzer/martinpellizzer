Pitcairnia compostelae

Pitcairnia compostelae, commonly known as the Holy Land pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage that forms a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright red bracts that contrast with its dark green leaves. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Pitcairnia compostelae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its unique appearance and resilience.
