Phlox longifolia

Phlox longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered phlox, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Phlox longifolia is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
