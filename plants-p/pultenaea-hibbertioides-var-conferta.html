Pultenaea hibbertioides var. conferta

Pultenaea hibbertioides var. conferta, commonly known as the dense pultenaea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, which provides habitat for various small animals and insects. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a distinctive blue-green hue that adds visual interest to the landscape.

This variety is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.
