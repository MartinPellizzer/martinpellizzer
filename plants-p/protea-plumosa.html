Protea plumosa

Protea plumosa, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, fluffy, white to pale pink inflorescence that resembles a plume, giving it its common name. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as birds and insects.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
