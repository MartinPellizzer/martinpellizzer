Pleurothallis rostriflora

Pleurothallis rostriflora, commonly known as the rostriflorous orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically arranged in racemes. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and decaying organic matter.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
