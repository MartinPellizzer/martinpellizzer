Paspalum echinotrichum

Paspalum echinotrichum, commonly known as the prickly paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture and small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or coastal soils and is often found in dune environments.

Paspalum echinotrichum is adapted to saline and arid conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh coastal climates.
