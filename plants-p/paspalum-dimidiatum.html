Paspalum dimidiatum

Paspalum dimidiatum, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and marshes. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Paspalum dimidiatum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
