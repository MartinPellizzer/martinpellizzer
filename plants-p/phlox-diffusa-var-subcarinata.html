Phlox diffusa var. subcarinata

Phlox diffusa var. subcarinata, commonly known as the spreading phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems that produce clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized landscapes.
