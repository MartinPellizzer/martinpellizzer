Peucedanum officinale f. italicum

Peucedanum officinale f. italicum, commonly known as Italian cow parsley, is a biennial or perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, slightly bitter leaves and stems, which contain compounds such as apiol and limonene. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The species is valued in herbal medicine for its potential digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
