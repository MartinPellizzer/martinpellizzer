Primula cusickiana

Primula cusickiana, commonly known as the Cusick's primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, and the plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Primula cusickiana is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
