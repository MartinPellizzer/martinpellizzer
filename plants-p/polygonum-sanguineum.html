Polygonum sanguineum

Polygonum sanguineum, commonly known as bloodweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-tinged stems and heart-shaped leaves that often have a waxy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found near water bodies.

Polygonum sanguineum is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine.
