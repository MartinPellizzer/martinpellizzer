Poa versicolor subsp. reverdattoi

Poa versicolor subsp.

reverdattoi, commonly known as the variable bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences.

The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a blue-green hue that gives it its common name.

The species is noted for its ecological value, providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
