Papaver chankheliense

Papaver chankheliense, commonly known as the Georgian poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red petals that resemble those of other poppy species. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.

Botanically, it belongs to the Papaveraceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
