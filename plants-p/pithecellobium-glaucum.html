Pithecellobium glaucum

Pithecellobium glaucum, commonly known as the silverleaf acacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features compound leaves with slender, pale green leaflets that give it its characteristic silvery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing abilities, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
