Paepalanthus inundatus

Paepalanthus inundatus, commonly known as the water lily of the Amazon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that form a dense mat on the water surface, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces striking white or pink flowers that bloom above the water, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the water, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-rich, slow-moving freshwater environments.

Paepalanthus inundatus plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat by supporting biodiversity and contributing to water filtration.
