Populus wilsonii f. pedicellata

Populus wilsonii f. pedicellata, commonly known as the pedicellate poplar, is a botanical variant of the poplar tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive pedicellate (stalked) flowers, which set it apart from other poplar species. The plant exhibits deciduous foliage with heart-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
