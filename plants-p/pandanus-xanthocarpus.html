Pandanus xanthocarpus

Pandanus xanthocarpus, commonly known as the yellow fruit pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces clusters of yellow, spiky fruits that are edible and used in local cuisines. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to salt spray and sandy soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
