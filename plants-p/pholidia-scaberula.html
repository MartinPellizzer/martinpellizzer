Pholidia scaberula

Pholidia scaberula, commonly known as the rough pholidia, is a small shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, elliptical leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

Pholidia scaberula is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in coastal and arid environments.
