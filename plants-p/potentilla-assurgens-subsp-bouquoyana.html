Potentilla assurgens subsp. bouquoyana

Potentilla assurgens subsp.

bouquoyana, commonly known as the Bouquoy's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically arranged in clusters.

The plant has wiry, branched stems and narrow, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
