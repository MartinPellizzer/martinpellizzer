Platypodium grandiflorum

Platypodium grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are alternate and simple, with a prominent midrib. Its bark is smooth and greyish, becoming fissured with age.

Platypodium grandiflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow up to 20 meters tall in its natural habitat.
