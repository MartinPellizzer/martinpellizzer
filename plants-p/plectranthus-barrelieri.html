Plectranthus barrelieri

Plectranthus barrelieri, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant is a succulent, meaning it stores water in its leaves and stems, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Plectranthus barrelieri is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
