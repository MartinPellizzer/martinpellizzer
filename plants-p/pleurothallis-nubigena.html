Pleurothallis nubigena

Pleurothallis nubigena, commonly known as the cloud-veiled orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude cloud forests of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with intricate patterns. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a rosette shape, aiding in moisture retention.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within the orchid family.
