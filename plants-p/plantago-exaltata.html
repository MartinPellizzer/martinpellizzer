Plantago exaltata

Plantago exaltata, commonly known as tall plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems that bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.
