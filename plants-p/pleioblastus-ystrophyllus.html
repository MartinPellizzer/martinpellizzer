Pleioblastus ystrophyllus

Pleioblastus ystrophyllus, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation, it is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant in gardens.
