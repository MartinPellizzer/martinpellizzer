Pandanus purpurascens

Pandanus purpurascens, commonly known as the crimson pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and broad, sword-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple hues. The plant produces large, branched inflorescences that bear fragrant, yellowish flowers. Its fibrous leaves are used in traditional crafts and thatching.

The species is well adapted to coastal and wetland environments, thriving in humid, saline conditions.
