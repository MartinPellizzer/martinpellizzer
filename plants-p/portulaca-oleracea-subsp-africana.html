Portulaca oleracea subsp. africana

Portulaca oleracea subsp.

africana, commonly known as African purslane, is a succulent plant native to Africa and parts of the Middle East. It features fleshy, erect stems and leaves that are often reddish or greenish in color.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. It is adapted to arid environments and can thrive in poor soil conditions.

African purslane is often used in local cuisines and has medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
