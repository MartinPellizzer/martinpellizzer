Pilocereus williamsii

Pilocereus williamsii, commonly known as the night blooming cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines and its ability to produce large, fragrant white flowers at night. The plant is part of the Cactaceae family and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers open in the evening and close by morning, attracting nocturnal pollinators like bats and moths.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
