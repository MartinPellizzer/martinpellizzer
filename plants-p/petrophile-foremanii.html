Petrophile foremanii

Petrophile foremanii, commonly known as the Western Grey Box, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its grey, rough, and flaky bark, which provides a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, typically during the cooler months of spring and autumn.

Petrophile foremanii is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and near-coastal areas.
