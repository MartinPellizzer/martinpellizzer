Phaseolus pardus

Phaseolus pardus, commonly known as the wild kidney bean, is a species native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy.

Phaseolus pardus is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
