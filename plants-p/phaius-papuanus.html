Phaius papuanus

Phaius papuanus, commonly known as the Papuan marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Guinea and nearby islands.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and thrives in humid, tropical climates. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Phaius papuanus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
