Piper pinuna-negroense

Piper pinuna-negroense, commonly known as the black negro pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, dark green leaves. It produces small, clustered flowers that are followed by the characteristic peppercorn-like berries.

The berries are typically black when mature and are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some local cuisines.
