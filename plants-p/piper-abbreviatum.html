Piper abbreviatum

Piper abbreviatum, commonly known as the short-stemmed pepper plant, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that mature to a dark purple color.

Piper abbreviatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
