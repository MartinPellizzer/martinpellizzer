Polygonum viviparum monstr. paniculatum

Polygonum viviparum monstr.

paniculatum, commonly known as the panicled knotweed, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its robust growth and distinctive inflorescence. It features upright stems that are often reddish in color and covered with fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicle-like clusters, which are borne on long, branching stems. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a waxy texture.

This species is typically found in moist habitats such as wet meadows and streambanks, where it thrives in partially shaded to sunny conditions.
