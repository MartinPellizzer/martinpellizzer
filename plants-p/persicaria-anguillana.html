Persicaria anguillana

Persicaria anguillana, commonly known as water hemp, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, often with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on elongated spikes. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Persicaria anguillana is adapted to moist or aquatic habitats and is often found in marshes, ditches, and along the edges of ponds.
