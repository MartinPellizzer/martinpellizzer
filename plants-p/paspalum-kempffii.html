Paspalum kempffii

Paspalum kempffii, commonly known as the Kempff's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Paspalum kempffii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in both wet and dry conditions.
