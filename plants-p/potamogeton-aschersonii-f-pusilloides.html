Potamogeton aschersonii f. pusilloides

Potamogeton aschersonii f. pusilloides, commonly known as the small-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Potamogetonaceae and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its morphological features, including reduced leaf size and compact inflorescences, distinguish it from other species within the genus Potamogeton.
