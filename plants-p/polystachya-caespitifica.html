Polystachya caespitifica

Polystachya caespitifica, commonly known as the tufted flame orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and produces striking, brightly colored inflorescences that resemble flames. The plant features strap-like leaves and has a rhizomatous root system, which helps it thrive in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in forest understories and requires high humidity and indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
