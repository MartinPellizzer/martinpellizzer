Porroglossum porphyreum

Porroglossum porphyreum, commonly known as the purpleleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking purple foliage and yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its flower heads are arranged in clusters.

Porroglossum porphyreum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
