Prunus cercocarpifolia

Prunus cercocarpifolia, commonly known as the red sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by small, red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for landscaping.
