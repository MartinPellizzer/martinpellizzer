Petastoma bradeanum

Petastoma bradeanum, commonly known as the Bradean sunflower, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, erect stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
