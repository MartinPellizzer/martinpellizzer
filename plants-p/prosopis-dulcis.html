Prosopis dulcis

Prosopis dulcis, commonly known as the carob tree, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and pods that contain sweet, edible seeds. The tree is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates. Its deep root system allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

The plant also has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, contributing to soil fertility.
