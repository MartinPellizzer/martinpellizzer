Pholidia oppositifolia

Pholidia oppositifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its opposite, narrow leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, hard seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry woodlands.

Pholidia oppositifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its resilience in arid environments.
