Piper decurtispicum

Piper decurtispicum, commonly known as the short-stemmed pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow and are borne in clusters, while the fruit is a small, three-lobed berry.

Piper decurtispicum is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local herbal medicine.
