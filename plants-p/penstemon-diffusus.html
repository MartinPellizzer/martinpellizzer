Penstemon diffusus

Penstemon diffusus, commonly known as the spreading beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
