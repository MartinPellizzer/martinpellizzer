Piper yinkiangense

Piper yinkiangense, commonly known as the Yinkiang pepper vine, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Vietnam and China. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by elongated, fleshy fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
