Primula cinerascens subsp. sylvicola

Primula cinerascens subsp.

sylvicola, commonly known as the woodland primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of East Asia. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth and rocky slopes.

The plant features oval, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with five petals. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators in woodland ecosystems.
