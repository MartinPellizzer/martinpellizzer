Polygonum purpuratum

Polygonum purpuratum, commonly known as the purple knotweed, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it often grows in moist, shady environments. It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other species like buckwheat and sorrel.

Polygonum purpuratum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
