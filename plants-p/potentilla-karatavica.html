Potentilla karatavica

Potentilla karatavica, commonly known as the Karatavica cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its compact, upright form. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided into five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla karatavica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.
