Parinari capensis subsp. latifolia

Parinari capensis subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf parinari, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stems.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

The plant is also valued for its timber and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
