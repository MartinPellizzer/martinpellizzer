Phyllanthus pedicellatus

Phyllanthus pedicellatus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough, textured surface.

The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
