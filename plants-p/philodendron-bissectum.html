Philodendron bissectum

Philodendron bissectum, commonly known as the split-leaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, deeply lobed, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length. The plant has a robust root system and can climb using aerial roots that attach to surfaces. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its striking appearance.
