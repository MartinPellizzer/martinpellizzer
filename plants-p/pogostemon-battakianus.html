Pogostemon battakianus

Pogostemon battakianus, commonly known as the Batak mint, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color.

Pogostemon battakianus is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
