Piper cuspidatum

Piper cuspidatum, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other species known for their aromatic properties. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into dark blue berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and are also a source of capsaicin, similar to chili peppers.
