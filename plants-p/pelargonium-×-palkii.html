Pelargonium × palkii

Pelargonium × palkii, commonly known as the African geranium, is a hybrid plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with multiple stems and can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
