Passiflora cuspidifolia

Passiflora cuspidifolia, commonly known as the sharp-pointed passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora cuspidifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
