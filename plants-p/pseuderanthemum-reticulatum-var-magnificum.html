Pseuderanthemum reticulatum var. magnificum

Pseuderanthemum reticulatum var. magnificum, commonly known as the magnificent nettleleaf, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy, opposite leaves. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a serrated margin and a pubescent surface. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by small, round berries.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
