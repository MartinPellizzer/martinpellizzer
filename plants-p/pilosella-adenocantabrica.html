Pilosella adenocantabrica

Pilosella adenocantabrica, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are hairy and spoon-shaped, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Pilosella adenocantabrica is often found at elevations above 1,500 meters, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
