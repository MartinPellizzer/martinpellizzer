Phlox bifida subsp. stellaria

Phlox bifida subsp.

stellaria, commonly known as starflower, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its showy, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white.

The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It is a perennial species that produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
