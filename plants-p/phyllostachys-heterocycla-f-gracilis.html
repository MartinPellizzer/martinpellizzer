Phyllostachys heterocycla f. gracilis

Phyllostachys heterocycla f. gracilis, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a variant of the giant bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, gracefully arching culms that often exhibit a silvery-blue sheen due to the dense, fine hairs on its surface. This ornamental bamboo thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure. Its rapid growth rate makes it suitable for erosion control and as a living fence.

However, it requires regular pruning to maintain its shape and prevent it from becoming invasive.
