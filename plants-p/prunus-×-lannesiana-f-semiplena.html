Prunus × lannesiana f. semiplena

Prunus × lannesiana f. semiplena, commonly known as the double-flowered Japanese cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus characterized by its distinctive floral morphology.

This plant is a hybrid between Prunus lannesiana and Prunus × yedoensis, exhibiting semi-double flowers with numerous petals that create a dense, showy bloom. It is typically deciduous, shedding its leaves in autumn, and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant's ornamental value lies in its elegant, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a notable example of floral evolution in cultivated cherry species.
