Physostegia veroniciformis

Physostegia veroniciformis, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features showy, tubular purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet.

Physostegia veroniciformis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
