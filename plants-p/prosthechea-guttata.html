Prosthechea guttata

Prosthechea guttata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers with a distinctive yellow and purple coloration. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks. Its flowers are typically arranged in a raceme and bloom during the rainy season.

Prosthechea guttata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
