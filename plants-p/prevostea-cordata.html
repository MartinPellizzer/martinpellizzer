Prevostea cordata

Prevostea cordata, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Prevostea cordata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
