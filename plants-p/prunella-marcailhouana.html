Prunella marcailhouana

Prunella marcailhouana, commonly known as the French deadnettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Prunella marcailhouana is often found growing in clusters and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
