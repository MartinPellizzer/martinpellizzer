Phyllanthus ventuarii

Phyllanthus ventuarii, commonly known as the desert date or Indian date tree, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and South Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fruit that resembles dates in appearance. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots and a slow growth rate.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
