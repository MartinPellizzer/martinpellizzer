Puya mitis

Puya mitis, commonly known as the Andean torch cactus, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Arecaceae, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, showy inflorescences. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. Puya mitis is adapted to harsh, cold environments and can survive in poor soil conditions.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
