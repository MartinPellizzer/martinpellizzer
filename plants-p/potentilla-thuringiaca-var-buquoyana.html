Potentilla thuringiaca var. buquoyana

Potentilla thuringiaca var. buquoyana, commonly known as the Thuringian cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, aggregate fruits known as achenes, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
