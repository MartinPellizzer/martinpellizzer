Passiflora hatschbachii

Passiflora hatschbachii, commonly known as the Schomburgk's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with red filaments that resemble threads. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tangy flavor. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded, moist forest understories.

Passiflora hatschbachii is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
