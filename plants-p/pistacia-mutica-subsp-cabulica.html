Pistacia mutica subsp. cabulica

Pistacia mutica subsp.

cabulica, commonly known as the Afghan pistachio, is a subspecies native to regions of Central and South Asia. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves. Its fruit is a drupe that contains a single seed, which is the edible nut known for its rich flavor and nutritional value.

This subspecies is valued for its economic importance in the production of pistachios.
