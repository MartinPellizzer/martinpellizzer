Peperomia obovata

Peperomia obovata, commonly known as the wax plant or velvetleaf peperomia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in shaded, humid environments. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically oval to obovate in shape, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or red, and it is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Peperomia obovata is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
