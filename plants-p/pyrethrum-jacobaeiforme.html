Pyrethrum jacobaeiforme

Pyrethrum jacobaeiforme, commonly known as the Jacob's weed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

The flowers are rich in pyrethrins, which are natural insecticides used in organic pest control.
