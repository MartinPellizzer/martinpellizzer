Prasophyllum cuneatum

Prasophyllum cuneatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved leek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or loamy soils within open woodlands or grasslands. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a single, narrow, grass-like leaf. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in a spike, often with a slight purple tinge.

Prasophyllum cuneatum is adapted to thrive in dry conditions and is often found in areas with low nutrient soils.
