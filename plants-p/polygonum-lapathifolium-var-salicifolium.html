Polygonum lapathifolium var. salicifolium

Polygonum lapathifolium var. salicifolium, commonly known as willow-leaf smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, often branching near the top. The leaves are lanceolate to ovate, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin, and are typically green with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in pink or reddish hues, which appear in late summer.

It thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
