Panicum pseudopubescens

Panicum pseudopubescens, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are small, bract-bearing flowering structures.

The species is characterized by its fine, pubescent texture and is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
