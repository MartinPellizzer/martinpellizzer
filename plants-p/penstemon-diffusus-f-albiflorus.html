Penstemon diffusus f. albiflorus

Penstemon diffusus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered penstemon, is a variant of the diffuse penstemon.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States, typically found in rocky or gravelly habitats. The plant features upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its distinguishing characteristic is the production of large, showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The flowers are tubular in shape and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
