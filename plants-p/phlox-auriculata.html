Phlox auriculata

Phlox auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate phlox, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens during that time.
