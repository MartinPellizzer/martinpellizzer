Pterospermadendron involucratum

Pterospermadendron involucratum, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces small, woody fruits containing several seeds.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
