Piper brachyrhachis

Piper brachyrhachis, commonly known as the short-stemmed pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into peppery fruits. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a distinctive aroma.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
