Phanera rubra

Phanera rubra, commonly known as the red-flowered phanera, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has palmate leaves with a velvety texture, and its stems are often woody and branched. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Phanera rubra is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
