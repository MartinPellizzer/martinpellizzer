Paysonia

Paysonia, commonly known as the prairie smoke, is a genus of flowering plants native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its unique, feathery seed pods that resemble smoke. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. Paysonia species are often found in the central and western United States.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
