Pisum sativum var. reginae

Pisum sativum var. reginae, commonly known as the Queen's pea, is a cultivated variety of the common pea.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, often variegated foliage. The plant produces pods that are typically shorter and more rounded compared to standard peas, with a sweeter flavor. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and edible pods.
