Poa roxburghiana

Poa roxburghiana, commonly known as Himalayan bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and forms dense, low-growing tussocks. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Poa roxburghiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
