Paraserianthes falcataria subsp. solomonensis

Paraserianthes falcataria subsp.

solomonensis, commonly known as Solomon Islands ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to the Pacific Islands. It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and can grow up to 30 meters in height.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and lanceolate, while the flowers are small and yellowish-green. It produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its hardiness and is often used in reforestation and timber production.
