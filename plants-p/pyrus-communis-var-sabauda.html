Pyrus communis var. sabauda

Pyrus communis var. sabauda, commonly known as the Sabauda pear, is a variety of the common pear tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its small, round, and often yellow or yellow-green fruits with a tender, juicy flesh. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree with broad, lobed leaves and produces white flowers in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, making it a popular choice in gardens and orchards.
