Phoenix dactylifera

Phoenix dactylifera, commonly known as the date palm, is a flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa. The plant produces clusters of sweet, fleshy fruits called dates, which are rich in sugars and nutrients. Its trunk is a thick, fibrous stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions.

The date palm is also valued for its ornamental appearance and historical significance in human culture.
