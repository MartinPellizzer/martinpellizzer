Physalis chenopodiifolia var. viridis

Physalis chenopodiifolia var. viridis, commonly known as the greenleaf gooseberry, is a variant of the physalis genus characterized by its distinctive green foliage.

It is a shrub-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries enclosed in a papery calyx, similar to other physalis species. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in dry environments.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.
