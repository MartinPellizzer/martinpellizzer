Piper mollipilosum

Piper mollipilosum, commonly known as the hairy pipevine, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are covered in fine, hairy structures. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

Piper mollipilosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
