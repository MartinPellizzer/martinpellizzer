Polygala britteniana var. phyllostigma

Polygala britteniana var. phyllostigma, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and sometimes opposite, while the flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow with distinctive, spurred petals.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
