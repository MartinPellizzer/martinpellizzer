Pavonia muricata var. punicea

Pavonia muricata var. punicea, commonly known as the scarlet pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to purple bracts that surround its small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
