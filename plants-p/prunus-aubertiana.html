Prunus aubertiana

Prunus aubertiana, commonly known as the Aubertian cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible drupes that are typically red or yellow in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while its bark is smooth and grayish in appearance.

Prunus aubertiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for various health benefits.
