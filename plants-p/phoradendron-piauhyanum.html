Phoradendron piauhyanum

Phoradendron piauhyanum, commonly known as the Piauhya mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant belonging to the Loranthaceae family.

It attaches itself to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus *Spondias*, through its haustoria, which extract water and nutrients. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers and red berries that are dispersed by birds. It is native to parts of Central America and the Caribbean.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecological interactions in its native habitat.
