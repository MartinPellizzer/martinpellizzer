Primula poissonii subsp. angustidens

Primula poissonii subsp.

angustidens, commonly known as the narrowleaf primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China. It features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded gardens. It has a fibrous root system and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom in early spring.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to other Primula species.
