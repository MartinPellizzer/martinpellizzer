Pycreus malabaricus

Pycreus malabaricus, commonly known as the Malabar spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its milky sap, which is a common feature among members of this family. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has alternate, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, which are modified inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
