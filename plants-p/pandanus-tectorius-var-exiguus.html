Pandanus tectorius var. exiguus

Pandanus tectorius var. exiguus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and cooking. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are aromatic and contain essential oils, which contribute to its use in both culinary and cultural practices.

The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove areas or near freshwater sources.
