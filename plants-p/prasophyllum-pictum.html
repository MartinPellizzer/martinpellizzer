Prasophyllum pictum

Prasophyllum pictum, commonly known as the painted leek orchid, is an Australian native plant belonging to the orchid family.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescence that resembles a leek, with vivid purple and white petals. The plant typically grows in heathland and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. It is a perennial herb with a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves.

Prasophyllum pictum is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
