Picris bompieyrei

Picris bompieyrei, commonly known as the African buttonweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Picris bompieyrei is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in dry to moderately moist conditions.
