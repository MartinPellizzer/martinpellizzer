Psidium celastroides

Psidium celastroides, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow when ripe, containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
