Pilosella kalksburgensis subsp. almonica

Pilosella kalksburgensis subsp.

almonica, commonly known as the Almonica hawk's-beard, is a small perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with a distinctive, woolly texture.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
