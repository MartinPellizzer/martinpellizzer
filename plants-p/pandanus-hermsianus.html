Pandanus hermsianus

Pandanus hermsianus, commonly known as the Herm's pandanus, is a species of palm native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible fruits, which are an important food source for local communities. Its adaptability to coastal and wetland environments makes it a common sight in mangrove ecosystems.

The plant's resilience to saltwater and wind contributes to its ecological significance in tropical regions.
