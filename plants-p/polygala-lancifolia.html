Polygala lancifolia

Polygala lancifolia, commonly known as the lancifoliate milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hence the species name, and it produces yellow or greenish flowers that bloom in summer.

Polygala lancifolia is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in dry gardens.
