Pinanga modesta

Pinanga modesta, commonly known as the modest bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

Pinanga modesta is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
