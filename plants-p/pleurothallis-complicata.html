Pleurothallis complicata

Pleurothallis complicata, commonly known as the twisted pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often twisted floral structures. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, which bloom during the rainy season. Pleurothallis complicata is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
