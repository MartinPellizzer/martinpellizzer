Pinus montana subsp. frutescens-erecta

Pinus montana subsp.

frutescens-erecta, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada whitebark pine, is a subspecies of the whitebark pine native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and short, twisted branches.

The needles are typically 5 to 8 cm long, and the cones are small, woody, and often persist on the tree for several years. This subspecies thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in open, sunny areas with well-drained soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
