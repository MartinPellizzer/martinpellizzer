Piper novae-hollandiae

Piper novae-hollandiae, commonly known as the New Holland pepper, is a climbing vine native to Australia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, fleshy berries. Its leaves are typically ovate with a glossy surface and a strong, peppery aroma.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in indigenous Australian medicine.
