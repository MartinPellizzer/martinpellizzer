Phlox pungens

Phlox pungens, commonly known as the firecracker flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

Phlox pungens is well-suited for rock gardens and borders due to its compact growth habit and vibrant floral display.
