Pyrus torminalis var. mollis

Pyrus torminalis var. mollis, commonly known as the soft-leaf pear, is a variant of the European pear species.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for temperate climates.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild-flavored fruit.
