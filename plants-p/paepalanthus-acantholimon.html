Paepalanthus acantholimon

Paepalanthus acantholimon, commonly known as the spiny leaf cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its robust, spiny stems and fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its unique morphology and adaptation to dry climates make it an interesting subject for botanical study.

It is often cultivated in succulent gardens for its ornamental value and resilience.
