Phyllanthus salicifolius

Phyllanthus salicifolius, commonly known as the willow-leaf phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, willow-like leaves that are alternate and often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruits are capsules that contain several seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Phyllanthus salicifolius is also noted for its medicinal uses in traditional systems of medicine.
