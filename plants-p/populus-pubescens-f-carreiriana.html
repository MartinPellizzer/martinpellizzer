Populus pubescens f. carreiriana

Populus pubescens f. carreiriana, commonly known as the Carreiriana poplar, is a botanical variant of the poplar tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive grayish-brown bark and heart-shaped leaves with a fuzzy underside. This form exhibits rapid growth and is often found in riparian zones, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in catkins, which are typical of the Salicaceae family.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in stabilizing riverbanks.
