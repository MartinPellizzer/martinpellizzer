Polygonum axillare

Polygonum axillare, commonly known as the axillary knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in high-altitude environments.
