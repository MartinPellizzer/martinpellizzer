Pleurothallis josephensis var. papillifera

Pleurothallis josephensis var. papillifera, commonly known as the Joseph's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, pale green to white flowers with a subtle fragrance. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a short, hairy stem.

This variety is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
