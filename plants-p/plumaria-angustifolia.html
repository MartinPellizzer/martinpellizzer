Plumaria angustifolia

Plumaria angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf plumtree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to yellow or red as it matures.
