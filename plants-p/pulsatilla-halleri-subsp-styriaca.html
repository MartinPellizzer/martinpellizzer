Pulsatilla halleri subsp. styriaca

Pulsatilla halleri subsp.

styriaca, commonly known as the Styrian pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Central Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, purple-pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has a short, thick rhizome and basal leaves that are palmately divided. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and protected in its natural habitats.
