Platycladus orientalis subsp. chengii

Platycladus orientalis subsp.

chengii, commonly known as the Cheng's Chinese tallowtree, is a subspecies native to China. It is a deciduous tree characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, scale-like leaves.

The plant produces yellowish-green to yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, fleshy fruits that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
