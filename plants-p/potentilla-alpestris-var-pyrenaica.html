Potentilla alpestris var. pyrenaica

Potentilla alpestris var. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough texture that helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous areas.
