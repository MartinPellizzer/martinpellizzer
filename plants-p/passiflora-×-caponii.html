Passiflora × caponii

Passiflora × caponii, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a hybrid species native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with intricate patterns of red and white. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in juices and desserts. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

Passiflora × caponii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
