Pouteria pisquiensis

Pouteria pisquiensis, commonly known as the Pichincha wax palm, is a species of palm native to Ecuador.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by oval, woody fruits. The fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a hard, woody coat.

This plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
