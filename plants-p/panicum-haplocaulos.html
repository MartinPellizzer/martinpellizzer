Panicum haplocaulos

Panicum haplocaulos, commonly known as slender panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are linear and often have a bluish-green hue, and the flowers are wind-pollinated.

Panicum haplocaulos plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
