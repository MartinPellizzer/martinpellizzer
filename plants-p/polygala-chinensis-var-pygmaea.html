Polygala chinensis var. pygmaea

Polygala chinensis var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf Chinese milkvetch, is a small, herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers, which are often pink or purple, bloom in late summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
