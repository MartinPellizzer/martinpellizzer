Phlox pilosa subsp. pilosa

Phlox pilosa subsp.

pilosa, commonly known as the woolly phlox, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. It is a perennial herbaceous plant that produces erect stems and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both ornamental and ecological value.
