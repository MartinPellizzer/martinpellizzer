Physostegia speciosa

Physostegia speciosa, commonly known as the whorled loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in shades of pink to purple. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Physostegia speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
