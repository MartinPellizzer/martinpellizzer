Phyllanthus nitidulus

Phyllanthus nitidulus, commonly known as the shining leaf plant, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.

Additionally, it has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
