Puya coarctata

Puya coarctata, commonly known as the narrowleaf puya, is a striking plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the bromeliad family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that can reach several meters in height. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom sequentially along the stem. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a rosette at the base, and it is adapted to grow in high-altitude, rocky environments.

Puya coarctata is noted for its unique appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
