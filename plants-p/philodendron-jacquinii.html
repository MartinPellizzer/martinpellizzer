Philodendron jacquinii

Philodendron jacquinii, commonly known as the golden phantom plant, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that often feature a yellow or white variegation pattern. The plant has a thick, waxy stem and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by red berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires regular watering to maintain its vibrant foliage.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
