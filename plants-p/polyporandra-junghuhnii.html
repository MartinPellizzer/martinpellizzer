Polyporandra junghuhnii

Polyporandra junghuhnii, commonly known as the Junghuhn's polyporus, is a member of the family Polyporaceae, characterized by its bracket-shaped fruiting bodies.

It is a saprophytic fungus that decomposes dead wood, playing a vital role in nutrient cycling within forest ecosystems. The fungus typically grows on decaying hardwoods, particularly in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are released from the undersurface of the basidia, dispersing via wind to colonize new substrates.

The structure of its pores and the coloration of its basidiomes are key features used in its identification.
