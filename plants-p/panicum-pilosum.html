Panicum pilosum

Panicum pilosum, commonly known as silky panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silky-textured leaves and dense, fluffy seed heads that resemble small clouds. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Panicum pilosum is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ecological value.
