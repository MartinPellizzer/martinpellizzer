Podalyria pedunculata var. minor

Podalyria pedunculata var. minor, commonly known as the lesser clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
