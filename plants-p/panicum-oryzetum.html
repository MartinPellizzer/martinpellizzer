Panicum oryzetum

Panicum oryzetum, commonly known as ricegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the warm temperate and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often used in ornamental landscaping. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and is adapted to well-drained soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for erosion control and wildlife habitat.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and shelter for various birds and small mammals.
