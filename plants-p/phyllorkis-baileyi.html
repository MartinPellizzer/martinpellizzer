Phyllorkis baileyi

Phyllorkis baileyi, commonly known as the slender rock heath, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is often found in coastal and inland regions, thriving in full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
