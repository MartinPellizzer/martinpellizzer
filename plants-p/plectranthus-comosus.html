Plectranthus comosus

Plectranthus comosus, commonly known as the purple verbena or African verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

Plectranthus comosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
