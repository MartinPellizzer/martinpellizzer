Pterocaulon virgatum f. alopecuroides

Pterocaulon virgatum f. alopecuroides, commonly known as the woolly pterocaulon, is a rare and distinctive succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features dense, woolly foliage that gives it a unique, almost fuzzy appearance. The leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps the plant conserve water in its arid habitat. It produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, adding a subtle ornamental value.

Due to its limited distribution and specific environmental requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized succulent gardens.
