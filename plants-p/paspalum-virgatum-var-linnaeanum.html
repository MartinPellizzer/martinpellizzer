Paspalum virgatum var. linnaeanum

Paspalum virgatum var. linnaeanum, commonly known as the slender paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, and thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
