Protasparagus acerosus

Protasparagus acerosus, commonly known as the hardstem asparagus, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rigid, succulent stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is cultivated in some regions for landscaping purposes.
