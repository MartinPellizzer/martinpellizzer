Pyrus nobilis

Pyrus nobilis, commonly known as the Bartlett pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It produces sweet, juicy fruits that are widely cultivated for their culinary and commercial value. The plant has compound leaves with a serrated margin and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring. Its fruit develops from these flowers and typically ripens in the fall.

The tree is valued for its adaptability to various climates and its economic importance in agriculture.
