Phaseolus ellipticus var. pictus

Phaseolus ellipticus var. pictus, commonly known as the painted kidney bean, is a species native to Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, mottled, and often colorful seed coat, which can range from green to red, brown, and even speckled patterns. The plant is an annual legume that produces small, yellow flowers followed by kidney-shaped pods. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being elliptical in shape.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes grown for its unique seed characteristics.
