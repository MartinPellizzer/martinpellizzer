Pyrus chamaemespilus proles sudetica

Pyrus chamaemespilus proles sudetica, commonly known as the Sudetic whitebeam, is a subspecies of the common whitebeam.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub native to Central and Eastern Europe, particularly the Sudetes mountain range. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. Its flowers are inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, edible berries that are yellowish in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often found in woodland habitats.
