Passiflora baileyana

Passiflora baileyana, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces blue or black berries that are edible and contain numerous seeds. Its rapid growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though it can become invasive in some areas.

Passiflora baileyana is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
