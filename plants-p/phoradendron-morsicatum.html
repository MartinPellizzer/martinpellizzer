Phoradendron morsicatum

Phoradendron morsicatum, commonly known as the parasitic mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the Americas.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis using its small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces sticky seeds that are dispersed by birds, which consume the fleshy aril surrounding the seed. Its growth can weaken the host tree over time, potentially leading to its death.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, contributing to the ecological dynamics of its host environments.
