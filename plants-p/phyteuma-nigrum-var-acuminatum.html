Phyteuma nigrum var. acuminatum

Phyteuma nigrum var. acuminatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, dark purple to black flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. It is part of the Onagraceae family, which includes other nightshade-related plants.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
