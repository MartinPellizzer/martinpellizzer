Phyllodes hieroglyphica

Phyllodes hieroglyphica, commonly known as the Egyptian flame tree, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of Egypt and Sudan.

It is characterized by its large, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in shades of red and orange, making it a visually dramatic species. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark that provides structural support. Its leaves are modified into phyllodes, which are flattened, leaf-like structures adapted for water conservation in its harsh environment.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
