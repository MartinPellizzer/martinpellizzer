Polygonum lapathifolium var. lanigerum

Polygonum lapathifolium var. lanigerum, commonly known as the woolly smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems covered in fine, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from pink to red in color. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along streambanks.

This species is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
