Potentilla olchonensis

Potentilla olchonensis, commonly known as the Mongolian cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are important for local wildlife.

Potentilla olchonensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
