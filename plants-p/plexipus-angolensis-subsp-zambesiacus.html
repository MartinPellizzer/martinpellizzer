Plexipus angolensis subsp. zambesiacus

Plexipus angolensis subsp.

zambesiacus, commonly known as the Zambesi plexipus, is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. It is native to southern Africa, particularly found in countries like Zambia and Zimbabwe.

The plant typically grows in woodland and savanna habitats, adapting to seasonal rainfall patterns. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to purple.

The subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity and soil stabilization.
