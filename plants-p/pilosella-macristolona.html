Pilosella macristolona

Pilosella macristolona, commonly known as the Macristolona hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pilosella macristolona is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.
