Philadelphus coronarius var. aureus

Philadelphus coronarius var. aureus, commonly known as the golden weigela, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to yellow, often with a pinkish tinge, blooming in early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and attractive foliage throughout the growing season.
