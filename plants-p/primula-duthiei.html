Primula duthiei

Primula duthiei, commonly known as the Duthie's primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing clusters of pink or white flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
