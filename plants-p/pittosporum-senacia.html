Pittosporum senacia

Pittosporum senacia, commonly known as the New Zealand Pittosporum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a distinctive yellow or orange-flushed underside. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Pittosporum senacia is also valued for its ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
