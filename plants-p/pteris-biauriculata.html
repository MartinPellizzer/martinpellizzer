Pteris biauriculata

Pteris biauriculata, commonly known as the two-auricle fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are divided into small, lance-shaped pinnae. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense, velvety layer of brownish spores.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
