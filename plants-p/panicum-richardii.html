Panicum richardii

Panicum richardii, commonly known as Richard's panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous plant that typically forms dense mats on the ground. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear delicate, feather-like flowers. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
