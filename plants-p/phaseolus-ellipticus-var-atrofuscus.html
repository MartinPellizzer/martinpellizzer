Phaseolus ellipticus var. atrofuscus

Phaseolus ellipticus var. atrofuscus, commonly known as the black bean plant, is a climbing legume native to Central and South America.

It produces small, elliptical pods that contain dark, glossy seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is characterized by its vigorous vining growth habit. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and adaptability to various agricultural conditions.
