Pelargonium × conchyllatum

Pelargonium × conchyllatum, commonly known as the double-leaf geranium, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from various geranium species.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features two leaves per stem, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Due to its decorative leaves and flowers, it is often used in containers and as a bedding plant.
