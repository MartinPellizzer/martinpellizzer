Phlox hoodii var. glabrata

Phlox hoodii var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to purple flowers in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, smooth texture, giving it its varietal name.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
