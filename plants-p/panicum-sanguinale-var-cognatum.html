Panicum sanguinale var. cognatum

Panicum sanguinale var. cognatum, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and bright red or bronze tassels that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a reddish tinge, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is often used in landscaping for its striking fall color and ability to attract wildlife.
