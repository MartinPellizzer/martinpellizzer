Plantago depressa f. glaberrima

Plantago depressa f. glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth-leaved plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in early spring, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
