Paspalum membranaceum

Paspalum membranaceum, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Paspalum membranaceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in riparian areas.
