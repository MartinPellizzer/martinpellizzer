Ptelea aboriginum

Ptelea aboriginum, commonly known as the Australian ash or blackwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by small, woody fruits containing seeds. It is often found in dry forests and woodlands, adapting well to nutrient-poor soils.

The species is valued for its timber and has cultural significance to some Indigenous Australian communities.
