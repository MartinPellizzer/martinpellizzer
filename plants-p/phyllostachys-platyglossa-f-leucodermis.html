Phyllostachys platyglossa f. leucodermis

Phyllostachys platyglossa f. leucodermis, commonly known as the silver-stem bamboo, is a variant of the broad-leafed bamboo species.

It is characterized by its pale, almost white, stems that contrast with its dark green leaves. The plant is native to eastern Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is a clumping bamboo, growing to a height of up to 10 feet, and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Its unique coloration makes it a popular choice in gardens and landscape design.
