Potentilla sundaica

Potentilla sundaica, commonly known as the Sunda cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Potentilla sundaica is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in its native habitat.
