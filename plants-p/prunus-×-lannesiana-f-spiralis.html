Prunus × lannesiana f. spiralis

Prunus × lannesiana f. spiralis, commonly known as the spiraled cherry, is a unique cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of branches, which gives it an ornamental appearance. The plant typically produces small, sweet-tart red or black fruits, similar to other cherry varieties. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This form is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
