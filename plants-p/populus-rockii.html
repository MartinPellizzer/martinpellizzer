Populus rockii

Populus rockii, commonly known as the Chinese poplar, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces catkins as its flowers, which are typically male and female, and it relies on wind pollination. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Populus rockii is valued for its fast growth and is sometimes used in reforestation projects.
