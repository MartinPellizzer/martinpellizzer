Pergularia divaricata

Pergularia divaricata, commonly known as the creeping thyme, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with fragrant, small leaves and showy pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its aromatic foliage and extended blooming period contribute to its ornamental value.

Pergularia divaricata is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat digestive issues and respiratory ailments.
