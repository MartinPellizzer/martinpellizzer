Panicum crus-galli var. sabulicola

Panicum crus-galli var. sabulicola, commonly known as sand panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to sandy and coastal regions.

It is characterized by its erect, cylindrical spikelets and fine, thread-like leaves. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, saline environments. It produces small, pale brown flowers that are wind-pollinated.

This variety is often found in dunes and coastal plains, contributing to soil stabilization and ecosystem resilience.
