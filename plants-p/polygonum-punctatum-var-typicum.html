Polygonum punctatum var. typicum

Polygonum punctatum var. typicum, commonly known as the spotted knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features rhizomatous growth and typically has heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purplish or reddish tinge. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and woodland edges.

Its rapid growth and aggressive spreading habit make it a potential invasive species in some regions.
