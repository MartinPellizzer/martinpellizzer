Prunus maackii f. rotunda

Prunus maackii f. rotunda, commonly known as the rounded form of the Chinese white cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its rounded growth habit and showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. The fruit is a small, dark red drupe that matures in late summer.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
