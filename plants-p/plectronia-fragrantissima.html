Plectronia fragrantissima

Plectronia fragrantissima, commonly known as the sweet-scented plectronia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and often covered in a fine pubescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in warmer climates.
