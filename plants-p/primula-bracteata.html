Primula bracteata

Primula bracteata, commonly known as the bracted primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or along rocky slopes.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.
