Phyllostachys purpurata

Phyllostachys purpurata, commonly known as the purple bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its striking purple stems and leaves. The plant is a clump-forming bamboo that can grow up to 6 meters in height. Its foliage is often variegated with shades of green and pink, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Phyllostachys purpurata is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and horticulture.
