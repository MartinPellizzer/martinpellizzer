Priva leptostachya

Priva leptostachya, commonly known as the slender-stemmed privet, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Priva leptostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
