Pholidia brevifolia

Pholidia brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved pittosporum, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically orange or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry sclerophyllous forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its compact growth and attractive appearance.
