Primula cockburniana

Primula cockburniana, commonly known as the Cockburn's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, oval leaves that are usually hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
