Philodendron membranaceum

Philodendron membranaceum, commonly known as the satin leaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that have a waxy texture and a striking deep green color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Due to its attractive foliage, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and hanging baskets.
