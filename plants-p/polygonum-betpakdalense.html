Polygonum betpakdalense

Polygonum betpakdalense, commonly known as the Betpakdala knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in moist, saline soils and is often found in river valleys and wetlands. The plant features upright, branched stems covered with soft, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and have a bluish-green hue. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Polygonum betpakdalense is notable for its adaptability to harsh environments and is sometimes used in phytoremediation due to its ability to tolerate high salinity.
