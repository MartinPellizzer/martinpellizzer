Pycreus mundii var. perrieri

Pycreus mundii var. perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's pyrethrum, is a variant of the pyrethrum plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, feathery foliage and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers are also used in the production of natural insecticides due to their pyrethrin content.
