Polygonum × hybridum

Polygonum × hybridum, commonly known as the hybrid knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, reddish stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple tinge. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It is native to temperate regions of Asia and has been introduced to other parts of the world.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural settings.
