Pyrus barthae

Pyrus barthae, commonly known as the Barth's pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its deciduous leaves, which are ovate to elliptic in shape and have a smooth margin. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with five petals, and appear in clusters.

The fruit, a small pear-like berry, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.
