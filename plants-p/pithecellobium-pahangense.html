Pithecellobium pahangense

Pithecellobium pahangense, commonly known as the Pahang legume, is a small shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with stipules, and its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in racemes. The fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils and secondary forests.
