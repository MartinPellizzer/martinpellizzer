Piper oblongibaccum

Piper oblongibaccum, commonly known as the broad-leafed pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, which resembles a small berry, contains the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense rainforests.
