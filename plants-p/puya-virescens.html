Puya virescens

Puya virescens, commonly known as the green torchflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of numerous bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces large, fibrous leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Puya virescens is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
