Pleroma ferricola

Pleroma ferricola, commonly known as the ironleaf pleroma, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, metallic-green leaves that reflect light in a unique way, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pale yellow during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
