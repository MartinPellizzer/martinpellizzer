Phaius grandiflorus

Phaius grandiflorus, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 cm in diameter, typically in shades of yellow and orange. The plant features a robust rhizome system and produces numerous leaves that are broad and strap-like. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Phaius grandiflorus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated for its striking floral display.
