Potentilla breviscissa

Potentilla breviscissa, commonly known as the short-haired cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and are covered in fine, short hairs.

Potentilla breviscissa is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
