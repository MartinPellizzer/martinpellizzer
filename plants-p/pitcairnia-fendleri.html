Pitcairnia fendleri

Pitcairnia fendleri, commonly known as the "Christmas cactus," is a tropical epiphytic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in the winter months. The plant typically has triangular or ovate leaves that are covered in a waxy cuticle, aiding in water retention. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique blooming pattern makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and homes.
