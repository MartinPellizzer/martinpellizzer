Pharmacum pileolatum

Pharmacum pileolatum, commonly known as the pileated pharmacum, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom during the cooler months. Its root system is notable for containing bioactive compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural environment.
