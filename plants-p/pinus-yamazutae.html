Pinus yamazutae

Pinus yamazutae, commonly known as the Japanese black pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and its needle-like leaves that are typically 3 to 5 centimeters long. The plant produces cones that are cylindrical and often remain attached to the branches for a long time. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Pinus yamazutae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and parks.
