Poecilostachys viguieri

Poecilostachys viguieri, commonly known as the Viguier's grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct midrib, and the inflorescence is often loosely arranged.

Poecilostachys viguieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
