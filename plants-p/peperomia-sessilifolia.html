Peperomia sessilifolia

Peperomia sessilifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape, with a glossy surface and a deep green color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish in color, which are followed by dark blue to black berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its low maintenance and attractive foliage make it a versatile addition to both indoor and outdoor spaces.
