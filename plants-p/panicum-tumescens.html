Panicum tumescens

Panicum tumescens, commonly known as giant panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and large, feathery inflorescences that can reach heights of up to 6 feet. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and disturbed areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers in late summer.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread, it is considered both a valuable forage plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
