Panicum laxum f. minus

Panicum laxum f. minus, commonly known as the narrow panicgrass, is a botanical variant of the Panicum genus.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, giving it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native plant gardens and restoration projects.
