Picea thunbergii

Picea thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese white pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and its silvery-blue needles that are arranged in bundles of five. The tree produces cones that are typically 5 to 8 centimeters long, containing edible seeds. Its foliage is dense and provides excellent habitat for various wildlife species.

The Japanese white pine is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
