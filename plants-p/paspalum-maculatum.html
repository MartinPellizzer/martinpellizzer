Paspalum maculatum

Paspalum maculatum, commonly known as the spotted paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive purple or reddish markings on its leaves and stems. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, making it an important species in aquatic ecosystems. Its seeds are small and often dispersed by water, contributing to its spread in wetland habitats.

Paspalum maculatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in riparian areas.
