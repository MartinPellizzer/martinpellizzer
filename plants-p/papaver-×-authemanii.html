Papaver × authemanii

Papaver × authemanii, commonly known as the hybrid poppy, is a cross between Papaver rhoeas (common poppy) and Papaver orientale (oriental poppy).

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, easy-to-grow flowers.
