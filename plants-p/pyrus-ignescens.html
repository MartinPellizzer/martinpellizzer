Pyrus ignescens

Pyrus ignescens, commonly known as the fire pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in jams and preserves. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Pyrus ignescens is also noted for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
