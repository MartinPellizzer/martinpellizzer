Polygonum polygaloides var. montanum

Polygonum polygaloides var. montanum, commonly known as mountain smartweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swales, and stream banks. The plant features upright, slender stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in wetland habitats and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
