Pyrus salicifolia var. salicifolia

Pyrus salicifolia var. salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are lanceolate and often have a silvery underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet to subacid berries that ripen in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
