Parita tiliaceus

Parita tiliaceus, commonly known as the Malabar chestnut, is a small evergreen tree native to the western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing one or two seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical regions.

Parita tiliaceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
