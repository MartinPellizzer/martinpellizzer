Polygala supina var. pseudohospita

Polygala supina var. pseudohospita, commonly known as the bent spurge or false spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats and has adapted to thrive in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish in color, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
