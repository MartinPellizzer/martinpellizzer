Populus fremontii var. mesetae

Populus fremontii var. mesetae, commonly known as the Meseta Fremont's cottonwood, is a variant of the Fremont's cottonwood native to the southwestern United States.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, fluffy seed pods. The plant thrives in riparian zones, often growing along rivers and streams in arid environments. It has a shallow root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
