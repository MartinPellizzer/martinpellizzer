Passiflora azeroana

Passiflora azeroana, commonly known as the Azeroan passionflower, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown-like structure of filaments. The plant produces edible, dark purple berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Passiflora azeroana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
