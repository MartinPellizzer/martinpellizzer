Polygonum vesulum

Polygonum vesulum, commonly known as the common knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features erect, slender stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, clustered flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a reddish or purple underside. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along stream banks and in wet meadows.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
