Prunus × lannesiana var. speciosa

Prunus × lannesiana var. speciosa, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a spectacular display. The tree has a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are ovate and turn a vibrant yellow in autumn.

It is widely cultivated in parks and gardens for its ornamental value and cultural significance.
