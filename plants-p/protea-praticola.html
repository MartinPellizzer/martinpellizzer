Protea praticola

Protea praticola, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pale pink flowers arranged in a spherical shape. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as fynbos vegetation, often on rocky soils. Its flowers are pollinated by birds, particularly sunbirds, which are attracted by the nectar.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
