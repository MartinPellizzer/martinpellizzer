Pterophylla dichotoma var. dichotoma

Pterophylla dichotoma var. dichotoma, commonly known as the two-branched fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive dichotomous branching pattern.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions with high humidity. The fronds are usually pinnately divided, with a delicate, feathery appearance that enhances its ornamental value. This plant is known for its ability to thrive in shaded forest floors, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of the understory.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
