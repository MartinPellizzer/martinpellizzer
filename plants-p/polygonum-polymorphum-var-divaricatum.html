Polygonum polymorphum var. divaricatum

Polygonum polymorphum var. divaricatum, commonly known as the variable knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its stout, often reddish stems that are somewhat woody at the base and have a distinctive, zigzagging growth pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are alternate, broadly ovate to elliptic, and often have a rough, slightly hairy texture.

This species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun conditions.
