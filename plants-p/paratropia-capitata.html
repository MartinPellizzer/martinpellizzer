Paratropia capitata

Paratropia capitata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Paratropia capitata is an important species for pollinators and contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
