Paphiopedilum philippinense

Paphiopedilum philippinense, commonly known as the Philippine slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Philippines.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short, thick rhizome and a single, fleshy, leaf-like structure. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a distinctive lip that resembles a slipper, typically in shades of pink or white. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
