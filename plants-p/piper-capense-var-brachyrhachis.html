Piper capense var. brachyrhachis

Piper capense var. brachyrhachis, commonly known as the short-stemmed pepper plant, is a variant of the African pepper species native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that develop into small, round berries. These berries are edible and have a peppery flavor, often used in traditional cuisines.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.
