Prunus domestica var. augustana

Prunus domestica var. augustana, commonly known as the Augustana plum, is a variety of European plum characterized by its compact growth habit and early ripening fruit.

It produces small, dark purple to black fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor, typically harvested in late summer. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in autumn, and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

The Augustana plum is resistant to some common plum diseases, making it a favored choice for home gardens.
