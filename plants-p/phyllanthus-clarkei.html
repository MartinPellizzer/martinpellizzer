Phyllanthus clarkei

Phyllanthus clarkei, commonly known as the Clarke's spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant produces capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
