Polypodium papuanum

Polypodium papuanum, commonly known as the Papuan holly fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Papua and nearby islands.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its robust, upright fronds that are often divided into feather-like segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, and they play a key role in its reproductive cycle.

Polypodium papuanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
