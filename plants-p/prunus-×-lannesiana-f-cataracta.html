Prunus × lannesiana f. cataracta

Prunus × lannesiana f. cataracta, commonly known as the weeping cherry, is a hybrid species of the Prunus genus, characterized by its distinctive cascading branches.

This ornamental tree features delicate, single-layered flowers that bloom in spring, typically in shades of white or pale pink. The leaves are ovate and have a smooth texture, turning yellow in the fall. It is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and parks.

The weeping cherry is relatively hardy but requires well-drained soil and partial sunlight to thrive.
