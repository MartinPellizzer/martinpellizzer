Plantaginorchis longifolia

Plantaginorchis longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered plantain orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers, which are usually pale green to yellowish, are adapted for pollination by specific insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
