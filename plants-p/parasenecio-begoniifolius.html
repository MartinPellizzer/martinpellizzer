Parasenecio begoniifolius

Parasenecio begoniifolius, commonly known as the begonia-leaved parasenecio, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features striking, palmately lobed leaves that resemble those of begonias, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Parasenecio begoniifolius is often found in rocky or open habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
