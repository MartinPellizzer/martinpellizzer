Pyrus eryostyla

Pyrus eryostyla, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves that are usually dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by white, sweet-smelling fruit in late summer. The fruit is typically eaten fresh or used in cooking, and the tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and fruit production.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
