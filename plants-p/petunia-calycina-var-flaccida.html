Petunia calycina var. flaccida

Petunia calycina var. flaccida, commonly known as the drooping petunia, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features trailing stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The flowers are typically violet or blue, with a distinctive calyx that remains prominent even after the petals fall off. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-draining soil.

This variety is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to cascade from containers or hanging baskets.
