Pultenaea luehmannii var. georgei

Pultenaea luehmannii var. georgei, commonly known as George's bush-pea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or loamy soils and is found in coastal and inland regions. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of yellow and orange. It is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and ecological role in its native habitat.
