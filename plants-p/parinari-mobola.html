Parinari mobola

Parinari mobola, commonly known as African cherry or African wild cherry, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and can grow up to 20 meters tall with a broad, umbrella-shaped crown. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape, with a smooth surface and a pointed tip. The tree is valued for its hard, heavy wood and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
