Polygonum forrestii var. pumilio

Polygonum forrestii var. pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive nodes and often triangular stems. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters, with small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of silky hair, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
