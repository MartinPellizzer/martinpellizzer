Ptilotrichum thymops

Ptilotrichum thymops, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandwort, is a small, mat-forming perennial plant native to arid and rocky habitats in southern Europe and North Africa.

It features succulent, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, powdery wax, which helps reduce water loss in dry conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of pink or white, typically arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with a deep root system that allows it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive indicator species of certain ecological conditions.
