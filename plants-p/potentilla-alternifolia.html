Potentilla alternifolia

Potentilla alternifolia, commonly known as the alternateleaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features alternate, palmately lobed leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive, fern-like appearance. Its flowers are bright yellow, with five petals, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Potentilla alternifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and forested areas.
