Pouteria reticulata subsp. surinamensis

Pouteria reticulata subsp.

surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam pouteria, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America. It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves.

The tree produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, orange-colored fruits resembling small mangoes. Its bark is thick and cork-like, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential economic value in agroforestry systems.
