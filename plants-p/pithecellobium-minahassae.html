Pithecellobium minahassae

Pithecellobium minahassae, commonly known as the Minahassa acacia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland areas and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers are small and yellow, often clustered in racemes, while the seeds are enclosed in flat, fibrous pods.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
