Pittosporum ochrosiifolium var. madagascariense

Pittosporum ochrosiifolium var. madagascariense, commonly known as the Madagascar pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It features elliptic to ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and montane forest habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
