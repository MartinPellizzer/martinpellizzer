Piper trichoneuron

Piper trichoneuron, commonly known as the purple pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, violet flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces tiny, peppery fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its botanical features include a woody vine structure and glandular hairs on its stems and leaves.

Piper trichoneuron is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
