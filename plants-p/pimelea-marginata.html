Pimelea marginata

Pimelea marginata, commonly known as the narrowleaf riceflower, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy yellow flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils in dry, open habitats such as heathlands and woodlands. It is a hardy species that is drought-tolerant and can thrive in full sun.

The flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.
