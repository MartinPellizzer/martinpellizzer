Phlomis umbrosa var. latibracteata

Phlomis umbrosa var. latibracteata, commonly known as the broad-bract phlomis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
