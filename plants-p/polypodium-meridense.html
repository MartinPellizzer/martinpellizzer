Polypodium meridense

Polypodium meridense, commonly known as the Southern Shield Fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and leathery in texture. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and release spores for propagation.

Polypodium meridense is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in maintaining ecological balance in its native habitats.
