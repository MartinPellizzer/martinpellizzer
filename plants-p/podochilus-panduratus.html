Podochilus panduratus

Podochilus panduratus, commonly known as the panduratus orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and attractive, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically features strap-shaped leaves and produces showy, pendulous inflorescences with brightly colored blooms. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
