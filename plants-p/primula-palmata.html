Primula palmata

Primula palmata, commonly known as the palmate primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
