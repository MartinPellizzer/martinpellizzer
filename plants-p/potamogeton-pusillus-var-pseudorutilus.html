Potamogeton pusillus var. pseudorutilus

Potamogeton pusillus var. pseudorutilus, commonly known as the narrowleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It typically grows in slow-moving or still waters, such as ponds, lakes, and marshes. The plant has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are usually less than 1 mm wide, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

This variety is often confused with other pondweed species due to its similar appearance.
