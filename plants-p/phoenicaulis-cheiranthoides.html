Phoenicaulis cheiranthoides

Phoenicaulis cheiranthoides, commonly known as the red-flowered wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other well-known garden plants. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright red, yellow, or orange flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils.
