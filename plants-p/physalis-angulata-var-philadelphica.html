Physalis angulata var. philadelphica

Physalis angulata var. philadelphica, commonly known as the Indian lantern or the edible winter cherry, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its inflated, orange-red calyx that encloses the small, yellow berries. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The berries are edible when ripe and are used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
