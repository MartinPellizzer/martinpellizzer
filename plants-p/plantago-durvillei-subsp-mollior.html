Plantago durvillei subsp. mollior

Plantago durvillei subsp.

mollior, commonly known as the smoothleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
