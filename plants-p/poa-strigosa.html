Poa strigosa

Poa strigosa, commonly known as slender meadow grass, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and forest edges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is a perennial species that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense mats.

Botanically, it is characterized by its tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
