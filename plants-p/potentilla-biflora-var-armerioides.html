Potentilla biflora var. armerioides

Potentilla biflora var. armerioides, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has narrow, silver-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant characteristics. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking resilient, decorative ground cover.
