Planichloa

Planichloa, commonly known as the creeping grass or carpetgrass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets. Planichloa species are often found in moist or wet environments, such as marshes and wetlands.
