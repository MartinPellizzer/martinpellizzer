Pandanus bothreus

Pandanus bothreus, commonly known as the mountain pandanus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically grouped into branched inflorescences. Its fruit, which resembles a pineapple, is edible and has a sweet, fibrous texture.

Pandanus bothreus thrives in coastal and mountainous regions, often growing in humid, well-drained soils.
