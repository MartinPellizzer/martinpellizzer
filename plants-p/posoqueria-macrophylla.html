Posoqueria macrophylla

Posoqueria macrophylla, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for aquatic life. The plant's showy white flowers bloom at night, attracting pollinators such as moths. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to anchor firmly in the muddy substrate of its aquatic environment.

The leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps to repel water and support the plant's buoyancy.
