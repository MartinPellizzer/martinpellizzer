Parodia tabularis subsp. bommeljei

Parodia tabularis subsp.

bommeljei, commonly known as the Bommeljei cactus, is a species native to South America, specifically Brazil. It is a columnar cactus characterized by its cylindrical stems and distinctive, ribbed structure.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it is known for its slow growth and resilience in arid environments.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardy nature and unique appearance.
