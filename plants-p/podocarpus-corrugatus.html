Podocarpus corrugatus

Podocarpus corrugatus, commonly known as the rough-leaved podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and rough, scaly bark that gives it its common name. The leaves are typically narrow and arranged in spirals along the branches. It produces small, spherical cones that contain edible seeds.

This species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
