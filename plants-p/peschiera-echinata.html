Peschiera echinata

Peschiera echinata, commonly known as the spiny echinata, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It features upright stems that are often covered in fine, bristly hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Peschiera echinata is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in sunny, well-drained environments.
