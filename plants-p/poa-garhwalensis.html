Poa garhwalensis

Poa garhwalensis, commonly known as the Garhwal mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Himalayan region of India.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ecological significance, it plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
