Polemonium boreale subsp. richardsonii

Polemonium boreale subsp.

richardsonii, commonly known as the Richardson's Jacob's ladder, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America. It is a member of the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its cascading clusters of blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its stems are upright and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
