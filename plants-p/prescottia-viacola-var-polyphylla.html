Prescottia viacola var. polyphylla

Prescottia viacola var. polyphylla, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its vibrant red-orange bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or white, contrasting with the showy bracts. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
