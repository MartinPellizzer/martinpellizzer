Pseuderanthemum versicolor

Pseuderanthemum versicolor, commonly known as the variable pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its strikingly variable flower colors, which can range from white to pink, red, and purple. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy bracts that often resemble true flowers.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
