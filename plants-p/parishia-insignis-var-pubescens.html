Parishia insignis var. pubescens

Parishia insignis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy parishia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss and protect against intense sunlight.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
