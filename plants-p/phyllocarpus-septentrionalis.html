Phyllocarpus septentrionalis

Phyllocarpus septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern phyllocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Phyllocarpaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange in color, which are an important food source for various bird species. It is native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere, including parts of Europe, Asia, and North America.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
