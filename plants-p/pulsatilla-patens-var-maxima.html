Pulsatilla patens var. maxima

Pulsatilla patens var. maxima, commonly known as the showy pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its early spring blooming period, typically in April or May. The plant features a rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers with numerous yellow stamens and a central pistil. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in meadows, rocky slopes, and alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
