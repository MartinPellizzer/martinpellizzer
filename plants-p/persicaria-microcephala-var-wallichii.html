Persicaria microcephala var. wallichii

Persicaria microcephala var. wallichii, commonly known as the dwarf persicaria, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in shady landscapes.
