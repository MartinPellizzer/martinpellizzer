Potentilla brauneana var. auricoma

Potentilla brauneana var. auricoma, commonly known as golden potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine, hairy textures. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant in temperate climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
