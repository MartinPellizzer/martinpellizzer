Phymatopsis similis

Phymatopsis similis, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds with tufts of hair for wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Phymatopsis similis plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is considered an important component of its ecosystem.
