Poa cusickii subsp. epilis

Poa cusickii subsp.

epilis, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada brome, is a grass species native to the western United States. It typically grows in open, dry habitats such as meadows and slopes.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, a common trait among grasses.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and nutrient-poor soils, showcasing its resilience in arid environments.
