Pleurothallis scolopax

Pleurothallis scolopax, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and green. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The flowers are usually short-lived, adding to the plant's ephemeral beauty in its natural habitat.
