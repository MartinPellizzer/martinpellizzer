Poa pratensis subsp. turfosa

Poa pratensis subsp.

turfosa, commonly known as the meadow grass, is a subspecies of the common bentgrass. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and is often found in moist, grassy areas.

The plant has narrow, blade-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is widely used in turfgrass management due to its dense growth and adaptability.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in natural habitats.
