Puccinellia distans subsp. sevangensis

Puccinellia distans subsp.

sevangensis, commonly known as the Sevangensis needlegrass, is a flowering plant species belonging to the grass family Poaceae. It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in saline or alkaline soils, often found in arid and semi-arid regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it blooms with small, two-lipped flowers.

This subspecies is of interest in ecological studies due to its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
