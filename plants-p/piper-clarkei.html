Piper clarkei

Piper clarkei, commonly known as Clarke's pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by elongated, berry-like fruits.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of aromatic compounds.
