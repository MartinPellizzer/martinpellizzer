Piper christyi

Piper christyi, commonly known as the Christy's pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, peppery fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
