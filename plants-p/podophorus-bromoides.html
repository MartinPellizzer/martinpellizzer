Podophorus bromoides

Podophorus bromoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, slender stems with lance-shaped leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale pink, blooming in late summer. Its root system is fibrous and contains compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal uses.

Podophorus bromoides thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.
