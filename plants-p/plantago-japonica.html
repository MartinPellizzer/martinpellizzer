Plantago japonica

Plantago japonica, commonly known as Japanese plantain, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in early summer, which are followed by oval, winged seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or along stream banks.

Its medicinal properties have been utilized in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
