Picris damascena subsp. desertorum

Picris damascena subsp.

desertorum, commonly known as desert chicory, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean and Middle East. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Its resilience and ornamental value make it a notable species in xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
