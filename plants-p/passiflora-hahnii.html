Passiflora hahnii

Passiflora hahnii, commonly known as the Hahn's passionflower, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and it thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.

Passiflora hahnii is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
