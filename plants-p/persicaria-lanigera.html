Persicaria lanigera

Persicaria lanigera, commonly known as woolly polygon, is a flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Native to Eurasia, it is often found in moist habitats such as wet meadows and stream sides.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural settings.
