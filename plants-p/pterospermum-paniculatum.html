Pterospermum paniculatum

Pterospermum paniculatum, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy, red or orange flowers that appear in panicles during the summer months. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces woody, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
