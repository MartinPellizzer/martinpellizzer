Pfaffia pulverulenta f. densepilosa

Pfaffia pulverulenta f. densepilosa, commonly known as the dense-hairy pogostemon, is a variant of the Pfaffia genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its flowers are usually small and greenish-white, arranged in spikes.

While not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and potential ecological significance.
