Pilea harrisii

Pilea harrisii, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to China.

It features rosettes of thick, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly variegated appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in spring, followed by unique, round, coin-like fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Pilea harrisii is relatively low maintenance and is valued for its attractive foliage and ease of care.
