Poa laxa var. occidentalis

Poa laxa var. occidentalis, commonly known as western slender grass, is a perennial grass species native to western North America.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and woodland edges. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is adapted to a range of soil types and is often found in regions with moderate to high rainfall.
