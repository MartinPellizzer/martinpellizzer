Phrynium malaccense

Phrynium malaccense, commonly known as the Malacca arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial with large, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that emerge from a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines for their starchy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
