Polyscias pachypedicellata

Polyscias pachypedicellata, commonly known as the broadleaf sycamore, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its foliage makes it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture due to its attractive appearance and adaptability.
