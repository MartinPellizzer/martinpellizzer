Phyllagathis pluriumbellata

Phyllagathis pluriumbellata, commonly known as the hairy-leafed myrtle, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, small, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Phyllagathis pluriumbellata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky hillsides.
