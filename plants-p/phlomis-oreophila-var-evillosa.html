Phlomis oreophila var. evillosa

Phlomis oreophila var. evillosa, commonly known as the rock phlomis, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, lavender-pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
