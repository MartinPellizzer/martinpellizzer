Pyrus paucinervia

Pyrus paucinervia, commonly known as the few-veined pear.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern Himalayas. The plant is characterized by its finely veined leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in early spring. Its fruits are edible, though they are typically small and not widely cultivated.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often found in moist, woodland habitats.
