Prunus domestica subsp. prisca

Prunus domestica subsp.

prisca, commonly known as the ancient European plum, is a subspecies of the domesticated European plum. It is characterized by its small, sweet, and often yellow or orange fruits that ripen in late summer.

The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces fragrant white flowers in the spring.

This subspecies is valued for its historical significance and is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental appeal.
