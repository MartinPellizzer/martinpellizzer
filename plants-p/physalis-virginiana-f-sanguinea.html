Physalis virginiana f. sanguinea

Physalis virginiana f. sanguinea, commonly known as the red-berried wild tomato, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant red calyces that enclose the small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and meadows. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with five petals.

The berries, which ripen in late summer, are a food source for various wildlife species.
