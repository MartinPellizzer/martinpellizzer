Polygonum punctatum var. ellipticum

Polygonum punctatum var. ellipticum, commonly known as the narrowleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or reddish in color, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
