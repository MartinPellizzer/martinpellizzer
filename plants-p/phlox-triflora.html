Phlox triflora

Phlox triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink to lavender in color, with five petals and a distinctive tubular throat.

Phlox triflora thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
