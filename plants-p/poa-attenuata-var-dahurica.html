Poa attenuata var. dahurica

Poa attenuata var. dahurica, commonly known as the Siberian grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, tufted shoots with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
