Pleurothallis cuzcoensis

Pleurothallis cuzcoensis, commonly known as the Cuzco orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Peru.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a subtle fragrance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and delicate species.
