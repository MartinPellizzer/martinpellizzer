Piper amphitrichum

Piper amphitrichum, commonly known as the swamp pepper or water pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct reddish or purple underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
