Polycarpaea rosulans

Polycarpaea rosulans, commonly known as the clustered polycarpaea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, often pink or purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Polycarpaea rosulans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
