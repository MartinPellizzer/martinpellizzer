Pleurothallis lipothrix

Pleurothallis lipothrix, commonly known as the hairy pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and often possesses hairy or pubescent stems and leaves. The plant typically produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, yellow, or green, often clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and specific ecological requirements, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
