Pleurothallis ypirangae

Pleurothallis ypirangae, commonly known as the Ypiranga orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly found in the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that often display a range of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and drawing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Its botanical features include narrow, strap-like leaves and a compact inflorescence that supports its delicate, fragrant blooms.
