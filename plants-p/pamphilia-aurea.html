Pamphilia aurea

Pamphilia aurea, commonly known as the golden pamphilia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
