Pyrus athenae

Pyrus athenae, commonly known as the Athens pear, is a small deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart pears in late summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface.

Pyrus athenae is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in Greek horticulture.
