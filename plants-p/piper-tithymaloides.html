Piper tithymaloides

Piper tithymaloides, commonly known as the pepperleaf vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
