Prunus provincialis

Prunus provincialis, commonly known as the Chinese bird cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.

The plant is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its potential health benefits.
