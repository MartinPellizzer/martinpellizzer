Piper frijolesanum var. grandifolium

Piper frijolesanum var. grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaf pepper plant, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds. It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.
