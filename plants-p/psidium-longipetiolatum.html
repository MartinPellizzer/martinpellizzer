Psidium longipetiolatum

Psidium longipetiolatum, commonly known as the Brazilian guava or long-petioled guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a rough, leathery texture.

Psidium longipetiolatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
