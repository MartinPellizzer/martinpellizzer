Paracaryum persicum subsp. macrocarpum

Paracaryum persicum subsp.

macrocarpum, commonly known as the Persian rose or Persian paracaryum, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, compact foliage.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture, while its fruits are fleshy drupes that contain a single seed.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
