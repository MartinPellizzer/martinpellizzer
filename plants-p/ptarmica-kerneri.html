Ptarmica kerneri

Ptarmica kerneri, commonly known as the Kern's pincushion plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiky inflorescence that resembles a pincushion. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Ptarmica kerneri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
