Peperomia angularis

Peperomia angularis, commonly known as the Angular Peperomia, is a compact, low-maintenance plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features succulent-like leaves that are often glossy and slightly textured, with a distinctive angular shape that gives it its common name. The plant typically produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or green, which are followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in well-draining soil and prefers bright, indirect light, making it ideal for indoor cultivation.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for tabletop displays and as a houseplant.
