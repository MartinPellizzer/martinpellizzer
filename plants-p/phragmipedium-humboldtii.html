Phragmipedium humboldtii

Phragmipedium humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt's slipper orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety green leaves and its unique, pouch-like flower that resembles a slipper. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers, which bloom in shades of yellow and orange, are highly prized by orchid enthusiasts for their beauty and rarity.

Phragmipedium humboldtii requires specific conditions, including high humidity and indirect light, to thrive in cultivation.
