Potentilla cinerea f. epipsila

Potentilla cinerea f. epipsila, commonly known as the grey cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

The species is also valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
