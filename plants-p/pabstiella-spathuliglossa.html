Pabstiella spathuliglossa

Pabstiella spathuliglossa, commonly known as the narrow-lipped stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
