Picris longifolia

Picris longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf picris, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, often found in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. The plant features upright, branched stems with narrow, linear leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers are arranged in composite inflorescences, typical of the daisy family, and bloom in late summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
