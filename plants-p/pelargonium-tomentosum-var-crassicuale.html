Pelargonium tomentosum var. crassicuale

Pelargonium tomentosum var. crassicuale, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a compact, trailing plant characterized by its dense, fuzzy foliage.

Its leaves are typically rounded with a thick, velvety texture, and they often display a range of colors from gray-green to silver. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom in clusters along the stems. It is native to South Africa and thrives in well-drained soil with moderate sunlight.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover in rock gardens.
