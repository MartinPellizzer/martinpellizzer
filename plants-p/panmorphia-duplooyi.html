Panmorphia duplooyi

Panmorphia duplooyi, commonly known as the double-faced fern, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

Its fronds exhibit a dual-faced structure, with one side displaying a typical fern-like appearance and the other side bearing a highly modified, almost floral form. This botanical feature suggests a possible evolutionary adaptation for specific ecological functions, such as pollination or protection. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unusual structure makes it a subject of interest in botanical research and horticultural cultivation.
