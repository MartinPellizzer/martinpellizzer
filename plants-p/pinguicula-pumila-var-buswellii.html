Pinguicula pumila var. buswellii

Pinguicula pumila var. buswellii, commonly known as the dwarf butterwort, is a small carnivorous plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in mucilaginous glands, which secrete a sticky substance to trap small insects. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices or forest floors. Its flowers are usually small and greenish-white, with a subtle fragrance that attracts pollinators.

Due to its delicate nature, it requires careful cultivation to thrive in captivity.
