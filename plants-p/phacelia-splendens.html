Phacelia splendens

Phacelia splendens, commonly known as the golden phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats like meadows and rocky slopes. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color.

Phacelia splendens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
