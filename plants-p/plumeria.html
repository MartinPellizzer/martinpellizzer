Plumeria

Plumeria, commonly known as the frangipani, is a genus of flowering plants native to the tropical regions of the Americas, Africa, and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, pink, and red. The plants typically have smooth, often aromatic bark and are known for their ornamental value in gardens and as street trees. Plumeria species are also valued for their ability to thrive in warm, sunny climates with well-draining soil.

Their flowers are often used in floral arrangements and traditional ceremonies due to their beauty and symbolism.
