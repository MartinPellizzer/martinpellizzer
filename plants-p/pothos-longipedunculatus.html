Pothos longipedunculatus

Pothos longipedunculatus, commonly known as the Variegated Pothos, is a striking member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that display a striking combination of green, white, and yellow variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by spathes, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in low-light conditions and is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular houseplant.

Its vigorous growth and attractive foliage make it a favorite for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
