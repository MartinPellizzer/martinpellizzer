Pilocereus celsianus var. bruennowii

Pilocereus celsianus var. bruennowii, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its swollen, colorful tubercles that give it a unique appearance. The plant has a short, thick stem with spines and produces small, fragrant flowers during the night. Its botanical features include a ribbed structure and a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking visual appeal.
