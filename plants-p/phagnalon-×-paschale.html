Phagnalon × paschale

Phagnalon × paschale, commonly known as the hybrid meadow grass, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are arranged in a composite inflorescence. It is typically found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations depending on its parent species.
