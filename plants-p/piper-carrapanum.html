Piper carrapanum

Piper carrapanum, commonly known as the Carrapanum pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that often require support. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its small white flowers are clustered in spikes.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that ripens to a dark purple color, containing three seeds.
