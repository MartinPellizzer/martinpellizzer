Poa nemoralis var. caesia

Poa nemoralis var. caesia, commonly known as the blue mountain grass, is a variant of the wood sedge that is characterized by its distinctive blue-gray foliage.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is well adapted to temperate climates and is often found in regions with cool, humid conditions.

Its unique coloration and growth habit make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
