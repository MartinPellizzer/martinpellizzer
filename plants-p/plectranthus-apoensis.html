Plectranthus apoensis

Plectranthus apoensis, commonly known as the Cape verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive mint-like fragrance. Its flowers are typically blue to purple, clustered in whorls along the stems, and bloom throughout the growing season.

Plectranthus apoensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
