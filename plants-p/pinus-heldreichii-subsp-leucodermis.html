Pinus heldreichii subsp. leucodermis

Pinus heldreichii subsp.

leucodermis, commonly known as the white-barked pine, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It is characterized by its distinctive white or silver-colored bark, which becomes more prominent with age.

The tree has slender, often twisted branches and produces cones that are typically reddish-brown in color. Its needles are long and slender, usually growing in bundles of five, and are a deep green in color.

This subspecies is well adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments and is often found in mixed forests.
