Parsonsia Bustamanta

Parsonsia bustamanta is a striking member of the Apocynaceae family known for its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, warm climates with ample sunlight. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and conservation efforts. The plant's flowers often display a mix of red, orange, and yellow hues, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Despite its beauty, Parsonsia bustamanta remains a relatively lesser-known species in the world of horticulture.
