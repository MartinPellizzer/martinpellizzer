Paspalum pulchellum

Paspalum pulchellum, commonly known as the silver paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, silver-gray blades that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish flowers.

Paspalum pulchellum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
