Penstemon davidsonii var. praeteritus

Penstemon davidsonii var. praeteritus, commonly known as the Davidson's beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from violet to pink. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often with a bluish-green hue. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
