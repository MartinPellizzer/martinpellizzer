Parkia agboensis

Parkia agboensis, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered pink or red flowers that bloom in the dry season. The tree produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant has also been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
