Piper pseudofimbriulatum

Piper pseudofimbriulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems and small, opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are typically green when immature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
