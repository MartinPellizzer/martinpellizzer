Phalaris arundinacea f. thyrsoidea

Phalaris arundinacea f. thyrsoidea, commonly known as the silvergrass or silver reed, is a grass species characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage.

It is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist meadows and along waterways. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers.

This ornamental grass is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in landscaping and wetland restoration projects.
