Populus tremuloides f. nana

Populus tremuloides f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf trembling aspen, is a naturally occurring variant of the trembling aspen.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller leaves compared to the typical form. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal and subalpine regions. Its root system forms a clonal colony, allowing it to spread vegetatively and establish dense stands.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
