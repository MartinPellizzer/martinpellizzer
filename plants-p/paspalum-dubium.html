Paspalum dubium

Paspalum dubium, commonly known as the common paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Paspalum dubium is a hardy species that can tolerate a range of environmental conditions, making it adaptable to various habitats.
