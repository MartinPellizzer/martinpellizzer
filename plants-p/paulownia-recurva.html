Paulownia recurva

Paulownia recurva, commonly known as the common empress tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its rapid growth rate and distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed. The tree produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in early spring, which are typically pale purple or white. Its wood is lightweight and strong, making it valuable for crafting and furniture.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to urban environments.
