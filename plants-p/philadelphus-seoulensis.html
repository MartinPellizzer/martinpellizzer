Philadelphus seoulensis

Philadelphus seoulensis, commonly known as Seoul honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to South Korea.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, simple, and ovate leaves. Its botanical features include a woody stem with thorn-like projections and small, elliptical berries that ripen to red.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant scent and attractive foliage.
