Pinus llaveana

Pinus llaveana, commonly known as the Llave pine, is a species of conifer native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in bundles of five. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown in color and contain seeds used for propagation. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sunlight.

This species plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitat.
