Polygala arvensis

Polygala arvensis, commonly known as field milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygalaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. The plant produces small, yellowish or greenish flowers arranged in spiny clusters, which are characteristic of the genus Polygala. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a hairy surface.

The seeds are enclosed in a unique, spiral-shaped pod that aids in seed dispersal.
