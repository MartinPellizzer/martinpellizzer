Pyrus alabaster

Pyrus alabaster, commonly known as the alabaster pear, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark that resembles alabaster, giving it its common name. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by pear-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or yellowish-orange. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.

Pyrus alabaster is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and historical significance in American horticulture.
