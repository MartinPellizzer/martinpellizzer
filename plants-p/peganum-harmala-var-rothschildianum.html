Peganum harmala var. rothschildianum

Peganum harmala var. rothschildianum, commonly known as the Rothschild's Syrian rue, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, spiny stems and yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that contain toxic alkaloids, including harmine and harmaline. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Due to its medicinal and toxic properties, it has been studied for potential pharmaceutical applications.
