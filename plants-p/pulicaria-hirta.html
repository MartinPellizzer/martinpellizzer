Pulicaria hirta

Pulicaria hirta, commonly known as hairy tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Pulicaria hirta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
