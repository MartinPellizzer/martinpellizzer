Podocarpus macrophyllus var. rubra

Podocarpus macrophyllus var. rubra, commonly known as the red-leaf podocarp or red-leaf kurige, is a variant of the common podocarp, native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is a evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green to reddish-brown leaves that change color with age. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, typical of the Podocarpaceae family. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive midrib, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a landscape plant.
