Panicum turfosum

Panicum turfosum, commonly known as tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its clumped growth habit, forming dense tussocks that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, bogs, and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences that release light seeds.

Panicum turfosum plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
