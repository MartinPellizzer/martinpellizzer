Prunus davidiana var. davidiana

Prunus davidiana var. davidiana, commonly known as the David's cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, dark red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in botanical studies.
