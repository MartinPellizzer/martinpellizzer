Pariana zingiberina

Pariana zingiberina, commonly known as the ginger lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and other aromatic plants. The plant features rhizomatous growth and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a glossy appearance.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
