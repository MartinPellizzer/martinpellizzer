Podocarpus nageia var. angustifolius

Podocarpus nageia var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture and reforestation projects.
