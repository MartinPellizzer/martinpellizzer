Podocarpus biformis

Podocarpus biformis, commonly known as the two-lobed podocarp, is a member of the Podocarpaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a evergreen coniferous tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has needle-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, cone-like structures. Its botanical features include a distinctive two-lobed seed scale, which is a key characteristic for identification.

The tree is often valued for its durable wood and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.
