Paspalum fournierianum var. maximum

Paspalum fournierianum var. maximum, commonly known as the giant paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, robust growth and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
