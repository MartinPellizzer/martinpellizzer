Potamogeton alpinus var. nerviger

Potamogeton alpinus var. nerviger, commonly known as the alpine pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to cold, freshwater environments.

It typically grows in shallow, nutrient-rich waters and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves with distinct veins. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like structures, which are often submerged or partially emergent. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its preference for cool, clear waters, it is often found in alpine lakes and streams.
