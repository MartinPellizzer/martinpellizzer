Pleurothallis elachopus

Pleurothallis elachopus, commonly known as the silver-stem pleurothallis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a small epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually dark green and fleshy, adapted for water storage.

It produces delicate, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season.
