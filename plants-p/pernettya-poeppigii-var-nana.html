Pernettya poeppigii var. nana

Pernettya poeppigii var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf cowberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional cuisine. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for alpine gardens. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in cold climates.
