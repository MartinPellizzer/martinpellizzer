Phyllostachys nuda f. purpurascens

Phyllostachys nuda f. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple bamboo, is a variant of the common bamboo species Phyllostachys nuda.

It is characterized by its striking reddish-purple stems and leaves, which provide a vibrant contrast to its usual green coloration. This ornamental bamboo is native to China and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes. The plant grows as a clump-forming bamboo, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, and prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Its unique coloration makes it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
