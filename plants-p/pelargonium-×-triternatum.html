Pelargonium × triternatum

Pelargonium × triternatum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive heart-shaped, waxy leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Its ornamental value and ease of care contribute to its widespread cultivation in gardens and as a houseplant.
