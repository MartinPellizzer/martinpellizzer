Polycnemum viaticum

Polycnemum viaticum, commonly known as the roadside nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Urticaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and alternate, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in wet meadows.

Polycnemum viaticum is a perennial herb that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
