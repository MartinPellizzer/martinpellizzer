Polypodium sauvinerrei

Polypodium sauvinerrei, commonly known as the sauvinier's polypody, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically borne on creeping rhizomes. The plant prefers humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the fronds, a feature common among ferns.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it an important component of forest understory ecosystems.
