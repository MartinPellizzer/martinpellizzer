Pharbitis pubescens

Pharbitis pubescens, commonly known as morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heliotropic flowers that open in the morning and close at dusk. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces long, twisting vines that can climb various structures. Its seeds are enclosed in spiral-shaped fruits that open when mature.

Pharbitis pubescens is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
