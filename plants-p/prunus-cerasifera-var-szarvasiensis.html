Prunus cerasifera var. szarvasiensis

Prunus cerasifera var. szarvasiensis, commonly known as the Szarvasi cherry, is a variant of the European cherry tree.

It is characterized by its small, dark red fruits and compact growth habit. This plant is native to Hungary and is often found in rocky or dry habitats. It has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy on the underside.

The Szarvasi cherry is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
