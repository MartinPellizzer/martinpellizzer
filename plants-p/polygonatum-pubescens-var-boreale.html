Polygonatum pubescens var. boreale

Polygonatum pubescens var. boreale, commonly known as the northern Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cooler regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features arching stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in loose racemes, followed by red berries that mature in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in woodland gardens for its graceful appearance.
