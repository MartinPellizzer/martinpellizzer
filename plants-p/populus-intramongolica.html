Populus intramongolica

Populus intramongolica, commonly known as the Inner Mongolian poplar, is a deciduous tree native to northern China.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in river valleys and lowlands.

Its wood is lightweight and used for various local crafts and construction purposes.
