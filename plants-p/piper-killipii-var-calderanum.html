Piper killipii var. calderanum

Piper killipii var. calderanum, commonly known as the Calderanum pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper killipii species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, slender berries that mature to a bright red color. It is valued for its aromatic leaves and fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The botanical features of this plant make it a unique and interesting subject for botanical study and horticultural cultivation.
