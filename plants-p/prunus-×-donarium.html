Prunus × donarium

Prunus × donarium, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Prunus mume and Prunus mandshurica. The plant is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the fruit is a small, yellowish stone fruit.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
