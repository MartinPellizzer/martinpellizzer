Pueraria montana

Pueraria montana, commonly known as Kudzu, is a vigorous vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in many regions. Its roots contain high levels of starch and are used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

Pueraria montana is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing properties, which enrich the soil.
