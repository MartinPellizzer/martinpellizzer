Pteris esculenta

Pteris esculenta, commonly known as the edible water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often submerged in water or partially exposed. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically near streams or in wetlands. Its fronds are used in traditional Asian cuisines, particularly in China and Japan, where they are cooked and consumed.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is valued for its nutritional content and unique flavor.
