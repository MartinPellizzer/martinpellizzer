Phymatanthus × intertinctus

Phymatanthus × intertinctus, commonly known as the hybrid flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a cross between Phymatanthus orbiculatus and Phymatanthus fraxinifolius, resulting in a hybrid with unique botanical characteristics. The plant is known for its striking, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in summer, adding vibrant color to its environment. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The leaves are typically large, ovate, and have a glossy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.
