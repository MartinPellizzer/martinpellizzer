Pineda ovata

Pineda ovata, commonly known as the oval pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, ovate-shaped cones and needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in clusters. The tree can grow to heights of up to 20 meters, with a broad, conical crown. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

Pineda ovata plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, offering shelter and food for various wildlife species.
