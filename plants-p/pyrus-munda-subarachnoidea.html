Pyrus munda subarachnoidea

Pyrus munda subarachnoidea, commonly known as the hairy pear, is a lesser-known variant of the pear genus.

It is characterized by its hairy or pubescent leaves and fruits, which distinguish it from other pear species. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and prefers well-drained soil. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, appearing in clusters.

While not widely cultivated, it holds interest for botanists due to its unique morphological traits.
