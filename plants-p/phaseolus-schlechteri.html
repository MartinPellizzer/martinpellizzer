Phaseolus schlechteri

Phaseolus schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's bean, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes known for their nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, similar to other beans in the genus Phaseolus. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential agricultural use.
