Phyllanthus involutus

Phyllanthus involutus, commonly known as the spiral leaf sandmat, is a species of flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spirally arranged leaves, which give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Phyllanthus involutus is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
