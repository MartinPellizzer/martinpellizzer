Pyrus aucuparia fructuluteo

Pyrus aucuparia fructuluteo, commonly known as the yellow-fruited crabapple, is a rare variant of the crabapple tree native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellowish-orange fruit, which sets it apart from the more common red or white-fruited varieties. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn a vibrant yellow in the fall.
