Phyllanthus microphyllus var. genuinus

Phyllanthus microphyllus var. genuinus, commonly known as the small-leaved phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length, with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact size and ornamental leaves, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
