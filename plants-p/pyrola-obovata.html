Pyrola obovata

Pyrola obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf wintergreen, is a low-growing evergreen perennial native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, commonly known as the heath or heather family. The plant features oval, leathery leaves that remain green throughout the year, and it produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.

Pyrola obovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
