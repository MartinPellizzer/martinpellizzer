Potentilla × engadinensis

Potentilla × engadinensis, commonly known as the Engadine cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardiness in cooler climates.
