Premna mwadimei

Premna mwadimei, commonly known as the Kenyan nettle, is a shrub native to East Africa.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
