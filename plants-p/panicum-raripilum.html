Panicum raripilum

Panicum raripilum, commonly known as the rare feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and marshes. It has small, spike-like flowers that are often pale green or brown in color.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
