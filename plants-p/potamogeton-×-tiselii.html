Potamogeton × tiselii

Potamogeton × tiselii, commonly known as the hybrid water plant, is a submerged aquatic plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton praevalidus.

It is typically found in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers, where it forms dense vegetation. The plant has long, flexible stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often with leaves arranged in whorls. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters, and it plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates and fish.

Due to its hybrid nature, it can exhibit a wide range of morphological variation, making it challenging to identify without detailed botanical analysis.
