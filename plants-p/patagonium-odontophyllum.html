Patagonium odontophyllum

Patagonium odontophyllum, commonly known as the Patagonian toothbrush plant, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tooth-like bracts that give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow.

Patagonium odontophyllum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various insect species.
