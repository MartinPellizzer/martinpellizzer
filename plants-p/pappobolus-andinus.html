Pappobolus andinus

Pappobolus andinus, commonly known as the Andean pappobolus, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in a pappus, which aids in its propagation across rugged mountainous terrain. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is considered an important species in the ecological dynamics of Andean ecosystems.
