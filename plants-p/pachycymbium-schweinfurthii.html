Pachycymbium schweinfurthii

Pachycymbium schweinfurthii, commonly known as the giant cycad, is a unique plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves and stout, unbranched trunk. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its dense, fern-like foliage and a thick, woody stem that can grow several meters tall. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered an important species for biodiversity conservation.
