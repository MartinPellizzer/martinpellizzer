Pandanus heterostigma

Pandanus heterostigma, commonly known as the hairy pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the Pacific Islands and is characterized by its tall, palm-like appearance with long, narrow leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, which are often used in traditional crafts and as ornamental plants. Its roots and leaves are also utilized in local cuisines and medicinal practices.

The species is adapted to coastal and wetland environments, showcasing its resilience to saline conditions.
