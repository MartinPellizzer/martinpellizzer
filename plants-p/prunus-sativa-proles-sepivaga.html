Prunus sativa proles sepivaga

Prunus sativa proles sepivaga, commonly known as the wild cherry tree, is a deciduous species characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed form and smooth, gray bark.

It produces small, white or pinkish flowers in spring, followed by edible cherries that ripen in late summer. The leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological landscapes.

The fruit is not only a food source for wildlife but also has historical uses in traditional medicine.
