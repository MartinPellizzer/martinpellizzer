Pleroma ciliare

Pleroma ciliare, commonly known as the fringed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet or blue flowers that are adorned with fine, fringed petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Pleroma ciliare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
