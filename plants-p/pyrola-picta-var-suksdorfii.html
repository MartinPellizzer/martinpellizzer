Pyrola picta var. suksdorfii

Pyrola picta var. suksdorfii, commonly known as the Suksdorf's fairy slipper, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the Appalachian Mountains.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that resemble tiny slippers, typically pink or white in color. The plant grows in cool, moist habitats such as shaded forest floors, where it thrives in rich, acidic soils. Its leaves are evergreen, glossy, and oval-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a conservation concern in many regions.
