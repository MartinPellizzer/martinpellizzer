Pachychilus smithianus

Pachychilus smithianus, commonly known as the Smith's pachychilus, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a compact, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, fibrous leaves that form a rosette shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, emerging from the center of the leaf rosette. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
