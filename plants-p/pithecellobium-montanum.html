Pithecellobium montanum

Pithecellobium montanum, commonly known as the mountain pithecellobium, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible pods that contain small, round seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential nutritional benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
