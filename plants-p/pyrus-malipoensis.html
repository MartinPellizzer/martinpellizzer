Pyrus malipoensis

Pyrus malipoensis, commonly known as the Malipo pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to China, specifically the Yunnan province, and is known for its ornamental value and edible fruit. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while the flowers are white and clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a pear-shaped berry that ripens to a yellowish color and is valued for its sweet flavor and medicinal properties.
