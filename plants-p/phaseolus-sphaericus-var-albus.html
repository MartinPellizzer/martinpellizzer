Phaseolus sphaericus var. albus

Phaseolus sphaericus var. albus, commonly known as the white kidney bean, is a variety of the common bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its spherical, white to pale yellow pods and is typically grown for its edible seeds. The plant is an annual legume that thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the plant produces flowers that range from white to pale pink.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is often cultivated for both culinary and agricultural purposes.
