Phaseolus longepedunculatus f. boliviensis

Phaseolus longepedunculatus f. boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian long pedunculate bean, is a unique botanical variant characterized by its elongated peduncles and distinctive foliage.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, producing small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a serrated margin, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and adaptability.
