Phaseolus truxillensis f. velutina

Phaseolus truxillensis f. velutina, commonly known as the velvet bean.

This plant is a variant of the common bean, characterized by its hairy, velvety foliage and pods. It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used as a protein source. The plant has a climbing habit and produces pods that contain edible seeds.

Its botanical features include compound leaves and flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color.
