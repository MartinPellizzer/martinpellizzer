Petrobium cordatum

Petrobium cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped, giving it its common name, and small white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand salt spray and harsh coastal conditions.

Petrobium cordatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
