Potentilla macrantha

Potentilla macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and alternate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, often in clusters of five. Its botanical features include small, elliptical leaves and a root system adapted to sandy or rocky soils.

Potentilla macrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
