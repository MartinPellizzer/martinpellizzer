Primula hirsuta var. pallida

Primula hirsuta var. pallida, commonly known as the pale primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white down. The flowers are usually pale yellow to white, with five petals and a distinctive, showy appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
