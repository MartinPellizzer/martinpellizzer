Pentaschistis trifida

Pentaschistis trifida, commonly known as the three-lobed rock cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are pinnately divided with three lobes. Native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, it typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies of arid and mountainous regions.
