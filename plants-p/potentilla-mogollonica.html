Potentilla mogollonica

Potentilla mogollonica, commonly known as the Mogollon potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Potentilla mogollonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh landscapes.
