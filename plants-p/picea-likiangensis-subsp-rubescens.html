Picea likiangensis subsp. rubescens

Picea likiangensis subsp.

rubescens, commonly known as the red spruce, is a subspecies of the fir tree native to the mountainous regions of China. It is characterized by its reddish-brown bark and conical shape, which helps it withstand harsh alpine conditions.

The needles are typically short and dark green, with a slightly resinous scent. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests.

This subspecies plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
