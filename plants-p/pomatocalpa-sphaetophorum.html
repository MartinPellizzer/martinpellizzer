Pomatocalpa sphaetophorum

Pomatocalpa sphaetophorum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories, where it thrives in humid and cool conditions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
