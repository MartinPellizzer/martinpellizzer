Paspalum arundinellum

Paspalum arundinellum, commonly known as silky paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, drooping flower spikes. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, poorly drained soils. Its rhizomes form dense mats, which help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to colonize disturbed sites effectively.
