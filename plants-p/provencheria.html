Provencheria

Provencheria, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and is native to the western United States. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Provencheria is often used in wildflower gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
