Prostanthera cryptandroides subsp. cryptandroides

Prostanthera cryptandroides subsp.

cryptandroides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mintbush, is a small shrub native to southeastern Australia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a fragrant, mint-like scent when crushed. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to violet in color.

This species is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.
