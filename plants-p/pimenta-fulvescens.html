Pimenta fulvescens

Pimenta fulvescens, commonly known as the yellow pepper tree, is a species of evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish flowers and small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and emit a strong, spicy aroma when crushed.

The fruit, which is a type of berry, is often used in traditional medicine and for its aromatic properties.
