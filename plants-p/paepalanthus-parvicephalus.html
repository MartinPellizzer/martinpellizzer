Paepalanthus parvicephalus

Paepalanthus parvicephalus, commonly known as the dwarf palm or small-headed palm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features a short, unbranched stem with a cluster of stiff, linear leaves at the base. Its flowers are small and clustered in a spadix, typical of many palms, and it produces small, round fruits.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
