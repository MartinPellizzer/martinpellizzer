Porophyllum tarapacanum

Porophyllum tarapacanum, commonly known as the Atacama parsley, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has narrow, aromatic leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. It is adapted to survive in harsh, saline environments, making it a resilient species in the Atacama Desert.

Due to its unique ecological role, it is also of interest for potential agricultural use in saline soils.
