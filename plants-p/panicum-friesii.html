Panicum friesii

Panicum friesii, commonly known as the African grass or red panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple hues. The plant produces small, fluffy panicles of seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across various habitats. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to a range of environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
