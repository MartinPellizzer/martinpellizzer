Polysphaeria macrophylla

Polysphaeria macrophylla, commonly known as the giant sphaerocarp, is a unique aquatic plant belonging to the order Sphaerocarpales.

It is characterized by its large, bladder-like fronds that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, particularly in slow-moving or still water bodies with muddy substrates. Its reproductive structures are enclosed within specialized, spherical sporocarps, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Polysphaeria macrophylla plays a minor role in aquatic ecosystems but is of interest to botanists due to its ancient lineage and morphological features.
