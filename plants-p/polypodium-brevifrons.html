Polypodium brevifrons

Polypodium brevifrons, commonly known as the short-faced shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, evergreen fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, clustered sporangia on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in rainforest habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
