Panicum hispidissimum var. gracile

Panicum hispidissimum var. gracile, commonly known as slender bristlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces fluffy, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is characterized by its hairy texture, particularly on the stems and leaves, which give it a rough, bristly appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical structure makes it a valuable plant for ecological studies and potential use in restoration projects.
