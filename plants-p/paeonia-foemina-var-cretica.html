Paeonia foemina var. cretica

Paeonia foemina var. cretica, commonly known as the Cretan peony, is a variety of the peony plant native to the island of Crete.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to white, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant is a herbaceous perennial, producing thick, fleshy rhizomes that allow it to survive in various soil conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
