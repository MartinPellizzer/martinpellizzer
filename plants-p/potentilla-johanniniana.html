Potentilla johanniniana

Potentilla johanniniana, commonly known as the Johanninian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features compound leaves with five leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
