Planchonella aurata

Planchonella aurata, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
