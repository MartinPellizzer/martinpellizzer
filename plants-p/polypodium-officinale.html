Polypodium officinale

Polypodium officinale, commonly known as the common holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds that are typically divided into numerous pinnae. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests and along stream banks. Its rhizomes are perennial, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies over time.

Polypodium officinale is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
