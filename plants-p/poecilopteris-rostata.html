Poecilopteris rostata

Poecilopteris rostata, commonly known as the rosette fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive rosette-like arrangement of fronds, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are usually lanceolate and possess a serrated margin.

Poecilopteris rostata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
