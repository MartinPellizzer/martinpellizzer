Plagianthus petiolaris

Plagianthus petiolaris, commonly known as the yellow mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to southern Africa.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along its slender stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, fleshy fruits that are dispersed by birds. It attaches itself to host trees, such as acacias, and derives some of its nutrients from them.

Its unique ecological role makes it an important species in its native habitat.
