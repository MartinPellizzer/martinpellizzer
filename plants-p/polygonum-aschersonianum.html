Polygonum aschersonianum

Polygonum aschersonianum, commonly known as red flame or Chinese lantern, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its striking red, orange, or yellow bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are actually modified leaves rather than true flowers. The plant is a member of the Polygonaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Polygonum aschersonianum is typically grown as an annual or biennial in temperate climates.
