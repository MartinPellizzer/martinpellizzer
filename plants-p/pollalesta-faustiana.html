Pollalesta faustiana

Pollalesta faustiana, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow flowers that resemble flames. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its botanical features include long, strap-like leaves and a slender stem that supports its showy inflorescence.

Pollalesta faustiana is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm climates.
