Piper lindbergii

Piper lindbergii, commonly known as the Lindberg's pepper or the white pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, a small berry, turns from green to red as it matures.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
