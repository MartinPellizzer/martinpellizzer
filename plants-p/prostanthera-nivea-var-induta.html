Prostanthera nivea var. induta

Prostanthera nivea var. induta, commonly known as the silky mint, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Prostanthera nivea var. induta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal remedies.
