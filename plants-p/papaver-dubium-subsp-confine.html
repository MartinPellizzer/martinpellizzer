Papaver dubium subsp. confine

Papaver dubium subsp.

confine, commonly known as the narrowleaf poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its delicate, nodding flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant features slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing capsule fruits that release seeds when mature.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
