Potentilla wimanniana proles andrzejowskii

Potentilla wimanniana proles andrzejowskii, commonly known as the Wiman's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
