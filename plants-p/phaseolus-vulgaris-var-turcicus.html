Phaseolus vulgaris var. turcicus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. turcicus, commonly known as the Turkish bean or Turkish kidney bean, is a variety of common bean native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, kidney-shaped seeds that are typically white or pale yellow in color. The plant produces upright, branching stems with trifoliate leaves and is often grown for its edible seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its culinary uses and has been cultivated for centuries in traditional agriculture.
