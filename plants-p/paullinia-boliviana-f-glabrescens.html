Paullinia boliviana f. glabrescens

Paullinia boliviana f. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous paullinia, is a variant of the species native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless foliage, which distinguishes it from other forms of the species. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a distinctive shape, often with serrated edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
