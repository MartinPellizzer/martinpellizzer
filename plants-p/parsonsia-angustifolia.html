Parsonsia angustifolia

Parsonsia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf parsonsia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family, characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces woody, branching stems. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy soils, preferring full sun exposure.

Parsonsia angustifolia is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking floral display.
