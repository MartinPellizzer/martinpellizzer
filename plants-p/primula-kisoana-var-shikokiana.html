Primula kisoana var. shikokiana

Primula kisoana var. shikokiana, commonly known as the Kiso primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features basal rosettes of velvety, ovate leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
