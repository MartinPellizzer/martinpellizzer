Primula melanodonta

Primula melanodonta, commonly known as the black-leaved primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dark green to almost black foliage, which contrasts strikingly with its showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces small, clustered blooms that typically appear in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
