Pleurogramme rostrata

Pleurogramme rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated pleurogramme, is a species of aquatic fern found in freshwater habitats.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are typically divided into small, serrated segments. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving waters, often in shaded environments. Its spores are released into the water, allowing for dispersal and propagation.

The botanical structure of Pleurogramme rostrata reflects its adaptation to aquatic life, with specialized tissues for buoyancy and nutrient absorption.
