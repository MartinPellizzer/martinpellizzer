Parasenecio hastatus var. hayachinensis

Parasenecio hastatus var. hayachinensis, commonly known as the Japanese hare's foot, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense heads. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
