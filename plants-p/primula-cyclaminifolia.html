Primula cyclaminifolia

Primula cyclaminifolia, commonly known as the cyclamen primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, purple, and white. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms. It belongs to the Primulaceae family, which includes other popular garden plants like primroses and lungworts.

Its foliage and flowers are both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable, supporting a range of pollinators.
