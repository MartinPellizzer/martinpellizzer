Polygala oxyphylla var. laureola

Polygala oxyphylla var. laureola, commonly known as the laurel polygala, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish, with a unique structure that resembles a bird's beak, and it produces small, round fruits.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in suitable conditions.
