Ptarmica multiflora

Ptarmica multiflora, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, shady environments.

Ptarmica multiflora is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it both a useful plant and a potential weed.
