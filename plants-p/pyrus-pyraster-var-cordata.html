Pyrus pyraster var. cordata

Pyrus pyraster var. cordata, commonly known as the heart-leaf pear, is a variant of the common pear species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves, which give it its botanical name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically small and tart. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along riverbanks.

The variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in horticulture.
