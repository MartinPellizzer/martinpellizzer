Procris frutescens

Procris frutescens, commonly known as the red-flowered rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features basal leaves that are oblanceolate and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are often branched and hairy. The plant produces small, bright red flowers with four petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Procris frutescens thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas within cloud forests.
