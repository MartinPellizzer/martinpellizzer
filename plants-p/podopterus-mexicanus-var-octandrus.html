Podopterus mexicanus var. octandrus

Podopterus mexicanus var. octandrus, commonly known as the Mexican spider flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils in open, sunny habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
