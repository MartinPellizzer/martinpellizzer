Prunus caudata var. obovata

Prunus caudata var. obovata, commonly known as the California laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often glossy and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark purple berries. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
