Philadelphus delavayi var. trichocladus

Philadelphus delavayi var. trichocladus, commonly known as the Trichocladus mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features showy, fragrant white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to gardens and botanical collections.
