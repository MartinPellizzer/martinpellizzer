Panicum subflabellatum

Panicum subflabellatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often wind-dispersed. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant communities and a potential candidate for ecological restoration projects.
