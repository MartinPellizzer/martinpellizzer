Pogostemon brachystachyus

Pogostemon brachystachyus, commonly known as Java mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often aromatic and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in spikes, typically ranging in color from white to pale purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Java mint is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
