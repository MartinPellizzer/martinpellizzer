Panicum tsaratananense var. diffusum

Panicum tsaratananense var. diffusum, commonly known as the Madagascar grass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, fluffy panicles of seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for erosion control in suitable environments.
