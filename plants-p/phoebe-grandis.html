Phoebe grandis

Phoebe grandis, commonly known as the broadleaf phoebe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and riverbanks. The plant has opposite, simple, and often elliptic-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale green and appear in clusters.

Phoebe grandis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
