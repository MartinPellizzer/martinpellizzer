Polypodium absidatum

Polypodium absidatum, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant's fronds are typically leathery and have a dark green color with a rough texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks.

Polypodium absidatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
