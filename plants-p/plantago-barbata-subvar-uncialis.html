Plantago barbata subvar. uncialis

Plantago barbata subvar. uncialis, commonly known as the narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and have a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
