Plantago rapensis

Plantago rapensis, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in meadows.

Botanically, it is valued for its medicinal properties and is part of the Plantaginaceae family.
