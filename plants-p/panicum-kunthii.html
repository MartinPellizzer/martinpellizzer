Panicum kunthii

Panicum kunthii, commonly known as silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, thriving in humid conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are often wind-pollinated.

Panicum kunthii plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
