Plananthus

Plananthus, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Plananthus is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
