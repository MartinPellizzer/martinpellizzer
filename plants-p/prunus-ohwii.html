Prunus ohwii

Prunus ohwii, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or mountain cherry, is a species of flowering shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to white blossoms that appear in early spring. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces small, dark red fruits in the summer.

Prunus ohwii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
