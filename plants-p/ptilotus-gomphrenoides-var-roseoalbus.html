Ptilotus gomphrenoides var. roseoalbus

Ptilotus gomphrenoides var. roseoalbus, commonly known as the pink saltbush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in arid environments. It thrives in sandy or saline soils and is often found in coastal regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh conditions.
