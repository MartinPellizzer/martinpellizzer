Pulsatilla vulgaris var. velezensis

Pulsatilla vulgaris var. velezensis, commonly known as the velezensis pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils and is adapted to cold climates. Its leaves are palmately divided and have a distinctive hairy texture.
