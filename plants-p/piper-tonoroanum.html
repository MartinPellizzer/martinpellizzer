Piper tonoroanum

Piper tonoroanum, commonly known as the Tonoroan pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of Madagascar and is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, peppery fruits. It is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.

Due to its restricted distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
