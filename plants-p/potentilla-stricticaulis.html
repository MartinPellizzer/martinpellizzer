Potentilla stricticaulis

Potentilla stricticaulis, commonly known as the upright potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in early summer, which are attractive to pollinators such as bees. Its stems are usually rigid and upright, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla stricticaulis is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for alpine gardens.
