Panicum airoides

Panicum airoides, commonly known as purpletop millet, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and vibrant purple to pink bracts. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its seeds are edible and have been used by indigenous peoples for food and medicinal purposes.

Panicum airoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
