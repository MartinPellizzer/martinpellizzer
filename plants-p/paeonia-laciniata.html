Paeonia laciniata

Paeonia laciniata, commonly known as the Japanese peony, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with intricate patterns. The plant features compound leaves composed of several lobes, which give it a distinctive, lace-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Paeonia laciniata is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its historical and cultural significance in East Asian horticulture.
