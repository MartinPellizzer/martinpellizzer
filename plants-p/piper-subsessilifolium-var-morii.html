Piper subsessilifolium var. morii

Piper subsessilifolium var. morii, commonly known as the Morii pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper subsessilifolium species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elongated leaves that are typically sessile or nearly sessile, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its botanical structure includes a woody stem with a rough, bark-like texture, and its leaves are usually arranged alternately along the stem.

This variant is valued for its aromatic properties and is often cultivated for both ornamental and practical purposes.
