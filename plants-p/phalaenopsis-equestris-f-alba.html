Phalaenopsis equestris f. alba

Phalaenopsis equestris f. alba, commonly known as the white orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, long-lasting white flowers that resemble moth wings, giving it its common name. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in low-light conditions. Its leaves are broad, dark green, and often have a waxy texture.

This variety is popular in horticulture for its beauty and ease of care in indoor environments.
