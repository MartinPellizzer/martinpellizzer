Pinalia hirsutipetala

Pinalia hirsutipetala, commonly known as the hairy-leaved pinalia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
