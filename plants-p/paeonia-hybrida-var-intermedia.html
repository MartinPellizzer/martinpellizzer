Paeonia hybrida var. intermedia

Paeonia hybrida var. intermedia, commonly known as the Intersectional peony, is a hybrid plant that combines traits of both tree peonies and herbaceous peonies.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including red, pink, white, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and has a bushy, upright growth habit. Its foliage consists of compound leaves with serrated edges, and it produces distinctive, fragrant blooms in late spring to early summer.

The plant is well-suited for gardens and is valued for its ornamental beauty and relatively low maintenance requirements.
