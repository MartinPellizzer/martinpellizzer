Pandanus thomensis

Pandanus thomensis, commonly known as the São Tomé pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the island of São Tomé in the Gulf of Guinea.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear yellowish-white flowers, which are often used in traditional crafts and rituals. Its adaptability to coastal and humid environments makes it an important component of the local ecosystem.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in the region.
