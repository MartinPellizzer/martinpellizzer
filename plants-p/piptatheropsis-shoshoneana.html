Piptatheropsis shoshoneana

Piptatheropsis shoshoneana, commonly known as the Shoshone grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, including sagebrush steppe and rocky soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in poor soil conditions.

Its botanical structure reflects its evolutionary adaptation to the harsh environments of its native range.
