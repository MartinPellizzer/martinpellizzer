Panicum semitectum

Panicum semitectum, commonly known as silver panicum, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fluffy, silver-gray seed heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

The plant has been used in traditional landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
