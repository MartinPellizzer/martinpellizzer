Peristrophe hereroensis

Peristrophe hereroensis, commonly known as the Herero grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brownish florets. It is often found in grasslands and open areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Peristrophe hereroensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, making it a resilient component of the local flora.
