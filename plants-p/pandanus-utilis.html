Pandanus utilis

Pandanus utilis, commonly known as the screw pine, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fibrous roots and tough, fibrous leaves have been traditionally used for making thatch, baskets, and other crafts.

The plant is well adapted to coastal and saline environments, making it a hardy and versatile species.
