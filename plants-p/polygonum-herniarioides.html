Polygonum herniarioides

Polygonum herniarioides, commonly known as the common knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive nodes and often zigzagging stems. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, along roadsides, and in woodland edges. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a prominent midrib, while the flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Polygonum herniarioides is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
