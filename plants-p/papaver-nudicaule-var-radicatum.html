Papaver nudicaule var. radicatum

Papaver nudicaule var. radicatum, commonly known as the slender poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features delicate, nodding flowers that range in color from white to pink, with a distinctive, slender stem. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a subtle yet striking floral display to its native environment.
