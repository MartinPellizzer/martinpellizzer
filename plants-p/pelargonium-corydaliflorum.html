Pelargonium corydaliflorum

Pelargonium corydaliflorum, commonly known as the sweet pepper geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has a compact growth habit with broad, dark green leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Pelargonium corydaliflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.
