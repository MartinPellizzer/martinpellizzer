Pisonia aculeata var. macranthocarpa

Pisonia aculeata var. macranthocarpa, commonly known as the red pisonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or limestone soils.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
