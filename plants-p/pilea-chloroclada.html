Pilea chloroclada

Pilea chloroclada, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its rosette of thick, waxy leaves that resemble small, rounded coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It is a popular houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Pilea chloroclada thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil.
