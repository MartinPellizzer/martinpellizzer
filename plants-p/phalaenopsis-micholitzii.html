Phalaenopsis micholitzii

Phalaenopsis micholitzii, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a sympodial orchid with a unique growth habit, characterized by its short, creeping rhizomes and pseudobulbs. The plant produces long, slender leaves that are typically light green in color. Its flowers are showy, with white petals and a distinctive pink or purple lip.

This epiphytic plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or branches in tropical rainforests.
