Pandiaka heudelotii var. subglobosa

Pandiaka heudelotii var. subglobosa, commonly known as the globular pandiaka, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its compact, spherical growth habit, with dense clusters of small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Native to southern Africa, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
