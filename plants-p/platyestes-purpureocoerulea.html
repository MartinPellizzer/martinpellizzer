Platyestes purpureocoerulea

Platyestes purpureocoerulea, commonly known as the purple-blue orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, bi-colored flowers that display shades of purple and cobalt blue. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, well-drained soils. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients efficiently from the surrounding environment.

The floral structure includes specialized petals and sepals that aid in pollination by attracting specific insect pollinators.
