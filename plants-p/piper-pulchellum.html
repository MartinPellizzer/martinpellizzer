Piper pulchellum

Piper pulchellum, commonly known as the beautiful pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus in the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, and these are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

The botanical characteristics of Piper pulchellum make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and pharmacological study.
