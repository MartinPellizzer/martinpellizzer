Phoebe laevis

Phoebe laevis, commonly known as the smooth phoebe, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon. The tree is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark purple berries that are edible for some bird species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
