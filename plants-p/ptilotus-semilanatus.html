Ptilotus semilanatus

Ptilotus semilanatus, commonly known as the sandhill flax-lily, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
