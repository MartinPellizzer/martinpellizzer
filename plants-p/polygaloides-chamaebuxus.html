Polygaloides chamaebuxus

Polygaloides chamaebuxus, commonly known as the dwarf milkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
