Potamogeton coloratus var. helodes

Potamogeton coloratus var. helodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter wide. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, and it thrives in shallow, freshwater habitats. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

This species is often found in lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.
