Polygonum manshuriense

Polygonum manshuriense, commonly known as the Manchurian knotweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and hollow stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a reddish or purple tinge.

The small, greenish flowers appear in clusters and are followed by triangular, beak-like fruits.
