Prunus pendula f. subsessilis

Prunus pendula f. subsessilis, commonly known as the weeping cherry with short pedicels, is a cultivar of the Prunus pendula species.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers in spring. The leaves are ovate and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the white blooms. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for landscape design.
