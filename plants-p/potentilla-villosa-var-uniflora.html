Potentilla villosa var. uniflora

Potentilla villosa var. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features a compact growth habit with wiry stems covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers are solitary, appearing in late spring to early summer, and are usually yellow or orange in color.

The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and covered with a dense layer of soft, white pubescence.
