Peperomia pachystachya

Peperomia pachystachya, commonly known as the velvet plant or Mexican peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It features thick, waxy, dark green leaves that are often adorned with silver or white veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.

Peperomia pachystachya is also valued for its air-purifying properties and its ability to enhance indoor environments.
