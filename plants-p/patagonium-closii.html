Patagonium closii

Patagonium closii, commonly known as the Patagonian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the harsh climate of Patagonia. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Patagonium closii plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various insects and small animals.
