Plectranthus chimanimanensis

Plectranthus chimanimanensis, commonly known as the Chimanimani plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
