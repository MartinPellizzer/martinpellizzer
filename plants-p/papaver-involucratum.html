Papaver involucratum

Papaver involucratum, commonly known as the velvetleaf poppy, is a species of flowering plant in the family Papaveraceae.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a short, upright stem with palmate leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant produces capsule-like fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Papaver involucratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
