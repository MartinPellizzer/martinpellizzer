Passiflora harlingii

Passiflora harlingii, commonly known as the Harling's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
