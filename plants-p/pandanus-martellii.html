Pandanus martellii

Pandanus martellii, commonly known as the mountain pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fibrous, spiky fruits. Its roots are fibrous and can be used for making ropes and mats.

Pandanus martellii thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove forests.
