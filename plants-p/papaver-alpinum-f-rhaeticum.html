Papaver alpinum f. rhaeticum

Papaver alpinum f. rhaeticum, commonly known as the Rhaetic Alpine poppy, is a botanical variant of the Alpine poppy.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe, particularly in the Alps and Carpathian mountains. The plant features showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in high-altitude environments.
