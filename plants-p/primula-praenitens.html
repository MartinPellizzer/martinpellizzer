Primula praenitens

Primula praenitens, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
