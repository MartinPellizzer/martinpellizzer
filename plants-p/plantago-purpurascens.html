Plantago purpurascens

Plantago purpurascens, commonly known as the purple plantain, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
