Pandanus cissei

Pandanus cissei, commonly known as the white pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the summer. Its roots are sometimes used in local medicine for their purported healing properties.

Overall, Pandanus cissei plays an important ecological and cultural role in its native regions.
