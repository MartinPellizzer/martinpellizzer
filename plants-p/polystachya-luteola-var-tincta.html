Polystachya luteola var. tincta

Polystachya luteola var. tincta, commonly known as the yellow-tinged spike orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces dense, yellow-green inflorescences with small, lance-shaped flowers. The plant typically thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
