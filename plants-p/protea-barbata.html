Protea barbata

Protea barbata, commonly known as the barbata protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, fluffy, pinkish-white flower heads that resemble a cluster of small, hairy spheres. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Protea barbata is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and is a symbol of South Africa's rich botanical diversity.
