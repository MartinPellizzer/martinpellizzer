Piper quentinii

Piper quentinii, commonly known as the Quentin's pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and potential medicinal properties.
