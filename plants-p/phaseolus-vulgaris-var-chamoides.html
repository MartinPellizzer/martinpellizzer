Phaseolus vulgaris var. chamoides

Phaseolus vulgaris var. chamoides, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a variant of the common bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers and long, edible pods that contain numerous seeds. The plant is typically a climbing vine with compound leaves and can grow up to several meters in height. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its edible beans.
