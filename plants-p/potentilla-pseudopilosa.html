Potentilla pseudopilosa

Potentilla pseudopilosa, commonly known as the woolly cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
