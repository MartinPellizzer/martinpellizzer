Ptychosperma nicolai

Ptychosperma nicolai, commonly known as the Nikoi palm, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, coconut-like fruits. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove ecosystems.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
