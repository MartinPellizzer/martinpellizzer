Pusaetha africana

Pusaetha africana, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Pusaetha africana also has potential uses in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
