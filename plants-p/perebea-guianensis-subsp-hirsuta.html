Perebea guianensis subsp. hirsuta

Perebea guianensis subsp.

hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy perebea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America. It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its rough, hairy bark and large, leathery leaves.

The plant typically grows in humid lowland forests and thrives in well-drained soils. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
