Prunus serrulata kabazakura

Prunus serrulata kabazakura, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is celebrated for its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a breathtaking floral display. The tree has a compact, rounded shape with smooth, reddish-brown bark that becomes flaky with age. Its leaves are oval-shaped and turn a vibrant yellow in autumn.

The plant is closely related to other cherry species and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and parks.
