Piper albidistigmatum

Piper albidistigmatum, commonly known as the white-stigma pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are typically small, round berries that turn red when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
