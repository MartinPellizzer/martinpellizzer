Prunus serrulata var. shirotae

Prunus serrulata var. shirotae, commonly known as the Shirotae cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate floral display to gardens and natural landscapes. The leaves are ovate with finely serrated edges, giving the plant its species name "serrulata.". This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a range of garden environments.
