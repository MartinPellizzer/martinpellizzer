Piper malacocarpum

Piper malacocarpum, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a peppery aroma, similar to other members of the genus Piper.

The plant is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for its unique flavor.
