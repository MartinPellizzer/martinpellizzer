Pseudanthus nitidus

Pseudanthus nitidus, commonly known as the smooth pseudanthus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in a compact inflorescence, giving it a daisy-like appearance. Its leaves are typically smooth and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Pseudanthus nitidus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas.
