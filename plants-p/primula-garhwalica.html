Primula garhwalica

Primula garhwalica, commonly known as the Garhwal primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.

Primula garhwalica plays a significant role in the local flora and is an important species for ecological studies in mountainous ecosystems.
