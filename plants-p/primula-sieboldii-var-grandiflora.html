Primula sieboldii var. grandiflora

Primula sieboldii var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Japanese primrose, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually broad and oval, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
