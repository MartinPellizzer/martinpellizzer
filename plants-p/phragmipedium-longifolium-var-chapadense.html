Phragmipedium longifolium var. chapadense

Phragmipedium longifolium var. chapadense, commonly known as the Chapada orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Brazil.

It features tall, erect stems that bear large, showy flowers with a distinctive greenish-yellow lip. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

Its unique floral structure and habitat preferences make it a notable species within the Phragmipedium genus.
