Phoenicanthus obliquus

Phoenicanthus obliquus, commonly known as the oblique flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with long, slender leaflets that give it a feathery appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The oblique flame tree is also valued for its ability to provide shade and support biodiversity in its ecosystem.
