Prunus heterocarpa

Prunus heterocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese plum or Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, which are typically white or pale pink. Its fruit is a small, yellow or orange drupe that ripens in summer.

Prunus heterocarpa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal interest.
