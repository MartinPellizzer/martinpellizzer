Paphiopedilum armeniacum f. markii

Paphiopedilum armeniacum f. markii, commonly known as the Markii Paphiopedilum, is a rare and highly sought-after orchid species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy flowers that typically feature a deep purple or violet coloration with intricate patterns. The plant produces a single, showy bloom per stem, often with a distinctive lip that is broader and more elaborate than in other Paphiopedilum species. It thrives in cool, humid environments with partial shade and requires well-draining, acidic soil.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is often cultivated in specialized orchid gardens and conservatories.
