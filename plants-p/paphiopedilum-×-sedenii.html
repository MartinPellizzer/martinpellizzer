Paphiopedilum × sedenii

Paphiopedilum × sedenii, commonly known as the hybrid lady's slipper orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from crosses between Paphiopedilum suaveolens and Paphiopedilum delenii.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, featuring a pseudobulb and fleshy roots adapted for storing water. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch, typically in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in warm, humid environments with partial shade and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
