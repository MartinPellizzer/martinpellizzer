Polygala urartu

Polygala urartu, commonly known as the Urartu milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually pinnately divided, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Polygala urartu is often found in mountainous areas and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
