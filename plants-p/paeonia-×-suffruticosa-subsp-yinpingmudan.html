Paeonia × suffruticosa subsp. yinpingmudan

Paeonia × suffruticosa subsp.

yinpingmudan, commonly known as the Yinping Mudan peony, is a deciduous shrub native to China. It is characterized by its robust stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces fragrant blooms in late spring. Its foliage consists of deeply lobed, dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to the vibrant flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
