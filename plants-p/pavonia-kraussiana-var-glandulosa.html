Pavonia kraussiana var. glandulosa

Pavonia kraussiana var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular pavonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This species thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
