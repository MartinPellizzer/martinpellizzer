Panicum utriculatum

Panicum utriculatum, commonly known as burr panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive seed heads that form small, round, bristly structures. The plant typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils and is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue when young.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
