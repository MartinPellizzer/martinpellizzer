Phyllostachys flexuosa

Phyllostachys flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is characterized by its slender, flexible stalks that often bend rather than break. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its culms are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of sustainable materials.

Phyllostachys flexuosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
