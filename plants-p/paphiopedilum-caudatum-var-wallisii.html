Paphiopedilum caudatum var. wallisii

Paphiopedilum caudatum var. wallisii, commonly known as the Wallisii lady's slipper, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped pouch that serves as a pollination trap, a feature typical of the Paphiopedilum genus. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid with fleshy, dark green leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. It prefers cool, humid environments with well-draining soil and partial shade.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its conservation.
