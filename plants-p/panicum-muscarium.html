Panicum muscarium

Panicum muscarium, commonly known as the witchgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled, dry fruits called caryopses. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Panicum muscarium is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
