Prunus cerasifera var. turcomanica

Prunus cerasifera var. turcomanica, commonly known as the Turcoman cherry, is a compact, deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It features small, ovate leaves that are dark green and turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and urban landscapes.
