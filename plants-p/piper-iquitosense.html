Piper iquitosense

Piper iquitosense, commonly known as the Iquitos pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest, particularly in Peru and Ecuador, where it thrives in humid, tropical conditions. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into peppery berries. It is closely related to other pepper species and shares similar chemical compounds that contribute to its spicy flavor.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
