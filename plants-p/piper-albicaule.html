Piper albicaule

Piper albicaule, commonly known as the white-stemmed pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, white-veined stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. It produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that develop into small, round berries.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
