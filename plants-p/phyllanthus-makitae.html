Phyllanthus makitae

Phyllanthus makitae, commonly known as the African date tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by fleshy, orange-red fruits that contain a single seed.

It is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
