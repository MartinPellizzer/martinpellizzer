Paepalanthus piscatorum

Paepalanthus piscatorum, commonly known as the fisherman's cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem covered in dense, woolly hair that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its distinct morphology and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists studying desert flora.
