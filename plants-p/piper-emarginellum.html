Piper emarginellum

Piper emarginellum, commonly known as the emarginate pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, woody fruits. Its botanical structure is characterized by a vigorous vine growth habit and a unique leaf morphology.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
