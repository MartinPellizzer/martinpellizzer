Palala macrophylla

Palala macrophylla, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for aquatic life. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and lakes. Its showy white flowers bloom at night, attracting specific pollinators such as beetles.

The plant's extensive root system helps stabilize the soil and prevent erosion in wetland areas.
