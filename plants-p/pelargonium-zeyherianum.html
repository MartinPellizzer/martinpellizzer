Pelargonium zeyherianum

Pelargonium zeyherianum, commonly known as the Zeyher's geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant produces vibrant blooms in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a distinctive dark center. Its leaves are typically rounded and covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

Pelargonium zeyherianum is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
