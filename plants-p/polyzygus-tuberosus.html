Polyzygus tuberosus

Polyzygus tuberosus, commonly known as the Jerusalem artichoke, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its edible tubers, which are rich in inulin, a type of dietary fiber. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant crop.

The tubers can be harvested in the fall and are used in various culinary applications.
