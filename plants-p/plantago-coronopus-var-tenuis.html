Plantago coronopus var. tenuis

Plantago coronopus var. tenuis, commonly known as slender plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, typically blooming in early summer. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist meadows and along streambanks.

This variant is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
