Penstemon attenuatus subsp. pseudoprocerus

Penstemon attenuatus subsp.

pseudoprocerus, commonly known as the slender beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its floral structure is adapted for pollination by long-tongued bees and hummingbirds.
