Psidium arasope-mi

Psidium arasope-mi, commonly known as the Hawaiian guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its oval, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, with a sweet and aromatic flavor. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
