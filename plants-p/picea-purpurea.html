Picea purpurea

Picea purpurea, commonly known as the purple spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Chile.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple-blue foliage, which is particularly striking during the winter months. The tree has needle-like leaves that are short and stout, arranged spirally along the branches. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Picea purpurea is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in areas with cool, moist climates.
