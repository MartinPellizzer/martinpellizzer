Pelargonium maximiliani

Pelargonium maximiliani, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that range from pink to red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.

Pelargonium maximiliani is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
