Potentilla × rosea

Potentilla × rosea, commonly known as the everblooming shrub or rose campion, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and profuse flowering throughout the growing season. The plant features small, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of its branches. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Potentilla × rosea is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
