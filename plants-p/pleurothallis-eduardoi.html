Pleurothallis eduardoi

Pleurothallis eduardoi, commonly known as the Eduardo's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem, contributing to its compact and elegant appearance.

Pleurothallis eduardoi is often grown as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid in cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
