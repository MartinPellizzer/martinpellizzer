Pyrus × superba

Pyrus × superba, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a hybrid species in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white to pink flowers in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet fruits that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Native to China, it thrives in temperate climates and well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and fruit cultivation.
