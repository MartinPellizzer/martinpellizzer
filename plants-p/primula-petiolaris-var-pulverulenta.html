Primula petiolaris var. pulverulenta

Primula petiolaris var. pulverulenta, commonly known as the dusty primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a slight purple tinge. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it its common name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or shaded habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
