Periploca oblongata

Periploca oblongata, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Periploca oblongata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
