Polygala pruinosa subsp. megaptera

Polygala pruinosa subsp.

megaptera, commonly known as the woolly milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features delicate, branched stems that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This species is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
