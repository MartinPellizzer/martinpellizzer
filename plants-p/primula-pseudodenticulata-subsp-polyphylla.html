Primula pseudodenticulata subsp. polyphylla

Primula pseudodenticulata subsp.

polyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China. It features basal rosettes of broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive wavy margin.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
