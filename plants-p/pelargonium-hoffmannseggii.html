Pelargonium hoffmannseggii

Pelargonium hoffmannseggii, commonly known as the Cape marigold, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as a shrub with upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and compact growth habit.
