Pilosella glaucescens subsp. decolor

Pilosella glaucescens subsp.

decolor, commonly known as the pale centaury, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features basal and stem leaves that are usually hairy and have a bluish-green color. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in loose heads, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

The subspecies decolor is distinguished by its reduced leaf hairs and a more pale appearance compared to other subspecies.
