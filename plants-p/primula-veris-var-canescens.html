Primula veris var. canescens

Primula veris var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy primrose, is a variant of the common primrose native to Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial shade. Its foliage is covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
