Phyllostegia macrophylla var. remyi

Phyllostegia macrophylla var. remyi, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
