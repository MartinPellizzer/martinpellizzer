Polygonum patens

Polygonum patens, commonly known as the prostrate knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit, with stems that often lie flat on the ground and produce roots at the nodes. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Polygonum patens is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in partial to full shade.
