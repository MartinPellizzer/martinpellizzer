Piper langlassei

Piper langlassei, commonly known as the Langlasse's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by elliptical, three-lobed berries.

The plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
