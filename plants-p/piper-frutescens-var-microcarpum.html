Piper frutescens var. microcarpum

Piper frutescens var. microcarpum, commonly known as the small-fruited pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces tiny, dark blue or black berries that are typically used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its botanical features include a branched stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a distinctive aromatic scent.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
