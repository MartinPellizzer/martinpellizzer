Pseudolysimachion orchideum

Pseudolysimachion orchideum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

The flowers bloom in late spring or early summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
