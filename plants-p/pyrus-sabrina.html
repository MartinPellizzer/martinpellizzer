Pyrus sabrina

Pyrus sabrina, commonly known as the sand pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible pears that are typically yellow or green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Pyrus sabrina is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
