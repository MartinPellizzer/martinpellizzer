Piper chagresianum

Piper chagresianum, commonly known as Chagres pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Central America and is characterized by its climbing habit and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, slender fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.
