Pandanus motuensis

Pandanus motuensis, commonly known as the Motu pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pandanus.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, specifically found on the island of Motu in the Solomon Islands. The plant is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that form a dense rosette, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often growing near the shoreline.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in the local communities.
