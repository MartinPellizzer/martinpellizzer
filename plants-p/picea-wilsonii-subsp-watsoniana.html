Picea wilsonii subsp. watsoniana

Picea wilsonii subsp.

watsoniana, commonly known as the Chinese white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to China. It belongs to the Pinaceae family and is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves.

The tree produces cones that are typically pendulous and contain winged seeds, facilitating wind dispersal. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its aesthetic features.
