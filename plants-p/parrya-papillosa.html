Parrya papillosa

Parrya papillosa, commonly known as the silky parrya, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in arid environments. The plant features small, clustered leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink and bloom in early spring.

Parrya papillosa is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in desert or semi-desert regions.
