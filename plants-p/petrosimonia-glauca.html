Petrosimonia glauca

Petrosimonia glauca, commonly known as the blue petrosimonia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, glaucous leaves that are covered in a waxy coating, helping to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom in the spring. It is adapted to survive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert habitats.

Petrosimonia glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
