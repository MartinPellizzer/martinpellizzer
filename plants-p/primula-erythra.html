Primula erythra

Primula erythra, commonly known as the red primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces clusters of flowers in early spring.

Primula erythra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
