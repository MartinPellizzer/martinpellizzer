Primula compsantha

Primula compsantha, commonly known as the Chinese primrose, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to China.

It features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in early spring and is well-suited for cool, moist environments. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its ornamental value and ease of cultivation contribute to its popularity among gardeners.
