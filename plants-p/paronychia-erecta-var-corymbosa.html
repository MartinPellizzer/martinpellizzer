Paronychia erecta var. corymbosa

Paronychia erecta var. corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing succulent with fleshy, branching stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, branched clusters. Its leaves are typically fleshy and adapted to store water, reflecting its succulent nature.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
