Pelargonium × rhodiola

Pelargonium × rhodiola, commonly known as the scented geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant leaves, which emit a variety of scents depending on the cultivar. such as citrus, mint, or rose. The plant typically has a trailing or bushy growth habit, with opposite, palmately lobed leaves. It produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, often followed by distinctive seed pods.

Native to South Africa, it is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
