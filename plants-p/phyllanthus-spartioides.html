Phyllanthus spartioides

Phyllanthus spartioides, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species adapted to drought conditions.

Phyllanthus spartioides is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
