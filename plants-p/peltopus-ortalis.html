Peltopus ortalis

Peltopus ortalis, commonly known as the common tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small yellow buttons. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas, and is known for its strong, aromatic scent.

Peltopus ortalis is also notable for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
