Piptochaetium verruculosum

Piptochaetium verruculosum, commonly known as the rough fescue, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, and its flowers are typically wind-pollinated. It is adapted to harsh environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

Piptochaetium verruculosum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and provide forage for livestock.
