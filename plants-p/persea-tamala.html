Persea tamala

Persea tamala, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, orange-yellow fruits that are edible when fully ripe. The fruit has a sweet, nutty flavor and is often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
