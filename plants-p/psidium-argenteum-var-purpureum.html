Psidium argenteum var. purpureum

Psidium argenteum var. purpureum, commonly known as the purple beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of deep purple berries that remain on the plant well into winter. The leaves are glossy and dark green, with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by the ornamental berries.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is relatively easy to grow in warm climates.
