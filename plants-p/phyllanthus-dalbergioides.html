Phyllanthus dalbergioides

Phyllanthus dalbergioides, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a globular, yellow berry that turns red when ripe, containing a single seed.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is widely used in traditional medicine systems.
