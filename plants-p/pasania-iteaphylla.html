Pasania iteaphylla

Pasania iteaphylla, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Pasania iteaphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
