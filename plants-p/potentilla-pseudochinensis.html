Potentilla pseudochinensis

Potentilla pseudochinensis, commonly known as the Chinese cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas and prefers well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
