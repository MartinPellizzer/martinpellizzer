Planchonella punctata

Planchonella punctata, commonly known as the spotted hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant has glossy, elliptical leaves with a smooth texture and a dark green color. Its flowers are yellow with red spots, and it produces small, round fruits that contain edible seeds.

Planchonella punctata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
