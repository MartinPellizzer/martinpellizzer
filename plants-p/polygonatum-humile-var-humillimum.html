Polygonatum humile var. humillimum

Polygonatum humile var. humillimum, commonly known as the lesser yellow flag or dwarf Solomon's seal, is a low-growing perennial herb native to East Asia.

It typically features slender, creeping rhizomes and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
