Primula moschophora

Primula moschophora, commonly known as the musk primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a slightly hairy texture.

Primula moschophora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
