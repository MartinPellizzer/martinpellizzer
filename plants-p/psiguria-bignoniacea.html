Psiguria bignoniacea

Psiguria bignoniacea, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are usually clustered in racemes and bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
