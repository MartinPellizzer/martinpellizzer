Plumeria arborescens

Plumeria arborescens, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has smooth, pale green bark and pinnately compound leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The flowers are typically borne on long, slender peduncles and bloom in the summer months.
