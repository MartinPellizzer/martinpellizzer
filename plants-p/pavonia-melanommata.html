Pavonia melanommata

Pavonia melanommata, commonly known as the black-stemmed pavonia, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, often hairy stems that range in color from green to black, depending on the season. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with finely toothed margins and a slightly pubescent surface.

Pavonia melanommata is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
