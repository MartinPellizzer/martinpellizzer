Prosopis mayana

Prosopis mayana, commonly known as the Maya mesquite, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry regions.

Its root system helps prevent soil erosion, contributing to its ecological importance in tropical and subtropical landscapes.
