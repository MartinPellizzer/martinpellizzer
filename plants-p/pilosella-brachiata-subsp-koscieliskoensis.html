Pilosella brachiata subsp. koscieliskoensis

Pilosella brachiata subsp.

koscieliskoensis, commonly known as the Koscielisko hairy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to central Europe. It is a perennial herb with basal leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive lobed shape.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Its subspecific name honors the Polish locality where it was first described.
