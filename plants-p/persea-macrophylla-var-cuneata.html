Persea macrophylla var. cuneata

Persea macrophylla var. cuneata, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits that are edible when fully ripe. The fruit has a high sugar content and a unique, sweet flavor when mature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of nutritious fruit.
