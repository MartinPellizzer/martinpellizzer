Portulaca erecta

Portulaca erecta, commonly known as the moss rose, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, typically during the warm seasons. It grows in a prostrate or upright manner, depending on environmental conditions, and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Portulaca erecta is a popular ornamental plant due to its resilience and attractive blooms.
