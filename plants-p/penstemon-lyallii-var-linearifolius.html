Penstemon lyallii var. linearifolius

Penstemon lyallii var. linearifolius, commonly known as the Lyall's beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of New Zealand.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
