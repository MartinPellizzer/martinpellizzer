Potentilla montenegrina f. minor

Potentilla montenegrina f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf potentilla, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of delicate yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, visually appealing plants.
