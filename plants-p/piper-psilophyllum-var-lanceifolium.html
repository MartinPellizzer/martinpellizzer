Piper psilophyllum var. lanceifolium

Piper psilophyllum var. lanceifolium, commonly known as the lanceleaf pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elliptical, peppery fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to tropical gardens and greenhouse collections.
