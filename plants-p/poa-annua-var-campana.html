Poa annua var. campana

Poa annua var. campana, commonly known as the bell bluegrass, is a cool-season grass species characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and dense growth habit.

It typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, lawns, and disturbed areas. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear blue to purple flowers, which are attractive to pollinators. Its leaves are usually narrow and bluish-green, with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and resilience.
