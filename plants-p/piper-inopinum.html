Piper inopinum

Piper inopinum, commonly known as the Malabar pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its vine-like growth habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a glossy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
