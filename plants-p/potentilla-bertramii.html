Potentilla bertramii

Potentilla bertramii, commonly known as Bertram's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, rounded leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suited to alpine and rocky environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
