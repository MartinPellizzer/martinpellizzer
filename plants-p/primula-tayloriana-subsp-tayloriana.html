Primula tayloriana subsp. tayloriana

Primula tayloriana subsp.

tayloriana, commonly known as Taylor's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white.

The leaves are typically oval and covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific climate requirements.
