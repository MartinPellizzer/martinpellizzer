Passiflora latifolia

Passiflora latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking, colorful flowers that resemble a crown, with intricate patterns of blue, purple, and white. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits known as passionfruits, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.

Passiflora latifolia also plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and birds.
