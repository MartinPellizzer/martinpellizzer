Prunus × hillieri

Prunus × hillieri, commonly known as the Hillieri cherry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Prunus lusitanica and Prunus africana.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 5–10 meters. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in spring. Its fruit is a drupe, often containing a single seed, and it is native to regions of southern Africa.

The Hillieri cherry is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
