Polygala zambesiaca

Polygala zambesiaca, commonly known as the Zambesi screwbean, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its unique, spirally arranged leaves and striking, spiny pods. The plant typically grows in woodland and grassland habitats, often in sandy or well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive shape that resembles a screw or a bean.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
