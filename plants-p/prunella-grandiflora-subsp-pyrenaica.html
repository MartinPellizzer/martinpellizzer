Prunella grandiflora subsp. pyrenaica

Prunella grandiflora subsp.

pyrenaica, commonly known as Pyrenean selfheal, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula and southern France. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with toothed margins. Its flowers are blue to purple, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
