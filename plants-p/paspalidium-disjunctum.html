Paspalidium disjunctum

Paspalidium disjunctum, commonly known as the disjointed grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand drought, making it a resilient species in harsh environments. Its growth habit is often sprawling or rhizomatous, allowing it to spread and colonize open areas effectively.

Paspalidium disjunctum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitats.
