Phlomis crinita

Phlomis crinita, commonly known as the curly beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
