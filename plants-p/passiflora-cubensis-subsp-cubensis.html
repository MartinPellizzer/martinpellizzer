Passiflora cubensis subsp. cubensis

Passiflora cubensis subsp.

cubensis, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a tropical climbing vine native to the Caribbean and northern South America. It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with five prominent sepals, five petals, and a crown-like structure of filaments.

The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

This species is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also valued for its medicinal properties.
