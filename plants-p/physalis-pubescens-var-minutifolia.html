Physalis pubescens var. minutifolia

Physalis pubescens var. minutifolia, commonly known as the dwarf groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale green fruits that enclose a cluster of seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Due to its unique appearance and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental or for its culinary potential.
