Pleurothallis curvifructa

Pleurothallis curvifructa, commonly known as the curling fruit orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are adapted for moisture retention in its natural habitat.

Pleurothallis curvifructa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
