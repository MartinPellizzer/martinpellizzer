Prunus × sieboldii var. sachalinensis

Prunus × sieboldii var. sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Far East of Russia and parts of Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched structure and small, oval-shaped leaves that turn a vibrant yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries that are popular in local cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowering display.

This variety is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
