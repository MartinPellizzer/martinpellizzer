Piper hirsutum var. carpinterae

Piper hirsutum var. carpinterae, commonly known as the Carpenters' pepper or wild pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and small, aromatic berries that range in color from yellow to red. The plant belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable subject for studies in plant morphology and ecological adaptation.
