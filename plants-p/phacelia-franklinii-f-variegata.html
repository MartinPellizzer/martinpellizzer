Phacelia franklinii f. variegata

Phacelia franklinii f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated blue flax, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and variegated leaves with white or yellow markings. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has a short growing season and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
