Phacelurus caespitosus

Phacelurus caespitosus, commonly known as the clustered beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy stems that often form compact clusters. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are opposite and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Phacelurus caespitosus is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, making it a hardy species found in alpine and mountainous regions.
