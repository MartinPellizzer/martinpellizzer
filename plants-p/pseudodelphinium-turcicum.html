Pseudodelphinium turcicum

Pseudodelphinium turcicum, commonly known as the Turkish delphinium, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a distinctive serrated edge.

Pseudodelphinium turcicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
