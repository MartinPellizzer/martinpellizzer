Potentilla multifida var. hypoleuca

Potentilla multifida var. hypoleuca, commonly known as the white-leaved cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that are usually white or pale green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
