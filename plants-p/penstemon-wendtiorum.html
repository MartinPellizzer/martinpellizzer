Penstemon wendtiorum

Penstemon wendtiorum, commonly known as Wendt's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing pink to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements contribute to its popularity among gardeners.
