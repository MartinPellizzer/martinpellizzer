Phoradendron herbert-smithii

Phoradendron herbert-smithii, commonly known as the Herbert-Smith's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that relies on host trees for water and nutrients.

It is native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia, and is typically found growing on the branches of eucalyptus and other hardwood trees. The plant produces small, white, fleshy berries that are an important food source for native birds and mammals. Its leaves are thick and leathery, adapted to reduce water loss in its humid, shaded habitat.

The species is also notable for its ability to form dense clusters, which can significantly impact the growth and health of its host trees.
