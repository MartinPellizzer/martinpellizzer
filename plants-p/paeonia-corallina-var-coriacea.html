Paeonia corallina var. coriacea

Paeonia corallina var. coriacea, commonly known as the coral peony, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central China.

It is characterized by its striking, coral-pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has deeply lobed, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and distinctive foliage.
