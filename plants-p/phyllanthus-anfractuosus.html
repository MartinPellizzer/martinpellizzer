Phyllanthus anfractuosus

Phyllanthus anfractuosus, commonly known as the African pear, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red berries that contain the seeds. It is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types and can thrive in both moist and dry conditions.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
