Plantago major var. flavovirens

Plantago major var. flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green plantain, is a variant of the common plantain, characterized by its distinctive yellow-green foliage.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant produces small, pale green flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are followed by oval, three-lobed capsules containing numerous seeds. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

This variety is often used in traditional medicine and as a hardy, low-maintenance ground cover in landscaping.
