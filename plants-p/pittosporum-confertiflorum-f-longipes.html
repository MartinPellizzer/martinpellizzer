Pittosporum confertiflorum f. longipes

Pittosporum confertiflorum f. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed pittosporum, is a unique variant of the pittosporum genus native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically evergreen and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its growth habit is generally shrub-like, with a compact form that makes it suitable for ornamental gardening.

This cultivar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
