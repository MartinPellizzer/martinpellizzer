Phanera aureifolia

Phanera aureifolia, commonly known as golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Phanera aureifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
