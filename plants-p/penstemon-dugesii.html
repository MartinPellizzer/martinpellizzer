Penstemon dugesii

Penstemon dugesii, commonly known as the desert penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh, dry conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Penstemon dugesii is often found in rocky soils and is a valuable species for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
