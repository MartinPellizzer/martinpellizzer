Pleioblastus chino f. tsukubensis

Pleioblastus chino f. tsukubensis, commonly known as the Tsukubensis bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to Japan.

It features slender, upright culms that are typically green to bluish-green in color, with a smooth, glossy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late spring, followed by narrow, linear leaves that are densely packed along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Due to its compact size and elegant appearance, it is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative filler in garden settings.
