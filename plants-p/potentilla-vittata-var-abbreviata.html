Potentilla vittata var. abbreviata

Potentilla vittata var. abbreviata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in alpine or subalpine regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant produces showy, yellowish to orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, often arranged in a compact, shrub-like form.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky or dry conditions.
