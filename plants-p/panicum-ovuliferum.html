Panicum ovuliferum

Panicum ovuliferum, commonly known as the ovule panic grass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed spikelets. Its reproductive structures, known as ovules, are unique and contribute to its classification within the genus Panicum.

Panicum ovuliferum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.
