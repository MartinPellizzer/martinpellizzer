Potentilla crantzii var. hirta

Potentilla crantzii var. hirta, commonly known as hairy cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
