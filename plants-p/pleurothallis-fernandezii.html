Pleurothallis fernandezii

Pleurothallis fernandezii, commonly known as the Fernandez's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, narrow leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare in the wild.
