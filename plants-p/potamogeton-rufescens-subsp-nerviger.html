Potamogeton rufescens subsp. nerviger

Potamogeton rufescens subsp.

nerviger, commonly known as the redstem pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green to reddish in color.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often found in shallow, slow-moving waters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its habitat.
