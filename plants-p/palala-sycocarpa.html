Palala sycocarpa

Palala sycocarpa, commonly known as the ghost gum, is a small evergreen tree native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Palala sycocarpa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
