Protium puncticulatum

Protium puncticulatum, commonly known as the punctate or spotted protium, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapindaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody capsules that contain numerous seeds. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
