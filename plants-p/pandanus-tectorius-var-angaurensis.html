Pandanus tectorius var. angaurensis

Pandanus tectorius var. angaurensis, commonly known as the Angaurensis pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, followed by edible fruits known as "pandanus nuts.". Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize coastal soils, making it useful for erosion control.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and cultural significance in island communities.
