Psychrogeton andryaloides var. poncinsii

Psychrogeton andryaloides var. poncinsii, commonly known as the Andean buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and is often found in rocky, alpine environments. The plant has a short, herbaceous stem with palmate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist conditions and is adapted to the harsh climate of its native habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
