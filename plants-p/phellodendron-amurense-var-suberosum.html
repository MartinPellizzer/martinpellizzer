Phellodendron amurense var. suberosum

Phellodendron amurense var. suberosum, commonly known as the Amur corktree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, cork-like bark that peels off in layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
