Planchonella parvifolia

Planchonella parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved sand apple, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually clustered at the base of the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, round fruits with a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland forest areas.

The species is valued for its fruit and has some traditional medicinal uses in its native regions.
