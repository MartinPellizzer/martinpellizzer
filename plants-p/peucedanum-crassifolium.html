Peucedanum crassifolium

Peucedanum crassifolium, commonly known as broad-leaved angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a robust, branched stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often hairy and reddish in color. The plant produces large, umbrella-shaped inflorescences with small white to greenish flowers, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its leaves are broad, pinnately compound, and have a distinctive, aromatic scent.

The roots are thick, fleshy, and valued for their medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
