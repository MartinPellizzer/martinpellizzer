Phragmites maritimus

Phragmites maritimus, commonly known as saltmarsh cordgrass, is a perennial grass that thrives in brackish or saline wetlands.

It has tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt. The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are wind-pollinated. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wetland species.

Its ability to tolerate high salinity makes it an important component of salt marsh ecosystems.
