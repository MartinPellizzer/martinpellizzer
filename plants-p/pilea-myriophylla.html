Pilea myriophylla

Pilea myriophylla, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its round, waxy leaves that resemble coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pilea myriophylla is a popular houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
