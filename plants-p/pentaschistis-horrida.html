Pentaschistis horrida

Pentaschistis horrida, commonly known as the roughleaf aster, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and showy yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in wetland environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
