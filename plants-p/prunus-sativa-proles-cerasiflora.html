Prunus sativa proles cerasiflora

Prunus sativa proles cerasiflora, commonly known as the cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces small, sweet, edible fruits that are typically harvested in the spring. The plant features smooth, grayish bark and alternately arranged, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are white or pink and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees.

The cherry plum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in culinary and medicinal applications.
