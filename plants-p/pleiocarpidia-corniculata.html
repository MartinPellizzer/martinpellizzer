Pleiocarpidia corniculata

Pleiocarpidia corniculata, commonly known as the horned pleiocarpidia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its distinctive feature is the horn-like shape of its seed pods, which give it its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
