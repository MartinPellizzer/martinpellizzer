Plantago bakeri

Plantago bakeri, commonly known as Baker's plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.

The species is also valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
