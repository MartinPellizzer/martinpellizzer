Pilosella vasconica

Pilosella vasconica, commonly known as the Spanish bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It features small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue.

Pilosella vasconica is often found in mountainous regions and is known for its delicate appearance and ecological significance.
