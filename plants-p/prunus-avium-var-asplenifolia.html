Prunus avium var. asplenifolia

Prunus avium var. asplenifolia, commonly known as the fern-leaved cherry, is a cultivar of the sweet cherry tree characterized by its distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are deeply lobed and resemble the structure of ferns, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, followed by sweet, edible fruits in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
