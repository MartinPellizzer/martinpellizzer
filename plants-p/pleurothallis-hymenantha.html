Pleurothallis hymenantha

Pleurothallis hymenantha, commonly known as the silver-veined orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid, typically growing on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with intricate patterns. Its flowers are often clustered in a raceme and bloom during the wet season.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in many regions.
