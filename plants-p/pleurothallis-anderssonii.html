Pleurothallis anderssonii

Pleurothallis anderssonii, commonly known as the Andes orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically during the rainy season. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and decaying organic matter.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
