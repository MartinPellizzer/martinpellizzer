Poivrea obscura var. boinensis

Poivrea obscura var. boinensis, commonly known as the Bornean firethorn, is a shrub native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery bloom. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
