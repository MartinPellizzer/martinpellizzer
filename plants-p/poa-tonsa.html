Poa tonsa

Poa tonsa, commonly known as slender meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, moist habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks. The plant has slender, tufted culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spike or panicle of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Poa tonsa is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
