Pyrus blinii

Pyrus blinii, commonly known as the Blinii pear, is a small deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring attractive foliage and showy white flowers in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
