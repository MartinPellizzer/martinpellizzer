Pyrus × platycarpa var. hoopesii

Pyrus × platycarpa var. hoopesii, commonly known as the Hoopes' pear, is a hybrid species within the genus Pyrus, characterized by its ornamental value and unique fruiting traits.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3–5 meters, featuring broad, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are usually yellow or green, though they are not typically consumed by humans. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and potential for fruit production.

The variety is noted for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
