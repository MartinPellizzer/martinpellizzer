Plectranthus drosocarpus

Plectranthus drosocarpus, commonly known as the purple-leaf plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple to pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a versatile plant for both ornamental and medicinal uses.
