Psephellus bagadensis

Psephellus bagadensis, commonly known as the Bagadense rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, preferring full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Psephellus bagadensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
