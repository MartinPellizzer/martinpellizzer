Psoralea intermedia

Psoralea intermedia, commonly known as the slender psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows in open, sandy or rocky habitats. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellowish-green and clustered in small inflorescences, attracting pollinators such as bees.

The species is noted for its adaptability to arid conditions and is often found in disturbed soils.
