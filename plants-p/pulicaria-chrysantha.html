Pulicaria chrysantha

Pulicaria chrysantha, commonly known as the yellow centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats like grasslands and coastal areas.

Pulicaria chrysantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
