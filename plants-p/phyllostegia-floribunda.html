Phyllostegia floribunda

Phyllostegia floribunda, commonly known as the flowering phyllostegia, is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are aromatic and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant's rapid growth and adaptability make it a common ornamental species in horticulture.
