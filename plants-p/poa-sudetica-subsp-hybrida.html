Poa sudetica subsp. hybrida

Poa sudetica subsp.

hybrida, commonly known as the Sudetic hybrid grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks.

The plant has slender, tufted culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose to dense panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
