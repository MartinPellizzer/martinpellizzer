Pilea muscosa var. portulacoides

Pilea muscosa var. portulacoides, commonly known as the pebble plant, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to Southeast Asia.

It features thick, fleshy, round leaves that resemble pebbles, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and ease of care, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
