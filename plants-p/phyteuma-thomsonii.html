Phyteuma thomsonii

Phyteuma thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's phyteuma, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. Thomson's phyteuma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
