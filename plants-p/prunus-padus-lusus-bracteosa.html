Prunus padus lusus bracteosa

Prunus padus lusus bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteosa bird cherry, is a rare and lesser-known variant of the bird cherry species.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, edible berries in the summer. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a slightly hairy texture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique botanical features.
