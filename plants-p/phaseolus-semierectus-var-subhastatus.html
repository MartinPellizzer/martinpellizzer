Phaseolus semierectus var. subhastatus

Phaseolus semierectus var. subhastatus, commonly known as the hairy pole bean, is a climbing legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces elongated, narrow pods that contain small, round beans. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its edible beans, which are used in various cuisines.
