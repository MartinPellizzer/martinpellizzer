Perityle bisetosa var. spathulata

Perityle bisetosa var. spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed perityle, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, spathulate (spade-shaped) leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense, flat-topped inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
