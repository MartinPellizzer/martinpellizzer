Pisum smyrnense

Pisum smyrnense, commonly known as the Syrian pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to pods containing several seeds. It is adapted to arid conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its historical significance and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
