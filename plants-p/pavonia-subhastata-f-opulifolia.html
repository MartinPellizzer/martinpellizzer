Pavonia subhastata f. opulifolia

Pavonia subhastata f. opulifolia, commonly known as the showy pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, velvety purple to red flowers. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, native flora.
