Poa trivialis f. prorepens

Poa trivialis f. prorepens, commonly known as the prostrate meadow grass, is a low-growing variant of the common bentgrass.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, forming dense mats on the ground. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats such as lawns, meadows, and roadside areas.

Its botanical features make it a resilient and adaptable species in various ecological settings.
