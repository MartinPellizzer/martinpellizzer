Piper virginicum

Piper virginicum, commonly known as American pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that develop into dark blue to black berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and are also a food source for certain wildlife species.
