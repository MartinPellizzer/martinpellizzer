Pseudosasa yuelushanensis

Pseudosasa yuelushanensis, commonly known as the Yuelushan bamboo, is a species native to China, specifically found in the Yuelushan area of Zhejiang Province.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright culms that can reach heights of up to 5 meters. The plant has narrow, lanceolate leaves that are typically a vibrant green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
