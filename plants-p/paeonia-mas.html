Paeonia mas

Paeonia mas, commonly known as the tree peony, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive fragrance. The plant has palmately compound leaves and produces woody stems that can grow up to several meters in height. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The botanical features of Paeonia mas make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
