Palala lemanniana

Palala lemanniana, commonly known as the Lemon Tree of Madagascar, is a unique species native to the island of Madagascar.

It is a small tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a round, fleshy drupe with a distinctive yellowish skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp.

This plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
