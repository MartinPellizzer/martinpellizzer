Ptilopteris craspedosora

Ptilopteris craspedosora, commonly known as the narrow-leaf huperzia, is a fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant has a unique reproductive structure called a synangium, which is a compact cluster of sporangia. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
