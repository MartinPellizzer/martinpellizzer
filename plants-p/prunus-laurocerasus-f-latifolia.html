Prunus laurocerasus f. latifolia

Prunus laurocerasus f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cherry laurel, is a cultivar of the common cherry laurel.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are typically darker green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark blue berries. It is a slow-growing evergreen shrub or small tree that thrives in USDA hardiness zones 6 through 9.

Its dense foliage and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for hedges and ornamental gardens.
