Panicum italicum var. macrochaetum

Panicum italicum var. macrochaetum, commonly known as Italian red millet, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, feathery panicles that produce numerous small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing reddish-brown grains that are used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and delicate appearance, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
