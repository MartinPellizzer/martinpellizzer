Potentilla frigida subsp. minima

Potentilla frigida subsp.

minima, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to cold regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with hairy, rounded leaves that help it retain heat in harsh environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine tundra and rocky slopes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience.
