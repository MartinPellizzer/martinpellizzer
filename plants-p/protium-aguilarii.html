Protium aguilarii

Protium aguilarii, commonly known as the Mexican tamarind.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves with a leathery texture. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a woody, spiny capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
