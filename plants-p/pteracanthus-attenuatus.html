Pteracanthus attenuatus

Pteracanthus attenuatus, commonly known as the slender thorned cactus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its adaptations include thick, fleshy stems for water storage and reduced leaves to minimize water loss.

Pteracanthus attenuatus is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to harsh, dry environments.
