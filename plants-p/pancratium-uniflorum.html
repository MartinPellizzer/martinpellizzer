Pancratium uniflorum

Pancratium uniflorum, commonly known as the yellow palm or sea daffodil, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has a single, erect stem with a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are typically trumpet-shaped and have a strong, sweet fragrance.

Pancratium uniflorum is often found growing in sandy soils near the coast and is adapted to tolerate salt spray and coastal conditions.
