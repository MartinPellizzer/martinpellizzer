Pistacia weinmanniifolia var. locii

Pistacia weinmanniifolia var. locii, commonly known as the Hawaiian pistache, is a shrub or small tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, reddish drupes. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to the unique climate of Hawaii.
