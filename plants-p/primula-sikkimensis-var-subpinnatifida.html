Primula sikkimensis var. subpinnatifida

Primula sikkimensis var. subpinnatifida, commonly known as the Sikkim primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in cool, moist environments at high altitudes. It is often found in forest understories and rocky slopes, where it contributes to the biodiversity of alpine ecosystems.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
