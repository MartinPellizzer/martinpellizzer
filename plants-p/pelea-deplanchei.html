Pelea deplanchei

Pelea deplanchei, commonly known as the Deplanche's pelea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
