Picris sonchoides

Picris sonchoides, commonly known as common groundsel, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas. It has a taproot system and produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is considered a weed in many regions.
