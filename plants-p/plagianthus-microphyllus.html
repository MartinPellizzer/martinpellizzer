Plagianthus microphyllus

Plagianthus microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense spikes. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
