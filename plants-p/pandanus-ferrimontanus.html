Pandanus ferrimontanus

Pandanus ferrimontanus, commonly known as the New Guinea pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the Pacific islands, including New Guinea and parts of Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the base of the leaves.

The plant is well adapted to coastal and wetland environments, thriving in humid, tropical climates.
