Phlox hirsuta

Phlox hirsuta, commonly known as the downy phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Phlox hirsuta is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
