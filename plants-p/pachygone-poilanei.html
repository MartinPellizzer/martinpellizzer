Pachygone poilanei

Pachygone poilanei, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant has a compact growth habit with thick, fleshy leaves that help it store water in its arid native habitat. It blooms during the dry season, producing fragrant flowers that attract specific pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
