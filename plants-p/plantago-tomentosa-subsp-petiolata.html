Plantago tomentosa subsp. petiolata

Plantago tomentosa subsp.

petiolata, commonly known as the hairy plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features a basal rosette of broad, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces erect flowering stems that bear dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a waxy surface, and it thrives in moist, disturbed soils.

This subspecies is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along riverbanks.
