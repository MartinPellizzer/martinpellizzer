Pleurothallis fornicata

Pleurothallis fornicata, commonly known as the "Cape Cod orchid," is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a distinct, intricate pattern on the petals. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Pleurothallis fornicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
