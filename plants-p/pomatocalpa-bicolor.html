Pomatocalpa bicolor

Pomatocalpa bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its strikingly contrasting coloration, with white and pink petals that create a visually appealing display. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are fragrant and bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to cultivation.
