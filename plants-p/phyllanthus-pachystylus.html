Phyllanthus pachystylus

Phyllanthus pachystylus, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, green berries that turn red when mature. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional African medicine.
