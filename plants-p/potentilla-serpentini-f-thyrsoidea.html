Potentilla serpentini f. thyrsoidea

Potentilla serpentini f. thyrsoidea, commonly known as the serpentine potentilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to grow in nutrient-poor, calcareous soils, often found in rocky or alpine environments. Its leaves are usually simple and alternate, with a slightly serrated margin.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
