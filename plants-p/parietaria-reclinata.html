Parietaria reclinata

Parietaria reclinata, commonly known as the creeping wall-rocket, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in coastal areas and dry habitats. The plant features small, alternate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and it produces tiny yellow flowers in clusters. It is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments due to its drought tolerance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
