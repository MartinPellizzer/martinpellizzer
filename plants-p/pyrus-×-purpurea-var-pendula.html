Pyrus × purpurea var. pendula

Pyrus × purpurea var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping pear, is a cultivar of the European pear.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that droop gracefully, creating a distinctive cascading effect. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically yellow or red in color. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

This ornamental tree is often planted for its aesthetic value in gardens and public spaces.
