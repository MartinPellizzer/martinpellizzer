Polygala lacei

Polygala lacei, commonly known as the lacey milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. It has small, hairy stems and produces small, bean-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
