Prunus runemarkii

Prunus runemarkii, commonly known as the Tibetan cherry, is a species of flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Tibet and adjacent regions.

It is characterized by its small, dark red or black berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in early spring.

Prunus runemarkii is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its resilience in harsh environments.
