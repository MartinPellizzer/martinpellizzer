Piper staminodiferum

Piper staminodiferum, commonly known as the pink pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, pinkish-purple berries that resemble peppercorns. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are often overlooked due to their modest appearance. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly glossy texture.

Piper staminodiferum is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in culinary applications, though it is not as widely cultivated as black or white pepper.
