Prunus avium subsp. sylvestris

Prunus avium subsp.

sylvestris, commonly known as the wild cherry, is a subspecies of the sweet cherry. It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces small, dark red to black fruits that are typically smaller than those of cultivated cherries. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
