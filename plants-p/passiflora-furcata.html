Passiflora furcata

Passiflora furcata, commonly known as the bifurcated passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and are used in some local herbal remedies.

Passiflora furcata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.
