Parodia salmonea var. applanata

Parodia salmonea var. applanata, commonly known as the Salmonea Paddle Cactus, is a compact, globular cactus native to Mexico.

It features a smooth, pale green to bluish-green body with a flattened, paddle-like shape. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on short, erect stems. It has shallow, widespread roots and is well adapted to arid environments.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
