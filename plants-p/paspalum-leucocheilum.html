Paspalum leucocheilum

Paspalum leucocheilum, commonly known as white-lipped paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Paspalum leucocheilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
