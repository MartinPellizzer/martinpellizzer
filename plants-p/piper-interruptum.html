Piper interruptum

Piper interruptum, commonly known as the interrupted pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and emits a mild peppery scent. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by elongated, berry-like fruits that turn red when mature.

Piper interruptum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
