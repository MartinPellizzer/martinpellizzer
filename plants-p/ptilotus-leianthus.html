Ptilotus leianthus

Ptilotus leianthus, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow floral spikes that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Ptilotus leianthus is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes many leguminous plants.
