Paspalum aristatum

Paspalum aristatum, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy areas, thriving in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, delicate flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Paspalum aristatum is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and adaptability to waterlogged environments.
