Protea incana f. transvaaliensis

Protea incana f. transvaaliensis, commonly known as the Transvaal protea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its striking, cone-shaped inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish bracts. The plant is adapted to nutrient-poor soils, typical of its natural habitat in the fynbos biome. It thrives in full sun and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

The Transvaal protea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
