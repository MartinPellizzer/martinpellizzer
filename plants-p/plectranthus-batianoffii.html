Plectranthus batianoffii

Plectranthus batianoffii, commonly known as the African violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of violet, pink, or white, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it both decorative and easy to care for.
