Piper konkintoense

Piper konkintoense, commonly known as the Kona pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, and the fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The botanical characteristics of Piper konkintoense make it an important species for both ecological and culinary purposes.
