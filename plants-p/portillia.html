Portillia

Portillia, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow flowers. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for urban landscapes. Its fruit is a legume pod that splits open to release its seeds, contributing to its natural spread.

Portillia is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
