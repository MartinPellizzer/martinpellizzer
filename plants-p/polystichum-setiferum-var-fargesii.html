Polystichum setiferum var. fargesii

Polystichum setiferum var. fargesii, commonly known as the Farges' deer fern, is a variant of the silver fern native to China.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped fronds that are adorned with silky, white tomentum on the underside. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are often clustered at the base, forming a dense, upright rosette.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
