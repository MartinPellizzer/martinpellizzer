Phrynium guppyi

Phrynium guppyi, commonly known as the Guppy's arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often heart-shaped or arrowhead-shaped, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale green flowers, which are typically borne on tall, erect stems. Phrynium guppyi is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
