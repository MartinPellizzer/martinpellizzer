Praealstonia coriacea

Praealstonia coriacea, commonly known as the rough-stemmed rock fig, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rugged, woody stems and thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round, fig-like structure that turns yellow when mature.

Praealstonia coriacea is adapted to arid environments and is often found growing on rocky outcrops.
