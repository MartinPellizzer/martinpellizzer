Perlarius repandus

Perlarius repandus, commonly known as the broadleaf perlarius, is a species of flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant in both wild and cultivated settings.
