Peperomia pilifera

Peperomia pilifera, commonly known as the hairleaf peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, ovate leaves with fine, hair-like veins that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of green and white, which are typically borne on short, upright spikes. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia pilifera is relatively low maintenance, requiring minimal water and tolerating a range of indoor conditions.
