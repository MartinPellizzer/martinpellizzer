Piper agellifolium

Piper agellifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into peppercorn-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and pharmacological study.
