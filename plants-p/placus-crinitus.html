Placus crinitus

Placus crinitus, commonly known as the hairy bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant belonging to the family Lentibulariaceae.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap tiny aquatic animals. The plant typically grows in shallow, nutrient-poor waters, where it uses its specialized organs to supplement its nutrient intake. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which aid in trapping prey.

The plant reproduces both sexually through seeds and vegetatively through runners, allowing it to spread in suitable habitats.
