Pyrus glabrescens

Pyrus glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth pear, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart pears in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and stream valleys.

Pyrus glabrescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in horticulture.
