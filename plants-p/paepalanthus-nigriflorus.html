Paepalanthus nigriflorus

Paepalanthus nigriflorus, commonly known as the black-flowered puya, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence topped with dark purple to black flowers. The plant produces showy, tubular bracts that surround the inflorescence, adding to its dramatic appearance. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cool, moist conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
