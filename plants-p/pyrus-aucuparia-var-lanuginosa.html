Pyrus aucuparia var. lanuginosa

Pyrus aucuparia var. lanuginosa, commonly known as the flaky pear, is a variant of the common pear tree characterized by its distinctive, flaky, and fuzzy bark.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a moderate height, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The flowers are white and clustered, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge. The fruit is small, yellowish, and somewhat tart, with a thin skin.

This variety is valued for its ornamental features and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
