Peutalis punctata

Peutalis punctata, commonly known as the spotted peperomia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and thick, waxy leaves. The leaves are often marked with distinctive spots or streaks, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color.

Peutalis punctata is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
