Passiflora bicrura

Passiflora bicrura, commonly known as the twin-veined passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a crown of filaments and a unique arrangement of sepals and petals. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by wildlife and humans alike. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture and a strong, distinctive scent.

Passiflora bicrura is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
