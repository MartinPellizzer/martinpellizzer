Pilosella caespitosa subsp. brevipila

Pilosella caespitosa subsp.

brevipila, commonly known as the short-pilose hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed sites.
