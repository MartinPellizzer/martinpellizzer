Peperomia verschaffeltii

Peperomia verschaffeltii, commonly known as the Verschaffelt's peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink, giving it an ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia verschaffeltii is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests, contributing to its appeal among plant enthusiasts.
