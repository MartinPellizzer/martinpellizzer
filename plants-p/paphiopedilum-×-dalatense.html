Paphiopedilum × dalatense

Paphiopedilum × dalatense, commonly known as the Darwin's orchid, is a hybrid species of the genus Paphiopedilum.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with a unique lip that resembles a pouch, a feature common among orchids of this genus. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid, depending on the environmental conditions. It thrives in humid, warm climates with indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its foliage consists of broad, dark green leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern.
