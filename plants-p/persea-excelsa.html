Persea excelsa

Persea excelsa, commonly known as the Brazilian avocado or the yellow avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree bears fruit that is typically pear-shaped with a smooth, thin skin and a creamy, oily pulp. The fruit is rich in healthy fats, vitamins, and minerals, making it a valuable food source.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value.
