Phaca caespitosa

Phaca caespitosa, commonly known as the clustered phaca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grassland habitats, often forming dense clusters of stems. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is adapted to survive in dry, well-drained soils.

Phaca caespitosa is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and ecological role in its native environment.
