Pelargonium × obtusidentatum

Pelargonium × obtusidentatum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming from spring to autumn. Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

It is popular in gardens and as a potted plant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
