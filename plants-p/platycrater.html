Platycrater

Platycrater, commonly known as the Chinese mountain ash, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Hamamelidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, flat, shield-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Platycrater thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Its unique foliage and seasonal color changes make it a visually appealing addition to landscapes.
