Pultenaea parviflora

Pultenaea parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes many legumes, and it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Pultenaea parviflora typically grows in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
