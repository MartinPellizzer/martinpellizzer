Phaeopappus obtusifolius

Phaeopappus obtusifolius, commonly known as the purple pappus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple or violet bracts that surround the flower heads, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, yellow flowers that are surrounded by the showy bracts.

Phaeopappus obtusifolius is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant color and unique structure.
