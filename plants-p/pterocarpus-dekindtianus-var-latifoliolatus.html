Pterocarpus dekindtianus var. latifoliolatus

Pterocarpus dekindtianus var. latifoliolatus, commonly known as the broad-leaf pterocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces distinctive, woody pods containing flat, fibrous seeds. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its hard, heavy wood that is often used in traditional crafts and furniture. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree also has cultural significance in some regions where it is used in religious ceremonies and traditional medicine.
