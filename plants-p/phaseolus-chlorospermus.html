Phaseolus chlorospermus

Phaseolus chlorospermus, commonly known as the yellow bean plant, is a species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and pods that contain yellow-seeded beans. The plant is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet having a serrated margin.

The botanical features of Phaseolus chlorospermus make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
