Panicum trachystachyum var. angustifolium

Panicum trachystachyum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swampy areas. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spikelet that produces small, spike-like flowers.

This variety is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
