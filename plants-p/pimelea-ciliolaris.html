Pimelea ciliolaris

Pimelea ciliolaris, commonly known as the silver leaf bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves and distinctive bottlebrush-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and dry forests.

Pimelea ciliolaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
