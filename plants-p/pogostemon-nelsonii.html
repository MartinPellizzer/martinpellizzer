Pogostemon nelsonii

Pogostemon nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's pogostemon, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue or violet, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Pogostemon nelsonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
