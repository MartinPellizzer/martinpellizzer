Piper metanum

Piper metanum, commonly known as the purple pepper plant, is a tropical vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant is characterized by its dark purple or black berries, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a glossy surface and a strong, peppery aroma.

Piper metanum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
