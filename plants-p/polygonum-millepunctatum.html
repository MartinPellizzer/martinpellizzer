Polygonum millepunctatum

Polygonum millepunctatum, commonly known as the dotted smartweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist soils along streambanks, wetlands, and disturbed areas.

The plant is notable for its numerous small, dot-like markings on the stems, which give it its common name.
