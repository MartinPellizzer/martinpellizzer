Pavonia kraussiana subsp. craspedocarpa

Pavonia kraussiana subsp.

craspedocarpa, commonly known as the Cape rose or red sandalwood, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces clusters of small, showy pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice in horticulture.
