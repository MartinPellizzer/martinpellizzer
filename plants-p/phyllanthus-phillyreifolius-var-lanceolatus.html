Phyllanthus phillyreifolius var. lanceolatus

Phyllanthus phillyreifolius var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant is characterized by its lanceolate leaves, which are usually opposite or alternate in arrangement. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
