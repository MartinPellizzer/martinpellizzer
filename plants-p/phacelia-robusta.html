Phacelia robusta

Phacelia robusta, commonly known as the robust phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Phacelia robusta is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting pollinators.
