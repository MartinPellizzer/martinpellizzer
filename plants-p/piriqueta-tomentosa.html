Piriqueta tomentosa

Piriqueta tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly piriqueta, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, or tomentose, leaves and stems, which provide a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical or subtropical regions.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
