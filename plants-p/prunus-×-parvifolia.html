Prunus × parvifolia

Prunus × parvifolia, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a small deciduous tree or shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, typically white or pale pink, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
