Parkia roxburghii

Parkia roxburghii, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a leguminous tree native to tropical Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by long, flat pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Parkia roxburghii is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in traditional medicine and local economies.
