Phlomoides codonocalyx

Phlomoides codonocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
