Polygala baccifera

Polygala baccifera, commonly known as the sweet flag, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, herbaceous stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in spikes, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
