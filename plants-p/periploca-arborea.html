Periploca arborea

Periploca arborea, commonly known as the tree of life, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stem and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries containing seeds. The plant has traditional medicinal uses and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
