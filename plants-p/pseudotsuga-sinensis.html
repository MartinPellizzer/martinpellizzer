Pseudotsuga sinensis

Pseudotsuga sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese hemlock, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of conifers. It thrives in temperate climates with moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
