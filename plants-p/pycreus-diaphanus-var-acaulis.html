Pycreus diaphanus var. acaulis

Pycreus diaphanus var. acaulis, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and striking member of the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, pale blue or white flowers that appear in umbels, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant is notable for its lack of a visible stem, as the leaves are directly attached to the root system, a feature reflected in its scientific name "acaulis.". It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or along stream banks.

Due to its rarity and distinctive beauty, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
