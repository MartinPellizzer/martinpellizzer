Paspalum distichum var. vaginatum

Paspalum distichum var. vaginatum, commonly known as the silvergrass or velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
