Polyscalis capitata

Polyscalis capitata, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flower heads that resemble a crown. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers arranged in a dense cluster at the top of the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Polyscalis capitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
