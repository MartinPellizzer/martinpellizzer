Puccinellia distans subsp. festuciformis

Puccinellia distans subsp.

festuciformis, commonly known as the slender glassworts, is a perennial grass-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions. It belongs to the family Poaceae and is characterized by its erect, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in sandy or saline soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it is often found in coastal or inland dune environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms.
