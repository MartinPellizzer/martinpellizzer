Passiflora faroana

Passiflora faroana, commonly known as the Faroan passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. The plant produces bright orange or yellow berries that are edible and contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject in horticulture and botanical studies.
