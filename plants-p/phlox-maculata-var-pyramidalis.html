Phlox maculata var. pyramidalis

Phlox maculata var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramid phlox, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy clusters of pink to red flowers in the summer. The plant has upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, low-maintenance floral displays.
