Pisonia subferruginosa

Pisonia subferruginosa, commonly known as the red pisonia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces small, round, orange fruits that contain numerous seeds covered in a sticky, viscid substance. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

Pisonia subferruginosa thrives in coastal areas and is often found in mangrove ecosystems.
