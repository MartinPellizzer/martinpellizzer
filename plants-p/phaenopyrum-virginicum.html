Phaenopyrum virginicum

Phaenopyrum virginicum, commonly known as the Virginia pignut, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes plants like carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.
