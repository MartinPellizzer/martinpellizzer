Pityrogramma ornithopteris f. denudata

Pityrogramma ornithopteris f. denudata, commonly known as the naked fern, is a variant of the bird-winged fern.

It belongs to the family Pityrogrammaceae and is characterized by its delicate, frond-like structures. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in shady gardens.
