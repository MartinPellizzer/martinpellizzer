Paspalum mucronatum

Paspalum mucronatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass or Mexican needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and open grasslands, and is adapted to both wet and dry conditions.

Paspalum mucronatum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
