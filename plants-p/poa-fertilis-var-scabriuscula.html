Poa fertilis var. scabriuscula

Poa fertilis var. scabriuscula, commonly known as the rough-stemmed meadow grass, is a variant of the meadow grass species Poa fertilis.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its coarse, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often slightly curved.

The plant is well adapted to nutrient-rich soils and is often found in areas with regular moisture.
