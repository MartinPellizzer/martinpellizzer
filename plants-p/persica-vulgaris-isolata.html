Persica vulgaris isolata

Persica vulgaris isolata, commonly known as the isolated peach.

This plant is a variant of the peach tree, characterized by its distinct genetic isolation. It typically exhibits features similar to the common peach, such as pink flowers and fuzzy fruit. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its hardy nature.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a genetic resource for breeding programs.
