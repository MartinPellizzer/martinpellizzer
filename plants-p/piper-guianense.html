Piper guianense

Piper guianense, commonly known as Guianese pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, including Guyana, Suriname, and parts of Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by small, round berries that contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

Piper guianense is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
