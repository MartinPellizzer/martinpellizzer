Pediomelum verdiense

Pediomelum verdiense, commonly known as the Verde Valley dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with a rough, serrated texture. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its yellow flowers bloom in the spring, followed by the characteristic puffball seed heads that disperse seeds via the wind.

The species is noted for its adaptability to harsh conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystem.
