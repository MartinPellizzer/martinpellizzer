Piper villipetiolatum

Piper villipetiolatum, commonly known as the slender-leafed pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that develop into reddish berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and antimicrobial properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued both for its ornamental appeal and medicinal uses.
