Piper leptocladum

Piper leptocladum, commonly known as the slender-leaved pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Piper leptocladum is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
