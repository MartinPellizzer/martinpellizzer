Poa nahuelhuapiensis

Poa nahuelhuapiensis, commonly known as the Nahuelhuapi grass, is a perennial species native to the Patagonian region of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in high-altitude grasslands and along riverbanks.

Botanically, it is noted for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions, making it an important component of local ecosystems.
