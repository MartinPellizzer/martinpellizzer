Phaulopsis obliqua

Phaulopsis obliqua, commonly known as the zigzag grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive zigzagging inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture, and it produces small, spike-like flowers during the summer months.

Phaulopsis obliqua plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
