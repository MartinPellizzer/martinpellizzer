Persea perseiphylla

Persea perseiphylla, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, orange to reddish fruits that are typically eaten raw or used in culinary applications. Persea perseiphylla thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
