Plantago argentina

Plantago argentina, commonly known as the Argentine plantain, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically pollinated by wind. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils, and is often found in temperate regions.

Due to its adaptability and medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
