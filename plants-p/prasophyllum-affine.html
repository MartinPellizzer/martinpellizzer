Prasophyllum affine

Prasophyllum affine, commonly known as the slender leek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Australia.

It typically grows in heathland and open forests, favoring well-drained soils. The plant features a slender, leafy stem with a single, showy greenish-yellow flower. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, contributing to its resemblance to leeks.

Prasophyllum affine is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its delicate and ephemeral blooms.
