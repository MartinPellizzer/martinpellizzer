Psilanthus

Psilanthus, commonly known as the Chinese sandalwood or sandalwood tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, deciduous leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Psilanthus species are valued for their aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
