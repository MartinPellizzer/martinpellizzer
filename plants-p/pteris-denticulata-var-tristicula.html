Pteris denticulata var. tristicula

Pteris denticulata var. tristicula, commonly known as the three-toothed water fern, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with finely serrated margins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its rhizomes are often covered in a layer of brownish, hairy tissue.

The fronds are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, giving it a distinctive, intricate appearance.
