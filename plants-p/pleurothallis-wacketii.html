Pleurothallis wacketii

Pleurothallis wacketii, commonly known as the Wacket's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding its survival in its humid, shaded habitat. Its leaves are usually long and narrow, adapted to capture limited sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
