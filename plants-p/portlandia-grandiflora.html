Portlandia grandiflora

Portlandia grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
