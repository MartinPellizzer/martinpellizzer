Potentilla clarkiana

Potentilla clarkiana, commonly known as Clark's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. It has a compact growth habit and produces fruit that disperses its seeds through wind.

Due to its limited range, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
