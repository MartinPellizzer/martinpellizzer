Paspalum capillifolium

Paspalum capillifolium, commonly known as slenderleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetland environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Paspalum capillifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
