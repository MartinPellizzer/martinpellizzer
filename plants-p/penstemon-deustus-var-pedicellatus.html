Penstemon deustus var. pedicellatus

Penstemon deustus var. pedicellatus, commonly known as the showy penstemon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features upright stems covered in dense, showy flowers that range in color from violet to blue. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract bees and butterflies, contributing to pollination and ecological balance.
