Prunus × autumnalis

Prunus × autumnalis, commonly known as the autumn cherry, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the United States, particularly in the eastern and midwestern regions. The plant is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, edible fruits in the fall. Its leaves turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn, contributing to its ornamental value.

Prunus × autumnalis is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal interest.
