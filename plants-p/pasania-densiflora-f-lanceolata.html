Pasania densiflora f. lanceolata

Pasania densiflora f. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate pasania, is a variant of the pasania densiflora species.

It is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The leaves are lanceolate in shape, which means they are elongated and tapering at both ends. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, adding ornamental value to its appearance.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
