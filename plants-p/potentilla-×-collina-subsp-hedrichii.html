Potentilla × collina subsp. hedrichii

Potentilla × collina subsp.

hedrichii, commonly known as the Hedrich's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated margins, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic value.
