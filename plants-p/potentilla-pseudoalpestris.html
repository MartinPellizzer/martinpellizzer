Potentilla pseudoalpestris

Potentilla pseudoalpestris, commonly known as alpine cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has wiry, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
