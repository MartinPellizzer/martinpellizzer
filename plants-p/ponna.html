Ponna

Ponna, commonly known as the Indian nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Ponna is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
