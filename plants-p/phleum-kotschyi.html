Phleum kotschyi

Phleum kotschyi, commonly known as Kotschyi's timothy, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, leafy stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lipped flowers. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated as a forage plant in certain regions.
