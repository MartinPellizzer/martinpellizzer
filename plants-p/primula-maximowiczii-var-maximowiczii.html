Primula maximowiczii var. maximowiczii

Primula maximowiczii var. maximowiczii, commonly known as the Korean primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or lilac, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Its foliage and floral structure make it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland plantings.
