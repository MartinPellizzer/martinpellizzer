Paloue bicolor

Paloue bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored paloue, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which display a bi-colored pattern of green and silver on the upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its unique leaf coloration is due to the presence of specialized pigments in the epidermis.

Paloue bicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
