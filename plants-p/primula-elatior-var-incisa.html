Primula elatior var. incisa

Primula elatior var. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

This variety is valued for its early spring bloom and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
