Peperomia evadens

Peperomia evadens, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically silver or gray-green in color, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white, followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements make it a desirable ornamental plant.
