Piper corei

Piper corei, commonly known as the Indian pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green, berry-like fruits that turn red when mature. These fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.

Its leaves are also utilized in herbal remedies for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
