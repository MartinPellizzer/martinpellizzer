Paspalum canaliculatum

Paspalum canaliculatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its flowering period usually occurs in the spring and early summer, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.
