Piper acutifolium

Piper acutifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and small, clustered flowers. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
