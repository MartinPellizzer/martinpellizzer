Phlox nuttalliana

Phlox nuttalliana, commonly known as Nuttall's phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically reaches heights of 1 to 3 feet and produces narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Nuttall's phlox is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
