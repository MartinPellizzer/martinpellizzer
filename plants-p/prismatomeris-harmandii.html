Prismatomeris harmandii

Prismatomeris harmandii, commonly known as the Harmand's orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Madagascar, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences and intricate, colorful flowers that often feature a distinctive prism-like structure. Its growth habit includes a short rhizome with fleshy roots, and it produces small, green leaves.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
