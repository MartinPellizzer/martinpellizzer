Phyllanthus faguetii var. gracilipedicellatus

Phyllanthus faguetii var. gracilipedicellatus, commonly known as the slender-fruited phyllanthus, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that dehisces to release tiny seeds.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
