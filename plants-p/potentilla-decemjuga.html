Potentilla decemjuga

Potentilla decemjuga, commonly known as the Alaska potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Potentilla decemjuga thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in tundra and alpine environments.
