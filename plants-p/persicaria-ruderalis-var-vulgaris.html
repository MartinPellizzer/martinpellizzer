Persicaria ruderalis var. vulgaris

Persicaria ruderalis var. vulgaris, commonly known as common smartweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is an annual or biennial herb that typically grows in disturbed soils such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Common smartweed is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
