Pilosella peleteriana subsp. ligerica

Pilosella peleteriana subsp.

ligerica, commonly known as the Ligerica mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open woodland or alpine meadows.

The plant features a low-growing rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that are lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in a loose inflorescence, blooming in late spring to early summer.
