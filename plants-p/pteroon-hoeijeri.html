Pteroon hoeijeri

Pteroon hoeijeri, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Pteroon hoeijeri is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
