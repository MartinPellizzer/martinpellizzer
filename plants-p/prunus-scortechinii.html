Prunus scortechinii

Prunus scortechinii, commonly known as the Japanese apricot or Chinese wild apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its oval, glossy leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.
