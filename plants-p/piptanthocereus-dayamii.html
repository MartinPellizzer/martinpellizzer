Piptanthocereus dayamii

Piptanthocereus dayamii, commonly known as the Dayami cactus, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, green stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant has spiny areoles and is adapted to grow in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for study in desert flora and ecological adaptation.
