Plectranthus ambiguus

Plectranthus ambiguus, commonly known as the purple heart plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has succulent, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Plectranthus ambiguus is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant color and easy maintenance.
