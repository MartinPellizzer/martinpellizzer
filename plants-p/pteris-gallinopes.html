Pteris gallinopes

Pteris gallinopes, commonly known as the broad-leaf water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in sori covered by indusia. Pteris gallinopes thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in humid forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for aquatic and terrarium settings.
