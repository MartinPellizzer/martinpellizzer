Pleurothallis picta

Pleurothallis picta, commonly known as the painted pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often colorful floral structures and its ability to thrive in high humidity and shaded environments. The plant typically produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom during the warmer months. Its roots are epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, and it requires well-draining, porous media for optimal growth.

Pleurothallis picta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique botanical features.
