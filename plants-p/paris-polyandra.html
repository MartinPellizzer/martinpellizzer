Paris polyandra

Paris polyandra, commonly known as the fragrant lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and its distinctive, spiral arrangement of flowers that resemble a tresses of hair. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that emit a subtle fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. Paris polyandra thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it a valuable component of its native habitat.
