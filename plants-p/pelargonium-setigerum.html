Pelargonium setigerum

Pelargonium setigerum, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its dense, fuzzy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium setigerum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
