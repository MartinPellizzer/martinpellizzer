Prunus × morioka-pendula

Prunus × morioka-pendula, commonly known as the Morokoka weeping cherry, is a hybrid species of cherry tree that exhibits distinctive weeping growth habits.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that create a soft, flowing appearance, making it a popular ornamental plant. The tree produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, adding to its aesthetic appeal. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a dark green color.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
