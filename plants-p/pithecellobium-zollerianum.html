Pithecellobium zollerianum

Pithecellobium zollerianum, commonly known as the yellow velvet tamarind, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow, edible pods that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it thrives in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
