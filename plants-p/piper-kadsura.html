Piper kadsura

Piper kadsura, commonly known as the Chinese pepper vine, is a climbing perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces berries that transition from green to red as they mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a lush, climbing plant.
