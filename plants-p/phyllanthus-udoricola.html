Phyllanthus udoricola

Phyllanthus udoricola, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers and bright red, fleshy fruits that resemble berries. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and typically have a slightly serrated edge.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
