Paepalanthus bisulcatus

Paepalanthus bisulcatus, commonly known as the two-ribbed cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to poor soil conditions with its shallow root system.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
