Pleurothallis montipelladensis

Pleurothallis montipelladensis, commonly known as the Andean pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and bloom during the cooler months.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
