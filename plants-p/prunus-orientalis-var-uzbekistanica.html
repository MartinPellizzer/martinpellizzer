Prunus orientalis var. uzbekistanica

Prunus orientalis var. uzbekistanica, commonly known as the Uzbekistan cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender branches and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet, edible fruits similar to cherries, which are often used in local cuisines. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to various climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional horticulture.
