Pyrus malus oblongus

Pyrus malus oblongus, commonly known as the oblong apple, is a species of apple tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped fruits that are typically smaller than common apples. The plant produces white flowers in the spring, followed by edible fruits that have a tart flavor. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in apple cultivation.
