Prunus tenuiputamenta

Prunus tenuiputamenta, commonly known as the slender-leaved cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, which are usually pink or white, followed by small, round berries. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and traditional Chinese medicine.
