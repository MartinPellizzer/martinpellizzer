Paraspalathus araneosa

Paraspalathus araneosa, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate, spider-like appearance due to its elongated, filamentous floral structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it can receive adequate light without direct sun exposure. Its flowers are usually small and delicate, with a complex arrangement of petals and sepals that mimic the appearance of spider legs.

Paraspalathus araneosa is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native habitats.
