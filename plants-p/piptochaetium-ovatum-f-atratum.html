Piptochaetium ovatum f. atratum

Piptochaetium ovatum f. atratum, commonly known as the blackleaf grass, is a variant of the grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green to almost black leaves, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves with a dense, hairy texture that helps reduce water loss in dry environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
