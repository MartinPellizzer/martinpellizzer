Pittosporum illicioides var. oligocarpum

Pittosporum illicioides var. oligocarpum, commonly known as the New Zealand pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically orange to red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and ecological restoration projects.
