Portulaca ferricola

Portulaca ferricola, commonly known as ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its foliage is typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly rough texture.

Portulaca ferricola is known for its resilience and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
