Placus lacerus

Placus lacerus, commonly known as the torn leaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive, frayed foliage.

The leaves are typically broad and lobed, with a tendency to split or tear easily, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white in color.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
