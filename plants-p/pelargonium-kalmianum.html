Pelargonium kalmianum

Pelargonium kalmianum, commonly known as the Kalmia geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Pelargonium kalmianum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
