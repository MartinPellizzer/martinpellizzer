Passiflora urubiciensis

Passiflora urubiciensis, commonly known as the Urubici passionflower, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
