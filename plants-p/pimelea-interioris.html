Pimelea interioris

Pimelea interioris, commonly known as the white pimelea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Pimelea interioris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
