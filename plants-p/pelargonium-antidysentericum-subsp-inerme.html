Pelargonium antidysentericum subsp. inerme

Pelargonium antidysentericum subsp.

inerme, commonly known as the African geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features upright, branched stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers with five petals, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is occasionally cultivated for both aesthetic and therapeutic purposes.
