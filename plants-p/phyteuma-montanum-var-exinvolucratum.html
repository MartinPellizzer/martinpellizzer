Phyteuma montanum var. exinvolucratum

Phyteuma montanum var. exinvolucratum, commonly known as the mountain phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It features upright, slender stems that bear lance-shaped, hairy leaves arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces showy, umbel-like clusters of bright yellow to orange-red flowers in late summer. Its flowers are typically tubular with two lobes, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to alpine and rock gardens.
