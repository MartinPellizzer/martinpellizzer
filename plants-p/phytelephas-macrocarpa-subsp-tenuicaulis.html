Phytelephas macrocarpa subsp. tenuicaulis

Phytelephas macrocarpa subsp.

tenuicaulis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed sacha inchi, is a member of the Malvaceae family and is native to the Amazon rainforest. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter.

The plant produces distinctive, elongated fruits that contain nutrient-rich seeds, which are highly valued for their oil content. It thrives in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its ecological importance and economic value, it is often cultivated in agroforestry systems.
