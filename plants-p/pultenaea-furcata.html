Pultenaea furcata

Pultenaea furcata, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in heathlands and open forests. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Pultenaea furcata is a key species in the Proteaceae family and is recognized for its ecological and ornamental value.
