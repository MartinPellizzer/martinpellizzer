Piper oradendron

Piper oradendron, commonly known as the orange pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as the common black pepper plant. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly bitter taste, while the stems are woody and can grow up to several meters in height.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
