Potentilla sylvestris var. neumayeriana

Potentilla sylvestris var. neumayeriana, commonly known as the Neumayer's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters. The plant has compound leaves composed of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, which are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and adaptability.
