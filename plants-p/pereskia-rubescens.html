Pereskia rubescens

Pereskia rubescens, commonly known as the red cactus, is a unique species native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is a shrub-like plant with thick, fleshy stems that are often covered in short, woolly hairs. The leaves are oval-shaped and can be green or reddish in color, depending on the light conditions. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

This plant is adapted to arid environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
