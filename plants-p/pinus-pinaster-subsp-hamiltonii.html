Pinus pinaster subsp. hamiltonii

Pinus pinaster subsp.

hamiltonii, commonly known as the Spanish fir or Hamilton's Aleppo pine, is a subspecies of the Aleppo pine native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a tall, coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its long, slender needles and distinctive blue-green foliage.

The cones are typically pendulous and can reach lengths of up to 15 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with mild, wet winters and hot, dry summers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in forestry and landscape design.
