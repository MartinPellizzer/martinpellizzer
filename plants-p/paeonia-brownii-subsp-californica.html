Paeonia brownii subsp. californica

Paeonia brownii subsp.

californica, commonly known as the California peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has palmate leaves with lobed margins and grows from a rhizomatous root system. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is relatively low maintenance once established.
