Pimelea virgata var. pilosa

Pimelea virgata var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy swamp bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that produce bright red or orange flowers, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant typically grows in wet or swampy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
