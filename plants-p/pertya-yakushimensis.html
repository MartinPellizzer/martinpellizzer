Pertya yakushimensis

Pertya yakushimensis, commonly known as the Japanese sea hare, is a species of sea slug found in the coastal waters of Japan.

It belongs to the family Aeolidae and is characterized by its elongated, soft body and vibrant coloration. The plant has no true roots, stems, or leaves, and instead relies on its gelatinous body for survival in marine environments. It is primarily herbivorous, feeding on algae and other microscopic organisms.
