Passiflora seemannii

Passiflora seemannii, commonly known as the Jamaican passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, fleshy berries that are typically orange or red in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and are often used in traditional medicine.

Passiflora seemannii thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical rainforests.
