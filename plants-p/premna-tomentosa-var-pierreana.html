Premna tomentosa var. pierreana

Premna tomentosa var. pierreana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or woolly morning glory, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
