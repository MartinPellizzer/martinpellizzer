Poa atacamensis

Poa atacamensis, commonly known as the Atacama grass, is a species native to the arid regions of Chile and Peru.

It is a perennial grass that thrives in extreme desert conditions, showcasing remarkable adaptations to drought and high salinity. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It plays a vital role in stabilizing sandy soils and preventing erosion in its harsh environment.

Due to its resilience, it is often used in ecological restoration projects in arid landscapes.
