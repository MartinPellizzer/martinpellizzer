Philadelphus × congestus

Philadelphus × congestus, commonly known as the Chinese mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters and has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

The flowers are often used in perfumery due to their strong, sweet scent.
