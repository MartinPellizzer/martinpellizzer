Potamogeton × johannis

Potamogeton × johannis, commonly known as the hybrid pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton praealtus.

It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are usually arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like structures.

Potamogeton × johannis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
