Pelargonium daucifolium

Pelargonium daucifolium, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, slightly serrated leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often blooming throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant.
