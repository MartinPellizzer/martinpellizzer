Polypogon interruptus var. breviaristatus

Polypogon interruptus var. breviaristatus, commonly known as the short-awned panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands, prairies, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces spikelets with short awns, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
