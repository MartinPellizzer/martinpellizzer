Polypodium pulchrum

Polypodium pulchrum, commonly known as the beautiful maidenhair fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically bright green with a slightly curled appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to its substrate while absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air.

Polypodium pulchrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
