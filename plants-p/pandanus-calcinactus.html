Pandanus calcinactus

Pandanus calcinactus, commonly known as the fire tree, is a distinctive palm-like plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional weaving practices. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into spiny, woody fruits. Its leaves are tough and fibrous, making them valuable for various utilitarian purposes.

Pandanus calcinactus thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often growing in shallow water or on rocky shores.
