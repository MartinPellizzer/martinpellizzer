Pimenta officinalis var. cumanensis

Pimenta officinalis var. cumanensis, commonly known as the Cumanese pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is valued for its essential oils and potential health benefits.

Due to its ornamental and practical uses, it is cultivated in tropical regions for both commercial and traditional purposes.
