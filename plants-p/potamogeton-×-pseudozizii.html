Potamogeton × pseudozizii

Potamogeton × pseudozizii, commonly known as the hybrid water milfoil, is a perennial aquatic plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Potamogeton pectinatus and Potamogeton zizanioides.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Potamogeton × pseudozizii is known for its ability to form dense mats on the water surface, which can impact water flow and light penetration.

Its hybrid origin contributes to its adaptability and ecological significance in aquatic ecosystems.
