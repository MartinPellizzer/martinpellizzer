Pelargonium × optabile

Pelargonium × optabile, commonly known as the scented geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, velvety leaves that emit a variety of scents, such as mint, citrus, or spice. The plant typically produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant and for its aromatic properties.
