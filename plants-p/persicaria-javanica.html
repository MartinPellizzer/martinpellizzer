Persicaria javanica

Persicaria javanica, commonly known as the Java knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as buckwheats. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly toothed margin. Persicaria javanica produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on tall, slender spikes.

It is often found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes and along stream banks.
