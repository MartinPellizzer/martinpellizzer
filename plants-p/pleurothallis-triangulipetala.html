Pleurothallis triangulipetala

Pleurothallis triangulipetala, commonly known as the triangular-petaled pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique floral structure and habitat preferences, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
