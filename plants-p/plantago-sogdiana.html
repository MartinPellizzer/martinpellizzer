Plantago sogdiana

Plantago sogdiana, commonly known as the Sogdian plantain, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Botanically, it is valued for its medicinal properties and is part of the Plantaginaceae family.
