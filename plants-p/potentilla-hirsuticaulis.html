Potentilla hirsuticaulis

Potentilla hirsuticaulis, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and compound leaves with five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and grassy areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
