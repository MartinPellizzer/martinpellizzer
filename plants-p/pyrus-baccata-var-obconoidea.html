Pyrus baccata var. obconoidea

Pyrus baccata var. obconoidea, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a variant of the European pear species.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers and edible, sweet fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in autumn, and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for small gardens or ornamental use.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal, with attractive foliage and a graceful form.
