Picris foetida

Picris foetida, commonly known as stinking weed, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features yellow flowers that are surrounded by pale green to brown bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and branched.

Picris foetida is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is particularly noticeable when the plant is crushed.
