Pterocephalus diandrus

Pterocephalus diandrus, commonly known as the Chinese thistle, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds with tufts of white hairs, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for various ailments.
