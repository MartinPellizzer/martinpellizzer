Poa bakuensis

Poa bakuensis, commonly known as the Baku grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the Caucasus region, specifically found in the mountainous areas of Azerbaijan. This perennial grass is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Poa bakuensis is noted for its ecological significance in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
