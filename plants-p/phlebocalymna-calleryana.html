Phlebocalymna calleryana

Phlebocalymna calleryana, commonly known as the Chinese cliff brake, is a member of the fern family.

It is characterized by its intricate, feather-like fronds that are typically bipinnate, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions of China. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
