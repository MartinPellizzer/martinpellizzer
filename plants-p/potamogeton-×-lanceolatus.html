Potamogeton × lanceolatus

Potamogeton × lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are typically submerged, contributing to its dense growth in ponds and lakes. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are arranged in spikes, and it is often found in shallow, still waters. Potamogeton × lanceolatus plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various waterfowl and invertebrates.

Its presence can indicate good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
