Paspalum doellii

Paspalum doellii, commonly known as the dwarf paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale green to brown flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions.

Paspalum doellii is sometimes used in ornamental landscaping due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in various climates.
