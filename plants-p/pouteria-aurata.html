Pouteria aurata

Pouteria aurata, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The tree is most famous for its fruit, which contains the edible seeds known as Brazil nuts. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in well-drained, fertile soils.

It plays a vital role in the ecological balance of the rainforest and is also economically significant for its nut production.
