Phelline erubescens

Phelline erubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered phelline, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to parts of the Mediterranean region. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, leguminous pods that contain its seeds.

Phelline erubescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
