Portlandia

Portlandia, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in moist, well-drained environments. Portlandia has small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, and its leaves are pinnately divided with a slightly hairy texture.

It is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
