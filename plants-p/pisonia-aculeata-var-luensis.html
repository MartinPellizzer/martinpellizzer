Pisonia aculeata var. luensis

Pisonia aculeata var. luensis, commonly known as the Luens Island pisonia, is a flowering plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often dispersed by birds. It thrives in coastal and tropical environments, often growing near the shoreline.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural gardens in warm climates.
