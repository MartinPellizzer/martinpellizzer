Persea grandibracteata

Persea grandibracteata, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree bears fruit that is typically a small, orange to reddish persimmon, often with a sweet, astringent flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and edible fruit, making it a significant species in both horticulture and local ecosystems.
