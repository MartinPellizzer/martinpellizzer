Padus schuebeleri

Padus schuebeleri, commonly known as the Schuebeler's bird cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as roses and apples. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters, featuring thorny stems and oval, serrated leaves. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
