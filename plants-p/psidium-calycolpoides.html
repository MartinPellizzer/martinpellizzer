Psidium calycolpoides

Psidium calycolpoides, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red when mature, containing a single large seed. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Psidium calycolpoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in home gardens.
