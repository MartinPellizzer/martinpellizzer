Paspalum puberulum

Paspalum puberulum, commonly known as silky grass or silky paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescences that produce small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its dense growth habit makes it useful for erosion control and as a ground cover in landscaping.

Paspalum puberulum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate periodic flooding.
