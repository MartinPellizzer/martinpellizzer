Phyllanthus elegans

Phyllanthus elegans, commonly known as the sacred lotus or the rose of Jericho, is a flowering plant native to Africa, Asia, and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its small, pale green or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its dense, fleshy leaves and unique, star-shaped flowers. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and resilience in various environmental conditions.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
