Platanthera pachyglossa var. hachijoensis

Platanthera pachyglossa var. hachijoensis, commonly known as the Hachijo Island marsh orchid, is a rare orchid species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as wet meadows and marshy areas. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a basal rosette, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
