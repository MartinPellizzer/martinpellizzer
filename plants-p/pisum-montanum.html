Pisum montanum

Pisum montanum, commonly known as mountain pea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by pods containing several seeds. Pisum montanum is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
