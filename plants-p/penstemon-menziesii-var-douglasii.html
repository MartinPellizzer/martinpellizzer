Penstemon menziesii var. douglasii

Penstemon menziesii var. douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinators.
