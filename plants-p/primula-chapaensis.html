Primula chapaensis

Primula chapaensis, commonly known as the Chapa primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories or along stream sides. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, which helps to reflect excess light.

Primula chapaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
