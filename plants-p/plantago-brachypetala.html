Plantago brachypetala

Plantago brachypetala, commonly known as the short-petaled plantain, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in dense, silky hairs, which help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. Its fibrous root system makes it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties, with leaves used traditionally for their soothing and healing effects.
