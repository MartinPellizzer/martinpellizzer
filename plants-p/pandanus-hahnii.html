Pandanus hahnii

Pandanus hahnii, commonly known as the Hahn's pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into fibrous, palm-like fruits. Its leaves are fibrous and can be harvested for making thatch and ropes.

Pandanus hahnii thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found near mangrove forests.
