Portula diffusa

Portula diffusa, commonly known as the common woodrush, is a perennial plant native to the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of narrow, leathery leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Portula diffusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
