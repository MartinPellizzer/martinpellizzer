Polygonum alpinum subsp. alaskanum

Polygonum alpinum subsp.

alaskanum, commonly known as the Alaskan knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America. It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as tundra and alpine meadows.

The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, clustered flowers. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Polygonum alpinum subsp.

alaskanum is adapted to cold climates and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its harsh environment.
