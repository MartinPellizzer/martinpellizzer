Prosopis cinerascens

Prosopis cinerascens, commonly known as the velvet mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, gray-green stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by long, slender pods containing edible seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in poor soils, making it a hardy species in harsh environments.

Prosopis cinerascens is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife and livestock.
