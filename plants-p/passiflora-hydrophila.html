Passiflora hydrophila

Passiflora hydrophila, commonly known as the water passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near water sources. Its fruit, which resembles a small, green, edible berry, is used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
