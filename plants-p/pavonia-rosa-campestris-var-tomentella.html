Pavonia rosa-campestris var. tomentella

Pavonia rosa-campestris var. tomentella, commonly known as the woolly pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and roadside ditches. Its foliage is covered in fine, white to grayish hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wet conditions.
