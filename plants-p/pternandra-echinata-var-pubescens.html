Pternandra echinata var. pubescens

Pternandra echinata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy pternandra, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically borne in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
