Piper subglaucescens

Piper subglaucescens, commonly known as the blue-leaved pepper or blue pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its glossy, bluish-green leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruits are small berries that mature to a dark purple color.

Piper subglaucescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has mild medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
