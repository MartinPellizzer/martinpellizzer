Paris longistigmata

Paris longistigmata, commonly known as the broad-leaved Paris, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, white to greenish flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in early spring. Its underground rhizomes are tuberous and have a distinctive, often aromatic scent.

Paris longistigmata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
