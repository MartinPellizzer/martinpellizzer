Paralamium

Paralamium, commonly known as the sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower spikes that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Paralamium thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds.

Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense mats in suitable habitats.
