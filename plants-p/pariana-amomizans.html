Pariana amomizans

Pariana amomizans, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms that typically appear in shades of white and pink. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and indoor cultivation.
