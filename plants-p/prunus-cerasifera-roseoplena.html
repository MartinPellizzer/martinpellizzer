Prunus cerasifera roseoplena

Prunus cerasifera roseoplena, commonly known as the cherry laurel or the purple leaf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that turn a striking red or purple in the fall. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark red, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a visually appealing addition to landscapes.
