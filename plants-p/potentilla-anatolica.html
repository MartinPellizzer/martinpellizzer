Potentilla anatolica

Potentilla anatolica, commonly known as Anatolian cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Turkey and surrounding regions.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it produces small, persistent fruits.

Potentilla anatolica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
