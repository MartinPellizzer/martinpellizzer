Pteris diversifolia

Pteris diversifolia, commonly known as the variegated water fern, is a species of fern native to Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like structures that are typically divided into smaller, lance-shaped segments. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near water sources such as streams and ponds. Its fronds are usually dark green with a lighter green or variegated pattern, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Pteris diversifolia is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in humid climates.
