Phacelia dubia var. georgiana

Phacelia dubia var. georgiana, commonly known as the Georgia phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.

The species is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in its native habitat.
