Phyllanthus somalensis

Phyllanthus somalensis, commonly known as the Somali phyllanthus, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Somalia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions, with deep roots that help it access groundwater. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
