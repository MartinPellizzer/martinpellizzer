Piper smilacifolium

Piper smilacifolium, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy berries. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a distinct aromatic scent.

It is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
