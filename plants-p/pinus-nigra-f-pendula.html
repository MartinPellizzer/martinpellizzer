Pinus nigra f. pendula

Pinus nigra f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping black pine, is a unique variant of the black pine species native to the Mediterranean region.

This cultivar is characterized by its distinctive cascading branches that droop gracefully, creating a striking, ornamental appearance. The foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are dark green and slightly twisted, adding to its visual appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Due to its unique form, it is often used as a specimen tree in horticultural settings.
