Phaseolus vulgaris

Phaseolus vulgaris, commonly known as the common bean, is a flowering plant in the Fabaceae family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its climbing habit, often supported by tendrils. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are widely cultivated for food. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, and the leaves are trifoliate.

Phaseolus vulgaris is valued for its nutritional content and plays a significant role in global agriculture.
