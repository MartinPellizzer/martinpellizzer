Ptyssiglottis virgata

Ptyssiglottis virgata, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or violet in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Ptyssiglottis virgata is often found in rocky or limestone habitats, where it blooms during the spring and early summer.
