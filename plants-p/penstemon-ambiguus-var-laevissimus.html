Penstemon ambiguus var. laevissimus

Penstemon ambiguus var. laevissimus, commonly known as the smooth beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and pairs of hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
