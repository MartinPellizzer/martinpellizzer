Pleurandra glaucophylla

Pleurandra glaucophylla, commonly known as the silver-leafed pleurandra, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on leafless stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens across temperate regions.
