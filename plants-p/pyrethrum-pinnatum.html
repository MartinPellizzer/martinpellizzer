Pyrethrum pinnatum

Pyrethrum pinnatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant produces natural insecticides called pyrethrins, which are used in organic pest control. Pyrethrum pinnatum thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its practical use in agriculture.
