Primula intricata subsp. intricata

Primula intricata subsp.

intricata, commonly known as the intricate primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in dense clusters at the top of its slender stems.

The leaves are lanceolate, with a hairy texture, and the plant thrives in cool, moist environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or woodland settings.

The subspecies is distinguished by its intricate floral structure and adaptability to high-altitude conditions.
