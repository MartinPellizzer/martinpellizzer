Pedicularis spicata subsp. bracteata

Pedicularis spicata subsp.

bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated bracts that resemble leaves, which are often tinged with red or purple. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
