Pouteria flava

Pouteria flava, commonly known as the yellow soursop, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, and its fruit is a large, spiny berry with a sweet, aromatic pulp.

The fruit is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications due to its unique flavor and nutritional value.
