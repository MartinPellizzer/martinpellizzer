Polystachya mauritiana var. anceps

Polystachya mauritiana var. anceps, commonly known as the Mauritius spike orchid, is a species native to the island of Mauritius.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces numerous flowering spikes during the rainy season.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also of interest in botanical research due to its unique adaptations to its native habitat.
