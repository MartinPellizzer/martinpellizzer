Polemonium carneum subsp. luteum

Polemonium carneum subsp.

luteum, commonly known as the yellow Jacob's ladder, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America. It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of showy, yellow or orange-red flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more vibrant floral coloration compared to the typical form of Polemonium carneum.
