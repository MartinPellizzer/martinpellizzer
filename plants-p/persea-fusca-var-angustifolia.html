Persea fusca var. angustifolia

Persea fusca var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spondias, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, round fruits that resemble small cherries. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
