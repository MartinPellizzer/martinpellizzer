Pabstiella versicolor

Pabstiella versicolor, commonly known as the variable pabstiella, is a rare and unique member of the Pabstiellaceae family.

It is characterized by its strikingly variable coloration, which can range from green to reddish-brown depending on environmental conditions. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually small, leathery, and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Due to its limited distribution and fragile growth requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
