Ptilotrichum longicaule subsp. cadevallianum

Ptilotrichum longicaule subsp.

cadevallianum, commonly known as the Cape Peninsula daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers.

The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained sandy soils. It produces small, narrow leaves and is often found in the Western Cape region.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
