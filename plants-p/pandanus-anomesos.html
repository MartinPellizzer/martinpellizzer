Pandanus anomesos

Pandanus anomesos, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear fragrant, yellowish flowers. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often growing near the shoreline.

Its foliage is particularly valued for weaving and thatching in local cultures.
