Polygonum acuminatum var. brachystemon

Polygonum acuminatum var. brachystemon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved smartweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red, appearing in late summer to early fall.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in supporting local wildlife.
