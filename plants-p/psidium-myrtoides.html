Psidium myrtoides

Psidium myrtoides, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitaya, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or red when ripe, with a sweet, tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Psidium myrtoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in home gardens.
