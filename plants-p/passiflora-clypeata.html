Passiflora clypeata

Passiflora clypeata, commonly known as the cup-shaped passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a unique combination of blue, purple, and white petals, along with a crown-like structure of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Passiflora clypeata is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
