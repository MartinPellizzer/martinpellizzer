Potamogeton densus var. lancifolius

Potamogeton densus var. lancifolius, commonly known as the dense pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown in color. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in lakes, ponds, and wetlands.

This variety is characterized by its dense growth habit, which can help stabilize sediment and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
