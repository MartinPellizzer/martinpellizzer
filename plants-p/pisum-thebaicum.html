Pisum thebaicum

Pisum thebaicum, commonly known as the Egyptian pea, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its pods contain several seeds, which are often used as a food source.

The plant is valued for its adaptability and has been cultivated for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
