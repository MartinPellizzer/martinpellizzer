Puya alpicola

Puya alpicola, commonly known as the alpine puya, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is a member of the bromeliad family, known for its striking rosette-shaped leaves and vibrant floral displays. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky soils at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its flowers, which bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, are adapted to attract specific pollinators in its harsh habitat.

Puya alpicola is also noted for its ability to survive in extreme climatic conditions, making it a resilient and unique botanical specimen.
