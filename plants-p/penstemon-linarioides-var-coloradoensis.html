Penstemon linarioides var. coloradoensis

Penstemon linarioides var. coloradoensis, commonly known as Colorado penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high elevations of Colorado.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are drought-tolerant and low-maintenance.
