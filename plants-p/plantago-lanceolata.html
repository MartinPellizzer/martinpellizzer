Plantago lanceolata

Plantago lanceolata, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. It is adapted to grow in a variety of environments, including roadsides, fields, and disturbed soils.

The plant has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
