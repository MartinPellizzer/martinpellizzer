Phreatia bulbophylloides

Phreatia bulbophylloides, commonly known as the bulbophyll orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a fleshy, bulb-like stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in seasonal habitats. The plant produces striking, orchid-like flowers with intricate patterns and a strong, sweet fragrance. Its leaves are typically broad and succulent, adapted to conserve moisture in its natural environment.

Phreatia bulbophylloides is often found in shaded, moist areas such as forest floors or rocky crevices.
