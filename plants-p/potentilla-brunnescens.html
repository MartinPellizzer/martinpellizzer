Potentilla brunnescens

Potentilla brunnescens, commonly known as the brown cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves composed of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Potentilla brunnescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive floral display.
