Polypodium lanceola

Polypodium lanceola, commonly known as the lance fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizome is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The fronds are usually dark green and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.
