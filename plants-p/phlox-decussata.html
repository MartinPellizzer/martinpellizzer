Phlox decussata

Phlox decussata, commonly known as the mountain phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable to local wildlife.
