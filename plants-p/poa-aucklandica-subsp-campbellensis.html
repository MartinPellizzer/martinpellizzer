Poa aucklandica subsp. campbellensis

Poa aucklandica subsp.

campbellensis, commonly known as Campbell's tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, slender leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and rocky areas, and is well adapted to the temperate climate of New Zealand.

This subspecies is of ecological importance, providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
