Pituranthos burchellii

Pituranthos burchellii, commonly known as the Burchell's cholla, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, segmented stems that are covered in small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the rainy season, which are followed by small, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is an important plant in its native habitat.
