Phyllanthus simplex var. pinifolius

Phyllanthus simplex var. pinifolius, commonly known as the pine-leaf phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its unique, pine-like foliage. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated margin, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
