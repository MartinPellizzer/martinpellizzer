Pilosella leucotricha

Pilosella leucotricha, commonly known as white-tawny hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It produces small, yellowish to white flowers that bloom in summer.

Pilosella leucotricha is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun.
