Philodendron basivaginatum

Philodendron basivaginatum, commonly known as the pink-veined philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent pink veins that stand out against the dark green foliage. The plant has a unique growth habit, often forming a dense, tangled mass as it climbs towards light. Its leaves are typically leathery and glossy, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
