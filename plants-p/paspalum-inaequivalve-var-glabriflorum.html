Paspalum inaequivalve var. glabriflorum

Paspalum inaequivalve var. glabriflorum, commonly known as the narrowleaf paspalum, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

This variety is notable for its ability to tolerate both wet and dry conditions, making it adaptable to a range of environments.
