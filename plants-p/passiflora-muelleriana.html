Passiflora muelleriana

Passiflora muelleriana, commonly known as the Mueller's passionflower, is a striking tropical vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, intricate flowers that display a mix of purple, blue, and yellow hues, with a distinctive crown of filaments at the center. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in nutrients and often consumed by local wildlife. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and climbing.

Passiflora muelleriana is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and fruit-eating birds.
