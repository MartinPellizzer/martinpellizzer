Paspalum reimarioides

Paspalum reimarioides, commonly known as the New Zealand paspalum, is a perennial grass native to New Zealand.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas. The plant has slender, tufted blades with a bluish-green hue and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and restoration projects.

Its adaptability to various environmental conditions makes it a resilient and ecologically important species.
