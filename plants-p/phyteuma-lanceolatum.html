Phyteuma lanceolatum

Phyteuma lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf phyteuma, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically blue to purple in color and bloom in late summer. The plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
