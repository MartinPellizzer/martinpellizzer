Primula watsonii subsp. cyanantha

Primula watsonii subsp.

cyanantha, commonly known as the blue-flowered primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China. It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces clusters of showy, blue to violet flowers.

The flowers are typically borne on slender stems and are pollinated by bees and other insects. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
