Penstemon grandiflorus

Penstemon grandiflorus, commonly known as the tall beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Penstemon grandiflorus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
