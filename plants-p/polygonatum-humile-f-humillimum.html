Polygonatum humile f. humillimum

Polygonatum humile f. humillimum, commonly known as the lesser Solomon's seal, is a low-growing perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, creeping rhizomes and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded woodland environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
