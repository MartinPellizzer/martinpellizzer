Potamogeton phialae

Potamogeton phialae, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown, and it relies on water currents for pollination. Potamogeton phialae plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
