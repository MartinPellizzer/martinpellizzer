Pleurothallis cuneifolia var. vinosa

Pleurothallis cuneifolia var. vinosa, commonly known as the violet-tinged orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid with pendant, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The flowers are typically small and clustered, with a distinctive violet or purple hue that gives the variety its name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high air humidity and moderate temperatures.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific care requirements, it is a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
