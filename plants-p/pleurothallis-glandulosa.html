Pleurothallis glandulosa

Pleurothallis glandulosa, commonly known as the sticky pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces fragrant blossoms that bloom in clusters.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
