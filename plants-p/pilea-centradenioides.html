Pilea centradenioides

Pilea centradenioides, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its rosette of thick, glossy, waxy leaves that resemble small coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by round, green fruits. It is a popular indoor plant due to its easy care requirements and attractive foliage.

Pilea centradenioides thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.
