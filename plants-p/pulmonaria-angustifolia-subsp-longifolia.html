Pulmonaria angustifolia subsp. longifolia

Pulmonaria angustifolia subsp.

longifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf lungwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that turn reddish in autumn.

The plant produces upright stems bearing clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of pink and white. It thrives in moist, shady woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to biodiversity in natural ecosystems.
