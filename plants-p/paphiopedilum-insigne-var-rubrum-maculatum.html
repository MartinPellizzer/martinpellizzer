Paphiopedilum insigne var. rubrum-maculatum

Paphiopedilum insigne var. rubrum-maculatum, commonly known as the Crimson-Marked Paphiopedilum, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety sepals and petals that display a deep red color with contrasting dark brown or black markings. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are highly fragrant and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
