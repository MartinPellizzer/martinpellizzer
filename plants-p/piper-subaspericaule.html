Piper subaspericaule

Piper subaspericaule, commonly known as the rough-stemmed pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, slender berries.

The berries are edible and have a mild, peppery flavor, making the plant useful in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
