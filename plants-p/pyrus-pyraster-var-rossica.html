Pyrus pyraster var. rossica

Pyrus pyraster var. rossica, commonly known as the Russian whitebeam, is a variant of the common whitebeam.

It is a deciduous tree native to eastern Europe and western Siberia, characterized by its small, rounded leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The fruit is a small, edible pome, typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
