Piper megacarpum

Piper megacarpum, commonly known as the giant pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous pepper species. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its fruit, which resembles a peppercorn, is typically harvested when fully mature.

The botanical features of Piper megacarpum make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
