Pelargonium argenteum

Pelargonium argenteum, commonly known as the silver geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, velvety foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, often blooming in summer. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in gardens around the world.
