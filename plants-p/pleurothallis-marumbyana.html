Pleurothallis marumbyana

Pleurothallis marumbyana, commonly known as the Marumbyana orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with intricate patterns that are characteristic of many pleurothallis species. It has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette, aiding in water retention.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
