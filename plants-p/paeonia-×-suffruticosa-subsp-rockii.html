Paeonia × suffruticosa subsp. rockii

Paeonia × suffruticosa subsp.

rockii, commonly known as the Chinese peony or rock peony, is a deciduous shrub native to China. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage, with dark green leaves that are oval to lance-shaped.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
