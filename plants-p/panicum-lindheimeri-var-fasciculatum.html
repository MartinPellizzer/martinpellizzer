Panicum lindheimeri var. fasciculatum

Panicum lindheimeri var. fasciculatum, commonly known as Lindheimer's panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, delicate spikelets that are typically arranged in clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
