Potentilla bolanderi var. bernardina

Potentilla bolanderi var. bernardina, commonly known as Bernard's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves composed of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, which are typically hairy and grayish-green in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and meadows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.
