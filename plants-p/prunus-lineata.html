Prunus lineata

Prunus lineata, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or dwarf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring, often preceding the leaves. The plant has slender, arching branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and parks.

Prunus lineata prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
