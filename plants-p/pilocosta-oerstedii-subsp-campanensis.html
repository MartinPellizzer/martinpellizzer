Pilocosta oerstedii subsp. campanensis

Pilocosta oerstedii subsp.

campanensis, commonly known as the blue bell lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for woodland gardens and naturalized areas.
