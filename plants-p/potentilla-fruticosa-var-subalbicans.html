Potentilla fruticosa var. subalbicans

Potentilla fruticosa var. subalbicans, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a low-growing shrub native to alpine regions of the northern hemisphere.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has wiry, thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value in mountainous gardens.
