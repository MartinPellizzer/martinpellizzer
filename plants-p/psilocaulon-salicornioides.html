Psilocaulon salicornioides

Psilocaulon salicornioides, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, salt-tolerant leaves and erect, branching stems that often appear grayish or bluish due to a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions, including strong winds and salt spray.

Psilocaulon salicornioides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various coastal organisms.
