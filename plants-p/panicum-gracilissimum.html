Panicum gracilissimum

Panicum gracilissimum, commonly known as slender panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are typically arranged in compact clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Panicum gracilissimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
