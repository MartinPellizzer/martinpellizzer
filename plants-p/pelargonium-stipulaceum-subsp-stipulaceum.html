Pelargonium stipulaceum subsp. stipulaceum

Pelargonium stipulaceum subsp.

stipulaceum, commonly known as the African violet, is a species native to South Africa. It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple.

The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for indoor cultivation and propagation through leaf cuttings.
