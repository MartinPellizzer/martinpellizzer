Papaya glandulosa

Papaya glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular papaya, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and yellow-orange, fleshy fruits that are typically harvested when ripe. The plant belongs to the Caricaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates. Its fruit is not only edible but also contains enzymes that aid in digestion.

The plant's distinctive glandular structures on its leaves and stems contribute to its unique botanical features.
