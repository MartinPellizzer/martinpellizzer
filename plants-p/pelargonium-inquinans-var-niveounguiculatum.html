Pelargonium inquinans var. niveounguiculatum

Pelargonium inquinans var. niveounguiculatum, commonly known as the white-veined geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geranium family.

It is characterized by its striking white veins on otherwise dark green foliage, creating a visually appealing contrast. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for both garden beds and container cultivation.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
