Parentucellia latifolia var. flaviflora

Parentucellia latifolia var. flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered broadleaf parentucellia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its flowers are attractive to pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
