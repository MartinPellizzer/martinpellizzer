Pleurothallis myrticola

Pleurothallis myrticola, commonly known as the myrtle orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in conditions with high air humidity and indirect light.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
