Pandanus satabiei

Pandanus satabiei, commonly known as the Maluku pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Maluku Islands of Indonesia.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by fibrous, spiky fruits. Its leaves are often used in local crafts and traditional practices, while its roots are sometimes used in herbal remedies.

The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.
