Polygonum lapathifolium subsp. lonchophyllum

Polygonum lapathifolium subsp.

lonchophyllum, commonly known as the narrowleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, pinkish to red flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated bracts that give it its subspecies name, *lonchophyllum*.
