Prunus domestica var. pruneauliana

Prunus domestica var. pruneauliana, commonly known as the pruneauliana plum, is a cultivar of the European plum.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and juicy fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree with dense, dark green foliage. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in jams and preserves.
