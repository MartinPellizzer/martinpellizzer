Pterocarpus ovalis

Pterocarpus ovalis, commonly known as the African padauk, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and showy red flowers. The tree produces flat, curved pods that contain flat, oval seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its rich reddish-brown color and is used in furniture and decorative crafts.

Pterocarpus ovalis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
