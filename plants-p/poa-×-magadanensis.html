Poa × magadanensis

Poa × magadanensis, commonly known as the Magadan bluegrass, is a hybrid grass species native to the Far East of Russia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green foliage. The plant typically forms dense, low-growing sods, making it suitable for erosion control and ornamental purposes. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a natural hybrid between Poa annua and Poa trivialis.
