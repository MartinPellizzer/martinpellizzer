Peperomia carpinterana

Peperomia carpinterana, commonly known as the Carvajal's peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, providing a dense, compact appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish or reddish in color, emerging from the leaf axils. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Peperomia carpinterana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care, making it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
