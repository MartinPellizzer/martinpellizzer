Premna ruttenii

Premna ruttenii, commonly known as the African nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are often followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
