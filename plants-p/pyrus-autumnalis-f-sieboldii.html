Pyrus autumnalis f. sieboldii

Pyrus autumnalis f. sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a cultivar of the white pear species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white flowers in spring and yellow-orange fruit in autumn. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing visual interest throughout the growing season. It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and mild fruit flavor.

This variety is relatively cold-hardy and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.
