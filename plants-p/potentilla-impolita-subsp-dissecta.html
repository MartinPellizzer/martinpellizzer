Potentilla impolita subsp. dissecta

Potentilla impolita subsp.

dissecta, commonly known as the dissected cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers during the summer months. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often growing in alpine or rocky environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Rosaceae family.
