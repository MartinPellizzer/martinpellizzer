Psidium pauciflorum

Psidium pauciflorum, commonly known as the small-fruited guava, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and edible fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The fruit is generally small, with a thin skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp, making it a valuable crop in certain regions.
