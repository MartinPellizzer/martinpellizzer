Parrya nudicaulis var. aspera

Parrya nudicaulis var. aspera, commonly known as the roughleaf parrya, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its sparse, clustered foliage and stems that often lie close to the ground. The leaves are typically narrow and covered with a rough, textured surface, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This plant is adapted to harsh conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
