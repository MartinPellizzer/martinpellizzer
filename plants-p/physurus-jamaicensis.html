Physurus jamaicensis

Physurus jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in disturbed soils near human habitation.

Physurus jamaicensis is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative propagation.
