Piper albidopunctatum

Piper albidopunctatum, commonly known as the white-punctured pepper plant, is a species of flowering vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white spots. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by elongated, three-lobed fruits that contain the aromatic seeds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
