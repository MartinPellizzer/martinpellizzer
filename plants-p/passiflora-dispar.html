Passiflora dispar

Passiflora dispar, commonly known as the variable passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces berries that are edible and contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture.

Passiflora dispar is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
