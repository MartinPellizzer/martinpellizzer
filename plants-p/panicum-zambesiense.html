Panicum zambesiense

Panicum zambesiense, commonly known as Zambesi grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in savannas and grasslands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping and for erosion control.
