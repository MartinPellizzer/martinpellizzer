Physetobasis macrocarpa

Physetobasis macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant pincushion plant, is a unique member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy, purple to pink capitula that resemble pincushions, composed of numerous tubular flowers. The plant is native to South Africa and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. It has a rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid conditions.

Physetobasis macrocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
