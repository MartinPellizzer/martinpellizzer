Pelargonium staphysagrioides

Pelargonium staphysagrioides, commonly known as the staphysagria geranium, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long flowering period.
