Potentilla argentea f. incanescens

Potentilla argentea f. incanescens, commonly known as silver potentilla or white cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its silvery-white underside on its leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along roadsides.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and hardiness in temperate climates.
