Podocarpus brassii

Podocarpus brassii, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a slow-growing evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It has a distinctive appearance with smooth, dark green leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody cones that contain edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Podocarpus brassii is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been traditionally used in construction and furniture-making.
