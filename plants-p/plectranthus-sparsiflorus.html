Plectranthus sparsiflorus

Plectranthus sparsiflorus, commonly known as the sparsiflorus plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and aromatic. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of violet or purple, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant.
