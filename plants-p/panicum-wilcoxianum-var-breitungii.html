Panicum wilcoxianum var. breitungii

Panicum wilcoxianum var. breitungii, commonly known as the Breitung panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
