Passiflora medusae

Passiflora medusae, commonly known as the crown of thorns, is a striking climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, showy flowers that feature a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and bracts, often in shades of red, yellow, and white. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in nutrients and have been used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine, sticky hairs.

Passiflora medusae thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
