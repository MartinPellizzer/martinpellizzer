Potentilla buguoyana

Potentilla buguoyana, commonly known as the Shasta dwarf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with five oval leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like roses and raspberries.
