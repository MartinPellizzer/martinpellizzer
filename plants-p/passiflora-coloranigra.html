Passiflora coloranigra

Passiflora coloranigra, commonly known as the dark purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a deep purple corona and vibrant blue and purple petals. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a rough texture and a strong, sometimes aromatic scent.

Passiflora coloranigra is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
