Phyllanthus ambatovolanus

Phyllanthus ambatovolanus, commonly known as the Madagascar spurge, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive orange or red capsules containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forest or scrubland habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and restricted distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
