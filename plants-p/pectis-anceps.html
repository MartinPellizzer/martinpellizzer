Pectis anceps

Pectis anceps, commonly known as the narrowleaf pectis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments with its narrow, linear leaves. Its yellow-orange flower heads bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pectis anceps is often found in grasslands and along canyon walls, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
