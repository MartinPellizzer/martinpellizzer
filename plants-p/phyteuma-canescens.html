Phyteuma canescens

Phyteuma canescens, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to blue. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream sides. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
