Pinus pumilio var. nasuta

Pinus pumilio var. nasuta, commonly known as the whitebark pine, is a variant of the dwarf pine native to the high-altitude regions of the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, cone-like fruits and dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it produces small, edible seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cold, dry environments and is adapted to survive in harsh mountainous conditions.

This variety is of particular interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and resilience in extreme climates.
