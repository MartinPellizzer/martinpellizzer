Potamogeton gemmiparus

Potamogeton gemmiparus, commonly known as the gemmiparous pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its linear, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant reproduces primarily through vegetative growth, with gemmae (small buds) forming on the stems and detaching to establish new plants. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a vital role in aquatic habitats by providing shelter and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its environment.
