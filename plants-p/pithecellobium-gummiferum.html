Pithecellobium gummiferum

Pithecellobium gummiferum, commonly known as the gumtree acacia, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its capacity to fix nitrogen, making it an important species in ecological restoration. Its bark contains a valuable gum, which has been used traditionally for various purposes.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting local wildlife.
