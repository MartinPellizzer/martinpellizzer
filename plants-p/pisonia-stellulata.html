Pisonia stellulata

Pisonia stellulata, commonly known as the starfruit or carambola, is a tropical plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive, five-lobed, star-shaped fruits. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.

The fruit, which is edible, turns from green to yellow or orange when mature and is widely cultivated for its unique appearance and flavor.
