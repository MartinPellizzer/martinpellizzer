Phacelophrynium bracteosum

Phacelophrynium bracteosum, commonly known as the velvetleaf arrowroot, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of many aroid species. Its rhizomes are edible and have been traditionally used in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
