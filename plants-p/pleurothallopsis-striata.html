Pleurothallopsis striata

Pleurothallopsis striata, commonly known as the striped pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on tree bark in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant features elongated, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and are often short-lived, blooming during the wet season.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
