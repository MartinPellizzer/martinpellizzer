Pithecellobium huberi

Pithecellobium huberi, commonly known as the Hawaiian velvet bean, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional benefits, it is cultivated in some areas for both horticultural and agricultural purposes.
