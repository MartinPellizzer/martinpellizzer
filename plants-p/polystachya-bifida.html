Polystachya bifida

Polystachya bifida, commonly known as the bifid sword fern, is a species of flowering plant in the family Polystachyaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, arching fronds that can reach up to 1 meter in length. Its reproductive structures include clustered spikes of small, greenish-white flowers.

Polystachya bifida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in humid, well-drained soil.
