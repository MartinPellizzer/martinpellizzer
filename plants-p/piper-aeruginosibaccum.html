Piper aeruginosibaccum

Piper aeruginosibaccum, commonly known as the blue-spotted pepper, is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to the tropical regions of South America.

This vine-like plant is characterized by its striking blue and purple spots on its leaves, which are caused by the presence of anthocyanin pigments. It produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that give way to elongated, fleshy berries. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage.

However, it is not typically used for culinary purposes, as its fruits are not considered edible.
