Pyrus Lochengensis

Locheng pear, scientifically known as Pyrus lochengensis, is a rare and ancient fruit tree native to the Yunnan Province in China. This unique pear variety is characterized by its delicate, sweet flavor and small, golden fruit that ripens in late autumn. Despite its limited geographical distribution, the Locheng pear holds cultural and historical significance in local communities. It is also prized for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Chinese remedies. Due to its endangered status, conservation efforts are underway to protect and propagate this remarkable species.
