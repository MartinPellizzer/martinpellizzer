Panax compactus

Panax compactus, commonly known as the Japanese ginseng, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that contain the seeds. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it distinct from other ginseng species, such as Panax ginseng.
