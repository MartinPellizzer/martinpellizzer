Piper peracutilimbum

Piper peracutilimbum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The botanical features of Piper peracutilimbum make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
