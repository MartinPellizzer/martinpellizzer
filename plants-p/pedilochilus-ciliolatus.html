Pedilochilus ciliolatus

Pedilochilus ciliolatus, commonly known as the ciliated pedilochilus, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which are often fragrant. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is typically found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture, though it remains relatively rare in the wild.
