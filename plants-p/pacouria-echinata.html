Pacouria echinata

Pacouria echinata, commonly known as the spiny pacouria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Pacouria echinata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and undergrowth.
