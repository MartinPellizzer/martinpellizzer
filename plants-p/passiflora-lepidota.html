Passiflora lepidota

Passiflora lepidota, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and bracts. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a silvery, velvety texture, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Passiflora lepidota is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological value.
