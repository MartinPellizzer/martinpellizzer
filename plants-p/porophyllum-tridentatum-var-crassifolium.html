Porophyllum tridentatum var. crassifolium

Porophyllum tridentatum var. crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf tarweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to arid environments.
