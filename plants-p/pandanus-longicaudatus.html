Pandanus longicaudatus

Pandanus longicaudatus, commonly known as the long-tailed pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces large, branched inflorescences bearing small, fragrant flowers. Its roots and leaves have been traditionally used in the construction of canoes and thatching materials.

The species is well adapted to coastal and sandy environments, often growing in mangrove swamps or on tidal flats.
