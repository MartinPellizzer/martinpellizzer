Pachypodium Lamerei Var. Ramosum

Pachypodium lamerei var. ramosum is a striking succulent native to Madagascar known for its unique, tree-like appearance. This variant features a thick, stem-like trunk with branching arms that give it a distinctive, almost cactus-like look. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping. The plant's ability to store water in its thick stems allows it to survive in harsh conditions. Its ornamental value has made it a sought-after addition to both indoor and outdoor gardens.
