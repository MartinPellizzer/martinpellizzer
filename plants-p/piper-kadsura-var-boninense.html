Piper kadsura var. boninense

Piper kadsura var. boninense, commonly known as the Bonin pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the Bonin Islands.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with smooth margins. The plant produces small, yellow-green flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy berries. Its leaves and fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to humid, tropical climates.
