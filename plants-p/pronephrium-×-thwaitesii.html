Pronephrium × thwaitesii

Pronephrium × thwaitesii, commonly known as the hybrid fern, is a rare and intriguing botanical specimen resulting from the cross between Pronephrium and Thwaitesia species.

It exhibits characteristics of both parent plants, showcasing a unique combination of morphological traits. The plant typically features delicate, feathery fronds that are often variegated in color, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its growth habit is typically epiphytic, meaning it thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Pronephrium × thwaitesii is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
