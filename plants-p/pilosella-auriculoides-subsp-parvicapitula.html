Pilosella auriculoides subsp. parvicapitula

Pilosella auriculoides subsp.

parvicapitula, commonly known as the small-headed alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its small, clustered heads of yellow flowers and hairy, spoon-shaped leaves.

The plant thrives in rocky and open habitats, often growing at high altitudes. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts.

Its compact growth form and distinctive foliage make it a notable species in alpine botany.
