Phoebe omeiensis

Phoebe omeiensis, commonly known as the Chinese phoebe, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to southern China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
