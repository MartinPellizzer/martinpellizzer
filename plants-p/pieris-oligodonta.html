Pieris oligodonta

Pieris oligodonta, commonly known as the Japanese pieris, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in early spring. It prefers partial to full shade and well-drained, acidic soil.

Pieris oligodonta is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
