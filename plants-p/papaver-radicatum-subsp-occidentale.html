Papaver radicatum subsp. occidentale

Papaver radicatum subsp.

occidentale, commonly known as the western poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a black center.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
