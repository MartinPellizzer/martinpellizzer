Paphiopedilum insigne f. sanderianum

Paphiopedilum insigne f. sanderianum, commonly known as the Sander's Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, showy flowers that typically feature a distinctive pouch-shaped lip and vibrant colors such as yellow and green. The plant has thick, fleshy roots adapted for storing water, which helps it survive in its natural habitat. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often grown for its long-lasting blooms.
