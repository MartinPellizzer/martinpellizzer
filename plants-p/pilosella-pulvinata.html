Pilosella pulvinata

Pilosella pulvinata, commonly known as the purple mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
