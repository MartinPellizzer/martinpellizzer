Pinus halepensis var. brutia

Pinus halepensis var. brutia, commonly known as the Syrian pine, is a variant of the Aleppo pine native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a medium-sized evergreen conifer with needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in fascicles of five. The plant produces cones that mature over several years, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

The species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife.
