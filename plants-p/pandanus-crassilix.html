Pandanus crassilix

Pandanus crassilix, commonly known as the broad-leaf pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the Pacific Islands and is characterized by its large, strap-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its roots are often used in traditional crafts and its leaves are utilized in basket weaving.

The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove forests or on sandy shores.
