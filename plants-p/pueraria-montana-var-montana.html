Pueraria montana var. montana

Pueraria montana var. montana, commonly known as Kwao Krua, is a vigorous climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy purple or pink flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
