Passiflora siamica

Passiflora siamica, commonly known as the Siam passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a crown of filaments and a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture and are often used in traditional medicine.

Passiflora siamica thrives in humid, tropical climates and is valued for both its ornamental beauty and medicinal properties.
