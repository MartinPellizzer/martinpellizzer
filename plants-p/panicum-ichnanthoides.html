Panicum ichnanthoides

Panicum ichnanthoides, commonly known as little bluestem, is a native North American grass species characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted inflorescence.

It is a warm-season grass that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairie and woodland habitats. The plant is known for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil, making it a valuable species for ecological restoration. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive bluish hue that becomes more pronounced in the summer months.

Panicum ichnanthoides plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining biodiversity in native plant communities.
