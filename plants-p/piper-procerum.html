Piper procerum

Piper procerum, commonly known as the slender pipevine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.

Piper procerum is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
