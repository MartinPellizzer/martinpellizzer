Polygala calcarea var. timbalii

Polygala calcarea var. timbalii, commonly known as the Timballo's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight hairy texture.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for various pollinators in its native habitat.
