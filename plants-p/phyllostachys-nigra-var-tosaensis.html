Phyllostachys nigra var. tosaensis

Phyllostachys nigra var. tosaensis, commonly known as the Tosa bamboo, is a variant of the black bamboo species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black culms that are often covered in a fine, silky sheen. The plant typically grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a striking ornamental plant. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a glossy green color.

This bamboo is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its unique appearance.
