Phylica barbata

Phylica barbata, commonly known as the barbed phylica, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, which are characteristic of the genus Phylica. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, often covered in fine hairs, and it is known for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to stabilize coastal dunes and prevent erosion.
