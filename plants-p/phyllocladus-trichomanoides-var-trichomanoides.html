Phyllocladus trichomanoides var. trichomanoides

Phyllocladus trichomanoides var. trichomanoides, commonly known as the silver pine, is a small evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It has distinctive silvery-blue foliage that provides a striking visual contrast against its dark bark. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. Its leaves are alternate and scale-like, contributing to its unique appearance.

The species is adapted to coastal and subalpine environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
