Podostemum pterophyllum

Podostemum pterophyllum, commonly known as the featherleaf waterwheel, is a unique aquatic plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It thrives in fast-flowing streams and rivers, where its specialized roots anchor it to submerged rocks. The plant's leaves are small, opposite, and often have a feathery appearance, which gives it its common name. It produces tiny, yellow flowers that are pollinated by insects, and its seeds are dispersed by water.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically important species in its native environment.
