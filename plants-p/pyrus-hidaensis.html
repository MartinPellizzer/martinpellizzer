Pyrus hidaensis

Pyrus hidaensis, commonly known as the Hida pear, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowers. The tree produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are typically not suitable for consumption but are valued for their ornamental appeal. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

The species is also noted for its ability to adapt to various soil types, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
