Prunus parviflora

Prunus parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or Japanese dwarf cherry, is a small deciduous shrub or tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant has small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Prunus parviflora is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate.
