Polygonum declinatum

Polygonum declinatum, commonly known as the drooping knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinctive veining pattern.

Polygonum declinatum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
