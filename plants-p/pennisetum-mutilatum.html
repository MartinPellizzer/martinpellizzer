Pennisetum mutilatum

Pennisetum mutilatum, commonly known as blue fescue, is a low-growing, ornamental grass native to the western United States.

It features fine, silver-blue foliage that turns reddish in the fall, creating a striking visual contrast. The plant has a compact growth habit, typically reaching heights of 6 to 12 inches, making it ideal for rock gardens or as a border plant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Pennisetum mutilatum is drought-tolerant and resistant to pests and diseases, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
