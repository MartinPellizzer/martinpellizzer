Passiflora storckii

Passiflora storckii, commonly known as the yellow passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which feature a unique structure with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy surface.

Passiflora storckii is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
