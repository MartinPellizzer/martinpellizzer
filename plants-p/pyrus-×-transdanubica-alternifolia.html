Pyrus × transdanubica alternifolia

Pyrus × transdanubica alternifolia, commonly known as the Transdanubian weeping pear, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or green in color. Native to Hungary, it thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to gardens and arboretums.
