Piper arboreum f. stamineum

Piper arboreum f. stamineum, commonly known as the white pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper arboreum species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its reduced floral structures, with stamen-like structures replacing the typical female flowers, leading to the production of white, peppercorn-like berries. The plant is cultivated for its seeds, which are dried and used as a spice. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it distinct from the standard Piper arboreum, which produces black pepper.
