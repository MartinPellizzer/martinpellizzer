Phaeopappus longispinus

Phaeopappus longispinus, commonly known as the longispined sandbur, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its fruit is a bur with long, stiff bracts that can adhere to clothing and animal fur, aiding in seed dispersal.

The species is known for its adaptability to arid environments and its role in stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
