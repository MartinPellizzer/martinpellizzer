Poa pratensis var. subcaerulescens

Poa pratensis var. subcaerulescens, commonly known as the bluegrass, is a variant of the common bluegrass species.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green foliage and dense growth habit, making it a popular choice for turfgrass. The plant typically produces slender, flowering stems with spikelets that are often tinged with blue or purple hues. It thrives in temperate climates and is well-suited for lawns and sports fields due to its durability and aesthetic appeal.

Botanically, it is a member of the Poaceae family and is known for its ability to form a dense sod through rhizomes and stolons.
