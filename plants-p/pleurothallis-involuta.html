Pleurothallis involuta

Pleurothallis involuta, commonly known as the curled lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant typically produces small, delicate flowers with a distinctive curled lip, which is a key feature in its taxonomic classification. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized orchid species.
