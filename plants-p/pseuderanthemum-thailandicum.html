Pseuderanthemum thailandicum

Pseuderanthemum thailandicum, commonly known as the Thai pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
