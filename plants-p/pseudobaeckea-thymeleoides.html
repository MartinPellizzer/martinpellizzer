Pseudobaeckea thymeleoides

Pseudobaeckea thymeleoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf baeckea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its flowering period occurs during the spring and summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact form and seasonal blooms.
