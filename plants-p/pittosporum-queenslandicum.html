Pittosporum queenslandicum

Pittosporum queenslandicum, commonly known as the Queensland Pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subtropical regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
