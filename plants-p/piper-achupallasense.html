Piper achupallasense

Piper achupallasense, commonly known as the Peruvian pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Andean region of South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.
