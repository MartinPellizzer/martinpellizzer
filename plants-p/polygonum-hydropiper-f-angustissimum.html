Polygonum hydropiper f. angustissimum

Polygonum hydropiper f. angustissimum, commonly known as narrowleaf smartweed, is a variant of the broader Polygonum hydropiper species.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer. It is typically found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and ditches.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in wetland ecosystems and a subject of interest in botany and ecological studies.
