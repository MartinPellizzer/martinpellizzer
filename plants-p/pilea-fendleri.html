Pilea fendleri

Pilea fendleri, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that resemble small coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is a compact, evergreen perennial that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Pilea fendleri is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
