Potentilla jezoensis

Potentilla jezoensis, commonly known as the Japanese cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically compound, with five leaflets, and the plant is often used in ornamental gardening.

Potentilla jezoensis is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
