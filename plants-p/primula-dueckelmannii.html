Primula dueckelmannii

Primula dueckelmannii, commonly known as the Dueckelmann's primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in the spring and early summer, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays. Its foliage consists of broad, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

Primula dueckelmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements in suitable climates.
