Pleurothallis deborana

Pleurothallis deborana, commonly known as the Debora's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Pleurothallis deborana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique beauty.
