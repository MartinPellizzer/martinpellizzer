Pyrrhocactus andreaeanus

Pyrrhocactus andreaeanus, commonly known as the Andean red cactus, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically red or orange in color. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the warm season. It is adapted to high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
