Paracaryopsis coelestina

Paracaryopsis coelestina, commonly known as the sky-blue grass, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, blue to purple florets arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that contributes to its common name.

Paracaryopsis coelestina is often found in grasslands and open habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
