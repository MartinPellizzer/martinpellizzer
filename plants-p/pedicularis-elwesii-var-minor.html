Pedicularis elwesii var. minor

Pedicularis elwesii var. minor, commonly known as the lesser yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers are tubular and often have a distinctive yellow throat, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
