Phoebe sheareri var. stenophylla

Phoebe sheareri var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf phoebe, is a rare and endemic plant species found primarily in the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often with a distinctive white or pale green coloration. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists studying tropical flora and plant adaptations.
