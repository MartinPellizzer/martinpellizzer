Paronychia argentea var. scariosissima

Paronychia argentea var. scariosissima, commonly known as silverleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and hairy, silver-gray foliage. The leaves are typically opposite or whorled, with a distinctive waxy coating that aids in water retention. It produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
