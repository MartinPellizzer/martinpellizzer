Penstemon breviflorus subsp. typicus

Penstemon breviflorus subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the short-flowered beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from purple to pink.

The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and gray-green in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
