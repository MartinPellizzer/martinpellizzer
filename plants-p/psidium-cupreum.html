Psidium cupreum

Psidium cupreum, commonly known as the copperleaf guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that range from dark green to coppery in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by edible, acidic berries similar to guavas. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Psidium cupreum is also valued for its ability to tolerate shade and poor soil conditions.
