Passerina uniflora var. alba

Passerina uniflora var. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its showy, white, single flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It is an important early nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
