Piper maxonii var. varium

Piper maxonii var. varium, commonly known as the Hawaiian pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and have a mild, peppery flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often grown as an ornamental or for its aromatic properties.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
