Prunus serrulata f. microflora

Prunus serrulata f. microflora, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or Japanese weeping cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub characterized by its small, delicate leaves and profuse pink or white flowers.

It is a naturally occurring form of Prunus serrulata, known for its graceful, drooping branches that create a weeping appearance. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden borders or container planting. Its botanical features include serrated leaf margins and a compact growth habit, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

It is often cultivated for its spring blossoms and is a popular choice in ornamental horticulture.
