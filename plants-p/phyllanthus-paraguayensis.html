Phyllanthus paraguayensis

Phyllanthus paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguay gooseberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and smooth, often reddish stems. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly waxy texture.
