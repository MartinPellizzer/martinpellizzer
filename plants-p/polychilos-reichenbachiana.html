Polychilos reichenbachiana

Polychilos reichenbachiana, commonly known as the Reichenbach's polychilos, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often colorful flowers. Its leaves are typically elongated and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of interest for botanical conservation efforts.
