Panicum molliculmum

Panicum molliculmum, commonly known as silky panicgrass, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that cover the leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often pale green or reddish in color.

Silky panicgrass is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
