Puya boyacana

Puya boyacana, commonly known as the "Boyaca grass" or "Andean torch," is a striking plant native to the Andean highlands of South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant features broad, strap-shaped leaves that form a rosette, creating a dense, grass-like appearance. Puya boyacana produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late summer.

It is a slow-growing, long-lived species often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
