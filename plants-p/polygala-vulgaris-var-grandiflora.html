Polygala vulgaris var. grandiflora

Polygala vulgaris var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often with a reddish or purple tinge. The plant produces small, greenish or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are characteristic of the Polygala genus. Its leaves are narrow and alternate, with a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.
