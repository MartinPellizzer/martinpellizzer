Pultenaea mutabilis var. mutabilis

Pultenaea mutabilis var. mutabilis, commonly known as the variable pultenaea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a slender, curved pod that splits open to release seeds.

This species is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.
