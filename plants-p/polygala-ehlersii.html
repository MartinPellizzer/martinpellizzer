Polygala ehlersii

Polygala ehlersii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has feathery, gray-green foliage. It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
