Primula forbesii subsp. barbeyana

Primula forbesii subsp.

barbeyana, commonly known as the Barbet's primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of southern China. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, with a distinctive greenish throat.

The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its compact appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
