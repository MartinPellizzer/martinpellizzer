Pyrus communis proles sativa

Pyrus communis proles sativa, commonly known as the common pear, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces pome fruits, which are typically harvested in autumn and are known for their sweet, juicy flesh. It is cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes, with numerous cultivars developed for specific fruit qualities.

The plant is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
