Paspalum paradisiacum

Paspalum paradisiacum, commonly known as paradise grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or purple in color, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage crop in certain regions.
