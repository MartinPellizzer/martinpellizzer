Protea harmeri

Protea harmeri, commonly known as the Harmer's protea, is a species native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its striking, cone-shaped inflorescence. The plant typically grows in fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with a central cone surrounded by leaf-like bracts.

Protea harmeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems.
