Piper scutellifolium

Piper scutellifolium, commonly known as the shield-leaf pepper, is a tropical climbing shrub native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into peppery berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
