Panicum arborescens

Panicum arborescens, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and its ability to form dense clumps. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a reddish tinge, especially noticeable in the fall. It produces fluffy, pinkish-white seed heads that are visually striking.

Panicum arborescens is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
