Pyrrosia distichocarpa

Pyrrosia distichocarpa, commonly known as the two-ranked moss fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, evergreen fronds that are typically two-ranked, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often growing on rocks or tree trunks. Its leaves are leathery and have a deep green color, with a serrated margin.

Pyrrosia distichocarpa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
