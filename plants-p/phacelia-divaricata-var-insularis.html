Phacelia divaricata var. insularis

Phacelia divaricata var. insularis, commonly known as the island phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to thrive in dry, rocky environments. This variety is often found in coastal and inland areas with well-drained soils.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
