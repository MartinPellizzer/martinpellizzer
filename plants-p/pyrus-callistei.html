Pyrus callistei

Pyrus callistei, commonly known as the Mexican whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible, round fruits resembling small apples. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
