Primula tangutica

Primula tangutica, commonly known as the Tangut primrose, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces showy inflorescences that bloom in late spring.

Primula tangutica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
