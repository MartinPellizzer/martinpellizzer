Prunus phaeosticta

Prunus phaeosticta, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or mountain cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet cherries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
