Panicum trinii

Panicum trinii, commonly known as the Trinidad grass, is a species of grass native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Panicum trinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
