Pittosporum tobira var. tobira

Pittosporum tobira var. tobira, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall, adding seasonal interest to the landscape. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in early spring, followed by ornamental red berries in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it popular in both landscape design and as a container plant.
