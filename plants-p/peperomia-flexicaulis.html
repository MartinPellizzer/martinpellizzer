Peperomia flexicaulis

Peperomia flexicaulis, commonly known as the drooping peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a bluish tinge, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or pale pink. The plant has a trailing growth habit, making it suitable for hanging baskets or as a ground cover. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, and it is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Peperomia flexicaulis is valued for its ornamental foliage and ease of care, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
