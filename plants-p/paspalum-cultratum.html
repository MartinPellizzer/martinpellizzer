Paspalum cultratum

Paspalum cultratum, commonly known as the cutleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and swamps, and is often found in association with water. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often folded in a V-shape, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowering spikes are usually short and clustered, producing small, pale green flowers.

Paspalum cultratum is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
