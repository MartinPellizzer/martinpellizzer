Polygala pisaurensis

Polygala pisaurensis, commonly known as the Pisaurensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
