Primula hirsuta var. angustata

Primula hirsuta var. angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, or purple. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom early in the spring.
