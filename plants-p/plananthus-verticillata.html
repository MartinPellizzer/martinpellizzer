Plananthus verticillata

Plananthus verticillata, commonly known as the spiral sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowering stems bear small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Plananthus verticillata plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food and shelter for various invertebrates and birds.
