Piper gaudichaudianum

Piper gaudichaudianum, commonly known as the Hawaiian pepper or kona pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, particularly Hawaii, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
