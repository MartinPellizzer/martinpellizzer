Passiflora ciliata var. orinocensis

Passiflora ciliata var. orinocensis, commonly known as the Orinoco passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a unique blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with filaments that resemble threads. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tangy flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and often have a reddish hue.

This species is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its potential use in traditional medicine.
