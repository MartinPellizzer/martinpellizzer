Polygala genistoides

Polygala genistoides, commonly known as the silverleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
