Pimpinella tragium subsp. polyclada

Pimpinella tragium subsp.

polyclada, commonly known as the clustered cow-wheat, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family. It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in umbels.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats. It has a taproot system and produces small, dry fruits called schizocarps.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
