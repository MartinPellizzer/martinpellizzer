Pediocactus sileri

Pediocactus sileri, commonly known as the Siler's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a compact, globular shape, often resembling a hedgehog. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green and blue-gray foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in cactus gardens and conservation efforts.
