Phanera alata

Phanera alata, commonly known as the winged phanera, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its distinctive winged stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Phanera alata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
