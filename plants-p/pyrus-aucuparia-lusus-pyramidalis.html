Pyrus aucuparia lusus pyramidalis

Pyrus aucuparia lusus pyramidalis, commonly known as the Pyramidal Crabapple, is a cultivar of the common crabapple tree.

It is characterized by its narrow, pyramidal shape and showy pink or white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in jams and jellies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

This variety is also valued for its disease resistance and ability to withstand urban environments.
