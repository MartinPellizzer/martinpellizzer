Pennisetum vulpinum

Pennisetum vulpinum, commonly known as red grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red-tipped foliage and feathery plumes that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its slender, upright stems can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Pennisetum vulpinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant color and texture.
