Phyllanthus albus

Phyllanthus albus, commonly known as the white phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Phyllanthus albus is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks.
