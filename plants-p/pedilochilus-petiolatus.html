Pedilochilus petiolatus

Pedilochilus petiolatus, commonly known as the long-stemmed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow with red or purple markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
