Potentilla macropetala

Potentilla macropetala, commonly known as the large-flowered cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features silver-green, lance-shaped leaves with a fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
