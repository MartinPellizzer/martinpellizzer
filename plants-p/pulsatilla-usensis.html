Pulsatilla usensis

Pulsatilla usensis, commonly known as the European pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, showy, cup-shaped flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, stout stem with basal leaves that are palmately divided. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, with prominent stamens and a central pistil.

Pulsatilla usensis is adapted to rocky or open habitats and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
