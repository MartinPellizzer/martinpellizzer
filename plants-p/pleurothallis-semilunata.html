Pleurothallis semilunata

Pleurothallis semilunata, commonly known as the moonlight orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, often resembling a crescent moon. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and decaying organic matter.

Pleurothallis semilunata is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique beauty.
