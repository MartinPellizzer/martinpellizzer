Phlomoides baldschuanica

Phlomoides baldschuanica, commonly known as the Caucasian phlomoides, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry habitats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with blue to purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
