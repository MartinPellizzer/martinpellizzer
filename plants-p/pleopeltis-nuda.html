Pleopeltis nuda

Pleopeltis nuda, commonly known as the naked pleopeltis, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and simple, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the leaves.

Pleopeltis nuda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
