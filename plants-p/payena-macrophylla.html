Payena macrophylla

Payena macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf payena, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, lowland forest environments and is often found in secondary growth areas.

The tree is valued for its hard wood and has some traditional medicinal uses among local communities.
