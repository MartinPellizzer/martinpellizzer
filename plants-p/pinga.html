Pinga

Pinga, commonly known as *Cyperus esculentus*, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found growing in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has cylindrical, grass-like stems that are often used as a vegetable in various cuisines, particularly in parts of Africa and South America. Its rhizomes are edible and have a nutty flavor, making it a valuable food source in some regions.

Pinga also plays a role in traditional medicine and is sometimes used for its purported health benefits.
