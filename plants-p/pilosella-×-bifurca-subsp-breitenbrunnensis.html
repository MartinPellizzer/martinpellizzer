Pilosella × bifurca subsp. breitenbrunnensis

Pilosella × bifurca subsp.

breitenbrunnensis, commonly known as the Alpine hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe. It is a perennial herb characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Due to its habitat preferences, it is often found in mountainous areas and is considered a rare and specialized species.
