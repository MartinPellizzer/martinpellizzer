Potentilla heynei

Potentilla heynei, commonly known as the Himalayan cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated edge.

Potentilla heynei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
