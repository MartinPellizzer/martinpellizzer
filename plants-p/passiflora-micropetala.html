Passiflora micropetala

Passiflora micropetala, commonly known as the small-petalled passionflower, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Argentina.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking, intricate flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a unique crown of filaments. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers, which bloom in summer, are pollinated by insects and attract a variety of wildlife.
