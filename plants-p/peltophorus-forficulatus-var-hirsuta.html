Peltophorus forficulatus var. hirsuta

Peltophorus forficulatus var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bladder fern, is a variant of the bladder fern species native to parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are clustered in small, bladder-like formations along the fronds.

The hairy texture of the leaves is a distinguishing feature that aids in its identification and adaptation to its habitat.
