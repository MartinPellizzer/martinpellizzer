Pandanophyllum palustre var. malesica

Pandanophyllum palustre var. malesica, commonly known as the swamp pandan, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional cooking. The plant thrives in wet, marshy environments and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soil. Its leaves contain aromatic compounds that contribute to its distinctive fragrance.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
