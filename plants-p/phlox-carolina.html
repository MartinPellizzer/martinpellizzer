Phlox carolina

Phlox carolina, commonly known as the eastern phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features erect, branching stems covered in opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy clusters of fragrant, pink to purple flowers in early to mid-summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies adds ecological value to its habitat.
