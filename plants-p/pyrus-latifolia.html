Pyrus latifolia

Pyrus latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf pear, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and fruit cultivation.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
