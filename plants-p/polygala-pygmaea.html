Polygala pygmaea

Polygala pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf milkvetch, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant belongs to the family Polygalaceae, which is known for its distinctive floral structures. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous minute seeds.

Polygala pygmaea is adapted to arid environments and is often found in desert scrub and limestone soils.
