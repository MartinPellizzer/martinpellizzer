Psidium dumetorum

Psidium dumetorum, commonly known as the wild guava or *carambola* in some regions, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a rough, pubescent surface. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by edible, berry-like fruits. The fruit is typically green when mature and has a mild, slightly acidic flavor.

It is often cultivated for its fruit and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
