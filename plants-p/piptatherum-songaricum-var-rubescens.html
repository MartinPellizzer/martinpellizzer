Piptatherum songaricum var. rubescens

Piptatherum songaricum var. rubescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed feathergrass, is a grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its distinctive reddish coloration in the stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, spike-like cluster of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its fine texture and color contrast.
