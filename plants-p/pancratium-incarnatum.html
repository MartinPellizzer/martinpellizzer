Pancratium incarnatum

Pancratium incarnatum, commonly known as the redflower yellow nutmeg, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant produces distinctive, red, fleshy berries that contain the seeds. Its foliage consists of long, strap-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine.

Pancratium incarnatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
