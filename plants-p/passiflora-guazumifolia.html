Passiflora guazumifolia

Passiflora guazumifolia, commonly known as the Guazumal passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature five prominent sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by birds and mammals. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor.

Passiflora guazumifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
