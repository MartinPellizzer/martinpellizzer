Pescatoria fimbriata

Pescatoria fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed seashore plant, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky coastal soils and is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds. Its leaves are narrow and fimbriate, meaning they have fringed edges, which help reduce water loss.

Pescatoria fimbriata plays a role in stabilizing dune systems and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
