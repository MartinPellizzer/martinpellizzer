Primula bulgarica

Primula bulgarica, commonly known as the Bulgarian primrose, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing violet to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens or shaded rockeries.

Its ornamental value and ease of cultivation have made it a popular choice among gardeners in temperate climates.
