Potamogeton pectinatus proles mongolicus

Potamogeton pectinatus proles mongolicus, commonly known as Mongolian pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Asia.

It has long, slender stems that are often branched and bear small, narrow leaves arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne in clusters near the water surface. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in shallow to moderately deep waters with soft to muddy substrates.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
