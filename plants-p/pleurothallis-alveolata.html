Pleurothallis alveolata

Pleurothallis alveolata, commonly known as the "Tuberculate Orchid," is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees, drawing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its pseudobulbs are covered in small, tuberculate bumps, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
