Polypodium leucorhizon

Polypodium leucorhizon, commonly known as the northern shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its broad, shield-shaped fronds that are typically leathery and dark green in color. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices. Its rhizome is thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients efficiently.

Polypodium leucorhizon reproduces via spores, which are produced on the underside of its fronds in specialized structures called sorus.
