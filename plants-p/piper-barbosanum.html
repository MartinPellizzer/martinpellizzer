Piper barbosanum

Piper barbosanum, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
