Passiflora pallida

Passiflora pallida, commonly known as the white passionflower, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking, white flowers with intricate structures. The plant has palmate leaves and emits a mild, sweet fragrance. Its fruit is a small, edible berry with a tart flavor.

Passiflora pallida is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
