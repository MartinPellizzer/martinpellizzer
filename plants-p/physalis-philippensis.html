Physalis philippensis

Physalis philippensis, commonly known as the Philippine lantern or goldenberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the genus Physalis within the nightshade family, Solanaceae. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries enclosed in a papery calyx that turns red as the fruit matures. The leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant typically grows as an annual herb.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
