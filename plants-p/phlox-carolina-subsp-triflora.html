Phlox carolina subsp. triflora

Phlox carolina subsp.

triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered false bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It produces three to five flowers per stem, which gives it its subspecies name "triflora.".

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
