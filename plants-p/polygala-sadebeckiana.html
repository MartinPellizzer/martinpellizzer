Polygala sadebeckiana

Polygala sadebeckiana, commonly known as the Sadebeck's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
