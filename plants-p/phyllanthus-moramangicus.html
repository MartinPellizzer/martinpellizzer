Phyllanthus moramangicus

Phyllanthus moramangicus, commonly known as the Moramanga leaf plant, is a species native to specific regions in Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing minute seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or savannah-like habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
