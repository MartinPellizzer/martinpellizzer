Phlomis shepardii

Phlomis shepardii, commonly known as Shepard's blazingstar, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, yellow to orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
