Pyrus autumnalis f. pallescens

Pyrus autumnalis f. pallescens, commonly known as the whitish pear.

This variant is a form of the autumn pear, characterized by its pale, almost whitish foliage. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland areas. The leaves are typically smaller and more densely packed compared to the typical variety.

It produces small, yellowish flowers in spring, followed by edible fruits that are generally less abundant than in the standard species.
