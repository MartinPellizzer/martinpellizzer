Phaseolus ellipticus var. albus

Phaseolus ellipticus var. albus, commonly known as the white runner bean, is a cultivated variant of the common bean species.

It is characterized by its elongated, elliptical pods that mature to a white or pale green color. The plant is typically an annual legume with compound leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often grown for its edible beans.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and use in both culinary and agricultural contexts.
