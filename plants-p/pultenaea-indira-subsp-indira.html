Pultenaea indira subsp. indira

Pultenaea indira subsp.

indira, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It features slender, often twining stems and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in spikes, which bloom from late summer to early autumn. It is well adapted to sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or heathland environments.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
