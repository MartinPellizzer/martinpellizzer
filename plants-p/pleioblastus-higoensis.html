Pleioblastus higoensis

Pleioblastus higoensis, commonly known as the Japanese forest grass, is a species of bamboo native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is commonly found in forested areas. Its growth habit makes it suitable for use in ornamental landscaping and as a ground cover.

The plant is also valued for its ability to form dense clumps, providing both aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
