Pancratium cunninghamii

Pancratium cunninghamii, commonly known as the golden arum, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, yellow-orange spatulate inflorescence surrounded by a deep purple spathe. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and is adapted to sandy soils. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Pancratium cunninghamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
