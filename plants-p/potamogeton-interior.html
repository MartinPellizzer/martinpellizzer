Potamogeton interior

Potamogeton interior, commonly known as the interior pondweed, is a freshwater aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are often submerged, though some may rise to the water surface. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in lakes, ponds, and wetlands.

Potamogeton interior plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
