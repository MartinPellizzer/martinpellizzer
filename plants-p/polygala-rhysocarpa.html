Polygala rhysocarpa

Polygala rhysocarpa, commonly known as the red globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. The plant produces distinctive red or pink globular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Polygala rhysocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
