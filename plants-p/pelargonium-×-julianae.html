Pelargonium × julianae

Pelargonium × julianae, commonly known as the hybrid geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a variety of colors including red, pink, and white. The leaves are typically lobed and have a velvety texture, often with a distinctive reddish or purple underside. This plant is native to South Africa and is widely cultivated as a garden ornamental due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a range of garden environments.
