Phainantha myrteoloides

Phainantha myrteoloides, commonly known as the African myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
