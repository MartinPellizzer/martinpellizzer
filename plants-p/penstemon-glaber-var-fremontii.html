Penstemon glaber var. fremontii

Penstemon glaber var. fremontii, commonly known as Fremont's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial species characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces flowers in shades of purple, pink, and red, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in gardens.
