Potentilla chiloensis

Potentilla chiloensis, commonly known as Chilean cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, often in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are serrated and have a slightly hairy texture, and it is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

Potentilla chiloensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
