Paspalum stramineum

Paspalum stramineum, commonly known as slender grass or paspalum grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides, fields, and waste places. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and environmental conditions.

Paspalum stramineum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and rapid growth.
