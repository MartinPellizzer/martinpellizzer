Pleroma floribundum

Pleroma floribundum, commonly known as the common pleuroma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Pleroma floribundum is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in suitable climates.
