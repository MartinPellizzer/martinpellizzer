Papaya digitata

Papaya digitata, commonly known as the wild papaya, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and yellow-orange fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its fruit is edible, with a sweet and slightly acidic flavor, and is rich in vitamins and enzymes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for medicinal purposes.
