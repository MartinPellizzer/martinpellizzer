Periploca audicea

Periploca audicea, commonly known as the Madagascar periploca, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It is often found in dry forest and scrub habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
