Pyrus glaucophylla

Pyrus glaucophylla, commonly known as the silver-leaf pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive silvery-gray underside. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits resembling pears. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and orchards.
