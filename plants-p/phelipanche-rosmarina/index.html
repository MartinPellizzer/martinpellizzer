Phelipanche Rosmarina

Phelipanche rosmarina is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region known for its fragrant leaves and unique growth habits. This species belongs to the Orobanchaceae family, commonly referred to as the broomrape family. It is often found growing as a hemiparasitic plant, relying on host plants for nutrients while still performing photosynthesis. Its aromatic properties have made it a subject of interest in both traditional medicine and horticulture. Despite its benefits, it can be invasive and challenging to control in certain ecosystems.
