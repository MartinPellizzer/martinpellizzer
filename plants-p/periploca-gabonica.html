Periploca gabonica

Periploca gabonica, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the base. Its flowers open in the evening and close by dawn, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
