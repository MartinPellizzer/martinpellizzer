Polygonum leblebicii

Polygonum leblebicii, commonly known as the Turkish knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive swollen nodes and arrow-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands and along riverbanks. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing small, greenish flowers clustered in dense spikes.

Polygonum leblebicii is notable for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
