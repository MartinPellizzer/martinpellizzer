Paspalum connectens

Paspalum connectens, commonly known as the slender paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wetland and marshy environments, often growing in dense mats.

Paspalum connectens is notable for its ability to tolerate waterlogged soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
