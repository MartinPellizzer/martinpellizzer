Pelargonium exstipulatum

Pelargonium exstipulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically hairless and have a pointed tip. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium exstipulatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
