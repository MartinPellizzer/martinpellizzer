Pelargonium skelii

Pelargonium skelii, commonly known as the Skel's geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive reddish or purple tinge. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice among gardeners in Mediterranean climates.
