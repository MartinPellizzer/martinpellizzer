Potentilla freyniana var. nitens

Potentilla freyniana var. nitens, commonly known as the shining potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and is often found in rocky soils and desert environments. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in hot, dry conditions.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and adaptability to harsh climates, making it a resilient addition to xeriscaped gardens.
