Peperomia erosa

Peperomia erosa, commonly known as the creeping peperomia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its compact size and ornamental foliage, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and rock gardens.
