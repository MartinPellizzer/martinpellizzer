Prunella canadensis

Prunella canadensis, commonly known as Canadian selfheal, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Prunella canadensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
