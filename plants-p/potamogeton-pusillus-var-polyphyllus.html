Potamogeton pusillus var. polyphyllus

Potamogeton pusillus var. polyphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and has long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it is often found in association with other aquatic vegetation. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
