Pleurothallis lilianiae

Pleurothallis lilianiae, commonly known as the Lilian's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapting to the humid and shaded conditions of its natural habitat.

Pleurothallis lilianiae is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral structure and relatively easy care requirements.
