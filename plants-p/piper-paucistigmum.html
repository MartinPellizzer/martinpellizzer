Piper paucistigmum

Piper paucistigmum, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper vine, is a woody vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and often have a serrated margin. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, berry-like fruits.

The plant is typically found in humid, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.
