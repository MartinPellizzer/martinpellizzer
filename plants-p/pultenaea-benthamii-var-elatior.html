Pultenaea benthamii var. elatior

Pultenaea benthamii var. elatior, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sand dunes, thriving in well-drained soils. Its yellow flowers, which appear in spring, are clustered in racemes and attract various pollinators.

The varietal form elatior is distinguished by its more slender growth habit compared to the typical variety.
