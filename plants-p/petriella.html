Petriella

Petriella, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

The plant's roots form a symbiotic relationship with fungi, enabling it to absorb water and minerals from the air and rain. Petriella typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in the canopy of hardwood forests.
