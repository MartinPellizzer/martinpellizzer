Persea albiramea

Persea albiramea, commonly known as the white-leaf persea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America and northern South America. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces small, round fruits that are typically covered in a thick, waxy coating.

Persea albiramea is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
