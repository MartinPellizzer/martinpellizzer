Polygonum araraticum

Polygonum araraticum, commonly known as the red root or araraticum knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, often branched stems that are reddish in color, and its alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Polygonum araraticum is known for its medicinal properties and has been traditionally used in herbal remedies.
