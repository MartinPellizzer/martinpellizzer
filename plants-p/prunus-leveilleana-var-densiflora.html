Prunus leveilleana var. densiflora

Prunus leveilleana var. densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of southeastern China.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, giving it its varietal name "densiflora.". The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth margin and a glossy green upper surface. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters and prefers well-drained soils in full sun.

This plant is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms.
