Picea abies var. microsperma

Picea abies var. microsperma, commonly known as the dwarf spruce, is a variant of the Norway spruce characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller cones.

It is native to mountainous regions of Europe and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. The plant exhibits needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, providing a striking visual contrast in its natural habitat. It produces small, woody cones that contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its unique appearance.
