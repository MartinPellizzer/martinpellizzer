Piper mischocarpum

Piper mischocarpum, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a member of the Piperaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly rough texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
