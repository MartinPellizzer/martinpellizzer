Potamogeton polygonifolius subvar. terrestris

Potamogeton polygonifolius subvar. terrestris, commonly known as the terrestrial pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically narrow and tapering, arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown, and it thrives in shallow, nutrient-rich waters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

This subspecies is often found in marshes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.
