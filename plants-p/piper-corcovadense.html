Piper corcovadense

Piper corcovadense, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a bright red color, often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are often used as a spice, similar to black pepper, and are valued for their pungent flavor.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forested areas.
