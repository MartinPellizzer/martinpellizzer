Pilea hydrocotyliflora

Pilea hydrocotyliflora, commonly known as the water shield, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its floating leaves that are partially submerged.

The plant features a rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its roots are anchored in the sediment at the water's edge, while its stems and leaves float on the surface. Pilea hydrocotyliflora thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and small aquatic life.
