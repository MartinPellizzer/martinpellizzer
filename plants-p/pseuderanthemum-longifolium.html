Pseuderanthemum longifolium

Pseuderanthemum longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved pseuderanthemum, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and typically have two lips, with the lower lip being more prominent.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
