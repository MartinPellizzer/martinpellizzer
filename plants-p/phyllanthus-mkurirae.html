Phyllanthus mkurirae

Phyllanthus mkurirae, commonly known as the East African spurge, is a shrub native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and waxy, dark green leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces bright yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
