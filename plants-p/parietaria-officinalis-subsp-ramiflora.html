Parietaria officinalis subsp. ramiflora

Parietaria officinalis subsp.

ramiflora, commonly known as the creeping wallrocket, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features a mat-forming habit with stout, hairy stems that often root at the nodes.

The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a rough, hairy texture and a distinctive yellowish-green color. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are borne on short pedicels.

This species is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in rocky areas, where it thrives in sunny to partially shaded conditions.
