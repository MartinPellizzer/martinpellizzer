Pleurothallis calderae

Pleurothallis calderae, commonly known as the Caldera orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
