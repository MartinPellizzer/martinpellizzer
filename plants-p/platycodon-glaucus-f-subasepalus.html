Platycodon glaucus f. subasepalus

Platycodon glaucus f. subasepalus, commonly known as the narrow-sepal bellflower, is a variant of the perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
