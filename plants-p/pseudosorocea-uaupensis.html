Pseudosorocea uaupensis

Pseudosorocea uaupensis, commonly known as the Brazilian fern, is a unique species belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori, which are clustered on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
