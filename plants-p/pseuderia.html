Pseuderia

Pseuderia, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, delicate appearance, with pale green leaves and a single, elongated flower that resembles a ghostly figure. Native to the tropical regions of South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them.

Due to its specific habitat requirements and limited distribution, Pseuderia is considered a conservation concern.
