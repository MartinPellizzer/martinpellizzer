Passiflora bracteosa

Passiflora bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteosa passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Passiflora bracteosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
