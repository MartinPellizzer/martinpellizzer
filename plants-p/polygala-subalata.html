Polygala subalata

Polygala subalata, commonly known as the alpine milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool, temperate climates. Its flowers are usually pale blue or violet, with a distinctive hooded structure, and it produces small, round seeds.

Polygala subalata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and unique floral form.
