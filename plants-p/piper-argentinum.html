Piper argentinum

Piper argentinum, commonly known as the Argentine pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into three-winged, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a slightly rough texture.

Piper argentinum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
