Persea kusanoi

Persea kusanoi, commonly known as the Japanese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Japan and parts of Korea.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible when fully mature and astringent when unripe. Its botanical features include a smooth, gray bark and a tendency to form dense canopies.

Persea kusanoi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of persimmons.
