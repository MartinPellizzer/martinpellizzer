Pharbitis hederacea var. nil

Pharbitis hederacea var. nil, commonly known as morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.

The seeds are edible when fully mature but can be toxic in their raw form.
