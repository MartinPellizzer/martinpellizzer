Polygala glochidiata var. raddiana

Polygala glochidiata var. raddiana, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant produces tiny, spiny fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
