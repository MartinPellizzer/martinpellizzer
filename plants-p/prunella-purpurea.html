Prunella purpurea

Prunella purpurea, commonly known as self-heal, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Prunella purpurea is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
