Phrygilanthus suaveolens

Phrygilanthus suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented phrygilanthus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, often displaying shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are simple and opposite, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
