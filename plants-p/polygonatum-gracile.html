Polygonatum gracile

Polygonatum gracile, commonly known as the slender Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features long, arching stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces clusters of greenish-white, bell-shaped flowers in late spring, followed by red berries in the summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Polygonatum gracile is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
