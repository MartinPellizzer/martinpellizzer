Pinanga ridleyana

Pinanga ridleyana, commonly known as the Ridley's bamboo palm, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, clustered stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, white inflorescences that bear yellowish flowers, which are followed by triangular, dark blue-black berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a houseplant in many parts of the world.
