Polystachya pisobulbon

Polystachya pisobulbon, commonly known as the pineapple orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces a dense cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is noted for its ability to form a flowering spike that bears multiple small, fragrant flowers. It thrives in humid, well-drained environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Polystachya pisobulbon is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
