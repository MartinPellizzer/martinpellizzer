Pothos acaulis

Pothos acaulis, commonly known as the silver vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically variegated with silver and green patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, followed by orange-red berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pothos acaulis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
