Pimelea longiflora var. latiflora

Pimelea longiflora var. latiflora, commonly known as the broad-flowered waratah, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its showy, red or pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

The variety latiflora is distinguished by its broader petals compared to the typical form of Pimelea longiflora.
