Poa platyantha

Poa platyantha, commonly known as the New Zealand hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks. It has a clump-forming habit and is valued for its ornamental appearance.

Poa platyantha plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native invertebrates.
