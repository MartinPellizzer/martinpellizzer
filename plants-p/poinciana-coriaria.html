Poinciana coriaria

Poinciana coriaria, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves that are pinnately arranged, with small, ovate leaflets. Its flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Poinciana coriaria is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a street tree in warm climates.
