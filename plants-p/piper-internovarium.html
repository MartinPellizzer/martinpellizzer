Piper internovarium

Piper internovarium, commonly known as the New Guinea pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to the rainforests of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elliptical, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
