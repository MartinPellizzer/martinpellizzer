Palicourea drewiana

Palicourea drewiana, commonly known as blue hibiscus.

It is a flowering plant native to Central and South America, particularly found in the understory of tropical rainforests. The plant features striking blue-violet flowers that are highly attractive to pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy surface and a mild, aromatic scent.

Palicourea drewiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display and compact growth habit.
