Poivrea grandidieri var. glabrescens

Poivrea grandidieri var. glabrescens, commonly known as the Madagascar glorybower, is a unique variant of the native Madagascar shrub.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its striking, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth, glabrous surface, giving it its varietal name. This plant thrives in well-drained, sunny conditions and is adapted to the arid environments of its native habitat.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
