Panicum pedicellare

Panicum pedicellare, commonly known as the clustered panicgrass, is a species of grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow leaves and produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and the ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
