Philbornea magnifolia

Philbornea magnifolia, commonly known as the grand magnolia, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stem. Philbornea magnifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
