Petunia guarapuavensis

Petunia guarapuavensis, commonly known as the Brazilian petunia, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors including purple, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and ecological studies.
