Psidium molle

Psidium molle, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that give way to edible, red, cherry-like fruits. Its fruit is rich in vitamin C and is often used in jams, juices, and traditional medicines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
