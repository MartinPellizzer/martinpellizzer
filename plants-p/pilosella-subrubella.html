Pilosella subrubella

Pilosella subrubella, commonly known as the red-stemmed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are hairy and often have a reddish or purplish hue, especially along the veins.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
