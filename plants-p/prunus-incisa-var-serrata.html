Prunus incisa var. serrata

Prunus incisa var. serrata, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
