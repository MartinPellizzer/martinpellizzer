Potentilla Inclinata Subsp. Subrecta

Potentilla inclinata subsp. subrecta is a lesser-known member of the rose family known for its unique growth habits and adaptability. This subspecies thrives in harsh environments, often found in rocky soils and alpine regions across the northern hemisphere. Its narrow, upright stems and small, clustered flowers make it a striking yet subtle plant in its natural habitat. Despite its resilience, it remains underappreciated in horticultural circles. Understanding this plant offers insight into the ecological diversity of the Potentilla genus.
