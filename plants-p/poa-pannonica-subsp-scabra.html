Poa pannonica subsp. scabra

Poa pannonica subsp.

scabra, commonly known as the rough meadowgrass, is a grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and dense, tufted growth habit.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, riverbanks, and other wetland habitats. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
