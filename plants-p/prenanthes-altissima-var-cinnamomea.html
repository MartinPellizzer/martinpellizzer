Prenanthes altissima var. cinnamomea

Prenanthes altissima var. cinnamomea, commonly known as cinnamonleaf arrowhead, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a reddish or cinnamon-colored hue, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are arranged in a spicate inflorescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
