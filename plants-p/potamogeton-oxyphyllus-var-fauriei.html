Potamogeton oxyphyllus var. fauriei

Potamogeton oxyphyllus var. fauriei, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in slow-moving to still waters, such as lakes, ponds, and marshes, and plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its morphology is adapted to underwater life, with gas-filled structures that help it float and access sunlight for photosynthesis.
