Pteridium aquilinum var. osmundaceum

Pteridium aquilinum var. osmundaceum, commonly known as the bracken fern, is a species of fern native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its large, feather-like fronds that can reach lengths of up to two meters. The plant thrives in a variety of habitats, including forests, meadows, and disturbed soils, and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

The plant is also known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas, making it an important component of many ecosystems.
