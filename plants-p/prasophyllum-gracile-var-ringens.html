Prasophyllum gracile var. ringens

Prasophyllum gracile var. ringens, commonly known as the slender leek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in open heathlands and grassy areas, often in sandy or loamy soils. The plant produces a single, slender stem that bears a spike of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, contributing to its overall herbaceous appearance.

It is a flowering plant in the family Orchidaceae, known for its delicate floral structure and ecological significance in its native habitat.
