Persicaria nipponica

Persicaria nipponica, commonly known as Japanese knotweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is known for its rapid growth and invasive potential in some regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
