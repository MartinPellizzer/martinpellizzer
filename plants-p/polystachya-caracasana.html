Polystachya caracasana

Polystachya caracasana, commonly known as the Caracas orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing moisture from the soil.

Polystachya caracasana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
