Pycnanthemum virginianum var. verticillatum

Pycnanthemum virginianum var. verticillatum, commonly known as mountain betony, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter tall. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped, opposite, and have a slightly rough texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.
