Prostanthera behriana

Prostanthera behriana, commonly known as the slender lemon myrtle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are fragrant and contain high levels of citral, a compound responsible for its lemony scent.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in essential oil production.
