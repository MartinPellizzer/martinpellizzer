Passiflora venosa

Passiflora venosa, commonly known as the velvet passionflower, is a striking climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green foliage and intricate, showy flowers that display a mix of blue, purple, and white hues. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its unique floral structure includes a crown of filaments and a central column, which are features typical of the Passifloraceae family.

Passiflora venosa is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported calming and sedative properties.
