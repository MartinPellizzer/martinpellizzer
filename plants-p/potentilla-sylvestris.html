Potentilla sylvestris

Potentilla sylvestris, commonly known as the wild cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, usually from June to July. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, which give it the common name "cinquefoil.".

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and rocky areas.
