Phleum exaratum

Phleum exaratum, commonly known as rough fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often have a rough texture. The plant produces spikelets that are typically clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, with each spikelet containing two to three small, awned flowers. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Phleum exaratum is often found in dry, rocky, or disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
