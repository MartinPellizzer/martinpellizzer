Polygonum barbatum var. glabratum

Polygonum barbatum var. glabratum, commonly known as the smooth knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its erect, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves.

It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to red in color, blooming in late summer. The plant is native to regions of Asia, including China and Japan, and thrives in moist, sunny environments. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and disturbed soils.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively make it an invasive species in some areas.
