Paramyrciaria glomerata

Paramyrciaria glomerata, commonly known as the clustered mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
