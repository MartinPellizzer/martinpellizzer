Priva tuberosa

Priva tuberosa, commonly known as the tuberous root plant, is a perennial species characterized by its swollen underground stems, or tubers, which store nutrients.

It typically features erect, herbaceous stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white, depending on the variety. Its tubers are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

Priva tuberosa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for various climates.
