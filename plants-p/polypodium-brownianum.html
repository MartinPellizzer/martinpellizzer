Polypodium brownianum

Polypodium brownianum, commonly known as the Brown's water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically green with a subtle metallic sheen. This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The sori, which are clusters of spore-producing structures, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Polypodium brownianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in maintaining ecological balance in its native habitats.
