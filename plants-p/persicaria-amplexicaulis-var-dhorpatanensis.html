Persicaria amplexicaulis var. dhorpatanensis

Persicaria amplexicaulis var. dhorpatanensis, commonly known as the Dhorpatan waterplantain, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that often clasp the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in summer. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, preferring partial to full shade.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in wetland ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
