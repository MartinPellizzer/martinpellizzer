Podophyllum sikkimense var. majus

Podophyllum sikkimense var. majus, commonly known as the Sikkim giantpod, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of India.

It belongs to the family Podophyllaceae and is characterized by its large, fleshy, and often brightly colored leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by distinctive berries that contain toxic seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
