Phyllanthus retinervis

Phyllanthus retinervis, commonly known as the Indian false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple leaves with prominently visible veins, giving it its scientific name "retinervis," meaning "with visible veins.". The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by capsule fruits containing minute seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Phyllanthus retinervis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
