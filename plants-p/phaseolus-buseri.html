Phaseolus buseri

Phaseolus buseri, commonly known as the Buser bean, is a species of flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are edible when mature. It is adapted to grow in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential agricultural use, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
