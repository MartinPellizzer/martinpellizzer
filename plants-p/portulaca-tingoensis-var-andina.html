Portulaca tingoensis var. andina

Portulaca tingoensis var. andina, commonly known as the Andean moss rose, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by fleshy, succulent leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers. Its compact growth habit and long blooming period make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ability to withstand drought conditions, which is a common adaptation in its native arid environments.
