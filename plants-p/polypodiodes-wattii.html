Polypodiodes wattii

Polypodiodes wattii, commonly known as the Watt's polypody, is a small fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Polypodiodes wattii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
