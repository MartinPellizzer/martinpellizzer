Piper nobile var. parvulum

Piper nobile var. parvulum, commonly known as the small-leaf noble pepper, is a variant of the noble pepper plant native to tropical regions.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its small, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces tiny, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it distinct from other Piper species, particularly in leaf size and fruit morphology.
