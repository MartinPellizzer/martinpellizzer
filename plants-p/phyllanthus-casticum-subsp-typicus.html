Phyllanthus casticum subsp. typicus

Phyllanthus casticum subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, including India and Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
