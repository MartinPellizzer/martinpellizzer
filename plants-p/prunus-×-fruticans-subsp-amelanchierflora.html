Prunus × fruticans subsp. amelanchierflora

Prunus × fruticans subsp.

amelanchierflora, commonly known as the common serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America. It is characterized by its oval to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green and turn yellow or red in the fall.

The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible berries that range in color from yellow to dark purple. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and wildlife habitat benefits.
