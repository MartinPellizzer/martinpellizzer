Polemonium delicatum subsp. typicum

Polemonium delicatum subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the showy bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America. It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its stems are upright and often branched, supporting the clusters of bell-shaped flowers.

The species is valued in native gardens for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
