Prosthechea boothiana var. erythronioides

Prosthechea boothiana var. erythronioides, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The flowers are usually pink or red with yellow markings, giving it a striking appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
