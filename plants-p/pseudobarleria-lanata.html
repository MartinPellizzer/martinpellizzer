Pseudobarleria lanata

Pseudobarleria lanata, commonly known as the woolly false barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Pseudobarleria lanata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
