Pachysanthus macrophyllus

Pachysanthus macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leaf sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by woody pods containing flat, dark seeds. Its bark is thick and rugged, often with a reddish-brown color.

The plant is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
