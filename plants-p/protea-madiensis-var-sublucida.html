Protea madiensis var. sublucida

Protea madiensis var. sublucida, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant characterized by its large, showy inflorescences.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often ornamental flowering plants. The plant features a woody, shrub-like structure with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. Its flowers are typically pink to reddish in color, with a distinctive, rounded shape and numerous small, petal-like structures.

This species is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces of South Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
