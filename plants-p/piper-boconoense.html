Piper boconoense

Piper boconoense, commonly known as the Bocono pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
