Pleurothallis veliformis

Pleurothallis veliformis, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a range of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and strap-like, and it produces pseudobulbs that help store water and nutrients.

Pleurothallis veliformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
