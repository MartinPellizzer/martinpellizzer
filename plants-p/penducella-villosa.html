Penducella villosa

Penducella villosa, commonly known as the hairy pendunculate, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, pendulous stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are usually ovate or lanceolate with a hairy texture, giving it its common name.

Penducella villosa is often found in regions with temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
