Polygonum fibrilliferum

Polygonum fibrilliferum, commonly known as the fibrous knotweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive bract structures. The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Polygonum fibrilliferum is often found in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and woodland areas.
