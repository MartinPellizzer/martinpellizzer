Papaver olympicum

Papaver olympicum, commonly known as the Olympic poppy, is a striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, with a prominent black stigma. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Papaver olympicum is a symbol of the Olympic Games and is also valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens.
