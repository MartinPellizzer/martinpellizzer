Pentadenia inconspicua

Pentadenia inconspicua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and distinctive red or orange bottlebrush-like flowers that appear in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its unique floral structure attracts nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to local biodiversity.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
