Pandanus motleyanus

Pandanus motleyanus, commonly known as the Motley's pandanus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered at the base of the leaves. Its fibrous leaves are valued for their durability and are used in weaving and thatching.

Pandanus motleyanus thrives in humid, coastal environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
