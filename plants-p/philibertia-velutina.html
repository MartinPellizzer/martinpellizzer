Philibertia velutina

Philibertia velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf filbert, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several angular, edible seeds.

Philibertia velutina is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
