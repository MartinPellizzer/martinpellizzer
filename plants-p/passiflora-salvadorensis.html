Passiflora salvadorensis

Passiflora salvadorensis, commonly known as the Salvadoran passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a blend of violet, yellow, and white petals, along with prominent reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a unique, slightly tart flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs.

This species is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in local ecosystems.
