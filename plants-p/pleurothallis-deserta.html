Pleurothallis deserta

Pleurothallis deserta, commonly known as the desert pleurothallis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

This epiphytic orchid is adapted to grow on trees in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky or mountainous areas. It has small, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss, and its flowers are typically white or pale green, with a distinctive spurred lip. The plant produces a single, slender stem that bears a few flowers, which are often fragrant.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered rare and is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
