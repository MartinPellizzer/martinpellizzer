Piper pseudomatico

Piper pseudomatico, commonly known as the Java pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into oval, red berries. These berries are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
