Prunus bigarella var. juliana

Prunus bigarella var. juliana, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Japanese flowering cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its showy, single or double pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is a popular choice in ornamental horticulture.
