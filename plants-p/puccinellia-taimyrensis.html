Puccinellia taimyrensis

Puccinellia taimyrensis, commonly known as the Taimyren grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, particularly in Central Asia. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. It has adapted to thrive in saline and alkaline soils, making it an important component of steppe and desert ecosystems.

Due to its ecological significance, it is often studied for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
