Philotheca eremicola

Philotheca eremicola, commonly known as the desert philotheca, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It is well adapted to poor, sandy soils and can withstand prolonged drought conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to habitat disturbance, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
