Potosia zamororae

Potosia zamororae, commonly known as the Zamorora's poinsettia, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking red bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub with dark green, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural displays.
