Paepalanthus undulatus

Paepalanthus undulatus, commonly known as the undulating puya, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that bears numerous showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a water-holding cavity, a common feature among bromeliads. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and high humidity.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
