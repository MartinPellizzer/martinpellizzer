Passiflora tenerifensis

Passiflora tenerifensis, commonly known as the Canary Island passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

Passiflora tenerifensis thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and forested regions of its native habitat.
