Poinsettia pedunculata

Poinsettia pedunculata, commonly known as the Christmas star, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in warm, humid environments. Its foliage is typically dark green, and it produces clusters of yellowish flowers in the center of the bracts.

Poinsettia pedunculata is widely cultivated for its festive appearance, especially during the holiday season.
