Picea brachyphylla

Picea brachyphylla, commonly known as the dwarf spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and short, stiff needles that are typically blue-green in color. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cooler climates and is often found in subalpine and alpine environments.

Picea brachyphylla plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
