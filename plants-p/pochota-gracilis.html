Pochota gracilis

Pochota gracilis, commonly known as the slender pochote, is a leguminous plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Pochota gracilis is adapted to grow in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in dry forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
