Primula hubeiensis

Primula hubeiensis, commonly known as the Hubei primrose, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces showy inflorescences in spring.

Primula hubeiensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
