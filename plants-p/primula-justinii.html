Primula justinii

Primula justinii, commonly known as the Justin's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces upright flowering stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
