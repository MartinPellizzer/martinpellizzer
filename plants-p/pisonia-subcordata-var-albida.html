Pisonia subcordata var. albida

Pisonia subcordata var. albida, commonly known as the white pisonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its showy, white, clustered flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are often hairy and have a waxy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in coastal gardens.

Its fragrant flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.
