Phylica polifolia

Phylica polifolia, commonly known as the Natal glorybower, is a flowering shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Phylica polifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
