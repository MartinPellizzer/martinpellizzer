Panicum phyllomacrum

Panicum phyllomacrum, commonly known as the golden panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and bright yellow to golden-green foliage, which becomes more pronounced during the summer months. The plant produces fluffy, seed-bearing spikes that are often used in ornamental gardening due to their aesthetic appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping and low-maintenance landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ecological and decorative plantings.
