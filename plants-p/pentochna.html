Pentochna

Pentochna, commonly known as the mountain helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and showy, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forests and rocky areas. Pentochna has a rhizomatous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain its seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
