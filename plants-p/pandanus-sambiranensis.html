Pandanus sambiranensis

Pandanus sambiranensis, commonly known as the Sambiranensis pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear fragrant, yellowish flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for thatching due to their fibrous texture.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in coastal and wetland habitats.
