Pilea integrifolia

Pilea integrifolia, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are round and slightly wavy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular houseplant.

Pilea integrifolia is noted for its ease of care and ability to purify indoor air.
