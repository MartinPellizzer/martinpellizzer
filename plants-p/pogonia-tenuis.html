Pogonia tenuis

Pogonia tenuis, commonly known as the slender bog orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the eastern United States.

It thrives in wet, acidic habitats such as bogs and swamps, where it forms dense, low-growing colonies. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces striking, pale pink to white flowers with intricate patterns. Its reproductive strategy relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, highlighting its ecological interdependence.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, Pogonia tenuis is considered endangered in many regions.
