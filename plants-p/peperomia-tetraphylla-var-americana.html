Peperomia tetraphylla var. americana

Peperomia tetraphylla var. americana, commonly known as the American pepper plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features thick, waxy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish or purplish underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements make it an attractive addition to both indoor and outdoor spaces.
