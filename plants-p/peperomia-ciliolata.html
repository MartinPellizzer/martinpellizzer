Peperomia ciliolata

Peperomia ciliolata, commonly known as the velvet leaf peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia ciliolata is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
