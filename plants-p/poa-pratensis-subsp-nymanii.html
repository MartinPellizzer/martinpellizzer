Poa pratensis subsp. nymanii

Poa pratensis subsp.

nymanii, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a subspecies native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows and grasslands. It produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences that are often clustered at the base of the plant.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
