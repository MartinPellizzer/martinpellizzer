Pseudalbizzia decandra

Pseudalbizzia decandra, commonly known as the African wisteria, is a leguminous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, arranged in drooping clusters. Its root system has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and forage.
