Phoebe chekiangensis

Phoebe chekiangensis, commonly known as the Chinese phoebe, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
