Portulaca lanceolata var. minor

Portulaca lanceolata var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf portulaca, is a low-growing annual plant native to arid regions of North America.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or containers.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it an attractive ornamental plant in xeriscaped landscapes.
