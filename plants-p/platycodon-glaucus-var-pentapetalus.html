Platycodon glaucus var. pentapetalus

Platycodon glaucus var. pentapetalus, commonly known as the five-petaled bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue and a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
