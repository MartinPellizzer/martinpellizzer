Ptilepida acaulis

Ptilepida acaulis, commonly known as the naked-stemmed orchid, is a unique plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves, with photosynthesis occurring through its green, fleshy stems. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its specialized habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
