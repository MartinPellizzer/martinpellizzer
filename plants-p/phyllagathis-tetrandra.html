Phyllagathis tetrandra

Phyllagathis tetrandra, commonly known as the four-stamen phyllagathis, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Phyllagathis tetrandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its compact growth habit.
