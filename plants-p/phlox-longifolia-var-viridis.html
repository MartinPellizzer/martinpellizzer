Phlox longifolia var. viridis

Phlox longifolia var. viridis, commonly known as greenleaf phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features upright stems covered in opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its foliage and floral display contribute to its ornamental value in both garden and natural settings.
