Phaseolus robiniiflorus

Phaseolus robiniiflorus, commonly known as the golden bean or golden phaseolus, is a species of legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Phaseolus genus. The plant produces small, round, yellow to orange pods that contain edible seeds, often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm climates and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of legume botany and crop diversity.
