Parodia applanata

Parodia applanata, commonly known as the flat-topped cactus, is a species native to the deserts of Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white to pale green spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne on the upper parts of the stems. Parodia applanata is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
