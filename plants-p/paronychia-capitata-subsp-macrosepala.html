Paronychia capitata subsp. macrosepala

Paronychia capitata subsp.

macrosepala, commonly known as the giant sandbur, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces distinctive burrs with hooked bracts that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open grasslands and coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
