Plectranthus monachorum

Plectranthus monachorum, commonly known as the Monastic Plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for both garden beds and container planting.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice among gardeners.
