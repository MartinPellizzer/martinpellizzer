Potentilla recta f. kochii

Potentilla recta f. kochii, commonly known as the Koch's potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, upright stems and dense clusters of yellow flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and elliptical, with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
