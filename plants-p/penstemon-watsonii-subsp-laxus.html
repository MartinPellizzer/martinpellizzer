Penstemon watsonii subsp. laxus

Penstemon watsonii subsp.

laxus, commonly known as the lax flower penstemon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its drought tolerance and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
