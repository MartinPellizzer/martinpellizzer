Pogostemon nilagiricus

Pogostemon nilagiricus, commonly known as the Nilagiri pogostemon, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Western Ghats in India.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or violet in color and bloom during the summer months.

Pogostemon nilagiricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
