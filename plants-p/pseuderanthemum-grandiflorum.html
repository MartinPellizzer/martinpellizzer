Pseuderanthemum grandiflorum

Pseuderanthemum grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered false anthemum, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and bees, making it an important species in ecological systems.
