Pinus uncinata var. pendula

Pinus uncinata var. pendula, commonly known as the pendulous pine, is a rare and distinctive variant of the Spanish pine.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a weeping appearance, which sets it apart from typical pine species. The plant features needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, providing a striking visual contrast. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is considered a botanical curiosity and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
