Plectranthus griffithii

Plectranthus griffithii, commonly known as the Cape verbena, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive blue-green color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of violet or blue, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and rockeries.

Plectranthus griffithii is also valued for its aromatic foliage, which can be used in herbal teas or as a culinary herb.
