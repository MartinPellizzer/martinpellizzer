Pyrus nitidus

Pyrus nitidus, commonly known as the glossy pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, a small, edible pear, is typically yellow or green when ripe.

The glossy pear is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for both horticultural and medicinal purposes.
