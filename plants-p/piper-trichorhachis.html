Piper trichorhachis

Piper trichorhachis, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, opposite leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
