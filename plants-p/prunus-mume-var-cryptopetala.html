Prunus mume var. cryptopetala

Prunus mume var. cryptopetala, commonly known as the Chinese flowering cherry or Japanese cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that turn reddish in autumn, adding to its ornamental value. Its fruits are small, yellow stone fruits that ripen in summer, though they are not typically consumed by humans.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
