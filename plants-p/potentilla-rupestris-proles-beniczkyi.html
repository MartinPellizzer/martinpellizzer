Potentilla rupestris proles beniczkyi

Potentilla rupestris proles beniczkyi, commonly known as the Beniczky's stonecrop, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and arid environments.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh conditions. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of yellow or white, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
