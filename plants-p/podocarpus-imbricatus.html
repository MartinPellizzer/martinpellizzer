Podocarpus imbricatus

Podocarpus imbricatus, commonly known as the Chinese podocarp or Chinese umbrella tree, is a flowering plant in the family Podocarpaceae.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern China, Taiwan, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its cones are woody and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Podocarpus imbricatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
