Phreatia iridifolia

Phreatia iridifolia, commonly known as the blue orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and decaying organic matter.

Phreatia iridifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
