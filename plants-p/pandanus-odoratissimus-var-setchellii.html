Pandanus odoratissimus var. setchellii

Pandanus odoratissimus var. setchellii, commonly known as the sweet-scented pandanus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in terminal clusters, adding ornamental value. It thrives in coastal and wetland areas, preferring well-drained sandy soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
