Polygonum ensigerum

Polygonum ensigerum, commonly known as the redroot filaree, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems that often turn reddish with age. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Polygonum ensigerum thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in wet meadows.
