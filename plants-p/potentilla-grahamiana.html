Potentilla grahamiana

Potentilla grahamiana, commonly known as Graham's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in inflorescences at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

Potentilla grahamiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
