Pteris semidentata

Pteris semidentata, commonly known as the serrated water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, bi- or tri-pinnate fronds that are typically green with a slightly serrated margin. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. The fronds are often used in ornamental gardening due to their attractive appearance.

It reproduces via spores and is commonly found in wetlands, stream banks, and forest floors.
