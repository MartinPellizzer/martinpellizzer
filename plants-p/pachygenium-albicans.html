Pachygenium albicans

Pachygenium albicans, commonly known as the white-stemmed bladdergrass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are pollinated by insects. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.
