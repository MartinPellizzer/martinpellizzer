Panicum polystachyum

Panicum polystachyum, commonly known as the purpletop grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense clusters of purple to pinkish flower spikes that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands make it an effective erosion control plant.

The botanical features of Panicum polystachyum make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
