Potentilla × bishkekensis

Potentilla × bishkekensis, commonly known as the Bishkek cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage of dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
