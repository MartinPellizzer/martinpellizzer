Potamogeton gramineus f. terrestris

Potamogeton gramineus f. terrestris, commonly known as the terrestrial pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant that thrives in freshwater environments.

It has long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellowish in color. It is often found in shallow waters, such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams.

Its presence can indicate good water quality and is an important part of aquatic ecosystems.
