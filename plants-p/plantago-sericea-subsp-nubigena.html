Plantago sericea subsp. nubigena

Plantago sericea subsp.

nubigena, commonly known as the cloudland plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its silver-gray, silky-hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
