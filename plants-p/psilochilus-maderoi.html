Psilochilus maderoi

Psilochilus maderoi, commonly known as the Mader's psilotum, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments, where it absorbs moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its native habitat. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and it is noted for its delicate, fern-like appearance.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, Psilochilus maderoi is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
