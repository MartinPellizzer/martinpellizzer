Pilosella pachypilon subsp. multiflocca

Pilosella pachypilon subsp.

multiflocca, commonly known as the hairy leaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy appearance.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually hairy, giving it a distinctive texture, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

This subspecies is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in cooler, temperate climates.
