Phyllanthus ramiflorus var. macrophyllus

Phyllanthus ramiflorus var. macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leaf phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves that can reach up to 20 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
