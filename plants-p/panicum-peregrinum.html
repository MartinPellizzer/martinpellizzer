Panicum peregrinum

Panicum peregrinum, commonly known as wandering grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains, which are typically wind-pollinated. Panicum peregrinum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and disturbed areas.

Its adaptability and rapid growth make it an important species in ecological studies and restoration projects.
