Pritchardia kaalae

Pritchardia kaalae, commonly known as the Kona cycad, is a rare cycad species native to the Hawaiian island of Hawaii.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by insects, and it relies on specific bird species for seed dispersal. Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Pritchardia kaalae is critically endangered, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
