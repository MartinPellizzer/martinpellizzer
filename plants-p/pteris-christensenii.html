Pteris christensenii

Pteris christensenii, commonly known as the Christensen's water fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are typically light green in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing near streams or in humid forests. Its rhizomes are creeping and help in the spread of the plant, while its spore-producing structures are located on the underside of the fronds.

Pteris christensenii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a decorative foliage plant.
