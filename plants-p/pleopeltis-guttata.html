Pleopeltis guttata

Pleopeltis guttata, commonly known as the spotted hometree, is a fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, dark green fronds with distinctive yellow or brown spots. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with lanceolate pinnae that are often curled when young.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to tolerate indoor conditions.
