Polygonum bellardii var. densa

Polygonum bellardii var. densa, commonly known as the dense polygonum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features upright stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a distinctive reddish or purple hue on the underside.

The small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red and bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
