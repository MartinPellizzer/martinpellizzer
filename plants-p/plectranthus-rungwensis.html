Plectranthus rungwensis

Plectranthus rungwensis, commonly known as the African violet, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically oval to heart-shaped. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Plectranthus rungwensis is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
