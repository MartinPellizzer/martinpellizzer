Philadelphus floribundus

Philadelphus floribundus, commonly known as the common mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Philadelphus floribundus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
