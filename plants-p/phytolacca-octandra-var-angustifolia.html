Phytolacca octandra var. angustifolia

Phytolacca octandra var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf Indian inkberry, is a variant of the Phytolacca genus native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, fleshy, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
