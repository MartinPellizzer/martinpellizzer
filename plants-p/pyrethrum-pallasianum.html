Pyrethrum pallasianum

Pyrethrum pallasianum, commonly known as the Pallas's pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant produces fine, hair-like structures called phytolacca, which are responsible for its insecticidal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its natural pest-repelling qualities, it has been historically used in organic pest control.
