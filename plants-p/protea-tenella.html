Protea tenella

Protea tenella, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pale pink flowers arranged in a spherical or cylindrical shape. The plant features a sturdy, woody stem with broad, leathery leaves that are often covered in a silvery or whitish powdery coating. Native to the Western Cape region, it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun exposure.

Protea tenella is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also an important species in local ecosystems.
