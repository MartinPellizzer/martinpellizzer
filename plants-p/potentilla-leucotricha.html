Potentilla leucotricha

Potentilla leucotricha, commonly known as white-haired cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
