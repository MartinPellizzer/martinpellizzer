Pilosocereus coerulescens

Pilosocereus coerulescens, commonly known as the blue cactus or blue moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of blue-gray spines. The plant produces pale yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, adding a striking contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its unique coloration and nocturnal blooming make it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
