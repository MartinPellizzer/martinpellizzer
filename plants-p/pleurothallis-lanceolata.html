Pleurothallis lanceolata

Pleurothallis lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized orchid with slender, often hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil.

Due to its delicate appearance and unique flowering pattern, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
