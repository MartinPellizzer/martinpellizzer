Paspalum haumanii var. macranthum

Paspalum haumanii var. macranthum, commonly known as the large-flowered paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces dense clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers, which are typically arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in riparian areas.
