Puccinellia dolicholepis subsp. aksaica

Puccinellia dolicholepis subsp.

aksaica, commonly known as the Aksaica glassworts, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline and arid environments. It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its succulent, often prostrate growth habit.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, and it has narrow, linear leaves that help reduce water loss. It is native to Central Asia and is often found in coastal and inland salt marshes.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in saline habitats.
