Philadelphus rubricaulis

Philadelphus rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown stems that become more prominent with age. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a serrated margin.

Philadelphus rubricaulis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
