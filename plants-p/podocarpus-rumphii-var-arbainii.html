Podocarpus rumphii var. arbainii

Podocarpus rumphii var. arbainii, commonly known as the Java pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and small, cone-like structures. The plant is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types but preferring well-drained conditions. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for ornamental purposes, and it plays a role in local ecosystems as a habitat for various wildlife species.

The tree is also valued for its timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
