Pausandra flagellorhachis

Pausandra flagellorhachis, commonly known as the whipsnap fern, is a striking member of the fern family with a unique and intricate frond structure.

Its fronds are characterized by their long, slender segments that resemble whips, hence the common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas. It produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds.

Pausandra flagellorhachis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, feathery appearance.
