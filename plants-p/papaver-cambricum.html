Papaver cambricum

Papaver cambricum, commonly known as the Welsh poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the British Isles.

It produces striking orange-red flowers with a prominent central stigma, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves that are ovate and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges and grassy slopes.

The Welsh poppy is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
