Pleurothallis guimaraensii

Pleurothallis guimaraensii, commonly known as the Guimaraensii orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
