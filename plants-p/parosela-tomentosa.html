Parosela tomentosa

Parosela tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly parosela, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to the mild, maritime climate of its native habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
