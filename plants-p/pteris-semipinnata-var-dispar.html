Pteris semipinnata var. dispar

Pteris semipinnata var. dispar, commonly known as the Variegated Brake, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It features fronds that are bipinnate, with a distinctive variegation pattern of green and silver or white. The plant has a creeping rhizome that allows it to spread and form dense mats. Its leaves are typically lanceolate and have a leathery texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
