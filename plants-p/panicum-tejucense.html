Panicum tejucense

Panicum tejucense, commonly known as the Mexican dropseed, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that give it a distinctive appearance, especially in late summer and early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.

Panicum tejucense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting native pollinators and wildlife.
