Polygonum viridiflorum

Polygonum viridiflorum, commonly known as the green-flowered knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features upright stems that are often reddish or purple in color, covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

Polygonum viridiflorum is often found in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.
