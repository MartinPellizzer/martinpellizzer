Protea helvola

Protea helvola, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, white, spherical inflorescence that resembles a cluster of small flowers, giving it a unique and elegant appearance. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. Its flowers are pollinated by birds, particularly the sugarbird, which is attracted to its nectar.

Protea helvola is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
