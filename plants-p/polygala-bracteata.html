Polygala bracteata

Polygala bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygalaceae family.

It is native to regions of Asia, including China, India, and Nepal, and typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features slender, erect stems with small, clustered leaves and produces delicate, yellowish or greenish flowers. Its fruit is a follicle that contains several seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in arid environments.
