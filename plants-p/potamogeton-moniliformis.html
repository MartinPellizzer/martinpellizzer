Potamogeton moniliformis

Potamogeton moniliformis, commonly known as the thread-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has long, slender stems that are often branching and bear small, linear leaves arranged in whorls. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically male and female on separate plants. It is often found in shallow, slow-moving freshwater habitats such as lakes, ponds, and streams.

Potamogeton moniliformis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
