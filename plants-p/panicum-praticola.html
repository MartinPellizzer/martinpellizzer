Panicum praticola

Panicum praticola, commonly known as yellow panicgrass, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically yellow-green in color. The plant produces small, fluffy panicles of seeds that are often wind-dispersed. It thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for sandy or well-drained soils.

Panicum praticola is often used in native plant gardens and for erosion control due to its hardy nature.
