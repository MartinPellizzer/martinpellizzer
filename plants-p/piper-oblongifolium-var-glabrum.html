Piper oblongifolium var. glabrum

Piper oblongifolium var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its broad, smooth leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into peppery berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic foliage and edible fruits.

The variety 'glabrum' is distinguished by its lack of pubescence on the leaves, giving it a sleek appearance.
