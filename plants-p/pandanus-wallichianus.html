Pandanus wallichianus

Pandanus wallichianus, commonly known as the broadleaf pandanus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into three-winged fruits. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often growing in dense clusters.

Its adaptability to saline and waterlogged soils makes it an important species in tropical ecosystems.
