Perezia oleracea

Perezia oleracea, commonly known as the edible false dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are edible when young. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both ecological and culinary purposes.
