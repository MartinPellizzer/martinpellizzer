Picea abies var. veitchii

Picea abies var. veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's Norway spruce, is a variant of the Norway spruce native to Europe.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its dense, conical shape and needle-like leaves. The needles are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated edge, while the cones are cylindrical and woody. This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardiness.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a range of garden environments.
