Parnassia grandifolia

Parnassia grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered parnassia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its showy, white, cup-shaped flowers that resemble a bowl. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a distinctive veining pattern.

Parnassia grandifolia is also known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, making it an indicator species for certain ecological conditions.
