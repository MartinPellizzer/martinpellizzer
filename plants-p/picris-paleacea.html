Picris paleacea

Picris paleacea, commonly known as common goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and roadsides, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive yellow-orange central disk.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
