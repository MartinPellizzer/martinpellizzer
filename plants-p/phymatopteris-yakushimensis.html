Phymatopteris yakushimensis

Phymatopteris yakushimensis, commonly known as the Yakushimen huperzia, is a species of clubmoss native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and compact growth habit. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in two ranks, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
