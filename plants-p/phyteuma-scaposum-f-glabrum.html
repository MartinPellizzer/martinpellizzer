Phyteuma scaposum f. glabrum

Phyteuma scaposum f. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed bellflower, is a cultivar of the bellflower genus native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its glabrous, or hairless, stems and clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to lavender flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ease of cultivation.

Its foliage is lance-shaped and often persists through the winter, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.
