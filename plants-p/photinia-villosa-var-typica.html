Photinia villosa var. typica

Photinia villosa var. typica, commonly known as the roughleaf photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety, grayish-brown bark and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a rough, hairy texture on the upper surface. It produces small, red berries that are attractive to birds.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
