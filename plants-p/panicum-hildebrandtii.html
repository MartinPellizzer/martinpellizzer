Panicum hildebrandtii

Panicum hildebrandtii, commonly known as Hildebrandt's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, panicle-shaped seed heads that are often pink or reddish in color. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and heat resistance.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical research.
