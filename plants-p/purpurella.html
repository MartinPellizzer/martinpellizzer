Purpurella

Purpurella, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its striking purple or violet-colored flowers, which often have intricate patterns and a sweet fragrance. The plants typically grow as epiphytes or lithophytes, meaning they attach to other plants or rocks for support rather than growing in soil. They are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in areas with high humidity and warm climates.

Due to their ornamental value, some species of Purpurella are cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
