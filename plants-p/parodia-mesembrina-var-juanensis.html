Parodia mesembrina var. juanensis

Parodia mesembrina var. juanensis, commonly known as Juan's moon cactus, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to southern Africa.

It features a swollen, rounded stem that often develops a distinctive red or pink coloration, especially when exposed to sunlight. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation due to its hardiness and unique appearance.
