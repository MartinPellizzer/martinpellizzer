Phyllanthus javanicus

Phyllanthus javanicus, commonly known as the Java star, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring dense, evergreen foliage with small, leathery leaves. The plant produces small white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded areas within forests.

Phyllanthus javanicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
