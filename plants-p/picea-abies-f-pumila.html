Picea abies f. pumila

Picea abies f. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf Norway spruce, is a compact, low-growing form of the Norway spruce.

It features dense, conical foliage that is typically shorter and more rounded than the typical species. The plant has needle-like leaves that are dark green and often covered with a fine white bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is well adapted to harsh environments, making it a hardy choice for rocky or poor soils.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
