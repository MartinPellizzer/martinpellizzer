Pennisetum orientale

Pennisetum orientale, commonly known as fountain grass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted, fountain-like growth habit and slender, arching blades that often turn reddish or purple in color during the autumn. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pink to purple in hue, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Pennisetum orientale is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as birds and butterflies.
