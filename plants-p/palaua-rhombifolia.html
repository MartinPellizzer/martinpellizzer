Palaua rhombifolia

Palaua rhombifolia, commonly known as the diamondleaf or rhombifoliate, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its rhombic-shaped leaves, which are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

Palaua rhombifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some island cultures.
