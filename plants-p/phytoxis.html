Phytoxis

Phytoxis, commonly known as the poison ivy plant, is a toxic species native to North America.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant contains urushiol, a potent irritant that causes contact dermatitis in many people. Its growth habit includes both upright and creeping forms, allowing it to thrive in various environments.

Phytoxis is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and along trails, making it a common allergen for outdoor enthusiasts.
