Phymaspermum equisetoides

Phymaspermum equisetoides, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a primitive vascular plant belonging to the order Microspermales.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are typically clustered at the tips of its branches. The plant exhibits a unique life cycle involving both a gametophytic and sporophytic phase. Its leaves are usually small, scale-like, and arranged in whorls around the stem.

Phymaspermum equisetoides is often found in moist, shaded environments and plays a role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in its ecosystem.
