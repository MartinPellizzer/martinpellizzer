Platycodon grandiflorus f. bicolor

Platycodon grandiflorus f. bicolor, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower or two-colored bellflower, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant features a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a bluish-green hue. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are typically blue or purple with a white throat, showcasing a striking two-color pattern. Native to East Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
