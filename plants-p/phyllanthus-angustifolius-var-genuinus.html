Phyllanthus angustifolius var. genuinus

Phyllanthus angustifolius var. genuinus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along its stems. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, spherical fruits. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
