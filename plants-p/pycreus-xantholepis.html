Pycreus xantholepis

Pycreus xantholepis, commonly known as the yellow-leaved pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange inflorescences. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are surrounded by pale green to yellow ray florets. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Pycreus xantholepis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
