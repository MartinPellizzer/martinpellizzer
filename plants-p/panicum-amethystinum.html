Panicum amethystinum

Panicum amethystinum, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking purple to reddish-pink inflorescences that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is drought-tolerant and often found in sandy or well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and erosion control.
