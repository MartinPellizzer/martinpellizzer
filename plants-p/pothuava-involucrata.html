Pothuava involucrata

Pothuava involucrata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous areas. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a smooth texture.

Pothuava involucrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
