Passiflora curumbaensis

Passiflora curumbaensis, commonly known as the Curumba passionflower, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest of Brazil.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its showy, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and contain seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty.

Its botanical features make it a unique and attractive addition to tropical gardens.
