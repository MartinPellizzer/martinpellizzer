Patagonium latifolium

Patagonium latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf patagonia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
