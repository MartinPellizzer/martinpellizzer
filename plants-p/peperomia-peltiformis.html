Peperomia peltiformis

Peperomia peltiformis, commonly known as the broad-leaf peperomia, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, and often variegated foliage, which can range from deep green to shades of silver and pink. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red, clustered in dense spikes. It is a popular houseplant due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in indirect light.

Peperomia peltiformis prefers well-draining soil and moderate humidity, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
