Pyrus ringo trilobata

Pyrus ringo trilobata, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and alternate, pinnate leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by round, yellowish-green fruits. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and aromatic flavor, and is often used in traditional Japanese cuisine.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
