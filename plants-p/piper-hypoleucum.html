Piper hypoleucum

Piper hypoleucum, commonly known as the white-veined pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated, thin berries. Its leaves are typically ovate with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in certain cuisines.
