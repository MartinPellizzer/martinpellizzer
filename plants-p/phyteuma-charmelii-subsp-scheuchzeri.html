Phyteuma charmelii subsp. scheuchzeri

Phyteuma charmelii subsp.

scheuchzeri, commonly known as the Alpine phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous areas. It has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged in a basal rosette.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
