Prunus sphaerocarpa f. grandifolia

Prunus sphaerocarpa f. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered Japanese apricot, is a compact shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and its ability to produce showy, pink to white flowers in early spring. The foliage is typically dark green and glossy, providing an attractive backdrop for the blooms. The plant is relatively cold-hardy and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
