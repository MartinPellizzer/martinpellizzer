Paspalum pisinnum

Paspalum pisinnum, commonly known as the Pisinnum grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often reach heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

Paspalum pisinnum is often found in moist or wetland environments, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
