Papaver intricatum

Papaver intricatum, commonly known as the intricate poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, papery petals that range in color from pale pink to deep red. The plant produces a capsule-like fruit that contains numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
