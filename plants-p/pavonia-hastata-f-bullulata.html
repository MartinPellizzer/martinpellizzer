Pavonia hastata f. bullulata

Pavonia hastata f. bullulata, commonly known as the bullulata false mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red bract-like flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a velvety texture and a silvery-green underside. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
