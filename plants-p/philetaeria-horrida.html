Philetaeria horrida

Philetaeria horrida, commonly known as the spiked philetaeria, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescence and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
