Pittiera grandiflora

Pittiera grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered pittiera, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Pittiera grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in woodland gardens.
