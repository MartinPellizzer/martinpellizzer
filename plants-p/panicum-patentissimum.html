Panicum patentissimum

Panicum patentissimum, commonly known as the broadleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences that produce small, fluffy seeds. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.

Due to its adaptability, it is often found in disturbed areas and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
