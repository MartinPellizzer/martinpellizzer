Paphiopedilum × undulatum

Paphiopedilum × undulatum, commonly known as the wavy-lip orchid, is a hybrid species within the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its intricate, lip-shaped petals that often display a wavy or undulating pattern, giving it its common name. The plant is native to Southeast Asia, particularly regions of Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. It typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial plant, with broad, leathery leaves that help retain moisture.

Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white with intricate markings, making it a prized ornamental plant among orchid enthusiasts.
