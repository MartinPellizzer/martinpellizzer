Pandanus gossweileri

Pandanus gossweileri, commonly known as the African screw pine, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves that form a dense, umbrella-like canopy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is well adapted to coastal and sandy environments, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
