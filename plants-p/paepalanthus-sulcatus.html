Paepalanthus sulcatus

Paepalanthus sulcatus, commonly known as the zigzag cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, cylindrical stems. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, adding visual interest to its rugged appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh climates.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
