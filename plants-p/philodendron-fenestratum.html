Philodendron fenestratum

Philodendron fenestratum, commonly known as the split-leaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that often feature natural holes or splits, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant has a vigorous growth habit and can reach significant heights when given proper support. Its foliage is typically a deep green, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with consistent moisture.

Philodendron fenestratum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
