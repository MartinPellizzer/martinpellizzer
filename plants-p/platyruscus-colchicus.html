Platyruscus colchicus

Platyruscus colchicus, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and grows in rocky or mountainous habitats. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in partial shade.

Botanically, it belongs to the Campanulaceae family, known for its bell-shaped flowers.
