Pelargonium × obliquum

Pelargonium × obliquum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for hanging baskets and containers due to its attractive foliage and prolonged flowering period.
