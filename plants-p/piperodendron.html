Piperodendron

Piperodendron, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has a woody stem with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its roots and flowers are valued for their aromatic properties and are used in various cultural and medicinal practices.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.
