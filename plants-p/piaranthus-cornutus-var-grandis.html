Piaranthus cornutus var. grandis

Piaranthus cornutus var. grandis, commonly known as the giant horned lily, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, specifically the Cape Province, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its large, showy white or pale purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are strap-like and often form a rosette, while the stems are robust and can reach significant heights.

This species is notable for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its unique appearance and resilience.
