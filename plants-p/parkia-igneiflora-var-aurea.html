Parkia igneiflora var. aurea

Parkia igneiflora var. aurea, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that appear in clusters, creating a vivid display during the flowering season. The tree has bipinnate leaves that are alternate and can reach up to 30 cm in length. Its fruit is a flattened pod containing several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
