Primula monticola

Primula monticola, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. The plant features rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink or white flowers. It is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
