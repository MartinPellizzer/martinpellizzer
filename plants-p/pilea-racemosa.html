Pilea racemosa

Pilea racemosa, commonly known as the creeping pilea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale green flowers. The plant produces oval-shaped, slightly hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Pilea racemosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
