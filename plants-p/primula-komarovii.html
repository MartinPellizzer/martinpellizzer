Primula komarovii

Primula komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant features rosettes of broad, ovate leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, brightly colored flowers, often in shades of white, pink, or purple. Its flowers are typically borne on long, slender stems and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Primula komarovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
