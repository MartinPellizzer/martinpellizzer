Phacelia integrifolia

Phacelia integrifolia, commonly known as blue phacelia, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Phacelia integrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
