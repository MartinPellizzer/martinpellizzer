Pinus sylvestris f. nana

Pinus sylvestris f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf Scots pine, is a compact form of the Scots pine native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its low, spreading growth habit and short, dense needles. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
