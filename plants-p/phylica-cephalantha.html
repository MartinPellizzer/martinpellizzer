Phylica cephalantha

Phylica cephalantha, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has small, needle-like leaves and is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid climates.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
