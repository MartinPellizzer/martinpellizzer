Pseudomorus brunoniana subvar. castaneifolia

Pseudomorus brunoniana subvar. castaneifolia, commonly known as the red-leaved pseudomorus, is a variant of the pseudomorus genus native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, glossy, reddish-brown leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in panicles, and are usually white or pale yellow.

The botanical features of this subvariety make it valuable for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
