Petrea racemosa var. alba

Petrea racemosa var. alba, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark is smooth and may develop fissures with age, adding to its visual appeal.
