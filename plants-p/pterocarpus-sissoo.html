Pterocarpus sissoo

Pterocarpus sissoo, commonly known as Indian rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood that is highly valued for furniture and decorative purposes. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Pterocarpus sissoo also plays an important role in traditional medicine and is often used in ornamental landscaping.
