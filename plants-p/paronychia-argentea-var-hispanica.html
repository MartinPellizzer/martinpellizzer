Paronychia argentea var. hispanica

Paronychia argentea var. hispanica, commonly known as silverleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, white to pale pink flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. The leaves are narrow, silver-gray in color, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and unique foliage.
