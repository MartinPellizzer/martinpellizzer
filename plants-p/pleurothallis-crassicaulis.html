Pleurothallis crassicaulis

Pleurothallis crassicaulis, commonly known as the thick-stemmed orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often clustered in racemes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
