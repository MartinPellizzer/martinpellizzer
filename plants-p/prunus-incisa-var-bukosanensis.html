Prunus incisa var. bukosanensis

Prunus incisa var. bukosanensis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Japanese white cherry, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. The tree has a compact, rounded shape with smooth, gray bark that develops shallow fissures with age. Its leaves are ovate, serrated, and turn bright yellow in autumn.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
