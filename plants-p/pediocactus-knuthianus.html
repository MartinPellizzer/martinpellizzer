Pediocactus knuthianus

Pediocactus knuthianus, commonly known as the Knuth's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spineless, greenish-yellow stems that store water. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in desert gardens and conservation efforts.
