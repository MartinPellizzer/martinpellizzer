Peperomia condotoana

Peperomia condotoana, commonly known as the Condoto peperomia, is a species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is a compact, evergreen perennial with thick, waxy leaves that are typically green or variegated. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in spikes. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice for collectors and indoor gardeners.
