Phaseolus ceratonoides

Phaseolus ceratonoides, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It produces vibrant red or orange flowers that give way to elongated, bean-like pods. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is often cultivated for its edible seeds. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its nutritional content.
