Priva auricoccea

Priva auricoccea, commonly known as the auriculate privia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which have a distinctive ear-like lobes at the base. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the warm seasons. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
