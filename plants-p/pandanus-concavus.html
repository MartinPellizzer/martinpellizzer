Pandanus concavus

Pandanus concavus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often spirally arranged around the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its roots and leaves have been traditionally used in local crafts and for their aromatic properties.

Pandanus concavus thrives in coastal and wetland habitats, often growing near water bodies.
