Potentilla polyphylla var. interrupta

Potentilla polyphylla var. interrupta, commonly known as the Sierra plum or Sierra cinquefoil, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, broadly ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.
