Peristylus chloranthus

Peristylus chloranthus, commonly known as the yellow mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its botanical features make it a popular choice for native gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

Peristylus chloranthus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
