Piper pseudovelutinum

Piper pseudovelutinum, commonly known as the velvet pipevine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, viscid substance.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
