Polemonium caeruleum f. vulgare

Polemonium caeruleum f. vulgare, commonly known as the bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lanceolate, alternate, and often have a slight hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
