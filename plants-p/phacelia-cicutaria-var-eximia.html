Phacelia cicutaria var. eximia

Phacelia cicutaria var. eximia, commonly known as the silverleaf phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
