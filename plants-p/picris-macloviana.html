Picris macloviana

Picris macloviana, commonly known as the Maclovian goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
