Prosthechea punctulata

Prosthechea punctulata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America.

It produces erect, branching stems that bear numerous small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and have a distinctive spotted appearance.

Prosthechea punctulata is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
