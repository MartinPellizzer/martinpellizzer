Potamogeton natans var. polygonifolius

Potamogeton natans var. polygonifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow waters, forming dense mats that provide habitat for aquatic life. The plant has long, flexible stems and broad, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. Its small, green flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom in the summer months.

This variety is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize sediments and improve water quality.
