Pelargonium × deletum

Pelargonium × deletum, commonly known as the spotted geranium, is a hybrid plant within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety foliage that often features red or purple markings. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically during the spring and summer months. Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a garden plant and for use in floral arrangements.
