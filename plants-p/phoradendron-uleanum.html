Phoradendron uleanum

Phoradendron uleanum, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the Americas.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing photosynthesis using its leaves. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers and red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that eat the berries, facilitating its spread to new hosts.

Phoradendron uleanum plays a unique ecological role in forest ecosystems as both a parasite and a food source for various wildlife.
