Parinari capensis var. latifolia

Parinari capensis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved parinari, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are often glabrous on both surfaces. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits in native habitats.
