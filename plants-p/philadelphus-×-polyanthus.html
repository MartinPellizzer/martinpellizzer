Philadelphus × polyanthus

Philadelphus × polyanthus, commonly known as the mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters and has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The species is often used in hedges or as a specimen plant due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
