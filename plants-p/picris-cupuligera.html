Picris cupuligera

Picris cupuligera, commonly known as the cupular eryngo, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a pale green center.

The plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
