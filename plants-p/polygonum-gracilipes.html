Polygonum gracilipes

Polygonum gracilipes, commonly known as slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive leaf shapes and often red or purple stems. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. Its leaves are elongated and tapering, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by small, three-lobed berries.
