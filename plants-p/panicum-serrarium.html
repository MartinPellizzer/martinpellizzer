Panicum serrarium

Panicum serrarium, commonly known as the serrated panicgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaf margins and produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers. Native to various regions in Africa and Asia, it thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural applications.
