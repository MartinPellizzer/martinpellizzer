Paris biondii

Paris biondii, commonly known as the golden paris, is a rare and striking member of the Melanthiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and clusters of delicate, pale yellow to golden flowers. The plant produces small, bell-shaped blossoms that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its elegant appearance.

Native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, it thrives in cool, moist environments and is considered a delicate, hardy species.
