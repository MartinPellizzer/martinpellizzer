Persicaria × lenticularis

Persicaria × lenticularis, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Persicaria × lenticularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically for medicinal purposes.
