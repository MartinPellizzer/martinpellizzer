Piper pubinervulum

Piper pubinervulum, commonly known as the hairy nerve plant, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its finely pubescent (hairy) stems and leaves with prominent, visible veins. Its small, greenish-white flowers are typically arranged in spikes, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
