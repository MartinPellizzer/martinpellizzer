Phyllostachys pubescens var. taokiang

Phyllostachys pubescens var. taokiang, commonly known as the Taokiang bamboo, is a variety of Bambusa that is native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are covered in a fine layer of pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This bamboo is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
