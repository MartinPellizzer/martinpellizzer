Peperomia decumbens

Peperomia decumbens, commonly known as the prostrate peperomia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It features fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a unique texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its compact growth habit, it is often used as a ground cover or in hanging baskets.
