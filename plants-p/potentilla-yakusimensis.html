Potentilla yakusimensis

Potentilla yakusimensis, commonly known as Yakutsk cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the cold regions of eastern Siberia and northern Mongolia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a distinctive five-lobed shape, contributing to its common name.

Potentilla yakusimensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
