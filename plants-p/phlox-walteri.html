Phlox walteri

Phlox walteri, commonly known as Walter's phlox, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade. Its foliage consists of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Walter's phlox is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
