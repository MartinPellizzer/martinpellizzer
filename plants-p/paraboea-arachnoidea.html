Paraboea arachnoidea

Paraboea arachnoidea, commonly known as the spider's web vine, is a climbing plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its unique appearance and growth habit make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

However, it requires a humid, shaded environment to thrive and is often used in tropical landscapes.
