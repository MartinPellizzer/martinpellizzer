Pelargonium ovale var. dregei

Pelargonium ovale var. dregei, commonly known as the African geranium, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, ovate leaves that are typically pale green with a reddish tinge, and it produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a favored ornamental plant.
