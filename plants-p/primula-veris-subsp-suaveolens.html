Primula veris subsp. suaveolens

Primula veris subsp.

suaveolens, commonly known as the Sweet Primrose, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features upright, hairy stems that bear lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces clusters of showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
