Pilosella lychnea

Pilosella lychnea, commonly known as the pale mountain bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or white in color. The plant has hairy leaves and stems, which give it its scientific name "Pilosella," derived from the Greek word for "hairy.". It is native to mountainous regions in Europe and is often found in alpine meadows.

Pilosella lychnea is a perennial herb that blooms in late spring to early summer.
