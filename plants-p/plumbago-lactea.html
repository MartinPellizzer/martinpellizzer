Plumbago lactea

Plumbago lactea, commonly known as blue plumbago, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom throughout the summer months. The plant features slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
