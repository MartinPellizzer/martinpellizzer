Pilosocereus glaucescens

Pilosocereus glaucescens, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive green stem covered with dense, white spines. The plant typically grows as a low-growing shrub, often forming a compact, rounded shape. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange and bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
