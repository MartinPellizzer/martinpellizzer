Phlomis umbrosa var. sylvatica

Phlomis umbrosa var. sylvatica, commonly known as the woodland phlomis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. The plant features upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing clusters of pale blue to purple flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
