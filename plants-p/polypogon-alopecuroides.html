Polypogon alopecuroides

Polypogon alopecuroides, commonly known as the slender grass or narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and sandy soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, spikelet-like flowers that are wind-pollinated.

The plant is adapted to warm climates and is often found in areas with full sun exposure.
