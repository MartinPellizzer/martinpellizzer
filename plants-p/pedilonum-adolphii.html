Pedilonum adolphii

Pedilonum adolphii, commonly known as the Adolphii's slipper orchid, is a rare and fascinating orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped pouch that serves as a specialized structure for trapping pollinators. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks and branches in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually small and colorful, often displaying shades of yellow, orange, and red.

Due to its limited distribution and specific environmental requirements, Pedilonum adolphii is considered a vulnerable species in the wild.
