Peganon

Peganon, commonly known as the marsh mallow, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the family Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Peganon thrives in moist, wet environments such as marshes and stream banks.

Its root contains mucilage, which has been used in traditional medicine for its soothing properties.
