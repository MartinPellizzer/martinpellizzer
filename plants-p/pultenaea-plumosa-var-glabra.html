Pultenaea plumosa var. glabra

Pultenaea plumosa var. glabra, commonly known as the silky pultenaea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. Its foliage is typically a bright green, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often found in open woodlands and heathlands, preferring full sun to partial shade.
