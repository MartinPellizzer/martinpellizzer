Papaver rubroaurantiacum subsp. longiscapum

Papaver rubroaurantiacum subsp.

longiscapum, commonly known as the long-stemmed poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It features striking red and orange flowers with a distinctive, intricate pattern of spots and stripes.

The plant has slender, branching stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers open in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
