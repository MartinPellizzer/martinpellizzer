Protium ambrosiacum

Protium ambrosiacum, commonly known as the sacred wood or ambrosia tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its thick, aromatic bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and pleasant scent, often used in traditional crafts and religious ceremonies.

The plant also has medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
