Polygonum neolittorale

Polygonum neolittorale, commonly known as coastal polygonum, is a flowering plant species native to the coastal regions of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in brackish or saline environments, often found in wetlands and tidal areas.

Polygonum neolittorale plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
