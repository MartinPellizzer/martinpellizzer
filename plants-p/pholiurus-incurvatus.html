Pholiurus incurvatus

Pholiurus incurvatus, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa. The plant is characterized by its zigzagging stems and opposite, ovate leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals, and it produces small, round berries.

The plant contains various alkaloids and has been used traditionally in some cultures for medicinal purposes.
