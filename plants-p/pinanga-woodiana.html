Pinanga woodiana

Pinanga woodiana, commonly known as the Sunda bamboo or giant bamboo, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Pinanga woodiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional landscaping and horticulture.
