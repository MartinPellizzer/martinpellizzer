Pteryxia terebinthina

Pteryxia terebinthina, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark red wood, which is highly valued for its durability and unique scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
