Potentilla agrimonioides var. kobdoensis

Potentilla agrimonioides var. kobdoensis, commonly known as Kobdo potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has holly-like leaves that are typically evergreen, providing year-round interest in the landscape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh conditions.
