Piper asymmetricum

Piper asymmetricum, commonly known as the asymmetric pepper or the Maluku pepper, is a climbing vine native to the Indonesian archipelago.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, elongated fruits that resemble peppercorns. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with slightly serrated edges and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its unique asymmetrical fruit shape gives it its common name and distinguishes it from other pepper species.

It is primarily cultivated for its aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
