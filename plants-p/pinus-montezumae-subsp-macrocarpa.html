Pinus montezumae subsp. macrocarpa

Pinus montezumae subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the Mexican bristlecone pine, is a subspecies native to the high elevations of central Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in bundles of five.

The cones of this species are large and woody, containing edible seeds that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its longevity and slow growth, it is considered a valuable species for ecological and conservation studies.
