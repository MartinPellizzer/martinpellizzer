Potentilla halleriana

Potentilla halleriana, commonly known as the Haller's cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with five leaflets, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

Potentilla halleriana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardiness in cooler climates.
