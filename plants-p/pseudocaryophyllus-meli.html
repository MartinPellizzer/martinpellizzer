Pseudocaryophyllus meli

Pseudocaryophyllus meli, commonly known as the melon bug, is a species of plant in the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its small, herbaceous growth habit and features small, clustered flowers. The plant typically thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open, disturbed habitats. Its leaves are usually opposite and ovate, with a rough texture.

The flowers are pink to purple in color, adding ornamental value to the plant.
