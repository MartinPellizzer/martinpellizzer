Penstemon punctatus

Penstemon punctatus, commonly known as the spotted beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The plant's unique appearance and adaptability make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
