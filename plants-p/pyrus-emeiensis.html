Pyrus emeiensis

Pyrus emeiensis, commonly known as the Emei pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to China, specifically found in the mountainous regions of Sichuan Province. The plant is characterized by its deciduous leaves and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like fruit, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Pyrus emeiensis is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its ecological and medicinal significance.
