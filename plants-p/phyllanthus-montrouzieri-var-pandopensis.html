Phyllanthus montrouzieri var. pandopensis

Phyllanthus montrouzieri var. pandopensis, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, green, star-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, berry-like fruits that are popular in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
