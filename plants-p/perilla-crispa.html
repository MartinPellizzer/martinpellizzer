Perilla crispa

Perilla crispa, commonly known as curled perilla, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, crinkled leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Perilla crispa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its flowering stems produce small, pale purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
