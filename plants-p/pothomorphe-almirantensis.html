Pothomorphe almirantensis

Pothomorphe almirantensis, commonly known as the Saint Lucia aster, is a flowering plant native to the island of Saint Lucia in the Lesser Antilles.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forest floors and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Pothomorphe almirantensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and contributes to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
