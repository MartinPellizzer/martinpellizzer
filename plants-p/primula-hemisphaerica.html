Primula hemisphaerica

Primula hemisphaerica, commonly known as the hemispherical primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, waxy, dark green leaves that form a compact, hemispherical shape. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded, rocky habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts interested in rare flora.
