Potentilla bannehalensis

Potentilla bannehalensis, commonly known as the Bannehalen cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing shrub form with dense, sprawling stems. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it is well adapted to rocky and arid environments.

Potentilla bannehalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
