Paphiopedilum concolor f. sulphurinum

Paphiopedilum concolor f. sulphurinum, commonly known as the sulphur-flowered paphiopedilum, is a hybrid orchid species characterized by its unique floral structure and coloration.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia. The plant features a thick, fleshy rhizome and produces showy, lip-shaped flowers with a distinctive yellowish-orange hue. Its flowers are typically borne on terminal inflorescences and have a complex, spurred lip that aids in pollination.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking appearance and ease of care.
