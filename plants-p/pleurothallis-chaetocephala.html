Pleurothallis chaetocephala

Pleurothallis chaetocephala, commonly known as the hairy-headed pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, hairy sepals that give it its common name. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its unique appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
