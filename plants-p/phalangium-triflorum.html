Phalangium triflorum

Phalangium triflorum, commonly known as the three-flowered phalangium, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive floral structure.

It features three small, clustered flowers that bloom sequentially, giving it a subtle and elegant appearance. The plant is native to specific regions in South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and slightly serrated, contributing to its ornamental value.

Botanically, it is notable for its unusual reproductive strategy, which involves a specialized pollination mechanism.
