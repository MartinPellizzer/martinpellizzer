Pollinia japonica

Pollinia japonica, commonly known as the Japanese orchid bee plant, is a species of orchid native to Japan.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which includes specialized pollinia that adhere to the bodies of pollinators. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Pollinia japonica thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its reproductive strategy relies heavily on specific pollinators, making it an important subject in ecological studies.
