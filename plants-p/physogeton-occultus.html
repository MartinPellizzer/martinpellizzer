Physogeton occultus

Physogeton occultus, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a rare and enigmatic fern species native to the understory of moist, shaded forests in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale green fronds that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant has a unique reproductive system, relying on specialized spore structures for propagation. Its leaves are typically small and clustered, with a distinct, almost translucent appearance.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, Physogeton occultus is considered a rare and poorly understood botanical specimen.
