Philodendron cardosoi

Philodendron cardosoi, commonly known as the Brazilian shield fern, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
