Pilosella caespitosa var. suecica

Pilosella caespitosa var. suecica, commonly known as the Swedish hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to northern Europe.

It features dense, tufted growth with fine, hairy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting insect populations.
