Pelargonium adriaanii

Pelargonium adriaanii, commonly known as the Adriaan's geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun exposure. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Pelargonium adriaanii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
