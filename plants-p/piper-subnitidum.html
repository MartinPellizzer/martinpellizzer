Piper subnitidum

Piper subnitidum, commonly known as the velvetleaf pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature.

Piper subnitidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
