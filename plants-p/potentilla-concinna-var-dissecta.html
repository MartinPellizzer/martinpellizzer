Potentilla concinna var. dissecta

Potentilla concinna var. dissecta, commonly known as the dissected potentilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, silvery-white hairs. The leaves are deeply lobed and dissected, giving the plant a feathery appearance. It produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers in early summer.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to rocky or well-drained soils.
