Persicaria truellum

Persicaria truellum, commonly known as redstem filaree, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and ditches. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color, hence its common name. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink to purple and bloom in late summer.

Persicaria truellum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
