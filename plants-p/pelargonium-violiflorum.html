Pelargonium violiflorum

Pelargonium violiflorum, commonly known as the violet geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The leaves are typically rounded and have a velvety texture, often with a reddish or purple underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
