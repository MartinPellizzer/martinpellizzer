Potentilla potaninii var. potaninii

Potentilla potaninii var. potaninii, commonly known as the Tibetan cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to high-altitude regions of Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
