Padus pseudocerasus

Padus pseudocerasus, commonly known as the Chinese date tree, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy white flowers that appear in spring. The tree produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a sweet, sticky texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in temperate climates.
