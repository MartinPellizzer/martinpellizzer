Piper eucalyptophyllum

Piper eucalyptophyllum, commonly known as the eucalyptus pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, peppery leaves that emit a strong scent when crushed. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its unique fragrance and appearance, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
