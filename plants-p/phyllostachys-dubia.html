Phyllostachys dubia

Phyllostachys dubia, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or bamboo grass, is a species of grass in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish-yellow flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Phyllostachys dubia is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in landscaping and traditional Chinese medicine.
