Pholidota tixieri

Pholidota tixieri, commonly known as the African boxwood, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal.

Pholidota tixieri is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.
