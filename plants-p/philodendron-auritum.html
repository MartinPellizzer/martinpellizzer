Philodendron auritum

Philodendron auritum, commonly known as the ear-shaped philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that often have a waxy, glossy surface. The plant features a climbing habit, with aerial roots that allow it to attach to supports as it grows. Its foliage typically displays a deep green color with prominent veins.

Philodendron auritum thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and humidity to flourish.
