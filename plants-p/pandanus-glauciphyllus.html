Pandanus glauciphyllus

Pandanus glauciphyllus, commonly known as the silver-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are typically covered in a waxy, silver-gray coating. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear fragrant, white flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

The species is well adapted to coastal and saline environments, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
