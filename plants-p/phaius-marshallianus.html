Phaius marshallianus

Phaius marshallianus, commonly known as the Marshall's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its leaves are long and narrow, often forming a basal rosette.

The orchid's flowers are usually yellow or orange with red or purple markings, making it a striking addition to botanical gardens.
