Pinus sinclairiana

Pinus sinclairiana, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada red fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It typically grows in high-altitude regions, reaching heights of up to 60 meters with a conical shape and dark green needles. The tree produces cones that range from 5 to 10 centimeters in length, containing winged seeds dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and furrowed, providing protection against forest fires.

The Sierra Nevada red fir plays a vital role in the ecosystem, offering habitat and food for various wildlife species.
