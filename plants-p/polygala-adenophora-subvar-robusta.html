Polygala adenophora subvar. robusta

Polygala adenophora subvar. robusta, commonly known as the robust polygala, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that typically range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers are often adorned with distinctive, thread-like structures.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
