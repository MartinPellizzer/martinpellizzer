Persea pyrifolia

Persea pyrifolia, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, yellowish fruits that mature into orange-red berries, which are edible and have a sweet, honey-like flavor. Persea pyrifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit and fruiting patterns make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
