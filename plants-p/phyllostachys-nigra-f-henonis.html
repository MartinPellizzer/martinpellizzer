Phyllostachys nigra f. henonis

Phyllostachys nigra f. henonis, commonly known as the black bamboo, is a variant of the black bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, culms that are often adorned with a silvery sheen. The plant exhibits a clumping growth habit, forming dense clusters that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a glossy, dark green surface.

This ornamental bamboo is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its visual impact.
