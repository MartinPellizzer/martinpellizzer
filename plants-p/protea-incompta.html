Protea incompta

Protea incompta, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pale pink flower heads. The plant has narrow, silvery-green leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun exposure.

Protea incompta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
