Protasparagus pilosus

Protasparagus pilosus, commonly known as the hairy asparagus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense clumps.

Protasparagus pilosus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.
