Paspalum furcatum

Paspalum furcatum, commonly known as the forked grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along waterways.

Paspalum furcatum is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
