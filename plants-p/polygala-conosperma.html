Polygala conosperma

Polygala conosperma, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are characteristic of the Polygala genus. Its leaves are narrow and linear, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowers.
