Pseudolobivia callichroma

Pseudolobivia callichroma, commonly known as the yellow-topped cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white to yellow spines, which provide protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to conserve water and withstand high temperatures.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
