Pachyphyllum falcifolium

Pachyphyllum falcifolium, commonly known as the sword cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the rainy season, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and deterring herbivores.

Pachyphyllum falcifolium is well adapted to extreme climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
