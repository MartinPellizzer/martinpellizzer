Paliurus australis

Paliurus australis, commonly known as the Australian thornapple, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Paliurus australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
