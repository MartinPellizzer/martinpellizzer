Pisonia aculeata var. typica

Pisonia aculeata var. typica, commonly known as the silky pisonia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, white to pink flowers arranged in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by birds. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or limestone soils.

The silky pisonia is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
