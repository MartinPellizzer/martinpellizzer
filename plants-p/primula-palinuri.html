Primula palinuri

Primula palinuri, commonly known as the Palinuri primrose, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are oval and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions.

Primula palinuri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
