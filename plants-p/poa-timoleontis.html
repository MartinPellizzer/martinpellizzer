Poa timoleontis

Poa timoleontis, commonly known as the Timoleontis bentgrass, is a species of grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as rocky soils and coastal environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptability to harsh climates and its role in stabilizing soil in arid regions.
