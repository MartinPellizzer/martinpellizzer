Phlegmatospermum andraeanum

Phlegmatospermum andraeanum, commonly known as the Andraeanum phlegmatospermum, is a rare and ancient plant species belonging to the phlegmatosperm group, which represents one of the oldest lineages of land plants.

It is characterized by its simple, non-vascular structure and its ability to survive in nutrient-poor environments. The plant exhibits a unique reproductive system, relying on spore dispersal for propagation. Its leaves are typically small, broad, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for studying plant evolution and adaptation.
