Plantago montana var. sudetica

Plantago montana var. sudetica, commonly known as the Sudetic plantain, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. The plant features rosettes of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowering stems bear dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that develop into capsules containing numerous seeds.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
