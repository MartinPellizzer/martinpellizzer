Pyrenacantha globosa

Pyrenacantha globosa, commonly known as the globular thornapple, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rounded, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, spherical berries that are attractive to birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a notable species in its native habitat.
