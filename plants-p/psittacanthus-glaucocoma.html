Psittacanthus glaucocoma

Psittacanthus glaucocoma, commonly known as the blue-veined fern, is a species of cycad-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
