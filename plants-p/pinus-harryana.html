Pinus harryana

Pinus harryana, commonly known as Harry's pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are typically 2 to 4 inches long. The cones of Pinus harryana are relatively small and often persist on the branches for several years. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species in its native habitat.
