Physalis geniculata

Physalis geniculata, commonly known as the bird's nest fruit or golden gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated calyx that encases the fruit. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible when ripe. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with hairy surfaces.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
