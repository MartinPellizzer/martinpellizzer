Ptychosperma streimannii

Ptychosperma streimannii, commonly known as the African cycad, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces cycad-like cones, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, dry climate.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
