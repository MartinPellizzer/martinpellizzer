Persea erythropus

Persea erythropus, commonly known as the red-leaf persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America and northern South America. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves that turn reddish in color before falling in the dry season. It produces small, white flowers followed by edible, orange-colored fruits that are highly prized.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is rich in nutrients and has a sweet, creamy texture.
