Pelargonium × anthriscifolium

Pelargonium × anthriscifolium, commonly known as the parsley-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a member of the Geraniaceae family and is distinguished by its finely divided, parsley-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden use, though it is less commonly cultivated than other pelargonium species.
