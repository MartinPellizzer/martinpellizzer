Pueraria longicarpa

Pueraria longicarpa, commonly known as the long-stemmed cowpea or Chinese kudzu, is a climbing legume native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, purple-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is known for its vigorous growth and ability to form dense thickets, often invading disturbed areas. Its roots contain compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Pueraria longicarpa is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
