Ptilotus spicatus

Ptilotus spicatus, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and spiky, cylindrical flower heads. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils and coastal dunes. Its growth habit is low and mat-forming, making it well adapted to harsh climatic conditions.

Ptilotus spicatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
