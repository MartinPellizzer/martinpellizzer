Plectopoma

Plectopoma, commonly known as the sea hare, is a type of marine gastropod mollusk belonging to the family Aplysiidae.

These creatures are characterized by their soft, often colorful bodies and the presence of a muscular foot used for crawling along the seafloor. They possess a radula, a ribbon-like structure used for grazing on algae and other plant matter. Plectopoma species are typically found in tropical and temperate coastal waters, where they play a role in marine ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling.

Their anatomy and behavior make them important subjects in studies of marine biology and ecology.
