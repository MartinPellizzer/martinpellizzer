Papaver caucasicum var. microstigmum

Papaver caucasicum var. microstigmum, commonly known as the Caucasian poppy, is a variant of the broader Papaver caucasicum species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep crimson, with a distinctive, small stigma at the center. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are covered in a fine, hairy layer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
