Plumbago montis-elgonis

Plumbago montis-elgonis, commonly known as the Elgon plumbago, is a flowering plant native to the montane regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, often found in the high-altitude areas of Mount Elgon. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
