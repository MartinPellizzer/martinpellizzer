Phyteuma michelii var. persicifolium

Phyteuma michelii var. persicifolium, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep purple, often with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
