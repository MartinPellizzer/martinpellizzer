Pyrus sicula

Pyrus sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental white flowers and edible fruit. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while the bark is smooth and grayish in color. The fruit is typically small, sweet, and has a high water content, making it suitable for both fresh consumption and drying.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its aesthetic and culinary value.
