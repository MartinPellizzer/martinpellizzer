Pavonia propinqua

Pavonia propinqua, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet. The plant features slender, branching stems and produces small, showy flowers in shades of red and yellow. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Pavonia propinqua thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.
