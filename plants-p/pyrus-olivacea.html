Pyrus olivacea

Pyrus olivacea, commonly known as the olive pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, yellow-green fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. Its botanical features include a rough, fissured bark and a tendency to form a rounded, compact shape.

Pyrus olivacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
