Paphiopedilum venustum var. rubrum

Paphiopedilum venustum var. rubrum, commonly known as the red-flowered slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper, a feature common among orchids of the Paphiopedilum genus. The plant produces showy, vibrant red flowers with intricate patterns, often with a contrasting yellow or orange throat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
