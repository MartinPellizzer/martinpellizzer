Polypodium polypodioides var. knoblochianum

Polypodium polypodioides var. knoblochianum, commonly known as the knobloch's maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has delicate, finely divided fronds that are usually dark green and have a distinctive, elegant appearance. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a characteristic feature of the Polypodiaceae family.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
