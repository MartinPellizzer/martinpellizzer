Pandanus basilocularis

Pandanus basilocularis, commonly known as the narrow-fruit pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by distinctive, spiky fruit clusters. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often growing in sandy or muddy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and cultural significance, it is cultivated in many tropical regions for both practical and aesthetic purposes.
