Potentilla evestita var. robusta

Potentilla evestita var. robusta, commonly known as the rough potentilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its sturdy, upright growth habit and its clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact size and long-lasting blooms.
