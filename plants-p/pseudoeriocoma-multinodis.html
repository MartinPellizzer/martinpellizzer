Pseudoeriocoma multinodis

Pseudoeriocoma multinodis, commonly known as the nodular eriocoma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive, soft-textured appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale pink, adding a delicate touch to its overall appearance.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, Pseudoeriocoma multinodis is often found in specialized ecosystems and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
