Prunus serrulata var. borealis

Prunus serrulata var. borealis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a variant of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree has a rounded shape with slender branches and is native to the northern regions of Japan. Its leaves are oval-shaped with finely serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is highly valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and along streets for its seasonal floral display.
