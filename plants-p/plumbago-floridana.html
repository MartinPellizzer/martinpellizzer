Plumbago floridana

Plumbago floridana, commonly known as blue plumbago, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
