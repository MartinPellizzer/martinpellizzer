Polystachya penangensis

Polystachya penangensis, commonly known as the Penang spike orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and linear leaves. The plant produces inflorescences with numerous small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
