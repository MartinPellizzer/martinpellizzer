Pediocactus unguispinus

Pediocactus unguispinus, commonly known as the spiny hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in sharp, spiny tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in arid gardens.
