Ptychandra glauca

Ptychandra glauca, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its blue-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Ptychandra glauca is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
