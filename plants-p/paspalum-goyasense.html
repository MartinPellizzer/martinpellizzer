Paspalum goyasense

Paspalum goyasense, commonly known as the Goyasense grass, is a perennial plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to thrive in a variety of soil types, including those that are sandy or rocky. It is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas, demonstrating a high degree of ecological tolerance.

Paspalum goyasense is also noted for its potential use in restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
