Physalodes peruvianum

Physalodes peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces violet to purple flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous seeds.

Physalodes peruvianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
