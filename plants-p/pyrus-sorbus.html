Pyrus sorbus

Pyrus sorbus, commonly known as whitebeam, is a genus of deciduous trees and shrubs native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed, and the bark is often fissured and grayish in color. Pyrus sorbus species are valued for their ornamental qualities, including their showy spring blossoms and persistent winter berries.

Some species are also used in horticulture for their hardiness and adaptability to various soil conditions.
