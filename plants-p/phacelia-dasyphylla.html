Phacelia dasyphylla

Phacelia dasyphylla, commonly known as the Sierra phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, such as meadows and slopes. It has small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Phacelia dasyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
