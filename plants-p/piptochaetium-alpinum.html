Piptochaetium alpinum

Piptochaetium alpinum, commonly known as alpine needlegrass, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is a perennial grass species that thrives in cool, dry environments, often found in mountainous regions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, including strong winds and low temperatures.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil in alpine ecosystems and providing forage for certain wildlife species.
