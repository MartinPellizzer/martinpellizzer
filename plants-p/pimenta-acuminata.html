Pimenta acuminata

Pimenta acuminata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, red, peppery fruits that are edible. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a fragrant aroma when crushed.

The flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
