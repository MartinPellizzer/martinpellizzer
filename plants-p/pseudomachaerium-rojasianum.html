Pseudomachaerium rojasianum

Pseudomachaerium rojasianum, commonly known as the red grass or red panic grass, is a tropical grass species native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that often display a reddish hue, especially in younger growth. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed florets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated for landscaping or as a decorative plant.
