Prunus mutabilis f. odorifera

Prunus mutabilis f. odorifera, commonly known as the sweet cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and juicy fruits that are typically harvested in late spring to early summer. The plant produces white or pink flowers in early spring, which are often fragrant and attract pollinators. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its culinary uses and ornamental appeal in gardens.
