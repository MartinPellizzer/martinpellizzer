Pleroma venetiense

Pleroma venetiense, commonly known as the Venetian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Adriatic Sea.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in sandy or calcareous soils. It is a perennial species that prefers full sun and is often found in dune environments.

Pleroma venetiense is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
