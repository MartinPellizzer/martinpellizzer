Pseudocaryophyllus emarginatus

Pseudocaryophyllus emarginatus, commonly known as the emarginate pseudocaryophyllus, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically display a range of pink to white hues. The plant has a low-growing habit with slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
