Paspalum nanum

Paspalum nanum, commonly known as dwarf paspalum, is a small, perennial grass native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to saltwater environments and can tolerate periodic flooding.

Paspalum nanum is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
