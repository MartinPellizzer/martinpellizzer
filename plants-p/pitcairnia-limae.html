Pitcairnia limae

Pitcairnia limae, commonly known as the Emerald Cactus, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to Brazil.

It is a terrestrial epiphyte, often found growing on trees in humid tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its dense rosette of sword-shaped, dark green leaves that form a water-holding cup. It produces striking red or orange flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a decorative plant in many parts of the world.
