Paraboea axillaris

Paraboea axillaris, commonly known as the rock wallaby orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pale green or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are linear and often clustered at the base of the plant.

Paraboea axillaris is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure and habitat preferences.
