Pyrrhocactus aconcaguensis

Pyrrhocactus aconcaguensis, commonly known as the Aconcagua torch cactus, is a species of cactus native to Chile.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem and bright red flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with spines that help reduce water loss and provide protection from herbivores. Its root system is shallow, allowing it to absorb moisture from light rains.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh conditions.
