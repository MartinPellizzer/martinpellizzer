Polygala vulgaris proles oxyptera

Polygala vulgaris proles oxyptera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. The plant features slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small and clustered in spicate inflorescences, usually in shades of pink or white.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
