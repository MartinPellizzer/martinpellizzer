Psidium tenuifolium

Psidium tenuifolium, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or small-leaf guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible berries that are typically red when ripe. Its fruits are valued for their sweet flavor and are often used in culinary applications.

The plant is also appreciated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
