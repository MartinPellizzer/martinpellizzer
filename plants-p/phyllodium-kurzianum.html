Phyllodium kurzianum

Phyllodium kurzianum, commonly known as the Kurz's phyllodium, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, and often curled leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to reddish-purple, typically blooming in spring. Its woody stems and drought-resistant nature make it well adapted to harsh, sandy soils.

Phyllodium kurzianum is often found in open woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
