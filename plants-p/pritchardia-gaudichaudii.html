Pritchardia gaudichaudii

Pritchardia gaudichaudii, commonly known as the Pritchardia tree, is a species of cycad native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of stiff, sword-shaped leaves. The plant is often found in coastal and lowland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its reproductive structures include a large, cone-like structure that produces both male and female spores.

This cycad is considered a keystone species in its native ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various native wildlife.
