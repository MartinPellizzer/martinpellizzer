Petrophile heterophylla var. intermedia

Petrophile heterophylla var. intermedia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to the south-western regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical inflorescences that produce bright red or orange flowers, which are particularly attractive to birds. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it exhibits variable leaf morphology depending on environmental conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
