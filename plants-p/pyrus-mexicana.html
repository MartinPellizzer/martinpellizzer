Pyrus mexicana

Pyrus mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican pear or wild pear, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pyrus.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its broad, oval leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in dry woodlands and rocky soils.

Pyrus mexicana is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
