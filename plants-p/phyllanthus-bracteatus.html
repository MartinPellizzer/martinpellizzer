Phyllanthus bracteatus

Phyllanthus bracteatus, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its smooth, thornless stems and alternate, elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright orange, fleshy fruits. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for its antioxidant properties.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and nutritional value.
