Physalis viscosa var. sinuatodentata

Physalis viscosa var. sinuatodentata, commonly known as the rough-hair figleaf gooseberry, is a variant of the common figleaf gooseberry.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia, particularly China. The plant features thick, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with wavy, toothed margins. Its fruit is a distinctive, inflated, yellowish capsule that turns red when mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its unique appearance and potential medicinal properties.
