Primula tyrolensis var. acuminata

Primula tyrolensis var. acuminata, commonly known as the Tyrol primrose, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or meadow habitats at high altitudes. Its leaves are oval and covered in a fine layer of down, which helps protect it from harsh weather conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
