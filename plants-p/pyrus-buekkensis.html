Pyrus buekkensis

Pyrus buekkensis, commonly known as the Buekkensis pear, is a rare and lesser-known species of the Pyrus genus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in the Tien Shan and Pamir ranges. The plant is characterized by its small, deciduous trees that produce clusters of white flowers in early spring. Its leaves are elliptical with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface.

The fruit, which is a small pear-like berry, is typically yellow or yellow-green when mature and is noted for its unique flavor and potential medicinal properties.
