Phaseoloides argenteum

Phaseoloides argenteum, commonly known as silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in the morning. It is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
