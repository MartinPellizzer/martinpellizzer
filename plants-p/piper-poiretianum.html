Piper poiretianum

Piper poiretianum, commonly known as the giant sarsaparilla, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper-like plants. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are berries that turn red when mature, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
