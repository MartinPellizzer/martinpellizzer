Pseuderanthemum liesneri

Pseuderanthemum liesneri, commonly known as the flame violet, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble traditional violets. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
