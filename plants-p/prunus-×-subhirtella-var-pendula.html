Prunus × subhirtella var. pendula

Prunus × subhirtella var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping almond, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that bear clusters of pink or white flowers in spring. The plant is a hybrid between Prunus subhirtella and Prunus × yedoensis, resulting in its distinctive weeping form. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental tree.

Due to its delicate appearance, it is often used in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
