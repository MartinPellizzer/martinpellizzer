Prunus cerasus var. duracina

Prunus cerasus var. duracina, commonly known as the duracina cherry, is a variety of sweet cherry characterized by its compact growth habit and early ripening.

It produces small, dark red fruits with a firm, juicy flesh and a sweet flavor. The plant is native to Europe and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

This variety is relatively disease-resistant and suitable for small-scale cultivation.
