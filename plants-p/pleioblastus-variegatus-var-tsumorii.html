Pleioblastus variegatus var. tsumorii

Pleioblastus variegatus var. tsumorii, commonly known as the Tsumorii bamboo, is a cold-hardy grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms that often exhibit a striking variegated pattern of green and white on the leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture. Its foliage remains evergreen throughout the year, providing visual interest in winter gardens.

It is also valued for its use in traditional Japanese landscaping and as a component in bonsai art.
