Penstemon barbatus subsp. torreyi

Penstemon barbatus subsp.

torreyi, commonly known as Torrey's beardplant, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as cliffs and slopes, where it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of hair.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
