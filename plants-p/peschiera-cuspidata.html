Peschiera cuspidata

Peschiera cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed water plantain, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, ditches, and wet meadows. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a waxy surface that helps repel water. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are pollinated by insects.

Peschiera cuspidata plays an important role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
