Persea sintoc

Persea sintoc, commonly known as the Philippine persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible when fully mature. Its botanical features include a smooth, grey-bark trunk and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers.

Persea sintoc is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
