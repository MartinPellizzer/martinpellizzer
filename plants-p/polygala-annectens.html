Polygala annectens

Polygala annectens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, and it produces small, indehiscent pods as fruit.

Polygala annectens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering habit.
