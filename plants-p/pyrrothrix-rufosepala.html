Pyrrothrix rufosepala

Pyrrothrix rufosepala, commonly known as the red-sepal pyrrothrix, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its striking red sepals and petals, which give it its common name. It typically grows in tropical rainforests, often as an epiphyte on tree trunks or branches.

The flowers are small but highly fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
