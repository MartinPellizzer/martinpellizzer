Pyrus chamaemespilus proles typica

Pyrus chamaemespilus proles typica, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, white flowers in early spring. The plant produces small, oval berries that ripen to a dark blue or black color in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and rocky areas.

The species is notable for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
