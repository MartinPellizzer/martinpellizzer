Porrum strictum

Porrum strictum, commonly known as the straight vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves, which are often hairy. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Porrum strictum is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and soil-improving properties.
