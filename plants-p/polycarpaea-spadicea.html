Polycarpaea spadicea

Polycarpaea spadicea, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, branched stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its most distinctive feature is the yellow to orange flower head, which consists of numerous small florets arranged in a spadix-like structure.

Polycarpaea spadicea is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
