Pterilema aceriflorum

Pterilema aceriflorum, commonly known as the maple-leaved pterilema, is a striking plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which resembles that of maple trees, with palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant colors in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in early spring. Its growth habit is shrub-like, with multiple stems arising from the base, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and specialty nurseries.
