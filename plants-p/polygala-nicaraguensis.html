Polygala nicaraguensis

Polygala nicaraguensis, commonly known as the Nicaraguan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, often clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a lanceolate shape.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
