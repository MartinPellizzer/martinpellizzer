Physematium molle

Physematium molle, commonly known as the soft-leafed sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, strap-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It is typically found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Physematium molle is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
