Polypodium pumilio

Polypodium pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf wall fern, is a small, terrestrial fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows on rocky surfaces or in shaded, moist environments, often forming dense, low-growing mats. The plant has finely divided, delicate fronds that are usually less than 10 centimeters in length. Its rhizome is short and produces both roots and spore-producing structures called sporangia.

Polypodium pumilio is well adapted to humid climates and is often found in forest understories or on shaded rock faces.
