Prioria

Prioria, commonly known as the Mexican ash or Mexican white ash, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Fabaceae.

It consists of a single species, Prioria copalis, which is native to the southern United States and northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces small, leathery fruits that contain several seeds.

Prioria is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
