Patagonium echinus

Patagonium echinus, commonly known as the Patagonian prickly pear, is a cactus native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, paddle-shaped stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by edible, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Patagonium echinus plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food and shelter for various desert species.
