Pelargonium tabulare

Pelargonium tabulare, commonly known as the African geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Pelargonium tabulare is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
