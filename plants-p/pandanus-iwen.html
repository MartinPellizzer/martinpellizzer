Pandanus iwen

Pandanus iwen, commonly known as the bottlebrush pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that form a dense rosette at the top. The plant produces inflorescences that resemble bottlebrushes, with bright red bracts that attract pollinators. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove ecosystems.

The leaves are used in traditional crafts and for thatching, highlighting its cultural and practical significance.
