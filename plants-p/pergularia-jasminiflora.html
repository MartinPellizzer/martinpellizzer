Pergularia jasminiflora

Pergularia jasminiflora, commonly known as the jasmine-flowered ivy, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, fragrant flowers that resemble jasmine. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. It is often cultivated as a decorative vine in gardens and greenhouses.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and adaptability to various soil types.
