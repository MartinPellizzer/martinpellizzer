Phyllanthus casticum subsp. microphyllus

Phyllanthus casticum subsp.

microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved castic, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures containing numerous tiny seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
