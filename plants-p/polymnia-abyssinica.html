Polymnia abyssinica

Polymnia abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian polymnia, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants such as buckwheat and knotweed. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white or pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
