Pinus monte-alleggri

Pinus monte-alleggri, commonly known as the Monte Allegri pine, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of southern Italy.

This coniferous tree is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. It produces cones that are woody and often remain attached to the branches for several years. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
