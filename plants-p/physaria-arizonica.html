Physaria arizonica

Physaria arizonica, commonly known as Arizona bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and distinctive seed pods. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often prostrate or ascending.

The species is also noted for its role in supporting local pollinators and its ecological importance in desert ecosystems.
