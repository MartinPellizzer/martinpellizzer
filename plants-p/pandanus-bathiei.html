Pandanus bathiei

Pandanus bathiei, commonly known as the Madagascar pandanus, is a member of the Pandanaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are typically borne at the base of the leaves. Its root system is fibrous and well-developed, allowing it to thrive in moist, sandy soils.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
