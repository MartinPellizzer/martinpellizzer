Pygeum robustum

Pygeum robustum, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by woody pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional African medicine for various health purposes.
