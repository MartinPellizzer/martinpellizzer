Poa schischkinii

Poa schischkinii, commonly known as the Schischkin's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green blades that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of small, spikelets that are arranged in a compact cluster.

Poa schischkinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
