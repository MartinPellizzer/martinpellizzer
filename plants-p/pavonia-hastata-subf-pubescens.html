Pavonia hastata subf. pubescens

Pavonia hastata subf.

pubescens, commonly known as the hairy pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in woodland areas.

The plant features showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, often covered with fine pubescence, giving it a soft, fuzzy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
