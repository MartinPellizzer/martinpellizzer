Prunella laciniata var. intermedia

Prunella laciniata var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate selfheal, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often serrated or toothed. The flowers are typically blue to purple, arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the stems, and bloom during the summer months. This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and healing qualities.
