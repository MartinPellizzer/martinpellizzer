Piper nottirbanum

Piper nottirbanum, commonly known as the Nott's pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by small, berry-like fruits.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, contributing to its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
