Pyrus sherriffii

Pyrus sherriffii, commonly known as Sherriff's pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a small, yellowish pear with a sweet, edible pulp. It is often found in moist, temperate forests and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
