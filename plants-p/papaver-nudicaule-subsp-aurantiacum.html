Papaver nudicaule subsp. aurantiacum

Papaver nudicaule subsp.

aurantiacum, commonly known as the golden poppy, is a striking member of the Papaveraceae family. It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is native to the western United States, particularly California, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. It is an annual herb with slender, upright stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
