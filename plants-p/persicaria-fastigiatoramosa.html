Persicaria fastigiatoramosa

Persicaria fastigiatoramosa, commonly known as the upright false smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that are often covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a slightly serrated edge, and they are typically arranged alternately along the stems. It produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late summer.

This plant thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other aquatic habitats.
