Plantago canescens var. turczaninowiana

Plantago canescens var. turczaninowiana, commonly known as the Turczaninow's plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
