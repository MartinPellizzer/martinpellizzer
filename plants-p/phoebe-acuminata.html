Phoebe acuminata

Phoebe acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf phoebe, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by dark blue-black berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
