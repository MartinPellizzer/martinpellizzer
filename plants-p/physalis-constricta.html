Physalis constricta

Physalis constricta, commonly known as the common ground cherry, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits enclosed in a papery calyx, which turns red when ripe. These fruits are edible and have a tangy, sweet flavor, often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found growing in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
