Pleuropteropyrum savatieri

Pleuropteropyrum savatieri, commonly known as the African sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that resemble those of the sunflower. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The African sunflower is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its ability to attract pollinators.
