Plectranthus tetragonus

Plectranthus tetragonus, commonly known as the square-stemmed plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, often in shades of purple or blue. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is widely grown as a decorative plant.
