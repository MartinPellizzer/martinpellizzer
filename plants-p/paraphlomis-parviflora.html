Paraphlomis parviflora

Paraphlomis parviflora, commonly known as small-flowered blueweed, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.
