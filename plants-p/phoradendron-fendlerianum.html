Phoradendron fendlerianum

Phoradendron fendlerianum, commonly known as the whip-poor-will vine, is a climbing plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the summer months. Its fruits are legumes containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is often found in dry, open woodlands and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.
