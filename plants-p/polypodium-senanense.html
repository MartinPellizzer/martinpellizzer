Polypodium senanense

Polypodium senanense, commonly known as the Senanense water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically divided into numerous small, serrated segments. This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Polypodium senanense prefers rich, organic soil and is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks in humid climates.

Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.
