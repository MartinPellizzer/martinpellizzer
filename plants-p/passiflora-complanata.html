Passiflora complanata

Passiflora complanata, commonly known as the flatleaf passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Passiflora complanata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
