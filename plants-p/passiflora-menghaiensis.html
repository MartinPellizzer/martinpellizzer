Passiflora menghaiensis

Passiflora menghaiensis, commonly known as Menghai passionflower, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that display a mix of blue, purple, and white hues. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
