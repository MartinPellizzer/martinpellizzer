Pyrus sieversii

Pyrus sieversii, commonly known as the Central Asian pear.

It is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia, characterized by its rough, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, yellow to brown fruits. It is closely related to the common pear and has been used in breeding programs to improve pear varieties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
