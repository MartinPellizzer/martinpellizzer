Picris prenanthoides

Picris prenanthoides, commonly known as yellow cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a creeping growth habit with hairy stems and serrated leaves. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The species is often found in disturbed areas and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
