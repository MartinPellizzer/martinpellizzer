Pilea symmeria var. subcoriacea

Pilea symmeria var. subcoriacea, commonly known as the velvet pilea, is a compact, trailing plant with thick, waxy leaves that have a textured, slightly fuzzy surface.

Its foliage is typically a deep green with a rich, velvety appearance, giving it a unique and ornamental look. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale green, which appear in clusters during the growing season. It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in bright, indirect light with moderate humidity.

This variety is prized for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
