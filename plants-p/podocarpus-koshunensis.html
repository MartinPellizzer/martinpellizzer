Podocarpus koshunensis

Podocarpus koshunensis, commonly known as the Koshun pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and distinctive cone structure. The plant is typically found in mountainous regions, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against environmental stressors.

Podocarpus koshunensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to forest biodiversity and soil stability.
