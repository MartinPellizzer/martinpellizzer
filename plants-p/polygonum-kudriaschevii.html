Polygonum kudriaschevii

Polygonum kudriaschevii, commonly known as the Siberian knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the colder regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Polygonum kudriaschevii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
