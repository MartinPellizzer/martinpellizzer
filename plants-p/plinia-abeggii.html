Plinia abeggii

Plinia abeggii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia abeggii*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil and is characterized by its small, oval leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to cherries, hence its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The plant is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and use in traditional remedies.
