Pilocereus claroviridis

Pilocereus claroviridis, commonly known as the White Star Cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems covered in white, tuberculate spines. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
