Prostanthera laricoides

Prostanthera laricoides, commonly known as the mountain mint bush, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, minty scent when crushed.

Prostanthera laricoides produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
