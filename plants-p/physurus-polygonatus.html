Physurus polygonatus

Physurus polygonatus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers indirect sunlight. Its leaves are typically velvety and dark green, while its flowers come in a range of colors including purple, blue, and white.

Physurus polygonatus is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
