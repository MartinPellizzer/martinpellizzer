Primula polyneura subsp. sataniensis

Primula polyneura subsp.

sataniensis, commonly known as the Satani's primrose, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China. It is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from pale yellow to deep orange.

The plant features hairy, ovate leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution and unique floral morphology, it is of interest to botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
