Piper macrorhizum

Piper macrorhizum, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a tropical flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes other species of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its roots are the primary part used, and they are known for their starchy, edible qualities.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
