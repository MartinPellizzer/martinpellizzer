Pleroma cristatum

Pleroma cristatum, commonly known as the crowned plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically violet or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches.

Pleroma cristatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
