Phytocrene macrophylla var. dasycarpa

Phytocrene macrophylla var. dasycarpa, commonly known as the hairy-fruited phytocrene, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in the spring. Its fruit is a distinctive, hairy capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphological features and potential ecological roles in its native habitat.
