Parageum × macranthum

Parageum × macranthum, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a hybrid species native to China.

It is characterized by its striking, inflated, orange-red calyces that resemble small lanterns, which persist through winter. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by bright red berries. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant.
