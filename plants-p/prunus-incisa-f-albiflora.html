Prunus incisa f. albiflora

Prunus incisa f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered Japanese apricot, is a botanical variant of the Japanese apricot species.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant is a small deciduous tree or shrub with oval, serrated leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy spring blossoms.
