Polycnemum arvense var. maximum

Polycnemum arvense var. maximum, commonly known as the greater field sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters. It is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in disturbed or cultivated soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.
