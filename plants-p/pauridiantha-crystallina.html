Pauridiantha crystallina

Pauridiantha crystallina, commonly known as the crystalline pauridiantha, is a unique plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, translucent leaves that display a glass-like appearance due to the presence of specialized cells. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season, adding to its ornamental value. Its growth habit is typically low and spreading, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and less commonly cultivated species.
