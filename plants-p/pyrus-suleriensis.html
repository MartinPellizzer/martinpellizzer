Pyrus suleriensis

Pyrus suleriensis, commonly known as the Sulerien pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in the Caucasus and parts of Turkey. The plant is characterized by its small, deciduous leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like berry, is typically yellow or orange when ripe and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

This species is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation in temperate climates.
