Plectranthus maximowiczii

Plectranthus maximowiczii, commonly known as Cape verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
