Parsonsia siamensis

Parsonsia siamensis, commonly known as the Siam lantern plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its striking, lantern-shaped flowers that are typically orange or red in color. The plant is evergreen, with glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are not only visually appealing but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
