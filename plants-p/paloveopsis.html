Paloveopsis

Paloveopsis, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that reflects sunlight, helping it conserve moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater and survive prolonged droughts.

Paloveopsis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
