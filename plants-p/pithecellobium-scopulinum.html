Pithecellobium scopulinum

Pithecellobium scopulinum, commonly known as the yellow pithecellobium, is a species of legume native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing plants.

The leaves are bipinnate, and the plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
