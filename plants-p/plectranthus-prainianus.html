Plectranthus prainianus

Plectranthus prainianus, commonly known as the Prain's plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple and blue. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus prainianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be used in gardens or as a houseplant.
