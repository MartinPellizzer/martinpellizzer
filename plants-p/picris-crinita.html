Picris crinita

Picris crinita, commonly known as the curlycup gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, often reddish stems. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive, rugged appearance.
