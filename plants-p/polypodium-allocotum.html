Polypodium allocotum

Polypodium allocotum, commonly known as the Mexican shield fern, is a species of fern native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizome is often covered in a dense layer of brownish or reddish scales.

Polypodium allocotum is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
