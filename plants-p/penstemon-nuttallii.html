Penstemon nuttallii

Penstemon nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces tall spikes of tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Penstemon nuttallii is also known for its drought tolerance and adaptability to rocky or gravelly habitats.
