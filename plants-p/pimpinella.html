Pimpinella

Pimpinella, commonly known as bishop's weed, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Apiaceae.

These plants are characterized by their finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers that often appear in umbels. They are native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa. Pimpinella species are often used in traditional medicine and some are cultivated as ornamental plants.

The seeds of certain species are also used as a spice in some cuisines.
