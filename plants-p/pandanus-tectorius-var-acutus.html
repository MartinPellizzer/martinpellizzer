Pandanus tectorius var. acutus

Pandanus tectorius var. acutus, commonly known as the sharp-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its narrow, pointed leaves that are typically arranged in a fan-like pattern. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear yellowish-white flowers. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional construction and thatching.

The plant thrives in coastal and wetland areas, adapting well to saline and humid environments.
