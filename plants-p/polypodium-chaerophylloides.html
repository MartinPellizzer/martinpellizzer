Polypodium chaerophylloides

Polypodium chaerophylloides, commonly known as the slender wall fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinnate fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on rock surfaces or in crevices. Its spores are produced in clusters on the undersides of the fronds, and it reproduces through spore dispersal rather than seeds.

This fern is relatively small in size but plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems.
