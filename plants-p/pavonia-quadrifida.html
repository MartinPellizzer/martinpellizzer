Pavonia quadrifida

Pavonia quadrifida, commonly known as the four-lobed sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided into four lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Pavonia quadrifida is often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
