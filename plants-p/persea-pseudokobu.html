Persea pseudokobu

Persea pseudokobu, commonly known as the false spondias, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocados, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and showy flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by edible, round berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy surface, often with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions for its aesthetic appeal.
