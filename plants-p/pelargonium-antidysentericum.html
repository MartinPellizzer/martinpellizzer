Pelargonium antidysentericum

Pelargonium antidysentericum, commonly known as the dysentery geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or red, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their antidiarrheal properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.
