Pseudolysimachion × medium

Pseudolysimachion × medium, commonly known as the hybrid sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland areas in eastern North America.

It features aromatic, strap-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found near water bodies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in wetland restoration projects.
