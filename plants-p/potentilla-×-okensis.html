Potentilla × okensis

Potentilla × okensis, commonly known as the Okanese cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically dark green and slightly serrated, providing a handsome ornamental appearance.

Potentilla × okensis is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
