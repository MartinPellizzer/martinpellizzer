Phillyrea medianifolia var. corsica

Phillyrea medianifolia var. corsica, commonly known as Corsican Phillyrea, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by small, woody fruits. It is adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy species.

Its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal areas make it a popular choice for landscaping.
