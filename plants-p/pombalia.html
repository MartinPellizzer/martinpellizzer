Pombalia

Pombalia, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement, adding to its ornamental appeal. Pombalia thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical name, *Pombalia*, reflects its striking visual appearance and cultural significance in its native regions.
