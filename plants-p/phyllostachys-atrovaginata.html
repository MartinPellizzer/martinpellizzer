Phyllostachys atrovaginata

Phyllostachys atrovaginata, commonly known as black-barked bamboo, is a species of grass in the Bambusoideae subfamily.

It is native to China and is characterized by its dark, almost black, bark that distinguishes it from other bamboo species. The plant typically grows as a clump-forming bamboo with tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic and structural features.
