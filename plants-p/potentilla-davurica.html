Potentilla davurica

Potentilla davurica, commonly known as the Siberian cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with multiple stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated edge, while its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla davurica is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates.
