Passiflora brachyantha

Passiflora brachyantha, commonly known as the creeping passionflower, is a species native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, colorful flowers with intricate structures, including a crown of filaments and a central corona. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its fruit, though less commonly consumed, is edible and has a mild flavor.
