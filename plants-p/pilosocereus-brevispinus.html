Pilosocereus brevispinus

Pilosocereus brevispinus, commonly known as the short-spined cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a low-growing, columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance characterized by its short, spiny stems. The plant typically features dense clusters of white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Pilosocereus brevispinus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
