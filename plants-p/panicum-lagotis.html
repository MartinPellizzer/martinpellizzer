Panicum lagotis

Panicum lagotis, commonly known as silver panicum, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and delicate, silver-gray leaves. The plant produces small, spikelike inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open fields and disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and graceful appearance.
