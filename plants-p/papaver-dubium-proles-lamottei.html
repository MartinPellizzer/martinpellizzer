Papaver dubium proles lamottei

Papaver dubium proles lamottei, commonly known as the Lamotte's poppy, is a rare and distinctive variant of the common poppy.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited geographic distribution.
