Picramnia gardneri subsp. gardneri

Picramnia gardneri subsp.

gardneri, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and thick, fissured bark that provides protection against fire and pests.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological importance and has been used traditionally for construction and medicinal purposes.
