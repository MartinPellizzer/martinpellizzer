Prunus pubigera var. prattii

Prunus pubigera var. prattii, commonly known as the Chinese bird cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a serrated margin and a glossy upper surface. The fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to temperate climates.
