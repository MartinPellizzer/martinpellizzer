Prunus avium f. nana

Prunus avium f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf peach, is a compact variety of the peach tree characterized by its smaller size and unique growth habits.

It features small, rounded leaves and produces sweet, flavorful fruits that are typically smaller than those of standard peaches. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, often used in ornamental gardening due to its compact form and attractive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal fruit production.

This cultivar is valued for its adaptability and is often grown in smaller spaces where space is limited.
