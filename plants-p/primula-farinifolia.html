Primula farinifolia

Primula farinifolia, commonly known as the Chinese primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, white, and yellow. The plant has ovate, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it its scientific name "farinifolia," which means "farina-like" or "flour-like.". It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The flowers typically bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to gardens and indoor spaces.
