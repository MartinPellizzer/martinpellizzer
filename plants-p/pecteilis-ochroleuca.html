Pecteilis ochroleuca

Pecteilis ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats like prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties, with its seeds containing oils used in traditional and modern herbal remedies.
