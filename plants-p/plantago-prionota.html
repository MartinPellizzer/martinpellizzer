Plantago prionota

Plantago prionota, commonly known as the narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in disturbed soils such as roadsides, fields, and gardens. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in a spike, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This species is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
