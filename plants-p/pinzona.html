Pinzona

Pinzona, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Central America and has been cultivated in various regions for its ornamental value. The plant features large, showy yellow-orange flowers with a prominent central disk, typically blooming from summer to fall. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Pinzona is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies to gardens.
