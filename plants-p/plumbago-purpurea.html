Plumbago purpurea

Plumbago purpurea, commonly known as blue plumbago, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of cobalt-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has slender, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Plumbago purpurea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
