Pinanga nenga

Pinanga nenga, commonly known as the giant bamboo or giant bamboo palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, feather-like leaves. The plant can grow up to 20 meters in height, making it one of the tallest bamboo species. Its rapid growth rate and ability to thrive in humid, warm climates make it a popular ornamental plant.

The plant's unique structure and aesthetic appeal contribute to its significance in both horticulture and ecological studies.
