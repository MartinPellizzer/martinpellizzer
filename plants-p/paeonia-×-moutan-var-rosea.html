Paeonia × moutan var. rosea

Paeonia × moutan var. rosea, commonly known as the wood garden peony, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, single or semi-double flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant produces large, palmately compound leaves with a serrated margin. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features include a robust root system and a long blooming period in spring.
