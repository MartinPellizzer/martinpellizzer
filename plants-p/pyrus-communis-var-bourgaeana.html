Pyrus communis var. bourgaeana

Pyrus communis var. bourgaeana, commonly known as the common pear, is a cultivar of the pear tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, sweet fruits. The fruit has a crisp, juicy texture with a mild, aromatic flavor.

It is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
