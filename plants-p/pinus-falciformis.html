Pinus falciformis

Pinus falciformis, commonly known as the Mexican falcate pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its long, slender needles that are typically arranged in bundles of five. The tree produces cones that are woody and elongated, with scales that open to release seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
