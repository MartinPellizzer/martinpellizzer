Potamogeton natans var. explanatus

Potamogeton natans var. explanatus, commonly known as the broad-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are broader than those of the typical variety, often reaching up to 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically found in clusters near the water's surface. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its habitat.
