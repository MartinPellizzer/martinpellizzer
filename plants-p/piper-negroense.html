Piper negroense

Piper negroense, commonly known as black pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are harvested for their aromatic seeds used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
