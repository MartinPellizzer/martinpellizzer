Phlox multiflora subsp. patula

Phlox multiflora subsp.

patula, commonly known as the Pennsylvania phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet and features showy clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has opposite, linear to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in partial to full shade and is well-suited for woodland gardens due to its ability to tolerate moist, well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
