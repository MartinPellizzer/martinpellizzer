Padus × laucheana

Padus × laucheana, commonly known as the common bird cherry.

It is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia. The plant features oval, glossy leaves that turn yellow in autumn, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring. Its fruits are red drupes that attract birds, aiding in seed dispersal.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in gardens and natural habitats.
