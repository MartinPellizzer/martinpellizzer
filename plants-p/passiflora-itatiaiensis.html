Passiflora itatiaiensis

Passiflora itatiaiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a striking climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that display a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and bracts. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are covered in fine, white hairs.

Passiflora itatiaiensis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring ample sunlight and well-drained soil.
