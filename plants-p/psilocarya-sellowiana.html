Psilocarya sellowiana

Psilocarya sellowiana, commonly known as the Sellow's psilocybe, is a species of psychedelic mushroom native to South America.

It belongs to the Psilocybe genus and is characterized by its cap, which ranges from convex to flat with a distinct umbo. The gills are closely spaced and typically pale to brown in color, while the stem is slender and often covered in a fibrous layer. This species contains psilocybin and psilocin, psychoactive compounds that induce hallucinogenic effects.

It is often found in humid, shaded environments, particularly in areas with decomposing wood.
