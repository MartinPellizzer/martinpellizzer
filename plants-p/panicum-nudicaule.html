Panicum nudicaule

Panicum nudicaule, commonly known as slender panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its botanical characteristics include narrow, linear leaves and a fibrous root system.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
