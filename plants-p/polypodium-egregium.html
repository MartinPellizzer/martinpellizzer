Polypodium egregium

Polypodium egregium, commonly known as the golden polypody, is a fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant typically grows on rocky surfaces or in shaded, moist environments, thriving in humid climates. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The golden polypody is valued both for its ornamental appearance and its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
