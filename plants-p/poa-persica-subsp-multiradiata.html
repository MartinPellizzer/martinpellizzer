Poa persica subsp. multiradiata

Poa persica subsp.

multiradiata, commonly known as the Persian grass or Persian meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscaping.
