Paepalanthus schlimii

Paepalanthus schlimii, commonly known as the Schlim's pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that resemble a pincushion. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in inflorescences at the tips of its stems. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent structure.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
