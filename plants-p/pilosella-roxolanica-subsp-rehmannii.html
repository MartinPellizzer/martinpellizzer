Pilosella roxolanica subsp. rehmannii

Pilosella roxolanica subsp.

rehmannii, commonly known as the alpine pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, composite heads.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually hairy and narrow, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is noted for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions, making it a common sight in high-altitude ecosystems.
