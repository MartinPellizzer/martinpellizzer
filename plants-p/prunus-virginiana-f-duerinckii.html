Prunus virginiana f. duerinckii

Prunus virginiana f. duerinckii, commonly known as the Duerinckii American plum, is a compact, ornamental form of the American plum.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and its showy clusters of white flowers that appear in early spring. The leaves are typically dark green and glossy, providing an attractive backdrop for the floral display. This variety is well-suited for small gardens or as a specimen plant due to its manageable size.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
