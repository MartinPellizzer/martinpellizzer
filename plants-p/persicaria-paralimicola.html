Persicaria paralimicola

Persicaria paralimicola, commonly known as the water smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It typically grows in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and streams, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Persicaria paralimicola plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
