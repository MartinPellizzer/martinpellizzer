Polygala latouchei

Polygala latouchei, commonly known as the Cape coralroot, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its unique, coral-like inflorescence. The plant is notable for its lack of chlorophyll, making it a hemiparasitic species that derives some nutrients from its host plants. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its specialized habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
