Phyllostachys bissetii f. denigrata

Phyllostachys bissetii f. denigrata, commonly known as the dark-stemmed bamboo, is a unique variant of the Bisset's bamboo species.

It is characterized by its dark green to almost black culms, which contrast sharply with its lighter-colored leaves. This ornamental bamboo is native to China and is often cultivated for its striking appearance in gardens and landscapes. The plant grows in a clumping form, producing slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 10 feet.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
