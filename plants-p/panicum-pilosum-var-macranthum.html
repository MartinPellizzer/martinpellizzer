Panicum pilosum var. macranthum

Panicum pilosum var. macranthum, commonly known as silky grass or purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silky foliage and produces showy, purple to pink plume-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native plant gardens.

Its growth habit is clump-forming, making it suitable for erosion control and wildlife habitat.
