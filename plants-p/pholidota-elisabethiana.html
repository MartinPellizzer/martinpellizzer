Pholidota elisabethiana

Pholidota elisabethiana, commonly known as the Elizabeth's flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its vibrant floral display.
