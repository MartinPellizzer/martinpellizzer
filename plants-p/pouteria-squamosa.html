Pouteria squamosa

Pouteria squamosa, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody pods containing edible seeds. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and has a dense canopy that provides habitat for various wildlife species. Its bark is rough and fissured, and it thrives in humid, nutrient-rich soils.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its nuts and timber.
