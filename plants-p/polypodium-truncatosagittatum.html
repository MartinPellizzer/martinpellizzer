Polypodium truncatosagittatum

Polypodium truncatosagittatum, commonly known as the arrowhead fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit with pinnately compound leaves that are typically arrow-shaped, giving it its common name. The fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with spore-producing structures called sori located on the underside of the leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant's botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and recognizable member of the Polypodiaceae family.
