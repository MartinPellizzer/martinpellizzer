Pleurostylia pachyphloea

Pleurostylia pachyphloea, commonly known as the thick-stemmed pleurostylia, is a unique plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, a feature adapted to its often arid or seasonally dry habitat. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale in color, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its leaves are usually narrow and succulent, further contributing to its drought tolerance.

Due to its specialized habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
