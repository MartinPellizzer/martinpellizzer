Piper sanctum

Piper sanctum, commonly known as sacred pepper, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are peppery and used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it a unique subject of study in both horticulture and ethnobotany.
