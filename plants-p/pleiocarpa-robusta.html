Pleiocarpa robusta

Pleiocarpa robusta, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to fleshy, orange-red berries. These berries are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor, often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to different environments.
