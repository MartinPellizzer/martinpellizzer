Pyrus expansa

Pyrus expansa, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, yellowish-green fruits that ripen in autumn. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.

Pyrus expansa prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.
