Pelargonium acerifolium

Pelargonium acerifolium, commonly known as the maple-leaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive maple-like leaves that are deeply lobed and have a vibrant green color. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and blooms.
