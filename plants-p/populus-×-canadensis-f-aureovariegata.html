Populus × canadensis f. aureovariegata

Populus × canadensis f. aureovariegata, commonly known as the golden-variegated poplar, is a hybrid species within the Populus genus.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display a mix of yellow, white, and green hues. This cultivar is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The tree has a fast growth rate and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

Its leaves are alternate and heart-shaped, with a smooth margin and a prominent midrib.
