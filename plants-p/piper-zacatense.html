Piper zacatense

Piper zacatense, commonly known as the Mexican pepper or Mexican long pepper, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often glandular-dotted. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by elongated, slender fruits that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
