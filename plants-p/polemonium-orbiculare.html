Polemonium orbiculare

Polemonium orbiculare, commonly known as the showy lady's slipper, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
