Pastinaca pachyrhiza

Pastinaca pachyrhiza, commonly known as the broad-leaved scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features robust, fleshy roots that are often harvested for culinary use. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent. It produces yellow flowers in the summer, which are followed by small, round, brown seeds.

This species is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its historical use in traditional medicine.
