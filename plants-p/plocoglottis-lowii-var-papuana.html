Plocoglottis lowii var. papuana

Plocoglottis lowii var. papuana, commonly known as the Papuan spider-orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the rainforests of Papua, Indonesia.

It is characterized by its intricate, spider-like inflorescence and vibrant, often purple or pink, flower colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, adapted to capture moisture from the air.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
