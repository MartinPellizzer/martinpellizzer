Pilosella sysolskiensis

Pilosella sysolskiensis, commonly known as the Sysolski's pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant is known for its dense, woolly foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance and helps it retain moisture in arid environments. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Pilosella sysolskiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, long-lasting blooms.
