Polystachya beccarii

Polystachya beccarii, commonly known as the Beccari's spikegrass, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall appearance.

Polystachya beccarii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
