Passiflora cumbalensis var. cumbalensis

Passiflora cumbalensis var. cumbalensis, commonly known as the Cumbal passionflower, is a tropical climbing vine native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that display a blend of violet, yellow, and white hues, with prominent filaments and a crown-like structure. The plant produces edible berries that are typically orange to red in color, containing a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
