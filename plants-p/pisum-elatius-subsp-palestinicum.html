Pisum elatius subsp. palestinicum

Pisum elatius subsp.

palestinicum, commonly known as the Palestinian vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of the Middle East. It is a leguminous species characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.

The seeds are typically small and oval, often used as a traditional food source in some regions.
