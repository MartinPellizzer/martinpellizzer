Pelargonium pilosellifolium

Pelargonium pilosellifolium, commonly known as the woollyleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is widely cultivated in gardens around the world.
