Phlox intermedia

Phlox intermedia, commonly known as the common phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing a profusion of flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of downy hair.

Phlox intermedia thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
