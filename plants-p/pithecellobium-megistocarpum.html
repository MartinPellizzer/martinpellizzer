Pithecellobium megistocarpum

Pithecellobium megistocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces large, woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisine.

It is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment and reforestation efforts.
