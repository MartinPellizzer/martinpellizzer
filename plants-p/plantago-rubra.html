Plantago rubra

Plantago rubra, commonly known as red plantain, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in the summer months. Its seeds are enclosed in a distinctive, inflated, three-lobed capsule, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

Plantago rubra is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
