Pectis multiseta var. ambigua

Pectis multiseta var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny stems. The plant features gray-green, hairy leaves that are often lobed or toothed, providing a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant traits.
