Pedicularis bosniaca

Pedicularis bosniaca, commonly known as the Bosnian lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the genus Pedicularis, which is characterized by its unique spurred petals that resemble the shape of a louse. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in rocky and well-drained soils. Its flowers, which range in color from yellow to orange, are pollinated by insects such as bees and flies.

Pedicularis bosniaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
