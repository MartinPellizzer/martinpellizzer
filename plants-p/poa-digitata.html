Poa digitata

Poa digitata, commonly known as the finger grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and distinctive, finger-like inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spikelet-like flowers.

Poa digitata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
