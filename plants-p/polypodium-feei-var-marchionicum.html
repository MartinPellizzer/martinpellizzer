Polypodium feei var. marchionicum

Polypodium feei var. marchionicum, commonly known as the Marchionni's water fern, is a rare and distinctive variant of the polypody fern.

It is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lanceolate fronds with a unique, finely divided structure. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are closely clustered on the underside of the leaves.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
