Ptarmica macrophylla

Ptarmica macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Ptarmica macrophylla thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and as a nectar source for pollinators.
