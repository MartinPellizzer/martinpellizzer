Paspalum subarticulatum

Paspalum subarticulatum, commonly known as the slender paspalum, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

Paspalum subarticulatum is adapted to tolerate periodic flooding, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
