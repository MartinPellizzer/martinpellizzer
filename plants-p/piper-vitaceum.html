Piper vitaceum

Piper vitaceum, commonly known as the creeping boisais, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, fleshy berries.

Piper vitaceum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
