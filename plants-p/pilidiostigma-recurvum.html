Pilidiostigma recurvum

Pilidiostigma recurvum, commonly known as the African star grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its fibrous roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Pilidiostigma recurvum is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
