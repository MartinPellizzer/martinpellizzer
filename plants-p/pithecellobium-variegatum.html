Pithecellobium variegatum

Pithecellobium variegatum, commonly known as the variable legume or variegated pithecellobium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with a feathery appearance, and the fruit is a slender, woody pod containing several seeds.
