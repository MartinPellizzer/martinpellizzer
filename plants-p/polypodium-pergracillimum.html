Polypodium pergracillimum

Polypodium pergracillimum, commonly known as the slender holly fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on rocky surfaces or in dense forest understories. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
