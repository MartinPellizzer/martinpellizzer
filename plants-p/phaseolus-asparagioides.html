Phaseolus asparagioides

Phaseolus asparagioides, commonly known as the asparagus bean, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds used in various cuisines. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it both a valuable agricultural crop and an interesting subject for botanical study.
