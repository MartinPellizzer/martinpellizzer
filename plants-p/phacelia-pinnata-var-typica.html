Phacelia pinnata var. typica

Phacelia pinnata var. typica, commonly known as the fringed phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are pinnately divided, giving the plant a delicate, feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
