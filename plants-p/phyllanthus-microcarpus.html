Phyllanthus microcarpus

Phyllanthus microcarpus, commonly known as the dwarf gooseberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It features dense, dark green foliage with small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Phyllanthus microcarpus is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
