Parodia succinea

Parodia succinea, commonly known as the "Cushion Cactus," is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and short, cylindrical stems that form a dense, cushion-like structure. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
