Picea obovata var. schrenkiana

Picea obovata var. schrenkiana, commonly known as the Schrenk's white spruce, is a variant of the white spruce native to Central Asia.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The foliage is typically arranged in spirals along the branches, and the cones are small and woody. This species thrives in cold, mountainous climates and is adapted to well-drained, acidic soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
