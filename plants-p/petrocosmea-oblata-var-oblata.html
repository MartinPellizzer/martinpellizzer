Petrocosmea oblata var. oblata

Petrocosmea oblata var. oblata, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by insects.

Petrocosmea oblata var. oblata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to grow in challenging environments.
