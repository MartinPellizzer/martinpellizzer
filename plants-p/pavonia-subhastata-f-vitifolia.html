Pavonia subhastata f. vitifolia

Pavonia subhastata f. vitifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf pavonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, often silver- or gray-hairy stems. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a distinctive silver or whitish underside, giving the plant its common name. Its flowers are usually pink to red, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches.

This plant is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and blooms.
