Phaius vestitus

Phaius vestitus, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as swampy forests. It has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, aiding its survival in moist habitats.

Phaius vestitus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
