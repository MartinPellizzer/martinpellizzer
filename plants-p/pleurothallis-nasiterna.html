Pleurothallis nasiterna

Pleurothallis nasiterna, commonly known as the Ecuadorian orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air.

Its flowers are usually arranged in a spike and bloom during the wet season, making it a notable species in its native habitat.
