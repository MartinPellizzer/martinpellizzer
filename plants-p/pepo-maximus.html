Pepo maximus

Pepo maximus, commonly known as the giant pepo, is a striking member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, thick-walled fruits that typically mature to a vibrant yellow or orange hue. The plant produces sprawling vines with large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are often used in ornamental gardening due to their eye-catching appearance.

However, it is important to note that the plant is not edible and can be toxic in certain parts.
