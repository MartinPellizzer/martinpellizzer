Primula globifera

Primula globifera, commonly known as the globular primrose, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

Primula globifera is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
