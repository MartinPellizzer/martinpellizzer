Poa sterilis var. versicolor

Poa sterilis var. versicolor, commonly known as the variable meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including sandy and loamy soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its foliage is typically green to bluish-green, and it exhibits a range of color variations in its flowers, giving it its common name.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
