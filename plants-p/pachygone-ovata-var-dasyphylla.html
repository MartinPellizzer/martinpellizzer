Pachygone ovata var. dasyphylla

Pachygone ovata var. dasyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leafed ovate plant, is a variant of the Pachygone ovata species characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves.

It is native to specific regions in South America, where it thrives in humid, tropical conditions. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with dark green, ovate leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in shades of yellow or green.

This variant is valued for its unique foliage and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in warm climates.
