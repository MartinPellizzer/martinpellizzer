Pasania lamponga

Pasania lamponga, commonly known as the red-flowered pasania, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Pasania lamponga is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
