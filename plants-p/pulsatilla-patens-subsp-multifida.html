Pulsatilla patens subsp. multifida

Pulsatilla patens subsp.

multifida, commonly known as the common pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms with numerous yellow stamens.

The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, lobed leaves that are often divided into several segments. Its flowers typically have five to seven petals and are arranged in a cup-like structure. Pulsatilla patens subsp.

multifida is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
