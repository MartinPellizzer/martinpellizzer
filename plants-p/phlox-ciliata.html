Phlox ciliata

Phlox ciliata, commonly known as the pink phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces fragrant blooms in the early to mid-summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

Phlox ciliata thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.
