Primula sibirica var. integerrima

Primula sibirica var. integerrima, commonly known as the Siberian primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features rosettes of basal, hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, moist soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
