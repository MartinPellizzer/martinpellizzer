Prunus demissa f. hemitricha

Prunus demissa f. hemitricha, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or Japanese dwarf cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to Japan.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically a glossy dark green, with a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces delicate, fragrant white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a specimen plant.

Its compact size and seasonal blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
