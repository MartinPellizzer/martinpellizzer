Piper pennellii

Piper pennellii, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces tiny, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into small, rounded berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
