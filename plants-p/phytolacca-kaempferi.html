Phytolacca kaempferi

Phytolacca kaempferi, commonly known as the Japanese pokeweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 2 meters tall, covered with sticky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a waxy texture with a slightly bitter taste.

It is often found in disturbed soils and thrives in moist, temperate climates.
