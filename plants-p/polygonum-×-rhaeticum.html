Polygonum × rhaeticum

Polygonum × rhaeticum, commonly known as the red polygonum, is a hybrid species belonging to the buckwheat family, Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its striking reddish stems and leaves, which are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, producing both male and female parts.

Polygonum × rhaeticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
