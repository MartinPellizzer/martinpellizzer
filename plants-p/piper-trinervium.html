Piper trinervium

Piper trinervium, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with three main veins, giving it its scientific name "trinervium.". Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
