Podospermum intermedium

Podospermum intermedium, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing shrub that produces clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers, which bloom throughout the year. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy undersides and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Podospermum intermedium is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
