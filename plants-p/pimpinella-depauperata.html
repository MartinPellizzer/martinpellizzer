Pimpinella depauperata

Pimpinella depauperata, commonly known as the poor man's parsley, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves.

Its aromatic leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
