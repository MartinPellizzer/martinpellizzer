Panicum acuminatifolium

Panicum acuminatifolium, commonly known as little bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, blue-green leaves that turn bronze in the fall. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically tan to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie ecosystems.

Panicum acuminatifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native landscapes.
