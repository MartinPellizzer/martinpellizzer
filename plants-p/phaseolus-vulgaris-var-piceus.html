Phaseolus vulgaris var. piceus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. piceus, commonly known as the dark green bean, is a variety of common bean characterized by its deep green foliage and dark green pods.

It is native to the Americas and is cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with compound leaves and small white or pale purple flowers. It thrives in warm climates and well-drained soil, requiring full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is often used in traditional cuisines and is valued for its nutritional content and adaptability to various growing conditions.
