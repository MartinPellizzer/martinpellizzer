Pseudoliparis graminifolia

Pseudoliparis graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-like sea anemone, is a unique marine invertebrate that exhibits a plant-like appearance.

It belongs to the order Antennarioidea and is characterized by its leaf-like structures that resemble grass blades. These structures are actually modified tentacles used for capturing prey in its aquatic environment. The plant-like morphology is an evolutionary adaptation that helps it blend with its surroundings.

Despite its appearance, it is not a true plant but a cnidarian with complex biological features.
