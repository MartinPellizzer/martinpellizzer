Parodia nana

Parodia nana, commonly known as the dwarf moon cactus, is a compact cactus species native to South America.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with a thick, fleshy body that stores water, typical of cacti. The plant produces small, white flowers during the warmer months, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help protect it from herbivores and reduce water loss.

Parodia nana is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and unique aesthetic.
