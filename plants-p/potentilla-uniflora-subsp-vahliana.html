Potentilla uniflora subsp. vahliana

Potentilla uniflora subsp.

vahliana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with wiry stems and small, narrow leaves.

The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its hardiness, it is often used in xeriscaping and as a ground cover in dry landscapes.
