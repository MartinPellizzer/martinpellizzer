Pterygota alata var. irregularis

Pterygota alata var. irregularis, commonly known as the irregular-winged pterygota, is a rare and distinctive plant species characterized by its uniquely shaped leaves.

The plant exhibits a complex arrangement of lobed and serrated margins, which contribute to its striking visual appearance. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence that aids in water retention and light reflection.

The species is noted for its limited geographic distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
