Paepalanthus multicapitatus

Paepalanthus multicapitatus, commonly known as the many-headed pincushion cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny clusters of stems that form a spherical or cushion-like shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green, spiny structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated in arid gardens for ornamental purposes.
