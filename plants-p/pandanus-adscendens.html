Pandanus adscendens

Pandanus adscendens, commonly known as the bottlebrush palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and its distinctive, feather-like leaves that form a dense crown. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in long, cylindrical inflorescences, giving it its common name. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional crafts, while its leaves are utilized in thatching and basket weaving.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is well adapted to saline and humid environments.
