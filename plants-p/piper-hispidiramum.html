Piper hispidiramum

Piper hispidiramum, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruits are small, berry-like structures that mature to a dark purple color.

The plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in humid, shaded environments.
