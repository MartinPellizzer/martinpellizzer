Piper transpontinum var. ecordulatum

Piper transpontinum var. ecordulatum, commonly known as the West Indian pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, peppery leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically harvested when ripe for use in culinary or medicinal applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both traditional medicine and local cuisine.
