Peltopsis perfoliata

Peltopsis perfoliata, commonly known as the perfoliate peltopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, often lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features slender stems with whorled leaves that often appear to be perforated, giving it its common name. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in the summer and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
