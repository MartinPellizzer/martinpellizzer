Piper murrayanum

Piper murrayanum, commonly known as Murray's pepper, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing the aromatic compound responsible for its characteristic spicy flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique flavor profile, it is occasionally used in traditional cuisines and herbal remedies.
