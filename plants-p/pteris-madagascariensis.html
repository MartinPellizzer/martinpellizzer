Pteris madagascariensis

Pteris madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar water fern, is a species of fern native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is well adapted to humid climates. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Pteris madagascariensis is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
