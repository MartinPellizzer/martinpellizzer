Piper orenocanum var. koriaboense

Piper orenocanum var. koriaboense, commonly known as Koriabo pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the Amazonian regions of Peru and Ecuador.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, elongated leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces small, reddish-brown berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
