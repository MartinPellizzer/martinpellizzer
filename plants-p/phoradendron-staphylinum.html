Phoradendron staphylinum

Phoradendron staphylinum, commonly known as the silverleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the Americas.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using the sunlight it captures. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that are dispersed by birds. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, and it blooms in the spring.

The silverleaf mistletoe plays a role in ecological systems by providing food for birds and insects, and its presence can influence the health of its host trees.
