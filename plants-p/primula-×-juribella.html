Primula × juribella

Primula × juribella, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a perennial flowering plant that belongs to the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically produces showy blooms in early spring, making it a popular choice for gardeners. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, thriving in cool climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, Primula × juribella is widely grown in gardens and botanical collections.
