Peperomia liebmannii

Peperomia liebmannii, commonly known as the Liebmann's peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, often with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or red, clustered in dense spikes at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
