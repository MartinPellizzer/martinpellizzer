Piptadenia hassleriana

Piptadenia hassleriana, commonly known as the yellow wood or *pau branco*, is a leguminous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and large, compound leaves that are typically alternate. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by long, pod-like fruits containing flat, elliptical seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Piptadenia hassleriana is valued for its timber and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
