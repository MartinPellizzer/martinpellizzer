Polycarpaea corymbosa var. aurea

Polycarpaea corymbosa var. aurea, commonly known as the golden polycarpaea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that bloom in clusters, making it visually striking. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
