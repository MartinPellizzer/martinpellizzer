Primula apicicallosa

Primula apicicallosa, commonly known as the apicicallosa primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its high-altitude environment.

Primula apicicallosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
