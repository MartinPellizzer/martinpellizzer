Piper leucanthum

Piper leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered pepper plant, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, heart-shaped leaves, and small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to yellow when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
