Premna benthamiana

Premna benthamiana, commonly known as the Bentham's premna, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its foliage is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
