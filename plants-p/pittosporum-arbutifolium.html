Pittosporum arbutifolium

Pittosporum arbutifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters, typically in spring. These flowers are followed by small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
