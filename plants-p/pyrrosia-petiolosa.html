Pyrrosia petiolosa

Pyrrosia petiolosa, commonly known as the rock fern or Japanese rock fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and is often found growing on rock surfaces or in shaded, humid environments. The plant has leathery, dark green fronds with a distinctive, upright growth habit. It produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves.

Pyrrosia petiolosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
