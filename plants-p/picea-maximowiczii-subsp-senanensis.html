Picea maximowiczii subsp. senanensis

Picea maximowiczii subsp.

senanensis, commonly known as the Korean fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Korean Peninsula. It is characterized by its conical shape, dense foliage, and needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in spirals.

The tree produces small, cylindrical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and landscapes.
