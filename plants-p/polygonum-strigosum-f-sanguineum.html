Polygonum strigosum f. sanguineum

Polygonum strigosum f. sanguineum, commonly known as the red-stemmed knotweed, is a variant of the common knotweed species.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is found in temperate regions. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, three-lobed berries.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its unique appearance and hardy nature.
