Pinalia tenuiflora

Pinalia tenuiflora, commonly known as the narrow-flower pincushion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with thick, fleshy leaves that store water. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Pinalia tenuiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
