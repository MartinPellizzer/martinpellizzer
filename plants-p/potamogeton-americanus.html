Potamogeton americanus

Potamogeton americanus, commonly known as American pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has long, narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or just at the water surface. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, rivers, and ponds.

American pondweed plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
