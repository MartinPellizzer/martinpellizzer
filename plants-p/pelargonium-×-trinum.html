Pelargonium × trinum

Pelargonium × trinum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Pelargonium × trinum is popular in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and continuous blooming throughout the growing season.
