Picroxylon siamense

Picroxylon siamense, commonly known as Siam sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant wood. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by round, woody fruits.

The bark is thick and fissured, and the tree is often cultivated for its valuable timber and essential oils.
