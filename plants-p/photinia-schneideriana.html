Photinia schneideriana

Photinia schneideriana, commonly known as the Schneider's photinia, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its attractive, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes.

Photinia schneideriana thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
