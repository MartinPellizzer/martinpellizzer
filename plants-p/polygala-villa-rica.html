Polygala villa-rica

Polygala villa-rica, commonly known as the velvet tassel or velvet flower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its striking, spiny, purple to violet flowers that resemble tassels. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for dry, coastal environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine, velvety texture, contributing to its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
