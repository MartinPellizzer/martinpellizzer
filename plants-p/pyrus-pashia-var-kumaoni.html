Pyrus pashia var. kumaoni

Pyrus pashia var. kumaoni, commonly known as Kumaon pear, is a variant of the Pyrus pashia species native to the Himalayan regions of India.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows at high altitudes, often in moist, temperate climates. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, sweet fruits in the summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

The tree is valued not only for its fruit but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
