Pelargonium × pallidulum

Pelargonium × pallidulum, commonly known as the pale geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically rounded or heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant for gardens and hanging baskets due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
