Phaeanthus

Phaeanthus, commonly known as the flame flower, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in areas with warm, humid climates. These plants are often cultivated for their ornamental value and thrive in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.
