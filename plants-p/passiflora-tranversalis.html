Passiflora tranversalis

Passiflora tranversalis, commonly known as the Transverse Passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are rich in nutrients and often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Passiflora tranversalis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
