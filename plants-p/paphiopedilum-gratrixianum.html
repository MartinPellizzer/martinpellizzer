Paphiopedilum gratrixianum

Paphiopedilum gratrixianum, commonly known as the Gratrixian lady's slipper, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety dark purple petals and a distinctive pouch-shaped lip that resembles a slipper. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, producing a fragrant display that attracts pollinators.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
