Paspalum glaucescens

Paspalum glaucescens, commonly known as blue paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Paspalum glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
