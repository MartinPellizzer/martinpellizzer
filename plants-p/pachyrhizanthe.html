Pachyrhizanthe

Pachyrhizanthe, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are segmented and often display vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant is popular as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in indoor conditions with proper light and watering. It produces flowers during the winter months, typically around Christmas, hence its common name.

Pachyrhizanthe is also notable for its ability to store water in its stems, allowing it to survive in arid environments.
