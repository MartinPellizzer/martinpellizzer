Pasaccardoa dicomoides

Pasaccardoa dicomoides, commonly known as the two-spored cup fungus, is a small, saprophytic fungus found in forested areas.

It belongs to the family Trichophaeaceae and is characterized by its cup-shaped fruiting body with a smooth, pale to yellowish cap. Due to its specialized habitat, it is relatively rare and often overlooked in mycological studies.
