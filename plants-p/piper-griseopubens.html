Piper griseopubens

Piper griseopubens, commonly known as the gray-hairy pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, heart-shaped leaves with a grayish pubescence. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

Piper griseopubens is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
