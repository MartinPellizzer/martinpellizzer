Paratropia longifolia var. incurva

Paratropia longifolia var. incurva, commonly known as the curved-leaved paratropia, is a unique variant of the paratropia genus native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, recurved leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, thriving in humid tropical climates. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a pale green or yellowish hue.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
