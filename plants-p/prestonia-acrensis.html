Prestonia acrensis

Prestonia acrensis, commonly known as the Jamaican trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red. The plant has woody stems and produces milky sap, which is a common trait among species in its family. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth texture, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Prestonia acrensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
