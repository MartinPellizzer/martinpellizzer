Plantago lubenensis

Plantago lubenensis, commonly known as the Bulgarian plantain, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Europe.

It features rosettes of broad, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a dense spike, which are borne on long, slender stems. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its medicinal and ornamental value.
