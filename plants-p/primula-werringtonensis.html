Primula werringtonensis

Primula werringtonensis, commonly known as the Werrington primrose, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing pink or white petals with yellow stamens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
