Penstemon pseudoparvus

Penstemon pseudoparvus, commonly known as the plains penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the central and western United States.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant purple or red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its adaptability to arid environments and low maintenance requirements contribute to its ecological and horticultural significance.
