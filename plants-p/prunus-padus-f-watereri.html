Prunus padus f. watereri

Prunus padus f. watereri, commonly known as the water elm or water peach, is a rare and ornamental variant of the common bird cherry.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that are often more slender and pointed compared to the typical species. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by edible drupes that are similar in appearance to cherries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
