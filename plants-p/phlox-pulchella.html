Phlox pulchella

Phlox pulchella, commonly known as the showy phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Phlox pulchella is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and hardiness in temperate climates.
