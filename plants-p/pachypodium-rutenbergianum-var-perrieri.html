Pachypodium rutenbergianum var. perrieri

Pachypodium rutenbergianum var. perrieri, commonly known as the Madagascar moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and spiny, palm-like appearance. The plant has a unique structure with a swollen base that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its leaves are typically small and may be absent during periods of drought.

It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
