Portulaca pilosa subsp. papulosa

Portulaca pilosa subsp.

papulosa, commonly known as the hairy portulaca, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas. It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the morning.

The plant has a prostrate growth habit, often forming dense mats on the ground. Its stems and leaves are covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a distinctive textured appearance.

It is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
