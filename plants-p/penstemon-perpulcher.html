Penstemon perpulcher

Penstemon perpulcher, commonly known as the showy penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscapes. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed.

Penstemon perpulcher is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
