Panicum cayennense var. divaricatum

Panicum cayennense var. divaricatum, commonly known as the Cayenne panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of South America.

It features tall, slender stalks that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental or ecological landscaping.

Its rapid growth and drought tolerance make it a resilient choice for certain environments.
