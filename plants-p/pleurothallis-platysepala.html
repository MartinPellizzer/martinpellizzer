Pleurothallis platysepala

Pleurothallis platysepala, commonly known as the flat-lipped pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its flowers are characterized by their flat, spurred petals and are often arranged in clusters.

Pleurothallis platysepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
