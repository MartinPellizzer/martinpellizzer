Panicum torreyanum

Panicum torreyanum, commonly known as Torrey's panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soils. The plant has slender, tufted spikes of green to brownish-gray inflorescences, which are characteristic of the grass family Poaceae. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
