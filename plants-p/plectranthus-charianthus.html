Plectranthus charianthus

Plectranthus charianthus, commonly known as the Cape verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The leaves are typically hairy and have a mild, mint-like scent when crushed.
