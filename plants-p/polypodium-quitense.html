Polypodium quitense

Polypodium quitense, commonly known as the Andean shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its leathery, dark green fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocky surfaces, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

Polypodium quitense plays an important role in the ecological balance of its habitat by providing shelter and food for various small organisms.
