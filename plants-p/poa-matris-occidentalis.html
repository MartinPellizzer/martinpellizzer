Poa matris-occidentalis

Poa matris-occidentalis, commonly known as the western matrix grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
