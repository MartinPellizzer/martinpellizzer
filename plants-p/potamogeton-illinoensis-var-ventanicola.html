Potamogeton illinoensis var. ventanicola

Potamogeton illinoensis var. ventanicola, commonly known as the Illinois pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to the central United States.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various waterfowl and invertebrates.
