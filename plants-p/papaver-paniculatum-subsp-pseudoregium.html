Papaver paniculatum subsp. pseudoregium

Papaver paniculatum subsp.

pseudoregium, commonly known as the Italian poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, often with a dark center.

The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and wildflower gardens.
