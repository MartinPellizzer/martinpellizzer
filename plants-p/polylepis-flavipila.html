Polylepis flavipila

Polylepis flavipila, commonly known as the yellow polylepis, is a species of tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, deciduous leaves and showy, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, typically at elevations between 2,500 and 4,000 meters. Its bark is thick and flaky, providing protection against harsh weather conditions.

Polylepis flavipila plays an important ecological role in its habitat, supporting various bird and insect species.
