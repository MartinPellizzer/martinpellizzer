Polemonium caucasicum

Polemonium caucasicum, commonly known as Caucasian Jacob's ladder, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the family Polemoniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, cascading clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
