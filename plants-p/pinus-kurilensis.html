Pinus kurilensis

Pinus kurilensis, commonly known as the Kuril pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the northern Pacific islands and parts of eastern Russia.

It typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to cool, moist climates. The tree has needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in bundles of five. Its cones are woody and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its timber and plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
