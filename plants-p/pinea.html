Pinea

Pinea, commonly known as the pineapple plant, is a tropical species belonging to the Bromeliaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a dense, water-holding structure at the center. The plant produces a spiky, fleshy fruit that contains numerous seeds. Pinea thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it both an ornamental and a commercially important crop.
