Piper crassum

Piper crassum, commonly known as the broad-leaved pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
