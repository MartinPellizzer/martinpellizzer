Pyrus voronovii

Pyrus voronovii, commonly known as the Voronov's pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value due to its showy white flowers and colorful autumn foliage. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to pears but are often used for ornamental purposes rather than consumption. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable addition to gardens and landscapes.

Pyrus voronovii is also valued for its adaptability and resilience in various climatic conditions.
