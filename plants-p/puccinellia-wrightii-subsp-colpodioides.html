Puccinellia wrightii subsp. colpodioides

Puccinellia wrightii subsp.

colpodioides, commonly known as Wright's puccinellia, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It typically grows in saline or alkaline soils, often found in desert environments.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to withstand drought and high salinity, making it a hardy species in challenging conditions.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
