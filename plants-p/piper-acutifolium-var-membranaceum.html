Piper acutifolium var. membranaceum

Piper acutifolium var. membranaceum, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant or velvetleaf pepper, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive dark green color. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright red, peppercorn-like berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of medicinal compounds.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for humid, warm climates and partial shade.
