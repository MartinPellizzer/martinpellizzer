Pachychaeta eupatorioides

Pachychaeta eupatorioides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of small blue flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
