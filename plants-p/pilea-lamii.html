Pilea lamii

Pilea lamii, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features rosettes of glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are often referred to as "Chinese coins" due to their appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits. It is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is popular as a houseplant.

Pilea lamii is also valued for its air-purifying properties and ease of care.
