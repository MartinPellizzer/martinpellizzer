Paronychia anatolica subsp. anatolica

Paronychia anatolica subsp.

anatolica, commonly known as Anatolian sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Turkey. It is a low-growing perennial herb with fleshy, succulent leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
