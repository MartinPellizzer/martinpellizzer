Patagonium bracteatum

Patagonium bracteatum, commonly known as the Patagonian bract fern, is a species of fern native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing clusters, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Patagonium bracteatum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
