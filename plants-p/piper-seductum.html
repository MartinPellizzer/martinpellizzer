Piper seductum

Piper seductum, commonly known as the seductive pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, green, bell-shaped fruits that turn red when mature. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, pungent aroma.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of unique spices.
