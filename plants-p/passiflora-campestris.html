Passiflora campestris

Passiflora campestris, commonly known as the common passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a unique structure with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and can grow up to several meters in length.

Passiflora campestris is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and ecological value.
