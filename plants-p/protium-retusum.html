Protium retusum

Protium retusum, commonly known as the rough-leafed tamanu, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically rough in texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in local medicine for their purported healing properties.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in tropical forests.
