Pectis gardneri

Pectis gardneri, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as small, waxy leaves to reduce water loss. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, and it produces wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its survival in harsh conditions.

Pectis gardneri is often found in sandy soils and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
