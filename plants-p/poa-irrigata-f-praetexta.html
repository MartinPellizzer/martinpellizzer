Poa irrigata f. praetexta

Poa irrigata f. praetexta, commonly known as the purple false bentgrass, is a variant of the false bentgrass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green foliage with a distinct purple tinge along the leaf edges, which becomes more pronounced under certain environmental conditions. This grass thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks. Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping and erosion control.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.
