Pandanus tectorius var. sanderi

Pandanus tectorius var. sanderi, commonly known as the sanders' pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the Pandanaceae family.

It is native to the Pacific Islands and is characterized by its tall, slender leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green, arranged in a dense inflorescence. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often growing near the shoreline.

The leaves are fibrous and have been traditionally used for thatching and weaving.
