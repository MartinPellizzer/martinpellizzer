Phyllanthus baccatus

Phyllanthus baccatus, commonly known as the sacred fig or Indian date tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in medicine and as a food source.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its resilience to drought.
