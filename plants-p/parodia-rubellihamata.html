Parodia rubellihamata

Parodia rubellihamata, commonly known as the Red-veined Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a layer of white, waxy hair, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its root system is shallow and adapted to absorb moisture quickly from infrequent rains.

Parodia rubellihamata is well-suited for cultivation in dry, well-drained soils and requires minimal care once established.
