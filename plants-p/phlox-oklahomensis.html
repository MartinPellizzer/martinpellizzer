Phlox oklahomensis

Phlox oklahomensis, commonly known as the Oklahoma phlox, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is a member of the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. It produces erect stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and blooms in late spring to early summer.

Phlox oklahomensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
