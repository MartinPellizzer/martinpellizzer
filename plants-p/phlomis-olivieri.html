Phlomis olivieri

Phlomis olivieri, commonly known as the olive phlomis, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and spikes of pale purple to pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Phlomis olivieri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
