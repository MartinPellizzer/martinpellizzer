Pulsatilla alba var. lutescens

Pulsatilla alba var. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range from white to yellow in color. The plant features a hairy stem with basal leaves that are palmately divided, and it produces follicular fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is cultivated in gardens and protected in its natural habitat.
