Phyllostachys edulis f. epruinosa

Phyllostachys edulis f. epruinosa, commonly known as the silver-haired bamboo, is a variant of the giant bamboo species.

It is characterized by its striking silvery-white to pale blue waxy coating on the culms, which gives it a unique appearance. This coating is most prominent on the younger shoots and can be shed as the plant matures. The plant is native to eastern Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
