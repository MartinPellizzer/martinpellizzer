Polygonum lapathifolium var. danubiale

Polygonum lapathifolium var. danubiale, commonly known as the Danube knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as riverbanks and wet meadows. Its stems are often reddish or purple-hued, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
