Paspalum brachiatum

Paspalum brachiatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf beardgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle, producing small, two-lobed flowers.

Paspalum brachiatum is known for its adaptability to wet conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
