Pyrus communis var. microphylla

Pyrus communis var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf common pear, is a compact variant of the common pear tree.

It is characterized by its smaller leaves and reduced size compared to the standard Pyrus communis. The plant produces small, sweet fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
