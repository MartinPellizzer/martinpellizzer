Phytolacca acinosa

Phytolacca acinosa, commonly known as Chinese pokeberry, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and animals. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including saponins and alkaloids, which have been studied for their medicinal properties. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its leaves are alternate and have a rough texture, with a distinctive reddish stem.
