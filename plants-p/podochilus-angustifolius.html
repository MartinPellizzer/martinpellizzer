Podochilus angustifolius

Podochilus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with a distinctive lip that is broader than the other petals.

Podochilus angustifolius is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
