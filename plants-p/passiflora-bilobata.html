Passiflora bilobata

Passiflora bilobata, commonly known as the twin-lobed passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers that feature a distinctive crown of filaments and a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces bright orange or red berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a distinct scent.

Passiflora bilobata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential calming properties.
