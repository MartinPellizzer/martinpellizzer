Pterostylis barringtonensis

Pterostylis barringtonensis, commonly known as the Barrington grass-tongue orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often strikingly shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its flowers are usually green or yellow-green, with a distinctive elongated lip that resembles a grass leaf.

The orchid relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, highlighting its complex ecological relationships.
