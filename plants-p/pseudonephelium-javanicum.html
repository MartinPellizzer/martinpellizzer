Pseudonephelium javanicum

Pseudonephelium javanicum, commonly known as the Java starfruit, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its fragrant, white flowers and edible, star-shaped fruits. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

The fruit, which resembles a star, is typically yellow when mature and has a sweet, tangy flavor.
