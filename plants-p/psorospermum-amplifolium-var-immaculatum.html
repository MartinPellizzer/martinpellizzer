Psorospermum amplifolium var. immaculatum

Psorospermum amplifolium var. immaculatum, commonly known as the immaculate psorospermum, is a species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its simple, alternate, and ovate to elliptical leaves. The leaves are typically dark green and glossy, with a smooth texture and lack of stipules. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
