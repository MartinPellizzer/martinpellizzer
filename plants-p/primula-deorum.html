Primula deorum

Primula deorum, commonly known as the sacred primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with yellow centers, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a hairy texture, and it has a compact growth habit.

Primula deorum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
