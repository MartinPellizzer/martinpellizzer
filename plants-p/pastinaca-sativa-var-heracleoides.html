Pastinaca sativa var. heracleoides

Pastinaca sativa var. heracleoides, commonly known as the Hercules' club, is a variant of the wild parsnip.

It is a biennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features a thick, spindle-shaped root and large, lobed leaves that resemble those of a dandelion. Its flowers are yellow and occur in umbels, typical of the carrot family.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, and it can be toxic if ingested.
