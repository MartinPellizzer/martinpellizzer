Pisum sativum var. laurentii

Pisum sativum var. laurentii, commonly known as the Laurentian pea, is a rare and ancient variety of the pea plant.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, often wrinkled pods. The plant typically produces yellow or white flowers and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is valued for its historical significance and unique genetic traits.

Due to its rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts interested in heirloom plants.
