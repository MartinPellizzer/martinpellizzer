Passiflora weberbaueri

Passiflora weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer passionflower, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Passiflora weberbaueri is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
