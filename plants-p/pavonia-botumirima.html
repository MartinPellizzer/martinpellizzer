Pavonia botumirima

Pavonia botumirima, commonly known as the Brazilian pink velvet flower, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability contribute to its appeal in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
