Piper striatifolium

Piper striatifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pepper, is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its slender, striated leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces tiny, pale green berries that are typically harvested for their peppery flavor. Its botanical features include opposite, ovate leaves with prominent venation and small, unisexual flowers.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understorey of tropical forests.
