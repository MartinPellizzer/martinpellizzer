Peperomia galioides var. glauca

Peperomia galioides var. glauca, commonly known as the Blue Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, dark green leaves with a distinctive blue-gray waxy coating, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters during the growing season. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its tolerance for dry air.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental purposes in both home and office environments.
