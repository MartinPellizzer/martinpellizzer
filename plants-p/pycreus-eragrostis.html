Pycreus eragrostis

Pycreus eragrostis, commonly known as slender lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and tall, slender flowering stems that produce small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along roadsides. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in erosion control.

The species is also known for its ability to withstand drought and grazing.
