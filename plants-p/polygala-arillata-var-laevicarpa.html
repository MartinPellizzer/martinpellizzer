Polygala arillata var. laevicarpa

Polygala arillata var. laevicarpa, commonly known as the smooth-seeded milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The leaves are alternate and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture. Its fruits are capsules that mature into a smooth, fleshy structure, which aids in seed dispersal.

This plant is typically found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands.
