Polygonum bracteatum

Polygonum bracteatum, commonly known as the Chinese knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom in summer. It is often found in moist environments such as wetlands, streambanks, and marshy areas.

Polygonum bracteatum is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
