Pterocarpus discolor

Pterocarpus discolor, commonly known as the yellow-wood or Indian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark brown to black heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and have a distinctive glossy appearance.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
