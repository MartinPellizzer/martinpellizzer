Pleurothallis bogarinii

Pleurothallis bogarinii, commonly known as the Bogarin's spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are usually small, with vivid colors and intricate patterns, adapted for specific pollinators.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a delicately balanced species within its ecosystem.
