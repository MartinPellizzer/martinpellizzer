Poa flavescens

Poa flavescens, commonly known as the yellow meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows and along streambanks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Poa flavescens is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
