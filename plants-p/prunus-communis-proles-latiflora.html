Prunus communis proles latiflora

Prunus communis proles latiflora, commonly known as the broad-leaved cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its oval, broad leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart red berries. It is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The broad-leaved cherry is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
