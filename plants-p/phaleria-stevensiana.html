Phaleria stevensiana

Phaleria stevensiana, commonly known as the Java laurel, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy pulp.

Phaleria stevensiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
