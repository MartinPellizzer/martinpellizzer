Pediocactus simpsonii var. despainii

Pediocactus simpsonii var. despainii, commonly known as the Desert Ghost Cactus, is a rare and striking member of the Cactaceae family native to the deserts of Baja California, Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny, ribbed stems that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a dramatic contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun exposure, making it a challenging but rewarding plant for experienced growers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
