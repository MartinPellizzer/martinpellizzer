Penstemon holmgrenii

Penstemon holmgrenii, commonly known as Holmgren's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial species belonging to the Plantaginaceae family, characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically features bluish-green leaves and produces spikes of pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological significance.
