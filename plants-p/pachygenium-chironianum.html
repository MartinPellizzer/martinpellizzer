Pachygenium chironianum

Pachygenium chironianum, commonly known as the horned fig, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically consumed by specific bird and mammal species, playing a crucial role in seed dispersal. Its botanical features, including its specialized floral structures and growth habits, make it an intriguing subject for ecological and taxonomic study.
