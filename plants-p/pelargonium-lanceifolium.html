Pelargonium lanceifolium

Pelargonium lanceifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink or red. The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and containers.

Pelargonium lanceifolium is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
