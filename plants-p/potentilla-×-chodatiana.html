Potentilla × chodatiana

Potentilla × chodatiana, commonly known as the Chodat's cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spreading growth habit and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in the summer. The plant has finely divided, serrated leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Potentilla × chodatiana is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects, as it can stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
