Phaseolus hernandesii

Phaseolus hernandesii, commonly known as the Mexican runner bean, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated pods containing edible beans. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use, though it is less commonly cultivated than other Phaseolus species.
