Phacelia setigera

Phacelia setigera, commonly known as blue flax, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a soft, fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in dry, open habitats.

This plant is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators and its use in ecological restoration projects.
