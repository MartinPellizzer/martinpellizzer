Polygonum amphibium f. hirtuosum

Polygonum amphibium f. hirtuosum, commonly known as the hairy water pepper, is a variant of the common water pepper plant.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in wet or marshy environments. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually hairy, giving it the "hirtuosum" designation, which refers to the hairy texture.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to moist, nutrient-rich soils.
