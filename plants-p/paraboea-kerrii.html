Paraboea kerrii

Paraboea kerrii, commonly known as the Kerri's paraboea, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring heart-shaped, glossy green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
