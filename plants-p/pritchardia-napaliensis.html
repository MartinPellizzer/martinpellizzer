Pritchardia napaliensis

Pritchardia napaliensis, commonly known as the Napali palm, is a species of cycad native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and its feather-like, compound leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Pritchardia napaliensis is considered a rare and endangered species.
