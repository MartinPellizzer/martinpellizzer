Prunus havardii

Prunus havardii, commonly known as the Havard cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, dark red to black fruits, which are edible and often used in culinary applications. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated margin. It produces white to pale pink flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes.

Prunus havardii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
