Planotia ingens

Planotia ingens, commonly known as the giant flat fern, is a striking member of the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, broad fronds that can reach lengths of up to two meters. The plant features a thick, short rhizome with a dense network of roots, allowing it to anchor firmly in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically divided into broad, lance-shaped segments with a smooth, waxy surface.

Planotia ingens thrives in humid, temperate climates and is often found in forest understories where it receives indirect sunlight.
