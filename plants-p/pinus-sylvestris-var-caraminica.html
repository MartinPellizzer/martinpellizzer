Pinus sylvestris var. caraminica

Pinus sylvestris var. caraminica, commonly known as the Caraminica pine, is a variant of the Scots pine native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and long, needle-like leaves that are typically a dark green color. The cones of this variety are generally smaller and more elongated compared to the standard Scots pine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
