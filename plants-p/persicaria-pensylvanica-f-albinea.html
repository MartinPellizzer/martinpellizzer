Persicaria pensylvanica f. albinea

Persicaria pensylvanica f. albinea, commonly known as the white-stemmed smartweed, is a variant of the common smartweed characterized by its pale to white stems.

This plant is a herbaceous annual or biennial species native to North America, typically found in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, ditches, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a reddish or purplish tinge, while the flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by three-lobed, bladder-like seed pods.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
