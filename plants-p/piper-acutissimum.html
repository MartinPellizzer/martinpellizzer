Piper acutissimum

Piper acutissimum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf betel vine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like the betel nut tree. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, heart-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female structures, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions.
