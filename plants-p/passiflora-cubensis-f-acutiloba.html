Passiflora cubensis f. acutiloba

Passiflora cubensis f. acutiloba, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a tropical climbing vine characterized by its striking, complex flowers.

It features showy, five-petaled blooms with a central crown of filaments, often in shades of purple and white. The plant produces blue or black berries that are edible and contain small, white seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
