Poa macedonica var. tenuifolia

Poa macedonica var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a variant of the Macedonian grass species native to southeastern Europe.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its fine, slender leaves and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet environments.
