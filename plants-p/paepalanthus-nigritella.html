Paepalanthus nigritella

Paepalanthus nigritella, commonly known as the black nightshade of the Andes, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly found in the high-altitude regions of the Andes. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

Due to its toxic compounds, it is not suitable for consumption by humans or animals.
