Phelypaea elongata

Phelypaea elongata, commonly known as the slender water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated fronds that float on the water's surface. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and marshes. Its rhizomes spread along the substrate, allowing it to colonize new areas.

Phelypaea elongata plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
