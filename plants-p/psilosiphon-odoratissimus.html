Psilosiphon odoratissimus

Psilosiphon odoratissimus, commonly known as the fragrant bladderworts, is a carnivorous plant belonging to the genus Psilosiphon.

It is characterized by its small, aquatic or semi-aquatic habit, often found in wetlands and marshy areas. The plant possesses specialized bladder-like structures that trap and digest small aquatic organisms. Its flowers are usually small and inconspicuous, with a distinctive scent that aids in attracting pollinators.

The plant's botanical features reflect its adaptation to nutrient-poor environments, relying on insect prey for essential nutrients.
