Panicum stigmatisatum

Panicum stigmatisatum, commonly known as the silver panicum, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and slender, branched inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has small, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
