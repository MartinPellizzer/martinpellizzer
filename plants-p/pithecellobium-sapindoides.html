Pithecellobium sapindoides

Pithecellobium sapindoides, commonly known as the yellow glory pea, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
