Ptisana ambulans

Ptisana ambulans, commonly known as the wandering grass, is a unique plant characterized by its ability to spread across surfaces through its rhizomatous growth.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, temperate environments, often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing light green to yellow blooms that attract various pollinators.

Ptisana ambulans plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems due to its extensive root system.
