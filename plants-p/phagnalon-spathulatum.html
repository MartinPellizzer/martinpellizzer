Phagnalon spathulatum

Phagnalon spathulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf phagnalon, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and rocky soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and linear, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
