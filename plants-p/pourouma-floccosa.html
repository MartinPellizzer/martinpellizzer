Pourouma floccosa

Pourouma floccosa, commonly known as the "white areca," is a tall, herbaceous plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with white flowers, which are followed by fibrous, clustered fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as thatching material.
