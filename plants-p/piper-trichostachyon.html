Piper trichostachyon

Piper trichostachyon, commonly known as the slender pepper vine, is a climbing perennial plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, and its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture with a distinct aroma.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
