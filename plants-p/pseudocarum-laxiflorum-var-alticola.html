Pseudocarum laxiflorum var. alticola

Pseudocarum laxiflorum var. alticola, commonly known as the alpine false caraway, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand cold and windy conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
