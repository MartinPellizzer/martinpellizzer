Phrynium macrocephalum

Phrynium macrocephalum, commonly known as the giant flame plant, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, often with a waxy texture and a vibrant green to reddish coloration. The plant produces showy, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by colorful bracts, typically in shades of red or orange. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Phrynium macrocephalum is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
