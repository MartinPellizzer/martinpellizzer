Phoebe filamentosa

Phoebe filamentosa, commonly known as the filamentous phoebe, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.
