Plantago hypoleuca

Plantago hypoleuca, commonly known as the white plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its soothing effects on the digestive system.
