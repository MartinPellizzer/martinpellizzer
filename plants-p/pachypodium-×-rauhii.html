Pachypodium × rauhii

Pachypodium × rauhii, commonly known as the Madagascar palm hybrid, is a unique succulent native to Madagascar.

It features a thick, stem-like trunk with a dense cluster of spines at the top, resembling a palm tree. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the warmer months, followed by red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its hybrid origin results from crosses between Pachypodium leari and Pachypodium ambongense, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
