Panicum longipedunculatum

Panicum longipedunculatum, commonly known as the long-bracted panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and elongated, drooping inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological preferences, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying wetland flora.
