Pavonia betonicifolia

Pavonia betonicifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers resembling mist. The plant features palmately lobed leaves with a hairy texture and a compact, upright growth habit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Pavonia betonicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
