Polypodium penangianum

Polypodium penangianum, commonly known as the Penang huperzia, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Huperziaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender rhizome and erect, lanceolate fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its spores are released from the sori, which are located on the underside of the mature fronds.

Polypodium penangianum is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
