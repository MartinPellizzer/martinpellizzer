Phaseoloides nudiflorum

Phaseoloides nudiflorum, commonly known as the nakedflower legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes and beans. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale in color. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
