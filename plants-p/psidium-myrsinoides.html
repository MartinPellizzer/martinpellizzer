Psidium myrsinoides

Psidium myrsinoides, commonly known as the Brazilian guava or wild guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by edible, fleshy berries similar to guavas. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
