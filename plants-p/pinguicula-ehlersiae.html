Pinguicula ehlersiae

Pinguicula ehlersiae, commonly known as the Ehlers's butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of sticky, glandular leaves that trap and digest small insects. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires ample sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
