Prunus undulata f. venosa

Prunus undulata f. venosa, commonly known as the wavy leaf cherry, is a botanical variant of the Prunus undulata species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, undulating leaf margins, which give it a unique appearance. The plant typically produces small, pinkish-white flowers in spring, followed by dark red or black berries in autumn. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its foliage displays a striking variegation, with white veins contrasting against the dark green leaves.
