Platanus orientalis f. pyramidalis

Platanus orientalis f. pyramidalis, commonly known as the oriental plane tree in a pyramidal form, is a cultivar characterized by its narrow, conical shape.

It retains the broad, palmate leaves of the species, which are typically large and glossy. The tree produces small, spherical fruits that split open to release tufts of cotton-like seeds. It is often used in urban landscaping due to its upright growth habit and tolerance to pollution.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
