Polygonum hydropiperoides var. breviciliatum

Polygonum hydropiperoides var. breviciliatum, commonly known as the narrow-stipitate smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy underside. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red, blooming in late summer.

This variant is distinguished by its shorter stipules compared to other varieties of Polygonum hydropiperoides.
