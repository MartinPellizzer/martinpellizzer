Potamoxylon fluviatile

Potamoxylon fluviatile, commonly known as the riverwood plant, is a unique aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It has a distinctive, woody structure that allows it to remain upright in flowing water. The plant's stems are often covered in a thick, protective layer that aids in buoyancy and resistance to water currents. Its leaves are small and lance-shaped, adapted for efficient photosynthesis in low-light conditions.

Potamoxylon fluviatile plays a vital role in stabilizing riverbeds and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
