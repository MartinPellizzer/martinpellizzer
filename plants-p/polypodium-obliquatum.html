Polypodium obliquatum

Polypodium obliquatum, commonly known as the oblique polypody, is a fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are often covered in a layer of fine, hairy structures.

Polypodium obliquatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
