Piper poporense

Piper poporense, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 3,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
