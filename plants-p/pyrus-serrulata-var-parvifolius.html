Pyrus serrulata var. parvifolius

Pyrus serrulata var. parvifolius, commonly known as the dwarf Chinese white pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, typically measuring up to 5 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by yellowish or reddish fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional Chinese horticulture.
