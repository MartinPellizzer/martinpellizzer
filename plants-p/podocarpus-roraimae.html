Podocarpus roraimae

Podocarpus roraimae, commonly known as the Roraima podocarp, is a rare evergreen conifer native to the tepuis of Venezuela and Guyana.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant thrives in high-altitude, humid environments with well-drained soil. It plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat by providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
