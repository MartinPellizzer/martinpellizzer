Plectranthus burorum

Plectranthus burorum, commonly known as the Burro's plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus burorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
