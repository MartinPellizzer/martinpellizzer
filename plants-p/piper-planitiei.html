Piper planitiei

Piper planitiei, commonly known as the lowland pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly the Amazon basin. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, dark purple berries.

Piper planitiei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
