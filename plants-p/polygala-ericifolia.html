Polygala ericifolia

Polygala ericifolia, commonly known as the heath polygala, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in areas with high wind exposure. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, and it blooms during the summer months. It has narrow, leathery leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Polygala ericifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
