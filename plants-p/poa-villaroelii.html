Poa villaroelii

Poa villaroelii, commonly known as the Villaroel's bluegrass, is a species of grass native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, such as grasslands and rocky slopes, where it forms dense mats. Its inflorescence consists of compact spikes, and it produces small, two-lobed flowers.

Poa villaroelii is adapted to cold and arid conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
