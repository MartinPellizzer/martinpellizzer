Pseuderanthemum connatum

Pseuderanthemum connatum, commonly known as the Brazilian flameflower, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a shrub native to Brazil, characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces showy bracts that resemble petals, giving it a floral appearance despite being part of the foliage. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated margin.
