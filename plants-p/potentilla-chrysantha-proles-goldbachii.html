Potentilla chrysantha proles goldbachii

Potentilla chrysantha proles goldbachii, commonly known as the golden potentilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the summer months. The plant features small, ovate leaves with a hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Native to regions of Central Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
