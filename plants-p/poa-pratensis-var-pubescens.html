Poa pratensis var. pubescens

Poa pratensis var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent meadow grass, is a variant of the common bentgrass.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, bluish-green leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.
