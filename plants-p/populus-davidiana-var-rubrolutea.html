Populus davidiana var. rubrolutea

Populus davidiana var. rubrolutea, commonly known as the red-leaf poplar, is a variant of the David's poplar native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown to orange-yellow leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. It has smooth, gray bark and slender, often weeping branches.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscape gardening.
