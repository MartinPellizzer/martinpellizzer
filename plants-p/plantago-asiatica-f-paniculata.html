Plantago asiatica f. paniculata

Plantago asiatica f. paniculata, commonly known as the panicled plantain, is a variant of the common plantain with distinctive botanical features.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist or semi-shaded environments. The plant produces a tall, branching inflorescence with small, greenish flowers that are clustered in paniculate arrangements. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variety is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its adaptability to various soil conditions.
