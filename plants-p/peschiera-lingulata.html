Peschiera lingulata

Peschiera lingulata, commonly known as the Italian water clover, is a unique aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Italy.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that resemble the shape of a clover, with three to five lobes. The plant thrives in shallow, still waters and is often found in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically greenish-white and appear in clusters.

Peschiera lingulata plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to water filtration.
