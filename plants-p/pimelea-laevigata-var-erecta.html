Pimelea laevigata var. erecta

Pimelea laevigata var. erecta, commonly known as the erect pimelea, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late winter to early spring, which are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
