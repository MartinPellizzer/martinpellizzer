Plantinia phalaroidea

Plantinia phalaroidea, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the eastern Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flower heads surrounded by spiky, silver-green bracts. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salty, windy environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique appearance and hardy nature.
