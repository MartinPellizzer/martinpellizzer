Pithecellobium hassleri

Pithecellobium hassleri, commonly known as the yellow velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it a notable species in tropical and subtropical regions.
