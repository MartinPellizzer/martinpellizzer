Pandanus regalis

Pandanus regalis, commonly known as the royal pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, sword-shaped leaves that grow in dense, fan-like clusters. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in branched inflorescences. Its roots are often used in traditional crafts, and the leaves are utilized in thatching and weaving.

Pandanus regalis thrives in coastal and wetland environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils.
