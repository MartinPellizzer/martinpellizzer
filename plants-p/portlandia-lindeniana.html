Portlandia lindeniana

Portlandia lindeniana, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Portlandia lindeniana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
