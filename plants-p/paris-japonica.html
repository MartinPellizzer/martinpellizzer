Paris japonica

Paris japonica, commonly known as Japanese whiteflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features a rosette of broad, waxy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Paris japonica is also notable for its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
