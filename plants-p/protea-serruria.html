Protea serruria

Protea serruria, commonly known as the serrated protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of individual flowers, known as heads. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to the fynbos biome. Its leaves are usually serrated, giving it its common name, and it produces vibrant pink or red bracts.

Protea serruria is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also an important species in local ecosystems.
