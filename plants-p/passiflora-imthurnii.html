Passiflora imthurnii

Passiflora imthurnii, commonly known as the Imthurn passionflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Passiflora imthurnii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, attracting various pollinators.
