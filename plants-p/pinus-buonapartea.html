Pinus buonapartea

Pinus buonapartea, commonly known as the Napoleon pine, is a rare and endangered species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in Ecuador.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, straight trunk and needle-like leaves arranged in bundles of five. The tree produces cones that are typically pendulous and contain edible seeds, which are an important food source for local wildlife. Its slow growth rate and limited distribution make it highly susceptible to habitat loss and climate change.

Conservation efforts are crucial to preserve this unique botanical species.
