Pachystigma

Pachystigma, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

These plants are characterized by their thick, fleshy leaves and showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors. Pachystigma species typically grow as shrubs or small trees, often with a compact, bushy appearance. They prefer well-drained soil and bright, indirect light, making them popular houseplants.

The genus includes several species, such as Pachystigma glabrum and Pachystigma pulchellum, which are valued for their ornamental qualities.
