Phyllanthus hutchinsonianus

Phyllanthus hutchinsonianus, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and smooth, often reddish bark. The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
