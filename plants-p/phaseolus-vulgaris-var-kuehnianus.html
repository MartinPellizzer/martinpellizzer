Phaseolus vulgaris var. kuehnianus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. kuehnianus, commonly known as the Andean runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Andes region of South America.

It produces elongated, edible pods that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or brown when mature. The plant features compound leaves with palmate venation and produces small, white or pale purple flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is valued for its culinary uses and ornamental value in gardens.
