Pandanus aristatus

Pandanus aristatus, commonly known as the bottle palm, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive, palm-like appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible fruits, which are often consumed by both humans and animals. Its robust growth makes it well-suited for coastal environments, where it can tolerate salt spray and sandy soils.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in various island cultures.
