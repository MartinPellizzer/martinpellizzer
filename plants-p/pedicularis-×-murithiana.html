Pedicularis × murithiana

Pedicularis × murithiana, commonly known as the hybrid crowfoot orchid, is a member of the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers.

It is a hemiparasitic plant, meaning it partially relies on other plants for water and nutrients through its roots. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are often pink or purple, with a unique, elongated spur that houses the nectar.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
