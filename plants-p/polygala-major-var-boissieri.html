Polygala major var. boissieri

Polygala major var. boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, usually green or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, ovate leaves arranged alternately along the stem. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
