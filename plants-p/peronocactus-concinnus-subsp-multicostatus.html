Peronocactus concinnus subsp. multicostatus

Peronocactus concinnus subsp.

multicostatus, commonly known as the multicorn cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the warmer months, adding ornamental value. It is well adapted to dry climates and requires minimal water once established.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
