Phlox douglasii var. hendersonii

Phlox douglasii var. hendersonii, commonly known as Henderson's phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and are typically borne on erect, branching stems. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
