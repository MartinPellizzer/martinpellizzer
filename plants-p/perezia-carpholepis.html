Perezia carpholepis

Perezia carpholepis, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South America.

This plant is characterized by its bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests. The leaves are long, narrow, and slightly fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant color make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and orchid enthusiasts.
