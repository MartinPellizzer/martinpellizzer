Pleurothyrium amissum

Pleurothyrium amissum, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and a rhizomatous root system. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a food source, valued for their starchy texture.

The botanical features of Pleurothyrium amissum make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
