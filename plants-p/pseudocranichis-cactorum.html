Pseudocranichis cactorum

Pseudocranichis cactorum, commonly known as the desert orchid, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often showy flowers. The plant typically grows from a bulb-like structure called a corm, which allows it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and succulent, adapted to store water.

Pseudocranichis cactorum is known for its ability to bloom in the early spring, adding a splash of color to desert landscapes.
