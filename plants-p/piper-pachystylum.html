Piper pachystylum

Piper pachystylum, commonly known as the giant sylhet pepper, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruits are small berries that turn red when mature, often used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Piper pachystylum make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ethnobotanical studies.
