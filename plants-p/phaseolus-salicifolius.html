Phaseolus salicifolius

Phaseolus salicifolius, commonly known as the willow-leaf bean, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing legume with slender, willow-like leaves that are typically alternate and ovate. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated pods containing several oval seeds. It is often cultivated for its edible beans, which are rich in protein and used in traditional cuisines.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
