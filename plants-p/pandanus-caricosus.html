Pandanus caricosus

Pandanus caricosus, commonly known as the mountain pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that form a dense crown. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in a spadix, which is surrounded by a colorful bract. It thrives in coastal and mountainous areas, often growing in well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional crafts.
