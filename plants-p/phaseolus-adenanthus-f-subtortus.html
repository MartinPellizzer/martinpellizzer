Phaseolus adenanthus f. subtortus

Phaseolus adenanthus f. subtortus, commonly known as the dwarf runner bean, is a botanical variant of the common bean species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter vines compared to typical Phaseolus species. The plant produces small, edible pods that are often used in culinary applications. Its foliage is typically heart-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
