Paphiopedilum jackii

Paphiopedilum jackii, commonly known as the Chinese lady's slipper, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, pouch-shaped lip that resembles a slipper, a distinctive feature among orchids. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white with intricate patterns, and it produces small, greenish fruits.

Paphiopedilum jackii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
