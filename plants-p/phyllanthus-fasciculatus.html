Phyllanthus fasciculatus

Phyllanthus fasciculatus, commonly known as the clustered spurge, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its small, green, succulent leaves that are often arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by small, spherical berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.

Phyllanthus fasciculatus is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
