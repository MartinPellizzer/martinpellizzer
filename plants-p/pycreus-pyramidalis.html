Pycreus pyramidalis

Pycreus pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramid bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, pyramidal growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Pycreus pyramidalis is relatively drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
