Primula × huteri

Primula × huteri, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a species resulting from the cross between Primula vulgaris and Primula sinensis.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors such as pink, white, and yellow. The plant typically features a basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces clustered flowers on elongated stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Botanically, it is notable for its genetic diversity and adaptability, making it a popular choice for horticultural use.
