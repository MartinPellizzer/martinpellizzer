Pinguicula vallisneriifolia lusus brevifolia

Pinguicula vallisneriifolia lusus brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap small insects. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers are typically small and yellow, with a distinctive hooded structure.

The species is noted for its compact growth habit and is a popular choice for carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
