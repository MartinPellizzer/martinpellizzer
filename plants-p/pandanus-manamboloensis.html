Pandanus manamboloensis

Pandanus manamboloensis, commonly known as the Madagascar pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, typical of many pandanus species. Its fibrous leaves are used in traditional crafts and for thatching, highlighting its cultural and practical significance.

The species is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of its native habitat, often growing in coastal or lowland areas.
