Pseuderanthemum crenulatum var. ecorticatum

Pseuderanthemum crenulatum var. ecorticatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with wavy margins. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
