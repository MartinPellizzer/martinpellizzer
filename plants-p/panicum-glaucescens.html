Panicum glaucescens

Panicum glaucescens, commonly known as blue panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green foliage and tufted inflorescences that produce small, fluffy seeds. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. It has a compact growth habit and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
