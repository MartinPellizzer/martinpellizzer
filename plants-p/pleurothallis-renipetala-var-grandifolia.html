Pleurothallis renipetala var. grandifolia

Pleurothallis renipetala var. grandifolia, commonly known as the grandiflora orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized epiphytic orchid, typically found growing on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and lavender, often in clusters. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
