Paspalum tenellum var. bourgeaei

Paspalum tenellum var. bourgeaei, commonly known as dwarf paspalum, is a low-growing perennial grass native to South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. This variety is well adapted to dry and coastal environments, making it a hardy plant in various climates. Its dense growth habit makes it suitable for erosion control and as a ground cover.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
