Podocarpus rotundus

Podocarpus rotundus, commonly known as the Indian cedar or round-leaf podocarp, is a evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and cone-bearing structure. The leaves are typically oval-shaped, glossy, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It produces small, woody cones that contain edible seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This plant is valued for its timber and is often used in horticulture for ornamental purposes.
