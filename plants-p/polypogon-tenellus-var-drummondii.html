Polypogon tenellus var. drummondii

Polypogon tenellus var. drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's slender grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy habitats, preferring moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or gray on the underside. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to wet environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
