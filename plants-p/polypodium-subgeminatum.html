Polypodium subgeminatum

Polypodium subgeminatum, commonly known as the twin-leaf polypody, is a fern native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its delicate, bright green fronds that are typically divided into two pairs of lobes, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or tree trunks. Its rhizome is thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

The spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds in clusters, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
