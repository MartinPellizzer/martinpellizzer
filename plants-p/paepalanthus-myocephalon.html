Paepalanthus myocephalon

Paepalanthus myocephalon, commonly known as the green-flowered puya, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that bears numerous small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in cloud forests or rocky areas. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often covered in a waxy coating, which helps retain moisture.

The species is noted for its unique floral structure and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
