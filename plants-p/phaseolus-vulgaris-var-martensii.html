Phaseolus vulgaris var. martensii

Phaseolus vulgaris var. martensii, commonly known as the Martens's bean, is a lesser-known variety of common bean.

It is characterized by its distinctive pod and seed morphology, which set it apart from other Phaseolus species. This plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in warm, humid climates. Its botanical features include compound leaves and axial inflorescences, typical of the Fabaceae family.

The seeds are often used in traditional cuisines and have potential agricultural value due to their adaptability.
