Prunus mira subsp. nepalensis

Prunus mira subsp.

nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayan region. It typically grows at high altitudes, often in moist, temperate climates.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible berries that are a food source for various wildlife. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its bark is dark and fissured.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
