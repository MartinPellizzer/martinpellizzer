Phyllanthus concolor var. obovatus

Phyllanthus concolor var. obovatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed phyllanthus, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its simple, obovate leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
