Polypogon monspeliensis var. maximus

Polypogon monspeliensis var. maximus, commonly known as the broadleaf panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to colonize disturbed wetland areas.
