Phyteuma pyrenaicum f. pilosum

Phyteuma pyrenaicum f. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy phyteuma, is a botanical variant of the phyteuma genus native to the Pyrenees region.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which provide a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to orange, with a tubular shape that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists and gardeners due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
