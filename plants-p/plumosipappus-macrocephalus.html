Plumosipappus macrocephalus

Plumosipappus macrocephalus, commonly known as the large-headed plumose pappus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-dispersed seeds. The plant features showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fluffy, parachute-like seed heads aid in wind dispersal, contributing to its distribution across arid and semi-arid regions.

The plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in desert landscapes.
