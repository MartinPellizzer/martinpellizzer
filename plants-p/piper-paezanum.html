Piper paezanum

Piper paezanum, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or Paezan's pepper, is a tropical vine native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, a small berry, turns red when mature and is used in traditional medicine.

Piper paezanum is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is often found in humid, lowland forest environments.
