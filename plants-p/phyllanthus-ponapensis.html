Phyllanthus ponapensis

Phyllanthus ponapensis, commonly known as the Caroline Islands spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in coastal and disturbed habitats. The plant features simple, alternate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
