Pollinia nuda

Pollinia nuda, commonly known as the naked pollinia, is a unique plant species characterized by its specialized reproductive structures.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is native to certain regions of South America. The plant is distinguished by its absence of traditional petals, instead featuring a cluster of pollen masses called pollinia. These pollinia are adapted for attachment to the bodies of pollinators, facilitating efficient pollination.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, Pollinia nuda is relatively rare and often found in humid, shaded environments.
