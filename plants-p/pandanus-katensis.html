Pandanus katensis

Pandanus katensis, commonly known as the Katans Island pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the island of Katan in the South Pacific and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the tips of the leaves. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often growing near the shoreline.

The leaves of Pandanus katensis are sometimes used in traditional crafts and for thatching.
