Palaquium cupreum

Palaquium cupreum, commonly known as the copperleaf or copperwood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is often found in forested areas. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are drupes, typically reddish or yellowish when mature, and contain a single seed.

Palaquium cupreum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in gardens.
