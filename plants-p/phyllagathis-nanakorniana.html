Phyllagathis nanakorniana

Phyllagathis nanakorniana, commonly known as the Thai mountain myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that contain seeds, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
