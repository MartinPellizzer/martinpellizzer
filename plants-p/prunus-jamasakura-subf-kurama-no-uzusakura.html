Prunus jamasakura subf.

kurama-no-uzusakura, commonly known as the Kurama cherry, is a sub-species of the Japanese cherry tree known for its ornamental value. It is characterized by its pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a striking visual display to its native habitat.

The plant typically grows as a small deciduous tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a smooth margin, and it produces small, dark red fruits in the autumn.

This species is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and parks.
