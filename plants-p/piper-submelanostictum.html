Piper submelanostictum

Piper submelanostictum, commonly known as the black-spotted pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its dark green, ovate leaves with prominent veins and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns from green to yellow when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
