Perotis acanthoneuron

Perotis acanthoneuron, commonly known as the roughleaf perotis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, sandpapery texture on the upper surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and are usually greenish-white in color.

Perotis acanthoneuron is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
