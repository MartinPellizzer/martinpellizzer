Pyrola incarnata subsp. dahurica

Pyrola incarnata subsp.

dahurica, commonly known as the Siberian snowbell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the forests of eastern Siberia and northern China. It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its low-growing, evergreen foliage.

The plant produces bell-shaped, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. Its leaves are thick and leathery, adapted to withstand cold temperatures and acidic soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
