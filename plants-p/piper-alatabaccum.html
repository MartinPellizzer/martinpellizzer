Piper alatabaccum

Piper alatabaccum, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper or broadleaf long pepper, is a flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its vine-like growth habit and compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. Its fruits are small, dark berries that contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
