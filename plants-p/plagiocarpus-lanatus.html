Plagiocarpus lanatus

Plagiocarpus lanatus, commonly known as the hairy pomegranate, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that resemble pomegranates, though they are not related. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
