Paspalum rostratum

Paspalum rostratum, commonly known as the hairy fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brown flowers. Its leaves are narrow, slightly hairy, and often have a bluish-gray hue.

Paspalum rostratum is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas, grasslands, and along roadsides.
