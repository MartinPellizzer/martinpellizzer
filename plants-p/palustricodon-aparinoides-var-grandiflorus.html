Palustricodon aparinoides var. grandiflorus

Palustricodon aparinoides var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the grandiflorous marsh bellflower, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of large, showy blue or violet flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing nectar for pollinators.
