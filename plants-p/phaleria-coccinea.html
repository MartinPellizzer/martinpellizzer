Phaleria coccinea

Phaleria coccinea, commonly known as the red-flowered magnolia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. The plant has large, glossy, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The tree also has a long history of use in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
