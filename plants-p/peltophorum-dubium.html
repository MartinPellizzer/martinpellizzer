Peltophorum dubium

Peltophorum dubium, commonly known as the "silk cotton tree," is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are composed of numerous small leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern. The tree produces clusters of pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by woody pods containing fluffy, cotton-like seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and shade-providing canopy.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
