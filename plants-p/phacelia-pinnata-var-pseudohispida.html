Phacelia pinnata var. pseudohispida

Phacelia pinnata var. pseudohispida, commonly known as the slender phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped. The flowers are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in racemes, and bloom in early summer. This annual species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting pollinators.
