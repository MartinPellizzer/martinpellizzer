Papaver × bartuschianum

Papaver × bartuschianum, commonly known as the Bartuschian poppy, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Papaver rhoeas and Papaver somniferum.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow, often with a distinctive black or dark red blotch at the base. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring broad, lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

This ornamental plant is valued for its striking appearance and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
