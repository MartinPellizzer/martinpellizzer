Prunus × lannesiana f. dianthipetala

Prunus × lannesiana f. dianthipetala, commonly known as the double-flowered cherry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Prunus lannesiana and Prunus × yedoensis.

It is characterized by its ornamental, double blooms that resemble peonies, with numerous petals clustered closely together. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This cultivar is prized for its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
