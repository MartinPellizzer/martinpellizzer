Pelargonium reniforme subsp. reniforme

Pelargonium reniforme subsp.

reniforme, commonly known as the broad-leaf geranium, is a species native to South Africa. It is characterized by its broad, kidney-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pink or red flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial herb that produces attractive, fragrant blooms throughout the growing season.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
