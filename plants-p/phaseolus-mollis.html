Phaseolus mollis

Phaseolus mollis, commonly known as the velvet bean or *Phaseolus mollis*, is a leguminous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, pale green pods that contain several angular, yellowish seeds. It is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a protein source.

Phaseolus mollis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural systems.
