Potentilla saundersiana

Potentilla saundersiana, commonly known as the saunders' cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are alternate, with five leaflets, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
