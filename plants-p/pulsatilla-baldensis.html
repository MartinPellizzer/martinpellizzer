Pulsatilla baldensis

Pulsatilla baldensis, commonly known as the bald mountain pasqueflower, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, often in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, with numerous stamens surrounding a central pistil. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive hairy texture.

Pulsatilla baldensis is adapted to cold climates and is often found in areas with limited soil nutrients.
