Polypodiastrum someyae

Polypodiastrum someyae, commonly known as the Somya's polypody, is a rare and delicate fern native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, delicate fronds that often display a pale green to silvery hue. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments, typically growing on shaded rock faces or in dense forest undergrowth. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the underside of its leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
