Pfeiffera mataralensis

Pfeiffera mataralensis, commonly known as the Mataral pfeiffera, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, adapting to the harsh conditions of its environment. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers are yellowish to orange in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
