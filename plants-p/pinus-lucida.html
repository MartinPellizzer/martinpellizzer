Pinus lucida

Pinus lucida, commonly known as the silver fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and silvery-blue needles that are coated with a fine, waxy layer. The tree produces small, yellowish cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, releasing winged seeds in the fall. Its foliage provides year-round color, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The silver fir is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
