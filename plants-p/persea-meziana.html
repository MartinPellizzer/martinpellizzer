Persea meziana

Persea meziana, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its ornamental value, combined with its edible fruit, makes it a valued plant in both horticulture and local cuisine.
