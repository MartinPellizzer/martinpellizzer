Pleurothallis vilipensa

Pleurothallis vilipensa, commonly known as the "Lepidoptera orchid," is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and green. Its intricate floral structure is adapted for pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
