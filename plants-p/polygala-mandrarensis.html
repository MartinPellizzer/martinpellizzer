Polygala mandrarensis

Polygala mandrarensis, commonly known as the Madagascar milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as savannas and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually narrow and opposite, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
