Polygonum humifusum subsp. caurianum

Polygonum humifusum subsp.

caurianum, commonly known as the creeping knotweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Asia. It typically features prostrate or ascending stems covered in soft, glandular hairs.

The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin, and they often have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or reddish in color, appearing in late summer.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
