Paeonia corallina var. caucasica

Paeonia corallina var. caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features showy, coral-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately compound leaves with a serrated margin, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
