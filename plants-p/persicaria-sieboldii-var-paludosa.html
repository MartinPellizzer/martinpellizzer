Persicaria sieboldii var. paludosa

Persicaria sieboldii var. paludosa, commonly known as the swamp knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in East Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that often grow in clusters, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is adapted to moist or waterlogged soils, making it a common sight in marshes and along streambanks.
