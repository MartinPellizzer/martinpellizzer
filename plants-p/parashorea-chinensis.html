Parashorea chinensis

Parashorea chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese tecton, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dipterocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including China, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall and is characterized by its buttressed roots and smooth, grayish bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and is valued for its durable timber used in construction and furniture.
