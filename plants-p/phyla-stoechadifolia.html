Phyla stoechadifolia

Phyla stoechadifolia, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flower heads surrounded by spiny, leaf-like bracts. The plant is typically found in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its halophytic nature. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, adapting to harsh maritime environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
