Phaseolus angustissimus var. latus

Phaseolus angustissimus var. latus, commonly known as the broadleaf runner bean, is a variant of the runner bean species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and long, climbing vines that produce pods with edible beans. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires full sun and well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and edible produce.

This variety is valued in agricultural systems for its adaptability and nutritional benefits.
