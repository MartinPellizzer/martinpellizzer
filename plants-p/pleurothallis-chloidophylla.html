Pleurothallis chloidophylla

Pleurothallis chloidophylla, commonly known as the green-leafed orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves that are typically a vibrant green, contributing to its common name. Its flowers are small and often white or pale yellow, arranged in a spike-like inflorescence.

Pleurothallis chloidophylla is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts in cultivation.
