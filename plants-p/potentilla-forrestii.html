Potentilla forrestii

Potentilla forrestii, commonly known as the Chinese fireweed, is a flowering plant native to regions of China, Korea, and Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically reaches heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated edge, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla forrestii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
