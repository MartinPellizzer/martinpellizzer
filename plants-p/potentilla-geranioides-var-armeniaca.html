Potentilla geranioides var. armeniaca

Potentilla geranioides var. armeniaca, commonly known as Armenian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from May to June. The plant has finely divided, serrated leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
