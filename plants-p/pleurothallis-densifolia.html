Pleurothallis densifolia

Pleurothallis densifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. It thrives in high humidity and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
