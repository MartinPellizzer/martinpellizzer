Pleurothallis diazii

Pleurothallis diazii, commonly known as the Diaz's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with intricate patterns on the petals. Its growth habit is characterized by a compact rosette of leaves and a short, branched stem.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a challenging species for cultivation.
