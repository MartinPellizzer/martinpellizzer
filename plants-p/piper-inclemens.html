Piper inclemens

Piper inclemens, commonly known as the cold pepper or mountain pepper, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as the common black pepper. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their medicinal properties.

Piper inclemens thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to withstand harsh mountain climates.
