Polypodium fumarioides

Polypodium fumarioides, commonly known as the common holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, serrated leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces sporangia on the undersides of its fronds, which are clustered in strobila. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions.

Polypodium fumarioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
