Pilea acuminata var. cruzensis

Pilea acuminata var. cruzensis, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen, is a compact, low-growing plant native to Mexico.

It features rounded, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy and have a distinctive pointed tip. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters during the spring. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its compact size and attractive foliage make it an excellent addition to tabletop displays or hanging baskets.
