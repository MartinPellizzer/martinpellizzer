Podochilus calcaratus

Podochilus calcaratus, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and green. The plant thrives in rocky or shaded environments, often growing on limestone or sandstone. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces fruit that contains several seeds.

Podochilus calcaratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
