Piper pseudovelutinum var. flavescens

Piper pseudovelutinum var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, heart-shaped leaves, and small yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

This variant is distinguished by its unique yellowish hue in both flowers and young leaves, making it a visually distinct species within the Piper genus.
