Penducella exigua

Penducella exigua, commonly known as the small pendunculate liverwort, is a small, terrestrial liverwort belonging to the family Ptilidioraceae.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a prostrate or ascending growth habit with slender, branching stems. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and closely spaced, often with a distinct midrib.

Penducella exigua is characterized by its small, pendulous sporophytes that develop from the leaf axils.
