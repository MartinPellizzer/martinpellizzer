Pleopeltis fraseri

Pleopeltis fraseri, commonly known as the Fraser's rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features lanceolate, leathery leaves with a distinctive midrib and a dense network of veins. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical forests.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species within its native habitat.
