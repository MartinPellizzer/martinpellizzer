Pastinaca pratensis

Pastinaca pratensis, commonly known as the meadow burdock, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features tall, hollow stems with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem.

Pastinaca pratensis is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soil.
