Pouzolzia cinerascens

Pouzolzia cinerascens, commonly known as the silverleaf pouzolzia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are clustered along the stems. Its leaves are opposite and often have a waxy coating, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Pouzolzia cinerascens is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient conditions.
