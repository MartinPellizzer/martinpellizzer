Physalis obscura

Physalis obscura, commonly known as the common groundcherries, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, inflated, yellow or orange fruits that enclose the seeds. These fruits are often used in cooking and have a tangy, sweet flavor.

Physalis obscura thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
