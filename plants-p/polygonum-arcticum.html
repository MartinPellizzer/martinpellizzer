Polygonum arcticum

Polygonum arcticum, commonly known as Arctic knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. It plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food for various insects and animals.

Polygonum arcticum is well adapted to cold climates and can survive in low-nutrient environments.
