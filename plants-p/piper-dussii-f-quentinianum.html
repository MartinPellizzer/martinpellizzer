Piper dussii f. quentinianum

Piper dussii f. quentinianum, commonly known as the Quentin's pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, greenish-white flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardens and greenhouses.
