Paepalanthus saxicola var. conicus

Paepalanthus saxicola var. conicus, commonly known as the rock pincushion, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in arid and semi-arid regions.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small pincushions. The plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in crevices and on slopes. Its unique morphology includes a dense, spiky inflorescence that provides protection for its small, inconspicuous flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
