Pleurothallis diffusa

Pleurothallis diffusa, commonly known as the diffuse pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, strap-like leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its intricate floral structure and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
