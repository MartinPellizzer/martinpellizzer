Ptelea trifoliata subsp. pallida

Ptelea trifoliata subsp.

pallida, commonly known as the western winged elm, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States. It is characterized by its compound leaves, typically with three leaflets, which are often grayish-white in color.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by winged fruits that aid in wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its range.
