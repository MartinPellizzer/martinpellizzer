Poa grisebachii

Poa grisebachii, commonly known as the gray beard grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Poa grisebachii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude ecosystems.
