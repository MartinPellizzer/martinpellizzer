Piper pubivaginatum

Piper pubivaginatum, commonly known as the hairy bladderpipe, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, greenish fruits that mature to a yellowish color.

Piper pubivaginatum is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its potential medicinal properties.
