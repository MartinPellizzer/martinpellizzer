Pneumonanthe latifolia

Pneumonanthe latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf lungwort, is a striking plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often covered in distinctive patterns of waxy, white or silver markings. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shady areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
