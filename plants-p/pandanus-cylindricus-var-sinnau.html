Pandanus cylindricus var. sinnau

Pandanus cylindricus var. sinnau, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces inflorescences that bear small, fragrant flowers, which are often used in traditional crafts and rituals. Its roots and leaves are also utilized in local cuisines and medicinal practices.

This variant is well adapted to coastal and sandy environments, making it a resilient species in tropical regions.
