Pentapeltis

Pentapeltis, commonly known as the five-veined fern, is a genus of ferns belonging to the family Davalliaceae.

These plants are characterized by their delicate, feathery fronds that often have a distinctive five-veined pattern on the underside. They are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. Pentapeltis species typically thrive in humid, shaded environments, making them popular in indoor gardening.

Their ornamental value lies in their elegant appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor conditions.
