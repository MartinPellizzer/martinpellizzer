Potentilla opaciformis

Potentilla opaciformis, commonly known as the woolly cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
