Phaseolus superbus

Phaseolus superbus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and vibrant red flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and the plant thrives in warm, sunny climates.

Phaseolus superbus is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its nutritional contribution to diets in various regions.
