Picrasma ailanthoides var. dasycarpa

Picrasma ailanthoides var. dasycarpa, commonly known as the rough-fruited sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces distinctive, rough-textured fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
