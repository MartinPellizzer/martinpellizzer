Parasia grandis

Parasia grandis, commonly known as the giant parasol mushroom, is a species of fungus that belongs to the family Tricholomataceae.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-shaped cap that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The cap is usually pale yellow to brown in color, with a fibrous texture and a distinct ring around the stem. The stem is slender, hollow, and often has a bulbous base.

This fungus is typically found in temperate regions, growing in association with decaying wood in forests.
