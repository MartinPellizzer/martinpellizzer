Packera paupercula var. pseudotomentosa

Packera paupercula var. pseudotomentosa, commonly known as the Hoary Groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.
