Polygala wightiana

Polygala wightiana, commonly known as the Wight's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to South Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with a distinctive hooded structure.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
