Psidium gaudichaudianum

Psidium gaudichaudianum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or granadilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. The plant has simple, oval-shaped leaves and white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The fruit is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural pigments.
