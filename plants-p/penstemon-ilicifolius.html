Penstemon ilicifolius

Penstemon ilicifolius, commonly known as silverleaf penstemon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tall spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
