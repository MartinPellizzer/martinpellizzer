Pelargonium × tussilaginifolium

Pelargonium × tussilaginifolium, commonly known as the foxtail geranium, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from different species of geraniums.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and serrated leaves that resemble the shape of a foxtail, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.

Pelargonium × tussilaginifolium thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden beds and containers.
