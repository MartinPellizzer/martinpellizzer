Poa rigens

Poa rigens, commonly known as the bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils, particularly in meadows, lawns, and along stream banks. Its dense growth habit makes it a common turfgrass used in sports fields and golf courses.

Poa rigens is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
