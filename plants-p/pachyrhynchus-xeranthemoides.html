Pachyrhynchus xeranthemoides

Pachyrhynchus xeranthemoides, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its woody, shrub-like structure and succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in dry conditions. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Pachyrhynchus xeranthemoides is well adapted to harsh environments and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
