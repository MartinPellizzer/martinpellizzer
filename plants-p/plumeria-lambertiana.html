Plumeria lambertiana

Plumeria lambertiana, commonly known as the Lambert's frangipani, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, waxy flowers that come in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant produces fragrant blooms during the dry season, typically from November to March. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and ovate with a glossy green surface.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in warm climates.
