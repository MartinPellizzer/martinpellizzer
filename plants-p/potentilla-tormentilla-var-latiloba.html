Potentilla tormentilla var. latiloba

Potentilla tormentilla var. latiloba, commonly known as the broadleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in early summer. The leaves are typically palmately divided into five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

It is also valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
