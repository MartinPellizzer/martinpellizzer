Phylica spicata

Phylica spicata, commonly known as the silver bush or rock rose, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant has narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Phylica spicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
