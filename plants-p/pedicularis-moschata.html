Pedicularis moschata

Pedicularis moschata, commonly known as the musk orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the genus Pedicularis, which includes approximately 300 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often with a distinctive musky fragrance. It thrives in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and cool, moist environments.

Pedicularis moschata is often found growing in open woods, meadows, and rocky slopes at high altitudes.
