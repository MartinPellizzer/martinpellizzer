Pothos ovatifolius

Pothos ovatifolius, commonly known as the broad-leafed pothos, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically bright green with a slightly variegated pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, followed by yellowish berries. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and low maintenance requirements.

Its aerial roots allow it to climb and spread easily, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets or trellises.
