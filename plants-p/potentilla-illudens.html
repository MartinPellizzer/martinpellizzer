Potentilla illudens

Potentilla illudens, commonly known as the Illudens cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high elevations. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmate, with five to seven lobes, and are covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla illudens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
