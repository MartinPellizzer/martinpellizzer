Paeonia anomala

Paeonia anomala, commonly known as the meadow peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear large, showy flowers with five to eight petals. The plant has palmately divided leaves with a distinctive, hairy texture. It typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing fragrant, pink to red flowers.

Paeonia anomala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and historical medicinal uses.
