Phacelia ivesiana var. pediculoides

Phacelia ivesiana var. pediculoides, commonly known as the pediculate phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and plains. The plant features slender stems covered with fine hairs and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting pollinator populations.
