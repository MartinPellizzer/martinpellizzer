Potentilla canescens subsp. pindicola

Potentilla canescens subsp.

pindicola, commonly known as the Pindus potentilla, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe. It typically grows in rocky and dry habitats, often found in alpine and subalpine zones.

The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are covered in a fine, grayish-white pubescence. It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is valued for its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native range.
