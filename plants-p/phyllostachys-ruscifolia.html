Phyllostachys ruscifolia

Phyllostachys ruscifolia, commonly known as the ruscifolia bamboo, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is a clumping bamboo that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters, featuring slender, green culms with a smooth, pale surface. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a bright green color and a slightly hairy texture on the underside. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile ornamental plant in gardens.

This bamboo is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in landscaping for its graceful appearance and ease of maintenance.
