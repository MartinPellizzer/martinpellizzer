Palura chinensis f. leucocarpa

Palura chinensis f. leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited Chinese palura, is a unique cultivar of the Chinese palura plant.

It is characterized by its pale, almost white, fleshy fruits that contrast with the typical green or yellow fruit coloration of the species. The plant belongs to the family Solanaceae, which includes nightshades, and is native to China. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is not typically edible but is prized for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
