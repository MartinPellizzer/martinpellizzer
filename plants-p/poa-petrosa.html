Poa petrosa

Poa petrosa, commonly known as the rock grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, often found in rocky or coastal environments. The plant has narrow, tufted leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It is adapted to grow in well-drained, often calcareous soils.

Poa petrosa is typically perennial and is valued for its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil in harsh conditions.
