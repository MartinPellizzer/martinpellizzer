Polygala pterocarpa

Polygala pterocarpa, commonly known as the mountain glorybower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant is shrub-like, with slender, often branched stems and small, alternate leaves. Its flowers are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters and are pollinated primarily by birds, especially sunbirds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
