Poa chiloensis

Poa chiloensis, commonly known as the Chilean grass or mountain grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions and coastal areas.

Poa chiloensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
