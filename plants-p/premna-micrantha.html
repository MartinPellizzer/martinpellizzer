Premna micrantha

Premna micrantha, commonly known as small-flowered glorybower, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Verbenaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple, blooming in the spring and summer. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that release tiny seeds when mature.

Premna micrantha is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
