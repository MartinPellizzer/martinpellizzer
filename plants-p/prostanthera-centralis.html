Prostanthera centralis

Prostanthera centralis, commonly known as the Central Plains mint, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.

Prostanthera centralis is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
