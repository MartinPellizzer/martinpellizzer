Prangos corymbosa

Prangos corymbosa, commonly known as the Kashmiri poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
