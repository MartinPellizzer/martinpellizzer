Phyteuma halleri var. glabriflora

Phyteuma halleri var. glabriflora, commonly known as the smooth-flowered phyteuma, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing clusters of pale blue to lavender flowers. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or borders.

Its foliage is lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
