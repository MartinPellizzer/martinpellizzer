Plantago masoniae

Plantago masoniae, commonly known as Mason's plantain, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in a spike-like inflorescence. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is well-suited for xeriscaping and other low-maintenance landscaping applications.
