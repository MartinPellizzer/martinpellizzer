Pilea engleriana

Pilea engleriana, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to China and Vietnam.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are round and slightly fleshy, resembling small coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by spherical, red fruits containing seeds. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its easy care and attractive foliage, it is widely cultivated as a decorative plant.
