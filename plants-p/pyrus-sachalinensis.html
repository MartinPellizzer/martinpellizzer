Pyrus sachalinensis

Pyrus sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the Russian Far East, particularly the island of Sakhalin, and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces small, sweet, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to common pears. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a fruit-bearing plant in its native habitat.

Pyrus sachalinensis is adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
