Panicum grisebachianum

Panicum grisebachianum, commonly known as the gray panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as savannas and grasslands. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Panicum grisebachianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
