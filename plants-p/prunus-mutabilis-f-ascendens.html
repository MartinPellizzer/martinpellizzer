Prunus mutabilis f. ascendens

Prunus mutabilis f. ascendens, commonly known as the ascending cherry, is a unique cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive growth habit, where the branches rise vertically, creating a compact, upright form. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink, changing color as they progress through their development. It is a hardy shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for smaller garden spaces.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various climates.
