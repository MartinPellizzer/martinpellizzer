Paspalum virgatum var. ciliatum

Paspalum virgatum var. ciliatum, commonly known as the silky paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive silky texture along the margins, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
