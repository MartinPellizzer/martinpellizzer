Panicum madipirense

Panicum madipirense, commonly known as the Madipirense grass, is a species native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in swampy areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in wetland restoration projects.
