Pogostemon cablin

Pogostemon cablin, commonly known as patchouli, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale purple or white flowers. The plant is known for its strong, earthy fragrance, which comes from the essential oils produced in its leaves. Patchouli thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its essential oils used in perfumes and traditional medicine.

Its rhizomes and leaves are the primary parts used for extraction and medicinal purposes.
