Phyllanthus acidissimus

Phyllanthus acidissimus, commonly known as the stone apple or false apple, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa, southern Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its small, green, oval-shaped fruits that resemble apples but are typically harvested when they are unripe and green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has opposite, simple leaves with a slightly rough texture. It is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which has a tart flavor and is used in traditional cuisines.

The plant also has medicinal uses in various traditional systems of medicine.
