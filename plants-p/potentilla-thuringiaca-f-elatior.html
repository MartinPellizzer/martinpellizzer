Potentilla thuringiaca f. elatior

Potentilla thuringiaca f. elatior, commonly known as the tall potentilla, is a compact shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
