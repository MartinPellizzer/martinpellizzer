Polygonatum falcatum var. tenuiflorum

Polygonatum falcatum var. tenuiflorum, commonly known as the narrow-flowered Solomon's seal, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features elongated, arching stems that bear small, greenish-white flowers clustered in loose umbels. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its graceful foliage and delicate blooms.
