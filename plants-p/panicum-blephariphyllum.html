Panicum blephariphyllum

Panicum blephariphyllum, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its distinctive, silky, hair-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Panicum blephariphyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in wetland ecosystems.
