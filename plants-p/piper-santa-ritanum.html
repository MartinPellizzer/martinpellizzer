Piper santa-ritanum

Piper santa-ritanum, commonly known as the Santa Rita pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the humid lowland forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 2,000 species of flowering plants, many of which are used for their medicinal properties. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, berry-like fruits that are typically red when ripe. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a glossy surface and a strong, peppery aroma.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the potential medicinal uses of its leaves and fruits.
