Polygonum amgense

Polygonum amgense, commonly known as the red-rooted knotweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and wetlands. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color, especially near the base. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a distinctive waxy coating, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer.

Polygonum amgense is known for its aggressive growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
