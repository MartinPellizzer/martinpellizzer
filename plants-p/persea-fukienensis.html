Persea fukienensis

Persea fukienensis, commonly known as the Fukien persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its simple, alternate, ovate leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to reddish fruits. The fruit has a thick, leathery skin and is typically eaten raw or cooked.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
