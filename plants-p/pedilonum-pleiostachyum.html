Pedilonum pleiostachyum

Pedilonum pleiostachyum, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and clusters of golden-yellow inflorescences. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled, spike-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant color and elegant growth habit.
