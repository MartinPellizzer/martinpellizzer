Passiflora miniata

Passiflora miniata, commonly known as the miniature passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its climbing growth habit.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and botanical study.
