Prunus communis subvar. stenopetala

Prunus communis subvar. stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The species is relatively hardy and adaptable to a range of soil types and climates.
