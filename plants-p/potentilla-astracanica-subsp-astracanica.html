Potentilla astracanica subsp. astracanica

Potentilla astracanica subsp.

astracanica, commonly known as the Astracanica cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in dry, temperate climates.

The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, adding ornamental value to its habitat. Its leaves are compound, with five leaflets, and it has a compact, bushy growth habit.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
