Pyrus farsistanica

Pyrus farsistanica, commonly known as the Farsistanica pear, is a deciduous tree native to the mountains of Iran.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, often growing in rocky soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
