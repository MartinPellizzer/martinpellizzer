Potentilla sterilis

Potentilla sterilis, commonly known as the sterile cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with multiple stems. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are oval and slightly serrated.

Potentilla sterilis is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments.
