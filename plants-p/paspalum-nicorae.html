Paspalum nicorae

Paspalum nicorae, commonly known as the Nicora grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Paspalum nicorae is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in open fields.
