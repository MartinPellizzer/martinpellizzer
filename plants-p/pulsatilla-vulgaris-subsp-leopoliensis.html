Pulsatilla vulgaris subsp. leopoliensis

Pulsatilla vulgaris subsp.

leopoliensis, commonly known as the Leopoliensis pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in alpine or mountainous regions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant produces a crown of fibrous roots.

It is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early blooming and striking appearance.
