Prangos lamellata

Prangos lamellata, commonly known as the lamellate fritillary, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from violet to deep purple. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. Prangos lamellata is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its ornamental value has made it a sought-after species in botanical gardens and specialized horticulture.
