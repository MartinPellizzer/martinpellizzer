Pseudotsuga taxifolia var. brevifolia

Pseudotsuga taxifolia var. brevifolia, commonly known as the Japanese white pine, is a variant of the Japanese fir that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Japan. The plant has needle-like leaves that are shorter and more densely packed compared to the standard variety, giving it a distinct appearance. Its cones are typically small and cylindrical, containing two to three seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
