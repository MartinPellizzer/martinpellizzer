Picrococcus stamineus

Picrococcus stamineus, commonly known as the stamen fungus, is a unique plant-associated microorganism that inhabits the stamens of certain flowering plants.

It is a type of fungus that forms specialized structures called stromata, which are often embedded within the floral tissues. This fungus is known for its ability to produce pigments that can alter the color of the stamens, sometimes leading to striking visual effects in the flower. It plays a role in the ecological interactions between plants and fungi, contributing to nutrient cycling and possibly influencing pollination.

Due to its specific habitat and limited distribution, Picrococcus stamineus is of interest to mycologists and botanists studying plant-fungus relationships.
