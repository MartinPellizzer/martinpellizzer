Puya longisepala

Puya longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepal puya, is a striking member of the bromeliad family native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with vibrant purple and white flowers. The plant features strap-like, evergreen leaves that form a rosette, creating a dense, water-holding structure at the base. Its flowers are showy and long-lasting, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Puya longisepala is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
