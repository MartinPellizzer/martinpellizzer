Passiflora serrulata

Passiflora serrulata, commonly known as the serrated passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with a crown of filaments resembling a crown of thorns. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by both humans and wildlife. Its leaves are typically serrated, giving it its common name, and it thrives in humid, sunny environments.

Passiflora serrulata is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
