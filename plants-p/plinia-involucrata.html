Plinia involucrata

Plinia involucrata, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a species of shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and dense clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in a fine pubescence.

The fruit is edible, with a tart flavor, and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
