Pleurothallis platycardium

Pleurothallis platycardium, commonly known as the heartleaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that grows on trees in tropical rainforests. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It is notable for its unique heart-shaped leaf structure, which gives it its common name.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
