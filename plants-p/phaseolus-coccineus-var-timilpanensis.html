Phaseolus coccineus var. timilpanensis

Phaseolus coccineus var. timilpanensis, commonly known as the Timilpan scarlet runner bean, is a variant of the scarlet runner bean native to Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and yellow flowers and edible, tubular pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. The plant is a climbing vine with compound leaves and thrives in warm, sunny climates. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and culinary use, with the pods often harvested when young and tender.

This variety is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in traditional agriculture.
