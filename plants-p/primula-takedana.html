Primula takedana

Primula takedana, commonly known as the Takeda primrose, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a compact appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or shaded gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
