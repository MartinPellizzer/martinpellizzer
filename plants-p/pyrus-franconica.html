Pyrus franconica

Pyrus franconica, commonly known as the Franconian pear, is a deciduous tree native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The tree produces edible pears that are typically small to medium in size, with a sweet and slightly tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

Pyrus franconica is relatively hardy and can tolerate a range of soil types, making it a versatile addition to gardens and landscapes.
