Polygonum chelanicum

Polygonum chelanicum, commonly known as the redstem smartweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist soils along stream banks, wet meadows, and other wetland habitats. Its stems are often reddish in color, and the leaves are alternate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Polygonum chelanicum is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed environments.
