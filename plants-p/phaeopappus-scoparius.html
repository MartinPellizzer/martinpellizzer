Phaeopappus scoparius

Phaeopappus scoparius, commonly known as the yellow pappus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bract structures. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its yellowish to reddish bracts are often mistaken for flowers, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the center of the inflorescence.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in regions with well-drained soils.
