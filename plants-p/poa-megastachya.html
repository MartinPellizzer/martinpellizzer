Poa megastachya

Poa megastachya, commonly known as the giant tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and can form large, upright tussocks that dominate alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant produces tall, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with long, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Poa megastachya plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various alpine organisms.
