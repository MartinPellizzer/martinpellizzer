Phoradendron bolleanum var. densum

Phoradendron bolleanum var. densum, commonly known as the denseleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It lacks roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its stems, which are adapted for attachment to host bark. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers and berries, which are important for local wildlife. Its dense foliage provides habitat for various insects and birds.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity within its native ecosystems.
