Pyrrhocactus paucicostatus

Pyrrhocactus paucicostatus, commonly known as the spiny cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems.

This cactus is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
