Passiflora herbertiana var. caleyana

Passiflora herbertiana var. caleyana, commonly known as the Herbert's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that display a mix of red, yellow, and white hues. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in nutrients and have a unique, tangy flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and covered in small, hooked hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
