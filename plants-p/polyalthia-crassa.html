Polyalthia crassa

Polyalthia crassa, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has palmately compound leaves, which are typically opposite and can grow up to 30 cm in length. Its woody structure and rapid growth make it a popular choice for ornamental planting in warm climates.

The tree also produces small, round fruits that contain seeds, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.
