Paphia carrii

Paphia carrii, commonly known as the Carri's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Paphia carrii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
