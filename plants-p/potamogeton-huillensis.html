Potamogeton huillensis

Potamogeton huillensis, commonly known as the Huilliche pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in freshwater habitats such as lakes, rivers, and wetlands. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are arranged spirally along its stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or partially submerged.

Potamogeton huillensis plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
