Piper crassistipes

Piper crassistipes, commonly known as the broad-leafed pepper or the mountain pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus in the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in green or yellowish-green hues. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that ripens to a dark purple color.

Piper crassistipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
