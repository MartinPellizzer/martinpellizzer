Peristylus affinis

Peristylus affinis, commonly known as the slender peristylus, is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green to white in color, often arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
