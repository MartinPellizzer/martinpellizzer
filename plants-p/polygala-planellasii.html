Polygala planellasii

Polygala planellasii, commonly known as the Canary Islands milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or white in color. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to the dry, coastal climates of its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
