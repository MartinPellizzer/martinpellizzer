Polystichum suiyangense

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.
