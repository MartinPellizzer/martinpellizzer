Phragmites communis var. subbiflora

Phragmites communis var. subbiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved common reed, is a variant of the common reed found in wetland habitats.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and tall, cylindrical flowering stems that rise above the water. This plant plays a crucial role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial species. It is often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in brackish water make it a significant component of coastal and wetland ecosystems.
