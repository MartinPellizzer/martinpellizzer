Potentilla arguta

Potentilla arguta, commonly known as the silver cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Potentilla arguta thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.
