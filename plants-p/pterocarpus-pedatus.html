Pterocarpus pedatus

Pterocarpus pedatus, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Africa, particularly found in countries like Nigeria and Ghana.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces distinctive yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod containing flat, seed-like structures that are dispersed by wind.

The tree is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in traditional medicine and carpentry.
