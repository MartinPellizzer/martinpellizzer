Piper submolle

Piper submolle, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Central America and parts of Mexico, where it typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by small, peppery fruits.

Piper submolle is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
