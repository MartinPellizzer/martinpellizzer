Piper magnantherum

Piper magnantherum, commonly known as the Malabar pepper, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, green, oval-shaped berries that turn red when ripe, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are ovate with a glossy surface and emit a pungent odor when crushed.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and flavoring applications.
