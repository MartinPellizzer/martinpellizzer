Pleioblastus leucorhodus

Pleioblastus leucorhodus, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching culms that are covered in a fine, silvery-white powder, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are usually a light green color, and it grows in dense clumps. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Pleioblastus leucorhodus is relatively low maintenance and is valued for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
