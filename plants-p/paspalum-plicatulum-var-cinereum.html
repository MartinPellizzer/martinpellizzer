Paspalum plicatulum var. cinereum

Paspalum plicatulum var. cinereum, commonly known as black paspalum, is a perennial grass native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its dark green to almost black foliage and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
