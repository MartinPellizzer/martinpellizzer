Pterocarpus marginatus

Pterocarpus marginatus, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers. The tree produces hard, woody pods that contain a valuable seed kernel used in the production of shea butter. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to thrive in dry and poor soil conditions.

The plant is also valued for its timber, which is used in traditional crafts and construction.
