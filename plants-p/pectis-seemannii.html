Pectis seemannii

Pectis seemannii, commonly known as the Cape dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish to orange-yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually linear and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often succulent.

Pectis seemannii is often found in coastal and inland regions, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
