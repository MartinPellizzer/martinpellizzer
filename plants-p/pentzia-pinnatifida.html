Pentzia pinnatifida

Pentzia pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mountain grass, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in mountainous regions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
