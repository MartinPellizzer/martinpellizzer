Pachyanthus cordifolius

Pachyanthus cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved pachyanthus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a glossy surface. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in clusters. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in suitable environments.
