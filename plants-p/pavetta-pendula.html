Pavetta pendula

Pavetta pendula, commonly known as the hanging pavetta, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, characterized by its pendulous inflorescences and small, fragrant flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with five petals and numerous stamens, making it visually striking.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.
