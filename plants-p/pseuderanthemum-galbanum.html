Pseuderanthemum galbanum

Pseuderanthemum galbanum, commonly known as the golden pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of small, showy yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.
