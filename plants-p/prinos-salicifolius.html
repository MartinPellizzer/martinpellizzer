Prinos salicifolius

Prinos salicifolius, commonly known as the willow-leaf prinos, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of willow trees, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
