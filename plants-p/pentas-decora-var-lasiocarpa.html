Pentas decora var. lasiocarpa

Pentas decora var. lasiocarpa, commonly known as the wooly pentas, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has hairy, or pubescent, foliage that gives it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
