Prunus glandulosa var. albiplena

Prunus glandulosa var. albiplena, commonly known as the white-flowered cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth margin and a glossy, dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, red drupes that ripen in the summer, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
