Pyrus nantouensis

Pyrus nantouensis, commonly known as Nantou pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, sweet, yellowish-green fruits that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese cuisine. It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary attributes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
