Potentilla inclinata f. polydonta

Potentilla inclinata f. polydonta, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a flowering plant characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit.

It features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are usually arranged in a dense cluster at the base. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained, rocky soils.

This variety is notable for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
