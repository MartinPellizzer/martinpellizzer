Phyllorkis psittacoglossa

Phyllorkis psittacoglossa, commonly known as the parrot's tongue orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated, and often curved petals that resemble a parrot's beak, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within tropical forests. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a contrasting purple or brown lip, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Phyllorkis psittacoglossa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
