Papyrus odorata

Papyrus odorata, commonly known as sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in Asia and Africa.

It features tall, reed-like stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often with a distinct musky or sweet fragrance. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes, usually appearing in late summer or early autumn.

Papyrus odorata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
