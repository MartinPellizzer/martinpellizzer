Pelargonium × castaliifolium

Pelargonium × castaliifolium, commonly known as the Castaliifolium geranium, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a unique combination of traits from its parent species.

It typically features lobed or serrated leaves, which are often a striking shade of green, sometimes with a reddish or purple tinge. The plant produces showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, arranged in clusters that add visual interest. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for both garden beds and container cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for decorative purposes in horticulture.
