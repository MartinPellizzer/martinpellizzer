Phagnalon saxatile var. lagascae

Phagnalon saxatile var. lagascae, commonly known as the Alpine phagnalon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, contributing to its role in alpine ecosystems. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine meadows and slopes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is adapted to cold climates and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
