Psoralea micrantha

Psoralea micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub or herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are often borne on slender stalks. Its leaves are usually small, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Psoralea micrantha is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure and adaptations to arid environments.
