Peliosanthes inaperta

Peliosanthes inaperta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yew.

It is a rare and critically endangered plant species native to southern China. The plant is characterized by its slender, evergreen foliage and small, clustered yellow flowers. It produces red, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
