Parasenecio yakusimensis

Parasenecio yakusimensis, commonly known as the Yakushima ragged-roof aster, is a flowering plant native to the island of Yakushima in Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions with high humidity. Its foliage is usually dark green and leathery, adapted to the cool, humid climate of its habitat.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
