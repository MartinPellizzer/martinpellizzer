Pinus × naxiorum

Pinus × naxiorum, commonly known as the Naxi pine, is a hybrid conifer resulting from the cross between Pinus yunnanensis and Pinus armandii.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China, where it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. The tree typically grows to a height of 20–30 meters, featuring a conical shape and needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in bundles of five. Its cones are woody and contain two-winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
