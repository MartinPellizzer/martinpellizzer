Prunus × lannesiana f. idzuensis

Prunus × lannesiana f. idzuensis, commonly known as the Idzu cherry, is a unique botanical variant of the Prunus genus.

It is a hybrid species, resulting from the cross between Prunus lannesiana and Prunus × yedoensis. This plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
