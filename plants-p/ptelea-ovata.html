Ptelea ovata

Ptelea ovata, commonly known as the ovalleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the United States.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodlands and rocky slopes.
