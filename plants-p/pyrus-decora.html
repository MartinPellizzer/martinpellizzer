Pyrus decora

Pyrus decora, commonly known as the western whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet berries in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Pyrus decora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
