Psidium acutangulum

Psidium acutangulum, commonly known as the rough passionfruit, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that develop into orange to red berries. The fruit has a thick, leathery rind and contains a sweet, aromatic pulp with numerous seeds.
