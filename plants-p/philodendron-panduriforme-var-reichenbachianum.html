Philodendron panduriforme var. reichenbachianum

Philodendron panduriforme var. reichenbachianum, commonly known as the Reichenbach's philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy, dark green leaves that are often adorned with a distinctive white or pale margin. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, which are typically followed by elongated, red or orange berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

This variety is popular among indoor gardeners for its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
