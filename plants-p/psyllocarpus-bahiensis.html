Psyllocarpus bahiensis

Psyllocarpus bahiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian balsa tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pods containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its lightweight wood, which is used in various industrial applications.

However, it is also considered a threatened species due to habitat loss and overexploitation.
