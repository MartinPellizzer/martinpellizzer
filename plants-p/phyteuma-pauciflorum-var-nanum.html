Phyteuma pauciflorum var. nanum

Phyteuma pauciflorum var. nanum, commonly known as the dwarf phyteuma, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of no more than 30 centimeters, making it ideal for rock gardens or borders. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, blooming from late spring to early summer.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, visually appealing plants.
