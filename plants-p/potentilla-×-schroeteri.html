Potentilla × schroeteri

Potentilla × schroeteri, commonly known as the Schröter's cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage of dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climatic conditions.
