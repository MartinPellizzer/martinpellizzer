Phlomis anisodonta subsp. occidentalis

Phlomis anisodonta subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as Western betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, branching stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height.

The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in whorled clusters at the ends of the stems, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and gravelly areas.
