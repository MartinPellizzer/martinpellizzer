Phyteuma michelii var. involucratum

Phyteuma michelii var. involucratum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, violet-purple flower heads that are surrounded by a series of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
