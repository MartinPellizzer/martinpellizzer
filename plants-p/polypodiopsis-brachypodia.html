Polypodiopsis brachypodia

Polypodiopsis brachypodia, commonly known as the short-stemmed maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces spores in specialized structures called sporangia, which are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions.

Its unique morphology and reproductive features make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
