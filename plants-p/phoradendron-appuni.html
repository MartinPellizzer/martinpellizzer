Phoradendron appuni

Phoradendron appuni, commonly known as the red mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus *Eucalyptus*.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are often inconspicuous, followed by fleshy, red berries that contain seeds. These berries are dispersed by birds, which eat the fruit and excrete the seeds onto other host trees.

Its ecological role is significant in shaping the structure and diversity of forest ecosystems.
