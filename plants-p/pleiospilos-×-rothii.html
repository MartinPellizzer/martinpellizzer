Pleiospilos × rothii

Pleiospilos × rothii, commonly known as the hybrid moonstone plant, is a succulent belonging to the Aizoaceae family.

It is characterized by its fleshy, spiky leaves that form a dense rosette, often with a bluish or grayish hue. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on long, slender stalks. It is native to southern Africa and is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
