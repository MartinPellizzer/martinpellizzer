Prunus padus monstr. plena

Prunus padus monstr.

plena, commonly known as the double-flowered white cherry, is a cultivar of the common cherry tree. It is characterized by its dense, double flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a striking visual display.

The plant has a spreading habit with slender, arching branches that support the heavy clusters of blossoms. Its leaves are oval and serrated, turning yellow in the fall.

This ornamental tree is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often planted in gardens for its ornamental value.
