Polypodium varium

Polypodium varium, commonly known as the variable huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are typically divided into lance-shaped segments. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including China, India, and Southeast Asia. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

Polypodium varium is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
