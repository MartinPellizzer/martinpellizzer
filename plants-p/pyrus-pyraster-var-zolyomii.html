Pyrus pyraster var. zolyomii

Pyrus pyraster var. zolyomii, commonly known as the Zolyom pear, is a variant of the common pear tree native to Hungary.

It is characterized by its small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough upper surface and a hairy underside. It produces white flowers in early spring, followed by fruiting in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in Hungarian horticulture.
