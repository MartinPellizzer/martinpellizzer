Passiflora quelchii

Passiflora quelchii, commonly known as the Peruvian passionflower, is a tropical climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a crown of filaments and a central corona with intricate patterns. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Passiflora quelchii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
