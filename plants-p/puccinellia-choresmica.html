Puccinellia choresmica

Puccinellia choresmica, commonly known as the Caspian grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny florets, typical of many grass species. It is adapted to saline and drought conditions, making it a common component of steppe and desert ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important forage plant in certain regions.
