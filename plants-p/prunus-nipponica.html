Prunus nipponica

Prunus nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Japanese flowering cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are ovate with a pointed tip and turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
