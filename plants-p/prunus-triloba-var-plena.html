Prunus triloba var. plena

Prunus triloba var. plena, commonly known as the double-flowered Amur cherry, is a cultivar of the Amur cherry tree.

It is characterized by its dense, double blossoms that appear in early spring, giving it a lush, almost floral appearance. The plant is native to eastern Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. Its foliage is dark green and provides a striking contrast to the showy flowers.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile choice for temperate climates.
