Pittosporum fulvipilosum

Pittosporum fulvipilosum, commonly known as the golden pittosporum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that appear in late spring to early summer, followed by orange-red berries. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest areas.

Pittosporum fulvipilosum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
