Phacelia infundibuliformis var. infundibuliformis

Phacelia infundibuliformis var. infundibuliformis, commonly known as the desert bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender stems and small, clustered blue or purple flowers. The plant has hairy foliage and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important species in its ecosystem.
