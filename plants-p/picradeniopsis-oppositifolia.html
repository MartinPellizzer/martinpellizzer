Picradeniopsis oppositifolia

Picradeniopsis oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leafed picradeniopsis, is a rare shrub native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement, which is a distinctive botanical feature. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its flowers are small and clustered.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
