Passerina bruniades

Passerina bruniades, commonly known as the Bruniades pasqueflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, early spring blooms. The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers with prominent sepals. It thrives in alpine and subalpine habitats, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific environmental requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
