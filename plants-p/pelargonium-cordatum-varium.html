Pelargonium cordatum-varium

Pelargonium cordatum-varium, commonly known as the heartleaf geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in temperate climates.
