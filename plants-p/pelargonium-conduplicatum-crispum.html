Pelargonium conduplicatum-crispum

Pelargonium conduplicatum-crispum, commonly known as the crinkled geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crinkled leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners looking to add texture and color to their landscapes.
