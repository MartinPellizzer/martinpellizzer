Piper chiadoense

Piper chiadoense, commonly known as the Chiadoense pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest, specifically found in Brazil, and grows as a climbing vine. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, and the leaves are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish flowers.

Piper chiadoense is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is part of the diverse flora of the Amazon region.
