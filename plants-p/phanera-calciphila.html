Phanera calciphila

Phanera calciphila, commonly known as the limestone phanera, is a perennial herb native to calcareous soils in Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats. Its floral structure includes five petals and stamens, typical of the Malvaceae family.

Phanera calciphila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
