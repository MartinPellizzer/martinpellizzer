Primula laevigata

Primula laevigata, commonly known as the smooth primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It features oval, dark green leaves that are smooth and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
