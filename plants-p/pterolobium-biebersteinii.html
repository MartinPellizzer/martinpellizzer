Pterolobium biebersteinii

Pterolobium biebersteinii, commonly known as the African sarsaparilla, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of many legumes. It is often found in savanna and woodland habitats, where it contributes to soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.

Its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
