Pandorea floribunda

Pandorea floribunda, commonly known as the evergreen climbing vine, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping and as a climbing plant in gardens.
