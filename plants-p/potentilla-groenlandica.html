Potentilla groenlandica

Potentilla groenlandica, commonly known as Greenland cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats.

Potentilla groenlandica plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native ecosystems.
