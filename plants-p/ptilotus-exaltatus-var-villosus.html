Ptilotus exaltatus var. villosus

Ptilotus exaltatus var. villosus, commonly known as the silky bush morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, sprawling stems and clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces distinctive, orb-shaped seed pods that turn brown as they mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
