Pomatocalpa naevatum

Pomatocalpa naevatum, commonly known as the speckled orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often colorful flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its flowers are clustered in inflorescences.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
