Pterospermum venustum

Pterospermum venustum, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a broad, spreading canopy. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive yellowish-green color with a velvety texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and typically bloom in the summer months.

The tree is valued for its ornamental value and is often planted in parks and gardens for its striking foliage.
