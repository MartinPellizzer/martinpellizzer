Pyrrocoma racemosa var. pinetorum

Pyrrocoma racemosa var. pinetorum, commonly known as the pineleaf pyrrocoma, is a variant of the pyrrocoma genus native to certain regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that resemble pine needles, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its flowers are small and clustered in racemes, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptability to harsh environments and its role in local ecosystems.
