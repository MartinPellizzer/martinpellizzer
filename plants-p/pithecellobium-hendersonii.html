Pithecellobium hendersonii

Pithecellobium hendersonii, commonly known as the Henderson's pithecellobium, is a species of legume native to Central America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces edible, bean-like pods that are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils.

Its growth habit includes shrub-like forms, making it suitable for ornamental and agricultural purposes.
