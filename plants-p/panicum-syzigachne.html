Panicum syzigachne

Panicum syzigachne, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant typically grows in full sun and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open fields and disturbed areas. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with leaves that can reach up to 30 inches in height.

Panicum syzigachne is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
