Phalaenopsis erubescens

Phalaenopsis erubescens, commonly known as the red orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and clusters of showy, pinkish-red flowers. The plant typically blooms once a year, producing numerous flowers that can last several months. It prefers bright, indirect light and moderate humidity, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its striking appearance and ease of care contribute to its popularity among orchid enthusiasts.
