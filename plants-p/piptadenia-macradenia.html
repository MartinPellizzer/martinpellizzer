Piptadenia macradenia

Piptadenia macradenia, commonly known as the Brazilian cedar or yellow cedar, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain flat, winged seeds, facilitating wind dispersal. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid lowland forests.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture making.
