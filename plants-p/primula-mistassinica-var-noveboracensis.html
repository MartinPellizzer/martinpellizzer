Primula mistassinica var. noveboracensis

Primula mistassinica var. noveboracensis, commonly known as the New York primrose, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a basal rosette of hairy, ovate leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a native plant supporting local ecosystems and its ornamental value in gardens.
