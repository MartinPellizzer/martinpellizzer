Picris sonchoides var. monticola

Picris sonchoides var. monticola, commonly known as mountain sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are broadly lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and healing properties.
