Parasenecio kamtschaticus

Parasenecio kamtschaticus, commonly known as the Kamtschatic mountain ragwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe and Western Siberia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc of tubular florets.

The species is noted for its ecological significance in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
