Piper hemionitidifolium

Piper hemionitidifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It produces peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and ethnobotanical studies.
