Pogonia physurifolia

Pogonia physurifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pink.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia. The plant features a slender, leafless stem with a single, showy pink or purple flower that has a distinctive hood-like structure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often found at the base of the plant.

Pogonia physurifolia thrives in wet, acidic habitats such as swamps and pine flatwoods.
