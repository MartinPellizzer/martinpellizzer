Phlox cyanea

Phlox cyanea, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and has narrow, gray-green leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Phlox cyanea is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
