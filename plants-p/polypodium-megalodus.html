Polypodium megalodus

Polypodium megalodus, commonly known as the giant fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found growing on trees or rocks in rainforests. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are typically clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Polypodium megalodus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in maintaining ecological balance in its native habitats.
