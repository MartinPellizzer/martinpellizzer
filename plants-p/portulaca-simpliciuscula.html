Portulaca simpliciuscula

Portulaca simpliciuscula, commonly known as the dwarf portulaca, is a low-growing, succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features fleshy, erect stems and small, vibrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or orange. The plant has small, rounded leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
