Poa pratensis var. plicata

Poa pratensis var. plicata, commonly known as the folded meadow grass, is a variant of the common meadow grass.

It is characterized by its distinctive folded leaves, which give it a unique appearance compared to the standard variety. This plant is typically found in moist, temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in landscaping and turfgrass mixes due to its fine texture and adaptability.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is known for its clumping growth habit.
