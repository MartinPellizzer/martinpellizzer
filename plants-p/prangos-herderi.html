Prangos herderi

Prangos herderi, commonly known as the Herder's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau and adjacent regions.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Prangos herderi produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
