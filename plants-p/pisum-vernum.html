Pisum vernum

Pisum vernum, commonly known as the spring vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous pods and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in slender, cylindrical pods and are often used as forage or in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Pisum vernum make it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
