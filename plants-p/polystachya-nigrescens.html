Polystachya nigrescens

Polystachya nigrescens, commonly known as the black-flowered spike orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which contrast with its green, strap-like leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, elongated flowers that bloom in late summer.

Polystachya nigrescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of tropical flora.
