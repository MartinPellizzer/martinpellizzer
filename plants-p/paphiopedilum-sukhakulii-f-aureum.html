Paphiopedilum sukhakulii f. aureum

Paphiopedilum sukhakulii f. aureum, commonly known as the golden-flowered slipper orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to Thailand.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, pouch-shaped lip that resembles a slipper. The plant produces showy, golden-yellow flowers with intricate patterns and a strong, sweet fragrance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
