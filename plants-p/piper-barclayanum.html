Piper barclayanum

Piper barclayanum, commonly known as the Trinidad pepper or Trinidad wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated, slender fruits containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
