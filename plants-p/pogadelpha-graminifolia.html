Pogadelpha graminifolia

Pogadelpha graminifolia, commonly known as the grassy pogadelpha, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale blue in color. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open grasslands or along riverbanks.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying plant adaptation in arid environments.
