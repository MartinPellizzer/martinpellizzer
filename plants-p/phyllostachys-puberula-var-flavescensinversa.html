Phyllostachys puberula var. flavescensinversa

Phyllostachys puberula var. flavescensinversa, commonly known as the reverse golden bamboo, is a variant of the silver bamboo species.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-green culms that often display a reverse coloration pattern compared to the typical green. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are slightly pubescent, giving it a soft texture. It is a clump-forming grass that grows in dense stands, often reaching heights of up to 10 feet.

This ornamental bamboo is prized for its unique coloration and is commonly used in landscaping and bamboo gardens.
