Physalis sugunda

Physalis sugunda, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its hairy, yellowish-green stems and clusters of small, green, berry-like fruits that turn bright orange when mature. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for its antioxidant properties.
