Piper trigonodrupum

Piper trigonodrupum, commonly known as the three-lobed pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with three lobes and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated berries. The berries contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
