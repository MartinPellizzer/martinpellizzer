Polygala jaliscana

Polygala jaliscana, commonly known as the Jalisco milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spiny stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its flowers are often white or pale pink, and it produces small, bean-like fruits that contain seeds.

Polygala jaliscana is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
