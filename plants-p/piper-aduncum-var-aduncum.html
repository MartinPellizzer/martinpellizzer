Piper aduncum var. aduncum

Piper aduncum var. aduncum, commonly known as the creeping pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into three-lobed, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it a unique species within the Piper genus, with potential uses in traditional medicine.
