Psidium indicum

Psidium indicum, commonly known as the common guava, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, fleshy fruits with a sweet and aromatic flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its fruit, which is rich in vitamin C and other nutrients.

The plant's ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical areas.
