Polianthes geminiflora var. pueblensis

Polianthes geminiflora var. pueblensis, commonly known as the Pueblan double-flowered agave, is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces strikingly large, white or pale blue flowers in dense clusters, giving it its "double-flowered" appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique beauty.
