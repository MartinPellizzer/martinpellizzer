Poa laxa subsp. banffiana

Poa laxa subsp.

banffiana, commonly known as the Banff bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the alpine regions of Alberta, Canada. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and fine, blue-green foliage that thrives in cool, moist environments.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to greenish flowers, which are adapted to pollinate by wind. It is well-suited to rocky and gravelly soils, often found in mountain meadows and alpine tundra.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
