Pyrus communis var. fallaciosa

Pyrus communis var. fallaciosa, commonly known as the fallacious pear, is a botanical variant of the common pear.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often irregular leaf shape and a unique fruit morphology that differs from the standard pear. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and prefers well-drained soils. It is a deciduous tree that can reach heights of up to 10 meters.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in horticultural and taxonomic studies.
