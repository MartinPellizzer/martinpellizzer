Plectis

Plectis, commonly known as the common plectis, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and opposite, linear to lanceolate leaves. The flowers are typically small and clustered in composite heads, featuring both ray and disk florets. Plectis species are often found in disturbed soils and open habitats across Europe and parts of Asia.

They are generally not cultivated for ornamental purposes but are of interest in ecological studies.
