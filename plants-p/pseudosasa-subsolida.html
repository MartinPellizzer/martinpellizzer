Pseudosasa subsolida

Pseudosasa subsolida, commonly known as the subterranean bamboo, is a grass-like plant native to eastern China.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, forming dense clumps of narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories and mountainous regions. Its culms are typically short and stout, with a fibrous, woody texture.

Pseudosasa subsolida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
