Papaver orientale var. intermedium

Papaver orientale var. intermedium, commonly known as the Persian poppy, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow, with a prominent black center. The plant has smooth, waxy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and for its historical use in traditional medicine.
