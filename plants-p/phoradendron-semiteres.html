Phoradendron semiteres

Phoradendron semiteres, commonly known as the rubber vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing seeds. It is known for its ability to exude a milky sap, which has been historically used for rubber production.

Its growth habit and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitat.
