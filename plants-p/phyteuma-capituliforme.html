Phyteuma capituliforme

Phyteuma capituliforme, commonly known as the clustered phyteuma, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Phyteuma capituliforme is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
