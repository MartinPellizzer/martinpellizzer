Polystichum × takaosanense

Polystichum × takaosanense, commonly known as the Takaosan bracken, is a hybrid fern native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds that are deeply divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Polystichum × takaosanense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its dense, upright growth habit.
