Pterocarpus elisabethvillensis

Pterocarpus elisabethvillensis, commonly known as the Elizabethville flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family Fabaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces hard, woody pods that contain flat, seed-like structures.

Its bark is thick and often flaky, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.
