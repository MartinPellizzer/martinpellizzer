Pleopeltis macrocarpa var. laciniata

Pleopeltis macrocarpa var. laciniata, commonly known as the lace fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, often found on tree trunks or rocks in humid environments. Its fronds are arching and have a feathery appearance, with a pale green to bluish-green coloration.

The plant prefers indirect light and consistent moisture, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
