Potentilla × adscendens

Potentilla × adscendens, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with sprawling stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly serrated edge, and the plant is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Potentilla × adscendens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping for erosion control and ground cover.
