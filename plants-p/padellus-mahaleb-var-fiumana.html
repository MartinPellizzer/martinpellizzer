Padellus mahaleb var. fiumana

Padellus mahaleb var. fiumana, commonly known as the Mahaleb cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible, red, cherry-like fruits that are often used in culinary applications. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its seasonal color changes and fragrant blossoms.
