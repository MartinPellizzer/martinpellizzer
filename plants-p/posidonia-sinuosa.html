Posidonia sinuosa

Posidonia sinuosa, commonly known as the sinuous seagrass, is a marine flowering plant native to the coastal waters of the Mediterranean Sea.

It forms dense underwater meadows that play a crucial role in stabilizing sediments and maintaining water clarity. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length, adapted for life in shallow, sunlit waters. It reproduces through both sexual and asexual means, with its seeds dispersed by water currents.

Posidonia sinuosa also provides essential habitat and food for a variety of marine organisms.
