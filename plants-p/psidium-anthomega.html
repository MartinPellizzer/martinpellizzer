Psidium anthomega

Psidium anthomega, commonly known as the purple guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its attractive purple fruits. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy green upper surface.

The flowers are small, white, and fragrant, often appearing in clusters, and the fruit is a smooth, fleshy berry that ripens to a deep purple color.
