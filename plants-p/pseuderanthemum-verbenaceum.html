Pseuderanthemum verbenaceum

Pseuderanthemum verbenaceum, commonly known as the false pimpernel, is a member of the Verbenaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences and are usually white or pale blue in color.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
