Photinia magnoliifolia

Photinia magnoliifolia, commonly known as the magnolia leaf photinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves that resemble those of magnolia species, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in humid, subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its attractive foliage and flowers, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in warm regions.
