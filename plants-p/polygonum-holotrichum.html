Polygonum holotrichum

Polygonum holotrichum, commonly known as the hairy knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant is notable for its dense, hairy foliage, which gives it its common name and helps in identifying it in the wild. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Polygonum holotrichum is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
