Piper heteromallum

Piper heteromallum, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper or wild pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Peru, and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves, which are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by small, three-lobed berries.

The plant is often found in humid, shaded environments such as forests and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
