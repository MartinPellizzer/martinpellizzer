Piper dilatatum var. vincentianum

Piper dilatatum var. vincentianum, commonly known as the Vincentian pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as the black pepper plant. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow and are followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
