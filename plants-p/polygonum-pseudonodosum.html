Polygonum pseudonodosum

Polygonum pseudonodosum, commonly known as the nodding smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other species like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, pinkish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

Polygonum pseudonodosum is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of streams, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
