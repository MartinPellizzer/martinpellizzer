Philotheca acrolopha

Philotheca acrolopha, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ironwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Philotheca acrolopha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
