Protea carnosa

Protea carnosa, commonly known as the velvet bushveld protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and grassland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers come in shades of white, pink, and red, and they attract pollinators such as birds and insects.

Protea carnosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
