Pavia × carnea

Pavia × carnea, commonly known as the red pavia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, red or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, grayish bark. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Pavia × carnea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in native plant gardens.
