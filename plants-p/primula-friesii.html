Primula friesii

Primula friesii, commonly known as the Fries' primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, and its leaves are dark green with a slightly hairy texture. Primula friesii thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
