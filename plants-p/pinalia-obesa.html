Pinalia obesa

Pinalia obesa, commonly known as the fat plant, is a unique member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its swollen, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically thick and glossy, with a waxy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Pinalia obesa produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, often blooming during the warmer months.
