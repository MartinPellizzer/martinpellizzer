Pavonia tuberculata

Pavonia tuberculata, commonly known as the rough pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is characterized by oval, serrated leaves with a rough texture.

Pavonia tuberculata thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
