Pitcairnia breedlovei

Pitcairnia breedlovei, commonly known as the Brazilian pineapple plant, is a species of bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically bluish-green with a waxy coating. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences that are often red or purple in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Pitcairnia breedlovei is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
