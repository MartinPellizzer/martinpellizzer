Polygala nicaeensis var. italiana

Polygala nicaeensis var. italiana, commonly known as the Italian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showing a preference for well-drained soils. Its flowers, which are usually pink or purple, are adapted for pollination by insects.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
