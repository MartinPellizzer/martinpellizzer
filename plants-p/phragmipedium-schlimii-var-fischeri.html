Phragmipedium schlimii var. fischeri

Phragmipedium schlimii var. fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's phragmipedium, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers with vibrant purple and yellow hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, adapted to capture moisture from the humid environment.

This variety is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and rarity in cultivation.
