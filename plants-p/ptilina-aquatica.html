Ptilina aquatica

Ptilina aquatica, commonly known as the water fern, is a aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that resemble soft, flowing hair.

It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. The plant's structure consists of rhizomes that spread horizontally, supporting the growth of its floating leaves. Its reproductive system involves the production of spores, which are released into the water to propagate new plants.

Ptilina aquatica plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic life.
