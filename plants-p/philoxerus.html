Philoxerus

Philoxerus, commonly known as the elephant's foot, is a unique succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy, and often bulbous stems that resemble the foot of an elephant, hence its common name. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to store water, making it highly drought-resistant. Its leaves are typically small and scale-like, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Philoxerus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid environments.
