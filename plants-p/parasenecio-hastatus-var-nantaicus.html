Parasenecio hastatus var. nantaicus

Parasenecio hastatus var. nantaicus, commonly known as the Nantaic Mountain Daisy, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with white margins.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
