Prunus sativa proles rubescens

Prunus sativa proles rubescens, commonly known as the Crimson Cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, contributing to its seasonal beauty. Its fruit, a small cherry-like drupe, contains a single seed surrounded by a fleshy pulp.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
