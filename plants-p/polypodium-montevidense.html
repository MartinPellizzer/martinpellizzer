Polypodium montevidense

Polypodium montevidense, commonly known as the Montevidense shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its rhizome and fronds that are typically divided into segments. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually leathery and have a distinctive, shield-like appearance, which gives it its common name.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
