Piptatherum sogdianum subsp. fedtschenkoi

Piptatherum sogdianum subsp.

fedtschenkoi, commonly known as the Sogdian grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in steppes and dry grasslands.

The plant has slender, tufted culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.
