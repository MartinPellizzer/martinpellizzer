Plectocephalus

Plectocephalus, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their compact, bushy growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The leaves are typically velvety and heart-shaped, often with a waxy texture. Plectocephalus species are popular as ornamental plants due to their vibrant blooms and ease of cultivation.

They thrive in well-drained soil and require bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
