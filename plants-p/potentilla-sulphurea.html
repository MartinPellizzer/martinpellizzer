Potentilla sulphurea

Potentilla sulphurea, commonly known as golden potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters and are often accompanied by finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its botanical features include a low-growing shrub form with woody stems and small, oval-shaped leaves.

Potentilla sulphurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
