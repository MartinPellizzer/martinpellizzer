Pleiocarpidia opaca

Pleiocarpidia opaca, commonly known as the broad-leafed pleiocarpidia, is a rare and lesser-known plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a distinctive capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a botanically significant but under-studied species.
