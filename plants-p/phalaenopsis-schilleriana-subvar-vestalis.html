Phalaenopsis schilleriana subvar. vestalis

Phalaenopsis schilleriana subvar. vestalis, commonly known as the Schiller's Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. It has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, aiding in its survival during dry periods.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
