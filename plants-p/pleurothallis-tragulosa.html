Pleurothallis tragulosa

Pleurothallis tragulosa, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. It produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and white, often with intricate patterns on the petals. The plant has small, strap-like leaves and is known for its relatively compact size.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular species among orchid enthusiasts.
