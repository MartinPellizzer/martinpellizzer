Pseudanthus virgatus

Pseudanthus virgatus, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features a rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in spring, producing seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

The plant is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats.
