Phlomoides arctifolia

Phlomoides arctifolia, commonly known as Arctic phlomoides, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in harsh, cold environments and is often found in tundra and alpine habitats.

Phlomoides arctifolia is adapted to low light conditions and has a low-growing habit to withstand strong winds.
