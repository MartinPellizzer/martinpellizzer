Peperomia raiateensis

Peperomia raiateensis, commonly known as the Raiatea peperomia, is a species of flowering plant native to the island of Tahiti in the South Pacific.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and glossy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia raiateensis is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance and ornamental foliage.
