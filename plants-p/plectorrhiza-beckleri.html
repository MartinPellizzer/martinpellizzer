Plectorrhiza beckleri

Plectorrhiza beckleri, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant has simple, opposite leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. Its seeds are dispersed by ants, a process known as myrmecochory.

The plant is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of incense.
