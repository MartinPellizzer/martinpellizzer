Psilonema calycinum

Psilonema calycinum, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange bract-like flowers that resemble coral, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance.

Psilonema calycinum is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its vibrant floral display.
