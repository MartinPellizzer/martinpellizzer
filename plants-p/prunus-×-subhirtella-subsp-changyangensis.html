Prunus × subhirtella subsp. changyangensis

Prunus × subhirtella subsp.

changyangensis, commonly known as the Changyang cherry, is a hybrid species native to China. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically edible and have a sweet, tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
