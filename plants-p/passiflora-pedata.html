Passiflora pedata

Passiflora pedata, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of purple, blue, and white hues. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Passiflora pedata is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
