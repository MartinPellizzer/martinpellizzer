Prunus × lannesiana f. temari

Prunus × lannesiana f. temari, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its delicate, early-blooming flowers that display a range of pink to white hues. The plant is a hybrid between Prunus lannesiana and Prunus incisa, resulting in its unique floral characteristics. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The tree typically reaches a height of 4 to 8 meters and is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens and public spaces.
