Pastinaca glandulosa

Pastinaca glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular burdock, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and purple to brown flowers. The plant produces small, spiky fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
