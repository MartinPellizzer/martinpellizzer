Pyrus serikensis

Pyrus serikensis, commonly known as the Tien Shan pear, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruit with a crisp texture. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental and edible value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and orchards.
