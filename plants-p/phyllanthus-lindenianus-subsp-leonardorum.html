Phyllanthus lindenianus subsp. leonardorum

Phyllanthus lindenianus subsp.

leonardorum, commonly known as the Leonard's spurge, is a shrub native to parts of Central and South America. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and smooth, often hairy foliage.

The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sun. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a distinctive, slightly lobed shape.
