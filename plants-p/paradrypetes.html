Paradrypetes

Paradrypetes, commonly known as the African cycad, is a genus of cycads native to southern Africa.

These plants are characterized by their stout, unbranched stems and large, pinnately compound leaves. They produce cones rather than flowers, and their reproductive structures are typically separate, with male and female cones on different plants. Paradrypetes species are often found in dry, rocky habitats and are adapted to arid conditions.

Due to their slow growth and ecological significance, they are of interest in both botanical research and conservation efforts.
