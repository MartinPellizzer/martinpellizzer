Pseudalcantarea

Pseudalcantarea, commonly known as the false alcantarea, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Bromeliaceae.

These plants are native to South America, particularly Brazil, and are often found in humid, shaded environments. They are characterized by their rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, a feature common among bromeliads. The flowers, which are typically red or orange, bloom from the center of the rosette and attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Pseudalcantarea species are valued for their ornamental beauty and are sometimes cultivated as houseplants.
