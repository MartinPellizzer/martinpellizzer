Pithecellobium racemosum

Pithecellobium racemosum, commonly known as the racemose pithecolobium, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces edible pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
