Polygonum hastatotrilobum var. lenticulare

Polygonum hastatotrilobum var. lenticulare, commonly known as the three-lobed knotweed, is a variant of the polygonum genus characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to specific regions of Asia, typically found in moist, shaded environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red, adding ornamental value to its habitat. Its stems are often reddish and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its visual appeal.

The leaves are lance-shaped with three distinct lobes, giving it a unique and identifiable appearance.
