Pfaffia meridensis

Pfaffia meridensis, commonly known as the golden root, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, rounded fruits containing numerous seeds. Pfaffia meridensis is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its high concentration of alkaloids and flavonoids.

It is often cultivated for traditional use in herbal medicine due to its purported health benefits.
