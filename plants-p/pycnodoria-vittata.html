Pycnodoria vittata

Pycnodoria vittata, commonly known as the spotted pycnodoria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and its striking, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and purple, which bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Pycnodoria vittata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
