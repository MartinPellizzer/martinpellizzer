Phyllanthus polyanthus

Phyllanthus polyanthus, commonly known as the silver dollar plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with thick, leathery leaves that are typically oval or elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits resembling tiny capsules. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
