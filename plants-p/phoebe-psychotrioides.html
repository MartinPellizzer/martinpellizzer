Phoebe psychotrioides

Phoebe psychotrioides, commonly known as the psychotrioides phoebe, is a member of the Lauraceae family, characterized by its aromatic evergreen foliage.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features simple, alternate leaves with a glossy, dark green surface and a fragrant aroma. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in spring, followed by the production of dark, fleshy berries.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
