Petasites vulgaris subsp. niveus

Petasites vulgaris subsp.

niveus, commonly known as the white edelweiss, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy rhizomes and large, woolly, white to pale yellow flowering heads.

The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions and well-drained soil. Its flowers are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
