Potamogeton pectinatus subvar. validior

Potamogeton pectinatus subvar. validior, commonly known as the broad-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow waters, often forming dense mats that provide habitat for aquatic organisms. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are broad at the base and taper towards the tip, with a smooth or slightly serrated surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, and it produces elongated, cylindrical fruits.

This species plays an important role in water ecosystems by contributing to water clarity and supporting aquatic biodiversity.
