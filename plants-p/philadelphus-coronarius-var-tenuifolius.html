Philadelphus coronarius var. tenuifolius

Philadelphus coronarius var. tenuifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, arching branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators and provide a pleasant scent in the garden.
