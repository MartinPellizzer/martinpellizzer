Pedicularis orthantha

Pedicularis orthantha, commonly known as the spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which are often adorned with prominent spurred petals. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Pedicularis orthantha is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance in its native range.
