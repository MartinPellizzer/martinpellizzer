Potentilla rubella

Potentilla rubella, commonly known as the red sanders or red potentilla, is a flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth, with slender stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Potentilla rubella is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
