Paronychia riparia

Paronychia riparia, commonly known as the riverbank cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows along riverbanks and moist soils, forming dense mats with prostrate stems. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in rounded inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and often have a waxy coating.

Paronychia riparia is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in wet environments.
