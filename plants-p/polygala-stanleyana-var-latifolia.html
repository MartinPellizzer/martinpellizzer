Polygala stanleyana var. latifolia

Polygala stanleyana var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with a distinctive spurred structure that attracts pollinators.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.
