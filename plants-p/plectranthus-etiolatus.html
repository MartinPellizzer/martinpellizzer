Plectranthus etiolatus

Plectranthus etiolatus, commonly known as the silver dollar plant, is a member of the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, velvety, silver-gray leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate watering, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Plectranthus etiolatus is often used as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
