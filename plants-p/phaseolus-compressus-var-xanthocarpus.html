Phaseolus compressus var. xanthocarpus

Phaseolus compressus var. xanthocarpus, commonly known as the yellow-seed bush bean, is a variant of the common bush bean native to Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, and it thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and nutritional content, making it a useful crop in tropical and subtropical regions.
