Polygonatum oppositifolium var. oppositifolium

Polygonatum oppositifolium var. oppositifolium, commonly known as the Chinese Solomon's Seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces tall, slender stems with opposite, lanceolate leaves. The plant bears small, greenish-white flowers in loose panicles, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its berries are dark blue to black when mature and are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in herbal practices.
