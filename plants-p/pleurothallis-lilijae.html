Pleurothallis lilijae

Pleurothallis lilijae, commonly known as the Lilijae orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without being parasitic. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Pleurothallis lilijae prefers high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
