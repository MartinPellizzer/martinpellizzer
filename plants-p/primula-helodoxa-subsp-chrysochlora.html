Primula helodoxa subsp. chrysochlora

Primula helodoxa subsp.

chrysochlora, commonly known as the golden-leaf primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving them a soft, velvety texture. Its flowers are showy, with five petals that are usually yellow or orange, and they bloom in early spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
