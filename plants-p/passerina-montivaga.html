Passerina montivaga

Passerina montivaga, commonly known as the mountain pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers are usually pink or white with numerous stamens.

Passerina montivaga is an important nectar source for early pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
