Piper calvifolium

Piper calvifolium, commonly known as the smooth-leaved pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by small, fleshy berries that ripen to a bright red color.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
