Piper crassispicatum

Piper crassispicatum, commonly known as the thick-hair pepper or black pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a small, black peppercorn-like berry that is used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The plant's morphology and growth habits make it well adapted to humid, shaded environments in the understory of tropical forests.
