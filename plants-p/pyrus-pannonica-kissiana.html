Pyrus pannonica kissiana

Pyrus pannonica kissiana, commonly known as the Pannonian pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Carpathian Basin.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value due to its showy white flowers and attractive autumn foliage. The tree produces small, edible pears that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This species is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
