Potentilla canescens proles fallax

Potentilla canescens proles fallax, commonly known as the silverleaf potentilla, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from dense, fine hairs covering the leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
