Populus simonii var. latifolia

Populus simonii var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf poplar, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, which are typical of the Populus genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
