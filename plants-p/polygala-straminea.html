Polygala straminea

Polygala straminea, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The fruit is a follicle that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
