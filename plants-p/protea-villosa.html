Protea villosa

Protea villosa, commonly known as the hairy protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or acidic soils and is well adapted to the fynbos biome. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered within the bracts, and it is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Protea villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects.
