Phreatia ponapensis

Phreatia ponapensis, commonly known as the Ponape orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the island of Pohnpei in the Caroline Islands.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces a single, erect stem that bears several lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Phreatia ponapensis is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments, often found in coastal areas or near water sources.
