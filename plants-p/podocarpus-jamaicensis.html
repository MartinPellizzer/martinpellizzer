Podocarpus jamaicensis

Podocarpus jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican podocarp, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and aromatic resinous scent. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions of Jamaica. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
