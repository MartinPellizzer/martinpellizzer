Paeonia × maleevii

Paeonia × maleevii, commonly known as the Siberian peony, is a hybrid plant that originates from the cross between Paeonia suffruticosa and Paeonia tianshanica.

It is characterized by its robust, shrub-like growth habit and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of dark green, compound leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Paeonia × maleevii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
