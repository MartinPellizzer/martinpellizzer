Primula elwesiana

Primula elwesiana, commonly known as the Elwes's primrose, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The leaves are typically broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of silky hair. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
