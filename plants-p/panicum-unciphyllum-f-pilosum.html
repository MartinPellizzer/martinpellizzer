Panicum unciphyllum f. pilosum

Panicum unciphyllum f. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a grass species characterized by its distinctive botanical features.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a textured appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle, which is a branched cluster of small spikelets.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and is often found in regions with well-drained soils.
