Phyllostachys mannii

Phyllostachys mannii, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass in the Bambusoideae subfamily.

It is native to China and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically bright green, with a slightly waxy texture. It is a clump-forming bamboo that spreads slowly through rhizomes, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Phyllostachys mannii is also valued for its use in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of sustainable materials.
