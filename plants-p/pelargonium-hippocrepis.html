Pelargonium hippocrepis

Pelargonium hippocrepis, commonly known as the rock geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has hairy, aromatic leaves and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its hardy nature and attractive blooms.

Pelargonium hippocrepis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
