Petroselinum ternatum

Petroselinum ternatum, commonly known as parsley, is a biennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves that are often used as a garnish or flavoring in cooking. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in umbels, a cluster arrangement typical of the Apiaceae family. Its root system is fibrous, and it prefers well-drained soil with full sun to partial shade.
