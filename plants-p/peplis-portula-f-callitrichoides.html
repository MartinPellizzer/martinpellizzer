Peplis portula f. callitrichoides

Peplis portula f. callitrichoides, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or violet in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and coastal gardens.
