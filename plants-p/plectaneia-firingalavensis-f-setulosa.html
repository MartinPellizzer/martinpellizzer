Plectaneia firingalavensis f. setulosa

Plectaneia firingalavensis f. setulosa, commonly known as the hairy fireleaf plant, is a unique fern species characterized by its distinctive, serrated, and hairy leaflets.

Native to the humid forests of Central Africa, it thrives in shaded, moist environments with rich, well-drained soil. The plant's leaves are typically broad and have a feathery appearance, with a dense covering of fine, bristly hairs that give it its common name. It is often found growing along stream banks and in undergrowth, contributing to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
