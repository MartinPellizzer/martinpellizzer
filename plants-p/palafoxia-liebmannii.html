Palafoxia liebmannii

Palafoxia liebmannii, commonly known as the redroot ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swampy areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture, while its stems are reddish in color.

Palafoxia liebmannii produces showy purple to pink flower heads in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
