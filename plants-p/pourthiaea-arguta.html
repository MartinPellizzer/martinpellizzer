Pourthiaea arguta

Pourthiaea arguta, commonly known as the silverleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery, fuzzy leaves and bright yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

The flowers bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
