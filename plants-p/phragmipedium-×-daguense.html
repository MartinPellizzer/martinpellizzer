Phragmipedium × daguense

Phragmipedium × daguense, commonly known as the Daguense phragmipedium, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
