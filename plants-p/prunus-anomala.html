Prunus anomala

Prunus anomala, commonly known as the wild cherry or Japanese black cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are oval to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in spring, which are followed by small, dark purple berries. Prunus anomala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
