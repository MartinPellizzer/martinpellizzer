Passiflora cryptopetala

Passiflora cryptopetala, commonly known as the hidden-leaved passionflower, is a rare and lesser-known species within the Passifloraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its unique, intricate flowers that feature a blend of purple, white, and yellow hues, along with modified leaves that are often concealed beneath its foliage. Its fruit, though not widely cultivated, is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Passiflora cryptopetala is considered a species of conservation concern.
