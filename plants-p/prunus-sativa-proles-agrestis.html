Prunus sativa proles agrestis

Prunus sativa proles agrestis, commonly known as the wild cherry tree, is a species belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in spring and its edible, sweet fruits that ripen in summer. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in well-drained soils. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
