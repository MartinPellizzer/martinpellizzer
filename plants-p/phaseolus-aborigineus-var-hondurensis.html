Phaseolus aborigineus var. hondurensis

Phaseolus aborigineus var. hondurensis, commonly known as the Honduran runner bean, is a species of legume native to Central America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces elongated, edible pods that are typically green when mature. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is often cultivated for both culinary and agricultural purposes.
