Polypodium loculosum

Polypodium loculosum, commonly known as the shield fern, is a tropical fern native to Southeast Asia and parts of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its shield-shaped fronds, which are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or on rocky surfaces. Its rhizomes are thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

Polypodium loculosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
