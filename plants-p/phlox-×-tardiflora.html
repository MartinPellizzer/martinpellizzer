Phlox × tardiflora

Phlox × tardiflora, commonly known as the late-flowering phlox, is a hybrid perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped, dark green leaves. Its flowers range in color from pink to purple, with some varieties exhibiting bicolor patterns.

Phlox × tardiflora thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well-suited for garden landscapes due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
