Paraphlomis foliata subsp. montigena

Paraphlomis foliata subsp.

montigena, commonly known as the mountain paraphlomis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It features upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lanceolate with a slightly serrated margin.

The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically aromatic and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The subspecies is distinguished by its robust growth and adaptability to high-altitude environments.
