Podopogon

Podopogon, commonly known as the spike grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a spike, composed of dense clusters of small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in open areas such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a prominent midrib.

Podopogon species are primarily wind-pollinated, which is a common trait among grasses, and they play a role in stabilizing soil in their native habitats.
