Pseuderanthemum leptorhachis

Pseuderanthemum leptorhachis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed pseuderanthemum, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, often with a striking color contrast between the petals and sepals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
