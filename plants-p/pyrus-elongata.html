Pyrus elongata

Pyrus elongata, commonly known as the elongated pear, is a species of deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated shape and smooth, dark-barked trunk. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by edible, sweet fruit in the summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

Pyrus elongata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in traditional cuisine.
