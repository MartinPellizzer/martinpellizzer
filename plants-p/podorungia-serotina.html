Podorungia serotina

Podorungia serotina, commonly known as the late-flowering orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom late in the season. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are often clustered at the tips of the stems and have a distinctive, elongated shape.

Podorungia serotina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
