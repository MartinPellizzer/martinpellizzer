Polypogon zeylanicus

Polypogon zeylanicus, commonly known as the Ceylon grass, is a perennial herb native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Polypogon zeylanicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
