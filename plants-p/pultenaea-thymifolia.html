Pultenaea thymifolia

Pultenaea thymifolia, commonly known as the thyme-leaved bushwillow, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, thyme-scented leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, while the stems are woody and sometimes branched.

Pultenaea thymifolia is commonly found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and heathlands.
