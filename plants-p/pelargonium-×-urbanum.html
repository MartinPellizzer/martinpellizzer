Pelargonium × urbanum

Pelargonium × urbanum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive heart-shaped, ivy-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant for hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Pelargonium × urbanum is known for its adaptability to various growing conditions and its ability to thrive in both indoor and outdoor environments.
