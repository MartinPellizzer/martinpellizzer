Potentilla pulchella subsp. gracilicaulis

Potentilla pulchella subsp.

gracilicaulis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed cinquefoil, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its upright, slender stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant produces small, bead-like fruits known as achenes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
