Penstemon confertus var. caeruleopurpureus

Penstemon confertus var. caeruleopurpureus, commonly known as the blue-purple beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of tubular flowers, typically in shades of blue, purple, and pink. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
