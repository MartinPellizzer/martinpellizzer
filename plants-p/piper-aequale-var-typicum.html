Piper aequale var. typicum

Piper aequale var. typicum, commonly known as the common pepper plant, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine that produces small, green berries that turn red when mature. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white flowers. It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in tropical gardens.
