Philadelphus lancifolius

Philadelphus lancifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blooms and attractive autumn foliage.
