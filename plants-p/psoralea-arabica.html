Psoralea arabica

Psoralea arabica, commonly known as the Arabian psoralea, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and can thrive in harsh, semi-desert environments. It has small, rounded stipules and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Psoralea arabica is often found in rocky habitats and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
