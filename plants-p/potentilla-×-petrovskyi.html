Potentilla × petrovskyi

Potentilla × petrovskyi, commonly known as the Siberian cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring dense, upright stems covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and are often covered in a soft, fuzzy texture.

Potentilla × petrovskyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh, well-drained soils.
