Psidium incanescens var. cuneatum

Psidium incanescens var. cuneatum, commonly known as the Cuyabeno guava, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, grayish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow or orange fruits with a sweet and acidic flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
