Papaver nudicaule subsp. corydalifolium

Papaver nudicaule subsp.

corydalifolium, commonly known as the Chinese poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to China. It features delicate, nodding flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive central stigma.

The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
