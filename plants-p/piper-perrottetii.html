Piper perrottetii

Piper perrottetii, commonly known as the black pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its dark, peppery fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, green berries that turn black when mature, giving it its common name. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a distinctive aroma.

Piper perrottetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
