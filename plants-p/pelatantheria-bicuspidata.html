Pelatantheria bicuspidata

Pelatantheria bicuspidata, commonly known as the two-lobed orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers typically have a yellow or greenish hue with a distinct two-lobed lip, which is a notable feature in its morphology.

Pelatantheria bicuspidata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.
