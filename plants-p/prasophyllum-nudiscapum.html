Prasophyllum nudiscapum

Prasophyllum nudiscapum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved greenhood, is an orchid species native to the temperate regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique hood-shaped flower that is typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often adorned with intricate patterns, aiding in pollination by specific insect species. Prasophyllum nudiscapum thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as rainforests and undergrowth areas.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to conserve water in its natural habitat.
