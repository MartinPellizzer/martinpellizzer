Pterocarpus macrocarpus

Pterocarpus macrocarpus, commonly known as kingwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The tree is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its natural habitat.
