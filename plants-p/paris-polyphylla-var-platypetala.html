Paris polyphylla var. platypetala

Paris polyphylla var. platypetala, commonly known as the flat-petaled Paris, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Melanthiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, glabrous stems and clustered, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, white to pale purple flowers in late spring to early summer, which are arranged in umbels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
