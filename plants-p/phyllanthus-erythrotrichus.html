Phyllanthus erythrotrichus

Phyllanthus erythrotrichus, commonly known as red-haired phyllanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and usually have a rough texture.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
