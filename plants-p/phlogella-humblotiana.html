Phlogella humblotiana

Phlogella humblotiana, commonly known as the Humblot's flameleaf, is a rare and exotic plant native to the misty highlands of southern Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange foliage that changes with the seasons, providing a vivid contrast to its dark green stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically blooming during the late spring and early summer months. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its arid native environment.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, Phlogella humblotiana is highly sought after by botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
