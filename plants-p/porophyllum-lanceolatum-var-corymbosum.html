Porophyllum lanceolatum var. corymbosum

Porophyllum lanceolatum var. corymbosum, commonly known as the Mexican daisy, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
