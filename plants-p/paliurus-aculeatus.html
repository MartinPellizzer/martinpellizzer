Paliurus aculeatus

Paliurus aculeatus, commonly known as the thorned hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, small white flowers, and red or orange berries that appear in the fall. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
