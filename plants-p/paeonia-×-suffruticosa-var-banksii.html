Paeonia × suffruticosa var. banksii

Paeonia × suffruticosa var. banksii, commonly known as the Japanese tree peony, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, double or semi-double flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces oval, green leaves that are serrated along the edges. It typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its bark is smooth with a reddish-brown color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
