Peliosanthes khasiana

Peliosanthes khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and fragrant wood. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats within tropical and subtropical forests. It has a slow growth rate and is valued for its durable, aromatic timber.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
