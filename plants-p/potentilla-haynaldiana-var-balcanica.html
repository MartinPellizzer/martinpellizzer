Potentilla haynaldiana var. balcanica

Potentilla haynaldiana var. balcanica, commonly known as the Balkan potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant features hirsute (hairy) stems and leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
