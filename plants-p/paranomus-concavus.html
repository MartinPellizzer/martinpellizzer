Paranomus concavus

Paranomus concavus, commonly known as the concave sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowering spikes. Paranomus concavus typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
