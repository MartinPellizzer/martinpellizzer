Potamogeton rufescens var. rivularis

Potamogeton rufescens var. rivularis, commonly known as the river pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant found in freshwater habitats.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color, depending on the water conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

This variant is adapted to slow-moving or still waters, often growing in shallow, muddy substrates.
