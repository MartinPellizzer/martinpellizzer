Pentaphragma bicolor

Pentaphragma bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, two-toned flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.
