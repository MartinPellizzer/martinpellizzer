Plantago elongata var. californica

Plantago elongata var. californica, commonly known as California plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including roadsides, meadows, and disturbed soils.

Its fibrous root system makes it a hardy and adaptable species, often used in ecological restoration projects.
