Panicum isachnoides

Panicum isachnoides, commonly known as silver panicum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stalks and small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant produces fluffy, white to silver-gray seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across open habitats. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in marginal conditions.
