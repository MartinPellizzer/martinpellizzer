Panicum maximum var. coloratum

Panicum maximum var. coloratum, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and striking purple to reddish flower spikes that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental and erosion control purposes. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a silver-green hue that adds visual interest throughout the growing season.

The species is also valued for its ability to provide forage for livestock in certain regions.
