Piper heraldii var. cocleanum

Piper heraldii var. cocleanum, commonly known as the Cocleanum pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that develop into elongated, peppery fruits. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its fruits are often used in local cuisines for their spicy flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species.
