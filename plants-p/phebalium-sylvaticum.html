Phebalium sylvaticum

Phebalium sylvaticum, commonly known as the swamp hakea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and distinctive floral structure. The plant typically grows in wet or swampy environments, thriving in areas with high humidity and well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually pink or red, arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, and it produces small, leathery fruits.

Phebalium sylvaticum is often found in coastal and inland regions, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
