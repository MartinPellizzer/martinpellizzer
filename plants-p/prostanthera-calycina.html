Prostanthera calycina

Prostanthera calycina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mintbush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Prostanthera calycina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its fragrance and hardiness.
