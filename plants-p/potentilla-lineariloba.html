Potentilla lineariloba

Potentilla lineariloba, commonly known as the slenderleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in width, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
