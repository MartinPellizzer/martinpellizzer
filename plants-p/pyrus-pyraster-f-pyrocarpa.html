Pyrus pyraster f. pyrocarpa

Pyrus pyraster f. pyrocarpa, commonly known as the common spindle tree, is a variant of the common pear.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features elliptical, dark green leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive, twisted branching pattern. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and appear in clusters, followed by edible, round fruits that are typically yellow or brown.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in horticulture.
