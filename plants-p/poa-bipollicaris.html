Poa bipollicaris

Poa bipollicaris, commonly known as the tufted bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically forms low-growing mats and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters of small, two-rowed flowers.

Poa bipollicaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass management.
