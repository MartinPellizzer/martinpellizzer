Persicaria sieboldii f. espinulosa

Persicaria sieboldii f. espinulosa, commonly known as the spiny knotweed, is a flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, spiny bracts that enclose its small, greenish flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture, with a reddish hue on the underside. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
