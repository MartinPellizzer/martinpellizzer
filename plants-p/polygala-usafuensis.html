Polygala usafuensis

Polygala usafuensis, commonly known as the Usafu milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often pale pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, lance-shaped leaves. It is known for its unique floral structure, which includes a single central spur that extends from the flower's base.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its ecological significance and potential medicinal properties.
