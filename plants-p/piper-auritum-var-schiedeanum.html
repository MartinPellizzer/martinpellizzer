Piper auritum var. schiedeanum

Piper auritum var. schiedeanum, commonly known as Mexican pepper or chilhuacle, is a climbing shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with wavy margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, slender fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded, forested areas.
