Portulaca poliosperma var. bistigmatata

Portulaca poliosperma var. bistigmatata, commonly known as the two-stigma portulaca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Portulacaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, vibrant flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
