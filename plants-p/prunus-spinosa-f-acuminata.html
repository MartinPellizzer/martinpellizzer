Prunus spinosa f. acuminata

Prunus spinosa f. acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped blackthorn, is a compact shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark blue-black berries that ripen in late summer. The leaves are oval-shaped with a serrated margin and a matte green upper surface. This variety is noted for its dense growth habit and is often used in hedgerows or as a decorative plant.

It has moderate drought tolerance and prefers well-drained soils.
