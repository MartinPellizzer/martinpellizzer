Polygala glochidiata f. typica

Polygala glochidiata f. typica, commonly known as the typical glochidiate milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces delicate, yellowish-green flowers that are typically arranged in racemes, adding a subtle ornamental value. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water, showcasing features such as succulent stems and deep root systems.

This species is often found in rocky soils and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and ecological resilience.
