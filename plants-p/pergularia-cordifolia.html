Pergularia cordifolia

Pergularia cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf beggar-tick, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Pergularia cordifolia is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
