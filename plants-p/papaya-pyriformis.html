Papaya pyriformis

Papaya pyriformis, commonly known as the horned papaya, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and distinctive, horn-like projections on its fruit. The plant produces a fleshy, orange-colored fruit that is typically harvested when fully mature. Its botanical classification places it in the Caricaceae family, alongside other papaya species.

The fruit is not only ornamental but also has medicinal and culinary uses in some regions.
