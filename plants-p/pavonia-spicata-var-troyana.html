Pavonia spicata var. troyana

Pavonia spicata var. troyana, commonly known as the Troyana false mallow, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically dark green and may have a slight reddish tinge on the underside.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well-suited for moist, well-drained soils.
