Pilosella densiflora subsp. acrosciadia

Pilosella densiflora subsp.

acrosciadia, commonly known as the denseflower pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its compact growth habit and long-lasting flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

The subspecies acrosciadia is distinguished by its more robust growth and slightly larger flowers compared to other subspecies.
