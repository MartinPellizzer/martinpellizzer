Piper crispans

Piper crispans, commonly known as the rough pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by tiny, berry-like fruits that are typically green when mature.

Piper crispans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in warm, humid climates with partial shade.
