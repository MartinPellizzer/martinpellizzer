Phyllanthus klotzschianus var. racemulosus

Phyllanthus klotzschianus var. racemulosus, commonly known as the racemose phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glossy and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, which are typically borne on long, slender peduncles. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.
