Patagonium leptopodum

Patagonium leptopodum, commonly known as the slender pod grass, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in dry, open habitats.

Patagonium leptopodum is often found in Patagonian steppes and plays a minor role in local ecosystems.
