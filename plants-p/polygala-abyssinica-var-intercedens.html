Polygala abyssinica var. intercedens

Polygala abyssinica var. intercedens, commonly known as the Abyssinian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are often pale green or white, with a unique structure that resembles a spire, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
