Potentilla purpurascens var. congdonis

Potentilla purpurascens var. congdonis, commonly known as the Congdon's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are oval to lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. This variety is often found in rocky soils and alpine environments, where it thrives in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and colorful display.
