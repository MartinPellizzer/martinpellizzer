Peperomia dyscrita

Peperomia dyscrita, commonly known as the Variegated Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and yellow, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of pink or red, which are followed by tiny, berry-like fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its slow growth and compact size, it is ideal for container planting and requires minimal maintenance.
