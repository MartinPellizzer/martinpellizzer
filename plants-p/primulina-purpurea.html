Primulina purpurea

Primulina purpurea, commonly known as the purple primula, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub with lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to produce multiple blooms throughout the growing season.
