Polygonum rumicifolium var. cordatum

Polygonum rumicifolium var. cordatum, commonly known as heartleaf knotweed, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically green with a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Polygonaceae family, known for its distinctive nodes and often edible seeds.
