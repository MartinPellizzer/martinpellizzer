Portulaca oleracea var. macrantha

Portulaca oleracea var. macrantha, commonly known as the Indian cress or moss rose, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is an annual herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens. The plant features fleshy, succulent leaves and produces vibrant red or yellow flowers with five petals. Its flowers are relatively large compared to other portulaca species, hence the name *macrantha*, meaning "large flower.".

It is also known for its edible leaves and seeds, which are used in various culinary traditions.
