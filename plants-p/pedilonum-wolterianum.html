Pedilonum wolterianum

Pedilonum wolterianum, commonly known as the Wolterian's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine pubescence.

Pedilonum wolterianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its unique appearance and hardiness.
