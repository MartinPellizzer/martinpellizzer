Prunus × lannesiana f. vexillifera

Prunus × lannesiana f. vexillifera, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Prunus serrula and Prunus lannesiana. The plant is renowned for its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its botanical features include narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a compact, shrub-like growth habit.

It is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and holds cultural significance in Japan and other parts of East Asia.
