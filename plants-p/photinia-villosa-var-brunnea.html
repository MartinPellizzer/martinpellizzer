Photinia villosa var. brunnea

Photinia villosa var. brunnea, commonly known as the red tip photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

However, it can be susceptible to leaf spot diseases and scale insects.
