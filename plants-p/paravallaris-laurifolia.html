Paravallaris laurifolia

Paravallaris laurifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features broad, leathery leaves that are typically a striking silver or gray-green in color, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to retain moisture in well-drained soils.

Paravallaris laurifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
