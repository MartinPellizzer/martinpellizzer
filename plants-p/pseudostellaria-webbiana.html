Pseudostellaria webbiana

Pseudostellaria webbiana, commonly known as the Webb's starwort, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China and India, and typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine pubescence.

Pseudostellaria webbiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
