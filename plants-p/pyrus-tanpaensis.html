Pyrus tanpaensis

Pyrus tanpaensis, commonly known as the Tanpa apple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Turkey and is characterized by its small, edible fruits. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces white to pale yellow flowers in the spring.

The fruit, which is similar to a small apple, is used in local traditional medicine and culinary practices.
