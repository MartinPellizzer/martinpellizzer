Polemonium pulcherrimum var. lindleyi

Polemonium pulcherrimum var. lindleyi, commonly known as the Lindley's Jacob's ladder, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its cascading clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for woodland gardens and native plant landscapes.
