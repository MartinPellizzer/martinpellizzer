Pueraria argyi

Pueraria argyi, commonly known as the Chinese kudzu, is a climbing legume native to East Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves and produces purple or pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to form dense thickets. Its roots contain high levels of starch, making it valuable for both traditional medicine and food use.

Pueraria argyi is also known for its ecological impact, often acting as an invasive species in non-native environments.
