Pleiosorbus wardii

Pleiosorbus wardii, commonly known as the Ward's stonecrop, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to white, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
