Passiflora cyathophora

Passiflora cyathophora, commonly known as the red passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a central crown of filaments and a distinctive blue or purple corona. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients and often consumed by wildlife. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture and emit a mild, fragrant scent.

Passiflora cyathophora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
