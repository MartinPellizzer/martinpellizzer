Potentilla hirsuta

Potentilla hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are typically arranged in a five-leaf cluster. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas.

Potentilla hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
