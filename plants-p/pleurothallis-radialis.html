Pleurothallis radialis

Pleurothallis radialis, commonly known as the radial pleurothallis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its flowers are often arranged in a raceme and are noted for their intricate, intricate structure.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
