Petasites × sabaudus

Petasites × sabaudus, commonly known as the giant edelweiss, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Petasites alpestris and Petasites lanuginosus.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in alpine environments, typically found at high altitudes in mountainous regions. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
