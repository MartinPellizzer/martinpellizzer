Plagianthera oppositifolia

Plagianthera oppositifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed plagianthera, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in dry, open habitats. The plant features opposite, linear leaves and produces small, white, star-shaped flowers. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand saline and arid conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
