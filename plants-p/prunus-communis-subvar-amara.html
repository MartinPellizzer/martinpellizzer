Prunus communis subvar. amara

Prunus communis subvar. amara, commonly known as the bitter cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, dark red to black fruits, which are typically bitter and not suitable for eating raw. The plant has pinnate leaves and produces white or pale pink flowers in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
