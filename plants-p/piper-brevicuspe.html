Piper brevicuspe

Piper brevicuspe, commonly known as the short-lobed pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing the peppery seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in local cuisines for their pungent flavor.

The plant's botanical features include opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a climbing habit that supports its growth in dense vegetation.
