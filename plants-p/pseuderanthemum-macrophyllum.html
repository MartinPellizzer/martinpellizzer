Pseuderanthemum macrophyllum

Pseuderanthemum macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved false anthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and can grow up to 10 centimeters in length. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

The flowers are adapted for pollination by hummingbirds and other nectar-feeding insects.
