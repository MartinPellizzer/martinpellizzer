Psiadia retusa

Psiadia retusa, commonly known as the smoothleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of smooth, bluish-green leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a taproot system and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers produce small, wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Psiadia retusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
