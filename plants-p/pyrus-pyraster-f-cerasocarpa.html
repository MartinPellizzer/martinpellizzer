Pyrus pyraster f. cerasocarpa

Pyrus pyraster f. cerasocarpa, commonly known as the wild serviceberry, is a compact shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its rounded leaves and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, sweet, edible berries that ripen in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a food source for wildlife.

The f. cerasocarpa form is distinguished by its larger, more fleshy fruits compared to the typical species.
