Petermannia fasciculata

Petermannia fasciculata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming dense clumps that help it survive in dry conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its hardy and low-maintenance nature.
