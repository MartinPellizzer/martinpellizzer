Picridium vulgare var. crassifolium

Picridium vulgare var. crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf picridium, is a variant of the picridium plant native to certain regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun exposure, often growing in rocky or dry environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Picridium genus.
