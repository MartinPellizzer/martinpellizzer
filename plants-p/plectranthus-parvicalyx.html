Plectranthus parvicalyx

Plectranthus parvicalyx, commonly known as the small-leaved plectranthus, is a herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly aromatic scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus parvicalyx is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
