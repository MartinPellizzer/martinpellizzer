Premna oblongata f. longituba

Premna oblongata f. longituba, commonly known as the long-stemmed rubber vine, is a variant of the Premna oblongata species native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its slender, elongated stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rubbery texture and unique growth form make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
