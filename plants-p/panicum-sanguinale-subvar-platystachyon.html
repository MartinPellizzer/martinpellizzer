Panicum sanguinale subvar. platystachyon

Panicum sanguinale subvar. platystachyon, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and clusters of red or brown spikelets that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking color and adaptability to various soil conditions.
