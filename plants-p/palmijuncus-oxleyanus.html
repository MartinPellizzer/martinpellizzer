Palmijuncus oxleyanus

Palmijuncus oxleyanus, commonly known as the Oxleyan palm, is a unique member of the Arecaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and a crown of elongated, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, coconut-like fruits. Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.

Its botanical features make it an important subject for conservation and botanical study.
