Poa sieberiana var. cyanophylla

Poa sieberiana var. cyanophylla, commonly known as the blueleaf bluegrass, is a variant of the bluegrass genus characterized by its distinctive blue-green foliage.

It is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments and forms dense, low-growing turf. Its leaves are narrow and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are tightly clustered, contributing to its ornamental appeal in horticultural settings.
