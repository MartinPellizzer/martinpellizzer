Papaver decaisnei var. patulum

Papaver decaisnei var. patulum, commonly known as the broadleaf poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pink to reddish-purple petals that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
