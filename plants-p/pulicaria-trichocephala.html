Pulicaria trichocephala

Pulicaria trichocephala, commonly known as the hairy pulicaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features silver-gray, woolly foliage that is covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small yellow flowers appear in clusters during the summer months, adding a delicate touch to its appearance.

The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to dry, sunny environments.
