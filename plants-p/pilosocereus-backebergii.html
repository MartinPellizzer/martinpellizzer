Pilosocereus backebergii

Pilosocereus backebergii, commonly known as the Backeberg cactus, is a slow-growing, spineless cactus native to South Africa.

It features a cylindrical stem that is covered in white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance contribute to its appeal in both horticulture and ecological studies.
