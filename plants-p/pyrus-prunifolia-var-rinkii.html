Pyrus prunifolia var. rinkii

Pyrus prunifolia var. rinkii, commonly known as the Rink's pear, is a rare variant of the common pear tree.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or green in color. The plant produces white flowers in early spring, which are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, fertile soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticultural studies.
