Pavonia spinistipula

Pavonia spinistipula, commonly known as the spiny-stippled pavonia, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
