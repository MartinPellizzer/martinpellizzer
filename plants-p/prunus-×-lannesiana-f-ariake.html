Prunus × lannesiana f. ariake

Prunus × lannesiana f. ariake, commonly known as the Ariake cherry, is a botanical hybrid belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a compact, ornamental shrub or small tree characterized by its dense growth habit and showy, semi-double pink flowers. The plant is native to Japan and is prized for its aesthetic appeal in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.

Its foliage turns vibrant red in the fall, adding seasonal interest to landscapes.
