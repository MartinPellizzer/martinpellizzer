Pilosella kalksburgensis

Pilosella kalksburgensis, commonly known as the Kalksburg pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features silver-white to grayish hairs on its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive, frosty appearance. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Pilosella kalksburgensis is often found in open meadows and slopes, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
