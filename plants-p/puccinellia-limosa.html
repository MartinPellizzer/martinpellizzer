Puccinellia limosa

Puccinellia limosa, commonly known as saltgrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and saline environments.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color and adapted to withstand high salinity and drought conditions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline soils, making it an important component of these ecosystems.

Puccinellia limosa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
