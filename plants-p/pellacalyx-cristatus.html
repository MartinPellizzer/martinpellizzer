Pellacalyx cristatus

Pellacalyx cristatus, commonly known as the crown calyx plant, is a flowering species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive crown-like calyx that forms a rosette at the base of the plant. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in botanical gardens and private collections.
