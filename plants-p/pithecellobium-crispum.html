Pithecellobium crispum

Pithecellobium crispum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, yellow, edible pods that resemble beans and contain a sweet, sticky pulp. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fragrant white flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.

The seeds within the pods are typically used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
