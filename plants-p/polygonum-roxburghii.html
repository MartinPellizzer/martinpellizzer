Polygonum roxburghii

Polygonum roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian knotgrass, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Polygonum roxburghii is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
