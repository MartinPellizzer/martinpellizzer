Phrynium trifasciatum

Phrynium trifasciatum, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green stripes. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. Its rhizomes are edible and have a starchy texture similar to taro.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking foliage.
