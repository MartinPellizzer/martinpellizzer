Pothos enneaphyllus

Pothos enneaphyllus, commonly known as the Hawaiian pothos, is a climbing vine native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically light to dark green with a slightly variegated appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, followed by orange-red berries that contain seeds. It thrives in bright, indirect light and is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular houseplant.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal contribute to its popularity in both indoor and outdoor settings.
