Podocarpus cuspidatus

Podocarpus cuspidatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, cone-like seed structures. The leaves are typically needle-like and arranged in spirals along the stems. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
