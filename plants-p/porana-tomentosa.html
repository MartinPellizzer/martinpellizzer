Porana tomentosa

Porana tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy porana, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Porana tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
