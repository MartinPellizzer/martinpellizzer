Panicum lepidum

Panicum lepidum, commonly known as silky grass or silky panicum, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that cover the leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.

Panicum lepidum is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
