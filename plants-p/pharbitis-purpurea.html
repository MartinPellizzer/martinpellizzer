Pharbitis purpurea

Pharbitis purpurea, commonly known as sweet potato vine, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, heart-shaped leaves that come in various colors such as purple, green, and variegated patterns. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its foliage.

Its tuberous roots, while not typically eaten, can be used in traditional medicine.
