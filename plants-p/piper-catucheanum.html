Piper catucheanum

Piper catucheanum, commonly known as Ayahuasca, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest of South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus in the family Piperaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant contains psychoactive alkaloids, including DMT, which are responsible for its traditional use in spiritual and medicinal practices. It produces small, greenish flowers that are typically pollinated by insects.

The fruit is a small, round berry that turns yellow when mature.
