Polypodium macropodum

Polypodium macropodum, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant typically thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

The plant's robust and resilient nature makes it a common sight in forest understories across its native range.
