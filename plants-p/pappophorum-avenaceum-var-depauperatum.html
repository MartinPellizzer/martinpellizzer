Pappophorum avenaceum var. depauperatum

Pappophorum avenaceum var. depauperatum, commonly known as the slender oatgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces light, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its delicate appearance and ecological role, it is often found in meadows and along the edges of woodlands.
