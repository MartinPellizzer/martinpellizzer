Panzerina lanata subsp. argyracea

Panzerina lanata subsp.

argyracea, commonly known as the silver-haired bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and showy blue to violet bell-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense pubescence and the pale coloration of its foliage and inflorescence.
