Pulsatilla halleri subsp. halleri

Pulsatilla halleri subsp.

halleri, commonly known as the European pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple.

The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces a tall flowering stem with several flowers at the top. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky soils, and it is known for its early spring blooming period. Pulsatilla halleri subsp.

halleri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
