Polygonum pawlowskianum

Polygonum pawlowskianum, commonly known as the red polygonum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, vibrant red stems that become more pronounced as the plant matures. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late summer.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in moist, well-drained soils.
