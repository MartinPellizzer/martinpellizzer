Pharbitis lilacina

Pharbitis lilacina, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet. The plant produces seeds within elongated, spiral-shaped fruits that twist as they mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Pharbitis lilacina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
