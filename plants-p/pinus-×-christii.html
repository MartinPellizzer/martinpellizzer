Pinus × christii

Pinus × christii, commonly known as the Christ's pine, is a hybrid conifer native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a cross between Pinus ponderosa and Pinus juniperus, resulting in a tree with distinctive features that combine traits from both parent species. The Christ's pine typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters, featuring a broad, open crown and long, needle-like leaves. Its cones are often reddish-brown and can remain on the tree for several years before opening.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its unique appearance.
