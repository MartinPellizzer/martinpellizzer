Pinus gordoniana

Pinus gordoniana, commonly known as the Gordon fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender cones and needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The tree can reach heights of up to 30 meters and has a slow growth rate, making it a long-lived species. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various wildlife species and contributing to forest ecosystems.
