Phaseolus vulgaris var. ooleucus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. ooleucus, commonly known as the black bean.

This variety is characterized by its dark, glossy seeds that are typically used in culinary applications. It is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to the Americas. The plant produces erect, compound leaves and is typically grown as an annual.

Its pods contain the edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients.
