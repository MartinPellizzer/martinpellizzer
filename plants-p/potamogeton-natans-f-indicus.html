Potamogeton natans f. indicus

Potamogeton natans f. indicus, commonly known as the Indian water plant, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Asia.

It has long, slender, branching stems that are typically found in freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving rivers. The plant produces small, green to reddish flowers that are clustered in spike-like structures. Its leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, often with a slight curvature.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
