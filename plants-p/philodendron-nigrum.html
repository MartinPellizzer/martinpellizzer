Philodendron nigrum

Philodendron nigrum, commonly known as the black-veined philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, heart-shaped leaves with prominent, contrasting veins that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by green, pear-shaped fruits that turn red when mature. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and anchor itself to trees or other structures in its natural habitat.

Philodendron nigrum is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
