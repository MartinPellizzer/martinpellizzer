Panicum barbipulvinatum var. hirsutipes

Panicum barbipulvinatum var. hirsutipes, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for various small organisms.
