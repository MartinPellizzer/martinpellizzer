Parsonsia verticillata

Parsonsia verticillata, commonly known as the golden flame or the flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Australia and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and its striking clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The flowers are borne in terminal panicles and are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

Parsonsia verticillata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
