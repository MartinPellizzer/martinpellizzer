Pimelea sericeovillosa subsp. pulvinaris

Pimelea sericeovillosa subsp.

pulvinaris, commonly known as the silky woolly geebung, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive silvery appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in native plant communities.
