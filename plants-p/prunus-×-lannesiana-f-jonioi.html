Prunus × lannesiana f. jonioi

Prunus × lannesiana f. jonioi, commonly known as the Japanese flowering cherry, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly during the spring when it produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in the autumn, and thrives in temperate climates. It typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 meters.

Its botanical features include simple, ovate leaves and a compact growth habit that makes it suitable for ornamental gardening.
