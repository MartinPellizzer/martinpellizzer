Pyrus reticulata

Pyrus reticulata, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white flowers in spring. The plant produces small, sweet fruits that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

Pyrus reticulata is commonly cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and fragrance.
