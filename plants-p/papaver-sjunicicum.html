Papaver sjunicicum

Papaver sjunicicum, commonly known as the Sicilian poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange petals and a prominent, saucer-shaped bloom. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is erect and often branched.

Papaver sjunicicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
