Protea aurantiaca

Protea aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange, conical flower head composed of numerous small, petal-like bracts. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils, showcasing the family's unique ecological adaptations. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
