Panax schin-seng

Panax schin-seng, commonly known as Chinese ginseng, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, yellowish roots that are highly valued in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in forested areas with well-drained soil and partial shade. Its leaves are alternate, compound, and have a distinctive, aromatic scent.

The roots contain various bioactive compounds, including ginsenosides, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.
