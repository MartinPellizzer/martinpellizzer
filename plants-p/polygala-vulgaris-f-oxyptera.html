Polygala vulgaris f. oxyptera

Polygala vulgaris f. oxyptera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

The flowers, which are usually pink or purple, bloom in summer and attract various pollinators.
