Parsonsia celastrinea

Parsonsia celastrinea, commonly known as the flame tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant belongs to the family Celastraceae and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types. Its leaves are typically alternate and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.
