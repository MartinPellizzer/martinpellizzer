Pistia crispata

Pistia crispata, commonly known as water lettuce, is a floating aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has soft, waxy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in some areas, impacting local ecosystems.
