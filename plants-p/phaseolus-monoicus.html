Phaseolus monoicus

Phaseolus monoicus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and vibrant red flowers. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Phaseolus monoicus is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
