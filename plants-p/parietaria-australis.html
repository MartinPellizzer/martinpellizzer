Parietaria australis

Parietaria australis, commonly known as the Australian wallaby tree, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a small, deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

Parietaria australis is adapted to withstand drought and salt spray, making it a hardy species in its native habitat.
