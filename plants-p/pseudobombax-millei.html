Pseudobombax millei

Pseudobombax millei, commonly known as the giant poinciana, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The tree produces striking, showy inflorescences with bright red bracts that surround small yellow flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is often used in landscaping and reforestation projects.
