Peperomia quintuplinervia

Peperomia quintuplinervia, commonly known as the five-veined peperomia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and small, ovate leaves with a distinctive five-veined pattern. Its foliage is typically a vibrant green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes.

Peperomia quintuplinervia is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
