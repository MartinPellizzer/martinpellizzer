Phlox canadensis

Phlox canadensis, commonly known as Canada phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive tubular throat.

Canada phlox thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural habitats.
