Pulviniella algida

Pulviniella algida, commonly known as the cold pulvinia, is a unique plant species characterized by its specialized pulvini structures.

These pulvini are modified leaf bases that enable the plant to exhibit rapid movements in response to environmental stimuli. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in temperate regions with high humidity. Its botanical features include small, clustered leaves and a compact growth habit.

Pulviniella algida is of interest to botanists due to its adaptive mechanisms and potential ecological roles in its native habitat.
