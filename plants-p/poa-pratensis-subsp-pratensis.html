Poa pratensis subsp. pratensis

Poa pratensis subsp.

pratensis, commonly known as Kentucky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, slender blades, and a dense, sod-forming root system that makes it highly resilient.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is widely used in lawns and sports fields due to its durability and aesthetic appeal. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with blue-green flowers, and it is known for its ability to form a thick, durable turf.

This subspecies is also valued for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a popular choice in horticulture and agriculture.
