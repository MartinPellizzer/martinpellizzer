Promenaea ovatiloba

Promenaea ovatiloba, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, triangular fruits. Promenaea ovatiloba is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

It has been studied for its potential medicinal properties and ecological role in its native ecosystems.
