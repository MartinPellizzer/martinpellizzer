Pterocarpus ferrugineus

Pterocarpus ferrugineus, commonly known as the ironwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, dark brown wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers followed by bean-like pods.

Its root system is extensive, and it plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
