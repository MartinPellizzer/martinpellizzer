Piper cobarianum

Piper cobarianum, commonly known as the cobara pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in local remedies for their medicinal properties.

The plant's botanical characteristics include opposite, ovate leaves and a climbing or shrub-like growth habit.
