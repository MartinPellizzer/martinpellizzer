Podocarpus mairei

Podocarpus mairei, commonly known as the Maire's podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, cone-like structures. The tree typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and arranged spirally along the stems, contributing to its ornamental value.

Podocarpus mairei is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture making.
