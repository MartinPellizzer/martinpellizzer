Pithecellobium asplenifolium

Pithecellobium asplenifolium, commonly known as the laceleaf acacia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical gardens for its unique foliage.
