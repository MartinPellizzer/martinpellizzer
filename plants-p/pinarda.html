Pinarda

Pinarda, commonly known as the common pinarda, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil in partial shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are square and erect.

Pinarda is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces.
