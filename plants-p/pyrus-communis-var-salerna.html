Pyrus communis var. salerna

Pyrus communis var. salerna, commonly known as the Salerno pear, is a cultivar of the common pear that is native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its medium-sized, oval-shaped fruits with a smooth, yellowish-brown skin that often develops a slight blush when ripe. The fruit has a sweet, aromatic flavor with a slight tartness, making it suitable for both fresh consumption and cooking. It is typically harvested in late autumn and is valued for its culinary versatility.

This variety is also noted for its ornamental value, with attractive foliage and a compact growth habit.
