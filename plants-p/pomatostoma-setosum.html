Pomatostoma setosum

Pomatostoma setosum, commonly known as the hairy bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to freshwater environments in Australia.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap tiny aquatic organisms. The plant lacks true roots and instead relies on its specialized bladders for nutrient absorption. Its leaves are typically thin and ribbon-like, adapted for floating on the water's surface.

Pomatostoma setosum plays a unique role in its ecosystem by contributing to the control of microorganism populations in its habitat.
