Parodia riojensis

Parodia riojensis, commonly known as the Rioja Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, adapting to extreme drought conditions. Its flowers, which are usually yellow or orange, bloom in late summer, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance.

Parodia riojensis is prized by cactus enthusiasts for its unique form and resilience.
