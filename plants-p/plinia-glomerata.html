Plinia glomerata

Plinia glomerata, commonly known as the clustered guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Plinia glomerata is also valued for its use in traditional medicine.
