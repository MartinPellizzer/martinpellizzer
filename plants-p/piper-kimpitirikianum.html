Piper kimpitirikianum

Piper kimpitirikianum, commonly known as the Kimpitirikian pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by berries that contain the peppery seeds used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
