Phragmites communis subsp. nigricans

Phragmites communis subsp.

nigricans, commonly known as the black rush, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments. It is characterized by its dark green, stiff leaves and tall, cylindrical flower spikes that often stand above the surrounding vegetation.

This subspecies is native to North America and is often found in marshes, along lake shores, and in other moist habitats. Its rhizomes form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife.

The plant is also known for its ability to tolerate a wide range of environmental conditions, making it a common and persistent species in wetland ecosystems.
