Paspalum barclayi

Paspalum barclayi, commonly known as the Barclay's grass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it forms dense mats. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in panicles, which are covered in small, spikelet-like structures.

Paspalum barclayi is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
