Pandanus crassus

Pandanus crassus, commonly known as the broad-leaf pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the palm family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. It produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into spiny, woody fruits.

The plant is often used in traditional crafts and has cultural significance in many island communities.
