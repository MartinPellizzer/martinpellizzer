Pleurothallis punctata

Pleurothallis punctata, commonly known as the spotted pleurothallis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

This small, epiphytic orchid typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. It features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant is characterized by its unique pattern of dark spots on its petals and sepals.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts, though it remains relatively rare in horticultural trade.
