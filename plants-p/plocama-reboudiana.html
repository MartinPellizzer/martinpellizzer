Plocama reboudiana

Plocama reboudiana, commonly known as the silverleaf plocama, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Plocama reboudiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
