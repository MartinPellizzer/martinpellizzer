Psyllocarpus laricoides var. longicornu

Psyllocarpus laricoides var. longicornu, commonly known as the longhorned ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its hard, dense wood and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its durable wood, it has historical uses in construction and tool-making.
