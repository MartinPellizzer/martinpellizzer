Puya chilensis var. gigantea

Puya chilensis var. gigantea, commonly known as the Chilean giant puya, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to the Andes of southern Chile.

This plant is characterized by its towering rosette of long, sword-shaped leaves that can reach heights of up to 10 feet. The leaves are typically bright green with a waxy texture, and they form a central cup that collects rainwater, which is essential for the plant's survival in its arid habitat. It produces vibrant red or orange flowers in a spike that emerges from the center of the rosette, adding a dramatic visual display.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
