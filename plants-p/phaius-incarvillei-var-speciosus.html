Phaius incarvillei var. speciosus

Phaius incarvillei var. speciosus, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its striking yellow flowers and broad, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike bearing numerous showy, fragrant flowers.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
