Prunus serrulata f. kirin

Prunus serrulata f. kirin, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a cultivar of the Prunus serrulata species.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is a deciduous tree with small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is highly valued for its ornamental beauty and cultural significance in Japan.
