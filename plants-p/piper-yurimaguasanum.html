Piper yurimaguasanum

Piper yurimaguasanum, commonly known as the Yurimaguas pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest of Peru.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, and the fruit is used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and climbing habit, often growing along tree canopies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
