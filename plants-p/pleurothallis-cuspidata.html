Pleurothallis cuspidata

Pleurothallis cuspidata, commonly known as the cuspidate orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structures. The plant typically produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and green, often with a distinctive lip that is adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by enthusiasts, though it remains relatively rare in horticultural trade.
