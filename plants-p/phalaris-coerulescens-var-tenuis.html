Phalaris coerulescens var. tenuis

Phalaris coerulescens var. tenuis, commonly known as slender bluegrass, is a grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and slender, upright growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its delicate appearance.
