Pelargonium × castum

Pelargonium × castum, commonly known as the hybrid geranium, is a flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is a cross between species of Pelargonium, resulting in a variety that combines traits from its parent plants. The plant typically features showy, colorful flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Pelargonium × castum is often cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant due to its adaptability and aesthetic value.
