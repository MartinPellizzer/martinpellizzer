Pilosella pseudopelleteriana

Pilosella pseudopelleteriana, commonly known as the slender pearly everlasting, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features hairy, gray-green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Pilosella pseudopelleteriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
