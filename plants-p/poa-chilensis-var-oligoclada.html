Poa chilensis var. oligoclada

Poa chilensis var. oligoclada, commonly known as the Chilean hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of silky hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in coastal areas or along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
