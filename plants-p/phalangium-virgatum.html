Phalangium virgatum

Phalangium virgatum, commonly known as the slender centipede plant, is a unique species of flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. It has a creeping growth habit, with stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground.

Despite its name, it is not related to centipedes but is named for its segmented appearance.
