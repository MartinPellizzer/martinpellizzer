Prunus serrulata f. dilatata

Prunus serrulata f. dilatata, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a cultivar of the Prunus serrulata species.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant is a deciduous tree with a rounded, spreading habit and smooth, gray bark. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, which give it the species name *serrulata*.

This ornamental tree is highly valued for its aesthetic appeal and is widely planted in gardens and parks, especially in Japan and other parts of East Asia.
