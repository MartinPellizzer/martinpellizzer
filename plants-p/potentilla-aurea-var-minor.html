Potentilla aurea var. minor

Potentilla aurea var. minor, commonly known as golden cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from May to June. The plant has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and compound leaves composed of five oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
