Potentilla arenaria var. meridionalis

Potentilla arenaria var. meridionalis, commonly known as the southern sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. The plant features small, upright stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or pale yellow, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize sandy soils.
