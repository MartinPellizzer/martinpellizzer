Physostegia obovata

Physostegia obovata, commonly known as the narrowleaf blueflag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and showy blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
