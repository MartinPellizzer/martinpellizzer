Pelargonium inquinans var. pictum

Pelargonium inquinans var. pictum, commonly known as the painted geranium, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored leaves that display shades of red, purple, and green, often with contrasting patterns. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its unique foliage, it is widely cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in horticultural settings.
