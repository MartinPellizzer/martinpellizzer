Polemonium acutiflorum subsp. vanbruntiae

Polemonium acutiflorum subsp.

vanbruntiae, commonly known as Vanbrunt's Jacob's ladder, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a desirable addition to native plant gardens.
