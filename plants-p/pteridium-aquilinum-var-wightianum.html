Pteridium aquilinum var. wightianum

Pteridium aquilinum var. wightianum, commonly known as the bracken fern, is a widespread fern species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its large, triangular fronds that can grow up to 2 meters in length, with a dark green color and a serrated margin. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands, clearings, and disturbed areas. It reproduces via spores, which are released from the sporangia located on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open spaces, making it an important pioneer species in ecological succession.
