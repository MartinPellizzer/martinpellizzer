Prunella alpina

Prunella alpina, commonly known as alpine heal-all, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Prunella alpina is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
