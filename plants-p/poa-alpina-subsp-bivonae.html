Poa alpina subsp. bivonae

Poa alpina subsp.

bivonae, commonly known as the alpine bluegrass, is a subspecies native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage.

The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments such as alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-ranked spikelets.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and flowering structure, making it a notable component of alpine flora.
