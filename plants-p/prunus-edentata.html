Prunus edentata

Prunus edentata, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Edible cherry, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. Prunus edentata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
