Piper hirtellipetiolum var. tapianum

Piper hirtellipetiolum var. tapianum, commonly known as the Tapian pepper, is a variant of the Piper hirtellipetiolum species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of tiny, peppery fruits. The plant belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species. Its botanical features include a hairy stem and aromatic foliage, typical of many Piper species.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines for its pungent flavor and medicinal properties.
