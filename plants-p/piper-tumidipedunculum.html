Piper tumidipedunculum

Piper tumidipedunculum, commonly known as the swollen-stemd pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 2,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, peppery fruits that are edible.

Due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
