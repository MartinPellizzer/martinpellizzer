Polygala vulgaris var. collina

Polygala vulgaris var. collina, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its adaptability in harsh climates.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid and semi-arid habitats.
