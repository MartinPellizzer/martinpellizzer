Pouteria mensalis

Pouteria mensalis, commonly known as the Brazilian peach or pitanga, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different tropical environments.
