Potentilla friedrichsenii

Potentilla friedrichsenii, commonly known as the Friedrichsen's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has finely divided, serrated leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice among gardeners interested in native and drought-tolerant plants.
