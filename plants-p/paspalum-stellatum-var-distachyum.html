Paspalum stellatum var. distachyum

Paspalum stellatum var. distachyum, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy stems. The plant produces small, two-flowered spikelets that are often clustered at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green color.

It is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
