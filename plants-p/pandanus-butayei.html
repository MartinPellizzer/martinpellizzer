Pandanus butayei

Pandanus butayei, commonly known as the Butayi pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically grouped in spikes, followed by ornamental fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and requires well-drained soil.

The Butayi pandanus is also valued for its resilience to salt spray and strong winds.
