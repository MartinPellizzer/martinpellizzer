Paspalum conjugatum var. tristachyum

Paspalum conjugatum var. tristachyum, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in width. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
