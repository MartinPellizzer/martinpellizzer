Phoradendron warmingii var. rugulosum

Phoradendron warmingii var. rugulosum, commonly known as the roughleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in the southeastern United States.

It has a distinctive appearance with small, leathery, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a rough, waxy texture. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are usually red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species and contributing to the biodiversity of forest ecosystems.
