Phoebe pallida subsp. borii

Phoebe pallida subsp.

borii, commonly known as the white phoebe, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in woodland edges.

The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, white flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom in late spring.

The fruit is a small, dark blue drupe that develops from the flowers.
