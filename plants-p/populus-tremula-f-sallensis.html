Populus tremula f. sallensis

Populus tremula f. sallensis, commonly known as the Sallenskog poplar, is a variant of the aspen tree native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green catkins in early spring, which are a key feature of its reproductive system. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This form is notable for its ecological adaptability and role in supporting local wildlife.
