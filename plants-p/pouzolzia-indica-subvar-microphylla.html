Pouzolzia indica subvar. microphylla

Pouzolzia indica subvar. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaf pouzolzia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural use.
