Potentilla bifurca var. unijuga

Potentilla bifurca var. unijuga, commonly known as the single-jugate potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in the summer, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and prolonged flowering period.
