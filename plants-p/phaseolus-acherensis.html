Phaseolus acherensis

Phaseolus acherensis, commonly known as the Andean runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Andes region of South America.

It produces elongated, edible pods that are often used in traditional cuisines. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and bears yellow flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a genetic resource for breeding programs aimed at improving bean crops.
