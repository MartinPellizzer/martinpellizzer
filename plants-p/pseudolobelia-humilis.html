Pseudolobelia humilis

Pseudolobelia humilis, commonly known as the dwarf lobelia, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features small, delicate flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in clusters along its stems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Pseudolobelia humilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
