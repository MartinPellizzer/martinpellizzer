Panicum loreum var. angustifolium

Panicum loreum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites, thriving in full sun to partial shade. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of small, spikelet-like flowers that are wind-pollinated.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
