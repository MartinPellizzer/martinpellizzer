Polemonium caeruleum subsp. amygdalinum

Polemonium caeruleum subsp.

amygdalinum, commonly known as the white fringed gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It features upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers with prominent white fringes, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
