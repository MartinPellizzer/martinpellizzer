Prunus paramutabilis var. dentisepala

Prunus paramutabilis var. dentisepala, commonly known as the serrate-flowered cherry, is a rare and ornamental species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive serrated sepals that remain visible even after the petals have fallen. The plant typically grows as a small shrub or tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The species is valued for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
