Potamogeton × nitens f. maelarensis

Potamogeton × nitens f. maelarensis, commonly known as the Maelaren pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a submerged herb with long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne in clusters near the water surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

Its presence can indicate good water quality and is sometimes used as an indicator species in ecological studies.
