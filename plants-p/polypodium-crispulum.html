Polypodium crispulum

Polypodium crispulum, commonly known as the crested fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crisped fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizome is often covered in a dense, velvety layer of brownish hairs.

Polypodium crispulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
