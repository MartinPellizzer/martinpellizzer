Piper saltuum

Piper saltuum, commonly known as the saltbush pepper or saltbush pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants, and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant thrives in saline and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The fruit, which is small and berry-like, contains the plant's aromatic seeds used in local culinary applications.
