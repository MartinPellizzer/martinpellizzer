Pyrus sativa

Pyrus sativa, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and white or yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a pome, typically harvested in autumn, and is known for its sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.

It has been cultivated for centuries for both its fruit and ornamental value.
