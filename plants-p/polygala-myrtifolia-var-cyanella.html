Polygala myrtifolia var. cyanella

Polygala myrtifolia var. cyanella, commonly known as the blue-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, delicate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its most distinctive characteristic is its clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.

The species is also noted for its role in supporting local pollinators.
