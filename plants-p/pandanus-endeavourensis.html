Pandanus endeavourensis

Pandanus endeavourensis, commonly known as the Endeavour pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the Pandanaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, particularly around the island of Papua New Guinea. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that form a rosette at the top. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green in color.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional practices by local communities.
