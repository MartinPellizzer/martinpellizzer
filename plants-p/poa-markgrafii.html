Poa markgrafii

Poa markgrafii, commonly known as Markgraf's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its foliage is often blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Poa markgrafii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
