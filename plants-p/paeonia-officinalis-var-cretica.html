Paeonia officinalis var. cretica

Paeonia officinalis var. cretica, commonly known as the Cretan peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the芍药科 (Paeoniaceae) family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant features palmate leaves with deeply lobed margins and produces woody rhizomes that store nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its historical use in traditional medicine, it holds cultural and botanical significance in the Mediterranean.
