Pterococcus leucocladus var. flexuosus

Pterococcus leucocladus var. flexuosus, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring, giving it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with elliptical leaflets and produces woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
