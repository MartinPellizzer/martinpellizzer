Plectranthus almamii

Plectranthus almamii, commonly known as the Cape verbena, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive bluish-green hue. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, typically blooming from summer to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
