Pinanga clemensii

Pinanga clemensii, commonly known as the Bornean bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Borneo and is characterized by its slender, clustered stems and pinnate leaves. The plant typically grows as an understory species, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescences are borne in clusters and produce small, fragrant flowers.

Pinanga clemensii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
