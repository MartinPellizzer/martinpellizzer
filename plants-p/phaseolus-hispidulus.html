Phaseolus hispidulus

Phaseolus hispidulus, commonly known as the hairy runner bean, is a species native to the Americas.

It is a climbing vine that produces edible pods and beans, often cultivated for its nutritional value. The plant features trifoliate leaves and hairy stems, which give it its common name. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Phaseolus hispidulus is valued both for its culinary uses and its role in traditional agriculture.
