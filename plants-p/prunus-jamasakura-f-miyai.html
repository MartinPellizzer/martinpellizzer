Prunus jamasakura f. miyai

Prunus jamasakura f. miyai, commonly known as the Miyai cherry, is a unique variant of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a striking visual contrast against the green foliage. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional Japanese cuisine. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
