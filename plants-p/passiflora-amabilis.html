Passiflora amabilis

Passiflora amabilis, commonly known as the sweet passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a crown of red filaments and a central cluster of blue and white petals. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its climbing growth habit.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
