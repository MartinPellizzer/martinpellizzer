Persea curranii

Persea curranii, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces orange to reddish fruits that are typically harvested when fully ripe. The fruit has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor and is often eaten fresh or used in culinary applications.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
