Pituranthos chloranthus var. calvescens

Pituranthos chloranthus var. calvescens, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
