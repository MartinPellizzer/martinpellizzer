Philippia hispida subsp. angustifolia

Philippia hispida subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf philippia, is a shrub native to South Africa. It features small, narrow leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
