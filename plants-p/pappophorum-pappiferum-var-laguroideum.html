Pappophorum pappiferum var. laguroideum

Pappophorum pappiferum var. laguroideum, commonly known as the Laguroideum grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters at the tips of its stems. It is typically found in sandy or gravelly soils in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its low-growing nature, it is often used in landscaping for erosion control and as a decorative ground cover.
