Prunus × mochidzukiana

Prunus × mochidzukiana, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its small, sweet-tart fruits and is native to Japan. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are pink to white, appearing in early spring before the leaves, adding ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
