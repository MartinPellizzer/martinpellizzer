Parthenocissus dumetorum

Parthenocissus dumetorum, commonly known as poison ivy vine, is a climbing plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with three leaflets, often covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. Its sap contains urushiol, a toxic oil that can cause severe skin irritation in humans.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found along woodland edges and stream banks.
