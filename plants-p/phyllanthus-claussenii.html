Phyllanthus claussenii

Phyllanthus claussenii, commonly known as the African date palm, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with a broad, umbrella-like canopy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, fleshy fruits resembling dates. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with numerous small leaflets that give it a feathery appearance.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for coastal and coastal plain regions.
