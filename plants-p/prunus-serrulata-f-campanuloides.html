Prunus serrulata f. campanuloides

Prunus serrulata f. campanuloides, commonly known as the campanuloid cherry or the bell-shaped cherry, is a unique variant of the Japanese flowering cherry.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, often in shades of white or pale pink. The plant is a compact shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces small, edible fruits that ripen in late summer.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal blooms.
