Pilosella flagellaris subsp. hahnii

Pilosella flagellaris subsp.

hahnii, commonly known as the Hahn's hairweed, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

Native to the mountainous regions of South America, this species thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
