Pilosocereus mollispinus

Pilosocereus mollispinus, commonly known as the silver crown cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the night, which are pollinated by bats and other nocturnal animals. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and adapting to harsh desert conditions.

The plant's structure and flowering habits make it a unique example of desert flora adaptation.
