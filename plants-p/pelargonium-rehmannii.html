Pelargonium rehmannii

Pelargonium rehmannii, commonly known as the Cape Geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces bright pink to red blooms, which appear in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a velvety texture.

Pelargonium rehmannii is well-suited for rock gardens and containers due to its compact size and ornamental value.
