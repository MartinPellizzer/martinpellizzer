Passiflora eslavensis

Passiflora eslavensis, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Andes, is a climbing vine native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with a crown of filaments resembling a crown of thorns. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional medicine for their calming effects. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and twining.

Passiflora eslavensis thrives in cool, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as for its medicinal properties.
