Poa filiculmis

Poa filiculmis, commonly known as the featherleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often described as having a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny blue to purple flowers.

Poa filiculmis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
