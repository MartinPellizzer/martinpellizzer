Pavonia sagittata var. conjungens

Pavonia sagittata var. conjungens, commonly known as the scarlet false nettle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and forest edges. The plant features slender, ascending stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its showy, red bracts are clustered at the ends of the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, but the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
