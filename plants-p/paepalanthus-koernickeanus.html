Paepalanthus koernickeanus

Paepalanthus koernickeanus, commonly known as the Brazilian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and is adapted to tropical climates. It has a short stem with large, broad leaves and produces numerous small, yellow florets arranged in a composite flower head.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in warm, humid environments.
