Pyrus pyraster f. kitaibeliana

Pyrus pyraster f. kitaibeliana, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its grayish-barked trunk and oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries that are an important food source for birds and mammals.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.
