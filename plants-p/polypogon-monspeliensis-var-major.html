Polypogon monspeliensis var. major

Polypogon monspeliensis var. major, commonly known as the tall flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers. It is characterized by its tall stature, usually reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Botanically, it is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
