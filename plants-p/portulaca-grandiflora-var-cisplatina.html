Portulaca grandiflora var. cisplatina

Portulaca grandiflora var. cisplatina, commonly known as the Brazilian portulaca, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to yellow. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
