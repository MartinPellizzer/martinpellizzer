Parodia setifera f. betaniana

Parodia setifera f. betaniana, commonly known as the Betanian Hedgehog Cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the deserts of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny, globular shape and is often mistaken for a hedgehog due to its appearance. The plant features yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
