Polygonum bishirae

Polygonum bishirae, commonly known as the Bishir's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as smartweeds or knotweeds. The plant is characterized by its upright stems, which are often reddish in color, and its small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive reddish tint, especially when exposed to full sunlight.

Polygonum bishirae is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cool, moist environments.
