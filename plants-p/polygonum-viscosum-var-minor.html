Polygonum viscosum var. minor

Polygonum viscosum var. minor, commonly known as sticky smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems covered in a sticky, glandular substance. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a waxy coating. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer.

This plant is often found in moist soils along stream banks, wet meadows, and disturbed areas.
