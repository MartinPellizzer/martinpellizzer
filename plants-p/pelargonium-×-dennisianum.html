Pelargonium × dennisianum

Pelargonium × dennisianum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Native to South Africa, it is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for hanging baskets and containers.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal, it is popular in both indoor and outdoor gardening.
