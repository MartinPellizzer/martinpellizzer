Peponidium pallens

Peponidium pallens, commonly known as the white-flowered melon vine, is a tropical plant belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically light green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers that give way to spherical, pale green fruits with a sweet, melon-like flavor. Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ornamental and edible horticulture.

Peponidium pallens thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
