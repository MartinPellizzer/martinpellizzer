Piper maynasianum

Piper maynasianum, commonly known as the Maynasian pepper or Maynasian pepper vine, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
