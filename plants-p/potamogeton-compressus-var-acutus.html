Potamogeton compressus var. acutus

Potamogeton compressus var. acutus, commonly known as the narrowleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters such as lakes, ponds, and streams. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are narrow and tapering, with a smooth, compressed appearance. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
