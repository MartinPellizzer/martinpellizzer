Phyllocactus phyllanthus var. paraguayensis

Phyllocactus phyllanthus var. paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan phyllocactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and pointed, with a waxy texture that reduces water loss.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
