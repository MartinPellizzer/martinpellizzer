Potamogeton × nitens f. praelongifolius

Potamogeton × nitens f. praelongifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant characterized by its elongated, ribbon-like leaves.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The leaves are usually submerged, and the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or floating. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize sediment and improve water clarity.

This plant plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
