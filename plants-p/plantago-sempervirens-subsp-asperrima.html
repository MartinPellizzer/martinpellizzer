Plantago sempervirens subsp. asperrima

Plantago sempervirens subsp.

asperrima, commonly known as the rough plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It features upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, which are borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

Its fibrous roots and robust growth make it a hardy, adaptable species.
