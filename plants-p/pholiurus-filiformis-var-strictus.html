Pholiurus filiformis var. strictus

Pholiurus filiformis var. strictus, commonly known as the narrowleaf pholiurus, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 2 centimeters in length. Its flowers are small and clustered in loose inflorescences, often appearing in shades of white or pale pink.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance.
