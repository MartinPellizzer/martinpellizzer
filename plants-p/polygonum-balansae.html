Polygonum balansae

Polygonum balansae, commonly known as Balansae knotweed, is a species of flowering plant in the buckwheat family, Polygonaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish flowers. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
