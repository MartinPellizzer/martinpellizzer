Poincianella robinsoniana

Poincianella robinsoniana, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow inflorescences that appear in clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its role in supporting local wildlife.
