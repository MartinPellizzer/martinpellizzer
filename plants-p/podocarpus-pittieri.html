Podocarpus pittieri

Podocarpus pittieri, commonly known as the dwarf podocarp, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, cone-like structures. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often found in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive elliptical shape, while its cones are small and typically borne in clusters.

Podocarpus pittieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.
