Pilea jamesoniana

Pilea jamesoniana, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to China.

It features rosettes of thick, waxy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its tolerance of low light and ability to thrive in a variety of potting conditions.

Its unique leaf shape and ease of care make it a popular choice for both novice and experienced plant enthusiasts.
