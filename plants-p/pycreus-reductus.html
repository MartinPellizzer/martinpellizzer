Pycreus reductus

Pycreus reductus, commonly known as the reduced pycreus, is a rare and enigmatic plant species characterized by its unique botanical features.

It exhibits a compact growth habit with small, lance-shaped leaves that are densely packed along its short, wiry stems. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically pale green to white in color, often with subtle red or purple markings. It is adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments, such as along the edges of streams or in dense forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Pycreus reductus is considered a species of conservation concern.
