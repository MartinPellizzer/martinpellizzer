Pyrrhocactus chorosensis

Pyrrhocactus chorosensis, commonly known as the red-horned cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a compact, globular body with a spiny, reddish-brown exterior that helps protect it from intense sunlight and herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in drought conditions.

Overall, Pyrrhocactus chorosensis is a striking example of botanical adaptation to extreme environments.
