Panicum barbinode

Panicum barbinode, commonly known as the bristly panicgrass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and elongated, bristly inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, while the seeds are small and wind-dispersed.

Panicum barbinode is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
