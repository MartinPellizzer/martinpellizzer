Pappophorum mucronulatum

Pappophorum mucronulatum, commonly known as the slender spikegrass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in open grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its seeds are enclosed in a pappus, a feathery structure that aids in their spread by the wind.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
