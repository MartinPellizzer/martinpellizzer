Phryma pubescens

Phryma pubescens, commonly known as the rough phryma, is a herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Phrymaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and streambanks.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported medicinal properties.
