Porophyllum riedelii

Porophyllum riedelii, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in full sun and well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Porophyllum riedelii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
