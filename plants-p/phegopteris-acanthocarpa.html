Phegopteris acanthocarpa

Phegopteris acanthocarpa, commonly known as the hairy maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often covered in small, hair-like structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
