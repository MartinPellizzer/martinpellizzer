Phlox odorata

Phlox odorata, commonly known as sweet phlox, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is known for its attractive foliage and long blooming period.

Phlox odorata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
