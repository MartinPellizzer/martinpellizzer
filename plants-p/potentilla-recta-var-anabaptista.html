Potentilla recta var. anabaptista

Potentilla recta var. anabaptista, commonly known as the Anabaptist cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The leaves are typically palmately divided into five leaflets, which are hairy and have a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
