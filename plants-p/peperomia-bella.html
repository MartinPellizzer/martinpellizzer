Peperomia bella

Peperomia bella, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a compact, low-maintenance plant native to Central and South America.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are often tinged with silver or white markings, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or pink, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its attractive foliage and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
