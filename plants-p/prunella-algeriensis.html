Prunella algeriensis

Prunella algeriensis, commonly known as Algerian deadnettle, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically blue or violet, with two lips, and are arranged in whorls along the stem.

Prunella algeriensis is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
