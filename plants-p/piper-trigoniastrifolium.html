Piper trigoniastrifolium

Piper trigoniastrifolium, commonly known as the three-leaf pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its compound leaves with three leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is cultivated for its peppery fruits, which are used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features include a climbing habit, hairy stems, and aromatic foliage, making it a distinctive member of the pepper family.
