Pulsatilla alpina subsp. sulphurea

Pulsatilla alpina subsp.

sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its foliage consists of palmate, compound leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair.
