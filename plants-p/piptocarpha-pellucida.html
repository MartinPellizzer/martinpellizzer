Piptocarpha pellucida

Piptocarpha pellucida, commonly known as the silky blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features gray-green, hairy leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Piptocarpha pellucida is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
