Pulicaria vulgaris var. decumbens

Pulicaria vulgaris var. decumbens, commonly known as the creeping tansy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features wiry, decumbent stems that lie flat on the ground, often rooting at the nodes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, slightly hairy, and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and gravelly areas.
