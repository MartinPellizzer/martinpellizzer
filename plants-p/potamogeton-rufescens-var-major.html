Potamogeton rufescens var. major

Potamogeton rufescens var. major, commonly known as the red-stemmed pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It features long, slender stems that are typically reddish in color, especially when mature, and bear small, spiral-arranged leaves. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by small, elongated fruits. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

This plant is often found in slow-moving waters such as lakes, ponds, and wetlands.
