Phoenicanthemum setigerum

Phoenicanthemum setigerum, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red sandalwood tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, dark reddish-brown bark. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its valuable timber and ornamental qualities, it is sometimes cultivated for both ecological and economic purposes.
