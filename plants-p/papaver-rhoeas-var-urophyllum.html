Papaver rhoeas var. urophyllum

Papaver rhoeas var. urophyllum, commonly known as the scarlet poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its vibrant red petals and a distinctive, slender seed pod.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a striking visual impact in natural settings. Its botanical features include a simple, erect stem and palmate leaves, with the flowers being showy and often used in ornamental gardening.

The plant is also notable for its historical use in medicine and as a source of opium.
