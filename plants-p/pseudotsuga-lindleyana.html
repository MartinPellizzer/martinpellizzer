Pseudotsuga lindleyana

Pseudotsuga lindleyana, commonly known as the Lindley's hemlock, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing some resistance to forest fires.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, supporting a variety of wildlife.
