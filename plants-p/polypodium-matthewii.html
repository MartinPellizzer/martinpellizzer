Polypodium matthewii

Polypodium matthewii, commonly known as the Matthew's huperzia, is a rare and endangered fern species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the family Huperziaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in subtropical forests. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, conservation efforts are critical for its survival.
