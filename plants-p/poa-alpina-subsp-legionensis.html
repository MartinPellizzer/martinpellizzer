Poa alpina subsp. legionensis

Poa alpina subsp.

legionensis, commonly known as the Alpine bentgrass, is a low-growing grass species native to mountainous regions. It is characterized by its fine, tufted blades and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates. Its reproductive structures include small, spike-like inflorescences that produce wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in harsh environments.
