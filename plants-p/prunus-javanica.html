Prunus javanica

Prunus javanica, commonly known as the Java cherry or Javan plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, followed by edible drupes that turn from green to red or yellow when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.
