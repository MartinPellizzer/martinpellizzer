Procris rostrata

Procris rostrata, commonly known as the rostrate orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive rosette-shaped growth pattern. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a unique rostrum, or beak-like structure, on the lip of each bloom. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Procris rostrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
