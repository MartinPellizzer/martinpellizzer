Phyllopodium rustii

Phyllopodium rustii, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique plant characterized by its soft, velvety foliage.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to certain regions of South America. The plant exhibits a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping stems that produce clusters of small, heart-shaped fronds. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures that give it a distinctive texture.

This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.
