Polygala guilanica

Polygala guilanica, commonly known as the Guilan milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to the forests of northern Iran.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The flowers are usually pale yellow or white, with a distinctive spurred structure that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
