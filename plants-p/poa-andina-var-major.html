Poa andina var. major

Poa andina var. major, commonly known as the Andean grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Andes Mountains.

It typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in rocky or alpine environments. The plant has slender, tufted shoots with narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to cold and dry conditions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous areas.
