Pilosocereus ulei

Pilosocereus ulei, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a cactus species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its unique appearance, featuring a swollen, colorful upper portion called the "pseudostem" and a slender, green lower stem. The plant is a hybrid between the genus Hylocereus and Pilosocereus, which contributes to its striking floral display. It produces fragrant white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators.

This plant is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
