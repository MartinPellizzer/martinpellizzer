Penstemon pickettii

Penstemon pickettii, commonly known as Pickett's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Penstemon pickettii is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
