Poa multiradiata

Poa multiradiata, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and silver-gray foliage, which results from its fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Poa multiradiata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
