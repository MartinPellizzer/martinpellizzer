Phoradendron herminieri

Phoradendron herminieri, commonly known as the mistletoe of the cactus, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees, primarily in arid and semi-arid regions.

It possesses small, scale-like leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered together. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides nectar to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.
