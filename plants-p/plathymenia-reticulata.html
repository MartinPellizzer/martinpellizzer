Plathymenia reticulata

Plathymenia reticulata, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with a rounded crown and smooth, grey bark. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are arranged in clusters, followed by woody pods containing flat, elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

Due to its attractive foliage and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
