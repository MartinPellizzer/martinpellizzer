Puccinellia stricta

Puccinellia stricta, commonly known as spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often densely covered with small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy texture.

Puccinellia stricta plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
