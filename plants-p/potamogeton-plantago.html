Potamogeton plantago

Potamogeton plantago, commonly known as broad-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically broad at the base and tapering towards the tip. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, often visible above the water surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Potamogeton plantago is adapted to slow-moving or still waters and can form dense underwater vegetation.
