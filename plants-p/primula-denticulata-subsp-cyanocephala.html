Primula denticulata subsp. cyanocephala

Primula denticulata subsp.

cyanocephala, commonly known as the blue-headed primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China. It is characterized by its distinctive blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with finely toothed margins, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often found in rocky soils and forest understories, where it forms dense clumps.

Due to its ornamental value and unique coloration, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
