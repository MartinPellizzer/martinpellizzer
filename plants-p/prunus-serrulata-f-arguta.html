Prunus serrulata f. arguta

Prunus serrulata f. arguta, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or some varieties refer to it as the "Ume" or "Japanese apricot," is a compact, ornamental shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are serrated along the edges, giving it the species name "serrulata.". The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and parks.

This form is a natural variant of Prunus serrulata, known for its cold hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
