Paraderris piscatoria

Paraderris piscatoria, commonly known as the water lily of the southern seas, is a perennial aquatic plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as marshes and estuaries, where it forms dense, floating mats. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are typically white or pale blue and are pollinated by insects.

The roots of Paraderris piscatoria help stabilize sediment and improve water quality in its habitat.
