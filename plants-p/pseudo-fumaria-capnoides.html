Pseudo-fumaria capnoides

Pseudo-fumaria capnoides, commonly known as the false fumaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces delicate, yellowish-white blossoms that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Pseudo-fumaria capnoides is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
