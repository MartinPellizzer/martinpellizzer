Prismatocarpus nitidus var. ovatus

Prismatocarpus nitidus var. ovatus, commonly known as the round-leafed pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into orange-colored, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in coastal ecosystems.
