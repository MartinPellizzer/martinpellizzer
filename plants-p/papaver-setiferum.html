Papaver setiferum

Papaver setiferum, commonly known as the Armenian poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant produces numerous slender, feathery bracts that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The seeds of Papaver setiferum are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural spread in suitable habitats.
