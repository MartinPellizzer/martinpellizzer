Paranomus reflexus

Paranomus reflexus, commonly known as the reflexed parasol, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, reflexed leaves that curve backward, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Paranomus reflexus is adapted to cold, rocky environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
