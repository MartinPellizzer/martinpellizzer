Physosiphon bragae

Physosiphon bragae, commonly known as the Brazilian water lily, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It features broad, floating leaves that provide shade and oxygen to the surrounding water. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom above the water surface, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are anchored in the muddy substrate, allowing it to thrive in still or slow-moving water bodies.

Physosiphon bragae plays a vital role in maintaining aquatic ecosystems by supporting biodiversity and improving water quality.
