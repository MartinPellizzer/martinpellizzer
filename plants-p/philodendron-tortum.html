Philodendron tortum

Philodendron tortum, commonly known as the twisted philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that often have a distinctive wavy or undulating margin. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, which are typically followed by red, fleshy berries. Philodendron tortum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its unique leaf morphology makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening enthusiasts.
