Persea kingii

Persea kingii, commonly known as the Mexican avocado or the Guatemalan avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces large, pear-shaped fruits with a thick, smooth skin that turns from green to purple as it matures. The fruit contains a single large seed and is valued for its rich, creamy pulp used in various culinary applications.

Botanically, it is noted for its adaptability to a range of soil types and its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
