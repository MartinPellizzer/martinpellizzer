Penstemon venustus

Penstemon venustus, commonly known as the showy penstemon, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces tall spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
