Papaver radicatum f. lineatum

Papaver radicatum f. lineatum, commonly known as the lined poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its distinctive linear markings on the petals.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The flowers are typically bright red or orange, with a prominent central stigma surrounded by a series of narrow, elongated petals. The plant produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous small, dark seeds.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
