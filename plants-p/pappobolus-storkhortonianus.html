Pappobolus storkhortonianus

Pappobolus storkhortonianus, commonly known as the Stork's-horn or Stork's-bill, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny seed pods that resemble a stork's bill, which is a key botanical feature. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

The species is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.
