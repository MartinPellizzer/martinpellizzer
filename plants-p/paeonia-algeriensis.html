Paeonia algeriensis

Paeonia algeriensis, commonly known as the Algerian peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Algeria.

It belongs to the family Paeoniaceae and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with large, palmately compound leaves and thick, fleshy roots. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
