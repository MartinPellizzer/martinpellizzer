Potentilla argentea guentheri

Potentilla argentea guentheri, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its silver-white, fuzzy foliage that reflects sunlight, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are typically clustered in groups of five. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
