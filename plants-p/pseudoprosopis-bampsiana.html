Pseudoprosopis bampsiana

Pseudoprosopis bampsiana, commonly known as the Bamps' mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its deep root system, which allows it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically pollinated by insects, followed by the development of long, bean-like pods. Its leaves are usually small and bipinnate, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife in its native range.
