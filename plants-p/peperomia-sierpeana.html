Peperomia sierpeana

Peperomia sierpeana, commonly known as the Sierpe peperomia, is a species native to Central America, particularly Costa Rica.

It is a compact, low-growing plant with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or purple, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often grown as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage.

Peperomia sierpeana is relatively easy to care for, requiring well-draining soil and moderate watering.
