Piptatherum vavilovii

Piptatherum vavilovii, commonly known as the Vavilov's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are adapted for wind pollination. It is well-suited to harsh environmental conditions, including drought and poor soil, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Piptatherum vavilovii is often found in steppe and desert ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing forage for grazing animals.
