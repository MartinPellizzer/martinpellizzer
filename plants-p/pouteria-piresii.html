Pouteria piresii

Pouteria piresii, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody pods containing edible seeds. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and has a dense canopy, making it a significant component of the rainforest ecosystem. Its fruit, known as a cupuacu, is widely used in the food and cosmetic industries.

The plant also plays a crucial role in supporting local biodiversity and soil stability in its natural habitat.
