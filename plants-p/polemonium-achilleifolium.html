Polemonium achilleifolium

Polemonium achilleifolium, commonly known as bluebells, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight hairy texture.

Polemonium achilleifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
