Piper candelarianum var. latifolium

Piper candelarianum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper plant, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its large, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
