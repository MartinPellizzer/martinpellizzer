Pinanga plicata

Pinanga plicata, commonly known as the pleated bamboo or pleated reed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its narrow, pleated leaves that give it its common name, and it typically grows in wet, swampy areas. Pinanga plicata has a rhizomatous root system and produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant due to its unique foliage.
