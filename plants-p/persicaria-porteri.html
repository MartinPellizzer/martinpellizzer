Persicaria porteri

Persicaria porteri, commonly known as the redroot hogweed, is a member of the Polygonaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often reddish in color, which gives it its common name.

Persicaria porteri is native to North America and is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as wetlands and stream banks.
