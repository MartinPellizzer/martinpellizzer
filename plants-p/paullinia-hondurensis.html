Paullinia hondurensis

Paullinia hondurensis, commonly known as Honduran soursop, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green, inedible fruits that are typically harvested when mature. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

Paullinia hondurensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
