Pterophylla clemensiae

Pterophylla clemensiae, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a striking shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has leathery, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its showy blooms. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.

Pterophylla clemensiae is also known for its ability to grow in coastal areas, tolerating salty air and wind.
