Pavetta sphaerobotrys subsp. tanaica

Pavetta sphaerobotrys subsp.

tanaica, commonly known as the Tanaic pavetta, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with smooth margins and a glossy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
