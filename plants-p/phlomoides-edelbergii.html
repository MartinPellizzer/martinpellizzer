Phlomoides edelbergii

Phlomoides edelbergii, commonly known as Edelberg's panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, white to pale blue florets. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
