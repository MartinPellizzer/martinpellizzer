Pyrus taofuensis

Pyrus taofuensis, commonly known as the Taofu pear, is a rare species native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, ornamental flowers and edible fruit. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
