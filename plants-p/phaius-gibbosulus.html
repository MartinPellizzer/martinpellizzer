Phaius gibbosulus

Phaius gibbosulus, commonly known as the warty marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in wet, swampy habitats and is characterized by its distinctive, warty tubers. The plant produces tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a prominent lip, and it blooms during the rainy season.

Phaius gibbosulus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native wetland ecosystems.
