Polygala sibirica var. ciliata

Polygala sibirica var. ciliata, commonly known as the Siberian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, and it produces small, yellowish or greenish flowers in clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
