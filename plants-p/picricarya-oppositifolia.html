Picricarya oppositifolia

Picricarya oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaf picricarya, is a flowering plant species native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement, where leaves grow in pairs along the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the warmer months. Its botanical structure includes a woody stem and simple, ovate leaves with a smooth surface.

It is typically found in moist, shaded environments within tropical or subtropical forests.
