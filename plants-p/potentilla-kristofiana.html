Potentilla kristofiana

Potentilla kristofiana, commonly known as the Kristofian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features small, palmately lobed leaves that are hairy beneath, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
