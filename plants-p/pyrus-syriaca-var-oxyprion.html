Pyrus syriaca var. oxyprion

Pyrus syriaca var. oxyprion, commonly known as the Syrian white pear, is a variety of the common pear tree native to the Middle East.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has a rounded canopy with dark green, lobed leaves that provide shade. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

The fruit is not widely cultivated for commercial use but is appreciated for its ornamental value and historical significance.
