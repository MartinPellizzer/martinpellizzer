Phaius stuppeus

Phaius stuppeus, commonly known as the yellow ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble those of true gingers. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as swamps and marshes. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy surface.

Phaius stuppeus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical gardens for its vibrant floral display.
