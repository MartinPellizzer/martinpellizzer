Potentilla adinophylla

Potentilla adinophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense pubescence covering the leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climatic conditions.
