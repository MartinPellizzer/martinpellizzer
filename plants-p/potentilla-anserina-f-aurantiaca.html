Potentilla anserina f. aurantiaca

Potentilla anserina f. aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden cinquefoil, is a variant of the silver cinquefoil.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Europe, characterized by its bright yellow flowers. The plant features compound leaves with five oval leaflets, and its stems are often covered in silver-white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

The golden hue of its flowers makes it a striking addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
