Prunus densifolia

Prunus densifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Prunus densifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
