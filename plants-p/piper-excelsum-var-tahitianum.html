Piper excelsum var. tahitianum

Piper excelsum var. tahitianum, commonly known as the Tahitian pepper or *Piper excelsum* var. *tahitianum*, is a tropical vine native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, peppery fruits that are used in traditional Polynesian cuisine. It thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil and requires partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is cultivated in some tropical regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
