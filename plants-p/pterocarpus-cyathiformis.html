Pterocarpus cyathiformis

Pterocarpus cyathiformis, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, flame-like clusters of small white flowers. The tree produces long, flat pods that contain flat, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are compound, with multiple pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

The wood of Pterocarpus cyathiformis is valued for its durability and is used in traditional crafts and furniture.
