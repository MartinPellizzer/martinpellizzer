Pavonia sagittata var. genuina

Pavonia sagittata var. genuina, commonly known as the arrowleaf pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves, which are usually dark green and have a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
