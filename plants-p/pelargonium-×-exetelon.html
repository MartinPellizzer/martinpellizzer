Pelargonium × exetelon

Pelargonium × exetelon, commonly known as the hybrid geranium, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Pelargonium hortorum and Pelargonium × inodorum.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded foliage and vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors such as red, pink, and white. The plant is typically grown as an ornamental, thriving in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil. It is known for its compact growth habit and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.

Pelargonium × exetelon is often used in garden beds, containers, and as bedding plants due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of cultivation.
