Plantago paralias subsp. dasystachys

Plantago paralias subsp.

dasystachys, commonly known as the narrow-leaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Europe. It features upright, hairy stems that bear alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
