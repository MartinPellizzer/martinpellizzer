Pseudocytisus Integrifolius

Pseudocytisus integrifolius is a rare and striking shrub native to the rugged coastal regions of southern Africa. Known for its vibrant orange-red flowers and dense, woody structure, it thrives in harsh, saline environments. This plant is often found growing on rocky outcrops and sandy dunes where few other species can survive. Its unique appearance and ecological importance make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists. Due to habitat loss and climate change, Pseudocytisus integrifolius faces increasing threats to its survival.
