Phyllocactus jenkinsonii

Phyllocactus jenkinsonii, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a unique succulent native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its flattened, segmented stems that resemble leaves, which are actually modified stems called phylloclades. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during the holiday season, typically in late winter to early spring. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value and seasonal blooming, it is a popular houseplant worldwide.
