Phyllanthus chamaecerasus f. ripicola

Phyllanthus chamaecerasus f. ripicola, commonly known as the rock phyllanthus, is a rare and specialized plant species native to rocky and arid environments.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, which are pollinated by insects. Its foliage is often covered in a fine layer of hairs, providing additional protection against desiccation.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to harsh, nutrient-poor soils.
