Psoralea brachiata

Psoralea brachiata, commonly known as the slender psoralea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
