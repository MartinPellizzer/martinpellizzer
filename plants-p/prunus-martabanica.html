Prunus martabanica

Prunus martabanica, commonly known as the Martaban cherry, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Myanmar and Thailand.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, sweet fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated edge, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Prunus martabanica is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological and cultural significance in its native habitats.
