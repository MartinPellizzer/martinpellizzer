Potentilla verna var. asiatica

Potentilla verna var. asiatica, commonly known as the Asian creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are arranged alternately along its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

This variety is closely related to other Potentilla species and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
