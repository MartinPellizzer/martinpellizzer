Paspalum achlysophilum

Paspalum achlysophilum, commonly known as the narrowleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in wetland environments and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family.

Paspalum achlysophilum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native wetland habitats.
