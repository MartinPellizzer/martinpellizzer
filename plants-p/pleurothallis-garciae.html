Pleurothallis garciae

Pleurothallis garciae, commonly known as the Garcia's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
