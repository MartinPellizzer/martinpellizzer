Peruvocereus multangularis

Peruvocereus multangularis, commonly known as the angular night blooming cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are often covered in spines, and it produces striking white flowers that open at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its blooming period typically occurs during the warmer months, and it is prized for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments.
