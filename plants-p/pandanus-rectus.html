Pandanus rectus

Pandanus rectus, commonly known as the bottlebrush pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pandanus.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, including parts of Australia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its stiff, sword-like leaves that are often used in traditional thatching and basket-making. Its inflorescence consists of long, narrow spikes of small, fragrant flowers, which are typically yellow or green.

The plant is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a common sight in mangrove and coastal forest ecosystems.
