Pachychilus montanus

Pachychilus montanus, commonly known as the mountain pachychilus, is a species of flowering plant in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Bolivia. This perennial herb is characterized by its compact growth habit and unique, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its ecological role and its potential for horticultural interest due to its ornamental value.
