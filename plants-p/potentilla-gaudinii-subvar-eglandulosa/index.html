Potentilla Gaudinii Subvar. Eglandulosa

Potentilla gaudinii subvar. eglandulosa is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of Europe. It is known for its small, showy yellow blooms that appear in late spring. This subspecies is characterized by its glandless leaves, which distinguish it from other members of the Potentilla genus. Due to its limited habitat, it is considered vulnerable and requires careful conservation efforts. Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a subject of interest for botanists and nature enthusiasts alike.
