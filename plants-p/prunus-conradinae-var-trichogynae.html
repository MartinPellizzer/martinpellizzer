Prunus conradinae var. trichogynae

Prunus conradinae var. trichogynae, commonly known as the trichogynous cherry, is a rare and endemic species native to specific regions of the southwestern United States.

It is a small shrub or tree characterized by its distinctive floral morphology, featuring both functional stamens and staminodes, which contribute to its unique pollination strategy. The plant produces small, sweet-tart fruits that are adapted to specific ecological niches. Its leaves are typically narrow and evergreen, providing year-round structure in its habitat.
