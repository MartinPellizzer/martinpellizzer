Potentilla grandiflora var. pedemontana

Potentilla grandiflora var. pedemontana, commonly known as the Alpine cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and dry conditions. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
