Piper yapeanum

Piper yapeanum, commonly known as the Yapean pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species, and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical fruits. The fruits are typically harvested when mature and can be used in traditional culinary applications.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological and ethnobotanical studies in the region.
