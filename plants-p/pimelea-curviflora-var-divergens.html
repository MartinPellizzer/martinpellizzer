Pimelea curviflora var. divergens

Pimelea curviflora var. divergens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waratah, is a shrub native to the southern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and striking, cone-shaped flowers that are typically red or orange in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, and the leaves are narrow and leathery.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens and landscaping.
