Paeonia arietina var. carnea

Paeonia arietina var. carnea, commonly known as the Armenian peony, is a deciduous perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double blooms in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of palmate leaves that are green and slightly hairy.

The Armenian peony is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
