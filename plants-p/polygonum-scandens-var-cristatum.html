Polygonum scandens var. cristatum

Polygonum scandens var. cristatum, commonly known as the crested knotweed, is a vigorous climbing perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive wavy or crested margin, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found along stream banks and in wetlands.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
