Piper scabrellum

Piper scabrellum, commonly known as the rough pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by berries that mature to a dark purple color.

Piper scabrellum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
