Pycreus flavescens f. caucasicus

Pycreus flavescens f. caucasicus, commonly known as the Caucasian yellow gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which are typically tubular with a short throat and prominent lobes. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus, where it thrives in moist, shaded habitats. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a rough texture, while its stems are erect and branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its vibrant floral display.
