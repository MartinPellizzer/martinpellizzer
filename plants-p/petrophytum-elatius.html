Petrophytum elatius

Petrophytum elatius, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert plains. The plant features a rosette of gray-green, succulent leaves that help it conserve water. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Petrophytum elatius is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
