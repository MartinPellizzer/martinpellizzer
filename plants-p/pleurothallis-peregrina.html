Pleurothallis peregrina

Pleurothallis peregrina, commonly known as the wandering orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom during the rainy season. It has thin, wiry stems and lance-shaped leaves that help it conserve moisture.

Pleurothallis peregrina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
