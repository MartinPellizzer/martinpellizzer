Panax crispatus

Panax crispatus, commonly known as the Japanese ginseng, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, crinkled leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red berries that contain the seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. Its root system is the primary part used for medicinal purposes, similar to other ginseng species.

Panax crispatus is valued for its adaptogenic properties and is often used to enhance vitality and reduce stress.
