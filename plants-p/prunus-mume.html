Prunus mume

Prunus mume, commonly known as the Japanese plum or ume, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that ripen to a deep red or purple color by late summer. Prunus mume is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.

Its leaves, flowers, and fruits contain various bioactive compounds with potential health benefits.
