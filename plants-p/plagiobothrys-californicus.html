Plagiobothrys californicus

Plagiobothrys californicus, commonly known as the California thimbleweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has a slender, branching stem covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands and stream banks.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
