Paphiopedilum daoense

Paphiopedilum daoense, commonly known as the Dao's lady's slipper, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to China.

It is characterized by its unique, pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper, a defining feature of the Paphiopedilum genus. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in mountainous regions with rich, well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a threatened species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts.
