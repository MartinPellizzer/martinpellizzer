Polygala alba var. alcalina

Polygala alba var. alcalina, commonly known as the white milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is adapted to hot, dry climates and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
