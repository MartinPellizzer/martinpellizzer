Primula dentata

Primula dentata, commonly known as the toothed primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are toothed, giving it its scientific name, and it blooms in early spring.

Primula dentata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
