Palmijuncus caesius

Palmijuncus caesius, commonly known as the golden palm, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that give it a palm-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red fruits containing edible seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, Palmijuncus caesius is now considered endangered in its natural range.
