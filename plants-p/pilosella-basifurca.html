Pilosella basifurca

Pilosella basifurca, commonly known as the basal-fruited hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It features a hairy stem and small, yellow flowers that are surrounded by pale green ray florets.

Pilosella basifurca is often found in open habitats and is considered a perennial herb.
