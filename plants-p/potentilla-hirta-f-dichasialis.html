Potentilla hirta f. dichasialis

Potentilla hirta f. dichasialis, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cold, harsh environments. Its leaves are usually alternate, with five leaflets that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

This subspecies is noted for its hardiness and is often found in tundra and subarctic habitats.
