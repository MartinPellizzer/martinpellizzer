Pollia sambiranensis

Pollia sambiranensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure and vibrant pigmentation. Pollia sambiranensis grows in humid, forested areas and requires specific environmental conditions to thrive.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in the biodiversity of Madagascar's flora.
