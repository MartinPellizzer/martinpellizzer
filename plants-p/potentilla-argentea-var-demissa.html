Potentilla argentea var. demissa

Potentilla argentea var. demissa, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance and help it reflect sunlight. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
