Plantago media subsp. pindica

Plantago media subsp.

pindica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It features upright, hairy stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a rough, waxy texture.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes, which are clustered at the top of the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in disturbed habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
