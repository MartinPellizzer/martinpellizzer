Plantago quillotae

Plantago quillotae, commonly known as the Quillotan plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, rocky soils along stream banks and in coastal areas. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, producing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
