Pterostylis precatoria

Pterostylis precatoria, commonly known as the Precarious Duck Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, duck-like flower shape, which is a key feature in its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and understoreys. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom during late spring to early summer.

The species is known for its specialized pollination mechanism, involving specific insect pollinators.
