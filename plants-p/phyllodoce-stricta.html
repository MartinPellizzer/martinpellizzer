Phyllodoce stricta

Phyllodoce stricta, commonly known as dwarf mountain heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils found in boreal and alpine regions. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are arranged in a dense, mat-like structure. It produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in early spring.

Phyllodoce stricta plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its ecosystem.
