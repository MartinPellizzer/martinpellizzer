Pinus montana var. centrapedunculata

Pinus montana var. centrapedunculata, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada white pine, is a variant of the white pine species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upward-curving cones and long, needle-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The tree can grow to heights of up to 60 meters, with a conical shape and a dense crown. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in mountainous regions with cool, moist climates.

This variety is valued for its timber and is also an important species in the ecological systems of its native habitat.
