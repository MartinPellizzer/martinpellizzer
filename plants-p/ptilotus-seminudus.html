Ptilotus seminudus

Ptilotus seminudus, commonly known as the naked false flax, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub with wiry stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant produces small, dry fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and can tolerate high temperatures and low rainfall.

Ptilotus seminudus is often found in coastal and inland dunes, contributing to the ecological stability of these environments.
