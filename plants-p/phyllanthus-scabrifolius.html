Phyllanthus scabrifolius

Phyllanthus scabrifolius, commonly known as the rough-leaved phyllanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its rough, serrated leaves and small yellow flowers that are clustered in cyathia, a unique floral structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
