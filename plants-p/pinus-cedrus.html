Pinus cedrus

Pinus cedrus, commonly known as the Cedars of Lebanon, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Eastern Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The needles are long, flat, and arranged in crossed pairs, giving the tree a distinctive appearance. It produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain edible seeds.

This species is valued for its timber and has cultural significance in ancient civilizations.
