Potentilla neumanniana

Potentilla neumanniana, commonly known as the Neumann's cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Potentilla neumanniana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
