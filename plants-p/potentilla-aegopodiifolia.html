Potentilla aegopodiifolia

Potentilla aegopodiifolia, commonly known as the fernleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate, lacey appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
