Phyllanthus quadrangularis

Phyllanthus quadrangularis, commonly known as the Indian date palm, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits that resemble dates in appearance. The fruit contains a single seed surrounded by a fleshy, edible pulp.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
