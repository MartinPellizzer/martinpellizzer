Pyrus korshinskyi var. media

Pyrus korshinskyi var. media, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in the spring, followed by white to yellow fruits that are edible. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit production in suitable environments.
