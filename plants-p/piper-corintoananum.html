Piper corintoananum

Piper corintoananum, commonly known as the Malabar pepper, is a climbing vine native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by round, peppery berries. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to black pepper.
