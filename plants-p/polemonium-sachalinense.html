Polemonium sachalinense

Polemonium sachalinense, commonly known as the Sakhalin bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the family Polemoniaceae and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branching.

Polemonium sachalinense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
