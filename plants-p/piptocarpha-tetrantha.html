Piptocarpha tetrantha

Piptocarpha tetrantha, commonly known as the four-branch alkali sacaton, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in saline and arid environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It plays an important role in stabilizing soils in arid regions.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to harsh conditions, contributing to its ecological significance.
