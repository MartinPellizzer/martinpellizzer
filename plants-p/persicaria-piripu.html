Persicaria piripu

Persicaria piripu, commonly known as redroot smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist soils along stream banks and wetlands, preferring full sun to partial shade. The plant features upright stems that are reddish-purple in color, especially near the base, and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in dense clusters during the summer months.

Persicaria piripu is often found in disturbed sites and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
