Prunus cerasus var. bigarella

Prunus cerasus var. bigarella, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, single pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, sweet, yellow or orange fruits that are edible and often used in jams and desserts. Its foliage is dark green and becomes attractive in autumn, adding ornamental value to gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
