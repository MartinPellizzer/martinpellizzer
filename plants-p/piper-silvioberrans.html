Piper silvioberrans

Piper silvioberrans, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red when mature, attracting various bird species. It is often found in humid, tropical environments and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a problematic species in certain regions.
