Potentilla daucifolia var. caruifolia

Potentilla daucifolia var. caruifolia, commonly known as the white potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided leaves that resemble those of the carrot, giving it its botanical name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
