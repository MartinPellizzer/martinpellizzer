Phlox virginica

Phlox virginica, commonly known as wild phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to purple flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Phlox virginica is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
