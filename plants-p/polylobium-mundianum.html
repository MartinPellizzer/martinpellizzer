Polylobium mundianum

Polylobium mundianum, commonly known as the golden leaf fern, is a rare and unique plant species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow foliage, which contrasts vividly with its dark green stems. The plant has a complex root system that helps it anchor in the moist, nutrient-rich soil of its habitat. Its leaves are divided into multiple lobes, giving it a distinctive, almost lace-like appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and specific environmental requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
