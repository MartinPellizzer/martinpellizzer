Piper minahassae

Piper minahassae, commonly known as the Java pepper or Minahassa pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Indonesia and is characterized by its small, round, peppery fruits that are used in traditional cuisine. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and greenish-white.

The plant is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its potential medicinal properties.
