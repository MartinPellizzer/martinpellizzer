Philodendron cordifolium

Philodendron cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically deep green with a glossy texture. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to climb and anchor itself to supports in its natural habitat. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
