Passiflora tacsonioides

Passiflora tacsonioides, commonly known as the Tacsonioides passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Peru.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are often consumed by birds and mammals, aiding in seed dispersal. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and prominent veins.

Passiflora tacsonioides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
