Polypodium latipes lusus furcatum

Polypodium latipes lusus furcatum, commonly known as the forked polypody, is a fern species belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically bipinnate, with small, rounded pinnae. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced in sori located on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through both sexual and asexual means.

The forked polypody is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
