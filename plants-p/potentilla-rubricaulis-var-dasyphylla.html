Potentilla rubricaulis var. dasyphylla

Potentilla rubricaulis var. dasyphylla, commonly known as the red-stemmed potentilla, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its vibrant red stems that stand out against the surrounding vegetation. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in spherical inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
