Polygala serpens

Polygala serpens, commonly known as the serpent's tail, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, winding stems and delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, arranged in clusters. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
