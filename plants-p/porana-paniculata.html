Porana paniculata

Porana paniculata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Porana paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
