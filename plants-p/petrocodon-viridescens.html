Petrocodon viridescens

Petrocodon viridescens, commonly known as the green-flowered rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and bright green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments. Petrocodon viridescens is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and medicinal plants.

Its unique appearance and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
