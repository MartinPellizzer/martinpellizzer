Parsonsia elegans

Parsonsia elegans, commonly known as the elegant parson's flower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a red throat. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically consumed by birds and other animals. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Parsonsia elegans is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
