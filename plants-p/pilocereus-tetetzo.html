Pilocereus tetetzo

Pilocereus tetetzo, commonly known as the night blooming cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a strong, sweet scent.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
