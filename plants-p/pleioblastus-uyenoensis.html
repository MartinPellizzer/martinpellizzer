Pleioblastus uyenoensis

Pleioblastus uyenoensis, commonly known as the Uyeno bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often arching stems and fine, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually wind-pollinated.

Due to its delicate appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
