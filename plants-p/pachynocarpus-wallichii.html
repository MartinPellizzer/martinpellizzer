Pachynocarpus wallichii

Pachynocarpus wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan thimble cactus, is a unique succulent native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Pachynocarpus wallichii is well adapted to cold, rocky environments and is often found growing in alpine meadows.
