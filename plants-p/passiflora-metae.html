Passiflora metae

Passiflora metae, commonly known as the Mexican passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and twining.

Passiflora metae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
