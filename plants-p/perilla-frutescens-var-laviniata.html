Perilla frutescens var. laviniata

Perilla frutescens var. laviniata, commonly known as purple perilla, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple leaves, which are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, sharing traits with other members such as mint and basil. Its leaves are rich in essential oils and contain various bioactive compounds.

Perilla frutescens var. laviniata thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and nutritional value.
