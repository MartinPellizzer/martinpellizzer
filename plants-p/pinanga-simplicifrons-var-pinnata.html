Pinanga simplicifrons var. pinnata

Pinanga simplicifrons var. pinnata, commonly known as the broad-leafed bamboo palm, is a tropical plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery, pinnately compound leaves.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's leaves are typically broad and elongated, contributing to its elegant appearance. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its air-purifying properties.

The varietal form, pinnata, distinguishes it by its more pronounced leaf structure compared to the typical form.
