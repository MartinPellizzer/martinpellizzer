Photinia esquirolii

Photinia esquirolii, commonly known as the Chinese firethorn, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, bright red berries that persist through winter. The plant produces showy, pink to white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the ornamental fruits. It is often cultivated for its winter interest and compact growth habit.

Photinia esquirolii prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
