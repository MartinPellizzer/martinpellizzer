Prunus ghahremanii

Prunus ghahremanii, commonly known as the Persian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional Persian cuisine. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters.

Prunus ghahremanii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
