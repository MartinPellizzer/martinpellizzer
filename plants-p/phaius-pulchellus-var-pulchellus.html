Phaius pulchellus var. pulchellus

Phaius pulchellus var. pulchellus, commonly known as the elegant iris, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking, showy flowers with three upright petals and a spurred lip, typically in shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant has a pseudobulbous structure with fleshy leaves that are sword-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires consistent humidity and well-draining soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or tropical gardens.
