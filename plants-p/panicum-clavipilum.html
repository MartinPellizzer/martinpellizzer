Panicum clavipilum

Panicum clavipilum, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields, roadsides, and coastal areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and the seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing the plant to spread easily.

Panicum clavipilum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and tolerate various environmental conditions.
