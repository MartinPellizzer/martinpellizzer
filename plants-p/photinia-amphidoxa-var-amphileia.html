Photinia amphidoxa var. amphileia

Photinia amphidoxa var. amphileia, commonly known as the redleaf photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red leaves that turn reddish-brown in the winter, providing seasonal visual interest. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that attract birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant foliage.
