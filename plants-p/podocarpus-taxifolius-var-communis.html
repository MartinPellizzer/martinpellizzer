Podocarpus taxifolius var. communis

Podocarpus taxifolius var. communis, commonly known as the common podocarp, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in a compact, needle-like fashion. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody cones containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
