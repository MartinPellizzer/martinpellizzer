Pyrus reducta

Pyrus reducta, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-white flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

The plant is also valued for its role in traditional Japanese horticulture.
