Prunus japonica f. thunbergii

Prunus japonica f. thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum or Thunberg's cherry plum, is a compact, deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark grey bark and profuse clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are oval-shaped, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is often used in traditional Japanese landscapes.
