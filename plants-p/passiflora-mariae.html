Passiflora mariae

Passiflora mariae, commonly known as the Marie's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with red filaments that resemble a crown. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Passiflora mariae is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
