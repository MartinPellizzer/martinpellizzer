Pulsatilla occidentalis

Pulsatilla occidentalis, commonly known as the western pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces a distinctive, hairy seed head that matures in late summer.

Pulsatilla occidentalis is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
