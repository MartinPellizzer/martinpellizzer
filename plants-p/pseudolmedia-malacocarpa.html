Pseudolmedia malacocarpa

Pseudolmedia malacocarpa, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and wetland restoration projects.
