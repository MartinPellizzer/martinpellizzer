Pleurothallis imitor

Pleurothallis imitor, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often resemble other orchid species, a trait that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by attracting specific insects.

Pleurothallis imitor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
