Phagnalon abyssinicum

Phagnalon abyssinicum, commonly known as Abyssinian phagnalon, is a flowering plant species native to the high altitudes of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers, which are usually blue or purple, attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
