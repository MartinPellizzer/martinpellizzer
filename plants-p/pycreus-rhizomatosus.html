Pycreus rhizomatosus

Pycreus rhizomatosus, commonly known as the creeping rhizome plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread horizontally through underground stems. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fibrous roots make it suitable for erosion control in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is often used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
