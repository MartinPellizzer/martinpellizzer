Polygonum hissaricum

Polygonum hissaricum, commonly known as the Hissar knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks. The plant features upright stems that are usually reddish or purple in color, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its roots are fibrous and can spread extensively.

Polygonum hissaricum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
