Persicaria elatior

Persicaria elatior, commonly known as tall polygonum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and flowering structures. The plant features upright, branched stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, supported by fibrous roots. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and often have a reddish or purple tint, while its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense spikes.

Persicaria elatior is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vertical structure and seasonal color.
