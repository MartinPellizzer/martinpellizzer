Phoradendron roseanum

Phoradendron roseanum, commonly known as the roseleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees, primarily in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which is typically glossy and dark green, and its small, white or pinkish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Its life cycle involves a complex relationship with its host, as it derives some of its nutrients from the host tree while also providing habitat for various insects and birds.

The plant is also notable for its ability to survive in shaded environments, making it a common sight in forest canopies.
