Poa araratica subsp. ianthina

Poa araratica subsp.

ianthina, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America. It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted growth habit.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to blue-gray florets.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
