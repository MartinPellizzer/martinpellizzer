Platorheedia arborea

Platorheedia arborea, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, characterized by its broad, palmately lobed leaves that turn silver-white in the autumn. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
