Panicum sanguinale var. filiforme

Panicum sanguinale var. filiforme, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves and clusters of red or pink bracts that give it its striking appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its vibrant color and delicate foliage.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
