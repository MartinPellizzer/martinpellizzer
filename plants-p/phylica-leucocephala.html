Phylica leucocephala

Phylica leucocephala, commonly known as the white-headed resthoek, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive white, fluffy flower heads. The plant produces small, tough leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to dry conditions.

Phylica leucocephala is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
