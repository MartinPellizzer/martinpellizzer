Panicum multiflorum

Panicum multiflorum, commonly known as crimson lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with dense clusters of red to purple flowers. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that mature into fluffy, seed-filled spikelets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and erosion control.
