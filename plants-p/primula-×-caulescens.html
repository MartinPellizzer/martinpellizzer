Primula × caulescens

Primula × caulescens, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 15–30 cm and features upright, hairy stems with lance-shaped leaves.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
