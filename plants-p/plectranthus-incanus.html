Plectranthus incanus

Plectranthus incanus, commonly known as the hairy plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus incanus is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
