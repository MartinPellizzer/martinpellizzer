Polygonatum majale

Polygonatum majale, commonly known as the Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its arching stems and clusters of small, white or greenish-white flowers. The plant produces berries that are edible but typically not consumed by humans. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
