Panicum palackyanum

Panicum palackyanum, commonly known as the Palackyanum grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, particularly found in countries like India and Sri Lanka. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in disturbed environments.
