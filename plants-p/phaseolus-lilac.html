Phaseolus lilac

Phaseolus lilac, commonly known as the lilac bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, which are used for both culinary and agricultural purposes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
