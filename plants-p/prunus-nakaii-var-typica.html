Prunus nakaii var. typica

Prunus nakaii var. typica, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Japanese flowering cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often preceding the emergence of leaves. The plant has elliptical leaves that are dark green and turn yellow in the fall. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Prunus nakaii var. typica is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic floral display.
