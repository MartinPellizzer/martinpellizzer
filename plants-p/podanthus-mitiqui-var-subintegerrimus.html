Podanthus mitiqui var. subintegerrimus

Podanthus mitiqui var. subintegerrimus, commonly known as the Andean starflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its star-shaped, bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, showy blooms.
