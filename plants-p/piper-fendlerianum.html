Piper fendlerianum

Piper fendlerianum, commonly known as the Fendler's pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is well-suited for tropical and subtropical climates.
