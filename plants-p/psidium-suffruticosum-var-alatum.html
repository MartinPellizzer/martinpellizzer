Psidium suffruticosum var. alatum

Psidium suffruticosum var. alatum, commonly known as the silverleaf guava, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, silver-tomentose leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by edible, yellowish-orange fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and its role in ecological restoration projects.
