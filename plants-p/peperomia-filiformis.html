Peperomia filiformis

Peperomia filiformis, commonly known as the thread-leaf peperomia, is a compact, trailing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, elongated, waxy leaves that give it a unique, almost spider-like appearance. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate watering.

Due to its trailing habit, it is often used as a hanging plant or in containers.
