Potentilla franconica

Potentilla franconica, commonly known as the Franconian cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal leaves with five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
