Panicum digitarioides

Panicum digitarioides, commonly known as the digitate panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stalks and small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are wind-pollinated.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
