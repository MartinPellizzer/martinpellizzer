Piper vexans

Piper vexans, commonly known as the devil's weed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 3,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

The plant has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, though its toxicity requires caution in use.
