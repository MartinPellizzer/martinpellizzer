Pinus californiarum

Pinus californiarum, commonly known as the Coulter pine, is a tall evergreen conifer native to California and southern Oregon.

It is characterized by its long, slender needles and distinctive, thick, woody cones. The tree can reach heights of up to 60 meters, making it one of the tallest pine species in North America. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against fire and drought.

The Coulter pine plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a variety of wildlife.
