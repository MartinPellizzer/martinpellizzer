Passiflora pseudociliata

Passiflora pseudociliata, commonly known as the Ciliata Passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough, hairy texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Passiflora pseudociliata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming effects.
