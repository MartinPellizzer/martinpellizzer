Phegopteris phegopteris

Phegopteris phegopteris, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are covered in a dense layer of silvery hairs, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Phegopteris phegopteris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
