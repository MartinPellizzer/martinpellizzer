Porroglossum amethystinum

Porroglossum amethystinum, commonly known as the amethystine porroglossum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight hairy texture.

The flowers are arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family, and are pollinated primarily by insects.
