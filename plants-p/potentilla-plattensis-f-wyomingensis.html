Potentilla plattensis f. wyomingensis

Potentilla plattensis f. wyomingensis, commonly known as the Wyoming potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to the arid environments it inhabits.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky or sandy soils.
