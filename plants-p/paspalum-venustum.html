Paspalum venustum

Paspalum venustum, commonly known as the velvet paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Paspalum venustum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
