Primula × arctotis

Primula × arctotis, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a perennial flowering plant that combines traits from both Primula and Arctotis species.

It typically features showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which are arranged in dense clusters. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The hybrid is often cultivated for garden use due to its hardiness and vibrant floral display.
