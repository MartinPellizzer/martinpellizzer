Phyllanthus koniamboensis var. taomensis

Phyllanthus koniamboensis var. taomensis, commonly known as the Madagascar gooseberry, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are small, round berries that mature to a red or orange color.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
