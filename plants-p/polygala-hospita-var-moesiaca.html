Polygala hospita var. moesiaca

Polygala hospita var. moesiaca, commonly known as the Moesian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, delicate flowers that are often pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, bead-like fruits that contain the seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
