Pineda incana

Pineda incana, commonly known as the hairy pineda, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Pineda incana is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
