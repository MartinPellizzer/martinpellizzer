Potentilla hippiana subsp. effusa

Potentilla hippiana subsp.

effusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features compound leaves with five to seven elliptic leaflets, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to other Potentilla species.
