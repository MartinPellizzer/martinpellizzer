Paphiopedilum christensonianum

Paphiopedilum christensonianum, commonly known as the Christenson's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to China.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb and a single, showy flower that typically blooms in late spring. The plant has a dark green, leathery leaf and a unique, spurred lip that is characteristic of the Paphiopedilum genus. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its elegant, long-lasting blooms.
