Pourouma hirsutipetiolata subsp.

hirsutipetiolata, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed areca palm, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America. It is a tall, robust palm characterized by its large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length.

The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of the Arecaceae family. Its stems are covered in dense, hairy foliage, giving it a distinctive appearance.
