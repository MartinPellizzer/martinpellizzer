Psyllium arborescens subsp. maderensis

Psyllium arborescens subsp.

maderensis, commonly known as the Mader's psyllium, is a flowering plant native to Mexico. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are clustered in racemes. It is notable for its fibrous seed capsules, which contain tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This subspecies is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its potential medicinal and agricultural uses.
