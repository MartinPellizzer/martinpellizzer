Pocophorum

Pocophorum, commonly known as the water spinach or kangkong, is a tropical aquatic plant characterized by its soft, fibrous stems and arrow-shaped leaves.

It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments and is often found in ponds, marshes, and along the edges of rivers. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, followed by small, round fruits containing seeds. Pocophorum is known for its rapid growth rate, which makes it both a valuable vegetable in some cultures and a potential invasive species in others.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to wetland habitats and contribute to its ecological significance.
