Pulsatilla vernalis

Pulsatilla vernalis, commonly known as the spring pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant produces follicles that contain the seeds.

Pulsatilla vernalis is known for its early blooming habit, often appearing in late spring.
