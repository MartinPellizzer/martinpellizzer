Pulsatilla montana subsp. balcana

Pulsatilla montana subsp.

balcana, commonly known as the Balkan pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its showy, showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils and prefers well-drained environments. It has a basal rosette of hairy, lobed leaves that help it retain moisture.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as meadows and forest edges.
