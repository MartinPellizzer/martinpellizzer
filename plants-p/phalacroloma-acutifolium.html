Phalacroloma acutifolium

Phalacroloma acutifolium, commonly known as the sharp-leaved phalacroloma, is a species of flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
