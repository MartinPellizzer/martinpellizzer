Pancovia pedicellaris

Pancovia pedicellaris, commonly known as the slender-stemmed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and violet. The leaves are lanceolate, with a rough texture and a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
