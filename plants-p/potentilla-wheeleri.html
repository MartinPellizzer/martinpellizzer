Potentilla wheeleri

Potentilla wheeleri, commonly known as Wheeler's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically features wiry stems and trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and alpine habitats.
