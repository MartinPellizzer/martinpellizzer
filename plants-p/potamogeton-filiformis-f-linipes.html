Potamogeton filiformis f. linipes

Potamogeton filiformis f. linipes, commonly known as the slender thread-stemmed pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its long, thin, ribbon-like leaves.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and streams. The plant has a branched stem system and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged. Its leaves are often tightly spiraled and can be mistaken for algae due to their appearance.

This plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
