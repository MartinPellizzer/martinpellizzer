Polygala murbeckii

Polygala murbeckii, commonly known as the mountain polygala, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family, characterized by its unique and often intricate floral structures. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a variety of colors including white, pink, and purple.

Polygala murbeckii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
