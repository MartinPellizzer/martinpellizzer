Pinalia curranii

Pinalia curranii, commonly known as the Curran's pinyon, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Pinaceae family, which includes other conifers such as pines and firs. The plant is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure, typical of coniferous trees. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Pinalia curranii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species in its native range.
