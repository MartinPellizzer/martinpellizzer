Polhillia canescens

Polhillia canescens, commonly known as the woolly polhillia, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
