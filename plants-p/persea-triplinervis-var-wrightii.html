Persea triplinervis var. wrightii

Persea triplinervis var. wrightii, commonly known as the Wright's persimmon, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with glossy, ovate leaves that have three prominent veins on each side. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, orange to red berries in the fall. The fruit is often used in local cuisines and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sun.
