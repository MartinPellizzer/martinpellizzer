Pelargonium × rutilum

Pelargonium × rutilum, commonly known as the variegated geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, which often features combinations of green, white, and pink hues. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a favored ornamental plant in both indoor and outdoor settings.
