Pyrus dolichofoliolata

Pyrus dolichofoliolata, commonly known as the long-leaf pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The fruit is a pear-like apple, typically yellow or yellow-green when ripe.

It is native to parts of East Asia, including China and Korea, and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
