Paraprasophyllum canaliculatum

Paraprasophyllum canaliculatum, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed grass of the genus Paraprasophyllum, is a perennial plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, ribbed leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowering stems bear small, clustered inflorescences, which are often inconspicuous.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
