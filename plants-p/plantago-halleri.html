Plantago halleri

Plantago halleri, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and typically grows in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The plant is also valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
