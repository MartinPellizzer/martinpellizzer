Physalis longifolia

Physalis longifolia, commonly known as the longifoliate groundcherry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its hairy, egg-shaped fruits that enclose the seeds. The plant typically grows in dry, open areas and has small, alternate leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are yellow with five petals and are followed by the distinctive papery calyx that persists on the fruit.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
