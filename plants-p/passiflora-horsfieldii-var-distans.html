Passiflora horsfieldii var. distans

Passiflora horsfieldii var. distans, commonly known as the Horsfield's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, sometimes unpleasant aroma.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
