Paspalum canarae

Paspalum canarae, commonly known as the Canary grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, two-ranked spikelets.

Paspalum canarae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its adaptability and low maintenance requirements.
