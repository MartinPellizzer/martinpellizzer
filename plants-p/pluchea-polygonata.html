Pluchea polygonata

Pluchea polygonata, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small yellow flowers are clustered in heads, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Pluchea polygonata is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.
