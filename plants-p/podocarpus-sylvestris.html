Podocarpus sylvestris

Podocarpus sylvestris, commonly known as the common podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It has a straight, cylindrical trunk with a smooth, dark grey bark. Its needle-like leaves are arranged in opposite pairs and are dark green in color. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping and reforestation projects.
