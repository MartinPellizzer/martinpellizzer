Picridium hispanicum

Picridium hispanicum, commonly known as the Spanish picrid, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in Mediterranean climates. Its small, yellow flowers are followed by elongated seed pods that split open to release the seeds.

Picridium hispanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
