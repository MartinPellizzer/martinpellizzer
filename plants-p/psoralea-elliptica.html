Psoralea elliptica

Psoralea elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, often appearing in the summer months. It is typically found in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Psoralea elliptica is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
