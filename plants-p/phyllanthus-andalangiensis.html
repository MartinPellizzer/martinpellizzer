Phyllanthus andalangiensis

Phyllanthus andalangiensis, commonly known as the Andalangani phyllanthus, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as savannas and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
