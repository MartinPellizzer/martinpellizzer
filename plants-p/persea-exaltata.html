Persea exaltata

Persea exaltata, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by orange to reddish fruit that ripens in the fall. The fruit has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor and is edible when fully mature.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
