Pachyrhizus articulatus

Pachyrhizus articulatus, commonly known as the artichoke plant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, fleshy, spiny leaves that form a dense, leafy structure. The plant produces purple or violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its edible part is the fleshy base of the leaves, which is harvested before the flowers open.

Pachyrhizus articulatus is often cultivated for its culinary value and ornamental appeal.
