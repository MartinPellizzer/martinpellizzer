Parasenecio maximowiczianus

Parasenecio maximowiczianus, commonly known as the Mongolian thistle, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features. It has hairy stems and leaves that help reduce water loss.

Its ecological role includes providing food for local wildlife and stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
