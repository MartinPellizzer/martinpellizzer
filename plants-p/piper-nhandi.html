Piper nhandi

Piper nhandi, commonly known as the African pepper or nhandi pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing a spicy, peppery substance used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for both its culinary and therapeutic uses.
