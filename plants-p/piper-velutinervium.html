Piper velutinervium

Piper velutinervium, commonly known as the velvet-veined pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly found in countries like Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that have a velvety texture and prominent veins. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical fruits.

Piper velutinervium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
