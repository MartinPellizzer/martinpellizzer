Physalis stramoniifolia

Physalis stramoniifolia, commonly known as the common groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that develop into bright orange, lantern-shaped fruits. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in open fields and along roadsides. Its fruits are edible and have a tangy, sweet flavor, often used in jams and desserts.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits to pollinators.
