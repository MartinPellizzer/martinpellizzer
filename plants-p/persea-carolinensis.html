Persea carolinensis

Persea carolinensis, commonly known as the avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It produces a large, pear-shaped fruit that is rich in healthy fats and nutrients. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers. The fruit's pericarp, or outer layer, is fibrous and separates from the inner seed.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lauraceae family and is valued for its economic and culinary importance.
