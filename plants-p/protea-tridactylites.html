Protea tridactylites

Protea tridactylites, commonly known as the three-toed protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous petal-like bracts that surround the small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in nutrient-poor soils, often in fynbos vegetation, and is adapted to fire-prone environments. Its flowering period usually occurs in late winter to early spring, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens throughout the region.
