Pternandra gracilis

Pternandra gracilis, commonly known as the slender pternandra, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge.

Pternandra gracilis is often found growing in sandy or rocky soils along the coastline.
