Potamogeton pusillus var. vulgaris

Potamogeton pusillus var. vulgaris, commonly known as the small pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments.

It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in width, arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are often clustered in spike-like structures. It thrives in shallow, nutrient-rich waters and is often found in ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
