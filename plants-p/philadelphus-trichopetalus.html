Philadelphus trichopetalus

Philadelphus trichopetalus, commonly known as the Chinese mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and it produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its showy, clustered blooms that resemble those of true orange blossoms. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both landscape design and ecological habitats.
