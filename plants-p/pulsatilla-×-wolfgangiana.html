Pulsatilla × wolfgangiana

Pulsatilla × wolfgangiana, commonly known as the hybrid pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Pulsatilla vulgaris and Pulsatilla patens, characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine and subalpine regions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces follicles that contain numerous seeds.

Pulsatilla × wolfgangiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
