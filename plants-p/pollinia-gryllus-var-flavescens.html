Pollinia gryllus var. flavescens

Pollinia gryllus var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a variant of the genus Pollinia, characterized by its distinctive yellowish-orange coloration.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia. The plant produces specialized structures called pollinia, which are masses of pollen that adhere to the insect vectors for pollination. Its flowers are typically showy and fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
