Pulmonaria virginica

Pulmonaria virginica, commonly known as the Virginia lungwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, velvety foliage with a distinctive spotted pattern, which is more pronounced in the spring. The plant produces upright spikes of bell-shaped, pinkish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Pulmonaria virginica is also noted for its historical use in traditional medicine.
