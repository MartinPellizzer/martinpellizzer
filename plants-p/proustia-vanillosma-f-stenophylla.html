Proustia vanillosma f. stenophylla

Proustia vanillosma f. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf form of the vanilla orchid, is a rare botanical variant characterized by its slender, elongated leaves.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that are typically pollinated by specific bee species. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest in botanical research.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
