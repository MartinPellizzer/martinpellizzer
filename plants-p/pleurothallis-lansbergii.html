Pleurothallis lansbergii

Pleurothallis lansbergii, commonly known as the Lanberg's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate floral structure.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often difficult to cultivate outside its natural range.
