Pladaroxylon leucadendron

Pladaroxylon leucadendron, commonly known as the white thorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, spiny foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in heads, which are often visited by insects. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a leathery texture.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
