Poa compressa var. sylvestris

Poa compressa var. sylvestris, commonly known as the woodland bluegrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically forms dense, mat-like turf and has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its fine texture and ability to stabilize soil make it ecologically valuable in natural habitats.
