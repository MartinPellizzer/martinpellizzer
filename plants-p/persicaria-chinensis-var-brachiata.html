Persicaria chinensis var. brachiata

Persicaria chinensis var. brachiata, commonly known as the Chinese knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red, which appear in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively make it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
