Parodia cebilarensis

Parodia cebilarensis, commonly known as the Cebilaresis prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a "tuna," is edible and has a sweet, juicy pulp.

Parodia cebilarensis is well adapted to harsh desert environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.
