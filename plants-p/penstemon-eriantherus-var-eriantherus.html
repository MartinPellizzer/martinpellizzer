Penstemon eriantherus var. eriantherus

Penstemon eriantherus var. eriantherus, commonly known as the tall beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features upright, branched stems that can grow up to 2 to 3 meters in height. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native gardens.
