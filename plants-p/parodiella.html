Parodiella

Parodiella, commonly known as the Parodiella orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to South America, particularly Brazil, and are characterized by their delicate, often fragrant blooms. Parodiella species typically have narrow, grass-like leaves and produce small, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. They are epiphytic, meaning they grow on the surfaces of trees and rocks rather than in soil, and require high humidity and indirect light to thrive.

Due to their unique appearance and limited distribution, Parodiella orchids are valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
