Papyrus corymbosus

Papyrus corymbosus, commonly known as the giant papyrus, is a tall, aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Africa.

It features long, narrow leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length, forming a dense canopy over the water. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers at the top of its stems, which bloom in the summer months. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, allowing it to anchor itself in muddy substrates.

Papyrus corymbosus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
