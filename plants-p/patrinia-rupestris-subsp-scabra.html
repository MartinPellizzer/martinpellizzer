Patrinia rupestris subsp. scabra

Patrinia rupestris subsp.

scabra, commonly known as rough Patrinia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of Asia, including China and Korea. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in mountainous areas.

The plant features wiry stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
