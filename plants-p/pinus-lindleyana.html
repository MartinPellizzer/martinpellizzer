Pinus lindleyana

Pinus lindleyana, commonly known as the Lindley's pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, slender needles that grow in bundles of five, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, making it a valuable component of its native ecosystem.
