Puya harmsii

Puya harmsii, commonly known as the Chilean torchflower, is a striking plant native to the Andes mountains of Chile.

It is a member of the bromeliad family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that resembles a torch. The plant produces vibrant red bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. Puya harmsii is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
