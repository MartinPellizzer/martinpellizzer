Popowia diospyrifolia

Popowia diospyrifolia, commonly known as the starfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by the distinctive star-shaped fruits. The fruit, which is edible, has a sweet and tangy flavor with a unique, waxy rind.

Popowia diospyrifolia is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its fruit, which is used in various culinary applications.
