Physalis sulphurea

Physalis sulphurea, commonly known as the goldenberry or the Cape gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its bright yellow, lantern-shaped fruit that encloses the edible seeds. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and they bloom in clusters.

The fruit is rich in vitamin C and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
