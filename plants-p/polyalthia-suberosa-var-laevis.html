Polyalthia suberosa var. laevis

Polyalthia suberosa var. laevis, commonly known as the smooth polyalthia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses.
