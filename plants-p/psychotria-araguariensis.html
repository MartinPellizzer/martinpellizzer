Psychotria araguariensis

Psychotria araguariensis, commonly known as ayahuasca, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its small, white, tubular flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant contains psychoactive alkaloids, including dimethyltryptamine (DMT), which contribute to its traditional use in shamanic rituals. It is typically grown in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its role in both cultural practices and ethnobotanical research.
