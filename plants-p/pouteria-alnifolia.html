Pouteria alnifolia

Pouteria alnifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The tree's most notable feature is its fruit, which contains the edible seeds known as Brazil nuts. Pouteria alnifolia thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires a warm climate with high rainfall.

It plays an important ecological role in the rainforest, providing food for various wildlife species.
