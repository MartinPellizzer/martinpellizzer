Phyllostachys hirtivagina var. glabrivagina

Phyllostachys hirtivagina var. glabrivagina, commonly known as the glossy bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo species characterized by its smooth, glabrous sheaths.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is native to eastern Asia, particularly China. The plant is known for its tall, slender culms that can reach several meters in height, making it a striking ornamental plant. Its leaves are narrow and often have a glossy appearance, contributing to its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.

This variety is prized for its resilience and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
