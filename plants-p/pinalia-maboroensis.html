Pinalia maboroensis

Pinalia maboroensis, commonly known as the Maboro pine, is a unique coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and conical shape, which help it withstand strong winds and cold temperatures. The tree produces small, reddish-brown cones that mature over several years, releasing seeds that are dispersed by wind. Pinalia maboroensis plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing habitat for various wildlife species and contributing to soil stabilization.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
