Poa × intricata

Poa × intricata, commonly known as the hybrid bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Poa annua and Poa trivialis, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant forms dense, mat-like growth with slender, blue-green leaves that are often used in turfgrass applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form a uniform lawn surface.
