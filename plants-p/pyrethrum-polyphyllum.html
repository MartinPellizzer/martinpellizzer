Pyrethrum polyphyllum

Pyrethrum polyphyllum, commonly known as the multileaf pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is known for producing pyrethrin, a natural insecticide used in organic pest control. Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and long-lasting blooms, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Pyrethrum polyphyllum thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
