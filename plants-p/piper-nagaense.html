Piper nagaense

Piper nagaense, commonly known as the red pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, reddish berries that contain a spicy compound similar to capsaicin. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its use in traditional medicine.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy appearance.
