Pandanus pristis

Pandanus pristis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces inflorescences that are often used in traditional crafts and ceremonies. Its fibrous leaves are also utilized for thatching and weaving.

Pandanus pristis thrives in coastal and mangrove environments, adapting well to salty and humid conditions.
