Polygonum graminifolium

Polygonum graminifolium, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. Polygonum graminifolium thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also used as a ornamental plant in gardens.
