Polystichum uliginosum

Polystichum uliginosum, commonly known as the bog fern, is a species of fern native to wet, acidic environments such as bogs and heathlands.

It has dark green, leathery fronds that are often densely packed, forming a dense mat on the forest floor. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is typically found in temperate regions. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it in the wet substrate, while its spores are dispersed by wind.

The bog fern plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for small organisms.
