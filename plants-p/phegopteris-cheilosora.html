Phegopteris cheilosora

Phegopteris cheilosora, commonly known as the broad holly fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that are deeply divided into lance-shaped segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

The botanical features of Phegopteris cheilosora make it a distinctive and ecologically significant plant in its native habitat.
