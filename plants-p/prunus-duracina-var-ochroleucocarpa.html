Prunus duracina var. ochroleucocarpa

Prunus duracina var. ochroleucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruit cherry, is a rare and ornamental species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, preceding the development of its distinctive yellowish-white fruits. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a pale green hue. The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is often sought after by botanists and horticulturists for its aesthetic and ecological value.
