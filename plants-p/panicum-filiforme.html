Panicum filiforme

Panicum filiforme, commonly known as slender panicgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, thread-like leaves and slender, upright growth habit. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Panicum filiforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
