Polygala brachyanthema

Polygala brachyanthema, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in open, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bead-like fruits that contain the seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
