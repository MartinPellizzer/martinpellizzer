Pleurothallis tonduzii

Pleurothallis tonduzii, commonly known as the Tonduz's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are epiphytic, allowing it to grow on tree trunks without being parasitic.

This orchid is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its ornamental qualities.
