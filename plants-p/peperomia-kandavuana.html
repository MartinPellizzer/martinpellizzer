Peperomia kandavuana

Peperomia kandavuana, commonly known as the Kandavu peperomia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and thick, waxy leaves that often display variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Peperomia kandavuana is relatively low maintenance, requiring minimal watering and moderate humidity levels.
