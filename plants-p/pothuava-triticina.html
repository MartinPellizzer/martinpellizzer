Pothuava triticina

Pothuava triticina, commonly known as the wheat rust fungus, is a significant plant pathogen that affects wheat crops.

It belongs to the fungal genus Puccinia and causes a type of rust disease known as stem rust. The fungus overwinters as a sexual stage on barberry plants, which serve as alternate hosts. It spreads through airborne spores, leading to severe yield losses in wheat fields.

Its impact on global food security underscores the importance of monitoring and managing this disease.
