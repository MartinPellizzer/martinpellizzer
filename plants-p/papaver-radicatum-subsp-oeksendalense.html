Papaver radicatum subsp. oeksendalense

Papaver radicatum subsp.

oeksendalense, commonly known as the Oeksendalen poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with a prominent white center, which bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open grasslands and scrub habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
