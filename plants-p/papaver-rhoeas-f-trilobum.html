Papaver rhoeas f. trilobum

Papaver rhoeas f. trilobum, commonly known as the triple-lobed poppy, is a botanical variant of the common poppy.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed petals, which set it apart from the typical single-lobed form. This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value, showcasing vibrant red or orange flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, similar to its parent species.

The plant is also notable for its historical use in medicine and as a source of opium.
