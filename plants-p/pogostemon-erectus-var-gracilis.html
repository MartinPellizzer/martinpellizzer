Pogostemon erectus var. gracilis

Pogostemon erectus var. gracilis, commonly known as slender pogostemon, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or blue, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional herbal use.
