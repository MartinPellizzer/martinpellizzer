Prunus imanishii

Prunus imanishii, commonly known as the Imanishi cherry, is a species of flowering shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, dark red fruits. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

Prunus imanishii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blossoms.
