Petrophile teretifolia

Petrophile teretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or heathland environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds.

Petrophile teretifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
