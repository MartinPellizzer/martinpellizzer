Phlomidoschema parviflorum

Phlomidoschema parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered phlomis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens with Mediterranean climates.
