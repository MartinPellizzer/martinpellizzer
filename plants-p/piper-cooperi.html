Piper cooperi

Piper cooperi, commonly known as the yellow horned poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which is characterized by its unique, often aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves with a distinct yellowish-green coloration. Its flowers are tiny, greenish-yellow, and arranged in dense clusters, typical of the genus Piper.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often found in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in arid regions.
