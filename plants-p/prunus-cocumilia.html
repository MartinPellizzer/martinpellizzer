Prunus cocumilia

Prunus cocumilia, commonly known as the dwarf cherry, is a small shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It produces small, sweet, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval-shaped with a smooth surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
