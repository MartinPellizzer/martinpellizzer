Pittosporum megacarpum

Pittosporum megacarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of dark green, leathery leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that are followed by large, orange-red berries. These berries are edible for birds but toxic to humans and livestock.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
