Pleiospilos compactus subsp. canus

Pleiospilos compactus subsp.

canus, commonly known as the white-stemmed oryx plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
