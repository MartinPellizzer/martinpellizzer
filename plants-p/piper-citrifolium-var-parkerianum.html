Piper citrifolium var. parkerianum

Piper citrifolium var. parkerianum, commonly known as the Parker's citron pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often yellow-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow-green fruits that turn orange when mature, resembling tiny citrons. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is part of the Piper genus, which includes many pepper plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses.
