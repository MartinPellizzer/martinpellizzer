Phymatodes arguta

Phymatodes arguta, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and basal rosette of large, waxy leaves that help it conserve water. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, spiny fruits that disperse seeds by wind. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to desert conditions, though it can become invasive in some areas.
