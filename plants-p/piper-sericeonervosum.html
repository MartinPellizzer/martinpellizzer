Piper sericeonervosum

Piper sericeonervosum, commonly known as the silky-veined pepper, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 2,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its finely pubescent leaves with silky, hair-like veins. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, berry-like fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
