Phyllanthus philippinensis

Phyllanthus philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly the Philippines.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, tropical environments, often found in lowland areas and along riverbanks. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a rough, textured surface and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that contains several small, hard seeds.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
