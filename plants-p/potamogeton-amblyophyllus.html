Potamogeton amblyophyllus

Potamogeton amblyophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically less than 1 mm wide, arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually found in clusters near the water's surface. It plays an important role in aquatic habitats by providing food and shelter for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in lakes and slow-moving streams.
