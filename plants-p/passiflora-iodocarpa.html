Passiflora iodocarpa

Passiflora iodocarpa, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a blend of purple, blue, and white petals, along with a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients and have a tangy flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Passiflora iodocarpa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique floral display and its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
