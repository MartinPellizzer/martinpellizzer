Polystachya magnibracteata

Polystachya magnibracteata, commonly known as the giant bract polystachya, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces small, clustered orchid-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
