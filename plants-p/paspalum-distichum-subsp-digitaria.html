Paspalum distichum subsp. digitaria

Paspalum distichum subsp.

digitaria, commonly known as Digitaria ramosa, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in both wet and dry conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its use in forage and as a component in ecological restoration projects.
