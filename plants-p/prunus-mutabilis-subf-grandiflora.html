Prunus mutabilis subf. grandiflora

Prunus mutabilis subf.

grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its showy, white to pink flowers that change color as they bloom, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, edible fruits known as cherries, which are typically red or black when mature. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
