Pitcairnia jareckii

Pitcairnia jareckii, commonly known as the Jarecki's pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, a common adaptation among bromeliads. The plant produces striking, long-lasting inflorescences with bright red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are typically thick and waxy, which helps reduce water loss in its humid, shaded habitat.

Pitcairnia jareckii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
