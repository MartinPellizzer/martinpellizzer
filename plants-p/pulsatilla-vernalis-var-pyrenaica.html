Pulsatilla vernalis var. pyrenaica

Pulsatilla vernalis var. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, saucer-shaped flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant features a short, stout stem with palmate leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in some areas.
