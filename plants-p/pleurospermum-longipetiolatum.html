Pleurospermum longipetiolatum

Pleurospermum longipetiolatum, commonly known as the long-petioled pleurospermum, is a perennial herb native to China.

It features a basal rosette of gray-green, lance-shaped leaves with long petioles. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
