Perilla frutescens var. nankinensis

Perilla frutescens var. nankinensis, commonly known as Chinese basil or Shiso, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often purple or reddish on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round, black seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its spicy and minty flavor.
