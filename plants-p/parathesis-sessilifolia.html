Parathesis sessilifolia

Parathesis sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaf parathesis, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and sessile leaves that are attached directly to the stem without a stalk. Native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
