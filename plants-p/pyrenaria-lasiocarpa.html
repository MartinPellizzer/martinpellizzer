Pyrenaria lasiocarpa

Pyrenaria lasiocarpa, commonly known as the hairy-fruited pyrenaria, is a member of the Rutaceae family.

It is a small shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a drupe, covered in a fuzzy, hairy texture, which gives it its common name.

Pyrenaria lasiocarpa is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
