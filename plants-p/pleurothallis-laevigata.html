Pleurothallis laevigata

Pleurothallis laevigata, commonly known as the smooth-leaved orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually thick and fleshy, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Pleurothallis laevigata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
