Pogonia latipetala

Pogonia latipetala, commonly known as the broad-petaled bog orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the wetlands of southeastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple, with a distinctive pouch-like structure at the base of each blossom. The plant typically grows in acidic, boggy soils and requires specific environmental conditions to thrive. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it often forms dense clusters in its natural habitat.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, Pogonia latipetala is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
