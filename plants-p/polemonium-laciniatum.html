Polemonium laciniatum

Polemonium laciniatum, commonly known as the cutleaf Jacob's ladder, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features slender, branching stems that produce clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The leaves are finely divided, giving the plant a delicate, lace-like appearance. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in native plant gardens.
