Phyllocladus cunninghamii

Phyllocladus cunninghamii, commonly known as the mountain pine or the silver pine, is a unique evergreen shrub or small tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive phylloclades, which are flattened, leaf-like stems that perform the functions of leaves. The plant has a gnarled, twisted trunk and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats. Its cones are small and woody, and the species is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Phyllocladus cunninghamii plays an important ecological role in its native ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
