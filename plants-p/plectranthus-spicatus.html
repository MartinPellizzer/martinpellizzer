Plectranthus spicatus

Plectranthus spicatus, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features upright, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus spicatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic foliage.
