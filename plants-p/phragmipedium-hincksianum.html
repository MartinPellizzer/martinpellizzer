Phragmipedium hincksianum

Phragmipedium hincksianum, commonly known as the Hinck's slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, slipper-shaped pouch that serves as a specialized structure for trapping pollinators. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has long, strap-like leaves that help it thrive in humid conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow or green with a distinctive red or orange lip, and it produces a unique, fragrant scent to attract its pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
