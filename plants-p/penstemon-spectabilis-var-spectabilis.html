Penstemon spectabilis var. spectabilis

Penstemon spectabilis var. spectabilis, commonly known as the tall beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 to 4 feet in height. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
