Parahebe Formosa

Parahebe formosa is a striking evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of New Zealand. It is known for its vibrant blue-green foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings. Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a favorite among gardeners seeking both beauty and resilience. Parahebe formosa is not only ornamental but also plays a role in supporting local wildlife and ecosystems.
