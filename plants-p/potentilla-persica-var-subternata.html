Potentilla persica var. subternata

Potentilla persica var. subternata, commonly known as Persian rose or Persian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are alternate, pinnate, and often have a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
