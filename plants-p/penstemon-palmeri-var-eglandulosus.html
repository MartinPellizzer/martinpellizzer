Penstemon palmeri var. eglandulosus

Penstemon palmeri var. eglandulosus, commonly known as Palmer's beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
