Protea parvula

Protea parvula, commonly known as the small protea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its unique, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers are pollinated by birds, particularly sunbirds, which are attracted to its nectar.

Protea parvula is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
