Potentilla argentea collina

Potentilla argentea collina, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Potentilla argentea collina is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance.
