Passiflora pilosa subsp. dimidiata

Passiflora pilosa subsp.

dimidiata, commonly known as the hairy passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its hairy stems and large, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a distinctive crown of filaments.

The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
