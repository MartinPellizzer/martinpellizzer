Pandanus ambiglaucus

Pandanus ambiglaucus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its stiff, sword-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the base of the leaves. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in wet, swampy environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in various cultures.
