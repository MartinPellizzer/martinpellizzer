Platytaenia lasiocarpa subsp. thomsonii

Platytaenia lasiocarpa subsp.

thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's woolly thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has a rosette growth habit, with dense foliage that helps it retain moisture.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more robust stems and longer, woolly hairs compared to the typical form.
