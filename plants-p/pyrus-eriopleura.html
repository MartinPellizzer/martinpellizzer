Pyrus eriopleura

Pyrus eriopleura, commonly known as the woolly apple.

It is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of central Asia, including parts of China and the Himalayas. The plant is characterized by its hairy, grayish bark and small, oval-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, followed by yellow, edible fruits that are typically small and sour.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both its fruit and its ecological benefits.
