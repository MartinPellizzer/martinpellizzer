Pelargonium salamonce

Pelargonium salamonce, commonly known as the Salamonce geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Pelargonium salamonce is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
