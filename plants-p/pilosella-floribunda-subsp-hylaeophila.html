Pilosella floribunda subsp. hylaeophila

Pilosella floribunda subsp.

hylaeophila, commonly known as the Hylaeophila pilosella, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in composite flower heads. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to well-drained soils.
