Poa seredinii

Poa seredinii, commonly known as the Serediva bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender, often hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color, and it blooms during the summer months. Poa seredinii thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
