Pseuderanthemum parviflorum

Pseuderanthemum parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered false anthemum, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale pink and are borne on slender, branching stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
