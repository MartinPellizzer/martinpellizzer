Phyllocactus × pommer-eschei

Phyllocactus × pommer-eschei, commonly known as the hybrid phyllocactus, is a unique cactus species that combines traits from its parent plants.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a compact, bushy structure. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the warmer months, adding ornamental value. It is native to regions of South America, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid climates.

Due to its slow growth and specific care requirements, it is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
