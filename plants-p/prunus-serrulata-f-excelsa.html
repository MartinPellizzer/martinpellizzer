Prunus serrulata f. excelsa

Prunus serrulata f. excelsa, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or Yamazakura, is a variant of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with a broad, rounded crown.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and public parks, especially for its fleeting floral display.
