Pentaphragma philippinensis

Pentaphragma philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, typical of sedges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
