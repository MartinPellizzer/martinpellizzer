Peperomia pellucida var. pygmaea

Peperomia pellucida var. pygmaea, commonly known as the moss peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features dense, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it a moss-like appearance. The leaves are typically bright green, sometimes with a bluish or reddish tinge, and they are arranged in a rosette pattern. This variety is prized for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are rarely seen, with the plant's ornamental value primarily coming from its foliage.
