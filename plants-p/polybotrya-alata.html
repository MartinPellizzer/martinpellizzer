Polybotrya alata

Polybotrya alata, commonly known as the winged fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that have a wing-like appearance, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Polybotrya alata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
