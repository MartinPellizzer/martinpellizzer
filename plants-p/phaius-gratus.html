Phaius gratus

Phaius gratus, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features large, showy flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, with a distinctive three-lobed lip. Its leaves are broad and sword-shaped, often forming a rosette at the base.

Phaius gratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its vibrant blooms.
