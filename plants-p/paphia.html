Paphia

Paphia, commonly known as the orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to various regions, including Southeast Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. Paphia species are characterized by their showy, fragrant flowers that often feature intricate patterns and vibrant colors. They typically grow as epiphytes or terrestrials, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

The genus includes over 100 species, many of which are popular in horticulture for their ornamental value.
