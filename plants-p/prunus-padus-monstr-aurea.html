Prunus padus monstr. aurea

Prunus padus monstr.

aurea, commonly known as the golden-flowered wild cherry, is a rare and striking variant of the common bird cherry. It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which contrast beautifully with its dark green foliage.

The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of Europe. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.
