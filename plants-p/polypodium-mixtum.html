Polypodium mixtum

Polypodium mixtum, commonly known as the mixed fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that often grow in dense clusters. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found on tree trunks or rocks in the wild. Its rhizomes are creeping and help anchor it to its substrate while absorbing moisture and nutrients.

Polypodium mixtum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in controlled indoor conditions.
