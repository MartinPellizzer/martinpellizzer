Pluchea caspia

Pluchea caspia, commonly known as Caspian groundsel, is a herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a compact, low-growing structure. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a rough texture, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

Pluchea caspia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
