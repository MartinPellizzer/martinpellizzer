Picea glauca f. parva

Picea glauca f. parva, commonly known as the dwarf white spruce, is a compact variant of the white spruce tree.

It is characterized by its small, dense foliage and short, twisted branches, making it well-suited for ornamental gardening. The plant features needle-like leaves that are blue-gray in color, providing a striking visual contrast in landscapes. It thrives in cold climates and is often used in rock gardens or as a specimen plant.

Its botanical structure includes a conical shape with a slow growth rate, contributing to its unique appearance.
