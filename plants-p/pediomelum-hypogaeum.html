Pediomelum hypogaeum

Pediomelum hypogaeum, commonly known as the underground bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique growth habit, with its flowers developing underground and only emerging above ground when mature. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
