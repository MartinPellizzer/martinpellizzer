Polylychnis essequibensis

Polylychnis essequibensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian grass, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded habitats within its native range.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
