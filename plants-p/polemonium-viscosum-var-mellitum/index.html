Polemonium Viscosum Var. Mellitum

Showy bluebell, scientifically known as Polemonium viscosum var. mellitum, is a striking perennial wildflower native to the eastern United States. It is celebrated for its vibrant blue to purple bell-shaped blooms that appear in early spring. This plant thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is a key component of many forest ecosystems. Its showy flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to biodiversity. Despite its beauty, it is often overlooked in favor of more common garden plants.
