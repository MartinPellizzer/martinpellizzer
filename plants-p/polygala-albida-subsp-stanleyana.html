Polygala albida subsp. stanleyana

Polygala albida subsp.

stanleyana, commonly known as the Stanley's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa. It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in open woodland or grassland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and opposite, while the fruit is a small, hairy capsule that releases seeds when mature.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
