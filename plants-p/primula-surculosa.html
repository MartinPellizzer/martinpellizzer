Primula surculosa

Primula surculosa, commonly known as the Himalayan primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically features broad, hairy leaves and produces clusters of pink or white flowers in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and partial shade.
