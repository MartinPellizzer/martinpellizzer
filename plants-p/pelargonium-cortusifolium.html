Pelargonium cortusifolium

Pelargonium cortusifolium, commonly known as the creeping geranium, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically green with a slight purplish tinge. The plant produces clusters of five-petaled flowers in shades of pink or red, which bloom from spring to summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
