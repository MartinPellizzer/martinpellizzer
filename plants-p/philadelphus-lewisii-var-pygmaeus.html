Philadelphus lewisii var. pygmaeus

Philadelphus lewisii var. pygmaeus, commonly known as the dwarf Lewis's Philadelphus, is a compact shrub native to the western United States.

It features showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for small garden spaces. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and low maintenance requirements.
