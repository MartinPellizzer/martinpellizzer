Persicaria punctata var. robustior

Persicaria punctata var. robustior, commonly known as the robust marsh knotweed, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features erect, branched stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, often with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically arranged in spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is adapted to moist soils and can thrive in environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
