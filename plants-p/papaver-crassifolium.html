Papaver crassifolium

Papaver crassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved poppy, is a species of flowering plant in the family Papaveraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and the Tien Shan. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, with prominent black spots. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

Papaver crassifolium is typically found in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
