Phacelia minor var. campanularia

Phacelia minor var. campanularia, commonly known as the bell-shaped phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting pollinators.
