Pleurothallis ghillanyi

Pleurothallis ghillanyi, commonly known as the Guayaquil orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, narrow leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It is known for its intricate floral structure and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
