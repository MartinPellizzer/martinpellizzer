Phedimus florifer

Phedimus florifer, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features silver-gray, woolly foliage that reflects sunlight, helping to reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, showy yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a versatile addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
