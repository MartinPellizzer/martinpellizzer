Prunus discolor

Prunus discolor, commonly known as the variable cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its variable leaf coloration, ranging from green to red or purple, depending on the season and environmental conditions. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in spring, followed by edible, dark purple to black berries in summer. Prunus discolor thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
