Polystachya perrieri

Polystachya perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's bamboo orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its rhizomes are fibrous and contribute to its structural support.

Polystachya perrieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
