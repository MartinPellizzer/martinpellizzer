Pithecellobium tjendana

Pithecellobium tjendana, commonly known as the Tjendana burr legume, is a flowering plant native to Indonesia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces distinctive, woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. Its growth habit includes shrub-like forms, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The species is also valued for its potential in ecological restoration due to its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
