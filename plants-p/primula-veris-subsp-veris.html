Primula veris subsp. veris

Primula veris subsp.

veris, commonly known as the spring primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow and pink, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces a short, hairy flowering stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and meadows.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting early pollinators.
