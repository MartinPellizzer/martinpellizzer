Primula sinodenticulata

Primula sinodenticulata, commonly known as the Chinese primrose, is a species native to China and is part of the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with serrated edges, and the plant often forms dense mats. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or rocky areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
