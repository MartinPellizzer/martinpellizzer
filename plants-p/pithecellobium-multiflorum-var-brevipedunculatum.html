Pithecellobium multiflorum var. brevipedunculatum

Pithecellobium multiflorum var. brevipedunculatum, commonly known as the short-stalked crownflower, is a species native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is a small, deciduous shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release minute, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
