Porophyllum viridiflorum

Porophyllum viridiflorum, commonly known as greenflower fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Porophyllum viridiflorum is often found in arid environments and is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient soils.
