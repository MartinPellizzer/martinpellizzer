Phaseolus pubescens

Phaseolus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy runner bean, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces edible pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both agricultural and ecological studies.
