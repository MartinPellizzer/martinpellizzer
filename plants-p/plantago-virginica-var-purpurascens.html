Plantago virginica var. purpurascens

Plantago virginica var. purpurascens, commonly known as purple plantain, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist soils along stream banks and in wet meadows. The plant features a rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. Its flowering stems bear dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
