Paronychia condensata

Paronychia condensata, commonly known as the denseleaf sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with succulent, often hairy stems and leaves that are typically fleshy and arranged in a rosette. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its adaptations to dry environments include thick leaves that store water and a shallow root system for efficient moisture uptake.

Paronychia condensata is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to xerophytic habitats.
