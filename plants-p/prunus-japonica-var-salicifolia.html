Prunus japonica var. salicifolia

Prunus japonica var. salicifolia, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum with willow leaves, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are dark green and turn bright red in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its unique foliage and seasonal blooms make it a valued addition to landscape designs.
