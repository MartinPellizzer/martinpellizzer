Pycreus gratissimus

Pycreus gratissimus, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a distinct, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
