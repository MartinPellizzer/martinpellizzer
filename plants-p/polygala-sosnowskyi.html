Polygala sosnowskyi

Polygala sosnowskyi, commonly known as the Sosnowsky's hogweed, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, umbrella-like clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is known for its unique inflorescence structure. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is often protected in its native habitats.
