Paspalum texanum

Paspalum texanum, commonly known as Texas grass, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green blades and forms dense, low-growing turf. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for landscaping in arid regions. Its rhizomes allow it to spread and establish quickly, forming a mat-like growth.

Paspalum texanum is often used in erosion control and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature.
