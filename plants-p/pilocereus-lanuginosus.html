Pilocereus lanuginosus

Pilocereus lanuginosus, commonly known as the woolly night bloomer, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems covered in soft, white, woolly hairs. The plant produces large, fragrant, white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
