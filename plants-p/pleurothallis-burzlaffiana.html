Pleurothallis burzlaffiana

Pleurothallis burzlaffiana, commonly known as the Burzlaff's spider orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid, typically growing on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, slender stems and produces intricate, spider-like flowers with a unique coloration pattern. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink with a contrasting lip, and it blooms during the rainy season.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable and often protected species in its native range.
