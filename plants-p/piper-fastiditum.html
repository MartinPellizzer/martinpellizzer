Piper fastiditum

Piper fastiditum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its dark, peppercorn-like berries that are harvested for their pungent flavor. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit develops into a dark, glossy berry, which is the source of the spice black pepper.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
