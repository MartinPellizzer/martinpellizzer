Pseudolaelia regentii

Pseudolaelia regentii, commonly known as the Regent's orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically feature a combination of red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant produces intricate, spurred petals and a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by attracting specific insect pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
