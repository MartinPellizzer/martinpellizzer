Polygala pulchella

Polygala pulchella, commonly known as the pretty milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its small, compound leaves. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Polygala pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
