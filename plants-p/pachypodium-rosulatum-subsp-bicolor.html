Pachypodium rosulatum subsp. bicolor

Pachypodium rosulatum subsp.

bicolor, commonly known as the "Bicolored Pachypodium," is a succulent plant native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and sparse, spiny leaves.

The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its unique morphology allows it to store water efficiently, adapting to arid environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and resilience.
