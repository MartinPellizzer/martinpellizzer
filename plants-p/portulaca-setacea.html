Portulaca setacea

Portulaca setacea, commonly known as the hairy portulaca, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is a low-growing annual herb with succulent, fleshy leaves and vibrant flowers that bloom in shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant has a compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on the ground. Its flowers are typically short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.

Portulaca setacea is well adapted to hot, dry climates and requires minimal water once established.
