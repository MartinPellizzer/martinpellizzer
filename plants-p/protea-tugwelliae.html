Protea tugwelliae

Protea tugwelliae, commonly known as the Tugwell's protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers arranged in a spherical or cylindrical shape. The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. It is adapted to mild, Mediterranean climates with cool, wet winters and warm, dry summers.

Due to its ornamental value and habitat specificity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
