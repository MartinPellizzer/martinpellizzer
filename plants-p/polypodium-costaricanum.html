Polypodium costaricanum

Polypodium costaricanum, commonly known as the Costarican huperzia, is a species of fern native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Huperziaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

Polypodium costaricanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in tropical ecosystems.
