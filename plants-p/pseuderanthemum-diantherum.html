Pseuderanthemum diantherum

Pseuderanthemum diantherum, commonly known as the showy pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
