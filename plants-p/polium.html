Polium

Polium, commonly known as the common blueweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Polium species are often found in temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.

Its foliage is usually alternate and lance-shaped, with hairy surfaces that aid in water retention.
