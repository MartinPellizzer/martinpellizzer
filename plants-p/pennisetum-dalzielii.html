Pennisetum dalzielii

Pennisetum dalzielii, commonly known as the Dalziel's lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy seeds that are often used for ornamental purposes due to their delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens.

Pennisetum dalzielii is also valued for its ability to attract birds and insects, contributing to biodiversity in native landscapes.
