Penstemon speciosus subsp. typicus

Penstemon speciosus subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the showy penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant gardens.

Its lance-shaped leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
