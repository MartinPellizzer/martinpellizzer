Porochna brunnescens

Porochna brunnescens, commonly known as the brown porochna, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring upright stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough, textured surface and a distinctive brownish hue, giving the plant its common name. Its small, yellowish flowers appear in clusters during the summer months, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

The plant prefers well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.
