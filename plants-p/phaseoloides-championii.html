Phaseoloides championii

Phaseoloides championii, commonly known as the Brazilian bean vine, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
