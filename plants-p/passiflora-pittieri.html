Passiflora pittieri

Passiflora pittieri, commonly known as the Mexican passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
