Polygonum hydropiper var. hispidum

Polygonum hydropiper var. hispidum, commonly known as the hairy smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread via rhizomes make it both a useful wetland indicator plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
