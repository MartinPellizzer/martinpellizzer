Pelargonium appendiculatum

Pelargonium appendiculatum, commonly known as the trailing geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant features serrated, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season.

Pelargonium appendiculatum is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
