Pseudoliparis ramosii

Pseudoliparis ramosii, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, branching inflorescences that resemble coral, hence its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in a range of colors, including red, yellow, and orange. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas.

Pseudoliparis ramosii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in horticulture and landscape design.
