Pleurothallis crassilabia

Pleurothallis crassilabia, commonly known as the broad-lipped pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, tropical environments. The plant features slender, branching stems and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its leaves are usually dark green and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
