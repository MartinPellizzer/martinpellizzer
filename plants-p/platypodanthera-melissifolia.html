Platypodanthera melissifolia

Platypodanthera melissifolia, commonly known as the honeyleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the wet season. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in tropical climates.
