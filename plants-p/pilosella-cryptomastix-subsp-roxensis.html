Pilosella cryptomastix subsp. roxensis

Pilosella cryptomastix subsp.

roxensis, commonly known as the Roxen's hairy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant is typically found in dry grasslands and rocky slopes, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, while its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
