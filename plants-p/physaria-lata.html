Physaria lata

Physaria lata, commonly known as the broadleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

Physaria lata is often found in sandy or gravelly soils along dry slopes and in open habitats.
