Pithecellobium winzerlingii

Pithecellobium winzerlingii, commonly known as the Brazilian burr legume, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy, woody pods that split open when mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal uses.
