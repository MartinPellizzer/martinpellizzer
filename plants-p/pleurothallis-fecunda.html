Pleurothallis fecunda

Pleurothallis fecunda, commonly known as the golden pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small, yellow or greenish-yellow, and bloom in clusters.

Pleurothallis fecunda is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
