Plantago major var. ungavensis

Plantago major var. ungavensis, commonly known as the common plantain, is a variant of the broadleaf plantain native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette of broad, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in lawns, roadsides, and other urban environments.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
