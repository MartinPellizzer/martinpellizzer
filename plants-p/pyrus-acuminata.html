Pyrus acuminata

Pyrus acuminata, commonly known as the acuminata pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray-brown bark and alternate, serrated leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are followed by edible, sweet fruits that ripen in late summer. The fruit has a thin, russet-colored skin and is often used in culinary applications.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
