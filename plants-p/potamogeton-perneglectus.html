Potamogeton perneglectus

Potamogeton perneglectus, commonly known as narrowleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged or floating. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, rivers, and wetlands.

Potamogeton perneglectus plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
