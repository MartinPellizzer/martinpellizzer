Potentilla osterhoutii

Potentilla osterhoutii, commonly known as Osterhout's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It typically features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, wiry stems and pinnately compound leaves with five leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
