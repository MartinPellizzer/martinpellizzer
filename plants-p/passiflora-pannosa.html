Passiflora pannosa

Passiflora pannosa, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments that resemble a crown. The plant produces edible, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft texture.

Passiflora pannosa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display.
