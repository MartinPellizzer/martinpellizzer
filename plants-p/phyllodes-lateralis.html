Phyllodes lateralis

Phyllodes lateralis, commonly known as the slender phyllodinea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the genus Acacia and is characterized by its flattened, leaf-like structures called phyllodes. These phyllodes are typically narrow and linear, arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small yellow flowers in spikes, which are often followed by pod-like fruits.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.
