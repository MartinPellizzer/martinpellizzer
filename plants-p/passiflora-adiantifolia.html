Passiflora adiantifolia

Passiflora adiantifolia, commonly known as the laceleaf passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, feathery leaves that resemble lace, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a unique crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. Its fruit is a small, edible berry that turns from green to yellow as it ripens.

Passiflora adiantifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
