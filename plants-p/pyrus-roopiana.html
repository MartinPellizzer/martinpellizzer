Pyrus roopiana

Pyrus roopiana, commonly known as the Roop's pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its deciduous leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like berry, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Pyrus roopiana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for various wildlife species.
