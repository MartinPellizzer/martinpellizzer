Pentameris ampla

Pentameris ampla, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils in forested areas. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface and white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, red drupe that contains a single seed.

Pentameris ampla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
