Piper vaginans

Piper vaginans, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, often containing a single seed.
