Polypodium aristatum

Polypodium aristatum, commonly known as the cliff fern, is a species of fern native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like leaves that are typically divided into narrow, linear segments. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found growing on cliffs or in shaded, moist environments. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Polypodium aristatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
