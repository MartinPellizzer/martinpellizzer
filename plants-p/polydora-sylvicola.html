Polydora sylvicola

Polydora sylvicola, commonly known as the forest threadleaf, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually white or pale green, and are adapted for pollination by insects.

Polydora sylvicola plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
