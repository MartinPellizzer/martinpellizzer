Phyllocactus plukenetii

Phyllocactus plukenetii, commonly known as the Plukenet's phyllocactus, is a unique cactus species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its flattened, leaf-like stems that are often mistaken for foliage, which are actually modified stems adapted for photosynthesis. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green stems. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight to flourish.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is a sought-after plant among cactus enthusiasts and botanists alike.
