Pectis propetes

Pectis propetes, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant has a low, spreading growth habit with silver-gray foliage adapted to conserve water. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pectis propetes is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suited to harsh desert environments.
