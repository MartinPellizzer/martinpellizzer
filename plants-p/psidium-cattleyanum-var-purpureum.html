Psidium cattleyanum var. purpureum

Psidium cattleyanum var. purpureum, commonly known as the purple guava, is a variant of the guava species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its dark purple to almost black fruits, which are typically smaller than the standard guava. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and white, often arranged in clusters, and it produces edible fruit with a sweet, aromatic flavor.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and unique fruit characteristics.
