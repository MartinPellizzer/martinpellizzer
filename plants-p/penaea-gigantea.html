Penaea gigantea

Penaea gigantea, commonly known as the giant sea holly, is a unique marine plant found in coastal regions.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh saltwater environments. The plant exhibits a flowering structure that resembles a cluster of small, star-shaped blooms. Its reproductive system relies on both self-pollination and cross-pollination by wind and water.

Penaea gigantea plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal sediments and providing habitat for various marine organisms.
