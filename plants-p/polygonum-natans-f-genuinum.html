Polygonum natans f. genuinum

Polygonum natans f. genuinum, commonly known as the water pepper, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its floating stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has rounded, waxy leaves that help it remain buoyant on water surfaces. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in slow-moving freshwater habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
