Phlox scabra

Phlox scabra, commonly known as rough phlox, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Polemoniaceae and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink or purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Phlox scabra is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
