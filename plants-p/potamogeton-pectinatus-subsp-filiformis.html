Potamogeton pectinatus subsp. filiformis

Potamogeton pectinatus subsp.

filiformis, commonly known as the slender pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats across Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically narrow and flexible.

The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which are often partially submerged. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

This species is often found in slow-moving or still waters such as lakes, ponds, and canals.
