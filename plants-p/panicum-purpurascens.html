Panicum purpurascens

Panicum purpurascens, commonly known as purple foxtail, is a flowering grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that bear dense, spike-like inflorescences, which turn a striking purple color as they mature. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as fields and roadsides, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Panicum purpurascens is often used in ornamental gardening for its distinctive appearance and is also valued for its role in wildlife habitat.
