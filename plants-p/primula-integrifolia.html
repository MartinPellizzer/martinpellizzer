Primula integrifolia

Primula integrifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet or purple flowers. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have smooth margins, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
