Papaver rhoeas var. vestitum

Papaver rhoeas var. vestitum, commonly known as the velvet poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its dense, velvety foliage.

It produces striking red-orange flowers with a distinctive black center, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. It has a short growing season and is typically annual, completing its life cycle within a single year.

The velvet poppy is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique foliage texture.
