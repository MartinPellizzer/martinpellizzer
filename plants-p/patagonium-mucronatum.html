Patagonium mucronatum

Patagonium mucronatum, commonly known as the Patagonian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, glandular leaves and purple to violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils. It has a low-growing habit and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
