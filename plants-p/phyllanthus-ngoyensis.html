Phyllanthus ngoyensis

Phyllanthus ngoyensis, commonly known as the Ngoyensis spurge, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by green, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as forest edges and riverbanks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional African medicine for various ailments.
