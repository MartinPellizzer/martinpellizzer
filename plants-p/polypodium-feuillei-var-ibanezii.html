Polypodium feuillei var. ibanezii

Polypodium feuillei var. ibanezii, commonly known as the Ibanez's shield fern, is a variant of the polypodium genus native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that are typically divided into segments, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store moisture and nutrients effectively.

This variant is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
