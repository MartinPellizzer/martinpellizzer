Passiflora cochinchinensis

Passiflora cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Chinese passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It produces striking, fragrant flowers with intricate structures, including a crown of filaments and a unique reproductive system. The plant is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves and woody stems that support its vigorous growth. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The fruit, which is edible, develops from the flower and contains a sweet, pulpy interior with seeds.
