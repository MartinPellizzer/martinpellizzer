Porrum lineare

Porrum lineare, commonly known as the linear burr or slender burr, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that often appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is native to regions of southern Africa. It produces small, spiky fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
