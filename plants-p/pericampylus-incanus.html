Pericampylus incanus

Pericampylus incanus, commonly known as the silver-haired pericampylus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy, silver-tomentose leaves that give it its common name. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest understories.

Pericampylus incanus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
