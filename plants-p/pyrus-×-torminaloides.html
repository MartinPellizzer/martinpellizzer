Pyrus × torminaloides

Pyrus × torminaloides, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, often with a slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is a small, fleshy pome that ripens in late summer, providing food for various wildlife species.
