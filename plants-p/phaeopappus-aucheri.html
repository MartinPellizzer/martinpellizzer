Phaeopappus aucheri

Phaeopappus aucheri, commonly known as the purple pappus.

It is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its striking purple flowers and fuzzy, pappus-like structures that aid in seed dispersal. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Phaeopappus aucheri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
