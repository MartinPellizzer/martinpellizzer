Pamianthe andreana

Pamianthe andreana, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with intricate patterns resembling celestial motifs. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Pamianthe andreana is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its ecological significance in its native habitat.
