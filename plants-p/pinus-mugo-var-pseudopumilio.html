Pinus mugo var. pseudopumilio

Pinus mugo var. pseudopumilio, commonly known as the dwarf mountain pine, is a compact, slow-growing conifer native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features a dense, spherical shape with short, twisted branches that give it a distinctive, gnarled appearance. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, covered in a whitish bloom that helps reduce water loss in harsh environments. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain the plant's seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is well adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and can thrive in cold, high-altitude climates.
