Pterospartum rhiphaeum

Pterospartum rhiphaeum, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

The tree is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
