Primula filipes

Primula filipes, commonly known as the thread-leaved primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Primula filipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
