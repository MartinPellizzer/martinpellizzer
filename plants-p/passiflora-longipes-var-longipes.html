Passiflora longipes var. longipes

Passiflora longipes var. longipes, commonly known as the longipes passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a crown of filaments and a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients and often consumed by local communities. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture.

This species is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
