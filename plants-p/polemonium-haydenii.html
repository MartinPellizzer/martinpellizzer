Polemonium haydenii

Polemonium haydenii, commonly known as Hayden's bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemonium genus within the family Polemoniaceae, which includes several species of flowering plants. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in early summer. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
