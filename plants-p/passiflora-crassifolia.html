Passiflora crassifolia

Passiflora crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking, showy flowers that feature five prominent sepals and three petals. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Passiflora crassifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
