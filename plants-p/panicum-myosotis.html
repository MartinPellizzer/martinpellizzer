Panicum myosotis

Panicum myosotis, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple florets. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open areas, such as roadsides and fields. Its flowers bloom in late summer, and the plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance.

However, it is considered invasive in some regions due to its rapid spread and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
