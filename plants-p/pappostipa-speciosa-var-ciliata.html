Pappostipa speciosa var. ciliata

Pappostipa speciosa var. ciliata, commonly known as the hairy beardgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its distinctive, hairy inflorescence that resembles a beard. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a rough, hairy texture along the edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in poor soils.
