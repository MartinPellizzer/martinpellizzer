Poterium garganicum

Poterium garganicum, commonly known as the broad-leaved grass.

It is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its robust, grass-like foliage. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves that are often hairy and have a bluish-green hue. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family.

Poterium garganicum is often found in dry, open habitats such as roadsides and rocky soils.
