Paracryphia alticola var. suaveolens

Paracryphia alticola var. suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented waxplant, is a rare and attractive evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green and slightly pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring, releasing a subtle, sweet aroma. Its ornamental value lies in its compact growth habit and year-round visual interest.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.
