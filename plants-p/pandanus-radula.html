Pandanus radula

Pandanus radula, commonly known as the red pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. Its root system is fibrous and helps it thrive in coastal and sandy soils.

Pandanus radula is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
