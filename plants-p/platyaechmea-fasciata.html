Platyaechmea fasciata

Platyaechmea fasciata, commonly known as the Zebra Plant, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, striped leaves that give it its common name, with a deep green base and prominent white or yellow stripes. The plant produces showy, orchid-like flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, or yellow, adding visual interest. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Platyaechmea fasciata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
