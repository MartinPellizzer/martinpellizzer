Protea hirta subsp. glabrescens

Protea hirta subsp.

glabrescens, commonly known as the coastal protea, is a shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and striking, cone-shaped inflorescences that range in color from white to pink.

The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salt exposure. Its flowers are pollinated by birds, particularly sugarbirds, and it produces woody, cone-like structures that persist through the growing season.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in coastal gardens.
