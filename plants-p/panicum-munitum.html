Panicum munitum

Panicum munitum, commonly known as Idaho grass, is a perennial grass native to western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces fluffy, seed-filled spikes that are often visible in late summer and early autumn. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a valuable forage species.
