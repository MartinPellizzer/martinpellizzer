Piper hamiltonii

Piper hamiltonii, commonly known as the Hamilton's pepper plant, is a species of flowering vine native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes many species of pepper plants, though it is not related to the black pepper plant. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, and it has heart-shaped, glossy leaves. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found growing along the edges of rainforest streams.

Its botanical features make it a unique and interesting subject for horticultural study.
