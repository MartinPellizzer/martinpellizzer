Piper stenophyllum

Piper stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly found in countries like Brazil and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that are followed by small, round berries.

Piper stenophyllum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
