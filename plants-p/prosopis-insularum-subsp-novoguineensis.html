Prosopis insularum subsp. novoguineensis

Prosopis insularum subsp.

novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of New Guinea. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits.

The plant thrives in sandy or coastal soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Its wood is sometimes used for fuel or minor construction purposes.
