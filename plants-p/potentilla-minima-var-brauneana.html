Potentilla minima var. brauneana

Potentilla minima var. brauneana, commonly known as the dwarf cinquefoil, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has wiry, arching stems covered in fine, soft hairs. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.
