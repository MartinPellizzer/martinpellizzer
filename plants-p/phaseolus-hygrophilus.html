Phaseolus hygrophilus

Phaseolus hygrophilus, commonly known as the water bean.

It is a species of legume native to the Americas, particularly found in tropical and subtropical regions. This plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, white or pale green flowers. Its seeds are typically small and oval, and it is often grown as a forage crop or for ornamental purposes.

The plant is adapted to wet environments, which is reflected in its common name.
