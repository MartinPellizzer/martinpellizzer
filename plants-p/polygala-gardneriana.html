Polygala gardneriana

Polygala gardneriana, commonly known as the Gardner's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils or along dry streambeds. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Polygala gardneriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
