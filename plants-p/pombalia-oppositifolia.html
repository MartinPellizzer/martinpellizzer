Pombalia oppositifolia

Pombalia oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaf pombalia, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite arrangement of simple, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the wet season. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Pombalia oppositifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
