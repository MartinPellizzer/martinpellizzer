Peltopus octarrhenipetalus

Peltopus octarrhenipetalus, commonly known as the eight-petaled rock orchid, is a rare and unique flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature eight distinct petals, a feature that sets it apart from most other orchid species. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. Its botanical significance lies in its unusual floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
