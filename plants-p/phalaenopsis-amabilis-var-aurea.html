Phalaenopsis amabilis var. aurea

Phalaenopsis amabilis var. aurea, commonly known as the golden phalaenopsis orchid, is a hybrid cultivar prized for its vibrant yellow petals and velvety texture.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, where it typically grows as an epiphyte on trees. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its evergreen foliage and showy flowers. Its blooms, which can last several months, are characterized by a distinct golden hue and a subtle fragrance.

This variety is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of care in indoor environments.
