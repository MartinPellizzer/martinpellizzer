Pachyrhizus bulbosus

Pachyrhizus bulbosus, commonly known as the bulbous bean, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. Its leaves are compound and often used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
