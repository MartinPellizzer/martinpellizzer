Prunus spinosa f. variegata

Prunus spinosa f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated blackthorn, is a compact shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It features dark, thorny stems and small, ovate leaves with distinctive white or yellow variegation. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by dark blue berries in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

The variegation in its foliage makes it a visually striking addition to landscape designs.
