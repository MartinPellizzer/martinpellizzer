Prunus cerasifera subsp. georgica

Prunus cerasifera subsp.

georgica, commonly known as the Georgian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region. It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible, dark red berries.

The plant has elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated edge and a smooth, grayish bark. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
