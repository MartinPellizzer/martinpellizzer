Polygonum orthocladum

Polygonum orthocladum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed smartweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, ditches, and stream banks. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, clustered flowers with five pink or purple petals. Its botanical characteristics include a short, stout stem and a basal rosette of leaves.

Polygonum orthocladum is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize wet areas.
