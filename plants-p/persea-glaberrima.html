Persea glaberrima

Persea glaberrima, commonly known as the Mexican avocado or tepin, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by pear-shaped fruits that are typically harvested when they turn from green to yellow. Its fruit is valued for its creamy, oily flesh and is used in various culinary applications.

Persea glaberrima thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
