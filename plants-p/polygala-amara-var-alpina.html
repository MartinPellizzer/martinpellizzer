Polygala amara var. alpina

Polygala amara var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are alternate and pinnate, with finely divided segments that help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens for its resilience and aesthetic qualities.
