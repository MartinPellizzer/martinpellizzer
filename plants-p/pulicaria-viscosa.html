Pulicaria viscosa

Pulicaria viscosa, commonly known as sticky sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant is distinguished by its sticky, glandular hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive texture. It typically thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and arid environments.

Pulicaria viscosa produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.
