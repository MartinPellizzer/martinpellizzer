Paphinia dunstervillei

Paphinia dunstervillei, commonly known as the Dunsterville's paphiopedilum, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a velvety purple or brown hue with intricate patterns. The plant has a short, stocky rhizome and produces pseudobulbs that support its foliage and floral growth. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, often with a waxy texture that helps it thrive in its humid, shaded habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a delicate and endangered species in its natural environment.
