Pyrus folgneri

Pyrus folgneri, commonly known as the Sierra Madre pear, is a small, deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart pears in late summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
