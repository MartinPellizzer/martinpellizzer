Piper pentandrum

Piper pentandrum, commonly known as the five-stamen pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its small, green, heart-shaped leaves and produces tiny, clustered white flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
