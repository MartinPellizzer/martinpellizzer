Plectronia monstrosa

Plectronia monstrosa, commonly known as the monstrous plectron, is a striking member of the Araliaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its large, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, often with a vibrant green color and prominent veins. It produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its dramatic foliage and unique appearance.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.
