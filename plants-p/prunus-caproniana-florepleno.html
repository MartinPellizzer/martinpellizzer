Prunus caproniana florepleno

Prunus caproniana florepleno, commonly known as the double-flowered cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus native to the United States.

It is characterized by its dense, double blooms that appear in early spring, giving it a lush, ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree with a rounded canopy and peeling bark that becomes more pronounced with age. Its flowers are typically pink or white, and it produces small, edible fruits that are often used in jams and jellies.

This cultivar is valued for its showy blossoms and adaptability to various soil types.
