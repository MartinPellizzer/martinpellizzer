Pyrus lactea

Pyrus lactea, commonly known as the white pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its white, waxy flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible fruit that ripens in late summer. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a light green color. The tree typically grows to a height of 5-10 meters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
