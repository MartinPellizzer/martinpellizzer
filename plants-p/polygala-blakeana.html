Polygala blakeana

Polygala blakeana, commonly known as the Blake's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, often greenish or yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is found in coastal areas and heathlands. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
