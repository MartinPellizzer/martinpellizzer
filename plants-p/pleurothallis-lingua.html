Pleurothallis lingua

Pleurothallis lingua, commonly known as the tongue orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, often fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it conserve moisture.

Pleurothallis lingua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
