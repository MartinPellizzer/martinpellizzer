Potentilla inclinata var. kerneri

Potentilla inclinata var. kerneri, commonly known as the Kern's bentstem cinquefoil, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically oval to lance-shaped, with a hairy texture, and are arranged alternately along the stems. This variety is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, contributing to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.
