Pleurothallis pectinata

Pleurothallis pectinata, commonly known as the pectinata orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces intricate, fragrant blooms that attract various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
