Polygala canarana

Polygala canarana, commonly known as the Canary Island milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to the arid and semi-arid climates of its native habitat. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with a distinctive spurred structure that attracts pollinators.

Polygala canarana is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native environment.
