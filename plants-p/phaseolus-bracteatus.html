Phaseolus bracteatus

Phaseolus bracteatus, commonly known as the bracteatus bean or the Mexican runner bean, is a climbing legume native to Central and South America.

It produces elongated, tubular pods that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when mature. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is often cultivated for its edible beans. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers are usually white or pale purple, and the plant is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
