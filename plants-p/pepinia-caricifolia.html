Pepinia caricifolia

Pepinia caricifolia, commonly known as the waterleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that are often submerged or floating on water surfaces. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the wet season. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Pepinia caricifolia thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, making it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.
