Parasia platyptera

Parasia platyptera, commonly known as the flat-winged moth, is a species of moth belonging to the family Noctuidae.

It is characterized by its distinctive wing patterns, which often feature intricate markings in shades of brown and gray. The plant associated with this species is the common milkweed, Asclepias syriaca, which serves as a host plant for its larvae. The larvae feed on the leaves of the milkweed, contributing to the plant's ecological dynamics.

This species plays a role in pollination and serves as a food source for various predators in its habitat.
