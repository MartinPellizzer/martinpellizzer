Prunus × lannesiana f. amabilis

Prunus × lannesiana f. amabilis, commonly known as the double-flowered cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus that exhibits ornamental value due to its unique floral characteristics.

It is a hybrid resulting from the cross between Prunus lannesiana and Prunus × yedoensis, showcasing a compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of double flowers, often in shades of white, pink, or red, which bloom in early spring. It is typically grown as a flowering shrub or small tree, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is prized for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
