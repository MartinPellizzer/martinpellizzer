Parodia mutabilis

Parodia mutabilis, commonly known as the variable cactus, is a species native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape. The plant features cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in white to grayish spines. It produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically short-lived.

Parodia mutabilis is well adapted to arid environments, making it a popular choice for desert gardens.
