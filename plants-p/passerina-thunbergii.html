Passerina thunbergii

Passerina thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Passerina thunbergii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in gardens.
