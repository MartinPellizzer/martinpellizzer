Pocilla biloba

Pocilla biloba, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant prefers well-draining soil and indirect sunlight, making it a popular houseplant. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a sympodial growth pattern.

Pocilla biloba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
