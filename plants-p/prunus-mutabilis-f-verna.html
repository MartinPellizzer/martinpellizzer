Prunus mutabilis f. verna

Prunus mutabilis f. verna, commonly known as the spring-flowering cherry, is a cultivar of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its early blooming habit, with pink to white flowers appearing in late winter to early spring. The plant has a rounded, upright growth habit and produces small, edible fruits that ripen in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and urban landscapes.

The botanical features of this cultivar make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
