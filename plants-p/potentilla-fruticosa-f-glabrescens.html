Potentilla fruticosa f. glabrescens

Potentilla fruticosa f. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous potentilla, is a shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, rounded leaves.

It is a form of the common potentilla, which is native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically produces clusters of yellow flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is generally hairless, giving it a smooth, polished appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
