Phyllarthron subumbellatum

Phyllarthron subumbellatum, commonly known as the umbrella-leaved fern, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-like arrangement of fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features long, slender rhizomes that support its delicate, bipinnate leaves. Its reproductive structures consist of small, clustered sporangia located on the undersides of the fronds.

This fern thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist, forested areas.
