Pourretia floccosa

Pourretia floccosa, commonly known as the woolly mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, white, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and ecologically valuable plant in native gardens.
