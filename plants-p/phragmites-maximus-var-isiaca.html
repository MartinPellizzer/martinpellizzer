Phragmites maximus var. isiaca

Phragmites maximus var. isiaca, commonly known as the isiaca reed, is a variant of the common reed that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant is often found in marshes, along lake shores, and in other moist habitats. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its flowering spikes produce small, wind-pollinated flowers, contributing to its ecological significance.
