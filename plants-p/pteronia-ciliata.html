Pteronia ciliata

Pteronia ciliata, commonly known as the Cape buttercup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a low, spreading habit with hairy, linear leaves. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to Mediterranean climates.

Pteronia ciliata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and coastal landscapes.
