Phloeophila pelecaniceps

Phloeophila pelecaniceps, commonly known as the pelecaniceps phloeophila, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in its humid environment. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with vibrant yellow petals, which bloom during the wet season. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil of the forest floor.

Due to habitat destruction, it is currently listed as endangered by the IUCN.
