Prunus serrulata var. hupehensis

Prunus serrulata var. hupehensis, commonly known as the Chinese cherry or Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to China and Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by edible, sweet berries. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its graceful form and seasonal color changes.
