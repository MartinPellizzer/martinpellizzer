Psiadia aparine

Psiadia aparine, commonly known as sandspur, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Africa.

It is a low-growing annual or perennial herb with hairy stems and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its prickly, sticky seeds that are dispersed by wind and animals. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Psiadia aparine is known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient weed in many regions.
