Plectronia epiphytica

Plectronia epiphytica, commonly known as the epiphytic plectronia, is a unique member of the Plectroniaceae family.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. This species is characterized by its delicate, leaf-like structures that are often used for photosynthesis. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for researchers studying plant adaptations to specific ecological niches.
