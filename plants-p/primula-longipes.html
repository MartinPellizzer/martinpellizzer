Primula longipes

Primula longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing clusters of pink or white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are oval and slightly hairy, with a dark green color, and it prefers cool, moist environments.

Primula longipes is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
