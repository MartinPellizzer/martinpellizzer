Prunus laurocerasus f. serbica

Prunus laurocerasus f. serbica, commonly known as the Serbica laurel-cherry, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Balkan region.

It features glossy, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that provide dense foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, followed by red or black berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.

Its slow growth and hardy nature make it a popular choice for hedging and landscape design.
