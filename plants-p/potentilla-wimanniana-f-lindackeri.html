Potentilla wimanniana f. lindackeri

Potentilla wimanniana f. lindackeri, commonly known as the Lindacker's potentilla, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are attractive to pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and hardy nature in temperate climates.
