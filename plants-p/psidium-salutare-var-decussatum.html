Psidium salutare var. decussatum

Psidium salutare var. decussatum, commonly known as the clustered guava, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, often thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow or orange berries. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a glossy surface and a serrated margin.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
