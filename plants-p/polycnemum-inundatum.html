Polycnemum inundatum

Polycnemum inundatum, commonly known as water plantain, is a wetland plant belonging to the Alismataceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating in water. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically emerging from the water surface. It thrives in marshy areas, pond margins, and slow-moving streams.

Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
