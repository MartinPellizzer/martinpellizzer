Polygonum aviculare var. campestre

Polygonum aviculare var. campestre, commonly known as field knotgrass, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features prostrate or ascending stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly rough texture.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.
