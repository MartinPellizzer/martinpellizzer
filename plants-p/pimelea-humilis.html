Pimelea humilis

Pimelea humilis, commonly known as the dwarf bottlebrush, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Australia.

It features compact, cylindrical flowers that are typically red or orange in color, resembling a bottlebrush. The plant has small, needle-like leaves and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.

Pimelea humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native garden landscaping.
