Polygala pseudolaurifolia var. pilcomayensis

Polygala pseudolaurifolia var. pilcomayensis, commonly known as the Pilcomayo polygala, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Bolivia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color and are pollinated by insects.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
