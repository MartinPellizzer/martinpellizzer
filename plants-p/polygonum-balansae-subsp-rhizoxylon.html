Polygonum balansae subsp. rhizoxylon

Polygonum balansae subsp.

rhizoxylon, commonly known as the red-root knotweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its erect stems and alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often forming a dense network underground.

It is typically found in moist, disturbed habitats such as riverbanks and roadside areas.
