Pelargonium stipulaceum

Pelargonium stipulaceum, commonly known as the African geranium, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features upright, woody stems and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium stipulaceum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
