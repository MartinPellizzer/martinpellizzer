Platanthera solstitialis var. patula

Platanthera solstitialis var. patula, commonly known as the summer lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and its distinctive, fragrant, white to pale pink flower spikes that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded woodland environments. Its flowers are arranged in a loose, three-rowed pattern, typical of the orchid family.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for various pollinators.
