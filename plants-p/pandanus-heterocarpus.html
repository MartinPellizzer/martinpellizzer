Pandanus heterocarpus

Pandanus heterocarpus, commonly known as the broad-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers arranged in dense, branched inflorescences, which are often used in traditional crafts and culinary applications. Its roots and leaves are also utilized in local cultures for their aromatic properties and medicinal benefits.

The plant thrives in coastal and wetland environments, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
