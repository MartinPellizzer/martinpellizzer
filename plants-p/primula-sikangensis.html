Primula sikangensis

Primula sikangensis, commonly known as the Sichuan primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, often in moist, shaded environments, and is adapted to cool, temperate climates. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair.

Primula sikangensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
