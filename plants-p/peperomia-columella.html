Peperomia columella

Peperomia columella, commonly known as the columnar peperomia, is a compact, upright shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often glossy and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color, emerging from the leaf axils. Its succulent-like leaves make it drought-tolerant and suitable for indoor cultivation.

Peperomia columella is prized for its attractive appearance and ease of care, making it a popular choice for gardeners and plant enthusiasts.
