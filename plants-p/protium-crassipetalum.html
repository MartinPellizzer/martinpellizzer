Protium crassipetalum

Protium crassipetalum, commonly known as the thick-petaled Protium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by spherical fruits with a thick, leathery pericarp. Its bark is thick and fissured, often exuding a milky sap when wounded.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
