Puccinellia fontana

Puccinellia fontana, commonly known as the saltgrass, is a flowering plant native to arid and saline environments.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers. It is adapted to thrive in salty soils and is often found in coastal areas or salt marshes.
