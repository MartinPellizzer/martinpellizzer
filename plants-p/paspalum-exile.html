Paspalum exile

Paspalum exile, commonly known as slender paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green or pale yellow in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Paspalum exile is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
