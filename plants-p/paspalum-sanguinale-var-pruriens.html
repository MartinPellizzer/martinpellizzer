Paspalum sanguinale var. pruriens

Paspalum sanguinale var. pruriens, commonly known as the red paspalum, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to different climates and soil types.
