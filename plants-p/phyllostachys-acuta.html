Phyllostachys acuta

Phyllostachys acuta, commonly known as the sharp bamboo, is a fast-growing grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of fiber. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Phyllostachys acuta is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in horticultural and ecological settings.
