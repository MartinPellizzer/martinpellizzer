Pelargonium grossularioides

Pelargonium grossularioides, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated calyx that encloses the fruit, resembling a small lantern. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible and have a tangy, sweet flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium grossularioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique appearance.
