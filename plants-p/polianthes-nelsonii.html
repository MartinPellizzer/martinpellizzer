Polianthes nelsonii

Polianthes nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's spider lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and clusters of white, spider-like flowers. The plant produces showy, fragrant blooms that typically appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is widely appreciated by horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
