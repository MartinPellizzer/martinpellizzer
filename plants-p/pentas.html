Pentas

Pentas, commonly known as flame flower, are flowering plants belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

They are native to Africa, Asia, and Australia, and are known for their vibrant, star-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, making them visually striking in gardens and landscapes. Pentas are shrubs or small trees that thrive in warm climates and are often cultivated for their ornamental value.

Their foliage is typically glossy and evergreen, adding year-round interest to the landscape.
