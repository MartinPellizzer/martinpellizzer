Polygala brachistachyos

Polygala brachistachyos, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, while the flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering habit.
