Plinia orthoclada

Plinia orthoclada, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy, dark green appearance. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, red, fleshy berries.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and fruit.
