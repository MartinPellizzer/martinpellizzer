Penstemon angustifolius var. caudatus

Penstemon angustifolius var. caudatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved beardtongue with a tail, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture. This variety is particularly noted for its distinctive elongated flower clusters that resemble a tail, giving it its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
