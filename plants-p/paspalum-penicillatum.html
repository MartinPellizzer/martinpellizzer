Paspalum penicillatum

Paspalum penicillatum, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in wetland ecosystems and can form dense stands in suitable conditions.

Paspalum penicillatum is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
