Potamogeton obtusifolius var. latifolius

Potamogeton obtusifolius var. latifolius, commonly known as broad-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It has broad, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that develop into dark, spherical berries. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters with muddy or sandy substrates.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
