Pinguicula spathulata

Pinguicula spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaf butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that trap and digest insects. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires consistent humidity.

Pinguicula spathulata is a popular choice for carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and fascinating feeding mechanism.
