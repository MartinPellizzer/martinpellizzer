Phyllanthus nutans

Phyllanthus nutans, commonly known as the "shooting star" or "false sandalwood," is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often resembling a star shape. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that split open to release seeds.

Phyllanthus nutans is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
