Penicillanthemum latifolium

Penicillanthemum latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf penicillanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, pale blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a branching stem that produces multiple flowering shoots.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
