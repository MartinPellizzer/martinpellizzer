Phebalium glandulosum var. daviesii

Phebalium glandulosum var. daviesii, commonly known as the Davies' hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
