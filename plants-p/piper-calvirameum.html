Piper calvirameum

Piper calvirameum, commonly known as the Calvira pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tiny, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly spicy aroma, similar to other members of the genus Piper.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.
