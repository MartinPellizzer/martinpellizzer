Premna sterculiifolia

Premna sterculiifolia, commonly known as the Indian nettle or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Premna sterculiifolia is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
