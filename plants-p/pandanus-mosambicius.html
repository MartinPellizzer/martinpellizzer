Pandanus mosambicius

Pandanus mosambicius, commonly known as the Mozambique pandanus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically found at the ends of the leaf sheaths. Its rhizomes are sometimes utilized in local cuisines, adding a unique flavor to dishes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in coastal and wetland habitats.
