Phyllanthus sepiarius

Phyllanthus sepiarius, commonly known as the sea buckthorn, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green leaves and bright orange to red berries that ripen in late summer. The plant is adapted to saline and sandy soils, making it a hardy species found in coastal dunes and rocky shores. Its berries are rich in vitamins and antioxidants, often used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible oils.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and preventing erosion.
