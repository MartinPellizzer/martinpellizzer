Pandanus extralittoralis

Pandanus extralittoralis, commonly known as the coastal pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a large, woody shrub or small tree that typically grows near coastal areas, tolerating salty air and sandy soils. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fibrous, palm-like fruits. Its roots help stabilize coastal dunes, making it an important species for erosion control.

The plant's resilience to harsh coastal conditions makes it a significant component of coastal ecosystems.
