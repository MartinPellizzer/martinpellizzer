Pithecellobium waitzii

Pithecellobium waitzii, commonly known as the Hawaiian white bean, is a small evergreen tree native to Hawaii.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are used in traditional Hawaiian cuisine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ecological and cultural significance, it is also valued for its role in native ecosystems.
