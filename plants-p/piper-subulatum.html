Piper subulatum

Piper subulatum, commonly known as the Chilean pepper or mountain pepper, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features small, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are greenish-white and are followed by small, peppery fruits.

Piper subulatum is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
