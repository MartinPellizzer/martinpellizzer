Polygala tamariscea

Polygala tamariscea, commonly known as the sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has a compact, mat-like growth habit, with hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or arid regions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
