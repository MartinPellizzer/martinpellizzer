Picea abies var. viminalis

Picea abies var. viminalis, commonly known as the Weeping Norway Spruce, is a variant of the Norway spruce characterized by its pendulous branches that give it a weeping appearance.

This cultivar is a hybrid or natural variant that exhibits elongated, drooping shoots, creating a distinctive, ornamental shape. It is native to Europe and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal. The tree retains its needles year-round, providing evergreen foliage throughout the seasons.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
