Phlogacanthus wallichii

Phlogacanthus wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elliptical, woody fruits containing two seeds. It thrives in temperate to subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
