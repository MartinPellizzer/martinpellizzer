Poa Alpina F. Vivipara

Poa alpina f. vivipara is a rare and unique variant of the alpine bentgrass found in high-altitude environments. This plant is known for its ability to produce vegetative offspring through a process called viviparity. It thrives in cold, rocky soils where few other plants can survive. Its compact growth form and dense foliage make it an important component of alpine ecosystems. Despite its resilience, this subspecies faces threats from climate change and habitat loss.
