Piper tobagoanum

Piper tobagoanum, commonly known as the Tobago pepper or wild black pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into small, round berries. The berries contain a spicy, aromatic seed that is used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
