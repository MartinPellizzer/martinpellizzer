Pinus insignis var. macrocarpa

Pinus insignis var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant sequoia, is a variant of the sequoia tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its massive size, with some specimens reaching heights of over 300 feet and diameters exceeding 15 feet. The tree has needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in spirals along the branches. Its cones are large and woody, containing the seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variant is known for its longevity, with some trees living over 3,000 years.
