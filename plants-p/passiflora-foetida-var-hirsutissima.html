Passiflora foetida var. hirsutissima

Passiflora foetida var. hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are often consumed by birds and small mammals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique floral structure and hairy foliage make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
