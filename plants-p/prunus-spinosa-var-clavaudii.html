Prunus spinosa var. clavaudii

Prunus spinosa var. clavaudii, commonly known as the blackthorn or sloe, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark blue berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has small, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant white flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The berries are used in making alcoholic beverages and jams, and the plant has historical uses in herbal medicine.
