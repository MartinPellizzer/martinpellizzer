Prunus serrulata f. bullata

Prunus serrulata f. bullata, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a botanical variant of the cherry tree characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers.

It is native to Japan and is prized for its ornamental value, particularly during the spring bloom season. The plant features smooth, grayish-bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn reddish in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The flowers, which are white or pale pink, are clustered in drooping clusters, adding a graceful appearance to the tree.
