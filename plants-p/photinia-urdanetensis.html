Photinia urdanetensis

Photinia urdanetensis, commonly known as the Urdanet holly, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy red or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. The fruit it produces is small and berry-like, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.
