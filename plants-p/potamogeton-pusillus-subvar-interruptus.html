Potamogeton pusillus subvar. interruptus

Potamogeton pusillus subvar. interruptus, commonly known as the interrupted pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, contributing to its role in aquatic ecosystems. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of water depths and light conditions.

This subspecies is important for providing habitat and food for aquatic organisms.
