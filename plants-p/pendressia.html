Pendressia

Pendressia, commonly known as the pendulous bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that often hang in clusters, giving the plant its common name. Pendressia species are typically found in temperate regions, growing in woodland or rocky habitats. The leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stems.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their vibrant blooms.
