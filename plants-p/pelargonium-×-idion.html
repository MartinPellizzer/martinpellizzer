Pelargonium × idion

Pelargonium × idion, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming from spring to autumn. It is often used in hanging baskets and containers due to its cascading form and ornamental value.

Pelargonium × idion prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, low-maintenance ornamental plants.
