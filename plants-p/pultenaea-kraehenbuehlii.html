Pultenaea kraehenbuehlii

Pultenaea kraehenbuehlii, commonly known as the Kraehenbuehlii bush pea, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pea-like flowers and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to the dry, Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in local ecosystems.
