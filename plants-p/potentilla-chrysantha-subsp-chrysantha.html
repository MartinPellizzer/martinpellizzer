Potentilla chrysantha subsp. chrysantha

Potentilla chrysantha subsp.

chrysantha, commonly known as the golden potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It features wiry stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and hardiness in cooler climates.
