Phaseolus vulgaris var. sarcopardus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. sarcopardus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a variant of the common bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers and elongated, edible pods that contain numerous seeds. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and thrives in warm, temperate climates. Its leaves are heart-shaped and compound, with a matte green surface, and it produces a distinctive, aromatic scent.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and culinary use, particularly in soups and stews.
