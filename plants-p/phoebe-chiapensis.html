Phoebe chiapensis

Phoebe chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapan Phoebe, is a species of flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments, often in lowland forests. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Phoebe chiapensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant foliage and unique floral display.
