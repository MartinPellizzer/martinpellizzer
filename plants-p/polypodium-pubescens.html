Polypodium pubescens

Polypodium pubescens, commonly known as the hairy maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome is thick and often covered with a layer of moss-like growth, which helps in moisture retention.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its graceful foliage and ease of care.
