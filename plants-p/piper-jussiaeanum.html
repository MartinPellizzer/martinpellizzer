Piper jussiaeanum

Piper jussiaeanum, commonly known as the Java pepper or long pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its botanical structure includes a woody stem, and it thrives in humid, tropical climates.

Piper jussiaeanum is valued for its medicinal properties and is often cultivated for its spice.
