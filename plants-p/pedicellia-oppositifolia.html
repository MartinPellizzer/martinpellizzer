Pedicellia oppositifolia

Pedicellia oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leafed pedicellia, is a flowering plant characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement.

It typically features small, clustered flowers that are often borne on short pedicels. The plant belongs to the family Pedicellariaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are usually white or pale in color and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
