Panicum maximum var. heterotrichum

Panicum maximum var. heterotrichum, commonly known as Guinea grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its coarse, stiff leaves and panicles that are often loosely arranged. The plant is drought-resistant and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is widely used as forage for livestock due to its high nutritional value.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
