Poa kurtzii

Poa kurtzii, commonly known as the Kirtz's bentgrass, is a species of grass native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It is a low-growing perennial plant that forms dense mats, often inhabiting moist, temperate environments such as meadows and grasslands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and establish itself in various soil types.

Poa kurtzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its fine texture and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
