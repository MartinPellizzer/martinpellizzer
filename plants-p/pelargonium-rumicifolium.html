Pelargonium rumicifolium

Pelargonium rumicifolium, commonly known as the sour orange geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Pelargonium rumicifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
