Papyrus madagascariensis

Papyrus madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar papyrus, is a herbaceous plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features tall, narrow leaves that can grow up to several meters in height. It thrives in wetland environments, often found near water bodies such as lakes and marshes.

Its historical significance is notable, as it was used by ancient civilizations for paper-making and other purposes.
