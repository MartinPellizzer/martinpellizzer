Phyllorkis refracta

Phyllorkis refracta, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to Australia, characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, spikelets.

Phyllorkis refracta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
