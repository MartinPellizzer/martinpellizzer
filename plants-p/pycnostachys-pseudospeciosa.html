Pycnostachys pseudospeciosa

Pycnostachys pseudospeciosa, commonly known as the giant flame bamboo, is a striking plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bright red, orange, or yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its rapid growth rate makes it both a desirable and sometimes challenging ornamental plant to manage.
