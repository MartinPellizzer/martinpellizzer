Pinus sinclairii

Pinus sinclairii, commonly known as the Sierra pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The tree produces cones that are elongated and often remain attached to the branches for several years. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
