Potentilla × adulterina

Potentilla × adulterina, commonly known as the hybrid shrub, is a species resulting from the cross between Potentilla fruticosa and Potentilla argentea.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental value.

Potentilla × adulterina is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
