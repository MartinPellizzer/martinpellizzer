Palisota thyrsiflora

Palisota thyrsiflora, commonly known as the thyrse-flowered water plantain, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland environments in Southeast Asia.

It features arrow-shaped leaves that are typically submerged or floating, adapted to aquatic conditions. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences with small, greenish-white flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize shallow water areas effectively.

Palisota thyrsiflora is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.
