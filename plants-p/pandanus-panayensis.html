Pandanus panayensis

Pandanus panayensis, commonly known as the Panay screw pine, is a unique plant native to the island of Panay in the Philippines.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its spirally arranged leaves that form a dense, umbrella-like crown. The plant has a short, woody stem with a fibrous root system, and its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy coating. It thrives in coastal and brackish water environments, making it an important species for coastal stabilization.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
