Pelargonium diversifolium

Pelargonium diversifolium, commonly known as the variable-leaf geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its diverse leaf shapes, which can range from ovate to lanceolate. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
