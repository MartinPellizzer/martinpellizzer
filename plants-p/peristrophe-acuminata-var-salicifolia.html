Peristrophe acuminata var. salicifolia

Peristrophe acuminata var. salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are elongated and tapering. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and blooms.
