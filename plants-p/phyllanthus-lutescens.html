Phyllanthus lutescens

Phyllanthus lutescens, commonly known as the yellow phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with smooth, often hairy stems and alternate, ovate leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
