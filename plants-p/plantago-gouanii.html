Plantago gouanii

Plantago gouanii, commonly known as the Guanacaste plantain, is a perennial herb native to Central America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a ground cover.
