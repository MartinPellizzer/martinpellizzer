Papaver mairei var. hirtellum

Papaver mairei var. hirtellum, commonly known as the Hirtellum poppy, is a variant of the Maire poppy native to New Zealand.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its striking red or orange flowers with a black center. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
