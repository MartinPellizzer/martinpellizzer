Paspalum hyalinum

Paspalum hyalinum, commonly known as the silver paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale green or yellowish flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
