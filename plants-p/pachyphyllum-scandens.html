Pachyphyllum scandens

Pachyphyllum scandens, commonly known as the climbing stonecrop, is a succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white, typically during the spring and summer seasons. Its climbing habit allows it to attach to surfaces using specialized structures called holdfasts.

Due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
