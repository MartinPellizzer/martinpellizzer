Pultenaea subalpina

Pultenaea subalpina, commonly known as the alpine pultenaea, is a shrub native to the alpine regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late autumn to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, dry conditions.

Pultenaea subalpina is an important component of alpine ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for various native wildlife.
