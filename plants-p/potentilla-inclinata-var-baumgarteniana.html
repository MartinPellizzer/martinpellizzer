Potentilla inclinata var. baumgarteniana

Potentilla inclinata var. baumgarteniana, commonly known as the Baumann's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and adaptability.

This variant is closely related to other species in the Potentilla genus, sharing similar botanical characteristics.
