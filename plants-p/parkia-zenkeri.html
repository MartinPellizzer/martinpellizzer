Parkia zenkeri

Parkia zenkeri, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The tree produces pods containing seeds that are dispersed by wind. It is often found in moist lowland forests and can grow up to 20 meters in height.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.
