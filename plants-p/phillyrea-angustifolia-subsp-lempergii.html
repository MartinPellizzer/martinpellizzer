Phillyrea angustifolia subsp. lempergii

Phillyrea angustifolia subsp.

lempergii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Phillyrea, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, oval, dark green leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems.

The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, round berries. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
