Peperomia galapagensis var. ramulosa

Peperomia galapagensis var. ramulosa, commonly known as the Galápagos pepper plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Galápagos Islands.

It features glossy, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of green and red, which are followed by delicate, papery bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Its unique appearance and adaptation to the island's climate make it a distinctive member of the Piperaceae family.
