Prasophyllum copelandii

Prasophyllum copelandii, commonly known as the Copeland's leek orchid, is an endemic species of the Australian flora.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, leek-like appearance. The plant typically grows in heathland and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. It produces a single, tall stem with narrow, grass-like leaves and a spike of pale green to white flowers.

Prasophyllum copelandii is a rare and protected species, often found in specific regions of New South Wales.
