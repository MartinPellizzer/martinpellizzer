Psidium fragrans

Psidium fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant guava, is a tropical plant belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are often used in perfumes and aromatherapy. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and emit a sweet scent when crushed.

The fruit, though not typically consumed by humans, is valued for its ornamental qualities and aromatic properties.
