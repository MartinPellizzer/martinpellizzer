Pavonia brasiliensis

Pavonia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pavonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, often hairy stems. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, blooming in the spring and summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Pavonia brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
