Poa hypopsila

Poa hypopsila, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, upright blades and tufted growth habit, often forming dense mats in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with two to three florets, typical of the Poaceae family. Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

Poa hypopsila is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals.
