Pogostemon elsholtzioides

Pogostemon elsholtzioides, commonly known as the Indian betel plant, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or pink. It is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and as an ornamental plant.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
