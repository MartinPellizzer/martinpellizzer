Polygala bellidifolia

Polygala bellidifolia, commonly known as the pink milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are small and opposite, and it produces small, bead-like fruits that contain the seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
