Panicum lycopodioides

Panicum lycopodioides, commonly known as the clubmoss panicgrass, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is an important component of certain wetland and woodland ecosystems.
