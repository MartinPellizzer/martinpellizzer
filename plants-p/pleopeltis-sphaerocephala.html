Pleopeltis sphaerocephala

Pleopeltis sphaerocephala, commonly known as the globe-headed pleopeltis, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and distinctive, rounded, spore-bearing structures called sori. The fronds are typically short and broad, with a leathery texture and a dark green coloration. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests.

Its unique morphology makes it a visually striking addition to botanical collections and a subject of interest in horticultural research.
