Philadelphus columbarius

Philadelphus columbarius, commonly known as the common lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces opposite, simple, and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Philadelphus columbarius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beautiful floral display.
