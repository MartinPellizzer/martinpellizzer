Phacelia ramosissima var. subglabra

Phacelia ramosissima var. subglabra, commonly known as the hairy phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its branched stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically hairy, giving the plant its common name, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinator populations.
