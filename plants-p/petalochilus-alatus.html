Petalochilus alatus

Petalochilus alatus, commonly known as the winged orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often wing-like petals that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink and purple.

Petalochilus alatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
