Pierardia dasystachya

Pierardia dasystachya, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, floating fronds that can reach lengths of up to 2 meters. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. Its rhizomes form dense mats on the water surface, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

The fronds are divided into numerous narrow segments, which help in photosynthesis and buoyancy.
