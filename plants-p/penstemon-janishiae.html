Penstemon janishiae

Penstemon janishiae, commonly known as Janish's beardtongue, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically blooms in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Penstemon janishiae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
