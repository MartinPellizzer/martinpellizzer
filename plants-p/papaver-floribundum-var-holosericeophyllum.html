Papaver floribundum var. holosericeophyllum

Papaver floribundum var. holosericeophyllum, commonly known as the velvet poppy, is a striking herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, often with a velvety texture. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting flowers.
