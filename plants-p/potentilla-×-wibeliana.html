Potentilla × wibeliana

Potentilla × wibeliana, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, rounded fruits known as achenes.

Potentilla × wibeliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
