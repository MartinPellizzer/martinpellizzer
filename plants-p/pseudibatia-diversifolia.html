Pseudibatia diversifolia

Pseudibatia diversifolia, commonly known as the mountain saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its rosette-like growth habit and produces small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant has hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its cold, arid environment. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Pseudibatia diversifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
