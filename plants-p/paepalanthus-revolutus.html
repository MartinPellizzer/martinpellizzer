Paepalanthus revolutus

Paepalanthus revolutus, commonly known as the curled pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a compact, cushion-like structure. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
