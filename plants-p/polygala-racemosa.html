Polygala racemosa

Polygala racemosa, commonly known as the clustered milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features slender, branching stems and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
