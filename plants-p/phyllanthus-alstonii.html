Phyllanthus alstonii

Phyllanthus alstonii, commonly known as the African date palm, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its fruit is a fleshy, yellowish drupe that contains a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry woodlands.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
