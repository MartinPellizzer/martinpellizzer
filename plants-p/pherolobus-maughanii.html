Pherolobus maughanii

Pherolobus maughanii, commonly known as the Maughan's ghost orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and delicate, translucent flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its unique morphology and restricted distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and limited natural propagation, Pherolobus maughanii is considered endangered and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for preservation.
