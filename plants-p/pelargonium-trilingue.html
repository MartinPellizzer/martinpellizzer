Pelargonium trilingue

Pelargonium trilingue, commonly known as the Trinidadian geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Trinidad.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features rounded, velvety leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in containers or hanging baskets.

Pelargonium trilingue is also valued for its role in traditional landscaping and as a source of cut flowers.
