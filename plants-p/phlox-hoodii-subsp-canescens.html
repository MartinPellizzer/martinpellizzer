Phlox hoodii subsp. canescens

Phlox hoodii subsp.

canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf phlox, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of pink to lavender flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.

The subspecies canescens is distinguished by its fuzzy, silver leaves and smaller flowers compared to other phlox species.
