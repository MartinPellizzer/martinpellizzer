Patagonium obcordatum

Patagonium obcordatum, commonly known as the Patagonian water lily, is a perennial aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the surface of freshwater bodies. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in the mud and absorbing nutrients from the water.

Patagonium obcordatum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
