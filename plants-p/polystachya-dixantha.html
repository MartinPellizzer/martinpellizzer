Polystachya dixantha

Polystachya dixantha, commonly known as the dixantha bromeliad, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central water-holding structure. The plant produces striking, inflorescence-like structures that bear small, white to pale pink flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a waxy coating, which helps in water retention.

Polystachya dixantha is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
