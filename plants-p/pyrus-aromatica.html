Pyrus aromatica

Pyrus aromatica, commonly known as the sweet cherry tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to southwest Asia and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces sweet, juicy fruits that are typically harvested in the summer months. Pyrus aromatica is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its edible fruit.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight to thrive.
