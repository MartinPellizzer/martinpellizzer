Pteronia stoehelinoides

Pteronia stoehelinoides, commonly known as the Stoechlin's pterony, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and resilience.
