Pilosella galactina

Pilosella galactina, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features dense, silver-white hairs on its leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive, silvery appearance. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, streambanks, and woodland edges.

Pilosella galactina is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and astringent qualities.
