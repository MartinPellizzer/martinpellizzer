Polystachya galeata

Polystachya galeata, commonly known as the Galeata orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clustered inflorescence. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Polystachya galeata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
