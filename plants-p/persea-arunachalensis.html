Persea arunachalensis

Persea arunachalensis, commonly known as the Arunachal persimmon, is a species of evergreen tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible and have a sweet, astringent flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in dense forests at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters.

This plant is valued not only for its fruit but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
