Piper dulce

Piper dulce, commonly known as the sweet pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that turn from green to red as they mature. These berries have a sweet, peppery flavor and are used in various culinary and medicinal applications.

Piper dulce is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its unique fruit.
