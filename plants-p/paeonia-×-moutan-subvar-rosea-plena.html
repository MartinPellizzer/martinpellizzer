Paeonia × moutan subvar. rosea-plena

Paeonia × moutan subvar. rosea-plena, commonly known as the peony, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant produces compound, pinnately arranged leaves that are dark green and velvety in texture. Its roots are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing nutrients and water.

This variety is particularly valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
