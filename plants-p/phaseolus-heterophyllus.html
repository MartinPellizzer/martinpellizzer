Phaseolus heterophyllus

Phaseolus heterophyllus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a leguminous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers, followed by elongated pods containing edible beans. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
