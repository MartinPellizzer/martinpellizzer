Pavonia haematophthalmos

Pavonia haematophthalmos, commonly known as the blood-eyed pavonia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive red or orange flowers that resemble eyes, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three leaflets, and the flowers are borne in clusters.

Pavonia haematophthalmos is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
