Polystachya foliosa

Polystachya foliosa, commonly known as the feather-leaf torchflower, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its long, slender leaves that resemble feathers, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a tall, branched stem bearing numerous small, white to pale yellow flowers.

Polystachya foliosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
