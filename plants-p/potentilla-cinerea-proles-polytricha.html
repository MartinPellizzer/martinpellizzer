Potentilla cinerea proles polytricha

Potentilla cinerea proles polytricha, commonly known as the silver cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It features silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
