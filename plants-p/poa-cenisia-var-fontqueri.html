Poa cenisia var. fontqueri

Poa cenisia var. fontqueri, commonly known as the Fontqueri's bentgrass, is a low-growing grass species native to the western United States.

It typically forms dense, mat-like turf and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to tolerate salt spray and wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in coastal environments.
