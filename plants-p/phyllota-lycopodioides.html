Phyllota lycopodioides

Phyllota lycopodioides, commonly known as the common lycopod, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces tiny, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
