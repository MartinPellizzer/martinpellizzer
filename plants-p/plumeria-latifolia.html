Plumeria latifolia

Plumeria latifolia, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that come in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves and produces milky sap when damaged. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine.

The flowers are typically borne on long, slender peduncles and bloom during the dry season.
