Philippia multiglandulosa

Philippia multiglandulosa, commonly known as the hairy philippia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
