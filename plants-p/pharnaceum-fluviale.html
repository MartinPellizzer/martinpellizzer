Pharnaceum fluviale

Pharnaceum fluviale, commonly known as the river pharnaceum, is a unique aquatic plant found in slow-moving freshwater environments.

It has elongated, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, providing habitat for small aquatic organisms. The plant's roots are anchored in the substrate, helping to stabilize the sediment and prevent erosion. It blooms with small, pale yellow flowers that are often submerged, making it difficult to spot.

Pharnaceum fluviale plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of its aquatic ecosystem.
