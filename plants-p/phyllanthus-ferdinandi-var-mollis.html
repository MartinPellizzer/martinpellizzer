Phyllanthus ferdinandi var. mollis

Phyllanthus ferdinandi var. mollis, commonly known as the soft-leaved spurred gooseberry, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are soft to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is characterized by its unique leaf structure and adaptability to various soil types.
