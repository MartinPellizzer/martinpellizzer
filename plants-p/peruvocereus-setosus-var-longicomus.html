Peruvocereus setosus var. longicomus

Peruvocereus setosus var. longicomus, commonly known as the long-spined cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are covered in long, sharp spines, which provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically blooming at night. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert environments.

Due to its striking appearance and unique adaptations, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and xerophytic gardens.
