Pancratium coccineum

Pancratium coccineum, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features long, narrow leaves and produces showy, red-orange flowers with a distinctive spider-like appearance. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its bulbs are toxic, containing compounds that can be harmful if ingested.

Pancratium coccineum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
