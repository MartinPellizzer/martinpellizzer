Phyllactis regularis

Phyllactis regularis, commonly known as the common liverleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has distinctive, kidney-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the spring and summer months.

Its leaves and stems are sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies, though its medicinal properties remain understudied.
