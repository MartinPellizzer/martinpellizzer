Phyllanthus frodinii

Phyllanthus frodinii, commonly known as the African spurge, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Phyllanthus frodinii is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
