Piper pilgeri

Piper pilgeri, commonly known as the Pilger's pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal spikes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns from green to red as it matures.
