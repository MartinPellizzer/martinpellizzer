Pseuderanthemum stenostachyum

Pseuderanthemum stenostachyum, commonly known as the narrow-stalked pseuderanthemum, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of small, violet or purple flowers that bloom in the late afternoon and emit a mild fragrance. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its vibrant floral display.
