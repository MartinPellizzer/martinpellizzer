Poa tateyamensis

Poa tateyamensis, commonly known as the Tateyama grass, is a rare species native to Japan.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountainous regions and forest understories. Its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family.

Poa tateyamensis is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
