Plantago julieti

Plantago julieti, commonly known as Juliet's plantain, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are borne on slender stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
