Petrea volubilis var. alba

Petrea volubilis var. alba, commonly known as the white petrea, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to cover trellises or walls.

The species is also noted for its fragrant blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
