Peperomia hirta

Peperomia hirta, commonly known as the hairy peperomia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in fine, short hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia hirta is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its compact size and ease of care.
