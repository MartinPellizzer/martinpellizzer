Passiflora ornata

Passiflora ornata, commonly known as the crown of thorns, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown-like structure of filaments surrounding the reproductive organs. The plant produces a yellowish or orange fruit that contains numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing as a climbing vine or shrub.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and botanical collections.
