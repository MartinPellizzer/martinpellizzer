Pleurothallis cordatifolia

Pleurothallis cordatifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters along the stem. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on tree branches or rocks.

Due to its unique appearance and delicate blooms, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
