Pictetia spinifolia

Pictetia spinifolia, commonly known as the spiny leaf cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, segmented trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in small, sharp spines. Native to Madagascar, this plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil. Its slow growth rate and long lifespan make it a rare and valuable addition to botanical collections.

The plant's distinctive appearance and ecological significance contribute to its importance in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
