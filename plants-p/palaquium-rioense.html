Palaquium rioense

Palaquium rioense, commonly known as the Rio Grande wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are similar in appearance to small apples. Its fruits are valued for their sweet, acidic flavor and are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The tree is also appreciated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
