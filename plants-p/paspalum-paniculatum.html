Paspalum paniculatum

Paspalum paniculatum, commonly known as the panicled paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Paspalum paniculatum is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to different environments.
