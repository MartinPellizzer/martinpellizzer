Pfaffia sericea

Pfaffia sericea, commonly known as Brazilian ginseng, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Pfaffia sericea is valued for its adaptogenic properties and is often cultivated for commercial use.
