Paspalum varians

Paspalum varians, commonly known as variable grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and disturbed areas.

Paspalum varians is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
