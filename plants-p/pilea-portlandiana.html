Pilea portlandiana

Pilea portlandiana, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its round, glossy, waxy leaves that resemble small coins, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular houseplant.

Pilea portlandiana is relatively low maintenance and can be propagated through stem cuttings or division.
