Poa pratensis var. laxiflora

Poa pratensis var. laxiflora, commonly known as the looseflower meadow grass, is a variant of the common meadow grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its flowering spikes that are more loosely arranged compared to the typical variety. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of ecological and ornamental landscapes.
