Pelaphia laurifolia

Pelaphia laurifolia, commonly known as the fragrant laurel.

It is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its aromatic, dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by small, round berries. Pelaphia laurifolia thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its foliage is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
