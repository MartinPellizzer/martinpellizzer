Phaseolus marmoreus

Phaseolus marmoreus, commonly known as the marble bean, is a species of legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, mottled leaves that resemble marble, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many important food crops.
