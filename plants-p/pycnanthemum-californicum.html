Pycnanthemum californicum

Pycnanthemum californicum, commonly known as California mountain geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pycnanthemum californicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid environments.
