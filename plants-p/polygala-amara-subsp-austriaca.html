Polygala amara subsp. austriaca

Polygala amara subsp.

austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian milk-vetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
