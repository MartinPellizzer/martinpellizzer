Pimpinella platyphylla

Pimpinella platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved茴香, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, feather-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its seeds are small and can be used as a spice, similar to caraway or cumin.

Pimpinella platyphylla thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
