Paspalum longicilium

Paspalum longicilium, commonly known as the long-bearded grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and long, narrow leaves with a distinctive silver-gray sheen. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in wetland areas, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Paspalum longicilium is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
