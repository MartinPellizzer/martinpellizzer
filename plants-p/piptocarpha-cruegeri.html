Piptocarpha cruegeri

Piptocarpha cruegeri, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. Its root system is deep and drought-tolerant, allowing it to survive in harsh desert conditions.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance in its native habitat.
