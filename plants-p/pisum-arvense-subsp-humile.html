Pisum arvense subsp. humile

Pisum arvense subsp.

humile, commonly known as the dwarf field pea, is a low-growing legume native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically reaches heights of 10–30 cm and features trifoliate leaves with small, pea-like flowers.

The plant produces pods that contain 2–5 small, edible seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in open, sunny environments.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes cultivated as a cover crop or for forage.
