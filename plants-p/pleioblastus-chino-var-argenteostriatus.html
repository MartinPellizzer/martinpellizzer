Pleioblastus chino var. argenteostriatus

Pleioblastus chino var. argenteostriatus, commonly known as the silver-striped Chinese bamboo, is a variant of the Chinese bamboo species characterized by its striking silvery-white streaks on its green leaves.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to eastern Asia, particularly China. The plant is a clump-forming bamboo that grows to a moderate height, typically reaching up to 3 meters. Its foliage is narrow and elongated, with a distinctive pattern of silver stripes that add visual interest to gardens.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
