Porophyllum holwayanum

Porophyllum holwayanum, commonly known as the Holway's ragged robin, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in drought conditions. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
