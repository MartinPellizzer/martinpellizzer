Papyrus antiquorum

Papyrus antiquorum, commonly known as ancient papyrus, is a reed-like plant native to the Nile Delta region of Egypt.

It has long, narrow leaves that can grow up to several meters in length, making it ideal for making paper in ancient times. The plant thrives in wet, marshy environments and requires a lot of water to grow. Its fibrous stalks were processed by soaking, pounding, and pressing to create a smooth surface for writing.

Today, it is primarily cultivated for historical and horticultural purposes rather than practical use.
