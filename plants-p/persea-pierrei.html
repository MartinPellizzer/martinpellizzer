Persea pierrei

Persea pierrei, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Mexico and Central America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically harvested when fully ripe, characterized by their orange to reddish color. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface, while the bark is smooth and dark in color.

It is valued for its edible fruits and has some ornamental value due to its attractive foliage.
