Protea triandra

Protea triandra, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of flowers, with a central cone-like structure surrounded by a dense array of pink to white petals. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is typically found in fynbos vegetation. It is known for its long-lasting blooms, making it a popular choice for floral arrangements.

Protea triandra plays an important ecological role, providing nectar for various pollinators such as birds and insects.
