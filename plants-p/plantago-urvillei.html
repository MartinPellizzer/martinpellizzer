Plantago urvillei

Plantago urvillei, commonly known as the Urville's plantain, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for its healing and anti-inflammatory effects.
