Passiflora mayana

Passiflora mayana, commonly known as the Maya passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that display a blend of blue, purple, and white hues. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Passiflora mayana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
