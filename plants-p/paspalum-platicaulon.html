Paspalum platicaulon

Paspalum platicaulon, commonly known as the flat-stemmed grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, flat stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family.

Paspalum platicaulon is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
