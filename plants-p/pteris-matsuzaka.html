Pteris matsuzaka

Pteris matsuzaka, commonly known as the Matsuzaka water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Japan, China, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often covered in a fine layer of white, powdery spores. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in forest undergrowth or near water sources.

The fronds are usually bright green and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters, making it a visually appealing and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
