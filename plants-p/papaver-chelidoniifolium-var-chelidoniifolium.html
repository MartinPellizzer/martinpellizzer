Papaver chelidoniifolium var. chelidoniifolium

Papaver chelidoniifolium var. chelidoniifolium, commonly known as the yellow poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking yellow-orange flowers with a papery, delicate texture, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often adorned with wavy margins and a hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and historical use in traditional medicine.
