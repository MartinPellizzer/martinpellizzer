Pteris cuspidata

Pteris cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that give it a delicate, lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense layer of root hairs, which aid in water absorption.

Pteris cuspidata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
