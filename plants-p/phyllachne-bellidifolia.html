Phyllachne bellidifolia

Phyllachne bellidifolia, commonly known as the bellidifolia grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces delicate, spike-like inflorescences with tiny white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
