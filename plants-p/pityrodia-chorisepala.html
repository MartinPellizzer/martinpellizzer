Pityrodia chorisepala

Pityrodia chorisepala, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Pittosporaceae and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are often clustered in umbels. These flowers are followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature.

It is typically found in coastal and lowland forest habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
