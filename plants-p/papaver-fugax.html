Papaver fugax

Papaver fugax, commonly known as the transient poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its capsule fruits contain numerous small, elliptical seeds.

Papaver fugax is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting pollinators.
