Pavonia patuliloba

Pavonia patuliloba, commonly known as the broadleaf false mallow, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 2 to 3 feet tall. The plant produces showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with serrated edges and a rough texture.

Pavonia patuliloba is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as woodland edges and stream banks.
