Piper tajumulcoanum

Piper tajumulcoanum, commonly known as the Tajumulco pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America, particularly in southern Mexico and northern Guatemala. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of small, heart-shaped leaves. Its fruits are small, green berries that turn red when mature, and they are used in local cuisines for their pungent flavor.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
