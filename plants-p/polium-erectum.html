Polium erectum

Polium erectum, commonly known as upright grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Polium erectum is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ecological role in supporting insect populations.
