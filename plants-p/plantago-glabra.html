Plantago glabra

Plantago glabra, commonly known as broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are ribbed and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a dense spike, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including roadsides, meadows, and disturbed soils.

The leaves and seeds of Plantago glabra have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
