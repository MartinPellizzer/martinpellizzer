Piper molle

Piper molle, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries. The berries are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
