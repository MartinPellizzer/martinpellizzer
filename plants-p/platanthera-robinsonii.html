Platanthera robinsonii

Platanthera robinsonii, commonly known as Robinson's lady's tresses, is a rare orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and its distinctive, fragrant, white to pale pink flower spikes that resemble a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils along stream banks and in shaded woodlands. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered in many areas.
