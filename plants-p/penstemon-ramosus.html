Penstemon ramosus

Penstemon ramosus, commonly known as the slender beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in dry, open habitats such as meadows and slopes. It has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem.

Penstemon ramosus is a perennial species that blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
