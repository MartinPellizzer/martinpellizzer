Piptatherum pungens

Piptatherum pungens, commonly known as the sharpleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, sharp-edged leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Piptatherum pungens plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
