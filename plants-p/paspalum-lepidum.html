Paspalum lepidum

Paspalum lepidum, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along waterways. Its small, spike-like inflorescences produce wind-pollinated flowers, and it is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

Paspalum lepidum is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
