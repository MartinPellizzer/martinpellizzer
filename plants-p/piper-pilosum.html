Piper pilosum

Piper pilosum, commonly known as the hairy pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature.

The plant is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
