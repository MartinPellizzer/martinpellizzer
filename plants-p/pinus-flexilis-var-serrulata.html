Pinus flexilis var. serrulata

Pinus flexilis var. serrulata, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada bristlecone pine, is a variant of the flexible pine native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaf margins and slow growth rate, which contributes to its longevity. The plant typically grows in high-elevation environments, often in rocky soils, and is adapted to cold, dry climates. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is notable for its resilience and is a symbol of ecological endurance in mountainous regions.
