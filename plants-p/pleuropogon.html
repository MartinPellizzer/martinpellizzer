Pleuropogon

Pleuropogon, commonly known as the grassland bluemouth, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

These plants are native to North America and are typically found in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. They are characterized by their slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture.

Pleuropogon species are often used in native plant gardens due to their ornamental value and ecological benefits.
