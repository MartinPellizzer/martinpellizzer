Pinanga spectabilis

Pinanga spectabilis, commonly known as the ornamental bamboo or giant bamboo, is a striking plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers followed by distinctive, elongated fruits. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Pinanga spectabilis is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
