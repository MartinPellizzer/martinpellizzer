Pirigara alata

Pirigara alata, commonly known as the winged spur orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated petals that resemble wings. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often near rivers or in dense forests. Its flowers are usually a striking combination of yellow and purple, with a distinctive spur-like structure at the base of the petals.

Pirigara alata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its rare and exotic appearance.
