Piper remotinervium

Piper remotinervium, commonly known as the slender pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, opposite leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

The botanical features of Piper remotinervium make it an interesting subject for taxonomic and ecological studies in its native habitat.
