Potentilla verticillaris f. acutipetala

Potentilla verticillaris f. acutipetala, commonly known as the acute-leaved potentilla, is a compact, herbaceous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves that are sharply pointed, giving it its specific epithet "acutipetala.". The plant produces clusters of delicate, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine landscapes.

Its ornamental value lies in its compact growth habit and vibrant floral display.
