Pelargonium × implicatum

Pelargonium × implicatum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, slightly lobed leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It is native to South Africa and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and hanging baskets.

Pelargonium × implicatum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of conditions, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
