Papaver rhoeas var. serratifolium

Papaver rhoeas var. serratifolium, commonly known as the serrated poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its serrated leaf margins.

This plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides. It produces striking red-orange flowers with a white center, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive toothed edge, contributing to its botanical uniqueness.

It is an annual plant that is relatively easy to cultivate and has historical uses in medicine and dye production.
