Piper mirabile

Piper mirabile, commonly known as the sacred pepper or miracle pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, containing tiny seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies for their purported medicinal properties.

The plant's botanical features include its climbing habit and distinctive, heart-shaped leaves.
