Peperidia subpeltata

Peperidia subpeltata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows as a shrub with woody stems and hairy foliage. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive central disc surrounded by petal-like bracts.

Peperidia subpeltata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry, well-drained soils.
