Persicaria amphibia var. stipulacea

Persicaria amphibia var. stipulacea, commonly known as the water plantain, is a perennial herb native to wetland environments.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along stream banks.

Its adaptability to aquatic and semi-aquatic conditions makes it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
