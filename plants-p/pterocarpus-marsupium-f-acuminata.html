Pterocarpus marsupium f. acuminata

Pterocarpus marsupium f. acuminata, commonly known as the Indian satinwood, is a variant of the Indian rosewood tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its distinctive, acutely pointed leaflets and dark, durable wood that is highly valued in traditional craftsmanship. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure, with small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being elliptic to ovate in shape, and the plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
