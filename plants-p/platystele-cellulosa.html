Platystele cellulosa

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, typically in humid tropical environments. The plant features a short, leafless stem with a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water and nutrients. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in a spike, and it produces minute, capsule-like fruits.

Due to its specialized habitat and limited distribution, Platystele cellulosa is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
