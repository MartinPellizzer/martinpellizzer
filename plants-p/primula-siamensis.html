Primula siamensis

Primula siamensis, commonly known as the Siam primrose, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

Primula siamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
