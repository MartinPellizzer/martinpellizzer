Polygala vulgaris subsp. dubia

Polygala vulgaris subsp.

dubia, commonly known as the common milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant produces small, yellowish or greenish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are opposite and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is characterized by its distinctive seed pods that split open to release its seeds.
