Pisonia rosea

Pisonia rosea, commonly known as the pink pisonia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal areas and disturbed soils. The plant is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting birds and insects. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

Pisonia rosea is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
