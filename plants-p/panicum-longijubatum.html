Panicum longijubatum

Panicum longijubatum, commonly known as the long-haired panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, silky hairs that cover its leaves and inflorescence. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, disturbed areas, and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
