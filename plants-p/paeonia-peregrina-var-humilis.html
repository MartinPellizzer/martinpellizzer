Paeonia peregrina var. humilis

Paeonia peregrina var. humilis, commonly known as the Persian peony, is a rare and ancient species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant features large, palmately compound leaves and has a fibrous root system adapted to arid conditions. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional medicine.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and requires careful conservation efforts.
