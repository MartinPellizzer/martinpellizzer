Pellaea boivinii f. bipinnata

Pellaea boivinii f. bipinnata, commonly known as the bipinnate rock fern, is a unique fern species characterized by its finely divided, bipinnate fronds.

It is native to the Andes Mountains of South America, where it thrives in rocky, high-altitude environments. The plant's fronds are typically green and leathery, with a compact growth habit that helps it withstand harsh climatic conditions. It is often found in crevices and on rock faces, where it forms dense, low-growing colonies.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
