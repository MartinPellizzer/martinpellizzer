Piper fagifolium

Piper fagifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into berries. Its fruit is edible and has a peppery flavor, making it used in some traditional cuisines.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
