Panax pseudoginseng var. angustifolius

Panax pseudoginseng var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaf ginseng, is a member of the Araliaceae family and is native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant produces red berries containing several seeds, and its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine. It is often confused with Panax ginseng due to similar appearance and medicinal properties.

This variant is valued for its potential adaptogenic and tonic effects in herbal remedies.
