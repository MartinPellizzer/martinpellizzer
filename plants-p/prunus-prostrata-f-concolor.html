Prunus prostrata f. concolor

Prunus prostrata f. concolor, commonly known as the variegated dwarf cherry, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to China.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically variegated with white or yellow margins, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, followed by red or black berries in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Its compact size and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
