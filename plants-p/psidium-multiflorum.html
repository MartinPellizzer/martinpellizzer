Psidium multiflorum

Psidium multiflorum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or white guava, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, yellow or orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in tropical and subtropical regions.
