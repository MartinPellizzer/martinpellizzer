Pedicularis bietii

Pedicularis bietii, commonly known as the Biet's lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Pedicularis, which includes over 450 species of flowering plants commonly referred to as louseworts. This particular species is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to red, and its hairy, glandular leaves. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or moist soils at high elevations.

The plant's botanical features are adapted to its harsh, cold climate, with a short growing season and specialized structures for pollination and seed dispersal.
