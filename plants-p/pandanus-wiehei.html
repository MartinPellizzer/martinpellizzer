Pandanus wiehei

Pandanus wiehei, commonly known as the Hainan screw pine, is a species of flowering plant in the pandan family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Hainan Island in China. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing at the base of the plant.

Pandanus wiehei is well adapted to coastal and saline environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
