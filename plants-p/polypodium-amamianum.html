Polypodium amamianum

Polypodium amamianum, commonly known as the Amami island shield fern, is a species of fern native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest undergrowth or on rocky surfaces. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters, a common feature among ferns.

Polypodium amamianum plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat by providing shelter and food for various small organisms.
