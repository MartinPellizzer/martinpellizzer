Psilanthus melanocarpus

Psilanthus melanocarpus, commonly known as the black-fruited psilanthus, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces dark, glossy berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural and research purposes.
