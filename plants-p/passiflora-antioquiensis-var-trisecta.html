Passiflora antioquiensis var. trisecta

Passiflora antioquiensis var. trisecta, commonly known as the Antioquian passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Colombia.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The leaves are typically trifoliate or trisect, giving the plant its specific variety name. It produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral displays.
