Pteroceras incurvicalcar

Pteroceras incurvicalcar, commonly known as the curved shield fern, is a member of the Pteridaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, bioluminescent fronds that emit a faint glow in the dark. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its unique structure includes a shield-like rhizome that helps anchor it to rocky surfaces.

The species is notable for its rare occurrence and is of interest to botanists studying bioluminescent plants.
