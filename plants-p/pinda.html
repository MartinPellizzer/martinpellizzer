Pinda

Pinda, commonly known as the wild pinda or *Pindia* in some regions, is a shrub belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and waxy, paddle-shaped leaves. Its fruit, which resembles a capsule, contains several seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The botanical features of pinda make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
