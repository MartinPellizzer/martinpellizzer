Prosaptia serriformis

Prosaptia serriformis, commonly known as the serrated leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive serrated leaf margins. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in shades of white and yellow, and bloom during the wet season.

Prosaptia serriformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
