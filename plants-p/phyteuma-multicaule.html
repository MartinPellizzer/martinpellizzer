Phyteuma multicaule

Phyteuma multicaule, commonly known as the clustered phyteuma, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is noted for its ability to form dense clumps, which contribute to its ornamental value in horticultural settings.
