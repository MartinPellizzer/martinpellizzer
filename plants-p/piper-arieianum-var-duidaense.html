Piper arieianum var. duidaense

Piper arieianum var. duidaense, commonly known as the Duida pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in the Amazon rainforest regions of Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its fruits are small berries that turn from green to red when mature, and they are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of the tropical flora and a subject of interest in ethnobotanical research.
