Peristrophe brevibractea

Peristrophe brevibractea, commonly known as the small-bract peristrophe, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces inflorescences that consist of tightly packed spikelets, typical of many grasses. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ornamental grass.
