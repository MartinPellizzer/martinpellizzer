Prunus incisa f. watanabei

Prunus incisa f. watanabei, commonly known as the Watanabei cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that turn a striking red or orange in the autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark red, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its unique foliage and seasonal color changes, it is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
