Pyrus pangiensis

Pyrus pangiensis, commonly known as the Pangi pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in parts of Myanmar and Thailand. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, with lance-shaped leaves and white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like berry, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

The Pangi pear is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its use in local cuisines.
