Phelipanche libanotica

Phelipanche libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanese morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces striking blue or violet flowers, typically blooming in the early morning. The plant has a slender, branching stem and heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread across suitable habitats.
