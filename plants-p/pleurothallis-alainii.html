Pleurothallis alainii

Pleurothallis alainii, commonly known as the Alain's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that often resemble a slipper. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and a delicate, fragrant flower with a unique pouch-like structure.

Pleurothallis alainii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
