Plumeria mariaelenae

Plumeria mariaelenae, commonly known as the Mariaelenae frangipani, is a flowering plant native to the island of Mo'orea in French Polynesia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant produces large, waxy leaves and has a woody, branching stem. It thrives in tropical climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
