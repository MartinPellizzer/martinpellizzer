Pelargonium × incanum

Pelargonium × incanum, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fuzzy grayish-white hair covering the stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is widely cultivated in gardens around the world.
