Persea cicatricosa

Persea cicatricosa, commonly known as the scarred persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a smooth, orange-red berry that ripens in the fall and is edible when fully mature.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and fruit.
