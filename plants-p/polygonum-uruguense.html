Polygonum uruguense

Polygonum uruguense, commonly known as the Uruguay knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and often triangular stems. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Polygonum uruguense is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it an invasive species in some regions.
