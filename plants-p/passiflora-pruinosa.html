Passiflora pruinosa

Passiflora pruinosa, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and contain seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a soft, velvety texture.

Passiflora pruinosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates.
