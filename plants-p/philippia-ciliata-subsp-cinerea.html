Philippia ciliata subsp. cinerea

Philippia ciliata subsp.

cinerea, commonly known as the silver-haired philippia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, silver-green leaves with ciliate margins.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
