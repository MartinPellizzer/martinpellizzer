Psephellus colchicus var. pinnatipartitus

Psephellus colchicus var. pinnatipartitus, commonly known as the fringed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive fringed margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both horticultural and ecological landscapes.
