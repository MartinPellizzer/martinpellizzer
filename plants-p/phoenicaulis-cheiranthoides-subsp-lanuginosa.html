Phoenicaulis cheiranthoides subsp. lanuginosa

Phoenicaulis cheiranthoides subsp.

lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly-flowered wallflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

The flowers are typically bright yellow or orange, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, typical of the Brassicaceae family. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
