Panicum rubiginosum

Panicum rubiginosum, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and reddish-brown inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, often forming dense stands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Panicum rubiginosum is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
