Pterospermum havilandii

Pterospermum havilandii, commonly known as the Haviland's pterospermum, is a deciduous shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Bombacaceae and is characterized by its palmate leaves and showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody capsules containing numerous seeds with tufted hair, facilitating wind dispersal. Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
