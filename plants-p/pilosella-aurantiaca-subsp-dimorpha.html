Pilosella aurantiaca subsp. dimorpha

Pilosella aurantiaca subsp.

dimorpha, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies dimorpha is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology compared to other subspecies of Pilosella aurantiaca.
