Potamogeton vaginatus

Potamogeton vaginatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, green flowers that emerge from the water surface. The plant is characterized by its smooth, cylindrical stems and the presence of a leaf sheath at the base of each leaf. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton vaginatus is often found in ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams.
