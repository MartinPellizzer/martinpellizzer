Palmijuncus brevifrons

Palmijuncus brevifrons, commonly known as the short-nosed palm civet, is a unique plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and dense foliage, which helps it thrive in humid, tropical environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed, which is dispersed by animals.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, Palmijuncus brevifrons is considered a vulnerable species in its natural range.
