Piper camptostachys var. cuabanum

Piper camptostachys var. cuabanum, commonly known as Cuabanum pepper or Cuabanum long pepper, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, curved fruits that turn red when mature. The fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, valued for their pungent aroma and potential medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ethnobotanical research.
