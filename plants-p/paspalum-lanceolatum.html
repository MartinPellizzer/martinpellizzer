Paspalum lanceolatum

Paspalum lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf buffalograss, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and tufted inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-like flowers. This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for dryland environments. It is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration and native landscaping.
