Piper glabrescens

Piper glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, slender fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some tropical regions.
