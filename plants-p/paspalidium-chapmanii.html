Paspalidium chapmanii

Paspalidium chapmanii, commonly known as Chapman's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in restoration projects due to its adaptability.
