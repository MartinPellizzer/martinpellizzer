Papaver dubium var. schweinfurthii

Papaver dubium var. schweinfurthii, commonly known as the Schweinfurth's poppy, is a variant of the common poppy native to Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
