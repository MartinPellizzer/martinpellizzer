Porana truncata

Porana truncata, commonly known as the narrowleaf porana, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
