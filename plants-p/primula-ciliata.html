Primula ciliata

Primula ciliata, commonly known as the silverleaf primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of hairy, silver-tomentose leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
