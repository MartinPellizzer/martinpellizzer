Pelargonium triangulare

Pelargonium triangulare, commonly known as the triangular geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant is typically grown as an ornamental in gardens and is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun. It produces small, round fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Pelargonium triangulare is also valued for its aromatic foliage, which can be used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
