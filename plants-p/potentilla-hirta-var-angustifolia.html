Potentilla hirta var. angustifolia

Potentilla hirta var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, soft hairs, and its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation areas.
