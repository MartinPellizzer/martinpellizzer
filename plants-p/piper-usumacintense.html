Piper usumacintense

Piper usumacintense, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or yellow pepper plant, is a climbing shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow or orange berries that contain a spicy, peppery compound. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, green flowers that develop into the edible fruits. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The botanical features of Piper usumacintense make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
