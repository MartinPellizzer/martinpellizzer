Prunus cerasus f. rhexii

Prunus cerasus f. rhexii, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or compact cherry, is a cultivar of the common cherry.

It is characterized by its small, compact growth habit, making it ideal for small gardens or container planting. The plant produces small, sweet fruits that are typically harvested in late spring. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to Europe.

Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it blooms with pinkish-white flowers in early spring.
