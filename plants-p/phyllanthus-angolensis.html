Phyllanthus angolensis

Phyllanthus angolensis, commonly known as African date palm, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its dense, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
