Packera scalaris var. parrasiana

Packera scalaris var. parrasiana, commonly known as the Parry's groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow composite flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a flat-topped inflorescence. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its showy flowers and medicinal properties in some species.
