Pennellianthus frutescens

Pennellianthus frutescens, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a small, deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features dense clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It produces small, round fruits that split open to release seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
