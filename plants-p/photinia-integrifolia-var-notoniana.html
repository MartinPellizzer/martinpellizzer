Photinia integrifolia var. notoniana

Photinia integrifolia var. notoniana, commonly known as the Notonian firethorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright red berries that are a striking feature in the winter months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for hedges and ornamental gardens.
