Paracalanthe torifera

Paracalanthe torifera, commonly known as the ridged calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces striking, lance-shaped leaves with prominent midribs. The plant typically blooms with small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, arranged in a racemose inflorescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, loose soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
