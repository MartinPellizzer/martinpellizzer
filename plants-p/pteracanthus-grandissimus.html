Pteracanthus grandissimus

Pteracanthus grandissimus, commonly known as the giant feather fern, is a remarkable species of fern characterized by its large, feathery fronds.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant exhibits a unique rhizomatous growth habit, with long, creeping underground stems that support its expansive foliage. Its fronds are bi-directionally divided, creating a complex, lace-like appearance that enhances its ornamental value.

This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.
