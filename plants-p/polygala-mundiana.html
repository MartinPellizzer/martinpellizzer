Polygala mundiana

Polygala mundiana, commonly known as the African milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often resemble those of other legumes. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are alternate and trifoliate, while its fruit is a dry, dehiscent capsule that releases seeds upon maturity.

Polygala mundiana is often found in open grasslands and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
