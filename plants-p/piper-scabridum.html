Piper scabridum

Piper scabridum, commonly known as the rough pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, clustered flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Piper scabridum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
