Panicum pauciflorum var. fastigiatum

Panicum pauciflorum var. fastigiatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf dropseed, is a low-growing, grass-like perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, grassy leaves that are often tinged with silver or blue, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fluffy panicles of seeds that are released in late summer, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it an excellent choice for native gardens and erosion control.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate foliage and ability to provide habitat for beneficial insects.
