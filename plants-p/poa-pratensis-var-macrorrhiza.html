Poa pratensis var. macrorrhiza

Poa pratensis var. macrorrhiza, commonly known as the large-rooted meadow grass, is a variant of the common tall fescue.

It is characterized by its robust growth habit and deep root system, which allows it to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. This plant is often found in temperate regions, particularly in meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers, contributing to its role in ecological and agricultural systems.
