Papaver pseudovenustum

Papaver pseudovenustum, commonly known as the false poppy, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Papaveraceae family, which includes other well-known genera such as Papaver and Argemone. The plant is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, often with a distinctive central stigma. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed and covered in a fine, silky hair, while its stems are slender and often branched.

The seeds of Papaver pseudovenustum are small and hard, often dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
