Pulicaria discoidea

Pulicaria discoidea, commonly known as the woolly tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. The plant features silver-gray, hairy leaves and yellow flower heads that bloom from late spring to early summer. Its structure consists of a low-growing stem with basal leaves and a compact rosette formation.

Pulicaria discoidea is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
