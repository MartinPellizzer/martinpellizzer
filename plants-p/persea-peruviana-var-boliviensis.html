Persea peruviana var. boliviensis

Persea peruviana var. boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Andean regions of Bolivia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a large, pear-shaped drupe with a thick, smooth skin that turns from green to yellow or orange when ripe. The pulp is creamy and rich in oils, making it a valuable source of nutrition and a key component in traditional cuisines.

This variant is distinguished by its adaptability to high-altitude environments and its unique flavor profile compared to other avocado species.
