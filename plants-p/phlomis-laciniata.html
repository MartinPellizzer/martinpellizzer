Phlomis laciniata

Phlomis laciniata, commonly known as narrow-leaved phlomis, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of purple or pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has deeply lobed, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Phlomis laciniata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
