Protea simplex

Protea simplex, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, fluffy white or pale pink flower heads, which are actually composed of many small flowers clustered together. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

Protea simplex is also an important species for local biodiversity and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
