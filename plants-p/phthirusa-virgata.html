Phthirusa virgata

Phthirusa virgata, commonly known as the Virgin's shield, is a small, annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by oval, woody fruits containing several seeds. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its name is derived from the shape of its fruit, which resembles a shield.
