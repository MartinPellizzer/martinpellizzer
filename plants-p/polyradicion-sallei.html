Polyradicion sallei

Polyradicion sallei, commonly known as the Salamander plant, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens across southern Europe.
