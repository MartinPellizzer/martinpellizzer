Polypodium virginianum f. churchiae

Polypodium virginianum f. churchiae, commonly known as the Church's maidenhair fern, is a fern species native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky outcrops. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce spore-bearing sorus clusters on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
