Pipturus polynesicus var. samoensis

Pipturus polynesicus var. samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan tree fern, is a species native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Samoa and nearby regions.

It is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in coastal forests and moist lowland areas. Its rhizome system allows it to anchor firmly in soil while absorbing nutrients from the surrounding environment.

The Samoan tree fern plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
