Prunus lusitanica monstr. marginata

Prunus lusitanica monstr.

marginata, commonly known as the Portuguese cherry or wild cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe. It features simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a distinctive red or orange bark that becomes flaky with age.

The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural use.
