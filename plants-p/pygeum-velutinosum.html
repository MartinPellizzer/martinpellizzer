Pygeum velutinosum

Pygeum velutinosum, commonly known as the velvet-leafed African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest in both botanical research and local horticulture.
