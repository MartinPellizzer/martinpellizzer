Phyllanthus tenuicaulis

Phyllanthus tenuicaulis, commonly known as slender-stemmed phyllanthus, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its small, alternate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
