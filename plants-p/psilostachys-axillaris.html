Psilostachys axillaris

Psilostachys axillaris, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often branched at the base, with small, narrow leaves arranged in a grass-like manner. Its flowering occurs in summer, producing small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
