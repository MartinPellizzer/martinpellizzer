Potentilla argentea subsp. dissecta

Potentilla argentea subsp.

dissecta, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It features finely dissected, silver-velvet leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its silvery foliage and long blooming period.
