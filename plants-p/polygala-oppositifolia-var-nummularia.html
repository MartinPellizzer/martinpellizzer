Polygala oppositifolia var. nummularia

Polygala oppositifolia var. nummularia, commonly known as the silverleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive spurred structure. The plant has narrow, silver-gray leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions with a deep root system and low water requirements.

This variety is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological resilience.
