Penstemon azureus var. angustissimus

Penstemon azureus var. angustissimus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blue mistflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of blue to purple tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface, and are arranged alternately along the stem. This variety thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native gardens.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
