Persicaria hydropiper subsp. laetivirens

Persicaria hydropiper subsp.

laetivirens, commonly known as water pepper, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments. It features slender, upright stems and small, ovate leaves that are often reddish in color.

The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, which are typically borne on spikes. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, stream banks, and other aquatic habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
