Potamogeton javanicus subsp. variifolius

Potamogeton javanicus subsp.

variifolius, commonly known as the variable-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia. It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as lakes, rivers, and ponds.

The plant is characterized by its long, slender stems and narrow, elongated leaves that vary in shape and size along the stem. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small fish and invertebrates.

It is often used in water gardens and pond restoration projects due to its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
