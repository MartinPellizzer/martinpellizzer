Phyllanthus micromalus

Phyllanthus micromalus, commonly known as the small-leaved phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are usually less than 1 centimeter in length, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, often with yellow or white petals.

The plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
