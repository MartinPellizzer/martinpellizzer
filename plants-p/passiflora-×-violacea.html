Passiflora × violacea

Passiflora × violacea, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a hybrid species native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking violet and white flowers, which feature a complex structure with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
