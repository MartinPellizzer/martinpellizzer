Paepalanthus bellus

Paepalanthus bellus, commonly known as the beautiful paepalanthus, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that resemble small flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives in the understory. Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
