Poterium verrucosum var. multicaule

Poterium verrucosum var. multicaule, commonly known as the hairy pottershield, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
