Prunella vulgaris var. nanhutashanense

Prunella vulgaris var. nanhutashanense, commonly known as the Nanhu-Tashan blueweed, is a variant of the common selfheal plant.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered blue or purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This variety is native to eastern Asia and is noted for its ornamental value in gardens.
