Phleum pratense var. longiciliatum

Phleum pratense var. longiciliatum, commonly known as tall oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, pale green to blue-green spikelets that are typically clustered in panicles, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed sites.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for livestock and habitat for various insects and small animals.
