Pinus tecunumanii

Pinus tecunumanii, commonly known as the Guatemalan pine, is a coniferous tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and needle-like leaves arranged in bundles of five. The tree produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate to subtropical climates and is often found in montane forests.

The wood of Pinus tecunumanii is valued for its strength and durability in construction and furniture-making.
