Ptychosperma calapparia

Ptychosperma calapparia, commonly known as the giant pandanus, is a large, tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces large, fragrant inflorescences that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in coastal ecosystems.
