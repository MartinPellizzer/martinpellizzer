Phyllodes tuberosa

Phyllodes tuberosa, commonly known as the sandhill wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves that are often mistaken for flowers due to their similar appearance. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant.

Phyllodes tuberosa plays an important role in stabilizing coastal and inland dunes.
