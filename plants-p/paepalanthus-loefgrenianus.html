Paepalanthus loefgrenianus

Paepalanthus loefgrenianus, commonly known as the loefgren's pincushion cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of spiny, globular stems that resemble a pincushion, with a distinctive yellowish-green coloration. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange, blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
