Pitavia dumosa

Pitavia dumosa, commonly known as the hairy pitava, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, branched structure and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when ripe, containing several seeds.

Pitavia dumosa is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or secondary forest areas.
