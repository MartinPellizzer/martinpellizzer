Peperomia tetraphylla

Peperomia tetraphylla, commonly known as the four-leaf peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, waxy leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape, often with a reddish or purplish tinge on the undersides. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate moisture.

Peperomia tetraphylla is appreciated for its ornamental foliage and is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
