Potentilla webberi

Potentilla webberi, commonly known as the Webber's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with multiple stems. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically divided into five lobes, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

Potentilla webberi is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
