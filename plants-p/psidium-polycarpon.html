Psidium polycarpon

Psidium polycarpon, commonly known as the Brazilian guava or wild guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Psidium polycarpon is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
