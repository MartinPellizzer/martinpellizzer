Pelargonium × locusta

Pelargonium × locusta, commonly known as the African hybrid geranium, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Pelargonium hortorum and Pelargonium capense.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and purple. The plant typically features broad, rounded leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.

Pelargonium × locusta is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
