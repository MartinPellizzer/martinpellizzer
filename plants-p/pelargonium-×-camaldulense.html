Pelargonium × camaldulense

Pelargonium × camaldulense, commonly known as the Camaldulense geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, double flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
