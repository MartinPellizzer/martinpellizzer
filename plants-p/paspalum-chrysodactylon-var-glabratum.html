Paspalum chrysodactylon var. glabratum

Paspalum chrysodactylon var. glabratum, commonly known as the silky paspalum, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, soft foliage and dense growth habit, which makes it suitable for ornamental and turfgrass applications. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade conditions. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences that are often overlooked but contribute to its ecological value.

It is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
