Polystichum caruifolium

Polystichum caruifolium, commonly known as the caraway fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the eastern United States.

It features a tall, slender rhizome with dense, dark green, bipinnate fronds that are often adorned with silky, white hairs. The fronds are typically 1 to 2 meters long and have a delicate, lace-like appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in naturalized landscapes.
