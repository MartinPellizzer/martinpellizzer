Pastinaca pyrenaica

Pastinaca pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean burdock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots are thick and fibrous, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Pastinaca pyrenaica thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to mountainous environments.
