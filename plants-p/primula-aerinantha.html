Primula aerinantha

Primula aerinantha, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of pubescence, which helps to retain moisture.

Primula aerinantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific horticultural requirements.
