Phyteuma spicatum var. caeruleum

Phyteuma spicatum var. caeruleum, commonly known as blue phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features showy, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Blue phyteuma is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
