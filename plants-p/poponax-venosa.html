Poponax venosa

Poponax venosa, commonly known as the veined poponax, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with distinct venation patterns. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
