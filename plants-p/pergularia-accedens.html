Pergularia accedens

Pergularia accedens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually blue or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and lanceolate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Pergularia accedens is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
