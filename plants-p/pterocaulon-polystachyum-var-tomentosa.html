Pterocaulon polystachyum var. tomentosa

Pterocaulon polystachyum var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly pterocaulon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests or rocky outcrops.
