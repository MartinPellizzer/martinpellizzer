Phacelia lutea var. typica

Phacelia lutea var. typica, commonly known as yellow phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it is often used in native plant gardens.

Phacelia lutea var. typica is a short-lived perennial that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
