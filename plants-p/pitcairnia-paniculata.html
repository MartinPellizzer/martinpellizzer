Pitcairnia paniculata

Pitcairnia paniculata, commonly known as the panicle pitcairnia, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. This plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a dense, water-holding cup at the center. The inflorescence consists of a tall, branched panicle with numerous small, showy flowers.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well adapted to grow in shaded, humid environments.
