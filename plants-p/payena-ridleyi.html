Payena ridleyi

Payena ridleyi, commonly known as the Malabar ironwood, is a tropical tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its dense, hard, and heavy wood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has a tall, straight trunk with a smooth, greyish-brown bark that is resistant to pests and diseases. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a leathery texture and a dark green upper surface.

Payena ridleyi also produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.
