Parthenocissus sichuanensis

Parthenocissus sichuanensis, commonly known as the Sichuan ivy, is a vigorous climbing vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, often with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. Its roots can be used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
