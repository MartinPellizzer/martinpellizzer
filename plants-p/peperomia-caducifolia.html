Peperomia caducifolia

Peperomia caducifolia, commonly known as the shedding-leaf peperomia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and waxy, ovate leaves that often have a slightly serrated edge. The leaves are typically green with a pale underside, and the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia caducifolia is known for its ability to shed its leaves seasonally, which is a unique adaptation to its natural environment.
