Plantago hartwegii

Plantago hartwegii, commonly known as Hartweg's plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally for its potential health benefits.
