Paullinia polystachya

Paullinia polystachya, commonly known as the Brazilian sarsapilla, is a member of the Sapindaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and contain seeds. Its botanical features include rough, hairy stems and alternate leaves with serrated edges.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.
