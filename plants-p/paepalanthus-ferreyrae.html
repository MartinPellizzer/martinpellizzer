Paepalanthus ferreyrae

Paepalanthus ferreyrae, commonly known as the Ferreyra's cholla, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are often segmented. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring season, which are clustered in inflorescences at the ends of the stems. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its water-storing tissues.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
