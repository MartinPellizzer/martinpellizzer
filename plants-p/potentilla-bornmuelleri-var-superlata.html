Potentilla bornmuelleri var. superlata

Potentilla bornmuelleri var. superlata, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a rough, hairy texture.

This variety is well adapted to harsh alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
