Polygala alpina subsp. somedana

Polygala alpina subsp.

somedana, commonly known as the Alpine milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates.

The plant features slender, branching stems and small, delicate flowers that are often pink or white in color. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, aiding in water retention.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
