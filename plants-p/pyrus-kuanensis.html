Pyrus kuanensis

Pyrus kuanensis, commonly known as the Kuan pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, sweet, and edible pears that are typically harvested in autumn. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

Pyrus kuanensis is relatively rare in the wild and is primarily found in specific regions of southern China.
