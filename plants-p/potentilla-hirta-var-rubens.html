Potentilla hirta var. rubens

Potentilla hirta var. rubens, commonly known as the red-stemmed cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cold climates. Its leaves are usually alternate, with a rough texture and a slightly serrated edge.

It is often found in tundra and mountainous environments where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
