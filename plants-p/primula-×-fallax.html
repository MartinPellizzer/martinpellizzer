Primula × fallax

Primula × fallax, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing fragrant blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Primula × fallax thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
