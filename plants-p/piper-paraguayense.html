Piper paraguayense

Piper paraguayense, commonly known as the Paraguayan pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants.

It is a flowering plant that typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
