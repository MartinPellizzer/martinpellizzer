Polygonum debile

Polygonum debile, commonly known as slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant features slender, erect stems covered with small, heart-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color. Its flowers are tiny, clustered in dense spikes, and are usually pink or reddish-purple in hue.

Polygonum debile is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
