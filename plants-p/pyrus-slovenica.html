Pyrus slovenica

Pyrus slovenica, commonly known as the Slovenian pear, is a deciduous tree native to the temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart pears in late summer.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
