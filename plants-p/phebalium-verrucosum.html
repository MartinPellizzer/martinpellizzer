Phebalium verrucosum

Phebalium verrucosum, commonly known as the woolly phebalium, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a woolly appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a distinctive species within the Rutaceae family.
