Pyrus communis var. amygdaliformis

Pyrus communis var. amygdaliformis, commonly known as the almond-shaped pear, is a botanical variant of the common pear.

It is characterized by its unique fruit shape, which resembles an almond rather than the typical pear shape. The plant produces small, usually yellow or green fruits with a smooth skin. It is cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

This variety is relatively rare and is often found in botanical gardens or specialized collections.
