Pontederia stricta

Pontederia stricta, commonly known as pickerelweed, is a herbaceous wetland plant native to the Americas.

It features tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish at the base. The plant produces showy spikes of blue or purple flowers, which bloom from summer to fall. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize soil in wetland environments.

Pickerelweed is often found in ponds, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.
