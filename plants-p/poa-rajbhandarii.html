Poa rajbhandarii

Poa rajbhandarii, commonly known as the Rajbhandari bluegrass, is a species of grass native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and India.

It is a low-growing perennial plant that thrives in cool, moist environments, typically found in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant has slender, tufted shoots with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a compact cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

Poa rajbhandarii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing mountainous soils.
