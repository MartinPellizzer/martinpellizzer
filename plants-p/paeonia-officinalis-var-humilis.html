Paeonia officinalis var. humilis

Paeonia officinalis var. humilis, commonly known as the meadow peony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, which bloom in early to mid-summer. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of down. Its root system consists of thick, fleshy rhizomes that store nutrients and help the plant survive winter.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
