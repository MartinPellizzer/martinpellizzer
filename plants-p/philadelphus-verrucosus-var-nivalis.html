Philadelphus verrucosus var. nivalis

Philadelphus verrucosus var. nivalis, commonly known as the Snowy Mockorange, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early summer, often covering the plant in a snowy appearance. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
