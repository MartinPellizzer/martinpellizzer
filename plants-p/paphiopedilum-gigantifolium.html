Paphiopedilum gigantifolium

Paphiopedilum gigantifolium, commonly known as the giant-leafed lady's slipper, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, with a distinctive pouch-shaped lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and leathery, often reaching lengths of over 30 centimeters, contributing to its dramatic appearance.

Due to its rarity and unique beauty, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
