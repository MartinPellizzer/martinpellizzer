Phaseolus sublobatus

Phaseolus sublobatus, commonly known as the evergreen bean, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It produces small, yellow flowers and develops pods containing edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and hairy stems. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
