Plectranthus cordifolius

Plectranthus cordifolius, commonly known as the African violet or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are often covered in a dense layer of fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to white, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Plectranthus cordifolius is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
