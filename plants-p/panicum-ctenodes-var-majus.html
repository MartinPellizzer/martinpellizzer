Panicum ctenodes var. majus

Panicum ctenodes var. majus, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with silky, hairy bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in humid climates.
