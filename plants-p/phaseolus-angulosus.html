Phaseolus angulosus

Phaseolus angulosus, commonly known as the angular bean, is a species of legume native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its angular, ridged pods and small, yellowish seeds. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and hairy.

The species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and is sometimes cultivated for its edible seeds.
