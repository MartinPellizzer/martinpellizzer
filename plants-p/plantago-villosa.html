Plantago villosa

Plantago villosa, commonly known as hairy plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a basal rosette of broad, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems that bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in roadsides, meadows, and waste areas.

Botanically, it is valued for its medicinal properties and is commonly used in traditional herbal remedies.
