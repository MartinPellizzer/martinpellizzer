Primula maxima

Primula maxima, commonly known as the giant primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, typically in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has broad, waxy leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. Primula maxima is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.

It is also notable for its ability to bloom in early spring, making it a symbol of the arrival of warmer weather.
