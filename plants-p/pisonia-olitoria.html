Pisonia olitoria

Pisonia olitoria, commonly known as the beach hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy soils along shorelines. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a rough texture, while its fruits are capsules containing numerous seeds.

Pisonia olitoria is well adapted to salt spray and wind, making it a hardy species in coastal environments.
