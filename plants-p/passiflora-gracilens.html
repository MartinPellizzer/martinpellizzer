Passiflora gracilens

Passiflora gracilens, commonly known as the slender passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that feature a unique arrangement of petals, sepals, and bracts. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed raw or used in beverages. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are slender and twining.

Passiflora gracilens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
