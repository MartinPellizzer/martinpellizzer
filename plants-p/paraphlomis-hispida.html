Paraphlomis hispida

Paraphlomis hispida, commonly known as rough paroelia, is a perennial herb native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

Paraphlomis hispida is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
