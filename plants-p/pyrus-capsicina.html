Pyrus capsicina

Pyrus capsicina, commonly known as the Mexican white apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its small, white to yellowish fruits that are typically edible. The plant produces simple, alternate leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

The flowers are usually white or greenish-white, with five petals and numerous stamens.
