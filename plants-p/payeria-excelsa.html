Payeria excelsa

Payeria excelsa, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, floating leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often forming dense mats on the water surface. Its reproductive structures include a spadix and a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Payeria excelsa plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
