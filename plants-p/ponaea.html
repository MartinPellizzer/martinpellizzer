Ponaea

Ponaea, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and silver-gray foliage. These plants are native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe. Ponaea species are typically herbaceous perennials that thrive in well-drained soils.

They are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
