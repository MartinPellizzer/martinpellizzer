Perryodendron

Perryodendron, commonly known as the sacred fig, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fig-like fruits. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in humid climates and its rapid growth rate. Its roots can form a dense network, making it an effective soil stabilizer.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
