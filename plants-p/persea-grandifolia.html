Persea grandifolia

Persea grandifolia, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces fruit that is typically harvested when fully ripe, featuring a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in various traditional applications.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitats.
