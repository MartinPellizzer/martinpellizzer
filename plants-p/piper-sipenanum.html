Piper sipenanum

Piper sipenanum, commonly known as the Sipena pepper or Sipena long pepper, is a flowering plant in the Piperaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant produces small, elongated berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are often used in local cuisines for their aromatic properties.

The botanical features of Piper sipenanum include its climbing habit, opposite leaves, and small white flowers that develop into the characteristic peppery fruits.
