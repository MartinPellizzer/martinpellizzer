Prunus cerasus subsp. caproniana

Prunus cerasus subsp.

caproniana, commonly known as the Capronian cherry, is a subspecies native to the eastern United States. It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree with ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

The plant produces small, sweet, edible fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in jams and preserves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

The flowers are white and appear in clusters, adding ornamental value to the tree.
