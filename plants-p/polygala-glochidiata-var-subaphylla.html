Polygala glochidiata var. subaphylla

Polygala glochidiata var. subaphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and alternate, with a distinctive appearance that aids in its identification. The plant produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers that are often clustered in spikelike inflorescences.

It is adapted to survive in harsh environments, with mechanisms to conserve water and withstand high temperatures.
