Pimelea suaveolens var. tinctoria

Pimelea suaveolens var. tinctoria, commonly known as the sweet-smelling pimelea, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring and summer. It is often found in dry sclerophyll forests and is adapted to well-drained soils.

This variety is noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
