Pavonia parva

Pavonia parva, commonly known as the small pavonia or dwarf flamebeard, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink, blooming throughout the summer months. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Pavonia parva is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
