Pilosella iserana subsp. luchavica

Pilosella iserana subsp.

luchavica, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its small, fluffy white or yellowish flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring rocky or gravelly soils. It has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs.

This subspecies is native to the European Alps and is adapted to withstand cold and harsh environmental conditions.
