Plumeria milleri

Plumeria milleri, commonly known as the white frangipani, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange. The plant produces large, waxy leaves and has a smooth, pale bark. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are typically borne on long, branched inflorescences and are used in cultural and ceremonial contexts in some regions.
