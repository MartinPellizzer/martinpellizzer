Plantago grandiflora

Plantago grandiflora, commonly known as the largeflower plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces tall, branching stems that bear clusters of small, white to greenish-yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and gardens.

Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
