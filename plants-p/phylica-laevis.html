Phylica laevis

Phylica laevis, commonly known as the silver bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, clustered white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Phylica laevis plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and birds.
