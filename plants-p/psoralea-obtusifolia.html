Psoralea obtusifolia

Psoralea obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaf psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant features simple, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its small, yellowish flowers are arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
