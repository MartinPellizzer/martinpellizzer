Plectranthus glaucocalyx var. japonicus

Plectranthus glaucocalyx var. japonicus, commonly known as Japanese purple heart, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically glaucous blue-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, usually violet or purple, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
