Piper capitellatum

Piper capitellatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces tiny greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, three-lobed berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some tropical gardens.
