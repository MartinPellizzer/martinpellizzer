Pelargonium multiflorum

Pelargonium multiflorum, commonly known as the geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically rounded with a slightly serrated edge, and the plant often has a fragrant scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Pelargonium multiflorum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
