Potamogeton densus var. laxifolius

Potamogeton densus var. laxifolius, commonly known as the dense pondweed variant with lax leaves, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It features long, narrow leaves that are often loosely arranged, distinguishing it from other varieties of Potamogeton. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically found in clusters near the water surface. It plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in lakes and slow-moving streams.
