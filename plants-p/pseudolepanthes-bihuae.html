Pseudolepanthes bihuae

Pseudolepanthes bihuae, commonly known as the Bihua's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive yellow lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention.

Pseudolepanthes bihuae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
