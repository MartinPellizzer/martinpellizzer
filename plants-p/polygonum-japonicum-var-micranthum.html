Polygonum japonicum var. micranthum

Polygonum japonicum var. micranthum, commonly known as Japanese knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features upright stems that are often reddish in color and bear heart-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, appearing in late summer. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other plants such as buckwheat and sorrel.
