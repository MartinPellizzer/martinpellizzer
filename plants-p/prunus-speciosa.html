Prunus speciosa

Prunus speciosa, commonly known as the California laurel, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, edible drupes that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its adaptability and drought tolerance.

The bark is thick and flaky, providing a distinctive appearance throughout the year.
