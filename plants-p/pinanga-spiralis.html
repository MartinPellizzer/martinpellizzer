Pinanga spiralis

Pinanga spiralis, commonly known as the spiral fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged fronds that give it its common name. The plant has a short, rhizomatous stem with dense, overlapping leaves that form a compact rosette. Its foliage is typically dark green and glossy, with a leathery texture.

Pinanga spiralis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
