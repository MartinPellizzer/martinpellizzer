Pleurothallis calyptrostele

Pleurothallis calyptrostele, commonly known as the hooded pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant has a pseudobulbous structure with narrow, linear leaves that help it thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically arranged in a raceme and are noted for their intricate, often showy morphology.

This orchid is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for intermediate orchid growers.
