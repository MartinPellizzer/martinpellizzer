Potentilla clementei

Potentilla clementei, commonly known as the Clement's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and alpine environments, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are compound, with five leaflets, and the plant is noted for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Potentilla clementei is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
