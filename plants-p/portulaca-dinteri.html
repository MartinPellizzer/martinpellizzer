Portulaca dinteri

Portulaca dinteri, commonly known as the desert purslane, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing, succulent species that thrives in hot, dry environments. The plant features fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves and produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the morning. Its ability to store water in its thick stems and leaves makes it highly drought-tolerant.

Portulaca dinteri is often used in xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and minimal water requirements.
