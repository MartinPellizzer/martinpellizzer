Pyrus fantabulosa

Pyrus fantabulosa, commonly known as the Marvelous Crabapple, is a deciduous tree native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental flowers, which bloom in early spring with a mix of white, pink, and red hues. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are often used in jams and preserves. Its leaves are dark green and turn vibrant shades of yellow and orange in autumn.

Pyrus fantabulosa is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal beauty.
