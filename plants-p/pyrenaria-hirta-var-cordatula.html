Pyrenaria hirta var. cordatula

Pyrenaria hirta var. cordatula, commonly known as the broad-leafed pyrenaria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly heart-shaped, giving it its varietal name "cordatula.". Its flowers are typically yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.
