Pahudia galedupa

Pahudia galedupa, commonly known as the Natal orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are usually pink or white with a distinctive lip.

Pahudia galedupa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
