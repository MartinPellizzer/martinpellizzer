Piofontia floribunda

Piofontia floribunda, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Piofontia floribunda is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
