Pelargonium madagascariense

Pelargonium madagascariense, commonly known as the Madagascar geranium, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, colorful flowers. The plant typically features serrated leaves and produces clusters of five-petaled blooms in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
