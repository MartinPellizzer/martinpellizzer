Pectis tenella

Pectis tenella, commonly known as the narrowleaf pectis, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

Pectis tenella is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
