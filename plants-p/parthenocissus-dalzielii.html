Parthenocissus dalzielii

Parthenocissus dalzielii, commonly known as the Chinese ivy, is a vigorous climbing vine native to China.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. Its climbing habit makes it suitable for trellises or walls, and it is often used in ornamental landscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and light conditions.
