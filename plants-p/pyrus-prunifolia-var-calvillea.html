Pyrus prunifolia var. calvillea

Pyrus prunifolia var. calvillea, commonly known as the Calville pear, is a variant of the European pear species native to southern France.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and juicy fruits with a fine, crisp texture. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy green upper surface with a lighter underside.

The flowers are white and fragrant, appearing in clusters, and the tree is generally hardy in temperate climates.
