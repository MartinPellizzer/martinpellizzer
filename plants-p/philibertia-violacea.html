Philibertia violacea

Philibertia violacea, commonly known as the violetleaf filbert, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, nut-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Philibertia violacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
