Psilocaulon arenosum

Psilocaulon arenosum, commonly known as the sand psilocaulon, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate beauty to its desert habitat. Its leaves are typically reduced to scales, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Psilocaulon arenosum is often found growing in sandy soils, where it plays a role in stabilizing the landscape and supporting local biodiversity.
