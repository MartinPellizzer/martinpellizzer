Pavetta platyphylla

Pavetta platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved pavetta, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage with broad, oval leaves that are often glossy in appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by red or orange berries containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
