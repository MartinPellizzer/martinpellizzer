Pyrus albokermesina

Pyrus albokermesina, commonly known as the white-leaf pear.

It is a species of pear native to the eastern Himalayas, characterized by its smooth, white-veined leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. Its fruit, though not widely cultivated, is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and lesser-known member of the Pyrus genus.
