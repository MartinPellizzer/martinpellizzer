Plectranthus salicarius

Plectranthus salicarius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale purple to pink in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
