Polygonum aviculare var. angustissimum

Polygonum aviculare var. angustissimum, commonly known as narrowleaf smartweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically features slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and they are arranged alternately along the stem. This variant is closely related to other species in the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and flowering patterns.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in wet meadows.
