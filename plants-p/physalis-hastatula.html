Physalis hastatula

Physalis hastatula, commonly known as the Mexican groundcherry, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, inflated calyces that enclose the fruit. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible when fully ripe. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it typically grows in dry, open habitats.

The plant is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fruit and foliage.
