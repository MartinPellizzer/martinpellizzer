Philodendron laticiferum

Philodendron laticiferum, commonly known as the broad-leaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, often with a deep green color and a smooth texture. The plant has a vigorous growth habit and can climb using its aerial roots, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets or trained structures. Its stems are typically green or slightly reddish, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by reddish berries.

Philodendron laticiferum is relatively easy to care for, thriving in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.
