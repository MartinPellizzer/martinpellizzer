Prasophyllum mimulum

Prasophyllum mimulum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved greenhood, is an orchid native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is a terrestrial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. The plant features a slender, upright stem with a single, lance-shaped leaf. Its flowers are greenish-brown with a distinctive hood-like structure, and they bloom during the cooler months.

Prasophyllum mimulum is part of the orchid family and is known for its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
