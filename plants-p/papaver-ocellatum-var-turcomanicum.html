Papaver ocellatum var. turcomanicum

Papaver ocellatum var. turcomanicum, commonly known as the Turkmenian poppy, is a variant of the double-flowered poppy native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with a central dark red eye, which are typically borne on tall, slender stems. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and unique floral display.
