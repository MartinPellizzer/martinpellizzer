Polystachya trigonochila

Polystachya trigonochila, commonly known as the triangular-leafed bamboo orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are borne on elongated inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in forest understories.

Polystachya trigonochila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
