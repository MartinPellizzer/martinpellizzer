Psathyrostachys huashanica

Psathyrostachys huashanica, commonly known as the Huashan wild rice, is a grass species native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny grains that are similar to those of cultivated rice. It thrives in moist, temperate environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its genetic similarity to rice, it has potential significance in agricultural research and breeding programs.
