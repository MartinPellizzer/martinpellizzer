Penstemon suffrutescens

Penstemon suffrutescens, commonly known as the blue penstemon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, branching stems covered in soft, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of white wool.

Penstemon suffrutescens thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
