Petrophile filifolia subsp. laxa

Petrophile filifolia subsp.

laxa, commonly known as the slender hair-leaf banksia, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and distinctive yellow to orange flower spikes.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands. Its flowers are visited by a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds.

It is an important component of the local flora and contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
