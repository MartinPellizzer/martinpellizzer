Plantago asiatica var. decumbens

Plantago asiatica var. decumbens, commonly known as the prostrate plantain, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It features a low-growing, spreading habit with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, which are typically borne on slender stalks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.

This variant is well adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in moist, shady environments.
