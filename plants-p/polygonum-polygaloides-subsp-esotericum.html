Polygonum polygaloides subsp. esotericum

Polygonum polygaloides subsp.

esotericum, commonly known as the esoteric polygonum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Asia. It belongs to the buckwheat family, Polygonaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface, and the flowers are usually pink or red, appearing in late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
