Pouteria orinocoensis

Pouteria orinocoensis, commonly known as the Orinoco sapote, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces a smooth, yellowish fruit with a sweet, creamy pulp that is often used in culinary applications. Its growth habit includes a dense canopy and it is often found in lowland rainforests.

Pouteria orinocoensis is also valued for its wood, which is durable and used in construction and furniture-making.
