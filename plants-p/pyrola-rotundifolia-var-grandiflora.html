Pyrola rotundifolia var. grandiflora

Pyrola rotundifolia var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered wintergreen, is a low-growing evergreen perennial native to the northern forests of North America.

It features broad, waxy, evergreen leaves that remain throughout the year, providing a lush green presence in shaded environments. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coniferous or mixed forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a desirable plant for woodland gardens and conservation efforts.
