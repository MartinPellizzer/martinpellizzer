Psidium buxifolium

Psidium buxifolium, commonly known as the boxleaf guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It features dense, dark green leaves that are small and resemble those of boxwood, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow or red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
