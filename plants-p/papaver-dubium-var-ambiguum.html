Papaver dubium var. ambiguum

Papaver dubium var. ambiguum, commonly known as the ambiguous poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive black center. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
