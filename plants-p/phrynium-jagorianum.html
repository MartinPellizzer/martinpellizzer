Phrynium jagorianum

Phrynium jagorianum, commonly known as the jagori arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red or purple. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically borne on tall, erect stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or near water bodies.

Phrynium jagorianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage.
