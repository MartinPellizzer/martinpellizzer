Picris senecioides

Picris senecioides, commonly known as goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, feathery yellow flower heads that resemble a beard. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
