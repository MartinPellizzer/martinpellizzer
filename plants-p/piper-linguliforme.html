Piper linguliforme

Piper linguliforme, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper or the Indian pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Piper genus in the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruits are small berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and has traditional uses in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
