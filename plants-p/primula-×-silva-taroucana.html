Primula × silva-taroucana

Primula × silva-taroucana, commonly known as the Silvatic Tarouca primula, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its attractive, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. It features broad, velvety leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs, aiding in water retention.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom reliably in shaded garden settings.
