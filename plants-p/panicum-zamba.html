Panicum zamba

Panicum zamba, commonly known as Zamba grass, is a flowering plant native to the grasslands of South America.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry to semi-arid conditions and is adapted to withstand drought. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green hue, which helps reduce water loss.

Panicum zamba is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
