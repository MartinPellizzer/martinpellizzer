Polygala nicaeensis var. nemorivaga

Polygala nicaeensis var. nemorivaga, commonly known as the woodland milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in woodland and shrubland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender stems with opposite, pinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelike inflorescences, usually in shades of pink or white.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
