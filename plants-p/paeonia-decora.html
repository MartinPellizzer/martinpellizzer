Paeonia decora

Paeonia decora, commonly known as the showy peony, is a deciduous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range from white to deep pink, often with a distinctive red eye. The plant produces compound, trifoliate leaves that are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The showy peony is also valued for its fragrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
