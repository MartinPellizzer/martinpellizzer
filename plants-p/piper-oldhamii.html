Piper oldhamii

Piper oldhamii, commonly known as the Oldham's pepper or the Hawaiian pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, opposite leaves and clusters of tiny white flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its unique ecological role and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
