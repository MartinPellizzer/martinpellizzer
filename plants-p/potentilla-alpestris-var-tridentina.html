Potentilla alpestris var. tridentina

Potentilla alpestris var. tridentina, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three leaflets, giving it its specific epithet "tridentina.". It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and tundra environments.

This hardy species is adapted to cold climates and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh conditions.
