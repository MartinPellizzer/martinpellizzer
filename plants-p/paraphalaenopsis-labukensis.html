Paraphalaenopsis labukensis

Paraphalaenopsis labukensis, commonly known as the Labuk orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive appearance, featuring long, narrow leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil, and it is known for its resilience in humid climates.

The Labuk orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
