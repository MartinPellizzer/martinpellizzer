Primula × boni-auxilii

Primula × boni-auxilii, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Primula vulgaris and Primula obconica. The plant typically produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink, white, and yellow. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are attractive in their own right.
