Phillyrea virgata

Phillyrea virgata, commonly known as the Turkish laurel, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features simple, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter, sometimes whitish underside. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits. Phillyrea virgata is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

It is also valued for its aromatic foliage and historical use in traditional medicine.
