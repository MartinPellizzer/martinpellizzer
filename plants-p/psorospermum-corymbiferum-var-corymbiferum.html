Psorospermum corymbiferum var. corymbiferum

Psorospermum corymbiferum var. corymbiferum, commonly known as the Malabar chestnut, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, greyish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by edible, chestnut-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
