Potentilla pensylvanica var. communis

Potentilla pensylvanica var. communis, commonly known as common potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, arching stems and dark green, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.

Botanically, it is characterized by its simple, alternate leaf arrangement and its ability to form dense colonies.
