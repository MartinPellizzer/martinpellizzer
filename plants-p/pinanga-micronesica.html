Pinanga micronesica

Pinanga micronesica, commonly known as the Micronesian pineapple palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands, including Micronesia, where it thrives in humid, coastal environments. The plant is characterized by its clustered, fan-shaped leaves and produces small, white flowers that develop into edible fruits. It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.

Due to habitat loss and human activity, it is considered vulnerable and is often found in protected areas.
