Potentilla × ala-arczae

Potentilla × ala-arczae, commonly known as the Armenian cinquefoil, is a hybrid plant that belongs to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth and is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Central Asia. The plant typically features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Potentilla × ala-arczae is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
