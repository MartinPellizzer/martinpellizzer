Prunus serotina var. asplenifolia

Prunus serotina var. asplenifolia, commonly known as the asplenifolius cherry, is a variant of the black cherry tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, fern-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by dark purple berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
