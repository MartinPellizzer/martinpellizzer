Pelargonium amicum

Pelargonium amicum, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant pink or red blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually serrated and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Pelargonium amicum is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
