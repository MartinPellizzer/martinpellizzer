Picralima

Picralima, commonly known as the "devil's backbone," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Apocynaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The flowers are typically small, clustered in umbels, and have five petals with a prominent, spurred lower petal. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It contains toxic alkaloids and is sometimes used in traditional medicine, though with caution due to its poisonous nature.
