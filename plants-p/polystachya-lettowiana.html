Polystachya lettowiana

Polystachya lettowiana, commonly known as the Lettow's spikegrass, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features a slender rhizome and produces tall, branched inflorescences with numerous small, greenish-white flowers. Its botanical characteristics include lanceolate leaves and a unique floral structure typical of the orchid family.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
