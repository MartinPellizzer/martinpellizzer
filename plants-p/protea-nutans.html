Protea nutans

Protea nutans, commonly known as the leaning protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its unique floral structure attracts pollinators such as birds and insects, playing a vital role in its ecological niche.

Protea nutans is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
