Pseuderanthemum sorongense

Pseuderanthemum sorongense, commonly known as the Sorong skyflower, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble small umbrellas. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories, and thrives in tropical climates. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth surface and a glossy appearance.

The flowers, which are usually blue or purple, bloom in clusters and are adapted to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
