Pfaffia stenophylla var. foliosa

Pfaffia stenophylla var. foliosa, commonly known as Brazilian ginseng, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its roots are the primary part used for medicinal purposes, containing bioactive compounds believed to have adaptogenic properties.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
