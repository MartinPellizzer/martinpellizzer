Polypodium ghiesbreghtii

Polypodium ghiesbreghtii, commonly known as the Mexican shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows on rock surfaces or in shaded, moist environments, exhibiting a lithophytic or epiphytic growth habit. Its fronds are bi- or tri-pinnate, with a leathery texture and a deep green color.

Polypodium ghiesbreghtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
