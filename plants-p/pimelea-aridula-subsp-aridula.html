Pimelea aridula subsp. aridula

Pimelea aridula subsp.

aridula, commonly known as the aridula bottlebrush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and dense clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments and has a deep root system to access groundwater. It is often found in rocky soils and is resistant to drought and salt spray.

Its unique appearance and ecological resilience make it a notable species in its native habitat.
