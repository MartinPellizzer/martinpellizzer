Piper cucullatum

Piper cucullatum, commonly known as the hooded pepper or Mexican pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
