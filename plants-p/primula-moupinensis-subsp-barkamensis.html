Primula moupinensis subsp. barkamensis

Primula moupinensis subsp.

barkamensis, commonly known as the Barkam's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes. Its leaves are typically oval and have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
