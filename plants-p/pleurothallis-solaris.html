Pleurothallis solaris

Pleurothallis solaris, commonly known as the sun orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and dark green, adapted to maximize light absorption in its natural habitat.

Pleurothallis solaris is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
