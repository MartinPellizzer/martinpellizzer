Piper sublepidotum f. brevipilum

Piper sublepidotum f. brevipilum, commonly known as the short-hair pepper, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into berries containing a single seed. Its botanical features make it distinct from other Piper species due to its unique leaf morphology and compact growth habit.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild flavor in culinary applications.
