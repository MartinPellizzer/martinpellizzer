Pabstiella colorata

Pabstiella colorata, commonly known as the rainbow orchid, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that display a range of hues, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette near the ground.

Pabstiella colorata is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare and exotic plants.
