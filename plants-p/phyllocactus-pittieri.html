Phyllocactus pittieri

Phyllocactus pittieri, commonly known as the coral cactus, is a unique succulent native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, branching stems that resemble coral, hence its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and indoor settings.
