Pleurothallis portillae

Pleurothallis portillae, commonly known as the Portilla's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments within the cloud forests. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns on the petals. Its root system is adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
