Polygala paraguayensis

Polygala paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan milkweed, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
