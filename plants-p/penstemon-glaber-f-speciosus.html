Penstemon glaber f. speciosus

Penstemon glaber f. speciosus, commonly known as the naked penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has reduced leaves, giving it a "naked" appearance, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun conditions.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in gardens.
