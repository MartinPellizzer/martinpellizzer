Polypogon × lutosus

Polypogon × lutosus, commonly known as the swamp millet, is a hybrid grass species native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its adaptability, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
