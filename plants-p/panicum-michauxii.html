Panicum michauxii

Panicum michauxii, commonly known as Michaux's panicgrass, is a flowering plant species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and the plant is often found in association with other wetland vegetation.

Panicum michauxii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
