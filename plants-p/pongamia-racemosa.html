Pongamia racemosa

Pongamia racemosa, commonly known as the Indian beech or pongam, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are borne in racemose inflorescences, giving it its scientific name. Its fruit is a legume that contains a single, oily seed, which is rich in bioactive compounds.

The plant is also notable for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its potential use in phytoremediation.
