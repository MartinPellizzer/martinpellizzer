Pleurothallis biglandulosa

Pleurothallis biglandulosa, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Due to its delicate structure and specific habitat requirements, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts seeking a unique and challenging specimen.
