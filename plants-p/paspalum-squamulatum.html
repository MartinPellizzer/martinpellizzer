Paspalum squamulatum

Paspalum squamulatum, commonly known as the silvergrass or slender paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its inflorescence consists of dense, cylindrical spikelets that bloom in late summer.

Paspalum squamulatum is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in landscaping applications.
