Pogonotrophe macrocarpa

Pogonotrophe macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant sago palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries that contain seeds. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil in its natural habitat.

The giant sago palm is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical significance in traditional medicine.
