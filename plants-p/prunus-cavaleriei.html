Prunus cavaleriei

Prunus cavaleriei, commonly known as the Chinese bird cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its fruits are small, yellow drupes that ripen in late summer.

Prunus cavaleriei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine.
