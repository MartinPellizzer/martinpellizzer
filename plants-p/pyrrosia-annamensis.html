Pyrrosia annamensis

Pyrrosia annamensis, commonly known as the Annam spleenwort, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that grows on trees and rocks, typically in moist, shaded environments. The plant has lance-shaped, glossy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and contain spores for propagation.

Pyrrosia annamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
