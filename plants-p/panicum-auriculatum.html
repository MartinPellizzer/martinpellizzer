Panicum auriculatum

Panicum auriculatum, commonly known as little barley or hair grass, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas, wetlands, and along roadsides.

Panicum auriculatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
