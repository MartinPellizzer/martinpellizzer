Paspalum acutifolium

Paspalum acutifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf buffalograss, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and tufted inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-like flowers. This plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance and adaptability to poor soil conditions. It is often used in landscaping for its low maintenance and ability to form dense, attractive turf.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
