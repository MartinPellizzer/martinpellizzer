Poa pratensis var. urjanchaica

Poa pratensis var. urjanchaica, commonly known as the Altai meadowgrass, is a variant of the common meadowgrass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial grass species characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, hairless leaves. The plant typically thrives in well-drained, fertile soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine meadows. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
