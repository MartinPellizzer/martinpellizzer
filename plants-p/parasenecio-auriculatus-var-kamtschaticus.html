Parasenecio auriculatus var. kamtschaticus

Parasenecio auriculatus var. kamtschaticus, commonly known as the Kamchatka auriculate parasenecio, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold climates. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
