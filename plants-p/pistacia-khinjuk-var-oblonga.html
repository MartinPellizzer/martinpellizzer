Pistacia khinjuk var. oblonga

Pistacia khinjuk var. oblonga, commonly known as the Indian date palm, is a variant of the pistacia genus native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are wind-pollinated. The plant bears edible fruits that are similar to dates, though they are smaller and less sweet. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, requiring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional agriculture.
