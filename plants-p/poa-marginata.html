Poa marginata

Poa marginata, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery-blue foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Poa marginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and conservation efforts.
