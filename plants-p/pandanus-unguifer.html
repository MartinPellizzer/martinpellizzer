Pandanus unguifer

Pandanus unguifer, commonly known as the screw pine, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves that form a dense, umbrella-like canopy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, fleshy fruits known as "breadfruits.". Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional construction and crafts.

The species is well adapted to coastal and saline environments, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
