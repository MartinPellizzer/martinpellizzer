Physianthus megapotamicus

Physianthus megapotamicus, commonly known as the Andean starflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy inflorescences. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in temperate regions.
