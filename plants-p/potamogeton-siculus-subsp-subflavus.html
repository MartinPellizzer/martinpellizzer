Potamogeton siculus subsp. subflavus

Potamogeton siculus subsp.

subflavus, commonly known as the Sicilian pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, rivers, and ponds.

The plant has slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often reddish or yellowish in color. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
