Pomaderris discolor

Pomaderris discolor, commonly known as the silver-leafed pomaderris, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The leaves are elliptical, with a silvery underside that gives the plant its common name. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for birds.

This plant is often found in dry, open woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
