Pyrus koehnei var. crossotocalyx

Pyrus koehnei var. crossotocalyx, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray-brown bark and alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet, yellowish fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

This variety is adapted to temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.
