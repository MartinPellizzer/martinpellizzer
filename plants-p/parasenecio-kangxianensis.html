Parasenecio kangxianensis

Parasenecio kangxianensis, commonly known as the Kangxian mountain gentian, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool and moist environments. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
