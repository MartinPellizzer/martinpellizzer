Phyllostachys vivax f. aureocaulis

Phyllostachys vivax f. aureocaulis, commonly known as the golden stem bamboo, is a variant of the common bamboo species Phyllostachys vivax.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms that contrast with its typical green foliage. This ornamental bamboo is native to eastern Asia and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.

Its growth habit is clump-forming, making it suitable for use in bonsai and as a decorative plant.
