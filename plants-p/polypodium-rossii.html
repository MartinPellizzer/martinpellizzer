Polypodium rossii

It is a small, evergreen fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant has a rosette-shaped frond structure with lanceolate, serrated leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Polypodium rossii is characterized by its unique spore-producing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds.

This species is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
