Pinus schrenkiana

Pinus schrenkiana, commonly known as the Schrenk's pine, is a coniferous tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves, which are typically arranged in bundles of five. The tree produces cones that are often elongated and can reach lengths of up to 15 centimeters. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

This species is adapted to grow in arid and semi-arid climates, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
