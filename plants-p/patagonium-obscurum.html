Patagonium obscurum

Patagonium obscurum, commonly known as the dark patagonia, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Patagonium obscurum is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
