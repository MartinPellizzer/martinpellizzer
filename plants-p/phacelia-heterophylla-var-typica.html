Phacelia heterophylla var. typica

Phacelia heterophylla var. typica, commonly known as blue flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
