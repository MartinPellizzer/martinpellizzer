Pleurothallis warmingii

Pleurothallis warmingii, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, yellowish-green to white blooms that are highly ornamental. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
