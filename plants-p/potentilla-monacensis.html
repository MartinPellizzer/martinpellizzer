Potentilla monacensis

Potentilla monacensis, commonly known as the Monastery cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, aromatic leaves that are often covered in a fuzzy layer, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a valued plant in horticulture and conservation efforts.
