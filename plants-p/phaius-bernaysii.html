Phaius bernaysii

Phaius bernaysii, commonly known as the Bernays' orchid, is a striking terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, greenish-yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive red or purple lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often forming a rosette at the base.

Phaius bernaysii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
