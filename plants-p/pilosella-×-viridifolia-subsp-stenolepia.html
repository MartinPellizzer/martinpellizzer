Pilosella × viridifolia subsp. stenolepia

Pilosella × viridifolia subsp.

stenolepia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mouse-eared chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, pale green to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy, elongated leaves that are often narrowed at the base, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
