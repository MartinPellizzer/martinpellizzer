Pavia × mutabilis

Pavia × mutabilis, commonly known as the variable pavia, is a hybrid shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, with leaves ranging from ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in the spring. Its adaptability to different soil types makes it a resilient ornamental plant.

However, its hybrid nature can complicate propagation and taxonomic classification.
