Pterocephalus depressus subsp. depressus

Pterocephalus depressus subsp.

depressus, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to China. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged oppositely along the stem.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes.
