Panicum amplissimum

Panicum amplissimum, commonly known as giant panicgrass, is a tall, grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and panicle-shaped inflorescences that produce small, fluffy seeds. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides. Its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes make it an effective pioneer species in ecological succession.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
