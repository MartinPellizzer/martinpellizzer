Piper hondonadense

Piper hondonadense, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a flowering plant species native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 3,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, followed by small, fleshy berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
