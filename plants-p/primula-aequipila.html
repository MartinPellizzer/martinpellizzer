Primula aequipila

Primula aequipila, commonly known as the equal-leaved primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically oval and arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem, giving it its common name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
