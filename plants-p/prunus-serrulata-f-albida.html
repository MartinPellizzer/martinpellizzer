Prunus serrulata f. albida

Prunus serrulata f. albida, commonly known as the white-flowered cherry or Ume, is a variant of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, oval leaves with serrated edges, typical of the Prunus genus. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.

This form is prized in gardens and parks for its delicate floral display.
