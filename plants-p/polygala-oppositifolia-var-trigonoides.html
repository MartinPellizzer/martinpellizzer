Polygala oppositifolia var. trigonoides

Polygala oppositifolia var. trigonoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It features slender, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. The leaves are narrow and opposite, giving the plant its specific epithet "oppositifolia.". It is adapted to drought conditions with deep root systems and minimal water requirements.

This species is often found in sandy or gravelly soils in desert environments.
