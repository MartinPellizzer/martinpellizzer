Phyllostachys corrugata

Phyllostachys corrugata, commonly known as the corrugated bamboo, is a species of grass in the bamboo family, Poaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its unique, corrugated or ridged internodes along its culms. The plant typically grows as a clump-forming perennial, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a bluish-green color and a slightly hairy surface.

Phyllostachys corrugata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic and structural qualities.
