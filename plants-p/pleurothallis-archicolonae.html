Pleurothallis archicolonae

Pleurothallis archicolonae, commonly known as the "Columbian orchid," is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain, which is typical for many orchid species.

The botanical features of Pleurothallis archicolonae make it an interesting subject for study in both horticulture and ecological research.
