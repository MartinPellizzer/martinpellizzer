Polygonum chamaechyton

Polygonum chamaechyton, commonly known as the desert knotweed, is a perennial herb native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Polygonum chamaechyton is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
