Petunia spathulata

Petunia spathulata, commonly known as the broadleaf petunia, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant is typically herbaceous, with a trailing growth habit that makes it suitable for hanging baskets or containers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-draining soil.

Petunia spathulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
