Paspalum longissimum var. guadaloupense

Paspalum longissimum var. guadaloupense, commonly known as the Guadaloupe paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
