Piper archeri

Piper archeri, commonly known as the velvetleaf pepper or Hawaiian pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional Hawaiian cuisine. The plant produces small, round, green berries that turn red when ripe, containing a spicy, peppery flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is cultivated in various regions with suitable climates.
