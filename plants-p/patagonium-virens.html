Patagonium virens

Patagonium virens, commonly known as the green Patagonian grass, is a perennial herb native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are usually greenish or pale yellow. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
