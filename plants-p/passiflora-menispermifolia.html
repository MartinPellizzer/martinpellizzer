Passiflora menispermifolia

Passiflora menispermifolia, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in vitamin C. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture.

Passiflora menispermifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
