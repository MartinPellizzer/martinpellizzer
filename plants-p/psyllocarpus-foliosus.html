Psyllocarpus foliosus

Psyllocarpus foliosus, commonly known as the leafy psyllocarpus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. Its growth habit is typically upright, with a woody stem and trifoliate leaves that are hairy on the underside.

Psyllocarpus foliosus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage.
