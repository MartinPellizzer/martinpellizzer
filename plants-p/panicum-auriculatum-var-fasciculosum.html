Panicum auriculatum var. fasciculosum

Panicum auriculatum var. fasciculosum, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with reddish hues. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green to purplish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open fields and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental appeal and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
