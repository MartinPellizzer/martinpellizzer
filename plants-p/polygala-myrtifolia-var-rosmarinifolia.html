Polygala myrtifolia var. rosmarinifolia

Polygala myrtifolia var. rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the rosemary-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves that resemble rosemary. The plant produces small, yellow or greenish flowers arranged in compact inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and fragrance, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
