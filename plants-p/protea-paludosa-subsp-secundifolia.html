Protea paludosa subsp. secundifolia

Protea paludosa subsp.

secundifolia, commonly known as the marsh protea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers arranged in a cylindrical shape.

The plant typically grows in wet, boggy habitats and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clumps.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat for various insects and birds.
