Phacelia pinnatifida var. elatior

Phacelia pinnatifida var. elatior, commonly known as the tall phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its biennial or short-lived perennial growth habit and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has pinnately divided leaves, which are often hairy and aromatic. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
