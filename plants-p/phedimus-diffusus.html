Phedimus diffusus

Phedimus diffusus, commonly known as the creeping phedimus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with dense, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Phedimus diffusus is often used in ground cover in shaded garden settings due to its hardy nature and ability to suppress weeds.
