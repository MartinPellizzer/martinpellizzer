Potamogeton seemenii subvar. lanceolatifolius

Potamogeton seemenii subvar. lanceolatifolius, commonly known as the lanceolate pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Asia.

It features long, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which are often submerged or just barely above the water surface. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in lakes, ponds, and wetlands.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
