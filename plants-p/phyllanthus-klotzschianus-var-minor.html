Phyllanthus klotzschianus var. minor

Phyllanthus klotzschianus var. minor, commonly known as the lesser klotzschia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring small, elliptical leaves with a smooth surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
