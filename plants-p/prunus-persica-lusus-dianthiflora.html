Prunus persica lusus dianthiflora

Prunus persica lusus dianthiflora, commonly known as the Persian peach, is a unique botanical variant of the peach tree.

It is characterized by its delicate pink flowers that resemble those of the carnation, hence the "dianthiflora" reference. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with a short, sturdy trunk and broad, rounded leaves. It produces small, sweet fruits with a distinct flavor profile compared to typical peaches.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and unique floral characteristics.
