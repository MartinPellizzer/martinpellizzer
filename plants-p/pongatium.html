Pongatium

Pongatium, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, translucent, and often bioluminescent flowers that emit a soft glow at night. The plant's botanical features include its elongated, tubular petals and a distinctive, spiky calyx that protects its reproductive structures. Pongatium thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture to flourish.

Due to its rare and ethereal appearance, it is a subject of fascination among botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
