Pollinia parceciliata

Pollinia parceciliata, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant produces unique pollinia, which are compact masses of pollen that are transferred by specific insect pollinators. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the humid environment.

Pollinia parceciliata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
