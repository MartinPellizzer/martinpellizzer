Pholidostachys synanthera subsp. robusta

Pholidostachys synanthera subsp.

robusta, commonly known as the robust flame bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that form dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

The plant produces panicles of small, bright yellow flowers, which are often clustered in pairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

This species is native to Southeast Asia and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
