Protium amazonicum

Protium amazonicum, commonly known as the Amazonian copal tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, oval-shaped fruits containing sticky, resinous seeds. Its sap, known as copal, is used in traditional medicine and for making incense.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
