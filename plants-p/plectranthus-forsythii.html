Plectranthus forsythii

Plectranthus forsythii, commonly known as the yellow plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly aromatic scent. It produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Plectranthus forsythii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of growing conditions.
