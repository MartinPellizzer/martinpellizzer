Polygonum gymnopus

Polygonum gymnopus, commonly known as the narrowleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Polygonum gymnopus is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which is known for its distinctive nodes and sometimes edible seeds.
