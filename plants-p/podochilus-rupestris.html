Podochilus rupestris

Podochilus rupestris, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on rocky outcrops and in forested areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant has a slender, creeping rhizome and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. Its leaves are linear and often covered in a fine, silky hair.

Podochilus rupestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with specific horticultural care.
