Potentilla verna subvar. stenopetala

Potentilla verna subvar. stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to alpine and arctic regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in early summer. The leaves are narrow and have a distinctive five-lobed shape, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as tundra, meadows, and mountain slopes.
