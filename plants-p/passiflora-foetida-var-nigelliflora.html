Passiflora foetida var. nigelliflora

Passiflora foetida var. nigelliflora, commonly known as the black-flowered passionflower, is a striking member of the Passifloraceae family native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, velvety flowers that are adorned with intricate patterns of red and yellow. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a glossy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
