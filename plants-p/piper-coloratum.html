Piper coloratum

Piper coloratum, commonly known as the purple pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruits are small, round, and turn from green to a striking purple when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, peppery flavor that makes it suitable for culinary use.
