Picea schrenkiana f. robertii

Picea schrenkiana f. robertii, commonly known as the Robert's spruce, is a rare and unique variant of the Schrenk's spruce.

It is characterized by its distinctive conical shape and dense, dark green foliage. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in the Tien Shan and Pamir ranges. Its cones are typically small and woody, with scales that are often curved.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
