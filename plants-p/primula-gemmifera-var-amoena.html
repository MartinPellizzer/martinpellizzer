Primula gemmifera var. amoena

Primula gemmifera var. amoena, commonly known as the pink primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soil and prefers partial shade to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
