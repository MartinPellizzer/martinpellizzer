Panicum longifolium var. tusketense

Panicum longifolium var. tusketense, commonly known as Tusketense Panicgrass, is a variant of the panic grass genus native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open fields and along roadsides. Its growth habit is clump-forming, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for pollinators and small wildlife.
