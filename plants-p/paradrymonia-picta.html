Paradrymonia picta

Paradrymonia picta, commonly known as the painted paradrymonia, is a striking member of the Rubiaceae family, native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

This shrub is characterized by its vibrant, ornately patterned leaves that display a mix of green, yellow, and red hues, often with intricate vein patterns. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale pink, which bloom during the wet season. Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though it requires high humidity and well-drained soil to thrive.

Despite its aesthetic appeal, it is relatively rare in the wild and is often cultivated in specialized collections.
