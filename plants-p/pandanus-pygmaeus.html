Pandanus pygmaeus

Pandanus pygmaeus, commonly known as the dwarf pandanus, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that grow in dense, tufted clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green, which are followed by spiky, fibrous fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a landscape plant in tropical gardens.
