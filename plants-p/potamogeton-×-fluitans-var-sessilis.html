Potamogeton × fluitans var. sessilis

Potamogeton × fluitans var. sessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are typically sessile, meaning they lack a stalk and are directly attached to the stem. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often emerging from the water surface. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

Its morphology and growth habits make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
