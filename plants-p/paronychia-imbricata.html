Paronychia imbricata

Paronychia imbricata, commonly known as the rock rose or coastal rockrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and small, evergreen leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

Paronychia imbricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
