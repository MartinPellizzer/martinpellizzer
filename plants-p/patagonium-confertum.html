Patagonium confertum

Patagonium confertum, commonly known as the Patagonian thistle, is a perennial plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and dense clusters of purple to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in harsh, high-altitude environments with strong winds and low temperatures.

Patagonium confertum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing food for local wildlife.
