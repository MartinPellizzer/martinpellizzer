Poa kirkii var. cheesemanii

Poa kirkii var. cheesemanii, commonly known as the Cheeseman's bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, dark green leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
