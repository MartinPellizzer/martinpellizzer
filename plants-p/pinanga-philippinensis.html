Pinanga philippinensis

Pinanga philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces a spadix inflorescence surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of many arum species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its edible rhizomes, which are used in traditional cuisine.
