Phyllagathis erythrotricha

Phyllagathis erythrotricha, commonly known as the red-haired plectranthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Phyllagathis erythrotricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
