Prenanthes illinoensis

Prenanthes illinoensis, commonly known as Illinois waterplantain, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has heart-shaped, basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in a spike and bloom in late summer.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and microorganisms.
