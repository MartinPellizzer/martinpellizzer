Phaulanthus

Phaulanthus, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a genus of flowering plants native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

They have adapted to harsh environments with features such as thick, waxy leaves to reduce water loss and deep root systems to access underground moisture. Phaulanthus species are often found growing in sandy or gravelly soils, often along dry streambeds or in desert scrub habitats.
