Potentilla bifurca var. subsericea

Potentilla bifurca var. subsericea, commonly known as the silky cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features slender, arching stems covered in soft, silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The leaves are compound, consisting of five ovate to lanceolate leaflets with a finely toothed margin. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
