Pyrus turkmenorum

Pyrus turkmenorum, commonly known as the Turkmenian pear, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The tree produces small, round pomes that are typically edible, though they are not widely cultivated for commercial use. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to harsh environmental conditions.
