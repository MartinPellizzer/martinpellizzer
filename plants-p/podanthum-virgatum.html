Podanthum virgatum

Podanthum virgatum, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, star-shaped flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value is often appreciated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
