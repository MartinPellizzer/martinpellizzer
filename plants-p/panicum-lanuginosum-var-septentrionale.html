Panicum lanuginosum var. septentrionale

Panicum lanuginosum var. septentrionale, commonly known as northern silky grass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and slender, upright growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish-brown flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
