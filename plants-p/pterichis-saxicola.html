Pterichis saxicola

Pterichis saxicola, commonly known as the rock pterichis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in crevices or on rock faces, where it thrives in harsh, dry conditions. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. It is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low water availability, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
