Pilostigma muelleri

Pilostigma muelleri, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain small, edible seeds used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nutritional benefits.

Pilostigma muelleri is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agroforestry systems.
