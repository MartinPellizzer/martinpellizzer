Pilocereus lateribarbatus

Pilocereus lateribarbatus, commonly known as the zigzag cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed stems that curve and twist, giving it a zigzag appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It is a succulent plant, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems to survive in arid environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
