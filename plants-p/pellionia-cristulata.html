Pellionia cristulata

Pellionia cristulata, commonly known as the zigzag pellionia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and appear in clusters.

Pellionia cristulata is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
