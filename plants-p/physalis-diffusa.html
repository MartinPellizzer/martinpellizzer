Physalis diffusa

Physalis diffusa, commonly known as the common groundcherries or husk tomatoes, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, orange-yellow fruits enclosed in a papery, lantern-like calyx. These fruits are often eaten raw or cooked and are rich in vitamins and antioxidants.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
