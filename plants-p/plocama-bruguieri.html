Plocama bruguieri

Plocama bruguieri, commonly known as the Brazilian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, and it is often found in association with other wetland vegetation.

Its botanical features make it an important component of riparian and swamp ecosystems.
