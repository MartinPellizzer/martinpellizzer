Polystichum parasinense

Polystichum parasinense, commonly known as the parasinense fern, is a species of evergreen fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds that are typically divided into lance-shaped segments. Native to Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce dense clusters of spore-bearing sorus structures on the underside of the fronds.

The plant's ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.
