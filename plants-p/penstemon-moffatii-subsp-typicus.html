Penstemon moffatii subsp. typicus

Penstemon moffatii subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the Moffat's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers.

The plant typically produces pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is valued for both its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
