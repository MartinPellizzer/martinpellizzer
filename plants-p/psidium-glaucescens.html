Psidium glaucescens

Psidium glaucescens, commonly known as the wax apple or Surinam cherry, is a tropical plant belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. These berries are rich in vitamin C and are often used in culinary applications.

Psidium glaucescens thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.
