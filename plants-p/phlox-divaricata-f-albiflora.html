Phlox divaricata f. albiflora

Phlox divaricata f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered wood phlox, is a cultivar of the woodland phlox species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compact, mound-like growth habit and its distinctive white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The foliage is typically dark green and aromatic, with a slightly hairy texture. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in shady garden settings and its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
