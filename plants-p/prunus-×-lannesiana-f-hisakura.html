Prunus × lannesiana f. hisakura

Prunus × lannesiana f. hisakura, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that bloom in early spring, often in shades of pink and white. The plant is a hybrid between Prunus lannesiana and Prunus serrulata, resulting in a unique combination of traits. It is widely cultivated in Japan and other parts of East Asia for ornamental purposes.

The botanical features include a small, rounded tree form with smooth, reddish bark and trifoliate leaves.
