Prunus prionophylla

Prunus prionophylla, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Japanese white cherry, is a species of flowering shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Prunus prionophylla is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators and supporting local wildlife.
