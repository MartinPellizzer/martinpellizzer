Phanera ferruginea var. ferruginea

Phanera ferruginea var. ferruginea, commonly known as the red-flowered phanera, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, depending on the stage of bloom. Its fruit is a capsule containing several winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
