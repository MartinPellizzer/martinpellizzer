Populus suaveolens var. przewalskii

Populus suaveolens var. przewalskii, commonly known as the Przewalski's poplar, is a deciduous tree native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, which appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in river valleys and mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
