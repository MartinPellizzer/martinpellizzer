Primula sieboldii f. spontanea

Primula sieboldii f. spontanea, commonly known as the Japanese primrose or wild primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in a clustered inflorescence. The plant has a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland plantings.
