Paspalum virgatum var. pubiflorum

Paspalum virgatum var. pubiflorum, commonly known as the hairyflower paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
