Paronychia flavescens

Paronychia flavescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor itself in loose, sandy soils.

Paronychia flavescens is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
