Plectranthus kassneri

Plectranthus kassneri, commonly known as the Cape verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring aromatic, hairy leaves and tubular flowers. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and rockeries.

Its adaptability and ornamental value contribute to its widespread cultivation in subtropical regions.
