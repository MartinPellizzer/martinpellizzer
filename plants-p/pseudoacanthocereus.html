Pseudoacanthocereus

Pseudoacanthocereus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a genus of cacti native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and nocturnal flowers that open at night. The plants typically grow in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with thick, fleshy stems for water storage. They produce showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often attracting moths and other nocturnal pollinators.

The genus includes several species, some of which are popular among cactus enthusiasts for their unique appearance and blooming habits.
