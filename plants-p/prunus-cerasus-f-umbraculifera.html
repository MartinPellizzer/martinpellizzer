Prunus cerasus f. umbraculifera

Prunus cerasus f. umbraculifera, commonly known as the shade-leaf cherry, is a cultivar of the cherry tree characterized by its distinctive dark green, almost black foliage.

It is a compact, ornamental shrub or small tree that produces small, sweet, edible fruits similar to those of the common cherry. The leaves are thick, leathery, and retain their color throughout the growing season, providing year-round visual interest. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in ornamental horticulture.
