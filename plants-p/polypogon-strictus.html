Polypogon strictus

Polypogon strictus, commonly known as narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. This plant thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny blue or purple flowers.

Polypogon strictus is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
