Psydrax lividus

Psydrax lividus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants due to their striking appearance.
