Pyxidaria hookeri

Pyxidaria hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's pyxidaria, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Its flowering period typically occurs during the spring and early summer months.

Pyxidaria hookeri is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
