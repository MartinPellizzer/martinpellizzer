Printzia Nutans

Common evening primrose, scientifically known as Printzia nutans, is a striking member of the Onagraceae family native to the southwestern United States. This plant is renowned for its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the late afternoon and remain open through the night. Thriving in arid and semi-arid environments, it is well adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its unique flowering pattern makes it a favorite among nocturnal pollinators such as moths and bats. With its ecological significance and ornamental value, Printzia nutans plays an important role in both natural ecosystems and garden landscapes.
