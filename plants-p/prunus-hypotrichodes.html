Prunus hypotrichodes

Prunus hypotrichodes, commonly known as the smooth-chested cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, often grayish bark and small, ovate leaves. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, dark red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Prunus hypotrichodes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
