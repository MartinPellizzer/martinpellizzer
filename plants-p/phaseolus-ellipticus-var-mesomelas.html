Phaseolus ellipticus var. mesomelas

Phaseolus ellipticus var. mesomelas, commonly known as the Mexican runner bean, is a climbing legume native to Central America.

It produces elongated, elliptical pods that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when mature. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, white or pale pink flowers. It is cultivated for its edible beans, which are rich in protein and nutrients.

This variety is adapted to warm climates and requires a long growing season to produce a good yield.
