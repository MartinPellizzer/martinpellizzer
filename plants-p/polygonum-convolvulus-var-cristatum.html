Polygonum convolvulus var. cristatum

Polygonum convolvulus var. cristatum, commonly known as the crested smartweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 60 cm tall, characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems. The plant produces small, pink to reddish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a waxy coating.

The species is known for its distinctive, crested inflorescence, which gives it its common name.
