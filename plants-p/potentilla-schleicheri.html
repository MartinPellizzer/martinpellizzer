Potentilla schleicheri

Potentilla schleicheri, commonly known as the Schleicher's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately divided into five leaflets, which are typically hairy and have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
