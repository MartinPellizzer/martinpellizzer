Potentilla verna var. calliantha

Potentilla verna var. calliantha, commonly known as the early potentilla, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
