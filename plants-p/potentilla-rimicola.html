Potentilla rimicola

Potentilla rimicola, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, ovate leaves with a hairy texture, adapted to withstand harsh alpine conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its resilience and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
