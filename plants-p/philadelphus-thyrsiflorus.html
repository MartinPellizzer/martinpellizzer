Philadelphus thyrsiflorus

Philadelphus thyrsiflorus, commonly known as the sweet thimble or thimblebush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are typically serrated along the edges. Its growth habit is upright and bushy, with multiple branches that can reach heights of up to 6 feet.

Philadelphus thyrsiflorus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
