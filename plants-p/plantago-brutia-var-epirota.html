Plantago brutia var. epirota

Plantago brutia var. epirota, commonly known as the Epirotic plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region, particularly Greece and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces erect flowering stems with clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
