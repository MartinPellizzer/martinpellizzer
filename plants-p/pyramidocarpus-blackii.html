Pyramidocarpus blackii

Pyramidocarpus blackii, commonly known as the Black's pyramidocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and distinctive pyramid-shaped inflorescences. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are followed by woody, cone-like structures containing its seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
