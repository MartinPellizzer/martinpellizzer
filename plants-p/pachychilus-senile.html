Pachychilus senile

Pachychilus senile, commonly known as the silver leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, narrow leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the cooler months. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
