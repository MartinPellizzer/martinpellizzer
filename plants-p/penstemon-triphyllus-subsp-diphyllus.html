Penstemon triphyllus subsp. diphyllus

Penstemon triphyllus subsp.

diphyllus, commonly known as the two-leaved beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its leaves are opposite and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
