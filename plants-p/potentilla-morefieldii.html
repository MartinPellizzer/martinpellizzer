Potentilla morefieldii

Potentilla morefieldii, commonly known as Morefield's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are typically reddish-brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Potentilla morefieldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
