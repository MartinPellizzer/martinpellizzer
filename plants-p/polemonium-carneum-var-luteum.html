Polemonium carneum var. luteum

Polemonium carneum var. luteum, commonly known as the yellow Jacob's ladder, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its cascading clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to woodland gardens.
