Pyrus amygdaliformis var. lanceolata

Pyrus amygdaliformis var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate pear, is a variant of the pear tree characterized by its lance-shaped leaves.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or green when mature. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a deciduous canopy, making it suitable for ornamental and fruit cultivation.

The leaves are usually serrated and have a glossy surface, contributing to its visual appeal.
