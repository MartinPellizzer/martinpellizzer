Philibertia micrantha

Philibertia micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved filbert, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically arranged in compact heads, and it is known for its ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

Philibertia micrantha is often found in rocky or open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
