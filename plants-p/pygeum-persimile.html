Pygeum persimile

Pygeum persimile, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Theaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible fruits resembling small, yellowish persimmons. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
