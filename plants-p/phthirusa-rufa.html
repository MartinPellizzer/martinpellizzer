Phthirusa rufa

Phthirusa rufa, commonly known as the red-backed phthirusa, is a species of flowering plant native to specific regions in southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, herbaceous growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique floral structure and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
