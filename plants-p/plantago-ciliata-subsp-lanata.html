Plantago ciliata subsp. lanata

Plantago ciliata subsp.

lanata, commonly known as the hairy plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered with soft, hairy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces erect flowering stems bearing spikes of small, greenish-white flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides, fields, and gardens, and is adaptable to various environmental conditions.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
