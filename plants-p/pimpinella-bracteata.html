Pimpinella bracteata

Pimpinella bracteata, commonly known as the bracted cowparsley, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, typically growing in rocky or open habitats. The plant features slender stems and small, compound leaves with a fern-like appearance. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in umbels, and it produces small, dry fruits known as schizocarps.

It is often found in dry, sunny environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
