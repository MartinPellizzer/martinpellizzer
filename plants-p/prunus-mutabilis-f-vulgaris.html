Prunus mutabilis f. vulgaris

Prunus mutabilis f. vulgaris, commonly known as the variable cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus native to Europe.

It is characterized by its striking change in flower color from white to pink to red as the blooming season progresses. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. Its flowers appear in early spring, often before the leaves, and are followed by small, edible berries.

The botanical features of this plant make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
