Pleiotaxis oxylepis

Pleiotaxis oxylepis, commonly known as the mountain pleiotaxis, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
