Psilostrophe pumila

Psilostrophe pumila, commonly known as the dwarf psilostrophe, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands. The plant has slender, erect stems that are covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a reddish tinge. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months.

The species is noted for its low-growing habit and is sometimes used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.
