Potentilla anserina var. egedii

Potentilla anserina var. egedii, commonly known as the silver cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-white, hairy leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh alpine conditions. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
