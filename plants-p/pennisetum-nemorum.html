Pennisetum nemorum

Pennisetum nemorum, commonly known as woodland switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to moist woodlands in the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and features narrow, upright leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces fluffy, seed-bearing heads that are often seen in late summer and early fall. Pennisetum nemorum thrives in partial to full shade and requires consistently moist, well-drained soil.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for wildlife.
