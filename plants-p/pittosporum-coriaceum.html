Pittosporum coriaceum

Pittosporum coriaceum, commonly known as the rough-leaved pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features dark green, leathery leaves that are rough to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Pittosporum coriaceum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
