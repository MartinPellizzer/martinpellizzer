Polypodium alloiosorum

Polypodium alloiosorum, commonly known as the red-stemmed huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, and often reddish-colored stems that are covered in a dense layer of root hairs. The fronds are typically bipinnate, meaning they have two levels of division, and are usually arranged in a compact, rosette-like formation. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
