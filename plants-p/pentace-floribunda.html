Pentace floribunda

Pentace floribunda, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
