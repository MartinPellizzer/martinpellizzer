Pectis glabra f. pubescens

Pectis glabra f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy pectis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow and arranged in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
