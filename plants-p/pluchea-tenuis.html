Pluchea tenuis

Pluchea tenuis, commonly known as slenderleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wetlands and stream banks, thriving in temperate climates. Its small, blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall delicate appearance.
