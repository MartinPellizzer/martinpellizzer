Passiflora sanctae-barbarae

Passiflora sanctae-barbarae, commonly known as the sacred barbara passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped with prominent veins, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
