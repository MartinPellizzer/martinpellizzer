Pernettya myrtilloides var. linifolia

Pernettya myrtilloides var. linifolia, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It features small, dark blue berries that are edible and have a tart flavor, similar to other members of the Vacciniaceae family. The plant has oval, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, providing a dense canopy. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
