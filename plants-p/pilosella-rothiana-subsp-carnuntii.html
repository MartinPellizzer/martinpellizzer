Pilosella rothiana subsp. carnuntii

Pilosella rothiana subsp.

carnuntii, commonly known as the Carpathian hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has finely hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands. Pilosella rothiana subsp.

carnuntii is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
