Paspalum luxurians

Paspalum luxurians, commonly known as the velvet paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and fine, silky foliage that gives it a lush, carpet-like appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along waterways.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil, it is used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
