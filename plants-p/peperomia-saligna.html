Peperomia saligna

Peperomia saligna, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver or white veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of green or red, which are followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate moisture.

Peperomia saligna is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and low maintenance requirements.
