Polylobium tenellum

Polylobium tenellum, commonly known as the slender-leafed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in open, sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a soft, hairy texture, and it produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

It is often found in grasslands and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
