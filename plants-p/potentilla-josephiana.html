Potentilla josephiana

Potentilla josephiana, commonly known as Joseph's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided into five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla josephiana is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
