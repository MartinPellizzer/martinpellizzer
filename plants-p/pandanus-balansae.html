Pandanus balansae

Pandanus balansae, commonly known as the Balansa pandanus, is a species of palm native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant thrives in coastal and wetland areas, where it can tolerate salt spray and periodic flooding. Its fruit, which is a large, fibrous structure, contains seeds that are dispersed by water and animals.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various species.
