Poa papuana

Poa papuana, commonly known as the Papuan grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, dark green blades. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and alpine environments. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small and cylindrical.

Poa papuana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native insects and birds.
