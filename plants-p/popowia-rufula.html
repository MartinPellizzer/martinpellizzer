Popowia rufula

Popowia rufula, commonly known as the roughleaf popowia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, rough hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, leathery fruits containing several seeds. Popowia rufula is adapted to dry environments and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy species in xerophytic ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert flora and a subject of study in ecological and botanical research.
