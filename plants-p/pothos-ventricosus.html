Pothos ventricosus

Pothos ventricosus, commonly known as the swollen-leaf pothos, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its adaptability and ease of care make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
