Potamogeton densus f. minor

Potamogeton densus f. minor, commonly known as the dense pondweed.

It is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its tightly packed, dark green leaves that are typically narrow and elongated. This plant thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. Its rhizomes form dense mats near the bottom, contributing to water clarity by stabilizing sediments.

It plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
