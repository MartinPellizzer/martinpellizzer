Paeonia edulis var. sinensis

Paeonia edulis var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese peony, is a deciduous perennial plant native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow. The plant has palmately compound leaves and produces fleshy, edible roots that are used in traditional Chinese cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The Chinese peony is also valued for its ornamental beauty and cultural significance in Chinese history.
