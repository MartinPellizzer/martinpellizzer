Paspalum malacophyllum

Paspalum malacophyllum, commonly known as the coastal paspalum, is a perennial grass native to coastal regions of South America.

It thrives in sandy, saline soils and is often found in dune environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to withstand salt spray and wind, making it an important species in coastal stabilization.

Its ecological significance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife.
