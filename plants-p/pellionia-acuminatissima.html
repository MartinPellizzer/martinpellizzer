Pellionia acuminatissima

Pellionia acuminatissima, commonly known as the velvetleaf pellionia, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically bright green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in humid climates.
