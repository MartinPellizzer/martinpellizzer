Polygonum inundatum

Polygonum inundatum, commonly known as water pepper, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its erect, often reddish stems and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in dense clusters. The plant thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, stream banks, and other aquatic habitats. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a slightly hairy texture.

Polygonum inundatum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
