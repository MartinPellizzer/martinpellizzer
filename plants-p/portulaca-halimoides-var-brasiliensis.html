Portulaca halimoides var. brasiliensis

Portulaca halimoides var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian moss rose, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its propagation.

This species is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
