Philadelphus inodorus

Philadelphus inodorus, commonly known as the sweetpepper bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its botanical features include smooth, grayish bark and clusters of small, elliptical fruits that mature into red, fleshy drupes.

Philadelphus inodorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.
