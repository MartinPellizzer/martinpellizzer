Paspalum parvulum

Paspalum parvulum, commonly known as dwarf paspalum, is a small, perennial grass native to the Americas.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy areas, often forming dense, low-growing mats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers. It is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
