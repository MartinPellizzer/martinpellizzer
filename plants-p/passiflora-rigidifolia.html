Passiflora rigidifolia

Passiflora rigidifolia, commonly known as the rigid leaf passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, intricate flowers that feature colorful sepals, corona structures, and five petals. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are usually rigid and have a rough texture, contributing to its common name.

Passiflora rigidifolia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display.
