Pongamia velutina

Pongamia velutina, commonly known as velvet pongam, is a species of leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pods containing seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in various soil types.

Pongamia velutina is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
