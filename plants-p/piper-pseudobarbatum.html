Piper pseudobarbatum

Piper pseudobarbatum, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or false black pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 3,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its vine-like growth habit and produces small, green berries that turn red when mature. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and emit a mild peppery aroma.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
