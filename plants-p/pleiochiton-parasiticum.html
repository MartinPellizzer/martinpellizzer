Pleiochiton parasiticum

Pleiochiton parasiticum, commonly known as the parasitic orchid, is a unique flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a hemiparasitic species, meaning it derives some of its nutrients from host plants while also performing photosynthesis. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often attaching itself to the stems of other vegetation. Its roots form specialized structures called haustoria, which penetrate the host plant to absorb water and minerals.

The flowers of Pleiochiton parasiticum are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors that aid in attracting pollinators.
