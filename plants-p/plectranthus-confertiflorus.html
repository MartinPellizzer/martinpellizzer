Plectranthus confertiflorus

Plectranthus confertiflorus, commonly known as the African violet or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple, pink, and white. The leaves are typically rounded, velvety, and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

This plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
