Paederia scandens var. tomentosa

Paederia scandens var. tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy paederia, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or orange when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
