Pinguicula vulgaris var. juratensis

Pinguicula vulgaris var. juratensis, commonly known as the Juratensian butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to specific regions in Europe.

It is characterized by its glandular, sticky leaves that trap and digest small insects. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-poor environments such as bogs and marshes. Its flowers are usually small and white or pink, arranged in a raceme.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in humid conditions and its unique method of nutrient acquisition through insect digestion.
