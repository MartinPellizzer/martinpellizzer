Phyteuma occultans

Phyteuma occultans, commonly known as the hidden phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, and it is noted for its ornamental value in alpine gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
