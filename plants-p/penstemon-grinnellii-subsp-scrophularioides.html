Penstemon grinnellii subsp. scrophularioides

Penstemon grinnellii subsp.

scrophularioides, commonly known as the plains penstemon, is a perennial herb native to the central and western United States. It typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its showy, tubular flowers range in color from blue to purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
