Polystichopsis amabilis

Polystichopsis amabilis, commonly known as the sweet fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically light green to grayish in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Polystichopsis amabilis plays an important role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat by providing shelter and food for various small organisms.
