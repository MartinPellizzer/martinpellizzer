Palmijuncus cochleatus

Palmijuncus cochleatus, commonly known as the spiral palm, is a unique species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it an unusual and ornamental appearance. The plant has a slender, unbranched stem that grows upward, supporting the tightly coiled foliage. Its leaves are long and narrow, forming a dense, compact rosette at the top of the stem.

While it is not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and evolutionary adaptations.
