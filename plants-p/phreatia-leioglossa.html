Phreatia leioglossa

Phreatia leioglossa, commonly known as the smooth-lipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, slender stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive, smooth lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Phreatia leioglossa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
