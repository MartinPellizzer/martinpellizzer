Plectranthus anamudianus

Plectranthus anamudianus, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers. The flowers are usually violet or purple in color and are arranged in terminal spikes.

It is known for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
