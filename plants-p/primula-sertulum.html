Primula sertulum

Primula sertulum, commonly known as the Mexican primrose or Mexican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, often with a distinctive yellow or orange throat. Its leaves are oval-shaped, hairy, and arranged in a basal rosette.

Primula sertulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
