Pectis sinaloensis var. lancifolia

Pectis sinaloensis var. lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pectis, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers arranged in a cluster. It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
