Pultenaea praecipua subsp. praecipua

Pultenaea praecipua subsp.

praecipua, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pultenaea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is found in heathlands and open woodlands. It has small, pea-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often appearing in late winter to early spring.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrow, linear leaves and is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value.
