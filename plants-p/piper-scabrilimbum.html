Piper scabrilimbum

Piper scabrilimbum, commonly known as the rough-skinned pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
