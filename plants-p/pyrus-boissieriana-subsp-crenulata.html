Pyrus boissieriana subsp. crenulata

Pyrus boissieriana subsp.

crenulata, commonly known as the Crenulate Whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its elliptical, crenate leaves that are often densely hairy on the underside.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by edible, yellowish berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
