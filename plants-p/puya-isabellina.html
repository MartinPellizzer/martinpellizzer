Puya isabellina

Puya isabellina, commonly known as the Isabelline torchflower, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that bears vibrant red bracts. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom sequentially along the central axis. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Puya isabellina is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its dramatic, fiery appearance.
