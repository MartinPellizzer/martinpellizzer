Peliosanthes violacea var. clarkei

Peliosanthes violacea var. clarkei, commonly known as the Clarke's violet flame.

This variant is a rare and striking plant characterized by its deep violet to almost purple flowers, which bloom in dense clusters. It is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in shaded, humid environments. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically a darker green, contrasting beautifully with its vibrant floral display.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
