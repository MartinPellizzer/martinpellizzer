Phalaenopsis amabilis var. longifolia

Phalaenopsis amabilis var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered moth orchid, is a popular epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender petals and a compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive lip that resembles a moth's wing. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

This variety is prized for its longevity and ability to bloom repeatedly over several years.
