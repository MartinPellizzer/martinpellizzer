Pleurothallis lopezii

Pleurothallis lopezii, commonly known as the Lopez's pleurothallis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of South America.

This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees and has small, delicate leaves that are adapted to the humid, shaded conditions of its habitat. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with a unique structure that includes a labellum, which is a specialized petal used in pollination. The plant produces numerous tiny, elongated pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and relatively easy care requirements.
