Pleurothallis pisinna

Pleurothallis pisinna, commonly known as the Pisinna orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically greenish-white with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil.

Pleurothallis pisinna is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
