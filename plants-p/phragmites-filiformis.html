Phragmites filiformis

Phragmites filiformis, commonly known as slender reed, is a tall, grass-like plant that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its narrow, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense clusters at the top of the stalks. Its rhizomes form extensive underground networks, contributing to its ability to colonize and stabilize wetland soils.

Phragmites filiformis plays a vital role in filtering water and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
