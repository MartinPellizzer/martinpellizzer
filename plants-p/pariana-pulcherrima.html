Pariana pulcherrima

Pariana pulcherrima, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist lowland areas.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
