Primula cernua

Primula cernua, commonly known as the drooping primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically pale yellow or white, with five petals that curve downward, giving the plant its characteristic drooping appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
