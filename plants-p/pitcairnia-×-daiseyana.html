Pitcairnia × daiseyana

Pitcairnia × daiseyana, commonly known as the hybrid bromeliad, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Pitcairnia and another bromeliad species.

It is a perennial evergreen plant that typically grows as a compact rosette of fleshy, sword-shaped leaves. The plant is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid environments. Its foliage is often a striking green with a waxy texture, and it produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences in shades of red, orange, or yellow.

It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
