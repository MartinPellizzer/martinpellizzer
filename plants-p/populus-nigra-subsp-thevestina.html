Populus nigra subsp. thevestina

Populus nigra subsp.

thevestina, commonly known as the Spanish black poplar, is a subspecies native to southern Europe and North Africa. It is a deciduous tree characterized by its dark, fissured bark and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces catkins in early spring, which are essential for wind pollination. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and wetlands.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
