Passerina virescens

Passerina virescens, commonly known as the green-backed tube-nosed bat.

This plant is a species of flowering plant native to specific regions in South America. It is characterized by its slender, tubular flowers that are typically greenish in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by nocturnal insects, contributing to its ecological niche.
