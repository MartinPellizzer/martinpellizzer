Philodendron rubropunctatum

Philodendron rubropunctatum, commonly known as the red-spotted philodendron, is a climbing tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with prominent red or orange spots, which become more pronounced under bright light. The plant has a vining growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by green, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Due to its striking foliage, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and terrariums.
