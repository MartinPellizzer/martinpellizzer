Piper exserens var. tocotanum

Piper exserens var. tocotanum, commonly known as the Tocotanum pepper, is a variant of the Piper exserens species native to Central America.

It is a climbing vine with elongated, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, thin fruits that turn red when mature. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphological traits and potential medicinal properties.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional use in local herbal practices.
