Pinalia acervata

Pinalia acervata, commonly known as the clustered pincushion plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescence composed of numerous small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a decorative plant.

Pinalia acervata is also noted for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
