Polygala pinnata

Polygala pinnata, commonly known as the featherleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate leaves that resemble feathers. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish to greenish flowers, which are typically arranged in spikes. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Polygala pinnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
