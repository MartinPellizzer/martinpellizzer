Pentas lanceolata var. angustifolia

Pentas lanceolata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf pentas, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features small, five-petaled flowers that are typically bright red or orange, clustered in dense inflorescences. The leaves are lance-shaped and narrow, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
