Potentilla brassii

Potentilla brassii, commonly known as the golden potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, wiry stems and small, rounded leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Potentilla brassii thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
