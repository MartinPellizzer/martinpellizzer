Podocarpus papuanus

Podocarpus papuanus, commonly known as the Papuan podocarp, is a coniferous tree native to New Guinea.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and cone-bearing structure. The plant has scale-like leaves arranged in compact clusters, typical of many podocarps. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in mountainous regions with high humidity.

The wood of Podocarpus papuanus is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.
