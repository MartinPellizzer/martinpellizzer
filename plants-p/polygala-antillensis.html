Polygala antillensis

Polygala antillensis, commonly known as the Bahamian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, adapting to saline and dry conditions. Its flowers are often pale yellow or greenish-white, with a distinctive shape that attracts specific pollinators.

The species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in supporting local insect populations.
