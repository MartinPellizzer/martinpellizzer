Pilosella noguerensis

Pilosella noguerensis, commonly known as the Noguera's pearly weed, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while the flowers are small and white with a central yellow disk.

Pilosella noguerensis is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a characteristic species of Mediterranean vegetation.
