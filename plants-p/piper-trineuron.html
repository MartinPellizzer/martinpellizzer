Piper trineuron

Piper trineuron, commonly known as the three-nerve pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with three distinct nerves, giving it its common name. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
