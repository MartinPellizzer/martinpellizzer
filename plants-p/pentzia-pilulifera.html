Pentzia pilulifera

Pentzia pilulifera, commonly known as the yellow lily or African lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round seeds enclosed in a hard, pill-like capsule, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Pentzia pilulifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
