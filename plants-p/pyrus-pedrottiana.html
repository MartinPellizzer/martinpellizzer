Pyrus pedrottiana

Pyrus pedrottiana, commonly known as the Pedrotti pear, is a rare and ornamental species of pear native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically small and yellowish in color. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value rather than its fruit yield. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
