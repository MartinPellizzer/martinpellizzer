Perichlaena richardii

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It features simple, alternate leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, typical of the spurge family.

Due to its ornamental value and toxicity, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling.
