Penstemon palmeri var. palmeri

Penstemon palmeri var. palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often with a distinctive white or pale throat. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Penstemon palmeri var. palmeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a pollinator plant.
