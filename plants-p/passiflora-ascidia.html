Passiflora ascidia

Passiflora ascidia, commonly known as the sea passionflower, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers with intricate patterns resembling the Passion of Christ, a feature that gives it its common name. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces edible, yellow-orange berries that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in saline environments, making it a hardy species adapted to coastal habitats.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it a notable example of adaptive evolution in marine-influenced ecosystems.
