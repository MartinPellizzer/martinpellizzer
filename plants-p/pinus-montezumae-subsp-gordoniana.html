Pinus montezumae subsp. gordoniana

Pinus montezumae subsp.

gordoniana, commonly known as the Mexican bristlecone pine, is a subspecies native to the high elevations of central Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, dark green foliage.

The cones are typically elongated and have a distinctive bristle-like structure at the base. This subspecies is adapted to cold, dry conditions and can live for several centuries.

Its wood is durable and has been used historically by indigenous peoples for construction and tool-making.
