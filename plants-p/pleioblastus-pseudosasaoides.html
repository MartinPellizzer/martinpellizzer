Pleioblastus pseudosasaoides

Pleioblastus pseudosasaoides, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or the silver bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. Native to East Asia, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions. It is a clump-forming bamboo that grows slowly, making it suitable for ornamental planting in gardens.

Its foliage is typically a vibrant green, with a soft texture that adds visual interest to landscapes.
