Physalis esculenta

Physalis esculenta, commonly known as the Chinese lantern or Cape gooseberry, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is characterized by its inflated, papery calyx that encloses the fruit, which is typically orange or red in color. The fruit is edible, with a tangy, slightly sweet flavor, and is often used in culinary applications. The plant is native to South America but is now cultivated worldwide.

It thrives in temperate climates and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
