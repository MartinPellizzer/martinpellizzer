Pelargonium berteroanum

Pelargonium berteroanum, commonly known as the Brazilian geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of five-petaled blooms in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Pelargonium berteroanum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
