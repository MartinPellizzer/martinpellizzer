Passiflora populifolia

Passiflora populifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking, showy flowers that feature a complex structure with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, blue-black berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.

Passiflora populifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
