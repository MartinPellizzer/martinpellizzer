Pleurothallis eximia

Pleurothallis eximia, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient absorption.

Pleurothallis eximia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
