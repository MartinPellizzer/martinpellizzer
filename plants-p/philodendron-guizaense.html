Philodendron guizaense

Philodendron guizaense, commonly known as the Guizaensis Philodendron, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically a deep green with a velvety texture. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, known for its unique inflorescence structure consisting of a spadix and a spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse settings.
