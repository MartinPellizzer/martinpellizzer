Paspalum orbiculare

Paspalum orbiculare, commonly known as the roundleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed sites.

Paspalum orbiculare is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in certain environments.
