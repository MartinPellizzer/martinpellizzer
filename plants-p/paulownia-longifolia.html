Paulownia longifolia

Paulownia longifolia, commonly known as the common paulownia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed. The tree produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, which are typically pale purple or white. Its wood is lightweight and highly valued for furniture and musical instruments due to its strength and workability.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its role in ecological restoration.
