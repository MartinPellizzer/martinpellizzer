Palura pendula

Palura pendula, commonly known as the weeping palura, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, drooping branches that give it a distinctive, cascading appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy, dark green surface.

Palura pendula is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
