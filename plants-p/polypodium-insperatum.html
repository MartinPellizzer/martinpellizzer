Polypodium insperatum

Polypodium insperatum, commonly known as the golden polypody, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bright green with a slightly glossy appearance. The plant produces spores on the underside of its fronds, which are arranged in clusters called sori. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Polypodium insperatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
