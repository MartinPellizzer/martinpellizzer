Pilosella micromegas

Pilosella micromegas, commonly known as the small-headed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters.

Pilosella micromegas is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in cool, moist environments.
