Pyrus maderensis

Pyrus maderensis, commonly known as the Madeira pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the Azores, specifically the island of Madeira, where it grows in humid, subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, a small, yellowish pear, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Pyrus maderensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and unique foliage.
