Prunus spinosa subsp. rapaicsii

Prunus spinosa subsp.

rapaicsii, commonly known as the Caucasian blackthorn, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark blue to black berries.

The plant typically grows in mountainous and forested areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces white or pale pink flowers in spring.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
