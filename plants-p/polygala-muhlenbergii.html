Polygala muhlenbergii

Polygala muhlenbergii, commonly known as the Muhlenberg's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its flowers are usually pink to purple and have a distinctive, spurred structure.

It is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
