Potamogeton × malainoides

Potamogeton × malainoides, commonly known as the hybrid water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Potamogeton crispus and Potamogeton natans.

It has slender, branching stems that are often covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters. It is commonly found in shallow, freshwater habitats such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers.

Due to its hybrid nature, it can display a wide range of morphological variations, making it challenging to identify without detailed study.
