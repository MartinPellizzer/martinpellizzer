Pyrus pyraster f. sericea

Pyrus pyraster f. sericea, commonly known as the silver-leaved pear, is a botanical variant of the common pear.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a striking appearance in both garden and natural settings. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, with a compact and rounded form. Its flowers are white and clustered, appearing in early spring before the leaves fully emerge.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
