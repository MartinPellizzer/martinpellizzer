Plumeria floribunda var. calycina

Plumeria floribunda var. calycina, commonly known as the West Indian frangipani, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of yellow, orange, and white. The leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a waxy texture. The plant produces a unique, fleshy fruit that splits open to reveal numerous seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
