Pedilonum cuculliferum

Pedilonum cuculliferum, commonly known as the hooded bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that are often adorned with a hood-like structure, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers a sunny to partially shaded environment. Its flowers range in color from white to pale blue, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Pedilonum cuculliferum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
