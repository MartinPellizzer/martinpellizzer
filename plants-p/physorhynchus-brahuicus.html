Physorhynchus brahuicus

Physorhynchus brahuicus, commonly known as the Indian water fern, is a unique aquatic fern belonging to the family Marsileaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, floating leaves that resemble a delicate lace, allowing it to thrive in slow-moving freshwater environments. This species plays an important ecological role in maintaining water quality and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
