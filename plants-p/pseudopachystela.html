Pseudopachystela

Pseudopachystela, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated, and often translucent leaves that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant produces delicate, white or pale yellow flowers with intricate patterns, typical of many orchids. Pseudopachystela is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is highly sought after in botanical research and conservation efforts.
