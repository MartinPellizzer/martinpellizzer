Pouteria kingiana

Pouteria kingiana, commonly known as the purple sapote, is a tropical tree native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. The tree bears fruit that is typically pear-shaped with a thick, leathery skin and a sweet, creamy pulp. The fruit contains a single large seed and is often used in culinary applications.

Botanically, it belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
