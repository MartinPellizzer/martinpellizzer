Pongelion calycinum

Pongelion calycinum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological importance in its native regions.
