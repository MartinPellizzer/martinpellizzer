Panicum mesocomum

Panicum mesocomum, commonly known as dwarf panicgrass, is a low-growing grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically forms dense mats of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. This plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in ecological restoration projects.
