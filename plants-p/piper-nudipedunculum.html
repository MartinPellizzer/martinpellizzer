Piper nudipedunculum

Piper nudipedunculum, commonly known as the slender pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and emits a mild peppery scent. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
