Pyrus uzbezicus

Pyrus uzbezicus, commonly known as the Uzbek pear, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of white flowers in spring. The fruit is a small, edible pear with a sweet, aromatic flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

The plant is relatively hardy and can adapt to a variety of soil types.
