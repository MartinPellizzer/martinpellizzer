Pteridophyllum decipiens

Pteridophyllum decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive fern, is a unique member of the fern family with distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its intricate fronds that resemble those of other fern species, potentially leading to misidentification. The plant exhibits a complex life cycle involving both a sporophyte and a gametophyte stage. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are arranged in a specific pattern that aids in spore dispersal.

This species thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.
