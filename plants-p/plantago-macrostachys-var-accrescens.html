Plantago macrostachys var. accrescens

Plantago macrostachys var. accrescens, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture and produces spikes of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in various habitats.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
