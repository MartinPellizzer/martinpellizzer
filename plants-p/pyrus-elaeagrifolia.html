Pyrus elaeagrifolia

Pyrus elaeagrifolia, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are oval to elliptical in shape, often with a slightly serrated edge. Its fruit is a small, edible pome that matures to a yellowish color in autumn.

Pyrus elaeagrifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
