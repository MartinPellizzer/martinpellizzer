Picrocardia resinosa

Picrocardia resinosa, commonly known as the resinous heartleaf, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a waxy, resinous coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its unique morphology and adaptation to drought make it an interesting subject for botanical study.

The resinous substance it exudes has been historically used by indigenous communities for various purposes.
