Peucedanum vittijugum

Peucedanum vittijugum, commonly known as the purple anise or violet anise, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and flowers.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale purple flowers, which are followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. Its leaves are finely divided and emit a distinctive anise-like scent when crushed.

Peucedanum vittijugum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
