Potentilla bolanderi

Potentilla bolanderi, commonly known as Bolander's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, and its leaves are usually compound, with five leaflets. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its limited range and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
