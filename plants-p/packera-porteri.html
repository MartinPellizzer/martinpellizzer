Packera porteri

Packera porteri, commonly known as the mountain buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are arranged in a flat-topped cluster. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

Packera porteri is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
