Potamogeton perfoliatus f. ovatolanceolatus

Potamogeton perfoliatus f. ovatolanceolatus, commonly known as the ovate-lanceolate pondweed, is a variant of the pondweed species native to freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often slightly serrated along the edges. The plant typically grows in shallow waters, forming dense underwater stands. Its leaves are usually arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikelets.

This subspecies is often found in lakes, rivers, and slow-moving streams, contributing to aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
