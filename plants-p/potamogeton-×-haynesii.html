Potamogeton × haynesii

Potamogeton × haynesii, commonly known as the hybrid water plant, is a perennial aquatic plant that typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton crispus and Potamogeton obtusifolius, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged, with some leaves emerging above the water surface. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Due to its hybrid nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in managed water bodies.
