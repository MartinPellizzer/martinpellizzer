Pseudolysimachion spurium subsp. paniculatum

Pseudolysimachion spurium subsp.

paniculatum, commonly known as the panicled pseudolysimachion, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically produces long, branched inflorescences, which give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture for its unique floral display.
