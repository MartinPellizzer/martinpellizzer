Pseudophyllanthus ovalis

Pseudophyllanthus ovalis, commonly known as the oval leaf phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Phyllanthaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying tropical flora.
