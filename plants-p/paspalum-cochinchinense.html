Paspalum cochinchinense

Paspalum cochinchinense, commonly known as Chinese paspalum, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, bisexual flowers. It is often found in moist soils near water bodies, such as rivers and marshes.

Its adaptability to wet environments makes it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
