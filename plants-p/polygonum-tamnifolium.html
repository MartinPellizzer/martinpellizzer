Polygonum tamnifolium

Polygonum tamnifolium, commonly known as redroot smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or disturbed soils, such as along stream banks and in marshy areas. The plant has upright stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface, and they have a distinctive reddish tint at the base.

Polygonum tamnifolium is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
