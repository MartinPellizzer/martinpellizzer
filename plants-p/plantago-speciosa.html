Plantago speciosa

Plantago speciosa, commonly known as the showy plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is valued for its medicinal properties and ornamental appeal.
