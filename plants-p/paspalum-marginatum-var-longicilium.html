Paspalum marginatum var. longicilium

Paspalum marginatum var. longicilium, commonly known as the long-hair paspalum, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its adaptability makes it a common weed in agricultural and natural habitats.
