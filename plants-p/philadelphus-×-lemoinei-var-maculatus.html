Philadelphus × lemoinei var. maculatus

Philadelphus × lemoinei var. maculatus, commonly known as the spotted mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically ovate with a slightly serrated margin and a dark green upper surface. This variety is distinguished by its distinctive red or purple spots on the undersides of its leaves.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
