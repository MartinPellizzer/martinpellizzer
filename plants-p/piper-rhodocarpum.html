Piper rhodocarpum

Piper rhodocarpum, commonly known as the red-rooted pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and clusters of tiny, white flowers that develop into small, dark purple berries. It is often found in humid, lowland forests and thrives in shaded environments.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
