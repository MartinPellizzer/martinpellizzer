Pinanga kuhlii var. sumatrana

Pinanga kuhlii var. sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Sumatra and Borneo. This plant is characterized by its slender, clustered stems and long, pinnate leaves that give it a graceful appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, clustered fruits.
