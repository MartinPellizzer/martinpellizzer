Podocarpus gaussenii

Podocarpus gaussenii, commonly known as the Gaussen's podocarp, is a flowering plant belonging to the Podocarpaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and conifer-like appearance, with needle-like leaves arranged in spirals. It produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, a feature typical of many coniferous species.

Podocarpus gaussenii is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its dense, lush growth habit.
