Piper blancoi

Piper blancoi, commonly known as the white pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into the fruits used to make white pepper.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns from green to black as it matures.
