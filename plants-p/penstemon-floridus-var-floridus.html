Penstemon floridus var. floridus

Penstemon floridus var. floridus, commonly known as the Florida beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, which help it thrive in well-drained soils. It is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas, preferring full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
