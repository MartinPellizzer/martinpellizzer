Pterocarpus pubescens

Pterocarpus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy pterocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its woody, often twisted trunk and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing flat, dark seeds. Its bark and wood are valued for their medicinal properties and use in traditional practices.

The species is also appreciated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
