Polemonium oregonense

Polemonium oregonense, commonly known as Oregon garden phlox, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features upright stems that bear clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
