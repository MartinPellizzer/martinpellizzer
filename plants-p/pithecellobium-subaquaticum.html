Pithecellobium subaquaticum

Pithecellobium subaquaticum, commonly known as the aquatic pithecellobium, is a species of legume native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its aquatic or semi-aquatic habitat, often found in marshy areas or along waterways. The plant has long, slender stems that can float on the water's surface, supported by aerial roots. Its small, pea-like flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces pods containing edible seeds.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing food for various wildlife species.
