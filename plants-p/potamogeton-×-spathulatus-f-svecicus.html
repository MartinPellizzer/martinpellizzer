Potamogeton × spathulatus f. svecicus

Potamogeton × spathulatus f. svecicus, commonly known as the Svecian broadleaf pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves that are typically arranged in whorls along the stem. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater habitats such as lakes and slow-moving rivers. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with male and female flowers usually occurring on separate plants.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to varying water conditions and its role in providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
