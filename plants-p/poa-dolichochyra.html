Poa dolichochyra

Poa dolichochyra, commonly known as the long-awned bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green leaves and distinctive, elongated awns on its seed heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Poa dolichochyra is also known for its ability to thrive in partial shade, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
