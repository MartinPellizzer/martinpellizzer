Phaseolus opacus

Phaseolus opacus, commonly known as the dark runner bean, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and distinctive, elongated pods that mature to a dark brown color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and is typically grown for its edible beans, which are rich in protein. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Phaseolus opacus is a member of the legume family and plays an important role in traditional agriculture and cuisine.
