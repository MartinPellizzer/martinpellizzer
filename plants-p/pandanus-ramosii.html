Pandanus ramosii

Pandanus ramosii, commonly known as the bottlebrush palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces fragrant, bottlebrush-like inflorescences that are both ornamental and used in floral arrangements. Its adaptability to coastal and sandy soils makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.

Pandanus ramosii is also valued for its cultural significance in various island communities.
