Phlomis lycia

Phlomis lycia, commonly known as Lycian firethorn, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features upright stems covered in soft, grayish-green leaves with a fuzzy texture. Its showy, tubular flowers range in color from pink to red, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
