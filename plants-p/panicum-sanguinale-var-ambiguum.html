Panicum sanguinale var. ambiguum

Panicum sanguinale var. ambiguum, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a variant of the panic grass genus native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its reddish tinge in the inflorescence, which distinguishes it from other Panicum species. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it produces dense, spike-like clusters of small, grassy flowers.

This variant is often used in ornamental horticulture for its distinctive color and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
