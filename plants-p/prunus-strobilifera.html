Prunus strobilifera

Prunus strobilifera, commonly known as the Chinese sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China and neighboring regions.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart red berries in the summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, with finely toothed margins, and its bark is smooth and grayish-brown.

Prunus strobilifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fruit.
