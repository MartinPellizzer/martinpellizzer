Pelargonium nigrum

Pelargonium nigrum, commonly known as the black geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which contrast with its green foliage. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and produces small, rounded leaves. It blooms in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pelargonium nigrum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
