Pisonia discolor var. intermedia

Pisonia discolor var. intermedia, commonly known as the red pisonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that surround its small, white flowers, creating a visually dramatic display. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, while its fruits are capsules that release tiny seeds when mature.

This species is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
