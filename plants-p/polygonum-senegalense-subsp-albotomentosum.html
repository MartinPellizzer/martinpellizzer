Polygonum senegalense subsp. albotomentosum

Polygonum senegalense subsp.

albotomentosum, commonly known as white-hairy knotweed, is a perennial herb native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its stout, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
