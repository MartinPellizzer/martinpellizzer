Pyrus tiliifolia

Pyrus tiliifolia, commonly known as the Persian whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by edible, yellowish fruits that are similar to apples.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
