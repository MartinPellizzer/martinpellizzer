Pilosella megavasconica

Pilosella megavasconica, commonly known as the giant hairgrass, is a flowering plant species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant is notable for its dense, fluffy inflorescence, which consists of numerous small white to pale yellow flowers. Pilosella megavasconica thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
