Philodendron zhuanum

Philodendron zhuanum, commonly known as the Chinese heartleaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by bright red berries, which are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded gardens.
