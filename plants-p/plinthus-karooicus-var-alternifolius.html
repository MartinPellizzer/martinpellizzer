Plinthus karooicus var. alternifolius

Plinthus karooicus var. alternifolius, commonly known as the Karoo alternate-leaf plinthus, is a distinctive plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its alternate arrangement of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in compact spikes, which are typically borne on short, lateral branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
