Parodia castanea

Parodia castanea, commonly known as the orange fairy or orange ball cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its globular shape and smooth, pale green to yellowish skin. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on a short stem. Parodia castanea is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

It is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
