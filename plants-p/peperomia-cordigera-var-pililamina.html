Peperomia cordigera var. pililamina

Peperomia cordigera var. pililamina, commonly known as the velvet peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers, which are typically hidden among the foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a favored ornamental plant.
