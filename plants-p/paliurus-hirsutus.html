Paliurus hirsutus

Paliurus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy paliurus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to regions of China, Korea, and Japan.

It belongs to the Rhamnaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
