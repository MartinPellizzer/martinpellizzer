Piper rarum

Piper rarum, commonly known as the rare pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly the Amazon basin, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some local cuisines. Its leaves are often used in herbal preparations due to their aromatic properties.

However, due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Piper rarum is considered a rare and endangered species.
