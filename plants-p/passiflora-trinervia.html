Passiflora trinervia

Passiflora trinervia, commonly known as the pink passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a unique blend of colors, including pink, white, and blue, along with intricate patterns. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three distinct leaflets, and it thrives in warm, humid environments.

Passiflora trinervia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
