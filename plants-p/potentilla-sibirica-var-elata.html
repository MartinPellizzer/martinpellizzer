Potentilla sibirica var. elata

Potentilla sibirica var. elata, commonly known as the tall Siberian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the cold regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, serrated leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
