Panicum aristellum

Panicum aristellum, commonly known as the aristolochia paniculate, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy environments and is native to parts of Africa and Asia. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight hairy texture.

While it is not widely cultivated for ornamental purposes, it plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
