Panicum crus-galli var. brevisetum

Panicum crus-galli var. brevisetum, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a variant of the panic grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a grassy plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically reddish in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its distinctive appearance.
