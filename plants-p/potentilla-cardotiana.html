Potentilla cardotiana

Potentilla cardotiana, commonly known as Cardot's cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are usually hairy and serrated.

Potentilla cardotiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
