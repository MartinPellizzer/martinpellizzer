Potentilla wimanniana subsp. euwiemanniana

Potentilla wimanniana subsp.

euwiemanniana, commonly known as the Wiman's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with multiple stems that often form dense clusters.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters of five, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.". Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated margins and a hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and long blooming period.
