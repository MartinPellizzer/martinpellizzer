Potamogeton chakassiensis

Potamogeton chakassiensis, commonly known as Chakassian pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
