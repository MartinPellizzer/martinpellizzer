Polygonum dumetorum var. subalatum

Polygonum dumetorum var. subalatum, commonly known as the alpine knotweed, is a perennial herb native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. The plant features erect, slender stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink to reddish in color, appearing in late summer.

It plays a significant ecological role in stabilizing alpine soils and providing habitat for various insect species.
