Pachira amazonica

Pachira amazonica, commonly known as the money plant or Brazilian money tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by velvety, egg-shaped fruits. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its air-purifying properties.

Pachira amazonica is also valued for its cultural significance in some regions, where it is believed to bring good fortune.
