Pseudomariscus cyperoides

Pseudomariscus cyperoides, commonly known as the marsh sedge, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in dense clusters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are often inconspicuous, with three stamens and a single pistil, typical of the sedge family.

Pseudomariscus cyperoides plays an important role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
