Prolongoa hispanica

Prolongoa hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
