Paspalum paniculatum subsp. umbrosum

Paspalum paniculatum subsp.

umbrosum, commonly known as the dark paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, dark green leaves.

The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are often tinged with purple or brown hues. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
