Pereskia bahiensis subsp. minensis

Pereskia bahiensis subsp.

minensis, commonly known as the Minas pereskia, is a succulent shrub native to Brazil. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, a common adaptation in arid environments.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
