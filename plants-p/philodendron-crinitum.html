Philodendron crinitum

Philodendron crinitum, commonly known as the curly hair philodendron, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, waxy, and often curling leaves that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, though it is primarily grown for its foliage rather than its blooms. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its air-purifying qualities make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
