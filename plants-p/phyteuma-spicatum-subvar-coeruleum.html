Phyteuma spicatum subvar. coeruleum

Phyteuma spicatum subvar. coeruleum, commonly known as the blue phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems that bear clusters of blue to lavender-colored flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation areas.
