Prunus elaeagrifolia

Prunus elaeagrifolia, commonly known as the Chinese sand pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, spreading branches and small, dark green leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, round, yellow fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit, which is used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers a sunny to partially shaded location.
