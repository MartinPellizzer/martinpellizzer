Prosthechea semiaperta

Prosthechea semiaperta, commonly known as the semiapertum orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces striking, fragrant flowers with a distinctive violet and white coloration. The plant typically grows on trees in humid, tropical environments, where it thrives in well-lit conditions. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a raceme, and it blooms during the wet season.

The botanical features of Prosthechea semiaperta make it a popular choice for both horticultural collections and botanical studies.
