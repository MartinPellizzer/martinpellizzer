Physostemon lanceolatus

Physostemon lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceleaf physostemon, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often hairy underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plants for wet areas.
