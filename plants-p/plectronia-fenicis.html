Plectronia fenicis

Plectronia fenicis, commonly known as the fireweed or fireplant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Plectronia fenicis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and recognizable species within the Caryophyllaceae family.
