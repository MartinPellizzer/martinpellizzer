Pinus sylvestris subsp. pumilio

Pinus sylvestris subsp.

pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf Scots pine, is a subspecies of the Scots pine native to the northern regions of Europe and western Siberia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit, typically forming small trees or shrubs with a dense, rounded crown.

The leaves are short, needle-like, and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its cones are small and usually hang downward, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is well adapted to harsh, alpine or subarctic environments, showcasing resilience to cold and poor soil conditions.
