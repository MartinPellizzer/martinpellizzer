Phyllostachys boryana

Phyllostachys boryana, commonly known as the Boryana bamboo, is a cold-hardy grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Phyllostachys boryana is also valued for its use in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of sustainable bamboo products.
