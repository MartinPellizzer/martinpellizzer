Piper tepicanum

Piper tepicanum, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a slightly rough texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
