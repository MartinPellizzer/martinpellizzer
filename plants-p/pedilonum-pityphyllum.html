Pedilonum pityphyllum

Pedilonum pityphyllum, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems that produce clusters of bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers in the summer months. The plant has palmately divided leaves with three to five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, making it an important plant in ecological habitats.
