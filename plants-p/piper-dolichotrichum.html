Piper dolichotrichum

Piper dolichotrichum, commonly known as the long-hair pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is cultivated for its aromatic leaves and fruits. Its botanical features include a climbing habit, opposite leaves, and a pungent flavor similar to other members of the pepper family.

It is often found in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests.
