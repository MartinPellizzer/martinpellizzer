Pleopeltis lepidotricha

Pleopeltis lepidotricha, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a rare and unique fern species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety fronds that give it its common name, and it thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant has a short, creeping rhizome with a dense covering of brown, fuzzy hairs. Its leaves are usually divided into small, finger-like segments, and it produces sporangia on the undersides of its fronds.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and endangered species in its natural range.
