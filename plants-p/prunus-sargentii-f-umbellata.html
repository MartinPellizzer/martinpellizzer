Prunus sargentii f. umbellata

Prunus sargentii f. umbellata, commonly known as the Sargent cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a glossy green surface. It produces small, dark red berries that are edible but not widely cultivated for fruit.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its spring blossoms and winter structure.
