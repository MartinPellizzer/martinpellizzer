Phyllanthus myrsinites

Phyllanthus myrsinites, commonly known as the African date palm, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by fleshy, red berries that are edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
