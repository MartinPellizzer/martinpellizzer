Potentilla fruticosa typica

Potentilla fruticosa typica, commonly known as the common cinquefoil, is a shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense, bushy foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and are often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
