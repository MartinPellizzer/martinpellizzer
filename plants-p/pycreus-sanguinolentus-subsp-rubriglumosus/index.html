Pycreus Sanguinolentus Subsp. Rubriglumosus

Pycreus sanguinolentus subsp. rubriglumosus is a rare and striking plant known for its vibrant red blooms and unique foliage. Native to the misty highlands of South America, this subspecies thrives in cool, humid environments. Its name, meaning "blood-red glum," refers to the intense coloration of its flowers and seed pods. Despite its beauty, it remains obscure and under-studied by botanists. Cultivating this plant requires careful attention to soil and climate conditions to ensure its survival.
