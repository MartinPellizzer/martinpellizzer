Piper piluliferum

Piper piluliferum, commonly known as the "bead vine," is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its small, bead-like fruits that hang in clusters, giving it its common name. Its leaves are typically ovate with a glossy surface and emit a mild, peppery scent.

Piper piluliferum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
