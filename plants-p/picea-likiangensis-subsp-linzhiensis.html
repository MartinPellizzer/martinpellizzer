Picea likiangensis subsp. linzhiensis

Picea likiangensis subsp.

linzhiensis, commonly known as the Linzhi fir, is a subspecies of the Chinese fir native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves.

The tree produces small, cylindrical cones that contain winged seeds, typical of the Pinaceae family. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous forests at elevations above 3,000 meters.

This subspecies plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
