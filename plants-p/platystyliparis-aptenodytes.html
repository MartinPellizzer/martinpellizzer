Platystyliparis aptenodytes

Platystyliparis aptenodytes, commonly known as the African violet, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant has a compact growth habit, making it popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.

Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid environments, while its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
