Portulaca patens

Portulaca patens, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is an annual herb that thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and full sunlight. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, opening in the morning and closing in the afternoon. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Portulaca patens is often found growing along beaches, dunes, and disturbed coastal areas.
