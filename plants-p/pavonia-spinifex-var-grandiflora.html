Pavonia spinifex var. grandiflora

Pavonia spinifex var. grandiflora, commonly known as the broadleaf pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in wetlands.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for wetland restoration and ornamental gardening.
