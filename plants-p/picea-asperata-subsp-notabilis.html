Picea asperata subsp. notabilis

Picea asperata subsp.

notabilis, commonly known as the Himalayan fir, is a coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its needle-like leaves, which are typically dark green and have a rough texture.

The tree produces cones that are typically pendulous and contain seeds surrounded by a woody covering. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist climates.

This subspecies is valued for its timber and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
