Phylica vulgaris var. burchellii

Phylica vulgaris var. burchellii, commonly known as the silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-white foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky areas.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and erosion control.
