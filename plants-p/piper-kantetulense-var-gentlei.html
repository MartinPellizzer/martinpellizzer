Piper kantetulense var. gentlei

Piper kantetulense var. gentlei, commonly known as the gentle pepper plant, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, serrated leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a red color, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical gardens and herbal practices.
