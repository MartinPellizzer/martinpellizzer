Phaseolus monophyllus f. brevipetiolata

Phaseolus monophyllus f. brevipetiolata, commonly known as the short-petioled common bean, is a botanical variant of the common bean species.

It is characterized by its shorter petioles compared to the typical form, which affects its overall growth structure. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and is used in both culinary and agricultural contexts.
