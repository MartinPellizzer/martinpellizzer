Ptychosperma waitianum

Ptychosperma waitianum, commonly known as the Waiti palm, is a species of palm native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers followed by elongated, dark blue-black fruits. Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in various environmental conditions.
