Pedicularis cheilanthifolia var. albida

Pedicularis cheilanthifolia var. albida, commonly known as the white spurred gentian, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking white, spurred flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
