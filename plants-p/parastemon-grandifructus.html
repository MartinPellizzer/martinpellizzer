Parastemon grandifructus

Parastemon grandifructus, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are actually modified leaves rather than flowers. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of Parastemon grandifructus make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking colorful, low-maintenance plants.
