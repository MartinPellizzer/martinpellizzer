Phaseolus caribaeus

Phaseolus caribaeus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, vibrant flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and a robust root system that helps in nitrogen fixation.

This species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its agricultural significance.
