Piper colon-insulae

Piper colon-insulae, commonly known as the island pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found on the islands of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small berries that contain a spicy, peppery flavor, similar to black pepper.

It is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
