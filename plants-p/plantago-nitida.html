Plantago nitida

Plantago nitida, commonly known as slender plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters along its flowering stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges.

Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
