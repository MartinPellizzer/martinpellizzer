Panicum lasiocoleum var. calvescens

Panicum lasiocoleum var. calvescens, commonly known as silky panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and panicles that are covered in silky, fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green color, while the inflorescence is often drooping and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
