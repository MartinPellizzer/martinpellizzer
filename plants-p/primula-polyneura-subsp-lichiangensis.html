Primula polyneura subsp. lichiangensis

Primula polyneura subsp.

lichiangensis, commonly known as the Lichiang primrose, is a flowering plant native to Yunnan Province in China. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces small, showy blooms in early spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
