Patagonium leiocarpum var. elongatum

Patagonium leiocarpum var. elongatum, commonly known as the elongated Patagonian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Patagonian region of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, making it ecologically significant in its native environment.

It is often used in restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
