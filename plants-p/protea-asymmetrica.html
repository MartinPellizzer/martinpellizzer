Protea asymmetrica

Protea asymmetrica, commonly known as the asymmetric protea, is a striking South African native plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, asymmetrical floral structure, which sets it apart from other protea species. The plant features a tall, branched stem with dense clusters of bright pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are leathery and arranged in a spiral pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Protea asymmetrica thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation, showcasing its adaptability to the region's climate.
