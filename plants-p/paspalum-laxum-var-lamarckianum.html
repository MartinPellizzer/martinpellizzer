Paspalum laxum var. lamarckianum

Paspalum laxum var. lamarckianum, commonly known as Lamarck's paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are clustered along the stems. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along riverbanks.

Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively and establish dense colonies in suitable environments.
