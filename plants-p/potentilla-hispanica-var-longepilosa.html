Potentilla hispanica var. longepilosa

Potentilla hispanica var. longepilosa, commonly known as the long-haired Spanish cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically compound, with five to seven leaflets that are serrated and have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
