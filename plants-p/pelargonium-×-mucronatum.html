Pelargonium × mucronatum

Pelargonium × mucronatum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, velvety leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and containers.
