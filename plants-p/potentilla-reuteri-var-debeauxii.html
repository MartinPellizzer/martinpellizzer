Potentilla reuteri var. debeauxii

Potentilla reuteri var. debeauxii, commonly known as the Beaux's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically arranged in clusters of five, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.". The plant has slender, woody stems and dark green, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and vibrant floral display.
