Prunus koraiensis

Prunus koraiensis, commonly known as the Korean cherry or Korean maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly during the spring when it produces clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant has ovate to elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its seasonal appeal. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic beauty in gardens and parks.

Prunus koraiensis is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to adapt to various soil conditions.
