Pelargonium angulosum

Pelargonium angulosum, commonly known as the angular geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, and is characterized by its distinctive angular leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Pelargonium angulosum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
