Pectis meifolia

Pectis meifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf pectis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which attract various pollinators.

Pectis meifolia is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
