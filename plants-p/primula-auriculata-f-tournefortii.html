Primula auriculata f. tournefortii

Primula auriculata f. tournefortii, commonly known as the Tournefortii primrose, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, auriculate (ear-shaped) leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of central Asia, including parts of China and the Tibetan Plateau. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
