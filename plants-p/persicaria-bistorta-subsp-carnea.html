Persicaria bistorta subsp. carnea

Persicaria bistorta subsp.

carnea, commonly known as red bistort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features upright, branched stems that are often reddish in color, especially when mature.

The plant produces small, pinkish to reddish flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a distinctive wavy margin and a rough texture.

It is commonly found in moist meadows, streambanks, and woodland edges, where it thrives in partially shaded to sunny conditions.
