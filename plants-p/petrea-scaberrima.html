Petrea scaberrima

Petrea scaberrima, commonly known as the roughleaf pita, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its rough, often hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its bark is thick and corky, and the leaves are typically elliptical with serrated edges.

Petrea scaberrima is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
