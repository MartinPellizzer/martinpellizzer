Pleurothallis adrianae

Pleurothallis adrianae, commonly known as Adriana's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within the understory of forests. The plant features a slender, wiry stem and produces clusters of tiny, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its flowers are characterized by their intricate structure and are often pollinated by small insects.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
