Primulina rubella

Primulina rubella, commonly known as the red velvet begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red leaves that contrast beautifully with its dark green foliage. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
