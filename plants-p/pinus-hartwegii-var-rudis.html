Pinus hartwegii var. rudis

Pinus hartwegii var. rudis, commonly known as the Mexican bristlecone pine, is a variant of the Hartweg's pine native to Mexico.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and can live for several centuries, making it a long-lived species. It thrives in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates.

Its botanical features include a rugged bark and small, resinous cones that contain its seeds.
