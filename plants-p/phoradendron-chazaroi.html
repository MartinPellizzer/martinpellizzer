Phoradendron chazaroi

Phoradendron chazaroi, commonly known as the Brazilian dodder, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for nutrients.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its yellow, thread-like stems that coil around host plants. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is often found growing on trees in tropical and subtropical regions. Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause significant damage to its host plants if left unchecked.
