Phyllostachys vivax f. vittata

Phyllostachys vivax f. vittata, commonly known as the variegated bamboo, is a decorative variant of the silver bamboo species.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display a mix of green, white, and yellow hues. This bamboo is native to East Asia and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It grows as a clump-forming plant, reaching heights of up to 6 meters under optimal conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and garden design.
