Philadelphus texensis var. texensis

Philadelphus texensis var. texensis, commonly known as the Texas mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the southern United States.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters of 4 to 6. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blooms and aromatic foliage.
