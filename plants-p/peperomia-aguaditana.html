Peperomia aguaditana

Peperomia aguaditana, commonly known as the Andean peperomia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, succulent-like foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or shaded environments, adapting to high-altitude conditions with its thick, waxy leaves. Its leaves are usually oval or lance-shaped, with a glossy surface that helps retain moisture.

Peperomia aguaditana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
