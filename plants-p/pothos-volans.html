Pothos volans

Pothos volans, commonly known as the devil's ivy, is a vigorous climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly variegated pattern. The plant is characterized by its long, trailing stems that can develop aerial roots, allowing it to climb and attach to surfaces. It thrives in low-light conditions and is highly tolerant of neglect, making it a popular houseplant.

Pothos volans is also known for its ability to purify indoor air, enhancing its appeal as a decorative and functional plant.
