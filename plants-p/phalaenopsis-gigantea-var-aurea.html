Phalaenopsis gigantea var. aurea

Phalaenopsis gigantea var. aurea, commonly known as the golden phalaenopsis orchid, is a hybrid orchid prized for its striking golden-yellow petals and velvety texture.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant features a sympodial growth pattern, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its long blooming period. Its flowers typically bloom in winter and can last several months, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

The variety's unique coloration and elegance contribute to its status as a sought-after ornamental plant.
