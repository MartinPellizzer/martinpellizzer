Plectranthus oblongifolius

Plectranthus oblongifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, often clustered in terminal spikes. It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is popular in gardens and as a houseplant.
