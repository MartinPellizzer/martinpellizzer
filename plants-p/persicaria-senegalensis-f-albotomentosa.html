Persicaria senegalensis f. albotomentosa

Persicaria senegalensis f. albotomentosa, commonly known as the white-hairy knotweed, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive silvery-white pubescence on its stems and leaves.

This plant belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes various species known for their unique leaf structures and ecological roles. It is native to regions of Africa, particularly Senegal, where it thrives in moist, often disturbed habitats. The plant's foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fluffy appearance.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in plant morphology and ecological adaptation.
