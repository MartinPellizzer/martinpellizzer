Polypodium plantagineum

Polypodium plantagineum, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or rocky areas. Its rhizome is fibrous and helps anchor it to the substrate while absorbing nutrients.

Polypodium plantagineum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
