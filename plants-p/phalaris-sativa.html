Phalaris sativa

Phalaris sativa, commonly known as common barnyard grass, is an annual grass species native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue or greenish flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its rapid growth and competitive nature, it is considered a weed in many regions.
