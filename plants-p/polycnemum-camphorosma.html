Polycnemum camphorosma

Polycnemum camphorosma, commonly known as camphor grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with spikelets that contain both male and female flowers. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Polycnemum camphorosma is noted for its aromatic properties, particularly in its leaves and stems, which contain compounds like camphor.
