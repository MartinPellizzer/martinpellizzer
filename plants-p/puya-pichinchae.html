Puya pichinchae

Puya pichinchae, commonly known as the Pichincha puya, is a striking plant native to the Andean region of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that bears numerous violet to purple flowers. The plant features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a water-holding cup, typical of many bromeliads. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, humid conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
