Paronychia arabica var. fezzanica

Paronychia arabica var. fezzanica, commonly known as the Fezzan thistle, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the Sahara and the Horn of Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained sandy soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in dry gardens and rockeries.
