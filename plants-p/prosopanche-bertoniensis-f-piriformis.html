Prosopanche bertoniensis f. piriformis

Prosopanche bertoniensis f. piriformis, commonly known as the pear-shaped nightshade, is a unique variant of the Prosopanche genus native to certain regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, pear-shaped fruits that mature to a deep purple or black color. The leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant produces small, white flowers during the warmer months. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique morphology, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare plants.
