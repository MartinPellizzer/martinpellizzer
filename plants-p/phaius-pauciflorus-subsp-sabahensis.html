Phaius pauciflorus subsp. sabahensis

Phaius pauciflorus subsp.

sabahensis, commonly known as the Sabah orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the rainforests of Borneo. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in the summer months.

The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which helps it store water and nutrients in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
