Poa egorovae

Poa egorovae, commonly known as the Egorov's bentgrass, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it forms dense, low-growing mats. Poa egorovae is adapted to cold climates and is often found at high elevations.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
