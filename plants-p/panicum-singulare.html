Panicum singulare

Panicum singulare, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
