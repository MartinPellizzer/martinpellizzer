Prunus cavellana

Prunus cavellana, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or Japanese cherry, is a small deciduous shrub or tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of white or pink flowers in early spring. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or black when mature. Prunus cavellana thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both landscape design and botanical studies.
