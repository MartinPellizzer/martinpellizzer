Pouteria fimbriata

Pouteria fimbriata, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The tree produces elongated, woody pods that contain the edible seeds known as Brazil nuts. These seeds are rich in nutrients, particularly selenium and healthy fats.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various wildlife species.
