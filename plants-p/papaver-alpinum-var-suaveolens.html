Papaver alpinum var. suaveolens

Papaver alpinum var. suaveolens, commonly known as the Alpine poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking, bright red to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine climates.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
