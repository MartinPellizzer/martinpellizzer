Ptarmica ircutiana

Ptarmica ircutiana, commonly known as the Siberian tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and emit a strong scent when crushed. Native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Europe, it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Ptarmica ircutiana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
