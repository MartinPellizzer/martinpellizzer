Patrinia triloba var. triloba

Patrinia triloba var. triloba, commonly known as Chinese violet or three-parted false indigo, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and has a creeping rhizomatous root system.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties have made it a subject of interest in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
