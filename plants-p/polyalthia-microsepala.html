Polyalthia microsepala

Polyalthia microsepala, commonly known as the small-sepaled tree.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Polyalthia microsepala is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
