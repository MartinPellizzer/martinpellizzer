Piper cristinanum

Piper cristinanum, commonly known as the cristianum pepper or the sacred pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by small, spherical berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in some indigenous communities.
