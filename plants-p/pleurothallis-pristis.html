Pleurothallis pristis

Pleurothallis pristis, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a slender, wiry stem with a single, showy flower that has a pinkish-lavender hue. Its flowers are characterized by a pouch-like lip and are often adorned with intricate patterns.

Pleurothallis pristis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
