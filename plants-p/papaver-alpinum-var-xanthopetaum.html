Papaver alpinum var. xanthopetaum

Papaver alpinum var. xanthopetaum, commonly known as the yellow Himalayan poppy, is a variant of the alpine poppy native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals and is part of the Papaveraceae family. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, thriving in rocky and well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to alpine meadows.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
