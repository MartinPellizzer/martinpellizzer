Potentilla flabellifolia var. grayi

Potentilla flabellifolia var. grayi, commonly known as Gray's shrub cinquefoil, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and streambanks. The plant features compound leaves with five leaflets, often with a distinctive grayish-white underside. Its flowers are showy, appearing in clusters of five, and are usually yellow or orange in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
