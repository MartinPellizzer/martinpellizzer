Pinus × hakkodensis

Pinus × hakkodensis, commonly known as the Hakkoda pine, is a hybrid conifer resulting from the cross between Pinus strobus (eastern white pine) and Pinus parviflora (Japanese white pine).

It is native to the Hakkoda Mountains in Japan, where it thrives in cool, humid climates. The tree is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It produces cones that are smaller than those of its parent species, and the seeds are dispersed by wind.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic qualities.
