Pycnanthemum beadlei

Pycnanthemum beadlei, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and wet meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
