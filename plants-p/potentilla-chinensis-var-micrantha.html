Potentilla chinensis var. micrantha

Potentilla chinensis var. micrantha, commonly known as Chinese cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are compound, with five leaflets, and it produces small, red or orange fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Chinese medicine.
