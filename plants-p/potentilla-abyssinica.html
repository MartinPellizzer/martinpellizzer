Potentilla abyssinica

Potentilla abyssinica, commonly known as Ethiopian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

Potentilla abyssinica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
