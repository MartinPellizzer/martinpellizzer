Penstemon bicolor subsp. typicus

Penstemon bicolor subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the pink and white penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its striking bicolor flowers, which transition from pink to white as they mature.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces tall, erect stems that bear clusters of tubular flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
