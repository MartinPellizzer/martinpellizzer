Pleurostachys undulatifolia

Pleurostachys undulatifolia, commonly known as the wavy-leaf bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. The plant has long, narrow leaves with a wavy margin, which gives it its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in open, sunny areas.

Due to its ornamental value and structural properties, it is cultivated for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
