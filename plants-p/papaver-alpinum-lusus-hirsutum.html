Papaver alpinum lusus hirsutum

Papaver alpinum lusus hirsutum, commonly known as the Alpine poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange petals and a distinctive, hairy stem. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
