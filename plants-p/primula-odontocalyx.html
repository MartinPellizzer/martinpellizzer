Primula odontocalyx

Primula odontocalyx, commonly known as the toothed primrose, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clustered, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Primula odontocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
