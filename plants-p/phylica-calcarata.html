Phylica calcarata

Phylica calcarata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, arid environments. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow center. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, and it is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, coastal conditions.

Phylica calcarata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
