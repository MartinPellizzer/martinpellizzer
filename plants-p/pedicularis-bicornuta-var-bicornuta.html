Pedicularis bicornuta var. bicornuta

Pedicularis bicornuta var. bicornuta, commonly known as the two-horned lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive bilaterally symmetrical flowers with two prominent, horn-like projections. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is noted for its vibrant pink to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
