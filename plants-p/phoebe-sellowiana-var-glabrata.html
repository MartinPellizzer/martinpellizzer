Phoebe sellowiana var. glabrata

Phoebe sellowiana var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth phoebe, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and aromatic leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in the axils of the leaves.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
