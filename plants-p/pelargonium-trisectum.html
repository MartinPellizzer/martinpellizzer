Pelargonium trisectum

Pelargonium trisectum, commonly known as the three-lobed geranium, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features striking, dark green, three-lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in gardens around the world.
