Poa dactyloides

Poa dactyloides, commonly known as buffalograss, is a native grass species of North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, dense sod-forming habit and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has narrow, blue-green leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance, making it a popular choice for low-water landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ecological and ornamental significance.
