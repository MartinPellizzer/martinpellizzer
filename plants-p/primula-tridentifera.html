Primula tridentifera

Primula tridentifera, commonly known as the three-toothed primrose, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and mountainous regions. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in cooler climates.
