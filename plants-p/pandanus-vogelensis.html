Pandanus vogelensis

Pandanus vogelensis, commonly known as the Hainuwele palm, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in branched inflorescences. Its fruits are fibrous and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind and water.

Pandanus vogelensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
