Paspalum paspalodes

Paspalum paspalodes, commonly known as the slender paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, often creeping stems. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its small, spike-like inflorescences produce numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Paspalum paspalodes is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
