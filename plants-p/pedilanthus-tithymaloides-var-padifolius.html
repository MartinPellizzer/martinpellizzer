Pedilanthus tithymaloides var. padifolius

Pedilanthus tithymaloides var. padifolius, commonly known as the Paddleleaf Candelabra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive paddle-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in warm climates for its unique foliage and blooms.
