Pleione rumphii

Pleione rumphii, commonly known as the Rumph's pleione, is a rare and delicate orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant typically blooms in late winter or early spring, making it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, often with a waxy texture, and it thrives in cool, humid environments with partial shade.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Pleione rumphii is often cultivated in specialized orchid collections.
