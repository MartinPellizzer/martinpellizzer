Panicum desertorum

Panicum desertorum, commonly known as desert dropseed, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, silver-gray foliage that helps reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are adapted for wind dispersal, enhancing its ability to colonize disturbed or dry soils. Panicum desertorum is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor, well-drained soils, making it a common component of desert and steppe ecosystems.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing sandy soils in its native habitat.
