Polygonum conspicuum

Polygonum conspicuum, commonly known as the showy knotweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands and stream banks.

Polygonum conspicuum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
