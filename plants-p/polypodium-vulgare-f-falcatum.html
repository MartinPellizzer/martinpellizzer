Polypodium vulgare f. falcatum

Polypodium vulgare f. falcatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wall fern, is a fern species characterized by its slender, lance-shaped fronds.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodland floors or rocky crevices. The fronds are usually dark green and have a delicate, feathery appearance. This fern is native to parts of Europe and is often found in temperate regions.

It reproduces through spores and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
