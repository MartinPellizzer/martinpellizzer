Physalis angulata f. tenuis

Physalis angulata f. tenuis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved husk tomato, is a variant of the common husk tomato.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits enclosed in a papery calyx. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive angular shape.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along stream banks.
