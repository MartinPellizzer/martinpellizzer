Phymatopteris taeniata

Phymatopteris taeniata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and dimorphic fronds, with sterile fronds typically being shorter and more upright than the fertile ones. The plant produces spores on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in sori covered by a protective indusium. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
