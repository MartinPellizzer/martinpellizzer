Potentilla concinna var. curvata

Potentilla concinna var. curvata, commonly known as the curly potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

It is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy and ornamental plant in alpine gardens.
