Polymnia radiata

Polymnia radiata, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble a crown. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and branched.

Polymnia radiata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
