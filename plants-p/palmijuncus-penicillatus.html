Palmijuncus penicillatus

Palmijuncus penicillatus, commonly known as the slender palm, is a unique species of palm native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and pinnate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hairs. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, which are followed by elliptical, black fruits. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and wind-prone environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
