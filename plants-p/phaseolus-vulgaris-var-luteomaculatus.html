Phaseolus vulgaris var. luteomaculatus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. luteomaculatus, commonly known as the yellow-seeded kidney bean, is a variant of the common bean species.

It is characterized by its yellow seeds with distinct dark spots, which give it its unique appearance. This variety is typically cultivated for its edible beans, which are rich in protein and nutrients. The plant is a herbaceous annual that thrives in warm climates and well-drained soil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is widely grown in tropical and subtropical regions for both culinary and agricultural purposes.
