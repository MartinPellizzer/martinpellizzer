Peyrousia falcata

Peyrousia falcata, commonly known as the sickleleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, falcate (sickle-shaped) leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by seed capsules that release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Peyrousia falcata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
