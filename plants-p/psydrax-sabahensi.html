Psydrax sabahensi

Psydrax sabahensi, commonly known as the Sabah wax tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Brunei.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are glossy on the upper surface. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters.

The plant is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
