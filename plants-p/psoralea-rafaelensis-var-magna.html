Psoralea rafaelensis var. magna

Psoralea rafaelensis var. magna, commonly known as the giant Rafael's psoralea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and exhibits a shrub-like growth form. Its botanical features include stipules, which are small leaf-like structures at the base of the leaf stalks, and it produces legume pods that contain its seeds.

This variety is noted for its relatively large size compared to other members of the species.
