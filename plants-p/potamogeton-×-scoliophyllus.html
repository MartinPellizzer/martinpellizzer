Potamogeton × scoliophyllus

Potamogeton × scoliophyllus, commonly known as the hybrid water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton scolopendrium.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers, often forming dense underwater vegetation. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by wind or water. Potamogeton × scoliophyllus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can also indicate the health and stability of the water body it inhabits.
