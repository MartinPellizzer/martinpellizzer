Passiflora manora

Passiflora manora, commonly known as the Manora passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Passiflora manora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
