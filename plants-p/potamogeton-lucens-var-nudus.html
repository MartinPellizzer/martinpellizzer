Potamogeton lucens var. nudus

Potamogeton lucens var. nudus, commonly known as the slender pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It features long, narrow leaves that are typically hairless and slightly wavy in appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, which emerge from the water surface during the summer months. Its roots anchor it to the bottom of lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers, contributing to water clarity by stabilizing sediments.

This variety is often found in shallow waters and plays a role in supporting aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various invertebrates and fish.
