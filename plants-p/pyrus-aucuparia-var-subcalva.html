Pyrus aucuparia var. subcalva

Pyrus aucuparia var. subcalva, commonly known as the hairy whitebeam, is a variant of the whitebeam tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves that are hairy on both sides, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet berries in the summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, supporting a variety of wildlife.
