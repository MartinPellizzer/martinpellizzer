Persicaria roseoviridis

Persicaria roseoviridis, commonly known as the pink-veined knotweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and strikingly veined leaves that display a distinctive pink or reddish hue. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Persicaria roseoviridis is often found in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
