Psidium ooideum var. longipedunculatum

Psidium ooideum var. longipedunculatum, commonly known as the long-peduncled guava, is a tropical plant belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, white flowers that develop into edible, aromatic fruits. The leaves are typically ovate with a glossy surface and a serrated margin. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
