Pimenta tabasco

Pimenta tabasco, commonly known as tabasco pepper, is a species of Capsicum native to Central America.

It is characterized by its small, bright red, elongated fruits that are typically harvested when they are fully mature. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in warm, tropical climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit contains a high concentration of capsaicin, which gives it its distinctive spicy flavor.
