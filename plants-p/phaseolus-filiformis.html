Phaseolus filiformis

Phaseolus filiformis, commonly known as the slender bean, is a climbing legume native to Central and South America.

It produces small, elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
