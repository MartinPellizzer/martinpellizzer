Philodendron asperatum

Philodendron asperatum, commonly known as the rough-leaf philodendron, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by bright red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique foliage, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
