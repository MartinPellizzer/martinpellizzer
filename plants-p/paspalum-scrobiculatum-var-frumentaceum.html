Paspalum scrobiculatum var. frumentaceum

Paspalum scrobiculatum var. frumentaceum, commonly known as fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
