Psoralea foliolosa

Psoralea foliolosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. It produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Psoralea foliolosa is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
