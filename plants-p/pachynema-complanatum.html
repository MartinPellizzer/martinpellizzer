Pachynema complanatum

Pachynema complanatum, commonly known as the flat moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Pottiaceae.

It is characterized by its flat, ribbon-like thallus that grows in dense mats on moist, shaded surfaces. The plant lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, instead having a simple, undifferentiated body called a thallus. It reproduces through spores released from tiny, cup-like structures called peristomes.

Pachynema complanatum is often found in temperate regions and is an important indicator species for undisturbed, moist habitats.
