Polystichum × silviamontanum

Polystichum × silviamontanum, commonly known as the Silviamontanum wood fern, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Polystichum setiferum and Polystichum acrostichum.

It is characterized by its robust, upright fronds that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The fern produces spore-bearing structures called sori, which are typically clustered on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
