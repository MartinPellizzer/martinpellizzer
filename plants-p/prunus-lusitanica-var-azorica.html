Prunus lusitanica var. azorica

Prunus lusitanica var. azorica, commonly known as the Azores cherry or Azorean cherry, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Azores archipelago.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits resembling cherries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a notable species in the flora of the Azores.
