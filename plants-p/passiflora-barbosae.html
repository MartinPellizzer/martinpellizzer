Passiflora barbosae

Passiflora barbosae, commonly known as the Barboza passionflower, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a unique blend of colors and structures. The plant produces showy, five-petaled blooms with a central crown of filaments, often in shades of red, yellow, and white. Its fruits are edible, containing a sweet, fleshy pulp surrounded by a hard outer shell.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in gardens.
