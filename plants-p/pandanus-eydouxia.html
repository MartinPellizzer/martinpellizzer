Pandanus eydouxia

Pandanus eydouxia, commonly known as the giant pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces distinctive, clustered inflorescences that bear small, yellowish flowers. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional crafts and construction.

The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove ecosystems.
