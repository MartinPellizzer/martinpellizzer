Potamogeton gramineus subvar. lanceolatifolius

Potamogeton gramineus subvar. lanceolatifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in North America.

It has long, slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically 2–8 cm in length and 5–15 mm in width. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which emerge from the water surface. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a vital role in providing habitat and food for aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and ecological health.
