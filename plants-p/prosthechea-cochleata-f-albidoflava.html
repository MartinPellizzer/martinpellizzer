Prosthechea cochleata f. albidoflava

Prosthechea cochleata f. albidoflava, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a fascinating epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and its striking white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in a spiral pattern. The leaves are typically broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires good air circulation for optimal growth.

Its unique floral structure and coloration make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
