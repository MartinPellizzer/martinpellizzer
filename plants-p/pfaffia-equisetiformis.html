Pfaffia equisetiformis

Pfaffia equisetiformis, commonly known as curare plant or arrowroot, is a perennial herb native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves that resemble those of the horsetail plant. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including alkaloids and saponins, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties. It is often found growing in moist, shaded environments along riverbanks and in dense undergrowth.

Due to its unique chemical composition, it has attracted interest in both traditional medicine and modern pharmacological research.
