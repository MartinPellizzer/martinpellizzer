Pantathera avenacea

Pantathera avenacea, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a hairy stem and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

The golden cup plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
