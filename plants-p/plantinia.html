Plantinia

Plantinia, commonly known as the moonflower, is a striking flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant blooms that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. The plant has heart-shaped, broad leaves that provide a lush green backdrop to its showy flowers. Its botanical name, Plantinia, reflects its unique nocturnal blooming habit and aesthetic appeal.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
