Pseudosasa vicinia

Pseudosasa vicinia, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that are often covered in a golden hue, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a smooth texture and a light green color.

Pseudosasa vicinia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
