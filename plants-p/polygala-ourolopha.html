Polygala ourolopha

Polygala ourolopha, commonly known as the golden crown or golden polygala, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its striking, yellow-orange flowers that resemble small, delicate crowns. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, often found in fynbos vegetation. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, greenish flowers that bloom in spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
