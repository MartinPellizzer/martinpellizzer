Pleurothallis orbicularis

Pleurothallis orbicularis, commonly known as the orbicular orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical or ovate, and it prefers shaded, humid environments with well-draining soil.

This orchid is often grown as a terrestrial or epiphytic plant in cultivation due to its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
