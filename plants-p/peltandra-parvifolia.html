Peltandra parvifolia

Peltandra parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved arrowleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically light green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually yellow or green, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in botanical gardens and ecological studies.
