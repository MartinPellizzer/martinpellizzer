Pilosella glaucochroa

Pilosella glaucochroa, commonly known as the blue-green pilosella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically gray-green in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Pilosella glaucochroa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
