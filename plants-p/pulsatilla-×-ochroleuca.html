Pulsatilla × ochroleuca

Pulsatilla × ochroleuca, commonly known as the pasqueflower or meadow anemone, is a hybrid plant in the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to pink. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Pulsatilla × ochroleuca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
