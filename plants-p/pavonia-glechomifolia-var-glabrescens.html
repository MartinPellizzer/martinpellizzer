Pavonia glechomifolia var. glabrescens

Pavonia glechomifolia var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant collections and ecological restoration projects.
