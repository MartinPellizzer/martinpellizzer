Polystachya affinis var. nana

Polystachya affinis var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a compact, clump-forming plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features slender, green stems that grow close to the ground, often reaching only a few inches in height. The plant produces small, white to pale green inflorescences that are characteristic of the Polystachya genus. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its low-growing habit and ornamental appeal, it is cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
