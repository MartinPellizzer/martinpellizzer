Pileanthus peduncularis subsp. peduncularis

Pileanthus peduncularis subsp.

peduncularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pilea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate watering, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

This subspecies is often grown for its ornamental foliage rather than its flowers.
