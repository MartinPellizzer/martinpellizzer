Paepalanthus aquatilis

Paepalanthus aquatilis, commonly known as the water cactus, is a unique plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a succulent shrub that thrives in wetland and swampy environments, showcasing its adaptation to waterlogged soils. Its stems are thick and fleshy, storing water to help it survive in its moist habitat. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual element to its environment.

Due to its specialized needs, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
