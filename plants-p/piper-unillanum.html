Piper unillanum

Piper unillanum, commonly known as the purple pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. Its leaves are often used as a spice, similar to black pepper, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The botanical features of Piper unillanum make it an important plant in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
