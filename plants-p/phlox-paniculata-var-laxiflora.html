Phlox paniculata var. laxiflora

Phlox paniculata var. laxiflora, commonly known as the laxiflora garden phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features upright stems that bear large, showy clusters of pink to lavender flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and relatively easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
