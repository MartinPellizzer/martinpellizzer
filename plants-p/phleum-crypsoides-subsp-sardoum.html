Phleum crypsoides subsp. sardoum

Phleum crypsoides subsp.

sardoum, commonly known as the Sardinian bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in coastal and inland areas of Sardinia.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
