Paeonia coriacea var. maroccana

Paeonia coriacea var. maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan peony, is a variety native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically produces large, double or semi-double petals in shades of pink, red, and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional horticulture.
