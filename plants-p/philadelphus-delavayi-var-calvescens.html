Philadelphus delavayi var. calvescens

Philadelphus delavayi var. calvescens, commonly known as the Chinese mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features showy, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.

This variety is closely related to other species in the Philadelphus genus, which are known for their ornamental value in gardens.
