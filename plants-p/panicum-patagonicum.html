Panicum patagonicum

Panicum patagonicum, commonly known as Patagonian panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a loose, open panicle made up of small spikelets.

Panicum patagonicum is valued for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
