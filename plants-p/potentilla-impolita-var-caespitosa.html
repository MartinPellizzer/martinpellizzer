Potentilla impolita var. caespitosa

Potentilla impolita var. caespitosa, commonly known as the rough potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, compact growth habit and hairy, glandular stems. The leaves are narrow, ovate to lanceolate, and often covered with fine, stiff hairs. It produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers in late summer, which are borne on short pedicels.

This variant is adapted to harsh, cold environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
