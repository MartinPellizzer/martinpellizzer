Polypodium saximontanum

Polypodium saximontanum, commonly known as the saximonter rock fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its compact, densely packed fronds that form a rosette-like structure, typically growing on rocky outcrops or in shaded forest environments. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it anchor to steep or unstable terrain. Its fronds are usually dark green and leathery, with a serrated or lobed margin.

Polypodium saximontanum is adapted to humid, cool climates and is often found in cloud forests or high-altitude habitats.
