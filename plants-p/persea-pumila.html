Persea pumila

Persea pumila, commonly known as the dwarf avocado, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically followed by dark purple to black berries. Its fruit, while not commonly consumed by humans, is an important food source for various wildlife species.

Persea pumila thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
