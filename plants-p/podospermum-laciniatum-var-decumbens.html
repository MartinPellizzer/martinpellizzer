Podospermum laciniatum var. decumbens

Podospermum laciniatum var. decumbens, commonly known as the creeping podospermum, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often lobed or serrated, contributing to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its fibrous roots and sprawling growth habit make it suitable for ground cover in suitable climates.
