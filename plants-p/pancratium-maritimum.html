Pancratium maritimum

Pancratium maritimum, commonly known as the sea daffodil, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and strap-like, grassy leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky coastal areas, thriving in saline and well-drained soils. Its bulbs are adapted to withstand harsh maritime conditions, and it often blooms from late spring to early summer.

Pancratium maritimum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens along the coast.
