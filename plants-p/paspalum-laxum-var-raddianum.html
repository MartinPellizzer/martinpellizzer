Paspalum laxum var. raddianum

Paspalum laxum var. raddianum, commonly known as the Raddiana paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are arranged in panicles, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
