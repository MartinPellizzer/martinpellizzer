Parodia maassii var. carminatiflora

Parodia maassii var. carminatiflora, commonly known as the Crimson-flowered Parodia, is a rare and striking cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered in white to pale green spines. The plant produces vibrant red or pink flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a dramatic visual contrast to its otherwise subdued appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its rarity and unique floral display, it is highly sought after by succulent enthusiasts and collectors.
