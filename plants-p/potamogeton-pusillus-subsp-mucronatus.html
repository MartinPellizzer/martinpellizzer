Potamogeton pusillus subsp. mucronatus

Potamogeton pusillus subsp.

mucronatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves. It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually borne in dense clusters near the water surface. Its leaves are usually smooth and have a pointed tip, which distinguishes it from other pondweed species.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
