Pseudosasa tessellata

Pseudosasa tessellata, commonly known as the tessellated bamboo, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and finely divided, feather-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
