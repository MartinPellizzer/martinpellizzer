Pleurandra cistoidea

Pleurandra cistoidea, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens across southern Europe and parts of North Africa.
