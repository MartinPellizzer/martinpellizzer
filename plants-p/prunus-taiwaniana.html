Prunus taiwaniana

Prunus taiwaniana, commonly known as the Taiwan cherry or Taiwan peach, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, typically white or pale pink, which are followed by small, round drupes. It is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, acidic conditions.

Prunus taiwaniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
