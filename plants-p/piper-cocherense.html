Piper cocherense

Piper cocherense, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or wild pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into berries, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a mild, peppery aroma.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
