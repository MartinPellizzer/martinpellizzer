Prunus serrulata f. sirayuki

Prunus serrulata f. sirayuki, commonly known as the Sirayuki cherry, is a distinct form of the Japanese maple.

It is characterized by its striking white-pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features delicate, serrated leaves that turn vibrant red in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its unique floral display and seasonal color changes make it a prized addition to gardens and landscapes.
