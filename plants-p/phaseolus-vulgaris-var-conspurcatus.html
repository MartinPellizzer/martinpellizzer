Phaseolus vulgaris var. conspurcatus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. conspurcatus, commonly known as the black bean plant, is a variant of the common bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with climbing vines, producing small white or pink flowers. Its botanical features include compound leaves with three leaflets and a fibrous root system.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
