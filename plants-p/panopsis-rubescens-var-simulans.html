Panopsis rubescens var. simulans

Panopsis rubescens var. simulans, commonly known as the red-flowered panicled grass, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features slender, erect stems that bear clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
