Prunus spinosa subsp. coaetanea

Prunus spinosa subsp.

coaetanea, commonly known as the common blackthorn, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark blue-black berries that ripen in late summer.

The plant typically grows in hedgerows, woodland edges, and other disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces white or pale pink flowers in early spring.

The berries are a food source for birds and are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
