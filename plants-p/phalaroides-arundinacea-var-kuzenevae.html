Phalaroides arundinacea var. kuzenevae

Phalaroides arundinacea var. kuzenevae, commonly known as Kuzenev's reed, is a variant of the reed grass native to specific regions of Russia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, typically growing in wetland or marshy environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
