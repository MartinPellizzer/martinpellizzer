Panicum strictum var. linearifolium

Panicum strictum var. linearifolium, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
