Potamogeton pectinatus f. pinguis

Potamogeton pectinatus f. pinguis, commonly known as the broad-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, smooth, dark green leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically borne in spike-like clusters, and it is often found in still or slow-moving water bodies. Its leaves are rich in nutrients and can contribute to water clarity by reducing algae growth.

This plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
