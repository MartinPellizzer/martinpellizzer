Pyrus bucharica var. hissarica

Pyrus bucharica var. hissarica, commonly known as the Karakum pear, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in early spring. The tree produces small, sweet, and juicy fruits that are typically eaten fresh or used in jams. It prefers well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in regional horticulture.
