Plantago australis subsp. leioloma

Plantago australis subsp.

leioloma, commonly known as the narrow-leaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy on the underside.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, which are borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along waterways.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
