Prunus lusitanica var. hixa

Prunus lusitanica var. hixa, commonly known as the Spanish cherry or Hixa cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or black when mature. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and grayish in color.

It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
