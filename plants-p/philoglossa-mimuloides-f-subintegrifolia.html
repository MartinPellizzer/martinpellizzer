Philoglossa mimuloides f. subintegrifolia

Philoglossa mimuloides f. subintegrifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mimuloid daisy, is a variant of the Philoglossa mimuloides species, characterized by its distinctive foliage.

This plant is native to specific regions in South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a smooth, uninterrupted margin, giving it the subspecies name "subintegrifolia.". The flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in a daisy-like cluster, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
