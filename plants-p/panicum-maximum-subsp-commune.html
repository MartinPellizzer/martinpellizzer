Panicum maximum subsp. commune

Panicum maximum subsp.

commune, commonly known as Guinea grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to Africa. It is characterized by its robust growth and coarse, upright leaves.

The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its high nutritional value, it is widely used as forage for livestock.
