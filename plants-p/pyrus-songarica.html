Pyrus songarica

Pyrus songarica, commonly known as the Songarica pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The fruit is a pear-like berry that ripens in late summer, often used in traditional medicine. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

The plant has a historical significance in herbal medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
