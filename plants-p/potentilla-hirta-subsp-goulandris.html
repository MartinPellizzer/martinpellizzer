Potentilla hirta subsp. goulandris

Potentilla hirta subsp.

goulandris, commonly known as the Goulandris cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has dark green, serrated leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient species adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
