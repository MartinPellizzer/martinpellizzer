Piper centroense

Piper centroense, commonly known as the Centro American pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Central America and is characterized by its climbing habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elliptical, dark blue berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of medicinal compounds.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and pharmacological studies.
