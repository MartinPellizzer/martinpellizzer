Potamogeton corniculatus

Potamogeton corniculatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or just at the water's surface. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in lakes, ponds, and streams. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
