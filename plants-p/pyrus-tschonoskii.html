Pyrus tschonoskii

Pyrus tschonoskii, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during spring, followed by edible, sweet yellow or green fruits.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in traditional Chinese horticulture.
