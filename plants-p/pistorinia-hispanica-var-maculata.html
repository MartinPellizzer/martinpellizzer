Pistorinia hispanica var. maculata

Pistorinia hispanica var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted pistorinia, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves, which give it its varietal name "maculata.". The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
