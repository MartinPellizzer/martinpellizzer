Pandanus furcatus var. indica

Pandanus furcatus var. indica, commonly known as the Indian screw pine, is a tropical plant characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, including regions like India and the Pacific Islands. The plant features a woody, often twisted stem with long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are usually green with a waxy coating. It thrives in coastal areas and can tolerate salt spray, making it well-suited for maritime environments.

Its unique appearance and adaptability have made it a popular ornamental plant in tropical landscapes.
