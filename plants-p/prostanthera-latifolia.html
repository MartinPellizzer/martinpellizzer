Prostanthera latifolia

Prostanthera latifolia, commonly known as broad-leaved honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale purple flowers. It is often found in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

Prostanthera latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aromatic foliage and blooms.
