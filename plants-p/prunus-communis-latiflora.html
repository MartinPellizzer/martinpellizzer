Prunus communis latiflora

Prunus communis latiflora, commonly known as the broad-flowered cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, typically growing in woodland areas and along stream banks. The plant is characterized by its showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, often preceding the leaves. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the fruit is a small, dark red drupe.

This species is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
