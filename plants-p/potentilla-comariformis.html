Potentilla comariformis

Potentilla comariformis, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, woody stems and pinnately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and dry slopes.

Potentilla comariformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
