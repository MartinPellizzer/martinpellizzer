Pimenta acris var. pimentoides

Pimenta acris var. pimentoides, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant features aromatic, dark green leaves with a glossy texture and produces small, clustered white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. It is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils and is often found in dry, tropical climates.

The plant has a long history of use in traditional medicine and as a source of spices.
