Pandanus caudatifolius

Pandanus caudatifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in terminal spikes. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

The species is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a common sight in mangrove and coastal forest ecosystems.
