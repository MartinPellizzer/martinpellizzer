Potentilla recta var. tyraica

Potentilla recta var. tyraica, commonly known as the Tyrian potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to rocky or arid environments.
