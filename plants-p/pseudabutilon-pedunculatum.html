Pseudabutilon pedunculatum

Pseudabutilon pedunculatum, commonly known as the yellow sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow ray florets and a central disk of smaller, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a robust, branched stem and hairy leaves that help it retain moisture.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
