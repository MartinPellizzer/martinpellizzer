Polygala pseudohospita

Polygala pseudohospita, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygalaceae family.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of central and southern Africa. The plant is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
