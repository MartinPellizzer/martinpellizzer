Passiflora foetida var. acapulcensis

Passiflora foetida var. acapulcensis, commonly known as the Acapulco passionflower, is a climbing shrub native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and striking, intricate flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
