Psiadia rivalsii

Psiadia rivalsii, commonly known as the Rivals' dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a prominent central disk.

Psiadia rivalsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
