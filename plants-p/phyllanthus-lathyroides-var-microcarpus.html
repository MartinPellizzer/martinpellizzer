Phyllanthus lathyroides var. microcarpus

Phyllanthus lathyroides var. microcarpus, commonly known as the small-fruited spurge, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically smooth and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, spherical berries. It is adapted to well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy species in arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable plant in ecological studies and horticultural applications.
