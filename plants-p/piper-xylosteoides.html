Piper xylosteoides

Piper xylosteoides, commonly known as the black pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, green berries that turn dark when mature, containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in local remedies for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's botanical features include opposite, ovate leaves and a distinctive, woody stem that supports its climbing growth habit.
