Paspalum neesii

Paspalum neesii, commonly known as coastal paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the coastal regions of South America.

It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments and salt marshes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is highly tolerant of salt and wind, making it an important species in coastal stabilization.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
