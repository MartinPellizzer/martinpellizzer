Primula tschuktschorum

Primula tschuktschorum, commonly known as the Tschuktschorum primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Siberia and the Far East.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its leaves are usually basal, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine, downy texture.

Primula tschuktschorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens with specific climate conditions.
