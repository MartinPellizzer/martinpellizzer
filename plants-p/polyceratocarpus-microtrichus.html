Polyceratocarpus microtrichus

Polyceratocarpus microtrichus, commonly known as the dwarf African daisy, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with distinctive, horn-like bracts. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing drought tolerance and efficient water storage. Its foliage is often covered in fine, hair-like structures that help reduce water loss.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
