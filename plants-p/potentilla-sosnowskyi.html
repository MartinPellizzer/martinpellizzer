Potentilla sosnowskyi

Potentilla sosnowskyi, commonly known as Sosnowsky's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, hairy leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and resilience.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
