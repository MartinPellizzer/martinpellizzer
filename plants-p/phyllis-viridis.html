Phyllis viridis

Phyllis viridis, commonly known as the green phyllis, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Phyllisaceae.

It is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Phyllis viridis is relatively low maintenance and is often used in ornamental gardening for its subtle beauty.
