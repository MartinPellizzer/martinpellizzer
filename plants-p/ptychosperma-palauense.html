Ptychosperma palauense

Ptychosperma palauense, commonly known as the Palau cycad, is a unique cycad species native to the island of Palau in the Western Pacific.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched stem and a crown of large, pinnate leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces cones, with the male cones being cylindrical and the female cones spherical, typical of cycads. It thrives in tropical rainforest environments, often growing in shaded, moist areas with well-drained soil.

Due to habitat loss and overcollection, it is considered vulnerable, highlighting the need for conservation efforts to protect this rare botanical species.
