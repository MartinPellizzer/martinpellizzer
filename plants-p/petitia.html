Petitia

Petitia, commonly known as the small fern, is a delicate plant belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact size and intricate fronds that often exhibit a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are usually located on the underside of its leaves.

Petitia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in maintaining ecological balance in woodland habitats.
