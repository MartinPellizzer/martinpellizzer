Pinus nigra var. columnaris-pendula

Pinus nigra var. columnaris-pendula, commonly known as the weeping black pine, is a unique variant of the black pine species.

It is characterized by its pendulous, drooping branches that create a distinctive, cascading appearance. The tree has dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in clusters of five. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening for its striking visual form.
