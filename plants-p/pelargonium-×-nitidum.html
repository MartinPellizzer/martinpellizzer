Pelargonium × nitidum

Pelargonium × nitidum, commonly known as the velvetleaf geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive, almost fuzzy appearance. The plant typically produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Native to South Africa, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.

Its foliage and flowers make it a popular choice for garden borders and container gardening.
