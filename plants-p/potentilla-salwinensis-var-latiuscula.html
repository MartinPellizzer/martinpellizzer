Potentilla salwinensis var. latiuscula

Potentilla salwinensis var. latiuscula, commonly known as the broad-leaved potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant is often found in rocky or well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and horticultural settings.
