Papaver lapponicum subsp. scandinavicum

Papaver lapponicum subsp.

scandinavicum, commonly known as the Scandinavian poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to northern Europe. It features striking bright red petals with a dark red throat, forming showy, cup-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky habitats, often in alpine or Arctic regions. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is erect and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
