Phalaris arundinacea f. picta

Phalaris arundinacea f. picta, commonly known as the variegated reed grass, is a decorative grass species native to Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display shades of white, yellow, and green, creating a visually appealing contrast. This plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its slender, grass-like blades form dense clumps, and it produces fluffy, spike-like seed heads in the summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping and water garden settings.
