Passiflora minima

Passiflora minima, commonly known as the dwarf passionflower, is a small climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features delicate, palmate leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a distinctive crown of filaments. The flowers are typically white or pale blue with a central cluster of red filaments, making it visually striking. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
