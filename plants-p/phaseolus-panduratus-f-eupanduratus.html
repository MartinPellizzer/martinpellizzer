Phaseolus panduratus f. eupanduratus

Phaseolus panduratus f. eupanduratus, commonly known as the Mexican runner bean, is a variant of the common bean genus native to Central America.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical pods that are typically green and covered with small, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers and has compound leaves with three leaflets. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its edible beans, which are rich in protein and nutrients.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and is used in both culinary and agricultural contexts.
