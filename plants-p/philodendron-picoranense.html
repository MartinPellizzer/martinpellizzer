Philodendron picoranense

Philodendron picoranense, commonly known as the Picoranense Philodendron, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves that vary in color from deep green to variegated patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
