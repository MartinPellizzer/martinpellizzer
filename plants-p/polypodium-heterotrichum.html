Polypodium heterotrichum

Polypodium heterotrichum, commonly known as the heterotrichous polypody, is a fern species belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that typically grow in a rosette or clustered pattern. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia and northern Australia. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments, making it a common epiphyte on trees and rocks.
