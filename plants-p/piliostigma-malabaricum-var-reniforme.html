Piliostigma malabaricum var. reniforme

Piliostigma malabaricum var. reniforme, commonly known as the Malabar spurred tortoise tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive spurred thorns and compound leaves with a palmate arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
