Pentzia calcarea

Pentzia calcarea, commonly known as the rock lily, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. It is adapted to withstand drought and high salinity, making it resilient in harsh environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
