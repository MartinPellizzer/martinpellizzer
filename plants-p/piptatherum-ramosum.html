Piptatherum ramosum

Piptatherum ramosum, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Piptatherum ramosum is valued for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in erosion control and as a forage crop.
