Phylogyne × calathina

Phylogyne × calathina, commonly known as the hybrid hibiscus, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Hibiscus syriacus and Hibiscus acetosella.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
