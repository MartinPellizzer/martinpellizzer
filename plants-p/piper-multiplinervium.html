Piper multiplinervium

Piper multiplinervium, commonly known as the multi-nerved pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with multiple nerves, giving it its scientific name. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by tiny, berry-like fruits that are edible.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has potential medicinal uses in traditional practices.
