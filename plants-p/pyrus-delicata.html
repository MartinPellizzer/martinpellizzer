Pyrus delicata

Pyrus delicata, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey-brown bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The tree produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet, yellowish fruit in late summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in Japanese horticulture.
