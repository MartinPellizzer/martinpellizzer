Penstemon procerus var. formosus

Penstemon procerus var. formosus, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, such as meadows and slopes, at high elevations. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
