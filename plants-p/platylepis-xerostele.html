Platylepis xerostele

Platylepis xerostele, commonly known as the desert lily, is a striking flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, a key adaptation to its dry environment. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in the spring. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

The plant's ability to thrive in harsh, low-water conditions makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.
