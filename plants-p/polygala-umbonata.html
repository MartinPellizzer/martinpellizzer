Polygala umbonata

Polygala umbonata, commonly known as the horned milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces small, bead-like fruits that contain a single seed, and it is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
