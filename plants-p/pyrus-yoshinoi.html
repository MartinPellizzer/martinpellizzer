Pyrus yoshinoi

Pyrus yoshinoi, commonly known as the Yoshino pear, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its graceful, slender form and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated margin. The tree produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically not eaten by humans but are used in traditional Japanese medicine.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and parks.
