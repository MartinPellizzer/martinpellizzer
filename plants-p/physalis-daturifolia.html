Physalis daturifolia

Physalis daturifolia, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or golden berry, is a flowering plant in the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny calyx that encloses the fruit, which resembles a small, yellow-orange berry. The plant is native to South America and thrives in warm, temperate climates. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals, and it produces fruit that is both edible and used in culinary applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and medicinal properties.
