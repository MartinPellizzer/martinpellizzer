Polypodium grammitidis

Polypodium grammitidis, commonly known as the common holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically bright green and slightly glossy. The plant produces small, round spore cases called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions.

Polypodium grammitidis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture and landscaping.
