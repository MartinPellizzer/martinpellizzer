Potentilla intermedia var. macropetala

Potentilla intermedia var. macropetala, commonly known as the large-flowered cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and its most distinctive characteristic is its showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and long-lasting blooms.
