Paepalanthus humilis

Paepalanthus humilis, commonly known as the dwarf puya, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences that resemble spikes of flowers, often in shades of yellow or orange. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
