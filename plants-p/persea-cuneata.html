Persea cuneata

Persea cuneata, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible when fully mature. Its botanical features include a smooth, gray bark and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

Persea cuneata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique fruit.
