Pelargonium eupatoriifolium

Pelargonium eupatoriifolium, commonly known as the mintleaf geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped to ovate leaves with a distinctive minty aroma. The leaves are usually covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. It produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to rock gardens and containers.
