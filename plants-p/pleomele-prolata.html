Pleomele prolata

Pleomele prolata, commonly known as the dwarf breadfruit, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slightly wavy margin. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, breadfruit-like berries. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
