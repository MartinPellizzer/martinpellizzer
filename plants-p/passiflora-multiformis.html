Passiflora multiformis

Passiflora multiformis, commonly known as the variable passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a mix of blue, violet, and white petals, along with intricate filaments and a crown of stamens. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and often twining.

Passiflora multiformis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
