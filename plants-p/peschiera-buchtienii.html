Peschiera buchtienii

Peschiera buchtienii, commonly known as the Buchtienii dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellow composite flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions. Its flowers are typically short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.

The species is often found in rocky or sandy soils, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
