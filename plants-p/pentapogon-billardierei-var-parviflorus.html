Pentapogon billardierei var. parviflorus

Pentapogon billardierei var. parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered pentapogon, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a leathery texture typical of the Myrtaceae family.

This species is often found in heathlands and rocky areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
