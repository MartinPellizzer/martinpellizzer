Piper adreptum

Piper adreptum, commonly known as the African pepper or Adreptum pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into spherical, blue-black berries. The berries are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ethnobotanical research.
