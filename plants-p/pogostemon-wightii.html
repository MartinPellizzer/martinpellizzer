Pogostemon wightii

Pogostemon wightii, commonly known as the Indian balm or Indian coleus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically violet or purple, arranged in whorled clusters.

Pogostemon wightii is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
