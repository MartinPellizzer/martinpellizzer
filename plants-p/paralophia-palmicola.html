Paralophia palmicola

Paralophia palmicola, commonly known as the palmleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its palm-like leaves, which are divided into numerous narrow segments, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, often blooming in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Paralophia palmicola is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and vibrant blooms.
