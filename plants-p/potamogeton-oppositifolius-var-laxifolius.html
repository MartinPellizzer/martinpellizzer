Potamogeton oppositifolius var. laxifolius

Potamogeton oppositifolius var. laxifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It features long, ribbon-like leaves that are opposite in arrangement and often have a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in clusters near the water surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in lakes and ponds.
