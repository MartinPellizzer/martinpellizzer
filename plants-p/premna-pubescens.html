Premna pubescens

Premna pubescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf or hairy mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in the summer months. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Premna pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
