Platystemon arvorum

Platystemon arvorum, commonly known as the tree mallow, is a perennial shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, white, or purple, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Platystemon arvorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
