Passiflora engleriana

Passiflora engleriana, commonly known as the Engler's passionflower, is a striking tropical vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that display a mix of blue, purple, and white hues, with intricate structures including a crown of filaments and a distinctive central column. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tropical flavor. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Passiflora engleriana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential calming effects.
