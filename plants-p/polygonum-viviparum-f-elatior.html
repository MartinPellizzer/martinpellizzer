Polygonum viviparum f. elatior

Polygonum viviparum f. elatior, commonly known as the greater knotweed, is a variant of the common knotweed species.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and reddish stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a hairy texture and a reddish underside.

This form is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is known for its vigorous growth and ability to spread through rhizomes.
