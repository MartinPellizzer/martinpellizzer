Phaeopappus sintenisii

Phaeopappus sintenisii, commonly known as the red pappus plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown pappus, which is a feathery structure that aids in seed dispersal. Native to the alpine regions of Europe, it thrives in rocky and high-altitude environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its unique coloration and habitat make it a notable species in botanical studies and ecological research.
