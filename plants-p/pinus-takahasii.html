Pinus takahasii

Pinus takahasii, commonly known as the Japanese black pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and its dense, needle-like leaves that are typically 2 to 4 centimeters long. The tree produces small, yellowish to reddish cones that mature over several years. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

Pinus takahasii is also valued for its resilience to urban environments and its ability to withstand pollution.
