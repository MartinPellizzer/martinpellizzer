Piper marginatum var. anisatum

Piper marginatum var. anisatum, commonly known as the creeping pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
