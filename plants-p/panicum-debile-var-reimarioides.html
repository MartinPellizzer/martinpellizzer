Panicum debile var. reimarioides

Panicum debile var. reimarioides, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a low-growing, perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery-blue foliage and delicate, fluffy seed heads that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. Its ornamental value lies in its soft texture and subtle coloration, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens and native plant landscapes.

It is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions, enhancing its adaptability in various ecological settings.
