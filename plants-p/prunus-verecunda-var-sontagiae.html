Prunus verecunda var. sontagiae

Prunus verecunda var. sontagiae, commonly known as the Sonntag’s cherry, is a rare and ornamental species native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, dark red berries in the summer. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal interest.
