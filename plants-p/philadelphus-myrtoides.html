Philadelphus myrtoides

Philadelphus myrtoides, commonly known as the myrtleleaf mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval to elliptical in shape and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
