Pseudotaxus liana

Pseudotaxus liana, commonly known as the Chinese yew or yew vine, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Taxaceae.

It is characterized by its woody stems and evergreen foliage, with leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that contain the seeds. It is native to regions of East Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates.

Pseudotaxus liana is noted for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
