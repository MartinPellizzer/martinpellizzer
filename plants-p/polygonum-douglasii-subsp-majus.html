Polygonum douglasii subsp. majus

Polygonum douglasii subsp.

majus, commonly known as the giant knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a waxy coating.

It thrives in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.
