Potentilla intermedia subsp. delphinensis

Potentilla intermedia subsp.

delphinensis, commonly known as the Delphinine cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
