Phreatia pacifica

Phreatia pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific phreatia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Phreatia pacifica produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often blooming during the rainy season.
