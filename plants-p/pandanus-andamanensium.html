Pandanus andamanensium

Pandanus andamanensium, commonly known as the Andaman pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Andaman Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into fibrous, spiky fruits. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often growing near mangroves.

Its fibrous leaves are valued for their durability and are used in weaving and thatching.
