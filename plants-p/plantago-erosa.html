Plantago erosa

Plantago erosa, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features upright, leafy stems that often grow in dense clusters, with oval to lance-shaped leaves that have a rough, sandpapery texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters called inflorescences, which are typically found at the tips of the stems. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their healing properties, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

Plantago erosa is also a common weed in agricultural fields and along roadsides.
