Pandanus muricatus

Pandanus muricatus, commonly known as the rough pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its sword-shaped, stiff leaves that are often covered in small, sharp spines. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by edible, fibrous fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or wetland areas.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in its native ecosystems.
