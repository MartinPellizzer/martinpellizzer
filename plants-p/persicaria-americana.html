Persicaria americana

Persicaria americana, commonly known as American knotweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, stream banks, and marshes. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters.

Persicaria americana is often found in disturbed soils and can spread rapidly, making it both a common weed and a valuable ecological indicator species.
