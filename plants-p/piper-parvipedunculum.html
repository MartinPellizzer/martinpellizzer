Piper parvipedunculum

Piper parvipedunculum, commonly known as the small-stemmed pepper plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is a small, elongated berry that contains a single seed, typical of the genus Piper.
