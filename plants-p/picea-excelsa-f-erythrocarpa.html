Picea excelsa f. erythrocarpa

Picea excelsa f. erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited silver fir, is a rare variant of the silver fir species native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-orange cones, which set it apart from the typical green cones of the species. The plant exhibits coniferous traits, with needle-like leaves and a tall, slender growth habit. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine forests.

Due to its limited distribution and unique features, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
