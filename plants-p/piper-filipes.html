Piper filipes

Piper filipes, commonly known as the slender pipevine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruits are elongated berries that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy aril.

Piper filipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
