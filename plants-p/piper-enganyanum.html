Piper enganyanum

Piper enganyanum, commonly known as the Enganyanum pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often glossy in appearance. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in spikes, and the fruit is a small, elongated berry that turns from green to red as it matures.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and berries, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
