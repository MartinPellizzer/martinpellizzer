Pandanus planatus

Pandanus planatus, commonly known as the flat-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its broad, flat leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and cuisine. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its roots and leaves are valued for their aromatic properties and are used in various cultural practices.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive fragrance that attracts pollinators.
