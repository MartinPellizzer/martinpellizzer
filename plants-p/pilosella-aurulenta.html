Pilosella aurulenta

Pilosella aurulenta, commonly known as the golden pearlyweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features finely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a soft, velvety appearance. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Pilosella aurulenta is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in a variety of environmental conditions.
