Picris drummondii

Picris drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally by Native American tribes.
