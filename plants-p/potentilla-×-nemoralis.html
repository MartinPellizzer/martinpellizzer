Potentilla × nemoralis

Potentilla × nemoralis, commonly known as the shrub willow or creeping willow, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its stems are often reddish or purplish in color.

Potentilla × nemoralis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control.
