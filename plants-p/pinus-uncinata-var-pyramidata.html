Pinus uncinata var. pyramidata

Pinus uncinata var. pyramidata, commonly known as the Pyramidal Scots Pine, is a variant of the Scots pine native to the Pyrenees and southern France.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dense, pyramidal crown, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The needles are typically blue-green in color, and the cones are small and often pendulous. This variety is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
