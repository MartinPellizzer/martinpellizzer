Primula begoniiformis

Primula begoniiformis, commonly known as the begonia-like primula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often in late spring or early summer, and are known for their subtle fragrance.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses, though it requires cool, moist conditions to thrive.
