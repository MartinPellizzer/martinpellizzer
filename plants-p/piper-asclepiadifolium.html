Piper asclepiadifolium

Piper asclepiadifolium, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a flowering shrub native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants, and is characterized by its small, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its propagation in the wild.
