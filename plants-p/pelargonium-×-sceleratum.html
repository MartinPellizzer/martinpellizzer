Pelargonium × sceleratum

Pelargonium × sceleratum, commonly known as the scented geranium, is a hybrid plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves that emit a variety of scents, such as citrus, mint, or spice, depending on the cultivar. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with rounded, lobed leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique fragrance has led to its use in aromatherapy and as a natural insect repellent.
