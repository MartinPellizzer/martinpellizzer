Pseudognaphalium stereovirens

Pseudognaphalium stereovirens, commonly known as the blueweed or blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fluffy, blue-gray flower heads. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that are clustered in spherical or ovoid shapes, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are usually narrow, silvery-gray, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-tolerant nature.

It is often found in open habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and disturbed areas, where it thrives in full sun.
