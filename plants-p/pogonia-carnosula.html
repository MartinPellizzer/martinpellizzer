Pogonia carnosula

Pogonia carnosula, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a rare and striking orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, showy pink and white flowers that resemble a slipper or a lady's shoe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and requires specific soil conditions to thrive. Its leaves are grass-like and often form a rosette near the base of the plant.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Pogonia carnosula is listed as endangered in many states, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
