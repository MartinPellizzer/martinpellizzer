Pithecellobium indicum

Pithecellobium indicum, commonly known as the Indian acacia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces edible pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.

Additionally, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
