Polyalthia macrantha

Polyalthia macrantha, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has compound leaves composed of several elliptical leaflets, and it produces woody, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
