Parathesis papillosa

Parathesis papillosa, commonly known as the hairy parenthesis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also bear a fuzzy texture, contributing to its overall hairy appearance.

Parathesis papillosa is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream sides.
