Primula dujiangyanensis

Primula dujiangyanensis, commonly known as the Dujiangyan primrose, is a flowering plant native to China, specifically the Sichuan province.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
