Pastinaca esculenta

Pastinaca esculenta, commonly known as wild parsnip, is a biennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves.
