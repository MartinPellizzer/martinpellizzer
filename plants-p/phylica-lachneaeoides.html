Phylica lachneaeoides

Phylica lachneaeoides, commonly known as the silver bristled phylica, is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, which give it a distinctive, silvery appearance.
