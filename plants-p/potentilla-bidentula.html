Potentilla bidentula

Potentilla bidentula, commonly known as the two-toothed cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are pinnately compound with two teeth on each leaflet. Potentilla bidentula has a woody stem and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit.

It is often found in regions with cool, temperate climates.
