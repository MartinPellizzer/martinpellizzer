Polygala arillata

Polygala arillata, commonly known as the golden thread or aril plant, is a member of the Polygalaceae family.

It is a flowering plant native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant is characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. Its unique feature is the presence of arils, which are fleshy, seed-coated structures that aid in seed dispersal.

Polygala arillata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
