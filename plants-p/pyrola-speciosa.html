Pyrola speciosa

Pyrola speciosa, commonly known as the showy pyrola, is a herbaceous perennial native to the forests of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as woodlands and mountainous regions. Its flowers, which are pink to red in color, bloom in late spring and are often visited by pollinators like bees and moths.

Pyrola speciosa is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
