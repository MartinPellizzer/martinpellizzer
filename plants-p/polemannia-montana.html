Polemannia montana

Polemannia montana, commonly known as the mountain polemannia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polemoniaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss.
