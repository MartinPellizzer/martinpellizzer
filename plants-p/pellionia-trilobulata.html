Pellionia trilobulata

Pellionia trilobulata, commonly known as the three-lobed pellionia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves with three distinct lobes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are often surrounded by a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. Pellionia trilobulata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage.

It prefers well-draining soil and consistent moisture, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
