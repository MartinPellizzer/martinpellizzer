Ploiarium oblongifolium

Ploiarium oblongifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ploiarium, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
