Potentilla japonica

Potentilla japonica, commonly known as Japanese cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats. Its leaves are compound, with five elliptical leaflets, and it produces small, persistent fruits.

Potentilla japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
