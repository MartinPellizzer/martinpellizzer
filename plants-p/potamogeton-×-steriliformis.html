Potamogeton × steriliformis

Potamogeton × steriliformis, commonly known as the sterile pondweed, is a hybrid plant species that belongs to the family Potamogetonaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. This plant typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers. It is a submersible plant, meaning it lives entirely underwater, and plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a mix of traits from its parent species, Potamogeton crispus and Potamogeton praevalidus.
