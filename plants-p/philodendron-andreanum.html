Philodendron andreanum

Philodendron andreanum, commonly known as the Brazilian Philodendron, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits containing seeds. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Philodendron andreanum is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
