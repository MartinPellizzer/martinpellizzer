Pittosporum xanthanthum

Pittosporum xanthanthum, commonly known as the golden pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its ability to tolerate coastal conditions also makes it suitable for landscaping in maritime environments.
