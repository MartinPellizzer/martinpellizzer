Paspalum michauxianum

Paspalum michauxianum, commonly known as Michaux's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland areas, including marshes and swamps, and thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant has slender, spreading stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in loose clusters, and it produces small, three-angled seeds.

Paspalum michauxianum plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
