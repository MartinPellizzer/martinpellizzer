Pandanus kamerunensis

Pandanus kamerunensis, commonly known as the Cameroon pandanus, is a species of palm native to the rainforests of Cameroon and Nigeria.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into fibrous, spiky fruits. Its leaves are robust and can be used for thatching and weaving.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.
