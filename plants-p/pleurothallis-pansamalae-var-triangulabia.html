Pleurothallis pansamalae var. triangulabia

Pleurothallis pansamalae var. triangulabia, commonly known as the triangular pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, often fragrant flowers that come in a range of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically produces three to five flowers per inflorescence, which are arranged in a triangular pattern, giving it its specific epithet. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil to flourish.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
