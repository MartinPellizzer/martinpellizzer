Pristiglottis saprophytica

Pristiglottis saprophytica, commonly known as the ghost moss, is a rare and enigmatic moss species that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent appearance and unique growth patterns, often forming dense, low-lying mats. The plant exhibits a specialized morphology with short, branching stems and small, leaf-like structures that contribute to its cryptic appearance. It plays a vital role in nutrient cycling within its ecosystem by decomposing organic matter.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, it remains a subject of ongoing botanical study.
