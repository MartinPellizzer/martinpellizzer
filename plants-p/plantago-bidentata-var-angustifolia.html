Plantago bidentata var. angustifolia

Plantago bidentata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and a prominent midrib. The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
