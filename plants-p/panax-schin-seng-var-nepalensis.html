Panax schin-seng var. nepalensis

Panax schin-seng var. nepalensis, commonly known as Himalayan ginseng, is a variant of the Panax genus native to the mountainous regions of Nepal and northern India.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, followed by red berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments at high altitudes, often found in forests and rocky terrains.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
