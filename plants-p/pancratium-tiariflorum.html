Pancratium tiariflorum

Pancratium tiariflorum, commonly known as the crown flower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a tall, slender stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Pancratium tiariflorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery.
