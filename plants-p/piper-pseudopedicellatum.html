Piper pseudopedicellatum

Piper pseudopedicellatum, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper or Indian pepper, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries. Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional medicine and local agriculture.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
