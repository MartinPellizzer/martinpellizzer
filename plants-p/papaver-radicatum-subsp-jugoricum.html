Papaver radicatum subsp. jugoricum

Papaver radicatum subsp.

jugoricum, commonly known as the Sierra larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Papaveraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky habitats and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

This subspecies is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
