Piptadenia laxa

Piptadenia laxa, commonly known as the Brazilian bean tree, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is a fast-growing, deciduous tree that can reach heights of up to 20 meters, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The tree also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native ecosystems.
