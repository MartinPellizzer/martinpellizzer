Polygala glandulosa

Polygala glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish or greenish flowers arranged in spikes. Its fruit is a follicle that releases seeds through a specialized mechanism, aiding in seed dispersal.

The species is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also of interest in botanical research.
