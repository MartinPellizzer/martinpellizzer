Persoonia rigida var. microphylla

Persoonia rigida var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leafed burr-pear, is a species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by distinctive woody fruits resembling burrs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or heathland environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
