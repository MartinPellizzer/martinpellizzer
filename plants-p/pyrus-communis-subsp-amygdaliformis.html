Pyrus communis subsp. amygdaliformis

Pyrus communis subsp.

amygdaliformis, commonly known as the almond-shaped pear, is a botanical variant of the European pear. It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated fruit shape that resembles an almond.

The plant typically produces small, sweet, and aromatic fruits with a thin, edible skin. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible produce.

This subspecies is notable for its genetic diversity and historical significance in horticultural breeding programs.
