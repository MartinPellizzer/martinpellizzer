Prunus pseudocerasus f. sieboldii

Prunus pseudocerasus f. sieboldii, commonly known as the Siebold's Chinese date, is a cultivar of the loquat tree native to China.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to orange-red, fuzzy fruits that ripen in late spring to early summer. The plant features small, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin and produces fragrant, white flowers in early spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often grown for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The foliage and fruit are both used in traditional Chinese medicine and culinary applications.
