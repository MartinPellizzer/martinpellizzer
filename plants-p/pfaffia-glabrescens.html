Pfaffia glabrescens

Pfaffia glabrescens, commonly known as curaçao root, is a perennial plant native to the Caribbean islands of Curaçao and Aruba.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its root system is the primary part used for medicinal purposes, containing compounds believed to have adaptogenic properties.

Pfaffia glabrescens thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
