Phyla betulifolia

Phyla betulifolia, commonly known as the birchleaf plant, is a member of the Betulaceae family, which includes birches, alders, and hazels.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that often resemble those of birch trees, hence its common name. The plant typically has small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated, and its fruits are small, woody nuts enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The leaves are usually serrated and turn yellow in the fall, adding seasonal interest to its appearance.
