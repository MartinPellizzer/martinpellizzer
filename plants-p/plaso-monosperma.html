Plaso monosperma

Plaso monosperma, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces a fruit that is rich in nutrients, often used as a food source. Its bark contains compounds with potential medicinal properties.

The tree is also valued for its ability to thrive in diverse soil conditions.
