Piper cyphophyllum

Piper cyphophyllum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piper genus within the Piperaceae family, which includes over 200 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into the familiar black, white, and pink peppercorns. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated for its aromatic fruits used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant's botanical structure supports its role in traditional medicine and global spice trade.
