Persea rehderi

Persea rehderi, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces small, orange-red berries that are edible when fully ripe. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its dense foliage and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

Persea rehderi is also valued for its potential in agroforestry systems due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
