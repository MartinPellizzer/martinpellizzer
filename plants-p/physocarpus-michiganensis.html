Physocarpus michiganensis

Physocarpus michiganensis, commonly known as Michigan smudgebrush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with serrated edges, and turn red in the fall.

Physocarpus michiganensis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness in a variety of soil conditions.
