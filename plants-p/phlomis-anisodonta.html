Phlomis anisodonta

Phlomis anisodonta, commonly known as the woolly phlomis, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and tall, branched flower spikes that bloom in shades of purple and pink. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its aromatic leaves and showy flowers make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.
