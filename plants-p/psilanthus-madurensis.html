Psilanthus madurensis

Psilanthus madurensis, commonly known as the Madura acacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody pod, and the plant is often found in dry, tropical regions.

Psilanthus madurensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
