Plectronia pulchra

Plectronia pulchra, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive, lantern-like shape.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, bean-like seeds.
