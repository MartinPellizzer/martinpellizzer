Paspalum scrobiculatum var. orbiculatum

Paspalum scrobiculatum var. orbiculatum, commonly known as the orbiculate paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and potential for use in restoration projects.
