Pleiocarpidia capitata

Pleiocarpidia capitata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often woody and branched. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
