Pleioblastus longifolius

Pleioblastus longifolius, commonly known as the longleaf bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. This plant typically forms dense clumps and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and establish itself in various soil types.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its graceful appearance.
