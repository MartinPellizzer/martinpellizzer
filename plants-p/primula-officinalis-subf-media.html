Primula officinalis subf. media

Primula officinalis subf.

media, commonly known as the common primrose, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features basal rosettes of aromatic, dark green leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, white, and pink.

The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully unfurl. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued, contributing to its common name "officinalis.".
