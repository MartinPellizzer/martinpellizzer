Pervinca heterophyla

Pervinca heterophyla, commonly known as the common yellow arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often bright green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that are surrounded by a spathe, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Pervinca heterophyla is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wetland habitats.
