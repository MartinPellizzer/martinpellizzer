Piperanthera

Piperanthera, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its unique, fragrant, and often showy flowers that bloom in clusters. It is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters.

Despite its ornamental appeal, it is not commonly cultivated due to its specific environmental requirements.
