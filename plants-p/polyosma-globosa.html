Polyosma globosa

Polyosma globosa, commonly known as the globular thornapple, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive globular shape and spiny leaves, which give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in dry, rocky environments and is often found in shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
