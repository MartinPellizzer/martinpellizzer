Polypodium lungtauense

Polypodium lungtauense, commonly known as the Lungtau fern, is a species of polypody that belongs to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are typically divided into pinnae with a serrated or toothed margin. The plant is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as China, India, and the Philippines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on rock surfaces or in forest understories.

Its rhizomes are somewhat fibrous and can be used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.
