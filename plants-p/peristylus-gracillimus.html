Peristylus gracillimus

Peristylus gracillimus, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of trees and shrubs.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along its stems. The plant produces tiny white or pale yellow flowers, which are often clustered in small inflorescences. Its berries are usually red or orange and contain a single seed, which is dispersed by birds.

Peristylus gracillimus is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is often found in humid, shaded forest environments.
