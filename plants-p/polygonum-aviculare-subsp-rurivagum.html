Polygonum aviculare subsp. rurivagum

Polygonum aviculare subsp.

rurivagum, commonly known as the field knotgrass, is a herbaceous annual plant belonging to the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae). It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit, with slender, branched stems that often root at the nodes.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a distinctive glossy appearance.

This species is commonly found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas, and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.
