Papaver robertianella

Papaver robertianella, commonly known as the Robert's poppy, is a delicate annual herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces small, pale yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive, narrow corolla. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
