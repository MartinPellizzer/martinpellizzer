Peperomia aguacatensis

Peperomia aguacatensis, commonly known as the banana peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial shrub native to Central and South America.

It features thick, waxy, ovate leaves that are typically light to dark green, often with a slight variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or pale pink, which are followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its low maintenance and attractive foliage make it a favored ornamental plant.
