Penstemon fruticiformis subsp. typicus

Penstemon fruticiformis subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the tall beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It produces numerous flowering stems that bear clusters of flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
