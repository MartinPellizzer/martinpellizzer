Pelargonium aestivale

Pelargonium aestivale, commonly known as the summer geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically rounded with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden beds and containers.

Pelargonium aestivale is often used in ornamental horticulture for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various climates.
