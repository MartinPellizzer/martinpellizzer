Polygonum acmophyllum

Polygonum acmophyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved smartweed, is a flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is native to North America and typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as along stream banks and in wet meadows. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red in color. It is an annual or biennial herb that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, and it is often found in wetland habitats.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in wet environments and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
