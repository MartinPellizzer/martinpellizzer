Panicum leibergii var. baldwinii

Panicum leibergii var. baldwinii, commonly known as Baldwin's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and tufted inflorescences that appear in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and erosion control. Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

The botanical features of this variety include its compact growth habit and ability to form dense mats.
