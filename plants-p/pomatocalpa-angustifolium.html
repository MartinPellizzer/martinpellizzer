Pomatocalpa angustifolium

Pomatocalpa angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It features a slender, erect stem with a few leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike. The plant has a pseudobulb, which serves as a storage organ for water and nutrients. Its leaves are narrow and linear, typically ranging from 5 to 15 centimeters in length.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.
