Pulsatilla patens var. sandomiriensis

Pulsatilla patens var. sandomiriensis, commonly known as the Sandomirian pasqueflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
