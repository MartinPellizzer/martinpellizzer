Psidium albescens

Psidium albescens, commonly known as the white guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant produces small, white or pale green fruits that are typically harvested when they are fully mature. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
