Prunus conradinae semiplena

Prunus conradinae semiplena, commonly known as the Florida laurelcherry, is a rare and endangered species native to Florida.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in wetland habitats. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in panicles, blooming in the spring.

The fruit is a small, smooth, yellowish drupe that ripens in the summer.
