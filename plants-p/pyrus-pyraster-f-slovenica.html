Pyrus pyraster f. slovenica

Pyrus pyraster f. slovenica, commonly known as the Slovenian crabapple, is a rare horticultural form of the common crabapple.

It is characterized by its small, ornamental flowers and edible, tart fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.
