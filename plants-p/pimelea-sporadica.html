Pimelea sporadica

Pimelea sporadica, commonly known as the coastal banksia, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that produce small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in coastal environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapting to harsh maritime conditions.

Pimelea sporadica is also noted for its ability to withstand salt spray and strong winds.
