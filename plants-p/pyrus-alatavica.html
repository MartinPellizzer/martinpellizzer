Pyrus alatavica

Pyrus alatavica, commonly known as the Turkmenian pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Turkmenistan, Uzbekistan, and Afghanistan. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, with simple, alternate leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like berry, is edible and has been traditionally used in local cuisines.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.
