Polystachya similis

Polystachya similis, commonly known as the common flame orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that resemble flames, featuring vibrant red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, warm environments. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Polystachya similis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in the ecological systems of its native habitats.
