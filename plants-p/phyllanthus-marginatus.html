Phyllanthus marginatus

Phyllanthus marginatus, commonly known as the marginated phyllanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with wavy margins and small, inconspicuous flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.

The species is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
