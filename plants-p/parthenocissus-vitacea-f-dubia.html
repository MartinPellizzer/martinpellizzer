Parthenocissus vitacea f. dubia

Parthenocissus vitacea f. dubia, commonly known as the variable ivy grape.

This climbing vine is characterized by its compound leaves that vary in shape and size, often showing a range of lobes and serrations. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically followed by red or orange berries. It is native to parts of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden use, though it can be invasive in some regions.
