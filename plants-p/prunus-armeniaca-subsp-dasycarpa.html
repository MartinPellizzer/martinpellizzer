Prunus armeniaca subsp. dasycarpa

Prunus armeniaca subsp.

dasycarpa, commonly known as the dwarf apricot, is a subspecies of the apricot tree characterized by its smaller size and compact growth habit. It produces small, round fruits with a thin, fuzzy skin and yellow-orange flesh.

The plant is native to regions of Central Asia and is adapted to temperate climates with distinct seasons. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, appearing in early spring before the leaves.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also cultivated for its edible fruits.
