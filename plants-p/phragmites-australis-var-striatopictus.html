Phragmites australis var. striatopictus

Phragmites australis var. striatopictus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved common reed, is a variant of the common reed found in wetland habitats.

It is a tall, perennial grass that can grow up to 3 meters in height, with narrow, linear leaves that have a distinctive striped pattern. The plant produces dense clusters of brown, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind. It plays an important role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its growth is often influenced by water levels and soil salinity, making it adaptable to a range of wetland environments.
