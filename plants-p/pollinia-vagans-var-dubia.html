Pollinia vagans var. dubia

Pollinia vagans var. dubia, commonly known as the wandering pollinia variant, is a unique orchid species characterized by its specialized pollinarium structure.

Unlike typical orchids, it lacks the usual column and instead possesses a modified structure called a pollinium, which is loosely attached and can detach easily. This adaptation allows for a more dispersed pollination method, enhancing its reproductive success in various habitats. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in shaded, moist environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its unusual morphology and ecological role in pollination dynamics.
