Potentilla tabernaemontani longifrons

Potentilla tabernaemontani longifrons, commonly known as the long-fruited potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender, woody stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and they are covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to rocky, well-drained soils.
