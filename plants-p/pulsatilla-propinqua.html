Pulsatilla propinqua

Pulsatilla propinqua, commonly known as the pasque flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms that range from white to pink or purple. The plant features a short, thick stem with palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and hairy. Its flowers are typically solitary and surrounded by a crown of sepals, which give it a distinctive appearance.

Pulsatilla propinqua is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in alpine or subalpine environments.
