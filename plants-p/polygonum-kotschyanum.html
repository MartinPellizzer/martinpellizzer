Polygonum kotschyanum

Polygonum kotschyanum, commonly known as the Kotschyan's knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive nodes and often translucent leaf sheaths. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, riverbanks, and wetlands. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a reddish or purple tinge, while its small flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters.

Polygonum kotschyanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
