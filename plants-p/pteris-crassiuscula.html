Pteris crassiuscula

Pteris crassiuscula, commonly known as the broad-leafed water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, deeply divided fronds that are typically bright green. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in humid forests. Its rhizomes are creeping and help in the spread of the plant.

Pteris crassiuscula is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
