Platymiscium pinnatum var. diadelphum

Platymiscium pinnatum var. diadelphum, commonly known as the red-flowered ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a robust, woody stem with a smooth, grayish-brown bark. Its leaves are compound, with long, slender leaflets arranged in an alternate pattern.

This species is valued for its hardiness and is often used in ornamental horticulture and reforestation projects.
