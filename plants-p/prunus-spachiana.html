Prunus spachiana

Prunus spachiana, commonly known as the Caucasian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces edible drupes that are typically yellow or red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous areas.

Prunus spachiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
