Purpurella grossa

Purpurella grossa, commonly known as the purple bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet, often with a distinctive purplish tinge. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
