Passiflora hullettii

Passiflora hullettii, commonly known as the Hullett's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Passiflora hullettii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
