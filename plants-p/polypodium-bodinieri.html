Polypodium bodinieri

Polypodium bodinieri, commonly known as the Bodin's huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns.

Polypodium bodinieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
