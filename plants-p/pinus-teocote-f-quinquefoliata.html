Pinus teocote f. quinquefoliata

Pinus teocote f. quinquefoliata, commonly known as the five-leaf Mexican pine, is a variant of the Mexican pine characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It is native to southern Mexico and is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features needle-like leaves that are often in groups of five, giving it its common name. It produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
