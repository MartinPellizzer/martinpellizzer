Pulmonaria auriculata

Pulmonaria auriculata, commonly known as the ear-shaped lungwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features distinctive basal leaves with rounded, auriculate (ear-like) lobes, and its flowers are clustered in upright spikes. The plant produces pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in moist, shady woodland habitats and is often found in calcareous soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
