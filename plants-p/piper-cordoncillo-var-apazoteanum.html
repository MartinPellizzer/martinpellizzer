Piper cordoncillo var. apazoteanum

Piper cordoncillo var. apazoteanum, commonly known as the Apazote plant, is a perennial shrub native to Central America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly pubescent surface and emits a strong, pungent odor. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by berry-like fruits that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique aroma and medicinal properties have made it a subject of interest in traditional herbal practices.
