Pseudoroegneria heidemaniae

Pseudoroegneria heidemaniae, commonly known as the Heidemann's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
