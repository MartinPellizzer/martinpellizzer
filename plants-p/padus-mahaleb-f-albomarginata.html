Padus mahaleb f. albomarginata

Padus mahaleb f. albomarginata, commonly known as the white-margin crabapple, is a cultivated form of the common crabapple.

It is characterized by its distinctive white-edged leaves and small, ornamental white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in autumn, and produces small, yellowish fruits in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
