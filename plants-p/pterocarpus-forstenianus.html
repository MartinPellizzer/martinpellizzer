Pterocarpus forstenianus

Pterocarpus forstenianus, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive yellow flowers. The tree produces flat, elongated pods that contain a hard, woody seed surrounded by a fibrous pulp. Its roots have a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

The tree is valued for its timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
