Plantago polymorpha

Plantago polymorpha, commonly known as the common plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including roadsides, fields, and disturbed soils, due to its hardy nature.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with its leaves used in traditional remedies for their soothing and anti-inflammatory effects.
