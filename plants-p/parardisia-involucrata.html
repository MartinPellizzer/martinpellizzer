Parardisia involucrata

Parardisia involucrata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous tiny seeds.

Parardisia involucrata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and along riverbanks.
