Parthenium frutescens

Parthenium frutescens, commonly known as brittleweed, is an annual flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and waste places.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily, it can become invasive in certain regions.
