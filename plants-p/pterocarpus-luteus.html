Pterocarpus luteus

Pterocarpus luteus, commonly known as the yellow pterocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, ovate leaflets that are often hairy.

The tree is also known for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
