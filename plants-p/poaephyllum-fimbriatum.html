Poaephyllum fimbriatum

Poaephyllum fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed leaf cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, branching structure with fleshy, spiny stems that store water, adapting to its harsh environment. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and often have a fringed appearance, contributing to its distinctive look.

Poaephyllum fimbriatum is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and drought tolerance.
