Pavetta sansibarica subsp. sansibarica

Pavetta sansibarica subsp.

sansibarica, commonly known as the East African pavetta, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of East Africa. It typically grows in coastal forests and sandy soils, thriving in humid and warm climates.

The plant features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. Its flowers are small, clustered in cymes, and usually have five white or pale pink petals.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
