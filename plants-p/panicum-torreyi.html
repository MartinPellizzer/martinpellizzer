Panicum torreyi

Panicum torreyi, commonly known as Torrey's Panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, two-lobed bracts.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
