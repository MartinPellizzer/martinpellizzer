Pleopeltis tenuinervis

Pleopeltis tenuinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined wall fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, pinnately divided fronds with a slender, almost hair-like appearance. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are characterized by a narrow midrib and secondary veins that are finely spaced.

Pleopeltis tenuinervis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
