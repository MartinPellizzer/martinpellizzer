Polygala armata

Polygala armata, commonly known as the armed milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
