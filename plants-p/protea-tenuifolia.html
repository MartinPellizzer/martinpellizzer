Protea tenuifolia

Protea tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved protea, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, cone-shaped flower head surrounded by long, narrow bracts that are often brightly colored. The plant belongs to the Proteaceae family, which is known for its showy, often brightly colored flowers. Protea tenuifolia typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in areas with moderate to high rainfall.

Its unique floral structure makes it a popular choice for horticultural displays and garden landscapes.
