Protea grandiflora

Protea grandiflora, commonly known as the Giant Protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of flowers, though they are actually a single flower with many petal-like structures called tepals. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces of South Africa, where it thrives in nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and red, and are often used in floral arrangements.

Protea grandiflora is also valued for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators such as birds and insects.
