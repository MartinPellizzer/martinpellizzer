Piper tentatum

Piper tentatum, commonly known as the red pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes several species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit, which resembles a small pepper, is typically reddish when mature and contains a single seed.

Piper tentatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
