Philodendron radiatum var. pseudoradiatum

Philodendron radiatum var. pseudoradiatum, commonly known as the Radiatum Philodendron, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves that can grow quite large, often reaching up to 12 inches in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically hidden within the foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

This variety is popular among indoor gardeners due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
