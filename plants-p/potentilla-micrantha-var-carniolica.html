Potentilla micrantha var. carniolica

Potentilla micrantha var. carniolica, commonly known as the Carpathian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins, and its stems are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
