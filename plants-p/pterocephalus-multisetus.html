Pterocephalus multisetus

Pterocephalus multisetus, commonly known as the many-branched goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds enclosed in a spiky, bristly capsule. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
