Populus tremuloides var. pendula

Populus tremuloides var. pendula, commonly known as weeping trembling aspen, is a variant of the trembling aspen characterized by its weeping habit.

It features long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green leaves that are ovate and have a serrated margin. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones or near water sources.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping.
