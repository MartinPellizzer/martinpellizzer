Polygala vulgaris var. apiculata

Polygala vulgaris var. apiculata, commonly known as the common milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are alternate and trifoliate, with three leaflets that are usually oval-shaped.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
