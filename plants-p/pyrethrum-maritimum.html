Pyrethrum maritimum

Pyrethrum maritimum, commonly known as sea pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Europe and the Mediterranean.

It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are adapted to thrive in salty, windy environments. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its natural habitat includes sandy or rocky shores, where it is well-suited to withstand harsh coastal conditions.
