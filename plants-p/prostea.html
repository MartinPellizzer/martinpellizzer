Prostea

Prostea, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a member of the Bignoniaceae family.

It is a climbing shrub that features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are often velvety in texture. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

Prostea is native to the Americas and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
