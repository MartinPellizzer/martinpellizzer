Potentilla atrosanguinea var. cathaclines

Potentilla atrosanguinea var. cathaclines, commonly known as the black blood potentilla, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its dark red to black stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its ornamental value and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
