Phaseolus abyssinicus

Phaseolus abyssinicus, commonly known as the Abyssinian bean, is a climbing legume native to Ethiopia.

It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate arrangements and small white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important crop for both local consumption and agricultural research.
