Paradrymonia campostyla

Paradrymonia campostyla, commonly known as the Mexican white sage, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it well adapted to dry environments.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is often cultivated in xeriscapes and herbal gardens.
