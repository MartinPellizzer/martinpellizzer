Petrorhagia laconica

Petrorhagia laconica, commonly known as the Laconica sandthrift, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces vibrant yellow flowers in the spring. The plant has hairy, branching stems and is well adapted to dry, sandy soils. It is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Petrorhagia laconica is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in harsh conditions.
