Pereilema crinitum var. cirratum

Pereilema crinitum var. cirratum, commonly known as the curly hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive, hair-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spikelets, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
