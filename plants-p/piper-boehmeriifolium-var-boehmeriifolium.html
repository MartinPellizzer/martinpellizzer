Piper boehmeriifolium var. boehmeriifolium

Piper boehmeriifolium var. boehmeriifolium, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central America.

It features compound leaves with elongated, pointed lobes that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, peppery berries that are edible. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and medicinal properties.
