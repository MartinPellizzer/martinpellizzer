Prunus mutabilis f. hexapetala

Prunus mutabilis f. hexapetala, commonly known as the six-petaled cherry, is a unique variant of the variable cherry.

It is characterized by its distinctive six-petaled flowers, which are more prominent and showy compared to the typical five-petaled form. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to Japan. It produces small, edible fruits that are similar to those of other Prunus species.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
