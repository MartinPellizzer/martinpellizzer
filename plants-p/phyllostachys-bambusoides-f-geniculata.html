Phyllostachys bambusoides f. geniculata

Phyllostachys bambusoides f. geniculata, commonly known as the twisted bamboo or geniculate bamboo, is a unique variant of the bamboo species characterized by its distinctive, spirally twisted stems.

This ornamental bamboo is native to China and is prized for its striking, curling shoots that add visual interest to gardens. The plant typically grows in a clump form, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are a vibrant green, complementing its unusual stem structure.

While it is relatively low-maintenance, it requires adequate space to grow and can be propagated through rhizome division or seed.
