Plantago weldenii subsp. weldenii

Plantago weldenii subsp.

weldenii, commonly known as the Welden's plantain, is a perennial herb native to parts of North America. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture and produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in wet environments and is often found along stream banks.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
