Potentilla alchimilloides subsp. atlantica

Potentilla alchimilloides subsp.

atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the coastal regions of the Iberian Peninsula. It typically features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, which are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal and maritime climates.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
