Pleurothallis aggeris

Pleurothallis aggeris, commonly known as the silver-veined orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often feature a unique silver or white veining. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and glossy, adapting to the moist conditions of its natural habitat.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by enthusiasts interested in rare orchids.
