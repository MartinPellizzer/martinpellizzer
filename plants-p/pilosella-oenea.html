Pilosella oenea

Pilosella oenea, commonly known as the European goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features a hairy stem and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a dense, brush-like inflorescence. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance.

Pilosella oenea is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a characteristic species of the Central European flora.
