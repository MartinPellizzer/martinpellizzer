Pouteria surumuensis

Pouteria surumuensis, commonly known as the Surumu Brazil nut tree, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon basin.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The tree produces edible nuts enclosed in a hard, woody capsule, similar to the Brazil nut. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and plays a vital role in the ecological balance of its habitat.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
