Pimelea glauca var. subenervia

Pimelea glauca var. subenervia, commonly known as the silverleaf pimelea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as heathlands and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
