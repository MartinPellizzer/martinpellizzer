Protasparagus africanus

Protasparagus africanus, commonly known as the African asparagus, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, edible berries.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
