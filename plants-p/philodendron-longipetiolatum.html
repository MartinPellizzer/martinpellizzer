Philodendron longipetiolatum

Philodendron longipetiolatum, commonly known as the long-petioled philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant has long, slender petioles that help it climb and spread throughout its natural habitat. Its foliage is typically a deep green, with some varieties exhibiting variegation.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil for optimal growth.
