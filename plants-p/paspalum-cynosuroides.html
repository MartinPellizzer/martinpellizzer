Paspalum cynosuroides

Paspalum cynosuroides, commonly known as the silky paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, which can stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
