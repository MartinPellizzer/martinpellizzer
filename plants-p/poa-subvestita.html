Poa subvestita

Poa subvestita, commonly known as the velvet grass, is a species of grass native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, soft blades that give it a velvety texture. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is often concealed by the basal leaves, making it distinctive in appearance.

Poa subvestita is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
