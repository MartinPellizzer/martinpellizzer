Pollinia tenuis

Pollinia tenuis, commonly known as the slender pollinia orchid, is a type of orchid that exhibits unique botanical features.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its specialized reproductive structures called pollinia. These pollinia are small, dry, and sticky masses that adhere to the insect vectors during pollination. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments.

Its morphology includes slender stems and lanceolate leaves, making it a distinctive member of the orchid family.
