Prostanthera phylicifolia

Prostanthera phylicifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf mintbush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or pink. It is typically found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Prostanthera phylicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
