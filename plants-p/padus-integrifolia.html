Padus integrifolia

Padus integrifolia, commonly known as the white-fruited hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, oval-shaped leaves that are usually smooth and have a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by bright red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
