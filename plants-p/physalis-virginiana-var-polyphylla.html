Physalis virginiana var. polyphylla

Physalis virginiana var. polyphylla, commonly known as the common groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It produces small, yellow-orange fruits enclosed in a papery calyx, which ripens in late summer. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and along roadsides, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its stems are hairy and branching.

This species is valued for its edible fruits and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
