Persea austroguizhouensis

Persea austroguizhouensis, commonly known as the Guizhou persimmon, is a species of evergreen tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces orange to red fruits that are edible and have a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. It thrives in subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
