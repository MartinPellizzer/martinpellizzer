Padus avium var. discolor

Padus avium var. discolor, commonly known as the whitehorn cherry, is a variant of the common bird cherry.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant is characterized by its white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and it produces small, red berries in the fall.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
