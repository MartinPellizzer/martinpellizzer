Prostanthera magnifica var. asperata

Prostanthera magnifica var. asperata, commonly known as the rough-leaved mintbush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in spikes along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as woodlands and heathlands.

Prostanthera magnifica var. asperata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
