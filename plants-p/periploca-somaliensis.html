Periploca somaliensis

Periploca somaliensis, commonly known as the Somali periploca, is a flowering plant native to the Horn of Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions.

The species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in local ecosystems and potential medicinal uses.
