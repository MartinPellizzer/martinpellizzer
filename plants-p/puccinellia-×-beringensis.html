Puccinellia × beringensis

Puccinellia × beringensis, commonly known as the Beringian saltbush, is a hybrid grass species native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America and Asia.

It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, often found in coastal tundra and salt marshes. The plant is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and narrow, needle-like leaves. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Puccinellia × beringensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals in its harsh environment.
