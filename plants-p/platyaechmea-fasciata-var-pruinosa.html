Platyaechmea fasciata var. pruinosa

Platyaechmea fasciata var. pruinosa, commonly known as the Pruinose Sword Fern, is a striking evergreen fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features long, narrow, arching fronds that are typically a vibrant green with a silvery-blue sheen, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by spore-bearing structures called sori, typical of the fern family. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is well-suited for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardens and as a houseplant.
