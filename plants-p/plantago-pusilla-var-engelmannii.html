Plantago pusilla var. engelmannii

Plantago pusilla var. engelmannii, commonly known as Engelmann's narrowleaf plantain, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and thrives in open, sunny environments such as prairies and rocky areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in a spike-like inflorescence. Its leaves are covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes, which help reduce water loss.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
