Pyrrosia porosa var. tonkinensis

Pyrrosia porosa var. tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on trees or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features delicate, leathery fronds with a distinctive, serrated edge. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
