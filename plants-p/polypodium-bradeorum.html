Polypodium bradeorum

Polypodium bradeorum, commonly known as the Brade's fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in rainforests. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds and release spores for propagation.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitats.
