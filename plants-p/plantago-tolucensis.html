Plantago tolucensis

Plantago tolucensis, commonly known as the Tolucan plantain, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a fibrous root system that helps it retain water in its native habitat.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
