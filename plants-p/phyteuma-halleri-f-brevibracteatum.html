Phyteuma halleri f. brevibracteatum

Phyteuma halleri f. brevibracteatum, commonly known as the short-bracted phyteuma, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, violet to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant produces small, bract-like structures at the base of the flower clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and relatively low maintenance requirements.
