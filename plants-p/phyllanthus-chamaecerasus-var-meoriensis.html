Phyllanthus chamaecerasus var. meoriensis

Phyllanthus chamaecerasus var. meoriensis, commonly known as the Meoriensis false crosswort, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly India.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its botanical features include glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which contribute to its unique appearance and ecological interactions.

It is also noted for its potential medicinal uses in traditional systems of medicine.
