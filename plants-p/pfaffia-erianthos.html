Pfaffia erianthos

Pfaffia erianthos, commonly known as golden root, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its yellowish root system, which is the primary part used for its medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

Pfaffia erianthos is valued for its potential adaptogenic and anti-inflammatory properties in traditional medicine.
