Pera ekmanii

Pera ekmanii, commonly known as the Ethiopian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and other nightshades. The plant produces small, purple berries that are toxic to humans but may have traditional uses in local medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Its botanical features include hairy stems, lobed leaves, and showy flowers that bloom at night.
