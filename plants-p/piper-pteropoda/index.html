Piper Pteropoda

Piper pteropoda, scientifically known as Piper pteropoda, is a member of the Piperaceae family commonly found in tropical regions. This unique plant is characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive leaf morphology. It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's name derives from the Greek words "pteron" meaning wing and "podas" meaning foot, referring to its leaf shape. Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, Piper pteropoda is gaining attention among botanists and horticulturists alike.
