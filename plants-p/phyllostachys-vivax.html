Phyllostachys vivax

Phyllostachys vivax, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often covered with a silvery-gray sheath, giving it its common name. This plant is a perennial grass that can grow quite tall, reaching heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of climates.

Phyllostachys vivax is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and ease of maintenance.
