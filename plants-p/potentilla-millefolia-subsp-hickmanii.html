Potentilla millefolia subsp. hickmanii

Potentilla millefolia subsp.

hickmanii, commonly known as Hickman's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
