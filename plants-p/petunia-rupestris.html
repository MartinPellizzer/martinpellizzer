Petunia rupestris

Petunia rupestris, commonly known as the rock petunia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of blue, violet, and white. The plant produces small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Petunia rupestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
