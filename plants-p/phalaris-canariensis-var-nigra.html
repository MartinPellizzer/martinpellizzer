Phalaris canariensis var. nigra

Phalaris canariensis var. nigra, commonly known as black beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Canary Islands.

It features dark green, narrow leaves with a distinctive blackish or purplish sheath at the base, giving it its characteristic appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale blue or white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
