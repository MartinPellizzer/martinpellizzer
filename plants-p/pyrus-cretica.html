Pyrus cretica

Pyrus cretica, commonly known as the Crete pear, is a small, deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters and has a rounded, spreading crown. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin, while the flowers are white and clustered in cymes. The fruit is a small, edible pear that ripens in late summer, characterized by its sweet, juicy flesh.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in horticulture.
