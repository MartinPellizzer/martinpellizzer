Penstemon laetus subsp. filiformis

Penstemon laetus subsp.

filiformis, commonly known as the slender beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers.

The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
