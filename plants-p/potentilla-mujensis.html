Potentilla mujensis

Potentilla mujensis, commonly known as the Mujan cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with multiple stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically divided into five leaflets, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

Potentilla mujensis is adapted to harsh, alpine environments and is often found in rocky soils at high elevations.
