Prosopis algarobilla var. nandubay

Prosopis algarobilla var. nandubay, commonly known as the Nandubay acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that allow it to access water from lower soil layers. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically yellow or pale green, and it produces elongated, woody pods containing seeds.

The species is valued for its hardy nature and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
