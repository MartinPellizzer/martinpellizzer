Psammotropha mucronata

Psammotropha mucronata, commonly known as the sand mat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to drought conditions.

Psammotropha mucronata plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
