Pinus massoniana var. shaxianensis

Pinus massoniana var. shaxianensis, commonly known as the Shaxian fir, is a rare variant of the Chinese fir.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern China, particularly in Fujian and Zhejiang provinces. This coniferous tree is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and needle-like leaves that are typically dark green. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mixed forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited commercial use due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements.
