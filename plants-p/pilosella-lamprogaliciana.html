Pilosella lamprogaliciana

Pilosella lamprogaliciana, commonly known as the silverleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often on rocky slopes and meadows. The plant features a basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves, which give it its common name. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in a single head, surrounded by pale yellow ray florets.

Pilosella lamprogaliciana is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
