Polypodium fernandense

Polypodium fernandense, commonly known as the Brazilian shield fern, is a tropical fern native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are leathery and often have a distinctive, shield-like appearance.

Polypodium fernandense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
