Pyrus obscura

Pyrus obscura, commonly known as the obscure pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and somewhat obscure species within the genus Pyrus.
