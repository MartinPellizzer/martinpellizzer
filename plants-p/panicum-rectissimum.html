Panicum rectissimum

Panicum rectissimum, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant communities and a subject of ecological study.
