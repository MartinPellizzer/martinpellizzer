Polygala japonica var. angustifolia

Polygala japonica var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, often hairy stems that grow from 30 to 100 centimeters in height. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in spicate inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture, and it prefers well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
