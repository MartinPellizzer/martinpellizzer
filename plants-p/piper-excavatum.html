Piper excavatum

Piper excavatum, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, known as a drupe, is typically harvested when mature for use in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic properties.
