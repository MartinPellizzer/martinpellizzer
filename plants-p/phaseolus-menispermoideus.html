Phaseolus menispermoideus

Phaseolus menispermoideus, commonly known as the Andean runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, butterfly-shaped flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible beans, similar to common beans but with a distinct flavor and texture. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is cultivated in various regions for both food and garden purposes.
