Pachyanthus acunaeanus

Pachyanthus acunaeanus, commonly known as the Acunaean flame tree, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in dense clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets and produces woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare in the wild.
