Pothos philippinensis

Pothos philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine pothos, is a tropical climbing plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically green with a variegated pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spathes that enclose the true flowers. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its adaptability to low light and easy maintenance make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
