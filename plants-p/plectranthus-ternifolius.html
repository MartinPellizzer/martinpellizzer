Plectranthus ternifolius

Plectranthus ternifolius, commonly known as the curly mint or African violet, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features aromatic, hairy leaves that are often curled or twisted, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Plectranthus ternifolius is popular as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and pleasant mint-like fragrance.
