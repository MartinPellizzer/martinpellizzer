Piptocoma dentata

Piptocoma dentata, commonly known as the serrated pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Pittosporaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical with serrated margins, and it produces small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in spring.

Piptocoma dentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
