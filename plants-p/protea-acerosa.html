Protea acerosa

Protea acerosa, commonly known as the hard-leaved protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, cone-shaped inflorescences that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. Protea acerosa is well adapted to Mediterranean climates, with a dormant period during the summer months.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
