Psammophila muralis

Psammophila muralis, commonly known as the wall-growing sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It thrives in arid and rocky environments, often growing on walls and cliffs. The plant has small, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss, and it produces clusters of pink or purple flowers. It is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low moisture conditions.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
