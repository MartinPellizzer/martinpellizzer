Pulegium daghestanicum

Pulegium daghestanicum, commonly known as the Daghestan mint, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or purple. It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory effects.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
