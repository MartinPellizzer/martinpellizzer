Phaleria disperma

Phaleria disperma, commonly known as the Java magnolia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Phaleria disperma thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
