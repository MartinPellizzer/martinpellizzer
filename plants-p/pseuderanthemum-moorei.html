Pseuderanthemum moorei

Pseuderanthemum moorei, commonly known as Moore's false jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
