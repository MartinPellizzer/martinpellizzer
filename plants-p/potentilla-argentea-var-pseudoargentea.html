Potentilla argentea var. pseudoargentea

Potentilla argentea var. pseudoargentea, commonly known as silver potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has silvery-gray foliage due to dense pubescence, which helps reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
