Prunus pygmaeorum

Prunus pygmaeorum, commonly known as the dwarf cherry, is a small deciduous shrub or tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, typically reaching heights of less than 2 meters. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, sweet-tart berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

Prunus pygmaeorum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
