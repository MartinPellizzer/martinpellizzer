Phleum phalaroides

Phleum phalaroides, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Phleum phalaroides is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
