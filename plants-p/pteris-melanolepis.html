Pteris melanolepis

Pteris melanolepis, commonly known as the black-stem maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green with a contrasting reddish or brownish hue on the underside. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, humus-rich soil. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds and are covered by a protective indusium.

Pteris melanolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
