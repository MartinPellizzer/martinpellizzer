Philibertia cynanchoides var. subtruncata

Philibertia cynanchoides var. subtruncata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed filbert vine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a truncate base. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along canyon walls.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
