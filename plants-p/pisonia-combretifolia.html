Pisonia combretifolia

Pisonia combretifolia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

The species is also valued for its ecological role, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
