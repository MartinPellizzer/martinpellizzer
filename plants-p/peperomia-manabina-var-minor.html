Peperomia manabina var. minor

Peperomia manabina var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf peperomia, is a compact, low-maintenance plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features rosettes of glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish or purple tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers during the growing season, though it is more prized for its foliage than its blooms. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it ideal for indoor cultivation.

Its compact size and ornamental leaves make it a popular choice for tabletop displays and container gardening.
