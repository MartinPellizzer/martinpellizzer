Prainea frutescens

Prainea frutescens, commonly known as the dwarf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its flowers are showy, with five petals, often in shades of red, pink, or white, and are borne on short pedicels.

Prainea frutescens is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive blooms and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
