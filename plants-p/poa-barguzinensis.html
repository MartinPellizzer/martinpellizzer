Poa barguzinensis

Poa barguzinensis, commonly known as Barguzin grass, is a species of perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ecological significance, it plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
