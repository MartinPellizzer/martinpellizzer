Palenia delfinii

Palenia delfinii, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a striking perennial plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring finely divided, fern-like leaves. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil.

Palenia delfinii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with specific growing conditions.
