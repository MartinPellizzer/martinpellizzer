Papyrus elatus

Papyrus elatus, commonly known as the tall papyrus, is a tall, reed-like plant native to the wetlands of sub-Saharan Africa.

It grows in shallow, freshwater environments and can reach heights of up to 5 meters. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often used for making paper and other crafts. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Papyrus elatus is an important part of the ecosystem, providing habitat for various aquatic species.
