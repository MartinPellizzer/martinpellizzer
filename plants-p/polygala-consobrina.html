Polygala consobrina

Polygala consobrina, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate and narrow, while its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters.

Polygala consobrina is often found in rocky areas and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
