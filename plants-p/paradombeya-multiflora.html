Paradombeya multiflora

Paradombeya multiflora, commonly known as the multibract palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by bright red fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
