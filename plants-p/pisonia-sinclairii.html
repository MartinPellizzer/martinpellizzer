Pisonia sinclairii

Pisonia sinclairii, commonly known as the Christmas berry, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific and Indian Oceans.

It is characterized by its showy, pink or red bracts that surround its small, white flowers, which bloom in clusters. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitats.

Pisonia sinclairii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in coastal and island ecosystems.
