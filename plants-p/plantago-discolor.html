Plantago discolor

Plantago discolor, commonly known as the variable plantain, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant has broad, oval-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a prominent midrib. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes, producing a distinctive silver-green appearance.

The species is notable for its adaptability and is often used in herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
