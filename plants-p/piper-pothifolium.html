Piper pothifolium

Piper pothifolium, commonly known as the Indian pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into peppery, three-lobed berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions with high humidity and partial shade.

The berries are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
