Phemeranthus aurantiacus

Phemeranthus aurantiacus, commonly known as the golden ephemeral, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its short-lived, showy flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The plant has a compact, herbaceous growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are typically bright orange or yellow, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Due to its brief blooming period, it is considered a rare and delicate species within its habitat.
