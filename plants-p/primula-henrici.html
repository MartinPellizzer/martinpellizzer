Primula henrici

Primula henrici, commonly known as the Henrici's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing showy, violet or white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Primula henrici is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ability to thrive in cool, moist environments.
