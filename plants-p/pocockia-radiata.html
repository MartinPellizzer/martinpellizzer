Pocockia radiata

Pocockia radiata, commonly known as the radiate pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a cushion-like structure. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Pocockia radiata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
