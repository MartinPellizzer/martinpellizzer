Phrynium parvum

Phrynium parvum, commonly known as the small-leaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, usually appearing in shades of green or red.

Phrynium parvum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
