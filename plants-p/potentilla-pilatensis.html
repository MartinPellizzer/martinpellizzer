Potentilla pilatensis

Potentilla pilatensis, commonly known as the Pilat cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, with five to seven lobes, and are covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla pilatensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
