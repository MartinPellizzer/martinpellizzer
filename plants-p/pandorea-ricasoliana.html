Pandorea ricasoliana

Pandorea ricasoliana, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a silvery underside, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and blooms.
