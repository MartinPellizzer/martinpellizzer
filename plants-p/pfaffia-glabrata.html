Pfaffia glabrata

Pfaffia glabrata, commonly known as Brazilian ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Compositae family and is characterized by its upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers and is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Brazilian medicine. Pfaffia glabrata is often cultivated for its roots, which are used in herbal remedies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
