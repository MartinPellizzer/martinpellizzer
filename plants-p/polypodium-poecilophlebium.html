Polypodium poecilophlebium

Polypodium poecilophlebium, commonly known as the narrow-veined wall fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with closely spaced, narrow veins. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rocky crevices or forest floors. Its rhizome is creeping and produces both roots and new fronds.

The species is native to various regions in Asia, including China, Japan, and the Himalayas.
