Phlomis superba

Phlomis superba, commonly known as the tall blazing star, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and large, showy purple to pink flower heads that bloom in summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Phlomis superba is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
