Plantago exilis

Plantago exilis, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in spikes during the summer months. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, due to its adaptability.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
