Phlox austromontana var. prostrata

Phlox austromontana var. prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate phlox, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features dense clusters of fragrant, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has spreading stems that hug the ground, making it ideal for ground cover in shaded or partially shaded areas. Its foliage is typically dark green and remains attractive throughout the growing season.

It is well-suited for rock gardens and moist woodland environments.
