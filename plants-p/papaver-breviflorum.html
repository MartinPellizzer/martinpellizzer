Papaver breviflorum

Papaver breviflorum, commonly known as the short-flowered poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
