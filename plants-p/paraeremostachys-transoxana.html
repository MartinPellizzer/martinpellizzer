Paraeremostachys transoxana

Paraeremostachys transoxana, commonly known as the Transoxiana grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
