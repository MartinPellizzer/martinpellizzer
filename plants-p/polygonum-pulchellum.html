Polygonum pulchellum

Polygonum pulchellum, commonly known as the showy knotweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a reddish underside and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. It is often found in the eastern United States and parts of Canada.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
