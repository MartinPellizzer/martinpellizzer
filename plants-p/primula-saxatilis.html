Primula saxatilis

Primula saxatilis, commonly known as the rock primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are often hairy and slightly wrinkled.

Primula saxatilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
