Peucedanum diversifolium

Peucedanum diversifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, crowned with clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves that are often deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its roots are thick and aromatic, containing compounds used in traditional medicine.

Peucedanum diversifolium is valued for its medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
