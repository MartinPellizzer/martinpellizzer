Physosiphon pubescens

Physosiphon pubescens, commonly known as the hairy physosiphon, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude regions.

Due to its unique morphology and restricted distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
