Potamogeton berchtoldii var. mucronatus

Potamogeton berchtoldii var. mucronatus, commonly known as the sharp-tipped pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense spikes, and it is often found in slow-moving or still waters. Its rhizomes help anchor it to the substrate, and it plays a role in maintaining water quality by absorbing nutrients.

This species is important for aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
