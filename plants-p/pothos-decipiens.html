Pothos decipiens

Pothos decipiens, commonly known as the "devil's ivy," is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically light green with a slightly variegated pattern. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular houseplant. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, though these are rarely seen in cultivation.

Pothos decipiens is also notable for its adaptability and resilience, often surviving in a variety of environmental conditions.
