Plectranthus macilentus

Plectranthus macilentus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hair. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus macilentus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can be grown as a container plant or in the ground.
