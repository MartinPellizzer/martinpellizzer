Pereskia grandifolia

Pereskia grandifolia, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a succulent plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are often mistaken for leaves, and it produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant belongs to the Cactaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its stems, which helps it survive in arid conditions. It blooms in the winter months, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, while the stems are the primary photosynthetic organs.
