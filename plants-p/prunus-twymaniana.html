Prunus twymaniana

Prunus twymaniana, commonly known as the Twyman cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, dark red to black berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Prunus twymaniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in hybridization programs for developing new cherry varieties.
