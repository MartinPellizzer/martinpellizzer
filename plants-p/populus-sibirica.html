Populus sibirica

Populus sibirica, commonly known as Siberian poplar, is a deciduous tree native to the cold regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its white to gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in river valleys and mountainous areas.

Populus sibirica is valued for its hardiness and is used in reforestation efforts in arid and semi-arid regions.
