Polygonum komarovii

Polygonum komarovii, commonly known as Komarov's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, clustered flowers. Its leaves are typically lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Polygonum komarovii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
