Poterium strictum

Poterium strictum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, wiry growth habit and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
