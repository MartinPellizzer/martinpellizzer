Potentilla × commixta

Potentilla × commixta, commonly known as the hybrid cinquefoil, is a deciduous shrub that belongs to the Rosaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Potentilla fruticosa and Potentilla nemoralis, resulting in a plant with distinct botanical characteristics. The species typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, upright growth and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed sites.
