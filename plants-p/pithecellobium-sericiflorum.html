Pithecellobium sericiflorum

Pithecellobium sericiflorum, commonly known as the silky-flowered acacia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and compound leaves with small, elliptical leaflets. The plant produces delicate, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, giving it a feathery appearance. Its fruit is a pod that contains several edible, sweet, and fleshy seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
