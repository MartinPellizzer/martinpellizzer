Physalis ixocarpa

Physalis ixocarpa, commonly known as the husk tomato or golden berry, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its hairy, inflated calyx that encases the fruit. The fruit is small, round, and typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It is often used in culinary applications, including jams, sauces, and as a decorative element.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various growing conditions.
