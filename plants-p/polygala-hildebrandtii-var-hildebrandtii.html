Polygala hildebrandtii var. hildebrandtii

Polygala hildebrandtii var. hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often branching towards the upper part. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
