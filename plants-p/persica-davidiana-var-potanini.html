Persica davidiana var. potanini

Persica davidiana var. potanini, commonly known as the Potanin peach.

This variant is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. The fruit is a small, yellow peach with a thin skin and sweet, aromatic flesh.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
