Piper calvilimbum

Piper calvilimbum, commonly known as the rough-leaved pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its dark green, rough-textured leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
