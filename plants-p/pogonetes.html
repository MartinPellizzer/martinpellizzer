Pogonetes

Pogonetes, commonly known as the "false hellebore," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Polygonaceae.

It consists of several species, primarily found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plants are characterized by their distinctive, often spiny stems and simple, alternate leaves. They produce small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color.

Pogonetes species are generally herbaceous and can be found in moist, woodland habitats.
