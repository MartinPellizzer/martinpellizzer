Piper chiapasense

Piper chiapasense, commonly known as the Chiapas pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, elongated berry that turns red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
