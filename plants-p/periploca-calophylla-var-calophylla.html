Periploca calophylla var. calophylla

Periploca calophylla var. calophylla, commonly known as the silky ash or silky periploca, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange or red berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or semi-arid regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in various cultures.
