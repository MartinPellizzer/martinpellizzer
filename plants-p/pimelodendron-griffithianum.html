Pimelodendron griffithianum

Pimelodendron griffithianum, commonly known as the Indian sago palm, is a cycad species native to India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are arranged in a branched inflorescence. Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, red or orange aril, which is often consumed by birds and mammals.

Pimelodendron griffithianum plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
