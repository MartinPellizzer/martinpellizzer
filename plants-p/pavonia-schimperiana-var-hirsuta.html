Pavonia schimperiana var. hirsuta

Pavonia schimperiana var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy pavonia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright red or pink bract-like flowers. The plant has hairy, often glandular leaves that give it its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
