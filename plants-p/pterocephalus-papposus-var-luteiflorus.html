Pterocephalus papposus var. luteiflorus

Pterocephalus papposus var. luteiflorus, commonly known as the yellow-flowered pappose helenium, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and feathery bracts. The plant features yellow ray florets surrounded by a dense, pappose disk, which aids in wind pollination. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its ornamental value is appreciated in gardens for its vibrant yellow blooms.
