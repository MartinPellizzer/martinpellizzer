Piper colubrinum

Piper colubrinum, commonly known as the black pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its dark green, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into peppery berries. Its fruit contains the aromatic compound piperine, which is responsible for its sharp, spicy flavor.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in warm climates.
