Passiflora arida var. cerralbensis

Passiflora arida var. cerralbensis, commonly known as the Cerrado passionflower, is a species native to Brazil, particularly found in the Cerrado biome.

It is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces striking, colorful flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. The flowers feature a unique structure with filaments that resemble the Passion of Christ, giving the genus its common name. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
