Phelypaea brunneri

Phelypaea brunneri, commonly known as the Brunner's water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to South America.

It typically grows in freshwater habitats such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has finely divided, feather-like fronds that float on the water's surface. Its rhizomes are submerged, anchoring the plant and absorbing nutrients from the surrounding water.

Phelypaea brunneri plays a role in maintaining water quality by filtering sediments and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
