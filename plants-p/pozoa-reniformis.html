Pozoa reniformis

Pozoa reniformis, commonly known as the kidney-shaped pozoa, is a unique species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive kidney-shaped leaves, which are typically thick and succulent, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often yellow or greenish in color, adding a subtle aesthetic to its appearance. Pozoa reniformis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
