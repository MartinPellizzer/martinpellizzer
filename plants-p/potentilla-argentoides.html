Potentilla argentoides

Potentilla argentoides, commonly known as silver potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody.

Potentilla argentoides is often used in rock gardens and alpine settings due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
