Polemonium carneum f. amoenum

Polemonium carneum f. amoenum, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, pinkish-white flowers that resemble small slippers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical name reflects its distinctive appearance and its classification within the Polemonium genus.
