Passerina pubescens

Passerina pubescens, commonly known as the hairy pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms. The plant features a hairy stem and palmate leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
