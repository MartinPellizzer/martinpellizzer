Prunus serrulata f. glauca

Prunus serrulata f. glauca, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or some varieties as the weeping cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus serrulata species.

It is characterized by its distinctive bluish-gray foliage, which gives it a unique appearance compared to other cherry blossom varieties. The plant produces delicate pink or white flowers in early spring, which bloom before the leaves appear. It is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its ornamental value.

This form is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and traditional Japanese culture.
