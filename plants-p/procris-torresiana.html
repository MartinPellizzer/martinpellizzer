Procris torresiana

Procris torresiana, commonly known as the Torresiana orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a combination of purple, white, and green hues. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are strap-like and often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its natural habitat.

Procris torresiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
