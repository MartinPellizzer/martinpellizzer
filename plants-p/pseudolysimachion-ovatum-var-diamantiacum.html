Pseudolysimachion ovatum var. diamantiacum

Pseudolysimachion ovatum var. diamantiacum, commonly known as the Diamondleaf False Lythrum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers that are clustered in spikes, adding a delicate appearance to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
