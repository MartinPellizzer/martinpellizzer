Prunus brittoniana

Prunus brittoniana, commonly known as the Britton cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched structure. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, red drupes. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

Prunus brittoniana is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.
