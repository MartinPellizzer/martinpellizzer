Potentilla lignipes

Potentilla lignipes, commonly known as the hardy cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Potentilla lignipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
