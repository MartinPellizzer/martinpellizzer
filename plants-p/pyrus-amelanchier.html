Pyrus amelanchier

Pyrus amelanchier, commonly known as the serviceberry or juneberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that ripen in late summer, often used in jams and desserts. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a serrated margin.

Pyrus amelanchier is valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
