Papaver argemone var. laciniatum

Papaver argemone var. laciniatum, commonly known as the fringed poppy, is a variant of the argemone poppy characterized by its distinctive, fringed petals.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, often found in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orb-shaped seed capsules. Its foliage consists of deeply lobed, hairy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
