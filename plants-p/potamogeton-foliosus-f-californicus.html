Potamogeton foliosus f. californicus

Potamogeton foliosus f. californicus, commonly known as the California pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to the western United States.

It features long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually found in spike-like clusters. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers.

Its dense growth helps stabilize sediments and provides habitat for aquatic organisms.
