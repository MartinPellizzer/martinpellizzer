Polygonum deasyi

Polygonum deasyi, commonly known as the Deasy's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and flowering structures. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a prominent midrib. Small, clustered flowers appear in late summer, producing bright red or pink blooms that attract pollinators.

It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks.
