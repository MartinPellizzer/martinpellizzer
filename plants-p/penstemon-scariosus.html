Penstemon scariosus

Penstemon scariosus, commonly known as the desert penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to red. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Penstemon scariosus is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
