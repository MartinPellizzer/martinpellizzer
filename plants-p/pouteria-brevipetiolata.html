Pouteria brevipetiolata

Pouteria brevipetiolata, commonly known as the short-stalked spondias, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves with a short petiole. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, orange-colored berries. The fruit contains a single seed surrounded by a fleshy, aromatic pulp.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
