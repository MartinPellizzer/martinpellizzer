Pinus flexilis subsp. albicaulis

Pinus flexilis subsp.

albicaulis, commonly known as the whitebark pine, is a subspecies native to the western United States and parts of Canada. It is characterized by its distinctive white bark, which is covered in a layer of thick, scaly skin.

The tree typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in subalpine or alpine environments. Its cones are serotinous, meaning they remain closed until exposed to heat from a fire, allowing for seed dispersal after a wildfire.

This adaptation makes it an important component of fire-prone ecosystems.
