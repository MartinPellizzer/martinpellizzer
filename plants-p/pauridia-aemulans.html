Pauridia aemulans

Pauridia aemulans, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, often showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are designed to mimic the appearance of other plants, aiding in pollination by attracting specific insects.

Pauridia aemulans is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native habitat.
