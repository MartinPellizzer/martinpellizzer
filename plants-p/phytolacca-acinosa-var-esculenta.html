Phytolacca acinosa var. esculenta

Phytolacca acinosa var. esculenta, commonly known as Chinese red root or red madder, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces oval, fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing toxic seeds. Its roots are the primary part used, known for their deep red color and historical use in dyeing and traditional medicine.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
