Pinus × suevica

Pinus × suevica, commonly known as the Sitka cross pine, is a natural hybrid between Pinus sylvestris and Pinus muricata.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves, which are typically longer and more rigid than those of its parent species. The cones of Pinus × suevica are often larger and more robust, containing larger seeds that are dispersed by wind. This hybrid is commonly found in coastal regions where it thrives in moist, temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of hybridization in coniferous forests.
