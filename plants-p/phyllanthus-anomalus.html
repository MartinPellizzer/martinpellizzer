Phyllanthus anomalus

Phyllanthus anomalus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
