Patagonium patagonicum

Patagonium patagonicum, commonly known as the Patagonian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in grasslands and shrublands. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various insects and small animals.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is considered a symbol of the rugged landscapes of Patagonia.
