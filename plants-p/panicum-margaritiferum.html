Panicum margaritiferum

Panicum margaritiferum, commonly known as the beadgrass, is a flowering plant native to wetland and marshy areas in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, bead-like spikelets that give it its common name. The plant produces delicate, pale green to golden spikelets that are often adorned with small, translucent bracts. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in swamps, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

Panicum margaritiferum plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
