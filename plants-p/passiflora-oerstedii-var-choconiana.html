Passiflora oerstedii var. choconiana

Passiflora oerstedii var. choconiana, commonly known as the Choconia passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. This species produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a mild, sweet flavor.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.
