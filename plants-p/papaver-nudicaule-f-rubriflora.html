Papaver nudicaule f. rubriflora

Papaver nudicaule f. rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered poppy, is a striking variant of the meadow poppy.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals that contrast beautifully with its pale green foliage. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and thrives in well-drained, sunny soils. It produces delicate, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The f. rubriflora form is particularly valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
