Piper plumbeicolor

Piper plumbeicolor, commonly known as the blue-leaved pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking bluish-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's unique coloration and medicinal potential make it a subject of interest in both botanical and pharmacological research.
