Pithecellobium pendulum

Pithecellobium pendulum, commonly known as the hanging acacia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, pendulous branches and clusters of small, yellow, fragrant flowers. The plant produces flat, leathery pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
