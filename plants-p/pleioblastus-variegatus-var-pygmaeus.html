Pleioblastus variegatus var. pygmaeus

Pleioblastus variegatus var. pygmaeus, commonly known as the Japanese forest grass, is a compact, evergreen clump-forming plant native to Japan.

It features narrow, dark green leaves with distinctive white or silvery markings, giving it a striking visual appeal. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by fluffy, grayish seed heads. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
