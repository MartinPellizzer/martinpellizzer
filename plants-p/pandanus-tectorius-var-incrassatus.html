Pandanus tectorius var. incrassatus

Pandanus tectorius var. incrassatus, commonly known as the broad-leaf pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its broad, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material. It grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

The inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, and the plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are pollinated by insects.
