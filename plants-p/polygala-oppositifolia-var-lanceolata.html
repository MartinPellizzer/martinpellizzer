Polygala oppositifolia var. lanceolata

Polygala oppositifolia var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, often yellow or greenish flowers arranged in racemes. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This variant is distinguished by its elongated foliage compared to other varieties within the species.
