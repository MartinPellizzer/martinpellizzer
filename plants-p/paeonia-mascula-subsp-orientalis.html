Paeonia mascula subsp. orientalis

Paeonia mascula subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the Chinese peony, is a species native to China and parts of the Himalayas. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red.

The plant has compound, pinnately arranged leaves and produces woody underground stems called rhizomes. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The flowers are often used in traditional Chinese medicine and are also celebrated in cultural festivals.
