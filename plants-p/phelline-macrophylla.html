Phelline macrophylla

Phelline macrophylla, commonly known as the broadleaf phelline, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Phelline macrophylla is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
