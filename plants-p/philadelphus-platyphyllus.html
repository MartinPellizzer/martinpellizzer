Philadelphus platyphyllus

Philadelphus platyphyllus, commonly known as the flat-leaf mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has simple, opposite, and often heart-shaped leaves that are broad and flat, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and aesthetic purposes in landscaping.
