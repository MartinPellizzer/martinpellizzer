Pygeum ramiflorum

Pygeum ramiflorum, commonly known as the branched-flowered African dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces red, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Pygeum ramiflorum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
