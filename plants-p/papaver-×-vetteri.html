Papaver × vetteri

Papaver × vetteri, commonly known as the hybrid poppy, is a cultivated plant resulting from the crossbreeding of several species within the Papaver genus.

It is characterized by its striking, large, and often brightly colored flowers that can range from red to orange and even white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring sturdy stems and broad, lobed leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
