Pyrus communis var. cultrensis

Pyrus communis var. cultrensis, commonly known as the Cultrensis pear, is a variety of the common pear tree native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its medium-sized, oval-shaped fruits that are typically yellowish-green when ripe. The leaves are broad and lobed, with a slightly serrated edge, and the tree has a moderate growth habit. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild, sweet flavor, making it suitable for both ornamental gardening and limited fruit production.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
