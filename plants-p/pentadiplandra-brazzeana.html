Pentadiplandra brazzeana

Pentadiplandra brazzeana, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a leguminous shrub native to central and western Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by pod fruits containing edible, protein-rich seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

Due to its nutritional value, it is cultivated for both food and forage purposes.
