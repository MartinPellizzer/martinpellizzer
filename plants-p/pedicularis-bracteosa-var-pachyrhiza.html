Pedicularis bracteosa var. pachyrhiza

Pedicularis bracteosa var. pachyrhiza, commonly known as the broad-leafed lousewort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and lanceolate, often reddish leaves. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
