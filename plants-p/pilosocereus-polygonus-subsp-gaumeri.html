Pilosocereus polygonus subsp. gaumeri

Pilosocereus polygonus subsp.

gaumeri, commonly known as the Gaumer's hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its dense clusters of spiny, cylindrical stems that resemble a hedgehog.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-draining soil and minimal water.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in desert gardens.
