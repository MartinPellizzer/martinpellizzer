Pleurothallis seidelii

Pleurothallis seidelii, commonly known as the Seidel's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, often with intricate patterns on the petals. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular subject among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
