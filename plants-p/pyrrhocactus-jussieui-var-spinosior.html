Pyrrhocactus jussieui var. spinosior

Pyrrhocactus jussieui var. spinosior, commonly known as the spiny pyrrhocactus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, spiny stems and has clusters of bright yellow flowers during the blooming season. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, a common adaptation in desert plants.

The varietal name "spinosior" refers to its more pronounced spines compared to the typical variety.
