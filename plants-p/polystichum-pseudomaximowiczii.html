Polystichum pseudomaximowiczii

Polystichum pseudomaximowiczii, commonly known as the Japanese forest fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to eastern Asia.

It features tall, arching fronds with a dark green, leathery texture and a distinctive, finely divided structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, thriving in cool, humid climates. Its rhizomes are robust, allowing it to form dense clumps over time.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a houseplant.
