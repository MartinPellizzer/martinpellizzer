Phyteuma atropurpureum

Phyteuma atropurpureum, commonly known as the purple evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple petals and yellow stamens, which bloom in the late afternoon. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The species is part of the Onagraceae family, which includes other notable plants like the evening primrose and willowherb.
