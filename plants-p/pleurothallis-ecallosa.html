Pleurothallis ecallosa

Pleurothallis ecallosa, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a striking white or pale green coloration. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces intricate, fragrant blooms that attract various pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
