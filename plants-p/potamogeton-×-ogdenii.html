Potamogeton × ogdenii

Potamogeton × ogdenii, commonly known as the hybrid water plant, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton praelatus.

It is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, spike-like flowers. This plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
