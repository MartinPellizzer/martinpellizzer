Pyrus cordigastensis

Pyrus cordigastensis, commonly known as the Cordiloba pear, is a rare and ancient species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elongated leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible pomes that are typically yellow or orange in color. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.

Its botanical features make it of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
