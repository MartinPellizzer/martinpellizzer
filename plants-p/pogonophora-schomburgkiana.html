Pogonophora schomburgkiana

Pogonophora schomburgkiana, commonly known as the Schomburgk's beard-plant, is a unique member of the family Pogonophoraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, or bristly appearance, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. Its structure consists of a central stem with whorled leaves and specialized reproductive structures.

It is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
