Phyllanthus abditus

Phyllanthus abditus, commonly known as the Indian false shamrock, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elliptical, red or orange fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for container gardening.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
