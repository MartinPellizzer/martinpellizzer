Protea conocarpodendron

Protea conocarpodendron, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of flowers, though they are actually a single flower with many petal-like bracts. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are leathery and often covered in a silvery or greyish waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The protea is an important component of fynbos vegetation and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
