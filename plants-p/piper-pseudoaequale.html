Piper pseudoaequale

Piper pseudoaequale, commonly known as the false black pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by three-lobed, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically ovate with a slightly serrated margin and a glossy surface.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
