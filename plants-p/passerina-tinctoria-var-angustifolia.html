Passerina tinctoria var. angustifolia

Passerina tinctoria var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
