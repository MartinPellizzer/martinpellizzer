Pilea dauciodora var. uncidens

Pilea dauciodora var. uncidens, commonly known as the silver mohair plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to South America.

It features dense, velvety foliage with a silvery-white underside, giving it a distinctive, soft appearance. The leaves are typically oval to heart-shaped, and the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
