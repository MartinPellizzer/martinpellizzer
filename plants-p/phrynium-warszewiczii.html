Phrynium warszewiczii

Phrynium warszewiczii, commonly known as the Zanzibar arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to East Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and a prominent midrib. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often in shades of green or red.

The plant is valued for its edible tubers, which are rich in starch and used in local cuisines.
