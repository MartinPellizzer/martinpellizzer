Prunus jamasakura f. misogi

Prunus jamasakura f. misogi, commonly known as the "Ghost Cherry" or "Mishigi Cherry," is a unique cultivar of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its pale pink to almost white flowers, which appear in early spring, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks. Its leaves are dark green and oval-shaped, providing a striking contrast to its delicate blossoms.

This variety is particularly valued for its ethereal beauty and is often associated with the fleeting beauty of life in Japanese culture.
