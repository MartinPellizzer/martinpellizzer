Pfaffia brunae

Pfaffia brunae, commonly known as Brazilian ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Compositae family and is characterized by its upright stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers and is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its adaptogenic qualities. Pfaffia brunae is valued for its roots, which are used in traditional medicine to enhance vitality and reduce stress.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
