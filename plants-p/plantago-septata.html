Plantago septata

Plantago septata, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and prominent veins. The plant produces erect flowering stems topped with a dense cluster of small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
