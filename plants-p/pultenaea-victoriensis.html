Pultenaea victoriensis

Pultenaea victoriensis, commonly known as the Victoria bush pea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and woodland areas. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, attracting various pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
