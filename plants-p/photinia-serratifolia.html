Photinia serratifolia

Photinia serratifolia, commonly known as the serrate photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have serrated edges, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a versatile plant for landscaping purposes.
