Plectranthus bellus

Plectranthus bellus, commonly known as the beautiful plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a pleasant scent. It produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of purple and white, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Plectranthus bellus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its vibrant blooms and fragrant foliage.
