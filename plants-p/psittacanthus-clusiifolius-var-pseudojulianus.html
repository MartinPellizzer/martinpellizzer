Psittacanthus clusiifolius var. pseudojulianus

Psittacanthus clusiifolius var. pseudojulianus, commonly known as the golden star hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers, which are typically five-petaled and resemble stars. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy green leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for cultivation in gardens and as a houseplant.
