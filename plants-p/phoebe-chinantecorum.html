Phoebe chinantecorum

Phoebe chinantecorum, commonly known as the Chinantec phoebe, is a small evergreen tree native to the humid forests of southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, while its fruit is a small, dark berry that contains a single seed.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
