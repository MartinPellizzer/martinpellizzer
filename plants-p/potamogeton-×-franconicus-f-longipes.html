Potamogeton × franconicus f. longipes

Potamogeton × franconicus f. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant characterized by its elongated, flexible stems.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers. The plant produces small, green to reddish-brown flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, often floating or submergent depending on water conditions.

This species is notable for its adaptability and plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various water organisms.
