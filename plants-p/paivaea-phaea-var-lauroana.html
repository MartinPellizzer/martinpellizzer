Paivaea phaea var. lauroana

Paivaea phaea var. lauroana, commonly known as the Brazilian fern, is a striking evergreen fern native to South America.

It features large, leathery, dark green fronds that can grow up to 1.5 meters in length. The plant has a unique rhizomatous growth habit, with a thick, fleshy rootstock that helps it store moisture. Its leaves are compound, with a distinctive, almost palmate shape, and they are often adorned with a waxy coating.

This fern thrives in humid, shaded environments and is prized for its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
