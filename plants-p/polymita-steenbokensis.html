Polymita steenbokensis

Polymita steenbokensis, commonly known as the Swedish saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Scandinavia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons.

Polymita steenbokensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
