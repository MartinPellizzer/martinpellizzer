Poterium vulgare f. mauritanicum

Poterium vulgare f. mauritanicum, commonly known as the Mauritania thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slender stems and small, needle-like leaves that are densely packed. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
