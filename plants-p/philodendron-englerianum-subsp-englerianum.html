Philodendron englerianum subsp. englerianum

Philodendron englerianum subsp.

englerianum, commonly known as the Engler's philodendron, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America. It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
