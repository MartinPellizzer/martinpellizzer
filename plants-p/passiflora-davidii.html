Passiflora davidii

Passiflora davidii, commonly known as the David's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a vibrant blend of red, blue, and yellow hues. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients and have a tangy flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, velvety hairs.

Passiflora davidii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported calming effects.
