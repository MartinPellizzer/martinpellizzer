Phyllanthus dumbeaensis

Phyllanthus dumbeaensis, commonly known as the African date palm, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine. Its fruit, similar to a date, is edible and can be consumed raw or cooked.

Phyllanthus dumbeaensis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.
