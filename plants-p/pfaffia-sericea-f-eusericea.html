Pfaffia sericea f. eusericea

Pfaffia sericea f. eusericea, commonly known as the silky ginseng, is a rare and lesser-known variant of the Pfaffia genus.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny white flowers followed by small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It is native to specific regions of South America, particularly in the Andes, where it thrives in high-altitude, humid environments.

Due to its unique properties and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable but under-researched species in botanical studies.
