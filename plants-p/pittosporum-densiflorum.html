Pittosporum densiflorum

Pittosporum densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has smooth, greyish bark and produces small, black berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Pittosporum densiflorum is also valued for its ability to provide habitat and food for native wildlife.
