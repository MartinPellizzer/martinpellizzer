Prunus nana var. typica

Prunus nana var. typica, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or dwarf Japanese cherry, is a small deciduous shrub or tree native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 2 to 5 meters, featuring slender, upright branches and dense foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by bright red or dark purple berries. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in autumn.

Prunus nana var. typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal beauty.
