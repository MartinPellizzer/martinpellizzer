Phoebe tomentosa

Phoebe tomentosa, commonly known as the southern cowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries that persist into winter. Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and adaptable plant suitable for various landscaping purposes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
