Pseudanthus chryseus

Pseudanthus chryseus, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in dense, cup-shaped clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.

The golden cupplant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
