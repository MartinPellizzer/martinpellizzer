Paeonia × litvinskajae

Paeonia × litvinskajae, commonly known as the Litvinskaja peony, is a hybrid peony species originating from crosses between Paeonia officinalis and Paeonia lactiflora.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with intricate patterns and a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant is a herbaceous perennial, featuring palmately divided leaves and a short, thick rhizome. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its popularity among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
