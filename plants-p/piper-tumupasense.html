Piper tumupasense

Piper tumupasense, commonly known as the Tumupasense pepper vine, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, fleshy berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
