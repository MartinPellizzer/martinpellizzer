Persica narbonensis

Persica narbonensis, commonly known as the Narbonese peach, is a fruit-bearing plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other peach species. The plant produces a sweet, juicy fruit with a fuzzy skin and a yellow or white flesh. Its flowers are typically pink or white and bloom in the spring.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for both its fruit and aesthetic appeal.
