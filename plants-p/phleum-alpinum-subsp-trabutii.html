Phleum alpinum subsp. trabutii

Phleum alpinum subsp.

trabutii, commonly known as the alpine fescue, is a perennial grass species native to high-altitude regions of the European Alps. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

This subspecies is adapted to cold, rocky environments and thrives in well-drained soils. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals.

The plant is often found in alpine meadows and mountain pastures at elevations above 1,500 meters.
