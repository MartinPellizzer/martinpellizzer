Polygonum ajanense subsp. middendorfii

Polygonum ajanense subsp.

middendorfii, commonly known as the Middle Dorf polygon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.

The plant features slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting local biodiversity.
