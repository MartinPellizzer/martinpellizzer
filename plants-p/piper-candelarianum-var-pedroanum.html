Piper candelarianum var. pedroanum

Piper candelarianum var. pedroanum, commonly known as Pedro's pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with elongated stems that often trail along the ground or climb supports. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a glossy surface and a strong, peppery aroma.

The flowers are small and greenish-white, clustered in spikes, and the plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.
