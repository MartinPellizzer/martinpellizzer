Plantago atrata subsp. circassica

Plantago atrata subsp.

circassica, commonly known as the Caucasian plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It features a rosette of broad, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces upright flowering stems with dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Plantaginaceae family.
