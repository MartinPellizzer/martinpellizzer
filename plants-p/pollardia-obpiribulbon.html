Pollardia obpiribulbon

Pollardia obpiribulbon, commonly known as the golden spire orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated inflorescence that resembles a spire, with clusters of golden-yellow flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette at the base.

Pollardia obpiribulbon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
