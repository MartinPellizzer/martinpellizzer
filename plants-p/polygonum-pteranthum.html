Polygonum pteranthum

Polygonum pteranthum, commonly known as the red-rooted sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and distinctive, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its bright red flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Polygonum pteranthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
