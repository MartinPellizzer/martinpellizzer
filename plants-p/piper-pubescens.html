Piper pubescens

Piper pubescens, commonly known as the hairy pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by bright red berries that are edible.

Piper pubescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of mild spices.
