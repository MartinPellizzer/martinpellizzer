Pulsatilla albana var. caerulea

Pulsatilla albana var. caerulea, commonly known as the blue pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range from pale blue to deep blue in color. The plant produces a distinctive crown of silky, white to pale blue bracts beneath the flower, which are often mistaken for petals. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in a dense layer of soft, white hairs.

This species is adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils at high altitudes.
