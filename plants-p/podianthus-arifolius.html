Podianthus arifolius

Podianthus arifolius, commonly known as the silky podianthus, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a silky texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. These flowers are followed by small, woody pods that contain seeds.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
