Planchonella pohlmaniana var. asterocarpon

Planchonella pohlmaniana var. asterocarpon, commonly known as the red-fruited hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its most distinctive feature is the bright red or orange berries that develop from the flowers.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
