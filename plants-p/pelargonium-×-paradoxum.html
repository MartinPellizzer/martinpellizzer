Pelargonium × paradoxum

Pelargonium × paradoxum, commonly known as the scented geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant leaves, which emit a wide range of scents such as citrus, mint, and rose. The plant typically has rounded or lobed leaves and produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. It is native to South Africa and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Pelargonium × paradoxum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and indoor plant collections.
