Physaria hitchcockii subsp. rubicundula

Physaria hitchcockii subsp.

rubicundula, commonly known as the redstem bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, showy flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are linear to lance-shaped, and the stems are often reddish in color, which gives it its common name.

It produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
