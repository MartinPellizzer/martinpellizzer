Phyllopodium rudolphii

Phyllopodium rudolphii, commonly known as the African velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, often greenish-white in color, and it produces capsule fruits containing several seeds.

Phyllopodium rudolphii is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
