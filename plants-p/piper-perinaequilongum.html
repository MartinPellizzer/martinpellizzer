Piper perinaequilongum

Piper perinaequilongum, commonly known as the longleaf pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by berries that mature to a dark purple color. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture and emit a mild peppery aroma.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
