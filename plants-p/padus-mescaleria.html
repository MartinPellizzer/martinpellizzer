Padus mescaleria

Padus mescaleria, commonly known as the Mescalero hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some small mammals.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
