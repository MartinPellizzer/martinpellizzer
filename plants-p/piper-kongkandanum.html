Piper kongkandanum

Piper kongkandanum, commonly known as the Malabar pepper or Kottan pepper, is a flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the wet evergreen forests of the Western Ghats in India and is characterized by its aromatic, elongated fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while the flowers are small and greenish-white.

The fruit, which is the part used as a spice, develops in clusters and is harvested when fully mature.
