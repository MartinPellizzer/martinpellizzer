Pernettya mucronata var. angustifolia

Pernettya mucronata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cowberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly pointed. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in early summer, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats.

This variety is notable for its hardy nature and ability to survive in extreme climates.
