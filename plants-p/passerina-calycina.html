Passerina calycina

Passerina calycina, commonly known as the golden cupflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features heart-shaped, hairy leaves. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important plant in native gardens.

Passerina calycina thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.
