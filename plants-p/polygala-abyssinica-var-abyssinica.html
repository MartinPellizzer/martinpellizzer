Polygala abyssinica var. abyssinica

Polygala abyssinica var. abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features slender stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, which helps reduce water loss in dry climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
