Potamogeton polygonifolius var. terrestris

Potamogeton polygonifolius var. terrestris, commonly known as the terrestrial pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments.

It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in shallow, nutrient-rich waters and is often found in marshes and slow-moving streams.

Its presence can indicate good water quality and is important for aquatic ecosystems.
