Palanostigma crotonoides

Palanostigma crotonoides, commonly known as the African croton, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is adapted to dry and seasonal climates, making it a resilient species in arid environments.

Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
