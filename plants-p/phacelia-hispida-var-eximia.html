Phacelia hispida var. eximia

Phacelia hispida var. eximia, commonly known as the hairy phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Phacelia hispida var. eximia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
