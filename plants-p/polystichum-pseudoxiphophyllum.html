Polystichum pseudoxiphophyllum

Polystichum pseudoxiphophyllum, commonly known as the false sword fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, lance-shaped fronds that are often deeply divided. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its rhizomes are thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

The spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in forest ecosystems.
