Phoebe smithii

Phoebe smithii, commonly known as the Smith's phoebe, is a member of the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the Caribbean. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, while the fruit is a small, spherical drupe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
