Pistacia lentiscus var. latifolia

Pistacia lentiscus var. latifolia, commonly known as sweet box, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically eaten by birds. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often with a distinctive scent when crushed.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in rocky or dry soils.
