Peperomia guadaloupensis

Peperomia guadaloupensis, commonly known as the Guadaloupe peperomia, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, succulent-like foliage. The plant typically features dark green, glossy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish in color.

Peperomia guadaloupensis is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in moderate light conditions.
