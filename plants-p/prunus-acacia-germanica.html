Prunus acacia-germanica

Prunus acacia-germanica, commonly known as the German plum or European blackthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and small, dark purple berries that ripen in late summer. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

The bark is typically gray to brown and becomes fissured with age.
