Phinaea macrophylla

Phinaea macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf phinaea, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
