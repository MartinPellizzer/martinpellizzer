Panopsis rubescens

Panopsis rubescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed panic grass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
