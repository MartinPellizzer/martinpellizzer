Pothos cumingianus

Pothos cumingianus, commonly known as the Cuming's devil's ivy, is a trailing evergreen vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically variegated with shades of white and yellow. The plant is known for its vigorous growth and ability to thrive in low-light conditions. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red berries, though these are not commonly seen in cultivation.

Pothos cumingianus is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
