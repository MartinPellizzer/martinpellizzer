Plantago major var. pubescens

Plantago major var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy plantain, is a variant of the common plantain characterized by its pubescent (hairy) leaves.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, often found in disturbed soils and along pathways. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, which give the plant its distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes, which are typically wind-pollinated.

This plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for its soothing and healing effects.
