Prunus spinosa var. microcarpa

Prunus spinosa var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf blackthorn, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and small, dark blue to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has small, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive silvery-white underside. It typically grows in hedgerows, woodlands, and rocky soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

The berries are often used in making jams and wines, and the plant has historical uses in traditional medicine.
