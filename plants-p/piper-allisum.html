Piper allisum

Piper allisum, commonly known as wild betel, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other species such as the common betel plant. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and for chewing, similar to the betel nut.
