Platanus densicoma

Platanus densicoma, commonly known as the dense-leaved sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety foliage and its distinctive bark that peels in papery layers. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical forest environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
