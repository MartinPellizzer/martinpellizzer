Picris asplenioides subsp. asplenioides

Picris asplenioides subsp.

asplenioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along streambanks.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
