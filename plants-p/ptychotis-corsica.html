Ptychotis corsica

Ptychotis corsica, commonly known as the Corsican rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often blooming in spring. Its leaves are usually small, leathery, and covered in fine hairs.

Ptychotis corsica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
