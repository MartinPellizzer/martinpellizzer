Pimelea humilis var. myrtifolia

Pimelea humilis var. myrtifolia, commonly known as the coastal bottlebrush, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Australia.

It features dense, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically red or pink, resembling a bottlebrush in appearance. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. It thrives in sandy or coastal soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh coastal conditions.
