Poa glauca var. laxiuscula

Poa glauca var. laxiuscula, commonly known as the bluegrass meadow grass, is a variety of the bluegrass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and glaucous blue-green foliage. This grass thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and meadows. Its compact inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and fine texture.
