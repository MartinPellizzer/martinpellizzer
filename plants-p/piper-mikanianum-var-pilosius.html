Piper mikanianum var. pilosius

Piper mikanianum var. pilosius, commonly known as the hairy sarsaparilla, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and partial shade.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and pharmacological study.
