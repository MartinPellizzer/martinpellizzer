Patagonium oxalidiphyllum

Patagonium oxalidiphyllum, commonly known as the Patagonian oxalid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant has small, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is an important species in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
