Pleurothallis henrique-aragonii

Pleurothallis henrique-aragonii, commonly known as the Henry Aragon orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant typically features small, green to reddish-brown pseudobulbs and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and rarity, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
