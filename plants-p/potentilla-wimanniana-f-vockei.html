Potentilla wimanniana f. vockei

Potentilla wimanniana f. vockei, commonly known as the Vocke's potentilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, rounded leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early summer. It is native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and is adapted to harsh, cold environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
