Pyrus meliosmifolia

Pyrus meliosmifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Pyrus meliosmifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in suitable climates.
