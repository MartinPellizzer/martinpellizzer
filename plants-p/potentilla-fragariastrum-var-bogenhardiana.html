Potentilla fragariastrum var. bogenhardiana

Potentilla fragariastrum var. bogenhardiana, commonly known as the Bogenhard's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has wiry, branching stems covered with fine, soft hairs. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets that are slightly serrated.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
