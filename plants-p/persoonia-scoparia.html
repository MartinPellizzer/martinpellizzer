Persoonia scoparia

Persoonia scoparia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf geebung, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in heathlands and open forests, characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.

Persoonia scoparia is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal display.
