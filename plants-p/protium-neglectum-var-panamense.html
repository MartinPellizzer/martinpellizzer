Protium neglectum var. panamense

Protium neglectum var. panamense, commonly known as the Panamanian tamarind.

This tropical tree is native to Central America, particularly Panama, and thrives in humid, lowland environments. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.

It is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks, showcasing its adaptability to various soil types.
