Pitcairnia virginalis

Pitcairnia virginalis, commonly known as the Virgin's Tears, is a bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with vibrant, showy flowers that attract pollinators. Its foliage is often covered in fine, hairy structures that aid in water retention.

It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.
