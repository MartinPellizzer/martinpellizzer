Pulsatilla jankae var. bulgarica

Pulsatilla jankae var. bulgarica, commonly known as the Balkan pasqueflower, is a perennial herb belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and hairy, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. It thrives in meadows and rocky soils, preferring well-drained, alkaline conditions.

This variety is native to the Balkan Peninsula and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
