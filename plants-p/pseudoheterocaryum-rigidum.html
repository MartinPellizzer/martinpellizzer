Pseudoheterocaryum rigidum

Pseudoheterocaryum rigidum, commonly known as the rigid heterocaryum, is a rare and enigmatic fungal plant that exhibits unique morphological characteristics.

It is typically found in moist, shaded environments, often associated with decaying organic matter. The plant's structure consists of dense, fibrous filaments that form a compact, sponge-like mass. Its growth is slow and it is often difficult to distinguish from other similar species without microscopic examination.

Due to its elusive nature, it remains a subject of ongoing botanical study and classification.
