Potentilla tabernaemontani var. hirsuta

Potentilla tabernaemontani var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its botanical name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
