Phthirusa cothurnata

Phthirusa cothurnata, commonly known as the red-tailed grasshopper, is a species of grasshopper found in various regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Acrididae and is characterized by its distinctive red-tailed coloration. The plant it interacts with is primarily grasses and herbs, which form its main diet. Its nymphs undergo gradual metamorphosis, shedding their exoskeletons multiple times as they grow.

The species plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat by contributing to nutrient cycling through its feeding habits.
