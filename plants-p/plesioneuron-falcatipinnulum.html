Plesioneuron falcatipinnulum

Plesioneuron falcatipinnulum, commonly known as the narrow-pinnate water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and rice fields. The plant has delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically submerged or floating. Its rhizomes spread along the bottom of the water, anchoring the plant and facilitating nutrient absorption.

The leaves are thin and elongated, with a distinctive pinna arrangement that aids in photosynthesis in aquatic conditions.
