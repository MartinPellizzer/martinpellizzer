Piper betle var. marianum

Piper betle var. marianum, commonly known as the sacred betel leaf, is a variant of the betel pepper plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional rituals and as a chewing leaf. The plant belongs to the Piperaceae family and is cultivated for its aromatic leaves rather than its fruit. Its leaves contain essential oils and alkaloids, contributing to its cultural and medicinal significance.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and requires high humidity and well-drained soil for optimal growth.
