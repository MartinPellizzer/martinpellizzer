Psidium microcarpum

Psidium microcarpum, commonly known as the small-fruited guava, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, followed by small, round fruits that are typically less than 2 centimeters in diameter. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.
