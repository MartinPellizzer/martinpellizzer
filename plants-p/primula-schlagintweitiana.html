Primula schlagintweitiana

Primula schlagintweitiana, commonly known as the Schlagintweit's primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant features a rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
