Poterium alveolosum var. vestitum

Poterium alveolosum var. vestitum, commonly known as the velvetleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a dense, velvety pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical structure makes it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
