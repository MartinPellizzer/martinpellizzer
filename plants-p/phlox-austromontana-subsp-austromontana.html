Phlox austromontana subsp. austromontana

Phlox austromontana subsp.

austromontana, commonly known as the Australian phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southeastern Australia. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in open woodlands or heathlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
