Psidium cattleyanum

Psidium cattleyanum, commonly known as the Natal plum or Brazilian cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The tree is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
