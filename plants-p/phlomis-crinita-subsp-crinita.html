Phlomis crinita subsp. crinita

Phlomis crinita subsp.

crinita, commonly known as the curlyhead phlomis, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy, pink to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in full sun. Its slender, branching stems are covered in fine, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
