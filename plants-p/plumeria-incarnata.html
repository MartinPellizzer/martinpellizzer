Plumeria incarnata

Plumeria incarnata, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical flowering plant native to the Caribbean, Central America, and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, waxy flowers that come in shades of white, yellow, and pink, often with a distinctive red or orange center. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its showy blooms. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its beauty and fragrance.

The flowers are also used in traditional ceremonies and can be dried for use in leis and floral arrangements.
