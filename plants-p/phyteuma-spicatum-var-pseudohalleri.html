Phyteuma spicatum var. pseudohalleri

Phyteuma spicatum var. pseudohalleri, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized areas.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
