Platytaenia lasiocarpa

Platytaenia lasiocarpa, commonly known as the woolly bark fern, is a rare and unique plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, or woolly appearance on its stems and leaves, which gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in mountainous or forested areas. Its leaves are usually elongated and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
