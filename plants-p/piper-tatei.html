Piper tatei

Piper tatei, commonly known as the Tate's pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters, and it produces small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
