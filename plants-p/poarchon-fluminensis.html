Poarchon fluminensis

Poarchon fluminensis, commonly known as the river orchid, is a rare flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and requires a humid environment to flourish. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often forming dense mats along the water's edge.

Due to habitat loss, Poarchon fluminensis is considered a vulnerable species, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
