Plectranthus kameba var. excisinflexus

Plectranthus kameba var. excisinflexus, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in a fine layer of hair. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
