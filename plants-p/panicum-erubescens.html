Panicum erubescens

Panicum erubescens, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, fluffy panicles that turn a striking red or pink color as they mature. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open fields and disturbed habitats. Its fibrous roots help prevent soil erosion, and it is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its vibrant fall color.

The seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas rapidly.
