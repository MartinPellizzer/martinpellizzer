Phyteuma spicatum var. pyrenaicum

Phyteuma spicatum var. pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine and subalpine environments. The plant features showy, clustered inflorescences of violet or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in mountainous habitats.
