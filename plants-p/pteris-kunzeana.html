Pteris kunzeana

Pteris kunzeana, commonly known as the crested water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed fronds that often form a rosette shape. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically near streams or in forest understories. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Pteris kunzeana is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
