Protea fusciflora

Protea fusciflora, commonly known as the dark-flowered protea, is a striking South African species belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous pale to dark red bracts that enclose small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun exposure. Its unique floral structure serves as a pollinator attractant, particularly for birds and insects.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
