Pritchardia filifera

Pritchardia filifera, commonly known as the feather palm, is a species of cycad native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of long, pinnately compound leaves that resemble feathers. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, making it a long-lived species. Its leaves are often used in traditional Hawaiian crafts and ceremonies.

Pritchardia filifera plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various native animals.
