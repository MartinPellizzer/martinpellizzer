Prunella grandiflora

Prunella grandiflora, commonly known as self-heal, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and range in color from blue to purple, attracting various pollinators.

Prunella grandiflora is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
