Puccinellia pamirica subsp. vachanica

Puccinellia pamirica subsp.

vachanica, commonly known as the Pamir grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a significant role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for forage in regions with similar climatic conditions.
