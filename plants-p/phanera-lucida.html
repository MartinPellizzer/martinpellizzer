Phanera lucida

Phanera lucida, commonly known as the shining phanera, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy leaves that reflect light, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique leaf structure and its role in local ecosystems.
