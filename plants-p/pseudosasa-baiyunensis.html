Pseudosasa baiyunensis

Pseudosasa baiyunensis, commonly known as the Baiyun bamboo, is a species of grass-like bamboo native to China.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as mountainous regions and forest understories. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.

The plant's botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types, though it thrives best in well-drained, loamy soils.
