Polygonum nodosum var. incanum

Polygonum nodosum var. incanum, commonly known as the silver knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a waxy texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
