Ptilotus crosslandii

Ptilotus crosslandii, commonly known as the pink ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pink to purple bracts that resemble ghostly shapes, hence its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically surrounded by the showy bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Ptilotus crosslandii is often found in open habitats such as sandplains and rocky outcrops.
