Philodendron paxianum

Philodendron paxianum, commonly known as the silver-veined philodendron, is a climbing plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a striking silver-veined pattern that gives it its common name. The plant has a slender, vine-like stem that can grow up to several meters in length. Its leaves are typically a deep green with a waxy texture, and they often have a slightly glossy appearance.

Philodendron paxianum thrives in humid, warm environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.
