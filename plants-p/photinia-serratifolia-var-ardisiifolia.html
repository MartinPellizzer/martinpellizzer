Photinia serratifolia var. ardisiifolia

Photinia serratifolia var. ardisiifolia, commonly known as the redleaf photinia, is a cultivar of the photinia plant native to China.

It is characterized by its striking, glossy, deep red foliage that turns bronze in winter, making it a popular ornamental shrub. The plant has simple, serrated leaves and produces small, white, clustered flowers in spring. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its vibrant color and compact growth habit.
