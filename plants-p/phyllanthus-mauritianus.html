Phyllanthus mauritianus

Phyllanthus mauritianus, commonly known as the Mauritius gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to the Mascarene Islands.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and limestone soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are edible and have a tangy flavor. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with a distinct aroma.

Phyllanthus mauritianus plays an important role in the local ecosystem, providing food for various wildlife species.
