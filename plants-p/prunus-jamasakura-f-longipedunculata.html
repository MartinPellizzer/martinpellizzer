Prunus jamasakura f. longipedunculata

Prunus jamasakura f. longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-peduncled Japanese cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus jamasakura species.

It is characterized by its elongated peduncles, which are the stalks supporting the flower clusters. The plant produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a striking visual effect. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 5 meters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and parks for its seasonal beauty.
