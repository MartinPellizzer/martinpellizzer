Paspalum ischnocaulon

Paspalum ischnocaulon, commonly known as the narrowleaf buffalograss, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Paspalum ischnocaulon is valued for its drought tolerance and is often used in restoration ecology and forage production.
