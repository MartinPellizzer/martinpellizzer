Plantago australis subsp. pflanzii

Plantago australis subsp.

pflanzii, commonly known as the Australian plantain, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia. It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along watercourses.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
