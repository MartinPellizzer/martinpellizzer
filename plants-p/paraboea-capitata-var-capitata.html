Paraboea capitata var. capitata

Paraboea capitata var. capitata, commonly known as the mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty.

The flowers are typically pollinated by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
