Premna integrifolia var. obtusifolia

Premna integrifolia var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed morning glory, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, smooth, and often broad leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale blue, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its leaves and stems are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
