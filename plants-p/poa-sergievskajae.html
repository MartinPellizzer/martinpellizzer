Poa sergievskajae

Poa sergievskajae, commonly known as the Sergeyevskaya bluegrass, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of the Russian Far East.

This grass is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, dark green blades. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Poa sergievskajae is considered a botanical curiosity and a subject of scientific interest.
