Panicum volutans

Panicum volutans, commonly known as the curlleaf panicum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its distinctive rolled or curled leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are typically green or pale yellow. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Panicum volutans is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
