Passiflora franciscoi

Passiflora franciscoi, commonly known as the Francisco passionflower, is a species native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica and Panama.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant's flowers are typically white or pale blue with a central red or orange receptacle. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Passiflora franciscoi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
