Pisum elatius

Pisum elatius, commonly known as the tall pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many legumes known for their nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its pods contain several seeds, which are typically used as a food source.

Pisum elatius is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
