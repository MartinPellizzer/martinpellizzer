Polygonum douglasii subsp. spergulariiforme

Polygonum douglasii subsp.

spergulariiforme, commonly known as the Douglas's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and often triangular stems.

The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along streams and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a prominent midrib and a waxy surface.

It produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers in late spring to early summer.
