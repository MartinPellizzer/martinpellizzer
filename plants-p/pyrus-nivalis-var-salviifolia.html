Pyrus nivalis var. salviifolia

Pyrus nivalis var. salviifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf pear, is a variant of the European pear species characterized by its distinctive silvery-white leaf undersides.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a moderate height, with a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. The leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a pointed tip and a serrated margin. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible pomes that ripen in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
