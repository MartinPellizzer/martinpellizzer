Piper lagunaense

Piper lagunaense, commonly known as the Laguna pepper or Lagunense pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in humid lowland areas. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by small, berry-like fruits that contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
