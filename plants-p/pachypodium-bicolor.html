Pachypodium bicolor

Pachypodium bicolor, commonly known as the Madagascar moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and sparse, spiny leaves. The plant has a unique appearance with its swollen, bottle-shaped trunk and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of yellow or orange. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ornamental gardening.
