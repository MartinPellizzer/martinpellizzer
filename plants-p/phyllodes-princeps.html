Phyllodes princeps

Phyllodes princeps, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its flat, leaf-like structures called phyllodes, which are modified stems that perform the function of leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in spring. Its phyllodes are covered in a fine, silvery hair that gives it a distinctive appearance.

Phyllodes princeps is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
