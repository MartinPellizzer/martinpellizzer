Pyrenacantha capitata

Pyrenacantha capitata, commonly known as the Cape rose.

It is a flowering plant native to South Africa, characterized by its attractive, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a shrub-like appearance with woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically pink or white, and they are borne on terminal panicles.

Pyrenacantha capitata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
