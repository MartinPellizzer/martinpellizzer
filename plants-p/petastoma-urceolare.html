Petastoma urceolare

Petastoma urceolare, commonly known as the urn-shaped milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, urn-shaped seed pods that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Petastoma urceolare is a member of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is known for its milky sap, which is toxic to some animals.
