Philodendron scandens

Philodendron scandens, commonly known as the creeping philodendron, is a tropical vine native to the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically bright green with a slightly variegated appearance. The plant grows by climbing or trailing along surfaces, using its aerial roots to anchor itself. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a versatile plant for hanging baskets or shelves.
