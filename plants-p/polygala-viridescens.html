Polygala viridescens

Polygala viridescens, commonly known as the green-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grassland and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features slender, erect stems that bear small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Polygala viridescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
