Phyllanthus schomburgkianus var. antillanus

Phyllanthus schomburgkianus var. antillanus, commonly known as the Antillean spurge, is a shrub native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptic or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.
