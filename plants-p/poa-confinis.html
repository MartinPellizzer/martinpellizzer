Poa confinis

Poa confinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf poa, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and is typically found in high-altitude environments. The plant has a clumping growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It is adapted to cold and arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Poa confinis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its ecological niche and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
