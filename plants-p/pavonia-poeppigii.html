Pavonia poeppigii

Pavonia poeppigii, commonly known as the redroot pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to well-drained soils.

Pavonia poeppigii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
