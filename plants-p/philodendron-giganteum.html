Philodendron giganteum

Philodendron giganteum, commonly known as the giant philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length. The plant has a vigorous growth habit and can reach heights of over 20 feet in its natural habitat. Its stems are typically green or reddish and often have a waxy coating.

Philodendron giganteum thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil for optimal growth.
