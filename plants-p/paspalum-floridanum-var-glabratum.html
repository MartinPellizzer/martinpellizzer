Paspalum floridanum var. glabratum

Paspalum floridanum var. glabratum, commonly known as Florida grass or flatleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
