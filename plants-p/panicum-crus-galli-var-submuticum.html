Panicum crus-galli var. submuticum

Panicum crus-galli var. submuticum, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, often branched stems and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fluffy, pinkish to reddish inflorescences that are highly visible and attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, wetlands, and along waterways.

Its fibrous roots and drought tolerance make it a resilient plant in various ecological settings.
