Pleioblastus diversus

Pleioblastus diversus, commonly known as the Japanese forest grass, is a clumping bamboo species native to East Asia.

It features slender, arching culms that are often covered with a whitish bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in panicles during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a soft, grass-like texture.

Pleioblastus diversus is well-suited for ornamental use in gardens and is valued for its graceful form and adaptability to various growing conditions.
