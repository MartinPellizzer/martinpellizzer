Pseudopogonatherum setifolium

Pseudopogonatherum setifolium, commonly known as the silky beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and long, narrow leaves with a soft, silky texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks.

Its fibrous roots and rhizomes contribute to soil stabilization, making it ecologically significant in wetland ecosystems.
