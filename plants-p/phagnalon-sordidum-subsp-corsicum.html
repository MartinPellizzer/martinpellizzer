Phagnalon sordidum subsp. corsicum

Phagnalon sordidum subsp.

corsicum, commonly known as Corsican phagnalon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, rocky, or calcareous soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant has wiry stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in dense heads, and are typically pale yellow to brown in color.

It is part of the Asteraceae family and is often found in open habitats such as steppes and coastal areas.
