Pelargonium × jatrophifolium

Pelargonium × jatrophifolium, commonly known as the giant geranium, is a hybrid plant characterized by its large, palmate leaves that resemble those of the jatropha plant.

It is a member of the Geraniaceae family and is native to South Africa. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with showy flowers that range in color from pink to red.

Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
