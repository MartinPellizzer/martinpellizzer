Polypodium flexile

Polypodium flexile, commonly known as the common huperzia, is a fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is a terrestrial plant characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and dense, dark green foliage. The fronds are typically strap-like and have a leathery texture, with a prominent midrib and serrated margins. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

This plant is known for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and is often found in temperate regions.
