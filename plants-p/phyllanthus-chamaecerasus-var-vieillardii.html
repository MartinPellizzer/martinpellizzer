Phyllanthus chamaecerasus var. vieillardii

Phyllanthus chamaecerasus var. vieillardii, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged opposite along the stems. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
