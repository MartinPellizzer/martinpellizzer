Pterygocarpus

Pterygocarpus, commonly known as ironwood, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plants typically have compound leaves and produce small, pea-like flowers. The genus includes several species, such as Pterygocarpus indicus and Pterygocarpus marsupium, which are native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

These trees often grow in moist, shaded environments and are known for their ecological and medicinal significance.
