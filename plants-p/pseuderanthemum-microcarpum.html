Pseuderanthemum microcarpum

Pseuderanthemum microcarpum, commonly known as the small-fruited pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, often hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Pseuderanthemum microcarpum is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.
