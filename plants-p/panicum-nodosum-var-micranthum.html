Panicum nodosum var. micranthum

Panicum nodosum var. micranthum, commonly known as little bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. The plant has a tufted growth habit with slender, blue-green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, including erosion control and wildlife habitat.
