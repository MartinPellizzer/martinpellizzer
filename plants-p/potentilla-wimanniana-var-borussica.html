Potentilla wimanniana var. borussica

Potentilla wimanniana var. borussica, commonly known as the Siberian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters of five. The plant has compound leaves with five oval leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils. It is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.

This variety is particularly adapted to harsh environments, making it a resilient choice for alpine or subarctic gardens.
