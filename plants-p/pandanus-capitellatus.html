Pandanus capitellatus

Pandanus capitellatus, commonly known as the bottlebrush pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble bottlebrushes. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy texture.

Pandanus capitellatus is often used in traditional crafts and for its ornamental value in landscaping.
