Pimelea sericostachya

Pimelea sericostachya, commonly known as the silky-stemmed everlasting or silky pimelea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in heathlands and open forests, characterized by its narrow, evergreen leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces fluffy, white seed heads that are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature.

It is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in regions with well-drained soils.
