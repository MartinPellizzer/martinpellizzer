Periploca laeta

Periploca laeta, commonly known as the white periploca, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form with aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a distinctive scent.

Periploca laeta is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to coastal environments.
