Ptilotus unguiculatus

Ptilotus unguiculatus, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its unique, spiky, pink or purple flowers that resemble tiny ghosts. The plant is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy soils. It has a short growing season and is often seen blooming during the cooler months.

The ghost flower is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used by Indigenous Australians for various purposes.
