Pteris sotae

Pteris sotae, commonly known as the Sotao water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread along the surface of water bodies. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are typically arranged in a single row along the margins of the fronds.

Pteris sotae plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water filtration.
