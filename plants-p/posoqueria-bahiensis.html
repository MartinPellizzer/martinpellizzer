Posoqueria bahiensis

Posoqueria bahiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian grass or *P. bahiensis*, is a perennial grass species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats, such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are small, flowering units.

Posoqueria bahiensis is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
