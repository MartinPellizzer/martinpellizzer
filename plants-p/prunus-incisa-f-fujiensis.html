Prunus incisa f. fujiensis

Prunus incisa f. fujiensis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum or Fuji cherry plum, is a cultivar of the Prunus incisa species, which belongs to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, red or dark purple fruits. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowering display.

This variety is well-suited to temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
