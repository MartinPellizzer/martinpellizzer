Piper lateripilosum

Piper lateripilosum, commonly known as the hairy pipevine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of flowering vines and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It is often found in humid, tropical environments, thriving in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

The plant's name refers to the fine, hairy texture of its stems and leaves.
