Peristylus brevicalcar

Peristylus brevicalcar, commonly known as the short-spurred peristylus, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stem and a single, showy flower. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, and the flower has a distinctive spur that is shorter than the lip.

Peristylus brevicalcar is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
