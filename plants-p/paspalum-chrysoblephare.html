Paspalum chrysoblephare

Paspalum chrysoblephare, commonly known as the golden-eyed grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, yellowish to golden spikelets that give it its common name. The plant typically thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and swampy areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it has a fibrous root system.

Paspalum chrysoblephare is often found in wetland ecosystems, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
