Paspalum brunneum

Paspalum brunneum, commonly known as brown paspalum, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a dull green to brownish color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as pastures, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Paspalum brunneum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
