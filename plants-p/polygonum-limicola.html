Polygonum limicola

Polygonum limicola, commonly known as sea blite, is a halophyte plant that thrives in saline and coastal environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, often fleshy leaves that help it retain water in arid conditions. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its root system is adapted to tolerate high salt concentrations in the soil.

Polygonum limicola plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
