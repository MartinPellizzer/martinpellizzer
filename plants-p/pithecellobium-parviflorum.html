Pithecellobium parviflorum

Pithecellobium parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered acacia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry to moist forest understories.
