Polygala coerulea

Polygala coerulea, commonly known as blue milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiral-shaped seed pods that contain several tiny, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

Polygala coerulea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
