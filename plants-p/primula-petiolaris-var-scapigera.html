Primula petiolaris var. scapigera

Primula petiolaris var. scapigera, commonly known as the scapigerous primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its upright, slender stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically elliptical and have a slightly hairy surface, while the flowers are usually pink or white in color. This variant is distinguished by its elongated leaf stalks and the way its flowers are borne on slender, leafless stems.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
