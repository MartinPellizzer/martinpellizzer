Payena asiatica

Payena asiatica, commonly known as the African cherry or African mahogany, is a tropical evergreen tree native to western and central Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark reddish-brown wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptic to ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red berries containing several seeds.

Payena asiatica is also noted for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.
