Passiflora morifolia

Passiflora morifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that feature a unique blend of colors and intricate patterns. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits known as passionfruits, which are rich in nutrients and flavor. Its leaves are palmate, with five to seven lobes, and the stems are covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora morifolia thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
