Pachyrhizus strigosus

Pachyrhizus strigosus, commonly known as the hairy peanut vine, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds similar to peanuts. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its nutritional value, it is cultivated in some regions for both food and forage.
