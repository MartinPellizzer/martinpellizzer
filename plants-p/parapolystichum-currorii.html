Parapolystichum currorii

Parapolystichum currorii, commonly known as the Curror's sword fern, is a rare and distinctive fern species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polystichaceae and is characterized by its elongated, sword-like fronds that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories where it can receive indirect sunlight. Its fronds are bioluminescent, a unique trait that aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators.

This botanical feature makes it a subject of interest for both horticultural and scientific research.
