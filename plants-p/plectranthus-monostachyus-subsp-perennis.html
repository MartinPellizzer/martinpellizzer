Plectranthus monostachyus subsp. perennis

Plectranthus monostachyus subsp.

perennis, commonly known as the African violet, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of violet, pink, and white, typically in clusters at the ends of branches. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

This species is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
