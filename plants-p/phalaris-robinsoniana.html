Phalaris robinsoniana

Phalaris robinsoniana, commonly known as the Robinson's beardgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and typically grows in wetland or marshy environments. The plant produces tall, slender spikes of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Phalaris robinsoniana is often found in association with other wetland plants, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
