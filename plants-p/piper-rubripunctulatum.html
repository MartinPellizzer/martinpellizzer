Piper rubripunctulatum

Piper rubripunctulatum, commonly known as the red-spotted pepper plant, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bright red berries that mature in the summer. Its berries contain a spicy compound similar to capsaicin, though it is less intense than that of chili peppers.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has traditional uses in local medicine.
