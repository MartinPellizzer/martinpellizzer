Prunus cerasus f. aureovariegata

Prunus cerasus f. aureovariegata, commonly known as the golden variegated cherry, is a cultivar of the sweet cherry tree.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display a mix of white, yellow, and green patterns. The plant produces small, sweet, and juicy fruits similar to other cherry varieties. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This ornamental variety is valued both for its aesthetic appeal and its edible fruit.
