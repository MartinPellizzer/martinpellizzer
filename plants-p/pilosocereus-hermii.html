Pilosocereus hermii

Pilosocereus hermii, commonly known as the Hermi's cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, columnar stems that often branch out. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert conditions.

Its structure and growth habits make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and cactus enthusiasts.
