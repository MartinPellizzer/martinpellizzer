Papaver alpinum var. leucanthum

Papaver alpinum var. leucanthum, commonly known as the white Himalayan poppy, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking white petals that contrast with its green, hairy leaves. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
