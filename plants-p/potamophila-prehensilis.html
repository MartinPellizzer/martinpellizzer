Potamophila prehensilis

Potamophila prehensilis, commonly known as the water-holding fern, is a unique aquatic plant that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its intricate fronds that can store water, adapting to fluctuating water levels. The plant's rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it in muddy substrates. Its reproductive structures are concealed within specialized leaf bases, contributing to its survival in submerged conditions.

Potamophila prehensilis plays a vital role in maintaining water quality and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
