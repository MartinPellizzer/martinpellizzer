Platydesma campanulatum f. coriaceum

Platydesma campanulatum f. coriaceum, commonly known as the coriaceous bellflower, is a variant of the bellflower species characterized by its thick, leathery leaves.

This plant belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is native to regions with temperate climates. Its flowers are typically bell-shaped, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from blue to purple. The coriaceum form is notable for its enhanced leaf texture, which provides structural support and reduces water loss.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a hardy plant in horticultural collections.
