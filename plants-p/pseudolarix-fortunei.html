Pseudolarix fortunei

Pseudolarix fortunei, commonly known as the golden chincherinchee, is a deciduous conifer native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery, golden-yellow foliage that turns reddish-brown in autumn. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds dispersed by wind. Its leaves are needle-like and arranged in whorls around the stem.

Pseudolarix fortunei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
