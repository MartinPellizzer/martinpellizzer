Passiflora cisnana

Passiflora cisnana, commonly known as the Cisna passionflower, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in vitamin C and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and climbing.

Passiflora cisnana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
