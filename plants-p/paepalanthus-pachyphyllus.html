Paepalanthus pachyphyllus

Paepalanthus pachyphyllus, commonly known as the thick-leafed puya, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and vibrant, showy inflorescences. The plant features a rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to its dry environment. Its flowers, often brightly colored, attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Paepalanthus pachyphyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
