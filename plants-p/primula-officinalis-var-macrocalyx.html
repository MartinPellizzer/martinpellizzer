Primula officinalis var. macrocalyx

Primula officinalis var. macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and it produces a flowering stem that can reach up to 30 cm in height. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has a long history of use in traditional herbal medicine.
