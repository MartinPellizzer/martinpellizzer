Psathyrostachys juncea var. desertorum

Psathyrostachys juncea var. desertorum, commonly known as desert wild rye, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and narrow, blue-green leaves that are adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to greenish flowers, which appear in early spring. It is well-suited for erosion control and is often used in restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soils.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil and supports biodiversity in its native habitat.
