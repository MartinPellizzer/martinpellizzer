Phaseolus zembra

Phaseolus zembra, commonly known as the Zembra bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It produces small, edible pods that contain round, yellow seeds used in various culinary applications. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate structures and produces small, white or pale purple flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable crop for both nutrition and agricultural diversity.
