Persoonia virgata var. linearis

Persoonia virgata var. linearis, commonly known as the slender swamp bottlebrush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and striking red or pink inflorescences that resemble bottlebrushes. The plant thrives in wetland or swampy environments, often growing in poorly drained soils. Its flowers are pollinated by birds, particularly honeyeaters, and the plant produces small, woody fruits.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize waterlogged soils.
