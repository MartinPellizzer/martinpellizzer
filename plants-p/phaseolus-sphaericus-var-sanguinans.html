Phaseolus sphaericus var. sanguinans

Phaseolus sphaericus var. sanguinans, commonly known as the blood bean, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spherical seeds that range in color from red to dark purple, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an annual vine with trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers. Its seeds are rich in protein and have been traditionally used in local cuisines.

The botanical features of this species make it a valuable crop for both nutritional and agricultural purposes.
