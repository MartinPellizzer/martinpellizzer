Piper politaereum var. mucronatum

Piper politaereum var. mucronatum, commonly known as the rounded pepper plant, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces tiny, peppery fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include smooth, glossy leaves and a distinct, aromatic scent.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
