Piper amplum

Piper amplum, commonly known as the mountain pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its small, green, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small white flowers. Its fruit is a small, dark purple berry that contains several seeds.

Piper amplum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.
