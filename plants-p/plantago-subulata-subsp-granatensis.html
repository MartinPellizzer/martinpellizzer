Plantago subulata subsp. granatensis

Plantago subulata subsp.

granatensis, commonly known as the granate plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically borne on tall, slender stalks. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
