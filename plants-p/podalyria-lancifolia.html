Podalyria lancifolia

Podalyria lancifolia, commonly known as the lanceleaf podalyria, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Podalyria lancifolia thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.
