Piper tejucense

Piper tejucense, commonly known as the "Mexican pepper plant," is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants like the common black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its small greenish-white flowers are followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local herbal medicine.
