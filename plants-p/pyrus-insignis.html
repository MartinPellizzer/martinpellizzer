Pyrus insignis

Pyrus insignis, commonly known as the Chilean white pear, is a deciduous tree native to southern Chile.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The tree produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly serrated margin.

Pyrus insignis is adapted to temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
