Piper paso-anchoense

Piper paso-anchoense, commonly known as the Paso Ancho pepper plant, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and berries, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
