Protea welwitschii var. melliodora

Protea welwitschii var. melliodora, commonly known as the yellow protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous pale yellow to orange-colored floral bracts. The plant is native to the Eastern Cape and Western Cape provinces, where it thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. Its flowers are adapted for insect pollination, particularly by bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
