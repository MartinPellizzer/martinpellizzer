Potentilla canescens var. polyodonta

Potentilla canescens var. polyodonta, commonly known as the silverleaf potentilla, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
