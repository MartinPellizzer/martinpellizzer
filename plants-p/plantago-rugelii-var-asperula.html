Plantago rugelii var. asperula

Plantago rugelii var. asperula, commonly known as the roughleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and North America.

It features basal rosettes of oval, rough-textured leaves with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes during the summer months. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wetlands.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
