Panicum eminii

Panicum eminii, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a species of grass native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in arid or semi-arid regions where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
