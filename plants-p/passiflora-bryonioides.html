Passiflora bryonioides

Passiflora bryonioides, commonly known as the giant passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that feature intricate patterns of red, yellow, and blue. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces edible, orange-colored berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
