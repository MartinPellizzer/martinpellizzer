Portulaca napiformis

Portulaca napiformis, commonly known as the fatleaf portulaca, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to yellow, typically blooming in summer. It has a compact, sprawling growth habit and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Portulaca napiformis is a low-maintenance plant that is popular in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
