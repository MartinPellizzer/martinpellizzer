Pavonia elegans subsp. stellata

Pavonia elegans subsp.

stellata, commonly known as the star pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that resemble stars, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and streambanks. It has simple, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a rough texture and is often found in the understory of forests. Pavonia elegans subsp.

stellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
