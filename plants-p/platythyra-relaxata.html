Platythyra relaxata

Platythyra relaxata, commonly known as the soft rock cactus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It features a low-growing, sprawling form with fleshy, ribbed stems that store water, adapting to its harsh environment. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers during the warmer months, adding a decorative touch to its appearance. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Platythyra relaxata is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and unique aesthetic.
