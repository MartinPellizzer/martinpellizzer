Pediocactus schmiedickeanus

Pediocactus schmiedickeanus, commonly known as the Schmiedicke's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its globose, ribbed stems that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by edible, red fruits. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil and minimal water.

Pediocactus schmiedickeanus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh conditions.
