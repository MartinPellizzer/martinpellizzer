Pachystoma hirsuta

Pachystoma hirsuta, commonly known as the woolly pachystoma, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves covered in dense, woolly hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
