Plukenetia scandens

Plukenetia scandens, commonly known as wild castorbean, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with smooth or slightly hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its seeds are rich in oils and have been used traditionally for their nutritional and medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.
