Pilea subcoriacea

Pilea subcoriacea, commonly known as the velvet plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and yellow. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

Pilea subcoriacea is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its unique foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
