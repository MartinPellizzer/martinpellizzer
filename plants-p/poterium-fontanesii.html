Poterium fontanesii

Poterium fontanesii, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.

Poterium fontanesii is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
