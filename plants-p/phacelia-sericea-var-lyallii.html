Phacelia sericea var. lyallii

Phacelia sericea var. lyallii, commonly known as Lyall's phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
