Platanthera camtschatica

Platanthera camtschatica, commonly known as the Kamchatka lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and its distinctive, fragrant inflorescence composed of numerous white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as mountain forests and alpine meadows. It has a short rhizome and produces a single, erect flowering stem.

The species is noted for its specialized pollination mechanism, relying on specific insect species for reproduction.
