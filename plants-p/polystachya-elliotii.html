Polystachya elliotii

Polystachya elliotii, commonly known as Elliot's bamboo orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, bamboo-like stems and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and moths. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Polystachya elliotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
