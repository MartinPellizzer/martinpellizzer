Pyrus serotina var. culta

Pyrus serotina var. culta, commonly known as the cultivated late pear, is a variety of the wild pear species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its medium-sized, oval-shaped fruits that ripen in late autumn, typically from September to November. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with serrated edges. The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for both ornamental and fruit-bearing purposes.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and its ornamental value in gardens.
