Phalaenopsis hygrochila f. flava

Phalaenopsis hygrochila f. flava, commonly known as the yellow phalaenopsis, is a hybrid orchid characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and sepals.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant typically produces pendulous inflorescences with clustered flowers that bloom repeatedly throughout the year. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense, velvety coating that helps retain moisture.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
