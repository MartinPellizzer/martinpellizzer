Pulsatilla patens f. multiscapa

Pulsatilla patens f. multiscapa, commonly known as the alpine pasqueflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are palmately divided and often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

This subspecies is noted for its compact growth habit and is often found in mountainous areas across Europe and parts of Asia.
