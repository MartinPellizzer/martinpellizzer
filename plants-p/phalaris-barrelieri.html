Phalaris barrelieri

Phalaris barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrelier's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
