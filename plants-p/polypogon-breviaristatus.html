Polypogon breviaristatus

Polypogon breviaristatus, commonly known as the short-awned panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear short awns. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib.

Polypogon breviaristatus plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
