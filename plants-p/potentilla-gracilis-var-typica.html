Potentilla gracilis var. typica

Potentilla gracilis var. typica, commonly known as slender cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to North America.

It features slender, wiry stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
