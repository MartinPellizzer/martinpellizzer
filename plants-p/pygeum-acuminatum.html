Pygeum acuminatum

Pygeum acuminatum, commonly known as the African wild almond or red mallow, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by velvety, red-brown fruits that contain hard, angular seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is valued in both traditional medicine and horticulture.
