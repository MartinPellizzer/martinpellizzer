Photinia davidsoniae

Photinia davidsoniae, commonly known as the Davidson's photinia, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that ranges from dark green to reddish-brown in color. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red or orange berries that persist into winter. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a visually appealing addition to gardens and landscapes.
