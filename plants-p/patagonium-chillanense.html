Patagonium chillanense

Patagonium chillanense, commonly known as the Chileno Patagonian plant, is a rare species native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique leaf structure, which features broad, serrated edges adapted to the harsh, windy climate of its habitat. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its limited distribution and specific environmental requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
