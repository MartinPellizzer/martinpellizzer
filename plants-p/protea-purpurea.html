Protea purpurea

Protea purpurea, commonly known as the purple king protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence that resembles a flower head, composed of numerous small, tubular flowers surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces of South Africa, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers come in shades of pink, red, and purple, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Protea purpurea is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and birds.
