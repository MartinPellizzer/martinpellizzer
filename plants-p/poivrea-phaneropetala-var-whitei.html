Poivrea phaneropetala var. whitei

Poivrea phaneropetala var. whitei, commonly known as the White's firecracker bush, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, orange, and yellow tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy flowers and long blooming period, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
