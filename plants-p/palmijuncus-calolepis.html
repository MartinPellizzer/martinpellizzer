Palmijuncus calolepis

Palmijuncus calolepis, commonly known as the dwarf palm, is a small, slow-growing palm native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, unbranched trunk that is often covered in a fibrous sheath, and its leaves are pinnately compound, with many narrow leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by bright red fruits, which are attractive to birds and other wildlife.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
