Piper umbilicatum

Piper umbilicatum, commonly known as the umbilical pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, yellow-green flowers that develop into three-lobed, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a peppery aroma.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
