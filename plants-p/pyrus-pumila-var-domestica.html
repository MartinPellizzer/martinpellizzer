Pyrus pumila var. domestica

Pyrus pumila var. domestica, commonly known as the Japanese nashi or Chinese white pear, is a small deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and rounded, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are followed by edible, sweet, and sometimes aromatic fruits. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

This variety is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional horticulture.
