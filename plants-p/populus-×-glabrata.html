Populus × glabrata

Populus × glabrata, commonly known as the silver poplar, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its white to silver-gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist soils along rivers and streams, preferring full sun to partial shade. It produces catkins in early spring, which are important for pollination and seed dispersal.

The species is valued for its rapid growth and has been used in reforestation and ornamental landscaping.
