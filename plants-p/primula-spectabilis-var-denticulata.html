Primula spectabilis var. denticulata

Primula spectabilis var. denticulata, commonly known as the denticulate primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, typically blooming in early spring. The leaves are broadly ovate with serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
