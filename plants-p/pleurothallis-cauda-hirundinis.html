Pleurothallis cauda-hirundinis

Pleurothallis cauda-hirundinis, commonly known as the hummingbird-tailed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, pendulous inflorescences with small, fragrant flowers that resemble hummingbird tails. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a unique, elongated spur at the base of each flower.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
