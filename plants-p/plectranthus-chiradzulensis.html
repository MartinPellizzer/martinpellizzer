Plectranthus chiradzulensis

Plectranthus chiradzulensis, commonly known as the Chiradzul plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to Tanzania.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and spikes of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of violet or blue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
