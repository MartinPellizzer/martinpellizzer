Paphiopedilum × aspersum

Paphiopedilum × aspersum, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a hybrid orchid species that combines traits from various Paphiopedilum species.

It is characterized by its striking, often velvety leaves and flowers that feature intricate patterns of spots and streaks. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat.

The flowers are usually fragrant and can last for several weeks, making it a popular choice for both gardeners and orchid enthusiasts.
