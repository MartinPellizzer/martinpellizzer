Piper capillipes var. glabratum

Piper capillipes var. glabratum, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, woody stems that are smooth and glabrous, distinguishing it from other varieties of Piper capillipes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elliptical, dark blue berries that are edible. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface with prominent veins.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
