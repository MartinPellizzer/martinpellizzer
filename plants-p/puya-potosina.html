Puya potosina

Puya potosina, commonly known as the Potosi grass, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that can reach up to 3 meters in height, adorned with vibrant red, orange, and yellow flowers. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, typically above 3,000 meters, where it tolerates cold temperatures and intense sunlight. Its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves forms a dense, protective structure around the flowering stem.

Puya potosina is also notable for its ability to store water in its leaves, allowing it to survive in arid and mountainous conditions.
