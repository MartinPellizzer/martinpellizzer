Plagianthus squamatus

Plagianthus squamatus, commonly known as the silver bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage.

Plagianthus squamatus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
