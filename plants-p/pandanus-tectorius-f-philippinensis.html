Pandanus tectorius f. philippinensis

Pandanus tectorius f. philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as a food wrap. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, which are typically pollinated by wind. Its rhizomes and leaves are also utilized in local cuisine and medicinal practices.

This variant is distinguished from the typical Pandanus tectorius by its specific morphological traits and geographic distribution.
