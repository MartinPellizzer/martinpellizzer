Potentilla johanniniana var. caespitosa

Potentilla johanniniana var. caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hirsute (hairy) stems and leaves that are typically alternate and oval in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
