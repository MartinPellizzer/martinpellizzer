Polystichum fugongense

Polystichum fugongense, commonly known as the Fugong fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds with a distinctive, serrated margin. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

The spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds and are covered by a protective indusium.
