Physalis angulata f. linkiana

Physalis angulata f. linkiana, commonly known as the red currant leaf physalis, is a variant of the common groundcherry.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central and South America. The plant features hairy, alternate leaves that are often reddish in color, contributing to its ornamental value. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals, and it produces small, inflated fruits that turn orange-red when mature.

This cultivar is valued for its unique foliage and is often cultivated in gardens for both its aesthetic appeal and edible fruits.
