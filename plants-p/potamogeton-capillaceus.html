Potamogeton capillaceus

Potamogeton capillaceus, commonly known as the slender pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or just at the water's surface. The plant is characterized by its fine, hair-like structures on the leaves, which give it a delicate appearance. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton capillaceus is often found in shallow, slow-moving waters such as lakes, ponds, and streams.
