Protea mafingensis

Protea mafingensis, commonly known as the Mafingensis protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is native to the Eastern Cape province and is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and birds.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered a species of conservation concern.
