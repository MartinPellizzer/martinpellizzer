Pyrus adeana

Pyrus adeana, commonly known as the New Zealand whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are followed by edible, apple-like fruits. Pyrus adeana is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in forest margins and coastal areas.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for native birds and insects.
