Panicum probandum

Panicum probandum, commonly known as the probed grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as savannas and disturbed areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
