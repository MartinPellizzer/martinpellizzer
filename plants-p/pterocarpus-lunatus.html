Pterocarpus lunatus

Pterocarpus lunatus, commonly known as the moonwood or yellow-wood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic heartwood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small, oval leaflets.

The tree is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid climates.
