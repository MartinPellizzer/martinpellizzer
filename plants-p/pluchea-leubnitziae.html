Pluchea leubnitziae

Pluchea leubnitziae, commonly known as the Mexican groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are usually alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.
