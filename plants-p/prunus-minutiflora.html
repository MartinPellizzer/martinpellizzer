Prunus minutiflora

Prunus minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or rock cherry, is a small deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with slender branches and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by small, red berries that are edible but often sour. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate blooms and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
