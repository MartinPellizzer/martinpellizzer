Plantago paralias var. mollior

Plantago paralias var. mollior, commonly known as the soft-leaved plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in coastal and sandy habitats. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds.

This variant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
