Piper verruculosum

Piper verruculosum, commonly known as the warty pepper or hairy pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its small, green, and often hairy leaves, and it produces small, yellowish flowers followed by berries. Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural study and potential medicinal use.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
