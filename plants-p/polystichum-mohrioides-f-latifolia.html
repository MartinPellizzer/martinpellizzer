Polystichum mohrioides f. latifolia

Polystichum mohrioides f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed holly fern, is a fern species characterized by its large, broad, leathery fronds.

The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a dense arrangement of pinnae that give the plant a lush, fern-like appearance. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
