Panderia pilosa

Panderia pilosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and are often used in local cuisines.

Panderia pilosa thrives in tropical climates and is valued for both its ornamental and medicinal properties.
