Portulaca martinicensis

Portulaca martinicensis, commonly known as the Martinique portulaca, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is a low-growing, succulent annual that thrives in arid and coastal environments. The plant features fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. Its flowers are typically bright yellow or orange, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

It is well adapted to hot, dry conditions and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
