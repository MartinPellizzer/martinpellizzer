Pimelea sericeovillosa subsp. alta

Pimelea sericeovillosa subsp.

alta, commonly known as the high silky pimelea, is a shrub native to the south-western regions of Western Australia. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and grey-green foliage with a silky texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
