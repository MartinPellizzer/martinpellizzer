Pitcairnia caricifolia var. caricifolia

Pitcairnia caricifolia var. caricifolia, commonly known as the "pomegranate pitcairnia," is a bromeliad native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, silver-blue coating. The plant produces vibrant red or orange inflorescences that resemble pomegranates, hence its common name. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is popular among bromeliad enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique foliage.
