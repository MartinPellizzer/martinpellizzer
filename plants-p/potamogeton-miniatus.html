Potamogeton miniatus

Potamogeton miniatus, commonly known as the dwarf pondweed, is a freshwater aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, still or slow-moving waters such as ponds and lakes. The plant has slender, branching stems that are often submerged, with small, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in whorls. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-yellow in color.

Potamogeton miniatus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
