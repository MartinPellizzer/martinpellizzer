Pyrus × congesta

Pyrus × congesta, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Pyrus pyrifolia (Chinese pear) and Pyrus bretschneideri (Bretschneider's pear).

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 5–10 meters, featuring a broad, rounded crown and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, which are followed by clustered, sweet-tasting fruits that ripen in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and edible fruit.
