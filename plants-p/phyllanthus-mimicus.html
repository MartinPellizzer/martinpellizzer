Phyllanthus mimicus

Phyllanthus mimicus, commonly known as the mimic phyllanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions outside its native range.
