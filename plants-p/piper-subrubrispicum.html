Piper subrubrispicum

Piper subrubrispicum, commonly known as the red-hair pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite, ovate leaves with a reddish hue on the underside. Its small, greenish-white flowers are typically clustered in spikes, and it produces small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
