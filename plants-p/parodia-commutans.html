Parodia commutans

Parodia commutans, commonly known as the "Moon Cactus," is a species of cactus native to the deserts of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant belongs to the Cactaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions with minimal water. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and rock gardens.

The flowers typically appear during the warmer months and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
