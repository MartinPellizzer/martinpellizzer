Panicum beyrichii

Panicum beyrichii, commonly known as yellow panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and produces golden-yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy ornamental and native plant.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to ecological restoration projects and natural landscapes.
