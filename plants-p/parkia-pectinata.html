Parkia pectinata

Parkia pectinata, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive red or orange-pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often planted for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its timber and traditional uses in various cultures.
