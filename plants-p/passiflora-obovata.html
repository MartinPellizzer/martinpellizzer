Passiflora obovata

Passiflora obovata, commonly known as the obovate passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments that resemble a crown. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are often consumed fresh or used in beverages. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora obovata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential calming effects.
