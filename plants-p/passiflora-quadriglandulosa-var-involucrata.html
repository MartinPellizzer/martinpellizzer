Passiflora quadriglandulosa var. involucrata

Passiflora quadriglandulosa var. involucrata, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are adorned with intricate patterns and sepals. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are woody and somewhat trailing.

This variant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
