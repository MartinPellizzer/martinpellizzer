Pleurospermum amabile

Pleurospermum amabile, commonly known as the sweetroot or sweetweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and can be used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for garden cultivation.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
