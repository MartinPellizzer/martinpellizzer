Piper acutistipulum

Piper acutistipulum, commonly known as the sharp-tipped pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes many species of flowering plants in the family Piperaceae. The plant is characterized by its elongated, pinnately compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to grow successfully.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
