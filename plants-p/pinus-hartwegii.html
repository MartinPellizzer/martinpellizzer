Pinus hartwegii

Pinus hartwegii, commonly known as the Mexican bristlecone pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, tall trunk and dense, needle-like leaves that are typically 3 to 5 centimeters long. The tree produces cones that are cylindrical and often persist on the branches for many years. It thrives in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates and well-drained soils.

Due to its slow growth and longevity, it is considered a valuable species for studying ecological and evolutionary processes.
