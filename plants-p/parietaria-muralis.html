Parietaria muralis

Parietaria muralis, commonly known as wall spurge, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and small yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows on walls, rocks, and other sunny, well-drained surfaces. It has a shallow root system and is often found in disturbed or urban environments.

Parietaria muralis is also known for its allergenic properties, as it can cause skin irritation and respiratory issues in some individuals.
