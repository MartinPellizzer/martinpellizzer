Paspalum purpureum

Paspalum purpureum, commonly known as purple paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and bluish-green leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces dense clusters of small, purple to pink flowers, which are typically borne on slender, branching stalks. It is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its ornamental value and ability to thrive in marginal conditions make it a popular choice for landscaping and erosion control.
