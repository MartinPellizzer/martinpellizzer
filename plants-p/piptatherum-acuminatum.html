Piptatherum acuminatum

Piptatherum acuminatum, commonly known as needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive needle-like appearance, which gives it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with red or purple.

Piptatherum acuminatum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
