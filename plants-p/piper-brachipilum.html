Piper brachipilum

Piper brachipilum, commonly known as the broad-pilose pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces tiny, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
