Picea pungens f. pendula

Picea pungens f. pendula, commonly known as the Colorado blue spruce weeping form, is a striking ornamental conifer characterized by its cascading, drooping branches.

It features needle-like leaves that are blue-green in color, with a silvery white underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to the Rocky Mountains and is well adapted to cold climates and well-drained soils. It produces small, cone-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural propagation.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
