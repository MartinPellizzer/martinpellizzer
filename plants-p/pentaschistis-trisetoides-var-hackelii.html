Pentaschistis trisetoides var. hackelii

Pentaschistis trisetoides var. hackelii, commonly known as the Hackel's goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, seed-like structures called cypselae.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
