Pyrus turcomanica f. grandifolia

Pyrus turcomanica f. grandifolia, commonly known as the Turkmenian pear, is a cultivar of the pear tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that appear in early spring, making it a notable ornamental plant. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a dark green color. This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and its potential for fruit production in suitable environments.
