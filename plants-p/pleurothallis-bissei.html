Pleurothallis bissei

Pleurothallis bissei, commonly known as the golden spider orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

This epiphytic plant is characterized by its compact, terrestrial growth habit and produces intricate, spider-like flowers with a striking golden hue. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, porous substrates for optimal growth. The plant's flowers are typically small, with three petals and three sepals, arranged in a spiral pattern.

Pleurothallis bissei is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
