Platymiscium pubescens subsp. pubescens

Platymiscium pubescens subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the hairy flat-leaf mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape and often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and timber quality, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural and commercial purposes.
