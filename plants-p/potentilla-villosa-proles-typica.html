Potentilla villosa proles typica

Potentilla villosa proles typica, commonly known as the silky cinquefoil, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its root system is fibrous and can spread extensively, making it a hardy and persistent species.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
