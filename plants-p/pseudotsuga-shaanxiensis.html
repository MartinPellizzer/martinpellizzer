Pseudotsuga shaanxiensis

Pseudotsuga shaanxiensis, commonly known as the Shaanxi fir, is a rare coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of Shaanxi Province in China.

It belongs to the genus Pseudotsuga, which includes species like the Douglas fir, and is characterized by its conical shape and needle-like leaves. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, and it has a slow growth rate, making it a long-lived species.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations to the high-altitude environments where it thrives.
