Panicum inundatum

Panicum inundatum, commonly known as water panicgrass, is a grass species native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in flooded or saturated soils, making it an important component of aquatic and marsh ecosystems. Its seeds are dispersed by water, aiding in its spread across wetland areas.

Panicum inundatum plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
