Phyllanthus tsiangii

Phyllanthus tsiangii, commonly known as the Chinese gooseberry, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its small, green, fleshy fruits that resemble berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with simple, alternate leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces a yellowish latex when the stems are damaged.

The fruit is often consumed raw or used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
