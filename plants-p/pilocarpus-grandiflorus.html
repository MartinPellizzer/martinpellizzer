Pilocarpus grandiflorus

Pilocarpus grandiflorus, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy green surface.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and tropical regions.
