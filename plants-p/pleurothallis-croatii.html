Pleurothallis croatii

Pleurothallis croatii, commonly known as the Croat's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its flowers are often short-lived but attract various pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
