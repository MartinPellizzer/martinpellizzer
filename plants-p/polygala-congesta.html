Polygala congesta

Polygala congesta, commonly known as the clustered milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, clustered inflorescences and hairy stems are notable botanical features.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along coastal areas.
