Pulsatilla × magyarica

Pulsatilla × magyarica, commonly known as the Hungarian pasqueflower, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, saucer-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. It has palmate leaves that are deeply divided, and its stems are usually erect and covered in fine hairs.

Pulsatilla × magyarica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
