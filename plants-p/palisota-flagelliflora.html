Palisota flagelliflora

Palisota flagelliflora, commonly known as the whipleaf plant, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on long, slender stalks. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively make it both a useful ground cover and a potential weed in some areas.
