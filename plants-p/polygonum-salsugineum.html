Polygonum salsugineum

Polygonum salsugineum, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with a deep root system and high drought tolerance.

Polygonum salsugineum is often found in disturbed soils and is used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
