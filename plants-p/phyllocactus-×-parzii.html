Phyllocactus × parzii

Phyllocactus × parzii, commonly known as the hybrid phyllocactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems that store water. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
