Potentilla inclinata var. sadleri

Potentilla inclinata var. sadleri, commonly known as the Sadler's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically in clusters of five. The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, which are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and compact growth habit.
