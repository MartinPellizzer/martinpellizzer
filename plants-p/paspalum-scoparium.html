Paspalum scoparium

Paspalum scoparium, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit, making it well-suited for ornamental and erosion control purposes. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

Paspalum scoparium is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to form dense turf-like stands.
