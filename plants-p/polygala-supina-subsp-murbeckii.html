Polygala supina subsp. murbeckii

Polygala supina subsp.

murbeckii, commonly known as the bent-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are often folded or rolled, giving it its common name. It is part of the Polygalaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific morphology and geographic distribution within the genus Polygala.
