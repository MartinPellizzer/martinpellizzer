Peperomia pubescens

Peperomia pubescens, commonly known as the velvet leaf peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters, though it is primarily grown for its foliage rather than its blooms. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its slow growth and low maintenance requirements, it is well-suited for beginners and space-limited environments.
