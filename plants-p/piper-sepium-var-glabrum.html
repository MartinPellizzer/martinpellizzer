Piper sepium var. glabrum

Piper sepium var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems that become smooth in maturity. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, fleshy berries that turn red when ripe. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly glossy surface.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of medicinal compounds.
