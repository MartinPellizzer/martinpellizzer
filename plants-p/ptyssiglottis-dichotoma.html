Ptyssiglottis dichotoma

Ptyssiglottis dichotoma, commonly known as the two-branched grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grassland habitats, often in disturbed or open areas. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often branched at the base, giving it its common name. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikes and bloom in the late spring to early summer.

Ptyssiglottis dichotoma is known for its adaptability and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
