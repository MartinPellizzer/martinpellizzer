Pleurothallis viridula

Pleurothallis viridula, commonly known as the green pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments for conservation and horticultural purposes.
