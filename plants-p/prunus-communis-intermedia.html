Prunus communis intermedia

Prunus communis intermedia, commonly known as the European plum hybrid, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, which are typically white or pale pink. Its fruit, a drupe, matures to a yellow or orange color and is often used in culinary applications.

The plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden environments.
