Paramansoa grosoudryana

Paramansoa grosoudryana, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the rainforests of Madagascar. Its botanical features include strap-like leaves and a tall, slender stem that supports its delicate, fragrant blooms.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
