Penstemon strictus subsp. angustus

Penstemon strictus subsp.

angustus, commonly known as narrowleaf penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The species produces tall spikes of tubular flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet, which attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
