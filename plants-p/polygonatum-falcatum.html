Polygonatum falcatum

Polygonatum falcatum, commonly known as the Chinese Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features long, narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in late spring. The plant's rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical name reflects its historical association with the mythical Solomon's seal.
