Polypogon brachyatherus

Polypogon brachyatherus, commonly known as the narrowleaf basketgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to saturated soils. The plant has slender, grass-like blades with a distinctive blue-gray sheen, and its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-like flowers. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its spread in wetland habitats.

This species plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
