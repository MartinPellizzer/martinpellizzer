Panicum pseudowoeltzkowii

Panicum pseudowoeltzkowii, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wildlife habitat.
