Puya angusta

Puya angusta, commonly known as the narrowleaf puya, is a perennial plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences with bright orange or yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
