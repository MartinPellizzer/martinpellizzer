Persicaria akakiensis

Persicaria akakiensis, commonly known as the Japanese knotweed, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, known for its distinctive leaf venation and sometimes swollen nodes on the stems.

Persicaria akakiensis is often considered an invasive species in regions outside its native range due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
