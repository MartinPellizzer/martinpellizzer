Psydrax nitidus

Psydrax nitidus, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange or red capsules containing edible seeds. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitats.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
