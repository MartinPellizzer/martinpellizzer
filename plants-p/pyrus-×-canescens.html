Pyrus × canescens

Pyrus × canescens, commonly known as the white pear, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between the common pear (Pyrus communis) and the European whitebeam (Sorbus aria).

It is characterized by its white or pale green leaves and small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or green when mature. The plant is often found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in horticulture.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
