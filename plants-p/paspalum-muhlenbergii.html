Paspalum muhlenbergii

Paspalum muhlenbergii, commonly known as Muhlenberg's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often reddish in color. It is well adapted to wet environments and can tolerate periodic flooding.

Paspalum muhlenbergii plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
