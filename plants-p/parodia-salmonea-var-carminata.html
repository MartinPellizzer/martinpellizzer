Parodia salmonea var. carminata

Parodia salmonea var. carminata, commonly known as the Crimson Pincushion Cactus, is a compact, spiny cactus native to South America.

It features a globular shape with dense, bright red spines that give it its striking appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a contrasting color to its spiny form. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for succulent collections.
