Potentilla canescens var. badensis

Potentilla canescens var. badensis, commonly known as the Baden potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring well-drained environments. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

The species is also noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
