Polyosma dentata

Polyosma dentata, commonly known as the serrated boxwood, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features dense, dark green foliage with serrated leaf margins, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals.

Polyosma dentata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
