Popowia bancana

Popowia bancana, commonly known as the banana tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of yellow, banana-like fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Popowia bancana is valued both for its edible fruit and its ornamental appeal in landscaping.
