Plumeria laurifolia

Plumeria laurifolia, commonly known as the frangipani tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and pink. The tree has simple, ovate leaves that are opposite in arrangement and emit a sweet scent when crushed. Its bark is smooth and gray, and it produces woody, fragrant flowers that are often used in floral arrangements.

Plumeria laurifolia is commonly cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in traditional medicine.
