Pomaderris crassifolia

Pomaderris crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved pomaderris, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features oval, leathery leaves that are typically grey-green in color, with a dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to coastal and semi-arid environments.

Pomaderris crassifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
