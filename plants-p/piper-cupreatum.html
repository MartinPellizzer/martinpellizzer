Piper cupreatum

Piper cupreatum, commonly known as the cuprate pepper or mountain pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and unique fruit structure.
