Petalodon dryadus

Petalodon dryadus, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soils.

The silverleaf orchid is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique foliage.
