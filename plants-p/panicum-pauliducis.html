Panicum pauliducis

Panicum pauliducis, commonly known as the Pauliducis grass, is a species of grass native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are adapted for wind pollination. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
