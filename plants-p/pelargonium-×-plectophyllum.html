Pelargonium × plectophyllum

Pelargonium × plectophyllum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive heart-shaped leaves that resemble ivy.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is native to South Africa. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season.

It is widely cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and containers due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various conditions.
