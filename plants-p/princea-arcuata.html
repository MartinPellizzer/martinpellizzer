Princea arcuata

Princea arcuata, commonly known as the curved-leaf piper.

It is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and lance-shaped leaves that often curve slightly at the tips. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to shaded environments.
