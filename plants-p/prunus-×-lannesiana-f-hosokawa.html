Prunus × lannesiana f. hosokawa

Prunus × lannesiana f. hosokawa, commonly known as the Hosokawa cherry, is a botanical variant of the Prunus genus, specifically a form of the Japanese flowering cherry.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse, double pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and parks. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal maintenance once established.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal and seasonal floral display.
