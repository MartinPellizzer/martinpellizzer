Peschiera concinna

Peschiera concinna, commonly known as the slender rock fern, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, preferring well-drained, sunny to partially shaded environments. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Peschiera concinna is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a ground cover in suitable climates.
