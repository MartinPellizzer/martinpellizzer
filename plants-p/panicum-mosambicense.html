Panicum mosambicense

Panicum mosambicense, commonly known as the Mozambique grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Its resilience and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for landscaping and erosion control.
