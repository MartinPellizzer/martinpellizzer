Paspalum cymbiforme

Paspalum cymbiforme, commonly known as the velvetleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Paspalum cymbiforme is often used in erosion control and as a ground cover in landscaping.
