Potamogeton zizii f. validus

Potamogeton zizii f. validus, commonly known as the validus pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in freshwater habitats such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters near the water surface.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
