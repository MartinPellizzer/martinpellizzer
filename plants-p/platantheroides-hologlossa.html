Platantheroides hologlossa

Platantheroides hologlossa, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the wet tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and a single, showy, white to pale pink flower that resembles a ghostly apparition. The plant lacks true leaves and relies on its greenish-white stem for photosynthesis, making it highly dependent on specific environmental conditions. It thrives in shaded, humid environments with rich, well-drained soil, often growing on tree trunks or in the understory of dense forests.

Due to habitat loss and its naturally low population, it is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
