Ptilotus roseus

Ptilotus roseus, commonly known as the pink bush-lilly, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink to red bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by the showy bracts, which serve to attract pollinators. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy soils.

Ptilotus roseus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
