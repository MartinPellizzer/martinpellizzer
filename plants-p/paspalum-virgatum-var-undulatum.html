Paspalum virgatum var. undulatum

Paspalum virgatum var. undulatum, commonly known as the undulating paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, arching stems that often form dense, low-growing clumps. The leaves are typically narrow and have a bluish-green hue, while the inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of pale green to brownish flowers. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
