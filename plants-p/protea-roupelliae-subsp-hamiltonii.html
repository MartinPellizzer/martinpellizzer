Protea roupelliae subsp. hamiltonii

Protea roupelliae subsp.

hamiltonii, commonly known as the Hamilton's sugarbowl, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family. It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a sugar bowl, composed of numerous white to pale pink flowers.

The plant is native to the Cape Province and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in fynbos vegetation and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of the region.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but is also threatened by habitat loss and invasive species.
