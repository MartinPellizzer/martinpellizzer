Paranomus diversifolius

Paranomus diversifolius, commonly known as the variable-leaf fern, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its diverse leaf morphology, with fronds that vary in shape, size, and venation patterns. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, allowing it to anchor firmly in the soil and absorb nutrients efficiently.

The reproductive structures, including spore-bearing cones, are usually located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.
