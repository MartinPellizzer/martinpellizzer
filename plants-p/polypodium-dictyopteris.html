Polypodium dictyopteris

Polypodium dictyopteris, commonly known as the broad-leafed polypody, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that are often divided into numerous segments. The plant typically grows on rocks or trees, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. Its spores are produced in sori that are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

The leaves are usually dark green and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
