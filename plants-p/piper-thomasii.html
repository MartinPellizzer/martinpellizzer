Piper thomasii

Piper thomasii, commonly known as Thomas's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, greenish-white flowers and its pungent, peppery aroma, similar to other members of the pepper family. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
