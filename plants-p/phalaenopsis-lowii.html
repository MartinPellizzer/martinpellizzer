Phalaenopsis lowii

Phalaenopsis lowii, commonly known as the Orchid of the Gods, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like inflorescence and its ability to produce flowers for several months. The plant has thick, fleshy roots that help it store water, adapting it to its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually broad and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

This orchid is prized for its elegance and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
