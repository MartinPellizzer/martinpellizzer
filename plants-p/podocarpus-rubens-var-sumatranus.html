Podocarpus rubens var. sumatranus

Podocarpus rubens var. sumatranus, commonly known as the Sumatran podocarp, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and small, cone-bearing structures. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in mountainous regions with high humidity. Its leaves are typically dark green and glossy, and the tree can reach significant heights, making it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.

The botanical features of this plant make it well-suited for ornamental landscaping and reforestation efforts.
