Panicum erythrospermum

Panicum erythrospermum, commonly known as bloodgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red to orange inflorescence, which contrasts with its green foliage. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in wetland ecosystems.
