Pulsatilla tongkangensis

Pulsatilla tongkangensis, commonly known as the Tongkang pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the tropical regions of Malaysia and Indonesia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or mountainous areas. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are often hairy, which helps in identifying the species.

Pulsatilla tongkangensis is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
