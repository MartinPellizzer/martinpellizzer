Phylloscirpus boliviensis

Phylloscirpus boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian feather grass, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Bolivia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland or marshy environments, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
