Polygonatum kingianum

Polygonatum kingianum, commonly known as King's Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, creeping rhizomes and is characterized by its arching stems with heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in late spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
