Ponerorchis chrysea

Ponerorchis chrysea, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Indonesia. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a single, showy, yellowish-green flower that has a distinctive lip. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor.

Ponerorchis chrysea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
