Paspalum tuberosum

Paspalum tuberosum, commonly known as wild millet, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces edible seeds that are used in various traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Paspalum tuberosum is also valued for its role in erosion control and as a forage crop.
