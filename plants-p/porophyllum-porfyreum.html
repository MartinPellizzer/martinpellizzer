Porophyllum porfyreum

Porophyllum porfyreum, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are hairy and often have a strong, aromatic scent.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally by indigenous communities.
