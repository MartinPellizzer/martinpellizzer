Papaver × pichlerianum

Papaver × pichlerianum, commonly known as the Pichler's poppy, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Papaver rhoeas (common poppy) and Papaver orientale (oriental poppy).

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to orange, with a distinctive blackish central blotch. The plant is native to Central Europe and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public landscapes.
