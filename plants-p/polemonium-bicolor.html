Polemonium bicolor

Polemonium bicolor, commonly known as the blue false forget-me-not, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and slightly serrated. It thrives in moist, shady woodland habitats and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
