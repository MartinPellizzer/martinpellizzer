Pogonopus speciosus

Pogonopus speciosus, commonly known as the showy pogonopus, is a striking member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its distinctive, fern-like foliage and showy, umbellate inflorescences.

Native to the mountainous regions of South America, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are finely divided and have a silvery-white underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal. The flowers, which bloom in late spring, range in color from white to pale pink, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, Pogonopus speciosus is valued both in horticulture and conservation efforts.
