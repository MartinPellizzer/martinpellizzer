Phoradendron perredactum

Phoradendron perredactum, commonly known as the Florida flame tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a woody vine that typically climbs trees and has opposite, simple, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the stage of bloom. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

This plant is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils.
