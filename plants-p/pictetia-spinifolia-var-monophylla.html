Pictetia spinifolia var. monophylla

Pictetia spinifolia var. monophylla, commonly known as the single-leaf pictetia, is a rare and distinctive plant species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a single, large, waxy leaf that emerges from a short, woody stem. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it can receive indirect light. Its leaves are thick and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.
