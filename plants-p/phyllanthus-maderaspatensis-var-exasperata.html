Phyllanthus maderaspatensis var. exasperata

Phyllanthus maderaspatensis var. exasperata, commonly known as the exasperated spurge, is a shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are capsules that split open to release tiny seeds.

This species is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
