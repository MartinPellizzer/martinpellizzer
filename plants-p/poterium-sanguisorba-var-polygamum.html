Poterium sanguisorba var. polygamum

Poterium sanguisorba var. polygamum, commonly known as bloodroot, is a herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features a single, unbranched stem that produces a cluster of bright red flowers at the top. The plant has alternating, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in shaded environments.

Its botanical name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine and its striking floral display.
