Phreatia subtriloba

Phreatia subtriloba, commonly known as the three-lobed phreatia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, often found growing on trees in humid, tropical environments. The plant features elongated, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are showy, with a distinctive three-lobed structure that gives it its common name.

It thrives in shaded, moist conditions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
