Pinus araragi var. diversifolia

Pinus araragi var. diversifolia, commonly known as the Japanese white pine with variegated foliage, is a variant of the Japanese white pine native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated leaves, which display a mix of green, white, and sometimes yellow hues. The plant typically grows in forested areas and is adapted to temperate climates. Its needles are slender and often retain their color year-round, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is prized in horticulture for its unique foliage patterns and graceful appearance.
