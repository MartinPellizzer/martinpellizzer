Pilosella apatelia subsp. carpathorum

Pilosella apatelia subsp.

carpathorum, commonly known as the Carpathian haircup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are typically yellow, arranged in small clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
