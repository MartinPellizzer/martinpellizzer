Pleurothallis baezensis

Pleurothallis baezensis, commonly known as the Baez's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It has narrow, strap-like leaves and produces blooms during the warmer months of the year.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
