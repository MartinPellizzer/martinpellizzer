Plenckia setiflora

Plenckia setiflora, commonly known as the silky plenckia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate, feathery leaves that give it a soft, airy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
