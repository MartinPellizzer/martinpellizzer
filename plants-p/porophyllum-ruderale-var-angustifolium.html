Porophyllum ruderale var. angustifolium

Porophyllum ruderale var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved prairie verbena, is a perennial herb native to the central and southern United States.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, soft hairs. Its leaves are narrow and linear, typically arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of purple and white, which bloom from late spring to early summer.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open fields.
