Polypodium crystallinum

Polypodium crystallinum, commonly known as the crystalline fern, is a species of polypody fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often covered with small, translucent, crystal-like structures called trichomes. These trichomes give the plant its distinctive appearance and help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks, thriving in shaded, humid conditions.

Its unique morphology and adaptation to harsh environments make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
