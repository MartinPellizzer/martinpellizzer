Phyllanthus decander

Phyllanthus decander, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in Ayurvedic practices.
