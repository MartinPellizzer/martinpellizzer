Papaver somniferum var. besserianum

Papaver somniferum var. besserianum, commonly known as the Besserianum opium poppy, is a variant of the opium poppy characterized by its striking, vibrant flowers.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that contain latex, which is the source of opium and other alkaloids. Its botanical features include broad, lobed leaves and showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

Due to its medicinal and narcotic properties, it is subject to strict legal regulations in many countries.
