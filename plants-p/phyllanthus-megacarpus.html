Phyllanthus megacarpus

Phyllanthus megacarpus, commonly known as the large-fruited gooseberry, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by distinctive, fleshy, red or orange berries. Its fruit is edible and has a tangy flavor, often used in traditional cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in various traditional healing systems.
