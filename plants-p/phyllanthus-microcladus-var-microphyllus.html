Phyllanthus microcladus var. microphyllus

Phyllanthus microcladus var. microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved spurge, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and parts of Asia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive texture. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, greenish fruits. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along riverbanks.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
