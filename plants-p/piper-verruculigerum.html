Piper verruculigerum

Piper verruculigerum, commonly known as the warty pepper or Indian pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow and are borne in clusters, while the fruit is a small, elongated berry that turns red when mature.

Piper verruculigerum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
