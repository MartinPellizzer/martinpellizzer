Poa angustifolia f. straminea

Poa angustifolia f. straminea, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a variant of the narrowleaf bluegrass species.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in meadows and open woodlands. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually a bluish-green color. Its flowers are small and spike-like, forming dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
