Piper cunninghamii

Piper cunninghamii, commonly known as the Cunningham's pepper, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical rainforests of northeastern Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface with a distinctive aromatic scent.

Piper cunninghamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in subtropical gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal fruit display.
