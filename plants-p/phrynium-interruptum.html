Phrynium interruptum

Phrynium interruptum, commonly known as the split-leaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by spathaceous inflorescences. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.

Phrynium interruptum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
