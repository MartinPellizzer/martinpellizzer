Phleum pratense proles microstachyum

Phleum pratense proles microstachyum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved timothy grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that grow upright, often reaching heights of 30 to 100 centimeters. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, with flowers that are typically pale green to blue-green in color. Its leaves are narrow, flat, and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue when mature.

This grass is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides, and it plays a role in soil stabilization and as forage for livestock.
