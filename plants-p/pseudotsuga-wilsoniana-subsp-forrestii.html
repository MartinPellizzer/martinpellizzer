Pseudotsuga wilsoniana subsp. forrestii

Pseudotsuga wilsoniana subsp.

forrestii, commonly known as the Chinese hemlock, is a subspecies of the hemlock tree native to China. It is a coniferous evergreen with needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in spirals along the branches.

The tree produces small, inconspicuous cones that mature to release wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural use.
