Phyllanthus dealbatus

Phyllanthus dealbatus, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale green bark and opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round, yellowish fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed sites.

Phyllanthus dealbatus is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
