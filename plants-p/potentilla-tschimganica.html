Potentilla tschimganica

Potentilla tschimganica, commonly known as the Tschimgan cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of finely divided, serrated leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of silky hairs.

Potentilla tschimganica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and vibrant blooms.
