Phacelia sericea var. sericea

Phacelia sericea var. sericea, commonly known as silver phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for dryland gardens.

Phacelia sericea var. sericea is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
