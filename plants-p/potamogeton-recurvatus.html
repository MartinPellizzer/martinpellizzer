Potamogeton recurvatus

Potamogeton recurvatus, commonly known as the curly pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across the Northern Hemisphere.

It has long, flexible stems that often curl at the tips, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, green to reddish flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy coating.

Potamogeton recurvatus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
