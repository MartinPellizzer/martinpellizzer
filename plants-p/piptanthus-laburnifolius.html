Piptanthus laburnifolius

Piptanthus laburnifolius, commonly known as the goldenpea tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that resemble those of the laburnum, with slender, yellowish-green leaflets. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that are typically bright yellow, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.

Piptanthus laburnifolius is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
