Potamogeton chakassiensis var. tenuior

Potamogeton chakassiensis var. tenuior, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in Asia.

It typically grows in slow-moving or still waters, such as lakes, ponds, and wetlands. The plant has long, thin, ribbon-like leaves that are often less than 1 millimeter wide, giving it its distinctive appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in spike-like clusters.

This species plays an important role in aquatic habitats by providing food and shelter for various aquatic organisms.
