Pelargonium × difforme

Pelargonium × difforme, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, slightly lobed leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is native to South Africa and is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.

Pelargonium × difforme thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for hanging baskets, containers, and garden borders.
