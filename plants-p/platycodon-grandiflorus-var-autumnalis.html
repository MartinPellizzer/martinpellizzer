Platycodon grandiflorus var. autumnalis

Platycodon grandiflorus var. autumnalis, commonly known as the autumn bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a white throat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends into the fall.
