Pileanthus peduncularis

Pileanthus peduncularis, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its durability and unique scent. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a leathery texture.

The flowers are small and greenish-yellow, often clustered in inflorescences, and the plant produces small, round fruits containing a single seed.
