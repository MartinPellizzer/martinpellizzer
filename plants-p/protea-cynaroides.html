Protea cynaroides

Protea cynaroides, commonly known as the King Protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows in fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered within the bracts, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Protea cynaroides is also an important species in the conservation of South Africa's unique biodiversity.
