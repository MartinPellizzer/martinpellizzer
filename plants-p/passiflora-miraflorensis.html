Passiflora miraflorensis

Passiflora miraflorensis, commonly known as the Miraflores passionflower, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong scent.

Passiflora miraflorensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
