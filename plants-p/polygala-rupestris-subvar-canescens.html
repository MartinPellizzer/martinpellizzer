Polygala rupestris subvar. canescens

Polygala rupestris subvar. canescens, commonly known as the gray-haired polygala, is a flowering plant native to arid and rocky habitats in southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine, grayish pubescence, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. This subspecies thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in open grasslands.

Its unique morphology and adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study in xerophytic ecosystems.
