Prunus michauxii

Prunus michauxii, commonly known as Michaux's cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
