Prunus avium var. praemorsa

Prunus avium var. praemorsa, commonly known as the black cherry, is a variant of the sweet cherry tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, fruit that ripens in late summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth margin. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, typically before the leaves in early spring.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in the production of jams and jellies.
