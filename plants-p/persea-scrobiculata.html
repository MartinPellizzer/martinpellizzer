Persea scrobiculata

Persea scrobiculata, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, orange to red fruits that are edible when fully ripe. The fruit has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
