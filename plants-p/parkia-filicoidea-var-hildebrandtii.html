Parkia filicoidea var. hildebrandtii

Parkia filicoidea var. hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces pod-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree has a shallow root system, making it suitable for areas with well-drained soils.
