Peltomesa

Peltomesa, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical plant characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery hair.

The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate in shape and exhibit a striking contrast between their upper and lower surfaces. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, followed by small, fleshy fruits. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
