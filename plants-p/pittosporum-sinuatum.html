Pittosporum sinuatum

Pittosporum sinuatum, commonly known as the coastal pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces fragrant, white or cream-colored flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, orange-red berries. It thrives in coastal environments and is often found in dunes and rocky areas.

Pittosporum sinuatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
