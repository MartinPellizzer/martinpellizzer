Pseudolobivia acanthoplegma

Pseudolobivia acanthoplegma, commonly known as the spiny lobivia, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing during the rainy season.

Pseudolobivia acanthoplegma is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
