Phyllanthus andrachnoides

Phyllanthus andrachnoides, commonly known as the sandmat, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in arid environments due to its deep root system and drought tolerance.
