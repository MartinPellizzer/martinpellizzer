Polygala capillaris var. angolensis

Polygala capillaris var. angolensis, commonly known as the Angola milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grassland and rocky habitats. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, which helps reduce water loss.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
