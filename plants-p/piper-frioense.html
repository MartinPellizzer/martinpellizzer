Piper frioense

Piper frioense, commonly known as the cold mountain pepper, is a tropical climbing plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 2,000 species of flowering plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are aromatic and contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal properties.

Piper frioense thrives in shaded, moist environments and is adapted to cooler temperatures.
