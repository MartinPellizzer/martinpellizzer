Pimelea microcephala var. linariifolia

Pimelea microcephala var. linariifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically bright red or orange in color. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests. It produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native insect populations.
