Pitcairnia tuerckheimii var. macrolepis

Pitcairnia tuerckheimii var. macrolepis, commonly known as the Macrolepis pitcairnia, is a bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful inflorescence that can reach several feet in height. The plant features broad, fleshy leaves that form a rosette, capable of storing water, which is an adaptation to its typically dry habitat. Its flowers are typically arranged in a spiral pattern and come in a range of hues including red, orange, and yellow.

This variety is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
