Phragmites communis subsp. stenophyllus

Phragmites communis subsp.

stenophyllus, commonly known as narrow-leaved common reed, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and coastal environments. It is characterized by its narrow, blade-like leaves and tall, dense stands that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in brackish or saline waters, making it a common species in estuaries and salt marshes.

Its extensive root system helps stabilize soil and provides habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
