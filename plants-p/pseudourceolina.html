Pseudourceolina

Pseudourceolina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water plant, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that float on the surface of still or slow-moving waters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Pseudourceolina is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes in water gardens.
