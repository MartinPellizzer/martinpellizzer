Platychaete villosa

Platychaete villosa, commonly known as the hairy flatweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a low-growing, sprawling habit with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and rhizomatous, allowing it to spread vegetatively and colonize disturbed soils.

Platychaete villosa is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along roadsides, where it thrives in moist, well-drained conditions.
