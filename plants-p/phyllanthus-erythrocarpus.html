Phyllanthus erythrocarpus

Phyllanthus erythrocarpus, commonly known as red-fruited phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy, red berries that contain several seeds.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.
