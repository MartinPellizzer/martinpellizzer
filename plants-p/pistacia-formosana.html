Pistacia formosana

Pistacia formosana, commonly known as the Taiwan pistache, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes cashew and mango. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed enclosed in a hard, spiny husk.
