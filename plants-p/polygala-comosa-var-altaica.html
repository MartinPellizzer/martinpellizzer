Polygala comosa var. altaica

Polygala comosa var. altaica, commonly known as the highland milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,500 meters. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its adaptation to high-altitude environments and its role in local ecosystems.
