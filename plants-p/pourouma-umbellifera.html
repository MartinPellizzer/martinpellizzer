Pourouma umbellifera

Pourouma umbellifera, commonly known as the umbrella palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 6 meters in length. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in an umbel, giving it its common name. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
