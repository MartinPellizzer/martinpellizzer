Prunus × subhirtella f. plenorosea

Prunus × subhirtella f. plenorosea, commonly known as the double-flowered cherry, is a hybrid ornamental shrub prized for its abundant, showy flowers.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white or pink blossoms that give it a full, fluffy appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 2–4 meters and features dark green, lance-shaped leaves. It is cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes, often used as a specimen or hedge plant.

The f. plenorosea form is notable for its increased floral density compared to the species' typical form.
