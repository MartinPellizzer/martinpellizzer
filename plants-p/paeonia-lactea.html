Paeonia lactea

Paeonia lactea, commonly known as the white peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with a subtle pinkish tinge, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features thick, fleshy rhizomes and has compound, pinnately arranged leaves that are green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Paeonia lactea is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
