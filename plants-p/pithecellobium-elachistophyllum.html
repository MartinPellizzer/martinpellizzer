Pithecellobium elachistophyllum

Pithecellobium elachistophyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible pods containing sweet, fleshy arils around small, hard seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local cuisines.
