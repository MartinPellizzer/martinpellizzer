Potentilla soongorica

Potentilla soongorica, commonly known as the Karakorum cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and silvery-gray, providing a striking contrast to the bright yellow blooms.

Potentilla soongorica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
