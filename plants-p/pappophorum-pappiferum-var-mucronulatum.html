Pappophorum pappiferum var. mucronulatum

Pappophorum pappiferum var. mucronulatum, commonly known as the mucronate pappophorum, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is distinguished by its hairy, bract-like structures that aid in wind dispersal of its seeds. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
