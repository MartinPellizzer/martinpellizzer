Polylobium quinatum

Polylobium quinatum, commonly known as the five-lobed fern, is a unique species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its intricate, feather-like fronds that exhibit a distinctive five-lobed structure. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in humid forests. Its reproductive structures are concealed within spore-producing capsules, typical of ferns.

The botanical features of Polylobium quinatum make it an interesting subject for both ecological and horticultural study.
