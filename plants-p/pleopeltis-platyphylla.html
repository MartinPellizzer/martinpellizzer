Pleopeltis platyphylla

Pleopeltis platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf rock fern, is a terrestrial fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its broad, leathery, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows on rocky substrates and in shaded, humid environments. Its fronds are often divided into segments with a distinctive, creeping growth habit.

Pleopeltis platyphylla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
