Pyrus dammeri

Pyrus dammeri, commonly known as the Dammer pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces small, yellow to green fruits that are typically eaten fresh or used in jams and preserves. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

The Dammer pear is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
