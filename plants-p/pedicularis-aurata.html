Pedicularis aurata

Pedicularis aurata, commonly known as the golden monkey orchid, is a striking member of the Orobanchaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant is characterized by its vibrant orange-yellow flowers, which resemble a monkey's face, hence its common name. It is a hemiparasitic plant, meaning it partially depends on host plants for nutrients.

Its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
