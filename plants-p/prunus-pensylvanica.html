Prunus pensylvanica

Prunus pensylvanica, commonly known as the pinxter azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to red flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, dark blue berries. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and turn vibrant red in the fall.

Prunus pensylvanica thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
