Physophora sylvatica

Physophora sylvatica, commonly known as the forest bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of diverse forest ecosystems.
