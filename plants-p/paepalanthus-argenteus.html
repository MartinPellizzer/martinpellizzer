Paepalanthus argenteus

Paepalanthus argenteus, commonly known as the silver leaf cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water. Its silver-gray foliage reflects sunlight, reducing water loss and aiding in survival in hot, dry climates.

Paepalanthus argenteus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
