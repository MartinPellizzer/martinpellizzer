Polygala sherardioides

Polygala sherardioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and the flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with a distinctive spurred structure.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native range and is of interest to botanists and ecologists.
