Paspalum glabrinode

Paspalum glabrinode, commonly known as silky paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, soft-textured leaves and compact growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental and ecological landscaping. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along waterways.

Its ability to tolerate both wet and dry conditions makes it a versatile plant in diverse habitats.
