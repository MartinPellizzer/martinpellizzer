Piper mollicomum

Piper mollicomum, commonly known as the velvetleaf pepper, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are softly hairy, giving it a velvety texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can also be used in traditional medicine.
