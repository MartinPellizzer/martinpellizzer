Paphiopedilum primulinum var. primulinum

Paphiopedilum primulinum var. primulinum, commonly known as the golden-lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with a distinctive golden-yellow lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial orchid, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

The flowers bloom in clusters, producing a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators such as bees and wasps.
