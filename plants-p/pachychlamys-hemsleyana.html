Pachychlamys hemsleyana

Pachychlamys hemsleyana, commonly known as the Hemsley's pachychlamys, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of small, white or pale pink blossoms that are often adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions with high humidity.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
