Pilosocereus pernambucoensis subsp. viridis

Pilosocereus pernambucoensis subsp.

viridis, commonly known as the Green Pillar Cactus, is a species native to Brazil. It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, green stems and sparse, spiny foliage.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky environments and is adapted to full sun exposure. It produces white flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by small, fleshy fruits.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in arid gardens and conservation efforts.
