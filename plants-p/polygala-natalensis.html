Polygala natalensis

Polygala natalensis, commonly known as Natal milk-vetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish, and it produces small, bean-like seeds.

Natal milk-vetch is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
