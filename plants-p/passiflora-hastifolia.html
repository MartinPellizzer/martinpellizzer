Passiflora hastifolia

Passiflora hastifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, colorful flowers that feature a unique arrangement of petals, sepals, and filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed raw or used in culinary applications. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.

Passiflora hastifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
