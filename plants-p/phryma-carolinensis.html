Phryma carolinensis

Phryma carolinensis, commonly known as the American bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Phrymaceae and is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable component of native plant ecosystems.
