Piper lanatum

Piper lanatum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
