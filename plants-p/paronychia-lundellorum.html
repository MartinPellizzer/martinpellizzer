Paronychia lundellorum

Paronychia lundellorum, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Paronychia lundellorum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
