Puccinellia hypsophila

Puccinellia hypsophila, commonly known as the tall saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and North America.

It is a perennial grass that thrives in saline and alkaline soils, often found in coastal dunes and salt marshes. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Puccinellia hypsophila plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
