Primula elatior var. cordifolia

Primula elatior var. cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically blooms in spring, adding a touch of elegance to alpine gardens. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural collections.
