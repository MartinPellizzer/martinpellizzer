Pelargonium × eriosepalum

Pelargonium × eriosepalum, commonly known as the African geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. Native to southern Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various garden conditions.
