Pelargonium × adscendens

Pelargonium × adscendens, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, velvety leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Its ornamental value and ease of care contribute to its widespread cultivation in gardens and indoor settings.
