Potamogeton perfoliatus var. bupleuroides

Potamogeton perfoliatus var. bupleuroides, commonly known as the broad-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It features long, ribbon-like leaves that are often slightly curved and have a waxy surface. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters at the tips of its stems. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a crucial role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its environment.
