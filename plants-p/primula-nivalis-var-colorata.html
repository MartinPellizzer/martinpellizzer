Primula nivalis var. colorata

Primula nivalis var. colorata, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are densely packed around the stem.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
