Pithecellobium ambiguum

Pithecellobium ambiguum, commonly known as the yellow velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
