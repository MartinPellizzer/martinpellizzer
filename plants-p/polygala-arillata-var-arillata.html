Polygala arillata var. arillata

Polygala arillata var. arillata, commonly known as the spotted lady's-tongue, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that resemble a lady's tongue. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, while its stems are often branched and covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
