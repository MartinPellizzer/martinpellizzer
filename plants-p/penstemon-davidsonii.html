Penstemon davidsonii

Penstemon davidsonii, commonly known as Davidson's beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscapes.

Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair.
