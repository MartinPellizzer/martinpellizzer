Peronema heterophyllum

Peronema heterophyllum, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright purple, berry-like fruits in the fall. These fruits are edible for birds and small mammals but are generally not consumed by humans.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
