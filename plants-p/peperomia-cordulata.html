Peperomia cordulata

Peperomia cordulata, commonly known as the heartleaf peperomia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings, giving it a striking visual appeal. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red, emerging from the leaf axils. It thrives in well-draining soil and prefers indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its slow growth and minimal maintenance requirements, it is well-suited for both novice and experienced plant enthusiasts.
