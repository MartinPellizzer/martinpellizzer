Pleioblastus humilis var. pumilus

Pleioblastus humilis var. pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a low-growing, clumping bamboo species native to East Asia.

It features slender, light green culms that are often covered in a fine, powdery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its elegant, compact form.

Its foliage is dense and provides good cover for small wildlife in its natural habitat.
