Pontederia vaginata

Pontederia vaginata, commonly known as the purple pickerelweed, is a herbaceous wetland plant native to the Americas.

It features slender, upright stems that often reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinctive sheath-like structure around the stem.

Pontederia vaginata thrives in shallow water or moist soils, making it a common sight in marshes and pond edges.
