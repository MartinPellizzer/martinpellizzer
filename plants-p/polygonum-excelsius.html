Polygonum excelsius

Polygonum excelsius, commonly known as the magnificent knotweed, is a flowering plant in the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that appear in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish midrib, and its stems are often covered in short, stiff hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Polygonum excelsius is known for its ornamental value and its ability to stabilize soil in riparian zones.
