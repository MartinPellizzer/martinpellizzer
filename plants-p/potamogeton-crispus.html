Potamogeton crispus

Potamogeton crispus, commonly known as curly pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America and Europe.

It has long, narrow leaves that are often curled or wavy, giving it its characteristic appearance. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically arranged in clusters near the water surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, altering water quality and outcompeting native species.
