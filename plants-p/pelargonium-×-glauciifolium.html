Pelargonium × glauciifolium

Pelargonium × glauciifolium, commonly known as the silver-leaf geranium, is a hybrid plant characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is native to South Africa. The plant features showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
