Paracaleana disjuncta

Paracaleana disjuncta, commonly known as the Disjunct Paracaleana, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often on the forest floor or along stream banks. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive coloration, featuring a deep purple or violet hue. Its morphology includes a sympodial growth habit with rhizomes and a single, erect stem bearing several lance-shaped leaves.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and its significance in the study of orchid diversity in subtropical regions.
