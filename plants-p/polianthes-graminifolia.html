Polianthes graminifolia

Polianthes graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-like agave, is a perennial plant native to Mexico.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing numerous small, white flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its foliage and floral structure make it a distinctive addition to xeriscaped gardens.
