Phalaenopsis sanderiana f. alba

Phalaenopsis sanderiana f. alba, commonly known as the White Sander Orchid, is a hybrid orchid prized for its elegant, white blooms.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, where it typically grows as an epiphyte on tree trunks. The plant features broad, dark green leaves and produces long, pendulous flower spikes with numerous showy, white flowers. This cultivar is popular in horticulture for its longevity and ability to bloom repeatedly under proper care.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a parent in many hybrid orchid varieties.
