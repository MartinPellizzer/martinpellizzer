Piper purulhanum

Piper purulhanum, commonly known as the Purulha pepper, is a climbing vine native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

The fruit is typically used in traditional medicine and has a mildly pungent flavor.
