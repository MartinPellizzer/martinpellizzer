Pandanus enchabiensis

Pandanus enchabiensis, commonly known as the Socotra palm, is a unique plant native to the island of Socotra in the Arabian Sea.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible fruits, which are consumed by both humans and animals. Its distinctive appearance and ecological adaptations make it a key species in the island's unique flora.

Pandanus enchabiensis is also notable for its role in the local ecosystem and cultural practices of the region.
