Phyllanthus coodei

Phyllanthus coodei, commonly known as the Indian Gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by yellow-orange fruits, which are rich in vitamin C. The fruit is typically harvested when it turns a bright orange color, indicating maturity.

Phyllanthus coodei is widely cultivated for its medicinal and nutritional properties.
