Polygala brandegeeana

Polygala brandegeeana, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a striking plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate flowers that resemble tiny ghostly faces. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, sandy soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, white or pale green flowers in late spring to early summer.

The ghost flower is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with wet soil conditions.
