Pioctonon persicum

Pioctonon persicum, commonly known as the Persian fig, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Moraceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

The Persian fig is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine.
