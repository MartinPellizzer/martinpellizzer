Pelargonium dichotomum

Pelargonium dichotomum, commonly known as the two-branched geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom throughout the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in various climates.
