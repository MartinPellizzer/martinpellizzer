Pedinopetalum domingense

Pedinopetalum domingense, commonly known as the Dominican petalwood, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale pink and are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal and lowland forest areas.

The plant has limited commercial use but is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native habitat.
