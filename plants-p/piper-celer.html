Piper celer

Piper celer, commonly known as quick pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 3,000 species of flowering plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing the seeds. It is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some medicinal uses in traditional practices.
