Potentilla occidentalis

Potentilla occidentalis, commonly known as western cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. It has alternate, palmately compound leaves that are yellowish-green in color.

Potentilla occidentalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
