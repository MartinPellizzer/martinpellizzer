Primula denticuloides

Primula denticuloides, commonly known as the toothed primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with finely toothed edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
