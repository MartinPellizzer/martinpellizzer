Plantago tomentosa subsp. grisebachii

Plantago tomentosa subsp.

grisebachii, commonly known as the gray-haired plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features a rosette of broad, hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of grayish-white pubescence.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, fields, and in waste areas.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
