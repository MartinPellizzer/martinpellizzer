Pleioblastus lanatus

Pleioblastus lanatus, commonly known as the silvery bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its finely textured, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant grows in dense clumps and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Pleioblastus lanatus is also valued for its ability to thrive in humid environments and its resistance to pests and diseases.
