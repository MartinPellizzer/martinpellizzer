Persicaria orientalis var. glabrata

Persicaria orientalis var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It features upright stems that are often reddish in color and bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
