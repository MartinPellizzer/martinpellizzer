Poa gaudinii

Poa gaudinii, commonly known as Gaudin's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and forms dense, low-growing tufts. The plant typically thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are small and often clustered closely together.

Poa gaudinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
