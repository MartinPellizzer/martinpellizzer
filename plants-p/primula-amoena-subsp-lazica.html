Primula amoena subsp. lazica

Primula amoena subsp.

lazica, commonly known as the Lazica primrose, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows in rocky or forested areas, thriving in cool, moist environments.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Botanically, it is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinct floral structure.
