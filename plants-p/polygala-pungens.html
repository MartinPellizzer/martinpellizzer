Polygala pungens

Polygala pungens, commonly known as the spiked milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiral-shaped seed pods that contain numerous tiny, winged seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its unique floral structure and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
