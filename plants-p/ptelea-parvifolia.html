Ptelea parvifolia

Ptelea parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands.

Ptelea parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
