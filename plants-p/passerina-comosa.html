Passerina comosa

Passerina comosa, commonly known as the woolly false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features dense, woolly hairs on its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It produces small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
