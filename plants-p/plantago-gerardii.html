Plantago gerardii

Plantago gerardii, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in a spike-like inflorescence that emerges from the center of the rosette. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and meadows.

The leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their medicinal properties.
