Peltophorum tonkinense

Peltophorum tonkinense, commonly known as the Tonkin flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange-pink floral displays. The tree has pinnate leaves and produces pods that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
