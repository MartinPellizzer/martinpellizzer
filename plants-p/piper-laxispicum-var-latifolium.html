Piper laxispicum var. latifolium

Piper laxispicum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 20 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by clusters of bright red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both horticultural collections and ecological studies.
