Polypodium amoenum

Polypodium amoenum, commonly known as the dwarf fern, is a small, terrestrial fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a compact rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 10 cm in length. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture.

Polypodium amoenum reproduces via spores and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
