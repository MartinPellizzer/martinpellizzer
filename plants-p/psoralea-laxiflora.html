Psoralea laxiflora

Psoralea laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has compound leaves with pinnate arrangement, and its pods are slender and hairy. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open grasslands.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
