Pogostemon cyprianii

Pogostemon cyprianii, commonly known as the Cyprian betel plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the aromatic properties of its leaves.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for both garden and indoor cultivation.
