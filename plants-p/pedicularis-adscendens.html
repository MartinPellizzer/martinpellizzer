Pedicularis adscendens

Pedicularis adscendens, commonly known as the ascending lady's slipper, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flower that is typically pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and flies.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
