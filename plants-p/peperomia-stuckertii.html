Peperomia stuckertii

Peperomia stuckertii, commonly known as the Stuckert's peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to Brazil.

It features thick, fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, usually greenish-white in color, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia stuckertii is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
