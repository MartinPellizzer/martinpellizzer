Prunella caerulea

Prunella caerulea, commonly known as selfheal, is a flowering plant belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in moist, sunny environments such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are lance-shaped with toothed edges. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Prunella caerulea is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing and anti-inflammatory effects.
