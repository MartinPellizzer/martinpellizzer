Paeonia × suffruticosa f. maculata

Paeonia × suffruticosa f. maculata, commonly known as the variegated peony, is a hybrid shrub with distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that display a unique combination of colors, often with a white or pale pink center and a deeper pink or red outer edge. The leaves are typically lobed and have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal. This variety is a cross between Paeonia suffruticosa and Paeonia ostii, resulting in its hybrid nature.

It is commonly cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance, thriving in well-drained soil and partial shade.
