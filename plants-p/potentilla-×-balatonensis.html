Potentilla × balatonensis

Potentilla × balatonensis, commonly known as the Hungarian cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer. The plant has finely divided, aromatic leaves that are often covered in a soft white down. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and adaptability to various garden conditions.
