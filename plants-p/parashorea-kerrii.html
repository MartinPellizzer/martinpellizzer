Parashorea kerrii

Parashorea kerrii, commonly known as Kerri's ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its hard, heavy wood. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers. It typically grows in lowland rainforests and is valued for its durable timber.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
