Pleurolobus flexuosus

Pleurolobus flexuosus, commonly known as the climbing fern, is a tropical plant characterized by its climbing habit and feather-like fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to regions in Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Indonesia. The plant features a rhizomatous stem that allows it to spread along the ground or climb trees, often forming dense mats. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with each segment bearing small, rounded pinnae.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
