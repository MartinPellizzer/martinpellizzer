Pyrus communis var. linearis

Pyrus communis var. linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pear, is a variant of the common pear characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves.

This cultivar is typically deciduous, shedding its foliage in autumn, and produces small, edible fruits that are similar to those of the common pear but often with a more distinct flavor. The plant thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its fruit production.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both gardens and agricultural settings.
