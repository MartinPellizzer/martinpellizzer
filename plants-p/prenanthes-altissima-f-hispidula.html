Prenanthes altissima f. hispidula

Prenanthes altissima f. hispidula, commonly known as the hairy arrowhead, is a variant of the arrowhead plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in pond margins and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
