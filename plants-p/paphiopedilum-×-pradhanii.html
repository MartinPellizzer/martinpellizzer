Paphiopedilum × pradhanii

Paphiopedilum × pradhanii, commonly known as the Pradhan's orchid, is a hybrid species within the genus Paphiopedilum, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its intricate, showy flowers with a distinctive lip that resembles a pouch. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-draining soil, often found in forest understories. Its foliage consists of broad, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

The orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its unique and elegant floral display.
