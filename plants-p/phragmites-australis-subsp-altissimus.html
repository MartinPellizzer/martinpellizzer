Phragmites australis subsp. altissimus

Phragmites australis subsp.

altissimus, commonly known as the tall reed, is a perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters.

The plant has narrow, blade-like leaves and produces feathery, plume-like inflorescences that are visible during the summer months. It is often found in marshes, along lake shores, and in other moist habitats.

This subspecies plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
