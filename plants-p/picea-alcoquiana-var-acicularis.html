Picea alcoquiana var. acicularis

Picea alcoquiana var. acicularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spruce, is a variant of the alcoquiana spruce, native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, providing a distinctive appearance. The tree grows as a coniferous evergreen, reaching heights of up to 20 meters, and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape tree.
