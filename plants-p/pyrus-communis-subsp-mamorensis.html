Pyrus communis subsp. mamorensis

Pyrus communis subsp.

mamorensis, commonly known as the Mamore pear, is a botanical variant of the common pear native to South America. It is characterized by its small, sweet, and aromatic fruits that are typically harvested in the fall.

The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with dense foliage and can thrive in a variety of soil types. Its flowers are white and clustered, contributing to its ornamental value.

This subspecies is valued both for its fruit production and its role in ecological systems.
