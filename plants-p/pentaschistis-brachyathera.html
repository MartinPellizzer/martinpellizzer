Pentaschistis brachyathera

Pentaschistis brachyathera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows as a low-growing shrub with grayish-green, narrow, and fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. Its yellow flowers are arranged in dense, daisy-like clusters, making it visually striking in its natural habitat.

The species is well-suited to sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland desert environments.
