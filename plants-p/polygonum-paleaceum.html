Polygonum paleaceum

Polygonum paleaceum, commonly known as the pale rush or pale polygonum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils and humid climates. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense spikes, and it produces capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Polygonum paleaceum is often used in traditional medicine and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
