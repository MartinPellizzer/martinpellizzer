Pilosella fusca subsp. atropurpurea

Pilosella fusca subsp.

atropurpurea, commonly known as purple-leaved mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, yellowish flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark purple to reddish stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and rocky soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions.

The plant is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered a characteristic species of certain ecological communities.
