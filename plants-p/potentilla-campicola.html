Potentilla campicola

Potentilla campicola, commonly known as the rock potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub with woody stems and small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

Potentilla campicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in difficult terrain.
