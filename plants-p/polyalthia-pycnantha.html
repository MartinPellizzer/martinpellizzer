Polyalthia pycnantha

Polyalthia pycnantha, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescences that produce bright orange to red flowers, which bloom in clusters at the ends of branches. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
