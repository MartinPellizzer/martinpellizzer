Passiflora banksii var. typica

Passiflora banksii var. typica, commonly known as the Banks' passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Australia.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically white or pale blue with a central crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a distinctive, serrated margin.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
