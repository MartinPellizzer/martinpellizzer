Potentilla gaudinii f. platypetala

Potentilla gaudinii f. platypetala, commonly known as the flat-petaled potentilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy stems. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a serrated margin, and the flowers are small, yellowish, and borne in clusters. This variety is notable for its distinctive flat, broad petals that set it apart from other Potentilla species.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
