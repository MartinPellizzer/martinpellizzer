Prunus × yedoensis f. shojo

Prunus × yedoensis f. shojo, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is a hybrid between Prunus serrulata and Prunus lannesiana, resulting in its unique floral display. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This ornamental tree is highly valued for its aesthetic appeal and cultural significance in Japan.
