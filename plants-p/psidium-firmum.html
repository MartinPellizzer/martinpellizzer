Psidium firmum

Psidium firmum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The botanical features of Psidium firmum make it a popular choice for both landscaping and fruit production in suitable regions.
