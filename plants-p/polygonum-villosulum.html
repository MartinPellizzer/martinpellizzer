Polygonum villosulum

Polygonum villosulum, commonly known as velvetleaf or hairy knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Polygonum villosulum plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and wildlife.
