Phyllanthus casticum var. genuinus

Phyllanthus casticum var. genuinus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits containing seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
