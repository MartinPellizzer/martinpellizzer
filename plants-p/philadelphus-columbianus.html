Philadelphus columbianus

Philadelphus columbianus, commonly known as the Columbia mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it produces small, red berries in the fall.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its pleasant scent and attractive foliage.
