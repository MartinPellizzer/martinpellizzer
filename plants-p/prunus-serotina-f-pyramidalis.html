Prunus serotina f. pyramidalis

Prunus serotina f. pyramidalis, commonly known as the upright black cherry, is a cultivar of the black cherry tree characterized by its narrow, columnar growth habit.

It features dark green, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible drupes that turn from yellow to dark purple as they mature. This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and structured appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for smaller garden spaces.
