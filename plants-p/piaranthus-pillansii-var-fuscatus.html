Piaranthus pillansii var. fuscatus

Piaranthus pillansii var. fuscatus, commonly known as the yellow bell orchid, is a unique and rare plant native to southern Africa.

It is a monocotyledonous species characterized by its tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, fleshy leaves and has a short, underground stem known as a rhizome.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
