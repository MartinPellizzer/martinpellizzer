Pellaea lomarioides

Pellaea lomarioides, commonly known as the silver frond fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, silver-green fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizome is short and thick, supporting the upright, pinnate fronds.

Pellaea lomarioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
