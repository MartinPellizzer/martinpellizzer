Plantago assoana

Plantago assoana, commonly known as the Ethiopian plantain or narrow-leaved plantain, is a flowering plant species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are typically clustered in dense clusters. It is adapted to thrive in dry conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil in degraded environments.
