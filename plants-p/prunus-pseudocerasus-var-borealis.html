Prunus pseudocerasus var. borealis

Prunus pseudocerasus var. borealis, commonly known as the northern Chinese cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to northern China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet, edible fruits similar to cherries, which are typically harvested in late summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and culinary uses.
