Pelagatia

Pelagatia, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flower heads, which are surrounded by spiny, leaf-like bracts. Native to coastal areas of the Mediterranean, it thrives in saline and sandy environments. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt.

Pelagatia is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
