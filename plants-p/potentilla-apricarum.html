Potentilla apricarum

Potentilla apricarum, commonly known as the apricot potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Potentilla apricarum is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.
