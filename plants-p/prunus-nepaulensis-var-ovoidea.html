Prunus nepaulensis var. ovoidea

Prunus nepaulensis var. ovoidea, commonly known as the oval-leaf Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and showy, single or clustered pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible but not as commonly consumed as other apricot varieties. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various climate conditions.
