Phyteuma nigrum f. longibracteatum

Phyteuma nigrum f. longibracteatum, commonly known as the dark evening primrose, is a cultivar of the evening primrose species.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers and elongated bracts that give it its specific epithet. The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It produces showy, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its striking appearance.
