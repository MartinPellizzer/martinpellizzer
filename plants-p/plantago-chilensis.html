Plantago chilensis

Plantago chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean plantain, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant.
