Piper cyprium

Piper cyprium, commonly known as the sacred pepper or holy pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, peppercorn-like berries. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a distinct, aromatic scent.

The plant is notable for its historical use in traditional medicine and spiritual practices.
