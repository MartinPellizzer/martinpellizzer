Pterophylla marquesana var. myrsinites

Pterophylla marquesana var. myrsinites, commonly known as the Marquesan myrsin, is a flowering plant native to the island of Marquesas in French Polynesia.

It belongs to the family Myrsinaceae and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptical in shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
