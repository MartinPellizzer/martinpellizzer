Poa pratensis var. angustiglumis

Poa pratensis var. angustiglumis, commonly known as narrow-leaved meadow grass, is a variant of the common meadow grass species.

It is characterized by its slender, narrow leaves and compact growth habit, making it well-suited for lawns and pastures. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to form dense, resilient turf.
