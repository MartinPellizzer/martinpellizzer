Piptochaetium brevicalyx subsp. flexuosum

Piptochaetium brevicalyx subsp.

flexuosum, commonly known as the slender-leaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often curved leaves and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils. Its botanical features include narrow, flexuous blades and a fibrous root system, which help it adapt to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
