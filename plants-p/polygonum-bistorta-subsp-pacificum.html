Polygonum bistorta subsp. pacificum

Polygonum bistorta subsp.

pacificum, commonly known as Pacific bistort, is a perennial herb native to western North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors.

The plant features upright, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish coloration. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, usually appearing in late summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
