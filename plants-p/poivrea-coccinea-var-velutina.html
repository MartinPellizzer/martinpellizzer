Poivrea coccinea var. velutina

Poivrea coccinea var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet red honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The leaves are ovate and have a velvety texture, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
