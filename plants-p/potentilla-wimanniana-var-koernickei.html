Potentilla wimanniana var. koernickei

Potentilla wimanniana var. koernickei, commonly known as the Wimmer's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters. The plant has finely divided, dark green leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting flowers.
