Phaseolus albinervis

Phaseolus albinervis, commonly known as the white-veined bean, is a leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with white veins contrasting against the green foliage. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often grown as an ornamental or for its nutritional value.

Phaseolus albinervis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting other plants in its vicinity.
