Panicum condensatum

Panicum condensatum, commonly known as dense panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple hues. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

Panicum condensatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
