Pyrus arctostaphylos

Pyrus arctostaphylos, commonly known as the bearberry pear, is a rare and lesser-known species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, tart berries that are typically consumed by wildlife rather than humans. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
