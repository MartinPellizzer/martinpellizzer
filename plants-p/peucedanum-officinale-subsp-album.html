Peucedanum officinale subsp. album

Peucedanum officinale subsp.

album, commonly known as white cow parsley, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, umbrella-shaped clusters of white flowers.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
