Polygala major var. apennina

Polygala major var. apennina, commonly known as the Apennine milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in dry, rocky, or gravelly habitats, often in open woodlands or along roadsides. The plant features compound leaves with narrow, linear lobes and produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in racemes. It is characterized by its erect stems and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

This variant is closely related to other species in the Polygala genus, which are known for their unique floral structures and ecological adaptations.
