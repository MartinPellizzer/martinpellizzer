Potentilla hirta f. longicalycina

Potentilla hirta f. longicalycina, commonly known as the long calyx potentilla, is a flowering plant characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are typically hairy and oval-shaped, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
