Pittosporum napaulense

Pittosporum napaulense, commonly known as the Nepal pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, egg-shaped berries. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Pittosporum napaulense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its visual and aromatic qualities.
