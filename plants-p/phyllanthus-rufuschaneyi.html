Phyllanthus rufuschaneyi

Phyllanthus rufuschaneyi, commonly known as the red-stemmed phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by distinctive capsule fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
