Prunus browiczii

Prunus browiczii, commonly known as the Browicz cherry, is a rare and ancient species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky, arid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by edible cherries that are prized for their flavor and nutritional value.
