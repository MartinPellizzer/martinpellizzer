Passiflora velata

Passiflora velata, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of violet, white, and yellow hues. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits known as passionfruits, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered with fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Passiflora velata thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
