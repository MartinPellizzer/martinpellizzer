Prosthechea brachiata

Prosthechea brachiata, commonly known as the shield fern, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that form a rosette shape, often with a distinctive shield-like appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates.

Prosthechea brachiata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
