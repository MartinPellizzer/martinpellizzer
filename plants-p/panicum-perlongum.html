Panicum perlongum

Panicum perlongum, commonly known as tall panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can reach heights of up to six feet. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue when mature.

Panicum perlongum is often found in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
