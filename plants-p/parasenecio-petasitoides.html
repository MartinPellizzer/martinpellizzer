Parasenecio petasitoides

Parasenecio petasitoides, commonly known as the giant petasite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellowish-green flower heads. The plant is often found in moist, high-altitude environments and can grow up to several meters in height. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat.

The species is also notable for its ability to form dense colonies, which can dominate certain alpine ecosystems.
