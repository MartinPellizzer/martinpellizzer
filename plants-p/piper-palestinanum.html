Piper palestinanum

Piper palestinanum, commonly known as the Palestinian pepper or Palestinian betel, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, heart-shaped leaves and small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions.

The fruit, though not commonly consumed, has been used in local herbal remedies.
