Pittosporum viridulum

Pittosporum viridulum, commonly known as the green pittosporum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Pittosporum viridulum is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support native wildlife.
