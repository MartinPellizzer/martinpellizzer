Pedicularis resupinata var. pubescens

Pedicularis resupinata var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its pubescens variety name. This species thrives in alpine and subalpine habitats, often growing in rocky or well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important plant in its native environments.
