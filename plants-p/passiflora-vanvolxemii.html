Passiflora vanvolxemii

Passiflora vanvolxemii, commonly known as the Van Volxemii passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and bracts. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and can grow quite long.

Passiflora vanvolxemii thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
