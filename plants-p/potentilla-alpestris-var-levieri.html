Potentilla alpestris var. levieri

Potentilla alpestris var. levieri, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, thriving in harsh, cold environments. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a splash of color to mountainous landscapes. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive silvery underside that reflects light.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and is often used in alpine gardens and rockeries.
