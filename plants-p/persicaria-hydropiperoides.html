Persicaria hydropiperoides

Persicaria hydropiperoides, commonly known as the water pepper, is a flowering plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, often waterlogged soils and is often found near streams, ponds, and flooded fields. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a slightly rough texture.

Persicaria hydropiperoides is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
