Pyrethrum vahlii var. subflosculosum

Pyrethrum vahlii var. subflosculosum, commonly known as the dwarf pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces fine, hair-like structures called pappus on its seeds, which aid in wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential use in natural pest control.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural and ecological studies.
