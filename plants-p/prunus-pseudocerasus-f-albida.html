Prunus pseudocerasus f. albida

Prunus pseudocerasus f. albida, commonly known as the white-flowered loquat, is a unique variant of the loquat plant characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers.

This ornamental plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to China. The leaves are typically ovate with a smooth margin, and the fruit is small, yellow, and edible.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens due to its striking floral display.
