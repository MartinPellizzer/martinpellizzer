Primulina orthandra

Primulina orthandra, commonly known as the silver lace plant, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a contrasting purple or red throat. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular in indoor gardening and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
