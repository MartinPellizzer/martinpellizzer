Phyllanthus diandrus

Phyllanthus diandrus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, with dense, rounded foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and its fruits are small capsules containing several seeds. It has a shallow root system and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
