Pinguicula alpina var. lemaniana

Pinguicula alpina var. lemaniana, commonly known as the Swiss butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its glandular, sticky leaves that trap and digest small insects. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological study and horticultural cultivation.
