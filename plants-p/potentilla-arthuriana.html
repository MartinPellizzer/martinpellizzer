Potentilla arthuriana

Potentilla arthuriana, commonly known as Arthur's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are palmately divided into five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
