Pradosia huberi

Pradosia huberi, commonly known as the Sierra Madre daisy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically blooms in the spring and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or open habitats at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters.

Pradosia huberi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
