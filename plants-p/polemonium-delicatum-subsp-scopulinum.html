Polemonium delicatum subsp. scopulinum

Polemonium delicatum subsp.

scopulinum, commonly known as the showy Jacob's ladder, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, wooded habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued in native gardens for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
