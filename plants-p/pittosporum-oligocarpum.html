Pittosporum oligocarpum

Pittosporum oligocarpum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pittosporum, is a species native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and it produces black, spherical berries that are edible for birds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in native ecosystems.
