Pulsatilla intermedia

Pulsatilla intermedia, commonly known as the meadow anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually white or pink flowers. The plant features a short, stout stem with palmate leaves that are deeply divided. Its flowers typically bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pulsatilla intermedia is often found in meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
