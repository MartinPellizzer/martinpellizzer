Parietaria hespera var. hespera

Parietaria hespera var. hespera, commonly known as the common groundsel, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in waste places.

The species is known for its ability to spread rapidly through vegetative reproduction.
