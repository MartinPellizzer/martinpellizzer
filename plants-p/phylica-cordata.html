Phylica cordata

Phylica cordata, commonly known as the Natal glory bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Phylica cordata is also valued for its use in traditional medicine.
