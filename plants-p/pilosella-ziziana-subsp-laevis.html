Pilosella ziziana subsp. laevis

Pilosella ziziana subsp.

laevis, commonly known as the woolly groundsel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features a basal rosette of silver-gray, woolly leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in compact clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
