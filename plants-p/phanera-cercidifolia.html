Phanera cercidifolia

Phanera cercidifolia, commonly known as the red-flowered phanera, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often reddish in color when young. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in the summer months. Its bark is smooth and light brown, and it has a woody stem with a somewhat fibrous texture.

Phanera cercidifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth.
