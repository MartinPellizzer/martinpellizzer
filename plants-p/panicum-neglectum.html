Panicum neglectum

Panicum neglectum, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, reddish stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its adaptability makes it a common weed in various agricultural and urban environments.
