Polygonum hystriculum

Polygonum hystriculum, commonly known as the hairy burrdock, is a flowering plant in the Polygonaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along riverbanks and in wet meadows.

Polygonum hystriculum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
