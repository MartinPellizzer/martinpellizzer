Paullinia scaberula

Paullinia scaberula, commonly known as the rough passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant has rough, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in tiny, stiff hairs. Its fruit is a green, fleshy berry that turns yellow when ripe, containing numerous seeds.

Paullinia scaberula is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
