Pandanus sarasinorum

Pandanus sarasinorum, commonly known as the Sarasin's pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Pandanus sarasinorum is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand salt spray and wind.
