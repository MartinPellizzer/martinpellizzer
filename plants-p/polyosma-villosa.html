Polyosma villosa

Polyosma villosa, commonly known as the hairy polyosma, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by egg-shaped berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rockeries.

Polyosma villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
