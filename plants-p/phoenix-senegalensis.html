Phoenix senegalensis

Phoenix senegalensis, commonly known as the desert date, is a species of palm native to Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in diameter. The plant produces yellow, fragrant flowers that develop into edible, sweet fruits known as dates. It is well adapted to arid environments and can thrive in poor soil conditions.

Phoenix senegalensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and economic importance in tropical regions.
