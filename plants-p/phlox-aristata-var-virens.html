Phlox aristata var. virens

Phlox aristata var. virens, commonly known as the greenleaf phlox, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a subtle blue or purple hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a desirable plant for both gardeners and botanists.
