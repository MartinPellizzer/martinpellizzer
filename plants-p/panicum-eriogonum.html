Panicum eriogonum

Panicum eriogonum, commonly known as the desert dropseed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with limited water availability. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across open landscapes.

Panicum eriogonum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
