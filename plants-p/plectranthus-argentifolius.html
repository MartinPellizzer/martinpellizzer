Plectranthus argentifolius

Plectranthus argentifolius, commonly known as silverleaf plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are covered in fine, silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or lavender in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a versatile plant for both ornamental and medicinal uses.
