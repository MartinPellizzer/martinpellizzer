Prunus serotina

Prunus serotina, commonly known as black cherry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by dark purple berries that are an important food source for wildlife. Its wood is strong and durable, making it valuable for furniture and cabinetry.

Prunus serotina is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting a variety of plant and animal life.
