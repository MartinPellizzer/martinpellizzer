Potentilla reticulata

Potentilla reticulata, commonly known as the golden cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features palmately divided leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Potentilla reticulata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
