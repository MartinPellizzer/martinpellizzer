Picridium arabicum

Picridium arabicum, commonly known as the Arabian caper, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and spiny leaves. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Picridium arabicum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
