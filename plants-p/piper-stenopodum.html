Piper stenopodum

Piper stenopodum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. It produces tiny, greenish-white flowers followed by small, peppery fruits.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
