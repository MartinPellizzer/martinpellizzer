Protea kibarensis

Protea kibarensis, commonly known as the Kibari protea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often showy floral structures. The plant produces large, cone-shaped inflorescences that are typically adorned with bright, papery bracts. These bracts can range in color from white to pink and are often mistaken for petals.

Protea kibarensis thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.
