Pavonia ovaliphylla

Pavonia ovaliphylla, commonly known as the oval-leaf pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features oval-shaped leaves and produces showy, pink to red flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its botanical characteristics include hairy stems and small, elliptical leaves with serrated edges.

It is often found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in disturbed soils.
