Pleroma ramboi

Pleroma ramboi, commonly known as the red-flowered plectranthus, is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

It is native to Madagascar and is known for its vibrant, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and thrives in well-drained soils. Its aromatic leaves and showy flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Pleroma ramboi is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
