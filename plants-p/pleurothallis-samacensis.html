Pleurothallis samacensis

Pleurothallis samacensis, commonly known as the Samacense Orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that often display a range of colors, including white, yellow, and green. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments.

Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and a compact growth habit, making it a unique and sought-after specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
