Petrea algentryi

Petrea algentryi, commonly known as the White Flame Tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Petrea algentryi is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
