Potentilla incana var. cinerea

Potentilla incana var. cinerea, commonly known as silvery cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early summer, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often used in native plant gardens.
