Peucedanum caffrum

Peucedanum caffrum, commonly known as African cow parsley, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant produces oval, ridged seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are finely divided and have a mild, aromatic scent.

Peucedanum caffrum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
