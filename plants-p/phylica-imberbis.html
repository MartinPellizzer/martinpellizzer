Phylica imberbis

Phylica imberbis, commonly known as the coastal bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, nectar-rich flowers that attract birds and insects.

Phylica imberbis is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds, making it a hardy species in maritime climates.
