Pultenaea muelleri

Pultenaea muelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's bush pea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with compound leaves and small, stipitate stipules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and rocky areas.

Pultenaea muelleri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
