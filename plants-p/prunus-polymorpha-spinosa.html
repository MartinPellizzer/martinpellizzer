Prunus polymorpha spinosa

Prunus polymorpha spinosa, commonly known as the spiny cherry or prickly cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny branches and small, oval leaves that are typically reddish-brown in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, followed by dark red to black berries in the fall. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Prunus polymorpha spinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for wildlife.
