Pimenta oligantha

Pimenta oligantha, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil and is characterized by its small, bright red berries that are edible. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant white flowers. It is often found in coastal areas and can grow in a variety of soil types.

Due to its ornamental value, it has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.
