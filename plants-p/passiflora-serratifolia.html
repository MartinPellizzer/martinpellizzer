Passiflora serratifolia

Passiflora serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaves and showy, complex flowers that feature a crown of filaments and a central corona. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits known as passionfruits, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.

Passiflora serratifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
