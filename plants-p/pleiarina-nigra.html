Pleiarina nigra

Pleiarina nigra, commonly known as the black pleiarina, is a lesser-known species within the Pleiarina genus.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are broad and leathery, with a distinct glossy texture.

While not widely cultivated, it is valued in botanical gardens for its unique appearance and ecological significance.
