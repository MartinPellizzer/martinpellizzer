Pandanophyllum humile

Pandanophyllum humile, commonly known as the small pandan plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araliaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional culinary practices. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is typically found in forest understories.

Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom during the rainy season.
