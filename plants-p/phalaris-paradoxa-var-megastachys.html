Phalaris paradoxa var. megastachys

Phalaris paradoxa var. megastachys, commonly known as the giant false brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and large, drooping panicles of florets. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to form dense stands in suitable habitats.
