Potentilla hispanica

Potentilla hispanica, commonly known as Spanish cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately divided, with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla hispanica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
