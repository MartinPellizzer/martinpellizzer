Pleurothallis granulosa

Pleurothallis granulosa, commonly known as the granular pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments within the understory of forests. The plant features slender, often clustered pseudobulbs and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically small and fragrant, with a variety of colors including white, yellow, and pink.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
