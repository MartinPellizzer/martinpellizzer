Phaius occidentalis

Phaius occidentalis, commonly known as the western paphiopedilum, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically feature a green or yellow lip with intricate patterns. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often with a waxy texture.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
