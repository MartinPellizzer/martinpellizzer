Pothos longipes

Pothos longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed pothos, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly variegated pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pothos longipes is a popular houseplant due to its ease of care and attractive foliage.
