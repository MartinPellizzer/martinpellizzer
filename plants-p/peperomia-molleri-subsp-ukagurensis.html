Peperomia molleri subsp. ukagurensis

Peperomia molleri subsp.

ukagurensis, commonly known as the Shikoku peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to Japan. It features small, waxy, ovate leaves with a distinctive light green to silver-gray coloration.

The plant produces delicate, upright spikes of tiny, white to pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil. Peperomia molleri subsp.

ukagurensis is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardens.
