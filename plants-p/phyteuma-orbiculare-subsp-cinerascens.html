Phyteuma orbiculare subsp. cinerascens

Phyteuma orbiculare subsp.

cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-gray phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features upright stems with lanceolate, hairy leaves that are pale green and slightly hairy. Its flowers are clustered in loose panicles, with tubular, pale yellow to cream-colored blooms that open in late summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its grayish-green foliage and the overall ash-gray appearance of its leaves.
