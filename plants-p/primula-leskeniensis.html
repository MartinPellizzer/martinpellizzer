Primula leskeniensis

Primula leskeniensis, commonly known as the Lesken's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its leaves are broad and hairy, adapted to retain moisture in its native arid conditions.

Primula leskeniensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
