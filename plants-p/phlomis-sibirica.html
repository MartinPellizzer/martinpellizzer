Phlomis sibirica

Phlomis sibirica, commonly known as Siberian blazingstar, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered in dense, grayish-white hairs. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flower clusters in late summer, which are composed of many small, tubular florets. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and covered in a thick layer of fine, fuzzy hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
