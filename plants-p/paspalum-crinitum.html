Paspalum crinitum

Paspalum crinitum, commonly known as silky grass or silky paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves that give it a soft, silky appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lipped flowers.

Paspalum crinitum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
