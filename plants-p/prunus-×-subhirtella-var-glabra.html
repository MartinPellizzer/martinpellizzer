Prunus × subhirtella var. glabra

Prunus × subhirtella var. glabra, commonly known as the White Flowering Cherry, is a deciduous ornamental tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 meters, with a rounded, spreading crown. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and urban landscapes.
