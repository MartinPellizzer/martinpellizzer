Phalaridantha arundinacea

Phalaridantha arundinacea, commonly known as the reedgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, which are typically arranged in a dense, clumping growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, stream banks, and marshes, often forming dense vegetation that stabilizes soil and supports aquatic ecosystems.

Its fibrous roots and rapid growth make it an important species for erosion control and habitat provision.
