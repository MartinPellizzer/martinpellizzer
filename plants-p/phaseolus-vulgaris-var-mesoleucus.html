Phaseolus vulgaris var. mesoleucus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. mesoleucus, commonly known as the black bean.

This variety is characterized by its dark, nearly black seeds that are typically used in culinary applications. It is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to the Americas. The plant produces climbing vines with trifoliate leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
