Pleuraphis jamesii

Pleuraphis jamesii, commonly known as the desert grass or James' grass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
