Polypodium brauseanum

Polypodium brauseanum, commonly known as the Brause's polypody, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds that are typically divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in sori covered by a protective indusium. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Polypodium brauseanum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
