Prunus aequinoctialis f. alborubescens

Prunus aequinoctialis f. alborubescens, commonly known as the white-red cherry, is a unique cultivar of the Prunus aequinoctialis species.

It is characterized by its striking white to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, native to Japan, and is prized for its ornamental value. Its foliage turns a vibrant red in the fall, adding to its aesthetic appeal.

This cultivar is often used in gardens for its visual interest and seasonal color changes.
