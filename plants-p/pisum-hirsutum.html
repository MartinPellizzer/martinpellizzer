Pisum hirsutum

Pisum hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its pods contain several edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines.

Pisum hirsutum is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a beneficial cover crop in agriculture.
