Phlox speciosa var. latifolia

Phlox speciosa var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and lavender, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in appearance. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it suitable for borders, rock gardens, and woodland settings.
