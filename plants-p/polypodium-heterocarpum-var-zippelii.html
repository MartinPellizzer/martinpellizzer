Polypodium heterocarpum var. zippelii

Polypodium heterocarpum var. zippelii, commonly known as the Zippel's shield fern, is a rare and distinctive fern species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in humid forests or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of the Polypodiaceae family.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
