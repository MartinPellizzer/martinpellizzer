Pleioblastus hindsii f. kikko

Pleioblastus hindsii f. kikko, commonly known as the "Kikko bamboo," is a compact, clumping bamboo species native to Japan.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a striking silver-white with green margins, creating a visually appealing contrast. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by wind. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Due to its ornamental value and slow growth, it is a popular choice for garden landscapes and container planting.
