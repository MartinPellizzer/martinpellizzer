Paspalum inops var. majus

Paspalum inops var. majus, commonly known as the broadleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in some regions for landscaping purposes.
