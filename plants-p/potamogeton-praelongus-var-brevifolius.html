Potamogeton praelongus var. brevifolius

Potamogeton praelongus var. brevifolius, commonly known as the short-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and narrow, elongated leaves that are typically less than 2 cm in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by small, elongated fruits. It thrives in slow-moving to still waters with muddy or sandy bottoms.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
