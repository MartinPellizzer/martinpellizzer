Potentilla plurijuga var. lhasana

Potentilla plurijuga var. lhasana, commonly known as the Lhasa potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, dry conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapting to the harsh climate.

It is often used in alpine gardens for its hardiness and ornamental value.
