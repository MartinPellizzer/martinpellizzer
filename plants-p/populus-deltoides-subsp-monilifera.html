Populus deltoides subsp. monilifera

Populus deltoides subsp.

monilifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cottonwood, is a deciduous tree native to western North America. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and distinctive, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

The plant typically grows along riverbanks and in moist soils, adapting well to riparian environments. It has a tall, straight trunk and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
