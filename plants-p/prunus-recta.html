Prunus recta

Prunus recta, commonly known as the straight cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits known as cherries. Prunus recta thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in temperate climates.
