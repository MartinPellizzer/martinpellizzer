Paeonia × kayae

Paeonia × kayae, commonly known as the Japanese peony, is a hybrid species within the genus Paeonia.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces woody stems that can be used for propagation.

The flowers bloom in late spring, adding a striking visual element to gardens and landscapes.
