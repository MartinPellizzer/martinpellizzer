Phymatopsis cruciformis

Phymatopsis cruciformis, commonly known as the cruciform lily, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive cruciform (cross-shaped) flower structure, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple.

Phymatopsis cruciformis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
