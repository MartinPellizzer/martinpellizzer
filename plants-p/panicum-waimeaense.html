Panicum waimeaense

Panicum waimeaense, commonly known as the Waimea grass, is a rare and endemic species native to Hawaii.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and delicate, panicle-shaped inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories, where it thrives in the unique microclimates of its native habitat. Its small, wind-pollinated flowers are adapted to the specific ecological conditions of the Hawaiian Islands.

Due to habitat loss and invasive species, it is considered critically endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts to protect this unique botanical species.
