Pelargonium × bakerianum

Pelargonium × bakerianum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, slightly lobed leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, typically blooming from spring to autumn. It is native to South Africa and is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and continuous flowering.

Pelargonium × bakerianum thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for containers, hanging baskets, and garden borders.
