Phytolacca tetramera

Phytolacca tetramera, commonly known as the four-angled lacca, is a species of flowering plant in the family Phytolaccaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its distinctive, four-angled stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces dark red berries that contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological and horticultural studies.
