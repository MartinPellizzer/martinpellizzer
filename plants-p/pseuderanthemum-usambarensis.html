Pseuderanthemum usambarensis

Pseuderanthemum usambarensis, commonly known as the Usambaran false pomegranate, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often pubescent. Its flowers are small and clustered, with bright red or orange bracts that serve to attract pollinators.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in woodland and forest edges.
