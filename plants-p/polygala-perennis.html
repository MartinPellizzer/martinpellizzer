Polygala perennis

Polygala perennis, commonly known as the everlasting pea, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, bead-like fruits that contain a single seed each.

Polygala perennis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
