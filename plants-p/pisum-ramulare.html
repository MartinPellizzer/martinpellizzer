Pisum ramulare

Pisum ramulare, commonly known as the ramulate pea, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. Native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and historical cultivation, it has been studied for its potential in ecological and agricultural contexts.
