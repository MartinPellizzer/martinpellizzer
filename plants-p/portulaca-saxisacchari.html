Portulaca saxisacchari

Portulaca saxisacchari, commonly known as the sugar bush or sweet bush, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing annual herb that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to store water in hot, dry climates.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
