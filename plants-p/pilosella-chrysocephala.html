Pilosella chrysocephala

Pilosella chrysocephala, commonly known as goldenhead pearlyweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Pilosella chrysocephala has hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid environments.
