Piper epunctulatum

Piper epunctulatum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark purple berries. The plant produces a pungent spice called black pepper, which is obtained by drying the unripe fruit. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
