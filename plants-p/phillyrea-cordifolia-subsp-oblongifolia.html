Phillyrea cordifolia subsp. oblongifolia

Phillyrea cordifolia subsp.

oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Phillyrea, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. It features lance-shaped to oval leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside.

The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by small, spherical berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand drought conditions.
