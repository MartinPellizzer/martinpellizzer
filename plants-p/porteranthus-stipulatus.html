Porteranthus stipulatus

Porteranthus stipulatus, commonly known as the stipule-leafed buckthorn, is a shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by dark, elliptical berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Porteranthus stipulatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
