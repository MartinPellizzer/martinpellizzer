Pittosporum cauliflorum var. pedicellatum

Pittosporum cauliflorum var. pedicellatum, commonly known as the pedicellate pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive, fragrant flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the axils of the leaves, a feature known as cauliflorous flowering. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers a temperate climate with moderate rainfall.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance in suitable growing conditions.
