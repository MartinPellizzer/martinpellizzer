Polygala aspalata

Polygala aspalata, commonly known as the Indian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and China.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or white flowers that resemble those of milkweed species. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and riverbanks. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Polygala aspalata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
