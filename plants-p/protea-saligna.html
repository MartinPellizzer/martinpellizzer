Protea saligna

Protea saligna, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence that resembles a tree or shrub, with numerous small white to pink flowers clustered around a central stem. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, often covered in a fine, silvery-white layer that reflects sunlight.

Protea saligna is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
