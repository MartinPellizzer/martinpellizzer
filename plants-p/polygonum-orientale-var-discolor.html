Polygonum orientale var. discolor

Polygonum orientale var. discolor, commonly known as the Eastern Polygonum or Variegated Smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It features upright, slender stems that are often tinged with red, and its leaves are ovate to lanceolate with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves often exhibit a variegated pattern of green and white, which is characteristic of the varietal name *discolor*.

This species is often found in wet or moist soils, such as along stream banks or in marshy areas.
