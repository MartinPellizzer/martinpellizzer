Polycodium melanocarpum

Polycodium melanocarpum, commonly known as the black-fruited polycodium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often edible parts. The plant is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, heart-shaped leaves. Its most notable feature is the production of dark, almost black berries that ripen in late summer.

These berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various wildlife species.
