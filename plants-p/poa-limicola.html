Poa limicola

Poa limicola, commonly known as saltmeadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to coastal regions.

It thrives in saline and brackish environments, often found in salt marshes and tidal flats. The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand high salt concentrations and frequent flooding.

Poa limicola plays a crucial role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
