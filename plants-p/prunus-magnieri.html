Prunus magnieri

Prunus magnieri, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Japanese flowering cherry, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, often in shades of white, pink, or red. The tree has slender, branching structure and produces small, edible fruits known as cherries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and turn yellow in the fall.

Prunus magnieri is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and parks around the world.
