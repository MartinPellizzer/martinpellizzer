Potentilla pensylvanica var. ovium

Potentilla pensylvanica var. ovium, commonly known as the Pennsylvania cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, open fields, and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
