Poinsettia inornata

Poinsettia inornata, commonly known as the white poinsettia, is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts, which are modified leaves that surround small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, also known as the spurge family. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Poinsettia inornata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant, particularly during the holiday season.
