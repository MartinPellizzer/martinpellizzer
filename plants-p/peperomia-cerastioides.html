Peperomia cerastioides

Peperomia cerastioides, commonly known as the coral bells peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to Central and South America.

It features dense, upright clusters of small, ovate leaves that are usually dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces delicate, pinkish-white spathes that enclose tiny, inconspicuous flowers, typical of the Piperaceae family. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate watering.

Due to its attractive foliage and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
