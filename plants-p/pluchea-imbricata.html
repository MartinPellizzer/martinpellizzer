Pluchea imbricata

Pluchea imbricata, commonly known as the creeping groundsel, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing herbaceous species that typically forms dense mats on the ground. The plant features small, yellowish-green composite flowers arranged in clusters, which are characteristic of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.
