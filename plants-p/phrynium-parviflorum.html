Phrynium parviflorum

Phrynium parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial with broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Phrynium parviflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
