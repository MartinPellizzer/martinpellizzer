Phlox hentzii

Phlox hentzii, commonly known as Hentz's phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to purple flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen in mild climates.

Phlox hentzii is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
