Polypodium taliense

Polypodium taliense, commonly known as Taliense Shield Fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and fronds that are typically divided into pinnate segments. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in clusters on the underside of its fertile fronds.

Polypodium taliense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
