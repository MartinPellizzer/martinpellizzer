Potentilla × rhipidophylla

Potentilla × rhipidophylla, commonly known as the silver leaf potentilla, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
