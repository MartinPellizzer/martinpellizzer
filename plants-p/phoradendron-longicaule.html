Phoradendron longicaule

Phoradendron longicaule, commonly known as the long-stemmed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered together. The plant relies on its host for water and some nutrients, while it photosynthesizes to obtain other essential resources. Its berries are typically red or yellow and serve as a food source for birds and other animals.

The plant's unique growth habit and ecological interactions make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
