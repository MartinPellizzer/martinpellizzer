Piper halconense

Piper halconense, commonly known as the Halcon pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America, particularly in Mexico and Guatemala. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used by indigenous communities for various therapeutic purposes.
