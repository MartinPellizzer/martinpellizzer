Polygala nuttalliana

Polygala nuttalliana, commonly known as the Nuttall's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces delicate, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
