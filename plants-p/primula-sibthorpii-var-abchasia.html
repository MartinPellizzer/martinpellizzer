Primula sibthorpii var. abchasia

Primula sibthorpii var. abchasia, commonly known as the Abkhazian primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region, particularly Abkhazia.

It is a herbaceous perennial characterized by its rosette of velvety, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
