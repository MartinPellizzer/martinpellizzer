Panicum commutatum var. consanguineum

Panicum commutatum var. consanguineum, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and reddish-purple inflorescences that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
