Polygonum nakaii

Polygonum nakaii, commonly known as Japanese knotweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, stout stems that often have a reddish hue, and its heart-shaped leaves that are usually reddish or purple in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, appearing in late summer. It is known for its aggressive growth and ability to spread rapidly, making it an invasive species in many regions.

Polygonum nakaii thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along riverbanks and roadsides.
