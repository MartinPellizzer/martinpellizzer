Panicum denudatum

Panicum denudatum, commonly known as the naked panicgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in erosion control and as a forage crop.
