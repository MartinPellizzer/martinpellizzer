Pueraria caerulea

Pueraria caerulea, commonly known as blue cowpea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant features compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces cylindrical pods containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.

Pueraria caerulea thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight.
