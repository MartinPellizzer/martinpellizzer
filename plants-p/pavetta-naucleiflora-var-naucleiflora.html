Pavetta naucleiflora var. naucleiflora

Pavetta naucleiflora var. naucleiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pavetta, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, elliptic to lanceolate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The species is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.
