Peponidium venulosum

Peponidium venulosum, commonly known as the veined sponge gourd, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, veined leaves and elongated, sponge-like fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which have a mild, slightly sweet flavor. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms, and it relies on pollinators for reproduction.

The vine is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb, making it a common sight in tropical gardens and agricultural settings.
