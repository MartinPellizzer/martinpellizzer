Plantago psyllium var. bracteosa

Plantago psyllium var. bracteosa, commonly known as the bracted plantain, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that develop into capsules containing fine, white seeds. The plant is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is adapted to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is closely related to other species of the genus Plantago.

The bracted plantain is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
