Podocarpium podocarpum var. mandshuricum

Podocarpium podocarpum var. mandshuricum, commonly known as the Manchurian yew, is a coniferous evergreen shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in spirals around the stems. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, red aril. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
