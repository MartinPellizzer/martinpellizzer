Polycarena namaensis

Polycarena namaensis, commonly known as the Namib Desert grass, is a low-growing perennial plant adapted to the extreme arid conditions of the Namib Desert.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves that help reduce water loss through transpiration. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep underground water sources.

Polycarena namaensis plays a vital role in stabilizing the sandy soils of its habitat and providing minimal shelter for small desert animals.
