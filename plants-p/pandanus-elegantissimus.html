Pandanus elegantissimus

Pandanus elegantissimus, commonly known as the bottlebrush palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and its distinctive, feathery leaves that form a dense, umbrella-like canopy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, which are elongated, spike-like inflorescences. These flowers are pollinated by insects and birds, leading to the development of hard, woody fruits containing numerous seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional crafts and construction in its native regions.
