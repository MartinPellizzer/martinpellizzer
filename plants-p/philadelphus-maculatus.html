Philadelphus maculatus

Philadelphus maculatus, commonly known as the spotted mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated margin. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy blooms and pleasant fragrance, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
