Polygala pulchella f. major

Polygala pulchella f. major, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its striking, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

The showy milkvetch is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
