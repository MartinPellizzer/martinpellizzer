Padellus mahaleb

Padellus mahaleb, commonly known as the Mahaleb cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, edible fruits resembling cherries. The fruits are typically harvested in late summer and are used in culinary applications.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental and fruit-bearing plant in gardens.
