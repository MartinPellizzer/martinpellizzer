Pentanema persicum

Pentanema persicum, commonly known as Persian sandthrift, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its hardiness, it is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control in dry environments.
