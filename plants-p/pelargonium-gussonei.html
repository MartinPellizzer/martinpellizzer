Pelargonium gussonei

Pelargonium gussonei, commonly known as the Gussone's geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and purple. The leaves are typically rounded with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium gussonei is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
