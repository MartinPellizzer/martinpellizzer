Primula legendrei

Primula legendrei, commonly known as the Legendre's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

Primula legendrei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
