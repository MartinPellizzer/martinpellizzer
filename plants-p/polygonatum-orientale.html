Polygonatum orientale

Polygonatum orientale, commonly known as the Chinese Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features rhizomatous stems and lanceolate, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbel-like clusters during the summer months. Its berries are dark blue or black when mature and are often used in traditional medicine.

Polygonatum orientale is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in herbal remedies.
