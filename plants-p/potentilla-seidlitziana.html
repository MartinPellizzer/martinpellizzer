Potentilla seidlitziana

Potentilla seidlitziana, commonly known as the Seidlitz's cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant features dark green, serrated leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Potentilla seidlitziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
