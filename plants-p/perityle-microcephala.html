Perityle microcephala

Perityle microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed perityle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes with cool, moist conditions. Its small, compact stature and dense foliage make it well adapted to its harsh habitat.
