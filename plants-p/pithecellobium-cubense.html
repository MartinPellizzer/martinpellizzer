Pithecellobium cubense

Pithecellobium cubense, commonly known as the Cuban legume or Cuban acacia, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for erosion control, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
