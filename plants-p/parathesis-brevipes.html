Parathesis brevipes

Parathesis brevipes, commonly known as the short-footed parathesis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in loose inflorescences, and range in color from white to pale pink.

Parathesis brevipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its subtle beauty.
