Phyllanthus glaucus

Phyllanthus glaucus, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Phyllanthus glaucus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
