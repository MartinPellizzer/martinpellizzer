Paeonia albiflora f. pilifera

Paeonia albiflora f. pilifera, commonly known as the white peony with hairy leaves, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered with fine, silky hairs, giving it its specific epithet "pilifera.". It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in traditional medicine.
