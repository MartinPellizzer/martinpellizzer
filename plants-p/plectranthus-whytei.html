Plectranthus whytei

Plectranthus whytei, commonly known as the African violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often velvety and heart-shaped. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of violet, pink, or white, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a favored ornamental plant in both gardens and containers.
