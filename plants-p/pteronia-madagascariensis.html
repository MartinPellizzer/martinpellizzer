Pteronia madagascariensis

Pteronia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar pteronia, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow or orange inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
