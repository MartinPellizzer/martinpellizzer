Phytelephas aequatorialis

Phytelephas aequatorialis, commonly known as the areca palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. It produces yellowish-white flowers that are arranged in dense, paniculate inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, spiny capsule that contains a single seed, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
