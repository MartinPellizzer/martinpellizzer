Phaseolus vulgaris convar. carinatus

Phaseolus vulgaris convar. carinatus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a cultivated variant of the common bean.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical pods and vibrant red or orange flowers. The plant is a climbing vine with compound leaves and produces edible seeds enclosed in fibrous pods. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is widely grown for both its edible beans and ornamental value.
