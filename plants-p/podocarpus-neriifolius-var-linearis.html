Podocarpus neriifolius var. linearis

Podocarpus neriifolius var. linearis, commonly known as the linear-leaved umbrella pine, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dense forests or along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its unique foliage structure.
