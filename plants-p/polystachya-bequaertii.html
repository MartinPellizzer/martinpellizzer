Polystachya bequaertii

Polystachya bequaertii, commonly known as the Bequaert's spikegrass, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with elongated inflorescences. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically greenish or yellowish in color, often clustered in spikes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
