Premna sumatrana

Premna sumatrana, commonly known as Java nettle or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with tubular corollas, typically in shades of purple or white. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its leaves and stems are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
