Pistacia terebinthus subsp. palaestina

Pistacia terebinthus subsp.

palaestina, commonly known as the Palestine pistachio, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with 5 to 9 leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, stony fruits containing a single seed. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

The subspecies is notable for its historical and cultural significance in the region.
