Pouteria hondurensis

Pouteria hondurensis, commonly known as the Honduran sapote, is a tropical tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, glossy green leaves. The tree produces yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by oval, orange-yellow fruits with a sweet, creamy pulp. The fruit is often used in culinary applications, while the tree is also valued for its timber.

Pouteria hondurensis thrives in humid, warm climates and is commonly found in lowland tropical forests.
