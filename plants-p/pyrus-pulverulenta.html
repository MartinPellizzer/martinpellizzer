Pyrus pulverulenta

Pyrus pulverulenta, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, sweet fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is often cultivated in gardens and orchards.
