Phaius tahitensis

Phaius tahitensis, commonly known as the Tahitian ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, tropical environments and is often found in marshy or swampy areas. Its leaves are broad and sword-shaped, and the flowers are usually yellow or orange with a distinctive red lip.

Phaius tahitensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
