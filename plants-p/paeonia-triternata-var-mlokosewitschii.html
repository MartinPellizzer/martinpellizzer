Paeonia triternata var. mlokosewitschii

Paeonia triternata var. mlokosewitschii, commonly known as the Mlokosewitsch peony, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the mountains of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, large, and showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant features compound, trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed and have a distinctive appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.
