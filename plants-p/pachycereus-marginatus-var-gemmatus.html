Pachycereus marginatus var. gemmatus

Pachycereus marginatus var. gemmatus, commonly known as the Mexican moon cactus, is a succulent cactus native to Mexico.

It features a swollen, ribbed stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant typically has a short, cylindrical body with spines and produces vibrant, showy flowers in yellow or orange. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in containers.

Its varietal form, gemmatus, is distinguished by its unique growth pattern and coloration.
