Phragmites nigricans

Phragmites nigricans, commonly known as black reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, dark green blades and hollow, jointed stems. This plant often forms dense stands in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to colonize large areas rapidly.

Phragmites nigricans plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
