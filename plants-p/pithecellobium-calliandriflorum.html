Pithecellobium calliandriflorum

Pithecellobium calliandriflorum, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces clusters of showy, yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils with moderate to high humidity.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in agroforestry systems.
