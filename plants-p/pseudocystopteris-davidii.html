Pseudocystopteris davidii

Pseudocystopteris davidii, commonly known as the David's false water fern, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
