Pneumatopteris subappendiculata

Pneumatopteris subappendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate water fern, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, thread-like fronds that are typically submerged or floating in freshwater environments. The plant has a distinctive rhizome that is often covered in small, appendage-like structures, giving it its specific epithet. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many ferns.

This species is often found in slow-moving streams and ponds, where it plays a role in aquatic ecosystem dynamics.
