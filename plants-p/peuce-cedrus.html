Peuce cedrus

Peuce cedrus, commonly known as the Atlas cedar, is a coniferous tree native to the Atlas Mountains of North Africa.

It belongs to the genus Cedrus in the family Pinaceae, and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, evergreen foliage. The tree produces small, yellowish-green cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, and its wood is valued for its durability and aromatic properties.

Peuce cedrus plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
