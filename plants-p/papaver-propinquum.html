Papaver propinquum

Papaver propinquum, commonly known as the lesser poppy, is a species of flowering plant in the family Papaveraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in meadows and along woodland edges. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and covered in fine hairs.

The plant is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
