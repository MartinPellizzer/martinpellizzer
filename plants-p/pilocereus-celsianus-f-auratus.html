Pilocereus celsianus f. auratus

Pilocereus celsianus f. auratus, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a hybrid cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in white to pale green spines. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
