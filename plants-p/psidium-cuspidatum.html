Psidium cuspidatum

Psidium cuspidatum, commonly known as the pineapple guava, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It produces small, fragrant fruits that resemble pineapples in appearance and have a sweet, juicy flavor. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

The flowers are typically white or pale green, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.
