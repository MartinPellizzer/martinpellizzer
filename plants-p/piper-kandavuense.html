Piper kandavuense

Piper kandavuense, commonly known as the Kandavu pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in the islands of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is valued for its peppery flavor and is used in traditional cuisines and herbal remedies.
