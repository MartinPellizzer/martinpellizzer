Podophyllum emeiense

Podophyllum emeiense, commonly known as Emei Podophyllum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Podophyllaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions of Sichuan province.

Due to its medicinal properties and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species.
