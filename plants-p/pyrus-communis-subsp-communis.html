Pyrus communis subsp. communis

Pyrus communis subsp.

communis, commonly known as the common pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae. It is characterized by its deciduous leaves, which are alternate, ovate, and serrated.

The plant produces white or pink flowers in spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically pear-shaped and have a sweet or tart flavor. It is native to Europe and western Asia, and it has been widely cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and is often used in both landscaping and agriculture.
