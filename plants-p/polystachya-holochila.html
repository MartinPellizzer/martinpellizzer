Polystachya holochila

Polystachya holochila, commonly known as the golden spike rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and dense, spike-like inflorescences.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems covered in fine, hair-like structures, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a spiral pattern. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, clustered in dense spikes, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Polystachya holochila is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in wetland conditions.
