Peltogyne lecointei

Peltogyne lecointei, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its straight, durable wood and aromatic scent. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

The tree is valued for its timber and plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
