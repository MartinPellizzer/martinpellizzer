Pleopeltis variabilis

Pleopeltis variabilis, commonly known as the variable huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, strap-like leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its sporangia are arranged in clusters on the underside of the leaves, a feature common among ferns.

Pleopeltis variabilis is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in tropical and subtropical ecosystems.
