Pachypodium lamerei

Pachypodium lamerei, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant has a distinctive crown of spines and often features a colorful, non-photosynthetic top, which is grafted onto a green rootstock. Its leaves are typically small and may be reduced to spines, while its flowers are showy and appear in clusters.

Pachypodium lamerei is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
