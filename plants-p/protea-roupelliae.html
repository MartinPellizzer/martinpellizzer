Protea roupelliae

Protea roupelliae, commonly known as the Roupell's king protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers surrounded by a series of bracts. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, a unique biome known for its biodiversity.

Protea roupelliae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also an important species in local ecosystems.
