Polygonum tripterocarpum

Polygonum tripterocarpum, commonly known as the three-lobed knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and often triangular leaves. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers that appear in dense clusters during the summer months. Its stems are typically erect and covered with fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and used in traditional medicine.

Polygonum tripterocarpum thrives in moist, shady environments such as forests and stream banks.
