Picris aculeata

Picris aculeata, commonly known as goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, bristly edges, and the flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant in gardens.
