Pericaulon × microphylum

Pericaulon × microphylum, commonly known as the hybrid rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are usually bright yellow, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

Pericaulon × microphylum is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
