Prunus jamasakura f. katsumii

Prunus jamasakura f. katsumii, commonly known as the Katsumii cherry, is a botanical variant of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree typically grows to a moderate height, with a rounded crown and smooth, grey bark. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated edge, turning a vibrant green during the growing season.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
