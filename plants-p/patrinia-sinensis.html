Patrinia sinensis

Patrinia sinensis, commonly known as Chinese violet or false violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of blue or purple. Its flowering period typically occurs in the summer months, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Patrinia sinensis is also used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
