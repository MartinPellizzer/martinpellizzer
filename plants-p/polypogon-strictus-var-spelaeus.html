Polypogon strictus var. spelaeus

Polypogon strictus var. spelaeus, commonly known as the cave panicgrass, is a rare grass species adapted to subterranean environments.

It thrives in dark, humid caves and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, compact inflorescences. The plant exhibits specialized morphological features, such as reduced leaf surfaces and elongated nodes, to maximize light absorption in low-light conditions. Its reproductive structures are often modified to ensure efficient pollination in its unique habitat.

This variant is of particular interest to botanists due to its evolutionary adaptations to extreme underground ecosystems.
