Prosopis elephantina

Prosopis elephantina, commonly known as the Indian date palm, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Indian subcontinent and parts of the Middle East. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery, compound leaves that are adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing small, sweet, edible fruits that are highly valued in traditional cuisines.

The tree is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it an important species for soil enrichment in degraded lands.
