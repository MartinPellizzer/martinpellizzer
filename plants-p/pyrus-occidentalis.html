Pyrus occidentalis

Pyrus occidentalis, commonly known as the western pear.

It is a deciduous tree native to western North America, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible pears that ripen in late summer. Its fruit is typically sweet and aromatic, with a firm, juicy texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
