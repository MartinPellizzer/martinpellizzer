Parthenocissus

Parthenocissus, commonly known as Virginia creeper, is a climbing vine native to North America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which typically have five to seven lobes, and its ability to adhere to surfaces using tendrils. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright red berries that persist into winter. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland areas or along fences.

Its rapid growth and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping.
