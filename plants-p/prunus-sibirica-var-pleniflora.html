Prunus sibirica var. pleniflora

Prunus sibirica var. pleniflora, commonly known as the Siberian cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, yellowish-red berries. Its leaves are oval-shaped with finely serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in certain regions.
