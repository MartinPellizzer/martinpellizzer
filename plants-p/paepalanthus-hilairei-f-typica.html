Paepalanthus hilairei f. typica

Paepalanthus hilairei f. typica, commonly known as the Hilaire's pincushion cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small, compact cactus with a dense cluster of spiny, tuberculate stems. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators. Its structure is adapted to arid environments, with reduced leaves and a thick, fleshy stem for water storage.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
