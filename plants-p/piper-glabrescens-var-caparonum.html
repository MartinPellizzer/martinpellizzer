Piper glabrescens var. caparonum

Piper glabrescens var. caparonum, commonly known as the Caparonum pepper or wild pepper, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped, glossy leaves. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by elongated, peppery fruits that are typically harvested when mature.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and peppery flavor, often used in traditional cuisines and herbal remedies.
