Physodium corymbosum var. acuminatum

Physodium corymbosum var. acuminatum, commonly known as the acuminated physodium, is a variant of the physodium plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The varietal distinction lies in its more pointed leaf tips and slightly different flowering patterns compared to the standard physodium.
