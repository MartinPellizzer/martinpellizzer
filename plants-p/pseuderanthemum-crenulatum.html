Pseuderanthemum crenulatum

Pseuderanthemum crenulatum, commonly known as the crested pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that have serrated edges. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
