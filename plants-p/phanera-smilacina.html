Phanera smilacina

Phanera smilacina, commonly known as the climbing smilacina, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Smilacaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

Phanera smilacina is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
