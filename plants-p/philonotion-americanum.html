Philonotion americanum

Philonotion americanum, commonly known as the American sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its botanical characteristics make it a key component of wetland ecosystems.
