Pseuderanthemum palauense

Pseuderanthemum palauense, commonly known as the Palau beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the Caroline Islands.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature, containing seeds enclosed in a fleshy pulp. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
