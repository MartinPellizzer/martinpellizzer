Pavia × neglecta

Pavia × neglecta, commonly known as the hybrid pavia, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds.

Its botanical interest lies in its hybrid origin and adaptability to various soil types, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
