Pelargonium patentissimum

Pelargonium patentissimum, commonly known as the broad-leaved geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, rounded, and often hairy leaves that are arranged in a broad, spreading manner. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. It is a herbaceous perennial, often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its foliage is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
