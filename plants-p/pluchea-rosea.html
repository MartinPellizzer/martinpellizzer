Pluchea rosea

Pluchea rosea, commonly known as roseflower or roseweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces achene fruits with pappus for wind dispersal.

Pluchea rosea is also noted for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
