Phyllanthus microphyllus

Phyllanthus microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits containing single seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
