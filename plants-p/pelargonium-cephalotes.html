Pelargonium cephalotes

Pelargonium cephalotes, commonly known as the Cape marigold, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The leaves are typically lobed and have a rough texture, often covered in fine hairs. This plant is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
