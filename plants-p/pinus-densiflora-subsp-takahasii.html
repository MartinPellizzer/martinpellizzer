Pinus densiflora subsp. takahasii

Pinus densiflora subsp.

takahasii, commonly known as the Japanese black pine, is a subspecies native to southern Japan. It is a tall evergreen conifer with long, slender needles that are dark green and often have a slight bluish tinge.

The tree produces cone-shaped seed clusters that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing winged seeds dispersed by the wind. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged texture that becomes more pronounced with age.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its hardiness and aesthetic features.
