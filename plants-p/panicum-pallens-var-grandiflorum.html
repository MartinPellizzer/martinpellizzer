Panicum pallens var. grandiflorum

Panicum pallens var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the largeflower panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and loose, feathery inflorescences. The plant produces small, pale to whitish flowers that are often clustered in panicles, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

This variety is known for its drought tolerance and ability to withstand poor soil conditions.
