Phagnalon viride

Phagnalon viride, commonly known as green phagnalon, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads. Native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in disturbed or marginal environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological succession in its native habitats.
