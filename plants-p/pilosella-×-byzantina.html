Pilosella × byzantina

Pilosella × byzantina, commonly known as the Byzantine hawkweed, is a hybrid species within the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver- or gray-hairy leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.

Pilosella × byzantina is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and roadsides.
