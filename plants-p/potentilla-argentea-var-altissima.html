Potentilla argentea var. altissima

Potentilla argentea var. altissima, commonly known as silver potentilla or silver cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, fuzzy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
