Pyrus insulsa

Pyrus insulsa, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during late spring, followed by small, round fruits that are edible but not commonly cultivated.

It is often found in forested areas and thrives in well-drained soils with partial to full sunlight.
