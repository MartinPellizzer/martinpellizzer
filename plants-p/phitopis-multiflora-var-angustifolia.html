Phitopis multiflora var. angustifolia

Phitopis multiflora var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phitopis, is a flowering plant characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers.

It is native to specific regions in East Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant's leaves are typically lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a pointed tip. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
