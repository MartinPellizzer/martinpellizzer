Pleurothallis scapha

Pleurothallis scapha, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with a distinctive pouch-like structure that aids in pollination.

The botanical features of Pleurothallis scapha make it a striking and sought-after orchid among horticultural enthusiasts.
