Poa × austrohercynica

Poa × austrohercynica, commonly known as the hybrid meadow grass, is a sterile hybrid species resulting from the cross between Poa annua and Poa trivialis.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often hairy blades. The plant typically thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, ditches, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose to dense panicle, bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

Botanically, it is notable for its genetic diversity and adaptability to various wetland habitats.
