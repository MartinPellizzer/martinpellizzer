Primula verticillata

Primula verticillata, commonly known as the clustered primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features rosettes of oval, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy and arranged in a compact cluster. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, white, or pink, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

Primula verticillata is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a decorative plant in shady garden beds.
