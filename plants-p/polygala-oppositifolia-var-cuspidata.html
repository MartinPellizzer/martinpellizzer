Polygala oppositifolia var. cuspidata

Polygala oppositifolia var. cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are opposite, narrow, and often have a pointed tip, giving the plant its specific epithet. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

This plant is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
