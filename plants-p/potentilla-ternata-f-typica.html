Potentilla ternata f. typica

Potentilla ternata f. typica, commonly known as the typical form of the shrub potentilla, is a deciduous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, arching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rocky or alpine gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
