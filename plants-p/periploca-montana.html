Periploca montana

Periploca montana, commonly known as the mountain periploca, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries. Its bark is thick and corky, and it is often found growing in rocky or dry soils.

Periploca montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
