Physalis divaricata

Physalis divaricata, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or golden berry, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It produces small, orange-yellow fruits enclosed in a papery calyx, which becomes inflated and persistent after maturity. The plant features hairy, glandular leaves and stems, typical of the Solanaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The fruit is edible, with a tart flavor, and is used in culinary and medicinal applications.
