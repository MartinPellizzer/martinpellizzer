Pilosella incana

Pilosella incana, commonly known as the hairy groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are hairy and grayish-green, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Pilosella incana is often found in temperate climates and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
