Penstemon marcusii

Penstemon marcusii, commonly known as Marcus's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to violet. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Penstemon marcusii is also known for its ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
