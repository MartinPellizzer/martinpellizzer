Pigea

Pigea, commonly known as the common pigea, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to various regions in Australia and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a glossy surface, while its flowers are clustered in inflorescences.

Pigea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
