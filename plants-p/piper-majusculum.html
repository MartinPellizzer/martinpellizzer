Piper majusculum

Piper majusculum, commonly known as the giant pepper plant, is a tropical vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, warm climates. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It produces berries that mature to a bright red color, which are edible and have a peppery flavor.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
