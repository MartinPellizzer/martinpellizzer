Pandanus infundibuliformis

Pandanus infundibuliformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear fragrant, yellowish flowers. Its fibrous leaves are used in traditional crafts and basket weaving.

It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove forests or near the shoreline.
