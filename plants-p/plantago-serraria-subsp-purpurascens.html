Plantago serraria subsp. purpurascens

Plantago serraria subsp.

purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-hairy plantain, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 50 cm.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and a dense covering of fine, purple-tinged hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in a spike and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
