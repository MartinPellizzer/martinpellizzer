Piper laurinum

Piper laurinum, commonly known as the West Indian pepper or laurier, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species, and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by red, peppercorn-like berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
