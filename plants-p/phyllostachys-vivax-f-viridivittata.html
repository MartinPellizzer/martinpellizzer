Phyllostachys vivax f. viridivittata

Phyllostachys vivax f. viridivittata, commonly known as the green-striped bamboo, is a variant of the Phyllostachys vivax species.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and a distinctive green stripe running along the length of its culms. The plant is native to eastern Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage is typically a combination of green and yellow, creating an attractive visual effect.

This ornamental bamboo is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
