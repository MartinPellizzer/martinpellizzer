Polypodium taenifrons

Polypodium taenifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaf huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, strap-like leaves that are typically arranged in a compact rosette. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including China, India, and Southeast Asia. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices.

Polypodium taenifrons is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
