Penstemon caelestinus

Penstemon caelestinus, commonly known as skyblue penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and dry environments, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Penstemon caelestinus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
