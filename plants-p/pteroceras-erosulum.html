Pteroceras erosulum

Pteroceras erosulum, commonly known as the slender thornless acanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive midrib. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in dense spikes at the top of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Pteroceras erosulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
