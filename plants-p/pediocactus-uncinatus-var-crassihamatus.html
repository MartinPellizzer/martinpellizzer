Pediocactus uncinatus var. crassihamatus

Pediocactus uncinatus var. crassihamatus, commonly known as the thick-stemmed hooked cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its robust, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. Its thick stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

This variety is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
