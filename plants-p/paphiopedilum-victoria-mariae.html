Paphiopedilum victoria-mariae

Paphiopedilum victoria-mariae, commonly known as the Victoria Mariae lady's slipper, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the mountainous regions of Borneo.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple petals and a distinctive, pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid, thriving in shaded, humid environments with well-draining soil. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
