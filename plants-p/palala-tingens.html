Palala tingens

Palala tingens, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety red petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and emit a subtle fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Palala tingens is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in traditional medicine.
