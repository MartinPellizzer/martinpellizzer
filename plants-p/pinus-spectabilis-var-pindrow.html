Pinus spectabilis var. pindrow

Pinus spectabilis var. pindrow, commonly known as the Pindrow pine, is a variant of the Himalayan blue pine native to the higher elevations of the western Himalayas.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its blue-green needles and distinctive, often twisted, cone shapes. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments with poor soil conditions. Its foliage provides dense shade and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

The species plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, supporting diverse wildlife and contributing to forest ecosystems.
