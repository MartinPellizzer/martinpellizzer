Palaquium zeylanicum

Palaquium zeylanicum, commonly known as the Ceylon tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine. Its seeds are often used in local cuisines, while the bark and leaves have been traditionally employed for their medicinal properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its shade and aesthetic qualities.
