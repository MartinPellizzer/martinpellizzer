Pedilanthus diazlunanus

Pedilanthus diazlunanus, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble trumpets, which bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, grayish bark and produces small, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in dry forests and savannas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
