Penstemon angustifolius var. vernalensis

Penstemon angustifolius var. vernalensis, commonly known as the spring penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and tall spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing vivid purple to pink hues that attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
