Papaver uintaense

Papaver uintaense, commonly known as Uinta poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red petals and a prominent, orange-colored seed capsule. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and limited distribution.
