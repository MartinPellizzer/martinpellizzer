Piper lacunosum var. glabricaule

Piper lacunosum var. glabricaule, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed pepper plant, is a variant of the lacunosum species within the Piper genus.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its small, clustered flowers are usually greenish-white, and it produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

This variant is valued for its aromatic properties and is often cultivated for both ornamental and practical purposes.
