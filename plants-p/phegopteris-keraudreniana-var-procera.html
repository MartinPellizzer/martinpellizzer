Phegopteris keraudreniana var. procera

Phegopteris keraudreniana var. procera, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant has a unique structure with a central stem covered in a fibrous sheath, and its fronds are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
