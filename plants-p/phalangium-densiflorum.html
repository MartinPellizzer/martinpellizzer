Phalangium densiflorum

Phalangium densiflorum, commonly known as the dense-flowered phalangium, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces numerous tiny, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems.
