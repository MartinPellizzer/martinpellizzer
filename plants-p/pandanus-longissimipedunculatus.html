Pandanus longissimipedunculatus

Pandanus longissimipedunculatus, commonly known as the long-stalked pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its large, strap-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. It produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.
