Pandanus exiguus

Pandanus exiguus, commonly known as the little pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its stiff, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered at the base of the leaves. Its roots are fibrous and can be used for making ropes and mats.

Pandanus exiguus thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
