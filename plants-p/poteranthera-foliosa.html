Poteranthera foliosa

Poteranthera foliosa, commonly known as the leafy poteranthera, is a member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, with five petals and a bell-shaped corolla.

Poteranthera foliosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
