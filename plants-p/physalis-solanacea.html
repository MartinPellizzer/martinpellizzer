Physalis solanacea

Physalis solanacea, commonly known as the common groundcherry, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits enclosed in a papery calyx, which resemble tiny tomatoes. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

Physalis solanacea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
