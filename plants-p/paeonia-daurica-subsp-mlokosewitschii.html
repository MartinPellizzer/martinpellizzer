Paeonia daurica subsp. mlokosewitschii

Paeonia daurica subsp.

mlokosewitschii, commonly known as the Daurian peony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It features rhizomatous growth and produces showy, double or semi-double flowers in shades of pink, white, and red.

The plant has palmately lobed leaves with a hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and hardy nature in temperate climates.
