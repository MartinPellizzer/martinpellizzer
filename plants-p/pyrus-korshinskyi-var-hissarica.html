Pyrus korshinskyi var. hissarica

Pyrus korshinskyi var. hissarica, commonly known as the Hissar pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin and a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart pome fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential as a fruit-bearing plant in suitable environments.
