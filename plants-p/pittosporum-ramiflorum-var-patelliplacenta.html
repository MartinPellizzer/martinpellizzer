Pittosporum ramiflorum var. patelliplacenta

Pittosporum ramiflorum var. patelliplacenta, commonly known as the Patelliplacenta Pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a leathery texture. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.

It prefers a sunny to partially shaded location and is relatively low-maintenance once established.
