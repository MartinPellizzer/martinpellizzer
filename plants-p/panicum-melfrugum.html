Panicum melfrugum

Panicum melfrugum, commonly known as the red panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open fields and disturbed areas.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems as a habitat for various insects and small animals.
