Passiflora × lemicheziana

Passiflora × lemicheziana, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a hybrid plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple and white flowers with intricate structures, including a crown of filaments and a central corona. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tropical flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.

Passiflora × lemicheziana is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
