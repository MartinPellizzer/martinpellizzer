Papaver vagum

Papaver vagum, commonly known as the Alpine poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and covered in fine hairs.

Papaver vagum is a perennial species that blooms in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant splash of color to high-altitude ecosystems.
