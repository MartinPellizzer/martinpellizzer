Phyllagathis tuberculata

Phyllagathis tuberculata, commonly known as the tuberculate myrtle, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, and often glandular leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink in color. Its fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Phyllagathis tuberculata is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
