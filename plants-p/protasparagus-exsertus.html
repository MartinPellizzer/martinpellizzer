Protasparagus exsertus

Protasparagus exsertus, commonly known as the slender asparagus, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fibrous roots are adapted for anchoring in rocky or sandy soils.

Protasparagus exsertus is often found in arid or semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained environments.
