Phalangium elongatum

Phalangium elongatum, commonly known as the slender centipede plant, is a unique member of the Asparagales order.

It is characterized by its elongated, segmented stems that resemble the body of a centipede, hence its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or green. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its distinctive appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies.
