Piper brevipedicellatum

Piper brevipedicellatum, commonly known as the short-stemmed pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a dark red color, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its foliage is characterized by heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and a distinct, peppery scent.

Piper brevipedicellatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
