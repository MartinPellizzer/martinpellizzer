Paracaryum lamprocarpum

Paracaryum lamprocarpum, commonly known as the silverleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and reeds, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces distinctive, silver-colored bracts that give it its common name.

Paracaryum lamprocarpum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland environments.
