Polygonum douglasii var. microspermum

Polygonum douglasii var. microspermum, commonly known as the small-seeded smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, stream banks, and marshy areas. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers with five petals. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The variety microspermum is distinguished by its smaller, more numerous seeds compared to other varieties of Polygonum douglasii.
