Pteris × caridadiae

Pteris × caridadiae, commonly known as the Caridadia fern, is a hybrid species within the genus Pteris, characterized by its distinctive fronds and adaptability to various environments.

It is a fern that thrives in moist, shaded conditions, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features bipinnate fronds with a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are typically arranged in lines on the underside of the fronds.

Pteris × caridadiae is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
