Piper bidentatum

Piper bidentatum, commonly known as the two-toothed pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus in the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature.

Piper bidentatum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
