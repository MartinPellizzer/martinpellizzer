Primula elatior var. corcontica

Primula elatior var. corcontica, commonly known as the Corcontina primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features erect, slender stems that bear clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
