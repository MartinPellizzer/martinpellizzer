Poa seslerioides

Poa seslerioides, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish-brown flowers.

Poa seslerioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
