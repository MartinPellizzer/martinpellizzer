Psidium arasa-pe

Psidium arasa-pe, commonly known as the Brazilian guava or arasa-pe, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible berries that are typically yellow or red when ripe. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, aromatic flavor and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
