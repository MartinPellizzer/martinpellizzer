Podanthum cordifolium

Podanthum cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved podanthum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its elegant foliage and delicate floral display.
