Pseuderanthemum chaponense

Pseuderanthemum chaponense, commonly known as the Chapon's false anthemum, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble true flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are usually clustered in dense inflorescences, and they bloom in shades of red, orange, or yellow, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant display.
