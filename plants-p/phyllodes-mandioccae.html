Phyllodes mandioccae

Phyllodes mandioccae, commonly known as the giant silky oak, is a tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, feathery, light green phyllodes that are often used as ornamental foliage. The plant has a robust root system and can thrive in a variety of soil types. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody pods containing seeds.

The species is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often planted in gardens and landscapes.
