Persica ispahanensis

Persica ispahanensis, commonly known as the Ispahan peach, is a species of peach native to the region of Isfahan in Iran.

It is characterized by its large, sweet, and aromatic fruits with a distinctive pinkish-red skin. The plant produces white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its characteristic fruit. Persica ispahanensis is valued for its exceptional flavor and is often cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.

Its botanical features make it a notable example of the diversity within the Prunus genus.
