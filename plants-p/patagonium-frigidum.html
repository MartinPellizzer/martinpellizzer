Patagonium frigidum

Patagonium frigidum, commonly known as the Antarctic pearlwort, is a hardy plant adapted to extreme cold environments.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming species that thrives in the harsh conditions of the Antarctic and sub-Antarctic regions. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid, icy conditions. It produces tiny white flowers that bloom during the brief summer season.

Patagonium frigidum plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems in its extreme habitat.
