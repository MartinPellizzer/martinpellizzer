Pachyrhizus vernalis var. typicus

Pachyrhizus vernalis var. typicus, commonly known as the spring pachyrhizus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its robust, herbaceous stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible tubers that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a temperate climate with adequate rainfall.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
