Prunus lichoana

Prunus lichoana, commonly known as the Lichoana cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape with a smooth margin. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its fruits are small, round drupes that turn from green to red or black as they mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
