Pleurothallis quadriserrata

Pleurothallis quadriserrata, commonly known as the four-toothed orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers with intricate, serrated petals. Its unique floral structure and specific habitat requirements make it a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its limited distribution and vulnerability to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
