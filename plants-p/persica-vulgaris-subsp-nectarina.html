Persica vulgaris subsp. nectarina

Persica vulgaris subsp.

nectarina, commonly known as the nectarine, is a deciduous fruit tree belonging to the Rosaceae family. It is a subspecies of the peach, characterized by its fuzzy skin and sweet, juicy flesh.

The flowers are typically white or pink, and they bloom in the spring before the fruit develops. The fruit is usually smaller than a peach and has a more intense flavor.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
