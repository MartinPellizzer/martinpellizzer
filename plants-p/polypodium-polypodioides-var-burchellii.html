Polypodium polypodioides var. burchellii

Polypodium polypodioides var. burchellii, commonly known as the Burchell's shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to southern Africa.

It typically grows on rocks or trees, forming dense, compact foliage with lanceolate, glossy leaves. The fronds are usually divided into pairs of pinnae, which are often curled at the base. It prefers shaded, humid environments and is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
