Polygonum interruptellum

Polygonum interruptellum, commonly known as the narrowleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its bead-like seeds and distinctive leaf shapes. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, ditches, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers in late summer.

The species is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
