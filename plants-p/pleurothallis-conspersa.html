Pleurothallis conspersa

Pleurothallis conspersa, commonly known as the speckled orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces fragrant blooms in shades of white and yellow.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
