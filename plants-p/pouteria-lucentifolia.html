Pouteria lucentifolia

Pouteria lucentifolia, commonly known as the golden spondias, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and compound leaves with elongated leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, orange-red fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor.

Its fruits are often consumed fresh or used in local cuisines, and the tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
