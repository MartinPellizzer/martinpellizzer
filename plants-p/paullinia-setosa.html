Paullinia setosa

Paullinia setosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a member of the Sapotaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical South America. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, elliptical leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a yellowish, fleshy drupe.
