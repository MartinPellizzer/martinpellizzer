Prunella speciosa

Prunella speciosa, commonly known as the showy blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are square and often branched.

Prunella speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
