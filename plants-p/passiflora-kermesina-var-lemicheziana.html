Passiflora kermesina var. lemicheziana

Passiflora kermesina var. lemicheziana, commonly known as the Lemicheziana passionflower, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that feature a blend of red, yellow, and blue hues. The plant produces fleshy, edible berries that are often consumed by local wildlife and humans. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
