Plantago lanceolata var. latifolia

Plantago lanceolata var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

This species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
