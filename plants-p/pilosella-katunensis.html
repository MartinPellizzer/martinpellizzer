Pilosella katunensis

Pilosella katunensis, commonly known as the Katun Alps groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Eastern Alps.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant has small, yellowish-white ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. Pilosella katunensis typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its foliage is covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.
