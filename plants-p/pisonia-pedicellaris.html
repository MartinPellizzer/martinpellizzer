Pisonia pedicellaris

Pisonia pedicellaris, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often covered in a fine, white powder. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found growing on limestone substrates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
