Prunus divaricata f. nigra

Prunus divaricata f. nigra, commonly known as the black cherry tree, is a decorative shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in early spring. The leaves are ovate with a pointed tip and are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
