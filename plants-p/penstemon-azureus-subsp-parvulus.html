Penstemon azureus subsp. parvulus

Penstemon azureus subsp.

parvulus, commonly known as the blue penstemon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features slender, upright stems and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
