Platyaechmea tessmannii

Platyaechmea tessmannii, commonly known as the Tessmann's plumea, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, arching inflorescence that bears numerous small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that form a rosette, creating a central cup that collects rainwater. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Platyaechmea tessmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
