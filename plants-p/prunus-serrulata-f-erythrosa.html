Prunus serrulata f. erythrosa

Prunus serrulata f. erythrosa, commonly known as the crimson cherry or Japanese flowering cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus serrulata species.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to gardens and landscapes. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 8 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces small, edible fruits in the fall.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly used in horticulture for its seasonal beauty.
