Pleomele reflexa var. heyneana

Pleomele reflexa var. heyneana, commonly known as the Hainan cycad, is a unique cycad species native to the tropical regions of China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a dense crown of long, strap-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, and its seed cones are woody and contain hard, angular seeds.

Due to its slow growth and limited natural range, it is considered a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of interest in botanical conservation efforts.
