Panicum martinicense

Panicum martinicense, commonly known as Martin's panicum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a bluish-green hue and a hairy surface.

Panicum martinicense is often found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions.
