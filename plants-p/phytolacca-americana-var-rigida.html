Phytolacca americana var. rigida

Phytolacca americana var. rigida, commonly known as the rigid pokeweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features upright, slender stems that are often reddish in color and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark purple berries that contain toxic seeds. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in woodland edges and along roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it easily identifiable, though caution is advised due to its toxic properties.
