Panicum chrysostachyum

Panicum chrysostachyum, commonly known as golden panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based flowers.

Panicum chrysostachyum is often used in ornamental horticulture for its striking color and is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
