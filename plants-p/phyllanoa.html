Phyllanoa

Phyllanoa, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive silvery-gray foliage.

The leaves are typically narrow and elongated, with a dense covering of fine hairs that give them their characteristic shimmer. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its botanical features include small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions, where it has adapted to withstand drought conditions.
