Primula capitata subsp. capitata

Primula capitata subsp.

capitata, commonly known as the Alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and bears clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple.

The plant produces numerous stamens and a prominent pistil, typical of the Primulaceae family. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
