Phlomis amanica

Phlomis amanica, commonly known as narrowleaf plumevetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces tall spikes of pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its fibrous root system makes it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.
