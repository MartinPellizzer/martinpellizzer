Phyllanthus introductus

Phyllanthus introductus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small shrub characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
