Pedilonum delicatulum

Pedilonum delicatulum, commonly known as the delicate footplant, is a rare and delicate species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, intricately veined leaves that are often adorned with fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the wet season. Its root system is fibrous and shallow, allowing it to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, Pedilonum delicatulum is considered a vulnerable species.
