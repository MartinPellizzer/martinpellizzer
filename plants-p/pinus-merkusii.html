Pinus merkusii

Pinus merkusii, commonly known as the Sumatran pine, is a coniferous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves, which are typically arranged in bundles of five. The tree produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in mountainous regions.

The wood of Pinus merkusii is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.
