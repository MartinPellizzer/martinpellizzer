Palmijuncus monoecus

Palmijuncus monoecus, commonly known as the single-sex palm, is a unique species of flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its monoecious nature, meaning it has both male and female reproductive structures on the same individual. The plant features a slender, unbranched stem with a crown of elongated, sword-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing at the base of the leaf sheaths.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
