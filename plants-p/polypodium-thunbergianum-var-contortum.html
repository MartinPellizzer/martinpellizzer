Polypodium thunbergianum var. contortum

Polypodium thunbergianum var. contortum, commonly known as the twisted fern, is a variant of the common holly fern.

It is a perennial evergreen fern native to East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant features fronds that are typically twisted or curled, giving it its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fronds are leathery and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter, making it a striking addition to shaded gardens.
