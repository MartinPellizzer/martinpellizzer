Populus × canadensis var. serotina

Populus × canadensis var. serotina, commonly known as the lateleaf poplar, is a hybrid species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including moist and poorly drained sites. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth margin and a slightly serrated edge. This variety is known for its late-flushing foliage, which contributes to its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
