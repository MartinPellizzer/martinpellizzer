Parsonsia pseudosilene

Parsonsia pseudosilene, commonly known as the golden flame or paradise flower, is a striking tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red flowers, which are tubular and attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant belongs to the Apocynaceae family, known for its milky sap and often toxic compounds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its stems are woody and branched.

Parsonsia pseudosilene thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland forest areas.
