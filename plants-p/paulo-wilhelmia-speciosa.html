Paulo-wilhelmia speciosa

Paulo-wilhelmia speciosa, commonly known as the showy poinsettia, is a species native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its true flowers are small and clustered in a structure called a cyathium, surrounded by colorful leaf-like structures.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental choice during the holiday season.
