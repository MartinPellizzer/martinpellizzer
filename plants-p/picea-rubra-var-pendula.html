Picea rubra var. pendula

Picea rubra var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping red spruce, is a cultivar of the red spruce species native to the Appalachian Mountains.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that cascade downward, creating a unique, flowing appearance. The foliage consists of dark green, needle-like leaves that are slightly reddish when young. This variety thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers cool, moist climates.

It is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
