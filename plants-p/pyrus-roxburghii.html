Pyrus roxburghii

Pyrus roxburghii, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that ripen in late summer.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production, thriving in temperate climates with well-drained soil.
