Padus racemosa var. dolichocarpa

Padus racemosa var. dolichocarpa, commonly known as the long-fruited white crabsapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves and clusters of white, fragrant flowers that appear in spring. The fruit is a small, yellowish-red drupe that matures in late summer, often persisting into winter. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along streambanks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
