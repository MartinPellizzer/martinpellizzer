Phyllorkis cirrhata

Phyllorkis cirrhata, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, aiding in water retention in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming during the warmer months. Its berries are usually red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the health of its host trees if left unchecked.
