Psidium coriaceum var. obovatum

Psidium coriaceum var. obovatum, commonly known as the obovate guava, is a variant of the guava plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, obovate-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor. The fruit typically has a smooth, thin skin and is rich in vitamins and antioxidants.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
