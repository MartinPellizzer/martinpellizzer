Persea aurata

Persea aurata, commonly known as the golden persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, orange to yellow fruits that are edible when fully ripe. The fruit's skin turns a striking golden color as it matures, which is a key identifier.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also cultivated for its unique, sweet-tart fruit.
