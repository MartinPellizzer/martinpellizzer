Petroselinum sativum convar. radicosum

Petroselinum sativum convar. radicosum, commonly known as Italian parsley, is a biennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely divided, bright green leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant has a taproot system, which allows it to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are widely used in cooking for their aromatic and slightly peppery flavor.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties, including its ability to aid digestion and reduce inflammation.
