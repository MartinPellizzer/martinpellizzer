Passiflora bangii

Passiflora bangii, commonly known as the Bangi passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that display a blend of blue, purple, and white petals. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Passiflora bangii is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its potential medicinal properties.
