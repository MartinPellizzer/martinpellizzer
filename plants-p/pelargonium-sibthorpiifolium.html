Pelargonium sibthorpiifolium

Pelargonium sibthorpiifolium, commonly known as the Sibthorpiifolium geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The leaves are typically lobed and have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
