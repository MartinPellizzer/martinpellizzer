Pelexia spiranthoides

Pelexia spiranthoides, commonly known as the spiral gentian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant is part of the Gentianaceae family and is known for its showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers moist, wooded habitats and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

Pelexia spiranthoides is a perennial herb that blooms in late spring to early summer.
