Phreatia padangensis

Phreatia padangensis, commonly known as the Padang orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant typically produces a single, elongated flower spike bearing several showy, fragrant orchid flowers. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Phreatia padangensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
