Paeonia humilis var. gallica

Paeonia humilis var. gallica, commonly known as the Gallic peony, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has sturdy, upright stems covered in heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed. Its roots are thick and fleshy, adapted to store moisture in its arid native habitat.

The Gallic peony is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
