Passiflora perakensis

Passiflora perakensis, commonly known as the Perak passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or white, with a distinctive crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora perakensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and potential medicinal properties.
