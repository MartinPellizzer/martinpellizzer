Plantago incana var. capitellata

Plantago incana var. capitellata, commonly known as the woolly plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a basal rosette of soft, hairy, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces a tall, flowering stem topped with a dense, spike-like inflorescence composed of small, greenish flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their soothing properties.

This species is adaptable to various soil types and thrives in disturbed environments such as roadsides and fields.
