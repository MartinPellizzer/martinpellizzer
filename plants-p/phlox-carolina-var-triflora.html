Phlox carolina var. triflora

Phlox carolina var. triflora, commonly known as the Carolina phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically reaches heights of 1 to 3 feet and features lance-shaped, dark green leaves. Its flowers are usually pink to lavender in color, with five petals and a prominent central gland.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.
