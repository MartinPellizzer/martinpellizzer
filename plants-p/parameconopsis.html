Parameconopsis

Parameconopsis, commonly known as the sea hare, is a type of marine gastropod mollusk belonging to the family Aplysiidae.

It is characterized by its soft, gelatinous body and a distinctive, often colorful mantle that can resemble a hare's ear. Parameconopsis species are herbivorous, feeding primarily on algae and other plant matter.

Their reproductive system includes both male and female organs, allowing for both self-fertilization and cross-fertilization.
