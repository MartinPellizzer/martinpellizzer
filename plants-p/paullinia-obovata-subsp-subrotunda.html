Paullinia obovata subsp. subrotunda

Paullinia obovata subsp.

subrotunda, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree or *Schinus terebinthifolius*, is a species native to South America. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub with simple, alternate, obovate to elliptic leaves.

The leaves are typically glabrous, with a glossy surface and a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into bright red, fleshy berries.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.
