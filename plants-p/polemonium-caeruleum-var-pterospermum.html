Polemonium caeruleum var. pterospermum

Polemonium caeruleum var. pterospermum, commonly known as bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
