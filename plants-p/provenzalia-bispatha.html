Provenzalia bispatha

Provenzalia bispatha, commonly known as the two-lobed sandwort, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Provenzalia bispatha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
