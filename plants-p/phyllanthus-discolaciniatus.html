Phyllanthus discolaciniatus

Phyllanthus discolaciniatus, commonly known as the two-colored leaf spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that often display a distinct two-tone coloration, typically green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. Its flowers are unisexual and clustered in cyathia, a feature typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is adapted to grow in a variety of habitats, including moist soils and disturbed areas.
