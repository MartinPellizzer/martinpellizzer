Panax maralia

Panax maralia, commonly known as the American ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, yellowish root system that is highly valued in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, preferring rich, well-drained soil. Its leaves are compound, with lance-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the summer.

The plant is known for its adaptogenic properties and has been used for centuries to enhance vitality and support immune function.
