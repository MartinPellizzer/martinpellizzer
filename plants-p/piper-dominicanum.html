Piper dominicanum

Piper dominicanum, commonly known as the Dominican pepper or black pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, containing the seeds used for spice. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
