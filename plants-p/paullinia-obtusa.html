Paullinia obtusa

Paullinia obtusa, commonly known as the Brazilian soursop or Brazilian guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to soursop but have a distinct flavor. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
