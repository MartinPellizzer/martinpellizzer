Promenaea ovatiloba var. robertii

Promenaea ovatiloba var. robertii, commonly known as the Robert's broadleaf palm, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in diameter. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers arranged in a panicle, followed by orange-red fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
