Piper dilatatum var. pseudodilatatum

Piper dilatatum var. pseudodilatatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, opposite leaves. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in its native regions.
