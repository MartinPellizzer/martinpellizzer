Panicum boliviense

Panicum boliviense, commonly known as Bolivian millet, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
