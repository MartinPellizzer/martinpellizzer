Pilosella cymosa var. pubescens

Pilosella cymosa var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are also pubescent, contributing to its overall hairy texture.

It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.
