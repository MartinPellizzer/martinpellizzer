Pachira

Pachira, commonly known as the money tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its thick, trunk-like stem and large, waxy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits that contain seeds. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Pachira is also popular for its symbolic association with good fortune and prosperity.
