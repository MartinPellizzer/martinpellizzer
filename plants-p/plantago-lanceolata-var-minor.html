Plantago lanceolata var. minor

Plantago lanceolata var. minor, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including lawns, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
