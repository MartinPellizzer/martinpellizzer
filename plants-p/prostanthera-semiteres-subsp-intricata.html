Prostanthera semiteres subsp. intricata

Prostanthera semiteres subsp.

intricata, commonly known as the intricate narrow-leaved mintbush, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. Prostanthera semiteres subsp.

intricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to native wildlife.
