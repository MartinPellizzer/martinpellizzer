Phaseolus acinaciformis

Phaseolus acinaciformis, commonly known as the sword bean, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, sword-shaped pods. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with trifoliate leaves and clusters of small white or pale purple flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, pointed pods and are often used in traditional cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
