Polypodium pilosum

Polypodium pilosum, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The fronds are usually bipinnate, with a dense arrangement of small, linear pinnae.

Polypodium pilosum is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid conditions.
