Paracelsia obovata

Paracelsia obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate paracelsia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its lanceolate to obovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
