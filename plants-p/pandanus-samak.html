Pandanus samak

Pandanus samak, commonly known as the bottle brush pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces distinctive inflorescences that resemble bottle brushes, consisting of numerous small, fragrant flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and thatching.

The plant thrives in coastal and wetland habitats, preferring well-drained, sandy soils.
