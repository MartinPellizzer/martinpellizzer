Phyllostachys nidularia

Phyllostachys nidularia, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a slightly wavy margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Phyllostachys nidularia is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
