Peperomia purpurella

Peperomia purpurella, commonly known as the purple peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy, and often purple-tinged leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, emerging from the leaf axils. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia purpurella is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
