Pachypleurum xizangense

Pachypleurum xizangense, commonly known as the Yunnan spurge, is a unique plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas, particularly in Tibet and Sichuan.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its stout, fleshy stems and dark green, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine regions.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
