Populus trichocarpa subsp. hastata

Populus trichocarpa subsp.

hastata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cottonwood, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrower than those of the typical *Populus trichocarpa*.

The plant produces catkins as its primary floral structure, with separate male and female flowers. It thrives in riparian zones and is adapted to moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological and genetic studies in the genus *Populus*.
