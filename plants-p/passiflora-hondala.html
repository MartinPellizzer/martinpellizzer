Passiflora hondala

Passiflora hondala, commonly known as the Honduran passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces blue or violet berries that are edible and contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Passiflora hondala is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
