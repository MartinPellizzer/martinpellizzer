Pisum sativum var. gratiosum

Pisum sativum var. gratiosum, commonly known as the graceful pea, is a cultivated variety of the common pea.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant produces edible pods containing seeds that are often used in culinary applications. Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.

Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy.
