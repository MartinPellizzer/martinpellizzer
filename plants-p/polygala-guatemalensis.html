Polygala guatemalensis

Polygala guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers are usually greenish-white or yellowish, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds with silky hairs.

Polygala guatemalensis is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
