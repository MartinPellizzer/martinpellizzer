Poa lipskyi

Poa lipskyi, commonly known as the Lypsky's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green blades and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats in rocky or alpine environments. The plant typically grows in high-altitude areas with cool, moist conditions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters of small, blue-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Poa lipskyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
