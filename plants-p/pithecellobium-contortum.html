Pithecellobium contortum

Pithecellobium contortum, commonly known as the twisted pod soursop, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, serpentine pods. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are typically elongated and have a rough, spiny exterior, containing edible seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
