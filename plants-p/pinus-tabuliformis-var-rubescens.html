Pinus tabuliformis var. rubescens

Pinus tabuliformis var. rubescens, commonly known as the red-stained pine, is a variant of the Chinese pine native to northern China.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown bark and distinctive, flat, plate-like scales on its trunk. The leaves are needle-like, typically growing in pairs, and are a dark green color. This variety is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
