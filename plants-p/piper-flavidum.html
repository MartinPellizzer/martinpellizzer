Piper flavidum

Piper flavidum, commonly known as the yellow pepper plant, is a species of flowering vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant produces small, yellow berries that are often used in local cuisines for their spicy flavor. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy appearance.

Piper flavidum is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
