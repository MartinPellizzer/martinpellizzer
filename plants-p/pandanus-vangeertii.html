Pandanus vangeertii

Pandanus vangeertii, commonly known as the Solomon Islands pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the genus Pandanus in the family Pandanaceae, characterized by its distinctive leaves and inflorescences. The plant features long, narrow, fibrous leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. Its flowers are typically clustered in a spadix-like structure and are surrounded by a colorful bract.

Pandanus vangeertii is adapted to coastal and sandy environments, making it resilient to salt spray and wind.
