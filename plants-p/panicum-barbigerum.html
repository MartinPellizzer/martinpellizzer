Panicum barbigerum

Panicum barbigerum, commonly known as the bearded panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the warmer regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bear long, bristly hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous narrow, awn-tipped spikelets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its fibrous root system helps prevent soil erosion, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
