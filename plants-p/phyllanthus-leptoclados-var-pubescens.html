Phyllanthus leptoclados var. pubescens

Phyllanthus leptoclados var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellowish-green flowers that appear in axillary clusters. The plant has a woody stem and is typically shrub-like in habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
