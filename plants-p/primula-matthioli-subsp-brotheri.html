Primula matthioli subsp. brotheri

Primula matthioli subsp.

brotheri, commonly known as the Alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple.

The plant typically blooms in spring and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
