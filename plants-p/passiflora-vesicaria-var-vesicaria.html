Passiflora vesicaria var. vesicaria

Passiflora vesicaria var. vesicaria, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves and produces striking, showy flowers with a unique structure of blue and white petals, sepals, and a crown of filaments. The plant bears orange-red berries that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species in the Passifloraceae family.
