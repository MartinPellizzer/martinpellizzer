Phaseolus semierectus var. gracilis

Phaseolus semierectus var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender runner bean, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It produces elongated, slender pods that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when mature. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers. It is valued for its edible beans, which are rich in protein and nutrients.

This variety is often grown in temperate climates for both its culinary and ornamental qualities.
