Panicum miliiforme

Panicum miliiforme, commonly known as proso millet, is a warm-season grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, feather-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains. This plant is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for cultivation in arid environments. It is an important crop for animal feed and human consumption, particularly in parts of Asia and Africa.

The plant's rapid growth and short growing season make it a valuable crop for sustainable agriculture.
