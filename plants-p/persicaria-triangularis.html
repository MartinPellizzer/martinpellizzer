Persicaria triangularis

Persicaria triangularis, commonly known as narrowleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its triangular stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming from late summer into early autumn. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Persicaria triangularis is a member of the Polygonaceae family, known for its distinctive seed pods that split open to release seeds.
