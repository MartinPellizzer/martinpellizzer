Polygonum davuricum

Polygonum davuricum, commonly known as the Siberian knotweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Polygonum davuricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
