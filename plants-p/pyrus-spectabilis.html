Pyrus spectabilis

Pyrus spectabilis, commonly known as the showy pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

Pyrus spectabilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
