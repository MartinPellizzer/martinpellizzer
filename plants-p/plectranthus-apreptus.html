Plectranthus apreptus

Plectranthus apreptus, commonly known as the Cape fennel, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive aromatic scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and compact growth habit.
