Piper sarculatum

Piper sarculatum, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries, which are edible but have a mild, peppery flavor.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
