Poa lanigera var. stuckertii

Poa lanigera var. stuckertii, commonly known as the Stuckert's hairy grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose to dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or brown.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
