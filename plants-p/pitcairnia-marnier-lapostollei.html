Pitcairnia marnier-lapostollei

Pitcairnia marnier-lapostollei, commonly known as the Marnier-Lapostolle pitcairnia, is a bromeliad native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central tank to collect water, a feature common among bromeliads. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences with vibrant red and orange flowers that attract pollinators. Its foliage is typically dark green with a waxy texture, and it thrives in bright, indirect light.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in both indoor and outdoor gardens.
