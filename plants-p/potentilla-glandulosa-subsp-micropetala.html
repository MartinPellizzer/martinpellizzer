Potentilla glandulosa subsp. micropetala

Potentilla glandulosa subsp.

micropetala, commonly known as the small-leaved cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It features upright growth with narrow, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, glandular hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and hardy choice for native plant gardens.
