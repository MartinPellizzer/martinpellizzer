Perfoliata quinquedentata

Perfoliata quinquedentata, commonly known as the five-toothed leaf plant, is a perennial herb characterized by its distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have five teeth along the margins, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Perfoliata quinquedentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
