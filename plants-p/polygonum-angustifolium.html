Polygonum angustifolium

Polygonum angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf smartweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, three-lobed seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across various habitats.

Polygonum angustifolium is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
