Primula cadinensis

Primula cadinensis, commonly known as the Ceylon primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments and thrives in shaded woodland areas. Its leaves are elliptical and have a smooth texture, while the stems are often hairy.

Primula cadinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
