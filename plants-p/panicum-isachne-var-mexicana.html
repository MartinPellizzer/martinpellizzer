Panicum isachne var. mexicana

Panicum isachne var. mexicana, commonly known as Mexican dropseed, is a native grass species found in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage that turns coppery in the fall. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often mistaken for cotton, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

Due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements, it is well-suited for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
