Polygonum acuminatum

Polygonum acuminatum, commonly known as the sharp-tipped knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The plant features upright, branched stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lanceolate with a pointed tip. Small, greenish-white flowers appear in dense clusters during the summer months.

It is often found in moist habitats such as stream banks and wet meadows.
