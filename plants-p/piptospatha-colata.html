Piptospatha colata

Piptospatha colata, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist, sandy soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and somewhat fibrous.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
