Prunus incisa f. micrantha

Prunus incisa f. micrantha, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically a vibrant green during the growing season. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, tart red fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to temperate climates.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
