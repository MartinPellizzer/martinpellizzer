Pedicularis comosa subsp. schizocalyx

Pedicularis comosa subsp.

schizocalyx, commonly known as the hairy lousewort, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China. It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy leaves and stems, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral structure and the presence of specialized glands on its leaves.
