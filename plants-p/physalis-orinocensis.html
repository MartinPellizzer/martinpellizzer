Physalis orinocensis

Physalis orinocensis, commonly known as the Orinoco husk tomato, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive inflated calyx that encases the fruit. The plant produces small, orange-yellow berries that are covered by a papery, inflated husk. The leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and the flowers are typically yellow with five lobes.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
