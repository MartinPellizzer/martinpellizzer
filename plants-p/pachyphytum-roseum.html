Pachyphytum roseum

Pachyphytum roseum, commonly known as the Roseate Stonecrop, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that store water, typical of succulent plants. The plant produces vibrant pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and striking color make it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
