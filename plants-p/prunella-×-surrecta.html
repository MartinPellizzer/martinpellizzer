Prunella × surrecta

Prunella × surrecta, commonly known as the common selfheal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, two-lipped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Prunella × surrecta is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its medicinal properties.

It is also used in traditional herbal remedies for its anti-inflammatory and wound-healing properties.
