Priestleya tomentosa

Priestleya tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly priestleya, is a species of flowering plant in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to the high elevations of the Andes Mountains in South America, particularly in regions of Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, hairy foliage, and showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
