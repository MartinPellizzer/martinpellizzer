Paphinia neudeckeri var. mocoaensis

Paphinia neudeckeri var. mocoaensis, commonly known as the Mocoa paphiopedilum, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a vibrant pink or purple hue with intricate patterns. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its humid, shaded environment. Its leaves are elongated and leathery, adapted to withstand the high humidity and frequent rainfall of its habitat.

This orchid is highly sought after by collectors due to its rarity and distinctive floral morphology.
