Polystachya pleistantha

Polystachya pleistantha, commonly known as the orchid with the striking violet-blue flowers, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense clusters of showy, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Polystachya pleistantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
