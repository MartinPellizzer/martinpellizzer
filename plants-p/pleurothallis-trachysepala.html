Pleurothallis trachysepala

Pleurothallis trachysepala, commonly known as the rough-leafed pleurothallis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the cloud forests of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. This species is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, rough texture. The flowers are small and typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a spike-like inflorescence.

It is a terrestrial orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil.
