Phyllanthus avicularis

Phyllanthus avicularis, commonly known as the sand dollar plant, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, clustered leaves that are typically opposite or whorled in arrangement. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits that resemble sand dollars. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
