Psilosiphon erythranthus

Psilosiphon erythranthus, commonly known as the red-flowered lily, is a striking member of the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in cool, temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Psilosiphon erythranthus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
