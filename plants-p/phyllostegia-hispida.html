Phyllostegia hispida

Phyllostegia hispida, commonly known as the hairy phyllostegia, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its opposite, hairy, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, wooded habitats.

Phyllostegia hispida is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other areas with high humidity and well-drained soil.
