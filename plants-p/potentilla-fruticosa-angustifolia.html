Potentilla fruticosa angustifolia

Potentilla fruticosa angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf cinquefoil, is a low-growing shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, angular stems and narrow, elliptical leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and tundra environments.

Its hardy nature makes it an important component of cold-climate ecosystems.
