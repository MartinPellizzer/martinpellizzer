Plectranthus galeatus

Plectranthus galeatus, commonly known as the silverleaf plectranthus, is a herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features oval, silver-gray leaves with a velvety texture, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or blue, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Plectranthus galeatus is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
