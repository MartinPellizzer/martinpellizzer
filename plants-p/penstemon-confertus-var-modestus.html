Penstemon confertus var. modestus

Penstemon confertus var. modestus, commonly known as the modest penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces spikes of tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular plant among native plant enthusiasts.
