Potamogeton sibiricus subsp. subsibiricus

Potamogeton sibiricus subsp.

subsibiricus, commonly known as the Siberian pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in northern regions. It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

This species thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in lakes, rivers, and wetlands. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and stable environmental conditions.
