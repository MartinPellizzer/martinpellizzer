Psychotria tetragona

Psychotria tetragona, commonly known as the "ayahuasca vine," is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of white or pale green flowers. The plant contains various alkaloids, including harmine and tetrahydroprotoberberine, which contribute to its psychoactive properties. It is often used in traditional shamanic practices for spiritual and healing purposes.

Its botanical significance is further enhanced by its role in the preparation of ayahuasca, a ceremonial brew.
