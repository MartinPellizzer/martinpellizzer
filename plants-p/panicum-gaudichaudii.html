Panicum gaudichaudii

Panicum gaudichaudii, commonly known as the golden panicum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and typically grows as a herbaceous annual or perennial. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense clusters of golden-yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight reddish tinge, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Panicum gaudichaudii is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
