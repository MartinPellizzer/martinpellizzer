Plectranthus candelabriformis

Plectranthus candelabriformis, commonly known as the candleplant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, candle-like inflorescence, which consists of multiple whorls of tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

The flowers, which range in color from white to purple, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
