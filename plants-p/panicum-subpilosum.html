Panicum subpilosum

Panicum subpilosum, commonly known as the silky panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive silvery-gray sheen due to fine, soft hairs covering its surface. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential ornamental use.
