Pueraria pulcherrima

Pueraria pulcherrima, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces pods containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures.
