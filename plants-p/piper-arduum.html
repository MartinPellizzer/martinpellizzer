Piper arduum

Piper arduum, commonly known as the "devil's pepper" or "arduum pepper," is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piper genus in the family Piperaceae, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its long, trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its fruits are peppery and have a sharp, pungent flavor, often used in traditional cuisines.

However, the plant is also valued for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses attributed to its leaves and fruits.
