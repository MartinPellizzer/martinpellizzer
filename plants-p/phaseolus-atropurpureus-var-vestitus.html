Phaseolus atropurpureus var. vestitus

Phaseolus atropurpureus var. vestitus, commonly known as the purple hull cowpea, is a cultivated variant of the common cowpea.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black pods and is native to the Americas. The plant is an annual legume that thrives in warm climates and is often grown for its edible seeds. It has a bushy growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers.

The variety is valued for its high protein content and adaptability to various soil types.
