Pachystemon populifolius

Pachystemon populifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf pachystemon, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of poplar trees, hence its common name. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
