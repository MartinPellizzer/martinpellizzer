Palicourea simiarum subsp. simiarum

Palicourea simiarum subsp.

simiarum, commonly known as the black-eyed Susan vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America. It features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy, dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to yellow, often with a prominent dark center. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
