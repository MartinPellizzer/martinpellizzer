Pavonia bangii

Pavonia bangii, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its botanical characteristics include simple, opposite leaves and a preference for well-drained soils.

Pavonia bangii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
