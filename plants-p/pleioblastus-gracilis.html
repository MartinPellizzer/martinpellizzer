Pleioblastus gracilis

Pleioblastus gracilis, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and fine, elongated leaves. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its graceful appearance and ability to form dense clumps. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Pleioblastus gracilis is also valued for its use in traditional landscaping and as a decorative element in gardens.
