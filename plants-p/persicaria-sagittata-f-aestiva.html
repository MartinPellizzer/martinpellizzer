Persicaria sagittata f. aestiva

Persicaria sagittata f. aestiva, commonly known as the summer arrow-leaved bedstraw, is a flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and arrow-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

This variety is distinguished by its earlier blooming period compared to other forms of Persicaria sagittata.
