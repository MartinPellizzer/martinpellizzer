Pendulina crassifolia

Pendulina crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf pendulum plant, is a unique species characterized by its pendulous growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions, where it has adapted to store water in its succulent foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding ornamental value. Its roots are deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Due to its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
