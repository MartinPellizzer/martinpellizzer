Pyrethrum versicolor

Pyrethrum versicolor, commonly known as the variable pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant produces small, spiky seed heads that contain minute, toxic seeds used in natural insecticides. Pyrethrum versicolor thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and ornamental landscaping.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable.
