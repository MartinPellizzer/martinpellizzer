Pinus pinaster var. pendula

Pinus pinaster var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Maritime pine, is a variant of the Maritime pine native to the coastal regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive weeping growth habit, with long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in clusters of two. It produces cones that are often used in horticulture for ornamental purposes.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and adaptability to coastal environments.
