Pachyphyllum echinocarpon

Pachyphyllum echinocarpon, commonly known as the spiny barrel cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green, spiny structure. Its spines, which are modified leaves, provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss.

Pachyphyllum echinocarpon is well adapted to desert conditions, making it a resilient and visually distinctive plant.
