Poa petrophila

Poa petrophila, commonly known as the alpine bluegrass, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the western United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green leaves. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically inconspicuous and wind-pollinated.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity, Poa petrophila is considered a rare and specialized species.
