Phragmites oyyamensis

Phragmites oyyamensis, commonly known as the Oyyamensis reed, is a tall, perennial grass species native to specific regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hollow, cylindrical stems and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. This plant thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of lakes and rivers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands, which can stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some areas where it outcompetes native vegetation.
