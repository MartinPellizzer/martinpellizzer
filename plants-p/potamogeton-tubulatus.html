Potamogeton tubulatus

Potamogeton tubulatus, commonly known as the tubular pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It has long, slender stems that are often branched and bear small, tubular flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams.

Potamogeton tubulatus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
