Pavonia melanommata var. pringleana

Pavonia melanommata var. pringleana, commonly known as the Pringle's wood rose, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of small, showy pink or red flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable plant for gardens in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
