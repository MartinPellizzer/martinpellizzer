Passiflora oviformis

Passiflora oviformis, commonly known as the oval passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that feature a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and filaments. The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong scent.

Passiflora oviformis thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
