Passiflora divaricata

Passiflora divaricata, commonly known as the variable passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that feature intricate patterns of red, yellow, and blue. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tart flavor. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and serrated edges.

Passiflora divaricata thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
