Pleurothallis pelex

Pleurothallis pelex, commonly known as the pelex orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that help it conserve moisture.

The flowers are usually pale green or white, with a delicate appearance that blends well with its natural habitat.
