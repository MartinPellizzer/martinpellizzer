Pilosella fastigiata

Pilosella fastigiata, commonly known as the upright pilosella, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It features slender, upright stems with hairy surfaces, giving it its common name.

Its yellow flowers bloom in early summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
