Phaseolus lathyroides var. hastifolius

Phaseolus lathyroides var. hastifolius, commonly known as the hairy bean, is a climbing legume native to Central and South America.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, curved pods containing several seeds. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun.

This species is valued for its edible seeds and its role in traditional agriculture.
