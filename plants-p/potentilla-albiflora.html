Potentilla albiflora

Potentilla albiflora, commonly known as whiteflower cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or alpine environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, showy blooms.
