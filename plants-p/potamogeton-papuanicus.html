Potamogeton papuanicus

Potamogeton papuanicus, commonly known as the Papuan pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered at the base of the plant.

Potamogeton papuanicus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
