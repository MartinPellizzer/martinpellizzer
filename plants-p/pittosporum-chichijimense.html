Pittosporum chichijimense

Pittosporum chichijimense, commonly known as the Chichijima pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy surface, while its bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to coastal environments.
