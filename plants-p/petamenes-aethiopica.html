Petamenes aethiopica

Petamenes aethiopica, commonly known as the Ethiopian star grass, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
