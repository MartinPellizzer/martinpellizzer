Psychanthus tephrochlamys

Psychanthus tephrochlamys, commonly known as the ash-leaf psychanthus, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving them a distinctive ash-gray appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Psychanthus tephrochlamys is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
