Poa badensis var. nabelekii

Poa badensis var. nabelekii, commonly known as the Nabelek's bluegrass, is a variant of the bluegrass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its native range.
