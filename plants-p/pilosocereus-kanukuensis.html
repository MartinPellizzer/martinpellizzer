Pilosocereus kanukuensis

Pilosocereus kanukuensis, commonly known as the Kanuku cactus, is a species of cactus native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, white spines, which provide protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom at night, typically in shades of yellow or white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
