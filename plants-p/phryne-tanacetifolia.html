Phryne tanacetifolia

Phryne tanacetifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal umbels. These flowers are followed by oval, woody berries that turn red when mature.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.
