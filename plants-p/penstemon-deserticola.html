Penstemon deserticola

Penstemon deserticola, commonly known as the desert penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky soils and dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of tubular, violet to purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its deep root system allows it to access water from deeper soil layers, enhancing its drought tolerance.

Penstemon deserticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
