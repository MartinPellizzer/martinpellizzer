Phacelia pulchella var. sabulonum

Phacelia pulchella var. sabulonum, commonly known as the sand phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It has small, hairy leaves and a low-growing habit, making it well adapted to harsh conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
