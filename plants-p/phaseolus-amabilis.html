Phaseolus amabilis

Phaseolus amabilis, commonly known as the sweet bean or the pink bean, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces showy, pink or white flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous properties. Its pods contain edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines.

The botanical features of Phaseolus amabilis make it both ornamental and agriculturally significant.
