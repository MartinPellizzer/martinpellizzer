Piper cordilimbum

Piper cordilimbum, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a member of the Piperaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and emits a strong, peppery odor when crushed. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces berries that are initially green and turn red when mature.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other parts of the world, sometimes becoming invasive.
