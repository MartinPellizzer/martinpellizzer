Pyramidocarpus petiolaris

Pyramidocarpus petiolaris, commonly known as the Hawaiian candlewood, is a member of the family Arecaceae, or palms.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by clustered, woody fruits that are typically reddish in color. Native to the Hawaiian Islands, it thrives in coastal and lowland habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
