Phlomis bracteosa var. longifolia

Phlomis bracteosa var. longifolia, commonly known as long-leaved hemp-nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems covered in soft, grayish hairs and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped, often with serrated edges, and are arranged alternately along the stem. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive addition to gardens and a valuable species for ecological studies.
