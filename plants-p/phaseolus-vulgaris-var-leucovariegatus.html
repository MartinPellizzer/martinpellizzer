Phaseolus vulgaris var. leucovariegatus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. leucovariegatus, commonly known as the white variegated bean, is a cultivar of the common bean species.

It is characterized by its unique variegated leaf patterns, which feature a mix of white and green colors. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces flat, kidney-shaped seeds that are often used in culinary applications.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible beans.
