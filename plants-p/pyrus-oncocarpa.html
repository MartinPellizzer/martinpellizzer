Pyrus oncocarpa

Pyrus oncocarpa, commonly known as the blackcap crabapple, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple to black fruits that mature in late summer. The tree produces white or pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by the distinctive fruit. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge and turn yellow in autumn.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive flowers and berries.
