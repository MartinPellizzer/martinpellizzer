Piper maculosum

Piper maculosum, commonly known as the spotted pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive spotted or mottled pattern on the underside. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by oval, peppery fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and medicinal research.
