Porophyllum hasslerianum

Porophyllum hasslerianum, commonly known as the Hassler's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its blue or purple flowers bloom in clusters.

Porophyllum hasslerianum is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
