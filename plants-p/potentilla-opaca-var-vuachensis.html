Potentilla opaca var. vuachensis

Potentilla opaca var. vuachensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese cinquefoil, is a shrub native to Vietnam.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, rounded leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
