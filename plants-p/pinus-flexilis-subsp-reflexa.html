Pinus flexilis subsp. reflexa

Pinus flexilis subsp.

reflexa, commonly known as the creeping mountain pine, is a subspecies native to the western United States. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit, with needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The cones are small and often remain closed for many years before opening to release seeds. This subspecies is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine and subalpine ecosystems.
