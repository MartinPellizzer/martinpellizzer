Polygonum lineare

Polygonum lineare, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features upright, slender stems that are often reddish in color and covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and they often have a reddish tinge on the underside.

Polygonum lineare is typically found in moist environments such as wetlands, stream banks, and disturbed soils.
