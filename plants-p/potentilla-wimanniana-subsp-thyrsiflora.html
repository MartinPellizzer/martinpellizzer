Potentilla wimanniana subsp. thyrsiflora

Potentilla wimanniana subsp.

thyrsiflora, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing a compact growth habit with many small, upright stems.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are borne on elongated, branching inflorescences called thyrses. Its leaves are usually palmately divided into five narrow, serrated lobes, and the plant has a dense, mat-forming structure.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
