Pyrus esfandiarii

Pyrus esfandiarii, commonly known as the Esfandiari pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly Iran and Afghanistan. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, with lobed leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in early spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like apple, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

This species is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and potential for cultivation in arid environments.
