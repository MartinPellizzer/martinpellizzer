Prenanthes spicata

Prenanthes spicata, commonly known as stiff burrweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a spikelike cluster.

Prenanthes spicata is often found in disturbed habitats and can spread through its hairy, persistent seeds.
