Persicaria remota

Persicaria remota, commonly known as the remote polygonum, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and wetlands. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red. It has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil in wet environments.

Persicaria remota is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
