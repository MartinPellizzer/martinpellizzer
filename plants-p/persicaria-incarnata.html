Persicaria incarnata

Persicaria incarnata, commonly known as redstem filaree, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges.

Persicaria incarnata is a short-lived perennial that spreads by seed and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
