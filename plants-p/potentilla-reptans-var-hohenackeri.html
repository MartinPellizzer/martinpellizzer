Potentilla reptans var. hohenackeri

Potentilla reptans var. hohenackeri, commonly known as the Hohenacker's creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has wiry, spreading stems covered with fine, soft hairs. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets that are slightly toothed.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
