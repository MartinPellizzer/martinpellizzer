Polygala conferta

Polygala conferta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its flowers are usually pale blue or violet, and it produces small, bean-like pods that contain the seeds.

Polygala conferta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering habit.
