Passiflora foetida var. oaxacana

Passiflora foetida var. oaxacana, commonly known as the Oaxacan passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dark green, heart-shaped leaves and striking, fragrant flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a unique crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
