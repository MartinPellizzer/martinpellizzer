Prasophyllum albiglans

Prasophyllum albiglans, commonly known as the white leek orchid, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Prasophyllum albiglans is a perennial species that often forms dense clumps, and it is adapted to survive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
