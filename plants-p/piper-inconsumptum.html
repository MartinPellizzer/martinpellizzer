Piper inconsumptum

Piper inconsumptum, commonly known as the wild pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's structure and growth pattern make it well adapted to humid, shaded environments.
