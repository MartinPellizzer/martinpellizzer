Paeonia × suffruticosa var. humei

Paeonia × suffruticosa var. humei, commonly known as the Hume's tree peony, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces simple, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a serrated margin. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing fragrant flowers that are highly valued in horticulture.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
