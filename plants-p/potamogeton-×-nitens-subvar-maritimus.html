Potamogeton × nitens subvar. maritimus

Potamogeton × nitens subvar. maritimus, commonly known as the salt marsh pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that thrives in brackish and saline wetlands.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. This subvariety is adapted to fluctuating salinity levels and can tolerate both fresh and saltwater environments. It plays a vital role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of healthy coastal or estuarine ecosystems.
