Perovskia linczevskii

Perovskia linczevskii, commonly known as Siberian lavender, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and spikes of blue-violet flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.

The essential oils extracted from its leaves are used in aromatherapy and as a natural insect repellent.
