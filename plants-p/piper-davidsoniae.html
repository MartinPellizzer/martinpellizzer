Piper davidsoniae

Piper davidsoniae, commonly known as the Davidson's pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into fleshy, red berries. Its unique morphology and growth habit make it a distinctive species within its genus.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
