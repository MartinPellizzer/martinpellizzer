Persica vulgaris var. sympyrena

Persica vulgaris var. sympyrena, commonly known as the Sympyrene peach.

This variant is a botanical subspecies of the peach tree, characterized by its distinct morphological features. It typically exhibits a compact growth habit with smaller leaves compared to the standard peach. The flowers are usually white with a subtle pinkish hue, and the fruit is generally smaller and more intensely flavored.

It is native to the Caucasus region and is valued for its ornamental and culinary attributes.
