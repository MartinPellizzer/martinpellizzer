Prunus × kobukuzakura

Prunus × kobukuzakura, commonly known as the Kobukuzakura cherry, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Prunus serrulata and Prunus lannesiana.

It is native to Japan and is celebrated for its stunning, double-flowered blossoms that appear in early spring. The plant typically grows as a small deciduous tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are pale pink to white, with a delicate, fluffy appearance that makes it a popular ornamental plant.

The leaves are oval-shaped, with a smooth texture and a glossy green color, providing an attractive backdrop to the spring blooms.
