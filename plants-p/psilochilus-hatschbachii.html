Psilochilus hatschbachii

Psilochilus hatschbachii, commonly known as the Hatshbach's psilochilus, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees and other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of air humidity and indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and relatively obscure orchid species.
