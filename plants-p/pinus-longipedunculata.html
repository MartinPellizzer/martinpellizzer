Pinus longipedunculata

Pinus longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-shoot pine, is a species of conifer native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in bundles of five. The plant produces cones that are elongated and often hang downward, a feature that distinguishes it from other pine species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with ample sunlight.

The botanical features of Pinus longipedunculata make it an important species for ecological studies and forest management.
