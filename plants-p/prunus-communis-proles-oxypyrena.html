Prunus communis proles oxypyrena

Prunus communis proles oxypyrena, commonly known as the common cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits known as cherries, which are widely cultivated for their culinary and ornamental value. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or gardens.

The bark of the tree is smooth and reddish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
