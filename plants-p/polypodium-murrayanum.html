Polypodium murrayanum

Polypodium murrayanum, commonly known as the Murray's fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as Malaysia, Indonesia, and the Philippines. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests. Its fronds are bipinnate, with a delicate, lace-like appearance, and it produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves.

Polypodium murrayanum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
