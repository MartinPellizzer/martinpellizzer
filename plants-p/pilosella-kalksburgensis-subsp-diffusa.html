Pilosella kalksburgensis subsp. diffusa

Pilosella kalksburgensis subsp.

diffusa, commonly known as the Kalksburg diffusa, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

The subspecies diffusa is distinguished by its more diffuse growth habit compared to the typical form of P. kalksburgensis. Its leaves are typically hairy and lance-shaped, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This plant thrives in rocky and calcareous soils, often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
