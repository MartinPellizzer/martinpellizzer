Paeonia × moutan var. albida-plena

Paeonia × moutan var. albida-plena, commonly known as the white peony, is a cultivar of the peony plant native to China.

It is characterized by its large, double flowers that are predominantly white with a slight pinkish tinge. The plant produces showy, fragrant blooms in early spring, typically from April to May. It has dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged in an alternate pattern.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.
