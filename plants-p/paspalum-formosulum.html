Paspalum formosulum

Paspalum formosulum, commonly known as the Chinese paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, lowland areas and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Paspalum formosulum is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
