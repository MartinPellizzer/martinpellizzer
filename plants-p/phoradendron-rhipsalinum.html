Phoradendron rhipsalinum

Phoradendron rhipsalinum, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees.

It lacks roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its stems, which are covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are often clustered together. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain sticky seeds that adhere to birds, aiding in seed dispersal.

This species is found in various parts of the Americas and plays an important ecological role in its habitat.
