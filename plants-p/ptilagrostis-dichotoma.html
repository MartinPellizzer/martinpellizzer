Ptilagrostis dichotoma

Ptilagrostis dichotoma, commonly known as the two-branched grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often branch dichotomously, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous minute flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and covered with fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry environments.

Ptilagrostis dichotoma is adapted to survive in harsh conditions, making it an important component of its native ecosystems.
