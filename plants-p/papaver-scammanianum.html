Papaver scammanianum

Papaver scammanianum, commonly known as the Persian poppy, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, lobed leaves and grows from a short, stout stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The Persian poppy is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
