Phaca microcystis

Phaca microcystis, commonly known as the blue-green algae, is a type of cyanobacteria that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its filamentous structure and ability to form dense surface blooms. This organism is capable of photosynthesis and can fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a significant primary producer in aquatic ecosystems. However, it can also produce toxins that pose risks to aquatic life and human health.

Its rapid growth and potential for harmful algal blooms make it a subject of ecological and environmental concern.
