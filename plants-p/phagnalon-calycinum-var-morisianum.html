Phagnalon calycinum var. morisianum

Phagnalon calycinum var. morisianum, commonly known as the Morisian mouse-ear, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are small, velvety, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
