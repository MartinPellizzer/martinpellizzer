Parkia paryphosphaera

Parkia paryphosphaera, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white to pink flowers that bloom in pendulous clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility in agricultural settings.
