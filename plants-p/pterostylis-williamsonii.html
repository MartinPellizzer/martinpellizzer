Pterostylis williamsonii

Pterostylis williamsonii, commonly known as the Williamson's hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-like floral structure that resembles a bee in flight, a feature that aids in pollination by mimicking the appearance of a pollinator. The plant typically grows in wet sclerophyll forests and has a slender, upright stem with a single, showy flower. Its flowering period occurs during late winter to early spring, and it relies on specific pollinators such as bees and wasps.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
