Plantago paralias var. cordobensis

Plantago paralias var. cordobensis, commonly known as the Cordoba plantain, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in a spike that emerges from the center of the rosette. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional remedies for respiratory and digestive ailments.
