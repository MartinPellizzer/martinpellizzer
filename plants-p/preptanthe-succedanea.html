Preptanthe succedanea

Preptanthe succedanea, commonly known as the desert star.

It is a succulent plant native to arid regions, characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the dry season, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from underground water sources.

Preptanthe succedanea is well adapted to survive in harsh, low-nutrient environments.
