Potentilla samothracica

Potentilla samothracica, commonly known as the Samothrace cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the Greek island of Samothrake.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Potentilla samothracica is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in full sun conditions.
