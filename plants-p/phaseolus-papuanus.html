Phaseolus papuanus

Phaseolus papuanus, commonly known as the New Guinea bean, is a climbing legume native to the Pacific Islands.

It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typically with a yellow or brown coloration. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are usually white or pale purple. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This species is valued for its edible seeds, which are used in local cuisines across its native range.
