Polygonum balansae subsp. elongatum

Polygonum balansae subsp.

elongatum, commonly known as the elongated knotweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly known as buckwheats and smartweeds.

The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a prominent midrib and a rough texture.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
