Persicaria borneensis

Persicaria borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the island of Borneo.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes species such as buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its upright stems, heart-shaped leaves, and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.
