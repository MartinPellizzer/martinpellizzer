Pelargonium imbutum

Pelargonium imbutum, commonly known as the Natal geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant produces bright red or pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically rounded with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Pelargonium imbutum is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
