Petrophile conifera subsp. divaricata

Petrophile conifera subsp.

divaricata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bottlebrush, is a shrub native to the south-western regions of Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that produce a vibrant red or pink hue.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sun-exposed habitats. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and efficient water use.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
