Phaseolus vulgaris var. mesomelas

Phaseolus vulgaris var. mesomelas, commonly known as the dark-seeded kidney bean, is a variant of the common bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its dark brown seeds, which are typically larger and more robust than those of other bean varieties. The plant produces erect, bushy growth with trifoliate leaves and is often cultivated for its edible beans. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is used in various culinary traditions around the world.
