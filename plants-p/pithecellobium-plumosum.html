Pithecellobium plumosum

Pithecellobium plumosum, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
