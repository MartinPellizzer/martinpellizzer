Piper tortuosipilum

Piper tortuosipilum, commonly known as the curly-hair pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, curling hairs on the stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by dark, peppery fruits.

The plant is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
