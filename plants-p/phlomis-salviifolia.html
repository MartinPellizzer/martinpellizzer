Phlomis salviifolia

Phlomis salviifolia, commonly known as purple mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, covered with dense, woolly gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy clusters of purple or violet flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their soothing properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
