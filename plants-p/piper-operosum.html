Piper operosum

Piper operosum, commonly known as the Java pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into three-winged berries. Its fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, while its leaves are often used in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forested areas.
