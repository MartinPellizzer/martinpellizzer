Primulina bicolor

Primulina bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored primula, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bicolor leaves that typically feature a combination of green and white or silver markings. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, often with a contrasting throat color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage.

Primulina bicolor is valued for its unique leaf patterns and delicate floral display.
