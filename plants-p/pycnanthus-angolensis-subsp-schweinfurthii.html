Pycnanthus angolensis subsp. schweinfurthii

Pycnanthus angolensis subsp.

schweinfurthii, commonly known as the African tallow tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa. It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and dense, rounded crown.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green upper surface and a pale underside. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.
