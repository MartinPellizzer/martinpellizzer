Potamogeton gramineus var. myriophyllus

Potamogeton gramineus var. myriophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, and it relies on water currents for pollination. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

This variant is often found in shallow, slow-moving waters such as ponds and lakes.
