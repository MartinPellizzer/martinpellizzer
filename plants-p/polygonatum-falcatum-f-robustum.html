Polygonatum falcatum f. robustum

Polygonatum falcatum f. robustum, commonly known as the robust Solomon's seal, is a variant of the Solomon's seal plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its robust growth and distinctive, elongated, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and flowering display.
