Primula latifolia subsp. graveolens

Primula latifolia subsp.

graveolens, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive, fragrant scent, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has oval, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a member of the Primulaceae family, which includes many ornamental and ecologically important species.
