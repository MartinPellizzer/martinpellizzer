Pouteria glomerata

Pouteria glomerata, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. The tree's most notable feature is its fruit, which contains a single large, edible seed known as the Brazil nut. The plant thrives in humid, lowland areas with well-drained soil and requires a warm, stable climate.

It plays an important ecological role in the Amazon rainforest, supporting various wildlife species.
