Parodia curvispina

Parodia curvispina, commonly known as the hooked parodia, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white to yellowish spines. The plant produces small, white flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the base of the stems. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its compact size and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
