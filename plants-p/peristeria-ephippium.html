Peristeria ephippium

Peristeria ephippium, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

The plant has a unique structure, with a colorful "upper" part that is actually a grafted variety of the Hylocereus genus, while the lower part is the Peristeria ephippium itself. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse environments.
