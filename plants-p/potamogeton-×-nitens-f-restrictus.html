Potamogeton × nitens f. restrictus

Potamogeton × nitens f. restrictus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a hybrid plant species belonging to the family Potamogetonaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and is typically found in freshwater habitats such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant is submerged or floating, with small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its morphological variability, it can be challenging to distinguish from its parent species in the field.
