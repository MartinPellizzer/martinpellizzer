Potentilla diffusa

Potentilla diffusa, commonly known as shrubby cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, typically consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, which give it the common name "cinquefoil.".

Potentilla diffusa is also known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
