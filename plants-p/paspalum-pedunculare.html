Paspalum pedunculare

Paspalum pedunculare, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, making it a common component of wetland vegetation.

Paspalum pedunculare is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
