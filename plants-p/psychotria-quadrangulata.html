Psychotria quadrangulata

Psychotria quadrangulata, commonly known as the "angel's trumpet" or "devil's fig," is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, quadrangular (four-sided) stems and dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming during the warm season. Its fruit is a fleshy, berry-like capsule that turns from green to red as it matures.

Psychotria quadrangulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
