Plantago veratrifolia

Plantago veratrifolia, commonly known as the yellow plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring. Its seeds are encased in a tough, three-lobed capsule, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties.

It is often used in traditional herbal remedies for its anti-inflammatory and healing benefits.
