Pontederia tricolor

Pontederia tricolor, commonly known as the pickerelweed, is a wetland plant native to the Americas.

It features showy, tri-colored flowers that range from purple to blue and white, typically blooming in summer. The plant has heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often floating or partially submerged. Its roots help stabilize soil in aquatic environments, making it ecologically significant.

It is often found in ponds, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.
