Potentilla leuconota var. borneensis

Potentilla leuconota var. borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Borneo.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understoreys, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are usually compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
