Pittosporum viridiflorum var. commutatum

Pittosporum viridiflorum var. commutatum, commonly known as the golden pittosporum, is a flowering shrub native to New Zealand.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and emit a mild fragrance when crushed. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. These flowers are followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
