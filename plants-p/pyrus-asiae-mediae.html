Pyrus asiae-mediae

Pyrus asiae-mediae, commonly known as the Japanese white pear.

It is a deciduous tree native to Japan, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are sweet and juicy. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is often cultivated in gardens and orchards.
