Paraeremostachys aralensis

Paraeremostachys aralensis, commonly known as the Aral Sea grass, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, narrow leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh, dry conditions. The plant produces tiny, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the short growing season. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor firmly in sandy or saline soils.

This species plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion in its native habitat.
