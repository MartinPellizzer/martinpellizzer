Panicum acuminatum var. wrightianum

Panicum acuminatum var. wrightianum, commonly known as Wright's foxtail, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and ability to form dense clumps.
