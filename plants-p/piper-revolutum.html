Piper revolutum

Piper revolutum, commonly known as the rolling pepper or wild pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small berries that turn red when mature, often used in traditional medicine.

Piper revolutum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
