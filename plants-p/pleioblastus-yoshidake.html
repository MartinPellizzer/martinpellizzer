Pleioblastus yoshidake

Pleioblastus yoshidake, commonly known as the Yoshidake bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to Japan.

It features slender, gracefully arching culms that are typically green with a bluish tinge, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, followed by feathery seed heads. Its leaves are narrow and have a slight waxy coating, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded landscapes.
