Pterostylis galgula

Pterostylis galgula, commonly known as the black orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to blackish flowers, which are often mistaken for fungi due to their appearance. The plant has a slender, wiry stem with a single spike of flowers, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it grows in moist, shaded habitats such as rainforests.

The species is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique and striking floral morphology.
