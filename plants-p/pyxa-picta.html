Pyxa picta

Pyxa picta, commonly known as the painted pyx, is a striking member of the Aizoaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and vibrant colors, which help it survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in summer. Its unique morphology and ability to store water make it well adapted to dry conditions.

Pyxa picta is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
