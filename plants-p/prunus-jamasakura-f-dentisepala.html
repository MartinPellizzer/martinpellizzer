Prunus jamasakura f. dentisepala

Prunus jamasakura f. dentisepala, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom variant with notched sepals, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The foliage is typically dark green, and the leaves are oval with a slightly serrated edge. This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value, particularly for its unique floral structure and seasonal beauty.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sunlight to flourish.
