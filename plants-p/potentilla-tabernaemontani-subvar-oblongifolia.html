Potentilla tabernaemontani subvar. oblongifolia

Potentilla tabernaemontani subvar. oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in rocky soils and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features compound leaves with five elliptical leaflets, which are usually narrow and slightly serrated. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in spherical inflorescences, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude ecosystems.
