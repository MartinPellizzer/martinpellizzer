Poa molinerii var. multiflora

Poa molinerii var. multiflora, commonly known as the multiflora bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces dense, dark green foliage. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences that mature into seeds, contributing to its reproductive success.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
