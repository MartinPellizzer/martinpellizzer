Pyrus cordifolia

Pyrus cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by edible pomes that are typically small and sweet. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

Pyrus cordifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
