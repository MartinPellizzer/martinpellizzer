Papaver heterotricum

Papaver heterotricum, commonly known as the heterotricum poppy, is a member of the Papaveraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-colored petals, which are typically red and orange, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Papaver heterotricum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
