Phragmipedium warszewiczianum var. wallisii

Phragmipedium warszewiczianum var. wallisii, commonly known as the Wallis's phragmipedium, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the high-altitude cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically feature a distinctive yellow lip with intricate patterns. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments with dappled sunlight, making it a challenging but rewarding species for orchid enthusiasts. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited natural distribution.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and rarity.
