Potentilla tormentilla f. divergens

Potentilla tormentilla f. divergens, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically divided into five leaflets, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

This variety is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
