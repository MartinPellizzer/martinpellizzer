Protea madiensis var. intermedia

Protea madiensis var. intermedia, commonly known as the Cape protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous individual flowers arranged in a spherical or cylindrical shape. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in areas with mild, wet winters and dry summers. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and red, making it a popular ornamental species.

Due to its ecological and horticultural significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in floral arrangements.
