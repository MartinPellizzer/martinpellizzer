Potentilla thuringiaca f. humilior

Potentilla thuringiaca f. humilior, commonly known as the dwarf thuringian cinquefoil, is a low-growing, perennial shrub native to central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, wiry stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its compact growth habit and ornamental value.
