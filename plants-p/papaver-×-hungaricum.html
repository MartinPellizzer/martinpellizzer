Papaver × hungaricum

Papaver × hungaricum, commonly known as the Hungarian poppy, is a hybrid species native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, vibrant red or orange petals that surround a prominent, seed-filled capsule. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring smooth, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in meadows and disturbed habitats.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a dramatic visual element to the landscape.
