Pappobolus lanatus

Pappobolus lanatus, commonly known as the woolly burr grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and produces distinctive, spiky seed heads that are adapted for wind dispersal. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats. Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, making it an important pioneer species in disturbed areas.

The genus Pappobolus is part of the grass family, Poaceae, and is known for its unique seed structures.
