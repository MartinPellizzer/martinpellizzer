Pachira cardonae

Pachira cardonae, commonly known as the desert pachira, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic landscapes.

Pachira cardonae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
