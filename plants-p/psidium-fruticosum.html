Psidium fruticosum

Psidium fruticosum, commonly known as the Brazilian guava or wild guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and edible berries. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy texture, while its fruits are small, yellow or red, and have a tangy flavor.

Psidium fruticosum is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
