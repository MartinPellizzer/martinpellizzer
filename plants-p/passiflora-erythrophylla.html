Passiflora erythrophylla

Passiflora erythrophylla, commonly known as the crimson passionflower, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking, colorful flowers with intricate structures. The flowers feature a central crown of filaments surrounded by five sepals and five petals. The plant's fruit is a woody, spiny capsule that contains edible, seed-like structures.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
