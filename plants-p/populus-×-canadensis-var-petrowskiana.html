Populus × canadensis var. petrowskiana

Populus × canadensis var. petrowskiana, commonly known as the Siberian poplar, is a hybrid species within the Populus genus.

It is characterized by its tall, slender growth habit and smooth, grayish-brown bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in catkins, typical of the willow family.

This variety is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in riparian zones and open forests.
