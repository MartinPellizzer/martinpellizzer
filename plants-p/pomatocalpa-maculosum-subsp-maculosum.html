Pomatocalpa maculosum subsp. maculosum

Pomatocalpa maculosum subsp.

maculosum, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa. It features a sympodial growth habit with a short rhizome and linear, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a distinctive spotted or streaked pattern on the petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in grasslands or forest margins.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
