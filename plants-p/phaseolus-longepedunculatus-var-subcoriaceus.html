Phaseolus longepedunculatus var. subcoriaceus

Phaseolus longepedunculatus var. subcoriaceus, commonly known as the rough-pod bean, is a variety of the common bean genus.

It is characterized by its hairy, elongated pods that are typically rough to the touch, giving it its varietal name. The plant is an annual legume that thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and slightly hairy.

The flowers are usually pale purple or white, and they appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value as well as its agricultural importance.
