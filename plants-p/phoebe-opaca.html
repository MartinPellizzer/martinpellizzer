Phoebe opaca

Phoebe opaca, commonly known as the Southern magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces showy, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age.

Phoebe opaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
