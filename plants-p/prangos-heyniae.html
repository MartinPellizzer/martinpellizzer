Prangos heyniae

Prangos heyniae, commonly known as the Heyn's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistle and daisy species.

Prangos heyniae is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
