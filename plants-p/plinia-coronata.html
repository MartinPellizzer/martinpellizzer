Plinia coronata

Plinia coronata, commonly known as the white-flowered star apple, is a tropical fruit tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is typically round or oval, with a smooth, thin skin that turns yellow when ripe. It produces a sweet, aromatic pulp with a texture similar to a pear, and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
