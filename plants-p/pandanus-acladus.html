Pandanus acladus

Pandanus acladus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or green in color. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in sandy or coastal soils.

Pandanus acladus is often used in traditional crafts and for its ornamental value in landscaping.
