Penstemon roseus

Penstemon roseus, commonly known as the rose penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing vibrant pink to rose-colored flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Penstemon roseus prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance contribute to its appeal in both garden settings and ecological restoration projects.
