Protea thyrsoides

Protea thyrsoides, commonly known as the silver coin protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces a distinctive, waxy, leaf-like bract that encases the flower cluster, giving it a unique appearance. Native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in floral arrangements.
