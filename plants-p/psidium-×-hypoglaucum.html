Psidium × hypoglaucum

Psidium × hypoglaucum, commonly known as the golden guava, is a hybrid fruit tree that results from the cross between the common guava (Psidium guajava) and the white guava (Psidium cattleyanum).

It is characterized by its smooth, yellowish-orange fruit with a sweet and aromatic flavor. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with glossy, dark green leaves. Its flowers are small, white, and fragrant, often appearing in clusters.

The fruit is widely cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.
