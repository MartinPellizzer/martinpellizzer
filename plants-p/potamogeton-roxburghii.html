Potamogeton roxburghii

Potamogeton roxburghii, commonly known as the narrowleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Asia.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually found in clusters near the water surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton roxburghii thrives in still or slow-moving waters with muddy or sandy substrates.
