Pedilanthus linearifolius

Pedilanthus linearifolius, commonly known as the linear-leaved miracle fruit, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a distinctive red, fleshy fruit that contains a single seed. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the unique taste of its fruit, which can be used in culinary applications.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
