Papaver kluanense

Papaver kluanense, commonly known as the Kluan poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats at high elevations. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

The Kluan poppy is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
