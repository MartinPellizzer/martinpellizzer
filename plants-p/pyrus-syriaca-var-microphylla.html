Pyrus syriaca var. microphylla

Pyrus syriaca var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible, round, yellow fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
