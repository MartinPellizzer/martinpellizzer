Phiambolia mentiens

Phiambolia mentiens, commonly known as the whispering phoebe, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Phiambolia mentiens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique, fragrant blooms.
