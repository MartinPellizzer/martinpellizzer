Pandanus humericus

Pandanus humericus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves that grow in a rosette formation. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically borne in clusters at the base of the leaves. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to salty air and sandy soils.
