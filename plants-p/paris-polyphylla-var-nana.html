Paris polyphylla var. nana

Paris polyphylla var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf white adder's mouth, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
