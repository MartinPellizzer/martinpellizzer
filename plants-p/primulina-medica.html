Primulina medica

Primulina medica, commonly known as the medicinal primrose, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value, but it also has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The leaves and flowers are sometimes used in herbal remedies for their purported health benefits.
