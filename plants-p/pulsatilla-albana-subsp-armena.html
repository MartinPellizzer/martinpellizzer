Pulsatilla albana subsp. armena

Pulsatilla albana subsp.

armena, commonly known as Armenian pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range from white to pale yellow.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is noted for its early spring blooming habit, making it a notable wildflower in its native habitat.
