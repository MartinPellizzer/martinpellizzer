Plantago lanceolata subsp. eriophora

Plantago lanceolata subsp.

eriophora, commonly known as the narrow-leaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes, which are borne on slender stalks. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to tolerate a wide range of environmental conditions.
