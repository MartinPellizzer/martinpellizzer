Plantago amplexicaulis subsp. giacominii

Plantago amplexicaulis subsp.

giacominii, commonly known as the Giacomin's broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Argentina. It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
