Pilocereus sartorianus

Pilocereus sartorianus, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique appearance, featuring a rounded, fleshy stem covered in spines and a colorful, non-photosynthetic top that is actually a grafted variety of Hylocereus. The plant thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight to flourish. It is drought-tolerant and prefers warm temperatures, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
