Piriqueta flammea

Piriqueta flammea, commonly known as the flameleaf shrub, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Piriqueta flammea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
