Phyllota luehmannii

Phyllota luehmannii, commonly known as the Luehmann's phyllota, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its deep root system.

Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable in the soil for extended periods, contributing to its ecological resilience.
