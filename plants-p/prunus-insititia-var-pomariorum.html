Prunus insititia var. pomariorum

Prunus insititia var. pomariorum, commonly known as the wild cherry or European black cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by dark red, edible berries in late summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along riverbanks.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and can be processed into jams or wines.
