Passiflora edulis

Passiflora edulis, commonly known as the passion flower, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments that resemble a crown of thorns. The plant produces edible berries known as passion fruits, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

The plant is widely cultivated for both ornamental purposes and commercial fruit production.
