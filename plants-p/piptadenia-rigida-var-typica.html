Piptadenia rigida var. typica

Piptadenia rigida var. typica, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnate. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several edible seeds, known as Brazil nuts.

The plant plays a vital ecological role in supporting biodiversity and is economically significant for its nut production.
