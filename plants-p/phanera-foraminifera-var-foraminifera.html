Phanera foraminifera var. foraminifera

Phanera foraminifera var. foraminifera, commonly known as the common foraminiferal plant, is a unique species characterized by its intricate, calcareous shell structure.

This plant is notable for its complex, multi-chambered test that often exhibits a spiral or discoidal shape. The surface of the shell is typically adorned with fine, radial or concentric ridges, which provide structural support and aid in buoyancy. It thrives in marine environments, particularly in shallow coastal waters where it plays a role in sediment formation.

Due to its fossil record, it is also significant in biostratigraphy for dating geological layers.
