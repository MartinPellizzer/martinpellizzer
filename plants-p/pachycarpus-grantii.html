Pachycarpus grantii

Pachycarpus grantii, commonly known as the Grant's thickleaf, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the warmer months. Its growth habit is typically low and spreading, forming dense clumps.

Pachycarpus grantii is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
