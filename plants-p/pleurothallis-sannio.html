Pleurothallis sannio

Pleurothallis sannio, commonly known as the Sannio orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that often display a range of colors from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark and drawing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and a compact growth habit, making it well adapted to its humid, shaded habitat.

Pleurothallis sannio is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its delicate beauty and unique floral structure.
