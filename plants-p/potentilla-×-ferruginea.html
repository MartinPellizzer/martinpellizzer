Potentilla × ferruginea

Potentilla × ferruginea, commonly known as the Rusty Potentilla, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, compact foliage that ranges from dark green to reddish-brown. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often covered in a rusty-colored bloom, giving it its common name.

Potentilla × ferruginea is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
