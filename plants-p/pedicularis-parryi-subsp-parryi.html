Pedicularis parryi subsp. parryi

Pedicularis parryi subsp.

parryi, commonly known as the Parry's lousewort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its spurred flowers, which are typically bright red or orange, and its hairy, glandular leaves.

The plant thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils. Its unique morphology and specialized pollination mechanisms make it an important species in its ecological niche.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticultural settings.
