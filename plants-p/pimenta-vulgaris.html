Pimenta vulgaris

Pimenta vulgaris, commonly known as allspice, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It produces small, dark red berries that are dried and used as a spice. The plant's leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. The berries contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive flavor.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
