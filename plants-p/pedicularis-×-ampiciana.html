Pedicularis × ampiciana

Pedicularis × ampiciana, commonly known as the hybrid bishop's cap, is a member of the Orobanchaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, spurred flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its vibrant floral display.
