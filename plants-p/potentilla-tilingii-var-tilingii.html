Potentilla tilingii var. tilingii

Potentilla tilingii var. tilingii, commonly known as the Tilingii cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five elliptical leaflets, which give it its common name.

The species is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience in harsh conditions.
