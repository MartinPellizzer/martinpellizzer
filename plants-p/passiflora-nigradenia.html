Passiflora nigradenia

Passiflora nigradenia, commonly known as the black-veined passionflower, is a striking climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves with prominent black veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with intricate structures, including a crown of filaments and a central corona. Its fruit, a small, dark purple berry, contains edible seeds.

Passiflora nigradenia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
