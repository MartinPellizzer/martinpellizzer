Protea bequaertii var. pilosa

Protea bequaertii var. pilosa, commonly known as the woolly protea, is a striking South African shrub characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy inflorescences.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often ornamental flowering plants. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its inflorescences consist of a central cone-like structure surrounded by clusters of bracts that resemble petals.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
