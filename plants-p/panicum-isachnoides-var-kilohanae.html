Panicum isachnoides var. kilohanae

Panicum isachnoides var. kilohanae, commonly known as the Kiholana panicgrass, is a rare grass species native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and streamside areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
