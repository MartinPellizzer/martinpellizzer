Phragmipedium triandrum

Phragmipedium triandrum, commonly known as the three-lobed phragmipedium, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with a distinctive red or purple lip. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

Phragmipedium triandrum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
