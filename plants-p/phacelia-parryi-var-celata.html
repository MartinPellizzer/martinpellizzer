Phacelia parryi var. celata

Phacelia parryi var. celata, commonly known as Parry's phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
