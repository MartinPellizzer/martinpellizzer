Pseudibatia

Pseudibatia, commonly known as the American bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants native to North America.

It consists of several species, most of which are herbaceous perennials with showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plants typically have lance-shaped leaves and produce blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. Pseudibatia species are often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.

They are valued in horticulture for their ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
