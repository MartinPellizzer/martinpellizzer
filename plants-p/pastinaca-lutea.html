Pastinaca lutea

Pastinaca lutea, commonly known as the yellow burdock, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. Its root system consists of thick, fleshy roots that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local wildlife.
