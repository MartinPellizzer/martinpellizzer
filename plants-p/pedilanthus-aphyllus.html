Pedilanthus aphyllus

Pedilanthus aphyllus, commonly known as the leafless beauty leaf, is a unique member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. This plant is characterized by its lack of leaves, with its photosynthetic function carried out by its thick, fleshy stems. The flowers are small, clustered, and often have a striking yellow or orange color.

It is a slow-growing plant that prefers well-drained soil and bright, indirect light.
