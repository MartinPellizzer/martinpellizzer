Psoralea purpurea

Psoralea purpurea, commonly known as the purple psoralea, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its small, purple or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Psoralea purpurea is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
