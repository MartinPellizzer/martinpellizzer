Potamogeton natans subsp. ellipticus

Potamogeton natans subsp.

ellipticus, commonly known as the broadleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in Europe and parts of Asia. It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically elliptical in shape, which helps it thrive in slow-moving or still water.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it reproduces both sexually and through vegetative propagation. It plays an important role in aquatic habitats by providing oxygen and shelter for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in ponds and lakes.
