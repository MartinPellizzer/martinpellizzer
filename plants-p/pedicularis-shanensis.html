Pedicularis shanensis

Pedicularis shanensis, commonly known as the Shan mountain lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the genus Pedicularis, which includes approximately 450 species of flowering plants, many of which are found in the temperate and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant is characterized by its vivid, often red or orange flowers that resemble small lanterns, and its unique morphology includes a spurred petal that serves as a nectar source for pollinators. It typically grows in rocky or forested areas, thriving in cool, moist environments.

The species is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its ornamental value and ecological significance.
