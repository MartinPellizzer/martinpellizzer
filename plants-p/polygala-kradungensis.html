Polygala kradungensis

Polygala kradungensis, commonly known as the Thai milkweed or Kradung, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are often arranged in racemes.

Polygala kradungensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
