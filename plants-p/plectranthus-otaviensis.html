Plectranthus otaviensis

Plectranthus otaviensis, commonly known as the Otavi mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a popular choice among horticulturists.
