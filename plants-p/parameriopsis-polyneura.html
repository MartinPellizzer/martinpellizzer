Parameriopsis polyneura

Parameriopsis polyneura, commonly known as the slender-leafed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in its native range.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. The plant produces small white or pale green flowers, followed by red berries that are dispersed by birds. It is characterized by its ability to absorb water and nutrients from its host through specialized root structures called haustoria.

Due to its parasitic nature, it plays a role in shaping the ecology of its host species and the surrounding forest community.
