Prunus nitidifolia

Prunus nitidifolia, commonly known as the Chinese date or little date tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China and neighboring regions.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet, sticky fruits resembling dates. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
