Pitcairnia glaziovii

Pitcairnia glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's pitcairnia, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, upright rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces vibrant, showy inflorescences with red and orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
