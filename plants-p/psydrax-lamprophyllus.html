Psydrax lamprophyllus

Psydrax lamprophyllus, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in dense clusters, creating a visually stunning display. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are not only attractive but also attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and birds.
