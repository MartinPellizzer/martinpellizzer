Phyllanthus retroflexus

Phyllanthus retroflexus, commonly known as cradle bush or false sandalwood, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and smooth, often hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

Phyllanthus retroflexus is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
