Plumeria pudica

Plumeria pudica, commonly known as the sacred lotus of India, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to yellow and pink. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves and produces fleshy, spiny fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in coastal areas and mangrove forests.

The plant is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and cultural significance in religious practices.
