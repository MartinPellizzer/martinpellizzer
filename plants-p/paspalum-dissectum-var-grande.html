Paspalum dissectum var. grande

Paspalum dissectum var. grande, commonly known as the giant panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environments.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
