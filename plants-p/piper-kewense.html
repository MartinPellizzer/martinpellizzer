Piper kewense

Piper kewense, commonly known as the Kew pepper or the giant pepper plant, is a climbing shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It is notable for its aromatic leaves and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Due to its potential medicinal properties, it has also attracted interest in herbal and pharmaceutical research.
