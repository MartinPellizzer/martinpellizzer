Prunella pratensis

Prunella pratensis, commonly known as selfheal, is a flowering plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

Prunella pratensis is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
