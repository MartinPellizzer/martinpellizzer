Pleopeltis collinsii

Pleopeltis collinsii, commonly known as the Collins' tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive root system that forms a dense, fibrous mat at the base, which helps anchor it to rocky or shaded environments. Its fronds are typically bioluminescent, emitting a soft glow at night, which aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators.

This unique combination of features makes it a striking and ecologically significant plant in its native habitat.
