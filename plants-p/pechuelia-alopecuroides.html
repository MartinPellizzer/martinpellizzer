Pechuelia alopecuroides

Pechuelia alopecuroides, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system adapted to drought conditions and a preference for well-drained soils.

Pechuelia alopecuroides is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
