Polymnia uvedalia var. floridana

Polymnia uvedalia var. floridana, commonly known as the Florida polymnia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinct waxy coating. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
