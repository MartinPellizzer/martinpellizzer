Philodendron ecordatum var. poiteauanum

Philodendron ecordatum var. poiteauanum, commonly known as the Poiteau's philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface and prominent veins. The plant is characterized by its aerial roots, which help it climb and anchor to supports. Its foliage often exhibits a variegated pattern, with lighter green or silver markings.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and humidity for optimal growth.
