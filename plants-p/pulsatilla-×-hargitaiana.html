Pulsatilla × hargitaiana

Pulsatilla × hargitaiana, commonly known as the Hargita pasqueflower, is a hybrid species belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms that typically appear in shades of pink and white. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, dark green leaves and a short, sturdy stem that supports its single, showy flower. Native to Central Europe, it thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands or meadows.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but is also considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
