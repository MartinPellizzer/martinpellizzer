Podalyria canescens var. thunbergiana

Podalyria canescens var. thunbergiana, commonly known as the Thunberg's hairypod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and spikes of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, temperate climates. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
