Polygala apiculata

Polygala apiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with a distinctive spurred structure, and it produces small, smooth pods that contain seeds.

Polygala apiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering habit.
