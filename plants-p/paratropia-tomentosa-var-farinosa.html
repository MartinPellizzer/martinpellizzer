Paratropia tomentosa var. farinosa

Paratropia tomentosa var. farinosa, commonly known as the woolly paratropia, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern and are covered in fine, soft hairs.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
