Penstemon glaber var. wardii

Penstemon glaber var. wardii, commonly known as Ward's beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract bees and butterflies, contributing to pollinator biodiversity.
