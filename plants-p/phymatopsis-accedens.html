Phymatopsis accedens

Phymatopsis accedens, commonly known as the desert lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and showy, bell-shaped white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and along canyon walls. It has a deep taproot system that allows it to survive in drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to the harsh, dry climates of its native range.
