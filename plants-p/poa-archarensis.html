Poa archarensis

Poa archarensis, commonly known as the Argentine meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, leafy stems that often form dense turf-like mats. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, with each spikelet containing two to three bracteate florets. Its leaves are typically narrow and bluish-green, with a smooth surface and prominent veins.

Poa archarensis is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.
