Pleurothallis guatopensis

Pleurothallis guatopensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant has delicate, narrow leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. It is part of the large and diverse Pleurothallis genus, which includes over 1,000 species.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
