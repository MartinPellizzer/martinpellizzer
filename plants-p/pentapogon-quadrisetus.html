Pentapogon quadrisetus

Pentapogon quadrisetus, commonly known as the four-armed starwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped flowers and a basal rosette of leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often found in meadows or along stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
