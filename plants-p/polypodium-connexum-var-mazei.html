Polypodium connexum var. mazei

Polypodium connexum var. mazei, commonly known as the Maze's shield fern, is a rare and distinctive variant of the polypodium genus.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, bipinnate fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of brown, velvety spores. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are released in clusters, which aid in its propagation.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
