Phlomoides umbrosa var. latibracteata

Phlomoides umbrosa var. latibracteata, commonly known as the broad-bract phlomoides, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that often branch near the base, producing clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped, with hairy surfaces and serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Its bloom period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a delicate floral display to shaded landscapes.
