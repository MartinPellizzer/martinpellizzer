Pelargonium × markianum

Pelargonium × markianum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, velvety leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, brightly colored flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming from spring to autumn. It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for hanging baskets and containers due to its compact size and attractive foliage.

Pelargonium × markianum is relatively easy to care for, requiring well-draining soil and moderate sunlight.
