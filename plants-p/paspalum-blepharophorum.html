Paspalum blepharophorum

Paspalum blepharophorum, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy or swampy areas. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale flowers.

Paspalum blepharophorum is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
