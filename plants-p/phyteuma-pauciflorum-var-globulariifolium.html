Phyteuma pauciflorum var. globulariifolium

Phyteuma pauciflorum var. globulariifolium, commonly known as the globular-leaf phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The leaves are distinctive for their rounded, globe-like appearance, which gives the variety its specific name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This plant is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
