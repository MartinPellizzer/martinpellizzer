Phanera integrifolia subsp. cumingiana

Phanera integrifolia subsp.

cumingiana, commonly known as the Cuming's broadleaf phanera, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are typically entire or slightly lobed.

The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
