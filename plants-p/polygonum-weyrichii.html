Polygonum weyrichii

Polygonum weyrichii, commonly known as the Chinese knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are typically alternate, with a distinctive veining pattern and a slightly rough texture.

Polygonum weyrichii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.
