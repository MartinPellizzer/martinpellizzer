Pouteria garcinioides

Pouteria garcinioides, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The tree produces hard, woody fruits that contain edible seeds, which are commercially valuable as Brazil nuts. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant has a long lifespan and plays an important role in the ecological balance of its habitat.
