Phylica minutiflora

Phylica minutiflora, commonly known as the Cape snowball, is a small shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white, fluffy flowers that resemble small snowballs, blooming in spring and summer. The plant has small, narrow leaves and is well adapted to sandy, coastal soils. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and hardy nature.

Phylica minutiflora is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for small wildlife.
