Piper winkleri

Piper winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to the rainforests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, oval berries that are typically green when mature.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and understudied species in botanical research.
