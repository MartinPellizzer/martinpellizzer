Phyllanthus pulchroides

Phyllanthus pulchroides, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescences that produce small, yellow flowers, giving it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with opposite leaflets and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
