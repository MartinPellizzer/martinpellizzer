Polytrema isophyllum

Polytrema isophyllum, commonly known as the smoothleaf polytrema, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glabrous on both surfaces. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Due to habitat loss, Polytrema isophyllum is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
