Pseudomertensia anjumae

Pseudomertensia anjumae, commonly known as the Anjumae rock cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Pseudomertensia anjumae is native to regions of Southwestern Asia and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
