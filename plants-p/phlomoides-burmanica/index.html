Phlomoides Burmanica

Phlomoides burmanica is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, known for its aromatic properties and ornamental value. This species is characterized by its slender stems, small leaves, and clusters of pale blue or purple flowers. Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is now critically endangered and listed on the IUCN Red List. Conservation efforts are underway to protect and propagate this unique plant for future generations.
