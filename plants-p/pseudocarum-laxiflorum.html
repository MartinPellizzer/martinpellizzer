Pseudocarum laxiflorum

Pseudocarum laxiflorum, commonly known as the slender-flowered false peppergrass, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that contribute to its ornamental value. Its small, white flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its delicate structure and ecological significance, it is often considered a valuable component of native plant communities.
