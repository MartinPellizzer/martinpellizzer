Pilosocereus diersianus

Pilosocereus diersianus, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its segmented, branching stems that are covered in short, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the holiday season. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Despite its cactus classification, it is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental value.
