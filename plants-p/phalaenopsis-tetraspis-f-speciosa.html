Phalaenopsis tetraspis f. speciosa

Phalaenopsis tetraspis f. speciosa, commonly known as the Orchid of the South, is a rare and ornamental orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like inflorescences and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and lavender. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, often covered in a waxy cuticle that helps retain moisture.

This variety is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and indoor settings.
