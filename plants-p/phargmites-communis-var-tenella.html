Phargmites communis var. tenella

Phargmites communis var. tenella, commonly known as the narrow-leaved common reed, is a variant of the common reed found in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are narrower than those of the typical variety. This plant thrives in marshes, shallow waters, and along the edges of lakes and rivers. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its flowering stems produce small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across wetland areas.
