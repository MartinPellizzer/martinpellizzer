Proclesia bracteata

Proclesia bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, often with a white or yellow throat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
