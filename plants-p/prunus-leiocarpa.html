Prunus leiocarpa

Prunus leiocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese wild cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, yellowish-red fruits that are slightly tart. Prunus leiocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Its growth habit and fruit characteristics make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
