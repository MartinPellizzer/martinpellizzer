Psittacanthus grandifolius

Psittacanthus grandifolius, commonly known as the giant sago palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the cycad family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for many decades, making it a long-lived species. Its leaves are typically bright green and can reach lengths of up to 3 meters.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
