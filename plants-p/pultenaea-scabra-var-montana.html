Pultenaea scabra var. montana

Pultenaea scabra var. montana, commonly known as the mountain pultenaea, is a shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late summer to autumn, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
