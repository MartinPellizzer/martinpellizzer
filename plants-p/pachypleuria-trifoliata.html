Pachypleuria trifoliata

Pachypleuria trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaf fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three lobes, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are often leathery and can reach lengths of up to one meter.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
