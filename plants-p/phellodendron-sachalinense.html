Phellodendron sachalinense

Phellodendron sachalinense, commonly known as the Sakhalin phellodendron, is a deciduous tree native to the Russian Far East and northeastern China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark that becomes fissured with age. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by oval, winged fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated margin.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties, with its bark containing compounds used in traditional medicine.
