Phoebe dunniana

Phoebe dunniana, commonly known as the dwarf phoebe or dwarf phoebe tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the southeastern United States.

It has dense, dark green foliage with a compact growth habit, making it suitable for small garden spaces. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

Phoebe dunniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate urban conditions.
