Populus pseudosimonii var. patula

Populus pseudosimonii var. patula, commonly known as the Chinese poplar, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and catkin flowers. The tree typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers a temperate climate. It is known for its rapid growth rate and is often used in reforestation efforts.

Its wood is lightweight and has been historically used for construction and papermaking.
