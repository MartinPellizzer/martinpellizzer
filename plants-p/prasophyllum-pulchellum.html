Prasophyllum pulchellum

Prasophyllum pulchellum, commonly known as the slender leek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the temperate regions of southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, flowering stems with clusters of pale green to white flowers. Its inflorescence is often adorned with a single, showy, pinkish-tinged flower that resembles a leek.

Prasophyllum pulchellum is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its delicate appearance and ecological significance in its native habitat.
