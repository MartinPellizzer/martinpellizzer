Prunus × lannesiana f. senriko

Prunus × lannesiana f. senriko, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is a hybrid of Prunus lannesiana and Prunus sargentii, and it is cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and turn yellow in autumn, while its fruit is a small, hard drupe.

The tree is often planted in parks and along streets, symbolizing renewal and the transient beauty of life.
