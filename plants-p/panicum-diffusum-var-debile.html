Panicum diffusum var. debile

Panicum diffusum var. debile, commonly known as slender flatweed, is a low-growing annual herb native to temperate regions of the Americas.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family (Poaceae). Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue when fresh.

It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
