Pandanus rheophilus

Pandanus rheophilus, commonly known as the swamp pandanus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and broad, sword-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found near water bodies. Its roots can form dense mats that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The leaves are used in traditional crafts and have aromatic properties that are sometimes utilized in local cuisines.
