Prenanthes longifolia

Prenanthes longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf waterparsley, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom in late summer.

Prenanthes longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
