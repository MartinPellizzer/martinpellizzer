Petesioides nemoralis

Petesioides nemoralis, commonly known as the wood anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and has smooth, heart-shaped leaves. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread slowly through vegetative growth.

Petesioides nemoralis plays a role in supporting early pollinators and is often found in temperate forests across Europe.
