Passiflora longipes

Passiflora longipes, commonly known as the longipes passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a crown of filaments and a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and a distinctive scent.

Passiflora longipes is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy blooms and ecological value to pollinators.
