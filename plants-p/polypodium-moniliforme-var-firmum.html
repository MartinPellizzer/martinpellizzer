Polypodium moniliforme var. firmum

Polypodium moniliforme var. firmum, commonly known as the firm mossy fern, is a variant of the polypodium genus native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds that are often clustered in dense, moss-like formations. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories or on rock surfaces. Its rhizomes are wiry and creeping, allowing it to spread and form extensive mats.

The leaves are usually bipinnate, with small, linear pinnae that give the plant its distinctive, feathery appearance.
