Pyrus malus lusus nigra

Pyrus malus lusus nigra, commonly known as the Black Beauty apple, is a cultivar of the apple tree known for its dark, almost black skin.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, tart fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often grown as a ornamental due to its striking appearance. It requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive, and it is relatively resistant to pests and diseases.

Despite its ornamental value, it can also produce edible fruits that are prized for their unique flavor.
