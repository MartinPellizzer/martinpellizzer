Planchonella petaloides

Planchonella petaloides, commonly known as the "petal-like" or "flower-like" hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that resemble those of the hibiscus genus. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy coating.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.
