Phaseolus albus

Phaseolus albus, commonly known as the white kidney bean, is a leguminous plant native to the Americas.

It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that give way to pods containing round, white or yellow seeds. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect growth habit. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds.

The plant also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
