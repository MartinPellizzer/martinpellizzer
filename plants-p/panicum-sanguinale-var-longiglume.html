Panicum sanguinale var. longiglume

Panicum sanguinale var. longiglume, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive red-colored inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas or along waterways. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib, while the spikelets are elongated and often reddish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland environments.
