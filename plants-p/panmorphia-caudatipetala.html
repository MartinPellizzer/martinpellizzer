Panmorphia caudatipetala

Panmorphia caudatipetala, commonly known as the feather-petaled panmorph, is a rare and exotic plant native to the misty highlands of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated petals that resemble flowing feathers, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of violet and white, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and slightly serrated, with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture in its arid habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and delicate nature, it is considered a conservation concern.
