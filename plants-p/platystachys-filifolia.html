Platystachys filifolia

Platystachys filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems covered with small, needle-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of white to pale yellow, typically blooming in late spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, visually appealing plant.
