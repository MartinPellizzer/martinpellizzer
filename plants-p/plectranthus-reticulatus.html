Plectranthus reticulatus

Plectranthus reticulatus, commonly known as the African violet or velvetleaf, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that are often a deep purple or reddish hue. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, or white, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and indoor cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming season.
