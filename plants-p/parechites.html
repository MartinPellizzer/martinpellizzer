Parechites

Parechites, commonly known as the "dwarf dandelion," is a small flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and along roadsides. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small yellow flowers that develop into fluffy seed heads. It has a taproot system and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in cultivated areas.
