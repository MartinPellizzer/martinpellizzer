Pteronia thymifolia

Pteronia thymifolia, commonly known as the thyme-leaved pteronia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage with a strong thyme-like fragrance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
