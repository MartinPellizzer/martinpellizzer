Phyllanthus cristalensis

Phyllanthus cristalensis, commonly known as the crystalline phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its unique, clustered inflorescences known as cyathia. The plant features small, greenish-yellow flowers surrounded by specialized leaves called bracts. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
