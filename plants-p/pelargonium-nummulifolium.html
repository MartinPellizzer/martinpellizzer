Pelargonium nummulifolium

Pelargonium nummulifolium, commonly known as the Nummulite Geranium, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, coin-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to purple. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its unique foliage and ornamental value make it a prized addition to many botanical collections.
