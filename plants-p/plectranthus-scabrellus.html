Plectranthus scabrellus

Plectranthus scabrellus, commonly known as the woolly plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is often cultivated in both gardens and as a houseplant.
