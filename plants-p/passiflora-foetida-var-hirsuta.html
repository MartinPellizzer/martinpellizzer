Passiflora foetida var. hirsuta

Passiflora foetida var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy passionflower, is a species native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with intricate structures and bright, often red or orange hues. The plant has rough, hairy stems and leaves, which provide a unique texture. It produces edible berries that are typically small and tart.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
