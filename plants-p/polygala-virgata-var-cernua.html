Polygala virgata var. cernua

Polygala virgata var. cernua, commonly known as the drooping milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, branching stems that bear small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is characterized by its unique floral structure and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
