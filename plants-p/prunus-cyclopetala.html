Prunus cyclopetala

Prunus cyclopetala, commonly known as the Mexican cherry or cyclopetala cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its ornamental value due to its showy, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while its fruits are small, bright red drupes.

Prunus cyclopetala is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
