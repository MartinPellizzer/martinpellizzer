Pogostemon glaber

Pogostemon glaber, commonly known as the broad-leaved pogostemon, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that can grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant has lance-shaped, smooth, and glossy leaves that are often aromatic. Its small, pale yellow flowers are clustered in dense spikes, and it produces oval, dark brown seeds.

Pogostemon glaber is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
