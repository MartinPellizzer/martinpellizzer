Penstemon × bryantiae

Penstemon × bryantiae, commonly known as the Bryant's beardtongue, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like clusters of tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Penstemon × bryantiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
