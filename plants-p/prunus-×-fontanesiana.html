Prunus × fontanesiana

Prunus × fontanesiana, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, single or double flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a serrated margin.

The fruit is a small, yellow or orange drupe that ripens in late summer.
