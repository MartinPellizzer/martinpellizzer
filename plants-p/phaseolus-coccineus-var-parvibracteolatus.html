Phaseolus coccineus var. parvibracteolatus

Phaseolus coccineus var. parvibracteolatus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a tropical legume characterized by its vibrant red pods and edible seeds.

It is native to the Americas and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant features compound leaves with small, hairy stipules and produces clusters of yellow flowers. Its stems are hairy and often climb using tendrils, making it a popular choice for vertical gardening.

The variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use, particularly in traditional cuisines.
