Polygonatum canaliculatum var. americanum

Polygonatum canaliculatum var. americanum, commonly known as American Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features rhizomatous stems that produce clusters of pendulous, greenish-white flowers in early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its berries are dark blue to black when mature and are often consumed by birds.

It thrives in shaded, moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
