Perovskia botschantzevii

Perovskia botschantzevii, commonly known as Russian lavender or blue mist shrub, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with square stems and opposite leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its compact growth habit and long blooming period make it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
