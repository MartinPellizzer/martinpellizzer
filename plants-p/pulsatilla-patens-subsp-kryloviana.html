Pulsatilla patens subsp. kryloviana

Pulsatilla patens subsp.

kryloviana, commonly known as the Kryloviana pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant produces a single, showy flower per stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early blooming and distinctive appearance.
