Poa dolosa

Poa dolosa, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green color, contributing to its ornamental appeal in some landscapes.

Poa dolosa is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
