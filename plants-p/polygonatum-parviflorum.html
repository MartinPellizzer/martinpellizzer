Polygonatum parviflorum

Polygonatum parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous root system and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early summer, followed by red berries. It thrives in shady, moist woodland environments and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking shade-tolerant plants.
