Potentilla multisecta

Potentilla multisecta, commonly known as the multisection cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, palmately divided leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens despite its limited distribution in the wild.
