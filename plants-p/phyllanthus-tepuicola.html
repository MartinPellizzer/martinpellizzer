Phyllanthus tepuicola

Phyllanthus tepuicola, commonly known as the Andean spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in rocky or dry environments. The plant features small, clustered leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that contains tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Phyllanthus tepuicola is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and is often found in alpine ecosystems.
