Polygonatum canaliculatum var. rotundifolium

Polygonatum canaliculatum var. rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leaf Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features arching stems with lanceolate, often rounded leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in racemes, which are followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and valuable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
