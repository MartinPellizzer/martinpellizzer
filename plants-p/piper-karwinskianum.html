Piper karwinskianum

Piper karwinskianum, commonly known as the Karwinskianum pepper or Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible but have a sharp, peppery flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
