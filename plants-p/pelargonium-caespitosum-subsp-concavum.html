Pelargonium caespitosum subsp. concavum

Pelargonium caespitosum subsp.

concavum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa. It features upright, compact growth with small, ovate leaves that have a slightly curled margin.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
