Phlox acerba

Phlox acerba, commonly known as the bitter phlox, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polemoniaceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a serrated edge, while its stems are hairy and somewhat woody.

Phlox acerba is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies due to its nectar-rich blooms.
