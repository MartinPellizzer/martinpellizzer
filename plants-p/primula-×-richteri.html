Primula × richteri

Primula × richteri, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in early spring, with foliage consisting of dark green, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

Botanically, it is a result of crossbreeding between different Primula species, showcasing genetic diversity and horticultural significance.
