Pedicularis langsdorffii var. elatior

Pedicularis langsdorffii var. elatior, commonly known as the greater yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations. Its flowers are tubular with a short throat and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
