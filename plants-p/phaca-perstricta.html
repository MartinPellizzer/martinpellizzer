Phaca perstricta

Phaca perstricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf phaca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a distinctive blue-green color.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.
