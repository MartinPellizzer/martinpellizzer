Parodia turbinata subsp. calvescens

Parodia turbinata subsp.

calvescens, commonly known as the Turbinata Cactus, is a species native to Mexico. It is a compact, globular cactus with a short, cylindrical stem that often branches at the base.

The plant produces white, tubular flowers that bloom in spring, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is well adapted to arid environments, featuring a shallow root system and thick, fleshy stems for water storage.

This subspecies is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
