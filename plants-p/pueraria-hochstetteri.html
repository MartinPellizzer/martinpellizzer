Pueraria hochstetteri

Pueraria hochstetteri, commonly known as the purple velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and distinctive purple-pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and long, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

Pueraria hochstetteri thrives in warm, humid climates and is adaptable to various soil types.
