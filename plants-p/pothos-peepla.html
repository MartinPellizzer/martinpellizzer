Pothos peepla

Pothos peepla, commonly known as the devil's ivy, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly variegated pattern. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal care, making it a popular houseplant. Its stems can grow long and trailing, often used in hanging baskets or trained on trellises.

Botanically, it belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its aerial roots that help it climb and anchor itself to surfaces.
