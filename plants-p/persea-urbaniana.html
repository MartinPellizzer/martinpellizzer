Persea urbaniana

Persea urbaniana, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible when fully mature. The fruit has a high sugar content and a unique flavor, often described as sweet and slightly tart.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
