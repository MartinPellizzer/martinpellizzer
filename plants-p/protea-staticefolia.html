Protea staticefolia

Protea staticefolia, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is typically found in fynbos vegetation. It produces a distinctive, leathery seed capsule that opens to release its seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific conditions to flourish.
