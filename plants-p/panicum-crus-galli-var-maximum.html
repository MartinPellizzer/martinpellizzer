Panicum crus-galli var. maximum

Panicum crus-galli var. maximum, commonly known as lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in some regions for erosion control and as a decorative plant.
