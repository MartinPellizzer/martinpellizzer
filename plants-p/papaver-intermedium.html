Papaver intermedium

Papaver intermedium, commonly known as the meadow poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Papaveraceae family, which includes other well-known species like the opium poppy. The plant features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a velvety texture, while its stems are generally hairy and branched.

Papaver intermedium thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
