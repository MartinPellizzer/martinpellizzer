Polemonium foliosissimum subsp. verum

Polemonium foliosissimum subsp.

verum, commonly known as the eastern white fringed gentian, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, loamy soils.

The plant features striking blue to purple flowers with distinctive white fringes along the petals, which attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. Its leaves are lance-shaped, opposite, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
