Phlox suksdorfii

Phlox suksdorfii, commonly known as Suckdorff's phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant pink to purple flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem.

Phlox suksdorfii is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade environments.
