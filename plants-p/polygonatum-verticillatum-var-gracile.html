Polygonatum verticillatum var. gracile

Polygonatum verticillatum var. gracile, commonly known as the slender Solomon's seal, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that bear opposite or whorled leaves, which are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in late spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

This variety is distinguished by its more delicate and compact growth compared to other Polygonatum species.
