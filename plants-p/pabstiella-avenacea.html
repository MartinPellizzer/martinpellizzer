Pabstiella avenacea

Pabstiella avenacea, commonly known as the silverleaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its succulent, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a noxious weed in some regions.
