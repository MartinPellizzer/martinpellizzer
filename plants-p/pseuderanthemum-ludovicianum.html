Pseuderanthemum ludovicianum

Pseuderanthemum ludovicianum, commonly known as the Florida rainflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in humid climates and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
