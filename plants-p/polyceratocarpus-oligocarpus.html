Polyceratocarpus oligocarpus

Polyceratocarpus oligocarpus, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny pods. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into hairy, woody fruits. It is often found in open woodlands and savannas, thriving in well-drained soils.

The seeds of this species are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein in some regions.
