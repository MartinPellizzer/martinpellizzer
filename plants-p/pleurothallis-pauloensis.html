Pleurothallis pauloensis

Pleurothallis pauloensis, commonly known as the Paulo's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its inflorescence is usually a raceme of small, clustered blooms.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
