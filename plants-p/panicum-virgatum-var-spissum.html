Panicum virgatum var. spissum

Panicum virgatum var. spissum, commonly known as switchgrass, is a native North American grass species valued for its ecological and agricultural benefits.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The plant produces tall, leafy stalks with panicles of small, fluffy seeds that mature in late summer. It is a warm-season grass that is drought-tolerant and often used in restoration projects and bioenergy production.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native prairie ecosystems.
