Piper huacapistanum

Piper huacapistanum, commonly known as the Huacapista pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its fruits are typically small berries that develop from the flowers and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on biodiversity and ethnobotanical uses.
