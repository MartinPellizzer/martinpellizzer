Paepalanthus distichophyllus var. distichophyllus

Paepalanthus distichophyllus var. distichophyllus, commonly known as the two-ranked papyrus plant, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Paraguay.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the sedge family.

This variety is notable for its distinctive two-ranked leaf arrangement, which gives it its specific epithet.
