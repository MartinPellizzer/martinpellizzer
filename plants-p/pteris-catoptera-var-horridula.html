Pteris catoptera var. horridula

Pteris catoptera var. horridula, commonly known as the hairy water fern, is a variant of the common water fern.

It is a perennial fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in humid forests. The plant features fronds that are divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments, with a distinctive hairy texture on the underside. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of ferns.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
