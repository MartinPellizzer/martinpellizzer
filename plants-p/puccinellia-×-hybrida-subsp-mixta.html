Puccinellia × hybrida subsp. mixta

Puccinellia × hybrida subsp.

mixta, commonly known as the hybrid saltbush, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline and arid environments. It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically clustered at the ends of the stems, and its flowers are small and inconspicuous. It is often found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline soils where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.

Its ability to tolerate high salinity makes it an important species for ecological restoration projects.
