Pseudosasa guanxianensis

Pseudosasa guanxianensis, commonly known as the Guanxian bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to China.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountainous regions and forest understories. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

Pseudosasa guanxianensis is valued for both its ecological role and its cultural significance in Chinese horticulture.
