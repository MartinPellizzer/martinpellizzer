Pellionia grammica

Pellionia grammica, commonly known as the silver mite fern, is a small evergreen fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, finely divided leaves that give it a lacy appearance, and it thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant has a creeping rhizome that helps it spread along the forest floor. Its leaves are usually a dark green with a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Pellionia grammica is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic value in shaded garden settings.
