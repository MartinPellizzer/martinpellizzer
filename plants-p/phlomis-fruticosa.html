Phlomis fruticosa

Phlomis fruticosa, commonly known as upright mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Phlomis fruticosa is also known for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
