Plantago pachyphylla f. crassicaudex

Plantago pachyphylla f. crassicaudex, commonly known as the thick-tailed plantain, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps reduce water loss through evaporation.

This variety is valued for its drought tolerance and is often used in xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
