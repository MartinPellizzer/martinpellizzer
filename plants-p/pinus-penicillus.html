Pinus penicillus

Pinus penicillus, commonly known as the slender pine, is a species of conifer native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and long, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, red-brown cones that mature over several years, releasing seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in high-elevation environments.

The botanical features of Pinus penicillus make it well adapted to harsh, arid conditions.
