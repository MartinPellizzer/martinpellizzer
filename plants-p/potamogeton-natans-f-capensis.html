Potamogeton natans f. capensis

Potamogeton natans f. capensis, commonly known as the Cape water plant, is a submerged aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically dark green and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish in color and arranged in clusters. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds.

Due to its dense growth, it can play a significant role in stabilizing aquatic sediments and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
