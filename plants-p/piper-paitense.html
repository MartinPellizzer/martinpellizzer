Piper paitense

Piper paitense, commonly known as the Paitense pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants like black pepper. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in local remedies for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's botanical features reflect its adaptation to humid, shaded environments.
