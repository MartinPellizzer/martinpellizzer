Potentilla adscharica f. typica

Potentilla adscharica f. typica, commonly known as the Caucasian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright stems covered in fine, glandular hairs, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins, and its roots are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
