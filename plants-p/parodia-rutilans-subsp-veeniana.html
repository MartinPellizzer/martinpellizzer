Parodia rutilans subsp. veeniana

Parodia rutilans subsp.

veeniana, commonly known as the Veen's moon cactus, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to South America. It features a spherical or cylindrical stem with a ribbed structure and is covered in white to pale green spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is a popular choice for rock gardens and container cultivation due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Its subspecies status highlights its unique genetic and morphological distinctions within the Parodia genus.
