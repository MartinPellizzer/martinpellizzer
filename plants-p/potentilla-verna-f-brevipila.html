Potentilla verna f. brevipila

Potentilla verna f. brevipila, commonly known as the short-stemmed cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.
