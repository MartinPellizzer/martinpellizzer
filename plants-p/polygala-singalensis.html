Polygala singalensis

Polygala singalensis, commonly known as the Singal Hill Polygala, is a flowering plant native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, spathaceous inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually small and white or pale pink, with a unique structure that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
