Phaseolus compressus var. fuscocoerulescens

Phaseolus compressus var. fuscocoerulescens, commonly known as the black and blue bean, is a variant of the common bean species native to Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive dark green to black pods with a bluish tinge, which are typically slender and slightly curved. The plant is an annual legume that thrives in warm, tropical climates and produces small, white flowers. Its seeds are edible and have a mild, nutty flavor, often used in traditional cuisines.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal as well as its culinary uses.
