Persea verruculosa

Persea verruculosa, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, orange to reddish fruits that are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, honey-like flavor when ripe. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
