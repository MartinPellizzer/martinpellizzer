Protea inopina

Protea inopina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved protea, is a striking South African flowering plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the fynbos vegetation of the Western Cape. Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a popular choice for horticultural displays.

Protea inopina plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators such as birds and insects.
