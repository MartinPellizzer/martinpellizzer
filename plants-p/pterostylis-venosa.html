Pterostylis venosa

Pterostylis venosa, commonly known as the pink-stemmed duck orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, pinkish-green stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during late summer to autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are often clustered in a loose inflorescence.

Pterostylis venosa is a member of the Orchidaceae family and exhibits typical orchid features such as a specialized root system and a labellum structure.
