Psidium schiedeanum

Psidium schiedeanum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or carambola, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a tangy, citrus-like flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Psidium schiedeanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in subtropical regions.
