Peperomia austini

Peperomia austini, commonly known as the Austini peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, usually green or reddish, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its slow growth and minimal care requirements, it is well-suited for beginners and indoor spaces.
