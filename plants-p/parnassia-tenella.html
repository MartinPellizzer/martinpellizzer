Parnassia tenella

Parnassia tenella, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its striking white, bell-shaped flowers that resemble ghostly faces, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and have a waxy coating.

Parnassia tenella is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
