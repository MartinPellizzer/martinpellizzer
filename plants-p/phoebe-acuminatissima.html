Phoebe acuminatissima

Phoebe acuminatissima, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phoebe, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often blooming in the spring. Its bark is smooth and light gray, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments in the southeastern United States.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
