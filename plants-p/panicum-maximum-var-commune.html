Panicum maximum var. commune

Panicum maximum var. commune, commonly known as Guinea grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its robust growth, with sturdy stems and wide, flat leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are typically brownish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common species in grassland ecosystems.

Due to its high nutritional value, it is widely used as forage for livestock in many parts of the world.
