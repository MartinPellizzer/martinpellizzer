Pandanus seruaensis

Pandanus seruaensis, commonly known as the Solomon Islands pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanus genus, which includes many species of palms and related plants. The plant is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often growing near mangroves or in swampy areas.

Its distinctive appearance and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
