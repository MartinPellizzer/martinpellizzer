Phlomoides umbrosa var. stenocalyx

Phlomoides umbrosa var. stenocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-calyx phlomoides, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, and its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes. The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
