Polygonum amblyophyllum

Polygonum amblyophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved smartweed, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in moist soils along streambanks, wetlands, and roadside areas.

Polygonum amblyophyllum is often found in temperate regions of North America and is known for its adaptability to various soil conditions.
