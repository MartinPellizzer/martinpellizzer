Piper nervosovenosum

Piper nervosovenosum, commonly known as the veiny pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its finely veined, heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
