Phaca serpens

Phaca serpens, commonly known as the snake's head plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to arid conditions.

Phaca serpens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
