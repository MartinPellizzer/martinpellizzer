Potentilla obtusifolia

Potentilla obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa, often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas. The plant features upright stems with alternate, ovate to broadly ovate leaves that have a soft, hairy texture. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
