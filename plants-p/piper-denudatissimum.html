Piper denudatissimum

Piper denudatissimum, commonly known as the naked-stemmed pepper vine, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
