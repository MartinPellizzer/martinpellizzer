Polygonum sieboldii

Polygonum sieboldii, commonly known as Japanese knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features rhizomatous growth, allowing it to spread aggressively and form dense stands. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically reddish-purple in color, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist environments and is often found along riverbanks and in wetlands.

Due to its invasive nature, it can pose a challenge in ecological and agricultural settings.
