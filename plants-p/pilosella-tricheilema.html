Pilosella tricheilema

Pilosella tricheilema, commonly known as the slender pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features dense, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, often in clusters at the ends of stems. Its leaves are typically narrow, linear, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

Pilosella tricheilema is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
