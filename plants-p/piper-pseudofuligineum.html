Piper pseudofuligineum

Piper pseudofuligineum, commonly known as the black pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, green berries that turn dark when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in local herbal remedies for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical structure includes opposite, ovate leaves and a distinctive, fragrant aroma.
