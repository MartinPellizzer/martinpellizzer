Pleurothallis megachlamys

Pleurothallis megachlamys, commonly known as the giant pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture from the humid air. Its intricate floral structure and vibrant coloration make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
