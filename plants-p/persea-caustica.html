Persea caustica

Persea caustica, commonly known as the caustic persea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Mexico and Brazil. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are glossy and elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible berries.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and is often found in humid, lowland forests.
