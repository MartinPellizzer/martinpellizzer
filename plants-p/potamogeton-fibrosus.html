Potamogeton fibrosus

Potamogeton fibrosus, commonly known as the fibrous pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender, fibrous stems that are often branched and covered with small, narrow leaves. The plant produces tiny, green flowers that are arranged in spikes, typically emerging from the water surface. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a role in stabilizing sediment and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Due to its dense growth, it can sometimes become invasive in certain water bodies.
