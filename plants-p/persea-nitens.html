Persea nitens

Persea nitens, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, orange-yellow fruits that are edible when fully ripe. The fruit has a sweet, honey-like flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
