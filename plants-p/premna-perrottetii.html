Premna perrottetii

Premna perrottetii, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries. It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Premna perrottetii thrives in warm, humid climates and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
