Pinus firma var. jezoensis

Pinus firma var. jezoensis, commonly known as the Japanese white pine, is a variant of the Japanese pine that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and long, needle-like leaves. The needles are typically light green and grow in fascicles of five, which is a notable trait in the Pinus genus. The cones are generally small and cylindrical, with persistent bracts that add to its ornamental value.

This species is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
