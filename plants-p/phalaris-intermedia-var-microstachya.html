Phalaris intermedia var. microstachya

Phalaris intermedia var. microstachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wild oat, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm wide, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Its adaptability and rapid growth make it both a common weed and a valuable forage plant.
