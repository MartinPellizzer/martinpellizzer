Pleroma clinopodifolium

Pleroma clinopodifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pleroma, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, with a distinct minty scent when crushed.

Pleroma clinopodifolium produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.
