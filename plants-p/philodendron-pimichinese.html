Philodendron pimichinese

Philodendron pimichinese, commonly known as the Pimic Chinese Philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by bright red berries, which are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a houseplant.
