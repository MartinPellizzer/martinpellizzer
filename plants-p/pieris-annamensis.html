Pieris annamensis

Pieris annamensis, commonly known as the Annam bellflower, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in shady, moist environments, such as mountain forests, and prefers acidic soils. Its floral display, which ranges from white to pink or purple, occurs in late spring to early summer.

Pieris annamensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded garden settings.
