Plantago brasiliensis subsp. densa

Plantago brasiliensis subsp.

densa, commonly known as the dense plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America. It is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically borne on long, slender stalks. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their astringent and anti-inflammatory properties.
