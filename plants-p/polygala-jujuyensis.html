Polygala jujuyensis

Polygala jujuyensis, commonly known as the Jujuy bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Argentina.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its striking, bottlebrush-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is also of interest for conservation efforts.
