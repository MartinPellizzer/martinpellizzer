Pastinaca ligusticifolia

Pastinaca ligusticifolia, commonly known as the Italian burdock, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and range in color from green to purple.

The roots are thick, fleshy, and often used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in some cuisines.
