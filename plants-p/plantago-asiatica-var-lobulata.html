Plantago asiatica var. lobulata

Plantago asiatica var. lobulata, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features basal rosettes of broad, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and prominent veins. The plant produces erect flowering stems topped with a dense cluster of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
