Penstemon oklahomensis

Penstemon oklahomensis, commonly known as the Oklahoma beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Penstemon oklahomensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
