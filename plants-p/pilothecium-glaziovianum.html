Pilothecium glaziovianum

Pilothecium glaziovianum, commonly known as the Glaziovian pilothecium, is a unique plant species belonging to the family Pilothecaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cup-like structures that resemble small, hardened cups, which are used for seed dispersal. The plant is native to specific regions in South America, particularly in Brazil and Peru, where it thrives in humid, tropical environments. Its morphology includes a short stem with a single, persistent cup-like structure at the base.

Pilothecium glaziovianum is of interest to botanists due to its unusual reproductive biology and ecological adaptations.
