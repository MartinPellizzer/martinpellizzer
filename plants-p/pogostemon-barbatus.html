Pogostemon barbatus

Pogostemon barbatus, commonly known as the hairy pogostemon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Pogostemon barbatus is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
