Phaeanthus malabaricus

Phaeanthus malabaricus, commonly known as the Malabar starflower, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often near streams or in dense undergrowth. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are slender and branched.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
