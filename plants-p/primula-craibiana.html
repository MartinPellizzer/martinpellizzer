Primula craibiana

Primula craibiana, commonly known as the Craib's primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant features heart-shaped, toothed leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or on rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
