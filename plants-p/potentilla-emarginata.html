Potentilla emarginata

Potentilla emarginata, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive notched or scalloped margin.

Potentilla emarginata thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
