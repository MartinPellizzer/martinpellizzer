Panicum oreoboloides

Panicum oreoboloides, commonly known as the mountain panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in rocky, arid, or semi-arid environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it an important component of local ecosystems.
