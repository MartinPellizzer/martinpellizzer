Portulaca filsonii

Portulaca filsonii, commonly known as the Filson's moss rose, is a low-growing annual plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features fleshy, erect stems and small, fleshy leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The flowers are usually bright red or orange, with five petals and a prominent central column. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This species is known for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the summer months.
