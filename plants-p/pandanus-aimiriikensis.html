Pandanus aimiriikensis

Pandanus aimiriikensis, commonly known as the New Britain pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the Pandanaceae family.

It is native to the Pacific Islands, particularly New Britain in the Bismarck Archipelago. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. It produces small, fragrant flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by fibrous, spiky fruits.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in local cultures.
