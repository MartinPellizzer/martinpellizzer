Psylliostachys × myosuroides

Psylliostachys × myosuroides, commonly known as the hybrid bamboo, is a sterile hybrid species resulting from the cross between Psylliostachys and Myosurus.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and gracefully arching foliage, typical of many bamboo species. The plant exhibits a clumping growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and varying shades of green.

While it does not produce seeds, it can propagate through rhizomes, making it a popular choice for creating dense, attractive bamboo groves.
