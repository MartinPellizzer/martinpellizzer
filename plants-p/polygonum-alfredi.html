Polygonum alfredi

Polygonum alfredi, commonly known as Alfred's knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in hue.

Polygonum alfredi thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.
