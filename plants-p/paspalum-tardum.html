Paspalum tardum

Paspalum tardum, commonly known as rough paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its coarse, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Paspalum tardum is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in open fields.
