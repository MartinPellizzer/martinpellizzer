Passiflora palmatisecta

Passiflora palmatisecta, commonly known as the palmate passionflower, is a striking tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that give it a distinctive, almost palm-like appearance. The plant produces showy, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments, typical of the Passifloraceae family. Its fruit is a woody, spiny capsule that contains edible, sweet seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in warm climates for its unique foliage and floral display.
