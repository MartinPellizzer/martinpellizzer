Phlomoides pectinata

Phlomoides pectinata, commonly known as the woolly phlomoides, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and spikes of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

The flowers bloom in spring and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
