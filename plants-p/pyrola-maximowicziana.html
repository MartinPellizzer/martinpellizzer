Pyrola maximowicziana

Pyrola maximowicziana, commonly known as the Siberian snowbell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and showy white or pink flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and tundra, where it forms dense, mat-like growths. Its leaves are usually evergreen, and it produces small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring.

The species is notable for its ability to survive in cold climates and its role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
