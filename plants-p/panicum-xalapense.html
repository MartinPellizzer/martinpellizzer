Panicum xalapense

Panicum xalapense, commonly known as Mexican dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces delicate, feathery seed heads that add ornamental value to gardens. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant landscapes. Its foliage is typically bluish-green, with a fine texture that enhances its visual appeal.

Panicum xalapense is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
