Piper dilatatum f. naris-fractae

Piper dilatatum f. naris-fractae, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pepper plant, is a rare variant of the pepper plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into peppercorns. The plant belongs to the Piperaceae family and is often cultivated for its aromatic and medicinal properties. Its botanical features include a climbing habit and the presence of glandular hairs on its stems and leaves.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor profile and is used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
