Pleioblastus baviensis

Pleioblastus baviensis, commonly known as the Bavi bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its slender, upright culms and fine, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions. It is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The Bavi bamboo is also notable for its ability to form dense clumps, making it suitable for erosion control and habitat creation.
