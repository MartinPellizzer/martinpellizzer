Pterocarpus lucens subsp. lucens

Pterocarpus lucens subsp.

lucens, commonly known as the white sand ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its straight, dark brown to black heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and fine grain.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by flat, elongated pods containing seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
