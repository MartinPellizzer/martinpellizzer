Paris thibetica

Paris thibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and palmate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
