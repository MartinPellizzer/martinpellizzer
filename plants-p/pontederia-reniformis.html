Pontederia reniformis

Pontederia reniformis, commonly known as pickerelweed, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy spikes of blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in shallow water and moist soils, making it a common sight in ponds and marshes. Its roots help stabilize soil, while its seeds provide food for waterfowl and other wildlife.

Pickerelweed is also used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits.
