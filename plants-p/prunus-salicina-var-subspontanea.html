Prunus salicina var. subspontanea

Prunus salicina var. subspontanea, commonly known as the wild cherry or Chinese wild cherry, is a variant of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its small, often pale pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. Its fruit is a small, dark red drupe that matures in late summer.

This variety is often found in naturalized areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
