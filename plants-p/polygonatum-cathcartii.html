Polygonatum cathcartii

Polygonatum cathcartii, commonly known as the Cathcart's Solomon's Seal, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in late spring. Its berries are typically red or yellow and are edible, though not commonly consumed.

Polygonatum cathcartii is valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
