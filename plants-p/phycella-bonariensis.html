Phycella bonariensis

Phycella bonariensis, commonly known as the Buenos Aires sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and riparian zones, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its stems are triangular in cross-section, a characteristic feature of many sedges, and it produces small, clustered flowers.

Phycella bonariensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
