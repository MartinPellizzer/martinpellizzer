Peucedanum elatior

Peucedanum elatior, commonly known as greater cow parsley, is a tall biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white, umbrella-shaped flowers. The plant is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, hollow stems.

Its seeds are small, dark, and have a distinctive, pungent odor.
