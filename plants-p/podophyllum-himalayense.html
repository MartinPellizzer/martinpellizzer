Podophyllum himalayense

Podophyllum himalayense, commonly known as Himalayan mayapple, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of Asia.

It features a distinctive underground rhizome and produces large, palmately lobed leaves that are typically bright green. The plant blooms with yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by the development of red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The berries are toxic but have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
