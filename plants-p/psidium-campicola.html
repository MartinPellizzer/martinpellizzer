Psidium campicola

Psidium campicola, commonly known as the Brazilian guava, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruit, similar to a guava, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Psidium campicola is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield in tropical and subtropical regions.
