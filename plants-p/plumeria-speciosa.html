Plumeria speciosa

Plumeria speciosa, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that come in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant produces large, waxy petals and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

The tree is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
