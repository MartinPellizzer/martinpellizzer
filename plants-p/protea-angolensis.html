Protea angolensis

Protea angolensis, commonly known as the Angola protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous floral bracts that resemble petals. The plant is native to the coastal regions of KwaZulu-Natal and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils, typical of fynbos vegetation. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white, and are pollinated primarily by birds and insects.

Protea angolensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
