Potamogeton rigidus

Potamogeton rigidus, commonly known as rigid pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across the northern hemisphere.

It has long, narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically dark green and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, green flowers that are arranged in spike-like clusters, and it reproduces both sexually and asexually through vegetative propagation. Potamogeton rigidus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

It is often found in slow-moving waters such as lakes, ponds, and rivers.
