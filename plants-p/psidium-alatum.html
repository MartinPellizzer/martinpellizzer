Psidium alatum

Psidium alatum, commonly known as the velvet apple or huito, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Psidium alatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the consumption of its fruit.
