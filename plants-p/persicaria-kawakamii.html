Persicaria kawakamii

Persicaria kawakamii, commonly known as Kawakami's smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often edible berries. The plant typically features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or white. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands and stream banks, and is often found in shaded to partially sunny conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
