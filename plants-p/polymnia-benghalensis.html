Polymnia benghalensis

Polymnia benghalensis, commonly known as the Bengal polymnia, is a herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow, followed by small, dry fruits. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
