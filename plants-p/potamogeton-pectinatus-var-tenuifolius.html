Potamogeton pectinatus var. tenuifolius

Potamogeton pectinatus var. tenuifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and Asia.

It features long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically narrow and have a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often found in shallow, slow-moving waters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its habitat.
