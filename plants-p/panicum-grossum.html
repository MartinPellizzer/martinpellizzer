Panicum grossum

Panicum grossum, commonly known as rough panicgrass, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and coarse, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces dense, fluffy seed heads that are often used in ornamental landscaping. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.

Panicum grossum is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
