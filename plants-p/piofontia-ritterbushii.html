Piofontia ritterbushii

Piofontia ritterbushii, commonly known as Ritter's bush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features small, narrow leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and has a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh conditions.

Piofontia ritterbushii is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
