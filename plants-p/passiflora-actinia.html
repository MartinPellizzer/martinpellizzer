Passiflora actinia

Passiflora actinia, commonly known as the crown of thorns, is a striking tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that display a unique blend of red, blue, and purple hues. The plant produces bright orange or yellow berries that contain inedible, toxic seeds. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture.

Passiflora actinia thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
