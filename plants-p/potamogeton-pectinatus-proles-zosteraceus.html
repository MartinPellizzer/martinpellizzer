Potamogeton pectinatus proles zosteraceus

Potamogeton pectinatus proles zosteraceus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a aquatic plant native to freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 mm wide. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by cylindrical, spirally arranged fruits. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
