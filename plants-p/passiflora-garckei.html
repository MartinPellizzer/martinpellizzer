Passiflora garckei

Passiflora garckei, commonly known as the Ecuadorian passionflower, is a species native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant's botanical features include a hairy stem and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically blue or purple with a yellow center, and it produces edible, orange-colored berries.

Passiflora garckei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
