Phaseolus vulgaris var. xanthocarpus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. xanthocarpus, commonly known as the yellow-seed common bean, is a variant of the common bean characterized by its yellow pod and seed color.

It is an annual legume belonging to the Fabaceae family, known for its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are typically harvested when mature for culinary use. It thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for its nutritional value, providing protein-rich seeds.

This variety is also valued for its role in crop rotation due to its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen.
