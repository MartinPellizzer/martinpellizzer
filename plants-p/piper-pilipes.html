Piper pilipes

Piper pilipes, commonly known as the slender pipevine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers are typically clustered in spikes and are followed by small, round berries.

Piper pilipes is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.
