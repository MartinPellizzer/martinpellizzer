Psidium australe var. suffruticosum

Psidium australe var. suffruticosum, commonly known as the rough passionfruit, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, orange-red berries. Its fruits are typically edible, though they are less commonly cultivated compared to other passionfruit varieties.

The plant is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
