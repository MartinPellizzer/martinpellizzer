Ptilophyllum cristatum

Ptilophyllum cristatum, commonly known as the helmet orchid, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, helmet-like floral structure. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually small and pale green or yellow, with a distinctive hooded appearance.

Ptilophyllum cristatum is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unusual appearance.
