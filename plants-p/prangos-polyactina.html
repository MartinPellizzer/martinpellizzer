Prangos polyactina

Prangos polyactina, commonly known as the Persian ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and its root system is fibrous and deep-rooted. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Prangos polyactina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in some regions for medicinal purposes.
