Protea carinata

Protea carinata, commonly known as the serrated protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers arranged in a cone-like structure. The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are leathery, serrated, and often covered in a fine, white powder.

Protea carinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays an important role in local ecosystems.
