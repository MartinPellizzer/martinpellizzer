Phyllanthus carinatus

Phyllanthus carinatus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves with a distinctive ridge running along the central vein. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes, including treating skin conditions and as a remedy for digestive ailments.
