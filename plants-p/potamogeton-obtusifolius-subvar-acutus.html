Potamogeton obtusifolius subvar. acutus

Potamogeton obtusifolius subvar. acutus, commonly known as the sharp-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spikelets, and it is often found in association with other aquatic vegetation. Its rhizomes help stabilize sediments, contributing to the ecological balance of its habitat.

This species is an important food source for various aquatic invertebrates and herbivorous fish.
