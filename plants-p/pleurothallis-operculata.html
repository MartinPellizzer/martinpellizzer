Pleurothallis operculata

Pleurothallis operculata, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that often have a hood-like structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are usually pale green to white, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Pleurothallis operculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
