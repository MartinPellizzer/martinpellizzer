Potentilla erecta f. monacensis

Potentilla erecta f. monacensis, commonly known as the Monacense cinquefoil, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
