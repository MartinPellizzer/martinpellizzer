Panicum divergens

Panicum divergens, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a tendency to droop at the tips.

Panicum divergens is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
