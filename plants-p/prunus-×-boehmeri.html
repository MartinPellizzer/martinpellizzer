Prunus × boehmeri

Prunus × boehmeri, commonly known as the European bird cherry, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red, fleshy drupes. It is often found in woodland edges and moist soils, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in horticulture.

Prunus × boehmeri is also known for its role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds, due to its fruiting habits.
