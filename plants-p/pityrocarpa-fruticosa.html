Pityrocarpa fruticosa

Pityrocarpa fruticosa, commonly known as the desert date or tamarind tree, is a shrub or small tree native to arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and woody, branching structure adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the wood is valued for its durability.

Pityrocarpa fruticosa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in semi-arid environments.
