Prunus druidum

Prunus druidum, commonly known as the Druid's cherry, is a small deciduous shrub or tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, sweet, edible cherries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for gardens and natural landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is often cultivated for both aesthetic and ecological purposes.
