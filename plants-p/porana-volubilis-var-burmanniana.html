Porana volubilis var. burmanniana

Porana volubilis var. burmanniana, commonly known as the Burmannian porana, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, which bloom in clusters along the stems. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in shaded areas.

The varietal form burmanniana is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and floral characteristics compared to other Porana species.
