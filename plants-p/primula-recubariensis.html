Primula recubariensis

Primula recubariensis, commonly known as the rock primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine environments. Its leaves are basal, ovate to elliptic, and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

Primula recubariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
