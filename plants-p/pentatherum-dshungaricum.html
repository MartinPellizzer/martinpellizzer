Pentatherum dshungaricum

Pentatherum dshungaricum, commonly known as the Siberian grass, is a perennial grass native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern China.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its resilience makes it an important component of alpine and tundra ecosystems.
