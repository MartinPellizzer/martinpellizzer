Poa pratensis var. major

Poa pratensis var. major, commonly known as tall meadow grass, is a variant of the common bentgrass.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
