Piper elongatifolium

Piper elongatifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, pubescent layer. Its small, greenish-white flowers are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences, and it produces small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
