Prunus pilosiuscula var. barbata

Prunus pilosiuscula var. barbata, commonly known as the hairy cherry or barbate cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer. It prefers well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and flowers.
