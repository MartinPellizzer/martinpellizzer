Peperomia claudii

Peperomia claudii, commonly known as the Claudii Peperomia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a compact, low-growing plant with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in spikes, typical of the Piperaceae family. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia claudii is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
