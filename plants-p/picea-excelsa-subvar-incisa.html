Picea excelsa subvar. incisa

Picea excelsa subvar. incisa, commonly known as the Himalayan fir, is a coniferous tree native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The leaves are needle-like, dark green, and often have a distinct serrated edge, which is a key identifying feature of the subvariety. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is adapted to high-altitude conditions with limited soil nutrients.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
