Phaseolus gonospermos var. variegatus

Phaseolus gonospermos var. variegatus, commonly known as the variegated runner bean, is a variant of the runner bean species native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated leaf patterns, which often feature a mix of green, white, and yellow hues. The plant produces elongated, edible pods that are typically harvested when young and tender. Its flowers are usually pale purple or white, and it thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and mild, nutty flavor when cooked.
