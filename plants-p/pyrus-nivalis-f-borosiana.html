Pyrus nivalis f. borosiana

Pyrus nivalis f. borosiana, commonly known as the snow pear.

It is a frost-resistant ornamental plant characterized by its compact growth habit and attractive, slightly hairy leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically small and sweet. It thrives in cooler climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for rock gardens and as a hardy hedge.
