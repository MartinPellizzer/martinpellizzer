Protea hibbertii

Protea hibbertii, commonly known as the Hibbert's king protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers arranged in a rounded head. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique type of shrubland ecosystem.

Protea hibbertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
