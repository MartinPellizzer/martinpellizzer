Pyrus asakeensis

Pyrus asakeensis, commonly known as the Asake apple, is a species of apple tree native to the mountainous regions of eastern China.

It is characterized by its deciduous nature, producing small, round fruits with a yellowish-green skin. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

The flowers are white and clustered, appearing in spring before the leaves fully develop.
