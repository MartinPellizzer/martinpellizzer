Potamogeton strictus

Potamogeton strictus, commonly known as rigid pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across the Northern Hemisphere.

It has long, slender, cylindrical stems that are often branching and can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, green to reddish-brown flowers that are arranged in spike-like clusters, typically emerging from the water surface. Its leaves are narrow, ribbon-like, and arranged in opposite pairs along the stem.

Potamogeton strictus plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
