Poa kamtschatica

Poa kamtschatica, commonly known as Kamchatka bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the cold climates of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green blades that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subarctic conditions.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in cooler regions for landscaping purposes.
