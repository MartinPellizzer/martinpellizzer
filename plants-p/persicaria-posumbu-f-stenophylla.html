Persicaria posumbu f. stenophylla

Persicaria posumbu f. stenophylla, commonly known as narrow-leaf Persicaria, is a variant of the Persicaria genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in aquatic environments.
