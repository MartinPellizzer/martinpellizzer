Pentopetia ovalifolia subsp. glabrata

Pentopetia ovalifolia subsp.

glabrata, commonly known as the smooth-leafed pentopetia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its ovate, glabrous leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
