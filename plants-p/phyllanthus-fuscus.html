Phyllanthus fuscus

Phyllanthus fuscus, commonly known as the black phyllanthus, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense foliage with elliptical, dark green leaves that have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, round, black fruits that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas, disturbed sites, and forest edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
