Paramollugo angustifolia

Paramollugo angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf marshmallow, is a small herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often near water bodies such as ponds or streams. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, with pale yellow or greenish petals, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds.

Paramollugo angustifolia is known for its ability to thrive in moist environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
