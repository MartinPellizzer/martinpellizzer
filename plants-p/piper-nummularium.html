Piper nummularium

Piper nummularium, commonly known as the "coin plant," is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other species like black pepper. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, coin-shaped fruits that are edible. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
