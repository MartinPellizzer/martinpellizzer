Potentilla wilhelminensis

Potentilla wilhelminensis, commonly known as the Wilhelmine cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features palmate leaves with five lobes and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and colorful blooms.
