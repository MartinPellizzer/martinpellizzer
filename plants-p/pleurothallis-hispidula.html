Pleurothallis hispidula

Pleurothallis hispidula, commonly known as the hairy pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its hairy texture on the stems and leaves.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
