Piper fuscinervium

Piper fuscinervium, commonly known as the pink-veined pepper, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with distinctive pink veins, giving it its common name. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
