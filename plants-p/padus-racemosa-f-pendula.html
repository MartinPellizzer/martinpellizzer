Padus racemosa f. pendula

Padus racemosa f. pendula, commonly known as weeping white crabsapple.

This cultivar is a flowering plant in the Rosaceae family, characterized by its weeping growth habit and clusters of small, white flowers. It is a variant of the common white crabsapple, with a more pendulous form that gives it an ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, red or yellow fruits that are edible but often used for ornamental purposes.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
