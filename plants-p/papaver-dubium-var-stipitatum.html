Papaver dubium var. stipitatum

Papaver dubium var. stipitatum, commonly known as the stipitate poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features striking, papery flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive dark center. The plant has a taproot system and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue with a fuzzy texture.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and woodland edges.
