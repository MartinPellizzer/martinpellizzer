Panicum atrichum

Panicum atrichum, commonly known as black panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, dark green leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-based flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as fields, roadsides, and disturbed areas. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it useful for erosion control.

Panicum atrichum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential as a forage crop.
