Palaquium sericeum var. obtusocalyx

Palaquium sericeum var. obtusocalyx, commonly known as the silky palaquium, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
