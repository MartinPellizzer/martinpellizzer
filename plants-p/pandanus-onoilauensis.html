Pandanus onoilauensis

Pandanus onoilauensis, commonly known as the Tongan pandanus, is a member of the Pandanaceae family native to the island of Tonga.

It is a robust shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its thick, fibrous trunk and broad, paddle-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by edible berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Its leaves are also used in traditional weaving and basket-making, highlighting its cultural and practical significance in the region.
