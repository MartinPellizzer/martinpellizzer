Paeonia albiflora var. pottsii

Paeonia albiflora var. pottsii, commonly known as the White Peony or Potts' Peony, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in early spring, often with a subtle pinkish tinge. The plant features compound, trifoliate leaves that are dark green and heart-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blooms and long-lasting cut flowers.
