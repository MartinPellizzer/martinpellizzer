Primula homogama

Primula homogama, commonly known as the silver leaf primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a distinctive yellow center. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are broad and often have a silvery or grayish hue, which helps reflect excess light.

Primula homogama is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
