Pachyphyllum pamplonense

Pachyphyllum pamplonense, commonly known as the Pamplona stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of northern South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
