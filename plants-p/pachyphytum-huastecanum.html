Pachyphytum huastecanum

Pachyphytum huastecanum, commonly known as the Huastec stonecrop, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in regions with low rainfall. Its compact growth habit and vibrant foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring or early summer.
