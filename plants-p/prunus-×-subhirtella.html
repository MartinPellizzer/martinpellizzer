Prunus × subhirtella

Prunus × subhirtella, commonly known as the Kanzan cherry, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is prized for its showy, double-flowered pink buds that open into white petals, creating a striking visual display in spring. The plant has a compact, rounded growth habit and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, though it can tolerate partial shade.

Prunus × subhirtella is also valued for its ornamental fruit, which adds visual interest in the fall.
