Piper tachiranum

Piper tachiranum, commonly known as the Tachiranum pepper, is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, warm climates. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy in appearance. Its small, elongated fruits are typically green when unripe and turn red upon maturity, containing a spicy, peppery flavor.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and for research purposes.
