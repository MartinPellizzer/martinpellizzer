Plantago rigidiuscula

Plantago rigidiuscula, commonly known as rigid hair-grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its upright, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. It has a fibrous root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in dry regions.
