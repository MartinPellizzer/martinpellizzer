Phyllodes textilis

Phyllodes textilis, commonly known as the silky wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its long, slender phyllodes that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its phyllodes are modified leaves that serve as the primary photosynthetic organs.

Phyllodes textilis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
