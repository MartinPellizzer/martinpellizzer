Physalis arenicola

Physalis arenicola, commonly known as the sand physalis, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, inflated calyx that encloses the berry-like fruit, which turns orange-red when mature. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its flowers are small and yellow, appearing in clusters, and it produces edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines.

Physalis arenicola plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
