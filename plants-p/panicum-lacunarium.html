Panicum lacunarium

Panicum lacunarium, commonly known as slender panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle made up of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Panicum lacunarium plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
