Paeonia peregrina var. microcarpa

Paeonia peregrina var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf peony, is a rare and ancient species native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
