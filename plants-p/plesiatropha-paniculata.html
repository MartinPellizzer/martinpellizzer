Plesiatropha paniculata

Plesiatropha paniculata, commonly known as the panicled plesiatropha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in panicles. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
