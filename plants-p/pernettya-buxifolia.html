Pernettya buxifolia

Pernettya buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf pernettya, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage, with leaves that are oval-shaped and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Pernettya buxifolia thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a desirable plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
