Pyrus hakodatensis

Pyrus hakodatensis, commonly known as the Hakodate pear, is a rare species native to Japan.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in cool, temperate climates. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible pears in late summer. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge.

The Hakodate pear is valued for its unique flavor and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
