Phaseolus sphaericus var. haematocarpus

Phaseolus sphaericus var. haematocarpus, commonly known as the blood bean, is a unique variety of the genus Phaseolus native to Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, swollen pods that resemble small, rounded beans. The plant is an annual legume that thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, and it produces a high concentration of anthocyanin pigments, contributing to the vivid coloration of its pods.

This variety is notable for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant in gardens.
