Pueraria phaseoloides var. subspicata

Pueraria phaseoloides var. subspicata, commonly known as the creeping cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by cylindrical, yellowish pods containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its rapid growth makes it both a useful forage crop and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
