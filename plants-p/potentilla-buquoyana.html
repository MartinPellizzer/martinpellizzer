Potentilla buquoyana

Potentilla buquoyana, commonly known as the Chinese cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. It has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla buquoyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
