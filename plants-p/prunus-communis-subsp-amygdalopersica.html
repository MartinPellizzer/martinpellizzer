Prunus communis subsp. amygdalopersica

Prunus communis subsp.

amygdalopersica, commonly known as the Persian almond, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and Central Asia. It produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring, followed by edible drupes that are often used in cooking and traditional medicine.

The fruit contains a single seed surrounded by a hard shell, and the plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and economic importance. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various climates.

The subspecies is closely related to other Prunus species, including the domesticated almond.
