Polygala cruciata subsp. aquilonia

Polygala cruciata subsp.

aquilonia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in spike-like clusters.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with numerous narrow, linear leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Polygalaceae family.
