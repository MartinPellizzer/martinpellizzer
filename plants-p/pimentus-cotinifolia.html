Pimentus cotinifolia

Pimentus cotinifolia, commonly known as the silky filbert, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features palmate leaves with a soft, silvery underside that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by spherical, brown fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage and adaptability make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
