Polypodium loreum

Polypodium loreum, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocky surfaces. The plant has feather-like fronds with a distinctive, serrated margin. Its rhizomes are thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and establish itself in various habitats.

Polypodium loreum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
