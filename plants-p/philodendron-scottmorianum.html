Philodendron scottmorianum

Philodendron scottmorianum, commonly known as the Scott's Philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and cream. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by reddish-brown berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Its vigorous growth habit makes it a popular choice for indoor cultivation and vertical gardening.
