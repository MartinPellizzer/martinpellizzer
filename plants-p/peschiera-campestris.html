Peschiera campestris

Peschiera campestris, commonly known as the field gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along streambanks, preferring well-drained soils. It has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Peschiera campestris is often found in open habitats and is relatively hardy, though it may require specific conditions to thrive.
