Pyrus hodjingensis

Pyrus hodjingensis, commonly known as the Hodjing pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its small, ornamental flowers and edible fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while the fruit is a small, yellowish pear-like berry.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
