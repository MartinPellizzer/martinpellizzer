Prunus hirtipes

Prunus hirtipes, commonly known as the hairy cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Prunus hirtipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
