Persicaria strindbergii

Persicaria strindbergii, commonly known as Strindberg's knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The species is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are smooth and often reddish in color.

Persicaria strindbergii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands.
