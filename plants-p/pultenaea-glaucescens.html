Pultenaea glaucescens

Pultenaea glaucescens, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in heathlands and dry forests. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that appear in clusters, often in spring. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Pultenaea glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
