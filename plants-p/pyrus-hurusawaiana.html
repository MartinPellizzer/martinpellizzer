Pyrus hurusawaiana

Pyrus hurusawaiana, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its deciduous nature, with heart-shaped leaves and white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, sweet fruits that are edible and have a mild flavor. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.

Pyrus hurusawaiana prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
