Pholidia alternifolia

Pholidia alternifolia, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its alternate, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. These berries are edible and have a tangy, acidic flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions.
