Piper mocco-mocco

Piper mocco-mocco, commonly known as the Mocco-mocco pepper, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, and these berries are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The leaves are often used as a spice, similar to black pepper, and have a pungent, spicy flavor.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
