Polybotrya costaricensis

Polybotrya costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as tropical forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizome is short and subterranean, supporting the growth of its aerial fronds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat.
