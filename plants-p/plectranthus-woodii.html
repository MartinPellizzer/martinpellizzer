Plectranthus woodii

Plectranthus woodii, commonly known as the Cape nettle, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive net-like vein pattern. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus woodii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
