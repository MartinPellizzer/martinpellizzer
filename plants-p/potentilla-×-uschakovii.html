Potentilla × uschakovii

Potentilla × uschakovii, commonly known as the Uschakovii cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval to lance-shaped leaflets, and are often hairy as well.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and ornamental choice for gardens in temperate climates.
