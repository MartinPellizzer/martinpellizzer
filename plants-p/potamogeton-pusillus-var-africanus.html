Potamogeton pusillus var. africanus

Potamogeton pusillus var. africanus, commonly known as the African water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in Africa.

It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spikes. This species thrives in slow-moving or still water bodies, such as lakes and ponds, where it can form dense underwater vegetation. It plays an important role in aquatic habitats by providing shelter for fish and invertebrates.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and healthy aquatic ecosystems.
