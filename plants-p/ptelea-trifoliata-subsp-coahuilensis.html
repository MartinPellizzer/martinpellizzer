Ptelea trifoliata subsp. coahuilensis

Ptelea trifoliata subsp.

coahuilensis, commonly known as the Coahuiltecan hawthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, red berries in the fall.

The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, which are often serrated along the edges. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along canyon walls.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
