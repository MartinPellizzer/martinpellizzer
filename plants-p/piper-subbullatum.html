Piper subbullatum

Piper subbullatum, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper or subbullate pepper, is a tropical climbing shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by oval, fleshy berries that turn red when mature. Its leaves are usually broad and heart-shaped, with a slightly rough texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
