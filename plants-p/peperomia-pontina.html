Peperomia pontina

Peperomia pontina, commonly known as the Sicilian pepper plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems covered in small, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, round berries. Peperomia pontina thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
