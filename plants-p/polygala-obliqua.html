Polygala obliqua

Polygala obliqua, commonly known as the zigzag lily or Chinese lily, is a flowering plant native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, zigzag-shaped inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a waxy coating.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a unique structure that includes a spurred petal, which is a notable botanical feature.
