Piper striatum

Piper striatum, commonly known as the striped pepper or striated pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as the common black pepper. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
