Piper eglandulosum

Piper eglandulosum, commonly known as the creeping pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that are followed by small, elliptical berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.

The plant's botanical features make it suitable for ornamental use in shaded garden environments.
