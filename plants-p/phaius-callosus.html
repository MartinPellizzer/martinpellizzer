Phaius callosus

Phaius callosus, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has broad, strap-like leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a spike. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Phaius callosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
