Pleurothallis plantaginea

Pleurothallis plantaginea, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often have a pink or white coloration. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are notable for their unique shape, resembling a slipper, and it produces a fragrant scent to attract pollinators.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its aesthetic appeal.
