Phlox maculata var. gracilior

Phlox maculata var. gracilior, commonly known as the slender garden phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
