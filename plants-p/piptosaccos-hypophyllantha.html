Piptosaccos hypophyllantha

Piptosaccos hypophyllantha, commonly known as the lace fern, is a unique member of the Pteridaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that give it a lace-like appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizome is slender and creeping, allowing it to spread slowly across the forest floor.

The spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
