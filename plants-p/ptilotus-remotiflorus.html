Ptilotus remotiflorus

Ptilotus remotiflorus, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its soft, woolly foliage and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability. Its growth habit is typically prostrate or spreading, forming dense mats that help reduce soil erosion.

Ptilotus remotiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
