Prunus mume var. alboplena

Prunus mume var. alboplena, commonly known as the white-flowered Chinese plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange fruits known as prunes, which are edible but typically not consumed fresh. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its cultural significance extends to traditional Chinese medicine and as a symbol of resilience and renewal in various cultural contexts.
