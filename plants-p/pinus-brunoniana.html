Pinus brunoniana

Pinus brunoniana, commonly known as the Brunon's pine, is a rare and endemic conifer native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in cold, high-altitude environments. The tree has needle-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, and it produces distinctive cones that are often covered in a thick, resinous coating. It is adapted to harsh climatic conditions, including strong winds and limited soil nutrients.
