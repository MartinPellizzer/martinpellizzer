Potentilla etomentosa var. hallii

Potentilla etomentosa var. hallii, commonly known as the woolly cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and cliffs, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are glandular and covered in fine, soft hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
