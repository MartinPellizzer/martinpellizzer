Passiflora chrysophylla f. apaensis

Passiflora chrysophylla f. apaensis, commonly known as the golden-leaf passionflower, is a rare and striking variant of the passionflower genus.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow foliage, which contrasts beautifully with its typical white and purple floral structures. Native to specific regions in South America, this plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity. Its unique leaf coloration makes it a sought-after species among horticulturists and botanical enthusiasts.

The plant is also notable for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.
