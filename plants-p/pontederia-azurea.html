Pontederia azurea

Pontederia azurea, commonly known as blue water hyacinth, is a semi-aquatic flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and heart-shaped, floating leaves. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes. Its roots are submerged, and it can form dense mats on the water surface.

Pontederia azurea is also known for its ability to absorb heavy metals, making it useful in phytoremediation efforts.
