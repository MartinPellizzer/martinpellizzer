Potamogeton repens

Potamogeton repens, commonly known as curly pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across the Northern Hemisphere.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are often coiled or twisted, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters near the water's surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Potamogeton repens is often found in still or slow-moving waters such as ponds, lakes, and canals.
