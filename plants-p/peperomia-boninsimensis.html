Peperomia boninsimensis

Peperomia boninsimensis, commonly known as the Bonin peperomia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Bonin Islands, a group of islands located in the Pacific Ocean. This plant is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. The leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern, and the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

It is a popular houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements and attractive foliage.
