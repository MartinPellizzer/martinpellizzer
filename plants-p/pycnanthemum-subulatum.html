Pycnanthemum subulatum

Pycnanthemum subulatum, commonly known as slender mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. It is valued for its aromatic foliage and its role in supporting local pollinators.

Pycnanthemum subulatum is also used in herbal medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
