Podocarpus madagascariensis var. madagascariensis

Podocarpus madagascariensis var. madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, typical of the Podocarpus genus. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in montane rainforests.

This species is valued for its durable wood and is also important in local ecosystems.
