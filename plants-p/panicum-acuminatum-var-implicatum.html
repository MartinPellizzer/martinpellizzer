Panicum acuminatum var. implicatum

Panicum acuminatum var. implicatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, with narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it useful in erosion control.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
