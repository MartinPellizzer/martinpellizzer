Phragmipedium hirtzii var. anchicayense

Phragmipedium hirtzii var. anchicayense, commonly known as the Anchicayana phragmipedium, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its elongated, pendulous flower spikes that bear numerous showy, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, often with a waxy texture to aid in water retention.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display and adaptability in cultivation.
