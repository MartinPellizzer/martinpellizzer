Pickeringia montana var. tomentosa

Pickeringia montana var. tomentosa, commonly known as the tomentose golden bell, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that appear in late spring. The plant has hairy, grayish-green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
