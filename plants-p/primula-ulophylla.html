Primula ulophylla

Primula ulophylla, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, with a hairy texture that helps protect it from harsh weather.

Primula ulophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
