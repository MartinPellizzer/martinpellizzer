Premna moluccana

Premna moluccana, commonly known as the Indian nettle or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a rough, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
