Paspalum lanuginosum

Paspalum lanuginosum, commonly known as silky paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often forming dense mats. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread vegetatively.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a potential forage crop.
