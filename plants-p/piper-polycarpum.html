Piper polycarpum

Piper polycarpum, commonly known as the Indian black pepper plant, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its dark, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant has compound leaves with ovate lobes and produces small, green flowers that develop into the aromatic berries. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a distinctive, pungent aroma.

Piper polycarpum is valued for its medicinal properties and is often cultivated for its fruit.
