Pandanus loherianus

Pandanus loherianus, commonly known as the Loher's pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. Its inflorescence consists of a branched structure bearing small, fragrant flowers.

The plant is adapted to coastal and wetland environments, making it an important component of mangrove ecosystems.
