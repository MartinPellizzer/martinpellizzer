Polygonum laxum

Polygonum laxum, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive nodes and often triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a waxy coating.

The small, clustered flowers are usually greenish-white and appear in late summer, contributing to its role in ecological systems.
