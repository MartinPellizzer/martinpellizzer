Phaseolus unguiculatus

Phaseolus unguiculatus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with hollow, green stems and compound leaves. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers that give way to elongated, bean-like pods. The seeds inside the pods are typically white or yellow and are harvested when mature.

It is valued for its nutritional content and is cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
