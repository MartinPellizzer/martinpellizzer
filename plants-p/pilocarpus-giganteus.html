Pilocarpus giganteus

Pilocarpus giganteus, commonly known as the jurema or Brazilian pepper tree, is a leguminous plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in sandy or well-drained soils.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential stimulant and diuretic properties.
