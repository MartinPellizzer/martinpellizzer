Prunus watsonii

Prunus watsonii, commonly known as the Chinese dwarf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, dark red to black fruits, which are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Prunus watsonii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various climates.
