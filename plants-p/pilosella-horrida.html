Pilosella horrida

Pilosella horrida, commonly known as the hairy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features dense, woolly hairs on its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Pilosella horrida produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.
