Pleurothallis llamachoi

Pleurothallis llamachoi, commonly known as the Llamachoi orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered in a dense layer of fine, soft hairs.

This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
