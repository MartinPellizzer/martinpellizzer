Peltogyne parvifolia subsp. parvifolia

Peltogyne parvifolia subsp.

parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved ironwood, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically clustered at the base of the stem.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, winged seeds. It is known for its hard, dense wood, which is highly valued for construction and furniture.

The species is often found in lowland rainforests and is adapted to humid, warm climates.
