Porana confertifolia

Porana confertifolia, commonly known as the clustered porana, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Porana confertifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
