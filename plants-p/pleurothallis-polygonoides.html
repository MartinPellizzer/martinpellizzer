Pleurothallis polygonoides

Pleurothallis polygonoides, commonly known as the polygon-like orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees and other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts and horticulturists.
