Pinanga costata

Pinanga costata, commonly known as the serrated bamboo or broad-leaved bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, a common feature in the Araceae family. Pinanga costata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires moist, well-drained soil for optimal growth.
