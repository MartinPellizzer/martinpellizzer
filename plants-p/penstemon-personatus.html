Penstemon personatus

Penstemon personatus, commonly known as the blue penstemon, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, tubular blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Penstemon personatus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
