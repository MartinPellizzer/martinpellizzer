Polygala amaurocarpa

Polygala amaurocarpa, commonly known as the dark-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that are typically white or pale in color. The plant has compound leaves with a dark green hue, which helps it thrive in shaded environments. It produces small, yellowish fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
