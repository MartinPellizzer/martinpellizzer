Passerina thomasii

Passerina thomasii, commonly known as the Thomas's pasqueflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. It produces a single, erect stem with several leaves that are palmately divided.
