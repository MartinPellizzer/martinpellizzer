Prunus pubigera var. ohwii

Prunus pubigera var. ohwii, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum.

This variant is characterized by its small, sweet fruits that ripen in late spring. It is native to Japan and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It produces fragrant white flowers in early spring, adding to its aesthetic appeal.
