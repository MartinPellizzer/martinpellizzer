Peschiera umbrosa

Peschiera umbrosa, commonly known as the shadowy orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is characterized by its slender, dark green leaves and striking, violet-blue flowers. The plant has small, intricate flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting specific pollinators such as certain species of bees and moths. Due to its limited habitat and threats from deforestation, Peschiera umbrosa is classified as an endangered species.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations to low-light conditions and its role in the ecological balance of its native ecosystems.
