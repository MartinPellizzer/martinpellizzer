Psidium quinquedentatum

Psidium quinquedentatum, commonly known as the five-toothed guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have five lobes, giving it the common name.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
