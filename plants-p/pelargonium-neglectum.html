Pelargonium neglectum

Pelargonium neglectum, commonly known as the common geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
