Pulsatilla alpina subsp. cottianaea

Pulsatilla alpina subsp.

cottianaea, commonly known as the alpine pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces follicular fruits that contain seeds with tufts of silky hair.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
