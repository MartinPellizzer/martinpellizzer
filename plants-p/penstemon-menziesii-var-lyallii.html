Penstemon menziesii var. lyallii

Penstemon menziesii var. lyallii, commonly known as Lyall's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
