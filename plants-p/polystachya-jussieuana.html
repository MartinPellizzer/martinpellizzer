Polystachya jussieuana

Polystachya jussieuana, commonly known as the Jussieu's sword fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polystachyaceae and is characterized by its sword-shaped, lanceolate leaves that are often clustered in tufts. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spore-bearing capsules, typical of its reproductive strategy. It thrives in shaded, humid environments, often growing on trees or rocks in montane forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
