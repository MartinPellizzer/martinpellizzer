Prunus phaeosticta var. promecocarpa

Prunus phaeosticta var. promecocarpa, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or Japanese dwarf cherry, is a small deciduous tree or shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive spring blossoms.

This variety is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
