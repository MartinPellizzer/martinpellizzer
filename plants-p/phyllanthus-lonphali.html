Phyllanthus lonphali

Phyllanthus lonphali, commonly known as the African spurge, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and alternate, simple leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
