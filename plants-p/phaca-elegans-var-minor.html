Phaca elegans var. minor

Phaca elegans var. minor, commonly known as the slender phaca, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its slender stems and compact growth habit make it an attractive addition to water gardens or bog landscapes.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
