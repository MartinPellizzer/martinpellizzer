Potentilla patellifera

Potentilla patellifera, commonly known as the flat-leaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically rounded and arranged in a distinctive five-leaf pattern, giving it its common name.

Potentilla patellifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and adaptability.
