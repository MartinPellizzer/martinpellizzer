Pyrus communis var. pyraster

Pyrus communis var. pyraster, commonly known as the common pear, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It features a broad, rounded crown and produces white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The fruit is a sweet, edible pear that ripens in late summer to early autumn. The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in traditional agriculture.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
