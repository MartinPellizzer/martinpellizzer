Pleurostachys panicoides

Pleurostachys panicoides, commonly known as the panicle grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and panicle-like inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-bearing flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are linear and often have a rough texture, while its roots form dense networks that help stabilize soil.

Pleurostachys panicoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
