Penstemon laetus subsp. leptosepalus

Penstemon laetus subsp.

leptosepalus, commonly known as the slender-sepal penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and the plant thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

This subspecies is part of the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is known for its adaptability to arid environments.
