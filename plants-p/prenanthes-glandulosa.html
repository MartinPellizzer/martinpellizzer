Prenanthes glandulosa

Prenanthes glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular waterleaf, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clustered inflorescences during the summer months. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
