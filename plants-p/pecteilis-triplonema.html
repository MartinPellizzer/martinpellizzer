Pecteilis triplonema

Pecteilis triplonema, commonly known as the three-veined waterplantain, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating in shallow water. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its roots help stabilize soil and improve water quality in aquatic environments.

Pecteilis triplonema is an important species for both ecological and botanical study due to its unique adaptations to wetland ecosystems.
