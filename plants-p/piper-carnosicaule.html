Piper carnosicaule

Piper carnosicaule, commonly known as the roughleaf pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small berries that mature to a dark purple color.

Piper carnosicaule is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and is used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
