Polygala discolor

Polygala discolor, commonly known as the white milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to drought conditions and thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

Polygala discolor plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
