Polygonum hydropiper var. obtusifolium

Polygonum hydropiper var. obtusifolium, commonly known as water pepper, is a variant of the polygonum species characterized by its distinctive, slightly hairy leaves and small, clustered flowers.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist or wet environments, such as marshes and stream banks. The plant has a shallow root system and produces small, reddish-brown seeds that are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

This plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
