Pelargonium × fasciculatum

Pelargonium × fasciculatum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to cascade gracefully from elevated planters.
