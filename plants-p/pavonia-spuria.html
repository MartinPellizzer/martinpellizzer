Pavonia spuria

Pavonia spuria, commonly known as the slender pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along riverbanks and woodland edges. The plant features showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and serrated edges.

Pavonia spuria is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun conditions.
