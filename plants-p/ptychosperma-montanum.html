Ptychosperma montanum

Ptychosperma montanum, commonly known as the mountain cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a dioecious plant, meaning it has separate male and female plants, and produces cones rather than flowers. The plant has a stout, unbranched trunk with a crown of pinnately compound leaves that are leathery and dark green. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Its slow growth rate and long lifespan make it a unique and valuable species in botanical studies.
