Potentilla ochreata

Potentilla ochreata, commonly known as the yellow cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has compound leaves with five leaflets and produces small, fluffy fruits known as achenes.

Potentilla ochreata is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
