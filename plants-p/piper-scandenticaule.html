Piper scandenticaule

Piper scandenticaule, commonly known as the climbing pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
