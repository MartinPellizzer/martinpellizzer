Polygala bahiensis

Polygala bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahamian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the Bahamas.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, showing a preference for well-drained environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Polygala bahiensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
