Poa thessala

Poa thessala, commonly known as the Thessalian meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Poa thessala is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining soil stability.
