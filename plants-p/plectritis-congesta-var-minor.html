Plectritis congesta var. minor

Plectritis congesta var. minor, commonly known as the lesser purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small cones. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.

Its foliage is usually gray-green with hairy leaves, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
