Pilosella colobima

Pilosella colobima, commonly known as the woolly groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a low-growing habit with silver-gray, woolly foliage that gives it its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow with a central disk, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Pilosella colobima is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
