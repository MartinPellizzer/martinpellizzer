Peperomia puteolata

Peperomia puteolata, commonly known as the swamp peperomia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps it retain moisture in its humid, swampy habitat. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats in its natural environment.

Peperomia puteolata is well adapted to waterlogged soils and is often found in marshy areas along riverbanks and wetlands.
