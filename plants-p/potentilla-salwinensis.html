Potentilla salwinensis

Potentilla salwinensis, commonly known as the Salween potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Potentilla salwinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact size and seasonal blooms.
