Piper pseudogaragaranum

Piper pseudogaragaranum, commonly known as the Bornean pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in local cuisines for their pungent flavor.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for both its culinary and medicinal properties.
