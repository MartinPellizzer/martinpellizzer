Picrasma quassioides var. glabrescens

Picrasma quassioides var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth quassia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Simaroubaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, woody fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its medicinal properties, with parts of the plant used in traditional remedies.

The variety glabrescens is distinguished by its reduced hair coverage compared to other varieties of Picrasma quassioides.
