Pelargonium cordatum

Pelargonium cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and velvety in texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often blooming throughout the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium cordatum is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in herbal remedies.
