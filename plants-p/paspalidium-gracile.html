Paspalidium gracile

Paspalidium gracile, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, often forming dense mats. The plant has slender, tufted stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brown flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight bluish-gray hue.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
