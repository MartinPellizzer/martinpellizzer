Polypodium paradisiastrum f. pectinatum

Polypodium paradisiastrum f. pectinatum, commonly known as the pectinately veined fern, is a rare fern species characterized by its distinctive frond morphology.

Its fronds are typically pinnately compound, with pinnules that have a unique pectinate arrangement, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. It possesses a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

The spore-bearing structures, or sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
