Piper jamaicense f. cuneatum

Piper jamaicense f. cuneatum, commonly known as the Jamaican pepper or cuneatum pepper, is a variant of the Piper jamaicense species native to the Caribbean.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its elongated, heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, peppercorn-like fruits that are typically harvested when they turn red. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

This plant is valued for its aromatic foliage and potential medicinal properties.
