Pteris aquilina var. psittacina

Pteris aquilina var. psittacina, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a variant of the water fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features delicate, bright green fronds with a distinctive golden hue on the underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sori, which are arranged in clusters along the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
