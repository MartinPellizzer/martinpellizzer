Polygonum leptopodum

Polygonum leptopodum, commonly known as slender polygonum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in wetlands, stream banks, and other riparian habitats. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and are usually pink or red, attracting various pollinators.

Polygonum leptopodum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
