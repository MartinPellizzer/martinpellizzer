Parajubaea torallyi

Parajubaea torallyi, commonly known as the Toral's areca, is a species of palm native to the Andes Mountains of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are arranged in branched inflorescences, followed by black, spherical fruits. It thrives in humid, high-altitude environments and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered an endangered species.
