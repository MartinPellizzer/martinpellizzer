Pyrus divisa

Pyrus divisa, commonly known as the divided pear, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits in late summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Pyrus divisa is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
