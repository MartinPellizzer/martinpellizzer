Piper polisanum

Piper polisanum, commonly known as the Malayan pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a glossy surface and a strong aromatic scent.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
