Polygala purpusii

Polygala purpusii, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygalaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
