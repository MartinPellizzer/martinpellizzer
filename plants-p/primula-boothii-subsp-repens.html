Primula boothii subsp. repens

Primula boothii subsp.

repens, commonly known as the creeping primula, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in early spring.

The plant has a mat-forming habit, with hairy, oval leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for rock gardens and shady borders.
