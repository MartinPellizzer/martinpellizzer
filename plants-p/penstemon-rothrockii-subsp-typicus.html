Penstemon rothrockii subsp. typicus

Penstemon rothrockii subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the Rothrock's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Appalachian Mountains of the United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silky hair, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
