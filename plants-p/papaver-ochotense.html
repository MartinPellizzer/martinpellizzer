Papaver ochotense

Papaver ochotense, commonly known as the Ochotense poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, often with a distinctive black or purple blotch at the base. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Papaver ochotense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
