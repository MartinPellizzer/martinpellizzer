Pistolochia macrocentra

Pistolochia macrocentra, commonly known as the broad-leafed pistolpod, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody, branched stems and compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The fruit is a hard, pod-like structure that contains several seeds.
