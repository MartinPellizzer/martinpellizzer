Phyllanthus rosellus

Phyllanthus rosellus, commonly known as the Rosell's spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, with smooth, glabrous stems and alternate, elliptic or ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
