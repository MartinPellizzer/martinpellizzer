Polygonum watsonii

Polygonum watsonii, commonly known as the Watson's knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often tinged with red, and its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, and it is often found in moist, shady environments.

Polygonum watsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
