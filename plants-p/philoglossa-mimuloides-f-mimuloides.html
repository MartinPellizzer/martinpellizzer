Philoglossa mimuloides f. mimuloides

Philoglossa mimuloides f. mimuloides, commonly known as the yellow mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
