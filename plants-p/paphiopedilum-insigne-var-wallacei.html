Paphiopedilum insigne var. wallacei

Paphiopedilum insigne var. wallacei, commonly known as the Wallace's Phalaenopsis, is a rare and striking orchid native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive, intricate lip that resembles a butterfly. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, often found in shaded, moist forest environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing structural support for the heavy flower spikes.

This variety is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to its beauty and rarity in cultivation.
