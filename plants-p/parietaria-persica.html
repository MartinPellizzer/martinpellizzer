Parietaria persica

Parietaria persica, commonly known as Persian wall lettuce, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves that are often ribbed and have a slightly bitter taste. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found growing in rocky or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a unique and hardy plant suitable for both ornamental and medicinal use.
