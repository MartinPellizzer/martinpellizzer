Polygala alpestris subsp. croatica

Polygala alpestris subsp.

croatica, commonly known as the Croatian rock polygala, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in mountainous areas.

The plant has small, delicate flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in harsh, arid conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
