Pteracanthus calycinus

Pteracanthus calycinus, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pteracanthus calycinus is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and resilience to drought.
