Petrea asperifolia

Petrea asperifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, rough-textured leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas or near water. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a distinctive appearance.

The fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red as it matures.
