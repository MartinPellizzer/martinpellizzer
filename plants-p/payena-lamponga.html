Payena lamponga

Payena lamponga, commonly known as the red-flowered payena, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Payena lamponga is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
