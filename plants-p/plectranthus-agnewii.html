Plectranthus agnewii

Plectranthus agnewii, commonly known as Agnew's mint, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often gray-green in color. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue, arranged in whorls along the stems.

Plectranthus agnewii is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and fragrant leaves.
