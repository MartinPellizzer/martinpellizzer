Phyllanthus macraei

Phyllanthus macraei, commonly known as the African starfruit or wild starfruit, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glabrous and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fruiting structures known as cyathia. Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used in various African cultures for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to different soil types.
