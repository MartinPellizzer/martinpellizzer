Portulaca linifolia

Portulaca linifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf purslane, is a low-growing annual plant native to southern Africa.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine, thread-like hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close by afternoon. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to hot, dry climates.

Portulaca linifolia is often found in sandy soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
