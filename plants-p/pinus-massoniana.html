Pinus massoniana

Pinus massoniana, commonly known as the Chinese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to southeastern China.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, pyramidal crown, which provides it with a distinctive appearance. The tree produces needle-like leaves that are typically 2 to 5 centimeters long, arranged in bundles of five. Its cones are woody and contain two-winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Pinus massoniana is valued for its durable timber and is often planted for reforestation and ornamental purposes.
