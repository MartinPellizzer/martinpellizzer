Prunus jamasakura f. laeviflora

Prunus jamasakura f. laeviflora, commonly known as the flat-flowered cherry, is a variant of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its smooth, flat petals that give it a distinctive appearance compared to the typical rounded petals of other cherry blossoms. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value during the spring blooming season. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree with a graceful, spreading form.

The flowers are pink to white in color and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
