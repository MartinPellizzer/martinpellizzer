Pseudocymbidium canarinum

Pseudocymbidium canarinum, commonly known as the Canary Island orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Canary Islands.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit with a dense cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with a contrasting lip. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
