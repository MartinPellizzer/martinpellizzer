Panicum striatissimum

Panicum striatissimum, commonly known as the striped grass, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves with distinctive white or silver stripes running along the midrib. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, feather-like flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental and erosion control purposes.

Its drought tolerance and ability to form dense mats make it suitable for various landscaping applications.
