Phyteuma orbiculare var. inaequatum

Phyteuma orbiculare var. inaequatum, commonly known as the unequal globe flower, is a perennial herb native to central and eastern Europe.

It features showy, globe-shaped flower heads that are typically pink to purple in color, with a distinctive, slightly asymmetrical shape. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces a tall, branching stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its historical use in traditional medicine.
