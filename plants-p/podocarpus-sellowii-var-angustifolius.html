Podocarpus sellowii var. angustifolius

Podocarpus sellowii var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yellowwood, is a evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It features lance-shaped, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically narrow and arranged spirally along the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, cone-like structures containing edible, fleshy seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its hardy and adaptable nature.
