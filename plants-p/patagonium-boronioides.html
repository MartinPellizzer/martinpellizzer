Patagonium boronioides

Patagonium boronioides, commonly known as the Patagonian thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant is known for its robust, spiny stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in grasslands and shrublands.

Patagonium boronioides plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various insects and small animals.
