Papaver strictum

Papaver strictum, commonly known as the narrowleaf poppy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Papaver strictum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
