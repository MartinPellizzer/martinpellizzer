Pleurothallis apopsis

Pleurothallis apopsis, commonly known as the silver orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its flowers are typically clustered in a raceme and bloom during the rainy season.

Pleurothallis apopsis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
