Primula parryi var. wilcoxiana

Primula parryi var. wilcoxiana, commonly known as the Wilcox's primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate in shape, often with a hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
