Piper viticaule

Piper viticaule, commonly known as small-leaved pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant features slender, trailing stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of aromatic compounds.
