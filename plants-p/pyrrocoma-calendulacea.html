Pyrrocoma calendulacea

Pyrrocoma calendulacea, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces a single, cup-shaped inflorescence. It thrives in rocky soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
