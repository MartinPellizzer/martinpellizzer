Pandanus monotheca

Pandanus monotheca, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Indo-Pacific, including parts of Australia, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. Its inflorescence consists of a branched cluster of small, fragrant flowers, which are typically yellow or green in color.

The plant thrives in coastal and wetland environments, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat for various insects and birds.
