Plantago aitchisonii

Plantago aitchisonii, commonly known as the Aitchison's plantain, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Pakistan and India.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to its harsh environment. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are pollinated by wind. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
