Panicum simulans

Panicum simulans, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and tall, slender flowering spikes that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions. Its growth habit is clump-forming, and it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Panicum simulans is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
