Potentilla polymorpha var. major

Potentilla polymorpha var. major, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
