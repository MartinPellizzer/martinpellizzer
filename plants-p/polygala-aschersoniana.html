Polygala aschersoniana

Polygala aschersoniana, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Polygala genus. It has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through deep root systems and reduced leaf surfaces.

The botanical features of Polygala aschersoniana make it an important species for ecological studies in desert ecosystems.
