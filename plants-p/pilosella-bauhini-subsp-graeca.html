Pilosella bauhini subsp. graeca

Pilosella bauhini subsp.

graeca, commonly known as the Greek mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and daisy-like yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
