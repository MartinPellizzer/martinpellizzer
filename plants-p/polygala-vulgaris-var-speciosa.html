Polygala vulgaris var. speciosa

Polygala vulgaris var. speciosa, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically pale pink to white, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the base. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as coastal dunes and dry meadows.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
