Potamogeton densus var. serratus

Potamogeton densus var. serratus, commonly known as the serrated dense pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and serrated leaf margins, which distinguish it from other pondweed species. The plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and forms dense mats that provide habitat for aquatic life. Its rhizomes anchor it to the substrate, and it reproduces both sexually and asexually through vegetative propagation.

It plays a crucial role in water filtration and supports a diverse array of aquatic organisms.
