Phoebe belizensis

Phoebe belizensis, commonly known as the Belize phoebe, is a flowering plant native to Central America, particularly Belize.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
