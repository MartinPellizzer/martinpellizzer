Polygonum virescens

Polygonum virescens, commonly known as prostrate knotweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and distinctive heart-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purple in color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found along stream banks and in wetlands.

Polygonum virescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
