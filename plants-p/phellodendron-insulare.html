Phellodendron insulare

Phellodendron insulare, commonly known as the island phellodendron, is a small, deciduous tree native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits containing single seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
