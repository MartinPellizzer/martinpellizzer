Papuacedrus arfakensis

Papuacedrus arfakensis, commonly known as the Arfakensis cypress, is a rare and ancient conifer native to the high-altitude regions of Papua New Guinea.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and dense, scale-like foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous cones that contain the seeds, typical of coniferous species. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and its role in the ecological systems of its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
