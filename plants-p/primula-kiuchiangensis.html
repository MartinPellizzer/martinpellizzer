Primula kiuchiangensis

Primula kiuchiangensis, commonly known as the Kiu-chiang primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing clusters of pale yellow to white petals with a contrasting dark center. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Primula kiuchiangensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and shaded borders.
