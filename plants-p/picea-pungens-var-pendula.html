Picea pungens var. pendula

Picea pungens var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping spruce, is a cultivar of the white spruce tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive cascading foliage that hangs in long, drooping branches. The plant is native to the Rocky Mountains of North America and thrives in USDA hardiness zones 3 to 7. Its needle-like leaves are dark green and remain on the plant year-round, providing evergreen coverage.

The weeping spruce is often cultivated as an ornamental tree for its unique, flowing appearance.
