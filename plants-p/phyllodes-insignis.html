Phyllodes insignis

Phyllodes insignis, commonly known as the silky wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, and silky-hairy phyllodes, which are modified leaves used for photosynthesis. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Phyllodes insignis is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
