Protea heckmanniana

Protea heckmanniana, commonly known as the Hecksman protea, is a striking member of the Proteaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a rounded head. The plant typically grows in coastal sandstone formations and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers come in shades of pink, red, and white, often with a distinctive yellow or green center.

Protea heckmanniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and long-lasting blooms.
