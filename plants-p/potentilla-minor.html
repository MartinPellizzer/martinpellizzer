Potentilla minor

Potentilla minor, commonly known as dwarf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It typically reaches a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and features dense, mat-forming growth. The plant has small, five-lobed leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are yellow, often with five petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Potentilla minor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native plant landscaping.
