Poa dioeca

Poa dioeca, commonly known as the purple mountain grass, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Poa dioeca is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
