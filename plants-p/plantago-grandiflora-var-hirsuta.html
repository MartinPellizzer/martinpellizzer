Plantago grandiflora var. hirsuta

Plantago grandiflora var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in the summer, which are arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
