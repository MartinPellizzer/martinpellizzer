Pinguicula vallisneriifolia

Pinguicula vallisneriifolia, commonly known as the Mexican butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, sticky leaves that trap and digest small insects. The plant produces a single, showy white or pink flower during the growing season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a unique and fascinating example of adaptive evolution in the plant kingdom.
