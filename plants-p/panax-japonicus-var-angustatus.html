Panax japonicus var. angustatus

Panax japonicus var. angustatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ginseng, is a variant of the ginseng genus native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and a root system that is often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in shaded, forested environments with rich, moist soil. Its botanical features include a taproot and opposite, lanceolate leaves.

It is closely related to other Panax species but is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and habitat preferences.
