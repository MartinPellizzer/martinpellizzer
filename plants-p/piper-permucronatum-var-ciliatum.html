Piper permucronatum var. ciliatum

Piper permucronatum var. ciliatum, commonly known as the hairy sycamore pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with cilia along the margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, fleshy berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental foliage and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
