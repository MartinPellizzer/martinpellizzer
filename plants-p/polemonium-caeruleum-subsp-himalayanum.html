Polemonium caeruleum subsp. himalayanum

Polemonium caeruleum subsp.

himalayanum, commonly known as the HimalayanJacob's ladder, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia. It features slender, ascending stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
