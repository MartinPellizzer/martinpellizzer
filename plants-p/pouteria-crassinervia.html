Pouteria crassinervia

Pouteria crassinervia, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces distinctive, woody fruits that contain a single large seed, which is the source of the commercially valuable Brazil nut. Its bark and leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Pouteria crassinervia plays a vital role in the ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
