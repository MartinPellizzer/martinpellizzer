Pouteria salicifolia var. neriifolia

Pouteria salicifolia var. neriifolia, commonly known as the white-leafed spondias, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale green to white leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
