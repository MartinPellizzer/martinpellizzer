Pouteria belizensis

Pouteria belizensis, commonly known as the Belize sapote, is a tropical tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish-green fruits. The fruit has a thick, inedible rind and a sweet, creamy pulp that is edible. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and its fruit, which is used in local cuisines.
