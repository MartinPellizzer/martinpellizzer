Phaseolus costaricensis

Phaseolus costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican bean, is a species native to Central America.

It is a climbing legume with trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers, which are typical of the Phaseolus genus. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for cultivation and genetic diversity.
