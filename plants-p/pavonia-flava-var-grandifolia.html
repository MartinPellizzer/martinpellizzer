Pavonia flava var. grandifolia

Pavonia flava var. grandifolia, commonly known as yellow woodflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
