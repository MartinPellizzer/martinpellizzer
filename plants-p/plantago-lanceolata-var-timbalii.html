Plantago lanceolata var. timbalii

Plantago lanceolata var. timbalii, commonly known as the Timbalai plantain, is a variant of the common plantain, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and robust growth habit.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in a variety of soil types, including disturbed soils and urban environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes, which are often hidden among the foliage. Its leaves are thick and ribbed, with a hairy texture, and are commonly used in traditional medicine.

This variety is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas.
