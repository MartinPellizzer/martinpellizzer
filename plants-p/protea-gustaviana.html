Protea gustaviana

Protea gustaviana, commonly known as the Knysna protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that consist of multiple bracts and numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant is native to the Western Cape and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in coastal fynbos vegetation. Its flowers are typically pink to red in color, with a distinctive yellow center, and it blooms from late autumn to early spring.

Protea gustaviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
