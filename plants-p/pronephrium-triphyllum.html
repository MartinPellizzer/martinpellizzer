Pronephrium triphyllum

Pronephrium triphyllum, commonly known as the three-leaf bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to freshwater habitats in Southeast Asia.

It possesses specialized bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms, which it then digests for nutrients. The plant's leaves are typically three-lobed, giving it its scientific name, and it thrives in nutrient-poor water environments. Its botanical structure includes submerged and floating leaves, with the bladders located on the lower surfaces of the leaves.
