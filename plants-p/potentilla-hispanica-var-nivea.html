Potentilla hispanica var. nivea

Potentilla hispanica var. nivea, commonly known as the white potentilla, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silvery-white bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
