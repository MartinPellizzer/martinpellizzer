Pilosella albocinerea

Pilosella albocinerea, commonly known as the white-hairy groundsel, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and small, white to pale yellow flowers. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and often easily identifiable species within its native range.
