Protium plagiocarpium

Protium plagiocarpium, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, with yellowish-green petals, and it produces woody, oval fruits.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
