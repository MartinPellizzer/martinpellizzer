Passiflora × loudonii

Passiflora × loudonii, commonly known as the Purple Passionflower, is a hybrid species of the passionflower genus.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its striking purple flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, featuring heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that aid in its ascent. Its flowers are pollinated by various insects and bats, contributing to its ecological significance.

The fruit, which is edible, develops into a small, yellowish berry containing a single seed.
