Pultenaea parrisiae

Pultenaea parrisiae, commonly known as the Parrisiae bush pea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
