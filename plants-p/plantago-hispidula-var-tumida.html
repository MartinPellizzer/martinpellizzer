Plantago hispidula var. tumida

Plantago hispidula var. tumida, commonly known as the hairy plantain or woolly plantain, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and distinctive, broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in wetlands.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
