Persicaria nogueirae

Persicaria nogueirae, commonly known as the Nogueira knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as smartweeds or knotweeds. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with slender stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Persicaria nogueirae is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade environments.
