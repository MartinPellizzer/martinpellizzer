Poa sheldonii

Poa sheldonii, commonly known as Sheldon's bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant has fine, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Botanically, it is classified within the Poaceae family and is often found in high-elevation environments.
