Psidium macrophyllum

Psidium macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaf guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a shallow root system.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
