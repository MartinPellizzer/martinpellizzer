Piper nonconformans

Piper nonconformans, commonly known as the nonconforming pepper, is a tropical climbing vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by berries that range in color from yellow to orange. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough, waxy texture, and it has a distinctive, spicy aroma.

Piper nonconformans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
