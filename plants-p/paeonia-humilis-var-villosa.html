Paeonia humilis var. villosa

Paeonia humilis var. villosa, commonly known as the woolly peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in a dense layer of soft, white to grayish woolly hairs. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, often with a distinctive double or semi-double bloom. Its foliage consists of palmately compound leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained, loamy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
