Polygala hasskarlii

Polygala hasskarlii, commonly known as the Kashmir spurge or Himalayan milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually pink or purple in color.

It plays a role in supporting local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
