Piptadenia rigida var. grandis

Piptadenia rigida var. grandis, commonly known as the Brazilian rubber tree, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. The plant is known for its ability to produce latex, which has been historically used in the rubber industry. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in humid, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
