Pleurothallis lepanthiformis

Pleurothallis lepanthiformis, commonly known as the Lepanthiform Pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often exhibit a range of colors, including white, yellow, and green. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a compact rosette, aiding in water retention.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its unique and intricate floral structures.
