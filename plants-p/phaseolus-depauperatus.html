Phaseolus depauperatus

Phaseolus depauperatus, commonly known as the poor man's bean, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a small, annual legume that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system.

Phaseolus depauperatus is notable for its adaptability to poor soils and its historical use by indigenous communities for food and other purposes.
