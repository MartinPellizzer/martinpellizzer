Piptospatha viridistigma

Piptospatha viridistigma, commonly known as the green-stigma piper, is a member of the Piperaceae family, characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and distinctive floral structures.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes. Its fruits are typically three-lobed berries that contain a single seed.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists studying tropical plant diversity.
