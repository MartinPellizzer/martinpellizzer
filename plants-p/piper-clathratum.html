Piper clathratum

Piper clathratum, commonly known as the "corky pipevine," is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty, and often corky stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Piper clathratum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
