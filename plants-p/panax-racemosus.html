Panax racemosus

Panax racemosus, commonly known as Chinese ginseng, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China, Korea, and Siberia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, branched root system that resembles a human figure. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with large, serrated leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red berries. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine, containing compounds like ginsenosides that are believed to have various health benefits.

The plant thrives in cool, temperate climates and requires well-drained, loamy soil for optimal growth.
