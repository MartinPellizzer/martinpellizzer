Picea yunnanensis

Picea yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan spruce, is a coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, dark green foliage that turns a striking silver-gray in winter. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist climates at high altitudes, often found in temperate rainforests and alpine environments.

Due to its unique ecological role and ornamental value, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape tree.
