Piper longistipulum

Piper longistipulum, commonly known as the long-tipped pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits that mature to a dark purple color.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
