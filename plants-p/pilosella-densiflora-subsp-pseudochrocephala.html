Pilosella densiflora subsp. pseudochrocephala

Pilosella densiflora subsp.

pseudochrocephala, commonly known as the denseflower mouse-eared chickweed, is a flowering plant in the family Compositae. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and dense, hairy foliage.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
