Primula farinosa subsp. exigua

Primula farinosa subsp.

exigua, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

The plant features basal leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and a showy, clustered bloom.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
