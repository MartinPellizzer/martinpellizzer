Philodendron roraimae subsp. aracamuniense

Philodendron roraimae subsp.

aracamuniense, commonly known as the Roraima Philodendron, is a climbing plant native to the Amazon rainforest of South America. It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a glossy texture.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by bright orange berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among plant enthusiasts and conservationists.
