Petaloma myrtilloides

Petaloma myrtilloides, commonly known as the blueberry fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered fronds that resemble small blueberries, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are bioluminescent, emitting a faint blue glow at night, which aids in attracting pollinators.

This unique botanical feature makes it a fascinating subject for ecological and botanical study.
