Pyrenia sorbus

Pyrenia sorbus, commonly known as the rowan tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, aggregate fruits. The plant produces bright red berries that are a food source for birds and other wildlife. Pyrenia sorbus is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy flowers and colorful autumn foliage.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
