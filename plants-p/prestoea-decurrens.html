Prestoea decurrens

Prestoea decurrens, commonly known as the Cuban mahogany, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its straight, dark-colored wood and is often found in lowland rainforests. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits.

The tree is valued for its durable timber but is also important in local ecosystems and traditional medicine.
