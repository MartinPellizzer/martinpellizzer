Pilosella cymiflora

Pilosella cymiflora, commonly known as the clustered goatsbeard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. The plant features a basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are arranged in dense, clustered inflorescences.

Pilosella cymiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
