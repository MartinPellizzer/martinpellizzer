Pelargonium papilionaceum

Pelargonium papilionaceum, commonly known as the butterfly geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, with succulent leaves that are often velvety in texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and rockeries.

The flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
