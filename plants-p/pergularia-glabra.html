Pergularia glabra

Pergularia glabra, commonly known as the slender pogostemon, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 10-30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Pergularia glabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
