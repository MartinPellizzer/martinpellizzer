Pinaropappus roseus var. roseus

Pinaropappus roseus var. roseus, commonly known as the roseum pincushion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, spherical growth habit and dense clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has silver-gray foliage with fuzzy leaves, giving it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

This variety is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
