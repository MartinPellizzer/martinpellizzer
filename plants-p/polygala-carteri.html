Polygala carteri

Polygala carteri, commonly known as the Carter's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces small, elongated seed pods.

Polygala carteri is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
