Pyrus malus subsp. paradisiaca

Pyrus malus subsp.

paradisiaca, commonly known as the European pear, is a subspecies of the common pear native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The fruit is typically sweet and aromatic, with a crisp texture and a juicy pulp. This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
