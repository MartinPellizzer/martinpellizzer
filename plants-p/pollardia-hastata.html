Pollardia hastata

Pollardia hastata, commonly known as the slender pollardia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist, sandy soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Pollardia hastata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
