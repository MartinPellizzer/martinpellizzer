Penstemon glastifolius

Penstemon glastifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Penstemon glastifolius is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
