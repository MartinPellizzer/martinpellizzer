Piper tractifolium

Piper tractifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf pepper or wild pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small, berry-like structures that turn red when mature, containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Piper tractifolium is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in herbal remedies for various ailments.
