Phyllostachys stimulosa f. unifoliata

Phyllostachys stimulosa f. unifoliata, commonly known as the single-leaf bamboo, is a unique variant of the common bamboo species.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically only present on one side of the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. This form is a natural mutation of the parent species, Phyllostachys stimulosa, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant is native to China and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

It is a clump-forming bamboo that grows relatively slowly, making it suitable for small garden spaces.
