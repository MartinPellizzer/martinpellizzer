Ptilotus distans

Ptilotus distans, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its unique, small, pink or purple flowers that appear in dense clusters. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid and coastal environments, making it a hardy species. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The ghost flower is also notable for its brief blooming period, typically during the cooler months.
