Primula tschuktschorum subsp. arctica

Primula tschuktschorum subsp.

arctica, commonly known as the Arctic primrose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Arctic and alpine regions. It typically features rosettes of basal leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple.

The plant is adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils. It is often found in tundra and mountainous areas where temperatures remain low throughout the growing season.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of Arctic ecosystems.
