Porrum ampeloprasum

Porrum ampeloprasum, commonly known as the purple garlic.

It is a hardy perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its bulbous underground stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by round, purple-brown seeds. Its foliage is typically dark green and has a strong, pungent odor similar to garlic.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
