Podagrostis sesquiflora

Podagrostis sesquiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grass.

It is a flowering plant species belonging to the Poaceae family, characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is often hidden among the foliage.

Podagrostis sesquiflora is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in regions with well-drained soils.
