Pleurothallis condorensis

Pleurothallis condorensis, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of the Peruvian Andes.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features intricate, lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. It is part of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its unique floral structure and adaptation to high-altitude conditions.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
