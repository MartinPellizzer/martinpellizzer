Plantago tarattothrix

Plantago tarattothrix, commonly known as the narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features erect, slender stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fibrous root system allows it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

This species is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
