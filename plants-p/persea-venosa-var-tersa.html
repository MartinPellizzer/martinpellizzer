Persea venosa var. tersa

Persea venosa var. tersa, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a botanical variant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Lauraceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and fruit. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy, dark green upper surface.

The fruit is a small, orange to reddish berry that ripens in the fall and is edible when fully mature.
