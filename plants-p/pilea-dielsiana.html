Pilea dielsiana

Pilea dielsiana, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to China.

It features rosettes of thick, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slight sheen. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in spring, followed by distinctive round, coin-like fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements have contributed to its popularity as a decorative houseplant.
