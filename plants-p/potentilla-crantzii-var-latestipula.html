Potentilla crantzii var. latestipula

Potentilla crantzii var. latestipula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has slender, often hairy stems and narrow, elongated leaves with a slightly toothed margin. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This variety is distinguished by its shorter pedicels compared to other varieties of Potentilla crantzii.
