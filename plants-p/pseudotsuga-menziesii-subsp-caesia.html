Pseudotsuga menziesii subsp. caesia

Pseudotsuga menziesii subsp.

caesia, commonly known as the California hemlock, is a subspecies native to the western United States. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure.

The tree typically grows in moist, cool climates and is often found in mountainous regions. Its bark is reddish-brown and becomes furrowed with age.

This subspecies is distinguished by its relatively short stature compared to other hemlock species.
