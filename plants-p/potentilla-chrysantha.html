Potentilla chrysantha

Potentilla chrysantha, commonly known as the golden potentilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Potentilla chrysantha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
