Piptadenia affinis

Piptadenia affinis, commonly known as the yellow fever tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release flat, winged seeds.

Piptadenia affinis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in folk medicine.
