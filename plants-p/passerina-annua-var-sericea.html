Passerina annua var. sericea

Passerina annua var. sericea, commonly known as silverleaf flax, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. The flowers are typically blue or purple and are clustered in loose spikes, adding ornamental value to the plant. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and historical use in textile production.
