Pilosella sphaerocephala subsp. meiocephala

Pilosella sphaerocephala subsp.

meiocephala, commonly known as the small-headed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and woodland edges. Its leaves are usually basal, oblanceolate, and covered with fine hairs, while the inflorescence consists of a dense, spherical cluster of yellow flowers.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is a common weed in agricultural areas.
