Pouteria torta subsp. tuberculata

Pouteria torta subsp.

tuberculata, commonly known as the tuberculate soursop, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America. It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, spiny fruits resembling those of the soursop. Its fruit contains a white, juicy pulp with numerous black seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.
