Piper dendrophilum

Piper dendrophilum, commonly known as the climbing pepper plant, is a species of flowering vine native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its long, climbing stems and compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, typically clustered in spikes, and it produces berries that range in color from green to red as they mature.

Piper dendrophilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
