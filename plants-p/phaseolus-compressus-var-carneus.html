Phaseolus compressus var. carneus

Phaseolus compressus var. carneus, commonly known as the pink runner bean, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The flowers are typically pale pink to reddish in color, and the pods are elongated, containing edible seeds that are often used in culinary applications. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and nutritional content, making it a popular choice for both gardens and agricultural settings.

Its growth habit and adaptability make it a versatile plant in warm climates.
