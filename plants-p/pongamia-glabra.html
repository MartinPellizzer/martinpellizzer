Pongamia glabra

Pongamia glabra, commonly known as the Indian beech, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing oily seeds. The seeds are rich in triglycerides and are used for biodiesel production.

It is also valued for its ecological benefits, including soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.
