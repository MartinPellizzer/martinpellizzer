Portea petropolitana var. noettigii

Portea petropolitana var. noettigii, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its humid, shaded habitat. Its leaves are elongated and fleshy, adapted to conserve moisture in the tropical environment.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique floral structure.
