Panicum albomaculatum

Panicum albomaculatum, commonly known as the white-spotted bluestem, is a flowering grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, blue-green leaves with distinctive white or pale spots. The plant produces fluffy, pale blue to white inflorescences that are visually striking and attract various pollinators. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open fields and prairies.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and restoration projects.
