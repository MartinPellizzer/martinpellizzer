Plinia sessiliflora

Plinia sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flower guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers and oval, leathery leaves. It produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe.

Plinia sessiliflora is often grown for its ornamental value and has some use in traditional medicine.
