Prangos arcis-romanae

Prangos arcis-romanae, commonly known as the Roman mountain thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, thriving in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped, deeply lobed, and covered in stiff, bristly hairs.

The flowers, which appear in late summer, are yellow and are surrounded by a series of spiny bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance.
