Phlomis orientalis subsp. caucasica

Phlomis orientalis subsp.

caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, grayish-green, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, pink to purple flower heads that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
