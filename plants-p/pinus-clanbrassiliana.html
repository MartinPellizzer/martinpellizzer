Pinus clanbrassiliana

Pinus clanbrassiliana, commonly known as the Brazilian pine, is a species of conifer native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, dark green needles. The plant produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
