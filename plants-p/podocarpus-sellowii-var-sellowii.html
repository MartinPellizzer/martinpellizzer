Podocarpus sellowii var. sellowii

Podocarpus sellowii var. sellowii, commonly known as the Brazilian pine or Sellow's podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and conical shape. The plant produces small, woody cones that contain edible seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal regions and mountainous areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a landscape tree in various parts of the world.
