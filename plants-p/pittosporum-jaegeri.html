Pittosporum jaegeri

Pittosporum jaegeri, commonly known as the silver-leaf pittosporum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in temperate climates.
