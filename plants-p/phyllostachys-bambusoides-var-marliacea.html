Phyllostachys bambusoides var. marliacea

Phyllostachys bambusoides var. marliacea, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo genus Phyllostachys.

It is characterized by its striking silver-white coloration on the stems, which contrasts with its darker green nodes. This plant is native to China and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It grows as a clump-forming bamboo, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes and as a decorative plant in horticulture.
