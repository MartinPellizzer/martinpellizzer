Physaria multiceps

Physaria multiceps, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are typically linear and covered in fine hairs. It is well adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

Physaria multiceps plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
