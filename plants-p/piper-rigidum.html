Piper rigidum

Piper rigidum, commonly known as the rigid pepper or wild pepper, is a tropical climbing shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. It is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.

The plant has been used traditionally for its aromatic properties and in some local medicinal practices.
