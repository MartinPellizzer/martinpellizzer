Pleurothallis suiniana

Pleurothallis suiniana, commonly known as the pig's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on trees or rocks. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to capture moisture from the air.

Pleurothallis suiniana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
