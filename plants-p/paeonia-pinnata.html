Paeonia pinnata

Paeonia pinnata, commonly known as the Chinese peony, is a deciduous shrub native to China and Korea.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink and red. The plant has compound, pinnately arranged leaves that are typically green and hairy on the underside. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine.

Paeonia pinnata is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for centuries in gardens and landscapes.
