Panicum scoparium var. majus

Panicum scoparium var. majus, commonly known as switchgrass, is a tall, perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces fluffy, seed-bearing panicles that are often visible in late summer and early autumn. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

Panicum scoparium var. majus is also valued for its ecological benefits, including erosion control and wildlife habitat support.
