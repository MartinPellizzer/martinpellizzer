Polygonum runcinatum

Polygonum runcinatum, commonly known as the scarlet runner or redroot pigweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its smooth, often reddish stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that appear in late summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Polygonum runcinatum is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
