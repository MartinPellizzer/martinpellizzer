Panmorphia kuhniae

Panmorphia kuhniae, commonly known as the Kuhniae panmorph, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a compact rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of violet and blue, which bloom during the short growing season. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and requires specific environmental conditions to thrive.

Its botanical study offers insights into adaptive strategies of plants in extreme environments.
