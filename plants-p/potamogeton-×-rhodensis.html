Potamogeton × rhodensis

Potamogeton × rhodensis, commonly known as the redstem pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton praelatus.

It is typically found in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams, where it thrives in shallow, nutrient-rich waters. The plant is notable for its bright red stems and leaves that are often submerged, contributing to the aesthetic appeal of aquatic ecosystems. It plays a role in providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

However, its invasive potential in certain regions can lead to ecological imbalances if not managed properly.
