Papaver autumnale

Papaver autumnale, commonly known as autumn poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

The flowers are often used in gardens for their ornamental value and can also serve as a food source for pollinators.
