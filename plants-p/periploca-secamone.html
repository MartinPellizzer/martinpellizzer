Periploca secamone

Periploca secamone, commonly known as African milkwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange or red berries that contain toxic seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
