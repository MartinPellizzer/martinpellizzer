Pleurothallis auriculata

Pleurothallis auriculata, commonly known as the ear-shaped orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, elongated flowers that often resemble small ears. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence bears clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow blooms.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral structure.
