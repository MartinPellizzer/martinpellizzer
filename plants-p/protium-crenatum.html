Protium crenatum

Protium crenatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sike or sike tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapindaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by round, spiny fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
