Palala peltata

Palala peltata, commonly known as the broad-leaved palala, is a unique plant native to the tropical rainforests of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its humid environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, clustered in loose inflorescences. Palala peltata is a perennial shrub that thrives in shaded, moist conditions, making it a common understory plant in dense forest ecosystems.

Its foliage is sometimes used in traditional medicine by local communities for its purported health benefits.
