Potamogeton filiformis var. latifolius

Potamogeton filiformis var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf pondweed, is a aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are broad and flat, often reaching lengths of up to 30 centimeters. This plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters near the water's surface.
