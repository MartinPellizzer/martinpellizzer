Prunus ilicifolia subsp. occidentalis

Prunus ilicifolia subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, spiny branches and small, dark green leaves.

The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
