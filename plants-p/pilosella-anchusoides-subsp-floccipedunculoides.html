Pilosella anchusoides subsp. floccipedunculoides

Pilosella anchusoides subsp.

floccipedunculoides, commonly known as the woolly mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in a cylindrical shape, forming a unique inflorescence. It thrives in rocky or alpine environments and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
