Phoxanthus manausensis

Phoxanthus manausensis, commonly known as the Manaus phlox, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its showy, bright yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and somewhat branched.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
