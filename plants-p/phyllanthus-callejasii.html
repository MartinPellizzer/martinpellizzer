Phyllanthus callejasii

Phyllanthus callejasii, commonly known as the Mexican waterleaf, is a shrub native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in cyathia, a typical feature of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Phyllanthus callejasii is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
