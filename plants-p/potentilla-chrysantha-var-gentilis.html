Potentilla chrysantha var. gentilis

Potentilla chrysantha var. gentilis, commonly known as the golden potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and drought tolerance.
