Phytolacca lutea

Phytolacca lutea, commonly known as yellow nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Solanaceae, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in umbels, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries.

The plant contains various alkaloids and is used in traditional medicine, though it can be toxic in large quantities.
