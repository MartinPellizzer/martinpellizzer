Potamogeton pectinatus var. caespitosus

Potamogeton pectinatus var. caespitosus, commonly known as the tufted pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like leaves that are often clustered in tufts, giving it its common name. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne in spikes, and it produces elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.

Its presence can indicate good water quality and is often found in lakes, ponds, and canals.
