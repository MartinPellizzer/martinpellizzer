Panicum violascens var. remotiusculum

Panicum violascens var. remotiusculum, commonly known as the purple panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to wet soils. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, violet to purple flowers, which give it its common name.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland environments.
