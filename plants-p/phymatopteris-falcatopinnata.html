Phymatopteris falcatopinnata

Phymatopteris falcatopinnata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically arching and have a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological preferences make it an important component of its native habitat.
