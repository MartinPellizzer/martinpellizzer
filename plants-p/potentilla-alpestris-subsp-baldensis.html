Potentilla alpestris subsp. baldensis

Potentilla alpestris subsp.

baldensis, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and it produces red or orange berries in the fall.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
