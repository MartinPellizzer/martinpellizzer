Papaver radicatum subsp. intermedium

Papaver radicatum subsp.

intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features striking, showy flowers with bright orange to red petals that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has a robust root system, which helps it thrive in rocky and well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, fuzzy layer, aiding in water retention.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
