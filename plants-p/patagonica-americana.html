Patagonica americana

Patagonica americana, commonly known as the American Patagonia plant, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to the arid and high-altitude environments where it grows. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which bloom during the short growing season. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is often cultivated in rock gardens or as an ornamental plant in arid climates.
