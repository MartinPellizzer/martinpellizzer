Pentameris longiglumis subsp. gymnocolea

Pentameris longiglumis subsp.

gymnocolea, commonly known as the smooth-fruited pentameris, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage.

The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a smooth, glabrous surface, while the flowers are small and white, clustered in compound umbels.

The subspecies gymnocolea is distinguished by its lack of bracts and the smooth texture of its fruits, which are typically slender and ribbed.
