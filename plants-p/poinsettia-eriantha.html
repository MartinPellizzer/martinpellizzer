Poinsettia eriantha

Poinsettia eriantha, commonly known as the Natal euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that surround small yellow flowers, which are actually the true flowers of the plant. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates. Poinsettia eriantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value, particularly during the holiday season.

Its growth habit includes both shrub-like and tree-like forms, depending on the variety.
