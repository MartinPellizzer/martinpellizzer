Physalis virginiana var. campaniforma

Physalis virginiana var. campaniforma, commonly known as the common huskfruit or the Cape strawberry, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, egg-shaped capsules that enclose the fruit, which turns red when mature. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals and has alternate, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along streambanks.

The fruit is edible when fully ripe and has a sweet, tangy flavor.
