Phlox linearifolia var. longipes

Phlox linearifolia var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed linear phlox, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spikes of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and restoration projects.
