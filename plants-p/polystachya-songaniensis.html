Polystachya songaniensis

Polystachya songaniensis, commonly known as the Songani bromeliad, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. Its inflorescence consists of a single, showy flower that emerges from the center of the rosette.

Polystachya songaniensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
