Puebloa bradyi var. despainii

Puebloa bradyi var. despainii, commonly known as the Desert Puebloa, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers during the spring, adding a striking visual element to the desert landscape. Its leaves are typically reduced to small spines, which help minimize water loss.

This variety is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique adaptations to its harsh environment.
