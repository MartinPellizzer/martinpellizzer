Phaius magniflorus

Phaius magniflorus, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pinkish flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant has broad, waxy leaves that float on the water, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its rhizomes grow beneath the sediment, allowing it to anchor and spread in wetland environments.

Phaius magniflorus plays an important ecological role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
