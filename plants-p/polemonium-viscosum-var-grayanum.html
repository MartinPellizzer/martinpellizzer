Polemonium viscosum var. grayanum

Polemonium viscosum var. grayanum, commonly known as sticky bluebeard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, often sticky stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded to partially shaded areas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinators.
