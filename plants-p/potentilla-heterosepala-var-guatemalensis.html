Potentilla heterosepala var. guatemalensis

Potentilla heterosepala var. guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
