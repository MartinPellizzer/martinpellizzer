Penstemon heterophyllus var. azureus

Penstemon heterophyllus var. azureus, commonly known as the blue mist penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-blue coating.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
