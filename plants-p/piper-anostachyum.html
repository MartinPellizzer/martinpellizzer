Piper anostachyum

Piper anostachyum, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or wild pepper, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly the Amazon basin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. The fruits are typically harvested when they turn bright red, indicating maturity.

It is valued for its aromatic leaves and fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.
