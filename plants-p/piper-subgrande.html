Piper subgrande

Piper subgrande, commonly known as the giant pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are initially green and turn red when mature, resembling tiny peppers.

Piper subgrande is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
