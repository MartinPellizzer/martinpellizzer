Piper mombachanum

Piper mombachanum, commonly known as the Mombachanum pepper plant, is a species of flowering vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes various pepper plants, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a dark purple or black color, often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
