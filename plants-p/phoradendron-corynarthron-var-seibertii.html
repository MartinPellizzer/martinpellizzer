Phoradendron corynarthron var. seibertii

Phoradendron corynarthron var. seibertii, commonly known as the Seibert's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the Neotropical region.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using sunlight. The plant has a distinctive appearance with small, fleshy leaves and white, waxy flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that consume the fleshy, red berries.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by contributing to the biodiversity and nutrient cycling within forest ecosystems.
