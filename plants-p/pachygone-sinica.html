Pachygone sinica

Pachygone sinica, commonly known as the Chinese thickleaf plant, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and upright growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in its natural habitat.

Pachygone sinica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
