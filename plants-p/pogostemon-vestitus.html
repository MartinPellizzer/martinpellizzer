Pogostemon vestitus

Pogostemon vestitus, commonly known as the velvet pogostemon, is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia, and thrives in humid, tropical environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple and are arranged in whorls along the stems.

Pogostemon vestitus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
