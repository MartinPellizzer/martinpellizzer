Potamogeton × decipiens f. transiens, commonly known as the transitive pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a sterile form of the species, typically found in freshwater habitats such as lakes and slow-moving rivers. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are adapted for water pollination. Its morphology reflects a blend of features from its parent species, Potamogeton × decipiens.
