Phyllostegia × yamaguchii

Phyllostegia × yamaguchii, commonly known as the hybrid boxwood, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Phyllostegia hirsuta and Phyllostegia obovata.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with dense, compact foliage. Its leaves are usually oval to lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, tubular flowers in clusters.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and hardy nature.
