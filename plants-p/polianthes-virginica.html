Polianthes virginica

Polianthes virginica, commonly known as the Spanish dagger, is a striking perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, narrow, white to pale blue flower spikes that rise above sword-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant is characterized by its hardy nature and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is known for its showy, long-lasting blooms.
