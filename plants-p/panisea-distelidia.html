Panisea distelidia

Panisea distelidia, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has succulent leaves and stems, which help it retain water in harsh environments. It produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous coating.

Panisea distelidia is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
