Philodendron dyscarpium var. ventuarianum

Philodendron dyscarpium var. ventuarianum, commonly known as the Ventuarianum Philodendron, is a tropical vine native to the dense rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy, and dark green leaves that often have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by elongated, berry-like fruits that are typically green when mature. Its climbing habit and striking foliage make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant, though it can be toxic if ingested.
