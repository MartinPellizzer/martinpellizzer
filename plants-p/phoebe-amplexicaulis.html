Phoebe amplexicaulis

Phoebe amplexicaulis, commonly known as the climbing phoebe, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that often wrap around the stem, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark blue or black berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

Phoebe amplexicaulis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to climb or trail along structures.
