Parodia sanguiniflora var. comata

Parodia sanguiniflora var. comata, commonly known as the woolly red torch cactus, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, white, woolly hair, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces bright red flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth and compact size, it is well-suited for container gardening.
