Pittosporum revolutum var. tomentosum

Pittosporum revolutum var. tomentosum, commonly known as the silver tree, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in horticulture for its unique foliage.
