Pinus laricio proles austriaca

Pinus laricio proles austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian stone pine, is a subspecies of the stone pine native to the Alps.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its thick, scaly bark and needle-like leaves. The tree produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appearance and historical use in forestry and landscaping.
