Piper multinervium var. pubescenticaule

Piper multinervium var. pubescenticaule, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pubescent (hairy) stems and leaves with a prominent venation pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by berries that turn red when mature. It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ethnobotanical studies.
