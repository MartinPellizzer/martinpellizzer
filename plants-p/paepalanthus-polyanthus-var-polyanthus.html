Paepalanthus polyanthus var. polyanthus

Paepalanthus polyanthus var. polyanthus, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a smooth texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
