Phyllanthus warnockii

Phyllanthus warnockii, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a shrub native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing minute seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in dry, sandy soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant.
