Polygonum watsonii var. alpinum

Polygonum watsonii var. alpinum, commonly known as the alpine knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color, with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in late summer.

Polygonum watsonii var. alpinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh alpine conditions.
