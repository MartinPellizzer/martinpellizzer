Pinus pinea var. maderiensis

Pinus pinea var. maderiensis, commonly known as the Maderian stone pine, is a variant of the stone pine native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, evergreen foliage composed of long, needle-like leaves. The plant produces large, edible pine cones that contain seeds used in various culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it well adapted to the island's climate.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its ecological importance in its native habitat.
