Plantago major var. vulgaris

Plantago major var. vulgaris, commonly known as broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a basal rosette of broad, oval, and sometimes hairy leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters in length. The plant produces erect, slender flowering stems topped with a dense cluster of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing and healing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its fibrous root system and mucilage-rich tissues.
