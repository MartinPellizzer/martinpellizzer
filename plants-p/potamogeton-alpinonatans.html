Potamogeton alpinonatans

Potamogeton alpinonatans, commonly known as the alpine pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to cold, freshwater environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow, nutrient-rich waters and is characterized by its long, slender stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces tiny, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which are often submerged or just at the water's surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its preference for cold water, it is often found in lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams in temperate and alpine regions.
