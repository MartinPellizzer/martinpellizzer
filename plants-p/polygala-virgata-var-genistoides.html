Polygala virgata var. genistoides

Polygala virgata var. genistoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, raceme-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with distinctive, thread-like petals.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
