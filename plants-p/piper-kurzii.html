Piper kurzii

Piper kurzii, commonly known as the Kurz's pepper or the Bornean pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that contains the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
