Pygeum ledermannii

Pygeum ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's African chestnut, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and distinctive, urn-shaped fruits. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered in terminal umbels. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

Pygeum ledermannii is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
