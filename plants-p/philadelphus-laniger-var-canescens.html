Philadelphus laniger var. canescens

Philadelphus laniger var. canescens, commonly known as the silver-leaf mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has silvery-gray foliage that provides ornamental value throughout the growing season. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a hairy texture that gives it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental and aromatic qualities.
