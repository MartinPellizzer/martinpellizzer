Pisonia subcordata var. typica

Pisonia subcordata var. typica, commonly known as the silky pisonia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small, fluffy balls. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a soft, velvety texture.

This species is often found in coastal areas and thrives in sandy or limestone soils.
