Persicaria salicifolia

Persicaria salicifolia, commonly known as heartleaf knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purple-tinged, especially on the underside, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for wetland restoration projects.
