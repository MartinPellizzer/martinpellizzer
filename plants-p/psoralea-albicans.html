Psoralea albicans

Psoralea albicans, commonly known as the white psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish-white flowers clustered in spherical heads. The plant has compound leaves with small, paired leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
