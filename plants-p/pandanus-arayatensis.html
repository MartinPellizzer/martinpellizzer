Pandanus arayatensis

Pandanus arayatensis, commonly known as the Arawa palm, is a species of flowering plant in the palm family.

It is native to the island of Arawa in the Tonga archipelago and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into spiny, woody fruits. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to salty air and sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
