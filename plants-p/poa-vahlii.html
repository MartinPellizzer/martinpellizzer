Poa vahlii

Poa vahlii, commonly known as Vahl's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with cold winters. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed florets.

Poa vahlii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
