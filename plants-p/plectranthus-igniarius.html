Plectranthus igniarius

Plectranthus igniarius, commonly known as the flame plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Plectranthus igniarius is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
