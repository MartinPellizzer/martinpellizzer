Panicum animarum

Panicum animarum, commonly known as the soulgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, feathery leaves that shimmer in the wind, giving it a delicate and ethereal appearance. The plant produces small, delicate panicles of flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a soft pink or purple hue to the landscape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its graceful appearance and symbolic association with the soul, it is often used in ceremonial and spiritual contexts.
