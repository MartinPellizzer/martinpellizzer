Panicum bulbosum var. minus

Panicum bulbosum var. minus, commonly known as the smaller bulbous panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces dense clusters of small, spikelike inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and it is known for its ability to thrive in wetland environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
