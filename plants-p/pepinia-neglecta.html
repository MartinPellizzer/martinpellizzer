Pepinia neglecta

Pepinia neglecta, commonly known as the desert pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features small, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Pepinia neglecta is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with minimal water requirements.
