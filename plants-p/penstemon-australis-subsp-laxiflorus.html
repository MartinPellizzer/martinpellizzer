Penstemon australis subsp. laxiflorus

Penstemon australis subsp.

laxiflorus, commonly known as the slender-flowered penstemon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features upright, branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, adorned with opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and drought-tolerant addition to native plant landscapes.
