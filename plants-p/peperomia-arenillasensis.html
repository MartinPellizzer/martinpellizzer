Peperomia arenillasensis

Peperomia arenillasensis, commonly known as the Arenillasensis Peperomia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Colombia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish or purple hue on the underside. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in spadices, typical of the genus.

Peperomia arenillasensis is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
