Pseudoraphis brunoniana var. ukishiba

Pseudoraphis brunoniana var. ukishiba, commonly known as the Japanese bottlebrush, is a unique succulent plant native to Japan.

It features striking, cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, red or orange bristles, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by red, bottle-shaped fruits. It is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and prefers full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and limited natural range, it is often cultivated in arid gardens and rockeries.
