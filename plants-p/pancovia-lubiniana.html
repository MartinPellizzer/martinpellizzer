Pancovia lubiniana

Pancovia lubiniana, commonly known as the Lubinian sun-leaf, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that reflect sunlight, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both visually striking and ecologically beneficial to local pollinators.
