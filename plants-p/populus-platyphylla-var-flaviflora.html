Populus platyphylla var. flaviflora

Populus platyphylla var. flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered poplar, is a variant of the poplar tree native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, contributing to its ornamental value. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its aesthetic appeal in landscaping.
