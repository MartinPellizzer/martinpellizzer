Physalis miraflorensis

Physalis miraflorensis, commonly known as the miracle fruit plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the genus Physalis, which includes various species known for their colorful, lantern-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries enclosed in a papery calyx. These berries are rich in vitamin C and contain a unique compound called miraculin, which alters taste perception.

It is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
