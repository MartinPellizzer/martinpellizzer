Paphia shungolensis

Paphia shungolensis, commonly known as the Shungol orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of India.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a rhizomatous growth habit, often found in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration, typically featuring a combination of white, yellow, and green hues. Its flowers are borne on lateral spikes and are known for their intricate petal structures.

Paphia shungolensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
