Polygonum volchovense

Polygonum volchovense, commonly known as the Volchov polygonum, is a herbaceous plant native to eastern Europe and parts of Russia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as buckwheats. The plant typically grows in moist, wetland environments, often along the edges of ponds and marshes. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a distinctive veining pattern, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red or pink.

Polygonum volchovense is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
