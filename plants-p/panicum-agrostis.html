Panicum agrostis

Panicum agrostis, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields, roadsides, and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, reddish-brown seeds in panicles. It is often used as a forage plant for livestock due to its high nutritional value.

Botanically, it is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and adaptability to a wide range of soil conditions.
