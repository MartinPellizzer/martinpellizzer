Poa valdiviana

Poa valdiviana, commonly known as the Valdivia grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Poa valdiviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native ecosystems.
