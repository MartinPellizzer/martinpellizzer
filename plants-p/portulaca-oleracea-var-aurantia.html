Portulaca oleracea var. aurantia

Portulaca oleracea var. aurantia, commonly known as the golden purslane, is a succulent plant native to warm temperate regions.

It features fleshy, upright stems and leaves that are typically thick and smooth. The plant produces small, bright orange flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy annual in many climates.

The leaves and young shoots are edible and often used in salads or cooked dishes.
