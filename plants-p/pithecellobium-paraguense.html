Pithecellobium paraguense

Pithecellobium paraguense, commonly known as the Paraguayan burro's tail, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, cascading clusters of small, leathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance.

Its growth habit and adaptability make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscapes.
