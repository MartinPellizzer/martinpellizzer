Piper pellitum

Piper pellitum, commonly known as the small-leaved pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, heart-shaped leaves, and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small berries that turn red when mature, and they are used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological studies.
