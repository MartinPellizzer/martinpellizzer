Plectranthus lanceolatus

Plectranthus lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plectranthus, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or lilac in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

Plectranthus lanceolatus is also valued for its aromatic foliage, which can be used in culinary or medicinal applications.
