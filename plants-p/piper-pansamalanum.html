Piper pansamalanum

Piper pansamalanum, commonly known as the Panamanian pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species.

It is native to Central America, particularly Panama, and thrives in humid, tropical environments. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its small, greenish flowers are clustered in spikes.

The fruit, a small, dark berry, contains several seeds and is used in traditional medicine and cuisine in its native region.
