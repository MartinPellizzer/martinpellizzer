Pseuderanthemum palatiferum var. levicapsa

Pseuderanthemum palatiferum var. levicapsa, commonly known as the silverleaf pseuderanthemum, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a flowering plant characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery down. The flowers are small, white, and clustered in terminal inflorescences, giving the plant a delicate appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
