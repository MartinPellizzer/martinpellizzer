Phyllanthus ambiguus

Phyllanthus ambiguus, commonly known as the sand pear or false pear, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring simple, alternate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits. Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various soil types and climates, contributing to its widespread distribution.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
