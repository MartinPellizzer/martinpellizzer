Pentas carnea

Pentas carnea, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant is a shrub or small tree with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and ornamental use.

The flowers are not only visually striking but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
