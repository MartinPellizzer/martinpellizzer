Plantago pubescens

Plantago pubescens, commonly known as hairy plantain, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
