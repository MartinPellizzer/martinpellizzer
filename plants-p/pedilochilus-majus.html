Pedilochilus majus

Pedilochilus majus, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of multiple fragrant, violet-colored blooms that bloom sequentially over several weeks.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
