Poa laestadii

Poa laestadii, commonly known as the Laestadi's bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, dense tufts and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and restricted distribution, Poa laestadii is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation to high-altitude conditions.
