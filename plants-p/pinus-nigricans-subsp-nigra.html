Pinus nigricans subsp. nigra

Pinus nigricans subsp.

nigra, commonly known as the black fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dark green, needle-like leaves and distinctive cone structure.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its botanical features include a straight trunk with a conical crown and a slow growth rate.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
