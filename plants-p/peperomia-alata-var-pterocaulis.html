Peperomia alata var. pterocaulis

Peperomia alata var. pterocaulis, commonly known as the Shingle Plant, is a compact, low-growing plant native to Brazil.

It features dense, overlapping leaves that form a dense mat, giving it a shingle-like appearance. The plant has short, thick stems and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. Its foliage is often variegated with silver or white markings, adding visual interest.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
