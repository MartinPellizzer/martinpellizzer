Peschiera stenoloba

Peschiera stenoloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf marsh fern, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in southern Africa.

It features a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread and colonize moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant produces delicate, pinnately compound leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. Its spores are released from small, clustered sporangia located on the underside of the fronds.

Peschiera stenoloba thrives in shallow water or damp ground, making it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
