Polycarpaea staticiformis

Polycarpaea staticiformis, commonly known as the motionless thornweed, is a perennial herb native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spiny stems, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, blooming during the dry season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Polycarpaea staticiformis is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
