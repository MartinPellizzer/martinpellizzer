Paepalanthus neglectus

Paepalanthus neglectus, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly in arid and semi-arid regions.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant has a rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, adapting it to its dry environment. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy seeds dispersed by the wind.

Paepalanthus neglectus is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its resilience in harsh conditions.
