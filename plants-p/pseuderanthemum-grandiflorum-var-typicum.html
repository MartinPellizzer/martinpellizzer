Pseuderanthemum grandiflorum var. typicum

Pseuderanthemum grandiflorum var. typicum, commonly known as the large-flowered false anthemum, is a tropical shrub native to regions of South America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. These flowers are borne on peduncles and are adapted for pollination by birds and insects.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
