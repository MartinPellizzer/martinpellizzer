Polygonum borgoicum

Polygonum borgoicum, commonly known as the Borgo knotweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes many species of buckwheat and knotweeds. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, typically displaying shades of red or pink.

Polygonum borgoicum is often found in moist environments such as wetlands and stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
