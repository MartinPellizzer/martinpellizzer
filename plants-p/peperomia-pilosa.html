Peperomia pilosa

Peperomia pilosa, commonly known as the velvety peperomia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red, emerging from the leaf axils. It thrives in well-draining soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Peperomia pilosa is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
