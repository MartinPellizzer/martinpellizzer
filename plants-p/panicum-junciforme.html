Panicum junciforme

Panicum junciforme, commonly known as silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous delicate, greenish-yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Silky wheatgrass is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
