Pernettya reticulata

Pernettya reticulata, commonly known as the red raspberry bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and a fragrant aroma.

Pernettya reticulata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland environments.
