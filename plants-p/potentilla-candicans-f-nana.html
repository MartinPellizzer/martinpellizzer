Potentilla candicans f. nana

Potentilla candicans f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a compact, mat-forming growth habit with numerous slender stems and small, serrated leaves. Its foliage is typically silver-green, providing ornamental value in garden settings.

It is well adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
