Piper montealegreanum

Piper montealegreanum, commonly known as Montealegre's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the humid forests of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by the production of small, peppery berries.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
