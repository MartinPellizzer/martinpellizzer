Populus × canadensis var. gelrica

Populus × canadensis var. gelrica, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a hybrid species of willow native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically bright green during the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

The Weeping Willow is known for its rapid growth and is commonly used in landscaping and riparian restoration projects.
