Pyrus aria subsp. intermedia

Pyrus aria subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate pear, is a subspecies native to parts of eastern Asia. It is a deciduous tree characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring.

The fruit is typically a small, yellow pear with a smooth skin and a sweet, aromatic flavor. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its edible fruit.
