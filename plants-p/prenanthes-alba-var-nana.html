Prenanthes alba var. nana

Prenanthes alba var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf white rayflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features small, white, ray-like flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and produces slender, hairy stems that rise above the foliage. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a desirable plant for wetland restoration and native garden landscapes.
