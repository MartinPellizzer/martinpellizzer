Plantago atrata subsp. carpatica

Plantago atrata subsp.

carpatica, commonly known as the Carpathian plantain, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are typically grayish-green in color.

The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems that bear dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is also an important food source for various insect species.
