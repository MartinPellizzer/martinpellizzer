Pleurothallis killipii

Pleurothallis killipii, commonly known as the Killip's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and floral structures. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to the humid, shaded conditions of its natural habitat.

Pleurothallis killipii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
