Phlomostachys densiflora

Phlomostachys densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered phlomis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features upright, hairy stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, and it produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Phlomostachys densiflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
