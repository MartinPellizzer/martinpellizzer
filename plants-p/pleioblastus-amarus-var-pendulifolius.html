Pleioblastus amarus var. pendulifolius

Pleioblastus amarus var. pendulifolius, commonly known as the weeping bamboo, is a variant of the silver bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its graceful, drooping foliage that gives it a cascading appearance. The plant has slender, green culms that are often used in ornamental landscaping due to their aesthetic appeal. It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and bonsai cultivation.

This variety is valued for its delicate foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
