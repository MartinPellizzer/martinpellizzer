Platyopuntia chilensis

Platyopuntia chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean flat cactus, is a species native to the coastal regions of southern Chile and northern Argentina.

It is a low-growing, spineless cactus that typically forms dense mats or cushions of succulent, fleshy stems. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems. Its thick, water-storing stems help it survive in arid environments, and it is well adapted to coastal conditions with salt spray.

Platyopuntia chilensis is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental appearance and drought tolerance.
