Panicum kimpakoense

Panicum kimpakoense, commonly known as Kimpako grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Africa.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swampy areas and riverbanks. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils and its tolerance to periodic flooding.

Due to its ecological significance, it plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
