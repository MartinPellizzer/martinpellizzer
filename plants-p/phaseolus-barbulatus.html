Phaseolus barbulatus

Phaseolus barbulatus, commonly known as the hairy runner bean, is a climbing legume native to Central and South America.

It produces small, hairy pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. The plant has compound leaves with small, stiff hairs that give it its common name. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, and it is cultivated for both its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.
