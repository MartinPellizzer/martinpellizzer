Philodendron pertusum

Philodendron pertusum, commonly known as the curly leaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often curled or twisted, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to climb and spread along supports. Its leaves are typically dark green with a glossy surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red berries.

Philodendron pertusum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
