Paepalanthus blepharophorus

Paepalanthus blepharophorus, commonly known as the red-bellied penguin cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and clusters of bright red flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils. Its unique appearance and adaptation to harsh conditions make it a notable species in its native habitat.

The flowers attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
