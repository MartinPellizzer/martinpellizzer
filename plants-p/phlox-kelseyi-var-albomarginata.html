Phlox kelseyi var. albomarginata

Phlox kelseyi var. albomarginata, commonly known as the white-margin phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white with distinct white margins. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white hair.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
