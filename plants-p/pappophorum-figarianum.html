Pappophorum figarianum

Pappophorum figarianum, commonly known as the fig-leaf plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that resemble fig leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
