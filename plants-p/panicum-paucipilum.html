Panicum paucipilum

Panicum paucipilum, commonly known as the sparse-branched panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its low maintenance requirements, it is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ornamental plant.
