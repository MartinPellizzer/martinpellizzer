Pongamia elliptica

Pongamia elliptica, commonly known as the Indian beech or pongam tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pod-like fruits containing seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in various soil types.

Pongamia elliptica is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
