Photinia ardisiifolia

Photinia ardisiifolia, commonly known as the redleaf photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, glossy, red-veined leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
