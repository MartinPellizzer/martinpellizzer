Phebalium equestre

Phebalium equestre, commonly known as the horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces spiny, woody fruits containing one or two chestnuts. Its botanical features include a smooth, gray bark and a rounded crown.

It is often planted for its ornamental value and the shade it provides.
