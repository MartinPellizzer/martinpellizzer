Peperomia subgeminispica

Peperomia subgeminispica, commonly known as the twin-leaf peperomia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a compact, low-growing plant with thick, waxy leaves that are typically oval or heart-shaped. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia subgeminispica is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
