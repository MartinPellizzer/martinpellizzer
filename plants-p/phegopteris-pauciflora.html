Phegopteris pauciflora

Phegopteris pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered along the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Phegopteris pauciflora plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and small aquatic life.
