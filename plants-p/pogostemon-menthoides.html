Pogostemon menthoides

Pogostemon menthoides, commonly known as lemon balm mint, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

The essential oils extracted from its leaves contain compounds like linalool and menthone, contributing to its distinctive fragrance and therapeutic uses.
