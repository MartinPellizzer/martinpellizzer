Pultenaea tasmanica

Pultenaea tasmanica, commonly known as Tasmanian wattle, is a flowering plant native to Tasmania.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often forming dense shrubs. Its flowers are arranged in racemes and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds.

Pultenaea tasmanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
