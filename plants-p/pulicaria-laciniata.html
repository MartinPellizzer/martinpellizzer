Pulicaria laciniata

Pulicaria laciniata, commonly known as the fringed tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial with hairy stems and basal leaves that are deeply lobed or toothed. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands.

Its leaves and flowers contain essential oils and have been used historically in traditional medicine.
