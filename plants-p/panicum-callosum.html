Panicum callosum

Panicum callosum, commonly known as silky panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silky-textured leaves and panicles that produce small, pale green to brown spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland management.
