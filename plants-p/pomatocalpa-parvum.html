Pomatocalpa parvum

Pomatocalpa parvum, commonly known as the small orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, small flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a short, stocky rhizome and produces a single, erect stem with several narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that is often spotted or streaked.

Pomatocalpa parvum prefers moist, shaded habitats and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.
