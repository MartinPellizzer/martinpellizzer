Phyllanthus singalensis

Phyllanthus singalensis, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are rich in vitamin C and other nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Phyllanthus singalensis is widely cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses.
