Pectinaria arcuata

Pectinaria arcuata, commonly known as the zigzag mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of specific host trees.

It is characterized by its unique, zigzag-shaped stems and small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers followed by berries that are dispersed by birds. It thrives in temperate regions and is often found in forested areas where its host trees are present.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
