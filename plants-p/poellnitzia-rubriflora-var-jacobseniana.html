Poellnitzia rubriflora var. jacobseniana

Poellnitzia rubriflora var. jacobseniana, commonly known as the red-flowered poellnitzia, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, providing a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and native plant collections.
