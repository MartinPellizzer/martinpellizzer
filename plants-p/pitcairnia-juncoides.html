Pitcairnia juncoides

Pitcairnia juncoides, commonly known as the grass-like pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that forms a rosette structure, which helps collect and retain water in its natural habitat. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright red or orange flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and conservation efforts.
