Phagnalon ballii

Phagnalon ballii, commonly known as the Ball's phagnalon, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, demonstrating a high degree of adaptability to harsh conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
