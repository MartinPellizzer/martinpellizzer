Pachypleurum

Pachypleurum, commonly known as the tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, compound leaves. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to grow in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically evergreen and can reach significant lengths, contributing to its ornamental value.

Pachypleurum plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
