Piptochaetium tovarii

Piptochaetium tovarii, commonly known as the Tovar's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
