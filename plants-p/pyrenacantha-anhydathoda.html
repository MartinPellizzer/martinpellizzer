Pyrenacantha anhydathoda

Pyrenacantha anhydathoda, commonly known as the African star jasmine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, and it is well adapted to dry, rocky soils.

Pyrenacantha anhydathoda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant scent.
