Persica domestica var. suaveolens

Persica domestica var. suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet cherry.

This variety is characterized by its small to medium-sized fruits with a sweet, aromatic flavor. The plant typically has a compact growth habit with thorny branches and dark green, lobed leaves. It produces white flowers in spring, which are followed by cherries that ripen in late summer.

The fruit is valued for its taste and is commonly used in both fresh consumption and culinary applications.
