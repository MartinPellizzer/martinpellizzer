Piper pachyphyllum

Piper pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leaf pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its large, waxy leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into peppery fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
