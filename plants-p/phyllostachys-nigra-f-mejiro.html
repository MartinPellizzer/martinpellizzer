Phyllostachys nigra f. mejiro

Phyllostachys nigra f. mejiro, commonly known as the black bamboo or black-striped bamboo, is a striking ornamental bamboo species native to China.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, culms that are often streaked with lighter green or yellow, creating a visually appealing contrast. The plant grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it suitable for large gardens or as a privacy screen. Its leaves are long, narrow, and glossy, contributing to its aesthetic value.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, thriving in temperate climates.
