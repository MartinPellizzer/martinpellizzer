Psiadia incana

Psiadia incana, commonly known as the hairy psiadia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features wiry, branched stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Psiadia incana is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
