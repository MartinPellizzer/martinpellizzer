Pycreus plumbeonucea

Pycreus plumbeonucea, commonly known as the blue-veined orchid, is a unique orchid species characterized by its striking blue veins on its pale green leaves.

It is native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale blue, often arranged in a loose cluster. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture efficiently from the forest floor.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural range.
