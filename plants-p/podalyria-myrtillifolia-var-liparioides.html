Podalyria myrtillifolia var. liparioides

Podalyria myrtillifolia var. liparioides, commonly known as the Lipari Island thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage.
