Polygonum macranthum

Polygonum macranthum, commonly known as the giant knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often with reddish hues. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, appearing in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture, and can reach lengths of up to 20 centimeters.

Polygonum macranthum is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, disturbed soils.
