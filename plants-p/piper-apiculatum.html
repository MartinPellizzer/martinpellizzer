Piper apiculatum

Piper apiculatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, peppery aroma. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries containing aromatic seeds.

Piper apiculatum is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
