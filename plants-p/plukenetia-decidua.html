Plukenetia decidua

Plukenetia decidua, commonly known as the African butterfruit, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It produces small, yellow, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a mild, buttery flavor. The plant is characterized by its deciduous leaves, which fall off during the dry season. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and requires full sun exposure.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and has potential for cultivation as a nutritious food crop.
