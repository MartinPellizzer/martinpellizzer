Primula arvensis

Primula arvensis, commonly known as the field primrose, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically reaches a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and features basal rosettes of oval, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale yellow to orange flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Botanically, it belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy floral display.
