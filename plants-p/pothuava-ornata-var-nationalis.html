Pothuava ornata var. nationalis

Pothuava ornata var. nationalis, commonly known as the National Pothuava, is a rare and ornamental plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that often display vibrant green hues with subtle variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by ornate, spiky seed pods. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by botanists and collectors alike.
