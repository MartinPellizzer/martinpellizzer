Pistacia narbonnensis var. bauhini

Pistacia narbonnensis var. bauhini, commonly known as the Corsican pistachio, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green fruits. The plant produces edible nuts similar to true pistachios, though they are less commonly consumed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, temperate climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
