Piper longifilamentosum

Piper longifilamentosum, commonly known as the long-filament pepper, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by elongated, thin berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
