Pholidota pygmaea

Pholidota pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf pholidote, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it store water and survive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in botanical gardens and among succulent enthusiasts.
