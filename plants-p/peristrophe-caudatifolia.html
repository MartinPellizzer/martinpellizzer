Peristrophe caudatifolia

Peristrophe caudatifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved peristrophe, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Peristrophe caudatifolia produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, usually in shades of pink or purple, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
