Phaca gracilis

Phaca gracilis, commonly known as the slender phaca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. It produces small, daisy-like flowers that are usually yellow or white, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Phaca gracilis is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
