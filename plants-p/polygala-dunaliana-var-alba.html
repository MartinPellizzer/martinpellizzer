Polygala dunaliana var. alba

Polygala dunaliana var. alba, commonly known as the white milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately divided, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for traditional herbal use.
