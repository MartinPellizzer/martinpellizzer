Pyrus symondsii

Pyrus symondsii, commonly known as the golden apple.

This species is a small deciduous tree native to western North America, particularly found in regions of California and Oregon. It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The fruit, which is typically small and sour, is not commonly consumed by humans but is used in some traditional practices.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other apple species.
