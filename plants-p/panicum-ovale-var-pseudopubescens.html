Panicum ovale var. pseudopubescens

Panicum ovale var. pseudopubescens, commonly known as the silky panicgrass, is a variant of the panicgrass genus characterized by its distinctive floral structure and growth habits.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides and fields. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple. Its botanical significance lies in its morphological variations, which can aid in ecological studies and plant identification.

The variety is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its potential use in erosion control.
