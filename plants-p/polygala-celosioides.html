Polygala celosioides

Polygala celosioides, commonly known as the pink fairy-orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, orchid-like flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a small perennial herb with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, fragrant blooms during the summer months.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
