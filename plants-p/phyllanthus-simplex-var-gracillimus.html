Phyllanthus simplex var. gracillimus

Phyllanthus simplex var. gracillimus, commonly known as the slender leaf spurge, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
