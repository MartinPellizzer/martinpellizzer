Paramignya scandens var. scandens

Paramignya scandens var. scandens, commonly known as the climbing fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems that can climb and entangle other vegetation. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
