Phoradendron bilineatum

Phoradendron bilineatum, commonly known as the two-lined mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of oak trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its berries are red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays an important role in the ecological interactions of its host trees and the surrounding wildlife.
