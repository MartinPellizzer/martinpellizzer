Pericalia sessilifolia

Pericalia sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaf pericalia, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and often hollow stems.

It is native to South Africa and typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are sessile, meaning they attach directly to the stem without a petiole, and are often covered in fine hairs.

Pericalia sessilifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
