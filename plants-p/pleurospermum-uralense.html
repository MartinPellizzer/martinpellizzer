Pleurospermum uralense

Pleurospermum uralense, commonly known as the Siberian tansy, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Asia.

It features a robust root system and is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subarctic regions.

Pleurospermum uralense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
