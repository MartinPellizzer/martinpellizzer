Phillyrea microphylla

Phillyrea microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved Phillyrea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by small, woody fruits. Phillyrea microphylla is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
