Pinanga kuhlii var. alba

Pinanga kuhlii var. alba, commonly known as the white-leafed bamboo palm, is a variant of the Pinanga kuhlii species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, characterized by its tall, slender stems and clustered, feather-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are pale green to white, giving it a striking appearance that distinguishes it from other bamboo palms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and air-purifying capabilities in indoor settings.
