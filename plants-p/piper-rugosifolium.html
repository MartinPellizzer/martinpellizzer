Piper rugosifolium

Piper rugosifolium, commonly known as the rough-leaved pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its dark green, rough-textured leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to red when mature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of aromatic foliage.
