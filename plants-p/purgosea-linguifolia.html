Purgosea linguifolia

Purgosea linguifolia, commonly known as the tongue-leaved purgesea, is a unique plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, with elongated, tongue-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom during the rainy season. Its root system is known for containing compounds with mild laxative properties, which have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

However, due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is now considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
