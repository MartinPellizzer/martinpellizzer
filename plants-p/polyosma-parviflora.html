Polyosma parviflora

Polyosma parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered polyosma, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its botanical features include a woody stem, simple leaves, and small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked.

It is generally found in moist, shaded environments such as undergrowth in forests or along stream banks.
