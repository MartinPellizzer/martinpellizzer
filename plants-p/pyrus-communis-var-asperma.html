Pyrus communis var. asperma

Pyrus communis var. asperma, commonly known as the rough pear, is a variant of the common pear characterized by its rough, bark-like texture on the fruit's surface.

This variety is native to Europe and is known for its hard, edible fruit with a tart flavor. The plant is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with broad leaves and clusters of white flowers in spring. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The rough pear is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.
