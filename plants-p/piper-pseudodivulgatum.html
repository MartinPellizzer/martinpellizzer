Piper pseudodivulgatum

Piper pseudodivulgatum, commonly known as the red pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its long, trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
