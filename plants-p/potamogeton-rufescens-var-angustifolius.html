Potamogeton rufescens var. angustifolius

Potamogeton rufescens var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It features long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically narrow and have a smooth, grass-like texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, and it often grows in shallow, slow-moving waters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its habitat.
