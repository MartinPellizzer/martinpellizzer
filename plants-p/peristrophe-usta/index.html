Peristrophe Usta

Peristrophe usta, scientifically known as Peristrophe usta, is a lesser-known member of the Acanthaceae family native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia. This small, herbaceous plant is characterized by its delicate, opposite leaves and clusters of tiny, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple and pink. Despite its modest appearance, Peristrophe usta holds cultural and medicinal significance in various traditional practices. Its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments makes it a unique addition to understory plant communities. However, due to habitat loss and limited research, this species remains underappreciated in the broader botanical world.
