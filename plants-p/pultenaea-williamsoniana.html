Pultenaea williamsoniana

Pultenaea williamsoniana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pultenaea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Pultenaea williamsoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
