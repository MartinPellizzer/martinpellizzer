Populus × serotina var. erecta

Populus × serotina var. erecta, commonly known as the tall poplar, is a hybrid species within the Populus genus.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green during the growing season. This variety is native to parts of eastern North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in riparian zones and can tolerate a range of environmental conditions.

The plant is noted for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in reforestation and windbreak projects.
