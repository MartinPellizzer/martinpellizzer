Phlomis umbrosa f. albiflora

Phlomis umbrosa f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered stonecrop, is a flowering plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
