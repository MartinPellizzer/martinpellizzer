Penducella nummularia

Penducella nummularia, commonly known as the silver dollar plant, is a low-growing succulent native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically silver or blue-green in color. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers on slender stems during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its compact form and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
