Poterium ancistroides subsp. parviflorum

Poterium ancistroides subsp.

parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered bottlebrush plant, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble bottle brushes, typically composed of numerous small, tubular flowers.

The plant has dark green, narrow leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
