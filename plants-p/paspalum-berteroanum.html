Paspalum berteroanum

Paspalum berteroanum, commonly known as the Andean grass or Bertero's grass, is a perennial bunchgrass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Paspalum berteroanum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
