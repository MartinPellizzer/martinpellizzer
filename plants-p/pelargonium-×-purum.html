Pelargonium × purum

Pelargonium × purum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, velvety leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, depending on the cultivar. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it suitable for container gardening and hanging baskets.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant.
