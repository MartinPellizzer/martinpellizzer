Puya vargasiana

Puya vargasiana, commonly known as the Vargasian grass of fire, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, arching inflorescence that bears numerous vibrant red flowers, which are often described as resembling flames. The plant has long, narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette, providing structural support for the flowering stalk. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, dry conditions and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, Puya vargasiana is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
