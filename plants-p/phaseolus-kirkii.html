Phaseolus kirkii

Phaseolus kirkii, commonly known as the African runner bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It produces long, cylindrical pods that are typically green when immature and turn yellow when mature. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and bears small, yellow flowers. It is well adapted to warm, humid climates and thrives in well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its edible beans, which are rich in protein and commonly used in local cuisines.
