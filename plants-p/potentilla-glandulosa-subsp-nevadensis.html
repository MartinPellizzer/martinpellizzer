Potentilla glandulosa subsp. nevadensis

Potentilla glandulosa subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra strawberry. It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its glandular leaves and clusters of small, white to pink flowers.

Native to the western United States, particularly in mountainous regions, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by wildlife.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of alpine and subalpine ecosystems.
