Panicum ciliatiflorum var. rufum

Panicum ciliatiflorum var. rufum, commonly known as the redflower panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence that bears reddish bracts, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
