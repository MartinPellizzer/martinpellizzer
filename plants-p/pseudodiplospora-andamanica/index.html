Pseudodiplospora Andamanica

Pseudodiplospora andamanica is a rare and fascinating plant native to the Andaman Islands. It belongs to the family Podostomata, which includes aquatic and semi-aquatic species. This unique plant thrives in freshwater environments, often growing along the edges of streams and rivers. Its morphology is characterized by a dense, branching structure with specialized leaves adapted for underwater life. Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered an important subject for botanical research.
