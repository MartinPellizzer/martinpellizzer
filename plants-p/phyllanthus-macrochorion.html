Phyllanthus macrochorion

Phyllanthus macrochorion, commonly known as the large-leaf phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glossy and broad. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are typically surrounded by bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high levels of sunlight.

Phyllanthus macrochorion is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage.
