Pleioblastus amarus var. hangzhouensis

Pleioblastus amarus var. hangzhouensis, commonly known as the Hangzhou bamboo, is a variant of the pleioblastus amarus species native to eastern China.

It is a clumping bamboo characterized by its slender, green culms and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks. It has a slow growth rate and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid climates.

This variety is also noted for its resilience to urban conditions, making it a popular choice for landscaping in cities.
