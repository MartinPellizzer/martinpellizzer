Plantago nitrophila

Plantago nitrophila, commonly known as the nitrate plant, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in nitrogen-rich soils, often found in disturbed or cultivated areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
