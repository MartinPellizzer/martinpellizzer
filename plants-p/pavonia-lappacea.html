Pavonia lappacea

Pavonia lappacea, commonly known as the Mexican bush bean, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to red bract-like flowers. The plant produces small, legume-like pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental and edible gardening.

Pavonia lappacea is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
