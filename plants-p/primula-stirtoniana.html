Primula stirtoniana

Primula stirtoniana, commonly known as the Stirton's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats at high altitudes. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
