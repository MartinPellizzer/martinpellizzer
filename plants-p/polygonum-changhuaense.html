Polygonum changhuaense

Polygonum changhuaense, commonly known as the Changhua knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes species known for their distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant features upright, slender stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have a rough, textured surface. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Polygonum changhuaense is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
