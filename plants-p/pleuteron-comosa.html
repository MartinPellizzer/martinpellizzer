Pleuteron comosa

Pleuteron comosa, commonly known as the woolly pleuranthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
