Pyrethrum corymbosum subsp. pourretii

Pyrethrum corymbosum subsp.

pourretii, commonly known as the Corsican pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers.

The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family and is known for its high concentration of pyrethrins, which are natural insecticides. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for both ornamental purposes and its historical use in pest control.
