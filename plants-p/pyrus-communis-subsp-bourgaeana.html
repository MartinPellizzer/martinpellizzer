Pyrus communis subsp. bourgaeana

Pyrus communis subsp.

bourgaeana, commonly known as the Bourgogne pear, is a subspecies native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or brown when mature.

The plant produces white flowers in the spring, which are clustered in clusters. Its leaves are broad and have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its fruit and is also appreciated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
