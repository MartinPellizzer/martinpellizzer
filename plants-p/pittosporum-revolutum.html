Pittosporum revolutum

Pittosporum revolutum, commonly known as the New Zealand pittosporum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and horticultural use.
