Paspalum paniculatum var. grandiflorum

Paspalum paniculatum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It features tall, slender stems and produces large, showy panicles of white to pink flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in wetland or marshy habitats. It is adapted to moist environments and can tolerate periodic flooding.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and wetland restoration projects.
