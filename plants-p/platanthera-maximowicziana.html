Platanthera maximowicziana

Platanthera maximowicziana, commonly known as the Maximowicz's orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette, while its flowers are usually white or pale yellow with a distinctive lip.

It relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, which is a common feature among orchids.
