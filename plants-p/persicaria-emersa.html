Persicaria emersa

Persicaria emersa, commonly known as water smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments.

It features upright, branching stems that often grow up to 1.5 meters tall, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth or slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming from late summer to early autumn. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in saturated soils, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Persicaria emersa is also known for its ability to spread via rhizomes, forming dense stands in wetland habitats.
