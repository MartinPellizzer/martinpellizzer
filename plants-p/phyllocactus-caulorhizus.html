Phyllocactus caulorhizus

Phyllocactus caulorhizus, commonly known as the golden crown cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems that are covered in bright yellow areoles, which are specialized structures for producing spines and flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers during the warmer months, adding to its ornamental value. Its root system is adapted to absorb moisture efficiently, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
