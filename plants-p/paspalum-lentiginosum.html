Paspalum lentiginosum

Paspalum lentiginosum, commonly known as the blackgrass or velvetgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, often forming dense mats near water bodies. The plant has dark green, narrow leaves with a distinctive velvety texture, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in groups. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated, and the seeds are dispersed by water or wind.

Paspalum lentiginosum is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions due to its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed soils.
