Pilosella bauhini subsp. thaumasioides

Pilosella bauhini subsp.

thaumasioides, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are densely hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

This subspecies is often found in open meadows and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
