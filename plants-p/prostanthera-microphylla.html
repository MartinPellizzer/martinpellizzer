Prostanthera microphylla

Prostanthera microphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved honeysuckle, is a small shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry woodland habitats.

Prostanthera microphylla is valued for its fragrant foliage and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.
