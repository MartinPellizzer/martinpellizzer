Parkia sumatrana subsp. sumatrana

Parkia sumatrana subsp.

sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran legume, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Indonesia. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers.

The tree produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.

Its growth is typically in tropical lowland areas with well-drained soils.
