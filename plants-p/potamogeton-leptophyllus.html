Potamogeton leptophyllus

Potamogeton leptophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically less than 2 millimeters wide, arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by cylindrical, spirally arranged fruits. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton leptophyllus is often found in slow-moving waters such as ponds, lakes, and marshes.
