Polygala vulgaris var. sardoa

Polygala vulgaris var. sardoa, commonly known as the Sardinian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sardinia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it is often found in coastal or semi-arid habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
