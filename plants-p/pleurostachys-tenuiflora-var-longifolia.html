Pleurostachys tenuiflora var. longifolia

Pleurostachys tenuiflora var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered feather grass, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and elongated, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The variety longifolia is distinguished by its longer floral bracts compared to the typical form of P. tenuiflora.
