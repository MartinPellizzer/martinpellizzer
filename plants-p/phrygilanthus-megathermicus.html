Phrygilanthus megathermicus

Phrygilanthus megathermicus, commonly known as the firebush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes used in horticulture and landscape design.
