Picea excelsa var. finedonensis

Picea excelsa var. finedonensis, commonly known as the Finedon pine, is a rare and ancient variant of the white spruce.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and conical shape, with dark green, needle-like leaves. Native to the Scottish Highlands, this variety thrives in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. Its distinctive features include long, drooping branches and a unique, slow growth rate.

Due to its rarity and historical significance, it is a subject of interest in botanical and conservation studies.
