Paronychia mexicana var. popocatepetliana

Paronychia mexicana var. popocatepetliana, commonly known as the Popocatepetli stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features fleshy, upright stems covered in small, blue-green leaves that are often coated with a waxy cuticle. The plant produces clusters of tiny, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pale pink during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.
