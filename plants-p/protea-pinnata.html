Protea pinnata

Protea pinnata, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant features a sturdy, woody stem with long, narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a silvery or grayish bloom. Native to the coastal regions of South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections around the world.
