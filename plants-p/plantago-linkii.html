Plantago linkii

Plantago linkii, commonly known as the Chinese plantain, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
