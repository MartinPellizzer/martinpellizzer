Pyrethrum paucifolium

Pyrethrum paucifolium, commonly known as the sparse-leaved pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and sparse, giving it its common name.

Pyrethrum paucifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has mild pesticidal properties due to the presence of pyrethrins.
