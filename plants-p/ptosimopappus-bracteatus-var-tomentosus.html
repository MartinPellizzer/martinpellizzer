Ptosimopappus bracteatus var. tomentosus

Ptosimopappus bracteatus var. tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly bract euphorbia, is a perennial shrub native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that provides insulation against extreme temperatures. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

This species is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
