Phyllanthus nyikae

Phyllanthus nyikae, commonly known as the Nyikae leafcutter, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often lobed or serrated. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a feature typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruits are capsules that release tiny seeds when mature.

The species is of interest in ethnobotanical studies due to its potential medicinal properties.
