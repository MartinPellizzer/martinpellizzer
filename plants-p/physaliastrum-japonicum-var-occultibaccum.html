Physaliastrum japonicum var. occultibaccum

Physaliastrum japonicum var. occultibaccum, commonly known as the Japanese false hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically hidden among the foliage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
