Polygonum aviculare var. triviale

Polygonum aviculare var. triviale, commonly known as common knotgrass, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features prostrate or ascending stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically inconspicuous and borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a hairy surface.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides.
