Polystachya holtzeana

Polystachya holtzeana, commonly known as the Holtzeana bromeliad, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central pool to collect rainwater. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, colorful flowers, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Its foliage is typically broad and glossy, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Polystachya holtzeana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in humid environments.
