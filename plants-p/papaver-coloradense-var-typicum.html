Papaver coloradense var. typicum

Papaver coloradense var. typicum, commonly known as the Colorado poppy, is a member of the Papaveraceae family and is native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking red to orange-pink petals, which are typically five to seven lobes in shape. The plant produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous small, brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

The Colorado poppy is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
