Polygala javana var. angustifolia

Polygala javana var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed or open habitats. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a slightly hairy surface, while the flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
