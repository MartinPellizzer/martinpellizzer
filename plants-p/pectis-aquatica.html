Pectis aquatica

Pectis aquatica, commonly known as water pectis, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant thrives in moist soils and is often found along riverbanks and in marshy areas. Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, which is typical for many plants in its habitat.

Pectis aquatica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
