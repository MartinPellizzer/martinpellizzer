Polypodium ceteraccinum

Polypodium ceteraccinum, commonly known as the slender shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern found primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that often grow in dense clusters. The plant's rhizomes are typically short and creeping, allowing it to anchor itself to tree trunks or other surfaces. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in many fern species.

Polypodium ceteraccinum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest canopies where it can access adequate moisture and light.
