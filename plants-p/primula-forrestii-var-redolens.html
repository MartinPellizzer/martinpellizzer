Primula forrestii var. redolens

Primula forrestii var. redolens, commonly known as the fragrant primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces a distinctive, sweet fragrance, which is particularly noticeable in its native habitat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique scent, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
