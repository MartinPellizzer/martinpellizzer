Persicaria hesperia

Persicaria hesperia, commonly known as the Western knotweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Persicaria hesperia is known for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
