Picea jezoensis f. rubrilepis

Picea jezoensis f. rubrilepis, commonly known as the red-barked fir, is a rare variant of the Japanese fir.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown bark, which becomes more pronounced with age. The plant is native to the Korean Peninsula and parts of northeastern China. It typically grows in cool, moist environments at higher elevations.

Its foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are dense and provide a unique visual contrast to its vibrant bark.
