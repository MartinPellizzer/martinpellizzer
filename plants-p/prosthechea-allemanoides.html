Prosthechea allemanoides

Prosthechea allemanoides, commonly known as the Alleman's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, greenish-brown pseudobulbs and long, pendulous inflorescences that bear showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow with red or brown markings, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Prosthechea allemanoides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
