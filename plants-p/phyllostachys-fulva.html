Phyllostachys fulva

Phyllostachys fulva, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms. The plant is a clump-forming perennial that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its rapid growth rate makes it popular for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a landscape plant.

Phyllostachys fulva is also valued for its use in traditional crafts and as a source of sustainable materials.
