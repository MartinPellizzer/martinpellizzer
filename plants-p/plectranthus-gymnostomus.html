Plectranthus gymnostomus

Plectranthus gymnostomus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf plectranthus, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and as a houseplant.
