Potamogeton gramineus f. maximus

Potamogeton gramineus f. maximus, commonly known as the broad-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features long, narrow leaves that are often broad at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance in water bodies. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets, which are submerged or floating near the water surface. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in lakes, ponds, and wetlands.

Its presence is an indicator of good water quality and is important for aquatic ecosystems as it provides habitat and food for various water organisms.
