Pleurothallis leucosepala

Pleurothallis leucosepala, commonly known as the white-sepaled pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate, white flowers with a subtle fragrance. Its sepals and petals are pale in color, giving it its common name.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
