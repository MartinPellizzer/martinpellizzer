Pleopeltis insignis f. aperta

Pleopeltis insignis f. aperta, commonly known as the broad-leafed rock fern, is a variant of the rock fern genus Pleopeltis.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant is native to tropical regions of Asia and is often found growing on rocky substrates. Its fronds are usually dark green with a smooth texture and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, moist environments.
