Peucedanum ruthenicum

Peucedanum ruthenicum, commonly known as Ruthenian angelica, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

It is native to Central and Eastern Europe, often found in moist meadows and woodland edges. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white to greenish-yellow flowers in clustered umbels. Its seeds are small and ridged, typical of the genus, and it is valued for its medicinal and culinary properties.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, contributing to its traditional use in herbal medicine.
