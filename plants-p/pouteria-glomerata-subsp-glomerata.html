Pouteria glomerata subsp. glomerata

Pouteria glomerata subsp.

glomerata, commonly known as the clustered soursop, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers.

The plant produces a fruit with a thick, spiny rind and a soft, aromatic pulp. It is often found in humid lowland forests and thrives in warm, moist climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
