Ptilotus indivisus

Ptilotus indivisus, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with hairy stems and leaves. Its flowers are tubular and attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Ptilotus indivisus is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with low water availability.
