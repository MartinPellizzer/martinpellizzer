Portulaca okinawensis var. okinawensis

Portulaca okinawensis var. okinawensis, commonly known as the Okinawa morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is a low-growing annual herb with succulent, fleshy leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, a characteristic that gives it its common name.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its colorful blooms.
