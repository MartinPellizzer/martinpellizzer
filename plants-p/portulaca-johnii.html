Portulaca johnii

Portulaca johnii, commonly known as the desert purslane, is a low-growing, succulent plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features fleshy, thick leaves and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant is well adapted to hot, dry environments, storing water in its leaves and stems. Its flowers are typically five-petaled and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Portulaca johnii is often found in sandy soils and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
