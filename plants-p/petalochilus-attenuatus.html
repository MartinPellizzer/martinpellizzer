Petalochilus attenuatus

Petalochilus attenuatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and delicate, elongated flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant prefers shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with a distinct lip that aids in pollination.

Petalochilus attenuatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
