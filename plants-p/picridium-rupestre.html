Picridium rupestre

Picridium rupestre, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant native to rocky and arid regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in crevices and on limestone, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are often narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss.

The fruit is a dry, capsule that releases seeds when mature, contributing to its survival in challenging habitats.
