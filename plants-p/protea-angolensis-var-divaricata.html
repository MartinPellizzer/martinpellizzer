Protea angolensis var. divaricata

Protea angolensis var. divaricata, commonly known as the Angolan protea, is a striking evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous papery bracts that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, sandy soils. Its floral structure is adapted for pollination by birds and insects, with nectar-rich flowers that attract various pollinators.

This species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and long-lasting blooms.
