Protea lepidocarpon

Protea lepidocarpon, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. Its unique floral structure attracts a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
