Polygala nicaeensis subsp. pedemontana

Polygala nicaeensis subsp.

pedemontana, commonly known as the Alpine milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
