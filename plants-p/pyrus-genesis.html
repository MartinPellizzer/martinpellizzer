Pyrus genesis

Pyrus genesis, commonly known as the ancient apple.

This species is a rare and extinct member of the Rosaceae family, characterized by its unique morphology and genetic makeup. It is believed to have originated in the ancient region of Persia, where it was cultivated for its fruit. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with broad, lobed leaves and small, fragrant flowers.

Due to its historical significance and genetic uniqueness, it is of great interest to botanists and horticulturists.
