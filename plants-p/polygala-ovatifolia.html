Polygala ovatifolia

Polygala ovatifolia, commonly known as the ovate-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are ovate in shape, with a smooth texture, and the flowers are usually pale pink or white.

It is often found in coastal and inland regions, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
