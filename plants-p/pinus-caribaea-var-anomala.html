Pinus caribaea var. anomala

Pinus caribaea var. anomala, commonly known as the Cuban white pine, is a variant of the Caribbean pine.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Caribbean islands, including Cuba, Hispaniola, and Jamaica. The tree typically grows to a height of 20-30 meters and has long, needle-like leaves that are arranged in bundles of five. Its cones are woody and contain two-winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its timber and plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
