Pseudobromus silvaticus

Pseudobromus silvaticus, commonly known as the forest brome, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and forest understories. The plant has slender, tufted shoots with narrow, blade-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a compact panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

Pseudobromus silvaticus plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various small invertebrates.
