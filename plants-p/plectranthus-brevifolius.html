Plectranthus brevifolius

Plectranthus brevifolius, commonly known as the Mexican mint or honey mint, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple to lavender in color, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens and containers.

Its strong mint-like fragrance makes it a popular ornamental and aromatic plant.
