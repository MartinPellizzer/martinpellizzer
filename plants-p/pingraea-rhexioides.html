Pingraea rhexioides

Pingraea rhexioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved greenbottle bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces fleshy, berry-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Pingraea rhexioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
