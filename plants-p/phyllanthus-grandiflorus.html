Phyllanthus grandiflorus

Phyllanthus grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features small, green leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its fruit is a small, spherical capsule that contains several seeds.

Phyllanthus grandiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
