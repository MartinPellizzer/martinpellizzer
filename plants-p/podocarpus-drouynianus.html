Podocarpus drouynianus

Podocarpus drouynianus, commonly known as the Drouyn's podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and woody cones. The tree typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in mountainous areas with high humidity. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged texture, while its foliage offers a deep green color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in horticulture for its adaptability to various soil types.
