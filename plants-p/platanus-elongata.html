Platanus elongata

Platanus elongata, commonly known as the Chinese sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces spherical, woody fruits that split open to release seeds. Its bark is thick, flaky, and often has a pale, whitish color.

Platanus elongata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in urban environments.
