Polygala major var. major

Polygala major var. major, commonly known as the greater milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with a distinctive spurred structure that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
