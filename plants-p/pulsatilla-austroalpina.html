Pulsatilla austroalpina

Pulsatilla austroalpina, commonly known as the Austrian pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, hairy stem with palmately divided leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and mountainous areas.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in several European countries.
