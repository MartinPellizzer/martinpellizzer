Pimelea gigandra

Pimelea gigandra, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

Pimelea gigandra is an important species in the ecological makeup of its native habitat.
