Potentilla californica

Potentilla californica, commonly known as California potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has silvery-gray foliage and produces fruiting structures called achenes, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas.

Botanically, it is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and its ability to tolerate drought conditions.
