Pleioblastus maculatus

Pleioblastus maculatus, commonly known as the variegated bamboo, is a species of grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and distinctive variegated leaves that feature white or yellow markings. This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden settings.

Pleioblastus maculatus is also valued for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, enhancing its versatility in landscaping.
