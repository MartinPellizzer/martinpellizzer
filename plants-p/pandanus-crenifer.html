Pandanus crenifer

Pandanus crenifer, commonly known as the coastal pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to salt spray and sandy soils. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color.

The fruit, known as a "breadfruit," is edible and has been an important food source for indigenous communities.
