Pelargonium × diminutum

Pelargonium × diminutum, commonly known as the dwarf geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features rounded, velvety leaves with a distinctively lobed shape, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium × diminutum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
