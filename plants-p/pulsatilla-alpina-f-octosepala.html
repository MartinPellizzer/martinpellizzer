Pulsatilla alpina f. octosepala

It is characterized by its showy, pinkish-white flowers with eight sepals, a distinctive feature of this particular form. Native to alpine regions of Europe, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations. The plant produces a single, erect stem bearing a cluster of flowers, which bloom in early spring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
