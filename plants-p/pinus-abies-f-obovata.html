Pinus abies f. obovata

Pinus abies f. obovata, commonly known as the Norwegian fir, is a horticultural form of the Norway spruce.

It is characterized by its obovate, broad, and often flattened needles that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as a compact, conical shrub or small tree, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, thriving in temperate climates.

This form is valued for its unique foliage and is often used in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
