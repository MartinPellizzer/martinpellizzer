Panicum disjunctum

Panicum disjunctum, commonly known as the disjointed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial grass species characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wetlands, and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
