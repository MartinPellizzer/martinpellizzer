Pachypodanthium gossweileri

Pachypodanthium gossweileri, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a unique flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of bright red, orange, or yellow flowers during the holiday season, which is why it is popular as a festive decoration. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ability to bloom reliably makes it a favorite among indoor gardeners.
