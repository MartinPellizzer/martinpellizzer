Picea menziesii var. parryana

Picea menziesii var. parryana, commonly known as the Parry's Douglas-fir, is a variant of the Douglas-fir native to the western United States.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves. The cones are typically reddish-brown and contain winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal. Its bark is thick and furrowed, providing natural protection against wildfires.

This variant is often found in high-elevation forests and is valued for its resilience and ecological importance.
