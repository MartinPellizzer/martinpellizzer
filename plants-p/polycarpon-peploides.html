Polycarpon peploides

Polycarpon peploides, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant features hairy, sprawling stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers are usually blue to purple in color and bloom in the early morning.

Polycarpon peploides is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
