Piper mornicola var. longifolium

Piper mornicola var. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by oval, dark purple berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
