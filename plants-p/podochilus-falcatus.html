Podochilus falcatus

Podochilus falcatus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Podochilus falcatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical gardens.
