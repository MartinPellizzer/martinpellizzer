Piper pseudomollicomum

Piper pseudomollicomum, commonly known as the velvet pipevine, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other species known for their aromatic properties. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique appearance and fragrance make it a notable ornamental plant in botanical gardens.
