Polygala formosa

Polygala formosa, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in woodland or shrubland habitats. Its leaves are usually alternate and pinnately compound, while its fruits are dry pods that release seeds upon maturity.

Polygala formosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
