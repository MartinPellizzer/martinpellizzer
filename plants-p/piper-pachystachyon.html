Piper pachystachyon

Piper pachystachyon, commonly known as the giant pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are elongated berries that mature to a bright red color, often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
