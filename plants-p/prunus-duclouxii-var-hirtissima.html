Prunus duclouxii var. hirtissima

Prunus duclouxii var. hirtissima, commonly known as the Hirtissima cherry, is a small deciduous shrub or tree native to China.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are hairy on both sides, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-sour red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
