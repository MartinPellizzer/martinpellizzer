Pelargonium platanifolium

Pelargonium platanifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is widely cultivated in gardens around the world.
