Peperomia alpina

Peperomia alpina, commonly known as the Alpine pepper plant, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It features glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers indirect light, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Its compact size and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for container gardening and as a houseplant.
