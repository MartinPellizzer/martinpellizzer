Phyllanthus ovalifolius

Phyllanthus ovalifolius, commonly known as the African date palm, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
