Protasparagus penicillatus

Protasparagus penicillatus, commonly known as the slender asparagus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its ornamental value is limited, but it is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
