Phacelia violacea

Phacelia violacea, commonly known as blue phacelia, is an annual flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for wildflower gardens.

Phacelia violacea is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
