Passiflora wrightiana

Passiflora wrightiana, commonly known as Wright's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a blend of purple, blue, and white petals, along with a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Its unique floral structure and ecological significance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
