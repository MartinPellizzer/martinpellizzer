Picris kochiana

Picris kochiana, commonly known as the Kashmir wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble clusters of small flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in the spring and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its hardy nature in harsh climates.
