Pyrus submultiflora

Pyrus submultiflora, commonly known as the multiflowered pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The fruit, which is a small pear-like berry, matures in late summer and is edible.
