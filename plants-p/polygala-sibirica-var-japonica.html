Polygala sibirica var. japonica

Polygala sibirica var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also valued for its traditional medicinal uses in herbal remedies.
