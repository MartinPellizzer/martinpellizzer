Pittosporum tobira var. macrophylla

Pittosporum tobira var. macrophylla, commonly known as the sweet box, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves that are often fragrant when crushed. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it suitable for hedges and garden borders.
