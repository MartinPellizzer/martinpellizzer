Phyllanthus emblicoides

Phyllanthus emblicoides, commonly known as Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to orange-red as they mature. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and contains various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional and medicinal properties.
