Polygala baetica var. pallescens

Polygala baetica var. pallescens, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters of two to four. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky habitats.
