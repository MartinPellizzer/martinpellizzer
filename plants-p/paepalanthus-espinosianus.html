Paepalanthus espinosianus

Paepalanthus espinosianus, commonly known as the Espinosa's puya, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, grass-like leaves that form a water-holding cup. The plant produces tall flowering stalks that bear showy, spadix-like inflorescences with small, clustered flowers. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
