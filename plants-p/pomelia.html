Pomelia

Pomelia, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and showy, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant is typically shrub-like, with thick, fleshy stems adapted to store water. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a strong, sweet scent that attracts nocturnal pollinators like moths.

Pomelia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid environments.
