Paradisea bulbulifera

Paradisea bulbulifera, commonly known as the bird-of-paradise plant, is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its vibrant, feather-like bracts that resemble the plumage of a paradise bird. The plant belongs to the Araliaceae family and is known for its showy, orchid-like flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are large, glossy, and heart-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Paradisea bulbulifera thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
