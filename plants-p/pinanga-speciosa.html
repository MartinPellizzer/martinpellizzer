Pinanga speciosa

Pinanga speciosa, commonly known as the giant bamboo or giant rattan, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Arecaeae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 20 meters in height. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by clustered fruits that contain edible seeds.
