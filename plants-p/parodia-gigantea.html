Parodia gigantea

Parodia gigantea, commonly known as the giant hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the deserts of South America.

It is characterized by its large, spiny stems that resemble the spines of a hedgehog, which help to reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged structure. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, enabling it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.

Parodia gigantea is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
