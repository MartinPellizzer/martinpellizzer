Prestonia plumeriifolia

Prestonia plumeriifolia, commonly known as the Caribbean starapple, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are divided into five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible berries that are yellow when ripe. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in local cuisines.
