Pilocarpus heterophyllus

Pilocarpus heterophyllus, commonly known as the wild jervisite, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces dark brown, woody pods containing ellipsoidal seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
