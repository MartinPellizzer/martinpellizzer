Pedicularis cacuminidenta

Pedicularis cacuminidenta, commonly known as the toothed-horned monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the genus Pedicularis, which includes over 300 species commonly referred to as louseworts. This particular species is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, making it a striking yet delicate addition to its high-altitude habitat.
