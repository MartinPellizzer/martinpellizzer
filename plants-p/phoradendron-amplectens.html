Phoradendron amplectens

Phoradendron amplectens, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees, primarily in the Americas.

It lacks roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its leaves, which are adapted for photosynthesis. The plant produces small, white or yellowish berries that are often consumed by birds, which then disperse the seeds. Its life cycle involves a complex relationship with its host, contributing to the ecological dynamics of forest ecosystems.

The plant's ability to thrive on host trees makes it an important component of certain habitats.
