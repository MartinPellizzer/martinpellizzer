Psidium cuneatum var. niveum

Psidium cuneatum var. niveum, commonly known as the white guava, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically white or yellowish in color, which are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The white guava is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits.
