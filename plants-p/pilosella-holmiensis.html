Pilosella holmiensis

Pilosella holmiensis, commonly known as the Holm's pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features dense, white or pale yellow ray florets surrounded by a ring of yellow disc florets. Its leaves are typically gray-green, covered in fine, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Pilosella holmiensis is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
