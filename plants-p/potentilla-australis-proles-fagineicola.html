Potentilla australis proles fagineicola

Potentilla australis proles fagineicola, commonly known as the European cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit with many stems. The plant features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically divided into five leaflets, which are oval to lance-shaped with serrated edges.

This species is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, heaths, and woodland edges.
