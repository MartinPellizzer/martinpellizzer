Pseudotsuga taxifolia var. elongata

Pseudotsuga taxifolia var. elongata, commonly known as the Japanese white fir, is a variant of the Japanese fir native to eastern Asia.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, cylindrical trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The leaves are needle-like, flat, and arranged spirally along the branches, giving the tree a distinctive appearance. It produces cones that are typically cylindrical and contain small, winged seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
