Portulaca mucronata var. mucronata

Portulaca mucronata var. mucronata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped purslane, is a low-growing, succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. It is adapted to thrive in hot, dry conditions with minimal water requirements.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds make it both a resilient weed and a hardy ornamental plant.
