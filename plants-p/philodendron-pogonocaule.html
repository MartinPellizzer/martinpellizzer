Philodendron pogonocaule

Philodendron pogonocaule, commonly known as the velvetleaf philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a velvety texture. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by bright yellow bracts, which are often more showy than the actual flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Philodendron pogonocaule is a popular houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
