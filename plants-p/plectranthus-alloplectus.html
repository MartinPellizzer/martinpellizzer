Plectranthus alloplectus

Plectranthus alloplectus, commonly known as the silver bush or silverleaf plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves covered in fine hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Plectranthus alloplectus is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional medicine.
