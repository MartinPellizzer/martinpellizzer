Peperomia cusilluyocana

Peperomia cusilluyocana, commonly known as the Cusilluyocan pepper plant, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a compact, evergreen shrub characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and silver. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is valued as both an ornamental plant and a subject of botanical study.
