Piper longichaetum

Piper longichaetum, commonly known as the long-haired pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its long, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by elongated, fleshy berries that contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

Due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and pharmacologists alike.
