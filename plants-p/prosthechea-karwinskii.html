Prosthechea karwinskii

Prosthechea karwinskii, commonly known as the Karwinsky's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air.

Prosthechea karwinskii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouse settings.
