Polygonum schinzii

Polygonum schinzii, commonly known as Schinz's smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often edible seeds. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, clustered flowers in early summer.

The species is noted for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.
