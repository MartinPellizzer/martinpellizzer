Phlomis franchetiana var. aristata

Phlomis franchetiana var. aristata, commonly known as the aristate phlomis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and ornamental value. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture. Its showy, pink to purple flowers appear in dense spikes during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
