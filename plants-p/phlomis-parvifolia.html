Phlomis parvifolia

Phlomis parvifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf hemp-nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale pink to violet, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly serrated edge.

Phlomis parvifolia is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
