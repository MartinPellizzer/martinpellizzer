Pavonia hastata f. tomentosa

Pavonia hastata f. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly flamebeard, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, red-orange to pink bract-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are often covered in a fine, white tomentum, giving it a woolly appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant gardens.
