Perovskia virgata

Perovskia virgata, commonly known as blue mist shrub, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, silver-gray foliage and clusters of tiny, blue-violet flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its drought tolerance and long-lasting floral display.
