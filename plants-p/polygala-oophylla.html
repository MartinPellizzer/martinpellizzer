Polygala oophylla

Polygala oophylla, commonly known as the golden crown or the orchid of the sea, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its striking, orchid-like flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant grows in sandy or rocky coastal areas and is adapted to saline and wind-swept environments. Its unique morphology and habitat make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.

It is often found in association with other coastal flora, contributing to the biodiversity of maritime ecosystems.
