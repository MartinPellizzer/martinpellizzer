Potentilla gombalana

Potentilla gombalana, commonly known as the Tibetan cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves composed of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, which are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Potentilla gombalana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
