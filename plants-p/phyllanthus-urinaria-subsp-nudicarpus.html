Phyllanthus urinaria subsp. nudicarpus

Phyllanthus urinaria subsp.

nudicarpus, commonly known as the "bladder nut," is a small shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a unique, bladder-like capsule that contains several small, hard seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.
