Phalaenopsis × intermedia

Phalaenopsis × intermedia, commonly known as the orchid hybrid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Phalaenopsis bellina and Phalaenopsis sanderiana.

It is a sympodial orchid with a compact growth habit and produces long, pendulous inflorescences bearing numerous showy flowers. The plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is prized for its longevity and ease of care. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple, and often retain their color for several months.

This hybrid is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
