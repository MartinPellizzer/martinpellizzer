Pseudotephrocactus subterraneus var. brevispinus

Pseudotephrocactus subterraneus var. brevispinus, commonly known as the short-spined subterranean cactus, is a unique cactus species native to specific regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, spiny stems that grow close to the ground, adapted to its arid and rocky habitat. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to efficiently absorb water from the surrounding soil.

Due to its specialized habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
