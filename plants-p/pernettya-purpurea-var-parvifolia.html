Pernettya purpurea var. parvifolia

Pernettya purpurea var. parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf purple pernettya, is a low-growing shrub native to the northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale pink during late spring. Its berries are edible, though often tart, and are used in local cuisines.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to moist, acidic soils.
