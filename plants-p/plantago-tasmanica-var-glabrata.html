Plantago tasmanica var. glabrata

Plantago tasmanica var. glabrata, commonly known as the Tasmanian plantain, is a herbaceous plant native to Tasmania.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairless, giving it its varietal name "glabrata.". The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in damp habitats such as stream banks and forest floors.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
