Phlox glabriflora

Phlox glabriflora, commonly known as the smooth-flowered phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color and are arranged in terminal clusters, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Phlox glabriflora thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, preferring moist, shady conditions in its natural habitat.
