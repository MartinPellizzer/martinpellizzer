Philotheca conduplicata

Philotheca conduplicata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mountain pepper, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic, peppery scent. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruits are woody, capsule-like structures that contain aromatic seeds.

Philotheca conduplicata is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Aboriginal use.
