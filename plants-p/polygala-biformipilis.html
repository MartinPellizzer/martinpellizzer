Polygala biformipilis

Polygala biformipilis, commonly known as the two-winged milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with distinctive wing-like structures on the petals.

It is often found in coastal and inland regions, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
