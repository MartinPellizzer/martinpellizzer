Phragmanthera cistoides

Phragmanthera cistoides, commonly known as the rock cist, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a compact growth habit with woody stems and evergreen leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
