Protea eucalyptifolia

Protea eucalyptifolia, commonly known as the eucalyptus protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of flowers, often with a distinctive central spike. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils, showcasing the resilience of proteas. Its leaves are leathery and often have a bluish or silver hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is also valued for its ecological role, supporting various pollinators and thriving in Mediterranean-type climates.
