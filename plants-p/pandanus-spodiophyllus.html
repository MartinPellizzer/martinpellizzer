Pandanus spodiophyllus

Pandanus spodiophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a member of the Pandanaceae family.

It is a tropical plant characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are often used in traditional crafts and cooking. It thrives in coastal and wetland habitats, where it can tolerate saline conditions.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
