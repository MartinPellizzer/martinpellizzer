Photinia blinii

Photinia blinii, commonly known as the Chinese photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and attractive, glossy dark green leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red or orange berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Photinia blinii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
