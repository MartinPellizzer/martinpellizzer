Physalis sanukimonticola

Physalis sanukimonticola, commonly known as the Sanuki mountain gooseberry, is a species native to Japan.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy, spiny calyx that encloses the fruit. The fruit is small, round, and typically turns a vibrant orange or red when ripe. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is closely related to other physalis species.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.
