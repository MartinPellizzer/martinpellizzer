Prunus bigarella

Prunus bigarella, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It produces showy, single or double white or pink flowers in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. Its fruit is a small, yellowish stone fruit that ripens in late summer.

Prunus bigarella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its floral display.
