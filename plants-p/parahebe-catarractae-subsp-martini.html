Parahebe catarractae subsp. martini

Parahebe catarractae subsp.

martini, commonly known as the Stewart's parahebe, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to New Zealand. It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically a dark green with a silvery underside, and it produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its dense growth habit and ornamental appeal.

This subspecies is noted for its hardiness and ability to tolerate cool, humid conditions.
