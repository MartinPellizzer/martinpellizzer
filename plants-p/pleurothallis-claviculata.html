Pleurothallis claviculata

Pleurothallis claviculata, commonly known as the club-stem orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its flowers are usually borne on short, club-shaped stems, which give the species its common name.

Pleurothallis claviculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
