Paspalum variabile

Paspalum variabile, commonly known as variable grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its variable growth habit, often appearing as a tufted or rhizomatous plant. The plant typically has narrow, blade-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in a range of soil types and is often found in moist or disturbed habitats.

Paspalum variabile is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soils in certain environments.
