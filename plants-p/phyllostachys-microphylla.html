Phyllostachys microphylla

Phyllostachys microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms that grow in dense clusters, typically reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a smooth, glossy surface, often with a bluish-green coloration. This bamboo thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

It is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
