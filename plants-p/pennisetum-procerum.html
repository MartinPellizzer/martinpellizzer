Pennisetum procerum

Pennisetum procerum, commonly known as tall bloodgrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, blood-red plumes that appear in late summer and early autumn. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Pennisetum procerum is also valued for its ability to attract birds and other wildlife.
