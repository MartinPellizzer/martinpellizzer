Papyrus spectabilis

Papyrus spectabilis, commonly known as the giant papyrus, is a tall, reed-like plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It has long, narrow leaves that can grow up to several meters in length, forming dense clumps. The plant thrives in wetland environments, often found near rivers and lakes. Its stems are fibrous and have been historically used for making paper and other crafts.

The species is notable for its ability to tolerate waterlogged soils and its role in traditional ecological systems.
