Piper fulgentifolium

Piper fulgentifolium, commonly known as the golden-leaf pepper, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that emit a strong, spicy aroma. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy berries.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique aromatic properties.
