Polystachya couloniana

Polystachya couloniana, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and tall, branched inflorescences. The plant produces numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
