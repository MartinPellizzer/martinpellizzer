Petasites georgicus

Petasites georgicus, commonly known as the Georgian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in a distinctive, rounded inflorescence. It thrives in moist, cool environments such as mountain meadows and forest edges.

Petasites georgicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local folklore.
