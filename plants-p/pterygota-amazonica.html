Pterygota amazonica

Pterygota amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian wing fern, is a unique species native to the dense rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the group Pteridophyta, which includes non-flowering vascular plants such as ferns and horsetails. The plant is characterized by its intricate fronds that display a delicate, lacy pattern, often with a vibrant green hue. Its reproductive structures, known as sporangia, are typically found on the underside of its leaves, facilitating spore dispersal.

Due to its habitat specificity, Pterygota amazonica plays a vital role in the ecological balance of the Amazonian ecosystem.
