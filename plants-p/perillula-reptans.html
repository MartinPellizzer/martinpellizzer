Perillula reptans

Perillula reptans, commonly known as creeping perilla, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, opposite leaves. Its flowers are typically violet or purple, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Perillula reptans is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and fragrance.
