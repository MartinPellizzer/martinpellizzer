Prunus × lannesiana f. fudanzakura

Prunus × lannesiana f. fudanzakura, commonly known as the Yoshino cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its early blooming habit, producing clusters of pale pink flowers in late winter to early spring. The tree has a slender, upright growth habit with smooth, grey bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn green in summer. It is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and urban landscapes due to its aesthetic appeal.

The flower display is short-lived but highly admired for its delicate beauty.
