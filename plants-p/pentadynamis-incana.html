Pentadynamis incana

Pentadynamis incana, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery, fuzzy foliage and yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a taproot system and produces a capsule fruit containing numerous small seeds.

Pentadynamis incana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
