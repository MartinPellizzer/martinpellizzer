Prenanthes dentata

Prenanthes dentata, commonly known as the toothed bladderwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, toothed leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. The plant is typically found in wetland habitats such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. It has a fibrous root system and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich environments.
