Petrophile longifolia var. lorea

Petrophile longifolia var. lorea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf squill, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is a member of the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

It is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
