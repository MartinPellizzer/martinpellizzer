Pilosella turolensis

Pilosella turolensis, commonly known as the Turol's pearly weed, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of small, tubular florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as slopes and meadows. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

Pilosella turolensis is relatively small, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters, and blooms with yellow flowers from late spring to early summer.
