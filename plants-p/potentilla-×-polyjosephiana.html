Potentilla × polyjosephiana

Potentilla × polyjosephiana, commonly known as the Josephine's potentilla, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features dark green, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens across temperate climates.
