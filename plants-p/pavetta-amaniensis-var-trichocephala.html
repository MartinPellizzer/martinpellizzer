Pavetta amaniensis var. trichocephala

Pavetta amaniensis var. trichocephala, commonly known as the hairy-headed pavetta, is a shrub native to eastern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky areas. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its flowers are small, white or pale green, and clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dry seeds.
