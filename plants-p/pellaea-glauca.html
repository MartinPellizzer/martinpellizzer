Pellaea glauca

Pellaea glauca, commonly known as the blue pellaea, is a species of fern native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and distinctive blue-green foliage. The fronds are typically narrow and feather-like, with a waxy coating that contributes to its unique appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or limestone soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
