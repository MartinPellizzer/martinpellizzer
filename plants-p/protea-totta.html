Protea totta

Protea totta, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pale pink flowers arranged in a spherical shape. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its foliage consists of broad, leathery leaves that are often covered in a silvery-white bloom, giving it its common name.

Protea totta is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also an important species in local ecosystems.
