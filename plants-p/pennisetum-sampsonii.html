Pennisetum sampsonii

Pennisetum sampsonii, commonly known as Sampson's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, contributing to its natural spread. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Pennisetum sampsonii is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
