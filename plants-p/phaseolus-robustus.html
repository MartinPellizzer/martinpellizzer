Phaseolus robustus

Phaseolus robustus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It produces vibrant red or orange flowers and long, cylindrical pods that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires full sun for optimal growth. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three heart-shaped leaflets, and it has a vigorous vine growth habit.

Phaseolus robustus is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use, particularly in traditional cuisines.
