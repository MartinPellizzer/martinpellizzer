Primula asarifolia

Primula asarifolia, commonly known as the Japanese primrose or mountain primrose, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and mountainous regions. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Primula asarifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cool, humid climates.
