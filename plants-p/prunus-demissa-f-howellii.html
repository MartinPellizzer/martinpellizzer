Prunus demissa f. howellii

Prunus demissa f. howellii, commonly known as the Howell's cherry, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and landscape design.
