Pleopeltis incana

Pleopeltis incana, commonly known as the gray pleated fern, is a tropical fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, frond-like structures. The plant features a short, creeping rhizome with clustered, bipinnate fronds that are often covered in a fine, silvery-gray pubescence. Its leaves are typically bright green and have a distinctive, pleated appearance.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks in tropical forests.
