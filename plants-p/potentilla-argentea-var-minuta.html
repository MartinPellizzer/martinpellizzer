Potentilla argentea var. minuta

Potentilla argentea var. minuta, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to northern regions of North America.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, silver-gray leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
