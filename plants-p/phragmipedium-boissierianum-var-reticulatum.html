Phragmipedium boissierianum var. reticulatum

Phragmipedium boissierianum var. reticulatum, commonly known as the reticulate phragmipedium, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, reticulated patterning on its leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its botanical features include long, strap-like leaves and a tall, slender stem that supports its clustered blooms.

This variety is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and rarity in cultivation.
