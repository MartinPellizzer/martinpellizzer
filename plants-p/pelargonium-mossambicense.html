Pelargonium mossambicense

Pelargonium mossambicense, commonly known as the Mossambique geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The leaves are typically rounded or heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and container planting.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various climate conditions.
