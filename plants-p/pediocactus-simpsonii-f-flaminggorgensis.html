Pediocactus simpsonii f. flaminggorgensis

Pediocactus simpsonii f. flaminggorgensis, commonly known as the Flame Gorgon Cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red to orange spines that give it its dramatic appearance. The plant has a globular shape with a short, thick stem and produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water to survive.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable and often protected species in its native range.
