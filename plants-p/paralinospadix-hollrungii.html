Paralinospadix hollrungii

Paralinospadix hollrungii, commonly known as the Hainan spider-orchid, is a rare and endangered orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Hainan Island in China.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated inflorescence that bears clusters of small, spider-like flowers with intricate, veined petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often on the forest floor or in rocky crevices. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insect species, highlighting its ecological niche within the ecosystem.

Due to habitat destruction and over-collection, it faces significant conservation challenges.
