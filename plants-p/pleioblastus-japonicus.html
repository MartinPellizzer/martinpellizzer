Pleioblastus japonicus

Pleioblastus japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese forest grass, is a species of bamboo native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and slender, arching culms that often have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by wind, a common trait among grasses. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slight sheen.

Pleioblastus japonicus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
