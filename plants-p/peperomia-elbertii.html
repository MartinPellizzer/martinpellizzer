Peperomia elbertii

Peperomia elbertii, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically silver or gray-green in color, often with a textured surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish in color, which are followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate humidity.

Peperomia elbertii is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance and attractive appearance.
