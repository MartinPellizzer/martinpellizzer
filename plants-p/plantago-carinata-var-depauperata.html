Plantago carinata var. depauperata

Plantago carinata var. depauperata, commonly known as the slender plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in roadsides, fields, and other open areas.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
