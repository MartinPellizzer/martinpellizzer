Porphyra

Porphyra, commonly known as sea lettuce, is a type of red algae that thrives in marine environments.

It has a flat, leaf-like structure and is typically found in shallow coastal waters. The plant contains a variety of pigments, including phycoerythrin, which gives it its characteristic reddish color. Porphyra is rich in nutrients such as vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants.

It is often used in traditional cuisines and has applications in food, medicine, and biotechnology.
