Ponthieva formosa

Ponthieva formosa, commonly known as the Singapore tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, glossy green foliage. The plant features showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth, waxy surface.

Ponthieva formosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
