Pachycarpus richardsiae

Pachycarpus richardsiae, commonly known as the Richardsia cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season, adding a splash of color to its desert habitat. Its roots are shallow and spread out, enabling it to absorb moisture quickly from infrequent rains.

Pachycarpus richardsiae is well adapted to extreme conditions, making it a resilient and unique example of desert flora.
