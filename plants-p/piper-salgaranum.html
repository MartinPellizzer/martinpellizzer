Piper salgaranum

Piper salgaranum, commonly known as the saltbush pepper, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of tiny, yellow-green flowers. Its fruits are berries that turn red when mature, and they contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forested areas.
