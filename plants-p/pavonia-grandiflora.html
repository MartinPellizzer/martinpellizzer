Pavonia grandiflora

Pavonia grandiflora, commonly known as the largeflower wood rose, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its botanical characteristics include opposite leaf arrangement and a hairy texture on the stems and leaves.

Pavonia grandiflora is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as stream banks and woodland edges.
