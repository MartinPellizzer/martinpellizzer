Papaver dubium subsp. obtusifolium

Papaver dubium subsp.

obtusifolium, commonly known as the obtuse-leaved poppy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface, and it grows from a short, stout rhizome. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
