Primula lactucoides

Primula lactucoides, commonly known as the meadow primrose, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, silky hair, while its stems are usually erect and slender.

Primula lactucoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
