Phalaris explicata

Phalaris explicata, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, erect stems that bear small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue or greenish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Phalaris explicata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
