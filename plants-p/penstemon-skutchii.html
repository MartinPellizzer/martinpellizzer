Penstemon skutchii

Penstemon skutchii, commonly known as Skutch's beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing pink to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping in arid environments.

Penstemon skutchii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
