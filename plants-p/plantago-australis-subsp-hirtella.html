Plantago australis subsp. hirtella

Plantago australis subsp.

hirtella, commonly known as hairy plantain, is a perennial herb native to parts of Australia. It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is valued for its adaptability and is used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
