Poa pogonantha

Poa pogonantha, commonly known as the tufted meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
