Polygala vulgaris var. lusitana

Polygala vulgaris var. lusitana, commonly known as the Lusitanian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe, particularly Portugal and Spain.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats. It belongs to the family Polygalaceae, which includes about 2,000 species of flowering plants.

Its botanical features make it an important component of Mediterranean flora and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
