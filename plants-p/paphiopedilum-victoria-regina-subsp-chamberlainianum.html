Paphiopedilum victoria-regina subsp. chamberlainianum

Paphiopedilum victoria-regina subsp.

chamberlainianum, commonly known as the Victoria Regina Subspecies Chamberlainianum, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the mountainous regions of southern China. It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting flowers that typically feature a deep purple or violet hue with intricate patterns.

The plant produces pendulous blooms that are often adorned with a distinctive lip, a key feature in the orchid family. It thrives in cool, humid environments with partial shade and well-draining soil.

This subspecies is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique floral structure.
