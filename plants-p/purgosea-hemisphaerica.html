Purgosea hemisphaerica

Purgosea hemisphaerica, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a striking plant with distinctive spherical flower heads.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is native to regions of Europe and Asia. The plant features spiky, blue to purple flowers that surround a central disk, giving it its characteristic globular shape. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Purgosea hemisphaerica is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique and eye-catching blooms.
