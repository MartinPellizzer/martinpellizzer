Pyrus × sinkiangensis

Pyrus × sinkiangensis, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a hybrid species native to China.

It is characterized by its large, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible fruits that are similar to common pears but have a slightly different texture and flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its fruit production.
