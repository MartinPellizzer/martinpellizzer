Pulmonaria glabra

Pulmonaria glabra, commonly known as lungwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is known for its early spring blooming period. Its common name derives from the resemblance of its leaf patterns to lung tissue.

Pulmonaria glabra is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
