Portulaca teretifolia var. cubensis

Portulaca teretifolia var. cubensis, commonly known as Cuban purslane, is a species of flowering plant in the family Portulacaceae.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the sun. Native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba, it thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it a resilient and drought-tolerant species, often used in ornamental gardening.
