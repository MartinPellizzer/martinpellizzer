Psittacanthus amazonicus

Psittacanthus amazonicus, commonly known as the Amazonian arrowhead plant, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. Native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly the Amazon rainforest, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
