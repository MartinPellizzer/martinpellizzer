Pyrethrum myriophyllum

Pyrethrum myriophyllum, commonly known as water pepper, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from June to September. The plant has slender, branching stems that often grow in shallow water or moist soil. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a distinctive waxy coating.

Pyrethrum myriophyllum is often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams.
