Poupartia minor

Poupartia minor, commonly known as the lesser poupartia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and white or pale green, often arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Poupartia minor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
