Pultenaea brunioides

Pultenaea brunioides, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

The golden wattle is a significant species in Australian flora and is also the national floral emblem of Australia.
