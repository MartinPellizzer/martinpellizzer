Penaea tomentosa

Penaea tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly penaea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and small, clustered white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its unique texture and appearance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Penaea tomentosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
