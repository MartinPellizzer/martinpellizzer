Phrynium basiflorum

Phrynium basiflorum, commonly known as the basal-flowered arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring broad, arrow-shaped leaves with a distinct basal rosette. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Phrynium basiflorum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or near water bodies.
