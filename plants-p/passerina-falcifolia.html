Passerina falcifolia

Passerina falcifolia, commonly known as the sickle-leaved pasqueflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, sickle-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with prominent, petal-like sepals that bloom in early spring. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
