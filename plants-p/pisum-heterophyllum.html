Pisum heterophyllum

Pisum heterophyllum, commonly known as the wild pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in slender, cylindrical pods and are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The botanical features of Pisum heterophyllum make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
