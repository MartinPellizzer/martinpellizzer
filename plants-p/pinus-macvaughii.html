Pinus macvaughii

Pinus macvaughii, commonly known as the MacVaugh's pine, is a rare and endangered species native to the high elevations of the Sierra Madre mountains in Mexico.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, straight trunk and needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The tree produces distinctive cones that are often reddish-brown and can remain closed for several years before opening to release its seeds. Due to habitat loss and limited natural range, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native environment, providing habitat and contributing to local biodiversity.
