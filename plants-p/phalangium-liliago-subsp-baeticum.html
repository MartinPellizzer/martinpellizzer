Phalangium liliago subsp. baeticum

Phalangium liliago subsp.

baeticum, commonly known as the Baetican lily, is a rare and endemic subspecies native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in mountainous regions, preferring partial shade to full sun. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing nectar that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
