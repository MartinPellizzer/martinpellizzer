Poa athroostachya

Poa athroostachya, commonly known as the clustered meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often hairy stems. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in clusters, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Poa athroostachya is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.
