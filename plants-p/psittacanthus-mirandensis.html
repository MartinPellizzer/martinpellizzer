Psittacanthus mirandensis

Psittacanthus mirandensis, commonly known as the Mirandense pitcher plant, is a unique carnivorous plant native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Sarraceniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, pitcher-shaped leaves that trap and digest insects. The plant's vibrant green foliage and red-brown inflorescence make it visually striking. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture to flourish.

Its specialized morphology reflects an adaptation to nutrient-poor soils through insectivory.
