Polycarpon tetraphyllum subsp. polycarpoides

Polycarpon tetraphyllum subsp.

polycarpoides, commonly known as the four-leafed knotweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants commonly known as buckwheats and knotweeds.

The species is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. Its leaves are often lance-shaped and have a distinctive sheath-like base that wraps around the stem.

This subspecies is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats including meadows and woodland edges.
