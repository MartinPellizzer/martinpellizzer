Pelargonium karrooense

Pelargonium karrooense, commonly known as the Karroo geranium, is a South African native plant characterized by its distinctive foliage and flowers.

It is a perennial herb with erect, branching stems and oval, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Pelargonium karrooense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
