Pulsatilla montana var. calyciflormis

Pulsatilla montana var. calyciflormis, commonly known as the Pasque flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and are often adorned with a distinctive yellow center. The plant has hairy, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in rocky or meadow habitats.

Pulsatilla montana var. calyciflormis is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its early spring blooming period.
