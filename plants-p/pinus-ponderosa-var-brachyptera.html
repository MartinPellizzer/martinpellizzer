Pinus ponderosa var. brachyptera

Pinus ponderosa var. brachyptera, commonly known as the short-needle ponderosa pine, is a variant of the ponderosa pine native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its shorter, more densely packed needles compared to the typical ponderosa pine, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The tree produces cones that are typically shorter and more rounded, and its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against wildfires. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and mountainous regions.

This subspecies plays an important role in forest ecosystems, offering habitat and food for various wildlife species.
