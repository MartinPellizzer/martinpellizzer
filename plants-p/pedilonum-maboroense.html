Pedilonum maboroense

Pedilonum maboroense, commonly known as the Maboro sand orchid, is a rare flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers during the winter months. The plant thrives in well-drained sandy soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation areas.
