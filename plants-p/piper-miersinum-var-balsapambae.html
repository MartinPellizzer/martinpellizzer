Piper miersinum var. balsapambae

Piper miersinum var. balsapambae, commonly known as the Balsam Pepper, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its fruits are peppery and edible, often used in local cuisines.

The variety is distinguished by its unique fragrance and slightly different morphology compared to the typical Piper miersinum.
