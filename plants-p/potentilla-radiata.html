Potentilla radiata

Potentilla radiata, commonly known as the golden cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to August. The plant features a bushy growth habit with many slender, arching stems covered in small, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are composed of five petals and are often clustered in groups of five, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

Potentilla radiata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.
