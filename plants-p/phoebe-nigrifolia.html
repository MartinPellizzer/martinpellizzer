Phoebe nigrifolia

Phoebe nigrifolia, commonly known as the black-leaf phoebe, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy pulp.

Phoebe nigrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
