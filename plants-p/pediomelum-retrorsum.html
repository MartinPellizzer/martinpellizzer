Pediomelum retrorsum

Pediomelum retrorsum, commonly known as the backward-pointing pediomelum, is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits.

Its fruit is a dehiscent pod that opens to release seeds, a common reproductive strategy in the legume family.

Pediomelum retrorsum is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its drought-resistant properties.
