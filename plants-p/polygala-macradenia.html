Polygala macradenia

Polygala macradenia, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky habitats and is known for its unique floral structure. It produces small, clustered inflorescences that attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
