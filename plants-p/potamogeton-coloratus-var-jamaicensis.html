Potamogeton coloratus var. jamaicensis

Potamogeton coloratus var. jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican redstem pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to the freshwater ecosystems of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically bright green to reddish in color. The plant produces tiny, single-seeded fruits that are dispersed by water currents. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters with soft, muddy or sandy substrates.

Due to its rapid growth, it can form dense underwater vegetation that provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
