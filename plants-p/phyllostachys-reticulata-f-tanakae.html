Phyllostachys reticulata f. tanakae

Phyllostachys reticulata f. tanakae, commonly known as the Tanaka bamboo, is a variant of the silver bamboo species.

It is characterized by its striking, silver-green culms that are often covered in a fine, powdery bloom. This ornamental bamboo is native to China and is widely cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes. The plant typically grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 6 meters.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile choice for temperate climates.
