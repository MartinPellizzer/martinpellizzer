Potentilla × riddensis

Potentilla × riddensis, commonly known as the Riddens potentilla, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is widely cultivated in gardens across temperate climates.
