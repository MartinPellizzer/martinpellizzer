Pulegium vulgare f. linearifolium

Pulegium vulgare f. linearifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved pennyroyal, is a flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and stream banks. Its aromatic foliage has been historically used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic properties.

The plant's distinctive appearance and strong scent make it a notable species within its genus.
