Physalis amphitricha

Physalis amphitricha, commonly known as the common groundcherry, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are enclosed in a papery, inflated calyx. These fruits, which resemble tiny tomatoes, are edible when fully mature and often used in culinary applications.

Physalis amphitricha thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.
