Phyteuma spicatum f. microdon

Phyteuma spicatum f. microdon, commonly known as the dwarf spirea, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features upright, spire-like clusters of small, pale yellow to cream-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are densely packed along the stems, giving it a neat, tidy appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its compact growth habit and delicate flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, ornamental plant.
