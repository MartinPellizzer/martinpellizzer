Pothos rigidus

Pothos rigidus, commonly known as the hardy pothos, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage.

Pothos rigidus is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
