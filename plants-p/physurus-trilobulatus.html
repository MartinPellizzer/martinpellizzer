Physurus trilobulatus

Physurus trilobulatus, commonly known as the three-lobed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as wetlands and stream banks.

Physurus trilobulatus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
