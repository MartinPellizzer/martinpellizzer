Pelargonium × lepidum

Pelargonium × lepidum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dark green, slightly lobed leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is widely cultivated in gardens and indoor spaces.
