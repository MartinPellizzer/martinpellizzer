Potamogeton × nitens var. occidentalis

Potamogeton × nitens var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are typically submerged in freshwater environments. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spikes, which are often found near the water's surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often used as an indicator of water quality and ecosystem health.
