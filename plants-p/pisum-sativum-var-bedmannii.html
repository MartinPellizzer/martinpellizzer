Pisum sativum var. bedmannii

Pisum sativum var. bedmannii, commonly known as the Bedmann's pea, is a cultivated variety of the common pea.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and typically produces small, edible pods. The plant is often grown for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is also valued for its use in traditional gardening and as a source of legumes in certain cuisines.
