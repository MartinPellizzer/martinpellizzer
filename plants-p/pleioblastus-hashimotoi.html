Pleioblastus hashimotoi

Pleioblastus hashimotoi, commonly known as the Hashimoto's bamboo, is a species of grass found primarily in East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green. This plant is known for its ornamental value, particularly in gardens and landscapes where its delicate appearance is appreciated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Pleioblastus hashimotoi is also valued for its ability to form dense clumps, making it an effective ground cover in shaded areas.
