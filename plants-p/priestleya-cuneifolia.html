Priestleya cuneifolia

Priestleya cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf priesthood lily, is a striking member of the Alstroemeriaceae family, native to the high Andes of South America.

This perennial plant is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its flowers are showy, with six petals that are typically a vibrant shade of red or orange, and they bloom in late summer. Priestleya cuneifolia thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found growing in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after plant among botanical enthusiasts and conservationists.
