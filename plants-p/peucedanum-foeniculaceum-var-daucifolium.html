Peucedanum foeniculaceum var. daucifolium

Peucedanum foeniculaceum var. daucifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved anise, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its seeds are similar in appearance to those of fennel and have a licorice-like scent.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is often cultivated for its aromatic foliage and seeds.
