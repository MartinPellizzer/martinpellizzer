Poa trivialis var. occidentalis

Poa trivialis var. occidentalis, commonly known as western meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides, meadows, and prairies. The plant has slender, tufted blades with a bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its fibrous root system and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

This grass plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms.
