Pluchea marilandica

Pluchea marilandica, commonly known as Maryland false fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has alternate, hairy, and usually lobed leaves, and produces small, yellowish flower heads that bloom from late spring to early fall. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower structure.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.
