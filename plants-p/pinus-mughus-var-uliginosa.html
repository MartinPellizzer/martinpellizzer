Pinus mughus var. uliginosa

Pinus mughus var. uliginosa, commonly known as the swamp pine, is a variant of the Mughus pine native to certain regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in wet, swampy environments, which influences its growth habits and ecological niche. The tree typically exhibits a conical shape with long, slender needles that are often bluish-green in color. Its cones are relatively small and release seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

This variety plays a significant role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its wetland habitats.
