Polygonum alaskanum

Polygonum alaskanum, commonly known as Alaska knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils along streambanks and in alpine environments. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color and bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Polygonum alaskanum is known for its adaptability to cold climates and its role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
