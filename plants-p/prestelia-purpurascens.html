Prestelia purpurascens

Prestelia purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-stemmed bluemink, is a striking perennial plant native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that turn a vivid purple as they mature, contrasting with its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a silvery-green. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, arranged in loose clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant collections.
