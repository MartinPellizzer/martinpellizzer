Pyrus coronaria var. lancifolia

Pyrus coronaria var. lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed common pear, is a variety of the common pear species native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is typically a small, sweet pear that ripens in late summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in woodland edges or open fields.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in horticulture.
