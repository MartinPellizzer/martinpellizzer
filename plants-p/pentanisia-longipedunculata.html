Pentanisia longipedunculata

Pentanisia longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-peduncled sandverbena, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
