Passiflora unipetala

Passiflora unipetala, commonly known as the single-petaled passionflower, is a striking tropical vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments resembling a crown. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed raw or used in jams. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

Passiflora unipetala is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
