Potamogeton × kyushuensis

Potamogeton × kyushuensis, commonly known as the Kyushu pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically submerged in freshwater environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are arranged in spikelets, typical of the Potamogeton genus. It thrives in slow-moving or still waters, often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
