Pulicaria dysenterica subsp. dentata

Pulicaria dysenterica subsp.

dentata, commonly known as the serrated pulicaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its gray-green, woolly leaves and small yellow flowers.

The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are typically serrated, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and drought tolerance.
