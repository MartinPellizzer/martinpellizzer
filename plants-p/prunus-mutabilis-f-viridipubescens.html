Prunus mutabilis f. viridipubescens

Prunus mutabilis f. viridipubescens, commonly known as the green-hairy cherry, is a unique variant of the Prunus mutabilis species.

It is characterized by its distinctive greenish-brown pubescence on the stems and leaves, which gives it a striking appearance. The plant typically produces white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, edible fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
