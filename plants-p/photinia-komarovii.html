Photinia komarovii

Photinia komarovii, commonly known as the Chinese firethorn, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and bright red berries that appear in late summer. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that turn red in the fall, adding ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its berries are a food source for birds, contributing to ecological diversity.
