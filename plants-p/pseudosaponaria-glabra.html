Pseudosaponaria glabra

Pseudosaponaria glabra, commonly known as the smooth saponaria, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of pink or white flowers with five petals, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
