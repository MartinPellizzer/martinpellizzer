Puya coerulea

Puya coerulea, commonly known as the blue puya, is a striking plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a member of the bromeliad family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that bears numerous blue-violet flowers. The plant produces large, sword-shaped leaves that form a rosette, creating a dense, water-holding structure at the base. Puya coerulea thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
