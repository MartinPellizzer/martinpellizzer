Plantago sodiroana

Plantago sodiroana, commonly known as the Andean plantain, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. It is part of the Plantaginaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like the common plantain.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous areas.
