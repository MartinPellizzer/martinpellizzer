Pithecellobium berteroanum

Pithecellobium berteroanum, commonly known as the yellow mamey or yellow mamey tree, is a species of legume native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often trifoliate or pinnate, and its yellow to orange flowers. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and the tree is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and potential use in agroforestry systems.
