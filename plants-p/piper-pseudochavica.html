Piper pseudochavica

Piper pseudochavica, commonly known as the red pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are edible and have a spicy flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly peppery aroma, making it a unique addition to both ornamental and culinary gardens.

The plant is often grown for its ornamental value and as a source of mild spices.
