Piliostigma foveolatum

Piliostigma foveolatum, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a shade plant.
