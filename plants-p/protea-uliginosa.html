Protea uliginosa

Protea uliginosa, commonly known as the bog protea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, cone-shaped inflorescence composed of numerous small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in wet, boggy environments and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it produces a distinctive, woody stem.

Protea uliginosa is a member of the Proteaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
