Piofontia mutiscuana

Piofontia mutiscuana, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. Piofontia mutiscuana thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements contribute to its cultivation in both gardens and natural habitats.
