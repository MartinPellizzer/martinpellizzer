Planchonella mindanaensis

Planchonella mindanaensis, commonly known as the Mindanao plum, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits resembling plums. Its fruit is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor, making it valuable both nutritionally and commercially.

The species is also noted for its potential in agroforestry and conservation efforts due to its ecological importance.
