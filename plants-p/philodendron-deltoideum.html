Philodendron deltoideum

Philodendron deltoideum, commonly known as the creeping philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy, dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant has aerial roots that allow it to climb and attach to supports, making it well-suited for hanging baskets or trained growth. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and humidity.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
