Phlox nivalis subsp. nivalis

Phlox nivalis subsp.

nivalis, commonly known as the snow phlox, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the alpine regions of North America. It is characterized by its dense clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
