Pyrethrum paradoxum

Pyrethrum paradoxum, commonly known as the paradoxical pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces small, spherical seed heads that contain numerous fine seeds, which are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements, it is widely cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
