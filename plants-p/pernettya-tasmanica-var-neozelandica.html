Pernettya tasmanica var. neozelandica

Pernettya tasmanica var. neozelandica, commonly known as the New Zealand cranberry, is a compact evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, red, edible berry similar to a cranberry, often used in culinary applications.

This species thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.
