Potentilla tenuiloba

Potentilla tenuiloba, commonly known as slenderleaf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to North America.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has thin, wiry stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and adaptable plant suitable for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
