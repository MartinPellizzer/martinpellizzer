Psorothamnus fremontii var. attenuatus

Psorothamnus fremontii var. attenuatus, commonly known as the slender burrobrush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, gray-green leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry climates. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.

Its structure and adaptations make it an important component of the local flora, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
