Persea leytensis

Persea leytensis, commonly known as the Leyte avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, oval fruits with a smooth, thin skin and a creamy, buttery flesh. It thrives in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and potential as a food source, it is of interest for horticultural and ecological studies.
