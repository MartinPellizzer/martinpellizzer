Pratia montana f. variegata

Pratia montana f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated mountain pratia, is a striking ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that display a striking pattern of white and green variegation, enhancing its visual appeal. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its unique foliage, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
