Pachypodium rosulatum var. eburneum

Pachypodium rosulatum var. eburneum, commonly known as the Madagascar moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its swollen, bottle-shaped stem and a crown of spiny, paddle-like leaves. The plant produces striking yellow or orange flowers during the dry season. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for xerophytic gardens.
