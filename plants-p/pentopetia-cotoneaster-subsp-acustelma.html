Pentopetia cotoneaster subsp. acustelma

Pentopetia cotoneaster subsp.

acustelma, commonly known as the Acustelma cotoneaster, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit.

The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is well adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
