Paullinia nobilis

Paullinia nobilis, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruit is a bright purple, spherical berry that ripens in the fall and is known for its intense flavor.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
