Pyrus × irregularis

Pyrus × irregularis, commonly known as the irregular pear, is a hybrid species within the genus Pyrus, belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its variable morphology, exhibiting a range of leaf shapes and fruit sizes, which contribute to its common name. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, with deciduous leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are usually white or pink, appearing in clusters, and the fruit is a small, edible pear-like berry.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its genetic diversity and potential for horticultural use.
