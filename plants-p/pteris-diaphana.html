Pteris diaphana

Pteris diaphana, commonly known as the glassy water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its fronds are pinnately divided, with a slender and arching structure.

Pteris diaphana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
