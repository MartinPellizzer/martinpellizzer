Piper pergeniculatum

Piper pergeniculatum, commonly known as the golden piper or mountain pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other species known for their aromatic properties. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, opposite leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

The leaves and fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
