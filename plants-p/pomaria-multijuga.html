Pomaria multijuga

Pomaria multijuga, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green inflorescences that consist of spikelets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Pomaria multijuga plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
