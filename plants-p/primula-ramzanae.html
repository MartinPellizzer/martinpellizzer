Primula ramzanae

Primula ramzanae, commonly known as the Ramzanae primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves, which help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
