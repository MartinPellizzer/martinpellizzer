Pyrus karatavica

Pyrus karatavica, commonly known as the Karatavica pear, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers. The tree produces small, sweet, and juicy pears that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
