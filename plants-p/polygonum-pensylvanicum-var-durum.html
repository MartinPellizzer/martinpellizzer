Polygonum pensylvanicum var. durum

Polygonum pensylvanicum var. durum, commonly known as scarlet polygonum, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a reddish tinge, while its stems are erect and somewhat hairy.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
