Pyrus immortalis

Pyrus immortalis, commonly known as the Immortal Pear, is a rare and ancient species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and edible, sweet-tart fruits that have been prized for their unique flavor. The plant exhibits remarkable resilience, thriving in harsh alpine conditions with well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its leaves are dark green and oval-shaped, providing a striking visual contrast to the pale blossoms.

Due to its historical significance and limited natural habitat, Pyrus immortalis is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
