Passiflora cyanea

Passiflora cyanea, commonly known as the blue passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers, which are adorned with intricate patterns of red, yellow, and white. The plant produces edible, dark blue berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs.

Passiflora cyanea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
