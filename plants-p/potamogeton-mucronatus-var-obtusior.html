Potamogeton mucronatus var. obtusior

Potamogeton mucronatus var. obtusior, commonly known as the blunt-tipped pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender, branching stems that are often covered with small, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically arranged in dense spikes, and it is often found in slow-moving or still waters. Its leaves are generally ovate to elliptical in shape, with a blunt or rounded tip, which distinguishes it from other pondweed species.

This plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
