Paepalanthus superbus

Paepalanthus superbus, commonly known as the giant puya, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the bromeliad family and is characterized by its tall, upright stem and large, showy inflorescence. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically long and narrow, forming a rosette that helps collect water in its native arid environment.

Paepalanthus superbus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
