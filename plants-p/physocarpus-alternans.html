Physocarpus alternans

Physocarpus alternans, commonly known as Ninebark, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that alternate along the stems, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers in early summer, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its bark is thick and peels in papery layers, providing a distinctive texture.

Physocarpus alternans is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
