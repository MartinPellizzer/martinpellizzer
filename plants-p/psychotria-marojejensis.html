Psychotria marojejensis

Psychotria marojejensis, commonly known as the "Kichwa sacred plant," is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are rich in alkaloids, including harmine and harmaline, which have psychoactive properties. It is often found in moist, shaded forest understories at high elevations.

Due to its cultural and medicinal significance, it is also of interest in ethnobotanical and pharmacological research.
