Poa versicolor subsp. varia

Poa versicolor subsp.

varia, commonly known as the variable bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including moist and well-drained conditions.

The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, and its leaves are typically bluish-green in color, giving it its common name. It is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscaping.
