Pelargonium ambiguum

Pelargonium ambiguum, commonly known as the variable geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant features showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with contrasting markings. Its leaves are typically lobed or serrated, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Pelargonium ambiguum is also valued for its adaptability to various growing conditions.
