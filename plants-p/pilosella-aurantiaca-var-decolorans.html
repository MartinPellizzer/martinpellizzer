Pilosella aurantiaca var. decolorans

Pilosella aurantiaca var. decolorans, commonly known as the yellow hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounded by a ring of shorter, tubular disk florets. Its leaves are hairy and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.
