Pavetta owariensis var. opaca

Pavetta owariensis var. opaca, commonly known as the glossy pavetta, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage.
