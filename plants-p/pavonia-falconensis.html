Pavonia falconensis

Pavonia falconensis, commonly known as the Falcon's beak, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly found in Jamaica and the Cayman Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and showy bracts that enhance its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry forests.
