Piper arborigaudens

Piper arborigaudens, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces berries that are initially green and turn red when mature, attracting various bird species. It is often found in humid lowland forests and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

Due to its invasive nature in some regions, it is sometimes considered a weed in tropical ecosystems.
