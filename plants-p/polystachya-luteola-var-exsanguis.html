Polystachya luteola var. exsanguis

Polystachya luteola var. exsanguis, commonly known as the blood orchid, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, pale yellow to reddish flowers that resemble blood, hence its common name. The plant is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. It typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial plant, with long, strap-like leaves and clustered inflorescences.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
