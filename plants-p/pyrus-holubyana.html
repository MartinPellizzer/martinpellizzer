Pyrus holubyana

Pyrus holubyana, commonly known as Holubyan pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its deciduous leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like berry, is typically edible but not widely cultivated for commercial use.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential genetic value in pear breeding programs.
