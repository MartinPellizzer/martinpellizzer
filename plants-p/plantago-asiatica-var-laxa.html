Plantago asiatica var. laxa

Plantago asiatica var. laxa, commonly known as broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It features rosettes of broad, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes during the summer months. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and gardens.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
