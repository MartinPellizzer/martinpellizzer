Poa arctica subsp. aperta

Poa arctica subsp.

aperta, commonly known as the Arctic bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the Arctic and subarctic regions. It thrives in cold, nutrient-poor environments, often found in tundra and alpine habitats.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds. Its root system is extensive, helping it anchor in unstable, frozen soils.

This subspecies is adapted to withstand extreme cold and short growing seasons.
