Phlomis herba-venti var. laxiflora

Phlomis herba-venti var. laxiflora, commonly known as the looseflower betony, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features erect, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
