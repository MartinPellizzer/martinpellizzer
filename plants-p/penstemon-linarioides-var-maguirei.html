Penstemon linarioides var. maguirei

Penstemon linarioides var. maguirei, commonly known as Maguire's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
