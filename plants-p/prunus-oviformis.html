Prunus oviformis

Prunus oviformis, commonly known as the ovalleaf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves, which are typically green on top and hairy underneath. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet berries in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Prunus oviformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various wildlife species.
