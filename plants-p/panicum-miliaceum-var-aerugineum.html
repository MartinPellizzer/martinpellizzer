Panicum miliaceum var. aerugineum

Panicum miliaceum var. aerugineum, commonly known as blue panicgrass, is a variant of the common panicgrass species.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance compared to its green-leaved relatives. This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic value and drought tolerance.

The blue coloration is due to the presence of anthocyanin pigments, which can vary in intensity depending on environmental conditions.
