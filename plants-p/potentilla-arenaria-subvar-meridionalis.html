Potentilla arenaria subvar. meridionalis

Potentilla arenaria subvar. meridionalis, commonly known as the southern sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils. It is often found in coastal areas and dry grasslands.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
