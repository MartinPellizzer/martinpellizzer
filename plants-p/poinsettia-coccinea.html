Poinsettia coccinea

Poinsettia coccinea, commonly known as the Christmas star, is a tropical shrub native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts, which are modified leaves that surround small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in warm, humid environments. It prefers well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight.

Poinsettias are popular holiday plants due to their striking appearance and seasonal availability.
