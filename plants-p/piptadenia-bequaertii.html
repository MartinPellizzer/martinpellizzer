Piptadenia bequaertii

Piptadenia bequaertii, commonly known as the African locust bean tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisine and medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for wildlife and supporting soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.
