Plagiotaxis grandiflora

Plagiotaxis grandiflora, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, often displaying vibrant colors such as pink, purple, and white. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, meaning it grows in segments rather than from a single stem, with each segment producing a cluster of leaves and flowers. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native humid environments.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
