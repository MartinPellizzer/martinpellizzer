Peucedanum aberdaricum

Peucedanum aberdaricum, commonly known as the Crimean cow parsley, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of Asia, particularly the Caucasus and Crimea. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along riverbanks, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are finely divided and emit a mild, spicy scent when crushed.

The flowers, which appear in late spring, are white or pale green and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
