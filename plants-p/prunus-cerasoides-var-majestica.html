Prunus cerasoides var. majestica

Prunus cerasoides var. majestica, commonly known as the Kashmiri cherry or Himalayan cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces small, white or pink flowers in spring. The plant has elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, and it bears small, edible cherries in summer. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand cold climates.
