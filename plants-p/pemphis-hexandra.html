Pemphis hexandra

Pemphis hexandra, commonly known as the waterleaf, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Pemphis hexandra is often found in wetland or riparian habitats, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
