Phialanthus resinifluus

Phialanthus resinifluus, commonly known as the resinous firetree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often reddish bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or yellowish in color. One of its notable features is the secretion of a sticky, aromatic resin from its bark and leaves.

This resin has been used traditionally in various cultural and medicinal practices.
