Pleopeltis squamulosa

Pleopeltis squamulosa, commonly known as the scaly pleopeltis, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its intricate, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows on rocky surfaces or tree trunks, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. Its leaves are covered in small, scale-like structures, which provide protection and aid in moisture retention.

Pleopeltis squamulosa is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
