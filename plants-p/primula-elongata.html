Primula elongata

Primula elongata, commonly known as the elongated primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features basal leaves that are elongated and lance-shaped, often with a hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland landscapes.
