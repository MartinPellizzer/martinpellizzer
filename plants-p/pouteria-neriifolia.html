Pouteria neriifolia

Pouteria neriifolia, commonly known as the black-leafed soursop, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that eventually develop into yellow, spiny fruits. Its fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.
