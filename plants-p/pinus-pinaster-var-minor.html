Pinus pinaster var. minor

Pinus pinaster var. minor, commonly known as the Corsican pine, is a variant of the maritime pine native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant has a robust root system that helps it thrive in well-drained soils. Its cones are typically small and contain winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is often valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
