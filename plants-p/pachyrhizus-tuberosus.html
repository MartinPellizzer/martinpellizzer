Pachyrhizus tuberosus

Pachyrhizus tuberosus, commonly known as the Andean tuber or mashua, is a tuberous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its unique, swollen root system that stores nutrients. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has compound leaves with trifoliate patterns. It thrives in cool, high-altitude environments and is often cultivated for its edible tubers.

Due to its adaptability and nutritional value, it is gaining attention as a potential crop for sustainable agriculture.
