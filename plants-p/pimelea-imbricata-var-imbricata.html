Pimelea imbricata var. imbricata

Pimelea imbricata var. imbricata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed riceflower, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing delicate, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native gardens.
