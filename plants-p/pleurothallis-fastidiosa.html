Pleurothallis fastidiosa

Pleurothallis fastidiosa, commonly known as the golden torch orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that resemble torches, which bloom during the dry season. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it has a compact growth habit.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique and vibrant floral display.
