Papaver laevigatum var. erosum

Papaver laevigatum var. erosum, commonly known as the velvet poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its smooth, velvety petals.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. The plant produces striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange. Its leaves are broad and lobed, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
