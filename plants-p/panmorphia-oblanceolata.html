Panmorphia oblanceolata

Panmorphia oblanceolata, commonly known as the broadleaf panmorph, is a unique flowering plant characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves.

It belongs to the family Panmorphaceae and is native to specific regions of Southeast Asia. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the spring and early summer. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
