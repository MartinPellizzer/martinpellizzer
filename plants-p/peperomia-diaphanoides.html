Peperomia diaphanoides

Peperomia diaphanoides, commonly known as the translucent peperomia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that often have a translucent or semi-transparent appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage makes it a striking addition to any plant collection.
