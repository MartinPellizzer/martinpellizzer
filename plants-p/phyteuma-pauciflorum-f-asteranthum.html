Phyteuma pauciflorum f. asteranthum

Phyteuma pauciflorum f. asteranthum, commonly known as the showy phyteuma, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of showy, lavender-blue to violet flowers with a distinctive, star-shaped appearance. The foliage consists of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. This variety is noted for its extended blooming period, typically from late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
