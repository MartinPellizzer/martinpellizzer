Prunus jamasakura var. incisoides

Prunus jamasakura var. incisoides, commonly known as the Japanese maple with incised leaves, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn vibrant red or orange in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its foliage and seasonal color changes make it a visually striking addition to landscapes.
