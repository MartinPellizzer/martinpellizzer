Piper rivinoides

Piper rivinoides, commonly known as the river pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by berries. Its roots and leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical studies.
