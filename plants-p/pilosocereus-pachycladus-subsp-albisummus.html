Pilosocereus pachycladus subsp. albisummus

Pilosocereus pachycladus subsp.

albisummus, commonly known as the White-crested Cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its columnar stem with distinctive white spines and a dense covering of fine, white hairs.

The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is a slow-growing, drought-tolerant species well adapted to harsh desert conditions.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
