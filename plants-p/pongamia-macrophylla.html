Pongamia macrophylla

Pongamia macrophylla, commonly known as the Indian beech, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces pods containing seeds that are rich in oil, making it valuable for biofuel production. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important species in reforestation and ecological restoration projects.
