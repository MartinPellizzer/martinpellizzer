Phyllanthus purpurascens

Phyllanthus purpurascens, commonly known as the red-veined sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant features small, fleshy leaves with distinctive red veins, which give it its common name. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in inflorescences, often appearing in late summer.

Phyllanthus purpurascens is adapted to arid environments and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
