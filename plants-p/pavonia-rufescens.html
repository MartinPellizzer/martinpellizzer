Pavonia rufescens

Pavonia rufescens, commonly known as scarlet bushcrack, is a species of flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in open woodlands and prairies. The plant produces showy, red or pink bract-like flowers that are arranged in clusters at the ends of its branches. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy texture and a reddish or purplish hue.

Pavonia rufescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
