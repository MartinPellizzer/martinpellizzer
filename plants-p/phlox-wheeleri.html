Phlox wheeleri

Phlox wheeleri, commonly known as Wheeler's phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. The plant features showy clusters of pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Phlox wheeleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
