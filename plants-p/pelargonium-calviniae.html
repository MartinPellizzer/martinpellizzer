Pelargonium calviniae

Pelargonium calviniae, commonly known as the Calvinia geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa, specifically the Western Cape province, where it grows in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink to red, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Pelargonium calviniae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
