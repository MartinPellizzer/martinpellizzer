Potamogeton × gallicus

Potamogeton × gallicus, commonly known as the European pondweed, is a hybrid species of aquatic plant that occurs in freshwater habitats across Europe.

It is characterized by its submerged, branching stems and long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which emerge above the water surface. Potamogeton × gallicus plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

However, it can also become invasive in certain environments, impacting native plant communities.
