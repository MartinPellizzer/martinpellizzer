Pyrus exquisita

Pyrus exquisita, commonly known as the exquisite pear, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pyrus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Pyrus exquisita is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
