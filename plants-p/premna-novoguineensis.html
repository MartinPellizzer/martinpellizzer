Premna novoguineensis

Premna novoguineensis, commonly known as the Guinean glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and typically have four petals.

It is often found in moist, shady environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
