Pyrus bracteata

Pyrus bracteata, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, white flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its fruit, a small pear-like apple, is edible but not commonly cultivated for commercial use.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in traditional Chinese medicine.
