Piratinera ovatifolia

Piratinera ovatifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or piratinera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a soft, velvety texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in terminal or axillary inflorescences.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world as a garden plant.
