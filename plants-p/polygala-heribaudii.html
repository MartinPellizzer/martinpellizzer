Polygala heribaudii

Polygala heribaudii, commonly known as the Heribaud's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae, which is characterized by its distinctive floral structures and often small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows as a low shrub with wiry stems and small, opposite leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with a unique structure that includes a spurred petal, which is a feature common in the genus Polygala.

This species is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often with deep root systems to access water from deeper soil layers.
