Prunus avium subsp. actiana

Prunus avium subsp.

actiana, commonly known as the cherries of the Caucasus, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It is characterized by its small, dark red to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in local culinary traditions.
