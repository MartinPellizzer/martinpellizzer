Peperomia gerardoi

Peperomia gerardoi, commonly known as the Gerald's Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically oval or heart-shaped, often with a waxy texture and vibrant green or variegated coloration. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically green or reddish, which are followed by tiny, round berries. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.

Peperomia gerardoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and air-purifying qualities.
