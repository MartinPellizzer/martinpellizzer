Polypodium vulgare f. hastatum

Polypodium vulgare f. hastatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed wall fern, is a fern species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, and often broad leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive, upright growth habit that makes it a striking addition to shaded gardens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in woodland areas.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
