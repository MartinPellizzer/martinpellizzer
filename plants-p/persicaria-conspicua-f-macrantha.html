Persicaria conspicua f. macrantha

Persicaria conspicua f. macrantha, commonly known as the showy knotweed, is a variant of the species Persicaria conspicua.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America. The plant features striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and wetland habitats.
