Pappostipa barrancaensis

Pappostipa barrancaensis, commonly known as the Barranca grass, is a perennial grass native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in high-altitude grasslands and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.

Due to its unique ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
