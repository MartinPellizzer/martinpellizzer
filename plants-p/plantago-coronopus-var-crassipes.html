Plantago coronopus var. crassipes

Plantago coronopus var. crassipes, commonly known as broad-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture and produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in wetlands. It is part of the Plantaginaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties.

The varietal form, crassipes, is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to the typical species.
