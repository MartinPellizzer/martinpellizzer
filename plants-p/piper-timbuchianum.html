Piper timbuchianum

Piper timbuchianum, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It is an evergreen shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall.

Due to its invasive nature in some regions, it is often considered a problematic species in ecological contexts.
