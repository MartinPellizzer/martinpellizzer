Phyllanthus lunifolius

Phyllanthus lunifolius, commonly known as the moon-leaf plant, is a small shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy leaves that have a silvery appearance, especially when viewed in the light. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, smooth seeds.

Phyllanthus lunifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
