Polygonum cespitosum var. laxiflorum

Polygonum cespitosum var. laxiflorum, commonly known as looseflower knotweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist soils along stream banks, wet meadows, and other wetland habitats. The plant features upright, branched stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a waxy surface and serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed environments.
