Paliurus asiaticus

Paliurus asiaticus, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, compound leaves, and small, white to pinkish flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky areas.

Paliurus asiaticus is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
