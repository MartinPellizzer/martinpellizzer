Patagonium villosum

Patagonium villosum, commonly known as the hairy Patagonian herb, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in Patagonian steppe ecosystems.

Patagonium villosum plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
