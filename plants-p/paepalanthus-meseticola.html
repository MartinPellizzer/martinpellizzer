Paepalanthus meseticola

Paepalanthus meseticola, commonly known as the Peruvian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, showy flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, thriving in the cool and humid climates of the Andes.
