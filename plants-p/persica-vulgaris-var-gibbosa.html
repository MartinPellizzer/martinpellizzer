Persica vulgaris var. gibbosa

Persica vulgaris var. gibbosa, commonly known as the knotted peach.

This variant is characterized by its distinctive knotted or twisted growth pattern in its stems and branches. It is a less common cultivar of the peach tree, exhibiting unique morphological features that set it apart from typical peach varieties. The plant retains the general botanical traits of the peach species, including its deciduous nature and fruiting habits.

Its ornamental value is often appreciated in horticultural settings due to its unusual appearance.
