Pandanus graminifolius

Pandanus graminifolius, commonly known as the grass-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces inflorescences that bear small, fragrant flowers, which are often used in traditional crafts and cuisine. Its roots and leaves are also utilized in various cultural practices.

Pandanus graminifolius thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near coastal areas or in humid lowland regions.
