Parosela saffordii

Parosela saffordii, commonly known as the Safford's parosela, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and heat. Its small, yellow to orange flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Parosela saffordii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
