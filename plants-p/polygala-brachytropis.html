Polygala brachytropis

Polygala brachytropis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with distinctive spurred petals that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and is often found in wetland restoration projects.
