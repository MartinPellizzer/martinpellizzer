Pittosporum truncatum

Pittosporum truncatum, commonly known as the New Zealand pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of dark green, leathery leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom in spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several small, flat seeds.

Pittosporum truncatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
