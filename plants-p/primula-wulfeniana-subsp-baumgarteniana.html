Primula wulfeniana subsp. baumgarteniana

Primula wulfeniana subsp.

baumgarteniana, commonly known as the Baumann's primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in early spring, making it a notable species in high-altitude botanical gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
