Prunus × graeca

Prunus × graeca, commonly known as the Turkish cherry or Caucasian cherry, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus.

It is native to the eastern Mediterranean and parts of the Caucasus region. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in clusters, and are followed by small, edible fruits that are often used in jams and preserves.
