Plantago major subvar. ovata

Plantago major subvar. ovata, commonly known as broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a rosette of broad, oval-shaped leaves with a rough, sandpapery texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes during the summer months. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in lawns, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.
