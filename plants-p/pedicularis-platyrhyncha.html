Pedicularis platyrhyncha

Pedicularis platyrhyncha, commonly known as the broad-beaked lousewort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the genus Pedicularis, which is characterized by its unique, spurred flowers that resemble the beaks of lice. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, with a distinctive elongated lower lip that aids in pollination by insects.

Pedicularis platyrhyncha is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
