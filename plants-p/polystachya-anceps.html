Polystachya anceps

Polystachya anceps, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature vibrant purple and white markings. The plant produces a single, erect stem that bears multiple racemes of flowers, each with three petals and a distinctive lip. Its leaves are elliptical and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Polystachya anceps is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
