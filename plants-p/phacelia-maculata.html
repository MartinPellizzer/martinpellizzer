Phacelia maculata

Phacelia maculata, commonly known as the spotted phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate blue to purple flowers and hairy, branching stems. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a fuzzy texture.

Phacelia maculata is valued for its nectar-rich blossoms, which attract a variety of pollinators like bees and butterflies.
