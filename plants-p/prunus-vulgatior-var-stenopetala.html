Prunus vulgatior var. stenopetala

Prunus vulgatior var. stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-petaled cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a glossy green surface. The fruit is a small, dark red drupe that ripens in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
