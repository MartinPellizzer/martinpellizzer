Potamogeton × dubius

Potamogeton × dubius, commonly known as the hybrid water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Potamogeton crispus and Potamogeton praelatus.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, rivers, and ponds, often forming dense vegetation mats. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often ribbon-like in appearance, and it produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Potamogeton × dubius is known for its rapid growth rate, which can lead to dense underwater coverage, impacting water quality and aquatic biodiversity.

It plays a role in providing habitat for various aquatic organisms but can also be considered a nuisance in some ecosystems.
