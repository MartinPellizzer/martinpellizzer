Potentilla bornmuelleri var. dobrogensis

Potentilla bornmuelleri var. dobrogensis, commonly known as the Dobrogian potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with multiple stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
