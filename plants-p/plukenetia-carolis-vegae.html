Plukenetia carolis-vegae

Plukenetia carolis-vegae, commonly known as the African butterfruit, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It produces small, yellow, fleshy fruits that are rich in oils and have a buttery texture. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture.

The species is valued for its oil-rich seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible oil.
