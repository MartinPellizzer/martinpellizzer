Prunus domestica var. cyanocarpa

Prunus domestica var. cyanocarpa, commonly known as the blueberry cherry, is a rare variant of the common cherry tree.

It is characterized by its striking blue-black fruit, which develops from small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are dark green and oval-shaped, providing a lush canopy.

The fruit, while not commonly cultivated for commercial use, is prized for its unique color and flavor.
