Pseudalbizzia berteroana

Pseudalbizzia berteroana, commonly known as the Potosi acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow to orange flower clusters. It is often found in dry, disturbed habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

The seeds are encased in hard pods and are dispersed by animals or through mechanical means.
