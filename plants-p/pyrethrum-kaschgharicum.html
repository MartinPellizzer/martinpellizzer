Pyrethrum kaschgharicum

Pyrethrum kaschgharicum, commonly known as the Kashmir pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan and India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and finely divided, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, spherical seed heads that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its natural insecticidal properties, it has been used in organic pest control.
