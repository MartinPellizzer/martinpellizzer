Prasophyllum suaveolens

Prasophyllum suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented leek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a spike of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. Its flowers are characterized by their delicate structure and sweet, honey-like fragrance.

Prasophyllum suaveolens is a protected species due to habitat loss and is often found in conservation reserves.
