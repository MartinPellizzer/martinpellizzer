Polygonatum lasianthum f. variegatum

Polygonatum lasianthum f. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated Solomon's seal, is a striking variant of the Solomon's seal plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated leaves with white and green patterns, which add visual interest to gardens. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in late spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and unique flowering display.
