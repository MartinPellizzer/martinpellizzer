Polygonella brevifolia

Polygonella brevifolia, commonly known as the shortleaf polygonella, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the wetlands and stream banks of North America.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants commonly referred to as buckwheats. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in small, oval-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and typically bloom in early spring.

Polygonella brevifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil in moist environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
