Polygala amara var. nivea

Polygala amara var. nivea, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive spurred structure that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds.

It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
