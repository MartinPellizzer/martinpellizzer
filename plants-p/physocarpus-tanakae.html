Physocarpus tanakae

Physocarpus tanakae, commonly known as Japanese beautybush, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of pink or white. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn vibrant red or orange in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a valuable addition to landscape designs.
