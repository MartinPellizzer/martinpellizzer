Pouteria cuspidata subsp. dura

Pouteria cuspidata subsp.

dura, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers.

The tree is notable for its hard, woody fruit, which contains a single large seed surrounded by a toxic, fibrous husk. The seed, once processed, is the source of the commercially valuable Brazil nut.

This species plays a significant ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
