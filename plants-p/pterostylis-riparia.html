Pterostylis riparia

Pterostylis riparia, commonly known as the river orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as along riverbanks and in rainforests. The plant features a slender, leafless stem with a single spike of flowers. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Pterostylis riparia is known for its specialized pollination mechanism, relying on specific insect species for reproduction.
