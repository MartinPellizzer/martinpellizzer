Pleioblastus communis

Pleioblastus communis, commonly known as the common bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive, tufted growth habit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Pleioblastus communis is also valued for its ability to form dense clumps, making it a popular choice in landscaping and garden design.
