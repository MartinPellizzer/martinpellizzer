Pterocarpus ancylocalyx var. angustifolius

Pterocarpus ancylocalyx var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody pods containing flat, elliptical seeds. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both ornamental and traditional uses.
