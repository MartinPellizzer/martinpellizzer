Persicaria lapathifolia subsp. leptoclada

Persicaria lapathifolia subsp.

leptoclada, commonly known as narrowleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and stream banks.

The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, pink to red flowers appear in dense clusters at the tips of the stems during late summer.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in wet environments and its distinctive foliage structure.
