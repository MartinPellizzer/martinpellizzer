Protea obtusa

Protea obtusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sugarbush, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in fynbos vegetation, which is characteristic of the region's Mediterranean climate. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves and produces large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small flowers. Its flowers are usually pink or red, and they bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Protea obtusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
