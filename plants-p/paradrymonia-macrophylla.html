Paradrymonia macrophylla

Paradrymonia macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed paradrymonia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological significance.
