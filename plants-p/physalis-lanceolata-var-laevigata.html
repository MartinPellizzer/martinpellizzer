Physalis lanceolata var. laevigata

Physalis lanceolata var. laevigata, commonly known as the smooth-leafed ground cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, smooth leaves and produces small, lantern-like fruits that are typically orange or red in color. The plant is native to South America and thrives in warm, temperate climates. It is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which have a tangy flavor and are used in culinary applications.

The plant also has ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique fruiting structure.
