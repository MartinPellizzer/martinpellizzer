Psoralea carnea

Psoralea carnea, commonly known as the hairy psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or inland regions. It has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Psoralea carnea is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
