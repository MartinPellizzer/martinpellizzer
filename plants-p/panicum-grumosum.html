Panicum grumosum

Panicum grumosum, commonly known as hairy panicgrass, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often used in ornamental landscaping. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for erosion control and wildlife habitat.
