Pseudomaxillaria horichii

Pseudomaxillaria horichii, commonly known as the Horichii pterostigma orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often dark-colored flowers. The plant typically produces showy, spurred flowers that are adapted for specific pollinators, such as bees or wasps. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette, helping to conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

Pseudomaxillaria horichii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
