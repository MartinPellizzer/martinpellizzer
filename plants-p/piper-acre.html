Piper acre

Piper acre, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 3,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small berries that turn red when mature, and they contain the seeds.

Piper acre is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
