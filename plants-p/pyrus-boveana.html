Pyrus boveana

Pyrus boveana, commonly known as the Bovean pear, is a species of tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, ornamental flowers and edible fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

The fruit, which is small and yellowish, is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
