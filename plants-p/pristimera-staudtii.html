Pristimera staudtii

Pristimera staudtii, commonly known as the Staudt's cypress, is a rare and unique coniferous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and scale-like leaves that are tightly pressed against its branches. The plant produces small, inconspicuous cones that contain the seeds necessary for its reproduction. Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.

Its botanical features make it an important subject for studies on alpine flora and adaptation to extreme environments.
