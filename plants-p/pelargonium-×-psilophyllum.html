Pelargonium × psilophyllum

Pelargonium × psilophyllum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, slightly lobed leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant in hanging baskets or as a ground cover due to its vigorous growth.

Pelargonium × psilophyllum prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
