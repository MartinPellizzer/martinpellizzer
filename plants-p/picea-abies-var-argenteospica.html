Picea abies var. argenteospica

Picea abies var. argenteospica, commonly known as the silver-spined fir, is a variant of the Norway spruce.

It is characterized by its striking silvery-white bark and foliage with a distinctive silver-green hue. The leaves are typically shorter and more densely packed compared to the standard Norway spruce. This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance.

It thrives in cooler climates and is relatively hardy, making it a popular choice for gardens and parks.
