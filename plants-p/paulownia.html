Paulownia

Paulownia, commonly known as the empress tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Paulowniaceae.

It is characterized by its rapid growth, making it one of the fastest-growing trees in the world. The leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture. The flowers are showy and typically appear in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The wood is lightweight, strong, and resistant to rot, making it valuable for various applications.
