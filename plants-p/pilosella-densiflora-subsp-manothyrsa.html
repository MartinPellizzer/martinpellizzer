Pilosella densiflora subsp. manothyrsa

Pilosella densiflora subsp.

manothyrsa, commonly known as the denseflower pilosella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, fluffy inflorescences composed of small, yellowish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually hairy and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in horticultural settings for ornamental purposes.
