Pancovia floribunda

Pancovia floribunda, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Pancovia floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
