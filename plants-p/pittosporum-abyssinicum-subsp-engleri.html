Pittosporum abyssinicum subsp. engleri

Pittosporum abyssinicum subsp.

engleri, commonly known as the Abyssinian pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to Ethiopia. It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
