Platycodon glaucus

Platycodon glaucus, commonly known as the bellflower or Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces cylindrical, blue to purple fruits. Platycodon glaucus is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
