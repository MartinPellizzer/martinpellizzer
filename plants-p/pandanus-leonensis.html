Pandanus leonensis

Pandanus leonensis, commonly known as the giant pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish flowers that are often used in traditional ceremonies. Its roots and leaves are utilized in various cultural practices and crafts.

The species is well adapted to coastal and sandy environments, making it a resilient component of tropical ecosystems.
