Pachychilus pubescens

Pachychilus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy pachychilus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, pubescent hairs. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural collections.
