Pueraria candollei

Pueraria candollei, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is valued for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.

Pueraria candollei is also recognized for its potential in soil conservation and as a forage crop.
