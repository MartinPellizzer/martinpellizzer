Panicum sciurotis var. breviglume

Panicum sciurotis var. breviglume, commonly known as the short-lemma panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in disturbed soils or open habitats. Its botanical structure includes fibrous roots and a rhizomatous stem system, which aids in its spread.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in erosion control.
