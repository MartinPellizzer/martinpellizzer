Podostemum dichotomum var. longifolium

Podostemum dichotomum var. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved water willow, is a rare aquatic plant found in fast-flowing streams and rivers.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are adapted to aquatic environments. The plant typically grows in submerged or partially submerged conditions, with its roots anchoring it to rocky substrates. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in the water column.

It plays an important ecological role in maintaining water quality and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
