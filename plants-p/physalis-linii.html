Physalis linii

Physalis linii, commonly known as the Lin's husk or the common husk, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, inflated calyx that encloses the fruit, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are typically covered by a papery, lantern-like covering. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Physalis linii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
