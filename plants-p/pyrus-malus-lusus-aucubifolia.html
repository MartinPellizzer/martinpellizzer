Pyrus malus lusus aucubifolia

Pyrus malus lusus aucubifolia, commonly known as the aucuba-leaved apple, is a unique hybrid plant that combines characteristics of the apple tree (Pyrus malus) and aucuba (Aucuba japonica).

It is known for its striking, broad, aucuba-like leaves that are glossy and often variegated, providing ornamental value in gardens. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, with a compact form that makes it suitable for hedges or container gardening. While it does not produce edible fruit like its parent species, it is valued for its foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.

Its botanical interest lies in its hybrid nature and the aesthetic appeal of its foliage.
