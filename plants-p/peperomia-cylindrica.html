Peperomia cylindrica

Peperomia cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical peperomia, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features upright, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a waxy, smooth cuticle. The plant produces small, clustered leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are inconspicuous, consisting of tiny, spadix-like structures surrounded by a single white bract.

Peperomia cylindrica is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its tolerance for low light and minimal watering requirements.
