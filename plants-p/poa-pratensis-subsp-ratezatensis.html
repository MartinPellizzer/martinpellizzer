Poa pratensis subsp. ratezatensis

Poa pratensis subsp.

ratezatensis, commonly known as the alpine meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of short hairs.

This subspecies thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and grasslands at high elevations. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

The plant is adapted to harsh climatic conditions, making it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
