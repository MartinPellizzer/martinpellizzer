Pilocarpus pennatifolius subf. sursum-bracteolatus

Pilocarpus pennatifolius subf.

sursum-bracteolatus, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree or *Schinus terebinthifolius*, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its compound leaves and small, red, berry-like fruits.

The plant is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is known for its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in traditional medicine for its stimulant effects.

The subspecies *sursum-bracteolatus* is distinguished by its unique bracteolate structures and leaf morphology.
