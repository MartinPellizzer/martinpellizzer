Peperomia oxycarpa

Peperomia oxycarpa, commonly known as the sharp-leaved peperomia, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, ovate leaves that are often dark green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of green or red, which are followed by tiny, round berries. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its ability to thrive in low light conditions and minimal water requirements.

Its compact size and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for container gardening and as a houseplant.
