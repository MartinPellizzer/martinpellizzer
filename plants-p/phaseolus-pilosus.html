Phaseolus pilosus

Phaseolus pilosus, commonly known as the hairy runner bean, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces edible pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds, often used in culinary applications. Its flowers are typically pale purple or white, and it thrives in warm, temperate climates.

Phaseolus pilosus is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
