Penstemon digitalis f. baueri

Penstemon digitalis f. baueri, commonly known as the Bauer's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
