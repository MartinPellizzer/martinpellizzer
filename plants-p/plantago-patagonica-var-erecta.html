Plantago patagonica var. erecta

Plantago patagonica var. erecta, commonly known as the Patagonian plantain, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a dense spike, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
