Psophiza undulata

Psophiza undulata, commonly known as the undulating lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Psophiza undulata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
