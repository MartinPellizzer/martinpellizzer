Peperomia parvifolia

Peperomia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaf peperomia, is a compact, low-maintenance houseplant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features dense, dark green foliage with small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of tiny, reddish-brown spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typical of the Piperaceae family. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it ideal for indoor cultivation.

Its compact size and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for tabletop displays and hanging baskets.
