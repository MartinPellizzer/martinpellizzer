Potamogeton × bottnicus

Potamogeton × bottnicus, commonly known as the hybrid pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Potamogeton crispus and Potamogeton perfoliatus.

It is native to temperate regions of Europe and is often found in shallow, freshwater habitats such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in whorls, and it produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. Potamogeton × bottnicus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality, as it thrives in clean, well-oxygenated environments.
