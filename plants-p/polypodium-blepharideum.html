Polypodium blepharideum

Polypodium blepharideum, commonly known as the crested polypody, is a fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, recurved fronds with a serrated margin and a unique, wavy appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often on rocky substrates or in forest understories. Its spores are produced in small, clustered sporangia located on the underside of the fronds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fern-like foliage.
