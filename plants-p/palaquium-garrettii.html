Palaquium garrettii

Palaquium garrettii, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits with a thick, red outer layer and a sweet, segmented white or pink flesh inside. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
