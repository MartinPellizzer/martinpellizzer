Poa pendulina

Poa pendulina, commonly known as the hanging grass, is a species of grass native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping, pendulous inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Poa pendulina is often found in association with other grasses and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
