Picramnia bullata

Picramnia bullata, commonly known as the bullock's heart, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are elliptical and have a rough, hairy texture.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds when mature.
