Potamogeton heterophyllus var. latifolius

Potamogeton heterophyllus var. latifolius, commonly known as broadleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its broad, elongated leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged, followed by elongated, ribbon-like fruits. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its environment.
