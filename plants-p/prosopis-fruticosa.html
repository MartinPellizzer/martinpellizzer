Prosopis fruticosa

Prosopis fruticosa, commonly known as the mesquite tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its deep root system, which allows it to thrive in harsh, drought-prone environments. The plant produces small, green pods that contain edible seeds, which are a vital food source for both wildlife and humans. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, meaning they are divided into multiple pairs of small leaflets.

Prosopis fruticosa is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
