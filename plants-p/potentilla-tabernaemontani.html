Potentilla tabernaemontani

Potentilla tabernaemontani, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Potentilla tabernaemontani is often used in rock gardens and alpine landscapes due to its ornamental value and resilience.
