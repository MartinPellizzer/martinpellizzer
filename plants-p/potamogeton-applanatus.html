Potamogeton applanatus

Potamogeton applanatus, commonly known as flatleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are flat on both sides, which help it float and absorb sunlight efficiently. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like structures. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Potamogeton applanatus is often found in slow-moving waters such as lakes, ponds, and streams.
