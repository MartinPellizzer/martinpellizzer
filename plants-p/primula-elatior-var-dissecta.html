Primula elatior var. dissecta

Primula elatior var. dissecta, commonly known as the dissected primrose, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, with a distinctive, spurred corolla. The leaves are deeply lobed and dissected, giving the plant its common name and adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and unique foliage structure.
