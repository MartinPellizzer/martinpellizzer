Pleioceras barteri

Pleioceras barteri, commonly known as the Barter's pleioceras, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiral-shaped leaves that resemble those of a fern, though it is not a true fern. The plant exhibits a peculiar growth habit, often growing in a compact, rosette form with a central stem. Its botanical significance lies in its unusual morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered, highlighting the need for further research and protection efforts.
