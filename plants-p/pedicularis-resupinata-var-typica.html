Pedicularis resupinata var. typica

Pedicularis resupinata var. typica, commonly known as the hanging beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a basal rosette of leaves and stems that trail along the ground, often hanging downward. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with a distinctive spurred structure at the back. It thrives in rocky or alpine habitats and is adapted to cool, moist environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
