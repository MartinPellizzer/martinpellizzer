Persicaria maculata

Persicaria maculata, commonly known as spotted jewelweed, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems that often bear small, yellow-orange flowers. The leaves are heart-shaped with a distinctive reddish or purplish blotch on the underside, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often found in disturbed soils.
