Phaseolus vulgaris f. minor

Phaseolus vulgaris f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf bean, is a compact variant of the common bean.

It is characterized by its shorter stature and smaller leaves compared to standard Phaseolus vulgaris. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. It is often cultivated in limited spaces due to its compact growth habit.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and suitability for container gardening.
