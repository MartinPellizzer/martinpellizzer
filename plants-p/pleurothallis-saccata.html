Pleurothallis saccata

Pleurothallis saccata, commonly known as the sac-like orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

This epiphytic plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, loose soil to prevent root rot. The plant's unique sac-like structure on its stem is a distinguishing feature, aiding in water retention.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and ease of care.
