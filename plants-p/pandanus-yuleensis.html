Pandanus yuleensis

Pandanus yuleensis, commonly known as the Christmas palm, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are typically bright green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange or red fruits that are edible. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
