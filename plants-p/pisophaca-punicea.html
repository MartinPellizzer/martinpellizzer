Pisophaca punicea

Pisophaca punicea, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are simple and alternate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, Pisophaca punicea is sometimes cultivated for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
