Pouzolzia indica var. vulgaris

Pouzolzia indica var. vulgaris, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into round, orange-red fruits with a tart flavor. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and is widely used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional and medicinal value.
