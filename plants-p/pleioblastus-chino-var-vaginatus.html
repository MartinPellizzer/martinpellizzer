Pleioblastus chino var. vaginatus

Pleioblastus chino var. vaginatus, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or the velvet bamboo, is a variety of the Pleioblastus chino species.

It is a clump-forming grass that features slender, arching stems covered in a distinctive velvety sheath. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a soft, fuzzy texture that gives it its common name.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
