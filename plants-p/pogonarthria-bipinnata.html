Pogonarthria bipinnata

Pogonarthria bipinnata, commonly known as the two-lobed grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, wiry stems that often grow close to the ground. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Pogonarthria bipinnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
