Pinus grandis var. doelleriana

Pinus grandis var. doelleriana, commonly known as the Doeller's giant pine, is a variant of the grand fir that is native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and conical shape, with dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals. The cones are typically reddish-brown and contain edible seeds that are a food source for various wildlife species. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of forest ecosystems in its native habitat.
