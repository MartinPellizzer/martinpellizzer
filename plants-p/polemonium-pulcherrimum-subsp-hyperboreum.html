Polemonium pulcherrimum subsp. hyperboreum

Polemonium pulcherrimum subsp.

hyperboreum, commonly known as the northern Jacob's ladder, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America. It features delicate, blue to purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters along its stem, giving it a ladder-like appearance.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
