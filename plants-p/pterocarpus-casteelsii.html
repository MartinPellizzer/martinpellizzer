Pterocarpus casteelsii

Pterocarpus casteelsii, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its strength and aesthetic qualities. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters.

Its fruit is a legume pod containing flat, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind, aiding in its natural propagation.
