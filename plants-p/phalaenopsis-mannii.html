Phalaenopsis mannii

Phalaenopsis mannii, commonly known as the Malagasy phalaenopsis, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences that bear clusters of showy, lip-like flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in the arid conditions of its environment.

Phalaenopsis mannii is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its elegant, long-lasting blooms.
