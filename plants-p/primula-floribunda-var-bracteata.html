Primula floribunda var. bracteata

Primula floribunda var. bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate primrose, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

The bracteate primrose is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
