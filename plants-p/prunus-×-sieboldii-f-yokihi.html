Prunus × sieboldii f. yokihi

Prunus × sieboldii f. yokihi, commonly known as the Yokohama cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus native to Japan.

It is a compact, ornamental shrub or small tree that produces showy, single or double pink to white flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and dark green, glossy leaves. It is highly valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and public spaces.

The Yokohama cherry is relatively cold-hardy and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
