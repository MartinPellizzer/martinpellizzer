Podochilus buxifolius var. striatus

Podochilus buxifolius var. striatus, commonly known as the striated boxleaf.

It is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and compact growth habit. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, fleshy berries.

This plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
