Piper chinense

Piper chinense, commonly known as Chinese pepper or long pepper, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its botanical features include aromatic leaves and a distinctive, elongated fruit structure.

Piper chinense thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary value.
