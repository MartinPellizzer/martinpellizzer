Polypodium asahinae

Polypodium asahinae, commonly known as the Asahina's shield fern, is a rare and endangered species native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen fronds that form a dense, shield-like structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as rocky crevices and forest floors. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds, and it relies on spore dispersal for propagation.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
