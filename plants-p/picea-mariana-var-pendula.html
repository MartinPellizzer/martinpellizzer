Picea mariana var. pendula

Picea mariana var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping spruce, is a variant of the black spruce native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive pendulous branches that hang in a cascading manner, giving it a unique, ornamental appearance. The tree typically grows in boreal forests and can reach heights of up to 15 meters. Its foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are dark green and slightly twisted.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal in horticulture and its ability to thrive in cooler, moist environments.
