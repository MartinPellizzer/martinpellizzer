Potentilla pulcherrima var. communis

Potentilla pulcherrima var. communis, commonly known as the golden potentilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is known for its hardiness and ability to withstand cold temperatures, making it suitable for temperate climates.
