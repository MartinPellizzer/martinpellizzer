Primula klaveriana

Primula klaveriana, commonly known as the Dutch primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Netherlands and parts of northern Europe.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy to the touch. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
