Paraboea neurophylla

Paraboea neurophylla, commonly known as the slender paraboea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and possesses opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
