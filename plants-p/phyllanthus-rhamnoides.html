Phyllanthus rhamnoides

Phyllanthus rhamnoides, commonly known as the African spurge, is a perennial shrub native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually arranged oppositely on the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Phyllanthus rhamnoides is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
