Paspalum carajasense

Paspalum carajasense, commonly known as Brazilian paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny florets. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Paspalum carajasense is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and tolerate environmental stress.
