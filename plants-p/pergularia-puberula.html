Pergularia puberula

Pergularia puberula, commonly known as the hairy pegmatite, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Pergularia puberula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
