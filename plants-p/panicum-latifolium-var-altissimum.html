Panicum latifolium var. altissimum

Panicum latifolium var. altissimum, commonly known as tall flatsedge, is a grass-like plant native to wetland and marshy environments in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, and its broad, flat leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomes are extensive, allowing it to form dense colonies in shallow water or moist soil.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
