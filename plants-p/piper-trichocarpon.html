Piper trichocarpon

Piper trichocarpon, commonly known as the rough-seeded pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by elongated, berry-like fruits. It is native to Central and South America and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
