Parodia warasii

Parodia warasii, commonly known as the Warasii cactus, is a species of succulent native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
