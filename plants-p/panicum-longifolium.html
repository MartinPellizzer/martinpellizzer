Panicum longifolium

Panicum longifolium, commonly known as slender panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a potential candidate for ecological restoration projects.
