Primula mistassinica f. intercedens

Primula mistassinica f. intercedens, commonly known as the Mistassinica primrose, is a flowering plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Canadian Rocky Mountains, particularly in Alberta and British Columbia. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, with hairy stems and clusters of bell-shaped, pale yellow to white flowers. Its foliage consists of broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs.

This rare and ornamental species is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
