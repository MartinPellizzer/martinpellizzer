Papaver lapeyrouseanum var. endressii

Papaver lapeyrouseanum var. endressii, commonly known as the Endress's poppy, is a striking member of the Papaveraceae family characterized by its large, showy flowers.

It features vibrant red petals with a contrasting black center, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. It has a taproot system and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
