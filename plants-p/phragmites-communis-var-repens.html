Phragmites communis var. repens

Phragmites communis var. repens, commonly known as common reed, is a perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, sword-like leaves. This plant forms dense stands that can dominate wetland ecosystems, often outcompeting other vegetation. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to colonize large areas rapidly.

The plant plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
