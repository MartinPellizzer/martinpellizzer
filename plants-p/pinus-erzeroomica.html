Pinus erzeroomica

Pinus erzeroomica, commonly known as the Erzeroom pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in bundles of five. The cones of this plant are woody and can persist on the tree for several years before opening to release seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with a temperate climate.

The Erzeroom pine plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
