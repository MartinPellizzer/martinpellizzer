Plantago lagopoides

Plantago lagopoides, commonly known as the sheep's barley or the broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, oval-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in a dense spike that emerges from the center of the rosette. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its medicinal properties have been recognized for centuries, with uses ranging from treating wounds to alleviating respiratory ailments.
