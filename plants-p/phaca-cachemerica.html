Phaca cachemerica

Phaca cachemerica, commonly known as the Indian nettle or wild nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including India and China.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks.

The leaves and stems are covered in fine hairs that can cause a stinging sensation when touched.
