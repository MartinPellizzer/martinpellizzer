Pleopeltis fallacissima

Pleopeltis fallacissima, commonly known as the false bird's nest fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its fiddlehead-like fronds and a thick, fibrous rootstock. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually leathery and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are clustered in a rosette-like structure.
