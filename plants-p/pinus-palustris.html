Pinus palustris

Pinus palustris, commonly known as the longleaf pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, slender needles that grow in bundles of three, and its distinctive, often fire-resistant cones. The tree can reach heights of up to 60 meters and has a lifespan that can exceed 300 years. Its root system is adapted to wet, sandy soils, and it plays a crucial role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat.

The longleaf pine is also notable for its ability to regenerate after wildfires, which contributes to the biodiversity of the ecosystems it inhabits.
