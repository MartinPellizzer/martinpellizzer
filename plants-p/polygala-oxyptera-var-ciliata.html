Polygala oxyptera var. ciliata

Polygala oxyptera var. ciliata, commonly known as the hairy polygala, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and covered with fine hairs, giving the plant its common name. The flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with a distinctive spurred structure.

It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or dry inland regions.
