Pleurothallis mastophora

Pleurothallis mastophora, commonly known as the golden rosette orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often arranged in a rosette-like pattern. Its leaves are typically dark green and arranged in a compact, circular formation.

Pleurothallis mastophora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
