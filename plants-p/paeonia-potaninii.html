Paeonia potaninii

Paeonia potaninii, commonly known as the Potanin peony, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a distinctive red or purple eye. The plant is a perennial herb with thick, fleshy rhizomes and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and of interest to horticulturists and botanists alike.
