Polemonium pulcherrimum var. haydenii

Polemonium pulcherrimum var. haydenii, commonly known as Hayden's Jacob's ladder, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its cascading clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
