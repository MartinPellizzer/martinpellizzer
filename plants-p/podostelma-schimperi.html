Podostelma schimperi

Podostelma schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian water moss, is a unique aquatic plant found in fast-flowing streams in Ethiopia.

It belongs to the family Podostomataceae and is characterized by its ribbon-like leaves that are adapted to thrive in turbulent water environments. The plant lacks true roots and instead has specialized structures called rhizoids that anchor it to submerged surfaces. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are pollinated by insects, and it plays a vital role in the aquatic ecosystem by providing habitat for microorganisms.

Due to its restricted distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically important species.
