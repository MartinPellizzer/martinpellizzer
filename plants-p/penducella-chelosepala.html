Penducella chelosepala

Penducella chelosepala, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Penducella chelosepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
