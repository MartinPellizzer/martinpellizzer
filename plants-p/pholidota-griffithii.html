Pholidota griffithii

Pholidota griffithii, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink or red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The tree has a straight trunk with smooth, grey bark and produces woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval leaflets, and it thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

Pholidota griffithii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
