Pleurothallis × karremansiana

Pleurothallis × karremansiana, commonly known as the Karremans' pleurothallis, is a hybrid orchid species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and a compact, pseudobulbous structure, which help it thrive in its humid, shaded habitat.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
