Prunus × yasujensis

Prunus × yasujensis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Prunus serrula and Prunus campanulata.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its small, sweet, and edible fruits that ripen in early summer. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, and it produces clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring.

The botanical features of Prunus × yasujensis make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and parks.
