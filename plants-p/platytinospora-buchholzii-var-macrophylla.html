Platytinospora buchholzii var. macrophylla

Platytinospora buchholzii var. macrophylla, commonly known as the giant leaf platytinospora, is a unique species within the Tectaria genus.

It is characterized by its large, elongated, and often lobed fronds that can reach impressive sizes. The plant exhibits a distinctive frond morphology with a broad, flat rachis and long, pinnate pinnae. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for specific microhabitats and its ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
