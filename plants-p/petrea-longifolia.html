Petrea longifolia

Petrea longifolia, commonly known as the blue mist vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems that can climb or trail, often forming dense, cascading foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant, white to blueish flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a mist-like appearance. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a soft, velvety texture.

Petrea longifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
