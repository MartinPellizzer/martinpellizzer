Polygala maritima

Polygala maritima, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and distinctive, spiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, making it well adapted to coastal environments. It is often found growing along sea cliffs and dunes, where it withstands strong winds and salt spray.

Its unique appearance and ecological resilience make it a notable species in maritime flora.
