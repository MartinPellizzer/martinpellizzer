Pilocarpus pennatifolius subf. deorsum-bracteolatus

Pilocarpus pennatifolius subf.

deorsum-bracteolatus, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Brazil. It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible but often have a spicy taste. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal regions.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional medicine.
