Potentilla nitida var. typica

Potentilla nitida var. typica, commonly known as silverleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-white leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in the summer, which are attractive to pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping in arid and semi-arid climates.
