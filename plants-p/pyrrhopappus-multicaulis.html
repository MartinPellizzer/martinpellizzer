Pyrrhopappus multicaulis

Pyrrhopappus multicaulis, commonly known as the yellow coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and conical disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and open woodland habitats. It has a compact, upright growth habit with branching stems and is known for its drought tolerance.

Pyrrhopappus multicaulis is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
