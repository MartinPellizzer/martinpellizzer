Pyrostria alluaudii

Pyrostria alluaudii, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a unique plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, tree-like structure with a thick, woody stem and a crown of spiny, palm-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Its distinctive appearance and hardy nature make it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.
