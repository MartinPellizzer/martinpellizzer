Phaca trifoliata

Phaca trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaved phaca, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Phaca trifoliata is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it adapts well to harsh environmental conditions.
