Perymenium acuminatum

Perymenium acuminatum, commonly known as the slender spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often toxic species. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant produces a milky sap that can be irritant, a common characteristic among members of its family.
