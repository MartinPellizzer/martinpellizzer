Polygonum banka

Polygonum banka, commonly known as the narrow-leaf smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually pink or red in color.

Polygonum banka is known for its ability to thrive in wet environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
