Phytolacca icosandra var. anomala

Phytolacca icosandra var. anomala, commonly known as the hairy phytolacca, is a variant of the phytolacca genus native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically alternate and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is notable for its medicinal uses in traditional medicine, though its berries contain harmful compounds.
