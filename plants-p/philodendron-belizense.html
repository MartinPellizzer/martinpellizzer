Philodendron belizense

Philodendron belizense, commonly known as the Belize philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular houseplant, though it can be toxic if ingested by humans or pets.
