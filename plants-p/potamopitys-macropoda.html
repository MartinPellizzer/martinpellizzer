Potamopitys macropoda

Potamopitys macropoda, commonly known as the giant water pine.

It is a rare and endangered coniferous tree native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia. The plant has a unique morphology, characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, needle-like leaves. It thrives in wet, swampy environments and plays a vital role in its ecosystem.

Due to habitat loss, it is critically endangered and requires conservation efforts to survive.
