Phacelia popei var. typica

Phacelia popei var. typica, commonly known as the desert blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Phacelia popei var. typica is an important plant for pollinators and contributes to the ecological diversity of its native range.
