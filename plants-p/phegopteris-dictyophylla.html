Phegopteris dictyophylla

Phegopteris dictyophylla, commonly known as the lace fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant features bipinnate leaves with a finely divided structure, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

The spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
