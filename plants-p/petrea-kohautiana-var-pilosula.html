Petrea kohautiana var. pilosula

Petrea kohautiana var. pilosula, commonly known as the silky petrea, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.

Its leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
