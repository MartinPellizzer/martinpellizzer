Pterocelastrus typicus

Pterocelastrus typicus, commonly known as the Chinese lantern tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, bright red, lantern-like fruits that persist through winter. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
