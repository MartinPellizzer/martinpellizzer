Prunus umbellata var. injucunda

Prunus umbellata var. injucunda, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its clusters of white or pink flowers that appear in early spring, followed by small, edible fruits. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, contributing to its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental planting and fruit cultivation in temperate climates.
