Primula bellidifolia subsp. hyacinthina

Primula bellidifolia subsp.

hyacinthina, commonly known as the Hyacinthine Primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant typically blooms in spring, producing vibrant purple or pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland borders.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its popularity among gardeners and botanists alike.
