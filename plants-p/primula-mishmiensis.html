Primula mishmiensis

Primula mishmiensis, commonly known as the Mishmi primrose, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are broadly ovate and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Primula mishmiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also of interest in botanical research due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude conditions.
