Piper duidaense var. bolivianum

Piper duidaense var. bolivianum, commonly known as the Bolivian pepper plant, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is a small, elongated berry that turns from green to yellow when mature.
