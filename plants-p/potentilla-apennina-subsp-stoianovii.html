Potentilla apennina subsp. stoianovii

Potentilla apennina subsp.

stoianovii, commonly known as the Albanian cinquefoil, is a shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
