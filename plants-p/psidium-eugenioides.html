Psidium eugenioides

Psidium eugenioides, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible, bright red berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, sweet flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The bark and leaves have also been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
