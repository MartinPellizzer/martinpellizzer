Pseudocydonia

Pseudocydonia, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a genus of flowering plants in the Rosaceae family.

It consists of a single species, Pseudocydonia sinensis, which is native to China. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, yellowish fruits resembling pears. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional Chinese medicine.
