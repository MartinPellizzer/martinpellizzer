Platanthera decipiens

Platanthera decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive orchid, is a species of orchid native to North America.

It is a perennial herb with a tall, leafless stem that bears showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive lip that resembles a bee, aiding in pollination.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, often relying on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.
