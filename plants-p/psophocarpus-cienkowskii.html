Psophocarpus cienkowskii

Psophocarpus cienkowskii, commonly known as the Chinese wing bean, is a leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive winged pods that encase its edible seeds. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring warm temperatures and ample sunlight. It has a climbing habit, often supported by trellises or other structures.

Botanically, it is valued for its nutritional content and potential agricultural applications.
