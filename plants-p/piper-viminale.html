Piper viminale

Piper viminale, commonly known as creeping pepper or wild betel, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forests.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
