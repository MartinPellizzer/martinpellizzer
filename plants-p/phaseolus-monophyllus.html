Phaseolus monophyllus

Phaseolus monophyllus, commonly known as the evergreen bean, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing legume that produces small, yellow flowers and oval, green pods. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its seeds are typically small and white. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and potential nutritional benefits, it is gaining interest in both horticulture and agriculture.
