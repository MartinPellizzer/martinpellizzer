Paphiopedilum bellatulum

Paphiopedilum bellatulum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and has a rhizomatous root system. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Paphiopedilum bellatulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
