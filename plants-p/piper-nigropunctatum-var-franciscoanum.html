Piper nigropunctatum var. franciscoanum

Piper nigropunctatum var. franciscoanum, commonly known as the Francisco's black-spotted pepper, is a tropical vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its dark green, heart-shaped leaves with black dots, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, dark purple berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
