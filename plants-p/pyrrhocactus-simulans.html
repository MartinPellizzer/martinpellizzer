Pyrrhocactus simulans

Pyrrhocactus simulans, commonly known as the false cactus or desert cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a splash of color to its otherwise muted appearance. It is native to Mexico and is often found in rocky soils and dry landscapes.

Pyrrhocactus simulans is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.
