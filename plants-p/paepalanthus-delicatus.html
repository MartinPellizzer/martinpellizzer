Paepalanthus delicatus

Paepalanthus delicatus, commonly known as the delicate puya, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family, native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in areas with low rainfall. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences that can reach up to 2 meters in height, featuring vibrant red, orange, or yellow flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a rosette structure that helps retain moisture.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in botanical gardens.
