Peperomia sirupayana

Peperomia sirupayana, commonly known as the sticky peperomia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a compact, low-growing plant with thick, fleshy leaves that often have a waxy texture. The leaves are typically oval or heart-shaped and can range in color from light green to deep green, sometimes with variegation. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color.

This plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its ability to thrive in low light conditions and its minimal water requirements.
