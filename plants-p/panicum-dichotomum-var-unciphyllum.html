Panicum dichotomum var. unciphyllum

Panicum dichotomum var. unciphyllum, commonly known as the two-branched grass, is a variant of the panic grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive dichotomous branching pattern, where each stem splits into two equal branches. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, fluffy inflorescences.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
