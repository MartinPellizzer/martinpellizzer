Pleopeltis fimbriata

Pleopeltis fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed lip fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It features a delicate, wiry stem with closely spaced, lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive fringed margin. The leaves are typically bright green and can grow up to 30 cm in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is popular in tropical horticulture.
