Paracaryum lithospermifolium var. erectum

Paracaryum lithospermifolium var. erectum, commonly known as the upright stonecrop, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, succulent leaves that store water, aiding in survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically blooming in the spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and low-maintenance plant for drought-tolerant landscapes.
