Papaver oligophyllum

Papaver oligophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats and is often found in alpine environments.

Papaver oligophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
