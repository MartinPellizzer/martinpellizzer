Piper copelandii

Piper copelandii, commonly known as the Christmas pepper or copeland's pepper, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
