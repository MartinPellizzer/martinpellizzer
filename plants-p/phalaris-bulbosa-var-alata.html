Phalaris bulbosa var. alata

Phalaris bulbosa var. alata, commonly known as the winged bulblet grass, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, wing-like structures on the lower leaf blades, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue or greenish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
