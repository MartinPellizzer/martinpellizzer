Polypodium sanctum

Polypodium sanctum, commonly known as the sacred fern, is a species of polypodiaceae found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are typically divided into narrow segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or tree trunks. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of its fronds, a typical feature of ferns.

Polypodium sanctum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in certain regions.
