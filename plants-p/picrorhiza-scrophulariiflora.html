Picrorhiza scrophulariiflora

Picrorhiza scrophulariiflora, commonly known as Kuth, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Labiatae and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant is notable for its aromatic roots, which are rich in iridoid glycosides and other bioactive compounds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is widely used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
