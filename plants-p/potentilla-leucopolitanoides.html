Potentilla leucopolitanoides

Potentilla leucopolitanoides, commonly known as the white alpine cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
