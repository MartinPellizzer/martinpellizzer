Pelargonium × latifolium

Pelargonium × latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf geranium, is a hybrid plant within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a variety of colors such as pink, red, and white. This species is native to South Africa and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for both indoor and outdoor settings.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in containers, adding aesthetic appeal to patios and balconies.
