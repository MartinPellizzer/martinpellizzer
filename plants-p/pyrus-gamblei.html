Pyrus gamblei

Pyrus gamblei, commonly known as the Gambier pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the Andes mountains in South America, specifically in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its deciduous habit, with lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a small, edible pear that is typically consumed fresh or used in local cuisines.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and unique flavor profile.
