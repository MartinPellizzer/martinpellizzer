Pentasacme pulcherrima

Pentasacme pulcherrima, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, five-petaled flowers that range in color from red to orange, resembling flames. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Its leaves are typically ovate and have a glossy texture.

Pentasacme pulcherrima is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
