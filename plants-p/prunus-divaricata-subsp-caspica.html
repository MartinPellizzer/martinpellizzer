Prunus divaricata subsp. caspica

Prunus divaricata subsp.

caspica, commonly known as the Caspian cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus and parts of Central Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with a rough texture and produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible but not commonly cultivated.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
