Pachycereus hollianus f. cristatus

Pachycereus hollianus f. cristatus, commonly known as the Cristate Holly Cactus, is a unique variant of the Holly Cactus characterized by its distinctive crown-like growth pattern.

This plant is native to Mexico and is part of the Cactaceae family, known for its succulent stems that store water. The f. cristatus form exhibits a flattened, fan-shaped structure due to a genetic mutation called "cristation," which causes the stems to grow outward in a rosette pattern. It produces white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding ornamental value to its appearance.

Due to its slow growth and specific care requirements, it is a popular choice for collectors and cactus enthusiasts.
