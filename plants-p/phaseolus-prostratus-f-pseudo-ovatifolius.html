Phaseolus prostratus f. pseudo-ovatifolius

Phaseolus prostratus f. pseudo-ovatifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf runner bean, is a botanical variant characterized by its elongated, ovate leaves.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas, typically growing in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces edible pods that contain seeds, often used in culinary applications. Its foliage is notable for its distinctive shape, which differs from the standard form of Phaseolus prostratus.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and potential in both ornamental and agricultural settings.
