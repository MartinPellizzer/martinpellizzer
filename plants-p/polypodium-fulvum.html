Polypodium fulvum

Polypodium fulvum, commonly known as the golden polypody, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually bright green, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Polypodium fulvum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
