Potentilla subsalsa

Potentilla subsalsa, commonly known as the saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. The plant thrives in saline soils, making it a valuable species for reclaiming degraded lands. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Potentilla subsalsa is also used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
