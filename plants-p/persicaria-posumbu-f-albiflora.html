Persicaria posumbu f. albiflora

Persicaria posumbu f. albiflora, commonly known as white-flowered smartweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late summer to early autumn. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
