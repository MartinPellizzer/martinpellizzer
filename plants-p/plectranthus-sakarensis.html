Plectranthus sakarensis

Plectranthus sakarensis, commonly known as the Malawi plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. This species is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers a warm, sunny climate.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, mint-like fragrance.
