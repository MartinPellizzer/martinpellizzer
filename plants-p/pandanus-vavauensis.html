Pandanus vavauensis

Pandanus vavauensis, commonly known as the Vavau pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Pandanaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear fragrant, yellowish flowers. Its roots and leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for their aromatic properties.

Pandanus vavauensis thrives in coastal and lowland areas with high humidity and well-drained soil.
