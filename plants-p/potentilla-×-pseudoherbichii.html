Potentilla × pseudoherbichii

Potentilla × pseudoherbichii, commonly known as the hybrid cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, yellow or orange flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant typically features finely toothed, ovate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens across temperate regions.
