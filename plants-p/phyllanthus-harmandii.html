Phyllanthus harmandii

Phyllanthus harmandii, commonly known as the African buttonwood, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits resembling small buttons. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or used in traditional medicine.
