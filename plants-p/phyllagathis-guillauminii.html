Phyllagathis guillauminii

Phyllagathis guillauminii, commonly known as the Guillaumin's arrowleaf, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its narrow, arrow-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing minute seeds.

This species is often found in lowland forest understories, where it thrives in humid and shaded environments.
