Psilobium capillare

Psilobium capillare, commonly known as the slender psilobium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often dispersed by wind or water.
