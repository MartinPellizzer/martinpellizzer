Pollia condensata

Pollia condensata, commonly known as the velvet tunicate, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often eaten by various animals. Pollia condensata is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
