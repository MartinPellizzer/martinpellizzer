Penstemon procerus subsp. brachyanthus

Penstemon procerus subsp.

brachyanthus, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, such as meadows and slopes. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine, fuzzy coating.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
