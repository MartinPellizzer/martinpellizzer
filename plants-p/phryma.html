Phryma

Phryma, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the family Phrymaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name.

Phryma is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
