Portulaca johnstonii

Portulaca johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's moss rose, is a species of flowering plant in the family Portulacaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, vibrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, yellow, or orange. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The plant's ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in harsh, dry environments.
