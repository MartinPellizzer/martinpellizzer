Phyllobolus

Phyllobolus, commonly known as the leaf beetle, is a genus of insects belonging to the family Chrysomelidae.

These beetles are characterized by their flattened, leaf-like bodies that provide excellent camouflage among foliage. They are primarily herbivorous, feeding on a variety of plants, including grasses and shrubs. The larvae of Phyllobolus species are often found in soil or on plant roots, where they feed on organic matter.

Due to their role in plant decomposition and their impact on agricultural crops, they are both ecologically significant and sometimes considered pests.
