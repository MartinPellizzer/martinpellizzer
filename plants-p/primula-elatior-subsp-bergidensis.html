Primula elatior subsp. bergidensis

Primula elatior subsp.

bergidensis, commonly known as the Bergidensis primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a hairy surface, and the plant has a compact, low-growing habit.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
