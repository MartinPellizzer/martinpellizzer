Polypodiodes fieldingiana

Polypodiodes fieldingiana, commonly known as the Fielding's polypodiodes, is a fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are often adorned with small, spore-producing structures called sori. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizome is creeping and often covered with a thin layer of soil, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
