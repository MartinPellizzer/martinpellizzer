Pourouma tessmannii

Pourouma tessmannii, commonly known as the Amazonian bamboo palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to the rainforests of South America.

It is a tall, slender palm with a smooth, dark green trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, coconut-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
