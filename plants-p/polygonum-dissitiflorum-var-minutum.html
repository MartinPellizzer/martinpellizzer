Polygonum dissitiflorum var. minutum

Polygonum dissitiflorum var. minutum, commonly known as dwarf polygonum, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically features small, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its stems are often reddish in color and can spread horizontally, forming dense mats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
