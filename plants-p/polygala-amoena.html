Polygala amoena

Polygala amoena, commonly known as the sweet sedge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage. The plant produces delicate, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, white to pale pink flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it prefers a humid climate.
