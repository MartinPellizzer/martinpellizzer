Parageum calthifolium

Parageum calthifolium, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and produces small, bell-shaped white flowers. Its flowering season occurs in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
