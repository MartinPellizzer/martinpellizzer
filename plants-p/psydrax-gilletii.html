Psydrax gilletii

Psydrax gilletii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers open in the evening and close by dawn, a trait that aids in pollination by reducing competition with diurnal pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique blooming pattern.
