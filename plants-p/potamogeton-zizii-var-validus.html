Potamogeton zizii var. validus

Potamogeton zizii var. validus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and has long, narrow leaves that are often ribbon-like in appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Its roots help stabilize sediments, contributing to water clarity and habitat structure for aquatic life.

It is often found in ponds, lakes, and slow streams, playing a role in maintaining ecological balance in these ecosystems.
