Perrierophytum viscosum

Perrierophytum viscosum, commonly known as the sticky perrierophytum, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, hairy stems and leaves that secrete a sticky substance, which is thought to deter herbivores. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is considered a valuable species for ecological studies in arid environments.
