Pratia grandifolia

Pratia grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered pratia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its flowers are often borne on short pedicels and are arranged in dense, terminal inflorescences.

Pratia grandifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
