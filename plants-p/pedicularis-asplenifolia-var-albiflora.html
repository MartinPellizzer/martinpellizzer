Pedicularis asplenifolia var. albiflora

Pedicularis asplenifolia var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a hairy texture and produces clusters of pale yellow to white flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
