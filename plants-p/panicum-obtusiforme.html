Panicum obtusiforme

Panicum obtusiforme, commonly known as broadleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences that produce small, wind-dispersed seeds. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and can form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization in wetland environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
