Panicum dichotomum var. sphaerocarpon

Panicum dichotomum var. sphaerocarpon, commonly known as the clustered panicgrass, is a grass species characterized by its unique growth habit.

It forms dense tufts with branches that split into two, giving it a distinctive, bushy appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats. Its inflorescence consists of compact, spherical clusters of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
