Piper cinereonervosum

Piper cinereonervosum, commonly known as the ash-veined pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its finely serrated leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that ripens to a dark purple color.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
