Phyllitis rotundifolia

Phyllitis rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leafed fern, is a small, delicate fern native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a short, creeping rhizome with clusters of lance-shaped, serrated leaves. Its fronds are usually less than 10 centimeters in length, with a distinctive rounded leaf shape.

Phyllitis rotundifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded garden settings.
