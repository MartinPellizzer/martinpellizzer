Parestia caudata

Parestia caudata, commonly known as the hairy false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features palmately divided leaves with hairy surfaces, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically violet to purple, arranged in racemes, and are attractive to pollinators.

Parestia caudata is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
