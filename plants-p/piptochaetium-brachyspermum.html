Piptochaetium brachyspermum

Piptochaetium brachyspermum, commonly known as the slender spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland habitats. Its small, pale blue to purple flowers are arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Piptochaetium brachyspermum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
