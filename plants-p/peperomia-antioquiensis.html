Peperomia antioquiensis

Peperomia antioquiensis, commonly known as the Antioquian peperomia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia. This plant is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. The leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

It is a popular houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements and attractive foliage.
