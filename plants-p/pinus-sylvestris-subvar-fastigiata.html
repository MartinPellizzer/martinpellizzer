Pinus sylvestris subvar. fastigiata

Pinus sylvestris subvar. fastigiata, commonly known as the Irish pine, is a compact, coniferous evergreen with a columnar growth habit.

It features needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in fascicles of five, giving it a dense, upright appearance. The cones are typically reddish-brown and contain edible seeds, which are a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and reforestation projects.
