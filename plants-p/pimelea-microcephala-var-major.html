Pimelea microcephala var. major

Pimelea microcephala var. major, commonly known as the narrow-leaved geebung, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils, often in heathlands and open woodlands. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which appear during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a dense foliage that helps it thrive in harsh environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
