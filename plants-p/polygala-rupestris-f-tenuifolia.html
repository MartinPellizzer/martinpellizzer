Polygala rupestris f. tenuifolia

Polygala rupestris f. tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and rocky regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its unique morphology and ability to thrive in harsh environments make it a notable species in its ecological niche.

The subspecies tenuifolia is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical form of Polygala rupestris.
