Philadelphus columbiensis

Philadelphus columbiensis, commonly known as the western mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in horticulture for its pleasant fragrance.
