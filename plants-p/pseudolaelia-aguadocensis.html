Pseudolaelia aguadocensis

Pseudolaelia aguadocensis, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in cool, humid environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
