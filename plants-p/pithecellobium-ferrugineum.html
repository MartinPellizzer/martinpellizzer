Pithecellobium ferrugineum

Pithecellobium ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty pithecellobium, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to warm, humid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agroforestry systems.
