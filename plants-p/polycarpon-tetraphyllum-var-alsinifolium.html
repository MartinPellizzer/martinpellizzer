Polycarpon tetraphyllum var. alsinifolium

Polycarpon tetraphyllum var. alsinifolium, commonly known as the four-leafed chickweed, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, alternate leaves that are typically oval or lance-shaped with a hairy surface. The flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It produces small, dry fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This plant thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and gardens.
