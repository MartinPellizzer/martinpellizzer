Pollia siamensis

Pollia siamensis, commonly known as the Siam velvet tunicate, is a unique plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful, and fleshy leaves that resemble the appearance of a tunic. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant hues and unusual texture. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires well-drained soil.

Despite its beauty, it is relatively rare in the wild and is primarily grown in specialized horticultural settings.
