Phalaris paradoxa var. megastachya

Phalaris paradoxa var. megastachya, commonly known as the giant false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy panicles that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
