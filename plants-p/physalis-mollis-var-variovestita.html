Physalis mollis var. variovestita

Physalis mollis var. variovestita, commonly known as the golden berry or Cape gooseberry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into the distinctive lantern-shaped fruits. The fruits are encased in a papery calyx that turns red or orange as they ripen.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, which have a sweet-tart flavor.
