Pinanga bicolana

Pinanga bicolana, commonly known as the two-colored bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines and Indonesia. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnately compound leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are often a mix of green and silver hues, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
