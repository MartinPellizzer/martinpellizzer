Phyllanthus ruscifolius

Phyllanthus ruscifolius, commonly known as the Indian spurge or leafy spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds. The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
