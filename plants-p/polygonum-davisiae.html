Polygonum davisiae

Polygonum davisiae, commonly known as Davis' knotweed, is a flowering plant species native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants commonly referred to as smartweeds or knotweeds. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as streambanks and wet meadows. It features basal leaves that are arrowhead-shaped and small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies in riparian zones.
