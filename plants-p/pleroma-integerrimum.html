Pleroma integerrimum

Pleroma integerrimum, commonly known as the hardy geranium, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing shades of pink, red, and white. Its leaves are heart-shaped and arranged alternately along the stems, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Pleroma integerrimum is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
