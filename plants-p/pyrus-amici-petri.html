Pyrus amici-petri

Pyrus amici-petri, commonly known as the Friendship and Petri pear, is a rare and lesser-known species of the pear genus.

It is characterized by its small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or green in color. The plant is native to specific regions in Central Asia and exhibits a preference for well-drained, loamy soils. It produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its distinctive fruit.

Due to its limited distribution and unique characteristics, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
