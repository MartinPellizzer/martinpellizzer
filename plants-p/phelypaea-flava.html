Phelypaea flava

Phelypaea flava, commonly known as the yellow water clover, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It features small, floating leaves that are typically light green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive clover-like shape. The plant produces tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in shallow, still waters and is often found in marshes and pond edges.

Phelypaea flava plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
