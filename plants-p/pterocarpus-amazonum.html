Pterocarpus amazonum

Pterocarpus amazonum, commonly known as the Amazonian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and aesthetic qualities. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing flat, seed-like structures. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval leaflets.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
