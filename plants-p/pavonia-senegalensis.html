Pavonia senegalensis

Pavonia senegalensis, commonly known as the Senegal hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the warm seasons. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various tropical and subtropical environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
