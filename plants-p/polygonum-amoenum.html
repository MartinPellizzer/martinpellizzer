Polygonum amoenum

Polygonum amoenum, commonly known as the Japanese morning glory, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Polygonum amoenum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
