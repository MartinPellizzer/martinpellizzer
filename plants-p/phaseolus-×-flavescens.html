Phaseolus × flavescens

Phaseolus × flavescens, commonly known as the yellow bean, is a hybrid species within the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its yellow pods and seeds, which are typically smaller than those of common beans. The plant is native to Central and South America and thrives in warm, tropical climates. It is valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of protein and essential minerals.

Due to its unique coloration and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental or for specialty food products.
