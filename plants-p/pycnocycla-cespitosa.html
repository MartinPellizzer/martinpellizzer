Pycnocycla cespitosa

Pycnocycla cespitosa, commonly known as the tufted panic grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant has tufted, grass-like foliage with dense, compact growth habits. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are clustered in dense, cylindrical clusters.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
