Polygonum carneum

Polygonum carneum, commonly known as the fleshy knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other species like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its small, greenish flowers appear in clusters and are typically pollinated by insects.

Polygonum carneum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks.
