Phlox bridgesii

Phlox bridgesii, commonly known as Bridges' phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and produces showy clusters of pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy.

Phlox bridgesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
