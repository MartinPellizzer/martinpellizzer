Passiflora lonchophora

Passiflora lonchophora, commonly known as the long-fruited passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that feature a unique blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its stems are covered in fine, hair-like structures.

Passiflora lonchophora is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its potential medicinal properties.
