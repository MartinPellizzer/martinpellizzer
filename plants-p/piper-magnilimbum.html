Piper magnilimbum

Piper magnilimbum, commonly known as the large-leaved pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its potential medicinal properties.

The plant prefers moist, well-drained soil and thrives in partial to full shade.
