Physalis cinerascens var. cinerascens

Physalis cinerascens var. cinerascens, commonly known as the silverleaf gooseberry, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries enclosed in a papery, inflated calyx. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The berries are edible when ripe but are generally tart and used sparingly in culinary applications.
