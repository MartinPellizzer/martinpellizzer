Panicum alopecuroideum

Panicum alopecuroideum, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal and erosion control capabilities.

Due to its preference for wet conditions, it is often used in restoration projects in riparian zones.
