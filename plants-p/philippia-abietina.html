Philippia abietina

Philippia abietina, commonly known as the silver fir.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of South America. The tree has needle-like leaves that are dark green and covered in a silvery-white bloom, giving it its common name. It produces cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
