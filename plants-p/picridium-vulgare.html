Picridium vulgare

Picridium vulgare, commonly known as the common picrid, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to various regions in Europe and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Picridium vulgare is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
