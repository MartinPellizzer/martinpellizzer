Phyteuma tenerum subvar. brevifolium

Phyteuma tenerum subvar. brevifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved evening-primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the late summer and early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
