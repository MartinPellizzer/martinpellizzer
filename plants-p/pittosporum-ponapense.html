Pittosporum ponapense

Pittosporum ponapense, commonly known as the Ponape Pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which is dark green and glossy, and its fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth margin and emit a mild, pleasant scent when crushed.

The fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red as it matures.
