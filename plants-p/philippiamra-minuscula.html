Philippiamra minuscula

Philippiamra minuscula, commonly known as the dwarf philippia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters, featuring dense, glossy green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, drought-tolerant plant.
