Pandanus boemiensis

Pandanus boemiensis, commonly known as the Boeheim's pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional crafts and construction.

The plant thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often growing near the shoreline.
