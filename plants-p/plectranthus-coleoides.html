Plectranthus coleoides

Plectranthus coleoides, commonly known as African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and long blooming period.

Plectranthus coleoides prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
