Plectranthus buckia

Plectranthus buckia, commonly known as the silver bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple and pink. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

Plectranthus buckia is drought-tolerant and prefers full sun to partial shade.
