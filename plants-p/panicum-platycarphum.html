Panicum platycarphum

Panicum platycarphum, commonly known as broad-leaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to thrive in disturbed soils. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and for soil stabilization.
