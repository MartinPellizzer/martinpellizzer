Peplis erecta

Peplis erecta, commonly known as the upright peplis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, covered with fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

Peplis erecta is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
