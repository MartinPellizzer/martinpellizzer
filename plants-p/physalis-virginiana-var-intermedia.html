Physalis virginiana var. intermedia

Physalis virginiana var. intermedia, commonly known as the Indian lantern or winter cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflated calyx that encloses the berry, which turns red when mature. The plant is native to eastern North America and thrives in moist, woodlands or open fields. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

It produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by the characteristic lantern-shaped fruits.
