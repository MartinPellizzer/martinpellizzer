Populus pseudoglauca var. weixii

Populus pseudoglauca var. weixii, commonly known as the Weixi poplar, is a variant of the poplar tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-green leaves that turn yellow in autumn, giving it a striking appearance. The tree typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It has a fast growth rate and is often used in urban landscaping for its ornamental value.

The bark is smooth and grayish, with a slight tendency to peel as the tree matures.
