Phyteuma spicatum f. fissum

Phyteuma spicatum f. fissum, commonly known as the fissured phyteuma, is a distinctive variant of the phyteuma species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated, spiky flower heads that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight sheen, and it prefers well-drained soils.

This ornamental plant is valued in gardens for its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
