Pilosella bulgarica

Pilosella bulgarica, commonly known as the Bulgarian hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are hairy and often covered in fine white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Pilosella bulgarica is typically found in dry meadows and rocky soils, where it thrives in sunny conditions.
