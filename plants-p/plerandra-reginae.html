Plerandra reginae

Plerandra reginae, commonly known as the Queen's arum, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a spathe that can vary in color depending on the variety.

Plerandra reginae thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its exotic appearance and ecological value.
