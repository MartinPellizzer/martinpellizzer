Peraxilla decussata

Peraxilla decussata, commonly known as the New Zealand broom, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its dense, branching structure. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are opposite, narrow, and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Peraxilla decussata is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
