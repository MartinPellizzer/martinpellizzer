Physaria kingii subsp. kaibabensis

Physaria kingii subsp.

kaibabensis, commonly known as Kaibab bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces distinctive seed pods that are inflated and ribbed, typical of the Physaria genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

This subspecies is adapted to withstand drought and is an important component of its native ecosystem.
