Pleiospilos compactus subsp. sororius

Pleiospilos compactus subsp.

sororius, commonly known as the compact spider flower, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiky foliage that resembles a cluster of small, green spines.

The plant produces vibrant yellow-orange flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
