Potentilla × grandiloba

Potentilla × grandiloba, commonly known as the hybrid shrub, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, with leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of yellow or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy and adaptable choice for gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
