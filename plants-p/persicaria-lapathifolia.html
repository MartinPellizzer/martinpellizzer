Persicaria lapathifolia

Persicaria lapathifolia, commonly known as slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that are often reddish in color and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with toothed margins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in spikes during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it a useful plant for erosion control.
