Potentilla chrysocraspeda var. elata

Potentilla chrysocraspeda var. elata, commonly known as the tall golden potentilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are densely arranged along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and compact form.
