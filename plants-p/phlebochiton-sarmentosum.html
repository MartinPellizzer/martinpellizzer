Phlebochiton sarmentosum

Phlebochiton sarmentosum, commonly known as the climbing tamarind, is a woody vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, flexible stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing flat, seed-like structures. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.
