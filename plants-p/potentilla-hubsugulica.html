Potentilla hubsugulica

Potentilla hubsugulica, commonly known as the Hubsugulica cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, with five leaflets, and are covered in a fine layer of hair.

Potentilla hubsugulica is adapted to harsh, alpine environments and is often found in rocky soils at high elevations.
