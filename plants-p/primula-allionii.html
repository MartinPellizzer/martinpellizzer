Primula allionii

Primula allionii, commonly known as the Allion's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
