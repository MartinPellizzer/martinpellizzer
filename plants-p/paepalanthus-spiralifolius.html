Paepalanthus spiralifolius

Paepalanthus spiralifolius, commonly known as the spiral-leaved pincushion cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny clusters of stems that form a cushion-like structure. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate contrast to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss in its harsh environment.

Paepalanthus spiralifolius is well adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
