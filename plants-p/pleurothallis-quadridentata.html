Pleurothallis quadridentata

Pleurothallis quadridentata, commonly known as the four-toothed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and rarity, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
