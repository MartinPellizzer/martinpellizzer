Phacelia orbicularis

Phacelia orbicularis, commonly known as the roundleaf phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its orbicular, or nearly round, leaves that are often hairy and have a bluish-green color. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer. It is typically found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Phacelia orbicularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
