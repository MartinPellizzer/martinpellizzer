Prunus × yedoensis var. rubriflora

Prunus × yedoensis var. rubriflora, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom with red flowers, is a variety of Prunus species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking red blooms that appear in early spring, contrasting with the typical pink or white flowers of other cherry blossom varieties. The plant is a deciduous tree with smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

This ornamental tree is highly valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often planted in gardens and public spaces.
