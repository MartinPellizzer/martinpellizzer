Phoradendron puberulum var. chihuahuense

Phoradendron puberulum var. chihuahuense, commonly known as the Chihuahua mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees, primarily in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, short hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and contributing to the nutrient cycle through its parasitic relationship with host trees.

Its presence is often an indicator of healthy, mature forest ecosystems.
