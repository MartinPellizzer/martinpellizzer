Paullinia cupana f. typica

Paullinia cupana f. typica, commonly known as guarana, is a climbing plant native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright red berries containing hard, stimulant-rich seeds. These seeds are the primary source of caffeine and other alkaloids, making guarana a popular ingredient in energy drinks and supplements.

Its botanical features contribute to its adaptability in tropical environments and its use in traditional medicine.
