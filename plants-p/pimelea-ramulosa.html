Pimelea ramulosa

Pimelea ramulosa, commonly known as the slender bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from yellow to orange, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged, drought-resistant nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and dry forests.

Pimelea ramulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
