Pinus mugo var. uncinata

Pinus mugo var. uncinata, commonly known as the hooked mountain pine, is a variant of the dwarf mountain pine native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive hooked or curved needles, which give it a unique appearance. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, cold climates. Its cones are small and often remain attached to the branches for a long time, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
