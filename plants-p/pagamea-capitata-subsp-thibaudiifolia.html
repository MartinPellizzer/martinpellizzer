Pagamea capitata subsp. thibaudiifolia

Pagamea capitata subsp.

thibaudiifolia, commonly known as the African daisy, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking, daisy-like flower heads composed of yellow disk flowers surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets.

The plant typically grows as a perennial herb with upright, branching stems and narrow, linear leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden use in warm climates.
