Pyrus robiniana

Pyrus robiniana, commonly known as the American whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by edible, yellowish fruits that are similar to apples. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Pyrus robiniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
