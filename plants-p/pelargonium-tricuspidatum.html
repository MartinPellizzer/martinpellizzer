Pelargonium tricuspidatum

Pelargonium tricuspidatum, commonly known as the three-lobed geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves and clusters of showy pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil. It is a perennial herb that prefers full sun and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
