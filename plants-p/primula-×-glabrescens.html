Primula × glabrescens

Primula × glabrescens, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and features heart-shaped, hairy leaves. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its hybrid origin results from crosses between different Primula species, contributing to its genetic diversity and adaptability.
