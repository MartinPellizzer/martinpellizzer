Papaver smirnovii

Papaver smirnovii, commonly known as the Smirnov's poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its capsule fruit contains numerous small, smooth seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Papaver smirnovii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
