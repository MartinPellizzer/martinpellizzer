Piper havilandii

Piper havilandii, commonly known as the Haviland's pepper plant, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes many species of flowering plants in the family Piperaceae. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that eventually develop into peppery, edible berries. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a peppery aroma when crushed.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its unique flavor in culinary applications.
