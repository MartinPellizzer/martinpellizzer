Pyrus vinetorum

Pyrus vinetorum, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers and smooth, gray bark. The leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall. It produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

This species is often found in woodlands and is valued for its ecological role in supporting wildlife.
