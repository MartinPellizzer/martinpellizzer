Pappophorum nigricans var. glabrescens

Pappophorum nigricans var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth bristlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, bristly seeds, which are adapted for wind dispersal. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
