Pothos mirabilis

Pothos mirabilis, commonly known as the Devil's Ivy, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a trailing vine that produces heart-shaped leaves with a glossy texture. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its rapid growth rate. Pothos mirabilis is also known for its air-purifying qualities, making it a popular houseplant.

Its flowers, though rare, are typically small and greenish-white in color.
