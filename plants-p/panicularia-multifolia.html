Panicularia multifolia

Panicularia multifolia, commonly known as the many-leaved panicled grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically greenish or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

Panicularia multifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
