Potentilla breweri

Potentilla breweri, commonly known as Brewer's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and canyon walls. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and drought tolerance.
