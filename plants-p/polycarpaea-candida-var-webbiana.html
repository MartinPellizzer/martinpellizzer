Polycarpaea candida var. webbiana

Polycarpaea candida var. webbiana, commonly known as the white-flowered polycarpaea, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
