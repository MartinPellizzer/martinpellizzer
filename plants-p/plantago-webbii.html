Plantago webbii

Plantago webbii, commonly known as the Webb's plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
