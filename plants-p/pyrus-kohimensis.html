Pyrus kohimensis

Pyrus kohimensis, commonly known as the Khamti apple, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pyrus.

It is native to the eastern Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Myanmar. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, with simple, alternate leaves that are often serrated. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, and it produces small, edible apples that are prized for their flavor.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude environments.
