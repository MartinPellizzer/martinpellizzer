Protea callosa

Protea callosa, commonly known as the woolly protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to cream-colored floral bracts. The plant features a robust, woody stem with a dense covering of woolly, silver-gray hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Native to the Western Cape region, it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
