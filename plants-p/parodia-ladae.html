Parodia ladae

Parodia ladae, commonly known as the Lada's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that often appear segmented, giving it a unique, segmented appearance. The plant produces small, white or yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in arid environments.
