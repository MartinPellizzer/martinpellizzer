Pereskia marcanoi

Pereskia marcanoi, commonly known as the Cuban cactus or the prickly pear cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and are often mistaken for leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by edible fruits known as tunas. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sunlight.

Pereskia marcanoi is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
