Piper penangense

Piper penangense, commonly known as Penang pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, round berries that turn red when mature.

This species is often grown for its ornamental value and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
