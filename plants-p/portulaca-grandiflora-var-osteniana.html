Portulaca grandiflora var. osteniana

Portulaca grandiflora var. osteniana, commonly known as the Mexican flameflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom throughout the summer. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a favored plant in both gardens and landscapes.
