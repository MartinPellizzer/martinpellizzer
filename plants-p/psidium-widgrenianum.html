Psidium widgrenianum

Psidium widgrenianum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, warm climates. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tart flavor. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, while its flowers are white and fragrant.

The plant is often cultivated for both ornamental purposes and for its nutritious fruit.
