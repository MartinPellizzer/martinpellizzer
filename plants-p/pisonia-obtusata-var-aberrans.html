Pisonia obtusata var. aberrans

Pisonia obtusata var. aberrans, commonly known as the red-veined pisonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Indo-Pacific.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color, with prominent veining. The plant has compound leaves that are opposite and often have a rough texture. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or limestone soils.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
