Polygonum savatieri

Polygonum savatieri, commonly known as the red root or velvetleaf, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as buckwheats. The plant is characterized by its upright stems, heart-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, red or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Polygonum savatieri is also noted for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential weed in certain environments.
