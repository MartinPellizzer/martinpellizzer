Piptochaetium uruguense var. microcarpum

Piptochaetium uruguense var. microcarpum, commonly known as the Uruguay grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences with narrow, awn-tipped lemma blades. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.
