Pogonarthria falcata

Pogonarthria falcata, commonly known as the sickleleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, sickle-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and grasslands.

Pogonarthria falcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in erosion control due to its dense growth habit.
