Piper araneatum

Piper araneatum, commonly known as the "vine of the spider," is a climbing plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its finely toothed leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in catkins. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that contains the seeds.

Piper araneatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
