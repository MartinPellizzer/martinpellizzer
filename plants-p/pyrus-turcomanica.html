Pyrus turcomanica

Pyrus turcomanica, commonly known as the Turkestan pear, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad ovate leaves and showy white flowers that appear in spring. The fruit is a small, sweet pear with a thin skin and is often used in local cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
