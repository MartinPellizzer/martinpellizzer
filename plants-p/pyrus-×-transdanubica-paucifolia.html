Pyrus × transdanubica paucifolia

Pyrus × transdanubica paucifolia, commonly known as the Transdanubian pear, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are similar to common pears but smaller and more acidic. It is native to the Danubian Basin and is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit production.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations in its natural habitat.
