Pelargonium × potteri

Pelargonium × potteri, commonly known as the Zonal Geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its rounded, velvety leaves with distinct color zones, typically in shades of green, silver, and red. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, which bloom in clusters atop slender stems. Native to South Africa, it thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and container planting.

Its compact growth habit and long blooming season contribute to its widespread cultivation as an ornamental plant.
