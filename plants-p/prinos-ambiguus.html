Prinos ambiguus

Prinos ambiguus, commonly known as the ambiguous prion.

It is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Prinos ambiguus is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
