Potentilla pensylvanica var. arabica

Potentilla pensylvanica var. arabica, commonly known as the Pennsylvania cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided into five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
