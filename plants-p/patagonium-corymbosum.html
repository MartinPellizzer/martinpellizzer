Patagonium corymbosum

Patagonium corymbosum, commonly known as the Patagonian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine areas. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, edible berries that are utilized by local wildlife.

Patagonium corymbosum plays an important role in its ecosystem, supporting pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
