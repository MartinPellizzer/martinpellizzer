Paspalum blodgettii

Paspalum blodgettii, commonly known as Blodgett's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and can tolerate high temperatures and low moisture levels.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes used in restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
