Phelline confertifolia

Phelline confertifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved phelline, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, narrow leaves that are often arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

Phelline confertifolia is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
