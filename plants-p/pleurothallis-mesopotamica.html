Pleurothallis mesopotamica

Pleurothallis mesopotamica, commonly known as the Mesopotamian orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly found in the Amazon Basin.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, tropical environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in high light conditions but requires consistent moisture and good air circulation.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or specialized orchid setups.
