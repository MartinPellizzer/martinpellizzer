Pandanus galeatus

Pandanus galeatus, commonly known as the silver pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a silvery or white powdery coating. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its roots are fibrous and help it thrive in coastal and sandy soils.

The leaves are used in traditional crafts and for thatching, highlighting its cultural and practical significance.
