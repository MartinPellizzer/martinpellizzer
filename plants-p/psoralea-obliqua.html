Psoralea obliqua

Psoralea obliqua, commonly known as the zigzag psoralea, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, where it typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features narrow, zigzagging stems and small, clustered leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces small, flat pods that contain several seeds.

Psoralea obliqua is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects.
