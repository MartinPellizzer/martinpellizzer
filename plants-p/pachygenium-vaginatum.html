Pachygenium vaginatum

Pachygenium vaginatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed habitats. Pachygenium vaginatum is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, though it can also be invasive in some regions.
