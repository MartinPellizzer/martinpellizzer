Philibertia candolleana

Philibertia candolleana, commonly known as the Candolleana filbert, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, adapting well to high-altitude environments.

Philibertia candolleana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
