Piptatherum thomasii

Piptatherum thomasii, commonly known as Thomas' grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and prairies. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
