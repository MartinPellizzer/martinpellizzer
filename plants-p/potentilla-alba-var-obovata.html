Potentilla alba var. obovata

Potentilla alba var. obovata, commonly known as white cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves composed of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, which are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
