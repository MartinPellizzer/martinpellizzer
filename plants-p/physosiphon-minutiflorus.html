Physosiphon minutiflorus

Physosiphon minutiflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered physosiphon, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the moist, shaded environments it inhabits.

Physosiphon minutiflorus is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
