Pseudolycopodium

Pseudolycopodium, commonly known as the false clubmoss, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, spore-producing structures called strobili, which are typically clustered at the tips of its branching stems. The plant produces spores rather than seeds, and its reproductive structures are often mistaken for those of true clubmosses. Pseudolycopodium species are typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

These plants play a role in soil stabilization and provide habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
