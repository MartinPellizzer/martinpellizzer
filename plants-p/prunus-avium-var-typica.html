Prunus avium var. typica

Prunus avium var. typica, commonly known as the European sweet cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southwestern Asia.

It produces small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and serrated along the edges. The plant flowers with white or pinkish blossoms in early spring, followed by the development of sweet, fleshy fruits. The fruits are usually red to dark red when ripe and are highly valued for their flavor and culinary use.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for orchard cultivation.
