Phyllanthus riedelianus

Phyllanthus riedelianus, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, yellow fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

It is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
