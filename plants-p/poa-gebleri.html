Poa gebleri

Poa gebleri, commonly known as Gebler's meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

Poa gebleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
