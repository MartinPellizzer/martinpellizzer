Psidium raimondii

Psidium raimondii, commonly known as the Raimondi guava, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to southern Mexico and Central America, where it typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, followed by edible berries similar to guavas. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a glossy appearance.

The Raimondi guava is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
