Premna quadrifolia var. warneckeana

Premna quadrifolia var. warneckeana, commonly known as the four-leafed creeping premna, is a variant of the Premna quadrifolia species native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a trailing or climbing shrub characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, spherical berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in botanical gardens and ecological studies.
