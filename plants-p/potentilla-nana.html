Potentilla nana

Potentilla nana, commonly known as dwarf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It typically reaches heights of less than 10 centimeters and features small, rounded leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
