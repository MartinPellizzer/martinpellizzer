Physalis decumbens

Physalis decumbens, commonly known as the ground cherry, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits enclosed in a papery calyx, which turn red when mature. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, and it thrives in warm, sunny conditions.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruits and is also used in traditional medicine.
