Pilocarpus trachylophus

Pilocarpus trachylophus, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant has simple, opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by red, fleshy berries.

The plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its stimulant and antiseptic effects.
