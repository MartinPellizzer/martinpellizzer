Pimpinella arussorum

Pimpinella arussorum, commonly known as the Italian cow-wheat, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in umbels. Native to southern Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, calcareous soils. The plant is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
