Pultenaea purpurea

Pultenaea purpurea, commonly known as the purple wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple, blooming in spring. The plant has compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and inland environments.

Pultenaea purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
