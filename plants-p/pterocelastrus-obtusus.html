Pterocelastrus obtusus

Pterocelastrus obtusus, commonly known as the bluntleaf Chinese mahogany, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in traditional crafts and furniture.
