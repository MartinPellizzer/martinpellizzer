Pelargonium × aurareum

Pelargonium × aurareum, commonly known as the golden geranium, is a hybrid plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers and succulent, hairy leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers. Its foliage is often tinged with red or purple, adding visual interest to gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
