Potentilla canescens subvar. polytricha

Potentilla canescens subvar. polytricha, commonly known as the silverleaf potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are typically woody and can persist for several years, contributing to its hardy nature.

This variety is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, making it a resilient plant in challenging ecological conditions.
