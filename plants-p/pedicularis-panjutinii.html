Pedicularis panjutinii

Pedicularis panjutinii, commonly known as the Tibetan lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Pedicularis, which includes over 300 species of flowering plants, many of which are found in alpine and subarctic regions. This particular species is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, reddish-purple flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

The plant is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism, which involves insect mimicry to attract pollinators.
