Polygonum cuspidatum var. compactum

Polygonum cuspidatum var. compactum, commonly known as Japanese knotweed, is a vigorous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are usually green with a reddish or purple tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. Its rhizomatous growth system allows it to spread rapidly, making it an invasive species in many regions.

Botanically, it is characterized by its robust stems, dense foliage, and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
