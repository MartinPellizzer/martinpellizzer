Pilosocereus tweedyanus

Pilosocereus tweedyanus, commonly known as the Honeycomb Cactus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, white, woolly spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and minimal water.

Pilosocereus tweedyanus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique morphology and resilience.
