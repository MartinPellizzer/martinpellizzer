Phaius klabatensis

Phaius klabatensis, commonly known as the Klabat orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and green. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Phaius klabatensis is often cultivated for its striking floral display and is valued in horticulture for its adaptability to various growing conditions.
