Piper cumanense

Piper cumanense, commonly known as the Cumané pepper or Cumané vine, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

Piper cumanense is valued for its aromatic properties and has traditional uses in local medicine.
