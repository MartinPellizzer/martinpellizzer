Pentalepis ecliptoides

Pentalepis ecliptoides, commonly known as the slender eclipta, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or inland regions. It has a creeping growth habit and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
