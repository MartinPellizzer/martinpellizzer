Pellaea quinquelobata

Pellaea quinquelobata, commonly known as the five-lobed maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Pellaeaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and often covered in a layer of brown, velvety tissue.

The fronds are bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive, almost lace-like appearance.
