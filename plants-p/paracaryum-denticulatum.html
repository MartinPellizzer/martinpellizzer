Paracaryum denticulatum

Paracaryum denticulatum, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers, which are typically arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often grown as a ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Paracaryum denticulatum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
