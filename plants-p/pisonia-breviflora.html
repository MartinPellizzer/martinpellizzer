Pisonia breviflora

Pisonia breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered pisonia, is a tropical flowering plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn red when mature, containing several seeds. Pisonia breviflora is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to grow in sandy or rocky soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in tropical and subtropical climates.
