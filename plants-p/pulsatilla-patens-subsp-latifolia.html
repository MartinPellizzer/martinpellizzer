Pulsatilla patens subsp. latifolia

Pulsatilla patens subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its early spring blooming habit, with showy, showy yellow or greenish-yellow flowers surrounded by prominent, silky white bracts.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The subspecies latifolia is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to other subspecies of Pulsatilla patens.
