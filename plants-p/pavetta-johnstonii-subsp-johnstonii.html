Pavetta johnstonii subsp. johnstonii

Pavetta johnstonii subsp.

johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's pavetta, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and are usually white or pale pink in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
