Pteris almeidiana

Pteris almeidiana, commonly known as the Almeida's water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce numerous sporangia, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

The botanical features of Pteris almeidiana make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
