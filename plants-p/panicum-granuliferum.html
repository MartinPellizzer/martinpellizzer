Panicum granuliferum

Panicum granuliferum, commonly known as the granular panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often covered in fine, granular structures. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is often used in wetland restoration projects.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting wildlife.
