Parodia andreaeoides

Parodia andreaeoides, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and vibrant, colorful flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has a shallow root system adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils. Its foliage consists of fleshy, spiny stems that store water, and it produces small, white, woolly bracts beneath its flowers.

Parodia andreaeoides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
