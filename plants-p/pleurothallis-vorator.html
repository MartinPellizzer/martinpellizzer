Pleurothallis vorator

Pleurothallis vorator, commonly known as the devouring pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and its ability to thrive in high humidity and partial shade. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often arranged in clusters. Its unique name is derived from the mythological association with a creature that "devours" its surroundings, though this is more symbolic than literal.

Botanically, it is part of the Orchidaceae family and exhibits typical orchid features such as a specialized root system and a single, often hairy leaf.
