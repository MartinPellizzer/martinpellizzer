Plantago pentasperma

Plantago pentasperma, commonly known as the five-seeded plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its seeds are enclosed in small, capsule-like structures, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Plantago pentasperma is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
