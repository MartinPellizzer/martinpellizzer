Piper partiticuspe

Piper partiticuspe, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces berries that mature to a dark red or black color, which are the source of the spice known as black pepper. The fruit's chemical composition includes alkaloids such as piperine, which contribute to its pungent flavor and medicinal properties.

Its cultivation is closely tied to the global spice trade and has significant economic importance in many countries.
