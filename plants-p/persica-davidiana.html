Persica davidiana

Persica davidiana, commonly known as the David plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically not edible for humans but are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Persica davidiana is also noted for its ability to adapt to various soil types and its role in supporting local wildlife.
