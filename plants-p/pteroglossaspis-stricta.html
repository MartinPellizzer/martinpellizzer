Pteroglossaspis stricta

Pteroglossaspis stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pteroglossaspis, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in whorls along the stems. These flowers are often brightly colored, with a distinctive two-lipped structure typical of the Acanthaceae family.

Pteroglossaspis stricta is native to tropical regions of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
