Pyrus bucharica subsp. korshinskyi

Pyrus bucharica subsp.

korshinskyi, commonly known as the Korshinsky pear, is a species native to Central Asia. It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and white flowers that bloom in spring.

The fruit is typically small, with a yellowish-green skin and a sweet, aromatic flavor. This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

It is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its edible fruit.
