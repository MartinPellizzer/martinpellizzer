Pilosella flagellaris subsp. glaucochlora

Pilosella flagellaris subsp.

glaucochlora, commonly known as the glaucous pilosella, is a flowering plant species native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a small perennial herb with a creeping growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky or gravelly soils.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically hairy and have a bluish-green hue, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
