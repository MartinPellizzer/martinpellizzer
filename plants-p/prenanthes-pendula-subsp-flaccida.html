Prenanthes pendula subsp. flaccida

Prenanthes pendula subsp.

flaccida, commonly known as the drooping water shield, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America. It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its slender, pendant stems that bear small, arrowhead-shaped leaves.

The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically submerged or floating on the surface of shallow water. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important species for aquatic ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
