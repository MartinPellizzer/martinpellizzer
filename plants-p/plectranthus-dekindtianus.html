Plectranthus dekindtianus

Plectranthus dekindtianus, commonly known as the Cape lavender, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to white. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, giving it a distinct appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
