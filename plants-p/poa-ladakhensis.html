Poa ladakhensis

Poa ladakhensis, commonly known as the Ladakh grass, is a species of flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, particularly in Ladakh.

It is a perennial grass that thrives in cold, arid environments, often found at elevations above 3,000 meters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny blue or purple flowers. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its harsh habitat.

Due to its resilience, it is an important component of the local flora and ecosystem in high-altitude regions.
