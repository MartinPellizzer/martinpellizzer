Prevostea umbellata

Prevostea umbellata, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Prevostea umbellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
