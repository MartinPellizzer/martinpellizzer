Pachyanthus reticulatus

Pachyanthus reticulatus, commonly known as the red torch tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and striking red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its durable timber and has cultural significance in some regions.
