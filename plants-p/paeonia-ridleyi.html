Paeonia ridleyi

Paeonia ridleyi, commonly known as the Ridley peony, is a species of herbaceous perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the genus Paeonia, which includes a variety of ornamental and medicinal plants. The plant features large, showy flowers with five to eight petals, typically in shades of pink, red, or white. Its foliage consists of palmately compound leaves, and it produces yellowish-green follicles as its fruit.

Paeonia ridleyi is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
