Pilosella

Pilosella, commonly known as mouse-ear or goat's beard, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their hairy, leafy stems and small, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or yellow. Pilosella species are often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, thriving in temperate climates. They have a compact growth habit and are sometimes used in horticulture for their ornamental value.

The genus includes around 60 species, many of which are native to Europe, Asia, and North America.
