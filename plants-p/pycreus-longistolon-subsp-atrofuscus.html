Pycreus longistolon subsp. atrofuscus

Pycreus longistolon subsp.

atrofuscus, commonly known as the black-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features slender, creeping stems that often form dense mats, with dark purple to black stems that become woody with age.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lanceolate, hairy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, exhibiting a range of morphological variations depending on environmental conditions.
