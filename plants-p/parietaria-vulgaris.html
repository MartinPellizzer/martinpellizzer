Parietaria vulgaris

Parietaria vulgaris, commonly known as smooth wallworts, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Urticaceae.

It is characterized by its small, inconspicuous flowers and hairy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as rocky crevices and walls. Its leaves are often covered with fine hairs, which can cause skin irritation in some individuals.

Parietaria vulgaris is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
