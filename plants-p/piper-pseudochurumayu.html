Piper pseudochurumayu

Piper pseudochurumayu, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper vine, is a member of the Piperaceae family and is native to the rainforests of South America.

This climbing plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark purple or black color, containing several seeds. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which have a peppery scent reminiscent of other Piper species.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.
