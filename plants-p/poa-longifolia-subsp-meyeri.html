Poa longifolia subsp. meyeri

Poa longifolia subsp.

meyeri, commonly known as the Meyer's bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its delicate appearance.
