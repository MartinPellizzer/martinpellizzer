Paphia meiniana

Paphia meiniana, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow petals and a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The botanical features of Paphia meiniana make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and ease of cultivation.
