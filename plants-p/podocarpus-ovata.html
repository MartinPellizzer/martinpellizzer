Podocarpus ovata

Podocarpus ovata, commonly known as the Japanese umbrella pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan and southern Korea.

It is characterized by its distinctive pyramidal shape and dense, scale-like leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, typical of the Podocarpaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental tree in horticulture.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal contribute to its cultivation in gardens and landscapes worldwide.
