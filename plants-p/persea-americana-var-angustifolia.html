Persea americana var. angustifolia

Persea americana var. angustifolia, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a variant of the persimmon tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and produces small, orange to reddish fruits that are typically harvested when fully ripe. The plant thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, honey-like flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

The tree is also notable for its ornamental value, with its attractive foliage and seasonal fruit display.
