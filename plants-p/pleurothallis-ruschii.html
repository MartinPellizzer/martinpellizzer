Pleurothallis ruschii

Pleurothallis ruschii, commonly known as the Rusch's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is part of the vast Pleurothallis genus, which includes over 1,000 species.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral structure.
