Paspalum buchtienii

Paspalum buchtienii, commonly known as the desert paspalum, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, particularly South Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in poor soil and extreme temperatures.

Paspalum buchtienii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soils in degraded environments.
