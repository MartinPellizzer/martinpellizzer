Piptocarpha costaricensis

Piptocarpha costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has small, linear leaves and produces seeds in a composite head structure.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
