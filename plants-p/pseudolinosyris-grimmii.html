Pseudolinosyris grimmii

Pseudolinosyris grimmii, commonly known as the dwarf lichen, is a small, crustose lichen that thrives in dry, rocky environments.

It is characterized by its tightly adherent, pale yellow to orange thallus that often blends with the surrounding substrate. The lichen lacks distinct lobes and is typically found on soil or stone in arid or semi-arid regions. It is a symbiotic organism composed of a fungal partner and algae or cyanobacteria, which together carry out photosynthesis.

Due to its limited geographic distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within the lichen community.
