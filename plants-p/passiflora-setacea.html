Passiflora setacea

Passiflora setacea, commonly known as the spiny passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, intricate flowers that feature a crown of filaments and a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with spiny margins, contributing to its defensive adaptations.

Passiflora setacea thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
