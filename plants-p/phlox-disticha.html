Phlox disticha

Phlox disticha, commonly known as the doubleleaf phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink, purple, and white, typically in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Phlox disticha is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
