Panicum maximum

Panicum maximum, commonly known as Guinea grass, is a tropical perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and coarse, hairy leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets that bear small, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Guinea grass is widely used for forage and has ecological importance in maintaining soil stability.
