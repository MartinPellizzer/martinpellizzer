Paspalum scoparium var. vestitum

Paspalum scoparium var. vestitum, commonly known as the velvet paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It features dense, tufted growth with narrow, slightly hairy leaves that give it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, pale-green to brown flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
