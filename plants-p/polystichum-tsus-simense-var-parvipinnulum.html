Polystichum tsus-simense var. parvipinnulum

Polystichum tsus-simense var. parvipinnulum, commonly known as the dwarf fern, is a variant of the Japanese wood fern.

It is a compact, evergreen fern characterized by its narrow, dark green fronds that grow in a rosette pattern. The plant typically reaches a height of about 15 to 20 centimeters, making it suitable for small garden spaces or container cultivation. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil.

This variety is prized for its elegant appearance and adaptability to woodland gardens.
