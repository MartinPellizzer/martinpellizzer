Pithecellobium williamsii

Pithecellobium williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' pithecellobium, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible pods that contain seeds encased in a fibrous pulp, making it valuable in some regions for food and traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as secondary forests and along riverbanks.
