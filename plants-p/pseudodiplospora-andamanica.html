Pseudodiplospora andamanica

Pseudodiplospora andamanica, commonly known as the Andaman dwarf orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the Andaman Islands.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, compact growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in shaded, humid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
