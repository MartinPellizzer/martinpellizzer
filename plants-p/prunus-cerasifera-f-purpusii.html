Prunus cerasifera f. purpusii

Prunus cerasifera f. purpusii, commonly known as the cherry laurel or Prunus purpusii, is a cultivar of the common cherry tree.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant is native to the Caucasus region and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal. It has a compact growth habit and is relatively low-maintenance, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

However, it may produce small, edible fruits that can be a nuisance in some environments.
