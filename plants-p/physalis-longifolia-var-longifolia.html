Physalis longifolia var. longifolia

Physalis longifolia var. longifolia, commonly known as the common husk tomato, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny calyces that enclose the orange-red, berry-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The fruit is edible when ripe, and the plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary use.
