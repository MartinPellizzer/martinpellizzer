Phaseolus acutifolius var. tenuifolius

Phaseolus acutifolius var. tenuifolius, commonly known as the slender-leaf common bean, is a cultivated variant of the common bean species.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, yellowish-green pods that contain edible seeds. This plant is native to the Americas and is often grown for its nutritional value, particularly in Mediterranean and Middle Eastern regions. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, and it is relatively drought-tolerant.

The plant is an important legume for both human consumption and as a cover crop in sustainable agricultural systems.
