Protea thunbergii

Protea thunbergii, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pale pink flower heads. The plant features a tall, sparsely branched stem with narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a silvery-gray waxy coating. Native to the Western Cape region, it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
