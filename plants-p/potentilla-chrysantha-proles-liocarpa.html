Potentilla chrysantha proles liocarpa

Potentilla chrysantha proles liocarpa, commonly known as the golden potentilla, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the summer. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and resilience.
