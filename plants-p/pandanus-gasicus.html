Pandanus gasicus

Pandanus gasicus, commonly known as the New Caledonian pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the island of New Caledonia.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, which are leaf-like bracts. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional crafts by local communities.

Pandanus gasicus thrives in humid, coastal environments and is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.
