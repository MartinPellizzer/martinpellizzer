Piper denudans

Piper denudans, commonly known as the naked pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruits are tiny, three-lobed berries that turn from green to yellow when mature.

Piper denudans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
