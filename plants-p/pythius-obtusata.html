Pythius obtusata

Pythius obtusata, commonly known as the bittercress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and has a widespread distribution across temperate regions. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in gardens and along roadsides. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Pythius obtusata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
