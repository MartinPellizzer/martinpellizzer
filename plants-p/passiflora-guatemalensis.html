Passiflora guatemalensis

Passiflora guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a crown of filaments and a central crown of stamens. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, tropical flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Passiflora guatemalensis is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
