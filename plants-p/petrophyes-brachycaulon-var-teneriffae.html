Petrophyes brachycaulon var. teneriffae

Petrophyes brachycaulon var. teneriffae, commonly known as the Canary Island rock rose, is a succulent shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features a compact, branching structure with thick, fleshy leaves adapted to store water. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique morphology and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
