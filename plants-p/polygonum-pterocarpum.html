Polygonum pterocarpum

Polygonum pterocarpum, commonly known as the Indian nettle or Chinese nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and alternate, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Polygonum pterocarpum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is commonly found in forests and along stream banks.
