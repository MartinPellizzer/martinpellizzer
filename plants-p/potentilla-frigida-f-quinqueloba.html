Potentilla frigida f. quinqueloba

Potentilla frigida f. quinqueloba, commonly known as the five-lobed sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for use in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

The species is notable for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
