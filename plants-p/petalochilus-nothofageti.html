Petalochilus nothofageti

Petalochilus nothofageti, commonly known as the Nothofagus petalochilus, is a rare orchid species native to the subtropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated petals and a unique floral structure that resembles the leaves of its namesake, Nothofagus, a genus of southern beech trees. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks and branches in the canopy layer of humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from pale yellow to greenish-white.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
