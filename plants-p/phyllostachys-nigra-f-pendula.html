Phyllostachys nigra f. pendula

Phyllostachys nigra f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping bamboo, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its cascading, arching stems.

This cultivar belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is native to eastern Asia, particularly China. Its dark purple to black shoots contrast beautifully with the pale green foliage, creating a dramatic visual effect. The plant is known for its elegant, drooping appearance, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes and container planting.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
