Pisum nigrum

Pisum nigrum, commonly known as black gram, is an annual leguminous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant is cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines. Black gram is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

Its botanical features make it an important crop in tropical and subtropical regions.
