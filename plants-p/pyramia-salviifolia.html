Pyramia salviifolia

Pyramia salviifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf pyramia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The leaves are typically arranged oppositely along the stems, giving the plant a delicate and airy appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white, followed by small, round fruits.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
