Phlomis salicifolia

Phlomis salicifolia, commonly known as willow leaf phlomis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that resemble willow leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered spikes of purple to pink flowers in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
