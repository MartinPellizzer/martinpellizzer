Prunus spinosa var. heterochroa

Prunus spinosa var. heterochroa, commonly known as the blackthorn, is a thorny shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are produced in late summer. The plant has small, oval leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodlands.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
