Primula fangii

Primula fangii, commonly known as the Fang's primrose, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Primula fangii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
