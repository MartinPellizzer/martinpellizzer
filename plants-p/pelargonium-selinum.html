Pelargonium selinum

Pelargonium selinum, commonly known as the lesser geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to violet. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and are often covered in fine, white to grayish hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This plant is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
