Pterocarpus pubipetalus

Pterocarpus pubipetalus, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by flat, leathery pods containing flat, winged seeds. Its leaves are odd-pinnate, with small, oval leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs.

The tree is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
