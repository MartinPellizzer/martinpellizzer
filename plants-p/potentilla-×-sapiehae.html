Potentilla × sapiehae

Potentilla × sapiehae, commonly known as the Polish cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has finely divided, serrated leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of silky white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, low-maintenance plant.
