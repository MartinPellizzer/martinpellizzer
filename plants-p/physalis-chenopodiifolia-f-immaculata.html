Physalis chenopodiifolia f. immaculata

Physalis chenopodiifolia f. immaculata, commonly known as the white-berried groundcherry, is a variant of the groundcherry species characterized by its pale, almost white fruits.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States, particularly California. The plant features hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive, sandpaper-like texture. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals, and the fruit is a small, round capsule that turns white when mature.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and unique fruit color, making it a popular choice in native plant gardens.
