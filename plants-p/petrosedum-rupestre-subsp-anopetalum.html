Petrosedum rupestre subsp. anopetalum

Petrosedum rupestre subsp.

anopetalum, commonly known as the rock rose or stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent plant native to rocky habitats in the Mediterranean region. It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it an ideal plant for arid and semi-arid environments.
