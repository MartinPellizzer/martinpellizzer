Phyllanthus chamaecristoides subsp. baracoensis

Phyllanthus chamaecristoides subsp.

baracoensis, commonly known as the Baracoa phyllanthus, is a shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly found in parts of Cuba and the Bahamas. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils.

It is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
