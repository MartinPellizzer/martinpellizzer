Pectis decemcarinata

Pectis decemcarinata, commonly known as the ten-ribbed pectis, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers arranged in a spherical cluster. Its stems are hairy and often branching, and it thrives in dry, sandy soils.

Pectis decemcarinata is typically found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.
