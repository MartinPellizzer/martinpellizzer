Podochilus intricatus

Podochilus intricatus, commonly known as the intricate orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, branched inflorescence and a variety of colorful, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.

Podochilus intricatus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
