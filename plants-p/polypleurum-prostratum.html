Polypleurum prostratum

Polypleurum prostratum, commonly known as the creeping polypody, is a fern native to the wet tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with rhizomes that spread along the ground. The fronds are typically pinnately compound, with a delicate, feathery appearance. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest undergrowth.

The plant is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
