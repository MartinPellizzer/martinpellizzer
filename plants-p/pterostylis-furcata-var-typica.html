Pterostylis furcata var. typica

Pterostylis furcata var. typica, commonly known as the typical forked helmet orchid, is an epiphytic plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, helmet-shaped inflorescence. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, with its roots anchoring it to these substrates. Its flowers are usually green or brown, with a single, elongated lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns.

This orchid is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism, relying on specific insect species for reproduction.
