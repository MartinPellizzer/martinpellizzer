Pyrus nankaiensis

Pyrus nankaiensis, commonly known as the Nankai pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The tree produces small, yellow or green fruits that are typically eaten fresh or used in traditional Chinese medicine.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for centuries for both its fruit and medicinal properties.
