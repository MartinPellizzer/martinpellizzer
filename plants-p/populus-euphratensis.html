Populus euphratensis

Populus euphratensis, commonly known as the Euphrates poplar, is a deciduous tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and smooth, gray bark. The plant typically grows in riparian zones, thriving near rivers and streams. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with both male and female flowers occurring on the same tree.

Populus euphratensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
