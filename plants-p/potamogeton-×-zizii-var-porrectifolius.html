Potamogeton × zizii var. porrectifolius

Potamogeton × zizii var. porrectifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant characterized by its elongated, ribbon-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color.

It is found in freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers, where it often forms dense underwater stands. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively and colonize new areas. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spikes, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

This species plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms and contributing to water quality through nutrient uptake.
