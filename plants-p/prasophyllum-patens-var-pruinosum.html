Prasophyllum patens var. pruinosum

Prasophyllum patens var. pruinosum, commonly known as the pink leek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats. It is often found in heathlands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
