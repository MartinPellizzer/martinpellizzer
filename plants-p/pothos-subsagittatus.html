Pothos subsagittatus

Pothos subsagittatus, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a slightly variegated pattern. The plant thrives in humid environments and requires indirect light to flourish. It is known for its ability to climb using aerial roots, often attaching itself to surfaces such as trellises or walls.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
