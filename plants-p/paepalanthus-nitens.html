Paepalanthus nitens

Paepalanthus nitens, commonly known as the silverleaf puya, is a striking plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy inflorescences. The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it its common name and help reflect sunlight in its native habitat. Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservatories.
