Phaleria calantha

Phaleria calantha, commonly known as the white-berried evergreen or the sweet-scented tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a sweet, honey-like aroma. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to yellow when mature.

Phaleria calantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in folk medicine.
