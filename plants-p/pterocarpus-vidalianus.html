Pterocarpus vidalianus

Pterocarpus vidalianus, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Central and Western Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark brown wood with a high oil content. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, ovate leaflets, and it thrives in humid, tropical climates.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood used in furniture and musical instruments.
