Polygala scoparioides

Polygala scoparioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has linear leaves and produces pods that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open shrublands and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
