Pouteria rivicoa

Pouteria rivicoa, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The tree's most notable feature is its fruit, which contains the edible, oil-rich seeds known as Brazil nuts. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in humid, nutrient-rich soils.

It plays a vital role in the ecosystem by providing food for various wildlife species.
