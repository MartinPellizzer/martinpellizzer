Poa banatica

Poa banatica, commonly known as the Banat grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, leafy stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Its leaves are narrow and have a fine texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal in certain landscapes.

Poa banatica is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in meadows and grasslands.
