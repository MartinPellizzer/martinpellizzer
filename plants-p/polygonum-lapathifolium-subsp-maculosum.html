Polygonum lapathifolium subsp. maculosum

Polygonum lapathifolium subsp.

maculosum, commonly known as the spotted knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia. It features upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive spotted or mottled appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found along stream banks and in wet meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Polygonaceae family.
