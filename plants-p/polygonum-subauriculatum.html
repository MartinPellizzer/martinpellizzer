Polygonum subauriculatum

Polygonum subauriculatum, commonly known as the auriculate knotweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in wetlands, stream banks, and other aquatic environments. Its small, clustered flowers are usually greenish-white and bloom in late summer.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in riparian areas.
