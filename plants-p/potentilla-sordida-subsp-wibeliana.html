Potentilla sordida subsp. wibeliana

Potentilla sordida subsp.

wibeliana, commonly known as the black potentilla, is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features small, dark green leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow, occurring in clusters and blooming in late summer, adding a striking contrast to the plant's dark foliage. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
