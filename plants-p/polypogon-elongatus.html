Polypogon elongatus

Polypogon elongatus, commonly known as the elongated beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spikelet-based inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and prairies, and is adapted to moist environments. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with three stamens and a single pistil.

Polypogon elongatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
