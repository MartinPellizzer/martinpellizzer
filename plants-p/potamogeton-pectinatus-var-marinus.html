Potamogeton pectinatus var. marinus

Potamogeton pectinatus var. marinus, commonly known as sea pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to coastal and brackish water environments.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically smooth and slightly curved. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spikelets, often floating at the water's surface. It thrives in shallow, saline or brackish waters and is often found in estuaries and coastal marshes.

This species plays a vital role in providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
