Polygonum ciliinode var. breve

Polygonum ciliinode var. breve, commonly known as the short-stalked knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive bracteate flowers and rhizomatous growth habits. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often covered in soft, hairy texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in dense clusters, typically in late summer.

The leaves are lance-shaped with ciliate margins, contributing to its botanical uniqueness.
