Piptocarpha oxyphylla

Piptocarpha oxyphylla, commonly known as the silky bluebunch grass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and tufted growth habit, which makes it visually striking in natural landscapes. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Piptocarpha oxyphylla plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
