Pandanus tectorius var. oahuensis

Pandanus tectorius var. oahuensis, commonly known as the Oahu pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional Hawaiian crafts. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fibrous leaves are also used for making thatch and baskets.

This variety is adapted to coastal and lowland environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
