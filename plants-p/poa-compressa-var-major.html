Poa compressa var. major

Poa compressa var. major, commonly known as the tall meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, tufted stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a single, dense spike that bears small, two-lobed spikelets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
