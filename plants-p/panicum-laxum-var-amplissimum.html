Panicum laxum var. amplissimum

Panicum laxum var. amplissimum, commonly known as the giant panicgrass, is a tall, perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves and panicles that can reach several feet in height. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas. Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, making it useful for erosion control.

The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are wind-pollinated, contributing to its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
