Pitcairnia caduciflora

Pitcairnia caduciflora, commonly known as the pink pitcairnia, is a striking bromeliad native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of elongated, waxy leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces vibrant pink or red flowers that emerge from the leaf rosette, often in a showy inflorescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
