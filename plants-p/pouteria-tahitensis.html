Pouteria tahitensis

Pouteria tahitensis, commonly known as Tahitian breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to the South Pacific islands.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces a fruit that is similar in appearance to breadfruit but has a distinct flavor and texture. Its bark contains latex, which has been traditionally used for various purposes by indigenous communities.

The tree is valued for both its ecological role and its potential economic importance in sustainable agriculture.
