Passiflora incarnata var. major

Passiflora incarnata var. major, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces orange-red berries that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by birds and mammals. Its foliage consists of palmately lobed leaves, and it thrives in humid, sunny environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
