Pisum clymenum

Pisum clymenum, commonly known as the Alpine pea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial legume that typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale violet. Its pods contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind.

Pisum clymenum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
