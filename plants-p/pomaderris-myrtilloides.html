Pomaderris myrtilloides

Pomaderris myrtilloides, commonly known as the blueberry bush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, oval, dark blue to black berries that are edible and have a sweet, tart flavor. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and often have a bluish tinge. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and heathlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
