Psittacanthus carinatus

Psittacanthus carinatus, commonly known as the humped fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, humped-shaped fronds. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
