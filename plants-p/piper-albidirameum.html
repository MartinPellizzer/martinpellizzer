Piper albidirameum

Piper albidirameum, commonly known as the white-stemmed pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, pale stems and small, opposite leaves. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Piper albidirameum make it an interesting subject for ecological and pharmacological studies.
