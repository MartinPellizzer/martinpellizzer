Pterolobium integrum

Pterolobium integrum, commonly known as the African senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with stipules, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in racemes. It is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.

The plant also plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for wildlife.
