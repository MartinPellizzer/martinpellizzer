Portulaca canariensis

Portulaca canariensis, commonly known as Canary beach spinach, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb that thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and full sun. The plant features fleshy, succulent leaves that are edible and have a slightly salty, tangy flavor. Its small, yellow flowers bloom throughout the summer, adding a delicate touch to coastal landscapes.

Portulaca canariensis is often used in local cuisine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
