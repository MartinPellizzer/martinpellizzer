Phytolacca abyssinica

Phytolacca abyssinica, commonly known as Abyssinian physalis, is a shrub native to Ethiopia and parts of East Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers that develop into red, lantern-like fruits. The plant contains toxic compounds, including phytolaccatoxin, which can be harmful to humans and animals. Its leaves are alternate and heart-shaped, with a rough texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.
