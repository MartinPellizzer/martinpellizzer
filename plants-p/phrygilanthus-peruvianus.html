Phrygilanthus peruvianus

Phrygilanthus peruvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian flame tree, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and public spaces.
