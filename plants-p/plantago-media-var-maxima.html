Plantago media var. maxima

Plantago media var. maxima, commonly known as the greater plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes, which are typically arranged in a terminal inflorescence. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained environments.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and as a natural remedy for various ailments.
