Primula flavicans

Primula flavicans, commonly known as the yellow primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are usually arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Primula flavicans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and delicate appearance.
