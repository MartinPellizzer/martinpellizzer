Psoralea glandulosa

Psoralea glandulosa, commonly known as the hairy psoralea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and hairy stems. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and the plant produces small, pod-like fruits.
