Papaver dubium subsp. albiflorum

Papaver dubium subsp.

albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its striking white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant features slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
