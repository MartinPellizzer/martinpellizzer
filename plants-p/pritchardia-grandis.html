Pritchardia grandis

Pritchardia grandis, commonly known as the giant pisonia, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 10 feet in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers that are pollinated by native birds and insects. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing numerous winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to habitat loss and invasive species, Pritchardia grandis is considered endangered, making it a priority for conservation efforts.
