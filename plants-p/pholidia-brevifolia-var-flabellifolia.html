Pholidia brevifolia var. flabellifolia

Pholidia brevifolia var. flabellifolia, commonly known as the Florida hibiscus, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features simple, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.

This species is adapted to wetland and coastal environments, thriving in humid, well-drained soils.
