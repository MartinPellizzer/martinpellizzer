Primula denticulata

Primula denticulata, commonly known as the Chinese primrose, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The leaves are typically oval with finely serrated edges, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often grown as an ornamental plant in gardens.

This species is also valued for its ability to bloom early in the spring.
