Pentameris patula

Pentameris patula, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Pentameris patula is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
