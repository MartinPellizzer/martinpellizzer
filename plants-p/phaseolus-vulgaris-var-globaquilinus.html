Phaseolus vulgaris var. globaquilinus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. globaquilinus, commonly known as the globe bean, is a unique variant of the common bean.

It is characterized by its spherical, almost globular pods that are typically green when immature. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to the Americas. Its small, round seeds are often used in traditional cuisines and for ornamental purposes.

Due to its distinct appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.
