Piper geniculatum var. latifolium

Piper geniculatum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf betel vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and as a wrapping for betel nuts. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into reddish berries, which are edible but have a spicy flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

The leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.
