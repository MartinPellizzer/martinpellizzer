Protea madiensis subsp. occidentalis

Protea madiensis subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the Western Mountain Protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family. It is native to the mountainous regions of the Western Cape, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

The plant is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pink flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. It is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of the region, with a dormant summer period and active growth during the cooler months.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
