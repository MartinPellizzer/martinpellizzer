Phyllanthus lancifolius

Phyllanthus lancifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or near water sources.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.
