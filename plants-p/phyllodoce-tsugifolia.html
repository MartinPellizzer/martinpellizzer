Phyllodoce tsugifolia

Phyllodoce tsugifolia, commonly known as the Sitka mountain-mahogany, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as coniferous forests. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, with a distinctive bluish-green color.

Phyllodoce tsugifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
