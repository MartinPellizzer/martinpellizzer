Patagonium pulchrum

Patagonium pulchrum, commonly known as the beautiful Patagonian plant, is a flowering species native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue petals that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, silvery down, which helps reduce water loss.

Patagonium pulchrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
