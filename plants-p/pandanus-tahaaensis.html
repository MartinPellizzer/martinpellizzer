Pandanus tahaaensis

Pandanus tahaaensis, commonly known as the Tahaa pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the island of Tahaa in French Polynesia.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and cuisine. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are fragrant and have a distinctive, sweet aroma, which contributes to its cultural significance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
