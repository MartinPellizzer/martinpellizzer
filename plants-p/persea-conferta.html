Persea conferta

Persea conferta, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, orange to reddish fruits that are edible when fully ripe. Persea conferta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to botanical gardens and ecological landscapes.
