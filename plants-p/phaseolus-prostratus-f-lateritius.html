Phaseolus prostratus f. lateritius

Phaseolus prostratus f. lateritius, commonly known as the creeping pole bean, is a cultivated variant of the common bean.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with sprawling stems that often lie close to the ground. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with small, rounded leaflets. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, elongated pods that contain edible seeds.

This variety is often grown for its ornamental value and as a source of nutritious beans in certain regions.
