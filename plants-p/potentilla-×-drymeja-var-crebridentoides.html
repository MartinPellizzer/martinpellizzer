Potentilla × drymeja var. crebridentoides

Potentilla × drymeja var. crebridentoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved potentilla, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, upright growth habit with numerous branches and dense foliage. The leaves are narrow, serrated, and often have a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It produces clusters of yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens and dry, well-drained soils, making it a popular choice in alpine gardens.
