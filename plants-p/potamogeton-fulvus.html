Potamogeton fulvus

Potamogeton fulvus, commonly known as the yellow pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically green to yellowish in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. Its rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively, forming dense underwater stands.

Potamogeton fulvus plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various waterfowl and invertebrates.
