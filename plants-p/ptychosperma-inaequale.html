Ptychosperma inaequale

Ptychosperma inaequale, commonly known as the unequal pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the leaves.

Ptychosperma inaequale is often used in traditional medicine and for its aromatic leaves, which are utilized in cooking and crafts.
