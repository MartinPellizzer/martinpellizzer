Piper subpanduriforme

Piper subpanduriforme, commonly known as the Panduriforme pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its elongated, heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in yellow or green. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
