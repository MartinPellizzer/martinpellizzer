Phlomis lanata

Phlomis lanata, commonly known as woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and spikes of purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is drought-tolerant. It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Phlomis lanata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
