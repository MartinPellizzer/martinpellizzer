Peperomia ebingeri

Peperomia ebingeri, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are usually silver or grayish in color, with a textured surface that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish, emerging from the leaf axils. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia ebingeri is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
