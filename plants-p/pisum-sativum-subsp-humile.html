Pisum sativum subsp. humile

Pisum sativum subsp.

humile, commonly known as the dwarf pea, is a lesser-known subspecies of the common pea. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, more delicate pods compared to its domesticated relatives.

This subspecies is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are typically violet or white, and it produces edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisines.

Due to its ornamental value and unique morphology, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
