Pinus picea f. columnaris

Pinus picea f. columnaris, commonly known as the columnar spruce, is a compact, upright form of the white spruce.

It features dense, needle-like foliage that gives it a distinctive, tree-like appearance. This variety is characterized by its narrow, columnar growth habit, making it ideal for small spaces or as a ornamental plant. The needles are typically blue-green in color, adding a striking visual contrast to the landscape.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
