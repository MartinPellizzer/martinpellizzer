Picea pungens subvar. pendula

Picea pungens subvar. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Colorado blue spruce, is a variant of the blue spruce native to the Rocky Mountains.

It is characterized by its distinctive cascading branches that droop downward, giving it a unique, ornamental appearance. The foliage consists of short, blue-gray needles that are densely packed, contributing to its striking visual effect. It is a hardy evergreen that thrives in full sun to partial shade and is resistant to many common pests and diseases.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
