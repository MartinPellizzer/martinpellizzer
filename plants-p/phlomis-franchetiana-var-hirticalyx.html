Phlomis franchetiana var. hirticalyx

Phlomis franchetiana var. hirticalyx, commonly known as the woolly phlomis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in dense, white to grayish hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to orange flower clusters in late summer, which are arranged in dense, spherical heads. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a rough texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
