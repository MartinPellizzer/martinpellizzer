Phlox drummondii subsp. glabriflora

Phlox drummondii subsp.

glabriflora, commonly known as the smooth-flowered phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences of pink to red flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smooth, glabrous petals, which set it apart from other phlox varieties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in native landscapes.
