Phyllophyton tibeticum

Phyllophyton tibeticum, commonly known as the Tibetan leaf plant, is a rare and unique species native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that resemble paper, hence its scientific name derived from the Greek words for leaf and paper. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low oxygen levels. Its growth is slow, and it often forms dense mats on rocky surfaces.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
