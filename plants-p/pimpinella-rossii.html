Pimpinella rossii

Pimpinella rossii, commonly known as the Mexican cowpea, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbel-like flower clusters.

It is native to Mexico and is often found in dry, rocky soils. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers are small and white or pale green, arranged in flat-topped umbels.

The seeds are small, smooth, and often used in traditional herbal remedies.
