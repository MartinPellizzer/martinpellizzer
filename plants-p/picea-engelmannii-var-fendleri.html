Picea engelmannii var. fendleri

Picea engelmannii var. fendleri, commonly known as the Mexican fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its conical shape, dark green needle-like leaves, and distinctive cone structures. The tree typically grows in high-altitude forests, preferring cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include a rough, scaly bark and a dense canopy that provides habitat for various wildlife species.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native ecosystems.
