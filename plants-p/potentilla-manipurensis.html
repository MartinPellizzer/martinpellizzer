Potentilla manipurensis

Potentilla manipurensis, commonly known as the Manipur cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern region of India.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth and along stream banks. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a creeping growth habit.

Potentilla manipurensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects.
