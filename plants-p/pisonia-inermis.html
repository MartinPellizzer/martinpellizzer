Pisonia inermis

Pisonia inermis, commonly known as the white pisonia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows up to 6 meters tall. The plant is characterized by its clusters of white, waxy flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Pisonia inermis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
