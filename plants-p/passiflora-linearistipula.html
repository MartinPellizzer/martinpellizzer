Passiflora linearistipula

Passiflora linearistipula, commonly known as the slender-stipule passionflower, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, vine-like growth habit and its distinctive flowers, which feature a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and bracts. The plant produces showy, fragrant blooms with a unique structure that includes a crown of filaments and a central corona. Its leaves are elongated and have finely serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Passiflora linearistipula is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
