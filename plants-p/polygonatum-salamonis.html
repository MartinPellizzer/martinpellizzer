Polygonatum salamonis

Polygonatum salamonis, commonly known as the Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its arching stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces berries that mature to a deep red color, which are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and form a rosette at the base, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
