Phrynium clavatum

Phrynium clavatum, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by orange-red berries. Its rhizomes are edible and are used as a starch source in some regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Araceae, which includes other plants like calla lilies and peace lilies.
