Pelargonium grossulariifolium

Pelargonium grossulariifolium, commonly known as the gooseberry-leaved geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rounded, fleshy leaves that resemble the shape of a gooseberry, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium grossulariifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
