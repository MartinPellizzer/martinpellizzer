Paspalum anderssonii

Paspalum anderssonii, commonly known as the Andes paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types, including those with poor drainage.

Paspalum anderssonii is often found in disturbed areas and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
