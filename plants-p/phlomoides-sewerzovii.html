Phlomoides sewerzovii

Phlomoides sewerzovii, commonly known as the narrowleaf phlomis, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
