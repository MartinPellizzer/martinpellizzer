Papaver alpinum var. endressii

Papaver alpinum var. endressii, commonly known as the Alpine poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals and a distinctive, narrow, spire-like shape. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. It produces a capsule fruit containing numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by the wind.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
