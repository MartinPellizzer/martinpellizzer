Poecilocarpus ispahanicus

Poecilocarpus ispahanicus, commonly known as the Persian mountain bean, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive violet or purple hue.

The seeds are enclosed in narrow, elongated pods, which contribute to its ecological role in local ecosystems.
