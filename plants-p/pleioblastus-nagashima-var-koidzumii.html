Pleioblastus nagashima var. koidzumii

Pleioblastus nagashima var. koidzumii, commonly known as the Japanese silver grass, is a variety of bamboo characterized by its fine, silvery-blue foliage.

It is native to Japan and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant typically grows in a clumping form, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a soft, feathery texture that gives it a delicate appearance.

It is often used in ornamental landscaping for its aesthetic value and ability to create a subtle, elegant atmosphere in gardens.
