Plerandra seemanniana

Plerandra seemanniana, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a striking terrestrial orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces intricate, pendulous inflorescences with numerous small, showy flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
