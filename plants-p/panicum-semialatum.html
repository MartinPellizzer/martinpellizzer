Panicum semialatum

Panicum semialatum, commonly known as silky grass or silky millet, is a warm-season grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, silky-textured leaves and panicles that are often tinged with red or purple. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for ornamental and erosion control purposes. It is drought-tolerant and can be used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.
