Prunus aurantiaca

Prunus aurantiaca, commonly known as the Chinese sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible but not particularly flavorful. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Prunus aurantiaca is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive fall foliage and unique fruiting display.
