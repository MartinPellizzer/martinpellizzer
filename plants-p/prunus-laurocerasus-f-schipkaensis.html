Prunus laurocerasus f. schipkaensis

Prunus laurocerasus f. schipkaensis, commonly known as the Schipkaensis laurel-cherry, is a rare and ornamental variant of the common cherry laurel.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are glossy and dark green, providing year-round visual interest. This form is typically smaller than the standard species, making it suitable for compact garden spaces.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in horticulture.
