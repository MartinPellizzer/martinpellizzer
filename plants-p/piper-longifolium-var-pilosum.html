Piper longifolium var. pilosum

Piper longifolium var. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy sarsaparilla, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns dark purple when mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
