Pseuderanthemum lapathifolium

Pseuderanthemum lapathifolium, commonly known as the false spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble spider-like flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, ranging from red to violet, and are borne in terminal clusters.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
