Pellaea intermedia f. pubescens

Pellaea intermedia f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent cliff fern, is a fern species characterized by its dense, hairy foliage.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The plant typically grows on rocky outcrops and in dry, shaded environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its fronds are lance-shaped with a distinctive pubescent (hairy) texture, contributing to its unique appearance.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a decorative plant in humid environments.
