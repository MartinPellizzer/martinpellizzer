Peucephyllum schottii var. latisetum

Peucephyllum schottii var. latisetum, commonly known as the broad-leaved peucephyllum, is a unique plant species native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette formation. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests or humid valleys.

Its botanical significance lies in its distinct morphology and ecological role within its native habitat.
