Potentilla tormentilla f. polypetala

Potentilla tormentilla f. polypetala, commonly known as the whiteflower cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
