Pavonia alia

Pavonia alia, commonly known as the redroot pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it a suitable choice for gardens and landscapes.

Pavonia alia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
