Polygala sanguinea

Polygala sanguinea, commonly known as the red milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Polygala sanguinea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
