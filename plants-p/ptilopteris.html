Ptilopteris

Ptilopteris, commonly known as the feather fern, is a genus of ferns belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

These plants are characterized by their delicate, feathery fronds that are often divided into many small, finger-like segments. Ptilopteris species are typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. They have rhizomatous growth habits, with underground stems that help in spreading and anchoring the plant.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, contributing to their reproductive strategy.
