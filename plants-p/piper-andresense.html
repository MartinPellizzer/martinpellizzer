Piper andresense

Piper andresense, commonly known as the Andes pepper or Andean pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that often ascend through shrubs or trees. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a slightly rough texture.

The small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and the fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature.
