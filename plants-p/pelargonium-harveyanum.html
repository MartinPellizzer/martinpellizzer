Pelargonium harveyanum

Pelargonium harveyanum, commonly known as the Harvey's geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family, characterized by its upright growth habit and showy flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are typically rounded with a slightly hairy texture, and it prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Pelargonium harveyanum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and flowers.
