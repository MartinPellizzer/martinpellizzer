Pilea hilariana

Pilea hilariana, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, waxy leaves that resemble small coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. It is a popular indoor plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Pilea hilariana thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
