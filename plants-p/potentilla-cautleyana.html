Potentilla cautleyana

Potentilla cautleyana, commonly known as the Himalayan cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla cautleyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to withstand cold climates.
