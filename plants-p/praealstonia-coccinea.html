Praealstonia coccinea

Praealstonia coccinea, commonly known as the red-flowered praealstonia, is a striking member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red flowers, which are clustered in umbels and attract various pollinators. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Praealstonia coccinea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
