Plectranthus malabaricus var. leptostachys

Plectranthus malabaricus var. leptostachys, commonly known as the Malabar spurge or lemon verbena, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern India.

It features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a mild, lemony scent. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale purple, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and containers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic foliage.
