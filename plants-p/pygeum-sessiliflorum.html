Pygeum sessiliflorum

Pygeum sessiliflorum, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing angular seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ecological importance and historical use in traditional medicine, it is also of interest for conservation efforts.
