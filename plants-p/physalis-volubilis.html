Physalis volubilis

Physalis volubilis, commonly known as the common groundcherry, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, orange berries enclosed in a papery calyx that turns red as the fruit matures. These berries are edible when fully ripe and have a tangy, slightly sweet flavor.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant flowers and unique fruit structure.
