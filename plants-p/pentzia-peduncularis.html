Pentzia peduncularis

Pentzia peduncularis, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spike-like clusters of bright orange-red flowers. The plant produces bract-like leaves that are often mistaken for flowers, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to both gardens and botanical collections.
