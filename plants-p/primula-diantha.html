Primula diantha

Primula diantha, commonly known as the white primrose, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant typically blooms in spring and is often found in woodland edges and moist meadows. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in partial shade and well-drained soil.
