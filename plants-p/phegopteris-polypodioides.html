Phegopteris polypodioides

Phegopteris polypodioides, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bright green and have a leathery texture. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a common reproductive feature in ferns.

Phegopteris polypodioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining soil stability in its native habitats.
