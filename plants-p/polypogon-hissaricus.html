Polypogon hissaricus

Polypogon hissaricus, commonly known as the Hissar grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and usually have a rough texture.

It is often used for erosion control and as a forage plant in certain regions.
