Phyllocactus macropterus

Phyllocactus macropterus, commonly known as the giant leaf cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, paddle-shaped stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red, spherical fruits. Its succulent leaves store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Phyllocactus macropterus is often cultivated for its striking appearance and adaptability to semi-shade conditions.
