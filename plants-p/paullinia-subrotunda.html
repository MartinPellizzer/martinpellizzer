Paullinia subrotunda

Paullinia subrotunda, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its dark red, edible fruits that resemble small cherries. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The fruit's high vitamin C content makes it popular in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
