Paracryphia alticola

Paracryphia alticola, commonly known as the alpine wax tree, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help conserve moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its growth habit is typically low and compact, making it well-suited for rocky or gravelly soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude ecosystems.
