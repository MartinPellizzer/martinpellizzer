Persea chrysotricha

Persea chrysotricha, commonly known as the golden-hair persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America and northern South America. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers. The fruit is a smooth, orange-red berry that ripens in the dry season.

It is valued for its edible fruit and has cultural significance in some indigenous communities.
