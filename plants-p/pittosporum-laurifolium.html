Pittosporum laurifolium

Pittosporum laurifolium, commonly known as the silver tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that turn a striking silver-white color in the autumn. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring, followed by small, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate coastal conditions and its role in ecological restoration projects.
