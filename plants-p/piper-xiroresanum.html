Piper xiroresanum

Piper xiroresanum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces tiny, three-lobed berries that mature to a dark red or black color, which are the source of the spice known as black pepper. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

The plant is widely cultivated for its aromatic and medicinal properties.
