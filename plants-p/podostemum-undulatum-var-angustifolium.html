Podostemum undulatum var. angustifolium

Podostemum undulatum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water primrose, is a aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in fast-flowing streams and rivers, anchoring itself to rocks with its specialized roots. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are often submerged, with small, white flowers that appear above the water surface. It plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small fish.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it an important subject for botanical and ecological studies.
