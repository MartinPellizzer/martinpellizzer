Pleurothallis brachyglottis

Pleurothallis brachyglottis, commonly known as the short-lip pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its flowers are often clustered in racemes and bloom during the warmer months.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
