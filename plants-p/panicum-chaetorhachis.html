Panicum chaetorhachis

Panicum chaetorhachis, commonly known as the silky panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fluffy, pale green to golden seed heads that are highly visible during the late summer and fall. Its leaves are narrow and slightly rolled, with a bluish-gray hue that helps it blend into its natural habitat.

Panicum chaetorhachis is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
