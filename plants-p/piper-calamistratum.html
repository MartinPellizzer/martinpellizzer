Piper calamistratum

Piper calamistratum, commonly known as the "bitter pepper" or "calamistratum pepper," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that often trail along the ground or climb supports. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a glossy surface and a strong, aromatic scent.

The small, greenish-white flowers are followed by elongated, slender fruits that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature.
