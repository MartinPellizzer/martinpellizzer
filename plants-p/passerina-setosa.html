Passerina setosa

Passerina setosa, commonly known as the hairy pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, early spring blooms. The plant produces small, usually yellow or white flowers with numerous stamens, and its leaves are palmately divided with serrated edges. Passerina setosa thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
