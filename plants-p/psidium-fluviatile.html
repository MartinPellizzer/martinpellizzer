Psidium fluviatile

Psidium fluviatile, commonly known as the river guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, tropical environments along riverbanks and in swampy areas. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered together, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wet conditions.
