Piper bivalvantherum

Piper bivalvantherum, commonly known as the two-valved pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with small, opposite leaflets. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in spikes, and it produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature.

This species is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
