Panicum meridionale

Panicum meridionale, commonly known as southern panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern United States.

It typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields, thriving in full sun. The plant produces dense, fluffy seed heads that are often seen in the fall, contributing to its ornamental value. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to various soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
