Passiflora subpurpurea

Passiflora subpurpurea, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and modified leaves. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits known as passionfruits, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs.

Passiflora subpurpurea is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
