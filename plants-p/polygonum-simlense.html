Polygonum simlense

Polygonum simlense, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive nodes and often reddish stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth margin and often have a reddish or purple tinge.

The small, greenish flowers appear in clusters and are followed by three-lobed seed capsules.
