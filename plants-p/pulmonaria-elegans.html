Pulmonaria elegans

Pulmonaria elegans, commonly known as the lungwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features basal rosettes of velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are often spotted with purple or silver. The plant produces spikes of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves and flowers contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in herbal medicine.

Pulmonaria elegans thrives in moist, shady woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
