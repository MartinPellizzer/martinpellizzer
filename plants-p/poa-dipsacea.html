Poa dipsacea

Poa dipsacea, commonly known as the pink moss grass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its distinctive pinkish or reddish coloration, which is most prominent during the flowering season. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

Poa dipsacea plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
