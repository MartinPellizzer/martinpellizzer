Polypogon paniceus var. monspeliensis

Polypogon paniceus var. monspeliensis, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silky texture and blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental appeal makes it a popular choice for landscaping in humid climates.
