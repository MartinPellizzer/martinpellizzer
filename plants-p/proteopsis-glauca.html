Proteopsis glauca

Proteopsis glauca, commonly known as the blue protea, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family, which includes many other well-known ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its striking, rounded inflorescence that resembles a pom-pom, composed of numerous small, colorful flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its native arid environment.

Proteopsis glauca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun.
