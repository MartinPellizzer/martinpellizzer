Prunus compressa

Prunus compressa, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or Japanese cherry, is a small deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in spring. The plant has oval, dark green leaves that turn reddish in autumn. Its fruit is a small, sweet cherry that ripens in summer.

Prunus compressa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and seasonal blooms.
