Piper epunctatum

Piper epunctatum, commonly known as the punctate pepper or the spiny pepper, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a serrated margin and its clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
