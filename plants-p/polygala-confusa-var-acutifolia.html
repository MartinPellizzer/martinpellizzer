Polygala confusa var. acutifolia

Polygala confusa var. acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands. The plant features slender, branching stems with small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive acuminate tip, contributing to its common name.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
