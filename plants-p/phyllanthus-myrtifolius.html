Phyllanthus myrtifolius

Phyllanthus myrtifolius, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often elliptical or ovate leaves that are usually glabrous and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by bright orange to red berries containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
