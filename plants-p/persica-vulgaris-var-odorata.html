Persica vulgaris var. odorata

Persica vulgaris var. odorata, commonly known as the sweet cherry.

This variety is characterized by its fragrant flowers and edible fruits. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 8 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy green upper surface.

The fruit is a drupe, usually red to dark red when ripe, and contains a single seed.
