Petasites officinalis var. hybridus

Petasites officinalis var. hybridus, commonly known as the hybrid edelweiss, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette of large, silver-gray, hairy leaves and its showy, white to pale yellow flower heads. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing a distinctive, daisy-like appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
