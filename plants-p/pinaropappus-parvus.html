Pinaropappus parvus

Pinaropappus parvus, commonly known as the small pappus grass, is a small annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, hair-like leaves and small, wind-dispersed seeds. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its short stature and efficient water use. Its flowers are inconspicuous, consisting of tiny spikelets that produce lightweight seeds for wind propagation.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
