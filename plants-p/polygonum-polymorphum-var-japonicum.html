Polygonum polymorphum var. japonicum

Polygonum polymorphum var. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its stout, hollow stems that are often reddish in color and its heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale pink, which bloom in late summer. It is known for its rapid growth and aggressive spreading habit, often becoming invasive in non-native environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel.
