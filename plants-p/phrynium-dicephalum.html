Phrynium dicephalum

Phrynium dicephalum, commonly known as the two-headed arrow plant, is a tropical perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking, broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by spiny, three-lobed berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
