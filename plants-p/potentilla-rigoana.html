Potentilla rigoana

Potentilla rigoana, commonly known as the creeping potentilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features small, delicate yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with wiry, slender stems that root at the nodes. Its leaves are oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture, and are arranged alternately along the stems.

Potentilla rigoana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
