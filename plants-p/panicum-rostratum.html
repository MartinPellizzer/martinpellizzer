Panicum rostratum

Panicum rostratum, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, spike-like inflorescence produces numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Panicum rostratum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
