Potamogeton × inbaensis

Potamogeton × inbaensis, commonly known as the hybrid water milfoil, is a hybrid plant species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton crispus.

It is characterized by its submerged or floating aquatic growth habit and possesses whorled leaves that are often serrated or toothed. The plant typically produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are pollinated by wind or insects. Potamogeton × inbaensis is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and canals.

Its presence can indicate nutrient-rich waters and may contribute to aquatic ecosystem dynamics by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
