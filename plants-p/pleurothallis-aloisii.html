Pleurothallis aloisii

Pleurothallis aloisii, commonly known as the Aloiis's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a subtle fragrance, often blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, adapted to capture moisture from the air.

This orchid is appreciated for its unique appearance and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
