Pedicularis cernua subsp. latifolia

Pedicularis cernua subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved lousewort, is a herbaceous perennial native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its drooping, spurred flowers that are typically pink or purple, and its broad, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats. It is a member of the Orobanchaceae family, which includes many parasitic and hemiparasitic species.
