Polygonum convolvulus f. glabrum

Polygonum convolvulus f. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth knotgrass, is a variant of the common knotgrass species.

It is a trailing annual herb that belongs to the Polygonaceae family. The plant is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves, which are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. It produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed through wind and animal activity.

This form is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, showcasing its adaptability to various environments.
