Piper monostigmum

Piper monostigmum, commonly known as the West African pepper or African black pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper and long pepper. The plant produces small, green berries that turn dark when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies for their purported medicinal properties.

The plant's botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts across its native range.
