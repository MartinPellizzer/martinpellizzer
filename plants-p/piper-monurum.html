Piper monurum

Piper monurum, commonly known as the Java pepper or long pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, peppercorn-like berries that grow in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into the aromatic, elongated fruits. Its berries are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, valued for their pungent flavor.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
