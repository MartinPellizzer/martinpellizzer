Piper selangorense

Piper selangorense, commonly known as the Malayan pepper or Selangor pepper, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is valued in herbal remedies for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
