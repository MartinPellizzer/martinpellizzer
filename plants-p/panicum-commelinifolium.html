Panicum commelinifolium

Panicum commelinifolium, commonly known as the grassland bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of grasses. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils, often found in sandy or loamy conditions.

Due to its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat, it is of interest in conservation and restoration efforts.
