Paspalum dasypleurum

Paspalum dasypleurum, commonly known as the rough paspalum, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Paspalum dasypleurum is often found in open habitats such as grasslands, heathlands, and along coastal areas.
