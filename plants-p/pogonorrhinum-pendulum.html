Pogonorrhinum pendulum

Pogonorrhinum pendulum, commonly known as the hanging beard plant, is a unique member of the Commelinaceae family.

It is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences that resemble a beard, hence its common name. The plant features elongated, strap-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple hues. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically displaying a mix of white, pink, and red colors.

Pogonorrhinum pendulum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
