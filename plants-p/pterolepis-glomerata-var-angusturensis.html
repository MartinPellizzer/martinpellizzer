Pterolepis glomerata var. angusturensis

Pterolepis glomerata var. angusturensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pterolepis, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role within its native habitat.
