Phyla linearis

Phyla linearis, commonly known as the linear phyla, is a unique plant species characterized by its elongated, ribbon-like leaves.

It belongs to the phylum Chlorophyta, which includes green algae, and is primarily found in freshwater environments. The plant exhibits a simple structure with no true roots, stems, or leaves, relying instead on its flat, ribbon-shaped structures for photosynthesis. It thrives in nutrient-rich, shallow waters and plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms.

Due to its delicate nature, it is often used in scientific studies to understand algal growth and ecological interactions.
