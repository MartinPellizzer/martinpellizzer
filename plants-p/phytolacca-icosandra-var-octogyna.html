Phytolacca icosandra var. octogyna

Phytolacca icosandra var. octogyna, commonly known as the purple-root phytolacca, is a variant of the phytolacca genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its distinctive purple or reddish roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by fleshy, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used in ornamental horticulture due to its striking foliage and fruit.
