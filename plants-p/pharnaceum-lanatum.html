Pharnaceum lanatum

Pharnaceum lanatum, commonly known as the woolly pharnaceum, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
