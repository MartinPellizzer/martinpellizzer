Podocarpus bracteatus

Podocarpus bracteatus, commonly known as the Christmas tree or Indian cedar, is a evergreen coniferous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and dark green, glossy foliage. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish color, containing seeds surrounded by a fleshy, orange aril. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.
