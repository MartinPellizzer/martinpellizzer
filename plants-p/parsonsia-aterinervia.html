Parsonsia aterinervia

Parsonsia aterinervia, commonly known as the black-veined parsonsia, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with contrasting black veins. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with prominent, dark veins that give it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Parsonsia aterinervia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
