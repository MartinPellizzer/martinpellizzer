Paulownia tomentosa f. pallida

Paulownia tomentosa f. pallida, commonly known as the silver paulownia, is a cultivar of the Paulownia tree characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on its leaves.

This variety is native to China and is prized for its rapid growth and ornamental value. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and emit a mild fragrance, especially when crushed. It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in spring, typically in shades of pink or purple.

The tree is also valued for its lightweight, durable wood, which is used in crafting and furniture making.
