Prasophyllum gibbosum

Prasophyllum gibbosum, commonly known as the globular orchid, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of southern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, globular-shaped inflorescence. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with a distinct, spurred petal that aids in pollination by insects.

Prasophyllum gibbosum is a perennial herb that produces its flowers from late winter to early spring.
