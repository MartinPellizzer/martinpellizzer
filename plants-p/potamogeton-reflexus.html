Potamogeton reflexus

Potamogeton reflexus, commonly known as the reflexed pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. Its reproductive structures are adapted for underwater pollination, and it plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.

Potamogeton reflexus is often found in shallow waters with soft, muddy or sandy substrates.
