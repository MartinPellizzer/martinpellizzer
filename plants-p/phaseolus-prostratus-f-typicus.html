Phaseolus prostratus f. typicus

Phaseolus prostratus f. typicus, commonly known as the creeping pole bean, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Americas.

It produces small, edible pods that contain several seeds, and it is typically grown for its nutritional value. The plant has a sprawling growth habit, with trailing stems that can reach lengths of several meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it flowers with small, white or pale purple blooms.

This variety is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in warm climates.
