Panicum acutissimum

Panicum acutissimum, commonly known as slender panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant communities and a potential candidate for ecological restoration projects.
