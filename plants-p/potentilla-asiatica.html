Potentilla asiatica

Potentilla asiatica, commonly known as the Asian cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant has serrated, alternate leaves that are typically hairy beneath. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, rocky areas, and woodland edges.

Potentilla asiatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
