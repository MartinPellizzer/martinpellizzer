Platygonia

Platygonia, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and pinnately arranged, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking display in the spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Platygonia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
