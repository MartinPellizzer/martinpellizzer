Phlox pilosa var. pubiflora

Phlox pilosa var. pubiflora, commonly known as the pubescent phlox, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shady areas. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
