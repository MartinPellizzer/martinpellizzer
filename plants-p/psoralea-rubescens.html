Psoralea rubescens

Psoralea rubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seed pods are typically hairy and contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Psoralea rubescens is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
