Phyla × intermedia

Phyla × intermedia, commonly known as the hybrid phlox, is a flowering plant in the family Polemoniaceae.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Phlox divaricata and Phlox paniculata, characterized by its showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
