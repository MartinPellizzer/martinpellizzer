Panicum huachucae var. silvicola

Panicum huachucae var. silvicola, commonly known as the Andean bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the high Andes of South America.

It typically grows in moist, mountainous environments and is adapted to cool, temperate climates. The plant features narrow, blue-green leaves and produces inflorescences of small, spike-like clusters. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare in the wild.
