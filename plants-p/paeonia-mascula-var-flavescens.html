Paeonia mascula var. flavescens

Paeonia mascula var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow peony, is a herbaceous perennial native to China.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, with a distinctive, slightly hairy texture. The plant has compound, bipinnate leaves that are green and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, often blooming in early to mid-spring.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valued ornamental plant in gardens and botanical collections.
