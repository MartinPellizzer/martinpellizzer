Physaria lateralis

Physaria lateralis, commonly known as the bluemink cress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces bright yellow flowers in early spring. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves that help it retain water in arid environments. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a distinctive member of the Brassicaceae family.
