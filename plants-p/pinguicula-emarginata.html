Pinguicula emarginata

Pinguicula emarginata, commonly known as the Margined Sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant's leaves are typically green with red or purple hues, and they curl around captured prey to aid in digestion. It thrives in moist, acidic environments and is often found in bogs or wet meadows.

Pinguicula emarginata is a low-growing perennial that plays a unique role in its ecosystem by controlling insect populations.
