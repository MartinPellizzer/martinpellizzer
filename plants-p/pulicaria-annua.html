Pulicaria annua

Pulicaria annua, commonly known as annual ragwort, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. The plant has yellow flowers that bloom from June to August, and its leaves are covered in fine hairs. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

Pulicaria annua is often considered a weed but can serve as a food source for certain insects.
