Piper nepalense var. parvifolium

Piper nepalense var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved black pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper nepalense species native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its slender stems and small, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into dark red to black berries, which are the source of its aromatic and pungent properties. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses.

The leaves and fruits contain essential oils and alkaloids, contributing to its traditional applications in herbal medicine.
