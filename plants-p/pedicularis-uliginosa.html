Pedicularis uliginosa

Pedicularis uliginosa, commonly known as the bog gentian, is a flowering plant native to wet, acidic environments in northern regions.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and grows from a tuberous root system. It is often found in bogs, marshes, and other moist habitats.

Pedicularis uliginosa is a member of the gentian family (Gentianaceae) and is known for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
