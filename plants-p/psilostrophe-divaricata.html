Psilostrophe divaricata

Psilostrophe divaricata, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and is often found in marshy or seasonally flooded habitats. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

Psilostrophe divaricata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
