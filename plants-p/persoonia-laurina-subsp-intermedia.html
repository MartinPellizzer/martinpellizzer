Persoonia laurina subsp. intermedia

Persoonia laurina subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the Grey Box, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters, typically during spring. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a popular choice for native gardens.
