Penstemon davidsonii var. menziesii

Penstemon davidsonii var. menziesii, commonly known as the Menzies' beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright, sparsely branched stems and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
