Pelargonium bijugum

Pelargonium bijugum, commonly known as the double-leaved geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring two pairs of opposite leaves on each stem, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, Pelargonium bijugum is widely cultivated in temperate climates.
