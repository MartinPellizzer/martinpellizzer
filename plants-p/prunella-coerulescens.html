Prunella coerulescens

Prunella coerulescens, commonly known as blue beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features square, hairy stems. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, opposite, and covered in fine hairs.

Prunella coerulescens is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
