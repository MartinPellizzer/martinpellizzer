Pithecellobium schaffneri

Pithecellobium schaffneri, commonly known as the Schaffner's mountain bean, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant features compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures, and its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The fruit is a legume pod that contains several edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines.

Due to its ornamental value and potential nutritional benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agroforestry systems.
