Pachyphyllum pastii

Pachyphyllum pastii, commonly known as the desert rose or stone plant, is a succulent native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and yellow, which bloom during the rainy season. Its compact growth habit makes it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Pachyphyllum pastii is valued for its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
