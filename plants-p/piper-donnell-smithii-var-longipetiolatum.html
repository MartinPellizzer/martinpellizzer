Piper donnell-smithii var. longipetiolatum

Piper donnell-smithii var. longipetiolatum, commonly known as the long-petioled pepper plant, is a tropical species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper.

This variant is distinguished by its elongated petioles, which give it a unique appearance among its relatives.
