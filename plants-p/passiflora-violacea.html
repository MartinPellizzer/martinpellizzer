Passiflora violacea

Passiflora violacea, commonly known as the violet passionflower, is a striking vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant violet flowers, which feature a unique structure with five sepals, five petals, and a crown-like array of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Passiflora violacea is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators.
