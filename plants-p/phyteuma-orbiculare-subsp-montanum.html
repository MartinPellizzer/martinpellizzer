Phyteuma orbiculare subsp. montanum

Phyteuma orbiculare subsp.

montanum, commonly known as the alpine phyteuma, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange.

The plant typically grows in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized flora of mountain ecosystems.
