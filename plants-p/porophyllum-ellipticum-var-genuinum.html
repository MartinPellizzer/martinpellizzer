Porophyllum ellipticum var. genuinum

Porophyllum ellipticum var. genuinum, commonly known as the narrowleaf blueweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine, white hairs, giving the plant a silvery appearance. The flowering stems are upright and bear small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
