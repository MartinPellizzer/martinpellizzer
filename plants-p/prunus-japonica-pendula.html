Prunus japonica pendula

Prunus japonica pendula, commonly known as the weeping Japanese cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that create a distinctive weeping appearance, with delicate pink flowers appearing in early spring. The plant has small, oval leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its seasonal beauty. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Prunus japonica pendula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of transience in Japanese culture.
