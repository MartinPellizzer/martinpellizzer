Pittosporum pauciflorum var. pauciflorum

Pittosporum pauciflorum var. pauciflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in temperate climates.
