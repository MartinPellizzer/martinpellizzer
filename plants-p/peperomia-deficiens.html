Peperomia deficiens

Peperomia deficiens, commonly known as the laceleaf peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, waxy leaves with a distinctive veined pattern that resembles lace, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-15 cm and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or pale pink. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its slow growth and compact size, it is ideal for container cultivation and requires minimal maintenance.
