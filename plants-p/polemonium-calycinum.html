Polemonium calycinum

Polemonium calycinum, commonly known as bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in shaded, rich soils.

Polemonium calycinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
