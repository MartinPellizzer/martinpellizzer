Papaver laevigatum

Papaver laevigatum, commonly known as the smooth poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers with prominent, saucer-shaped petals. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Papaver laevigatum is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
