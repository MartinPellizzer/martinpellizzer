Phebalium ozothamnoides

Phebalium ozothamnoides, commonly known as the honey bush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves and small, clustered white or yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine by Indigenous Australians for their therapeutic properties.
