Plectranthus otostegioides

Plectranthus otostegioides, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of tubular flowers that range in color from violet to white. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Plectranthus otostegioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
