Piper discriminatum

Piper discriminatum, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 3,000 species of flowering plants. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. Its fruits are small, berry-like structures that contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The botanical characteristics of Piper discriminatum make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
