Panicum proliferum var. paludosum

Panicum proliferum var. paludosum, commonly known as swamp panicum, is a perennial grass native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It thrives in moist to saturated soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, upright stems that can grow up to 2 meters tall, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically pale green to brownish in color.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
