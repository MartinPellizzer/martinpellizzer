Pyrus philippinensis

Pyrus philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by edible, sweet fruits that are similar to pears. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and horticultural settings.
