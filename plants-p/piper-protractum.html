Piper protractum

Piper protractum, commonly known as the long-stalked pepper plant, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus in the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, slender fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy coating.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fruit structure.
