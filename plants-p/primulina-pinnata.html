Primulina pinnata

Primulina pinnata, commonly known as the featherleaf primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its foliage is often covered in fine, feathery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Primulina pinnata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
