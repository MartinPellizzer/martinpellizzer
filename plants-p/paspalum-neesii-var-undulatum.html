Paspalum neesii var. undulatum

Paspalum neesii var. undulatum, commonly known as the undulating paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and marshy areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
