Prockia villosa

Prockia villosa, commonly known as the hairy prockia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of North America.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in the early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Prockia villosa is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.
