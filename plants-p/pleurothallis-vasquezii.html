Pleurothallis vasquezii

Pleurothallis vasquezii, commonly known as the Vasquez's orchid, is a species of orchid belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. This plant typically grows as an epiphyte on trees, utilizing its roots to anchor itself while absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are small and often resemble those of other pleurothallis species, with a delicate appearance and a range of colors.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
