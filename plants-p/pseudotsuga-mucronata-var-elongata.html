Pseudotsuga mucronata var. elongata

Pseudotsuga mucronata var. elongata, commonly known as the Sierra red fir, is a variant of the red fir native to the western United States.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The leaves are typically longer and more slender compared to other varieties of the species. It thrives in cool, moist environments at higher elevations.

This variant is known for its ecological importance in forest ecosystems and its role in providing habitat for various wildlife species.
