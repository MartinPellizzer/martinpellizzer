Polycarpon balfourianum

Polycarpon balfourianum, commonly known as the Balfour's knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as buckwheats. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often growing in alpine or subalpine zones.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
