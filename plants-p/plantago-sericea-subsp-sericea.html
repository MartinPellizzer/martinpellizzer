Plantago sericea subsp. sericea

Plantago sericea subsp.

sericea, commonly known as silky plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It features lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
