Physocarpus opulifolius f. luteus

Physocarpus opulifolius f. luteus, commonly known as the golden smoketree, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant panicles of small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The foliage turns a striking orange-red in the fall, adding ornamental value to the landscape. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and has a rounded, upright form.

This cultivar is prized for its vibrant fall color and adaptability to a variety of soil conditions.
