Potentilla gaudinii

Potentilla gaudinii, commonly known as the Gaudin's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its leaves are palmately divided into five oval-shaped leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla gaudinii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and colorful blooms.
