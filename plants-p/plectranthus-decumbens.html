Plectranthus decumbens

Plectranthus decumbens, commonly known as creeping mint, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and a strong, minty aroma. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ground cover in gardens.

Its aromatic properties make it useful in herbal remedies and as a natural insect repellent.
