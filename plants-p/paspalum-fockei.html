Paspalum fockei

Paspalum fockei, commonly known as coastal paspalum, is a perennial grass native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dune environments and coastal grasslands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to withstand salt spray and wind, making it an important species for coastal stabilization.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
