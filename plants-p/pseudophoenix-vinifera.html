Pseudophoenix vinifera

Pseudophoenix vinifera, commonly known as the wine palm, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that eventually develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, dry climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
