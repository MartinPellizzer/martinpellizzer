Penstemon heterophyllus subsp. spinulosus

Penstemon heterophyllus subsp.

spinulosus, commonly known as the spiked beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny bracts that give it its common name.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its foliage is typically lance-shaped with a fuzzy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.
