Prunus myrtellina

Prunus myrtellina, commonly known as the Japanese white cherry, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, red berries. Prunus myrtellina thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
