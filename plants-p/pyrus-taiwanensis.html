Pyrus taiwanensis

Pyrus taiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan pear, is a deciduous tree native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that are similar to common pears.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in suitable climates.
