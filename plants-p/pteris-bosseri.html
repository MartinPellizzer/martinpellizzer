Pteris bosseri

Pteris bosseri, commonly known as the Bosser's water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lobed fronds that are typically green with a bluish tinge. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce a dense network of roots, which help in anchoring the plant and absorbing nutrients.

Pteris bosseri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
