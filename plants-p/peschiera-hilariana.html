Peschiera hilariana

Peschiera hilariana, commonly known as the Hilarian's beauty, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped white petals and clusters of small, yellow stamens. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often near streams or in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and slightly lobed, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Peschiera hilariana blooms in late spring, producing a subtle fragrance that attracts pollinators like bees and moths.
