Potentilla × guentheri

Potentilla × guentheri, commonly known as the Guenther's cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils. The plant produces small, golden-yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in rounded inflorescences. Its leaves are typically compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

This species is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
