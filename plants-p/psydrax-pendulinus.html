Psydrax pendulinus

Psydrax pendulinus, commonly known as the hanging psidium, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by edible, berry-like fruits. Its botanical features make it suitable for ornamental purposes and in some regions, it is cultivated for its fruit.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
