Peltopus

Peltopus, commonly known as the giant sea hare, is a type of marine gastropod mollusk belonging to the family Aplysiidae.

It is characterized by its large, soft body and a distinctive, often brightly colored mantle that can extend beyond its body. The peltopus lacks a shell and instead uses its muscular foot for movement along the ocean floor. Its diet primarily consists of algae, which it grazes on using a specialized radula.

This species is commonly found in shallow coastal waters of the Pacific Ocean.
