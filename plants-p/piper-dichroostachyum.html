Piper dichroostachyum

Piper dichroostachyum, commonly known as the two-colored stamen pepper, is a tropical climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
