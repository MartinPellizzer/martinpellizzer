Penstemon glaber f. cyananthus

Penstemon glaber f. cyananthus, commonly known as the blueflower penstemon, is a cultivar of the penstemon genus native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
