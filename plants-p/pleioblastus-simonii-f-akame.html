Pleioblastus simonii f. akame

Pleioblastus simonii f. akame, commonly known as the red tip bamboo, is a variant of the Pleioblastus simonii species.

It is characterized by its striking reddish-brown tips on the new shoots, which contrast with its green foliage. This bamboo is native to China and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is a clumping bamboo, forming dense stands that are often used in ornamental landscaping.

The plant is relatively low maintenance and can be propagated through division or rhizome cuttings.
