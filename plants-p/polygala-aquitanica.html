Polygala aquitanica

Polygala aquitanica, commonly known as the Aquitaine milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink, often arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it has a fibrous root system.

Polygala aquitanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
