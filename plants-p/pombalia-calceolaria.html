Pombalia calceolaria

Pombalia calceolaria, commonly known as the slipper orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, pouch-like flower that resembles a slipper, which is a key feature in its common name. The plant typically grows in shady, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom during the warmer months.

Pombalia calceolaria is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
