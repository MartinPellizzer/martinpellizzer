Phacelia marshall-johnstonii var. marshall-johnstonii

Phacelia marshall-johnstonii var. marshall-johnstonii, commonly known as Marshall-Johnston's phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
