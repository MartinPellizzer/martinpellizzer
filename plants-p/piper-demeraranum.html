Piper demeraranum

Piper demeraranum, commonly known as the devil's weed, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically green when mature and turn red upon ripening. It is known for its pungent, spicy aroma, which is due to the presence of various essential oils.

Due to its medicinal properties and potential toxicity, it is often cultivated with caution.
