Plectranthus striatus

Plectranthus striatus, commonly known as the purple heart plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking purple to violet leaves, which are often marked with white or silver veins. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus striatus is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
