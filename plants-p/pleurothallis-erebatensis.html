Pleurothallis erebatensis

Pleurothallis erebatensis, commonly known as the erect pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly found in the Andean regions of Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit, often growing on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in high-altitude cloud forests, where it benefits from the cool, moist climate and indirect sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
