Pyrus pannonica longicarpa

Pyrus pannonica longicarpa, commonly known as the long-fruited pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Carpathian Basin.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, edible fruits that can reach up to 15 cm in length. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while the flowers are white and clustered in corymbs. This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.

It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas across Central Europe.
