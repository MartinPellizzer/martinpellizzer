Pocockia arabica

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and succulent, helping to conserve water.
