Platylepis rufa

Platylepis rufa, commonly known as the red-flowered flatleaf heath, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to parts of southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, evergreen foliage, and clusters of small, showy red flowers that bloom in spring. It is adapted to dry, nutrient-poor environments and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
