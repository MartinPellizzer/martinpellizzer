Pyrus hondoensis

Pyrus hondoensis, commonly known as the Hondo pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the island of Hondo, which is part of the Ryukyu Islands in Japan. The plant is characterized by its small, deciduous leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like fruit, is edible but not widely cultivated.

Pyrus hondoensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to its island habitat.
