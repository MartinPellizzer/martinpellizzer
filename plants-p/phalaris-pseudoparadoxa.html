Phalaris pseudoparadoxa

Phalaris pseudoparadoxa, commonly known as the ghost grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, dark green leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in late summer. The plant produces distinctive, elongated seed heads that can resemble ghostly shapes, contributing to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is noted for its unique morphology and ecological adaptability.
