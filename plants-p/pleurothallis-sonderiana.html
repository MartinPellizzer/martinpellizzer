Pleurothallis sonderiana

Pleurothallis sonderiana, commonly known as the Sonder's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the understory of tropical forests. The plant features small, delicate flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, often with a slight greenish tint. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral characteristics.
