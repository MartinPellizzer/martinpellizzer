Pilea aquarum subsp. brevicornuta

Pilea aquarum subsp.

brevicornuta, commonly known as the water pilea, is a rare aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia. It features delicate, floating leaves that are typically oval or rounded in shape.

The plant has a short, stout stem and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments with moderate light exposure.

Its unique morphology makes it a sought-after species for specialized aquatic gardens and conservation efforts.
