Pterygota bureavii

Pterygota bureavii, commonly known as the Bureavii winged fern, is a unique plant characterized by its distinctive pteridophyte features.

It belongs to the group of plants that reproduce via spores rather than seeds, showcasing a primitive reproductive system. The plant exhibits a fern-like structure with fronds that have a complex arrangement of leaves, or pinnae. Its leaves are often adorned with specialized structures called indusia, which protect the developing sporangia.

Pterygota bureavii is typically found in moist, shaded environments, thriving in tropical and subtropical regions.
