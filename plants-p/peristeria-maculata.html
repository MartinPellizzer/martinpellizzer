Peristeria maculata

Peristeria maculata, commonly known as the spotted nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple. It produces small, toxic berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe.

Due to its ornamental value and toxicity, it is often cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling.
