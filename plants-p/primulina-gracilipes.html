Primulina gracilipes

Primulina gracilipes, commonly known as the slender leaf primulina, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically produces small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which are arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy texture, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
