Pomaderris betulina subsp. actensis

Pomaderris betulina subsp.

actensis, commonly known as the Actean Pomaderris, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or heathlands.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger leaves and more robust growth compared to other Pomaderris species.
