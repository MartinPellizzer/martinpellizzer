Papaver nudicaule var. seticarpum

Papaver nudicaule var. seticarpum, commonly known as the clustered poppy, is a variant of the meadow poppy characterized by its distinctive clustered flowers and hairy stems.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant produces small, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a yellow center. Its leaves are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
