Poa densiflora

Poa densiflora, commonly known as denseflower bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are tightly packed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Poa densiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
