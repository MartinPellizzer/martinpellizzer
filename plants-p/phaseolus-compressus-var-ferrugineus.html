Phaseolus compressus var. ferrugineus

Phaseolus compressus var. ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty bush bean, is a leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, slender pods containing several kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
