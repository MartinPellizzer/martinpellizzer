Plantago virginica var. viridescens

Plantago virginica var. viridescens, commonly known as the greenleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides, fields, and wetlands. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are usually a vibrant green, with a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in spikes during the summer months.

This variety is often used in herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
