Penstemon procerus f. albescens

Penstemon procerus f. albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white, fuzzy hairs. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are predominantly white or pale pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to rocky or well-drained soils.
