Pomaderris paniculosa subsp. paralia

Pomaderris paniculosa subsp.

paralia, commonly known as beach pomaderris, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is adapted to sandy soils and salt-laden environments, making it resilient to coastal conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for coastal landscaping.
