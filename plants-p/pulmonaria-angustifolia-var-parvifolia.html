Pulmonaria angustifolia var. parvifolia

Pulmonaria angustifolia var. parvifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf lungwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with silver or violet. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, partially shaded environments and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

The leaves and flowers contain various compounds, making it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
