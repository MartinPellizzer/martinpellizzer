Pleurothallis venosa

Pleurothallis venosa, commonly known as the Veined Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and often have a distinctive veined pattern on the petals.

Pleurothallis venosa is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
