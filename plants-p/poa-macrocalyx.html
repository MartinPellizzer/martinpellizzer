Poa macrocalyx

Poa macrocalyx, commonly known as the giant bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces spikelets that are typically blue-green in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Poa macrocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in natural habitats.
