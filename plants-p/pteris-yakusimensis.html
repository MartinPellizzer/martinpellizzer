Pteris yakusimensis

Pteris yakusimensis, commonly known as the Yakushima Brake, is a species of fern native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions with rich, well-drained soil. Its spores are dispersed through the wind, contributing to its propagation in suitable habitats.

The botanical features of Pteris yakusimensis make it a distinctive and visually appealing member of the fern family.
