Protea glaberrima

Protea glaberrima, commonly known as the silver protea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its striking, cone-shaped inflorescence covered in dense, white to pale pink bracts. The plant is typically found in fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered within the bracts, and it is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.

Protea glaberrima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of the unique biodiversity of South Africa.
