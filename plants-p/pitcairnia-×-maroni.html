Pitcairnia × maroni

Pitcairnia × maroni, commonly known as the hybrid pitcairnia, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a terrestrial epiphyte, often found growing on trees in humid tropical environments. The plant features rosette-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy coating, which helps retain moisture. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by colorful bracts, often in shades of red or orange.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in many botanical gardens and private collections.
