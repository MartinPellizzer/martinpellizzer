Pyrus ligustrifolia

Pyrus ligustrifolia, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white flowers and edible fruits. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin. The fruit is a pome, typically white or yellow in color, and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
