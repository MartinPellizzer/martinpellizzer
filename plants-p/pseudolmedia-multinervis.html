Pseudolmedia multinervis

Pseudolmedia multinervis, commonly known as the broad-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers have a complex structure with multiple petals and sepals, typical of the orchid family.

Pseudolmedia multinervis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
