Plectranthus zenkeri

Plectranthus zenkeri, commonly known as the African violet or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for both garden and indoor cultivation.

Plectranthus zenkeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown as a decorative plant.
