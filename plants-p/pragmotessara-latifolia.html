Pragmotessara latifolia

Pragmotessara latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf pragmotessara, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, which bloom during the rainy season. Its root system is fibrous and well-developed, allowing it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Pragmotessara latifolia is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
