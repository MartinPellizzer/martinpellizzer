Peperomia antonii var. fertilior

Peperomia antonii var. fertilior, commonly known as the silver mounding peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to South America.

It features dense, velvety foliage with a silvery-gray hue, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers under suitable conditions. It thrives in well-draining soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

This variety is popular as a houseplant due to its ease of care and ornamental appeal.
