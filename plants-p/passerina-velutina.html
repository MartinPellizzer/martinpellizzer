Passerina velutina

Passerina velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf pasqueflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Passerina velutina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
