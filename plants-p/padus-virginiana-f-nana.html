Padus virginiana f. nana

Padus virginiana f. nana, commonly known as dwarf American whitebeam, is a compact form of the American whitebeam shrub.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and produces small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, acidic soils. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to native plant gardens.
