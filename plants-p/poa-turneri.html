Poa turneri

Poa turneri, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, often forming dense mats in mountainous regions. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its inflorescence consists of slender spikelets that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

Poa turneri plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
