Polygala quartiniana var. minor

Polygala quartiniana var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces delicate, spiral-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice among gardeners interested in native and drought-tolerant plants.
