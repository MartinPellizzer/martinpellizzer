Parodia neglectoides

Parodia neglectoides, commonly known as the "Neglectoid" cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a small, globular cactus with a thick, ribbed stem that can store water efficiently. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically short-lived but striking. Its spines are relatively soft and sparse, making it less intimidating than other cacti.

Parodia neglectoides is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its slow growth and low maintenance requirements.
