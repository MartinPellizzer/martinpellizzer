Pterocarpus megalocarpus

Pterocarpus megalocarpus, commonly known as the kingwood or ironwood tree, is a tropical hardwood species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree produces distinctive, flat, winged fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it flowers with yellowish or reddish blooms.

The plant is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.
