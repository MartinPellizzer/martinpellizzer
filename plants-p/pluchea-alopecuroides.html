Pluchea alopecuroides

Pluchea alopecuroides, commonly known as Mexican sunflower or yellow mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and marshes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers produce yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
