Polygala mucronata

Polygala mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds.

Polygala mucronata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
