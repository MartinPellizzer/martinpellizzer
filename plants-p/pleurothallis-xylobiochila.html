Pleurothallis xylobiochila

Pleurothallis xylobiochila, commonly known as the "spiny pleurothallis," is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to conserve moisture in its humid, shaded habitat.

It is a relatively rare species, making it a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
