Polygonum ferrugineum var. incanum

Polygonum ferrugineum var. incanum, commonly known as the silverleaf knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which results from dense pubescence covering the leaves. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
