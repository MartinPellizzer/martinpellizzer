Prunus sargentii var. verecunda

Prunus sargentii var. verecunda, commonly known as the Sargent cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, single or double pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 feet.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms.
