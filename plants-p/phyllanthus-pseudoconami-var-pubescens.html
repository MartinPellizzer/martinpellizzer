Phyllanthus pseudoconami var. pubescens

Phyllanthus pseudoconami var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy phyllanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy foliage, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded or moist environments. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a rough texture due to the presence of fine hairs.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
