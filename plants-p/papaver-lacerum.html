Papaver lacerum

Papaver lacerum, commonly known as the scarlet poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking red, papery petals and a slender, erect stem. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky soils.

Papaver lacerum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
