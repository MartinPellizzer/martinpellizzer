Pyrus meridionalis

Pyrus meridionalis, commonly known as the southern pear.

It is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces edible pears that are typically small to medium in size, with a crisp, sweet flesh. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in horticulture.
