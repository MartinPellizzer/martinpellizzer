Pulegium tomentellum

Pulegium tomentellum, commonly known as woolly pulegium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of blue or purple. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often growing in moist, rocky habitats.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
