Pinus massoniana var. henryi

Pinus massoniana var. henryi, commonly known as the Chinese fir, is a variant of the Masson's fir native to China.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The leaves are typically dark green and arranged in spirals along the branches. It produces small, woody cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its timber and is often used in reforestation efforts due to its adaptability to various soil types.
