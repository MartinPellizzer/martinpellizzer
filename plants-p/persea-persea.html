Persea persea

Persea persea, commonly known as the avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces a large, pear-shaped fruit with a smooth, green skin. The fruit contains a single large seed surrounded by a rich, creamy pulp that is highly valued for its nutritional content. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it both economically and ecologically significant in its native and cultivated regions.
