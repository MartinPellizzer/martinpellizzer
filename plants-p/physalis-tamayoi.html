Physalis tamayoi

Physalis tamayoi, commonly known as the golden gooseberry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its hairy, yellow-orange calyces that enclose the small, edible berries. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are often covered in a papery, inflated husk. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are typically white or pale green.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a fruit crop in certain regions.
