Piper prunifolium

Piper prunifolium, commonly known as the purpleleaf pepper plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that often display a striking purple or reddish hue. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that eventually develop into small, round berries.

These berries are edible and have a slightly spicy flavor, making the plant both ornamental and potentially useful in culinary applications.
