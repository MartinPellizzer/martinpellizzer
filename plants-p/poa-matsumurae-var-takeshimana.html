Poa matsumurae var. takeshimana

Poa matsumurae var. takeshimana, commonly known as the Shikoku bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to Japan.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growths and is characterized by its fine, hairless blades. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is small and inconspicuous.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
