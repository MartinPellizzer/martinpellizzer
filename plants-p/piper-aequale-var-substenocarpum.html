Piper aequale var. substenocarpum

Piper aequale var. substenocarpum, commonly known as the narrow-fruit pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper aequale species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a distinctive, narrow fruit shape and a strong, pungent aroma.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary properties.
