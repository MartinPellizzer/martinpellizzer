Polygonum nodosum var. incarnatum

Polygonum nodosum var. incarnatum, commonly known as the redstem smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems that are often reddish in color, especially when mature. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and typically have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red, blooming from late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.
