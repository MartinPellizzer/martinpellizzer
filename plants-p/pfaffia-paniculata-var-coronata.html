Pfaffia paniculata var. coronata

Pfaffia paniculata var. coronata, commonly known as sacred lotus or yellow lotus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish. Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and medicinal properties.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and botanical collections.
