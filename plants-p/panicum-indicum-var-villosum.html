Panicum indicum var. villosum

Panicum indicum var. villosum, commonly known as hairy barnyardgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, leafy stems that often form a mat-like structure. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with bristly bracts, which are typically greenish to reddish in color. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along waterways.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a common weed in many parts of the world.
