Pithecellobium velutinum

Pithecellobium velutinum, commonly known as the velvet acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is distinguished by its soft, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences.

The seeds are enclosed in narrow, fibrous pods and are typically dispersed by wind or animals.
