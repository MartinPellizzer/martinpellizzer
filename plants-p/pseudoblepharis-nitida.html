Pseudoblepharis nitida

Pseudoblepharis nitida, commonly known as the narrowleaf blepharis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Pseudoblepharis nitida is also known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
