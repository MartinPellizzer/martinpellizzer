Phyllanthus nanellus

Phyllanthus nanellus, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a small shrub native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes, including treating liver ailments and skin conditions.
