Potentilla gaudinii proles longifolia

Potentilla gaudinii proles longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved potentilla, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage of dark green, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its hardy nature and ability to attract pollinators.
