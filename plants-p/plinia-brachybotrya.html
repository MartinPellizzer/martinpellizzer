Plinia brachybotrya

Plinia brachybotrya, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is typically small, round, and has a sweet, aromatic pulp with a texture similar to a star fruit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its ability to provide shade and support biodiversity in its native habitat.
