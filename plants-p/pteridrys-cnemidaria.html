Pteridrys cnemidaria

Pteridrys cnemidaria, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a species of aquatic fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and streams, where it thrives in moist, shaded conditions. The plant's rhizomes spread along the substrate, anchoring it firmly while absorbing nutrients from the surrounding water. Its leaves are usually elongated and narrow, with a feathery appearance that helps increase surface area for photosynthesis.

Pteridrys cnemidaria plays a role in maintaining water quality by filtering sediments and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
