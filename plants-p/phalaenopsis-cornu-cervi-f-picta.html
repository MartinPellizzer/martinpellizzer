Phalaenopsis cornu-cervi f. picta

Phalaenopsis cornu-cervi f. picta, commonly known as the painted horned orchid, is a hybrid orchid that exhibits striking botanical features.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The flowers are pendulous, with a unique coloration pattern that includes shades of pink, white, and purple, often with intricate markings. This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a houseplant.

Its growth habit is sympodial, with pseudobulbs that support its upright and compact structure.
