Pyrus calocarpa

Pyrus calocarpa, commonly known as the golden crabapple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruits that ripen in late summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval-shaped with a smooth margin. It produces white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are clustered in inflorescences.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in a variety of soil types.
