Paspalum propinquum

Paspalum propinquum, commonly known as the common paspalum, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Paspalum propinquum is valued for its use in erosion control and as a forage crop for livestock.
