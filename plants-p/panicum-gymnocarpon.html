Panicum gymnocarpon

Panicum gymnocarpon, commonly known as the smooth panicgrass, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico, typically growing in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous slender, hairless spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Panicum gymnocarpon is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and withstand drought conditions.
