Picea mariana var. brevifolia

Picea mariana var. brevifolia, commonly known as the dwarf spruce, is a variant of the white spruce native to northern North America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and short, dense shoots. The needles are dark green, stiff, and have a slightly resinous scent. It typically grows in boreal forests and is adapted to cold climates and nutrient-poor soils.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardiness.
