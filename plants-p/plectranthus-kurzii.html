Plectranthus kurzii

Plectranthus kurzii, commonly known as the velvetleaf plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are a striking deep purple to dark green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

Plectranthus kurzii is often used as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
