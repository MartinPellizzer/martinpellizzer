Prunus × lannesiana f. miyako

Prunus × lannesiana f. miyako, commonly known as the Miyako cherry, is a unique botanical hybrid belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a compact, ornamental shrub or small tree known for its vibrant, double pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and red-orange bark that becomes more pronounced with age. Native to Japan, it thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid that showcases the genetic diversity within the Prunus genus.
