Potamogeton turionifer var. mucronulatus

Potamogeton turionifer var. mucronulatus, commonly known as the sharp-tipped pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces turions, which are specialized vegetative propagules that allow it to survive harsh conditions and regenerate quickly. These turions are often found at the water's edge or in shallow areas.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
