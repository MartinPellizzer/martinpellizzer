Platea bullata

Platea bullata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or bullate platea, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that have a distinctive velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Platea bullata is often found in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
