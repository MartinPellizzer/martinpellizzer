Phyllorkis leopardina

Phyllorkis leopardina, commonly known as the leopard orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, leopard-like patterns on its leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming during the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
