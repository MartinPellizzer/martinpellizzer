Paphiopedilum richardianum

Paphiopedilum richardianum, commonly known as the Richardian Lady's Slipper, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a broad, fleshy rhizome and produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and thrives in well-drained, loamy soil. Its flowers are usually pink or purple with a yellow lip, and it blooms once or twice a year.

Paphiopedilum richardianum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
