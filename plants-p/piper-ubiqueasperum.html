Piper ubiqueasperum

Piper ubiqueasperum, commonly known as the rough-skinned pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and elongated, heart-shaped leaves. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is notable for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
