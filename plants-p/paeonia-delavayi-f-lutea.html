Paeonia delavayi f. lutea

Paeonia delavayi f. lutea, commonly known as the Yellow Peony, is a hybrid form of the Peony plant native to China.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in early spring and have a distinctive, fragrant aroma. The plant features large, compound leaves with a serrated edge, and its stems are covered in a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This variety is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and its historical significance in traditional Chinese horticulture.
