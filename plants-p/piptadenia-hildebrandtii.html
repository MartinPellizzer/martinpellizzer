Piptadenia hildebrandtii

Piptadenia hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Brazilian cacao tree, is a tropical legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with 10 to 20 pairs of leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. It is valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of cocoa.

The tree also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
