Plantago alismatifolia f. supina

Plantago alismatifolia f. supina, commonly known as the prostrate plantain, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its fibrous roots and succulent leaves make it a hardy plant with potential ecological and medicinal uses.
