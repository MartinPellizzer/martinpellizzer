Potamogeton lucens var. longipedunculatus

Potamogeton lucens var. longipedunculatus, commonly known as the long-stemmed pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It features long, slender leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, which can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, with the male flowers typically located above the female ones. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its native habitats.
