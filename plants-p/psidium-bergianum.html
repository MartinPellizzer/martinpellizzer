Psidium bergianum

Psidium bergianum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or surinam cherry, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green, and it blooms with small white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit is often used in culinary applications, while the plant itself is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
