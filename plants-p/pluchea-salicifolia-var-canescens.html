Pluchea salicifolia var. canescens

Pluchea salicifolia var. canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in a fine, silvery-gray pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-white composite flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often have a fuzzy texture.

It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.
