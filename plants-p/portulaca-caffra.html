Portulaca caffra

Portulaca caffra, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, succulent perennial that thrives in hot, arid climates. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. Its fleshy leaves are thick and waxy, which helps it conserve water.

Portulaca caffra is often used in ornamental gardening for its drought tolerance and colorful blooms.
