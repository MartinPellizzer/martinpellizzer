Polemonium lanatum subsp. boreale

Polemonium lanatum subsp.

boreale, commonly known as the northern bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to northern regions of North America. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, downy coating. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and valuable component of woodland ecosystems.
