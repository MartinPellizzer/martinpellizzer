Pollinia filiformis

Pollinia filiformis, commonly known as the slender pollinia orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family characterized by its specialized pollination structures.

The plant produces elongated, thread-like pollinia that are adhered to the insect vectors, facilitating efficient pollination. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a complex morphology adapted for specific pollinators. The botanical features of Pollinia filiformis are closely linked to its ecological niche in tropical and subtropical environments.

This orchid is often found in shaded, moist habitats, where it thrives in the understory of forests.
