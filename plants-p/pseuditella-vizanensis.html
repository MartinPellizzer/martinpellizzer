Pseuditella vizanensis

Pseuditella vizanensis, commonly known as the Vizan's false mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to specific regions of southern Europe.

It attaches itself to host trees, primarily oaks, and derives some of its nutrients from the host while also performing photosynthesis. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces inconspicuous white or yellow flowers. It is often found in woodland habitats and is relatively rare.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique parasitic relationship and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
