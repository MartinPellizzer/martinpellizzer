Pinalia salwinensis

Pinalia salwinensis, commonly known as the Salwin pine, is a rare and unique coniferous species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect trunk and needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in spirals. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate, which contributes to its rarity in the wild. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Pinalia salwinensis is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
