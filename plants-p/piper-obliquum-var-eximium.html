Piper obliquum var. eximium

Piper obliquum var. eximium, commonly known as the eximium pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper obliquum species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, slender fruits. These fruits are typically harvested when they turn a vibrant red or orange, indicating maturity.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and unique flavor profile, often used in traditional culinary and medicinal practices.
