Patagonium aphananthum

Patagonium aphananthum, commonly known as the Patagonian burr.

It is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by distinctive, spiny fruits. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to the harsh climate of the Patagonian region.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in local ecosystems.
