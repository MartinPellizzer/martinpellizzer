Physalis caudella var. parva

Physalis caudella var. parva, commonly known as the dwarf strawberry bush, is a compact shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features small, hairy leaves and produces clusters of orange, lantern-shaped fruits that are edible when fully mature. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant fruit and drought tolerance.

It is often used in gardens for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
