Pitcairnia attenuata

Pitcairnia attenuata, commonly known as the velvet leaf or the Brazilian velvet plant, is a species of bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety blue-green leaves that form a rosette shape, which helps collect rainwater. The plant produces inflorescences with bright red flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and conservation efforts.
