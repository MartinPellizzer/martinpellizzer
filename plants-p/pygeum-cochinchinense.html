Pygeum cochinchinense

Pygeum cochinchinense, commonly known as the Vietnamese tamarind tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, edible seeds. Its botanical features make it an important species in tropical ecosystems.

The plant is also valued for its timber and traditional medicinal uses.
