Pheidochloa

Pheidochloa, commonly known as the silvergrass or silverblades, is a genus of grasses native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

These plants typically grow in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, meadows, and along stream banks. They are characterized by their fine, silver-colored foliage, which gives them their common name. Pheidochloa species often have slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences.
