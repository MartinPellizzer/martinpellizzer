Phillyrea latifolia var. serrata

Phillyrea latifolia var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated Phillyrea, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it its botanical name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, leathery fruits. It is adapted to rocky soils and coastal environments, showing tolerance to drought and salt spray.

Its evergreen nature makes it a hardy and ornamental plant in Mediterranean climates.
