Phanera campanulata

Phanera campanulata, commonly known as the bellflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly rough texture.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, making it an important species in its native ecosystems.
