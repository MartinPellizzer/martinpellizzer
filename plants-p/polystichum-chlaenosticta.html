Polystichum chlaenosticta

Polystichum chlaenosticta, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds that are densely covered with a distinctive golden-yellow spore-bearing structures called sori. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a rigid, upright structure that gives it a formal appearance.

The golden shield fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
