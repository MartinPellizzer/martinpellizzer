Phyllanthus kunthiana

Phyllanthus kunthiana, commonly known as the stonebreaker or false pomegranate, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The plant produces small, round, fleshy fruits that resemble pomegranates but are not related. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Phyllanthus kunthiana is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and can tolerate drought conditions.
