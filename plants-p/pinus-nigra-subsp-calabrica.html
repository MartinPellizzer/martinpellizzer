Pinus nigra subsp. calabrica

Pinus nigra subsp.

calabrica, commonly known as the Italian black pine, is a subspecies of the black pine native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a medium to tall evergreen conifer with long, needle-like leaves that are typically dark green in color.

The cones of this subspecies are woody and can reach lengths of up to 10 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
