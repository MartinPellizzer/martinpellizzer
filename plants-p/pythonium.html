Pythonium

Pythonium, commonly known as the serpent vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant's most distinctive characteristic is its serpentine-shaped fruit, which resembles a coiled snake. Pythonium thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
