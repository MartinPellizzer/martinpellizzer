Phaeiris × nelsonii

Phaeiris × nelsonii, commonly known as the hybrid phaeiris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Phaeiris caerulea and Phaeiris glabra.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous plant with distinctive blue-green foliage and produces small, showy flowers in shades of blue and purple. The plant is native to South America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded garden settings.

Phaeiris × nelsonii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
