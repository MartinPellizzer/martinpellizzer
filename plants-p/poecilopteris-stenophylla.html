Poecilopteris stenophylla

Poecilopteris stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed water fern, is a prehistoric plant belonging to the genus Poecilopteris.

It is characterized by its fronds with a distinct, narrow leaf shape and a complex venation pattern. This plant thrived during the late Paleozoic era, particularly in swampy and wetland environments. Its fossils are often found in coal-bearing strata, indicating its role in ancient ecosystems.

The study of Poecilopteris stenophylla provides valuable insights into the evolution of early ferns and paleoenvironmental conditions.
