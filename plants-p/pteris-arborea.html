Pteris arborea

Pteris arborea, commonly known as the tree fern, is a species of fern characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery fronds.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific. The plant has a rhizome that grows vertically, supporting the upright structure of the fern. Its fronds are bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive, glossy appearance that contributes to its ornamental value.

Pteris arborea thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
