Phania matricarioides

Phania matricarioides, commonly known as the common groundsel, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The species is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
