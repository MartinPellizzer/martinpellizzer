Piper regresivanum

Piper regresivanum, commonly known as the backward-growing pepper plant, is a unique species within the Piper genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive growth pattern, where the shoots grow downward rather than upward, creating an unusual appearance. Native to the cloud forests of South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a bright red color.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
