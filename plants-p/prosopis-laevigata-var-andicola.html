Prosopis laevigata var. andicola

Prosopis laevigata var. andicola, commonly known as the Andean mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly the Andean regions.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ecological role and has traditional uses in local communities.
