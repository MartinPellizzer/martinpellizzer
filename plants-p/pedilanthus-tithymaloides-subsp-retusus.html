Pedilanthus tithymaloides subsp. retusus

Pedilanthus tithymaloides subsp.

retusus, commonly known as the black orchid, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its striking appearance, it is cultivated as an ornamental plant in some regions.
