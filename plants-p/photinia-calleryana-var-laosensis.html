Photinia calleryana var. laosensis

Photinia calleryana var. laosensis, commonly known as the Laos photinia, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Due to its attractive foliage and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and urban environments.
