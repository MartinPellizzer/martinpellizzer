Psiguria trilobata

Psiguria trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in tropical and subtropical regions.

It possesses a distinctive appearance with its three-lobed leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it produces its own chlorophyll for partial photosynthesis. Its fruit is typically a small, fleshy berry that attracts birds, aiding in seed dispersal.

Psiguria trilobata plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat, influencing host tree health and supporting bird populations.
