Persea montana

Persea montana, commonly known as the mountain avocado, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits with a rough, greenish-brown skin and a creamy, nutty-flavored pulp. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a dense canopy and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

Persea montana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in its native habitat.
