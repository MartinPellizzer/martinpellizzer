Paspalum jaguaense

Paspalum jaguaense, commonly known as jagua grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.

Paspalum jaguaense is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
