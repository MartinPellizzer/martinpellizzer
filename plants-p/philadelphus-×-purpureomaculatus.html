Philadelphus × purpureomaculatus

Philadelphus × purpureomaculatus, commonly known as the spotted mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive red or purple speckling on the petals. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy beneath. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

The shrub is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and pleasant floral scent.
