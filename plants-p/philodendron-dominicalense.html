Philodendron dominicalense

Philodendron dominicalense, commonly known as the Dominican philodendron, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of the Caribbean, particularly the Dominican Republic.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and inflorescence structure. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with light green and white patterns, making it a popular ornamental species. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread through underground stems, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The flowers, though small and inconspicuous, are enclosed within a spadix and surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.
