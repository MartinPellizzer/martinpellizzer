Pancheria obovata var. crassifolia

Pancheria obovata var. crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved pancheria, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features broad, ovate leaves that are often densely covered with fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
