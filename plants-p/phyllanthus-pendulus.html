Phyllanthus pendulus

Phyllanthus pendulus, commonly known as the hanging sandmat, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically opposite or whorled. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often followed by small, rounded fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Phyllanthus pendulus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
