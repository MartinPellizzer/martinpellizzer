Phytolacca exiensis

Phytolacca exiensis, commonly known as the Chinese pokeweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture and can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark purple berries. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often containing toxic compounds that require caution during handling.

Phytolacca exiensis is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is part of the Euphorbiaceae family.
