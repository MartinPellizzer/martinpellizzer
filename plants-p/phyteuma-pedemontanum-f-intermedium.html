Phyteuma pedemontanum f. intermedium

Phyteuma pedemontanum f. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, exhibiting a compact growth habit. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
