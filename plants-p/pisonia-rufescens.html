Pisonia rufescens

Pisonia rufescens, commonly known as the red-leaf pisonia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal areas and dry forests. The plant is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often red or reddish-brown leaves. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and are typically white or pale pink in color.

Pisonia rufescens is adapted to tolerate salt spray and is often found in sandy or rocky soils along the coastline.
