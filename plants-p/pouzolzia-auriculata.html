Pouzolzia auriculata

Pouzolzia auriculata, commonly known as the earleaf pouzolzia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a distinctive ear-shaped base.

Pouzolzia auriculata is known for its ability to thrive in harsh coastal environments and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
