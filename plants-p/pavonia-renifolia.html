Pavonia renifolia

Pavonia renifolia, commonly known as the kidneyleaf pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its showy, pink to red bract-like flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Pavonia renifolia thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
