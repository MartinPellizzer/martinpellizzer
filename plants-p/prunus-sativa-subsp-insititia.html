Prunus sativa subsp. insititia

Prunus sativa subsp.

insititia, commonly known as the wild cherry or sour cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically red or black when mature, with a tart flavor.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. Its flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in clusters, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The species is closely related to the domesticated cherry varieties used in agriculture.
