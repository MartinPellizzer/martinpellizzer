Pedilonum sophronites

Pedilonum sophronites, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and grows in a shrub-like form, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its flowers are typically bell-shaped, with five petals that enclose the reproductive structures.

Pedilonum sophronites is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
