Passiflora hederacea

Passiflora hederacea, commonly known as the creeping passionflower, is a vigorous vine native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking flowers that feature a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and filaments. The plant produces blue or black berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Passiflora hederacea is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
