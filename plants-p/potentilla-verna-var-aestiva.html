Potentilla verna var. aestiva

Potentilla verna var. aestiva, commonly known as early potentilla, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 15-30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, white or yellowish flowers in early spring, which are borne on slender pedicels. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rounded to ovate shape with serrated edges.

This variety is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and rocky soils, thriving in partial shade to full sun.
