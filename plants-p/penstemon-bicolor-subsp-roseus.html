Penstemon bicolor subsp. roseus

Penstemon bicolor subsp.

roseus, commonly known as the rose-flowered beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its striking rose-purple to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open woodland or mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
