Papaver pinardianum

Papaver pinardianum, commonly known as the Pinard's poppy, is a species of flowering plant in the family Papaveraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting black center. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, typically growing in alpine or rocky environments.

The seeds of Papaver pinardianum are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native habitat.
