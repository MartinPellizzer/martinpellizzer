Physosiphon bradei

Physosiphon bradei, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a unique aquatic fern species native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant thrives in shallow, still waters and is often found in swampy or marshy habitats. Its reproductive structures are specialized, with sporangia arranged in clusters along the fronds.

Physosiphon bradei plays an important ecological role in maintaining the biodiversity of its wetland ecosystems.
