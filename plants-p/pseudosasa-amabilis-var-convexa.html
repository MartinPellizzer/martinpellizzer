Pseudosasa amabilis var. convexa

Pseudosasa amabilis var. convexa, commonly known as the convex bamboo, is a variant of the amabilis bamboo species.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its thick, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and its structural properties in horticulture and landscaping.
