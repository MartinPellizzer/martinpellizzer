Polygonum tubulosum

Polygonum tubulosum, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes and stream banks. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in spikes, and the plant produces small, three-lobed seeds.

It is often found in wetland habitats and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects.
