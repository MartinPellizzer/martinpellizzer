Physolychnis uralensis

Physolychnis uralensis, commonly known as the Ural bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Asia, particularly Russia and China.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. The plant features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy, bell-shaped flowers are usually blue or violet, with five petals, and bloom in late summer.

Physolychnis uralensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
