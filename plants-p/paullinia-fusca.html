Paullinia fusca

Paullinia fusca, commonly known as the black soursop, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its dark, leathery leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a fruit that is typically dark purple to black when ripe, with a thick, bumpy rind. Its botanical features include a robust root system and the ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses.
