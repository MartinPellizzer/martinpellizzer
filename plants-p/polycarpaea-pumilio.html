Polycarpaea pumilio

Polycarpaea pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf polycarpaea, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or rocky areas. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of white or pale purple.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in semi-shaded conditions.
