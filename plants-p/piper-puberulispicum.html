Piper puberulispicum

Piper puberulispicum, commonly known as the hairy pubescent pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are green when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
