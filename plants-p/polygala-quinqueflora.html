Polygala quinqueflora

Polygala quinqueflora, commonly known as the five-flowered milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, often fragrant, five-lobed flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a lanceolate shape, while its stems are slender and somewhat woody.

The species is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
