Philodendron clarkei

Philodendron clarkei, commonly known as the Clarke's philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and prominent veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, green berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and greenhouses.
