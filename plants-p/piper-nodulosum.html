Piper nodulosum

Piper nodulosum, commonly known as the nodular pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by oval, red berries that are edible but have a spicy, peppery flavor.

Piper nodulosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of medicinal compounds.
