Pilea plicatidentata

Pilea plicatidentata, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, round, and waxy leaves that resemble coins, giving it its common name. The plant has a compact growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular houseplant.

Pilea plicatidentata is valued for its ornamental foliage and ease of care.
