Primula capitata subsp. craibiana

Primula capitata subsp.

craibiana, commonly known as the Sierra Madre primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its dense, showy clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forested areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are broadly ovate and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
