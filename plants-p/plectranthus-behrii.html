Plectranthus behrii

Plectranthus behrii, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive blue-green color. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of violet, pink, or white, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Plectranthus behrii is also valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
