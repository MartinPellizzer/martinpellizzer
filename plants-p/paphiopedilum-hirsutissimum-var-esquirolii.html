Paphiopedilum hirsutissimum var. esquirolii

Paphiopedilum hirsutissimum var. esquirolii, commonly known as the Esquiroli's velvet orchid, is a rare and striking terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and its unique, intricate flower structure, which features a prominent lip and spurred column. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers, which can be pale yellow to greenish-white, are highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to their beauty and rarity.

This variety is noted for its slow growth and specific care requirements, making it a challenging but rewarding plant for experienced growers.
