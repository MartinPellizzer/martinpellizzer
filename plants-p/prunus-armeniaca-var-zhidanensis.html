Prunus armeniaca var. zhidanensis

Prunus armeniaca var. zhidanensis, commonly known as the Zhidan peach, is a variant of the peach tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit. The leaves are typically dark green and ovate, while the flowers are pink to white, appearing in early spring. The fruit is generally smaller than common peaches and has a distinct, often aromatic flavor.

This variety is valued for its resilience to certain pests and its ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
