Pugetia acanthochlamys

Pugetia acanthochlamys, commonly known as the Pacific coast hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a deep red throat. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments.

Pugetia acanthochlamys plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for small wildlife.
