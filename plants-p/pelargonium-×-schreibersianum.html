Pelargonium × schreibersianum

Pelargonium × schreibersianum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive heart-shaped leaves that resemble ivy. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming throughout the summer. Native to South Africa, it is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant flowers and foliage.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for hanging baskets and container gardens.
