Plinia petiolata

Plinia petiolata, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its dense, dark purple berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with elliptical leaves that have a leathery texture. Its flowers are small and white, clustered in inflorescences, and it produces edible fruits that are rich in antioxidants.

The plant is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its use in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
