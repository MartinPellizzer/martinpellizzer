Pseudocarex

Pseudocarex, commonly known as false sedges, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Cyperaceae.

These plants are typically herbaceous and form dense clumps with triangular stems and narrow leaves. They are often found in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Pseudocarex species vary in height and leaf color, but they generally lack the showy flowers of other Cyperaceae genera.

Their ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
