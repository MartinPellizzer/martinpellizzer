Psidium dubium

Psidium dubium, commonly known as the sour sop or guanabana, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, spiky, green fruit that turns yellow when mature. The fruit's pulp is juicy and has a strong, musky aroma, with a flavor that is both sweet and tart.

It is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
