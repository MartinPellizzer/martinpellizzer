Polygonum serratum

Polygonum serratum, commonly known as the serrated knotweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other species like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant features upright stems with heart-shaped leaves that have serrated edges. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in summer, and its root system is fibrous and rhizomatous.

Polygonum serratum is often found in moist soils along streambanks and in wetlands.
