Pluchea carolinensis

Pluchea carolinensis, commonly known as Carolina blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
