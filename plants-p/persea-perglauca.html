Persea perglauca

Persea perglauca, commonly known as the pale green persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Central America and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible fruit that is typically harvested when fully mature, though it may remain on the tree for an extended period. Persea perglauca is often found in moist, shaded environments such as tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
