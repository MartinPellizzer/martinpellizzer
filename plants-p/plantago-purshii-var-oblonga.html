Plantago purshii var. oblonga

Plantago purshii var. oblonga, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures. Its flowering spikes produce small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
