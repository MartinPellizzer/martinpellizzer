Pastinacha

Pastinacha, commonly known as goatweed, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are annual or perennial herbs. The plants typically have slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Pastinacha species are native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

They are often found growing in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in meadows.
