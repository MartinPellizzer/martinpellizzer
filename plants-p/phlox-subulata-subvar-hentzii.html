Phlox subulata subvar. hentzii

Phlox subulata subvar. hentzii, commonly known as Hentz's sand phlox, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, silver-gray leaves that are adapted to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils. It is drought-tolerant and prefers full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
