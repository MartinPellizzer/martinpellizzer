Pseudolysimachion paniculatum subsp. foliosum

Pseudolysimachion paniculatum subsp.

foliosum, commonly known as the leafy panicled larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features upright, slender stems that bear whorled leaves with a lanceolate shape and a bluish-green hue.

The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, and it is typically found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its habitat preferences, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
