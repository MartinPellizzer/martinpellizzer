Polypodium felinum

Polypodium felinum, commonly known as the cat's tail fern, is a fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated fronds that resemble a cat's tail, giving it its common name. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping stems that can produce new plants. Its leaves are typically bioluminescent, emitting a faint glow in the dark.

Polypodium felinum is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
