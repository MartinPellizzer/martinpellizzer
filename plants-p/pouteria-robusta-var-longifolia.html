Pouteria robusta var. longifolia

Pouteria robusta var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved soursop, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are often elongated, giving it its specific epithet "longifolia.". The tree produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers that develop into large, spiny fruits resembling those of the soursop. The fruit has a sweet, aromatic pulp with a distinctive flavor, and the plant is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and timber.
