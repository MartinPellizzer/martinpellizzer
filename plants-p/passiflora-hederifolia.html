Passiflora hederifolia

Passiflora hederifolia, commonly known as the creeping passionflower, is a vigorous, trailing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, colorful flowers that feature a central crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and green spaces.
