Poa eragrostiformis

Poa eragrostiformis, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often hairy blades. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of spikelets, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

Poa eragrostiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
