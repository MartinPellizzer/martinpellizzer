Pentaphylloides fruticosa subsp. floribunda

Pentaphylloides fruticosa subsp.

floribunda, commonly known as the flowering five-leaf plant, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage with five leaflets per leaf, giving it its scientific name.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
