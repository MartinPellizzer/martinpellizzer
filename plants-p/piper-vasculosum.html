Piper vasculosum

Piper vasculosum, commonly known as the veined pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with prominent venation. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by berries that turn from yellow to red as they mature.

Piper vasculosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in folk medicine.
