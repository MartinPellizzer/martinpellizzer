Polemonium mexicanum

Polemonium mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican Jacob's ladder, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a stair-step pattern, giving it the common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
