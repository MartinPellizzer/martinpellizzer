Persea borbonia subsp. pubescens

Persea borbonia subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the hairy soursop, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands. It is characterized by its rough, bark-like leaves that are covered in fine pubescence, giving it a distinctive texture.

The plant produces large, egg-shaped fruits with a spiny exterior and a soft, white, fibrous pulp. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique foliage and potential medicinal properties.
