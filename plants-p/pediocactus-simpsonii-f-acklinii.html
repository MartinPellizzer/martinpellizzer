Pediocactus simpsonii f. acklinii

Pediocactus simpsonii f. acklinii, commonly known as the Acklin's cactus, is a rare and compact columnar cactus native to the arid regions of Baja California, Mexico.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with a smooth, pale green to bluish-green surface that becomes more spiny with age. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise subdued appearance. It is highly adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in the wild.
