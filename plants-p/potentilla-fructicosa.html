Potentilla fructicosa

Potentilla fructicosa, commonly known as cinquefoil, is a deciduous shrub native to the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has wiry, thorny stems and alternate, palmately compound leaves with five leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Potentilla fructicosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
