Protium heptaphyllum subsp. heptaphyllum

Protium heptaphyllum subsp.

heptaphyllum, commonly known as the seven-leafed garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and elliptical, glossy green leaves that are typically arranged in whorls of seven, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by yellowish-orange berries that contain several seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
