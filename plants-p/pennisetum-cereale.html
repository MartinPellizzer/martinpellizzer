Pennisetum cereale

Pennisetum cereale, commonly known as finger millet, is a flowering plant native to Africa and southern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikes of small, fluffy flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soils, making it a hardy crop for arid regions. Its seeds are enclosed in a hard shell and are typically processed into flour for food.

Finger millet is also valued for its high nutritional content, including calcium and iron.
