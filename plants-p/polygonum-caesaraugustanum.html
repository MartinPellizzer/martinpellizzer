Polygonum caesaraugustanum

Polygonum caesaraugustanum, commonly known as the Caesar Augustus knotweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist soils along stream banks and in wet meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinct veining pattern, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Polygonum caesaraugustanum is often found in disturbed habitats and can thrive in partial to full shade.
