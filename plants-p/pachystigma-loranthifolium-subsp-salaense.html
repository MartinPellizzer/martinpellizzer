Pachystigma loranthifolium subsp. salaense

Pachystigma loranthifolium subsp.

salaense, commonly known as the salaense mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in arid and semi-arid regions. It has small, elliptical leaves that are usually arranged in opposite pairs, and its flowers are typically white or pale green, with a distinctive, tubular shape. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are dispersed by birds, aiding in its propagation.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecological interactions within its native habitat.
