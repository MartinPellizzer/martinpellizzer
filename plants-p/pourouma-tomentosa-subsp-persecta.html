Pourouma tomentosa subsp. persecta

Pourouma tomentosa subsp.

persecta, commonly known as the silky bamboo or velvet bamboo, is a member of the Bambusoideae subfamily. It is native to the Amazon basin and is characterized by its tall, slender stems covered in a dense, velvety pubescence.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers, followed by triangular, winged fruits. Its leaves are elongated and have a smooth, waxy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and structural form.
