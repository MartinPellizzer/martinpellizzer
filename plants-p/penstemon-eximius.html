Penstemon eximius

Penstemon eximius, commonly known as the tall beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that is well adapted to dry, arid environments.

Penstemon eximius is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and drought tolerance.
