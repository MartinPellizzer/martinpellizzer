Plazia parviflora

Plazia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered plazia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the temperate regions of southern Europe.

It features delicate, clustered flowers that range in color from pale pink to white, typically blooming in early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and it thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sun exposure. Plazia parviflora is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.

It is also known for its subtle fragrance, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
