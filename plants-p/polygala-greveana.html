Polygala greveana

Polygala greveana, commonly known as the Cape jasmine, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often resemble those of the genus Jasminum. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a trailing or shrub-like habit. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
