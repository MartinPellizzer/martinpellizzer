Padus nakatakei

Padus nakatakei, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that mature in the fall.

It is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
