Pulsatilla montana var. europaea

Pulsatilla montana var. europaea, commonly known as the European pasqueflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, hairy stem with basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
