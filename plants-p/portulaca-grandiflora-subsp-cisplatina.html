Portulaca grandiflora subsp. cisplatina

Portulaca grandiflora subsp.

cisplatina, commonly known as the Brazilian sun rose, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves, which are adapted to arid environments.

The plant produces vibrant red, yellow, or orange flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its short growth cycle and drought tolerance make it a resilient ornamental plant.
