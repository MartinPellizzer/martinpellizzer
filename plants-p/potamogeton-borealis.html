Potamogeton borealis

Potamogeton borealis, commonly known as the northern pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to cold, temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, with male and female flowers usually occurring on separate plants.
