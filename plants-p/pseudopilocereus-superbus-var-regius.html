Pseudopilocereus superbus var. regius

Pseudopilocereus superbus var. regius, commonly known as the royal night bloomer, is a cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white to pale blue spines. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, typically in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance and nocturnal blooming habit, it is a popular ornamental plant in arid gardens.
