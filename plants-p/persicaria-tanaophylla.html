Persicaria tanaophylla

Persicaria tanaophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hogweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is native to regions of Asia, including China, India, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, riverbanks, and marshy areas.

Persicaria tanaophylla is often found in shaded or semi-shaded habitats and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.
