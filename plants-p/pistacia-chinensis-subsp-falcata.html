Pistacia chinensis subsp. falcata

Pistacia chinensis subsp.

falcata, commonly known as the Chinese pistache, is a deciduous tree native to China and parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a distinctive, narrow, sickle-shaped leaflets.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, sweet-tart drupes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The subspecies is notable for its adaptability to various climates and its role in traditional medicine.
