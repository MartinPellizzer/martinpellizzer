Pogostemon kachinensis

Pogostemon kachinensis, commonly known as the Kachin betel plant, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It is often cultivated for its leaves, which are used in traditional practices, particularly in the preparation of betel quids.

The plant's essential oils and medicinal properties have also been studied for their potential health benefits.
