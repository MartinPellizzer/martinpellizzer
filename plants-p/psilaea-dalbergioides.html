Psilaea dalbergioides

Psilaea dalbergioides, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes, and the seeds are enclosed in slender, cylindrical pods.

Psilaea dalbergioides is valued for its edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines and as a protein source.
