Piper filipedunculum

Piper filipedunculum, commonly known as the slender-stemmed pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its thin, woody stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature.
