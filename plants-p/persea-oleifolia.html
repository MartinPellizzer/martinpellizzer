Persea oleifolia

Persea oleifolia, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible when fully ripe. Its oil-rich seeds have been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Persea oleifolia thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade.
