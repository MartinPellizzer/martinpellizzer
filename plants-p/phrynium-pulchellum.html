Phrynium pulchellum

Phrynium pulchellum, commonly known as the golden arrow plant, is a tropical perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green patterns. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by colorful bracts, typically in shades of red or orange. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and landscape design.
