Polygonum glastifolium

Polygonum glastifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks. The plant features upright, branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have a distinctive waxy coating, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

It produces small, three-lobed berries that are a food source for various birds and wildlife.
