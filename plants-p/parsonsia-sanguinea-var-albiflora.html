Parsonsia sanguinea var. albiflora

Parsonsia sanguinea var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered parsonsia, is a striking tropical shrub native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red flowers, which contrast beautifully with its white petals, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near rivers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
