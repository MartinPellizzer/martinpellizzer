Parasenecio hastatus subsp. orientalis

Parasenecio hastatus subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern hairy gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and showy blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cooler climates. Its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped, contributing to its common name.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
