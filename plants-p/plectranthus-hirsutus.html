Plectranthus hirsutus

Plectranthus hirsutus, commonly known as the woolly plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus hirsutus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can also be grown as a houseplant.
