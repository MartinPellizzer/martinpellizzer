Podogyne

Podogyne, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of grasses. Podogyne is often found in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
