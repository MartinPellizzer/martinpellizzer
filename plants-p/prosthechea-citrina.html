Prosthechea citrina

Prosthechea citrina, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in the summer months. The plant has long, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in length, with small, alternate leaves. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Prosthechea citrina is valued in horticulture for its vibrant color and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
