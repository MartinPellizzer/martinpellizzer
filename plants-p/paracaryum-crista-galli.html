Paracaryum crista-galli

Paracaryum crista-galli, commonly known as the cock's comb, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescence that resembles a rooster's comb, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its vibrant red, pink, or purple bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Paracaryum crista-galli is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
