Passiflora eueidipabulum

Passiflora eueidipabulum, commonly known as the yellow passion flower, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets and produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

The species is also valued for its ornamental beauty and mild psychoactive properties in its flowers.
