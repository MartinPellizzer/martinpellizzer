Piper novogranatense

Piper novogranatense, commonly known as the granadilla or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into bright orange or red berries. These berries are edible and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor, often used in local cuisines.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and culinary contexts.
