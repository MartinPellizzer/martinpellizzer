Protohopea tinctoria

Protohopea tinctoria, commonly known as the red mangrove, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

Its flowers are typically small and clustered, and it produces dark, fleshy berries that contain its seeds.

Protohopea tinctoria plays an important ecological role in coastal ecosystems by stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
