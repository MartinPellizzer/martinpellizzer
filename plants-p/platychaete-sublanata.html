Platychaete sublanata

Platychaete sublanata, commonly known as the woolly flatleaf, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered in white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat, disc-like structure, typical of the genus Platychaete.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions.
