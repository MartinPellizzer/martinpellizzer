Pandorea ceramica

Pandorea ceramica, commonly known as the Ceramica climbing plant, is a woody vine native to Australia.

It features showy, fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in spring. The plant has compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets and produces small, dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Pandorea ceramica is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or fences.
