Pilea insolens

Pilea insolens, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern China and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that resemble small, round coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is a popular houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Pilea insolens thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
