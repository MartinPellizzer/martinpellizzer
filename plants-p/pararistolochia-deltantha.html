Pararistolochia deltantha

Pararistolochia deltantha, commonly known as the Delta horseshoe vine, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horseshoe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and along streams.
