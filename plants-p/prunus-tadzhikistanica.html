Prunus tadzhikistanica

Prunus tadzhikistanica, commonly known as the Tajikistan cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible but not widely cultivated for commercial use. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
