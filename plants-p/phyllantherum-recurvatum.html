Phyllantherum recurvatum

Phyllantherum recurvatum, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems that often curve in a zigzag pattern, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Phyllantherum recurvatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
