Potentilla saricana var. monstruosa

Potentilla saricana var. monstruosa, commonly known as the monstrous potentilla, is a striking shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to rocky landscapes. The plant has deeply lobed, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white fuzzy coating, enhancing its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among gardeners seeking to add bold visual interest to their landscapes.
