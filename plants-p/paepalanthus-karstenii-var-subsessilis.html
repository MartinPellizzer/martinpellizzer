Paepalanthus karstenii var. subsessilis

Paepalanthus karstenii var. subsessilis, commonly known as the Chilean burro's tail, is a succulent plant native to the Andes mountains of Chile.

It features dense clusters of fleshy, blue-green leaves that form a compact, mat-like structure. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by distinctive, spherical seed pods. It is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, thriving in full sun and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
