Poa filiformis

Poa filiformis, commonly known as slender meadowgrass, is a low-growing grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growths and has fine, thread-like leaves that are often covered in a fuzzy layer. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which contain both male and female flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along streambanks.

Poa filiformis is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various invertebrates.
