Pyrrhocactus setosiflorus

Pyrrhocactus setosiflorus, commonly known as the spiny-flowered cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its root system is adapted to absorb moisture efficiently from the dry soil.

Overall, Pyrrhocactus setosiflorus exemplifies the adaptive strategies of desert flora.
