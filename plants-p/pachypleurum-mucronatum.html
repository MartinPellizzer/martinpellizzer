Pachypleurum mucronatum

Pachypleurum mucronatum, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal conditions. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.
