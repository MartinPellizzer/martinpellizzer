Phlegmatospermum eremaeum

Phlegmatospermum eremaeum, commonly known as the desert phlegmatosperm, is a rare and ancient plant belonging to the order Phlegmatospermales.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring thick, fleshy stems and small, scale-like leaves adapted to arid environments. The plant produces distinctive, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are crucial for its reproductive cycle. It is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is considered a relic species with limited geographic distribution.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists studying evolutionary adaptations in extreme environments.
