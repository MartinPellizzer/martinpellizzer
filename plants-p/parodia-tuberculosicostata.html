Parodia tuberculosicostata

Parodia tuberculosicostata, commonly known as the Costate Parodia, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and distinctive, ribbed structure. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water.

Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
