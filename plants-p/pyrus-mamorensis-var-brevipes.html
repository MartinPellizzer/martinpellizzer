Pyrus mamorensis var. brevipes

Pyrus mamorensis var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed pear, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, rounded leaves. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for horticulturists and botanists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
