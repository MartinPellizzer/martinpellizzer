Panicum connivens

Panicum connivens, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed spikelets. Its flowers are typically greenish or reddish in color and are wind-pollinated.

Panicum connivens thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.
