Ponerorchis kiraishiensis

Ponerorchis kiraishiensis, commonly known as the Kiraishi's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated floral structure and its ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments. The plant exhibits a monopodial growth habit, with a single, upright stem that bears a single, showy flower. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of ghostly figures, hence its common name.

Due to its restricted habitat and limited distribution, Ponerorchis kiraishiensis is considered a critically endangered species.
