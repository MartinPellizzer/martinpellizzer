Philippia floribunda subsp. pilulifera

Philippia floribunda subsp.

pilulifera, commonly known as the red-flowered piper, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America. It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bright red flowers.

The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
