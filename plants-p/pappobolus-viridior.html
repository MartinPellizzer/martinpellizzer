Pappobolus viridior

Pappobolus viridior, commonly known as the green pappobolus, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions such as reduced leaf size and deep root systems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that aggregate into a single head, surrounded by bracts. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine layer of hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
