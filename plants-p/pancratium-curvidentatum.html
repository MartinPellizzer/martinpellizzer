Pancratium curvidentatum

Pancratium curvidentatum, commonly known as the Cape daffodil, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive red-orange center. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Pancratium curvidentatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
