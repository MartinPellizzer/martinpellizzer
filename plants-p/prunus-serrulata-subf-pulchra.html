Prunus serrulata subf. pulchra

Prunus serrulata subf.

pulchra, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or Somei Yoshino, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan. It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, preceding the leaves.

The plant has ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges, which give it its scientific name. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes, particularly for its aesthetic spring blossoms.
