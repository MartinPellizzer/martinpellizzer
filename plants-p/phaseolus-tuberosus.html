Phaseolus tuberosus

Phaseolus tuberosus, commonly known as the Andean tuber or oca, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its edible underground tubers that are rich in starch and nutrients. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has compound leaves with trifoliate structures. Its tubers are typically tubular in shape and come in various colors, including red, yellow, and purple.

The plant is cultivated for its nutritional value and is adapted to grow in cooler, high-altitude environments.
