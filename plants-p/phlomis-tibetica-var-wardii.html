Phlomis tibetica var. wardii

Phlomis tibetica var. wardii, commonly known as Tibetan phlomis, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its robust, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences of pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to its arid and cold environment.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
