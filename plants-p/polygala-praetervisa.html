Polygala praetervisa

Polygala praetervisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with a distinctive spurred structure.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
