Potamogeton franchetii

Potamogeton franchetii, commonly known as the Franchet's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, cylindrical stems that are often branched, and its leaves are narrow, ribbon-like, and arranged spirally along the stem. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.

Potamogeton franchetii is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
