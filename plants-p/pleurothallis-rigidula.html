Pleurothallis rigidula

Pleurothallis rigidula, commonly known as the rigid lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within the understory of forests. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the humid air.

Pleurothallis rigidula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
