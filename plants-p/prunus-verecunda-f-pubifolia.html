Prunus verecunda f. pubifolia

Prunus verecunda f. pubifolia, commonly known as the pubescent cherry, is a unique cultivar of the Prunus verecunda species.

It is characterized by its hairy or pubescent leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance compared to other cherry varieties. The plant typically produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and its potential for fruit production in suitable growing conditions.
