Persea cavaleriei

Persea cavaleriei, commonly known as the Chinese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange to reddish fruit with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. The fruit is typically harvested in autumn and is prized for its nutritional value and culinary uses.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for both landscaping and cultivation in subtropical regions.
