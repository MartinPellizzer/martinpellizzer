Polygonum striatulum var. texense

Polygonum striatulum var. texense, commonly known as the Texas knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color and covered with fine hairs. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive whitish or silver-colored midrib. Its small, clustered flowers are typically greenish-white and appear in late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other waterlogged habitats.
