Polygala filifera

Polygala filifera, commonly known as the threadleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish flowers, which are typically arranged in spicate inflorescences. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
