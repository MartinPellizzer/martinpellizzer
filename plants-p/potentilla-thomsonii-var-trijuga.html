Potentilla thomsonii var. trijuga

Potentilla thomsonii var. trijuga, commonly known as the Thomson's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from May to July. The plant has compound leaves composed of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing habitat for small wildlife.
