Piper cinerascens

Piper cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-colored pepper or black pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical berries containing the aromatic seeds used in spice blends. Its botanical structure supports its role as a climbing plant, with adventitious roots aiding in attachment to supports.

The leaves and fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
