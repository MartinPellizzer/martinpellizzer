Potamogeton × fluitans f. typicus

Potamogeton × fluitans f. typicus, commonly known as the floating pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically submerged, contributing to its reproductive strategy in aquatic habitats. It is often found in slow-moving or still waters such as ponds, lakes, and canals.

Its rapid growth can lead to dense vegetation, impacting water flow and aquatic ecosystems.
