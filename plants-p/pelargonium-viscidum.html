Pelargonium viscidum

Pelargonium viscidum, commonly known as the sticky geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its sticky, glandular hairs on its leaves and stems. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red, often blooming in spring and summer. Its leaves are typically rounded or heart-shaped, and the plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive appearance.

Pelargonium viscidum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.
