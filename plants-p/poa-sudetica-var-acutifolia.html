Poa sudetica var. acutifolia

Poa sudetica var. acutifolia, commonly known as the acuteleaf mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, acute leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous environments.
