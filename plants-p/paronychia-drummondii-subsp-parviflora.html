Paronychia drummondii subsp. parviflora

Paronychia drummondii subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as Drummond's sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink to lavender.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often found in open plains, rocky slopes, and along streambanks.
