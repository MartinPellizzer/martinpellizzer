Polyscias sambucifolia

Polyscias sambucifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sycamore, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value is due to its striking foliage, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
