Pygeum junghuhnii

Pygeum junghuhnii, commonly known as African wild almond, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable in some regions.
