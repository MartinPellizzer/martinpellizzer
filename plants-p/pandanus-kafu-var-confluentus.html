Pandanus kafu var. confluentus

Pandanus kafu var. confluentus, commonly known as the coastal pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional thatching. The plant thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often growing in brackish or saltwater environments. Its inflorescence consists of small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered at the tips of the leaves.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local cultures for practical and ceremonial uses.
