Paepalanthus erigeron var. pubescens

Paepalanthus erigeron var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy blueweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy leaves and stems, which give it its common name. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely known outside of botanical circles.
