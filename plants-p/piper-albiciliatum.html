Piper albiciliatum

Piper albiciliatum, commonly known as white-stalked pepper or white-stemmed pepper, is a tropical vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, white-veined stems and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Piper albiciliatum is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
