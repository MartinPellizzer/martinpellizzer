Phanera maximiliani

Phanera maximiliani, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the rainy season. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and showy, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
