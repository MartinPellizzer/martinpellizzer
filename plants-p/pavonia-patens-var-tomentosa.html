Pavonia patens var. tomentosa

Pavonia patens var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly pavonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
