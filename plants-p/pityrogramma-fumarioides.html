Pityrogramma fumarioides

Pityrogramma fumarioides, commonly known as the fumarioid fern, is a small, terrestrial fern belonging to the family Pityrogrammaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spores are produced in sori that are located on the underside of the fronds.

Pityrogramma fumarioides is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid conditions.
