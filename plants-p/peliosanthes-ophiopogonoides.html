Peliosanthes ophiopogonoides

Peliosanthes ophiopogonoides, commonly known as the Chinese yew or Japanese yew, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asparagaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces blue-black berries that are edible but should be consumed with caution due to their potential toxicity. Its ornamental value and medicinal properties have made it a subject of interest in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
