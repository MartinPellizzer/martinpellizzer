Picea purpurea var. hirtella

Picea purpurea var. hirtella, commonly known as the purple spruce, is a rare and unique variant of the spruce genus native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple-blue foliage, which is more intense than that of typical spruce species. The plant exhibits a conical shape with dense, compact growth, and its needles are short, stiff, and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil, often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
