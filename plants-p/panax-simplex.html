Panax simplex

Panax simplex, commonly known as American ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the forests of North America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, furred roots and its opposite, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red berries. It thrives in cool, moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade.

Panax simplex is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
