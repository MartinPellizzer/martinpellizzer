Prunus valida

Prunus valida, commonly known as the California wild cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple to black fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Prunus valida is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
