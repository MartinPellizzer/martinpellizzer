Peperomia toledoana

Peperomia toledoana, commonly known as the Toledo peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne on spikes, which are often hidden beneath the foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia toledoana is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal.
