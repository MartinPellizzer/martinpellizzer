Padus racemosa var. asiatica

Padus racemosa var. asiatica, commonly known as the Asian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its foliage provides seasonal color changes, making it a valuable ornamental plant in gardens.
