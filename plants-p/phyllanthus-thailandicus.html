Phyllanthus thailandicus

Phyllanthus thailandicus, commonly known as Thai phyllanthus, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
