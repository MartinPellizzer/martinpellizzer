Pleurothallis rubens var. longifolia

Pleurothallis rubens var. longifolia, commonly known as the red pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of red and pink. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with numerous blooms, which are often arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered in a dense, velvety pubescence.

This variety is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses due to its specific humidity and light requirements.
