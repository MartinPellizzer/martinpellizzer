Pseudobersama mossambicensis

Pseudobersama mossambicensis, commonly known as the Mossambique fern, is a unique aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that resemble those of a fern, though it is not a true fern. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as rivers and lakes, and prefers moderate to high light conditions. Its reproductive structures are inconspicuous, with spores released into the water rather than flowers.

Pseudobersama mossambicensis plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic life.
