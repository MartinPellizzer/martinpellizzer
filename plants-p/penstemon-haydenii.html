Penstemon haydenii

Penstemon haydenii, commonly known as Hayden's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Penstemon haydenii is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
