Paspalum inops

Paspalum inops, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Paspalum inops is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
