Pothos cuspidatus

Pothos cuspidatus, commonly known as the arrowhead vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that have a distinctive arrowhead shape, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in humid, warm environments and is often grown as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ornamental value. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, followed by orange-red berries that contain seeds.

Pothos cuspidatus is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
