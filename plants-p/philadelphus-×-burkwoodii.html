Philadelphus × burkwoodii

Philadelphus × burkwoodii, commonly known as the common mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

The shrub is also valued for its adaptability and resistance to pests and diseases.
