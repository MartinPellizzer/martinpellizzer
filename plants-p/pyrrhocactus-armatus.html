Pyrrhocactus armatus

Pyrrhocactus armatus, commonly known as the armed pyrrhocactus, is a unique cactus species native to the deserts of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, spiny bracts that provide protection against herbivores. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, adding a subtle ornamental value. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized desert gardens.
