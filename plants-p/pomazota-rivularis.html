Pomazota rivularis

Pomazota rivularis, commonly known as the riverweed, is a flowering plant native to wetland and aquatic environments.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water's surface. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.

Pomazota rivularis is typically found in slow-moving or still freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes.
