Piper tecutlanum

Piper tecutlanum, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is a climbing vine native to Mexico and Central America, characterized by its small, opposite leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The botanical features of Piper tecutlanum make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ethnobotanical study.
