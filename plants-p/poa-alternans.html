Poa alternans

Poa alternans, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a species of grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Poa alternans plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various small invertebrates.
