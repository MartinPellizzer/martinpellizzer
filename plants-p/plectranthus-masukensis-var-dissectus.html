Plectranthus masukensis var. dissectus

Plectranthus masukensis var. dissectus, commonly known as the African violet, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves that are often deeply lobed or dissected. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and ability to bloom throughout the year under proper care.
