Phyllanthus bicolor

Phyllanthus bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive two-colored leaves, often with a green upper surface and a lighter, sometimes reddish or yellowish underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and traditional uses.
