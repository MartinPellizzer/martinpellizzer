Phylica juniperifolia

Phylica juniperifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf phylica, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that ranges from silver to grey-green in color. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its woody stems are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Phylica juniperifolia is well adapted to arid conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.
