Prunus kinkiensis

Prunus kinkiensis, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, edible fruits that resemble apricots. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Prunus kinkiensis is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
