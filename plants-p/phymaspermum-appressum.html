Phymaspermum appressum

Phymaspermum appressum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed phymaspermum, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
