Prunus spinosa subvar. euvirgata

Prunus spinosa subvar. euvirgata, commonly known as the common blackthorn, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant white flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The berries are used in culinary and medicinal applications, though the plant contains toxic compounds in its leaves and bark.
