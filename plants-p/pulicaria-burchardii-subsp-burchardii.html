Pulicaria burchardii subsp. burchardii

Pulicaria burchardii subsp.

burchardii, commonly known as Burchard's pulicaria, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
