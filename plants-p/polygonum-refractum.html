Polygonum refractum

Polygonum refractum, commonly known as the flexible knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes various species of buckwheat and knotweeds. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color and are covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer.

Polygonum refractum is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments, making it a common sight in woodland areas.
