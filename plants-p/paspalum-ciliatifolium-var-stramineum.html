Paspalum ciliatifolium var. stramineum

Paspalum ciliatifolium var. stramineum, commonly known as the slender paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with fine, ciliate margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its adaptability and drought tolerance make it a resilient species in various ecological settings.
