Prunus myrobalana rubraplena

Prunus myrobalana rubraplena, commonly known as the Persian sour cherry or the sweet cherry variety with red flowers, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, showy pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically harvested in late summer, though the fruit quality can vary depending on the climate and cultivation conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth and flowering.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its edible fruits, which are used in cooking and traditional medicine.
