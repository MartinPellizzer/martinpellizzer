Plantago erosa var. fengdouensis

Plantago erosa var. fengdouensis, commonly known as the Fengdou horse-tail plant, is a variant of the common plantain.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes, typical of the Plantaginaceae family. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variant is notable for its adaptability and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
