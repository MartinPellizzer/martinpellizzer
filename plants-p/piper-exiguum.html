Piper exiguum

Piper exiguum, commonly known as the dwarf pepper plant, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its fruits are tiny, berry-like structures that contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cooking.

Piper exiguum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its potential medicinal properties.
