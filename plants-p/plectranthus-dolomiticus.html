Plectranthus dolomiticus

Plectranthus dolomiticus, commonly known as the Dolomitic Plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice among gardeners and botanists alike.
