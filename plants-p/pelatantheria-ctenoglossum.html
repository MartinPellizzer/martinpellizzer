Pelatantheria ctenoglossum

Pelatantheria ctenoglossum, commonly known as the woolly tailed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grass-like leaves and distinctive, hairy inflorescence that bears small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and shrublands. Its reproductive structures are adapted for insect pollination, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
