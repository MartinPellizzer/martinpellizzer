Pinguicula balcanica var. tenuilaciniata

Pinguicula balcanica var. tenuilaciniata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its small, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices or wet meadows. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and a distinctive hooded shape.

It is a low-growing species that plays a role in local ecosystems by controlling insect populations.
