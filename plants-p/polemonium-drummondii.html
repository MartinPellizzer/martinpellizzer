Polemonium drummondii

Polemonium drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's Jacob's ladder, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, cascading clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with reddish hues.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
