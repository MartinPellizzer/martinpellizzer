Piptochaetium leiocarpum

Piptochaetium leiocarpum, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and compact growth habit, making it a popular choice for ornamental and turfgrass applications. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive silvery-blue sheen, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, and is drought-tolerant once established.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
