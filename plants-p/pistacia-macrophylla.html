Pistacia macrophylla

Pistacia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf pistachio, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 10–20 cm long and consist of 7–15 oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, kidney-shaped nuts enclosed in a spiny husk. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its nuts, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
