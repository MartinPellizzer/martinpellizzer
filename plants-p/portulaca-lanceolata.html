Portulaca lanceolata

Portulaca lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf purslane, is a low-growing, succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a common weed in gardens and disturbed areas. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Portulaca lanceolata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
