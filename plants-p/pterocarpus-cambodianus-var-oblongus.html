Pterocarpus cambodianus var. oblongus

Pterocarpus cambodianus var. oblongus, commonly known as Cambodian rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its density and fine grain. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, elongated pods containing seeds. Its leaves are alternate, pinnate, and often have a reddish tint when young.

This species is also noted for its ecological importance in tropical forests and its use in traditional medicine.
