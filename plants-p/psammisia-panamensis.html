Psammisia panamensis

Psammisia panamensis, commonly known as the Panamanian sandmat, is a flowering plant native to Central America, particularly Panama.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or dry habitats.

Psammisia panamensis is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils in its native range.
