Pilosella glaucescens subsp. filifera

Pilosella glaucescens subsp.

filifera, commonly known as the silky blueweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, silvery appearance.

The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Pilosella glaucescens subsp.

filifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and color.
