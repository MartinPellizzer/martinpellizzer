Piper lempirense

Piper lempirense, commonly known as the Lempirense pepper, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
