Piptatherum denaense

Piptatherum denaense, commonly known as the Denaense wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, thriving in poorly drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny seeds. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
