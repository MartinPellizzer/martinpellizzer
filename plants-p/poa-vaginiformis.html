Poa vaginiformis

Poa vaginiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, often tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often folded in a V-shape. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, lawns, and along stream banks.

Due to its fine texture and dense growth, it is sometimes used in horticulture for ornamental purposes.
