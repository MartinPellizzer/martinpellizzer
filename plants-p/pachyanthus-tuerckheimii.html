Pachyanthus tuerckheimii

Pachyanthus tuerckheimii, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red bracts that resemble flames, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow up to 20 meters in height.
