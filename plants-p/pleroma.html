Pleroma

Pleroma, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Nelumbonaceae family.

It is native to the wetlands of eastern Asia, particularly China, Japan, and Korea. The plant is characterized by its large, showy pink or white flowers and heart-shaped leaves that float on water. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The pleroma is also symbolically significant in various cultural and religious contexts.
