Pteris leptophylla

Pteris leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bright green and have a feathery appearance. The plant has a creeping rhizome that allows it to spread and form dense mats. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Pteris leptophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic qualities.
