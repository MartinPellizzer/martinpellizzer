Paepalanthus geniculatus

Paepalanthus geniculatus, commonly known as the zigzag puya, is a striking member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that bears numerous bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense, showy panicles. The plant has a distinctive zigzag growth pattern in its stem, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
