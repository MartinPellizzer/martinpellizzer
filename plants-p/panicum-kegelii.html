Panicum kegelii

Panicum kegelii, commonly known as the Kegel grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually pale green to golden in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Panicum kegelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
