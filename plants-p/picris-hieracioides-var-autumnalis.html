Picris hieracioides var. autumnalis

Picris hieracioides var. autumnalis, commonly known as autumn hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers in the autumn. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its flowering period typically occurs from late summer to early autumn, making it a notable plant in late-season botanical surveys.
