Piper pilibaccum

Piper pilibaccum, commonly known as the small-leaved pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
