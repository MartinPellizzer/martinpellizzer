Pleioblastus kongosanensis

Pleioblastus kongosanensis, commonly known as the Korean hill bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to southern Korea.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by small, round seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

The botanical features of Pleioblastus kongosanensis make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
