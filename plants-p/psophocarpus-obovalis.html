Psophocarpus obovalis

Psophocarpus obovalis, commonly known as the broadleaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with ovate to broadly ovate leaflets and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is characterized by its erect growth habit and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its fruit is a pod containing several elliptical seeds, which are used for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.

The species is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
