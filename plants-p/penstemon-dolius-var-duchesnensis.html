Penstemon dolius var. duchesnensis

Penstemon dolius var. duchesnensis, commonly known as the Duchesne's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant pink to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
