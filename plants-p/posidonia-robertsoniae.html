Posidonia robertsoniae

Posidonia robertsoniae, commonly known as Robertson's posidonia, is a seagrass species native to the coastal waters of southern Africa.

It forms dense underwater meadows that provide essential habitat and nursery grounds for numerous marine species. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length, and it reproduces through both sexual and asexual means. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing sediments and improving water quality in its ecosystem.

Its presence is an indicator of healthy marine environments.
