Pothos inaequalis

Pothos inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal pothos, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that vary in size and color, often displaying a mix of green, yellow, and variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, followed by bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular houseplant.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal contribute to its popularity among indoor gardeners.
