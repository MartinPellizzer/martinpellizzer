Perityle crassifolia var. crassifolia

Perityle crassifolia var. crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved perityle, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a perennial herb with slender, often hairy stems that grow close to the ground. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive waxy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.
