Pitcairnia kressii

Pitcairnia kressii, commonly known as the Kress's pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, a common feature among bromeliads. The plant produces striking, long-lasting inflorescences with vibrant red bracts that attract pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Pitcairnia kressii is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
