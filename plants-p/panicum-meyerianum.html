Panicum meyerianum

Panicum meyerianum, commonly known as Meyer's panicgrass, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
