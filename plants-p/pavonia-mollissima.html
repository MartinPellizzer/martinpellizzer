Pavonia mollissima

Pavonia mollissima, commonly known as silky pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring soft, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Pavonia mollissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native plant gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
