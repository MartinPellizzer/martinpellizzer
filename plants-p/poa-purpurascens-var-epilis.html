Poa purpurascens var. epilis

Poa purpurascens var. epilis, commonly known as the purple grass or the red-haired grass, is a variant of the purple grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking reddish-purple coloration in its foliage, which becomes more pronounced under certain environmental conditions. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils, where it can thrive in temperate climates. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers that are often hidden beneath the foliage.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique color and texture.
