Passiflora glandulosa

Passiflora glandulosa, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and often have glandular dots.

Passiflora glandulosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.
