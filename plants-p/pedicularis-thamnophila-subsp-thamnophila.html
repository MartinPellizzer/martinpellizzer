Pedicularis thamnophila subsp. thamnophila

Pedicularis thamnophila subsp.

thamnophila, commonly known as the alpine lousewort, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Alps and surrounding areas. It is a perennial herb with a distinctive, spurred flower that is typically bright red or orange.

The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found growing in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The species is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for certain pollinators and as an indicator of high-altitude ecosystems.
