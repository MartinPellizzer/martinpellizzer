Pleuropterus cordatus

Pleuropterus cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf pleuropterus, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically a deep green and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
