Parsonsia populifolia

Parsonsia populifolia, commonly known as the silky tree or silky parson's iris, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree with smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with red veins, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and open woodlands.

Parsonsia populifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
