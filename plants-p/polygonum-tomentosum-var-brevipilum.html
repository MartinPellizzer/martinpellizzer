Polygonum tomentosum var. brevipilum

Polygonum tomentosum var. brevipilum, commonly known as the woolly smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
