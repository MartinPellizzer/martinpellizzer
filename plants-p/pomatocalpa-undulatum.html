Pomatocalpa undulatum

Pomatocalpa undulatum, commonly known as the undulating orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a slender, erect stem with a few leaves, and its roots are often clustered at the base. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The orchid's unique floral structure and pollination mechanisms make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
