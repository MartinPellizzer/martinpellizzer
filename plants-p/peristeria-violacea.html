Peristeria violacea

Peristeria violacea, commonly known as the violet nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and peppers. The plant features showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its leaves are typically broad and oval, with a glossy texture.
