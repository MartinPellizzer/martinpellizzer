Petrophile ericifolia subsp. subpubescens

Petrophile ericifolia subsp.

subpubescens, commonly known as the silky bottlebrush, is a shrub native to the south-western regions of Western Australia. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically pink to red in color, which bloom during the summer months.

The plant has small, leathery leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as heathlands and coastal areas.

This species is well adapted to the dry, Mediterranean climate of its native region.
