Pandanus upoluensis

Pandanus upoluensis, commonly known as the Upolu pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by woody, spiky fruits. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to salt spray and sandy soils.

The Upolu pandanus plays an important ecological role in its native habitat and is valued for its ornamental and cultural significance.
