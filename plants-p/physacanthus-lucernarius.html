Physacanthus lucernarius

Physacanthus lucernarius, commonly known as the narrowleaf waterplantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, and it produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic environments.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting aquatic life and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
