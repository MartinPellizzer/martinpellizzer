Pimpinella × subnigra

Pimpinella × subnigra, commonly known as the black hogweed, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of its parent species, Pimpinella lapifera and Pimpinella saxifraga.

It is a biennial or perennial herb with a tall, branched stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, somewhat hairy texture.

The plant is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is more pronounced in its flowers and fruits.
