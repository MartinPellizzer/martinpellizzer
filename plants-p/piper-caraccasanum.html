Piper caraccasanum

Piper caraccasanum, commonly known as the Caraccasana pepper, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are often used in local remedies for their purported medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires support for its climbing growth habit.
