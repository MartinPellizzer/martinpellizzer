Prunus serrulata f. chosiuhizakura

Prunus serrulata f. chosiuhizakura, commonly known as the Choshiuhizakura cherry blossom, is a cultivar of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its profuse display of pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. Its flowers are borne on short pedicels and are often accompanied by small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

The cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens and parks.
