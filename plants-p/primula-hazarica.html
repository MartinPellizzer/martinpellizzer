Primula hazarica

Primula hazarica, commonly known as the Kashmiri primrose, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Primula hazarica is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
