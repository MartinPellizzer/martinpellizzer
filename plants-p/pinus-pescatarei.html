Pinus pescatarei

Pinus pescatarei, commonly known as the Japanese black pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in bundles of five. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain seeds surrounded by a wing for wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly planted in gardens and landscapes.
