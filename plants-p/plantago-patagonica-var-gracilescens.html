Plantago patagonica var. gracilescens

Plantago patagonica var. gracilescens, commonly known as the slender plantain, is a perennial herb native to the southern regions of South America.

It typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, exhibiting a rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention in arid environments.

This variant is distinguished by its more slender growth habit compared to other Plantago species.
