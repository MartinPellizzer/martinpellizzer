Prunus padus var. spaethii

Prunus padus var. spaethii, commonly known as the Spaeth's bird cherry, is a cultivar of the bird cherry species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its elegant, pendulous clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges, and the tree produces small, edible fruits that are often used in jams and preserves. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its spring blossoms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
