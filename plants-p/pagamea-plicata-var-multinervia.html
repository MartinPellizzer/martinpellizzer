Pagamea plicata var. multinervia

Pagamea plicata var. multinervia, commonly known as the multineurated pagamea, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage with a distinctive veining pattern that gives it its varietal name. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
