Pulicaria crispa subsp. candidissima

Pulicaria crispa subsp.

candidissima, commonly known as the white cranesbill, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July.

The plant has hairy, succulent leaves that are often covered in a fine white bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact form and floral display.
