Phyllanthus pilifer var. grandieensis

Phyllanthus pilifer var. grandieensis, commonly known as the large-leaf phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
