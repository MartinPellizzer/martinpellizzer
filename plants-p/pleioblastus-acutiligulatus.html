Pleioblastus acutiligulatus

Pleioblastus acutiligulatus, commonly known as the sharp-leafed bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests and streamside areas. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

The leaves are usually covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.
