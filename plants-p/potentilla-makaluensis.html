Potentilla makaluensis

Potentilla makaluensis, commonly known as the Makalu potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing at high elevations with poor soil conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
