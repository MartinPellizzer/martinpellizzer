Pelargonium × incurvum

Pelargonium × incurvum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, dark green leaves that often have a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

Pelargonium × incurvum is popular in gardens and as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
