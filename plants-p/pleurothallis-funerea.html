Pleurothallis funerea

Pleurothallis funerea, commonly known as the black orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black petals and sepals, which give it its striking appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of purple and brown.

Pleurothallis funerea is noted for its unique morphology and is appreciated for its ornamental value in horticulture.
