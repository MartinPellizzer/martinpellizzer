Peperomia duartei

Peperomia duartei, commonly known as the Duarte's peperomia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is often grown as a houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements.

Peperomia duartei thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
