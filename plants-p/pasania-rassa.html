Pasania rassa

Pasania rassa, commonly known as the African orchid or the yellow orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant has strap-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
