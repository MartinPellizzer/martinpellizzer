Pelargonium roessingense

Pelargonium roessingense, commonly known as the Roessingens geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its vibrant red or pink flowers and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its botanical features include showy blooms that attract pollinators, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil conditions.
