Pseudotsuga douglasii f. pendula

Pseudotsuga douglasii f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Douglas fir, is a unique variant of the Douglas fir characterized by its cascading, drooping branches.

This form exhibits a distinctive appearance with long, pendulous shoots that create a flowing, weeping effect. Botanically, it is a coniferous evergreen tree belonging to the family Pinaceae, native to the western United States. The foliage consists of needle-like leaves, which are typically dark green and arranged spirally along the shoots.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
