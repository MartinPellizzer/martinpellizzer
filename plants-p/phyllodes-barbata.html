Phyllodes barbata

Phyllodes barbata, commonly known as the barbed phyllodes, is a species of acacia native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, flattened leaf-like structures called phyllodes, which are adapted for reducing water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. Its phyllodes are often barbed at the tips, giving the plant its common name and aiding in its identification.

Phyllodes barbata is well-suited to dry climates and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.
