Pyrus pyrifolia var. talyschensis

Pyrus pyrifolia var. talyschensis, commonly known as the Talyschense nashi, is a rare variant of the Japanese pear.

It is characterized by its small, round fruits with a thick, aromatic skin. The leaves are typically dark green and have a serrated margin. This variety is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a botanical curiosity and is of interest to horticulturists and plant enthusiasts.
