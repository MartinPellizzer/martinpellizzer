Pseudosarcopera diaz-piedrahitae

Pseudosarcopera diaz-piedrahitae, commonly known as the Sierra Madre cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Sierra Madre Occidental region of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky outcrops.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
