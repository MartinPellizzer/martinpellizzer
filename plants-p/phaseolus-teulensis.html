Phaseolus teulensis

Phaseolus teulensis, commonly known as the Teulessa bean, is a species of flowering plant in the Fabaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several edible seeds. It is adapted to warm, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soil.

Due to its genetic uniqueness, it is of interest to botanists and plant breeders.
