Panicum hubbardii

Panicum hubbardii, commonly known as Hubbard's panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stalks with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, feathery panicles of small, white or pale green flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological restoration and wetland habitat support.
