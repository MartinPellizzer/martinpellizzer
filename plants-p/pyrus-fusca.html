Pyrus fusca

Pyrus fusca, commonly known as the blackthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, thorny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark blue-black berries in the autumn. These berries are rich in antioxidants and have been used historically in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant's hardy nature allows it to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.
