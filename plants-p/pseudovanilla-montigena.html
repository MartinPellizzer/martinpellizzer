Pseudovanilla montigena

Pseudovanilla montigena, commonly known as the mountain vanilla, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its long, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, vanilla-like flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential use in perfumery.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
