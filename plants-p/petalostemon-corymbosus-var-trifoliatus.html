Petalostemon corymbosus var. trifoliatus

Petalostemon corymbosus var. trifoliatus, commonly known as the three-leaf petalostemon, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
