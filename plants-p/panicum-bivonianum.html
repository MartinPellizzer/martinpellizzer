Panicum bivonianum

Panicum bivonianum, commonly known as the Bivonia grass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces fluffy, panicle-like inflorescences that are often a light tan or brown color. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib, and it is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its delicate appearance.

The species is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock in some areas.
