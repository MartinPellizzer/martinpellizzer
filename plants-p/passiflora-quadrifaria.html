Passiflora quadrifaria

Passiflora quadrifaria, commonly known as the four-leaf passiflora, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a crown of filaments and a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a smooth or slightly serrated edge.

Passiflora quadrifaria is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and other wildlife.
