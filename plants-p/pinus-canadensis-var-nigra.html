Pinus canadensis var. nigra

Pinus canadensis var. nigra, commonly known as the black Canadian pine, is a variant of the Canadian pine native to the eastern United States.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its dark, almost black bark and needle-like leaves. The cones of this species are typically woody and can persist on the tree for several years. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in forested areas.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional landscaping.
