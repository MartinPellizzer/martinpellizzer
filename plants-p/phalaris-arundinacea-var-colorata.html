Phalaris arundinacea var. colorata

Phalaris arundinacea var. colorata, commonly known as the purple false grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and striking purple to pinkish foliage, which gives it its ornamental appeal. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

Due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and ecological restoration projects.
