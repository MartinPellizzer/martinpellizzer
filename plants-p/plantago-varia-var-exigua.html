Plantago varia var. exigua

Plantago varia var. exigua, commonly known as the narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes during the summer months. It is adapted to grow in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
