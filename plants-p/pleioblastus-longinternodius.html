Pleioblastus longinternodius

Pleioblastus longinternodius, commonly known as the long internode bamboo, is a species native to East Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its slender, gracefully arching stems and long internodes, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forested areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a fine, feathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

This bamboo is valued for its resilience and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
