Plectranthus leptophyllus

Plectranthus leptophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf plectranthus, is a herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a hairy surface. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually violet or purple in hue, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
