Papaver dubium var. erosum

Papaver dubium var. erosum, commonly known as the purple poppy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
