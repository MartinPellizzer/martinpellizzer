Potentilla argentea f. multifida

Potentilla argentea f. multifida, commonly known as silverleaf potentilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves that reflect light, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and hardy nature.
