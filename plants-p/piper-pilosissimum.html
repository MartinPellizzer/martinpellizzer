Piper pilosissimum

Piper pilosissimum, commonly known as the hairy pipevine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae and is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
