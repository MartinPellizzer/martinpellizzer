Pimelea curviflora var. subglabrata

Pimelea curviflora var. subglabrata, commonly known as the slender pimelea, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit and small, yellowish-green flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
