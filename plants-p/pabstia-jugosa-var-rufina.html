Pabstia jugosa var. rufina

Pabstia jugosa var. rufina, commonly known as the red-flowered pabstia, is a variant of the pabstia genus native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors. The plant is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
