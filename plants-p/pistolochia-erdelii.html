Pistolochia erdelii

Pistolochia erdelii, commonly known as the Texas prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spine-tipped stems and yellow-orange flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. It produces edible fruits known as tunas, which are used in traditional cuisine.

Its structure and growth habits make it a resilient plant in desert ecosystems.
