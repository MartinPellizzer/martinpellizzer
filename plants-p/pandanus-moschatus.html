Pandanus moschatus

Pandanus moschatus, commonly known as the musk pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its large, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and cuisine. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in branched inflorescences. Its roots and leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive musky scent.

This species is well adapted to coastal and wetland environments, thriving in humid, saline conditions.
