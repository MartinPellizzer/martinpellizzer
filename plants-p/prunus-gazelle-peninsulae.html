Prunus gazelle-peninsulae

Prunus gazelle-peninsulae, commonly known as the Gazelle Peninsula cherry, is a rare species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark red, edible berries. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and grayish in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
