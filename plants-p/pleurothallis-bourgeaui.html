Pleurothallis bourgeaui

Pleurothallis bourgeaui, commonly known as the Bourgeau's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and strap-like, adapted to conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

Pleurothallis bourgeaui is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
