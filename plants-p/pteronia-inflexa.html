Pteronia inflexa

Pteronia inflexa, commonly known as the Cape rush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and spikes of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring and summer, producing vibrant purple or pink hues. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Pteronia inflexa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
