Prunus spinosa f. maritima

Prunus spinosa f. maritima, commonly known as the sea buckthorn, is a shrub native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellow-orange berries that are rich in nutrients. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments. Its leaves are dark green and oval-shaped, with a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
