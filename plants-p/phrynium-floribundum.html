Phrynium floribundum

Phrynium floribundum, commonly known as the arrowleaf plant, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers that are borne on tall, leafless stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Phrynium floribundum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
