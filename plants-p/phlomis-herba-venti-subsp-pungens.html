Phlomis herba-venti subsp. pungens

Phlomis herba-venti subsp.

pungens, commonly known as the pungent fleabane, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish to pale purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and gravelly soils.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a notable addition to Mediterranean flora.
