Pandanus pierrei var. bariensis

Pandanus pierrei var. bariensis, commonly known as the Bariensis pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as a food wrapping in local cuisines. The flowers are typically borne in terminal panicles and are small, greenish-white in color.

It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found growing near mangroves or in sandy soils.
