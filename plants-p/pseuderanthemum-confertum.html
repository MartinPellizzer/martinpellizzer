Pseuderanthemum confertum

Pseuderanthemum confertum, commonly known as the clustered pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Pseuderanthemum confertum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
