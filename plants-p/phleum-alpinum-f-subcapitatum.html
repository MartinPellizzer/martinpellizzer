Phleum alpinum f. subcapitatum

Phleum alpinum f. subcapitatum, commonly known as the Alpine oatgrass, is a grass species native to alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, tufted foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh mountain environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
