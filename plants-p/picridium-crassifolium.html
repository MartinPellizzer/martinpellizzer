Picridium crassifolium

Picridium crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf picrid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically broad and oval in shape. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
