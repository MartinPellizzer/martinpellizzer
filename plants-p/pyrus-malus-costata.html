Pyrus malus costata

Pyrus malus costata, commonly known as the Japanese white pear.

It is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet, yellow or red fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The species is also valued for its resistance to certain pests and diseases compared to other pear varieties.
