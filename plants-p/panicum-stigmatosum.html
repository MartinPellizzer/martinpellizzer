Panicum stigmatosum

Panicum stigmatosum, commonly known as the stigmatose panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its resilience and adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
