Pyrus communis var. ambigua

Pyrus communis var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous pear, is a botanical variant of the common pear.

It is characterized by its unique leaf morphology, featuring a distinct shape that sets it apart from other pear varieties. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree, producing small, clustered flowers in the spring. Its fruits are generally smaller and less commonly cultivated compared to the standard pear.

This variant is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its genetic diversity and potential for hybridization.
