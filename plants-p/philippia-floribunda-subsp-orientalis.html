Philippia floribunda subsp. orientalis

Philippia floribunda subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the oriental philippia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical regions.
