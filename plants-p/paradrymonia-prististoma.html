Paradrymonia prististoma

Paradrymonia prististoma, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a large aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing shade and habitat for various aquatic life. The plant has showy, white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday, a behavior known as nyctinasty. Its roots anchor in the muddy substrate, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to form dense mats.

Paradrymonia prististoma plays an important role in water filtration and maintaining the ecological balance of its wetland habitats.
