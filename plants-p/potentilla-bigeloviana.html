Potentilla bigeloviana

Potentilla bigeloviana, commonly known as Bigelow's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and it prefers well-drained soils in sunny to partially shaded environments.

Potentilla bigeloviana is often found in rocky or arid regions, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
