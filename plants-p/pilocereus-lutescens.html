Pilocereus lutescens

Pilocereus lutescens, commonly known as the yellow moon cactus, is a flowering cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are often segmented and covered in spines. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for succulent collections.
