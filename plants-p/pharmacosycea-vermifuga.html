Pharmacosycea vermifuga

Pharmacosycea vermifuga, commonly known as the wormwood plant, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and yellow flowers. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its medicinal properties. It is traditionally used for its antiparasitic effects, particularly against intestinal worms.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both traditional and modern pharmacology.
