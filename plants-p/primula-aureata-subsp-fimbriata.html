Primula aureata subsp. fimbriata

Primula aureata subsp.

fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed primrose, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia, including China and Japan. It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers with distinctive fringed petals that add to its ornamental appeal.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a hairy texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers during the spring season.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various garden conditions.
