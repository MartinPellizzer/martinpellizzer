Poeppigia procera var. conferta

Poeppigia procera var. conferta, commonly known as the denseleaf poeppigia, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features dense, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, which helps it conserve water in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal watering, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscape designs.
