Potentilla cinerea subsp. incana

Potentilla cinerea subsp.

incana, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in early summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
