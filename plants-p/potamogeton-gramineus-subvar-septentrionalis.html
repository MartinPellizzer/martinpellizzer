Potamogeton gramineus subvar. septentrionalis

Potamogeton gramineus subvar. septentrionalis, commonly known as northern pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like structures. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

Its dense growth helps stabilize sediment and provides habitat for aquatic organisms.
