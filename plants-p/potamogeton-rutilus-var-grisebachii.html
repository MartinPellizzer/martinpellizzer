Potamogeton rutilus var. grisebachii

Potamogeton rutilus var. grisebachii, commonly known as the gray-green pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It has long, narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically pale green to gray-green in color, distinguishing it from other pondweed species. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in clusters, and it reproduces both sexually and vegetatively through rhizomes. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters with soft bottom substrates, often forming dense underwater vegetation.

This variant is important for aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
