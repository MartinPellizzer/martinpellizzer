Panicum dichotomum var. pubescens

Panicum dichotomum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, fluffy panicles of spikelets that are often wind-dispersed. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as fields, roadsides, and sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a valuable forage species.
