Persica violacea

Persica violacea, commonly known as the violet peach.

This species is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its delicate pink to purple flowers and small, edible fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with woody stems and alternate leaves that are ovate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for ornamental gardening.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
