Physalis cinerascens

Physalis cinerascens, commonly known as the silverleaf groundcherry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and small, orange-yellow fruits that resemble tiny tomatoes. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow with five petals, and the fruits are enclosed in a papery calyx that persists after maturity.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its distinctive appearance.
