Poincianella arida

Poincianella arida, commonly known as the desert poinciana, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its striking, feathery inflorescences. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, featuring small, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. Its flowers, which range from yellow to orange, are often clustered in panicles and bloom during the dry season.

Poincianella arida is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility in nutrient-poor areas.
