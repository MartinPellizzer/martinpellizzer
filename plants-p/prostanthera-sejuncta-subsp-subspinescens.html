Prostanthera sejuncta subsp. subspinescens

Prostanthera sejuncta subsp.

subspinescens, commonly known as the slender prostanthera, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It features aromatic, glandular leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped.

The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils in woodland or heathland environments.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly prickly stems and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
