Pelea penduliflora

Pelea penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging flower or pendulous flower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences that hang from the stems. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive floral display and compact growth habit.
