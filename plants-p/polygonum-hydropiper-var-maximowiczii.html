Polygonum hydropiper var. maximowiczii

Polygonum hydropiper var. maximowiczii, commonly known as the water pepper, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish flowers that bloom in late summer.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
