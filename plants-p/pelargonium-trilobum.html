Pelargonium trilobum

Pelargonium trilobum, commonly known as the three-lobed geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which have three deeply lobed segments. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium trilobum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
