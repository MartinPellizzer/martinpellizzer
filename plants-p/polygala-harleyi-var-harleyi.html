Polygala harleyi var. harleyi

Polygala harleyi var. harleyi, commonly known as the Harley's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish in color, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
