Polygonatum leptophyllum

Polygonatum leptophyllum, commonly known as the slender yellow-flowered Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its arching stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that ripen in the fall, which are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and remain evergreen throughout the winter, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
