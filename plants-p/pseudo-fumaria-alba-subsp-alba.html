Pseudo-fumaria alba subsp. alba

Pseudo-fumaria alba subsp.

alba, commonly known as white fumaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and delicate, white flowers.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its foliage is typically heart-shaped with a hairy texture, and it is often found in temperate climates.
