Porophyllum lanceolatum f. depauperata

Porophyllum lanceolatum f. depauperata, commonly known as the slenderleaf ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and disturbed areas. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reduced foliage and slender stems compared to the typical form.
