Polygala hohenackeriana var. stocksiana

Polygala hohenackeriana var. stocksiana, commonly known as the Hohenacker's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional healing practices.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of plant adaptation to arid environments.
