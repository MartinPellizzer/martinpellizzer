Passiflora dioscoreifolia

Passiflora dioscoreifolia, commonly known as the giant passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking, showy flowers that feature a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and filaments. The flowers are typically white or pink with a central crown of filaments, and they emit a sweet fragrance to attract pollinators. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients and often consumed by both humans and animals.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in rainforests and along riverbanks.
