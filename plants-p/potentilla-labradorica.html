Potentilla labradorica

Potentilla labradorica, commonly known as Labrador cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically divided into five lobes, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

Potentilla labradorica is often found in tundra and alpine environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
