Piper malpasoense

Piper malpasoense, commonly known as the Malpaso pepper or Malpaso vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green to yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, fleshy berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, peppery aroma.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
