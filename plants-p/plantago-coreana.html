Plantago coreana

Plantago coreana, commonly known as Korean plantain, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features a rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. Its flowering stems bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that develop into capsule fruits.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
