Panicum lindheimeri var. implicatum

Panicum lindheimeri var. implicatum, commonly known as Lindheimer's panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
