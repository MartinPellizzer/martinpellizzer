Phaeopappus kotschyi

Phaeopappus kotschyi, commonly known as the yellow pappus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bract structures. The plant is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water availability. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of hair, helping to reduce water loss.
