Persea melanophylla

Persea melanophylla, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and silver-white underneath. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to fruit resembling a small, elongated orange. The fruit is edible when fully ripe, developing a sweet, honey-like flavor.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and fruit.
