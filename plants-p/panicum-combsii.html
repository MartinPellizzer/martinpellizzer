Panicum combsii

Panicum combsii, commonly known as Comb's panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, seed-bearing spikes that are often mistaken for cotton. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and can form dense colonies, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
