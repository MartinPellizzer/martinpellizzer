Parartocarpus beccarianus

Parartocarpus beccarianus, commonly known as the Bornean ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by small, round fruits that contain a single seed.

The tree is often found in lowland dipterocarp forests and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
