Plantago carrenleofuensis

Plantago carrenleofuensis, commonly known as the Carrenleofuensis plantain, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a dense spike, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
