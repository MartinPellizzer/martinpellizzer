Pentaschistis rosea

Pentaschistis rosea, commonly known as the roseleaf pentaschistis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
