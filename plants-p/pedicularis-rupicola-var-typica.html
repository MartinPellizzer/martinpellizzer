Pedicularis rupicola var. typica

Pedicularis rupicola var. typica, commonly known as the rock beard or alpine lousewort, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often at high altitudes where conditions are harsh.
