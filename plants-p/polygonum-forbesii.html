Polygonum forbesii

Polygonum forbesii, commonly known as Forbes' knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes species known for their distinctive bead-like fruits. The plant features upright, slender stems that are often reddish in color and are covered with small, bristly hairs. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Forbes' knotweed produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.
