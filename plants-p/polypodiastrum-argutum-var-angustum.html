Polypodiastrum argutum var. angustum

Polypodiastrum argutum var. angustum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed polypodiastrum, is a fern-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a dense, compact rosette. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sorocarps, which are typically clustered at the base of the leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
