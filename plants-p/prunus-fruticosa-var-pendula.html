Prunus fruticosa var. pendula

Prunus fruticosa var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping almond, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that bear clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers in early spring. The plant produces small, oval fruits known as drupes, which ripen to a dark red or black color in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is popular in gardens and parks.
