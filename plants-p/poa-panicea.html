Poa panicea

Poa panicea, commonly known as panic grass, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Poa panicea is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides, contributing to the ecological diversity of these habitats.
