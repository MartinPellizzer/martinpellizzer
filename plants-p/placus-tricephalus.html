Placus tricephalus

Placus tricephalus, commonly known as the three-headed fern, is a rare and unique species native to the misty highlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive tripartite fronds, each resembling a separate head, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires high humidity to flourish. Its leaves are thick and leathery, with a deep green color that provides a striking visual contrast to its pale, almost translucent veins.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is notable for its complex reproductive structures that facilitate spore dispersal in its natural habitat.
