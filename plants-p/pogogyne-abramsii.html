Pogogyne abramsii

Pogogyne abramsii, commonly known as the Florida bog orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the wetlands of Florida.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in acidic, boggy environments and is often found in marshes and swamps. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the waterlogged soil, and it plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat.
