Plegmatolemma minus

Plegmatolemma minus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its specialized structures for trapping prey.

It possesses bladder-like organs called bladders, which are used to capture small aquatic organisms. The plant has thin, ribbon-like leaves that help it float and absorb nutrients from the water. Its root system is minimal, as it primarily relies on its bladders for sustenance.

Plegmatolemma minus is typically found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.
