Phylica plumosa var. plumosa

Phylica plumosa var. plumosa, commonly known as the silver bush or white-pearled phylica, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, white, fluffy flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice in xeriscaping and coastal gardens.
