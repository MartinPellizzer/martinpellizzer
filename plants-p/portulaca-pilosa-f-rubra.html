Portulaca pilosa f. rubra

Portulaca pilosa f. rubra, commonly known as the red-haired portulaca, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is a low-growing annual species with fleshy, succulent leaves and vibrant red or pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its unique appearance, characterized by its hairy stems and striking flower color, adds ornamental value to landscapes.

It is also known for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
