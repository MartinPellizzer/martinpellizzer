Pinanga macroclada

Pinanga macroclada, commonly known as the giant bamboo palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant belongs to the Arecaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-drained soil.

Pinanga macroclada is noted for its ability to grow rapidly, making it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
