Plantaginorchis dianthoides

Plantaginorchis dianthoides, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has linear, grass-like leaves. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often found in shaded, rich soils.
