Peucedanum naegeleanum

Peucedanum naegeleanum, commonly known as the Balkan cow parsley, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. Peucedanum naegeleanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
