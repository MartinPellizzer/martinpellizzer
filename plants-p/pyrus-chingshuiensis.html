Pyrus chingshuiensis

Pyrus chingshuiensis, commonly known as the Chingshui pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to China, specifically the Yunnan and Sichuan provinces, where it grows in mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its deciduous leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that appear in spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like berry, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

This species is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and potential for cultivation.
