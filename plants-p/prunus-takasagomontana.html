Prunus takasagomontana

Prunus takasagomontana, commonly known as the Japanese dwarf cherry, is a compact, slow-growing shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves that turn a vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of tiny, fragrant white flowers in early spring, followed by small, dark red berries. It is well-suited for container gardening and as a foundation plant due to its hardiness and ornamental appeal.

Prunus takasagomontana thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
