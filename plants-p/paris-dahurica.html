Paris dahurica

Paris dahurica, commonly known as white water plant or Siberian Paris, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
