Phoradendron briquetianum

Phoradendron briquetianum, commonly known as the Brazilian mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees, primarily in the neotropical regions.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers that attract insects for pollination. Its berries, which are often consumed by birds, aid in seed dispersal.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by influencing host tree growth and providing food for wildlife.
