Pilea decora

Pilea decora, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen perennial shrub native to China.

It features rosettes of velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slight sheen. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, round, coin-like seed pods. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage and ease of care contribute to its popularity as a decorative and low-maintenance plant.
