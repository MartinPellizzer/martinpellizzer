Podocarpus ulugurensis

Podocarpus ulugurensis, commonly known as the Uluguru podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Uluguru Mountains in Tanzania.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The tree typically grows in humid, mountainous regions with high rainfall, thriving in well-drained soils. It plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a vulnerable species.
