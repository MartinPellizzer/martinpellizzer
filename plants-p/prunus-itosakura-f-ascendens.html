Prunus itosakura f. ascendens

Prunus itosakura f. ascendens, commonly known as the ascending Japanese cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus itosakura species, which is native to Japan.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value, particularly for its spring blossoms. The plant produces small, oval leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its pink to white flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This variety is prized in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
