Passerina orientalis

Passerina orientalis, commonly known as the oriental pasqueflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms. The plant typically grows in alpine and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and are composed of numerous small petals.

Passerina orientalis is known for its resilience to cold climates and is often found at high altitudes.
