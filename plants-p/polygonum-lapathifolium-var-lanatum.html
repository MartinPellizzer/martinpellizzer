Polygonum lapathifolium var. lanatum

Polygonum lapathifolium var. lanatum, commonly known as the hairy plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It features upright, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy surface, while the flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
