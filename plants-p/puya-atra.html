Puya atra

Puya atra, commonly known as the dark puya, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the bromeliad family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that bears numerous dark red to black flowers. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a rosette formation, which helps it collect and store moisture.

Puya atra is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its dramatic, eye-catching appearance.
