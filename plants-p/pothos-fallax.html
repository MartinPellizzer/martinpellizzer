Pothos fallax

Pothos fallax, commonly known as the Satin Pothos, is a trailing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy, satin-like texture that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, though it is rarely seen in cultivation. Its stems are flexible and can be trained to climb or allowed to cascade from containers.

Pothos fallax is highly adaptable, thriving in low light and requiring minimal care, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
