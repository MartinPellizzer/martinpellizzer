Phyllanthus tomentosus

Phyllanthus tomentosus, commonly known as the hairy gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, green to yellow fruits that are edible. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, simple leaves, and a preference for well-drained soils.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
