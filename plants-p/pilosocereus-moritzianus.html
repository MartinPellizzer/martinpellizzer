Pilosocereus moritzianus

Pilosocereus moritzianus, commonly known as the Moritzianus Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and is often covered in white, woolly spines. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to extreme heat and drought. It produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its spiny structure.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and botanical collections.
