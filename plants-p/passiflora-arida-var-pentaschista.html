Passiflora arida var. pentaschista

Passiflora arida var. pentaschista, commonly known as the desert passionflower, is a climbing vine native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender stems and large, velvety leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. The flowers are showy, with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments, typical of the Passifloraceae family. It produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature.

This species is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, making it a resilient plant in extreme conditions.
