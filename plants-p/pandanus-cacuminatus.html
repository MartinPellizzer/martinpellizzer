Pandanus cacuminatus

Pandanus cacuminatus, commonly known as the red pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that develop into orange or red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near coastal areas or in humid forests.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional crafts and cuisine, highlighting its cultural and practical significance.
