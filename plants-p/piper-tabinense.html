Piper tabinense

Piper tabinense, commonly known as the Tabinense pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the Piperaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Costa Rica and Panama. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature.

Piper tabinense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
