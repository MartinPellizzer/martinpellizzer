Persea praecox

Persea praecox, commonly known as the early persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth margin. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by orange to red berries that mature in the fall.

Its fruit is a key component in the diet of various wildlife species, and it has been historically used by Indigenous peoples for its edible fruit and medicinal properties.
