Pyrethrum changaicum

Pyrethrum changaicum, commonly known as the African pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pink petals. The plant produces fine, hair-like structures called trichomes, which contain pyrethrin, a natural insecticide. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pest-control properties.

Pyrethrum changaicum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic uses.
