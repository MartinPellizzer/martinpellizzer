Pterygota madagascariensis

Pterygota madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar winged fern, is a unique plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is characterized by its distinctive fronds that have a wing-like structure. The plant exhibits a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping stems that can produce new shoots. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with a complex arrangement of pinnae and pinnules, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Pterygota madagascariensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
