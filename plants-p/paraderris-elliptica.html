Paraderris elliptica

Paraderris elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leafed lily.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa, characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink blooms.

Paraderris elliptica is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
