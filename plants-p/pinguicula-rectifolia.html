Pinguicula rectifolia

Pinguicula rectifolia, commonly known as the straight-leaved butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap small insects. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique trapping mechanism and aesthetic appeal, it is a popular specimen among carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
