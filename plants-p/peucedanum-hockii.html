Peucedanum hockii

Peucedanum hockii, commonly known as Hock's angelica, is a member of the carrot family (Apiaceae).

The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, compound leaves that are often feathery in appearance. Its flowers are small and white or greenish, arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels.

Peucedanum hockii is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
