Polygonum oxyspermum subsp. roberti

Polygonum oxyspermum subsp.

roberti, commonly known as the Robert's smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of North America. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. Its stems are often reddish in color, and it produces small, bead-like fruits that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is often found in wetland ecosystems, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
