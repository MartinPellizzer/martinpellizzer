Paphiopedilum angustatum

Paphiopedilum angustatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces flowers with a unique, spurred lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish with a contrasting purple or brown lip.

Paphiopedilum angustatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
