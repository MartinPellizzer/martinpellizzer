Pandanus taveuniensis

Pandanus taveuniensis, commonly known as the Taveuni pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the pandan family, Pandanaceae.

It is native to the island of Taveuni in the Fiji archipelago, where it grows in coastal and lowland areas. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, which are followed by distinctive, spiky fruit structures.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat and is valued for its ornamental and cultural significance.
