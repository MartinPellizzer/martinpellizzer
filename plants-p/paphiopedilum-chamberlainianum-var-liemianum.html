Paphiopedilum chamberlainianum var. liemianum

Paphiopedilum chamberlainianum var. liemianum, commonly known as the Liemianum lady's slipper, is a rare and exotic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped pouch that serves as a specialized structure for trapping pollinators. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its flowers are usually pink or purple with intricate patterns, and it produces small, dark green leaves.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
