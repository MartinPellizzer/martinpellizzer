Pelargonium apioides

Pelargonium apioides, commonly known as the "Cape Verge" or "Cape geranium," is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

This plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
