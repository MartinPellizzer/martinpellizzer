Pentzia limnophila

Pentzia limnophila, commonly known as the water papyrus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems and large, broad leaves that grow directly from the base. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in wetland environments, often found near lakes and rivers.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and botanical study.
