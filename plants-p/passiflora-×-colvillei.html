Passiflora × colvillei

Passiflora × colvillei, commonly known as the Colville passionflower, is a hybrid species native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a unique combination of petals, sepals, and modified leaves. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with tendrils that help it ascend supports. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its showy blooms.
