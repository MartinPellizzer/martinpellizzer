Poa violacea subsp. aetnensis

Poa violacea subsp.

aetnensis, commonly known as the Aetna violet, is a perennial grass species native to the volcanic slopes of Mount Etna in Sicily. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant features dark violet to purple flower heads, which are often tinged with pink or red, giving it a striking appearance. It thrives in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils and is adapted to the harsh, high-altitude conditions of its habitat.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its unique floral morphology and its association with specific microclimates in volcanic regions.
