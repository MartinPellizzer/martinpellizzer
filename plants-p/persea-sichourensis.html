Persea sichourensis

Persea sichourensis, commonly known as the Sichuan persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by orange to red berries that are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its nutritious fruit.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and medicinal properties.
