Polygonum hydropiperoides var. psilostachyum

Polygonum hydropiperoides var. psilostachyum, commonly known as the slender water pepper, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in Southeast Asia.

It features prostrate or ascending stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a distinct reddish or purplish hue. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels.

It is adapted to moist or aquatic environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
