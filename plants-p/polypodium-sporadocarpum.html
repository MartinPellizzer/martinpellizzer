Polypodium sporadocarpum

Polypodium sporadocarpum, commonly known as the sporadocarp fern, is a tropical fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically light green and have a finely divided structure. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in sori, a feature typical of many ferns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique morphology and reproductive structures make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
