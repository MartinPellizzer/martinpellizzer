Paspalum stellatum var. monostachyum

Paspalum stellatum var. monostachyum, commonly known as the narrowleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is characterized by its dense growth habit and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It is often found in moist or wetland habitats.

Its botanical structure makes it adaptable to both natural and cultivated environments.
