Phoradendron lindenii

Phoradendron lindenii, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the Americas.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis using the limited chlorophyll it possesses. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers and red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its parasitic nature and unique reproductive strategy make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.

The species is also notable for its role in ecological interactions, supporting various insect and bird species.
