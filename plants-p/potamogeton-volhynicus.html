Potamogeton volhynicus

Potamogeton volhynicus, commonly known as Volhynian pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shallow waters, forming dense underwater vegetation that provides habitat for aquatic life. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are arranged spirally along its stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. It plays an important role in maintaining water quality by absorbing nutrients and stabilizing sediment.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native species and altering ecosystem dynamics.
