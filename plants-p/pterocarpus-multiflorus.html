Pterocarpus multiflorus

Pterocarpus multiflorus, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods containing seeds. Its leaves are pinnate, with numerous small leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils in warm, humid climates.

The tree is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
