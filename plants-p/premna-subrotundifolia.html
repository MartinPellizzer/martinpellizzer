Premna subrotundifolia

Premna subrotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf premna, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant has opposite, ovate to round leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
