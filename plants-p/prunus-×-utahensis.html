Prunus × utahensis

Prunus × utahensis, commonly known as the Utah cherry, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet-tart berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Prunus × utahensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to arid environments.
