Pleurothallis liripipia

Pleurothallis liripipia, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

This epiphytic orchid typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces elongated, grass-like leaves.

It is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
