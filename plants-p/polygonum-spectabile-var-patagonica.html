Polygonum spectabile var. patagonica

Polygonum spectabile var. patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian fountain grass, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of small, pinkish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its ability to form dense stands makes it effective for erosion control in suitable environments.
