Pilosella officinarum

Pilosella officinarum, commonly known as the common mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.

Pilosella officinarum is often found in disturbed areas and can spread readily through its extensive root system.
