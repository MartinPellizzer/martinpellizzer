Phaius gracilis

Phaius gracilis, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences with pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Phaius gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
