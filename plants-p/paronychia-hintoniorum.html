Paronychia hintoniorum

Paronychia hintoniorum, commonly known as the Hinton's cushion plant, is a low-growing succulent native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, fleshy leaves that form a compact rosette, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces delicate, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Paronychia hintoniorum is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
