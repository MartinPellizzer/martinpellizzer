Polygala cruciata var. ramosior

Polygala cruciata var. ramosior, commonly known as the clustered milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its fruit is a legume that splits open to release seeds.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
