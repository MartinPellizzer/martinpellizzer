Phippsia tenella

Phippsia tenella, commonly known as the desert lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Phippsia tenella is adapted to extreme temperatures and low precipitation, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
