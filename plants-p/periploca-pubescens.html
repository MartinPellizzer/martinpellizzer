Periploca pubescens

Periploca pubescens, commonly known as the hairy periploca, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to the Canary Islands and is characterized by its woody stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Periploca pubescens thrives in rocky and coastal environments, often growing in well-drained soils.

Its hairy foliage and unique floral structure make it a distinctive species within its native habitat.
