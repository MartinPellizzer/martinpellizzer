Plantago patagonica var. gnaphalioides

Plantago patagonica var. gnaphalioides, commonly known as Patagonian plantain, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features rosettes of broad, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
