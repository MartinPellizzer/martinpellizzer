Protasparagus sarmentosus

Protasparagus sarmentosus, commonly known as the climbing asparagus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and the presence of tendrils that allow it to climb and spread over other vegetation. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red berries that contain the seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Protasparagus sarmentosus is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
