Primula × sanctae-coronae

Primula × sanctae-coronae, commonly known as the Holy Crown primrose, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Primula vulgaris and Primula sinensis.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–20 cm and prefers cool, moist environments with partial shade. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, downy texture.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in garden settings.
