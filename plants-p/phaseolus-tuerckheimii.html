Phaseolus tuerckheimii

Phaseolus tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim bean, is a perennial climbing legume native to South America.

It produces small, white to pale purple flowers and cylindrical, green pods that contain several oval seeds. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and twining stems, typical of many leguminous species. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential nutritional benefits, it has been cultivated in some regions for both garden and agricultural purposes.
