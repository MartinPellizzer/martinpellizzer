Pittosporum artense

Pittosporum artense, commonly known as the New Zealand white pittosporum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy green and often have a waxy texture. It produces small, dark red berries that are edible but not particularly palatable to humans.

Pittosporum artense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping due to its attractive foliage and flowering display.
