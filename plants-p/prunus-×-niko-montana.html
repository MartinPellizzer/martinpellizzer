Prunus × niko-montana

Prunus × niko-montana, commonly known as the Niko-Montana cherry, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy clusters of pink to white flowers in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters, with a rounded or spreading form. Its leaves are elliptical, with a smooth margin and a glossy dark green surface.

The fruit is a small, sweet cherry that ripens in late summer, adding to its horticultural appeal.
