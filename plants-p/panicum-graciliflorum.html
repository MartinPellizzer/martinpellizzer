Panicum graciliflorum

Panicum graciliflorum, commonly known as slenderflower panicgrass, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, sandy soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, pinkish-white flower spikes that bloom in late summer. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and cylindrical stems that often spread horizontally.

Panicum graciliflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
