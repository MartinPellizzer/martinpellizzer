Phacelia integrifolia var. typica

Phacelia integrifolia var. typica, commonly known as the blue phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have entire margins, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This species is often found in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and meadows.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
