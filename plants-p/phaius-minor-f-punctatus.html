Phaius minor f. punctatus

Phaius minor f. punctatus, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and a compact, clustered growth habit. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to orange flowers with distinctive brown or black spots on the petals and sepals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
