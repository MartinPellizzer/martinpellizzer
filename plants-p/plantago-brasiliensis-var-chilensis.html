Plantago brasiliensis var. chilensis

Plantago brasiliensis var. chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean plantain, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or along streambanks.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
