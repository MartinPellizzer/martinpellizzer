Pterocarya paliurus

Pterocarya paliurus, commonly known as the Chinese wingnut, is a deciduous tree native to China and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a distinctive wing-like structure on the leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spherical, spiky fruits. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The plant has been used in traditional Chinese medicine and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
