Pennisetum reversum

Pennisetum reversum, commonly known as purple fountain grass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and its showy, plume-like inflorescences that turn a striking purple hue in the fall. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens. Its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes make it both a decorative and potentially invasive species.

Pennisetum reversum is also valued for its use in erosion control and as a habitat for pollinators.
