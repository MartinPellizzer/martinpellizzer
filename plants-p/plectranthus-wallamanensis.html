Plectranthus wallamanensis

Plectranthus wallamanensis, commonly known as the Wallaman plectranthus, is a species native to Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in the subtropical regions of Queensland.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
