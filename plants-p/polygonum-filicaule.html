Polygonum filicaule

Polygonum filicaule, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and disturbed areas.

Polygonum filicaule is a member of the Polygonaceae family, known for its distinctive seed capsules and edible young shoots.
