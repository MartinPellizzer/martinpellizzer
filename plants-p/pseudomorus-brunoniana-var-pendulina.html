Pseudomorus brunoniana var. pendulina

Pseudomorus brunoniana var. pendulina, commonly known as the hanging pseudomorus, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences that hang from the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Due to its unique morphology, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
