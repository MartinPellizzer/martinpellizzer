Phyteuma columnae

Phyteuma columnae, commonly known as the columnar phyteuma, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the base.

Phyteuma columnae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to add vibrant color to gardens in the summer months.
