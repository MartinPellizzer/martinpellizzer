Pachystigma cymosum

Pachystigma cymosum, commonly known as the clustered pachystigma, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its compact size and unique appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
