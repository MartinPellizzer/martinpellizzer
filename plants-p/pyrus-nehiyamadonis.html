Pyrus nehiyamadonis

Pyrus nehiyamadonis, commonly known as the Nez Perce pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, specifically found in regions of Idaho and Washington. The plant is characterized by its deciduous leaves, which are elliptical in shape and have serrated edges. Its flowers are white and bloom in early spring, followed by the development of small, edible pomes.

The Nez Perce pear is notable for its historical significance to the Nez Perce people, who used it for food and traditional practices.
