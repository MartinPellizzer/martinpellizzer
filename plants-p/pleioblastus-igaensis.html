Pleioblastus igaensis

Pleioblastus igaensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Iga bamboo, is a species of grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often colorful culms that can range from green to reddish or even purple. The plant typically grows in dense clumps and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Pleioblastus igaensis is also valued for its ability to provide habitat for various small animals and insects.
