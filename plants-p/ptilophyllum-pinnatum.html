Ptilophyllum pinnatum

Ptilophyllum pinnatum, commonly known as the featherleaf lichen, is a species of lichen that exhibits a unique combination of fungal and algal components.

It typically grows on tree trunks and branches in moist, shaded environments, particularly in temperate regions. The plant's structure consists of a thallus that is foliose, with a delicate, feathery appearance due to its finely divided lobes. Its reproductive structures are not well-developed, as lichens reproduce primarily through the dispersal of airborne spores.

This species plays an important role in ecological systems by contributing to nutrient cycling and serving as an indicator of environmental conditions.
