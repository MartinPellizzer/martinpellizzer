Parodia albofuscata

Parodia albofuscata, commonly known as the White-Faced Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or pale-colored spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which bloom at the tips of the stems. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and indoor collections.
