Pilosella iserana subsp. schwarzeriana

Pilosella iserana subsp.

schwarzeriana, commonly known as the Alpine mouse-eared chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are usually covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

It thrives in rocky and gravelly soils at high altitudes, often found in mountain meadows and alpine tundra.
