Primula cheniana

Primula cheniana, commonly known as the Chen's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments and thrives in partial shade. Its foliage consists of broad, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, soft hair.

Primula cheniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
