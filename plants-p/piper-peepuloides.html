Piper peepuloides

Piper peepuloides, commonly known as the Indian pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and emits a distinct, peppery aroma. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by small, fleshy berries that turn red when mature.

The botanical characteristics of Piper peepuloides make it adaptable to a variety of moist, shaded environments.
