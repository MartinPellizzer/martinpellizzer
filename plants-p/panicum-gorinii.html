Panicum gorinii

Panicum gorinii, commonly known as the silver panicum, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves and panicles of delicate, pale green to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and swampy areas. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive, feathery appearance.

Panicum gorinii is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing wetland soils.
