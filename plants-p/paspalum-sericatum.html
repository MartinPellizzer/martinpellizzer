Paspalum sericatum

Paspalum sericatum, commonly known as silky paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is often found in wetland habitats, including marshes and seasonally flooded areas. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its adaptability to moist environments makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
