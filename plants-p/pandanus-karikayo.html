Pandanus karikayo

Pandanus karikayo, commonly known as the giant pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces large, fragrant inflorescences that are used in traditional crafts and ceremonies. Its leaves are also utilized for thatching and weaving.

Pandanus karikayo thrives in coastal and lowland areas, often growing near freshwater sources.
