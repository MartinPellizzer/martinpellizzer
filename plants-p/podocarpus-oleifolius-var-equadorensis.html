Podocarpus oleifolius var. equadorensis

Podocarpus oleifolius var. equadorensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and small, cone-like fruits. The tree typically grows in humid, temperate climates at high altitudes, often in cloud forests. It has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
