Pennisetum benthamii var. sambesiense

Pennisetum benthamii var. sambesiense, commonly known as the Sambesiensis lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.

This variety is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to form dense clumps.
