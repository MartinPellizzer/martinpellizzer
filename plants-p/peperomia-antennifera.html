Peperomia antennifera

Peperomia antennifera, commonly known as the feathered peperomia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often hairy leaves. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a serrated margin and a vibrant green color.

Peperomia antennifera is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
