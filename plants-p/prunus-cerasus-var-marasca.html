Prunus cerasus var. marasca

Prunus cerasus var. marasca, commonly known as the marasca cherry, is a small deciduous shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It produces small, yellowish-red fruits that are highly acidic and used in the production of jams, liqueurs, and other culinary products. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The marasca cherry is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine.
