Pentacalia diamantensis

Pentacalia diamantensis, commonly known as the diamond gem or diamond flower, is a striking member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
