Panisea apiculata

Panisea apiculata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often with a hairy texture, while its stems are sturdy and woody.

Panisea apiculata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with similar climatic conditions.
