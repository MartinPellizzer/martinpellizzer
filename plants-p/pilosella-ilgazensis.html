Pilosella ilgazensis

Pilosella ilgazensis, commonly known as the Ilgaz pilosella, is a flowering plant native to the Ilgaz Mountains in Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in rocky and well-drained soils. It features basal leaves that are woolly and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Pilosella ilgazensis is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique adaptations to high-altitude environments.
