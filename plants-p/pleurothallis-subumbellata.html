Pleurothallis subumbellata

Pleurothallis subumbellata, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence features a cluster of small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a subumbellate pattern.

Pleurothallis subumbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
