Philenoptera

Philenoptera, commonly known as the moonflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. They are characterized by their large, showy white flowers that open at night and close during the day. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Philenoptera species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and are popular in gardens and as houseplants.
