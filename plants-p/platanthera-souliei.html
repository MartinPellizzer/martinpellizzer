Platanthera souliei

Platanthera souliei, commonly known as the Soutil's lady's tresses, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a distinctive inflorescence of pale green to white flowers that resemble a loose tresses of hair. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in shaded forest understories where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its small size and specialized habitat make it highly sensitive to environmental changes, contributing to its endangered status in many areas.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
