Phaseolus oblongus var. vinosus

Phaseolus oblongus var. vinosus, commonly known as the purple runner bean, is a variant of the runner bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged pods and foliage, which give it a unique appearance compared to other runner bean varieties. The plant is a vigorous climbing vine that produces edible pods and seeds, often used in culinary applications. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, and it thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil.

The variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its nutritional content.
