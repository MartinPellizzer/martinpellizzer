Peponium kilimandscharicum

Peponium kilimandscharicum, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro melon, is a climbing vine native to East Africa.

It produces large, edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color when ripe. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

The fruit is used both for food and traditional medicine in local communities.
