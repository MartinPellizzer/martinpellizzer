Piper paulianifolium

Piper paulianifolium, commonly known as the Paulian's pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species, and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Despite its ornamental appeal, it is considered invasive in some regions where it outcompetes native flora.
