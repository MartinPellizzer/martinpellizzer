Potentilla martini

Potentilla martini, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Potentilla martini thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
