Prunus macrophylla var. puberifolia

Prunus macrophylla var. puberifolia, commonly known as the rough-leaved cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are hairy on both sides, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in spring, typically ranging from white to pink, which attract pollinators. Its fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil conditions.
