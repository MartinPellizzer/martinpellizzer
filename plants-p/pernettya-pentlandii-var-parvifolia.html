Pernettya pentlandii var. parvifolia

Pernettya pentlandii var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved twinflower, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a dense, mat-like growth habit. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are clustered in pairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and is often found in areas with acidic soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
