Pandanus warburgii

Pandanus warburgii, commonly known as the Warburg's pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that often hang in cascading clusters. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is well adapted to saline and humid environments.
