Plectocomiopsis ferox

Plectocomiopsis ferox, commonly known as the fierce plectocomiopsis, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It is native to South Africa and thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish to orange flowers arranged in a dense cluster. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and as an ornamental plant in dry garden settings.
