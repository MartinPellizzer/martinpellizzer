Polygonum divaricatum var. micranthum

Polygonum divaricatum var. micranthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and typically have five petals.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils and is often found in riparian zones.
