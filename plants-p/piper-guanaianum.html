Piper guanaianum

Piper guanaianum, commonly known as Guianese pepper, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their medicinal properties.

The plant's botanical characteristics include opposite, ovate leaves and a distinctive, pungent aroma.
