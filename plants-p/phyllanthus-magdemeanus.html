Phyllanthus magdemeanus

Phyllanthus magdemeanus, commonly known as the Magdemean spurge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant is characterized by its small, green, ovate leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. It produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Phyllanthus magdemeanus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
