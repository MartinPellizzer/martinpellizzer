Pitcairnia sodiroi

Pitcairnia sodiroi, commonly known as the Sodiro's pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage, which forms a central tank to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red flowers that bloom in the late afternoon. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
