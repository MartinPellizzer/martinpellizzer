Parablechnum chauliodontum

Parablechnum chauliodontum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It has small, narrow, succulent leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring or early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep water sources.

It is often found in sandy soils and is well adapted to withstand drought conditions.
