Peristrophe bicalyculata

Peristrophe bicalyculata, commonly known as the two-lobed piper, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes other aromatic plants like pepper. The plant is characterized by its small, opposite leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are capsules that split open to release tiny seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
