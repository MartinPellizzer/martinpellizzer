Psorothamnus emoryi var. emoryi

Psorothamnus emoryi var. emoryi, commonly known as Emory's greasewood, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are typically covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
