Panicularia fendleriana

Panicularia fendleriana, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces delicate, blue to purple flowers arranged in panicle-like clusters. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.

Its botanical features reflect its evolutionary adaptations to survive in extreme climatic conditions.
