Polygonum humifusum var. mandshuricum

Polygonum humifusum var. mandshuricum, commonly known as the Far Eastern knotgrass, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant has slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture and are often tinged with red or purple.

The small, greenish flowers appear in clusters and are followed by three-lobed seed capsules.
