Pterocarpus obtusatus

Pterocarpus obtusatus, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark reddish-brown wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic and commercial qualities. The tree has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a flat, pod-like structure containing several flat, kidney-shaped seeds.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in traditional medicine.
