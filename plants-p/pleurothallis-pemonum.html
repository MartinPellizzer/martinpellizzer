Pleurothallis pemonum

Pleurothallis pemonum, commonly known as the Pemon orchid, is a species native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate and often colorful floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are small but striking, often displaying a mix of white, pink, and purple hues.

Pleurothallis pemonum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
