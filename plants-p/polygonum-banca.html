Polygonum banca

Polygonum banca, commonly known as the common knotgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that often branch near the base, and its alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish midrib. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Polygonum banca is known for its adaptability and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
