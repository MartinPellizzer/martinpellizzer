Plumeria leucantha

Plumeria leucantha, commonly known as the white frangipani, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its woody stem is smooth and can become branched with age.

Plumeria leucantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
