Panicum juergensii

Panicum juergensii, commonly known as purpletop millet, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and spikes of purple or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Panicum juergensii is a hardy perennial that is drought-tolerant and suitable for native plant landscaping.
