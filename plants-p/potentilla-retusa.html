Potentilla retusa

Potentilla retusa, commonly known as the shrub cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, giving it its common name, and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes, where it tolerates full sun to partial shade.

Potentilla retusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
