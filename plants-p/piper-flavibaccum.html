Piper flavibaccum

Piper flavibaccum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers and elongated, slender leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
