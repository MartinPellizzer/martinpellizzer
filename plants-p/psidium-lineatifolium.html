Psidium lineatifolium

Psidium lineatifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, yellowish fruits. Its botanical features include smooth, greyish bark and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in tropical regions.
