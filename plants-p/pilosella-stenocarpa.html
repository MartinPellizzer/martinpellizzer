Pilosella stenocarpa

Pilosella stenocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruited hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, with small, yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular florets. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Pilosella stenocarpa is typically perennial, with a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
