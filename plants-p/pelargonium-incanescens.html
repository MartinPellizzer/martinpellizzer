Pelargonium incanescens

Pelargonium incanescens, commonly known as the silver geranium, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its pale grayish-green foliage, which is covered in fine, white hairs that give it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires plenty of sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is a low-maintenance plant ideal for arid or semi-arid climates.
