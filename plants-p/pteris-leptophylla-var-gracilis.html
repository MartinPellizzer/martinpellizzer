Pteris leptophylla var. gracilis

Pteris leptophylla var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It features finely divided, arching fronds that are typically bright green and have a soft, feathery appearance. The plant thrives in shady, humid environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
