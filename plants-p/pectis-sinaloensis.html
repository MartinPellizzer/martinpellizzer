Pectis sinaloensis

Pectis sinaloensis, commonly known as the Sinaloan pectis, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pectis sinaloensis is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
