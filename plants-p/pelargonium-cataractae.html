Pelargonium cataractae

Pelargonium cataractae, commonly known as the waterfall geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range from pink to red. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found growing near waterfalls or in humid environments.

Pelargonium cataractae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to cascade from containers or hanging baskets.
