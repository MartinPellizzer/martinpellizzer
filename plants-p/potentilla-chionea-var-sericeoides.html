Potentilla chionea var. sericeoides

Potentilla chionea var. sericeoides, commonly known as silverleaf potentilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, silvery-gray leaves that give it a distinctive, almost metallic appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting foliage.
