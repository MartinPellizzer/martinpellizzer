Plantago depressa f. minor

Plantago depressa f. minor, commonly known as the lesser plantain, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features broad, oval-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in meadows.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
