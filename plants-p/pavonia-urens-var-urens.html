Pavonia urens var. urens

Pavonia urens var. urens, commonly known as the common pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, upright stems. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and woodland edges.
