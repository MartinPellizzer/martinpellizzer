Prasophyllum uvidulum

Prasophyllum uvidulum, commonly known as the slender leek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and delicate, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or loamy soils in open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands. It is a short-lived perennial, often flowering for only one season before dying, making it a rare and ephemeral species.

Due to habitat loss and fragmentation, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
