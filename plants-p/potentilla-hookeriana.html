Potentilla hookeriana

Potentilla hookeriana, commonly known as Hooker's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
