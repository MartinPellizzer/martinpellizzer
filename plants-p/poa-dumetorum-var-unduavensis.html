Poa dumetorum var. unduavensis

Poa dumetorum var. unduavensis, commonly known as the undulating meadowgrass, is a variant of the common bentgrass.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically thrives in moist, temperate environments, often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers.

The species is notable for its adaptability to varying soil conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland areas.
