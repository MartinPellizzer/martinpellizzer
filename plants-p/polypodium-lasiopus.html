Polypodium lasiopus

Polypodium lasiopus, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of fern found in various regions around the world.

It is characterized by its creeping rhizome and the production of fertile fronds that bear sporangia. The plant typically grows in rocky or shaded environments, often on soil or stone. Its leaves are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive appearance that aids in identification.

Polypodium lasiopus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
