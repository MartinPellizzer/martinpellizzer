Pilosella cochlearis subsp. subauricula

Pilosella cochlearis subsp.

subauricula, commonly known as the auricled cat's ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb with a rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically clustered in a single head, surrounded by white or pale green bracts. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Its name refers to the ear-like structures on the leaves, which are a distinctive botanical feature.
