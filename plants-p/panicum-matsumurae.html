Panicum matsumurae

Panicum matsumurae, commonly known as Japanese foxtail grass, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue.

It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into bristly spikelets, contributing to its ornamental appeal in some regions.
