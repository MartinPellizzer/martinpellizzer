Pyrus aureola

Pyrus aureola, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers and small, edible fruits. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while the bark is smooth and grayish in color. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.

Its fruit, though not as widely consumed as other apples, is valued for its unique flavor and nutritional content.
