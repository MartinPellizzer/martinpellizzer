Ptilagrostis malyschevii

Ptilagrostis malyschevii, commonly known as the Malyschev's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, arching stems that produce feathery panicles of small, spikelet-bearing flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some regions for landscaping purposes.
