Potentilla rydbergii

Potentilla rydbergii, commonly known as Rydberg's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features wiry, hairy stems and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in the spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, which are often covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla rydbergii thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and mountain slopes.
