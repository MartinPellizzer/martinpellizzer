Parosela porteri

Parosela porteri, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blossoms. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, thriving in cool and moist environments. Its roots are adapted to anchor in loose soil, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinct scent.

Parosela porteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
