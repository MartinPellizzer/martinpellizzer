Prunus serrulata f. caudata

Prunus serrulata f. caudata, commonly known as the weeping cherry, is a botanical variant of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that droop gracefully, creating a distinctive weeping appearance. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in spring, which are followed by red berries in the autumn. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and parks.

Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice for landscape design.
