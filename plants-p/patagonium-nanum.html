Patagonium nanum

Patagonium nanum, commonly known as the dwarf Patagonian grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its compact size and resilience make it an important component of the local flora in Patagonia.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
