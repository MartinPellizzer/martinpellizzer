Primula helvetica

Primula helvetica, commonly known as the Swiss primrose, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils. It is often found in mountain meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
