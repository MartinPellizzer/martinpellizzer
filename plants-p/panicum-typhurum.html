Panicum typhurum

Panicum typhurum, commonly known as yellow panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fluffy, yellowish-green inflorescences that resemble a tuft of hair. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soil conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.

Its fibrous roots help prevent soil erosion, and it is often used in ecological restoration projects.
