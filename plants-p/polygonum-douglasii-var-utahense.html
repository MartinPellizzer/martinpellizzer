Polygonum douglasii var. utahense

Polygonum douglasii var. utahense, commonly known as Utah jointweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and canyon walls. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red, blooming in late summer.

Utah jointweed is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environments.
