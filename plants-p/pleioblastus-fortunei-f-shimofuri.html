Pleioblastus fortunei f. shimofuri

Pleioblastus fortunei f. shimofuri, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a variant of the fortune bamboo characterized by its striking silvery-blue foliage.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is native to East Asia, particularly Japan. The plant features slender, arching culms that are often used in ornamental gardening for their elegant appearance. Its leaves are narrow, glossy, and have a unique metallic sheen that enhances its visual appeal.

This variety is prized for its cold hardiness and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
