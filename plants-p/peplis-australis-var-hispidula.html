Peplis australis var. hispidula

Peplis australis var. hispidula, commonly known as the hairy peplis, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence, which gives it its common name. Its small, pale green leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture.

It produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
