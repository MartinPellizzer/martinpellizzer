Plectranthus marquesii

Plectranthus marquesii, commonly known as the Cape marquesia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Plectranthus marquesii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
