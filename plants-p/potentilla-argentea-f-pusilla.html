Potentilla argentea f. pusilla

Potentilla argentea f. pusilla, commonly known as silver potentilla dwarf form, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, silver-white leaves that are densely covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
