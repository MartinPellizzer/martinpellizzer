Pelargonium pumilum

Pelargonium pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf geranium, is a compact, trailing plant native to South Africa.

It features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. The flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive, showy appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
