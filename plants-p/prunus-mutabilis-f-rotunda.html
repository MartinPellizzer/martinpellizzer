Prunus mutabilis f. rotunda

Prunus mutabilis f. rotunda, commonly known as the round-leaved cherry, is a unique variant of the Prunus mutabilis species.

It is characterized by its distinctive rounded, velvety leaves that are broader and more rounded than those of the typical species. The plant produces small, white flowers that transition to pink and then red as they age, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal color changes.
