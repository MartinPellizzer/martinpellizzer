Paspalum falcatum subsp. microcarpum

Paspalum falcatum subsp.

microcarpum, commonly known as the narrowleaf buffalograss, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its seeds are small and have a distinctive, three-angled shape.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
