Prunus divaricata var. nachitschevanica

Prunus divaricata var. nachitschevanica, commonly known as the Caucasian cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
