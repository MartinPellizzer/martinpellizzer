Prunus dubia

Prunus dubia, commonly known as the Chinese white cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its fruits are small drupes that turn red when mature, often attracting birds and other wildlife.

Prunus dubia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
