Pittosporum ligustrifolium

Pittosporum ligustrifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf pittosporum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine white or grey pubescence, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Pittosporum ligustrifolium is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and support native wildlife.
