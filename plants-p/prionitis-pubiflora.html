Prionitis pubiflora

Prionitis pubiflora, commonly known as the hairy fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Prionitis pubiflora is noted for its short flowering period, often blooming in late summer to early autumn.
