Psiadia altissima subsp. serrata

Psiadia altissima subsp.

serrata, commonly known as the Sierra dandelion, is a flowering plant native to western North America. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, serrated leaves and produces yellow composite flowers.

The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more pronounced leaf serrations compared to other subspecies of Psiadia altissima.
