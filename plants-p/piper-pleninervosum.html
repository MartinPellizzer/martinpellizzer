Piper pleninervosum

Piper pleninervosum, commonly known as the veined pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with prominently veined foliage, and its small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
