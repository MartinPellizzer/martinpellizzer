Prunus cerasifera subsp. turcomanica

Prunus cerasifera subsp.

turcomanica, commonly known as the Turkman cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia. It features ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated margin.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping.
