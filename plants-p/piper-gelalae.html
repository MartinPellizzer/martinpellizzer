Piper gelalae

Piper gelalae, commonly known as the Tuluva pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper and long pepper. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines. Its leaves are also utilized in herbal remedies due to their aromatic properties.

The botanical characteristics of Piper gelalae make it an important species in the region's biodiversity and ethnobotanical practices.
