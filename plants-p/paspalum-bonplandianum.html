Paspalum bonplandianum

Paspalum bonplandianum, commonly known as the bonplandian grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Paspalum bonplandianum is often found in disturbed areas and is used in some regions for forage or erosion control.
