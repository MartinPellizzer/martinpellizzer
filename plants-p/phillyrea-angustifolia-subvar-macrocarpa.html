Phillyrea angustifolia subvar. macrocarpa

Phillyrea angustifolia subvar. macrocarpa, commonly known as the broad-leaved Phillyrea, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are typically broader than those of the typical variety. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, woody fruits. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to coastal and arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience in Mediterranean climates.
