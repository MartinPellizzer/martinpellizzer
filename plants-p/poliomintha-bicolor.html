Poliomintha bicolor

Poliomintha bicolor, commonly known as the pink and blue germander, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, narrow leaves and its striking, two-toned flowers that range from pink to blue. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open areas.

Poliomintha bicolor is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.
