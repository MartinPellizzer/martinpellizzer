Primula × crocata

Primula × crocata, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm, with hairy stems and oval, dark green leaves. Native to Europe, it thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural landscapes.
