Palafoxia linearis var. glandulosa

Palafoxia linearis var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular palafoxia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils and is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The glandular hairs on its stems and leaves are a distinguishing botanical feature.
