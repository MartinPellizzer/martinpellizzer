Paxistima myrsinites var. major

Paxistima myrsinites var. major, commonly known as the rock pineapple, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features fleshy, waxy, and succulent leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits containing seeds. It is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy plant for xerophytic gardens.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for landscaping in arid environments.
