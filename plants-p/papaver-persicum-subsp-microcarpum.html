Papaver persicum subsp. microcarpum

Papaver persicum subsp.

microcarpum, commonly known as the Persian poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caucasus and parts of Central Asia. It features striking, showy flowers that range from deep red to orange, with a distinctive saucer-shaped corolla.

The plant has smooth, lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. Its capsule fruits are small and dehiscent, releasing seeds through pores.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
