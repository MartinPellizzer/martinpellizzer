Phyllostachys stimulosa

Phyllostachys stimulosa, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a fast-growing grass species native to eastern China.

It belongs to the bamboo family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant yellow-green color, which intensifies in sunlight. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to form dense clumps.

Phyllostachys stimulosa is also valued for its use in landscaping and as a sustainable material for crafts and construction.
