Polygonum aequale subsp. oedocarpum

Polygonum aequale subsp.

oedocarpum, commonly known as the broadleaf knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive arrow-shaped leaves.

The plant features basal leaves that are broad and heart-shaped, often with a reddish or purple tint on the underside. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, typically appearing in late summer.

The subspecies is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in moist, disturbed soils.
