Posoqueria maxima

Posoqueria maxima, commonly known as the giant swamp taro, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to three meters in diameter, making it one of the largest leaves of any plant species. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments with rich, muddy substrates. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in certain regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other familiar plants like calla lilies and peace lilies.
