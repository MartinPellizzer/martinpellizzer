Pleurothallis nubensis

Pleurothallis nubensis, commonly known as the cloud forest orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, intricate structure. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is notable for its ability to grow on tree branches without harming its host.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
