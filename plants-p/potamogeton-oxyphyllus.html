Potamogeton oxyphyllus

Potamogeton oxyphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically less than 1 cm wide, arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are either male or female, with the female flowers often found at the base of the stem. It thrives in slow-moving or still waters and plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its habitat.
