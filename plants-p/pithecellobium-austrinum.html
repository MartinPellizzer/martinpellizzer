Pithecellobium austrinum

Pithecellobium austrinum, commonly known as the Australian burr legume, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain hard, seed-like structures. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small animals.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in reforestation projects.
