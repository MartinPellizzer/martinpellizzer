Pleurothallis oxyglossa

Pleurothallis oxyglossa, commonly known as the silver-tongued orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its inflorescence is a raceme of small, showy blooms that appear during the rainy season.

Pleurothallis oxyglossa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
