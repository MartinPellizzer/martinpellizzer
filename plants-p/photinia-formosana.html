Photinia formosana

Photinia formosana, commonly known as the Taiwan photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its bright red to orange-red flowers that appear in clusters during the spring. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy green upper surface with a lighter underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it an attractive choice for landscaping and horticultural purposes.
