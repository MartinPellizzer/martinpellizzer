Pachysa ardens

Pachysa ardens, commonly known as the fiery pachysandra, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, glossy leaves that range from dark green to almost black in color. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
