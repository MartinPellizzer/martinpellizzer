Phyteuma spicatum subsp. occidentale

Phyteuma spicatum subsp.

occidentale, commonly known as Western spiked gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

The plant features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in native plant communities.
