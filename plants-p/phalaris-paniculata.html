Phalaris paniculata

Phalaris paniculata, commonly known as the panicled lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems with long, narrow leaves that are usually blue-green in color. The plant produces feathery, branched inflorescences that bloom in summer, adding a delicate ornamental appeal. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ecological value includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
