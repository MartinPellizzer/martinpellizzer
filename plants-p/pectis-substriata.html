Pectis substriata

Pectis substriata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pectis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Pectis substriata is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
