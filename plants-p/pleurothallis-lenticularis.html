Pleurothallis lenticularis

Pleurothallis lenticularis, commonly known as the lens-shaped orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its unique, lens-shaped pseudobulbs are a distinguishing characteristic.

Pleurothallis lenticularis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
