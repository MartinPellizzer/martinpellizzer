Phyllostegia fagerlindii

Phyllostegia fagerlindii, commonly known as the Fagerlind's mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the understory of forests.

Phyllostegia fagerlindii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
