Parietaria linguifolia

Parietaria linguifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf lungwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodland floors and along stream banks. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and it produces achenes as its fruit.

Parietaria linguifolia is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.
