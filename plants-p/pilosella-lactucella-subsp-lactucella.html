Pilosella lactucella subsp. lactucella

Pilosella lactucella subsp.

lactucella, commonly known as the common mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features dense, woolly foliage with small, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common and recognizable member of the Asteraceae family.
