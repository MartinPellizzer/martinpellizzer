Prunus padus f. pulchra

Prunus padus f. pulchra, commonly known as the white-flowered bird cherry, is a cultivar of the bird cherry tree.

It is characterized by its showy, white blossoms that appear in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant has small, oval leaves that turn yellow in autumn, contributing to its seasonal appeal. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for a variety of climates.

This cultivar is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities and relatively low maintenance requirements.
