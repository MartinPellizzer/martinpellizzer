Periloba longifolia

Periloba longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved periloba, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential pharmacological applications.
