Pothos armatus

Pothos armatus, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb using its aerial roots. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers indirect light.

Its foliage is not only ornamental but also has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
