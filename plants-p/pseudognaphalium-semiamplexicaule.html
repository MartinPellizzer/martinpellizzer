Pseudognaphalium semiamplexicaule

Pseudognaphalium semiamplexicaule, commonly known as the narrow-leaf everlasting flower, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features basal and alternate leaves that are narrow and often slightly clasping the stem, giving it its specific epithet. It produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a common sight in arid and semi-arid environments.
