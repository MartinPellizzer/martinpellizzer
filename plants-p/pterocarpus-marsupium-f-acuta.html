Pterocarpus marsupium f. acuta

Pterocarpus marsupium f. acuta, commonly known as the Indian bedellium or the red sandalwood, is a variant of the Pterocarpus marsupium species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its thick, flaky bark and distinctive red or reddish-brown heartwood. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a woody pod containing several seeds, and the plant is valued for its medicinal properties due to the presence of tannins and other bioactive compounds.
