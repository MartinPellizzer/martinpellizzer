Papaver alaskanum var. macranthum

Papaver alaskanum var. macranthum, commonly known as the Alaska poppy, is a striking wildflower native to the coastal regions of Alaska and western Canada.

It is characterized by its large, showy, orange-red to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils along shorelines and in coastal meadows. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique appearance.
