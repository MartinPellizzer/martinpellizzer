Phrynium griffithii

Phrynium griffithii, commonly known as the giant arrowhead plant, is a tropical perennial belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length, often with a distinctive reddish or purple midrib. The plant produces striking spadices, which are inflorescences composed of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

Phrynium griffithii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
