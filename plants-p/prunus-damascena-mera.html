Prunus damascena mera

Prunus damascena mera, commonly known as the Damask rose, is a species of shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated edge.

Prunus damascena mera is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of rose oil and other essential oils.
