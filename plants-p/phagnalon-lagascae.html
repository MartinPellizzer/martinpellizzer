Phagnalon lagascae

Phagnalon lagascae, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phagnalon, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
