Phaius fragrans

Phaius fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant marsh orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves and large, showy flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy habitats and requires high humidity and consistent moisture. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-yellow with a distinctive sweet fragrance.

Phaius fragrans is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
