Penthysa glauca

Penthysa glauca, commonly known as the blue glume grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, blue or purple flowers in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Penthysa glauca is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
