Piper arboreum var. paucinervium

Piper arboreum var. paucinervium, commonly known as the slender pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, vine-like growth habit and small, opposite leaves. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin, and they often have a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
