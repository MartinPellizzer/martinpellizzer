Pseudotsuga menziesii subsp. oaxacana

Pseudotsuga menziesii subsp.

oaxacana, commonly known as the Oaxacan fir, is a subspecies native to the high elevations of central Mexico. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and conical shape.

The tree typically grows in temperate rainforests, thriving in moist, cool climates with well-drained soil. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against environmental stressors.

This subspecies plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting diverse flora and fauna.
