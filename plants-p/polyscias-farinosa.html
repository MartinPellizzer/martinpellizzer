Polyscias farinosa

Polyscias farinosa, commonly known as the silver soursop, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with a waxy, silver-tinged underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by round, green berries with a sour taste. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Polyscias farinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
