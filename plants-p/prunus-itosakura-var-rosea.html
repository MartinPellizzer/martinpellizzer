Prunus itosakura var. rosea

Prunus itosakura var. rosea, commonly known as the pink-flowered Japanese cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is prized for its showy, single or double pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant has small, oval leaves that turn yellow in autumn, contributing to its seasonal appeal. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for temperate climates.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic beauty and is a popular choice in traditional Japanese gardens.
