Polygala tristis

Polygala tristis, commonly known as the weeping lady's-tresses, is a species of orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that resemble a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along forest margins, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
