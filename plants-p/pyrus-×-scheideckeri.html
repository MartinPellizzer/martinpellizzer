Pyrus × scheideckeri

Pyrus × scheideckeri, commonly known as the Scheidecker pear, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Pyrus communis (European pear) and Pyrus pyraster (sloe).

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 5–10 meters, featuring broad, lobed leaves and clusters of white flowers in spring. The fruit is small, typically less than 2 cm in diameter, with a thick, dark purple skin and a tart, aromatic flavor.

This plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its unique appearance and potential use in hybrid breeding programs.
