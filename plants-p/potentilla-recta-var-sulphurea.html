Potentilla recta var. sulphurea

Potentilla recta var. sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, and its stems are often covered in a fine, sulfur-yellow coating, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for alpine gardens.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
