Prostanthera lepidota

Prostanthera lepidota, commonly known as the velvet-leaved mintbush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color. Its leaves are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a soft texture.

Prostanthera lepidota is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in coastal and inland regions.
