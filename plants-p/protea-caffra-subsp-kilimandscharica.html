Protea caffra subsp. kilimandscharica

Protea caffra subsp.

kilimandscharica, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family. It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous papery bracts that resemble flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing a contrasting texture to the floral display.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
