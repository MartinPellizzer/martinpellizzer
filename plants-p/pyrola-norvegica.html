Pyrola norvegica

Pyrola norvegica, commonly known as the Norwegian cowberry, is a herbaceous evergreen plant native to the northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, and it thrives in acidic, moist soils.

Pyrola norvegica is often found in shaded forest environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
