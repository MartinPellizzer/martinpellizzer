Phyllostachys zhejiangensis

Phyllostachys zhejiangensis, commonly known as Zhejiang bamboo, is a species of grass in the bamboo family, Poaceae.

It is native to China, particularly the Zhejiang province, and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a bright green color, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.

This bamboo is also valued for its strong and durable stalks, which are used in construction and crafts.
