Polypodium lepidopteris var. sepultum

Polypodium lepidopteris var. sepultum, commonly known as the buried shield fern, is a variant of the broad-leafed shield fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a fern with a distinctive, leathery, evergreen frond that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant typically thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest undergrowth. Its fronds are deeply divided, with a unique appearance that resembles a shield, giving it its common name.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
