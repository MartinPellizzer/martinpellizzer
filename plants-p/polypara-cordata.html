Polypara cordata

Polypara cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf polypara, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Polypara cordata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
