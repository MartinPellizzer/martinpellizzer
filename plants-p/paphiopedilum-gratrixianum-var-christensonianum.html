Paphiopedilum gratrixianum var. christensonianum

Paphiopedilum gratrixianum var. christensonianum, commonly known as the Christenson's lady's slipper, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple petals and a distinctive, pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper, a feature typical of the Paphiopedilum genus. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments with partial shade, often growing on tree roots or in rocky crevices. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer, are highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts for their beauty and rarity.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
