Pitcairnia staminea var. longicauda

Pitcairnia staminea var. longicauda, commonly known as the long-tailed pitcairnia, is a bromeliad native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces striking, pendulous inflorescences with showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

This variety is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological significance in its native habitat.
