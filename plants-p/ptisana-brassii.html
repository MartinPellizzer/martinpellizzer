Ptisana brassii

Ptisana brassii, commonly known as the blueberry lily, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its attractive blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are similar in taste to blueberries, though they are not true berries botanically. Ptisana brassii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its foliage, which remains attractive throughout the growing season.
