Persea haenkeana

Persea haenkeana, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, orange-yellow fruits that are edible when fully mature. Its botanical features include a smooth, gray bark and a tendency to grow in tropical and subtropical climates.

Persea haenkeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
