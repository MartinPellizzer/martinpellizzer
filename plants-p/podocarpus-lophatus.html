Podocarpus lophatus

Podocarpus lophatus, commonly known as the New Zealand pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The plant has scale-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture and landscaping.
