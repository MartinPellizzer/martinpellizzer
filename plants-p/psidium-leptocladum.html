Psidium leptocladum

Psidium leptocladum, commonly known as the Brazilian guava or small-leaved guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible berries similar to guavas. Its leaves are typically small, elliptical, and have a smooth texture.

The botanical characteristics of Psidium leptocladum make it an interesting subject for horticultural and ecological studies.
