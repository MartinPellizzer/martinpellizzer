Polygala subandina

Polygala subandina, commonly known as the Andean milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, adapting to high-altitude conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, while its stems are wiry and branching.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
