Polystachya euspatha

Polystachya euspatha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flame plant, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly found in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stem and clusters of bright orange to red flowers that resemble flames. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern at the base.

Polystachya euspatha thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
