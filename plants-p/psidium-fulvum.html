Psidium fulvum

Psidium fulvum, commonly known as the golden guava, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, with a strong, aromatic scent.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
