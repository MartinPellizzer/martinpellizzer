Peperomia subroseispica

Peperomia subroseispica, commonly known as the rose-veined peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, ovate leaves with a distinctive rose-colored vein pattern that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of green or red, which are followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its low maintenance and attractive foliage make it a favored ornamental plant.
