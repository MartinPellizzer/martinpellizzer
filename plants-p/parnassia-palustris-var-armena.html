Parnassia palustris var. armena

Parnassia palustris var. armena, commonly known as the Armenian marsh gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine meadows. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
