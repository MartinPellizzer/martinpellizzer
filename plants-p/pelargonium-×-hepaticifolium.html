Pelargonium × hepaticifolium

Pelargonium × hepaticifolium, commonly known as the liverleaf geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves that resemble the shape of a liver, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to South Africa and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and flowers.

Pelargonium × hepaticifolium prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for gardens and containers.
