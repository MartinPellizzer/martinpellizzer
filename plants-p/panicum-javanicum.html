Panicum javanicum

Panicum javanicum, commonly known as Java grass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces inflorescences that are spike-like, with small, pale green to golden flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
