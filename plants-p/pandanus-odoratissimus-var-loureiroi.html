Pandanus odoratissimus var. loureiroi

Pandanus odoratissimus var. loureiroi, commonly known as the sweet-scented pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, aromatic leaves that emit a fragrant scent, especially when crushed. The plant produces distinctive, spadix-like inflorescences that bear small, white flowers. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is well adapted to sandy or rocky soils.
