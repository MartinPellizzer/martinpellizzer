Pyrus pannonica pseudosativa

Pyrus pannonica pseudosativa, commonly known as the Hungarian pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Carpathian Basin.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
