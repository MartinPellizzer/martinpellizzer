Poa poecila

Poa poecila, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a species of grass native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Poa poecila is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for invertebrates.
