Prunus armeniaca var. communis

Prunus armeniaca var. communis, commonly known as the common apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It produces showy, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are typically white or pale pink. The plant is characterized by its alternate, simple, ovate leaves and yellowish-orange fruit that ripens in late summer. The fruit contains a single hard pit surrounded by a fleshy, sweet pulp.

It is widely cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
