Phyllanthus micromeris var. mughessensis

Phyllanthus micromeris var. mughessensis, commonly known as the Kashmiri stonecrop, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water and withstand harsh climatic conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
