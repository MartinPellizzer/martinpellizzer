Psoralea hirta

Psoralea hirta, commonly known as hairy psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are hairy and often have a distinctive, lance-shaped appearance.

Psoralea hirta is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation in its ecosystem.
