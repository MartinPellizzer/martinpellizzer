Piper plagiophyllum

Piper plagiophyllum, commonly known as the white pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into berries containing the aromatic seeds used as a spice. Its botanical features include a woody stem, compound leaves, and a distinctive, pungent aroma.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is cultivated for its peppercorn-like seeds.
