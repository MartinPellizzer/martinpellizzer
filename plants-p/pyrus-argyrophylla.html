Pyrus argyrophylla

Pyrus argyrophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf pear, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by edible pears that are typically small and sweet. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in horticulture.
