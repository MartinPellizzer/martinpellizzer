Parapholis incurva subsp. longiflora

Parapholis incurva subsp.

longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered parapholis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale green to yellowish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and a slight sheen.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
