Psalina utriculosa

Psalina utriculosa, commonly known as the bladderleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, succulent leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.
