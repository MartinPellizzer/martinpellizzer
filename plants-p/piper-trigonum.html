Piper trigonum

Piper trigonum, commonly known as the Trinidad pepper or Mexican pepper plant, is a tropical climbing shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into three-lobed, berry-like fruits. Its aromatic leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
