Pinalia braccata

Pinalia braccata, commonly known as the feathered pincushion cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery spines that give it a distinctive, almost ornamental appearance. The plant has a short, thick stem that serves as a water storage organ, typical of desert flora. Its flowers, which are usually yellow or orange, bloom during the warmer months, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its striking appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil and full sun.
