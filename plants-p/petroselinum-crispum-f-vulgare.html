Petroselinum crispum f. vulgare

Petroselinum crispum f. vulgare, commonly known as parsley, is a biennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves that are often used as a garnish or flavoring in various cuisines. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its roots are also edible and have a slightly stronger flavor compared to the leaves.

Parsley is rich in vitamins and minerals, making it both a culinary and nutritional staple.
