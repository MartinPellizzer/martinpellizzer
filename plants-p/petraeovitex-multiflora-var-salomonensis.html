Petraeovitex multiflora var. salomonensis

Petraeovitex multiflora var. salomonensis, commonly known as the Solomon Islands vitex, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal or lowland areas. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white or pale purple. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in its native regions.
