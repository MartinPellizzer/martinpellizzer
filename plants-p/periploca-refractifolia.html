Periploca refractifolia

Periploca refractifolia, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has woody stems and produces a milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature. Its leaves are typically opposite and have a slightly hairy texture.

Periploca refractifolia is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
