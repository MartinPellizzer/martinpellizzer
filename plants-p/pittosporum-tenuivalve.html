Pittosporum tenuivalve

Pittosporum tenuivalve, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that are often clustered in terminal panicles, followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Pittosporum tenuivalve is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
