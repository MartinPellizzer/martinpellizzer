Parthenium argentatum var. angustifolium

Parthenium argentatum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and wetlands. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters. Its botanical characteristics include hairy stems and bracts that are often mistaken for petals.

It is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
