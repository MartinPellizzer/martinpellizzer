Piper ottonoides

Piper ottonoides, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and often have a peppery scent. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into three-lobed berries.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
