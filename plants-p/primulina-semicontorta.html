Primulina semicontorta

Primulina semicontorta, commonly known as the silver-leafed begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage, which has a velvety texture and is often marked with reddish veins. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white, typically appearing in late spring or early summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to maintain its vibrant appearance.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular choice for indoor gardens and shaded outdoor spaces.
