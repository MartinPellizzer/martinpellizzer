Polycnemum echinus

Polycnemum echinus, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, cylindrical inflorescences that bear small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Polycnemum echinus plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
