Polypodium tenuinerve

Polypodium tenuinerve, commonly known as the slender-veined rock fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with fine, parallel veins. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizome is usually creeping and produces spore-bearing leaves called sporangia.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
