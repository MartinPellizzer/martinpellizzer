Prunus mandshurica f. barbinervis

Prunus mandshurica f. barbinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined Manchurian cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its lanceolate leaves with prominent, narrow veins. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in spring, followed by edible, dark red berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.
