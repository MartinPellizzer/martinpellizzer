Potamogeton epihydrus subsp. nuttallii

Potamogeton epihydrus subsp.

nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America. It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually borne on separate male and female inflorescences.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
