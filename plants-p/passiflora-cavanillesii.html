Passiflora cavanillesii

Passiflora cavanillesii, commonly known as the Cavanilles passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed fresh or used in jams and beverages. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Passiflora cavanillesii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
