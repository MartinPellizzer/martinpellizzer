Persicaria interrupta

Persicaria interrupta, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other species like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its reddish stems and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slight pubescence.

Persicaria interrupta is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
