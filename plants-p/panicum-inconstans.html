Panicum inconstans

Panicum inconstans, commonly known as the variable panicgrass, is a flowering plant species belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it blooms during the summer months.

Panicum inconstans is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
