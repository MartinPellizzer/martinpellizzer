Potentilla aurea var. pectinata

Potentilla aurea var. pectinata, commonly known as the golden cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves with five leaflets. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The species is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
