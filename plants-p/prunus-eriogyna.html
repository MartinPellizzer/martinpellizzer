Prunus eriogyna

Prunus eriogyna, commonly known as the Japanese white cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Prunus eriogyna is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often planted for its seasonal floral display.
