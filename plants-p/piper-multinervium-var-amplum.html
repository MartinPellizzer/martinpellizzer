Piper multinervium var. amplum

Piper multinervium var. amplum, commonly known as the broad-veined pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with prominent, broad veins that give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
