Priestleya ericifolia

Priestleya ericifolia, commonly known as the Ericaceous Priestleya, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats. It has small, leathery leaves and produces pink or white blooms in the spring.

Priestleya ericifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
