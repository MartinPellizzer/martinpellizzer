Piper lucbanense

Piper lucbanense, commonly known as the Lucban pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its small, elongated fruits that are often used as a spice. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, with dark green leaves and yellow flowers. Its botanical features include pungent, aromatic fruits and a preference for tropical climates.

It is valued both for its culinary uses and its ornamental appeal.
