Pleurothallis papillisepala

Pleurothallis papillisepala, commonly known as the hairy-sepal pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers and unique, hairy sepals that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a subtle fragrance.

Pleurothallis papillisepala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
