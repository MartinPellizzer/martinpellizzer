Persicaria maximowiczii

Persicaria maximowiczii, commonly known as slender hogweed.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia, characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in late summer, which are arranged in dense, terminal inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
