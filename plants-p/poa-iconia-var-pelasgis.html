Poa iconia var. pelasgis

Poa iconia var. pelasgis, commonly known as the Pelasgian bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with mild winters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
