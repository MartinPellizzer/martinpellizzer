Phlox adsurgens

Phlox adsurgens, commonly known as the climbing phlox, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial vine that produces showy clusters of pink or red flowers in the spring. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and a woody stem that can climb by means of tendrils. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
