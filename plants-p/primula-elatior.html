Primula elatior

Primula elatior, commonly known as the Alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically features rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers cool, moist conditions.

Primula elatior is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and alpine settings.
