Parodia mutabilis var. carneospina

Parodia mutabilis var. carneospina, commonly known as the red-spined pincushion cactus, is a succulent native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically green or reddish in color, with spines that can vary in hue from yellow to red. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its spiny structure. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a desirable addition to both indoor and outdoor gardens.
