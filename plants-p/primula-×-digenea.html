Primula × digenea

Primula × digenea, commonly known as the Japanese primrose, is a hybrid species within the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors, including pink, white, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and features oval, hairy leaves with a pointed tip. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to early-season gardens.
