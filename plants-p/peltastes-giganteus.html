Peltastes giganteus

Peltastes giganteus, commonly known as the giant peltastes, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to two meters in length, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant produces strikingly colorful flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in high humidity and partial shade, requiring well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to habitat loss, Peltastes giganteus is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
