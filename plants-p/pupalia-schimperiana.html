Pupalia schimperiana

Pupalia schimperiana, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are striking, with five purple or violet petals arranged in a bell-like shape, and it blooms from late winter to early spring.

Pupalia schimperiana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
