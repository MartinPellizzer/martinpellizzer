Pseudopinanga urosperma

Pseudopinanga urosperma, commonly known as the hairy palm, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its clustered growth habit and pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are dark green and covered with fine, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
