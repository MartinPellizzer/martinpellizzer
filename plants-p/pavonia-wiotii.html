Pavonia wiotii

Pavonia wiotii, commonly known as the scarlet pavonia, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes. The plant is characterized by its showy, red or pink bract-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Pavonia wiotii plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, particularly pollinators like bees and butterflies.
