Phaseolus coccineus subsp. darwinianus

Phaseolus coccineus subsp.

darwinianus, commonly known as the Darwin's scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to South America. It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers and long, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds.

The plant produces compound leaves with three to five leaflets and has a vining growth habit. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible produce.

This subspecies is closely related to other Phaseolus species and shares similar botanical traits.
