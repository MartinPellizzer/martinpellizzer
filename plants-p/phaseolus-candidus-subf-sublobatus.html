Phaseolus candidus subf. sublobatus

Phaseolus candidus subf.

sublobatus, commonly known as the white bean or white phaseolus, is a member of the Fabaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with small, white or pale green leaflets.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing small, white or cream-colored seeds. It is native to the Americas and is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein.

The botanical features of this species make it an important plant in both traditional agriculture and ecological studies.
