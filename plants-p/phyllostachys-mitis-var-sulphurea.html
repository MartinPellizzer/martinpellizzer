Phyllostachys mitis var. sulphurea

Phyllostachys mitis var. sulphurea, commonly known as the yellow bamboo, is a variant of the silver bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-green culms that turn golden in the sun, giving it a unique visual appeal. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky sheen. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

This variety is also valued for its resilience and ability to form dense clumps over time.
