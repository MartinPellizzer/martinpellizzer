Prunus plantierensis

Prunus plantierensis, commonly known as the Madagascar cherry or plantier's cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its small, bright red berries that are edible. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth margin, while its flowers are white with five petals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
