Papaver muscicola

Papaver muscicola, commonly known as the musk poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking red petals with a dark purple center and a distinctive, fragrant scent. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and aroma, it is occasionally cultivated for ornamental purposes.
