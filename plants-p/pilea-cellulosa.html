Pilea cellulosa

Pilea cellulosa, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that resemble small coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that develop into round, berry-like fruits. It is a popular indoor plant due to its ease of care and attractive foliage.

Pilea cellulosa thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
