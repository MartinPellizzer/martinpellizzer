Pseudolithos caput-viperae

Pseudolithos caput-viperae, commonly known as the viper's head stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
