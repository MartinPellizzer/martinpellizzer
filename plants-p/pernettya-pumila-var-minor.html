Pernettya pumila var. minor

Pernettya pumila var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf cowberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It features small, dark blue to black berries that are edible and often used in traditional cuisine. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies of alpine and tundra environments.
