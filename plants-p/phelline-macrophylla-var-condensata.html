Phelline macrophylla var. condensata

Phelline macrophylla var. condensata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed phelline, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and ability to attract pollinators.
