Pyranthus lucens

Pyranthus lucens, commonly known as the shining pyranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaryllidaceae and is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Pyranthus lucens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
