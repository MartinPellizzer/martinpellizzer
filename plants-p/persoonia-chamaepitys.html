Persoonia chamaepitys

Persoonia chamaepitys, commonly known as the low shrub waratah, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a small, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and leathery, elliptical leaves. The plant produces striking, cone-shaped inflorescences that bear numerous small, white or cream-colored flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the dry, sunny conditions of its natural habitat.

Persoonia chamaepitys is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in coastal and sandy environments.
