Phaius longicruris

Phaius longicruris, commonly known as the long-leg orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which helps it store water and nutrients during dry periods. Its leaves are usually long and strap-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Phaius longicruris thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.
