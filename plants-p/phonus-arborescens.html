Phonus arborescens

Phonus arborescens, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy foliage that helps reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by winged fruits that aid in wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
