Passiflora organensis var. costata

Passiflora organensis var. costata, commonly known as the Costate Passionflower, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The leaves are typically ovate with a rough, hairy texture and prominent veins. This plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its fruit, a small, edible berry, is also used in traditional medicine.
