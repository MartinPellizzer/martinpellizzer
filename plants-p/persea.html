Persea

Persea, commonly known as the avocado tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a broadleaf evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit, which is botanically classified as a berry, contains a single large seed and is rich in healthy fats. The tree thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
