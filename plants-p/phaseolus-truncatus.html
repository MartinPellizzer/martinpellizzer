Phaseolus truncatus

Phaseolus truncatus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and large, showy flowers that are typically red or pink in color. The plant produces edible pods that contain kidney-shaped beans, which are often used in culinary applications. Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and serrated.
