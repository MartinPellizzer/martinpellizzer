Pinellia ternata f. atropurpurea

Pinellia ternata f. atropurpurea, commonly known as purple-flowered pinellia, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Pinelliaceae and is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Its rhizomes are valued for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.

The varietal form 'atropurpurea' is distinguished by its deep purple floral coloration, making it a popular ornamental plant.
