Poa bactriana subsp. bactriana

Poa bactriana subsp.

bactriana, commonly known as the Bactrian grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, tufted growth and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and grasslands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers.

This subspecies is well adapted to arid conditions and is often used for forage in agricultural settings.
