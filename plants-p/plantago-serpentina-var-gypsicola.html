Plantago serpentina var. gypsicola

Plantago serpentina var. gypsicola, commonly known as the gypsum plant or desert plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. This plant is adapted to grow in arid and semi-arid environments, particularly in soils rich in gypsum. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, typically appearing in late spring to early summer.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in dry landscapes.
