Poa nemoralis var. mongolica

Poa nemoralis var. mongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian wood sedge, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia, particularly Mongolia and parts of China.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to cool climates and can thrive in partial to full shade.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
