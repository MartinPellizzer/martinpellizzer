Primula × widmerae

Primula × widmerae, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Europe.

It is characterized by its semi-evergreen foliage and produces clusters of bell-shaped, brightly colored flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically blooms in early spring, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking early-season color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in shaded or partially shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is widely grown in both garden beds and containers.
