Payera marojejyensis

Payera marojejyensis, commonly known as the Marojejy star grass, is a rare plant species endemic to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in the unique microclimates of the Marojejy Mountains, where it is adapted to high altitudes and cool temperatures. Its conservation status is critically endangered due to habitat loss and limited distribution.

Payera marojejyensis plays a vital role in the ecological balance of its native environment.
