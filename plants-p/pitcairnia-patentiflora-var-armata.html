Pitcairnia patentiflora var. armata

Pitcairnia patentiflora var. armata, commonly known as the velvet pitcairnia, is a bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red bracts that surround the inflorescence. The plant produces showy, white flowers that emerge from a central spike. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and greenhouse cultivation.
