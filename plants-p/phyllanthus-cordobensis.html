Phyllanthus cordobensis

Phyllanthus cordobensis, commonly known as the Cordoban phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a feature common in the Euphorbiaceae family. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
