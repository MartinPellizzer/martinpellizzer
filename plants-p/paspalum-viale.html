Paspalum viale

Paspalum viale, commonly known as velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and fine, silky-textured leaves that give it a soft appearance. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Paspalum viale is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
