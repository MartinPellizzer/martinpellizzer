Pygeum parvifolium

Pygeum parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved African maple, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange-red drupes. Its bark is smooth and grayish, often exuding a milky sap when damaged.

Pygeum parvifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
