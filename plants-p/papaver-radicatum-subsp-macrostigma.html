Papaver radicatum subsp. macrostigma

Papaver radicatum subsp.

macrostigma, commonly known as the large-seeded poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It features showy, bright red to orange flowers with a distinctive, broad central stigma.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

The seeds are large and enclosed in a capsule, which splits open to release the seeds when mature.
