Peperomia bakeri

Peperomia bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the humid lowlands of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a subtle variegation, often adorned with red or purple veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish in color, which appear in clusters along the stems. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate humidity.

Peperomia bakeri is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
