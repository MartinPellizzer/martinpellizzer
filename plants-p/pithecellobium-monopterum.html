Pithecellobium monopterum

Pithecellobium monopterum, commonly known as the single-winged pithecellobium, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a single, elongated leaflet that gives it its common name. It produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by edible, bean-like pods.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of traditional food in its native habitat.
