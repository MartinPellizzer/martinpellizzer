Pyrus fontanesiana

Pyrus fontanesiana, commonly known as the Fontanesia pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white flowers that appear in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a silvery underside, adding to its aesthetic appeal.

The fruit is a small, edible pear that ripens in late summer, though it is not as widely cultivated as other pear species.
