Philodendron wilburii var. longipedunculatum

Philodendron wilburii var. longipedunculatum, commonly known as the Wilburii Philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length, often with a glossy green surface and prominent veins. The plant produces striking, elongated inflorescences with small, white flowers, though it is not typically cultivated for its blooms. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

This variety is prized for its dramatic foliage and is a popular choice among indoor plant enthusiasts.
