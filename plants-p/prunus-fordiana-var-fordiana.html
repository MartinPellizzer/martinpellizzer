Prunus fordiana var. fordiana

Prunus fordiana var. fordiana, commonly known as the Chinese bird cherry, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and smooth, gray bark. The tree produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, followed by bright red, edible berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms and autumn foliage.
