Penstemon acaulis

Penstemon acaulis, commonly known as the rock penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial species that thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and arid environments. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, tubular flowers come in shades of red, purple, and yellow, attracting pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
