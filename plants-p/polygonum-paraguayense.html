Polygonum paraguayense

Polygonum paraguayense, commonly known as the Paraguay knotweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as buckwheats. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, lance-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as riverbanks and wetlands.

Polygonum paraguayense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
