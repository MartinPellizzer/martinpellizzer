Polylepis racemosa subsp. triacontandra

Polylepis racemosa subsp.

triacontandra, commonly known as the Andean wax tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the high Andes of South America. It is characterized by its dense, silver-white foliage and distinctive, cone-like inflorescences.

The plant thrives in cool, humid climates at elevations between 2,500 and 4,000 meters. Its leaves are leathery and covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

This subspecies is often found in cloud forests and is adapted to the extreme conditions of the Andean region.
