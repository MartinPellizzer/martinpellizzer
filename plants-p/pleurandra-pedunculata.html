Pleurandra pedunculata

Pleurandra pedunculata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
