Prunus × koshiensis

Prunus × koshiensis, commonly known as the Koshi cherry, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its ornamental value due to its showy pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while the fruit is a small, dark red drupe.

Prunus × koshiensis is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
