Piper pulchrum var. costaricense

Piper pulchrum var. costaricense, commonly known as the Costa Rican pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is native to Central America, particularly Costa Rica, and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into tiny, peppery berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable species in botanical studies and horticulture.
