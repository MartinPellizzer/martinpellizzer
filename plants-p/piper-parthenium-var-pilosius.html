Piper parthenium var. pilosius

Piper parthenium var. pilosius, commonly known as the hairy pepper plant, is a variant of the common black pepper plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into dark, peppery fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and fruits.

The varietal name "pilosius" refers to the hairy texture of its foliage, a key botanical feature.
