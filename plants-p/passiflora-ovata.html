Passiflora ovata

Passiflora ovata, commonly known as the yellow passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that are edible and contain a sweet, pulpy interior. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and are often used in traditional medicine.

Passiflora ovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
