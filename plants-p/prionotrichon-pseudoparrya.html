Prionotrichon pseudoparrya

Prionotrichon pseudoparrya, commonly known as the false parry's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spikelet-like flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its ecosystem.
