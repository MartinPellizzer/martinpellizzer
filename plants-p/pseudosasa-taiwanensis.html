Pseudosasa taiwanensis

Pseudosasa taiwanensis, commonly known as Taiwan bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Taiwan and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it an important species in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
