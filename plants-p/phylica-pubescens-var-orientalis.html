Phylica pubescens var. orientalis

Phylica pubescens var. orientalis, commonly known as the oriental bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble a bottlebrush, composed of numerous small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is relatively drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid regions.
