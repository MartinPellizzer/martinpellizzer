Philodendron ricardoi

Philodendron ricardoi, commonly known as the golden knight philodendron, is a climbing vine native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that often exhibit a striking golden or variegated pattern. The plant has a robust stem that allows it to climb and trail through the dense canopy of its natural habitat. Its foliage is often adorned with a waxy texture and can vary in color depending on light exposure.

Philodendron ricardoi is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
