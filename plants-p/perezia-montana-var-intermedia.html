Perezia montana var. intermedia

Perezia montana var. intermedia, commonly known as the mountain perezia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pale purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is a favored choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
