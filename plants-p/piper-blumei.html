Piper blumei

Piper blumei, commonly known as the blue passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five blue petals and a vibrant yellow corona. The plant belongs to the Piperaceae family and is known for its aromatic leaves and showy blooms. Its flowers are pollinated by both insects and birds, making it an important species in tropical ecosystems.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine.
