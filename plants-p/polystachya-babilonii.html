Polystachya babilonii

Polystachya babilonii, commonly known as the Babylonian spikegrass, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
