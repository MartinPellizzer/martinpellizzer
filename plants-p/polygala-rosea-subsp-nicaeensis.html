Polygala rosea subsp. nicaeensis

Polygala rosea subsp.

nicaeensis, commonly known as the Nicaean milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight pubescence.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
