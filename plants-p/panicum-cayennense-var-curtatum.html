Panicum cayennense var. curtatum

Panicum cayennense var. curtatum, commonly known as the Cayenne panicgrass, is a variant of the panic grass genus native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, delicate spikelets that are often wind-pollinated. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and have a slight sheen, while the inflorescence is usually compact and erect.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
