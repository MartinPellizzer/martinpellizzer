Platysepalum violaceum var. bequaertii

Platysepalum violaceum var. bequaertii, commonly known as the purple-flowered flatsepal orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking violet to purple flowers. The plant features lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its flowers are arranged in dense inflorescences, with showy sepals and petals that are typically longer than the dorsal sepal.

This variety is prized in horticulture for its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
