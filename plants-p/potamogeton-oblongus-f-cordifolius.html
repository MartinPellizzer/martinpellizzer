Potamogeton oblongus f. cordifolius

Potamogeton oblongus f. cordifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It features long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically broad and heart-shaped, giving it its specific epithet "cordifolius.". The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, usually emerging from the water surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often indicative of good water quality and stable ecological conditions.
