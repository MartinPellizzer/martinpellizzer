Pharmacum storckii

Pharmacum storckii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique floral biology and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest in both botanical research and traditional medicine.
