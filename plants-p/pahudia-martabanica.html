Pahudia martabanica

Pahudia martabanica, commonly known as the Martaban sand orchid, is a rare and specialized plant found in the mountainous regions of Myanmar.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, delicate flowers that bloom in the dry season. The plant thrives in sandy soils and requires specific microclimatic conditions to survive. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered.

Conservation efforts are essential to protect this botanical gem from further decline.
