Parthenocissus texana

Parthenocissus texana, commonly known as the Texas ivy, is a climbing vine native to the southern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a slightly glossy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its vigorous growth habit makes it suitable for covering walls, trellises, and fences.

However, it can be invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
