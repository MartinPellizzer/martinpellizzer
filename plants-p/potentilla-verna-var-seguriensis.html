Potentilla verna var. seguriensis

Potentilla verna var. seguriensis, commonly known as the spring potentilla, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, delicate yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has slender, upright stems and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
