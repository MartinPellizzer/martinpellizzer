Philgamia glabrifolia

Philgamia glabrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf philgamia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southwestern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to hot, dry environments and has a deep root system to access groundwater. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Philgamia glabrifolia is often found in rocky soils and is an important species in desert ecosystems.
