Pseudotsuga glauca var. argentea-pendula

Pseudotsuga glauca var. argentea-pendula, commonly known as the silver-haired hemlock, is a rare and ornamental variant of the hemlock tree.

It is characterized by its striking silvery-white undersides on its juvenile leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to specific regions in the Pacific Northwest of North America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its foliage remains evergreen, providing year-round visual interest in gardens or natural landscapes.

Due to its rarity and unique characteristics, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in specialized horticultural settings.
