Plantago shastensis

Plantago shastensis, commonly known as Shasta plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is valued for its medicinal and ecological benefits.
