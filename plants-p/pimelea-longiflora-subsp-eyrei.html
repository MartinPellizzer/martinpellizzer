Pimelea longiflora subsp. eyrei

Pimelea longiflora subsp.

eyrei, commonly known as the Eyre's candlebush, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is a shrub characterized by its erect, spindly stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated flowers and specific geographic distribution.
