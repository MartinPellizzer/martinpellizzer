Poa platyantha var. vivipara

Poa platyantha var. vivipara, commonly known as the viviparous meadow grass, is a variant of the meadow grass species native to certain regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences that are typically green to purplish in color. One notable feature is its viviparous nature, where seeds may germinate while still attached to the parent plant.

This trait can aid in vegetative propagation and is a unique adaptation in its ecological niche.
