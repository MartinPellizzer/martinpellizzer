Persicaria hydropiper var. sarbhanganica

Persicaria hydropiper var. sarbhanganica, commonly known as Indian water pepper, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purple-tinged, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in spikes. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize wetlands make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
