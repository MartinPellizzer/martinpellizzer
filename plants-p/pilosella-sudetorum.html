Pilosella sudetorum

Pilosella sudetorum, commonly known as the Sudetic hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, typical of the family. It grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring sunny, well-drained soils.

Pilosella sudetorum is notable for its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its species name "pilosella.".
