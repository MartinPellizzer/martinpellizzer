Pelargonium × labyrinthicum

Pelargonium × labyrinthicum, commonly known as the cascading geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces small, five-lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and container gardens.

Its ability to produce copious amounts of flowers makes it a favorite among gardeners seeking colorful, long-blooming displays.
