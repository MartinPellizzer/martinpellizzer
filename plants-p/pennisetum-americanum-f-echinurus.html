Pennisetum americanum f. echinurus

Pennisetum americanum f. echinurus, commonly known as purple muhly, is a ornamental grass native to the United States.

It features slender, upright stems that produce dense clusters of purple to pinkish plumes during the summer months. The plant is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade. It is drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking color and feathery appearance, which make it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
