Pongamia volubilis var. glaucophylla

Pongamia volubilis var. glaucophylla, commonly known as the blue-leaf pongam, is a variant of the pongam tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its glaucous, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive bluish-gray appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by leguminous pods containing oily seeds. These seeds are rich in fatty acids and have been used in traditional medicine and as a source of biofuel.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological and industrial applications.
