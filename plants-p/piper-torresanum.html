Piper torresanum

Piper torresanum, commonly known as the Torresanum pepper or mountain pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by three-lobed berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
