Primula lungchiensis

Primula lungchiensis, commonly known as the Lungchi primrose, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments at high altitudes, often in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a hairy surface, and it produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
