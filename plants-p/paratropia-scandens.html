Paratropia scandens

Paratropia scandens, commonly known as the climbing paratropia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a vine that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often climbing trees or other structures for support. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly heart-shaped base.

The botanical features of Paratropia scandens make it an interesting subject for study in plant morphology and ecology.
