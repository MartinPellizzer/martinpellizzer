Pachyphyllum micranthum

Pachyphyllum micranthum, commonly known as the rock cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Pachyphyllum micranthum is well adapted to harsh climates and is often found growing on rocky outcrops.
