Potentilla fragarioides var. caespitosa

Potentilla fragarioides var. caespitosa, commonly known as silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, silver-green leaves that are covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine, rocky, or disturbed areas.

Silverweed is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in challenging environments.
