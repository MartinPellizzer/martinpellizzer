Pelea clusiifolia var. dumosa

Pelea clusiifolia var. dumosa, commonly known as the woolly evergreen hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a red or purple center. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a leathery texture.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its vibrant flowers and unique foliage.
