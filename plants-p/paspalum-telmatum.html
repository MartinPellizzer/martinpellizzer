Paspalum telmatum

Paspalum telmatum, commonly known as marsh paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It thrives in wetland and marshy environments, often found in regions with high humidity and saturated soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It is adapted to tolerate waterlogged conditions, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
