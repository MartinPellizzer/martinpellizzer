Plantago collipubens

Plantago collipubens, commonly known as the common plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It features a basal rosette of broad, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces upright flowering stalks bearing small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in cultivated fields.

Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
