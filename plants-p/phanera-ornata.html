Phanera ornata

Phanera ornata, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood in some regions.
