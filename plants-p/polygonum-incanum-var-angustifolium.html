Polygonum incanum var. angustifolium

Polygonum incanum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf sandmat, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a bluish-green hue, and are arranged alternately along the stems. Small, clustered flowers appear in late summer, producing pale pink or reddish blooms.

This plant is adapted to drought conditions and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.
