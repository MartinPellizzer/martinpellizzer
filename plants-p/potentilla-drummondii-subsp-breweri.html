Potentilla drummondii subsp. breweri

Potentilla drummondii subsp.

breweri, commonly known as Drummond's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and canyon walls.

The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval leaflets, and it has a compact, bushy growth habit.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native range.
