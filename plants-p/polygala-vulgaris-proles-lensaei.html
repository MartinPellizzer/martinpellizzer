Polygala vulgaris proles lensaei

Polygala vulgaris proles lensaei, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spurred flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Its ecological role includes supporting pollinators and contributing to biodiversity in its native range.
