Potentilla montenegrina

Potentilla montenegrina, commonly known as the Montenegrin cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves are compound, with five oval leaflets, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

Potentilla montenegrina is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
