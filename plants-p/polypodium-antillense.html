Polypodium antillense

Polypodium antillense, commonly known as the Antillean shield fern, is a terrestrial fern native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its creeping rhizome and dense, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive shield-shaped appearance.

Polypodium antillense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its lush, evergreen foliage.
