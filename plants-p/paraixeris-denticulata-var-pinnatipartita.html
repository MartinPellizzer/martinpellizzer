Paraixeris denticulata var. pinnatipartita

Paraixeris denticulata var. pinnatipartita, commonly known as the toothed paraixeris, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and subtle floral display.
