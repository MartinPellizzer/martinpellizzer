Pyrus pollweriana

Pyrus pollweriana, commonly known as the Pollwerian pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in parts of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its deciduous leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like apple, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
