Phoebe effusa var. parvifolia

Phoebe effusa var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved phoebe, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in umbels, which are often white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions with high humidity.

The leaves and bark contain essential oils, which contribute to its aromatic properties and have been used in traditional medicine.
