Piper uncatum var. levyanum

Piper uncatum var. levyanum, commonly known as the West Indian pepper or Levyan's pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruits are typically three-lobed berries that mature to a dark purple color.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
