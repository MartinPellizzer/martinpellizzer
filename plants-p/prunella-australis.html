Prunella australis

Prunella australis, commonly known as Australian heal-all, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. The plant features slender, upright stems and produces small, blue or purple flowers in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

Prunella australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world.
