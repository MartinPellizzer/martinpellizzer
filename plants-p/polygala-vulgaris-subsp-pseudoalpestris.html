Polygala vulgaris subsp. pseudoalpestris

Polygala vulgaris subsp.

pseudoalpestris, commonly known as the Alpine milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, contributing to its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in alpine ecosystems.
