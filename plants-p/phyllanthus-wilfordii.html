Phyllanthus wilfordii

Phyllanthus wilfordii, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a shrub native to regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small greenish-yellow flowers that give way to bright orange, berry-like fruits. These fruits are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine for their antioxidant properties.

The plant's botanical features make it well-suited for cultivation in tropical and subtropical climates.
