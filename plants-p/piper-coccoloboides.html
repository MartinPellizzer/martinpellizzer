Piper coccoloboides

Piper coccoloboides, commonly known as the Caribbean pepper or sweet pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It produces small, red berries that are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of culinary ingredients.
