Pycnostachys remotifolia

Pycnostachys remotifolia, commonly known as the remote-leaved evergreen, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and indoor cultivation.
