Piper uleanum

Piper uleanum, commonly known as the West Indian pepper or piperac, is a flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies for its purported health benefits.
