Polygala martiana var. decumbens

Polygala martiana var. decumbens, commonly known as the decumbent milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
