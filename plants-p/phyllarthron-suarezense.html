Phyllarthron suarezense

Phyllarthron suarezense, commonly known as the Suarez's phyllarthron, is a unique plant species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate leaf structure, which consists of multiple layers of tightly packed, overlapping foliage. The plant exhibits a distinctive growth habit, often forming dense, compact clusters that help it thrive in shaded environments. Its leaves are typically green with a waxy surface, aiding in water retention in humid climates.

Phyllarthron suarezense is of particular interest to botanists due to its unusual morphology and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
