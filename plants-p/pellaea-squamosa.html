Pellaea squamosa

Pellaea squamosa, commonly known as the silver frond fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its distinctive, silvery-gray fronds covered in minute scales. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

Pellaea squamosa is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
