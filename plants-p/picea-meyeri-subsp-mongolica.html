Picea meyeri subsp. mongolica

Picea meyeri subsp.

mongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian fir, is a coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of Mongolia and northern China. It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure typical of the fir genus.

The plant has a slender, upright growth habit and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

The species is valued for its timber and is also used in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.
