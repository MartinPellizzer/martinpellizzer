Phacelia pulchella f. rubella

Phacelia pulchella f. rubella, commonly known as the red-flowered phacelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking deep red to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting pollinator populations.
