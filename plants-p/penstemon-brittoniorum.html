Penstemon brittoniorum

Penstemon brittoniorum, commonly known as Britton's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial species that thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically gray-green and hairy, helping to reduce water loss in dry climates.

Penstemon brittoniorum is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
