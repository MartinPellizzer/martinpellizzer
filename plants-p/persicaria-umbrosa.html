Persicaria umbrosa

Persicaria umbrosa, commonly known as shadowplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a suitable candidate for wetland restoration projects.
