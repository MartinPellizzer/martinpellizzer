Potentilla tabernaemontani proles pseudoincisa

Potentilla tabernaemontani proles pseudoincisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It typically features slender, upright stems covered in fine, grayish hairs. The leaves are narrow, elliptical, and have a serrated margin, often with a hairy underside. Its small, yellow flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

This plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
