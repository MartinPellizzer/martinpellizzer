Parthenium argentatum var. deltoideum

Parthenium argentatum var. deltoideum, commonly known as silverleaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, white hairs, and its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces smooth, green, egg-shaped fruits that contain toxic seeds, making it a potential weed in agricultural areas. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Due to its allelopathic properties, it can inhibit the growth of other plants, contributing to its competitive advantage in arid environments.
