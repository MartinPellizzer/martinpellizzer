Polemonium foliolatum

Polemonium foliolatum, commonly known as the leafy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctively toothed margin. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its foliage and floral structure make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
