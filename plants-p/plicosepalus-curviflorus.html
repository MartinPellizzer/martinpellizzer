Plicosepalus curviflorus

Plicosepalus curviflorus, commonly known as the wavy-petaled flamebush, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy-edged petals that give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in tropical or subtropical environments, often in moist, shaded areas. Its flowers are usually bright red or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The leaves are alternate and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.
