Protea reticulata

Protea reticulata, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous tubular flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The plant features a robust, woody stem with a fibrous, spongy bark that aids in water retention. Its leaves are typically broad, leathery, and covered in a fine, silvery-white waxy coating, which reflects sunlight and reduces water loss.

Protea reticulata thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation, adapting well to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.
