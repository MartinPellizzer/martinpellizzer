Phyllanthus sieboldianus

Phyllanthus sieboldianus, commonly known as the Japanese false flax, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and riverbanks. The plant has small, clustered leaves and produces yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain numerous seeds.

Phyllanthus sieboldianus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and resilience.
