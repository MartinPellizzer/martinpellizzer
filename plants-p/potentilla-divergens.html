Potentilla divergens

Potentilla divergens, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to North America.

It features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or rocky areas.

Potentilla divergens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native plant gardens.
