Phaseolus coccineus var. pringlei

Phaseolus coccineus var. pringlei, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a cultivated variant of the common bean.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers and elongated, edible pods. The plant is a vigorous climber, often requiring support such as trellises or stakes. It thrives in warm climates and well-drained soil, producing beans that are rich in protein and nutrients.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and culinary use in various cuisines.
