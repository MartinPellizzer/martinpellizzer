Panicum orinocanum

Panicum orinocanum, commonly known as Orinoco grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and floodplains. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration.
