Perrierangraecum pseudodidieri

Perrierangraecum pseudodidieri, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated leaves and striking, showy flowers that often resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees without deriving nutrients from them. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to flourish.

Due to its rarity and ecological sensitivity, it is often protected and studied for conservation purposes.
