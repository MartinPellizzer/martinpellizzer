Pectis cubensis

Pectis cubensis, commonly known as Cuban pectis, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, showcasing a robust root system adapted to saline conditions. Its foliage is usually hairy, and the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Pectis cubensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
