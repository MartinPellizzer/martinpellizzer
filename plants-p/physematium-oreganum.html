Physematium oreganum

Physematium oreganum, commonly known as Oregon grape, is a perennial shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces dark blue to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional herbal medicine. Its rhizomes contain berberine, a compound with antimicrobial properties.

Oregon grape thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland habitats.
