Persica vulgaris f. camelliiflora

Persica vulgaris f. camelliiflora, commonly known as the camellia-flowered peach.

This botanical variant is a cultivar of the peach tree, characterized by its unique floral structure that resembles camellia flowers. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with dense foliage and showy, double-petaled blossoms in shades of pink and white. It is primarily grown as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal rather than for fruit production.

The cultivar is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various climate conditions.
