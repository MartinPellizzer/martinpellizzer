Pimela laxiflora

Pimela laxiflora, commonly known as the broad-leaved water spinach, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often submerged or floating, with long, slender stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in wetlands and rice paddies.

Pimela laxiflora is valued for its edible leaves, which are used in various culinary traditions across its native range.
