Passiflora lineariloba

Passiflora lineariloba, commonly known as the slender passionflower, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a unique blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with intricate filaments and a crown of stamens. The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and can climb by means of tendrils.

Passiflora lineariloba is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
