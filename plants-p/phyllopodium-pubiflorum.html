Phyllopodium pubiflorum

Phyllopodium pubiflorum, commonly known as the hairy-flowered phyllopodium, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring broad, leaf-like structures that resemble modified leaves or stems. The plant produces small, hairy flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural range.
