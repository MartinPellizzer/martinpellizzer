Potentilla mayana

Potentilla mayana, commonly known as the yellow cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in the spring and early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided into five leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla mayana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
