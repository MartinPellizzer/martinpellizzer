Prunella × intermedia

Prunella × intermedia, commonly known as common selfheal, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Prunella vulgaris and Prunella lanceolata. The plant typically grows to a height of 20–60 cm, featuring square stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, tubular flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem.

It is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and woodland edges, and is known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.
