Punctillaria sesquiuncialis

Punctillaria sesquiuncialis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water-milfoil, is a small aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around its stem. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and lakes, where it can anchor itself to the substrate. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often appearing in clusters near the water's surface.

Punctillaria sesquiuncialis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
