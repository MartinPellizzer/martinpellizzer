Pentocnide

Pentocnide, commonly known as the sandalwood tree, is a member of the Santalaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a lanceolate shape.

The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal.
