Protea rubropilosa

Protea rubropilosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun. Its unique floral structure attracts pollinators such as birds and insects, playing a vital role in its reproductive cycle.

Protea rubropilosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
