Polypodium boreale

Polypodium boreale, commonly known as the northern wall fern, is a species of fern native to the northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are typically light green in color. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments such as rock crevices and shaded forest floors. Its rhizome is short and often covered in a thick layer of brownish mycorrhizal sheath.

Polypodium boreale is known for its ability to tolerate low light and is often found growing on limestone or sandstone substrates.
