Passiflora compar

Passiflora compar, commonly known as the yellow passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a vibrant yellow corona with intricate filaments. The plant produces orange to yellow berries that contain inedible, toxic seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough, hairy texture.

Passiflora compar is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in warm climates.
