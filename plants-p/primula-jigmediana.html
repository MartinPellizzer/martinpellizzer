Primula jigmediana

Primula jigmediana, commonly known as the Tibetan primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in early spring and is adapted to cold, mountainous environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a slightly hairy surface, and it is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
