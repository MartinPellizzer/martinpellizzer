Petalostemon purpureus

Petalostemon purpureus, commonly known as the purple petalostemon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-colored petals that are arranged in a distinctive spiral pattern. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
