Pelargonium grandiflorum

Pelargonium grandiflorum, commonly known as the geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The leaves are typically rounded or heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden beds and containers.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
