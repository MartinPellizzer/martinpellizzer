Pistolochia oppositifolia

Pistolochia oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved pistolpod, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite arrangement of lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is a shrub that can grow up to 1 meter in height, with woody stems and small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
