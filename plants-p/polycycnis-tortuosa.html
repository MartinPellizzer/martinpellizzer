Polycycnis tortuosa

Polycycnis tortuosa, commonly known as the spiral sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and distinctive spiral arrangement of its flowering spikes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in a helical pattern along its stem. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its unique morphology makes it an important species for ecological studies of wetland ecosystems.
