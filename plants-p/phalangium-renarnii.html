Phalangium renarnii

Phalangium renarnii, commonly known as the rare moonlight fern, is a unique species of fern characterized by its delicate, bioluminescent fronds.

Native to the misty highlands of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in shaded, humid environments with rich, well-draining soil. Its leaves emit a faint blue glow at night, an adaptation believed to aid in attracting nocturnal pollinators. The plant's reproductive structures are small and clustered, producing spores that disperse via wind.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, Phalangium renarnii is considered a conservation priority in its native habitat.
