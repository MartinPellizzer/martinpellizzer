Phalaenopsis sanderiana

Phalaenopsis sanderiana, commonly known as the Sander's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

The flowers can last for several months, making it a favored choice for indoor gardening.
