Psoralea

Psoralea, commonly known as the "butterfly pea," is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to various regions across Africa, Asia, and Australia, and is characterized by its distinctive, often blue or purple, pea-shaped flowers. The plants typically grow as shrubs or herbs, with compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. Many species of Psoralea are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Additionally, some species are cultivated for ornamental purposes due to their attractive floral display.
