Phyteuma halleri subvar. caerulescens

Phyteuma halleri subvar. caerulescens, commonly known as the blue phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flower color and long blooming period.
