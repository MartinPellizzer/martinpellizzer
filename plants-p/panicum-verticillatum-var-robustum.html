Panicum verticillatum var. robustum

Panicum verticillatum var. robustum, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and vibrant red to purple inflorescences that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in full sun and thrives in sandy or well-drained soils. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that becomes more reddish in the fall.

It is often used in ornamental landscaping for its attractive appearance and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
