Piper fallax

Piper fallax, commonly known as the black pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its dark green, ovate leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into three-lobed berries. Its fruit, which is typically black when mature, contains the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental and medicinal value.
