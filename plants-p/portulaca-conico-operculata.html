Portulaca conico-operculata

Portulaca conico-operculata, commonly known as the conical-capped purslane, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, fleshy leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in the morning and close by afternoon. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for arid environments.

Its unique conical-shaped seed capsules are a distinguishing botanical feature.
