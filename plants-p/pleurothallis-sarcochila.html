Pleurothallis sarcochila

Pleurothallis sarcochila, commonly known as the crimson spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red petals and sepals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting specific pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
