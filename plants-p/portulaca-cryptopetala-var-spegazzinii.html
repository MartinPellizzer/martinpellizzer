Portulaca cryptopetala var. spegazzinii

Portulaca cryptopetala var. spegazzinii, commonly known as the Spegazzini's moss rose, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, succulent perennial that features fleshy, hairy stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and yellow, which bloom in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and adaptability to harsh environments make it a notable species in botanical studies.
