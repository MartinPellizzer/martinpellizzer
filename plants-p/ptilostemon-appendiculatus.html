Ptilostemon appendiculatus

Ptilostemon appendiculatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ptilostemon, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in spring. It is adapted to survive in low-water environments, with deep roots and a waxy leaf surface to reduce water loss.

Ptilostemon appendiculatus is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in dry gardens.
