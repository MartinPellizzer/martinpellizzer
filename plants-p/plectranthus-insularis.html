Plectranthus insularis

Plectranthus insularis, commonly known as the island mint, is a perennial herb native to Madagascar.

It features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of violet or purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its aromatic leaves and ornamental value, it is popular in gardens and as a houseplant.
