Polygala forbesii

Polygala forbesii, commonly known as Forbes' milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, spindly stems and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Forbes' milkvetch is often found in arid or semi-arid regions and is adapted to thrive in poor soils.
