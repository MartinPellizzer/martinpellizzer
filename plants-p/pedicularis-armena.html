Pedicularis armena

Pedicularis armena, commonly known as the Armenian lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Pedicularis, which is part of the family Orobanchaceae, known for its hemiparasitic species. The plant features showy, spurred flowers that range in color from red to purple, often resembling the appearance of a louse, hence its common name. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Pedicularis armena is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
