Palmorchis pubescentis

Palmorchis pubescentis, commonly known as the pubescent orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, strap-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it an important subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.

Due to habitat destruction, it faces significant threats to its survival in the wild.
