Phlox longifolia var. tenuifolia

Phlox longifolia var. tenuifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf phlox, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
