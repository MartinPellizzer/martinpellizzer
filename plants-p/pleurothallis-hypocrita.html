Pleurothallis hypocrita

Pleurothallis hypocrita, commonly known as the white-lipped orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often epiphytic on trees or rocks. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Pleurothallis hypocrita is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its delicate appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
