Pinguicula immaculata

Pinguicula immaculata, commonly known as the white butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, sticky leaves that trap and digest small insects. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and requires high humidity to flourish.

Its unique adaptations make it an interesting subject for both botanists and plant enthusiasts.
