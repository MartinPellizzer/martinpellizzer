Papaver nudicaule var. pleiopetalum

Papaver nudicaule var. pleiopetalum, commonly known as the clustered poppy, is a flowering plant characterized by its delicate, papery petals.

It is a variant of the meadow poppy, known for its distinctive clustered arrangement of flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and meadows. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with a subtle, fragrant aroma.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
