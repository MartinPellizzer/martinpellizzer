Psammogeton canescens subsp. cabulicus

Psammogeton canescens subsp.

cabulicus, commonly known as the Afghan sandspurry, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The plant has small, fleshy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It is often found in habitats such as steppes, deserts, and along riverbanks.
