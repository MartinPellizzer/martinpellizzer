Pyrus doumeri

Pyrus doumeri, commonly known as the Doumer pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam and China. The plant is characterized by its deciduous leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like berry, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Pyrus doumeri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
