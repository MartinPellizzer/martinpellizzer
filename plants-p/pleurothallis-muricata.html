Pleurothallis muricata

Pleurothallis muricata, commonly known as the hairy pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and its ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often arranged in clusters. Its leaves are usually leathery and dark green, with a textured surface that aids in water retention.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique appearance and resilience.
