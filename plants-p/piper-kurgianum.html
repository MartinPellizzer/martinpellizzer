Piper kurgianum

Piper kurgianum, commonly known as the Kurgian pepper or Kurgianum pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants like black pepper. The plant produces small, elongated berries that are typically harvested when they turn red or yellow. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is valued for its aromatic fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some regional cuisines.
