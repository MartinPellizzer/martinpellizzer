Phyllodes straminea

Phyllodes straminea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, linear phyllodes that resemble leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which bloom in spring and attract pollinators like bees and birds. Its root system has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, contributing to soil fertility.

Phyllodes straminea is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
