Pimenta pseudocaryophyllus var. pseudocaryophyllus

Pimenta pseudocaryophyllus var. pseudocaryophyllus, commonly known as the West Indian allspice, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown berries that resemble small peppercorns. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into the aromatic fruit used in cooking and traditional medicine. Its leaves are oval-shaped, glossy, and emit a spicy fragrance when crushed.

The fruit is harvested when fully mature and dried to enhance its flavor and aroma.
