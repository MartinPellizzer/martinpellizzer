Prunus × cerea

Prunus × cerea, commonly known as the cherry plum, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus, characterized by its small to medium-sized fruit and ornamental value.

It typically produces sweet, edible fruits that range in color from yellow to dark red, depending on the variety. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, often cultivated for its attractive flowers and foliage. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Prunus × cerea is also valued for its use in landscaping and as a rootstock for other stone fruits.
