Plananthus fastigiatus

Plananthus fastigiatus, commonly known as the tall spikeplant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in a compact rosette at the base.

Plananthus fastigiatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
