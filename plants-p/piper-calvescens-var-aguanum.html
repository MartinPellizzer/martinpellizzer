Piper calvescens var. aguanum

Piper calvescens var. aguanum, commonly known as the Guatemalan pepper or wild pepper, is a perennial shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elongated berries that turn red when mature, containing a spicy, pungent seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications due to their unique flavor and medicinal properties.
