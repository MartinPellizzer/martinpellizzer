Paeonia wittmanniana f. macrophylla

Paeonia wittmanniana f. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved peony, is a rare and striking cultivar of the peony plant.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 20 cm in length, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, double flowers in shades of pink and white, typically in late spring. It is a deciduous herbaceous perennial that thrives in well-drained, fertile soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often grown in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
