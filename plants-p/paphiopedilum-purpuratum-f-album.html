Paphiopedilum purpuratum f. album

Paphiopedilum purpuratum f. album, commonly known as the white-petaled lady's slipper orchid, is a rare and striking hybrid of the Paphiopedilum genus.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with intricate patterns and a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch. This orchid is native to parts of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. It requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture to flourish.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
