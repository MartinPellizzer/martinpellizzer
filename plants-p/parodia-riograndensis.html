Parodia riograndensis

Parodia riograndensis, commonly known as the Rio Grande prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is a low-growing, spineless plant with a globular shape and a thick, fleshy stem that stores water. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
