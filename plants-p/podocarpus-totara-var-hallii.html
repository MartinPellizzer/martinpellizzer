Podocarpus totara var. hallii

Podocarpus totara var. hallii, commonly known as the kauri tree, is a coniferous evergreen native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dark green, needle-like foliage. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds. It thrives in temperate rainforests and is known for its slow growth and longevity.

The bark is thick and often flaky, providing protection against environmental stressors.
