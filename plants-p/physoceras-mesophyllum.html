Physoceras mesophyllum

Physoceras mesophyllum, commonly known as the common marsh mallow.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and marshes. Its flowers are borne on tall, slender stems and are pollinated by insects.

The plant is valued for its mucilaginous roots, which have been used in traditional medicine.
