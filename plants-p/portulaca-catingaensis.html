Portulaca catingaensis

Portulaca catingaensis, commonly known as the Brazilian moss rose, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is a low-growing, succulent species that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, vibrant flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom throughout the dry season. Its fleshy leaves and stems store water, enabling it to survive in drought conditions.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Portulacaceae and is characterized by its ability to photosynthesize through its leaves during the day and at night when temperatures are cooler.
