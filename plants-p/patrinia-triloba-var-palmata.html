Patrinia triloba var. palmata

Patrinia triloba var. palmata, commonly known as palmate patrinia, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
