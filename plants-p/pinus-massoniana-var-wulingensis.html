Pinus massoniana var. wulingensis

Pinus massoniana var. wulingensis, commonly known as the Wuling Chinese fir, is a rare variant of the Chinese fir native to central China.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant exhibits distinct morphological features, including its bark texture and foliage arrangement. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and moderate rainfall.

Due to its limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
