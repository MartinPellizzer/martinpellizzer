Pandanus freycinetioides

Pandanus freycinetioides, commonly known as the New Caledonian pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pandanus.

It is native to the island of New Caledonia and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often growing in sandy or muddy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other tropical regions for horticultural purposes.
