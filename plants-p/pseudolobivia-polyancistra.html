Pseudolobivia polyancistra

Pseudolobivia polyancistra, commonly known as the desert rose, is a striking cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a short, cylindrical stem covered in dense, woolly spines that provide insulation against extreme temperatures. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, which bloom during the warmer months. Its deep root system allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand long periods of drought.
