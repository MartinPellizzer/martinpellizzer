Panicum ternatum f. glabrispiculum

Panicum ternatum f. glabrispiculum, commonly known as the glabrous spike panicgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its reduced leaf hairs, giving it a smooth, glabrous appearance, and its compact, spike-like inflorescence. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows, across North America. Its small, two-lobed spikelets are often clustered closely together, contributing to its distinctive look.

The species is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
