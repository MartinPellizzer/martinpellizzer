Phyllostachys iridescens f. striata

Phyllostachys iridescens f. striata, commonly known as the variegated bamboo, is a striking ornamental grass native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that display a unique variegation of green, white, and yellow along the leaf blades. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, followed by fluffy seed heads that add visual interest. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This bamboo is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to create a natural, flowing landscape.
