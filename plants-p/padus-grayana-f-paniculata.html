Padus grayana f. paniculata

Padus grayana f. paniculata, commonly known as the grayana paniculate form, is a variant of the grayana species native to certain regions of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its compound leaves and showy, panicle-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a distinctive branching pattern that contributes to its ornamental value.

The species is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
