Pilosella lactucella subsp. magnauricula

Pilosella lactucella subsp.

magnauricula, commonly known as the large-leaf mouse-ear, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its hairy, basal leaves that form a rosette and small, yellowish composite flowers.

The subspecies is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in dry, rocky habitats. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.

The plant is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
