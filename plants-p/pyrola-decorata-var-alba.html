Pyrola decorata var. alba

Pyrola decorata var. alba, commonly known as the white flame azalea, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that remain throughout the year, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for woodland gardens and shaded landscapes.
