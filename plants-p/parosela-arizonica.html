Parosela arizonica

Parosela arizonica, commonly known as Arizona parosela, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
