Potentilla gracilis var. dichroa

Potentilla gracilis var. dichroa, commonly known as the silverleaf potentilla, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves that reflect light and provide a striking visual contrast against its green stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
