Pittosporoides

Pittosporoides, commonly known as the coastal pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces yellowish-orange berries that are an important food source for native birds. It thrives in coastal environments and is often found in sandy soils.

Pittosporoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in New Zealand's coastal ecosystems.
