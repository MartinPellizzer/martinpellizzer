Paphiopedilum henryanum var. christae

Paphiopedilum henryanum var. christae, commonly known as the Christa's lady's slipper, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety petals and a unique pouch-like lip that serves as a pollinator trap. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, showcasing a blend of white, pink, and purple hues.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
