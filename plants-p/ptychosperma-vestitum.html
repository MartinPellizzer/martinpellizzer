Ptychosperma vestitum

Ptychosperma vestitum, commonly known as the silky palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The silky palm is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in tropical gardens.
