Polypodium vulgare f. pygmaeum

Polypodium vulgare f. pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf maidenhair fern, is a fern species characterized by its small, compact fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are lance-shaped and have a delicate, feathery appearance.

This fern is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
