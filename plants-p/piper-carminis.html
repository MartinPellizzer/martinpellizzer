Piper carminis

Piper carminis, commonly known as the pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of small, heart-shaped leaves. Its fruit, a small berry, contains the aromatic compound responsible for its distinctive spicy flavor.

Piper carminis is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal properties.
