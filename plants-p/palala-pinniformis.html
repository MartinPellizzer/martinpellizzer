Palala pinniformis

Palala pinniformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf palala, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are pinnately divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish or white, and it produces tiny, capsule-like fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Palala pinniformis is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
