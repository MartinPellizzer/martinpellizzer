Phlomis spinidens

Phlomis spinidens, commonly known as spiked beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features upright stems covered in stiff, bristly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its narrow, lance-shaped leaves are typically gray-green and have a rough texture.

The flowers are pink to purple, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
