Phlegmariurus phlegmaria var. laxosporophyllus

Phlegmariurus phlegmaria var. laxosporophyllus, commonly known as the laxosporophyllous thongmoss, is a variety of the thongmoss family (Ptychothallaceae).

It is a small, mat-forming liverwort characterized by its flat, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in width. The leaves are usually green or reddish in color, and the plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporophytes. This variety is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its botanical features make it an important species for studying liverwort morphology and ecological adaptations.
