Penstemon clevelandii subsp. angelicus

Penstemon clevelandii subsp.

angelicus, commonly known as the Angel's Penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
