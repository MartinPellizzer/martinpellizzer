Phegopteris difformis

Phegopteris difformis, commonly known as the variable holly fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed fronds that vary in shape and size depending on the environment. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forests and stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
