Piper laterifissum

Piper laterifissum, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, opposite leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on different plants, and it produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
