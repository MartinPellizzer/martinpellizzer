Picris sonchoides var. leteae

Picris sonchoides var. leteae, commonly known as the summer sowthistle, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and basal rosettes of narrow, lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is considered a weed in many regions.
