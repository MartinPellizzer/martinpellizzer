Piper gatunense

Piper gatunense, commonly known as the Gatun pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly found in countries like Panama, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long stems that twine around supporting structures. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly rough texture.

The small, greenish-yellow flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by three-lobed berries that are typically reddish when mature.
