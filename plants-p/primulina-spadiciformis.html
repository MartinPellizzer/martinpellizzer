Primulina spadiciformis

Primulina spadiciformis, commonly known as the spadix-leafed gloxinia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and showy inflorescence. The plant features a short, erect stem with broad, heart-shaped leaves that have a waxy texture. Its flowers are typically clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe, creating an ornamental display.

Primulina spadiciformis is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
