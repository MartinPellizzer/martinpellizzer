Psidium aromaticum var. grandiflorum

Psidium aromaticum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the Natal plum or Natal cherry, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It features glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its edible, yellow to orange fruits, which are similar in appearance to cherries but have a distinctive, sweet scent. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The plant's botanical structure supports its adaptation to diverse environmental conditions in its native habitat.
