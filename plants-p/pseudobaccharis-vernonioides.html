Pseudobaccharis vernonioides

Pseudobaccharis vernonioides, commonly known as the Mexican flamebush, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Pseudobaccharis vernonioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for various insects and birds.
