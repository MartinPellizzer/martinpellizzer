Pultenaea hartmannii

Pultenaea hartmannii, commonly known as the Hartmann's hakea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces woody pods that contain several seeds.

Pultenaea hartmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
