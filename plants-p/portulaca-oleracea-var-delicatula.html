Portulaca oleracea var. delicatula

Portulaca oleracea var. delicatula, commonly known as the dwarf portulaca, is a low-growing annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features fleshy, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The leaves are typically oval and succulent, adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in disturbed soils or garden beds.

This variety is cultivated for its ornamental value and edible leaves, which have a slightly salty, peppery flavor.
