Phytarrhiza anceps

Phytarrhiza anceps, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a tuberous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its underground storage organs, which are used for propagation and sustenance. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit with broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are small and clustered in an inflorescence, typical of the Araceae family.

The botanical significance of Phytarrhiza anceps lies in its adaptability to various soil types and its potential as a food and medicinal resource.
