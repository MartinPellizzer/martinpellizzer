Pavetta graveolens

Pavetta graveolens, commonly known as the fragrant pavetta, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.

Pavetta graveolens is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
