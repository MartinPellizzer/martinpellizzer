Phyllostachys primotina

Phyllostachys primotina, commonly known as early bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its slender, upright culms that often grow in clusters. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow panicles of flowers, which are typically visible in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside.

Phyllostachys primotina is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its graceful appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
