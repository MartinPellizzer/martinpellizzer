Polygonum punctatum var. eciliatum

Polygonum punctatum var. eciliatum, commonly known as the spotted knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive spotted or mottled appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Its rapid growth and spreading habit make it both a common weed and an important food source for various insects and wildlife.
