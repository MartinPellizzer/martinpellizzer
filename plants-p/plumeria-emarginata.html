Plumeria emarginata

Plumeria emarginata, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that come in white, yellow, or pink hues. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its bark is smooth and pale, and it produces a milky sap that is toxic.

The plant is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
