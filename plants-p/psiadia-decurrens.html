Psiadia decurrens

Psiadia decurrens, commonly known as the creeping sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

Psiadia decurrens is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and its ornamental appeal in xeriscaped gardens.
