Poa spegazziniana

Poa spegazziniana, commonly known as the Spegazzini's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
