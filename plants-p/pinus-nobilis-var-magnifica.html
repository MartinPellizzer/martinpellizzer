Pinus nobilis var. magnifica

Pinus nobilis var. magnifica, commonly known as the Mediterranean stone pine, is a variant of the noble pine native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, evergreen foliage composed of long, needle-like leaves. The tree produces large, woody cones that contain edible seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to warm, dry climates.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in Mediterranean cultures.
