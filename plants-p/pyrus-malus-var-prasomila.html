Pyrus malus var. prasomila

Pyrus malus var. prasomila, commonly known as the Prasomila apple, is a lesser-known variety of apple with distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a cultivar of the domesticated apple, Malus domestica, and exhibits unique morphological traits such as a compact growth habit and a rounded shape. The leaves are typically dark green with a slightly pubescent underside, and the flowers are white with five petals. This variety is noted for its small, tart fruits that are often used in traditional culinary applications.

Its genetic makeup and phenotypic features make it an interesting subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
