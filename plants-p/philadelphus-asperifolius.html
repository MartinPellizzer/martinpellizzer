Philadelphus asperifolius

Philadelphus asperifolius, commonly known as the roughleaf mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and a rough texture, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Philadelphus asperifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscape design.
