Platydesma campanulatum var. pallidum

Platydesma campanulatum var. pallidum, commonly known as the pale bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to white flowers. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

The pale bellflower is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
