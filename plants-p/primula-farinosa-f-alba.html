Primula farinosa f. alba

Primula farinosa f. alba, commonly known as the white primrose, is a variant of the common primrose characterized by its pale white petals.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, powdery coating. It blooms in early spring, producing clusters of small, showy flowers.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its delicate appearance.
