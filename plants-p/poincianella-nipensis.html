Poincianella nipensis

Poincianella nipensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian flame tree, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Poincianella nipensis is also valued for its ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
