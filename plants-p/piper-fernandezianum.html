Piper fernandezianum

Piper fernandezianum, commonly known as the Fernandezian pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species for studying plant diversity in tropical ecosystems.
