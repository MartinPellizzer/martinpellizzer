Plantago tandilensis

Plantago tandilensis, commonly known as the Tandil plantain, is a flowering plant native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its adaptability, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
