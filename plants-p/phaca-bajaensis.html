Phaca bajaensis

Phaca bajaensis, commonly known as the Baja phaca, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Baja California.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline, adapting to the harsh coastal environment. Its flowers are usually small and yellow, with a distinctive central disc surrounded by ray florets.

Phaca bajaensis plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
