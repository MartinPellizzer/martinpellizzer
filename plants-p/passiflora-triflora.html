Passiflora triflora

Passiflora triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a crown of filaments and a vibrant blue or purple corona. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed raw or used in jams. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.

Passiflora triflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
