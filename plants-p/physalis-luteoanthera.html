Physalis luteoanthera

Physalis luteoanthera, commonly known as the yellow anther physalis, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and distinctive papery calyces that enclose the fruit. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible when ripe, though they are often consumed in moderation due to their mild toxicity. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
