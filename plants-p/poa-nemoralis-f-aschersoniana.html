Poa nemoralis f. aschersoniana

Poa nemoralis f. aschersoniana, commonly known as the Ascherson's wood sedge, is a variant of the wood sedge species native to Europe.

It is a perennial grass-like plant characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
