Phymatopteris macrochasma

Phymatopteris macrochasma, commonly known as the broad-leaf huperzia, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often slightly undulate in appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it thrives in humid conditions. Its sporophytes are simple in structure, with sporangia clustered on the underside of the leaves.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
