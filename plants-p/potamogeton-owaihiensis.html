Potamogeton owaihiensis

Potamogeton owaihiensis, commonly known as the Owaihiensis watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Potamogetonaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by small, canoe-shaped fruits. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds, often forming dense underwater vegetation.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
