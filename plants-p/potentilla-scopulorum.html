Potentilla scopulorum

Potentilla scopulorum, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it has a compact growth habit.

Potentilla scopulorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or alpine settings.
