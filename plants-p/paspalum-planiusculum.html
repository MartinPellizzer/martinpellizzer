Paspalum planiusculum

Paspalum planiusculum, commonly known as flatleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wetlands. The plant has flat, narrow leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in coastal areas and can tolerate saline conditions.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats in suitable habitats.
