Parsonsia brachiata

Parsonsia brachiata, commonly known as the flame vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Australia and Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its bright orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its striking floral display makes it a popular choice for vertical gardens and coastal landscapes.
