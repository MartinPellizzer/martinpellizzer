Primula turkestanica

Primula turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, often with toothed margins. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
