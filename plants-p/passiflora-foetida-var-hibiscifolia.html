Passiflora foetida var. hibiscifolia

Passiflora foetida var. hibiscifolia, commonly known as the Hibiscifoliate Passionflower, is a striking climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that feature a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and bracts, often in vibrant shades of red, yellow, and white. The plant produces edible berries that are typically green when unripe and turn orange or red when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and the plant thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
