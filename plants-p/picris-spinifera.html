Picris spinifera

Picris spinifera, commonly known as the prickly sow-thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems. The plant features spiny leaves and yellow ray florets, which are typical of the family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

Picris spinifera is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
