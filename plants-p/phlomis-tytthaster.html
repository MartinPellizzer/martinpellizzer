Phlomis tytthaster

Phlomis tytthaster, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, erect stems covered in soft, velvety hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences of yellow to orange flowers, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Phlomis tytthaster is adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a resilient plant in challenging climates.
