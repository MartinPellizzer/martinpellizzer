Protium firmum

Protium firmum, commonly known as the Indian beech, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing three seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Protium firmum is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
