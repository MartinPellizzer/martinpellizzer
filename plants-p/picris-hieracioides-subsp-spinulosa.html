Picris hieracioides subsp. spinulosa

Picris hieracioides subsp.

spinulosa, commonly known as the prickly sow-thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The subspecies spinulosa is distinguished by its more pronounced spines and hairy foliage compared to other subspecies.
