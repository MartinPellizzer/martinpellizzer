Petunia cumingiana

Petunia cumingiana, commonly known as the Cuming's petunia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors, including purple, blue, and white. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
