Paspalum virgatum

Paspalum virgatum, commonly known as the silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions. It is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites.

Paspalum virgatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
