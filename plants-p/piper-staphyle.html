Piper staphyle

Piper staphyle, commonly known as the staphyle pepper or staphyle pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, and these berries contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly glossy texture, while its stems are woody and often covered in fine hairs.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental appeal.
