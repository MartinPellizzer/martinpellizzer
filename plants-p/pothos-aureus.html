Pothos aureus

Pothos aureus, commonly known as golden pothos, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically variegated with shades of gold and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. It is a vigorous climber, often used as a hanging plant or trained to grow on trellises.

Pothos aureus is highly tolerant of low light and is popular as a houseplant due to its ease of care and aesthetic appeal.
