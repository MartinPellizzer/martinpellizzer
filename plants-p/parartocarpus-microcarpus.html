Parartocarpus microcarpus

Parartocarpus microcarpus, commonly known as the small-fruited soursop, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, round fruits with a spiny exterior and a sweet, aromatic pulp. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow and are borne in clusters.
