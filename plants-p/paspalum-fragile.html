Paspalum fragile

Paspalum fragile, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, bluish-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale green flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Paspalum fragile is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone regions.
