Pugetia dimorpha

Pugetia dimorpha, commonly known as the Pacific coast shrub, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along shorelines and in coastal forests. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Pugetia dimorpha plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
