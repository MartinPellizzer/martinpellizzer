Plexipus adenostachyus

Plexipus adenostachyus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires indirect sunlight to flourish. Its ornamental value is due to its vibrant flower colors and compact growth habit.
