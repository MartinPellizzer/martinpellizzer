Pinalia tomentosa

Pinalia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly pinalia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, or tomentose, leaves and stems, which provide a fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
