Paphiopedilum chamberlainianum

Paphiopedilum chamberlainianum, commonly known as the Chamberlain's lady's slipper, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure, featuring a pouch-like lip that is both visually striking and functionally important for pollination. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to conserve water in its natural habitat.

The orchid's flowers are usually pale yellow with deep red or brown markings, adding to its ornamental value.
