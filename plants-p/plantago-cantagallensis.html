Plantago cantagallensis

Plantago cantagallensis, commonly known as the Cantabrian plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
