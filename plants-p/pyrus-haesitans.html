Pyrus haesitans

Pyrus haesitans, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and oval-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by white, round fruits that are edible but not commonly cultivated for consumption.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive form and seasonal interest.
