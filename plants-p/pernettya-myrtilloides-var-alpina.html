Pernettya myrtilloides var. alpina

Pernettya myrtilloides var. alpina, commonly known as alpine blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It produces small, dark blue to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy surface and emit a faintly aromatic scent.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
