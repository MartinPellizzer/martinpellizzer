Pinus parviflora

Pinus parviflora, commonly known as the Japanese white pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The tree produces small, yellowish-conic cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Pinus parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
