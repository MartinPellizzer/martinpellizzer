Pedicularis rotundifolia

Pedicularis rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped or rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
