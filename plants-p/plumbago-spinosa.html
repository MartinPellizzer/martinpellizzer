Plumbago spinosa

Plumbago spinosa, commonly known as blue plumbago, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a woody stem with thorny branches, and its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
