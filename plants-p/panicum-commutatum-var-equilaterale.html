Panicum commutatum var. equilaterale

Panicum commutatum var. equilaterale, commonly known as the three-sided panicgrass, is a grass species characterized by its distinctive three-sided stems.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy panicles of seeds. It is native to North America and is often found in the central and southern United States.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
