Physaria didymocarpa var. contractoreplum

Physaria didymocarpa var. contractoreplum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, inflated seed pods. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments, showcasing features such as reduced leaf size and deep root systems. Its flowers are small and yellow, with four petals, and it produces a unique type of silique, a dry fruit that splits open to release seeds.

This variety is notable for its morphological variation, particularly in leaf shape and pod structure, which contribute to its ecological adaptability.
