Polypodium surucuchense

Polypodium surucuchense, commonly known as the Surucu fern, is a species of polypody found in the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic fern that thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree trunks or rocks. The plant has a distinctive frond structure with a lanceolate shape and serrated margins. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
