Peperomia junghuhniana

Peperomia junghuhniana, commonly known as the Junghuhn's peperomia, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are often glossy and arranged in a rosette pattern. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically borne in clusters and are pollinated by insects.

Peperomia junghuhniana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
