Portulaca fosbergii var. substellata

Portulaca fosbergii var. substellata, commonly known as the star-studded portulaca, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with succulent, fleshy leaves and vibrant, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are typically small, measuring about 1 to 2 centimeters in diameter, and they open in the morning, closing by midday.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
