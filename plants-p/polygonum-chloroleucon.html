Polygonum chloroleucon

Polygonum chloroleucon, commonly known as the white knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its upright stems, heart-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along riverbanks and in wet meadows.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively, it can become invasive in certain environments.
