Piper lagenibaccum

Piper lagenibaccum, commonly known as the large-leaf pepper or the mountain pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Mexico, Guatemala, and Panama. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cooking.

Piper lagenibaccum is valued for its medicinal properties and is often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary uses.
