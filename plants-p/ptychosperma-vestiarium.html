Ptychosperma vestiarium

Ptychosperma vestiarium, commonly known as the sacred palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces edible fruits that are consumed by various wildlife.
