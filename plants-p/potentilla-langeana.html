Potentilla langeana

Potentilla langeana, commonly known as the Lange's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features small, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to nine oval-shaped leaflets.

Potentilla langeana is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.
