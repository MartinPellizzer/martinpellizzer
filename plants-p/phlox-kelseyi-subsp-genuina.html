Phlox kelseyi subsp. genuina

Phlox kelseyi subsp.

genuina, commonly known as the Colorado phlox, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically blooms in early summer and prefers well-drained, rocky soils in full sun. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
