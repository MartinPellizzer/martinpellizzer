Panicum crus-galli var. oryzoides

Panicum crus-galli var. oryzoides, commonly known as proso millet, is an annual grass species native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, two-lobed grains that are harvested for their high nutritional value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for cultivation in various climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable crop for both human consumption and animal feed.
