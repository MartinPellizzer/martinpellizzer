Pentagramma triangularis subsp. semipallida

Pentagramma triangularis subsp.

semipallida, commonly known as the silver leaf pentagramma, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It features delicate, trifoliate leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters, and it produces tiny, berry-like fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
