Polygala berlandieri

Polygala berlandieri, commonly known as the Mexican milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky, arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spiky inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, and it has a deep root system adapted to drought conditions.

It is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of these regions.
