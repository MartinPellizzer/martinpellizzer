Phlox stolonifera f. violacea

Phlox stolonifera f. violacea, commonly known as violet leaf phlox, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polemoniaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly hairy texture. It is a perennial herb that spreads via stolons, making it a suitable choice for ground cover in shaded or partially shaded areas.

Its ornamental value comes from both its vibrant flowers and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
