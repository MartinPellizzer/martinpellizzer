Pyrethrum elongatum

Pyrethrum elongatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish to white flower heads that are surrounded by pale green bracts. Its leaves are elongated and narrow, often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Pyrethrum elongatum is cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
