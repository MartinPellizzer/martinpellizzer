Prunus insititia var. spinosa

Prunus insititia var. spinosa, commonly known as the prickly gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green berries that ripen in the fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pale pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The berries are edible when ripe but can be sour and are sometimes used in jams or wines.
