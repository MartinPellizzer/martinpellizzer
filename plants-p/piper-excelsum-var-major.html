Piper excelsum var. major

Piper excelsum var. major, commonly known as the giant pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to the humid rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, fleshy fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in warm, moist environments with high humidity and partial shade.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in tropical botanical gardens.
