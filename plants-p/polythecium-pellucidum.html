Polythecium pellucidum

Polythecium pellucidum, commonly known as the translucent shield fern, is a unique species of fern characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with rhizomes that spread horizontally beneath the soil. Its leaves are typically narrow and elongated, with a waxy surface that aids in water retention.

The spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are arranged in clusters on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
