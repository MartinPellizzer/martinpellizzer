Protea bruniifolia

Protea bruniifolia, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of flowers, with long, narrow bracts that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are pollinated by insects and birds, and it produces woody, branched seed pods that release seeds when mature.

Protea bruniifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
