Polygonatum oppositifolium

Polygonatum oppositifolium, commonly known as the Chinese Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features rhizomatous growth and lanceolate, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in early summer, followed by red berries that ripen in late summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forests and mountainous regions.

Its rhizomes are used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.
