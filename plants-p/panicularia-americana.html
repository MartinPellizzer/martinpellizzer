Panicularia americana

Panicularia americana, commonly known as the American panicled grass, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces panicles of small, white or pale green flowers. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
