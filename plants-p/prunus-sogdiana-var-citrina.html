Prunus sogdiana var. citrina

Prunus sogdiana var. citrina, commonly known as the Citrin Sogdian Cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow leaves, which turn orange or red in the fall, making it a striking ornamental plant. The species produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by edible, tart cherries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

Due to its unique coloration and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
