Phlox undulata

Phlox undulata, commonly known as the meadow phlox, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along streambanks, preferring well-drained soil. It has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and emit a slight fragrance.

Phlox undulata is a perennial herb that blooms in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
