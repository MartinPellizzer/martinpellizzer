Pycnospatha arietina

Pycnospatha arietina, commonly known as the red-leaf cycad, is a unique cycad species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, trunk-like stem and its striking, fan-shaped leaves that are often a deep red or maroon color. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of cycads. Its leaves are arranged in a tight spiral, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
