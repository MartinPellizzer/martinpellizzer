Pyrus malus proles domestica

Pyrus malus proles domestica, commonly known as the domestic apple.

This species is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its deciduous nature, with simple, alternate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The fruit, which is the most economically important part, is typically a pomaceous apple, containing a core with seeds. It is cultivated worldwide for its edible fruit, which varies in size, color, and flavor depending on the variety.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in horticulture and agriculture.
