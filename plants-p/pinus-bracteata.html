Pinus bracteata

Pinus bracteata, commonly known as the Chinese white pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and its distinctive blue-green needles that are typically bundled in groups of five. The tree produces small, yellowish cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, and its bark is thick, scaly, and grayish in appearance. Pinus bracteata thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It is also valued for its hardiness and ability to adapt to a variety of environmental conditions.
