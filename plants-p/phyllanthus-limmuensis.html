Phyllanthus limmuensis

Phyllanthus limmuensis, commonly known as the Limmu phyllanthus, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by capsule fruits containing minute seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
