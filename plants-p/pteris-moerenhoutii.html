Pteris moerenhoutii

Pteris moerenhoutii, commonly known as the African water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble a lace-like pattern, typically growing on moist, shaded ground or in aquatic environments. The plant thrives in humid conditions and is often found near streams, ponds, or in dense vegetation. Its rhizomes are creeping and can form dense mats, contributing to its ability to colonize various habitats.

Pteris moerenhoutii is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
