Phellodendron japonicum

Phellodendron japonicum, commonly known as Japanese yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark that develops fissures with age. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinctive waxy coating.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its medicinal properties.
