Polygonum tenerum

Polygonum tenerum, commonly known as softleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five stamens and a single pistil, typically in shades of pink or red. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of streams.

Polygonum tenerum is an annual or biennial species that plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects.
