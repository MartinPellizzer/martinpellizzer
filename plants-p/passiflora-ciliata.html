Passiflora ciliata

Passiflora ciliata, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a central crown of filaments surrounded by five sepals and petals. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange berries that are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. Its leaves are velvety and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

Passiflora ciliata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy blooms and is also valued in traditional medicine.
