Premna balakrishnanii

Premna balakrishnanii, commonly known as the Kudumiya plant, is a flowering shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, which bloom during the monsoon season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Premna balakrishnanii is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
