Pleienta

Pleienta, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive silvery-white underside, giving the plant its common name. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters, while the fruit is a small, fleshy drupe.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
