Pterocladon sprucei

Pterocladon sprucei, commonly known as the spruce pine, is a species of conifer native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain the seeds, typical of coniferous species. Its foliage is evergreen, providing year-round greenery in its natural habitat.

The botanical features of Pterocladon sprucei make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
