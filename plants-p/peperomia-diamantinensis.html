Peperomia diamantinensis

Peperomia diamantinensis, commonly known as the diamond peperomia, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to Brazil.

It features dense, thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a bluish or silver sheen, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers in clusters, which are usually hidden among the foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its compact size and attractive foliage, it is often used as a decorative plant in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
