Palmijuncus javensis

Palmijuncus javensis, commonly known as the Java palm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the base of the leaves.

The Java palm is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
