Poincianella subglauca

Poincianella subglauca, commonly known as the blue glaucous poincianella, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle blue hue. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, powdery blue-gray coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Poincianella subglauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
