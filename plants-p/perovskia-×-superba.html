Perovskia × superba

Perovskia × superba, commonly known as Russian blue geranium, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, blue-violet flowers and silver-gray foliage that emit a strong mint-like scent. Native to the Caucasus region, it thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. The plant produces small, cylindrical flower clusters that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a favored addition to both garden and landscape designs.
