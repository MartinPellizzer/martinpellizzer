Polypodium manabyanum

Polypodium manabyanum, commonly known as the Manabyanum huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are typically divided into toothed segments. The plant is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Vietnam. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Polypodium manabyanum is noted for its slow growth and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage.
