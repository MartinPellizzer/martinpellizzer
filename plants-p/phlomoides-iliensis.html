Phlomoides iliensis

Phlomoides iliensis, commonly known as the Ili Valley phlomoides, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly found in the Ili Valley region of Kazakhstan.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and are usually pale blue or purple in color.

Phlomoides iliensis is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
