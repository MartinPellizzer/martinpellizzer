Philodendron aceriferum

Philodendron aceriferum, commonly known as the red-veined philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that often have a vibrant red or purple vein pattern. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is popular in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
