Passiflora marryattae

Passiflora marryattae, commonly known as the Marryatt's passionflower, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white hues. The plant produces edible, fleshy berries that are often consumed raw or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Passiflora marryattae is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
