Primula hirsuta f. exscapa

Primula hirsuta f. exscapa, commonly known as the hairy primrose or the scapose primrose, is a botanical variant of the hairy primrose.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are covered in fine, soft pubescence. The plant typically produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white. It is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
