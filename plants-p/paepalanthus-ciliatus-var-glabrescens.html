Paepalanthus ciliatus var. glabrescens

Paepalanthus ciliatus var. glabrescens, commonly known as the silky pincushion cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of pale green or white hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
