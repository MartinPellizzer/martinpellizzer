Paspalum plicatulum var. arenarium

Paspalum plicatulum var. arenarium, commonly known as sand paspalum, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate drought conditions due to its deep root system.

This species is often used in restoration projects and as a forage plant for livestock.
