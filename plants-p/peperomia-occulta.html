Peperomia occulta

Peperomia occulta, commonly known as the hidden pepper plant, is a compact, low-growing species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a velvety texture and produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for indoor cultivation. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia occulta is prized for its attractive appearance and ease of care, making it a popular choice for houseplants.
