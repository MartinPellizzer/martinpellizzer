Philadelphus delavayi

Philadelphus delavayi, commonly known as the Chinese mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and a woody stem that exudes a milky sap when broken. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional Chinese horticulture.
