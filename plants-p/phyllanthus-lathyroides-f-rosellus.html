Phyllanthus lathyroides f. rosellus

Phyllanthus lathyroides f. rosellus, commonly known as the Rosell's bladder nut, is a variant of the bladder nut plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, rounded leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or semi-arid habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
