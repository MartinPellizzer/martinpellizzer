Pyrus laxiflora

Pyrus laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered pear.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape with a smooth margin. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
