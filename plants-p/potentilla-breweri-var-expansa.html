Potentilla breweri var. expansa

Potentilla breweri var. expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The leaves are typically broad and oval, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh conditions.
