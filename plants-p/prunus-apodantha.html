Prunus apodantha

Prunus apodantha, commonly known as the African cherry or red cherry tree, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in spring, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Prunus apodantha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature and adaptability to different soil types.
