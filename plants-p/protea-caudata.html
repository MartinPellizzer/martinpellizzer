Protea caudata

Protea caudata, commonly known as the tufted protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to cream-colored flowers arranged in a rounded, tuft-like structure. The plant is native to the Eastern and Western Cape provinces of South Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. Protea caudata is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring leathery, evergreen leaves.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also an important species in the conservation of local biodiversity.
