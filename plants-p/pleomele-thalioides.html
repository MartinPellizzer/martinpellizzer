Pleomele thalioides

Pleomele thalioides, commonly known as the golden pleomele, is a striking member of the Dracaena family native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically yellow to orange in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Pleomele thalioides is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
