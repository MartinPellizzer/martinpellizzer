Pilosella niveocastellana

Pilosella niveocastellana, commonly known as the white-leaved hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pilosella niveocastellana is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments.
