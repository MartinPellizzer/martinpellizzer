Pyrus pannonica obtusa

Pyrus pannonica obtusa, commonly known as the Hungarian pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Carpathian Basin.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves with a rough upper surface. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that ripen in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

The plant is also used in horticulture for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
