Pilosella Mollicaulis Subsp. Bauhiniflora

Pilosella mollicaulis subsp. bauhiniflora is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and diverse ecological roles. This subspecies is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and small, daisy-like blossoms that bloom in late summer. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species. Conservation efforts are crucial to protect this unique plant from environmental threats and habitat loss.
