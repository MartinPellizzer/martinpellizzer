Pelargonium × saepeflorens

Pelargonium × saepeflorens, commonly known as the constant-flowering geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geranium family (Geraniaceae).

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse, continuous blooming throughout the growing season. The plant typically features rounded, lobed leaves and clusters of five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden beds and containers.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in temperate regions.
