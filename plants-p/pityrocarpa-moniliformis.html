Pityrocarpa moniliformis

Pityrocarpa moniliformis, commonly known as the African Christmas tree, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical crown and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by fleshy, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

The tree is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and seasonal fruiting.
