Primula × muretiana

Primula × muretiana, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of pink, white, or purple blooms during the spring season. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded garden settings.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and early blooming flowers.
