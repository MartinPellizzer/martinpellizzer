Pterococcus glaberrimus

Pterococcus glaberrimus, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody pods containing flat, oval seeds. It is valued for its hard, heavy wood used in furniture and carving.

The tree also has medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
