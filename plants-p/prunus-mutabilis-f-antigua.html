Prunus mutabilis f. antigua

Prunus mutabilis f. antigua, commonly known as the Antigua cherry, is a rare and unique variant of the Prunus mutabilis species.

This plant is characterized by its strikingly variable flower colors, which can range from white to pink, red, and even purple, depending on environmental conditions. It is native to the island of Antigua in the Caribbean and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested when fully ripe.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after addition to botanical gardens and private collections.
