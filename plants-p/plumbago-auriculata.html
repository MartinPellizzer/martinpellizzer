Plumbago auriculata

Plumbago auriculata, commonly known as blue plumbago, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that produces clusters of sky-blue flowers throughout the growing season. The plant has woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
