Primula spectabilis var. longobarda

Primula spectabilis var. longobarda, commonly known as the Longobard primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, low-growing habit with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
