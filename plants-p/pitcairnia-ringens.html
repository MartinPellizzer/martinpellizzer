Pitcairnia ringens

Pitcairnia ringens, commonly known as the showy pitcairnia, is a striking bromeliad native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescence that can range from red to orange, often resembling a torch. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its rosette-shaped leaves are thick and waxy, designed to collect and store water.

Pitcairnia ringens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
