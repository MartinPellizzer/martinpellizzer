Passerina capitata

Passerina capitata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to white, typically blooming in summer. It has a compact growth habit with upright stems and is often used in rock gardens.

Passerina capitata is relatively drought-tolerant and prefers full sun to partial shade.
