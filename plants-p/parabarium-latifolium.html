Parabarium latifolium

Parabarium latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf parabarium, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the southern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
