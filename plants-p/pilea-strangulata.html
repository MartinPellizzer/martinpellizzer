Pilea strangulata

Pilea strangulata, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its round, fleshy leaves that resemble coins, hence its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are surrounded by a spathe, a modified leaf-like structure. It is a popular houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Pilea strangulata thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
