Phytolacca littoralis

Phytolacca littoralis, commonly known as coastal phytolacca, is a herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of China.

It typically grows in saline or brackish environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh coastal conditions. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a distinct reddish hue on the underside. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters, while the fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains several seeds.

This species plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and provides habitat for various small organisms.
