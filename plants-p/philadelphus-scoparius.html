Philadelphus scoparius

Philadelphus scoparius, commonly known as the common mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The bark is smooth and greyish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
