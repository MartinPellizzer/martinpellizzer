Piper muricatum var. parvifolium

Piper muricatum var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaf Surinam pepper, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and clusters of tiny, peppery fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The fruit, while edible, has a sharp, spicy flavor similar to black pepper.
