Pyrrhocactus vallenarensis

Pyrrhocactus vallenarensis, commonly known as the Vallenarensis Pyrrhocactus, is a rare cactus species native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, spiny areoles, which provide protection against herbivores. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
