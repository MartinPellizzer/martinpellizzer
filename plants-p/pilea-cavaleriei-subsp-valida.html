Pilea cavaleriei subsp. valida

Pilea cavaleriei subsp.

valida, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to China. It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically light green with a slightly waxy texture.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by distinctive, round, coin-like fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a favored ornamental plant.
