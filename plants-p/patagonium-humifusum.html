Patagonium humifusum

Patagonium humifusum, commonly known as the Patagonian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically thrives in temperate and cold climates. The plant features dark green, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. It is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, where it adapts well to poor soil conditions.

Patagonium humifusum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various insects and contributing to soil stability.
