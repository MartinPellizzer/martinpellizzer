Pachystachys coccinea

Pachystachys coccinea, commonly known as the red-flowered pachystachys, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, commonly referred to as the grass family, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit. The plant features vibrant red inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding a bold visual impact to gardens. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a grass-like texture, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Pachystachys coccinea is relatively low maintenance, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.
