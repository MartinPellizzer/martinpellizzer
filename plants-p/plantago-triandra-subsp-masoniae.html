Plantago triandra subsp. masoniae

Plantago triandra subsp.

masoniae, commonly known as Mason's plantain, is a perennial herb native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
