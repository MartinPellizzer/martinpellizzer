Penstemon purpusii

Penstemon purpusii, commonly known as the Purpus's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Penstemon purpusii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
