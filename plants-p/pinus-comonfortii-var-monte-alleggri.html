Pinus comonfortii var. monte-alleggri

Pinus comonfortii var. monte-alleggri, commonly known as the Monte Allegri pine, is a variant of the Comonfort pine native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The foliage is typically a deep green, and the tree produces small, woody cones that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with distinct seasonal changes.

This variety is often valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
