Panicum stoloniferum var. majus

Panicum stoloniferum var. majus, commonly known as the giant panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, feathery plumes that can reach heights of up to 6 feet. The plant has a rhizomatous and stoloniferous growth habit, allowing it to spread aggressively through both underground runners and above-ground stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces showy, fluffy seed heads in late summer.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
