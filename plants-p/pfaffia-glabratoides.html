Pfaffia glabratoides

Pfaffia glabratoides, commonly known as golden root, is a perennial shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal purposes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

Due to its high concentration of bioactive compounds, it is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
