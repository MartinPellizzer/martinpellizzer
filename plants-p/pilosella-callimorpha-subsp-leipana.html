Pilosella callimorpha subsp. leipana

Pilosella callimorpha subsp.

leipana, commonly known as the Leipan alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is a low-growing perennial herb with finely divided, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant is native to the alpine regions of Central Europe and thrives in rocky or dry soils. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in mountain meadows and slopes.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments.
