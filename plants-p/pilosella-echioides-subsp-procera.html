Pilosella echioides subsp. procera

Pilosella echioides subsp.

procera, commonly known as the tall mouse-ear chickweed, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is distinguished by its taller growth habit compared to other members of the Pilosella species.
