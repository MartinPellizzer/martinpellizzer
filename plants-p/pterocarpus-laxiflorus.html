Pterocarpus laxiflorus

Pterocarpus laxiflorus, commonly known as the slender-flowered ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark wood and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated for landscaping and timber.
