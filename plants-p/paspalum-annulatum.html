Paspalum annulatum

Paspalum annulatum, commonly known as the annual paspalum, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and has a fibrous root system.

Paspalum annulatum is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in both moist and dry conditions.
