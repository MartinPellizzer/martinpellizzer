Picris jessoensis

Picris jessoensis, commonly known as the Japanese tansy, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
