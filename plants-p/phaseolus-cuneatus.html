Phaseolus cuneatus

Phaseolus cuneatus, commonly known as the evergreen bean or wild bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a branching stem covered in fine hairs.

Phaseolus cuneatus is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
