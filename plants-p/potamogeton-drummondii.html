Potamogeton drummondii

Potamogeton drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually found in clusters near the water's surface.

Drummond's pondweed plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
