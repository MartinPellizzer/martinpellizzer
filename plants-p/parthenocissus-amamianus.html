Parthenocissus amamianus

Parthenocissus amamianus, commonly known as the Japanese ivy, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are not showy but are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its climbing habit is facilitated by tendrils that allow it to attach to various surfaces.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to cover walls or trellises.
