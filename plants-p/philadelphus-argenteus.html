Philadelphus argenteus

Philadelphus argenteus, commonly known as silverleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
