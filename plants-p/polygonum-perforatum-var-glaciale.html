Polygonum perforatum var. glaciale

Polygonum perforatum var. glaciale, commonly known as the Himalayan knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and lanceolate leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of high-altitude flora and traditional medicine.
