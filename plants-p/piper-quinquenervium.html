Piper quinquenervium

Piper quinquenervium, commonly known as the pepper plant or long pepper, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, green, peppercorn-like fruits. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with five prominent veins, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

The fruits are harvested when mature and dried to produce the spice used in various cuisines.
