Pachystigma macrocalyx

Pachystigma macrocalyx, commonly known as the giant pachystigma, is a striking member of the Araceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention. Its growth habit is typically clump-forming, with tall, erect stems that can reach several feet in height.

Pachystigma macrocalyx is valued in horticulture for its dramatic appearance and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
