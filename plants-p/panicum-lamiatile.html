Panicum lamiatile

Panicum lamiatile, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and panicles of fine, silver-gray bristles that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds to its ornamental value.

Panicum lamiatile is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
