Pellaea pellaeastrum

Pellaea pellaeastrum, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, silvery-white fronds that are covered in minute scales, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in mountainous regions. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.

Pellaea pellaeastrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
