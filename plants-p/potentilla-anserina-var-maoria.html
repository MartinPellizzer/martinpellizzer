Potentilla anserina var. maoria

Potentilla anserina var. maoria, commonly known as Maori cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in alpine or subalpine regions.

This variant is distinguished by its compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
