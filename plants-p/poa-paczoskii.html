Poa paczoskii

Poa paczoskii, commonly known as the Patagonian bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments where it forms dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization. Poa paczoskii is adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh environmental conditions, including strong winds and low nutrient soils.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a pioneer species in mountainous ecosystems.
