Passiflora lowei

Passiflora lowei, commonly known as the Hawaiian passionflower, is a species native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking, colorful flowers with intricate structures. The flowers feature a unique combination of sepals, petals, and modified leaves called bracts. The plant's fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

Passiflora lowei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its ecological role in Hawaiian ecosystems.
