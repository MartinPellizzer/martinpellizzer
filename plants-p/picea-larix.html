Picea larix

Picea larix, commonly known as the larch, is a coniferous evergreen tree belonging to the family Pinaceae.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves that turn golden and fall off annually, making it a deciduous conifer. The tree typically grows in cold climates and is found in boreal and subarctic regions. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The bark is usually thin and reddish-brown, providing a distinctive appearance.
