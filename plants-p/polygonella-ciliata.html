Polygonella ciliata

Polygonella ciliata, commonly known as the fringed mossy grass, is a species of flowering plant in the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves that have fine, fringed edges. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Polygonella ciliata is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually clustered in dense inflorescences, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
