Polystichum moritzianum f. genuina

Polystichum moritzianum f. genuina, commonly known as the Genuina Shield Fern, is a fern species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds that are typically upright and closely spaced. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in sori. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its elegant foliage.
