Phlox atrocaulis

Phlox atrocaulis, commonly known as blackstem phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are typically dark purple to black in color, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Phlox atrocaulis thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
