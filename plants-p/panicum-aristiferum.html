Panicum aristiferum

Panicum aristiferum, commonly known as the spike grass, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikes of small, spikelet-based flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It produces wind-pollinated flowers, and its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread across various regions.

The species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
