Pedicularis fragarioides

Pedicularis fragarioides, commonly known as the strawberry gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often in rocky or grassy areas. Its leaves are opposite and lanceolate, with a hairy texture, and it produces small, edible berries that are not commonly consumed by humans.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
