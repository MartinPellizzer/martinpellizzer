Piper hebecarpum

Piper hebecarpum, commonly known as the rough-seeded pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by elongated, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

Piper hebecarpum is typically found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
