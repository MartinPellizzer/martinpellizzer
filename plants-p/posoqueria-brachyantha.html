Posoqueria brachyantha

Posoqueria brachyantha, commonly known as the silverleaf arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery wax. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used traditionally for their starchy content.

Posoqueria brachyantha thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
