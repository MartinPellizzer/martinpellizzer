Pteris thalictroides

Pteris thalictroides, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically bright yellow to golden in color. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a decorative foliage plant. Its fronds are pinnately divided with a dense arrangement of small, linear pinnae.

Pteris thalictroides thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ease of cultivation.
