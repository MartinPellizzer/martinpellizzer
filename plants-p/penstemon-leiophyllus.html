Penstemon leiophyllus

Penstemon leiophyllus, commonly known as the smoothleaf beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and hairless. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Penstemon leiophyllus is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
