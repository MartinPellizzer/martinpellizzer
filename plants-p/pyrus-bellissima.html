Pyrus bellissima

Pyrus bellissima, commonly known as the beauty pear, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white flowers in early spring and attractive foliage that turns vibrant yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, edible pomes that are typically harvested in late summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance, it is often planted for ornamental purposes in temperate climates.
