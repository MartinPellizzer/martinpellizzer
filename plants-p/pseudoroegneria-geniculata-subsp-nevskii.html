Pseudoroegneria geniculata subsp. nevskii

Pseudoroegneria geniculata subsp.

nevskii, commonly known as the Nevskii's bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing, tufted habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed spikelets.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
