Polysphaeria multiflora subsp. multiflora

Polysphaeria multiflora subsp.

multiflora, commonly known as the multibranched coral tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive coral-like inflorescences, which consist of numerous small, white to pinkish flowers clustered together.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite and elliptical, with a glossy green surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.
