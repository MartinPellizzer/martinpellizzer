Polycnemum brachiatum

Polycnemum brachiatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved water clover, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its floating leaves that are divided into small, rounded lobes, giving it a clover-like appearance. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

Polycnemum brachiatum plays a role in water ecosystems by providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates and stabilizing water surfaces.
