Ptiloria pleurocarpa

Ptiloria pleurocarpa, commonly known as the roughleaf sandveld orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a dense spike, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open grasslands and shrublands.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
