Plantago hirtella var. annua

Plantago hirtella var. annua, commonly known as the annual plantain, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are pollinated by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along streambanks.

This species is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed due to its rapid growth and spread.
