Pavonia hexaphylla

Pavonia hexaphylla, commonly known as the purple false mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant's leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and prominent veins, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and woodland edges.

Pavonia hexaphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
