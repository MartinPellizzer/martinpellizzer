Pelargonium × ramulosum

Pelargonium × ramulosum, commonly known as the ivy-leaf geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, dark green leaves that often have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for hanging baskets, window boxes, and garden borders.
