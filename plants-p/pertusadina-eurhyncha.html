Pertusadina eurhyncha

Pertusadina eurhyncha, commonly known as the red-tipped plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, tubular flowers are typically purple or violet, and they bloom in clusters along the stems.

Pertusadina eurhyncha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant color and fragrance.
