Planchonella pierreana

Planchonella pierreana, commonly known as the Pierre's hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic or ovate in shape. Its flowers are showy, with five petals, often in shades of yellow or white, and it produces small, spherical fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic qualities.
