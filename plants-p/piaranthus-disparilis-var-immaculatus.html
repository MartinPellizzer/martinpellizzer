Piaranthus disparilis var. immaculatus

Piaranthus disparilis var. immaculatus, commonly known as the white-flowered piaranthus, is a rare and striking plant native to South Africa.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and showy, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves to survive drought conditions. Its unique appearance makes it a sought-after specimen in specialized succulent collections.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
