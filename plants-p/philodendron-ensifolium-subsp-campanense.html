Philodendron ensifolium subsp. campanense

Philodendron ensifolium subsp.

campanense, commonly known as the campanulatum philodendron, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slight variegation.

The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers, which are borne in clusters and are not typically showy. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

This species is popular among indoor gardeners due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
