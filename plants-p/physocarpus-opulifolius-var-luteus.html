Physocarpus opulifolius var. luteus

Physocarpus opulifolius var. luteus, commonly known as the golden smokebush, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking golden foliage, which turns vibrant shades of orange and red in the fall. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. Its compound leaves are opposite and have a distinctive, finely toothed margin.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
