Pogonatherum majus

Pogonatherum majus, commonly known as the tall spikegrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Pogonatherum majus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
