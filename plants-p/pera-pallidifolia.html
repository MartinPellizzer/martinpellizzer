Pera pallidifolia

Pera pallidifolia, commonly known as the paleleaf pera, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
