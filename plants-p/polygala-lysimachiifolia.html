Polygala lysimachiifolia

Polygala lysimachiifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive floral structures and often showy blooms. The plant features slender, branching stems with opposite, narrow, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
