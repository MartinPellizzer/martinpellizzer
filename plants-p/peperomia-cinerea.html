Peperomia cinerea

Peperomia cinerea, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, giving it a distinctive, almost metallic appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia cinerea is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance and unique aesthetic.
