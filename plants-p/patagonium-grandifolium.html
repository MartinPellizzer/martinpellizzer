Patagonium grandifolium

Patagonium grandifolium, commonly known as the Patagonian giant fern, is a large, herbaceous plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its expansive, palmate leaves that can reach several meters in diameter, making it one of the largest fern species in the world. The plant thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks. Its fronds are typically bioluminescent at night, a unique adaptation that aids in attracting pollinators.

The plant plays a vital ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
