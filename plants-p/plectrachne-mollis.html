Plectrachne mollis

Plectrachne mollis, commonly known as the slender grass or small-leaved grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Australia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually less than 10 centimeters in length. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spikelet that is often concealed within the foliage.

Plectrachne mollis is characterized by its low-growing habit and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
