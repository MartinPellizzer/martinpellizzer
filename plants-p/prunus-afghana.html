Prunus afghana

Prunus afghana, commonly known as the Afghan cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Prunus afghana is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
