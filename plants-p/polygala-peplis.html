Polygala peplis

Polygala peplis, commonly known as the woolly milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

Polygala peplis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
