Polygala alpestris subsp. angelisii

Polygala alpestris subsp.

angelisii, commonly known as the Alpine milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It is a perennial species that produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered plant in many areas.
