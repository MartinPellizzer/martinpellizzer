Prunus mahaleb var. bernatskyi

Prunus mahaleb var. bernatskyi, commonly known as the Caucasian cherry, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically reddish-brown underneath, and it produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in early spring. The plant bears small, dark red or black berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

This variety is also noted for its hardiness and adaptability to different climates.
