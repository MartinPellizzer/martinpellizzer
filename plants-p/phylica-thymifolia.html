Phylica thymifolia

Phylica thymifolia, commonly known as the sweet thyme bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Phylica thymifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
