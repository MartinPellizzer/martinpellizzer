Pterophora

Pterophora, commonly known as the plume moths, are a family of small moths characterized by their distinctive, feathery wings.

These plants are not actually plants but rather insects, so they do not have botanical features in the traditional sense. However, the term "Pterophora" refers to the wing structure, which is a key taxonomic feature. The family Pterophoridae includes over 1,400 species, many of which are found in temperate regions.

Their unique wing morphology is an adaptation for flight and dispersal, making them an interesting subject in entomology.
