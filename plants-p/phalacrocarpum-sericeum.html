Phalacrocarpum sericeum

Phalacrocarpum sericeum, commonly known as the silky bladderwrack, is a marine algae belonging to the family Fucaceae.

It is characterized by its dark brown, leathery thallus with a smooth, silky surface. This species is typically found in intertidal zones along rocky shores in temperate regions. It plays a crucial role in coastal ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various marine organisms.

Its structure consists of a holdfast, stipe, and blade, with air bladders that aid in buoyancy.
