Pilocereus

Pilocereus is a genus of cacti native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America. These plants are known for their striking night-blooming flowers that open only under moonlight. Despite their delicate appearance, Pilocereus species are highly resilient, able to survive in extreme desert conditions. They are popular among succulent enthusiasts for their unique beauty and low maintenance requirements. However, their toxic sap and thorny stems require caution when handling.
