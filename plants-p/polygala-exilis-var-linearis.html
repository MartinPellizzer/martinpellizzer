Polygala exilis var. linearis

Polygala exilis var. linearis, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces yellowish or greenish flowers that are often arranged in spikes, and it is adapted to arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
