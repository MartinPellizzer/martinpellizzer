Prosthechea venezuelana

Prosthechea venezuelana, commonly known as the Venezuelan pterostigma orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that grows on trees in humid, tropical environments. The plant features a compact, pseudobulbous structure with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Prosthechea venezuelana is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in specialized horticultural settings.
