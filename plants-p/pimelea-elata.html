Pimelea elata

Pimelea elata, commonly known as the slender bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically bright yellow or orange in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are clustered in dense, brush-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Pimelea elata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
