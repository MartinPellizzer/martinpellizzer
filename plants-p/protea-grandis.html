Protea grandis

Protea grandis, commonly known as the giant protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous bright pink to red flowers arranged in a spherical or cylindrical shape. The plant is evergreen, with leathery, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. Native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun.

Protea grandis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
