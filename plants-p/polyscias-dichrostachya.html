Polyscias dichrostachya

Polyscias dichrostachya, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent red veins, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Polyscias dichrostachya is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
