Piper escuadranum

Piper escuadranum, commonly known as the Escuadra pepper, is a climbing vine native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, and these berries contain the aromatic compound responsible for its distinctive flavor. It is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.

The leaves are also used for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
