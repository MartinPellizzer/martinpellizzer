Pyrrocoma ilicifolia

Pyrrocoma ilicifolia, commonly known as the prickly pear cactus, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its spiny, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces vibrant, often yellow or orange, flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and contains a juicy, seed-filled pulp.

Pyrrocoma ilicifolia plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
