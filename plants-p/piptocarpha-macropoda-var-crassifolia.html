Piptocarpha macropoda var. crassifolia

Piptocarpha macropoda var. crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, silver-gray, woolly leaves and purple to violet flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flowers.
