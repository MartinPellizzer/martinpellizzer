Pseuderanthemum glomeratum

Pseuderanthemum glomeratum, commonly known as the clustered false anthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
