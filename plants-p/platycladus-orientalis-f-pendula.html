Platycladus orientalis f. pendula

Platycladus orientalis f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Chinese tallowtree, is a unique cultivar of the Chinese tallowtree.

It is characterized by its cascading, drooping branches that give it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged spirally along the stems. It produces yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by orange to red berries in the fall.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its visual appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
