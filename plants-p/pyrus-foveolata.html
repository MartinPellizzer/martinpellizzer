Pyrus foveolata

Pyrus foveolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pear.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruits are small pomes, typically edible and used in local cuisines.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
