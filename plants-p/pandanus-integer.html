Pandanus integer

Pandanus integer, commonly known as the bottlebrush palm, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that grow in a rosette pattern. The plant produces striking, bottlebrush-shaped inflorescences that are often used in traditional crafts and ceremonies. Its roots are fibrous and can be used for making ropes and mats.

Pandanus integer thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and humid environments.
