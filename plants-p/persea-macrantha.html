Persea macrantha

Persea macrantha, commonly known as the large-fruit spondias, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy white flowers. The tree produces large, edible fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its botanical features include a smooth, often peeling bark and a long, tapering leaf shape.

Persea macrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional medicine.
