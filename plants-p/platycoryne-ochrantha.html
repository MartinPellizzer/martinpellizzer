Platycoryne ochrantha

Platycoryne ochrantha, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green floral structure, with a distinctive star-shaped lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often displaying a vibrant yellow color that attracts pollinators like bees and wasps.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
