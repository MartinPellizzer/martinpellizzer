Paeonia delavayi f. trollioides

Paeonia delavayi f. trollioides, commonly known as the Tibetan peony, is a unique hybrid form of the Delavay peony.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau. The plant features large, showy flowers with rich pink to red hues, often with a contrasting white or yellow center. Its foliage consists of deeply lobed, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is adapted to cold, arid environments.
