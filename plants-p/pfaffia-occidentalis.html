Pfaffia occidentalis

Pfaffia occidentalis, commonly known as curcuma, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth and produces striking orange-red flowers. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a rough, waxy texture.
