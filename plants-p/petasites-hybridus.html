Petasites hybridus

Petasites hybridus, commonly known as the edelweiss, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are adapted to survive in cold, high-altitude environments. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in mountain meadows.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a symbol of resilience and is protected in many areas.
