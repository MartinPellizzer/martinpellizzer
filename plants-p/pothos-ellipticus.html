Pothos ellipticus

Pothos ellipticus, commonly known as the heartleaf pothos, is a popular houseplant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically bright green with a slightly variegated pattern. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are not particularly showy, and its vines can grow up to several feet in length. It thrives in indirect light and requires moderate watering, making it an easy plant to care for.

Pothos ellipticus is well-suited for hanging baskets or shelves, where its trailing vines can cascade gracefully.
