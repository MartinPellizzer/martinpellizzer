Parodia scopa

Parodia scopa, commonly known as the "Cactus of the Moon," is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white, fuzzy spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
