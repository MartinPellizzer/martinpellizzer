Phyllanthus boehmii

Phyllanthus boehmii, commonly known as the African spurge, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Phyllanthus boehmii is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
