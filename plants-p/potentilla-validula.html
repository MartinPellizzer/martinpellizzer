Potentilla validula

Potentilla validula, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla validula is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
