Petalocaryum dulce

Petalocaryum dulce, commonly known as the sweet petal tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy petals that resemble small, delicate leaves, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a sweet, edible pulp. It thrives in humid environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest in both botanical research and horticultural cultivation.
