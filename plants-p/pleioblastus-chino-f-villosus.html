Pleioblastus chino f. villosus

Pleioblastus chino f. villosus, commonly known as the hairy Chinese bamboo, is a variant of the Chinese bamboo species characterized by its soft, hairy foliage.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to eastern Asia, particularly China. This plant is known for its slender, upright growth habit and its ability to form dense clumps over time. The leaves are typically a vibrant green, with a distinctive fuzzy texture that sets it apart from other bamboo varieties.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a decorative plant in indoor settings.
