Polygonum persicaria var. vernicosa

Polygonum persicaria var. vernicosa, commonly known as silky knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, waxy coating, giving them a silky texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist environments such as wet meadows and stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a persistent weed and a valued plant in certain ecological contexts.
