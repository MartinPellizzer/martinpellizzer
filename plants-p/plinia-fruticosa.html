Plinia fruticosa

Plinia fruticosa, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry or araçá-preto, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically black when ripe, with a sweet and tart flavor. These berries are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

Plinia fruticosa is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
