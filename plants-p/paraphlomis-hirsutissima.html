Paraphlomis hirsutissima

Paraphlomis hirsutissima, commonly known as hairy paralytic grass, is a perennial herb native to southern China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces small, blue or purple flowers in clusters.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
