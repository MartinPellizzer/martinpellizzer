Populus tremula var. glandulosa

Populus tremula var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular aspen, is a variant of the aspen tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive glandular hairs on the leaves and stems, which give it its scientific name. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a serrated margin and a slightly hairy surface. This variety often grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in mixed forests.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
