Polemonium stenocalyx

Polemonium stenocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Jacob's ladder, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemonium genus, which is part of the family Polemoniaceae. The plant features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, partially shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
