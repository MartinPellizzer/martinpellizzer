Plinia baracoensis

Plinia baracoensis, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit, is a species of plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow, star-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in forested areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent.

The fruit is edible, though it is less commonly cultivated compared to other starfruit varieties.
