Putoria tenella

Putoria tenella, commonly known as the small-flowered piper, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slight pubescence.

Putoria tenella is typically found in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
