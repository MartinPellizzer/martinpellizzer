Passiflora campanulata

Passiflora campanulata, commonly known as the bell passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that resemble a bell, with five waxy, violet petals and a crown of filaments. The plant produces blue or violet berries that are edible and have a sweet, tart flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs.

Passiflora campanulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
