Podospermum laciniatum var. muricatum

Podospermum laciniatum var. muricatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf podo, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in warm climates as a decorative plant.
