Piper aegrum

Piper aegrum, commonly known as the bitter pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, berry-like fruits. Its leaves and fruits contain various bioactive compounds, including piperine and other alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Piper aegrum is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and potential health benefits.
