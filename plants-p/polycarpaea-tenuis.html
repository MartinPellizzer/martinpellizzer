Polycarpaea tenuis

Polycarpaea tenuis, commonly known as the slender polycarpaea, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
