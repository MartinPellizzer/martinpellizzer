Potentilla rivae

Potentilla rivae, commonly known as the river potentilla, is a flowering plant native to regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils near water sources. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Potentilla rivae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and ability to attract pollinators.
