Polygala boykinii

Polygala boykinii, commonly known as the Texas milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features small, spiny leaves and produces clusters of delicate, yellowish-green flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
