Piper cuyunianum

Piper cuyunianum, commonly known as the Cuyabeno pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper and long pepper. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, dark red berry that contains a single seed, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in the study of tropical flora and its ecological role in the rainforest.
