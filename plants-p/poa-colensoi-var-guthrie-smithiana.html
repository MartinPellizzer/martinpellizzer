Poa colensoi var. guthrie-smithiana

Poa colensoi var. guthrie-smithiana, commonly known as the Colenso's bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
