Portulaca centrali-africana

Portulaca centrali-africana, commonly known as the African moss rose, is a flowering plant native to central Africa.

It is a succulent species that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing thick, fleshy leaves adapted for water storage. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, which bloom throughout the dry season. Its botanical features include a prostrate or upright growth habit, with small, lobed leaves and a short, branching stem.

Portulaca centrali-africana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
