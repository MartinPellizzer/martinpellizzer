Panicum teneriffae

Panicum teneriffae, commonly known as the Canary Island grass, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in coastal and rocky habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and saline environments, making it resilient to harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native range.
