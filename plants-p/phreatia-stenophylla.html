Phreatia stenophylla

Phreatia stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a rare and delicate epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree trunks or branches in the understory of tropical forests. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are adapted to capture moisture from the air, and its flowers are small, clustered, and often pale green or white in color. It requires specific conditions to flourish, including high humidity and indirect light, making it a challenging species for cultivation.

Due to habitat loss, Phreatia stenophylla is considered vulnerable, highlighting the need for conservation efforts to protect this unique botanical species.
