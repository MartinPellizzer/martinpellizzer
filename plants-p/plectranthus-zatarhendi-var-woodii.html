Plectranthus zatarhendi var. woodii

Plectranthus zatarhendi var. woodii, commonly known as the Woolly Mint, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive, almost velvety appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic foliage, it is often cultivated as a decorative and fragrant plant.
