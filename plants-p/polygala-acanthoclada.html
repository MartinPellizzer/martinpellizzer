Polygala acanthoclada

Polygala acanthoclada, commonly known as the hairy false goat's beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, sparsely hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which are usually pink or purple, are adapted for pollination by insects, particularly bees.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
