Piper clypeatum

Piper clypeatum, commonly known as the Indian pepper or wild pepper, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia, including India and Southeast Asia. The plant produces small, green, egg-shaped berries that turn red when mature. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
