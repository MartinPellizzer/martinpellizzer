Pseudabutilon harleyi

Pseudabutilon harleyi, commonly known as the Harley's false abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to xeriscapes and native plant gardens.
