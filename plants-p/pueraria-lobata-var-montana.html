Pueraria lobata var. montana

Pueraria lobata var. montana, commonly known as the mountain cowpea, is a climbing legume native to East Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves and produces showy purple or white flowers. The plant is characterized by its extensive root system, which can grow deep into the soil. It is often found in forested areas and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and traditional medicinal uses.
