Polypodium pyrrholepis

Polypodium pyrrholepis, commonly known as the red-backed polypody, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and distinctive reddish-brown margins on its fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its rhizome is short and thick, supporting the development of its delicate, lance-shaped leaves.

Polypodium pyrrholepis is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid, temperate climates.
