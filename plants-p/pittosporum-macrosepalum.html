Pittosporum macrosepalum

Pittosporum macrosepalum, commonly known as the large-sepal pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant white or cream-colored flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are often visited by native bees and other pollinators.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, brown seeds.
