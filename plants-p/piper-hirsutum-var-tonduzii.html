Piper hirsutum var. tonduzii

Piper hirsutum var. tonduzii, commonly known as the Tonduzii pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper hirsutum species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, short hairs, giving it a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. These fruits are edible and have a mild, peppery flavor, making them suitable for culinary use.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and edible purposes.
