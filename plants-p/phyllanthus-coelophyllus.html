Phyllanthus coelophyllus

Phyllanthus coelophyllus, commonly known as the African false sandalwood, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its fruit is a capsule that contains small, hard seeds.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
