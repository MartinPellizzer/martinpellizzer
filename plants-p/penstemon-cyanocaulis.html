Penstemon cyanocaulis

Penstemon cyanocaulis, commonly known as the blue curl penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Penstemon cyanocaulis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
