Ponera nicaraguensis

Ponera nicaraguensis, commonly known as the Nicaragua spider orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America, particularly Nicaragua. The plant is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, thin petals that resemble legs. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments within the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered an endangered species.
