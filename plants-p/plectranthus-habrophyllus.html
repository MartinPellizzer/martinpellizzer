Plectranthus habrophyllus

Plectranthus habrophyllus, commonly known as the silver bush or silverleaf plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features hairy, silvery-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance, and it produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant is known for its aromatic foliage, which releases a minty scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and container planting.

Its ornamental value and ease of care contribute to its popularity among horticultural enthusiasts.
