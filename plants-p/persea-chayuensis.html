Persea chayuensis

Persea chayuensis, commonly known as Chayuensis avocado, is a species of evergreen tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, green fruits that mature to a dark purple color, containing a single large seed. The fruit is valued for its high oil content and is used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and cultural contexts.
