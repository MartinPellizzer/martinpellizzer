Puccinellia festuciformis var. caerulea

Puccinellia festuciformis var. caerulea, commonly known as bluegrass puccinellia, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along streambanks. Its blue-green foliage gives it a distinctive appearance, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.

It is also noted for its ability to tolerate periodic flooding, which contributes to its adaptability in various wetland environments.
