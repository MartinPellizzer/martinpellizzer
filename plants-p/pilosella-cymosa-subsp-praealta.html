Pilosella cymosa subsp. praealta

Pilosella cymosa subsp.

praealta, commonly known as the tall alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, sunny conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The subspecies praealta is distinguished by its taller stature and more robust growth compared to other subspecies of Pilosella cymosa.
