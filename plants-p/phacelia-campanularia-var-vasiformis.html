Phacelia campanularia var. vasiformis

Phacelia campanularia var. vasiformis, commonly known as the broad-leaf phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a hairy texture, while the stems are often branched and covered in fine pubescence. This variety is known for its attractive appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for various garden settings.
