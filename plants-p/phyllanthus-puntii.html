Phyllanthus puntii

Phyllanthus puntii, commonly known as the African date palm, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, fleshy fruits resembling dates. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its edible fruits.
