Puccinellia przewalskii

Puccinellia przewalskii, commonly known as Chinese glassworts, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a halophyte, capable of thriving in harsh environmental conditions. Its small, wind-pollinated flowers are typically greenish or reddish in color.

Puccinellia przewalskii plays a role in stabilizing soils in degraded landscapes and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
