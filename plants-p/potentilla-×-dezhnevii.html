Potentilla × dezhnevii

Potentilla × dezhnevii, commonly known as the Siberian cinquefoil, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold regions of Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subarctic environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant features small, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh climates.
