Passiflora albida

Passiflora albida, commonly known as the white passionflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and vibrant yellow stamens that create a visually appealing bloom. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Passiflora albida is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
