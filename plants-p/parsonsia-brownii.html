Parsonsia brownii

Parsonsia brownii, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped or elliptical. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed sites.

Parsonsia brownii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
