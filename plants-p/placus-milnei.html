Placus milnei

Placus milnei, commonly known as the Milne's spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a hairy, upright stem that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a distinctive texture.

Placus milnei is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
