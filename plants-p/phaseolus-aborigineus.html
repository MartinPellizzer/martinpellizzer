Phaseolus aborigineus

Phaseolus aborigineus, commonly known as the Andean bean or the wild kidney bean, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, kidney-shaped seeds and climbing habit, often growing along fences or trellises. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is adapted to poor soils.

The seeds are rich in protein and have been traditionally used in indigenous cuisines.
