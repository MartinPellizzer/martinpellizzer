Pittosporum deplanchei

Pittosporum deplanchei, commonly known as the New Zealand Pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, often with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Pittosporum deplanchei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
