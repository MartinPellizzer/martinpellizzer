Potentilla chrysantha var. trimera

Potentilla chrysantha var. trimera, commonly known as the yellow cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in early summer, and its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in meadows, streambanks, and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
