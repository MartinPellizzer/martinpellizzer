Polystichum aculeatum var. contortum

Polystichum aculeatum var. contortum, commonly known as the twisted holly fern, is a variant of the holly fern species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is a fern characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds that are often curled or twisted, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are bipinnate, with small, toothed pinnae that contribute to its ornamental value.

This variant is prized in horticulture for its unique foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
