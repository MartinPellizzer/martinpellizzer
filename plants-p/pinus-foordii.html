Pinus foordii

Pinus foordii, commonly known as the Foord's pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the highlands of southern China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces cones that are initially yellow but turn brown as they mature, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Pinus foordii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
