Physurus densiflorus

Physurus densiflorus, commonly known as the denseflower sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, swampy habitats and is found in parts of Southeast Asia. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats in wetland environments.

The flowers are usually inconspicuous, with the plant's ornamental value lying more in its foliage than its blooms.
