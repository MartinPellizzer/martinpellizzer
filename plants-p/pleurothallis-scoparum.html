Pleurothallis scoparum

Pleurothallis scoparum, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, greenish-white flowers with intricate, spider-like petals and sepals. It has fibrous roots that help it absorb moisture and nutrients from its substrate.

Pleurothallis scoparum is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
