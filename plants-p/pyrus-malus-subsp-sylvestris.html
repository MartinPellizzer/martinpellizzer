Pyrus malus subsp. sylvestris

Pyrus malus subsp.

sylvestris, commonly known as the wild apple, is a subspecies of the apple tree native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its small, sour apples and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces white, fragrant flowers in the spring.

This subspecies is often used in horticulture for its hardiness and genetic diversity.
