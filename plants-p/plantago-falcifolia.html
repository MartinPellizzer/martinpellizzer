Plantago falcifolia

Plantago falcifolia, commonly known as the swordleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or along stream banks.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
