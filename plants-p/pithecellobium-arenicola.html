Pithecellobium arenicola

Pithecellobium arenicola, commonly known as the sand pithecellobium, is a small, shrub-like legume native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is adapted to grow in sandy soils and can tolerate drought conditions due to its deep root system. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often consumed by local wildlife. It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
