Polypodium rajaense

Polypodium rajaense, commonly known as the Rajaense cliff fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows on rocky substrates in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizome is creeping and produces adventitious roots, aiding in its attachment to rocky surfaces.

Polypodium rajaense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
