Pombalia lanata

Pombalia lanata, commonly known as the woolly pombalia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a soft, velvety texture.

Pombalia lanata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
