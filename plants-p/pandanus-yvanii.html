Pandanus yvanii

Pandanus yvanii, commonly known as the New Caledonian pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the island of New Caledonia in the South Pacific and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the leaves. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove swamps or near the shoreline.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
