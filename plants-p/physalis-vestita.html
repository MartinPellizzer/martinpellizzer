Physalis vestita

Physalis vestita, commonly known as the woolly groundcherry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, inflated calyx that persists on the fruit. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are enclosed within a papery husk. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open fields or along roadsides.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique fruit.
