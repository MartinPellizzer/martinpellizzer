Potamogeton plantagineus var. intermedius

Potamogeton plantagineus var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are often smooth and slightly ribbed. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in clusters near the water surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and stable environmental conditions.
