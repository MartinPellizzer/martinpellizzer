Phaseolus lunatus var. longepedunculatus

Phaseolus lunatus var. longepedunculatus, commonly known as the long pedunculate common bean, is a variant of the common bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its elongated peduncles, which are the stalks that support the flower clusters. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with compound leaves and produces pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for its nutritional value, particularly its high protein content.

This variety is valued in agriculture for its adaptability and yield potential in suitable growing conditions.
