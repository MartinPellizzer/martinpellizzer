Ptycholobium biflorum subsp. biflorum

Ptycholobium biflorum subsp.

biflorum, commonly known as the two-flowered ptycholobium, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces small, flat pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forests and savannas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and for its potential ecological benefits.
