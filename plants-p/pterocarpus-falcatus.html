Pterocarpus falcatus

Pterocarpus falcatus, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a tropical tree native to Africa, particularly found in countries like Nigeria and Ghana.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and produces distinctive, woody pods that contain seeds. The tree is valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction. Additionally, its seeds yield a valuable oil used in cosmetics and traditional medicine.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, supporting various wildlife species.
