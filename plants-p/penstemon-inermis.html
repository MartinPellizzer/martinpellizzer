Penstemon inermis

Penstemon inermis, commonly known as the slender beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes.

Penstemon inermis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
