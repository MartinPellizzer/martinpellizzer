Phoebe helicterifolia

Phoebe helicterifolia, commonly known as the silky phoebe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing its seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
