Phyllanthus biantherifer

Phyllanthus biantherifer, commonly known as the African buttertree, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
