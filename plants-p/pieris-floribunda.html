Pieris floribunda

Pieris floribunda, commonly known as the Japanese pieris, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, broad, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

This species is often used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and spring blooms.
