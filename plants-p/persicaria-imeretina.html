Persicaria imeretina

Persicaria imeretina, commonly known as the Imereti knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that appear in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in mountainous areas and along stream banks.

Persicaria imeretina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
