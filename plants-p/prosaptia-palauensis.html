Prosaptia palauensis

Prosaptia palauensis, commonly known as the Palau skyflower, is a unique flowering plant native to the island nation of Palau in the western Pacific Ocean.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom throughout the year, often appearing above the canopy in the dense rainforest. The plant has adapted to thrive in the humid, tropical climate with high rainfall and nutrient-rich soil. Its leaves are broad and waxy, which helps reduce water loss in the moist environment.

Prosaptia palauensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
