Potamogeton samariformis

Potamogeton samariformis, commonly known as the samariform water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has slender, branching stems and small, whorled leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. The plant produces tiny, floating fruits that are dispersed by water currents, aiding in its spread. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
