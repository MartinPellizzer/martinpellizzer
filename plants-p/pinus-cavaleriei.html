Pinus cavaleriei

Pinus cavaleriei, commonly known as the Chinese red pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to China.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves, which are typically arranged in bundles of five, and its distinctive cones that are often reddish in color. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and is known for its slow growth rate and longevity. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and is often found in mountainous regions.

The botanical features of Pinus cavaleriei make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
