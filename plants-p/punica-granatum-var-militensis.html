Punica granatum var. militensis

Punica granatum var. militensis, commonly known as the military pomegranate, is a variety of the pomegranate tree native to the Middle East and South Asia.

It is characterized by its small, red, and often spiny fruits that are typically smaller than those of the common pomegranate. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, exhibiting drought tolerance and a preference for well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and glossy, while its flowers are pink to red in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits in suitable climates.
