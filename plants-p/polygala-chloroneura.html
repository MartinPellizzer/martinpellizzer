Polygala chloroneura

Polygala chloroneura, commonly known as the green-veined milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are often green with prominent veins, and the flowers are usually pale or greenish in color.

Polygala chloroneura is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
