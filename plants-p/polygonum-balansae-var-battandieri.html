Polygonum balansae var. battandieri

Polygonum balansae var. battandieri, commonly known as the Battandieri's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
