Polygala vulgaris proles serpyllacea

Polygala vulgaris proles serpyllacea, commonly known as the common milk-vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygalaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are alternate and linear, with a slightly hairy texture.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
