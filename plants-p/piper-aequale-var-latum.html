Piper aequale var. latum

Piper aequale var. latum, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
