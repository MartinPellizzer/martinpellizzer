Phyllanthus myrtaceus

Phyllanthus myrtaceus, commonly known as the white sand daphne, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
