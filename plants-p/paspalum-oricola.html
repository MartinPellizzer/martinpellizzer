Paspalum oricola

Paspalum oricola, commonly known as the Florida paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes and swampy areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to tolerate standing water and is often used in wetland restoration projects.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
