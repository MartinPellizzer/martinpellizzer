Pulsatilla albana var. sibirica

Pulsatilla albana var. sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, showy, cup-shaped flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
