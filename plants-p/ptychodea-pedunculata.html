Ptychodea pedunculata

Ptychodea pedunculata, commonly known as the slender leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and have a smooth texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

Ptychodea pedunculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
