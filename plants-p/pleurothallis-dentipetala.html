Pleurothallis dentipetala

Pleurothallis dentipetala, commonly known as the toothed lip orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically features dark green, strap-shaped leaves and produces small, colorful blooms in shades of white, yellow, and pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Pleurothallis dentipetala is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
