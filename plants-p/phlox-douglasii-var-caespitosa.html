Phlox douglasii var. caespitosa

Phlox douglasii var. caespitosa, commonly known as Douglas's phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white coating.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid environments.
