Plagiostachys parviflora

Plagiostachys parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, often tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is occasionally grown in gardens and as a decorative plant.
