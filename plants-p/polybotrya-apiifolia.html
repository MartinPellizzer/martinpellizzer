Polybotrya apiifolia

Polybotrya apiifolia, commonly known as the ivy-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, although it can be invasive in certain regions.
