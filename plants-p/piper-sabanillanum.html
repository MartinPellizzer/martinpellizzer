Piper sabanillanum

Piper sabanillanum, commonly known as the Ecuadorian pepper or sabanilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into dark purple berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
