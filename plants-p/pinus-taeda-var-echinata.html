Pinus taeda var. echinata

Pinus taeda var. echinata, commonly known as the longleaf pine, is a variant of the longleaf pine species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, slender needles that are typically 5 to 9 inches in length, and its cones are often covered in a distinctive, spiny bracts. The tree is known for its slow growth and can reach heights of up to 100 feet, with a straight, tall trunk. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against wildfires.

This variant plays a vital role in the ecological systems of its native habitat, supporting a diverse array of wildlife.
