Pourouma amacayacuensis

Pourouma amacayacuensis, commonly known as the Amazonian areca palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its long, pinnate leaves and smooth, grayish trunk. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of many palm species. Its fruit is a small, fibrous drupe that contains a single seed.

This plant plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
