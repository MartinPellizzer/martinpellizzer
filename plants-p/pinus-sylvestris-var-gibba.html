Pinus sylvestris var. gibba

Pinus sylvestris var. gibba, commonly known as the Scots pine, is a variant of the European pine native to northern Europe and western Asia.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant exhibits a distinctive shape with a tall, slender trunk and a crown that is often conical or irregular. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against environmental stressors.

The species is valued for its ecological role and is often used in reforestation efforts.
