Panicum siccaneum

Panicum siccaneum, commonly known as the dry grass or sand grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant produces small, pale brown spikelets that are typically wind-dispersed, aiding in its propagation across sandy or rocky soils. Panicum siccaneum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
