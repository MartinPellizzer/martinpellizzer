Ptychosperma ramosissimum

Ptychosperma ramosissimum, commonly known as the giant cycad, is a large, ancient plant species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its massive, fern-like leaves and a stout, trunk-like stem that can reach several meters in height. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of cycads. Its leaves are often divided into numerous segments, giving it a distinctive, lush appearance.

Ptychosperma ramosissimum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also studied for its ecological and evolutionary significance.
