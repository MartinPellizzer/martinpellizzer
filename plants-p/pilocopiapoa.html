Pilocopiapoa

Pilocopiapoa, commonly known as the "Coppiaoa" or "Pilcopiapoa," is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the brief rainy season, typically in spring. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss and deter herbivores.

Pilocopiapoa is also noted for its slow growth rate and is often cultivated as a decorative succulent.
