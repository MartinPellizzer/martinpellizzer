Poa bidentata

Poa bidentata, commonly known as the two-toothed bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Poa bidentata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of diverse grassland ecosystems.
