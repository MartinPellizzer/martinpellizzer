Prunus spinosa var. ericiflora

Prunus spinosa var. ericiflora, commonly known as the brambles or blackthorn, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark purple berries that ripen in late summer. The leaves are typically oval with serrated edges and a dark green upper surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and historical use in hedging and as a source of fruit for wildlife.
