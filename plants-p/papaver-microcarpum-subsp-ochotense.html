Papaver microcarpum subsp. ochotense

Papaver microcarpum subsp.

ochotense, commonly known as the Ochotense poppy, is a perennial herb native to the Russian Far East and northeastern China. It is characterized by its delicate, nodding flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late summer.

The plant features basal leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with hairy surfaces. Its capsule fruits are small and globose, containing numerous tiny seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
