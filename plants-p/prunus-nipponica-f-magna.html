Prunus nipponica f. magna

Prunus nipponica f. magna, commonly known as the Japanese cherry magnifica, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in spring, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a smooth texture and a glossy green color, providing an attractive backdrop to the spring blossoms.

This cultivar is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is commonly planted in gardens and public parks.
