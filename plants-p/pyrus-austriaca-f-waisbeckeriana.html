Pyrus austriaca f. waisbeckeriana

Pyrus austriaca f. waisbeckeriana, commonly known as the Waisbeckerian apple, is a rare and ornamental cultivar of the European pear.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, finely toothed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red in autumn. The plant produces small, sweet, and aromatic fruits that are often used in jams and culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in horticulture.
