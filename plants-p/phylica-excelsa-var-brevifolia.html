Phylica excelsa var. brevifolia

Phylica excelsa var. brevifolia, commonly known as the dwarf silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, silvery-gray foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and ability to provide habitat for local wildlife.
