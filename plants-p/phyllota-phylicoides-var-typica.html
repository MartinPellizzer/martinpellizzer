Phyllota phylicoides var. typica

Phyllota phylicoides var. typica, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble sunflowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow to orange flower heads. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, low-maintenance ornamental plants.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
