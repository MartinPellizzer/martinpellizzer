Polypodium diaphanum

Polypodium diaphanum, commonly known as the transparent huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often covered in a thin, waxy cuticle. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Polypodium diaphanum is noted for its unique appearance and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
