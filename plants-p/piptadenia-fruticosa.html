Piptadenia fruticosa

Piptadenia fruticosa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pink poinsettia, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by woody pods containing flat, dark seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Piptadenia fruticosa is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
