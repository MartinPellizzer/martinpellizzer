Puccinellia nevadensis

Puccinellia nevadensis, commonly known as Nevada glassworts, is a flowering plant native to the western United States, particularly in arid and semi-arid regions.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, grass-like appearance with dense, compact growth. The plant typically grows in saline or alkaline soils, often found in desert environments or along dry streambeds. Its small, clustered flowers are usually greenish or reddish, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Puccinellia nevadensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments and is sometimes used in restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
