Plantago squamata

Plantago squamata, commonly known as the velvetleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It features a basal rosette of broad, waxy, and velvety leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are borne on slender stalks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is commonly found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
