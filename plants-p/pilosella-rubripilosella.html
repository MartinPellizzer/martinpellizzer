Pilosella rubripilosella

Pilosella rubripilosella, commonly known as the red-hairy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Pilosella rubripilosella is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
