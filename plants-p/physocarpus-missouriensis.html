Physocarpus missouriensis

Physocarpus missouriensis, commonly known as Missouri smudge.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the central United States, characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces red, fuzzy fruits that persist into autumn, providing food for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall.

Physocarpus missouriensis is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
