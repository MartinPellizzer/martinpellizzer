Pyrus communis f. slovenica

Pyrus communis f. slovenica, commonly known as the Slovenian pear, is a botanical variant of the common pear.

It is characterized by its smaller, more compact growth habit compared to the typical pear tree. The leaves are usually narrower and have a more pronounced serration. The fruit is smaller and often has a more intense flavor.

This form is native to the mountainous regions of Slovenia and is adapted to cooler climates.
