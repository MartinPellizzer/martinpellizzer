Piper ivonense

Piper ivonense, commonly known as the Ivonese pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds.

The botanical features of Piper ivonense make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ethnobotanical studies.
