Primula sikkimensis subsp. pudibunda

Primula sikkimensis subsp.

pudibunda, commonly known as the Sikkim primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or on rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
