Pithecellobium lovelliae

Pithecellobium lovelliae, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain a sweet, sticky pulp and seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruits in its native habitat.
