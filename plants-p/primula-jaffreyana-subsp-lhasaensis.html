Primula jaffreyana subsp. lhasaensis

Primula jaffreyana subsp.

lhasaensis, commonly known as the Lhasa primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet and Nepal. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant typically blooms in spring, producing clusters of pale yellow to orange-red petals. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and habitat specificity, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
