Pithecellobium chacoense

Pithecellobium chacoense, commonly known as the Chaco passion vine, is a tropical shrub native to South America, particularly found in Paraguay, Argentina, and Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruit is a flat, leathery pod containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
