Platyopuntia rubrogemmia

Platyopuntia rubrogemmia, commonly known as the red-gemmed prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are often tinged with a reddish hue, especially when mature. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, orange-colored fruits. Its leaves are modified into spines, which provide protection from herbivores.

Platyopuntia rubrogemmia is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick stems and having a shallow root system to absorb moisture efficiently.
