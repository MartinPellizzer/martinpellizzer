Portulaca oleracea var. sylvestris

Portulaca oleracea var. sylvestris, commonly known as wild purslane, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Portulacaceae.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region but has been naturalized in many parts of the world. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to spread readily in various environments.
