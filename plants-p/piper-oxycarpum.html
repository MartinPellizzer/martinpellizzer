Piper oxycarpum

Piper oxycarpum, commonly known as the sharp-fruit pepper or mountain pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, elongated berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent, while the stems are covered in fine hairs.

Piper oxycarpum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
