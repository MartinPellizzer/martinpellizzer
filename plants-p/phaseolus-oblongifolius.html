Phaseolus oblongifolius

Phaseolus oblongifolius, commonly known as the broad-leafed bean, is a species of legume native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and pods that contain small, edible seeds. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both agricultural and ecological studies.
