Pteris livida

Pteris livida, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically gray-green in color. The plant has a creeping rhizome that produces upright, bipinnate leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Pteris livida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant.
