Potentilla flabellifolia f. alba

Potentilla flabellifolia f. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered cinquefoil, is a variant of the species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

This plant is characterized by its delicate, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The leaves are palmately divided, giving the plant a distinctive, fern-like appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
