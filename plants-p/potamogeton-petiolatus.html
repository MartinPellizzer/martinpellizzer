Potamogeton petiolatus

Potamogeton petiolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across North America.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically less than 1 cm wide, arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown, and it relies on water for reproduction and nutrient uptake. Its roots form dense mats that can stabilize sediments and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton petiolatus is often found in shallow lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.
