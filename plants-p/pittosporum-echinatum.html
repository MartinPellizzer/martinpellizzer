Pittosporum echinatum

Pittosporum echinatum, commonly known as the rough-leaved pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in small, raised bumps. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. These flowers are typically followed by small, orange-red berries that are edible for birds.

Pittosporum echinatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
