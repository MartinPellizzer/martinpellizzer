Phippsia capillaris

Phippsia capillaris, commonly known as the slender phippsia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white or pale blue flowers during the spring. Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, which is common among plants in its ecological niche.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and limited geographic distribution.
