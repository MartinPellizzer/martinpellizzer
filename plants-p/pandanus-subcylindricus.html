Pandanus subcylindricus

Pandanus subcylindricus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into fibrous, spathate fruits. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often growing near the shoreline.

Its unique appearance and cultural significance make it an important species in the region's ecology and heritage.
