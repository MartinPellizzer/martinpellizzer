Physurus argyrostictus

Physurus argyrostictus, commonly known as the silver-streaked fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually light green with silver or white streaks along the veins, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.
