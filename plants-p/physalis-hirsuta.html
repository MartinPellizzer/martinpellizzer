Physalis hirsuta

Physalis hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy groundcherry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its hairy, inflated calyces that enclose the fruit. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open fields or along roadsides.

The hairy leaves and stems are a distinguishing feature of this species.
