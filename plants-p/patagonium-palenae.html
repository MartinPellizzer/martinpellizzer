Patagonium palenae

Patagonium palenae, commonly known as the Patagonian dwarf lichen, is a unique lichen species found in the extreme southern regions of South America.

It is a symbiotic organism composed of a fungus and a green alga, which together form a composite organism capable of surviving in harsh, arid environments. The plant exhibits a crust-like growth form, adhering tightly to rock surfaces to conserve moisture. It plays a crucial role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
