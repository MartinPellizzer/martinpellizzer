Potentilla sylvestris var. curiensis

Potentilla sylvestris var. curiensis, commonly known as the Curien's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five elliptical leaflets, and it is often found in high-altitude environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native range.
