Poinsettia heterophylla

Poinsettia heterophylla, commonly known as the Christmas star, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in warm, sunny conditions. Its growth habit varies depending on environmental factors, with some forms exhibiting distinct leaf shapes.

Poinsettia heterophylla is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant, particularly during the holiday season.
