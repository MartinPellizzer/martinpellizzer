Pinanga perakensis

Pinanga perakensis, commonly known as the Borneo bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnate leaves that give it a palm-like appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
