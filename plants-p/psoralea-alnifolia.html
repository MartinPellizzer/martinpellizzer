Psoralea alnifolia

Psoralea alnifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in shrubland or woodland habitats. It has small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Psoralea alnifolia is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.
