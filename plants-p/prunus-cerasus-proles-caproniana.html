Prunus cerasus proles caproniana

Prunus cerasus proles caproniana, commonly known as the Capronian cherry, is a rare and ancient variety of cherry native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, dark red fruits with a tart flavor, which are typically harvested in late summer. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with thorny branches and oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, blooming in early spring before the leaves emerge.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in traditional horticulture.
