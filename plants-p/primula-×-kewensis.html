Primula × kewensis

Primula × kewensis, commonly known as the Kew primrose, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Primula vulgaris and Primula veris.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing clusters of flowers that are highly attractive to pollinators. Native to the UK, it thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural collections.
