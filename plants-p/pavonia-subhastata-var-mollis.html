Pavonia subhastata var. mollis

Pavonia subhastata var. mollis, commonly known as the softleaf pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, upright stems. The plant produces showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a soft, velvety texture that gives it its common name.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.
