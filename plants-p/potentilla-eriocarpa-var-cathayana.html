Potentilla eriocarpa var. cathayana

Potentilla eriocarpa var. cathayana, commonly known as the Chinese silverweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with hairy stems and leaves that are usually covered in fine, soft hairs. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
