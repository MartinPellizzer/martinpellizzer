Prunus microlepis var. ternata

Prunus microlepis var. ternata, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, which are usually pink or white, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
