Psidium firmum var. subcordatum

Psidium firmum var. subcordatum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It features elliptical to ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has a sweet, tangy flavor, making it popular in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value and fruit yield make it a valued plant in both horticulture and agriculture.
