Phaca astragalina f. occidentalis

Phaca astragalina f. occidentalis, commonly known as the western milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions, and it is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

The species is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for wildlife.
