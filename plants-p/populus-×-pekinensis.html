Populus × pekinensis

Populus × pekinensis, commonly known as the Chinese silver birch, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping form and silvery-white bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The leaves are ovate, serrated, and turn yellow in the fall, adding ornamental value to the landscape. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for temperate climates.

This species is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ability to tolerate urban environments.
