Philodendron rhodoaxis var. angustifolium

Philodendron rhodoaxis var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf red-vein philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves with deep green veins that contrast sharply against the lighter green background. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb using aerial roots. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
