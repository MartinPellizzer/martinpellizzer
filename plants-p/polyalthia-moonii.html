Polyalthia moonii

Polyalthia moonii, commonly known as the Moon's Lantern or White Flame Tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a luminous appearance, especially at night. The plant has compound leaves composed of multiple oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is smooth with a pale greyish hue. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
