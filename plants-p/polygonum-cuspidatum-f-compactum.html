Polygonum cuspidatum f. compactum

Polygonum cuspidatum f. compactum, commonly known as Japanese knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often reddish underneath. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is known for its aggressive growth and is considered an invasive species in many regions.

Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread rapidly and is difficult to eradicate.
