Potentilla guilliermondii

Potentilla guilliermondii, commonly known as the Guizhou cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it is known for its hardy nature.

Potentilla guilliermondii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
