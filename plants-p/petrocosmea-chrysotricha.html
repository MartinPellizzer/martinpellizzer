Petrocosmea chrysotricha

Petrocosmea chrysotricha, commonly known as the golden-haired petrocosmea, is a striking member of the Gesneriaceae family native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

This perennial herb is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with long, hairy stamens that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or shaded habitats, showcasing adaptability to challenging environments. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface, enhancing its ornamental appeal.

Petrocosmea chrysotricha is valued in horticulture for its unique floral display and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
