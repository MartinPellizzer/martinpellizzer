Pachyrhachis pineliana

Pachyrhachis pineliana, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, floating fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes. Its rhizomes form dense mats on the water surface, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

The fronds are divided into small, lobed segments that help in photosynthesis and oxygenation of the water.
