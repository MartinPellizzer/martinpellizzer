Paullinia selenoptera var. setuligera

Paullinia selenoptera var. setuligera, commonly known as the hairy selenoptera, is a variant of the selenoptera species within the Sapindaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, featuring serrated edges and a hairy texture on both the upper and lower surfaces. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The flowers are small and greenish-yellow, producing fruits that are edible and have a tangy flavor.
