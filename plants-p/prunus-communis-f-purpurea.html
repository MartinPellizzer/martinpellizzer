Prunus communis f. purpurea

Prunus communis f. purpurea, commonly known as the purple leaf cherry, is a cultivar of the common cherry tree.

It is characterized by its striking purple foliage, which transitions to red and orange in the fall. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are similar to those of the common cherry. It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 meters.

This ornamental tree is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
