Pleione barbarae

Pleione barbarae, commonly known as the Barba's pleione, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its cool, high-altitude habitat. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, and it prefers shaded, moist environments.

Pleione barbarae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
