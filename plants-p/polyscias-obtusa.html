Polyscias obtusa

Polyscias obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf sedge, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage.

Polyscias obtusa is also valued for its ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions and its resilience to pests and diseases.
