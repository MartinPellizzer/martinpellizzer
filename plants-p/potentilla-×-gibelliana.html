Potentilla × gibelliana

Potentilla × gibelliana, commonly known as the silver weigela, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink or red flowers in early summer, which are followed by small, red berries. Its foliage is typically silver-white or gray-green, adding ornamental value throughout the growing season.

Potentilla × gibelliana is often used in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
