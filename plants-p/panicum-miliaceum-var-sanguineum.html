Panicum miliaceum var. sanguineum

Panicum miliaceum var. sanguineum, commonly known as red millet, is a variant of the millet species native to warm climates.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-pink inflorescences that contrast with its grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional agriculture.
