Passiflora hermannii

Passiflora hermannii, commonly known as Hermann's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces edible, orange-colored berries that are rich in vitamin C. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor.

Passiflora hermannii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
