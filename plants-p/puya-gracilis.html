Puya gracilis

Puya gracilis, commonly known as the slender puya, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the bromeliad family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom sequentially along the central stem. Puya gracilis is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
