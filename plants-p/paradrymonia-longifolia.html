Paradrymonia longifolia

Paradrymonia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf paradrymonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming during the wet season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Paradrymonia longifolia is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and along riverbanks.
