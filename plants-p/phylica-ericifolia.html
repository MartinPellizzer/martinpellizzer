Phylica ericifolia

Phylica ericifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Phylica ericifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine.
