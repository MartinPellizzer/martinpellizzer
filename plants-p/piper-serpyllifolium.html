Piper serpyllifolium

Piper serpyllifolium, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, peppery fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its aromatic leaves and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
