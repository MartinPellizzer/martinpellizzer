Poa angustifolia var. angustiglumis

Poa angustifolia var. angustiglumis, commonly known as narrow-leaved bluegrass, is a variant of the narrowleaf bluegrass species.

It is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the northern hemisphere. The plant typically grows in open, moist habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish tint, contributing to its common name.

The inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed florets.
