Phyllodes virgata

Phyllodes virgata, commonly known as the silky wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its long, slender phyllodes, which are modified leaves that serve as the primary photosynthetic structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Phyllodes virgata is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
