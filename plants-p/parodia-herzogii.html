Parodia herzogii

Parodia herzogii, commonly known as the Herzog's moon cactus, is a succulent native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and pale green to white coloration. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
