Psammophiliella kermanensis

Psammophiliella kermanensis, commonly known as the Kerman sand orchid, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is adapted to grow in sandy and rocky soils. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It has a short, creeping rhizome and narrow, grass-like leaves.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
