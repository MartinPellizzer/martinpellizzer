Pleioblastus variegatus

Pleioblastus variegatus, commonly known as the Variegated Bambu or the Japanese Reed, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that often display a striking variegation of green and white or yellow. This plant is a member of the Poaceae family and is closely related to bamboo, though it is not a true bamboo. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

Pleioblastus variegatus is also valued for its ability to form dense clumps and its adaptability to various soil types.
