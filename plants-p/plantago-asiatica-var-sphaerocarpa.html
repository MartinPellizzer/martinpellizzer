Plantago asiatica var. sphaerocarpa

Plantago asiatica var. sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe plantain, is a variant of the common plantain characterized by its distinctive spherical seed heads.

It is a perennial herb native to Asia and thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy underside and produces small, pale green flowers followed by round, inflated seed capsules. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The globe plantain is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
