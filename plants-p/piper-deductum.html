Piper deductum

Piper deductum, commonly known as the West Indian pepper, is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are three-lobed berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
