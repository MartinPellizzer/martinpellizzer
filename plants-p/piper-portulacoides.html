Piper portulacoides

Piper portulacoides, commonly known as the creeping pepper plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants, and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit. The plant features small, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that develop into small, berry-like fruits.

Its botanical structure is adapted to thrive in high-altitude, rocky environments, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
