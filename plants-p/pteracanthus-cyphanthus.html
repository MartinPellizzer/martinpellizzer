Pteracanthus cyphanthus

Pteracanthus cyphanthus, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines that provide protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically clustered at the ends of its stems. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

The botanical features of Pteracanthus cyphanthus make it a unique and resilient addition to desert ecosystems.
