Pinanga glauca

Pinanga glauca, commonly known as the blue-leafed bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, often blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are borne on a spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

Pinanga glauca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
