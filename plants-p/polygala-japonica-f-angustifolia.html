Polygala japonica f. angustifolia

Polygala japonica f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf milkvetch, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, contributing to its distinguishing appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
