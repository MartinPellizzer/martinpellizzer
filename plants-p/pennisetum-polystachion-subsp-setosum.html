Pennisetum polystachion subsp. setosum

Pennisetum polystachion subsp.

setosum, commonly known as the hairy finger grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, feathery inflorescences that give it a striking appearance.

The plant has hairy nodes and leaves, which are typically narrow and grass-like in texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping and as a source of thatch.
