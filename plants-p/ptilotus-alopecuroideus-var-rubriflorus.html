Ptilotus alopecuroideus var. rubriflorus

Ptilotus alopecuroideus var. rubriflorus, commonly known as the red-flowered spinifex, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its foliage consists of small, needle-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments.

This species is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
