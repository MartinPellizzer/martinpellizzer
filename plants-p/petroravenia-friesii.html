Petroravenia friesii

Petroravenia friesii, commonly known as the red-flowered rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, woody structure and vibrant red or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun exposure, making it well-suited for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its leaves are typically small, elliptical, and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Petroravenia friesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
