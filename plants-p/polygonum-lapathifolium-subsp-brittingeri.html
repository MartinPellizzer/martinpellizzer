Polygonum lapathifolium subsp. brittingeri

Polygonum lapathifolium subsp.

brittingeri, commonly known as Brittinger's knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the stems are reddish in color.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
