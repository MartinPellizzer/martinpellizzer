Potentilla pensylvanica var. sanguisorbifolia

Potentilla pensylvanica var. sanguisorbifolia, commonly known as the scarlet potentilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are often tinged with red, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well-suited for moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability in garden settings.
