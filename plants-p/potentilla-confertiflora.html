Potentilla confertiflora

Potentilla confertiflora, commonly known as the denseflower cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, yellow, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, woody stems and alternate, pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies of high-altitude flora.
