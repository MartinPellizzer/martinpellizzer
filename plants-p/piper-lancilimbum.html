Piper lancilimbum

Piper lancilimbum, commonly known as the long-leaved pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, greenish-white flowers are typically clustered in spikes, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and pharmacological studies.
