Prunus domestica subsp. insititia

Prunus domestica subsp.

insititia, commonly known as the wild plum or European blackthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer.

The plant has small, oval leaves with a rough texture and produces white or pale pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and can be fermented into beverages like brandy or wine.
