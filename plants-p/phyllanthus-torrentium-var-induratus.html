Phyllanthus torrentium var. induratus

Phyllanthus torrentium var. induratus, commonly known as the hardy phyllanthus, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces tiny, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its hardy nature makes it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
