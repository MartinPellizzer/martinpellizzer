Physalis spruceana

Physalis spruceana, commonly known as the yellow gooseberry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its distinctive yellow, inflated calyx that encloses the fruit. The plant produces small, acidic berries that are typically harvested when they turn orange or red. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.
