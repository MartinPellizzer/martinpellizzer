Pleurothallis bistuberculata

Pleurothallis bistuberculata, commonly known as the two-ridged pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with delicate, lance-shaped leaves and slender, branching inflorescences. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with intricate markings that help in pollination by specific insect species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often found in conservation areas and is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
