Perama setulosa

Perama setulosa, commonly known as the hairy perama, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Perama setulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
