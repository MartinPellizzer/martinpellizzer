Prunus sachalinensis f. umbellata

Prunus sachalinensis f. umbellata, commonly known as the Sakhalin cherry or Sakhalin plum, is a botanical variant native to the Russian Far East.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy surface and turn red in the fall. The fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
