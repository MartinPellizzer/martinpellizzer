Polygonatum × azegamii

Polygonatum × azegamii, commonly known as the hybrid snake plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Polygonatum sibiricum and Polygonatum multiflorum, characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is native to regions of East Asia, including China and Korea, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are long, sword-shaped, and often have a waxy texture, while its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

This plant is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its medicinal properties.
