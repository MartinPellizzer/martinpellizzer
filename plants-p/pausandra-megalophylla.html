Pausandra megalophylla

Pausandra megalophylla, commonly known as the giant fan-leaf phyllanthus, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 12 inches in diameter, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stalks. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its bold, textured leaves.

Pausandra megalophylla thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland settings.
