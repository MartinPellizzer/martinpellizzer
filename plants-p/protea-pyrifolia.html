Protea pyrifolia

Protea pyrifolia, commonly known as the fireblush protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The plant typically grows in fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers come in a range of colors, from pink to red, and are often used in floral arrangements due to their longevity and aesthetic appeal.

Protea pyrifolia is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and birds.
