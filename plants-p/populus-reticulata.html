Populus reticulata

Populus reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined poplar, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by samaroid seeds that are dispersed by wind. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

Its rapid growth rate makes it an important species for reforestation and ecological restoration projects.
