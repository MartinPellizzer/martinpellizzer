Persea americana var. guatemalensis

Persea americana var. guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces large, pear-shaped fruits with a smooth, dark green skin and a creamy, oily pulp. Its fruit is valued for its rich, buttery texture and high nutritional content, including monounsaturated fats.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
