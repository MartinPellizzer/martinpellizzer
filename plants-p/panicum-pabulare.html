Panicum pabulare

Panicum pabulare, commonly known as yellow panicgrass, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and golden-yellow inflorescences that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Panicum pabulare is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
