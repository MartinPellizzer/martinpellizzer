Polygonum acanthophyllum

Polygonum acanthophyllum, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its succulent leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Polygonum acanthophyllum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
