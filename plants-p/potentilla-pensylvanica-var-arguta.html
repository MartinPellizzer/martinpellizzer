Potentilla pensylvanica var. arguta

Potentilla pensylvanica var. arguta, commonly known as the Pennsylvania cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the northeastern United States.

It features small, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has upright, branching stems covered with fine, soft hairs. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and typically have three to five lobes.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky habitats.
