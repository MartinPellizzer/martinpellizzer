Pyrus dissimilis

Pyrus dissimilis, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring white flowers and edible fruits. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin.

The fruit is a pome, usually white or yellow, and is used both for culinary and ornamental purposes.
