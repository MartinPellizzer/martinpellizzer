Phoradendron dimidiatum

Phoradendron dimidiatum, commonly known as the mistletoe of the southeastern United States, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees such as oaks and hickories.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis using the limited chlorophyll it possesses. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that are dispersed by birds, which eat the fruit and excrete the seeds onto other host trees. Its life cycle involves a complex relationship with its host, contributing to the ecological dynamics of forest ecosystems.

The plant's ability to survive in a parasitic manner while maintaining some degree of photosynthetic activity makes it a unique example of plant adaptation.
