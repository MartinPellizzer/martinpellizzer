Philadelphus stramineus

Philadelphus stramineus, commonly known as the yellow mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are typically hairy underneath. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Philadelphus stramineus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
