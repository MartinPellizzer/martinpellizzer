Periclymenum sempervirens

Periclymenum sempervirens, commonly known as evergreen honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in early spring. Its berries are small, red, and edible, often attracting birds.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
