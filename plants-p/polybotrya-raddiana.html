Polybotrya raddiana

Polybotrya raddiana, commonly known as the red-stemmed rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with reddish-brown stems and dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The species belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are typically pale yellow to orange in color.

Polybotrya raddiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
