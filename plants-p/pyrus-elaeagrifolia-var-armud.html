Pyrus elaeagrifolia var. armud

Pyrus elaeagrifolia var. armud, commonly known as the Armenian pear, is a variant of the pear tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its small, ornamental leaves that are elliptical with a pointed tip and a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically small and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

The tree is relatively hardy and can tolerate a range of soil conditions, making it a versatile choice for gardens and landscapes.
