Piper singkojan

Piper singkojan, commonly known as the Singkojan pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its small, clustered flowers are typically greenish-white in color, and it produces small, peppery fruits that are used in local cuisines.

The botanical features of Piper singkojan make it an important plant in both ecological and culinary contexts.
