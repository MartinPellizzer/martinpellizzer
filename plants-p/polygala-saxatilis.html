Polygala saxatilis

Polygala saxatilis, commonly known as the rock polygala, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and arid environments in southern Africa.

It typically grows in crevices and on rocky outcrops, where it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale blue or violet, arranged in spike-like clusters, and are characteristic of the Polygala genus.

It is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
