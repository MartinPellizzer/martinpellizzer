Prunus japonica f. fauriei

Prunus japonica f. fauriei, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in jams and preserves. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping and bonsai.
