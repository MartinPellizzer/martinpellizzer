Protea speciosa

Protea speciosa, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous papery bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers come in shades of white, pink, and red, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Protea speciosa plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators such as birds and insects.
