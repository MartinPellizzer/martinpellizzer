Pandanus tectorius var. tubuaiensis

Pandanus tectorius var. tubuaiensis, commonly known as the tubuai pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to salt spray and sandy soils. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and cultural significance in Polynesian communities.
