Persicaria wallichii var. tomentosa

Persicaria wallichii var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly smartweed, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its hairy, or tomentose, stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or red. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
