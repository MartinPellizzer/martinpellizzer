Phoebe kerrii

Phoebe kerrii, commonly known as the Kerri's phoebe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small, deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and woodland edges. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, waxy texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in loose inflorescences, and are usually white or pale green, making it a subtle yet attractive addition to gardens.

Phoebe kerrii is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
