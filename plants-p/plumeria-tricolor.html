Plumeria tricolor

Plumeria tricolor, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, three-color flowers that range from white to pink to yellow. The plant produces large, waxy leaves and has a showy, tree-like appearance. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are used in various cultural and ceremonial contexts in its native regions.
