Podochrosia

Podochrosia, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and showy, yellowish inflorescences that resemble small umbrellas. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. Podochrosia species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

They are also valued for their nitrogen-fixing capabilities, which benefit the surrounding ecosystem.
