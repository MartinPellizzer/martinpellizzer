Pachyphytum chloranthum

Pachyphytum chloranthum, commonly known as the Blue Ghost Aloe, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapted to arid environments. The leaves are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that reduces water loss. It produces small, yellow flowers during the spring or early summer.

This plant is popular in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal.
