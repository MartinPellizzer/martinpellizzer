Plantago coronopus subsp. coronopus

Plantago coronopus subsp.

coronopus, commonly known as sea plantain, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, greenish flowers in clusters.

The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found along shorelines and in coastal dunes. It is a perennial species with a robust root system that helps stabilize coastal environments.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
