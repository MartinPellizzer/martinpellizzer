Persea areolatocostae

Persea areolatocostae, commonly known as the bumpy-costate persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed, and bumpy bark, which gives it its scientific name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, orange-red fruits. It is native to Central America and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental value.
