Phyllanthus poumensis var. poumensis

Phyllanthus poumensis var. poumensis, commonly known as the African spurge, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface and a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional purposes.
