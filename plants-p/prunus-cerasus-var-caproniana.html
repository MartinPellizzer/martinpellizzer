Prunus cerasus var. caproniana

Prunus cerasus var. caproniana, commonly known as the Capronian cherry, is a variety of the sweet cherry tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, dark red fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has small, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and its flowers are white with a pinkish tinge. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits.
