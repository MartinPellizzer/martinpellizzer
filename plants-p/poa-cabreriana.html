Poa cabreriana

Poa cabreriana, commonly known as the Cabrera's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and bluish-green foliage. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually purple to brown in color.

Poa cabreriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
