Pellaea calomelanos var. leucomelas

Pellaea calomelanos var. leucomelas, commonly known as the white-stemmed maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds with a distinctive white or pale coloration on the stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of small, lance-shaped leaves.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and indirect light.
