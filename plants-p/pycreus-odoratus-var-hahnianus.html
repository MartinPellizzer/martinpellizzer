Pycreus odoratus var. hahnianus

Pycreus odoratus var. hahnianus, commonly known as the Hahnianum orchid, is a rare and highly sought-after orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and lavender, often with intricate patterns on the petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
