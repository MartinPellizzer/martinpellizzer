Pilosella auriculoides subsp. huljakii

Pilosella auriculoides subsp.

huljakii, commonly known as the Huljak's cat's ear, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly enlarged leaf bases that resemble ears, giving it its common name. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

This plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
