Pteroceras chrysanthum

Pteroceras chrysanthum, commonly known as the golden star fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that emit a faintly sweet, vanilla-like fragrance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

However, it is important to note that it is not commonly cultivated due to its specific care requirements.
