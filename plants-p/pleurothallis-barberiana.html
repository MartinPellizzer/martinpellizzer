Pleurothallis barberiana

Pleurothallis barberiana, commonly known as the Ecuadorian orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees without deriving nutrients from them. It has narrow, strap-like leaves and produces intricate, fragrant blooms that attract various pollinators.

Due to its habitat specificity and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
