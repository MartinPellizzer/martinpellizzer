Prunus tsuechinii

Prunus tsuechinii, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Japanese yuzu, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, yellow-orange fruits. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Prunus tsuechinii is also valued for its use in traditional Japanese culture, including in tea and culinary applications.
