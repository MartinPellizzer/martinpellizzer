Petasites laevigatus

Petasites laevigatus, commonly known as the smoothleaf edelweiss, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy rhizomes and large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in meadows and rocky slopes at high altitudes.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
