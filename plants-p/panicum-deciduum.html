Panicum deciduum

Panicum deciduum, commonly known as the deciduous panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, gray-green leaves that turn bronze in the fall. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often wind-dispersed, contributing to its ecological role in seed propagation. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various habitats.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its seasonal color changes and delicate appearance.
