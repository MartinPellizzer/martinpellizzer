Phrynium grandifolium

Phrynium grandifolium, commonly known as the giant arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers that are often surrounded by prominent bracts. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Phrynium grandifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
