Pinus contorta var. yukonensis

Pinus contorta var. yukonensis, commonly known as the Yukon white pine, is a variant of the lodgepole pine native to the Yukon region of Canada.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and dense, soft needles. The tree produces cone-like structures that contain seeds dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across open landscapes. It thrives in cold climates and is adapted to grow in well-drained, rocky soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
