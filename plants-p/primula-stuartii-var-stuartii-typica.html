Primula stuartii var. stuartii-typica

Primula stuartii var. stuartii-typica, commonly known as the Stuart's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The leaves are typically oval and slightly hairy, adapted to the cold, moist environments in which the plant thrives. It prefers well-drained, acidic soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and resilience in cool climates.
