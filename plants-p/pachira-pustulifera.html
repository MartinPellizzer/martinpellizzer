Pachira pustulifera

Pachira pustulifera, commonly known as the bumpy pachira, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and large, paddle-shaped leaves that are often covered in small, raised bumps. The plant produces yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often grown as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
