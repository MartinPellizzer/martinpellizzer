Passiflora lancifolia

Passiflora lancifolia, commonly known as the lancifoliate passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature intricate structures including a crown of filaments and a central reproductive cluster. The leaves are typically lance-shaped, hence the species name, and are often deeply lobed. This plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.
