Phyllanthus palakondensis

Phyllanthus palakondensis, commonly known as the Kurnool phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences and glossy, ovate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in regions such as Andhra Pradesh and Karnataka. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
