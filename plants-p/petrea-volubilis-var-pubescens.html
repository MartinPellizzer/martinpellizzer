Petrea volubilis var. pubescens

Petrea volubilis var. pubescens, commonly known as the velvet creeper, is a vigorous climbing shrub native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to cover trellises or walls.

Its adaptability to various soil types and moderate sunlight makes it a popular choice for landscaping.
