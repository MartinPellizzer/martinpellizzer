Piper phytolaccifolium var. caraboboense

Piper phytolaccifolium var. caraboboense, commonly known as the Carabobo pepper, is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to Venezuela.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with lanceolate lobes and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, humid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
