Pulicaria lozanoi

Pulicaria lozanoi, commonly known as the Lozano's sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, thriving in arid and coastal environments. Its small, yellowish flowers appear in clusters, and it has a low, spreading growth habit.

Pulicaria lozanoi is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
