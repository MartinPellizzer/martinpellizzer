Picea nigra pendula

Picea nigra pendula, commonly known as the weeping black spruce, is a variant of the black spruce native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a unique appearance. The plant has needle-like leaves that are dark green and slightly glossy, typical of coniferous species. It produces small, cone-shaped seed structures that contain its reproductive seeds.

This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in boreal forests and mountainous regions.
