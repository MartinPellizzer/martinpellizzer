Piper pallidirameum

Piper pallidirameum, commonly known as the pale-stemmed pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, pale-green stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, three-lobed berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical regions.
