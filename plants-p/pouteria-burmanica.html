Pouteria burmanica

Pouteria burmanica, commonly known as Burmese sapodilla, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by oval, yellow fruits with a sweet, creamy pulp. The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in its native region.

Its wood is also valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture making.
