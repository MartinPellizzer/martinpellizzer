Primula bonatii

Primula bonatii, commonly known as the Bonati's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its foliage consists of oval, dark green leaves with a hairy texture. Primula bonatii prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value and adaptability have made it a popular choice among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
