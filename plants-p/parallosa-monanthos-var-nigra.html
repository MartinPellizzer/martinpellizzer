Parallosa monanthos var. nigra

Parallosa monanthos var. nigra, commonly known as black-flowered parallosa, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which contrast sharply with its green foliage. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine conditions. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
