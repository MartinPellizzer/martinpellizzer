Plantago fauriei

Plantago fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie's plantain, is a species of flowering plant in the plantain family.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are pollinated by wind. It is often found in moist, disturbed soils in meadows and along roadsides.

This plant is noted for its adaptability and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
