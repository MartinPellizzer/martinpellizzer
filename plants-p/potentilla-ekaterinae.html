Potentilla ekaterinae

Potentilla ekaterinae, commonly known as the Katerina's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and are covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla ekaterinae is adapted to harsh alpine environments and is often found in rocky soils.
