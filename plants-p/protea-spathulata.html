Protea spathulata

Protea spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers arranged in a spherical shape. The plant features broad, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its native arid environment. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun exposure.

Protea spathulata is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of the fynbos biome in South Africa.
