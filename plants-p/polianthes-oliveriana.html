Polianthes oliveriana

Polianthes oliveriana, commonly known as the Oliver's onion flower, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces bulbs that are used for propagation. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its beauty and unique scent.
