Pandanus longipes

Pandanus longipes, commonly known as the long-fruited pandanus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Indo-Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces large, branched inflorescences bearing small, fragrant flowers. Its fruits are fibrous and edible, often used in local cuisines.

Pandanus longipes plays an important ecological role in coastal and wetland habitats.
