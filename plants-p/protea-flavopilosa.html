Protea flavopilosa

Protea flavopilosa, commonly known as the yellow-haired protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to yellowish flowers surrounded by long, silky bracts. It is often found in fynbos vegetation and is adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.
