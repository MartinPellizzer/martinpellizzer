Paederia tomentosa var. purpureocaerulea

Paederia tomentosa var. purpureocaerulea, commonly known as the purple-blue paederia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from purple to blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
