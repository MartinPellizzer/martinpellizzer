Poa alpina f. longifolia

Poa alpina f. longifolia, commonly known as the alpine bentgrass, is a variant of the alpine bentgrass species.

It is a low-growing perennial grass native to mountainous regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant features slender, tufted shoots with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

This variant is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine tundras and rocky slopes.
