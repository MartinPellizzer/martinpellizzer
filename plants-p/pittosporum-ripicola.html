Pittosporum ripicola

Pittosporum ripicola, commonly known as the coastal pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to salt-laden winds and sandy soils. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy surface and a distinctively aromatic scent.

Pittosporum ripicola is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness in coastal environments.
