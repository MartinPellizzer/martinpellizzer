Polypodium subfurfuraceum

Polypodium subfurfuraceum, commonly known as the silky polypody, is a fern native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are covered in a silky, hairy texture. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
