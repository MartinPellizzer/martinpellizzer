Paeonia versicolor

Paeonia versicolor, commonly known as the variable peony, is a species of flowering plant in the family Paeoniaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and China. The plant is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, red, and even purple, depending on the variety. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several leaflets that are lance-shaped and serrated.

Paeonia versicolor is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
