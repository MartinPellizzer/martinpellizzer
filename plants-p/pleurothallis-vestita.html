Pleurothallis vestita

Pleurothallis vestita, commonly known as the white-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and often display a combination of white and green hues.

Pleurothallis vestita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
