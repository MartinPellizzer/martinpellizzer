Piper pseudochurumayu var. membranaceum

Piper pseudochurumayu var. membranaceum, commonly known as the membranous pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper pseudochurumayu species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, wrinkled berries. Its botanical features include a distinct aroma and the presence of essential oils in its leaves and fruits.

This variant is often studied for its potential medicinal properties and adaptability to various growing conditions.
