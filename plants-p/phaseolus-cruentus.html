Phaseolus cruentus

Phaseolus cruentus, commonly known as the crimson bean, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by pods containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types.

The plant's ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia makes it an important crop in sustainable agriculture.
