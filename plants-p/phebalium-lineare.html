Phebalium lineare

Phebalium lineare, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phebalium, is a small shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale yellow, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in native plant landscaping.
