Pisum sativum var. capucinorum

Pisum sativum var. capucinorum, commonly known as the purple snow pea, is a variant of the common pea that features distinctive violet or purple pod colors.

This variety is botanically notable for its edible pods, which are harvested before the seeds fully mature. The plant is an annual legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and produces small, pea-shaped seeds within its pods. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the plant has a trailing habit, making it suitable for trellises or support structures.
