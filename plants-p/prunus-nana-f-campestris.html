Prunus nana f. campestris

Prunus nana f. campestris, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or rock cherry, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, dark red to black berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant has a dense, spreading habit with short, stout stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in cold climates.
