Panicum diagonale var. hirsutius

Panicum diagonale var. hirsutius, commonly known as hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy texture and tall, slender stalks that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces fluffy, panicle-like inflorescences that are often used in ornamental landscaping due to their aesthetic appeal. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

This variety is drought-tolerant and often found in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
