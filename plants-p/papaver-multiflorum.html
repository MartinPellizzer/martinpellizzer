Papaver multiflorum

Papaver multiflorum, commonly known as the Persian poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a slightly hairy texture.

Papaver multiflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
