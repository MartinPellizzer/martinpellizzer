Panicum miliaceum var. cinereum

Panicum miliaceum var. cinereum, commonly known as gray millet, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, gray-green foliage and produces small, fluffy panicles of seeds. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments. Its seeds are often used as a grain in traditional cuisines and can be processed into flour.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
