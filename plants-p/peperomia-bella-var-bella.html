Peperomia bella var. bella

Peperomia bella var. bella, commonly known as the Variegated Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, waxy leaves with striking variegation of green, white, and pink, making it a visually appealing houseplant. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red, followed by spore-bearing structures called sporangia. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, requiring moderate watering to prevent root rot.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
