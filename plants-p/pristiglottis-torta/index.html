Pristiglottis Torta

Tortured liverwort, scientifically known as Pristiglottis torta, is a rare and fascinating member of the liverwort family. This plant thrives in damp, shaded environments, often found in moist soils or on decaying wood. Its name derives from the intricate, twisting patterns of its leaves, which resemble the contorted forms of its name. Despite its delicate appearance, it plays a vital role in its ecosystem by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for microorganisms. Studying Pristiglottis torta offers insight into the resilience and adaptability of bryophytes in diverse habitats.
