Potamogeton diversifolius var. trichophyllus

Potamogeton diversifolius var. trichophyllus, commonly known as the hairy pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered with fine, hairy structures. The leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variety is known for its ability to thrive in shallow, slow-moving waters, where it plays a role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Its reproductive structures include small, green flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical fruits.
