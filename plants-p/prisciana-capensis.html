Prisciana capensis

Prisciana capensis, commonly known as the Cape primrose, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that come in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant typically grows in a shrub form, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Prisciana capensis is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
