Polycarena minima

Polycarena minima, commonly known as the small polycarp, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are tiny and clustered in inflorescences, often producing small, white or pale green fruits.

Polycarena minima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
