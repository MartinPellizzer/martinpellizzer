Pseudosasa amplexicaulis

Pseudosasa amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often clasping around the stems, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Pseudosasa amplexicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
