Phyteuma spicatum f. ebracteatum

Phyteuma spicatum f. ebracteatum, commonly known as the naked bract phyteuma, is a cultivar of the phyteuma plant characterized by its lack of leafy bracts.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is native to southern Europe. The plant produces striking, spire-like inflorescences of tubular blue or violet flowers. It is typically grown as an ornamental plant in gardens for its attractive appearance and ease of cultivation.

This form is prized for its refined look and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
