Polygala crenata

Polygala crenata, commonly known as the crested milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white, and the leaves are alternate and often lobed.

Polygala crenata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
