Penstemon pallidus

Penstemon pallidus, commonly known as the pale penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pale pink to white. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
