Phelipanche simplex

Phelipanche simplex, commonly known as the common morning glory, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, opening in the early morning. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds, it can become a weed in agricultural settings.
