Poa campyle

Poa campyle, commonly known as the bentgrass, is a species of grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Poa campyle is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.
