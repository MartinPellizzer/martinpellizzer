Pimelea nervosa

Pimelea nervosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from white to pink, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open forests and heathlands.

Pimelea nervosa is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract native pollinators.
