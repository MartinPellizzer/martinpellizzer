Polygonum dissitiflorum var. virgatum

Polygonum dissitiflorum var. virgatum, commonly known as the upright knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, slender stems that are often branched and covered in small, reddish-brown hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found along stream banks and in woodland areas.
