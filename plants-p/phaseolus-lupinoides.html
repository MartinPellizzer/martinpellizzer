Phaseolus lupinoides

Phaseolus lupinoides, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It produces vibrant red flowers that give way to elongated pods containing edible seeds. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its culinary uses. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its biological classification places it within the Fabaceae family, which is known for nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
