Piper mexicanum

Piper mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican pepper plant or wild pepper, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into three-chambered berries, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves and fruits contain essential oils and have been used for their purported medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
