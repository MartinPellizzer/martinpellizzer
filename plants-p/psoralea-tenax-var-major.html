Psoralea tenax var. major

Psoralea tenax var. major, commonly known as the rigid psoralea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, compound leaves. The flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in racemes, and are adapted for insect pollination. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.
