Protea transvaaliensis

Protea transvaaliensis, commonly known as the Transvaal protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is native to the grasslands of South Africa, particularly in the Eastern Cape and Western Cape provinces. The plant is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of multiple bracts that resemble petals. Its flowers are typically pink to red in color, with a distinctive, elongated shape.

Protea transvaaliensis thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate.
