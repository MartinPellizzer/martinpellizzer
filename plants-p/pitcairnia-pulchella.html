Pitcairnia pulchella

Pitcairnia pulchella, commonly known as the velvet leaf pitcairnia, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that forms a rosette shape, often with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and botanical collections.
