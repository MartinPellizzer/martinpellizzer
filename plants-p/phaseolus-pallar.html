Phaseolus pallar

Phaseolus pallar, commonly known as the Andean runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Andes region of South America.

It produces elongated, green pods that contain edible seeds, similar to other Phaseolus species. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures, and its flowers are typically white or pale purple. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The botanical features of Phaseolus pallar make it a valuable crop for both local consumption and agricultural research.
