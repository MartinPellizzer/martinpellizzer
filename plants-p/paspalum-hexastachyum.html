Paspalum hexastachyum

Paspalum hexastachyum, commonly known as Florida paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in restoration projects to stabilize soil in moist habitats.
