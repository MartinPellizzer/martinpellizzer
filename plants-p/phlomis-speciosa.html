Phlomis speciosa

Phlomis speciosa, commonly known as showy phlomis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of showy, lavender-pink flowers in late summer, which are borne on branched inflorescences. Its root system is fibrous and robust, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, demonstrating its adaptability to arid conditions.
