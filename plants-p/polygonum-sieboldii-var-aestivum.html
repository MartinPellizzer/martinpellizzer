Polygonum sieboldii var. aestivum

Polygonum sieboldii var. aestivum, commonly known as Japanese knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features upright, hollow stems that are often reddish in color and covered with small, reddish-brown bracts. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems. Its leaves are heart-shaped, with a smooth margin and a slightly hairy surface.

It is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in non-native regions.
