Penstemon rattanii subsp. typicus

Penstemon rattanii subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the typical rattan penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
