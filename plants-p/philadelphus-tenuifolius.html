Philadelphus tenuifolius

Philadelphus tenuifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, arching branches with alternate, elliptic leaves that are narrow and have a serrated margin. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to landscapes in temperate climates.
