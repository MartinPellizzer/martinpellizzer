Papaver dahlianum

Papaver dahlianum, commonly known as the Dahlian poppy, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow, with a distinctive black center. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural spread in its native habitat.
