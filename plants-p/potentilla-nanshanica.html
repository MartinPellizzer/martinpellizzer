Potentilla nanshanica

Potentilla nanshanica, commonly known as the Nan Shan cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Potentilla nanshanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
