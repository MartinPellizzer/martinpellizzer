Pholidia phillipsii

Pholidia phillipsii, commonly known as the Christmas berry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and small, bright red berries that ripen during the winter months. The plant is adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate salt spray, making it a common sight in coastal dunes. Its berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
