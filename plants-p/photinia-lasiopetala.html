Photinia lasiopetala

Photinia lasiopetala, commonly known as the glossy leaf photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage that turns vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are attractive to birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often used in landscaping for its striking seasonal color changes.
