Pilosella septentrionalis var. assimilata

Pilosella septentrionalis var. assimilata, commonly known as the northern pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

It is often used in dried floral arrangements due to its long-lasting flowers and delicate beauty.
