Pinellia ternata f. angustata

Pinellia ternata f. angustata, commonly known as narrow-leaved pinellia, is a variant of the Chinese wild ginger plant.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and compact, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests and wetlands. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, sharing features with other ginger relatives.
