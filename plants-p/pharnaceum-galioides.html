Pharnaceum galioides

Pharnaceum galioides, commonly known as the marsh gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in North America.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces striking blue or violet flowers during the summer months. The plant is adapted to moist, nutrient-poor soils and often grows in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and streams. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to the ecological health of its habitat.

Pharnaceum galioides is an important indicator species for wetland ecosystems due to its specific environmental requirements.
