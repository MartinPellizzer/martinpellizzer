Paulownia imperialis var. pallida

Paulownia imperialis var. pallida, commonly known as the pale imperial paulownia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its rapid growth rate and distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are typically pale green in color. The tree produces showy, clustered flowers in the spring, which are usually pink or white. Its wood is highly valued for its lightweight and strong properties, making it popular in woodworking.

The pale imperial paulownia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
