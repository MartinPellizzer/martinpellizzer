Patagonium dumosum

Patagonium dumosum, commonly known as the Patagonian dwarf shrub, is a low-growing plant native to the arid regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woody stems and small, leathery leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to survive in poor, sandy soils.

Patagonium dumosum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
