Paronychia hispanica var. frutescens

Paronychia hispanica var. frutescens, commonly known as the Spanish thimblecush, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, white or pink flowers in spring. The plant has hairy, fleshy leaves that are often clustered at the base. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in alpine or coastal environments.
