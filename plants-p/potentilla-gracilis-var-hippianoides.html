Potentilla gracilis var. hippianoides

Potentilla gracilis var. hippianoides, commonly known as the slender cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically features slender, wiry stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
