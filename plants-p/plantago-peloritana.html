Plantago peloritana

Plantago peloritana, commonly known as the Sicilian plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a dense spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a resilient species in its native habitat.
