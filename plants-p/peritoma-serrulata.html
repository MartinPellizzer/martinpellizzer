Peritoma serrulata

Peritoma serrulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf peritoma, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features narrow, serrated leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are often pink or red in color. Peritoma serrulata is known for its adaptability to drought conditions and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
