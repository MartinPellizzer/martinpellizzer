Potamogeton pusillus var. longipedunculatus

Potamogeton pusillus var. longipedunculatus, commonly known as the slender pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It has long, slender stems that are often branched and bear small, oval leaves. The plant produces tiny, spike-like inflorescences that are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

This variety is often found in shallow, nutrient-rich waters and plays a role in supporting aquatic ecosystems.
