Polystichum craspedosorum f. dissectum

Polystichum craspedosorum f. dissectum, commonly known as the dissected wood fern, is a fern species characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a delicate, lacy appearance due to the deep lobing.
