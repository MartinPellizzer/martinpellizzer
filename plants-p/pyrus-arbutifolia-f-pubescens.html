Pyrus arbutifolia f. pubescens

Pyrus arbutifolia f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy alderleaf pear, is a cultivar of the alderleaf pear, characterized by its hairy, pubescent leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and have a serrated margin, with a distinctive silvery pubescence on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart fruits that ripen in late summer.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a range of soil types.
