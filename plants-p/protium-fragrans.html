Protium fragrans

Protium fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant protium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its leaves and bark contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
