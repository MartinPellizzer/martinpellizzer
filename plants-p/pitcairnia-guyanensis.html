Pitcairnia guyanensis

Pitcairnia guyanensis, commonly known as the Guyana pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences that can last for several months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and botanical study.
