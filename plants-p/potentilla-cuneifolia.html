Potentilla cuneifolia

Potentilla cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of these environments.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
