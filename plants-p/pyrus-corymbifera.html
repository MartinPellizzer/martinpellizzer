Pyrus corymbifera

Pyrus corymbifera, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and dense, rounded crown. The tree produces small, white flowers in corymbs, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.

Pyrus corymbifera is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
