Phoradendron nitens

Phoradendron nitens, commonly known as the silver mist mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees, primarily in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and white, waxy flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are a food source for birds, which in turn aid in seed dispersal. Its parasitic nature allows it to draw water and nutrients from its host, while still performing photosynthesis.

The silver mist mistletoe is an important component of forest ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity through its interactions with host trees and wildlife.
