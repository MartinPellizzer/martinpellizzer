Phylica curvifolia

Phylica curvifolia, commonly known as the spiral bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of inflorescences. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Phylica curvifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils.
