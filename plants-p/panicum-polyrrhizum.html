Panicum polyrrhizum

Panicum polyrrhizum, commonly known as wild millet, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that contain tiny grains, which are often used as a food source in some cultures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Panicum polyrrhizum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
