Phymatodes polycarpos

Phymatodes polycarpos, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its deep taproot system, which allows it to survive in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by distinctive bracts that resemble a crown. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water conservation.

Phymatodes polycarpos is also known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
