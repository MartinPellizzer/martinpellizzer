Peperomia megalanthera

Peperomia megalanthera, commonly known as the giant peperomia, is a compact shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish or purple tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, often emerging from the leaf axils. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia megalanthera is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
