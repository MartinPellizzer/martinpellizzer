Paspalidium grandispiculatum

Paspalidium grandispiculatum, commonly known as the large-spined paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its robust, tufted growth habit and long, rigid leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its adaptability makes it a common species in regions with temperate to subtropical climates.
