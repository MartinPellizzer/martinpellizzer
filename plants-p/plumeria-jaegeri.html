Plumeria jaegeri

Plumeria jaegeri, commonly known as the white frangipani, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces distinctive, fleshy, red or orange fruits. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The bark and flowers have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
