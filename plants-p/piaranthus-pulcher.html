Piaranthus pulcher

Piaranthus pulcher, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

This perennial herb features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive golden hue. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette at the base of the plant. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
