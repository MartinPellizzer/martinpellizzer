Paranomus capitatus

Paranomus capitatus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from purple to pink, blooming in late summer. The plant has a compact, upright growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Paranomus capitatus is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
