Paraboea amplexicaulis

Paraboea amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping paraboea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and opposite, ovate leaves that often wrap around the stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale green, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it requires careful management to prevent it from becoming invasive.
