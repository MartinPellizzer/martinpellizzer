Phyllanthus scandens

Phyllanthus scandens, commonly known as the climbing buttonweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically opposite or whorled in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, greenish fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Phyllanthus scandens is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
