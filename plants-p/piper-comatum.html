Piper comatum

Piper comatum, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a spicy flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, where it can become invasive.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
