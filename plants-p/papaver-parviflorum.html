Papaver parviflorum

Papaver parviflorum, commonly known as the dwarf poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has a compact growth habit, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It blooms in the summer months and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Papaver parviflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
