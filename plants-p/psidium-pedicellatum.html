Psidium pedicellatum

Psidium pedicellatum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitahaya, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically bright red or yellow when mature, containing a sweet, juicy pulp. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The plant has a shallow root system, making it adaptable to various soil types but requiring well-drained conditions.
