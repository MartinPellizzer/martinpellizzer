Panicum xerophilum

Panicum xerophilum, commonly known as the desert dropseed, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation in dry environments. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Panicum xerophilum is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
