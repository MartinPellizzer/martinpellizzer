Pelargonium australe var. glabratum

Pelargonium australe var. glabratum, commonly known as the smoothleaf geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems covered in fine, smooth hairs, giving it its varietal name "glabratum.". The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a slightly serrated margin, and are often covered in a waxy cuticle.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
