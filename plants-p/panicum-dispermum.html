Panicum dispermum

Panicum dispermum, commonly known as the two-seeded grass, is a species of grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces two seeds per spikelet, which is a distinguishing feature of its reproductive biology. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed areas.

Its fibrous roots make it a useful plant for soil stabilization and erosion control.
