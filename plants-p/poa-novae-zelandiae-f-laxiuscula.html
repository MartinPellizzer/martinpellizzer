Poa novae-zelandiae f. laxiuscula

Poa novae-zelandiae f. laxiuscula, commonly known as the slender New Zealand grass, is a rare and distinctive botanical variant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, fine leaves and compact growth habit. This grass is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky outcrops. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
