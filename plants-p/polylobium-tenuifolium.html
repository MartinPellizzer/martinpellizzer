Polylobium tenuifolium

Polylobium tenuifolium, commonly known as the slender-leaf polylobium, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are often arranged in a spiral pattern, and the flowers are pollinated primarily by insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological roles in certain habitats.
