Paspalum plicatulum var. subrotundum

Paspalum plicatulum var. subrotundum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false brome, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and narrow, linear leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
