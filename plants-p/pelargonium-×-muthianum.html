Pelargonium × muthianum

Pelargonium × muthianum, commonly known as the scented geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its aromatic leaves that emit a variety of scents depending on the cultivar. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, featuring rounded or lobed leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and indoor cultivation.

Its unique fragrance and ornamental value have made it a favored plant in horticulture.
