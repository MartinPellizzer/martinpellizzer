Pleurothallis cardiochila

Pleurothallis cardiochila, commonly known as the heart-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, strap-like leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its flowers are often clustered in a raceme and have a unique, heart-shaped appearance.

Pleurothallis cardiochila is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right growing conditions.
