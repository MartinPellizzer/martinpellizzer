Phylica propinqua

Phylica propinqua, commonly known as the coastal bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange bottlebrush-like inflorescences that appear during the winter months. The plant thrives in coastal areas and sandy soils, often growing in open, sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Phylica propinqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
