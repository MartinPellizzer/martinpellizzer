Psidium chrysophyllum

Psidium chrysophyllum, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces a fleshy, aromatic fruit with a rough, spiny exterior and a sweet, creamy pulp. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a distinctive floral structure.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and has various traditional medicinal uses.
