Polygala aquilonia

Polygala aquilonia, commonly known as the cloud flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its unique floral structure resembles a cluster of tiny clouds, giving it its common name.

Polygala aquilonia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
