Puya quillotana

Puya quillotana, commonly known as the Andean grass of the Andes, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like rosette structure. The plant produces vibrant, showy inflorescences that can reach several meters in height, often displaying red, orange, or yellow hues. Puya quillotana is adapted to harsh, cold climates and can survive in poor soil conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it an important species in Andean biodiversity.
