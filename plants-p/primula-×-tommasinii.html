Primula × tommasinii

Primula × tommasinii, commonly known as the Tommasini primrose, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in early spring, showcasing its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are densely packed around the stem.

Primula × tommasinii prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a versatile choice for woodland gardens.
