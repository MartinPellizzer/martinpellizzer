Parillax pumila

Parillax pumila, commonly known as the dwarf parillax, is a small flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features compact, rounded foliage with a dense, mat-like growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
