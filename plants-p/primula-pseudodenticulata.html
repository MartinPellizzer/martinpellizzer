Primula pseudodenticulata

Primula pseudodenticulata, commonly known as the Japanese primrose or the mountain primrose, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in a dense, velvety pubescence.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.
