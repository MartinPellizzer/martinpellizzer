Primula leucophylla subsp. ruprechtii

Primula leucophylla subsp.

ruprechtii, commonly known as the Alpine primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a distinctive purple or brownish throat.

The plant has hairy, basal leaves that are lanceolate in shape and often covered in a dense layer of fine pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
