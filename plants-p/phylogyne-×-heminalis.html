Phylogyne × heminalis

Phylogyne × heminalis, commonly known as the hybrid hibiscus, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Hibiscus syriacus and Hibiscus cannabinus.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of 2 to 6 meters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its foliage is often dark green and glossy, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.
