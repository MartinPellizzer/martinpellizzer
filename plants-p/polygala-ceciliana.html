Polygala ceciliana

Polygala ceciliana, commonly known as the Cecil's milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators like butterflies and bees.
