Phrynium giganteum

Phrynium giganteum, commonly known as the giant arrowroot or swamp arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding visual interest to its appearance. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines for their starchy texture.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Araceae and is often found in wetland or marshy environments.
