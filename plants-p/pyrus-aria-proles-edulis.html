Pyrus aria proles edulis

Pyrus aria proles edulis, commonly known as the edible apple pear, is a species belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, yellow to red fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

The fruit is typically harvested in late summer and can be used in various culinary applications.
