Papaver alpinum subsp. suaveolens

Papaver alpinum subsp.

suaveolens, commonly known as the alpine poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It produces striking, showy flowers with bright red petals and a prominent, saucer-shaped corona.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is characterized by its subtle fragrance and compact growth habit, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
