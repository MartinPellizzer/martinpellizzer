Pelargonium lanceolatum

Pelargonium lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and blooms. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-draining soil.

Pelargonium lanceolatum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
