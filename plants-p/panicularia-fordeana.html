Panicularia fordeana

Panicularia fordeana, commonly known as the Forde's panicled grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in paniculate inflorescences, which are branched and often drooping. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly found in its natural habitat.
