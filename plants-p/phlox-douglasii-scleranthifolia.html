Phlox douglasii scleranthifolia

Phlox douglasii scleranthifolia, commonly known as the Sierra phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are typically covered in a layer of fine, white to grayish pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to rocky and dry environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
