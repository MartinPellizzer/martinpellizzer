Poa czernjajevii

Poa czernjajevii, commonly known as the Czernjajev's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that gives it its common name.

Poa czernjajevii is often found in alpine meadows and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and genetic significance.
