Piper uncinulatum

Piper uncinulatum, commonly known as the hooked pepper or wild pepper, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
