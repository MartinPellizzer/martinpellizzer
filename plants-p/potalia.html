Potalia

Potalia, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Caesalpiniaceae and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and showy, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Potalia thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
