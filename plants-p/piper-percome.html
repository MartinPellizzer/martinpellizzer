Piper percome

Piper percome, commonly known as the white pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often pubescent. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are borne in clusters, and the fruit is a three-lobed berry that turns red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
