Papaver miniatum

Papaver miniatum, commonly known as the dwarf poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has a short stem and produces capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly found in the wild.
