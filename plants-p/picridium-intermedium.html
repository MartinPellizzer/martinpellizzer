Picridium intermedium

Picridium intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate picrid, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Picridaceae, which is now classified under the broader family of Apocynaceae. The plant is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and palmate leaves, which are often lobed and have a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
