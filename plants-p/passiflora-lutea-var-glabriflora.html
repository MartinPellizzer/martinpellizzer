Passiflora lutea var. glabriflora

Passiflora lutea var. glabriflora, commonly known as the smooth-flowered passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which feature a unique corona of filaments surrounding a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces orange to red berries that are edible and often consumed by wildlife. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and serrated edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
