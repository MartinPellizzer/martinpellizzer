Pulsatilla vulgaris var. nigella

Pulsatilla vulgaris var. nigella, commonly known as the black peony, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, dark purple to black stems and its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces follicles that contain seeds covered in a white, silky coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial shade to full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
