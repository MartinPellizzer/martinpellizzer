Plectranthus melanocarpus

Plectranthus melanocarpus, commonly known as the black fruit plectranthus, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly aromatic scent. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive black, egg-shaped fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique foliage and ease of cultivation.
