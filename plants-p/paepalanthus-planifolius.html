Paepalanthus planifolius

Paepalanthus planifolius, commonly known as the flat-leaved puya, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and showy, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
