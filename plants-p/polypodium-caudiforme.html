Polypodium caudiforme

Polypodium caudiforme, commonly known as the horse's tail fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a perennial evergreen fern characterized by its long, trailing rhizomes and dense clusters of small, bright green, needle-like fronds. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's unique appearance resembles a horse's tail, giving it its common name.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense ground covers.
