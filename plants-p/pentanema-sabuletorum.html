Pentanema sabuletorum

Pentanema sabuletorum, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in sandy or coastal soils, often found in dune environments. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming in the early morning. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and sprawling.

The species is adapted to salt spray and can tolerate periodic flooding, making it a characteristic plant of coastal ecosystems.
