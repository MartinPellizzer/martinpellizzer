Paeonia rhodia

Paeonia rhodia, commonly known as the Korean peony, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink and red. The plant features palmate leaves with a serrated edge, and its stems are covered in a fine layer of white down. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in traditional medicine.
