Polygala illepida

Polygala illepida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in grassland habitats. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces pods that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
