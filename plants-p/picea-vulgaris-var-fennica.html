Picea vulgaris var. fennica

Picea vulgaris var. fennica, commonly known as the Finnish spruce, is a variant of the white spruce native to northern Europe and parts of Russia.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green needles that are slightly twisted. The cones are typically short and cylindrical, with reddish-brown scales. This variety is adapted to cold climates and is often found in boreal forests.

Its wood is valued for its strength and durability, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.
