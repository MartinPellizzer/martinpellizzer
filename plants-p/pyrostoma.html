Pyrostoma

Pyrostoma, commonly known as the firetail orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to various regions in South America, particularly in the Andes. They are characterized by their distinctive, often brightly colored flowers that resemble tails, hence the common name. Pyrostoma species typically thrive in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Their botanical significance lies in their unique floral structures and their role in local ecosystems.
