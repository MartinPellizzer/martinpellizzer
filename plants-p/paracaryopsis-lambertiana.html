Paracaryopsis lambertiana

Paracaryopsis lambertiana, commonly known as Lambert's grass, is a flowering plant native to the grasslands of southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that are typically green or brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and a fibrous texture.

Lambert's grass is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
