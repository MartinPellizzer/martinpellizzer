Pernettya purpurascens

Pernettya purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered cowberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its berries, which are edible, are typically red or orange and contain a single seed.

Pernettya purpurascens thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic soils and is adapted to cold climates.
