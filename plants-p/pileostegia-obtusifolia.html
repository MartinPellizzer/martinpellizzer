Pileostegia obtusifolia

Pileostegia obtusifolia, commonly known as the Mexican flameball, is a shrub native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and striking red or orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a woody stem covered with thick, leathery leaves that provide a textured appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The flowers are borne in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
