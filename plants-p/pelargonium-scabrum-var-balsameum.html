Pelargonium scabrum var. balsameum

Pelargonium scabrum var. balsameum, commonly known as the balsam geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant is evergreen and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.

Pelargonium scabrum var. balsameum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
