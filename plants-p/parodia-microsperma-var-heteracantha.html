Parodia microsperma var. heteracantha

Parodia microsperma var. heteracantha, commonly known as the "Cactus of the Desert," is a compact, globular cactus native to South America.

It features a dense covering of white, radial spines that give it a distinctive, almost ethereal appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Its slow growth rate and unique morphology make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
