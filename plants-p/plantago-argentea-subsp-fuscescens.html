Plantago argentea subsp. fuscescens

Plantago argentea subsp.

fuscescens, commonly known as silver plantain or woolly plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its silver-gray, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine, soft pubescence.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
