Piper sinuclausum

Piper sinuclausum, commonly known as the zigzag pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive zigzag venation. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by elongated, curved fruits that contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The plant's unique morphology and chemical composition make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
