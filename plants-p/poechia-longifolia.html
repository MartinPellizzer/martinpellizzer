Poechia longifolia

Poechia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf poechia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in the spring. Poechia longifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
