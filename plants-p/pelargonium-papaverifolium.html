Pelargonium papaverifolium

Pelargonium papaverifolium, commonly known as the poppy-leaf geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of the poppy, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in gardens across temperate regions.
