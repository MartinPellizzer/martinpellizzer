Potentilla × yamanakae

Potentilla × yamanakae, commonly known as the Japanese cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to Japan.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and is characterized by its dense, rounded form. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by red or orange berries. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and blooms.

Potentilla × yamanakae prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for garden landscapes.
