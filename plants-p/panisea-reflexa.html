Panisea reflexa

Panisea reflexa, commonly known as the reflexed panisea, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This species thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Panisea reflexa is considered a botanically significant but endangered plant.
