Primula auricula

Primula auricula, commonly known as the auricled primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or wooded areas and prefers cool, moist conditions. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully develop.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
