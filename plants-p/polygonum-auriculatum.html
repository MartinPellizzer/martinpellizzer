Polygonum auriculatum

Polygonum auriculatum, commonly known as the earleaf groundcherry, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes over 3,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its upright stems, broad leaves, and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It produces edible, pear-shaped fruits that are often referred to as "groundcherries" or "physalis.".

Polygonum auriculatum is commonly found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
