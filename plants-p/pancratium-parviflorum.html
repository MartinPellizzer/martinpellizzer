Pancratium parviflorum

Pancratium parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered yellow pomegranate, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has strap-shaped leaves and produces red, berry-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in sandy or coastal soils and is often found in dune environments.

Pancratium parviflorum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
