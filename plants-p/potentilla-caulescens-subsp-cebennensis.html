Potentilla caulescens subsp. cebennensis

Potentilla caulescens subsp.

cebennensis, commonly known as the Cebenna cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, rounded leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to alpine environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies of high-altitude flora.
