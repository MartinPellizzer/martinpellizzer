Piper dolabriforme

Piper dolabriforme, commonly known as the broad-leafed pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, warm climates. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
