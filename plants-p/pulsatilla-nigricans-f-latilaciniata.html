Pulsatilla nigricans f. latilaciniata

Pulsatilla nigricans f. latilaciniata, commonly known as the broad-leaf pasqueflower, is a botanical variant of the pasqueflower species.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, pale yellow to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
