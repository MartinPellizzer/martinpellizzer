Pilocarpus goudotianus subsp. heterochromus

Pilocarpus goudotianus subsp.

heterochromus, commonly known as the yellow wild coffee, is a shrub native to the dry regions of South America. It is characterized by its yellowish-green leaves and small, greenish-white flowers.

The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in scrublands and rocky soils.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its use in treating eye conditions.
