Potentilla vvedenskyi

Potentilla vvedenskyi, commonly known as the Siberian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Siberia and Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Potentilla vvedenskyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
