Piper trinerve

Piper trinerve, commonly known as the three-veined pepper, is a tropical vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with three distinct veins, giving it its scientific name. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
