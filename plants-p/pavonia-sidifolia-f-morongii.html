Pavonia sidifolia f. morongii

Pavonia sidifolia f. morongii, commonly known as the Morong's sand verbena, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, blooming from spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to Mediterranean climates.
