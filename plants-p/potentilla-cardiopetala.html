Potentilla cardiopetala

Potentilla cardiopetala, commonly known as the heart-leaved cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers during the summer months. The leaves are palmately divided, with five to seven lobes, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and vibrant blooms.
