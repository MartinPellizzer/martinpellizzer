Photinia benthamiana var. obovata

Photinia benthamiana var. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf photinia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, obovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its hardy nature and attractive foliage make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
