Petunia violacea subsp. depauperata

Petunia violacea subsp.

depauperata, commonly known as the poorflower petunia, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pale blue.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often trailing.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
