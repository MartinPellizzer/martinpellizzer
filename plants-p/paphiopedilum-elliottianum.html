Paphiopedilum elliottianum

Paphiopedilum elliottianum, commonly known as the Elliott's lady's slipper, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a unique pouch-shaped lip that is a defining characteristic of the Paphiopedilum genus. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments with rich, well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses due to its specific care requirements.
