Passiflora cubensis f. vaginata

Passiflora cubensis f. vaginata, commonly known as the velvet passionflower, is a unique variant of the passionflower genus native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is distinguished by its velvety, pubescent leaves and striking, intricate flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white hues. The plant produces edible, fleshy berries that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its botanical characteristics include a climbing habit, compound leaves, and a distinctive floral structure with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments.

This species is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
