Phylica callosa

Phylica callosa, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, adding vibrant color to gardens. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Phylica callosa is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
