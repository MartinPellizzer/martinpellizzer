Potentilla tephrodes

Potentilla tephrodes, commonly known as the sandstem cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils. It is often found in disturbed areas, such as along roadsides or in dry open habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and erosion control.
