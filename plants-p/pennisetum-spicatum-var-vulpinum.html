Pennisetum spicatum var. vulpinum

Pennisetum spicatum var. vulpinum, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and its distinctive purple-tinted inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant produces dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to purple flowers that are arranged in a panicle structure. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens and landscapes.

Its fine, grass-like leaves are often used in floral arrangements due to their soft texture and long-lasting appearance.
