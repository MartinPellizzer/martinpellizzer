Panicum antipodum

Panicum antipodum, commonly known as New Zealand sugar cane, is a grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along waterways.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain environments.
