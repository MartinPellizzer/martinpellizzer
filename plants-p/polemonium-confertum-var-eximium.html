Polemonium confertum var. eximium

Polemonium confertum var. eximium, commonly known as the showy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, showy flowers in shades of blue to purple. The leaves are lanceolate, alternate, and often covered with fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
