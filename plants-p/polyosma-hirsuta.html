Polyosma hirsuta

Polyosma hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy polyosma, is a shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature.

Polyosma hirsuta is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys.
