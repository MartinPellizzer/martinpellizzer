Potentilla heptaphylla var. gentilis

Potentilla heptaphylla var. gentilis, commonly known as the silverleaf potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climatic conditions.
