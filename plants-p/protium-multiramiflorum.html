Protium multiramiflorum

Protium multiramiflorum, commonly known as the multiramiflorum or the Philippine tamanu, is a member of the Burseraceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines, particularly found in lowland and montane rainforests. The plant has smooth, grayish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are alternate and have a leathery texture. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and clustered in dense panicles, typically blooming in white or pale yellow.

The tree produces small, spherical fruits that enclose a single seed, contributing to its ecological role in tropical forest ecosystems.
