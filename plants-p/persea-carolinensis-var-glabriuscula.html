Persea carolinensis var. glabriuscula

Persea carolinensis var. glabriuscula, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces orange to red, fleshy fruits that are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and the sweet, honey-like flavor of its fruit.
