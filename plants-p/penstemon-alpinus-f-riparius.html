Penstemon alpinus f. riparius

Penstemon alpinus f. riparius, commonly known as the alpine penstemon, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in well-drained, rocky soils at high elevations, thriving in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy coating.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
