Polygala persicariifolia

Polygala persicariifolia, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygalaceae family.

It is native to regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellow or greenish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Polygala persicariifolia is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
