Prunus cerasus monstr. rhexii

Prunus cerasus monstr.

rhexii, commonly known as the twisted cherry, is a unique cultivar of the cherry tree characterized by its distinctive, contorted growth pattern. This plant exhibits vigorous branching and twisted stems, giving it an unusual and ornamental appearance.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions with temperate climates. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, appearing in spring before the leaves emerge.

The fruit is a small cherry, though it is often ornamental rather than edible.
