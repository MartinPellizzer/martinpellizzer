Polygonum statice

Polygonum statice, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, spiny stems and striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in salty, well-drained soils. It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other plants like buckwheat and sorrel.

Polygonum statice is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in harsh coastal environments.
