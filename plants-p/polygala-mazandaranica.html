Polygala mazandaranica

Polygala mazandaranica, commonly known as the Mazandaran milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves are usually small and opposite, while its fruits are follicles that release seeds upon maturity.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native habitat.
