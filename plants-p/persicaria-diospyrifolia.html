Persicaria diospyrifolia

Persicaria diospyrifolia, commonly known as the Japanese knotweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a robust root system that can grow rapidly, making it an invasive species in many regions. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found near waterways or in disturbed habitats.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a significant ecological concern in areas where it has become naturalized.
