Primula petiolaris var. stracheyi

Primula petiolaris var. stracheyi, commonly known as the Strachey's primrose, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It features basal rosettes of broadly ovate, hairy, and dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and woodland settings.
