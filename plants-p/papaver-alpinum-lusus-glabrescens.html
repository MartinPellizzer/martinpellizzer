Papaver alpinum lusus glabrescens

Papaver alpinum lusus glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous alpine poppy, is a rare and striking member of the Papaveraceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, often with a distinctive central stigma. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments.
