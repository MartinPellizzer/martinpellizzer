Photinia glomerata var. cuneata

Photinia glomerata var. cuneata, commonly known as the glossy leaf photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and striking red or orange-red inflorescences that appear in late winter to early spring. The leaves are typically glossy, dark green, and oval in shape, providing a lush backdrop for the vibrant flowers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

This variety is particularly noted for its ability to attract birds and its resilience in various soil conditions.
