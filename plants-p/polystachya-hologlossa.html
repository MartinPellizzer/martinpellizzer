Polystachya hologlossa

Polystachya hologlossa, commonly known as the silver-leafed bromeliad, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, fragrant white flowers. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Polystachya hologlossa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and air-purifying qualities.
