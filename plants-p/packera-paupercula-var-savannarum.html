Packera paupercula var. savannarum

Packera paupercula var. savannarum, commonly known as the eastern prairie ragwort, is a herbaceous plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and sandy soils. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowering stems are usually unbranched. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is adapted to full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant, making it a characteristic component of dry, open ecosystems.
