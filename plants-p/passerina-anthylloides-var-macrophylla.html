Passerina anthylloides var. macrophylla

Passerina anthylloides var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes, and it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are palmately divided and can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its early spring blooming habit, often emerging before the forest canopy fully develops.
