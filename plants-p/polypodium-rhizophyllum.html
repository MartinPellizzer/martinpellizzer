Polypodium rhizophyllum

Polypodium rhizophyllum, commonly known as the creeping polypody, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with the rhizome often covered in a dense mat of roots. Its fronds are usually delicate and pinnately divided, with a dark green color and a leathery texture.

Polypodium rhizophyllum is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
