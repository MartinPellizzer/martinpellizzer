Persica domestica var. vinosa

Persica domestica var. vinosa, commonly known as the grapefruit tree, is a variant of the domestic peach that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is distinguished by its large, thick-skinned fruits that are typically orange to red in color, with a segmented interior resembling grapefruit. The plant produces showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its distinctive fruit. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a glossy surface, and the plant thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.
