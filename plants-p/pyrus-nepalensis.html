Pyrus nepalensis

Pyrus nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal pear, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pyrus.

It is native to the Himalayas, particularly in Nepal, India, and Bhutan, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are similar to pears in appearance and taste. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in horticulture for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.
