Peperomia dawsonii

Peperomia dawsonii, commonly known as Dawson's peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to Brazil.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its slow growth and attractive foliage, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
