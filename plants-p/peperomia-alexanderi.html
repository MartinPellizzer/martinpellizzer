Peperomia alexanderi

Peperomia alexanderi, commonly known as the Alexander's peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to Brazil.

It features small, waxy, ovate leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish or purple hue on the underside. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, usually in late spring or early summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its slow growth and attractive foliage make it a desirable addition to shaded or low-light environments.
