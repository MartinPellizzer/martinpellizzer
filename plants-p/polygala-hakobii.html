Polygala hakobii

Polygala hakobii, commonly known as the Japanese milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale green to white, with a distinctive, spurred structure.

Polygala hakobii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
