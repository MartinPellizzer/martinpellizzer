Papaver persicum subsp. fulvum

Papaver persicum subsp.

fulvum, commonly known as the Persian poppy, is a member of the Papaveraceae family and is native to regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to orange, with a distinctive, saucer-shaped corona.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
