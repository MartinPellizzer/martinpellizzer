Penstemon absarokensis

Penstemon absarokensis, commonly known as the Absaroka penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Wyoming and Montana.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rocky or alpine environments.

Penstemon absarokensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
