Pandanus mangarevaensis

Pandanus mangarevaensis, commonly known as the Mangareva pandanus, is a unique species native to the island of Mangareva in the Pacific Ocean.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to the humid, saline conditions of its environment. Its ecological role is significant in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from habitat loss and climate change.
