Pandanus latericius

Pandanus latericius, commonly known as the bottlebrush pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that form a dense, cylindrical shape, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, arranged in long, narrow clusters. Its unique structure makes it well adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

The plant is often used in traditional crafts and has cultural significance in various island communities.
