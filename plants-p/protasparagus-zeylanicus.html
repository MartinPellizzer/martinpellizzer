Protasparagus zeylanicus

Protasparagus zeylanicus, commonly known as the Ceylon asparagus, is a climbing plant native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
