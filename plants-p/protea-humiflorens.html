Protea humiflorens

Protea humiflorens, commonly known as the coastal protea, is a South African native plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers grouped into a rounded head. The plant typically grows in coastal regions, thriving in well-drained, sandy soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers are often pink or red, with a distinctive yellow center, and it blooms during the spring and summer months.

Protea humiflorens is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
