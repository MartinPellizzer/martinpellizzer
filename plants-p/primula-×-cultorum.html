Primula × cultorum

Primula × cultorum, commonly known as the common primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm, with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine downy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens and shaded borders.
