Pleomele acaulis

Pleomele acaulis, commonly known as the tree cycad, is a striking plant native to the island of Moorea in French Polynesia.

It is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, making it a long-lived species. Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native habitat.

However, it is also considered endangered due to habitat loss and over-collection.
