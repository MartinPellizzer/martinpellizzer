Piper heterophyllum

Piper heterophyllum, commonly known as the mountain pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant features compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes. Its fruits are berries that turn red when mature, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
