Panicum inaequilatum

Panicum inaequilatum, commonly known as the unequal panicgrass, is a species of grass native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects or as a forage plant for livestock.
