Piper cubeba

Piper cubeba, commonly known as long pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark red berries that resemble peppercorns. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small green flowers that develop into the aromatic fruits. The berries are harvested when fully mature and are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Its botanical features make it distinct from black and white pepper, which are derived from different species.
