Prunus spinosa proles fastigiata

Prunus spinosa proles fastigiata, commonly known as the upright blackthorn, is a compact, shrub-like species with a columnar growth habit.

It features small, dark purple to black berries that appear in late summer, and its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges. The plant is native to Europe and western Asia, often found in hedgerows and woodland edges. It has a slow growth rate and is valued for its ornamental appeal and wildlife value.

The species is also used in horticulture for its dense, thorny structure and seasonal interest.
