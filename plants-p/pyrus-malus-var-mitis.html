Pyrus malus var. mitis

Pyrus malus var. mitis, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a variety of the common pear tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers and sweet, juicy fruit with a delicate flavor. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its edible fruit, making it a popular choice in gardens and orchards.
