Poa chorizanthe

Poa chorizanthe, commonly known as the Chilean bluegrass, is a species of grass native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, blue to violet-colored flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grassy areas and along coastal regions.

Its foliage is typically used in ornamental gardening due to its delicate appearance and hardy nature.
