Pilosella × dybowskiana

Pilosella × dybowskiana, commonly known as the Dybowskiana hawkweed, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and is native to Central and Eastern Europe. The plant produces small, yellowish to white flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are usually hairy and lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pilosella × dybowskiana is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
