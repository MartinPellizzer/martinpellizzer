Potentilla × burjatica var. bigemina

Potentilla × burjatica var. bigemina, commonly known as the Turk's cap or mountain sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the high mountain regions of Central Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its unique floral structure and hardy nature make it a notable species in botanical studies and horticulture.
