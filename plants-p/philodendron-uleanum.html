Philodendron uleanum

Philodendron uleanum, commonly known as the Uleanum philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
