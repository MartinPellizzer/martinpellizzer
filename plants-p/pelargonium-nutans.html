Pelargonium nutans

Pelargonium nutans, commonly known as the drooping geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its pendulous flowers that bloom in shades of pink and red. The plant has ovate to lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Pelargonium nutans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular in gardens and as a potted plant.
