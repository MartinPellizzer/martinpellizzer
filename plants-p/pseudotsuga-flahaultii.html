Pseudotsuga flahaultii

Pseudotsuga flahaultii, commonly known as the Flahault's hemlock, is a rare and endangered conifer native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Pseudotsuga, which includes other hemlock species, but it is distinct due to its unique morphological features. The plant is characterized by its conical shape, needle-like leaves, and small, spherical cones. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in mountainous regions with cool, humid climates.

Due to its limited distribution and threats from habitat loss and climate change, it is a subject of conservation interest.
