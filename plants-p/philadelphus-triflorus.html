Philadelphus triflorus

Philadelphus triflorus, commonly known as the three-flowered mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun.

Philadelphus triflorus is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its pleasant fragrance and showy blooms.
