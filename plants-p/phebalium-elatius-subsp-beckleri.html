Phebalium elatius subsp. beckleri

Phebalium elatius subsp.

beckleri, commonly known as the Grey Box, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its grey-green, flaky bark and small, elliptical leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, Mediterranean climates.

This subspecies is often found in open woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
