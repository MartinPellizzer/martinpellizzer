Pseudorchis

Pseudorchis, commonly known as the false orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are typically small herbs with tubers and are native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. They produce showy, often fragrant flowers that are similar in appearance to true orchids. Pseudorchis species are characterized by their linear leaves and intricate floral structures.

They are often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
