Pinanga bowiana

Pinanga bowiana, commonly known as the Bow's bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, arrow-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in length. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

Pinanga bowiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
