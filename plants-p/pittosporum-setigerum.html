Pittosporum setigerum

Pittosporum setigerum, commonly known as the silver tree, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray bark and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest areas.

Pittosporum setigerum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
