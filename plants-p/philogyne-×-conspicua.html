Philogyne × conspicua

Philogyne × conspicua, commonly known as the showy philogyne, is a hybrid plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are elongated and narrow, often with a slight waxy coating.

Philogyne × conspicua is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
