Piper capitarianum

Piper capitarianum, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant in the Piperaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces small, dark berries that are edible but have a spicy, peppery flavor. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
