Polypodium subrepandum

Polypodium subrepandum, commonly known as the creeping polypody, is a fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a leathery texture and a pale green to yellowish color. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Polypodium subrepandum typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas.
