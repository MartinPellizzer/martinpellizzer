Panicum glabripes subsp. subjunceum

Panicum glabripes subsp.

subjunceum, commonly known as slender panicgrass, is a grass species native to parts of North America. It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves.

The inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that are often loosely arranged. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.

This subspecies is noted for its adaptability and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.
