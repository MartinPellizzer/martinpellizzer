Piper villibracteum

Piper villibracteum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by elongated, dark purple berries that are edible.

Piper villibracteum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of mild spices.
