Potentilla canescens var. laciniosa

Potentilla canescens var. laciniosa, commonly known as the laceleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, which are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
