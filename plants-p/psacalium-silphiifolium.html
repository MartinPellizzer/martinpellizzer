Psacalium silphiifolium

Psacalium silphiifolium, commonly known as the silphium leaf milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of elongated, silvery-green leaves that resemble those of the plant silphium. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to full sun.

Psacalium silphiifolium is a member of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is known for its medicinal and historical significance.
