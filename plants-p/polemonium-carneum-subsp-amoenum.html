Polemonium carneum subsp. amoenum

Polemonium carneum subsp.

amoenum, commonly known as the pink monkeyflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
