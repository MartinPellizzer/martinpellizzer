Pleurothallis limbata

Pleurothallis limbata, commonly known as the white-lipped pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on tree branches or rocky surfaces. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its flowers are often clustered in a spike and bloom during the warmer months.

Pleurothallis limbata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
