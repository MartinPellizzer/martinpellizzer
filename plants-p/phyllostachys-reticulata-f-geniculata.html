Phyllostachys reticulata f. geniculata

Phyllostachys reticulata f. geniculata, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a variant of the common bamboo species characterized by its striking silver-green culms.

It is a clumping bamboo that grows in dense, upright clusters, forming a compact and ornamental shape. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are a distinctive silvery-blue color, providing a unique visual appeal in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for a variety of climates.

This ornamental bamboo is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value and ability to create a natural, textured backdrop.
