Panicum naviculare

Panicum naviculare, commonly known as little barley, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist soils, often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces wind-dispersed seeds.

Panicum naviculare is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
