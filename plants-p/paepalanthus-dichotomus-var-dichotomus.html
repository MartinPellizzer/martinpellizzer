Paepalanthus dichotomus var. dichotomus

Paepalanthus dichotomus var. dichotomus, commonly known as the two-branched pincushion cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It is a small, succulent plant characterized by its dense, spiny stems that branch dichotomously, forming a compact, cushion-like structure. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a striking contrast to its green, spiky foliage. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and coastal areas.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
