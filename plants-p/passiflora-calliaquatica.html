Passiflora calliaquatica

Passiflora calliaquatica, commonly known as the Mexican passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central America and parts of South America.

It features striking, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a unique crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor.

Passiflora calliaquatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
