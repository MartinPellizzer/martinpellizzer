Potentilla alpestris var. araratica

Potentilla alpestris var. araratica, commonly known as the Alpina cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces showy, yellow flowers in early summer. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated margins and thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it tolerates cold temperatures and strong winds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing rocky soils.
