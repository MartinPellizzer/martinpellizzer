Prenanthes alba var. quercifolia

Prenanthes alba var. quercifolia, commonly known as the white helenium or oak-leaved helenium, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features finely dissected, fern-like leaves that resemble those of oak trees, giving it its common name. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
