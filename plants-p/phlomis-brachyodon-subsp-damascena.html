Phlomis brachyodon subsp. damascena

Phlomis brachyodon subsp.

damascena, commonly known as Damascene plumevetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and drought tolerance.
