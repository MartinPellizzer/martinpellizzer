Pelargonium × conquestor

Pelargonium × conquestor, commonly known as the African marigold, is a hybrid geranium species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, double-flowered blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically reaches a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, rounded leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for container gardening and rockeries.

Pelargonium × conquestor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its vibrant color and compact form.
