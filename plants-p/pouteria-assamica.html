Pouteria assamica

Pouteria assamica, commonly known as the Assam nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed surrounded by a fleshy, aromatic pulp. The seeds are highly valued for their aromatic properties and are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
