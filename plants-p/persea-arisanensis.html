Persea arisanensis

Persea arisanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, yellowish-green fruits that ripen to a reddish-orange color. Its fruit is edible, though it is typically consumed when fully mature and sweet.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in its native region.
