Premna papuana

Premna papuana, commonly known as the New Guinea glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are usually clustered in inflorescences and are often brightly colored, attracting pollinators.

Premna papuana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
