Primula roylei

Primula roylei, commonly known as the Kashmir primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. Primula roylei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to alpine and woodland gardens.
