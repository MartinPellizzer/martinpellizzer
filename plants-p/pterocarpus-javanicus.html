Pterocarpus javanicus

Pterocarpus javanicus, commonly known as the Java ebony, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange in color. It produces distinctive woody pods that contain flat, seed-like structures.

The tree thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.
