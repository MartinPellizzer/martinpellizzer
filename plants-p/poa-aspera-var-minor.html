Poa aspera var. minor

Poa aspera var. minor, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a variety of the meadow grass species native to temperate regions.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, spikelet-based inflorescence produces wind-pollinated flowers, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

This grass plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
