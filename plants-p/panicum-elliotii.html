Panicum elliotii

Panicum elliotii, commonly known as Elliot's panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. Its flowering period occurs in the summer, producing small, spike-like inflorescences that are often pale green to reddish in color.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
