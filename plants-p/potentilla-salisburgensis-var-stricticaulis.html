Potentilla salisburgensis var. stricticaulis

Potentilla salisburgensis var. stricticaulis, commonly known as the Salzburg cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to alpine regions of Central Europe.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, rounded leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
