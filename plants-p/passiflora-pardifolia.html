Passiflora pardifolia

Passiflora pardifolia, commonly known as the yellow passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with intricate, veined structures that resemble a crown. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Passiflora pardifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
