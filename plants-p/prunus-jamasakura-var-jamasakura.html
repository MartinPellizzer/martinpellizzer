Prunus jamasakura var. jamasakura

Prunus jamasakura var. jamasakura, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or sakura, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a striking visual display. The tree has a slender, upright growth habit with smooth, reddish-brown bark. Its leaves are ovate and turn a vibrant yellow in autumn.

This species is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and holds cultural significance in Japanese traditions.
