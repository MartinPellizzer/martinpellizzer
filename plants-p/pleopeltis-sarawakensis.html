Pleopeltis sarawakensis

Pleopeltis sarawakensis, commonly known as the Sarawak fern, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense layer of roots, which help it absorb moisture and nutrients.
