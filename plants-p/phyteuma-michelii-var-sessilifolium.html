Phyteuma michelii var. sessilifolium

Phyteuma michelii var. sessilifolium, commonly known as the sessile-leaved phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright, branching stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a distinct basal rosette. The flowers are typically pale yellow to orange, with tubular corollas and prominent lobes, blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
