Puya lokischmidtiae

Puya lokischmidtiae, commonly known as the Chilean torchflower, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to the Andes Mountains of Chile.

This plant is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that can reach up to 3 meters in height, adorned with vibrant red and orange bracts. The leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a rosette formation, often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and moderate rainfall.

Puya lokischmidtiae is known for its longevity, with some individuals surviving for over a century.
