Phytelephas microcarpa

Phytelephas microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited sago palm, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers. Its fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that contains a single seed.

Phytelephas microcarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local communities.
