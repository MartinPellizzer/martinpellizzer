Polygonum campanulatum var. lichiangense

Polygonum campanulatum var. lichiangense, commonly known as the Lichiang bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan, China.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves and hollow stems. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
