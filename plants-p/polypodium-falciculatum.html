Polypodium falciculatum

Polypodium falciculatum, commonly known as the sickle-crested polypody, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are often arched or sickle-shaped. The plant typically grows on rocks, trees, or soil in shaded, moist environments. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
