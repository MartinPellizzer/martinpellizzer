Pterocarpus amazonicus

Pterocarpus amazonicus, commonly known as the Amazonian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and aesthetic qualities. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are typically pinnately compound, with numerous leaflets that provide shade in the dense forest undergrowth.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various forms of wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of the region.
