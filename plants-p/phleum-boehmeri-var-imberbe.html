Phleum boehmeri var. imberbe

Phleum boehmeri var. imberbe, commonly known as the slender oat, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of grassland ecosystems.
