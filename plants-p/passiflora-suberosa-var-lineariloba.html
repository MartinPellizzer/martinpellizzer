Passiflora suberosa var. lineariloba

Passiflora suberosa var. lineariloba, commonly known as the slender passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces blue or purple berries that contain numerous seeds.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
