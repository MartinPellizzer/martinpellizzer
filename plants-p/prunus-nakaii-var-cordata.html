Prunus nakaii var. cordata

Prunus nakaii var. cordata, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Japanese flowering cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other cherry species. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small wildlife.
