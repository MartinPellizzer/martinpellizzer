Perymenium oxycarphum

Perymenium oxycarphum, commonly known as the sharp-leaved perymenium, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring. Its growth habit is herbaceous, with a tendency to form dense mats or low shrubs.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to moist, shaded environments.
