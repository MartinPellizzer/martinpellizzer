Polylobium intermedium

Polylobium intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate polylobium, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, lobed leaves that vary in shape depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited habitat and susceptibility to habitat loss, it is considered a conservation concern.
