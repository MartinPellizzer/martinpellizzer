Paschanthus jaeggii

Paschanthus jaeggii, commonly known as the velvetleaf paschanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with red veins, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often grown as an ornamental plant in subtropical regions.
