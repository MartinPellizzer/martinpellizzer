Pittosporum chelidospermum

Pittosporum chelidospermum, commonly known as the golden pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slight waxy coating. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. These flowers are followed by orange-red berries that are edible but not particularly palatable.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
