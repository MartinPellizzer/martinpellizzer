Pachyanthus monocephalus

Pachyanthus monocephalus, commonly known as the single-headed flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.
