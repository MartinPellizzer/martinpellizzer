Phyllobolus amabilis

Phyllobolus amabilis, commonly known as the golden leaf orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that are often a vibrant green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Phyllobolus amabilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
