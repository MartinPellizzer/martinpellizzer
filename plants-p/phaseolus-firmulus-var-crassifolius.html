Phaseolus firmulus var. crassifolius

Phaseolus firmulus var. crassifolius, commonly known as the thick-leaved common bean, is a species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both agricultural and ecological studies.
