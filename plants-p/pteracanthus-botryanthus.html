Pteracanthus botryanthus

Pteracanthus botryanthus, commonly known as the star-shaped leaf fern, is a tropical plant characterized by its unique foliage.

It features broad, wavy, and often star-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly textured surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or pale yellow, followed by spherical, spore-bearing fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical structure makes it an interesting subject for studying fern morphology and adaptation to moist, low-light conditions.
