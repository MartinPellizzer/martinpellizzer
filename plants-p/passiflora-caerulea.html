Passiflora caerulea

Passiflora caerulea, commonly known as the blue passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blue corona with filaments resembling threads. The plant produces edible, blue-black berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Passiflora caerulea is also valued in traditional medicine for its calming properties.
