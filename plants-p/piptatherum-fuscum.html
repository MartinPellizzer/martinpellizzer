Piptatherum fuscum

Piptatherum fuscum, commonly known as black bentgrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and dark green, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Piptatherum fuscum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
