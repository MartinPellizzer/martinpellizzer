Parapolystichum decompositum

Parapolystichum decompositum, commonly known as the fragmented shield fern, is a rare and elusive fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring fronds that are often divided into distinct, almost separate segments. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically within the understory of dense tropical forests. Its reproductive structures are relatively simple, with spores produced in sori located on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
