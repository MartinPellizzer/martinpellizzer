Papaver somniferum var. austroviolaceum

Papaver somniferum var. austroviolaceum, commonly known as the Austrian purple poppy, is a rare variant of the opium poppy characterized by its striking violet to purple flower petals.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe, particularly Austria and the surrounding areas. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves and a tall, slender stem. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.

Botanically, it is noted for its morphological differences from the more commonly cultivated varieties of Papaver somniferum.
