Puya huancavelicae

Puya huancavelicae, commonly known as the Huancavelica puya, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces a tall flowering stem with numerous small, spadix-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white flowers. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, dry conditions and is adapted to the harsh climate of the Andes.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
