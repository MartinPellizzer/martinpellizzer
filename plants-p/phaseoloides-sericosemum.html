Phaseoloides sericosemum

Phaseoloides sericosemum, commonly known as the silky phaseolus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant produces lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
