Prunus pectinata

Prunus pectinata, commonly known as the Chinese flowering cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape with serrated edges. It produces small, dark red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Prunus pectinata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
