Pinillosia berteroi

Pinillosia berteroi, commonly known as the Mexican rosewood, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its rough, fissured bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by small, leathery fruits containing one or two seeds.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in limestone soils and is valued for its hard, aromatic wood.
