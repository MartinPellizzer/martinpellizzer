Potentilla crantzii var. brotheriana

Potentilla crantzii var. brotheriana, commonly known as the Brother's potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers in early summer. The plant has finely divided, silver-gray leaves that are covered in a fine layer of silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a favored plant among gardeners interested in cultivating native and resilient flora.
