Parosela canescens

Parosela canescens, commonly known as the hairy parosela, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy, grayish-green foliage. Its flowers are small and yellow, often appearing in clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
