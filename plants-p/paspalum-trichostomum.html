Paspalum trichostomum

Paspalum trichostomum, commonly known as the narrowleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

Its adaptability makes it a common species in various ecological and agricultural settings.
