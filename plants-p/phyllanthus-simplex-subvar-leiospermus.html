Phyllanthus simplex subvar. leiospermus

Phyllanthus simplex subvar. leiospermus, commonly known as the smooth-seed phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round, smooth-seeded fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
