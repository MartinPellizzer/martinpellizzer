Phyllostachys edulis f. aureovariegata

Phyllostachys edulis f. aureovariegata, commonly known as the golden variegated bamboo, is a variegated form of the common bamboo, Phyllostachys edulis.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green striped leaves, which provide a distinctive visual appeal in gardens and landscapes. This bamboo is a clumping species that grows to a moderate height, making it suitable for both ornamental and functional planting. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, requiring minimal maintenance once established.

The plant is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is often used in ornamental horticulture.
