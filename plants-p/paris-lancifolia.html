Paris lancifolia

Paris lancifolia, commonly known as the white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with reddish veins. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark blue, waxy berries. Paris lancifolia is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
