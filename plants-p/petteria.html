Petteria

Petteria, commonly known as the "buttercup plant," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Brassicaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to South America, particularly the Andean regions. The plants typically have basal leaves that are lobed or dissected, and they produce small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Their flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
