Piper multinervium var. protractifolium

Piper multinervium var. protractifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by berries containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The varietal form protractifolium is distinguished by its notably longer and narrower foliage compared to the typical species.
