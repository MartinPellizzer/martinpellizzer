Passaveria lancifolia

Passaveria lancifolia, commonly known as the slender arrowgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

Passaveria lancifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
