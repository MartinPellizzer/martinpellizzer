Persica triloba

Persica triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed peach.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy pink or white flowers. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are edible but less commonly cultivated than other peach varieties. Persica triloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its spring blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable plant for temperate climates.
