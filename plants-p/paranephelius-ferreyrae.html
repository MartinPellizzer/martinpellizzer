Paranephelius ferreyrae

Paranephelius ferreyrae, commonly known as the Ferreyra's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves and its unique, fragrant flowers that bloom in a striking array of white and purple hues. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it relies on specific symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered critically endangered.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect this rare botanical treasure from further decline.
