Paronychia kapela subsp. baetica

Paronychia kapela subsp.

baetica, commonly known as the Cape Town daisies, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center.

The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, green, oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy to the touch.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
