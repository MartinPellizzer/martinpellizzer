Pelargonium boyleae

Pelargonium boyleae, commonly known as the Natal geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its vibrant red or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Pelargonium boyleae is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
