Phaseoloides ovalifolium

Phaseoloides ovalifolium, commonly known as the oval-leaf phaseoloid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features palmate leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, clustered flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
