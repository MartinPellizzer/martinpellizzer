Pyrrocoma crocea var. crocea

Pyrrocoma crocea var. crocea, commonly known as the red-flowered pyrrocoma, is a striking member of the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals, which contrast sharply with its green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodland floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
