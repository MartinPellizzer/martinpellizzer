Polycnemum alternifolium

Polycnemum alternifolium, commonly known as the alternate-leaved water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate arrangement of feather-like leaves that float on the water surface. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and ditches. Its rhizomes form dense mats that help stabilize the sediment and provide habitat for small aquatic organisms.

The fronds are typically dark green and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.
