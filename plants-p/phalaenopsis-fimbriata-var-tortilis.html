Phalaenopsis fimbriata var. tortilis

Phalaenopsis fimbriata var. tortilis, commonly known as the twisted phalaenopsis, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Borneo.

It is characterized by its long, gracefully curling leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is often marked with distinctive, wavy patterns, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its rarity and specific environmental requirements, it is a sought-after specimen in horticultural circles.
