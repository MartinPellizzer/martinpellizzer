Pedilonum lawesii

Pedilonum lawesii, commonly known as the Lawes' slipper orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, pouch-like flower that resembles a slipper, which is a distinguishing feature of the Orchidaceae family. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Pedilonum lawesii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
