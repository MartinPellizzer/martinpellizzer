Periploca americana

Periploca americana, commonly known as American periploca, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
