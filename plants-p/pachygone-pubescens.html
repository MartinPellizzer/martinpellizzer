Pachygone pubescens

Pachygone pubescens, commonly known as the hairy pachygone, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, pubescent foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
