Psychotria reticulatissima

Psychotria reticulatissima, commonly known as the "Crimson Beauty," is a tropical flowering plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant has compound leaves with a distinctive reticulate pattern, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Psychotria reticulatissima is also notable for its presence in traditional medicine and as a species of interest in botanical research.
