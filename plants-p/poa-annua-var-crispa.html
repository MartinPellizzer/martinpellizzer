Poa annua var. crispa

Poa annua var. crispa, commonly known as the curly bentgrass, is a variant of the annual bluegrass species.

It is characterized by its distinctive curled leaves and compact growth habit. This plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lawns, gardens, and disturbed areas. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Poa annua var. crispa is a short-lived perennial that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
