Pinus clausa var. immuginata

Pinus clausa var. immuginata, commonly known as the Mexican bristlecone pine, is a rare and ancient conifer native to the high-altitude regions of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its slow growth, twisted trunk, and dense, needle-like foliage. The species is adapted to harsh, arid environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is of particular interest to botanists due to its longevity and unique ecological adaptations.
