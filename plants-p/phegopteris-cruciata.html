Phegopteris cruciata

Phegopteris cruciata, commonly known as the cross-veined water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to North America.

It thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has a distinctive frond structure with a cross-shaped vein pattern, giving it its common name. Its rhizomes form dense colonies, and it produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds.

Phegopteris cruciata plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to water quality maintenance.
