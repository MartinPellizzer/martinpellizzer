Parkia nana

Parkia nana, commonly known as the dwarf capuchin bean, is a small, deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in tropical habitats.
