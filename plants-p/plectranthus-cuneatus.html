Plectranthus cuneatus

Plectranthus cuneatus, commonly known as the Cape verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, pointed leaves and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Plectranthus cuneatus is also valued for its aromatic foliage, which can be used in herbal teas or as a natural remedy.
