Plantago crispa

Plantago crispa, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features upright, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant is also a common weed in agricultural settings.
