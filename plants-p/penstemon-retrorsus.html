Penstemon retrorsus

Penstemon retrorsus, commonly known as the reverseleaf beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Penstemon retrorsus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
