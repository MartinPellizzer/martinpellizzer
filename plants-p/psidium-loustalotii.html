Psidium loustalotii

Psidium loustalotii, commonly known as the Loustalot's guava, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and edible, berry-like fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a rough texture with a strong aromatic scent.

The fruit is usually yellow or red when ripe and is valued for its flavor and nutritional content.
