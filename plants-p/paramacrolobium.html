Paramacrolobium

Paramacrolobium, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a large aquatic plant native to tropical regions.

It features broad, floating leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter, providing shade over water bodies. Its flowers are showy, with white petals that open in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has a robust root system that anchors it in shallow water.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by offering habitat and shelter for various aquatic organisms.
