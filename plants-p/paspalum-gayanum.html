Paspalum gayanum

Paspalum gayanum, commonly known as the Sri Lankan paspalum, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has slender, tufted culms with dense foliage, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle structure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Paspalum gayanum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various small organisms.
