Paepalanthus cearaensis

Paepalanthus cearaensis, commonly known as the Ceara starbursts, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy inflorescence. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in a dense, spherical cluster. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and dry shrublands.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
