Plantago adspersa

Plantago adspersa, commonly known as the common plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, fields, and gardens.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.
