Pteris propinqua

Pteris propinqua, commonly known as the Japanese spleenwort, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It is a small, terrestrial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has a delicate, creeping rhizome with fronds that are usually triangular in shape. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a distinctive, feathery appearance.

Pteris propinqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
