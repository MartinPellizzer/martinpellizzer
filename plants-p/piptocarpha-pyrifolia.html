Piptocarpha pyrifolia

Piptocarpha pyrifolia, commonly known as the flamebrush or desert flamebrush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair, helping to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Piptocarpha pyrifolia plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
