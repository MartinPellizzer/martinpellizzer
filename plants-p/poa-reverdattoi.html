Poa reverdattoi

Poa reverdattoi, commonly known as the alpine bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically blue-green in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish hue that contributes to its distinctive appearance.

Poa reverdattoi plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude habitats.
