Picris mairei

Picris mairei, commonly known as the Maire's wormwood, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its feathery, finely divided leaves and small, yellowish flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showing a preference for well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The species is noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
