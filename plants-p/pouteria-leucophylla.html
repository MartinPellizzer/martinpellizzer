Pouteria leucophylla

Pouteria leucophylla, commonly known as the white-leafed spondias, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that are typically pale green on the underside. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, round fruits resembling small apples. Its fruit is often consumed fresh or used in traditional culinary practices.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
