Pyrus bodinieri

Pyrus bodinieri, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy white flowers that appear in early spring. The fruit is a small, sweet, yellowish pear that ripens in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The plant is adaptable to a range of soil types and prefers full sun to partial shade.
