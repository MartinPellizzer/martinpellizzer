Phyllanthus taxifolius

Phyllanthus taxifolius, commonly known as the sacred lotus of India, is a small shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features opposite, simple, and often elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are capsules containing numerous tiny seeds.

The plant is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine for various health benefits.
