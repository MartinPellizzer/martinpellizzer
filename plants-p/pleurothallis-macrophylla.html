Pleurothallis macrophylla

Pleurothallis macrophylla, commonly known as the giant pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, strap-like leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters along the stem. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
