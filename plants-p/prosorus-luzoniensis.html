Prosorus luzoniensis

Prosorus luzoniensis, commonly known as the Luzon tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish flowers. The tree produces long, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp. It is often found in lowland and montane forests, contributing to local biodiversity and ecological balance.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in various local crafts and construction.
