Primula licentii

Primula licentii, commonly known as the Licent's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats at high altitudes. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy coating.

Primula licentii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
