Pitcairnia alata var. andreetae

Pitcairnia alata var. andreetae, commonly known as the Pink Lady's Slipper, is a striking bromeliad native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

This variety is characterized by its vibrant pink and white bracts that resemble a slipper, giving it its common name. The plant features a rosette of narrow, glossy leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. It produces strikingly colorful inflorescences with showy, long-lasting flowers.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
