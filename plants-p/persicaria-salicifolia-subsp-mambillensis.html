Persicaria salicifolia subsp. mambillensis

Persicaria salicifolia subsp.

mambillensis, commonly known as the African smartweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Africa. It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of pink or white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
