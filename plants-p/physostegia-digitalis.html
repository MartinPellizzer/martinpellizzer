Physostegia digitalis

Physostegia digitalis, commonly known as the whorled loosestrife, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in whorled clusters at the ends of its branches. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland gardens and conservation efforts.
