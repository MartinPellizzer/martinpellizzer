Petrophile pulchella var. canescens

Petrophile pulchella var. canescens, commonly known as the silver-leafed rock-heath, is a shrub native to the south-western regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its silvery-grey foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs that help reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in late winter to early spring, typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland heathlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a popular subject in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
