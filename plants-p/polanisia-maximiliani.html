Polanisia maximiliani

Polanisia maximiliani, commonly known as the giant lily, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, often displaying vibrant shades of pink and white. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, contributing to its dramatic appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections worldwide.
