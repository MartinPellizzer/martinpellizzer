Pseuderanthemum tetrasepalum

Pseuderanthemum tetrasepalum, commonly known as the four-lobed pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a glossy surface. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with four petals and prominent stamens, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.
