Passiflora hartwiesiana

Passiflora hartwiesiana, commonly known as the Hartweg's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a unique arrangement of petals, sepals, and bracts. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by various wildlife species. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.

Passiflora hartwiesiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
