Potentilla archboldiana

Potentilla archboldiana, commonly known as the archbold's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, yellowish-white flowers and silver-green foliage. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to desert environments. Its botanical features include compound leaves with five leaflets and a compact growth habit.

Potentilla archboldiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh conditions.
