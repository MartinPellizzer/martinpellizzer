Pandanus scortechini

Pandanus scortechini, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into fibrous, spiky fruits. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found near mangrove forests.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
