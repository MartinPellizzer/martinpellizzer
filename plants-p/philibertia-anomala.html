Philibertia anomala

Philibertia anomala, commonly known as the narrowleaf filbert, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces edible nuts similar to those of the hazelnut.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its nut-producing potential.
