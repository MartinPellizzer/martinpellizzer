Populus fremontii subsp. mesetae

Populus fremontii subsp.

mesetae, commonly known as the Meseta cottonwood, is a subspecies native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, slender growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

This plant thrives in riparian zones, often along rivers and streams, where it plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife. Its flowers are catkins, which are wind-pollinated, and it produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

The subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
