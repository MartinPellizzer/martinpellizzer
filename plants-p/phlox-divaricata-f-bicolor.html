Phlox divaricata f. bicolor

Phlox divaricata f. bicolor, commonly known as the white and pink phlox, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a compact perennial that produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically blooms in early to mid-summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its foliage is dark green and slightly hairy, adding texture to garden landscapes.
