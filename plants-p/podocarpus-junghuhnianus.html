Podocarpus junghuhnianus

Podocarpus junghuhnianus, commonly known as the Java podocarp, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, cone-like structures. The plant typically reaches heights of up to 20 meters and is often found in humid, tropical climates. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive aromatic scent, while its cones are woody and contain seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
