Polygala calcarea var. irimargarum

Polygala calcarea var. irimargarum, commonly known as the limestone polygala, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats. The plant features slender, erect stems with small, clustered flowers that are usually pale pink or white. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
