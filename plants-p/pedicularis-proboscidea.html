Pedicularis proboscidea

Pedicularis proboscidea, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orobanchaceae family.

It is a hemiparasitic plant, meaning it partially relies on host plants for nutrients through its specialized root system. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forested areas with rich, organic soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in many regions.
