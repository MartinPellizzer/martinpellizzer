Passiflora maximiliana var. retusa

Passiflora maximiliana var. retusa, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bi-color flowers that feature a deep purple corona with yellow filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, aromatic flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
