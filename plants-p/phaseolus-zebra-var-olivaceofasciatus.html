Phaseolus zebra var. olivaceofasciatus

Phaseolus zebra var. olivaceofasciatus, commonly known as the green-seeded zebra bean, is a variant of the zebra bean species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive mottled or striped leaf patterns, which give it its common name. The plant is an annual legume that typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with compound leaves and small, pale yellow flowers. Its pods are usually elongated and contain 4–6 oval, green seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for both decorative and culinary purposes.
