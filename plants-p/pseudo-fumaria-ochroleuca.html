Pseudo-fumaria ochroleuca

Pseudo-fumaria ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow fumaria, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, ascending stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are alternate, pinnate, and often have a slight pubescence.

Pseudo-fumaria ochroleuca is typically found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
