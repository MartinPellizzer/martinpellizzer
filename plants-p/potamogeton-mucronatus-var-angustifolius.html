Potamogeton mucronatus var. angustifolius

Potamogeton mucronatus var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm wide, with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, green flowers that are arranged in dense spikes, and it is often found in shallow, slow-moving waters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its rapid growth can also lead to dense vegetation that affects water flow and light penetration.
