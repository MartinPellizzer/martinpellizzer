Piper injucundum

Piper injucundum, commonly known as the bitter pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, heart-shaped leaves, and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is known for its pungent and bitter flavor, which is used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.
