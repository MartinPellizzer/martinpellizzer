Prasophyllum grimwadeanum

Prasophyllum grimwadeanum, commonly known as the Grimwade's leek orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid native to the southeastern region of Australia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a single, upright stem that bears a single or occasionally two narrow, strap-like leaves. The plant produces a spike of small, pale green to brownish flowers, which are typically less than 1 cm in length. It is closely related to other members of the Prasophyllum genus, which are often referred to as leek orchids due to their leaf structure.

This species is listed as vulnerable due to habitat loss and is primarily found in heathland and woodland environments.
