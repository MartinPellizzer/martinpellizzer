Phyllodium siamense

Phyllodium siamense, commonly known as the Siam phyllodium, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive phyllodes, which are modified leaf stems that function as leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in open woodlands or along forest edges. Its small, clustered flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Phyllodium siamense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
