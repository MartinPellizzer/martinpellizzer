Paepalanthus andicola

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family, which includes many epiphytic and terrestrial plants. The plant is characterized by its rosette of long, narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense, water-holding structure. Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
