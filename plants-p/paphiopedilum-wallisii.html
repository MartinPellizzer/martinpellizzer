Paphiopedilum wallisii

Paphiopedilum wallisii, commonly known as the Wallisii orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a thick, fleshy rhizome and produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually pink or white with a yellow or orange lip.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is relatively easy to cultivate in warm, moist conditions.
