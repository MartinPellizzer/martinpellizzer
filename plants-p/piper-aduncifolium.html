Piper aduncifolium

Piper aduncifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pipevine, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
