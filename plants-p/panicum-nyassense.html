Panicum nyassense

Panicum nyassense, commonly known as the Nyassa grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lipped flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Panicum nyassense is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and can also serve as forage for livestock.
