Psidium cerasoides

Psidium cerasoides, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or granadilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or yellow when ripe, containing a sweet, juicy pulp surrounded by a hard, inedible shell. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The plant is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
