Polypodium brasiliense

Polypodium brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazil fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically light green in color. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizome is thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

Polypodium brasiliense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
