Pteris medogensis

Pteris medogensis, commonly known as the Medog water fern, is a species of fern native to the Himalayan region, particularly in China.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are typically light green and have a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous areas with high humidity. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of the fern family.

Pteris medogensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
