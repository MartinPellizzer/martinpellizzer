Paepalanthus sessiliflorus

Paepalanthus sessiliflorus, commonly known as the sessile-flower arrowroot, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, native to South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments. The plant features slender, grass-like stems and produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale to brownish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, which helps in water retention.

While not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
