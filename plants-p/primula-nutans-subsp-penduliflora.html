Primula nutans subsp. penduliflora

Primula nutans subsp.

penduliflora, commonly known as the drooping primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It features upright stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often slightly hairy.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that hang downward, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, pendulous blooms.
