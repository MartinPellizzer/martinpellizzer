Philodendron edwinii

Philodendron edwinii, commonly known as the Edwin's philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly variegated pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil.

Philodendron edwinii is valued for its ornamental foliage and is popular among houseplant enthusiasts.
