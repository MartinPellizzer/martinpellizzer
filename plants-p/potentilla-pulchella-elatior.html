Potentilla pulchella elatior

Potentilla pulchella elatior, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters, with many small, upright stems. The plant produces clusters of yellow or white flowers during the summer months, which are borne on slender pedicels. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

The species is adapted to harsh alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
