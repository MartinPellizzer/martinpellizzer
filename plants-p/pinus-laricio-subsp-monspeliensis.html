Pinus laricio subsp. monspeliensis

Pinus laricio subsp.

monspeliensis, commonly known as the Monspeliensis stone pine, is a subspecies of the stone pine native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, evergreen foliage composed of long, needle-like leaves.

The cones of this plant are typically reddish-brown and contain edible seeds that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
