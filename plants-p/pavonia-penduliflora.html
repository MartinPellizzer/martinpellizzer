Pavonia penduliflora

Pavonia penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant has slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Pavonia penduliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
