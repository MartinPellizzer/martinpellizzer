Pfaffia iresinoides var. villosa

Pfaffia iresinoides var. villosa, commonly known as Brazilian ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in humid tropical climates.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Amaranthaceae family.
