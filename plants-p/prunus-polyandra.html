Prunus polyandra

Prunus polyandra, commonly known as the Japanese white cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Prunus polyandra is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for wildlife.
