Pilea japonica

Pilea japonica, commonly known as the Japanese begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features rosettes of heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often variegated with silver and green. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers on slender stalks, which bloom in spring. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular houseplant.

Its ornamental foliage and ease of care contribute to its popularity among plant enthusiasts.
