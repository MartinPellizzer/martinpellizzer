Potamogeton baenitzii

Potamogeton baenitzii, commonly known as the narrowleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like structures. This species is often found in shallow, slow-moving waters such as lakes, ponds, and rivers. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

The plant is also used in phytoremediation efforts due to its ability to absorb pollutants from the water.
