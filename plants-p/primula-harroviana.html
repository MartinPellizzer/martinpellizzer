Primula harroviana

Primula harroviana, commonly known as the Harrovian primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for rock gardens and shady borders.
