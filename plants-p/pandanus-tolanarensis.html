Pandanus tolanarensis

Pandanus tolanarensis, commonly known as the Tolana pandanus, is a species of palm native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clustered inflorescences, which are typically found at the base of the leaves. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material in local communities.

The species is adapted to grow in coastal and wetland environments, showcasing its resilience to humid and saline conditions.
