Phacelia integrifolia var. robusta

Phacelia integrifolia var. robusta, commonly known as the robust phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with smooth edges. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
