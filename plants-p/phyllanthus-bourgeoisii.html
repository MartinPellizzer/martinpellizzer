Phyllanthus bourgeoisii

Phyllanthus bourgeoisii, commonly known as the African spurge, is a shrub native to tropical and southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers and thick, fleshy leaves. The plant produces a milky latex when the stems or leaves are broken, which is a common trait in the Euphorbiaceae family. It typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Phyllanthus bourgeoisii is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
