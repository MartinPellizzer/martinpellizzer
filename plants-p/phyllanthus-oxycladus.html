Phyllanthus oxycladus

Phyllanthus oxycladus, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features small, greenish-white flowers and produces small, round, yellowish fruits. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

The species is notable for its medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
