Phacelia grandiflora

Phacelia grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Phacelia grandiflora is known for its role in attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
