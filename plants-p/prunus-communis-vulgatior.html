Prunus communis vulgatior

Prunus communis vulgatior, commonly known as the common cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible drupes that range in color from red to black. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The species is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it has been widely introduced elsewhere.
