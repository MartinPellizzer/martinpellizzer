Palaquium formosanum

Palaquium formosanum, commonly known as the Formosan wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The fruit is a small, round drupe with a thick, waxy skin and a sweet, juicy pulp. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
