Passiflora servitensis

Passiflora servitensis, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Andes, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It produces striking, colorful flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments that resemble a crown. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing along riverbanks and in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional herbal use.
