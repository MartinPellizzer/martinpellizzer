Plantago atrata subsp. graeca

Plantago atrata subsp.

graeca, commonly known as the Greek plantain, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands.

The plant features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. It produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
