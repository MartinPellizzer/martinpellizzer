Poa pratensis f. glauca

Poa pratensis f. glauca, commonly known as the blue meadowgrass, is a variant of the common meadowgrass characterized by its distinctive bluish-gray foliage.

This form exhibits a compact growth habit and is often found in moist, temperate regions. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a waxy coating that gives them their characteristic color. It is a perennial plant that thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers.
