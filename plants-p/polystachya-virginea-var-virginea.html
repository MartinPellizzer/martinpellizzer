Polystachya virginea var. virginea

Polystachya virginea var. virginea, commonly known as the Virgin's Tears orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing numerous small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, aiding in moisture retention.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
