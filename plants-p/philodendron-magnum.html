Philodendron magnum

Philodendron magnum, commonly known as the giant philodendron, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length. The plant has a vigorous growth habit and can reach heights of over 20 feet in its natural habitat. Its stems are aerial and can root at the nodes, allowing it to climb trees and other structures.

Philodendron magnum prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
