Philodendron sulcatum

Philodendron sulcatum, commonly known as the "sulcate philodendron," is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves with a distinctive, deep green color and prominent veins. The plant has a unique, grooved stem that gives it its common name, and it produces small, pale green flowers that are not typically showy. Philodendron sulcatum thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.

It is a popular houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
