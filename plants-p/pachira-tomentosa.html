Pachira tomentosa

Pachira tomentosa, commonly known as the Mexican beauty or woolly pachira, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces yellow, clustered flowers that are followed by round, green fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Pachira tomentosa is also valued for its ability to purify indoor air and its resilience to low light conditions.
