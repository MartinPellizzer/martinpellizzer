Pleopeltis glossophylla

Pleopeltis glossophylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree trunks or rocky surfaces. Its rhizomes are short and often covered in small, scale-like structures.

Pleopeltis glossophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
