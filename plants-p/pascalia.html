Pascalia

Pascalia, commonly known as sweet William, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered blooms that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Pascalia thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
