Parsonsia baillonis

Parsonsia baillonis, commonly known as the red-flowered parsonsia, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal or forested areas. The plant is characterized by its showy, bright red or orange-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are usually simple, opposite, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Parsonsia baillonis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
