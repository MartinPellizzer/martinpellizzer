Phlox × rosea

Phlox × rosea, commonly known as garden phlox, is a hybrid flowering plant belonging to the Polemonium family.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, purple, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Botanically, it is valued for its adaptability to different soil types and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
