Phoradendron craspedophyllum

Phoradendron craspedophyllum, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of trees and shrubs.

It has small, scale-like leaves and produces white or pale green berries that contain sticky seeds. The plant relies on its host for water and some nutrients, while photosynthesis provides it with additional energy. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, including parts of Central and South America.

Its parasitic nature and unique appearance make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
