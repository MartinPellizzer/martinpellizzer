Primulina repanda var. guilinensis

Primulina repanda var. guilinensis, commonly known as the Guilin primula, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in mountainous regions. It has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged in a rosette pattern.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
