Pancratium littorale var. dryanderi

Pancratium littorale var. dryanderi, commonly known as the coastal yellow nutmeg, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking yellow-orange flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dune environments. It is a perennial species that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, with a spreading habit.

The flowers are pollinated by insects and the plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
