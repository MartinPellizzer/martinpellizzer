Piper subasperatum

Piper subasperatum, commonly known as the roughleaf pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing a mild, peppery flavor.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
