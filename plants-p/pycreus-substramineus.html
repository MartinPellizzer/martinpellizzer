Pycreus substramineus

Pycreus substramineus, commonly known as the swamp bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle purple hue. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, thriving in swampy or marshy environments. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often floating on the water's surface.

Pycreus substramineus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
