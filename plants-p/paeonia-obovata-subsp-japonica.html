Paeonia obovata subsp. japonica

Paeonia obovata subsp.

japonica, commonly known as the Japanese peony, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a subtle fragrance.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring heart-shaped, doubly serrated leaves. Its root system consists of thick, fleshy rhizomes that store nutrients and help the plant survive winter.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
