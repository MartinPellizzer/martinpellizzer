Papaver rhoeas var. trifidum

Papaver rhoeas var. trifidum, commonly known as the trifid poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its distinctive trifid (three-lobed) petals.

This plant is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. It produces vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, and the plant is known for its short stature and rapid growth cycle.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
