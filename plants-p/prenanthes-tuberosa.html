Prenanthes tuberosa

Prenanthes tuberosa, commonly known as the tuberous sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features a robust underground tuber that serves as a storage organ for nutrients. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are heart-shaped. Its flowers are yellow and daisy-like, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
