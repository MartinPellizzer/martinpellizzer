Pittosporum virgatum var. serratum

Pittosporum virgatum var. serratum, commonly known as the serrated pittosporum, is a deciduous shrub native to New Zealand.

It features lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. These flowers are followed by bright red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
