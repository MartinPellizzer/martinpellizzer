Potentilla geoides subsp. halacsyana

Potentilla geoides subsp.

halacsyana, commonly known as the narrowleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments.

The plant features narrow, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in the spring. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
