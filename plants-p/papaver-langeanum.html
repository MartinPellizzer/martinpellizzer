Papaver langeanum

Papaver langeanum, commonly known as the Langean poppy, is a species of flowering plant in the family Papaveraceae.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, where it typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, depending on the variety. Its petals are usually papery and delicate, while the seed pods are elongated and contain numerous small, dark seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
