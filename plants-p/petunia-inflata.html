Petunia inflata

Petunia inflata, commonly known as the balloon flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its inflated, balloon-like blooms that come in a variety of colors, including pink, purple, and white. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its flowers are tubular at the base and expand into a rounded shape, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
