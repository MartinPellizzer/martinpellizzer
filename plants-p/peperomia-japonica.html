Peperomia japonica

Peperomia japonica, commonly known as Japanese pepper plant, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features thick, waxy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a subtle reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate moisture.

Peperomia japonica is prized for its attractive foliage and ease of care, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
