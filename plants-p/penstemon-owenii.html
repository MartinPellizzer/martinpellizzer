Penstemon owenii

Penstemon owenii, commonly known as Owen's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces tall spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple, pink, or white. It is valued in xeriscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.

Penstemon owenii is also an important nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
