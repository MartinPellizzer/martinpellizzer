Phalaenopsis decumbens

Phalaenopsis decumbens, commonly known as the drooping orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit with fleshy, often reddish roots that help it survive in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers with a lip that is often marked with purple or brown. It thrives in high humidity and requires moderate light, making it suitable for greenhouse or shaded garden settings.

Its unique morphology and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
