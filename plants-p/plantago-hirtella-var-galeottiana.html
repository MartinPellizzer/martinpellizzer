Plantago hirtella var. galeottiana

Plantago hirtella var. galeottiana, commonly known as the Galeottian plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a dense spike that develops from the center of the rosette. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

The species is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
