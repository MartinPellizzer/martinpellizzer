Physalis heterophylla

Physalis heterophylla, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or golden gooseberry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its distinctive inflated calyx that encloses the fruit. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically oval or heart-shaped, with a rough texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Physalis heterophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits.
