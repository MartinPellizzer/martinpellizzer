Phaseolus vulgaris var. aborigineus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. aborigineus, commonly known as the Andean runner bean, is a variety of common bean native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender pods and edible seeds, which are typically harvested when young and tender. The plant is an annual legume that thrives in warm climates and well-drained soils. It produces compound leaves and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is cultivated for both food and ornamental purposes.
