Prunus × compta

Prunus × compta, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or sakura, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is celebrated for its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, often preceding leaf emergence. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender branches and fragrant blossoms. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, turning green in summer and yellow in autumn.

Prunus × compta is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and holds cultural significance in Japanese traditions.
