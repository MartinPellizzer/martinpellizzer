Pterocarpus martinii

Pterocarpus martinii, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its density and appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils with adequate sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and timber, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
