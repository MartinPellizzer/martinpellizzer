Pharnaceum pusillum

Pharnaceum pusillum, commonly known as the small bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its tiny, bladder-like structures that trap small organisms, aiding in its nutrient acquisition. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are simple and often clustered at the base, while its stems are short and branched.

Pharnaceum pusillum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to the food web and maintaining water quality.
