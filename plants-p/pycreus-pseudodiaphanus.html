Pycreus pseudodiaphanus

Pycreus pseudodiaphanus, commonly known as the False Ghost Orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance and its unique, pale greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy habitats and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, often forming a dense rosette near the ground.

Due to its habitat specificity and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species.
