Passiflora cubensis var. profundelobata

Passiflora cubensis var. profundelobata, commonly known as the deep-lobed passionflower, is a rare and striking member of the Passifloraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, intricately lobed leaves and vibrant, showy flowers with a unique blend of colors and patterns. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers emit a strong fragrance, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
