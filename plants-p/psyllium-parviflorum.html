Psyllium parviflorum

Psyllium parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered psyllium, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale green flowers. The plant produces a seed capsule that contains tiny, mucilaginous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a dietary supplement. Its growth habit is typically annual, with a life cycle that includes flowering and seed production within a single growing season.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or cultivated areas.
