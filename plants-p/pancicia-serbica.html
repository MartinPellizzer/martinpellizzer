Pancicia serbica

Pancicia serbica, commonly known as the Serbian pancicia, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and appear in clusters.

Pancicia serbica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
