Pelargonium albiflorum

Pelargonium albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and lobed leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
