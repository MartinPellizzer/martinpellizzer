Polygala validiflora

Polygala validiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, and the plant produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds with silky appendages.

It is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role and unique morphological features.
