Pithecellobium scandens

Pithecellobium scandens, commonly known as the climbing pithecellobium, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with 5 to 11 leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
