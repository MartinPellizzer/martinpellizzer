Ptelea pinnata

Ptelea pinnata, commonly known as the silky dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, almost lace-like appearance. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers in the spring, which are followed by blue-black berries in the fall. Its bark is smooth and gray, often exfoliating to reveal a mottled pattern.

Ptelea pinnata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
