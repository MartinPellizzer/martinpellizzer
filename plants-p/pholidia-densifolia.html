Pholidia densifolia

Pholidia densifolia, commonly known as the denseleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, the Caribbean, and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a leathery texture, while its flowers bloom in clusters.

Pholidia densifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
