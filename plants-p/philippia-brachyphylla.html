Philippia brachyphylla

Philippia brachyphylla, commonly known as the dwarf philippia, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
