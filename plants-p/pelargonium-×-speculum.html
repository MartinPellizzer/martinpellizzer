Pelargonium × speculum

Pelargonium × speculum, commonly known as the silver-leaf geranium, is a hybrid species of the geranium genus.

It is characterized by its striking silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often with a velvety texture. Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for gardens and container growing.
