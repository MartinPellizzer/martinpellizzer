Pinus balfouriana subsp. aristata

Pinus balfouriana subsp.

aristata, commonly known as the Balfour's pine, is a subspecies native to the western United States. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its long, needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure.

The tree typically grows in high-elevation forests, preferring well-drained soils and cool, moist climates. Its bark is thick and furrowed, providing protection against fire and environmental stress.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining forest biodiversity.
