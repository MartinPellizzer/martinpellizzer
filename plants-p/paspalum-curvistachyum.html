Paspalum curvistachyum

Paspalum curvistachyum, commonly known as the curly-stalked paspalum, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often concealed within the foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy environments.

Paspalum curvistachyum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet habitats.
