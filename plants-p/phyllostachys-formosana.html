Phyllostachys formosana

Phyllostachys formosana, commonly known as the Formosan bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to Taiwan and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green, providing ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It is a clumping bamboo, forming dense stands that can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Phyllostachys formosana is also valued for its ecological benefits, supporting biodiversity and stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
