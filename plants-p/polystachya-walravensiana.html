Polystachya walravensiana

Polystachya walravensiana, commonly known as the Walravens' bamboo orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along riverbanks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it a common sight in its native habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
