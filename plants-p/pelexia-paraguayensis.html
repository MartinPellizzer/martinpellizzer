Pelexia paraguayensis

Pelexia paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay pelexia, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically yellow or white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Pelexia paraguayensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
