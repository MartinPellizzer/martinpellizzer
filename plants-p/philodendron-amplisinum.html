Philodendron amplisinum

Philodendron amplisinum, commonly known as the Mexican marble queen, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a glossy texture. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves often have a marbled pattern, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance.

It prefers bright, indirect light and consistent moisture, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
