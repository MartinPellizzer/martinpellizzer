Polanisia dodecandra subsp. uniglandulosa

Polanisia dodecandra subsp.

uniglandulosa, commonly known as the African butter tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, oval-shaped fruits that contain a single, large, edible seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forest and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its edible seeds, which are rich in oil and nutrients.
