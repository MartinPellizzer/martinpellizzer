Plagiosetum refractum

Plagiosetum refractum, commonly known as the refractive plagiostemon, is a species of grass native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves that often exhibit a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
