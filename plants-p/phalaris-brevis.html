Phalaris brevis

Phalaris brevis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved beakgrass, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains numerous tiny flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Phalaris brevis is generally short-lived and can be found in a variety of ecological settings, including disturbed soils and sandy areas.
