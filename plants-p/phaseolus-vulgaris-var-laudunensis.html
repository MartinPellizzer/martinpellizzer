Phaseolus vulgaris var. laudunensis

Phaseolus vulgaris var. laudunensis, commonly known as the French bean or haricot vert, is a variety of common bean native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated pods that are typically green and contain small, tender beans. The plant is an annual legume that thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being heart-shaped and slightly hairy.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible beans, which are used in a variety of culinary dishes.
