Patagonium resinosum

Patagonium resinosum, commonly known as the resinous Patagonian tree, is a hardy shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces a sticky resin, which has been historically used by indigenous communities for various purposes. It typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited rainfall.

Its slow growth rate makes it a long-lived species in its native habitat.
