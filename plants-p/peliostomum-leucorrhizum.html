Peliostomum leucorrhizum

Peliostomum leucorrhizum, commonly known as the white-rooted water starwort, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, floating leaves that are typically green with a white underside. The plant produces tiny, yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize the sediment in its habitat.
