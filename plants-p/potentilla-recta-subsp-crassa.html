Potentilla recta subsp. crassa

Potentilla recta subsp.

crassa, commonly known as broadleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands, rocky slopes, and disturbed soils.

The plant features upright stems with alternate, pinnately compound leaves that have five to seven leaflets. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, and it produces small, achenes as its fruit.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
