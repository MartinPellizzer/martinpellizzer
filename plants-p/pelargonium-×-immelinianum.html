Pelargonium × immelinianum

Pelargonium × immelinianum, commonly known as the African marigold, is a hybrid geranium species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers and dark green, velvety foliage. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its prolonged blooming period, which extends from spring through early autumn.
