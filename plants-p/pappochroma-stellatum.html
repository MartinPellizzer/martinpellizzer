Pappochroma stellatum

Pappochroma stellatum, commonly known as the star-stemmed pappochroma, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
