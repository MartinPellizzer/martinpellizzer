Poa diaphana

Poa diaphana, commonly known as the ghost grass, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, pale green blades that give it a ghostly appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in loose panicles.

Poa diaphana is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in poor soil conditions.
