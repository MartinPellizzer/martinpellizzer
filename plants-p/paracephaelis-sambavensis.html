Paracephaelis sambavensis

Paracephaelis sambavensis, commonly known as the Sambava ghost orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its unique, delicate appearance with translucent, waxy leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree branches or rocks. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, are often pale green or white, with a subtle fragrance.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
