Petunia helianthemoides

Petunia helianthemoides, commonly known as the rock petunia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has hairy, oval-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. It blooms from late spring to early autumn and is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
