Psoralea badocana var. grandiflora

Psoralea badocana var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered psoralea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodland and grassland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
