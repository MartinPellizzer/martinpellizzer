Persicaria insignis

Persicaria insignis, commonly known as the redroot pigweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive red stems, which become more prominent as the plant matures. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along stream banks.

Persicaria insignis is an annual plant that blooms from late spring to early fall, producing seeds that are dispersed by wind.
