Phalaris coerulescens var. major

Phalaris coerulescens var. major, commonly known as bluegrass or tall bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces spikelets that are typically blue or purple in color, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for landscaping in temperate climates.

This variety is often used in turfgrass mixes due to its durability and aesthetic appeal.
