Pararchidendron pruinosum var. sumbawaense

Pararchidendron pruinosum var. sumbawaense, commonly known as the Sumbawa hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the island of Sumbawa in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
