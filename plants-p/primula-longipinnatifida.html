Primula longipinnatifida

Primula longipinnatifida, commonly known as the long-pinnate primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive spurred shape. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Due to its unique foliage and floral structure, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and horticulture.
