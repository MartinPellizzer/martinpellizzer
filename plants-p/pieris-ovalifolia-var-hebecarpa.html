Pieris ovalifolia var. hebecarpa

Pieris ovalifolia var. hebecarpa, commonly known as the Japanese pieris, is a variant of the broadleaf pieris native to Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that turn bronze in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark blue, waxy berries. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its foliage and floral display contribute to its aesthetic value in woodland gardens.
