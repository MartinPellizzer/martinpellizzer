Phebalium elaeagnifolium

Phebalium elaeagnifolium, commonly known as silver-leafed phebalium, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are simple and opposite, with a leathery texture and a tendency to retain moisture.

Phebalium elaeagnifolium is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
