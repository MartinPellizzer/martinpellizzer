Pleurothallis blumenavii

Pleurothallis blumenavii, commonly known as the Blumenau orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It is a small epiphytic orchid, typically growing on tree branches or rocks in humid environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is part of the Pleurothallis genus, which includes over 1,000 species.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural range.
