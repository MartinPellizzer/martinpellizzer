Paepalanthus helminthorrhizus

Paepalanthus helminthorrhizus, commonly known as the zigzag cactus, is a species of flowering plant in the cacti family, Cactaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in Brazil and Paraguay, where it thrives in dry, rocky environments. The plant is characterized by its unique, spiral arrangement of stems and spiny, segmented structures. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces small, fleshy fruits.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species, requiring conservation efforts to protect its natural environment.
