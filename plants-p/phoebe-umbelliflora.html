Phoebe umbelliflora

Phoebe umbelliflora, commonly known as the umbrella phoebe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant features opposite, elliptic leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and are usually white or pale green.

It plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, particularly insects and birds.
