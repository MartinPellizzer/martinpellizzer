Pterocarpus officinalis

Pterocarpus officinalis, commonly known as Indian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
