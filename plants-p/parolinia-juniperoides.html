Parolinia juniperoides

Parolinia juniperoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf juniper, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in clusters. The plant produces blue or black berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Parolinia juniperoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
