Pentadactylon angustifolium

Pentadactylon angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved starwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in grassland habitats. Its narrow, linear leaves are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring.

It is relatively uncommon and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
