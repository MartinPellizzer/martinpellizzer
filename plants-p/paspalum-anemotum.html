Paspalum anemotum

Paspalum anemotum, commonly known as the windgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often rolled at the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as prairies and disturbed sites.

Paspalum anemotum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone environments.
