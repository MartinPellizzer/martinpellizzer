Polygonum diclinum

Polygonum diclinum, commonly known as the male and female knotweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and green inflorescences, which consist of separate male and female flowers on different stems. The plant is a member of the Polygonaceae family, known for its distinctive bracts and small flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Polygonum diclinum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
