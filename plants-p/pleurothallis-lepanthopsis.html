Pleurothallis lepanthopsis

Pleurothallis lepanthopsis, commonly known as the Lepanthopsis orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in countries like Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in the understory of rainforests.

Its botanical features include pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients, and long, slender leaves that help in photosynthesis in low-light conditions.
