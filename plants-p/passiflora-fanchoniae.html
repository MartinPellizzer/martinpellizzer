Passiflora fanchoniae

Passiflora fanchoniae, commonly known as the Fanchonia passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing a sweet, pulpy flesh. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and dark green upper surface.

Passiflora fanchoniae is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique floral display and is also valued in traditional medicine.
