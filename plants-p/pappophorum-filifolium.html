Pappophorum filifolium

Pappophorum filifolium, commonly known as the slender grass or filifolium grass, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its fine, thread-like leaves.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is typically found in temperate regions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of grass species. Its lightweight, wind-dispersed seeds are enclosed in a pappus, aiding in its propagation.

Pappophorum filifolium is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in disturbed environments.
