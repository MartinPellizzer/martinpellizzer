Polygala subvirgata

Polygala subvirgata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insect species.
