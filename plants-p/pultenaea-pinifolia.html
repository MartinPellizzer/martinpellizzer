Pultenaea pinifolia

Pultenaea pinifolia, commonly known as the needle-leaf pultenaea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is found in regions such as Western Australia and South Australia. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, bright yellow flowers appear in late winter to early spring, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

Pultenaea pinifolia is well adapted to arid conditions and is often used in native gardens for its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
