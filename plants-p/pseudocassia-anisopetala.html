Pseudocassia anisopetala

Pseudocassia anisopetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved golden glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
