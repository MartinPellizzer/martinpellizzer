Protea vestita

Protea vestita, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pale pink flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, often covered in a fine silvery-white pubescence, giving it its common name.

Protea vestita is not only ornamental but also plays a role in supporting local pollinators and ecosystems.
