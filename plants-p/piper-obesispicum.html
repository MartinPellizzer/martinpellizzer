Piper obesispicum

Piper obesispicum, commonly known as the fat-leaved pepper or the waxy pepper, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species.

Due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
