Penstemon crassifolius

Penstemon crassifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, broad leaves and produces showy spikes of tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, or purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. It is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

The species is also noted for its hardy nature and adaptability to various garden conditions.
