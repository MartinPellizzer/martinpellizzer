Polygala szovitsiana

Polygala szovitsiana, commonly known as the Szovits' milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, often pale or yellowish flowers are arranged in clusters, giving it a unique appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
