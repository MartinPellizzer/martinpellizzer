Peristeria leucoxantha

Peristeria leucoxantha, commonly known as the white-flowered peristeria, is a striking member of the Araceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its large, showy white bracts that enclose the small, yellowish flowers. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings.

Peristeria leucoxantha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
