Plantago eriopoda

Plantago eriopoda, commonly known as velvetleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically borne on tall, slender stalks. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The leaves are frequently used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
