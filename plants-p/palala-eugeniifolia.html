Palala eugeniifolia

Palala eugeniifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf palala, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
