Potentilla valderia var. lupinoides

Potentilla valderia var. lupinoides, commonly known as the lupine-like potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has compound leaves with serrated margins and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. It is adapted to harsh conditions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
