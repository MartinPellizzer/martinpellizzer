Papaver nudicaule subsp. baicalense

Papaver nudicaule subsp.

baicalense, commonly known as the Baical poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Far East. It is characterized by its delicate, nodding flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant features a slender, hairy stem and palmate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
