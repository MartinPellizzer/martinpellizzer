Pseudosempervivum karsianum

Pseudosempervivum karsianum, commonly known as the Karsianum stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it an ideal plant for xeriscaping and container gardening.
