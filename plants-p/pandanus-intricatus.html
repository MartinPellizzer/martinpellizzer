Pandanus intricatus

Pandanus intricatus, commonly known as the intricate pandanus, is a species of palm native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, typical of many palm species. Its fibrous leaves are used in traditional crafts and construction, while the fruit is edible and used in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in coastal and wetland environments, demonstrating adaptability to humid and saline conditions.
