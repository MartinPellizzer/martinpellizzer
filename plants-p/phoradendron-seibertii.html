Phoradendron seibertii

Phoradendron seibertii, commonly known as the mistletoe of the Caribbean, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the tropical regions of the Americas.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis using its small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that are pollinated by insects, followed by berries that are dispersed by birds. Its life cycle involves a complex relationship with its host, contributing to the ecological dynamics of forest ecosystems.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can weaken its host tree if left unchecked.
