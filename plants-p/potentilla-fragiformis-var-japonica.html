Potentilla fragiformis var. japonica

Potentilla fragiformis var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, bright yellow flowers. The leaves are typically compound, with five leaflets that are oval and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
