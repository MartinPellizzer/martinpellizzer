Psidium guajava var. minor

Psidium guajava var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf guava, is a compact variant of the common guava.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of the Americas. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that give way to round, fleshy fruits with a sweet, aromatic pulp. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a glossy surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its edible fruit and aesthetic qualities.
