Passiflora securiclata

Passiflora securiclata, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture.

Passiflora securiclata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral displays.
