Patagonium elongatum

Patagonium elongatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed Patagonian herb, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated stems that can reach up to two meters in height. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Patagonium elongatum plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying drought-resistant flora.
