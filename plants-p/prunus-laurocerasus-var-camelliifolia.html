Prunus laurocerasus var. camelliifolia

Prunus laurocerasus var. camelliifolia, commonly known as the Camellialeaf laurel, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves that are often described as resembling those of camellia plants, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by dark purple berries. It is valued for its dense foliage and ornamental appeal, making it a popular choice for hedging and landscape design.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
