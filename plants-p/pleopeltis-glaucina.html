Pleopeltis glaucina

Pleopeltis glaucina, commonly known as the glaucous pleopeltis, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows on shaded, moist rock surfaces or in forest understories. Its leaves are usually covered in a waxy, bluish-gray coating, which helps reduce water loss.
