Phaseolus rigidus

Phaseolus rigidus, commonly known as the rigid bean or prickly bush bean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy.

Phaseolus rigidus is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines.
