Pelargonium × pseudozonale

Pelargonium × pseudozonale, commonly known as the zonal geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically has rounded, velvety leaves with distinct zonation patterns, which give it its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and containers.

Pelargonium × pseudozonale is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
