Pycnocephalum angustifolium

Pycnocephalum angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pycnocephalum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and bloom during the summer months.

Pycnocephalum angustifolium is known for its resilience and is often found in disturbed or dry habitats.
