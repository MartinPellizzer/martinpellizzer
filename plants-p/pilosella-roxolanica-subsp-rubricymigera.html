Pilosella roxolanica subsp. rubricymigera

Pilosella roxolanica subsp.

rubricymigera, commonly known as the red-stemmed pearly everlasting, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in arid or semi-arid environments. It is native to parts of Central Asia and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is generally low maintenance.
