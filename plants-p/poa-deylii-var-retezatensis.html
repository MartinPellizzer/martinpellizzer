Poa deylii var. retezatensis

Poa deylii var. retezatensis, commonly known as the Retezat meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of the Carpathian Basin.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine meadows. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in cool, temperate climates and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
