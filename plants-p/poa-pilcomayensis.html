Poa pilcomayensis

Poa pilcomayensis, commonly known as the Pilcomayo grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often silver-haired leaves. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is adapted to various soil types, including those that are dry or sandy. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, two-rowed flowers.

Poa pilcomayensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
