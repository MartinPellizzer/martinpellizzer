Paronychia argentea var. velata

Paronychia argentea var. velata, commonly known as silverleaf sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with densely packed, silver-gray foliage that reflects sunlight, aiding in heat reduction. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle ornamental value. Its succulent leaves are adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for rocky or sandy soils.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
