Pavonia hastata var. consobrina

Pavonia hastata var. consobrina, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, pink to purple flower heads that resemble coneflowers, with a central disk surrounded by petal-like bracts. The plant has compound, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and native plant landscapes.
