Polygonum newberryi var. glabrum

Polygonum newberryi var. glabrum, commonly known as Newberry's smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glabrous, giving it its varietal name. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and appear in late summer to early fall.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
