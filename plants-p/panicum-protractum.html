Panicum protractum

Panicum protractum, commonly known as protracted panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, poorly drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are arranged in panicles, giving it its characteristic appearance. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
