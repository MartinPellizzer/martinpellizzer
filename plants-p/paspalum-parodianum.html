Paspalum parodianum

Paspalum parodianum, commonly known as Parod's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairless blades that often have a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas and along riverbanks.

Paspalum parodianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
