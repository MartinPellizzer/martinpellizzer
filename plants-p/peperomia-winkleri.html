Peperomia winkleri

Peperomia winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and silver, giving it an ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of green or red, typically emerging from the leaf axils. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its slow growth and ease of care, it is well-suited for container cultivation and can be propagated through stem cuttings.
