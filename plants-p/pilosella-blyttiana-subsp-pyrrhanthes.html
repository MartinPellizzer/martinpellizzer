Pilosella blyttiana subsp. pyrrhanthes

Pilosella blyttiana subsp.

pyrrhanthes, commonly known as the red-haired pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and woolly, hairy stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reddish-brown bracts and small, white to pale yellow flowers. It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
