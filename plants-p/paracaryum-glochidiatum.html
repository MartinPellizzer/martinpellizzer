Paracaryum glochidiatum

Paracaryum glochidiatum, commonly known as the spiny burrgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, woody stems and spiny, glochidiate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by fruiting bodies that contain hard, spiny seeds. Its spines are adapted for protection against herbivores and may also aid in seed dispersal.

Paracaryum glochidiatum is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
