Primula griffithii

Primula griffithii, commonly known as the Chinese primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, white, and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
