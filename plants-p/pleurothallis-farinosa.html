Pleurothallis farinosa

Pleurothallis farinosa, commonly known as the powdered pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil.

Due to its unique appearance and specific care requirements, it is a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
