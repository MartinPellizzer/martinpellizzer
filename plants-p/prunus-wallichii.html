Prunus wallichii

Prunus wallichii, commonly known as the Kashmir cherry or Himalayan cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by small, round fruits that resemble cherries. Prunus wallichii is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, growing at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some parts of Asia.
