Papaver chakassicum

Papaver chakassicum, commonly known as the Kashmir poppy, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange petals and a prominent central stigma, which are typical features of the Papaver genus. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
