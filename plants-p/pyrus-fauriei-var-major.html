Pyrus fauriei var. major

Pyrus fauriei var. major, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray-brown bark and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruit that ripens in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and fruit production.
