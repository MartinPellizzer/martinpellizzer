Pedilonum auroroseum

Pedilonum auroroseum, commonly known as the dawn-bell flower, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its elongated, bell-shaped petals that display a gradient of colors from deep violet to pale yellow, resembling the hues of dawn. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring, often in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand the cool, arid conditions of its habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, Pedilonum auroroseum is considered a valuable species in botanical research and conservation efforts.
