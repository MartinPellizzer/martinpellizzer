Phlomis scariosa

Phlomis scariosa, commonly known as the scarious phlomis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in sunny environments. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in grayish-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The flowers are usually pale purple to pink, appearing in dense spikes during the summer months.
