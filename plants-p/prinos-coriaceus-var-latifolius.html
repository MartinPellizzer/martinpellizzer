Prinos coriaceus var. latifolius

Prinos coriaceus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf prinos, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features woody stems covered in a thick, leathery bark that provides protection against drought and fire. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in late spring. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a glossy surface that reflects sunlight.

This species is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a resilient plant in arid environments.
