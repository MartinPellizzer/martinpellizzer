Piptochaetium stipoides var. verruculosum

Piptochaetium stipoides var. verruculosum, commonly known as the verruculose sand grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered with a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous pale green to brownish florets. It is adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate drought conditions, making it a hardy species in harsh environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion in its native habitat.
