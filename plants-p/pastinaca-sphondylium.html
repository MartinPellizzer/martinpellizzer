Pastinaca sphondylium

Pastinaca sphondylium, commonly known as the common burdock.

It is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its rough, prickly stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces purple or pink flowers in clusters, followed by spiny, bur-like fruits that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in waste areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and thistles.
