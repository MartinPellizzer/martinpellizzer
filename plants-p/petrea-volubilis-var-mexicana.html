Petrea volubilis var. mexicana

Petrea volubilis var. mexicana, commonly known as Mexican petrea, is a climbing shrub native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Its vigorous growth habit makes it suitable for use as a ground cover or in vertical gardening applications.
