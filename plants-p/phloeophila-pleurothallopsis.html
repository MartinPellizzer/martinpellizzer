Phloeophila pleurothallopsis

Phloeophila pleurothallopsis, commonly known as the pleurothallis orchid, is a flowering plant native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit, often growing on trees or rocks. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
