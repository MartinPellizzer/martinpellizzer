Pseudoarabidopsis

Pseudoarabidopsis, commonly known as the false Arabidopsis, is a genus of flowering plants in the mustard family (Brassicaceae).

It is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits, typical of the Brassicaceae family. The plants typically grow in dry, rocky habitats and are often found in arid regions. Pseudoarabidopsis species are known for their ability to tolerate extreme environmental conditions, making them of interest in ecological and genetic studies.

Their genome size and structure are also studied for comparative purposes with Arabidopsis thaliana.
