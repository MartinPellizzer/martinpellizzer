Pyrus lobata

Pyrus lobata, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and oval, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by round, yellowish fruits. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.

Pyrus lobata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
