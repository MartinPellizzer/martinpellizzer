Pandanus angolensis f. cacondensis

Pandanus angolensis f. cacondensis, commonly known as the African screw pine, is a variant of the screw pine species native to parts of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its spirally arranged leaves and distinctive, often fragrant, inflorescences. The plant thrives in coastal and mangrove environments, adapting well to saline and humid conditions. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and for thatching, while its roots are sometimes employed in local medicine.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and supporting local biodiversity.
