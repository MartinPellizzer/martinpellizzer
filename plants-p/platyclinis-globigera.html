Platyclinis globigera

Platyclinis globigera, commonly known as the globigera daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Platyclinis globigera is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
