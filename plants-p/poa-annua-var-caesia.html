Poa annua var. caesia

Poa annua var. caesia, commonly known as blue grass, is a variant of the annual bluegrass species.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green blades and tufted growth habit. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lawns and disturbed areas. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to purple florets.

It is a cool-season grass that is relatively easy to establish but can be invasive in some environments.
