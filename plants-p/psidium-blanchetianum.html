Psidium blanchetianum

Psidium blanchetianum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, sweet flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for both landscaping and culinary use.
