Patagonium vallis-pulchrae

Patagonium vallis-pulchrae, commonly known as the "Beautiful Valley Plant," is a rare flowering species native to the high-altitude regions of southern South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, which helps reduce water loss.

The flowers bloom in late spring, producing vibrant yellow petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
