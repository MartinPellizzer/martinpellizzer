Pedatyphonium omeiense

Pedatyphonium omeiense, commonly known as the Omeiense Bristle Fern, is a rare and unique fern species native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely divided fronds that resemble a delicate lace, with a pale green hue and a soft, velvety texture. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in mountainous regions with high humidity. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing clusters, are positioned on the underside of its leaves, a common feature among ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Pedatyphonium omeiense is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
