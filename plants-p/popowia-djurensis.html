Popowia djurensis

Popowia djurensis, commonly known as the Desert Honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its deep root system and thick, waxy leaves. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Popowia djurensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for small animals.
