Parnassia chinensis var. sechuanensis

Parnassia chinensis var. sechuanensis, commonly known as the Chinese mountain buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive white, cup-shaped flowers with a yellow center. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountain streams. Its leaves are palmately divided and have a slightly hairy surface.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
