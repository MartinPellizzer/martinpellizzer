Prunus persica f. aganopersica

Prunus persica f. aganopersica, commonly known as the Persian peach or aganopersica peach, is a botanical variant of the peach tree characterized by its unique floral and fruiting traits.

This form exhibits a more compact growth habit compared to typical peaches, with flowers that are often more showy and fragrant. The fruits are generally smaller and have a distinct, sweeter flavor profile. It is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various climates.

The plant's genetic makeup contributes to its resilience and makes it a valuable subject in plant breeding programs.
