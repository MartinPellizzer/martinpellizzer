Paracyclea densiflora

Paracyclea densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like structures, which are typically pollinated by insects. Paracyclea densiflora is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to thrive.

Due to its slow growth and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
