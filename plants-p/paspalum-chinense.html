Paspalum chinense

Paspalum chinense, commonly known as Chinese paspalum, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in moist or wetland habitats, thriving in areas with high humidity.

Paspalum chinense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
