Parodia maldonadensis

Parodia maldonadensis, commonly known as the Maldonado's pincushion cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically short-lived but bright. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and compact size, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container cultivation.
