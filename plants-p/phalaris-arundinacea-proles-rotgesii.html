Phalaris arundinacea proles rotgesii

Phalaris arundinacea proles rotgesii, commonly known as the reed canary grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, tufted inflorescences that produce small, spike-like flowers. The plant is adapted to wet or marshy environments and is often found in riparian zones and wetlands. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread aggressively, making it both a valuable forage crop and a potential invasive species.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that distinguishes it from other grasses.
