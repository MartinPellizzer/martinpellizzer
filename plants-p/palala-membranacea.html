Palala membranacea

Palala membranacea, commonly known as the paperleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, papery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a unique texture. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for dry environments.

Palala membranacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to add a touch of elegance to rocky or sandy gardens.
