Paphiopedilum × glanzii

Paphiopedilum × glanzii, commonly known as the hybrid velvet lily, is a hybrid orchid resulting from the cross between Paphiopedilum niveum and Paphiopedilum suaveolens.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green to brownish leaves and its striking, showy flowers with a unique lip structure. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers, which can be white, pink, or red, often have a waxy texture and emit a faint fragrance.

This hybrid is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its beauty and resilience in various horticultural settings.
