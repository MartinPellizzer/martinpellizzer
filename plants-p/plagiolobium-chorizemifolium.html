Plagiolobium chorizemifolium

Plagiolobium chorizemifolium, commonly known as the coastal lantana, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salinity and wind exposure.

Plagiolobium chorizemifolium is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience.
