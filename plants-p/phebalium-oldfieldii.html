Phebalium oldfieldii

Phebalium oldfieldii, commonly known as Oldfield's hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves and produces small, urn-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

Phebalium oldfieldii is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
