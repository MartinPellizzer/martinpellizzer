Pseudolysimachion Spicatum Subsp. Transcaucasicum

Pseudolysimachion spicatum subsp. transcaucasicum is a rare and fascinating plant native to the mountainous regions of the Transcaucasus. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, known for its aromatic and often edible members. This subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral structure and medicinal properties. It thrives in rocky and semi-arid environments, showcasing remarkable adaptability. Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is now considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
