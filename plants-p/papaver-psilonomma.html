Papaver psilonomma

Papaver psilonomma, commonly known as the slender poppy, is a species of flowering plant in the family Papaveraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant is characterized by its slender stem and showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Papaver psilonomma is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
