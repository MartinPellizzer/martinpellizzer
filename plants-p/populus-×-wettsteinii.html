Populus × wettsteinii

Populus × wettsteinii, commonly known as the Wettstein's poplar, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Populus balsamifera and Populus trichocarpa.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and wetlands.

This species is valued for its adaptability and is used in ecological restoration projects.
