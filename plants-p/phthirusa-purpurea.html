Phthirusa purpurea

Phthirusa purpurea, commonly known as the purple flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, purple to pink flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Phthirusa purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
