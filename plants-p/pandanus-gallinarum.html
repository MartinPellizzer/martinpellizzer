Pandanus gallinarum

Pandanus gallinarum, commonly known as the chicken pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible fruits, which are consumed by both humans and animals. Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize coastal soils, making it useful for erosion control.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appearance and cultural significance in indigenous communities.
