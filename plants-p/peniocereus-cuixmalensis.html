Peniocereus cuixmalensis

Peniocereus cuixmalensis, commonly known as the Cuixmalense cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, spineless cactus that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers during the night, which are often fragrant. It has a short, cylindrical stem and is adapted to arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
