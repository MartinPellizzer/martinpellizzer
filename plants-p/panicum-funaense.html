Panicum funaense

Panicum funaense, commonly known as the African grass or funa grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces fluffy, light brown panicles that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for ornamental and erosion control purposes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping and as a forage crop in certain regions.
