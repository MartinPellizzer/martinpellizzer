Piper umbrosum

Piper umbrosum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piper genus within the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its dark, peppery fruits. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a dark purple or black color, which are used as a spice. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated edge, while its stems are woody and climbing.

The botanical features of Piper umbrosum make it well adapted to humid, shaded environments where it thrives.
