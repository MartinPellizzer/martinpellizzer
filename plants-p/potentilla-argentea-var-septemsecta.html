Potentilla argentea var. septemsecta

Potentilla argentea var. septemsecta, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, hairy leaves and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its stems are often branched and covered with fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
