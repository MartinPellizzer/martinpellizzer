Passiflora glaucophylla

Passiflora glaucophylla, commonly known as the blue-leaf passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a waxy, bluish-green appearance. This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.

It produces edible berries that are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
