Panicum ferrugineum

Panicum ferrugineum, commonly known as crimson grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stalks and vibrant red to purple inflorescences that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight pubescence, while the seeds are small and wind-dispersed.

Panicum ferrugineum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
