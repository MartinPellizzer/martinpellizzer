Passiflora myriadenia

Passiflora myriadenia, commonly known as the giant passionflower, is a striking climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that feature intricate patterns of red, yellow, and blue, with prominent filaments and a crown of stamens. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are highly sought after for their sweet, tropical flavor. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its lush, tropical appearance.

Passiflora myriadenia is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and other wildlife.
