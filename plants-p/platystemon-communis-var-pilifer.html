Platystemon communis var. pilifer

Platystemon communis var. pilifer, commonly known as the hairy platystemon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and also bear a hairy texture, contributing to its overall rugged look. It produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
