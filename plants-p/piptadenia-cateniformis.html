Piptadenia cateniformis

Piptadenia cateniformis, commonly known as the chain vine or yellow bell, is a tropical leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, vine-like growth habit and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are compound and trifoliate, with a feathery appearance.

Piptadenia cateniformis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
