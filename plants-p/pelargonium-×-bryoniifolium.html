Pelargonium × bryoniifolium

Pelargonium × bryoniifolium, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, velvety leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming from spring to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Its ornamental value and ease of care contribute to its widespread cultivation in gardens and landscapes.
