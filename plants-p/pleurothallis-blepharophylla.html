Pleurothallis blepharophylla

Pleurothallis blepharophylla, commonly known as the "eyelid orchid," is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale green with a subtle fragrance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

Pleurothallis blepharophylla is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
