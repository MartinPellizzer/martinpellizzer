Prunus leveilleana f. semiplena

Prunus leveilleana f. semiplena, commonly known as the partially double-flowered cherry, is a botanical variant of the Prunus leveilleana species.

It is characterized by its semi-double flowers, which have a partially developed corolla with a central cluster of stamens. The plant typically produces pink to white flowers in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun.

Its botanical interest lies in its unique floral structure and potential for horticultural use in ornamental landscaping.
