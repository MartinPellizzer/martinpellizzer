Petalogyne cassioides

Petalogyne cassioides, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Its reproductive structures are adapted for wind pollination, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Petalogyne cassioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in aquatic environments.
