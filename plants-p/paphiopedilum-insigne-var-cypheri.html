Paphiopedilum insigne var. cypheri

Paphiopedilum insigne var. cypheri, commonly known as the Cypher's lady's slipper, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety petals and a distinctive pouch-shaped lip that serves as a specialized structure for attracting pollinators. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety coating. Its flowers, which bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple, are highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to their rarity and beauty.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and requires careful conservation efforts.
