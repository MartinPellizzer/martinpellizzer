Palala hypargyraea

Palala hypargyraea, commonly known as the golden palala, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense, showy inflorescences. The plant has a slender, woody stem with smooth, dark green leaves that are elongated and lance-shaped. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
