Peperomia subdichotoma

Peperomia subdichotoma, commonly known as the silver mitten plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically silver or gray-green in color, with a velvety texture. The plant has a branching habit, with stems that often split into two at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia subdichotoma is relatively low-maintenance, requiring minimal water and moderate temperatures.
