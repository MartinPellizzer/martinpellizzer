Psoralea balsamica

Psoralea balsamica, commonly known as the balsam psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous structure and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

The species is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
