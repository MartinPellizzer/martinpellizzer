Polygonum fusco-ochreatum

Polygonum fusco-ochreatum, commonly known as the dark ochre knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes species known for their distinctive bracts and edible rhubarb-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a reddish or brownish tinge, and it produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters.

The species is characterized by its robust root system and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
