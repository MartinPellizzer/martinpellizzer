Polypodium toppingii

Polypodium toppingii, commonly known as the Topping's fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to the Appalachian region of the United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that typically grow in a compact, rosette-like formation. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a slightly serrated margin.

Polypodium toppingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
