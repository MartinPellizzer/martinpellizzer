Potentilla uniflora var. ampla

Potentilla uniflora var. ampla, commonly known as the broadleaf potentilla, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, upright growth. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
