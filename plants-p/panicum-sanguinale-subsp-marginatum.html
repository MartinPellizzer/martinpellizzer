Panicum sanguinale subsp. marginatum

Panicum sanguinale subsp.

marginatum, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a distinct reddish hue along the margins.

The plant produces fluffy, pinkish to reddish inflorescences that are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping and erosion control.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and aesthetic purposes in suitable climates.
