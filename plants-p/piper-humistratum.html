Piper humistratum

Piper humistratum, commonly known as the lowland pepper or swamp pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of flowering plants, many of which are used for their aromatic properties. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that often twine around other vegetation. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough, textured surface, while the flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

The fruit, which is a small, fleshy berry, contains the seeds and is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
