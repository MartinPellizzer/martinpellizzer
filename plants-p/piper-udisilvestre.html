Piper udisilvestre

Piper udisilvestre, commonly known as the "silvestre pepper," is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the humid forests of Central and South America, particularly in regions of Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with dark green, ovate leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruits are small berries that turn from green to red as they mature, and they are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of the understory vegetation in tropical rainforests.
