Passiflora suberosa var. hederacea

Passiflora suberosa var. hederacea, commonly known as the creeping passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers that resemble a crown, with intricate patterns of filaments and sepals. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are often used in jams and beverages. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.
