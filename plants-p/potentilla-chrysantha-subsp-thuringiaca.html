Potentilla chrysantha subsp. thuringiaca

Potentilla chrysantha subsp.

thuringiaca, commonly known as the Thuringian cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, aggregate fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
