Piper daniel-gonzalezii var. daniel-gonzalezii

Piper daniel-gonzalezii var. daniel-gonzalezii, commonly known as the Daniel-Gonzalezii pepper plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper and chili peppers. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that often require support. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate in shape, and have a glossy green surface.

The flowers are small and greenish-white, and the fruit, which is a berry, is used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
