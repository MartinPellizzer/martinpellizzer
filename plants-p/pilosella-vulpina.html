Pilosella vulpina

Pilosella vulpina, commonly known as the fox's tail or wolf's tail, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in compact clusters. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Pilosella vulpina is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.
