Potentilla vorobievii

Potentilla vorobievii, commonly known as the Vorobievii cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and adaptability.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
