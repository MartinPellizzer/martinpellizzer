Paederia argentifolia

Paederia argentifolia, commonly known as silverleaf piper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature.

Paederia argentifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
