Picea grandis

Picea grandis, commonly known as the grand fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The needles are flat, dark green, and arranged in spirals along the shoots. Its cones are slender and typically hang downward, containing two to three seeds.

The tree prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
