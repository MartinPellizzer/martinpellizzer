Pithecellobium mangense

Pithecellobium mangense, commonly known as the mangrove pithecellobium, is a tropical shrub native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, compound leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to saline environments, making it a common species in mangrove ecosystems. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by water.

This species plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and supporting local biodiversity.
