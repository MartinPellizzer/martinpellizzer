Pyrus caucasi

Pyrus caucasi, commonly known as the Caucasian pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is typically small, sweet, and edible, with a skin that turns yellow or brown when ripe. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
