Pycreus stramineus var. decumbens

Pycreus stramineus var. decumbens, commonly known as the decumbent pycreus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Asia.

It features slender, creeping stems that often lie close to the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a pale green coloration.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.
