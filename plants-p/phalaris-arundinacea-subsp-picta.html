Phalaris arundinacea subsp. picta

Phalaris arundinacea subsp.

picta, commonly known as the painted reed, is a grass-like plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, variegated foliage that features green, white, and sometimes pinkish streaks.

The plant produces tall, slender inflorescences with small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, ditches, and along waterways.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a striking, low-maintenance plant.
