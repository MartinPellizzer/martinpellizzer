Poa glauca var. montana

Poa glauca var. montana, commonly known as mountain bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky slopes and meadows. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue or silver flowers, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It is well adapted to cold climates and can thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy choice for landscaping in alpine environments.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and ability to withstand harsh weather conditions.
