Pseudobrachiaria

Pseudobrachiaria, commonly known as false bluestem, is a genus of grasses native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

These plants are characterized by their upright growth habit and typically have narrow, grass-like leaves. Many species within the genus are drought-tolerant, making them well-suited for arid or semi-arid environments. They often serve as important components of native grasslands and are sometimes used in restoration ecology.

The flowers are usually small and arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family.
