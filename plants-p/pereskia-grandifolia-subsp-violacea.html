Pereskia grandifolia subsp. violacea

Pereskia grandifolia subsp.

violacea, commonly known as the violet-flowered cactus, is a succulent plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are covered in short, pale green spines.

The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in the spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its leaves are typically small and may be reduced or absent in mature plants.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
