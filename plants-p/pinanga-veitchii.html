Pinanga veitchii

Pinanga veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's areca, is a species of palm native to the Philippines.

It is a medium-sized tree that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, characterized by its slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of many palm species. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a deep green color and a smooth texture.

Pinanga veitchii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
