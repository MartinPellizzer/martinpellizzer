Pollia hasskarlii

Pollia hasskarlii, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, hood-like floral structure that protects the reproductive organs. The plant produces vibrant blue or violet flowers, which are notable for their intense coloration due to specialized pigments called anthocyanins. Pollia hasskarlii is an epiphytic orchid, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees without deriving nutrients from them.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
