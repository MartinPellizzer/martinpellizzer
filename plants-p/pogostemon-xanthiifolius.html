Pogostemon xanthiifolius

Pogostemon xanthiifolius, commonly known as the golden-leafed pogostemon, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are bright yellow to green in color, often with a metallic sheen. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically ranging from white to pale pink. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its aromatic leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb in some cultures.
