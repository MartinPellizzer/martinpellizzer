Phagnalon scalarum

Phagnalon scalarum, commonly known as the scalycup grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, wiry stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowering heads are borne in compact, spike-like clusters, producing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Phagnalon scalarum is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions and is often found in disturbed soils.
