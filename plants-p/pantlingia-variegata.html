Pantlingia variegata

Pantlingia variegata, commonly known as the variegated pantlingia, is a striking perennial plant native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that display a unique variegation of green, white, and gold patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and purple during the spring and summer months. It thrives in partial to full sunlight and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
