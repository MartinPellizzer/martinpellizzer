Padus napaulensis

Padus napaulensis, commonly known as the Nepal maple, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by winged seeds. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils at moderate elevations.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
