Pleurothallis quisqueyana

Pleurothallis quisqueyana, commonly known as the Ecuadorian orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with a distinctive lip petal. It thrives in high humidity and partial shade, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
