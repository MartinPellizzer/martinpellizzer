Phyteuma orbiculare subsp. tenerum

Phyteuma orbiculare subsp.

tenerum, commonly known as the softleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in sunny to partially shaded environments.

The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a distinctive orange or red throat. Its leaves are broadly ovate and have a soft, velvety texture, contributing to its common name.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
