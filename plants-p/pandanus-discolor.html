Pandanus discolor

Pandanus discolor, commonly known as the white pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and cooking. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to saline environments. Its inflorescence consists of small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in a spadix, which is surrounded by a leaf-like bract.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appearance and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
