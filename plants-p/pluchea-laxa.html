Pluchea laxa

Pluchea laxa, commonly known as slenderleaf groundsel, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters.

Pluchea laxa is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
