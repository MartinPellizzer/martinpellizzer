Phaius × sedenianus

Phaius × sedenianus, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Phaius tankervillii and Phaius calvus.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its large, showy, pale yellow to greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive orange or reddish lip. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as marshes and swampy areas, thriving in humid and cool climates. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies.

The botanical features of Phaius × sedenianus make it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.
