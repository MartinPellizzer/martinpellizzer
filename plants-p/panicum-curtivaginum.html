Panicum curtivaginum

Panicum curtivaginum, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like with a smooth texture. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of spikelets, which are small clusters of flowers.

This species plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
