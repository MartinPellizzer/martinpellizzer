Pulsatilla integrifolia

Pulsatilla integrifolia, commonly known as the pasque flower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, crown-like inflorescence composed of numerous small, pale yellow to white petals. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. It has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and its flowers bloom in early spring.

Pulsatilla integrifolia is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, also known as the buttercup family.
