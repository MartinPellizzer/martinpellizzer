Pyrus rufoferruginea var. aromatica

Pyrus rufoferruginea var. aromatica, commonly known as the fragrant pear, is a variant of the rust-red pear species native to China.

It is characterized by its aromatic foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge and a distinctive reddish-brown hue on the underside. The fruit is small, usually less than 2 inches in diameter, with a thin, edible skin and a sweet, juicy pulp.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild, fragrant scent.
