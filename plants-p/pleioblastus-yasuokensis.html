Pleioblastus yasuokensis

Pleioblastus yasuokensis, commonly known as the Japanese silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its narrow, linear leaves are arranged in a dense, tufted form, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring.

Pleioblastus yasuokensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
