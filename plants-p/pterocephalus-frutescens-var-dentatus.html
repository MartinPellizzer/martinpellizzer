Pterocephalus frutescens var. dentatus

Pterocephalus frutescens var. dentatus, commonly known as the toothed pterocephalus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its branched, shrub-like growth habit and hairy stems that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are typically narrow and have serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
