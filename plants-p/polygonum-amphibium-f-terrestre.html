Polygonum amphibium f. terrestre

Polygonum amphibium f. terrestre, commonly known as the terrestrial common knotgrass, is a variant of the common knotgrass species.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant typically features alternate, lanceolate leaves with a hairy texture. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikes, which are often obscured by the foliage.

This form is adapted to moist, terrestrial environments and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.
