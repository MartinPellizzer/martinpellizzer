Piper banksii

Piper banksii, commonly known as the Banks' pepper, is a flowering plant native to the wet tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that often trail along the forest floor. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and usually have a rough texture with a distinctive scent.

The small greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes and are followed by small, three-lobed fruits.
