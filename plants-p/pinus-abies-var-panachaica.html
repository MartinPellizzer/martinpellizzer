Pinus abies var. panachaica

Pinus abies var. panachaica, commonly known as the Panachai pine, is a variant of the European silver fir.

It is characterized by its unique needle morphology and cone structure, which distinguish it from other subspecies. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in the Tien Shan and Pamir Mountains. Its foliage is typically dark green, with a slightly bluish tinge, and it produces cones that are often larger than those of the standard species.

The plant exhibits adaptations to high-altitude environments, including dense foliage and a compact growth form.
