Phoradendron cheirocarpum

Phoradendron cheirocarpum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
