Polanisia tenuifolia

Polanisia tenuifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf polanisia, is a herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical structure makes it a subject of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
