Protea baumii

Protea baumii, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of flowers, with a central cone-like structure surrounded by petal-like bracts. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that are typically clustered around the central axis. Native to the Western Cape region of South Africa, it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun.

Protea baumii is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice in gardens and floral arrangements.
