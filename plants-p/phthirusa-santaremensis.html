Phthirusa santaremensis

Phthirusa santaremensis, commonly known as the Santarem papyrus, is a unique aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Brazil.

It is characterized by its broad, paddle-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, aiding in photosynthesis. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize the sediment in its aquatic habitat. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color.

This species plays an important role in maintaining the ecological balance of its wetland environment.
