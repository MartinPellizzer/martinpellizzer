Poa caucasica

Poa caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reclining or lying flat on the ground. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a smooth texture.

The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.
