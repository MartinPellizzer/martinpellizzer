Pharus urceolatus

Pharus urceolatus, commonly known as the bottlebrush plant, is a unique species belonging to the family Myrtaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflorescences, which resemble a bottlebrush due to their dense, cylindrical clusters of small, red flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Pharus urceolatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
