Pulsatilla violacea var. georgica

Pulsatilla violacea var. georgica, commonly known as the Georgian pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, stout stem with basal leaves that are palmately lobed. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
