Polygala spicata

Polygala spicata, commonly known as the spike polygala, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, spiky inflorescences that resemble small spikes or cones. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white, with five petals, and it produces small, bean-like seed pods.

Polygala spicata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to pollinators.
