Pawia hippocastanum

Pawia hippocastanum, commonly known as the horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets and its showy, white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces spiny capsules containing shiny, chestnut-like seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also known for its medicinal uses.

The bark and leaves contain compounds that have been studied for their potential health benefits.
