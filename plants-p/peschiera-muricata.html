Peschiera muricata

Peschiera muricata, commonly known as the roughleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Peschiera muricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
