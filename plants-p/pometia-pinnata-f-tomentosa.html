Pometia pinnata f. tomentosa

Pometia pinnata f. tomentosa, commonly known as the tomentose pometia, is a variant of the pometia tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a prominent midrib. This plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural study and ecological research.
