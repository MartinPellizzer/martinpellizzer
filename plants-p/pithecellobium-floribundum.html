Pithecellobium floribundum

Pithecellobium floribundum, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, drooping clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year, making it a striking ornamental plant. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.

It is also valued for its potential use in traditional medicine.
