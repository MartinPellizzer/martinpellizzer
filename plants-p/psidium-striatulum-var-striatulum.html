Psidium striatulum var. striatulum

Psidium striatulum var. striatulum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, bright red berries. The fruit has a sweet, tangy flavor and is rich in antioxidants.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
