Panicum crus-galli var. atra

Panicum crus-galli var. atra, commonly known as black panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black inflorescence, which distinguishes it from other panic grass species. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Black panicgrass is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions, making it a resilient and common weed in many regions.
