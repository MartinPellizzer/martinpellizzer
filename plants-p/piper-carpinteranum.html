Piper carpinteranum

Piper carpinteranum, commonly known as the Carpinthia pepper or Carpinthia plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, peppery fruits. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

Piper carpinteranum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
