Pithecellobium bauchei

Pithecellobium bauchei, commonly known as the yellow velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant produces pendant, yellowish-green pods that contain edible, bean-like seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, ovate leaflets that give it a feathery appearance.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the seeds, which can be used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.
