Puya obconica

Puya obconica, commonly known as the Andean lily, is a striking plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves that form a rosette structure. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers that can range in color from red to orange, often blooming in late summer. Puya obconica is known for its long-lasting blooms and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.
