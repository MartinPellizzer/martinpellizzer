Persica davidiana var. rubra

Persica davidiana var. rubra, commonly known as the red-flowered peach tree, is a variant of the Davidian peach native to China.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals that bloom in early spring, adding a striking visual element to gardens. The plant produces small, edible fruits similar to peaches, though they are typically less sweet and more tart. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticultural cultivation.
