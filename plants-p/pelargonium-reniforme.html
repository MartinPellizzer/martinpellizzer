Pelargonium reniforme

Pelargonium reniforme, commonly known as the kidney-shaped geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive kidney-shaped leaves, which are typically hairy and have a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium reniforme is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
