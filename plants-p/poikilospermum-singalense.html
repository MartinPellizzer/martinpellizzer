Poikilospermum singalense

Poikilospermum singalense, commonly known as the Sri Lankan velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods containing numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.

Its botanical features include a woody stem, trifoliate leaves, and a unique seed coat that exhibits a marbled pattern.
