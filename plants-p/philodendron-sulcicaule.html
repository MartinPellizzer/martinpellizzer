Philodendron sulcicaule

Philodendron sulcicaule, commonly known as the waxy-leaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often waxy and have a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by green, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
