Palaeocyanus crassifolius

Palaeocyanus crassifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved bluebell, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy surface. The plant produces clusters of vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its habitat preferences include moist, shaded environments in forests or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
