Passiflora caerulea var. regnellii

Passiflora caerulea var. regnellii, commonly known as the blue passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue and white flowers, which feature intricate structures including a crown of filaments and a central corona. The plant produces edible, blue-black berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
