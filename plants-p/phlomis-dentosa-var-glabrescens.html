Phlomis dentosa var. glabrescens

Phlomis dentosa var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smoothleaf gumweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered with soft, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flower heads that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
