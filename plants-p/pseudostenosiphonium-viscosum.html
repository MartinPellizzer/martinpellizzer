Pseudostenosiphonium viscosum

Pseudostenosiphonium viscosum, commonly known as the sticky sponge spore plant, is a unique member of the Lepidostomatellaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, gelatinous spore sacs that adhere to surfaces, facilitating its dispersal. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its structure includes a short, branched stalk with a single spore sac at the tip.

The sticky texture of its spores plays a crucial role in its ecological interactions and survival strategies.
