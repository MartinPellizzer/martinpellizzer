Polygala piliophora

Polygala piliophora, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, elongated fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
