Pimpinella × supermagna

Pimpinella × supermagna, commonly known as the giant cow parsley, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Pimpinella orientalis and Pimpinella polychroma.

It is characterized by its large, feathery leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to heights of 1.5 to 2 meters, making it one of the tallest members of the Apiaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wet meadows.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and horticultural interest.
