Polygonum myriocladum

Polygonum myriocladum, commonly known as the many-leafed knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and the presence of numerous small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments.

Polygonum myriocladum is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with its deep root system and succulent leaves.
