Ptychosperma vitiense

Ptychosperma vitiense, commonly known as the Vitiense palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of the Pacific, particularly found in parts of Fiji and other nearby islands. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, followed by clustered, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color.

This palm is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.
