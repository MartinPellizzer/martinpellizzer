Pisum macrospermum

Pisum macrospermum, commonly known as the large-seeded pea, is a species of legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several large, oval seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, heart-shaped leaflets.

Pisum macrospermum is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients.
