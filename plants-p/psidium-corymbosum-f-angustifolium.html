Psidium corymbosum f. angustifolium

Psidium corymbosum f. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved guava, is a botanical variant of the common guava.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically darker in color compared to the standard variety. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible, aromatic fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique foliage.
