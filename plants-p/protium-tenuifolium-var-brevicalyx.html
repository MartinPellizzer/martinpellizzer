Protium tenuifolium var. brevicalyx

Protium tenuifolium var. brevicalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandarac tree, is a member of the Burseraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a smooth surface and a distinct midrib. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, fragrant fruits that contain winged seeds.

This species is valued for its aromatic resin, which has been used traditionally in incense and medicinal applications.
