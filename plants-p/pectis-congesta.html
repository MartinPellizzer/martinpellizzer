Pectis congesta

Pectis congesta, commonly known as the clustered pectis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Pectis congesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
