Puccinellia phryganodes subsp. vilfoidea

Puccinellia phryganodes subsp.

vilfoidea, commonly known as the desert sandgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions. It typically forms dense, mat-like growths that help stabilize sandy soils and prevent erosion.

The plant has narrow, wiry leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to thrive in harsh environments with limited water availability.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
