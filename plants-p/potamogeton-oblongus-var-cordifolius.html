Potamogeton oblongus var. cordifolius

Potamogeton oblongus var. cordifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has elongated, ribbon-like leaves that are broad at the base and taper toward the tip, with a distinctive cordate (heart-shaped) base. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like clusters, and it is often found in shallow, slow-moving waters. Its rhizomes form dense underwater mats, which can help stabilize sediments and provide habitat for aquatic life.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to water clarity and supporting biodiversity.
