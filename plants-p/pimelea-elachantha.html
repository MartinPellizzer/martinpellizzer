Pimelea elachantha

Pimelea elachantha, commonly known as the dwarf banksia, is a small shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and produces small, yellow flower spikes that bloom in spring. The plant is typically found in heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in full sun. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental appearance.

Pimelea elachantha is also valued for its ability to attract native pollinators such as bees and birds.
