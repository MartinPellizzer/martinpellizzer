Piper roseovenulosum

Piper roseovenulosum, commonly known as the Roseovenulose pepper vine, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters, and it produces berries that are typically yellow or red when mature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of aromatic foliage.
