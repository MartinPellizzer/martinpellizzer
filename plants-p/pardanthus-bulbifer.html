Pardanthus bulbifer

Pardanthus bulbifer, commonly known as the bulbous thornapple, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, bulb-like stems and spiny leaves, which help it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation in the wild.

Pardanthus bulbifer is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
