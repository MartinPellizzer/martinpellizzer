Prunus mirabilis

Prunus mirabilis, commonly known as the Chinese date or the Japanese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad ovate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in spring. The tree produces small, yellow-orange fruits that ripen in autumn, often with a sweet, honey-like flavor. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also cultivated for its edible fruit.

Prunus mirabilis thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
