Pyrenacantha repanda

Pyrenacantha repanda, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its milky sap, which is toxic and used in traditional medicine. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a waxy texture and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that contains numerous seeds.

Pyrenacantha repanda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to arid conditions.
