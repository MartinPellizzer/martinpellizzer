Phaseolus vulgaris var. truncatus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. truncatus, commonly known as the dwarf bean or Mexican dwarf bean, is a variety of common bean native to Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter stems compared to standard Phaseolus vulgaris varieties. The plant typically produces small, round to oval pods that mature to a yellowish or brown color. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and slightly hairy.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible beans, which are used in various traditional dishes.
