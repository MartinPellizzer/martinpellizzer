Pectiantia latiflora

Pectiantia latiflora, commonly known as the broadleaf pectiantia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, which bloom during the wet season. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Pectiantia latiflora is often found in dense undergrowth of rainforests, where it plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat for various insect species.
