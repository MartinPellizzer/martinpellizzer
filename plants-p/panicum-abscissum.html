Panicum abscissum

Panicum abscissum, commonly known as redtop grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, reddish-purple inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue that turns reddish in the fall.

Panicum abscissum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
