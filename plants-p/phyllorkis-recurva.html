Phyllorkis recurva

Phyllorkis recurva, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phyllorkis, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming in spring. Its foliage is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Phyllorkis recurva thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands.
