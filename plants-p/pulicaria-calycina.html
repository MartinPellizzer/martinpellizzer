Pulicaria calycina

Pulicaria calycina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandthrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, linear leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, helping it retain moisture in arid conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pulicaria calycina is often found in sandy or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
