Polygala vulgaris f. angustifolia

Polygala vulgaris f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems and small, usually green or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. The leaves are linear and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
