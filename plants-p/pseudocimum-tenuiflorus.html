Pseudocimum tenuiflorus

Pseudocimum tenuiflorus, commonly known as the slender-flower pseudocimum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland areas or along stream banks. Its stems are usually slender and often branched, supporting the delicate inflorescence.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and ecological niche.
