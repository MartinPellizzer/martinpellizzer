Ptilotus grandiflorus

Ptilotus grandiflorus, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species in arid environments.

Ptilotus grandiflorus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
