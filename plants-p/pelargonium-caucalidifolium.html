Pelargonium caucalidifolium

Pelargonium caucalidifolium, commonly known as the caucalidifolium geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or red. The plant features broad, waxy leaves that are often deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its unique foliage and floral display, it is valued in horticulture for both its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
