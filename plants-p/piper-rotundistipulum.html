Piper rotundistipulum

Piper rotundistipulum, commonly known as the round-leaved pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into fleshy, red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The berries are edible and have a mild, peppery flavor, making it a potential candidate for culinary or medicinal use.
