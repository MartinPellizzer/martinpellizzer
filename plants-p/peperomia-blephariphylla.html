Peperomia blephariphylla

Peperomia blephariphylla, commonly known as the earleaf peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It features small, ovate leaves with a waxy texture and a distinctive dark green color. The plant produces delicate, reddish-brown spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically found on the undersides of its leaves. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil.

Peperomia blephariphylla is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
