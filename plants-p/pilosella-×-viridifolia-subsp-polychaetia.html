Pilosella × viridifolia subsp. polychaetia

Pilosella × viridifolia subsp.

polychaetia, commonly known as the green-haired hawk's-beard, is a perennial flowering plant characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and bright yellow composite flowers. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The subspecies polychaetia is distinguished by its more densely hairy leaves compared to other subspecies of Pilosella × viridifolia.
