Pyrus aria var. rugosa

Pyrus aria var. rugosa, commonly known as the rough pear, is a variant of the pear tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or green in color. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or well-drained soils. Its botanical features include a short trunk, deciduous foliage, and a preference for full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its historical use in horticulture.
