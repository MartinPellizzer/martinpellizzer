Prunus rhamnoides

Prunus rhamnoides, commonly known as the red elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible when ripe but contain toxic seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and wetlands.

Prunus rhamnoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
