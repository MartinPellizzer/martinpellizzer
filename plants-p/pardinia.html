Pardinia

Pardinia, commonly known as the desert lily, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaryllidaceae.

These plants are native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, particularly Namibia and South Africa. Pardinia species are characterized by their tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white, yellow, and orange. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to conserve water in harsh environments.

They play an important ecological role in their native habitats by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in sandy areas.
