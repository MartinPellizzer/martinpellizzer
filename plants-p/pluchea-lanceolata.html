Pluchea lanceolata

Pluchea lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf mistletoe, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, white to yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Pluchea lanceolata is often found in disturbed habitats and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
