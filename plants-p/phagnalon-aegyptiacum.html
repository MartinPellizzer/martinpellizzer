Phagnalon aegyptiacum

Phagnalon aegyptiacum, commonly known as Egyptian phagnalon, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, particularly in the Mediterranean and parts of Africa. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Phagnalon aegyptiacum is also noted for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
