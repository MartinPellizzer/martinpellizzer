Pleurothallis bevilacquana

Pleurothallis bevilacquana, commonly known as the Bevilacqua's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Ecuador.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that grows on trees in humid, tropical rainforest environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
