Polygala nicaeensis subsp. caesalpini

Polygala nicaeensis subsp.

caesalpini, commonly known as the blue milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as scrublands and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
