Pseuderanthemum leiophyllum

Pseuderanthemum leiophyllum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often arranged in clusters. Its leaves are smooth and opposite, with a slightly glossy surface.

It is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact size.
