Potentilla arcadiensis

Potentilla arcadiensis, commonly known as the Florida potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Potentilla arcadiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
