Potamogeton × navicularis

Potamogeton × navicularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that occurs naturally in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which emerge from the water surface. It thrives in shallow to moderately deep waters with soft to muddy substrates.

Potamogeton × navicularis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
