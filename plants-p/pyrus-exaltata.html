Pyrus exaltata

Pyrus exaltata, commonly known as the golden apple or the exalted pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of western North America, particularly in California and Oregon. The plant is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and produces showy, yellow flowers in early spring. Its fruit, a small, yellowish apple, is edible but not widely cultivated for commercial use.

Pyrus exaltata is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
