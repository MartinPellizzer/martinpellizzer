Primula bicolor

Primula bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its showy, bilaterally symmetric flowers that typically feature two distinct color zones, often white and purple or pink. The plant produces oval, hairy leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. It is a member of the Primulaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
