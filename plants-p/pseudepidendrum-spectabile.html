Pseudepidendrum spectabile

Pseudepidendrum spectabile, commonly known as the showy pseudepidendrum, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful inflorescences. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its flowers are usually large and showy, with a complex structure typical of orchids.

It requires high humidity and indirect light to thrive, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
