Prunus × hallasenensis

Prunus × hallasenensis, commonly known as the Hallasen cherry, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, bright red fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant produces showy white to pink flowers in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and turn vibrant shades of red or orange in the fall, enhancing its seasonal appeal.
