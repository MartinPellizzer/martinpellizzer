Polygonum patulum var. virgatum

Polygonum patulum var. virgatum, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, stream banks, and marshes.

This plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive.
