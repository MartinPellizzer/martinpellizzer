Polystachya zuluensis

Polystachya zuluensis, commonly known as the Zulu orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, rhizomatous stem and produces tall, flowering spikes with numerous small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators such as bees and moths.

Polystachya zuluensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
