Podocarpus costalis subsp. muroii

Podocarpus costalis subsp.

muroii, commonly known as the Japanese umbrella pine, is a species of conifer native to Japan. It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody cones containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often cultivated as an ornamental tree in gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and ability to adapt to various environmental conditions.
