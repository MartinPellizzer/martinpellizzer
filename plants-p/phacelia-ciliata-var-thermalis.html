Phacelia ciliata var. thermalis

Phacelia ciliata var. thermalis, commonly known as the thermal phacelia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, clustered blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped with fine cilia along the edges, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting pollinators in its native environment.
