Plectronia floribunda

Plectronia floribunda, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
