Parinari costata subsp. rubiginosa

Parinari costata subsp.

rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed parinari, is a tropical tree species native to regions of Central and South America. It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses among indigenous communities.
