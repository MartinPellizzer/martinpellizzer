Piper furcatum

Piper furcatum, commonly known as the split-leaf pepper, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
