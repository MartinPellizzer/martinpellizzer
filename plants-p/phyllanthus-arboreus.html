Phyllanthus arboreus

Phyllanthus arboreus, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains several seeds.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
