Plantago patagonica

Plantago patagonica, commonly known as Patagonian plantain, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are borne on a slender stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
