Polygala americana var. caracasana

Polygala americana var. caracasana, commonly known as the Caracas polygala, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive spurred petal that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
