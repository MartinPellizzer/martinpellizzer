Paspalum mauritanicum

Paspalum mauritanicum, commonly known as the Mauritania grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, narrow leaves, and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for landscaping in hot, dry climates. It is also used for forage in some regions due to its hardiness and ability to regrow after grazing.

Paspalum mauritanicum plays a role in stabilizing soils and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
