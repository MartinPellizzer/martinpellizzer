Pedilanthus tithymaloides subsp. bahamensis

Pedilanthus tithymaloides subsp.

bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian candleplant, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the Bahamas. It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, exhibiting succulent leaves and stems adapted to arid conditions.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in branched inflorescences. Its botanical features include a distinctive, candle-like appearance due to its elongated, cylindrical stems.

It is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
