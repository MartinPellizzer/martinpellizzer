Piper faviculiferum

Piper faviculiferum, commonly known as the white-veined pepper, is a tropical climbing plant belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems and compound leaves with prominent white veins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into berries, which are used as a spice in local cuisines. Its aromatic leaves and fruits contain bioactive compounds that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Piper faviculiferum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its unique flavor profile.
