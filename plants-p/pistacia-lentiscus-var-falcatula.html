Pistacia lentiscus var. falcatula

Pistacia lentiscus var. falcatula, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mastic, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is known for its resin, which has been historically used in various applications, including medicine and incense. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for certain insects and birds.
