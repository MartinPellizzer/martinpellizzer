Pubilaria

Pubilaria, commonly known as the common pubescent bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces showy blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist meadows and woodland edges.

Pubilaria is typically perennial and thrives in well-drained soil with partial shade.
