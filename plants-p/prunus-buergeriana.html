Prunus buergeriana

Prunus buergeriana, commonly known as the Chinese wild cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by small, red drupes that ripen in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Prunus buergeriana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
