Pityphyllum laricinum

Pityphyllum laricinum, commonly known as the mountain pine or white pine, is a coniferous tree native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, needle-like leaves arranged in pairs. The tree produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Pityphyllum laricinum plays an important role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
