Pseuderanthemum metallicum

Pseuderanthemum metallicum, commonly known as the silverleaf pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens and greenhouses.
