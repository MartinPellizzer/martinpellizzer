Philodendron schmidtiae

Philodendron schmidtiae, commonly known as the Schmidt's philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a houseplant.
