Phaseolus gentryi

Phaseolus gentryi, commonly known as the Gentry's bean, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes known for their nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are compound, with trifoliate arrangements, typical of many Phaseolus species.

It is primarily cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines.
