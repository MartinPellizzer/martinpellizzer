Polygala monspeliaca subsp. straminea

Polygala monspeliaca subsp.

straminea, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikes.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, bean-like pods that contain the seeds.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as scrublands and limestone areas.
