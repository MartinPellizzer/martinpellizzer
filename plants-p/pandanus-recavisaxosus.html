Pandanus recavisaxosus

Pandanus recavisaxosus, commonly known as the narrow-sheathed pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the Pandanaceae family.

It is native to the Pacific Islands, particularly found in regions like Samoa and Tonga. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. Its inflorescence consists of a large, branched cluster of small, fragrant flowers.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and humid conditions.
