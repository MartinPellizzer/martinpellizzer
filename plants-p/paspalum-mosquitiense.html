Paspalum mosquitiense

Paspalum mosquitiense, commonly known as mosquito grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes and swampy soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
