Potentilla venusta

Potentilla venusta, commonly known as the showy cinquefoil, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with five oval-shaped leaflets that are glossy and dark green.

Potentilla venusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
