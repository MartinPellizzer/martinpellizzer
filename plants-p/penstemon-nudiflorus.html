Penstemon nudiflorus

Penstemon nudiflorus, commonly known as the nakedflower beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces showy, naked flowers that bloom early in the spring before the leaves fully develop. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
