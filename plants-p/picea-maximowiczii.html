Picea maximowiczii

Picea maximowiczii, commonly known as the Korean fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It is characterized by its conical shape, dark green needle-like leaves, and distinctive blue-gray bark. The tree produces small, cylindrical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
