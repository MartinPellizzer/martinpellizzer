Polygonum strigosum var. benguetense

Polygonum strigosum var. benguetense, commonly known as the Benguet knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its stout, hairy stems and lanceolate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as mountainous regions and forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Polygonaceae family, often found in high-altitude habitats.
