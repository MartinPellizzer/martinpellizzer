Pterophylla dichotoma

Pterophylla dichotoma, commonly known as the two-branched fern, is a unique plant belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its dichotomous branching pattern, where the fronds split into two equal parts at each node. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the undersides of its fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its delicate, feathery appearance makes it a distinctive addition to botanical collections.
