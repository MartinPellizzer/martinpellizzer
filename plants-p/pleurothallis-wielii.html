Pleurothallis wielii

Pleurothallis wielii, commonly known as the Wiel's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, facilitating moisture retention.

Pleurothallis wielii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
