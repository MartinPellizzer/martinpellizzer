Physinga polygonata

Physinga polygonata, commonly known as the polygonate, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system and lanceolate, serrated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Physinga polygonata is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
