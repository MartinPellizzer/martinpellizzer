Polystichum hartwigii

Polystichum hartwigii, commonly known as Hartwig's wood fern, is a species of fern native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender fronds that can reach up to 1.5 meters in length. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are bipinnate, with a distinctive, elegant structure that makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
