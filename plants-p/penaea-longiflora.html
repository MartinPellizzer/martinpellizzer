Penaea longiflora

Penaea longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered penaea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is known for its robust growth habit and adaptability to various soil types. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Penaea longiflora is primarily valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
