Psychanthus macropycnanthus

Psychanthus macropycnanthus, commonly known as the large-fruited psychanthus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces large, fleshy fruits that are typically orange or red in color, containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
