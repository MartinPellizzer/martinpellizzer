Primula muscarioides subsp. conica

Primula muscarioides subsp.

conica, commonly known as the conical primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers in early spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its flowers are typically borne on upright, hairy stems and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
