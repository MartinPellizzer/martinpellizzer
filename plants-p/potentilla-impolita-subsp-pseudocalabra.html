Potentilla impolita subsp. pseudocalabra

Potentilla impolita subsp.

pseudocalabra, commonly known as the rough potentilla, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes with well-drained soils.

The plant features slender, often hairy stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptic, with a rough texture and slightly serrated edges.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental appeal.
