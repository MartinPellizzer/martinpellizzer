Penstemon rubicundus

Penstemon rubicundus, commonly known as the red-horned penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its striking red to orange tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Penstemon rubicundus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
