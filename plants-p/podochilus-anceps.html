Podochilus anceps

Podochilus anceps, commonly known as the blue orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue and violet flower spikes that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are small and clustered, often with a waxy texture and a subtle fragrance.

Podochilus anceps is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique coloration.
