Prunella asiatica

Prunella asiatica, commonly known as self-heal, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant grows in a variety of habitats, including meadows, woodlands, and disturbed soils, and can reach heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are often used in traditional medicine.

Prunella asiatica is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
