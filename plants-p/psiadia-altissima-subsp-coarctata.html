Psiadia altissima subsp. coarctata

Psiadia altissima subsp.

coarctata, commonly known as the narrowleaf muhly, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
