Phyllanthus purpureus

Phyllanthus purpureus, commonly known as purple leaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with milky sap.

Phyllanthus purpureus is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
