Piper subcaudatum

Piper subcaudatum, commonly known as the long-tailed pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are typically berries that mature to a dark red color, containing several seeds.

Piper subcaudatum is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
