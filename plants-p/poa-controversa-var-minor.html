Poa controversa var. minor

Poa controversa var. minor, commonly known as the lesser bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, tufted stems with narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often tinged with silver. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
