Phyllanthus juglandifolius

Phyllanthus juglandifolius, commonly known as the walnut-leaved phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a small shrub or tree with smooth, dark green leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape, often resembling the leaves of a walnut tree. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, spherical fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate, making it a common species in moist lowland forests.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
