Pepo farinosus

Pepo farinosus, commonly known as the dusty pumpkin, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy surface and a distinctive, elongated shape. The fruit typically has a thick, waxy skin that can vary in color from green to yellowish-orange. The plant produces yellow flowers that are separate from the fruit, a common trait in the gourd family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
