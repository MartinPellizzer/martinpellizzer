Piper lanceolatum

Piper lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceleaf pepper or narrow-leaved pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruits are small, berry-like structures that contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Piper lanceolatum is often grown for its ornamental value as well as its medicinal properties.
