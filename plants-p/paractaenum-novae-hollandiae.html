Paractaenum novae-hollandiae

Paractaenum novae-hollandiae, commonly known as the Australian flame tree, is a striking shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in coastal and inland regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly planted in gardens and along roadsides.
