Pelargonium peltatum

Pelargonium peltatum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Its ornamental value and ease of care contribute to its widespread use in gardens and indoor settings.
