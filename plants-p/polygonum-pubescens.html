Polygonum pubescens

Polygonum pubescens, commonly known as the hairy knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Polygonum pubescens is often found in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.
