Polygonum thevenaei

Polygonum thevenaei, commonly known as the Cape Verde morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Cape Verde Islands.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its succulent stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in arid and coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Polygonum thevenaei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in its native region.
