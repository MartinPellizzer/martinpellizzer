Phaseolus foecundus

Phaseolus foecundus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a climbing annual plant native to the Americas, particularly Central and South America. The plant produces vibrant red or yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in warm, sunny climates.

The scarlet runner bean is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary use in various dishes.
