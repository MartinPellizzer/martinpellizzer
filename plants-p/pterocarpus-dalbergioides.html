Pterocarpus dalbergioides

Pterocarpus dalbergioides, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its beauty and durability. The plant has compound leaves with alternate arrangement and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a pod containing flattened seeds, typical of the legume family.

The tree plays an important role in its native ecosystems and is also used in traditional medicine.
