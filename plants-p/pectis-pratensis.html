Pectis pratensis

Pectis pratensis, commonly known as the meadow pectis, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to white ray florets.

Pectis pratensis is often found in open habitats and is a common species in ecological studies due to its role in supporting pollinators.
