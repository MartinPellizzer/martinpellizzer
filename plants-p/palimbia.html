Palimbia

Palimbia, commonly known as the "tamarind tree," is a tropical plant native to Africa, southern Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a thick, smooth bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated pods containing edible seeds surrounded by a sticky, acidic pulp.

The botanical features of Palimbia include its compound leaves, fibrous roots, and the unique structure of its fruit, which is rich in tannins and used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
