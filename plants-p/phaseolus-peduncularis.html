Phaseolus peduncularis

Phaseolus peduncularis, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a tropical legume native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, producing vibrant red or yellow flowers and elongated, edible pods. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets and is known for its rapid growth and high yield potential. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The seeds inside the pods are typically used for culinary purposes and are rich in protein and nutrients.
