Pterospermum longipes

Pterospermum longipes, commonly known as the longipes flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, red-orange flowers. The plant produces distinctive, winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal by wind. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a unique floral structure with five petals.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
