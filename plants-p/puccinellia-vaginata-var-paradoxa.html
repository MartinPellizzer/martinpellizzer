Puccinellia vaginata var. paradoxa

Puccinellia vaginata var. paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxical bottlebrush grass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescences that resemble a bottlebrush, composed of numerous small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.

The species is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
