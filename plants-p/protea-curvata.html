Protea curvata

Protea curvata, commonly known as the bent-stem protea, is a South African native plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers arranged in a rounded, urn-shaped structure. The plant typically grows in fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating.

Protea curvata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
