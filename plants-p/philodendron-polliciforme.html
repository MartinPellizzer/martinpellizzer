Philodendron polliciforme

Philodendron polliciforme, commonly known as the fingerleaf philodendron, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by green, fleshy berries. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and attach to surfaces, making it well-suited for hanging baskets or trellises.

Philodendron polliciforme thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and humidity to flourish.
