Pleurothallis purpurascens

Pleurothallis purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple blossoms. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
