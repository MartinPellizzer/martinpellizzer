Phyllodes bachemiana

Phyllodes bachemiana, commonly known as the Bachem's wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features phyllodes, which are flattened, leaf-like stems that perform photosynthesis. Its flowers are typically yellow or golden in color, arranged in racemes, and it produces small, pod-like fruits.

Phyllodes bachemiana is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils.
