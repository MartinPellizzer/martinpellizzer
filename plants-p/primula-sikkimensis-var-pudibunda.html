Primula sikkimensis var. pudibunda

Primula sikkimensis var. pudibunda, commonly known as the Sikkim primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
