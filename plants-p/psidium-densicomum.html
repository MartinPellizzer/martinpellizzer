Psidium densicomum

Psidium densicomum, commonly known as the dense-leaved guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its dense foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are similar to guavas but are less commonly cultivated. Its leaves are thick and leathery, providing a habitat for various insects and small animals.

Psidium densicomum is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
