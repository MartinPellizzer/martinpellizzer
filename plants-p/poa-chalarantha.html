Poa chalarantha

Poa chalarantha, commonly known as the alpine bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, blue-green colored leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude meadows and alpine tundra.

Due to its ecological significance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
