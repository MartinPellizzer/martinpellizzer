Pelargonium australe var. majus

Pelargonium australe var. majus, commonly known as the Australian geranium, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. This variety thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
