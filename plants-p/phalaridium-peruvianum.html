Phalaridium peruvianum

Phalaridium peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian sandmat, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Peru.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale green or yellowish flowers in clusters. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Phalaridium peruvianum is adapted to withstand drought and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
