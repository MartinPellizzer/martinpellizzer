Pediocactus lophophoroides

Pediocactus lophophoroides, commonly known as the desert rose cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, spineless cactus with a globular or cylindrical stem that often branches at the base. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are borne on short, erect stems. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to drought conditions.

The plant is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
