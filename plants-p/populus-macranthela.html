Populus macranthela

Populus macranthela, commonly known as the large-flowered poplar, is a deciduous tree native to parts of China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for landscaping purposes.

Populus macranthela plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
