Potamogeton plantagineus

Potamogeton plantagineus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically less than 2 millimeters wide, arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which emerge from the water surface. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, rivers, and ponds, often forming dense underwater vegetation.

Its rapid growth makes it an important component of aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
