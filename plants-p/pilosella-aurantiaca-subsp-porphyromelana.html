Pilosella aurantiaca subsp. porphyromelana

Pilosella aurantiaca subsp.

porphyromelana, commonly known as the red-centred pearly bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with a red center, which are typically arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

The plant is generally low-growing, with hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and sandy soils.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
