Panicum fatmense

Panicum fatmense, commonly known as the Fatmense grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny grains. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for use in erosion control, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
