Pandanus kraussii

Pandanus kraussii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a species of palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fibrous, pineapple-like fruits. Its leaves are fibrous and can be woven into mats and baskets.

Pandanus kraussii thrives in coastal and wetland areas, often growing in saline or brackish soils.
