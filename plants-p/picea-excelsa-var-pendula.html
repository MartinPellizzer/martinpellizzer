Picea excelsa var. pendula

Picea excelsa var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping silver fir, is a variant of the silver fir native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that give it a distinctive, cascading appearance. The tree has needle-like leaves that are dark green on top and silvery white underneath, providing a striking visual contrast. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

This ornamental tree is valued for its unique form and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and landscapes.
