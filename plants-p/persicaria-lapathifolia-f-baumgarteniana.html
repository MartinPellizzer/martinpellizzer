Persicaria lapathifolia f. baumgarteniana

Persicaria lapathifolia f. baumgarteniana, commonly known as the Baumann's smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a compact growth habit with upright stems and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its botanical features include hairy leaves and a short, stout rhizome system.
