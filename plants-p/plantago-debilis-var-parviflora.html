Plantago debilis var. parviflora

Plantago debilis var. parviflora, commonly known as the slender plantain, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
