Piper hispidum var. ellipticifolium

Piper hispidum var. ellipticifolium, commonly known as the roughleaf pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, hairy leaves that give it its varietal name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique foliage and adaptability make it a notable species in botanical studies and horticulture.
