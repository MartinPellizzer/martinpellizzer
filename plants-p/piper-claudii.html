Piper claudii

Piper claudii, commonly known as the Claudius pepper or white pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its climbing habit and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical berries. The fruit contains a single seed and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it distinct within the genus Piper, with unique morphological and chemical properties.
