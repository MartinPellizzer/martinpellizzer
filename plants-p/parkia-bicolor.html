Parkia bicolor

Parkia bicolor, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display during the warmer months. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often trifoliate, and its fruit is a long, slender pod containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy flowers.

The tree also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
