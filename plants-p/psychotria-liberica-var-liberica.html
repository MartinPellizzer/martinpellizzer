Psychotria liberica var. liberica

Psychotria liberica var. liberica, commonly known as the African coffee bush, is a member of the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is notable for its role in the cultivation of coffee, as it is the parent plant of the Liberica variety.
