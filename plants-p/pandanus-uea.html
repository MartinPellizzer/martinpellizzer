Pandanus uea

Pandanus uea, commonly known as the Tahitian pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, including Tahiti and other islands in French Polynesia. The plant is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green in color.

The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found growing near the shoreline or in wetlands.
