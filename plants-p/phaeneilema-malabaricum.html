Phaeneilema malabaricum

Phaeneilema malabaricum, commonly known as the Malabar orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Phaeneilema malabaricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
