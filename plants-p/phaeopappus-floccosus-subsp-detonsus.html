Phaeopappus floccosus subsp. detonsus

Phaeopappus floccosus subsp.

detonsus, commonly known as the white sand bladdergrass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its soft, fluffy appearance, with pale green to white foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats. Its reproductive structures are adapted for wind pollination, and it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern in some areas.
