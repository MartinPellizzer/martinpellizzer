Pupalia alopecurus

Pupalia alopecurus, commonly known as the lesser cow-wheat, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically features hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, with small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Pupalia alopecurus is often found in association with other early-flowering plants in temperate regions.
