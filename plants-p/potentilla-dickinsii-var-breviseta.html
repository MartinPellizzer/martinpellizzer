Potentilla dickinsii var. breviseta

Potentilla dickinsii var. breviseta, commonly known as the short-stemmed potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has grayish-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in harsh climates.
