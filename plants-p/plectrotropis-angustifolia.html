Plectrotropis angustifolia

Plectrotropis angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed plectrotropis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
