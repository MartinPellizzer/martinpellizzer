Pithecellobium compactum

Pithecellobium compactum, commonly known as the dwarf pisonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers, which are often followed by bright red or orange fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
