Pyrus malus subsp. conocarpa

Pyrus malus subsp.

conocarpa, commonly known as the Mexican apple or conocarpus apple, is a subspecies of the apple tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It is characterized by its small, yellow to orange fruits that are typically harvested when they are still immature.

The plant has a compact growth habit with small, oval leaves and produces flowers in clusters. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit.

The subspecies is closely related to the common apple but differs in its geographic distribution and fruit characteristics.
