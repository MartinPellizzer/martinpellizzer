Panicum dorsisetum

Panicum dorsisetum, commonly known as the rough bristlegrass, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, tufted foliage. The plant produces fluffy, spike-like inflorescences that are often used in ornamental landscaping. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough, hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Panicum dorsisetum is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy choice for native plant gardens.
