Piper parvilimbum

Piper parvilimbum, commonly known as the small-leaved pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The plant prefers humid, shaded environments and is relatively easy to grow in warm climates.
