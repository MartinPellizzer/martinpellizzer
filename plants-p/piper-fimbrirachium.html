Piper fimbrirachium

Piper fimbrirachium, commonly known as the hairy pipevine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 2,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The plant's name refers to the fine hairs that cover its stems and leaves.
