Potentilla taliensis

Potentilla taliensis, commonly known as Taliensian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that mature in the fall.

Potentilla taliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
