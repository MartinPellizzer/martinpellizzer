Pleurothallis imbricata

Pleurothallis imbricata, commonly known as the "Cushion Orchid," is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees, relying on air roots for moisture and nutrients. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, forming a compact, cushion-like structure. Its flowers are usually small and delicate, often displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink.

Pleurothallis imbricata is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental qualities.
