Panicum anisotrichum

Panicum anisotrichum, commonly known as black panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dark-colored inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands, roadsides, and agricultural fields. Its seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a weed in some regions.
