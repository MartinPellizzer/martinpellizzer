Paspalum uniseriatum

Paspalum uniseriatum, commonly known as slender paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, erect stems. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and seasonally flooded areas. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Paspalum uniseriatum is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
