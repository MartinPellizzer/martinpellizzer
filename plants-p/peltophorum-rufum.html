Peltophorum rufum

Peltophorum rufum, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The tree has pinnately compound leaves that turn yellow in autumn before shedding. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it produces long, narrow pods that contain flat, winged seeds.

Peltophorum rufum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks due to its vibrant floral display.
