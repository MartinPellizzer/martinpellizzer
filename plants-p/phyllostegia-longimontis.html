Phyllostegia longimontis

Phyllostegia longimontis, commonly known as the long-stemmed mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, woodland environments.

Phyllostegia longimontis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
