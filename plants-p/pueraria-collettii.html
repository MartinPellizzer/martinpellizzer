Pueraria collettii

Pueraria collettii, commonly known as the wild licorice or Japanese kudzu, is a vigorous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces purple to white pea-like flowers. The plant is characterized by its extensive root system, which can grow deep and spread widely. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Pueraria collettii is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
