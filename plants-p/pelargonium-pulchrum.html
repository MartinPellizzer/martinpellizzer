Pelargonium pulchrum

Pelargonium pulchrum, commonly known as the Cape pelargonium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom throughout the warmer months. Its leaves are often serrated and have a distinctive, fragrant scent.

Pelargonium pulchrum is well-suited for cultivation in warm climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.
