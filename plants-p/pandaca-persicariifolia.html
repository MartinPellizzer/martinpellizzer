Pandaca persicariifolia

Pandaca persicariifolia, commonly known as the roseleaf pandaca, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble those of the rose, with pinkish-red petals and a distinctive yellow center. The plant has broad, ovate leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Pandaca persicariifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
