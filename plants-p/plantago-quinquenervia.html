Plantago quinquenervia

Plantago quinquenervia, commonly known as the five-veined plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a rosette of broad, oval-shaped leaves with five prominent veins running from the base to the tips. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in disturbed areas.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
