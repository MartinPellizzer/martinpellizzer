Pinguicula michoacana

Pinguicula michoacana, commonly known as the Michoacan butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, sticky leaves that trap and digest insects. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or forested areas. Its flowers are typically small and yellow, with a distinctive hood-like structure.

Pinguicula michoacana plays an important ecological role in its habitat by controlling insect populations and contributing to nutrient cycling.
