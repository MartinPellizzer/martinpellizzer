Parathelypteris japonica var. musashiensis

Parathelypteris japonica var. musashiensis, commonly known as the Musashi fern, is a rare and specialized fern species native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories where it can receive filtered sunlight. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in lines along the margins of the fronds.

This variety is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique morphological features.
