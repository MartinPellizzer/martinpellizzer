Polygala telephioides

Polygala telephioides, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its unique, delicate, and often pale-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

The ghost flower is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unusual appearance.
