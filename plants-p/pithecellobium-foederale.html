Pithecellobium foederale

Pithecellobium foederale, commonly known as the red-leaf poinciana, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically a deep green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a pod that contains several edible, seed-like structures.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
