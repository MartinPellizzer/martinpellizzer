Pentapteris

Pentapteris, commonly known as the five-winged fern, is a genus of fossilized plants from the Paleozoic era.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds with five pairs of lateral lobes, which give it its name. These ancient plants were likely aquatic or semi-aquatic, growing in swampy environments. The structure of their fronds suggests they were adapted for efficient photosynthesis in waterlogged conditions.

Pentapteris provides important insights into the evolution of early vascular plants and the development of complex leaf morphology.
