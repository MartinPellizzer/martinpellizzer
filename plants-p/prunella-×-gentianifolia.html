Prunella × gentianifolia

Prunella × gentianifolia, commonly known as blueweed or gentianleaf verbena, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. This perennial plant thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
