Protea namaquana

Protea namaquana, commonly known as the Namaqua protea, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that consist of a central cone surrounded by numerous bracts. The plant produces white to pinkish flowers that are adapted for pollination by birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky, coastal areas.

The protea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a key species in the fynbos biome.
