Potentilla nivea vulgaris

Potentilla nivea vulgaris, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine climates. Its leaves are usually divided into five lobes, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

Potentilla nivea vulgaris is often found in open meadows and scree slopes at high altitudes.
