Pisonia ovalifolia

Pisonia ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf pisonia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow, often attracting birds and insects. The plant has compound leaves with ovate to elliptical leaflets, and its bark is smooth with a reddish-brown hue. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Pisonia ovalifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
