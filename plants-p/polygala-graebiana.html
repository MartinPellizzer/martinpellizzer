Polygala graebiana

Polygala graebiana, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features small, yellow flowers clustered in spikes, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Polygala graebiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
