Philodendron remifolium subsp. sabulosum

Philodendron remifolium subsp.

sabulosum, commonly known as the sandy soil philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are typically glossy and have a slightly waxy texture.

The plant prefers well-draining soil and indirect sunlight, making it suitable for indoor cultivation. Its growth habit is vine-like, often trailing or climbing, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
