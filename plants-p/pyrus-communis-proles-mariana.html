Pyrus communis proles mariana

Pyrus communis proles mariana, commonly known as the Marianas pear, is a variety of the common pear tree.

It is characterized by its small, round fruits with a yellowish-green skin and a sweet, aromatic flavor. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in autumn, and produces flowers in the spring before the fruit develops. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is valued for both its ornamental appeal and edible fruit.
