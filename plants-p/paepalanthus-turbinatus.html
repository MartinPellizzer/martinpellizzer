Paepalanthus turbinatus

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a water-holding cup. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences with brightly colored flowers, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific care to mimic its natural habitat.
