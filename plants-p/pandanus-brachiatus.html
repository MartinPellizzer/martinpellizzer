Pandanus brachiatus

Pandanus brachiatus, commonly known as the bottlebrush palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, brush-like inflorescences, giving it its common name. Its fibrous leaves are used for making thatch and baskets, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Pandanus brachiatus thrives in humid, coastal environments and requires well-drained soil.
