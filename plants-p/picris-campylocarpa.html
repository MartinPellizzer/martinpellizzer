Picris campylocarpa

Picris campylocarpa, commonly known as the bent-leaf wormwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of individual flowers. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often curled or bent, giving it its common name. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

The plant is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
