Persea guatemalensis

Persea guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Central America, particularly Guatemala, and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to persimmons but have a distinct flavor. Persea guatemalensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local cultures.
