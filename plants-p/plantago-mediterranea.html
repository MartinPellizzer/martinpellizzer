Plantago mediterranea

Plantago mediterranea, commonly known as Mediterranean plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which are borne on slender stalks above the foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its adaptability makes it a resilient species in arid and semi-arid environments.
