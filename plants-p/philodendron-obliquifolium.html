Philodendron obliquifolium

Philodendron obliquifolium, commonly known as the twisted-leaf philodendron, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that often have a distinctive twist or curl, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a decorative plant.
