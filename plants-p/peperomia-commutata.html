Peperomia commutata

Peperomia commutata, commonly known as the Variegated Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, oval-shaped leaves with striking variegation of white, yellow, and green, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of green and red, though it is primarily grown for its ornamental foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia commutata is relatively low maintenance, making it ideal for beginners and those with limited space.
