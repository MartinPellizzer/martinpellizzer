Prunus fruticosa f. pendula

Prunus fruticosa f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping almond, is a decorative shrub characterized by its cascading branches that create a weeping effect.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is a compact form of Prunus fruticosa, which is native to the Mediterranean region. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible drupes that turn from red to dark purple as they mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique form and seasonal blooms make it a visually striking addition to landscapes.
