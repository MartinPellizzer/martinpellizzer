Peltaria obcordata

Peltaria obcordata, commonly known as the heartleaf pellitory, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, opposite, ovate to obcordate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive heart-shaped base. The plant produces small, clustered white or greenish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves and stems are sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
