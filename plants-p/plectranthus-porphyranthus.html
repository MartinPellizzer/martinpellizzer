Plectranthus porphyranthus

Plectranthus porphyranthus, commonly known as the purple-leaf plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
