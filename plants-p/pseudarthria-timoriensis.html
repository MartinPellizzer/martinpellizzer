Pseudarthria timoriensis

Pseudarthria timoriensis, commonly known as the Timor grass, is a flowering plant native to the island of Timor.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is typically found in open grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates.

Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals, contributing to its ecological role in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.
