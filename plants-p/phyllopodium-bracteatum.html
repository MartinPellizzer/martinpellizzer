Phyllopodium bracteatum

Phyllopodium bracteatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Phyllopodium bracteatum is often found in moist, shady environments such as forest understories.
