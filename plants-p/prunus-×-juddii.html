Prunus × juddii

Prunus × juddii, commonly known as the Judd cherry, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy pink to white flowers in spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in jams and jellies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while its bark is smooth and grayish-brown.

Prunus × juddii thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
