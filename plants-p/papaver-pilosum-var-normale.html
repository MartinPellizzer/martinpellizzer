Papaver pilosum var. normale

Papaver pilosum var. normale, commonly known as the common poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features striking, bright red to orange flowers with a central crown of stamens, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, often hairy stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually green and slightly pubescent. Its capsule fruit contains numerous tiny, dark seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in opium production.
