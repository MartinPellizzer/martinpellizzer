Panicum carinatum var. procerius

Panicum carinatum var. procerius, commonly known as tall bristlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, hairy inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as fields and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, while its flowering spikes are usually erect and branched.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
