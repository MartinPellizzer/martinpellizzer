Piper pilobracteatum

Piper pilobracteatum, commonly known as the woolly pipevine, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

The fruit is a three-lobed capsule that contains several small, dark seeds.
