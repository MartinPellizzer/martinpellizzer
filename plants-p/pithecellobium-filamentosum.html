Pithecellobium filamentosum

Pithecellobium filamentosum, commonly known as the threadleaf acacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features slender, thread-like leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in racemes. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

The seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and can be used for various traditional purposes.
