Piper parvifolium

Piper parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes many species of flowering plants in the family Piperaceae. The plant is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small, spherical berries that turn red when mature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of aromatic leaves used in traditional medicine.
