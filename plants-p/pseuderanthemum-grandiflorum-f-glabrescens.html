Pseuderanthemum grandiflorum f. glabrescens

Pseuderanthemum grandiflorum f. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-flowered pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically bright yellow or orange. The foliage is generally lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stems. This variety is distinguished by its smooth, glabrous leaves, which give it its specific epithet "glabrescens.".

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
