Peperomia cereoides

Peperomia cereoides, commonly known as the waxleaf peperomia, is a perennial flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and thick, waxy leaves that are typically light green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or pale pink, which are borne on short, erect spikes. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate humidity.

Peperomia cereoides is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
