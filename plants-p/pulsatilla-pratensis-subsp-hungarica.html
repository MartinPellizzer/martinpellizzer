Pulsatilla pratensis subsp. hungarica

Pulsatilla pratensis subsp.

hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its showy, showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

The plant features a short, thick rhizome and produces a basal rosette of hairy, lanceolate leaves. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands, often in temperate regions of Europe.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
