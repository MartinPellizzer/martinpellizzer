Penstemon hesperius

Penstemon hesperius, commonly known as the desert penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Penstemon hesperius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance in xeriscaped gardens.
