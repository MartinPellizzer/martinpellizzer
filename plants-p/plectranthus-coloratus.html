Plectranthus coloratus

Plectranthus coloratus, commonly known as the purple heart plant, is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

It is native to South Africa and features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that range in color from deep purple to green. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers in shades of violet or blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
