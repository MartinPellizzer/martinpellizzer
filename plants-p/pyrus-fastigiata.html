Pyrus fastigiata

Pyrus fastigiata, commonly known as the upright pear.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia, characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically small and sour. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface.

Pyrus fastigiata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
