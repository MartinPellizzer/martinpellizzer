Pilosella lactucella

Pilosella lactucella, commonly known as the milk thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, fuzzy leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties. Its most notable feature is the milky sap that exudes from the stems and leaves when broken.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive plant in some regions.
