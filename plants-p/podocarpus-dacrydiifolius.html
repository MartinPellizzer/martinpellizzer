Podocarpus dacrydiifolius

Podocarpus dacrydiifolius, commonly known as the silver pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its foliage provides habitat for various wildlife, and it is valued for its ornamental and timber qualities.
