Paepalanthus refractifolius

Paepalanthus refractifolius, commonly known as the glassy leaf cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that give it its common name, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces striking yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise desolate habitat. It is a stemless cactus, meaning its photosynthesis occurs primarily through its leaves.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
