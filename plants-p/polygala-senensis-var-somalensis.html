Polygala senensis var. somalensis

Polygala senensis var. somalensis, commonly known as the Somali milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Somalia and Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, hot climates. Its leaves are opposite and often hairy, while its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
