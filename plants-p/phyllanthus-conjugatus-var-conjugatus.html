Phyllanthus conjugatus var. conjugatus

Phyllanthus conjugatus var. conjugatus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, hairy foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It is characterized by its unique leaf structure, with opposite, oval-shaped leaves that have a waxy texture.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
