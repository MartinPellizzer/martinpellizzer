Passiflora tenella

Passiflora tenella, commonly known as the slender passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its climbing growth habit.

Passiflora tenella is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique floral structure and aesthetic appeal.
