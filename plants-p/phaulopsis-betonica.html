Phaulopsis betonica

Phaulopsis betonica, commonly known as betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and spikes of blue or purple flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported calming properties.

The plant's botanical structure supports its role in both ornamental gardening and herbal remedies.
