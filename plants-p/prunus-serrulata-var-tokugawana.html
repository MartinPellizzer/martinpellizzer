Prunus serrulata var. tokugawana

Prunus serrulata var. tokugawana, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or some varieties as the Tokugawa cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often preceding the leaves. The leaves are ovate with serrated edges, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The plant is also valued for its cultural significance in Japan, particularly in traditional flower viewing festivals.
