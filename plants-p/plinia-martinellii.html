Plinia martinellii

Plinia martinellii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia martinellii*, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Plinia martinellii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
