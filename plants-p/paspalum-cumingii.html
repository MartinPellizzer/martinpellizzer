Paspalum cumingii

Paspalum cumingii, commonly known as Cuming's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. It is often found in wetland habitats, including marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions.

Paspalum cumingii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
