Pyrus gonggashanica

Pyrus gonggashanica, commonly known as the Gonggashan pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its deciduous nature, producing small, clustered flowers in the spring. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. Its fruit, a small pear-like fruit, is edible and has a sweet flavor.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
