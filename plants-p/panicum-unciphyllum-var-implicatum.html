Panicum unciphyllum var. implicatum

Panicum unciphyllum var. implicatum, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
