Plantago patagonica var. californica

Plantago patagonica var. californica, commonly known as the California plantain, is a perennial herb native to western North America.

It features broad, oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
