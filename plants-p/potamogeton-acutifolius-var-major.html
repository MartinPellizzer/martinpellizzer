Potamogeton acutifolius var. major

Potamogeton acutifolius var. major, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, which help reduce water resistance as it grows in deep water. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, often floating at the water's surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in ponds and lakes.
