Picris laciniata

Picris laciniata, commonly known as the cut-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, somewhat fuzzy texture.

The flowers are small and yellow, forming dense clusters that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
