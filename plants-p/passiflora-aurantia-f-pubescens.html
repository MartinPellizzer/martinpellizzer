Passiflora aurantia f. pubescens

Passiflora aurantia f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy yellow passionflower, is a variant of the yellow passionflower characterized by its hairy foliage and stems.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with intricate structures, including a crown of filaments and a central corona. Its fruit, a vibrant orange-red passionfruit, is edible and contains numerous seeds.

This cultivar is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and medicinal properties.
