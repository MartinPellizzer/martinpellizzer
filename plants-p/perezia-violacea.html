Perezia violacea

Perezia violacea, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, fragrant blossoms.

Perezia violacea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
