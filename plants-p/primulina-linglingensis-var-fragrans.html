Primulina linglingensis var. fragrans

Primulina linglingensis var. fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant primulina, is a species of flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as mountain forests, where it thrives in the undergrowth. Its fragrant flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
