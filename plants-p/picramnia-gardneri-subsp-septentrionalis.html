Picramnia gardneri subsp. septentrionalis

Picramnia gardneri subsp.

septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern picramnia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. Its fruits are dry, capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

This species is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in woodland or wetland habitats.
