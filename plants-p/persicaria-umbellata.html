Persicaria umbellata

Persicaria umbellata, commonly known as the clustered smartweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and disturbed areas.

Persicaria umbellata is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other species known for their medicinal and ornamental uses.
