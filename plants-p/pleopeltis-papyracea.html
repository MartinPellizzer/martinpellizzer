Pleopeltis papyracea

Pleopeltis papyracea, commonly known as the paper-leaf fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, papery fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a soft, leathery texture.

Pleopeltis papyracea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
