Pleurothallis dryadum

Pleurothallis dryadum, commonly known as the dryad's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate floral structure.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
