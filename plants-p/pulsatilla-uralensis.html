Pulsatilla uralensis

Pulsatilla uralensis, commonly known as the western pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Pulsatilla uralensis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early-blooming flowers.
