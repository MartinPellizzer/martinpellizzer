Pachylepis cupressoides

Pachylepis cupressoides, commonly known as the African cypress, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by woody, cone-like structures that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

The wood of Pachylepis cupressoides is also valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture making.
