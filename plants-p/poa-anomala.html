Poa anomala

Poa anomala, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-haired leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, well-drained environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Poa anomala is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
