Peperomia fortipes var. spathifolia

Peperomia fortipes var. spathifolia, commonly known as the Spatulate Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy, ovate leaves with a distinctive spatulate shape, often adorned with a pale green or white midrib. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color, emerging from the leaf axils. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its slow growth and minimal care requirements, it is well-suited for beginners and space-constrained environments.
