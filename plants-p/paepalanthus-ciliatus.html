Paepalanthus ciliatus

Paepalanthus ciliatus, commonly known as the silky pincushion, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, cushion-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
