Pilosella aurantiaca subsp. porphyranthes

Pilosella aurantiaca subsp.

porphyranthes, commonly known as the purpleleaf globeflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and its striking, globe-shaped flower heads.

The subspecies is distinguished by its deep purple to reddish-purple foliage, which contrasts vividly with its yellowish-white flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
