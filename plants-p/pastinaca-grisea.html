Pastinaca grisea

Pastinaca grisea, commonly known as the gray burdock, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a biennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are composite, consisting of yellow disk flowers surrounded by purple to brown bracts.

The root system produces a thick, fibrous root that is often harvested for its medicinal and culinary uses.
