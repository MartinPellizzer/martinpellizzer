Polycarpon filifolium

Polycarpon filifolium, commonly known as the threadleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves and trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
