Pulsatilla angustifolia subsp. flavescens

Pulsatilla angustifolia subsp.

flavescens, commonly known as the yellow pasqueflower, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, yellow to orange flowers in early spring.

The plant has a short, upright stem with palmately divided, hairy leaves. It is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a preference for well-drained, calcareous soils.
