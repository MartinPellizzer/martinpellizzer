Philodendron hooveri

Philodendron hooveri, commonly known as the "Hoover's Philodendron," is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length, often with a velvety texture and a deep green hue. The plant thrives in high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation. Its unique foliage and relatively easy care requirements have made it a favored ornamental plant among collectors.

Philodendron hooveri is also noted for its ability to climb, using aerial roots to attach itself to supports in its natural habitat.
