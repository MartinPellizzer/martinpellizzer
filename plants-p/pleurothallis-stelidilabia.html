Pleurothallis stelidilabia

Pleurothallis stelidilabia, commonly known as the silver-lipped pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically feature a white or pale pink coloration with a distinctive, elongated labellum. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include narrow, strap-like leaves and a compact inflorescence.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is a subject of interest for orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
