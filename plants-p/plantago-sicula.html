Plantago sicula

Plantago sicula, commonly known as Sicilian plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental or for its medicinal properties.
