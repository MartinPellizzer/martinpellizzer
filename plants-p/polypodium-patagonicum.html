Polypodium patagonicum

Polypodium patagonicum, commonly known as the Patagonian shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its leathery, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizome is thick and forms a dense network, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

The fronds are usually bipinnate, with a distinctive appearance that makes it easily identifiable in its natural habitat.
