Pluchea multiflora

Pluchea multiflora, commonly known as swamp sunflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or marshy habitats. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in wetlands and along waterways.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
