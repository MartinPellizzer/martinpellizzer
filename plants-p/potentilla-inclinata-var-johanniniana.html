Potentilla inclinata var. johanniniana

Potentilla inclinata var. johanniniana, commonly known as the Johanninian shrub, is a low-growing, woody perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
