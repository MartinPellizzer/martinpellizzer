Pittosporum lineare

Pittosporum lineare, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy upper surface and a pale underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Pittosporum lineare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
