Panicum diversinerve

Panicum diversinerve, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces small, fluffy panicles of seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
