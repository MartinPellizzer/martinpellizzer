Piper friedrichsthalii

Piper friedrichsthalii, commonly known as the Friedrichsthal pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped, glossy leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, fleshy berries.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
