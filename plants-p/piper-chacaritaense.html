Piper chacaritaense

Piper chacaritaense, commonly known as the Chacarita pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, peppery fruits that are used in local cuisines.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and culinary contexts.
