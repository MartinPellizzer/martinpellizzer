Piper jagori

Piper jagori, commonly known as the Java pepper or long pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces tiny, peppercorn-like fruits that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its botanical features include a woody vine structure and aromatic foliage.

Piper jagori thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary properties.
