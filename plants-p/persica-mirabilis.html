Persica mirabilis

Persica mirabilis, commonly known as the miraculous peach, is a species of peach native to China.

It is characterized by its large, fuzzy fruits with a sweet and aromatic flavor. The plant produces showy pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of the fruit. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside.

The tree is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its edible fruit.
