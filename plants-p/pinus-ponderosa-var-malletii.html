Pinus ponderosa var. malletii

Pinus ponderosa var. malletii, commonly known as the Mallet's ponderosa pine, is a variant of the ponderosa pine native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, needle-like leaves that are typically lighter in color compared to the standard ponderosa pine. The cones of this variety are generally smaller and more compact, with a reddish-brown hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to certain environmental conditions.
