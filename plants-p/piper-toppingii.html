Piper toppingii

Piper toppingii, commonly known as the Topping's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its vine-like growth habit and small, glossy, dark green leaves. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, slender berries.

The berries are often used in traditional medicine and are known for their pungent, spicy flavor.
