Phlox drummondii var. mcallisteri

Phlox drummondii var. mcallisteri, commonly known as the McAllister's garden phlox, is a compact, flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It produces showy clusters of pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has upright, branching stems covered in soft, fuzzy hairs. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are also hairy.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, low-maintenance blooms.
