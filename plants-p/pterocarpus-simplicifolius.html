Pterocarpus simplicifolius

Pterocarpus simplicifolius, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and compound leaves with trifoliate arrangements. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also listed as a species of concern in some conservation efforts.
