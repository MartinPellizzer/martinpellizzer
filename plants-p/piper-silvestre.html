Piper silvestre

Piper silvestre, commonly known as wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, peppery leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are rich in essential oils and contain compounds such as piperine, which contribute to its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.

Piper silvestre thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
