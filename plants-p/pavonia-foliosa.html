Pavonia foliosa

Pavonia foliosa, commonly known as the leafy pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to red bract-like flowers. The plant features palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, broad shape. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Pavonia foliosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in native plant gardens.
