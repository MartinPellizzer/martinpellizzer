Polygonum muricatum

Polygonum muricatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian spinach, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from red to purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Polygonum muricatum is also valued for its edible leaves, which are used in traditional cuisines.
