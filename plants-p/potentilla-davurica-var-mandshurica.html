Potentilla davurica var. mandshurica

Potentilla davurica var. mandshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian cinquefoil, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in forest edges, slopes, and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are yellow, occurring in clusters. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cold climates.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
