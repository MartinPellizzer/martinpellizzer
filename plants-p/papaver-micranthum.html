Papaver micranthum

Papaver micranthum, commonly known as the lesser poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, nodding flowers with bright orange to reddish petals, typically less than 2 centimeters in diameter. The plant grows from a short, stout rhizome and has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
