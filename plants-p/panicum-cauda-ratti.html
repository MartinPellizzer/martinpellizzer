Panicum cauda-ratti

Panicum cauda-ratti, commonly known as rat's tail grass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping panicles that resemble a rat's tail, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers in the summer.

Panicum cauda-ratti is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
