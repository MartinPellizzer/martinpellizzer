Piper betleoides var. glabrifolium

Piper betleoides var. glabrifolium, commonly known as the smooth-leaved betel vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like the betel leaf (Piper betle). The plant is characterized by its glabrous, lanceolate leaves that emit a strong, spicy aroma when crushed. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark purple berries.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
