Protea tenera

Protea tenera, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of flowers, with a central cone-like structure surrounded by petal-like bracts. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, silver-green leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Protea tenera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine.
