Pepinia rubiginosa

Pepinia rubiginosa, commonly known as the rusty peperomia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a reddish or rust-colored bloom, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its compact size and unique foliage make it an attractive addition to rock gardens or as a houseplant.
