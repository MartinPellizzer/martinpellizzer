Potamogeton malaianus

Potamogeton malaianus, commonly known as the Malayan pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or floating. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton malaianus is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.
