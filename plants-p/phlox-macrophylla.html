Phlox macrophylla

Phlox macrophylla, commonly known as the grandiflora phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, showy clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Phlox macrophylla is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
