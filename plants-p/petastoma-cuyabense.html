Petastoma cuyabense

Petastoma cuyabense, commonly known as the Cuyabeno burrowing plant, is a unique species native to the Amazon rainforest of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its underground stems and specialized leaves adapted for survival in nutrient-poor soils. The plant exhibits a symbiotic relationship with certain fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption. Its flowers are small and clustered, often hidden beneath the soil surface.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
