Phyllocactus vogelii

Phyllocactus vogelii, commonly known as the Vogel's phyllocactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It has a shallow root system adapted to absorb moisture from the soil efficiently.

Phyllocactus vogelii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
