Parapholis cylindrica

Parapholis cylindrica, commonly known as the slender spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and forest edges. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often hairless or sparsely hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spike that bears small, two-ranked spikelets.

Parapholis cylindrica is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
