Poa orthoclada

Poa orthoclada, commonly known as the narrow leaf bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a resilient species in various habitats.

Poa orthoclada is often found in meadows, lawns, and along the edges of water bodies.
