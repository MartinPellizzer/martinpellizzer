Patagonium dichotomum

Patagonium dichotomum, commonly known as the split-leaf fern, is a distinctive plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dichotomous branching pattern, where each stem splits into two equal parts, creating a unique and intricate structure. The plant produces small, delicate fronds that are often feather-like in appearance, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its specialized habitat, Patagonium dichotomum is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
