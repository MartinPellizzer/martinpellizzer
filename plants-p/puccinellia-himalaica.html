Puccinellia himalaica

Puccinellia himalaica, commonly known as Himalayan bulrush, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, thriving in moist to waterlogged soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
