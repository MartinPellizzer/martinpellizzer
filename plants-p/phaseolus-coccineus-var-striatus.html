Phaseolus coccineus var. striatus

Phaseolus coccineus var. striatus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It produces vibrant red or orange flowers and edible pods that mature to a yellow or brown color. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is often grown for its ornamental value as well as its nutritious beans. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun and well-drained soil.

The variety 'striatus' is distinguished by its striped pod patterns, adding to its visual appeal.
