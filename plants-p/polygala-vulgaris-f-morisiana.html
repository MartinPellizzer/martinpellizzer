Polygala vulgaris f. morisiana

Polygala vulgaris f. morisiana, commonly known as the Morisiana milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to certain regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and often have a slight pubescence.

The flowers are usually pale pink or white, arranged in clusters, and are adapted for pollination by insects.
