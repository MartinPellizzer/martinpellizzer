Phacelia heterophylla f. frigida

Phacelia heterophylla f. frigida, commonly known as the frigid phacelia, is a variant of the phacelia genus native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its distinctive blue to violet flower clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, varying in shape depending on the environmental conditions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
