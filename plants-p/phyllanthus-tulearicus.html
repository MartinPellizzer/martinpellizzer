Phyllanthus tulearicus

Phyllanthus tulearicus, commonly known as the Madagascar spurge, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, green, succulent leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences called cyathia. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry, rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant.
