Pyrus chingiana

Pyrus chingiana, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that are similar to pears.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in temperate climates.
