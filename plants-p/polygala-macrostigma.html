Polygala macrostigma

Polygala macrostigma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with a distinctive spurred structure.

It is a perennial herb that produces small, oval-shaped pods containing seeds.
