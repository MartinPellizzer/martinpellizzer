Paphiopedilum philippinense f. alboflavum

Paphiopedilum philippinense f. alboflavum, commonly known as the white-flowered Philippine orchid, is a rare and highly sought-after orchid species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its unique, showy flowers with a distinctive lip that is often pale yellow or white. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, thriving in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually fragrant and can bloom multiple times a year under optimal conditions.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists.
