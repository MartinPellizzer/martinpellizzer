Phaseolus vulgaris var. sulphureovirescens

Phaseolus vulgaris var. sulphureovirescens, commonly known as the golden snake bean, is a variant of the common bean that exhibits unique botanical features.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to golden-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as an annual vine with compound leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its pods are usually slender and contain small, edible beans that are often used in local cuisines.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and potential agricultural significance in certain regions.
