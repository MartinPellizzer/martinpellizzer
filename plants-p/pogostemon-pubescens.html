Pogostemon pubescens

Pogostemon pubescens, commonly known as the hairy pogostemon, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite leaves with a rough texture. It produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

Pogostemon pubescens is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
