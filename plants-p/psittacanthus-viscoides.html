Psittacanthus viscoides

Psittacanthus viscoides, commonly known as the viscous psittacanthus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cycadaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, spherical cones that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
