Populus × canadensis f. brabantica

Populus × canadensis f. brabantica, commonly known as the Brabant poplar, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to Europe.

It is a cultivated form of the white poplar, characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant exhibits a high degree of variability in leaf color, often displaying shades of green, yellow, and even red in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in urban landscaping due to its adaptability and ornamental value.

The Brabant poplar is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to tolerate pollution, making it a popular choice for street trees.
