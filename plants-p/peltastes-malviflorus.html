Peltastes malviflorus

Peltastes malviflorus, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its velvety, hairy foliage and showy, pink to purple flowers. It typically grows in wetlands and disturbed soils, thriving in humid climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
