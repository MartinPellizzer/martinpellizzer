Phialanthus parvifolius

Phialanthus parvifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phialanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, characterized by its small, narrow leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds. It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
