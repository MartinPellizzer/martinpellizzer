Pseudopodospermum libanoticum

Pseudopodospermum libanoticum, commonly known as the Lebanon fig, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and coastal conditions.
