Pogonarthria menyharthii

Pogonarthria menyharthii, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluemink, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms and narrow, linear leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale blue to white florets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
