Persea scortechinii

Persea scortechinii, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, orange to reddish fruits that are edible when fully ripe. Its botanical features include a smooth, greyish-bark trunk and a dense, rounded canopy.

Persea scortechinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
