Picris bracteatus

Picris bracteatus, commonly known as the bracted hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and ragworts. The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are often lobed or toothed. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and can be invasive in certain regions.
