Pentas glabrescens subsp. glabrescens

Pentas glabrescens subsp.

glabrescens, commonly known as the velvet flower or five-finger plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a contrasting yellow center.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture, contributing to its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
