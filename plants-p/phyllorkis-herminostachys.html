Phyllorkis herminostachys

Phyllorkis herminostachys, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to Australia.

This plant is characterized by its vibrant golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the inflorescence consists of a raceme of showy, fragrant flowers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas to support local biodiversity.
