Podolepis canescens var. affinis

Podolepis canescens var. affinis, commonly known as the silverleaf podolepis, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, gray-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure to maintain its vibrant coloration.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid and semi-arid regions.
