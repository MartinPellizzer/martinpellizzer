Phrynium vandenheckei

Phrynium vandenheckei, commonly known as the golden torch plant, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant yellow or green, with a waxy texture. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Phrynium vandenheckei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dramatic appearance and air-purifying properties.
