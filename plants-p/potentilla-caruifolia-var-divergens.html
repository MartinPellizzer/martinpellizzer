Potentilla caruifolia var. divergens

Potentilla caruifolia var. divergens, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to North America.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a fuzzy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in riparian areas.
