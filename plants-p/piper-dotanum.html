Piper dotanum

Piper dotanum, commonly known as the Java pepper or wild pepper, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a climbing habit.

Piper dotanum is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental and medicinal uses.
