Perotis patens

Perotis patens, commonly known as the prairie verbena, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Perotis patens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
