Pelargonium schlechteri

Pelargonium schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb with succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often with a distinctive dark central spot. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a garden plant in various regions with a suitable climate.
