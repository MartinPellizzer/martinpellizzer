Potentilla xanthochlora

Potentilla xanthochlora, commonly known as the yellow cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in early summer and are composed of five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Potentilla xanthochlora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
