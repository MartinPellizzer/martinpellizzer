Pediocactus simpsonii f. flinspachii

Pediocactus simpsonii f. flinspachii, commonly known as the Simpson's cactus, is a rare and striking member of the Cactaceae family.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
