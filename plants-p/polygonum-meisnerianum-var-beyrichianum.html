Polygonum meisnerianum var. beyrichianum

Polygonum meisnerianum var. beyrichianum, commonly known as the Meisner's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants characterized by their distinctive nodes and often edible stems. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in wetlands or along streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer.

It is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions due to its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively.
