Pyrola rossica

Pyrola rossica, commonly known as the Ross's wintergreen, is a perennial herb native to the boreal and subarctic regions of North America and Eurasia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils under the canopy of coniferous forests. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Pyrola rossica is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
