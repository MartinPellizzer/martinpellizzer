Phyllanthus piranii

Phyllanthus piranii, commonly known as Indian cheesewood, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and smooth, often waxy leaves. The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.
