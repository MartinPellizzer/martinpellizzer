Polystachya ramulosa var. angustifolia

Polystachya ramulosa var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
