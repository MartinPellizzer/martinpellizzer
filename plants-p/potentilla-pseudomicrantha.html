Potentilla pseudomicrantha

Potentilla pseudomicrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is native to regions of western North America. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while its stems are often hairy.

Potentilla pseudomicrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
