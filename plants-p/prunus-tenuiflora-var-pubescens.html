Prunus tenuiflora var. pubescens

Prunus tenuiflora var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and have a serrated margin with a hairy underside. It produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits known as cherries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
