Plantago asiatica f. folioscopa

Plantago asiatica f. folioscopa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a variant of the common plantain.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. It is native to Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and as a ground cover in gardens.
