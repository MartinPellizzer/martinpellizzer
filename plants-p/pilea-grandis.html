Pilea grandis

Pilea grandis, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are oval and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. Pilea grandis is a popular indoor plant due to its ease of care and aesthetic appeal.

It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
