Porochna davilliflora

Porochna davilliflora, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes many species with milky sap. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with five petals and a distinctive spurred structure.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dry seeds.
