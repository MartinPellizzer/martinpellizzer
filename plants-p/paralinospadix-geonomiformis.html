Paralinospadix geonomiformis

Paralinospadix geonomiformis, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a unique species belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that resemble ghostly apparitions, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizome is slender and often covered in a fine, white coating.

Botanically, it is notable for its intricate sporangium arrangement and its role in maintaining forest biodiversity.
