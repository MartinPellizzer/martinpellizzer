Pittierella calcarata

Pittierella calcarata, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in arid and rocky environments, making it well adapted to dry conditions. Its leaves are typically thick and waxy, which helps reduce water loss.

Pittierella calcarata is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
