Pimentus aromatica

Pimentus aromatica, commonly known as the aromatic piment, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its fragrant leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves, are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

Pimentus aromatica is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and indoor spaces.
