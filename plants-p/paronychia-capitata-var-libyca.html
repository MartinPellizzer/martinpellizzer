Paronychia capitata var. libyca

Paronychia capitata var. libyca, commonly known as the Libyan sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and limited rainfall.

Its unique morphology makes it well-suited for xerophytic habitats and it is often found in rocky or sandy terrains.
