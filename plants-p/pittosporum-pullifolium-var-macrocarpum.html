Pittosporum pullifolium var. macrocarpum

Pittosporum pullifolium var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers followed by large, orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers a sunny to partially shaded location.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
