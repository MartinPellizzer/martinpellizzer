Polygonum japonicum f. brevistylum

Polygonum japonicum f. brevistylum, commonly known as Japanese knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features upright, bamboo-like stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular subject in horticulture and ecological studies.
