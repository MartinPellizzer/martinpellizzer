Pycnophyllopsis laevis

Pycnophyllopsis laevis, commonly known as the smooth frond fern, is a distinctive member of the fern family, characterized by its dense, clustered fronds.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions, often found in humid, shaded environments. The plant exhibits a unique structure with tightly packed, leathery leaves that form a compact rosette. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are typically located on the undersides of the fronds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
