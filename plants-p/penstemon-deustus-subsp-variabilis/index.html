Penstemon Deustus Subsp. Variabilis

Variable beardtongue, scientifically known as Penstemon deustus subsp. variabilis, is a strikingly unique wildflower native to the western United States. This subspecies is noted for its variable flower colors and shapes, making it a favorite among botanists and gardeners alike. It thrives in rocky habitats and dry slopes, adapting well to harsh environmental conditions. Its vibrant blooms, ranging from purple to pink and even white, add a splash of color to the landscape during late spring and summer. Due to its ecological importance and aesthetic appeal, it is a valuable plant for conservation and native landscaping efforts.
