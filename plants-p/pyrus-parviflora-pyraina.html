Pyrus parviflora pyraina

Pyrus parviflora pyraina, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It features small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, yellow or golden fruits that are edible but not commonly cultivated for consumption. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge.
