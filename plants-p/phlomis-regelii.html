Phlomis regelii

Phlomis regelii, commonly known as Russian sage, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and striking lavender-pink flower spikes that bloom in summer. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Phlomis regelii is also valued for its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
