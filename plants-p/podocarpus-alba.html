Podocarpus alba

Podocarpus alba, commonly known as the white podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture and reforestation projects.
