Pouteria furcata

Pouteria furcata, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces an edible fruit known as a "brazil nut," which contains seeds used for culinary and medicinal purposes. Pouteria furcata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
