Persea longipedunculata

Persea longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-stemmed persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by edible, orange-yellow fruits. Its bark is thick and aromatic, often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
