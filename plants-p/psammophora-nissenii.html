Psammophora nissenii

Psammophora nissenii, commonly known as the desert lichen, is a unique symbiotic organism composed of a fungus and a green alga.

It thrives in arid and rocky environments, particularly in desert regions where it forms a crust-like structure on soil surfaces. The plant's ability to survive extreme drought conditions is due to its efficient water retention and photosynthetic capabilities. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in harsh climates.

Its simple yet resilient structure makes it an important indicator of environmental conditions in desert ecosystems.
