Piper pearcei

Piper pearcei, commonly known as the Sierra Madre pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Mexico and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, peppery fruits. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its botanical features include a climbing or shrub-like growth habit and a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Piper pearcei is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
