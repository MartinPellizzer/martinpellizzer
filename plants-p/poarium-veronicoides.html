Poarium veronicoides

Poarium veronicoides, commonly known as the silverleaf veronica, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The leaves are typically silver or gray-green, giving the plant its common name, and they are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
