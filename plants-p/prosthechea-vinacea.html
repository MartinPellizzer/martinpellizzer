Prosthechea vinacea

Prosthechea vinacea, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red-tipped leaves and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees in tropical rainforests.

Prosthechea vinacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
