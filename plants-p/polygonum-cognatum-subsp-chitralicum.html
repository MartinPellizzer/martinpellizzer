Polygonum cognatum subsp. chitralicum

Polygonum cognatum subsp.

chitralicum, commonly known as the Chitral knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of northern Pakistan and parts of Central Asia. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and often triangular stems.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological roles in high-altitude ecosystems.
