Pyrus aperta

Pyrus aperta, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, yellowish-green fruits. Pyrus aperta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
