Perilla setoyensis

Perilla setoyensis, commonly known as the Japanese perilla, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple, and it produces small, pale purple flowers. Perilla setoyensis is valued for its culinary uses, particularly in traditional Asian cuisines, where its leaves are used as a seasoning.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.
