Pulsatilla × mathildae

Pulsatilla × mathildae, commonly known as the hybrid pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Pulsatilla vulgaris and Pulsatilla patens, resulting in a plant with distinctive features from both parents. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a central tuft of stamens. Its leaves are palmately compound, consisting of several oval to lance-shaped leaflets.

Pulsatilla × mathildae is native to Europe and thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils, often found in meadows and rocky habitats.
