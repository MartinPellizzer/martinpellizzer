Pseudolysimachion rotundum subsp. coreanum

Pseudolysimachion rotundum subsp.

coreanum, commonly known as the Korean roundleaf lythrum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia. It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
