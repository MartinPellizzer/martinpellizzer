Panicum scariosum

Panicum scariosum, commonly known as the scarious panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery bloom. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.

Panicum scariosum is a hardy, drought-tolerant species that plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects.
