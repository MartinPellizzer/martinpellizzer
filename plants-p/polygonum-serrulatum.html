Polygonum serrulatum

Polygonum serrulatum, commonly known as the serrated knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems that are often reddish in color. The leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red in color. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, stream banks, and marshes.

The plant has a robust root system and is known for its ability to spread rapidly in suitable habitats.
