Pourouma garciana

Pourouma garciana, commonly known as the pink river bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height, forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

This plant is often found in wetland habitats and is adapted to thrive in humid, tropical environments.
