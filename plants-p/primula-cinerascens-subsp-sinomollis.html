Primula cinerascens subsp. sinomollis

Primula cinerascens subsp.

sinomollis, commonly known as the Chinese primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to China. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a hairy texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers in early spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
