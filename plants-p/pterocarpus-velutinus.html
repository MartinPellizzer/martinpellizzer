Pterocarpus velutinus

Pterocarpus velutinus, commonly known as the Malacca ebony, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic and commercial properties. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody pods containing flat, dark seeds. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The leaves are trifoliate, with three elliptical leaflets that are hairy beneath.
