Piper hirtulum

Piper hirtulum, commonly known as the hairy pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
