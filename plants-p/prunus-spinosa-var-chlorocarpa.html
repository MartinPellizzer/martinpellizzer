Prunus spinosa var. chlorocarpa

Prunus spinosa var. chlorocarpa, commonly known as the green-fruited blackthorn, is a variant of the blackthorn shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and small, dark green leaves that remain on the plant throughout the winter. The fruit, which is typically a dark purple or black drupe, is notable for its high content of bioactive compounds and is used in traditional medicine. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
