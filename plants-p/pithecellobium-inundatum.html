Pithecellobium inundatum

Pithecellobium inundatum, commonly known as the water acacia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in wet, swampy environments, which is reflected in its common name. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod and are often dispersed by water, contributing to its natural distribution.

The plant also has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it valuable for soil enrichment in ecological restoration projects.
