Pelargonium × nervosum

Pelargonium × nervosum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often adorned with fine, parallel veins, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically with five petals and a prominent central stigma. It is native to South Africa and is commonly cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.

Pelargonium × nervosum prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens and container planting.
