Pogostemon hortensis

Pogostemon hortensis, commonly known as the Japanese mint or shiso, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its broad, slightly serrated leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its fragrant leaves emit a strong, minty aroma when crushed, and it produces small, pale blue to purple flowers.

Pogostemon hortensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and ornamental value.
