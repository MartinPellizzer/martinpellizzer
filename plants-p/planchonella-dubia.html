Planchonella dubia

Planchonella dubia, commonly known as the Saddleback hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub with simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, which are typically borne in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dry seeds.

Planchonella dubia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its attractive foliage and blooms.
