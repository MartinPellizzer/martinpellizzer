Pelargonium ovale var. veronicifolium

Pelargonium ovale var. veronicifolium, commonly known as the Veronica-leaved geranium, is a cultivar characterized by its distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a finely serrated edge, resembling the leaves of the plant Veronica. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in summer. It is a popular choice for ornamental use in gardens and containers due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance.

This variety thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
