Prunus cyclamina var. cyclamina

Prunus cyclamina var. cyclamina, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or sakura, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, single or double pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn a vibrant yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, hard black fruits that contain one or two seeds.

It is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and holds cultural significance in Japan.
