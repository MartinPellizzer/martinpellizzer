Pyrus gergeriana var. macrophylla

Pyrus gergeriana var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf pear, is a variant of the Gerger pear native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 15 cm in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are similar to common pears in taste and texture. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

The species is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
