Pentameris calcicola

Pentameris calcicola, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pentameris calcicola plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
