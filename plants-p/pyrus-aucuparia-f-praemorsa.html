Pyrus aucuparia f. praemorsa

Pyrus aucuparia f. praemorsa, commonly known as the white-flowered crabapple, is a cultivar of the common crabapple tree.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its fruit, which is edible but tart, matures to a yellow or red color in late summer.

This ornamental tree is valued for its ornamental flowers and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
