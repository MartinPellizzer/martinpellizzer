Pleurothallis cucullata

Pleurothallis cucullata, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-like flower structure, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are usually small and come in a range of colors, including white, yellow, and pink.

Pleurothallis cucullata is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
