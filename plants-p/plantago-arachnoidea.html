Plantago arachnoidea

Plantago arachnoidea, commonly known as the spider plant or spidergrass, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiky leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It has a rosette growth form, with leaves radiating outward from a central point.

The plant is noted for its ability to tolerate drought conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
