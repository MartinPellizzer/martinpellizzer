Phyllostegia serrata

Phyllostegia serrata, commonly known as the serrated phyllostegia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Phyllostegia serrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
