Pyrus complexa

Pyrus complexa, commonly known as the Pacific golden apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its yellow fruits and deciduous leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are small and white, appearing in clusters, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The fruit, while edible, is not commonly consumed due to its tart flavor and small size.
