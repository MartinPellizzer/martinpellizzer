Pleioblastus ohmiensis

Pleioblastus ohmiensis, commonly known as the Ohmi bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, dark green leaves. The plant forms dense clumps and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its elegant appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Pleioblastus ohmiensis is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
