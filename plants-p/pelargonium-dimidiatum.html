Pelargonium dimidiatum

Pelargonium dimidiatum, commonly known as the Zebra Geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crinkled leaves with a mottled pattern resembling a zebra's stripes. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Pelargonium dimidiatum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
