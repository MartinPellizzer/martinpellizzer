Paspalum membranaceum var. inaequiglume

Paspalum membranaceum var. inaequiglume, commonly known as the narrowleaf paspalum, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like blades and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and marshes. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
