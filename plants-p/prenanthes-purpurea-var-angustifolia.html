Prenanthes purpurea var. angustifolia

Prenanthes purpurea var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues along the veins. The plant produces tall, spiky inflorescences with tubular purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, rich soils and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
