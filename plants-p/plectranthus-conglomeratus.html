Plectranthus conglomeratus

Plectranthus conglomeratus, commonly known as the clustered plectranthus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of violet or purple. The plant has aromatic leaves that emit a strong scent when crushed, which is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a versatile plant for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
