Persea sikkimensis

Persea sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that contain a single seed, typical of the Lauraceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in dense forests at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
