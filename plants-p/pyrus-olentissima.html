Pyrus olentissima

Pyrus olentissima, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The tree produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

The plant is also used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
