Plantago subulata

Plantago subulata, commonly known as the needleleaf plantain, is a species of flowering plant in the family Plantaginaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its narrow, pointed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated.

It is often used in traditional medicine and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
