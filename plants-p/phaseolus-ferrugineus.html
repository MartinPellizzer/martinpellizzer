Phaseolus ferrugineus

Phaseolus ferrugineus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It produces vibrant red flowers and long, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is characterized by its vigorous growth habit. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
