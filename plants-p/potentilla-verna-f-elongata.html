Potentilla verna f. elongata

Potentilla verna f. elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its foliage consists of opposite, pinnately compound leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
