Penstemon richardsonii subsp. dentatus

Penstemon richardsonii subsp.

dentatus, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The subspecies is distinguished by its serrated leaf margins, which give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.
