Portlandia albiflora

Portlandia albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered portlandia, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the pale flowers.

Portlandia albiflora is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained, acidic soils.
