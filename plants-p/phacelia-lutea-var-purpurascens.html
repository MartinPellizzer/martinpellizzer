Phacelia lutea var. purpurascens

Phacelia lutea var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purplestem phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that often display a reddish-purple hue, especially under stress or in full sun. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow to golden flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
