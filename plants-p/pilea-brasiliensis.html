Pilea brasiliensis

Pilea brasiliensis, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its round, rosette-like leaves that resemble coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are typically hidden within a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It is a popular indoor plant due to its ease of care and attractive foliage.

Pilea brasiliensis thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a favored choice for houseplants.
