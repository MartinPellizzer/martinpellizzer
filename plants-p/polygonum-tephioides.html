Polygonum tephioides

Polygonum tephioides, commonly known as the golden knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink to purple, blooming in late summer.

Polygonum tephioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
