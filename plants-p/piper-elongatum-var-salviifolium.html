Piper elongatum var. salviifolium

Piper elongatum var. salviifolium, commonly known as the sacred pepper or salvia-leaved pepper, is a variant of the Piper elongatum species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive salvia-like appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into peppery, aromatic berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its unique flavor profile in culinary applications.

Its botanical features make it a valuable subject in both horticulture and ethnobotanical studies.
