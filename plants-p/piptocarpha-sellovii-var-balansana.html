Piptocarpha sellovii var. balansana

Piptocarpha sellovii var. balansana, commonly known as the Balansana thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
