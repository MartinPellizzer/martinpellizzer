Piper baccans

Piper baccans, commonly known as the sacred pepper or black pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, berry-like fruits. These fruits, when dried, are used as a spice and have a pungent, peppery flavor.

The plant's botanical features make it well adapted to humid, shaded environments in tropical regions.
