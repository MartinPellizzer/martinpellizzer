Phlomis nepetifolia

Phlomis nepetifolia, commonly known as cat's ear or woolly mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust, upright stem covered in dense, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, with a slightly fuzzy surface.

Phlomis nepetifolia is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy addition to xeriscapic gardens.
