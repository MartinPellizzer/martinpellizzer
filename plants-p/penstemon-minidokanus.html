Penstemon minidokanus

Penstemon minidokanus, commonly known as the narrowleaf penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and dry environments, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Penstemon minidokanus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
