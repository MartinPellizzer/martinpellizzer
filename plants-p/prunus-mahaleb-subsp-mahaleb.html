Prunus mahaleb subsp. mahaleb

Prunus mahaleb subsp.

mahaleb, commonly known as cherry laurel, is a species of shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often considered bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The subspecies is distinguished by its compact growth habit and is closely related to other Prunus species such as the common cherry.
