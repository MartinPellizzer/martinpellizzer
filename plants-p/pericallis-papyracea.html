Pericallis papyracea

Pericallis papyracea, commonly known as the paper-leaf orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, papery-textured leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often arranged in a spike-like cluster. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Pericallis papyracea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
