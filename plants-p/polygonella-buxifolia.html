Polygonella buxifolia

Polygonella buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf polygonella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants commonly known as buckwheats. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy layer. Polygonella buxifolia typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
