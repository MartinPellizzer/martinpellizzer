Piptocoma spruceana

Piptocoma spruceana, commonly known as the Spruce's pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Pittosporaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are usually white or pale yellow.

Piptocoma spruceana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
