Paspalum protensum

Paspalum protensum, commonly known as slender grass or slender paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Paspalum protensum is often found in disturbed areas, grasslands, and along roadsides.
