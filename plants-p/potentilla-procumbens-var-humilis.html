Potentilla procumbens var. humilis

Potentilla procumbens var. humilis, commonly known as creeping potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has slender, spreading stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to form dense mats. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
