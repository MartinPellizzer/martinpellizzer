Plectranthus subincisus

Plectranthus subincisus, commonly known as Cape verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, which bloom from late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and long-lasting blooms.
