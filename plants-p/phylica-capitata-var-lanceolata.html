Phylica capitata var. lanceolata

Phylica capitata var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved silver bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, giving the plant its specific variety name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for coastal and inland gardens.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
