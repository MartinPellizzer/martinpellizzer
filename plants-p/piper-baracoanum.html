Piper baracoanum

Piper baracoanum, commonly known as the Bornean pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in greenish-yellow clusters. Its fruits are initially green and turn red when mature, resembling tiny peppers.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
