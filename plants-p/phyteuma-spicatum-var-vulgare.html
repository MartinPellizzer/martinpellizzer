Phyteuma spicatum var. vulgare

Phyteuma spicatum var. vulgare, commonly known as the common phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing pink to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

Phyteuma spicatum var. vulgare is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
