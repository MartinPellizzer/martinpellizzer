Papaver rhoeas var. glabellum

Papaver rhoeas var. glabellum, commonly known as the glabellum poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its smooth, hairless stem and leaves.

It produces striking red or orange flowers with a distinctive, rounded seed capsule. This plant is typically found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as fields and roadsides. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The glabellum poppy is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
