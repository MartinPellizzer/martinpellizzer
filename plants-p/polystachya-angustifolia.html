Polystachya angustifolia

Polystachya angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf polystachya, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and undergrowth. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is occasionally grown in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
