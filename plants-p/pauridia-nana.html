Pauridia nana

Pauridia nana, commonly known as the dwarf pauridia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical regions.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and shallow, allowing it to thrive in well-drained, moist soils.

Pauridia nana is often found in shaded, humid environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.
