Psoralea plumosa

Psoralea plumosa, commonly known as the feathery psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Psoralea plumosa is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
