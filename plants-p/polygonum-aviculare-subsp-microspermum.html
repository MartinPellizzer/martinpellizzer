Polygonum aviculare subsp. microspermum

Polygonum aviculare subsp.

microspermum, commonly known as the small-seeded smartweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas, and thrives in moist to wet environments.

The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a hairy surface, and its small, clustered flowers are usually pink to red in color. It produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to spread quickly.

The species is characterized by its short stature and rapid growth cycle, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
