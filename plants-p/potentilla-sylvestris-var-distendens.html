Potentilla sylvestris var. distendens

Potentilla sylvestris var. distendens, commonly known as the broadleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, compound leaves with five leaflets, which are typically ovate to lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
