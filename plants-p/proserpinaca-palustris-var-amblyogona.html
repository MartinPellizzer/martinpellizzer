Proserpinaca palustris var. amblyogona

Proserpinaca palustris var. amblyogona, commonly known as swamp spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical structure is adapted for aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions, with rhizomes that help in anchoring and nutrient absorption.
