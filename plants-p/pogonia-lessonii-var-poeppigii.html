Pogonia lessonii var. poeppigii

Pogonia lessonii var. poeppigii, commonly known as the Poeppig's pink.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its striking pink and white flowers with a distinctive hooded labellum. Native to the southeastern United States, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as pine flatwoods and wetlands. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, fragrant blooms during the spring.

Its conservation status is threatened due to habitat loss and over-collection.
