Passiflora araujoi

Passiflora araujoi, commonly known as the Araujo passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with prominent crimson filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Passiflora araujoi is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
