Polygala nicaeensis var. pubescens

Polygala nicaeensis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically alternate and have a hairy texture, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
