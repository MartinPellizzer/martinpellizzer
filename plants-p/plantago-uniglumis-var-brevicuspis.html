Plantago uniglumis var. brevicuspis

Plantago uniglumis var. brevicuspis, commonly known as the short-spiculate plantain, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It features a rosette of broad, ovate leaves with a hairy underside and a short, terminal spike of small, greenish flowers. The plant produces capsules that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
