Piper tumidicondyli

Piper tumidicondyli, commonly known as the swollen-venter pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of small, heart-shaped leaves. Its fruits are clustered in dense, elongated spikes and are typically used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Piper tumidicondyli make it an interesting subject for studies in plant morphology and ethnobotany.
