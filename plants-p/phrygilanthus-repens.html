Phrygilanthus repens

Phrygilanthus repens, commonly known as the creeping phrygilanthus, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its stems are succulent and can store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
