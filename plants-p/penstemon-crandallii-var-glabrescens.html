Penstemon crandallii var. glabrescens

Penstemon crandallii var. glabrescens, commonly known as Crandall's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often glabrous, giving the variety its name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
