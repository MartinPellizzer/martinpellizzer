Polygala amara subsp. amblyptera

Polygala amara subsp.

amblyptera, commonly known as the bitter milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with a distinctive bitter taste due to the presence of alkaloids.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest in ecological and pharmacological studies.
