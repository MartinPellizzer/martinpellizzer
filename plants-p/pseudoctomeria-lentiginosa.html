Pseudoctomeria lentiginosa

Pseudoctomeria lentiginosa, commonly known as the spotted candelabra cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its branched, tree-like structure with multiple stems that bear clusters of small, green, spiny leaves. The plant produces striking yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its thick, water-storing stems.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
