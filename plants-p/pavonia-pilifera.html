Pavonia pilifera

Pavonia pilifera, commonly known as the silky pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, silky foliage and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and along roadsides, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its slender, branching stems are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Pavonia pilifera is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its delicate beauty.
