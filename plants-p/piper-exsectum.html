Piper exsectum

Piper exsectum, commonly known as the fragrant pepper vine, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits that mature to a dark purple color.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
