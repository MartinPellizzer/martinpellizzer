Piratinera discolor

Piratinera discolor, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple bell-shaped flowers and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, white or grayish pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Piratinera discolor is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
