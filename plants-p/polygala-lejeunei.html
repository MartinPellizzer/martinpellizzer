Polygala lejeunei

Polygala lejeunei, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are typically arranged in spikes, and it has narrow, elongated leaves that are often slightly hairy. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or inland grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
