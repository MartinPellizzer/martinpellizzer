Paeonia morisii

Paeonia morisii, commonly known as the Morisii peony, is a species native to China.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its showy, double flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with large, palmately compound leaves. Its root system consists of thick, fleshy rhizomes, which are essential for its survival and propagation.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens and landscapes.
