Polygonum sieboldii var. aesticum

Polygonum sieboldii var. aesticum, commonly known as the Japanese knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features upright stems that are often reddish in color and bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, appearing in late summer. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
