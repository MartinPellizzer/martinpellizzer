Penstemon gairdneri subsp. oreganus

Penstemon gairdneri subsp.

oreganus, commonly known as the Oregon penstemon, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands and meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
