Phyllodoce × erecta

Phyllodoce × erecta, commonly known as the tall mountain moss, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly waxy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually borne in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Phyllodoce × erecta is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various woodland organisms.
