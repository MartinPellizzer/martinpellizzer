Phyllostachys propinqua

Phyllostachys propinqua, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to East Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. This plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green. It grows in dense clumps and is well-suited for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Phyllostachys propinqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions.
