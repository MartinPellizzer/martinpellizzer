Pediocactus conoideus

Pediocactus conoideus, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a tall, cylindrical stem and typically has 10 to 20 ribs. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are borne in clusters at the top of the stem. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil and minimal water.

Pediocactus conoideus is often cultivated for its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
