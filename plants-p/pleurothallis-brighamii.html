Pleurothallis brighamii

Pleurothallis brighamii, commonly known as Brigham's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant typically produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a fragrant scent. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by enthusiasts, though it is not widely commercialized.
