Patagonium macrostachyum

Patagonium macrostachyum, commonly known as the Patagonian giant feathergrass, is a tall, perennial grass native to the high-altitude regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves and dense, feathery inflorescences that can reach several meters in height. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to withstand strong winds and low nutrient soils. Its fibrous roots help stabilize the soil, making it an important component of the local ecosystem.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is often studied for its potential in restoration ecology and sustainable land management.
