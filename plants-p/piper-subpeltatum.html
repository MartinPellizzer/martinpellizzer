Piper subpeltatum

Piper subpeltatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature, containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its botanical structure includes opposite leaf arrangement and a distinct, ridged stem.

Piper subpeltatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
