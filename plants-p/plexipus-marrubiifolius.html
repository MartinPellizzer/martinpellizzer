Plexipus marrubiifolius

Plexipus marrubiifolius, commonly known as the Marrubiifolius plant, is a perennial herbaceous species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
