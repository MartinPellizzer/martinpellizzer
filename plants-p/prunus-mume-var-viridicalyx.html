Prunus mume var. viridicalyx

Prunus mume var. viridicalyx, commonly known as the green-leafed Chinese plum, is a variant of the Japanese apricot.

It is characterized by its distinctive dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It is native to China and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.

The leaves remain green throughout the growing season, distinguishing it from other Prunus species.
