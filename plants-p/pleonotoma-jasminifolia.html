Pleonotoma jasminifolia

Pleonotoma jasminifolia, commonly known as the jasmine leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that resemble jasmine. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture.

The flowers bloom in clusters, producing a sweet aroma that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
