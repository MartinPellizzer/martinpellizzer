Plagiocheilus soliviformis subsp. soliviformis

Plagiocheilus soliviformis subsp.

soliviformis, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive lip that is often spurred. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
