Paragonia pyramidata var. tomentosa

Paragonia pyramidata var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow center. The plant has a woody stem and produces leaves that are covered in a dense, woolly layer of fine hairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
