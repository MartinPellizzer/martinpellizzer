Potentilla sarmentosa

Potentilla sarmentosa, commonly known as silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has wiry, sprawling stems covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs.

Silverweed is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.
