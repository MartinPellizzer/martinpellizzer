Pilosella fallacina

Pilosella fallacina, commonly known as the false hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually hairy and alternate, while the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Pilosella fallacina is often considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and ability to colonize new areas.
