Persica vulgaris var. cydonia

Persica vulgaris var. cydonia, commonly known as the Chinese sand pear.

This variety is a cultivar of the domesticated pear, characterized by its small, round fruits with a rough, yellowish skin. It is native to China and is often grown for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit. The plant typically thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its leaves are broad and lobed, and it produces white flowers in the spring.
