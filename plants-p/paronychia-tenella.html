Paronychia tenella

Paronychia tenella, commonly known as the silverleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and rocky environments in southern Africa.

It features small, fleshy, silver-gray leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry conditions. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, four-petaled flowers during the spring and summer months. Its root system is fibrous and helps it anchor in loose, sandy soils.

Paronychia tenella is often found in gravelly or rocky habitats, where it thrives in full sun.
