Prunus domestica f. pomariorum

Prunus domestica f. pomariorum, commonly known as the Italian prune or the Tuscan prune, is a fruiting shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a compact, bushy plant that produces small, dark purple to black fruits resembling plums. The fruit is typically harvested when fully ripe, and it is valued for its intense flavor and high sugar content. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its edible fruits.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.
