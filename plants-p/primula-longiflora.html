Primula longiflora

Primula longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, tubular flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The leaves are typically oval and slightly hairy, with a dark green color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
