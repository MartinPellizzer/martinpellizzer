Yucca × virgilius

Yucca × virgilius, commonly known as the Virgin's yucca, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Yucca flaccida and Yucca tenuifolia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces panicles of white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It is native to the southwestern United States and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Yucca × virgilius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
