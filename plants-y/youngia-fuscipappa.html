Youngia fuscipappa

Youngia fuscipappa, commonly known as the brown-veined dandelion, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, fuscous-green leaves and a single, short stem that supports a single flower head. The flowers are typically yellow and are followed by a spherical, pappus-equipped seed head, typical of the genus Youngia. It is native to eastern Asia and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

The plant is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
