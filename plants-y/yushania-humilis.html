Yushania humilis

Yushania humilis, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and glossy. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories. Yushania humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to form dense clumps over time.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for use in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
