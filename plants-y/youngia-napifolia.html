Youngia napifolia

Youngia napifolia, commonly known as the Japanese yarrow, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its flowers, which bloom in the summer, are usually pale yellow to white and attract various pollinators.

Youngia napifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in partial shade.
