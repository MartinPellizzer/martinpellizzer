Youngia mauritiana

Youngia mauritiana, commonly known as the Mauritius dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Mascarene Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Youngia mauritiana is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils.
