Yushania vicina

Yushania vicina, commonly known as the narrowleaf yushania, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

Yushania vicina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic qualities.
