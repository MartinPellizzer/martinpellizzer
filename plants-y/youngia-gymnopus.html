Youngia gymnopus

Youngia gymnopus, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single flower head. The flower head consists of yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular florets, which develop into fluffy white seeds with pappus for wind dispersal. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in disturbed areas, lawns, and along roadsides.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
