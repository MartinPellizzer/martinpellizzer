Yucca × andreana

Yucca × andreana, commonly known as the hybrid yucca, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and large, sword-shaped leaves that are covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by blue-gray berries. Yucca × andreana is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and striking appearance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in arid regions.
