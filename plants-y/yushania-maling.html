Yushania maling

Yushania maling, commonly known as the mountain bamboo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments such as mountain forests and grasslands. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Yushania maling plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
