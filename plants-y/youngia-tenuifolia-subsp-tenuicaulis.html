Youngia tenuifolia subsp. tenuicaulis

Youngia tenuifolia subsp.

tenuicaulis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed yarrow, is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its finely divided, grass-like leaves. It produces small, white to pale yellow composite flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The plant is native to regions of East Asia, including China and Korea, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and other damp habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
