Ypsilopus graminifolius

Ypsilopus graminifolius, commonly known as the slender ypsilopus, is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered together.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses and related plants.
