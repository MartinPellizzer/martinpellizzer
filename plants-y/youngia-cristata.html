Youngia cristata

Youngia cristata, commonly known as the crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its daisy-like appearance with yellow flowers and a distinctive crown-shaped center. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands and woodland edges. It has small, narrow leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

Youngia cristata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
