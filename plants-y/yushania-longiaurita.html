Yushania longiaurita

Yushania longiaurita, commonly known as the long-tailed bamboo, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and long, narrow leaves that often have a bluish-green hue. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, where it forms dense clumps. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Yushania longiaurita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
