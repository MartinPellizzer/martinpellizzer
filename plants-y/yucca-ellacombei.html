Yucca ellacombei

Yucca ellacombei, commonly known as the Desert Yucca, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating. The plant produces large, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Yucca ellacombei plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
