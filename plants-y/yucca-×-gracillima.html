Yucca × gracillima

Yucca × gracillima, commonly known as the narrowleaf yucca, is a hybrid plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow, evergreen leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating. The plant produces large, white, bell-shaped flowers in the summer, which are typically clustered in a panicle inflorescence. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Yucca × gracillima is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
