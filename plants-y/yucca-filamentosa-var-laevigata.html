Yucca filamentosa var. laevigata

Yucca filamentosa var. laevigata, commonly known as the stiffleaf yucca, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, narrow, evergreen leaves that are stiff and smooth, giving it its varietal name "laevigata.". The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and architectural form make it a popular choice for ornamental landscapes.
