Yucca flexilis f. patens

Yucca flexilis f. patens, commonly known as the silverleaf yucca, is a distinctive plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, silver-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery bloom. The plant produces large, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique foliage and ornamental value make it a popular specimen in arid landscape designs.
