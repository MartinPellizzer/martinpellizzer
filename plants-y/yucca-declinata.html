Yucca declinata

Yucca declinata, commonly known as the drooping yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow, bluish-green leaves that droop gracefully from the plant. The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by cylindrical, woody fruits. Yucca declinata is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
