Yucca parmentieri

Yucca parmentieri, commonly known as the Parmentier yucca, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, stiff, sword-shaped leaves that are covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with clusters of white to pale blue flowers, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and striking appearance make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.
