Yucca baileyi subsp. intermedia

Yucca baileyi subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the intermountain yucca, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and sword-shaped, gray-green leaves that are arranged in a dense rosette.

The plant produces white, bell-shaped flowers on long, branched inflorescences during the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The species is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
