Yucca baccata var. baccata

Yucca baccata var. baccata, commonly known as the banana yucca, is a shrub-like plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features tall, stiff, sword-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
