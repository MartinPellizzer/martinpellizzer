Yushania glandulosa

Yushania glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular yushania, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive glandular surface. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments such as forests and meadows at high altitudes. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often covered in fine hairs.

Yushania glandulosa plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
