Yucca aloifolia var. quadricolor-variegata

Yucca aloifolia var. quadricolor-variegata, commonly known as the variegated yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features strikingly colorful foliage with variegated leaves that display shades of green, white, and yellow. The plant is evergreen, with sword-like leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length. It produces tall, branched clusters of white or pale blue flowers in the spring.

This variety is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
