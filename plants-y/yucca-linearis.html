Yucca linearis

Yucca linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, sword-shaped leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months, which are pollinated by bats and insects. Yucca linearis is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its architectural form and unique appearance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
