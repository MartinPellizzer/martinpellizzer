Yushania longispiculatus

Yushania longispiculatus, commonly known as the long-spined yushania, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in forest understories or on rocky slopes.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
