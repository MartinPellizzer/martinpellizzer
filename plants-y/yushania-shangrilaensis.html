Yushania shangrilaensis

Yushania shangrilaensis, commonly known as the Shangrila yushania, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of Yunnan Province in China, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant typically grows in mountainous areas with well-drained soil, often found in forest understories or on rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dense, tufted growth habit that contributes to its ornamental appeal.

Yushania shangrilaensis is valued for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ecological and aesthetic significance.
