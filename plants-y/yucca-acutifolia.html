Yucca acutifolia

Yucca acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf yucca, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, stiff, grass-like leaves that are sharp and pointed, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking white or pale yellow flowers in clusters at the end of its stems during the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-drained soil.

Yucca acutifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
