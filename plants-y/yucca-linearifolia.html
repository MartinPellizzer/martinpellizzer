Yucca linearifolia

Yucca linearifolia, commonly known as the linear-leaf yucca, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces striking white or pale yellow flowers in panicles during the summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Yucca linearifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
