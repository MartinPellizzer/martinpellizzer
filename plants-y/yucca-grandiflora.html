Yucca grandiflora

Yucca grandiflora, commonly known as the largeflower yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features tall, sword-like leaves that are blue-green in color and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss. Its showy, white or pale yellow flowers bloom in summer, forming pendulous clusters at the ends of the stems.

Yucca grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its resilience and unique appearance.
