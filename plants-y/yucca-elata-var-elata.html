Yucca elata var. elata

Yucca elata var. elata, commonly known as the Mojave yucca, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and long, sword-shaped leaves that are covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers at the top of the stem during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.

The Mojave yucca plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife species.
