Yucca falcata var. semicylindrica

Yucca falcata var. semicylindrica, commonly known as the narrowleaf yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that can reach heights of up to 6 feet, adorned with long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers at the top of the stem during the summer months. Its leaves are fibrous and have a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments.

This variety is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
