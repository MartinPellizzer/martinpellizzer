Youngia atripappa

Youngia atripappa, commonly known as the black-leafed dandelion, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to temperate regions of Asia and is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, velvety leaves. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into spherical seed heads with white pappus. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Youngia atripappa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
