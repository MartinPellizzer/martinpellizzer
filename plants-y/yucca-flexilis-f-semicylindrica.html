Yucca flexilis f. semicylindrica

Yucca flexilis f. semicylindrica, commonly known as the narrowleaf yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features tall, narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in the spring. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Its unique foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
