Yushania maculata

Yushania maculata, commonly known as the speckled bamboo, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often marked with dark speckles, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments, such as forest understories or rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds to its ornamental appeal.

Yushania maculata is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities and its ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
