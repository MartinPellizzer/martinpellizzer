Yucca gloriosa var. recurvifolia

Yucca gloriosa var. recurvifolia, commonly known as the twisted-leaf yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and long, narrow, recurved leaves that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
