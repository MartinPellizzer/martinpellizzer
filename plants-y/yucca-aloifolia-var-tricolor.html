Yucca aloifolia var. tricolor

Yucca aloifolia var. tricolor, commonly known as the three-colored yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its foliage is often adorned with a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments.

This variety is particularly notable for its vibrant, variegated leaf patterns, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
