Yucca whipplei f. graminifolia

Yucca whipplei f. graminifolia, commonly known as the grassy yucca, is a unique variant of the yucca plant characterized by its grass-like foliage.

This plant is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a bluish-gray hue, resembling grass rather than the typical sword-like leaves of other yucca species. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with white or pale yellow flowers, typical of the Asparagaceae family.

It is drought-tolerant and requires minimal care, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping and desert gardens.
