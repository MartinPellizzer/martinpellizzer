Yucca filamentosa foliis-aureovariegata

Yucca filamentosa foliis-aureovariegata, commonly known as the variegated yucca, is a striking evergreen perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves that are pale green with yellowish variegation, creating a distinctive visual appeal. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences of white to pale blue flowers in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its architectural form and low maintenance requirements make it a valuable addition to drought-tolerant gardens.
