Yucca × princeps

Yucca × princeps, commonly known as the Spanish dagger, is a hybrid plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often blue-green in color and have sharp, spiny margins. The plant produces striking white or pale yellow flowers in summer, which are arranged in a raceme at the tip of the stem. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Yucca × princeps is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
