Yucca filamentosa f. puberula

Yucca filamentosa f. puberula, commonly known as the silver thread yucca, is a variant of the common yucca characterized by its distinctive silvery-gray foliage.

The leaves are narrow, linear, and covered with a fine, silky pubescence that gives the plant its name. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with white to pale yellow flowers in the spring.

It is a hardy, drought-tolerant species often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.
