Yucca patens

Yucca patens, commonly known as the threadleaf yucca, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, thread-like leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with clusters of white or pale blue flowers, which bloom in the spring. Yucca patens is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
