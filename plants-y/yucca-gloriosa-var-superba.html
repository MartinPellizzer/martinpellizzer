Yucca gloriosa var. superba

Yucca gloriosa var. superba, commonly known as the panhandle yucca, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the southern United States.

It features tall, stiff, sword-like leaves that are gray-green in color and covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces large, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer, adding a dramatic visual impact. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions.
