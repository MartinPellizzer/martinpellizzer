Yushania tenuicaulis

Yushania tenuicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yushania, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit, typically forming dense clumps. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or on rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Yushania tenuicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and resilience.
