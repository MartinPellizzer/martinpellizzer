Yucca aloifolia var. draconis

Yucca aloifolia var. draconis, commonly known as the dragon yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like clusters of sword-shaped, evergreen leaves that can reach up to 10 feet in height. The plant produces white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are arranged in a panicle inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy, drought-tolerant species.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
