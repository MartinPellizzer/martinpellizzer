Yushania hirsuta

Yushania hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy yushania, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or brown.

Yushania hirsuta is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
