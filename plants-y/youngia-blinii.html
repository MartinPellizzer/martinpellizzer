Youngia blinii

Youngia blinii, commonly known as the Siberian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Asia, including Siberia and the Russian Far East.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as tundras and rocky slopes. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its botanical classification places it in the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Youngia blinii is notable for its adaptability to harsh climates and its role in local ecosystems.
