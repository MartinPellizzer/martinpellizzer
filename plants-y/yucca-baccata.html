Yucca baccata

Yucca baccata, commonly known as the banana yucca, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its stiff, sword-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern and often have a waxy, bluish-green appearance. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to white flowers on tall, branched stalks during the summer months. Its fibrous roots are adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

Yucca baccata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally by indigenous peoples for various purposes.
