Yucca filamentosa var. grandiflora

Yucca filamentosa var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Adam's needle, is a striking evergreen perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color and lined with sharp, spiny margins. The plant produces showy, white, bell-shaped flowers on tall, branched inflorescences during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and architectural form make it both ornamental and low-maintenance.
