Youngia lanata

Youngia lanata, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and bright yellow ray flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Youngia lanata is known for its drought tolerance and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
