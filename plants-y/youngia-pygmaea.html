Youngia pygmaea

Youngia pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf sunflower, is a small flowering plant native to the central and eastern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its compact stature, typically growing to a height of only a few inches, and features a single, small yellow flower head. The plant has hairy, alternate leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wet meadows, ditches, and along stream banks.

Youngia pygmaea is known for its ability to bloom early in the growing season, making it a notable wildflower in its habitat.
