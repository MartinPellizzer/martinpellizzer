Yamala cylindrica

Yamala cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical yam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Araceae, which includes plants such as calla lilies and peace lilies. The plant features a thick, tuberous root system that serves as a storage organ for nutrients. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Yamala cylindrica produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are typically borne on a spadix, a characteristic feature of the Araceae family.
