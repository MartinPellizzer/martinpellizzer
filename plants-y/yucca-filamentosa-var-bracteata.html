Yucca filamentosa var. bracteata

Yucca filamentosa var. bracteata, commonly known as the Spanish dagger, is a striking evergreen perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, sword-like leaves that are blue-gray in color and have a distinctive white margin. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with white or pale yellow flowers in the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its architectural form and drought tolerance make it a versatile addition to gardens and landscapes.
