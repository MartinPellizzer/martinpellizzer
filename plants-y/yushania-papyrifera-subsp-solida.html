Yushania papyrifera subsp. solida

Yushania papyrifera subsp.

solida, commonly known as the solid yushania, is a member of the grass family Poaceae. It is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly in China and the Himalayas.

The plant is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and stiff, grass-like leaves. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil conditions.
