Youngia afghanica

Youngia afghanica, commonly known as Afghan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a compact cluster.

Youngia afghanica is valued for its potential use in traditional medicine and its role in ecological systems.
