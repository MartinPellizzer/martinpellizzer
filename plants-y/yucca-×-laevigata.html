Yucca × laevigata

Yucca × laevigata, commonly known as the silverleaf yucca, is a hybrid plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, straight stem and narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are covered in a silvery-blue waxy coating. The plant produces large, pendulous clusters of white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates.

Yucca × laevigata is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
