Yulania denudata subsp. pubescens

Yulania denudata subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the hairy tree peony, is a deciduous shrub native to China. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its subspecies name.

The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring. It is a member of the Papaveraceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.

The hairy texture of its foliage distinguishes it from other peony species.
