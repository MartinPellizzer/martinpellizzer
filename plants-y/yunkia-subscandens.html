Yunkia subscandens

Yunkia subscandens, commonly known as the climbing yunkia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports and structures. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Yunkia subscandens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
