Yushania longissima

Yushania longissima, commonly known as the giant bamboo or longleaf bamboo, is a species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of wax, which helps reduce water loss. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Yushania longissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
