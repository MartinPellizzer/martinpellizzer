Youngia wilsonii

Youngia wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's dandelion, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a single, erect stem. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in the spring. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Youngia wilsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
