Youngia ambigua

Youngia ambigua, commonly known as the common ragwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions, including North America. Youngia ambigua is often considered a weed but has historical use in traditional medicine.
