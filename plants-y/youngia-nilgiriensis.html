Youngia nilgiriensis

Youngia nilgiriensis, commonly known as the Nilgiri dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dandelion-like appearance with yellow flowers and pappus structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are alternate, serrated, and covered in fine hairs.

Youngia nilgiriensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
