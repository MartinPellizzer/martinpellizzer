Yucca × carrierei

Yucca × carrierei, commonly known as the hybrid yucca or sometimes the Spanish yucca, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like stems that can grow up to 10 feet in height, adorned with long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces striking white or pale yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in panicles at the top of the stem. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Yucca × carrierei is also known for its ability to store water in its fleshy stems and leaves, adapting well to arid environments.
