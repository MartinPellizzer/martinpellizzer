Yucca × pilosa

Yucca × pilosa, commonly known as the Spanish dagger, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both Yucca filamentosa and Yucca flaccida.

It is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features tall, narrow, sword-like leaves that are white-veined and often have a bluish tint. Its flowers are typically pale blue or white, arranged in panicles at the end of the stems.

Yucca × pilosa is well adapted to drought conditions and requires minimal water once established.
