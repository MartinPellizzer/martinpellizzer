Yucca filamentosa var. flaccida

Yucca filamentosa var. flaccida, commonly known as the Florida yucca, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color and have a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
