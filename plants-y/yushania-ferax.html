Yushania ferax

Yushania ferax, commonly known as the Tibetan forest bamboo, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 5 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a bluish-green color and a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Yushania ferax is adapted to high-altitude environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
