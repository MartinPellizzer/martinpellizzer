Yucca gloriosa f. acuminata

Yucca gloriosa f. acuminata, commonly known as the Acuminata Yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that can reach heights of up to 10 feet, adorned with long, sword-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces striking white or pale yellow flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for arid and semi-arid landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
