Yucca pendula var. aurea

Yucca pendula var. aurea, commonly known as the golden threadleaf yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous, pale green to golden-yellow leaves that cascade from a central stem. The plant produces striking white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low water requirements make it both ornamental and drought-tolerant.
