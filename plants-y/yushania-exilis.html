Yushania exilis

Yushania exilis, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and are arranged in a basal rosette.

Yushania exilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its elegant appearance.
