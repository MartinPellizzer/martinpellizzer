Yushania polytricha

Yushania polytricha, commonly known as the broad-leaved yushania, is a species of grass found primarily in the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are covered in fine, hairy structures.

Yushania polytricha plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
