Yucca gloriosa f. obliqua

Yucca gloriosa f. obliqua, commonly known as the oblique yucca, is a variant of the yucca plant characterized by its unique growth habit.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features tall, sword-shaped leaves that are often slightly curved, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, arranged in a tall, branched inflorescence.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
