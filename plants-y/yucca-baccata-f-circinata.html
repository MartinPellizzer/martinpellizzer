Yucca baccata f. circinata

Yucca baccata f. circinata, commonly known as the twisted yucca, is a unique variant of the yucca plant characterized by its distinctive, spiraling growth pattern of its leaves.

This ornamental plant is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The leaves are typically narrow, rigid, and have a bluish-gray coloration, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh habitat. It produces tall, branched inflorescences with white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low rainfall, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens.
