Yucca × magnifica

Yucca × magnifica, commonly known as the Spanish dagger, is a hybrid plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color and have a waxy coating. The plant produces striking white or pale yellow flowers in summer, which are borne on tall, branched inflorescences. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Yucca × magnifica is often used in ornamental gardening for its architectural form and low maintenance requirements.
