Yucca gloriosa var. pruinosa

Yucca gloriosa var. pruinosa, commonly known as the Prunose Yucca, is a striking evergreen perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery white coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with clusters of white to pale yellow flowers in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for arid landscapes.

Its architectural form and ornamental value make it a popular plant in xeriscaping and rock gardens.
