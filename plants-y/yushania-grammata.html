Yushania grammata

Yushania grammata, commonly known as the silvergrass or the white grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, silvery-green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Yushania grammata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and mountain valleys.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
