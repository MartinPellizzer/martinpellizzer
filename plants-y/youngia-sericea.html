Youngia sericea

Youngia sericea, commonly known as silverleaf sunflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces bright yellow flower heads that resemble those of the sunflower genus, though it is not closely related. It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Youngia sericea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
