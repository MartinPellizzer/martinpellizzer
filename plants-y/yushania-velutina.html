Yushania velutina

Yushania velutina, commonly known as the velvet bamboo, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, feathery foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, velvety layer of hairs, giving it its common name.

Yushania velutina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
