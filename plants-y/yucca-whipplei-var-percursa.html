Yucca whipplei var. percursa

Yucca whipplei var. percursa, commonly known as the "Candelabra Yucca," is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems that resemble a candlestick, supporting large, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique appearance and resilience in xeriscaping.
