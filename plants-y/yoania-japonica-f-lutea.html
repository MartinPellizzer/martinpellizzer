Yoania japonica f. lutea

Yoania japonica f. lutea, commonly known as the yellow-flowered yew, is a variant of the yew tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellowish-green foliage and small, clustered yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant has a slow growth rate and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its rarity, it is considered a valuable specimen in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
