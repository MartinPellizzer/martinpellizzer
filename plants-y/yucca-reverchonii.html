Yucca reverchonii

Yucca reverchonii, commonly known as the Everglades yucca, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky flower stalks that can reach up to 10 feet in height, topped with clusters of white or pale blue flowers. The plant has sword-shaped leaves that are stiff and covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and swampy areas.

Yucca reverchonii plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and moths.
