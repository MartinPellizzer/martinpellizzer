Yucca × treleasei

Yucca × treleasei, commonly known as the hybrid yucca, is a plant that results from the cross between Yucca filamentosa and Yucca flaccida.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky flower stalks that can reach up to 10 feet in height, bearing clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant has narrow, sword-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color and may have a waxy coating. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Yucca × treleasei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
