Yushania glauca

Yushania glauca, commonly known as the blue yushania, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Yushania glauca typically grows in cool, moist environments such as forests and meadows at high elevations.

Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in panicle-like clusters, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
