Yushania edulis

Yushania edulis, commonly known as the edible yushania, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in local cuisines. The plant thrives in cool, humid climates and is typically found in forested areas at high altitudes. Its rhizomes are rich in starch and are a traditional food source for indigenous communities.

Botanically, it belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is closely related to other edible grasses like bamboo.
