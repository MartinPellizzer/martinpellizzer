Ypsilopus longifolius subsp. erectus

Ypsilopus longifolius subsp.

erectus, commonly known as the upright ypsilopus, is a perennial herb native to certain regions of South America. It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often branching near the base.

The leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, with a smooth surface and a slightly hairy underside. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and typically have five petals, displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
