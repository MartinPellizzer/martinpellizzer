Yoania japonica var. squamipes

Yoania japonica var. squamipes, commonly known as the Japanese mountain cedar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, scaly texture, giving it the specific epithet "squamipes.". The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by winged fruits that aid in wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
