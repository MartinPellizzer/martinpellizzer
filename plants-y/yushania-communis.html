Yushania communis

Yushania communis, commonly known as the common yushania, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or on rocky slopes.

Yushania communis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
