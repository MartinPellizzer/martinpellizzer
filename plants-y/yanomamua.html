Yanomamua

Yanomamua, commonly known as the "forest onion," is a bulbous plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces edible bulbs that are rich in nutrients and have been traditionally used by indigenous communities for food and medicinal purposes. Yanomamua thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
