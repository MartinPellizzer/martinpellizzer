Yushania lacera

Yushania lacera, commonly known as the narrowleaf yushania, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and compact inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and bamboo thickets. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization in its native habitat.

Yushania lacera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
