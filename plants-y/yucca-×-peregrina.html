Yucca × peregrina

Yucca × peregrina, commonly known as the Spanish dagger, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Yucca filamentosa and Yucca flaccida.

It is a striking evergreen perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant features tall, narrow, sword-like leaves that are blue-green in color and often have a white bloom at the base. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a panicle-like inflorescence.

Yucca × peregrina is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure.
