Yucca × titanus

Yucca × titanus, commonly known as the giant yucca, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Yucca filifera and Yucca rigida.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces large, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are long, sword-shaped, and covered in sharp spines along the edges.

Yucca × titanus is native to the southwestern United States and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
