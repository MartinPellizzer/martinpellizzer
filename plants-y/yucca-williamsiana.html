Yucca williamsiana

Yucca williamsiana, commonly known as the Williams yucca, is a shrub-like plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and sword-shaped, evergreen leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months, which are pollinated by bats and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Yucca williamsiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
