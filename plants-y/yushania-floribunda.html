Yushania floribunda

Yushania floribunda, commonly known as the fragrant bamboo, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a subtle aromatic scent.

Yushania floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its pleasant fragrance.
