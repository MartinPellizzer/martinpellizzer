Yinquania

Yinquania, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped or tubular flowers. The plants typically have opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves and produce showy, often blue or violet-colored blooms. Yinquania species are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

They thrive in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
