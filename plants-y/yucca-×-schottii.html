Yucca × schottii

Yucca × schottii, commonly known as the giant yucca, is a hybrid plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and striking, sword-like leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces large, showy white flowers in clusters at the top of the stem, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Yucca × schottii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
