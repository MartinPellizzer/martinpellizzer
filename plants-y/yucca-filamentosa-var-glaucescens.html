Yucca filamentosa var. glaucescens

Yucca filamentosa var. glaucescens, commonly known as the blue yucca, is a striking perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, stiff, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with white to pale blue flowers in the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
