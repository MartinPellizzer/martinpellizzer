Yucca flexilis f. hildrethii

Yucca flexilis f. hildrethii, commonly known as the Hildreth's yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often bluish-green in color and have a waxy texture. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are sword-shaped and can grow up to several feet in length, making it a striking addition to arid landscapes.

It is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
