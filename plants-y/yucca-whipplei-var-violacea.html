Yucca whipplei var. violacea

Yucca whipplei var. violacea, commonly known as the violet yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky, grass-like foliage and large, showy white or violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are tough and fibrous, and the plant is drought-tolerant, requiring minimal water once established.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
