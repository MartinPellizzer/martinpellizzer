Yucca elephantipes var. ghiesbreghtii

Yucca elephantipes var. ghiesbreghtii, commonly known as the Ghiesbreght yucca, is a rare and unique variant of the elephant foot yucca.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy, tree-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers at the top of the stem during the summer months. It is native to the arid regions of southern Africa, specifically Namibia.

This species is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
