Youngia scaposa

Youngia scaposa, commonly known as the slender dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, upright stem that bears a single flower head. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy, white seeds with parachute-like structures for wind dispersal. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is native to temperate regions of Asia.

Youngia scaposa is generally considered a weed but has some medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
