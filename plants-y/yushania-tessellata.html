Yushania tessellata

Yushania tessellata, commonly known as the tessellated yushania, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests and alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale flowers.

Yushania tessellata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native ecosystems.
