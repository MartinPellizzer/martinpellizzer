Youngia japonica subsp.

japonica, commonly known as Japanese cudweed, is a flowering plant in the daisy family, Asteraceae. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is native to East Asia, particularly Japan.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense heads, and its leaves are narrow and hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas. Youngia japonica subsp.

japonica is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural compounds with potential pharmacological applications.
