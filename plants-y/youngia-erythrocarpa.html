Youngia erythrocarpa

Youngia erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its most distinctive feature is its bright red or orange berries, which contain the seeds.

Youngia erythrocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers and unique fruiting structure.
