Yucca × passiflora

Yucca × passiflora, commonly known as the yucca-passionflower hybrid, is a unique botanical cross between a yucca and a passionflower.

This hybrid plant exhibits characteristics of both parent species, including the succulent leaves of yucca and the intricate, showy flowers of passiflora. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions where both parent plants naturally occur. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and floral display.

Botanically, it is a sterile hybrid, meaning it does not produce viable seeds, and is usually propagated through vegetative methods.
