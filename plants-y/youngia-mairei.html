Youngia mairei

Youngia mairei, commonly known as the Maire's dandelion, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has basal leaves that are heart-shaped. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Youngia mairei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
