Yucca acrotricha

Yucca acrotricha, commonly known as the Hawaiian yucca, is a perennial shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its stiff, sword-like leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating called farina. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, white to greenish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the dry, tropical climate of Hawaii.

Yucca acrotricha plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various native insects and birds.
