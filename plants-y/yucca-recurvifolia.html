Yucca recurvifolia

Yucca recurvifolia, commonly known as the twisted leaf yucca, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that curve downward, giving it its common name. The plant produces large, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Yucca recurvifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
