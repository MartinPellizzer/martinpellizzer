Yucca albospica

Yucca albospica, commonly known as the white-spined yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that can reach heights of up to 10 feet. The plant produces large, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer, adding a dramatic visual element to its appearance. Its leaves are long, narrow, and covered in sharp, spiny margins, which help to deter herbivores.

Yucca albospica is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
