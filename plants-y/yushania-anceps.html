Yushania anceps

Yushania anceps, commonly known as the Yunnan bamboo, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of China, particularly Yunnan Province.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant produces inflorescences that are typically panicle-like, with small, spikelet-based flowers. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a waxy coating that helps it retain moisture in its high-altitude habitat.

Yushania anceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of sustainable materials.
