Youngia paosia

Youngia paosia, commonly known as the narrowleaf ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and rough, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
