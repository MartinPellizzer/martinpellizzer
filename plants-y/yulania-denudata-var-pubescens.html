Yulania denudata var. pubescens

Yulania denudata var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent tree peony, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its floral display.
