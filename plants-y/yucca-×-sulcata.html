Yucca × sulcata

Yucca × sulcata, commonly known as the foxtail yucca, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both Yucca filamentosa and Yucca flaccida.

It is a tall, perennial shrub that can grow up to 6 feet in height, featuring long, narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces striking, tall, panicle-like inflorescences with white to pale yellow flowers in the summer months. It is native to the southern United States and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Yucca × sulcata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
