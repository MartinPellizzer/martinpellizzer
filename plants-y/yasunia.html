Yasunia

Yasunia, commonly known as the "Amazonian orchid," is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly the Amazon rainforest. Yasunia species are typically epiphytic, meaning they grow on other plants without being parasitic. They are characterized by their showy, colorful flowers that often bloom in clusters.

Due to their habitat and beauty, Yasunia plants are of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
