Youngia gracilipes

Youngia gracilipes, commonly known as the slender youngia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. It has slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

Its flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
