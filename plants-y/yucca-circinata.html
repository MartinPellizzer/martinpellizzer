Yucca circinata

Yucca circinata, commonly known as the desert yucca, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and stiff, sword-like leaves that are often recurved at the edges. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers during the spring and summer months. Yucca circinata is well adapted to hot, dry climates and can thrive with minimal water.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and desert gardens.
