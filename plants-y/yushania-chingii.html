Yushania chingii

Yushania chingii, commonly known as the Chinese yushania, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, such as forests and meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small flowering structures typical of grasses.

Yushania chingii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also studied for its ecological role in mountain ecosystems.
