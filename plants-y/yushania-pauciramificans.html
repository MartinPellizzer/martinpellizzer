Yushania pauciramificans

Yushania pauciramificans, commonly known as the few-branched yushania, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often densely packed. This plant typically grows in cool, moist environments such as forest understories or high-altitude meadows. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Yushania pauciramificans plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
