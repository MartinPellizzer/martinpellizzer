Youngia chrysantha

Youngia chrysantha, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small yellow daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Youngia chrysantha is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright yellow flowers.
