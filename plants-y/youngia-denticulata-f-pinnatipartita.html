Youngia denticulata f. pinnatipartita

Youngia denticulata f. pinnatipartita, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dandelion, is a variant of the common dandelion with distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, pinnately divided leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The leaves are typically narrow and serrated, with a hairy surface that helps reduce water loss. It produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

This form is often found in disturbed soils and open areas, showcasing its adaptability to various environments.
