Yucca arkansana subsp. louisianensis

Yucca arkansana subsp.

louisianensis, commonly known as the Louisiana yucca, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves and clusters of white, bell-like flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks.
