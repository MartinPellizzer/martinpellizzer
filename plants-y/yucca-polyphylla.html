Yucca polyphylla

Yucca polyphylla, commonly known as the Joshua tree, is a desert plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers during the spring and early summer. Its root system is extensive and adapted to survive in arid conditions with minimal rainfall.

Yucca polyphylla plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various desert species.
