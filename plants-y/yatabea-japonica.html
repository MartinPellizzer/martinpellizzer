Yatabea japonica

Yatabea japonica, commonly known as the Japanese yatabea, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Yatabea japonica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
