Yucca filamentosa f. glaucescens

Yucca filamentosa f. glaucescens, commonly known as the blue yucca, is a variant of the yucca plant characterized by its striking blue-green foliage.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. The plant features sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish coating, giving it its distinctive appearance. It produces tall, branched flower stalks with clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in the summer.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
