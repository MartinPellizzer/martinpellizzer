Yucca muscipula

Yucca muscipula, commonly known as the death blossom, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant's leaves are long, sword-shaped, and covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. Yucca muscipula is adapted to thrive in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy species in desert landscapes.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable plant in its native habitat.
