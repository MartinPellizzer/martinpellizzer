Yucca gloriosa f. genuina

Yucca gloriosa f. genuina, commonly known as the Spanish dagger, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender, white to bluish-green leaves that are stiff and sword-like, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and minimal maintenance requirements, it is well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates.
