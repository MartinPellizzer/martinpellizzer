Yucca × wittmackiana

Yucca × wittmackiana, commonly known as the Spanish dagger, is a hybrid plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color and have sharp, pointed tips. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
