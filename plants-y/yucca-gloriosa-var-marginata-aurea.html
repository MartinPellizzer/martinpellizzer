Yucca gloriosa var. marginata-aurea

Yucca gloriosa var. marginata-aurea, commonly known as the Aureate Yucca, is a striking ornamental plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves with a distinctive golden margin and a pale yellowish-green central stripe. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer, adding a dramatic visual impact. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates.

Its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
