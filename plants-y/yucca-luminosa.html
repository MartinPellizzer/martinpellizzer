Yucca luminosa

Yucca luminosa, commonly known as the luminous yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like stems and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer. The leaves are long, narrow, and covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and ability to withstand drought conditions make it a popular choice for desert gardens.
