Yucca baccata f. periculosa

Yucca baccata f. periculosa, commonly known as the dangerous yucca, is a rare and striking plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that bears clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant has sword-shaped leaves that are stiff and pointed, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
