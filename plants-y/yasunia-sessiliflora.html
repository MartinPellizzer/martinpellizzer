Yasunia sessiliflora

Yasunia sessiliflora, commonly known as the Yasunia orchid, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in its humid, tropical habitat.

Yasunia sessiliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
