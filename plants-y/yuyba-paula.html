Yuyba paula

Yuyba paula, commonly known as the purple yuyba, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive purple to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and has a compact, herbaceous structure. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense clusters.

Yuyba paula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
