Yucca guatemalensis

Yucca guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan yucca, is a perennial plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence and narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue and are borne on a tall stalk.

Yucca guatemalensis is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
