Yucca mohavensis

Yucca mohavensis, commonly known as the Mohave yucca, is a desert plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are covered in a white powdery coating. The plant produces large, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is well adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to access water from underground sources.

Yucca mohavensis is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and drought tolerance.
