Yushania cartilaginea

Yushania cartilaginea, commonly known as the cartilaginous yushania, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that contain tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests at high altitudes.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
