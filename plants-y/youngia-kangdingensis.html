Youngia kangdingensis

Youngia kangdingensis, commonly known as the Kangding dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant features a basal rosette of elongated, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its inflorescence consists of yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, typical of the genus Youngia.

The species is adapted to cold, mountainous environments and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.
