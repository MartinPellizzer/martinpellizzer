Yucca gloriosa var. plicata

Yucca gloriosa var. plicata, commonly known as the crested yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow, grass-like leaves that are often curled or twisted, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer, adding a dramatic visual element to its form. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and full sun.

Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
