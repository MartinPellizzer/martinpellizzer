Yushania acuminata

Yushania acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yushania, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or on rocky slopes.

Yushania acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant foliage.
