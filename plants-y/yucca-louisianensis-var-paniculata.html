Yucca louisianensis var. paniculata

Yucca louisianensis var. paniculata, commonly known as the Louisiana yucca, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, fibrous stalks that can grow up to 6 feet in height, supporting clusters of long, narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, white, panicle-like flower clusters in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens.

Its resilience and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
