Yucca rupicola

Yucca rupicola, commonly known as the cliff yucca, is a striking succulent native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and long, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, green berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and rocky environments.

Yucca rupicola is drought-tolerant and requires minimal care once established, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
