Ypsilorchis

Ypsilorchis, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale greenish-white flowers that resemble a ghostly apparition, hence its common name. The plant lacks a visible stem and has a symbiotic relationship with specific fungi, which provide essential nutrients for its survival. Its leaves are broad and flat, adapted to capture moisture from the humid environment.

Due to its elusive nature and habitat specificity, Ypsilorchis is considered a rare and endangered species.
