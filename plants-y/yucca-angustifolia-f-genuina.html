Yucca angustifolia f. genuina

Yucca angustifolia f. genuina, commonly known as the narrowleaf yucca, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like stems and narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces panicles of white, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water retention.

Its foliage and floral structure make it a distinctive and drought-tolerant ornamental plant.
