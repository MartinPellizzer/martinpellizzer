Yucca filamentosa var. elmensis

Yucca filamentosa var. elmensis, commonly known as the Elm-leaf Yucca, is a striking perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color and have a fibrous texture. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

Its unique foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular addition to native plant collections.
