Yazdana

Yazdana, commonly known as the sacred fig tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clustered, small greenish-white flowers. The tree produces a fruit known as the "breadfruit," which is widely used in various cuisines. Yazdana is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its rapid growth rate.

Its cultural and spiritual significance is profound in many indigenous communities.
