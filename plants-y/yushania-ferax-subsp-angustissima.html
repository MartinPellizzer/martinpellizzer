Yushania ferax subsp. angustissima

Yushania ferax subsp.

angustissima, commonly known as the narrowleaf yushania, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and compact, upright growth habit.

The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in forest understories or on rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of dense, cylindrical spikelets that bear small, two-ranked florets. Yushania ferax subsp.

angustissima plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates in its native ecosystem.
