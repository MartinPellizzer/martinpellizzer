Yucca filamentosa var. nobilis

Yucca filamentosa var. nobilis, commonly known as the Adam's needle, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, stiff, grass-like leaves that are blue-green in color and often have a white powdery coating. The plant produces striking white, bell-shaped flowers on tall, leafless stalks during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This variety is prized for its architectural form and low maintenance requirements in landscaping.
