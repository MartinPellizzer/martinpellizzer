Yucca gloriosa f. maculata

Yucca gloriosa f. maculata, commonly known as the variegated yucca, is a striking perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or yellow variegation pattern along the edges. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear clusters of white or pale blue flowers in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its architectural form and unique foliage, which add visual interest to gardens.
