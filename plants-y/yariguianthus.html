Yariguianthus

Yariguianthus, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets, and its bark is smooth and reddish in color. Yariguianthus thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
