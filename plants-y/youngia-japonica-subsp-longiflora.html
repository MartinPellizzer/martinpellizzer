Youngia japonica subsp. longiflora

Youngia japonica subsp.

longiflora, commonly known as the Japanese fleabane, is a flowering plant native to East Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
