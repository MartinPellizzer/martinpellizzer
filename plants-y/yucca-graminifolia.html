Yucca graminifolia

Yucca graminifolia, commonly known as the grassy yucca, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing white or pale blue flowers, which bloom in the spring. Yucca graminifolia is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its fibrous roots and sturdy structure make it an important plant in its native ecosystem.
