Yucca × moraea

Yucca × moraea, commonly known as the hybrid yucca, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Yucca flaccida and Yucca rigida.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, white to pale blue flowers. It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments.

Yucca × moraea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
