Yucca × adenophora

Yucca × adenophora, commonly known as the Adam's needle, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from Yucca flaccida and Yucca filamentosa.

It is a tall, evergreen perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant features stiff, sword-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color and often covered in a whitish bloom. It produces tall, branched inflorescences with clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in the spring.

Yucca × adenophora is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
