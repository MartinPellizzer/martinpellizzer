Yucca boerhaavii

Yucca boerhaavii, commonly known as the desert spoon, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces large, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Yucca boerhaavii is adapted to arid environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.

Its resilience and unique appearance make it a popular choice for desert gardens.
