Yushania farinosa

Yushania farinosa, commonly known as the farinose yushania, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

Yushania farinosa plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
