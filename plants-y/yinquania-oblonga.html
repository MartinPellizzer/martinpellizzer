Yinquania oblonga

Yinquania oblonga, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for ornamental gardening.

Yinquania oblonga is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
