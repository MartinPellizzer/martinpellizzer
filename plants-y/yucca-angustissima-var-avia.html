Yucca angustissima var. avia

Yucca angustissima var. avia, commonly known as the narrowleaf yucca, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub or small tree characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and sword-shaped, evergreen leaves. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with clusters of white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to desert conditions and a valuable plant for xeriscaping.
