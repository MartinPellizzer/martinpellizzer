Yucca elephantipes var. gigantea

Yucca elephantipes var. gigantea, commonly known as the Spanish dagger, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color and covered in a powdery bloom. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers at the end of its stems, which bloom in spring. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Yucca elephantipes var. gigantea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
