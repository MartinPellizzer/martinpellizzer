Yucca rostrata

Yucca rostrata, commonly known as the rostrate yucca, is a striking desert plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that can grow up to 10 feet in height, topped with a dense cluster of long, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking white or pale yellow flowers in the spring, which are arranged in a panicle-like inflorescence. Yucca rostrata is adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in hot, dry conditions.

Its leaves are covered in a waxy cuticle, reducing water loss and making it well-suited for desert life.
