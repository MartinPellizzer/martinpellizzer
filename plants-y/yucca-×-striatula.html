Yucca × striatula

Yucca × striatula, commonly known as the stripleaf yucca, is a hybrid plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow, sword-like leaves that are often marked with distinctive white or yellow stripes. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers at the top of the stem during the summer months. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.

Yucca × striatula is valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
