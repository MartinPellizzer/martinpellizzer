Yucca rostrata var. integra

Yucca rostrata var. integra, commonly known as the White Yucca, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are typically a deep green color with a waxy coating. The plant produces striking white or pale yellow flowers in panicles during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant's resilience and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
