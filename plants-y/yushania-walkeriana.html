Yushania walkeriana

Yushania walkeriana, commonly known as the Walker's yushania, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in cool and humid conditions. Its flowering stems bear small, clustered inflorescences, which produce delicate, spikelet-like flowers.

Yushania walkeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and ecological significance.
