Yucca aloifolia f. arcuata

Yucca aloifolia f. arcuata, commonly known as the curlleaf yucca, is a variant of the yucca plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, waxy leaves that curl inward at the tips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
