Yucca lutescens

Yucca lutescens, commonly known as the yellow yucca, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces white to yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Yucca lutescens is often found in rocky soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
