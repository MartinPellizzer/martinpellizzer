Yucca ghiesbreghtii

Yucca ghiesbreghtii, commonly known as the Ghiesbreght yucca, is a distinctive species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of white or pale green flowers at the top of the stem during the summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Yucca ghiesbreghtii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
