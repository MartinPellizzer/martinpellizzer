Yucca angustissima subsp. avia

Yucca angustissima subsp.

avia, commonly known as the slender yucca, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom.

The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with clusters of white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
