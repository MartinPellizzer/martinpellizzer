Yucca filamentosa var. patens

Yucca filamentosa var. patens, commonly known as the Adam's needle, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow, evergreen leaves that form a dense rosette. The plant produces striking white, bell-shaped flowers on long, branched inflorescences during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its architectural form and low maintenance requirements make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
