Yucca gloriosa var. rufocincta

Yucca gloriosa var. rufocincta, commonly known as the Red-banded Yucca, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features tall, sword-like leaves that are bluish-gray in color and have a distinctive red or pink band near the base. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer, adding an ornamental display to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Its drought tolerance and architectural form make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
