Yucca × atropos

Yucca × atropos, commonly known as the dark red yucca, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Yucca flaccida and Yucca tenuifolia.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow, evergreen leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is native to the southwestern United States and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Yucca × atropos is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
