Yucca filamentosa var. latifolia

Yucca filamentosa var. latifolia, commonly known as the Adam's needle, is a species of flowering plant in the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States and is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers at the top of the stem during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
