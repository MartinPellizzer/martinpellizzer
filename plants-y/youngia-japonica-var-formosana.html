Youngia japonica var. formosana

Youngia japonica var. formosana, commonly known as the Formosan dandelion, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has a taproot system and produces a milky sap when damaged.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
