Yushania emeryi

Yushania emeryi, commonly known as the Emery's bamboo, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments at high altitudes. Its rhizomes form dense clumps, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

Yushania emeryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
