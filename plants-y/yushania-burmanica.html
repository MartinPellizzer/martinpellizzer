Yushania burmanica

Yushania burmanica, commonly known as the Burmese yushania, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments, often found in forest understories or on rocky slopes. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny grains, which are dispersed by wind.

Yushania burmanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
