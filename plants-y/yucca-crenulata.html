Yucca crenulata

Yucca crenulata, commonly known as the crested yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky stems and large, sword-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in a panicle-like structure. Yucca crenulata is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
