Yucca × vomerensis

Yucca × vomerensis, commonly known as the foxtail yucca, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Yucca filamentosa and Yucca flaccida.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense, spiky rosette. The plant produces striking, long, narrow flower stalks that bear white or pale green flowers. It is native to the southern United States and thrives in well-drained soils.

Yucca × vomerensis is drought-tolerant and often used in xeriscaping due to its hardy nature.
