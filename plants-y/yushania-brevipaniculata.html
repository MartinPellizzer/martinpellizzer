Yushania brevipaniculata

Yushania brevipaniculata, commonly known as the short-bracted bamboo, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to brown spikelets, typical of many bamboo species. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Yushania brevipaniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
