Yucca gloriosa f. rufocincta

Yucca gloriosa f. rufocincta, commonly known as the red-belted yucca, is a variant of the yucca plant characterized by its striking reddish-brown markings on the leaves.

This plant is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are long, sword-shaped, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

It is a hardy, drought-tolerant species well suited for xeriscaping and low-maintenance gardens.
