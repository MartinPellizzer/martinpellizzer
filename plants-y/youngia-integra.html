Youngia integra

Youngia integra, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a stem that bears a cluster of yellow flowers. The flowers develop into fluffy, white seed heads that are easily dispersed by the wind. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and diuretic effects.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.
