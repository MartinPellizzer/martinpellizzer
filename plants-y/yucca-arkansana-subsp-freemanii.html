Yucca arkansana subsp. freemanii

Yucca arkansana subsp.

freemanii, commonly known as Freeman's yucca, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and broad, sword-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This subspecies is valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal in gardens.
