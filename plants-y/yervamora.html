Yervamora

Yervamora, commonly known as the blueberry shrub, is a deciduous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. Its fruit, a small, sweet berry, is rich in antioxidants and vitamins.

Yervamora thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
