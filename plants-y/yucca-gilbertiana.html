Yucca gilbertiana

Yucca gilbertiana, commonly known as the Gilbert's yucca, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its stiff, sword-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color and covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Yucca gilbertiana is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
