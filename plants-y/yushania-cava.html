Yushania cava

Yushania cava, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China and Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial grass-like plant that belongs to the Poaceae family, characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps.

Yushania cava is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
