Yucca elata var. verdiensis

Yucca elata var. verdiensis, commonly known as the White Mountain yucca, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and sword-like, evergreen leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

This variety is well adapted to harsh desert conditions and is often found in rocky habitats.
