Yushania wuyishanensis

Yushania wuyishanensis, commonly known as the Wuyi mountain bamboo, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Yushania wuyishanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining soil stability.

Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences that contribute to its botanical interest.
