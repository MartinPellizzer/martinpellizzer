Yakirra foliolosa

Yakirra foliolosa, commonly known as the leafy yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a dense, grass-like manner. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Yakirra foliolosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
