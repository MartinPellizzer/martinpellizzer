Yucca concava var. longifolia

Yucca concava var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf yucca, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers at the top of the stem during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its striking appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
