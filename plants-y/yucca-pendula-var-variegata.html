Yucca pendula var. variegata

Yucca pendula var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated threadleaf yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow, gray-green leaves with distinctive white or yellow variegation. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a desirable addition to arid gardens.
