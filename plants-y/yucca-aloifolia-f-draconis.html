Yucca aloifolia f. draconis

Yucca aloifolia f. draconis, commonly known as the dragon yucca, is a variant of the yucca plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its stiff, spiky leaves that are typically blue-green in color and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, white or pale yellow flowers in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
