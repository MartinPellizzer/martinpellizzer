Youngia terminalis

Youngia terminalis, commonly known as the common yarrow.

It is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family, characterized by its flat, clustered inflorescences and finely divided leaves. Native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, it thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. The plant produces small white or yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
