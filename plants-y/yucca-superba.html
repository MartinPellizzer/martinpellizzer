Yucca superba

Yucca superba, commonly known as the desert dagger, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing a rosette of thick, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing clusters of white or pale blue flowers. Its leaves are covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Yucca superba is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in low-water landscapes.
