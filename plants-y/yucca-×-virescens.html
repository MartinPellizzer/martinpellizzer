Yucca × virescens

Yucca × virescens, commonly known as the foxtail yucca, is a hybrid plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, grass-like leaves that form a dense, tufted appearance. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne on tall, branching inflorescences. Yucca × virescens is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
