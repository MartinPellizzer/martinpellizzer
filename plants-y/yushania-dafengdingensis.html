Yushania dafengdingensis

Yushania dafengdingensis, commonly known as the Dafengding bamboo, is a species native to China, specifically found in the mountainous regions of Sichuan and Yunnan provinces.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
