Yucca crassifila

Yucca crassifila, commonly known as the thickleaf yucca, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, fleshy leaves that are thick and waxy, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are typically blue-gray in color, with a rough, textured surface.

Yucca crassifila is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
