Yucca filamentosa

Yucca filamentosa, commonly known as Adam's needle, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves that are blue-green in color and have a waxy coating. The plant produces striking clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its architectural form and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for rock gardens and landscapes.
