Yucca angustifolia var. elata

Yucca angustifolia var. elata, commonly known as the narrowleaf yucca, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and sword-shaped, narrow leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating. The plant produces striking white or pale blue flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient plant in desert ecosystems.
