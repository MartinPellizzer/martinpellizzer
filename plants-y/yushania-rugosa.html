Yushania rugosa

Yushania rugosa, commonly known as the rough bamboo, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features narrow, bluish-green leaves with a rough, textured surface, which gives it its common name. Yushania rugosa is often found in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
