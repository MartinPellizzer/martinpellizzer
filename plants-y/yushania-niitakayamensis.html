Yushania niitakayamensis

Yushania niitakayamensis, commonly known as the Niitakayama yushania, is a species of grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring cool and moist environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.

Its flowering inflorescence consists of spikelets that are clustered at the tips of the stems.
