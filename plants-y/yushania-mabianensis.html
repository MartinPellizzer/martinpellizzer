Yushania mabianensis

Yushania mabianensis, commonly known as the Mabian bamboo, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Yushania mabianensis thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
