Youngia formosana

Youngia formosana, commonly known as the Formosan dandelion, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers consist of yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Youngia formosana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
