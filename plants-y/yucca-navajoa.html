Yucca navajoa

Yucca navajoa, commonly known as Navajo yucca, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that can reach heights of up to 6 feet. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are long, narrow, and covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

Yucca navajoa is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and semi-desert regions.
