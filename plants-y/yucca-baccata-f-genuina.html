Yucca baccata f. genuina

Yucca baccata f. genuina, commonly known as the Spanish dagger, is a striking succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features tall, narrow, white to pale blue-green leaves that are sword-shaped and often have a red or purple tinge at the base. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and full sun.

Yucca baccata f. genuina is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
