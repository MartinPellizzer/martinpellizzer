Youngia pilifera

Youngia pilifera, commonly known as the hairy youngia, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head, with ray florets surrounding disk florets.

Youngia pilifera is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
