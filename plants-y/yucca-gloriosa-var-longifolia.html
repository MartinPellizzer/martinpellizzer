Yucca gloriosa var. longifolia

Yucca gloriosa var. longifolia, commonly known as the Spanish dagger, is a striking evergreen perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features tall, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are stiff and often covered in a whitish bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers at the end of its stems, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements make it well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates.
