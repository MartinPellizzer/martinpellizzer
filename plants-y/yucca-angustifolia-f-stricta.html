Yucca angustifolia f. stricta

Yucca angustifolia f. stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf yucca, is a compact, upright shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, arranged in a dense rosette. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with white or pale yellow flowers in the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to arid environments, with reduced leaf surface area to minimize water loss.
