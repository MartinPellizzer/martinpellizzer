Yushania grossa

Yushania grossa, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its robust, hollow stems and large, lance-shaped leaves. This plant typically grows in cool, moist environments and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its rhizomes form dense clumps, allowing it to spread and dominate forest understories.

Yushania grossa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
