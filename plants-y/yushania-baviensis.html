Yushania baviensis

Yushania baviensis, commonly known as the Bavi mountain bamboo, is a species of grass found in the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Yushania baviensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
