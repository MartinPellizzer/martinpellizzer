Yushania megalothyrsa

Yushania megalothyrsa, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands, making it an important component of forest understories.

Yushania megalothyrsa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in steep, forested areas.
