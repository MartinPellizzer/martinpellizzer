Yucca × purpurascens

Yucca × purpurascens, commonly known as the purple filaments yucca, is a hybrid plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces striking clusters of white to pale pink flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid climates.

Yucca × purpurascens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
