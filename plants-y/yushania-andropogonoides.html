Yushania andropogonoides

Yushania andropogonoides, commonly known as the Chinese silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fine, silvery-green foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Yushania andropogonoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
