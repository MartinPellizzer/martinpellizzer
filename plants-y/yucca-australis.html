Yucca australis

Yucca australis, commonly known as the Australian yucca, is a shrub-like plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It features tall, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer, adding a striking visual element to its habitat. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access groundwater.

Yucca australis is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and unique appearance.
