Yamala

Yamala, commonly known as the sacred yam, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and bears small, greenish flowers that develop into fruit. Its roots are rich in starch and have been used for centuries in both culinary and spiritual practices.

Yamala is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
