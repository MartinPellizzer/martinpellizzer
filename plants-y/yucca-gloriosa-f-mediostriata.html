Yucca gloriosa f. mediostriata

Yucca gloriosa f. mediostriata, commonly known as the narrow-striped yucca, is a striking ornamental plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and stiff, sword-shaped leaves that are often tinged with blue or grayish hues. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers at the top of the stem during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
