Yushania angustifolia

Yushania angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yushania, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically narrow and grass-like in appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicle-like inflorescences, which are typical of the grass family. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in forest understories or on slopes.

Yushania angustifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
