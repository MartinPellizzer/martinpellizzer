Yucca flexilis f. peacockii

Yucca flexilis f. peacockii, commonly known as the Peacock Yucca, is a striking ornamental plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces striking white to pale blue flowers in summer, which are borne on tall, branched inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a desirable addition to arid landscapes.
