Yakirra pauciflora

Yakirra pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered yakirra, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

Native to arid regions of Australia, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its small, white flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.

Yakirra pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
