Yucca × massiliensis

Yucca × massiliensis, commonly known as the Massiliensis yucca, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Yucca filamentosa and Yucca tenuifolia.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features tall, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a fibrous texture. It produces striking white or pale yellow flowers in panicles during the summer months.

Yucca × massiliensis is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
