Youngia cineripappa

Youngia cineripappa, commonly known as the Chinese yarrow, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and has finely divided, grass-like leaves. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Youngia cineripappa is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
