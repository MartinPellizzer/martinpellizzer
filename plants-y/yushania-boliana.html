Yushania boliana

Yushania boliana, commonly known as the Bolian yushania, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. Yushania boliana is adapted to cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insect species.
