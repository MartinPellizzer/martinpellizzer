Yuyba dakamana

Yuyba dakamana, commonly known as the Andean moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its large, white, nocturnal blooms that open at night and close by dawn. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional Andean cuisine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Yuyba dakamana thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of high-altitude regions.
