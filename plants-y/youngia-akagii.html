Youngia akagii

Youngia akagii, commonly known as the Japanese thistle or Japanese burweed, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, spiny fruits that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
