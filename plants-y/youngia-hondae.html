Youngia hondae

Youngia hondae, commonly known as the Honduran ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, thriving in temperate to cold climates. Its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Youngia hondae is valued for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of bioactive compounds.
