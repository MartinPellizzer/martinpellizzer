Yucca rostrata var. linearis

Yucca rostrata var. linearis, commonly known as the linear yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers in late summer, which are borne on tall, branched inflorescences. It is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and desert gardens.
