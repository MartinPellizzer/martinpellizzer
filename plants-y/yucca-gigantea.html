Yucca gigantea

Yucca gigantea, commonly known as the giant yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem that can grow up to 20 feet in height, topped with a dense cluster of white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant has long, sword-like leaves that are covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to store water and nutrients efficiently.

Yucca gigantea is well adapted to desert conditions, making it a hardy and drought-resistant species.
