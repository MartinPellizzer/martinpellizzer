Yushania gigantea

Yushania gigantea, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, culm-like stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height, forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers in panicle-like inflorescences, which are typically borne at the top of the stems. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy coating, aiding in water retention in its high-altitude habitat.

Yushania gigantea plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
