Yucca gloriosa f. nobilis

Yucca gloriosa f. nobilis, commonly known as the noble yucca, is a cultivar of the yucca plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates.

The botanical features of Yucca gloriosa f. nobilis make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
