Yucca flaccida f. integra

Yucca flaccida f. integra, commonly known as the smoothleaf yucca, is a variant of the yucca plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, smooth, and waxy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for arid landscape designs.
