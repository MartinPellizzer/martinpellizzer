Youngia racemifera

Youngia racemifera, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale pink to white ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Youngia racemifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
