Yucca × columnaris

Yucca × columnaris, commonly known as the stiff leaf yucca, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Yucca flaccida and Yucca rigida.

It is characterized by its stiff, narrow, evergreen leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing small, white or pale green flowers. It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments.

Yucca × columnaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
