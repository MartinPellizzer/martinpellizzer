Yushania vigens

Yushania vigens, commonly known as the evergreen bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of China, Myanmar, and Thailand. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, evergreen foliage that remains vibrant throughout the year. Its growth habit is clump-forming, with slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters.

Yushania vigens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
