Yulania amoena

Yulania amoena, commonly known as the Japanese chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

Yulania amoena is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to different climates.
