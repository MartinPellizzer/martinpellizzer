Yavia cryptocarpa

Yavia cryptocarpa, commonly known as the narrowleaf yavia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Yavia cryptocarpa is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its resilience in arid environments.
