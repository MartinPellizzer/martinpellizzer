Yucca spinosa

Yucca spinosa, commonly known as the sharp-leaf yucca, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and narrow, spiny leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers at the top of the stem during the summer months. Its leaves are fibrous and can be used for various practical purposes, including rope-making and thatching.

Yucca spinosa is well adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy plant for xeriscaping.
