Yucca × formosa

Yucca × formosa, commonly known as the Mojave yucca, is a hybrid plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence and sword-shaped, evergreen leaves. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue and bloom in the summer months.

Yucca × formosa is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
