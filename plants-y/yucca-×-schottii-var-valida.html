Yucca × schottii var. valida

Yucca × schottii var. valida, commonly known as the stiffleaf yucca, is a hybrid plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and narrow, stiff, evergreen leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces large, white, candle-like flowers that bloom in summer, typically emerging from the base of the plant. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appearance and hardy nature in arid environments.
