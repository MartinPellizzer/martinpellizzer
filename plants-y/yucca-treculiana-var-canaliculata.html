Yucca treculiana var. canaliculata

Yucca treculiana var. canaliculata, commonly known as the canyon yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender, evergreen stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces large, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer, adding a dramatic visual display. Its leaves are long, narrow, and bluish-green, with a waxy coating that helps it conserve water.

This variety is well adapted to arid environments and thrives in full sun with minimal water requirements.
