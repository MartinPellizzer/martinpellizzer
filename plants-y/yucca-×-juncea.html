Yucca × juncea

Yucca × juncea, commonly known as the Adam's needle, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Yucca filamentosa and Yucca flaccida.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and sword-like, evergreen leaves. The plant produces panicles of white or pale yellow flowers in the summer, which are followed by blue-black berries. Yucca × juncea is adapted to well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it drought-tolerant.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
