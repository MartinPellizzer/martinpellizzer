Yersinochloa dalatensis

Yersinochloa dalatensis, commonly known as the Dalat grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
