Yucca baccata subsp. vespertina

Yucca baccata subsp.

vespertina, commonly known as the evening yucca, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that bear clusters of long, narrow, evergreen leaves.

The plant produces striking white or pale yellow flowers in late summer, which are borne on terminal panicles. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a robust, drought-tolerant structure adapted to arid environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in xeriscaping.
