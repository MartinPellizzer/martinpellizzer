Yucca gloriosa var. maculata

Yucca gloriosa var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted yucca, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features tall, sword-like leaves with a distinctive white or yellowish mottling along their edges. The plant produces tall, branched flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer, showcasing white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates.

Its drought tolerance and architectural form make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardens.
