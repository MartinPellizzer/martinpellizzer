Yushania arunachalensis

Yushania arunachalensis, commonly known as the Arunachal yushania, is a grass species native to the eastern Himalayas, particularly in Arunachal Pradesh.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes, often in montane forests. Its flowering stems bear panicle inflorescences with small, spikelet-based flowers.

Yushania arunachalensis plays a role in the ecological structure of its habitat, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
