Yucca aloifolia var. serratifolia

Yucca aloifolia var. serratifolia, commonly known as the sawleaf yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and sword-like, serrated leaves that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers at the top of the stem during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and desert gardens.
