Yushania adpressa

Yushania adpressa, commonly known as the flatleaf bamboo, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and narrow, flat leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in forests or on rocky slopes. Yushania adpressa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique foliage.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the understory vegetation in its natural habitat.
