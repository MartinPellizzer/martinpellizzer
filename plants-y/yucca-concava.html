Yucca concava

Yucca concava, commonly known as the narrow-leaf yucca, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, woody berries. Yucca concava is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and resilience make it a notable species in desert ecosystems.
