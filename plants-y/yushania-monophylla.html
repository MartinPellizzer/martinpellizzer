Yushania monophylla

Yushania monophylla, commonly known as the single-leaf bamboo, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a single, elongated leaf that emerges from a rhizomatous base. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a smooth surface and a subtle, grassy aroma.

Yushania monophylla plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability in its native range.
