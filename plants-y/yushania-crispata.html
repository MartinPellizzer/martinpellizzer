Yushania crispata

Yushania crispata, commonly known as the silvergrass or mountain silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Yushania crispata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
