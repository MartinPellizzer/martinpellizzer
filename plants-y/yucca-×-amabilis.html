Yucca × amabilis

Yucca × amabilis, commonly known as the Spanish dagger, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Yucca flaccida and Yucca filamentosa.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color and have sharp, spiky tips. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers at the end of its stems, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Yucca × amabilis is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and striking appearance.
