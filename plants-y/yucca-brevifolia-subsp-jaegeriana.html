Yucca brevifolia subsp. jaegeriana

Yucca brevifolia subsp.

jaegeriana, commonly known as the Mojave yucca, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are covered in a white powdery coating.

The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers at the top of the stem during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in desert ecosystems.
