Yulania biondii var. angustitepala

Yulania biondii var. angustitepala, commonly known as the narrow-leafed yulania, is a variant of the yulania species native to specific regions of China.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in forested areas and thrives in partial shade, preferring moist, well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is closely related to other members of the yulania genus.
