Yucca gloriosa var. glaucescens

Yucca gloriosa var. glaucescens, commonly known as the blue yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, stiff, sword-shaped leaves that are blue-green in color, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and architectural form make it a valuable addition to arid landscapes.
