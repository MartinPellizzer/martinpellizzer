Youngia szechuanica

Youngia szechuanica, commonly known as the Sichuan dandelion, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

Youngia szechuanica is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
