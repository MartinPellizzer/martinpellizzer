Yucca × macrocarpa

Yucca × macrocarpa, commonly known as the Adam's needle, is a hybrid plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a tall, spiky perennial shrub with stiff, sword-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces large, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Yucca × macrocarpa is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its resilience and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
