Yucca arkansana var. paniculata

Yucca arkansana var. paniculata, commonly known as the panicle yucca, is a perennial plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that can reach up to 10 feet in height. The plant produces clusters of long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are gray-green in color. Its most distinctive feature is its large, showy panicle inflorescence, which consists of numerous white to pale blue flowers.

This variety is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
