Yua thomsonii var. glaucescens

Yua thomsonii var. glaucescens, commonly known as the Thomson's yua, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a waxy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
