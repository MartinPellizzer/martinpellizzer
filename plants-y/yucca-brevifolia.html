Yucca brevifolia

Yucca brevifolia, commonly known as the Joshua tree, is a plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and sword-like, evergreen leaves that are gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to poor soils and extreme temperature fluctuations.

The Joshua tree plays an important role in the ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various desert wildlife species.
