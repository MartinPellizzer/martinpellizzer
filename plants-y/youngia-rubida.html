Youngia rubida

Youngia rubida, commonly known as the red hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and prairies, and it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are bright yellow with a reddish tint.

Youngia rubida is often considered a weed but has ecological value by supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil.
