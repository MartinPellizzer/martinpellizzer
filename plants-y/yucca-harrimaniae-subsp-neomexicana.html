Yucca harrimaniae subsp. neomexicana

Yucca harrimaniae subsp.

neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico yucca, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States. It features tall, fibrous stalks with narrow, sword-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and resilient species within the Asparagaceae family.
