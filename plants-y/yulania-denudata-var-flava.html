Yulania denudata var. flava

Yulania denudata var. flava, commonly known as the yellow-flowered bush honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features slender, arching branches and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy flowers and seasonal color changes.
