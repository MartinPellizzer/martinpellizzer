Yucca whipplei subsp. percursa

Yucca whipplei subsp.

percursa, commonly known as the Baja yucca, is a perennial shrub native to the Baja California peninsula. It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and large, narrow, evergreen leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is notable for its resilience and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
