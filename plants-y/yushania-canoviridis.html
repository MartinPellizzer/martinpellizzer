Yushania canoviridis

Yushania canoviridis, commonly known as the silver fir or Chinese fir, is a species of evergreen coniferous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender form and dense, dark green needles that are often covered in a silvery bloom. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by cone-like structures that contain its seeds. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Yushania canoviridis plays an important role in forest ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental and timber qualities.
