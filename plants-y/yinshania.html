Yinshania

Yinshania, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

These plants are characterized by their upright growth habit and showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors. Yinshania species typically have thick, waxy leaves that help them retain moisture in their native arid environments. They are popular as ornamental plants due to their vibrant blooms and compact size.

The genus includes several species, such as Yinshania lutea and Yinshania violacea, which are prized for their beauty and ease of cultivation.
