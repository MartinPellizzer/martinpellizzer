Yucca whipplei subsp. parishii

Yucca whipplei subsp.

parishii, commonly known as the Paraguay yucca, is a species native to parts of Central and South America. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its tall, spire-like flower stalks that bear clusters of white or pale blue flowers.

The plant has sword-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

The Paraguay yucca is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
