Yucca gloriosa var. acuminata

Yucca gloriosa var. acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed yucca, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features tall, sword-like leaves that are stiff and covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces large, showy white flowers in summer, which are arranged in a tall, cylindrical spike. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.
