Yucca gloriosa f. pendula

Yucca gloriosa f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous leaf clusters that give it a cascading appearance. The plant produces striking white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its architectural form and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
