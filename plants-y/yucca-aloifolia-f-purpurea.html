Yucca aloifolia f. purpurea

Yucca aloifolia f. purpurea, commonly known as the purple leaf yucca, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features striking, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically a deep purple or bluish-green in color. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with white, bell-shaped flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its unique foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
