Yushania linearis

Yushania linearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf yushania, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are often wind-pollinated. Yushania linearis thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or on rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine and subalpine ecosystems.
