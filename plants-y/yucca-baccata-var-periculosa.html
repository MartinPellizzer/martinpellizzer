Yucca baccata var. periculosa

Yucca baccata var. periculosa, commonly known as the dangerous yucca, is a unique variant of the yucca plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky leaves and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its common name reflects the potential danger posed by its sharp, needle-like leaves.

Botanically, it is notable for its resilience and ability to survive in harsh, desert-like conditions.
