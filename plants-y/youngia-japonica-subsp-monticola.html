Youngia japonica subsp. monticola

Youngia japonica subsp.

monticola, commonly known as the alpine groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, preferring cool and moist environments. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and appear in late summer.

This subspecies is often found in high-altitude meadows and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
