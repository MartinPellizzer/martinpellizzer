Yucca × darwinii

Yucca × darwinii, commonly known as the Darwin yucca, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Yucca flaccida and Yucca recurvifolia.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features tall, upright stems covered in sharp, spiny leaves that are typically blue-green in color. Its flowers are large, white, and clustered in panicles, producing a striking display during the summer months.

Yucca × darwinii is well adapted to drought conditions and requires minimal water once established.
