Yucca pruinosa

Yucca pruinosa, commonly known as the silver yucca, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and stiff, silver-gray leaves covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces white, bell-shaped flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Yucca pruinosa is also known for its drought tolerance and ability to withstand extreme temperatures.
