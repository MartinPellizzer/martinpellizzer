Youngia taiwaniana

Youngia taiwaniana, commonly known as the Taiwan dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and stream banks. It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Youngia taiwaniana is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local insect populations.
