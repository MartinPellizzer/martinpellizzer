Yucca harrimaniae subsp. gilbertiana

Yucca harrimaniae subsp.

gilbertiana, commonly known as the Gilbert's yucca, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It features tall, narrow, evergreen leaves that are typically blue-green in color and covered in a powdery coating.

The plant produces striking, candle-like white flower clusters during the summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
