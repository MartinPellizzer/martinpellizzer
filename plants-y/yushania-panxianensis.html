Yushania panxianensis

Yushania panxianensis, commonly known as the Panxian bamboo, is a species of grass native to China.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. This plant typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring cool and moist environments. Yushania panxianensis is often found in forest understories, where it forms dense clumps.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its high-altitude habitat, making it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
