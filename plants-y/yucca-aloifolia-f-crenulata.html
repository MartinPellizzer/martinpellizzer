Yucca aloifolia f. crenulata

Yucca aloifolia f. crenulata, commonly known as the silverleaf yucca, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color, often with a waxy coating that gives it a silvery appearance. The plant produces tall, branched clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are succulent, adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for arid environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
