Yucca flexilis var. falcata

Yucca flexilis var. falcata, commonly known as the banana yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender, grass-like leaves that are often curved and can reach lengths of up to 6 feet. The plant produces large, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for arid landscapes.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular ornamental plant in desert gardens.
