Yucca filamentosa f. concava

Yucca filamentosa f. concava, commonly known as the concave yucca, is a compact, low-growing variant of the common yucca.

It features narrow, waxy, blue-green leaves that curve inward, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in well-drained soils.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and drought tolerance in landscaping.
