Yucca brevifolia f. herbertii

Yucca brevifolia f. herbertii, commonly known as the Herbert's Joshua tree, is a rare and distinctive variant of the Joshua tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its shorter, more compact growth habit compared to the typical Joshua tree, with narrower leaves and a more stunted overall form. The plant features a tall, unbranched stem that terminates in a dense cluster of white, bell-shaped flowers during the spring. Its leaves are sword-like, gray-green in color, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This subspecies is adapted to arid environments and plays a vital role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
