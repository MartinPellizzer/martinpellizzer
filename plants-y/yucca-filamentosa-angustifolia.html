Yucca filamentosa angustifolia

Yucca filamentosa angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf yucca, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens.
