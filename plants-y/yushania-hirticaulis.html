Yushania hirticaulis

Yushania hirticaulis, commonly known as the hairy stem yushania, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly rough texture.

Yushania hirticaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
