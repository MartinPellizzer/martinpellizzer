Yucca thompsoniana

Yucca thompsoniana, commonly known as Thompson's yucca, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features a woody stem with narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. Its flowers are clustered in panicles and are typically white or pale blue, blooming in the spring.

Yucca thompsoniana is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
