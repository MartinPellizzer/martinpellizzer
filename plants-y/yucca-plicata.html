Yucca plicata

Yucca plicata, commonly known as the threadleaf yucca, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette and have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing white or pale green flowers, which are typically pollinated by insects. Yucca plicata is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy plant for xeriscaping.

Its fibrous roots and leaves have been used by indigenous peoples for various practical purposes.
