Yushania fungosa

Yushania fungosa, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, feathery foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy surface.

Yushania fungosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in gardens.
