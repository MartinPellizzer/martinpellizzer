Yucca peacockii

Yucca peacockii, commonly known as the Peacock Yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of long, sword-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, white or pale blue flowers in late spring, which resemble a peacock's tail, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for arid landscapes.

Yucca peacockii is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
