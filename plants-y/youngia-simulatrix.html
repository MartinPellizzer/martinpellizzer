Youngia simulatrix

Youngia simulatrix, commonly known as the mimic dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant produces a distinctive seed head that resembles a dandelion, which is a key feature in its common name. It typically grows in disturbed soils and open areas, thriving in temperate climates.

Youngia simulatrix is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along the edges of forests.
