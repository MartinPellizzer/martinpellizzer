Yushania mairei

Yushania mairei, commonly known as the Maire bamboo, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue to white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

Yushania mairei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
