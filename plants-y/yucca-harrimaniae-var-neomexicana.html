Yucca harrimaniae var. neomexicana

Yucca harrimaniae var. neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico yucca, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color and often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and resilient plant in its native habitat.
