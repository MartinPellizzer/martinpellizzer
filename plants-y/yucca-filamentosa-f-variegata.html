Yucca filamentosa f. variegata

Yucca filamentosa f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated yucca, is a striking ornamental plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features sword-shaped, bluish-green leaves with distinctive white or yellow variegation along the edges. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences of white to pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

Its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for gardeners in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 9.
