Yucca × flora

Yucca × flora, commonly known as the hybrid yucca, is a cross between different yucca species, resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics.

It typically features tall, sword-shaped leaves that are stiff and covered in a whitish bloom, giving them a distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, pendulous clusters of white or cream-colored flowers during the summer months. It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid conditions.

Yucca × flora is drought-tolerant and requires minimal care, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
