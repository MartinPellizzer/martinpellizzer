Yushania ailuropodina

Yushania ailuropodina, commonly known as the giant panda bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to the mountainous regions of southwest China.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and long, narrow leaves. This plant is adapted to cool, moist climates and thrives in high-altitude forests. It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.

Due to its unique appearance and association with the giant panda, it is also of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
