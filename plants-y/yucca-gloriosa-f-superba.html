Yucca gloriosa f. superba

Yucca gloriosa f. superba, commonly known as the stiffleaf yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and narrow, stiff, evergreen leaves that can reach several feet in length. The plant produces large, white, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in a panicle inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant specimen.

Its architectural form and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and arid landscape designs.
