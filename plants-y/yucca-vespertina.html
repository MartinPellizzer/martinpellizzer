Yucca vespertina

Yucca vespertina, commonly known as the evening yucca, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features tall, sword-shaped leaves that are usually blue-green in color and covered in a white powdery coating. Its flowers are clustered in panicles and bloom in shades of white to pale yellow, often at night.

Yucca vespertina is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.
