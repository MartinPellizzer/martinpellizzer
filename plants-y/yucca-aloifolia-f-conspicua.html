Yucca aloifolia f. conspicua

Yucca aloifolia f. conspicua, commonly known as the silver thread yucca, is a cultivar of the yucca plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, waxy bloom, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, pale green to white flowers that bloom in late spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its striking foliage and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
