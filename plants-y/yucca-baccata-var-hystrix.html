Yucca baccata var. hystrix

Yucca baccata var. hystrix, commonly known as the spiny yucca, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features tall, rigid, sword-shaped leaves that are covered in sharp spines along their margins. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The plant is characterized by its drought tolerance and ability to grow in well-drained soils.
