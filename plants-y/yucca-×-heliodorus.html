Yucca × heliodorus

Yucca × heliodorus, commonly known as the hybrid yucca or Spanish dagger, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence and sword-shaped, evergreen foliage. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Yucca × heliodorus is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in arid and semi-arid regions.
