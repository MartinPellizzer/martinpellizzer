Youngia jiulongshanensis

Youngia jiulongshanensis, commonly known as the Jiulongshan dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a single, erect stem that terminates in a bright yellow flower head. Native to China, this species is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing a high degree of drought tolerance. Its flowers are typically visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The plant's unique morphology and habitat preferences make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
