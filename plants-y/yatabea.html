Yatabea

Yatabea, commonly known as the sacred lotus of Southeast Asia, is a species of water lily native to tropical regions.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, while its striking white or pink flowers bloom above the water. The plant has a thick, fleshy rhizome that anchors it in the mud and stores nutrients. Yatabea is known for its symbolic significance in various cultures and its use in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
