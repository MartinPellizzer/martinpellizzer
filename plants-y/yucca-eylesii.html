Yucca eylesii

Yucca eylesii, commonly known as the Eyles yucca, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of white or pale blue flowers during the summer months, which are borne on terminal panicles. Yucca eylesii is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
