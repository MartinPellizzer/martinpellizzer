Yushania collina

Yushania collina, commonly known as the Himalayan bamboo, is a perennial grass native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically green and covered in a fine layer of hair. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Yushania collina plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
