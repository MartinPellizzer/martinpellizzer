Youngia elegans

Youngia elegans, commonly known as the elegant sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and slender, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Youngia elegans is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
