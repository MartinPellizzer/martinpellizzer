Yucca angustissima subsp. kanabensis

Yucca angustissima subsp.

kanabensis, commonly known as Kanab yucca, is a plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a shrub-like species characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert landscapes.

This subspecies is notable for its resilience to drought and its role in local ecosystems.
