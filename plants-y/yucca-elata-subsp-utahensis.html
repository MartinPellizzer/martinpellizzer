Yucca elata subsp. utahensis

Yucca elata subsp.

utahensis, commonly known as the Utah yucca, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its tall, stiff, sword-like leaves that are gray-green in color and often have a waxy coating.

The plant produces white, bell-shaped flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to survive in harsh desert conditions.
