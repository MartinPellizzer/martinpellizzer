Yucca × augusta

Yucca × augusta, commonly known as the Aurea Yucca, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Yucca filamentosa and Yucca flaccida.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage with a bluish tinge, which becomes more pronounced in full sun. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Yucca × augusta is native to the southern United States and is often used in ornamental gardening for its architectural form and low maintenance requirements.
