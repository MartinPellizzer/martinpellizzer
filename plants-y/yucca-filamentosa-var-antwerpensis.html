Yucca filamentosa var. antwerpensis

Yucca filamentosa var. antwerpensis, commonly known as the Antwerp yucca, is a cultivar of the yucca plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer, and its sword-like, bluish-green leaves that are stiff and arranged in a rosette. This variety is known for its compact growth habit, making it suitable for small garden spaces. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, exhibiting drought tolerance once established.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
