Yucca glauca f. rosea

Yucca glauca f. rosea, commonly known as the pink flowered yucca, is a cultivar of the yucca plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features tall, sword-like leaves that are blue-green in color and covered in a whitish bloom. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
