Yucca whipplei subsp. newberryi

Yucca whipplei subsp.

newberryi, commonly known as the Newberry yucca, is a plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a shrub-like species characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and large, sword-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

The Newberry yucca is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
