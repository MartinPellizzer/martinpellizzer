Yucca gloriosa f. tortulata

Yucca gloriosa f. tortulata, commonly known as the twisted yucca, is a unique variant of the yucca plant characterized by its distinctive, spiraled leaves.

This cultivar originates from the southwestern United States and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens. The leaves are long, narrow, and often curl in a tight spiral, giving the plant a striking, otherworldly appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences of white flowers in the spring, adding visual interest to the landscape.
