Yucca × ismene

Yucca × ismene, commonly known as the hybrid yucca, is a cross between Yucca flaccida and Yucca gloriosa.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months. It is native to the southwestern United States and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Yucca × ismene is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
