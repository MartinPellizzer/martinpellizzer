Yunquea

Yunquea, commonly known as the "tree of life," is a tropical plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and can grow up to 30 feet tall. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests. Its bark contains compounds used in traditional medicine for their healing properties.

The Yunquea also plays a vital role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
