Yulania japonica

Yulania japonica, commonly known as the Japanese chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that come in a wide range of colors. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, with hairy leaves and a compact, bushy growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Yulania japonica is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been cultivated for centuries in traditional Chinese medicine.
