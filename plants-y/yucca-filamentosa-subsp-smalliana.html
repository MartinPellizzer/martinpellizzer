Yucca filamentosa subsp. smalliana

Yucca filamentosa subsp.

smalliana, commonly known as the chicken-foot yucca, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a white, powdery coating.

The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing clusters of white or pale green flowers in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
