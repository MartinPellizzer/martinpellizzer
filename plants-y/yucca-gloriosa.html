Yucca gloriosa

Yucca gloriosa, commonly known as the Spanish dagger, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features tall, narrow, sword-like leaves that are stiff and covered in a white powdery coating called bloom. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with clusters of white or pale blue flowers that bloom in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid climates.

Its architectural form and striking appearance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
