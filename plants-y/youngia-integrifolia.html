Youngia integrifolia

Youngia integrifolia, commonly known as the slender aster, is a flowering plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the late summer and early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Youngia integrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
