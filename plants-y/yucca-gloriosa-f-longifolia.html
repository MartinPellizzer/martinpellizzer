Yucca gloriosa f. longifolia

Yucca gloriosa f. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, which are stiff and sword-shaped. The plant produces clusters of white to pale green flowers in the summer, which are arranged in panicles and attract various pollinators. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to arid conditions, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in drought-prone environments.
