Yucca tricolor

Yucca tricolor, commonly known as the three-color yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves that are usually a mix of green, white, and blue-gray in color. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months. Yucca tricolor is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique foliage and ability to withstand harsh conditions make it a notable addition to arid landscapes.
