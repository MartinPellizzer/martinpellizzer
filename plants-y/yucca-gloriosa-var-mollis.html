Yucca gloriosa var. mollis

Yucca gloriosa var. mollis, commonly known as the softleaf yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and large, sword-shaped leaves that are softer and more flexible than those of other yucca species. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with clusters of white or pale blue flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
