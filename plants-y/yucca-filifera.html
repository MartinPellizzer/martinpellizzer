Yucca filifera

Yucca filifera, commonly known as the threadleaf yucca, is a striking succulent native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem and narrow, sword-like leaves that are covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months, which are followed by blue-gray berries. Yucca filifera is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
