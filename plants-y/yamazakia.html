Yamazakia

Yamazakia, commonly known as the Japanese yam, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Dioscoreaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are heart-shaped and can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

Yamazakia is valued not only for its nutritional content but also for its traditional use in herbal medicine.
