Yucca gloriosa var. tristis

Yucca gloriosa var. tristis, commonly known as the Spanish dagger, is a striking evergreen perennial native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features tall, stiff, white to bluish-green foliage that resembles dagger blades, giving it its common name. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, bell-shaped white flowers in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its architectural form and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
