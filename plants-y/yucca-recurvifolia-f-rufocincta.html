Yucca recurvifolia f. rufocincta

Yucca recurvifolia f. rufocincta, commonly known as the red-banded yucca, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features striking, sword-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a reddish hue along the edges, giving it its common name. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with clusters of white to pale blue flowers. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy leaves and stems.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and resilient species within the Asparagaceae family.
