Yucca thornberi

Yucca thornberi, commonly known as the Thornberg yucca, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and stiff, sword-shaped leaves that are covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with clusters of white to pale blue flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use by indigenous peoples.
