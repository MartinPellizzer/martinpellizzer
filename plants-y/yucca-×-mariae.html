Yucca × mariae

Yucca × mariae, commonly known as the hybrid yucca, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that bears clusters of white to pale blue flowers. The plant has sword-shaped, evergreen leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Yucca × mariae is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
