Youngia denticulata var. alpina

Youngia denticulata var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine toothed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cold and harsh conditions. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, toothed leaves and produces small, yellow composite flowers during the summer months. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear several flower heads.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in mountainous habitats with limited soil cover.
