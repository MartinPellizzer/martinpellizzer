Yushania auctiaurita

Yushania auctiaurita, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, silver-gray blades that give it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are clustered in panicles. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or on rocky slopes.

Yushania auctiaurita is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in marginal habitats.
