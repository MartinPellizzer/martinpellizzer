Yushania qiaojiaensis

Yushania qiaojiaensis, commonly known as the Qiaojia bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to China, specifically found in the Yunnan Province, where it thrives in mountainous regions with high altitude and cool climates. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. It plays a significant role in the local ecosystem by contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
