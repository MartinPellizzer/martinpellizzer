Yucca conspicua

Yucca conspicua, commonly known as the showy yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow, evergreen leaves that are sword-shaped and covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces large, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer, adding a dramatic visual impact to its appearance. Yucca conspicua is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its architectural form and showy blooms make it a popular ornamental plant in desert gardens.
