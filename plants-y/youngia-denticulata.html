Youngia denticulata

Youngia denticulata, commonly known as the toothed hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a distinctive toothed edge, giving it its common name.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.
