Yucca boscii

Yucca boscii, commonly known as the desert candle, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem that resembles a candle, topped with a dense cluster of white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant has tough, fibrous leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem, providing structural support and reducing water loss. Yucca boscii is adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in dry conditions.

It plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
