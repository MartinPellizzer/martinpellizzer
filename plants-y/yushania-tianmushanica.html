Yushania tianmushanica

Yushania tianmushanica, commonly known as Tianmushan yushania, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, two-lipped flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
