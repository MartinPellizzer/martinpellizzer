Yucca gloriosa subvar. parviflora

Yucca gloriosa subvar. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf yucca, is a compact, drought-tolerant perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features tall, sword-like leaves that are blue-gray in color and covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers at the end of its stems during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
