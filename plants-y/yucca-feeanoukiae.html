Yucca feeanoukiae

Yucca feeanoukiae, commonly known as the Hawaiian yucca, is a species native to the islands of Hawaii.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, typical of its coastal and upland habitats.

Yucca feeanoukiae is adapted to the tropical climate of Hawaii and is often found in dry, rocky areas.
