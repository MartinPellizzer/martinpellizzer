Yucca × viridiflora

Yucca × viridiflora, commonly known as the hybrid yucca or green-flowered yucca, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Yucca flaccida and Yucca filamentosa.

It is characterized by its tall, sword-like leaves that are usually bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of white or greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Yucca × viridiflora is native to the southern United States and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
