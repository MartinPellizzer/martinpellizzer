Yucca filamentosa var. ramosa

Yucca filamentosa var. ramosa, commonly known as the Spanish dagger, is a striking plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, stiff, white or bluish-green leaves that are often adorned with a white marginal border. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences of white, bell-shaped flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its architectural form and minimal maintenance requirements make it a desirable ornamental plant in arid and semi-arid landscapes.
