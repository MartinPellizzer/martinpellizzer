Yucca arcuata

Yucca arcuata, commonly known as the banana yucca, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender, sword-shaped leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around a central stem. The plant produces large, pendulous clusters of white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Yucca arcuata is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and striking appearance.
