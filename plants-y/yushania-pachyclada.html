Yushania pachyclada

Yushania pachyclada, commonly known as the broad-leaf yushania, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are typically greenish or pale yellow. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or on rocky slopes.

Yushania pachyclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping and horticulture.
