Yucca rupicola var. tortifolia

Yucca rupicola var. tortifolia, commonly known as the twisted-leaf yucca, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy, sword-shaped leaves that are often twisted or curled, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with clusters of white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in arid and rocky environments, demonstrating adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
