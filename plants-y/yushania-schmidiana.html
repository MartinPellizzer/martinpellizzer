Yushania schmidiana

Yushania schmidiana, commonly known as the Schmidian hillbilly bamboo, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Yushania schmidiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
