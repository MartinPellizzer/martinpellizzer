Yucca newberryi subsp. mckelveyana

Yucca newberryi subsp.

mckelveyana, commonly known as the Mckelvey's yucca, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and large, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a white bloom.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, emerging from the leaf axils. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Its resilience and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping in desert landscapes.
