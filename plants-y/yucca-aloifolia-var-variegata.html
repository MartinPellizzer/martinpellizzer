Yucca aloifolia var. variegata

Yucca aloifolia var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and sword-like, bluish-green leaves with striking white and yellow variegation. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences of white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it both ornamental and adaptable to arid environments.
