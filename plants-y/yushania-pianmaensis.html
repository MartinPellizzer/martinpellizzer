Yushania pianmaensis

Yushania pianmaensis, commonly known as the Pianma bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China, where it thrives in cool, humid climates. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It plays an important role in the local ecosystem by contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
