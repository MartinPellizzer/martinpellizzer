Yushania densifolia

Yushania densifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved yushania, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, overlapping leaves that form a compact rosette, typically growing in high-altitude environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are often wind-pollinated. Yushania densifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine and subalpine ecosystems.
