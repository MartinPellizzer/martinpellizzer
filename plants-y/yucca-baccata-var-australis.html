Yucca baccata var. australis

Yucca baccata var. australis, commonly known as the Australian banana yucca, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fleshy, banana-like leaves that are gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments with well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh climates.
