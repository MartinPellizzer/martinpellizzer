Yucca flaccida var. glaucescens

Yucca flaccida var. glaucescens, commonly known as the blue yucca, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a tall, unbranched stem with a bluish-gray sheen, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking white or pale yellow flowers in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a waxy texture, adapted to store water in arid environments.

It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
