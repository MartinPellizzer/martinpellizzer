Youngia gongshanensis

Youngia gongshanensis, commonly known as the Gongshan dandelion, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its composite flower heads and rough, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing adaptations to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive silvery underside, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

Youngia gongshanensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited geographic distribution.
