Yucca verdiensis

Yucca verdiensis, commonly known as the Verde yucca, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, stiff, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces striking clusters of white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Yucca verdiensis is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient plant in desert ecosystems.
