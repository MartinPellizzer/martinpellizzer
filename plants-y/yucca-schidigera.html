Yucca schidigera

Yucca schidigera, commonly known as the banana yucca, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and large, fleshy, banana-like leaves that are often used for ornamental purposes. The plant produces clusters of white to yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. Its root system is extensive and adapted to arid environments, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Yucca schidigera is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to withstand harsh desert conditions.
