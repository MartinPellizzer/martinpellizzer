Youngia tenuifolia subsp. altaica

Youngia tenuifolia subsp.

altaica, commonly known as the Altai hairgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool climates.

The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in summer. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its ecological adaptability and potential medicinal properties.
