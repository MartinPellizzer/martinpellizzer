Yucca canaliculata var. pendula

Yucca canaliculata var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its long, cascading leaves that give it a unique, almost tree-like appearance. The plant produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers. Its leaves are sword-shaped, with a waxy texture and a bluish-green hue.

This variety is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
