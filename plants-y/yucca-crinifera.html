Yucca crinifera

Yucca crinifera, commonly known as the threadleaf yucca, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and narrow, thread-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces large, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. Yucca crinifera is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a distinctive addition to desert gardens.
