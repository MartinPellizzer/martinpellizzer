Yucca aloifolia f. menandii

Yucca aloifolia f. menandii, commonly known as the silver yucca, is a distinctive plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow, evergreen leaves that are covered in a silvery-gray bloom, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with white or pale green flowers, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for arid landscapes.

Its unique foliage and drought tolerance make it a popular ornamental plant in xeriscaping.
