Yushania longiuscula

Yushania longiuscula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yushania, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically narrow and grass-like in appearance. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or on rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often adorned with pale to brownish flowers.

Yushania longiuscula plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is of interest to botanists studying alpine and subtropical flora.
