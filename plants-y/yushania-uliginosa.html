Yushania uliginosa

Yushania uliginosa, commonly known as the bog bamboo, is a perennial grass species native to the wetlands and mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in peat bogs and swampy areas. Yushania uliginosa produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically pollinated by wind.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a stabilizer of wetland ecosystems and its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions.
