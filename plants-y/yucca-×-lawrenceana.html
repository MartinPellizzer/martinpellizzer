Yucca × lawrenceana

Yucca × lawrenceana, commonly known as the stiffleaf yucca, is a hybrid plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and narrow, stiff, evergreen leaves that are often blue-gray in color. The plant produces large, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

Yucca × lawrenceana is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
