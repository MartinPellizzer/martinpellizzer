Yucca funifera

Yucca funifera, commonly known as the rope yucca, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height, often resembling a rope due to its fibrous texture. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers at the top of its stem during the summer months. Its leaves are long, narrow, and covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

Yucca funifera is well adapted to desert conditions and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and resilience.
