Yushania hsuehiana

Yushania hsuehiana, commonly known as the Hsueh's yushania, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, elongated leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hair. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or on rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a dense panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

Yushania hsuehiana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native ecosystem.
