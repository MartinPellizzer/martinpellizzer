Yucca gloriosa var. mediopicta

Yucca gloriosa var. mediopicta, commonly known as the Medio-picta yucca, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sword-like leaves that are often bluish-green with a distinctive white or cream-colored midrib. The plant produces striking, tall flower stalks that bear clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its drought tolerance and ability to add vertical interest to landscapes.
