Yucca acuminata

Yucca acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed yucca, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that can reach heights of up to 10 feet. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers at the top of the stem during the summer months. Its leaves are long, narrow, and covered in sharp spines, which help deter herbivores.

Yucca acuminata is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
