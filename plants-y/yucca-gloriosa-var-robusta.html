Yucca gloriosa var. robusta

Yucca gloriosa var. robusta, commonly known as the robust yucca, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and stiff, sword-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces striking white or pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are arranged in a panicle-like inflorescence. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates.

Its hardy nature and architectural form make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
