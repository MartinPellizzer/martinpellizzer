Yucca toftiae

Yucca toftiae, commonly known as the Toft's yucca, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its tall, fibrous stalks and clusters of long, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces white or pale green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Yucca toftiae is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
