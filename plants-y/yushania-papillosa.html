Yushania papillosa

Yushania papillosa, commonly known as the silky yushania, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Yushania papillosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
