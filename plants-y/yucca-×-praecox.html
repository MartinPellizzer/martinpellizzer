Yucca × praecox

Yucca × praecox, commonly known as the foxtail yucca, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Yucca flaccida and Yucca treculii.

It is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, characterized by its tall, branched inflorescences that resemble a foxtail. The plant features sword-shaped, blue-green leaves that are often lined with sharp spines along their margins. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Yucca × praecox is adapted to arid environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
