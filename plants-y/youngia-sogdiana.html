Youngia sogdiana

Youngia sogdiana, commonly known as the Sogdian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and linear leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showing adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. It has a compact growth habit and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Youngia sogdiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits in its native habitat.
