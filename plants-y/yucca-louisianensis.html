Yucca louisianensis

Yucca louisianensis, commonly known as the Louisiana yucca, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of white to pale blue flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, three-lobed berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid environments.

Yucca louisianensis is also notable for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
