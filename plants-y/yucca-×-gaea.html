Yucca × gaea

Yucca × gaea, commonly known as the hybrid yucca, is a cultivated plant resulting from the crossbreeding of different yucca species.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are typically bluish-gray in color and have a waxy texture. The plant produces striking white or pale yellow flowers in panicles during the summer months. It is native to the southwestern United States and has adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Yucca × gaea is often grown for ornamental purposes due to its drought tolerance and architectural appearance.
