Yucca lacandonica

Yucca lacandonica, commonly known as the Lacandon yucca, is a rare and striking plant native to the humid lowland forests of southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its tropical habitat. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer, adding a dramatic visual element to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Yucca lacandonica is considered a vulnerable species.
