Youngia japonica

Youngia japonica, commonly known as the Japanese dandelion, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and its bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces a capsule fruit containing numerous seeds with white pappus for wind dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in gardens and disturbed areas.

Youngia japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
