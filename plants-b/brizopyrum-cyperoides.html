Brizopyrum cyperoides

Brizopyrum cyperoides, commonly known as the swamp arrowgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or saturated soil, exhibiting a grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are often submerged or just above the water surface. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic habitats.

Brizopyrum cyperoides plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
