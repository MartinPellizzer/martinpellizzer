Bulbine francescae

Bulbine francescae, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, triangular leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for low-maintenance gardens.
