Biscutella didyma var. choulettei

Biscutella didyma var. choulettei, commonly known as the Choulette's biscuit flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pale pink to white in color, with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant has basal leaves that are lanceolate and hairy, contributing to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with specific growing conditions.
