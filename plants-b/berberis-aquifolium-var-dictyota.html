Berberis aquifolium var. dictyota

Berberis aquifolium var. dictyota, commonly known as the Oregon grape, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features compound leaves with a striking dark green color and spiny margins, resembling holly. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by blue-black berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in native plant landscaping.

Its berries also contain berberine, a compound with potential medicinal properties.
