Boronia citriodora subsp. paulwilsonii

Boronia citriodora subsp.

paulwilsonii, commonly known as the Wilson's boronia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and showy, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to semi-shaded environments. It is a shrub that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aromatic foliage and delicate blooms.
