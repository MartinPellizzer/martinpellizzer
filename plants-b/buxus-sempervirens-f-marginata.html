Buxus sempervirens f. marginata

Buxus sempervirens f. marginata, commonly known as the silver-edged boxwood, is a compact evergreen shrub characterized by its slow growth and dense, foliage.

Its leaves are typically dark green with a distinctive white or silver margin, creating a striking visual contrast. The plant is native to western and southern Europe, where it thrives in well-drained, slightly acidic soils. It is often used in formal garden designs due to its ability to be shaped into intricate topiaries.

This variety is particularly valued for its ornamental appeal and versatility in landscaping.
