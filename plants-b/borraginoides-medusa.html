Borraginoides medusa

Borraginoides medusa, commonly known as the Medusa's hair, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance contribute to its ornamental value in horticulture.
