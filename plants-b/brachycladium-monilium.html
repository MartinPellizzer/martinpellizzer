Brachycladium monilium

Brachycladium monilium, commonly known as the chain grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a tendency to curl at the tips.

Brachycladium monilium is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in poor soil conditions.
