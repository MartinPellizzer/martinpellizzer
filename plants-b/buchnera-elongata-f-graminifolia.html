Buchnera elongata f. graminifolia

Buchnera elongata f. graminifolia, commonly known as the grassy Buchnera, is a variant of the Buchnera genus characterized by its grass-like foliage.

This plant typically features narrow, elongated leaves that give it a distinctive appearance, often resembling grass species. It is native to specific regions in South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant's botanical structure includes a basal rosette of leaves with a short, unbranched stem.

Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in spring, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
