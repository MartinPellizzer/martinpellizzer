Bletia gracilis

Bletia gracilis, commonly known as the slender bletia, is a perennial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its erect, grass-like foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals.

Bletia gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
