Bryonia punctata

Bryonia punctata, commonly known as the spotted bryony, is a climbing plant belonging to the family Cucurbitaceae.

It is characterized by its pale green, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spiny, orange-red fruits. It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Bryonia punctata is known for its toxic properties, containing compounds such as bryonin and cucurbitacin.
