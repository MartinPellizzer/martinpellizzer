Boronia verticillata

Boronia verticillata, commonly known as the mountain boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Boronia verticillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
