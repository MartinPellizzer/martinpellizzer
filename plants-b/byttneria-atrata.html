Byttneria atrata

Byttneria atrata, commonly known as the black mangrove, is a flowering plant species native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that thrives in saline and brackish water environments. The plant has small, ovate leaves with a distinctive dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are small, clustered, and typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color.

Byttneria atrata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
