Beyeriopsis brevifolia

Beyeriopsis brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers, followed by small, fleshy berries that are a food source for various birds. Its root system forms haustoria, which attach to the host tree's vascular system to extract nutrients.

Beyeriopsis brevifolia is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it contributes to the ecological dynamics of its host species.
