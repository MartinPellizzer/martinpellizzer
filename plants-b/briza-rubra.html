Briza rubra

Briza rubra, commonly known as red feather grass, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features slender, reddish stems with fine, grass-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, pale green to reddish flowers in panicles, which are typically visible in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, streambanks, and woodland edges.

Briza rubra is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
