Bignonia pearcei

Bignonia pearcei, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is a vigorous climber with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to well-drained soils.

Bignonia pearcei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and as a landscape vine.
