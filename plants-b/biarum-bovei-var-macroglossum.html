Biarum bovei var. macroglossum

Biarum bovei var. macroglossum, commonly known as the large-lipped bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
