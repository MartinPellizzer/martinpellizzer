Begonia bissei

Begonia bissei, commonly known as the velvet begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and vibrant, showy flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving them a soft, textured appearance. The flowers are usually red or orange, with five petals and a prominent yellow center.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds and indoor cultivation.
