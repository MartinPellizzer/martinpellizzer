Berberis hamiltoniana

Berberis hamiltoniana, commonly known as the Hamilton's barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.
