Biscutella laevigata var. cuneata

Biscutella laevigata var. cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are borne on short pedicels. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
