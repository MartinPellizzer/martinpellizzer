Babiana auriculata

Babiana auriculata, commonly known as the golden babiana, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bulbous structures called corms, which store nutrients for future growth. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Babiana auriculata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
