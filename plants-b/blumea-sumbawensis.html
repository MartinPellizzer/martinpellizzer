Blumea sumbawensis

Blumea sumbawensis, commonly known as the Sumbawan chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Blumea sumbawensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
