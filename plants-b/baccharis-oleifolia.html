Baccharis oleifolia

Baccharis oleifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the fall and winter seasons. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a leathery texture.

Baccharis oleifolia is often found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in other moist habitats.
