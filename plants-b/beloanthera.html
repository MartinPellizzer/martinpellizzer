Beloanthera

Beloanthera, commonly known as the flame orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plants are characterized by their showy, colorful flowers that often resemble flames, with bright red, orange, or yellow petals. Beloanthera species typically grow as epiphytes or lithophytes, often found on trees or rocks in humid environments.

Their unique floral structure and vibrant colors make them popular among orchid enthusiasts and horticulturists.
