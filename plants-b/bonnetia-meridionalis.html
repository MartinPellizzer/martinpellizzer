Bonnetia meridionalis

Bonnetia meridionalis, commonly known as the Southern bonnetia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to several meters in height, featuring a woody stem and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Bonnetia meridionalis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
