Baccharis clavata

Baccharis clavata, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic leaves.

Baccharis clavata is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
