Betula myrsinoides

Betula myrsinoides, commonly known as the dwarf birch, is a small shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America and Eurasia.

It is a member of the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, deciduous leaves. The plant typically grows in harsh, rocky environments and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons. Its flowers are inconspicuous, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Betula myrsinoides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in tundra and alpine habitats.
