Bersama abyssinica subsp. rosea

Bersama abyssinica subsp.

rosea, commonly known as the Abyssinian rosewood, is a small evergreen tree native to the highlands of Ethiopia. It is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and distinctive rose-like floral structures.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a leathery texture and produces small, clustered white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in horticulture.
