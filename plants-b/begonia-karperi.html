Begonia karperi

Begonia karperi, commonly known as the velvetleaf begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Begonia karperi is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
