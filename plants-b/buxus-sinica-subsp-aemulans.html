Buxus sinica subsp. aemulans

Buxus sinica subsp.

aemulans, commonly known as the Chinese boxwood, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub native to China. It features dense, dark green foliage that remains consistent throughout the year, making it ideal for topiary and hedging.

The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves with a leathery texture and a waxy surface. It produces inconspicuous yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by small, red berries that are edible for birds.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its compact growth habit and ability to tolerate pruning.
