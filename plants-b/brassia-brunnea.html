Brassia brunnea

Brassia brunnea, commonly known as the brown orchid, is a tropical orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety leaves and striking, fragrant flowers that range from pale yellow to deep brown. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are borne on slender stems and can last for several weeks.

Brassia brunnea is prized in horticulture for its unique coloration and ornamental value.
