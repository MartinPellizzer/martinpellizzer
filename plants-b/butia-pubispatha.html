Butia pubispatha

Butia pubispatha, commonly known as the Paraguay palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the dry regions of Paraguay and northern Argentina. The plant is characterized by its slender, clustered stems and palmate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It produces yellowish-pink flowers that bloom in branched clusters, followed by small, round fruits.

Butia pubispatha is adapted to arid conditions and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
