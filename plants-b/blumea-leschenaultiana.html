Blumea leschenaultiana

Blumea leschenaultiana, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Blumea leschenaultiana is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
