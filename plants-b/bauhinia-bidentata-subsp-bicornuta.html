Bauhinia bidentata subsp. bicornuta

Bauhinia bidentata subsp.

bicornuta, commonly known as the two-toothed bauhinia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves.

The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen make it ecologically and horticulturally significant.
