Briza media var. serotina

Briza media var. serotina, commonly known as the late briza or late meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, stream banks, and other wetland habitats. The plant features slender, arching stems that bear small, spike-like inflorescences with delicate, feathery flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
