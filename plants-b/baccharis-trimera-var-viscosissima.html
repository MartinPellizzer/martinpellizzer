Baccharis trimera var. viscosissima

Baccharis trimera var. viscosissima, commonly known as sticky baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular foliage that exudes a sticky resin, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers appear in clusters and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
