Bambusa minutiligulata

Bambusa minutiligulata, commonly known as the fine-leaved bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, gracefully arching culms and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its foliage is a deep green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Bambusa minutiligulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
