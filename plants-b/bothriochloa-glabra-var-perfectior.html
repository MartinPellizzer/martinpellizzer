Bothriochloa glabra var. perfectior

Bothriochloa glabra var. perfectior, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, hairless blades and compact growth habit, often forming dense, low-growing mats. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions. Its inflorescence consists of slender, open panicles with small, spikelet-like flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover in warm climates.
