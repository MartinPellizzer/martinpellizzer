Bacopa congesta

Bacopa congesta, commonly known as the small-leaf water hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves and clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes and along stream banks. It has a creeping growth habit with stolons that root at the nodes.

Bacopa congesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
