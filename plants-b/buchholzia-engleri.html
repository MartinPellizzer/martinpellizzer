Buchholzia engleri

Buchholzia engleri, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique blooming habit.
