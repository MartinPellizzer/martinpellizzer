Brucea quercifolia

Brucea quercifolia, commonly known as the Indian mangosteen or wild mangosteen, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which resemble those of the oak tree, hence its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fleshy, red berries. Brucea quercifolia is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional medicine.

Its fruit contains bioactive compounds that have been studied for their potential health benefits.
