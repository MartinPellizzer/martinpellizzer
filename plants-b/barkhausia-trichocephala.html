Barkhausia trichocephala

Barkhausia trichocephala, commonly known as the Natal grass or Natal grassveld, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, fibrous rhizomes that help it thrive in various soil types. The plant produces spikelets that are often concealed within the foliage, making it less conspicuous. Its botanical features include narrow, flat leaves and a flowering structure that is typical of the grass family Poaceae.

Barkhausia trichocephala is adapted to both moist and dry environments, contributing to its ecological versatility.
