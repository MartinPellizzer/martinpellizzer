Bidens ciliolata

Bidens ciliolata, commonly known as the hairy beggar-tick, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands and stream banks. Its small yellow flowers are followed by spiny, bracted fruits that aid in seed dispersal.

Bidens ciliolata is often found in disturbed habitats and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
