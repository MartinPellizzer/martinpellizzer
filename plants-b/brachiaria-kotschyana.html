Brachiaria kotschyana

Brachiaria kotschyana, commonly known as Kotschy's blady grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Brachiaria kotschyana is adapted to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, along roadsides, and in agricultural fields.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands make it both a weed and a potential forage species in certain regions.
