Babiana mononeura

Babiana mononeura, commonly known as the white-stemmed babiana, is a South African bulbous plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, white stems and grass-like leaves, which are typically evergreen. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a distinctive black tip. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Babiana mononeura is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
