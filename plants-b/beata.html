Beata

Beata, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Nymphaeaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, slender stalks. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and floating, often covered in a waxy cuticle that repels water.

The flowers, which can be pink, white, or yellow, are known for their symbolic significance in various cultures.
