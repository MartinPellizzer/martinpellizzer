Bignonia parviflora

Bignonia parviflora, commonly known as the yellow trumpetbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically green and smooth on both sides. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in the spring. These flowers are adapted for pollination by bees and other insects.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
