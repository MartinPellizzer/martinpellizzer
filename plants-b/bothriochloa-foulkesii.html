Bothriochloa foulkesii

Bothriochloa foulkesii, commonly known as the African lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often used for decorative purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
