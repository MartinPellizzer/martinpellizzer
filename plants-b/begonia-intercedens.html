Begonia intercedens

Begonia intercedens, commonly known as the white-veined begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white veins. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Begonia intercedens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and modest floral display.
