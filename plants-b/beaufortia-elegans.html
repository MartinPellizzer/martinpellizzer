Beaufortia elegans

Beaufortia elegans, commonly known as the slender beaufortia, is a shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas. It is well adapted to dry conditions and can tolerate coastal salt spray.

Beaufortia elegans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
