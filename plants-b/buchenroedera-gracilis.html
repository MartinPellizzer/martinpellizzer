Buchenroedera gracilis

Buchenroedera gracilis, commonly known as the slender red-rooted orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture efficiently, allowing it to survive in humid, low-light conditions.

Buchenroedera gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitats.
