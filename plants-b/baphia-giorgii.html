Baphia giorgii

Baphia giorgii, commonly known as the African baphia, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its seeds are encased in hard pods, a common feature among legumes, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation in the soil.

Baphia giorgii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage.
