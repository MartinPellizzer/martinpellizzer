Berendtia coulteri

Berendtia coulteri, commonly known as Coulter's lily, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, making it an important species for local pollinators.
