Boronia humilis

Boronia humilis, commonly known as the dwarf boronia, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It typically grows to a height of around 30 centimeters and features delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded conditions.

Boronia humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
