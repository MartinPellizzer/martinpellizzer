Bidens odorata var. rosea

Bidens odorata var. rosea, commonly known as the roseate tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and pink to rose-colored petals. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in prairies, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, spiny fruits called pappi, which aid in wind dispersal.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
