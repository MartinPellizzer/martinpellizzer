Bupleurum triradiatum subsp. arcticum

Bupleurum triradiatum subsp.

arcticum, commonly known as Arctic bupleurum, is a perennial herb native to Arctic and alpine regions. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

The plant has slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptations to extreme climates.
