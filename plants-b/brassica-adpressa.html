Brassica adpressa

Brassica adpressa, commonly known as the broadleaf mustard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has lobed leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the spring.

Brassica adpressa is often found in disturbed soils and can be an invasive species in some regions.
