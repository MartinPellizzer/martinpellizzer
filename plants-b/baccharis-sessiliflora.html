Baccharis sessiliflora

Baccharis sessiliflora, commonly known as Brazilian broom, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit. The plant features small, narrow leaves and produces yellowish to brownish flower heads that appear in clusters. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Additionally, it has been utilized in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
