Bignonia selloi

Bignonia selloi, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the family Bignoniaceae, which is characterized by its showy, often tubular flowers. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and vine-like.

Bignonia selloi is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
