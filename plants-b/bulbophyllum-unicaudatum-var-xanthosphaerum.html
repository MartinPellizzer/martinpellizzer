Bulbophyllum unicaudatum var. xanthosphaerum

Bulbophyllum unicaudatum var. xanthosphaerum, commonly known as the golden globe orchid, is a unique orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulb-like structure and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its striking floral display makes it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.

The variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
