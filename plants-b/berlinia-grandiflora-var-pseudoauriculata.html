Berlinia grandiflora var. pseudoauriculata

Berlinia grandiflora var. pseudoauriculata, commonly known as the African bell tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody pods containing flat, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
