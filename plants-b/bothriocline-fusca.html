Bothriocline fusca

Bothriocline fusca, commonly known as the black-flowered ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dark violet to black flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, cylindrical flower heads. It thrives in moist to wet soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

Bothriocline fusca is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
