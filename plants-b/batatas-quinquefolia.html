Batatas quinquefolia

Batatas quinquefolia, commonly known as the five-leaf yam, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with five distinct lobes. The plant produces tubers that are often used as a food source in traditional cuisines. Its growth habit involves twining stems that ascend support structures, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The tubers have a starchy texture and are typically boiled or roasted before consumption.
