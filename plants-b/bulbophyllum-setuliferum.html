Bulbophyllum setuliferum

Bulbophyllum setuliferum, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with a distinctive lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
