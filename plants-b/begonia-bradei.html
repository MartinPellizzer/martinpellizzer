Begonia bradei

Begonia bradei, commonly known as the Brade's begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while the flowers come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This plant is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
