Bactris bifida var. humaitensis

Bactris bifida var. humaitensis, commonly known as the Amazonian palm or humaita palm, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a tall, slender palm with a straight trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, coconut-like fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for thatching.

The plant is adapted to the humid, tropical conditions of the Amazon and plays a role in local ecosystems.
