Baikiaea ghesquiereana

Baikiaea ghesquiereana, commonly known as the African teak, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its straight, durable wood that is highly valued for its strength and resistance to decay. The tree has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellowish flowers that bloom in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution across the continent.

Baikiaea ghesquiereana is also noted for its ecological importance in providing habitat and stabilizing soil in forest ecosystems.
