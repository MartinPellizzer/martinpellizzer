Bowdichia racemosa var. parvifolia

Bowdichia racemosa var. parvifolia, commonly known as the parvifolia burr oak, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in racemes, followed by small, round fruits that split open to release seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and riparian areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
