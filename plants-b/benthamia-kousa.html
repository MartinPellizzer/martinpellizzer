Benthamia kousa

Benthamia kousa, commonly known as the Kousa dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cornaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white bracts that appear in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters and features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, clustered in umbels, while the fruit is a small, red drupe.

The Kousa dogwood is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
