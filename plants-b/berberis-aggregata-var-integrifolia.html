Berberis aggregata var. integrifolia

Berberis aggregata var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth barberry, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets that are smooth-edged, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens or as an ornamental plant.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
