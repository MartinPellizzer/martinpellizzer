Bassia sedoides subsp. ucranica

Bassia sedoides subsp.

ucranica, commonly known as the Ukrainian sandmat, is a perennial herb native to the steppes of Eastern Europe. It typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.

The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming habit with small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces yellow flowers in dense clusters during the summer months.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xerophytic gardens.
