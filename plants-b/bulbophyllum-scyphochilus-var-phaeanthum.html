Bulbophyllum scyphochilus var. phaeanthum

Bulbophyllum scyphochilus var. phaeanthum, commonly known as the golden torch orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that resemble torches, which bloom during the warm season. The plant has a compact growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, typical of many orchid species. Its leaves are fleshy and dark green, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

This variety is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
