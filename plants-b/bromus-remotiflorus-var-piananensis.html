Bromus remotiflorus var. piananensis

Bromus remotiflorus var. piananensis, commonly known as the Piananensis brome, is a flowering plant native to the grasslands of southern Africa.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to reddish bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with distinct wet and dry seasons.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals.
