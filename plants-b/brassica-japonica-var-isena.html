Brassica japonica var. isena

Brassica japonica var. isena, commonly known as the Japanese kale or Chinese kale, is a cultivar of the mustard family (Brassicaceae).

It is characterized by its thick, dark green leaves that are often curly or crinkled, with a slightly bitter taste. The plant is typically grown for its edible leaves and stems, which are used in various Asian cuisines. It prefers cool temperatures and well-drained soil, making it a suitable crop for temperate climates.

This variety is also valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of vitamins A and C.
