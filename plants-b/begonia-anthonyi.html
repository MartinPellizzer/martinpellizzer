Begonia anthonyi

Begonia anthonyi, commonly known as the Anthony's begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and clusters of bright red or orange flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a waxy texture, while the stems are often hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
