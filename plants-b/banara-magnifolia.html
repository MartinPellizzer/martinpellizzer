Banara magnifolia

Banara magnifolia, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in dense clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that provide a lush green canopy. Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions due to its aggressive spread and ecological impact.
