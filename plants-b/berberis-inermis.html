Berberis inermis

Berberis inermis, commonly known as the smooth barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thornless stems and lance-shaped leaves that turn vibrant red in the autumn. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red-orange berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Berberis inermis is also valued for its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
