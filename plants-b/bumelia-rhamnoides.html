Bumelia rhamnoides

Bumelia rhamnoides, commonly known as the greenbriar, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhamnaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, smooth, green berries that are typically eaten by various bird species. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

Bumelia rhamnoides is known for its ability to form dense thickets, which can play a role in soil stabilization and habitat provision for wildlife.
