Bromus cespitosus

Bromus cespitosus, commonly known as smooth brome, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are often tinged with purple, and its inflorescence is typically a loose panicle. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Smooth brome is widely used in forage and erosion control due to its hardiness and rapid establishment.
