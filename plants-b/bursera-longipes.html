Bursera longipes

Bursera longipes, commonly known as the longipes franklin tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Burseraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in dry, tropical climates and is often found in limestone soils.

Bursera longipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
