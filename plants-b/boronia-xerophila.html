Boronia xerophila

Boronia xerophila, commonly known as the coastal boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, tubular flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant has small, narrow leaves and is adapted to grow in sandy or coastal soils. It is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes, thriving in well-drained environments.

Boronia xerophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
