Brasiliastrum americanum

Brasiliastrum americanum, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, star-shaped fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and its fruit, which is often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
