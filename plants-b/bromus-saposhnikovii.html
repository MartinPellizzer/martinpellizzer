Bromus saposhnikovii

Bromus saposhnikovii, commonly known as the Siberian brome, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces spikelets that are often pale green to brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib.

Bromus saposhnikovii is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals.
