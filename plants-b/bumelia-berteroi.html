Bumelia berteroi

Bumelia berteroi, commonly known as the smooth bumelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in open woodlands. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly serrated margin.

Bumelia berteroi is valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various insect species.
