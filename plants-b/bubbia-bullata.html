Bubbia bullata

Bubbia bullata, commonly known as the inflated burr cactus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cactaceae.

It is native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, globular shape and spiny stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing at the base of the stems during the warmer months.

Bubbia bullata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
