Bulbophyllum apertum

Bulbophyllum apertum, commonly known as the open-flower orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, showy flowers that are often brightly colored and have a distinctive shape. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in conditions with irregular rainfall.

The flowers bloom in clusters, making it a striking addition to botanical collections and gardens.
