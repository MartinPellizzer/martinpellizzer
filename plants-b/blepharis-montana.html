Blepharis montana

Blepharis montana, commonly known as the mountain evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, showy yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Blepharis montana is a member of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
