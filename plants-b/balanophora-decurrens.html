Balanophora decurrens

Balanophora decurrens, commonly known as the yellow sandalwood, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a root-parasitic species that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow and are arranged in a dense, spike-like inflorescence.

Balanophora decurrens is found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in association with other plants in shaded forest environments.
