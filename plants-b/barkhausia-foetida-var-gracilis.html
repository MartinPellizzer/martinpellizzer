Barkhausia foetida var. gracilis

Barkhausia foetida var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender barkhausia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a strong, often unpleasant odor. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. Its bark is thick and rugged, providing structural support and protection against environmental stress.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry, rocky habitats.
