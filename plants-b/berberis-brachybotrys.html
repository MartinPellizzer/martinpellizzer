Berberis brachybotrys

Berberis brachybotrys, commonly known as the dwarf barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, arching stems with small, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often used for ornamental purposes. It is valued in horticulture for its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes.

Berberis brachybotrys thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
