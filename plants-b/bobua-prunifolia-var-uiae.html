Bobua prunifolia var. uiae

Bobua prunifolia var. uiae, commonly known as the Queensland hibiscus, is a species native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features large, showy flowers with five petals, often in shades of red, pink, or white. Its leaves are ovate and have a glossy surface, while the stems are woody and often covered in fine hairs.

The Queensland hibiscus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
