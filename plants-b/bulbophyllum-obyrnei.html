Bulbophyllum obyrnei

Bulbophyllum obyrnei, commonly known as the Yucatan orchid, is a species of orchid native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking, often fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are usually clustered at the base and are thick, fleshy, and adapted for water storage.

Bulbophyllum obyrnei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
