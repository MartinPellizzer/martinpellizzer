Bonplandia erythrochiton

Bonplandia erythrochiton, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with deep red veins. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, with a distinctive three-lobed lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
