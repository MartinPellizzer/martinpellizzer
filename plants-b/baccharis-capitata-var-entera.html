Baccharis capitata var. entera

Baccharis capitata var. entera, commonly known as the silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, clustered yellow flowers that appear in the spring. The plant thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats. Its leaves are aromatic and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is also valued in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
