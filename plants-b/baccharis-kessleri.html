Baccharis kessleri

Baccharis kessleri, commonly known as Kessler's baccharis, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.

Baccharis kessleri also plays a role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
