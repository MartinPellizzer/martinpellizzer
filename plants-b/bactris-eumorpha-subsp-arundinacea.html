Bactris eumorpha subsp. arundinacea

Bactris eumorpha subsp.

arundinacea, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that give it a palm-like appearance.

The plant produces long, cylindrical inflorescences that bear small, white flowers, followed by edible, starchy fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal, it is a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor landscaping.
