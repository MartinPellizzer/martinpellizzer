Brevipodium sylvaticum

Brevipodium sylvaticum, commonly known as the forest dwarf sedge, is a small, herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, where it thrives in the understory. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats that stabilize soil.

Brevipodium sylvaticum plays a role in maintaining ecosystem health by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
