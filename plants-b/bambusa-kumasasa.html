Bambusa kumasasa

Bambusa kumasasa, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a fast-growing grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-green culms that contrast with its dark green leaves. This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Bambusa kumasasa is also valued for its use in construction and as a source of sustainable materials.
