Bulbophyllum edentatum

Bulbophyllum edentatum, commonly known as the toothless orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and its striking, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps in reducing water loss.

Bulbophyllum edentatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
