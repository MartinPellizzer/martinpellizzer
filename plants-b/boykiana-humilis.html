Boykiana humilis

Boykiana humilis, commonly known as the lowland orchid, is a delicate flowering plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and marshes. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Boykiana humilis is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local insect populations.
