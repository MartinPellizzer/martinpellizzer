Bellis caerulescens

Bellis caerulescens, commonly known as the blue-eyed daisy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features rosettes of dark green, velvety leaves and produces showy, daisy-like flowers with blue to violet petals and a yellow center. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual appeal to gardens.

Bellis caerulescens is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
