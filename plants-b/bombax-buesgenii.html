Bombax buesgenii

Bombax buesgenii, commonly known as the Buesgen's ceiba, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters at the base of the branches. The plant produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds enclosed in a woody capsule, which is a distinctive botanical feature. It has a straight, cylindrical trunk with a smooth, often flaky bark.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems.
