Brickellia microphylla

Brickellia microphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. The plant features small, narrow leaves and produces clusters of yellow to orange flowers in the spring. Its botanical characteristics include glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Brickellia microphylla is often found in wetland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
