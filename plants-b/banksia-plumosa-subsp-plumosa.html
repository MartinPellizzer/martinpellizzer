Banksia plumosa subsp. plumosa

Banksia plumosa subsp.

plumosa, commonly known as the feathered banksia, is a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery cylindrical inflorescences that range in color from yellow to orange.

The plant produces woody cone-like structures that contain numerous small, fluffy seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in heathlands and open forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for native wildlife.
