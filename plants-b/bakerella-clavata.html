Bakerella clavata

Bakerella clavata, commonly known as the clustered bakerella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. Bakerella clavata thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
