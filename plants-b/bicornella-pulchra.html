Bicornella pulchra

Bicornella pulchra, commonly known as the beautiful bicorn, is a striking member of the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, such as woodlands and stream banks. Its distinctive two-lobed fruit, known as a schizocarp, is a notable botanical feature.

Bicornella pulchra is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
