Botryanthus neglectus subsp. odorus

Botryanthus neglectus subsp.

odorus, commonly known as the fragrant bottlebrush. This plant is characterized by its unique inflorescences, which resemble dense clusters of small, tubular flowers.

The botanical features include its woody stems and specialized floral structures that facilitate pollination by specific insect species. It is native to certain regions of South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more pronounced aromatic properties compared to other members of the species.
