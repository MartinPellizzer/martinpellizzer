Berberis tsarica var. tsarica

Berberis tsarica var. tsarica, commonly known as the Tsar's barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant seasonal color changes and architectural form.
