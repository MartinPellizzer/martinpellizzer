Betonica grandiflora

Betonica grandiflora, commonly known as greater betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, while its flowers bloom in clusters at the top of the stems.

Betonica grandiflora is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
