Bocconia frutescens subvar. glaucescens

Bocconia frutescens subvar. glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous bocconia, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, often glaucous (bluish-gray) stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
