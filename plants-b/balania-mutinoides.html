Balania mutinoides

Balania mutinoides, commonly known as the Mutinoides balsam tree, is a rare and unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen foliage and distinctive, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a dense, compact growth habit and produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural study and conservation efforts.
