Bignonia perforata

Bignonia perforata, commonly known as the scarlet trumpetvine, is a vigorous climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive reddish underside. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically orange to red in color, blooming from spring to early summer. Its stems are covered in fine, hairy glands, and it has a strong, woody vine structure.

Bignonia perforata is often used in ornamental gardening but can be invasive in certain regions.
