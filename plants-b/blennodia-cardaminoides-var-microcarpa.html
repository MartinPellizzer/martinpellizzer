Blennodia cardaminoides var. microcarpa

Blennodia cardaminoides var. microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along stream banks and wetlands. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds upon maturity.

This variety is often distinguished by its compact growth habit and reduced fruit size compared to other varieties within the species.
