Baccharis melanopotamica

Baccharis melanopotamica, commonly known as the black river baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina, Brazil, and Paraguay.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical or subtropical environments, often near rivers or in wetlands. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Baccharis melanopotamica is known for its adaptability to wet conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in riparian zones.
