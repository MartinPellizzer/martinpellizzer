Bigelowia douglasii var. latifolia

Bigelowia douglasii var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved bigelowia, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
