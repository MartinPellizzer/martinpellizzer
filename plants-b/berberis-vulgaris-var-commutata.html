Berberis vulgaris var. commutata

Berberis vulgaris var. commutata, commonly known as the barberry, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

The variety commutata is distinguished by its more compact growth and narrower leaves compared to the typical form.
