Berberis lucida

Berberis lucida, commonly known as the shining barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces orange-red flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and as a hedge.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant seasonal color changes and compact growth habit.
