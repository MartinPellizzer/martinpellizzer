Berberis nepalensis

Berberis nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayan region.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
