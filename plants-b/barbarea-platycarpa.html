Barbarea platycarpa

Barbarea platycarpa, commonly known as the broadleaf winter cress, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are broad and lobed, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Barbarea platycarpa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and woodland edges.
