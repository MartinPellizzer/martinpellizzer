Baccharis marginalis var. viminea

Baccharis marginalis var. viminea, commonly known as silky baccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, silver-green leaves that are softly hairy, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the fall and winter months. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its adaptability to moist soils makes it a common component of coastal and freshwater habitats.
