Baldellia ranunculoides var. repens

Baldellia ranunculoides var. repens, commonly known as the creeping water buttercup, is a perennial aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds, marshes, and wet meadows. The plant has a creeping growth habit, with rhizomes that allow it to spread across the substrate. Its leaves are alternate, floating or submerged, and have a distinctive heart-shaped base.

The flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in a terminal umbel, often appearing in late spring to early summer.
