Bambusa somdevae

Bambusa somdevae, commonly known as the Somdeva bamboo, is a species native to southern China and northern Vietnam.

It is a tall, slender grass that can grow up to 15 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, dark green culms. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Bambusa somdevae is valued for its strong, lightweight culms, which are used in construction and crafts.
