Boea darrisii

Boea darrisii, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature. Its leaves are typically ovate with a waxy, silvery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Boea darrisii is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in partial shade.
