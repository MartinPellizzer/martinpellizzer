Berardia sphaerocephala

Berardia sphaerocephala, commonly known as the globe-headed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical flower heads, which are typically blue or purple in color. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Berardia sphaerocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
