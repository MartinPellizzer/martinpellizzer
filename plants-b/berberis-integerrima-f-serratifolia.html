Berberis integerrima f. serratifolia

Berberis integerrima f. serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated barberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of southern China.

It features prickly, thorned stems and compound leaves with serrated margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, though its toxicity should be considered.
