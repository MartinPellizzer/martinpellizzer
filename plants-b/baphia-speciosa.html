Baphia speciosa

Baphia speciosa, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pendulous yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with alternate arrangement and can grow up to 20 meters in height. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Baphia speciosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
