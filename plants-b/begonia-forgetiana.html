Begonia forgetiana

Begonia forgetiana, commonly known as the silver leaf begonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery or white powdery coating, known as a bloom. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white, typically arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor and shaded garden settings.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its unique foliage rather than its flowers.
