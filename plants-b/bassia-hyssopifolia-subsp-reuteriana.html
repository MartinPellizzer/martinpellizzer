Bassia hyssopifolia subsp. reuteriana

Bassia hyssopifolia subsp.

reuteriana, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple.

The plant has gray-green, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in dry environments. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

This subspecies is adapted to extreme heat and drought, making it a resilient component of its ecosystem.
