Beadlea glabrescens

Beadlea glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth beadlea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, upright stems that are usually covered with fine, short hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

Beadlea glabrescens is characterized by its simple morphology and its ability to thrive in wet environments.
