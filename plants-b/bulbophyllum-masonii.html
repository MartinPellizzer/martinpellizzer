Bulbophyllum masonii

Bulbophyllum masonii, commonly known as the Mason's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the presence of pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients. The plant produces intricate, often fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and unique foliage.
