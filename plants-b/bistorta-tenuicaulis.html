Bistorta tenuicaulis

Bistorta tenuicaulis, commonly known as narrow-stemmed bistorta, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes buckwheat and sorrel. The plant features upright, slender stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late summer.

Bistorta tenuicaulis is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.
