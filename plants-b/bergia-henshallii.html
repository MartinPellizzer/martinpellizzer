Bergia henshallii

Bergia henshallii, commonly known as the Cape Peninsula bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, often found in the fynbos biome. The plant produces showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clumps.

Bergia henshallii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
