Betula-alnus maritima

Betula-alnus maritima, commonly known as the coastal birch, is a hybrid species that exhibits characteristics of both birch and alder trees.

It is a small, deciduous tree or shrub native to coastal regions of northern Europe and North America. The plant has smooth, white bark that peels in thin layers, and its leaves are simple, alternate, and oval in shape. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in tidal zones and salt marshes.

Betula-alnus maritima plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
