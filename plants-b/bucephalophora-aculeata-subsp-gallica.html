Bucephalophora aculeata subsp. gallica

Bucephalophora aculeata subsp.

gallica, commonly known as the Gallic thorned bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more pointed leaves compared to other members of the species. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
