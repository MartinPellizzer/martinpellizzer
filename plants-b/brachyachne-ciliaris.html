Brachyachne ciliaris

Brachyachne ciliaris, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandreed, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spikelet-based flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small wildlife.

Brachyachne ciliaris is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
