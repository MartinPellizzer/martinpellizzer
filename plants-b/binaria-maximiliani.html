Binaria maximiliani

Binaria maximiliani, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
