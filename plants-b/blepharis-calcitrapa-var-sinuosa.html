Blepharis calcitrapa var. sinuosa

Blepharis calcitrapa var. sinuosa, commonly known as the sinuous wiregrass, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, grass-like leaves that are often rolled or folded. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color, blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native range.
