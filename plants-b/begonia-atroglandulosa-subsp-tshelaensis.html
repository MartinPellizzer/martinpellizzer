Begonia atroglandulosa subsp. tshelaensis

Begonia atroglandulosa subsp.

tshelaensis, commonly known as the Tshelaensis begonia, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of Central Africa. It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display deep green hues with prominent veins.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, typically blooming during the wet season. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and less commonly cultivated species.
