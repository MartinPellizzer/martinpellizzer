Brasiliopuntia brasiliensis subsp. bahiensis

Brasiliopuntia brasiliensis subsp.

bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahia prickly pear, is a cactus species native to Brazil. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its flattened, ribbed stems that store water.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the dry season, which are followed by edible, red fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in coastal regions.

Its structure and adaptations make it well-suited for survival in harsh, dry climates.
