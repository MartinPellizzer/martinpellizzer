Barleria mpandensis subsp. mpandensis

Barleria mpandensis subsp.

mpandensis, commonly known as the Mpandeni barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
