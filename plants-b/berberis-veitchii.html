Berberis veitchii

Berberis veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but not widely consumed. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Berberis veitchii is adaptable to various soil types and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
