Barleria chlamydocalyx

Barleria chlamydocalyx, commonly known as the velvetleaf barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. Its flowers are clustered in dense, terminal spikes and are usually pink to reddish in color.

Barleria chlamydocalyx is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
