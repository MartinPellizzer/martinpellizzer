Biscutella auriculata subsp. brevicalcarata

Biscutella auriculata subsp.

brevicalcarata, commonly known as the narrow-fruited bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple.

The leaves are lanceolate, with a hairy surface, and the plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in rock gardens and alpine collections.
