Brownea neglecta

Brownea neglecta, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds.

Brownea neglecta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various growing conditions.
