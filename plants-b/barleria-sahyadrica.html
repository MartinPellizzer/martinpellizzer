Barleria sahyadrica

Barleria sahyadrica, commonly known as the Sahyadri barleria, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive violet or purple coloration. Its flowers are small, clustered in terminal racemes, and are usually pink or purple in color.

Barleria sahyadrica is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
