Baccharis leucanthemifolia

Baccharis leucanthemifolia, commonly known as white thistle bush, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites. Its foliage and flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their ornamental appearance.

Baccharis leucanthemifolia is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
