Bulbophyllum marginatum

Bulbophyllum marginatum, commonly known as the margined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulb-like structure and linear, often hairy leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Bulbophyllum marginatum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
