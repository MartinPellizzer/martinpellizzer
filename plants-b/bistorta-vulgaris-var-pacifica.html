Bistorta vulgaris var. pacifica

Bistorta vulgaris var. pacifica, commonly known as Pacific bistorta, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features rhubarb-like leaves that are thick and fleshy, with a reddish or purple tint on the underside. Its stems are often reddish and bear clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and resilience.
