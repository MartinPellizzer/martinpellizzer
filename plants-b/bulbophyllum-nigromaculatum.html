Bulbophyllum nigromaculatum

Bulbophyllum nigromaculatum, commonly known as the black-spotted orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers with purple and white petals. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often pollinated by insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
