Bauhinia versicolor

Bauhinia versicolor, commonly known as the rainbow bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves that are often divided into small, feather-like segments. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom throughout the year in suitable climates. Bauhinia versicolor thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability contribute to its appeal in both landscape design and horticultural collections.
