Brunsvigia appendiculata

Brunsvigia appendiculata, commonly known as the firecracker lily, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features striking red or orange flowers that resemble tiny firecrackers, hence its common name. The plant has a distinctive, segmented stem with a swollen base that stores water and nutrients. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to dry, Mediterranean climates.
