Bulbophyllum entobaptum

Bulbophyllum entobaptum, commonly known as the sacred ghost orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale pink, with intricate patterns resembling ghostly figures. Its unique appearance and habitat preferences make it a rare and sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered in many areas.
