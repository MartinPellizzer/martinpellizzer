Blennodia nasturtioides

Blennodia nasturtioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, alternate leaves that are typically narrow and slightly succulent. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils near water bodies and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Due to its peppery flavor, it is sometimes used in culinary applications, though it is more valued for its ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
