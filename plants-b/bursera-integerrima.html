Bursera integerrima

Bursera integerrima, commonly known as the Mexican bitter ash, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and leathery, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the branches. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by orange-red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The tree is also valued for its aromatic resins, which have been used traditionally in medicinal and ritual contexts.
