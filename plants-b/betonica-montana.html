Betonica montana

Betonica montana, commonly known as mountain betony, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive blue-green hue.

Betonica montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
