Banksia croajingolensis

Banksia croajingolensis, commonly known as the Croajingolensis banksia, is a striking shrub or small tree native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical or conical flower spikes, which are typically orange to yellow in color. The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, a feature typical of the Proteaceae family. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a valued species in both conservation and horticultural contexts.
