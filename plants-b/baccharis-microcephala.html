Baccharis microcephala

Baccharis microcephala, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the spring and summer months. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Baccharis microcephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
