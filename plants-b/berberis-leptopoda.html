Berberis leptopoda

Berberis leptopoda, commonly known as the slender barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Berberis leptopoda is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses.
