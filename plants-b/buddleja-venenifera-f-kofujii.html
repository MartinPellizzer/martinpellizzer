Buddleja venenifera f. kofujii

Buddleja venenifera f. kofujii, commonly known as the Japanese butterfly bush, is a cultivar of the Buddleja genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 3 meters in height.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its showy flower display.
