Bromus rubens var. villosulus

Bromus rubens var. villosulus, commonly known as the hairy false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, hairy stems that often have a reddish tinge. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of spikelets, which are typically greenish or reddish in color.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or rocky habitats.
