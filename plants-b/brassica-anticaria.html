Brassica anticaria

Brassica anticaria, commonly known as wild mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in various habitats across Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its wide distribution.

Brassica anticaria is also known for its potential medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
