Betula bhojpattra var. genuina

Betula bhojpattra var. genuina, commonly known as the sacred birch, is a unique variant of the birch genus native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its small, triangular leaves that are typically white or pale green on the underside. The plant is noted for its aromatic bark, which emits a distinctive scent when crushed. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at high altitudes.

This variety holds cultural and medicinal significance in traditional practices of the region.
