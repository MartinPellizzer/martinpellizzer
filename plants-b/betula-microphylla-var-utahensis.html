Betula microphylla var. utahensis

Betula microphylla var. utahensis, commonly known as the Utah paper birch, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals layers of yellow and orange beneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, catkin-like flowers and oval, green leaves that turn gold in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The Utah paper birch plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
