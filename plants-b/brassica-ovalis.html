Brassica ovalis

Brassica ovalis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, oval-shaped leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Brassica ovalis is an important species in ecological studies due to its role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
