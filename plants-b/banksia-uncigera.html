Banksia uncigera

Banksia uncigera, commonly known as the narrow-leaf banksia, is a species native to southern Western Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its cylindrical, yellow to orange flower spikes that appear in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a greyish-green hue.

The species is adapted to fire-prone environments and relies on fire for seed release.
