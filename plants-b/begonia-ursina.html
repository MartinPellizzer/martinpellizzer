Begonia ursina

Begonia ursina, commonly known as the bear's breech, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Begonia ursina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
