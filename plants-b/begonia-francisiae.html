Begonia francisiae

Begonia francisiae, commonly known as the French begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, brightly colored bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its foliage is usually glossy and dark green, with some varieties displaying variegated patterns.

Begonia francisiae prefers warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
