Berberis linearifolia

Berberis linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with elongated, linear leaflets that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant autumn foliage, which turns a striking orange-red.

Berberis linearifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual appeal.
