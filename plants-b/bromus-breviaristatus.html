Bromus breviaristatus

Bromus breviaristatus, commonly known as the short-awned brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it is characterized by its short awns on the lemma tips.

Bromus breviaristatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
