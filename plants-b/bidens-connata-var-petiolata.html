Bidens connata var. petiolata

Bidens connata var. petiolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features narrow, petioled leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers with prominent central cones. It thrives in moist, sunny environments such as wetlands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for pollinators and as a component of wetland ecosystems.
