Bidens messerschmidii

Bidens messerschmidii, commonly known as the yellow beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features yellow flowers with ray florets and a central disk, typical of the genus Bidens. It grows in moist habitats such as marshes and wetlands, often forming dense colonies.

Its leaves are typically oval with rough textures, and it produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by wind.
