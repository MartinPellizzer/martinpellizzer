Bromelia concentrica

Bromelia concentrica, commonly known as the concentric bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that surround the central inflorescence, creating a visually appealing display. The plant features a rosette of broad, fleshy leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater to support its growth. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, adding to the plant's ornamental value.

Bromelia concentrica thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
