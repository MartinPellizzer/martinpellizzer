Bauhinia subrhombicarpa

Bauhinia subrhombicarpa, commonly known as the scarlet bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain flat, seed-like structures called arils. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Bauhinia subrhombicarpa is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
