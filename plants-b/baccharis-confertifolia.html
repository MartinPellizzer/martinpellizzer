Baccharis confertifolia

Baccharis confertifolia, commonly known as goldenrod baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, winged fruits.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
