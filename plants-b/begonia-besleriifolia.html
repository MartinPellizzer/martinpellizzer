Begonia besleriifolia

Begonia besleriifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-white variegated leaves, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
