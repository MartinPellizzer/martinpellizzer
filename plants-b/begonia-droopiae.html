Begonia droopiae

Begonia droopiae, commonly known as the drooping begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with intricate patterns on the petals. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, providing a unique visual texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to produce multiple blooms throughout the growing season.
