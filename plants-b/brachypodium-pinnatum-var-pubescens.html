Brachypodium pinnatum var. pubescens

Brachypodium pinnatum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy little grass, is a perennial grass species native to regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
