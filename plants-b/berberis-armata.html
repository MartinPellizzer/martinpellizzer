Berberis armata

Berberis armata, commonly known as the thorny barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, spiny stems and small, dark green leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, followed by red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Berberis armata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
