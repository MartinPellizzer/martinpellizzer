Blumea trigona

Blumea trigona, commonly known as the three-angled blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its stems are often hairy and can become woody with age.

Blumea trigona is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
