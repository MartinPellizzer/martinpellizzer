Banisteria nummifera

Banisteria nummifera, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with alternating leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in tropical regions.
