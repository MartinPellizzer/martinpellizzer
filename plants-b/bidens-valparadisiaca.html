Bidens valparadisiaca

Bidens valparadisiaca, commonly known as the yellow beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers with five petals and distinctive bracts that resemble ticks. The plant is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and fields, due to its adaptability to various soil types. It produces seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
