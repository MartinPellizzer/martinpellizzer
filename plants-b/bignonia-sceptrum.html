Bignonia sceptrum

Bignonia sceptrum, commonly known as the trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces showy, orange-red tubular flowers that attract hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and can grow up to 30 feet in length. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Bignonia sceptrum is also valued for its ability to climb and its long blooming season from spring to early summer.
