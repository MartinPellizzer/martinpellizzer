Beckera dioica

Beckera dioica, commonly known as the two-horned bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive pod-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, woody pods, which split open to release the seeds.

Beckera dioica is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
