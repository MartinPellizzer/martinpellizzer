Bromus hordeaceus var. typicus

Bromus hordeaceus var. typicus, commonly known as the barley grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

Bromus hordeaceus var. typicus is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional herbal medicine and as a forage crop.
