Boquila trifoliolata

Boquila trifoliolata, commonly known as the three-leaved vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a fan-like shape. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. Boquila trifoliolata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
