Bursera jerzyi

Bursera jerzyi, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for producing aromatic resins. The tree has smooth, gray bark that peels in thin layers, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spherical fruits that split open to release seeds.

This plant is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal and tropical regions.
