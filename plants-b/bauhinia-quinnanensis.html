Bauhinia quinnanensis

Bauhinia quinnanensis, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive papery, fern-like leaves. The plant produces showy clusters of red or pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Bauhinia quinnanensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
