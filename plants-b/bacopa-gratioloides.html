Bacopa gratioloides

Bacopa gratioloides, commonly known as the small-leaf water hyssop, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, narrow leaves, and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies such as ponds and streams. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its aromatic foliage.

Bacopa gratioloides is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential cognitive and therapeutic benefits.
