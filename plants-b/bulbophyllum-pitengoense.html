Bulbophyllum pitengoense

Bulbophyllum pitengoense, commonly known as the Pitengo orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces strikingly colorful, fragrant flowers. The plant typically features pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, which is a common adaptation in epiphytic orchids. Its flowers are usually borne on branching inflorescences and can vary in color from white to pink and purple.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated for its unique appearance and resilience.
