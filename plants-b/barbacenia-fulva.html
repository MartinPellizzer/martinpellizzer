Barbacenia fulva

Barbacenia fulva, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with numerous small, clustered blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically broad and elliptical, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss in its humid habitat.

Barbacenia fulva is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
