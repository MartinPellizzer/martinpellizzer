Brassica monensis var. arvensis

Brassica monensis var. arvensis, commonly known as field mustard, is a flowering plant in the mustard family (Brassicaceae).

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and alternate, lobed leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the Brassicaceae family. Its fruit is a long, slender pod containing several seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a common weed in agricultural fields.
