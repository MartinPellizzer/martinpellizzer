Byttneria aculeata

Byttneria aculeata, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its woody stems and showy, hibiscus-like flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture with prominent veins.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.
