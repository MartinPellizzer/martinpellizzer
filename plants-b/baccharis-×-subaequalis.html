Baccharis × subaequalis

Baccharis × subaequalis, commonly known as the hybrid sandbar willow, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is a hybrid between Baccharis halimifolia and Baccharis sarmentosa, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found along the edges of ponds and marshes.

Its adaptability to moist soils makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
