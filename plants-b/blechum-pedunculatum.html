Blechum pedunculatum

Blechum pedunculatum, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
