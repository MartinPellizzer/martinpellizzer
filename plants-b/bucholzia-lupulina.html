Bucholzia lupulina

Bucholzia lupulina, commonly known as the yellow moonseed, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, woody fruits. Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, orange-colored aril, which is often used in traditional medicine.

Bucholzia lupulina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates.
