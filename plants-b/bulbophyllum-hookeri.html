Bulbophyllum hookeri

Bulbophyllum hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of white, yellow, or green.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
