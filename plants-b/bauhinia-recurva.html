Bauhinia recurva

Bauhinia recurva, commonly known as the velvetwood or flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often covered in a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of vibrant, flame-colored flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a woody pod containing several flat, kidney-shaped seeds.

Bauhinia recurva is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and along roadsides for its visual impact.
