Brassica virgata

Brassica virgata, commonly known as the woolly mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces slender, elongated pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. Brassica virgata thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in dry, open habitats.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard.
