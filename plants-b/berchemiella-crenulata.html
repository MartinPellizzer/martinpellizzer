Berchemiella crenulata

Berchemiella crenulata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like inflorescence with yellow ray florets and purple disk florets. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Berchemiella crenulata is drought-tolerant and prefers full sun to partial shade.
