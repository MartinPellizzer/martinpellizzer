Bidens warszewicziana var. bipinnata

Bidens warszewicziana var. bipinnata, commonly known as the bipinnate ironweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plants with both aesthetic and ecological benefits.
