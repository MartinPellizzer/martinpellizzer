Berrya ammonilla

Berrya ammonilla, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green berries that ripen to a bright orange-red color. The plant is valued for its high vitamin C content and is widely used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while its flowers are white and fragrant.

Berrya ammonilla thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
