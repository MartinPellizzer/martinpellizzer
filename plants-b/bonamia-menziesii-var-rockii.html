Bonamia menziesii var. rockii

Bonamia menziesii var. rockii, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its growth habit is low and spreading, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
