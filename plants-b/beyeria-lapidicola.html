Beyeria lapidicola

Beyeria lapidicola, commonly known as the stone-root, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features a stout, succulent stem with opposite, fleshy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Beyeria lapidicola is adapted to arid environments and is often found in regions with limited rainfall.
