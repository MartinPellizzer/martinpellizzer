Baccharis cabrerae

Baccharis cabrerae, commonly known as the Mexican baccharis, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

The flowers are small and clustered, often producing a fluffy appearance that aids in wind pollination.
