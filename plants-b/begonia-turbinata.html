Begonia turbinata

Begonia turbinata, commonly known as the Turban Begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink and white. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Begonia turbinata thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, low-maintenance foliage.
