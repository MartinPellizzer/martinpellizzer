Bruschia

Bruschia, commonly known as the "Mexican morning glory," is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and Mexico.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces showy blooms in shades of purple, blue, and white, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often climb or trail along the ground.

Bruschia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
