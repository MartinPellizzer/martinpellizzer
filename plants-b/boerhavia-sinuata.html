Boerhavia sinuata

Boerhavia sinuata, commonly known as velvetleaf or redroot, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often pubescent, giving the plant a velvety texture. The flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

The plant is known for its ability to spread rapidly through seeds and vegetative reproduction.
