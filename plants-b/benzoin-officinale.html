Benzoin officinale

Benzoin officinale, commonly known as the gum benzoin tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces a resinous sap, which is harvested as benzoin, a substance used in perfumes and traditional medicine. Benzoin officinale thrives in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
