Bromus secalinus var. hordeaceus

Bromus secalinus var. hordeaceus, commonly known as the barley grass or false barley, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in disturbed areas.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it can become invasive in certain environments.
