Berberis spraguei var. pedunculata

Berberis spraguei var. pedunculata, commonly known as the Sprague barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it suitable for erosion control and as a landscape plant in suitable climates.
