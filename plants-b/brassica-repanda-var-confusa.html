Brassica repanda var. confusa

Brassica repanda var. confusa, commonly known as the broadleaf mustard, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and segmented fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are usually yellow or pale yellow, and it produces siliques, which are elongated, narrow pods containing several seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its ornamental value and potential use in herbal medicine.
