Bulbostylis paraensis

Bulbostylis paraensis, commonly known as the Paraensis bulbostylis, is a small, grass-like plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family. This species is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or wetlands.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, typical of the Cyperaceae family.
