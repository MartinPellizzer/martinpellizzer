Bauhinia meeboldii

Bauhinia meeboldii, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing before the leaves in spring. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are compound and divided into numerous small leaflets. Its flowers are typically tubular with five lobes, and they emit a sweet fragrance.

Bauhinia meeboldii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil types.
