Brachychiton × carneus

Brachychiton × carneus, commonly known as the red velvet maple, is a hybrid plant that exhibits striking botanical features.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub native to Australia, characterized by its velvety, red-brown bark that peels in thin layers. The plant produces showy, maple-like leaves that are palmately lobed and often have a reddish tinge. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow and appear in clusters, followed by distinctive, elongated seed pods.

Brachychiton × carneus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental bark and seasonal foliage changes.
