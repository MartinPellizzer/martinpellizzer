Bupleurum pyrenaeum

Bupleurum pyrenaeum, commonly known as the Pyrenean angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and umbellate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils at high altitudes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its slender stems bear pinnately compound leaves with a distinctive silvery underside.

The small white flowers appear in late spring, followed by cylindrical fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.
