Brachanthemum kasakhorum

Brachanthemum kasakhorum, commonly known as the Kazakh daisy, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers. The plant features showy yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, typical of the family. It is often found in rocky or arid environments, adapted to thrive in harsh conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in regions with suitable climates.
