Blysmus compressus var. brevifolius

Blysmus compressus var. brevifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blysmus, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
