Bromus sterilis var. viridis

Bromus sterilis var. viridis, commonly known as green false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in grasslands, open woodlands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of spikelets in the summer. Its flowering period occurs from June to August, and it is characterized by its bright green coloration.

The species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
