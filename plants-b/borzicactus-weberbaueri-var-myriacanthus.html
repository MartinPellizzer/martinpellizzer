Borzicactus weberbaueri var. myriacanthus

Borzicactus weberbaueri var. myriacanthus, commonly known as the Weberbauer's borzicactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to the Sonoran Desert of North America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and clusters of long, spiny bracts that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often found in protected areas and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
