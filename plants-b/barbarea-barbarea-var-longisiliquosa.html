Barbarea barbarea var. longisiliquosa

Barbarea barbarea var. longisiliquosa, commonly known as the long silique winter cress, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces long, slender pods called silique, which are a distinguishing feature of the species. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along riverbanks and in meadows.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
