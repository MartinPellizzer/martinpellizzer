Baccharis resediflora

Baccharis resediflora, commonly known as the redflower baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Brazil.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in warmer regions.
