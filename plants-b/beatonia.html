Beatonia

Beatonia, commonly known as the bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, sky-blue flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, soft hair, while its stems are often hairy as well.

Beatonia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
