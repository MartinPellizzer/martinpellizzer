Bulbophyllum illecebrum

Bulbophyllum illecebrum, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. The plant produces striking, spider-like flowers with elongated petals and a distinctive, narrow lip. Its growth habit involves a pseudobulb, which stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in variable conditions.

Bulbophyllum illecebrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
