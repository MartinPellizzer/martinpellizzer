Berberis taronensis var. trimensis

Berberis taronensis var. trimensis, commonly known as the Armenian barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the autumn. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.

The species is also notable for its potential medicinal properties due to the presence of alkaloids in its berries.
