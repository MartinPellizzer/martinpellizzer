Brassica fruticulosa subsp. rifana

Brassica fruticulosa subsp.

rifana, commonly known as the Rif mustard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing a compact, shrub-like form with small, alternate leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are characteristic of the Brassicaceae family. Its seeds are enclosed in siliques, a type of dry fruit typical of the mustard family.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
