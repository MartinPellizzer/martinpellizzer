Bidens steppia

Bidens steppia, commonly known as the steppe beggarticks, is a herbaceous annual plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers with five petals, typical of the Asteraceae family. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
