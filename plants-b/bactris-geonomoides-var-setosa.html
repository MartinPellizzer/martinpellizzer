Bactris geonomoides var. setosa

Bactris geonomoides var. setosa, commonly known as the hairy palm, is a species of palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that are covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers, which are followed by elongated, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland areas with rich, well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
