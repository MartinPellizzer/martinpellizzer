Brickellia atractyloides var. arguta

Brickellia atractyloides var. arguta, commonly known as roughleaf brickellia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are rough and hairy, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
