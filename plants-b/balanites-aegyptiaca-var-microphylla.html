Balanites aegyptiaca var. microphylla

Balanites aegyptiaca var. microphylla, commonly known as the little leaf date tree, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its small, thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that contain a single large seed. Its deep root system allows it to access groundwater, making it highly drought-tolerant.

It is also valued for its ornamental appearance and traditional uses in local cultures.
