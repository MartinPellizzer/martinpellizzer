Bromus sterilis var. trichanthus

Bromus sterilis var. trichanthus, commonly known as the trichanthus brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often lie flat on the ground. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish florets. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and grasslands.
