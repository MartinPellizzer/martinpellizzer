Ballota rugosa

Ballota rugosa, commonly known as the rough ballota, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Leguminosae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its hairy, rugose leaves give it a distinctive texture, and it often grows in rocky or dry soils.

Ballota rugosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
