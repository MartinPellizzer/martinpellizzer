Baliospermum analayanum

Baliospermum analayanum, commonly known as the Malabar balsam, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in racemes, often displaying shades of pink or white.

Baliospermum analayanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
