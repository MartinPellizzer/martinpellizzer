Bromidium hygrometricum var. villosum

Bromidium hygrometricum var. villosum, commonly known as the woolly bromidium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and compact size, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
