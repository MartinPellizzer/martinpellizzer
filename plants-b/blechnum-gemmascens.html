Blechnum gemmascens

Blechnum gemmascens, commonly known as the gemma fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its intricate, finely divided fronds. The plant produces gemmae, which are small vegetative propagules that allow for asexual reproduction. Its fronds are typically evergreen and can grow up to 1 meter in length.

Blechnum gemmascens thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.
