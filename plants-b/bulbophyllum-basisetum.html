Bulbophyllum basisetum

Bulbophyllum basisetum, commonly known as the hairy-stalked orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, hairy stems and small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are often elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The flowers, which can be white or pale yellow, are adorned with intricate patterns and are highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts.
