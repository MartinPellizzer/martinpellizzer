Bulbine macrocarpa

Bulbine macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant bulbine, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features succulent, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces showy, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its robust root system allows it to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun.

Bulbine macrocarpa is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
