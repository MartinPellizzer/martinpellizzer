Baccharis lutescens

Baccharis lutescens, commonly known as the yellow baccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and small, white to yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Baccharis lutescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
