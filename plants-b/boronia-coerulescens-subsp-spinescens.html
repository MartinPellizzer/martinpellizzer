Boronia coerulescens subsp. spinescens

Boronia coerulescens subsp.

spinescens, commonly known as the Spiny Boronia, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, spiny leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs during the cooler months, usually from late winter to early spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in supporting local wildlife.
