Brickellia filipes

Brickellia filipes, commonly known as slenderleaf brickellia, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. It has a woody base and can reach heights of up to 30 inches.

Brickellia filipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in native plant gardens.
