Bigelowia nudata var. australis

Bigelowia nudata var. australis, commonly known as the Southern Bigelowia, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and salt-laden environments. It produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.

Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.
