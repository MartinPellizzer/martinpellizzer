Bambusa papillatoides

Bambusa papillatoides, commonly known as the papillate bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as India, China, and Indonesia. This bamboo is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
