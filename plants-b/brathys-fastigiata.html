Brathys fastigiata

Brathys fastigiata, commonly known as the upright brathys, is a unique plant species characterized by its tall, columnar growth habit.

It is native to specific regions in South Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Brathys fastigiata is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
