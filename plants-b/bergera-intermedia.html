Bergera intermedia

Bergera intermedia, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange ray florets and dark brown disk florets. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Bergera intermedia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy flowers and hardy nature.

It is also used in traditional medicine in some regions for its purported healing properties.
