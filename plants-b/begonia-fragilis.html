Begonia fragilis

Begonia fragilis, commonly known as the delicate begonia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular in horticulture for indoor and shaded garden settings.
