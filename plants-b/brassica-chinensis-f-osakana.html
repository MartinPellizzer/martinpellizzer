Brassica chinensis f. osakana

Brassica chinensis f. osakana, commonly known as Osaka mustard, is a cultivar of the mustard plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, round, green pods that contain edible seeds used in traditional Asian cuisine. It thrives in cool climates and is often grown as an ornamental or for its edible parts.

This variety is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and culinary applications.
