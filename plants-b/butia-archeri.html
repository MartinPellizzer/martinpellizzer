Butia archeri

Butia archeri, commonly known as the Paraguayan palm, is a small, cold-hardy palm native to South America.

It is characterized by its clustering growth habit and stiff, grayish-blue leaves that form a compact rosette. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, red, fleshy fruits. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique appearance and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
