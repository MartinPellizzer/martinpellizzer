Bambusa gurgandii

Bambusa gurgandii, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of giant bamboo native to southern China.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms that contrast with its dark green leaves. This plant is a clump-forming bamboo, growing in dense stands and reaching heights of up to 15 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

Bambusa gurgandii is also valued for its strong and lightweight culms, which are used in construction and crafts.
