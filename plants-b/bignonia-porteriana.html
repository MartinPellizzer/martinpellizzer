Bignonia porteriana

Bignonia porteriana, commonly known as the scarlet trumpetbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces long, slender seed pods that split open to release seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Bignonia porteriana is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
