Blechnum camfieldii

Blechnum camfieldii, commonly known as the New Zealand maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky areas. Its fronds are pinnately divided and have a distinctive, arching appearance.

Blechnum camfieldii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
