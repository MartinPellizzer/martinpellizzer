Bonannia resinosa

Bonannia resinosa, commonly known as the resinous flame tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant produces a distinctive resin that has been historically used by Indigenous communities for various purposes. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with a glossy green appearance.

Bonannia resinosa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas with a warm climate.
