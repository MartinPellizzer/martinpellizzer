Bulbophyllum hispidum

Bulbophyllum hispidum, commonly known as the hairy bulbophyllum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulb-like structure and hairy, dark green leaves. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Bulbophyllum hispidum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral characteristics.
