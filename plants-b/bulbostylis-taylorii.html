Bulbostylis taylorii

Bulbostylis taylorii, commonly known as Taylor's bulbostylis, is a small herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clustered inflorescences. The plant typically produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which are often pale green or brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Bulbostylis taylorii is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.
