Bernardia gambosa

Bernardia gambosa, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical tree native to Central and Eastern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that are borne in clusters, followed by edible, star-shaped fruits with a sweet, juicy pulp.

Its fruit is often consumed fresh or processed into jams and beverages, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
