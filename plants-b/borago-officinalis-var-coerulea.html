Borago officinalis var. coerulea

Borago officinalis var. coerulea, commonly known as blue borage, is a variant of the common borage plant.

It is characterized by its striking blue flowers, which are typically five-petaled and arranged in a star shape. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. This plant is native to Europe and thrives in temperate climates, often found in meadows and along stream banks.

It is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its historical use in herbal medicine.
