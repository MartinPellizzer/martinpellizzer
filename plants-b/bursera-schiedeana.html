Bursera schiedeana

Bursera schiedeana, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes species known for their aromatic resins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a leathery texture with a fragrant scent when crushed.

Bursera schiedeana is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
