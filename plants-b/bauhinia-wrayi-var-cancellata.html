Bauhinia wrayi var. cancellata

Bauhinia wrayi var. cancellata, commonly known as the white bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
