Bromus richardsonii subsp. flexuosus

Bromus richardsonii subsp.

flexuosus, commonly known as the flexible brome, is a perennial grass native to western North America. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and grasslands.

The plant has slender, tufted stems that often bend or flex, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color.

It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.
