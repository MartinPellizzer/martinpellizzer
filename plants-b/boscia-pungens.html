Boscia pungens

Boscia pungens, commonly known as the spiky boschia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round, reddish fruits that are edible but have a strong, acidic taste. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Boscia pungens is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often used in traditional medicine.
