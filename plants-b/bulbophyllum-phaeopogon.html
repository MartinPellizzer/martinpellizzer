Bulbophyllum phaeopogon

Bulbophyllum phaeopogon, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated pseudobulbs and dark red to purple inflorescences. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with contrasting coloration, often featuring yellow or greenish sepals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Bulbophyllum phaeopogon is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
