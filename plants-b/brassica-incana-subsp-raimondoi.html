Brassica incana subsp. raimondoi

Brassica incana subsp.

raimondoi, commonly known as the Raimondo's hoary cabbages, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small white or pale yellow flowers.

Native to the Mediterranean region, this plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats. It produces small, round, and usually hairy fruits known as siliques.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
