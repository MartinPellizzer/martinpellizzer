Brosimum refractum

Brosimum refractum, commonly known as the rubber tree, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its thick, smooth bark and large, palmate leaves. The plant is known for its latex-producing capabilities, which have been historically used in the rubber industry. Its leaves are typically green and have a waxy texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The tree thrives in tropical climates and is often found in humid lowland forests.
