Brassica chinensis f. indivisa

Brassica chinensis f. indivisa, commonly known as the Chinese kale or gai lan, is a cultivated variant of the Chinese cabbage.

It is characterized by its dark green, stalked leaves that are typically harvested before they become too fibrous. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cauliflower. Its botanical structure features a thick, edible stem and broad, serrated leaves.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is widely grown in Asian cuisines for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
