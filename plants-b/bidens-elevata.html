Bidens elevata

Bidens elevata, commonly known as the common beggar's ticks, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers with five petals. The plant produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along stream banks.

Bidens elevata is known for its ability to tolerate a wide range of environmental conditions.
