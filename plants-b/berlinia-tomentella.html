Berlinia tomentella

Berlinia tomentella, commonly known as the African velvet tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety bark and large, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spherical pods containing flat, edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The tree has traditional uses in local medicine and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
