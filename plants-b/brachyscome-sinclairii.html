Brachyscome sinclairii

Brachyscome sinclairii, commonly known as the Sinclair's everlasting.

It is a flowering plant native to Australia, characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
