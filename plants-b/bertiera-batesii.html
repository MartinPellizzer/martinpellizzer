Bertiera batesii

Bertiera batesii, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and fragrant, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, winged fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
