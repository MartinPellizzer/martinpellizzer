Biscutella valentina subsp. lusitanica

Biscutella valentina subsp.

lusitanica, commonly known as the Lusitanian biscuitflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and aromatic foliage.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in grasslands and rocky areas. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers in early spring.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
