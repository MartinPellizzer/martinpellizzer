Barbarea balcana

Barbarea balcana, commonly known as the Balkan rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and basal rosette of fleshy, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Barbarea balcana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and distinctive foliage.
