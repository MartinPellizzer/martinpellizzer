Boea crassifolia

Boea crassifolia, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen or broad-leafed ivy, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover in gardens.

Boea crassifolia is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
