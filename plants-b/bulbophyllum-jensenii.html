Bulbophyllum jensenii

Bulbophyllum jensenii, commonly known as Jensen's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This epiphytic plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and often produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers. It typically grows on trees or rocks, deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. The plant features pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its survival in its natural habitat.

Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects.
