Begonia modesta

Begonia modesta, commonly known as the silver velvet begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety silver-green foliage and clusters of small, delicate pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a distinctive waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Begonia modesta thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers moist, well-draining soil.
