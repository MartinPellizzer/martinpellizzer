Bauhinia geminata

Bauhinia geminata, commonly known as the "crown of thorns," is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa, the Americas, and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pods containing flat, edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. Bauhinia geminata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been utilized in traditional medicine for various therapeutic purposes.

Its growth habit includes both shrub and tree forms, depending on environmental conditions.
