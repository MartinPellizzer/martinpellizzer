Berberis sanguinea var. microphylla

Berberis sanguinea var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf barberry, is a compact shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features small, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red-orange berries that are edible but often considered ornamental. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its hardy nature and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
