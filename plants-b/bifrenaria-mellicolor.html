Bifrenaria mellicolor

Bifrenaria mellicolor, commonly known as the honey-colored fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that display a striking honey-yellow hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by spore-bearing structures called sori. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Bifrenaria mellicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
