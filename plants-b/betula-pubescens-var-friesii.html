Betula pubescens var. friesii

Betula pubescens var. friesii, commonly known as the silver birch, is a variant of the silver birch tree native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark and slender, often pendulous branches. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the autumn. This tree thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

It plays an important role in ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
