Breynia pseudorostrata

Breynia pseudorostrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf breynia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and typically have four petals.

Breynia pseudorostrata is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is often found in coastal or dry environments.
