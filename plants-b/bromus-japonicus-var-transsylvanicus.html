Bromus japonicus var. transsylvanicus

Bromus japonicus var. transsylvanicus, commonly known as the Transylvanian brome, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It is characterized by its adaptability to a range of soil types and its ability to thrive in moderate climates.

This variety is often studied for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
