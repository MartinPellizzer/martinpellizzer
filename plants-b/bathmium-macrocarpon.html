Bathmium macrocarpon

Bathmium macrocarpon, commonly known as the giant fruit-bearing plant, is a unique species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter, containing numerous seeds enclosed in a thick, fibrous pulp. The plant has broad, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in humid environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor firmly in nutrient-rich soil.

Bathmium macrocarpon plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing food for various wildlife species and contributing to forest biodiversity.
