Berberis wawrana

Berberis wawrana, commonly known as the Himalayan barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges, typical of the Berberis genus. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

The plant also has historical uses in traditional medicine.
