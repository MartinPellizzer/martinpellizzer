Baccharis lingulata

Baccharis lingulata, commonly known as narrowleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and desert scrub habitats. It has a woody stem and can reach heights of up to 3 meters.

Baccharis lingulata is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and its drought tolerance.
