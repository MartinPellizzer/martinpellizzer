Bursera toledoana

Bursera toledoana, commonly known as the Toledo frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees, and is characterized by its aromatic resins. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and produces small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture, and it is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky soils.

Bursera toledoana is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
