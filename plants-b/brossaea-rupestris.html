Brossaea rupestris

Brossaea rupestris, commonly known as the rock brossa, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Brossaea rupestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in rocky or sandy habitats.
