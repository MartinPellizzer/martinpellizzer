Barleria gueinzii

Barleria gueinzii, commonly known as the African rock rose, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, or purple. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Barleria gueinzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
