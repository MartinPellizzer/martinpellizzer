Bulbophyllum ogoouense

Bulbophyllum ogoouense, commonly known as the Ogoouense orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to West Africa.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, serving as water storage structures to help it survive in its native habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
