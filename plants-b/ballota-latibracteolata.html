Ballota latibracteolata

Ballota latibracteolata, commonly known as the broad-bract ballota, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its seed pods are typically hairy and contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Ballota latibracteolata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in sunny, dry environments.
