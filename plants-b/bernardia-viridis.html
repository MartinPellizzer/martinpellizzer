Bernardia viridis

Bernardia viridis, commonly known as the green Bernardia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its foliage is evergreen, and it produces small, edible berries that are sometimes used in local cuisines.
