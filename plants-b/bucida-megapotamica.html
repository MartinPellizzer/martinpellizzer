Bucida megapotamica

Bucida megapotamica, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, orange-red flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in coastal and dry regions, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical areas.
