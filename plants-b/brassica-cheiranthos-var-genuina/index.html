Brassica Cheiranthos Var. Genuina

Brassica cheiranthos var. genuina is a rare and lesser-known member of the Brassicaceae family. Native to specific regions in Europe, this plant thrives in rocky and alpine environments. Its distinctive appearance includes small, white or purple flowers and hairy stems that help it survive in harsh climates. Despite its beauty, it remains underappreciated and often overlooked by botanists and gardeners alike. Studying this plant offers insights into adaptation and biodiversity in extreme habitats.
