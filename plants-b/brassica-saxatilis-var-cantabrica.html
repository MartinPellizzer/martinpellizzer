Brassica saxatilis var. cantabrica

Brassica saxatilis var. cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a perennial species characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lobed and often covered in a fine layer of hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
