Bauhinia polyacantha

Bauhinia polyacantha, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, papery, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in bright orange or red hues during the dry season. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and its flowers are typically clustered in racemes. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree also has cultural and medicinal significance in various regions.
