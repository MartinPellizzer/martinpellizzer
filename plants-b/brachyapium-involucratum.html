Brachyapium involucratum

Brachyapium involucratum, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
