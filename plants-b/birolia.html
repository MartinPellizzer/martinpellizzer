Birolia

Birolia, commonly known as the bluebell of the south, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in a basal rosette. Birolia thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
