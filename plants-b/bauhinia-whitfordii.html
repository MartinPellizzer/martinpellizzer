Bauhinia whitfordii

Bauhinia whitfordii, commonly known as the white bauhinia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, papery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and delicate floral display.

Bauhinia whitfordii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
