Blechnum lanceola

Blechnum lanceola, commonly known as the lanceleaf holly fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped, leathery fronds that are often dark green in color. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to soil or rock surfaces.

Blechnum lanceola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
