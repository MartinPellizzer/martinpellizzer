Bromus paniculatus

Bromus paniculatus, commonly known as the panicled brome, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, blade-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a paniculate arrangement of spikelets, which are small, clustered flower clusters.

Bromus paniculatus is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in stabilizing soil in agricultural and natural landscapes.
