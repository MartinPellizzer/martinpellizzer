Buddleja calycina

Buddleja calycina, commonly known as the Cape rose or velvet bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough, velvety texture.

Buddleja calycina is adapted to dry conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.
