Billbergia gracilis

Billbergia gracilis, commonly known as the slender billbergia, is a striking epiphytic plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences with colorful flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette. Its foliage is often bluish-green with a waxy texture, and it thrives in bright, indirect light.

Billbergia gracilis is popular as a decorative plant due to its ornamental appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
