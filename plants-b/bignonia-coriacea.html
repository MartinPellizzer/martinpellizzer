Bignonia coriacea

Bignonia coriacea, commonly known as the rough bignonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody vine structure and heart-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces striking yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. These flowers are adapted for pollination by bees and other pollinators.

Bignonia coriacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
