Buddleja verleyseniana

Buddleja verleyseniana, commonly known as the Verley's bushmaster, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant, tubular flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to both drought and poor soil conditions. Its flowers are typically purple to violet in color, attracting a wide range of pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Buddleja verleyseniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in gardens and landscapes.
