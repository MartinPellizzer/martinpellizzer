Brasilidium pectorale

Brasilidium pectorale, commonly known as the chestnut tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters in height, characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a habitat for various epiphytic plants and insects.

The tree is valued for its timber and has cultural significance in several indigenous communities.
