Brassia jipijapensis

Brassia jipijapensis, commonly known as the Jipijapa orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces strikingly colorful flowers, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom during the rainy season. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils, making it suitable for cultivation in shaded, humid environments.

Brassia jipijapensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
