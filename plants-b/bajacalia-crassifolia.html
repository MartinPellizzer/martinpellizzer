Bajacalia crassifolia

Bajacalia crassifolia, commonly known as the thickleaf desert willow, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and showy, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, playing a vital role in local ecosystems.

Bajacalia crassifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
