Benthamia japonica var. sinensis

Benthamia japonica var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese benthamia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often tinged with reddish or purple hues. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and arranged in dense clusters at the ends of its stems.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and blooms.
