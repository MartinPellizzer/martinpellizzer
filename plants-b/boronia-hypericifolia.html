Boronia hypericifolia

Boronia hypericifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. It blooms during the autumn season, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
