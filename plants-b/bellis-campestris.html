Bellis campestris

Bellis campestris, commonly known as the field daisy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features rosettes of basal leaves that are typically hairy and spoon-shaped. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with white petals surrounding a yellow center, blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Bellis campestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden settings.
