Bauhinia nitida

Bauhinia nitida, commonly known as the white-veined bauhinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, each bearing prominent white veins that contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, typically blooming during the spring and summer months. Bauhinia nitida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.
