Brassaiopsis speciosa var. typica

Brassaiopsis speciosa var. typica, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow inflorescences that resemble flames, which appear during the dry season. The plant has glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.

Its rapid growth and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to landscapes seeking vibrant color.
