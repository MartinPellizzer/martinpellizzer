Baccharis tarchonanthoides

Baccharis tarchonanthoides, commonly known as the Mexican fragrant baccharis, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features aromatic foliage with silver-white undersides, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Baccharis tarchonanthoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
