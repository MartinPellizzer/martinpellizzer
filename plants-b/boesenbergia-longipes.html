Boesenbergia longipes

Boesenbergia longipes, commonly known as the purple ginger or wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant features rhizomatous stems with long, narrow leaves that are often tinged with purple. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to green, with three petals and three sepals.

Boesenbergia longipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
