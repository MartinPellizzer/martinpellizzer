Berberis × rubrostilla var. chealii

Berberis × rubrostilla var. chealii, commonly known as the Cheal's barberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy appearance. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its hardiness and attractive foliage, it is often used in landscaping for its decorative and protective qualities.
