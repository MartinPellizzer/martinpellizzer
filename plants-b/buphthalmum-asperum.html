Buphthalmum asperum

Buphthalmum asperum, commonly known as the rough ox-eye daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy yellow flower heads surrounded by white ray florets. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Buphthalmum asperum is known for its resilience and ability to withstand dry conditions.
