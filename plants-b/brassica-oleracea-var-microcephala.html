Brassica oleracea var. microcephala

Brassica oleracea var. microcephala, commonly known as cauliflower, is a cultivar of the cabbage species characterized by its dense, white curd formed by tightly clustered flower buds.

It is a biennial plant typically grown as an annual, originating from the Mediterranean region. The plant has thick, stalk-like stems and broad, dark green leaves that surround the edible head. The curd, which is the main edible part, develops at the apex of the plant and is harvested before the flowers open.

It is rich in nutrients and is widely cultivated for its culinary and nutritional value.
