Bellium minutum

Bellium minutum, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in alpine environments. The plant produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or violet in color, which bloom in the summer months. Its compact size and delicate appearance make it a notable species in its native habitat.

Bellium minutum is often found in open meadows and is adapted to withstand harsh weather conditions.
