Bulbophyllum mahakamense

Bulbophyllum mahakamense, commonly known as the Mahakam orchid, is a species of orchid native to Indonesia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often strikingly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of small, fragrant blossoms that can vary in color from white to pink.

The plant's pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in the humid, tropical environments of its native region.
