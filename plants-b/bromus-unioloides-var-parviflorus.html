Bromus unioloides var. parviflorus

Bromus unioloides var. parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flower brome, is a grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled seeds.
