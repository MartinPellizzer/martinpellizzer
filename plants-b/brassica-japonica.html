Brassica japonica

Brassica japonica, commonly known as Japanese mustard or Japanese rapeseed, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to East Asia and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and upright, branching stems. The plant produces small, round pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional Asian cuisine. Brassica japonica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a cover crop.

Its leaves and seeds contain glucosinolates, which contribute to its pungent flavor and potential health benefits.
