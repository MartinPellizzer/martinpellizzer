Berberis tachiensis

Berberis tachiensis, commonly known as Japanese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features spiny, thorned stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
