Bromopsis tomentella

Bromopsis tomentella, commonly known as the woolly bromus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a woolly appearance. It typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Bromopsis tomentella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and appearance.
