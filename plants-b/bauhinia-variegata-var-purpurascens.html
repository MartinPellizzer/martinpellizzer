Bauhinia variegata var. purpurascens

Bauhinia variegata var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple flame acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically three to five lobes, and its striking purple or pink floral clusters. The flowers are usually borne in racemes and are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide shade in warm climates.
