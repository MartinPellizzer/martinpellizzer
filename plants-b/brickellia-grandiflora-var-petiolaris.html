Brickellia grandiflora var. petiolaris

Brickellia grandiflora var. petiolaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaf brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas. It has a compact growth habit with multiple stems and is known for its drought tolerance.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important plant in native pollinator habitats.
