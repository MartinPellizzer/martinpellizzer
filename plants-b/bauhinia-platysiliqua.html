Bauhinia platysiliqua

Bauhinia platysiliqua, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flame-like clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often divided into small, feathery segments. Its flowers are actinomorphic and consist of five petals, with the outer two petals being larger and more prominent.

Bauhinia platysiliqua is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
