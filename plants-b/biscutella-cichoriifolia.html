Biscutella cichoriifolia

Biscutella cichoriifolia, commonly known as the chicory-leaved biscuitroot, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, exhibiting a low, compact growth habit. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a distinctive hairy texture, while the flowers are pale yellow to white.

Biscutella cichoriifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
