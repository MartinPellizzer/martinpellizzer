Buddleja auriculata

Buddleja auriculata, commonly known as the silver-leaf buddleja, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees.

Buddleja auriculata is also valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions.
