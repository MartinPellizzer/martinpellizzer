Bombacopsis trinitensis var. mucronulata

Bombacopsis trinitensis var. mucronulata, commonly known as the Trinidadian ceiba, is a deciduous tree native to the Caribbean, particularly Trinidad and Tobago.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and smooth, greyish bark. The tree produces showy, pale yellow flowers followed by large, woody pods containing flat, fibrous seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests and coastal areas.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood and has cultural significance in local communities.
