Banisteria metallicolor var. typica

Banisteria metallicolor var. typica, commonly known as the silverleaf banisteria, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture. It produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, often in shades of white and pink.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded garden settings.
