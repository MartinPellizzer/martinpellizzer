Buchnera heyneana

Buchnera heyneana, commonly known as the mountain balm, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. Buchnera heyneana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in suitable climates.
