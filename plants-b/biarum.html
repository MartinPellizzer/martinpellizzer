Biarum

Biarum, commonly known as the bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the Campanulaceae family.

These plants are characterized by their bell-shaped or tubular flowers, which can range in color from blue to purple. Biarum species are typically herbaceous perennials, often found in woodland or shaded areas. They have lance-shaped leaves and produce showy blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The genus includes several species, some of which are cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens.
