Barleria lanceolata subsp. lanceolata

Barleria lanceolata subsp.

lanceolata, commonly known as narrowleaf barleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
