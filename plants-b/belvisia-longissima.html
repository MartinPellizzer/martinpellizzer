Belvisia longissima

Belvisia longissima, commonly known as the long-stemmed belvisia, is a unique plant species characterized by its elongated, slender stems.

It is native to specific regions in South America, particularly in the Andean foothills. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

Belvisia longissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its distinctive appearance.
