Baissea aframensis

Baissea aframensis, commonly known as the African water plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white. Baissea aframensis is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality in wetland ecosystems.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
