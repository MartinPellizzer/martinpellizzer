Borzicactus fieldianus subsp. tessellatus

Borzicactus fieldianus subsp.

tessellatus, commonly known as the tessellated borzicactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its ribbed, cylindrical stems that are covered in small, tuberculate areoles.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are clustered at the top of the stems. Its distinctive tessellated pattern on the stem surface is a key identifying feature.

This subspecies is adapted to extreme drought conditions and thrives in well-drained sandy soils.
