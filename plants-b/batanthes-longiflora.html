Batanthes longiflora

Batanthes longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered batanthes, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems and clusters of small, delicate white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Batanthes longiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
