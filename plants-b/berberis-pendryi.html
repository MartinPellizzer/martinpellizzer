Berberis pendryi

Berberis pendryi, commonly known as the Himalayan barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant autumn foliage, which turns a striking orange or red.
