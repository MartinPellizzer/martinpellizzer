Begonia anemoniflora

Begonia anemoniflora, commonly known as the anemone begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that resemble those of the anemone, with five to seven petals in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often grown as a houseplant or in shaded garden beds.

Begonia anemoniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
