Baccharis incisa

Baccharis incisa, commonly known as coastal baccharis, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is often found in dunes and coastal scrub habitats. It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Baccharis incisa is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
