Barringtonia integrifolia

Barringtonia integrifolia, commonly known as the white mangosteen or simply mangosteen, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The tree produces a fruit with a thick, colorful rind enclosing a segmented, sweet flesh. Its leaves are broad, glossy, and entire, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and the edible fruit it produces.
