Boronia haloragoides

Boronia haloragoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boronia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It is often found in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
