Bellardiochloa carica

Bellardiochloa carica, commonly known as the Caribbean grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Bellardiochloa carica is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to salt spray and sandy soils.
