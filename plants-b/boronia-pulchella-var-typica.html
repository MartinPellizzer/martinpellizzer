Boronia pulchella var. typica

Boronia pulchella var. typica, commonly known as the pink boronia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators like birds and insects.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species in some regions.
