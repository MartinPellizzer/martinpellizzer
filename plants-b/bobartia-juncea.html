Bobartia juncea

Bobartia juncea, commonly known as the slender bobartia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It typically features small, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora.
