Brownlowia elata

Brownlowia elata, commonly known as the African butter tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. Brownlowia elata is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It also has traditional uses in some African cultures for medicinal purposes.
