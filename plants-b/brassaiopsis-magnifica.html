Brassaiopsis magnifica

Brassaiopsis magnifica, commonly known as the giant cliffbrass, is a striking member of the Araliaceae family native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length, giving it a dramatic and lush appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by berries that are edible for some bird species.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity in cultivation, Brassaiopsis magnifica is a sought-after plant among collectors and botanists.
