Begonia rhizocarpa

Begonia rhizocarpa, commonly known as the root-bearing begonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features fleshy, tuberous roots that store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with intricate patterns on the petals. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Begonia rhizocarpa is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of growing conditions.
