Begonia glabra var. physalifolia

Begonia glabra var. physalifolia, commonly known as the bubble begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves and clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, inflated seed pods that resemble bubbles, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners interested in unique foliage and ornamental value.
