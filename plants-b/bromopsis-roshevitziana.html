Bromopsis roshevitziana

Bromopsis roshevitziana, commonly known as the Roshevitz's bromus, is a species of grass native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
