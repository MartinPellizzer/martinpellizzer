Brassica torulosa

Brassica torulosa, commonly known as the woolly cabbage, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are edible and have a mild, cabbage-like flavor.

Brassica torulosa is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
