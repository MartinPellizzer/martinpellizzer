Bulbophyllum chrysoglossum

Bulbophyllum chrysoglossum, commonly known as the golden-tongue orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs. The plant produces striking, golden-yellow flowers with a distinctive, tongue-like lip that gives it its common name. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
