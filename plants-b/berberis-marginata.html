Berberis marginata

Berberis marginata, commonly known as the African bushberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. The plant has yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its leaves are evergreen, with a distinctive waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Berberis marginata is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils.
