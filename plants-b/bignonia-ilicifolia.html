Bignonia ilicifolia

Bignonia ilicifolia, commonly known as the leatherleaf bignonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.

Bignonia ilicifolia is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in supporting local wildlife.
