Boscia rehmanniana

Boscia rehmanniana, commonly known as the wild quinine tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fissured bark and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Boscia rehmanniana has traditional medicinal uses and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
