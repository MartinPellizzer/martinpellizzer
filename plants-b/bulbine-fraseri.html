Bulbine fraseri

Bulbine fraseri, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers, which range in color from yellow to orange, bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Bulbine fraseri is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
