Berberis nervosa var. mendocinensis

Berberis nervosa var. mendocinensis, commonly known as the Mendocino barberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
