Berberis wuchuanensis

Berberis wuchuanensis, commonly known as the Wuchuan barberry, is a shrub native to China, specifically the Wuchuan region in Sichuan Province.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties, with its roots containing berberine, a compound used in traditional Chinese medicine.
