Bonyunia aquatica

Bonyunia aquatica, commonly known as water bonyunia, is a perennial aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow waters, such as ponds and slow-moving streams, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich environments. The plant has long, narrow leaves that float on the water surface, while its roots anchor it to the substrate. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of white or pale yellow.

Bonyunia aquatica plays a role in stabilizing water ecosystems by preventing erosion and providing habitat for aquatic life.
