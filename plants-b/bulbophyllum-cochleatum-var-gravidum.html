Bulbophyllum cochleatum var. gravidum

Bulbophyllum cochleatum var. gravidum, commonly known as the "Pregnant Orchid," is a fascinating epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, spiraled pseudobulbs that resemble a coiled shell, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and ease of cultivation.
