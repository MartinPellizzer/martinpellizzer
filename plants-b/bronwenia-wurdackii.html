Bronwenia wurdackii

Bronwenia wurdackii, commonly known as the Wurdack's rock fig, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. It is often found growing in coastal heathlands and is valued for its ornamental appearance and hardiness.

Bronwenia wurdackii is also notable for its ability to withstand salt spray and strong winds, making it suitable for coastal gardens.
