Bignonia imperatoris-maximilianii

Bignonia imperatoris-maximilianii, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is a vigorous climber with long, slender stems that can reach lengths of up to 15 meters. The plant produces striking yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
