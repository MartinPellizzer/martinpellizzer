Brassica rapa subsp. trilocularis

Brassica rapa subsp.

trilocularis, commonly known as the Chinese kale or Chinese broccoli, is a biennial or annual plant native to East Asia. It is characterized by its thick, dark green leaves that are often curled or crinkled, and it produces small, yellow flowers in a compact cluster.

The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like cabbage and mustard. It is valued for its edible leaves and stems, which are used in various Asian cuisines.

The plant prefers cool climates and well-drained soil for optimal growth.
