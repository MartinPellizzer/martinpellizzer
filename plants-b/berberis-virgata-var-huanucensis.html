Berberis virgata var. huanucensis

Berberis virgata var. huanucensis, commonly known as the Huanuco barberry, is a shrub native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny branches and small, yellowish-green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces bright orange-red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
