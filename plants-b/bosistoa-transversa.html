Bosistoa transversa

Bosistoa transversa, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a rare and valuable tree species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly resistant to decay, making it prized for construction and furniture. The plant has a long, straight trunk with a smooth, dark bark that becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

Bosistoa transversa plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
