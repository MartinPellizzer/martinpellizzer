Bambusa multiplex var. solida

Bambusa multiplex var. solida, commonly known as the solid bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and thick, solid culms that provide structural strength. The leaves are long, narrow, and dark green, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This variety is highly resistant to wind and drought, making it suitable for landscaping in various climates.

Its compact growth makes it ideal for small gardens or as a decorative feature in larger landscapes.
