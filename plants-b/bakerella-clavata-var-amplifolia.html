Bakerella clavata var. amplifolia

Bakerella clavata var. amplifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed bakerella, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are broader than the typical variety, with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
