Buxus rheedioides

Buxus rheedioides, commonly known as the African boxwood, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features dense, compact foliage with small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a dark green color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Buxus rheedioides is often used in horticulture for topiary and as a foundation plant in gardens.
