Blumea dasycoma var. pinnatifida

Blumea dasycoma var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the hairy blumea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has opposite, pinnately lobed leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

This variant is distinguished by its hairy foliage and is often found in forest understories.
