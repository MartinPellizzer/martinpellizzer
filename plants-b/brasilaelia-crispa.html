Brasilaelia crispa

Brasilaelia crispa, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its rapid growth and showy flowers make it a notable species in horticulture and ecological studies.
