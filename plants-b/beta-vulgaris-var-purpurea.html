Beta vulgaris var. purpurea

Beta vulgaris var. purpurea, commonly known as beetroot or chard, is a member of the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots and broad, arrow-shaped leaves that can vary in color from green to red and purple. The plant is cultivated for its edible roots and leaves, which are rich in nutrients such as iron and beta-carotene. Its leaves are often used in cooking, while the roots are popular in salads and as a cooked vegetable.

The plant thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, making it a versatile crop in temperate regions.
