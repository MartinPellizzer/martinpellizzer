Bidaria latifolia

Bidaria latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf bidaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
