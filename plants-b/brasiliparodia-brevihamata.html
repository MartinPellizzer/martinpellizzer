Brasiliparodia brevihamata

Brasiliparodia brevihamata, commonly known as the Brazilian short-horned orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, hairy leaves that are short and broad. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and green, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
