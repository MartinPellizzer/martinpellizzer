Bassovia macrophylla

Bassovia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved bassovia, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length, which are typically glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
