Boehmeria platyphylla f. foliosa

Boehmeria platyphylla f. foliosa, commonly known as the broad-leafed nettle, is a variant of the Boehmeria genus native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The leaves are usually a vibrant green, with a rough texture and serrated edges. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
