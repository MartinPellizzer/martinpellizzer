Biscutella didyma var. taraxacifolia

Biscutella didyma var. taraxacifolia, commonly known as the meadow bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically lanceolate and have a slightly hairy surface, while the flowers are usually pink or purple in color. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
