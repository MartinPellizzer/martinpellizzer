Bomarea pumila

Bomarea pumila, commonly known as the dwarf bomarea, is a herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with six petals and a distinctive, elongated spur.

Bomarea pumila thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
