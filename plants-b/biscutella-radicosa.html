Biscutella radicosa

Biscutella radicosa, commonly known as the root biscuit plant, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant has fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.

Biscutella radicosa is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry grasslands.
