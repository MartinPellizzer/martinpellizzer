Begonia herveyana

Begonia herveyana, commonly known as the Hervey's begonia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from deep green to variegated patterns. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Begonia herveyana is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden beds.
