Betula cylindrostachya var. resinosa

Betula cylindrostachya var. resinosa, commonly known as the resinous birch, is a variant of the cylindrostachya birch species.

It is native to certain regions of Asia, particularly in the eastern Himalayas. This plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and slender, upright growth habit. The leaves are alternate, triangular, and have a serrated margin.

It produces small, catkin-like flowers and is adapted to grow in temperate to subalpine environments.
