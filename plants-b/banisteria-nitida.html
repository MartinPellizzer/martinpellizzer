Banisteria nitida

Banisteria nitida, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a climbing shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces elongated, woody pods containing flat, edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
