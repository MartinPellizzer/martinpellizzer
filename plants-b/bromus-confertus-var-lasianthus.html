Bromus confertus var. lasianthus

Bromus confertus var. lasianthus, commonly known as the dense brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
