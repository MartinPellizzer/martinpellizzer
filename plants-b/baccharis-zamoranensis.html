Baccharis zamoranensis

Baccharis zamoranensis, commonly known as the Andean baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
