Bromus sinensis

Bromus sinensis, commonly known as Chinese brome, is a flowering plant species native to China.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of spikelets, each bearing two to three small, awned lemmas.

Bromus sinensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in challenging environments.
