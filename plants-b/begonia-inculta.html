Begonia inculta

Begonia inculta, commonly known as the wild begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and thrives in humid climates. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Begonia inculta is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
