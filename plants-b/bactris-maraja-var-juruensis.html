Bactris maraja var. juruensis

Bactris maraja var. juruensis, commonly known as the Jurua palm, is a species of palm native to the Amazon Basin, particularly in Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by elongated, fibrous fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses among local communities.
