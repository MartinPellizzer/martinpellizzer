Babiana mucronata subsp. minor

Babiana mucronata subsp.

minor, commonly known as the dwarf babiana, is a striking bulbous plant native to the Western Cape of South Africa. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces upright, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

The flowers are typically borne on tall, slender stems and bloom in late winter to early spring. This subspecies is notable for its compact size and vibrant, showy inflorescences.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
