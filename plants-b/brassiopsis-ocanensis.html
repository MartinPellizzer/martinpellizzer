Brassiopsis ocanensis

Brassiopsis ocanensis, commonly known as the Ocan's brassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
