Buda diandra

Buda diandra, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous flowers that resemble small bells, typically ranging in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, round fruits that are edible and often used in traditional cuisines. Buda diandra thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and as a climbing plant.
