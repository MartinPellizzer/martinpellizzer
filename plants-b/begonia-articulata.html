Begonia articulata

Begonia articulata, commonly known as the jointed begonia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, segmented stems that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a glossy surface.

Begonia articulata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
