Bidens chilensis var. apiifolia

Bidens chilensis var. apiifolia, commonly known as the Chilean ironweed, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as grasslands and riverbanks. Its leaves are usually pinnately divided, giving it a fern-like appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
