Begonia moneta

Begonia moneta, commonly known as the silver-leaf begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features velvety, silver-green leaves with a distinctive pattern of deep green veins, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
