Bernardia polymorpha f. angustifolia

Bernardia polymorpha f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bernardia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, and it produces distinctive, elongated fruits that contain seeds. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to the cool, high-altitude environments where it thrives.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
