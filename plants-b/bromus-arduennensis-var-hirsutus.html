Bromus arduennensis var. hirsutus

Bromus arduennensis var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hirsute brome, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy leaves and culms, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at higher elevations. It is a perennial species that thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
